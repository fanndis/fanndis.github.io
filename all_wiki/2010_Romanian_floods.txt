The '''2010 Romanian floods''' ({{lang-ro|Inundațiile din iunie 2010 din România}}) were the result of an [[extreme weather]] event that struck [[Romania]] in late June 2010. Currently, at least 21 people died. ==Overview==
On 29 June 2010, the [[Siret River|River Siret]] threatened to break through the [[Dyke (construction)|dykes]] protecting the town of [[Şendreni]], as locals and [[emergency services]] reinforced the dykes with sandbags to prevent the river breaking out and flooding the town.<ref name="Romania floods kill 21">[http://www.hindustantimes.com/Romania-floods-kill-21/Article1-565018.aspx Romania floods kill 21] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100703154155/http://www.hindustantimes.com/Romania-floods-kill-21/Article1-565018.aspx |date=2010-07-03 }}</ref>

Romanian Prime Minister [[Emil Boc]] visited the area, touring [[Suceava County|Suceava]] and [[Botoşani County|Botoşani]] counties, both hard-hit by flooding and still under weather advisories. Officials said hundreds of people have been forced from their homes, dozens of houses have collapsed and hundreds of homes were damaged.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.realitatea.net/apele-au-devastat-moldova--meteorologii-au-emis-un-nou-cod-galben-de-inundatii-video_720859.html |title=Apele au devastat Moldova. Meteorologii au emis cod galben de inundaţii VIDEO |publisher=Realitatea TV |language=Romanian |date=June 30, 2010 |accessdate=June 30, 2010 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100701025856/http://www.realitatea.net/apele-au-devastat-moldova--meteorologii-au-emis-un-nou-cod-galben-de-inundatii-video_720859.html |archivedate=1 July 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

According to an IGSU press release, the storms and flooding affected the counties of [[Alba County|Alba]], [[Arad County|Arad]], [[Bacău County|Bacău]], [[Botoşani County|Botoşani]], [[Brașov County|Brașov]],  [[Cluj County|Cluj]], [[Hunedoara County|Hunedoara]], [[Iaşi County|Iași]], [[Mehedinţi County|Mehedinţi]], [[Neamţ County|Neamț]], [[Olt County|Olt]], [[Prahova County|Prahova]], [[Sălaj County|Sălaj]], [[Sibiu County|Sibiu]], [[Suceava County|Suceava]], [[Timiș County|Timiș]], [[Tulcea County|Tulcea]], [[Vâlcea County|Vâlcea]] and [[Vrancea County|Vrancea]].

One person in [[Alba County]] died and at least 160 people all over the country were evacuated.

Romanian Interior Minister [[Vasile Blaga]] said that two people in [[Cluj County]] lost their lives.

The north-eastern town of [[Dorohoi]] witnessed 6 deaths in the night of June 28–29 as floods rose to just over 1 metre, 3,3 feet in some places. Several roads into Dorohoi remained either washed away or under water.<ref name="Romania floods kill 21"/> The heavy rain that had been falling for much of the past week in the Balkan country and forecasters have warned that it will continue in northeast Romania until Wednesday morning.<ref name="Romania floods kill 21"/> The emergency situations spokeswoman [[Dorina Lupu]] from [[Botoşani County|Botoşani judet]] said unusually heavy rain had killed 6 people, most in the town of Dorohoi on the 29th.<ref>[http://www.nce.co.uk/news/water/10-dead-in-romanian-floods/8602367.article 10 dead in Romanian floods]</ref>

On July 5, 2010, the [[Danube]] in [[Galaţi]] recorded a flow rate of 676&nbsp;cm (76&nbsp;cm above the rate of flooding) and a flow of 16,055&nbsp;m/s. At [[Frumuşica, Botoşani|Şendreni]], [[Siret]] had a rate of 728&nbsp;cm (78&nbsp;cm over the danger level).<ref name="Dig de 4,5 km la Galaţi">{{cite web|url=http://www.click.ro/actualitate/din_tara/dig-suprainaltare-dunare-valea_orasului-pompieri-jandarmi_0_987501267.html|title=Dig de 4,5 km la Galaţi| accessdate= 6 July 2010 <!--DASHBot-->}}</ref> The local emergency services reinforced the [[Dyke (construction)|dykes]] in the afternoon and narrowly averted any more flooding. It was flooded during the July 2010 flood as was its names sake in [[Galaţi County]].

In Galaţi, the city was building a dam 1.5 m high, 4.5&nbsp;km long, with the support of 1,400 military personnel from the [[Ministry of Administration and Interior (Romania)|Ministry of Administration and Interior]] and the [[Ministry of National Defense (Romania)|Ministry of Defence]]. If the dam breaches, the resulting flood would drown one-quarter of the city, including its shipyard, port and customs office. Nearly 12,000 jobs exist in the area, and about €45 million is lost daily and around €250 million in property losses have been caused by the flooding in Galaţi.<ref name="Dig de 4,5 km la Galaţi"/>

== Casualties ==
The flooding has affected 1,873 homes and farms, 1,065 hectares of farmland, 1,928 hectares of forest and pasture, two kilometers of national roads, {{Nowrap|37 km}} of county roads, {{Nowrap|91 km}} of communal roads and clogged 424 wells. Authorities say at least 21  people have died and three are missing after heavy rains and flooding in northeastern Romania. Hundreds of people had to be evacuated from their homes, mainly in Suceava and Botoşani departments, close to the [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] border. They spent the night in schools and sports halls or with relatives.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.antena3.ro/romania/localitatile-de-pe-siret-din-suceava-vor-fi-evacuate_102690.html |title=România sub ape: Debitul Siretului se va dubla. Mai multe localităţi din Suceava, evacuate (VIDEO) |language=Romanian |date=June 29, 2010 |accessdate=June 29, 2010 |publisher=Antena 3 (Video) |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100701232656/http://www.antena3.ro/romania/localitatile-de-pe-siret-din-suceava-vor-fi-evacuate_102690.html |archivedate=1 July 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On June 30, 2010, 21 people were killed by the flood in Romania and Ukraine while another 9,500 people were evacuated, according to authorities from both countries.<ref name=Bloomberg/> The majority of victims were Romanians; 1 Ukrainian was killed by the flood and 1,083 were evacuated.<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/71590/ One killed, three missing after floods in Chernivtsi region], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (July 1, 2010)</ref>

==Losses==
The Romanian Interior Minister Vasile Blaga told parliament that losses were the equivalent to  0.6% GDP. The agriculture ministry has estimated that of 6,700,000 tonnes of wheat for and  2,000,000 hectares of land were by the 30th.<ref>[http://uk.reuters.com/article/idUKLDE65T176._CH_.2420 Floods kill 24, Romania to seek emergency EU aid]</ref> [[Moldova]] and [[Ukraine]] had yet to assess the crisis in their flooded regions.

== Effects ==
Most of the victims [[drowned]] when they were trying to escape, while two people were struck by lightning.
Three trains were stuck in northeastern Suceava county, due to the flooding of a 50-meter railroad segment. A southwestern customs in [[Mehedinţi County]] was flooded, and alluvial deposits were reported on two national roads in northeastern [[Neamţ County]]. Floods spawned by heavy rainfall also affected thousands of acres of pasture and farmland, roads, bridges and power lines in the rural areas in the northeastern part of the country.

== Emergency response ==
Local emergency committees and volunteer emergency services rallied 530 firefighters and 160 volunteers, who took part in damage control efforts. Russia has also delivered 70 tonnes of humanitarian aid to Ukraine in response to the flooding.<ref name=KyivP3/>
