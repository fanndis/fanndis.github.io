'''Letitia Michelle Wright''' (born 31 October 1993) is a Guyanese-British actress. Beginning her professional career in 2011, she has played roles in ''[[Black Panther (film)|Black Panther]]'' and several British TV series, including ''[[Top Boy]]'', ''[[Coming Up (TV series)|Coming Up]]'', ''[[Chasing Shadows (TV series)|Chasing Shadows]]'', ''[[Humans (TV series)|Humans]]'', the ''[[Doctor Who]]'' episode "[[Face the Raven]]" and the ''[[Black Mirror]]'' episode "[[Black Museum (Black Mirror)|Black Museum]]"; for the latter she received a [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nomination for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or Movie|Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or Movie]].

The [[British Academy of Film and Television Arts]] (BAFTA) named Wright among the 2015 group of BAFTA Breakthrough Brits for her role in the award-winning film ''[[Urban Hymn]]''.<ref>[http://www.bafta.org/media-centre/press-releases/bafta-and-burberry-reveal-2015-breakthrough-brits "BAFTA and Burberry Reveal 2015 Breakthrough Brits"]. BAFTA.org, 10 November 2015. Retrieved 22 February 2018.</ref> In 2018, she achieved global recognition for her portrayal of [[Shuri (comics)|Shuri]] in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] film ''[[Black Panther (film)|Black Panther]]''. She reprised the role in ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]]'' and ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'', the latter of which is the [[List of highest-grossing films#Highest-grossing films|highest-grossing film of all time]]. In 2019, she received the [[BAFTA Rising Star Award]].

==Early life==
Wright was born in [[Georgetown, Guyana]]. Her family moved to [[London]] when she was seven years old and she attended school there.<ref name=TheGuardian>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/apr/05/letitia-wright-britains-rising-screen-star-michael-caton-jones-leonardo-dicaprio|title=Letitia Wright, Britain's newest rising screen star, says black actors need more positive roles|author=Alberge, Dalya|work =[[The Guardian]]|date=4 April 2015|accessdate=16 February 2017|quote=Wright, 21....}}</ref> Having been brought up in [[Tottenham]] and attending [[Duke's Aldridge Academy|Northumberland Park Community School]], she has stated, "I'll always be a north London girl."<ref>Aron, Isabelle (8 February 2018), [https://www.timeout.com/london/film/black-panther-star-letitia-wright-ill-always-be-a-north-london-girl "Black Panther' star Letitia Wright: 'I’ll always be a [[North London]] girl’]. ''Time Out''. Retrieved 22 February 2018.</ref>

==Career==
Wright performed in school plays, but credits her desire to be a professional actress to the 2006 film ''[[Akeelah and the Bee]]''. She found [[Keke Palmer]]'s performance inspiring, remarking that the role "resonated. It's one of the reasons why I'm here".<ref name=THR5>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/letitia-wright-5-things-you-didnt-know-black-panther-star-1158768|title=Letitia Wright: Secret Donald Glover Project, Spelling Bees and More Things Left Out of Hollywood Reporter's Cover Story|date=8 November 2018|last=Rittman|first=Alex|accessdate=6 March 2019|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]}}</ref> She attended the [[Identity School of Acting]], enrolling at the age of 16.<ref name=BBCEmmy/><ref name=THRRittman>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/features/black-panther-star-letitia-wright-how-faith-saved-her-1158459|title='Black Panther' Breakout Letitia Wright on How Faith Rescued Her From a "Very Dark Place"|date=7 November 2018|last=Rittman|first=Alex|accessdate=6 March 2019|work=The Hollywood Reporter}}</ref> She appeared in two episodes of ''[[Holby City]]'' and ''[[Top Boy]]'' in 2011.<ref name=THRRittman/> She had a small role in ''[[My Brother the Devil]]'' in 2012, where she was recognized by [[Screen International]] as one of its 2012 Stars of Tomorrow. [[Michael Caton-Jones]] cast Wright in her first leading role in ''[[Urban Hymn]]'' (2015),<ref name=THRRittman/> which brought her to the attention of [[Hollywood cinema|Hollywood]].<ref name=TheGuardian/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.houseofblack.co.uk/blog/rising-star-letitia-wright/|title=Rising star Letitia Wright 'Black Actors need more positive roles'|author=Wiggan, Dylan|website=House of Black|accessdate=16 February 2017|date=15 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170217162220/http://www.houseofblack.co.uk/blog/rising-star-letitia-wright/|archive-date=17 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> The same year, she appeared in an episode of ''[[Doctor Who]]'', and the following year, she began a recurring role as Renie on ''[[Humans (TV series)|Humans]]''.<ref name=THRRittman/> During this time, she also appeared in the play ''[[Eclipsed (play)|Eclipsed]]'' in the London's [[Gate Theatre (London)|Gate Theatre]].<ref>https://www.gatetheatre.co.uk/whats-on/eclipsed</ref> In 2017, Wright starred in the ''[[Black Mirror]]'' episode "[[Black Museum (Black Mirror)|Black Museum]]"; her performance earned her a [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nomination for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or Movie|Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or Movie]].<ref name=BBCEmmy>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-46739479|title=Black Panther's Letitia Wright gets Bafta nod|date=3 January 2019|accessdate=6 March 2019|publisher=[[BBC]]}}</ref>

Wright co-starred in the 2018 film ''[[Black Panther (film)|Black Panther]]'', playing the role of [[Shuri (comics)|Shuri]], [[Black Panther (comics)|King T'Challa]]'s sister and princess of [[Wakanda]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.scmp.com/magazines/style/people-events/article/2134286/who-letitia-wright-black-panther-breakout-star-just|title=Who is Letitia Wright? The 'Black Panther' breakout star is just getting started|work=South China Morning Post|agency=Associated Press|date=23 February 2018|access-date=27 February 2018}}</ref> Part of the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]], the film also starred [[Chadwick Boseman]], [[Michael B. Jordan]], [[Lupita Nyong'o]], and [[Danai Gurira]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heyuguys.com/letitia-wright-interview-urban-hymn/|title=Exclusive: Letitia Wright on Urban Hymn and working with Spielberg on Ready Player One|author=Pape, Stefan|website=heyuguys|date=26 September 2016|accessdate=16 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://variety.com/2016/film/news/black-panther-letitia-wright-ready-player-one-1201895737/|title='Black Panther' Adds 'Ready Player One' Actress Letitia Wright (EXCLUSIVE)|last=Kroll|first=Justin|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=20 October 2016|accessdate=20 October 2016|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6lPfb3DEH?url=http://variety.com/2016/film/news/black-panther-letitia-wright-ready-player-one-1201895737/|archivedate=21 October 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> Wright won the [[NAACP Image Award]] for Outstanding Breakthrough Performance in a Motion Picture for her work in the film,<ref name=2019NAACPwin/> and reprised the role in ''[[Avengers: Infinity War]]'', which was released two months later.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/black-panther-shuri-actress-letitia-wright-is-a-marvel-breakout-1083958|title='Black Panther': How Letitia Wright Became a Marvel Breakout|work=The Hollywood Reporter|first=Joi|last=Childs|date=16 February 2018|access-date=27 February 2018|language=en}}</ref> Also in 2018, Wright appeared as Reb in [[Steven Spielberg]]'s [[Ready Player One (film)|film adaptation]] of the 2011 science-fiction novel ''[[Ready Player One]]''.<ref>Carr, Mary Kate (16 February 2018), [https://ew.com/movies/2018/02/16/black-panther-letitia-wright-5-things-to-know/ "5 things to know about Black Panther breakout star Letitia Wright"], ''EW''. Retrieved 22 February 2018.</ref> Wright features as one of the cameos in [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s music video for "[[Nice for What]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/04/07/drake-nice-for-what-tiffany-haddish-issa-rae-olivia-wilde|title=A breakdown of all the cameos in Drake's 'Nice For What' video|work=The FADER|first= Salvatore| last=Maicki|date=7 April 2018|access-date=7 April 2018|language=en}}</ref>

In 2018 Wright was also featured in a play called ''The Convert'', which was played at the Young Vic theatre in England/London. The play was based on an English-speaking missionary in the 19th century, where the Africans were trained to speak Victorian English and engage in Christianity. This play was set in 1895, when a Black male catholic teacher and missionary called Chilford occupies a mission house in Rhodesian Salisbury.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Billington|first=Michael|date=2017-01-22|title=The Convert review – coming to grips with family and faith in a British colony|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2017/jan/22/the-convert-review-christopher-haydon-the-gate-danai-gurira|access-date=2020-05-22|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Wright plays the character of a Jekesai, a young Rhodesia girl that is being forced into marriage by her uncle, but luckily is saved by Chilford.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Review: The Convert (Young Vic) |website= WhatsOnStage|url=https://www.whatsonstage.com/london-theatre/reviews/the-convert-young-vic-letitia-wright_48189.html|first=Sarah|last= Crompton|language=en-GB|date=15 December 2018|access-date=2020-05-22}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Wright has been vocal about her struggles with [[Depression (mood)|depression]]. In 2018, she told ''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]'' that when she first experienced depression at the age of 20, she "was in the dark going through so many bad things".<ref name=VFRobinson/> Wright credits her Christian faith for helping her overcome her depression, which she discovered after attending a London actors' [[Bible study (Christian)|Bible study]] meeting.<ref name=VFRobinson/> To focus on her recovery and her faith, she declined film roles.<ref name=VFRobinson/> She later explained she "needed to take a break from acting" and "went on a journey to discover my relationship with God, and I became a Christian."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://relevantmagazine.com/culture/black-panther-breakout-star-letitia-wright-shares-testimony-british-talk-show/|title='Black Panther' Breakout Star Letitia Wright Shares How Christianity Changed Her Life|work=RELEVANT Magazine|date=21 February 2018|access-date=3 March 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> 

On 4 December 2020 Wright faced backlash after she publicly shared an [[Vaccine hesitancy|anti-vaccination]] video on her Twitter account. She later stated that she "wasn't against vaccines but it was important to 'ask questions'" and that her "ONLY intention of posting the video was it raised my concerns with what the vaccine contains and what we are putting in our bodies."<ref>{{cite news|title=Actress Letitia Wright criticised for sharing vaccine doubter's video|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-55185119|date=4 December 2020|access-date=4 December 2020|website=[[BBC News]]}}</ref> Wright subsequently deleted her Twitter and [[Instagram]] accounts.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://variety.com/2020/film/news/letitia-wright-deletes-twitter-1234847112/ |title= Letitia Wright Deletes Social Media Accounts After Posting Anti-Vax Video |website= Variety |Fandango]] |date= 5 December 2020 |accessdate= 5 December 2020}}</ref>
