==Early life and education==
Hanna Tetteh was born in [[Szeged]], [[Hungary]], to a Ghanaian father and Hungarian mother.<ref name="ReferenceA">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/people/person.php?ID=1169#|title=Hannah Tetteh, Foreign Affairs Minister|website=www.ghanaweb.com|access-date=2019-04-03}}</ref> Her secondary education was at the [[Wesley Girls' Senior High School|Wesley Girls High School]] at [[Cape Coast]] in the [[Central Region, Ghana|Central Region]] of Ghana from 1978 to 1985. Between 1986 and 1989, she studied law at the [[University of Ghana]] where she obtained the [[Bachelor of Law]] ([[LL.B]]) degree.<ref name="ReferenceA"/> She then attended the [[Ghana School of Law]], becoming a [[Barrister|Barrister-at-Law]] in 1992.<ref name="un.org">[https://www.un.org/sg/en/content/sg/personnel-appointments/2018-07-13/ms-hanna-s-tetteh-ghana-director-general-united-nations Ms. Hanna S. Tetteh of Ghana - Director-General of the United Nations Office at Nairobi (UNON)] [[United Nations]], press release of July 13, 2018.</ref>

==Career==
Hanna Tetteh did her National Service as a [[Legal Officer]] with the [[International Federation of Women Lawyers]] (FIDA) from 1992 to 1993.<ref name="ghanaweb.com"/> After completing her National Service she worked in Private legal practice with the Law firm Ansa-Asare and Company, of Hencil Chambers in [[Accra]], Ghana.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.myjoyonline.com/news/2014/April-4th/tedxlabone-2014-comes-off-tomorrow-read-profile-of-speakers.php|title=TEDxLabone 2014 comes off tomorrow: Read profile of speakers - MyJoyOnline.com|website=www.myjoyonline.com|access-date=2019-04-03}}</ref>

After two years of private legal practice Tetteh joined the [[Commission on Human Rights and Administrative Justice]] as a Legal Officer, but later that same year, she joined the Ghana Agro Food Company (GAFCO) as a Legal Adviser; GAFCO was a food processing company that produced  wheat flour, poultry and animal feed, fish meal and canned tuna. The company also marketed other food and veterinary products and was located in the Tema Harbour Area in [[Tema]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://business.everythinghana.com/index.php?option=com_mtree&task=viewlink&link_id=129&Itemid=26 |title=Ghana Agro Food Company Limited - GAFCO |accessdate=2010-06-07 |work=Ghana Business Listings  |publisher=EverythingGhana.com}}</ref> she held other management positions in the company namely Human Resources and Legal Services Manager and thereafter Deputy General Manager (Finance and Administration Manager until she went into politics in 2000. After serving a term in Parliament she did not immediately contest for re-election and re-joined GAFCO as General Manager (Corporate, Administration and Legal) where she worked until December 2009.<ref name="ghanaweb.com"/>

==Political career==
===Career in national politics===
Hanna Tetteh won the [[Awutu-Senya (Ghana parliament constituency)|Awutu Senya constituency]] seat in the [[2000 Ghanaian parliamentary election|December 2000 parliamentary election]]<ref name="ghanaweb.com"/> and served for one term as a [[National Democratic Congress (Ghana)|National Democratic Congress]] [[MPs elected in the Ghanaian parliamentary election, 2000|Member of Parliament]] on the opposition benches. She did not contest her seat in the next election. She was elected as a National Executive Member of the National Democratic Congress in 2005 and in 2008 she was appointed as National Communications Director of the NDC, replacing [[John Dramani Mahama]], who had become the vice presidential candidate of the NDC as the running mate of [[John Atta Mills]], the party's presidential candidate. The 2008 presidential campaign and the role she played in managing the party's communication strategy for that election made her a more prominent political figure and she became one of the main spokespersons for the NDC.

=== Minister of Trade and Industry, 2009â€“2013 ===
After the NDC victory in the [[2008 Ghanaian general election|2008 elections]] Tetteh became the Spokesperson for the newly elected Government while the process of making new appointments was ongoing and in February 2009 [[President of Ghana|President]] [[John Atta Mills]] nominated her for the position of [[Minister for Trade and Industry (Ghana)|Minister of Trade and Industry]]. Following her confirmation by Parliament she took over the portfolio and held the position of Minister of Trade and Industry from February 2009<ref name="ghanaweb.com"/> to January 2013. During her tenure as Minister of Trade and Industry she was also a member of the Government's economic management team, a member of the board of the [[Millennium Development Authority]] responsible for the oversight of the implementation of the first [[Millennium Challenge Corporation|Millennium Challenge Corporation Compact]] in Ghana. She also served on the National Development Planning Commission, and was the Chairperson of the Ghana Free Zones Board (GFZB).

When [[John Mahama]] succeeded the deceased President Atta Mills in 2012, he also appointed Tetteh as the Communications Director for his 2012 election campaign. She also decided to contest for a parliamentary seat once again and was elected to the newly created [[Awutu-Senya West (Ghana parliament constituency)|Awutu Senya West]] constituency seat in the [[2012 Ghanaian general election|December 2012 election]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Detailed Parliamentary Election Results|url=http://www.ec.gov.gh/assets/CENTRAL.pdf|work=Election results|publisher=[[Electoral Commission of Ghana]]|accessdate=31 January 2013}}</ref> She lost the seat to [[George Andah]] in the 2016 parliamentary election.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ghanamps.com/news-events/details.php?id=5220|title=Ghana MPs - News & Events Details - Hannah Tetteh loses seat to George Andah|website=www.ghanamps.com|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref>

===Minister of Foreign Affairs, 2013â€“2017===
Following the elections, Tetteh was nominated by President Mahama as [[Minister for Foreign Affairs (Ghana)|Minister for Foreign Affairs]] and appointed as such in January 2013 after parliamentary approval.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mahama sends first Ministerial appointees to Parliament; Oye Lithur, Ayariga in|url=http://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/NewsArchive/artikel.php?ID=261871|work=General news|publisher=Ghana Home Page|accessdate=31 January 2013 |date= 11 January 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Parliament approves first batch of Ministers|url=http://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/NewsArchive/artikel.php?ID=263530|work=General news|publisher=Ghana Home Page|accessdate=31 January 2013 |date=29 January 2013}}</ref> While serving as Minister for Foreign Affairs, she was also a member of the National Security Council and the Armed Forces Council.<ref name="un.org"/>

When President Mahama became the Chairman of the Authority of [[ECOWAS]] Heads of State and Government in March 2014, Tetteh became the Chairperson of the ECOWAS Council of Ministers at the same time.
