'''Wayne A. Abernathy''' is a [[United States]] [[economist]] and congressional staffer who served as [[Assistant Secretary of the Treasury for Financial Institutions]] from 2002 to 2005.

==Biography==

Wayne A. Abernathy was educated at the [[Johns Hopkins University]], receiving a [[bachelor's degree]] in [[International Studies]] in 1978, and a [[master's degree]] in International Studies in 1980.<ref name="wammdc">[http://www.wammdc.org/bio/wabernathy.shtml Profile from Washington Association of Money Managers]</ref>

From 1981 to 1986, Abernathy worked as an [[economist]] with the [[United States Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs]]'s Subcommittee on International Finance & Monetary Policy.<ref name="wammdc" />  From 1987 to 1989, he was a senior legislative assistant of [[United States Senate|Sen.]] [[Phil Gramm]] ([[Republican Party (United States)|R]]–[[List of United States Senators from Texas|Tex.]]).<ref name="wammdc" />  He was economist for the Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs from 1989 to 1994.<ref name="wammdc" />  From 1995 to 1998, he was the Staff Director of the Committee's Subcommittee on [[Securities]].<ref name="wammdc" />  He then served as the full Committee's Staff Director from 1999 to 2002.<ref name="wammdc" />

In 2002, [[President of the United States]] [[George W. Bush]] nominated Abernathy to be [[Assistant Secretary of the Treasury for Financial Institutions]] and Abernathy subsequently held this office until 2005.<ref name="wammdc" />

Upon leaving government service in 2005, Abernathy joined the [[American Bankers Association]] as Executive Vice President for Financial Institutions Policy and Regulatory Affairs.<ref name="wammdc" />
