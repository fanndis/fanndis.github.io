{{short description|American businessman}}
'''Robert F. Greenhill''' (born 1936) is an [[United States|American]] businessman widely credited with helping create and pioneer the modern [[mergers and acquisitions]] advisory business on [[Wall Street]].<ref name="forbes">[https://www.forbes.com/profile/robert-greenhill/ Forbes]</ref><ref name="businessweek">[http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=164016&ticker=GHL Bloomberg BusinessWeek]</ref> He is the Founder and Chairman of [[Greenhill & Co.]], an investment bank headquartered in [[New York City]].<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="greenhill">[http://www.greenhill.com/content/robert-f-greenhill Greenhill & Co. biography]</ref><ref name="aei">[http://www.aei.org/about/board-of-trustees/ American Enterprise Institute Board of Trustees]</ref><ref name="hbs">[http://www.alumni.hbs.edu/awards/2009/greenhill.html Harvard Business School Alumni Achievement Awards]</ref>

==Biography==

===Early life===
Greenhill was born in 1936 in [[Minneapolis, Minnesota]].<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20040208092248/http://www.greenhill-co.com/people/partners/partnerbio.asp?partner=0 Greenhill & Co. (archived)]</ref> He graduated from [[Yale University]] in 1958, and he received an M.B.A. from the [[Harvard Business School]] in 1962, where he was a Baker Scholar.<ref name="businessweek"/>

===Career===
He joined [[Morgan Stanley]] in 1962 and became a partner in 1970.<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="greenhill"/> In 1972, he created and directed its mergers and acquisitions department, the first of its kind on Wall Street, during which time he was credited with pioneering the modern investment banking analyst program.<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="greenhill"/><ref name="hbs"/> In the 1980s, he sat on its management committee.<ref name="forbes"/> From January 1989 to January 1999, he served as its vice chairman, and from January 1991 to June 1993, as its president.<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="greenhill"/> From 1993 to 1996, he served as chairman and chief executive officer of [[Morgan Stanley Wealth Management|Smith Barney]].<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="greenhill"/><ref name="hbs"/> He also served on the board of directors of [[The Travelers Companies]] during that time.<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="greenhill"/>

In 1996, he founded [[Greenhill & Co.]]<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="greenhill"/> He served as its chief executive officer from its creation in 1996 to 2007.<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="greenhill"/> It was incorporated in 2004.<ref name="forbes"/>

He sits on the board of trustees of the [[American Enterprise Institute]], and on the international advisory board of the [[British-American Chamber of Commerce]].<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="aei"/><ref name="hbs"/>

===Personal life===
He is married to Gayle Greenhill.<ref name="hbs"/> She is the former chair of the Board of Trustees of the [[International Center of Photography]].<ref>[http://www.icp.org/about-icp/leadership International Center of Photography]</ref> Harvard Business School named Greenhill House in their honor, and their three children and their spouses are all graduates of the school.<ref>[http://www.hbs.edu/about/campus-and-culture/campus-built-on-philanthropy/Pages/greenhill-house.aspx Greenhill House]</ref>
