The '''Wilbur Cross Medal''', or '''Wilbur Lucius Cross Medal''', is an award by the [[Yale Graduate School Alumni Association]] for "distinguished achievements in scholarship, teaching, academic administration, and public service".<ref>[http://www.aya.yale.edu/content/wilbur-cross-medal "Wilbur Cross Medal"], [[Yale University]] Retrieved 04 October 2014</ref><ref>[http://www.albertus.edu/policy-reports/advancement-publications/documents/from-the-hill-archive-apr-2002.pdf ''From the Hill'' Albertus Magnus College Alumni Association 2002]</ref> The award is made to a small number of individuals (up to six) annually, and was first awarded in 1966.

The award is named after [[Wilbur Lucius Cross]], who was a [[Yale University]] dean, and also a noted scholar, editor, literary critic, author and governor of the state of Connecticut.<ref>{{cite web|title=Wilbur Lucius Cross|url=http://www.cslib.org/gov/cross.htm|publisher=Connecticut State Library|accessdate=December 16, 2012}}</ref>
