The '''''Dhaka Tribune''''' is a national English-language broadsheet daily newspaper published in [[Dhaka]] Bangladesh. Its founding editor [[Zafar Sobhan]] is regularly asked to comment on Bangladesh related news stories by international broadcasters, on issues such as free speech<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/03/30/396405033/bangladeshi-blogger-known-as-free-thinker-violently-killed-in-dhaka|title=Bangladeshi Blogger, Known As Free-Thinker, Violently Killed In Dhaka|work=NPR.org|access-date=2017-10-09|language=en}}</ref> and refugees.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/09/12/550492769/nearly-400-000-of-myanmars-muslim-rohingya-seek-asylum-in-bangladesh|title=Nearly 400,000 Of Myanmar's Muslim Rohingya Seek Asylum In Bangladesh|work=NPR.org|access-date=2017-10-09|language=en}}</ref>

Launched in 2013, it has print and online versions with readers both in Bangladesh and internationally. The newspaper owned by Gemcon Group. The group also owned Bangla Tribune and the literary journal Bengal Lights. The publisher of the news paper is [[Kazi Anis Ahmed]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2015/nov/07/bangladesh-ideological-murders-attack-liberal-ideals|title=Bangladesh’s ideological murders are a further attack on its liberal ideals|first=K. Anis|last=Ahmed|date=7 November 2015|via=The Guardian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://press.bangladeshinfo.com/2013/04/21/dhaka-tribunes-online-version-launched/ |title=Press release in bangladesh » Dhaka Tribune’s online version launched |publisher=Press.bangladeshinfo.com |date=2013-04-21 |access-date=2013-06-11 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130620224329/http://press.bangladeshinfo.com/2013/04/21/dhaka-tribunes-online-version-launched/ |archive-date=2013-06-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Dhaka Tribune's online version launched |url=http://www.businessnews-bd.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=8581 |newspaper=Bangladesh Business News |date=2013-04-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203030648/http://www.businessnews-bd.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=8581 |archive-date=2013-12-03 |access-date=2013-06-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==History==
''Dhaka Tribune'' was first published on April 19, 2013 [[Bangladesh]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/2013/apr/19/dhaka-tribune-launched|title=Dhaka Tribune launched |publisher= Dhaka Tribune |date=2013-07-09 |access-date=2015-09-14}}</ref>

With its launch virtually coinciding with the [[2013 Savar building collapse|Rana Plaza]] disaster, its reporters and photographers have covered many major stories with global impact and resonance such as the [[Rohingya persecution in Myanmar (2016–present)|Rohingya]] refugee crisis.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/hashtag/rohingya-crisis/|title=Rohingya crisis Archives {{!}} Dhaka Tribune|website=www.dhakatribune.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-10-09}}</ref>

In print, the newspaper started as a broadsheet before going compact on March 1, 2015. <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/2015/mar/01/introducing-compact-dhaka-tribune|title=Introducing the compact Dhaka Tribune|publisher= Dhaka Tribune |date=2015-03-01 |access-date=2015-09-14}}</ref> Since May 1, 2019 it has reverted to broadsheet editions. <ref>{{Citation|title=Dhaka Tribune - From this May, Dhaka Tribune is switching...|url=https://www.facebook.com/DhakaTribune/videos/from-this-may-dhaka-tribune-is-switching-to-broadsheet-to-have-more-space-for-mo/414572706027552/|language=en|access-date=2019-09-28}}</ref>

The motto of the newspaper is ''The news you want. No more, no less.'' It is well known for its award-winning marketing campaigns ''Glad to be a Bangladeshi''  and ''I am made in Bangladesh''. Since 2015, ''Dhaka Tribune'' has been the main media partner for the [[Dhaka Literary Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://dhakalitfest.com/story.html|title=Dhaka Lit Fest|last=DESIGNARIUM|website=dhakalitfest.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-10-09}}</ref>

==Political position==
The newspaper describes its editorial policy as [[Liberalism|liberal]] economically and socially.

''Dhaka Tribune'' actively supports [[Gender equality|equal gender rights]] in Bangladesh.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/editorial/2016/mar/08/make-equal-rights-everyday-reality|title=Make equal rights everyday reality |publisher= Dhaka Tribune |date=2016-03-08 |access-date=2015-09-14}}</ref>  and in its editorials has called for discriminatory anti-gay laws in Bangladesh to be repealed.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://76crimes.com/2013/08/29/bangladesh-newspaper-urges-repeal-of-anti-gay-law/|title=Bangladesh newspaper urges repeal of anti-gay law|date=2013-08-29|work=76 CRIMES|access-date=2017-10-09|language=en-US}}</ref> The paper's op-ed pages are open to diverse contributors.

2A Media Limited, a concern of [[Awami League]] leader [[Kazi Nabil Ahmed]]'s [[Gemcon Group]], is its leading shareholder.

==Supplements==
The paper publishes a variety of regular supplements including a monthly [http://www.dhakatribune.com/tribune-supplements/2017/09/07/arts-letters-september-2017/ Arts & Letters] magazine.
