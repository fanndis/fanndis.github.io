'''Zélia Duncan''' ({{IPA-pt|ˈzɛliɐ ˈdũkɐ̃}}, 28 October 1964), born '''Zélia Cristina Gonçalves Moreira''', is a Brazilian singer and composer.

==Biography==
Zélia was born in [[Niterói]], in the state of [[Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro]]. She moved with her family to [[Brasília]], where she lived for 16 years. She started her professional career in 1981 after she won a contest organized by the ''Fundação Nacional de Artes'' (National Foundation of Arts). When she was 22 years old, she returned to Rio.<ref name=duncan>{{in lang|pt}} Duncan, Zélia (1994). [http://www2.uol.com.br/zeliaduncan/index2.html "03-Biografia"]. [[Universo Online|UOL]]. Retrieved 20 December 2010.</ref> Using the name "Zélia Cristina", she first performed as a [[Solo (music)|soloist]] in Botanic in [[Rio de Janeiro]].<ref name=uol>{{in lang|pt}} [http://cliquemusic.uol.com.br/artistas/ver/zelia-duncan "Zélia Duncan"] (2010). CliqueMusic. [[Universo Online|UOL]]. Retrieved 20 December 2010.</ref> In 1990 she launched the [[LP record|LP]] "Outra Luz" ("Another Light"),<ref name=uol/> and she began performing in cities like [[São Paulo]], [[Florianópolis]], and [[Porto Alegre]] and participating in tv shows.<ref name=duncan/>

In late 1991, she accepted an offer to go play in the [[United Arab Emirates]]. She stayed there for 5 months, returning to Brazil in May 1992.<ref name=duncan/> It was in this year that she recorded a track in the [[songbook]] of [[Dorival Caymmi]], and she also adopted the name Duncan, the maiden name of her mother. In 1994, she launched her first CD, ''Zélia Duncan'', and two years later she recorded ''Intimidade'' (Intimacy), which made her spend a season in Europe and Japan.<ref name=uol/>

In 2006, Zélia was invited to join the tour of the [[psychedelic rock]] band [[Os Mutantes]], replacing [[Rita Lee]] as the vocalist. The tour was a success and Duncan became a full member of the band.<ref name=uol/> In 2007, however, she decided to leave the band to continue her solo career.<ref>{{in lang|pt}} [http://g1.globo.com/Noticias/Musica/0,,MUL107798-7085-4470,00.html "Arnaldo Baptista e Zélia Duncan saem dos Mutantes"] (20 September 2007). [[G1 (website)|G1]]. Retrieved 20 December 2010.</ref> In 2008, she released a CD and DVD of the shows she performed with [[Simone Bittencourt de Oliveira]] in 2006 and 2007. The title of the CD and DVD was ''Amigo é a Casa'' (Friend is the House).<ref>{{in lang|pt}} França, Jamari (2 May 2008). [http://oglobo.globo.com/cultura/mat/2008/04/30/em_nova_mutacao_zelia_duncan_lanca_dvd_ao_vivo_com_simone_em_repertorio_impecavel-427149322.asp "Em nova mutação, Zélia Duncan lança DVD ao vivo com Simone em repertório impecável"]. ''[[O Globo]]''. Retrieved 20 December 2010.</ref> Simone and Zélia performed shows in three Portuguese cities—[[Figueira da Foz]], [[Oporto]], and [[Lisbon]].<ref>{{in lang|pt}} [https://jn.sapo.pt/PaginaInicial/Interior.aspx?content_id=1060934 "Música: Simone e Zélia Duncan juntas em Portugal"] (12 December 2008). ''[[Jornal de Notícias]]''. Retrieved 20 December 2010.</ref>

In [[20th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|2019]], her album ''[[Tudo É Um]]'' was nominated for the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best MPB Album]].<ref name="billboardLG19">{{cite web |last1=Cabo |first1=Leila |title=Latin Grammys 2019 Winners: Complete List |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8543699/latin-grammys-2019-winners-list |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[MRC (company)|MRC]] |accessdate=9 September 2020 |date=14 November 2019}}</ref>
