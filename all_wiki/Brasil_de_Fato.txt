'''''Brasil de Fato''''' ({{lang-en|Brazil de facto}}) is a [[Brazil]]ian [[online newspaper]] and a [[radio news|radio agency]], in addition to having regional newspapers in [[Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro]], [[Minas Gerais]], [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], [[Paraná (state)|Paraná]] and [[Pernambuco]].<ref>{{cite web| title= Quem Somos |url= https://www.brasildefato.com.br/quem-somos |website=Brasil de Fato |language= pt |date=}}</ref>

Launched on January 25, 2003, on the [[World Social Forum]] of 2003<ref>{{cite book|author1=Kate Coyer|author2=Tony Dowmunt|author3=Alan Fountain|title=The Alternative Media Handbook|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Tu2rAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA160|accessdate=29 May 2016|date=25 January 2011|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-136-75573-6|page=160}}</ref> in [[Porto Alegre]] by [[social movement]] organizations like the [[Landless Workers' Movement]], [[Via Campesina]], and [[Pastoral Care|Pastoral Care Social Commission]], it circulated for more than ten years with a national weekly print version.

The newspaper, of national circulation, gathers [[left-wing]] journalists, writers, commentators, and other national and international intellectuals, who joined to form ''Brasil de Fato'' after they realized the need to a democratization of the press. It intends the debate of ideas and the analysis of facts from the standpoint of the need for social change in the country.
