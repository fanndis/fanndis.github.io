'''Hamish McDouall''' is a New Zealand local government politician and current mayor of [[Whanganui]]. He is a member of the [[New Zealand Labour Party]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/wanganui-chronicle/news/article.cfm?c_id=1503426&objectid=11329145 |last=MacDonald |first=Anne-Marie|date= 22 September 2014|title= McDouall rues 'side issues' |work= [[Wanganui Chronicle]] |access-date= 11 November 2016}}</ref> 

==Biography==
===Early life and career===
McDouall was born in Wanganui to Shirley (née Sanson) and Gerald McDouall<ref>{{cite news |title=Whanganui Mayor, family farewell loved dad |first=Liz |last=Wylie |url= https://www.nzherald.co.nz/wanganui-chronicle/news/article.cfm?c_id=1503426&objectid=12072553 |newspaper=[[Whanganui Chronicle]] |date=18 June 2018 |accessdate=5 July 2020}}</ref> and attended [[Whanganui Collegiate School|Wanganui Collegiate School]]. He studied law and arts at the [[University of Otago]]. McDouall was a winner on the gameshow [[Sale of the Century]] and was the champion of [[Television New Zealand|TVNZ's]] ''Mastermind'' programme in 1990, collectively winning nearly $100,000 worth in prizes.<ref>{{cite news |last=Barrett |first=Emily |date=15 October 1990 |title='Mastermind' gives second quiz title |work=[[The Dominion (Wellington)|The Dominion]] |page=17 }}</ref> His winning specialist subject was ''The Life & Works of [[David Bowie]].''<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/music/75811657/NZ-Mastermind-Hamish-McDouall-shares-his-David-Bowie-moment |last=Croot|first=James|date=12 January 2016 |title=NZ Mastermind Hamish McDouall shares his David Bowie moment| work=[[Wanganui Chronicle]] |access-date= 11 November 2016 }}</ref> He played both soccer, cricket and represented Whanganui in roller hockey.<ref name="candidate bio">{{cite news |url=https://www.whanganuionline.com/elections-2016/candidates/mcdouall-hamish-c |title=McDouall, Hamish |work=Whanganui Online |date=2016 |access-date=2 December 2019 }}</ref>

He then lived abroad in England, Sweden and Russia before returning to Wanganui in 2001. He then took up work as a barrister and solicitor and worked in corporate, family, and community law. He is the author of four published books.<ref name="candidate bio"/>

===Political career===
McDouall joined the [[New Zealand Labour Party|Labour Party]] in 2007 and was Labour's candidate for the [[Whanganui (New Zealand electorate)|Whanganui electorate]] in [[2008 New Zealand general election|2008]], [[2011 New Zealand general election|2011]] and [[2014 New Zealand general election|2014]], but lost to incumbent [[Chester Borrows]].

At the 2010 local body elections he was elected a member of the Whanganui District Council. After serving one term as deputy mayor to [[Annette Main]], McDouall succeeded the [[Whanganui]] mayoralty in the [[2016 New Zealand local elections|2016 local government elections]]. After winning the 2016 election with 39.18% of the popular vote and 2,900 more votes than that of his nearest rival,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.whanganui.govt.nz/our-council/elections/Pages/Election-2016-Results.aspx |title=Election 2016 Results |author=<!--Not stated--> |date= |website=whanganui.govt.nz |publisher=Whanganui District Council|access-date=17 August 2019 |quote=}}</ref> McDouall was returned to the mayoralty in 2019 unopposed.<ref>{{cite news |author=Stowell, Laurel |url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/wanganui-chronicle/news/article.cfm?c_id=1503426&objectid=12259550 |title=Whanganui Mayor Hamish McDouall elected unopposed |work=[[Whanganui Chronicle]] |date=19 August 2019 |access-date=2 December 2019 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
He has a wife, Elinor, and two children.<ref name="candidate bio"/> He is a second cousin of Prime Minister [[Jacinda Ardern]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Toby |last=Manhire |authorlink=Toby Manhire |url= https://thespinoff.co.nz/politics/19-09-2017/my-final-final-plea-a-day-in-whanganui-with-jacinda-ardern/ |title='My final, final plea': a day in Whanganui with Jacinda Ardern |work=The Spinoff |date=19 September 2017 |accessdate=6 July 2020}}</ref>
