A '''precinct captain''', also known as a '''precinct chairman''', '''precinct delegate''', or '''precinct committee officer''', is an elected official in the [[United States|American]] [[Political parties in the United States|political party system]]. The office establishes a direct link between a [[political party]] and the [[voters]] in a local [[election district]].<ref name="collincountygop.com">[https://web.archive.org/web/20130228225909/http://www.collincountygop.com/wp-content/uploads/precinct_chair_handbook.pdf] </ref>

Election to the office is by [[ballot]] or via the [[executive committee|county party executive committee]]. Voters file their declaration of candidacy with their party in their voting district. If elected during the primary, the Precinct Captain shall serve as long as one remains eligible, or until seeking reelection in the subsequent district primary. Requirements vary among states and counties.<ref name="eac.gov">[https://web.archive.org/web/20060209220352/http://www.eac.gov/election_survey_2004/chapter_table/Chapter13_Polling_Places.htm ]</ref>

Responsibilities of the post include facilitating [[voter registration]] and [[absentee ballot]] access; leading [[get out the vote]] outreach efforts; distributing [[canvassing|campaign and party literature]]; promoting the party; and addressing voter concerns. In many states Precinct Captains are also eligible to establish party unit committees for fundraising.<ref name="collincountygop.com"/>

It is a [[grassroots|grassroots position]] with officials generally serving as volunteers, though in some states they receive a [[stipend]].<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090926182635/http://www.txdemocrats.org/take-action/become-a-county-or-precinct-chair/ ]</ref> Positions of county captain have also been created, but are usually used for election campaigns rather than party organizing.<ref name="eac.gov"/>

In [[Illinois]], [[Michelle Obama]] got her initial introduction to Democratic party politics when her father served as a Precinct Captain and the then-[[Michelle Obama|Michelle Robinson]] accompanied the elder Robinson on his rounds through his precinct.<ref name="obama-becoming">{{cite book|last=Obama |first=Michelle|title=Becoming |date=2018|publisher=[[Viking Press]]|language=English|isbn=0-241-33414-4}}</ref>

In the [[Washington (state)|State of Washington]], this office is known as the "Precinct Committee Officer".<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.wa-democrats.org/index.php?page=display&id=20 |title=Washington State Democratic Party &#124; |website=Wa-democrats.org |date= |accessdate=2016-12-18}}</ref> In Washington, in case of a vacancy in the [[Washington House of Representatives|state legislature]] or [[Washington State Senate|senate]], the precinct committee officers will nominate candidates to fill the vacancy, which then need to be approved by the [[Washington_State_Democratic_Party#County_party_organizations|county council]].<ref name="pco-the-stranger">{{cite news |newspaper=[[The Stranger (newspaper)|The Stranger]]  |author=Rich Smith |url=https://www.thestranger.com/slog/2019/07/01/40639365/a-legislative-shake-up-in-bothell-rep-derek-stanford-fills-palumbos-old-seat-and-deputy-mayor-davina-duerr-fills-stanfords |date=2019-07-01 |accessdate=2020-01-19 |title=A Legislative Shake-Up in Bothell: Rep. Derek Stanford Fills Palumbo's Old Seat, and Deputy Mayor Davina Duerr Fills Stanford's }}</ref> In the [[Oregon|State of Oregon]], this office is defined in state law under Oregon Revised Statue 248.015 as "Precinct Committee Person".
