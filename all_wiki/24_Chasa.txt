'''''24 Chasa''''' ({{lang-bg|italic=yes|24 часа}}, translated as ''24 Hours'') is one of the most influential mainstream newspapers in [[Bulgaria]]. Its headquarters are located in [[Sofia]].

==History and profile==
The newspaper, part of the ''168 Chasa'' (meaning ''168 Hours'' in English) Press Group founded by [[Petyo Blaskov]], was launched in April 1991,<ref>[http://www.presseurop.eu/en/content/source-information/566051-24-chasa 24 Chasa] ''Presseurop''. Retrieved 3 December 2013</ref><ref name=veu>{{cite web|title=24 Chasa|url=http://www.voxeurop.eu/en/content/source-information/566051-24-chasa|work=VoxEurop|accessdate=12 March 2015}}</ref> a few months after the launch of the ''168 Hours'' weekly newspaper.<ref name=mariab>{{cite journal|author=Maria Bakardjieva|title=The New Media Landscape in Bulgaria|journal=Canadian Journal of Communication|date=1995|volume=20|issue=1|url=http://cjc-online.ca/index.php/journal/article/view/845/751|accessdate=27 October 2014}}</ref> 

The [[tabloid (newspaper format)|tabloid]] format and the colloquial, somewhat derisive, writing style of ''24 Chasa'' quickly gained wide popularity.<ref name=mariab/>

One of the most popular features in the newspaper is the daily [[cartoon]] ''Ivancho i Mariyka'', drawn by the well-known Bulgarian cartoonist [[Ivaylo Ninov]], which also exists in animation version.<ref>[https://www.24chasa.bg/mnenia/6006562/ Animation cartoon] first page of animated cartoons (in Bulgarian)</ref>

The newspaper was a part of the German [[WAZ-Mediengruppe|WAZ]] media group until 2010.<ref>{{cite web|author=Vassil Vassilev|title=A Bulgarian newspaper group working with Müller Martini equipment|url=http://www.wan-ifra.org/system/files/field_ifra_mag_file/E_nt976_70-71.pdf|work=WAN IFRA|accessdate=12 March 2015|date=June 1997}}</ref> Since then it's part of Media Group Bulgaria, LLC.<ref>[https://www.mgb.bg/AboutUs Media Group Bulgaria] About us (in Bulgarian)</ref> 

Its current editor-in-chief is Borislav Zumbulev.

The circulation of the paper is 50,000 copies during weekdays, up to 90,000 during weekends.<ref name=mariab/>
