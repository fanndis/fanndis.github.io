'''Carlos Ricardo Brathwaite''' (born 18 July 1988) is a [[cricket]]er from [[Barbados]] and a former captain of the West Indies [[Twenty20 International]] (T20I) team.<ref name="Debut">{{cite web |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/westindies/content/player/457249.html |title=Carlos Brathwaite |accessdate=25 December 2014 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref>

==International career==
Brathwaite made his T20I debut for [[West Indies cricket team|the West Indies]] against [[West Indian cricket team in Bangladesh in 2011–12|Bangladesh]] on 11 October 2011. He made his [[One Day International]] debut seven days later in the same series.<ref name="Debut"/>

He made his [[Test cricket|Test]] debut for the West Indies in the Second Test [[West Indian cricket team in Australia in 2015–16|against Australia]] at the [[Melbourne Cricket Ground]] on 26 December 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/engine/match/892517.html |title=Scorecard: 2nd Test: Australia v West Indies at Melbourne, Dec 26–30, 2015 |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=26 December 2015}}</ref>

Needing 19 to win in the last over against England, Brathwaite hit four consecutive sixes in the first four balls of the last over in the final of [[2016 ICC World Twenty20]] to enable the West Indies to win their second World Twenty20 title.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehindu.com/sport/cricket/world-cup/world-t20-who-is-carlos-brathwaite/article8430568.ece|title=Who is Carlos Brathwaite?}}</ref> This was his debut World Cup. He was the first player for the West Indies to hit four consecutive sixes in a T20I match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/sport/other-sports/t20-world-cup-final-2016-carlos-brathwaite-hits-four-sixes-in-a-row-to-help-west-indies-beat-england-a3217051.html|title=T20 World Cup final 2016: Carlos Brathwaite hits four sixes in a row to help West Indies beat England by four wickets|author=Vaishali Bhardwaj|date=3 April 2016|work=Evening Standard|accessdate=3 April 2016}}</ref>

In August 2016, Brathwaite was named the captain of the [[West Indies national cricket team|West Indies]] team for their [[Twenty20 International]] matches [[Indian cricket team in the West Indies in 2016|against India]] in [[Florida]] later that month.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/west-indies-v-india-2016/content/story/1044151.html|title=Carlos Brathwaite named West Indies T20 captain|date=9 August 2016|work=ESPN Cricinfo|accessdate=9 August 2016}}</ref>

On 8 March 2018, during the [[2018 Cricket World Cup Qualifier]] match against [[Papua New Guinea national cricket team|Papua New Guinea]] at the [[Old Hararians]] in [[Harare]], Brathwaite took his first [[Glossary of cricket terms#F|five-wicket haul]] in ODIs.<ref name="Brathwaite5wkt1">{{cite web |url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/637989 |title=Windies avoid scare to edge out PNG |accessdate=8 March 2018 |work=International Cricket Council}}</ref><ref name="Brathwaite5wkt2">{{cite web |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/series/8038/report/1133007/papua-new-guinea-vs-west-indies-10th-match-group-world-cup-qualifier |title=Holder and Brathwaite ward off PNG threat |accessdate=8 March 2018 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref>

In April 2019, he was named in the [[2019 Cricket World Cup squads#West Indies|West Indies' squad]] for the [[2019 Cricket World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/26595791/andre-russell-west-indies-world-cup-squad,-kieron-pollard-misses-out |title=Andre Russell in West Indies World Cup squad, Kieron Pollard misses out |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=24 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/1194397 |title=Andre Russell picked in West Indies' World Cup squad |work=International Cricket Council |accessdate=24 April 2019}}</ref> On 22 June 2019, in the match against [[New Zealand national cricket team|New Zealand]], Brathwaite scored his first century in ODIs.  He was caught on the boundary for what would have been a match-winning six.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2019/jun/22/new-zealand-v-west-indies-cricket-world-cup-live |title=New Zealand beat West Indies by five runs: Cricket World Cup 2019 |work=The Guardian |accessdate=22 June 2019}}</ref>

==Domestic and T20 franchise career==
In April 2016, he made his [[Indian Premier League]] (IPL) debut with franchise [[Delhi Daredevils]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/indian-premier-league-2016/content/story/996323.html |title=KKR bowl; Brathwaite debuts for Daredevils |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=10 April 2016}}</ref> having been bought at auction for [[Indian rupee|Rs.]] 4.2 crore (having had a base price of [[Indian rupee|Rs.]] 30 lakh).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thehindu.com/sport/cricket/world-cup/world-t20-who-is-carlos-brathwaite/article8430568.ece |title = The Hindu: Who is Carlos Brathwaite? |accessdate = 4 April 2016}}</ref>

He also spent a number of years playing domestic cricket in Ireland, with Dublin-based [[Leinster Cricket Club]], with whom in 2009 he won the Bob Kerr Irish Senior Cup, defeating Donemena CC in the final.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rte.ie/sport/cricket/2016/0404/779315-carlos-braithwaite/ |title = Carlos Braithwaite: From Leinster to T20 glory |accessdate = 4 April 2016}}</ref>

In January 2018, he was bought by the [[Sunrisers Hyderabad]] in the [[List of 2018 Indian Premier League personnel changes|2018 IPL auction]].<ref name="IPL2018">{{cite web |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/22218394/ipl-2018-player-auction-list-sold-unsold-players |title=List of sold and unsold players |accessdate=27 January 2018 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref> In May 2018, he was signed by [[Kent County Cricket Club]] to play in the [[2018 Vitality Blast]] tournament in England.<ref name="Kent">{{cite web |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/23382200/billings-twenty20-links-help-kent-seal-brathwaite-deal |title=Billings' Twenty20 links help Kent seal Brathwaite deal |accessdate=2 May 2018 |work=ESPN Cricinfo}}</ref>

In October 2018, [[Cricket West Indies]] (CWI) awarded him a [[limited overs cricket|white-ball]] contract for the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/24866534/kemar-roach-gets-all-format-west-indies-contract |title=Kemar Roach gets all-format West Indies contract |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/867947 |title=Cricket West Indies announces list of contracted players |work=International Cricket Council |accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> Later the same month,  he was named in the squad for the [[Khulna Titans]] team, following the draft for the [[2018–19 Bangladesh Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tigercricket.com.bd/2018/10/29/full-players-list-of-the-teams-following-players-draft-of-bpl-t20-2018-19/ |title=Full players list of the teams following Players Draft of BPL T20 2018-19 |work=Bangladesh Cricket Board |accessdate=29 October 2018}}</ref>

In December 2018, he was bought by the [[Kolkata Knight Riders]] in the [[List of 2019 Indian Premier League personnel changes|player auction]] for the [[2019 Indian Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/25390461/ipl-2019-auction-was-sold-whom |title=IPL 2019 auction: The list of sold and unsold players |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=18 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/cricket/ipl/top-stories/ipl-auction-2019-who-got-whom/articleshow/67144375.cms |title=IPL 2019 Auction: Who got whom |work=The Times of India |accessdate=18 December 2018}}</ref> In October 2019, he was named as the captain of the Combined Campuses team for the [[2019–20 Regional Super50]] tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://guyanatimesgy.com/selection-to-ccc-marooners-squad-means-a-lot-for-akshaya-persaud/ |title=Selection to CCC Marooners squad means a lot for Akshaya Persaud |work=Guyana Times |accessdate=1 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191101193105/https://guyanatimesgy.com/selection-to-ccc-marooners-squad-means-a-lot-for-akshaya-persaud/ |archive-date=1 November 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was released by the Kolkata Knight Riders ahead of the [[List of 2020 Indian Premier League personnel changes|2020 IPL auction]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/28087425/where-do-eight-franchises-stand-2020-auction |title=Where do the eight franchises stand before the 2020 auction? |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=15 November 2019}}</ref> In July 2020, he was named in the [[Jamaica Tallawahs]] squad for the [[2020 Caribbean Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/29417692/nabi,-lamichhane,-dunk-earn-big-cpl-2020-draft |title=Nabi, Lamichhane, Dunk earn big in CPL 2020 draft |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=6 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.windiescricket.com/news/teams-selected-hero-cpl-2020/ |title=Teams Selected for Hero CPL 2020 |work=Cricket West Indies |accessdate=6 July 2020}}</ref>

In October 2020, he was drafted by the [[Dambulla Viiking]] for the [[2020 Lanka Premier League|inaugural edition]] of the [[Lanka Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/30148161/lanka-premier-league-2020-chris-gayle-andre-russell-shahid-afridi-big-names-taken-lpl-draft |title=Chris Gayle, Andre Russell and Shahid Afridi among big names taken at LPL draft |work=ESPN Cricinfo |accessdate=22 October 2020}}</ref>
