'''Luis Lobo''' (born 9 November 1970) is a retired professional male [[tennis]] player from [[Argentina]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Luis Lobo: Profile|publisher=Association of Tennis Professionals|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Players/Lo/L/Luis-Lobo.aspx|accessdate=26 January 2011}}</ref> who won the gold medal in the men's doubles competition at the [[1995 Pan American Games]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Pan American Games History|publisher=United States Tennis Association|url=http://www.usta.com/Pro-Tennis/Olympics-Pan-Am/Pan-American/Information/21398_Pan_American_Games_History/|accessdate=26 January 2011|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6JLg7rFwC?url=http://www.usta.com/Pro-Tennis/Olympics-Pan-Am/Pan-American/Information/21398_Pan_American_Games_History/|archivedate=3 September 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

He reached his career high doubles ranking, World No. 12, on 21 July 1997.<ref>{{cite web|title=Luis Lobo: Rankings History|publisher=Association of Tennis Professionals|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Players/Lo/L/Luis-Lobo.aspx?t=rh|accessdate=26 January 2011}}</ref> He is currently a coach, and has worked with players including Spain's [[Carlos Moyà]] and Argentina's [[Juan Mónaco]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Biography: Monaco, Juan (ARG)|publisher=International Tennis Federation|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/players/player/profile.aspx?playerid=100003032|accessdate=26 January 2011}}</ref>
