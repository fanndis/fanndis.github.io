"'''Girls Like You'''" is a song recorded by American band [[Maroon 5]] from their sixth studio album ''[[Red Pill Blues]]'' (2017). The track was written by lead singer [[Adam Levine]], [[Starrah]], Gian Stone, [[Cirkut (record producer)|Cirkut]], and [[Jason Evigan]], with the latter two serving as producers. A second version featuring and co-written by American rapper [[Cardi B]], was released on May 30, 2018, as the album's <!-- WP:BRD; discuss any changes to order. Doesn't matter what other articles have recently been changed to say. --> fifth and final single. The single version was included on the re-release edition of the album.

The song's accompanying music video was directed by [[David Dobkin (director)|David Dobkin]], which features the band and Levine performing the song in the center of a room and a number of women making their appearances. The single achieved commercial success, spending seven weeks at number one on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, making it Maroon 5's fourth and Cardi B's third chart-topper, who extended her record for most number-ones among female rappers.<ref name="No1"/> It also spent 33 weeks in the top 10, tying both [[Ed Sheeran]]'s "[[Shape of You]]" and [[Post Malone]] and [[Swae Lee]]'s "[[Sunflower (Post Malone and Swae Lee song)|Sunflower]]" for the longest top 10 run in the chart's archives at the time<ref name="weeks top 10 (1)"/><ref name="weeks top 10 (2)"/> and a record-setting 36 weeks at number one on the [[Adult Contemporary (chart)|Adult Contemporary]] chart. Additionally, it reached number one in eleven other countries, including Canada and New Zealand. According to the [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] (IFPI) it was the [[List of best-selling music artists|fifth best selling song]] of 2018, with equivalent sales of 11.9 million units globally.<ref name="Ifpi2019">{{cite web |title=Havana by Camila Cabello (featuring Young Thug) named best-selling single of 2018 |publisher=[[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] |date=March 6, 2019 |accessdate=March 23, 2019 |url=https://www.ifpi.org/news/Havana-by-Camila-Cabello-(featuring-Young-Thug)-named-best-selling-single-of-2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20190330234811/https://www.ifpi.org/news/Havana-by-Camila-Cabello-(featuring-Young-Thug)-named-best-selling-single-of-2018 |archive-date= March 30, 2019 |url-status= live}}</ref> "Girls Like You" ranked at number five on the ''Billboard'' Decade-End chart for the 2010s, with Cardi B being the only female rapper on the top 40.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/decade-end/hot-100 |title=Decade End Hot 100 |work=Billboard |access-date=November 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200321134657/https://www.billboard.com/charts/decade-end/hot-100 |archive-date=March 21, 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref> The song was certified [[RIAA certification|octuple Platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).

"Girls Like You" has won multiple awards including at the [[2019 Billboard Music Awards|2019 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]] ([[Billboard Music Award for Top Hot 100 Song|Top Hot 100 Song]]), and the [[iHeartRadio]] Titanium Awards, while its music video won for Winning Video at the [[MTV (UK and Ireland)|2018 MTV Video Play Awards]]. The song received a nomination for [[Best Pop Duo/Group Performance]] at the [[61st Grammy Awards]].

==Background==
"Girls Like You" was released as the ninth track on ''Red Pill Blues'' via [[Apple Music]].<ref name="originalversion"/> On May 30, 2018, Maroon 5 announced a new version of the song with rapper Cardi B, as the fifth and final single from the album,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8458535/maroon-5-confirm-girls-like-you-cardi-b-song |title=Maroon 5 Confirm Cardi B Collaboration 'Girls Like You' |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |last=Jones |first=Abby |date=May 30, 2018 |accessdate=May 30, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180601075929/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8458535/maroon-5-confirm-girls-like-you-cardi-b-song |archive-date=June 1, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> following the song "[[Wait (Maroon 5 song)|Wait]]", which was later released on [[iTunes]] on that day.<ref name="officialsingle"/> The band released four remix versions of "Girls Like You" on August 2, 2018, featuring [[St. Vincent (musician)|St. Vincent]], [[Tokimonsta]], Cray and [[WondaGurl]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.maroon5.com/news/girls-you-remix-ep-out-ft-st-vincent-and-more |title=Girls Like You Remix EP Is Out, Ft. St. Vincent and More |website=[[Maroon 5]].com |date=August 2, 2018 |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190120232907/https://www.maroon5.com/news/girls-you-remix-ep-out-ft-st-vincent-and-more |archive-date=January 20, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'', Levine stated he told Cardi before she wrote her verse, "I want you to put something down that shows your fierceness as a woman and say it however you want."<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2018-11-27-what-adam-levine-told-cardi-b-before-she-wrote-her-girls-like-you-verse/ |title=What Adam Levine Told Cardi B Before She Wrote Her 'Girls Like You' Verse |last=Brooks |first=Hayden |date=November 27, 2018 |work=iHeartRadio |accessdate=November 28, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181127232752/https://www.iheart.com/content/2018-11-27-what-adam-levine-told-cardi-b-before-she-wrote-her-girls-like-you-verse/ |archive-date=November 27, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Composition==
"Girls Like You" is a [[Pop music|pop]] and [[pop rock]] song.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://moneyish.com/heart/maroon-5s-new-girls-like-you-video-features-these-powerful-women/ |title=Maroon 5's new 'Girls Like You' video features these powerful women |website=[[Dow Jones & Company#Consumer media|Moneyish]] |first=Jeanette |last=Settembre |date=June 4, 2018 |accessdate=June 12, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612140054/https://moneyish.com/heart/maroon-5s-new-girls-like-you-video-features-these-powerful-women/ |archive-date=June 12, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/maroon-5-cardi-b-team-on-cameo-packed-girls-like-you-video-w520932 |title=Maroon 5, Cardi B Team on Cameo-Packed 'Girls Like You' Video |website=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=May 31, 2018 |accessdate=June 12, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612142637/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/maroon-5-cardi-b-team-on-cameo-packed-girls-like-you-video-w520932 |archive-date=June 12, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> It has a length of three minutes and 35 seconds in the original version, while the extended single version is 20 seconds longer. It was written by [[Adam Levine]], [[Cirkut (record producer)|Cirkut]], [[Cardi B]], [[Starrah]], [[Jason Evigan]] and Gian Stone and was produced by Cirkut and Evigan.

The song is written in the key of [[C major]] with a [[tempo]] of 125 beats per minute.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tunebat.com/Info/Girls-Like-You-feat-Cardi-B-Maroon-5-Cardi-B/6FRLCMO5TUHTexlWo8ym1W|title=Key & BPM for Girls Like You (feat. ==Critical reception==
"Girls Like You" received negative reviews from critics. Shaad D'Souza of ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'' called the song "God awful", described it as an example of "horrid, weird, marshmallow-soft songs sung by dudes pining after a ‘good girl’ who will finally fix all of their problems through the power of love", and deemed Cardi B's verse the best part of the song.<ref>{{cite web |title=Maroon 5, Please Don’t Ruin Cardi B |url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/nek33m/maroon-5-please-dont-ruin-cardi-b-girls-like-you-video-watch |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |last=D'Souza |first=Shaad Z. |accessdate=February 26, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200226130612/https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/nek33m/maroon-5-please-dont-ruin-cardi-b-girls-like-you-video-watch |archive-date=February 26, 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref>
It also appeared on multiple year-end lists of the worst songs of 2018, with ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' staff calling it "more headache than hit" but acknowledging "Cardi B's verse is the only saving grace" of the song,<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://time.com/5475647/worst-songs-2018/ |title=The 10 Worst Songs of 2018 |date=December 13, 2018 |work=Time |access-date=February 26, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190518185329/http://time.com/5475647/worst-songs-2018/ |archive-date=May 18, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> and ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'' staff describing it as "profoundly shallow" as well as a "snoozefest, which trafficks in sentimental and borderline meaningless platitudes" and stated "[the song] dominated the charts thanks in no small part to walking charisma factory Cardi B."<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.spin.com/2018/12/worst-songs-of-2018/ |title=The Worst Songs We Heard in 2018 |date=December 28, 2018 |work=Spin |access-date=February 26, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200127072225/https://www.spin.com/2018/12/worst-songs-of-2018/ |archive-date=January 27, 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Commercial performance==
"Girls Like You" debuted at number one on the US [[iTunes chart]], along with the song's original version at number six.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2018/05/31/maroon-5-original-joins-top-10/|title=Maroon 5 & Cardi B’s “Girls Like You” Claims #1 On US iTunes Sales Chart; Original Joins Top 10|work=Headline Planet|last=Cantor|first=Brian|date=May 31, 2018|accessdate=August 10, 2020}}</ref> The single version also debuted at number 94 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]],<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8459439/selena-gomez-maroon-5-hot-100-chart-moves |title=Hot 100 Chart Moves: Selena Gomez's 'Back to You' Hits Top 40, Maroon 5's 'Girls Like You' Debuts |magazine=Billboard |first=Gary |last=Trust |date=June 5, 2018 |accessdate=June 6, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191024132146/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8459439/selena-gomez-maroon-5-hot-100-chart-moves |archive-date=October 24, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> and ascended to number four following its first full week of tracking after the music video's arrival. The single made the fourth-biggest jump (90 positions) in the Hot 100's history, and became Maroon 5's 14th and Cardi B's sixth top-ten single overall.<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8460299/post-malone-psycho-no-1-hot-100 |title=Post Malone's 'Psycho' Hits No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100, Maroon 5's 'Girls Like You' Leaps to Top Five |magazine=Billboard |first=Gary |last=Trust |date=June 11, 2018 |accessdate=June 12, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612151524/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8460299/post-malone-psycho-no-1-hot-100 |archive-date=June 12, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> After spending six weeks at number two behind [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s "[[In My Feelings]]",<ref name=No2>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8469837/drake-in-my-feelings-travis-scott-debuts-hot-100 |title=Drake Rules Hot 100 for Fifth Week With 'In My Feelings,' Travis Scott Debuts Two Songs in Top 10 |work=Billboard |last=Trust |first=Gary |date=August 13, 2018 |accessdate=August 14, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180813210704/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8469837/drake-in-my-feelings-travis-scott-debuts-hot-100 |archive-date=August 13, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> the single reached number one on its 17th charting week, becoming Maroon 5's fourth chart topper–their first since 2012's "[[One More Night (Maroon 5 song)|One More Night]]"–and Cardi B's third. ===Alternative videos===
Two alternate versions of the video were released, both directed by Dobkin. The first version is a [[vertical video]] was released exclusively on [[Spotify]], on August 27, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://open.spotify.com/show/1Lu17oqo8Evy8AzqPKjzLY |title=Maroon 5 – Girls Like You (Vertical Video) featuring Cardi B |website=[[Spotify]]|access-date=December 28, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181219043921/https://open.spotify.com/show/1Lu17oqo8Evy8AzqPKjzLY|archive-date=December 20, 2018|url-status=live |df=}}</ref> Similar to the original video, it focuses on the same women singing and dancing in rotation, with the exception of Cabello. The second version simply titled ''Volume 2'', released on October 16, 2018, on YouTube.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8480249/maroon-5-alternate-version-girls-like-you-music-video |title=Maroon 5 Releases New Version of 'Girls Like You' Music Video: Watch |last=Glicksman |first=Josh |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=October 16, 2018 |accessdate=October 17, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181103161839/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8480249/maroon-5-alternate-version-girls-like-you-music-video |archive-date=November 3, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> This version includes additional footage and Cabello. ===Lyric video===
The band released a [[lyric video]] of the original song on February 14, 2019 ([[Valentine's Day]]).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/maroon-5-valentine-girls-like-you/ |title=Maroon 5 Share Valentine’s Gift: ‘Girls Like You’ Lyric Video |work=UDiscoverMusic |last=Peacock |first=Tim |date=February 14, 2019 |accessdate=February 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190215102745/https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/maroon-5-valentine-girls-like-you/ |archive-date=February 15, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> The animated video follows Adam Levine's journey growing up, becoming a musician, forming the band now known as Maroon 5 and performing a sold-out concert. Levine is shown driving with various women in his car then spending time alone and focusing on his music. Finally, he meets and falls in love with Behati Prinsloo. They date, get married, and start a family at the end.
