'''Steven S. Reinemund''' (born {{birth date|1948|04|06}}) is an American business man who was [[chairman]] and [[chief executive officer]] of [[PepsiCo]] between 2001 and 2006 and dean of the Schools of Business at [[Wake Forest University]] between 2008 and 2014. Reinemund spent 22 years working for PepsiCo in various capacities. During his CEO tenure at PepsiCo, revenues grew by $9 billion, net income rose 70%, earnings per share were up 80% and PepsiCo's market cap exceeded $100 billion.<ref>[http://www.pepsico.com/PressRelease/PepsiCos-Board-of-Directors-Appoints-Indra-K-Nooyi.html PepsiCo press release] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100425000644/http://www.pepsico.com/PressRelease/PepsiCos-Board-of-Directors-Appoints-Indra-K-Nooyi.html |date=April 25, 2010 }}</ref> He led the acquisitions of several other food and beverage companies including [[Quaker Oats]], [[Naked Juice]], [[Izze Beverage Company|Izze]] and Stacy's Chips.

==Early career==
Reinemund is a former [[Captain (OF-2)|Captain]], serving for five years in the [[United States Marine Corps]] after he graduated from the [[United States Naval Academy]] with a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in 1970. Part of his five years in the military, Reinemund served as a guard at the [[White House]] during the administrations of [[Richard Nixon]] and [[Gerald Ford]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.bizjournals.com/triad/stories/2008/09/29/story7.html | first=Matt | last=Evans | title=Steve Reinemund: WFU dean of business knows a thing or two about realigning large bodies of business | date=29 September 2008| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130117094301/http://www.bizjournals.com/triad/stories/2008/09/29/story7.html | archivedate=2013-01-17 }}</ref>

==PepsiCo==
In 1984, Reinemund joined PepsiCo's [[Pizza Hut]] division (subsequently divested) as the Senior Vice President of Operations. Two years later, he was named President & CEO of Pizza Hut in North America where he introduced home-delivery as a distribution method, overtaking market share of rival [[Domino's Pizza]] within 2 years. In 1991, Reinemund assumed the role of president and CEO of Pizza Hut Worldwide. Reinemund then moved to PepsiCo's [[Frito-Lay]] division in 1992, serving as president and CEO for seven years before being promoted to the position of PepsiCo president and COO in 1999. In 2001 the board of PepsiCo named Reinemund chairman and CEO.<ref name="Steven S. Reinemund"/>

In each of his last two full years as CEO of PepsiCo., Reinemund was paid a base salary of $1 million and an annual bonus of $4.5 million.<ref>{{cite web|title=FY 2006 PepsiCo Inc. proxy statement|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/77476/000119312506062537/ddef14a.htm|publisher=SEC|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130116124848/http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/77476/000119312506062537/ddef14a.htm|archivedate=2013-01-16}}</ref>

==Academia==
On April 22, 2008, Reinemund was named dean of the Calloway School of Business and Accountancy and [[Babcock Graduate School of Management]] at [[Wake Forest University]].

On June 30, 2014 Reinemund stepped down as Dean of the Business, electing to remain with the university as Executive-in-Residence.

==Boards==
In 2003, Reinemund was elected to the boards of [[Johnson & Johnson]] and the [http://www.cooperinstitute.org/ Cooper Institute]. He was named to the Board of Trustees at [[Furman University]] effective July 2012. Reinemund also serves on the board of the U.S. Naval Academy Foundation.<ref>[https://www.usna.com/NetCommunity/SSLPage.aspx?pid=468&srcid=425 USNA Board of Directors]{{dead link|date=May 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> and served on the advisory board of the [[Salvation Army]].

In 2007, as he was preparing to retire from Pepsi, Reinemund joined the corporate boards of [[American Express]]{{Citation needed|date=August 2017}}, [[Marriott International]]{{Citation needed|date=August 2017}}, and [[ExxonMobil]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://corporate.exxonmobil.com/en/investors/corporate-governance/board-of-directors/overview|title=Board of Directors {{!}} ExxonMobil|website=ExxonMobil|language=en|access-date=2017-08-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20070530005966/en/Steven-S.-Reinemund-Elected-ExxonMobil-Board|title=Steven S. Reinemund Elected to ExxonMobil Board|website=www.businesswire.com|language=en|access-date=2017-08-18}}</ref> Three years later he joined the Walmart board. In 2010, according to the companies' proxy statements, he was paid $927,318 to serve on these boards.<ref>{{cite web|title=FY 2010 American Express proxy statement|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/4962/000119312511073746/ddefc14a.htm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130116141138/http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/4962/000119312511073746/ddefc14a.htm|archivedate=2013-01-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=FY 2010 Exxon Mobil proxy statement|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/34088/000119312511095944/ddef14a.htm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130116125126/http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/34088/000119312511095944/ddef14a.htm|archivedate=2013-01-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=FY 2010 Marriott International proxy statement|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1048286/000119312511086123/ddef14a.htm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130116140503/http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1048286/000119312511086123/ddef14a.htm|archivedate=2013-01-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=FY 2011 Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. proxy statement|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/104169/000119312511100605/ddef14a.htm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130116140827/http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/104169/000119312511100605/ddef14a.htm|archivedate=2013-01-16}}</ref>
