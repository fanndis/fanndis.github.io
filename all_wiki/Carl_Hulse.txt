'''Carl E. Hulse''' (born October 19, 1954) is the chief Washington correspondent for ''[[The New York Times]]'' and managing editor of First Draft, a political news stream and morning email newsletter.<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/weta/washingtonweek/profile/carl-hulse PBS profile for Carl Hulse]</ref>  His regular ''New York Times'' column "On Washington", described developments in Washington DC.  His writing has also appeared online with MSN, MSN UK, MSN Canada, and CNBC, and in the ''[[Sydney Morning Herald]]'', ''[[Albany Business Review]]'', [[Boston.com]], ''[[The Economic Times]]'', American City Business Journals, and ''[[Miami Herald]]''.<ref>[https://muckrack.com/hillhulse MuckRack profile for Carl Hulse]</ref><ref>[https://www.newspapers.com/search/#query=Carl+Hulse Newspapers.com search for articles written by Carl Hulse shows 148,628 matches]</ref>

== Early life ==
Hulse was born in Illinois on October 19, 1954, and reared in [[Ottawa, Illinois]]. His father, also Carl E. Hulse Sr.,<ref>[https://www.ancientfaces.com/person/carl-hulse/46994201 AncientFaces.com profile for Carl E. Hulse Sr.]</ref> was a plumbing contractor after World War II, and his mother worked in their home after trying other work.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://signup.politics.uchicago.edu/page/-/site/PODCAST/transcripts/af-ep92-hulse.pdf |title=Transcript of podcast. The Axe Files - Ep. 92: Carl Hurlse Released October 31, 2016.  David Axelrod interviewed Carl Hulse. |access-date=December 8, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181209124109/https://signup.politics.uchicago.edu/page/-/site/PODCAST/transcripts/af-ep92-hulse.pdf |archive-date=December 9, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  In 1976, he received an undergraduate degree in Mass Communications from the School of Communication of the [[Illinois State University]], where as a student he was a news editor for ''The Vidette''.  In 2007, the newspaper admitted him to The Vidette Hall of Fame.  Ten years later, in 2017, the Schools College of Arts and Sciences elected him to its Hall of Fame.<ref>[http://www.videtteonline.com/carl-hulse-bio-box/article_03cf9efe-0fd3-11e7-b91f-f787829566fa.html Carl Hulse Bio Box. == Career ==
Immediately after his college graduation, he worked for the ''News Tribune'' in [[LaSalle, Illinois]]-[[Peru, Illinois]]. Before relocating to Washington DC in 1985, he spent the early years of his career working at newspapers in Illinois and Florida, The Daily Journal in [[Kankakee, Illinois]], and the Sun-Sentinel in [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]].<ref>[https://cas.illinoisstate.edu/alumni/hof/2016/ 2016-2017 Inductees, Hall of Fame, Illinois State University]</ref>

In 1985 he began working at the [[Washington, D.C.]] bureau of ''The New York Times'' and covered its regional editions, first as night editor working the 3 p.m. to midnight shift.  He began covering Capital Hill in May 2002.  From 2011 to 2014, he became Washington Editor for ''The Times'', coordinating its Washington coverage of the White House and executive branch, Congress, the courts, and the Pentagon.  For more than a decade he had served as the paper's Chief Congressional Correspondent.  He is considered a leading American authority on Congressional affairs. 

He appears occasionally on [[PBS]]'s ''[[Washington Week]]'' with [[Robert Costa (journalist)|Robert Costa]], on Friday evenings.<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/weta/washingtonweek/profile/carl-hulse PBS profile for Carl Hulse]</ref> 

== Personal life ==
He lives in Washington with his wife, Kimberly Hamer Hulse, a longtime ''[[National Geographic]]'' employee.<ref>[https://www.nationalgeographic.org/leadership/kim-hulse/ National Geographic Leadership. Kim Hamer Hulse. Vice President, Education Programs. National Geographic Society online website.]</ref>  They have two grown sons, Nicolas and Benjamin.<ref>[https://thehill.com/capital-living/media-sketch/20945-thats-carl-with-a-c-and-pulse-with-an-h Rothstein B. Thats Carl with a C and pulse with an H. The Hill.com.  Published online 12/07/05 12:00 AM EST]</ref>

He declares no political party.  Hulse is in a local band called the Native Makers, where he plays drums, maraca, and other percussion instrument; they have written a song called "This Town."<ref>[https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2013/07/just-another-night-in-this-town/278108/ Ball M.
