'''Bangladesh Statue of Justice controversy''' is a controversy that surrounds the building of a [[Statue of justice]] in [[Bangladesh Supreme Court]] premises. Islamic groups like [[Hefazat-e-Islam Bangladesh|Hefazat-e-Islam]] and [[Bangladesh Awami Olama League]] have protested the statue and have taken [[legal action]]s calling for its removal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-39082395|title=Bangladesh Muslims protest against justice goddess statue|date=24 February 2017|website=BBC News|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=http://www.abnews24.com/english/2017/03/04/8302/Remove-Greek-statue-from-HC-premises-:-Ulama-League|title=Remove Greek statue from HC premises : Ulama League|date=4 March 2017|publisher=ABNews|accessdate=17 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.prothom-alo.com/bangladesh/news/140305/Hefajat-demands-Themis-statue-demolition|title=Hefajat demands SC statue demolition|website=Prothom Alo|accessdate=25 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170224215034/http://en.prothom-alo.com/bangladesh/news/140305/Hefajat-demands-Themis-statue-demolition|archive-date=24 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Background==
Bangladesh is a Muslim majority country with a legal system based on [[English common law]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Bangladeshi Muslim hardliners seek removal of Justice statue|url=http://www.newindianexpress.com/world/2017/feb/24/bangladeshi-muslim-hardliners-seek-removal-of-justice-statue-1574297.html|website=The New Indian Express|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-international/islamists-protest-courts-greek-statue/article17364065.ece|title=Islamists protest court’s Greek statue|website=The Hindu|publisher=Agence France-Presse|language=en|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref> Bangladesh has seen rising tension between the religious hardliners and secularism supporters.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hefazat makes good on protest threat, demands SC sculpture removal|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/politics/2017/02/24/hefazat-supreme-court-sculpture/|website=Dhaka Tribune|accessdate=25 February 2017|date=24 February 2017}}</ref> A statue of Justice was erected in the premises of the Bangladesh Supreme Court by the Government.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bangladesh Islamists Protest Court's Greek Goddess Statue|url=http://www.naharnet.com/stories/en/225993-bangladesh-islamists-protest-court-s-greek-goddess-statue|website=Naharnet Newsdesk|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref> The Statue was that of [[Themis]] or [[Lady Justice]], ancient Greek [[Titaness]] and the personification of Justice and the goddess of wisdom and good counsel.<ref>{{cite web|title=Themis Greek goddess|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Themis-Greek-goddess|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|accessdate=25 February 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> The Statue has blindfolds, is holding a sword, and wearing a [[Sari]]. The Sari is a local variation, the statue is usually wearing a gown.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bangladeshi Muslim hardliners seek removal of Justice statue|url=http://www.houstonchronicle.com/news/world/article/Bangladeshi-Muslim-hardliners-seek-removal-of-10956358.php|website=Houston Chronicle|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref>

===Opposition and lawsuits===
On 24 February 2017 thousands of Muslims protested in [[Dhaka]] against the statue. Supporters of Hefazat-e-Islam marched from the national mosque, [[Baitul Mukarram]], after Friday prayers.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bangladeshi Muslim hardliners seek removal of Justice statue|url=http://www.thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/25/02/2017/Bangladeshi-Muslim-hardliners-seek-removal-of-Justice-statue|work=The Peninsula |location=Qatar|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref> A similar rally was held at the same time in [[Chittagong]], the second largest city in Bangladesh.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bangladesh Islamists seek removal of court’s statue|url=http://www.gulf-times.com/story/534180/Bangladesh-Islamists-seek-removal-of-court-s-statu|website=Gulf-Times|accessdate=25 February 2017|language=ar|date=24 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Islamists protest court’s Greek statue|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/international/Islamists-protest-court%E2%80%99s-Greek-statue/article17362635.ece|website=The Hindu|accessdate=25 February 2017}}</ref> Hefazat later submitted a petition to the Supreme Court to demolish it, which was supported by the [[Awami League]] affiliated [[Bangladesh Awami Olama League]] and an online news editor.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hefajat, Olama league demand removal of SC sculpture|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/backpage/hefajat-olama-league-demand-removal-sc-sculpture-1356049|accessdate=17 March 2017|work=[[The Daily Star (Bangladesh)|The Daily Star]]|date=5 February 2017}}</ref> They called it conspiracy to undermine Islam in Bangladesh.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/asia/south-asia/bangladesh-islamists-protest-courts-greek-goddess-statue|title=Bangladesh Islamists protest court's Greek goddess statue|work=[[The Straits Times]]|access-date=17 March 2017|archive-date=24 February 2017}}</ref>

[[Sheikh Hasina]] Prime Minister of Bangladesh supported calls to remove the Statue of justice in Bangladesh Supreme Court with some people saying the government was bowing down to the pressure of those who have used religion for political ends.<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/may/26/lady-justice-statue-bangladesh-removed-islamist-objections</ref>

=== Support ===
The Supreme Court officials defended the statue as a symbol of Justice.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bangladesh Islamists protest court's Greek goddess statue|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/asia/south-asia/bangladesh-islamists-protest-courts-greek-goddess-statue|website=The Straits Times|accessdate=25 February 2017|language=en|date=24 February 2017}}</ref> The Dhaka Tribune wrote an article defending the statue.<ref>{{cite web|title=The truth about sculptures and idols|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/opinion/op-ed/2017/02/13/truth-sculptures-idols/|website=Dhaka Tribune|accessdate=25 February 2017|date=13 February 2017}}</ref> The Daily Star defended the statue and pointed out that only 7 percent of the Judges are female.<ref>{{cite web|title=I dissent|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/law-our-rights/i-dissent-1356949|website=The Daily Star|accessdate=25 February 2017|language=en|date=7 February 2017}}</ref>

==2017 removal==
On 26 May, the Lady Justice statue was completely removed at 2am after a court decision and taken away by a truck at around 4am.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/may/26/lady-justice-statue-bangladesh-removed-islamist-objections|title=Lady Justice statue in Bangladesh is removed after Islamist objections|work=The Guardian|date=26 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.bdnews24.com/en/detail/bangladesh/1340207|title=Lady Justice statue is being removed from Supreme Court premises|work=bdnews24.com|date=26 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/2017/05/bangladesh-lady-justice-statue-removed-protests-170526101110620.html|title=Bangladesh: Lady Justice statue removed after protests|work=Al Jazeera|date=26 May 2017}}</ref> Two days later, the statue was reinstalled in place a few hundred meters from its original location.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/05/28/bangladesh-reinstalls-statue-lady-justice-despite-complainst/|title=Bangladesh reinstalls statue of Lady Justice despite complaints from Islamist hardliners|work=The Telegraph|date=28 May 2017}}</ref>

The order was given from the Supreme Court, and the difference was stated to be one where the statue was on display (initial location) and one where it was inside and therefore not 'on focus'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/court/2017/05/28/lady-justice-statue-re-erected/|title=Lady Justice statue re-erected on Supreme Court premises|work=Dhaka Tribune|date=28 May 2017}}</ref>
