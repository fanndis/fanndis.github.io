'''Dane William DeHaan''' ({{IPAc-en|d|ə|ˈ|h|ɑː|n}} {{respell|də|HAHN|'}}; born February 6, 1986)<ref name="twitter">{{cite tweet |user=danedehaan |number=286708631951511553 |title=hellesbelles: 'RT: Random Question of the Day: What your middle name?' Dane DeHaan: 'William.' |access-date=June 7, 2015 |date=January 2, 2013 |first=Dane |last=DeHaan}}</ref> is  an American actor, known for his roles as Andrew Detmer in ''[[Chronicle (film)|Chronicle]]'' (2012), [[Harry Osborn]] in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man 2]]'' (2014), Lockhart in ''[[A Cure for Wellness]]'' (2016), and Valerian in ''[[Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets]]'' (2017).<ref name="interviewmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/film/dane-dehaan|title=Dane Dehaan|first=Mark|last=Jacobs|date=August 1, 2011|work=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview Magazine]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life==
DeHaan was born in [[Allentown, Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/03/25/dane-dehaan-the-place-beyond-the-pines_n_2949500.html|title=Dane DeHaan, 'The Place Beyond The Pines' Star, On 'The Amazing Spider-Man 2' And Being Older Than You Think He Is|first=Mike|last=Ryan|date=March 25, 2013|work=[[HuffPost]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> His father, Jeff DeHaan, is a computer programmer, and his mother, Cynthia Boscia, is an executive at Metlife. He has an older sister, Meghann,<ref name=themorningcall1>{{cite web|url=http://articles.mcall.com/2012-02-09/entertainment/mc-chronicle-dane-dehaan-20120209_1_wettest-county-mia-wasikowska-juno-temple|title=Former Emmaus High student Dane DeHaan: "I was obsessed with acting"|first=Amy|last=Longsdorf |date=February 9, 2012|work=[[The Morning Call]]|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/65NhKw8Ag?url=http://articles.mcall.com/2012-02-09/entertainment/mc-chronicle-dane-dehaan-20120209_1_wettest-county-mia-wasikowska-juno-temple|archivedate=February 11, 2012}}</ref> and had what the ''[[The New York Times Magazine|New York Times Magazine]]'' has described as a "very normal, super-supportive childhood."<ref name=independent1>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/features/why-dane-dehaans-kiss-with-daniel-radcliffe-isnt-the-only-reason-his-career-is-taking-off-8970204.html|title=Why Dane DeHaan's kiss with Daniel Radcliffe isn't the only reason his career is taking off|work=[[The Independent]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> He attended [[Emmaus High School]] in [[Emmaus, Pennsylvania]], for three years and appeared in community theater. He switched to the [[University of North Carolina School of the Arts]] for his senior year of high school, where he was "around artists for the first time."<ref name=independent1/><ref name=themorningcall2>{{cite news|url=http://articles.mcall.com/2012-08-23/entertainment/mc-dane-dehaan-lawless-emmaus-20120823_1_lawless-hot-commodity-emmaus-high-school|title=Emmaus native Dane DeHaan, star of 'Lawless,' is a hot commodity in Hollywood|first=Amy |last=Longsdorf|date=August 23, 2012|work=[[The Morning Call]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> He continued at [[University of North Carolina School of the Arts|UNCSA]] for undergraduate studies, graduating in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.journalnow.com/relishnow/film_tv/article_a63e3ebe-39bf-11e2-b834-0019bb30f31a.html|title=Bigger roles keep coming for UNCSA grad DeHaan|first=Tim|last=Clodfelter|date=November 29, 2012|work=[[Winston-Salem Journal]]}}</ref>

==Career==
DeHaan got his start on stage as [[understudy]] for [[Haley Joel Osment]] in the short-lived Broadway revival of ''[[American Buffalo (play)|American Buffalo]]''. In 2008, he made his television debut, guest-appearing on an episode of ''[[Law & Order Special Victims Unit]]'', and his 2010 feature film debut in [[John Sayles]]' ''[[Amigo (film)|Amigo]]''. He played Jesse in the third season of ''[[In Treatment (U.S. TV series)|In Treatment]]'' and played Timbo in the fourth season of ''[[True Blood]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.indiewire.com/2011/01/newcomer-dane-dehaan-joins-john-hillcoats-the-wettest-county-in-the-world-120622/|title=Newcomer Dane DeHaan Joins John Hillcoat's 'The Wettest County In The World'|first=Simon|last=Dang|date=January 28, 2011|work=[[Indiewire]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref>

In 2012, DeHaan starred in the camcorder drama film ''[[Chronicle (film)|Chronicle]]'', and as Cricket in ''[[Lawless (2012 film)|Lawless]]''. In 2013, he played [[Lucien Carr]], a contemporary of Allen Ginsberg and Jack Kerouac, in ''[[Kill Your Darlings (2013 film)|Kill Your Darlings]]'', alongside [[Daniel Radcliffe]]. It was a role for which he has received critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/entertainment-us-danedehaan/actor-dane-dehaan-explores-beat-generation-origins-in-kill-your-darlings-idUSBRE99G1F120131019|title=Actor Dane DeHaan explores Beat Generation origins in 'Kill Your Darlings'|first=Piya|last=Sinha-Roy|date=October 18, 2013|agency=[[Reuters]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> He was the cover star of ''Hero'' magazine issue 10, shot by [[Hedi Slimane]] and published in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hero-magazine.com/article/12342/hero-10-california-future/|title=HERO — HERO 10: CALIFORNIA FUTURE (English)|work=hero-magazine.com|accessdate=June 7, 2015|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150304045720/http://store.hero-magazine.com/product/hero-10-california-future-english|archivedate=March 4, 2015|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> In 2014, [[Annie Leibovitz]] photographed DeHaan for a [[Prada]] clothing "men's spring advertising campaign".<ref name="Prada Taps Annie Leibovitz for Men's Spring Campaign">{{cite news|url=http://wwd.com/business-news/media/prada-taps-annie-leibovitz-for-mens-spring-campaign-7339004/|title=Prada Taps Annie Leibovitz for Men's Spring Campaign|work=WWD|date=January 8, 2014|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> Also that year, he played [[Harry Osborn]]  in ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man 2]]'',<ref name=interviewmag1/> and Zach Orfman in ''[[Life After Beth]]''. DeHaan played a fictional version of himself in the music video for [[Imagine Dragons]]' song "[[I Bet My Life]]". 

[[File:Cara Delevingne & Dane DeHaan (28439120622).jpg|thumb|DeHaan with Cara Delevingne at the 2016 San Diego Comic Con.]]
In 2015, DeHaan starred as [[James Dean]] in the drama ''[[Life (2015 film)|Life]]'', opposite [[Robert Pattinson]] as photographer [[Dennis Stock]]. In September 2015, Dehaan was the cover star of ''[[Another Man]]'' issue 20, shot by Willy Vanderperre.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.anothermag.com/fashion-beauty/7123/another-man-s-s15-all-stars|title=Another Man S/S15: All Stars|work=[[Another Magazine|AnOther]]|date=March 9, 2015|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> DeHaan then played Roman in the 2016 independent drama film ''Two Lovers and a Bear'' and Lockhart in ''[[A Cure for Wellness]]'' (2016). 

In 2017, DeHaan starred in [[Luc Besson]]'s science fiction film ''[[Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets]]'', with [[Cara Delevingne]], based on the French comics series ''[[Valérian and Laureline]]''.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=lucbesson |number=598140891048488960 |title=Starring @danedehaan & @Caradelevingne. Shooting in December. In theaters 2017. pic.twitter.com/R3STb1aPsf |first=Luc |last=Besson |date=May 12, 2015 |accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> He also starred in the 17th-century romance ''[[Tulip Fever]]''.

In July 2017, it was announced that [[Vincent D'Onofrio]] would be the director of a new [[western film]], ''[[The Kid (2019 film)|The Kid]]'',<ref>{{cite journal |first=Mike |last=Fleming, Jr. |title=Vincent D'Onofrio Taps 'Valerian's Dane DeHaan To Play Billy The Kid In Suretone Western |url=https://deadline.com/2017/07/dane-dehaan-billy-the-kid-vincent-donofrio-valerian-the-kid-suretone-pictures-1202131369/ |date=July 19, 2017 |access-date=October 5, 2017 |journal=[[Deadline (magazine)|Deadline]]}}</ref> starring DeHaan in the role of [[Billy the Kid]] and costarring [[Ethan Hawke]].<ref>{{cite news |author=Jescri |title=Dane DeHaan será Billy el Niño en el western 'The Kid' |url=https://www.elseptimoarte.net/noticias/actualidad/dane-dehaan-sera-billy-el-nino-en-el-western-the-kid-30751.html |date=July 20, 2017 |access-date=October 5, 2017 |journal=El Séptimo Arte |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Vuelve el western: Dane DeHaan será Billy el Niño, junto a Ethan Hawke |url=http://decine21.com/noticias/113831-vuelve-el-western-dane-dehaan-sera-billy-el-nino-junto-a-ethan-hawke |date=July 21, 2017 |access-date=October 5, 2017 |journal=DeCine21 |language=es}}</ref>

From 2019-2020, DeHaan starred as Chris Lynwood in the [[Amazon Prime]] series, ''[[ZeroZeroZero]]''. He starred in the [[Quibi]] mini-series, ''[[The Stranger (2020 American TV series)|The Stranger]]'' (2020).

==Personal life==
DeHaan married American actress [[Anna Wood (actress)|Anna Wood]] in a private ceremony on June 30, 2012. The two have been together since 2006 and both appeared in ''[[Chronicle (film)|Chronicle]]'' (2012).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fadeinonline.com/top-100-people-in-hollywood-you-need-to-know-2.html/3|title=Top 100 People in Hollywood - Part 3|work=fadeinonline.com|accessdate=7 June 2015}}</ref> They live in [[Williamsburg, Brooklyn]], [[New York City, New York]], U.S. They have a daughter, born in 2017, and a son, born in 2020.<ref>{{cite web|last=Juneau|first=Jen|title=Dane DeHaan and Anna Wood Welcome Daughter Bowie Rose|url=http://people.com/babies/dane-dehaan-anna-wood-welcome-daughter-bowie-rose/|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=April 17, 2017|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Dane DeHaan and Anna Wood Welcome Second Child, a Son|url=https://people.com/parents/dane-dehaan-anna-wood-welcome-son-bert-apollo/|website=PEOPLE.com|language=EN|access-date=2020-05-28}}</ref>
