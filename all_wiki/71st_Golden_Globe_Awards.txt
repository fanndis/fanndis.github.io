The '''71st [[Golden Globe Award]]s''', honoring the best in [[2013 in film|film]] and [[2013 in American television|American television of 2013]], was broadcast live from the [[Beverly Hilton Hotel]] in [[Beverly Hills, California]], on January 12, 2014, by [[NBC]], as part of the [[2013-14 film awards season]]. The ceremony was produced by [[Dick Clark Productions]] in association with the [[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]].<ref name=GGAnnouncement>{{cite web|url=http://www.goldenglobes.org/timetable71/|title=The Hollywood Foreign Press Association and Producer Dick Clark Productions Bring 'The 71st Annual Golden Globe Awards' Live Coast-To-Coast On NBC Sunday, January 12, 2014|date=April 18, 2013|work=goldenglobes.org|accessdate=October 10, 2013}}</ref> [[Woody Allen]] was announced as the [[Golden Globe Cecil B. DeMille Award|Cecil B. DeMille Award]] honoree for his lifetime achievements on September 13, 2013,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/golden-globes-woody-allen-receive-629107 |title=Golden Globes: Woody Allen to Receive Cecil B. DeMille Award |date=September 13, 2013 |work=The Hollywood Reporter |accessdate=November 14, 2013}}</ref> and [[Diane Keaton]] accepted the award for him. On October 15, [[Tina Fey]] and [[Amy Poehler]] were announced as the co-hosts for the second time in a row and as the co-hosts for the [[72nd Golden Globe Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/tina-fey-amy-poehler-host-648283|title=Tina Fey, Amy Poehler to Host Golden Globes for Next Two Years|accessdate=October 17, 2013|date=October 15, 2013|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|author=Belloni, Matthew}}</ref> The nominations were announced on December 12, 2013, by [[Aziz Ansari]], [[Zoe Saldana]] and [[Olivia Wilde]].<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/moviesnow/la-et-mn-golden-globes-nominees-winners-complete-list,0,6845028.story#axzz2nHEfg9AW|title=Golden Globes 2014: The complete list of nominees and winners|date=December 12, 2013|work=Los Angeles Times|accessdate=December 12, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/film-news/10513775/Golden-Globes-nominations-2014-12-Years-a-Slave-and-American-Hustle-lead-way.html|title=Golden Globes nominations 2014: 12 Years a Slave and American Hustle lead way|date=December 12, 2013|work=Daily Telegraph|accessdate=December 13, 2013}}</ref> ''[[American Hustle]]'', ''[[Behind the Candelabra]]'', ''[[Breaking Bad]]'', ''[[Brooklyn Nine-Nine]]'', and ''[[Dallas Buyers Club]]'' were among the films and television shows that received multiple awards.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.moviethatmatters.com/gold-dicaprio-lawrence-poehler-win-globes/|title=Golden Globes nominations 2014: DiCaprio, Lawrence, Poehler win Globes|date=January 12, 2014|work=Movie That Matters|accessdate=January 13, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/jan/12/golden-globes-2014-full-list-of-winners|title= Golden Globes 2014: full list of winners|date=January 13, 2014|work=Guardian UK|accessdate=January 13, 2014}}</ref>
