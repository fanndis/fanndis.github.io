'''Nonso Anozie''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|n|ɒ|n|s|oʊ|_|ə|ˈ|n|oʊ|z|i|eɪ}};<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TidE_vPHIhw|title=The Powerlist Interviews - Nonso Anozie|publisher=Powerful Media|date=6 July 2017|accessdate=14 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7suseYuBE98|title=Dracula (NBC): Nonso Anozie "R.M. Renfield" Official TV Interview|publisher=ScreenSlam|date=15 October 2013|accessdate=14 May 2020}}</ref> born <!--see talk page-->17 November 1978<ref name=birthdaytweet/><ref name=prague/>) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] actor who has worked on stage, film, and television. He is best known for his role as Tank in ''[[RocknRolla]]'', Sergeant Dap in ''[[Ender's Game (film)|Ender's Game]]'', Abraham Kenyatta in ''[[Zoo (TV series)|Zoo]]'', Captain of the Guards in ''[[Cinderella (2015 Disney film)|Cinderella]]'' and Xaro Xhoan Daxos in the [[HBO]] television series ''[[Game of Thrones]]''.

==Career==
Anozie was hired in 2006 to provide the voice for the armoured bear [[Iorek Byrnison]] in the [[The Golden Compass (film)|film adaptation]] of [[Philip Pullman]]'s ''[[Northern Lights (novel)|Northern Lights]]''.<ref name="btts">{{cite web |url= http://www.bridgetothestars.net/news/1156776262/|title=The Real Iorek|accessdate=10 November 2007 |publisher= BridgeToTheStars.net}}</ref> Anozie was replaced by [[Ian McKellen]] two months before the film was set to be released.<ref name="btts2">{{cite web |url= http://www.bridgetothestars.net/news/ian-mckellen-voicing-iorek/|title=Ian McKellen Voicing Iorek|accessdate=10 November 2007 |publisher= BridgeToTheStars.net}}</ref> The film's director, [[Chris Weitz]], told ''[[Empire (magazine)|Empire]]'': "It was a studio decision ... You can understand why you would cast Ian McKellen for anything. But letting go of Nonso was one of the most painful experiences on this movie for me. I need to say about Nonso that he is one of the most promising and soulful young actors I have encountered in England and I've worked here for quite a bit now and he's actually in the next [[Mike Leigh]] [film] ... But it was, uh, that was kind of a dark day for me. I kinda wanna go out of my way to point out how much I love Nonso's work. And that's that."<ref name="empire">{{cite web |url= http://www.empireonline.com/news/story.asp?NID=21229|title=Kristin Scott Thomas in Golden Compass|accessdate=10 November 2007 |publisher= [[Empire (magazine)|Empire]]}}</ref>

Anozie played small roles as Think Tank in [[Guy Ritchie]]'s film ''[[RocknRolla]]'' and Frank Mace in [[Joe Wright]]'s ''[[Atonement (film)|Atonement]]''. He played the lead in ''[[Cass (2008 film)|Cass]]'' a 2008 British crime drama film recreating the life of [[Cass Pennant]]. In 2009, he appeared in the supporting cast of the BBC TV three-part [[2003 Iraq War]] drama, ''[[Occupation (TV serial)|Occupation]]'', where he played a [[US Marine]] turned [[private military contractor]]. In 2011, he played Artus, a Zamoran pirate and close friend of Conan, in ''[[Conan the Barbarian (2011 film)|Conan the Barbarian]]''. That year, he was also cast to play the role of Xaro Xhoan Daxos in the HBO series ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' and was cast as Jackson Burke in ''[[The Grey (film)|The Grey]]''.<ref name="AGoT2">{{cite web|url=http://winter-is-coming.net/2011/08/nonso-anozie-cast-as-xaro-xhoan-daxos/ |title=Nonso Anozie cast as Xaro Xhoan Daxos |accessdate=2 August 2011 |publisher=WinterIsComing.net |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110819172826/http://winter-is-coming.net/2011/08/nonso-anozie-cast-as-xaro-xhoan-daxos/ |archivedate=19 August 2011 }}</ref> He also plays the role of [[Samson]] in the [[History (U.S. TV channel)|History Channel]]'s television miniseries ''[[The Bible (TV series)|The Bible]]''.  In 2013, Anozie appeared in the film ''[[Ender's Game (film)|Ender's Game]]'' as Sergeant Dap,<ref>{{cite news|last=Schaefer|first=Sandy|title='Ender's Game' gains three new cast members|url=http://www.csmonitor.com/The-Culture/Culture-Cafe/2012/0221/Ender-s-Game-gains-three-new-cast-members|newspaper=[[The Christian Science Monitor]]|date=21 February 2012}}</ref> and from 2013 to 2014 he played the role of [[Renfield]] on the NBC series ''[[Dracula (2013 TV series)|Dracula]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/dracula-nbc-jonathan-rhys-meyers-cast-game-thrones-merlin-416780 |title=NBC's 'Dracula' Series Casts 'Game of Thrones,' 'Merlin' Actors |last=Goldberg |first=Lesley |date=30 January 2013 |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |accessdate=9 November 2013}}</ref>

In November 2014, Anozie was cast in the CBS summer drama ''[[Zoo (TV series)|Zoo]]'', based on the [[Zoo (James Patterson novel)|novel]] by [[James Patterson]] and [[Michael Ledwidge]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Goldberg |first=Lesley |date=26 November 2014 |title='Game of Thrones' Alum Nabs Key Role in CBS' 'Zoo' (Exclusive) |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/game-thrones-alum-nabs-key-752507 |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |accessdate=27 November 2014}}</ref> ''Zoo'' premiered on 30 June 2015.

Anozie's radio work includes the role of Joe in the 2011 BBC Radio 4 Classic Serial production of Edna Ferber's ''[[Show Boat]]''.{{fact|date=May 2018}}

In 2019, Anozie portrayed the antagonist, Cargo, in the [[Prime Video]] film ''[[Guava Island]]'', starring [[Donald Glover]] and [[Rihanna]].
