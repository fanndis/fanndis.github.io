'''Mary Page Keller''' (born March 3, 1961, in Monterey Park, California)<ref name=tvg>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/mary-page-keller/credits/165118/202678/|title=Mary Page Keller|publisher=[[TV Guide|TVGuide.com]]| archivedate=March 20, 2016| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160320161711/http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/mary-page-keller/credits/165118/202678/}}</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20110306000232/http://www.forbes.com/feeds/ap/2011/03/03/general-history_8335833.html "Today in History"] Forbes.com 03 March 2011</ref> is an American actress known for roles on television. Keller began her career on the daytime soap operas ''[[Ryan's Hope]]'' (1982–1983) and ''[[Another World (TV series)|Another World]]'' (1983–1985) and later starred in a number of television sitcoms. She starred as Laura Kelly in the Fox comedy series ''[[Duet (TV series)|Duet]]'' (1987-1989) and in the show's spin-off, ''[[Open House (1989 TV series)|Open House]]'' (1989–1990). Keller later had lead roles in the short-lived sitcoms ''[[Baby Talk (TV series)|Baby Talk]]'' (1991–1992), ''[[Camp Wilder]]'' (1992–1993), and ''[[Joe's Life]]'' (1993).

In film, Keller is known for her performance in the comedy-drama ''[[Beginners]]'' (2010). In 2000s, she had recurring and guest-starring roles in a number of television dramas. From 2014 to 2015, Keller starred as the mother of the lead character in the [[ABC Family]] drama series ''[[Chasing Life]]''.

==Career==
As a child, Keller participated in productions at [[Toby's Dinner Theatre]] under the direction of director [[Toby Orenstein]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/014900/014919/html/14919bio.html#26.|title=Toby Barbara Orenstein|last=Shird|first=Shannon|date=2009-03-06|website=Maryland State Archives|access-date=2018-03-05}}</ref> She later began her acting career on daytime soap operas. She played [[Amanda Kirkland]] (1982–1983) on ''[[Ryan's Hope]]'' and Sally Frame (1983–85) on ''[[Another World (TV series)|Another World]]''.  Moving to primetime, she played the part of Laura Kelly on two [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] sitcoms, ''[[Duet (TV series)|Duet]]'' (1987–89) and its spinoff ''[[Open House (1989 TV series)|Open House]]'' (1989–90).<ref>{{cite web|last=Bernstein |first=Fred |url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20096621,00.html |title=That Bubbly Soap Charmer Mary Page Keller Gets Pressed into Beefing Up Duet's Tv Ratings |publisher=People.com |date=1987-06-29 |accessdate=2014-06-03}}</ref> In 1992, Keller was lead actress in Season 2 of the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] comedy series ''[[Baby Talk (TV series)|Baby Talk]]''. She also starred in another short-lived ABC [[sitcom]], ''[[Camp Wilder]]'' (1992–93).<ref>[https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=KQ7zX_ltr48C&dat=19921002&printsec=frontpage "Mary Page Keller Takes On Extended Family"] The Item - Oct 2, 1992</ref> In 1993, she starred in ''[[Joe's Life]]'', also on ABC. During the same years, Keller acted in a number of television films and had a dramatic role in ''[[Life Goes On (TV series)|Life Goes On]]''.

As of mid-1990s, Keller has made many guest appearances on television series, including ''[[Ellen (TV series)|Ellen]]'', ''[[The Practice]]'', ''[[NCIS (TV series)|NCIS]]'', ''[[Criminal Minds]]'', ''[[24 (TV series)|24]]'', ''[[Mad Men]]'', ''[[Castle (TV series)|Castle]]'', ''[[The Closer]]'', ''[[CSI: Crime Scene Investigation]]'', ''[[Supernatural (U.S. TV series)|Supernatural]]'', ''[[Pretty Little Liars (TV series)|Pretty Little Liars]]'' and ''[[Scandal (TV series)|Scandal]]''. She had recurring roles in ''[[Cybill]]'',  ''[[JAG (TV series)|JAG]]'', ''[[NYPD Blue]]'', ''[[Nip/Tuck]]'', ''[[Commander in Chief (TV series)|Commander in Chief]]'' and ''[[Hart of Dixie]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/mary-page-keller/credits/165118 |title=Mary Page Keller Credits |publisher=Tvguide.com |date= |accessdate=2014-06-03}}</ref> She also was regular on ''[[Zoe, Duncan, Jack & Jane]]'' (1999) and starred as Johnny Kapahala's mother in the [[Disney Channel]] original movies ''[[Johnny Tsunami]]'' (1999) and ''[[Johnny Kapahala: Back on Board]]'' (2007). She played the lead character's mother in the 2010 comedy-drama film ''[[Beginners]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/05/29/movies/beginners-mike-millss-autobiographical-film.html|title='Beginners,' Mike Mills's Autobiographical Film|first=Dennis|last=Lim|date=27 May 2011|publisher=|via=NYTimes.com}}</ref>

In 2011, Keller was cast as the lead character's mother in the [[ABC Family]] drama pilot ''[[Chasing Life]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/abc-family-terminales-mary-page-keller-378073 |title=Soap Vet Mary Page Keller Joins ABC Family Pilot 'Terminales' |publisher=Hollywoodreporter.com |date=2011-11-17 |accessdate=2013-06-18}}</ref> In April 2013, ABC Family picked up the pilot to series, for airing in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Andreeva |first=Nellie |url=https://www.deadline.com/2013/04/abc-family-pilot-terminales-picked-up-to-series-titled-chasing-life/ |title=ABC Family Pilot 'Terminales' Picked Up - Titled 'Chasing Life' |publisher=Deadline.com |date= |accessdate=2013-06-18}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Keller has been married to her former ''Another World'' costar [[Thomas Ian Griffith]] since 1991. They have two sons, Conner and Eamon.<ref name=filmref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/61/Thomas-Ian-Griffith.html |title=Thomas Ian Griffith Biography (1960?-)| accessdate= August 27, 2017| archivedate= September 6, 2012| archiveurl=https://archive.is/XsLG|url-status=live|quote=some sources cite 1962...}}</ref>
