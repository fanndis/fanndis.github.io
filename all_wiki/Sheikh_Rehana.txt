'''Sheikh Rehana Siddiq''' (born 13 September 1957) is a [[Bangladesh Awami League]] politician.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.clickittefaq.com/sheikh-rehanas-60th-birthday-today/|title=Sheikh Rehana's 60th birthday today |newspaper=The Daily Ittefaq |language=en-US|access-date=2017-12-01}}</ref> She is the younger sister of Prime Minister [[Sheikh Hasina]] and the daughter of [[Sheikh Mujibur Rahman]].<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://bdnews24.com/politics/2016/10/12/pm-hasinas-sister-rehana-daughter-putul-will-attend-awami-league-national-council|title = PM Hasina’s sister Rehana, daughter Putul will attend Awami League National Council|newspaper = bdnews24.com|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url = http://bdnews24.com/politics/2016/10/20/bangabandhu-s-grandchildren-bobby-putul-not-joining-awami-league-council-as-councillors|title = Bangabandhu's grandchildren Bobby, Putul not joining Awami League council as councillors|newspaper = bdnews24.com|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref> She is also the mother of [[Tulip Siddiq]], who is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] politician and elected [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for the [[Hampstead and Kilburn (UK Parliament constituency)|Hampstead and Kilburn]] constituency as a result of the [[2015 United Kingdom general election|2015 general election]].

== Early life ==
Sheikh Rehana was born to [[Sheikh Mujibur Rahman]], the first president of [[Bangladesh]], and [[Sheikh Fazilatunnesa Mujib]]. She was placed under house arrest in [[Dhanmondi Thana|Dhanmondi]] along with her family by the [[Pakistan Army]] during [[Bangladesh Liberation War]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/op-ed/politics/i-would-rather-die-sign-any-false-statement-1242298|title=I would rather die than sign any false statement|date=2016-06-20|newspaper=The Daily Star|language=en|access-date=2017-01-19}}</ref> She was in Belgium<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2010/01/11/hasina-revisits-delhi-her-home-from-1975-81|title=Hasina revisits Delhi, her home from 1975-81 |newspaper=bdnews24.com |access-date=2017-12-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://bdnews24.com/politics/2011/08/15/none-wanted-to-shelter-mujib-s-daughters|title='None wanted to shelter Mujib's daughters' |newspaper=bdnews24.com |access-date=2017-12-01}}</ref> with her sister Sheikh Hasina when her family was [[Assassination of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman|assassinated in a military coup]] by the [[Bangladesh Army]].<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://www.deccanchronicle.com/world/neighbours/200816/bangladesh-govt-mulling-commission-to-unmask-1975-coup-plotters.html|title = 'Bangladesh govt mulling commission to unmask 1975 coup plotters'|date = 2016-08-20|newspaper = Deccan Chronicle|language = en|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url = http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2016/08/11/nation-must-repay-the-debt-of-blood-to-bangabandhu-martyrs-hasina-says|title = Nation must repay the ‘debt of blood’ to Bangabandhu, martyrs, Hasina says|newspaper = bdnews24.com|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref>

== Career ==
Sheikh Rehana was allocated a government house in Dhanmondi for "Token" 100 taka in 2001 by the government of Bangladesh. The building was used to house her personal staff. The allocation was cancelled when Bangladesh Nationalist Party came to power. In 2014 she was allocated a house in Gulshan after Awami League came to power for a token price of 1001 taka.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2014/05/07/sheikh-rehana-getting-a-govt-house-in-gulshan|title=Sheikh Rehana getting a govt house in Gulshan|newspaper=bdnews24.com|access-date=2017-01-19}}</ref> In 2015 she and her children were given lifelong protection by the government of Bangladesh through the Special Security Forces. The government also announced free utility for life for her and her family.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/country/bangabandhu-family-get-more-security-free-utility-foreign-treatment-87319|title=Bangabandhu family to get more security, free utility, foreign treatment|date=2015-05-25|newspaper=The Daily Star|language=en|access-date=2017-01-19}}</ref> She was made councilor of Awami League Dhaka South unit in 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://www.thedailystar.net/politics/sheikh-rehana-putul-made-al-councilors-1298749|title = Sheikh Rehana, Putul made AL councilors|date = 2016-10-14|newspaper = The Daily Star|language = en|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Sheikh Rehana is married to Shafiq Ahmed Siddiqu, retired professor of Department of Accounting & Information Systems, Faculty of Business Studies (FBS), University of Dhaka.<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://www.thedailystar.net/frontpage/its-too-much-574102|title = It's too much|date = 2016-02-18|newspaper = The Daily Star|language = en|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref> Her son, Radwan Mujib Siddiq Bobby, is a councilor of Bangladesh Awami League.<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://bdnews24.com/politics/2016/10/11/radwan-mujib-siddiq-bobby-joining-awami-league-council-with-cousin-sajeeb-wazed-joy|title = Radwan Mujib Siddiq Bobby joining Awami League council with cousin Sajeeb Wazed Joy|newspaper = bdnews24.com|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref> Her daughter, [[Tulip Siddiq]], is a Member of Parliament for the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] in the [[United Kingdom]] [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] for the [[Hampstead and Kilburn (UK Parliament constituency)|Hampstead and Kilburn]] constituency, her other daughter is Azmina Siddiq. <ref>{{Cite news|url = http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2015/05/08/sheikh-rehana-proud-of-tulip-siddiq-for-her-victory-in-british-election|title = Sheikh Rehana proud of Tulip Siddiq for her victory in British election|newspaper = bdnews24.com|access-date = 2017-01-19}}</ref>
