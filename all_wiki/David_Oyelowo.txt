'''David Oyetokunbo Oyelowo''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%|OBE}} ({{IPAc-en|oʊ-|ˈ|j|ɛ|l|ə|w|oʊ}} {{respell|oh|YEL|ə|woh}};<ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/david-oyelowo-finally-reveals-how-you-pronounce-his-name-10013505.html "David Oyelowo finally reveals how you pronounce his name"] by Daisy Watt, ''[[The Independent]]'', 30 January 2015</ref> born 1 April 1976<ref>{{cite book|title= The International Who's Who 2004 | work= [[Europa Publications]] / [[Routledge]] | year = 2003 | isbn= 978-1857432176| page= [https://books.google.com/books?id=SK-81iI6NJQC&pg=PA1271&lpg=PA1271&dq=David+Oyetokunbo+oyelowo#v=onepage&q=David%20Oyetokunbo%20oyelowo&f=false 1,271]| title-link= International Who's Who }}</ref><!--cite is for both birth date and full name-->) is a British-American actor and producer.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2014/11/david-oyelowo-martin-luther-king-jr-selma |title= How David Oyelowo Approached the Daunting role of Martin Luther King Jr. in ''Selma''| work=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]| date= November 7, 2014| first= David | last= Handy| access-date= October 10, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/31107292/oyelowo-british-tv-ignoring-black-actors | title=  Oyelowo: British TV 'ignoring' black actors| date= 3 February 2015| publisher= BBC| access-date= October 10, 2016}}</ref> His highest-profile role to date was as [[Martin Luther King Jr.]] in the 2014 biographical drama film ''[[Selma (film)|Selma]]''. He also took the lead role in ''[[A United Kingdom]]'' (2016) as well as playing the role of a chess coach in ''[[Queen of Katwe]]'' (2016). He has played supporting roles in the films ''[[Rise of the Planet of the Apes]]'' (2011),  ''[[Lincoln (film)|Lincoln]]'' (2012), ''[[Jack Reacher (film)|Jack Reacher]]'' (2012), and garnered praise for portraying Louis Gaines in ''[[The Butler]]'' (2013). On television, he played [[MI5]] officer [[Danny Hunter]] on the British drama series ''[[Spooks (TV series)|Spooks]]'' (2002–2004) and [[Javert]] in the BBC miniseries ''[[Les Misérables (2018 miniseries)|Les Misérables]]'' (2018).

== Early life ==
Oyelowo was born in [[Oxford]], [[Oxfordshire]], England,<ref name= rebels>{{cite news | url = http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/entertainment/news/oyelowo-for-star-wars-rebels-29606307.html | title = Oyelowo for ''Star Wars: Rebels''? | work = [[Belfast Telegraph]] | date = 24 September 2013 }}</ref> to [[Nigeria]]n parents.<ref>[http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/11/28/david-oyelowo-and-daniel-craig-face-off-in-othello "David Oyelowo and Daniel Craig Face Off in ''Othello''] by Michael Schulman, ''[[The New Yorker]]'', 28 November 2016</ref> His father is from [[Oyo State]], Western Nigeria while his mother is from [[Edo State]], Southern Nigeria.<ref name="independent1">{{cite news|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=hoL7m4nVajg|title=Charlize Theron and David Oyelowo Answer The Web's Most Searched Questions.|date=6 March 2018|work=Wired|access-date=6 March 2018|location=U.S.A}}</ref> He was brought up as a [[Baptists|Baptist]].<ref name=ind1/> He grew up in [[Tooting Bec]], [[South London]], until he was six, when his family moved to [[Lagos]],<ref name= big /> Nigeria, where his father Stephen<ref name=ind1/> worked for the national airline and mother for a railway company. David attended a "'military-style' boarding school."<ref name=ind1/> They returned to London when Oyelowo was fourteen,<ref>{{cite news| first= Naomi| last= West| url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/3667799/As-he-likes-it.html |title= As he likes it | work= The Daily Telegraph| date= 8 September 2007}}</ref><ref name=ind1>{{cite news| first=Gerard | last=Gilbert | url = https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/upwardly-mobile-david-oyelowo-on-going-from-tooting-bec-to-tom-cruises-jet-8483779.html | title=Upwardly mobile: David Oyelowo on going from Tooting Bec to Tom Cruise's jet | work=The Independent | location = UK |date=9 February 2013| access-date=7 April 2013 | archive-date= 19 July 2013 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130719224008/http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/upwardly-mobile-david-oyelowo-on-going-from-tooting-bec-to-tom-cruises-jet-8483779.html | url-status=live}}</ref> settling in [[Islington]].<ref name=ind1/> He has been said to have 8 middle names, among which are "Animamoma", "Chukwudi", and "Oyerogba".

While enrolled in [[theatre studies]] at [[City and Islington College]], his teacher suggested that he become an actor. Oyelowo enrolled for a year in an acting [[foundation course]], at the [[London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art]] (LAMDA).<ref name=ind1/> He finished his three-year training in 1998.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.lamda.org.uk/publications/notices.pdf | title=Notices| work=[[London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art]]| date= Spring 2011|access-date= 7 April 2013 | archive-date= 25 May 2012| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20120525022944/http://www.lamda.org.uk/publications/notices.pdf }}</ref> He also spent time with the [[National Youth Theatre]].<ref>{{cite tweet|author= NationalYouthTheatre |user= NYTofGB |number= 603511737330114560 |title= Both #NYTalumni ! |date=2015-05-27 |accessdate= 2015-08-14}}</ref>

==Career==

===Stage===
He began his stage career in 1999 when he was offered a season with the [[Royal Shakespeare Company]] playing roles in [[Ben Jonson]]'s ''[[Volpone]]'', as the title character in ''[[Oroonoko]]'' (which he also performed in the BBC radio adaptation) and [[William Shakespeare|Shakespeare's]] ''[[Antony and Cleopatra]]'' (1999) alongside [[Guy Henry (actor)|Guy Henry]], [[Frances de la Tour]] and [[Alan Bates]]. However, he is best known for his next stage performance as [[Henry VI of England|King Henry VI]]<ref name= big /> in the Royal Shakespeare Company's 2001 productions of Shakespeare's trilogy of plays about the king as a part of its season ''[[This England: The Histories]]''. In a major landmark for [[colour-blind casting]], Oyelowo was the first black actor to play an English king in a major production of Shakespeare, and although this casting choice was initially criticised by some in the media, Oyelowo's performance was critically acclaimed and later won the 2001 [[Ian Charleson Awards|Ian Charleson Award]] for best performance by an actor under 30 in a classical play.

In 2005, he appeared in a production of ''[[Prometheus Bound]]'', which was revived in New York City in 2007. In 2006, he made his directorial debut on a production of ''[[The White Devil]]'', produced by Inservice, his theatre company in [[Brighton]] which is co-run with fellow Brighton-based actors [[Priyanga Burford]], [[Israel Aduramo]], Penelope Cobbuld, and his wife, Jessica.<ref>{{cite news | first=Emma | last= John | url = https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2005/aug/01/theatre | title=In my head this is massive | work=[[The Guardian]] | date= 1 August 2005 | archive-date= 17 December 2014 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141217091335/http://www.theguardian.com/stage/2005/aug/01/theatre | url-status=live}}</ref> He played the title role in ''[[Othello]]'' in 2016 at the [[New York Theatre Workshop]] with [[Daniel Craig]] as [[Iago]], directed by [[Sam Gold]].<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/20/theater/david-oyelowo-othello-new-york-theater-workshop.html "For David Oyelowo, the Time Has Come to Play Othello"] by Alexis Soloski, ''[[The New York Times]]'', 16 November 2016</ref>

===Television===
Oyelowo is best known for playing MI5 officer [[Danny Hunter]] on the British drama series ''[[Spooks (TV series)|Spooks]]'' (known in [[North America]] as ''MI-5'') from 2002 to 2004. He had before that appeared in ''[[Tomorrow La Scala]]'' (2002), ''[[Maisie Raine]]'' (1998) and ''Brothers and Sisters'' (1998). Soon after the end of his time on ''Spooks'' Oyelowo also appeared in the two Christmas specials of ''[[As Time Goes By (UK TV series)|As Time Goes By]]'' (2005). In 2006, he appeared in the television film ''[[Born Equal]]'' alongside [[Nikki Amuka-Bird]] as a couple fleeing persecution in [[Nigeria]] – they also both appeared in ''[[Shoot the Messenger (film)|Shoot the Messenger]]'' (2006), and in ''[[The No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency (TV series)|The No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency]]'' (2008) as a husband and wife. Other cameos have included ''[[Mayo (TV series)|Mayo]]'' (guest-starring on 30 April 2006) and the television film ''[[Sweet Nothing in My Ear]]'' (2008, as [[defence attorney]] Leonard Grisham), while he has played recurring or main characters on ''[[Five Days (TV series)|Five Days]]'' (2007) and ''[[The Passion (TV serial)|The Passion]]'' (2008, as [[Joseph of Arimathea]]).

In December 2009, he played the leading role of Gilbert in the BBC TV adaptation of [[Andrea Levy]]'s novel ''[[Small Island (TV film)|Small Island]]''.<ref>{{cite web |work=[[BBC]] | title = Small Island: David Oyelowo plays Gilbert | url = http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2009/10_october/14/small3.shtml |date =14 October 2009| access-date= December 31, 2013}}</ref> In March 2010, he played the role of Keme Tobodo in the BBC's drama series ''Blood and Oil''.<ref>{{cite news |first= Andrew| last= Anthony|newspaper= [[The Guardian]] | title= Blood and Oil; Canoe Man; Ashes to Ashes | url= https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2010/apr/04/tv-review-andrew-anthony |date= 4 April 2010| access-date= February 28, 2014}}</ref> He starred in the [[HBO]] original film ''[[Nightingale (film)|Nightingale]]'' (2014).

===Voice acting===
He appeared as [[Olaudah Equiano]] in ''Grace Unshackled – The Olaudah Equiano Story'', a radio play adapting Equiano's autobiography, ''[[The Interesting Narrative of the Life of Olaudah Equiano]]''. This was first broadcast on [[BBC 7]] on 8 April 2007, with his wife Jessica Oyelowo as Mrs. Equiano.

In 2007, Oyelowo was the reader for [[John le Carré]]'s ''[[The Mission Song]]''. ''[[AudioFile (magazine)|AudioFile]]'' magazine stated: "Think of David Oyelowo as a single musician playing all the instruments in a symphony. That is essentially what he manages in this inspired performance of John le Carré's suspense novel.... Can it really have been only one man in the narrator's recording booth? This virtuoso performance makes that seem impossible."<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.audiofilemagazine.com/dbsearch/showreview.cfm?Num=27688 |title=The Mission Song |quote=By John le Carré, Read by David Oyelowo |work=[[AudioFile (magazine)|AudioFile]] |date=August 2007 |access-date=16 December 2014 | archive-date= 9 October 2014 | archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20141009150027/http://www.audiofilemagazine.com/reviews/read/27688/ | url-status=live}}</ref> In 2015, he was selected to portray [[James Bond]] in ''[[Trigger Mortis]]'', written by [[Anthony Horowitz]].<ref name="bond">{{cite web |url=http://www.avclub.com/article/selma-star-david-oyelowo-play-james-bond-audiobook-223799 |title=''Selma'' Star David Oyelowo to Play James Bond—in Audiobook Form |last=Hughes |first=William |date=August 12, 2015 |work=[[The A.V. ===Film===

In 2012, Oyelowo appeared in ''[[Middle of Nowhere (2012 film)|Middle of Nowhere]]''. Writer-director [[Ava DuVernay]] had been a fan of his work and had considered asking him to take the role, however before she could, Oyelowo received the script coincidentally from a friend of a friend of DuVernay's who happened to be sitting next to him on the plane and was considering investing in the project.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Philbrick|first1=Jami|title=IAR Exclusive Interview: David Oyelowo Talks ''Middle of Nowhere'', ''The Paperboy'', ''Lincoln'' and ''Jack Reacher''|url=http://www.iamrogue.com/news/interviews/item/7470-iar-exclusive-interview-david-oyelowo-talks-middle-of-nowhere-the-paperboy-lincoln-and-jack-reacher.html|date=11 October 2012 |work=[[Iamrogue.com]] |access-date= 13 September 2014}}</ref> The film premiered at the [[2012 Sundance Film Festival]] to critical raves. That same year Oyelowo appeared in [[Lee Daniels]]' ''[[The Paperboy (2012 film)|The Paperboy]]'', which competed for the [[Palme d'Or]] at the [[2012 Cannes Film Festival]].<ref name="timeout">{{cite web|url=http://www.timeout.com/london/feature/2547/cannes-film-festival-2012-line-up-announced|title=Cannes Film Festival 2012 line-up announced|first= Dave| last= Calhoun| access-date= 19 April 2012|magazine=[[Time Out (magazine)|Time Out]]}}</ref> Oyelowo reunited with Daniels the following year in ''[[The Butler]]''.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Contemporary Black Biography, Volume 126|publisher=Gale Cengage Learning|year=2015|isbn=978-1-57302-425-9|pages=124–127}}</ref>

In 2014, Oyelowo formed his own independent production company, Yoruba Saxon Productions which has co-produced movies that featured him including, ''[[Nightingale (film)|Nightingale]]'', ''[[Captive (2015 film)|Captive]]'', ''[[Five Nights in Maine]]'', and most recently, ''[[A United Kingdom]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/exec/david-oyelowo|title=David Oyelowo|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|access-date=January 18, 2016|date=2013-12-16}}</ref>

He worked DuVernay again for ''[[Selma (film)|Selma]]'' (2014), playing civil rights activist [[Martin Luther King Jr.]] The film, based on the [[Selma to Montgomery marches|1965 Selma to Montgomery voting rights marches]], had originally been set to be directed by [[Lee Daniels]], but the project was dropped by Daniels so he could focus on ''The Butler''.

He is slated to star with [[Lupita Nyong'o]] in a film adaptation of the [[Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie]] novel ''[[Americanah]]''. The story follows a pair of young Nigerian immigrants who face a lifetime of struggle while their relationship endures.

In February 2019, it was announced that Oyelowo had joined the [[Peter Rabbit (film)|Peter Rabbit]] cast with [[James Corden]], [[Rose Byrne]] and  [[Domhnall Gleeson]] reprising their roles as [[Peter Rabbit|the title character]], Bea and Thomas McGregor for [[Peter Rabbit 2|its sequel]] due to be released in January 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kroll|first=Justin|date=2019-02-22|title=David Oyelowo Joins the Cast of Sony's 'Peter Rabbit' Sequel (EXCLUSIVE)|url=https://variety.com/2019/film/news/david-oyelowo-peter-rabbit-sequel-1203146201/|access-date=2020-09-21|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Sony Delays Release of 'Morbius,' 'Ghostbusters,' More Films Due to Coronavirus {{!}} Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/sony-delays-release-morbius-ghostbusters-due-coronavirus-1285759|access-date=2020-09-21|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref>

==Awards and honours==
For his portrayal of Martin Luther King Jr. in ''Selma'', Oyelowo received the [[NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actor in a Motion Picture]]. He received his first [[Golden Globe Award]] nomination for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Drama|Best Actor in a Motion Picture – Drama]], while also receiving a nomination for [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Actor]].<ref name=GGV2014>{{cite  news|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/news/golden-globe-nominations-announced-updated-1201376916/|title=Golden Globe: ‘Birdman,’ ‘Boyhood’ and ‘Imitation Game’ Top Nominations|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=11 December 2014|access-date=11 December 2014 | archive-date= 6 January 2015 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150106055547/http://variety.com/2014/film/news/golden-globe-nominations-announced-updated-1201376916/ | url-status =live}}</ref>

Also in 2014, for his performance in ''Nightingale'', he won the [[Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actor in a Movie/Miniseries]] and was nominated for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Limited Series or Movie]], [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Miniseries or Television Film]], [[NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actor in a Television Movie, Mini-Series or Dramatic Special]] and a [[Satellite Award for Best Actor – Miniseries or Television Film]].

Oyelowo was appointed Officer of the [[Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the [[2016 New Year Honours]] for services to drama.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=61450 |supp=y|page=N14|date=30 December 2015}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is married to actress [[Jessica Oyelowo]],<ref name="independent1"/> with whom he has four children. They live in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name=big>{{cite web|url=http://metro.co.uk/2013/02/15/from-spooks-to-lincoln-complicit-star-david-oyelowo-is-about-to-make-it-big-in-hollywood-3477715/|title=From Spooks to Lincoln, Complicit star David Oyelowo is about to make it big in Hollywood|work=[[Metro UK]] |date=15 February 2013 |access-date=15 February 2013| archive-date= 6 October 2014| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141006070407/http://metro.co.uk/2013/02/15/from-spooks-to-lincoln-complicit-star-david-oyelowo-is-about-to-make-it-big-in-hollywood-3477715/ | url-status=live}}</ref>

A devout [[Christians|Christian]],<ref name= big /> Oyelowo has stated that he believes God called him to play Rev. Martin Luther King Jr. Reflecting on his portrayal of King in the film ''[[Selma (film)|Selma]]'', Oyelowo has asserted that "I always knew that in order to play Dr. King, I had to have God flow through me because when you see Dr. King giving those speeches, you see that he is moving in his anointing."<ref name="Sojourners">{{cite journal|url=http://sojo.net/blogs/2015/01/08/selmas-david-oyelowo-playing-mlk-and-what-it-means-be-christian|title=''Selma''{{'}}s David Oyelowo on Playing MLK and What It Means to Be a Christian| access-date=19 April 2012|journal=[[Sojourners]]}}</ref>

A [[dual citizen]], Oyelowo and his wife became [[naturalised]] [[US citizen]]s on 20 July 2016.<ref name=Oregonlive>{{Cite web | url=https://www.oregonlive.com/celebrity-news/index.ssf/2016/09/david_oyelowo_was_inspired_to.html | title=David Oyelowo was inspired to become a citizen after filming Selma | access-date=18 November 2018 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181118081851/https://www.oregonlive.com/celebrity-news/index.ssf/2016/09/david_oyelowo_was_inspired_to.html | archive-date=18 November 2018 | url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/david-oyelowo-on-his-first-thanksgiving-as-an-american-citizen/ | title=David Oyelowo on his first Thanksgiving as an American citizen}}</ref> On doing so he stated, "I did a film called ''Selma''... and that film centers on voting rights and I've lived here for nearly 10 years now and to be walking around and doing a film about voting rights and telling people to vote, and you can't vote yourself is a little hypocritical. I decided it's time to do it and no time better than now."<ref name=Oregonlive/>

Oyelowo is an [[omoba]] (or [[prince]]) of the [[List of Nigerian traditional states|kingdom]] of [[Awe, Nigeria]], a part of the [[Nigerian Chieftaincy|Nigerian chieftaincy system]]. He commented, "it was useful for getting dates but probably not much else".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/features/david-oyelowo-interview-the-selma-actor-on-being-an-african-prince-struggling-with-american-red-tape-10316538.html|title=David Oyelowo interview: The 'Selma' actor on being an African prince|date=2015-06-14|work=The Independent|access-date=2018-04-05|language=en-GB}}</ref>
