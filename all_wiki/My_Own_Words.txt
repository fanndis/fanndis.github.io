'''''My Own Words''''' is a 2016 book by American [[Supreme Court Justice]] [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]] and her biographers Mary Hartnett and Wendy W. Williams. The book is a collection of Bader Ginsburg's speeches and writings dating back to the eighth grade. It was Bader Ginsburg's first book since becoming a Supreme Court Justice in 1993.

==Background==
In March 2016, [[Simon & Schuster]] announced  ''My Own Words'' was slated to release in January 2017.<ref name="Ziv2">{{cite news|last1=Ziv|first1=Stav|title=7 WAYS TO ADMIRE RUTH BADER GINSBURG WHILE AWAITING HER BOOK RELEASE|url=http://www.newsweek.com/7-ways-admire-ruth-bader-ginsburg-while-awaiting-her-book-release-435626|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=Newsweek|date=10 Mar 2016}}</ref><ref name="Hickson">{{cite news|last1=Hickson|first1=Ally|title=Ruth Bader Ginsburg Is Publishing A New Book — & It Sounds Epic|url=http://www.refinery29.com/2016/03/105662/ruth-bader-ginsburg-book-my-own-words|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=Refinery 29|date=15 Mar 2016}}</ref><ref name="Gajanan">{{cite news|last1=Gajanan|first1=Mahita|title=Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg co-writing new book called My Own Words|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2016/mar/10/ruth-bader-ginsburg-supreme-court-justice-new-book-my-own-words|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=The Guardian|date=10 Mar 2016}}</ref> The book was released early, however, being published on October 4, 2016. 

==Synopsis==
Bader Ginsburg writes the preface to the book, with Hartnett and Williams contextualizing "each part of the sections, which include law review articles, speeches, briefs and dissents."<ref name="Ziv">{{cite news|last1=Ziv|first1=Stav|title=WITH ‘MY OWN WORDS,’ THE 'NOTORIOUS RBG' LOOKS BACK ON HER LEGACY|url=http://www.newsweek.com/my-own-words-notorious-rbg-looks-back-her-legacy-507717|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=Newsweek|date=8 Oct 2016}}</ref> Many topics are covered in ''My Own Words'', including Bader Ginsburg's life as a Jewish woman, gender equality, the Supreme Court, and interpreting the U.S. Constitution.<ref name="Makers">{{cite news|title="My Own Words": Ruth Bader Ginsburg Publishes First Book Since Becoming Supreme Court Justice in 1993|url=http://www.makers.com/blog/ruth-bader-ginsburg-supreme-court-justice-1993|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=Makers|date=4 Oct 2016}}</ref> One chapter consists of excerpts from Derrick Wang's opera ''[[Scalia/Ginsburg]]'', with forewords by Ginsburg and Justice [[Antonin Scalia]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/ruth-bader-ginsburg/my-own-words-ginsburg/|title=MY OWN WORDS {{!}} Kirkus Reviews|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Chemerinsky|first=Erwin|date=2016-11-08|title=Book Review: My Own Words|url=http://www.washingtonindependentreviewofbooks.com/index.php/bookreview/my-own-words|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-01|website=Washington Independent Review of Books}}</ref>

One of the treatises in the book is the speech she gave when accepting the position of Supreme Court Justice.<ref name="Ziv" /> Another is a writing on why the United States needs the [[Equal Rights Amendment]].<ref name="PBS">{{cite news|title=Ruth Bader Ginsburg on becoming ‘Notorious’|url=https://www.pbs.org/newshour/bb/ruth-bader-ginsburg-becoming-notorious/|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=PBS|date=10 Oct 2016}}</ref>

Bader Ginsburg also recalls her relationship with [[Sandra Day O'Connor]] and [[Antonin Scalia]]. Of Scalia, she stated, "some very good people have some very bad ideas." Of O'Connor, she stated she learned to "waste no time on anger, regret or resentment, just get the job done."<ref name="Wolf">{{cite news|last1=Wolf|first1=Richard|title=Ruth Bader Ginsburg, in her 'Own Words'|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/books/2016/10/03/my-own-words-ruth-bader-ginsburg-book-review/90838696/|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=USA Today|date=3 Oct 2016}}</ref>

==Reception==
Reception for the book was generally positive.

''[[USA Today]]'' stated, "What emerges is not a portrait of a take-no-prisoners advocate but a strategic legal plotter who understands how to bring her audience around to her point of view."<ref name="Wolf" />

''[[Kirkus Reviews]]'' stated, "Only the most dedicated Ginsburg fans, and there are many, will devour everything here, but most readers will find items of interest from this icon of women’s rights."<ref name="Kirkus">{{cite news|title=My Own Words|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/ruth-bader-ginsburg/my-own-words-ginsburg/|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=Kirkus Reviews}}</ref>

''[[Yahoo! News]]'' stated, "In ''My Own Words''... the vim and vigor that she is famous for expressing from the Supreme Court bench is just as apparent in her high school editorials."<ref name="Boxer">{{cite news|last1=Boxer|first1=Sarah B.|title=Ruth Bader Ginsburg on Trump, Kaepernick and her lifelong love of the law|url=https://www.yahoo.com/katiecouric/ruth-bader-ginsburg-on-trump-kaepernick-and-her-lifelong-love-of-the-law-132236633.html|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=Yahoo! News|date=10 Nov 2016}}</ref>

''[[The New Republic]]'' stated, "The selection showcases her astonishing intellectual range, from law and lawyers in opera, to tributes to [[Louis Brandeis]], [[William Rehnquist]], and [[Gloria Steinem]], to the significance and form of dissenting opinions."<ref name="Showalter">{{cite news|last1=Showalter|first1=Elaine|title=Fighting Words|url=https://newrepublic.com/article/137040/fighting-words|accessdate=16 October 2016|publisher=The New Republic|date=3 Oct 2016}}</ref>
