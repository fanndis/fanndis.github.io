'''Matthew David McConaughey''' ({{IPAc-en|m|ə|ˈ|k|ɒ|n|ə|h|eɪ}}; born November 4, 1969) is an American actor and producer. He first gained notice for his supporting performance in the coming-of-age comedy ''[[Dazed and Confused (film)|Dazed and Confused]]'' (1993), this was considered by many to be his breakout role.<ref name="halperin2012">{{Cite news|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/matthew-mcconaughey-wooderson-dazed-confused-butch-walker-283627|title=Matthew McConaughey Reprises 'Dazed and Confused' Breakout Role for Music Video|last=Halperin|first=Shirley|date=January 20, 2012|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120124114538/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/matthew-mcconaughey-wooderson-dazed-confused-butch-walker-283627|archive-date=January 24, 2012|access-date=March 27, 2020|quote=McConaughey is reprising his breakout role of Wooderson, the iconic stoner from Richard Linklater’s 1993 film Dazed and Confused, who famously demured, “That's what I love about them high school girls... "When it comes to classic characters, Matthew McConaughey's Wooderson took the cake... and the high school girls with him," says Walker, who also released his first book this fall, Drinking with Strangers: Music Lessons From a Teenage Bullet Belt.}}</ref><ref name="grossman2015">{{cite news|url=https://time.com/3665375/matthew-mcconaughey-dazed-and-confused-audition-tape/|title=Treat Yourself to Matthew McConaughey's Dazed and Confused Audition Tape|last=Grossman|first=Samantha|date=January 13, 2015|work=[[Time (magazine)|TIME]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191120214357/https://time.com/3665375/matthew-mcconaughey-dazed-and-confused-audition-tape/|archive-date=November 20, 2019|access-date=March 27, 2020|quote=Sometimes it’s easy to forget that before he was a serious actor with a serious actor Oscar and a serious actor beard, Matthew McConaughey was just another handsome twenty-something from Texas. He followed this with leading performances in the science fiction film ''[[Contact (1997 American film)|Contact]]'' (1997), the historical drama ''[[Amistad (film)|Amistad]]'' (1997), the comedy-drama ''[[The Newton Boys]]'' (1998), the satire ''[[EDtv]]'' (1999), the war film ''[[U-571 (film)|U-571]]'' (2000), and the psychological thriller ''[[Frailty (2001 film)|Frailty]]'' (2001).

In the 2000s, McConaughey became best known for starring in [[romantic comedy|romantic comedies]],<ref name="pulver2013">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/jun/28/matthew-mcconaughey-killer-joe|title=Matthew McConaughey: from himbo to highbrow|last=Pulver|first=Andrew|date=June 28, 2012|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131231080356/https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/jun/28/matthew-mcconaughey-killer-joe|archive-date=December 31, 2013|access-date=April 26, 2013|quote=[[Romantic comedy|Romcoms]] are hard in a lot of ways: they're built to be buoyant. It's easy to demean them. I did a few romantic comedies. I enjoyed them. They paid well; they were fun. I didn't know if I wanted to do any more. I decided to sit out, and I had to endure for a while. Another one comes with a big old paycheck; I had to say no. I was looking for something to be turned on by.|journal=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> including ''[[The Wedding Planner]]'' (2001), ''[[How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days]]'' (2003), ''[[Failure to Launch]]'' (2006), ''[[Fool's Gold (2008 film)|Fool's Gold]]'' (2008), and ''[[Ghosts of Girlfriends Past]]'' (2009), establishing him as a [[sex symbol]]. After a two year hiatus from film acting, McConaughey began to appear in more dramatic roles beginning with the legal drama ''[[The Lincoln Lawyer (film)|The Lincoln Lawyer]]'' (2011). He was acclaimed for his supporting performances in ''[[Bernie (2011 film)|Bernie]]'' (2011), ''[[Magic Mike]]'' (2012) and ''[[The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film)|The Wolf of Wall Street]]'' (2013), and for his leading roles in ''[[Killer Joe (film)|Killer Joe]]'' (2011) and ''[[Mud (2012 film)|Mud]]'' (2012).<ref name="syme2014">{{Cite news|journal=[[The New Yorker]]|url=https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/the-mcconaissance|title=The McConaissance|last=Syme|first=Rachel|language=en|access-date=March 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191014152321/https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/the-mcconaissance|archive-date=October 14, 2019|date=January 16, 2014|quote=This morning, Matthew McConaughey woke up to his [[86th_Academy_Awards#Awards|first Oscar nomination]]. There’s no denying the McConaissance now, a bold second act in the American actor’s life which somehow feels as novel as it does deliberate. McConaughey’s return to the Hollywood firmament in the past two years has had an unusually organic quality to it, in that critics and audiences alike have quickly made room for his new oddball intensity and his desire to make interesting choices again after a decade of just livin’ and relying on his dimples and his baritone drawl.}}</ref>

McConaughey's portrayal of [[Ron Woodroof]], a cowboy diagnosed with [[HIV/AIDS|AIDS]], in the biopic ''[[Dallas Buyers Club]]'' (2013) earned him widespread praise and numerous accolades, including the [[Academy Award for Best Actor]]. In 2014, he starred as [[Rust Cohle]] in the [[True Detective (season 1)|first season]] of [[HBO]]'s crime anthology series ''[[True Detective]]'', for which he was nominated for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series]]. His film roles since have included ''[[Interstellar (film)|Interstellar]]'' (2014), ''[[The Sea of Trees]]'' (2015), ''[[Free State of Jones (film)|Free State of Jones]]'' (2016), ''[[Gold (2016 film)|Gold]]'' (2016), ''[[The Dark Tower (2017 film)|The Dark Tower]]'' (2017), and ''[[The Gentlemen (2019 film)|The Gentlemen]]'' (2019), earning varying degrees of commercial and critical success, as well as voice work in ''[[Kubo and the Two Strings]]'' (2016) and ''[[Sing (2016 American film)|Sing]]'' (2016).

== Career ==
=== Early 1990s to 1997: Rise to prominence and breakthrough ===
In the early 1990s, McConaughey began working in television commercials.<ref name="bio">{{cite web |title=Matthew McConaughey |url=http://www.biography.com/people/matthew-mcconaughey-16599996 |publisher=[[Biography.com]] |access-date=August 19, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141031183007/http://www.biography.com/people/matthew-mcconaughey-16599996 |archive-date=October 31, 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref>

He was not originally cast in the film, as the role of Wooderson was originally small and meant to be cast locally for budget purposes. At the time of casting, he was a film student at the [[University of Texas at Austin|University of Texas]] in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], who went out with his girlfriend at the Hyatt hotel bar,<ref>name=https://austin.eater.com/2015/11/23/9782500/matthew-mcconaughey-hyatt-bar-alamo-drathouse-franchise</ref> where he approached casting director Don Phillips in the bar of the Hyatt. <ref name="Marlow">{{cite web|last=Stern|first=Marlow|date=September 24, 2013|title='Dazed and Confused' 20th Anniversary: 20 Craziest Facts About the Cult Classic|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/09/24/dazed-and-confused-20th-anniversary-20-craziest-facts-about-the-cult-classic|access-date=May 7, 2017|work=The Daily Beast}}</ref> Phillips recalls, "The bartender says to him, 'See that guy down there? Due to the behavior of the actor playing Pickford with other cast members, his screen time was cut in favor of McConaughey's character, Wooderson. Linklater recalled "There was another actor who was kind of the opposite [of McConaughey]. He wasn’t really getting along with everybody. I could tell the actors weren’t responding to him."<ref name="Maxim">{{cite web|last=Spitz|first=Marc|date=December 26, 2013|title=An Oral History of ''Dazed and Confused''|url=http://www.maxim.com/entertainment/oral-history-dazed-and-confused|access-date=May 7, 2017|work=Maxim}}</ref> Much of the Wooderson role was improvised or written on the spot.<ref name="Maxim2">{{cite web|last=Spitz|first=Marc|date=December 26, 2013|title=An Oral History of ''Dazed and Confused''|url=http://www.maxim.com/entertainment/oral-history-dazed-and-confused|access-date=May 7, 2017|work=Maxim}}</ref> ''Dazed and Confused'' was released on September 24, 1993 in 183 theaters, grossing $918,127 on its opening weekend. The nations of the world fund the construction of the machine and an international panel is assembled to choose a candidate to travel in the machine. Although Arroway is a frontrunner to go, her hopes are scuppered by [[Christian philosophy|Christian philosopher]] Palmer Joss (McConaughey), a panel member whom Arroway met and briefly became romantically involved with.
