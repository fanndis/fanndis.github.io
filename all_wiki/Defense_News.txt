'''''Defense News''''' is a global website and magazine about politics, business and technology of defense. ''Defense News'' serves an audience of senior military, government and industry decision-makers throughout the world.<ref name="DN">{{cite web | last =  | first =  | authorlink =  | title = Help - About - Defense News | publisher = Defense News | date =  |url= http://www.defensenews.com/help.php| format =  | doi =  | accessdate = 2008-12-09 }}{{Dead link|date=July 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

''Defense News'' is published by the [[Defense News Media Group]], a unit of [[Sightline Media Group]], which was once a part of [[Gannett Company]].<ref name="gannett">{{cite web | last = | first = | authorlink = | title = Army Times Publishing Company, Inc. | publisher = Army Times Publishing Company | date = | url = http://www.atpco.com/| format = | doi = | accessdate = 2008-12-09}}</ref><ref>[http://www.gannett.com/article/20140512/PRESSRELEASES2014/140512001/Mark-Flinn-Named-President-of-Gannett-Government-Media/ '' May 2014 Gannett Press Release'']</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.globalprblogweek.com/archives/bradley_peniston_tal.php |title=''Interview: Brad Peniston of ''Defense News'' talks about Blogs and their impact on Defense News'' web site |access-date=2008-09-24 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080819175933/http://www.globalprblogweek.com/archives/bradley_peniston_tal.php |archive-date=2008-08-19 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  Sightline Media Group was sold to Los Angeles-based private equity firm [[Regent, L.P.|Regent]] in 2016 by [[TEGNA]].<ref>http://www.bizjournals.com/washington/news/2016/03/22/heres-how-much-the-tegna-ceo-earned-in-2015.html</ref>

It is the leading defense publication in the United States{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}} and is often cited in official reports and in Congressional testimony. 

''Defense News'' has a weekly television show about international defense and military issues. It first aired March 2, 2008, as ''This Week in Defense News with [[Vago Muradian]]'' on [[WUSA (TV)|WUSA 9]], a [[Washington D.C.|Washington, D.C.]], [[CBS]] affiliate. It later aired on [http://wjla.com/ ABC 7 WJLA] and the [[American Forces Network|Armed Forces Network]].<ref name="bc">{{cite web|url=http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/CA6526472.html|title=WUSA Teams Up with Weekly ''Defense News''|date=2008-01-28|publisher=Broadcasting & Cable|url-status=dead|accessdate=2008-12-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081202002842/http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/CA6526472.html|archive-date=2008-12-02}}</ref> In April 2017, the show relaunched on [[WETA-TV]] as ''Defense News Weekly''<ref>{{cite web|title=Defense News unveils enhancements to TV show|url=http://www.defensenews.com/articles/defense-news-unveils-enhancements-to-tv-show|website=Defense News|accessdate=4 April 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Defense News TV|url=http://www.weta.org/tv/program/defense-news-tv|website=WETA-TV|accessdate=4 April 2017}}</ref> with two co-hosts: Jill Aitoro, ''Defense News'' executive editor; and Tony Lombardo, executive editor of ''[[Military Times]]'', another Sightline brand. In September 2017, Jeff Martin, a former TV reporter from Alabama, took over hosting and producing duties. The show later moved off WETA to AFN and various online platforms.
