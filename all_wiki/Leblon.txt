'''Leblon''' ([[Brazilian Portuguese]]: [[Help:IPA/Portuguese|/leˈblõ/]]) is a neighborhood of [[Rio de Janeiro]], Brazil. It is also the name of the local beach. The neighborhood is located in the [[South Zone (Rio de Janeiro)|South Zone]] of the city, between [[Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas]], Morro Dois Irmãos and the Jardim de Alah channel, bordering the [[Gávea, Rio de Janeiro|Gávea]], [[Ipanema, Rio de Janeiro|Ipanema]], [[Lagoa, Rio de Janeiro|Lagoa]], and [[Vidigal, Rio de Janeiro|Vidigal]] neighborhoods. It is regarded as having the most expensive price per residential square meter in [[Latin America]].<ref>http://www.diariodorio.com/leblon-tem-o-metro-quadrado-mais-caro-do-brasil-e-a-maior-valorizao/</ref>

Leblon began as a [[quilombo]] of escaped slaves. (See [[:pt:Quilombo do Leblon]].)

==Etymology==
The neighborhood is named for Carlos Leblon, a [[whaling]] empresario [[French Brazilian|of French origin]] who possessed a [[chacra|chácara]] in the region since 1845. Before the area was urbanized it was known as ''Campo do Leblon'' (Leblon's Field).

== Characteristics ==
[[File:Praia do Leblon.JPG|thumb|left|Leblon Beach]]
[[File:Mapa de Leblon.jpg|thumbnail|300px|Map of Leblon.]]
It is located west of [[Ipanema]]. In the north, it is bordered by [[Gávea, Rio de Janeiro|Gávea]] and, in the west, by a towering hill called Dois Irmãos, which translates as "two brothers", because of its split peak.

== Leblon in popular culture ==
Leblon is known for being a very wealthy and cosmopolitan neighborhood, with a lively nightlife across its bars, restaurants and nightclubs. Leblon has been either referenced or depicted in the following media:

In television:

* [[Viver a Vida]], a [[telenovela]] produced by [[Rede Globo]], aired from September 2009 to May 2010;
* [[Páginas da Vida]], a telenovela produced by Rede Globo, aired from July 2006 to March 2007;
* [[Mulheres Apaixonadas]], a telenovela produced by Rede Globo, aired from February to October 2003;
* [[Laços de Familia]], a telenovela produced by Rede Globo, aired from June 2000 to February 2001;
* [[Por Amor (1997 telenovela)|Por Amor]], a telenovela produced by Rede Globo, aired from October 1997 to May 1998; 
* [[História de Amor]], a telenovela produced by Rede Globo, aired from July 1995 to March 1996.
