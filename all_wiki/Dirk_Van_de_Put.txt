'''Dirk van de Put''' (born 1959/60) is a Belgian businessman, and the chairman and chief executive officer (CEO) of [[Mondelez International]]. CEO since November 2017,<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2017/08/01/mondelez.html|title=Dirk Van de Put named CEO of Mondelēz as long-time CEO Irene Rosenfeld retires|first=|last=CNBC|date=2 August 2017|website=cnbc.com|accessdate=4 August 2017}}</ref><ref name="reuters.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-mondelez-ceo-idUSKBN1AI1DY|title=Outsider replaces long-time CEO at Oreo cookie maker Mondelez|author=|date=2 August 2017|publisher=|accessdate=4 August 2017|via=Reuters}}</ref> he also became chairman in April 2018.<ref name="chicagobusiness.com"/>

==Early life==
Van de Put is a native of [[Mechelen]], [[Antwerp Province]], Belgium, and has a veterinary medicine degree from [[Ghent University]], and a master's degree in business from the [[University of Antwerp]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chicagobusiness.com/article/20170802/NEWS07/170809945/who-is-dirk-van-de-put-the-new-ceo-of-mondelez|title=Who is Dirk Van de Put, the new CEO of Mondelez?|author=|date=|website=chicagobusiness.com|accessdate=4 August 2017}}</ref> He is fluent in Dutch, English, French, Spanish, and Portuguese.{{citation needed|date=June 2018}}

==Career==
Van de Put worked for [[Coca-Cola]], [[Mars Inc]], [[Novartis]] and [[Groupe Danone]], before becoming CEO of the Canadian frozen foods manufacturer [[McCain Foods]] in 2010.<ref name="auto"/><ref name="reuters.com"/> He is a non-executive director of [[Mattel]].<ref name="chicagobusiness.com"/>

In November 2017, he succeeded [[Irene Rosenfeld]] as CEO of [[Mondelez International]].<ref name="auto"/><ref name="reuters.com"/> He also became chairman in April 2018.<ref name="chicagobusiness.com"/>

==Personal life==
Van de Put has Belgian and US citizenship.<ref name="chicagobusiness.com"/>
