'''Christina Marietta McHale''' (born May 11, 1992)<ref name="CBSSports" /> is an [[United States of America|American]] [[tennis]] player. Her highest-ever [[WTA rankings]] are No. 24 in singles and No. 35 in doubles.

Known for an aggressive baseline game,<ref name=styleone>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com/player/671/christina-mchale/bio/|title=Christina McHale Bio|work=Tennis.com|date=|accessdate=September 19, 2016}}</ref> McHale has been recognized by ''[[The New York Times]]'' for her "booming" groundstrokes and fast footwork.<ref name=styletwo>{{cite news |first=John |last=Martin |title=McHale's Patience Propels Her Into Third Round |work=The New York Times |date=18 January 2012 |accessdate=19 September 2016 |url=http://straightsets.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/01/18/mchales-patience-propels-her-into-third-round/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131204104909/http://straightsets.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/01/18/mchales-patience-propels-her-into-third-round/ |archive-date=4 December 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She has reached the third round of all four [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments, and has represented the United States in Fed Cup and Olympic competitions. In September 2016, McHale won her first WTA title at the [[Japan Women's Open|Japan Open]].

==Early life==
Christina is the daughter of John and Margarita McHale. Her father John is an [[Irish American]], while her mother Margarita was born in [[Cuba]]. She resided at 56 Locust Avenue in [[Dumont, New Jersey]] until she was three years old. Her family lived in Hong Kong from the time she was three until she was eight, and she speaks a degree of [[Standard Mandarin|Mandarin Chinese]], along with fluent Spanish. In 2000, the McHale family returned to the United States and bought a home for $720,000 in [[Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey]]. In June 2006, she graduated from Upper School of the [[Englewood Cliffs Public Schools]] as the eighth-grade valedictorian.<ref>Coffey, Samantha. [http://www2.scholastic.com/browse/article.jsp?id=3756472 "Christina McHale Courts Greatness"], [[Scholastic News Kids Press Corps]], September 9, 2011. Accessed September 19, 2011. "Five years ago, Christina McHale was the valedictorian of her middle school in Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey. Today, she is the youngest women's tennis player in the top 100 of the world."</ref>

For her freshman year of high school, she attended the Academy of Law and Public Safety within [[Dwight Morrow High School]]. At the age of 15, she left home to train at the USTA Training Center headquarters in [[Boca Raton, Florida]]. She was homeschooled through Kaplan Online High School since age 15. Her sister Lauren played collegiate tennis at [[UNC-Chapel Hill]] and is married to ATP Tour player [[Ryan Harrison (tennis)|Ryan Harrison]].

==Career==
McHale began professional training at the USTA Training Center in [[Carson, California]].<ref>Waltz, Nicholas J. (January 31, 2013). [https://www.usta.com/About-USTA/Player-Development/usta_training_center-east_expands_programming/ "USTA Training Center-East expands programming"]. [[USTA]].</ref>

===2009===
McHale was granted a wildcard into the main draw of the [[2009 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]] where she lost a three-set match in the first round to [[Jessica Moore (tennis)|Jessica Moore]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Moore Secures First Win at Australian Open |url=https://wais.org.au/other/news_detail.php?id=324 |accessdate=25 May 2020 |publisher=wais.org.au |date=21 January 2009}}</ref>

In March, McHale played at a 25k tournament in Redding, California, where she was defeated in the first round by seventh seed [[Kimiko Date-Krumm]]. Playing in Miami at the [[2009 Sony Ericsson Open|Sony Ericsson Open]], McHale lost in the first round of qualifying to [[Anastasiya Yakimova]].

Competing at a 25k tournament in Szczecin, Poland, McHale advanced to the semifinal round where she was defeated by [[Stephanie Gehrlein]]. Seeded fourth at a 10k tournament in Alkmaar, McHale lost in her quarterfinal match to qualifier [[Laura Pous Tió]]. Coming through qualifying at the [[2009 Odlum Brown Vancouver Open – Women's Singles|Vancouver Open]], McHale was defeated in the first round by [[Olga Puchkova]]. Playing in Connecticut at the [[2009 Pilot Pen Tennis – Women's Singles|Pilot Pen]] tournament, McHale lost in the first round of qualifying to [[Yanina Wickmayer]]. Entering as a wildcard at the [[2009 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]], McHale won her first Grand Slam match by beating [[Polona Hercog]] in the first round.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Stephenson |first1=Colin |title=Englewood Cliffs native Christina McHale wins first round match at the U.S. Open |url=https://www.nj.com/sports/njsports/2009/09/englewood_cliffs_native_christ.html |accessdate=25 May 2020 |publisher=www.nj.com |date=1 September 2009}}</ref> She was defeated in the second round by 29th seed, former world No. 1, and 2006 champion, [[Maria Sharapova]].<ref>{{cite news |title=A fresh face gets taste of big stage |url=https://www.si.com/more-sports/2009/09/04/christina-mchale |accessdate=25 May 2020 |publisher=www.si.com |date=4 September 2009}}</ref>

In Canada at the Challenger de Saguenay, McHale lost in the second round to [[Anastasia Pivovarova]]. At the USTA Tennis Classic of Troy in Alabama, McHale reached the final where she was defeated by qualifier and compatriot [[Alison Riske]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Duke |first1=Nick |title=Riske wins Tennis Classic of Troy |url=https://www.troymessenger.com/2009/10/12/riske-wins-tennis-classic-of-troy/ |accessdate=25 May 2020 |publisher=www.troymessenger.com |date=12 October 2009}}</ref> Playing in Kansas City at the Pro Tennis Classic, she lost in the second round to second seed and compatriot Varvara Lepchenko. At the Goldwater Classic in Phoenix, McHale was defeated in the first round by Alison Riske. At her final tournament of the season, the Tevlin Challenger in Toronto, she lost in the second round to Alexandra Stevenson.

McHale ended the year ranked 218.

She lost in the final round of qualifying to Australian wildcard [[Destanee Aiava]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Thanasi Kokkinakis seals Brisbane International qualification |url=https://wwos.nine.com.au/tennis/kokkinakis-eyes-brisbane-qualification/a25b2aa8-d6fe-41c7-9054-380ba291534c |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=wwos.nine.com.au |date=30 December 2018}}</ref> At the [[2019 Australian Open – Women's Singles Qualifying|Australian Open]], McHale failed to make the main draw since she was defeated in the final round of qualifying by Varvara Lepchenko.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Marshall |first1=Ashley |title=FOUR AMERICANS QUALIFY FOR 2019 AUSTRALIAN OPEN |url=https://www.usta.com/en/home/pro/pro-media---news/qualifiers-eubanks--krueger-to-make-australian-open-debuts.html |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=www.usta.com |date=11 January 2019}}</ref>

Playing in Newport Beach at the [[2019 Oracle Challenger Series – Newport Beach – Women's Singles|Oracle Challenger Series – Newport Beach]], McHale fell in the third round to second seed [[Tatjana Maria]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Townsend and Davis to Meet in Quarterfinals |url=https://oraclechallengerseries.com/townsend-and-davis-to-meet-in-quarterfinals/ |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=oraclechallengerseries.com |date=24 January 2019}}</ref> At the [[2019 Dow Tennis Classic – Singles|Dow Tennis Classic]], Mchale was defeated in her quarterfinal match by fourth seed and eventual finalist Jessica Pegula.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Chalk |first1=Dan |title=McNally upsets defending champ Brengle in DTC quarterfinals |url=https://www.ourmidland.com/sports/article/McNally-upsets-defending-champ-Brengle-in-DTC-13582287.php |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=www.ourmidland.com |date=1 February 2019}}</ref> Coming through qualifying at the [[2019 Abierto Mexicano Telcel – Women's Singles|Abierto Mexicano Telcel]], McHale reached the second round where she lost to third seed Donna Vekić.<ref>{{cite news |title=HADDAD SURPRISES AND LEAVES STEPHENS OUT |url=https://abiertomexicanodetenis.com/en/2019/02/27/9274/ |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=abiertomexicanodetenis.com |date=27 February 2019}}</ref> McHale qualified for the [[2019 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|BNP Paribas Open]] beating [[Fanny Stollár]] and [[Vera Zvonareva]]. In the main draw, she beat thirtieth seed Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova in her second-round match.<ref>{{cite news |title=Novak Djokovic rallies; Naomi Osaka starts well at BNP Paribas Open |url=https://www.latimes.com/sports/more/la-sp-novak-djokovic-naomi-osaka-20190309-story.html |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=www.latimes.com |date=9 March 2019}}</ref> She was defeated in the third round by compatriot and former world number one Venus Williams.<ref>{{cite news |title=Venus Williams Dispatches Christina McHale in Latest Test |url=https://bnpparibasopen.com/news/venus-williams-dispatches-christina-mchale-in-latest-test/ |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=bnpparibasopen.com |date=11 March 2019}}</ref> At the [[2019 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami Open]], McHale lost in the first round of qualifying to [[Mandy Minella]]. In Mexico at the [[2019 Monterrey Open – Singles|Monterrey Open]], McHale was defeated in the first round of qualifying by Kristína Kučová.

McHale started her clay-court season at the [[2019 Copa Colsanitas – Singles|Copa Colsanitas]]. She lost in the first round to seventh seed Sara Sorribes Tormo.<ref>{{cite news |title=WTA review: Belinda Bencic bows out in Lugano after shock defeat to Antonia Lottner |url=https://www.sportinglife.com/tennis/news/bencic-crashes-out-in-switzerland/164978 |accessdate=5 May 2020 |publisher=www.sportinglife.com |date=10 April 2019}}</ref> At the [[2019 J&T Banka Prague Open – Singles|J&T Banka Prague Open]], McHale was defeated in the first round of qualifying by [[Anna Kalinskaya]]. ==World TeamTennis==
McHale has played three seasons with [[World TeamTennis]], making her debut in 2016 with the [[New York Empire (tennis)|New York Empire]]. She has since played for the [[Washington Kastles]] and the [[San Diego Aviators]] in 2019.  It was announced she will be joining the [[San Diego Aviators]] during the 2020 WTT season set to begin July 12.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://wtt.com/2020/06/16/world-teamtennis-adds-stars-tiafoe-puig-raonic-bouchard-sock-as-rosters-set-for-2020/ |title=World TeamTennis Adds Stars Tiafoe, Puig, Roanic, Bouchard, & Sock As Rosters Set For 2020 |website=WTT.com |date=June 16, 2020 }}</ref>

==Playing style==
An aggressive baseliner, McHale is noted for her powerful forehand groundstrokes, as well as for her speed around the court.<ref name=styleone/><ref name=styletwo/> During her second-round match at Wimbledon in 2016, Eurosport commended McHale for displaying "superb court coverage".<ref>{{cite news |title=LIVE Serena Williams - Christina McHale - Wimbledon |url=https://www.eurosport.com/tennis/wimbledon-women/2016/live-serena-williams-christina-mchale_mtc861477/live.shtml |accessdate=24 May 2020 |publisher=www.eurosport.com |date=1 July 2016}}</ref> ''[[The New York Times]]'' has noted McHale's "booming" groundstrokes as one of her primary strengths.<ref name=styletwo/>
