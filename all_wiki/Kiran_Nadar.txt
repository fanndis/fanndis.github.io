'''Kiran Shiv Nadar''' is an [[India]]n [[art collector]] and philanthropist.<ref name="treasuringart">{{cite news|url=http://www.deccanherald.com/content/149011/treasuring-art-kiran-nadar-way.html|title=Treasuring art, the Kiran Nadar way|last=Goel|first=Poonam|publisher=[[Deccan Herald]]|accessdate=14 May 2012}}</ref> Kiran is the wife of [[Shiv Nadar]], the founder of [[HCL Technologies]],<ref>{{cite news |title=When Kiran Nadar surprised husband Shiv with an unusual painting |url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/magazines/panache/when-kiran-nadar-surprised-husband-shiv-with-an-unusual-painting/articleshow/74295804.cms |work=The Economic Times}}</ref> and is a trustee of the Shiv Nadar Foundation and the founder of the [[Kiran Nadar Museum of Art]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jury|url=http://www.theskodaprize.com/2011/jury-2011/kiran-nadar-jury-chairperson.html|publisher=The Skoda Art prize|accessdate=12 May 2012}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Kiran  met her husband, [[Shiv Nadar]], at an advertising agency where she worked and the two got married.<ref name="artofsharing"/> Nadar have a daughter called [[Roshni Nadar]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Nisa Godrej, Roshni Nadar among Forbes billionaire heiresses|url=http://www.business-standard.com/india/news/nisa-godrej-roshni-nadar-among-forbes-billionaire-heiresses/98874/on|accessdate=14 May 2012|newspaper=[[Business Standard]]|date=22 June 2010}}</ref>
Nadar is also one of the top [[contract bridge]] players in India [http://www.hcl.com/leadership/hcl-corporation].

== Career ==
Nadar started her career in advertising as a communications and brands professional at MCM. Nadar then joined [[NIIT]] and helped shape the brand.<ref>{{cite news|last=Singh|first=Pallavi|title=The Kiran Nadar Museum of Art|url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/features/people-places/the-kiran-nadar-museum-of-art/articleshow/7344989.cms?curpg=1|accessdate=12 May 2012|newspaper=Economic Times|date=23 January 2012}}</ref>

Currently, her roles include managing the SSN Trust, Public Health Foundation of India (PFHI), Rasaja Foundation and the Rajiv Gandhi Foundation to support young Muslim girls in their education in [[Uttar Pradesh]].<ref name="artofsharing">{{cite news|title=Kiran Nadar: The art of sharing|url=http://www.business-standard.com/india/news/kiran-nadarartsharing/123681/on|publisher=[[Business Standard]]|accessdate=8 June 2012|date=27 January 2011|location=Mumbai|last=R|first=Prerna}}</ref>

== Art collections and museum ==
Nadar's fascination in collecting artworks began in 1988 when she was purchasing pieces for her home.

In 2005, Nadar decided to open her own museum To house her vast collection of art. "It used to pinch me that the bulk of my artworks were in storage when I really wanted to share them with the world at large," Nadar explains.
Today, the Kiran Nadar Museum of Art attracts over 100,000 visitors annually. According to Gaurav Bhatia, managing director of Sotheby's India, Nadar's museum has made "some incredible artwork accessible to common citizens". Bhatia also acclaimed that Nadar's collection is "a wonderful mix of instinct, study and enthusiasm".

== Awards and accolades ==
In 2010, Kiran Nadar is acknowledged by Forbes Asian Magazine as a “hero of philanthropy” thanks to her launch of India's first private philanthropic museum.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://2010/03/08/asia-heroes-charity-personal-finance-philanthropy-india|title=Asia's Heroes Of Philanthropy|last=Alberts|first=Hana R.|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref>

Nadar is considered the [[wiktionary:maharáni|maharani]] of the Indian art world thanks to her collection of 5,500 and more modern Southern Asian arts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/naazneenkarmali/2018/10/24/kiran-nadars-groundbreaking-museum-of-indian-art/|title=Kiran Nadar's Groundbreaking Museum Of Indian Art|last=Karmali|first=Naazneen|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref> She is an international council member of the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York and is also one of the top commonwealth bridge players in India. She is also a member of the “Formidable” and has brought back many laurels. Nadar also represented India in various international competitive bridge events and managed to earn the gold medal for India after 12 years.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/magazines/panache/bridge-champions-kiran-nadar-brings-the-gold-to-india-after-12-years/articleshow/63029473.cms|title=Bridge champions! Kiran Nadar brings the gold to India after 12 years|date=2018-02-22|work=The Economic Times|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref>
