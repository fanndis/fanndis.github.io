The '''Environmental Law Alliance Worldwide (ELAW)''' is a [[public interest]], [[nonprofit]], environmental organization that helps communities protect the environment and public health through law. ELAW helps partners strengthen and enforce laws to protect themselves and their communities from toxic [[pollution]] and [[environmental degradation]].  ELAW provides legal and scientific tools and support that local advocates need to challenge environmental abuses.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elaw.org/about|title=Mission and History |website=Environmental Law Alliance Worldwide (ELAW)}}</ref>

== History ==
ELAW was founded in 1989 by lawyers from [[Australia]], [[Canada]], [[Chile]], [[Ecuador]], [[Indonesia]], [[Malaysia]], [[Peru]], the [[Philippines]], [[Sri Lanka]], and the [[United States]].  The founders were gathered at the [[University of Oregon]]'s [[Public Interest Environmental Law Conference]].  ELAW's U.S. office is in [[Eugene, Oregon]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.uuworld.org/articles/elaw-helps-lawyers-fight|title=ELAW helps lawyers around the world fight for environmental justice|date=2016-05-09|work=UU World Magazine|access-date=2018-11-12|language=en}}</ref>

== Grants ==
ELAW has received grants from the [[MacArthur Foundation]] and the [[Charles Stewart Mott Foundation]] for projects in [[Conservation movement|conservation]] and [[sustainable development]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macfound.org/grantees/285/|title=Environmental Law Alliance Worldwide - MacArthur Foundation|website=www.macfound.org|language=en|access-date=2018-11-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.mott.org/news/articles/global-alliance-elevating-environmental-standards-developing-nations/|title=Global alliance elevates environment standards|last=|first=|date=December 17, 2015|work=Mott Foundation|access-date=2018-11-12|language=en-US}}</ref>
