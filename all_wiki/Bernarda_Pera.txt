==Early life and career==
Bernarda was born in Zadar in Croatian family. Besides English, she speaks Croatian as well. Pera has won nine singles and eight doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]]. On 2 March 2020, she reached her career-high singles ranking of world No. 59.

She made her [[WTA Tour]] debut at the [[2014 US Open – Women's Doubles|2014 US Open]], having received a wildcard with [[Tornado Alicia Black]] in the doubles tournament.<ref name="USOpen-2014WD">{{cite web

==World TeamTennis==
Pera wanted to make her [[World TeamTennis]] debut in 2020, joining the [[Washington Kastles]] when the WTT season should begin July 12, at [[The Greenbrier]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://wtt.com/2020/06/16/world-teamtennis-adds-stars-tiafoe-puig-raonic-bouchard-sock-as-rosters-set-for-2020/ |title=World TeamTennis Adds Stars Tiafoe, Puig, Roanic, Bouchard, & Sock As Rosters Set For 2020 |website=WTT.com |date=June 16, 2020 }}</ref>
