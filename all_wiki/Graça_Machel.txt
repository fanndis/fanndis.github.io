'''Graça Machel ''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|DBE}} {{Post-nominals|list=[[Fellow of the British Academy|HonFBA]]}} ({{IPA-pt|ˈɡɾasɐ mɐˈʃɛɫ}}; [[née]] '''Simbine''', {{IPA-pt|sĩˈbĩni|pron}}, born 17 October 1945) is a [[Mozambique|Mozambican]] politician and humanitarian. She is the widow of both former presidents of [[List of presidents of Mozambique#Presidents of Mozambique (1975–present)|Mozambique]] and [[President of South Africa#Presidents of South Africa (1994–present)|South Africa]]; Mozambican president [[Samora Machel]] and [[South African people|South African]] president [[Nelson Mandela]]. Machel is an international advocate for women's and children's rights and was made an honorary British [[Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire|Dame]] by Queen [[Elizabeth II]] in 1997 for her humanitarian work. She is the only woman in modern history to have served as First Lady of two countries.

Graça Machel is a member of the [[Africa Progress Panel|Africa Progress Panel (APP)]], a group of ten distinguished individuals who advocate at the highest levels for equitable and sustainable development in Africa. As a panel member she facilitates coalition building to leverage and broker knowledge, and convenes decision-makers to influence policy for lasting change in Africa. 

She was [[Chancellor (education)|chancellor]] of the [[University of Cape Town]] between 1999 and 2019.

==Early life and education==
[[File:Nicolae Samora Graça.jpg|thumb|left|Graça and Samora Machel hosting [[Socialist Republic of Romania|Romanian Communist]] leader [[Nicolae Ceauşescu]], [[Maputo]], 1979]]
Graça Simbine was born 17 days after her father's death, the youngest of six children,<ref name=ThisDayLive-2014-08-16>{{cite news |url= http://www.thisdaylive.com/articles/graca-machel-there-is-nothing-exceptional-about-me-/186451 |title= Graca Machel: There Is Nothing Exceptional About Me... |date= 16 August 2014 |publisher= This Day Live |url-status= dead |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20150713231840/http://www.thisdaylive.com/articles/graca-machel-there-is-nothing-exceptional-about-me-/186451 |archivedate= 13 July 2015 |df= dmy-all }}</ref> in rural [[Incadine]], [[Gaza Province]], [[Portuguese East Africa]] (modern-day [[Mozambique]]). She attended [[Methodism|Methodist]] mission schools before gaining a scholarship to the [[University of Lisbon]] in [[Portugal]], where she studied [[German language|German]] and first became involved in independence issues.

Machel also speaks [[French language|French]], [[Spanish language|Spanish]], [[Italian language|Italian]], [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]] and [[English language|English]], as well as her native [[Tsonga language|Xitsonga]] language.

== Political career ==
Simbine returned to Portuguese East Africa in 1973, joining the [[Mozambican Liberation Front]] (Frelimo) and working as a schoolteacher. Following [[Mozambique]]'s independence in 1975, Simbine was appointed Mozambican first Minister of Education and Culture on 25 June 1975. 

During her tenure, the number of students enrolled in primary and secondary schools rose from about 40 percent of all school-aged children to over 90 percent for males and 75 percent for females.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sahistory.org.za/people/graca-simbine-machel|title=Graça Simbine Machel|date=2011|publisher=sahistory|access-date=4 May 2020}}</ref>

== Later career ==
Graça Machel received the 1992 Africa Prize, awarded annually to an individual who has contributed to the goal of eliminating hunger in Africa by the year 2000. Machel received the 1995 [[Nansen Medal]] from the [[United Nations]] in recognition of her longstanding humanitarian work, particularly on behalf of [[refugee]] children.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unhcr.org/nansen/50374dc66.html|title=Nansen Refugee Award|first=United Nations High Commissioner for|last=Refugees|accessdate=13 December 2016}}</ref>

In 1997, Machel was honored with the [[Dame Commander]] of the [[Order of the British Empire]] by Queen [[Elizabeth II]] for her contributions and services in the field of human rights protection. In the same year, and received the 1997 Global Citizen Award of the New England Circle. 1998, Machel was one of the two winners of the [[North–South Prize]] awarded by the North-South Centre of the [[Council of Europe]].<ref>{{cite web | title = The North South Prize of Lisbon | work = North-South Centre | publisher = Council of Europe | url = http://www.coe.int/t/e/north%2Dsouth_centre/programmes/7_north%2Dsouth_prize/winners_PNS.asp  | accessdate =21 January 2008 |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080215230739/http://www.coe.int/t/e/north-south_centre/programmes/7_north-south_prize/winners_PNS.asp <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = 15 February 2008}}</ref>

Machel has been chancellor of the [[University of Cape Town]] from 1999 to 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ewn.co.za/2019/12/14/graca-machel-steps-down-as-uct-chancellor|website=Eyewitness News|title=Graca Machel steps down as UCT chancellor|first= Jarita|last=Kassen|date=15 December 2019|access-date=15 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.news.uct.ac.za/article/-2019-12-13-i-am-not-leaving-uct-i-am-taking-uct-with-me|website=University of Cape Town|title=I am not leaving UCT, I am taking UCT with me|first= Niémah |last=Davids|date=13 December 2019|access-date=15 December 2019}}</ref> In 2009, Machel was appointed to the [[Commonwealth of Nations]]' [[Commonwealth Eminent Persons Group|Eminent Persons Group]]. She was named president of the [[School of Oriental and African Studies]] at the [[University of London]] in 2012.<ref>[https://www.soas.ac.uk/news/newsitem75798.html Graça Machel Appointed as President of SOAS]</ref> In 2016, Machel was named chancellor of the [[African Leadership University]], a role that she still holds today.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ayibamagazine.com/an-evening-with-graca-machel-and-fred-swaniker/|title=Evening with Graca Machel and Fred Swaniker|date=4 November 2015|website=Evening with Graca Machel and Fred Swaniker|publisher=Ayiba Team}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://alueducation.com/about/our-leadership/|title=Our Leadership – ALU|work=ALU|access-date=1 May 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

In July 2017, Machel was elected an [[Fellow of the British Academy|Honorary Fellow]] of the [[British Academy]] (HonFBA), the United Kingdom's [[national academy]] for the humanities and social sciences.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.britac.ac.uk/news/elections-british-academy-celebrate-diversity-uk-research|title=Elections to the British Academy celebrate the diversity of UK research|date=21 July 2017}}</ref>
In 2018, she was awarded by the [[World Health Organization]] for her contributions to the health and wellbeing of women, children and adolescents.

On 17 July 2018, Machel attended the [[The Nelson Mandela Annual Lecture|16th Nelson Mandela Annual Lecture]], which was located at the [[Wanderers Stadium]], Johannesburg, alongside South African President [[Cyril Ramaphosa]] and former US President [[Barack Obama]]. The event was visited by nearly 15,000 people, commemorating the 100th anniversary of Nelson Mandela's birth.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/world/barack-obama-makes-coded-attack-on-donald-trump-in-most-significant-speech-since-the-end-of-his-a3889671.html|author=Ella Wills|title=Barack Obama in coded attack on Donald Trump in his most significant speech since the end of his presidency|date=17 July 2018|newspaper= London Evening Standard}}</ref>

=== United Nations ===
Following her retirement from the Mozambique ministry, Machel was appointed as the expert in charge of producing the groundbreaking [[United Nations]] report on the impact of armed conflict on children.<ref>[http://www.unicef.org/graca The impact of Armed Conflict on Children]. Unicef.org. Retrieved 7 November 2011.</ref> From 2008 until 2009, she was a member of the High Level Taskforce on Innovative International Financing for Health Systems, co-chaired by [[Gordon Brown]] and [[Robert Zoellick]].<ref>[https://www.who.int/pmnch/media/news/2009/hltfh_report/en/ High Level Taskforce on International Innovative Financing for Health Systems: Report released] [[WHO]], press release of 29 May 2009.</ref> She currently serves as the chair of [[The Partnership for Maternal, Newborn & Child Health]] (PMNCH). In January 2016, she was also appointed by [[United Nations Secretary-General]] [[Ban Ki-moon]] to the High-level Advisory Group for ''Every Woman Every Child.''<ref>[http://www.everywomaneverychild.org/news-events/news/1291-un-secretary-general-announces-members-of-the-high-level-advisory-group-for-every-woman-every-child UN Secretary-General Announces Members of the High-Level Advisory Group for Every Woman Every Child] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160828191637/http://everywomaneverychild.org/news-events/news/1291-un-secretary-general-announces-members-of-the-high-level-advisory-group-for-every-woman-every-child |date=28 August 2016 }} ''Every Woman Every Child'', press release of 21 January 2016.</ref>

[[File:Graca Machel, World Economic Forum on Africa 2010.jpg|thumb|Machel during the [[World Economic Forum|WEF]] 2010]]

On January 17th 2016, she was announced by the [[UNESCO]] as a [[Sustainable Development Goals]] Advocate<ref>{{Cite web|title=graca-machel|url=https://www.unsdgadvocates.org/graca-machel|access-date=2020-09-19|website=SDG Advocates|language=en-US}}</ref> alongside 16 others, all appointed by [[Secretary-General of the United Nations]]

=== The Elders ===
On 18 July 2007 in [[Johannesburg]], South Africa, [[Nelson Mandela]], Graça Machel, and [[Desmond Tutu]] convened [[The Elders (organization)|The Elders]]. Mandela announced its formation in a speech on his 89th birthday. The group works on thematic as well as geographically specific subjects. The Elders' priority issue areas include the [[Israeli–Palestinian conflict]], the [[Korean Peninsula]], [[Sudan]] and [[South Sudan]], [[sustainable development]], and [[Women's rights|equality for girls and women]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theelders.org/our-work |title=The Elders: Our Work |publisher=TheElders.org |accessdate=7 March 2013}}</ref>

Machel has been particularly involved in The Elders' work on [[child marriage]], including the founding of [[Girls Not Brides: The Global Partnership to End Child Marriage]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/elisabeth-braw/graca-machel-within-ten-y_b_1003051.html |title=Graca Machel: Within Ten Years Women Will Have Changed Africa |author=Elisabeth Braw, Metro International |work=The Huffington Post |date=10 October 2011 |accessdate=7 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/child-marriage-robs-girls-of-their-opportunities/2012/07/31/gJQAUd7kNX_story.html |title=Early marriage robs children of their opportunities |author=Graça Machel and Desmond Tutu |work=The Washington Post |date=1 August 2012 |accessdate=7 March 2013}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Obama and Machel.jpg|thumb|US President [[Barack Obama]] greets Graça Machel at the memorial service for her late husband [[Nelson Mandela]], [[Johannesburg]], 2013]]
Simbine married [[Samora Machel]], the first president of Mozambique, in 1975. Together they had two children: daughter Josina (born April 1976) and son Malengane (born December 1978). Samora Machel died in office in 1986 when his presidential aircraft crashed near the [[Mozambique]]-[[South Africa]] border.

Graça Machel Mandela married her second husband, [[Nelson Mandela]], in Johannesburg on 18 July 1998, Mandela's 80th birthday. At the time, Mandela was serving as the first post-apartheid president of [[South Africa]]. Mandela died of pneumonia on 5 December 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nelson Mandela Has Died, A Look Back at His Legacy|url=https://www.biography.com/news/mandela-dead-at-95|website=Biography|accessdate=6 May 2018|language=en-us}}</ref>
