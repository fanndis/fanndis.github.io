'''Steven Levy''' (born 1951) is an American journalist who has written several books on computers, technology, [[cryptography]], the internet, [[cybersecurity]], and [[privacy]].

==Career==
Levy is writer and Editor at Large for ''[[Wired magazine|Wired]]''.<ref>https://www.wired.com/author/steven-levy/</ref> He was previously chief technology writer and a senior editor for ''[[Newsweek]]''. Levy has had articles published in ''[[Harper's]]'', ''[[Macworld]]'', ''[[The New York Times|The New York Times Magazine]]'', ''[[The New Yorker]]'', ''[[Premiere (magazine)|Premiere]]'', and ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.  He is regarded as a prominent and respected critic of [[Apple Inc.]] In July 2004, Levy wrote a cover story (which also featured an interview with Apple CEO [[Steve Jobs]]) which unveiled the 4th generation of the [[iPod]] to the world before Apple had officially done so.{{Citation needed|date=May 2017}}

In 1984, he wrote a book called ''[[Hackers: Heroes of the Computer Revolution]]'', in which he described a "[[hacker ethic]]", which became a guideline to understanding how computers have advanced into the machines that we know and use today. He identified this hacker ethic to consist of key points such as that all information is free, and that this information should be used to "change life for the better".

Levy won the "Computer Press Association Award" for a report he co-wrote in 1998 on the [[Year 2000 problem]].

Levy was a contributor to [[Stewart Brand]]'s [[Whole Earth Software Catalog]], first published in 1984.

In 1978, Steven Levy rediscovered [[Albert Einstein's brain]] in the office of the pathologist who removed and preserved it.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stevenlevy.com/index.php/einsteins-brain|title=Einstein's Brain|work=About Steven|accessdate=July 21, 2011}}</ref>

Levy received his [[bachelor's degree]] from [[Temple University]] and earned a master's degree in literature from [[Pennsylvania State University]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.stevenlevy.com/index.php/about |title=About Steven Levy |publisher=Stevenlevy.com}}</ref> He lives in New York City with his wife, [[Pulitzer Prize]] winner [[Teresa Carpenter]], and son.
