''' Dinendra Ruwan Wijewardene''' ([[Sinhala language|Sinhala]]:දිනෙන්ද්‍ර රුවන් විජෙවර්ධන) (born 4 August 1975) (known as ''Ruwan Wijewardene'') is a [[Sri Lanka]]n politician. He is the former State Minister of Defence and former Mass Media non-cabinet Minister.<ref>[http://www.dailymirror.lk/breaking_news/Ruwan-Wijewardene-appointed-Mass-Media-non-cabinet-Minister/108-162784  Ruwan Wijewardene appointed Mass Media non-cabinet Minister].</ref> He is a former [[Member of Parliament]] for [[Gampaha District]].<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20100513040940/http://www.slelections.gov.lk/pdf/GE2010_preferences/Gampaha_pref_GE2010.pdf Parliamentary Elections (2010) Electoral District No:- 02 - Gampaha].</ref> Currently He is the Deputy Leader of the [[United National Party]] and chief organiser for [[Kelaniya Electoral District|Kelaniya Electorate]].<ref>[http://www.dailymirror.lk/top_story/Ruwan-Wijewardene-elected-Deputy-Leader-of-UNP/155-195851 Ruwan Wijewardene elected Deputy Leader of UNP]. Daily Mirror (Sri Lanka), Retrieved on 14 September 2020.</ref><ref>[https://srilankamirror.com/news/17860-ruwan-appointed-to-kelaniya-electorate Ruwan appointed to Kelaniya electorate]. Sri Lanka Mirror, Retrieved on 21 April 2020.</ref>  
 
Prior to entering parliament in 2010, he was a member of the [[Western Province, Sri Lanka|Western]] Provincial Council.<!--  Reference in Sinhala:  http://www.divaina.com/2010/03/28/politics04.html  --><ref>[http://www.dailymirror.lk/61073/new-cabinet-ministers-sworn-in New Cabinet ministers sworn in].</ref> He was appointed as the acting minister of defence few times after 2019.<ref>[http://www.sundaytimes.lk/article/1087743/ruwan-wijewardene-appointed-as-acting-defence-minister Ruwan Wijewardene appointed as Acting Defence Minister]</ref><ref>[http://www.sundaytimes.lk/article/1094480/ruwan-appointed-as-acting-defence-minister-as-president-leaves-for-britain Ruwan appointed as Acting Defence Minister as President leaves for Britain]</ref>

==Early life==
Born to a political dynasty, he is the youngest son of Ranjani Senanayake and Ranjith Wijewardene (Chairman of [[Wijeya Newspapers]]). His maternal great grandfather, Rt Hon [[DS Senanayake|D.S Senanayake]], was the first prime minister of Ceylon and his grand uncle, [[Dudley Senanayake]],  was the second prime minister of Ceylon and went on to become prime minister two more times during the 1950s and 1960s. His paternal grandfather, [[D. R. Wijewardena]], was a [[press baron]] who was a leader in the [[Sri Lankan independence movement]]. A successful entrepreneur, he established [[Associated Newspapers of Ceylon Limited|Lake House]] newspapers and played a major role in the independence movement. Ruwan Wijewardene is a nephew of the first executive president  [[J.R. Jayewardene]]  of Sri Lanka and a cousin of [[Ranil Wickremesinghe]], the former Prime Minister of Sri Lanka.<!--  Reference in Sinhala:  http://www.divaina.com/2010/03/28/politics04.html  --><ref>[http://www.nation.lk/2009/03/22/inter2.htm Ruwan Wijewardene wants to revive UNP].</ref><ref>[http://www.nation.lk/2010/03/07/inter4.htm “I’m more of a liberal democrat like Dudley”].</ref>

==Education==
Wijewardene completed his primary and secondary education at  [[St. Thomas' Preparatory School]]. He then pursued further studies at the [[University of Sussex]]  in the United Kingdom, where he earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]]. <!--  Reference in Sinhala:  http://www.divaina.com/2010/03/28/politics04.html  -->

==Political career==
Wijewardene contested the [[Sri Lanka Provincial Council elections, 2008–2009#Western Provincial Council election|Western Provincial elections in 2009]] and was a member of the Western Provincial Council until 2010. He stood for [[2010 Sri Lankan parliamentary election|parliamentary elections 2010]] and secured the highest number of preference votes from [[Gampaha District]]. He lost his seat in parliament in [[2020 Sri Lankan parliamentary election|2020 general elections]].
