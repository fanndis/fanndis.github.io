'''The Vamps''' are a British [[pop rock]] band consisting of Bradley Simpson, James McVey, Connor Ball and Tristan Evans.<ref name="new music">{{cite news|title=The Vamps - New Music|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/new-music/10284021/The-Vamps-New-Music.html|last=Vincent|first=Alice|date=4 September 2013|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|publisher=[[Telegraph Media Group]]|access-date=4 September 2013}}</ref> They formed in 2012 and signed to [[Mercury Records]] (now [[Virgin EMI Records]]) in the November of that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thevampsfansite.wordpress.com/2012/11/28/big-announcement/|title=The Vamps Fansite - Big Announcement|date=28 November 2012|publisher=The Vamps Fansite|access-date=10 February 2014}}</ref>

In 2017, they scored their first number 1 on the [[UK Albums Chart]] with ''[[Night & Day (The Vamps album)|Night & Day]] (Night Edition)''. Their first two albums, ''[[Meet the Vamps|Meet The Vamps]]'' and ''[[Wake Up (The Vamps album)|Wake Up]]'' are both certified [[Music recording certification|Gold]] in the UK. The Vamps have also launched their own record label, Steady Records, and they are working in partnership with EMI/Universal. The first act signed was American band [[The Tide (band)|The Tide]].<ref name="own record label">{{cite news|url=http://www.capitalfm.com/artists/the-vamps/news/record-label/|title=The Vamps Have Launched Their Own Record Label ‚Äì And Already Signed Up A Boyband!}}</ref>

In 2018, guitarist McVey participated in the [[I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (British series 18)|eighteenth series]] of [[I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (British TV series)|''I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!'']] and finished in fifth place. They have continuously toured worldwide since their debut in 2012, and they are the first band to headline London's [[The O2 Arena|O2 Arena]] five years in a row.

==History==

===2011‚Äì2012: Formation===
James McVey was already managed by Richard Rashman and Joe O'Neill of Prestige Management. Deciding he wanted to form a band, McVey subsequently discovered Bradley Simpson in 2011 through [[YouTube]].<ref name="new music"/> Together, the pair wrote songs towards the later months of 2011, with Simpson later becoming the lead singer.<ref name="new music"/> In 2012, Simpson and McVey met Tristan Evans through [[Facebook]]. The trio then met Connor Ball via a mutual friend.<ref name="new music"/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.birminghammail.co.uk/whats-on/music/vamps-we-dont-mind-being-6098829|title='We don't mind being chased by girls!' Sutton Coldfield singer of new boy band The Vamps|last=Dix|first=Samuel|date=26 September 2013|work=[[Birmingham Mail]]|publisher=[[Trinity Mirror]]|access-date=30 January 2014}}</ref> In mid-2012, the band started uploading cover songs to their YouTube channel. By October, they were being described as a new [[boy band]], with special attention given to their YouTube performance of [[One Direction]]'s song "[[Live While We're Young]]".<ref name=BSCKids>{{cite web|url=http://www.bsckids.com/2012/10/meet-the-vamps-uks-new-upcoming-boy-band/ |access-date=13 December 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131016203944/http://www.bsckids.com/2012/10/meet-the-vamps-uks-new-upcoming-boy-band/|title=Meet the Vamps, the UK's New Upcoming Boy Band |archive-date=16 October 2013 }}</ref>

===2013‚Äì2014: ''Meet the Vamps''===

beaten to number one by [[OneRepublic]]'s "[[Counting Stars]]",<ref name="number two">{{cite news|url=http://www.stratford-herald.com/local-news/7182-former-stratford-busker-shoots-to-number-two-in-the-uk-singles-chart.html|title=Former Stratford busker shoots to number two in the UK singles chart|last=Lloyd|first=James|date=7 October 2013|work=Stratford Herald|access-date=4 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202224957/http://www.stratford-herald.com/local-news/7182-former-stratford-busker-shoots-to-number-two-in-the-uk-singles-chart.html|archive-date=2 December 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> which sold 1,250 copies more.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.clickmusic.com/news/article/onerepublic-beat-the-vamps-to-uk-number-one-after-heated-chart-battle|title=OneRepublic beat The Vamps to UK number one after heated chart battle|date=6 October 2013|publisher=clickmusic.com|access-date=30 January 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140102191655/http://www.clickmusic.com/news/article/onerepublic-beat-the-vamps-to-uk-number-one-after-heated-chart-battle|archive-date=2 January 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref>  On 19 November 2013, the band announced that they would release their debut album around [[Easter]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sugarscape.com/main-topics/music/984005/vamps-tease-exciting-debut-album-news-%E2%80%98we%E2%80%99re-releasing-it-around-easter%E2%80%99|title=The Vamps tease exciting debut album news: 'We're releasing it around Easter‚Äô|last=Wood|first=Lucy|date=19 November 2013|publisher=[[sugarscape.com]]|access-date=5 February 2014}}</ref> On 22 November 2013, the Vamps announced that their second single would be "Wild Heart".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.entertainmentwise.com/news/133454/Watch-Out-One-Direction-The-Vamps-Announce-Wild-Heart-As-Second-Single|title=Watch Out One Direction! On 13 March 2014, the Vamps announced that their debut album would be released on 14 April 2014. On 22 March, it was revealed that the album would be titled ''[[Meet the Vamps]]''.<ref name="album release date">{{cite web|url=http://primetime.unrealitytv.co.uk/vamps-talk-debut-album-finally-confirm-release-date/|title=The Vamps talk about their debut album and FINALLY confirm its release date|last=Lamont|first=Martin|date=14 March 2014|publisher=Unreality TV|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140314154858/http://primetime.unrealitytv.co.uk/vamps-talk-debut-album-finally-confirm-release-date/|archive-date=14 March 2014|url-status=dead|access-date=15 March 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref> On 6 April, they released "[[Last Night (The Vamps song)|Last Night]]" as the third single from the album, debuting at number two in the UK. On 17 April, ''Meet the Vamps'' debuted at number two on the [[Irish Albums Chart]] and the [[UK Albums Chart]], kept off the top spot by [[Paolo Nutini]]'s ''[[Caustic Love]]''.

In May 2014, it was announced that the Vamps would be making a cameo appearance in the soap opera ''[[Hollyoaks]]'' on 14 May 2014.<ref name="Hollyoaks1">{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/soaps/s13/hollyoaks/news/a568802/the-vamps-to-make-hollyoaks-cameo.html|title=The Vamps to make Hollyoaks cameo|last=Blay|first=Zeba|date=7 May 2014|work=[[Digital Spy]]|publisher=[[Hearst Magazines UK]]|access-date=10 June 2014}}</ref> ''Hollyoaks'' released a teaser clip about the Vamps' appearance on [[YouTube]]. On 18 May 2014, a new version of "[[Somebody to You]]", featuring American singer [[Demi Lovato]], was released as the fourth single from ''Meet the Vamps'' as well as their debut single in the United States followed by an [[Somebody to You (EP)|EP of the same title]] in August 2014. The collaboration also served as the lead single from the US version of the Vamps' debut album. Its accompanying music video has since accumulated over 108 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0go2nfVXFgA|title=The Vamps - Somebody To You ft. On 12 October 2014, a new version of "[[Oh Cecilia (Breaking My Heart)]]", featuring Canadian singer [[Shawn Mendes]], was released as the fifth and last single from their album. It debuted and peaked at number nine on the UK Singles Chart, becoming their fifth consecutive top 10 single. It was released with the ''b-side'' track "Hurricane", from ''[[Alexander and the Terrible, Horrible, No Good, Very Bad Day]]'' soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itun.es/gb/2cpF2 |title=iTunes - Music - Oh Cecilia (Breaking My Heart) - Single by The Vamps |publisher = [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] [[iTunes Store|iTunes]] UK}}</ref> The music videos for both tracks were released one month earlier.
 
On 4 November 2014, ''Meet the Vamps'' was officially released in North America.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/meet-the-vamps-deluxe/id928655704|title=Meet The Vamps (Deluxe) by The Vamps|publisher=[[iTunes]]|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref> On 1 December 2014, the album was re-released as ''Meet the Vamps (Christmas Edition)'', including eight Christmas bonus tracks and the single version of "Somebody to You", being available worldwide apart from the United States and Mexico.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/meet-the-vamps-christmas-edition/id925655725|title=Meet The Vamps (Christmas Edition) by The Vamps|publisher=iTunes|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref>

===2015-2016: ''Steady Records'' and ''Wake Up''===
In February 2015, The Vamps announced that they had launched their own record label along with [[Universal Music]] and [[EMI Records]], having signed the American pop rock band [[The Tide (band)|The Tide]] as their first act, but the label didn't have a name yet.<ref name="The Vamps Have Launched Their Own Record Label ‚Äì And Already Signed Up A Boyband!">{{cite web|url=http://www.capitalfm.com/artists/the-vamps/news/record-label/|title=The Vamps Have Launched Their Own Record Label ‚Äì And Already Signed Up A Boyband!|publisher=Capital FM|access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> Only in October it was revealed it would be called [[Steady Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-vamps-interview-this-album-is-completely-different-__10965/|title=The Vamps interview: "This album is completely different"|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=21 November 2015}}</ref>
 
On 15 September 2015, the band announced their new single "[[Wake Up (The Vamps song)|Wake Up]]"<ref name="Wake Up album and single">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-vamps-announce-new-single-and-album-wake-up__10741/|title=The Vamps announce new single and album Wake Up|date=15 September 2015|publisher=Official Charts|access-date=17 September 2015}}</ref> which was released on 2 October 2015. Their second studio album, "[[Wake Up (The Vamps album)|Wake Up]]",<ref name="Wake Up album and single"/> was released on 27 November 2015. They performed at fanfests in Europe <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thevamps.net/fanfests/|title=Fanfests - The Vamps|date=15 September 2015|publisher=The Vamps Website|access-date=17 September 2015}}</ref> and embarked on a world tour called ''Wake Up World Tour''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thevamps.net/wake-up-world-tour/|title=Wake Up World Tour|date=15 September 2015|publisher=The Vamps Website|access-date=17 September 2015}}</ref> which started in January 2016 to promote the album. The song debuted and peaked at number 12 on the UK Singles Chart and the album debuted at number 10 on the UK Albums Chart, selling over 27 thousand copies in the country in its first week. The second single off the album, "Rest Your Love", was released on the same day as the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://getmusicasia.com/2015/11/25/the-vamps-announces-new-single-rest-your-love-out-this-friday/#2VOEjjzYOhG1VFsX.97|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151204054951/http://getmusicasia.com/2015/11/25/the-vamps-announces-new-single-rest-your-love-out-this-friday/#2VOEjjzYOhG1VFsX.97|url-status=dead|archive-date=4 December 2015|title=The Vamps announces new single 'Rest Your Love' out this friday|publisher=Get Music Asia|access-date=29 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/TheVampsCon/status/667394861511876610|title=We can now announce that REST YOUR LOVE is our next single and video will be out the same day as the albumüë®üë®üë®|publisher=Twitter|access-date=19 March 2016}}</ref>
 
In December 2015, The Vamps announced that they had signed the British band [[New Hope Club]] and that they would be one of the opening acts in the UK leg of their ''Wake Up World Tour''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/music/news/a776609/the-vamps-have-signed-new-band-new-hope-club-to-their-record-label/|title=The Vamps have signed new band 'New Hope Club' to their record label|access-date=30 December 2015}}</ref>

In January 2016, The Vamps released their version of "[[Kung Fu Fighting]]" for the animated film ''[[Kung Fu Panda 3]]'', which was accompanied by a music video. On 1 April 2016, a new version of "[[I Found a Girl (The Vamps song)|I Found a Girl]]", featuring Jamaican rapper [[Omi (singer)|Omi]], was released as the third and last single off their second album. The song debuted at number 30 on the UK Singles Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/i-found-a-girl-feat.-omi-single/id1092165456|title=I Found A Girl (feat. ===2016‚Äìpresent: Autobiography, "All Night", ''Night & Day'' and ''Cherry Blossom''===
On 7 October 2016, The Vamps announced in a livestream via [[Facebook]] that the first single of their third studio album, both with no names revealed at the time, would be released on 14 October 2016, with an exclusive release on [[Capital (radio network)|Capital FM]] on the previous day.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.capitalfm.com/charity/auction/the-vamps-new-single-listening-party/|title=Meet The VampsVamps and hear the BRAND NEW single first|publisher=[[Capital (radio network)|Capital FM]]|access-date=9 October 2016}}</ref> On the same day, hints of the name and lyrics of the single started being posted on the band's social medias and [[Spotify]], following the same style as the hits for "Wake Up" single and album in September 2015. It was also announced that the album would be out in Summer 2017. On 12 October 2016, the band announced via livestream that the single would be called "[[All Night (The Vamps and Matoma song)|All Night]]" and would be a collaboration with Norwegian DJ [[Matoma]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-vamps-announce-their-new-single-is-a-collaboration-with-matoma__16621/|title=The Vamps announce their new single is a collaboration with Matoma|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref> The song peaked at number 24 on the UK Singles Chart, number six on the [[Irish Singles Chart]], number 13 on the Norwegian Singles Chart, number 12 on the New Zealand Singles Chart, and also became their first entry on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] chart, peaking at number 72. It became the most charted and certified single of the band and their most streamed song on Spotify. On 20 October 2016, The Vamps released their autobiography ''The Vamps: Our Story 100% Official'', which had been announced months earlier.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Vamps-Our-Story-100-Official/dp/147224043X|title=The Vamps: Our Story: 100% Official|publisher=[[Amazon.com|Amazon]]|access-date=7 November 2016}}</ref>

"[[Middle of the Night (song)|Middle of the Night]]", a collaboration with Danish DJ [[Martin Jensen (DJ)|Martin Jensen]], was announced as the second single of the album on 21 April 2017 and was released on 28 April 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebmix.com/the-vamps-announce-middle-of-the-night/|title=The Vamps announce Middle Of The Night|publisher=CelebMix|access-date=23 April 2017}}</ref> the same day their Middle of the Night Tour started. During the first show of the tour, it was announced that the name of their third album is ''[[Night & Day (The Vamps album)|Night & Day]]'' and will be released in two parts. The first part, ''Night Edition'', was set to be released in July 2017<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/TheVampsCon/status/858037055313637381?p=v|title=3rd album, "Night and Day" out 14th July|publisher=Twitter|access-date=29 April 2017}}</ref> and the second one, ''Day Edition'', was set to be released in July 2018. On 3 May 2017, a video explaining more about the album was uploaded on their social media.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/TheVampsband/status/859781690830581762|title=Night & Day ‚Äì Pre Order from Friday|publisher=Twitter|access-date=3 May 2017}}</ref> On 19 May 2017, Swedish DJ [[Mike Perry (DJ)|Mike Perry]] released the single "[[Hands (Mike Perry, Sabrina Carpenter and The Vamps song)|Hands]]", a collaboration between himself, The Vamps, and American singer [[Sabrina Carpenter]], which was included in the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/hands-single/id1235577509|title=Hands ‚Äì Single|publisher=[[Apple Inc.|iTunes]]|access-date=19 May 2017}}</ref> The first part of the album, ''Night Edition'', was released on 14 July 2017 and debuted atop the UK Albums Chart, becoming the band's first number one album.

On 20 April 2018, the band released the singles "[[Too Good to Be True (Danny Avila and The Vamps song)|Too Good to Be True]]", "[[Personal (The Vamps song)|Personal]]",<ref>{{cite web|last=Wass|first=Mike|title=The Vamps Recruit Maggie Lindemann For Catchy New Single "Personal"|url=http://www.idolator.com/7669844/vamps-personal-maggie-lindemann-review?view-all|website=idolator|access-date=17 October 2017|date=13 October 2017}}</ref> "Hair Too Long", and "Just My Type" from their album's ''Day Edition''. On 13 July 2018, they released the ''Day Edition'' album which debuted at number one on the Official Scottish Albums Chart and number two on the UK Albums Chart.

On 27 July 2018, they released the single "[[We Don't Care (Sigala song)|We Don't Care]]", a collaboration with English DJ [[Sigala]]. In March 2019, "All the Lies", a collaboration with Brazilian DJ Alok and German producer Felix Jaehn, was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8502712/alok-felix-jaehn-the-vamps-all-the-lies-listen|title=Alok, Felix Jaehn and The Vamps Deliver Breezy Collab 'All The Lies': Listen|publisher=Billboard|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref>

The ''Missing You'' EP was released on 25th April 2019. The four-track EP consists of title track "Missing You", "Right Now" (a collaboration with [[Krept and Konan]]), "Waves" and an acoustic version of "All the Lies". The EP marked the band taking charge of their music and creative direction as they co-wrote and co-produced the entire body of work.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Essentially Pop |title=The Vamps Release 'Missing You' EP |url=https://essentiallypop.com/epop/2019/04/the-vamps-release-missing-you-ep/ |website=Essentially Pop |access-date=26 February 2020}}</ref> On 30 July 2020, they announced their fifth studio album ''[[Cherry Blossom (album)|Cherry Blossom]]''. The lead single "[[Married in Vegas (The Vamps song)|Married in Vegas]]" was released on the same day as the album pre-order. The album was released on 16 October 2020 <ref>https://twitter.com/thevampsband/status/1288820112334692352?s=21</ref> and debuted and number one on the UK charts. The album later set the record for the largest drop from number one in UK history in its second week, dropping 71 places to 72 in the album charts. <ref>https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/cherry-blossom/</ref>

==Members==
