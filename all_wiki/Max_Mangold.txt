'''Max Mangold''' ({{IPA-de|ˈmaks ˈmaŋɡɔlt}};<ref>{{cite book|author1=Dudenredaktion|last2=Kleiner|first2=Stefan|last3=Knöbl|first3=Ralf|year=2015|orig-year=First published 1962|title=Das Aussprachewörterbuch|trans-title=The Pronunciation Dictionary|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=T6vWCgAAQBAJ|language=German|edition=7th|location=Berlin|publisher=Dudenverlag|isbn=978-3-411-04067-4|pages=578, 588}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Krech|first1=Eva-Maria|last2=Stock|first2=Eberhard|last3=Hirschfeld|first3=Ursula|last4=Anders|first4=Lutz Christian|title=Deutsches Aussprachewörterbuch|trans-title=German Pronunciation Dictionary|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=E-1tr_oVkW4C&q=deutsches+ausspracheworterbuch|language=German|year=2009|publisher=Walter de Gruyter|location=Berlin|isbn=978-3-11-018202-6|pages=722, 732}}</ref> 8 May 1922 – 3 February 2015) was a [[Swiss German people|Swiss-German]] linguist and phonetician. He was born in the village of [[Pratteln]] near [[Basel]], [[Switzerland]] and taught [[phonetics]], [[phonology]] and [[theoretical linguistics|linguistic theory]] at the [[University of the Saarland]] in Germany.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://saarbruecker-zeitung.trauer.de/Traueranzeige/Max-Mangold|title=Traueranzeige: Prof. Dr. Max Mangold|date= 21 February 2015|publisher=[[Saarbrücker Zeitung]]|accessdate=27 May 2015}}</ref> He produced [[phonetic notation]] for numerous [[reference works]] and [[dictionary|pronunciation dictionaries]],<ref>{{Citation

He studied in [[Basel]], [[Geneva]], [[Paris]] and [[London]]. His work afterward included service as an [[interpreter]] for the [[United Nations]] from 1953–1954 during the [[Korean War]] for French, German, English, Russian, Polish, Czech, Slovak, Swedish and Chinese.<ref>{{Citation
|author=Dogil, Grzegorz
|year=2009

|pages=357
|publisher=Mouton de Gruyter 
|isbn=978-3110205183
}}</ref> After receiving his doctorate under [[Walther von Wartburg]] and [[habilitation]] in Basel in 1956,<ref>[http://idw-online.de/de/news475668 "Prof. Dr. Max Mangold 90 Jahre", Saarland University press release]</ref> he was appointed a full professor of phonetics at the University of the Saarland in Germany in 1957 after lecturing at universities in Basel, [[Zurich]] and [[Bonn]].
