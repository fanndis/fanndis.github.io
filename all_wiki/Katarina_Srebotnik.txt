'''Katarina Srebotnik''' (born March 12, 1981) is a professional tennis player from Slovenia. She reached her career-high singles ranking of world No. 20 on 7 August 2006. On 4 July 2011, she reached No. 1 of the WTA doubles rankings.

Srebotnik won four singles titles on the [[WTA Tour]] and was a steady top-30 player for several years. However, she had her best results on the doubles circuit, winning 39 titles, including one Grand Slam title ([[2011 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Doubles|Wimbledon 2011]]) as well as five Grand Slam titles in mixed doubles.

==Career==
[[File:Srebotnik RG13 (2) (9383642510).jpg|thumb|Srebotnik, 2013]]
As junior, she won the [[1998 Wimbledon]] singles title and was singles runner-up at the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]]. Srebotnik attained the second spot on the junior rankings in 1997 and 1998. She was mentored by [[Gabriela Sabatini]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://canchallena.lanacion.com.ar/1630170-gaby-sabatiniahora-disfruto-de-todo-lo-que-no-hice-cuando-jugaba|title=Gaby Sabatini."Ahora disfruto de todo lo que no hice cuando jugaba"|publisher=|accessdate=13 May 2015}}</ref>

===Tour debut (1995–1999)===
Srebotnik made her ITF debut in 1995.
She won the ITF singles tournament in [[Ismailia]] (1996), [[Zadar]] (1997); [[Šibenik]] (1998) and in [[Dubai]] (1999). In 1998, Srebotnik won her first Tour doubles title at the Makarska Open (with [[Tina Križan]]), and later that year was a doubles finalist at Maria Lankowitz (also with Križan).

In 1999, her win at the ITF tournament in Dubai, gave her direct entry into her first singles Tour event at Estoril, where she became the fourth player to win her Tour debut event by defeating [[Rita Kuti-Kis]] in the final. She broke into the top 100 on April 12, 1999 at No. 88.

Srebotnik reached the semifinals at Palermo, and played in her first Grand Slam main draw at [[1999 French Open|Roland Garros]]. She won the mixed-doubles tournament with [[Piet Norval]].

===2000–2004===
Srebotnik reached her first Tier I semifinal in Tokyo at the [[Toray Pan Pacific Open|Pan Pacific]], which she lost to [[Sandrine Testud]]. On 7 February 2000, Srebotnik broke into the top 50 at No. 49. She won her fourth career doubles title at [[Portugal Open|Estoril]] (with Tina Križan).

Srebotnik and Križan won their only doubles title of 2001 at Hawaii. They reached their biggest doubles final of their career in Toronto at the [[Canadian Open (tennis)|Canadian Open]] by defeating [[Martina Navratilova]] and [[Arantxa Sánchez Vicario]] in the semifinals, and they were doubles finalists at Estoril. They qualified for their debut doubles season-ending championships. Srebotnik reached a career-high doubles of No. 19 on 8 October.

In 2002, Srebotnik reached the finals at [[Copa Colsanitas|Bogotá]] (losing to [[Fabiola Zuluaga]]) and [[Abierto Mexicano TELCEL|Acapulco]] (defeating [[Paola Suárez]]) in the final. She reached the fourth round at [[2002 French Open|Roland Garros]], which is her career-best Grand Slam. Srebotnik later achieved her then-best win at Los Angeles by defeating No. 6 [[Kim Clijsters]]. She reached the semifinals in [[BGL Luxembourg Open|Luxembourg]]. She achieved her second appearance at the doubles season-ending championships with Krizan.

2003 saw Srebotnik reaching her fourth tour final at Palermo. She won the Bogotá doubles title with [[Asa Svensson]], and reached her second Tier I quarterfinals in Toronto at the Canadian Open. She won her second Grand Slam mixed-doubles title at the [[2003 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], this time with [[Bob Bryan]].

Her 2004 season was highlighted by reaching the semifinals at Palermo and the quarterfinals at [[Internationaux de Strasbourg|Strasbourg]] and [[2004 Forest Hills Tennis Classic|Forest Hills]].

Srebotnik won her seventh doubles title in Tokyo at the [[Japan Open Tennis Championships|Japan Open]] (with [[Shinobu Asagoe]]).

===2005===
Her best season to date was highlighted by two singles and four doubles titles as well as a career-best victory over [[Amélie Mauresmo]].

Srebotnik captured her third and fourth career WTA Tour singles titles at [[WTA Auckland Open|Auckland]] (defeating Shinobu Asagoe in the final, and she teamed with Asagoe for the doubles title) and in [[Nordea Nordic Light Open|Stockholm]] (defeating world No. 14 [[Anastasia Myskina]] in the final and teaming with [[Émilie Loit]] for the doubles title).

She was the only player in 2005 to sweep singles and doubles titles twice. She also finished runner-up at [[Banka Koper Slovenia Open|Portorož]], losing to [[Klára Zakopalová]] (now Koukalová) in three sets in the final. She also became runner-up in doubles with [[Jelena Kostanic]].

Srebotnik reached the quarterfinals five times: at Tier II at [[Proximus Diamond Games|Antwerp]] (lost to [[Anastasia Myskina]]), Tier I [[Family Circle Cup|Charleston]] (losing to [[Elena Dementieva]] in three sets), [[Budapest Grand Prix|Budapest]] (losing to [[Laura Pous Tió]] in a third set tie-break), Tier I [[Zurich Open|Zurich]] (losing to Ana Ivanovic), and Hasselt (losing to [[Dinara Safina]] in a third set tie-break).

Her best finish in a major was a third-round loss at [[2005 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] to [[Maria Sharapova]].

A new career-high singles ranking of No. 28 came on 7 November.

In addition to Auckland and Stockholm, Srebotnik won doubles titles at Budapest and Hasselt (both with Émilie Loit). She reached the [[2005 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] mixed-doubles final (with [[Nenad Zimonjić]], losing to [[Daniela Hantuchová]] and [[Mahesh Bhupathi]]).

===2006===
Srebotnik opened the 2006 season with an early exit at the [[WTA Auckland Open|Auckland Open]]. Two weeks later at the [[2006 Australian Open|Australian Open]], with partner [[Shinobu Asagoe]], she made it to the semifinals in doubles, losing to [[Yan Zi (tennis)|Yan Zi]] and [[Zheng Jie]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/Draws/2006/901.pdf | title=2006 Australian Open Doubles draw | accessdate=15 September 2016 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121005192928/http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/Draws/2006/901.pdf | archivedate=5 October 2012 }}</ref> She won doubles titles in [[Diamond Games|Antwerp]]<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/Draws/2006/1016.pdf | title=2006 Diamond Games draw sheet | accessdate=15 September 2016 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150225024531/http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/Draws/2006/1016.pdf | archivedate=25 February 2015 }}</ref> (with Dinara Safina) and [[Amelia Island Championships|Amelia Island]] (with Shinobu Asagoe).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/Draws/2006/803.pdf |title=2006 Bausch & Lomb Championships |accessdate=15 September 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131021154729/http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/Draws/2006/803.pdf |archivedate=21 October 2013 }}</ref> At the French Open, she won the [[2006 French Open – Mixed Doubles|mixed doubles]] championship with [[Nenad Zimonjić]].

At the [[2006 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], she reached the doubles final partnering [[Dinara Safina]]. In [[Women's Stuttgart Open|Stuttgart]], she reached the semifinals in doubles with Dinara Safina. At the [[Zurich Open]], Srebotnik reached the semifinals of a [[WTA Tier I|Tier I]] tournament for the first time in six years ([[Pan Pacific Open]], Japan). Also, Srebotnik and [[Liezel Huber]] reached the doubles final. In her final event of the season at the [[Linz Open]], Srebotnikreached the doubles final with [[Corina Morariu]].

===2008===
At the [[2008 French Open|French Open]], Srebotnik caused an upset when she defeated Serena Williams, whom she had never beaten in four previous attempts, in the third round.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2008-05-30/serena-dumped-at-roland-garros/2454692|title=Serena dumped at Roland Garros|work=ABC News|accessdate=13 May 2015}}</ref> At the [[2008 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] in the same year, she upset former champion Svetlana Kuznetsova in the third round.<ref>[http://www.foxnews.com/story/2008/08/30/kuznetsova-upset-by-srebotnik-at-us-open/ Kuznetsova Upset by Srebotnik at U.S. Open], Fox News</ref> On both occasions, she lost to [[Patty Schnyder]] in the next round.

===2010===
In that year, Srebotnik teamed with [[Květa Peschke]], and won the WTA tournaments of [[2010 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]] (defeating Nadia Petrova and Sam Stosur in the finals) and [[New Haven]] (defeating [[Bethanie Mattek-Sands]] and [[Meghann Shaughnessy]]), and reached the final of the [[WTA Championships]] in Doha.

Srebotnik had an excellent doubles outing at the [[2010 French Open|French Open]]. In the ladies' doubles, she and Peschke defeated the second seeds [[Nuria Llagostera Vives]] and [[María José Martínez Sánchez]] in the semifinals, but lost to Serena and Venus Williams in the [[2010 French Open – Women's Doubles|final]]. She also partnered with Nenad Zimonjić to win the [[2010 French Open – Mixed Doubles|mixed doubles]] title with a thrilling tiebreak win against [[Yaroslava Shvedova]] and [[Julian Knowle]].

Srebotnik and Peschke reached the [[Rogers Cup (tennis)|Rogers Cup]] doubles final. It was the second time this year that Srebotnik and Peschke reached a final of a Premier-5 tournament after [[Barclays Dubai Tennis Championships|Dubai]] in February.

At the end of the 2010 season, Srebotnik announced that she would focus on doubles for the remainder of her career.

* In both singles ([[1999 Estoril Open – Women's Singles|Estoril, 1999]]) and doubles ([[1998 Makarska International Championships – Doubles|Makarska, 1998]]), Srebotnik won the first WTA Tour events she entered, the second player to do so after [[Mirjana Lučić-Baroni|Mirjana Lučić]]. Srebotnik is the only player to also win the first mixed doubles event she entered ([[1999 French Open – Mixed Doubles|Roland Garros, 1999]]).
* In [[2011 WTA Tour|2011]], Srebotnik won seven titles (six in doubles, one in mixed doubles), more than any other player on the WTA Tour.
