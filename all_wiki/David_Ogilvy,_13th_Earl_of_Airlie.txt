'''David George Coke Patrick Ogilvy, 8th (or 13th) Earl of Airlie''', {{postnominals|country=GBR|size=100|sep=,|KT|GCVO|PC|JP}}<ref name="chanthis">{{London Gazette|date=13 November 2007|issue=58510|page=16433}}</ref> (born 17 May 1926)<ref name=Birth>{{cite web|title=Birthday's today|url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/archive/2013-5-17.html |work=The Telegraph|accessdate=15 May 2014|date=17 May 2013|quote=the Earl of Airlie, Lord–Lieutenant for Angus, 1989–2001; Lord Chamberlain, 1984–97, 87}}</ref> is a Scottish peer.

==Background and education==
Airlie is the eldest son of [[David Ogilvy, 12th Earl of Airlie]] and Lady Alexandra Coke. His younger brother was [[Sir Angus Ogilvy]], the husband of [[Princess Alexandra of Kent]]. He served as a page to his father at the [[coronation of King George VI and Queen Elizabeth]] in [[Westminster Abbey]] on 12 May 1937. With [[Queen Elizabeth II]], {{as of|2018|09|lc=on}}, he is one of the last surviving participants of the 1937 coronation.

Born in [[Westminster]], David Ogilvy was educated at [[Eton College|Eton]] and served in the [[Scots Guards]] during the [[Second World War]]. In 1946, he was appointed ADC to the C-in-C and High Commissioner to Austria. He remained in the army until 1950, when he left to attend the [[Royal Agricultural College]] in [[Cirencester]], to learn more about estate management. He currently maintains two homes on the family's {{convert|69000|acres|km2}} estate in [[Angus, Scotland|Angus]]: [[Cortachy Castle]] and [[Airlie Castle]]. He also has a home in [[Chelsea, London|Chelsea]], London.

==Merchant banker==
He subsequently took up merchant banking, joining [[Schroders|J. Henry Schroder]] in 1953. He was appointed a director of the company in 1961 and chairman of Henry Schroder Wagg & Co in 1973, and then of Schroders plc from 1977.<ref name="debretts">{{cite web|title=The Rt Hon the Earl of Airlie, KT, GCVO, PC|url=http://www.debretts.com/people/biographies/browse/a/6993/David%20George%20Coke%20Patrick%20Ogilvy+AIRLIE.aspx|publisher=debretts.com|accessdate=2 June 2012}}</ref> In 1984, Lord Airlie resigned from Schroder to take up the position of [[Lord Chamberlain]],<ref>{{cite news|last=Tomlinson|first=Richard|title=They also serve, who only ush|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/they-also-serve-who-only-ush-why-is-the-queen-followed-by-people-in-antique-clothes-richard-tomlinson-on-the-lords-ladies-women-masters-silver-sticks-and-white-staves-at-court-1564751.html|newspaper=Independent|date=20 December 1992}}</ref> when he was also sworn of the [[Privy Council of the United Kingdom|Privy Council]] and appointed [[Royal Victorian Order|GCVO]]. 

Lord Airlie as Lord Chamberlain initiated changes in the early 1990s under the auspices of “The Way Ahead Group”. Under these plans The Queen agreed to pay tax, greater transparency for the public subsidy of the monarchy began and a greater emphasis on public relations started.<ref>[https://www.1843magazine.com/features/can-meghan-markle-modernise-the-monarchy]</ref>

He was made a [[Knight of the Thistle]] in 1985.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=21848|date=29 November 1985|page=1822}}</ref><ref name="debretts"/> Two years after that he became Chairman of General Accident Fire and Life Assurance plc.

He followed in the footsteps of his late father, who served as Lord Chamberlain to [[Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother]]. He remained in the post until 1997.

Lord Airlie has also served as the [[Lord Lieutenant of Angus]] in Scotland,<ref>{{London Gazette|date=6 October 1964|issue=43455|page=8411}}</ref> and as the Captain General of The [[Royal Company of Archers]] and [[Gold Stick]] for Scotland. He was also the founding Chancellor of the University of Abertay, Dundee (1994–2009).<ref>[http://www.abertay.ac.uk/about/theuni/chancellor University of Abertay, Dundee website] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151107051626/http://www.abertay.ac.uk/about/theuni/chancellor/ |date=7 November 2015 }}, abertay.ac.uk. Retrieved 7 April 2016.</ref> In 1998, he was asked by the Labour government to stay on as a Lord in Waiting for life.

Lord Airlie's ancestral home in Tayside is Cortachy Castle near the ancient burgh of [[Kirriemuir, Angus]]. On 13 November 2007 he was asked to be Chancellor of the Order of the Thistle. His wife, the Countess of Airlie, is a [[Lady of the Bedchamber]] to Queen Elizabeth II.<ref>{{London Gazette|date=2 January 1973|issue=45868|page=105}}</ref> His grandmother, [[Mabell, Countess of Airlie]], was a [[Lady-in-Waiting]] and subsequently [[Lady of the Bedchamber]] to [[Mary of Teck|Queen Mary]].

==Family==
On 23 October 1952, he married [[Virginia Ogilvy, Countess of Airlie|Virginia Ryan]]. The wedding took place at [[St Margaret's, Westminster]], in the presence of Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother and [[Princess Margaret]].<ref>{{cite web|title=London Pageantry AKA Queen Mother Attends… 1952|url=http://www.britishpathe.com/video/london-pageantry-aka-queen-mother-attends-ogilvy-r|publisher=British Pathe}}</ref>

They have six children:
* Lady Doune Mabell Ogilvy (b. 13 August 1953); married Sir Hereward Charles Wake, 15th Bt. on 16 April 1977; they had four children, and divorced in July 1995.
* Lady Jane Fortune Margaret Ogilvy (b. 24 June 1955); married François Nairac on 30 August 1980. The couple have two daughters.
* David John Ogilvy, Lord Ogilvy (b. 9 March 1958); married, firstly, in 1981, Hon. Geraldine Harmsworth, daughter of [[Vere Harmsworth, 3rd Viscount Rothermere]]. They had a daughter, Augusta (b. 1981), before divorcing in 1990. Lord Ogilvy married, secondly, in 1991, Tarka Kings, with whom he has three sons; David Huxley, Master of Ogilvy (b. 1991), Joseph (b. 1995) and Michael (b. 1997). 
* Hon. Bruce Patrick Mark Ogilvy (b. 7 April 1959)
* Lady Elizabeth Clementine Ogilvy (b. 4 June 1965); married Jonathan Baring, with whom she has one son.
* Hon. Patrick Alexander Ogilvy (b. 24 March 1971)

The numbering of the titles varies, depending on whether the [[attainder|attainted]] holders of the earldom and their successors are counted or not. He is a recipient of the [[Royal Victorian Chain]].<ref>{{London Gazette|date=19 December 1997|issue=54984|page=14236}}</ref> In November 2007, [[Buckingham Palace]] announced Lord Airlie was appointed [[Chancellor of the Order of the Thistle]], following the death of the previous Chancellor, the [[John Scott, 9th Duke of Buccleuch|Duke of Buccleuch]].<ref name="chanthis" /> Late in 2009, His Lordship announced that he and his wife were retiring. His son has considered the future of Cortachy Castle, the family home for more than 500 years.
