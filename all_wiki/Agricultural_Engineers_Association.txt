'''Agricultural Engineers Association''' (AEA) is a [[trade association]] in the [[United Kingdom]] which represents manufacturers and importers of agricultural machinery and outdoor equipment to Government, Parliament, and the media.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nationalrural.org/organisation.aspx?id=d8ed1b26-e09a-44ba-b73b-821f04112dd2 |title=AEA (Agricultural Engineers Association) |publisher=National Rural |date= |accessdate=2012-12-28 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130415095240/http://www.nationalrural.org/organisation.aspx?id=d8ed1b26-e09a-44ba-b73b-821f04112dd2 |archive-date=2013-04-15 |url-status=dead |df= }}</ref>

== History ==

The AEA was established in 1875 to promote the commercial, technical and trade interests of British manufacturers and suppliers of agricultural machinery and celebrated its 125th anniversary in London on 18 April 2000.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bankofengland.co.uk/publications/Documents/speeches/2000/speech83.pdf |title=Speech by John Vickers |publisher=[[Bank of England]] |date=April 18, 2000 |accessdate=2012-12-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120803162948/http://www.bankofengland.co.uk/publications/Documents/speeches/2000/speech83.pdf |archive-date=August 3, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> It was incorporated in 1955 as private company limited by guarantee without share capital.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.duedil.com/company/00544745/the-agricultural-engineers-association |title=The Agricultural Engineers Association |publisher=[[Duedil]] |date= |accessdate=2012-12-28}}</ref> It now also champions the cause of manufacturers of outdoor power equipment and its current members cover a broad spectrum of manufacturers of land based equipment.

The AEA issues trade statistics and commentary for the national media<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/motoring/news/2727032/Dicing-with-death.html |title=Dicing with death |publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |date=February 24, 2004 |accessdate=2012-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/may/26/ruralaffairs.food?INTCMP=SRCH |title=Land prices offer farmers a rich harvest |publisher=[[The Guardian]] |date=May 26, 2008 |accessdate=2012-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Aslet |first1=Clive |year=2008 |title=Busy LAMMA |journal=[[The Times]]|volume= |issue= |pages= |publisher=Times Publishing Group |doi= |url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/Migration/article111638.ece|accessdate=December 28, 2012}}</ref> and organises large events for the industry such as [[Tillage Live]], an annual UK cultivations event focusing on all aspects of [[crop]] establishment,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thescottishfarmer.co.uk/arable/tillage-live-cultivating-new-ideas.14949178 |title=Tillage-Live cultivating new ideas |publisher=The Scottish Farmer |date=August 31, 2011 |accessdate=2012-12-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304071425/http://www.thescottishfarmer.co.uk/arable/tillage-live-cultivating-new-ideas.14949178 |archive-date=2016-03-04 |url-status=dead |df= }}</ref> and [[ScotGrass]], a triennial grassland event.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.farmersguardian.com/home/machinery/machinery-news/crowds-flock-to-scotgrass-2010/31921.article |title=Crowds flock to Scotgrass 2010 |publisher=[[Farmers Guide]] |accessdate=2012-12-28}}</ref>

The AEA became a member of the Engineering and Machinery Alliance, UK, in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eama.info/pr_20.htm |title=Agricultural Engineers Association joins  Engineering and Machinery Alliance |date= March 15, 2012 |publisher=EAMA |accessdate=2012-12-28}}</ref>

== Objectives ==
* To safeguard and promote the interests of manufacturers and wholesale distributors
* To promote, support or oppose legislation or other measures affecting the interests of the industry
* To co-operate and negotiate with Government and other bodies interested or concerned in export and import trade
* To promote, maintain and assist scientific research, invention and technical development
* To promote, conduct, manage and co-operate with Associations, Clubs, Societies and other bodies in promoting, conducting or managing shows, exhibitions and demonstrations
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aea.uk.com/aea-objectives |title=AEA Objectives |publisher=AEA |date= |accessdate=2012-12-28}}</ref>
