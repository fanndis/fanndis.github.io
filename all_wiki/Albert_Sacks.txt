'''Albert Martin Sacks''' (August 15, 1920 – March 22, 1991) was an [[United States|American]] lawyer and former [[Dean of Harvard Law School]].<ref name="NYT obit">{{cite news |first=Alfonso A. |last=Narvaez |title=Albert M. Sacks, 70, Harvard Law Dean and Noted Teacher |date=March 23, 1991 |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1991/03/23/obituaries/albert-m-sacks-70-harvard-law-dean-and-noted-teacher.html }}</ref>

Born in [[New York City]] to Jewish immigrants from [[Russian Empire|Russia]], he attended [[City College of New York]] graduating in 1940.<ref name="Harvard Law obit">{{cite journal |first1=Howard |last1=Raiffa |first2=Erwin N. |last2=Griswold |first3=Clark |last3=Byse |first4=Sheldon |last4=Oliensis |first5=David L. |last5=Shapiro |first6=Norman |last6=Dorsen |first7=Gary |last7=Bellow |first8=Ruth |last8=Bader Ginsburg |first9=Frank |last9=Michelman |first10=David B. |last10=Wilkins |title=In Memoriam: Albert M. Sacks |journal=[[Harvard Law Review]] |volume=105 |issue=1 |year=1991 |pages={{Roman|10}}+1–22 |jstor=1341568 }}</ref> After serving in [[World War II]], he attended [[Harvard Law School]], where he was president of the ''[[Harvard Law Review]]'', and graduated in 1948.<ref name="NYT obit" />

He then served as a [[law clerk]] for judge [[Augustus Noble Hand|Augustus N. Hand]] of the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit]] and from 1949 to 1950 for Justice [[Felix Frankfurter]] of the [[Supreme Court of the United States]].<ref name="NYT obit" /> After working as an associate at [[Covington & Burling]] in [[Washington, D.C.|Washington]] for two years, he joined the faculty of Harvard Law School in 1952 and served for 39 years.<ref name="Harvard Law obit" /> He succeeded [[Derek Bok]] as Dean of Harvard Law School in 1971.<ref>{{cite news|title=Associated Dean to Replace Bok |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/6356119/associated_law_dean_to_replace_bok/ |newspaper=[[The Berkshire Eagle]] |date=January 28, 1971 |page=23 |via = [[Newspapers.com]]|accessdate = August 24, 2016 }} {{Open access}}</ref>

He died [[Massachusetts General Hospital]] in 1991.<ref name="NYT obit" />
