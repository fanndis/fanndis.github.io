The '''Canadian Hot 100''' is a music industry [[record chart]] in [[Canada]] for songs, published weekly by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine. The Canadian Hot 100 was launched on the issue dated March 31, 2007, and is currently the standard record chart in Canada; a new chart is compiled and officially released to the public by ''Billboard'' every Tuesday.

The chart is similar to ''Billboard''{{'}}s US-based [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] in that it combines [[CD single|physical]] and [[Music download|digital]] sales as measured by [[Nielsen SoundScan]], [[Streaming media|streaming]] activity data provided by online music sources, and [[Airplay|radio airplay]] as measured by [[Nielsen Broadcast Data Systems|Nielsen BDS]]. Canada's radio airplay is the result of monitoring more than 100 stations representing [[Rock music|rock]], [[Country music|country]], [[Adult contemporary music|adult contemporary]] and [[Contemporary hit radio|Top 40]] genres.<ref name="Launches">{{cite news|title=Billboard Launches Canadian Hot 100 Chart|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1051685/billboard-launches-canadian-hot-100-chart|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=June 7, 2007|access-date=June 7, 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Billboard's Canadian Hot 100 Now Incorporates Spotify Listening|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6415093/canadian-hot-100-spotify-data|work=Billboard|date=December 23, 2014|access-date=December 23, 2014}}</ref><!--<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2019-11-02|title=Canadian Hot 100: November 2, 2019|work=Billboard|access-date=October 29, 2019}}</ref>-->

The first number-one song of the Canadian Hot 100 was "[[Girlfriend (Avril Lavigne song)|Girlfriend]]" by [[Avril Lavigne]] on March 31, 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2007-03-31/canadian-hot-100|title=Canadian Hot 100 – Week of March 31, 2007|work=Billboard|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|access-date=October 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/archive/charts/2007/CAN|title=2007 Archive - Canadian Hot 100|work=Billboard|access-date=December 30, 2019}}</ref> As of the issue for the week ending December 5, 2020, the Canadian Hot 100 has had 162 different number-one songs. The current number-one song is "[[Monster (Shawn Mendes and Justin Bieber song)|Monster]]" by [[Shawn Mendes]] and [[Justin Bieber]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2020-12-05|title=Canadian Hot 100: December 5, 2020|work=Billboard|access-date=December 1, 2020}}</ref>

==History==
The chart was launched on the issue dated March 31, 2007 and was made available for the first time via ''Billboard'' online services on June 7, 2007. With this launch, it marked the first time that ''Billboard'' created a Hot 100 chart for a country outside the United States. 

''Billboard'' charts manager Geoff Mayfield announced the premiere of the chart, explaining "the new ''Billboard'' Canadian Hot 100 will serve as the definitive measure of Canada's most popular songs, continuing our magazine's longstanding tradition of using the most comprehensive resources available to provide the world's most authoritative music charts."<ref>{{cite news|title=Billboard to rank Canada's hottest songs|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/billboard-to-rank-canada-s-hottest-songs-1.663422|work=CBC News|date=June 6, 2007|access-date=February 17, 2019}}</ref>

The ''Billboard'' Canadian Hot 100 is managed by Paul Tuch, director of Canadian operations for Nielsen BDS, in consultation with Silvio Pietroluongo, ''Billboard''{{'}}s associate director of charts and manager of the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name="Launches" />

* On the issue dated October 24, 2009, "[[3 (Britney Spears song)|3]]" by [[Britney Spears]] broke the record for the biggest jump to number one, leaping from number 86 to number one.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2009-10-24|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of October 24, 2009|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=January 19, 2011}}</ref>
* "[[Counting Stars]]" by [[OneRepublic]] had the longest climb to number one taking 34 weeks to reach that peak.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2014-02-08|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of February 8, 2014|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=January 30, 2014}}</ref>
* On the issue dated October 31, 2015, [[The Weeknd]]'s "[[The Hills (song)|The Hills]]" reached the top spot seven weeks after "[[Can't Feel My Face]]", becoming the first time in Canadian Hot 100 history that an album's lead single hit #1 after the second single did.
* In 2016, [[Justin Bieber]] became the first Canadian act to top the Year-End chart with "[[Sorry (Justin Bieber song)|Sorry]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/year-end/2016/canadian-hot-100|title=Canadian Hot 100 - Year End 2016|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|access-date=December 13, 2016}}</ref>
* On the issue dated January 28, 2017, [[Ed Sheeran]] became the first act to simultaneously debut at the top two positions with "[[Shape of You]]" at number one and "[[Castle on the Hill]]" at number two.<ref name=january282017/>
* On the issue dated July 14, 2018, [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] became the artist with the most simultaneous top 10 singles (6).<ref name=July142018/>
* On the issue dated July 14, 2018, Drake became the artist with the most simultaneously charted Canadian Hot 100 songs in a single week (27) and the most Canadian Hot 100 debuts in a week (22).<ref name=July142018/>
* On the issue dated January 12, 2019, "[[All I Want for Christmas Is You]]" by [[Mariah Carey]] had the biggest drop out of the Canadian Hot 100, dropping off from #1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2019-01-05|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of January 5, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=January 12, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2019-01-12|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of January 12, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=January 12, 2019}}</ref>
* On the issue dated May 25, 2019, "[[I Don't Care (Ed Sheeran and Justin Bieber song)|I Don't Care]]" by [[Ed Sheeran]] and [[Justin Bieber]] jumped 90 spots to number 2, becoming the biggest single-week jump on the Canadian Hot 100's history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2019-05-25|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of May 25, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=May 21, 2019}}</ref>
* "[[All I Want for Christmas Is You]]" by [[Mariah Carey]] holds the record for largest gap between turns at #1, falling from the position on the week ending January 12, 2019 and returning the week ending January 4, 2020, a 51-week gap.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2019-01-12|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of January 12, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=January 11, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/canadian-hot-100/2020-01-04|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of January 4, 2020|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media]]|access-date=January 11, 2020}}</ref>
