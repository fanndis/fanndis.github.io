The '''2011 Wimbledon Championships''' was a tennis tournament played on [[grass court]]s at the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in [[Wimbledon, London]] in England.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> It was the 125th edition of the [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]] and were held from 20 June to 3 July 2011. It was the third [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tennis event of the year and was part of the [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP World Tour]], the [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA Tour]], the [[Junior tennis#The ITF junior tour|ITF Junior Tour]] and the [[ITF Wheelchair Tennis Tour|NEC Tour]]. The championships were organised by the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] and the [[International Tennis Federation]].

In the professional tournaments there were two new singles champions for the first time since [[2002 Wimbledon Championships|2002]]: [[Novak Djokovic]] and [[Petra Kvitová]]. By reaching the final Djokovic also claimed the World No. 1 in the ATP rankings from [[Rafael Nadal]], while Kvitová became the first Grand Slam event winner born in the 1990s. In the doubles the [[Bryan brothers]] claimed the men's title for a second time, and equalled the overall Grand Slam tournament record of 11 set by [[the Woodies]], [[Mark Woodforde]] and [[Todd Woodbridge]]. In the women's doubles [[Katarina Srebotnik]] won her first ladies major title after making four previous major finals. Srebotnik won alongside [[Květa Peschke]]; this was Peschke's first major title. In the mixed doubles [[Jürgen Melzer]] won his second major title, and first in the mixed doubles as he partnered [[Iveta Benešová]] to her first major title. In total, players from the [[Czech Republic]] (Kvitová, Peschke, and Benešová) were champions in three of the five main tour events in the tournament.

In the junior tournaments both the boys and girls singles titles were won by Australians. [[Luke Saville]] won the boys title while [[Ashleigh Barty]] became the first Australian in 31 years to win the girls title. In the doubles there was home success as Brit [[George Morgan (tennis)|George Morgan]] and Croatian [[Mate Pavić]] won their maiden junior Grand Slam tournament titles. The girls doubles title was claimed by Canadian [[Eugenie Bouchard]] and American [[Grace Min]].

In the wheelchair events [[Esther Vergeer]] and [[Sharon Walraven]] retained their doubles title. This was Vergeer's third successive win at the championships and meant that she was still unbeaten at Wimbledon. In the men's event [[Maikel Scheffers]] and [[Ronald Vink]] completed a team career Grand Slam, as they won the only title they had previously failed to win as a team.

The legends events titles were won by the teams of: [[Lindsay Davenport]] and [[Martina Hingis]], the Dutch pair of [[Jacco Eltingh]] and [[Paul Haarhuis]], and the Australian pair of [[Pat Cash]] and [[Mark Woodforde]].

==Tournament==
[[File:Flickr - Carine06 - 2011 champions.jpg|thumb|220px|2011 Wimbledon champions]]
The 125th edition of the tournament saw two new courts opened. A new showcourt, [[No. 3 Court (Wimbledon)|Court No. 3]], and a new Court No. 4 opened on the first day of the championships. Court No. 3 was opened by [[Prince Edward, Duke of Kent|The Duke of Kent]], President of the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club, who unveiled a plaque marking the occasion.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/news/articles/2011-05-27/201105271306517303402.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180626030203/http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/news/articles/2011-05-27/201105271306517303402.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=26 June 2018 |author=Alexandra Willis |publisher=All England Club |website=Wimbledon.com |date=27 May 2011 |title=A new Show Court at Wimbledon }}</ref> A total of 19 courts were used for competition play and a further 22 for practice. The capacity of the grounds was thus increased by 1,000 to 38,500.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://championships.wimbledon.org/en_GB/news/articles/2010-07-06/201007071278484605882.html|title=Wimbledon 2010 in numbers|date=6 July 2010|accessdate=8 June 2011|website=Wimbledon.com|publisher=All England Club}}{{Dead link|date=May 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref name="general">{{cite web|url=http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/footer/press-and-media/facts-figures.html|title=Facts and figures|accessdate=8 June 2011|website=Wimbledon.com|publisher=All England Club|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110616233946/http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/footer/press-and-media/facts-figures.html|archive-date=16 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On a commercial front, [[Sony]] became a sponsor of the championships for the first time, while [[Jacob's Creek (wine)|Jacob's Creek]] and [[Lavazza]] replaced [[Blossom Hill (wine)|Blossom Hill]] and [[Nescafe]] as official wine and coffee of the tournament.<ref name="BBC13789076">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-13789076|title=Wimbledon eyes another profitable year|date=19 June 2011|author=Shanaz Musafer|work=[[BBC News]]|access-date=13 October 2018}}</ref> Qualifying for all events took place at the Bank of England Sports Ground, Roehampton.<ref name="general"/> The grass was of the [[Perennial Ryegrass]] type and cut to 8mm.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/footer/press-and-media/grass-courts.html|title=Grass Courts – General|website=Wimbledon.com|publisher=All England Club|accessdate=8 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110615015204/http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/footer/press-and-media/grass-courts.html|archive-date=15 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===125th anniversary===
The 2011 championships were the 125th to be held since 1877, excluding the years 1915–1918 and 1940–1945, when the event was not held due to the two world wars. To mark the occasion a number of special events and activities occurred. ''[[Blue Peter]]'' broadcast a special programme looking at the championships, past, present and future, which was screened on the second Monday of the tournament. Four 30-minute documentaries charting the history of the championships were commissioned. A new exhibition known as the queue was held in the [[Wimbledon Lawn Tennis Museum|All England Club's Museum]] celebrating the people who queue each year for tickets to the championship.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://2011.wimbledon.com/en_GB/news/articles/2011-05-27/201105271306517908245.html|author=Sarah Edworthy|date=27 May 2011|title=The Queue Exhibition|publisher=All England club|website=Wimbledon.com|access-date=5 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110609072621/http://2011.wimbledon.com/en_GB/news/articles/2011-05-27/201105271306517908245.html|archive-date=9 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> In addition, a range of licensed merchandise featuring the "125" logo was released; the ball boy and ball girl uniforms had this logo. The shoes provided by [[Fila (company)|Fila]] had the words "125 years" and the logo printed on them. The balls provided by [[Slazenger]] also had "125 years" stamped onto them, and a special can design was used. [[Lanson]] champagne, which is served on the grounds, had "125 years" stamped on the bottle. Finally, to celebrate the anniversary there was a community art project in which participants were asked to "interpret" an unstrung wooden tennis racket "in a medium of their choosing".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/125th-championships/125th-celebrations/index.html|accessdate=5 June 2011|title=125TH Championships Celebrations|publisher=All England club|website=Wimbledon.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110608015857/http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/125th-championships/125th-celebrations/index.html|archive-date=8 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

[[HSBC]] held a series of polls on the Wimbledon website to find the 10 greatest things about the championships. The polls consisted of anything from greatest character to best final.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://hsbc.wimbledon.com/default.aspx |title=Archived copy |access-date=19 June 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110624042408/http://hsbc.wimbledon.com/default.aspx |archive-date=24 June 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In addition the bank also teamed up with the Sports Technology Institute at [[Loughborough University]]; to predict how tennis would develop over the next 25 years up to 2036; the 150th Wimbledon and 100 years since [[Fred Perry]], the last British male winner of the championships, won.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hsbc.wimbledon.com/futures.htm |title=HSBC Celebrating 125 years of The Wimbledon Championships |publisher=Hsbc.wimbledon.com |date=15 June 2011 |accessdate=15 July 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110622194943/http://hsbc.wimbledon.com/futures.htm |archive-date=22 June 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Prize money===
The total prize money for 2011 championships was £14,600,000. The winner of the men's and women's singles title earned £1,100,000.<ref name=little2013>{{cite book|last=Little|first=Alan|title=Wimbledon Compendium 2013|year=2013|publisher=All England Lawn Tennis & Croquet Club|location=London|isbn=978-1899039401|pages=327–334|edition=23}}</ref><ref name="Prizemoney">{{cite web|title=About Wimbledon – Prize Money and Finance|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/aboutwimbledon/prize_money_and_finance.html|website=wimbledon.com|publisher=[[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]]|accessdate=8 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/content/documents/visiting-wimbledon/2011-prizemoney.pdf|title=The Championships, Wimbledon, 2011 Prize Money|website=Wimbledon.com|publisher=All England Club|accessdate=19 May 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110626042544/http://aeltc2011.wimbledon.com/content/documents/visiting-wimbledon/2011-prizemoney.pdf|archive-date=26 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Broadcast==

The 2011 tournament was broadcast in 185 countries.<ref name="BBC13789076"/> The [[BBC]] was the host broadcaster and, since the All England Club had made a deal with Sony, some of the tournament was broadcast in 3D for the first time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/oh-i-say-wimbledon-finals-action-to-be-shown-in-3d-cinemas-across-the-world-6574544.html|title=Oh, I say! Wimbledon finals action to be shown in 3D cinemas across the world|date=7 March 2011|work=[[Evening Standard]]|access-date=13 October 2018}}</ref> To mark the 125th anniversary, the BBC broadcast a documentary the night before the start of the tournament (19 June 2011), called ''125 years of Wimbledon: You Cannot Be Serious'', looking back at memorable moments.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/13696944|title=125 years of Wimbledon: You Cannot Be Serious|date=8 June 2011|work=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=19 June 2011}}</ref>

In the United States, the championship matches aired on [[NBC]] for the 43rd and final year. The network issued a statement saying it had been outbid for the rights to future broadcasts.<ref name=Wimbledon>{{cite web|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/sports_blog/2011/07/nbc-out-espn-in-no-more-wimbledon-tape-delay.html|title=NBC out, ESPN in, no more Wimbledon tape delay|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=3 July 2011}}</ref> Cable sports channel [[ESPN]], which had already been sharing Wimbledon coverage with NBC, became the exclusive American broadcaster of the tournament for a 12-year period, beginning in 2012. Under the agreement, all matches were to air live, as opposed to tape delaying some matches, a practice for which NBC had been criticised.<ref name=ESPNwim>{{cite web|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/los-angeles/news/story?id=6739076|title=ESPN acquires all rights to Wimbledon|date=5 July 2011|publisher=[[ESPN.com]]}}</ref>

==Attendance==
[[File:Duke and Duchess of Cambridge at 2011 Wimbledon.jpg|150px|thumb|right|Duke and Duchess of Cambridge watching the action from the Royal box of Centre Court]] Members of the [[British Royal Family]] attended the championships. With [[Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall|The Duchess of Cornwall]] attending the tournament on the first Wednesday, on official duty, where she met six ball boys and girls before watching the days play on Centre court from the Royal box.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.princeofwales.gov.uk/newsandgallery/news/the_duchess_of_cornwall_attends_the_125th_wimbledon_champion_5946093.html|title=The Duchess of Cornwall attends the 125th Wimbledon Championships|date=22 June 2011|website=princeofwales.gov.uk|publisher=Prince of Wales|access-date=27 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110805180938/http://www.princeofwales.gov.uk/newsandgallery/news/the_duchess_of_cornwall_attends_the_125th_wimbledon_champion_5946093.html|archive-date=5 August 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> While on the second Monday [[Prince William, Duke of Cambridge|The Duke]] and [[Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge|Duchess of Cambridge]] attended the championships, while on a private visit. The pair took in all three matches on Centre Court.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/home-news/duke-and-duchess-of-cambridge-at-wimbledon-2303494.html|work=The Independent|location=UK|date=27 June 2011|title=Duke and Duchess of Cambridge at Wimbledon}}</ref> After the first match, which was won by British player [[Andy Murray]], the pair briefly met him, after the Scot bowed towards them while on court at the end of the match.<ref>{{Cite AV media | title = 2011 Wimbledon Championships | type=Television production| publisher = [[BBC One]] | location = London | date = 27 June 2011}}</ref>

This was a significant rise compared to other days as in the two days previous days of the championships 90 and 87 people were treated respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-13936324 |title=BBC News – More than 140 treated on hot day at Wimbledon |publisher=BBC |date=27 June 2011 |accessdate=15 July 2011}}</ref>

==Protests==
On the middle Saturday, 14 people were arrested at the gate when trying to obtain access to the grounds. The All England Club shut the gates of the ground forcing spectators who had camped overnight to wait outside for 45 minutes before letting them in at 11.15&nbsp;am. The group wore yellow shirts and had paint and other equipment to make banners once inside of the ground. A source stated that the group were planning to demonstrate against government policy.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2011/jun/25/wimbledon-protest-gates-shut |title=Wimbledon 2011: Planned protest forces officials to shut gates |work=The Guardian|location=UK |date=25 June 2011}}</ref>
