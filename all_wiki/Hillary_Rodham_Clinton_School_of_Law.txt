The '''Hillary Rodham Clinton School of Law''' ({{lang-cy|Ysgol y Gyfraith Hillary Rodham Clinton Prifysgol Abertawe}}) is part of [[Swansea University]] and located in [[Swansea]], [[Wales]].

It confers [[Bachelor of Laws|LLB degrees]], postgraduate courses, and professional conversion and training courses including the [[Graduate Diploma in Law]], [[Legal Practice Course]], and [[Master of Laws]] degrees.

==Background==
Swansea University was founded in 1920 as the fourth college of the University of Wales.

Established in 1994 as the College of Law and Criminology, the School is the largest Law School in Wales{{cn|date=October 2020}} and ranks third in the UK for Criminology, and twenty-second in the UK for Law according to the ''Times''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.swansea.ac.uk/media-centre/news-archive/2017/guardianuniversityguideputsswanseauniversityintop20forwiderangeofsubjects.php|title=Guardian University Guide puts Swansea University in top 20 for wide range of subjects|website=swansea.ac.uk|access-date=19 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171019220536/http://www.swansea.ac.uk/media-centre/news-archive/2017/guardianuniversityguideputsswanseauniversityintop20forwiderangeofsubjects.php|archive-date=19 October 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

Academically, Swansea Law School offers [[Master of Laws|LLB degrees]], postgraduate courses, and professional conversion and training courses including the [[Graduate Diploma in Law]], [[Legal Practice Course]], and [[Master of Laws]] degrees.

== Location ==

The School is based at the University's Singleton Park campus, in the Richard Price building. The School intends to move to purpose-built premises on the University's Bay Campus within the next few years. This new development will house commercial law firms, technology companies, national and international agencies, along with the academics and students of the School.
