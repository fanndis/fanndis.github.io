'''George Timothy Clooney''' (born May 6, 1961) is an American actor, film director, producer, and screenwriter. He is the recipient of three [[Golden Globes|Golden Globe Awards]] and two [[Academy Awards]], one for acting in ''[[Syriana]]'' (2006) and the other for co-producing ''[[Argo (2012 film)|Argo]]'' (2012). In 2018, he was the recipient of the [[American Film Institute|AFI]] Lifetime Achievement Award.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.afi.com/laa/laa18.aspx |title=American Film Institute - 2018 George Clooney Tribute |website=www.afi.com|access-date=October 11, 2018}}</ref>

Clooney made his acting debut on television in 1978, and later gained wide recognition in his role as Dr. [[Doug Ross]] on the medical drama ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'' from 1994 to 1999, for which he received two [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nominations. While working on ''ER'', he began attracting a variety of leading roles in films, with his breakthrough role in ''[[From Dusk till Dawn]]'' (1996),<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://variety.com/1996/film/reviews/from-dusk-till-dawn-1200444406/ |title=From Dusk Till Dawn |last=McCarthy |first=Todd |date=January 22, 1996 |work=Variety|access-date=September 12, 2018 |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.amiannoying.com/(S(h1nq2y424dtgh2xkboty2hue))/collection.aspx?collection=1301 |title=AmIAnnoying.com - MTV Movie Awards: Breakthrough Performance |website=www.amiannoying.com|access-date=September 12, 2018}}</ref> and the crime comedy ''[[Out of Sight (1998 film)|Out of Sight]]'' (1998), in which he first worked with director [[Steven Soderbergh]], who would become a long-time collaborator. In 1999, he took the lead role in ''[[Three Kings (1999 film)|Three Kings]]'', a well-received war satire set during the [[Gulf War]].

In 2001, Clooney's fame widened with the release of his biggest commercial success, Soderbergh's heist comedy remake ''[[Ocean's Eleven]]'', the first of what became [[Ocean's (film series)|a trilogy]], starring Clooney. He made his directorial debut a year later with the biographical spy comedy ''[[Confessions of a Dangerous Mind (film)|Confessions of a Dangerous Mind]]'', and has since directed the historical drama ''[[Good Night, and Good Luck]]'' (2005), the sports comedy ''[[Leatherheads]]'' (2008), the political drama ''[[The Ides of March (2011 film)|The Ides of March]]'' (2011), and the war film ''[[The Monuments Men]]'' (2014). Clooney won an [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor]] for the Middle East thriller ''[[Syriana]]'' (2005), and subsequently earned [[Academy Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]] nominations for the legal thriller ''[[Michael Clayton (film)|Michael Clayton]]'' (2007) and the comedy-dramas ''[[Up in the Air (2009 film)|Up in the Air]]'' (2009) and ''[[The Descendants]]'' (2011). In 2013, he received the [[Academy Award for Best Picture]] for producing the political thriller ''[[Argo (2012 film)|Argo]]''. 

He has been nominated for Academy Awards in six different categories, a record he shares with [[Walt Disney]] and [[Alfonso Cuarón]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://uproxx.com/hitfix/ |title=HitFix |publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://deadline.com/2013/02/oscars-george-clooney-king-academy-awards-436446/ |title=OSCARS: Is George Clooney Now King Of The Academy Awards? |last=Hammond |first=Pete |date=February 21, 2013 |work=Deadline|access-date=September 6, 2018 |language=en-US}}</ref> Clooney was included on ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'s}} annual [[Time 100]] list, which identifies the  most influential people in the world, every year from 2006 to 2009.<ref>{{cite journal |author1=Bono |title=George Clooney – The 2009 TIME 100 |url=http://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,1894410_1894289_1894280,00.html |journal=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |accessdate=June 10, 2014 |date=April 30, 2009|author1-link=Bono}}</ref> He is also noted for his political and economic activism, and has served as one of the [[United Nations Messengers of Peace]] since January 31, 2008.<ref name="peace">{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/domesticNews/idUSN1833517620080118?feedType=RSS&feedName=domesticNews&rpc=22&sp=true |title=George Clooney named UN messenger of peace |agency=Reuters |date=January 18, 2008 |first=Patrick |last=Worsnip}}</ref><ref name="bbc1">{{cite news |title=UN gives actor Clooney peace role |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/7220701.stm |work=BBC News |date=February 1, 2008 |accessdate=July 5, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.betterworldcampaign.org/peace |title=Clooney PSA Announcement |publisher=Better World Campaign |accessdate=September 19, 2009}}</ref> Clooney is also a member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cfr.org/about/membership/roster.html?letter=C |title=Membership Roster |publisher=Council on Foreign Relations |accessdate=January 31, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110803033745/http://www.cfr.org/about/membership/roster.html?letter=C |archive-date=August 3, 2011 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He is married to human rights lawyer [[Amal Clooney]].

==Career==

===Early work, 1978–1993===
Clooney's first role was as an [[Extra (acting)|extra]] in the television mini-series ''[[Centennial (miniseries)|Centennial]]'' in 1978, which was based on the [[Centennial (novel)|novel of the same name]] by [[James A. Michener]] and was partly filmed in Clooney's hometown of Augusta, Kentucky.<ref>{{cite book |author1=Kimberly Potts |title=George Clooney : The Last Great Movie Star |date=2011 |publisher=Applause Theatre & Cinema Books |location=Montclair, NJ |isbn=978-1-55783-785-1 |page=217 |edition=Rev. and updated |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=q6EHHXqbXpQC&q=george+clooney%2C+Centennial&pg=PA284 |accessdate=June 17, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |author1=Mark Browning |title=George Clooney : an actor looking for a role |date=2012 |publisher=Praeger |location=Santa Barbara, Calif. |isbn=978-0-313-39621-2 |page=1 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BXaA1mCr93IC&q=george+clooney%2C+Centennial&pg=PA1 |accessdate=June 17, 2014}}</ref> Clooney's first major role came in 1984 in the short-lived sitcom ''[[E/R]]'' (not to be confused with ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'', the better-known hospital drama, on which Clooney also co-starred a decade later). He played a handyman on the series ''[[The Facts of Life (TV series)|The Facts of Life]]'' and appeared as Bobby Hopkins, a detective, on an episode of ''[[The Golden Girls]]''. His first prominent role was a semi-regular supporting role in the sitcom ''[[Roseanne (TV series)|Roseanne]]'', playing [[Roseanne Barr]]'s supervisor Booker Brooks, followed by the role of a construction worker on ''[[Baby Talk (TV series)|Baby Talk]]'', a co-starring role on the CBS drama ''[[Bodies of Evidence (TV series)|Bodies of Evidence]]'' as Detective Ryan Walker, and then a year-long turn as Det. James Falconer on ''[[Sisters (U.S. TV series)|Sisters]]''. In 1988, Clooney played in the comedy-horror film ''[[Return of the Killer Tomatoes]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title=George Clooney; Wrong 'Tomatoes' |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2000/07/09/movies/l-george-clooney-wrong-tomatoes-090387.html |work=The New York Times |accessdate=July 28, 2014 |date=July 9, 2000}}</ref> In 1990, he starred in the short-lived [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] police drama ''[[Sunset Beat]]''.<ref>Terrace, Vincent. ''Encyclopedia of Television Shows, 1925 through 2007 (Jefferson, North Carolina: McFarland & Co., 2008), pp.1468-9.</ref> During this period, Clooney was a student at the [[Beverly Hills Playhouse]] acting school for five years.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bhplayhouse.com/george-clooney |title=George Clooney on the Beverly Hills Playhouse Acting School |accessdate=July 28, 2014}}</ref>

===Breakthrough, 1994–1999===
[[File:George Clooney 1995.jpg|thumb|upright|left|Clooney in 1995]]
Clooney rose to fame when he played Dr. [[Doug Ross]], alongside [[Anthony Edwards]], [[Julianna Margulies]], and [[Noah Wyle]], on the hit NBC medical drama ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'' from 1994 to 1999. After leaving the series in 1999, he made a [[cameo appearance]] in the 6th season and returned for a guest spot in the show's final season.<ref>{{cite web |title=ER Bringing Back Clooney with Margulies before Checking Out |url=https://www.tvguide.com/News/ER-Clooney-Margulies-1002020.aspx |work=TV Guide |year=2009 |accessdate=January 21, 2009}}</ref> For his work on the series, Clooney nominations for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Television Series Drama|Best Actor – Television Series Drama]] in 1995, 1996, and 1997 (losing to co-star [[Anthony Edwards]]).<ref>{{cite web Clooney began appearing in films while working on ''ER''. His first major Hollywood role was in the horror comedy-crime thriller ''[[From Dusk till Dawn]]'', directed by [[Robert Rodriguez]] and co-starring [[Harvey Keitel]]. He followed its success with the romantic comedy ''[[One Fine Day (film)|One Fine Day]]'' with [[Michelle Pfeiffer]], and the action-thriller ''[[The Peacemaker (1997 film)|The Peacemaker]]'' with [[Nicole Kidman]]. Clooney was then cast as [[Batman]] in [[Joel Schumacher]]'s ''[[Batman & Robin (film)|Batman & Robin]]'', which was a modest box office performer, but a critical failure (with Clooney himself calling the film "a waste of money").<ref>{{cite web |first=Jack |last=Shepherd |title=George Clooney apologises for 'destroying' Batman & Robin 18 years later |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/news/george-clooney-apologises-for-destroying-batman--robin-18-years-later-10276879.html |newspaper=[[The Independent]] |date=May 26, 2015 |accessdate=September 19, 2015}}</ref> In 1998, he co-starred in the crime-comedy ''[[Out of Sight]]'' opposite [[Jennifer Lopez]], marking the first of his many collaborations with director [[Steven Soderbergh]].<ref>{{cite news |title=He's a Thief, She's a Marshal, They're an Item |url=https://www.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9B05E5DB103AF935A15755C0A96E958260 |accessdate=September 19, 2015 |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |date=June 26, 1998}}</ref> He also starred in ''[[Three Kings (1999 film)|Three Kings]]'' during the last weeks of his contract with ''ER''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Three Kings |url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/three-kings-1999 |publisher=rogerebert.com |accessdate=September 19, 2015}}</ref>

===After ''ER'', 2000–2010===
[[File:GeorgeClooneyGraumanHandFootprintsOct10.jpg|thumb|Clooney cast his hands and shoes in the [[Grauman's Chinese Theatre]] in 2007<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/6725463.stm |title=Clooney adds mark to Walk of Fame |date=June 6, 2007 |publisher=BBC |accessdate=September 13, 2009}}</ref>]]

After leaving ''ER'', Clooney starred in the commercially successful films ''[[The Perfect Storm (film)|The Perfect Storm]]'' (2000), a disaster drama; and ''[[O Brother, Where Art Thou?]]'' (2000), a [[Coen brothers]] adventure comedy. In 2001, he teamed up with Soderbergh again for the heist comedy ''[[Ocean's Eleven (2001 film)|Ocean's Eleven]]'', a remake of the 1960s [[Rat Pack]] film [[Ocean's Eleven (1960 film)|of the same name]], with Clooney playing [[Danny Ocean (character)|Danny Ocean]], originally portrayed by [[Frank Sinatra]]. It is Clooney's most successful film with him in the lead role, earning $451&nbsp;million worldwide (he appeared, but did not star, in ''Gravity'', which has a $723 million worldwide box office).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=gravity.htm |title=Gravity (2013) - Box Office Mojo |website=www.boxofficemojo.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=oceanseleven.htm |title=Ocean's Eleven (2001) - Box Office Mojo |website=www.boxofficemojo.com}}</ref> ''Ocean's Eleven'' inspired two sequels starring Clooney, ''[[Ocean's Twelve]]'' in 2004<ref>{{cite magazine |first=Lisa |last=Schwartzbaum |title=Ocean's Twelve |url=https://www.ew.com/article/2004/12/08/oceans-twelve |magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |date=December 8, 2004 |accessdate=September 19, 2015}}</ref> and ''[[Ocean's Thirteen]]'' in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ocean's Thirteen GEORGE CLOONEY AND MATT DAMON INTERVIEW |url=http://movieweb.com/movie/oceans-thirteen/george-clooney-and-matt-damon-interview/ |website=movieweb.com |accessdate=September 19, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160921020555/http://movieweb.com/movie/oceans-thirteen/george-clooney-and-matt-damon-interview/ |archive-date=September 21, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2001, Clooney and Soderbergh co-founded [[Section Eight Productions]], for which [[Grant Heslov]] was president of television. Clooney made his directorial debut in the 2002 film ''[[Confessions of a Dangerous Mind (film)|Confessions of a Dangerous Mind]]'', based on the autobiography of TV producer [[Chuck Barris]]. Though the film did not do well at the box office, critics stated that Clooney's directing showed promise.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://jackasscritics.com/movie.php?movie_key=275 |title=Jackass Critics – Confessions of a Dangerous Mind |publisher=Jack Ass Critics |accessdate=June 17, 2012}}</ref>

In 2005, Clooney starred in ''[[Syriana]]'', which was based loosely on former [[Central Intelligence Agency]] agent [[Robert Baer]]'s memoirs of his service in the Middle East. Clooney suffered an accident on the set of ''Syriana'', which caused a brain injury with complications from a punctured [[dura mater|dura]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.contactmusic.com/new/xmlfeed.nsf/mndwebpages/clooney%20contemplated%20suicide%20over%20brain%20injury |title=George Clooney – Clooney Contemplated Suicide Over Brain Injury |publisher=Contact Music |accessdate=September 19, 2009 |date=October 23, 2005}}</ref> The same year he directed, produced, and starred in ''[[Good Night, and Good Luck]]'', a film about 1950s television journalist [[Edward R. Murrow]]'s famous war of words with Senator [[Joseph McCarthy]]. At the 2006 Academy Awards, Clooney was nominated for [[Academy Award for Best Director|Best Director]] and [[Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay|Best Original Screenplay]] for ''Good Night, and Good Luck'', as well as [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor|Best Supporting Actor]] for ''Syriana''. He won the Oscar for his role in ''Syriana''.<ref>{{cite news |title=George Clooney Wins Supporting Actor: 2006 Oscars |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NqDbG9h-f7c |accessdate=September 19, 2015 |website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

[[File:George Clooney-4 The Men Who Stare at Goats TIFF09 (cropped).jpg|thumb|left|Clooney at the premiere of ''[[The Men Who Stare at Goats (film)|The Men Who Stare at Goats]]'' in the [[2009 Toronto International Film Festival]]]]

Clooney next appeared in ''[[The Good German]]'' (2006), a [[film noir]] directed by Soderbergh that is set in post-World War II Germany. In August 2006, Clooney and Heslov started the production company [[Smokehouse Pictures]]. He is one of the chief associates of the 100 Lives Initiative, a project which aims to remember the lives lost during the event.<ref name=huffpost>{{cite news |last1=Sieczkowski |first1=Cavan |title=George And Amal Clooney Attend '100 Lives' Commemoration |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/11/george-clooney-amal-clooney-100-lives_n_6846536.html |agency=Huffington Post |date=March 11, 2015}}</ref> As part of the initiative, Clooney launched the [[Aurora Prize]], which awards to those who risk their lives to prevent genocides and atrocities.<ref name=huffpost /><ref name=asbarez>{{cite news |title='There was a genocide of Armenians in 1915,' Says George Clooney |url=http://asbarez.com/141109/there-was-a-genocide-of-armenians-in-1915-says-george-clooney/ |agency=Asbarez |date=October 27, 2015}}</ref> Clooney had also urged various American government officials to support the United States' recognition of the Armenian Genocide.<ref name=asbarez /> Clooney visited Armenia to commemorate the 101st anniversary of the event in April 2016.<ref>{{cite news |title=George and Amal Clooney to visit Armenia next year |url=http://www.panarmenian.net/eng/news/199686/ |agency=PanArmenian |date=October 29, 2015}}</ref>

===Syria===
In May 2015, Clooney told the [[BBC]] that the Syrian conflict was too complicated politically to get involved in and he wanted to focus on helping the refugees.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-32791065 |title=George Clooney: Syria conflict 'incredibly complex' – BBC News |website=BBC News |language=en-GB|access-date=April 29, 2016}}</ref> In March 2016, he and his wife, Amal Clooney, met with Syrian refugees living in Berlin to mark the fifth anniversary of the conflict, before meeting with German Chancellor [[Angela Merkel]] to thank her for Germany's open-door policy.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/amal-clooney-and-george-clooney-meet-with-refugees-to-mark-fifth-anniversary-of-syrian-crisis-a6934041.html |title=Amal Clooney tells refugees how her family were once forced to flee their homeland too |date=March 16, 2016 |website=The Independent |language=en-GB|access-date=April 29, 2016}}</ref>

===LGBT rights===
On March 28, 2019, Clooney wrote an open letter calling for the boycott of the Sultan of Brunei's hotels over a new law that came into force on April 3, 2019 that punishes homosexual sex and adultery with death by stoning. Clooney lists 9 hotels including [[The Dorchester]], 45 Park Lane, [[Coworth Park]], [[The Beverly Hills Hotel]], [[Hotel Bel-Air]], [[Le Meurice]], [[Hotel Plaza Athenee]], Hotel Eden and Hotel Principe di Savoia and asks readers to consider how "we are putting money directly into the pockets of men who choose to stone and whip to death their own citizens for being gay or accused of adultery."<ref>{{cite news |title=Brunei to punish gay sex and adultery with death by stoning |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/03/27/asia/brunei-anti-lgbt-stoning-law-intl/index.html |accessdate=March 29, 2019 |publisher=CNN}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Clooney |first1=George |title=George Clooney: Boycott Sultan Of Brunei's Hotels Over Cruel Anti-Gay Laws |url=https://deadline.com/2019/03/george-clooney-sultain-of-brunei-hotels-boycott-beverly-hills-hotel-anti-gay-laws-brunei-1202584579/ |magazine=Deadline Hollywood |date=March 28, 2019}}</ref>
