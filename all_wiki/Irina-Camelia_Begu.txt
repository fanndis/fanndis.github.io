'''Irina-Camelia Begu''' (born 26 August 1990) is a [[Romanians|Romanian]] professional [[tennis]] player. 

Begu has won four [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]], one [[WTA 125K series|WTA 125K]], and 12 [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] singles titles. On 22 August 2016, she reached her career-high singles ranking of world No. 22. 

She has also won nine [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] and 19 [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] doubles titles. Her highest doubles ranking of No. 22 was achieved on 1 October 2018.

==Biography==
Begu was born in [[Bucharest]]. Her mother Steluţa is a civil servant, while her father Paul is an electrician. She has an older brother, Andrei.<ref name="Begu">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/page/GettingToKnow/Read/0,,12781~2334619,00.html|title=Biography|first=Irina|last=Begu|work=wtatennis.com|date=9 April 2011|accessdate=24 July 2011}}</ref>

She started playing tennis when she was three-and-a-half years old as her aunt, former tennis player Aurelia Gheorghe, introduced her to the sport. At 14 years old, Irina took part in a one-month tour promoted by the ITF with seven other girls and eight boys, joining the likes of [[Grigor Dimitrov]] and [[Ričardas Berankis|Ricardas Berankis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/5596672/title/begu-slides-in-as-clay-court-surprise|title=Begu Slides In As Clay Court Surprise|website=Women's Tennis Association|access-date=2016-05-28}}</ref>

Begu recently became a brand ambassador for TENA Lady sanitary products.

==Professional career==
===2011: Breakthrough===
[[File:Irina-Camelia Begu preparing to serve at the 2011 BCR Open Romania Ladies.jpg|thumb|left|Irina won both the singles and the doubles trophies at Copa Bionaire and at the BCR Open Romania Ladies]]
Begu, whose favorite surface is [[Clay court|clay]], started the year at No. 234, never winning a main-draw match at [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] level.

Begu won the first $100,000 ITF event of her career in February in [[2011 Copa Bionaire|Cali]], defeating world No. 82 [[Laura Pous Tió]] in the final in straight sets.

In April, she entered the [[2011 Andalucia Tennis Experience|Andalucia Tennis Experience]] as a qualifier and eventually made the qualifications without losing a set. Then she defeated [[Alberta Brianti]] in the first round and followed that up with a two-sets win over [[Estrella Cabeza Candela]] to advance to the quarterfinals. She made to the semifinals after she beat 34th-ranked [[Klára Zakopalová]] in straight sets. In the semifinal, she stunned world No. 14 and former world No. 2 [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] to reach her first WTA final, eventually losing to [[Victoria Azarenka]] in straight sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com/articles/templates/news.aspx?articleid=11406&zoneid=25|title=Azarenka dominates Begu, wins in Marbella|publisher=tennis.com|accessdate=10 April 2011}}</ref>

Begu neared her first ever WTA Tour title, reaching her second final of the year in [[2011 Poli-Farbe Budapest Grand Prix|Budapest]] on 10 July. The Romanian played the semifinal more aggressively than the fifth seed [[Anabel Medina Garrigues]] who allowed the youngster to break her when it counted most. Irina had previously dominated another [[Spanish people|Spaniard]] [[Estrella Cabeza Candela]]. Her first round brought her a win over another Spanish player, [[Laura Pous Tió]] whom she defeated in straight sets, and a second straight similar win over [[Ajla Tomljanović]] of [[Croatia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metrolic.com/budapest-begu-sets-vinci-final-172797/|title=Budapest: Begu Sets Vinci Final|publisher=metrolic.com|accessdate=9 July 2011}}</ref> She eventually lost the last act against top-seeded [[Roberta Vinci]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/2011/tennis/07/10/budapest.gp.ap/index.html|title=Vinci beats Begu in final of Budapest Grand Prix|publisher=sportsillustrated.cnn.com|accessdate=10 July 2011}}</ref>

In July she posted the lone upset of second-round at the [[2011 Internazionali Femminili di Palermo|Internazionali Femminili di Palermo]] tournament in Italy, defeating second-seeded Roberta Vinci, after eliminating another local in the first round, [[Maria Elena Camerin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.upi.com/Sports_News/2011/07/14/Begu-upset-is-ticket-to-quarterfinals/UPI-38651310678510/|title=Begu upset is ticket to quarterfinals|publisher=upi.com|accessdate=14 July 2011}}</ref> Begu lost eventually to the fifth-seed [[Anabel Medina Garrigues]] in the quarterfinals, who was also the winner of the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metrolic.com/palermo-medina-cruises-through-the-last-term-173557/|title=Palermo: Medina Cruises Through The Last Term|publisher=metrolic.com|accessdate=16 July 2011}}</ref>

In the same month, Begu won the [[2011 BCR Open Romania Ladies|BCR Open Romania Ladies]] title, by defeating the Spanish opponent Laura Pous Tió in the final. Begu achieved her third consecutive win against Laura Pous Tió in 2011, after the two in [[2011 Copa Bionaire|Cali]] and [[2011 Poli-Farbe Budapest Grand Prix|Budapest]]. It was her fifth final of the year and her second trophy. The 50 WTA points allowed her to enter the top 50 best women players in the world, becoming also the highest ranked Romanian player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bcropen.ro/en/news/article/the-trophy-stays-in-bucharest-103.html|title=The trophy stays in Bucharest!|publisher=bcropen.ro|accessdate=23 July 2011}}</ref>

===2012: First WTA title===
Begu competed at the [[2012 Summer Olympics]] in the women's singles, but lost in the first round to Victoria Azarenka.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/be/irina-begu-1.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200418120440/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/be/irina-begu-1.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2020-04-18|title=Irina Begu Bio, Stats, and Results|website=Olympics at Sports-Reference.com|language=en|access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref>

Ranked world No. 96, Begu won her opening match at the [[2012 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], defeating the eighth seed, 2009 finalist and two-time semifinalist [[Caroline Wozniacki]] in straight sets. She subsequently lost to [[Sílvia Soler Espinosa]] of Spain in round two.

In September of that year, she won her first WTA title in Tashkent, beating Donna Vekic in straight sets in the final.

[[File:Irina-Camelia Begu, Wimbledon 2013 - Diliff.jpg|thumb|right|At the 2013 Wimbledon Championships]]
===2014: First WTA Premier final and two WTA doubles titles===
Begu started the season losing in the qualifying of [[Sydney]] and [[Brisbane]]. At the [[2014 Australian Open|Australian Open]] she passed through the qualifying draw, beating [[Stephanie Vogt]], [[Jovana Jakšić]] and [[Madison Brengle]] before losing in the main draw to Kazakh [[Galina Voskoboeva]] in three sets. In [[2014 Rio Open|Rio]], she played the qualifying rounds and reached the main draw, where she made it to the quarterfinals after beating eighth seed [[Vania King]] and [[Verónica Cepede Royg]]. She then lost to [[Teliana Pereira]] in two sets. However, she won the title in the doubles, teaming up with [[María Irigoyen]].

At [[2014 Estoril Open|Oeiras]] she reached the semifinals, beating en route [[Kurumi Nara]], [[Kaia Kanepi]] and [[Timea Bacsinszky]] before losing to eventual champion [[Carla Suárez Navarro]]. At [[2014 Korea Open|Seoul]] in singles she lost in the first round to [[Chanelle Scheepers]] in three sets. In doubles she paired with [[Lara Arruabarrena]] defeating [[Mona Barthel]] and [[Mandy Minella]] in the final 6–3, 6–3, claiming her second WTA doubles title of the year. At [[2014 Kremlin Cup|Moscow]] she reached her first Premier final, beating [[Donna Vekić]], 2nd seed [[Ekaterina Makarova]], [[Tsvetana Pironkova]] and 4th seed [[Lucie Šafářová]] before losing to seed No. 6 and eventual champion [[Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova]]. With this result, she returned to the top 40.

===2015: First major fourth round, second WTA title, top 30===
Begu achieved her best singles Grand Slam result at the [[2015 Australian Open|Australian Open]], reaching the fourth round for the first time before being defeated by [[Eugenie Bouchard]] in three sets. Prior to that she upset ninth seed [[Angelique Kerber]] in the first round.

In [[Rio Open|Rio]], Begu made semifinals by beating in her route [[Olivia Rogowska]], [[Bethanie Mattek-Sands]] and [[Julia Glushko]] in two sets before losing to eventual finalist [[Anna Schmiedlová]] in three sets.

As No. 13 at [[2015 Family Circle Cup|Charleston]], she made quarterfinals after Makarova's withdrawal, losing in two tie-breaks to Angelique Kerber.

At [[2015 Mutua Madrid Open|Madrid]] she made the quarterfinals beating en route Kurumi Nara, [[Andrea Petkovic]] (who had to withdraw), and Barbora Strýcová before losing to [[Petra Kvitová]]. At [[2015 Internazionali BNL d'Italia|Rome]] she made third-round losing in three sets to Victoria Azarenka.

At the [[2015 French Open|French Open]], Begu reached the third round for the first time beating Bethanie Mattek-Sands and [[Ana Konjuh]] en route before losing to Petra Kvitová.

Prior to [[2015 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Begu played in [[Birmingham Classic (tennis)|Birmingham]] and [[Eastbourne International|Eastbourne]], but lost in the first round in both tournaments. She then reached the third round of the Grand Slam by beating [[Daria Gavrilova]] and [[Lesia Tsurenko]] before losing to fourth seed Maria Sharapova in straight sets.

At [[Citi Open|Washington]] as seed 6, Begu made quarterfinals by beating [[Madison Brengle]] and [[Polona Hercog]] before losing to top seed of the tournament Ekaterina Makarova. Despite only two wins in six matches in the American season, with her US Open opener lost, Begu reached a career high as the world No. 28 ranking.

Seeded No. 1, she then won her second WTA title in [[Korea Open (tennis)|Seoul]] by defeating [[Kateryna Kozlova]], Polona Hercog, [[Johanna Larsson (tennis)|Johanna Larsson]], [[Alison Van Uytvanck]], and in the final WTA rising star [[Aliaksandra Sasnovich]].

===2016: First Premier-5 semifinal and third WTA title===
Begu started her season in [[WTA Shenzhen Open|Shenzhen]], beating Lara Arruabarrena in the first round. In the second round, she had to withdraw in the second set in her match against [[Anna Lena Friedsam]] due to knee injury. Begu also withdrew from [[2016 Apia International Sydney|Sydney]]. She then lost in the first round of the Australian Open to Johanna Larsson.
 
In February she could not play at all. She had to withdraw from the Fed Cup, [[2016 St. Petersburg Ladies' Trophy|St. Petersburg]] and [[2016 Qatar Total Open|Doha]] because of her knee injury.

In March, Irina played the 2016 BNP Paribas Open, where she lost in the first round to Laura Siegemund, falling in three sets. She then played at the Miami Open, where she beat qualifier [[Maria Sakkari]], [[Sabine Lisicki]] and [[Kristýna Plíšková]], before losing to [[Madison Keys]].

Begu started her clay-court season in [[2016 Volvo Car Open|Charleston]] where she was seeded No. 13. She beat [[Caroline Garcia]], Bethanie Mattek-Sands and [[Monica Puig]] (saving a match point) before losing to Angelique Kerber. After Charleston, Begu played for Romania in the Fed Cup, but lost both singles and doubles in their tie against Germany.  At [[2016 Grand Prix SAR La Princesse Lalla Meryem|Rabat]], Begu was surprisingly defeated by [[lucky loser]] [[Richel Hogenkamp]] in straight sets.

Begu's next tournament was [[2016 Mutua Madrid Open|Madrid]] where she arrived for the 2nd consecutive time in the quarterfinals of the tournament, beating [[Eugenie Bouchard]], No. 3 seed [[Garbine Muguruza]] and [[Christina McHale]] en route. In the quarterfinals, she played against fellow Romanian [[Simona Halep]], losing in three sets. She then played at the [[Italian Open (tennis)|Italian Open]] and defeated [[Margarita Gasparyan]] before getting a surprising win over world No. 5 Victoria Azarenka. She then beat [[Daria Kasatkina]] and [[Misaki Doi]] en route to her first Premier-5 semifinal where she lost to eventual champion [[Serena Williams]]. Her next tournament was [[French Open|Roland Garros]], where she beat Bethanie Mattek-Sands, [[CoCo Vandeweghe]] and [[Annika Beck]] before losing to [[Shelby Rogers]] in the fourth round.

In June, Begu began her grass-court season in [[2016 Aegon Classic Birmingham – Singles|Birmingham]], eliminating fourth seed [[Belinda Bencic]], before being upset in the second round by the in-form qualifier [[Tsvetana Pironkova]] in straight sets. She then lost to a resurgent Eugenie Bouchard in her first match at the [[Eastbourne International]]. Her grass-court campaign ended with a first-round loss at [[2016 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] to [[Carina Witthöft]]. Prior to the [[2016 Summer Olympics|Olympic Games]] in Rio, Begu played at the [[Brasil Tennis Cup]], where she beat [[Laura Pigossi]], [[Paula Cristina Gonçalves]], [[Nao Hibino]], Monica Puig and [[Tímea Babos]] en route to her third WTA title.  At the Olympics she competed in the women's singles (losing in the first round to Nao Hibino), the women's doubles (with [[Monica Niculescu]], they lost in the first round) and the mixed doubles (with [[Horia Tecău]], reaching the quarterfinals).<ref name=":0" />

===2017: Fourth WTA title===
At the [[2017 Australian Open|Australian Open]], Irina defeated [[Yaroslava Shvedova]] but lost in the second round to Kristýna Plíšková. On 23 July, she won the [[2017 BRD Bucharest Open|Bucharest Open]] without losing a set in the entire tournament.

===2020: First WTA 125K series title, 12th ITF title===
Irina won an [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] title in Cairo in February, the [[2020 Zed Tennis Open]], by defeating Lesia Tsurenko in the final. 

She continued her good form in March and won a [[WTA 125K series]] title, the [[2020 Oracle Challenger Series – Indian Wells|Oracle Challenger Series – Indian Wells]]. She did not lose a set in the entire tournament and defeated [[Misaki Doi]] in the final.
