'''Köyliö''' ({{lang-sv|Kjulo}}) is a [[List of former municipalities of Finland|former municipality]] of [[Finland]]. It was merged to the municipality of [[Säkylä]] on 1 January 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://yle.fi/uutiset/koylion_ja_sakylan_kuntaliitos_uuden_hallituksen_ensimmaisia_paatoksia/7946206|title=Köyliön ja Säkylän kuntaliitos uuden hallituksen ensimmäisiä päätöksiä|accessdate=April 24, 2015}}</ref>

It was located in the [[Satakunta (region)|Satakunta]] [[regions of Finland|region]]. The population of Köyliö was {{formatnum: 2665}} ({{date|2015-06-30}})<ref name="population_count" /> and covered a land area of {{convert|246.06|km2|abbr=on}}.<ref name="total_area" /> The population density was {{pop density|2665|246.06|km2|sqmi}}.

The municipality was unilingually [[Finnish language|Finnish]].

It is said that the peasant [[Lalli]] murdered the English [[bishop Henry]] on the ice of [[Köyliönjärvi|Lake Köyliö]] in 1156 AD, during the [[First Swedish Crusade|first Swedish Crusade into Finland]]. A statue to Lalli was erected at Köyliö in 1989.

==Twinnings==

Before the 2016 consolidation, Köyliö was [[Town twinning|twinned]] with;
 
* [[Nora Municipality|Nora]], Sweden (1944)
* [[Kõo Parish|Kõo]], Estonia (1991)<ref name="kõo">{{cite web|url=http://www.vald.koo.ee/index.php?id=10581|title=Sõprusvallad|publisher=Kõo Parish|language=Estonian|accessdate=24 May 2010|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720125324/http://www.vald.koo.ee/index.php?id=10581|archivedate=20 July 2011}}</ref>
