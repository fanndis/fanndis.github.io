The '''2013 Wimbledon Championships''' was a tennis tournament played on [[grass court]]s at the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in [[Wimbledon, London]] in England.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> It was the 127th edition of the [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]] and were held from 24 June to 7 July 2013.<ref name=On-Court>{{cite web|title=Wimbledon 2013 Men’s Schedule of Play|url=http://www.oncourtadvantage.com/grand-slams-wimbledon-2/wimbledon-2013-mens-schedule-of-play/|work=Oncourtadvantage.com|publisher=OnCourt Advantage|accessdate=12 August 2012|date=9 July 2012}}</ref> It was the third [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tennis event of the year and was part of the [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP World Tour]], the [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA Tour]], the [[Junior tennis#The ITF junior tour|ITF Junior Tour]] and the [[ITF Wheelchair Tennis Tour|NEC Tour]]. The championships were organised by the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] and the [[International Tennis Federation]].

[[Roger Federer]] and [[Serena Williams]] were the defending champions in singles events, but neither was able to repeat their success: Federer was eliminated in the second round by [[Sergiy Stakhovsky]], and Williams lost in the fourth round to [[Sabine Lisicki]]. This marked the first time since [[1927 Wimbledon Championships|1927]] that both defending champions were eliminated before the quarterfinals.<ref>[http://www.foxsports.com.au/tennis/serena-williams-crashes-out-of-wimbledon-as-germanys-sabine-lisicki-charges-into-quarter-finals/story-e6frf4mu-1226672898030 Serena Williams crashes out of Wimbledon as Germany's Sabine Lisicki charges into quarter-finals], Fox Sports Australia</ref> Federer and Williams were two of a number of big-name casualties in the early rounds, along with two-time champion [[Rafael Nadal]], two-time semifinalist [[Jo-Wilfried Tsonga]], 2004 champion [[Maria Sharapova]] and former World No. 1s [[Victoria Azarenka]], [[Ana Ivanovic]], [[Lleyton Hewitt]], [[Caroline Wozniacki]] and [[Jelena Janković]].

[[Andy Murray]] became the first man from Great Britain to win the singles title since [[Fred Perry]] in [[1936 Wimbledon Championships|1936]]. [[Marion Bartoli]] won the women's singles title. [[Bob Bryan|Bob]] and [[Mike Bryan]] completed the "Bryan Slam" and became the first team to hold all four Grand Slams and the Olympic Gold at the same time.

==Tournament==
[[File:Centre Court roof.jpg|150px|thumb|left|Centre Court, where the Finals of Wimbledon take place]]
The 2013 Wimbledon Championships was the 127th edition of the tournament and was held at [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in London.

The tournament was an event run by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) and was part of the [[2013 ATP World Tour]] and the [[2013 WTA Tour]] calendars under the Grand Slam category. The tournament consisted of both men's and women's singles and doubles draws as well as a mixed doubles event.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/scores/draws/ms/index.html|title=Draws|accessdate=10 June 2013}}</ref>

There were singles and doubles events for both boys and girls (players under 18), which was part of the Grade A category of tournaments, and doubles events for men's and women's [[wheelchair tennis]] players as part of the NEC tour under the Grand Slam category. The tournament was played on grass courts and was taking place over a series of 19 courts, including the four main showcourts, [[Centre Court]], [[No. 1 Court (Wimbledon)|No. 1 Court]], [[No. 2 Court (Wimbledon)|No. 2 Court]] and [[No. 3 Court (Wimbledon)|No. 3 Court]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.wimbledon.com/pdf/THmaplayeredApril2013.pdf|title= The Grounds|accessdate=10 June 2013}}</ref>

==Notable events==
[[File:Sergiy Stakhovsky.jpg|thumb|right|[[Sergiy Stakhovsky]] caused an upset in the second round by defeating seven-time champion [[Roger Federer]]]]
[[File:Sabine Lisicki 2012 (1).jpg|thumb|right|[[Sabine Lisicki]] caused one of the biggest upsets of the tournament by defeating reigning Wimbledon, US Open and French Open champion, world number one, [[Serena Williams]], in the fourth round]]
* [[Rafael Nadal]] suffered his first ever opening round defeat at a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament when he lost to [[Steve Darcis]] in the first round. This also marked the first time since [[1997 Wimbledon Championships|1997]] in which the reigning French Open men's champion lost in the first round at Wimbledon.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-06-25/nadal-crashes-out-in-wimbledon-first-round/4777420 Wimbledon: Lleyton Hewitt ousts Stanislas Wawrinka as Rafael Nadal drops out in first round], ABC News (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)</ref> [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 5|Women's fifth seed]] [[Sara Errani]] also made a first round exit, losing to [[Monica Puig]] in straight sets.
* By defeating [[Benjamin Becker]] in the first round, [[Andy Murray]] won his 107th Grand Slam match, thus becoming the most successful British man at a Major and breaking [[Fred Perry]]'s total Grand Slam match win record of 106.<ref>[http://www.smh.com.au/sport/tennis/nadal-suffers-shock-firstround-wimbledon-exit-20130625-2otb8.html Rafael Nadal Beaten at Wimbledon By Steve Darcis], smh.com.au</ref>
* The 2013 championship was also known for having numerous players retire in the first two rounds due to fatigue or injury, most notably [[Marin Čilić]], [[Jo-Wilfried Tsonga]], [[Victoria Azarenka]], [[John Isner]], and first round surprise [[Steve Darcis]]. On Wednesday, 26 June a total of eight players, including seven in singles events, withdrew from the tournament; four of those made the decision before the starts of their matches.
* Third-ranked [[Roger Federer]] fell in the second round to world number 116 [[Sergiy Stakhovsky]], his first loss in a Grand Slam earlier than the Quarterfinals since [[2004 French Open]], a streak spanning 36 majors. Federer's world ranking dropped to No. 5 as a result of this early exit.<ref>{{cite news|last=Walker|first=Randy|title=Roger Federer Suffers Shock Second-Round Upset Loss at Wimbledon|url=http://www.worldtennismagazine.com/archives/9524|accessdate=26 June 2013|newspaper=World Tennis Magazine|date=26 June 2013}}</ref>
* Federer's second round loss also marked the worst collective performance by him and [[Rafael Nadal]] at a Grand Slam tournament, in which both men were entered.<ref name="upsets">[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/06/27/wimbledon-2013-upsets-galore-on-day-three/ Wimbledon 2013: Federer, and more – upsets galore on day three], The Roar</ref> This also marked the first time since [[2002 Wimbledon Championships|2002]] in which neither man made the Wimbledon final.<ref>[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/07/07/wimbledon-2013-mens-final-preview/ Wimbledon 2013: Men's final preview], The Roar</ref>
* For the first time since 1912, no U.S. man was represented in the third round at Wimbledon, following [[Bobby Reynolds]]'s second round defeat to World No. 1 [[Novak Djokovic]].<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-06-28/djookvic-overcomes-slow-strat-to-progress/4786590?section=sport Wimbledon 2013: Novak Djokovic overcomes slow start against Bobby Reynolds to progress], ABC Grandstand Sport (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)</ref>
* [[Kimiko Date|Kimiko Date-Krumm]] became the oldest woman to reach the third round at Wimbledon, by defeating [[Alexandra Cadanțu]] in the second round. It is also the first time since the [[1996 Wimbledon Championships]] in which she has gone this far. Her opponent, number one seed [[Serena Williams]], combined for an age of 73, and this was their first ever meeting.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-06-28/williams-sets-up-27battle-of-the-senior-citizens27-with-date-/4786924?section=sport Wimbledon 2013: Serena Williams sets up 'battle of the senior citizens' with Kimiko Date-Krumm], ABC Grandstand Sport (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)</ref> Date-Krumm lost in straight sets in the late evening encounter on Saturday 29 June 2013.
* Of the top ten women's seeds that started the Championships, six fell before the third round: [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 8|second seed]] Victoria Azarenka (second round, withdrew), [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 6|third seed]] Maria Sharapova (second round), [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 5|fifth seed]] Sara Errani (first round), [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 2|seventh seed]] [[Angelique Kerber]] (second round), [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 5|ninth seed]] [[Caroline Wozniacki]] (second round) and [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles#Section 2|tenth seed]] [[Maria Kirilenko]] (first round). This marked the worst performance by any top ten seeds at a Grand Slam tournament in the Open Era.<ref name="WTAseeds">[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/23103703 Wimbledon 2013: Angelique Kerber beaten by Kaia Kanepi], BBC Sport</ref>
* [[Laura Robson]] became the first British woman in 15 years to reach the fourth round at Wimbledon; by getting this far, she entered the Top 30 for the first time in her career. She thus became the first British woman since [[Jo Durie]] in 1987 to enter the WTA's Top 30.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-06-30/djokovic-storms-into-fourth-round-at-wimbledon/4789918?section=sport Wimbledon 2013: Novak Djokovic storms into fourth round after victory over Jeremy Chardy], ABC Grandstand Sport (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)</ref>
* [[Łukasz Kubot]] and [[Fernando Verdasco]] both reached the quarter-finals of Wimbledon for the first time,<ref name="MensR4Preview">[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/07/01/wimbledon-2013-mens-round-of-16-preview/ Wimbledon 2013: Men's round of 16 preview], The Roar</ref> whilst [[Juan Martín del Potro]] reached his first Grand Slam semi-final since winning the [[2009 US Open (tennis)|2009 US Open]].<ref>[https://www.bostonglobe.com/sports/2013/07/05/novak-djokovic-into-wimbledon-final-after-epic-match/subKYyu5YhpoSejFwOwonK/story.html Novak Djokovic into Wimbledon final after epic match], The Boston Globe</ref>
* [[Sloane Stephens]] and [[Kirsten Flipkens]] both reached the quarter-finals at Wimbledon for the first time.<ref name="WomensR4Preview">[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/07/01/wimbledon-2013-womens-round-of-16-preview/ Wimbledon 2013: Women's round of 16 preview], The Roar</ref>
* [[Sabine Lisicki]] continued her record of beating the reigning [[French Open]] champion at Wimbledon. She defeated [[Serena Williams]] in the fourth round, having also beaten [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] in 2009, [[Li Na]] in 2011 and [[Maria Sharapova]] in 2012.<ref>[http://www.theage.com.au/sport/tennis/wimbledon-shocks-continue-as-williams-shown-the-door-20130702-2p88g.html Sabine Lisicki Beats Serena Williams at Wimbledon], theage.com.au</ref> Lisicki's victory also denied Serena her 35th consecutive victory, a record which was achieved by her older sister, [[Venus Williams|Venus]], in [[2000 WTA Tour|2000]].<ref name="Lisicki">[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/07/02/wimbledon-2013-womens-quarter-finals-preview/ Wimbledon 2013: Women's quarter-finals preview], The Roar</ref>
* This tournament marked the worst collective performance by Williams, Victoria Azarenka and Maria Sharapova at a Grand Slam event since the [[2008 French Open]],<ref name="Lisicki"/> and also the first tournament since the [[2011 French Open]] in which none of the three reached a Grand Slam final.<ref name="Lisicki2"/>
* [[Jerzy Janowicz]] became the first male [[Poland|Polish]] player to reach the semi-finals of a Grand Slam tournament.<ref name="Janowicz">[https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/jul/03/jerzy-janowicz-lukasz-kubot-wimbledon Wimbledon 2013: Jerzy Janowicz pounds past fellow Pole Lukasz Kubot], The Guardian</ref>
* By reaching the Wimbledon final, [[Sabine Lisicki]] became the first German Grand Slam singles finalist since [[Rainer Schüttler]] reached the final of the [[2003 Australian Open]], and the first German singles finalist at Wimbledon since [[Steffi Graf]] in [[1999 Wimbledon Championships|1999]].<ref name="Lisicki2">[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/07/06/wimbledon-2013-womens-final-preview/ Wimbledon 2013: Women's final preview], The Roar</ref>
* [[Novak Djokovic]] won his semi-final match against Juan Martín del Potro in five sets in 4 hours 44 minutes. This was the longest semi-final in the history of the [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Clarke |first=Liz |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/othersports/wimbledon-2013-andy-murray-novak-djokovic-reach-the-mens-singles-final/2013/07/05/6015d60e-e5bd-11e2-aef3-339619eab080_story.html |title=Wimbledon 2013: Andy Murray, Novak Djokovic reach the men’s singles final |work=The Washington Post|date=6 July 2013 |accessdate=6 July 2013}}</ref>
* With her win in the Ladies' Singles final, [[Marion Bartoli]] surpassed the female record set by [[Jana Novotná]] at the [[1998 Wimbledon Championships]] for most appearances in a Grand Slam tournament before winning a title, with 47.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/jul/06/marion-bartoli-sabine-lisicki-wimbledon |title=Marion Bartoli overwhelms Sabine Lisicki to win Wimbledon title|work=The Guardian|date=6 July 2013 |accessdate=6 July 2013}}</ref>
* In winning the Gentlemen's Doubles, [[Bob Bryan|Bob]] and [[Mike Bryan]] became the first men's doubles team in the Open era to hold all four Grand Slam titles at the same time, as well as the only team in history to hold all four Majors and the Olympic Gold medal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://espn.go.com/tennis/wimbledon13/story/_/id/9454607/2013-wimbledon-bob-bryan-mike-bryan-lay-claim-historic-bryan-slam |title=Bryan Bros. win fourth straight slam |agency=Associated Press |work=[[ESPN.com]] |date=6 July 2013 |accessdate=6 July 2013}}</ref>
*Andy Murray defeated Novak Djokovic in the final in straight sets to win the 2013 Wimbledon Men Singles title, becoming the first British man in 77 years to do so, and the first [[Scottish people|Scot]] of either sex in 117 years to win a Wimbledon singles title.

===First Wednesday===
The first Wednesday (Wednesday 26 June) in the 2013 Wimbledon Championship saw a number of former world number ones knocked out in the second round of the draw either by being beaten by a much lower rank, having to retire early or having been "[[walkover|walked over]]". These players include [[Roger Federer]], [[Maria Sharapova]] and [[Victoria Azarenka]], as well as former ranking leaders [[Lleyton Hewitt]], [[Ana Ivanovic]], [[Jelena Janković]] and [[Caroline Wozniacki]]. Other notable players, including [[Jo-Wilfried Tsonga]], [[Marin Čilić]], [[John Isner]], [[Steve Darcis]], [[Yaroslava Shvedova]] and [[Radek Štěpánek]] also went out. A total of twelve seeded players (five men and seven women) finished their 2013 Wimbledon campaign on that day.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/23067270|title=Tsonga and Azarenka among seven to pull out of Wimbledon|date=27 June 2012|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=27 June 2013 }}</ref>

Events from "Black Wednesday" were highly commented,<ref>[http://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/blogs/tramlines/csi-tramlines-blame-wimbledon-black-wednesday-102351119.html CSI Tramlines: Who is to blame for Wimbledon’s Black Wednesday?]</ref> inter alia by the [[Association of Tennis Professionals]]<ref>[http://www.atpworldtour.com/News/Tennis/2013/06/26/Wimbledon-Wednesday-Diary-Federer-Isner-Stakhovsky.aspx WIMBLEDON DIARY – BLACK WEDNESDAY]</ref> and players<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/23067270 Tsonga and Azarenka among seven to pull out of Wimbledon]</ref><ref>[http://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/blogs/tramlines/azarenka-blasts-wimbledon-courts-withdrawal-143412607.html Azarenka anger after ‘dangerous’ Wimbledon claims ‘Black Wednesday’ victims]</ref> and a statement from tournament [[Chief Executive Officer]] has been released.<ref>[http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/news/articles/2013-06-26/201306261372272535913.html STATEMENT FROM RICHARD LEWIS, CEO, REGARDING PLAYER WITHDRAWALS]</ref>

===Prize money===
The Wimbledon total prize money for 2013 has been increased by forty percent to £22,560,000 (around $34m). The winners of the men's and women's singles titles earned £1.6m, up £450,000 from last year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/pdf/Press_Release_Prize_Money_2013.pdf |title=Prize Money|accessdate=21 June 2013}}</ref><ref name="Prizemoney">{{cite web|title=About Wimbledon – Prize Money and Finance|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/aboutwimbledon/prize_money_and_finance.html|website=wimbledon.com|publisher=[[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]]|accessdate=8 October 2017}}</ref> In the 2013 season, the Wimbledon prize money was the highest out of four grand slam tournaments, compared to $30m at the [[2013 Australian Open|Australian Open]], $29m at [[2013 French Open|French Open]], and $32m at the [[2013 US Open (tennis)|US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/sport/tennis/wimbledon-prizemoney-hits-34m-2013-grand-slam-jackpot-at-124m/story-fnbe6xeb-1226628792427 |title=Wimbledon prizemoney hits $34m, 2013 grand slam jackpot at $124m |publisher=Theaustralian.com.au |date=28 September 2012 |accessdate=21 June 2013}}</ref>

==Champions==
===Seniors===
====Men's Singles====
{{main|2013 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2013 Wimbledon Championships – Men's singles final}}
{{flagicon|GBR}} '''[[Andy Murray]]''' def. {{flagicon|SRB}} [[Novak Djokovic]], 6–4, 7–5, 6–4 <ref name="menss">{{cite web|title=Gentlemen's Singles Finals 1877–2017|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/draws_archive/champions/gentlemenssingles.html|website=wimbledon.com|publisher=[[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]]|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref>
* It was Murray's fourth title of the year and first Grand Slam title of the year. It was his first Wimbledon title, second Grand Slam title and 28th career title.

====Women's Singles====
{{main|2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|FRA}} '''[[Marion Bartoli]]''' def. {{flagicon|GER}} [[Sabine Lisicki]], 6–1, 6–4 <ref name="womenss">{{cite web|title=Ladies' Singles Finals 1884–2017|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/draws_archive/champions/ladiessingles.html|website=wimbledon.com|publisher=[[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]]|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref>
* It was Bartoli's first (and only) Grand Slam title in her career and eighth singles title overall. It was also her final Grand Slam appearance before she would retire in August 2013.

====Men's Doubles====
{{main|2013 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Bob Bryan]]''' / {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Mike Bryan]]''' def. {{flagicon|CRO}} [[Ivan Dodig]] / {{flagicon|BRA}} [[Marcelo Melo]], 3–6, 6–3, 6–4, 6–4 <ref name="mensd">{{cite web|title=Gentlemen's Doubles Finals 1884–2017|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/draws_archive/champions/gentlemensdoubles.html|website=wimbledon.com|publisher=[[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]]|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref>
* It was the [[Bryan brothers]]' ninth title of the year and third Grand Slam title of the year. It was their third Wimbledon title, 15th Grand Slam title and 91st career title. They completed a [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Non-calendar year Golden Slam|non-calendar year Golden Slam]] and thus became the first double team in tennis history to hold all four majors as well as [[Tennis at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's doubles|Olympic gold medal]] at the same time.

====Women's Doubles====
{{main|2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|TPE}} '''[[Hsieh Su-wei]]''' / {{flagicon|CHN}} '''[[Peng Shuai]]''' def. {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Ashleigh Barty]] / {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Casey Dellacqua]], 7–6<sup>(7–1)</sup>, 6–1 <ref name="womensd">{{cite web|title=Ladies' Doubles Finals 1913–2017|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/en_GB/draws_archive/champions/ladiesdoubles.html|website=wimbledon.com|publisher=[[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon Championships]]|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref>
* It was their second title of the year and the first Grand Slam title for both players. It was Hsieh's 11th and Peng's 9th title in their careers.

==Singles seeds==
The following are the seeded players and notable players who withdrew from the event. Seeds based on ATP and WTA rankings are as of 17 June 2013 and the rankings and points are as of 24 June 2013.

===[[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|Men's Singles]]===
The Men's singles seeds is arranged on a surface-based system to reflect more accurately the individual player's grass court achievement as per the following formula, which applies to the top 32 players, according to ATP Ranking on 17 June 2013:
* Take Entry System Position (ESP) points at 17 June 2013
* Add 100% points earned for all grass court tournaments in the past 12 months (18 June 2012 – 16 June 2013).
* Add 75% points earned for best grass court tournament in the 12 months before that (13 June 2011 – 17 June 2012)
