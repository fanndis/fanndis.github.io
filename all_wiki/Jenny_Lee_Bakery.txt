'''Jenny Lee Bakery''' was a prominent bakery in [[McKees Rocks, Pennsylvania]], near [[Pittsburgh]].<ref>{{Cite news | last = Lindeman | first = Teresa F. |author2=Elwin Green| title = Jenny Lee Bakery turning off the ovens| newspaper = [[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]] | url = http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/08228/904524-28.stm | date=August 15, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news | last = Lindeman| first = Teresa F.| title = Historic Jenny Lee Bakery to close Saturday | newspaper = [[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]] | date = August 14, 2008 | url = http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/08227/904322-100.stm}}</ref>

The bakery was founded in 1938 by cousins Paul Baker and Bernard McDonald. The name came from the song "Sweet Jenny Lee from Sunny Tennessee", which was playing on the radio while the pair was traveling to a business meeting.<ref>{{cite book|last=Agreen|first=Bernadette Sulzer|title=McKees Rocks and Stowe Township|year=2009|publisher=Arcadia Publishing|page=114}}</ref> It closed in 2008.

In 2009, several members of the family restarted the company under the name '''5 Generation Bakers'''.<ref>{{Cite news | last = Batz| first = Bob | title = Jenny Lee Bakery is back!| newspaper = [[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]] | date = February 11, 2010| url = http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/10042/1034919-34.stm}}</ref><ref>[http://www.5generationbakers.com/our-story/ The History of Baker Family Baking], 5 Generation Bakers website</ref> The new version sells baked goods to local retailers such as the supermarket [[Giant Eagle]].<ref>{{Cite news | title = Jenny Lee bread is baking, to be in stores soon| newspaper = [[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]]| date = February 10, 2010| url = http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/10041/1034895-100.stm}}</ref>
