'''Ella Marija Lani Yelich-O'Connor''' (born 7 November 1996), known professionally as '''Lorde''' (pronounced "lord"), is a New Zealand singer and songwriter.<!--only occupations integral to artist's notability per reliable sources; [[MOS:LEADBIO]]--> Taking inspiration from [[aristocracy (class)|aristocracy]] for her stage name, she is known for employing unconventional musical styles and introspective songwriting. Lorde's music is primarily [[electropop]] and contains elements of [[Music genres|subgenres]] such as [[dream pop]] and [[indie-electro]].

Born in the [[Auckland]] suburb of [[Takapuna]] and raised in neighbouring [[Devonport, New Zealand|Devonport]], Lorde expressed interest in performing at local venues in her early teens. She signed with [[Universal Music Group]] (UMG) in 2009 and collaborated with producer [[Joel Little]] in 2011 to start recording material. Initially [[self-release (music)|self-released]] in 2012 for free download on [[SoundCloud]], UMG commercially released the pair's first collaborative effort, an [[extended play]] (EP) titled ''[[The Love Club EP|The Love Club]]'', in 2013. The EP's international chart-topping single "[[Royals (song)|Royals]]" helped Lorde rise to prominence.

Her debut studio album ''[[Pure Heroine]]'' was released that same year to critical and commercial success. The following year, Lorde curated [[The Hunger Games: Mockingjay, Part 1 (soundtrack)|the soundtrack]] for the 2014 film ''[[The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1]]'' and recorded several tracks, including the single "[[Yellow Flicker Beat]]". Her second studio album ''[[Melodrama (Lorde album)|Melodrama]]'' (2017) received widespread critical acclaim and debuted atop the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

Lorde's [[List of awards and nominations received by Lorde|accolades]] include two [[Grammy Awards]], two [[Brit Awards]] and a [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe]] nomination. She appeared in ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s list of the most influential teenagers in 2013 and 2014, and the 2014 edition of [[Forbes 30 Under 30|''Forbes'' 30 Under 30]]. In addition to her solo work, she has co-written songs for other artists, including [[Broods]] and [[Bleachers (band)|Bleachers]]. {{As of|2017|6}}, Lorde has sold over five million albums worldwide.

==Life and career==
===1996–2009: Early life===
[[File:Bayswater Marina and Takapuna.jpg|thumb|left|upright=0.92|[[Bayswater, New Zealand|Bayswater]], New Zealand, the [[suburb]] Lorde was raised in.]]
Ella Marija Lani Yelich-O'Connor was born on 7 November 1996 in [[Takapuna]], [[Auckland]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thehairpin.com/2014/01/here-is-lordes-birth-certificate/|title=Here Is Lorde's Birth Certificate|website=[[The Hairpin]]|date=28 January 2014|first=Emma|last=Carmichael|access-date=19 February 2019}}</ref> to poet [[Sonja Yelich]] ({{lang-hr|Jelić}}) and civil engineer Vic O'Connor.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1847971/lorde-parents-engaged/|title=Lorde's Parents Finally Got Engaged&nbsp;– After 30 Years|first=Brenna|last=Ehrlich|publisher=[[MTV News]]|date=17 June 2014|access-date=6 July 2014}}</ref> Her mother was born to [[Croatian New Zealanders|Croatian]] immigrants from the region of [[Dalmatia]], while her father is of [[Irish New Zealanders|Irish]] descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.total-croatia-news.com/lifestyle/21869-singer-lorde-talks-croatian-heritage|title=Grammy Award Winner Lorde Talks Croatian Heritage|website=Total-croatia-news.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180818052331/https://www.total-croatia-news.com/lifestyle/21869-singer-lorde-talks-croatian-heritage|archive-date=18 August 2018}}</ref> They announced their engagement in 2014, after a 30-year relationship,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rothman |first1=Michael |title=Lorde's Parents Engaged, at Last |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/lordes-parents-engaged/story?id=24171630 |access-date=20 August 2019 |work=ABC News |date=17 June 2014}}</ref> and in 2017, they married in a private ceremony in [[Cheltenham, New Zealand|Cheltenham Beach]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://i.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/92299673/lordes-parents-get-married-in-auckland|title=Lorde's parents get married in Auckland|work=Stuff.co.nz}}</ref>

Second of four children, she has three siblings: older sister Jerry, younger sister India, and younger brother Angelo.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lorde's younger sister makes musical debut|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/lordes-younger-sister-makes-musical-debut-20140121-315t0.html|access-date=25 January 2014|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=21 January 2014}}</ref> They were raised in the nearby Auckland [[suburb]]s of [[Devonport, New Zealand|Devonport]] and [[Bayswater, New Zealand|Bayswater]].<ref name="CoverStory"/><ref name="BlackMagazine"/> At age five, she joined a drama group and developed [[public speaking]] skills.<ref name="Weiner" /> Her mother encouraged her to read a range of genres, which Lorde cited as a lyrical influence. More specifically, she cites the [[young adult literature|young adult]] [[dystopian]] novel ''[[Feed (Anderson novel)|Feed]]'' (2002) by [[Matthew Tobin Anderson|M.T. Anderson]] as well as authors [[J.D. Salinger]], [[Raymond Carver]] and [[Janet Frame]] for influencing her songwriting.<ref name="BlackMagazine">{{cite web|title=Lorde, the year|work=Black Magazine|first=Grant|last=Fell|url=http://www.blackmagazine.co.nz/blk-list/lorde-the-year/|date=30 January 2014|access-date=30 August 2014}}</ref>

After a suggestion from a school instructor, her mother had her take the [[Woodcock–Johnson Tests of Cognitive Abilities]] to determine her intelligence. The results concluded that Lorde, age 6, was a [[intellectual giftedness|gifted]] child.<ref name="celebritystudies">{{cite journal |last1=Mitchell |first1=Tony |title=Lorde: a mole in the mainstream? |journal=Celebrity Studies |date=26 July 2016 |volume=8 |issue=1 |pages=51–70 |doi=10.1080/19392397.2016.1202122 |s2cid=193353639 |url=https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19392397.2016.1202122 |access-date=23 October 2020}}</ref> She was briefly enrolled at [[Gifted Education Centre|George Parkyn Centre]], a [[gifted education]] organisation. Sonja, unenrolled her, however, citing [[social emotional development|social development]] concerns.<ref name="celebritystudies"/> As a child, Lorde attended [[Vauxhall School]] and then [[Belmont Intermediate School]] in her early teens.<ref name="stuff">{{cite news|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/auckland/local-news/north-shore-times/8990736/Singer-now-on-centre-stage|title=Singer now on centre stage: Shore kid makes good at Splendour in the Grass|date=2 August 2013|first=Jess|last=Etheridge|work=North Shore Times|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921053757/http://www.stuff.co.nz/auckland/local-news/north-shore-times/8990736/Singer-now-on-centre-stage|archive-date=21 September 2013}}</ref> While attending Vauxhall, she placed third and first respectively in the North Shore Primary Schools' Speech competition, a national contest, in 2006 and 2007.<ref>{{cite web |title=Vauxhall School achievers |url=https://natlib.govt.nz/records/44338141 |website=National Library of New Zealand |publisher=The Devonport Flagstaff |page= 29 |access-date=21 November 2020 |date=25 August 2006}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Pet project goes down a treat with judges |url=https://natlib.govt.nz/records/44258443 |website=National Library of New Zealand |publisher=The Devonport Flagstaff |page= 6 |access-date=21 November 2020 |date=10 August 2007}}</ref> Lorde and her Belmont team were named the runner-up in the 2009 [[Kids' Lit Quiz]] World Finals, a global [[literature]] competition for students aged 10 to 14.<ref name="stuff"/>

===2009–2011: Career beginnings===
[[File:Ella Yelich O'Connor.png|thumb|upright|Lorde performing at the Victoria Theatre in Devonport, 2011|alt=A black-and-white photo of Lorde performing. She is seen touching her hair and smiling while looking downwards]]

In May 2009, Lorde and her friend Louis McDonald won the Belmont Intermediate School annual talent show as a duo.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/celebrity/lorde-returns-to-belmont-intermediate-school-to-judge-talent-show/story-fni0b8dw-1226761816655|title=Lorde returns to Belmont Intermediate School to judge talent show|work=[[Herald Sun]]|date=17 November 2013|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/76Dfyut0H?url=https://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/celebrity/lorde-returns-to-belmont-intermediate-school-to-judge-talent-show/news-story/fd9f2d49397cd63a45031e9f245da8e8|archive-date=16 February 2019|access-date=17 November 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> In August that year, Lorde and McDonald made a guest appearance on [[Jim Mora (broadcaster)|Jim Mora]]'s ''Afternoons'' show on [[Radio New Zealand]]. There, they performed [[cover version|covers]] of [[Pixie Lott]]'s "[[Mama Do (Uh Oh, Uh Oh)]]" and [[Kings of Leon]]'s "[[Use Somebody]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=Ella Yelich-O'Connor|url=http://www.radionz.co.nz/national/programmes/afternoons/audio/2033420/ella-yelich-o%27connor|publisher=Radio New Zealand|access-date=8 September 2014|date=17 March 2011}}</ref> McDonald's father then sent his recordings of the duo covering "Mama Do" and [[Duffy (singer)|Duffy]]'s "[[Warwick Avenue (song)|Warwick Avenue]]" to [[Universal Music Group]] (UMG)'s [[A&R]] executive Scott Maclachlan.<ref name="telegraph1">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/10433978/Lorde-interview-Dream-Teen.html|title=Lorde interview: Dream Teen|author=McNulty, Bernadette|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=8 November 2013|access-date=14 November 2013}}</ref> Maclachlan subsequently signed her to UMG for development.<ref name="hitquarters.com"/>

===2016–2018: ''Melodrama''===

At the [[2016 Brit Awards]] in February, Lorde and [[David Bowie]]'s final touring band gave a tribute performance of his 1971 song "[[Life on Mars (song)|Life on Mars]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/feb/25/david-bowie-brits-tribute-lorde-duncan-jones|title=David Bowie's son thanks Brits for 'beautiful' tribute by Lorde|work=The Guardian|date=25 February 2016|access-date=29 February 2016}}</ref> Pianist [[Mike Garson]], a frequent band member for Bowie, explained that Bowie's family and management selected Lorde because he admired her and felt she was "the future of music".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/mar/22/david-bowie-lorde-future-of-music|title=David Bowie saw Lorde as 'the future of music'|work=The Guardian|date=22 March 2016|access-date=23 February 2019}}</ref> Later that year, Lorde co-wrote "[[Heartlines]]", a song by New Zealand music duo [[Broods]] from their 2016 album ''[[Conscious (Broods album)|Conscious]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7401141/lorde-broods-heartlines-co-wrote|title=Lorde Returns! {{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/dec/04/the-top-100-tracks-of-2017|title=The top 100 tracks of 2017|work=The Guardian|date=4 December 2017|first=Ben|last=Beaumont-Thomas|access-date=1 February 2019}}</ref> It achieved moderate commercial success, reaching number one in New Zealand, number four in Australia and number nine in Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Lorde&titel=Green+Light&cat=s|title=Lorde – Green Light|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 February 2019}}<br />{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Lorde/chart-history/canadian-hot-100/song/1016733|title=Lorde – Green Light Chart History – Canadian Hot 100|work=Billboard|access-date=20 February 2019}}</ref> Later that month, she co-wrote and provided background vocals for American indie pop band [[Bleachers (band)|Bleachers]]'s song "[[Don't Take the Money]]",<ref>{{cite news |last1=Geslani |first1=Michelle |title=Bleachers and Lorde link up on new song 'Don't Take the Money' — listen |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/03/bleachers-and-lorde-link-up-on-new-song-dont-take-the-money-listen/ |access-date=29 December 2018 |work=Consequence of Sound |date=31 March 2017}}</ref> taken from their 2017 record ''[[Gone Now]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Legaspi |first1=Althea |title=Jack Antonoff Details Bleachers' 'Gone Now' LP |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jack-antonoff-details-bleachers-gone-now-lp-107182/ |access-date=17 March 2019 |work=Rolling Stone |date=14 April 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
From late 2013 to early 2016, Lorde was in a relationship with New Zealand photographer James Lowe.<ref>{{cite news|last=Roxborough|first=Scott|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6028981/lordes-boyfriend-on-singers-accomplishments-i-couldnt-be-prouder|title=Lorde's Boyfriend on Singer's accomplishments: 'I Couldn't Be Prouder'|date=27 March 2014|work=Billboard|access-date=9 September 2014}}<br>{{cite news|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/celebrities/75984352/Reports-Lorde-has-split-from-her-boyfriend-James-Lowe-after-three-years|title=Reports: Lorde has split from her boyfriend James Lowe after three years|date=19 January 2016|access-date=14 March 2018|website=Stuff.co.nz}}</ref> In January 2016, she relocated from Devonport to [[Herne Bay, New Zealand|Herne Bay]], an affluent suburb in Auckland, where she purchased a NZ$2.84 million house.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/news/article.cfm?c_id=3&objectid=11574655|title=Lorde pays $2.84m for her city villa|date=16 January 2016|newspaper=The New Zealand Herald|access-date=8 July 2017}}</ref> She holds dual New Zealand and Croatian citizenship.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Bayer|first=Kurt|date=12 September 2017|title=Pop star Lorde reveals she has dual citizenship with Croatia|language=en-NZ|work=The New Zealand Herald|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=11921395|access-date=5 July 2020|issn=1170-0777}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
Lorde has been involved in several philanthropic causes. "[[The Love Club (song)|The Love Club]]" was included in the 2013 charity album ''[[Songs for the Philippines]]'' to support the people in the Philippines who suffered from [[Typhoon Haiyan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.3news.co.nz/Lorde-features-on-Typhoon-Haiyan-charity-album/tabid/418/articleID/322873/Default.aspx|title=Lorde features on Typhoon Haiyan charity album|publisher=[[3News]]|date=26 November 2013|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6Q7NSBnwu?url=http://www.3news.co.nz/Lorde-features-on-Typhoon-Haiyan-charity-album/tabid/418/articleID/322873/Default.aspx|archive-date=6 June 2014}}</ref> In 2015, Lorde recorded "[[Team Ball Player Thing]]", a [[charity record|charity single]], as part of the [[supergroup (music)|supergroup]] Kiwis Cure Batten. All sales from the song went towards research for the cure of [[Batten disease]], a fatal [[neurodegeneration|neurodegenerative]] disorder.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hunt |first1=Elle |title=Lorde, Flight of the Conchords, Peter Jackson and All Blacks record charity song |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/sep/12/lorde-flight-of-the-conchords-peter-jackson-and-all-blacks-record-charity-song |access-date=10 September 2020 |work=The Guardian |date=11 September 2015}}</ref> Later that year, the singer was featured in the compilation album ''The Art of Peace: Songs for Tibet II'' to raise funds for the preservation of the [[Tibetan culture]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Kate Bush and Lorde feature on birthday album for Dalai Lama |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/music-news/11714461/Kate-Bush-and-Lorde-feature-on-birthday-album-for-Dalai-Lama.html |access-date=10 September 2020 |work=The Daily Telegraph |date=2 July 2015}}</ref> The following year, Lorde made a NZ$20,000 donation to Fuel the Need, a New Zealand charity that provides lunches for underprivileged schoolchildren.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kaufman |first1=Gil |title=Lorde Makes Big Donation to New Zealand Children's Charity |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7423875/lorde-donates-14k-to-new-zealand-children-charity |access-date=10 September 2020 |work=Billboard |date=30 June 2016}}</ref> In 2018, she donated NZ$5,000 to [[Starship Hospital]] to fund the purchase of "five new portable [[intraoperative neurophysiological monitoring|neurology monitors]]."<ref>{{cite news |title=Lorde donates $5000 to Starship Children's Hospital |url=https://www.newshub.co.nz/home/entertainment/2018/06/lorde-donates-5000-to-starship-children-s-hospital.html |access-date=10 September 2020 |publisher=Newshub |date=24 June 2018}}</ref> Lorde became a patron of MusicHelps, formerly the New Zealand Music Foundation, a musical charity helping New Zealanders who are vulnerable to or experiencing serious health issues, in November 2018.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lorde becomes patron of MusicHelps, a musical charity helping Kiwis in need |url=https://www.newshub.co.nz/home/entertainment/2018/11/lorde-becomes-patron-of-musichelps-a-musical-charity-helping-kiwis-in-need.html |access-date=23 May 2019 | publisher=[[Newshub]] |date=7 November 2018}}</ref>
