'''Luz para Todos''' ("Light for All") is a program of the [[Federation|Federal]] Government of [[Brazil]], launched in November 2003, with a goal of bringing [[electricity]] to more than 10 million rural people by the year 2008. It was initiated by [[Dilma Rousseff]], then [[Minister of Mines and Energy of Brazil]], operated by the large power utility company [[Eletrobras]], and executed by electricity [[Concession (contract)|concessionaires]] and [[cooperative]]s.<ref name="TemasBlog">Keith R., [http://www.temasactuales.com/temasblog/environmental-protection/energy-the-environment/brazils-light-for-all-program-promotes-renewable-energy/ "Brazil’s Light for All Program Promotes Renewable Energy"] ''The Temas Blog'', 27 February 2009. Retrieved: 8 November 2010.</ref>

The project promotes [[renewable energy]] as the most practical solution in remote areas. To encourage utilizing that kind of energy, the federal government pays up to 85% of the costs for renewable energy projects in those areas.<ref name="TemasBlog"/>

During the program execution, more families without power at home were located, and the program was extended to be completed in 2010.<ref name="OfficialSite">[http://luzparatodos.mme.gov.br/luzparatodos/asp/ "Programa Luz para Todos"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101124085014/http://luzparatodos.mme.gov.br/luzparatodos/asp/ |date=24 November 2010 }} ''Ministry of Mines and Energy of Brazil'' – web site. Retrieved: 8 November 2010</ref>
