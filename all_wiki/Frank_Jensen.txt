'''Frank Jensen''' (born 28 May 1961) is a former<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/frank-jensen-traekker-sig-efter-kraenkelsessager|title=Frank Jensen trækker sig efter krænkelsessager|date=2020-10-19|publisher=[[Danmarks Radio]]|lang=da}}</ref> [[Danes|Danish]] [[politician]] of the Danish [[Social Democrats (Denmark)|Social Democrats]] who served as [[Lord Mayor of Copenhagen]] between 1 January 2010 and 19 October 2020. He was [[Ministry of Science, Innovation and Higher Education (Denmark)|Minister for Research]] 27 September 1994 to 30 December 1996 in the [[Cabinet of Poul Nyrup Rasmussen II]] and [[Justice Minister of Denmark|Justice Minister]] from 30 December 1996 to 27 November 2001 in the [[Cabinet of Poul Nyrup Rasmussen III|Cabinets of Poul Nyrup Rasmussen III]] and [[Cabinet of Poul Nyrup Rasmussen IV|IV]].

==Early life and education==
Jensen is an educated economist and holds a master's degree in economics from [[Aalborg University]] which he received in 1986.<ref name=Frank>{{cite web| title=Om Frank Jensen| url=http://frank-jensen.dk/om-frank-jensen/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131014051249/http://frank-jensen.dk/om-frank-jensen/| url-status=dead| archive-date=2013-10-14| trans-title=About Frank Jensen| publisher=FrankJensen.dk| accessdate=2015-08-20| language=Danish}}</ref>

==Career==
When [[Mogens Lykketoft]] resigned as leader of the [[Social Democrats (Denmark)|Social Democrats]] after losing the [[2005 Danish parliamentary election]], Jensen and [[Helle Thorning-Schmidt]] were the two candidates to succeed him. The party members voted on 12 April 2005, electing Thorning-Schmidt as leader with 24,261 votes (53%) against 21,348 votes (47%) for Frank Jensen.

Jensen is the author of several scientific papers and articles. In 2018 he stated on the online magazine Impakter.com that Copenhagen has decreased its CO2 emissions by 40% since 2005.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://impakter.com/copenhagen-wants-inspire-cities-take-climate-action/|title=Copenhagen wants to inspire cities to take climate action|date=2018-11-19|website=Impakter|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-09}}</ref>

===Sexual harassing===
In 2011, Jensen was accused of trying to lick a female coworker at a [[julefrokost]] at the [[Copenhagen City Hall]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://archive.is/E9mXr|title=Sexchikane: Frank Jensen kyssede og slikkede|lang=da|date=2020-10-18|publisher=[[Ekstra Bladet]]}}</ref>

In 2020, Jensen admitted that he had been sexually harassing various women in the previous ~30 years of his career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/jeg-har-vaeret-en-kraenker-se-hele-frank-jensens-pressemoede-her|title='Jeg har været en krænker': Se hele Frank Jensens pressemøde her|date=2020-10-18|publisher=[[Danmarks Radio]]|lang=da}}</ref>

On 19 October 2020, Jensen announced he would stop in politics and step down as Lord Mayor and Deputy Leader as a result of the allegations.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Wismann |first1=Ida Marie Lomholt |title=Lars Weiss bliver fungerende overborgmester i København |url=https://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2020-10-19-lars-weiss-bliver-fungerende-overborgmester-i-koebenhavn |accessdate=19 October 2020 |work=nyheder.tv2.dk |agency=TV2 |date=19 October 2020 |language=da}}</ref>
