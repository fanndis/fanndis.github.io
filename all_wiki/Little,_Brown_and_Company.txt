'''Little, Brown and Company''' is an American publisher founded in 1837 by [[Charles Coffin Little]] and his partner, [[James Brown (publisher)|James Brown]], and for close to two centuries has published fiction and nonfiction by American authors. Early lists featured [[Emily Dickinson]]'s poetry and ''[[Bartlett's Familiar Quotations]]''. As of 2016, Little, Brown and Company is a division of the [[Hachette Book Group]].<ref name="175th Anniversary"/>

==19th century==
Little, Brown and Company had its roots in the book selling trade. It was founded in 1837 in Boston by Charles Little and James Brown.<ref name="175th Anniversary">{{Cite web|url=http://www.littlebrown.com/175.html|title=Little, Brown.com - 175th Anniversary|website=www.littlebrown.com|access-date=2016-05-01|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160513033053/http://www.littlebrown.com/175.html|archive-date=2016-05-13}}</ref><ref name="RVHudson">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QntiAAAAMAAJ|title=Mass Media: A Chronological Encyclopedia of Television, Radio, Motion Pictures, Magazines, Newspapers, and Books in the United States|last=Hudson|first=Robert Vernon|date=1987-01-01|publisher=Garland Publishing, Incorporated|isbn=9780824086954|language=en}}</ref> They formed the partnership "for the purpose of Publishing, Importing, and Selling Books."<ref name="175th Anniversary" /> It can trace its roots before that to 1784 to a bookshop owned by Ebenezer Batelle on Marlborough Street.<ref name="175th Anniversary" /> They published works of [[Benjamin Franklin]] and [[George Washington]] and they were specialized in legal publishing and importing titles.<ref name="Lynde">{{Cite news|title=Editorial Correspondence|last=Lynde|first=John H.|date=October 28, 1969|work=Whig & Courier}}</ref> For many years, it was the most extensive law publisher in the United States, and also the largest importer of standard English law and miscellaneous works, introducing American buyers to the ''[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]'',<ref name="Lynde" /> the dictionaries of [[William Smith (lexicographer)|William Smith]], and many other standard works.<ref name="appletons2">{{Appletons'|wstitle=Little, Charles Coffin|year=1892|inline=1}}</ref> In the early years Little and Brown published the ''Works of [[Daniel Webster]]'', [[George Bancroft]]'s ''History of the United States'',<ref name="Lynde" /> [[William H. Prescott]]'s ''Ferdinand and Isabella'', [[Jones Very]]'s first book of poetry (edited by [[Ralph Waldo Emerson]]), ''Letters of [[John Adams]]'' and works by [[James Russell Lowell]] and [[Francis Parkman]]. Little, Brown and Company was the American publisher for [[Edward Gibbon]]'s ''[[The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire|The Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire]]''.<ref name="175th Anniversary" /><ref name="Lynde" />

The firm was the original publisher of ''[[United States Statutes at Large]]'' beginning in 1845, under authority granted by a [[joint resolution of Congress]]. In 1874, Congress transferred the authority to publish the ''Statutes at Large'' to the [[United States Government Publishing Office|Government Printing Office]], which has been responsible for producing the set since that time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Statutes at Large|url=http://rs6.loc.gov/ammem/amlaw/lwsl.html|publisher=[[Library of Congress]]|access-date=6 June 2012}}</ref> {{USC|1|113}} still recognizes their edition of the laws and treaties of the United States are competent evidence of the several public and private Acts of Congress, treaties, and international agreements other than treaties of the United States.

In 1853, Little, Brown began publishing the works of British poets from [[Geoffrey Chaucer|Chaucer]] to [[William Wordsworth|Wordsworth]]. Ninety-six volumes were published in the series in five years.<ref name=Oliver>Oliver 1986</ref>

In 1859, [[John Bartlett (publisher)|John Bartlett]] became a partner in the firm. He held the rights to his ''[[Bartlett's Familiar Quotations|Familiar Quotations]]'', and Little, Brown published the 15th edition of the work in 1980, 125 years after its first publication. John Murray Brown, James Brown's son, took over when Augustus Flagg retired in 1884. In the 1890s, Little, Brown expanded into general publishing, including fiction. In 1896, it published ''[[Quo Vadis (novel)|Quo Vadis]]''. In 1898, Little, Brown purchased a list of titles from the [[Roberts Brothers (publishers)|Roberts Brothers]] firm.<ref name=Oliver/> 19th century employees included [[Charles Carroll Soule]].<ref>{{citation |title=Obituary |work=Publishers Weekly |date=January 11, 1913 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AOMxAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA107 }}</ref>

==20th century==
[[File:Little, Brown, and Company insignia.png|thumb|200px|alt=Triangular logo bearing the initials, "L, B & Co." and the subtitle, "Books, the best companions|A Little, Brown and Co. insignia used in 1906.]]

John Murray Brown died in 1908 and James W. McIntyre became managing partner. When McIntyre died in 1913, Little, Brown incorporated. In 1925, Little, Brown entered into an agreement to publish all [[Atlantic Monthly]] books. This arrangement lasted until 1985. During this time the joint Atlantic Monthly Press/Little Brown imprint published ''[[All Quiet on the Western Front]]'', [[Herge|Herge's]] ''[[The Adventures of Tintin]]'', [[James Truslow Adams]]'s ''The Adams Family'', [[Charles Nordhoff]] and [[James Norman Hall]]'s ''[[Mutiny on the Bounty (novel)|Mutiny on the Bounty]]'' and its sequels, [[James Hilton (novelist)|James Hilton]]'s ''[[Goodbye, Mr. Chips]]'', [[Walter D. Edmonds]]'s ''[[Drums Along the Mohawk (novel)|Drums Along the Mohawk]]'', [[William Least Heat-Moon]]'s ''[[Blue Highways]]'', [[Tracy Kidder]]'s ''[[The Soul of a New Machine]]'', and [[J. D. Salinger]]'s ''[[The Catcher in the Rye]]''.<ref name=Oliver/>

Salinger later terminated his contract with the publishing house sometime in the 1970s, though his novel was still published by Little, Brown.

Other prominent figures published by Little, Brown in the 20th and early 21st centuries have included [[Nagaru Tanigawa]], [[Donald Barthelme]], [[Louisa M. Alcott]], [[Catherine Drinker Bowen]], [[Bernie Brillstein]], [[Thornton Burgess]], [[Hortense Calisher]], [[Bruce Catton]], [[A. J. Cronin]], [[Peter De Vries]], [[J. Frank Dobie]], [[C. S. Forester]], [[John Fowles]], [[Malcolm Gladwell]], [[Pete Hamill]], [[Cynthia Harrod-Eagles]], [[Lillian Hellman]], [[Oliver Wendell Holmes, Sr.]], [[Henry Kissinger]], [[Elizabeth Kostova]], [[Norman Mailer]], [[William Manchester]], [[Nelson Mandela]], [[John P. Marquand]], [[Masters and Johnson]], [[Stephenie Meyer]], [[Rick Moody]], [[Ogden Nash]], [[Edwin O'Connor]], [[Erich Maria Remarque]], [[Alice Sebold]], [[David Sedaris]], [[George Stephanopoulos]], [[Gwyn Thomas (novelist)|Gwyn Thomas]], [[Gore Vidal]], [[David Foster Wallace]], [[Evelyn Waugh]], [[P. G. Wodehouse]], [[James Patterson]] and [[Herman Wouk]].  Little, Brown also published the photography of [[Ansel Adams]].<ref name=Oliver/>

The imprint was purchased by [[Time Inc.]] in 1968,<ref name=Oliver/> and was made part of the Time Warner Book Group when Time merged with Warner Communications to form [[Time Warner]] in 1989. All editing staff moved from Boston to Time Warner Book Group offices in New York City by 2001.<ref>{{Cite news| issn = 0743-1791| pages = –1| last = Mehegan| first = David| title = LITTLE, BROWN SHRINKING AGAIN: [THIRD Edition]| work = Boston Globe; Boston, Mass.| location = Boston, Mass., United States, Boston, Mass.| date = 2002-01-26| id = {{ProQuest|405447754}}}}</ref>

In 1996, Little, Brown's legal and medical publishing division was purchased by Wolters Kluwer.

In 2001, Michael Pietsch became Publisher of Little, Brown.

Little, Brown expanded into the UK in 1992 when TWBG bought [[MacDonald & Co]] from [[Maxwell Communications Corporation|Maxwell Communications]], taking on its Abacus (upmarket paperback) and [[Orbit Books|Orbit]] (science fiction) lists, and authors including [[Iain Banks]]. Feminist publisher [[Virago Press]] followed in 1996. Also in 1996, [[Wolters Kluwer]] acquired Little, Brown's professional division and incorporated it into its [[Aspen Publishers|Aspen]] and [[Lippincott Williams & Wilkins|Lippincott-Raven]] imprints.

==21st century==
In 2006, the Time Warner Book Group was sold to French publisher [[Hachette Livre]]. Following this, the Little, Brown imprint is used by Hachette Livre's U.S. publishing company, [[Hachette Book Group USA]].

In 2011, Little, Brown launched an imprint devoted to suspense publishing: [[Mulholland Books]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Little, Brown and Company Announces New Suspense Imprint’s Name and Launch List|url = http://www.bookbusinessmag.com/article/little-brown-company-announces-new-suspense-imprint-s-name-launch-list/|website = Book Business|access-date = 2016-02-08|language = en-US}}</ref> In February 2013, Reagan Arthur was selected to be publisher of Little, Brown, while closing her five-year-old imprint, Reagan Arthur Books.<ref>{{cite news |title=News Briefs: Week of February 11, 2013 |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/publisher-news/article/55896-news-briefs-week-of-february-11-2013.html |access-date=April 20, 2020 |work=Publishers Weekly |date=February 8, 2013 |language=en}}</ref>

In October 2017, Little, Brown started an unnamed imprint devoted to health, lifestyle, psychology, and science with the appointment of Tracy Behar as the imprint's vice president, publisher, and editor-in-chief.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Maher |first1=John |title=Tracy Behar Named Publisher, Editor-in-Chief of New Little, Brown Imprint |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/people/article/75237-behar-named-publisher-e-i-c-of-new-little-brown-imprint.html |access-date=April 20, 2020 |work=Publishers Weekly |date=October 30, 2017}}</ref> The imprint [[Little, Brown Spark]] launched in fall 2018.<ref>{{cite press release |title=Tracy Behar Names New Imprint, Little, Brown Spark |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/newsbrief/index.html?record=1748 |access-date=20 April 2020 |via=PublishersWeekly.com |publisher=Little, Brown |date=March 15, 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

In February 2020, [[Hachette Book Group]] acquired 1,000 titles for young readers from [[Disney Publishing Worldwide|Disney Book Group]] for [[Little, Brown and Company|Little, Brown Books for Young Readers]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Milliot |first1=Jim |title=HBG Buys More Than 1,000 Disney Book Group Titles |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/childrens/childrens-industry-news/article/82353-hbg-buys-more-than-1-000-disney-book-group-titles.html |access-date=April 20, 2020 |work=Publishers Weekly |date=February 6, 2020}}</ref>
