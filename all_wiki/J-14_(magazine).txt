'''''J-14''''' is a monthly [[teen magazine|teenage magazine]] marketed at preteen and teenage girls around age 11–19. It is one of the earliest teen celebrity magazines.<ref name=forbes>{{cite news|title=Top Children Magazines In 2012|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jasoncollazo/2012/03/06/top-children-magazines-in-2012/|accessdate=3 October 2013|work=Forbes|date=3 March 2012|author=Jason Collazo}}</ref> The magazine was among the top children's magazines in the 2012 list of ''[[Forbes]]''.<ref name=forbes/>

==History and profile==
''J-14'' was founded in 1998.<ref>{{cite web|title=Media Kit 2015|url=http://www.myintouchweekly.com/bauerpublishing/mediakits/BTG-MK-2015.pdf|work=My In Touch Weekly|accessdate=28 November 2015}}</ref> The first issue of the magazine was released in January 1999. It was started by [[Bauer Publishing]], the [[United States]] division of the [[Germany|German]] firm [[Bauer Verlagsgruppe]]. The contents of these magazines include features like teen gossip, quizzes, fashion, posters, and information on celebrities that pertain to the readers. The name of the publication is a sound-alike abbreviation of its tagline "Just For Teens".

The headquarters of ''J-14'' is in Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey.<ref>{{cite news|author=Nadine Cheung|title=J-14 Seeks New Editorial Team Member, Like, Seriously|url=http://www.adweek.com/fishbowlny/j-14-seeks-new-editorial-team-member-like-seriously/314416|accessdate=28 November 2015|work=FishbowlNY|date=9 February 2011}}</ref>

In April 2015, the [[Spanish language]] online edition of ''J-14'' was launched.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bauer Xcel Media Announces the WorldWide Launch of J-14 Espanol: es.j-14.com|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/bauer-xcel-media-announces-the-worldwide-launch-of-j-14-espanol-esj-14com-300060913.html|accessdate=28 November 2015|work=PR News}}</ref>

[[American Media, Inc.]] acquired Bauer's US children's magazines in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Bauer Media sells most of US portfolio – 17 titles down to just four| work = Mediaweek| accessdate = 2018-07-29| date = 2018-06-18| url = https://mediaweek.com.au/bauer-media-sells-us-portfolio-ami-2018/}}</ref>

==Circulation==
An annual survey in 2007 by [[Experian]] Simmons Research of [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]] found that ''J-14'' tied the former ''[[Nickelodeon Magazine]]'' among American girls 8–14 for familiarity, with nearly one in three girls in that age group surveyed saying they had read or looked at the magazine. Circulation was 217,183 copies in 2006.<ref name="NYT">Olson, Elizabeth [https://www.nytimes.com/2007/05/28/business/media/28fanzine.html OMG! Cute Boys, Kissing Tips and Lots of Pics, as Magazines Find a Niche], ''[[New York Times]]'', 28 May 2007, Retrieved 24 January 2009</ref>
