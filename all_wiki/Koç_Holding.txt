'''Koç Holding A.Ş.''' ({{IPA-tr|kotʃ}}) is the largest industrial [[Conglomerate (company)|conglomerate]] in [[Turkey]], and the only company in the country to enter the [[Fortune Global 500]] list as of 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://todayszaman.com/news-147240-koc-only-turkish-entry-on-fortune-global-500-list.html|title=Koç only Turkish entry on Fortune Global 500 list|work=TodaysZaman|accessdate=24 April 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160127064628/http://www.todayszaman.com/news-147240-koc-only-turkish-entry-on-fortune-global-500-list.html|archivedate=27 January 2016}}</ref> The company, headquartered in Nakkaştepe, [[Istanbul]],<ref>http://www.koc.com.tr/en-us/Corporate/Headquarters/Pages/Nakkastepe.aspx</ref> is controlled by the [[Koç family]], one of Turkey's wealthiest families.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.milliyet.com.tr/forbes-2010-yilinin-milyarderler-listesini-acikladi/ekonomi/sondakika/11.03.2010/1209900/default.htm|title=Forbes, 2010 yÄ±lÄ±nÄ±n milyarderler listesini aÃ§Ä±kladÄ±|date=11 March 2010|publisher=|accessdate=24 April 2015}}</ref>

The company was formed in 1963 when founder [[Vehbi Koç]], who established his first firm in 1926, combined all of the companies bearing his name into Koç Holding.<ref name="koc.com.tr">http://www.koc.com.tr/en-us/Corporate/History/Pages/1926_until_Today.aspx</ref>

== History ==
The first firm that was to become Koç Holding was established in 1926 by [[Vehbi Koç]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last=Salihovic|first=Elnur|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=aSa1DAAAQBAJ&q=Ko%C3%A7+Holding+was+established+in+1926+by+Vehbi+Ko%C3%A7.&pg=PA262|title=Major Players in the Muslim Business World|date=2015-10-05|publisher=Universal-Publishers|isbn=978-1-62734-052-6|language=en}}</ref>

In 1984, Vehbi Koç handed his position as chairman of the board over to his son [[Rahmi Koç|Rahmi M. Koç]].<ref name=":0" /> 

In September 1988, the company moved its headquarters from Fındıklı, Istanbul, to Nakkaştepe on the [[Anatolia]]n part of Istanbul.<ref name=":0" />

On April 4, 2003, Rahmi Koç retired and handed his position over to his eldest son [[Mustafa Vehbi Koç|Mustafa V. Koç]]. Rahmi Koç retained the title of honorary chairman and a seat on the board of directors.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=R-ztAAAAMAAJ&q=Rahmi+Ko%C3%A7+retired+and+handed+his+position+over+to+his+eldest+son+Mustafa+V.+Ko%C3%A7|title=The Economist|date=2003|publisher=Economist Newspaper Limited|language=en}}</ref>

In February 2015, Levent Çakıroğlu replaced [[Turgay Durak]] as CEO.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/koc-holding-ceo-idUSL5N0VR25C20150217 |title=UPDATE 1-Turkish conglomerate Koc Holding names new CEO |website=reuters.com |date=2015-02-17 |accessdate=2017-04-03}}</ref>

In 2016 Q1, [[Omer Koc]] became the chairman of the board following the death of Mustafa V. Koç.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ömer Koç becomes chairman of Turkey's largest conglomerate after death of brother Mustafa Koç|url=https://www.dailysabah.com/business/2016/02/22/omer-koc-becomes-chairman-of-turkeys-largest-conglomerate-after-death-of-brother-mustafa-koc|last=Wires|first=Daily Sabah with|date=2016-02-22|website=Daily Sabah|language=en|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref>

==Operations==
The shares of 16 Koç Group companies are traded on the [[Istanbul Stock Exchange]]; together, the groups comprise 113 companies, 90,000 employees, and 14,000 dealers, agencies, and after-sales services people.<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Yamak|first1=Sibel|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8Td4DgAAQBAJ&q=16+Ko%C3%A7+Group+companies+are+traded+on+the+Istanbul+Stock+Exchange&pg=PT84|title=A Primer on Corporate Governance: Turkey|last2=Ertuna|first2=Bengi|date=2017-03-22|publisher=Business Expert Press|isbn=978-1-60649-883-5|language=en}}</ref>
