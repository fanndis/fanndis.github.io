'''The Press Trust of India Ltd.''', commonly known as '''PTI''', is the largest [[news agency]] in India.<ref>[http://indianembassy.ru/cms/index.php?Itemid=592&id=539&option=com_content&task=view Embassy of India (Moscow) – NEWS AGENCIES] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090605095110/http://indianembassy.ru/cms/index.php?Itemid=592&id=539&option=com_content&task=view |date=5 June 2009 }}</ref> It is headquartered in [[New Delhi]] and is a [[nonprofit]] [[cooperative]] among more than 500 Indian [[newspaper]]s and has  about 500 full-time employees as of Jan 1,2020 including about 400 journalists and 500 part time correspondents located in most of the district headquarters in the country..<ref>{{cite web|title=Overview of PTU|url=http://www.ptinews.com/aboutpti/aboutus.aspx|publisher=Press Trust of India|accessdate=1 February 2014}}</ref> A few correspondents are based in major capitals and important business centres around the world. It took over the operations of the [[Associated Press|Associated Press of India]] from [[Reuters]] after [[India's independence]] in 1947.<ref name=about>[http://www.ptinews.com/aboutpti/aboutus.aspx About PTI], Press Trust of India, retrieved 14 March 2017.</ref><ref name=UNESCO>{{citation |title=News Agencies: Their Structure and Operation |url=http://unesdoc.unesco.org/images/0007/000734/073446eo.pdf |publisher=UNESCO |year=1953 |pages=16–18}}</ref> It provides news coverage and information of the region in both English and [[Hindi]]. <ref>{{Cite web|title=Communal attack in Bawana shared with false claim of Muslim man injecting fruits with spittle|url=https://www.altnews.in/communal-attack-in-bawan-shared-with-false-claim-of-muslim-man-injecting-fruits-with-spittle/|last=Mehta|first=Archit|date=2020-04-09|website=Alt News|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=2 Cases of Coronavirus Confirmed in Kolkata? No, Media Misreported|url=https://www.thequint.com/news/webqoof/two-cases-of-coronavirus-confirmed-in-kolkata-news-fact-check|date=2020-02-14|website=The Quint|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=PTI Misreports Maharashtra Home Minister On Palghar Lynching|url=https://www.boomlive.in/fake-news/pti-misreports-maharashtra-home-minister-on-palghar-lynching-7773|last=Alphonso|first=Anmol|date=2020-04-22|website=www.boomlive.in|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Has the UP Govt Slashed Funds for Education? Here's a Fact Check|url=https://www.thequint.com/politics/2017/07/19/up-government-slashed-funds-for-education|date=2017-07-19|website=The Quint|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref>. Its corporate office is located at [[Sansad Marg]], New Delhi and registered office in D N Road, [[Mumbai]].

==Overview                                                                                                ==
[[File:Press Trust of India 1999 stamp of India.jpg|thumb|A 1999 stamp dedicated to the 50th anniversary of PTI, featuring its logo on top.]]
It exchanges information with several other news agencies including 100 news agencies based outside India, such as [[Associated Press]], [[Agence France-Presse]], ''[[The New York Times]]'' and [[Bloomberg L.P.]]. Major Indian subscribers of PTI include ''[[The Hindu]]'', ''[[Times of India]]'', the ''[[Indian Express]]'', the ''[[Hindustan Times]]'', [[The Statesman (India)|The Statesman]], [[The Tribune (Chandigarh)|The Tribune]], the [[All India Radio]] and [[Doordarshan]]. PTI has offices in [[Bangkok]], [[Beijing]], [[Colombo]], [[Dubai]], [[Islamabad]], [[Kuala Lumpur]], [[Moscow]], [[New York City|New York]] and [[Washington D.C.]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.asianetnews.net/agencies/press-trust-of-india|title=AsiaNet – Agencies|website=www.asianetnews.net}}</ref>

Press Trust of India is the only news agency in [[South Asia]] which operates its own  [[communication satellite]], an [[INSAT]], to broadcast news and information.

Its current chairman is Aveek Sarkar, the Chairman of ABP group.<ref>https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/vijay-kumar-chopra-vineet-jain-elected-pti-chairman-vice-chairman/articleshow/71013872.cms</ref>
