'''Diane von Fürstenberg''' (born '''Diane Simone Michelle Halfin'''; 31 December 1946),<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.mylifetime.com/she-did-that/december-31-1946-diane-von-furstenberg-designer-of-the-wrap-dress-was-born |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181228174632/https://www.mylifetime.com/she-did-that/december-31-1946-diane-von-furstenberg-designer-of-the-wrap-dress-was-born |url-status=dead |archive-date=28 December 2018 |title=December 31, 1946: Diane von Fürstenberg, Designer of the Wrap Dress, Was Born |website=[[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]] |language=en |access-date=28 December 2018 }}</ref> is a Belgian [[fashion designer]] best known for her [[wrap dress]].<ref name="Diane von Furstenberg RTW Fall 2014">{{cite web |url=http://www.wwd.com/runway/fall-ready-to-wear-2014/review/diane-von-furstenberg?src=nl/mornReport/20140210 |title=Diane von Furstenberg RTW Fall 2014 |publisher=WWD |date=9 February 2014 |accessdate=10 February 2014}}</ref><ref name="voguepedia">{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/voguepedia/Diane_von_Furstenberg |title=Diane von Furstenberg |work=Vogue |accessdate=17 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120808022300/http://www.vogue.com/voguepedia/Diane_von_Furstenberg |archive-date=8 August 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Diane-von-Furstenberg|title=Diane von Furstenberg {{!}} Belgian-born American fashion designer and businesswoman|work=Encyclopedia Britannica|access-date=30 December 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Diane: A Signature Life|last=von Furstenberg|first=Diane|publisher=Simon and Schuster|year=2013}}</ref> She initially rose to prominence when she married into the German princely [[House of Fürstenberg]], as the wife of [[Prince Egon von Fürstenberg]]. Following their separation in 1972 and divorce in 1983, she has continued to use his family name.

Her fashion company, Diane von Furstenberg (DVF)<ref name="topics.nytimes.com">{{cite news |url=http://topics.nytimes.com/topics/reference/timestopics/people/v/diane_von_furstenberg |title=Diane von Furstenberg |work=The New York Times |accessdate=17 July 2012 |first=Matthew |last=Schneier}}</ref> is available in over 70 countries and 45 free-standing shops worldwide,<ref name="Guardian">Jess Cartner-Morley, [https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2014/jul/01/diane-von-furstenburg-stuido-54-google-wrap-dress-oprah-warhol-anna-wintour Diane von Furstenberg: "I danced at Studio 54. Now I work with Google"], theguardian.com, 1 July 2014.</ref> with the company's headquarters and [[flagship]] boutique located in [[Meatpacking District, Manhattan|Manhattan's Meatpacking District]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.meatpacking-district.com/locations/diane-von-furstenberg |title=Diane von Furstenberg |work=Meatpacking district |accessdate=16 July 2012}}</ref>

She is the past Chairwoman  of the [[Council of Fashion Designers of America]] (CFDA), a position she held from 2006 to 2019 ;<ref name ="voguepedia" /> in 2014 was listed as the 68th most powerful woman in the world by ''Forbes'';<ref name=Forbes14>{{cite web |title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women |url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/2/#tab:overall |website=Forbes |publisher=Forbes |accessdate=26 June 2014}}</ref> and in 2015 was included in the [[Time 100]], as an Icon, by ''[[Time Magazine]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/world/how-we-pick-the-time-100/ar-AAb5qjA |title=How We Pick the TIME 100 |accessdate=14 July 2016}}</ref> In 2016, she was awarded an honorary doctorate from [[The New School]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.newschool.edu/pressroom/pressreleases/2016/commencement2016.htm |title=LAVERNE COX, DIANE VON FURSTENBERG, DERAY MCKESSON NAMED HONORARY DEGREE RECIPIENTS BY THE NEW SCHOOL |date=31 March 2016 }}</ref> In 2019, she was inducted into the [[National Women's Hall of Fame]].<ref>[https://www.womenofthehall.org/inductee/diane-von-furstenberg-2/ National Women's Hall of Fame, Diane von Fürstenberg]</ref>

==Early years==
Fürstenberg was born Diane Simone Michelle Halfin in [[Brussels]], [[Belgium]] to [[Jews|Jewish]] parents.<ref>{{cite web |title=Top 50 most influential Jews 2013: Places 31–40 |url=http://www.jpost.com/Jewish-World/Jewish-Features/Top-50-most-influential-Jews-2013-Places-31-40-313147 |work=[[The Jerusalem Post]] |accessdate=19 October 2013}}</ref> Her father, [[Bessarabia]]n-born Leon (Lipa) Halfin, migrated to Belgium in 1929 from [[Chişinău]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.forward.com/articles/2610 |title=Interview with Diane von Furstenberg |work=Forward|accessdate=22 September 2010}}</ref> Her mother was [[Greeks|Greek]]-born Liliane Nahmias, a [[Holocaust]] survivor.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/diane-von-furstenberg-icon-1970s|last1=Kuczynski|first1=Alex|title=Diane von Furstenberg|accessdate=21 April 2013|date=1 November 2012|work=[[W (magazine)|WMagazine.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Diane Von Furstenberg – MAKERS PROFILE |url=http://www.makers.com/diane-von-furstenberg |work=[[Makers: Women Who Make America]] |accessdate=20 March 2014}}</ref> 18 months before Fürstenberg was born, her mother was a prisoner at [[Auschwitz concentration camp]]. Fürstenberg has spoken broadly about her mother's influence in her life, crediting her with teaching her that "fear is not an option".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ujafedny.org/stories/view/46-diane-von-furstenberg-on-her-work |title=Diane von Furstenberg on Her Work |work=Ujafedny |url-status=dead |accessdate=16 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121113224719/http://www.ujafedny.org/stories/view/46-diane-von-furstenberg-on-her-work/ |archive-date=13 November 2012 }}</ref>

Fürstenberg attended a boarding school in Oxfordshire.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/esmagazine/my-london-diane-von-furstenberg-a3996321.html|title=My London: Diane Von Furstenberg|date=21 November 2018|website=Evening Standard}}</ref> She studied at [[Complutense University of Madrid|Madrid University]] before transferring to the [[University of Geneva]] to study [[economics]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/spy/biographies/diane-von-furstenberg |title=Diane von Furstenberg profile |accessdate=14 July 2016}}</ref> She then moved to Paris and worked as an assistant to [[Fashion photography|fashion photographer]]'s agent Albert Koski.<ref name="voguepedia" /> She left Paris for Italy to apprentice to the textile manufacturer [[Angelo Ferretti]] in his factory, where she learned about cut, color and fabric.<ref name="voguepedia" /> It was here that she designed and produced her first silk jersey dresses.

==Career and brand==
[[File:Diane von Fürstenberg.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.8|alt=A woman with wavy and curly hair wearing a white dress with multi-colored stripes smiles on a fashion runway|von Fürstenberg at the 2008 [[New York Fashion Week]]]]
A year after marrying, Fürstenberg began designing women's clothes:  "The minute I knew I was about to be Egon's wife, I decided to have a career. I wanted to be someone of my own, and not just a plain little girl who got married beyond her deserts."<ref name="Maynard1977"/> After the Fürstenbergs separated in 1973, Egon also became a fashion designer.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wohlfert-Wihlborg|first1=Lee|title=The Original Von Furstenberg, Egon, Wakes Up to His Own Potential|url=http://people.com/archive/the-original-von-furstenberg-egon-wakes-up-to-his-own-potential-vol-16-no-25/|accessdate=27 June 2017|work=PEOPLE.com|issue=25|date=21 December 1981|volume=16}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Rourke|first1=Mary|title=Egon von Furstenberg, 57; Gave Up Banking Career for Fashion Design|url=https://articles.latimes.com/2004/jun/12/local/me-egon12|accessdate=27 June 2017|work=Los Angeles Times|date=12 June 2004}}</ref> After moving to New York, she met high-profile Vogue editor [[Diana Vreeland]], who declared her designs "absolutely smashing". She had her name listed on the Fashion Calendar for [[New York Fashion Week]], and so her business was created.<ref name="voguepedia" />

In 1974, she introduced the knitted [[Jersey (fabric)|jersey]] "[[wrap dress]]", which became an iconic piece in women's fashion; it is included in the collection of the Costume Institute of the Metropolitan Museum of Art.<ref name="Diane von Furstenberg RTW Fall 2014" /><ref name="topics.nytimes.com" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.instyle.com/instyle/fashiondesigners/keymoments/0,,20225945_diane-von-furstenberg_20237857,00.html |title=Madonna's New Video "Turn Up the Radio" |work=In Style |accessdate=17 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090715031908/http://www.instyle.com/instyle/fashiondesigners/keymoments/0,,20225945_diane-von-furstenberg_20237857,00.html |archive-date=15 July 2009 |url-status=dead  }}</ref><ref name=huff>Amanda Christine Miller,[https://www.huffingtonpost.com/amanda-christine-miller/diane-von-furstenberg-on-_b_81590.html "Diane von Furstenberg On Wrap Dresses And The Joys Of Aging Gracefully"], HuffingtonPost.com, 28 March 2008.</ref> After the success of the wrap dress, Furstenberg was featured on the cover of ''[[Newsweek]]'' magazine in 1976.<ref>[http://www.vintagefashionguide.com/2014/09/iconic-wrap-dress-diane-von-furstenberg/ "The Iconic Wrap Dress : Diane von Furstenberg"], 09/21/14, vintagefashionguide.com</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1998/12/01/style/01iht-fash.t.html |title=The Charmed Lives and Free Spirit of Diane Von Furstenberg: It's a Wrap: The Image of an Era |work=The New York Times |date=1 December 1998 |accessdate=17 July 2012 |first=Suzy |last=Menkes}}</ref> The accompanying article declared her "the most marketable woman since [[Coco Chanel]]."<ref name="nymag.com/fashion">{{cite web |url=https://nymag.com/fashion/fashionshows/designers/bios/dianevonfurstenberg |title=Diane Von Furstenberg – Designer Fashion Label |work=New York |accessdate=17 July 2012}}</ref> She launched a cosmetic line and her first fragrance, "Tatiana", named after her daughter.<ref name="nymag.com/fashion" /> [[The New York Times]] reported that by 1979 the annual retail sales for the company were $150 million.<ref name="voguepedia" />

In 1985, Fürstenberg moved to [[Paris]], [[France]] where she founded Salvy, a French-language publishing house.<ref name="voguepedia" /> Fürstenberg started a number of other businesses including a line of cosmetics and a home-shopping business, which she launched in 1991. In 1992, Fürstenberg sold $1.2 million dollars of her Silk Assets collection in two hours on [[QVC]].<ref name="nymag.com/fashion" /> She credits the success with giving her the confidence to relaunch her company.{{citation needed|date=August 2016}}

Fürstenberg relaunched her company in 1997, and reintroduced the wrap dress, which gained traction with a new generation of women. In 1998, she published her business memoir, ''Diane: A Signature Life''.<ref name="voguepedia" /> In 2004, she introduced the DVF by H. Stern fine jewelry collection, and launched scarves and beachwear. In 2006, she was elected as president of the Council of Fashion Designers of America, a position she still holds. In 2008, she received a star on Seventh Avenue's Fashion Walk of Fame.<ref name="voguepedia" />

In 2009, [[Michelle Obama]] wore the DVF signature Chain Link print wrap dress on the official White House Christmas card.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2011/05/22/sunday/main20065126.shtml |title=One dress changed Diane von Furstenberg's life |accessdate=18 July 2012 |publisher=CBS News}}</ref> That same year, a large-scale retrospective exhibition entitled "Diane von Furstenberg: Journey of a Dress" opened at the Manezh, one of Moscow's largest public exhibition spaces. It was curated by Andre Leon Talley and attracted a lot of media attention. In 2010, the exhibition traveled to [[São Paulo]]; and in 2011, to the Pace Gallery in [[Beijing]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/12/19/fashion/19Diane.html |title=Diane Von Furstenberg and China: A Perfect Fit? |accessdate=17 July 2012 |work=The New York Times |first=David |last=Barboza |date=17 December 2010}}</ref>

In 2010, Fürstenberg was awarded a Gold Medal at the annual Queen Sofia Spanish Institute Gold Medal Gala.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://spanishinstitute.org/about/gala |title=Gala – Queen Sofía |publisher=Spanish Institute |accessdate=17 July 2012}}</ref> In 2011, DVF introduced a home collection, and a signature fragrance, DIANE.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.harpersbazaar.co.uk/beauty-health/diane-von-furstenberg-flash-mob-001 |title=Diane von Furstenberg celebrates fragrance launch with flash mob |work=Harper's Bazaar |place=[[United Kingdom|UK]] |accessdate=18 July 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130420163215/http://www.harpersbazaar.co.uk/beauty-health/diane-von-furstenberg-flash-mob-001 |archive-date=20 April 2013 }}</ref>

In 2012, Fürstenberg launched her first children’s collection with GapKids <ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nbcnewyork.com/blogs/threadny/THREAD-Diane-von-Furstenberg-Fetes-New-Gap-Kids-Line-Set-to-Launch-March-15-141407923.html |title=Diane von Furstenberg Fetes New Gap Kids Line, Set to Launch March 15 |work=NBC New York |accessdate=18 July 2012}}</ref> and a denim collaboration with CURRENT/ELLIOTT.<ref>{{cite news |date=2 February 2012 |url=https://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/02/02/now-collaborating-diane-von-furstenberg-currentelliot |title=Now Collaborating – Diane Von Furstenberg + Current/Elliot |work=T magazine |accessdate=18 July 2012 |first=Chelsea |last=Zalopany}}</ref>

Her clothes have been worn by many celebrities including [[Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge]], [[Gwyneth Paltrow]], [[Kate Beckinsale]], [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]], [[Tina Brown]], [[Jessica Alba]], [[Susan Sarandon]] and [[Jennifer Lopez]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/07/08/kate-middleton-los-angeles_n_893633.html |title=Kate Middleton Wears Roksanda Ilincic, DVF & Jenny Packham In California |work=The Huffington Post |date=8 July 2011 |accessdate=18 July 2012 |first=Hilary |last=Moss}}</ref>
[[Google Glass]] made its New York Fashion Week Debut at the designer's Spring 2013 fashion show.<ref>{{Citation |title=NY Fashion week: Diane von Furstenburg (sic) |date=September 2012 |url=http://www.msfabulous.com/2012/09/ny-fashion-week-diane-von-furstenburg.html |work=MS fabulous}}.</ref>

In 2014, Fürstenberg joined the [[Ban Bossy]] campaign as a spokesperson advocating leadership roles for girls.<ref>{{cite news |author=Jolie Lee |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation-now/2014/03/10/ban-bossy-sheryl-sandberg-beyonce-gardner/6262309 |title=Beyonce, Jennifer Garner, Jane Lynch join 'Ban Bossy" campaign |work=USA Today |date=10 May 2014 |accessdate=8 August 2014}}</ref><ref name="NYPost1">{{cite web |title=Facebook COO Sandberg's ludicrous crusade against bossy |url=https://nypost.com/2014/03/17/facebook-coo-sandbergs-ludicrous-crusade-against-bossy |work=[[New York Post]] |accessdate=20 March 2014 |date=17 March 2014}}</ref><ref name="NYDNews1">{{cite web |title=Beyoncé, Jennifer Garner, Jane Lynch join prominent women in #BanBossy campaign |url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/beyonce-jennifer-garner-join-banbossy-campaign-article-1.1716554#xWipvjtst1sqRs3J.97 |work=[[New York Daily News]] |accessdate=8 August 2014 |date=10 March 2014}}</ref>

In 2018, the brand banned [[mohair]] use after a [[People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals|PETA]] exposé showed workers mutilating and killing goats to obtain it.<ref>Lauretta Roberts, [https://www.theindustry.fashion/diane-von-furstenberg-bans-mohair-after-disturbing-peta-expose/ "Diane Von Furstenberg bans mohair after disturbing PETA exposé"], ''The Industry'', 19 July 2018.</ref> All fur, angora and exotic skins were also banned from future collections.<ref>Alice Newbold, [https://www.vogue.co.uk/article/diane-von-furstenberg-to-stop-using-fur "Diane Von Furstenberg To Stop Using Fur"], ''Vogue'', 4 October 2018.</ref>

==Philanthropy==
Fürstenberg is a director of The Diller – von Furstenberg Family Foundation, which provides support to nonprofit organizations in the area of community building, education, human rights, arts, health and the environment.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dvfff.org/directors |publisher=The Diller – von Furstenberg Family Foundation |title=Directors |accessdate=19 April 2012}}</ref> The foundation supports ''[[The DVF Awards]]'', presented annually to four women who display leadership, strength and courage in their commitment to women's causes.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dvf.com/The-DVF-Awards/philanthropy-awards,default,pg.html |work=The DFV awards |title=Philanthropy |publisher=DvF |place=USA |accessdate=19 April 2012}}</ref> In 2011, the foundation made a $20 million commitment to the [[High Line]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thehighline.org/news/2011/10/27/diller-%E2%80%93-von-furstenberg-family-foundation-makes-historic-20m-commitment-to-the-futu |title=Diller – von Furstenberg Family Foundation Makes Historic $20M Commitment to the Future of |publisher=The High Line |date=27 October 2011 |accessdate=19 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425135522/http://thehighline.org/news/2011/10/27/diller-%E2%80%93-von-furstenberg-family-foundation-makes-historic-20m-commitment-to-the-futu |archive-date=25 April 2012 }}</ref>

Fürstenberg sits on the board of [[Vital Voices]], a women’s leadership organization,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vitalvoices.org/about-us/board-directors |title=Board of Directors |publisher=Vital Voices |accessdate=19 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006122554/http://www.vitalvoices.org/about-us/board-directors |archive-date=6 October 2014 }}</ref> and served as one of the project chairs for New York City Mayor [[Michael Bloomberg]]'s review of the future of NYC's Fashion industry,<ref>{{cite book|author-link1=Steven Strauss |last1=Strauss |first1=Steven |title=Fashion.NYC.2020 |year=2012 |publisher=NYCEDC |url=http://www.nycedc.com/system/files/files/resource/Fashion_study.pdf |first2=Kristy |last2=Sundjaja |first3=Meghana |last3=Gandhi |first4=Victor |last4=Wong |first5=Jennifer |last5=Yoo}}</ref> which was prepared by [[New York City Economic Development Corporation|NYCEDC]].

In 2016, Fürstenberg designed shirts for [[Hillary Clinton]]'s [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2016|presidential campaign]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://shop.hillaryclinton.com/products/diane-von-furstenberg-tee |title=Diane von Furstenberg Tee |accessdate=14 July 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160818175645/https://shop.hillaryclinton.com/products/diane-von-furstenberg-tee |archive-date=18 August 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/13434085/hillary-clinton-dvf-eva-fehren-campaign-t-shirts |title=Diane von Furstenberg and Eva Fehren Join Hillary Clinton's Made for History T-Shirt Project |first=Steff |last=Yotka |accessdate=14 July 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Diane von Furstenberg and Barry Diller Shankbone NYC 2009.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.8|alt=A woman with wavy and curly hair wearing a white dress lined with a teal fabric smiles as she looks to her right while standing next to a man wearing a tuxedo|von Fürstenberg with her second husband [[Barry Diller]] at the 2009 [[Metropolitan Opera]] premiere]]
At university, when she was 18, she met [[Prince Egon von Fürstenberg]], the elder son of Prince Tassilo zu Fürstenberg, a German Roman Catholic prince, and his first wife, [[Clara Agnelli]], an heiress to the [[Fiat]] automotive fortune and member of the [[Italian nobility]]. Married in 1969,<ref name=huff /> the couple had two children, [[Prince Alexander von Fürstenberg|Alexander]]<ref name="Maynard1977">{{cite news |last=Maynard |first=Joyce |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1977/02/16/archives/the-princess-who-is-everywhere-von-furstenberg-the-princess-who-is.html |title=The Princess Who is Everywhere |work=The New York Times |date=16 February 1977}}</ref> and [[Tatiana von Fürstenberg|Tatiana]], who were born in [[New York City]]. She is now the grandmother of five, including [[Talita von Fürstenberg]].

The Fürstenbergs' marriage, although unpopular with the groom's family because of the bride's Jewish ethnicity, was considered dynastic, and on her marriage she became Her Serene Highness Princess Diane of Fürstenberg.<ref>{{Citation |language=de |title=Genealogisches Handbuch Des Adels: Fürstliche Häuser |trans-title=Genealogical Handbook of the nobility: Princely Houses |publisher=CA Starke |year=1991 |page=261}}</ref>{{Better source|date=August 2020|reason=entry in royalty genealogy book not sufficient to support style}} However, she lost any claim to the title following their separation in 1972 and divorce in 1983.<ref>{{cite news |last=Morris |first=Bernardine |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1975/04/18/archives/fashion-talk-basic-dresses-in-sexy-prints-and-washable.html |title=Basic Dresses in Sexy Prints – and Washable |work=The New York Times |date=18 April 1975}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.townandcountrymag.com/leisure/a9588648/diane-von-furstenberg-dvf-facts/|title=7 Things You Never Knew About Diane von Fürstenberg|magazine=Town & Country|last=Robinson|first=Katie|date=2 June 2017|access-date=10 April 2020}}</ref>

In 2001, she married American media mogul [[Barry Diller]].<ref name="Maynard1977" />

On 28 February 2020, Fürstenberg was made a Chevalier de la [[Legion of Honour|Legion D’Honneur]] for her contributions to fashion, women's leadership, and philanthropy. She was presented the award by [[Christine Lagarde]], president of the [[European Central Bank]], in a ceremony at the [[Ministry of Europe and Foreign Affairs]] headquarters on the [[Quai d'Orsay]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/diane-von-furstenberg-france-legion-of-honor|title=Diane von Furstenberg Receives the Chevalier de la Legion d'Honneur From Christine Lagarde in Paris|magazine=Vogue|last=Phelps|first=Nicole|date=29 February 2020|access-date=10 April 2020}}</ref>

Details of her ancestry were included in an episode of the [[PBS]] show ''[[Finding Your Roots]]''. <ref>https://www.pbs.org/weta/finding-your-roots/about/meet-our-guests/diane-von-furstenberg</ref>
