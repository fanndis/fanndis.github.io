[[Tennis]] was part of the [[Summer Olympic Games]] program from the inaugural [[1896 Summer Olympics]], but was dropped after the [[1924 Summer Olympics]] due to disputes between the [[International Tennis Federation|International Lawn Tennis Federation]] and the [[International Olympic Committee]] over how to define amateur players.<ref>{{cite web | author=Soltis, Greg | date=July 27, 2012 |  url=http://www.livescience.com/2782-olympic-events-history.html | title=Olympic Events Through History | publisher=[[LiveScience]] | access-date=2012-08-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | author=Williams, Wythe | date=July 27, 1928 | url=https://www.nytimes.com/1928/07/27/archives/soccer-and-tennis-barred-in-olympics-international-body-by-1614.html | title=SOCCER AND TENNIS BARRED IN OLYMPICS | publisher=[[The New York Times]] | access-date=2012-08-01}}</ref> After two appearances as a [[demonstration sport]] in 1968 and 1984 (with a U-21 age limit),<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.itftennis.com/olympics/history/history/overview.aspx | title=Olympic Tennis Event – History: Overview | publisher=[[International Tennis Federation]] | access-date=2012-08-01}}</ref> it returned as a full medal sport at the [[1988 Summer Olympics]] open for all players regardless of their age and status and has been played at every edition of the Games since then.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/10/02/sports/2-more-olympic-games.html | title=2 More Olympic Games | publisher=[[The New York Times]] | date=October 2, 1981 | access-date=2012-08-01}}</ref>

[[File:Victoria Azarenka, Serena Williams and Maria Sharapova with medals 2012.jpg|right|thumb|2012 Women's Singles medalists, [[Serena Williams]] (''center''), [[Maria Sharapova]] (''right'') and [[Victoria Azarenka]] (''left'').]]

In 1896, 1900, 1904, 1988, and 1992, semifinal losers shared bronze medals. In all other years, a playoff match for the bronze medal was staged.

In the past, results from the Olympics used to have ranking points that the [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP]] and [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] added to their players annual totals in singles for that calendar year. This occurred from the [[2004 Summer Olympics|2004 Athens]] until the [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012 London]] editions. No points were awarded for the editions prior and for the [[2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Rio Olympics]]. While the ranking points amount did not equate with those given at the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments, the Olympic tournaments have increased in perceived importance since their reintroduction, with some players, critics and sports pundits considering winning the gold at the Olympics just as prestigious as winning a Grand Slam title.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.letsecondserve.com/2012/07/olympics-or-slams-whats-more-important.html|title=Olympics or Slams – What's More Important For Tennis Players? |publisher=Let, Second Serve|date=July 12, 2012|access-date=April 5, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.worldtennismagazine.com/archives/8213|title=How Important Is an Olympic Gold Medal in Tennis?|author=Paul Fein|publisher=World Tennis Magazine|date=September 20, 2012|access-date=October 29, 2013}}</ref>

[[Serena Williams]] and [[Venus Williams]] have each won a record four gold medals, three each as a doubles pairing, the only players to win the same Olympic event on three occasions. [[Kathleen McKane Godfree]] (one gold, two silvers, and two bronzes) and [[Venus Williams]] (four gold, one silver) are the all-time record holders for the most Olympic medals in tennis. [[Andy Murray]] is the only player to have won two singles gold medals, and the only singles player to have retained the Olympic title.  [[Gigi Fernández]] and [[Mary Joe Fernández]] and Serena and Venus Williams achieved this feat in women’s doubles in 1992 and 1996, and 2008 and 2012 respectively.

A player who wins an Olympic gold medal and all four [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] events in the same year is said to have won a [[Golden Slam]]. As of 2016, [[Steffi Graf]] is the only player to have  completed this achievement.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Tignor|first1=Steve|title=1988: Steffi Graf wins the Golden Slam|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2015/07/1988-steffi-graf-wins-golden-slam/55786/|access-date=17 September 2017|publisher=[[Tennis (magazine)|Tennis.com]]|date=30 July 2015}}</ref>

== Surface ==

The playing surface of the court varies between Olympic Games. It has been on [[hard court]] for every game since 1984 except for the [[Tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics|1992 Olympics]] (which was on a [[clay court]]) and the [[Tennis at the 2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Olympics]] (which was played on a [[grass court]]). The changing playing surface gives certain players different advantages and disadvantages not seen in most other Olympic sports.
