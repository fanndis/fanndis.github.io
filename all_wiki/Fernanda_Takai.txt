'''Fernanda Barbosa Takai''' (born August 25, 1971) is a [[Brazil]]ian singer, better known as the lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist of rock band [[Pato Fu]]. She has also been working on a solo career since 2007.

In 2011, she collaborated with [[Atom™]], Toshiyuki Yasuda and [[Moreno Veloso]] on the track "Águas de Março" for the [[Red Hot Organization]]'s most recent charitable album ''Red Hot+Rio 2.'' The album is a follow-up to the 1996 ''[[Red Hot + Rio]]''. Proceeds from the sales will be donated to raise awareness and money to fight AIDS/HIV and related health and social issues.

==Life and career==
Born in [[Serra do Navio]], Amapá, Takai was raised in [[Belo Horizonte]], Minas Gerais. She is half [[Portuguese people|Portuguese]] descent from her mother's side and half [[Japanese people|Japanese]] descent from her father's side.<ref>[http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/folha/ilustrada/ult90u364469.shtml Fernanda Takai "despertou" para idioma japonês já adulta]</ref> Since the year 2007, she's been working on a solo career, but hasn't left Pato Fu.
