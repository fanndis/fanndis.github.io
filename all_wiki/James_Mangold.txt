'''James Mangold''' (born December 16, 1963) is an American film and [[television director]], [[screenwriter]] and producer. He is best known<ref>[https://www.bfi.org.uk/news-opinion/news-bfi/interviews/logan-wolverine-james-mangold-influences The films that influenced Logan: director James Mangold on the new Wolverine movie | BFI<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> for the films ''[[Cop Land]]'' (1997), ''[[Girl, Interrupted (film)|Girl, Interrupted]]'' (1999), ''[[Walk the Line]]'' (2005), ''[[The Wolverine (film)|The Wolverine]]'' (2013) and ''[[Logan (film)|Logan]]'' (2017), the last of which earned him a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay]].<ref>[https://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2018 2018|Oscars.org]</ref> He then directed and produced the sports drama film ''[[Ford v Ferrari]]'' (2019), which earned him a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Picture]].<ref>[https://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2020 2020|Oscars.org]</ref>

==Early life==
Mangold was born in [[New York City]] in 1963, and is the son of artists [[Robert Mangold]] and [[Sylvia Plimack Mangold]].<ref name=cineaste>{{cite news|url=http://www.cineaste.com/articles/an-interview-with-james-mangold.htm |title=Avoiding Labels and Lullabies: An Interview with James Mangold |first=John |last=Esther |work=[[Cineaste (magazine)|Cineaste]] |volume=33 |issue=1 |date=Winter 2007 |access-date=July 21, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120206122326/http://www.cineaste.com/articles/an-interview-with-james-mangold.htm |archive-date=February 6, 2012 }}</ref> He was raised in [[New York State]]'s [[Hudson River Valley]] and graduated from [[Washingtonville High School]].<ref name=cineaste /><ref name="Ivry 00" />

He was admitted into and later attended the [[California Institute of the Arts]] film/video program.<ref name="Ivry 00" /> While there, he mentored under [[Alexander Mackendrick]]. During Mangold's third year, Mackendrick suggested that he should study at CalArts School of Theater as an actor, alongside his regular film studies.<ref>''James Mangold Interview on ''Sammy Going South'' DVD</ref>

==Career==
In 1985, Mangold secured a writer/director deal at [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]].<ref name="Ivry 00" /> He wrote a television movie and co-wrote the animated feature ''[[Oliver and Company]]''.<ref name="Ivry 00" /> A few years later, Mangold moved to New York and applied to [[Columbia University]]'s film school,<ref name="Ivry 00" /> where he graduated with an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] in film.<ref>[http://arts.columbia.edu/film/james-mangold James Mangold | Columbia University School of the Arts] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212030501/http://arts.columbia.edu/film/james-mangold |date=December 12, 2013 }}. Arts.columbia.edu (June 24, 2010). Retrieved on March 11, 2017.</ref> While there, he studied under [[Miloš Forman]] and developed both ''Heavy'' and ''Cop Land''.

He has worked as a feature writer and director since 1995, when his first feature, the independent film ''[[Heavy (film)|Heavy]]'', won the best directing prize at the [[Sundance Film Festival]].

Mangold subsequently wrote and directed ''[[Cop Land]]'' (1997), starring [[Sylvester Stallone]], [[Robert De Niro]], [[Harvey Keitel]], and [[Ray Liotta]];<ref>{{cite news|last=Blair|first=Ian|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1309&dat=19970821&id=5v1OAAAAIBAJ&pg=5953,286323 |title=Mangold gets big names to come on board|work=[[New Straits Times]]|date=August 21, 1997|page=5|accessdate=July 22, 2013}}</ref> ''[[Girl, Interrupted (film)|Girl, Interrupted]]'',<ref name="Ivry 00">{{cite news|last=Ivry|first=Bob|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1955&dat=20000111&id=DYs0AAAAIBAJ&pg=4204,6160375 |title=Director brought edge to 'chick flick'|work=[[Reading Eagle]]|date=January 11, 2000|page=D5|accessdate=July 22, 2013}}</ref> which won the Best Supporting Actress Oscar in 1999 for [[Angelina Jolie]]; ''[[Kate & Leopold]]'',<ref>{{cite news|last=Turner|first=Miki|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1346&dat=20011226&id=jtxOAAAAIBAJ&pg=6377,3897235 |title=Hugh Jackman's Charm|work=[[Lakeland Ledger]]|date=December 26, 2001|page=D5|accessdate=July 22, 2013}}</ref> starring [[Meg Ryan]] and [[Hugh Jackman]], for which Jackman was nominated for a Golden Globe as best actor in a musical or comedy in 2001, and the 2003 thriller ''[[Identity (film)|Identity]]'' which starred [[John Cusack]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Tinsley|first=Brad|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1876&dat=20030912&id=UQooAAAAIBAJ&pg=3900,4343753 |title='Identity' is a hair-raiser; 'Anger Management' frustrates (Review)|work=[[Spartanburg Herald-Journal]]|date=September 12, 2003|page=D7|accessdate=July 22, 2013}}</ref>

In 2005, Mangold co-wrote (with [[Gill Dennis]]) and directed ''[[Walk the Line]]'', a film about the young life of singer-songwriter [[Johnny Cash]] and his relationship with [[June Carter Cash]]. It stars [[Joaquin Phoenix]] and [[Reese Witherspoon]] and was released on November 18, 2005. He also served as producer under his production banner, Tree Line Film.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/exec/james-mangold|title=James Mangold|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}}</ref> It was nominated for five Oscars and Witherspoon won Best Actress for her performance as June Carter Cash.

Mangold also appeared as an actor in ''[[The Sweetest Thing]]'' as a doctor and love interest to [[Christina Applegate]] as well as in his own ''[[Kate & Leopold]]'' playing a movie director.

In 2009, Mangold was signed by Universal Pictures to direct ''Juliet,'' based on a novel by the Danish author [[Anne Fortier]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/wherefore-art-thou-juliet-uni-77225|title=Wherefore art thou, 'Juliet'? It's at Uni|first=Borys |last=Kit |date=January 6, 2009|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|language=en|access-date=August 15, 2019}}</ref> In 2011 Paramount acquired the rights<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.firstshowing.net/2011/paramount-sets-james-mangold-to-direct-adaptation-of-juliet-novel/|title=Paramount Sets James Mangold to Direct Adaptation of 'Juliet' Novel|first=Ethan |last=Anderton|date=September 8, 2011|website=FirstShowing.net|language=en-US|access-date=August 15, 2019}}</ref> of the movie with the writers [[Eyal Podell]], Jonathon E. Stewart, Aryan Saha, Chad and Dara Creasey on board to develop the script.
[[File:James Mangold by Gage Skidmore.jpg|right|thumb|Mangold at the San Diego Comic-Con to promote ''The Wolverine'', 2013.]]
In June 2011, Mangold was hired, initially just to direct the X-Men movie ''[[The Wolverine (film)|The Wolverine]]''. Along with screenwriters [[Christopher McQuarrie]], [[Scott Frank]] and Mark Bomback, Mangold also adapted the screenplay based upon [[Frank Miller (comics)|Frank Miller]] and [[Chris Claremont]]'s [[Wolverine (comic book)|Japanese Wolverine saga]] and entered production in Japan and Australia in July 2012. He completed photography in November of the same year. On release, it was a box office success, ending up with a worldwide gross of $414,828,246 with a budget of $120 million, according to [[Box Office Mojo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Wolverine|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=wolverine2.htm|work=[[Box Office Mojo]]}}</ref>

Following the box office success and moderate critical response to ''The Wolverine'', Mangold signed on to write the story and direct the sequel, ''[[Logan (film)|Logan]]'' (2017). The film marked Mangold and Jackman's third collaboration. Scott Frank was hired to return as co-screenwriter, working as a team with Mangold and Michael Green.<ref>{{cite news|last=Vejvoda|first=Jim|date=March 28, 2015|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2015/03/28/hugh-jackman-wolverine-one-last-time |title=Hugh Jackman: 'Wolverine … One Last Time'|work=[[IGN]]|accessdate=March 11, 2017}}</ref> The development of the film was lengthy, with Jackman citing his and Mangold's desire to do the character justice for his last time in the role.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/wolverine-3-hugh-jackman-offers-935009 |title=Hugh Jackman Offers 'Wolverine 3' Update: It's "Very Different in Tone"|date=October 4, 2016|first=Aaron |last=Couch |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|accessdate=March 11, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.christianpost.com/news/wolverine-3-update-hugh-jackman-reveals-script-is-almost-finished-release-date-expected-2017-156220/ |title='Wolverine 3' Update: Hugh Jackman Reveals Script Is Almost Finished, Release Date Expected 2017|date=February 3, 2016|first= Charmaine |last=Montes|work=[[The Christian Post]]|accessdate=March 11, 2017}}</ref> The film incorporated elements from [[Mark Millar]]'s ''[[Old Man Logan]]'' run on the comics.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://screenrant.com/wolverine-hugh-jackman-old-man-logan/ |title='Wolverine': Hugh Jackman Teases 'Old Man Logan' During Comic-Con|first=Gregg |last=Katzman|date=July 12, 2015 |work=[[Screen Rant]]|accessdate=March 11, 2017}}</ref> Mangold has stated that the plot primarily focuses on character development, rather than superhero spectacle.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://comicbook.com/marvel/2016/12/30/james-mangold-reveals-how-logan-is-different-from-other-super-he/ |title=James Mangold Reveals How Logan Is Different From Other Super Hero Movies|first=Brandon|last=Davis|work=Comicbook.com|date=March 2, 2017|accessdate=March 11, 2017}}</ref> ''Logan'' was a commercial success, and received high praise for its gritty approach on the titular character and emotional depth. Often called one of the [[List of films considered the best|greatest superhero films of all time]], the movie also earned an [[Academy Award]] nomination for [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay|Best Adapted Screenplay]], becoming the first live-action superhero movie to be nominated for Adapted Screenplay, as well as Mangold's first Oscar nomination.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/la-et-mn-oscar-reactions-logan-james-mangold-nomination-20180123-story.html |title='Logan' Director James Mangold Surprised that Wolverine Film Got Him His First Oscar Nod for Adapted Screenplay |last=Saad |first=Nardine |date=January 23, 2018 |website=[[The Los Angeles Times]] |access-date=January 25, 2018}}</ref><ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BNtvTr4hADk "Call Me by Your Name" wins Best Adapted Screenplay - Oscars on YouTube]</ref>

In March 2017, Mangold was in negotiations with Fox to develop and direct an adaptation of [[Don Winslow]]'s upcoming novel ''The Force'' about corrupt NYPD officers.<ref name="force">{{cite news|last=McNary|first=Dave|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/logan-director-james-mangold-cop-drama-the-force-1202010578/|title='Logan's' James Mangold to Direct Fox's NYPD Drama 'The Force'|date=March 16, 2017|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=March 17, 2017}}</ref> In June 2017, Fox set a release date for ''The Force'' for March 1, 2019.<ref>{{cite news|last=McNary|first=Dave|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/fox-nypd-thriller-the-force-2019-release-1202483648/|title=Fox to Release NYPD Thriller 'The Force' in March 2019|date=June 29, 2017|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=August 22, 2019}}</ref> The release date was not met, as the film was still in development. Later in February 2018, it was announced that Mangold was set to direct a film about the [[1966 24 Hours of Le Mans]], with [[Christian Bale]] and [[Matt Damon]] starring as [[Ken Miles]] and [[Carroll Shelby]] respectively, and [[Jez Butterworth]], [[John-Henry Butterworth]] and [[Jason Keller]] writing the script.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/film/news/james-mangold-next-film-ford-ferrari-logan-1202687686/|title=James Mangold to Direct Ford vs. Ferrari Film as 'Logan' Follow-Up (EXCLUSIVE)|first=Brent|last=Lang|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=February 5, 2018|accessdate=August 22, 2019}}</ref> The film, titled ''[[Ford v. Ferrari]]'', was released in November 2019 to critical acclaim. The film received 4 nominations including [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]], with two wins for [[Academy Award for Best Film Editing|Best Film Editing]] and [[Academy Award for Best Sound Editing|Best Sound Editing]] at the [[92nd Academy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/news/2020-oscar-nominations-list-academy-awards-nominees-1203461985/|title=Oscar Nominations 2020: The Complete List|date=January 13, 2020|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200113151810/https://variety.com/2020/film/news/2020-oscar-nominations-list-academy-awards-nominees-1203461985/|archive-date=January 13, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wg_Ql89fWy4 "Parasite" wins Best Picture - Oscars on YouTube]</ref>

In January 2020, it was announced that Mangold would direct a biopic featuring the life of the singer [[Bob Dylan]], with [[Timothée Chalamet]] starring as Dylan.<ref>{{cite news|last=Stedman|first=Alex|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/news/timothee-chalamet-bob-dylan-james-mangold-1203458755/|title=Timothee Chalamet to Play Bob Dylan in Film Directed by James Mangold|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=January 6, 2020|accessdate=January 7, 2020}}</ref>

In February 2020, it was announced that Mangold was in talks to direct the [[Indiana Jones 5|untitled fifth ''Indiana Jones'' film]], taking over for [[Steven Spielberg]], who will instead act as producer.<ref>{{cite news|last=Gaulppo|first=Mia|url=https://hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/james-mangold-talks-replace-steven-spielberg-as-indiana-jones-5-director-1281340|title=James Mangold in talks to replace steven spielberg as 'indiana jones 5' director|date=February 26, 2020|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref> In May 2020, it was officially confirmed that Mangold will serve as director.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lawrence|first=Gregory|date=May 25, 2020|title='Indiana Jones 5': Frank Marshall Updates Us on Script, Praises New Director James Mangold|url=https://collider.com/indiana-jones-5-update-script-james-mangold-frank-marshall/|access-date=June 14, 2020|website=Collider|language=en-US}}</ref>
