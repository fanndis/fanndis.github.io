"'''Glory'''" is a song by American rapper [[Common (rapper)|Common]] and American singer [[John Legend]]. It was written by John Legend, Common, and [[Rhymefest]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2015/film/news/selma-glory-oscars-original-song-academy-awards-2015-1201439270/|title='Selma' Wins Oscar for Best Original Song with 'Glory'|first=Dave|last=McNary|date=23 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2015/01/common-john-legend-to-perform-selma-song-glory-at-oscars-1201360594/|title=Common, John Legend To Perform 'Selma' Song "Glory" At Oscars|author=The Deadline Team|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|date=January 27, 2015|accessdate=February 2, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.paramountguilds.com/selma/song/|title=Paramount Pictures: For Your Consideration 'Selma'|author=Paramount Pictures|publisher=Paramountguilds.com|date=January 27, 2015|accessdate=February 21, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150214044658/http://www.paramountguilds.com/selma/song/|archive-date=2015-02-14|url-status=dead}}</ref> The song was released on December 11, 2014, by [[Columbia Records]] as the theme song from the 2014 film [[Selma (film)|''Selma'']], which portrays the 1965 [[Selma to Montgomery marches]]. Common also co-starred in the film as [[Civil Rights Movement]] leader [[James Bevel]].

Commercially, the song peaked at number 49 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. A music video for the song was directed by [[Paramount Pictures]] and was released on January 12, 2015. The song won the award for [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] at the [[87th Academy Awards]] (2015) and the [[72nd Golden Globe Awards]] (2015), as well as the award for [[Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media|Best Song Written for Visual Media]] at the [[58th Annual Grammy Awards]] (2016).

==Music video==
[[File:9.13.11CommonByLuigiNovi3.jpg|right|thumb|200px|Common co-starred in the film ''Selma'', and sang "Glory" as the end-credits rolled.]]
The music video was directed by [[Paramount Pictures]].<ref name="JL">{{cite web|url=http://freeonsmash.com/music/common-john-legend-glory-official-music-video-watch/|title=John Legend & Common – Glory|publisher=freeonsmash.com|accessdate=January 13, 2015}}</ref> It was put on Common's official [[Vevo]] account on January 12, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?x-yt-ts=1422579428&x-yt-cl=85114404&v=HUZOKvYcx_o#t=135|title=Common, John Legend - Glory |publisher=[[YouTube]]|accessdate=January 12, 2015}}</ref> The video stars Legend and Common. The video, which includes footage from the film, begins with Legend playing the piano and then singing, with Common [[rapping]] in a different location.

==Live performances==
"Glory" was performed by Legend and Common on the American morning television show ''[[Good Morning America]]'' on January 5, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.illroots.com/read/common-john-legend-glory-live-on-good-morning-america|title=Common & John Legend - Glory (Live On 'Good Morning America')|publisher=illroots.com|accessdate=January 5, 2015}}</ref>

On February 8, 2015, the duo performed the song as culmination to the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]] ceremonies held at the [[Staples Center]] in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].

Legend and Common performed "Glory" during the [[87th Academy Awards]] show on February 22, 2015 using a stage-setting of Selma's [[Edmund Pettus Bridge]]. The song won the Oscar for [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.32298/title.common-john-legend-to-perform-glory-at-the-oscars-this-february|title=Common & John Legend To Perform "Glory" At The Oscars|publisher=hiphopdx.com|accessdate=January 28, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150503215904/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.32298/title.common-john-legend-to-perform-glory-at-the-oscars-this-february|archive-date=May 3, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In October 2015, Common surprised a student choir in [[Compton, California]] with a live performance of "Glory" in support of [[My Brother's Keeper Challenge]]. The promotion was run by [[Omaze]], a private internet company that raises funds for charity through online auctions and merchandise sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1bdNQSyFJGM|title=Common surprises youth choir with Glory performance|publisher=omaze.com|accessdate=March 8, 2016}}</ref>

It was performed by Legend and Common, backed by a choir, at the [[2020 Democratic National Convention]].

==Commercial performance==
"Glory" debuted on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] at number 92. It became Common's fourth charted song of his own,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://scottfeinberg.com/few-no-1-billboard-hits-win-big-at-the-oscars|title=Few No. 1 Billboard Hits Win Big at the Oscars|publisher=scottfeinberg.com|accessdate=January 22, 2015}}</ref> and peaked at number 49.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/299567/Common/chart?f=379|title=Common Album & Song Chart History|publisher=Billboard|accessdate= March 17, 2015}}</ref>
