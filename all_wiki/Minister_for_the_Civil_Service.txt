{{PoliticsUK}}
In the [[Government of the United Kingdom]], the '''Minister for the Civil Service''' is responsible for regulations regarding [[Civil Service (United Kingdom)|Her Majesty's Civil Service]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.civilservicecommissioners.org/web-resources/resources/0d89ba78b7c.pdf |title=Civil Service Order in Council 1995 |publisher=UK Civil Service Commissioners |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140720152324/http://www.civilservicecommissioners.org/web-resources/resources/0d89ba78b7c.pdf |archive-date=20 July 2014}}</ref> the role of which is to assist the governments of the [[United Kingdom]] in formulating and implementing policies. The position is invariably held by the [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.number10.gov.uk/Page19564 |title=Her Majesty's Government |date=9 June 2009 |publisher=Government of the United Kingdom |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100216081044/http://www.number10.gov.uk/Page19564 |archive-date=16 February 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The ministership was created for [[Harold Wilson]] on 1 November 1968 when responsibilities for the pay and management of the Civil Service was transferred from [[HM Treasury]] to a new Civil Service Department.{{sfn|Daintith|Page|1999}} In recognition of the primary authority of the [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] over the Civil Service, it is a [[Constitutional convention (political custom)|constitutional convention]] that the Ministry would always be held by the Prime Minister.<ref>{{cite newspaper The Times |articlename=Ministers in merger dilemma |author=David Wood |date=17 October 1968 |page_number=1 |issue=57384}}</ref> The list of Ministers for the Civil Service is therefore identical to the [[list of Prime Ministers of the United Kingdom]] from 1968 onwards.

By the terms of the [[Civil Service (Management Functions) Act 1992]], the Minister may delegate his or her power to ministers and others such as the [[Scottish Government]].{{sfn|Pilkington|1999|page=153}} Prime Minister [[Gordon Brown]] appointed [[Tom Watson (Labour politician)|Tom Watson]] to be responsible for the Civil Service as "Minister for Digital Engagement and Civil Service Issues",<ref>{{cite web |last=Sparrow |first=Andrew |title=Profile: Tom Watson |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2009/jun/02/tom-watson-profile |date=2 June 2009 |newspaper=The Guardian |accessdate=2 September 2017}}</ref> while Prime Minister [[Boris Johnson]] has given [[Michael Gove]], the [[Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster]] and [[Minister for the Cabinet Office]], responsibility for the Civil Service.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hansard.parliament.uk/Commons/2020-03-02/debates/ACE798E4-1624-4BF6-95EC-DFD60C266F05/MinisterialCode#contribution-A3D4B966-A718-4935-AB51-7E5A2111A2BD|title=Ministerial Code|website=Hansard|date=2 March 2020|accessdate=3 March 2020|quote=As the Minister responsible for the civil service, I am pleased to be here in order to be able to uphold the ministerial code|last=Gove|first=Michael}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/politics/2020/03/02/priti-patel-allegations-will-investigated-cabinet-office-government/|title= Priti Patel allegations will be investigated by Cabinet Office, Government says|website=The Telegraph|date=2 March 2020|accessdate=2 March 2020|last1=Yorke|first1=Harry|last2=Hymas|first2=Charles|quote=Michael Gove, the Cabinet minister responsible for the civil service}}</ref>

[[Mark Sedwill]] has been the [[Cabinet Secretary (United Kingdom)|Cabinet Secretary]] and [[Head of the Home Civil Service]] since 2018.<ref>{{cite blog |title=Statement on Sir Jeremy Heywood|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/statement-on-sir-jeremy-heywood |website=[[Gov.uk]] |publisher=Government of the United Kingdom |accessdate=27 October 2018}}</ref>
