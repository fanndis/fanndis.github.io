'''Joseph Tyree Sneed III''' (July 21, 1920 – February 9, 2008) was formerly a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] United States [[Deputy Attorney General]] and then a [[United States federal judge|United States Circuit Judge]] of the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit]] for nearly 35 years until his death. He was the father of [[Carly Fiorina]], a former [[CEO]] of [[Hewlett-Packard]] and [[2016 Republican presidential primary|2016 Republican Presidential Primary]] candidate.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.newsmax.com/Politics/Carly-Fiorina-judges-father-constitutionalist/2015/06/11/id/650139/|title=Fiorina: 'I Would Appoint Judges That Remind Me of My Father'|work=Newsmax|access-date=2017-07-17}}</ref>

==Early life==

Joseph Tyree Sneed III was born on July 21, 1920 in [[Calvert, Texas|Calvert]], [[Texas]].<ref name="latimesobit">{{cite news|last1=Stewart|first1=Jocelyn Y.|title=Senior judge on U.S. 9th Circuit Court of Appeals, former law school professor|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2008/feb/15/local/me-sneed15|accessdate=February 9, 2016|work=The Los Angeles Times|date=February 15, 2008}}</ref><ref name="sfgateobit">{{cite news|last1=Egelko|first1=Bob|title=Joseph Sneed dies - longtime 9th Circuit judge|url=http://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/Joseph-Sneed-dies-longtime-9th-Circuit-judge-3226653.php|accessdate=February 9, 2016|work=The San Francisco Chronicle|date=February 14, 2008}}</ref> He was the son of Cara Carlton (Weber) and Harold Marvin Sneed (Jan. 6th 1883- Dec 27th 1934), a rancher and landowner.<ref name="latimesobit"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-XEbAQAAMAAJ&q=%22HAROLD+MARVIN+SNEED%22&dq=%22HAROLD+MARVIN+SNEED%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=ZCFrVMfUIYqnyQSv-oHoBQ&ved=0CCIQ6AEwAg|title=Descendants of Nicholas Perkins of Virginia|work=google.ca}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YNJFAQAAIAAJ&q=%22Born+Calvert+Texas+July+21,+1920+to+Harold+Marvin+and+Cara+Weber+Sneed.%22&dq=%22Born+Calvert+Texas+July+21,+1920+to+Harold+Marvin+and+Cara+Weber+Sneed.%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=kiFrVMXoBIeayQTz9YKQDQ&ved=0CBUQ6AEwAA|title=The American Bench|work=google.ca}}</ref> He spent his youth working summers as a [[cowboy]] on his uncle's [[ranch]] in the [[Texas Panhandle]].<ref name="sfgateobit"/>

Sneed received his [[Bachelor of Business Administration]] degree from [[Southwestern University]] in 1941.<ref name="latimesobit"/> He served as a [[Staff Sergeant]] in the [[United States Army Air Corps|Army Air Corps]] during [[World War II]].<ref name="latimesobit"/> Sneed attended the [[University of Texas School of Law]], where he received his [[Bachelor of Laws]], [[Order of the Coif]] in 1947.<ref name="latimesobit"/><ref name="sfgateobit"/> He was also a visiting student at the [[London School of Economics]] and the [[University of Ghana]].<ref name="sfgateobit"/> He subsequently received a [[Doctor of Juridical Science]] from [[Harvard Law School]] in 1958.<ref name="latimesobit"/>

==Career==

===Academia===

Sneed was an assistant professor of law at the University of Texas School of Law from 1947 to 1951.<ref name="latimesobit"/> He became an associate professor in 1951 and was made a full professor in 1954. He taught at the University of Texas until 1957.<ref name="latimesobit"/>

Sneed was a professor of law at [[Cornell Law School]] from 1957 to 1962, followed by the [[Stanford Law School]] from 1962 to 1971.<ref name="latimesobit"/> He was professor of law and dean of the [[Duke University School of Law]] from 1971 to 1973.<ref name="latimesobit"/><ref name="sfgateobit"/>

===Judicial service===

Sneed was nominated by [[President of the United States|President]] [[Richard Nixon]] to a seat vacated (formerly occupied) by Judge [[Frederick George Hamley]] on the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit]] on July 25, 1973.<ref name="latimesobit"/><ref name="sfgateobit"/> He was [[advice and consent|confirmed]] by the [[United States Senate]] on August 3, 1973, and received his judicial commission on August 24, 1973. He served as an active judge of the court until taking [[senior status]] on July 21, 1987.

Sneed ruled in favor of [[three-strikes law]]; [[LGBT employment discrimination in the United States|LGBT employment discrimination]]; and the eviction of substance abusers by their landlords.<ref name="bloombergiscarlyachipoff">{{cite news|title=Is Carly Fiorina a Chip Off the Old Block?|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/politics/articles/2015-05-04/is-carly-fiorina-a-chip-off-the-old-block-|accessdate=February 9, 2016|work=Bloomberg Politics|date=May 4, 2015}}</ref> He was part of a three-judge panel that replaced Whitewater special prosecutor Robert B. Fiske with [[Kenneth Starr]] in 1994.<ref name="sfgateobit"/><ref name="bloombergiscarlyachipoff"/>

==Personal life==

Sneed married Madelon Juergens Sneed in 1944.<ref name="latimesobit"/> She was a [[portrait]] and [[abstract art|abstract]] [[artist]] who died in 1998.<ref name="sfgateobit"/> Together they had a son and two daughters, including [[Carly Fiorina]].<ref name="latimesobit"/> They resided in [[San Francisco]], [[California]].<ref name="sfgateobit"/>

==Death==

Sneed died on February 9, 2008, in San Francisco at the age of 87.<ref name="latimesobit"/><ref name="sfgateobit"/>
