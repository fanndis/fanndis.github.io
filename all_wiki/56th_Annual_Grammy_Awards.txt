The '''56th Annual [[Grammy Award]]s''' presentation was held on January 26, 2014, at [[Staples Center]] in [[Los Angeles]].<ref>Jon Weisman, [https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/sns-201305130912reedbusivarietyn1200479888-20130513,0,1926471.story Grammy Awards Set for Jan. 26], ''Los Angeles Times'', May 13, 2013</ref> The show was broadcast on [[CBS]] at 8 p.m. ET/PT and was hosted for the third time by [[LL Cool J]]. The show was moved to January to avoid competing with the [[2014 Winter Olympics]] in [[Sochi]], as was the case in 2010.

The eligibility period for the 56th Annual Grammy Awards was October 1, 2012, to September 30, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.org/files/pages/56reccotimetable.pdf |title=According to an Adobe Reader file from GRAMMYS.com |format=PDF |date= |accessdate=2014-01-31}}</ref> The nominations were announced on December 6, 2013 during a live televised concert on [[CBS]], ''The Grammy Nominations Concert Live – Countdown to Music's Biggest Night.''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/news/the-grammy-nominations-concert-live-to-be-held-in-la-on-dec-6 |title="The GRAMMY Nominations Concert Live!!" To Be Held In L.A. On Dec. 6 |publisher=GRAMMY.com |date=2013-09-18 |accessdate=2014-01-13}}</ref> [[Jay Z]] received the most nominations with nine. [[Justin Timberlake]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Macklemore & Ryan Lewis]] and [[Pharrell Williams]] each received seven nominations. [[Daft Punk]] and Pharrell Williams were nominated twice for both [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] and [[Record of the Year]]. Sound engineer [[Bob Ludwig]] received the most nominations by a non-performing artist, with five.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jay Z Tops 56th Grammy Nominations With Nine|url=http://www.grammy.com/news/jay-z-tops-56th-grammy-nominations-with-nine|publisher=GRAMMY.com|date=November 6, 2013}}</ref>

Daft Punk won four awards,<ref name="grammy">EDM and Rap Duos and Prolific Producer Win Four Awards Each,[http://www.grammy.com/news/daft-punk-pharrell-and-macklemore-ryan-lewis-heist-the-grammys], grammy.com Jan 26, 2014</ref> including [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] for ''[[Random Access Memories]]'' and [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]], with [[Pharrell Williams]], for "[[Get Lucky (Daft Punk song)|Get Lucky]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://edit.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/tv-film/5885384/the-2014-grammy-awards-recap-weddings-french-robots-paul-ringo|title=The 2014 Grammy Awards Recap: Weddings, French Robots, Paul & Ringo|date=27 January 2014|work=Billboard.com|accessdate=28 January 2014}}{{Dead link|date=April 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/the-2014-grammy-awards-rapper-macklemore-cleans-up-early/2014/01/26/8b8f5442-86b5-11e3-916e-e01534b1e132_story.html |title= The 2014 Grammy Awards: Oldies, but not goodies|date=27 January 2014|work=Washington Post
|accessdate=28 January 2014}}</ref>
[[Macklemore]] & [[Ryan Lewis]] won four trophies, including [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], and led an industry show of support for [[gay marriage]] with a performance of their song "[[Same Love]]" to accompany a mass wedding of gay and heterosexual couples, which was presided over by [[Queen Latifah]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jan/26/grammy-awards-grammys-live-coverage|title=2014 Grammy awards – as it happened|date=27 January 2014|work=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=27 January 2014}}</ref> [[Lorde]]'s "[[Royals (song)|Royals]]" received awards for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance|Best Pop Solo Performance]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/grammys/10598372/Grammys-2014-as-it-happened.html |title=Grammys 2014: as it happened|date=27 January 2014|work=DailyTelegraph|accessdate=27 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/27/arts/music/grammys-laud-giants-and-upstarts.html|title=Grammys Laud Giants and Upstarts|author=[[Ben Sisario]]|date=27 January 2014|work=The New York Times|accessdate=6 June 2020}}</ref> [[Carole King]] was honored as [[MusiCares Person of the Year]] on January 24, two days prior to the awards ceremony.<ref name="Carole">{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/news/carole-king-named-2014-musicares-person-of-the-year |title=Carole King Named 2014 MusiCares Person of the Year |publisher=Grammy.com |date= |accessdate=2014-01-31}}</ref>

On June 4, 2013, the Recording Academy approved a number of changes recommended by its Awards & Nominations Committee, including adding a new category for Best American Roots Song to the American Music field. This songwriters' award will encompass all the subgenres in this field such as Americana, bluegrass, blues, folk, and regional roots music. The [[Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock/Metal Performance|Best Hard Rock/Metal Performance]] category was renamed Best Metal Performance and became a stand-alone category. Hard rock performances will now be screened in the [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Performance|Best Rock Performance]] category. The Music Video field will become the Best Music Video/Film field. Its two categories will be renamed: [[Grammy Award for Best Music Video|Best Short Form Music Video]] will now be known as Best Music Video and Best Long Form Music Video will change into [[Grammy Award for Best Music Film|Best Music Film]]. These changes bring the total number of categories at the 2014 Grammy Awards to 82, up from 81 at the [[55th Annual Grammy Awards|2013 Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.org/recording-academy/press-release/the-recording-academy-elects-new-national-officers-and-approves |title=Press release, June 4, 2013 |publisher=Grammy.org |date= |accessdate=2014-01-31}}</ref>

==Live-GIFs==

The 56th Annual Grammy Awards were the first in the show's history to incorporate comprehensive Live-GIF integration through [[Tumblr]]. Creative agency Deckhouse Digital was hired to facilitate the integration, producing more than 50 [[Gif|animated GIFs]] during the live broadcast and publishing them to the official Grammy tumblr page in real time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://deckhousedigital.com/tagged/grammys |title=Deckhouse Digital Grammy Posts |accessdate=2014-08-24}}</ref> The images contributed to the more than 5.1 million reblogs and likes that Grammy related posts received on the blogging site, and the record breaking 34 million combined social media interactions related to the live broadcast.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.org/recording-academy/press-release/the-56th-annual-grammy-awards-unites-music-fans-with-record-breaking |title=The 56th Annual Grammy Awards® Unites Music Fans With Record-Breaking Social Media |publisher=GRAMMY.com |date=2014-01-30 |accessdate=2014-08-24}}</ref>
