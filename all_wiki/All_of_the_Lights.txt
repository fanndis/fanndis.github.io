"'''All of the Lights'''" is a song by American [[rapping|rapper]] [[Kanye West]], as the fourth [[single (music)|single]] from his fifth studio album, ''[[My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy]]'' (2010). It was produced by West and features additional vocals from several other recording artists, including [[John Legend]], [[The-Dream]], [[Elly Jackson]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[Fergie (singer)|Fergie]], [[Elton John]], [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], [[Kid Cudi]], and [[Rihanna]]; the latter two are credited on music video, while solely the latter is credited on the single.

"All of the Lights" was well received by music critics, who complimented its detailed production and dramatically-themed style. The single was a moderate international success, reaching number 18 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], was the 59th most popular song of 2011, and number two on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart in the United States, being the 12th most popular song on that chart in 2011, while it also attained respectable chartings in several other countries. It won [[Grammy Award]]s for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song|Best Rap Song]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration|Best Rap/Sung Collaboration]] and was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] at the [[54th Grammy Awards]]. As of December 2011, the song has sold over 1,561,000 digital units in the US.<ref>Grein, Paul (December 7th, 2011). [http://music.yahoo.com/blogs/chart-watch/week-ending-dec-4-2011-songs-katy-goes-020228383.html Week Ending Dec. 4, 2011. Songs: Katy Goes On Sale]. [[Yahoo! Music]]. Retrieved 2011-12-8.</ref> By August 2013, it had been certified triple platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].

The song's accompanying music video, directed by [[Hype Williams]],<ref name="Hype"/> featured strobe-lit images of Rihanna and West, as well as Kid Cudi. It was given a discretionary warning by [[Epilepsy Action]], stating that the video "potentially triggers seizures for people with [[photosensitive epilepsy]]".<ref name="Iandoli"/> West and Rihanna performed the song at the [[2011 NBA All-Star Game]], while it was also featured in the promo for the same event.<ref>{{cite web|title=2011 NBA All-Star Game Halftime Show: Kanye West, Drake Join Rihanna|url=https://www.sbnation.com/nba/2011/2/20/2005091/2011-nba-all-star-game-halftime-show-kanye-west-drake-rihanna|website=SBNation|accessdate=10 February 2011}}</ref>

== Background and recording ==
[[File:Rihanna concert in Washington DC (2).jpg|thumb|200px|left|Fourteen artists lent their voices as background vocals on "All of the Lights" including Rihanna (''pictured'') who also sang the song's hook.]]

"All of the Lights" was written by Kanye West, [[Jeff Bhasker]], [[Malik Yusef|Malik Jones]], [[Really Doe|Warren Trotter]], [[Fergie (singer)|Stacy Ferguson]] and [[The-Dream|Terius Nash]].<ref name="liner">{{cite AV media notes |others=Kanye West |title=My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy|year=2010 |publisher=Roc-A-Fella Records}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/awards/54th-annual-grammy-awards|title=Winners - 54th Annual GRAMMY Awards|publisher=[[Grammy Awards|GRAMMY]]|accessdate=2018-07-17}}</ref> Its production was helmed by West himself and co-produced by Bhasker. Fourteen artists lent their voices as background vocals to the song: [[Alicia Keys]], [[John Legend]], [[The-Dream]], [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], [[Fergie (singer)|Fergie]], [[Kid Cudi]], [[Elton John]], [[Ryan Leslie]], [[Charlie Wilson (singer)|Charlie Wilson]], [[Tony Williams (soul singer)|Tony Williams]], [[La Roux]], Alvin Fields, [[Ken Lewis (musician)|Ken Lewis]], and [[Rihanna]], who also sang the song's [[Hook (music)|hook]] and is labeled as a featured artist on the single version.<ref name="liner"/><ref name="hook">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1651991/rihanna-loved-working-with-kanye-west-on-all-lights.jhtml|title=Rihanna 'Loved' Working With Kanye West On 'All Of The Lights'|publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[Viacom (2005–present)|Viacom]]|last=Montgomery|first=James|date=November 10, 2010|accessdate=February 13, 2012}}</ref> West and Rihanna previously collaborated with [[Jay-Z]] on "[[Run This Town]]" (''[[The Blueprint 3]]'', 2009).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1616871/20090724/jay_z.jhtml|title=Jay-Z's 'Run This Town' Makes Radio Debut|publisher=MTV News. Viacom|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=July 24, 2009|accessdate=February 19, 2012}}</ref>

The early working title for "All of The Lights" was previously "Ghetto University" when the production on the song was just beginning.<ref>https://www.facebook.com/drfordmusic/videos/10216575381249752/UzpfSTE2MTU2NDAyNTQ6MTAyMTY1ODE3ODQzMjk4MjU/ "The Dr.’s Office Ep. 4 - The Ken Lewis interview!" 59 minutes and 6 seconds into the video. Retrieved on March 25, 2019</ref> During the [[2010 MTV Europe Music Awards]], on November 10, 2010, Rihanna was interviewed by [[MTV News]]' staff. In the interview she explained that, West already played his album to her three months ago and that "All of the Lights" was one of her favorite songs. About it, she further commented, "So when he asked me to come up to the studio at 2 o'clock in the morning, I had to, because I loved it, I knew it was that song."<ref name="hook"/>

In an interview for the same publication, Elly Jackson said of the song's vocal layering, "He got me to layer up all these vocals with other people, and he just basically wanted to use his favorite vocalists from around the world to create this really unique vocal texture on his record, but it's not the kind of thing where you can pick it out".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapfix.mtv.com/2010/11/22/kanye-west-my-beautiful-dark-twisted-fantasy-all-of-the-lights-a-vocal-breakdown/|title=Kanye's 'All Of The Lights': A Vocal Breakdown|publisher=MTV Rap Fix. Viacom|last=Roberts|first=Steven|date=November 22, 2010|accessdate=February 13, 2012}}</ref> [[Andrew Dawson (record producer)|Andrew Dawson]], Anthony Kilhoffer, [[Mike Dean (producer)|Mike Dean]], HI and Noah Goldstein recorded West's vocals at Avex Recording Studios in [[Honolulu]], [[Hawaii]] and [[Electric Lady Studios]] in [[New York City]]. Rihanna's vocals were recorded by Marcos Tovar at the [[Westlake Recording Studios]] in [[West Hollywood]], California.<ref name="liner"/>

In a 2013 interview with ''[[The Breakfast Club (radio show)|The Breakfast Club]]'', West said that the song took two years to finish, and detailed the creation and process of the song;  
<blockquote>"'All The Lights' is a futurist song that started out as a [[Jeezy]] record with horns on it, then we put in another bridge, then Dream wrote the hook, then Rihanna sang it, and by the time you got it, it was to the level of like, the Nike Flyknit or something like that.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Rose Lilah |title=Kanye West On "Bound 2" Video: "I Wanted It To Look As Phony As Possible" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kanye-west-on-bound-2-video-i-wanted-it-to-look-as-phony-as-possible-news.8310.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=20 February 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160321223930/https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kanye-west-on-bound-2-video-i-wanted-it-to-look-as-phony-as-possible-news.8310.html |archive-date=21 March 2016 |date=26 November 2013 |url-status=live}}</ref></blockquote>

== Reception ==
"All of the Lights" received critical acclaim. [[AllMusic]]'s Andy Kellman stated "At once, the song features one of the year’s most rugged beats while supplying enough opulent detail to make ''[[Late Registration]]'' collaborator [[Jon Brion]]'s head spin".<ref name="Kellman"/> Alex Denney of ''[[NME]]'' called it "the sleb-studded centrepiece", commenting that "In anyone else’s hands it’d be an A-list circle-jerk of horrid proportions, but through Kanye’s bar-raising vision it becomes a truly wondrous thing".<ref name="Denney">Denney, Alex (November 19, 2010). [https://www.nme.com/reviews/kanye-west/11723 Review: ''My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy'']. ''[[NME]]''. Retrieved 2011-01-30.</ref> Zach Baron of ''[[The Village Voice]]'' found the song's lyrics relevant to the "year of economic suffering", writing that "West interrupted his own wealthy anomie to pen 'All of the Lights,' an incongruously star-stuffed song about a disoriented parolee trying to beat a restraining order and see his daughter, working out a brief reunion with her estranged mother: 'Public visitation, we met at [[Borders Group|Borders]]'".<ref>Baron, Zach (January 19, 2011). [http://www.villagevoice.com/2011-01-19/music/kanye-west-wins-one-for-the-douchebags/ Kanye West Wins One for the Douchebags]. ''[[The Village Voice]]''. Retrieved 2011-02-19.</ref> ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' writer Thomas Conner viewed that "as crowded as 'All of the Lights' is [,] it maintains an almost operatic drama, telling a tale of adultery and its aftermath that winds up being quite moving".<ref name="Conner">Conner, Thomas (November 21, 2010). [http://blogs.suntimes.com/music/2010/11/cd_review_kanye_west_my_beauti.html Review: ''My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy''] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121019235105/http://blogs.suntimes.com/music/2010/11/cd_review_kanye_west_my_beauti.html |date=October 19, 2012 }}. ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]''. Retrieved 2011-01-30.</ref> ''[[The Guardian]]''{{'}}s [[Kitty Empire]] cited the song as "the album's most magnificent high", writing that it "backs up operatic levels of sound with great drama".<ref name="Empire">Empire, Kitty (November 19, 2010). [https://www.theguardian.com/music/2010/nov/19/kanye-west-review Review: ''My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy'']. ''[[The Guardian]]''. Retrieved 2011-01-30.</ref>

Ann Powers of [[NPR]] included "All of the Lights" in her list of the Top 10 Top 40 singles of 2011, commenting "its like an action painting: the artist scatters elements across its canvas to form a whole that's all motion and colorful build."<ref>{{cite web |author1=Ann Powers |title=The Top 10 Top 40 of 2011 |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/therecord/2011/12/19/143799960/the-top-10-top-40-of-2011 |website=NPR |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191210120228/https://www.npr.org/sections/therecord/2011/12/19/143799960/the-top-10-top-40-of-2011 |archive-date=10 December 2019 |date=19 December 2011}}</ref> ''[[Slant Magazine]]'' named it the best single of 2011, describing the song as "perhaps the most acute example of Kanye West’s pitched mania for theatrical expressions of manic-depressive instability, his mixture of self-destruction and self-love",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.slantmagazine.com/music/feature/the-25-best-singles-of-2011/293/page_3|title=The 25 Best Singles of 2011 – Slant Magazine|work=[[Slant Magazine]]|date=2011-12-13|accessdate=2011-12-27}}</ref> they later listed it second in their list of the best singles of the 2010s in 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=The 100 Best Singles of the 2010s |url=https://www.slantmagazine.com/features/the-100-best-singles-of-the-2010s/5/ |website=Slant Magazine |accessdate=19 January 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101191614/https://www.slantmagazine.com/features/the-100-best-singles-of-the-2010s/5/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref>  

''[[Tampa Bay Times]]'' named it the second best pop song of the decade, proclaiming "Everything Kanye West has ever believed himself to be came to life in this song: The stadium-sized horns, the chilling Rihanna hook, the snarl of cockiness in his voice, the insane list of uncredited cameos .. It’s the moment King Midas figured out how to use his touch."<ref>{{cite web |author1=Jay Cridlin |title=The best pop songs of the 2010s: Taylor Swift, Bruno Mars, Lorde, more |url=https://www.tampabay.com/arts-entertainment/music/2019/12/26/the-best-pop-songs-of-the-2010s-taylor-swift-bruno-mars-lorde-more/ |website=Tampabay |accessdate=19 January 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191229123625/https://www.tampabay.com/arts-entertainment/music/2019/12/26/the-best-pop-songs-of-the-2010s-taylor-swift-bruno-mars-lorde-more/ |archive-date=29 December 2019 |date=26 December 2019 |url-status=live}}</ref> Elsewhere, ''[[Nothing but Hope and Passion]]'' listed it 13th on its list of "100 Must Listen Songs of the 2010s".<ref>{{cite web |title=100 Must Listen Songs Of The 2010s: Numbers #25– #01 |url=https://nbhap.com/sounds/must-listen-songs-of-the-2010s-part-4 |website=NBHAP |accessdate=24 February 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200224051806/https://nbhap.com/sounds/must-listen-songs-of-the-2010s-part-4 |archive-date=24 February 2020 |date=20 November 2019 |url-status=live}}</ref> ''[[Time Out (magazine)|Time Out]]'' named it the 13th best song of all-time in 2016.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Best Songs Ever |url=https://www.timeout.com/newyork/music/best-songs-ever?zone_id=1354046&page_number=2 |website=Time Out |accessdate=14 April 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200229033331/https://www.timeout.com/newyork/music/best-songs-ever?zone_id=1354046&page_number=2 |archive-date=29 February 2020 |date=5 December 2016 |url-status=live}}</ref>
