'''Paola Suárez''' ({{IPA-es|paˈola ˈswaɾes|am}}; born 23 June 1976) is a retired [[tennis]] player from [[Argentina]]. She was one of the most prominent women's doubles players throughout the early and mid-2000s, winning eight Grand Slam titles, all of them with [[Virginia Ruano Pascual]], and holding the No. 1 doubles ranking for 87 non-consecutive weeks. She was also a singles semifinalist at the [[2004 French Open – Women's Singles|2004 French Open]].

==Career==
Suárez began playing professional tennis at the age of 15. In 1994, she joined the professional tour as a singles player.

Suárez won four WTA titles (2004 Canberra, 2003 Vienna, 1998 & 2001 Bogotá) and 12 other minor tournaments. In 2004, she reached her only [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles semi-final by defeating the 18th seed and [[2004 Wimbledon Championships|future Wimbledon champion]] [[Maria Sharapova]], but lost to [[Elena Dementieva]]. That year, she reached her highest WTA ranking of No. 9, to become the highest-ranked Argentine women's player since [[Gabriela Sabatini]] achieved the No. 3 ranking in 1989. Also in 2004, she won the bronze medal at the [[2004 Summer Olympics|Olympic Games in Athens]] for the women's doubles with [[Patricia Tarabini]].

She had the best results in doubles, playing with [[Virginia Ruano Pascual]] of Spain for 32 of her 39 titles. They won the [[French Open]] on four occasions, the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]] three times, the Australian Open in 2004. Suárez and Ruano Pascual were the No. 1 female pair for three consecutive years after 9 September 2002 and were the [[WTA Awards#Doubles Team of the Year|WTA Tour Doubles Team of the Year]] in 2002, 2003 and 2004. They also reached nine straight Grand Slam finals, two short of [[Martina Navratilova|Navratilova]] and [[Pam Shriver|Shriver]]'s record of eleven.

In 2005, she announced her forthcoming retirement for personal reasons. In June 2005, she went on [[Acetabular labrum|labrum]] hip clinical intervention, with a recovery time of three to four months. She restarted playing in Sydney in January 2006 with Ruano Pascual, reaching the final, but suffered a [[Triceps surae muscle|calf]] injury short after. Later that year, she also reached the final of Wimbledon with Ruano Pascual, marking her third appearance in a final there.

Suárez returned to the circuit with a victory over [[Dinara Safina]], ranked No. 15, in the [[Acura Classic|San Diego]] singles tournament.

On 1 September 2007, Suárez retired after losing in the mixed doubles second round at the [[2007 US Open (tennis)|US Open]]. She partnered [[Kevin Ullyett]] and lost to [[Jamie Murray]] and [[Liezel Huber]], 5–7, 4–6.

She briefly returned to the WTA doubles tour in [[2012 WTA Tour|2012]], partnering her fellow Argentinian [[Gisela Dulko]]. The pair played at the [[2012 Summer Olympics|London Summer Olympics]] but lost in the first round.<ref>[http://www.wtatennis.com/doublesnews/20120216/suarez-back-on-court-plays-first-match-back_2256567_2612586 Suárez Back On Court, Plays First Match Back] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120220024753/http://www.wtatennis.com/doublesnews/20120216/suarez-back-on-court-plays-first-match-back_2256567_2612586 |date=20 February 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.london2012.com/tennis/event/women-doubles/phase=tew002500/index.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=19 April 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130402024714/http://www.london2012.com/tennis/event/women-doubles/phase=tew002500/index.html |archive-date=2 April 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.london2012.com/athlete/suarez-paola-1087942/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2013-04-19 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130404001427/http://www.london2012.com/athlete/suarez-paola-1087942/ |archivedate=4 April 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

In her career, Suárez earned more than $5.2 million, with four singles titles on the [[WTA Tour]], and eight doubles Grand Slam titles.
