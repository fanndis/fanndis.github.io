"'''A Whole New World'''" is a song from [[Walt Disney Animation Studios|Disney]]'s 1992 animated feature film [[Aladdin (1992 Disney film)|''Aladdin'']], with music by [[Alan Menken]] and lyrics by [[Tim Rice]].<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums"/> A [[duet]] originally recorded by singers [[Brad Kane]] and [[Lea Salonga]] in their respective roles as the singing voices of the main characters [[Aladdin (Disney character)|Aladdin]] and [[Princess Jasmine|Jasmine]], the [[ballad]] serves as both the film's [[Love song|love]] and [[theme song]]. Lyrically, "A Whole New World" describes Aladdin showing the confined princess a life of freedom and the pair's acknowledgment of their love for each other while riding on a [[magic carpet]]. The song garnered an [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]] at the [[65th Academy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/1993 |title=Best Original Song - The 65th Academy Awards (1993) |publisher=[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] |accessdate=February 15, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180202000120/http://oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/1993 |archivedate=February 2, 2018}}</ref> "A Whole New World" also won the [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year]] at the [[36th Annual Grammy Awards]], the first and so far only Disney song to win in the category for the version sung by      [[Peabo Bryson]] and [[Regina Belle]]. [[Zayn Malik]] and [[Zhavia Ward]] did their version of the song for the [[Aladdin (2019 film)]]

== International versions ==
When the movie was first released in 1992, it numbered 22 versions worldwide in 20 languages overall. Over the years, this number has increased to 40 official versions.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationalvoices.weebly.com/aladdin.html|title=Aladdin|website=Charguigou|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Jasmine|url=https://disneyinternationalvoices.weebly.com/jasmine.html|access-date=2019-06-05|website=Charguigou}}</ref> Aladdin was the first movie to be dubbed into [[Icelandic language|Icelandic]], [[Tamil language|Tamil]] and [[Telugu language|Telugu]]; Disney was so satisfied with the Icelandic dubbing that they sent a letter to Stúdíó Eitt, the Icelandic dubbing company that took over the job, stating they had never seen such a good result for a first dubbing of their movies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin--icelandic-cast.html|title=Aladdin / Icelandic cast|website=CHARGUIGOU|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin--tamil-cast.html|title=Aladdin / Tamil cast|website=CHARGUIGOU|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin--telugu-cast.html|title=Aladdin / Telugu cast|website=CHARGUIGOU|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref> [[India]]n singers [[Mano (singer)|Mano]] and [[Annupamaa]] sang for Aladdin and Jasmine respectively both in the Tamil and Telugu versions, while Mano also sang the [[Genie (Disney)|Genie]]'s songs.<ref name=":Genie international">{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationalvoices.weebly.com/genie.html|title=Genie|website=Charguigou|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref>

{{illm|Sami Aarva|fi}} and {{illm|Ulla Renko|fi}} (together with the whole [[Finnish language|Finnish]] cast) were prized as the best foreign version,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin--finnish-cast.html|title=Aladdin / Finnish cast|website=CHARGUIGOU|language=fr|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref> while {{illm|Joël Legendre|fr}}'s Aladdin ([[Canadian French]]) was so appreciated by Disney USA that in 1997 he was asked to come back as [[Hercules (1997 film)|Hercules]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationalvoices.weebly.com/hercules.html|title=Hercules|website=Charguigou|access-date=2019-06-05}}</ref>

===2019 live-action version===
[[File:Hiba1.JPG|thumb|left|[[Lebanon|Libanese]]-[[State of Palestine|Palestinian]] singer [[Hiba Tawaji]] recorded the song twice in [[French language|French]]: with the [[Canadian French|Canadian]] and [[French of France|European]] lyrics]]
On its theater release in 2019, the [[Aladdin (2019 film)|live-action remake]] numbered 39 dubbings worldwide, a number which rose to 40 when an [[Indonesian language|Indonesian]] version was released the following year, and the song ''A Whole New World'' counting as many versions.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aladdin 2019|url=https://disneyinternationalvoices.weebly.com/aladdin-2019.html|access-date=2020-08-08|website=CHARGUIGOU|language=fr}}</ref> Even though no [[Flemish]], [[Serbian language|Serbian]] or [[Vietnamese language|Vietnamese]] versions of the original movie has ever been released, a dubbing in these languages was made for the live-action nonetheless, while no [[Arabic]], [[Cantonese]] or [[Icelandic language|Icelandic]] dubbings were made for the live-action, despite the existence of such versions for the original movie.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />

Originally, the two [[French language|French]] versions of the animated movie were completely independent, with different casts and lyrics.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aladdin / Canadian French cast|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin--canadian-french-cast.html|access-date=2020-08-08|website=CHARGUIGOU|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Aladdin / French cast|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin--french-cast.html|access-date=2020-08-08|website=CHARGUIGOU|language=en}}</ref> However, in the live-action, the three main characters were voiced in both versions by the same voice-actors in the songs. This means that, while a few lines were identical in the two versions, most of the songs had to be sung twice by the dubbers, with different lyrics. So, [[France|French]] actor {{illm|Julien Alluguette|fr}} ([[Aladdin (Disney character)|Aladdin]]) and [[Lebanon|Libanese]]-[[State of Palestine|Palestinian]] singer [[Hiba Tawaji]] ([[Jasmine (Disney character)|Jasmine]]) sang the song ''A Whole New World'' twice in French, with different lyrics for the [[Canadian French|Canadian]] and [[French of France|European French]] versions, titled "Un nouveau monde" and "Ce rêve bleu" respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aladdin 2019 / French cast|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin-2019--french-cast.html|access-date=2020-08-08|website=CHARGUIGOU|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Aladdin 2019 / Canadian French cast|url=https://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/aladdin-2019--canadian-french-cast.html|access-date=2020-08-08|website=CHARGUIGOU|language=en}}</ref>

A [[Single (music)|single]] version of the song was released that same year and was performed by American recording artists [[Peabo Bryson]] and [[Regina Belle]]. This version is played over the film's end credits and is referred on the soundtrack as "''Aladdin'''s Theme".<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums"/> The version peaked at number one on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart on March 6, 1993,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/1993-03-06/hot-100|title=The Hot 100 chart listing for the week of March 06, 1993|work=[[Billboard magazine|Billboard]], Inc|accessdate=2010-04-23}}</ref> replacing [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Will Always Love You#Whitney Houston version|I Will Always Love You]]", (which was originally done by [[Dolly Parton]]) which had spent a then-record 14 weeks at the top of the chart. It went [[RIAA certification|gold]] and sold 600,000 copies domestically.<ref>{{cite certification|region=United States|artist=Belle, Regina & Peabo Bryson|title=A Whole New World}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FwgEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA73 |title=Best-Selling Records of 1993 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=BPI Communications|date=January 15, 1994 |accessdate=May 4, 2015 |page=73 |issn=0006-2510|volume=106|number=3}}</ref> The track peaked at number 12 in the [[UK Singles Chart]] in 1993.<ref name="uk"/> The song is the first and so far only song from a Disney animated film to top the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100. The single version was later included on Belle's studio album ''[[Passion (Regina Belle album)|Passion]]'' (1993) and on Bryson's studio album ''[[Peabo Bryson discography|Through the Fire]] '' (1994).<ref>{{cite web|title=Passion - Regina Belle|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/passion-mw0000093724|website=[[Allmusic]]|publisher=[[Rovi Corporation]]|accessdate=6 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Through the Fire - Peabo Bryson|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/through-the-fire-mw0000117871|website=Allmusic|publisher=Rovi Corporation|accessdate=6 November 2014}}</ref>
