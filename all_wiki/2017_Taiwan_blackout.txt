On 15 August 2017 at 4:52 p.m., massive [[power outage|blackout]] hit northern half of [[Taiwan]], affecting 6.68 million households. [[Energy rationing|Electricity rationing]] was implemented at 6:00 p.m. and the blackout fully ended at 9:40 p.m.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://focustaiwan.tw/news/aeco/201708150033.aspx|title=Electricity supply restored around Taiwan: Taipower - Economics - FOCUS TAIWAN - CNA ENGLISH NEWS|author=|date=|website=focustaiwan.tw|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref>

==Event==

===Power plant===
During the [[power supply]] equipment replacement for a [[control system]] of the [[Electricity meter|metering station]] at [[Tatan Power Plant]] in [[Guanyin District]], [[Taoyuan City]] by a contractor of [[CPC Corporation]], the worker did not switch the system from auto mode to manual mode before starting the work, causing the two [[Pipeline transport|gas supply pipe]] valves to close and stop the supply of [[liquefied natural gas]] fuel source for two minutes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-08-16/taiwan-s-president-apologizes-for-blackout-affecting-millions|title=Mishap Triggers Taiwan Blackout as Power Policies Draw Scrutiny|author=|date=16 August 2017|publisher=|via=www.bloomberg.com|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref> Six [[Electric generator|generators]] of the power plant fully [[Power outage|tripped]] due to that, disrupting the supply of 4 GW of electricity.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://topics.amcham.com.tw/2017/10/keeping-taiwan-going-dark/|title=Keeping Taiwan from Going Dark - Taiwan Business TOPICS|author=|date=13 October 2017|website=amcham.com.tw|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref>

===Public===
Residents in major cities in northern Taiwan reported slow-moving traffic, stalled lifts or gondolas, and no air conditioning.<ref>http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/minister-resigns-over-massive-taiwan-blackout-reports-9125906</ref><ref>https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/en/news/3232877</ref><ref>http://www.straitstimes.com/asia/east-asia/widespread-power-outages-hit-taiwan</ref> Some emergency services were called to rescue trapped people and the police were manually directing traffic.<ref>http://www.scmp.com/news/china/society/article/2106935/taiwanese-minister-resigns-after-error-power-plant-plunges-668000</ref> And before [[2017 Summer Universiade]].<ref>{{cite news|title=全台大停電 世大運美國籃球隊受影響|publisher=聯合新聞網|date=2017-08-15|author=王敏旭|url=https://udn.com/news/story/6656/2643927|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170816153400/https://udn.com/news/story/6656/2643927|archivedate=2017-08-16|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Reactions==
Taipower responded by offering one day electricity charge cut from each household bill, which resulted in NT$270 million of revenue loss to the company.

[[President of the Republic of China|President]] [[Tsai Ing-wen]] apologized to the people of Taiwan through her [[Facebook page]], stating that electricity supply is a national security issue.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://time.com/4902500/taiwan-power-cut/|title=Massive Power Cut Affects Millions in Taiwan|author=|date=|website=Time|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref>

Economic Affairs Minister [[Chih-Kung Lee|Lee Chih-kung]] resigned shortly afterwards to take responsibility. [[Premier of the Republic of China|Premier]] [[Lin Chuan]] appointed Deputy Minister [[Shen Jong-chin]] as acting Minister to replace Lee.<ref>https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/en/news/3232877</ref>

CPC Corporation Chairperson [[Chen Chin-te]] resigned three days afterwards to take responsibility of the event which was then replaced by acting Chairperson [[Yang Wei-fuu]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/taiwan-power-outage/update-1-chief-of-taiwan-gas-supplier-cpc-resigns-after-power-blackout-idUSL4N1L41UF|title=UPDATE 1-Chief of Taiwan gas supplier CPC resigns after power blackout|author=|date=18 August 2017|publisher=|via=Reuters|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref> His resignation also was accepted by Premier Lin.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-taiwan-power-outage/taiwan-accepts-resignation-of-gas-supplier-chief-after-blackout-idUSKCN1AY0B8|title=Taiwan accepts resignation of gas supplier chief after blackout|author=|date=18 August 2017|publisher=|via=Reuters|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref>

==Damages==
The blackout caused at least US$3 million of loss or damages to over 150 companies in industrial parks and export processing zones.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-taiwan-power-outages/taiwan-power-outage-affected-151-companies-caused-3-million-in-damages-idUSKCN1AX0S3|title=Taiwan power outage affected 151 companies, caused $3 million in damages|author=|date=17 August 2017|publisher=|via=Reuters|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref>

==Aftermaths==
President Tsai promised that her administration would conduct a comprehensive review of the [[electrical grid]] of Taiwan to reexamine and strengthen it.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/asia/east-asia/taiwans-cpc-says-blackout-caused-by-flawed-power-replacement-process|title=Taiwan to review electrical grid after flawed power supply replacement process caused massive blackout|first=|last=hermesauto|date=16 August 2017|website=straitstimes.com|accessdate=4 November 2017}}</ref>
