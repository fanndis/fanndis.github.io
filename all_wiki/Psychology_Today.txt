'''''Psychology Today''''' is a [[magazine]] published every two months in the [[United States]] since 1967.

==History and mission==
Founded in 1967<ref name=bvlew>{{cite journal|author=Bruce V. Lewenstein|title=Was There Really a Popular Science" Boom"?|journal=Science, Technology, & Human Values|date=1987|url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/231582087|accessdate=24 June 2016}}</ref> by Nicolas Charney, [[Ph.D]], its intent is to make psychology literature more accessible to the general public. The magazine focuses on behavior and covers a range of topics including [[psychology]], [[neuroscience]], relationships, [[sexuality]], [[parenting]], health (including from the perspectives of [[alternative medicine]]), work,<ref name="britannica">{{cite encyclopedia|encyclopedia=Encyclopædia Britannica|title=Psychology Today| year=2013|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/1569831/Psychology-Today}}</ref> and the psychological aspects of current affairs.<ref name=MK/>

The magazine's website features [[Therapist|therapy]] and [[health professionals]] directories<ref name=MK/> and hundreds of [[blogs]] written by a wide variety of [[psychologist]]s, [[psychiatrist]]s, [[social worker]]s, [[medical doctor]]s, [[anthropologist]]s, [[sociologist]]s, and [[science journalist]]s. Its current [[editor-in-chief]] is Kaja Perina.<ref>{{cite web|last=Perina|first=Kaja|title=Psychology Today: Experts|url=http://www.psychologytoday.com/experts/kaja-perina|work=Psychology Today|publisher=Sussex Publishers, LLC|accessdate=27 February 2014}}</ref>

==Circulation==
In 1976 ''Psychology Today'' sold 1,026,872 copies.<ref name="bvlew"/> The circulation of the magazine was 1,171,362 copies in 1981 and 862,193 copies in 1986.<ref name="bvlew"/>

It has a circulation of 300,000 copies per issue as of 2017 and claims 15.02 people read each copy for a total audience of 3,755,000.<ref name=MK/> From June 2010 to June 2011, it was one of the top ten consumer magazines by [[newsstand]] sales.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://adage.com/article/media/magazines-fastest-growing-titles/229157/|title=Top 10 Consumer Magazines by Single Copy Sales (chart)|last=Ives|first=Nat|work=AdAge|date=9 August 2011}}</ref> In 2013 ''[[Adweek]]'' noted a 36 percent increase in the number of readers of ''Psychology Today'', while many magazines suffered readership declines.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.adweek.com/news/press/magazine-readership-growing-survey-shows-149863|title=Magazine Readership Growing, Survey Shows Digital readership up more than 80% in past year|last=Bazilian|first=Emma|work=Adweek|date=29 May 2013}}</ref>

==Leadership==
Owned and managed by the [[American Psychological Association]] from 1983 to 1987,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1983/02/23/business/advertising-psychology-today-sale-to-group-completed.html |title=Advertising: Psychology Today Sale To Group Completed|date= February 23, 1983|last=Dougherty|first=Phillip H.|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref> the publication is currently endorsed by the [[National Board for Certified Counselors]], which promotes [[Subscription business model|subscription]]s and offers professional credit for a small fee and assigned assessment for each article read.<ref>{{cite web|title=Certification Benefits|url=http://www.nbcc.org/benefits/pt-ce |department=NCC Service Center|publisher=National Board for Certified Counselors and Affiliates, Inc|accessdate=27 February 2014}}</ref>
