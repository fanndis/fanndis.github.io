'''Krissah Williams Thompson''' (born 1979) is an American journalist at ''[[The Washington Post]]''. In 2020 Thompson was named the Managing Editor of Diversity and Inclusion, and is the first African American woman to hold the position of Managing Editor at ''The Washington Post''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Farhi|first=Paul|date=2020-07-28|title=Krissah Thompson named The Washington Post's first managing editor for diversity and inclusion|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/media/krissah-thompson-named-the-washington-posts-first-managing-editor-for-diversity-and-inclusion/2020/07/28/2e033ffa-d0dc-11ea-8d32-1ebf4e9d8e0d_story.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Washington Post}}</ref>

== Research and career ==
Thompson has spent her entire professional career at ''The Washington Post''. She has written for Financial, National Politics and Style.<ref name=":0" /> She contributed to Being a Black Man,<ref name=":1">{{Cite book|title=Being a black man : at the corner of progress and peril|date=2007|publisher=PublicAffairs|others=Merida, Kevin., Washington Post Company.|isbn=978-1-58648-522-1|edition=1st|location=New York|oclc=133465423}}</ref> a collection of essays that covered race and Black identity.<ref name=":1" /> During the presidency of [[Barack Obama]], Thompson served as an editor of Style section.<ref name=":0" /> She covered [[Michelle Obama]] during the second term of Barack Obama.<ref name=":0" />

In the aftermath of the [[shooting of Michael Brown]], Thompson was acting bureau chief during the [[Ferguson unrest]]. She co-led the 2018 ''The Washington Post'' video-driven programme "The N-Word Project", which encouraged readers of the Post to document and share their views of the [[n-word]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=The N-Word|url=https://awards.journalists.org/entries/the-n-word/|access-date=2020-07-30|website=Online Journalism Awards|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=When was the last time you said the n-word? {{!}} The N-Word Project|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/national/when-was-the-last-time-you-said-the-n-word--the-n-word-project/2018/12/28/900f800b-a2b0-4bcd-b302-7f16e2d06111_video.html|access-date=2020-07-30|website=Washington Post|language=en}}</ref> The N-Word Project was nominated for an [[Emmy Award]] and shortlisted for an [[Online Journalism Award]].<ref name=":2" />

In 2020 Thompson was made Managing Editor of Diversity and Inclusion at ''The Washington Post''.<ref name=":0" /> She is the first African American woman to hold the position of Managing Editor.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-30|title=Washington Post Appoints First Black Woman Managing Editor|url=https://www.blackenterprise.com/the-washington-post-elects-first-black-woman-to-become-a-managing-editor/|access-date=2020-07-30|website=Black Enterprise|language=en-US}}</ref> At the same time, the Post announced it would hire several journalists to cover issues related to race.<ref name=":0" /> Thompson has said that she wants ''The Washington Post to look like America and the communities we cover‚Äù.<ref name=":0" />
