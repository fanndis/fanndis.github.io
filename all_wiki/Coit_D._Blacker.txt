'''Coit Dennis Blacker''' is the Olivier Nomellini Professor in International Studies in the School of Humanities and Sciences at [[Stanford University]]. He served as [[Executive Office of the President|Special Assistant to the President of the United States]] for National Security Affairs and Senior Director for Russian, Ukrainian and Eurasian Affairs at the [[United States National Security Council|National Security Council]] under [[National Security Advisor (United States)|National Security Advisor]] [[Anthony Lake]] during the [[Presidency of Bill Clinton|Clinton administration]]. From 2003 to 2012, he was the director of the [[Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imperialvalleynews.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=4420&Itemid=2 |title=Barack Obama Tabs Stanford Russia Expert Michael McFaul to National Security Posts |date=22 February 2009 |work=Imperial Valley News |accessdate=9 August 2010 |location=[[Yuma, Arizona]] |url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100208103744/http://imperialvalleynews.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=4420&Itemid=2 |archivedate=8 February 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://fsi.stanford.edu/people/coitdblacker/|title=FSI &#124; CISAC - Coit D. Blacker}}</ref> at [[Stanford University]] and is a current Study Group Member of the [[National Commission on Terrorism]].
