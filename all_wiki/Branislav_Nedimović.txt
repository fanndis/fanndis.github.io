'''Branislav Nedimović''' ({{Lang-sr-Cyrl|Бранислав Недимовић}}, born 27 November 1977) is a Serbian politician. He has served as the [[Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Economy (Serbia)|Minister of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Economy]] in the [[Government of Serbia]] since 11 August 2016.

== Biography ==
Nedimović completed elementary school and high school in his hometown of [[Sremska Mitrovica]]. He graduated from the [[University of Novi Sad Faculty of Law|University of Novi Sad, Faculty of Law]] with a bachelor's degree.<ref name=":0" />{{Clarify|date=April 2020|reason=Degree name?}}

He was elected a Member of the [[National Assembly (Serbia)|National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia]] in 2003 as a member of the [[Democratic Party of Serbia]]. He was re-elected MP in 2008, but resigned due to accepting the position of the mayor of [[Sremska Mitrovica]]. He left the Democratic Party of Serbia in the same year, and was active in the "Mitrovica European Region" movement, which has had cooperation with the [[Democratic Party (Serbia)|Democratic Party]] (not the same as the Democratic Party of Serbia) since 2009 which he joined at the end of 2010. He served as the mayor of Sremska Mitrovica until 2016.<ref name=":0" />

In 2015, he joined the [[Serbian Progressive Party]], and since 2016 he has been a member of the presidency. He is also the Vice President of the board of directors of the National Alliance for Local and Economic Development.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Branislav Nedimović|url=https://www.istinomer.rs/akter/branislav-nedimovic/|website=Istinomer|language=sr-RS|access-date=13 May 2020}}</ref>

On 11 August 2016, he was appointed as a [[Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Economy (Serbia)|Minister of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Economy]] in [[Second cabinet of Aleksandar Vučić|Vučić's administration]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Састав Владе|url=https://www.srbija.gov.rs/sastav/177/sastav-vlade.php|last=srbija.gov.rs|website=www.srbija.gov.rs|language=sr|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

In February 2017, the [[Prime Minister of Serbia]] [[Aleksandar Vučić]] declared that he will run for president at the [[2017 Serbian presidential election]]s.<ref>{{Cite web|title=SNS: Jednoglasno – Vučić kandidat za predsednika|url=https://www.b92.net/info/vesti/index.php?yyyy=2017&mm=02&dd=14&nav_category=11&nav_id=1230658|website=B92.net|language=sr-Latn-RS|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> He won the elections in the first round beating [[Saša Janković]] with 55.06% of the votes and was sworn in as the [[President of Serbia]] on 31 May 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Vucic Sworn In as Serbia’s Next President|url=https://balkaninsight.com/2017/05/31/vucic-sworn-in-as-serbia-s-next-president-05-31-2017/|date=3 May 2017|website=Balkan Insight|language=en-US|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> Weeks later, he gave mandate to [[Ana Brnabić]] to form the new cabinet. On 29 June 2017, the cabinet of Ana Brnabić was formed, with Nedimović keeping his office.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Surk|first=Barbara|date=2 June 2017|title=Serbia Gets Its First Female, and First Openly Gay, Premier|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/28/world/europe/serbia-ana-brnabic-prime-minister.html|access-date=12 May 2020|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

In April 2020, he was named as the head of the Serbian Crisis Staff for [[Nišava District|Nišava]] and [[Toplica District]] to combat the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ministar Nedimović na čelu kriznog štaba za nišavski i toplički okrug|url=https://www.blic.rs/vesti/drustvo/ministar-nedimovic-na-celu-kriznog-staba-za-nisavski-i-toplicki-okrug/tlq68xe|last=Tanjug|website=Blic.rs|language=sr|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>

He was selected as a ballot carrier for the Serbian Progressive Party for the [[2020 Serbian parliamentary election]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nedimović prvi na listi SNS odmah iza Vučića, nema Nikolića; Brnabić i Vulin podržali SNS|url=https://www.rtv.rs/sr_lat/politika/nedimovic-prvi-na-listi-sns-odmah-iza-vucica-nema-nikolica_1099370.html|last=Vojvodine|first=Javna medijska ustanova JMU Radio-televizija|website=JMU Radio-televizija Vojvodine|access-date=18 May 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Nedimović has been playing [[Association football|football]] since elementary school, and currently plays for FK Sloga Zasavica in the Sremska Mitrovica League. Among all registered [[UEFA]] players, except for Branislav Nedimović, there is no minister of any government in Europe to officially play football for a team competing in an organized league.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ministar daje golove i dobija žute kartone|url=http://www.politika.rs/sr/clanak/381615/Fudbal/Ministar-daje-golove-i-dobija-zute-kartone|last=Filipović|first=Miomir|website=Politika Online|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref> Nedimović is married and has two children.<ref name=":0" />
