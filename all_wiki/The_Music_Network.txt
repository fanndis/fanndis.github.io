'''''The Music Network''''' is an Australian [[magazine]] launched in 1994 by founder John Woodruff and acquired by Jake Challenor in 2017, who serves as its Publisher & Editor.

== History ==
''The Music Network'' was founded by John Woodruff in 1994, initially as a paper-based publication for the Australian music industry.<ref name="musicoz"/> This included record labels, media networks, music and DVD retailers, radio and television programmers and music directors, sales representatives, artist managers, music publishers and concert promoters.<ref name="musicoz">{{cite web|url=http://www.musicoz.org/about/MUSICOZ_AWARDS/prizes/The_Music_Network/|title=The Music Network|publisher=Musicoz.org|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/68aI517Ba?url=http://www.musicoz.org/about/MUSICOZ_AWARDS/prizes/The_Music_Network/|archivedate=2012-06-21}}</ref> In January 2009, the magazine was sold to the music marketing and publishing group Peer Group Media, which is under the ownership of Adam Zammit, who replaced Woodruff as the new owner of ''The Music Network''.<ref name="musicoz"/><ref name="apra09">{{cite web|url=http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/news/Industrynews/TheMusicNetworkMagazineentersanewera.aspx|title=The Music Network Magazine enters a new era|publisher=[[Australasian Performing Right Association]] (APRA)|date=2009-01-13|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/68aJ5lWFF?url=http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/news/Industrynews/TheMusicNetworkMagazineentersanewera.aspx|archivedate=2012-06-21}}</ref> In March 2009, former [[Future Entertainment]] general manager Jade Harley, became the magazine's new managing editor.<ref name="reboots">{{cite web|url=http://peergroupmedia.com/news/tmn/the-music-network-re-boots-for-a-new-generation/|title=The Music Network Re-Boots For A New Generation!|publisher=Peergroupmedia.com|date=2009-06-09|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/68b7ACpjI?url=http://peergroupmedia.com/news/tmn/the-music-network-re-boots-for-a-new-generation/|archivedate=2012-06-21}}</ref> ''The Music Network'''s website re-launched on 8 June 2009, with a new design, logo and layout.<ref name="musicoz"/>

== Content ==
''The Music Network'' is issued weekly and features the latest music news, radio airplay charts and statistics, music sales data for both [[Compact disc|physical]] and [[Music download|digital]] releases, tour and live event information, interviews with local and international artists, as well as information on new singles released to Australian radio, retail and other media outlets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themusicnetwork.com/about|title=About|publisher=Themusicnetwork.com|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/68b8PVUbQ?url=http://www.themusicnetwork.com/about|accessdate=2012-06-22|archivedate=2012-06-21}}</ref> It also publishes the TopSwaps chart which monitors the number of songs downloaded from file-sharing sites each week.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/news/opinion/face-the-music-thieves/story-e6frezz0-1225779666072|title=Face the music, thieves|work=[[The Daily Telegraph (Sydney)|The Daily Telegraph]]|publisher=[[News Limited]]|first=Kathy|last=McCabe|date=2009-09-26|accessdate=2012-06-22}}</ref>

===Country Hot 50===
''The Music Network'' operates the "TMN Country Hot 50" chart in co-operation with [[Radiomonitor|Radio Monitor]]. The chart includes airplay data and runs Friday to Thursday, with a new chart published every Friday morning. The chart replaced the previous "TMN Country Airplay Chart", and is the "the most comprehensive country airplay chart in the history of Australian radio".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://themusicnetwork.com/tmm-radio-monitor-country-hot-50/|title=Miranda Lambert tops TMNâ€™s all-new Country Hot 50 airplay chart|work=[[The Music Network]]|first=Zanda|last=Wilson|date=November 8, 2020|accessdate=December 5, 2020}}</ref>
