'''Jahron Anthony Brathwaite''' (born July 3, 1993),<ref name="Schwartz, Danny">{{cite web|last1=Delerme|first1=Felipe|title=Is This Proof That PartyNextDoor Has Been Lying About His Age for Years?|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/is-partynextdoor-actually-31-years-old-news.23092.html}}</ref> known professionally as '''PartyNextDoor''' (stylized in [[all caps]]), is a Canadian singer, songwriter, rapper and record producer.

PartyNextDoor was the first artist signed to [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s [[OVO Sound]] [[record label]] in 2013, in a joint-venture with [[Warner Records]].<ref name="Coscarelli, Joe">{{cite journal  |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/17/arts/music/partynextdoor-drake-rihanna-interview.html |title=PartyNextDoor Is Finding His Voice, and a Bigger Spotlight |journal=[[New York Times]] |publisher=New York Times |location=New York |date=August 16, 2016 |accessdate=September 6, 2016 |author=Coscarelli, Joe}}</ref> He then released his first [[extended play|EP]] self-titled ''[[PartyNextDoor (EP)|PartyNextDoor]]'' later that year.<ref>{{cite journal  |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/listen-partynextdoor-wus-goodcurious |title=Listen: PartyNextDoor'Wus Good/Curious' |journal=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |publisher=Complex Media |location=New York |date=May 12, 2013 |accessdate=August 24, 2013 |author=Josephs, Brian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapfix.mtv.com/2013/07/01/drakes-ovo-signee-partynextdoor-drops-self-titled-debut/ |title=Drake's OVO Signee PartyNextDoor Drops Self-Titled Debut |publisher=[[MTV]] |date=July 1, 2013 |accessdate=August 24, 2013 |author=Alexis, Nadeska}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/singles/id.24793/title.PartyNextDoor+f.+Drake-Over+Here |title=PartyNextDoor f. Drake – Over Here |work=HipHopDX |publisher=Cheri Media Group |date=June 24, 2013 |accessdate=August 24, 2013}}</ref> He subsequently released ''[[PartyNextDoor Two]]'' and ''[[PNDColours]]'' in 2014 and ''[[PartyNextDoor 3]]'' in 2016. PartyNextDoor has collaborated with a number of artists, including [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Big Sean]] and [[Jeremih]]. He has also seen success as a songwriter, having penned "[[Work (Rihanna song)|Work]]", which was released on [[Rihanna]]'s album ''[[Anti (album)|Anti]]'' and peaked at number-one on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], along with more songs such as "[[Wild Thoughts]]" which peaked at number 2 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name="Coscarelli, Joe" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/ogdenpayne/2016/11/21/inside-partynextdoors-ascent-from-chart-topping-songwriter-to-methodical-businessman/#61567e7a6b87|title=Inside PartyNextDoor's Ascent From Chart-Topping Songwriter To Methodical Businessman|last=Payne|first=Ogden|newspaper=Forbes|access-date=2017-01-22}}</ref>

==Early life==
Jahron Anthony Brathwaite was born to a [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] mother and a [[Trinidadians|Trinidadian]] father in [[Mississauga]], [[Ontario]], Canada.<ref name="Delerme, Felipe">{{cite web|last1=Delerme|first1=Felipe|title=PartyNextDoor Speaks About his Music for the First Time|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/04/21/partynextdoor-cover-story-interview|website=Fader|accessdate=28 November 2017}}</ref> Braithwaite was inspired by the music his father played to him when he was younger such as [[Jodeci]], [[Boyz II Men]], [[Blackstreet]] and [[112 (band)|112]].

==Musical career==
===2007–2014: Career beginnings and debut album===
Having made [[Electronic music|electronic]]-infused [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] under his real name Jahron Brathwaite [Jahron B],  he signed a publishing deal with [[Warner/Chappell Music|Warner/Chappell]] as a songwriter, under the name PartyNextDoor, at age 18.<ref name="Delerme, Felipe"/>{{failed verification|date=June 2018}} His first mixtape, ''[[PartyNextDoor (EP)|PartyNextDoor]]'', was released to the [[iTunes Store]] on July 1, 2013.<ref name="apple1">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/partynextdoor/id666647689 |title=PARTYNEXTDOOR by PARTYNEXTDOOR |work=[[iTunes Store]] |publisher=[[Apple Inc.|Apple]] |accessdate=August 24, 2013}}</ref> It entered on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart at number six with sales of 2,000 copies<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1569442/chance-the-rapper-partynextdoor-chart-with-free-mixtapes |title=Chance the Rapper, PARTYNEXTDOOR Chart With Free Mixtapes |journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |location=New York |date=July 11, 2013 |accessdate=November 8, 2013 |author=Greenwald, David}}</ref> and peaked at number 34 on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart for the week of July 20, 2013.<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/2013-07-20/r-and-b-hip-hop-albums |title=Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums |journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |location=New York |date=July 20, 2013 |accessdate=November 8, 2013|url-access=subscription }}</ref> He performed background vocals on "Own It" and "Come Thru" from Drake's third studio album, ''[[Nothing Was the Same]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ca.complex.com/music/2014/07/partynextdoor-announces-new-album-release-date|title=PARTYNEXTDOOR Announces "PARTYNEXTDOOR Two" Release Date}}</ref>

His debut studio album ''[[PartyNextDoor Two]]'' was released on July 30, 2014. The record featured singles like "Thirsty", "FWU", "East Liberty" and the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charted "[[Recognize (song)|Recognize]]", featuring [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]. Later that year, on December 3, 2014, PartyNextDoor released the 4-track EP titled ''PNDColours'', with the follow up COLOURS 2, released in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ortiz|first1=Edwin|title=PARTYNEXTDOOR Shares Four New Songs and Reveals World Tour Dates|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/12/partynextdoor-world-tour-dates-and-four-song-package|website=Complex|accessdate=31 January 2015|date=3 December 2014}}</ref> In 2015, he produced three songs on Drake's ''[[If You're Reading This It's Too Late]]'': "Legend", "[[Preach (Drake song)|Preach]]" and "Wednesday Night Interlude".<ref name="Delerme, Felipe"/>

===2016–present: ''PartyNextDoor 3'', ''Partymobile'' and ''Partypack''===
[[File:PartyNextDoor November 2016.jpg|thumbnail|upright|right|180px|PartyNextDoor performing in November 2016.]]
PartyNextDoor earned his first number-one song as a songwriter when on January 27, 2016, Rihanna released the lead single "[[Work (Rihanna song)|Work]]" from her eighth studio album, ''[[Anti (album)|Anti]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BCHDsmVCy8E/|title=PND on Instagram: "My first #1 as a songwriter. Thank you @badgalriri and @champagnepapi for the opportunity. Album on the way"|website=Instagram|access-date=2016-04-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/partynextdoors-reference-track-for-rihannas-work-surfaces-online-news.20333.html|title=PartyNextDoor's Reference Track For Rihanna's "Work" Surfaces Online|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2016-04-10}}</ref> The song maintained its spot at number-one on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] for nine consecutive weeks. He also penned the song "[[Anti (album)|Sex with Me]]" on the same album.<ref name="Coscarelli, Joe"/> On March 25, 2016, PartyNextDoor released "[[Come and See Me]]", which features fellow artist [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], from his upcoming second studio album, ''[[PartyNextDoor 3]]'' (also known as ''P3''). A music video directed by affiliate and collaborator Adrian Martinez and featuring appearances by [[Kylie Jenner]], [[Big Sean]], and [[Jhené Aiko]] was released on [[Snapchat]] on June 23, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/03/01/amir-obe-the-only-video-premiere|title=Amir Obe Bathes In Pink And Purple Light In "The Only" Video|website=The FADER|access-date=2016-07-28}}</ref> On June 15, 2016, Jeremih called into Real 92.3 LA to announce a joint album with PartyNextDoor called ''Late Night Party''. On July 2, 2016, he released another single, "Like That", featuring [[Jeremih]] and [[Lil Wayne]], on OVO Sound radio.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.highsnobiety.com/2016/07/09/partynextdoor-like-dat-lil-wayne-jeremih/|title=PARTYNEXTDOOR Drops Official Version of "Like Dat" Featuring Lil Wayne & Jeremih|date=7 April 2017}}</ref> On July 21, 2016, PartyNextDoor announced the release date for his second studio album ''PartyNextDoor 3'' for August 12, 2016 and released "Not Nice", the record's second single.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2016/07/partynextdoor-announces-new-album-shares-upbeat-track-not-nice-listen/|title=PartyNextDoor announces new album, shares upbeat track "Not Nice" — listen|date=22 July 2016}}</ref>

He and Jeremih toured in 2016 and had plans to release a joint project.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/new-partynextdoor-music-previewed-on-model-chantel-jeffries-snapchat-news.24510.html? |title=PartyNextDoor and Jeremih to release music |last=Smith |first=Trevor |date=October 3, 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=October 3, 2016}}</ref>

On June 4, 2017, PartyNextDoor released ''[[Colours 2]]'' without any prior announcement. A short film for the EP was released on June 12, 2017.

On September 29, 2017, he released an EP titled [[Seven Days (EP)|''Seven Days'']], which included guest appearances from [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]] and [[Rick Ross]]. Seven Days is also considered as one of his best projects.

After a lead artist hiatus PartyNextDoor returned in December of 2019 with two singles "The News" and "[[Loyal (PartyNextDoor song)|Loyal]]", with the latter featuring OVO Sound co-founder, Drake. They served as the lead singles of his album ''[[Partymobile]]'' which he tweeted would be released in February.<ref name="partymobile">{{cite web |url=http://hiphop-n-more.com/2020/01/partynextdoor-new-album-partymobile-delayed-to-february/ |title=PARTYNEXTDOOR announces new album 'PARTYMOBILE'; delays it to February |work=HipHopNMore |date=January 29, 2020 |accessdate=January 30, 2020}}</ref> It then got pushed back to March 27, 2020 and released on that date.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/partynextdoor-announces-new-album-partymobile-shares-new-song-listen/ |title=PARTYNEXTDOOR Announces New Album PARTYMOBILE, Shares New Song: Listen |work=Pitchfork |date=February 28, 2020 |accessdate=February 28, 2020 |author=Minsker, Evan}}</ref>

On October 15, 2020, Party surprise-announced the release of ''Partypack'', a 7-song EP containing "previously unreleased" songs, including the 2014 song "Persian Rugs". The EP was released the following day.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/partynextdoor-reveals-partypack-tracklist-and-midnight-release-news.119677.html|title=Partynextdoor Reveals "Partypack" Tracklist & Midnight Release|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Lilah|first=Rose|date=October 15, 2020|accessdate=October 16, 2020}}</ref>
