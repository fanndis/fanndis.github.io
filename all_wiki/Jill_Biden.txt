<!--DO NOT PUT "Dr." HERE, PER WP:CREDENTIAL-->'''Jill Tracy Jacobs Biden'''<!--DO NOT PUT "Ph.D." or "Ed.D." HERE, PER WP:CREDENTIAL--> (née '''Jacobs''', formerly '''Stevenson'''; born June 3, 1951) is an American educator who was the [[second lady of the United States]] from 2009 to 2017. She is married to [[Joe Biden]], the [[president-elect of the United States]], who is set to be inaugurated on January 20, 2021, at which time she will become [[First Lady of the United States|first lady]].

Born in [[Hammonton, New Jersey]], she grew up in [[Willow Grove, Pennsylvania]]. She married Joe Biden in 1977, becoming stepmother to [[Beau Biden|Beau]] and [[Hunter Biden|Hunter]], his two sons from his first marriage. Biden and her husband also have a daughter together, [[Ashley Biden|Ashley]], born in 1981. She has a bachelor's degree and a doctoral degree from the [[University of Delaware]], as well as master's degrees from [[West Chester University of Pennsylvania|West Chester University]] and [[Villanova University]]. She taught English and reading in high schools for 13 years and taught adolescents with emotional disabilities at a psychiatric hospital.

From 1993 to 2008, Biden was an English and writing instructor at [[Delaware Technical & Community College]]. Since 2009, she has been a professor of English at [[Northern Virginia Community College]] and is thought to be the first wife of a vice president to hold a paying job during her husband's tenure. She is the founder of the Biden Breast Health Initiative non-profit organization, co-founder of the Book Buddies program, co-founder of the Biden Foundation, is active in Delaware [[Boots on the Ground]], and is co-founder of Joining Forces with [[Michelle Obama]].

==Role in 2008 presidential campaign==
[[File:Obamas and Bidens.jpg|thumb|left|188px|Biden at the August 2008 announcement of her husband becoming Barack Obama's running mate]]
Despite personally opposing the [[Iraq War]], Biden had not wanted her husband to run in the [[United States presidential election, 2004|2004 presidential election]], to the point where she interrupted one strategy meeting discussing the possibility by entering in a swimsuit with the word "NO" inscribed on her stomach.<ref name="nyt-why"/>  But following [[George W. Bush presidential campaign, 2004|George W. Bush's reelection in 2004]], she urged her husband to run again for president,<ref name="npr010108"/> later saying: "I literally wore black for a week. And I said to Joe, 'You've got to change this, you have to change this.'"<ref name="wapo-cc"/>  During [[Joe Biden presidential campaign, 2008|Joe Biden's 2008 campaign to be the Democratic nominee]], she continued to teach during the week and would join him for campaigning on weekends.<ref name="npr010108"/> She said she would have taken an activist role in addressing education as her chief focus of concern as a potential first lady.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/2007/article/0,28804,1660946_1661078,00.html | title=Democrat Candidate Spouses: Jill Biden | magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] | accessdate=August 23, 2008 | date=September 13, 2007 | archive-date=August 24, 2008 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080824045651/http://www.time.com/time/specials/2007/article/0,28804,1660946_1661078,00.html | url-status=live }}</ref> She also said that she took an apolitical stance when she was with her husband and would not seek inclusion in Cabinet meetings.<ref name="npr010108">{{cite news|author=Norris, Michelle|first=|date=January 1, 2008|title=Presidential Candidates' Spouses: Jill Biden|work=[[All Things Considered]]|publisher=[[NPR]]|url=https://www.npr.org/transcripts/17764989|url-status=live|accessdate=November 17, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200608143807/https://www.npr.org/transcripts/17764989|archive-date=August 28, 2008}}</ref>

Once her husband was selected as the running mate to Democratic presidential nominee [[Barack Obama]],  she began campaigning again.

===Role in 2012 presidential campaign===
In the [[2012 United States presidential election|2012 U.S. presidential election]], in which her husband was running for re-election as vice president, Biden played a modest role.<ref name="pol-2012">{{cite news | url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1012/81877.html | title=Jill Biden tiptoes into 2012 election | author=Slack, Donovan | newspaper=[[Politico]] | date=October 1, 2012 | access-date=October 20, 2012 | archive-date=May 29, 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150529131425/http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1012/81877.html | url-status=live }}</ref>    She did not cut back on her teaching schedule and made few solo campaign appearances.<ref name="pol-2012"/> This reflected her continuing distaste for both politics and public speaking, even though the Obama campaign considered her valuable in connecting to military families, teachers, and women.<ref name="pol-2012"/>

===Second term===

Following the re-election of Obama and her husband on November 6, 2012, Biden began a second term as second lady. ==Role in 2020 presidential campaign==
[[Image:Jill Biden (48537868461).jpg|thumb|left|Biden at an August 2019 campaign event]]

The [[Joe Biden 2020 presidential campaign]] was officially announced on April 25, 2019.<ref name="tc2020"/>  A ''[[Town & Country (magazine)|Town and Country]]'' magazine headline declared that "Jill Biden Might Just Be Joe Biden's Greatest Political Asset".<ref name="tc2020">{{cite news | url=https://www.townandcountrymag.com/society/politics/a26944878/jill-biden-joe-biden-wife-facts/ | title=Why Jill Biden Might Just Be Joe Biden's Greatest Political Asset | first=Maggie | last=Maloney | date=April 25, 2019 | magazine=[[Town & Country (magazine)|Town and Country]] | access-date=April 27, 2019 | archive-date=April 25, 2019 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190425153716/https://www.townandcountrymag.com/society/politics/a26944878/jill-biden-joe-biden-wife-facts/ | url-status=live }}</ref>

Days later, Biden addressed the matter of women who had [[Joe Biden#Allegations of inappropriate physical contact|accused her husband of physical contact that had made them feel uncomfortable]] by saying, "I think what you don't realize is how many people approach Joe. Biden continued to teach at NOVA during 2019, at one point telling a reporter, "I'm here grading research papers in between interviews."<ref name="dt-talks-book"/> She staged appearances without her husband in early contest states [[2020 Iowa Democratic caucuses|such as Iowa]], in some cases accompanied by a granddaughter.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://whotv.com/2019/06/28/jill-biden-visits-iowa-supporters-react-to-joe-bidens-debate-performance/ | title=Jill Biden Visits Iowa; Supporters React to Joe Biden's Debate Performance | date=June 28, 2019 | first=Monica | last=Madden | publisher=[[WHO-TV]] | access-date=June 30, 2019 | archive-date=June 30, 2019 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190630113045/https://whotv.com/2019/06/28/jill-biden-visits-iowa-supporters-react-to-joe-bidens-debate-performance/ | url-status=live }}</ref> She attracted notice during one campaign stop in New Hampshire when she emphasized the electability argument in favor of her husband, saying, "you know, your candidate might be better on, I don't know, health care, than Joe is, but you've got to look at who's going to win this election, and maybe you have to swallow a little bit and say, 'OK, I personally like so-and-so better,' but your bottom line has to be that we have to beat Trump."<ref>{{cite news | url=https://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/458027-jill-biden-tells-voters-to-pick-husband-your-candidate-may-be-better-on | title=Jill Biden tells voters to pick husband: 'Your candidate may be better' on some policies, but 'we have to beat Trump' | first=Justin | last=Wise | newspaper=[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]] | date=August 19, 2019 | access-date=September 28, 2019 | archive-date=September 28, 2019 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190928170533/https://thehill.com/homenews/campaign/458027-jill-biden-tells-voters-to-pick-husband-your-candidate-may-be-better-on | url-status=live }}</ref> 

Once [[Hunter Biden]] became a Republican political focus during the [[Trump–Ukraine scandal|scandal related to the Ukraine that led to presidential impeachment]], she was outspoken: "Hunter did nothing wrong. And that's the bottom line."<ref name="nyt-why"/> The strain of the subsequent [[Impeachment trial of Donald Trump|impeachment trial]] was enough to fracture a friendship she had with South Carolina senator [[Lindsey Graham]], who repeatedly called for Hunter Biden to be questioned as a witness at the trial.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/jill-biden-longer-considers-gop-sen-lindsey-graham/story?id=68726067 | title=Jill Biden says she no longer considers GOP Sen. Lindsey Graham a friend | author-first=Shoshana | author-last=Dubnow | publisher=[[ABC News]] | date=February 3, 2020 | access-date=February 16, 2020 | archive-date=February 15, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200215234715/https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/jill-biden-longer-considers-gop-sen-lindsey-graham/story?id=68726067 | url-status=live }}</ref>

Biden played a more active role in this presidential campaign than she had in her husband's two prior ones,<ref name="wapo-finally"/> and for the first time, Biden reluctantly took a leave of absence from NOVA for the spring 2020 semester so that she could be on the campaign trail full-time.<ref name="nyt-why">{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/02/01/us/politics/joe-jill-biden-2020.html | title=In Iowa, a Former Second Lady Campaigns to Be the First | author-first=Katie | author-last=Glueck | author2-first=Steve | author2-last=Eder | newspaper=[[The New York Times]] | date=February 2, 2020 | page=A16 | access-date=February 12, 2020 | archive-date=July 19, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200719231401/https://www.nytimes.com/2020/02/01/us/politics/joe-jill-biden-2020.html | url-status=live }}</ref> She took training in [[online teaching]] once the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States]] struck.<ref name="wapo-finally"/> She indicated that she intended to resume teaching at NOVA even if her husband were to be elected.<ref name="wapo-finally"/>

In the weeks leading up to the Iowa caucuses, she sometimes staged more campaign appearances in that state than her husband did.<ref name="nyt-why"/> She gave out her campaign e-mail address to voters in case they wanted to ask her follow-up questions.<ref name="wapo-closer"/> In joint appearances, she sometimes spoke after he did, acting in the "closer" role.<ref name="wapo-closer">{{cite news | url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2020/01/16/jill-biden-tries-close-deal-her-husband-one-tiny-iowa-town-time/ | title=Jill Biden tries to close the deal for her husband, one tiny Iowa town at a time | author-first=Holly | author-last=Bailey | newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] | date=January 16, 2020 | access-date=February 13, 2020 | archive-date=May 23, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200523173836/https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2020/01/16/jill-biden-tries-close-deal-her-husband-one-tiny-iowa-town-time/ | url-status=live }}</ref> After experiencing a number of victories around the nation, she gained some media attention at the March 3 [[Results of the 2020 Democratic Party presidential primaries#Super Tuesday|Super Tuesday primaries]] during her husband's speech when she physically blocked a protester from getting at him.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.tampabay.com/news/nation-world/2020/03/04/were-jill-biden-and-symone-sanders-the-real-winners-of-super-tuesday-twitter-thinks-so/?utm_expid=.rZxRlJI0T86fmAabR1Jv8w.0&utm_referrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.bing.com%2F | title=Were Jill Biden and Symone Sanders the real winners of Super Tuesday? Twitter thinks so | author-first=Daniel | author-last=Figueroa IV | newspaper=[[Tampa Bay Times]] | date=March 4, 2020 | access-date=March 14, 2020 | archive-date=October 8, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201008170021/https://www.tampabay.com/news/nation-world/2020/03/04/were-jill-biden-and-symone-sanders-the-real-winners-of-super-tuesday-twitter-thinks-so/?utm_expid=.rZxRlJI0T86fmAabR1Jv8w.0&utm_referrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.bing.com%2F | url-status=live }}</ref> Asked about the [[stiff-arm]] she employed, she said, "I'm a good Philly girl."<ref name="pi-philly"/>

With her husband having become the presumptive Democratic nominee, in June 2020, she published the children's book ''Joey: The Story of Joe Biden'', which portrayed him as having been "brave and adventurous" as a child despite having a stutter that he was bullied for.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://people.com/politics/dr-jill-biden-talks-her-new-campaign-trail-project-and-what-she-learned-about-her-husband/ | title=Dr. Jill Biden on Her Campaign Trail Project & What She Wants People to Know About Her Husband | author-first=Sam | author-last=Gillette | author2-first=Ally | author2-last=Mauch | date=June 30, 2020 | magazine=[[People (magazine)|People]] | access-date=July 5, 2020 | archive-date=July 5, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200705195034/https://people.com/politics/dr-jill-biden-talks-her-new-campaign-trail-project-and-what-she-learned-about-her-husband/ | url-status=live }}</ref> In July 2020, she spoke out about the [[impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on education]], appearing in a video with her husband to emphasize that she understood the frustrations that children, parents, and teachers were having with virtual education substitutes but saying that "Schools and parents alike want a clear, science-based strategy, not mixed messages and ultimatums."<ref>{{cite news | url=https://news.yahoo.com/biden-outlines-school-reopening-plan-194338722.html | title=Biden outlines school reopening plan amid pandemic | agency=[[Associated Press]] | publisher=[[Yahoo! News]] | date=July 17, 2020 | access-date=July 18, 2020 | archive-date=July 18, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200718105836/https://news.yahoo.com/biden-outlines-school-reopening-plan-194338722.html | url-status=live }}</ref> She criticized U.S. Secretary of Education [[Betsy DeVos]] for what she saw as political motivations in advocating a reopening of schools no matter what and said that "the first thing [Joe Biden]'s going to do is pick a secretary of education, who is a public school educator and has experience in the classroom. I mean I hear that, again and again and again – no more Betsy DeVos."<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/politics/jill-biden-promises-if-bidens-elected-no-more-betsy-devos/ar-BB16xtQs | title=Jill Biden promises if Biden's elected 'no more Betsy DeVos' | author-first=Tal | author-last=Axelrod | publisher=[[MSN]] | date=July 9, 2020 | access-date=July 18, 2020 | archive-date=July 18, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200718110751/https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/politics/jill-biden-promises-if-bidens-elected-no-more-betsy-devos/ar-BB16xtQs | url-status=live }}</ref>

She was heavily involved in the vice-presidential selection process that resulted in Senator [[Kamala D. Harris]] being chosen.<ref name="wapo-finally"/> On the second night of the virtual [[2020 Democratic National Convention]], Biden spoke from the classroom at Brandywine High School, where she had once taught English.<ref name="nyt-brandywine"/> She drew parallels between family suffering and the plight of the country, saying, "How do you make a broken family whole? The same way you make a nation whole. With love and understanding and with small acts of kindness, with bravery, with unwavering faith."<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.politico.com/news/2020/08/19/jill-biden-dnc-speech-398335 | title=Jill Biden opens up about family in candid DNC appearance | author-first=Matthew | author-last=Choi | work=[[Politico]] | date=August 19, 2020 | access-date=August 21, 2020 | archive-date=August 20, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200820233042/https://www.politico.com/news/2020/08/19/jill-biden-dnc-speech-398335 | url-status=live }}</ref> During the final stretch of the general election, she campaigned in the [[Delaware Valley]] region of Pennsylvania, near her home town, emphasizing the importance of the [[swing state]] and of women voting, saying, "You will decide, you, the women, will decide the future of this state and this state may determine the entire election."<ref>{{Cite news|last=Do|first=Trang|date=October 8, 2020|title=Dr. Jill Biden Campaigns In Delaware, Montgomery Counties, Where 'Women Will Decide The Future Of This State'|publisher=[[KYW-TV]]|url=https://philadelphia.cbslocal.com/2020/10/08/dr-jill-biden-campaigns-in-delaware-montgomery-counties/|url-status=live|access-date=October 10, 2020|archive-date=October 16, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201016204822/https://philadelphia.cbslocal.com/2020/10/08/dr-jill-biden-campaigns-in-delaware-montgomery-counties/}}</ref>

== 2020 presidential transition==
Biden's husband was elected president and will take office on January 20, 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Martin|first=Jonathan|last2=Burns|first2=Alexander|date=November 7, 2020|title=Biden Wins Presidency, Ending Four Tumultuous Years Under Trump|newspaper=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/11/07/us/politics/biden-election.html|access-date=November 7, 2020|archive-date=November 7, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201107171052/https://www.nytimes.com/2020/11/07/us/politics/biden-election.html|url-status=live}}</ref> She will become the first ever [[Italian American]] first lady.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.repubblica.it/dossier/esteri/elezioni-usa-2020-presidenziali-biden-trump/2020/11/07/news/jill_biden_presidenziali_stati_uniti-273533926/ | title=Jill Biden, mai una First Lady italoamericana.
