'''Ryan Destiny Irons''' (born January 8, 1995)<ref>{{cite tweet |first= Ryan |last= Destiny |author-link= Ryan Destiny |user=RyanDestiny |number=818145024697335809 |date= January 8, 2017|title= Ryan Destiny on Twitter: "23 years young"|link= https://twitter.com/RyanDestiny/status/818145024697335809 |access-date= January 18, 2017 }}</ref> is an American actress, singer and songwriter. While she had been working in the music industry since childhood, Destiny first rose to fame as a member of the girl-group Love Dollhouse in 2013. Destiny is best known for her role in the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] TV musical drama ''[[Star (TV series)|Star]]''.

==Early life and education==
Destiny was born in Detroit, Michigan.<ref name=Yak052611>{{cite journal|title=Never Say Never to Detroit Teen Singer|url=http://nieonline.com/yakscorner/20110526/4.pdf|first=Janis|last=Campbell|work=Yak's Corner|publisher=[[The Detroit News]]|issue=775|date=May 26, 2011|page=4}}</ref> Her father is a member of the 90s R&B group ''Guesss''.<ref name=DollHouseBio>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/6161795/love-dollhouse/biography|title=Love Dollhouse|department=Biography|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://heavy.com/entertainment/2017/01/ryan-destiny-alexandra-crane-star-cast-boyfriend-bio-photos-age-spoilers/|title=Ryan Destiny as Alexandra Crane on ‘Star’: 5 Fast Facts|website=[[Heavy.com]]|first=Daniel S|last=Levine|date=January 4, 2017|accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref><ref name=Detroit010217>{{cite web|url=http://www.detroitnews.com/story/entertainment/television/2017/01/03/ryan-destiny-fox-tv-star-drama/96023724/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170104153557/http://www.detroitnews.com/story/entertainment/television/2017/01/03/ryan-destiny-fox-tv-star-drama/96023724/|title=Ryan Destiny: A ‘Star’ is born|date=January 2, 2017|first=Jim|last=McFarlin|work=[[The Detroit News]]|publisher=[[Digital First Media]]|archivedate=January 4, 2017|accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref> Destiny attended [[West Bloomfield High School]]. In January 2011, Destiny won a singing contest to attend the red carpet premiere of [[Justin Bieber]]'s ''[[Justin Bieber: Never Say Never|Never Say Never]]''.<ref name=Yak052611/><ref>{{cite journal|title=Destiny To Sing|url=http://nieonline.com/yakscorner/20110127/6.pdf|date=January 27, 2011|department=Kids Making News|work=Yak's Corner|publisher=[[The Detroit News]]|issue=760|page=6}}</ref>

==Career==
===Music===
In junior high school, the 12-year-old Destiny formed musical trio ''New Limit''. Managed by her mother Dawn, the trio auditioned for ''[[America's Got Talent]]'' in 2010 and they made it to the finals until they decided it was not for them. By 2011, Destiny had signed with Universal Republic.<ref name=Yak052611/> The group would later split, with Destiny and her manager holding auditions forming a new group called "Love Dollhouse" The trio signed to All Def/Capitol Records, a label partnership with [[Russell Simmons]], [[Steve Rifkind]] and [[Brian Robbins]]. The group released their debut single "Can I."<ref name=DollHouseBio/><ref name=Detroit010217/> The group disbanded in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blackfilm.com/read/2015/06/exclusive-singer-ryan-destiny-talks-a-girl-like-grace/|title=Exclusive: Singer Ryan Destiny Talks ‘A Girl Like Grace’|date=June 19, 2015|first=Wilson|last=Morales|website=Blackfilm.com|accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref>
In 2016, Destiny announced plans for a solo [[Extended Play|EP]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theidleman.com/manual/web-roundup/ryan-destiny-chats-idle-man/|title=Ryan Destiny Chats to the Idle Man|author=Harriet|work=[[The Idle Man]]|date=March 16, 2016|accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref>

===Acting===
In 2010, Destiny appeared in an episode of the web series ''The Wannabes Starring Savvy''. In 2011, she traveled to Los Angeles with her mother to start auditioning for projects.<ref name=Yak052611/> In 2013, she appeared in several episodes of the Detroit-based crime drama, ''[[Low Winter Sun (U.S. TV series)|Low Winter Sun]]''.<ref name=Detroit010217/> Destiny later appeared as the lead in the independent film ''A Girl Like Grace'' opposite [[Garcelle Beauvais]], [[Meagan Good]], and [[Raven-Symoné]]. The [[Ty Hodges]] produced film premiered at the [[Los Angeles Film Festival]] in June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2015/06/a-girl-like-grace-clip-los-angeles-film-festival-meagan-good-1201435750/|title=‘A Girl Like Grace’ LA Film Festival Clip: Grieving, Bullying & Consequences|date=June 1, 2015|first=Erik|last=Pedersen|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|accessdate=January 18, 2017}}</ref>

Destiny initially booked the role of Tiana on [[Lee Daniels]]'s hit musical drama ''[[Empire (2015 TV series)|Empire]]'' but due to her contract with All Def Music, she could not accept the role.<ref name=Detroit010217/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2017/09/29/ryan-destiny-devastated-to-lose-empire-role-but-thrilled-with-star-part/|title=Ryan Destiny ‘devastated’ to lose ‘Empire’ role, but thrilled with ‘Star’ part|date=September 29, 2017|first=Tashara|last=Jones|work=[[New York Post]]|publisher=[[News Corp (2013–present)|News Corp]]|accessdate=October 1, 2017}}</ref> In December 2015, Destiny booked one of the lead roles as Alexandra in Daniels' series ''[[Star (U.S. TV series)|Star]]'' – opposite [[Queen Latifah]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/news/queen-latifah-lee-daniels-fox-music-pilot-girlband-star-1201656013/|title=Queen Latifah Lands Lead Role in Lee Daniels’ Fox Music Pilot|date=December 7, 2015|first=Elizabeth |last=Wagmeister|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=January 19, 2017}}</ref> The series premiered on Fox December 14, 2016 and ended after three seasons in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2016/tv/news/star-premiere-fox-cast-jude-demorest-ryan-destiny-brittany-ogrady-1201942351/|title=‘Star's’ Newcomers Reveal Audition Stories, What It's Like Working With Lee Daniels|date=December 14, 2016|first=Joshua|last=Alston|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=January 19, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=‘Star’ Canceled After Three Seasons at Fox|url= https://variety.com/2019/tv/news/star-canceled-fox-1203188063/ |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=May 10, 2019}}</ref>

In November 2019 it was announced that Destiny was cast to play [[Claressa Shields]] in ''[[Flint Strong]]'', a [[Universal Pictures]] biopic written by [[Barry Jenkins]] and directed by [[Rachel Morrison]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Rachel Morrison’s Directional Debut ‘Flint Strong’ Casts Ryan Destiny as Claressa Shields|url= https://variety.com/2019/film/news/rachel-morrison-flint-strong-ryan-destiny-claressa-shields-1203403694/ |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=November 13, 2019}}</ref>

Destiny currently has a recurring role on season 3 of ''[[Grown-ish]]'' as Jillian, a transfer student from Spelman College.<ref>{{cite web|title=TVLine Items: Star Vet to grown-ish, S.W.A.T. Adds Series Regular and More|url=https://tvline.com/2019/07/08/grownish-ryan-destiny-cast-season-3-freeform/|work=[[TVLine]]|date=July 8, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title= Watch an exclusive clip of Ryan Destiny joining grown-ish season 3|url=https://ew.com/tv/2020/01/30/ryan-destiny-grown-ish-season-3-clip/ |work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=January 30, 2020}}</ref>

==Influences==
Destiny cites entertainers [[Michael Jackson]], [[Freddie Mercury]], [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] and [[Aaliyah]] as her primary influences.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Anassi |first1=Pennie |title=#LiveCivilInterview: Ryan Destiny Talks STAR, Colorism, Gabrielle Union, & The Secret To Her Glowing Skin |url=http://livingcivil.com/livecivilinterview-ryan-destiny-talks-star-colorism-gabrielle-union-secret-glowing-skin/ |website=Live Civil |date=December 6, 2017}}</ref><ref name="You are being redirected">{{cite web |title=You are being redirected... |url=https://theknockturnal.com/ryan-destiny-brittany-ogrady-quincy-talk-star-season-2/ |website=theknockturnal.com}}</ref> Destiny has also expressed admiration for singers such as [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Brandy Norwood|Brandy]], [[India Arie]], and [[Beyoncé]].<ref name="You are being redirected"/>

==Personal life==
Destiny has been in a relationship with actor [[Keith Powers]] since late-2017.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.wetheurban.com/ryan-keith-cover|title=We The Urban|date=July 2019|accessdate=January 4, 2020}}</ref>
