'''Robert Davidson''' (born 17 December 1965) is an Australian composer.  He holds a Bachelor of Music from the University of Queensland, a Graduate Diploma in Music from Griffith University, and a PhD from the University of Queensland.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://researchers.uq.edu.au/researcher/394|title=Dr Robert Davidson - UQ Researchers|website=researchers.uq.edu.au|access-date=2017-01-23}}</ref> He studied composition with [[Terry Riley]] in 1995 following studies with [[Philip Bračanin]] at the University of Queensland where he now teaches Modern techniques and composition.<ref name="Spearritt">{{cite encyclopedia|last=Spearritt|first=G.|editor=[[Stanley Sadie]] and [[John Tyrrell (professor of music)|John Tyrrell]]|encyclopedia=[[The New Grove Dictionary of Music and Musicians]]|title=Brisbane|year=2001|publisher=[[Macmillan Publishers|Macmillan]]|location=London|isbn=0-333-60800-3}}</ref> He lectures in music at the University of Queensland. He was a bassist in the [[Opera Australia Orchestra|Opera Australia]], [[Sydney Symphony Orchestra|Sydney Symphony]] and [[Queensland Symphony Orchestra|Queensland Symphony]] orchestras.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.topologymusic.com/about/principal-artists/|title=Principal Artists – TOPOLOGY|website=www.topologymusic.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-01-23}}</ref>

Since 1996, Davidson has directed the post classical quintet [[Topology (musical ensemble)|Topology]], with whom he plays double bass and occasionally sings. He is a member of the Topology Board and is responsible for the artistic direction of the organisation, including establishing and maintaining collaborative network relationships, composing music, performing and teaching in the education program. He also influences the strategic partnerships and direction of the group.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.topologymusic.com/about/board-members/|title=Board Members – TOPOLOGY|website=www.topologymusic.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-01-23}}</ref>

Davidson's works focus on relationships between speech and music, often including video elements.<ref name="McCombe">{{cite journal|last=McCombe|first=Christine|year=2006|title=Videomusicvideo - Composing across media|journal=Contemporary Music Review|publisher=[[Routledge]]|location=London|volume=25|issue=4|pages=299–310|doi=10.1080/07494460600760973}}</ref> His works have been commissioned and performed by numerous international artists, such as the Brodsky Quartet (London), [[Australian String Quartet]], Karin Schaupp, Southern Cross Soloists, Paul Dresher Ensemble (San Francisco), and Newspeak (New York).<ref name=":0" />
