'''William R. McDermott''' (born August 18, 1961)<ref>{{cite news |last1=Koenen |first1=Jens |last2=Schürmann |first2=Hans |title=Mit dem Zweiten läuft es besser |url=https://www.handelsblatt.com/unternehmen/management/neue-sap-chefs-mit-dem-zweiten-laeuft-es-besser/3364528-all.html |accessdate=11 October 2019 |publisher=[[Handelsblatt]] |date=2010-02-08}}</ref> is an American businessman and is currently CEO of [[ServiceNow]]. ==Early years==
Bill McDermott was born in [[Amityville, New York|Amityville]] on [[Long Island]], where he also grew up.<ref name="VonBergenInquirer">{{cite web|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/columnists/jane-von-bergen/20141215_SAP_CEO_is_telling_a_winner_s_story.html|title=SAP CEO is telling a winner's story|author=Jane M. Von Bergen|date=December 15, 2014|work=[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]]|accessdate=January 26, 2018}}</ref> He is one of four children to Kathleen and Bill McDermott<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/fortwayne/obituary.aspx?n=kathleen-a-mcdermott&pid=146128042|title=Kathleen A. McDermott Obituary|author=Published in Fort Wayne Newspapers|date=October 10, 2010|work=[[Legacy.com]]|accessdate=January 26, 2018}}</ref> and a grandson to [[Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame]] player [[Bobby McDermott]].<ref name="BusinessInsiderProfile">{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/saps-bill-mcdermott-rags-to-riches-2014-11|title=This Man Beat Poverty, Cancer, And Fleas To Lead A $21 Billion Company|author=Julie Bort|date=November 10, 2014|work=[[Business Insider]]|accessdate=January 26, 2018}}</ref>
At age 16, he bought the Amityville Country Delicatessen in Long Island for $7,000.<ref name="BusinessInsiderProfile"/>

==Education==
McDermott studied Business Management at [[Dowling College]]. His deli business helped him pay for his undergraduate education.<ref name="BusinessInsiderProfile"/> After completing his undergraduate studies, McDermott attended [[Northwestern University]]'s Kellogg School of Management where he earned his MBA,<ref>[http://alumni.kellogg.northwestern.edu/profiles/alumni/bmcdermott.htm McDermott profile], alumni.kellogg.northwestern.edu; accessed March 31, 2015.</ref><ref>[http://kellogg.northwestern.edu/centennial/alumni/bmcdermott.htm Kellogg School of Management alumni profile] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100223013709/http://kellogg.northwestern.edu/centennial/alumni/bmcdermott.htm |date=2010-02-23 }}, kellogg.northwestern.edu; accessed March 31, 2015.</ref> and then completed the Executive Development Program at the [[Wharton School of Business]].

==Career==
===Xerox, Gartner and Siebel Systems===
McDermott worked for 17 years at Xerox<!-- March 2018  http://www.sap.com/about/company/executives/mcdermott/index.epx McDermott profile, sap.com; accessed March 31, 2015.--->
<ref name="CNBC17" /> and then became the President of Gartner in 2000.<ref name="HBR16"/><ref name="CNBC17"/> From 2001 to 2002, he served as Executive Vice President of Worldwide Sales and Operations at [[Siebel Systems]].<ref name="HBR16" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Callaghan |first1=Dennis |title=Former Siebel Exec Resurfaces at SAP |url=https://www.eweek.com/it-management/former-siebel-exec-resurfaces-at-sap |website=eweek.com |publisher=QuinStreet |accessdate=11 October 2019}}</ref>

===SAP===
[[File:Ilham Aliyev met with Chief Executive Officer of SAP SE 02.jpg|thumb|McDermott meeting with [[Azerbaijan]]'s President [[Ilham Aliyev]]]]
In 2002, McDermott was appointed by SAP as the CEO of  [[SAP America]]. He was designated to the SAP Executive Board in 2008, and in February 2010, he became the co-CEO of SAP AG.<ref name="HBR16"/><ref name="CNBC17"/>
After four years, on May 21, 2014, McDermott became the first American to become the CEO of the company, now known as [[SAP SE]].
Since 2010, SAP's market cap has increased from $39 billion to $144.7 billion by February 2018.<ref name="Barrons18" /><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/02/12/sap-will-do-small-tuck-in-acquisitions-not-mega-deals-ceo-bill-mcdermott.html|title=SAP Will Consider Small Acquisitions, Not Mega-Deals|last=Kharpal|first=Arjun|date=2018-02-12|accessdate=2018-02-12|publisher=CNBC.com}}</ref>
In 2016 McDermott became the best-paid executive leading the companies that form the German stock index [[DAX]] by earning a direct remuneration of €11.6 million.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.spiegel.de/wirtschaft/unternehmen/dax-so-viel-verdienen-die-unternehmenschefs-a-1139318.html|title=So viel verdienen die Dax-Chefs|date=17 March 2017|newspaper=[[Der Spiegel]]|language=German|trans-title=This is how much the Dax bosses earn}}</ref> On October 10, 2019, McDermott decided to leave SAP SE.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/sap-sets-course-for-the-future-with-next-generation-leadership-team-300936937.html|title=SAP Sets Course for the Future With Next Generation Leadership Team|date=10 October 2019|publisher=[[PR Newswire]]}}</ref>

===ServiceNow===
At the end of October after McDermott stepped down from SAP, [[ServiceNow]] announced his appointment as CEO.  He succeeded [[John Donahoe]] at the end of 2019 as Donahoe finished his tenure at the company.<ref name="businesswire.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20191022006131/en/ServiceNow-Announces-Bill-McDermott-CEO-Succeeding-John|title=ServiceNow Announces Bill McDermott to Become CEO, Succeeding John Donahoe, Who Is Stepping Down to Become CEO of Nike|date=2019-10-22|website=www.businesswire.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-22}}</ref> He cited "the company’s strength in cloud computing and enterprise software, and the challenge of building an already vibrant company further" as reasons for him to join [[ServiceNow]] as CEO.<ref>{{cite web |title=ServiceNow Shakeup: No One Saw This Coming |url=https://www.channelnomics.com/2019/10/23/servicenow-shakeup-no-one-saw-this-coming/ |website=Channelnomics |accessdate=23 October 2019 |date=23 October 2019}}</ref>
