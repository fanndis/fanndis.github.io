The '''Michel II Government''' was the [[Federal Government of Belgium]], led by [[Prime Minister of Belgium|Prime Minister]] [[Charles Michel]] from 18 December 2018 until 27 October 2019 when it was succeeded by the [[Wilmès I Government]]. It was a [[Centre politics|centre]] [[Minority government|minority]] [[Coalition government|coalition]] [[Cabinet (government)|cabinet]] of [[Christen-Democratisch en Vlaams|Christian Democratic and Flemish]] (CD&V), the [[Open Vlaamse Liberalen en Democraten|Open Flemish Liberals and Democrats]] (Open Vld) and the [[Mouvement Réformateur|Reformist Movement]] (MR). On 26 October 2019, it was announced that [[Sophie Wilmès]] would take over the role of Prime Minister from Michel on 1 November 2019, and form a new government.<ref name=wilmes>{{cite news|url=https://vrtnws.be/p.L7kpLY49v|title=Sophie Wilmès (MR) wordt nieuwe premier in lopende zaken: "Alles aan doen om de stabiliteit te verzekeren"|trans-title=Sophie Wilmès (MR) to become new Prime Minister of caretaker cabinet: "Give it all to ensure stability"|publisher=vrt.be|date=26 October 2019|accessdate=27 October 2019|language=Dutch}}</ref>

== History ==
The government came into existence on 9 December 2018 following the exit of [[New Flemish Alliance]] (N-VA) from the [[Michel I Government]]. As the Prime Minister did not offer the government's resignation to the King until the 18th, it is [[Constitution of Belgium|constitutional]]ly still the same government as the one that started in 2014, albeit a heavily reshuffled government following the resignation of all N-VA ministers and secretaries of state.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20181210_04023704|title=Grondwetsspecialisten zijn formeel: "Juridisch gezien is dit geen nieuwe regering en is er dus geen probleem"|newspaper=[[Het Nieuwsblad]]|date=10 December 2018|accessdate=10 December 2018|language=Dutch}}</ref> Politically, however, it is in an entirely different situation, as it has no support from N-VA and thus operates as a new government.

On 10 December, several parties had called for the government to propose a motion of confidence.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2018/12/10/n-va-en-sp-a-vragen-een-vertrouwensstemming-in-het-parlement/|title=N-VA, SP.A, Groen en Vlaams Belang vragen een vertrouwensstemming in het parlement|publisher=[[Vlaamse Radio- en Televisieomroeporganisatie|VRT]]|website=vrtnws.be|date=10 December 2018|accessdate=23 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2018/12/10/moet-de-regering-het-vertrouwen-van-het-parlement-vragen/|title=Wat is een vertrouwensstemming en moet de regering het vertrouwen van het parlement vragen?|publisher=[[Vlaamse Radio- en Televisieomroeporganisatie|VRT]]|website=vrtnws.be|date=10 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref>

Michel attempted to continue with the minority cabinet with support from the [[Centre-left politics|centre-left]] opposition, who instead announced on 18 December that they would submit a [[motion of no confidence]] against the cabinet.<ref name=dm>{{cite news|url=https://www.demorgen.be/politiek/michel-dient-ontslag-regering-in-na-groen-rode-motie-van-wantrouwen-be594ff3/|title=Michel dient ontslag regering in na groen-rode motie van wantrouwen|publisher=[[De Morgen]]|date=18 December 2018|accessdate=23 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref>

On 18 December, [[Premier]] Michel offered his resignation to [[Monarchy of Belgium|King]] [[Philippe of Belgium|Philippe]].<ref name=dm /><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2018/12/18/liveblog-krijgt-de-regering-een-motie-van-wantrouwen/|title=Herlees: Koning houdt ontslag regering in beraad, "Beste scenario om te ontsnappen aan vervroegde verkiezingen"|website=vrtnws.be|date=18 December 2018|accessdate=19 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref>

On 20 December, the [[Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)|Chamber of Representatives]] almost unanimously voted a draft [[government budget|budget]] to enable [[Provision (accounting)|provision]]al spending for the first three months of 2019 based on the 2018 budget.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.knack.be/nieuws/belgie/politieke-crisis-kamer-keurt-noodbegroting-goed/article-normal-1409521.html|title=Politieke crisis: Kamer keurt noodbegroting goed|magazine=[[Knack (magazine)|Knack]]|date=20 December 2018|accessdate=23 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.standaard.be/cnt/dmf20181220_04053344|title=Kamer keurt ‘noodbegroting’ goed|newspaper=[[De Standaard]]|date=20 December 2018|accessdate=23 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref>

The King accepted Michel's resignation on 21 December after consulting party leaders.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hln.be/nieuws/binnenland/koning-zet-consultaties-ook-morgen-nog-verder-geen-gesprek-met-vlaams-belang~a9ed2733/|title=Koning zet consultaties ook morgen nog verder: geen gesprek met Vlaams Belang|newspaper=[[Het Laatste Nieuws]]|date=20 December 2018|accessdate=23 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref> The outgoing cabinet continues governing as a [[caretaker government]], likely until after the [[2019 European Parliament election|European Parliament]], [[2019 Belgian regional elections|Belgian regional]] and [[2019 Belgian federal election|federal elections]] of 26 May 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.demorgen.be/politiek/koning-filip-aanvaardt-ontslag-regering-michel-regering-in-lopende-zaken-en-geen-vervroegde-verkiezingen-bab248b0/|title=Koning Filip aanvaardt ontslag regering-Michel, regering in lopende zaken en geen vervroegde verkiezingen|publisher=De Morgen|date=21 December 2018|accessdate=23 December 2018|language=nl}}</ref>

On 2 July 2019, Prime Minister Charles Michel was elected to become the next [[President of the European Council]] as from 1 December 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.demorgen.be/politiek/wat-met-de-federale-regering-en-de-mr-nu-michel-europees-president-wordt~b3768e50/|title=Wat met de federale regering en de MR nu Michel Europees president wordt?|trans-title=What will happen to federal government and MR with Michel becoming European president?|publisher=[[De Morgen]]|date=2 July 2019|accessdate=15 August 2019|language=nl}}</ref> With no new government formed, [[Sophie Wilmès]] was appointed to take over the role of Prime Minister from 27 October 2019, to allow Charles Michel sufficient time to prepare for his role as President of the European Council.<ref name=wilmes/>

===Changes in composition===
* On June 25, 2019, one month after the [[2019 European Parliament election in Belgium|2019 European Parliament elections]], [[Kris Peeters]] offered his resignation as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Consumer Affairs, Disabled Persons, Economy, Employment, Equal Rights and Fighting Poverty to take up his seat in the [[European Parliament]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://vrtnws.be/p.ywK83BE8a|trans-title=Kris Peeters (CD&V) offers resignation to prime minister Michel and chooses European Parliament|title=Kris Peeters (CD&V) biedt ontslag aan bij premier Michel en kiest nu voluit voor het Europees Parlement|author=vrtnws.be}}</ref> with [[Christen-Democratisch en Vlaams|CD&V]] appointing party president [[Wouter Beke]] as his replacement two days later, with the exception that the role of Deputy Minister shifted to [[Koen Geens]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://vrtnws.be/p.dldPbEqaY|trans-title=Wouter Beke vervangt Kris Peeters in de federale regering|title=Wouter Beke replaces Kris Peeters in federal government|author=vrtnws.be}}</ref>
* On October 2, 2019, [[Wouter Beke]] was in turn replaced by [[Nathalie Muylle]] as Beke took up the position as Flemish Minister for Welfare in the [[Flemish Government]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://vrtnws.be/p.A7MAolBJ4|trans-title=CD&V Chamber of Representatives member Nathalie Muylle replaces Wouter Beke in Federal Government|title=CD&V-Kamerlid Nathalie Muylle vervangt Wouter Beke in de federale regering|author=vrtnws.be}}</ref>
