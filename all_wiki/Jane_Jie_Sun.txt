'''Jane Jie Sun''' (born 1969/1970) is a Chinese businesswoman, and the CEO of the [[Trip.com Group]], the largest online travel agency in China, since November 2016.<ref name="bloomberg.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/profiles/people/15412700-jie-sun-jane|title=Jane Jie Sun, Ctrip.Com International Ltd: Profile & Biography - Bloomberg|website=Bloomberg|accessdate=4 July 2018}}</ref>

==Early life==
Sun earned a bachelor's degree in business from the [[University of Florida]], and master's degree in law (LLM) from [[Peking University]]'s Law School.<ref name="bloomberg.com"/><ref name="ctrip.com">{{cite web|url=http://ir.ctrip.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=148903&p=irol-govBio&ID=153540|title=Ctrip - Investor Relations - Biography|website=ir.ctrip.com|accessdate=4 July 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Sun worked for [[KPMG]] as an audit manager in [[Silicon Valley]], California for five years.<ref name="ctrip.com"/> Sun was the head of the SEC and external reporting division of [[Applied Materials]] from 1997 to 2005.<ref name="ctrip.com"/>

Sun joined Ctrip (now known as the Trip.com Group) in 2005 as CFO, rising to COO in May 2012, and CEO in November 2016, when she succeeded the company's founder [[James Liang]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/pictures/5aea072931358e612fb7c9e1/jane-jie-sun-49-chief-exe/#24a9187edce8|title=Jane Jie Sun, 49, Chief Executive Officer, Ctrip.com International – China - pg.22|first=|last=merrileebarton|website=Forbes|accessdate=4 July 2018}}</ref><ref name="ctrip.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chinamoneynetwork.com/2016/11/17/ctrip-appoints-jane-jie-sun-as-ceo-james-liang-remains-executive-chairman|title=Ctrip Appoints Jane Jie Sun As CEO, James Liang Remains Executive Chairman – China Money Network|website=www.chinamoneynetwork.com|accessdate=4 July 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1995, Sun married John Wu, one of [[Yahoo! Search|Yahoo]]'s earliest employees and later, [[Chief technology officer|CTO]] of [[Alibaba Group|Alibaba]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/russellflannery/2017/02/25/how-one-china-power-couple-successfully-balances-work-and-home-life/#b9708306c261|title=How One China Power Couple Balances Work And Home Life|first=Russell|last=Flannery|publisher=|accessdate=4 July 2018}}</ref> They have two children, and live in [[Shanghai]].<ref name="auto"/> Wu runs his own investment company, FengHe Fund Management.<ref name="auto"/>
