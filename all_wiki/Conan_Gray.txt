'''Conan Lee Gray''' (born December 5, 1998)<ref name="Notion">{{Cite web|url=https://notion.online/conan-gray/|title=Conan Gray|last=Dean Stone|first=Russell|date=August 2, 2019|website=[[Notion (magazine)|Notion]]|quote=The story starts back in Lemon Grove, San Diego, where Conan Gray was born on 5th December 1998.|access-date=December 28, 2019}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter and social media personality. He started out uploading [[vlog]]s, [[Cover version|covers]] and original songs to [[YouTube]] as a teenager from [[Georgetown, Texas]]. Gray signed a record deal with [[Republic Records]] in 2018, where he released his debut EP ''[[Sunset Season]]'' (2018)''.'' His debut studio album ''[[Kid Krow]]'' (2020) debuted at number 5 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], making it the biggest US new artist debut of 2020. ''Kid Krow'' also spawned the commercially successful singles "[[Maniac (Conan Gray song)|Maniac]]" and "[[Heather (Conan Gray song)|Heather]]".

==Early life==
Gray was born on December 5, 1998, in [[Lemon Grove, California]],{{Dubious|Birthplace|date=March 2020}} to a white father of mostly [[Irish people|Irish]] ancestry and a [[Japanese people|Japanese]] mother.<ref>{{Citation|last=Conan Gray|title=Being Mixed Race|date=September 25, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kEYAudxO8HY|access-date=November 19, 2020}}</ref> As an infant, his family moved to [[Hiroshima|Hiroshima, Japan]], due to his grandfather requiring medical care. After living there for two years, the family moved back to California. Gray used to speak Japanese fluently, but has since lost his proficiency.<ref>{{Citation|last=Gray|first=Conan|title=50 Facts About Me !!!|date=March 18, 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cxqOJd6w4ds&t=88|work=[[YouTube]]|access-date=July 6, 2019}}</ref>

Gray's parents divorced when he was three years old. In a "Draw My Life" video, he details his experiences with divorce as a young child.<ref name=":4">{{Citation|last=Conan Gray|title=Draw My Life ( For 100,000 )|date=June 12, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UIvzS03Jra0|access-date=July 6, 2019}}</ref> As his father was in the military, Gray moved twelve times throughout his childhood, moving three times during sixth grade alone. Gray was frequently bullied throughout grade school. At one point, he was one of only five half Asian children at his school.

Gray eventually settled in [[Georgetown, Texas]], as a preteen where he remained for the rest of his teenage years. Gray's life in central Texas inspired much of his art and music. He was accepted to the [[University of California, Los Angeles|UCLA]], and moved to an apartment complex in [[Los Angeles|Los Angeles, California]] in September 2017.

== Career ==

=== 2015–2017: Career beginnings on YouTube ===
He created his official channel in 2013,<ref name="Metro">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/ConanXCanon/about|title=Conan Gray|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> and began creating videos at age nine.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-intersect/wp/2018/06/25/they-became-famous-youtubers-a-new-generation-of-kids-wants-to-take-their-place/|title=YouTube is new way to get famous; at VidCon, tweens want to be next in line|last=Ohlheiser|first=Abby|date=June 25, 2018|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=November 8, 2019}}</ref> Gray's vlog content centers heavily on his life in small-town [[Texas]]. He is often hailed for his appreciation of Americana-related [[nostalgia]].<ref name="Paper" /> Gray has recorded music, showed his art, and created other videos for his vlog with more than 25 million views.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":1" />

=== 2018–2019: ''Sunset Season'' ===
Conan Gray self-released his debut single "Idle Town", in March 2017. The song gained over 14 million streams on [[Spotify]] and 12 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref name="ido">{{Cite web|url=https://www.idolator.com/7686607/conan-gray-makes-major-label-debut-with-generation-why|title=One To Watch: Conan Gray Makes His Major Label Debut With "Generation Why"|last=Wess|first=Mike|date=October 8, 2018|website=[[Idolator (website)|idolator]]|access-date=November 8, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Paper">{{Cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/conan-gray-sunset-season-2633801871.html?rebelltitem=4|title=Conan Gray Hits a Small Town Crescendo|last=Wetmore|first=Brendan|date=April 9, 2019|website=[[Paper (magazine)|PAPER]]|access-date=April 9, 2019}}</ref> In October 2018, Gray released the single "Generation Why" on [[Republic Records]],<ref name="ido" /> which was described as "a rallying call to [[millennials|generation Z]]."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thegate.ca/music/036712/watch-conan-grays-generation-why/|title=Watch: Conan Gray's 'Generation Why'|last=Andrew Powell|first=W.|date=November 15, 2018|website=The Gate|access-date=November 8, 2019}}</ref> In November 2018, Gray released the five-track EP ''[[Sunset Season]]'' which includes the songs "Idle Town", "Generation Why", "Crush Culture", "Greek God", and "Lookalike". The EP peaked at No. 2 on the [[Top Heatseekers|Billboard ''Heatseekers Albums'']] chart and at No. 116 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="USHeat SunsetSeason">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/heatseekers-albums/2018-12-01|title=Heatseekers Albums Charts - December 1, 2018|work=Billboard|access-date=July 11, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Billboard 200">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/conan-gray/chart-history/TLP|title=Conan Gray Chart History: Billboard 200|website=Billboard|access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref> Gray embarked on a [[North America]]n tour in support of the EP with [[Girl in Red]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/conan-gray-sets-out-for-the-sunset-shows-tour-2019-134884|title=Conan Gray sets out for The Sunset Shows tour 2019|last=Lee|first=Craig|date=November 12, 2018|website=[[AXS (company)|AXS]]|access-date=November 8, 2019}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
=== Influences ===
[[File:Taylor Swift Red Tour 2, 2013.jpg|thumb|250x250px|Gray has stated that [[Taylor Swift]] raised him "both as a writer and a human"]]
Gray has cited [[Taylor Swift]] as his biggest inspiration, stating that he "was raised by [her]" and that he is "the largest Swiftie".<ref>{{Citation|title=Conan Gray Guesses the Lyrics to Taylor Swift Songs {{!}} Lyric Challenge|date=December 11, 2019|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ur4I78ebQ6k|work=[[YouTube]]|publisher=[[Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen]]|language=en|access-date=December 12, 2019}}</ref> He told ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'', "I love her so much. I love her music. I love what she does. I love her lyricism. I love how she's handled her career. She's just such a fucking boss. I grew up listening to her music since I was nine years old. I feel like she shaped me so much as a person."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/music/conan-gray-kid-krow/|title=Conan Gray Says His Forthcoming Album Kid Krow Was Inspired by His 'Pretty Tough' Childhood|last=Mier|first=Tomás|date=January 10, 2020|website=[[People (magazine)|People]]|language=en|access-date=January 11, 2020}}</ref> Swift praised Gray's debut album ''[[Kid Krow]]'' and the track "Wish You Were Sober" on her personal [[Instagram]] story, to which Gray responded, "Thank you for being my lifelong songwriting inspiration and icon. I honestly feel like you raised me both as a writer and a human and I cannot express in words how much the means to me. Thank you for everything. Swifty for life".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.somagnews.com/conan-gray-reacted-recognition-taylor-swift/|title=This is how Conan Gray reacted to the recognition of Taylor Swift|last=Kubrick|first=Kaitlyn|date=March 27, 2020|website=Somag News|language=en-US|access-date=March 28, 2020}}</ref> In an interview with [[Zane Lowe]], for [[Apple Music]], Gray stated that Swift is his "number one above all", and continued that "she's just my songwriting icon, and I'll never understand how she does what she does.
