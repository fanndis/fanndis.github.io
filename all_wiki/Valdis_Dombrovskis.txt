'''Valdis Dombrovskis''' (born 5 August 1971) is a [[Latvia]]n [[politician]] serving as [[Executive Vice President of the European Commission for An Economy that Works for People]] since 2019 and [[European Commissioner for Trade]] since 2020. He previously served as [[European Commissioner for Financial Stability, Financial Services and Capital Markets Union]] from 2016 to 2020 and [[Prime Minister of Latvia]] from 2009 to 2014. 

Dombrovskis served as [[Minister for Finance of Latvia]] from 2002 to 2004.<ref name="financeministers">{{Cite web|url=http://www.fm.gov.lv/en/ministry_of_Finance/history/43378-history|title=History|website=Finanšu Ministrija}}</ref> He then served as a [[Member of the European Parliament]] (MEP) for the [[New Era Party]] from 2004 to 2009. He became the [[Prime Minister of Latvia]] 2009, serving until his resignation in 2014.<ref name=prime>{{cite news|date=26 February 2009|accessdate=26 February 2009|publisher=BBC News  |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7911983.stm|title=Dombrovskis chosen as Latvian PM}}</ref> 
He was [[European Commissioner for Economic and Monetary Affairs and the Euro|Vice-President of the European Commission for the Euro and Social Dialogue]] from 2014 to 2019. Following the resignation of [[Jonathan Hill, Baron Hill of Oareford|Lord Jonathan Hill]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2016/jun/25/uk-european-commissioner-jonathan-hill-quits-brexit|title=UK's European commissioner quits in wake of Brexit vote|last=Rankin|first=Jennifer|date=2016-06-25|website=the Guardian|access-date=2018-09-04}}</ref> Dombrovskis served as [[European Commissioner for Financial Stability, Financial Services and the Capital Markets Union]] from 2016 to 2020. Following the resignation of [[Phil Hogan]], it was announced that he would take over the portfolio for [[European Commissioner for Trade|Trade]].<ref name="IrishTimes">{{cite news |date=2020-09-08 |title=Mairead McGuinness named as Ireland’s European commissioner, given 'important financial portfolio' |work=The Irish Times |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/mairead-mcguinness-named-as-ireland-s-european-commissioner-given-important-financial-portfolio-1.4349511}}</ref>

==Education and science career==
Born in [[Riga]] to a family with [[Poles in Latvia|Polish]] roots (the original Polish surname is [[Dąbrowski]]), Dombrovskis earned a bachelor's degree in economics for engineers from [[Riga Technical University]] in 1995 and a master's degree in physics from the [[University of Latvia]] in 1996.{{citation needed|reason=Your explanation here|date=October 2014}} He worked as a laboratory assistant at the Institute of Physics of the [[University of Mainz]] in [[Mainz]], [[Germany]], from 1995 to 1996, as an assistant at the University of Latvia's Institute of [[Solid-state physics|Solid-State Physics]] in 1997, and as a PhD student at the [[A.&nbsp;James Clark School of Engineering]] at the [[University of Maryland, College Park]] for electrical engineering in 1998.{{citation needed|reason=Your explanation here|date=October 2014}}

==Political career==
{{More citations needed section|date=June 2018}}

===Career in national politics===
In 2002 Dombrovskis became a board member of the [[New Era Party]]. He was [[List of Ministers of Finance of Latvia|Minister of Finance]] from 2002 to 2004 and a Member of the [[Saeima|Latvian Parliament]] during its 8th parliamentary term (2002–2004). Then he was Observer at the [[Council of the European Union]] (2003–2004).{{citation needed|reason=Your explanation here|date=October 2014}}

===Member of the European Parliament, 2004–2009===
As [[Member of the European Parliament]], Dombrovskis was a member of three European Parliament Committees: Committee on Budgets, Delegation to the [[ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly]], Delegation to the [[Euro-Latin American Parliamentary Assembly]].  He is also a Substitute at Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs, Committee on Budgetary Control and delegation to the EU-[[Kazakhstan]], EU-[[Kyrgyzstan]], and EU-[[Uzbekistan]] Parliamentary Cooperation Committees, and for relations with [[Tajikistan]], Turkmenistan and [[Mongolia]].{{citation needed|reason=Your explanation here|date=October 2014}}

Dombrovskis was also one of six Members of the European Parliament participating in the European Union's observer mission in [[Togo]] for the [[2007 Togolese parliamentary election|October 2007 Togolese parliamentary election]].<ref>[http://www.republicoftogo.com/central.php?o=5&s=83&d=3&i=1132 "Arrivée à Lomé des députés européens"], Republicoftogo.com, 11 October 2007 {{in lang|fr}}. {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090617235412/http://www.republicoftogo.com/central.php?o=5&s=83&d=3&i=1132 |date=17 June 2009 }}</ref>{{citation needed|reason=Your explanation here|date=October 2014}}

===Prime Minister of Latvia, 2009–2014===
On 26 February 2009, following the resignation of [[Ivars Godmanis]], [[President of Latvia|President]] [[Valdis Zatlers]] nominated Dombrovskis to succeed Godmanis as Prime Minister.<ref name=prime /> It was believed that his government would consist of three of the four previously governing parties (all but Godmanis' [[Latvia's First Party|LPP]]/[[Latvian Way|LC]]), his own New Era Party, and a smaller right-wing party (the [[Civic Union (Latvia)|Civic Union]]); the government was approved on 12 March 2009.<ref>http://www.javno.com/en-world/latvia-government-named-differences-emerge_239733</ref>

Dombrovskis resigned as Prime Minister on 27 November 2013 following the [[Zolitūde shopping centre roof collapse]] in which 54 people were killed. He announced that a new government is needed with strong support in the parliament after the tragedy, considering all related circumstances. His spokesman said that "the government takes political responsibility for the tragedy".<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-25120936 Latvian government falls over Riga supermarket disaster], ''[[BBC News]]'', 27 November 2013.</ref> He denied the president had urged him to step down, stating that he had considered the decision for days and that the country needs government with strong support in parliament in the moment of crisis.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.delfi.lv/news/national/politics/dombrovskis-uznemas-atbildibu-par-tragediju-zolitude-krit-valdiba-teksta-tiesraide.d?id=43851844 |title=Dombrovskis uzņemas atbildību par traģēdiju Zolitūdē - krīt valdība |date=27 November 2013 |publisher=delfi.lv |language=Latvian |accessdate=27 November 2013}}</ref>

===Member of the European Commission, 2014–present===
In February 2014, Dombrovskis officially lodged his application to be the candidate of the centre-right [[European People’s Party]] (EPP) for the [[President of the European Commission|presidency of the European Commission]];<ref>[https://www.politico.eu/article/dombrovskis-seeks-epp-nomination-for-commission-presidency/ Dombrovskis seeks EPP nomination for Commission presidency] ''[[Politico Europe|European Voice]]'', 19 February 2014.</ref> shortly after he withdrew his candidacy to endorse [[Jean-Claude Juncker]] instead.<ref>Dave Keating (6 March 2014), [https://www.politico.eu/article/dombrovskis-withdraws-from-epp-candidate-race/ Dombrovskis withdraws from EPP candidate race] ''[[Politico Europe|European Voice]]''.</ref> The Latvian government later nominated Dombrovskis to be the country’s [[European Commissioner]].<ref>Andrew Gardner (5 June 2014), [https://www.politico.eu/article/dombrovskis-gets-latvias-nod/ Dombrovskis gets Latvia’s nod] ''[[Politico Europe|European Voice]]''.</ref>

Dombrovskis served as [[European Commissioner for Economic and Monetary Affairs and the Euro|European Commission Vice-President for the Euro and Social Dialogue]] from 2014 to 2019. From July 2016, he was also in charge of the financial services portfolio formerly overseen by British Commissioner [[Jonathan Hill, Baron Hill of Oareford|Jonathan Hill]], who resigned after the [[Brexit]] vote.<ref>Jim Brunsden (30 June 2016), [https://www.ft.com/content/7d43ccd8-3de7-11e6-9f2c-36b487ebd80a Brexit gives Valdis Dombrovskis big sway over banks] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref> In addition, he has been serving as co-chair of the [[European People's Party|EPP Economic and Financial Affairs Ministers Meeting]] – alongside [[Petteri Orpo]] (2016-2019) and [[Paschal Donohoe]] (since 2019) – which gathers the center-right EPP ministers ahead of meetings of the [[Economic and Financial Affairs Council]] (ECOFIN).<ref>[https://www.epp.eu/structure/ministerial-meetings/ Council of the EU and Ministerial meetings] [[European People’s Party]] (EPP).</ref> 

Following the [[2019 European Parliament election|2019 European election]], Dombrovskis was nominated by the [[Kariņš cabinet|coalition government]] of [[Prime Minister of Latvia|Prime Minister]] [[Arturs Krišjānis Kariņš]] for a second term as Latvia's [[European Commissioner]].<ref>Bjarke Smith-Meyer (11 June 2019), [https://www.politico.eu/article/valdis-dombrovskis-brussels-commission-latvia-back-to-commission/ Latvia to send Dombrovskis back to Commission] ''[[Politico Europe]]''.</ref> He subsequently decided to relinquish the seat he won in the election; he was succeeded by [[Inese Vaidere]].<ref>Bjarke Smith-Meyer (20 June 2019), [https://www.politico.eu/article/dombrovskis-gives-up-mep-seat-to-remain-commissioner/ Dombrovskis gives up MEP seat to remain commissioner] ''[[Politico Europe]]''.</ref> [[Ursula von der Leyen]] has since proposed that [[Frans Timmermans]], [[Margrethe Vestager]] and Dombrovskis all serve as Executive Vice-Presidents of the commission with Dombrovskis having responsibility for an "Economy that Works for People".

==== Supporter of the University of Latvia Foundation ====
Dombrovskis is a supporter of the University of Latvia Foundation. In 2018, he donated 1,500 euros, awarding 3 one-time Valdis Dombrovskis Excellence Scholarships to Bachelor of Science students of the University of Latvia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fonds.lv/lepojamies/mecenati/atbalstitaji/valdis-dombrovskis/|title=Valdis Dombrovskis|website=www.fonds.lv}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is married to Ārija Dombrovska. The two have no children. Dombrovskis plays basketball and go skiing in his freetime.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.politico.eu/article/the-young-pretender/|title=Valdis Dombrovskis – The young pretender|first=European|last=Voice|date=1 April 2009|website=POLITICO}}</ref>
