'''Benjamin J. Rhodes''' (born November 14, 1977) is an American writer, political commentator and former Deputy National Security Advisor for Strategic Communications and Speechwriting under President [[Barack Obama]]. With [[Jake Sullivan]], he is the co-chair of National Security Action, a political NGO.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/democrats-marshal-a-strike-force-to-counter-trump-on-national-security-in-2018-2020-elections/2018/02/26/6b08540a-1b5b-11e8-b2d9-08e748f892c0_story.html|title=Democrats marshal strike force to counter Trump on national security in 2018, 2020 elections|last=Gearan|first=Anne|date=27 February 2018|website=Washington Post|access-date=10 December 2018}}</ref> He contributes to [[NBC News]] and [[MSNBC]] regularly as a political commentator.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://adweek.it/2HkMKzC|title=Former Obama Adviser Ben Rhodes Joins NBC News and MSNBC|website=adweek.it|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-10}}</ref> He is also a [[Crooked Media]] contributor, and co-host of the foreign policy podcast ''[[Pod Save the World]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://crooked.com/articles/crooked-media-announces-new-site-pod-store-and-network-of-very-fine-people-on-both-sides/|title=Crooked Media Announces New Site, Pod, Store, and Network of Very Fine People on Both Sides|website=Crooked Media|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-10}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Rhodes was born on November 14, 1977, in the [[Upper East Side]] neighborhood of [[Manhattan]]. news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/03/16/world/middleeast/benjamin-rhodes-obamas-voice-helps-shape-policy.html?pagewanted=all|title=Worldly at 35, and Shaping Obama's In the summer of 1997, Rhodes volunteered with the [[Rudy Giuliani]] mayoral campaign.<ref name=":0" /> In the summer of 2001, he worked on the [[New York City Council]] campaign of [[Diana Reyna]].<ref>{{cite news|author=Jason Horowitz|url=https://articles.washingtonpost.com/2010-01-12/news/36821288_1_obama-team-obama-campaign-robert-gibbs|title=Obama speechwriter pens a different script for the world stage|work=Washington Post|date=January 12, 2010 |url-status=live|accessdate=September 24, 2013}}</ref> In 2002, James Gibney, editor of ''[[Foreign Policy]]'', introduced Rhodes to [[Lee H. Hamilton|Lee Hamilton]], former member of the House of Representatives and director of the [[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]], who was looking for a speechwriter.<ref name="Jason Horowitz" /> Rhodes then spent five years as an assistant to Hamilton, helping to draft the [[Iraq Study Group Report]] and the recommendations of the [[9/11 Commission]].<ref>{{cite web|title=White House Profile: Ben Rhodes|url=http://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/author/Ben%20Rhodes|access-date=September 23, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/08/magazine/the-aspiring-novelist-who-became-obamas-foreign-policy-guru.html|title=The Aspiring Novelist Who Became Obama's Foreign-Policy Guru|last=Samuels|first=David|date=2016-05-05|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-01-15|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

[[File:Ben Rhodes and Aung San Suu Kyi in Naypyidaw.jpg|thumb|200px|Ben Rhodes and [[Aung San Suu Kyi]] discussing U.S. support for [[National League for Democracy|NLD]]-led government in [[Myanmar]], July 2016]]
In 2007, Rhodes began working as a speechwriter for the [[Barack Obama 2008 presidential campaign|2008 Obama presidential campaign]].<ref name="Samuels">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/08/magazine/the-aspiring-novelist-who-became-obamas-foreign-policy-guru.html|title=The Aspiring Novelist Who Became Obama's Foreign-Policy Guru|last=Samuels|first=David|date=2016-05-05|newspaper=The New York Times Magazine |issn=0362-4331|accessdate=2016-11-30}}</ref>

Rhodes wrote Obama's 2009 Cairo speech "[[A New Beginning]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://laurencejarvikonline.blogspot.com/2009/06/who-wrote-obamas-cairo-speech.html|title=Who Wrote Obama's Cairo Speech?|date=June 5, 2009|publisher=}}</ref> Rhodes was the adviser who counseled Obama to withdraw support from Egyptian leader [[Hosni Mubarak]],<ref name=":0" /> becoming a key adviser during the 2011 [[Arab Spring]].<ref name="LanderNYT"/><ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/03/16/world/middleeast/benjamin-rhodes-obamas-voice-helps-shape-policy.html|title=Worldly at 35, and Shaping Obama's Voice|last=Landler|first=Mark|date=2013-03-15|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-01-15|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Rhodes supported [[Israel]] in the 2012 [[Israel–Gaza conflict]].<ref>{{cite news |title=White House says Israel 'has a right to defend itself' in Gaza |url=https://www.politico.com/blogs/politico44/2012/11/white-house-says-israel-has-a-right-to-defend-itself-in-gaza-149833 |work=Politico |date=November 17, 2020}}</ref>

Rhodes was instrumental in the conversations that led to Obama reestablishing the United States' [[diplomatic relations]] with [[Cuba]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/national/obama-legacy/us-cuba-diplomatic-relations.html|title=How Obama's Trip to Havana finally ended the cold war|last=De Young|first=Karen|date=2016-11-16|website=Washington Post|access-date=2019-01-15}}</ref> which had been cut off since 1961. ''The New York Times'' reported that Rhodes spent "more than a year sneaking off to secret negotiations in Canada and finally at the Vatican" in advance of the official announcement in December 2014.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/08/14/world/americas/a-secretive-path-to-raising-us-flag-in-cuba.html |title=A Secretive Path to Raising U.S. Flag in Cuba |last1=Davis |first1=Julie Hirschfeld |date=2015-08-13|last2=Baker |first2=Peter|newspaper=The New York Times|issn=0362-4331|access-date=2016-11-30}}</ref>

After leaving the Obama administration, Rhodes began working as a commentator.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2018/10/jamal-khashoggi-and-us-saudi-relationship/572905/|title=A Fatal Abandonment of American Leadership|last=Rhodes|first=Ben|date=2018-10-12|website=The Atlantic|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-15}}</ref> He began contributing to [[Crooked Media]], [[NBC News]] and [[MSNBC]]. In 2018, he co-founded National Security Action.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nationalsecurityaction.org/who-we-are/|title=National Security Action — WHO WE ARE|website=National Security Action|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-15}}</ref>

In 2015-16, Rhodes defended U.S. political and military backing of [[Saudi Arabia]]'s war in [[Yemen]].<ref name="inthesetimes"/> He later criticized Trump administration's involvement in the [[Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen]].<ref name="atlantic"/><ref name="inthesetimes">{{cite news |title=When Will Obama Aides Come Clean About U.S.-Saudi War Crimes? |url=http://inthesetimes.com/article/21522/saudi-arabia-obama-ben-rhodes-samantha-power-jamal-khashoggi-yemen-war |work=[[In These Times]] |date=October 22, 2018}}</ref> He wrote of the war in Yemen, "Looking back, I wonder what we might have done differently, particularly if we’d somehow known that Obama was going to be succeeded by a President Trump."<ref name="atlantic">{{cite news |title=A Fatal Abandonment of American Leadership |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2018/10/jamal-khashoggi-and-us-saudi-relationship/572905/ |work=[[The Atlantic]] |date=October 12, 2018}}</ref>

Rhodes said Obama's administration was too worried about offending [[Turkey]]. He said Obama should have recognized the [[Armenian Genocide]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Top Obama aides 'sorry' they did not recognize Armenian genocide |url=https://www.politico.com/blogs/politico44/2012/11/white-house-says-israel-has-a-right-to-defend-itself-in-gaza-149833 |work=Politico |date=January 19, 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, Random House published Rhodes's memoir, ''[[The World as It Is (book)|The World as It Is]]'', a behind-the-scenes account of [[Presidency of Barack Obama|Barack Obama's presidency]]. Rhodes has written opinion articles for newspapers and magazines including the ''New York Times'' and ''The Atlantic''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/11/25/opinion/a-chance-to-repair-americas-image-abroad.html|title=Opinion {{!}} How to Check Trump and Repair America's Image|last1=Rhodes|first1=Ben|date=2018-11-25|work=The New York Times|access-date=2018-12-10|last2=Sullivan|first2=Jake|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2018/10/jamal-khashoggi-and-us-saudi-relationship/572905/|title=A Fatal Abandonment of American Leadership|last=Rhodes|first=Ben|date=2018-10-12|website=The Atlantic|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-10}}</ref> Rhodes was featured in the HBO documentary ''[[The Final Year]]'', along with [[John Kerry]], [[Samantha Power]] and [[Susan Rice]]. The documentary portrays the events of Obama's final year in office, with a focus on his foreign policy team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://politi.co/2mFu6eb|title=How Does Obama's Foreign Policy Look a Year Into Trump?|last=Glasser|first=Susan B.|website=POLITICO Magazine|language=en|access-date=2018-12-10}}</ref>

== Awards and honors ==
In 2011, Rhodes was on [[Time (magazine)|''Time'' magazine]]'s "40 Under 40" list of powerful and prominent young professionals.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ben Rhodes: 40 Under 40 |url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2023831_2023829_2025191,00.html |work=TIME |access-date=April 9, 2012 |date=October 14, 2010}}</ref> Rhodes was number 13 on ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'' magazine's "40 Under 40" list of the most influential young people in business in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fortune.com/40-under-40/2014/ben-rhodes-13/|title=Ben Rhodes|date=2014-10-09|website=Fortune|language=en|access-date=2018-12-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181210202753/http://fortune.com/40-under-40/2014/ben-rhodes-13/|archive-date=December 10, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2015, Rhodes was named one of [[Foreign Policy|''Foreign Policy'' magazine]]'s top 100 global thinkers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://2015globalthinkers.foreignpolicy.com/|title=The Leading Global Thinkers of 2015 - Foreign Policy|website=2015globalthinkers.foreignpolicy.com|language=en|access-date=2018-12-10}}</ref>
