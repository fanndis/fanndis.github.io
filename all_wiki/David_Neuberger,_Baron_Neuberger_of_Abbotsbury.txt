'''David Edmond Neuberger, Baron Neuberger of Abbotsbury''' {{postnominals|country=HKG|GBS}} {{postnominals|country=GBR|PC|HonFRS}} ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|nj|uː|b|ɜr|ɡ|ər}}; born 10 January 1948) is an English judge. He served as [[President of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom]] from 2012 to 2017. He was a [[Lord of Appeal in Ordinary]] until the [[House of Lords]]' [[Judicial functions of the House of Lords|judicial functions]] were transferred to the new [[Supreme Court of the United Kingdom|Supreme Court]] in 2009, at which point he became [[Master of the Rolls]], the second most senior judge in England and Wales. Neuberger was appointed to the Supreme Court, as its President, in 2012. He now serves as a [[Court of Final Appeal (Hong Kong)|Non-Permanent Judge of the Hong Kong Court of Final Appeal]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.judiciary.gov.hk/en/organization/judges.htm|title=About Us – List of Judges and Judicial Officers|website=Judiciary.gov.hk|accessdate=29 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100123112819/http://www.judiciary.gov.hk/en/organization/judges.htm|archive-date=23 January 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> and the Chair of the High-Level Panel of Legal Experts on Media Freedom.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lord Neuberger and Amal Clooney announce Media Freedom Legal Panel members|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/lord-neuberger-and-amal-clooney-announce-media-freedom-legal-panel-members|access-date=2020-11-04|website=GOV.UK|language=en}}</ref>

==Early life==
Neuberger was born on 10 January 1948, the son of [[Albert Neuberger]],<ref name="whoswho">{{Who's Who | author=Anon|surname = NEUBERGER OF ABBOTSBURY  | othernames = Baron | id = U29330 | year = 2017 | doi = 10.1093/ww/9780199540884.013.29330 | edition = online [[Oxford University Press]]|location=Oxford}} {{subscription required}}</ref> Professor of Chemical [[Pathology]] at [[St Mary's Hospital, London|St Mary's Hospital]], [[University of London]], and his wife, Lilian. His uncle was the noted rabbi [[Herman N. Neuberger]]. All three of his brothers are or were professors: [[James Neuberger]] is Professor of Medicine at the [[University of Birmingham]], [[Michael Neuberger]] was Professor of Molecular Immunology at the University of Cambridge, while [[Anthony Neuberger]] is Professor in Finance at [[Warwick Business School]], [[University of Warwick]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Anthony Neuberger|url=http://www2.warwick.ac.uk/fac/soc/wbs/subjects/finance/faculty1/anthony_neuberger|website=2.warwick.ac.uk|accessdate=27 July 2009}}</ref>

He was educated first at [[The Hall School, Hampstead]], then [[Westminster School]], and studied [[chemistry]] at [[Christ Church, Oxford]].<ref name="whoswho"/><ref name="cabinetoffice">{{cite web|title=The Panel on Fair Access to the Professions: David Neuberger|url=http://www.cabinetoffice.gov.uk/strategy/work_areas/accessprofessions/david_neuberger.aspx|archive-url=http://webarchive.nationalarchives.gov.uk/20090316085739/http://www.cabinetoffice.gov.uk/strategy/work_areas/accessprofessions/david_neuberger.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 March 2009|publisher=[[Cabinet Office]]|date=23 February 2009|accessdate=28 July 2009}}</ref>

==Career==
After graduation, Neuberger worked at the [[merchant bank]], [[N M Rothschild & Sons]], from 1970–73.<ref name="whoswho"/><ref>[https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601102&sid=aNLSTDfvAyUI&refer=uk U.K.'s Youngest Law Lord David Neuberger Joins Court at Age 59], [[Bloomberg L.P.|Bloomberg]], 10 January 2007.</ref> Neuberger was [[called to the Bar]] at [[Lincoln's Inn]] in 1974,<ref name="whoswho"/><ref name="no10">{{cite web|title=Judicial Appointment for Sir David|url=http://www.number10.gov.uk/Page10592|publisher=[[10 Downing Street]]|date=13 December 2006|accessdate=27 July 2009|url-status=dead|archiveurl=http://webarchive.nationalarchives.gov.uk/20091112080204/http%3A//www.number10.gov.uk/Page10592|archivedate=12 November 2009}}</ref> where he became a [[Bencher]] in 1993. He became a [[Queen's Counsel]] in 1987. He was a [[Recorder (judge)|Recorder]] from 1990 to 1 October 1996, when he was appointed a [[High Court judge (England and Wales)|High Court Judge]] in the [[Chancery Division]] and received the customary [[knight bachelor|knighthood]].<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=54543|date=4 October 1996|page=13211}}</ref>

In 2001, he was made Supervisory Chancery Judge of [[English Midlands|Midland]], Wales and [[Chester]], and of the Western Circuits, a post he held until 12 January 2004, when he was appointed a [[Lord Justice of Appeal]] and a member of the [[Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy Council|Privy Council]].<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=57179|date=15 January 2004|page=504}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.privy-council.org.uk/files/word/11%20February%202004.doc|archive-url=http://webarchive.nationalarchives.gov.uk/20101103140224/http://www.privy-council.org.uk/files/word/11%20February%202004.doc|url-status=dead|archive-date=3 November 2010|publisher=Privy Council Office|title=Orders for 11 February 2004}}</ref>

Since 2005, he has been co-chair (with [[Richard Susskind]]) of ITAC (Lord Chancellor's Information Technology and Courts Committee).

On 11 January 2007, he succeeded [[Donald Nicholls, Baron Nicholls of Birkenhead|Lord Nicholls of Birkenhead]] as a [[Lord of Appeal in Ordinary]]<ref name="no10"/> and was made a [[life peer]] as '''Baron Neuberger of Abbotsbury''', of [[Abbotsbury]] in the County of [[Dorset]], and [[Introduction (House of Lords)|introduced]] in the [[House of Lords]] on 15 January 2007 between [[Thomas Bingham, Baron Bingham of Cornhill|Lord Bingham of Cornhill]] and his sister-in-law, [[Julia Neuberger, Baroness Neuberger|Baroness Neuberger]].<ref>[https://publications.parliament.uk/pa/ld200607/minutes/070116/ldordpap.htm#minproc Minutes of Proceedings of Monday 15 January 2007]; Publications.parliament.uk, retrieved 27 July 2009.</ref>

His rise to the [[Court of Appeal]]<ref name=coa>{{cite web|url=http://www.hmcourts-service.gov.uk/cms/1287.htm|title=Lord Neuberger's entry on Court of Appeal webpage|website=hmcourts-service.gov.uk|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100718191227/http://www.hmcourts-service.gov.uk/cms/1287.htm|archivedate=18 July 2010|df=dmy-all}}</ref> and then to the House of Lords is one of the quickest in recent times. Although [[Patrick Devlin, Baron Devlin|Lord Devlin]] was, at 55, even younger on his own appointment to the House of Lords in 1960, Neuberger was the youngest sitting Law Lord.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thelawyer.com/cgi-bin/item.cgi?id=123470|title=Neuberger LJ in fast-track promotion to the Lords]|work=[[The Lawyer]]|date=13 December 2006|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927233951/http://www.thelawyer.com/cgi-bin/item.cgi?id=123470|archivedate=27 September 2007}}</ref>

It was announced on 23 July 2009 that he would be appointed the next [[Master of the Rolls]], succeeding [[Anthony Clarke, Baron Clarke of Stone-cum-Ebony|Lord Clarke of Stone-cum-Ebony]], who became one of the inaugural [[Justice of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom|Justices]] of the [[Supreme Court of the United Kingdom|Supreme Court]] on the retirement of [[Richard Scott, Baron Scott of Foscote|Lord Scott of Foscote]]. This appointment took effect on 1 October 2009.<ref>{{cite book|author1=Bill Jones|author2=Philip Norton|title=Politics UK|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ybXcAwAAQBAJ&pg=PA442|year=2014|publisher=Routledge|page=442|isbn=9781317581031}}</ref>

Between 2006 and 2007, he led an investigation for the Bar Council into widening access to the Bar. He also served on the Panel on Fair Access to the Professions, led by former [[Secretary of State for Health|Health Secretary]] [[Alan Milburn]], which reported in July 2009. Other Panel members included [[Trevor Phillips]], head of the [[Commission for Equalities and Human Rights]], [[Michael Grade]], Chairman of [[ITV plc|ITV]], and [[Martin Rees]], [[Astronomer Royal]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Panel on Fair Access to the Professions|url=http://www.cabinetoffice.gov.uk/strategy/work_areas/accessprofessions.aspx|archive-url=http://webarchive.nationalarchives.gov.uk/20090316085058/http://www.cabinetoffice.gov.uk/strategy/work_areas/accessprofessions.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 March 2009|date=27 July 2009|accessdate=28 July 2009}}</ref>

In May 2010, Neuberger gave an ''[[Ex tempore#Jurisprudence|ex tempore]]'' dissenting judgment that the trade union [[Unite the Union|Unite]] had not complied with ballot rules under [[TULRCA 1992|trade union legislation]].<ref>{{cite news|title=BA strike: Ban lifted by High Court|publisher=BBC News|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/10130274.stm|date=20 May 2010|accessdate=21 May 2010}}</ref>

In July 2010 Neuberger ruled that peace protesters in [[Parliament Square]] who had camped out in Democracy Village should be evicted after the protesters lost an appeal.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-10659518 |publisher=BBC News |title=Parliament Square protesters lose eviction appeal |date=16 July 2010}}</ref>

In May 2011, while commenting on [[2011 British super-injunction controversy|super injunctions]], he said that social media sites like Twitter were "totally out of control" and society should consider ways to bring such websites under control.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-13489775|publisher=BBC News|title=Journalist's Twitter posts spark prosecution call|date=22 May 2011}}</ref>

In July 2012, it was announced that Neuberger would succeed [[Nicholas Phillips, Baron Phillips of Worth Matravers|Lord Phillips of Worth Matravers]] as [[President of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom|President of the Supreme Court]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.supremecourt.uk/news/new-president-of-the-supreme-court.html|title=New President of the Supreme Court|date=12 July 2012|publisher=[[Supreme Court of the United Kingdom]]|accessdate=12 July 2012}}</ref> which post he took up on 1 October 2012.<ref>{{cite news|title=New Supreme Court chief Lord Neuberger sworn in|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/home-news/new-supreme-court-chief-lord-neuberger-sworn-in-8192155.html|newspaper=Independent|location=London, UK|first=Brian|last=Farmer|date=1 October 2012}}</ref>

In February 2017, it was announced that Neuberger was stepping down "in the Summer" from his role in the Supreme Court and retiring.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.supremecourt.uk/news/judicial-vacancies.html|title=Supreme Court – Judicial Vaccancies|date=16 February 2017|website=UK Supreme Court|access-date=16 February 2016}}</ref><ref>In July 2017, it was announced that [[Brenda Hale, Baroness Hale of Richmond|Baroness Hale of Richmond]] would succeed him as President of the Supreme Court in September {{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/president-of-the-supreme-court-appointment-baroness-hale|title=President of the Supreme Court appointment: Baroness Hale|publisher=[[10 Downing Street]]|date=21 July 2017|accessdate=23 July 2017}}</ref>

He now sits as a voting cross-bench member of the House of Lords<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://members.parliament.uk/member/3827/contact|title=Contact information for Lord Neuberger of Abbotsbury - MPs and Lords - UK Parliament|website=members.parliament.uk|language=en|access-date=2020-01-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://parliamentlive.tv/event/index/ab8180c6-0b80-4cba-8141-30c6d824f6e5?in=11:17:14&out=11:18:03|title=Parliamentlive.tv|website=parliamentlive.tv|access-date=2020-01-16}}</ref> and is the Chair<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lord Neuberger and Amal Clooney announce Media Freedom Legal Panel members|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/lord-neuberger-and-amal-clooney-announce-media-freedom-legal-panel-members|access-date=2020-11-04|website=GOV.UK|language=en}}</ref> of the High-Level Panel of Legal Experts on Media Freedom, an independent body convened at the request of the UK and Canadian governments.<ref>{{Cite web|title=IBA - IBAHRI Secretariat to the High Level Panel of Legal Experts on Media Freedom|url=https://www.ibanet.org/IBAHRISecretariat.aspx|access-date=2020-11-04|website=www.ibanet.org|language=en-gb}}</ref>

===Awards and honours===
He was elected an Honorary [[Fellow of the Royal Society]] in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://royalsociety.org/people/david-neuberger-13407|title=David Neuberger|publisher= Royal Society|location=London|year=2017|author=Anon|website=royalsociety.org|accessdate=27 May 2017}}</ref>

In 2018, Neuberger was awarded the [[Gold Bauhinia Star]] by the [[Chief Executive of Hong Kong|Chief Executive]] of [[Hong Kong]].<ref>[https://www.gld.gov.hk/egazette/pdf/20182229e/egn2018222932.pdf "G.N.(E.) 32"] [[Hong Kong Government Gazette]] (No. 29 Vol. 22 (Extraordinary), 1 July 2018)</ref>

===Significant lectures===
On 24 February 2014, Neuberger delivered at Cambridge University the [[Freshfields]] annual lecture, which he entitled "The British and Europe".<ref>https://www.supremecourt.uk/docs/speech-140212.pdf</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:1 David Neuberger, Baron Neuberger of Abbotsbury 2016.jpg|thumb|Lord Neuberger of Abbotsbury speaking at a conference in [[Singapore]] in 2016]]
In 1976, Neuberger married Angela Holdsworth, the TV producer and writer. They have three children, Jessica, Nicholas and Max, who are all solicitors.<ref name="cabinetoffice"/> Neuberger's sister-in-law, through his brother [[Anthony Neuberger]], is [[Julia Neuberger, Baroness Neuberger]], Senior Rabbi of the [[West London Synagogue]].

Neuberger was Chairman of the Schizophrenia Trust from 2003 to 2013, when it merged with and was subsumed by [[Mental Health Research UK]]: he is now a Trustee of MHRUK. He was a Governor of the [[University of the Arts London]] between 2000 and 2010. He was President of the [[British Records Association]] from 2009 to 2012, in his capacity as Master of the Rolls.

On 18th May 2020 he was appointed as Deputy President of [[The Academy of Experts]] due to replace [[Mark Saville, Baron Saville of Newdigate]] as President in late 2020.
