'''Rodrick Wayne Moore, Jr.'''<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=550580472&subid=0 | title=BMI &#124; Repertoire Search |work=BMI}}</ref> (born October 22, 1998), known professionally as '''Roddy Ricch''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter.<ref name="Genius">{{cite web |url=https://genius.com/artists/Roddy-ricch |title=Roddy Ricch|publisher=Genius |accessdate=21 November 2018}}</ref> He released his first two mixtapes ''Feed Tha Streets'' (2017) and ''[[Feed Tha Streets II]]'' (2018).<ref name="XXLABC">{{cite news | last=Seabrook III| first=Robby| title =Roddy Ricch Pulls Out a Rack in XXL's ABCs| work =XXL| date =18 July 2019 | url =https://www.xxlmag.com/video/2019/07/roddy-ricch-interview-abcs/| accessdate =27 March 2020}}</ref> The latter peaked at number 67 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and featured his first [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] charting single "[[Die Young (Roddy Ricch song)|Die Young]]". In 2019, Ricch was featured on [[Nipsey Hussle]]'s single "[[Racks in the Middle]]", which earned him a [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance]], and on [[Mustard (record producer)|Mustard]]'s Grammy-nominated single "[[Ballin' (Mustard song)|Ballin']]", which reached the top 20 of the Hot 100.

Ricch's debut studio album ''[[Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial]]'' (2019) was released through [[Atlantic Records]] and Bird Vision Entertainment, and debuted atop the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8546369/roddy-ricch-please-excuse-me-for-being-antisocial-no-1-billboard-200|title=Roddy Ricch Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 With 'Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial'|work=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=December 15, 2019|accessdate=December 16, 2019}}</ref> It spawned the Hot 100 number-one single "[[The Box (Roddy Ricch song)|The Box]]", becoming his most popular song worldwide, as well as the single "[[High Fashion (song)|High Fashion]]", featuring [[Mustard (record producer)|Mustard]]. Ricch's feature on [[DaBaby]]'s single "[[Rockstar (DaBaby song)|Rockstar]]" became his second number-one single on the Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite web|title=DaBaby & Roddy Ricch's 'Rockstar' Rises to No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100 — Billboard|url=https://apple.news/Aoc14Kwx-SGSFzoFdq1BM1g|accessdate=2020-06-08|website=apple.news|language=en-US}}</ref> 

Ricch has won [[List of awards and nominations received by Roddy Ricch|several accolades]], including a [[Grammy Award]] from nine nominations, as well as two [[BET Awards]], two [[BET Hip Hop Awards]], and an [[American Music Award]].

==Early life==
Rodrick Wayne Moore, Jr. was born on October 22, 1998 in [[Compton, California]], where he was raised. Ricch attended Carson Senior High School and [[Westchester Enriched Sciences Magnets]].<ref name="XXLBreak">{{cite magazine | last=Seabrook III| first=Robby| title =The Break Presents: Roddy Ricch| magazine =XXL| date =8 November 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/11/roddy-ricch-interview-the-break/| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> He spent some time living in [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] in his youth.<ref name="Passion" /> Roddy Ricch started rapping and singing as young as 8, and he began making beats in earnest at age 16.<ref name="XXLBreak" /><ref name="O4L">{{cite news | title =Roddy Ricch Interview Talks Rapping, Record Labels, NBA| publisher =O4L Online Network | date = 20 February 2018 | url =https://o-4-l.com/o4l/2018/02/20/roddy-rich-interview-the-dinner-club/| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> In Compton, Ricch was a member of the Park Village Compton [[Crips]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VhITJYste6o|title=Roddy Ricch: speaks on growing up in Compton, being a crip & hit record Die Young}}</ref> and has two "C"s tattooed, symbolizing his previous affiliation with the gang.<ref name="Passion" /> He also had a brief stint in [[county jail]] at the time when his mixtape, ''[[Feed Tha Streets II]]'' was released.<ref name="Passion" /> He listened to [[Lil Wayne]] growing up, as well as [[Young Thug]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], and Speaker Knockerz.<ref>https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/30/roddy-ricch-feed-tha-streets-meek-mill-interview</ref>

==Career==
===2017–2018: ''Feed Tha Streets'' mixtapes and collaborations===
In November 2017, he released his debut mixtape, ''Feed Tha Streets'', which is also his first music project. It featured songs like "Chase Tha Bag", "Hoodricch", and "Fucc It Up". The tape earned him praise from established rappers like [[Meek Mill]], [[Nipsey Hussle]], and [[03 Greedo]], as well as established record producer [[Mustard (record producer)|Mustard]].{{Citation needed|date=January 2020}} In March 2018, he released an [[Extended play|EP]] entitled ''Be 4 Tha Fame''. In May of that year, Nipsey Hussle brought Ricch out as his guest at a [[KPWR|PowerHouse]] concert in [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="XXLBreak"/><ref name="Passion"/>

In July 2018, he released the [[London on da Track]]-produced single, "Die Young", which he had written for a childhood friend, who was lost in a high speed chase and said in a Genius interview he had written it the night American rapper [[XXXTentacion]] died.<ref name="XXLBreak"/><ref name="Passion02">{{cite news | last=Hagle| first=Will| title =Compton's Roddy Ricch Gets Existential| website =Passion of the Weiss | date =25 July 2018 | url =https://www.passionweiss.com/2018/07/25/roddy-ricch-die-young/| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> The song—which was dedicated, in part, to the childhood friend and its music video would go on to accumulate over 80 million views on [[YouTube]] and 120 million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name="RS">{{cite magazine | last=Holmes| first=Charles| title =Song You Need To Know: Roddy Ricch, 'Die Young' | magazine =Rolling Stone | date =13 November 2018 | url =https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/song-you-need-to-know-roddy-ricch-die-young-752029/| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> In August 2018, he released the single "Ricch Forever", produced by DJ Bugsy.<ref name="Fest">{{cite news | title =Buzzing LA Rapper Roddy Ricch Teams Up With DJ Bugsy For New Single "Ricch Forever"|website =Rap Fest | date =23 August 2018 | url =http://therapfest.com/buzzing-la-rapper-roddy-ricch-teams-dj-bugsy-new-single-ricch-forever/| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> That month, [[Marshmello]] released a preview of a them with a collaborative track single with Ricch titled "[[Project Dreams]]", which was released on December 7, 2018.

In October 2018, [[Meek Mill]] brought Ricch out as his guest at a PowerHouse concert in [[Philadelphia]]. Mill also gifted him a "Dreamchasers" chain, appearing on his fourth studio album ''[[Championships (album)|Championships]]'' (2018), in which he was a feature alongside [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[Young Thug]] on the song "Splash Warning" from the album.<ref name="XXLBreak"/><ref name="UPR">{{cite news | last=Williams| first=Aaron| title =Compton Rapper Roddy Ricch Is Already A Bigger Star Than You Know| website =Uproxx | date =2 November 2018 | url =https://uproxx.com/hiphop/roddy-ricch-feed-the-streets-ii-meek-mill/| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> On November 2, 2018, Ricch released his second mixtape, ''[[Feed Tha Streets II]]''. The album, which features the singles "Die Young" and "Every Season",<ref name="UPR"/><ref name="HB">{{cite news | last=Montes| first=Patrick| title =Roddy Ricch Represents for the West Coast on 'Feed Tha Streets II'| website =HypeBeast | date =2 November 2018 | url =https://hypebeast.com/2018/11/roddy-ricch-feed-tha-streets-ii-2018-new-mixtape-stream| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> peaked at number 67 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart<ref name="BB200">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2019-04-27 |title=Billboard 200 - The week of April 27, 2019|work=Billboard|accessdate=April 23, 2019}}</ref> and at number 36 on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart.<ref name="BBRBHH">{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/r-b-hip-hop-albums/2018-11-17 |title=Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums - The week of November 17, 2018|magazine=Billboard |accessdate=21 November 2018}}</ref> Later in the month, Marshmello previewed a new track with Ricch.<ref name="BBMarsh">{{cite magazine | last=Bein| first=Kat| title =Marshmello Teases Tune With Roddy Ricch 'Coming Soon': Watch| magazine =Billboard | date =13 November 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8484793/marshmello-roddy-rich-watch| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial'' and "The Box"===
On February 15, 2019, Ricch was featured alongside American record producer [[Hit-Boy]] on now-late American rapper [[Nipsey Hussle]]'s single "[[Racks in the Middle]]", later becoming both first top 40 hit on the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]],<ref name="HNHH">{{cite news | last=Goddard| first=Kevin| title =Meek Mill Previews Upcoming Record With Roddy Ricch | website =HotNewHipHop | date =25 August 2018 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/meek-mill-previews-upcoming-record-with-roddy-ricch-news.58174.html| accessdate =21 November 2018}}</ref> and earning him a [[Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance|Best Rap Performance]].<ref name="Grammy" /> Exactly one month later, on March 15, he was featured alongside fellow American rapper [[Tyga]] on the remix of American singer [[Post Malone]]'s hit single "[[Wow (Post Malone song)|Wow]]". On June 28, he collaborated with American DJ and record producer [[Mustard (record producer)|Mustard]] on the single, "[[Ballin' (Mustard song)|Ballin']]", off of Mustard's third studio album, [[Perfect Ten (album)|''Perfect Ten'']], which became Mustard's highest charting song on the Hot 100 as a lead artist, peaking at number 12.<ref name="Hot 100">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/roddy-ricch/chart-history/HSI|title=Roddy Ricch Chart History: Hot 100|work=Billboard|accessdate=December 24, 2019}}</ref> The song received a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Performance|Best Rap/Sung Performance]] at the [[2020 Grammy Awards]].<ref name="Grammy">{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/roddy-ricch|title=Roddy Ricch Grammy Awards History|date=November 19, 2019|website=[[The Recording Academy]]|accessdate=May 10, 2020}}</ref> 

On October 11, 2019, Ricch released the single "[[Big Stepper]]", the lead single of his debut studio album, ''[[Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial]]''. Two weeks later, on October 25, he released the album's second single, "[[Start wit Me]]", featuring fellow American rapper [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]]. Exactly one month later, on November 25, he released the album's third and final pre-release single "[[Tip Toe (Roddy Ricch song)|Tip Toe]]", featuring fellow American rapper [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]]. On December 6, 2019, the album was released, debuting and spending four non-consecutive weeks at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200. It became the longest running number one debut rap album in the US since 2003.<ref name="Time Viral">{{Cite web|last=Lang|first=Cady Lang|date=February 18, 2020|title=Going Viral Helped Catapult Roddy Ricch and 'The Box' to #1 — But There's More to the Story|url=https://time.com/5784337/roddy-ricch-the-box-interview/|accessdate=March 25, 2020|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref> The album featured Ricch's highest charting single, "[[The Box (Roddy Ricch song)|The Box]]", which topped the [[Hot 100]] for eleven weeks, as well as "[[Start wit Me]]" featuring [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], which peaked at number 56. Because of the strength of "The Box", a collaboration with Mustard, "[[High Fashion (song)|High Fashion]]", which appeared on the album, reached the top 40 at number 35, the week he hit number one on the Hot 100. The song later peaked at number 20.<ref name="Hot 100"/> 

In 2020, he appeared on a number of songs by other artists, including the [[Nipsey Hussle]]-ode "[[Letter to Nipsey]]" by [[Meek Mill]], "[[Numbers (A Boogie wit da Hoodie song)|Numbers]]" by [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]] alongside Gunna and [[London on da Track]], and "[[Walk Em Down]]" by [[NLE Choppa]]. In April 2020, Ricch was featured on [[DaBaby]]'s "[[Rockstar (DaBaby song)|Rockstar]]", which became his second number one single on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, spending seven weeks on top, as well as reaching number one in the UK, and in several other countries. Ricch became the first artist to achieve his first two number-one singles in the same year since [[Ed Sheeran]] did it in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9398276/da-baby-rockstar-number-one-hot-100|title=DaBaby & Roddy Ricch's 'Rockstar' Rises to No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=June 8, 2020|accessdate=June 9, 2020}}</ref> Ricch won [[BET Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] at the [[2020 BET Awards]].<ref name="bet20">{{cite web|url=https://www.etonline.com/bet-awards-2020-see-the-full-nominations-list-148102|title=BET Awards 2020: See the Full Nominations List|work=ET Online}}</ref> He has spent eighteen cumulative weeks atop the Hot 100 so far, the most for any artist in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9417430/dababy-rockstar-number-one-fifth-week-hot-100|title=DaBaby's 'Rockstar' Rules Hot 100 For Fifth Week, Pop Smoke's 'For the Night' Debuts in Top 10|work=Billboard|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=July 13, 2020|accessdate=July 14, 2020}}</ref> On July 3, 2020, Ricch was featured alongside fellow American rapper [[50 Cent]] on late fellow American rapper [[Pop Smoke]]'s posthumous single "[[The Woo]]", from Smoke's debut studio album, ''[[Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon]]''. The track reached number nine in the UK and number 11 on the Hot 100. On August 27, Ricch featured on fellow American rapper [[Cordae]]'s single "[[Gifted (song)|Gifted]]".

During an interview with ''[[GQ]]'', Ricch revealed that his second studio album is completed, however, he said that he is waiting for the right time to release it. Ricch called the album "a full-blown masterpiece. A real idea. A real body of work".<ref name="SecondAlbum">{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2020/08/11/roddy-rich-full-blown-masterpiece/|title=Roddy Ricch Says He is Aiming to Create a 'Full Blown Masterpiece'|work=[[The Source]]|last=Grant|first=Shawn|date=August 11, 2020|accessdate=September 8, 2020}}</ref> On November 24, 2020, he teased towards an upcoming project titled "Love Is Barely Real Anymore".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rapfavorites.com/roddy-ricch-teases-new-project/|title=Roddy Ricch Teases New Project|website=Rapfavorites|last=Medina|first=Nick|date=November 24, 2020|accessdate=November 25, 2020}}</ref>

Ricch tied with [[The Weeknd]] as the most nominated artists at the [[American Music Awards of 2020|2020 American Music Awards]], both with eight nominations.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/9472677/12-takeaways-2020-american-music-award-nominations|title=12 Top Takeaways from the 2020 American Music Award Nominations|website=Billboard}}</ref>  He received six nominations at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]], including for [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] for "The Box" and "Rockstar", respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/11/24/arts/music/grammy-nominees-list.html|title=Grammy Nominations 2021|work=The New York Times}}</ref> ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' named him Breakthrough Artist of 2020,<ref name="variety">{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/roddy-ricch-variety-breakthrough-artist-hitmakers-please-excuse-me-for-being-antisocial-1234843745/|title=Roddy Ricch Is Variety’s Breakthrough Artist of 2020|work=Variety}}</ref> and [[Apple Music]] awarded him Album and Song of the Year in 2020,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2020/11/apple-announces-second-annual-apple-music-awards/|title=Apple announces second annual Apple Music Awards|website=Apple Music}}</ref> with ''Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial'' and "The Box" being the most streamed album and song of the year globally on the platform.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/streaming/9493053/apple-music-roddy-ricch-year-end-charts/|title=Roddy Ricch Dominates Apple Music's Year-End Charts|work=Billboard}}</ref> He was ranked third on the [[Billboard Year-End|''Billboard'' Year-End]] chart for Top Artists.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/year-end/2020/top-artists|title=Top Artists|work=Billboard}}</ref>

==Artistry==

On the transcending themes of his music, Ricch draws inspiration from his life, stating: "As I experience life, my music is gonna evolve. At the same time, I still do tell the stories from my world because there are unlimited stories and unlimited people from that place. I'll always represent them. As my life begins to change and I do different things, I still want to be able to tap in and relate to them".<ref name="GQ">{{Cite web|last=Schube|first=Will|date=January 24, 2020|title=Chart-Topping Rapper Roddy Ricch Won't Let Superstardom Change Him|url=https://www.gq.com/story/roddy-ricch-the-box-interview-2020|accessdate=February 16, 2020|website=[[GQ]]}}</ref>

Cady Lang of [[Time (magazine)|''Time'']] magazine noted Ricch's "remarkable musical style as a rapper combines his [[West Coast hip hop|West Coast [hip hop]]] roots with the sound of [[Trap music|trap]] music and Chicago [[Drill music|drill]] rap, which gets an extra [[gravitas]] with his lyrics that range from pondering the tough realities of life to bouts of uninhibited bravado".<ref name="Time Viral" /> 

Ricch has been recognized for his raspy voice "that works wonders with vocal filters".<ref name="bittersweet">{{Cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2019-12-05/roddy-ricch-nipsey-hussle-grammys-2020-racks-in-the-middle|title=For Nipsey Hussle friend Roddy Ricch, Grammy recognition is bittersweet|last=Roberts|first=Randall|date=December 5, 2019|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]|accessdate=January 16, 2020}}</ref> Paul Thompson of ''[[Vulture (website)|Vulture]]'' called Ricch "an undeniably talented vocalist and occasionally a compelling songwriter", opining how he "frequently adopts the same [[Musical syntax|syntax]] and vocal [[Intonation (music)|intonations]] as [[Young Thug]]".<ref name="Vulture">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/01/roddy-ricch-the-box-explained.html|title=How Roddy Ricch's 'The Box' Just Became Rap's First Viral No. 1 Hit of 2020|last=Thompson|first=Paul|date=January 13, 2020|website=[[New_York_(magazine)#Digital_expansion_and_blogs|Vulture]]|accessdate=February 16, 2020}}</ref> His music style blends rapping and singing.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/roddy-ricch-interview-craziest-moment-meeting-j-cole/|title=Roddy Ricch's Craziest Moment So Far Involves Meeting J Cole|work=XXL}}</ref>

Ricch has cited [[Kendrick Lamar]], who is also from his hometown of Compton, as a musical influence.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/roddy-ricch-explains-how-meeting-kendrick-lamar-as-a-teenager-inspired-his-career|title=Roddy Ricch Explains How Meeting Kendrick Lamar As A Teenager Inspired His Career|website=Genius}}</ref> He has stated that rapper Speaker Knockerz is his biggest influence, along with the "basis" of his music coming from him. Ricch also credits [[Young Thug]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[Gucci Mane]] as influences.<ref name="Passion">{{Cite web|url=https://www.passionweiss.com/2018/06/13/roddy-ricch-interview/|title="At the End of the Day, I Hold My Own Nuts": An Interview with Roddy Ricch|website=Passionweiss|last=Nukem|first=Nick|date=June 13, 2018|accessdate=May 10, 2020}}</ref>

{{quote|I feel like the problem with us nowadays is we want everything right now. But the music isn’t gonna be progressive when you’re putting something out every three months, because you ain’t been through nothing; you still feel like how you felt when you [last] dropped the music. To me, stuff doesn’t happen in my life every single day. Maybe for some people it does, but my life don’t happen that fast. I have to give myself time to actually go through things so I can speak on it, and have a new understanding of life. It’s not like I make music that’s not my life, so I feel like I need to give my life time to inspire me.|Ricch in an interview with ''Variety'', 2020.<ref name="variety"/>}}
