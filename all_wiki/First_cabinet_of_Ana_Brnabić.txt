The cabinet of the [[Government of Serbia]], led by [[Prime Minister of Serbia|prime minister]] [[Ana Brnabić]], was elected on 29 June 2017 by a majority vote in the [[National Assembly of Serbia|National Assembly]]. It succeeded the [[second cabinet of Aleksandar Vučić]], formed after the [[Serbian parliamentary election, 2016|2016 parliamentary election]], after Vučić resigned the prime minister post following his [[Serbian presidential election, 2017|election]] as the [[President of Serbia]]. Vučić appointed Ana Brnabić, previously the [[Ministry of Public Administration and Local Self-Government (Serbia)|Minister of Public Administration]], as his successor on 15 June 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/jun/15/serbia-gains-its-first-female-and-gay-prime-minister-ana-brnabic |title=Serbia gets its first female – and gay – prime minister |publisher=[[The Guardian]] |date=15 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/06/15/serbias-president-formally-nominates-countrys-first-openly-gay/ |title=Serbia's president formally nominates country's first openly gay prime minister |publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph]]| date=15 June 2017}}</ref>

==History==
The cabinet comprises ministers from the [[Serbian Progressive Party]] (SNS), [[Socialist Party of Serbia]] (SPS), [[Social Democratic Party of Serbia]] (SDPS), [[Movement of Socialists]] (PS), [[Party of United Pensioners of Serbia]] (PUPS), and [[Serbian National Party (2014)|Serbian National Party]] (SNP), as well as some without a party affiliation. It consists mostly of the same ministers from the previous cabinet, with three new members introduced: [[Branko Ružić (politician)|Branko Ružić]] (SPS) taking the Brnabić's previous ministry, [[Goran Trivan]] (SPS) taking the new post of [[Ministry of Environmental Protection (Serbia)|Minister of Environmental Protection]], and [[Nenad Popović]] (SNP) a minister without portfolio in charge of innovations. [[Aleksandar Vulin]], formerly the [[Ministry of Labour, Employment, Veteran and Social Policy (Serbia)|Minister of Labour]], and [[Zoran Đorđević (politician)|Zoran Đorđević]], formerly the [[Ministry of Defence (Serbia)|Minister of Defence]], swapped places. [[Jadranka Joksimović]], formerly a minister without portfolio, assumed the new [[Ministry of European Integration (Serbia)|Ministry of European Integration]]. The cabinet was approved by 157 votes for and 57 against, out of 250 members of the National Assembly.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blic.rs/vesti/politika/srbija-dobila-novu-vladu-premijerka-ana-brnabic-i-ministri-polozili-zakletvu/8hfjk85 |title=SRBIJA DOBILA NOVU VLADU Premijerka Ana Brnabić i ministri položili zakletvu |trans-title=Serbia gets a new government: Prime minister Ana Brnabić and the ministers took the oath |date=29 June 2017 |publisher=[[Blic]]}}</ref>

On 7 May 2018, [[Ministry of Finance (Serbia)|Minister of Finance]] [[Dušan Vujović]] resigned from the position due to personal reasons.<ref name="resignationMay18">{{cite news|title=Vujović podneo ostavku|url=https://www.b92.net/biz/vesti/srbija.php?yyyy=2018&mm=05&dd=07&nav_id=1389939|access-date=7 May 2018|work=b92.net|agency=Tanjug|date=7 May 2018|language=Serbian}}</ref> On 28 May 2018, [[Siniša Mali]], at the time [[Mayor of Belgrade]], was appointed as the new Minister of Finance.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Antonijević|first1=M.|title=Mali od danas više neće biti gradonačelnik, evo ko će VODITI BEOGRAD do izbora novog|url=https://www.blic.rs/vesti/beograd/mali-od-danas-vise-nece-biti-gradonacelnik-evo-ko-ce-voditi-beograd-do-izbora-novog/krwvq0w|access-date=28 May 2018|work=blic.rs|date=28 May 2018|language=Serbian}}</ref>
