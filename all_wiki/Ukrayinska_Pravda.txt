'''''Ukrayinska Pravda''''' ({{lang-uk|Українська правда}}, literally ''Ukrainian Truth'') is a [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] [[online newspaper]] founded by [[Georgiy R. Gongadze|Georgiy Gongadze]] on 16 April 2000 (the day of the [[2000 Ukrainian constitutional referendum|Ukrainian constitutional referendum]]).<ref name=dateob>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=eSVpAAAAMAAJ&q=+referendum+took+place|title=Beheaded: The Killing of a Journalist|first=J. V.|last=Koshiw|publisher=Artemia Press|year=2002|isbn=978-0-9543764-0-6|page=63}}</ref> Published mainly in [[Ukrainian language|Ukrainian]] with selected articles published in or translated to [[Russian language|Russian]] and [[English language|English]], the newspaper is tailored for a general readership with emphasis on the hot issues of the [[politics of Ukraine]]. The Ukrainian government has at times reportedly exerted pressure on the publication to [[freedom of information|restrict access to information]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://helsinki.org.ua/en/index.php?id=1349967143|title=Українська Гельсінська спілка з прав людини|website=helsinki.org.ua}}</ref>

Along with [[Hromadske TV]] and Center UA (Center for United Action), Ukrayinska Pravda is part of the [[Kyiv MediaHub]].

The newspaper was often first to investigate and publish the material on corruption or unethical conduct of, or related to, Ukrainian politicians from all political camps.{{Citation needed|date=June 2020}} In December 2002, Ukrayinska Pravda was refused a press accreditation by the (then) [[General Prosecutor of Ukraine]] [[Svyatoslav Piskun]] (an offence against the Criminal Code of Ukraine).<ref>[http://www2.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2002/12/5/2280.htm JOURNALISTS BOYCOTT PROSECUTOR GENERAL'S OFFICE], Ukrayinska Pravda (December 5, 2002)</ref>

Staff and contributors of the ''Ukrayinska Pravda'' have pioneered many legal and research techniques aimed at advancing [[freedom of information]] in Ukraine, particularly those concerning the [[public spending]], [[Government procurement|public procurement]] and [[Offshore company|offshore]] [[Tax noncompliance|tax evasion]]. Staff journalists routinely participate in non-partisan [[activism|public actions]] promoting democracy and press freedom in the country.

==Offspring Web sites of the Ukrayinska Pravda project==
Ukrayinska Pravda is also the umbrella site for the following more recent sister Web sites:
*''[https://blogs.pravda.com.ua/ Ukrayinska Pravda – Blohy]'' ("Blogs") – selected bloggers
*''[https://www.epravda.com.ua/ Ekonomichna Pravda]'' ("Economic Truth") – economy and business news and publications
*''[https://www.istpravda.com.ua/ Istorychna Pravda]'' ("Historical Truth") – non-news [[history]] magazine
*''[https://kiev.pravda.com.ua/ Ukrayinska Pravda – Kyiv]'' – local news and articles on [[Kyiv]]
*''[[Tablo ID]]'' – celebrity illustrated news site, paying significant attention to the public life of Ukrainian politicians and statesmen
*''[https://www.eurointegration.com.ua/ Yevropeiska Pravda]'' ("European Truth") – [[international security]] and [[European integration]] in Ukraine
*''[https://life.pravda.com.ua/ Ukrayinska Pravda – Zhyttia]'' ("Life") – social [[ezine]]

Editorial [[copyright]] disclaimers<ref>[http://www.pravda.com.ua/rules/ Правила використання матеріалів сайтів Інтернет-холдингу "Українська правда"], [http://www.narodnapravda.com.ua/faq/ Часті запитання]{{in lang|uk}}</ref> collectively describe these sites as the "Ukrayinska Pravda Internet Holding", not specifying the legal nature of the holding.

Among regular bloggers at ''Ukrayinska Pravda'' are [[Anatoliy Hrytsenko]], [[Ruslana Lyzhychko]], [[Inna Bohoslovska]], [[Tetyana Chornovol]], [[Yuriy Lutsenko]] and many others.
