Pride Live is a national not-for-profit 501(c)(3) organization elevating awareness and support for the [[LGBTQ+]] community via campaign development and social advocacy.

== Campaigns ==

=== Stonewall Day ===
[[Stonewall Day]] is a global campaign launched in 2018<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pinknews.co.uk/2018/06/26/stonewall-riots-anniversary-day-lgbt-rights-us/|title=Stonewall Day established to mark anniversary of the riots|date=2018-06-26|website=PinkNews - Gay news, reviews and comment from the world's most read lesbian, gay, bisexual, and trans news service|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-10-26}}</ref> to elevate awareness and support for [[Stonewall riots|the Stonewall riots]] legacy and the continuing fight for LGBTQ+ equality.

The second annual Stonewall Day was held on June 28, 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/pride-live-stonewall-day-concert-lady-gaga-alicia-keys-celebrate-lgbtq-progress-1221847|title=Lady Gaga, Alicia Keys and More Stars Celebrate LGBTQ Progress at Pride Live's Stonewall Day Concert|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en|access-date=2019-10-26}}</ref> the exact date of the 50th anniversary of the Stonewall Riots. Events featured a reception at the Stonewall Inn, a partnership with the [[New York Red Bulls]] and their Pride Night, and the Stonewall Day concert featuring performances by [[Alicia Keys]] and [[Alex Newell]], and special appearances by [[Lady Gaga]], [[Whoopi Goldberg]], [[Donatella Versace]], [[Josephine Skriver]], [[Angelica Ross]], [[Ryan Jamaal Swain]], [[Geena Rocero]], Blossom C. Brown, [[Chelsea Clinton]], and [[Valerie Jarrett]].

=== Stonewall Ambassadors ===

The Stonewall Ambassadors<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.insideradio.com/free/iheartmedia-new-york-and-pride-live-host-second-annual-stonewall/article_c3e78080-978f-11e9-9fd1-b75b92461fa0.html|title=iHeartMedia New York And Pride Live Host Second Annual Stonewall Day.|website=Insideradio.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-26}}</ref> are a collective of activists, influencers, business leaders, academics, and celebrities who joined together in support of the 50th anniversary of the Stonewall Riots.

=== REBEL 628 ===
Launched in April 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prweb.com/releases/pride_live_to_launch_rebel628_campaign_encouraging_all_generations_to_carry_on_the_stonewall_legacy/prweb16213186.htm|title=Pride Live to Launch REBEL628 Campaign Encouraging All Generations to Carry on the Stonewall Legacy|website=PRWeb|access-date=2019-10-26}}</ref> REBEL628 is a multi-year initiative that will empower and advance the fight for LGBTQ+ equality, with a focus on engaging today's generations.

Founding members include<ref>https://www.sagaftra.org/sag-aftra-stands-united-lgbtq-rights-rebel628-launch</ref> Amber Wittington, [[Adam Rippon]], Blossom Brown, [[David Cooley]], Elliot Carlyle, and Levi Foster.
