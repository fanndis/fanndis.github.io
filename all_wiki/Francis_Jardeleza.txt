'''Francis H. Jardeleza''' (born September 26, 1949) is a former Associate Justice of the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines]].<ref name="retired"/> Serving as the [[Office of the Solicitor General of the Philippines|Solicitor General of the Philippines]] from 2012 to 2014, he was appointed by President [[Benigno Aquino III]] to the High Court on August 19, 2014.

==Background==

Jardeleza grew up in [[Jaro, Iloilo City]], where he finished his elementary and secondary education. He earned his [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in Political Science from the [[University of the Philippines Visayas]] in 1970. He earned his law degree at the [[University of the Philippines College of Law]] at [[University of the Philippines Diliman|UP Diliman]], where he graduated as salutatorian in 1974. As a student at UP Law, Jardeleza was Vice-Chairman of the [[Philippine Law Journal]]. He was admitted to the bar in 1974, having placed third in the [[Philippine Bar Examination|bar examinations]] held that year. In 1977, Jardeleza obtained a Masters of Law degree from [[Harvard Law School]].

==Professional career==

Jardeleza was in private practice until 1996, including a long-term stint at the ACCRA Law Offices.<ref name=gma>{{cite news |url=http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/story/248691/news/aquino-leads-oath-taking-of-new-solicitor-general-100-other-appointees |publisher=GMA News Online |title=Aquino leads oath-taking of new Solicitor General, 100 other appointees |date=2012-02-10 }}</ref> In 1996, he was named Senior Vice-President and General Legal Counsel of [[San Miguel Corporation]]. He remained in San Miguel Corporation until 2010.

==Government service==

In July 2010, Jardeleza was appointed [[Ombudsman of the Philippines|Deputy Ombudsman for Luzon]] by [[President of the Philippines|President]] [[Benigno Aquino III]].<ref name=abs>{{cite news |url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/nation/07/08/11/former-smc-lawyer-new-deputy-ombudsman |title=Former SMC lawyer is new deputy Ombudsman |publisher=ABS-CBN News }}</ref> On February 6, 2012, Jardeleza's appointment as [[Solicitor General of the Philippines]] was announced, following the resignation of Jose Anselmo Cadiz.<ref name=pdisg>{{cite news |title=Francis Jardeleza is new Solicitor General |url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/140469/francis-jardeleza-is-new-solicitor-general |publisher=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]] |date=2012-02-06 }}</ref> He took his oath of office as Solicitor General on February 20, 2012.<ref name=gma/>

On 19 August 2014, President Aquino appointed Jardeleza as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines, succeeding retired Justice Roberto Abad.<ref name=pdisc>{{cite news |title=Aquino appoints SolGen Jardeleza to SC |url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/630953/aquino-appoints-solgen-jardeleza-to-sc |publisher=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]] |date=2014-08-20 }}</ref><ref name=gazette>{{cite news |title=Appointments and Designations: August 19, 2014 |url=https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2014/08/19/appointments-and-designations-august-19-2014/ |publisher=[[Official Gazette (Philippines)]] |date=2014-08-20 }}</ref>

On 26 September 2019, Jardeleza retired upon reaching his 70th birthday.<ref name="retired">https://www.manilatimes.net/2019/09/27/news/top-stories/jardeleza-retires/622478/</ref>

==In academia==

Jardeleza has been a professorial lecturer at the [[University of the Philippines College of Law]] since 1993, teaching [[constitutional law]] among other subjects.<ref name=abs/>  His wife, Concepion Lim-Jardeleza is also a professor in said law school.
