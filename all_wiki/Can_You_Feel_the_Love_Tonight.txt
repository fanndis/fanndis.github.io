"'''Can You Feel the Love Tonight'''" is a song from [[Walt Disney Animation Studios|Disney's]] 1994 animated film ''[[The Lion King]]''<ref>{{cite book |first=Alex P. |last=King |title=Hit-parade — 20 ans de tubes |publisher=Pascal |year=2004 |place=Paris |page=339 |language=fr |isbn=2-35019-009-9}}</ref> composed by [[Elton John]] with lyrics by [[Tim Rice]].<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums">{{cite book
| first= David
| last= Roberts
| year= 2006
| title= British Hit Singles & Albums
| edition= 19th
| publisher= Guinness World Records Limited
| location= London
| isbn= 1-904994-10-5
| page= 137}}</ref> It was described by [[Don Hahn]] (the film's producer), [[Roger Allers]] and [[Rob Minkoff]] (the film's directors) as having "the most diverse history" in the film.<ref name="Director's Commentary">{{cite video|title=The Lion King: Platinum Edition, Disc 1, Director's Commentary|publisher=[[Disney]]|location=Los Angeles, California|people=[[Roger Allers]] ([[Animation director|director]]), [[Rob Minkoff]] (director)|medium=DVD|date=October 7, 2003}}</ref> It was a chart hit in the UK, peaking at number 14 on the [[UK Singles Chart]],<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums"/> and achieved even more success in the US, reaching number four on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The song was a number-one hit in Canada and France. At the [[67th Academy Awards]] in March 1995 it won the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]]. The same year the song also won Elton John the [[Grammy Award for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance]].

==History==
The song, written by Tim Rice and Elton John, was performed in the film by [[Kristle Murden|Kristle Edwards]] (also known as Kristle Murden), [[Joseph Williams (musician)|Joseph Williams]], [[Sally Dworsky]], [[Nathan Lane]], and [[Ernie Sabella]], while another version used in the film's closing credits was performed by [[Elton John]]. It won the 1994 [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]],<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums"/> and the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song]]. It also earned Elton John the [[Grammy Award for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance]].

Within around one and a half months before the film was released in June 1994, John's recording was released throughout radio stations and as a commercial single and entered the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="DisneyIsLord">{{cite news |first=Dan |last=DeLuca |date=26 June 1994 |title=Disney Is Lord of the Pop Charts |newspaper=The Philadelphia Inquirer |via=ProQuest |pages=H1+ }}</ref> It became the bestselling single.{{clarify|date=October 2019}}<ref name="song encyclopedia">{{cite encyclopedia |first1=Thomas S. |last1=Hischak |first2=Mark A. |last2=Robinson |year=2009 |article=Can You Feel the Love Tonight |encyclopedia=The Disney Song Encyclopedia |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dimhg9t2TrUC&q=%22can%20you%20feel%20the%20love%20tonight%22%20lyrics%20%22elton%20john%22&pg=PA29 |page=29 |isbn=978-0-8108-6937-0 |publisher=[[Rowman & Littlefield]] |access-date=27 November 2017 }}</ref> The music video of John's recording contains montages of John performing the song and scenes from the film.<ref name="DisneyIsLord"/>

In 2003, a remixed version of the song was included in the Special Edition soundtrack of ''The Lion King'', again sung by Elton John.

In the followup ''[[The Lion King 1½]]'', the romantic scene where the song was originally featured also had the song playing, but with a difference: interspersed with the romantic scenes were short comedic shots of [[Timon and Pumbaa]] trying to disrupt [[Simba]] and [[Nala (The Lion King)|Nala]]'s night out with the "[[Peter Gunn (song)|Peter Gunn Theme]]" playing while they try.

==Early production==
The song was planned to be sung only by Timon and Pumbaa, but [[Elton John]] disliked the comical nature of the concept. John declared that "Can You Feel the Love Tonight" was meant to follow "Disney's tradition of great love songs", and that it could "express the lions' feelings for each other far better than dialogue could". The final result was the song mainly sung by an off-screen voice (Kristle Edwards) with short lines from Simba ([[Joseph Williams (musician)|Joseph Williams]]) and Nala ([[Sally Dworsky]]), and the beginning and end parts by Timon ([[Nathan Lane]]) and Pumbaa ([[Ernie Sabella]]).<ref name="Director's Commentary"/>  It also included Zulu vocals that, while mostly muted in the on-screen version, were much more prominently featured in the audio-only releases.<ref>{{cite book|title=The art of The Lion King |author=Finch, Christopher |page=143|chapter=Afterword|publisher=Hyperion|year=1994|isbn=978-0-7868-6028-9}}</ref>

==International versions==
When the movie was first released in 1994, it numbered complexively 28 versions worldwide, including a special [[Zulu language|Zulu]] version made in [[South Africa]]. This is not just the only Zulu dubbing ever made by Disney, but also the only one made in any [[Languages of Africa|African language]], other than [[Arabic language|Arabic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/zulu.html|title=Zulu|website=Charguigou|access-date=25 August 2016}}</ref> Over the years, the number of official versions has increased to 45 official versions, to which some unofficial versions have been added by local TV stations and studios in their local languages.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://disneyinternationalvoices.weebly.com/soloist-the-lion-king.html|title=TLK Soloists|website=Charguigou}}</ref>

==''Festival of the Lion King''==
In [[Walt Disney World]]'s [[Disney's Animal Kingdom|Animal Kingdom]]'s ''[[Festival of the Lion King]]'', the song is sung by Nakawa and Kibibi. As they sing, two ballet dancers (one male, one female) dressed as birds dance on the stage. After the main chorus is sung, the male bird dancer attaches his partner to a harness that allows her to fly through the air.

==Cover versions==
English [[Pop music|pop]] group [[S Club 7|S Club]], [[Sara Paxton]] and [[Elliott Yamin]] all covered the song for ''[[Disneymania]]'', ''[[Disneymania 4]]'' and ''[[Disneymania 6]]'', respectively.  [[Jackie Evancho]] covered the song for her album ''[[Songs from the Silver Screen]]''. Harajuku, a [[Eurodance]] band covered a dance version of the song in a 1995 release with singer Stephanie O'Hara, also known as Stefanie Obst, performing vocals.<ref>{{cite web|title=Harajuku|work=[[Discogs]]|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/67003-Harajuku}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|work=[[Discogs]]|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/417696-Stephanie-OHara|title=Stephanie O'Hara}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Harajuku-Can-You-Feel-The-Love-Tonight-Dance-Version/release/1065987|title=Harajuku – Can You Feel The Love Tonight (Dance Version)|work=[[Discogs]]}}</ref> [[Italian language|Italian]] trio [[Il Volo]] covered the song releasing it in the special edition of their album ''[[We Are Love]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/we-are-love-special-edition/id649482751|title=We Are Love (Special Edition) by Il Volo on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=2016-08-25}}</ref> In 2017 [[D-Metal Stars]] created a Heavy Metal cover of the song on the album "Metal Disney" featuring Mike Vescera and Rudy Sarzo. [[Beyoncé]], [[Donald Glover]], [[Seth Rogen]], and [[Billy Eichner]] performed the song as Nala, Simba, Pumbaa, and Timon, respectively, in the [[The Lion King (2019 film)|2019 remake of ''The Lion King'']], directed by [[Jon Favreau]].<ref>https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/film-tv/a26025999/beyonce-lion-king-can-you-feel-the-love-tonight/</ref> [[Pentatonix]] released an a capella rendition in conjunction with the 2019 remake.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0WjY0Hn4Eg]</ref> In April 2020 [[Christina Aguilera]] performed the song for the [[The Disney Family Singalong|ABC's]] television special, ''[[The Disney Family Singalong]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://etcanada.com/news/627908/christina-aguilera-joins-disney-singalong-to-perform-can-you-feel-the-love-tonight/|archive-url=https://archive.today/20200417102522/https://etcanada.com/news/627908/christina-aguilera-joins-disney-singalong-to-perform-can-you-feel-the-love-tonight/|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 17, 2020|title=Christina Aguilera Joins Disney Singalong To Perform 'Can You Feel The Love Tonight'|work=[[Entertainment Tonight Canada]]|access-date=April 17, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/VIDEO-Christina-Aguilera-Sings-Can-You-Feel-the-Love-Tonight-on-THE-DISNEY-FAMILY-SINGALONG-20200416|archive-url=https://archive.today/20200417101631/https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/VIDEO-Christina-Aguilera-Sings-Can-You-Feel-the-Love-Tonight-on-THE-DISNEY-FAMILY-SINGALONG-20200416|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 17, 2020|title=VIDEO: Christina Aguilera Sings 'Can You Feel the Love Tonight' on THE DISNEY FAMILY SINGALONG|work=[[BroadwayWorld]]|access-date=April 17, 2020}}</ref>
