'''Karolína Muchová''' ({{IPA-cs|ˈkaroliːna ˈmuxovaː}}; born 21 August 1996) is a [[Czech Republic|Czech]] [[tennis]] player. She first rose to prominence at the [[2018 US Open (tennis)|2018 US Open]], defeating world No. 12 and two-time [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] champion [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the second round. The following year, Muchová would reach her first Slam quarterfinal at [[2019 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] after upsetting world No. 3 and tournament favourite [[Karolína Plíšková]].

On 28 October 2019, she achieved her career-high singles ranking of world No. 21. On 24 February 2020, she peaked at No. 344 in the doubles rankings.

Her father is Czech footballer [[Josef Mucha]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Miroslav|last=Němý|url=https://sport.idnes.cz/us-open-2018-karolina-muchova-garbine-mugurazova-ohlas-2-kolo-pa4-/tenis.aspx?c=A180830_075825_tenis_mne|title= Půlnoční drama. Jak kvalifikantka Muchová složila šampionku: Je to sen|language=Czech|publisher=idnes.cz |date=30 August 2018 |accessdate=30 August 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Muchová made her [[WTA Tour]] main-draw debut at the [[2017 Korea Open]] after defeating [[Lee So-ra (tennis)|Lee So-ra]] and [[Anna Morgina]] in qualifying. She made her main-draw debut at a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event at the [[2018 US Open (tennis)|2018 US Open]] winning three qualifying matches. After winning her opening-match against [[Dayana Yastremska]], Muchová upset two-time major champion and 12th seed [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the second round to score her first top-100 victory, advancing to the third round of the tournament.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kane |first1=David |title=Marvelous Muchova revels in 'perfect' Muguruza US Open upset |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/marvelous-muchova-revels-perfect-muguruza-us-open-upset |accessdate=9 July 2019 |work=WTA Tennis |date=30 August 2018}}</ref>

Her first WTA final came at the 2019 [[WTA Prague Open|Prague Open]], where she lost to [[Jil Teichmann]]. Her performance was enough to take her for the first time into the top 100 of the [[WTA rankings]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Teichmann wins Prague Open for first WTA title |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/tennis/teichmann-wins-prague-open-for-1st-wta-title/2019/05/04/cf424cf8-6e68-11e9-bbe7-1c798fb80536_story.html?noredirect=on |accessdate=6 May 2019 |work=Washington Post |agency=Associated Press |date=4 May 2019}}</ref> In June she beat [[Anett Kontaveit]] to reach  the second round of the [[2019 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]], where she lost to [[Irina-Camelia Begu]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Video - French Open 2019: Highlights - Begu beats Muchova |url=https://video.eurosport.co.uk/tennis/french-open/2019/french-open-2019-highlights-begu-beats-muchova_vid1203566/video.shtml |accessdate=11 June 2019 |work=Eurosport |date=30 May 2019}}</ref> In July, she reached the quarter-final of [[2019 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]], beating third seed [[Karolína Plíšková]] 13–11 in the final set of their fourth round match, which lasted over three hours.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Livaudais |first1=Stephanie |title='I never played such a long match': Marathon Muchova pitch perfect in Pliskova upset at Wimbledon |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/i-never-played-such-long-match-marathon-muchova-pitch-perfect-pliskova-upset-wimbledon |accessdate=9 July 2019 |work=WTA Tennis |date=8 July 2019}}</ref> She became the first player to reach the quarter-finals at her Wimbledon debut since [[Li Na]] in 2006.<ref>{{cite web| title=Pecka, stoprocentně největší úspěch kariéry. Muchová děkovala i za prasátko | url=http://www.tenisovysvet.cz/article/detail/9771--pecka-stoprocentne-nejvetsi-uspech-kariery-muchova-dekovala-i-za-prasatko | date = 8 July 2019 | accessdate = 10 July 2019 | language=Czech}}</ref> In the quarterfinal, Muchová lost to [[Elina Svitolina]].<ref>{{cite web| title=Svitolina stops Muchova to reach first Grand Slam SF: 'It's something special' | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/svitolina-stops-muchova-reach-first-grand-slam-sf-its-something-special | work=WTA Tennis | date = 9 July 2019 | accessdate = 10 July 2019}}</ref> Her maiden WTA title came at 2019 [[Korea Open (tennis)|Korea Open]], where she defeated [[Magda Linette]] in the final.

==Playing style==
Muchová is an aggressive all-court player, possessing an intelligent game with exceptional variety. She has powerful groundstrokes from both wings, using both her forehand and backhand to hit winners from any position on the court. She has been praised for her ability to incorporate softer shots, such as drop shots, lobs, and sliced backhands, into her game, constantly breaking up the pace of baseline rallies, and being able to hit winners with these typically defensive shots. She possesses a strong serve, with her first serve peaking at 110 mph (177 km/h) and averaging 103 mph (165 km/h), allowing her to serve aces frequently; she also possesses a second serve that, despite averaging 83 mph (134 km/h), possesses a high amount of topspin, meaning that it can be deployed effectively without being attacked by aggressive players. Muchová is also proficient at defending her second serve. Her footwork, speed, and anticipation allow her to be one of the strongest return players on the WTA Tour. As a result of her exemplary fitness, she is capable of sustaining long rallies, and is an effective counterpuncher, extending rallies until she can create an opportunity to hit a winner. Due to her doubles experience, Muchová is a strong net player, and is one of the strongest volleyers on tour, frequently approaching the net to finish off points. Throughout 2019, her breakout season, she was praised for her style of play by [[Mats Wilander]], who stated that she could "achieve great things". With her on-court demeanour, fluid style, grace, and variety, she has been compared by some commentators to [[Justine Henin]] and [[Roger Federer]].

==Endorsements==
Muchová is sponsored by [[Adidas]] for her clothing, and by [[Head (company)|Head]] for her rackets.
