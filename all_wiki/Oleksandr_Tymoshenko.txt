}}'''Oleksander Hennadiyovych Tymoshenko''' ({{lang-uk|Олекса́ндр Генна́дійович Тимоше́нко}}; born 11 June 1960) is the husband of former Ukrainian prime minister, [[Yulia Tymoshenko]] and a businessman.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.unian.net/ukr/news/news-309126.html Тимошенко наголошує, що її чоловік займається бізнесом], [[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency]] (2 April 2009)</ref> Tymoshenko owns an egg farm and is the founder of two companies producing equipment for medical institutions.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.radiosvoboda.org/content/backgrounderfullpage/1891597.html Юлія Тимошенко ], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (30 November 2009)</ref> According to his wife, Tymoshenko only does business outside Ukraine "after all the events that took place in Ukraine" and owns a "construction business".<ref>[http://tabloid.pravda.com.ua/person/4d4a92dc5f4cb/ Тимошенко у домогосподарки не піде, а у Facebook – обіцяє], [[Tablo ID]] (3 February 2011)</ref>

==Biography==
Oleksandr was the son of a mid-level [[Communist Party of the Soviet Union|Communist Party]] official<ref name=OTReuters111asylum/> [[Hennady Tymoshenko]].<ref name=Rutland>

</ref> According to ''[[The Times]]'', he met [[Yulia Telegina]] when he misdialed her number. He called back and they eventually agreed to meet and quickly fell in love.<ref>[http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/life_and_style/article534324.ece The Fairest Premier of Them All?], [[The Sunday Times]] (26 June 2005)</ref>
The pair were married in 1979 in [[Dnipropetrovsk]], [[Ukraine]], and have a daughter, [[Yevhenia Tymoshenko|Yevhenia]] (Eugenia) (b. 1980).<ref name="tymoshenko.com.ua">[http://www.rferl.org/content/Tymoshenko_Profile/1291005.html Ukraine's Gold-Plaited Comeback Kid], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (23 September 2008 )</ref>

On 18 August 2000<ref name=photooty>{{in lang|uk}} [http://photo.unian.net/ukr/keywords/4718?fp=48 Ключові слова: Олександр Тимошенко], [[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency]]</ref> Oleksandr, a (along with his father Hennadiy Tymoshenko<ref name=MBUESU>{{in lang|uk}} [http://ua.korrespondent.net/world/230034 Олександр Тимошенко звинувачується в тому, що давав хабарі Лазаренку], [[Korrespondent]] (7 November 2000)</ref>) board member of the [[United Energy Systems of Ukraine]] (UESU) Corporation (of which Yulia was general director), was arrested along with the company's managing director, Valery Falkovich.<ref name=photooty/><ref name=MBUESU/><ref name=USDoS2003>[https://2001-2009.state.gov/g/drl/rls/hrrpt/2003/27871.htm 2003 Country Reports on Human Rights Practices: Ukraine], [[US Department of State]] (25 February 2004)</ref><ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://ua.korrespondent.net/ukraine/231622 Закрити вікно Олександр Тимошенко переведений у СІЗО міста Житомир], [[Korrespondent]] (23 February 2001)</ref> He was charged with embezzling $800,000 in public funds and forging customs documents to import gas from Russia – all based on activities from the 1990s.  The following March, he was transferred to a [[Zhytomyr Oblast]] jail<ref name=photooty/> so as to avoid communication between Oleksandr, Falkovich (who was transferred to a [[Chernihiv]] jail at that time) and Yulia, who by that time was also under arrest.  On 8 August 2001, Russian prosecutors handed over information to Ukrainian officials implicating Yulia and Oleksandr in customs violations.   The following day, a Kyiv district court released Oleksandr and Falkovich due to lack of evidence.<ref>[http://www.rferl.org/newsline/2000/08/3-cee/cee-220800.asp Ukrainian Energy Sector Officials Arrested on Embezzlement Charges], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (22 August 2001)</ref><ref>[http://www.rferl.org/reports/pbureport/2001/08/30-140801.asp Yuliya Tymoshenko Faces Criminal Charges from Russia], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (14 August 2001)</ref> The [[General Prosecutor of Ukraine|prosecutor's office]], however, appealed the ruling and continued the case against Oleksandr, leading him to spend two years in hiding to avoid prosecution on charges he and his wife say are trumped up.<ref>"Oleksandr Tymoshenko Put on Wanted List," [[Kyiv Post]] (20 August 2003)</ref><ref>"Prosecutors Seek Oleksandr Tymoshenko," [[Kyiv Post]] (28 August 2003)</ref> In another ruling, the Kyiv district court ruled the charges against Oleksandr "groundless."<ref>[http://www.rferl.org/reports/pbureport/2002/08/29-060802.asp Ukraine Appoints new Prosecutor-General as Kuchma Targets Opposition] by [[Taras Kuzio]], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (22 August 2002)</ref> Charges were finally dropped in May 2002. Tymoshenko's father-in-law has also been arrested in connecting with this case, he was released in 2003.<ref name=USDoS2003/> According to Mrs. Tymoshenko her spouse has never been personally involved in [[Yulia Tymoshenko Bloc]].<ref name="Sasha Tymoshenko">[http://blog.tymoshenko.ua/en/article/acnonaa4 To a real man] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110812164339/http://blog.tymoshenko.ua/en/article/acnonaa4 |date=12 August 2011 }}, Blog of Yulia Tymoshenko (6 July 2010)</ref>

In December 2010, Tymoshenko was admitted to hospital, according to [[Oleksandr Turchynov]], "in rather grave condition".<ref>[http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-411491.html Turchynov confirmed that Tymoshenko’s husband is in hospital “in rather grave condition”], [[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency]] (15 December 2010)</ref>

Tymoshenko appears very rarely with his wife in public<ref name=OTReuters111asylum/><ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://tabloid.pravda.com.ua/focus/4af93b6393fae/ Всеядна дружина Ющенка, співоча Янукович та терплячий "пташник" Тимошенко], [[Табло ID]] (10 November 2009)</ref> and never gives interviews and does not publicly comment on the work of his wife and their personal life.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://tsn.ua/ukrayina/timoshenko-ne-vzyala-svogo-cholovika-v-byut.html Тимошенко розповіла, як не взяла свого чоловіка в БЮТ], TCH (6 July 2010)</ref> Tymoshenko's [[hypocorism]] for her husband is "Sasha".<ref name="Sasha Tymoshenko"/>

Early January 2012 Tymoshenko was granted [[Right of asylum|asylum]] in the [[Czech Republic]] that he had requested at the end of the previous year.<ref name=OTReuters111asylum>[http://uk.reuters.com/article/2012/01/06/uk-ukraine-tymoshenko-czech-idUKTRE8050Q520120106?feedType=RSS&feedName=worldNews Czechs give asylum to spouse of Ukraine's Tymoshenko] [[Reuters]] (6 January 2012)</ref><ref name=BBCOTasylum111>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-16439321|title=Husband of Ukraine's Tymoshenko wins Czech asylum|date=6 January 2012|publisher=[[BBC]]|accessdate=6 January 2012}}</ref> According to him (in February 2012) "This decision was made so that we should not give the authorities additional opportunities to exert pressure on my wife".<ref name=KP1322012>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/122371/ Tymoshenko's husband not to return to Ukraine under current regime], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (13 February 2012)</ref> He also claimed he could not return to Ukraine because "the first thing after arrival I will be certainly arrested".<ref name=KP1322012/> Since 2000 Tymoshenko owned 33% share of ''International Industrial Projects, s.r.o.'', a small company in the Czech city [[Ústí nad Labem]].<ref name=BBCOTasylum111/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://obchodnirejstrik.cz/international-industrial-projects-s-r-o-26185661/|title=International Industrial Projects, s.r.o.|language=Czech|accessdate=6 January 2012}}</ref> The company did not have any web presence and no contact could be found on the internet.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://zpravy.idnes.cz/manzel-ukrajinske-expremierky-tymosenkove-zada-v-cesku-o-azyl-p7x-/domaci.aspx?c=A120106_072453_domaci_wlk|title=Husband of Ukrainian former prime minister granted asylum in the Czech Republic|language=Czech|publisher=[[MF Dnes]]|date=6 January 2012|accessdate=6 January 2012|quote="The company was registered in June 2000, now it resides in a [[panelák]] in [[Ústí nad Labem]]. The company has now website and no contact can be found using a browser."}}</ref> It didn't ever publish information mandated by the Czech law and was liquidated in February 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://zpravy.idnes.cz/manzel-tymosenkove-rusi-firmu-v-usti-nezverejnovala-co-ze-zakona-mela-1z6-/domaci.aspx?c=A120227_1739696_usti-zpravy_alh|title=Tymoshenko's husband liquidates company in Ústí. It didn't publish what it should according to the law.|language=Czech|publisher=[[MF Dnes]]|date=29 February 2012|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref> Tymoshenko also has lasting contacts with several top Czech politicians.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.euro.cz/detail.jsp?id=106426|title=Tymoshenko knows how to deal with top Czech politicians|language=Czech|publisher=Euro (online edition)|date=21 January 2012|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref>
He is alleged to own a house in [[Lidice]] since 2000<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ceskapozice.cz/en/news/society/tymoshenko%E2%80%99s-husband-bought-house-czech-rep|title=Tymoshenko's husband bought house in Czech Rep.|publisher=Ceskapozice (online edition)|date=9 January 2012|accessdate=1 February 2014}}</ref> but denied it on a post in his wife's blog<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tymoshenko.ua/en/article/yulia_tymoshenko_31_05_2013_02|title=Oleksandr Tymoshenko: my wife and I hide nothing|publisher=Yulia Tymoshenko's Blog|date=31 May 2013|accessdate=1 February 2014}}</ref> saying that his asylum in the [[Czech Republic]] was temporary. On 24 February 2014, just after the "[[2014 Ukrainian revolution|Maidan revolution]]"<ref name="Yanuousted">[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-26312008 Ukraine: Speaker Oleksandr Turchynov named interim president], [[BBC News]] (23 February 2014)<br>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-26248275 Ukraine protests timeline], [[BBC News]] (23 February 2014)</ref> and the [[Yulia Tymoshenko#2014 Release from prison|release from prison of his wife]],<ref name=Tymorealse>[https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/feb/23/yulia-tymoshenko-returns-ukraine-politics Yulia Tymoshenko is back centre stage in Ukraine – but not all want her there], ''[[The Guardian]]'' (23 February 2014)</ref> he returned to Ukraine.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/02/24/7016085 Male Tymoshenko flew to his wife and daughter in Dnipropetrovsk], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (24 February 2014)</ref>
