'''''Boston''''' is a [[wikt:Monthly|monthly]] [[magazine]] concerning life in the [[Greater Boston]] area and has been in publication since 1805.<ref name=wcat/>

==History and profile==
''Boston'' magazine was started in 1805.<ref name=wcat>{{cite web|title=Boston by Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce |url=https://www.worldcat.org/search?fq=x0:jrnl&q=n2:0006-7989|work=WorldCat|accessdate=May 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author=Paul Lewis|title=The Citizen Poets of Boston: A Collection of Forgotten Poems, 1789-1820|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=b3OBCwAAQBAJ&pg=PA223|date=April 5, 2016|publisher=University Press of New England|isbn=978-1-61168-930-3|page=223|accessdate=May 4, 2020}}</ref> [[Metrocorp|Metrocorp, Inc.]] bought the magazine in 1970.<ref>{{cite news|author=David L. Harris|title=Owner of Boston magazine dies at 88|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/boston/news/2017/12/26/owner-of-boston-magazine-dies-at-88.html|accessdate=May 4, 2020|work=Boston Business Journal|date=December 26, 2017}}</ref> The company also owns ''[[Philadelphia (magazine)|Philadelphia]]'' magazine.<ref name="bbj"/> The magazine claims a publication of 500,000 issues per month, its percentage of newsstand copies sold is among the highest of any magazine of any kind in the [[United States]], and it has been named among the best city magazines in the nation nine times in the last ten years by the [[City and Regional Magazine Association]].<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20060523224008/http://www.bostonmagazine.com/EDITORIAL/AboutBoston "About Boston magazine"]. ''Boston''. via [[Internet Archive]].</ref>

Former [[editors-in-chief]] include Carly Carioli, John Wolfson and Andrew Putz.<ref name=bbj>{{cite news|author=David L. Harris|title=Another Boston magazine editor-in-chief out after short tenure |url=https://www.bizjournals.com/boston/blog/mass_roundup/2015/12/anotherboston-magazine-editor-in-chief-out-after.html|accessdate=May 4, 2020|work=Boston Business Journal|date=December 23, 2015}}</ref>

In May 2015 ''Boston'' magazine was awarded by the City and Regional Magazine Association.<ref name=bbj/>

==Best of Boston==
"Best of Boston" is an award given by ''Boston'' magazine in an annual issue which is "the definitive guide to the city’s finest". It was first awarded in 1974.<ref>{{cite news|author=Dirk Smillie|title=Urban Strongholds Resist Magazine Slump|url=https://www.forbes.com/2009/08/27/philadelphia-boston-magazines-business-media-platt.html#1831f6c646ec|accessdate=May 4, 2020|work=Forbes|date=August 27, 2009}}</ref>

This award is given in a wide range of categories that vary from year-to-year. Recent awards include best [[clam chowder]], [[cookware]], [[day spa]], [[gym]], [[jewelry]] store, martini, [[mojito]], [[pizza]] topping, [[shiatsu]], [[teeth whitening]], [[thai food]], and many more.

Many area businesses display these awards proudly, in the form of a [[witk:certificate|certificate]] or by using the award logo on company materials, store windows, and advertising.  Along with the annual awards, ''Boston'' magazine also runs feature articles intended to quantify local resources, such as "Top Schools", "Top Restaurants", and "Top Doctors".

==Ancillary publications==
''Boston'' magazine produces several ancillary publications:
* ''Boston Home'' is a quarterly publication geared towards readers with an interest in high-end [[interior design]], home decor, and architecture.
* ''Boston Weddings'' is a biannual publication for wedding planners and brides-to-be.
