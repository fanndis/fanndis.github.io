The '''''Taipei Times''''' is the only printed daily [[English language|English-language]] newspaper in [[Taiwan]], and the third established there.<ref>{{cite news |title=Taipei Times and Taiwan Timeline |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/supplement/archives/2004/06/15/2003175210 |accessdate=15 December 2015 |work=Taipei Times |date=15 June 2004 |page=24}}</ref> Online competitors include the state-owned ''[[Focus Taiwan]]'' and ''[[Taiwan News]]''; ''[[The China Post]]'' was formerly a competitor but today{{when|date=July 2020}} is mostly non-operational. Established on 15 June 1999,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Huang |first1=Sandy |title='Taipei Times' celebrates anniversary |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/front/archives/2002/06/16/0000140553 |accessdate=15 December 2015 |work=Taipei Times |date=16 June 2002 |page=1}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Lee |first1=Chang-kuei |title=Opening a window on Taiwan |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/supplement/archives/2004/06/15/2003175209 |accessdate=15 December 2015 |work=Taipei Times |date=15 June 2004 |page=23}}</ref> the ''Taipei Times'' is published by the Liberty Times Group, which also publishes a Chinese-language newspaper, the ''[[Liberty Times]]'', Taiwan's biggest newspaper by circulation, with a pro–[[Taiwan independence movement|Taiwan independence]] editorial line.<ref>{{cite web |author=郭賽華 |year=2002 |url=http://nthur.lib.nthu.edu.tw/handle/987654321/10698|script-title=zh:語言與意識型態：台灣媒體論述分析 |trans-title=Language and ideology: Taiwanese Media editorial analysis |publisher=[[National Tsing Hua University]] Department of Foreign Languages <!--清華大學外國語文系--> |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208051719/http://nthur.lib.nthu.edu.tw/handle/987654321/10698 |archivedate=2015-12-08 |url-status=live |postscript=none}} – The data page is in Chinese and there are abstracts in both Chinese and English, but the document body is only in English</ref>

On 15 May 2017, ''The China Post'' was the ''Times''{{'}}s last English-language competitor to go out of print and the ''Taipei Times'' is consequently offered at most points of sale, hotels and libraries as the English-language option.

It is a participant in [[Project Syndicate]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Member publications |url=https://www.project-syndicate.org/papers/asia |accessdate=15 December 2015 |publisher=Project Syndicate}}</ref>
