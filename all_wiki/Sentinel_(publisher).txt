{{short description|American conservative publishing house within Penguin Group}}

'''Sentinel''' was established in 2003 as a dedicated [[Conservatism in the United States|conservative]] imprint within [[Penguin Group]] (USA). It publishes a wide variety of right-of-center books on subjects like politics, history, public policy, culture, religion and international relations.<ref>http://www.penguin.com/publishers/sentinel
</ref> Its most notable books include [[Donald Rumsfeld]]’s memoir, ''Known and Unknown'', [[Mike Huckabee]]’s ''Do the Right Thing'', '' A Simple Christmas'', and ''A Simple Government'', and ''A Patriot’s History of the United States'' by [[Larry Schweikart]].
 
Founder, President, and Publisher [[Adrian Zackheim]] joined [[Penguin Group]] in September 2001 to launch Portfolio, the company's business book imprint. He took on the additional role of founder and publisher of Sentinel in April 2003. He has a long track record of publishing books by notable conservatives, including [[Margaret Thatcher]], [[Newt Gingrich]], and [[Bob Dole]]. Before coming to [[Penguin Group]], he was the associate publisher and editor-in-chief of HarperInformation (a division of [[HarperCollins]]) and has held various editorial positions at [[William Morrow and Company|William Morrow]], [[Doubleday (publisher)|Doubleday]], and [[St. Martin's Press]].
