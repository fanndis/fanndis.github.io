'''Jon Pareles''' (born October 25, 1953)<ref name="Current Biography">{{cite journal|title=Pareles, Jon|journal=[[Current Biography]]|date=November 2008|volume=69|issue=11|page=75|url=http://pdc-connection.ebscohost.com/c/biographies/35487780/pareles-jon|accessdate=October 7, 2014|publisher=[[H. W. Wilson Company]]|issn=0011-3344}}</ref> is an American [[journalist]] who is the chief [[popular music|popular-music]] critic in the arts section of ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/learning/students/ask_reporters/pareles.html Ask a report: Jon Pareles, Music Critic.] ''[[The New York Times]]''.</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Pareles was born in [[Connecticut]].<ref name="Current Biography"/> He played jazz flute and piano, and graduated from [[Yale University]] with a degree in music. He began working as a music critic in 1977.<ref name="bill">{{cite journal|last1=Hay|first1=Carla|title=Meet the Critics|journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 20, 2004|volume=116|issue=12|pages=1, 88|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ARAEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA88|accessdate=October 6, 2014|publisher=[[Nielsen Business Media, Inc.]]|issn=0006-2510}}</ref>

== Career ==
In the 1970s, he was an associate editor of ''[[Crawdaddy!]]'', where he published his first works (outside school publications);<ref name=rockcritics.com>{{cite web|last1=Ward|first1=Steven|title=The Grey Lady's Pop Music Man: Jon Pareles in Conversation|url=http://rockcritics.com/2013/05/22/from-the-archives-jon-pareles-2001/|website=rockcritics.com|accessdate=August 8, 2014|date=July 2001}}</ref> and in the 1980s, an associate editor at ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' and the music editor at ''[[The Village Voice]]''. He started contributing to ''The Times'' in 1982.<ref name="bill"/> He reviews popular music in the arts section of ''The Times''.<ref name=rockcritics.com/>
