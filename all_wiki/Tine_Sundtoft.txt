'''Kristine "Tine" Sundtoft''' (born 19 April 1967) is a [[Norway|Norwegian]] civil servant and politician for the [[Conservative Party (Norway)|Conservative party]]. She was [[Minister of Climate and the Environment]] in [[Solberg's Cabinet]] from October 2013 to December 2015.<ref>[https://www.regjeringen.no/en/historical-archive/solbergs-government/andre-dokumenter/kld/2013/minister-of-climate-and-the-environment-/id742913/ Former Minister of Climate and Environment Tine Sundtoft (Conservative Party)]</ref>

== Biography ==
She was born in [[Lillesand]] as a daughter of county mayor [[Kristian Sundtoft]] and traffic teacher Barbro Rønnevig Sundtoft. After finishing secondary school at [[Møglestu Upper Secondary School|Møglestu]] in 1985 and attending [[University of Agder|Agder District University College]] for one year, Sundtoft graduated with a degree in business administration from the [[BI Norwegian Business School]] in 1990.<ref name=bio>{{stortingetbio|TISU}}</ref>

She was regional director of the [[Confederation of Norwegian Enterprise]] in [[Agder]] 1995–2005 and was the first woman to serve as chief administrative officer in [[Vest-Agder County Municipality]] from 2005<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vaf.no/hoved.aspx?m=372&amid=4756|title=Tine Sundtoft - Vest-Agders første kvinnelige fylkesrådmann|publisher=Vest-Agder County Municipality|author=Torkelsen, Jan H.|date=27 October 2005|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070709032026/http://www.vaf.no/hoved.aspx?m=372&amid=4756|archivedate=9 July 2007}}</ref> until she became minister.

She was [[general secretary]] of [[Norwegian Young Conservatives]] from 1990–1992, political advisor for the [[Conservative Party (Norway)|Conservative Party]] in Parliament, and served as a deputy representative to the [[Parliament of Norway]] from [[Aust-Agder]] from 1989 to 1993. In total she met during 2 days of parliamentary session.<ref name=bio/>
