The '''Berggruen Prize''' is a US$1-million award given each year to a significant individual in the field of [[philosophy]]. It is awarded by the [[Berggruen Institute]] to "thinkers whose ideas have helped us find direction, wisdom, and improved self-understanding in a world being rapidly transformed by profound social, technological, political, cultural, and economic change."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://berggruen.org/prize|title=The Berggruen Prize|website=The Berggruen Institute|accessdate=12 April 2017}}</ref>

The Berggruen Prize was first awarded in 2016 with the overt purpose of becoming a "[[Nobel Prize|Nobel prize]] for philosophy".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/miguelforbes/2015/11/23/berggruen-institute-launches-1m-nobel-prize-for-philosophy/#18bf9f522deb|title=Berggruen Institute Launches $1M Nobel Prize for Philosophy|last=Forbes|first=Miguel|work=Forbes|access-date=2018-10-31|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://dailynous.com/2015/10/08/a-nobel-prize-for-philosophy/|title=A Nobel Prize for Philosophy? (updated) - Daily Nous|website=dailynous.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-10-31}}</ref>

==Overview==
The first recipient of the Berggruen Prize was the Canadian philosopher [[Charles Taylor (philosopher)|Charles Taylor]], whose work "urges us to see humans as constituted not only by their biology or their personal intentions, but also by their existence within language and webs of meaningful relationships."<ref name="huffingtonpost">{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/berggruen-million-dollar-philosophy-prize_us_5602d46ae4b08820d91b1b88|title=Think Tank Creates $1 Million Philosophy Prize Because 'Ideas Matter' |date= September 24, 2015 |publisher=huffingtonpost.com|accessdate=2016-05-13}}</ref><ref name="typepad">{{cite web|url=http://leiterreports.typepad.com/blog/2015/09/new-berggruen-institute-for-philosophy-and-culture-plus-an-annual-1-million-prize.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923004224/http://leiterreports.typepad.com/blog/2015/09/new-berggruen-institute-for-philosophy-and-culture-plus-an-annual-1-million-prize.html|archive-date=2015-09-23|url-status=dead|title=Leiter Reports: A Philosophy Blog: New Berggruen Institute for Philosophy and Culture, plus an annual $1 million prize|accessdate=2016-05-13|df=}}</ref><ref name="artnet">{{cite web|url=https://news.artnet.com/people/nicolas-berggruen-philosophy-prize-333452|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151009064208/https://news.artnet.com/people/nicolas-berggruen-philosophy-prize-333452 |archive-date=2015-10-09 |url-status=dead|title=Nicolas Berggruen&#039;s $1 Million Philosophy Prize - artnet News|accessdate=2016-05-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/10/05/books/canadian-philosopher-wins-1-million-prize.html|title=Canadian Philosopher Wins $1 Million Prize|last=Schuessler|first=Jennifer|date=October 4, 2016|newspaper=The New York Times|issn=0362-4331|access-date=October 4, 2016}}</ref><ref>Rothman, Joshua. [http://www.newyorker.com/culture/persons-of-interest/how-to-restore-your-faith-in-democracy "How to Restore Your Faith in Democracy"], ''[[The New Yorker]],'' November 11, 2016.</ref>

The prize is awarded annually in December, with a ceremony at the [[New York Public Library Main Branch|New York Public Library]]. In 2016, ceremony speakers included [[University of Pennsylvania]] president [[Amy Gutmann]] and journalist [[Fareed Zakaria]].<ref>Gordon, Amanda L. [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-12-02/billionaire-s-supper-club-directs-philosopher-s-arrow-at-trump "Billionaire’s Supper Club Directs Philosopher’s Arrow at Trump"], [[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]], December 2, 2016.</ref>

==Winners==
* 2016: [[Charles Taylor (philosopher)|Charles Taylor]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/montreal/charles-taylor-mcgill-bergguren-prize-1.3792438|title=McGill philosopher Charles Taylor wins $1M Berggruen Prize {{!}} CBC News|work=CBC|access-date=2018-10-31|language=en-US}}</ref> – Canadian philosopher and [[Professor emeritus|Professor Emeritus]] of Philosophy at [[McGill University]]
* 2017: [[Onora O'Neill]]<ref name="oneill">{{cite newspaper |title=Onora O’Neill Wins $1 Million Berggruen Prize for Philosophy |author=Jennifer Schuessler |date=3 October 2017 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/10/03/arts/onora-oneill-berggruen-prize.html |newspaper=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref> – English philosopher and member of the [[House of Lords]]
* 2018: [[Martha Nussbaum]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/30/arts/martha-nussbaum-berggruen-prize.html|title=Martha Nussbaum Wins $1 Million Berggruen Prize|access-date=2018-10-30|language=en}}</ref> – American philosopher and [[Ernst Freund]] Distinguished Service Professor of Law and Ethics at the [[University of Chicago]]
* 2019: [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/10/23/arts/ruth-bader-ginsburg-berggruen-prize.html|title=Ruth Bader Ginsburg Wins $1 Million Berggruen Prize|access-date=2019-10-23|language=en}}</ref> – American lawyer and jurist who was an Associate Justice of the [[U.S. Supreme Court]]
