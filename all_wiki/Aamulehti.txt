'''''Aamulehti''''' ([[Finnish language|Finnish]] for "morning newspaper") is a [[Finnish language]] [[daily newspaper]] published in [[Tampere]], [[Finland]].

==History and profile==
''Aamulehti'' was founded in 1881<ref name=ain>{{cite journal|author=Antti Ainamo|title=Between West and East: A Social History of Business Journalism in Cold War Finland|journal=Human Relations|date=May 2006|volume=59|issue=5|url=https://helda.helsinki.fi/bitstream/handle/10227/397/HR%2cAinamo%2cTienari%26Vaara%2c2006.pdf?sequence=3|doi=10.1177/0018726706066550|pages=611–636|hdl=10227/397}}</ref><ref name="History">{{cite web|title=History|url=http://www.almamedia.com/about-us/history/|work=Alma Media|accessdate=4 March 2015|archive-url=https://archive.today/20150309001417/http://www.almamedia.com/about-us/history/|archive-date=9 March 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> to "improve the position of the Finnish people and the Finnish language" during [[Russia]]'s rule over Finland.<ref>{{cite book|title=Willing's Press Guide|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RtA3AQAAIAAJ|year=2002|publisher=T. Skinner Directories|page=283}}</ref> The founders were [[nationalistic]] [[Finns]] in Tampere.<ref name=ain/><ref>{{cite book|editor=Georgios Terzis|title=European Media Governance: National and Regional Dimensions|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=68nbtqst-CsC&pg=PA99|year=2007|publisher=Intellect Books|isbn=978-1-84150-192-5|page=99}}</ref>

In the 1980s, Aamulehti Corporation acquired the paper ''[[Uusi Suomi]]'', which they shut down in 1991.<ref name=ain/> ''Aamulehti'' was published in [[Broadsheet|broadsheet format]]<ref name=kaa3/> until April 2014 when the paper switched to [[tabloid format]].<ref name="History"/><ref>{{cite web|title=From Broadsheet to Tabloid|url=http://www.hssaatio.fi/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/tabtiivis-eng.pdf|work=University of Tampere|accessdate=19 December 2014|date=January 2014}}</ref> [[Matti Apunen]] was the [[editor-in-chief]] of the paper until 2010.<ref>{{cite journal|author=Heikki Hellman|title=From aesthetes to reporters: The paradigm shift in arts journalism in Finland|journal=Journalism|date=2011|volume=13|issue=6|doi=10.1177/1464884911431382|pages=783–801|url=https://trepo.tuni.fi/handle/10024/100493}}</ref> The paper is based in Tampere and serves the [[Pirkanmaa|Pirkanmaa region]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Laura Ruusunoksa|title=Public Journalism and Public Sphere(s)|url=http://www15.uta.fi/arkisto/tacs/papers0506/ruusunoksa.pdf|work=University of Tampere|accessdate=10 February 2015|format=Conference paper|date=3 May 2006}}</ref>

Until 1992 the paper aligned itself with the [[National Coalition Party (Finland)|National Coalition Party]],<ref name=Cook2001/><ref>{{cite web|author=Raimo Salokangas |title=From Political to National, Regional and Local |url=http://web.iaincirebon.ac.id/ebook/moon/LocalMatters/31_salokangas.pdf |work=Cirebon |accessdate=14 December 2014 |format=Book chapter |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141214172635/http://web.iaincirebon.ac.id/ebook/moon/LocalMatters/31_salokangas.pdf |archivedate=14 December 2014 }}</ref> but it no longer has an official connection to any political party.

Aamulehti Corporation was the owner of ''Aamulehti''<ref>{{cite news|title=Recent Mergers And Acquisitions in Finland - Aamulehti And MTV Merge To Form Media Giant|url=http://www.mondaq.com/x/5855/Project+Finance+PPP+PFI/Recent+Mergers+And+Acquisitions+In+Finland+Aamulehti+And+MTV+Merge+To+Form+Media+Giant|accessdate=20 January 2015|work=Mondaq|date=8 May 1997}}</ref> until 2003 when the paper was acquired by [[Alma Media]], a large media corporation in Finland, for a reported 460 million euros.<ref name="WurffLauf2005">{{cite book|last1=Wurff|first1=R. van der|last2=Lauf|first2=Edmund|title=Print And Online Newspapers in Europe: A Comparative Analysis in 16 Countries|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yoSbUIqgPSMC&pg=PA124|year=2005|publisher=Het Spinhuis|isbn=978-90-5589-238-9|page=124}}</ref> The sister papers of the daily are ''[[Iltalehti]]'' and ''[[Kauppalehti]]''.<ref name=ain/><ref name=kat>{{cite web|author=Katja Lehtisaari |display-authors=etal |title=Media Convergence and Business Models: Responses of Finnish Daily Newspapers|url=http://www.helsinki.fi/crc/Julkaisut/Media_Convergence.pdf|work=University of Helsinki|accessdate=11 November 2014|format=Research Report|date=2012}}</ref>

Since 2006 ''Aamulehti'' has published four weekly supplements — ''Moro'' (meaning "Hi", in the dialect of the Tampere region, and devoted to the culture of Tampere), on Thursdays; the entertainment-centred ''Valo'' ("Light"), published on Fridays; ''Asiat'' ("Matters"), on Sundays; and ''Ihmiset'' ("People"), also on Sundays. The paper covers [[innovation journalism|journalism innovation]] at least once a month.<ref>{{cite journal|author=Antti Ainamo|title=Innovation Journalism for Bridging the Gap Between Technology and Commercialization|journal=Innovation Journalism|date=29 May 2006|volume=3|issue=4|citeseerx = 10.1.1.124.6596 }}</ref>

==Circulation==
Peaking in 2008, ''Aamulehti'' grew steadily, regularly reporting the third-highest newspaper circulation numbers in Finland.<ref>{{cite web|title=Media pluralism in the Member States of the European Union|url=http://ec.europa.eu/information_society/media_taskforce/doc/pluralism/media_pluralism_swp_en.pdf|work=Commission of the European Communities|accessdate=27 March 2015|location=Brussels|date=16 January 2007}}</ref> The circulation was 135,194 copies in 1993,<ref name="Cook2001">{{cite book|author=Bernard A. Cook|title=Europe Since 1945: An Encyclopedia|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ox_gXq2jpdYC&pg=PA384|year=2001|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-0-8153-4057-7|page=384}}</ref> reaching 135,478 copies in 2001.<ref>{{cite book|editor1=Mary Kelly|editor2=Gianpietro Mazzoleni|editor3=Denis McQuail|title=The Media in Europe: The Euromedia Handbook|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0moFhDLjTiwC&pg=PA62|year=2004|publisher=SAGE Publications|isbn=978-0-7619-4132-3|page=62}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=World Press Trends|url=http://www.wan-press.org/ecrire/upload/wpt2004.pdf|work=World Association of Newspapers|accessdate=8 February 2015|location=Paris|date=2004}}</ref> By 2004 the paper had an average daily circulation of 136,028 copies<ref name=olli>{{cite web|author1=Olli Nurmi|title=Colour quality control – The Finnish example|url=http://virtual.vtt.fi/virtual/proj2/managing_quality/printcert_fin.pdf|work=VTT|accessdate=19 December 2014|date=11 October 2004}}</ref> per day and 140,802 copies on Sundays, with an estimated readership of 329,000.<ref name=olli/> ''Aamulehti'''s circulation was 136,743 copies in 2005;<ref name=kaa3>{{cite journal|author=Kaarina Nikunen|title=Losing my profession: Age, experience and expertise in the changing newsrooms|journal=Journalism|date=2013|url=http://www.uta.fi/cmt/en/research/comet/projects/completed/lamaeng/Losing%20my%20profession.full.pdf|accessdate=10 February 2015|publisher=Sage Publications|doi=10.1177/1464884913508610|volume=15|issue=7|pages=868–888}}</ref><ref name=osf>{{cite web|author1=Sampsa Saikkonen|author2=Paula Häkämies|title=Mapping Digital Media:Finland|url=http://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/sites/default/files/mapping-digital-media-finland-20140214.pdf|work=Open Society Foundations|accessdate=23 April 2015|format=Report|date=5 January 2014}}</ref> 138,258 copies (2006);<ref>{{cite web|title=Top ten daily newspapers by circulation 2006|url=http://www.nordicom.gu.se/sites/default/files/medieforskning-statistik/1127_1060_topten_dailies_2006.xls|work=Nordicom|accessdate=8 March 2015}}</ref> 139,165 copies (2007)<ref name=kaa3/><ref>{{cite web|title=The Nordic Media Market|url=http://www.nordicom.gu.se/sites/default/files/publikationer-hela-pdf/nmt09_001-194.pdf|work=Nordicom|accessdate=7 March 2015|date=2009}}</ref> — reaching a high-water mark of 139,130 copies in 2008, then declining to 135,293 copies in 2009;<ref name=ifabc>{{cite web|title=National newspapers total circulation|url=http://www.ifabc.org/site/assets/media/National-Newspapers_total-circulation_IFABC_09-07-12.xls|work=International Federation of Audit Bureaux of Circulations|accessdate=5 December 2014}}</ref> 131,539 copies (2010); 130,081 copies (2011);<ref name=ifabc/> and 114,231 copies in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Circulation Statistics 2013|url=http://mediaauditfinland.fi/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Circulations2013.pdf|work=Media Audit Finland|accessdate=12 February 2015|date=23 June 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170329225311/http://mediaauditfinland.fi/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Circulations2013.pdf|archive-date=29 March 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

By 2014 ''Aamulehti'' had Finland's second-largest circulation and had the fifth-largest estimated readership.<ref>{{cite journal|author=Sanna Koskinen |display-authors=etal |title=Media portrayal of older people as illustrated in Finnish newspapers|journal=International Journal of Qualitative Studies on Health and Well-Being|date=2014|volume=9|pages=25304 |url=http://www.ijqhw.net/index.php/qhw/article/view/25304/35902|accessdate=13 December 2014|doi=10.3402/qhw.v9.25304|pmid=25261872 |pmc=4176674}}</ref> In 2010, with 262,947 weekly visitors, the online version of ''Aamulehti'' was the twenty-third most visited website in Finland.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Kari Karppinen|author2=Hannu Nieminen|author3=Anna-Laura Markkanen|title=High Professional Ethos in a Small, Concentrated Media Market|url=http://blogs.helsinki.fi/kekarppi/files/2014/01/05FINLANDkorr1.pdf|work=Blogipalvelut|accessdate=29 December 2014|date=2014}}</ref>
