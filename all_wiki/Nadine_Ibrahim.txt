'''Nadine Ibrahim''' (born {{Birth based on age as of date|25|2019|8|16|noage=1}}) is a Nigerian film director.

==Biography==
Ibrahim was born in [[Kaduna]] and was raised as a Muslim. Her mother, [[Amina J. Mohammed]] is the former Nigerian minister of the environment.<ref name=okoro/> At a young age, she developed an interest in storytelling and filmmaking rather than traditional academic subjects.<ref name=samanga/> She moved from Nigeria to Great Britain at the age of 14<ref>{{cite web |title=Lessons on Breaking into the movie industry- Nadine Ibrahim Films to the world. |url=https://mrsceonaija.com/2018/02/25/lessons-on-breaking-into-the-movie-industry-nadine-ibrahim-films-to-the-world/ |website=MRSCEONAIJA |accessdate=11 October 2020 |date=25 February 2018}}</ref> and studied media and film production at the [[University of Gloucestershire]]. Among other things, she worked on projects for the [[United Nations]] and Fictionless films.<ref name=okoro/> Ibrahim was the associate producer for ''Hakkunde'', about a Southern Nigerian who encounters Northern culture for the first time.<ref name=future>{{cite web |url=https://awards.thefutureafrica.com/the-future-awards-africa-prize-for-screen-producer/ |title=The Future Awards Africa Prize for Screen Producer |work=The Future Awards Africa |date=2 December 2018 |accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref> 

Her short documentary film ''[[Through Her Eyes (film)|Through Her Eyes]]'' was released in 2017. It tells the story of a Azeeza, a 12-year-old girl who was kidnapped and became a suicide bomber. The film was a semi-finalist at the [[Los Angeles Cinema Festival]] and was nominated in the short film category at the [[Africa International Film Festival]].<ref name=okoro>{{cite news |first=Enuma |last=Okoro |url=https://guardian.ng/guardian-woman/nadine-ibrahim-i-want-to-tell-stories-that-can-change-the-world/ |title=Nadine Ibrahim: I want to tell stories that can change the world |work=Guardian Woman |publisher=Guardian Newspapers |date=4 March 2017 |accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref> For the media group EbonyLife films and television she used film to tell stories of the ethnic groups of northern Nigeria, such as the [[Hausa people|Hausa]], [[Fulani]], [[Igbira]], [[Igala people|Igala]], [[Tiv people|Tiv]] and [[Gbagyi]].<ref name=future/> She is the CEO of a Nigerian-based multimedia company, Nailamedia, founded in 2017.<ref name=inmesit/> Her documentary short film ''Marked'', which she worked on for two years, made its debut at the Aké Festival in [[Lagos]] in 2019. It deals with traditional [[scarification]], which is widespread in Nigeria but also a taboo subject, and was inspired by a mark on an aunt's face.<ref>{{cite news |first=Dominic|last=Cadogan|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/body/article/46557/1/scarification-nadine-ibrahim-marked-documentary-film-nigeria-lagos-taboo |title=Immerse yourself in the taboo art of scarification via short film Marked |work=Dazed|date=24 October 2019 |accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref>

Ibrahim listed [[Tyler Perry]], [[Alfred Hitchcock]], [[Spike Lee]] and [[Ang Lee]] as her role models.<ref name=inmesit>{{cite news|first=Inemesit|last=Udodiong |url=https://www.pulse.ng/bi/lifestyle/nadine-ibrahim-nigerian-filmmaker-talks-about-the-essence-of-being-different/nzp71n0 |title=Nigerian filmmaker Nadine Ibrahim talks about the essence of being different |work=Pulse|date=16 August 2019 |accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref> Another important influence she mentions is her mother, through whose work in the government she learned of poverty and corruption in Nigeria.<ref name=samanga>{{cite news|first=Rumaro|last=Samanga |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/nadine-ibrahim-film-nigeria-nollywood/ |title=In Conversation with Nigerian Filmmaker Nadine Ibrahim: 'The local stories matter the most.' |work=OkayAfrica|date=22 October 2019 |accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref> She has been named one of the top young Nigerian filmmakers to watch.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://shadowandact.com/three-young-nigerian-filmmakers-to-watch |title=Three Young Nigerian Filmmakers to Watch |work=Shadow and Act|date=8 May 2017|accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref> Ibrahim married Nasir in [[Abuja]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nadine Ibrahim: The Beautiful Daughter Of Amina J. Muhammad |url=https://ng.opera.news/ng/en/entertainment/ca5431dadd89220cf93b4b4dac8b7074 |website=Opera News |accessdate=11 October 2020}}</ref>
