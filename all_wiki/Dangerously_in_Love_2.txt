"'''Dangerously in Love 2'''" is a song written and produced by [[Beyoncé Knowles]] and Errol McCalla, Jr. The [[ballad]] was first recorded by [[Destiny's Child]] for their third studio album ''[[Survivor (Destiny's Child album)|Survivor]]'' (2001), under the title "'''Dangerously in Love'''". The song later became the title track to Beyoncé's debut album with some minor adjustments instrumentally. "Dangerously in Love 2" is an [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[Soul music|soul]] [[Ballad (music)|ballad]], the lyrics of which detail romantic obsession.

"Dangerously in Love 2" received generally positive response from music critics, who wrote that the song effectively shows the vocal capabilities of Beyoncé. It won a [[Grammy Award for Best Female R&B Vocal Performance]] at the [[46th Annual Grammy Awards]]. Though not released as a single, "Dangerously in Love 2" charted at number 57 the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart and at number 17 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart. Beyoncé performed the ballad at the 46th Grammy Awards, the [[Verizon Ladies First Tour]], the [[Dangerously in Love Tour]], and [[The Beyoncé Experience]].

==Background and composition==
"Dangerously in Love" was written and produced by [[Beyoncé]] and Errol McCalla, Jr. It was recorded for the [[Destiny's Child]] album, ''[[Survivor (Destiny's Child album)|Survivor]]'' (2001).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/survivor/id169717715 |title=Survivor by Destiny's Child on iTunes |publisher=[[iTunes Store|iTunes US]]. [[Apple Inc.]] |accessdate=February 20, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110527083429/http://itunes.apple.com/us/album/survivor/id169717715 |archivedate=May 27, 2011 }}</ref> Beyoncé re-recorded the song at the [[SugarHill Recording Studios|SugarHill Studios]] in [[Houston]], [[Texas]] in 2002<ref name="Dangerously in Love"/> for her debut solo album, ''[[Dangerously in Love]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dangerously-in-love/id201274359 |title=Dangerously In Love by Beyoncé on iTunes |publisher=[[iTunes Store|iTunes US]]. [[Apple Inc.]] |accessdate=February 20, 2011}}</ref> Her version, which was titled "Dangerously in Love 2",<ref name="latimes2003"/> features a modified [[arrangement]].<ref name="jet"/> It was set as a remix track, but later became the title track to her debut studio album.<ref name="jet">{{cite book |title=House of hits: the story of Houston's Gold Star/SugarHill Recording Studios |last1=Bradley |first1=Andy |last2=Wood |first2=Roger |author-link= |year=2010 |publisher= |location= |isbn=978-0-292-71919-4 |page=239 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XsvWyg1u0UwC&q=dangerously+in+love+2&pg=PA239 |accessdate=February 20, 2011}}</ref>

==Reception==
"Dangerously in Love" received mostly positive reviews from music critics. Natalie Nichols of ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' stated that the song is just one of many wailing ballads designed to demonstrate "the soulful bleating of Beyonce, the centerpiece of R&B star trio [[Destiny's Child]]."<ref name="latimes2003"/> Sal Cinquemani of [[Slant Magazine]] gave a mixed review of the song, stating that it seemed a bit dated and that it was "a near-carbon copy of Survivor’s melodramatic 'Dangerously in Love' [that] is completely superfluous."<ref name="slantmagazine1"/> While covering the 2004 reunion of Destiny's Child, ''[[Jet (magazine)|Jet Magazine]]'' considered the song a major hit of Beyoncé's debut album.<ref name=people>{{cite magazine |year=2004 |title=Destiny's Child Reunites For New Album After Solo Success |magazine=[[Jet (magazine)|Jet]]|volume=106 |issue=December 6, 2004 |page=60|publisher=Johnson Publishing Company |issn =0021-5996  |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Hb8DAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA60|accessdate=February 20, 2011}}</ref> In an article from ''[[Teen Ink]]'', the song was noted as one of the top "lush ballads" of the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.teenink.com/reviews/music_reviews/article/6670/Beyonce-Knowles--Dangerously-in-Love/ |title=Beyonce Knowles – Dangerously in Love &#124; Teen Music Review |publisher=[[Teen Ink]] |author=BlissfullyMe |year=2010 |accessdate=February 20, 2011}}</ref> "Dangerously in Love" won in the category [[Grammy Award for Best Female R&B Vocal Performance|Best Female R&B Vocal Performance]] at the [[46th Grammy Awards]] on February 8, 2004.<ref name="gram">{{cite news|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,627723,00.html|title=Much Grammy 'Love' for Beyoncé, OutKast|last=Silverman|first=Stephen|date=February 8, 2004|work=[[People (magazine)|People]] |accessdate=September 8, 2011}}</ref>

Beyoncé's version charted on a few [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' component charts]], despite not officially being released as a single. "Dangerously in Love" made its first chart appearance on the US [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart at number 73 on July 3, 2004.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2004-07-03/r-b-hip-hop-songs?order=gainer |title=R&B/Hip-Hop Songs – Week of July 3, 2004 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]  |date=August 21, 2004 |accessdate=February 19, 2011}}</ref> For the following seven weeks, the song kept on charting in the lower positions of the chart, until it was titled as the greatest gainer on the chart issue dated August 21, 2004, ascending 28 places from number 71 to number 43.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2004-08-21/r-b-hip-hop-songs?order=gainer |title=R&B/Hip-Hop Songs – Week of August 21, 2004 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]  |date=August 21, 2004 |accessdate=February 19, 2011}}</ref> Its entry on the US [[Hot 100 Airplay (Radio Songs)|Radio Songs]] chart at number 75,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2004-09-18/radio-songs?order=gainer |title=Radio Songs – Week of September 18, 2004 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]  |date=September 18, 2004 |accessdate=February 18, 2011}}</ref> prompted its debut on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] issue dated September 18, 2004 at number 76.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2004-09-18/hot-100?order=gainer |title=Hot 100 – Week of September 18, 2004 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]  |date=September 18, 2004 |accessdate=February 18, 2011}}</ref> "Dangerously in Love 2" was propelled to number 60 on the Hot 100 chart the following week as it made the largest chart move on the Radio Songs chart by ascending to number 59.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2004-09-25/radio-songs?order=gainer |title=Radio Songs – Week of September 25, 2004 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] October 2, 2004.<ref>{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=beyoncé|chart=all}} |title=Dangerously in Love - Beyoncé |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]  |accessdate=February 18, 2011}}</ref> On June 14, 2006, the song was certified gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (2006) for sales of over 500,000 ringtones.<ref name="riaa">{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?table=SEARCH_RESULTS&title=Dangerously%20In%20Love&artist=Beyonce&format=SINGLE&perPage=25 |title=RIAA – Gold & Platinum : Dangerously in Love ==Live performances==
[[File:Beyonce Concert Barcelona 3.jpg|upright|thumb|Beyoncé performing "Dangerously in Love" during [[The Beyoncé Experience]].]]
Beyoncé performed "Dangerously in Love" live for the first time at the Pepsi Arena in Albany, New York in 2001.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1445292/destinys-child-open-trl-tour.jhtml |title=Destiny's Child, Eve, Nelly Get The Kids Riled On 'TRL' Tour Opener |publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[MTV Networks]] |date= July 19, 2001 |author=Shaheem Reid |accessdate=April 9, 2011}}</ref> The performance was also part of the setlist for their [[Destiny's Child World Tour|World Tour]]. Beyoncé also famously performed the song at the [[46th Grammy Awards|2004 Grammy Awards]] on February 8, 2004,<ref name="mtvb2"/> where it won in the category of [[Grammy Award for Best Female R&B Vocal Performance|Best Female R&B Vocal Performance]].<ref name="mtvb3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1484884/beyonce-wins-most-50-shut-out-at-grammys.jhtml |title=Beyonce Wins Most, Outkast Shine, 50 Cent Shut Out At Grammys |publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[MTV Networks]] |date= February 9, 2004 |author=Joseph Patel |accessdate=February 19, 2011}}</ref> Beyoncé was wearing a long, glittery turquoise and silver dress. She positioned herself between the edges of a giant picture frame and sung "Dangerously in Love 2" as if she were in a living oil painting.<ref name="mtvb2"/> She was joined by seventeen backup singers, musicians and dancers.<ref name="mtvb2"/> At the end, she held out her hand and a dove landed in her outstretched palm.<ref name="mtvb2"/> Jon Wiederhorn of [[MTV News]] commented that the performance was "nothing short of extravagant."<ref name="mtvb2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1484889/beyonce-outkast-rock-grammy-stage.jhtml?headlines=true |title=Beyonce Sings Twice, Christina Plays Nice, Outkast Cool As Ice On Grammy Stage |publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[MTV Networks]] |date= February 9, 2004 |author=Jon Wiederhorn |accessdate=February 19, 2011}}</ref>

Beyoncé also added the song to the track-listing of her first solo-tour ''[[Dangerously in Love Tour]]'', notably at [[Wembley Arena]] in [[London]], [[United Kingdom]]. There she performed a special rendition of the song that came in at well over 8-minutes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sonymusic.co.jp/Music/International/Arch/SR/Beyonce/SICP-605/index.html |script-title=ja:ビヨンセ : ライヴ・アット・ウェンブリー（初回仕様限定盤） |language=Japanese |publisher=Sony Music Online Japan |date=July 22, 2004 |accessdate=January 30, 2011}}</ref> During the [[Verizon Ladies First Tour]] which also featured [[Alicia Keys]], [[Missy Elliott]] and [[Tamia]], Beyoncé performed "Dangerously in Love 2" in [[New York City]] in front of more than 20,000 fans.<ref name="mtvb1"/> Before starting her performance, she asked the audience whether they have ever fell in love. As she started to sing, more dancers joined in on smaller and circular platforms. Simultaneously, fire shot from the floor and a long, rectangular screen merged video of flames with images of the performers, seemingly setting them on fire.<ref name="mtvb1"/> Shaheem Reid of [[MTV News]] complimented the performance writing: "[Beyoncé] had New Yorkers cheering like the Yankees had just won the [[Commissioner's Trophy (MLB)|World Series]] when she perfectly hit the high note toward the end of 'Dangerously in Love 2'."<ref name="mtvb1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1486349/beyonce-steals-show-at-ladies-first.jhtml |title=First Among Equals: Beyonce Steals The Show At Ladies First |publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[MTV Networks]] |date=April 13, 2004 |author=Shaheem Reid |accessdate=February 19, 2011}}</ref>

On August 5, 2007, Beyoncé performed the song at the [[Madison Square Garden]] in [[Manhattan]].<ref name="mui">{{Cite news |author=Shaheem Reid |title=Beyonce Puts On Flawless — And Fall-Less — NYC Show With Robin Thicke |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1566431/beyonce-puts-on-flawless-8212-fallless-8212-nyc-show.jhtml |publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[MTV Networks]] |date=August 7, 2007 |accessdate=January 31, 2011}}</ref><ref name="jiv">{{Cite web |author=Jon Pareles |title=Romance as a Struggle That She Will Win |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/08/06/arts/music/06beyo.html?ex=1344052800&en=c1ef16024c49370c&ei=5088 |work=[[The New York Times]]  |date=August 6, 2007 |accessdate=February 12, 2011}}</ref> While singing, Beyoncé was accompanied by her all-female band,<ref name="mui"/> and she incorporated [[Jill Scott]]'s "He Loves Me" in "Dangerously in Love 2". Shaheem Reid of [[MTV News]] praised the performance, writing: "For all the dancing she did, Beyoncé got an equally big — if not more resounding — response for displaying her undeniable vocal ability on the ballads [...] 'Dangerously in Love'."<ref name="mui"/>
