'''Xiye Bastida''' (born 18 April 2002) is a Mexican-Chilean climate activist and member of the indigenous Mexican [[Otomi]]-[[Toltec]] nation. She is one of the major organizers of [[Fridays for Future]] New York City and has been a leading voice for indigenous and immigrant visibility in climate activism.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.vox.com/identities/2019/10/11/20904791/young-climate-activists-of-color |title=Meet the young activists of color who are leading the charge against climate disaster|journal=Vox|first=Nylah|last=Burton|date=11 October 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> She is on the administration committee of the [[People's Climate Movement]] and a member of [[Sunrise Movement]] and [[Extinction Rebellion]].She is co-founder of [https://reearthin.org Re-Earth Initiative], an international non for profit organization that is inclusive and intersectional “just as the climate movement should be.” 

==Life and activism==
[[File:Xiye Bastida dock 2019.png|thumb|left|Bastida awaiting Thunberg's arrival, 2019]]
Bastida was born in [[Atlacomulco]], Mexico to parents Mindahi and Geraldine, who are also environmentalists,<ref name="aspen">{{cite web|url= https://www.aspentimes.com/news/youth-activists-stress-collaboration-urgency-to-respond-to-climate-change/ |title=Youth activists stress collaboration, urgency to respond to climate change|journal=Aspen Times|first=Maddie|last=Vincent|date=17 August 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> and raised in the town of [[San Pedro Tultepec]] in [[Lerma, State of Mexico|Lerma]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.teenvogue.com/video/watch/how-an-indigenous-teen-climate-activist-plans-to-change-the-world |title=How an Indigenous Teen Climate Activist Plans to Change the World|journal=Teen Vogue|date=19 December 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://e360.yale.edu/features/from-a-young-climate-movement-leader-a-determined-call-for-action |title=From a Young Climate Movement Leader, a Determined Call for Action|journal=Yale Environment 365|first=Katherine|last=Bagley|date=7 November 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> She is of [[Otomi]]-[[Toltec]] (indigenous Mexican) and Aztec on her father's side and Chilean and European descent on her mother's.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://yonearth.org/podcast-archive/episode-46-xiye-bastida-global-youth-leader-strike-with-us/ |title=Episode 46 – Xiye Bastida, Global Youth Leader: “Strike with Us!”|journal=Yale Environment 360|first=Aaron William|last=Perry|date=27 August 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.cnnchile.com/cop25/xiye-bastida-activista-madre-chilena-greta-thunberg_20190828/ |title=Xiye Bastida, la adolescente de madre chilena que recibió a Greta Thunberg en su llegada a Nueva York|journal=CNN Chile|first=Desafío|last=Tierra|date=28 August 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020|language=Spanish}}</ref> Bastida holds Mexican and Chilean citizenship.<ref name="interview mag"/>

Bastida and her family moved to [[New York City]] after extreme flooding hit their hometown of San Pedro Tultepec in 2015 following three years of drought.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.huffpost.com/entry/xiye-bastida-climate-activism_n_5d8a7ec9e4b0c6d0cef3023e |title=This Teen Climate Activist Is Fighting To Ensure Indigenous And Marginalized Voices Are Being Heard|journal=HuffPost|first=Anna|last=Lucente Sterling|date=25 September 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref>

Bastida began her activism with an environmental club. The club protested at [[Albany, New York|Albany]] and [[New York City Hall]] and lobbied for the [[CLCPA]] [the Climate and Community Leaders Protection Act] and the [[Dirty Buildings Bill]].<ref name="interview mag">{{cite web|url= https://www.interviewmagazine.com/culture/environmental-activist-xiye-bastida-says-ok-doomers |title=Environmental activist Xiye Bastida says “OK, Doomers”|journal=Interview Magazine|first=Evalena|last=Labayen|date=10 December 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> It was then she heard about [[Greta Thunberg]] and her climate strikes.

Bastida gave a speech on Indigenous Cosmology at the 9th [[United Nations]] World Urban Forum, and was awarded the “Spirit of the UN” award in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.eomega.org/workshops/teachers/xiye-bastida |title=Xiye Bastida|website=Omega|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref>

Bastida led her high school, [[The Beacon School]],<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.democracynow.org/2019/9/20/global_climate_strike_new_york_minnesota |title=“Young People Have Had Enough”: Global Climate Strike Youth Activists on Why They Are Marching Today|journal=Democracy Now|date=20 September 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> in the first major climate strike in [[New York City]] on 15 March 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.npr.org/2020/01/19/797298179/you-need-to-act-now-meet-4-girls-working-to-save-the-warming-world |title='You Need To Act Now': Meet 4 Girls Working To Save The Warming World|journal=NPR|first=Anya|last=Kamenetz|date=19 January 2020|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> She and [[Alexandria Villaseñor]] officially greeted Thunberg upon her [[Voyage of Greta Thunberg|arrival from Europe by boat]] in September 2019 to attend the UN Climate Summit.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.pbs.org/wnet/peril-and-promise/2019/09/meet-xiye-bastida-americas-greta-thunberg/ |title=Meet Xiye Bastida, America’s Greta Thunberg|journal=PBS|first=Marlene|last=Cimons|date=19 September 2020|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> Xiye has been coined "America's [[Greta Thunberg]]". <ref>{{Cite web|title=Meet Xiye Bastida, America's Greta Thunberg|url=https://www.pbs.org/wnet/peril-and-promise/2019/09/meet-xiye-bastida-americas-greta-thunberg/|date=2019-09-19|website=Peril & Promise|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-18}}</ref> 

''[[Teen Vogue]]'' released a documentary short ''We Rise'' on Bastida in December 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.teenvogue.com/story/xiye-bastida-climate-activist-documentary |title=Xiye Bastida Opens Up About the Personal Costs of Activism In Documentary ‘We Rise’|journal=Teen Vogue|first=Allegra|last=Kirkland|date=19 December 2019|accessdate=3 February 2020}}</ref> Xiye has also collaborated with 2040 film to create a short video titled ''[https://ed.ted.com/best_of_web/kpN3w89R Imagine the Future]'' exploring what future landscapes and cityscapes could look like in the future.

She began her studies at the [[University of Pennsylvania]] in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thedp.com/article/2020/03/penn-admitted-students-class-of-2024|title=Hailing from Tennessee to Indonesia, meet five members of the newly admitted class of 2024|journal=Daily Pennsylvanian|first=Elizabeth|last=Meisenzahl|date=28 March 2020|accessdate=22 September 2020}}</ref>
