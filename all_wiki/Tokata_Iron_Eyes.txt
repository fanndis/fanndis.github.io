'''Tokata Iron Eyes''' <ref>{{cite news |last=Nausch |first=Natasha |date=October 7, 2019 |title=Who is Tokata Iron Eyes and why did Greta Thunberg come to the Dakotas to see her? |url=https://www.grandforksherald.com/news/government-and-politics/4710375-Who-is-Tokata-Iron-Eyes-and-why-did-Greta-Thunberg-come-to-the-Dakotas-to-see-her |work=Grand Forks Herald |location= |access-date=August 17, 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Fowkes |first=Whitney |date=October 7, 2019 |title=Who is Tokata Iron Eyes? |url=https://www.keloland.com/keloland-com-original/who-is-tokata-iron-eyes/ |work=KELOLAND.com |location= |access-date=August 17, 2020 }}</ref> is an American [[activist]]. She is a youth leader of "Rezpect Our Water", a campaign against the proposed route of the [[Dakota Access Pipeline]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Petronzio |first=Matt |date=December 8, 2016 |title=How young Native Americans built and sustained the #NoDAPL movement |url=https://mashable.com/2016/12/07/standing-rock-nodapl-youth/ |work=Mashable |location= |access-date=August 17, 2020 }}</ref> The group gained media attention in 2016 after a campaign video, featuring Iron Eyes herself, went viral. She participated in a climate rally in [[Rapid City, South Dakota]] together with Swedish climate activist [[Greta Thunberg]] on October 7, 2019.

Iron Eyes is a [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] and member of the [[Standing Rock Sioux]] tribe. Her father is the [[Native American civil rights|Native American activist]] and attorney [[Chase Iron Eyes]].
