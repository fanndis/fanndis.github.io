Established in 1965, the '''Dubai Chamber of Commerce & Industry''' ({{lang-ar|غرفة تجارة وصناعة دبي|Ghurfet tijar'a wa sina'aht Dubayy}}), or simply the '''Dubai Chamber''' ({{lang-ar|غرفة دبي|Ghurfet Dubayy}}), is a public [[non-profit organization]], and the stated purpose of the organization is to support the business community in [[Dubai]] and promote Dubai as an international business hub. The Chairman of Dubai Chamber is Majid Saif Al Ghurair. Hamad Buamim is the Chamber’s President & CEO.<ref>{{cite web|title=Africa Global Business Forum to become an annual event|url=http://www.africanreview.com/events/event-news/africa-global-business-forum-to-be-an-annual-event|accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Bhatia|first=Rushika|title=Dubai Chamber explores CIS region|url=http://www.smeadvisor.com/2013/05/dubai-chamber-explores-cis-region/|accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref>

== History ==

The Dubai Chamber was established by a decree issued by the late Ruler of Dubai, Sheikh Rashid bin Saeed Al Maktoum in 1965. Dubai Chamber started its activities with 450 members and a 12-member board of directors. The number of members increased to over 150,000, from all economic sectors, in 2013. There are 24 board members.<ref>{{cite web|title=Certificate of Origin - Dubai Chamber|url=http://www.taqnyah.ae/success-story.php?id=15|accessdate=9 January 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140109183052/http://www.taqnyah.ae/success-story.php?id=15|archivedate=9 January 2014}}</ref>

== Memberships ==

The Chamber created 27 business groups that are divided by industry, and 43 business councils that are divided by country.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bitar|first=Zaher|title=Business councils in Dubai to be treated as commercial institutions|url=http://m.gulfnews.com/business/business-councils-in-dubai-to-be-treated-as-commercial-institutions-1.1114795|accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref>

== Local and international Offices ==

Dubai Chamber has the head office in Deira and another branch in Jebel Ali Free Zone Authority (JAFZA). Representative offices are located in Dubai Airport Free Zone (DAFZA) and the Land Transport Customs Building in Al Aweer.

The Dubai Chamber opened representative offices in Baku, Azerbaijan and in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. {{As of|2013}} twenty more were expected to open in emerging markets. In January 2015, an office was inaugurated in Accra, Ghana.<ref>{{cite web|last=Basit|first=Abdul|title=Dubai Chamber posts 7% growth|url=http://www.khaleejtimes.com/biz/inside.asp?xfile=/data/uaebusiness/2013/July/uaebusiness_July201.xml&section=uaebusiness|accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Dubai Chamber to open rep office in Ethiopia|url=http://www.emirates247.com/business/economy-finance/dubai-chamber-to-open-rep-office-in-ethiopia-2012-05-20-1.459523|accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref>

== Awards ==

The Dubai Chamber launched the Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum Business Award in 2005. The award recognizes and rewards firms that contribute to the UAE's economic development. It is the highest level of recognition for business excellence that an organization in the UAE can achieve.<ref>{{cite web|title=Awards|url=http://www.sheikhmohammed.co.ae/vgn-ext-templating/v/index.jsp?vgnextoid=0561fd70bdc04310VgnVCM1000004d64a8c0RCRD&vgnextfmt=default|accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref>
