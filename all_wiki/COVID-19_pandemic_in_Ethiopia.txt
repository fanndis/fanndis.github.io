The '''COVID-19 pandemic in Ethiopia''' is part of the ongoing [[COVID-19 pandemic|worldwide pandemic]] of [[coronavirus disease 2019]] ({{nowrap|COVID-19}}) caused by [[severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus&nbsp;2]] ({{nowrap|SARS-CoV-2}}). The virus was confirmed to have reached [[Ethiopia]] on 13 March 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=First case of covid-19 confirmed in Ethiopia|url=https://www.afro.who.int/news/first-case-covid-19-confirmed-ethiopia|website=WHO Regional Office for Africa|access-date=2020-05-26}}</ref> The national government, led by Prime Minister [[Abiy Ahmed]], declared a five-month [[state of emergency]] in April 2020 but has allowed economic activities to continue during the public health crisis.<ref>[https://www.weforum.org/agenda/2020/06/ethiopia-covid19-response/ Ethiopia’s unconventional COVID-19 response] Arkebe Oqubay, [[World Economic Forum]], 5 June 2020</ref>

== Background ==
On 12 January 2020, the [[World Health Organization]] (WHO) confirmed that a [[novel coronavirus]] was the cause of a respiratory illness in a cluster of people in Wuhan City, Hubei Province, China, which was reported to the WHO on 31 December 2019.<ref name=Elsevier>{{cite web |url=https://www.elsevier.com/connect/coronavirus-information-center |title=Novel Coronavirus Information Center |last=Elsevier |date= |website=Elsevier Connect|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200130171622/https://www.elsevier.com/connect/coronavirus-information-center|archive-date=30 January 2020|access-date=15 March 2020}}</ref><ref name=Reynolds4March2020>{{Cite news |last=Reynolds |first=Matt |url=https://www.wired.co.uk/article/china-coronavirus |title=What is coronavirus and how close is it to becoming a pandemic? |date=4 March 2020 |work=Wired UK|access-date=5 March 2020 |issn=1357-0978|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200305104806/https://www.wired.co.uk/article/china-coronavirus|archive-date=5 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> The [[Case fatality rate|case fatality ratio]] for COVID-19 has been much lower than [[Severe acute respiratory syndrome|SARS of 2003]],<ref name=Imperial13March2020>{{cite web |url= https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/196137/crunching-numbers-coronavirus/ |title=Crunching the numbers for coronavirus |website=Imperial News|access-date=15 March 2020|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20200319084913/https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/196137/crunching-numbers-coronavirus/|archive-date=19 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=Gov.ukHCIDDef>{{cite web |url=https://www.gov.uk/guidance/high-consequence-infectious-diseases-hcid |title=High consequence infectious diseases (HCID); Guidance and information about high consequence infectious diseases and their management in England |last= |first= |date= |website=GOV.UK |language=en|url-status=live|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20200303051938/https://www.gov.uk/guidance/high-consequence-infectious-diseases-hcid|archive-date=3 March 2020|access-date=17 March 2020}}</ref> but the [[Transmission (medicine)|transmission]] has been significantly greater, with a significant total death toll.<ref name=WFSA>{{cite web |url= https://www.wfsahq.org/resources/coronavirus |title=World Federation Of Societies of Anaesthesiologists – Coronavirus |last= |first= |date= |website=www.wfsahq.org|url-status=live|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20200312233527/https://www.wfsahq.org/resources/coronavirus|archive-date=12 March 2020|access-date=15 March 2020}}</ref><ref name=Imperial13March2020/> Model-based simulations for Ethiopia indicate that the 95% [[confidence interval]] for the [[Basic reproduction number#Effective reproduction number|time-varying reproduction number]] ''R<sub> t</sub>'' was higher than 1.0 until the end of July 2020, while in August and September it was around 1.<ref>''Future scenarios of the healthcare burden of COVID-19 in low- or middle-income countries'', [[MRC Centre for Global Infectious Disease Analysis]] at [[Imperial College London]].</ref>

===March 2020===
On 13 March, the first coronavirus case was reported in the country and the victim later identified was a Japanese citizen.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/03/stocks-collapse-coronavirus-global-pandemic-live-200312235606108.html|title=Ethiopia confirms first coronavirus case: Live updates|website=aljazeera.com|access-date=13 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200313090407/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/03/stocks-collapse-coronavirus-global-pandemic-live-200312235606108.html|archive-date=13 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> The process of contact tracing is currently taking place, according to [[Lia Tadesse]], the Health Minister of Ethiopia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aa.com.tr/en/africa/ethiopia-confirms-first-coronavirus-case/1765130|title=Ethiopia confirms first coronavirus case|website=aa.com.tr|access-date=14 March 2020}}</ref>

Despite worsening situations in other countries, Ethiopia will not be enforcing a travel ban, with Tadesse stating that the virus is in 134 countries, and enforcing a travel ban will not work.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.voanews.com/science-health/coronavirus-outbreak/ethiopia-confirms-first-case-coronavirus|title=Ethiopia Confirms First Case of Coronavirus {{!}} Voice of America – English|website=voanews.com|language=en|access-date=14 March 2020}}</ref>

On 15 March, three additional cases of the coronavirus were reported. The infected persons, one Ethiopian and two Japanese nationals, had contact with the individual who was reported to be infected by the virus on 13 March.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ethiopianmonitor.com/2020/03/15/coronavirus-3-new-ccases-identified-in-ethiopia|title=Coronavirus: 3 New Cases Identified in Ethiopia|website=ethiopianmonitor.com|access-date=15 March 2020}}</ref>

On 16 March, a 34-year-old male Ethiopian who was said to have arrived from Dubai on 12 March 2020 tested positive for the virus.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fanabc.com/english/ethiopia-suspends-schools-sporting-events-for-15-days-due-to-coronavirus|title=Ethiopia Suspends Schools, Sporting Events Due To Coronavirus|website=fanabc.com|access-date=16 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet |user=lia_tadesse |author=Lia Tadesse |number=1239494668909412352 |date= 15 March 2020 |title=Ethiopia confirmed the 5th case on March 15, 2020 since the confirmation of the 1st case on March 13, 2020. }}</ref>

On 17 March, a British diplomat who arrived from Dubai tested positive for the virus.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.myjournalcourier.com/news/world/article/Coronavirus-local-spread-beginning-to-take-off-15137595.php|title=Coronavirus' local spread beginning to take off in Africa|website=myjournalcourier.com|access-date=17 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://apanews.net/en/pays/ethiopie/news/ethiopia-reports-sixth-case-of-coronavirus|title=Ethiopia reports sixth case of coronavirus|website=apanews.net|access-date=18 March 2020}}</ref>

==Reactions==
On 16 March 2020, the office of the [[Prime Minister of Ethiopia|Prime Minister]] announced that schools, sporting events, and public gatherings shall be suspended for 15 days.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aa.com.tr/en/africa/covid-19-ethiopia-closes-schools-bans-public-events/1767683|title=COVID-19: Ethiopia closes schools, bans public events|website=aa.com.tr|access-date=16 March 2020}}</ref>

On 20 March 2020, [[Ethiopian Airlines]] suspended flights to 30 countries affected with the coronavirus. On the same date it was announced that anyone entering the country should undergo a mandatory self-quarantine for 14 days. Night clubs in [[Addis Ababa]] are also to remain closed until further notice.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fanabc.com/english/ethiopia-suspends-flights-to-30-countries|title=Ethiopia Suspends Flights To 30 Countries|publisher=fanabc.com|access-date=20 March 2020}}</ref>

On 23 March 2020, Ethiopia closed all land borders and deployed security forces to halt the movement of people along the borders.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-03-23/ethiopia-closes-land-border-deploys-troops-to-combat-virus|title=Ethiopia Closes Land Border, Deploys Troops to Combat Virus |publisher=bloomberg.com|access-date=23 March 2020}}</ref>

On 25 March 2020, 4,011 prisoners were granted pardon by the [[President of Ethiopia|Ethiopian President]] [[Sahle-Work Zewde]] in an effort to prevent the coronavirus spread. ==Impact==
===Economic===
The [[United Nations Economic Commission for Africa]] estimated that COVID-19 will shave 2.9 percentage points off of Ethiopia's economic growth for fiscal year 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.unicef.org/ethiopia/media/2741/file/Socio-economic%20impacts%20of%20COVID-19.pdf|title=Socio-economic impacts of COVID-19|publisher=UNICEF Ethiopia|page=1|access-date=9 April 2020}}</ref>

The pandemic has affected Ethiopia's flower export industry significantly. After [[COVID-19 pandemic in Europe|Europe]] was hit with the coronavirus, the demand for flowers has plummeted and the price dropped by more than 80%. A total of 150,000 employees in this industry are also at the risk of losing their jobs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://allafrica.com/stories/202003240613.html|title=Ethiopia: Covid-19 Outbreak Puts Flower Exports in Danger|website=allafrica.com|access-date=31 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.capitalethiopia.com/news-news/flower-export-plummets-to-zero|title=Flower export plummets to zero|website=capitalethiopia.com|access-date=31 March 2020}}</ref>

[[Ethiopian Airlines]], the country's flag carrier, reported that it is working at only 10% of its capacity because of the coronavirus pandemic. The CEO, Tewolde Gebremariam, reported a loss of $550 million in the months of January to April 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/health-coronavirus-ethiopian-airlines/ethiopian-airlines-has-lost-550-million-this-year-due-to-coronavirus-ceo-idUSL8N2BV655|title=Ethiopian airlines has lost $550 million this year due to coronavirus -CEO|website=reuters.com|access-date=8 April 2020}}</ref>

===Educational===
More than 26 million students are affected by school closures due to the coronavirus. Consequently, school feeding programmes for around 1 million children across multiple regions of the country have stopped.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.unicef.org/ethiopia/media/2741/file/Socio-economic%20impacts%20of%20COVID-19.pdf|title=Socio-economic impacts of COVID-19|publisher=UNICEF Ethiopia|pages=3–4|access-date=9 April 2020}}</ref>

===Political===
The [[2020 Ethiopian general election|general elections]] which were set to be held on 29 August 2020 won't be held on the scheduled date, according to a statement by the National Electoral Board of Ethiopia released on 31 March. The board also stated that it has temporarily ceased all activities related to the election.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fanabc.com/english/nebe-says-impossible-to-hold-election-as-per-scheduled-due-to-covid-19/|title=NEBE Says Impossible To Hold Election As Per Scheduled Due To COVID-19|website=fanabc.com|access-date=31 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200522035630/https://www.fanabc.com/english/nebe-says-impossible-to-hold-election-as-per-scheduled-due-to-covid-19/|archive-date=22 May 2020|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Some people have been [[Misinformation related to the 2019–20 coronavirus pandemic#Efforts to combat misinformation|arrested]] for allegedly spreading [[false information]] about the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref>{{cite news |title=Ethiopian journalist Yayesew Shimelis detained following COVID-19 report |url=https://cpj.org/2020/04/ethiopian-journalist-yayesew-shimelis-detained-fol.php |work=Committee to Protect Journalists |date=1 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Ethiopia: Free Speech at Risk Amid Covid-19 |url=https://www.hrw.org/news/2020/05/06/ethiopia-free-speech-risk-amid-covid-19 |work=Human Rights Watch |date=6 May 2020}}</ref>
