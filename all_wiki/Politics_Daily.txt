'''''Politics Daily''''' was an [[United States|American]] [[political journalism]] web site launched by [[AOL News]] in April 2009.<ref name=Kurtz>{{cite web |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2009/08/09/AR2009080901968.html?hpid=artsliving |first=Howard |last=Kurtz |title= Media Notes: Howard Kurtz on Politics Daily, AOL's Fledgling Web Site |format= |work=washingtonpost.com |date=2009-08-09 |accessdate=2009-08-15|quote=The three-month-old venture has become a reemployment program for middle-aged journalists who lack the flash and dash of young bloggers -- and that is by design.}}</ref> It described itself as a "political news magazine for the general reader."<ref name=About>{{cite web |url=http://www.politicsdaily.com/about |title=About Politics Daily |format= |work=Politics Daily |accessdate=2009-08-15}}</ref>
Melinda Henneberger, a former ''[[Newsweek]]'' and ''[[New York Times]]'' reporter,<ref name=Kurtz/>
was [[Editor in Chief]].<ref name=About/>
Carl M. Cannon was the Executive Editor and senior Washington correspondent.
Former ''[[Baltimore Sun]]'' reporter David Wood was chief military correspondent. Politics Daily columnist Jill Lawrence was a national political correspondent for ''[[USA Today]]''. ''[[The Washington Post|Washington Post]]'' columnist Donna Britt and ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' reporter [[Lynn Sweet]] wrote for the web site.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowlDC/online_media/the_people_that_power_politics_daily__123925.asp |title=The People that Power Politics Daily |first=Matt |last=Dornic |format= |work=Fishbowl DC |publisher=Media Bistro |date=2009-08-10 |accessdate=2009-08-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090814151014/http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowlDC/online_media/the_people_that_power_politics_daily__123925.asp |archive-date=2009-08-14 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
Bucking the general trend of layoffs in the media industry due to declining advertising revenue in the [[late-2000s recession]], ''Politics Daily'' had hired 22 professional writers and journalists by the end of April 2009,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.businessinsider.com/attention-journalists-aol-is-hiring-up-in-editorial-2009-4 |title=AOL Is Hiring Editors (TWX) |format= |work= Business Insider|first=Nicholas |last=Carlson|date=2009-04-15|accessdate=2009-08-15|quote=And, unlike some new media empires, MediaGlow isn't only hiring young writers willing to forgo health insurance and full-time pay. They're hiring people with experience. Check out the bios for all the 22 people MediaGlow hired for its new site Politics Daily.}}</ref>
with some reportedly earning salaries over US$100,000 annually.<ref name=Kurtz/>

On March 10, 2011, Melinda Henneberger and other top Politics Daily staffers were among 200 editorial employees laid off at AOL.<ref name=Rothstein>{{cite web |url=
http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowldc/politics-dailys-melinda-henneberger-laid-off_b33226 |first=Betsy |last=Rothstein |title= Politics Daily's Melinda Henneberger laid off |format= |work=mediabistro.com |date=2011-03-10 |accessdate=2011-03-10}}</ref> It was later merged into ''[[The Huffington Post]]''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s Politics section.
