The '''Center for American Women and Politics''' ('''CAWP''') is a unit of the [[Eagleton Institute of Politics]] at [[Rutgers University|Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey.]] Established in 1971, it is nationally and internationally recognized as the leading source of scholarly research and current data about U.S. women's political participation. Its mission is "to promote greater knowledge and understanding about women's participation in politics and government and to enhance women's influence and leadership in public life.

The Center provides current numbers and historical data about women in U.S. politics, including information about candidates and officeholders. It also reports information about gender patterns in [[voting]], including gender gaps and the women's vote.<ref>[http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/current-numbers current numbers]</ref>

CAWP offers education and outreach programs including:
* Ready to Run - a national network of [[nonpartisanism|nonpartisan]] campaign training programs aimed at electing more women to public office;<ref>[http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/education_training/ready_to_run/overview Ready to Run]</ref>
* New Leadership - a national nonpartisan program to educate college women about politics and leadership and encourage them to become effective leaders in the political arena;<ref>[http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/education_training/NEW_Leadership/overview NEW Leadership]</ref>
* Teach a Girl to Lead - an initiative to make women's leadership visible to America's youth and help boys and girls grow up with more inclusive ideas about who can lead<ref>[http://tag.rutgers.edu/ Teach a Girl to Lead]</ref>

CAWP's research addresses emerging questions about American women's political participation. Areas of interest include candidate recruitment, candidates and campaigns, civic and political [[activism]], the impact of women public officials, [[political parties]], women and [[term limits in the United States|term limits]], women [[political appointments in the United States|political appointees]], [[women of color]] in politics, and women voters and the gender gap.<ref>[http://www.cawp.rutgers.edu/research research]</ref>
