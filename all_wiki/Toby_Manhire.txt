'''Toby Manhire''' is a New Zealand journalist and columnist. He describes himself as a "freelance writer & editor, mostly scribbling for ''Listener'', ''Herald'', ''Guardian''".<ref name="Twitterviews">{{cite news|url= http://salient.org.nz/features/twitterviews |work=Salient |title=Twitterviews |date=7 September 2014}}</ref> He is the son of poet [[Bill Manhire]].

==Career==
Manhire was editor of student magazine ''[[Salient (magazine)|Salient]]'' in 1997.<ref name="Twitterviews" /> From 2000 to 2010 he worked at ''[[The Guardian]]''. He has edited ''The Guardian''{{'}}s comment pages.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/profile/tobymanhire | title=Toby Manhire | work=The Guardian | accessdate=6 February 2015}}</ref>

In 2012, he edited a book ''The Arab Spring: Rebellion, Revolution, and a New World Order'', published by Guardian Books. He was a columnist at ''[[The New Zealand Herald]]'' and the ''[[New Zealand Listener]]''.

Manhire is active on Twitter, and was included in Bryce Edwards and Geoffrey Miller's list of the top 100 tweeters to follow in the 2014 election.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11309423 |title=Top 100 tweeters to follow this election |work= The New Zealand Herald| first1=Bryce |last1=Edwards |first2=Geoffrey |last2=Miller|date=17 August 2014}}</ref> On reviewing the list, social media blogger Matthew Beveridge concluded that Manhire's place was deserved: "Toby always has a quick comment for whatever is happening. Engages in a lot of discussions, and doesnâ€™t retweet too much. Overall deserving of his place on the list."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.matthewbeveridge.co.nz/politics/a-closer-look-at-the-twitter-top-100-print-journalists/ |title=A closer look at the Twitter top 100: Print journalists |author=Beveridge, Matthew |work=Social media & the 2014 General Election |date=4 September 2014}}</ref>

In May 2017, he became the politics editor at the New Zealand online magazine ''[[The Spinoff]]'',<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://thespinoff.co.nz/author/toby-manhire/|title=Toby Manhire|work=[[The Spinoff]]|access-date=2017-05-13|language=en-US}}</ref> becoming editor in January 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://thespinoff.co.nz/media/22-01-2018/editorial-changes-at-the-spinoff/|title= Editorial changes at The Spinoff|work=The Spinoff|access-date=2018-02-13|language=en-US}}</ref>
