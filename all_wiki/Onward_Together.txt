'''Onward Together''' is an American [[501c4|political action organization]] founded in May 2017 by former [[United States Secretary of State|U.S. Secretary of State]] and [[2016 Democratic National Convention|2016 Democratic presidential nominee]] [[Hillary Clinton]] to fundraise for progressive political groups including: [[Swing Left]], Indivisible, [[Color of Change]], [[Emerge America]], and [[Run for Something]].<ref name="theguardiangambino">{{cite news|last1=Gambino|first1=Lauren|title=Hillary Clinton launches political action group Onward Together|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2017/may/15/hillary-clinton-onward-together-launch|accessdate=May 17, 2017|work=The Guardian|date=May 16, 2017}}</ref> Clinton described the group as an effort "to advance the vision that won nearly 66 million votes" of a "fairer, more inclusive, big-hearted America."<ref name="politicopalmerhillaryclinton">{{cite news|last1=Palmer|first1=Anna|title=Hillary Clinton launches new political group: ‘Onward Together’|url=http://www.politico.eu/article/hillary-clinton-launches-new-political-group-onward-together-progressive/|accessdate=May 17, 2017|work=Politico|date=May 15, 2017}}</ref>

==Overview==
Clinton co-founded the group with [[Howard Dean]].<ref name=buzz1>{{cite web |url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/rubycramer/hillary-clinton-hires-two-former-campaign-aides-for |title=Hillary Clinton Hires Two Former Campaign Aides For "Resistance" PAC |last=Cramer |first=Ruby |date=August 4, 2017 |website=www.buzzfeed.com |publisher=[[Buzzfeed]] |access-date=August 6, 2017 |quote=}}</ref> In August 2017, Dean confirmed that Onward Together had hired Emmy Ruiz and [[Adam Parkhomenko]] as consultants, both of whom were members of Clinton's [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2008|2008]] and 2016 presidential campaigns.<ref name=buzz1/>

On May 15, 2017, [[Hillary Clinton]] tweeted the launch of Onward Together from her personal Twitter account.<ref>https://twitter.com/HillaryClinton/status/864211960732295168</ref> Clinton transferred $800,000 from her 2016 presidential campaign to Onward Together shortly before announcing the group's launch in May, documents the campaign filed with the FEC revealed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/hillary-clinton-a-favorite-gop-foil-plans-discreet-2018-strategy/2018/02/11/ec02d02e-0cd0-11e8-95a5-c396801049ef_story.html|title=Hillary Clinton, a favorite GOP foil, plans discreet 2018 strategy|website=Washington Post|language=en|access-date=2018-11-24}}</ref>

==Activities==
During the [[2018 United States midterm elections]] the PAC funded Democratic candidates as well as grassroots organizations. <ref>http://www.politicspa.com/clinton-pac-boosts-three-pa-dems/88371/</ref>
