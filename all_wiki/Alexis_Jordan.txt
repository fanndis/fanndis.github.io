'''Alexis Jordan''' (born April 7, 1992)<ref name="official website" /> is an American singer and actress from [[Columbia, South Carolina]]. Jordan rose to fame as a contestant on the first season of ''[[America's Got Talent]]'' in 2006 at the age of 14. After being eliminated from the show, she began to upload cover songs to YouTube, which received millions of views. The exposure led Jordan to the attention of Norwegian production team [[Stargate (music producers)|Stargate]] and American rapper [[Jay Z]], who both went on to sign her to their joint label, [[StarRoc]].<ref>{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=alexis-jordan-p2178199/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: Alexis Jordan|last=Jeffries|first=David|publisher=[[Allmusic]]. [[Rovi Corporation]]|accessdate=October 19, 2010}}</ref>

Her debut single, "[[Happiness (Alexis Jordan song)|Happiness]]", was released in September 2010, and reached number one in the Netherlands and Norway,<ref name="NOR">{{cite web|url=http://norwegiancharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Alexis+Jordan |title=Discography Alexis Jordan |publisher=norwegiancharts.com. Hung Medien |accessdate=October 30, 2011}}</ref> and also became a top-three hit in Australia and the United Kingdom. Jordan's debut [[Alexis Jordan (album)|self-titled album]] was released on February 25, 2011.<ref name="DebutAlbum">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/ie/album/alexis-jordan/id414644695 |title=Alexis Jordan (Album) – Alexis Jordan |publisher=[[iTunes Store]] (Ireland). [[Apple Inc]] |accessdate=April 13, 2011}}</ref> "[[Good Girl (Alexis Jordan song)|Good Girl]]" was released as Jordan's second single in February 2011. Her self-titled debut album was released on February 25, 2011. It debuted on the UK Albums Chart at No. 9 and on the Australian Albums Chart at No. 11. Jordan also featured on Sean Paul's single "[[Got 2 Luv U]]", which reached number one in [[Switzerland]] and [[Bulgaria]].

==Life and career==

===1992–2008: Early life and career beginnings===
[[File:Fall skyline of Columbia SC from Arsenal Hill.jpg|right|thumb|230px|Jordan was born in [[Columbia, South Carolina]].]]
Jordan was born on April 7, 1992, in [[Columbia, South Carolina]], to a mother of [[African American]] and [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American / European]] descent and a [[Puerto Ricans|Puerto Rican]] father.<ref name="official website">{{cite web|url=http://www.alexisjordanofficial.com/be-en/biography |title=Biography – The Official Alexis Jordan Site |publisher=Official Alexis Jordan Site |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110215120612/http://www.alexisjordanofficial.com/be-en/biography |archive-date=February 15, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Jordan began writing songs while she was in third grade.<ref name="official website"/> At the age of 11, she moved with her parents and three younger siblings Taylor, Malichai and Malcolm to [[Santa Clarita, California]], to pursue a career in the arts.<ref name="official website"/> At the age of 12, Jordan served as an opening act for [[Smokey Robinson]] at a [[Stevie Wonder]] tribute concert.<ref name="official website"/>

In 2006, Jordan auditioned for the first season of ''[[America's Got Talent]]''. She sang [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Have Nothing]]" at her audition and made it through to next round, only to be eliminated in the semifinals. Judge [[Brandy Norwood|Brandy]] brought her back to compete in the semifinals, after the public didn't gather enough votes for Jordan. After being eliminated from the show, Jordan and her family moved to [[Atlanta]] to be closer to the music industry. While there, she began to upload cover songs on YouTube while submitting demos. By 2008, Jordan's YouTube page was racking up millions of views. The exposure led Jordan to the attention of production team [[Stargate (production team)|Stargate]] who called her to fly to New York and record a few songs with them.<ref name="boombox">{{cite web|last=Alexis |first=Nadeska |url=http://www.theboombox.com/2010/03/26/alexis-jordan-snags-deal-with-roc-nation/ |title=Alexis Jordan Snags Deal With Roc Nation |date=March 26, 2010 |work=The Boombox |publisher=[[AOL]] |accessdate=December 26, 2010}}</ref> While in the studio, rapper [[Jay-Z]] walked in,<ref name="boombox"/> which ultimately resulted in Jordan becoming the first artist signed to the new, Sony Music-affiliated label, [[StarRoc]]/[[Roc Nation]] – a joint venture between Stargate and Jay-Z's Roc Nation label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bluesandsoul.com/feature/676/alexis_jordan_quietly_confident/ |title=Alexis Jordan interview by Pete Lewis, 'Blues & Soul' May 2011|publisher=Bluesandsoul.com|accessdate=September 6, 2011}}</ref>

===2010–12: Record deal and ''Alexis Jordan''===
{{main|Alexis Jordan (album)}}
Her self-titled [[Alexis Jordan (album)|debut album]] was released on February 25, 2011.<ref name="DebutAlbum"/> The album was recorded from January 1, 2007, to October 15, 2010, in which Jordan worked with [[Stargate (production team)|StarGate]], [[Sandy Vee]], Nightwatch and [[Espionage (production team)|Espionage]]. The album debuted at number twenty-eight in Ireland on March 4, 2011. In the United Kingdom, the album debuted at number nine on March 6, 2011. The album also debuted at number eleven in Australia. On April 30, 2011, the album debuted at number 80 on the [[Dutch Album Chart]]. In its 13th week the album shot from 72 to 23, reaching its peak of 21 two weeks later.<ref>{{cite web|first=Steffen|last=Hung |url=http://www.dutchcharts.nl/showitem.asp?interpret=Alexis+Jordan&titel=Alexis+Jordan&cat=a |title=Alexis Jordan |publisher=dutchcharts.nl |accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref>
Jordan's single "[[Happiness (Alexis Jordan song)|Happiness]]" was released in September 2010. In the United States, the song reached No. 1 on the [[Hot Dance Club Songs]], becoming Jordan's first No. 1 single on that chart. It also reached No. 1 in Norway<ref name="NOR"/> and the Netherlands (where it stayed for 10 weeks), and peaked at No. 3 in Australia and the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/singles/Myths%20Of%20The%20Near%20Future |title=The Official Charts Company – Alexis Jordan – Happiness |publisher=[[Official Charts Company]] |accessdate=December 26, 2010 }}</ref> In Australia, "Happiness" was certified triple platinum by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA), for shipments of 210,000 copies. "[[Good Girl (Alexis Jordan song)|Good Girl]]" was released as Jordan's second single in February 2011. The song debuted at No. 6 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], giving Jordan her second top-10 hit in the United Kingdom. It also reached No. 15 in Ireland and became her second chart-topper on the ''Billboard'' Hot Dance Club Songs chart in April.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/#/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-pop-country-songs-more-1005141302.story|title=Chart Highlights: Pop, Country Songs & More|work=Billboard|publisher=Nielsen Business Media|accessdate=April 20, 2011| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20110416044749/http://www.billboard.com/| archive-date= April 16, 2011 | url-status= live}}</ref>  "Hush Hush" was released as the album's third single on May 8, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.com.au/music/singlereviews/ |title=Music – Singles Release Diary |work=[[Digital Spy]] |publisher=[[Hachette Filipacchi Médias]] |accessdate=April 13, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110412062851/http://www.digitalspy.com.au/music/singlereviews/ |archive-date=April 12, 2011 }}</ref>

On April 12, 2012 Alexis embarked on her first headline tour in the United Kingdom. Jordan performed in three cities – London (Scala) on Thursday June 30, Birmingham HMV (Institute) on Saturday July 2 and Manchester (Club Academy) on Sunday July 3. Tickets went on sale on Friday April 15.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.columbia.co.uk/artists/alexis_jordan/news/20851/0/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130420130845/http://www.columbia.co.uk/artists/alexis_jordan/news/20851/0/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=April 20, 2013 |title=Alexis announces first UK headline dates! |publisher=Columbia.co.uk |accessdate=February 21, 2013 }}</ref> In June and July 2011 Jordan also supported British boy band [[JLS]] on their eight-day UK tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.columbia.co.uk/artists/alexis_jordan/news/20852/0/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130419212727/http://www.columbia.co.uk/artists/alexis_jordan/news/20852/0/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=April 19, 2013 |title=Alexis Jordan On Tour With JLS |publisher=Columbia.co.uk |accessdate=February 21, 2013 }}</ref> She also appeared at [[T4 on the Beach]] in the UK and performed "Hush Hush" and "Good Girl".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/alexisjordan/view/28834/ |title=Alexis Jordan's debut fragrance would "smell of soap"|publisher=pressparty.com|date=July 15, 2011|accessdate=September 29, 2011}}</ref> Jordan featured on [[Sean Paul]]'s single "[[Got 2 Luv U]]", which reached number one in Switzerland, Netherlands, Norway, Bulgaria, Romania France,  United States Billboard (in the Latin Raps & Pop Songs) and Venezuela.<ref name="SWI">{{cite web|url=http://www.swisscharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Alexis+Jordan |title=Discography Alexis Jordan |publisher=swisscharts.com. Hung Medien |accessdate=May 1, 2011}}</ref> Jordan also made a cameo appearance in the film ''[[Honey 2]]''. In June 2012 Jordan performed at [[Birmingham Pride]] performing "[[Good Girl (Alexis Jordan song)|Good Girl]]", "[[Got 2 Luv U]]", "Shout Shout", "[[Hush Hush (Alexis Jordan song)|Hush Hush]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=490j4-Oi-bY |title=Alexis Jordan, The Nightingale Gay Pride 2012 |publisher=YouTube |date=June 5, 2012 |accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref> On November 25, 2012, she appeared as musical guest on [[X Factor (Romania season 2)|X Factor (Romania)]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://xfactor.a1.ro/a-intrat-pe-scena-x-factor-ca-o-regina-alexis-jordan-548.html|title=A intrat pe scena X Factor ca o regina: Alexis Jordan!|publisher=[[Antena 1 (Romania)|Antena 1]]|date=November 25, 2012|language=ro|access-date=December 16, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121230110810/http://xfactor.a1.ro/a-intrat-pe-scena-x-factor-ca-o-regina-alexis-jordan-548.html|archive-date=December 30, 2012|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

===2012–present: Second studio album===
In October 2011, she confirmed via Twitter that she was working on her second studio album.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=AlexisJordan|author=Alexis Jordan|number=126816482876342272|date=October 20, 2011|title=Recording album #2 ... #beastmode ;)}}</ref> In November 2011, Jordan went to a recording studio in New York where she recorded with Stargate, she stated that the album is nearly completed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0u5Jv3gMbSE |title=Alexis Jordan´s Glimpse No. 1 |publisher=YouTube |date=December 1, 2011 |accessdate=April 15, 2012}}</ref> In the same YouTube video uploaded by Jordan, she was asked what she was doing and she replied "Writing about different things 'cause I'm like all messed up cause I'm talking about girl power, then I'm talking about myself and how people don't really know me that well so they gonna get to know me in this song."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vPvrIoBhECU |title=Alexis Jordan's Glimpse No. 2 |publisher=YouTube |date=December 9, 2011 |accessdate=April 15, 2012}}</ref>

On January 15, 2013, a new song titled "[[Acid Rain (Alexis Jordan song)|Acid Rain]]" leaked online. It was intended to serve as the lead cut from her second album.<ref>{{cite web|first=Steffen|last=Hung |url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a451184/alexis-jordan-debuts-new-single-acid-rain-listen.html |title=Alexis Jordan debuts new single 'Acid Rain' – listen |publisher=[[Digital Spy]] |accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref> "Acid Rain" samples [[Bingo Players]]‘ song “[[Get Up (Rattle)|Rattle]],” and is written by [[Sia Furler]] and produced by Stargate.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/alexisj/acid-rain-mastered-version |title=Acid Rain by AlexisJordan |publisher=Soundcloud.com |accessdate=February 21, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130922190220/http://soundcloud.com/alexisj/acid-rain-mastered-version |archive-date=September 22, 2013 }}</ref> However it served only as a buzz single, as it was leaked in early 2013 and was never released on worldwide radios.<ref>{{cite web|first=Sam|last=Lansky |url=http://idolator.com/7389322/alexis-jordan-j-coles-acid-rain-hear#more-7389322 |title=Alexis Jordan & J. Cole's "Acid Rain": Hear The Sia-Penned Dance Banger |publisher=Idolator.com |date=January 15, 2013 |accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref> On December 28, 2013 Jordan released a new message on her Facebook page in which she confirms that the official lead single from her then upcoming second studio album would be titled "Gone" and it would be released on March 1, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Alexis Jordan confirms new single "Gone"|url=http://www.josepvinaixa.com/blog/alexis-jordan-new-single-gone/|publisher=Josep Vinaixa Blog|accessdate=December 28, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131230234755/http://www.josepvinaixa.com/blog/alexis-jordan-new-single-gone/|archive-date=December 30, 2013|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The official video was shot in late 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=For your patience new music ..already on the way. Alexis Jordan-Gone|url=https://twitter.com/AlexisJordan/status/416678424552673280/photo/1|publisher=[[Twitter]]|accessdate=December 27, 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110041835/https://twitter.com/AlexisJordan/status/416678424552673280/photo/1|archive-date=January 10, 2014}}</ref> Its official music video was premiered on February 28, 2014.<ref>{{YouTube|iX2J8qfcWLg}}</ref> Jordan was dropped by Jay-Z's Roc Nation label after the single failed to chart.<ref name="digitalspy"/>

==Personal life==

In 2014 Jordan married Luke Broadlick.<ref name="digitalspy"/> In 2016, she gave birth to their daughter, Anora. In 2017, she gave birth to their first son, Lincoln. In 2019, she gave birth to their second son Abel.<ref name="digitalspy">{{cite web|url=https://www.digitalspy.com/music/a803762/whatever-happened-to-alexis-jordan/|publisher=[[Digital Spy]]|first=Lewis |last=Corner|title=Whatever happened to Alexis Jordan? |date=August 5, 2016 |accessdate=February 9, 2019}}</ref>

Jordan's self-titled debut album contained [[dance-pop]], [[Pop music|pop]] and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] music, in an interview Jordan described the album's musical style saying "I don’t want to be kept in a box. I’m that pop artist, but I can switch it up and turn to R&B/soul and do lots of ballads and the hip-hop swag. It’s very versatile".<ref name="mtv.co.uk">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/music/urban/238897-alexis-jordan-the-interview |title=Alexis Jordan: The Interview! |publisher=Mtv.co.uk |accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref> Alexis described her album's genre as being "universal" saying in an interview "It's going to be very universal. It's not just a pop dance record. It has a little reggae, a little hip-hop swag, so it's going to be everything."<ref>{{cite web|author=ELLE Canada |url=http://www.ellecanada.com/living/music/alexis-jordan-records-universal-album/a/36661 |title=Alexis Jordan records 'universal' album |publisher=Elle Canada |accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref>

Jordan cites singers [[Christina Aguilera]], [[Whitney Houston]], [[Mariah Carey]] and [[Beyoncé]] as influences.<ref>{{cite web|author=Big Time Rush |url=http://www.j-14.com/2010/07/exclusive-qa-with-alexis-jordan.html |title=Exclusive Q&A with Alexis Jordan |publisher=J-14.com |date=July 21, 2010 |accessdate=February 21, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110101225625/http://www.j-14.com/2010/07/exclusive-qa-with-alexis-jordan.html |archive-date=January 1, 2011 }}</ref> Jordan also cites [[Michael Jackson]] as an influence calling him "a big inspiration" and also is inspired by Billie Holiday saying "I like Billie Holiday’s sound".<ref name="mtv.co.uk"/>
