'''Jane Fraser''' (born 1942) is an American philanthropist, author, editor, businesswoman, and the President of The [[Stuttering Foundation of America]], a non-profit charitable organization working toward the prevention and improved treatment of [[stuttering]].

==Early life==
Fraser was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]], [[Tennessee]], in 1942. Her father, [[Malcolm Fraser (philanthropist)|Malcolm Fraser]], was one of the founders of [[Genuine Parts Company]] and later founded the [[Stuttering Foundation of America]] after living with [[stuttering]] his entire life.

In 1964, Fraser received her bachelor's degree in Russian and linguistics from [[Bryn Mawr College]] in Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania. Fraser later attended the [[University of Strasbourg]] in France receiving her M.S. in Russian in 1966.

She worked as an interpreter and translator for the [[National Assembly of France]] in Paris, France, as well as served as editor and translator for the [[Institut Gustave Roussy]] until 1980.<ref name="brynmawr1">{{cite web |url=http://www.brynmawr.edu/alumnae/bulletin/fraser.htm |title=Profile of Jane Fraser '64 |publisher=Brynmawr.edu |date=2002-02-06 |accessdate=2013-08-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303182746/http://www.brynmawr.edu/alumnae/bulletin/fraser.htm |archive-date=2016-03-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==President of the Stuttering Foundation of America==
Beginning in 1981, Fraser has served as President of the [[Stuttering Foundation of America]].

Since the beginning of her presidency, the foundation's endowment has grown from $3,000,000 to $17,000,000. 
The Stuttering Foundation has also added a toll-free hotline and website. 
Under Fraser's presidency, the foundation has published 24 books and 13 brochures for stutterers.<ref name="brynmawr1"/>

==Author and editor==
Fraser co-authored “If Your Child Stutters: A Guide for Parents” for the Stuttering Foundation in 1988.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/If-Your-Child-Stutters-Parents/dp/0933388586/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1308069051&sr=1-1 |title=If Your Child Stutters: A Guide for Parents: Stanley Ainsworth, Ph.D., and Jane Fraser, Jane Fraser: 9780933388581: Amazon.com: Books |publisher=Amazon.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-10}}</ref>

She also edited “Counseling Stutterers” in 1981 and “Stuttering Therapy: Transfer and Maintenance” in 1982.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Stuttering-Transfer-Maintenance-Publication-Foundation/dp/0933388195 |title=Stuttering Therapy Transfer and Maintenance (Publication / Speech Foundation of America): Jane Fraser Gruss: 9780933388192: Amazon.com: Books |publisher=Amazon.com |date=2006-09-04 |accessdate=2013-08-10}}</ref>

==Other affiliations==
Fraser served as Member of the Advisory Council for the [[National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders|National Institute on Deafness and Other Communicative Disorders]], at the [[National Institutes of Health]] from 1996 until 2000.<ref name="theahi1">{{cite web|url=http://www.theahi.org/board-of-directors/ |title=Board of Directors |publisher=The AHI |date= |accessdate=2013-08-10}}</ref>

She also was a member of the Board of Trustees for [[Hamilton College (New York)|Hamilton College]] from 1991 until 1997.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mnsu.edu/comdis/isad8/papers/pws8/fraserpws8.html |title=Fraser |publisher=Mnsu.edu |date= |accessdate=2013-08-10}}</ref>

She is a member of the Board of Trustees for the [[Alexander Hamilton Institute for the Study of Western Civilization]].<ref name="theahi1"/>

In addition, she serves as a member of the Advisory Board for the Institute for Effective Governance in Washington D.C.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goacta.org/programs/IEG.cfm |title=ACTA |publisher=Goacta.org |date= |accessdate=2013-08-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120315091133/http://www.goacta.org/programs/IEG.cfm |archive-date=2012-03-15 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and a member of the Conservation Council at [[Panthera Corporation]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.panthera.org/conservation-council|title=Conservation Council}}</ref>

==Awards and honors==
Fraser was named Executive of the Year by the [[The NonProfit Times|Non-Profit Times]] for 2008–2009.<ref>[http://www.thenonprofittimes.com/news-articles/npt-executive-of-the-year-jane-fraser-took-on-the-cfc-and-won/][http://www.entrepreneur.com/tradejournals/article/172554674.html] {{dead link|date=January 2016}}</ref> Fraser is an Honorary Fellow of the [[Royal College of Speech and Language Therapists]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rcslt.org/honours_roll_call_1945-2016|title=RCSLT Honours Roll Call (1945 -2016)}}</ref>
