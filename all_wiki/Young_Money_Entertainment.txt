'''Young Money Entertainment''' is an American [[record label]] founded by [[Rapping|rapper]] [[Lil Wayne]]. Young Money's president is Lil Wayne's lifelong friend and fellow rapper [[Mack Maine]]. The label was an imprint of [[Cash Money Records]] and is distributed by [[Republic Records]].

The label has released fourteen [[Billboard 200|US]] albums that reached number one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart: ''[[Tha Carter III]]'', ''[[I Am Not a Human Being]]'', ''[[Tha Carter IV]]'' and ''[[Tha Carter V]]'' by Lil Wayne, ''[[Thank Me Later]]'', ''[[Take Care (album)|Take Care]]'', ''[[Nothing Was the Same]]'', ''[[If You're Reading This It's Too Late]]'', ''[[What a Time to Be Alive]]'' (along with [[Epic Records]] artist [[Future (rapper)|Future]]), ''[[Views (album)|Views]]'', ''[[More Life]]'' and ''[[Scorpion (Drake album)|Scorpion]]'' by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], and ''[[Pink Friday]]'' and ''[[Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded]]'' by [[Nicki Minaj]].

Current artists include Lil Wayne, Drake, Nicki Minaj, [[Mack Maine]], [[Lil Twist]], and [[Cory Gunz]], among others. The label has released three compilation albums â€” ''[[We Are Young Money]]'' (2009), ''[[Rich Gang (album)|Rich Gang]]'' (2013; with Cash Money Records), and ''[[Young Money: Rise of an Empire]]'' (2014).

==History==
[[File:Lil Wayne in Concert.jpg|thumb|left|212x212px|Lil Wayne (pictured) founded the label in 2005 and remained President until 2007]]

The label was formed in 2005. In 2007, In an interview with ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'' magazine, Lil Wayne detailed he stepped down as President of the label, and had given the position to Cortez Bryant.<ref name="vibe">{{cite web|last=Checkoway|first=Laura|title=Weezy F. Baby: Uncut|url=http://www.vibe.com/news/news_headlines/2007/10/lil_wayne_excerpts/|work=Vibe|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080611145950/http://www.vibe.com/news/news_headlines/2007/10/lil_wayne_excerpts/|archivedate=June 11, 2008|date=October 2, 2007}}</ref> As of 2009, [[Mack Maine]] is serving as President.<ref name="xxl">Fleischer, Adam. [http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=32126 Mack Maine: Mainstreamin'] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090413024557/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=32126 |date=April 13, 2009 }}, ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''. Published January 16, 2009. Retrieved April 28, 2009.</ref> Canadian rapper [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]] signed a joint venture between [[Cash Money Records]] and Young Money. President Mack Maine confirmed that [[Omarion]] was no longer on the label after many rumours were circulating the internet.

To promote the label's first collaboration album, ''[[We Are Young Money]]'', Young Money artists embarked on the Young Money Tour.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lil Wayne to Drop 3 Albums This Year? |url=http://www.rap-up.com/2009/10/09/lil-wayne-to-drop-3-albums-this-year/ |accessdate=14 December 2018 |work=Rap-Up |date=10 October 2009}}</ref> The collaborative album was released on December 21, 2009, and debuted at number nine on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, selling 142,000 copies in its first-week sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/latest-headlines/2009/12/young-money-racks-up-142k-in-first-week-sales-127k-eminem-fans-order-refill/|title=Young Money Racks Up 142K in First Week Sales, 127K Eminem Fans Order Refill|work=XXL Mag}}</ref> The album was certified gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) for selling over a shipments of 500,000 copies in the United States.<ref>{{Cite certification | region=United States | type=album | title=We Are Young Money | artist=Young Money | date=May 24, 2010 | accessdate=October 10, 2011}}</ref>

''We Are Young Money'' featured the highly successful single "[[BedRock]]", which debuted at thirty six on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] on the week ending December 12, 2009.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Hot 100 - March 12, 2010|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2009-12-12/hot-100|magazine=Billboard|accessdate=25 December 2010}}</ref> On the week ending March 13, 2010, the song reached a peak of two, making it the most successful single by the group on the chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Hot 100 - March 13, 2010|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2010-03-13/hot-100|magazine=Billboard|accessdate=25 December 2010}}</ref>
"BedRock" debuted in the [[UK Singles Chart]] on 6 February 2010 at forty.<ref>{{cite web|title=TOP 40 OFFICIAL UK SINGLES ARCHIVE |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20100131/7501/|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=25 December 2010}}</ref> On March 6, 2010, the single climbed to twenty two,<ref>{{cite web|title=TOP 40 OFFICIAL UK SINGLES ARCHIVE|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20100228/7501/|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=25 December 2010|df=mdy-all}}</ref> and the subsequent week the single climbed to eighteen, marking Young Money's first Top 20 hit in the UK.<ref>{{cite web|title= Top 40 official uk singles archive|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20100307/7501/|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=25 December 2010}}</ref> Peaking at number nine on April 3, 2010, making it Young Money's first Top 10 hit in the UK.<ref name="official UK peak">{{cite web|title=TOP 40 OFFICIAL UK SINGLES ARCHIVE|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20100328/7501/|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=25 December 2010|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

The label had three of the top 10 highest 2010 hip hop album sales with Drake's ''[[Thank Me Later]]'', and Lil Wayne's ''[[Rebirth (Lil Wayne album)|Rebirth]]'' and ''[[I Am Not a Human Being]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=JACOBS|first=ALLEN|title=Young Money, Eminem Dominate Half-Year Top Album Sales|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.11718/title.young-money-eminem-dominate-half-year-top-album-sales|accessdate=December 13, 2010|date=July 15, 2010}}</ref>

On March 6, 2010, Lil Wayne confirmed that rapper [[Cory Gunz]] was the latest artist to sign.{{Citation needed|reason=Previous citation did not have a reliable source.|date=December 2018}} In 2011, [[Konvict Muzik]] artist [[T-Pain]] signed to the Young Money roster after he and [[Akon]] agreed to part ways after his last Konvict release ''[[Revolver (T-Pain album)|RevolveR]]''. Later that year, T-Pain revealed that he was close to signing a deal with Young Money, but it never concluded.<ref>{{cite news|first=Andres|last=Vasquez|title=Akon Supports T-Pain's Decision To Join Young Money | date=July 14, 2011 | url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.15967/title.akon-supports-t-pains-decision-to-join-young-money|work=HipHop DX|accessdate=November 21, 2011}}</ref> In November 2011, Lil Wayne introduced 14-year-old singer [[Torion|Torion Sellers]] to Young Money.<ref name="Torion">{{cite news|first=Steven J.|last=Horowitz|title=Lil Wayne Announces Newest Young Money Member, Fourteen-Year-Old Torion Sellers|date=November 21, 2011|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.17702/title.lil-wayne-announces-|work=HipHop DX|accessdate=November 22, 2011}}</ref>

On February 15, 2013 Mack Maine announced group We the Future and Lil Wayne's 14-year-old daughter Reginae Carter had signed to Young Money.<ref name="hiphopdx.com">{{cite web|last=Horowitz |first=Steven J. |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22936/title.lil-wayne-signs-his-daughter-reginae-carter-to-young-money |title=Lil Wayne Signs His Daughter Reginae Carter To Young Money &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales|publisher=HipHop DX|date=2013-02-15|accessdate=2013-08-07}}</ref> In the same year, Young Money released Lil Wayne's ''[[I Am Not a Human Being II]]'', [[Tyga]]'s ''[[Hotel California (Tyga album)|Hotel California]]'' and Drake's ''[[Nothing Was the Same]]'' on March 26, April 7, and September 24, respectively.

In December 2012 Mack Maine announced a second Young Money album would be released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/mackmaine/status/275177572353052674 |title=Twitter / mackmaine: We Are Young Money Vol 2 |publisher=Twitter.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-07}}</ref> In February 2013, label co-owner Birdman released a compilation mixtape with Cash Money and Young Money titled ''Rich Gang: All Stars''. Shortly after, Birdman announced compilation album ''[[Rich Gang (album)|Rich Gang]]'' and its schedule for release to be on May 21, 2013. He added ''Rich Gang'' would feature members of Cash Money, Young Money and various Young Money collaborators.<ref>{{cite web|last=Harling |first=Danielle |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22966/title.tyga-s-hotel-california-album-pushed-back-will-now-be-released-in-april |title=Tyga "Hotel California" Tracklist & Cover Art |publisher=Hiphopdx.com|date=2013-03-12 |accessdate=2013-08-07}}</ref> The first single from the project, "Tapout", was released on March 12, 2013, and featured Lil Wayne, [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Mack Maine]], and Nicki Minaj and produced by [[808 Mafia]]'s [[Southside (producer)|Southside]] and [[808 Mafia|TM88]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/03/listen-birdman-f-lil-wayne-future-mack-maine-and-nicki-minaj-tapout |title=Listen: Birdman f/ Lil Wayne, Future, Mack Maine, and Nicki Minaj "Tapout" |magazine=Complex |accessdate=2013-08-07}}</ref> The album would then be pushed back to July 23, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.missinfo.tv/index.php/birdman-rich-gang-tapout-video-feat-lil-wayne-future-mack-maine-nicki-minaj/ |title=New Video: Rich Gang (Birdman, Lil' Wayne, Future, Mack Maine & Nicki Minaj) "Tapout" |publisher=MissInfo.tv |date=2013-05-05 |accessdate=2013-08-07}}</ref>

On January 22, 2014, it was revealed that Young Money would be releasing their own compilation album on March 11, 2014, titled ''[[Young Money: Rise of an Empire]]''. That same day, the first single "[[We Alright]]" featuring Lil Wayne, Birdman and Euro was released. Drake's ''[[Nothing Was the Same]]'' leftover "[[Trophies (song)|Trophies]]" would also be included on the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2014/01/22/young-money-readies-rise-of-an-empire-compilation-for-march/|title=Young Money Readies 'Rise of an Empire' Compilation for March|work=Rap-Up}}</ref>

The album's second single, "[[Senile (song)|Senile]]", was released February 14, 2014. It is by Tyga, Nicki Minaj and Lil Wayne and was produced by David D.A. Doman.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://mypinkfriday.com/news/201473 |title=Artwork: Lil Wayne, Tyga & Nicki Minaj â€“ Senile (Single) |work=mypinkfriday.com}}</ref> "Trophies" and Minaj's "[[Lookin Ass]]" were met with more chart success than the first two singles.

After years of tensions with Birdman and [[Universal Music Group]] over unpaid advances and rights to his catalogue, Lil Wayne filed for lawsuit in 2015 initially for $51 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ewn.co.za/2018/06/07/lil-wayne-receives-big-payout-from-universal|title=Lil Wayne receives big payout from Universal|date=7 June 2018|accessdate=14 September 2018}}</ref> In 2018, Lil Wayne and Universal settled, which details Lil Wayne would receive over $10 million that failed to be paid to him, and Young Money's ownership would be transferred from Cash Money to Universal directly.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/lil-wayne-is-now-the-sole-owner-of-young-money-723802/|title=Lil Wayne Talks Childhood Suicide Attempt, Owning Young Money â€“ Rolling Stone|last=Holmes|first=Charles|date=13 September 2018|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|accessdate=14 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/09/lil-wayne-young-money-sole-owner/|title=Lil Wayne Has Become the Sole Owner of Young Money - XXL|last=Burks|first=Tosten|date=13 September 2018|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|accessdate=14 September 2018}}</ref> Additionally, Birdman's 49 percent ownership in Young Money would be transferred to Wayne, which took effect on September 13, 2018.
