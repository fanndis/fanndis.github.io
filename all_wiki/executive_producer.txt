'''Executive producer''' ('''EP''') is one of the top positions in the making of a commercial entertainment product. Depending on the medium, the executive producer may be concerned with [[management accounting]] or associated with legal issues (like [[copyright]]s or [[royalties]]).<ref name=imdb>{{cite web|title=Typically an executive producer may handle business and legal issues|url=https://www.imdb.com/glossary/E#executive_producer|accessdate=10 March 2013|publisher=IMDB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130304223612/http://www.imdb.com/glossary/E#executive_producer|archive-date=4 March 2013|url-status=dead}} {{unreliable?|date=June 2020}}</ref> In films, the executive producer generally contributes to the [[Film budgeting|film's budget]] and their involvement depends on the project, with some simply securing funds and others being involved in the filmmaking process.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wisegeek.org/what-is-an-executive-producer.htm |title=What is an Executive Producer? |website=WiseGEEK |accessdate=21 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.careersinfilm.com/executive-producer/|title=Executive Producer |website=CareersinFilm.com |accessdate=16 May 2019}}</ref>

== Motion pictures ==
{{main|Film producer}}
In films, executive producers may [[film finance|finance]] the film, participate in the creative effort, or work on set. Their responsibilities vary from funding or attracting investors into the movie project to legal, [[screenplay|scripting]], marketing, advisory and supervising capacities.<ref>{{cite web |title=Executive Producer (aka Executive in Charge of Production)|url=http://creativeskillset.org/creative_industries/film/job_roles/3086_executive_producer_aka_executive_in_charge_of_production|accessdate= 12 January 2017}}</ref>

Executive producers vary in involvement, responsibility and power. Some executive producers have hands-on control over every aspect of production, some supervise the producers of a project, while others are involved in name only.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Program Doctor |url=https://programdoctor.com/what-is-an-executive-producer/}}</ref>

The crediting of executive producers in the film industry has risen over time. In the mid-to-late 1990s, there were an average of just under two executive producers per film. In 2000, the number jumped to 2.5 (more than the number of standard "film producer"). In 2013, there were an average of 4.4 executive producers per film, compared with 3.2 producers as of 2016.<ref>{{cite web |title=How many movie producers does a film need?|url=https://stephenfollows.com/how-many-movie-producers-per-film |accessdate= 4 May 2016}}</ref>

== Television ==
{{main|Television producer}}
In television, an executive producer usually supervises the creative content and the financial aspects of a production. Some writers, like [[Stephen J. Cannell]], [[Tina Fey]], and [[Ryan Murphy (writer)|Ryan Murphy]], have worked as both the creator and the producer of the same show.<ref>{{cite web|title=In television, an Executive Producer may also be the Creator/Writer of a series|url=http://www.producersguild.org/?page=faq|accessdate = 12 March 2013}}</ref> In the case of multiple executive producers on a television show, the one outranking the others is called the [[showrunner]],<ref>{{cite web|title=There can be multiple executive producers on a series, but the one in charge is called the showrunner.|url=http://filmtvcareers.about.com/od/basics/p/CP_TV_Writer.htm|accessdate = 10 March 2013}}</ref> or the leading executive producer.

==Music==
{{See also|Music executive}}
In recorded music, record labels distinguish between an executive producer and a record producer. The executive producer is responsible for business decisions and more recently, organizing the recordings along with the music producer, whereas the record producer produces the music. Sometimes the executive producer organises the recording and selects recording-related crew, such as [[sound engineer]]s and [[session musician]]s.<ref name="Discovery Channel">{{cite web|title=The executive producer usually chooses the musicians and technicians for a record, decides where and when to record, and the production budget and timetable|url=http://curiosity.discovery.com/question/what-music-executive-producer-do|accessdate=12 March 2013|archiveurl=https://archive.fo/20130410181827/http://curiosity.discovery.com/question/what-music-executive-producer-do|archivedate=10 April 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Video games ==
{{main|Video game producer}}
In the [[video game industry]], the title "executive producer" is not well-defined. It may refer to an external producer working for the [[video game publisher|publisher]], who works with the [[video game developer|developer]]s. 

For example, in 2012, [[Jay-Z]] was announced as executive producer for ''[[NBA 2K13]]''. His role consisted of appearing in an introduction, picking songs for the game's soundtrack and contributing to the design of its in-game menus "and other visual elements".<ref>{{cite news |title=Argent said Jay-Z's influence would be felt immediately in the game, starting during its video introduction. Jay-Z also handpicked the game's soundtrack |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2012/08/01/tech/gaming-gadgets/jay-z-nba2k13|accessdate = 1 August 2012 | publisher=CNN|date=1 August 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Jay-Z NBA 2K13: Rapper Announced As 'Executive Producer'|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/19079364|accessdate= 2 August 2012}}</ref>
