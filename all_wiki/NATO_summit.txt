A '''NATO summit'''  is a [[summit (meeting)|summit meeting]] that is regarded as a periodic opportunity for [[head of state|heads of state]] and [[head of government|heads of government]] of [[NATO]] member countries to evaluate and provide strategic direction for Alliance activities.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nato.int/issues/summits/index.html |title=NATO Summit Meetings |website=NATO.int |publisher=NATO |accessdate=27 September 2013 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061004140412/http://www.nato.int/issues/summits/index.html |archivedate=4 October 2006}}</ref>

NATO summits are not regular meetings like the more frequent NATO ministerial meetings, but rather are important junctures in the alliance's decision-making process on the highest level. Summits are often used to introduce new policy, invite new members into the alliance, launch major new initiatives, and build partnerships with non-NATO countries.

==List of NATO summits==
{{Expand section|date=December 2019}}

From the founding of NATO in 1949, there have been a total of thirty NATO summits; the last of which was the UK summit in Watford held in December 2019. Only the traditional summits have received an official number, thereby excluding the exceptional summit of 2001 in NATO headquarters.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nato.int/cps/en/natolive/topics_50115.htm |title=NATO Summit Meetings |website=NATO.int |publisher=NATO |date=4 October 2011 |accessdate=29 November 2011}}</ref>
