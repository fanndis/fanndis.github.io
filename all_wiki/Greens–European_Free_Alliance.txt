'''The Greens/European Free Alliance''' ('''Greens/EFA''') is the [[political group of the European Parliament]] composed primarily of [[Green politics|green]] and [[Regionalism (politics)|regionalist]] political parties.<ref name="Raunio2012">{{cite book|author=Tapio Raunio|chapter=Political Interests: The European Parliament's Party Groups|editor1=John Peterson|editor2=Michael Shackleton|title=The Institutions of the European Union|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=oaD01PaTFE0C&pg=PA341|year=2012|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-957498-8|page=341}}</ref><ref name="Staab2011">{{cite book|author=Andreas Staab|title=The European Union Explained, Second Edition: Institutions, Actors, Global Impact|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gePMlsVzZI0C&pg=PA67|year=2011|publisher=Indiana University Press|isbn=978-0-253-00164-1|page=67}}</ref><ref name="Thorlakson2013">{{cite book|author=Lori Thorlakson|chapter=Federalism and the European Party System|editor=Alexander H. Trechsel|title=Towards a Federal Europe|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=mTndAAAAQBAJ&pg=PT72|year=2013|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-1-317-99818-1|page=72}}</ref>

Formed following the [[1999 European Parliament election|1999 European elections]] for the [[Fifth European Parliament|5th European Parliament]], the Greens/EFA group now consists of three distinct [[European political party|European political parties]], namely the larger [[European Green Party]] (EGP) and the [[European Free Alliance]] (EFA)<ref>[http://www.greens-efa.eu/about-us/48-who-we-are.html "Who we are"]. Greens-efa.eu. {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120118235647/http://www.greens-efa.eu/about-us/48-who-we-are.html|date=18 January 2012}}</ref> and the smaller [[European Pirate Party]]. The EFA consists of parties representing [[stateless nations]], regionalist and [[Political parties of minorities|minority political]] interests. The group has generally limited its membership to [[Progressivism|progressive]] parties.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Gupta|first=Devashree|title=Nationalism across borders: transnational nationalist advocacy in the European Union|journal=[[Comparative European Politics]]|volume=6 | issue=1|pages=61–80|doi=10.1057/palgrave.cep.6110127|date=April 2008|ref=harv}}</ref> These European parties are joined by MEPs from non-aligned national parties, which have included the Dutch [[Europe Transparent]] (2004–2009) and the [[Pirate Party (Sweden)|Swedish]] (2009–2014), [[Pirate Party Germany|German]] (2014–) and [[Czech Pirate Party|Czech]] (2019–) [[Pirate Party|Pirate Parties]].<ref>[http://www.votewatch.eu/en/piratpartiet.html "VoteWatch Europe: European Parliament, Council of the EU"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130210153437/http://www.votewatch.eu/en/piratpartiet.html |date=10 February 2013 }}. Votewatch.eu. Retrieved 28 September 2013.</ref>
