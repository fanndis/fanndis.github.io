'''''Wisecracks''''' is a Canadian documentary film, directed by [[Gail Singer]] and released in 1991.<ref>Noel Taylor, "Wisecracks proves that women are funny too". ''[[Ottawa Citizen]]'', December 24, 1991.</ref> The film profiles a number of women who were active in [[comedy]] in the late 1980s and early 1990s, including [[Joy Behar]], [[Phyllis Diller]], [[Ellen DeGeneres]], [[Whoopi Goldberg]], [[Geri Jewell]], [[Paula Poundstone]], [[Sandra Shamas]] and [[Jenny Jones (presenter)|Jenny Jones]].<ref>Mark Bastien, "Funny bone is Canadian film-maker's new target". ''[[Montreal Gazette]]'', September 9, 1991.</ref>

The film premiered at the [[1991 Toronto International Film Festival|1991 Festival of Festivals]]. Singer's comedy film ''[[True Confections]]'' was also screened at the same festival, making her the first filmmaker in the festival's history to have both a documentary and a narrative fiction film screened at the festival in the same year.<ref>Craig MacInnis, "Singer: Cracking wise and felling lies". ''[[Toronto Star]]'', September 2, 1991.</ref>

The film subsequently received a [[Genie Award]] nomination for [[Canadian Screen Award for Best Feature Length Documentary|Best Feature Length Documentary]] at the [[13th Genie Awards]] in 1992.<ref>H. J. Kirchhoff, "French-Canadian films steal Genie show: Cronenberg's Naked Lunch leads the pack with 11 nominations". ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', October 14, 1992.</ref>
