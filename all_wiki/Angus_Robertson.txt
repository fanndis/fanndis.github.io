'''Angus Struan Carolus Robertson''' (born 28 September 1969) is a Scottish politician who served as <!--"DEPUTE" is the official spelling-->Depute Leader of the [[Scottish National Party]] (SNP) from 2016 to 2018 and Leader of the SNP in the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|British House of Commons]] from 2007 to 2017. He served as [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Moray (UK Parliament constituency)|Moray]] from 2001 to 2017.

A graduate of the [[University of Aberdeen]], Robertson previously worked as a [[journalist]]. He was first elected to the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] in [[2001 United Kingdom general election|2001]]. In 2017, he sought re-election as the [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|MP]] for [[Moray (UK Parliament constituency)|Moray]] and lost to the [[Scottish Conservative]] candidate, [[Douglas Ross (Scottish politician)|Douglas Ross]].<ref name="deputy">{{cite news|title=Angus Robertson named as SNP deputy leader|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-37640935|accessdate=13 October 2016|work=BBC News|date=13 October 2016}}</ref> He was succeeded as SNP Westminster Leader by [[Ian Blackford]]. 

Robertson resigned as SNP Depute Leader in February 2018, before launching the pro-independence think tank [[Progress Scotland]] in 2019, alongside Mark Diffley.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/17405933.marianne-taylor-the-moral-case-for-independence-is-clear-but-its-a-hard-sell-economically/|title=Marianne Taylor: The moral case for independence is clear, but it's a hard sell economically|website=HeraldScotland}}</ref>

==Early life and career==
Robertson was born in [[Wimbledon, London|Wimbledon]], London, to a Scottish father, Struan, who was an engineer, and a German mother, Anna, who was a nurse. Robertson was brought up in [[Edinburgh]] and speaks fluent German. He was educated at [[Broughton High School (Edinburgh)|Broughton High School]], Edinburgh and the [[University of Aberdeen]], where he graduated in 1991 with an [[Master of Arts (Scotland)|MA]] [[Honours degree]] in [[politics]] and [[international relations]].  After university he embarked on a [[journalism|journalistic career]], and worked as a foreign and diplomatic correspondent in Central Europe for the [[BBC World Service]], and then for the Austrian Broadcasting Corporation [[Österreichischer Rundfunk|ORF]].{{citation needed|date=November 2019}}

==Political activity and career==
Robertson joined the [[Scottish National Party]] in 1984, at the age of 15, after being given a leaflet about the party's youth wing by Charlie Reid of [[The Proclaimers]]. He was the European and International Affairs Adviser to the SNP Group in the [[Scottish Parliament]]. 

Robertson was first elected to the UK [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] in June 2001, representing the [[Moray (UK Parliament constituency)|Moray]] constituency. During his first [[parliamentary session]], Robertson was Scotland's youngest MP and was rated Scotland's "hardest working MP" according to statistics from the House of Commons.<ref>[http://www.johnsmithmemorialtrust.org/web/site/home/Angus_Robertson.asp ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090106124708/http://www.johnsmithmemorialtrust.org/web/site/home/Angus_Robertson.asp |date=6 January 2009 }}</ref> He was a member of the [[European Scrutiny Committee]] from 2001 to 2010, and served as the SNP's spokesman on [[defense (military)|Defence]] and [[International Relations]]. Robertson was well above average amongst MPs in the number of contributions he made in the House of Commons.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theyworkforyou.com/mp/angus_robertson/moray#numbers |title=Angus Robertson MP, Moray |publisher=TheyWorkForYou.com |date=2015-05-27 |accessdate=2015-06-01}}</ref> In January 2006, Robertson provided Swiss Senator [[Dick Marty]] a report containing what he calls 'a detailed report of numerous [[extraordinary rendition|suspect movements of aircraft]] transiting through Scotland.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/usa/story/0,,1693657,00.html |title=European governments 'knew of' CIA flights &#124; US news |publisher=Guardian.co.uk |accessdate=2015-06-01}}</ref>

In May 2007, he became SNP Leader in the House of Commons, following [[Alex Salmond]]'s election as [[First Minister of Scotland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://politics.guardian.co.uk/commons/story/0,,2086346,00.html |title=Robertson elected SNP's Westminster leader &#124; Politics &#124; The Guardian |publisher=Politics.guardian.co.uk |accessdate=2015-06-01}}</ref> Following the [[2015 United Kingdom general election|2015 general election]] and the election of Salmond as MP for [[Gordon (UK Parliament constituency)|Gordon]], it was confirmed that he would continue in his role as leader in the Commons. In September 2015, he was appointed to the [[Privy Council of the United Kingdom|Privy Council]] and as a member of the [[Intelligence and Security Committee of Parliament]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.snp.org/media-centre/news/2015/sep/westminster-snp-leader-appointed-privy-council |title=Westminster SNP leader appointed Privy Council |publisher=snp.org |date=2015-09-09 |accessdate=2015-10-11 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925043031/http://www.snp.org/media-centre/news/2015/sep/westminster-snp-leader-appointed-privy-council |archive-date=25 September 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/politics/political_news/13655829.SNP_s_Angus_Robertson_to_become_member_of_House_of_Commons_Intelligence_and_Security_Committee/?ref=rss |title=SNP's Angus Robertson to become member of House of Commons Intelligence and Security Committee |publisher=The Herald |date=2015-09-09 |accessdate=2015-10-11}}</ref>

In January 2016, Robertson said that British Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] should admit to British involvement in [[Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen]]: "Isn't it time for the Prime Minister to admit that Britain is effectively taking part in a war in Yemen that is costing thousands of civilians lives and he has not sought parliamentary approval to do this?"<ref>"[https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/david-cameron-accused-of-silently-taking-britain-into-saudi-arabia-s-brutal-war-in-yemen-a6822811.html David Cameron accused of silently taking Britain into Saudi Arabia’s war in Yemen]". ''The Independent.'' 20 January 2016.</ref>

On 13 October 2016, he was elected Depute Leader of the SNP, replacing [[Stewart Hosie]]. Robertson received 52.5% of the votes, defeating [[Tommy Sheppard]] (25.5%), [[Alyn Smith]] (18.6%) and Chris McEleny (3.3%) in the election.<ref name="deputy"/> He resigned in February 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-42931413|title=Robertson quits as SNP deputy leader|date=3 February 2018|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

He lost his parliamentary seat to the Conservatives in the [[2017 United Kingdom general election|June 2017 UK general election]].<ref name = central/> He subsequently established and ran ''Progress Scotland'', a pro-independence think-tank.<ref name = central/> 

In February 2020, Robertson announced his intention to contest the [[Edinburgh Central (Scottish Parliament constituency)|Edinburgh Central]] constituency in the [[next Scottish Parliament election]] (due to be held in May 2021).<ref name = central>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-51545141 |title=Former SNP MP Angus Robertson to run for Holyrood in 2021 |website=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=18 February 2020 |accessdate=18 February 2020}}</ref> He won selection ahead of [[Marco Biagi (politician)|Marco Biagi]], a former MSP for the area.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-54849589 |title=SNP select former MP Angus Robertson for Edinburgh Central seat |website=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=7 November 2020 |accessdate=7 November 2020}}</ref>

===Expenses claims===
In 2015, ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' reported that Robertson's second home expenses had included a television costing £1,119, a £400 [[home cinema]] system, £500 for a bed, £20 for a corkscrew and £2,324 for a sofa bed.<ref name="telegraph1">{{cite web|last=Watt |first=Holly |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/newstopics/mps-expenses/5314297/SNPs-Angus-Robertson-claims-80000-for-second-home-MPs-expenses.html |title=SNP's Angus Robertson claims £80,000 for second home: MPs' expenses |publisher=Telegraph.co.uk |date=2015-05-04 |accessdate=2015-06-01}}</ref> The home cinema system was initially denied by the expenses office; however, Robertson appealed this decision and it was subsequently awarded.

In 2017 it was reported in several newspapers that Robertson had sold his second home in London, the mortgage on which was paid on expenses, as part of his divorce settlement. Robertson had previously pledged to repay the value of the expenses on the property and donate any profit to charity.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://insidemoray.com/2017/05/robertson-denies-any-wrongdoing-over-london-flat/ |title=Robertson denies any wrongdoing over London flat| publisher=Inside Moray| date=2017-05-07 | accessdate=2020-10-05}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Robertson's wife, Jennifer Dempsie, is a former advisor to [[Alex Salmond]]. Dempsie campaigned to inherit Salmond's [[Scottish Parliament]] seat in [[Aberdeenshire East (Scottish Parliament constituency)|Aberdeenshire East]]<ref>{{cite web|last=Gordon |first=Tom |url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/news/13413786.Salmond_s_former_special_adviser_set_to_inherit_his_seat/ |title=Salmond's former special adviser set to inherit his seat |publisher=The Herald |date=21 June 2015 }}</ref> but withdrew to focus on her business career.<ref>{{cite news|last=Whitaker |first=Andrew |url=http://www.scotsman.com/what-s-on/music/fiona-hyslop-to-appear-before-msps-over-titp-funding-1-3886801 |title=Fiona Hyslop to appear before MSPs over TITP funding |work=The Scotsman |date=15 September 2015 }}</ref>

Outside politics Robertson is a music fan, and particularly likes [[Metallica]] and [[Belle and Sebastian]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/2015/07/10/angus-robertson-interview_n_7768626.html|title=Angus Robertson Interview: SNP Westminster Leader On Devolution, Independence, Greece...and Metallica|publisher=huffingtonpost.co.uk |date=2015-07-10 |accessdate=2015-07-11}}</ref> He is a supporter of the [[Heart of Midlothian F.C.|Heart of Midlothian F.C]] football team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/AngusRobertson/status/734054810006237185|title=Congrats to @HibsOfficial for a well deserved victory. Like many other Hearts fans I'm delighted for all Hibs supporters. #ScottishCupFinal|first=Angus|last=Robertson|date=21 May 2016}}</ref>
