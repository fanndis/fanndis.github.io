'''''The Daily Star''''' is the largest circulating daily English-language newspaper in [[Bangladesh]].<ref>[http://www.warc.com/LandingPages/Data/NewspaperTrends/PDF/Bangladesh.pdf Newspaper Trends: Bangladesh], World Advertising Research Center. Retrieved 14 September 2007</ref> Founded by [[Syed Mohammad Ali|Syed Mohammed Ali]] on 14 January 1991, as Bangladesh transitioned and restored [[parliamentary democracy]], ''The Daily Star'' emerged as a leading and influential national [[newspaper of record]].<ref>{{cite news |date=17 October 2007 |title=Remembering Syed Mohammad Ali |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/newDesign/news-details.php?nid=7686 |newspaper=The Daily Star}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.southbound.com.my/media/ali-cn.htm |title=SM Ali's World |website=Southbound.com.my |access-date=25 December 2016}}</ref>

Its motto, "Committed to People's Right to Know", appears on the [[above the fold]] middle section of the front page. The newspaper is led by [[Mahfuz Anam]], a member of [[Mukti Bahini]],<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Lalwani |first=Nikita |last2=Winter-Levy |first2=Sam |date=20 October 2016 |title=A Daughter of Bangladeshi Revolutionaries Makes Sense of Life After War |url=https://www.newyorker.com/books/page-turner/tahmima-anam-trilogy-of-life-after-war-in-bangladesh |magazine=The New Yorker |access-date=2018-02-12}}</ref> and former United Nations official.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.culturebase.net/artist.php?1271 |title=Mahfuz Anam artist portrait |website=culturebase.net |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070203100431/http://culturebase.net/artist.php?1271 |archive-date=3 February 2007 |access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref> Its principal bureaus are located in [[Dhaka]] and [[Chittagong]], with several representatives abroad. It is organized into sections: News, Opinion and Editorial, Star Business, Star Sports, Arts and Entertainment, National and Metropolitan. Its major supplements include ''[[The Star (Bangladesh)|The Star]]'', ''[[Forum (Bangladesh)|Forum]]'', ''Lifestyle'', ''Star Literature'', ''Law & Our Rights'', ''Shout'', ''[[Star Insight]]'' and ''Science and Life''.

''The Daily Star'' is owned by Mediaworld, in which a major share is held by Latifur Rahman's [[Transcom Group]].<ref>[http://www.transcombd.com/templatecontact.php Transcom Group]. Retrieved 14 September 2007</ref> The Transcom Group also owns ''[[Prothom Alo]]'', one of the most widely read [[Bengali language|Bengali]] newspapers in the world.<ref name=probe>{{cite news |date=14 September 2006 |title=The new generation of journalists has changed the face of the media |url=http://www.probenewsmagazine.com/index.php?contentId=216&index=2 |newspaper=Probe News Magazine |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071223080815/http://www.probenewsmagazine.com/index.php?contentId=216&index=2 |archive-date=23 December 2007 |access-date=14 September 2007}}</ref>

==Management==
[[Mahfuz Anam]], editor and publisher of ''The Daily Star'', is also on its six-member board of directors, of which [[Rokeya Afzal Rahman]] is chairman. The board deals with financial matters.<ref name="DStar">{{cite web |url=http://www.thedailystar.net/aboutus.htm |title=About Us |website=The Daily Star |access-date=6 September 2007}}</ref>

The general section of the newspaper is headed by Salehuddin Ahmed,<ref name="DStar"/> its managing editor, while the news team is led by Syed Ashfaqul Haque, [[Editing#Periodicals|chief news editor]].<ref name=media>[http://www.mediabangladesh.net/eng-daily.php English Daily: The Daily Star], Media Bangladesh; ''Retrieved: 2007-09-14''</ref> Ahmed Ali is in charge of the Metro. Other leaders are AFM Jamaluddin (establishment manager), Mizanur Rahman (accounts manager), Selim S.H. Chowdhury (production and events manager), and Sher Ali (advertising manager).<ref name=media/>

==Online version==
The online version of ''The Daily Star'' as a leading English daily newspaper of Bangladesh is updating 24/7 with latest, breaking, politics, business, technology, world, entertainment, sports, lifestyle and crime news.{{Citation needed|date=October 2016}}

==Pages, magazines and supplements==
[[File:star magazine.jpg|thumb|right|''[[The Star (Bangladesh)|The Star Magazine]]'']]

''The Daily Star'' publishes a number of [[supplement (publishing)|supplements]] along with its 24-page main publication.
* '''''[[The Star (Bangladesh)|The Star]]''''', a magazine-size weekly, is published every Friday.
* '''''Lifestyle''''', a weekly fashion and lifestyle [[Tabloid (newspaper format)|tabloid]], is published every Tuesday.
* '''''[[Star Showbiz]]''''', Weekly entertainment tabloid, focused on local international movie, music and TV.
* '''''Shout''''', a weekly tabloid-size supplement for teenagers, is published every week.
* '''''Bytes''''' Technology page
* '''''Next Step'''''
* '''''Shift'''''
* '''''Law and Our Rights''''', a weekly supplement on legal issues, published every Saturday.

===Previous publications===
* '''''RisingStars''''', a weekly tabloid-size supplement for teenagers, was published every Thursday.
* '''''Star Campus''''' was a university-based supplement of the paper. It was published every Sunday. Now publication closed. [defunct].
* '''''[[Forum (Bangladesh)|Forum]]''''', a monthly magazine, edited by Shah Husain Imam, is published with the newspaper on the first Monday of the month [defunct].
* '''''[[Star Insight]]''''', page 12, was published every Wednesday.

==Controversy==
In 2016, speaking at a panel discussion on [[ATN News]], editor [[Mahfuz Anam]] admitted that ''The Daily Star'' ran stories fed to the newspaper by military intelligence outfit [[DGFI]] in 2007 and 2008. The stories accused eventual prime minister [[Sheikh Hasina]] of corruption.<ref name="bdnews24.com">{{cite news |url=http://bdnews24.com/media-en/2016/02/04/daily-star-editor-mahfuz-anam-admits-to-publishing-dgfi-fed-baseless-stories |title=Daily Star Editor Mahfuz Anam admits to publishing DGFI-fed baseless stories |work=bdnews24.com |access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref> This led to demands in [[Jatiya Sangsad|parliament]] by several lawmakers calling for the trial of Mahfuz Anam and ''The Daily Star'' publisher Latifur Rahman.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lawmakers demand trial of Daily Star editor Mahfuz, publisher Latifur |url=http://www.observerbd.com/2016/02/07/135259.php |work=The Daily Observer |date=7 February 2016 |access-date=8 February 2016}}</ref>

===Website blocked===
The online website was blocked on 1 June 2018 without any notice by [[Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission]] (BTRC). It was unblocked after 18 hours. BTRC didn't explain why it took the action, but the media and public of Bangladesh thought that as the media ran a story regarding alleged killing of Teknaf municipal councillor Akramul Haque the authority had taken the steps.<ref>{{cite news |title=Star blocked, unblocked |url=https://www.thedailystar.net/frontpage/star-blocked-unblocked-1585441 |work=The Daily Star |date=3 June 2018 |access-date=23 August 2018}}</ref>
