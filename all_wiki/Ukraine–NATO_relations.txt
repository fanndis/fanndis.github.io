==History of relations==
[[Image:Ukrainian_BTR-80.jpg|thumb|Ukrainian [[armored personnel carriers|APC]] along a highway from [[Al Kut]] to [[Al-Suwaira|As Suwayrah]] in [[Iraq]] (late 2003)]]
[[File:Defense.gov News Photo 051024-F-5586B-016.jpg|thumb|Secretary of Defense [[Donald Rumsfeld]] and [[Victoria Nuland]] at the NATO–Ukraine consultations in Vilnius, Lithuania, on October 24, 2005]]
[[File:Feodosiya protest 2006.jpg|thumb|right|The 2006 [[Crimean anti-NATO protests of 2006|anti-NATO protests]] in [[Feodosiya]]]]
Relations officially began when Ukraine became the first [[Commonwealth of Independent States|CIS]] country to enter [[North Atlantic Treaty Organization|NATO's]] [[Partnership for Peace]] program in February 1994.<ref name="nato.int"/><ref name="Serhy">[[Serhy Yekelchyk]] ''"Ukraine: Birth of a Modern Nation"'', [[Oxford University Press]] (2007), {{ISBN|978-0-19-530546-3}}</ref> In the summer of 1995 NATO stepped up to help to mitigate consequences of the [[1995 Kharkiv Drinking Water Disaster|Kharkiv Drinking Water Disaster]]. This was the first cooperation between NATO and Ukraine.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nato.int/cps/en/natolive/topics_49158.htm |title=Civil preparedness |authors=<!--Not stated--> |date=7 June 2018 |website=nato.int |access-date=5 October 2019}}</ref> On May 7, 1997 the first-ever official NATO Information and Documentation Center opened in Kyiv, aimed to foster transparency about the alliance.<ref>Gerald B. H. Solomon, Center for Strategic and International Studies. '' The NATO enlargement debate, 1990–1997'', 1998, p. 120</ref> A Ukrainian public opinion poll of May 6 showed 37% in favor of joining NATO with 28% opposed and 34% undecided.<ref>Solomon, Center for Strategic and International Studies, p. 121</ref> On July 9, 1997, a NATO-Ukraine Commission was established.<ref>[http://www.nato.int/issues/nuc/index.html NATO-Ukraine Commission]</ref> In 2002 relations with the government of the [[United States]] and other NATO countries deteriorated after one of the recordings made during the [[Cassette Scandal]] revealed an alleged transfer of a sophisticated Ukrainian defense system to [[Saddam Hussein]]'s [[Ba'athist Iraq|Iraq]].<ref name="Serhy"/> At the NATO enlargement [[Summit (meeting)|summit]] in November 2002, the NATO–Ukraine commission adopted a Ukraine–NATO Action Plan. [[President of Ukraine|President]] [[Kuchma]]'s declaration that Ukraine wanted to join NATO (also in 2002) and the sending of [[Multinational force in Iraq#List of nations in the coalition|Ukrainian troops to Iraq]] in 2003<ref name="Serhy"/> could not mend relations between Kuchma and NATO.<ref name="Serhy"/> Currently, the [[Ukrainian Armed Forces]] are working with [[NATO]] in [[Iraq]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mil.gov.ua/index.php?lang=en&part=peacekeeping&sub=iraq |title=Armed Forces of Ukraine, Ukrainian peacekeeping personnel as a part of Multinational forces in Iraq (from Dec 2005) and NATO Training Mission in Iraq (NTM-I) (from Feb 2006) |publisher=Mil.gov.ua |date= |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref>

After the [[Orange Revolution]] in 2004 Kuchma was replaced by President [[Viktor Yushchenko]] who is a keen supporter of Ukraine's NATO membership.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7312045.stm |title=Europe &#124; Medvedev warns on Nato expansion |publisher=BBC News |date=2008-03-25 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> In January 2008 the second [[Yulia Tymoshenko]] [[government of Ukraine|cabinet]]'s proposal for Ukraine to join [[NATO]]'s [[Membership Action Plan]] was met with opposition. A [[petition]] of over 2 million signatures has called for a referendum on Ukraine's membership proposal to join NATO. The opposition have called for a national referendum to be held on any steps towards further involvement with NATO. In February 2008 57.8% of Ukrainians supported the idea of a national referendum on joining NATO, against 38.6% in February 2007.<ref>[http://www.interfax.com.ua/en/press-center/press-conference/106882/ Half of Ukrainians opposed to Ukraine's membership of NATO, poll indicates – Interfax Ukraine] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080604014018/http://www.interfax.com.ua/en/press-center/press-conference/106882/ |date=June 4, 2008 }}</ref>

===Ukrainian bid to join the NATO Membership Action Plan===
In January 2008, US [[United States Senate|Senator]] [[Richard Lugar]] said: "Ukrainian President Viktor Yushchenko, Prime Minister Yulia Tymoshenko and [[Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada|Parliamentary Chairman]] [[Arseniy Yatsenyuk]] have signed the statement calling for consideration on Ukraine's entry into the NATO membership action plan at the [[2008 Bucharest summit|Bucharest summit]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-231009.html |title=Ukraine asks to join NATO membership action plan : Ukraine News by UNIAN |publisher=Unian.net |date=2008-01-16 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref>

The Ukrainian parliament headed by [[Arseniy Yatsenyuk]] was unable to hold its regular parliamentary session following the decision of the Parliamentary Opposition to prevent the parliament from functioning in a protest against joining [[NATO]]. The parliament was blocked from January 25, 2008<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-232630.html |title=Party of Regions blocks rostrum in parliament : Ukraine News by UNIAN |publisher=Unian.net |date=2008-01-25 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> till March 4, 2008 (on 29 February 2008 faction leaders agreed on a protocol of mutual understanding).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-238859.html |title=Verkhovna Rada to resume work on Tuesday – Yatseniuk : Ukraine News by UNIAN |publisher=Unian.net |date=2008-02-29 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> [[US President]] [[George W. Bush]] and both nominees for President of the United States in the [[2008 United States presidential election|2008 election]], U.S. senator [[Barack Obama]] and U.S. senator [[John McCain]], did offer backing to Ukraine's membership of NATO.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7322830.stm | work=BBC News | title=Bush to back Ukraine's Nato hopes | date=April 1, 2008 | accessdate=May 26, 2010}}</ref><ref>[http://obama.senate.gov/press/080128-obama_statement_118/ Obama Statement on Ukraine's Commitment to Join NATO] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080625233932/http://obama.senate.gov/press/080128-obama_statement_118/ |date=2008-06-25 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nysun.com/national/mccain-backs-tougher-line-against-russia/73750/ |title=McCain Backs Tougher Line Against Russia – March 27, 2008 – The New York Sun |publisher=Nysun.com |date= |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> Russian reactions were negative.

At the [[2008 Bucharest summit|NATO summit]] in Bucharest in April 2008, NATO decided it would not yet offer membership to [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] and Ukraine; nevertheless, Nato Secretary General [[Jaap de Hoop Scheffer]] said that Georgia and Ukraine would eventually become members.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7328276.stm | work=[[BBC News]] | title=Nato denies Georgia and Ukraine | date=April 3, 2008 | accessdate=May 26, 2010}}</ref> Resistance was reportedly met from [[France]] and [[Germany]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/91750/ | work=[[Kyiv Post]] | title=At key time, French resist NATO membership for Ukraine, Georgia | date=November 30, 2010 | accessdate=November 30, 2010}}</ref>

In November 2008, Germany's Chancellor [[Angela Merkel]], Prime-Minister Yulia Tymoshenko and former Ukrainian minister of defence [[Anatoliy Hrytsenko|Anatolii Hrytsenko]] doubted Ukraine would be granted membership of MAP in December 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ukranews.com/eng/article/162006.html |title=Ukrainian News |publisher=Ukranews.com |accessdate=2014-04-04 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140407102404/http://www.ukranews.com/eng/article/162006.html |archivedate=April 7, 2014 }}</ref> In a ''[[The Times|Times of London]]'' interview in late November, President Yushchenko stated : "Ukraine has done everything it had to do. We are devoted to this pace. Everything else is an issue of political will of those allies who represent NATO."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-285767.html |title=European media publish interviews with President Yushchenko : UNIAN news |publisher=Unian.net |date=2008-11-21 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> Although NATO Deputy Assistant Secretary-General [[Aurelia Bouchez]]{{#tag:ref|During a NATO conference in [[Hungarian Parliament|Hungarian parliament]] on 20 November, she said "We should not make a choice between NATO enlargement and Russia as we need both."<ref>[http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-285669.html NATO official: NATO needs both enlargement, Russia], [[UNIAN]] (20 November 2008)</ref>|group=nb}} and [[Secretary General of NATO|NATO's Secretary General]], [[Jaap de Hoop Scheffer]]{{#tag:ref|He told a conference in [[Spain]]: "The emergence of independent states within the former Soviet space is a reality. The ability of these states to determine their own future is a litmus test for the new Europe. Do we have to choose between good relations with Russia and further enlargement? My answer is no – we will not choose, will not sacrifice one for the other. It would bring new dividing lines."<ref>[http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-285528.html Don’t turn deaf ear to Ukraine Nato bid, Viktor Yushchenko begs allies], [[UNIAN]] (20 November 2008)</ref>|group=nb}} still supported Ukraine's NATO bid at the time the [[George W. Bush administration|Bush administration]] seemed not to push for [[NATO-Georgia relations|Georgian]] and Ukrainian membership of MAP late November 2008.{{#tag:ref|[[Condoleezza Rice]] told a press conference, "We believe that the NATO-Georgia Commission and the NATO-Ukraine Commission can be the bodies with which we intensify our dialogue and our activities. And, therefore, there does not need at this point in time to be any discussion of MAP."<ref>[http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-286985.html US gives way on Nato for Georgia and Ukraine - FT], [[UNIAN]] (27 November 2008)</ref>|group=nb}} [[President of Russia]] [[Dmitry Medvedev]] responded that "reason has prevailed".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-287102.html |title=Russia happy US dropped push for new NATO members : UNIAN news |publisher=Unian.net |date=2008-11-28 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> On December 3, 2008 NATO decided it will work out an Annual National Programme of providing assistance to Ukraine to implement reforms required to accede the alliance without referring to MAP.<ref name="nomap">[http://www.ukranews.com/eng/article/166745.html NATO To Work Out National Programme Of Assistance To Ukraine For Acceding Alliance], [[Ukrainian News Agency]] (December 3, 2008)]</ref>

At the NATO-Ukraine consultations at the level of Defense Ministers held at the NATO headquarters in [[Brussels]] in November 2009, NATO Secretary General Anders Fogh Rasmussen praised Ukraine's first Annual National Program, which outlined the steps it intended to take to accelerate internal reform and alignment with Euro-Atlantic standards, as an important step on Ukraine's path to becoming a member of the Alliance.<ref>[http://www.interfax.com.ua/eng/main/25088/ NATO Secretary General says Annual National Program is important step on Ukraine's path to membership in Alliance], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (November 16, 2009)</ref>

===Yanukovych Presidency (2010–2014)===
Candidate during the [[2010 Ukrainian presidential election|2010 presidential election]] and [[Party of Regions]] leader [[Viktor Yanukovych]] stated during 2010 presidential election-campaign that the current level of Ukraine's cooperation with NATO was sufficient and that the question of the country's accession to the alliance was therefore not urgent.<ref name="theworldreporter.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.theworldreporter.com/2011/10/europe-or-russia-whom-will-ukraine.html|title=Europe or Russia? Whom will Ukraine Choose?|work=The World Reporter|publisher=|accessdate=2011-10-21}}</ref><ref>[http://www.interfax.com.ua/eng/main/29568/ Yanukovych describes current level of Ukraine's cooperation with NATO as sufficient], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (January 12, 2010)</ref>

Following the election, on 14 February 2010, the newly elected President Viktor Yanukovych said that Ukraine's relations with NATO were currently "well-defined", and that there was "no question of Ukraine joining NATO". He said the issue of Ukrainian membership of NATO might "emerge at some point, but we will not see it in the immediate future."<ref name="glo">[http://www.globalsecurity.org/wmd/library/news/ukraine/2010/ukraine-100213-rianovosti02.htm Yanukovych opens door to Russian navy keeping base in Ukraine] GlobalSecurity.org Retrieved on March 09, 2010</ref>

On 1 March 2010, during his visit to [[Brussels]], Yanukovych said that there would be no change to Ukraine's status as a member of the alliance's outreach program.<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/60720/ Ukraine's Yanukovych: EU ties a 'key priority'], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (March 1, 2010)</ref> He later reiterated during a trip to [[Moscow]] that Ukraine would remain a "European, non-aligned state."<ref>[http://www.thenews.com.pk/daily_detail.asp?id=227513 Ukraine vows new page in ties with Russia] Retrieved on March 09, 2010</ref>

As of May 2010, NATO and Ukraine continued to cooperate in the frame of the ''Annual National Program'',<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/67634/ NATO considers Ukraine's behavior 'unprecedented'], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (May 25, 2010)</ref> including [[Military exercise|joint exercises]].<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/67694/ NATO: Euro-Atlantic prospects a sovereign right of Ukraine], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (May 26, 2010)</ref> According to Ukraine the continuation of Ukraine-NATO cooperation does not exclude the development of a strategic [[Russia–Ukraine relations|partnership with Russia]].<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/67685/ Havrysh: Ukraine-NATO cooperation not excluding strategic partnership between Moscow, Kyiv], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (May 26, 2010)</ref>

On 27 May 2010, Yanukovych stated that he considered Ukraine's relations with NATO as a partnership, "And Ukraine can't live without this [partnership], because Ukraine is a large country".<ref name="NATOlove">[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/67830/ Yanukovych: Ukraine currently not ready to join NATO], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (May 27, 2010)</ref>

On 3 June 2010, the Ukrainian parliament passed a bill proposed by the President that excluded the goal of "integration into Euro-Atlantic security and NATO membership" from the country's national security strategy.<ref name="radanonato">[http://euobserver.com/24/30212 Ukraine drops NATO membership bid], [[EUobserver]] (June 6, 2010)</ref> The law precluded Ukraine's membership of any military bloc, but allowed for co-operation with alliances such as NATO.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/10229626.stm Ukraine's parliament votes to abandon Nato ambitions], [[BBC News]] (June 3, 2010)</ref> "[[Ukraine and the European Union|European integration]]" is still part of Ukraine's national security strategy.<ref name=radanonato/>

On 24 June 2010, the [[Ukrainian Cabinet of Ministers]] approved an action plan to implement an annual national program of cooperation with NATO in 2010.<ref name="NATO2010">[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/70823/ Cabinet approves action plan for annual national program of cooperation with NATO in 2010], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (June 24, 2010)</ref> This included:<ref name=NATO2010/>
* Involvement of Ukrainian aviation and transport material in the transportation of cargo and personnel of the armed forces of NATO's member states and partners participating in NATO-led peacekeeping missions and operations
* The continuation of Ukraine's participation in a [[Kosovo Force|peacekeeping operation in Kosovo]]
* Possible reinforcing of Ukraine's [[International Security Assistance Force|peacekeeping contingents in Afghanistan]] and [[NATO Training Mission – Iraq|Iraq]]
* Ukraine's participation in a number of international events organized by NATO
* Training of Ukrainian troops in the structures of [[NATO members]]

Ukraine and NATO continued to hold joint seminars and joint tactical and strategical exercises and operations during Yanukovych Presidency.<ref name="securityexer">[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/124954/ Ukraine, NATO to hold security exercises during Euro 2012], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (26 March 2012)<br />[http://www.interfax.co.uk/ukraine-news/yanukovych-signs-decree-on-ukraine-nato-annual-cooperation-programs/ Yanukovych signs decree on Ukraine-NATO annual cooperation programs], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (12 June 2013)<br />[http://www.euronews.com/nocomment/2011/08/04/military-manoeuvres-in-ukraine/ Military manoeuvres in Ukraine] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131016010439/http://www.euronews.com/nocomment/2011/08/04/military-manoeuvres-in-ukraine/ |date=2013-10-16 }}, [[Euronews]] (4 August 2011)<br />{{cite web |url=http://en.rian.ru/military_news/20130222/179631923.html |title=Ukraine Joins NATO's Counter-Piracy Operation |first=John |last=Thys |agency=[[RIA Novosti]] |date=22 February 2013 |accessdate=26 April 2013 }}</ref>

===Euromaidan and beyond===
Yanukovych fled Ukraine amid the [[Euromaidan]] uprising in February 2014.<ref name="result of Russia UNR" /> As a result of this [[2014 Ukrainian revolution|revolution]], the interim [[First Yatsenyuk Government|Yatsenyuk Government]] came to power in Ukraine.<ref name= "IU26214GNU">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/193035.html Maidan nominates Yatseniuk for prime minister], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (26 February 2014)</ref><ref>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/193222.html Ukrainian parliament endorses new cabinet], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 February 2014)</ref> The Yatsenyuk Government initially stated it did not have the intention of making Ukraine a member of NATO.<ref name = "NNUIU29314"/>

[[File:Poroschenko Merkel and Biden Security Conference February 2015.jpg|thumb|[[Petro Poroshenko]] with German Chancellor [[Angela Merkel]] and Vice President of the United States [[Joe Biden]], February 2015]]
NATO officials vowed support for Ukraine and worked to downplay tensions between the bloc and [[Russia]], which refused to recognize the impeachment of Yanukovych or the Yatsenyuk Government.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2014/02/27/us-ukraine-crisis-breedlove-idUSBREA1Q1F220140227|title=NATO commander plays down tension with Russia over Ukraine|date=27 February 2014|accessdate=27 February 2014|agency=Reuters}}</ref> In late February 2014, [[Anders Fogh Rasmussen]], [[Secretary General of NATO]], reaffirmed that NATO membership is still an option for Ukraine.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.euronews.com/2014/02/26/door-to-nato-remains-open-for-ukraine/|agency=Euronews|title=Door to NATO remains open for Ukraine|date=26 February 2014|accessdate=27 February 2014}}</ref>

On 29 August 2014, following reports that the [[2014 Russian military intervention in Ukraine|Russian military was operating within Ukraine]], Ukrainian Prime Minister [[Arseny Yatseniuk]] announced that he would ask the Ukrainian parliament to put Ukraine on a path towards NATO membership.<ref>{{cite news|title= Ukraine crisis: PM Yatsenyuk to seek Nato membership |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-28978699 |accessdate= 29 August 2014 | work = BBC News |date= 29 August 2014}}</ref> The government has also signaled that it hopes for [[major non-NATO ally]] status with the [[United States]], NATO's largest military power and contributor.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.kyivpost.com/content/ukraine/ukraine-hopes-for-us-assistance-in-granting-ukraine-major-non-nato-ally-status-362449.html |agency=Kyiv Post |title= Ukraine hopes for US assistance in granting Ukraine major non-NATO ally status |date= 28 August 2014|accessdate=29 August 2014}}</ref> As part of these efforts, and to rule out future Ukrainian membership in the [[Eurasian Economic Union]] and other Russian-led supranational entities, Yatseniuk also submitted a draft law to repeal Ukraine's non-bloc status previously instituted by Yanukovych.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://en.itar-tass.com/world/747206|agency=ITAR-TASS|title=Ukraine to initiate cancellation of Ukraine's non-bloc status, seek NATO membership|date=29 August 2014|accessdate=29 August 2014}}</ref> Following [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election|parliamentary elections]] in October 2014, the [[second Yatsenyuk Government|new government]] made joining NATO a priority.<ref name= "rferl.org" />

[[File:Soldiers of 6th Squadron, 8th Cavalry Regiment, 2nd Infantry Brigade Combat Team, 3rd Infantry Division train Ukrainian Soldiers on trigger squeeze.jpg|thumb|left|Soldiers of 6th Squadron, 8th Cavalry Regiment, 2nd Infantry Brigade Combat Team, 3rd Infantry Division train Ukrainian Soldiers on trigger squeeze during a 2016 drill.]]
On 23 December 2014, the Ukrainian parliament renounced Ukraine's [[Country neutrality (international relations)|non-aligned status]], a step harshly condemned by Russia.<ref name="result of Russia UNR"/><ref name="wsjends">[https://www.wsj.com/articles/ukraine-ends-nonaligned-status-earning-quick-rebuke-from-russia-1419339226 Ukraine Ends ‘Nonaligned’ Status, Earning Quick Rebuke From Russia], [[The Wall Street journal]] (23 December 2014)</ref> The new law states that Ukraine's previous non-aligned status "proved to be ineffective in guaranteeing Ukraine's security and protecting the A number of military exercises were planned between NATO members and Ukraine in 2015.<ref>http://ukraineunderattack.org/en/poroshenko-approved-plan-for-multinational-exercises-involving-armed-forces-units-in-2015.html</ref> Among them were [[Operation Fearless Guardian]] (total 2,200 participants, including 1,000 U.S. military). Initial personnel and equipment of the [[173rd Airborne Brigade]] arrived in [[Yavoriv]], [[Lviv Oblast]], on 10 April 2015. Fearless Guardian would train Ukraine's newly formed National Guard under the Congress-approved Global Contingency Security Fund. Under the program, the United States were to train three battalions of Ukrainian troops over a six-month period beginning in April 2015,<ref>U.S. Army Europe, [http://www.army.mil/article/146317/Fearless_Guardian_ground_convoy_arrives_in_Ukraine/ Fearless Guardian ground convoy arrives in Ukraine], April 10, 2015</ref> Others exercises include Exercise Sea Breeze 2015 (total 2,500 personnel of which 1,000 US military and 500 military from NATO or "[[Partnership for Peace]]" countries), "Saber Guardian/Rapid Trident – 2015" (total 2,100 members, including 500 US military and 600 NATO/PfP personnel), as well as the Ukrainian-Polish air exercise "Safe Skies – 2015" (total 350 participants, including 100 Polish military) and military police "Law and Order – 2015" (total 100 participants, 50 of which are Polish military).{{citation needed|date=June 2015}}

In September 2015, NATO launched five trust funds for €5.4 million for the Ukrainian army. €2 million are planned to be sent for the modernization of communication systems, €1.2 million – to reform the logistic and standardization systems, €845 thousand – for physical rehabilitation and prostheses, €815 thousand for cyber defense, and €410 thousand for retraining and resettlement.{{citation needed|date=February 2020}}

[[File:Ukraine – NATO Commission chaired by Petro Poroshenko (2017-07-10) 05.jpg|thumb|NATO Secretary General [[Jens Stoltenberg]] and Petro Poroshenko, 10 July 2017]]
In March 2016, [[President of the European Commission]] [[Jean-Claude Juncker]] stated that it would take at least 20–25 years for Ukraine to join the EU and NATO.<ref>[http://www.rferl.org/content/juncker-says-ukraine-not-likely-join-eu-nato-for-20-25-years/27588682.html Juncker Says Ukraine Not Likely To Join EU, NATO For 20-25 Years]. [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]]. Published on March 4, 2016.</ref> On 8 June 2017, Ukraine's [[Verkhovna Rada]] passed a law making integration with NATO a foreign policy priority.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/427216.html|title=Rada restores Ukraine's course for NATO membership as foreign policy priority|date=2017-06-08|work=Interfax-Ukraine|access-date=2017-06-08}}</ref> In July 2017 Poroshenko announced that he would seek the opening of negotiations on a [[Membership Action Plan]] with NATO.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-ukraine-nato-idUSKBN19V12V|title=Pledging reforms by 2020, Ukraine seeks route into NATO|date=2017-07-10|accessdate=2017-07-22|publisher=[[Reuters]]}}</ref> In that same month President Poroshenko began proposing a 'patronage system', tying individual regions with European States.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://calrev.org/2018/09/02/president-poroshenko-seeks-european-patronage-in-donbass-region/?v=7516fd43adaa|title=President Poroshenko Seeks European "Patronage" in Donbass Region|last=Genin|first=Aaron|date=September 2, 2018|work=The California Review|access-date=July 16, 2019}}</ref>

On the 10th of March 2018, [[NATO]] added [[Ukraine]] in the list of [[NATO]] aspiring members (others including [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]] and [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]]). Several months later, in late June, Ukraine's [[Verkhovna Rada]] passed a National Security bill: the bill defines the principles of state policy on national security and defence as well as focusing on Ukraine's integration into the European security, economic and legal system; improvement in mutual relations with other states and eventual membership in [[European Union|EU]] and [[NATO]].

On September 20, 2018, the Ukrainian parliament approved amendments to the constitution that would make the accession of the country to NATO and the EU a central goal and the main foreign policy objective.<ref>{{cite web |title= Ukraine pushes ahead with plans to secure NATO membership |url= https://www.apnews.com/dff40992fcc446f6808d02d03b35e4bc |website= Associated Press |accessdate= October 15, 2018}}</ref>

On 7 February 2019, the [[Verkhovna Rada|Ukrainian parliament]] voted with a majority of 334 out of 385 to change the [[Constitution of Ukraine|Ukrainian constitution]] in order to help Ukraine to join NATO and the [[European Union]].<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.unian.info/politics/10437570-ukraine-s-parliament-backs-changes-to-constitution-confirming-ukraine-s-path-toward-eu-nato.html |title= Ukraine's parliament backs changes to Constitution confirming Ukraine's path toward EU, NATO |date=7 February 2019|website= Unian | access-date= 2019-02-07}}</ref> After the vote, Ukrainian president Poroshenko declared: "This is the day when the movement of Ukraine to the European Union and the North Atlantic Alliance will be consolidated in the Constitution as a foreign political landmark."<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.president.gov.ua/en/news/mi-jdemo-svoyim-shlyahom-i-cej-shlyah-do-chlenstva-ukrayini-52982 |title=We are going our way and this is the way to Ukraine's membership in the EU and NATO - President | date=7 February 2019| publisher = The President of Ukraine | access-date= 2019-02-07}}</ref>

On 12 June 2020, Ukraine joined NATO's [[Enhanced opportunity partner]] interoperability program.{{#tag:ref|[[Australia]], [[Finland]], [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]], [[Jordan]], and [[Sweden]] are also NATO's [[Enhanced opportunity partner]] program partners.<ref name="natoukraine30667898"/>|group=nb}}<ref name="natoukraine30667898">[https://www.rferl.org/a/nato-grants-ukraine-enhanced-opportunities-partner-status/30667898.html NATO Grants Ukraine 'Enhanced Opportunities Partner' Status], [[Radio Free Europe]] (12 June 2020)</ref> According to an official, NATO statement the new status "does not prejudge any decisions on NATO membership."<ref name="natoukraine30667898"/>

On October 8, 2020, during a meeting with [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] [[Boris Johnson]] in [[London]], [[President of Ukraine|President]] [[Volodymyr Zelensky|Volodymyr Zelenskyi]] stated that Ukraine needs a NATO Membership Action Plan, as NATO membership will contribute to Ukraine's security and defense.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Zelenskyi said in Britain that Ukraine needed a MAP in NATO|url=https://www.unn.com.ua/uk/news/1896076-zelenskiy-u-britaniyi-zayaviv-scho-ukrayini-potriben-pdch-v-nato|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

On December 1, 2020, the Minister of Defense of Ukraine Andriy Taran stated that Ukraine clearly outlines its ambitions to obtain the NATO Membership Action Plan and hopes for comprehensive political and military support for such a decision at the next Alliance Summit in 2021. Addressing the ambassadors and military attaches of NATO member states, as well as representatives of the NATO office in Ukraine, they were urged to inform their capitals that Ukraine would hope for their full politico-military support in reaching such a decision at the next NATO Summit in 2021. This should be a practical step and a demonstration of commitment to the 2008 Bucharest Summit.<ref>https://www.mil.gov.ua/news/2020/12/01/ambiczii-ukraini-otrimati-plan-dij-shhodo-chlenstva-na-samiti-nato-u-2021-roczi-andrij-taran/</ref>

==Popular opinion in Ukraine==
{{Popular support to NATO integration of Ukraine in Ukraine}}
[[Western Ukraine]] has always been significantly more pro-NATO than the rest of the country.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=pbocXztNVsUC&pg=PA24 Endgame in NATO's Enlargement: The Baltic States and Ukraine], Yaroslav Bilinsky, 1999, [[Greenwood Press]], {{ISBN |0-275-96363-2}}/{{ISBN|978-0-275-96363-7}}, page 25</ref><ref name= RatingJuly12>[http://ratinggroup.com.ua/en/products/politic/data/entry/14004/ The language question, the results of recent research in 2012], [[Sociological group "RATING"|RATING]] (25 May 2012)</ref><ref name= "Nato support">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/204811.html Ukrainians supporting NATO membership in minority – poll], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (14 May 2014)</ref>

[[Eastern Ukraine]] is far more anti-NATO and pro-Russia than the rest of Ukraine.<ref name="Nato support" /><ref name="GallupMarch 14, 2014" />

A [[Gallup poll]] conducted in October 2008 showed that 43% of Ukrainians associated NATO as a threat to their country, while only 15% associated it with protection.<ref>[http://www.gallup.com/poll/110848/Ukrainians-May-Oppose-Presidents-ProWestern-Goals.aspx Ukrainians May Oppose President’s Pro-Western Goals] [[The Gallup Organization|Gallup]] Retrieved on August 26, 2009</ref> A November 2009 poll by Ukrainian Project System relieved 40.1% of Ukrainians polled said the [[Collective Security Treaty Organization]] (CSTO) was the best global security group for Ukraine to be a part of and 33.9% of the respondents supported Ukraine's full membership in CSTO; more than 36% of the respondents of the poll said that Ukraine should remain neutral and only 12.5% supported Ukraine's accession to NATO.<ref name="Ukrainian Project System poll 26 November 2009">[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/53640/ Poll: over 40 percent of Ukrainians prefer Collective Security Treaty Organization, 12.5 percent favor NATO], [[Kyiv Post]] (November 26, 2009)</ref> A 2009 Gallup poll showed that 40% of Ukrainian adults associate NATO with "Threat" and 17% with "Protection".<ref name="Gallup52010">[http://www.gallup.com/poll/127094/ukrainians-likely-support-move-away-nato.aspx Ukrainians Likely Support Move Away From NATO], [[The Gallup Organization|Gallup]] (April 2, 2010)</ref> According to a poll by [[Razumkov Center]] in March 2011 20.6% on average across Ukraine considered NATO a threat; this number was 51% in [[Crimea]].<ref name="IamUkie">[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/102113/ Poll: Most Crimean residents consider Ukraine their motherland], [[Kyiv Post]] (11 April 2011)</ref> A 2013 Gallup poll showed that 29% associated NATO with "Threat" and 17% with "Protection"; 44% viewed it as neither.<ref name="GallupMarch 14, 2014">[http://www.gallup.com/poll/167927/crisis-ukrainians-likely-nato-threat.aspx Before Crisis, Ukrainians More Likely to See NATO as a Threat], [[The Gallup Organization|Gallup]] (14 March 2014)</ref>

Following the [[Russian military intervention in Ukraine (2014–present)|Russian military intervention of 2014]], [[annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation|annexation of Crimea]] and the start of the [[Donbass War]], many Ukrainians changed their views of NATO: polls from the middle of 2014 till 2016 showed that the majority of Ukrainians supported NATO membership.<ref name="BsfNOTAaWiDa"/><ref name="BsfNOTAaWiDb"/><ref name=PUNR4215/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://sowiport.gesis.org/search/id/gesis-ssoar-48564|title=Umland, Andreas - Why Ukraine's Hope for NATO Membership Is Understandable, But Will Remain Unfulfilled {{!}} IndraStra Global - Sowiport|last=Sozialwissenschaften|first=GESIS Leibniz Institut für|date=|website=|access-date=2017-12-04}}</ref>

An electronic petition to the president of Ukraine Petro Poroshenko was filed on 29 August 2015<ref>{{cite web|url=https://president.gov.ua/petition/52|title=Electronic petitions to the president of Ukraine. "Joining NATO referendum" (replied). Petition text (A.S.Baturyn).|date=29 August 2015}}</ref> requesting that a referendum on joining NATO be conducted. The petition achieved the required 25,000 votes to be considered. The president's reply stated that "One of the main priorities of [[Ukraine]]'s foreign policy is to deepen cooperation with [[NATO]] to achieve the criteria required for membership in this organization. Today, we carry out security sector reform in Ukraine to reach NATO standards and to strengthen the country's defense system, which is necessary to counter Russian aggression. Once Ukraine fulfills all the necessary criteria to join the Alliance, final decision on this important issue will be approved by the Ukrainian people in a referendum."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://president.gov.ua/petition/52|title=Electronic petitions to the president of Ukraine. 'Joining NATO referendum' (replied). President's reply |date= 29 August 2015}}</ref>

In February 2017, President Poroshenko announced that a referendum would be held during his presidency, with polls showing that 54% of Ukrainians favor such a move.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.reuters.com/article/us-ukraine-nato-idUSKBN15H05K|title=Ukraine's Poroshenko plans referendum on NATO membership: German media | publisher = Reuters |date=1 February 2017}}<br />{{cite web |url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/02/02/ukraines-petro-poroshenko-will-hold-referendum-nato-membership/|title= Ukraine's Petro Poroshenko 'will hold referendum' on Nato membership | work = Telegraph |date=2 February 2017}}</ref>

==Russian opposition to Ukrainian NATO membership==
Russia is strongly opposed to any eastward expansion of NATO.<ref name="protest">{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7324035.stm |title=Bush backs Ukraine on Nato bid |publisher=BBC News |date=2008-04-01 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pewresearch.org/pubs/1542/ukrainie-president-blocks-nato-membership-russia-influence |title=Ukraine Says 'No' to NATO |publisher=[[Pew Research Center]] |date=29 March 2010}}</ref> On February 12, 2008, (then) [[Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]] said [[Russia]] may target its missiles at Ukraine if its neighbour joins NATO and accepts the deployment of a [[Anti-ballistic missile#European front|US missile defence shield]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7241470.stm | work=BBC News | title=Russia in Ukraine missile threat | date=February 12, 2008 | accessdate=May 26, 2010}}</ref> Former [[Ukrainian President]] [[Viktor Yushchenko]] has stated more than once his country would not allow foreign military bases on its territory;<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.russiatoday.com/Top_News/2008-06-16/Ukraine_says_no_to_foreign_bases.html |title=Ukraine says 'no' to foreign bases |work=[[Russia Today]] |date=June 16, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.interfax.com.ua/eng/main/16656/ |title=Ukraine's territory will never be used against Russia, says Yuschenko |agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]] |date=July 6, 2009}}</ref> as of December 2009, NATO was not planning to deploy military bases in Ukraine.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.kyivpost.com/news/nation/detail/55288/ |title=NATO not planning to deploy military bases in Ukraine |work=[[Kyiv Post]] |date=December 16, 2009}}</ref>

[[Prime Minister of Russia|Prime Minister]] [[Vladimir Putin]] reportedly declared at a NATO-Russia summit in 2008 that if Ukraine joined NATO his country could contend to annex the [[Ukraine|Ukrainian East]] and [[Crimea]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.pravda.com.ua/articles/2008/07/3/3482506/ |script-title=uk:Імперські комплекси братів росіян Або Не розсипайте перли перед свинями |title=Imperski kompleksy brativ rosiyan Abo Ne rozsypayte perly pered svynyamy |language=uk |trans-title=The Imperial complex of Russian brothers, or, Do not cast pearls before swine |work=[[Ukrayinska Pravda]] |date=July 3, 2008}}</ref>

During a NATO conference in [[Hungarian Parliament|Hungarian parliament]] on 20 November 2008, Deputy Assistant Secretary-General Aurelia Bouchez said: "We should not make a choice between NATO enlargement and Russia as we need both"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unian.net/eng/news/news-285669.html |title=NATO official: NATO needs both enlargement, Russia |work=UNIAN news |date=2008-11-20 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mathaba.net/0_index.shtml?x=612070 |title=NATO needs both enlargement, Russia |publisher=Mathaba |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160124092128/http://www.mathaba.net/news/?x=612070 |archive-date=2016-01-24 |url-status=dead |date=2008-11-21 |accessdate=2014-04-04}}</ref> and [[Secretary General of NATO|NATO's Secretary General]], [[Jaap de Hoop Scheffer]] told a conference in [[Spain]] twelve days later: "The emergence of independent states within the former Soviet space is a reality. The ability of these states to determine their own future is a litmus test for the new Europe. Do we have to choose between good relations with Russia and further enlargement? My answer is no – we will not choose, will not sacrifice one for the other. It would bring new dividing lines."<ref>{{cite news|author=Tony Halpin |work=[[The Times]] |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/dont-turn-deaf-ear-to-ukraine-nato-bid-viktor-yushchenko-begs-allies-0dpsjc78f9q |title=Don't turn deaf ear to Ukraine Nato bid, Viktor Yushchenko begs allies |date=2008-11-20 |accessdate=2019-05-10}}</ref>

In an interview with the [[BBC]] on 18 November 2014, [[Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]] spokesmen [[Dmitry Peskov]] called for "a 100% guarantee that no-one would think about Ukraine joining NATO"; 2 days later [[Secretary General of NATO]] [[Jens Stoltenberg]] rejected this call stating it would be "violating the idea of respecting the sovereignty of Ukraine, which is a fundamental".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-30107520 |title=Ukraine crisis: Russia demands guarantees from Nato |work=[[BBC News]] |date=18 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://m.baltictimes.com/article/jcms/id/35802/ |title=Stoltenberg dismisses Kremlin's request for guarantees over Ukraine membership of NATO |work=[[The Baltic Times]] |date=20 November 2014}}</ref>
