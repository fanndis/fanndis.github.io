The '''2010 Australian Open''' was a [[tennis]] tournament that took place in [[Melbourne Park]] in [[Melbourne]], Australia, from 18 to 31 January. It was the 98th edition of the [[Australian Open]], and the first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event of the year.

In the singles competition, [[Rafael Nadal]] and [[Serena Williams]] were the defending champions. Williams was able to retain her title with a win over [[Justine Henin]], 6–4, 3–6, 6–2 in the final,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://theglobalherald.com/australian-open-2010-tennis-serena-williams-is-womens-champion/1988/ |title=Australian Open 2010 Tennis: Serena Williams is Women's Champion |author=Robin Scott |date=30 January 2010 |work=The Global Herald |publisher=24 Hour Trading |accessdate=30 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100201153259/http://theglobalherald.com/australian-open-2010-tennis-serena-williams-is-womens-champion/1988/| archivedate= 1 February 2010 | url-status= live}}</ref> while Nadal retired in his quarterfinals match against [[Andy Murray]] owing to a quadriceps injury. [[Roger Federer]] was the men's champion, defeating finalist Andy Murray in straight sets 6–3, 6–4, 7–6(11).<ref name="BBC Federer">{{citation|title=Roger Federer beats Andy Murray to win Australian Open |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/8489107.stm |work=BBC Sport|date=31 January 2010 |accessdate=31 January 2010  |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100131053617/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/8489107.stm |archivedate=31 January 2010 |url-status=live}}.</ref>

In doubles, the 2009 champion pairs were successful in their respective title defenses – [[Bob and Mike Bryan]] in men's doubles and Serena and [[Venus Williams]] in women's doubles. [[Sania Mirza]] and [[Mahesh Bhupathi]] were not able to defend their mixed doubles title because they withdrew from the event beforehand. In mixed doubles, [[Cara Black]] and [[Leander Paes]] won the title, which made a mixed doubles career grand slam for Black.

== Seniors ==

=== Men's Singles ===
{{main|2010 Australian Open – Men's Singles}}
{{flagicon|SUI}} '''[[Roger Federer]]''' defeated {{flagicon|GBR}} [[Andy Murray]], 6–3, 6–4, 7–6<sup>(13–11)</sup>
* This was Federer's first title of the year and his 62nd overall. It was his 16th career Grand Slam title, and his 4th Australian Open title tying [[Andre Agassi]] for most Australian Open titles in the Open Era ([[Novak Djokovic]] later surpassed this record winning his 6th title in 2016, while Federer won the title for a 6th time in 2018). This was Federer's 5th [[List of Australian Open Singles Finals appearances|final]] at the Australian Open which tied him with [[Stefan Edberg]] for the Open Era record.

=== Women's Singles ===
{{main|2010 Australian Open – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]''' defeated {{flagicon|BEL}} [[Justine Henin]], 6–4, 3–6, 6–2

*This was Williams' first title of the year, a record 5th Australian Open title in the Open Era. The title was Williams' 12th major title, which tied her for 6th all-time with [[Billie Jean King]] and [[Suzanne Lenglen]], and in the Open Era solo 4th having surpassed [[Margaret Court|Court's]] 11 mark, which Williams' is behind [[Steffi Graf|Graf]] (22), [[Chris Evert|Evert]] and [[Martina Navratilova|Navratilova]] with (18) each.

=== Men's Doubles ===
{{main|2010 Australian Open – Men's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Bob Bryan]]''' / {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Mike Bryan]]''' defeated {{flagicon|CAN}} [[Daniel Nestor]] / {{flagicon|SRB|2004}} [[Nenad Zimonjić]], 6–3, 6–7<sup>(5–7)</sup>, 6–3

*This was the [[Bob and Mike Bryan|Bryan Brothers']] 57th doubles title together, 4th Australian Open title, and 8th major title.

=== Women's Doubles ===
{{main|2010 Australian Open – Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]''' / {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Venus Williams]]''' defeated {{flagicon|ZIM}} [[Cara Black]] / {{flagicon|USA}} [[Liezel Huber]], 6–4, 6–3

* This is the Williams Sisters' 11th Grand Slam doubles title together and 4th Australian Open title.

=== Mixed Doubles ===
{{main|2010 Australian Open – Mixed Doubles}}
{{flagicon|ZIM}} '''[[Cara Black]]''' / {{flagicon|IND}} '''[[Leander Paes]]''' defeated {{flagicon|RUS}} [[Ekaterina Makarova]] / {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Jaroslav Levinský]], 7–5, 6–3

This was the pair's 3rd consecutive grand slam final and the 4th overall. The victory makes Paes India's joint-lead grand slam winner alongside his ex- doubles partner Mahesh Bhupathi with a total of 11 grand slam doubles titles. This title gave Black a career mixed doubles grand slam, which Black has won one of each slam in mixed doubles.
