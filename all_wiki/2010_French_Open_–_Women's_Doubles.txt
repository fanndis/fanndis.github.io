{{main article|2010 French Open}}

[[Anabel Medina Garrigues]] and [[Virginia Ruano Pascual]] were the defending champions, but they decided not to compete together. Ruano Pascual partnered up with [[Meghann Shaughnessy]], but they lost in the first round to [[Cara Black]] and [[Elena Vesnina]]. Medina Garrigues partnered up with [[Liezel Huber]], but they lost in the semifinals to [[Serena Williams]] and [[Venus Williams]].

The [[Williams sisters]] won the title, defeating [[Květa Peschke]] and [[Katarina Srebotnik]] in the final 6–2, 6–3. With this win, the [[United States of America|Americans]] became the sixth and seventh women to have completed a [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Non-calendar year Grand Slam|non-calendar year Grand Slam]] in Women's Doubles. Additionally, the Williams sisters achieved the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players#World No. 1 ranked players 2|World No. 1]] doubles ranking for the first time, becoming the first sisters to be co-ranked World No. 1 in doubles. The win also gave them their 2nd Career Grand Slam in Women's Doubles.
