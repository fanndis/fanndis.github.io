'''Rosamunde Pilcher''', [[Order of the British Empire|OBE]] (née '''Scott'''; 22 September 1924 – 6 February 2019)<ref name=Guardian_Bryan>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2019/feb/07/rosamunde-pilcher-obituary|title=Rosamunde Pilcher obituary|first=Felicity|last=Bryan|date=7 February 2019|accessdate=7 February 2019|via=www.theguardian.com}}</ref> was a British writer of [[romance novels]], mainstream fiction, and short stories, from 1949 until her retirement in 2000. Her novels sold over 60 million copies worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/rosamunde-pilcher-obituary-xvmx8bw75|title=Rosamunde Pilcher obituary|author=|date=7 February 2019|publisher=|accessdate=8 February 2019|via=www.thetimes.co.uk}}</ref> Early in her career she was also published under the pen name '''Jane Fraser'''. In 2001, she received the [[Corine Literature Prize]]'s Weltbild Readers' Prize for ''Winter Solstice''.

==Personal life==
She was born Rosamunde Scott on 22 September 1924 in [[Lelant]], [[Cornwall]]. Her parents were Helen (née Harvey) and Charles Scott, a British commander.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2019/feb/07/rosamunde-pilcher-obituary|title=Rosamunde Pilcher obituary|last=Bryan|first=Felicity|date=2019-02-07|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-02-14|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Just before her birth her father was posted in [[Burma]], while her mother remained in England.<ref name="WorldAuthors">{{Citation|author1=Vineta Colby|title=World authors, 1985-1990|year=1995|publisher=H.W. Wilson|pages=970}}</ref> She attended the School of St. Clare in [[Penzance]] and [[Howell's School Llandaff]] before going on to Miss Kerr-Sanders' Secretarial College.<ref name="bookreporter">{{cite web|url=http://www.bookreporter.com/authors/rosamunde-pilcher|last=Bruns|first=Ann|title=Biography: Rosamunde Pilcher|publisher=Bookreporter.com|date=2000-08-11|accessdate=2012-09-01}}</ref> She began writing when she was seven, and published her first short story when she was 15.<ref name="binchy"/>

From 1943 until 1946, Pilcher served with the [[Women's Royal Naval Service]]. On 7 December 1946, she married Graham Hope Pilcher,<ref name="bookreporter"/>  a war hero and [[jute]] industry executive who died in March 2009.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/military-obituaries/army-obituaries/5268578/Graham-Pilcher.html| title=Army Obituaries: Graham Pilcher | date=2009-05-03 | accessdate=2012-09-01 | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100819104603/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/military-obituaries/army-obituaries/5268578/Graham-Pilcher.html|archivedate=2010-08-19|publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph]]}}</ref> They moved to [[Dundee]], Scotland. They had two daughters and two sons.<ref name="butt"/>  Her son, [[Robin Pilcher]], is also a novelist.<ref>{{cite web|title=Talking with Robin Pilcher|publisher=AudioFile|url=http://www.audiofilemagazine.com/features/A1435.html|date=April–May 2004|accessdate=2012-09-01}}</ref>

Pilcher died on 6 February 2019, at the age of 94, following a stroke.<ref name=Guardian_Flood />

==Writing career==
In 1949, Pilcher's first book, a romance novel, was published by [[Mills and Boon]], under the [[pseudonym]] Jane Fraser.  She published a further ten novels under that name.  In 1955, she also began writing under her real name with ''Secret to Tell''.  By 1965 she had dropped the pseudonym and was signing her own name to all of her novels.<ref name="bookreporter"/>

The breakthrough in Pilcher's career came in 1987, when she wrote the family saga ''[[The Shell Seekers]]'', her fourteenth novel under her own name.<ref name=Guardian_Flood>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2019/feb/07/rosamunde-pilcher-author-of-the-shell-seekers-dies-aged-94 |title=Rosamunde Pilcher, author of The Shell Seekers, dies aged 94  |author=Alison Flood |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |date=7 February 2019 |accessdate=7 February 2019}}</ref> It focuses on an elderly British woman, Penelope Keeling, who relives her life in flashbacks, and on her relationship with her adult children.  Keeling's life was not extraordinary, but it spans "a time of huge importance and change in the world."<ref name="binchy">{{Cite news|last=Binchy|first=Maeve|authorlink=Maeve Binchy|title=War and Change Come to Temple Pudley|newspaper=[[New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1988/02/07/books/war-and-change-come-to-temple-pudley.html|date=1988-02-07|accessdate=2012-09-01}}</ref>  The novel describes the everyday details of what life during [[World War II]] was like for some of those who lived in [[Great Britain|Britain]].<ref name="binchy"/> ''The Shell Seekers'' sold around ten million copies and was translated into more than forty languages.<ref name=Guardian_Bryan /> It was adapted for the stage by [[Terence Brady (writer)|Terence Brady]] and [[Charlotte Bingham]].<ref name="butt">{{Cite news|last=Butt|first=Riaza|title=Pilcher's winning formula|newspaper=[[Manchester Evening News]] |url=http://menmedia.co.uk/manchestereveningnews/entertainment/arts/s/82/82350_pilchers_winning_formula.html|date=2004-02-25|accessdate=2012-09-01}}</ref> Pilcher was said to be among the highest-earning women in Britain by the mid-1990s.<ref name=BBC_obit>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-47159456 |title=Rosamunde Pilcher, author of The Shell Seekers, dies at 94 |publisher=BBC |date=7 February 2019 |accessdate=7 February 2019 }}</ref>

Her other major novels include ''September'' (1990), ''Coming Home'' (1995) and ''Winter Solstice'' (2000).<ref name=Guardian_Flood /><ref name=Musumeci>{{cite book |chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JEvyadBdwYQC&pg=PT272 |chapter=Pilcher, Rosamunde (1924– ) |author=Robin Musumeci |title=Encyclopedia of American Popular Fiction |editor=Geoff Hamilton |editor2=Brian Jones|year=2010 |publisher=Infobase Publishing |pages=266–67 |isbn=9781438116945 }}</ref> ''Coming Home'' won the [[Romantic Novel of the Year Award]] by [[Romantic Novelists' Association]] in 1996.<ref name="RoNAAwards">{{Citation|title=Romantic Novel of the Year|url=http://www.romanticnovelistsassociation.org/index.php/awards/romantic_novel_of_the_year|date=2012-07-12}}</ref> The president of the association in 2019, the romance writer [[Katie Fforde]], considers Pilcher to be "groundbreaking as she was the first to bring family sagas to the wider public".<ref name=Guardian_Flood /> [[Felicity Bryan]], in her obituary for ''The Guardian'', writes that Pilcher took the romance genre to "an altogether higher, wittier level"; she praises Pilcher's work for its "grittiness and fearless observation" and comments that it is often more prosaic than romantic.<ref name=Guardian_Bryan />

Pilcher retired from writing in 2000.<ref name="bookreporter"/>  Two years later she was created an Officer of the [[Order of the British Empire]] (OBE).<ref name="obe">{{cite news|title=Honours in the arts world|publisher=BBC News|date=2001-12-31|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/1734545.stm|accessdate=2012-09-01}}</ref>

===TV adaptations===
Her books are especially popular in Germany because the national television station [[ZDF]] (Zweites Deutsches Fernsehen) has produced more than a hundred of her stories for television, starting with ''The Day of the Storm'' in 1993. These television films are some of the most popular programmes on ZDF.<ref name=BBC_obit /><ref name=Guardian_Jakat>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2013/oct/04/rosamunde-pilcher-german-tourists-cornwall|title=The Rosamunde Pilcher trail: why German tourists flock to Cornwall|website=The Guardian|date=4 October 2013|author=Lena Jakat|access-date=7 February 2019}}</ref> Pilcher was awarded the British Tourism Award in 2002 for the positive effect the books and the adaptations have had on Cornish tourism.<ref name=BBC_obit /> Notable film locations include [[Prideaux Place]], a 16th-century mansion near [[Padstow]].<ref name=Guardian_Jakat />

A [[The Shell Seekers (1989 film)|television adaptation of ''The Shell Seekers'']], starring [[Angela Lansbury]], was made in 1989.<ref name=BBC_obit />

A [[Coming Home (TV serial)|two-part television adaptation of ''Coming Home'']], made by [[Yorkshire Television]], was broadcast in 1998, starring [[Keira Knightley]], [[Emily Mortimer]], [[Peter O'Toole]], [[Joanna Lumley]], [[Penelope Keith]], [[David McCallum]], [[Paul Bettany]], [[Patrick Ryecart]] and [[Susan Hampshire]], among others.
