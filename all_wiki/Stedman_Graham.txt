'''Stedman Graham''' (born March 6, 1951) is an American educator, author, businessman, and [[Orator|speaker]]. He is the long-term partner of [[Oprah Winfrey]].

==Early life and education==
Graham was born on March 6, 1951, in the [[Whitesboro, New Jersey|Whitesboro]] section of [[Middle Township, New Jersey]], the son of Mary Jacobs Graham and Stedman Graham Sr. He is one of six children. He stands 6 feet 6 inches (198&nbsp;cm) tall. He received a [[bachelor's degree]] in social work from [[Hardin-Simmons University]] in 1974 and a master's degree in education from [[Ball State University]] in 1979.<ref>{{Citation |url=http://www.stedmangraham.com |title=About Stedman Graham – Bio|publisher=S. Graham & Associates|accessdate=2011-05-26}}</ref> Graham played college [[basketball]] at Hardin-Simmons.<ref>{{cite news|last=Mariott|first=Michel|title='They Used to Call Me Oprah's Boyfriend'|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/02/26/garden/they-used-to-call-me-oprah-s-boyfriend.html|website=[[New York Times]]|accessdate=October 24, 2017|date=February 26, 1997}}</ref>

==Business career==
Graham eventually moved to [[High Point, North Carolina]], to establish himself in public relations. At B & C Associates, Graham worked on behalf of black causes and worked with many distinguished clients, including author [[Maya Angelou]] and South African activist [[Winnie Mandela]].<ref name="people.com">{{cite web|title=Cover Story: Her Man Stedman – Vol. 38 No. 21|url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20109149,00.html|date=November 23, 1992|publisher=}}</ref>

He is also founder of [[Chicago, Illinois]]'s AAD (formerly, Athletes Against Drugs), a non-profit organization that provides services to youth and has awarded over $1.5 million in scholarships since its founding in 1985. It also arranged for sports figures to educate children about substance abuse.<ref name="people.com"/>

He is also a member of the Indiana Broadcasting Hall of Fame. 

In 1988 Graham created S. Graham & Associates, a Chicago-based corporate and educational marketing and consulting firm.<ref name="people.com"/>

Graham was an adjunct professor at [[Full Sail University]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Stedman Graham Net Worth 2020 {{!}} Bio, Age, Height {{!}} Richest Celebrities|url=https://latestcelebnetworth.com/stedman-graham-net-worth/|last=latestcelebnetworth|date=2019-07-05|website=Latest Celebrities Net Worth|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=An Oprah moment: Maximize your life, Graham tells class|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/os-xpm-2009-08-25-stedman-story.html|last=Critic|first=Hal Boedeker, Sentinel Television|website=OrlandoSentinel.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref>

==Books==
Graham is the author of several [[self-help]] and business-related books, including:
*''The Ultimate Guide to Sport Event Management and Marketing'' (1995).
*''You Can Make It Happen: A Nine-Step Plan for Success'' (1997)
*''You Can Make It Happen Every Day'' (1998)
*''Teens Can Make It Happen: Nine Steps for Success'' (2000)
*''Teens Can Make It Happen Workbook'' (2001)
*''The Ultimate Guide to Sports Marketing'' (2001)
*''Build Your Own Life Brand!: A Powerful Strategy to Maximize Your Potential and Enhance Your Value for Ultimate Achievement'' (2002)
*''Move Without the Ball: Put Your Skills and Your Magic to Work for You'' (2004)
*''Who Are You?'' (2005)
*''Diversity: Leaders Not Labels: A New Plan for the 21st Century'' (2006)
*''Identity: Your Passport to Success'' (2012)
*''Identity Leadership: To Lead Others You Must First Lead Yourself'' (2019)

==Other publications==
Graham became a columnist for ''The Huffington Post'' in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/author/stedman-graham|title=Stedman Graham {{!}} HuffPost|website=www.huffpost.com|access-date=2019-10-01}}</ref> He contributed an article on personal identity to the College Board School Counseling Series in 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://media.collegeboard.com/digitalServices/pdf/nosca/12b_5136_NOSCAJournal_120330.pdf|title= TRANSFORMING THE EDUCATIONAL EXPERIENCE OF YOUNG MEN OF COLOR|last= |first= |date= |website= media.collegeboard.com|format=PDF|access-date=2020-03-13}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Graham was the constant companion of WFLD-TV anchor [[Robin Robinson]] from 1982 to 1986

Graham has been the subject of much publicity since 1986, most notably through tabloid articles claiming to chronicle his ongoing relationship with Oprah Winfrey. Graham has been with Winfrey since 1986.{{cn|date=May 2020}}

He was conferred an honorary degree by [[Notre Dame de Namur University]] of Belmont, Calif., at the university's 167th Commencement ceremony on May 4, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ndnu.edu/media-center/ndnu-2019-commencement/|title=NDNU Confers Degrees on 481 Graduates {{!}} Media Center|website=www.ndnu.edu|access-date=2019-10-01}}</ref>
