'''Margaret Rhodes''' {{postnominals|country=GBR|LVO}} ({{nee|'''Elphinstone'''}}; 9 June 1925 &ndash; 25 November 2016) was a first cousin of [[Queen Elizabeth II]]. From 1991 to 2002, she served as [[Woman of the Bedchamber]] to [[Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother]].

==Early life and education==
Born Margaret Elphinstone in [[Westminster]], London, Rhodes was the youngest daughter of the [[Sidney Buller-Fullerton-Elphinstone, 16th Lord Elphinstone|16th Lord Elphinstone]] and his wife, [[Mary Elphinstone, Lady Elphinstone|Lady Mary Bowes-Lyon]], an elder sister of [[Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=7rGviTYk9%2FyHSckhdiBFYg&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=28 November 2016|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref><ref name="Knapton">{{cite news |last=Knapton |first=Sarah |date=27 November 2016 |title=Queen’s cousin and best friend Margaret Rhodes dies aged 91 |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/11/27/queens-cousin-best-friend-margaret-rhodes-dies-aged-91/ |newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |access-date=28 November 2016}}</ref> Less than a year older than the future queen, she was a frequent playmate of the young [[Elizabeth II|Princess Elizabeth]].<ref name=nnc>{{cite news|title=How Diana's death turned queen into 'proper granny'|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2012/05/30/world/europe/margaret-rhodes-queen|accessdate=16 August 2013|publisher=CNN|date=5 June 2012|author=Barry Neild|author2=Nick Glass|location=London}}</ref> During the Second World War she lived at [[Windsor Castle]] and [[Buckingham Palace]], and took a secretarial course.<ref name="Grice">{{cite news |last=Grice |first=Elizabeth |title=Margaret Rhodes: The Queen's cousin |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/queen-elizabeth-II/8446396/Margaret-Rhodes-The-Queens-cousin.html |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=13 April 2011 |access-date=28 November 2016}}</ref><ref name="Brandreth">{{cite book|last1=Brandreth|first1=Gyles|title=Philip & Elizabeth: Portrait of a Royal Marriage|url=https://archive.org/details/philipelizabethp00bran|url-access=registration|date=2005|publisher=W.W. Norton|location=New York|edition=First American}}</ref> On 20 November 1947, she was a bridesmaid to Princess Elizabeth at her [[Wedding of Princess Elizabeth and Philip Mountbatten, Duke of Edinburgh|wedding]] to [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh|Philip Mountbatten, Duke of Edinburgh]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Chen|first=Joyce|title=I'm "Not Terribly" Excited about Kate Middleton's Baby|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/queen-elizabeth-iis-cousin-margaret-rhodes-im-not-terribly-excited-about-kate-middletons-baby-2013177|accessdate=16 August 2013|newspaper=US Weekly|date=17 July 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
During World War II, she worked as a secretary for [[MI6]].<ref name=nnc/><ref name="Grice"/><ref name="Brandreth" /> She was a [[Woman of the Bedchamber]] – a mix of [[lady-in-waiting]] and companion – to her aunt, [[Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother]], from 1991 until the latter's death in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://royalfanzine.blogspot.co.uk/2011/11/whos-who-behind-palace-walls-ladies-in.html|title=The Who's Who Behind the Palace Walls: The Ladies in waiting of Royalty|publisher=The Royal Fanzine|date=3 November 2011|accessdate=15 May 2013}}</ref>

In the [[2000 Birthday Honours]] Rhodes was appointed [[Royal Victorian Order|Lieutenant of the Royal Victorian Order]] (LVO). She lived in the Garden House, a [[grace and favour]] residence in [[Windsor Great Park]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Amanpour|first=Christiane|title=The Queen's cousin|url=http://amanpour.blogs.cnn.com/2013/07/16/the-queens-cousin/|accessdate=16 August 2013|publisher=CNN|date=16 July 2013}}</ref> In the run-up to the Queen's 80th birthday in April 2006, Rhodes gave an interview to the [[British Broadcasting Corporation|BBC]] in which she stated her belief that the Queen would not [[abdication|abdicate]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Queen 'will never quit throne' claims cousin|url=http://www.scotsman.com/news/uk/queen-will-never-quit-throne-claims-cousin-1-982824|newspaper=The Scotsman|date=19 April 2006}}</ref>

Her autobiography, ''The Final Curtsey'', was published in 2011.<ref name="Grice"/> She was the castaway on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s ''[[Desert Island Discs]]'' on 3 June 2012.<ref>{{cite news|title=Margaret Rhodes|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/features/desert-island-discs/castaway/0e10fef8#b01jgb8t|work=[[BBC Radio 4]]|date=3 June 2012}}</ref>

Rhodes appeared in seven documentaries about her first cousin [[Queen Elizabeth II]].<ref>{{IMDb name|5097235|Margaret Rhodes}}</ref>

On 27 November 2016, Buckingham Palace confirmed that Rhodes had died, aged 91, on 25 November following a short illness.<ref name="Knapton"/> Queen Elizabeth and the [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh|Duke of Edinburgh]] attended her funeral in the [[Royal Chapel of All Saints]], [[Windsor Great Park]], on 12 December 2016, accompanied by the [[Prince Andrew, Duke of York|Duke of York]], the [[Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex|Earl]] and [[Sophie, Countess of Wessex|Countess]] of Wessex, the [[Prince Richard, Duke of Gloucester|Duke]] and [[Birgitte, Duchess of Gloucester|Duchess]] of Gloucester, and [[Princess Alexandra, The Hon Lady Ogilvy]].<ref>Court Circular, 12 December 2016</ref>

==Personal life==
On 31 July 1950, she married the writer [[Denys Rhodes|Denys Gravenor Rhodes]] (1919–1981), with [[Princess Margaret]] as one of the bridesmaids.{{cn|date=June 2020}} The couple had four children and one grandson:<ref name="BBC">{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-38124026 |title=Queen's cousin Margaret Rhodes dies |last=Hunt |first=Peter |date=27 November 2016}}</ref>

*Annabel Margaret Rhodes (born 21 February 1952); she married Christopher James Downing Strickland-Skailes in 1978. They have one son. She remarried (Geoffrey Vallack) Charles Cope in 1986
**Andrew James Downing Strickland-Skailes (born 1980)
*Victoria Ann Rhodes (born 27 September 1953), a goddaughter of [[Elizabeth II|Queen Elizabeth II]]; married Nicholas Deans in 1974
*Simon John Gravenor Rhodes (born 22 February 1957), a godson of [[Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon|Princess Margaret]];<ref>{{cite web |url=http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/godchildren.htm#Margaret |title=Royal Godchildren |publisher=Yvonne's Royalty Home Page |accessdate=15 June 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120502185803/http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/godchildren.htm#Margaret |archive-date=2 May 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> married Susan Simon in 1983. Mrs. Simon Rhodes was appointed an extra [[Lady-in-waiting]] to Queen Elizabeth II in July 2017.<ref>Appendix to the Court Circular, 13 July 2017</ref>
*Michael Andrew Gravenor Rhodes (born 8 June 1960)

==Ancestry==
Having been a first cousin to [[Elizabeth II]], Rhodes shared half her ancestry with the Queen, including several notable peers.<ref name="BBC"/>
