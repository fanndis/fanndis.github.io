{{main|2007 Wimbledon Championships}}

[[Venus Williams]] defeated [[Marion Bartoli]] in the final, 6–4, 6–1 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2007 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> Williams, ranked 31st at the time and seeded 23rd, remains the lowest ranked and lowest seeded woman ever to win Wimbledon.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2007/SPORT/07/07/tennis.williams/|title=Williams romps to her fourth title|date=8 July 2007|work=The New York Times|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> This was Williams' fourth Wimbledon singles title and she also became the first female champion to earn the same prize money as the male champion, following the tournament's decision to award equal pay for the first time that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/07/08/sports/tennis/08women.html|title=  For Venus Williams, From No. 23 to a Title|date=8 July 2007|work=The New York Times|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> [[Amélie Mauresmo]] was the defending champion, but was defeated by [[Nicole Vaidišová]] in the fourth round.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/6264824.stm|title= Vaidisova ends Mauresmo's reign|date=3 July 2007|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref>

Bartoli reached her first Grand Slam final after a semifinal victory over world No. 1 [[Justine Henin]] that was described as one of the biggest shocks in Wimbledon history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/6278644.stm|title=  Bartoli stuns Henin to make final|date=6 July 2007|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> Though she lost the final, she would eventually win the Wimbledon title [[2013 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|six years later]] in her final Grand Slam tournament.

This was also the first Wimbledon main draw appearance for the [[2018 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|2018]] champion and future World No. 1 [[Angelique Kerber]]. She was beaten by [[Anna Chakvetadze]] in the first round.
