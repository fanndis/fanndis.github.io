'''Alexander Schallenberg''' (born 20 June 1969) is an Austrian diplomat, [[jurist]] and [[Independent politician|independent]] politician who has been serving as [[Foreign Minister of Austria|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] in the government of [[Chancellor of Austria|Chancellor]] [[Sebastian Kurz]] since 2019.

==Early life and education==
Schallenberg was born in 1969 in [[Bern]].<ref name="OEN">{{cite web|url=https://www.nachrichten.at/politik/aussenpolitik/Das-Spielfeld-der-Diplomatie-ist-die-zweite-Reihe;art391,2607027|title=Das Spielfeld der Diplomatie ist die zweite Reihe|publisher=Oberösterreichische Nachrichten|date=26 June 2017|accessdate=3 June 2019|language=German}}</ref> As son of an Austrian ambassador he was raised in India, Spain and Paris.<ref name="OEN"/> From 1989 to 1994 he studied law at the [[University of Vienna]] and the [[University of Paris II Panthéon-Assas]]. After his graduation he continued his studies at the [[College of Europe]] until 1995.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kleinezeitung.at/politik/innenpolitik/5636938/KneisslNachfolger_Karrierediplomat-und-KurzVertrauter-Schallenberg|title=Kneissl-Nachfolger – Karrierediplomat und Kurz-Vertrauter Schallenberg wird Außenminister|work=Kleine Zeitung|date=30 May 2019|accessdate=3 June 2019|language=German}}</ref> 

==Career==
In 1997 Schallenberg entered Austria's foreign ministry.{{cn|date=October 2020}}

On 3 June 2019 Schallenberg succeeded [[Karin Kneissl]] as foreign minister of Austria.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bmeia.gv.at/en/the-ministry/the-minister/|title=The Federal Minister for Europe, Integration and Foreign Affairs|publisher=Foreign ministry of Austria|accessdate=3 June 2019}}</ref> He maintained his position as part of the [[Second Kurz government|Second Kurz cabinet]], which was sworn in on 7 January 2020. 

It was noted by ''[[Deutsche Welle]]'' that "after attending an EU summit"<ref name=dwsw/> at the [[Europa building]] in [[Luxembourg]] on 12 October<ref name="ecfac12">{{cite news |title=FOREIGN AFFAIRS COUNCIL Luxembourg, 12 October 2020 |url=https://www.consilium.europa.eu/media/46075/12-fac-participants.pdf |publisher=European Council |date=12 October 2020}}</ref> and 13 October<ref name="ecgac13">{{cite news |title=GENERAL AFFAIRS COUNCIL Luxembourg, 13 Octobre 2020 |url=https://www.consilium.europa.eu/media/46134/20201013-gac-presslist.pdf |publisher=European Council |date=13 October 2020}}</ref> with  [[Sophie Wilmes]], Schallenberg tested positive for [[Coronavirus]].<ref name="dwsw">{{cite news |title=Belgian Foreign Minister Sophie Wilmes in intensive care with COVID-19 |url=https://www.dw.com/en/belgian-foreign-minister-sophie-wilmes-in-intensive-care-with-covid-19/a-55358381 |publisher=Deutsche Welle |date=22 October 2020}}</ref> Wilmes revealed through ''[[Twitter]]'' on 17 October that she had tested covid positive.

==Other activities==
Since 2020, Schallenberg has been a Member of the Board of Trustees of the [[National Fund of the Republic of Austria for Victims of National Socialism]].<ref>[https://www.entschaedigungsfonds.org/organs/board-of-trustees#Board%20of%20Trustees Board of Trustees] [[National Fund of the Republic of Austria for Victims of National Socialism]].</ref>
