'''Ker Chien-ming''' ({{zh|c=柯建銘|p=Kē Jiànmíng}}; born 8 September 1951) is a Taiwanese politician.

==Early life==
He obtained his bachelor's degree in dental science from [[Chung Shan Medical University]] and master's degree in management science from [[Tamkang University]].

==Political career==
He is a member of the [[Democratic Progressive Party]] and has served in the [[Legislative Yuan]] since 1993, representing [[Hsinchu district]] from 1993 to 2008 and again starting in 2016. From 2008 to 2016, Ker was elected via proportional representation.<ref>{{cite web|title=Legislators: Ker Chien-Ming|publisher=The Legislative Yuan of the Republic of China|accessdate=2007-11-13|url=http://www.ly.gov.tw/en/03_leg/legIntro.action?lgno=00041&stage=8}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/editorials/archives/2007/11/09/2003386966|work=Taipei Times|title=Editorial: Gangsters, gangsters everywhere|date=9 November 2007|accessdate=2007-11-12}}</ref> For the 2020 legislative election, Ker returned to Democratic Progressive Party list.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Wen |first1=Keui-hsiang |last2=Huang |first2=Frances |title=2020 Elections: DPP legislator-at-large list shows diversity |url=http://focustaiwan.tw/news/aipl/201911150006.aspx |accessdate=15 November 2019 |agency=Central News Agency |date=15 November 2019}}</ref>
