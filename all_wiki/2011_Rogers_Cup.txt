The '''2011 Canada Masters''' (also known as the '''2011 Rogers Cup presented by National Bank''' and the '''2011 Rogers Cup''' for sponsorship reasons) was a [[tennis]] tournament played on [[Tennis court#Hard courts|outdoor hard courts]] in [[Canada]]. It was the 122nd edition of the [[Canada Masters]] (110th for the women), and was part of the [[ATP World Tour Masters 1000]] of the [[2011 ATP World Tour]], and of the [[2011 WTA Tour|Premier Series]] of the [[2011 WTA Tour]]. The women's and legends event was held at the [[Rexall Centre]] in [[Toronto|Toronto, Ontario]], [[Canada]] and the men's event took place at the [[Uniprix Stadium]] in [[Montreal|Montreal, Quebec]], [[Canada]], from August 8 to August 14.

==Finals==

===Men's singles===
{{main|2011 Rogers Cup – Men's Singles}}
{{flagicon|SRB}} [[Novak Djokovic]] defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Mardy Fish]], 6–2, 3–6, 6–4
* It was Djokovic's 9th title of the year and 27th of his career. It was his 5th Masters 1000 of the year and 10th of his career. It was his second win at the Rogers Cup, winning in 2007. The No. 1 of the world was 53 of 54 wins in the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2011/aug/15/novak-djokovic-rogers-cup-mardy-fish|title=Novak Djokovic wins Rogers Cup with victory over Mardy Fish in final|date= 15 August 2011|work=Guardian|accessdate=28 September 2011}}</ref>

===Women's singles===
{{main|2011 Rogers Cup – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} [[Serena Williams]] defeated {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Samantha Stosur]], 6–4, 6–2
*It was Williams' 2nd title of the year and 39th of her career. It was her 2nd title in Canada, also winning in 2001.

===Men's doubles===
{{main|2011 Rogers Cup – Men's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|FRA}} [[Michaël Llodra]] / {{flagicon|SRB}} [[Nenad Zimonjić]] defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Bob Bryan]] / {{flagicon|USA}} [[Mike Bryan]], 6–4, 6–7<sup>(5–7)</sup>, [10–5]

===Women's doubles===
{{main|2011 Rogers Cup – Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} [[Liezel Huber]] / {{flagicon|USA}} [[Lisa Raymond]] defeated {{flagicon|BLR}} [[Victoria Azarenka]] / {{flagicon|RUS}} [[Maria Kirilenko]], walkover <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rogerscup.com/women/english/currentNews.php?id=Serena_crowned_Rogers_Cup_queen |title=Serena crowned Rogers Cup queen |publisher=Rogers Cup.com |accessdate=14 August 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111002073726/http://www.rogerscup.com/women/english/currentNews.php?id=Serena_crowned_Rogers_Cup_queen |archivedate=2 October 2011 }}</ref>
