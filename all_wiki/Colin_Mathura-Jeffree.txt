'''Colin Mathura-Jeffree''' (born 12 May 1972) is a New Zealand model, actor, television host and [[spokesperson]]. He is best known to audiences for being one of the judges of ''[[New Zealand's Next Top Model]]'' and New Zealand's ''Hottest Home Baker''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://spy.nzherald.co.nz/spy-news/how-to-party-like-colin-mathura-jeffree/|title=How to party like Colin Mathura-Jeffree|last=Murray|first=Anna|website=Spy|access-date=2019-03-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/celebrities/85144912/colin-mathurajeffree-says-swastika-is-a-beautiful-symbol-wants-it-back-from-nazi-filth|title=Colin Mathura-Jeffree says swastika is a 'beautiful symbol', wants it back from 'Nazi filth'|website=Stuff|language=en|access-date=2019-03-26}}</ref>

==Career==

===Modelling===
Mathura-Jeffree has been a model for 20 years. He has graced the catwalk for iconic fashion houses such as Working Style and [[Versace]] and modelled for many international fashion magazines.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv3.co.nz/Colin-Mathura-Jeffree/tabid/1188/articleID/65057/Default.aspx|title=Colin Mathura-Jeffree biography|accessdate=29 August 2011}}</ref> Mathura-Jeffree is currently the face of [[Tourism Australia]] "shopping & shows campaign" for New Zealand working for [[APN New Zealand]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/travel/news/article.cfm?c_id=7&objectid=11141728 | work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] | title=Gold Coast: Model behaviour - Travel - NZ Herald News | date=21 October 2013}}</ref>

===Fashion and beauty===
Mathura-Jeffree was awarded the title of Special Ambassador representing the [[Miss World]] franchise holder, the New Zealand Asia Pacific Trust, at the 2007 Miss World competition in Sanya, China. He has judged pageants including the [[Ellerslie Racecourse]] Fashion in the Field contest.

In 2011 to 2017, he was the VIP Ambassador for New Zealand Fashion Week and opened the event in 2016. In the 2017 event he only wore clothes from Hospice Opportunity Shops.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://spy.nzherald.co.nz/spy-news/who-is-this-famous-kiwi/ |title=Who is this famous Kiwi?|work=New Zealand Herald|date=7 September 2017}}</ref>

Mathura-Jeffree was named New Zealand's sexiest man in 2012 by [[Metro (magazine)|Metro]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/lifestyle/news/article.cfm?c_id=6&objectid=10760999|title=Gene lottery win an easy road to riches |accessdate=2 July 2012 | work=The New Zealand Herald|date=23 October 2011}}</ref> and was named New Zealand's Man of the Year in 2012 by social page photographer Norrie Montgomery.

Mathura-Jeffree was a judge for the Miss South Pacific Beauty Pageant 2013 in the [[Solomon Islands]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.solomonstarnews.com/news/national/20270-pacific-pageant-judge-impressed|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131211173229/http://www.solomonstarnews.com/news/national/20270-pacific-pageant-judge-impressed|url-status=dead|archive-date=11 December 2013|title=Pacific pageant judge impressed|date=11 December 2013}}</ref> In 2017 he was named a REMIX Magazine Icon at their 20-year anniversary Ball featuring their 20 favourite celebrities in print.{{citation needed|date=August 2018}} He was voted by the public in the METRO Magazine Awards issue to be the 'Best Dressed Celebrity 2017'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theweekend.nz/2019/04/14/the-weekend-with-colin-mathura-jeffree/|title=The Weekend With Colin Mathura-Jeffree|date=14 April 2019|publisher=The Weekend}}</ref>

===Television===
Mathura-Jeffree's acting debut was as the Prince of India in the ''[[Xena: Warrior Princess]]'' television series (1999), where he played the reincarnation of [[Gabrielle (Xena: Warrior Princess)|Gabrielle]] into a powerful and handsome warrior willing to sacrifice himself to save his Kingdom.

Other roles include a case solving scientist in [[Lifetime Television]]'s ''[[Reckless Behavior: Caught on Tape]]'' (also called ''Stolen Life'') helping Emma Norman ([[Odette Yustman]]) opposite [[Antonio Sab√†to Jr.]]

Mathura-Jeffree has appeared as a judge and runway mentor on the New Zealand model reality series ''[[New Zealand's Next Top Model]]''. He has also hosted other TV shows, such ''as New Zealand's Hottest Home Baker' and 'Dance Masala'. In 2010, he was a guest judge on two episodes of ''[[America's Next Top Model]]''.

He is also the face of [[Tourism New Zealand]]'s American advertising campaign.

===Movies===
Mathura-Jeffree played the lead protagonist in the [[Bollywood]] movie and romantic comedy ''[[Love Has No Language]]'' opposite [[Celina Jaitley]], [[Femina Miss India|Miss India]] 2001.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1620884/|title=Love Has No Language - IMDB|accessdate=29 August 2011}}</ref>

2017 Doc Edge celebrated excellence and achievement with presenting Documentary Film Awards during the Festival. Local and international awards were presented in various categories. The 2017 awards night was held as part of the opening night gala in Auckland on Wednesday, 24 May at Q Theatre. The 2017 jury panel included Mathura-Jeffree.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://docedge.nz/festival-old/awards/|title=Festival - Documentary Edge}}</ref>

===Public speaker===
Mathura-Jeffree is a regular guest speaker on youth issues at events such as Pathways to Employment. He was a guest speaker at [[The Art of Living]] seminar alongside [[Miss Universe]] 2000 [[Lara Dutta]].{{citation needed|date=August 2018}}

==Charitable work==
In 2010 Mathura-Jeffree became a goodwill ambassador and spokesperson for the UK-based charity The Railway Children. The charity works worldwide as emergency interventions for children arriving vulnerable and alone at railway stations, particularly [[Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus|Victoria Station]] in [[Mumbai]] where the problem is acute and where Mathura-Jeffree has had personal experience. Inspired by the work of the charity, he has given a promise to raise awareness and funds for ''The Railway Children'' and to return to India to see the charity's work for himself.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/tv/3333479/TV-judge-to-be-charity-ambassador |title=TV judge to be charity ambassador|accessdate=21 February 2010}}</ref>

In 2013 Mathura-Jeffree became Alzheimers New Zealand's first champion for dementia. In this role he works alongside Alzheimers NZ in promoting wider understanding of the condition. Mathura-Jeffree's grandmother Eileen died in 1998 after a long struggle with [[Alzheimers disease]], the most common form of [[dementia]].<ref name="Alzheimers NZ">{{cite web | url=http://www.alzheimers.org.nz/41-misc-pages/280-colin-mathura-jeffree-lends-support-to-alzheimers-nz | title=Colin Mathura-Jeffree lends support to Alzheimers NZ | publisher=Alzheimers New Zealand | accessdate=16 June 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150121015317/http://www.alzheimers.org.nz/41-misc-pages/280-colin-mathura-jeffree-lends-support-to-alzheimers-nz | archive-date=21 January 2015 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

Mathura-Jeffree is a quiet supporter of a number of charities including StarShip Hospice and Cure kids.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/CU1012/S00070/celebrities-for-starship.htm/|title=Celebrities for Starship- |accessdate=6 December 2010}}</ref>
