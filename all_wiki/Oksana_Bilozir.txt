'''Oksana Volodymyrivna Bilozir''' ({{lang-uk|Оксана Володимирівна Білозір}}), née '''Rozumkevych''' ({{lang-uk|Розумкевич}}), is a [[People's Artist of Ukraine]] (1994), former [[People's Deputy of Ukraine]] and in 2005 [[Ministry of Culture (Ukraine)|Minister of Culture and Tourism of Ukraine]].

==Biography==
Oksana Bilozir was born on 30 May 1957 in Smyha [[Urban-type settlement|settlement]], [[Dubno Raion]], [[Rivne Oblast]], [[Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic|Ukrainian SSR]] (now [[Ukraine]]).<ref name="bilozir" >http://bilozir.com/index.php?Itemid=379&id=150&option=com_content&task=view&lang=ru</ref><ref name="file99"/><ref>http://muslib.ru/b12074/%D0%9E%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%B0+%D0%91%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%BE%D0%B7%D0%B8%D1%80/</ref>

===Education===
*1976 – Lviv Musical-Pedagogical College named after F. Kolessi<ref name="file99" >http://file.liga.net/person/99.html</ref>
*1981 – [[Lviv Conservatory|Lviv State Conservatory named after M. Lysenka]]
*1999 – Diplomatic Academy of Ukraine, affiliated with [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine|MFA of Ukraine]], with qualification of Master of foreign policy and diplomacy.<ref name="bilozir" /><ref name="file99" />

===Family===
'''Father''', Volodymyr Rozumkevych (b. 1930), retired<br />
'''Mother''', Nina Vasylivna (1933–2004)<br />
1st spouse, '''Ihor Bilozir''' (1955–2000), Ukrainian famous composer and singer, the frontman of [[VIA music|VIA]] "Vatra", was tragically murdered in May 2000, in [[Lviv]]<ref name="file99" /><br />
2nd spouse, '''Roman Nedzelsky''', is the Director of the state enterprise "Ukraina Gastrol'na" (eng. – Touring Ukraine) under Ministry of Culture of Ukraine<br />  
'''Sons''': Andriy Bilozir and Yaroslav Nedzelsky. Andriy is a former member of the [[Kyiv city council]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.epravda.com.ua/news/2018/11/2/642270/ Son Bilozir headed a large state-owned company], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (2 November 2018)</ref> Andriy's wife Larisa was elected to parliament in the [[2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] as an independent candidate in single-seat constituency 15 ([[Vinnytsya Oblast]]).<ref name="tabloid.pravda.com.ua">https://tabloid.pravda.com.ua/person/5d383523bda9f/</ref> Larisa's father is fellow politician [[Mykola Kucher]].<ref name="tabloid.pravda.com.ua"/>

Oksana Bilozir is close to the family of former Ukrainian President [[Viktor Yushchenko|Yushchenko]]. She is as well the [[Godparent|godmother]] of one of the daughters of [[Petro Poroshenko|Poroshenko]].<ref name="file99" />

==Artistic achievements==
Oksana Bilozir started her concert activity in 1979, as soloist of [[VIA music|VIA]] "Vatra", founded by her first husband.<ref name="bilozir" /> Since 1994 she has been the lead singer of music band "Oksana". She released 15 albums, a DVD, and 10 music films.<ref name="file99" />

==Teaching activities==
1996 – Professor, Pop Singing Department at [[Kyiv National University of Culture and Arts]], Head of Department, professor of the Kyiv State School of variety and circus arts. In 1996–1997 Oksana Bilozir was teaching at [[Kyiv State Institute of Culture]].<ref name="file99" />

==Politics==
Oksana Bilozir became the People's Deputy of Ukraine for three times, twice from the bloc [[Our Ukraine–People's Self-Defense Bloc|Our Ukraine]] (in 2002–2005 and in 2006), and from the bloc [[Our Ukraine-People's Self-Defense Bloc|Our Ukraine – People's Self-Defense]] during [[2007 Ukrainian parliamentary election|early parliamentary elections]] in 2007.<ref merged with [[United Centre]]),<ref>{{in lang|uk}}[http://zik.ua/ua/news/2008/10/16/153928 Соціально-Християнська партія України cамоліквідувалася], [[z i k]] (16 October 2008)</ref> later became a
