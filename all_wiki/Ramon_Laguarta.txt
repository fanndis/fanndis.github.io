'''Ramon Laguarta''' (born 1963<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businesstoday.in/current/corporate/who-is-ramon-laguarta-next-ceo-of-pepsico/story/281045.html|title=Who is Ramon Laguarta, the next CEO of PepsiCo- Business News|website=www.businesstoday.in|access-date=2018-08-15}}</ref> in [[Barcelona]], [[Spain]]<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.barrons.com/articles/3-things-to-know-about-pepsis-ceo-swap-1533576702|title=3 Things to Know About Pepsi’s CEO Swap|last=Strauss|first=Lawrence C.|access-date=2018-08-12|language=en-US}}</ref>) is the chairman and [[chief executive officer]] of [[PepsiCo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pepsico.com/About/Leadership|title=Leadership - PepsiCo|website=www.pepsico.com|language=en|access-date=2018-10-01}}</ref> He became CEO on October 3, 2018 after his predecessor [[Indra Nooyi]] stepped down.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/meet-pepsicos-next-ceo-ramon-laguarta-1533553260|title=Meet PepsiCo’s Next CEO: Ramon Laguarta|last=Maloney|first=Jennifer|date=2018-08-06|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2018-08-12|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> He is the sixth CEO in the company's history, and the first Spanish CEO of a large American multinational company.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/08/06/pepsicos-indra-nooyi-to-step-down-after-12-years-as-ceo-president-ra.html|title=PepsiCo's Indra Nooyi to step down as CEO, President Ramon Laguarta to succeed her|last=Cullen|first=Lauren Hirsch, Terri|date=2018-08-06|website=www.cnbc.com|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elmundo.es/economia/empresas/2018/08/08/5b69e91de5fdea410b8b467b.html|title=Ramón Laguarta, el canterano de Chupa-Chups que lanzó la Pepsi Max|date=2018-08-07|website=ELMUNDO|language=es|access-date=2020-03-09}}</ref>

== Education ==
Laguarta graduated with bachelor's and master's degrees in business administration from [[ESADE Business School]] in [[Barcelona]] in 1985. In 1986 he received a master's in international management from the [[Thunderbird School of Global Management]] at [[Arizona State University]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://fortune.com/2018/08/06/key-facts-new-pepsico-ceo-ramon-laguarta/|title=5 Things to Know About the Next PepsiCo CEO|work=Fortune|access-date=2018-08-13|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thunderbird.asu.edu/about/news-events/thunderbird-alum-ramon-laguarta-named-pepsico-ceo|title=Thunderbird Alumnus Ramon Laguarta Named PepsiCo CEO|website=Thunderbird School of Global Management|language=en|access-date=2018-10-01}}</ref> 

== Career ==
Before joining PepsiCo he worked at [[Chupa Chups]], a candy company based in Spain known for its lollipops.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.foxbusiness.com/business-leaders/pepsis-new-ceo-ramon-laguarta-five-things-to-know|title=Pepsi’s new CEO Ramon Laguarta: Five things to know|date=2018-08-06|work=Fox Business|access-date=2018-09-23|language=en-US}}</ref> Laguarta joined PepsiCo in January 1996.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/person/ramon-laguarta|title=Ramon Laguarta - Chief Executive Officer, Europe Sub-Saharan Africa @ PepsiCo {{!}} Crunchbase|website=Crunchbase|language=en|access-date=2018-09-23}}</ref> His first role was in the company’s European business, and in 2014 became the CEO of the entire Europe and Sub-Saharan Africa (ESSA) sector.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businesstoday.in/current/corporate/who-is-ramon-laguarta-next-ceo-of-pepsico/story/281045.html|title=Who is Ramon Laguarta, the next CEO of PepsiCo- Business News|website=www.businesstoday.in|access-date=2020-03-09}}</ref> While working in Europe, Laguarta helped lead the acquisition in 2010 of Russian dairy and juice company [[Wimm-Bill-Dann]], a deal valued at $5.4 billion, the company's second largest acquisition after its purchase of [[Quaker Oats Company|Quaker Oats]] in 2001.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Maloney|first=Jennifer|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/meet-pepsicos-next-ceo-ramon-laguarta-1533553260|title=Meet PepsiCo’s Next CEO: Ramon Laguarta|date=2018-08-06|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2020-03-09|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref>

Laguarta was named president of PepsiCo in September 2017. He oversaw PepsiCo's Global Category Groups, its Global Operations, Corporate Strategy, and Public Policy & Government Affairs functions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/08/06/pepsicos-indra-nooyi-to-step-down-after-12-years-as-ceo-president-ra.html|title=PepsiCo's Indra Nooyi to step down as CEO, President Ramon Laguarta to succeed her|last=Cullen|first=Lauren Hirsch, Terri|date=2018-08-06|website=www.cnbc.com|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref> As a result of the promotion, he moved to the United States.<ref name=":0" />

Laguarta was unanimously voted in as PepsiCo’s next CEO on August 6, 2018, the same day [[Indra Nooyi]] announced she was stepping down.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stewart |first1=Rebecca |title=Who is Ramon Laguarta? The marketer-turned-Pepsi CEO taking over from Indra Nooyi |url=https://www.thedrum.com/news/2018/08/06/who-ramon-laguarta-the-marketer-turned-pepsi-ceo-taking-over-indra-nooyi |website=Thedrum.com |accessdate=16 March 2020 |date=6 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/pepsico-ceo-indra-nooyi-to-step-aside-1533553261|title=PepsiCo CEO Indra Nooyi to Step Aside; Veteran Set to Take Helm|last=Maloney|first=Jennifer|date=2018-08-06|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2018-08-12|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/08/06/pepsicos-indra-nooyi-to-step-down-after-12-years-as-ceo-president-ra.html|title=PepsiCo's Indra Nooyi to step down as CEO, President Ramon Laguarta to succeed her|last=Cullen|first=Lauren Hirsch, Terri|date=2018-08-06|work=CNBC|access-date=2018-08-13}}</ref> He officially took over the role on October 3, 2018 and became Chairman of the Board of Directors on February 1, 2019.<ref name=":2" /> Laguarta has been working at PepsiCo for 24 years, his previous roles including CEO for Europe Sub-Saharan Africa, president for the PepsiCo Eastern Europe Region, commercial Vice President for PepsiCo Europe, general manager for Iberia Snacks and Juices and General Manager for Greece Snacks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fortune.com/2018/08/06/key-facts-new-pepsico-ceo-ramon-laguarta/|title=5 Things to Know About the Next PepsiCo CEO|last=Tozer|first=Alice|date=2018-08-06|website=Fortune|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-10-22}}</ref> Since becoming CEO of PepsiCo, Laguarta established three priorities to lead the company: Accelerating the company's rate of organic revenue growth; becoming a stronger company; and becoming a better company.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.foodbusinessnews.net/articles/13332-pepsico-sets-goal-to-become-faster-stronger-and-better?v=preview|title=PepsiCo sets goal to become ‘faster, stronger and better’|website=www.foodbusinessnews.net|language=en|access-date=2020-03-17}}</ref>

As part of making PepsiCo a better company, Laguarta has been tasked with implementing a new purpose behind PepsiCo’s sustainability agenda: helping to build a more sustainable food system. Under his leadership, the company is focusing its efforts and goals around agriculture, water usage, plastics, products, climate change, and human rights.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fortune.com/2019/09/24/pepsi-responsibilities-ceo-daily/|title=PepsiCo's Responsibilities: CEO Daily|website=Fortune|language=en|access-date=2020-04-28}}</ref>

This includes the company’s effort to reduce absolute greenhouse gas emissions by at least 20 percent for their entire value chain over a 2015 baseline (roughly 30-35 million metric tons of GHG)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pepsico.com/sustainability/climate-change|title=Climate Change - PepsiCo|website=www.pepsico.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>, as well as their goals by 2025 to make 100% of their packaging recyclable, compostable, or biodegradable and use 25% recycled plastic content in all plastic packaging. Laguarta has also led attempts to reduce waste by acquiring [[SodaStream]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pepsico.com/plastics|title=Plastics - PepsiCo|website=www.pepsico.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> Through the expansion of SodaStream's business, the need for an estimated 67 billion plastic bottles will be avoided through 2025.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.packagingdigest.com/sustainable-packaging/strategic-plastic-use-drives-sustainable-packaging-at-pepsico-2019-09-20|title=Strategic plastic use drives sustainable packaging at PepsiCo|date=2019-09-20|website=Packaging Digest|language=en|access-date=2020-03-19}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Laguarta speaks English, Catalan, Spanish, French, German, and Greek.<ref name=":1" /> He is married with three children.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pepsico.com/About/Leadership|title=Leadership - PepsiCo|website=www.pepsico.com|language=en|access-date=2018-10-03}}</ref>
