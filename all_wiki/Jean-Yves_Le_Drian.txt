'''Jean-Yves Le Drian''' ({{IPA-fr|ʒɑ̃.iv lə.dʁi.(j)ɑ̃}}; born 30 June 1947) is a French politician serving as [[Ministry of Europe and Foreign Affairs|Minister of Europe and Foreign Affairs]] since 2017. He previously was [[Minister of the Armies (France)|Minister of Defence]] from 2012 to 2017 under President [[François Hollande]].<ref name=point /><ref name=cv>{{cite web|title=CV: Jean-Yves LE DRIAN |url=http://ec.europa.eu/regional_policy/conferences/od2011/Open-Days-FTP-2011/docs/762-11A14-Microsoft_Word_-_00__CV__LE_DRIAN__ANGLAIS.pdf |publisher=[[European Commission]] |date=25 January 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140223083415/http://ec.europa.eu/regional_policy/conferences/od2011/Open-Days-FTP-2011/docs/762-11A14-Microsoft_Word_-_00__CV__LE_DRIAN__ANGLAIS.pdf |archive-date=23 February 2014 }}</ref> He was nominated by [[Emmanuel Macron]] to serve as Foreign Minister in the [[First Philippe government]] on 17 May 2017. A former member of the [[Socialist Party (France)|Socialist Party]], he has been an [[Independent politician|Independent]] since 2018.

==Family and education==
Jean-Yves Le Drian was born in [[Lorient]] to working-class parents, Jean and Louisette, who were active members of the [[Young Christian Workers]] (''Jeunesse ouvrière chrétienne'', JOC). He completed his studies at the [[University of Rennes 2 – Upper Brittany|University of Rennes 2]], where he was an activist for the [[Union Nationale des Étudiants de France]] (UNEF).<ref name=point>{{cite news|title=Actualité – Personnalité – Jean-Yves Le Drian|url=http://www.lepoint.fr/tags/jean-yves-le-drian|access-date=20 January 2013|newspaper=[[Le Point]]}}</ref> First of all interested by [[Breton Democratic Union]] (UDB) in the 1970s, he joined the [[Socialist Party (France)|Socialist Party]] (PS) in May 1974.<ref>{{cite news |last=Raynaudon-Kerzerho |first=Maiwenn |date=1 March 2014 |title=L'Union démocratique bretonne : un demi-siècle de lutte pour l'autonomie |trans-title=The Breton Democratic Union: half a century of fight for the autonomy |url=http://bretons-mag.com/bretons/148-numero-96-mars-2014.html |language=fr |issue=96 |newspaper=Bretons |location=Vannes/Gwened, Brittany |publisher=Les Éditions Blanc et Noir |page=38 |issn=0245-9507 }}</ref>

==Political career==
===Early functions===
In 1977, he assumed the position of Deputy Mayor of [[Lorient]]; one year later, at the age of 30, he became a member of the [[National Assembly (France)|National Assembly]] for [[Morbihan]]. He served until 1993 and then again from 1997 until 2007.<ref name="trombinoscope">"Monsieur Jean-Yves LE DRIAN" [http://www.trombinoscope.com/index.php ''Le Trombinoscope''] 29 May 2008, a commercial biographical database in French.</ref> He became Mayor of Lorient in 1981 and retained the office until 1988; he also served as [[Minister of the Sea (France)|Secretary of State for the Sea]] under President [[François Mitterrand]] from 1991 to 1992.

In the 2004 regional elections, leading the ''Bretagne à gauche, Bretagne pour tous'' (PS-PCF-PRG-Les Verts-UDB) list, he won 58.66% of the vote in the runoff and a total of 58 seats in the [[Regional Council of Brittany]]. He thus became [[List of current presidents of the regional councils of France and the Corsican Assembly|President of the Regional Council]] of [[Brittany (region of France)|Brittany]]. In October 2010 he became President of the Conference of Peripheral Maritime Regions of Europe (CPMR).<ref name=cpmr>{{cite news|title=Jean-Yves Le Drian is the new French Minister for Defence |url=http://news.cpmr.org/uncategorized/jean-yves-le-drian-is-the-new-french-minister-for-defence/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131019152403/http://news.cpmr.org/uncategorized/jean-yves-le-drian-is-the-new-french-minister-for-defence/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=19 October 2013 |access-date=20 January 2013 |newspaper=Conference of Peripheral Maritime Regions of Europe |date=18 May 2012 }}</ref>

===Minister of Defence===
[[File:Nouveau plan stratégique de l’École polytechnique cropped.jpg|thumb|left|Jean-Yves Le Drian at [[École Polytechnique]] in 2015, with [[Emmanuel Macron]], then Minister of the Economy, Industry and Digital Affairs]]
[[File:Prime Minister Narendra Modi in talks with French Defence Minister of France Jean-Yves Le Drian.jpg|thumb|left|Le Drian with Indian Prime Minister [[Narendra Modi]], 2016]]
He was nominated to serve as [[Minister of the Armies (France)|Minister of Defence]] under President [[François Hollande]] on 16 May 2012.<ref name=cpmr /> He managed the withdrawal of French troops from [[War in Afghanistan (2001–present)|Afghanistan]] and the deployment of French troops in the [[Northern Mali conflict (2012–present)|Northern Mali conflict]] and [[Operation Barkhane]]. He is also credited with leading a resurgence in French weapons' exports that have resulted in billions of euros in deals, including the first exports of the [[Dassault Rafale]] fighter jet.<ref>[https://www.reuters.com/article/us-france-election-government-ministers-idUSKCN18D1SA Factbox: Ministers in new French government] ''[[Reuters]]'', May 17, 2017.</ref>

On 23 March 2017, Le Drian endorsed [[Emmanuel Macron]]'s candidacy for President of the Republic.<ref>{{cite web|title=French election: Defence Minister Le Drian defects to Macron|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-39375491|website=BBC|access-date=23 March 2017|date=23 March 2017}}</ref> After Macron won the [[2017 French presidential election|presidential election]], he appointed Le Drian as Foreign Minister in the [[First Philippe government]].

===Minister of Europe and Foreign Affairs===
Le Drian remained in office when the [[Second Philippe government]] was inaugurated; on 8 March 2018, he officially resigned from the Socialist Party.

In August 2019, Le Drian called on [[Hong Kong]] authorities to renew talks with [[2019–20 Hong Kong protests|Hong Kong protesters]] to find a peaceful solution to the then ongoing crisis.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.yahoo.com/france-urges-hong-kong-authorities-173045521.html|title=France urges Hong Kong authorities to renew dialogue with protesters|first= Gareth |last= Jones|date=15 August 2019|work=Yahoo news}}</ref> On 9 October 2019, Le Drian condemned the unilateral [[2019 Turkish offensive into north-eastern Syria|Turkish operation in Northeastern Syria]] and declared that Turkey's military incursion "is jeopardising the anti-Islamic State coalition’s security and humanitarian efforts and is a risk for the security of Europeans".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-syria-security-turkey-france/france-says-turkey-operation-jeopardises-security-and-humanitarian-efforts-idUKKBN1WO2LW|title=France says Turkey operation jeopardises security and humanitarian efforts|date=9 October 2019}}</ref>
