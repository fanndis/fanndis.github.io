'''William Cowan Rennie''' (born 27 September 1967), also known as '''Willie Rennie''', is a Scottish politician serving as Leader of the [[Scottish Liberal Democrats]] since [[2011 Scottish Liberal Democrats leadership election|2011]]. He has served as the [[Member of the Scottish Parliament]] (MSP) for [[North East Fife (Scottish Parliament constituency)|North East Fife]] since [[2016 Scottish Parliament election|2016]], and previously as MSP for [[Mid Scotland and Fife (Scottish Parliament electoral region)|Mid Scotland and Fife]] (from [[2011 Scottish Parliament election|2011]] to 2016) and as [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Dunfermline and West Fife (UK Parliament constituency)|Dunfermline and West Fife]] (from [[2006 Dunfermline and West Fife by-election|2006]] to [[2010 United Kingdom general election|2010]]).

After college, Rennie was a Liberal Democrat election campaigner and official before working as a [[public relations]] consultant in the private sector. He became the MP for Dunfermline and West Fife after a by-election win in February 2006. He later lost this seat to [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] in the May 2010 UK general election. He briefly served as a [[Special advisers (UK government)|Special Government Adviser]] for the Liberal Democrat [[Secretary of State for Scotland|Scottish Secretaries of State]] [[Danny Alexander]] MP and [[Michael Moore (UK politician)|Michael Moore]] MP at the [[Scotland Office]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/politics/10285825.stm|title=BBC News – David Cameron's PR director Andy Coulson paid 拢140,000|work=BBC News}}</ref>

He was then elected to the [[Scottish Parliament]] in the May 2011 election. He was elected as an [[Mixed member proportional representation|additional member]] for the Mid Scotland and Fife region.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://perthlibdems.blogspot.com/2010/09/regional-list-results.html|title=Perth and Kinross Liberal Democrats|author=Victor Clements|work=perthlibdems.blogspot.com|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708060336/http://perthlibdems.blogspot.com/2010/09/regional-list-results.html|archivedate=8 July 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> He was soon after elected unopposed as leader of the Scottish Liberal Democrats, replacing [[Tavish Scott]]. Since the 2016 Scottish Parliament election, Rennie has held the constituency seat of North East Fife.

==Early life and education==
Rennie was born in [[Fife]] and grew up in [[Strathmiglo]], where his family ran the village shop and still live today. His mother was secretary of the local community association and his grandfather was the local [[Minister (Christianity)|Minister]].

Rennie went to [[Bell Baxter High School]] in [[Cupar|Cupar, Fife]], before going to [[Paisley College of Technology]], where he graduated with a B.Sc. degree in Biology. After that, he received a Diploma in Industrial Administration at [[Glasgow College]].

Rennie lives in [[Kelty]] with his wife Janet and their two sons, Alexander and Stephen.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thecourier.co.uk/News/National/article/13908/kelty-boy-willie-rennie-to-replace-tavish-scott-as-leader-of-scottish-liberal-democrats.html|title=Error messages – The Courier – At the heart of Tayside and Fife|work=thecourier.co.uk|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110517150240/http://www.thecourier.co.uk/News/National/article/13908/kelty-boy-willie-rennie-to-replace-tavish-scott-as-leader-of-scottish-liberal-democrats.html|archivedate=17 May 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> He is a keen runner and is a member of [[Dunfermline]]'s PH Racing Club. He was also runner-up in the 2006 Scottish Coal-Carrying Championships held in Kelty. Rennie was one of the 50 MPs who ran a mile to raise money for [[Sport Relief]]<ref>[http://icwales.icnetwork.co.uk/news/politics-news/2008/02/28/welsh-glory-as-mps-run-a-mile-for-charity-91466-20534694/ Welsh glory as MPs run a mile for charity]</ref> finishing close behind the winner, [[David Davies (Welsh politician)|David Davies]].

==Early political career: 1990–2001==
While a student at the [[Paisley College of Technology]] he was deputy president of the [[students' union|student union]]. Rennie ran the Scottish Young Liberal Democrats (later reformed as [[Liberal Youth Scotland]]) and after graduation went on to work for the [[English Liberal Democrats]] in [[Cornwall]].

He then went on to work for the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]]' [[political campaign|campaigns department]], and was the successful [[Election agent|agent]] in the [[1993 Christchurch by-election]] in [[Dorset]].

After managing the party's campaigns in the [[South West England]] region, securing the return of a sizeable number of new MPs in the [[1997 United Kingdom general election|1997 General Election]], he moved back to Scotland where he was Chief Executive of the [[Scottish Liberal Democrats]] from 1997 to 1999, and then the party's [[Chief of Staff]] in the new [[Scottish Parliament]] from 1999–2001.

== McEwan Purvis: 2001–2006 ==
From 2001 to 2006 he worked for the small Scottish communications firm [[McEwan Purvis]] as an account director helping advise businesses and charities such as the [[Royal Society of Chemistry]] and [[Asthma UK]]. During his time at McEwan Purvis, Rennie was a press adviser to [[Fife Council]]'s Liberal Democrat Opposition Group and a member of the Dunfermline Focus editorial team, working with Dunfermline's Lib Dem councillors on local issues.

== Member of Parliament: 2006–2010 ==
After the Labour MP [[Rachel Squire]] died, Rennie stood in the [[2006 Dunfermline and West Fife by-election|Dunfermline and West Fife by-election]] on 9 February 2006 and overturned a Labour majority of 11,800 to win the seat with a swing to his party of 16.24%.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/4695270.stm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-03-23 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061223231741/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/4695270.stm |archivedate=23 December 2006 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> In the House of Commons, he was a member of the Liberal Democrat defence spokesperson team, chair of their parliamentary campaigns unit, and a member of the [[Commons Defence Select Committee]].

During his time as an MP, he campaigned on local constituency issues such as abolishing the bridge tolls, changing the law to protect female learner drivers from sex offenders, improvements to cancer services at [[Queen Margaret Hospital]], and local jobs (including at [[Longannet Power Station]] and [[Rosyth Dockyard]]).

In the [[2010 United Kingdom general election|General Election of 6 May 2010]], Rennie lost his seat to the Labour candidate [[Thomas Docherty (politician)|Thomas Docherty]].

==Special Adviser and consultant: 2010–2011==

He was then for a time [[Special advisers (UK government)|Special Adviser]] to the new Liberal Democrat Scottish Secretary [[Michael Moore (UK politician)|Michael Moore]] [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|MP]].

==Elected MSP and Leader of the Scottish Liberal Democrats: 2011–present==
[[File:Willie Rennie MSP.jpg|thumb|left|Rennie speaking in 2013]]
Willie Rennie returned to front-line politics as an [[Member of the Scottish Parliament|MSP]] when he won a regional list seat for the Liberal Democrats in the Scottish Parliament's [[Mid Scotland and Fife electoral region|Mid Scotland and Fife region]] at the Holyrood elections on 5 May 2011. He was the only new Liberal Democrat MSP to win a seat in this election.

After the resignation of the [[Scottish Liberal Democrats|Scottish Liberal Democrats's]] leader [[Tavish Scott]], Willie Rennie stood in the [[2011 Scottish Liberal Democrats leadership election|resulting leadership election]]. He was the only candidate to be nominated, and was declared elected when the nominations closed on 17 May. He vowed to stand up to the "[[Scottish National Party|SNP]] bulldozer" majority, and refused to distance his party from the UK [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-13411906|title=BBC News – Willie Rennie named new Scottish Lib Dem leader|work=BBC News|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110829082131/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-13411906|archivedate=29 August 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

In the [[2016 Scottish Parliament election]], the Liberal Democrats again elected five MSPs, while gaining two constituency seats and holding their existing two with increased majorities. Rennie was elected in [[North East Fife (Scottish Parliament constituency)|North East Fife]], gaining it from the SNP with a 9.5% swing.

===Home Rule commission plan===
{{further|Full fiscal autonomy for Scotland}}
In September 2011, Willie Rennie announced plans for a commission building on the work of 2006 [[Lord Steel]] Commission to develop a blueprint for [[Home Rule]] and [[full fiscal federalism]] that would map out further devolution of powers after the [[Scotland Bill 2011]] was passed into statute.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.holyrood.com/articles/2011/09/19/lib-dems-to-float-commission-on-home-rule-for-scotland/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-09-25 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111116202908/http://www.holyrood.com/articles/2011/09/19/lib-dems-to-float-commission-on-home-rule-for-scotland/ |archivedate=16 November 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-14977877|title=BBC News – Scottish Lib Dems to set up new powers commission|work=BBC News|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110920104958/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-14977877|archivedate=20 September 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Armed Forces Legal Action===
Willie Rennie was appointed as an honorary patron of Armed Forces Legal Action (AFLA) in April 2014. AFLA is a network of UK law firms committed to offering discounted legal services to members of the UK Armed Forces community. AFLA was founded by Scottish solicitor and former Scottish Liberal Democrat parliamentary candidate, Allan Steele, WS.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://armedforceslegalaction.org.uk|title=AFLA|publisher=AFLA|accessdate=6 June 2014|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140528071854/http://armedforceslegalaction.org.uk/|archivedate=28 May 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
