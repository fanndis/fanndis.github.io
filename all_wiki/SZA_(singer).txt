'''Solána Imani Rowe''' (born November 8, 1990),<ref>{{Cite web|last=Roth|first=Madeline|date=2017-11-09|title=SZA Got The ‘Best Bday Gift Ever’ When Her Fans Made A Non-Single Go Platinum|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3046668/sza-the-weekend-platinum-birthday-gift/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-10-02|website=MTV News|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hahn|first=Rachel|date=2017-11-08|title=Birthday Girl SZA Puts a Chic Spin on the Camo Trend|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/sza-birthday-scorpio-camouflage-pant-heels|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-10-02|website=Vogue|language=en-us}}</ref> known professionally as '''SZA''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|ɪ|z|ə}}),<ref name="MeetSZA">{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/meet-sza-kendrick-lamars-newest-labelmate-54484/ |title=Meet SZA, Kendrick Lamar's Newest Labelmate |first=Marissa G. |last=Muller |date=November 4, 2013 |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |accessdate=11 May 2018 |quote=For the record, her name is pronounced Sizza, like RZA and GZA. |archive-date=March 13, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200313233142/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/meet-sza-kendrick-lamars-newest-labelmate-54484/ |url-status=live }}</ref> is an American [[Singing|singer]], rapper, and [[songwriter]]. In October 2012, SZA [[self-released]] her debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[See.SZA.Run]]'', which she then followed up with her second EP, ''[[S (EP)|S]]'', in April 2013. In July 2013, she signed with the [[hip hop]] record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]], through which she released ''[[Z (EP)|Z]]'', her third EP and first retail release, in April 2014.<ref name="Billboard-Z" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.biography.com/people/sza |title=SZA |website=Biography |language=en-us |access-date=2019-04-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414174719/https://www.biography.com/people/sza |archive-date=2019-04-14 |url-status=dead}}</ref> In March 2020, it was declared by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' that SZA had signed with [[Endeavor (company)|WME]] for representation in all areas.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite web |url=https://www.revolt.tv/news/2020/3/3/21163364/sza-signs-wme-representation-all-areas |title=SZA signs with WME talent agency |last=Gunn |first=Tamantha |date=March 3, 2020 |website=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]] |access-date=March 6, 2020}}</ref>

SZA's first studio album, ''[[Ctrl (SZA album)|Ctrl]]'', was released on June 9, 2017, to universal acclaim from music critics.<ref>{{Cite web |title=CTRL by SZA |url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/ctrl/sza |work=Metacritic |accessdate=2017-12-01 |archive-date=October 8, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171008072915/http://www.metacritic.com/music/ctrl/sza |url-status=live }}</ref> It debuted at number three on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and was eventually certified [[Platinum certification|platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). "The Weekend" from her ''Ctrl'' album peaked at #29 which is her highest charting solo song.<ref name="SZAChartHistory">{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/sza |title=SZA Chart History |website=Billboard |access-date=2019-05-07 |archive-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414233319/https://www.billboard.com/music/sza |url-status=live }}</ref> The album and its songs were nominated for four [[Grammy Award]]s, while SZA was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] at the [[60th Annual Grammy Awards|60th annual ceremony]]. ''Ctrl'' was ranked as the best album of 2017 by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''.<ref name=":3" /> SZA collaborated with [[Maroon 5]] for their 2017 record "[[What Lovers Do]]," earning SZA her first US Top 10 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name="SZAChartHistory" /> She later followed that by featuring on the 2018 ''[[Black Panther (soundtrack)|Black Panther]]'' record with [[Kendrick Lamar]], "[[All the Stars]]," which peaked at number 7 in the US and was also nominated for the [[Golden Globe Award|Golden Globe]] and the [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] for Best Original Song.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.essence.com/entertainment/sza-kendrick-lamar-all-the-stars-oscar-nomination/ |title=All The Stars: SZA And Kendrick Lamar Are Now Oscar-Nominated |first=Rachaell |last=Davis |date=January 22, 2019 |website=Essence |language=en-US |access-date=2019-05-21 |archive-date=July 9, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190709034914/https://www.essence.com/entertainment/sza-kendrick-lamar-all-the-stars-oscar-nomination/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

SZA is a [[neo soul]] singer, whose music has been described as [[alternative R&B]], with elements of [[soul music|soul]], [[hip hop]], minimalist R&B, [[cloud rap]], [[witch house (music genre)|witch house]], and [[chillwave]].<ref name="Guardian" /> SZA's lyrics are described as "unravelling" and her songs often revolve around themes of [[Human sexuality|sexuality]], [[nostalgia]], and [[Abandonment (emotional)|abandonment]].<ref name="neontommy.com" />

==Early life==
Solána Imani Rowe was born in [[St. Louis, Missouri]] and raised in [[Maplewood, New Jersey]].<ref name="Complex">{{cite web |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/who-is-sza/ |first=Insanul |last=Ahmed |title=Who is SZA? |work=Complex Music |date=September 8, 2013 |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=April 29, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190429221725/http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/who-is-sza/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Her father was an executive producer at [[CNN]], while her mother was an executive at [[AT&T]].<ref name="Morris">{{Cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/how-sza-beat-depression-and-cracked-the-top-10-with-ctrl-128463/ |title=How SZA Beat Depression and Cracked the Top 10 With 'Ctrl' |last=Morris |first=Alex |date=2017-08-30 |website=Rolling Stone |language=en-US |access-date=2019-04-14 |archive-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414181021/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/how-sza-beat-depression-and-cracked-the-top-10-with-ctrl-128463/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Rowe was born to a [[Christians|Christian]] mother and a [[Muslim]] father.<ref name="Morris" /> She has an older half-sister named Tiffany Daniels, and an older brother, Daniel, a rapper named Manhattan.<ref name="Morris" /><ref name="Complex" /> She was raised as an orthodox Muslim.<ref name="Complex" />
<blockquote>It's like the belief in one God, all the pillars of Islam et cetera, and I think those are ideas that will never leave me, those make sense in my spirit. It's the way that I connect with God; it has always made sense to me. I think I would love to wear my [[hijab]] but I feel like I don't wanna wear my hijab and talk crazy on stage and be in videos with [[Travis Scott]]. Like I don't wanna be disrespectful because I have too much love and respect for the religion, for my father, and for myself.<ref name="HOT97">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XnN7p1lZU2k |title=Sza On CTRL, Relating to Rihanna, Standing for Pharrell & Her Faith |author=HOT 97 |date=6 June 2017 |work=YouTube |access-date=15 April 2020}}</ref></blockquote>
She attended a [[Muslim]] prep school every day after her regular schooling. Rowe attended [[Columbia High School (New Jersey)|Columbia High School]], where she was very active in sports, including [[gymnastics]] and [[cheerleading]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-sza-new-video.41999.html |title=Everything You Need To Know About SZA |first=Danny |last=Schwartz |date=June 14, 2017 |work=HotNewHipHop |access-date=2017-10-17 |archive-date=October 18, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171018074412/https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-sza-new-video.41999.html |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="HOT97" /> However, due to the [[September 11 attacks]], Rowe was subjected to bullying in 7th grade, leading her to stop wearing her [[hijab]].<ref name="Complex"/> As a high school sophomore, she was ranked among the best gymnasts in the United States.<ref name=":0" />

After graduating from high school in 2008,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/06/sza-columbia-high-school-the-weekend/ |title=SZA gives motivational speech at old high school, performs "The Weekend": Watch |first=Lake |last=Schatz |date=2018-06-05 |website=Consequence of Sound |language=en-US |access-date=2019-05-01}}</ref> Rowe later went to three separate colleges, finally settling at [[Delaware State University]] to study marine biology.<ref name="Morris" /><ref name="Complex" /> She eventually dropped out in her last semester; however, she immediately started taking on random jobs in order to make money.<ref name="Morris" /><ref name="BillboardMay2013">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1563547/sza-talks-quitting-her-day-job-working-with-other-emile-haynie |first=Reggie |last=Ugwu |title=SZA Talks Quitting Her Day Job, Working With Holy Other & Emile Haynie |work=Billboard |date=May 23, 2013 |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=May 11, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190511112454/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1563547/sza-talks-quitting-her-day-job-working-with-other-emile-haynie |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theringer.com/2017/6/9/16039410/sza-ctrl-30bc177676ef |title=SZA Takes Back Her 'Ctrl' |last=Peters |first=Micah |date=2017-06-09 |website=The Ringer |access-date=2019-04-14 |archive-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414183145/https://www.theringer.com/2017/6/9/16039410/sza-ctrl-30bc177676ef |url-status=live }}</ref> Rowe formed her stage name from the [[Supreme Alphabet]], taking influence from rapper [[RZA]]. The last two letters in her name stand for Zig-Zag and [[Allah]], while the first letter S can mean either savior or sovereign.<ref name="Complex" />

==Career==
===2011–14: Career beginnings and EPs===
{{Main|See.SZA.Run|S (EP)|l2=S|Z (EP)|l3=Z}}
[[File:SZA, August 2013.jpg|thumb|left|236x236px|SZA performing in [[Toronto]] in 2013]]

SZA first met members of [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] during the CMJ New Music Report in 2011, when her boyfriend's clothing company sponsored a show in which [[Kendrick Lamar]] was performing. Her early music was given to TDE president [[Punch (rapper)|Terrence "Punch" Henderson]], who was surprised with the quality of the material.<ref name="Billboard-Z">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6041313/sza-talks-z-album-being-the-only-girl-in-top-dawg-entertainment |title=SZA Talks 'Z' Album & Being the Only Girl In Top Dawg Entertainment |first=Reggie |last=Ugwu |date=April 7, 2014 |website=Billboard |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=June 14, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180614224345/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6041313/sza-talks-z-album-being-the-only-girl-in-top-dawg-entertainment |url-status=live }}</ref> The two stayed in touch, and after SZA began generating buzz with the release of her two EPs, TDE stepped in to sign her the label's first female artist in 2013. SZA's early music was recorded with her friends and neighbors in which they "stole a bunch of beats off the Internet".<ref name="BillboardMay2013" /> On October 29, 2012, SZA [[self-released]] her debut [[extended play|EP]] ''[[See.SZA.Run]]''.<ref name="Complex-S-EP">{{cite web |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/ep-premiere-sza-see-sza-run |title=EP Premiere: SZA "See SZA Run" |first=Zach |last=Frydenlund |date=October 29, 2012 |website=Complex Music |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=August 1, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180801004224/http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/ep-premiere-sza-see-sza-run |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Artistry==

SZA's vocal style has been described as taking on the "lilt" of a [[jazz]] singer.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nowtoronto.com/music/story.cfm?content=197575 |title=SZA: "Z" (Top Dawg Entertainment) |first=Kevin |last=Ritchie |date=April 17, 2014 |website=Toronto Now |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=July 22, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140722172142/http://www.nowtoronto.com/music/story.cfm?content=197575 |url-status=dead }}</ref> SZA is known as TDE's first female signee and first singer, which also drew attention during the early stages of her career.<ref name="huffingtonpost.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/07/23/sza-interview_n_5610659.html |title=You Probably Don't Know Rising R&B Star SZA Yet, But You Will |first=Joseph |last=Erbentraut |date=July 23, 2014 |work=The Huffington Post |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=September 24, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924204519/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/07/23/sza-interview_n_5610659.html |url-status=live }}</ref> According to Marissa G. Muller of ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine, Rowe's vocals alternate between a "vapory husk and a sky-high falsetto".<ref name="MeetSZA" /> Jordan Sargent of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' magazine labelled Rowe's vocals as being "chillwave" and "ethereal".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19278-sza-z/ |title=SZA "Z" |first=Jordan |last=Sargent |date=April 18, 2014 |work=Pitchfork |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=April 11, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190411211108/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19278-sza-z/ |url-status=live }}</ref> SZA disputes her music being labelled as hip-hop, R&B and pop, stating she often listens to a variety of music including [[Stevie Nicks]], classical jazz, folk, and rap. Further, she said: "when you try to label it [her music], you remove the option for it to be limitless. It diminishes the music."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rap-up.com/2017/10/21/sza-reveals-album-with-tame-impala-mark-ronson/ |title=SZA Reveals Album with Tame Impala and Mark Ronson |date=October 21, 2017 |work=Rap-Up |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=July 31, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180731161900/http://www.rap-up.com/2017/10/21/sza-reveals-album-with-tame-impala-mark-ronson/ |url-status=live }}</ref> SZA began writing songs due to being "passionate" about writing, and enjoyed poetry; when writing lyrics, SZA "freestyles" them in order to express whatever comes to her "mind", noting that it does not always make sense to herself.<ref name="BillboardMay2013" /> Thematically, SZA's work contains "unravelling lyrics", that touch upon themes of [[Human sexuality|sexuality]], [[nostalgia]], and [[Abandonment (emotional)|abandonment]].<ref name="neontommy.com" />

SZA's musical style has been described as "alt R&B".<ref name="Guardian">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/mar/08/sza |title=New Band Of The Day: SZA |first=Paul |last=Lester |date=March 8, 2013 |newspaper=The Guardian |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=November 17, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181117081536/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/mar/08/sza |url-status=live }}</ref> SZA's songs are built over "layers of sliced, delayed, and reversed vocals" and contains "twists and mutates".<ref name="neontommy.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.neontommy.com/news/2014/04/sza-z-album-review |title=SZA: 'Z' Album Review |first=Ben |last=Benjamin |date=April 10, 2014 |website=Neon Tommy |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180731213458/http://www.neontommy.com/news/2014/04/sza-z-album-review |archive-date=2018-07-31 |access-date=15 April 2020}}</ref><ref name="Complex-S-EP" /> Reggie Ugwu from [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] magazine finds her musical style to feature an "agnostic utopia dripping with mood", that straddles the "line between minimalist R&B, '80s synth pop and soul".<ref name="Billboard-Z" /> Rowe's music is primarily [[PBR&B]] and [[neo-soul]], but has been noted for taking influences from a broad variety of genres including [[soul music|soul]], [[hip hop]], minimalist R&B, cloud rap, ethereal R&B, [[witch house (music genre)|witch house]] and [[chillwave]] elements.<ref name="Guardian" /> Michael Madden described SZA's musical genre as being "agnosticism corresponds", noting that her work is not just one style of music and is versatile, noting the musical style is not just "R&B, pop, soul, or one thing at all".<ref name="consequenceofsound1">{{cite magazine |url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/04/album-review-sza-z/ |title=SZA – Z &#124; Album Reviews |last=Madden |first=Michael |date=April 8, 2014 |magazine=Consequence of Sound |accessdate=April 12, 2014 |archive-date=April 13, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140413131104/http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/04/album-review-sza-z/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

SZA listens to [[Ella Fitzgerald]] for vocal influence,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.redbull.com/en/music/stories/1331643712041/interview-with-up-and-coming-rnb-star-sza |title=Five things you should know about SZA |first=Aimee |last=Cliff |date=April 7, 2014 |website=Redbull.com |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=May 18, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160518173347/http://www.redbull.com/en/music/stories/1331643712041/interview-with-up-and-coming-rnb-star-sza |url-status=live }}</ref> and has said that [[Lauryn Hill]] is one of her personal influences.<ref name="Vogue" /> SZA also cites a wide range of musical artists as influences,<ref name="BillboardMay2013" /> including [[Kameelah Williams|Meelah]], the [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]], [[LFO (British band)|LFO]], [[Macy Gray]], [[Common (rapper)|Common]], [[Björk]], [[Jamiroquai]], and "a lot of [[Wu-Tang Clan|Wu]], [[Nas]], [[Mos Def]], [[Jay Z|Hov]]".<ref name="Complex" /> Speaking on her influences, SZA said: "[My] personal influences came from dancing with [[American Ballet Theatre]] and doing pieces to Björk [music]. That's the only time I had really any outside influence to music. So, the people that I fell in love with on a musical level were always much older. [[Jamiroquai]] is just, like, the shit for me."<ref name="refinery29.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.refinery29.com/sza-interview |title=SZA's Unlikely Path To Pop Stardom |first=Nathan |last=Reese |date=October 9, 2013 |website=Refinery29 |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=August 9, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180809090838/http://www.refinery29.com/sza-interview |url-status=live }}</ref> In an interview for [[Live Nation Entertainment]], SZA described the meeting she had with [[Beyoncé]] for the writing of the song "[[Feeling Myself (Nicki Minaj song)|Feeling Myself]]", affirming "Beyoncé might be the most perfect, beautiful being I've ever met in my whole life. She's the most inspirational woman on earth, next to my mother". SZA also spoke about [[Rihanna]], admiring her strong and confident attitude, of someone who does only the music she wants to do, and that if there's anybody that she could imagine singing her words, it would be Rihanna.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sza Talks Beyoncé, Rihanna, and the Importance of Staying Present|url=http://www.lntvglobal.com/en-fr/article/sza-talks-beyonce-rihanna-and-the-importance-of-staying-present/|access-date=2020-08-30|website=LNTV}}</ref>

During an interview, SZA said she is less inspired by strictly music, and more inspired by creating [[art]] in general; she has looked up to people who were not "typical artists" including her "favorite gymnast, ice-skater, saxophonist, painter, or movie director," continuing to say she was particularly inspired by film director [[Spike Lee]].<ref name="refinery29.com" /> During an interview with ''[[W (magazine)|W]]'', SZA spoke on her style influences, saying a large amount of her style inspiration comes from movies, including [[Wes Anderson]] films, praising his use of "pantone color palette" and that she "would love to dress like a character from ''[[Moonrise Kingdom]]''. Or perhaps [[Bill Murray]] in ''[[The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou|The Life Aquatic]]''."<ref name="WMag">{{cite web |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/sza-hair-style/ |title=She's Just SZA |first=Sarah |last=Leon |date=April 9, 2014 |website=W Magazine |access-date=15 April 2020}}</ref> Along with her music, SZA's image has been compared to neo-soul artists Lauryn Hill and [[Erykah Badu]].<ref name="huffingtonpost.com" /> SZA's hair became a point of interest during the early stages of her career and she discussed it in interviews with [[Vogue (magazine)|''Vogue'']] and ''[[Harper's Bazaar]]''.<ref name="Vogue">{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/vogue-daily/article/why-sza-huge-natural-hair-is-our-new-obsession/#1 |title=Why SZA's Huge Natural Hair is Our New Obsession |first=Chioma |last=Nnadi |date=March 27, 2014 |website=Vogue |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140801075903/http://www.vogue.com/vogue-daily/article/why-sza-huge-natural-hair-is-our-new-obsession/#1 |archive-date=2014-08-01 |access-date=15 April 2020}}</ref> During her performances, SZA tends to wear "free-flowing" clothes that are easy to move around in and has also been known to wear pajamas or baggy clothing onstage.<ref name="nytimes.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/21/fashion/sza-pitchfork-festival-style.html |title=SZA's Pitchfork Festival Style |first=Elizabeth |last=Bristow |date=July 21, 2014 |work=The New York Times |access-date=15 April 2020 |archive-date=August 9, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180809073817/https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/21/fashion/sza-pitchfork-festival-style.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Awards and nominations ==
{{Main|List of awards and nominations received by SZA}}
Throughout her career, SZA has earned nine [[Grammy]] nominations, a [[Golden Globe]] nomination, and an [[Academy Award]] nomination. She has earned one [[Billboard Music Awards|Billboard Music Award]] and received the "Rulebreaker Award" at the [[Billboard Women in Music]] event in 2018. SZA also won the [[Soul Train Music Award for Best New Artist]] in 2017 and the [[BET Award for Best New Artist]] in 2018.
