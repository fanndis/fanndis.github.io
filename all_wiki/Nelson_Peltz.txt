'''Nelson Peltz''' (born June 24, 1942) is an American [[billionaire]] businessman and investor. He is a founding partner, together with Peter W. May and Edward P. Garden, of Trian Fund Management, an [[alternative investment]] management fund based in New York.<ref name="BW">{{cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=26222634|title=Company Overview of Trian Fund Management, L.P.|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]]|accessdate=April 4, 2012|archive-date=October 18, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121018200908/http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=26222634|url-status=live}}</ref> He is non-executive chairman of [[Wendy's Company]], and a director of [[Legg Mason]],<ref name="Wendys" /> [[Procter & Gamble]], [[Sysco]], and [[The Madison Square Garden Company]]. He is a former director of [[H.J. Heinz Company]], [[Mondelēz International]],<ref name="bloomberg1">{{cite web |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-01-21/mondelez-international-adds-investor-nelson-peltz-to-its-board.html |title=Mondelez Names Peltz Director as Investor Seen Pushing Cuts |author=Duane D. Stanford |publisher=[[Bloomberg L.P.|Bloomberg]] |date=January 21, 2014 |access-date=2017-03-10 |archive-date=2014-08-18 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140818051056/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-01-21/mondelez-international-adds-investor-nelson-peltz-to-its-board.html |url-status=live }}</ref> and [[Ingersoll Rand]]<ref name="wsj1">{{cite web |url=https://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10000872396390444318104577585463456683338 |title=Activist Peltz Wins Board Seat At Ingersoll |author=Anupreeta Das and Sharon Terlep |publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]] |date=August 13, 2012 |access-date=March 14, 2017 |archive-date=March 7, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140307064415/http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10000872396390444318104577585463456683338 |url-status=live }}</ref> and a former CEO of Triangle Industries.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/fortune500_archive/full/1988 |title=Fortune 500 |publisher=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]] |accessdate=April 3, 2012 |archive-date=May 1, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120501055752/http://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/fortune500_archive/full/1988/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Early life and education==
Peltz was born to a [[Jewish]] family in 1942 in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]], the son of Claire (''née'' Wechsler; 1905–2007) and Maurice Herbert Peltz (1901–1977).<ref name="JewishVirtualLibrary"/><ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/1977/09/06/archives/maurice-peltz-76-built-family-food-business-into-a-major-marketer.html | work=The New York Times | title=Maurice Peltz, 76, Built Family Food Business Into a Major Marketer | date=1977-09-06 | access-date=2018-07-23 | archive-date=2018-07-23 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180723212435/https://www.nytimes.com/1977/09/06/archives/maurice-peltz-76-built-family-food-business-into-a-major-marketer.html | url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F04E3DD123AF931A25757C0A9619C8B63 | work=The New York Times | title=Paid Notice: Deaths Peltz, Claire | date=2007-04-12 | access-date=2017-02-11 | archive-date=2012-11-10 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121110102510/http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F04E3DD123AF931A25757C0A9619C8B63 | url-status=live }}</ref> He was the second of their two children, and grew up in the [[East_New_York,_Brooklyn#Cypress_Hills|Cypress Hills]] section of Brooklyn, a sub-section of the [[East New York]] neighborhood.<ref>[https://www.google.com/books/edition/Current_Biography_Yearbook/EWERAQAAMAAJ?hl=en&gbpv=1&bsq=Nelson+Peltz+brooklyn+wechsler&dq=Nelson+Peltz+brooklyn+wechsler&printsec=frontcover]</ref> 

He attended [[Horace Mann School]] in [[the Bronx]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3erIDwAAQBAJ&q=Nelson+Peltz+cypress+hills&pg=PA109|title=The Predators' Ball: The Inside Story of Drexel Burnham and the Rise of the Junk Bond Raiders|first=Connie|last=Bruck|date=February 4, 2020|publisher=Simon and Schuster|isbn=9781982144265|via=Google Books}}</ref> Peltz attended the undergraduate program at the [[Wharton School]] of the [[University of Pennsylvania]] starting in 1960,<ref name="auto"/> where he joined the fraternity of [[Phi Gamma Delta]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Wall Street Frats|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/wall-street-frats-2013-2#phi-gamma-delta--17|publisher=Business Insider|accessdate=20 February 2013|archive-date=16 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130216040516/http://www.businessinsider.com/wall-street-frats-2013-2#phi-gamma-delta--17|url-status=live}}</ref> but dropped out in 1963 and  never completed a degree.<ref name=JewishVirtualLibrary /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.google.com/books/edition/Big_Data_Revolution/UN8WBgAAQBAJ?hl=en&gbpv=1&dq=Nelson+Peltz+%22university+of+Pennsylvania%22&pg=PA3&printsec=frontcover |title=Archived copy |access-date=2020-09-03 |archive-date=2020-10-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201027161119/https://www.google.com/books/edition/Big_Data_Revolution/UN8WBgAAQBAJ?hl=en&gbpv=1&dq=Nelson+Peltz+%22university+of+Pennsylvania%22&pg=PA3&printsec=frontcover |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.in/finance/17-fraternities-with-top-wall-street-alumni/slidelist/21355808.cms|title=17 Fraternities With Top Wall Street Alumni|website=Business Insider|access-date=2020-09-03|archive-date=2020-10-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201027161124/https://www.businessinsider.in/finance/17-fraternities-with-top-wall-street-alumni/slidelist/21355808.cms|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Business career==
In 1963, he dropped out of the Wharton School with the intention of becoming a ski instructor in [[Oregon]].<ref name="bloomberg.com">{{Cite news |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2012-09-20/peltz-gets-1-billion-as-activist-strategy-impresses.html |title=Peltz Gets $1 Billion as Activist Strategy Impresses |author=Katherine Burton and Sree Vidya Bhaktavatsalam |publisher=[[Bloomberg News]] |date=September 20, 2012 |access-date=2017-03-10 |archive-date=2015-01-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150125094912/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2012-09-20/peltz-gets-1-billion-as-activist-strategy-impresses.html |url-status=live }}</ref> However, he ended up driving a delivery truck for A. Peltz & Sons, a wholesale food distribution business founded by his grandfather, Adolph, in 1896, which delivered fresh produce and Snow Crop brand [[frozen food]].<ref>[https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1961&dat=19620115&id=5HItAAAAIBAJ&sjid=lIwFAAAAIBAJ&pg=3532,2914960 Palm Beach Daily News: "Matisse - The Younger Coming" by Cholly Knickerbocker] January 15, 1952 | "''Frozen food heiress Gladys Peltz (Poppa owns Snow Crop)...''"</ref> to restaurants in New York.<ref name="Fortune" /><ref name=JewishVirtualLibrary>[https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/jsource/biography/NelsonPeltz.html Jewish Virtual Library: "Nelson Peltz" by Nancy A. Ruhling] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141213015529/https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/jsource/biography/NelsonPeltz.html |date=2014-12-13 }} retrieved January 14, 2012</ref>

His father gave him free rein with the company, and over the next 15 years he and his older brother, Robert B. Peltz, grew the business, gradually shifting the product line from produce to institutional frozen foods.<ref name="The New York Times">{{Cite news| url = https://www.nytimes.com/1988/08/26/business/business-people-sale-won-t-end-career-of-peltz-food-s-owner.html| title = Sale Won't End Career Of Peltz Food's Owner| work = The New York Times| date = August 26, 1988| access-date = February 11, 2017| archive-date = September 13, 2017| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170913020529/http://www.nytimes.com/1988/08/26/business/business-people-sale-won-t-end-career-of-peltz-food-s-owner.html| url-status = live}}</ref> Over the next 10 years, Peltz bought up several food companies, and in 1973, he and his brother together with Peltz's business partner, Peter May, who joined Peltz in 1972, took their company, then called Flagstaff Corp., with $150 million in sales, public.<ref name=JewishVirtualLibrary /><ref name="bloomberg.com"/> In 1979, Peltz sold Flagstaff's foodservice business division to a group of investors. Two years later, the foodservice business went bankrupt and the lender asked Peltz to salvage their outstanding loan. Within a year, the loans were repaid as Peltz rebuilt the business.<ref name="The New York Times" />

In the 1980s, Peltz and his business partner, [[Peter W. May|Peter May]], who had joined Flagstaff as chief financial officer after having been its accountant, went looking for new acquisitions. In April 1983, the two bought a stake in vending-machine and wire company Triangle Industries Inc. with the idea of using it to make acquisitions, building it into a Fortune 100 industrial company and the largest packaging company in the world.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1986/10/06/business/business-people-companies-built-up-by-buyout-partners.html|title=Companies Built Up By Buyout === Triarc Companies, Inc ===
In 1997, through an investment vehicle they controlled, Triarc Companies, Inc.,<ref name="Juicy">{{Cite news | url = http://hbswk.hbs.edu/item/2752.html | title = How a Juicy Brand Came Back to Life | author = John Deighton | work = [[Harvard Business School]] | date = February 4, 2002 | access-date = April 6, 2012 | archive-date = May 11, 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120511050412/http://hbswk.hbs.edu/item/2752.html | url-status = live }}</ref> Peltz and May acquired [[Snapple]] from [[Quaker Oats]]. Snapple, together with other beverage brands, was sold to [[Cadbury Schweppes]] in 2000. The Snapple turnaround was featured as a [[Harvard Business School]] case study.<ref name="Juicy" />

In February 2011, Trian announced it had accumulated an 8% stake in the Family Dollar company and indicated a willingness to participate in a take private [[LBO]] for the company with a total value of about $7 to $8 billion. === Ingersoll-Rand ===
Peltz was appointed to the [[Ingersoll-Rand]] board of directors in 2012.<ref name="wsj20120813">{{cite news|last=Das|first=Anupreeta|title=Activist Peltz Wins Board Seat At Ingersoll|newspaper=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=August 13, 2012|author2=Terlep, Sharon|page=B1}}</ref> The company announced major initiatives to enhance shareholder value in late 2012. In 2014, Peltz resigned from the Ingersoll-Rand board, after the spin-off of Allegion.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/peltz-wont-stand-for-re-election-to-ingersoll-rand-board-1396303638|title=Peltz Won't Stand for Re-Election to Ingersoll-Rand Board|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=March 31, 2014|access-date=March 11, 2020|archive-date=September 9, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180909215759/https://www.wsj.com/articles/peltz-wont-stand-for-re-election-to-ingersoll-rand-board-1396303638|url-status=live}}</ref>

In August 2013, it was reported that Trian held an approximate $1.25 billion stake in [[DuPont]].<ref name = "pont"/>

In January 2014, as one of the company's largest shareholders, with a current [[beneficial ownership]] of more than 46 million shares, Peltz was appointed to the board of directors of global snacking company, [[Mondelēz International]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/business/breaking/sns-rt-mondelez-peltz-20140121,0,55332.story|title=Activist investor Nelson Peltz joins Mondelez board|publisher=ChicagoTribune|accessdate=7 February 2014|archive-date=27 January 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140127203328/http://www.chicagotribune.com/business/breaking/sns-rt-mondelez-peltz-20140121,0,55332.story|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.foxbusiness.com/investing/2014/01/21/proxy-war-averted-mondelez-adds-activist-nelson-peltz-to-board/ |title=Proxy War Averted: Mondelez Adds Activist Nelson Peltz to Board |author=Matt Egan |publisher=[[Fox Business Network]] |date=January 21, 2014 |access-date=March 12, 2014 |archive-date=March 26, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140326234020/http://www.foxbusiness.com/investing/2014/01/21/proxy-war-averted-mondelez-adds-activist-nelson-peltz-to-board/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In May 2015, Trian was unsuccessful in a bitter proxy contest to appoint four of its nominees to the board of DuPont.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/dupont-appears-poised-to-win-over-peltz-1431521564 |title=DuPont Defeats Peltz, Trian in Board Fight - WSJ |first=Jacob |last=Bunge |work=wsj.com |date=May 13, 2015 |accessdate=May 14, 2015 |archive-date=May 14, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150514155531/http://www.wsj.com/articles/dupont-appears-poised-to-win-over-peltz-1431521564 |url-status=live }}</ref> Five months later the CEO of DuPont, [[Ellen Kullman]], resigned; at the time DuPont acknowledged lower than expected earnings and the need to accelerate a cost-cutting plan.<ref>{{Cite news|title = DuPont Chief Executive Ellen Kullman to Retire|url = https://www.nytimes.com/2015/10/06/business/dealbook/dupont-chief-executive-ellen-kullman-to-retire.html|newspaper = The New York Times|date = 2015-10-05|access-date = 2015-10-06|issn = 0362-4331|first = Michael J. In October 2015, Trian bought a $2.5 billion stake in [[General Electric]].<ref>{{cite web|title= Nelson Peltz's Trian takes $2.5 billion stake in General Electric|url= https://www.reuters.com/article/2015/10/05/us-general-electric-stake-idUSKCN0RZ0HY20151005|website= Reuters|date= 5 October 2015|access-date= 1 July 2017|archive-date= 2 November 2015|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20151102013034/http://www.reuters.com/article/2015/10/05/us-general-electric-stake-idUSKCN0RZ0HY20151005|url-status= live}}</ref>

=== Procter & Gamble ===
In October 2017, Peltz tried but failed to acquire a seat on the board of [[Procter & Gamble]], in which Trian has a 1.5% stake.<ref name="veconomist">{{cite news|title=An epic but inconsequential proxy vote at Procter & Gamble|url=https://www.economist.com/news/business/21730236-nelson-peltz-fails-get-consumer-goods-giants-board-epic-inconsequential-proxy|work=[[The Economist]]|date=12 October 2017|access-date=13 October 2017|archive-date=13 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171013172210/https://www.economist.com/news/business/21730236-nelson-peltz-fails-get-consumer-goods-giants-board-epic-inconsequential-proxy|url-status=live}}</ref> On November 15, 2017, it was discovered that per a revision of all votes, which Peltz acknowledged to have resulted in a remarkably close battle, Peltz had in fact won the proxy battle, recognized as the largest in corporate history. On December 15, Procter & Gamble named Peltz to its board, although it stated that Peltz had nominally lost the proxy vote.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-15/p-g-names-billionaire-nelson-peltz-to-board-after-proxy-battle|title=P&G Names Activist Nelson Peltz to Board After Proxy Battle|date=2017-12-15|work=Bloomberg.com|access-date=2018-03-19|language=en|archive-date=2018-03-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180319214047/https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-15/p-g-names-billionaire-nelson-peltz-to-board-after-proxy-battle|url-status=live}}</ref>

In February 2018, Peltz announced his departure from the board of Mondelez International, to be succeeded by Trian partner Peter May.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-mondelez-intl-board-peltz/peltz-to-step-down-from-oreo-maker-mondelezs-board-idUSKCN1FX1VB|title=Peltz steps down from Mondelez board, Trian president to take seat|date=2018-02-13|work=Reuters|access-date=2018-02-13|archive-date=2018-02-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180213183813/https://www.reuters.com/article/us-mondelez-intl-board-peltz/peltz-to-step-down-from-oreo-maker-mondelezs-board-idUSKCN1FX1VB|url-status=live}}</ref> In March 2018, Peltz joined the [[Procter & Gamble]] board of directors.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Orol|first1=Ronald|title=Nelson Peltz Begins Showdown Over P&G CEO, R&D|url=https://www.thestreet.com/story/14507431/1/nelson-peltz-begins-showdown-over-p-g-ceo-r-d.html|accessdate=30 March 2018|publisher=The Street|date=March 1, 2018|archive-date=31 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180331040511/https://www.thestreet.com/story/14507431/1/nelson-peltz-begins-showdown-over-p-g-ceo-r-d.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Dan|first1=Avi|title=P&G's Marc Pritchard Puts Agencies On Notice|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/avidan/2018/03/09/pgs-marc-pritchard-puts-agencies-on-notice/#5be5ce0a2101|accessdate=30 March 2018|work=Forbes|date=March 9, 2018|archive-date=31 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180331041249/https://www.forbes.com/sites/avidan/2018/03/09/pgs-marc-pritchard-puts-agencies-on-notice/#5be5ce0a2101|url-status=live}}</ref>

In March 2019, Peltz joined Canadian [[cannabis]] producer [[Aurora Cannabis]].{{Citation needed|date=June 2020}} In June 2019, Peltz's Trian announced an investment in Ferguson, plc, a distributor of plumbing and heating products in North America.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2019-06-13/ferguson-plumbing-famed-investor-nelson-peltz-likes-what-he-sees|title=Nelson Peltz Likes the Look of American Plumbing|publisher=[[Bloomberg L.P.]]|date=June 13, 2019|access-date=2020-03-13|archive-date=2019-06-30|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190630021745/https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2019-06-13/ferguson-plumbing-famed-investor-nelson-peltz-likes-what-he-sees|url-status=live}}</ref>

In February 2014, ''Forbes'' listed Peltz as one of the 25 highest-earning [[hedge fund]] managers in 2013, with total earnings of $430 million, ranked 16th.<ref>{{Citation |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/nathanvardi/2014/02/26/the-highest-earning-hedge-fund-managers-and-traders/ |title=The 25 Highest-Earning Hedge Fund Managers And Traders |date=Feb 26, 2014 |magazine=[[Forbes]] |last=Vardi |first=Nathan |access-date=September 6, 2017 |archive-date=July 29, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170729085722/https://www.forbes.com/sites/nathanvardi/2014/02/26/the-highest-earning-hedge-fund-managers-and-traders/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
