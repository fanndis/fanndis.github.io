'''''Greenleaf''''' is an [[United States|American]] television drama series, created by [[Craig Wright (playwright)|Craig Wright]], and executive produced by [[Oprah Winfrey]] and [[Lionsgate Television]]. [[Clement Virgo]] also serves as an executive producer and director. It stars [[Keith David]], [[Lynn Whitfield]], and [[Merle Dandridge]]. ''Greenleaf'' premiered on the [[Oprah Winfrey Network]] on June 21, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|author=Denise Petski|title='Greenleaf': OWN's Megachurch Drama Series Gets Premiere Date|url=https://deadline.com/2016/01/greenleaf-own-megachurch-drama-series-premiere-date-oprah-winfrey-1201687643/|work=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|accessdate=21 January 2016}}</ref>

On November 23, 2018, OWN renewed the series for a fourth season.<ref name="Happypillsjr">{{cite web|last=Cameron K.|first=McEwan|title=Greenleaf season 4: Cast, episodes, trailer, air date, and everything you need to know|url=https://www.digitalspy.com/tv/ustv/a867167/greenleaf-season-4-cast-episodes-netflix-trailer-air-date-premiere-release/|work=[[Digital Spy]]|accessdate=11 November 2018}}</ref> The fourth season premiered on September 3, 2019. On November 15, 2019, OWN confirmed the renewal of a fifth and final season of the series, which premiered on June 23, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|last=Wani|first=Jibran|title=Greenleaf Season 5 Confirmed: Release Date, Renewal, And Updates|url=https://news.otakukart.com/261115/greenleaf-season-5-confirmed-release-date-renewal-and-updates/|work=Otakukart|accessdate=15 November 2019}}</ref> A spinoff of ''Greenleaf''  in works on OWN, and will focus on Whitfield's character and her new role as church master.<ref>https://shadowandact.com/greenleaf-spinoff-details-revealed-e28095-heres-what-to-expect</ref>

==Premise==
''Greenleaf'' follows the unscrupulous world of the Greenleaf family with scandalous secrets and lies, their palatial family mansion compound, and their sprawling [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]] megachurch with predominantly [[African-American]] members. The series' lead characters are Bishop James Greenleaf (David) and Lady Mae Greenleaf (Whitfield), who are the patriarch and matriarch of the Greenleaf family, and Grace Greenleaf (Dandridge), their estranged daughter who has returned home after 20 years following the mysterious death of her sister, Faith. Deep down, this family cares for and loves each other, but secrets, lies, adultery, sibling rivalry and other issues swarm the family as they try to keep themselves together. there are a variety of people who try to run the family out of their beloved church but they lay it all on the line to keep each other tight and close like any family.

===Main cast===
*[[Keith David]] as '''Bishop James Greenleaf''', the charismatic and strong leader of Calvary Fellowship World Ministries and Greenleaf family patriarch.
*[[Lynn Whitfield]] as '''"First Lady" Daisy Mae Greenleaf, née McCready''', Bishop Greenleaf's wife, [[First Lady]] of Calvary Fellowship and family matriarch.
*[[Merle Dandridge]] as '''Pastor Grace "Gigi" Greenleaf''', Mae and Bishop's eldest child. She returns home 20 years after escaping from the family. Her mother and siblings consistently deplore her "desertion". She proudly serves as Executive Pastor of Ministry at Calvary Fellowship under Bishop and First Lady Greenleaf Leadership. 
*[[Desiree Ross]] as '''Sophia Greenleaf''', Grace's teenage daughter, who shares her mother's charms and whose deep religious faith is challenged.
*[[Lamman Rucker]] as '''Pastor Jacob Greenleaf''', the second-born child and only son of the Bishop. He resents Grace for abandoning the family and leaving him to take her place in the ministry, crushing his dream of a career in professional baseball. He previously and proudly served as Associate Pastor of Discipleship, and now serves as Senior Pastor of Triumph Fellowship Church. 
*[[Kim Hawthorne]] as '''Kerissa Greenleaf''', Jacob's ambitious and controlling wife and headmistress of Excellence Academy.
*[[Deborah Joy Winans]] as '''Charity Greenleaf''', the youngest Greenleaf child and also Minister of Music at Calvary Fellowship. Being the youngest, she is furious at being left out of family situations. Like Jacob, she resents Grace for leaving the family, forcing Charity to stay bound to her family's church.
*[[Tye White]] as '''Kevin Satterlee''', Charity's husband and director of Outreach at Calvary Fellowship (Seasons 1-3)
*[[Gregory Alan Williams]] as '''Robert "Mac" McCready''', Lady Mae's brother. (Recurring character Season 1, main Season 2).
*[[Lovie Simone]] as '''Zora Greenleaf''', Kerissa and Jacob's rebellious teenage daughter. (Recurring Season 1, main Season 2 to present)
*[[Rick Fox]] as '''Darius Nash''', a journalist for the Tennessee Statesman. (Recurring Season 2 and 5, main Season 3)

==Reception==
The first season of ''Greenleaf'' received mainly positive reviews from critics,<ref name=meta>{{cite web|title=Greenleaf|url=https://www.metacritic.com/tv/greenleaf|work=[[Metacritic]]|accessdate=18 June 2016}}</ref> who particularly praised the acting performances of the series' lead actress, Merle Dandridge, as well as Lynn Whitfield and Keith David.<ref name=var>{{cite web|last=Ryan|first=Maureen|title=TV Review: 'Greenleaf'|url=https://variety.com/2016/tv/reviews/greenleaf-own-tv-review-oprah-winfrey-lynn-whitfield-keith-david-merle-dandridge-1201795621/|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=17 June 2016|accessdate=18 June 2016}}</ref> On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the first season of ''Greenleaf'' has an approval rating of 79%, based on 18 reviews, with an average rating of 6.9/10.<ref name=root>{{cite web|title=Greenleaf: Season 1|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/tv/greenleaf/s01/|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=21 June 2016}}</ref> The site's critical consensus reads, "Immersed in a unique megachurch setting, Greenleaf is a juicy family soap bolstered by a provocative spirituality."<ref name=root/> Metacritic gave season one of the show a score of 68 out of 100, based on 20 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews."<ref name=meta/>

Maureen Ryan, television critic for ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' stated "Dandridge is naturalistic in her portrayal of a practical, cautious woman re-examining her family history and her own choices and mistakes, while Whitfield has the imperious aura of a grand soap opera diva in the tradition of [[Joan Collins]]."<ref name=var/> Daniel Fienberg from ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]''  wrote in his review "effectively balance the melodrama, vast ensemble and church details and contribute a level of technical proficiency that goes far beyond what the Tyler Perry Factory brings to ''[[The Haves and the Have Nots (TV series)|The Haves and the Have Nots]]'', OWN's big scripted hit."<ref>{{cite web|title='Greenleaf': TV Review|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/greenleaf-tv-review-901219|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|accessdate=18 June 2016}}</ref> ''[[The Boston Globe]]''{{'}}s  Matthew Gilbert compared ''Greenleaf'' with primetime soaps like ''[[Revenge (TV series)|Revenge]]'' and the similarly black-cast ''[[Empire (2015 TV series)|Empire]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=A soap opera that goes to church|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/2016/06/17/soap-opera-that-goes-church/m3UGuPAgMp9jrHKnPIgpVN/story.html|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|accessdate=18 June 2016}}</ref>

''Greenleaf'' has received positive reviews from critics, with most praising Dandridge, Whitfield and David's performances.<ref name=meta/><ref name=root/> The series premiere drew 3.04 million viewers, making it the No. 1 series debut in OWN history.<ref>{{cite web|last=Patten|first=Dominic|title=Oprah-Starring 'Greenleaf' Delivers OWN's Best Debut Ratings Ever|url=https://deadline.com/2016/06/oprah-winfrey-greenleaf-debut-ratings-best-ever-for-own-tyler-perry-the-haves-and-the-have-nots-1201777540/|work=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|date=22 June 2016|accessdate=23 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Holloway|first=Daniel|title=TV Ratings: Oprah Winfrey's 'Greenleaf' Premieres to OWN's Biggest Audience Ever|url=https://variety.com/2016/tv/ratings/tv-ratings-oprah-winfreys-greenleaf-premiere-1201801597/|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=22 June 2016|accessdate=23 June 2016}}</ref> On April 21, 2016, the series was renewed for a second season ahead of its television premiere.<ref>{{cite web|last=Andreeva|first=Nellie|title='Greenleaf' Drama Series Gets Season 2 Renewal By OWN Ahead Of Premiere|url=https://deadline.com/2016/04/greenleaf-season-2-renewal-own-oprah-winfrey-network-1201741654/|work=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|date=21 April 2016|accessdate=18 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Obenson|first=Tambay A.|title=OWN Shows Confidence in 'Greenleaf' – Orders Second Season Ahead of Season One Premiere. Season Two will premiere Spring 2017|url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/own-shows-confidence-in-greenleaf-orders-second-season-ahead-of-season-one-premiere-20160421|work=[[IndieWire]]|date=21 April 2016|accessdate=18 June 2016}}</ref> The second season premiered on March 15, 2017. On August 7, 2017, the series was renewed for a third season.<ref>{{cite web|last=Petski|first=Denise|title='Greenleaf' Renewed For Season 3 By OWN; Rick Fox Upped To Series Regular|url=https://deadline.com/2017/08/greenleaf-renewed-season-3-own-rick-fox-upped-to-series-regular-1202144103/|work=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|date=August 7, 2017|access-date=September 8, 2017}}</ref> The third season premiered in a two-episode special on August 28 and August 29, 2018. On September 19, 2018, the series was renewed for a fourth season.<ref>{{cite web|last=Schwartz|first=Ryan|title=Greenleaf Renewed for Season 4|url=https://tvline.com/2018/09/19/greenleaf-renewed-season-4-own/|work=[[TVLine]]|date=September 19, 2018}}</ref>
