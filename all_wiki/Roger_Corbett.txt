'''Roger Campbell Corbett''' {{post-nominals|country=AUS|AO}} (born 30 January 1942) is an [[Australia]]n businessman. From January 1999 to September 2006, Corbett served as CEO of [[Woolworths Limited]], a large retailing conglomerate.

==Career==
Educated at [[Shore School]], Corbett graduated from [[UNSW Sydney]] with a Bachelor of Commerce.<ref name=bloomberg>{{cite news |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=8626996&privcapId=30402 |title=Roger Campbell Corbett AO, BCom, FAIM, FRMIA: Executive Profile & Biography |work=[[Bloomberg L.P.|Bloomberg]] |date=2017 |access-date=19 June 2017 }}</ref>

In 2003, Corbett was appointed a Member of the [[Order of Australia]] (AM) for service to the retail industry, particularly as a contributor to the development of industry policy and standards, and to the community.<ref>[http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=1043270&search_type=quick&showInd=true CORBETT, Roger Campbell AM], ''It's an Honour'', 9 June 2003.</ref> In 2008, he was promoted to an officer of the Order of Australia (AO) for service to business, particularly through leadership and executive roles in the retail sector and a range of allied organisations, and to the community.<ref>[http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=1138593&search_type=quick&showInd=true CORBETT, Roger Campbell AO], ''It's an Honour'', 9 June 2008.</ref> He is also a member of the [[Liberal Party of Australia]]<ref>http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-09-04/rudd-dismisses-criticism-from-fairfax-chairman-corbett/4933856</ref> and serves as president of its [[Division of Warringah|Warringah]] branch.<ref name="Koziol 2020">{{cite web | last=Koziol | first=Michael | title='Completely made up': Factional tricks in Liberal preselection battle for Warringah | website=The Sydney Morning Herald | date=August 8, 2020 | url=https://www.smh.com.au/politics/federal/completely-made-up-factional-tricks-in-liberal-preselection-battle-for-warringah-20200807-p55jo3.html | access-date=August 9, 2020}}</ref>

There is a bitter relationship between the former Fairfax Media chairman and its largest shareholder [[Gina Rinehart]]. The relationship developed to the personal level once Fairfax Media's incumbent board of directors declined Gina Rinehart's request to offer her three seats on the board of directors.<ref>http://www.theage.com.au/business/rineharts-fairfax-battle-gets-personal-20120629-217gz.html</ref>

==Personal life==
Corbett is married and has three children.<ref>{{cite news |title=Chief concerns |work=[[The Sun-Herald]] |first=Steve |last=Dow |date=11 June 2006 }}</ref>He is a committed Christian<ref>https://www.acc.edu.au/podcast/roger-corbett/''The Inspiration Project''</ref>.
