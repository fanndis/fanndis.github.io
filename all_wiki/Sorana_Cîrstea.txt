'''Sorana Mihaela Cîrstea'''<ref>{{cite news|title=Sorana Cirstea|work=wtatennis.com|access-date=11 April 2012|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/player/sorana-cirstea_2257889_11720|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120415134054/http://www.wtatennis.com/player/sorana-cirstea_2257889_11720|archive-date=15 April 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref> ({{IPA-ro|soˈrana ˈkɨrste̯a}}; born 7 April 1990) is a [[Romania|Romanian]] [[tennis]] player. In singles, she achieved her career-high ranking of world No. 21 on 12 August 2013.<ref name="WTA3">{{cite web |url=http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/page/Player/Stats/0,,12781~11720,00.html |title=Players &#124; Stats &#124; Sorana Cîrstea |publisher=Sony Ericsson WTA Tour |date=9 October 2011 |access-date=13 October 2011}}</ref> In doubles, her career-high ranking is No. 35, which she reached on 9 March 2009. Her biggest achievements: reaching the quarterfinals at the [[2009 French Open – Women's Singles|2009 French Open]] and reaching the final at the [[2013 Rogers Cup – Women's Singles|2013 Rogers Cup]]. Over her career, Cîrstea has defeated top-ten players [[Anna Chakvetadze]], [[Sara Errani]], [[Jelena Janković]], [[Angelique Kerber]], [[Petra Kvitová]], [[Li Na]], [[Karolína Plíšková]], [[Francesca Schiavone]], [[Samantha Stosur|Sam Stosur]], [[Caroline Wozniacki]], [[Vera Zvonareva]], [[Johanna Konta]].

==Playing style==
Cîrstea is a power player. Her groundstrokes are often erratic with an emphasis on going for a winner leading her to make a high amount of unforced errors per match but with a high number of winners.<ref name="TS">''Tennis Spy'': [http://www.soranacirstea.org/articles/tennis-spy-sorana-cirstea/ Sorana Cîrstea] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090814022124/http://www.soranacirstea.org/articles/tennis-spy-sorana-cirstea/ |date=14 August 2009 }} (8 June 2009)</ref> According to the ''Tennis Spy'', her long stride means she covers ground while not appearing to move particularly quickly.<ref name="TS" /> As a doubles player, she is also a very solid [[volley (tennis)|volleyer]].<ref name="TS" /> Her [[forehand]]s and [[backhand]]s are delivered quickly on both sides.<ref name="TS" />

==Career==
===2005–2006: Last junior years and turning professional===
Cîrstea is a former [[ITF Junior Circuit]] top-ten player, reaching her highest ranking of No. 6 on 26 June 2006. Her best results include a title in the 2005 German Junior Open (Grade 1), a tournament in which she was finalist the year before, defeating Erika Zanchetta in three sets in the final, and a runner-up place in the 2006 Trofeo Bonfiglio (Grade A), where she lost in the final to compatriot [[Ioana Raluca Olaru]] after having defeated the top-ranked world junior player [[Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova]] in the semifinals. She also made final appearances at the year-ending Grade-1 juniors tournaments, Eddie Herr International and Yucatán World Cup, in 2005 and 2006, and in the Opus Nottinghill International in 2006.<ref>{{cite news |first=Jaydip |last=Sengupta |title=Agnieszka Radwanska: The fan favourite |work=gulfnews.com |date=16 February 2012 |access-date=16 February 2012 |url=http://www.tennis.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Sorana+Cirstea |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111205191518/http://tennis.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Sorana+Cirstea |archive-date=5 December 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

She turned professional in 2006 and ended the year at No. 353 in the [[WTA rankings]].

===2007–2008: First WTA title and reaching top 40===
[[File:Sorana Cirstea 2008.jpg|thumb|left|Sorana Cîrstea in 2008]]
In April 2007, she reached the final of the [[Budapest Grand Prix]], a Tier-III event, as a qualifier. All of her main draw matches went to three sets. She beat [[Martina Müller (tennis)|Martina Müller]] in the second round, [[Eleni Daniilidou]] in the quarterfinals and [[Karin Knapp]] in the semifinals before losing to [[Gisela Dulko]] in the final. She was the first Romanian to reach a WTA Tour final since [[Ruxandra Dragomir]] in June 2000. During her run, she beat two players ranked inside the top 40. The following month, Cîrstea played in the junior tournament of the [[2007 French Open]] where she reached the doubles final with [[Alexa Glatch]], losing to the third seeds [[Ksenia Milevskaya]] and [[Urszula Radwańska]].

She competed for Romania in the women's singles at the [[2008 Summer Olympics]], losing in the first round to [[Shahar Pe'er]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ci/sorana-cirstea-1.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200418082053/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ci/sorana-cirstea-1.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2020-04-18|title=Sorana Cirstea Bio, Stats, and Results|website=Olympics at Sports-Reference.com|language=en|access-date=2019-07-20}}</ref> In October 2008, Cîrstea won her first WTA title in [[2008 Tashkent Open|Tashkent]] by defeating fourth seed and world No. 64, [[Sabine Lisicki]], in three sets.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cirstea wins Tashkent Open|url=https://www.upi.com/Sports_News/2008/10/05/Cirstea-wins-Tashkent-Open/UPI-40421223232544/|website=[[UPI]]|date=5 October 2008}}</ref> She also won her first two WTA titles in doubles that year. At the end of 2008, she was No. 36 in the WTA singles rankings and the No. 1 Romanian, aged only 18 years.

===2009: First Grand Slam quarterfinals at Roland Garros===
[[File:Cirstea Roland Garros 2009 2.jpg|thumb|Cirstea at the [[2009 French Open]]]]
Cîrstea began new season by losing to [[Dinara Safina]] at the [[2009 Medibank International Sydney – Women's Singles|Medibank International]] in Sydney, in straight sets. In the doubles tournament, she partnered [[Vera Dushevina]] and reached the second round, defeating Kuznetsova/Petrova before losing to Black/Huber. At the [[2009 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], she lost in the first singles round to [[Melinda Czink]], but reached the second round of the doubles with [[Monica Niculescu]], defeating Ditty/Gullickson and losing to Dechy/Santangelo.<ref name=Stats>{{cite news|title=Stats|work=tennis.com|access-date=11 April 2012|url=http://www.tennis.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Sorana+Cirstea|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111205191518/http://tennis.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Sorana+Cirstea|archive-date=5 December 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Cîrstea lost in the first rounds of the [[2009 Open GDF Suez|Open GdF Suez]] and [[Barclays Dubai Tennis Championships|Dubai Championships]], to [[Nathalie Dechy]] and [[Dominika Cibulková]] respectively. In doubles, she again partnered Monica Niculescu in Suez, reaching the semifinals before injury forced them to lose in a walkover to Peschke/Raymond. In Dubai, she partnered [[Arina Rodionova]] and they lost in the first round to Kirilenko/Radwańska. Cîrstea received a bye in the first round at [[2009 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|Indian Wells]], but lost in the second to [[Elena Vesnina]]. In the doubles tournament, she partnered [[Galina Voskoboeva]] and lost in the first round to Mattek-Sands/Washington. Cîrstea finished her hardcourt season with a loss in the first round of the [[2009 Sony Ericsson Open – Women's Singles|Sony Ericsson Open]] in Miami to the qualifier [[Mariya Koryttseva]]. Her doubles effort in Miami with [[Caroline Wozniacki]] also ended in the first round with a loss to Kuznetsova/Mauresmo.

Her clay-court season began at the inaugural [[2009 Andalucia Tennis Experience|Andalucia Tennis Experience]] in Marbella. She reached the semifinals, defeating [[Ioana Olaru]], Andreja Klepač and [[Kaia Kanepi]] before losing to [[Carla Suárez Navarro]]. In the doubles, she partnered Ioana Olaru and they reached the quarterfinals, before losing to Hercog/Ulirova. At the [[Barcelona Ladies Open]], Cîrstea lost in the first round of the singles tournament to [[Anastasiya Yakimova]], but reached the finals of the doubles tournament with [[Andreja Klepač]]. They defeated Grönefeld/Senoglu, Ani/Voráčová, and Hlaváčková/Hradecká, before falling to Vives/Sanchez in the final. A week later, at [[Grand Prix SAR La Princesse Lalla Meryem|Fes]], Cîrstea again lost in the first round of the singles tournament, to [[Lourdes Domínguez Lino]], and again reached the finals of the doubles tournament, with [[Maria Kirilenko]]. Cîrstea/Kirilenko defeated Fernandez-Brugues/Thorpe, Czink/Keothavong, and Hercog/Olaru, before losing to Kleybanova/Makarova in three sets.

At the [[2009 Estoril Open|Estoril Open]], Cîrstea reached the quarterfinals of the singles tournament, defeating [[Kimiko Date-Krumm]] and [[Maret Ani]], before falling to the eventual champion [[Yanina Wickmayer]]. In the doubles, she again partnered Kirilenko, defeating Ivanova/Yakimova, before losing to Coin/Pelletier in the quarterfinals. At [[Mutua Madrileña Madrid Open|Madrid]], she lost in the first rounds of both the singles and the doubles tournaments, falling to [[Alona Bondarenko]] in two sets in the singles, and partnering [[Vladimíra Uhlířová]] in the doubles to lose to Makarova/Kudrayvtseva in the super-tiebreaker.

Cîrstea then had her breakthrough tournament at the [[2009 French Open|French Open]]. She began with wins over [[Carly Gullickson]] and an upset of the 21st seed [[Alizé Cornet]] to advance to the third round of a Grand Slam for the first time. She then defeated her doubles partner Wozniacki, the tenth seed. Cîrstea continued her unlikely run with a 3–6, 6–0, 9–7 upset over the fifth-seeded [[Jelena Janković]] to reach her first Grand Slam quarterfinal,<ref name="JJ">{{cite news |agency=Associated Press |title=Janković upset by Romanian teen Cîrstea in three sets at French |url=http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/2009/tennis/06/01/jelena.jankovic.ap/ |publisher=SI.com |date=1 June 2009 |access-date=1 June 2009}}</ref> where she faced the 30th seed [[Samantha Stosur|Sam Stosur]], losing in straight sets. Her doubles effort with Wozniacki ended in the first round with a loss to Pennetta/Kirilenko.

[[File:Cîrstea 2009 US Open 01.jpg|thumb|Cîrstea at the [[2009 US Open (tennis)|2009 US Open]]]]
At the [[2009 Ordina Open|Ordina Open]] in the Netherlands, she defeated Niculescu in three sets but lost in the second round to Yanina Wickmayer. She paired with [[Dinara Safina]] for the doubles tournament, defeating Grönefeld/Niculescu in the first round and losing to Errani/Pennetta in the second round. At [[2009 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Cîrstea was seeded 28th. She defeated [[Edina Gallovits]] and [[Sania Mirza]] in the first two rounds, but lost to the eighth seed [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the third round. In the doubles, she once again partnered Wozniacki, but lost in the second round to Koryttseva/Poutchek. At the [[2009 Swedish Open – Women's Singles|Swedish Open]] in Båstad, Cîrstea defeated the qualifier [[Johanna Larsson (tennis)|Johanna Larsson]] before losing to [[Gisela Dulko]] in the second round. She and Wozniacki lost in the second round to Kondratieva/Lefevre. Two weeks later, Cîrstea lost in the first round of the [[2009 Bank of the West Classic – Singles|Bank of the West Classic]] to [[Agnieszka Radwańska]]. Partnering Maria Kirilenko, she reached the semifinals of the doubles, defeating Granville/Gullickson and Coin/Pelletier, before losing to Chan/Niculescu.

Cîrstea had another improbable run at the [[2009 LA Women's Tennis Championships|LA Championships]] in August. She upset Wozniacki and Radwańska, despite Radwańska serving for the match in the third set, but lost to Stosur in the semifinals. Cîrstea/Wozniacki lost in the first round of the doubles to Chang/Yan. At the [[Western & Southern Financial Group Masters and Women's Open|Cincinnati Masters]], she defeated [[Meghann Shaughnessy]] and [[Anna-Lena Grönefeld]] before losing to the fourth seed [[Elena Dementieva]], but achieved a career high ranking of No. 23. At the [[2009 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]], Cîrstea was seeded 24th. She defeated [[Ayumi Morita]] and [[Stéphanie Dubois]]  before losing in the third round to the eventual finalist Wozniacki, the No. 9 seed and her doubles partner. Cîrstea/Wozniacki defeated Bammer/Schruff and Azarenka/Zvonareva before losing in the third round to the eventual champions, Williams/Williams. After the US Open, Cîrstea lost her next five matches, in the [[Hansol Korea Open|Korea Open]], [[Toray Pan Pacific Open|Pan Pacific Open]], [[China Open (tennis)|China Open]], [[Generali Ladies Linz|Linz Open]] and [[Kremlin Cup]].

She finished 2009 ranked 43rd in the world, with a 21–24 match record.<ref name=Stats />

===2010: Struggles with form===
[[File:Sorana Cîrstea at the 2010 US Open 03.jpg|thumb|Cîrstea at the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|2010 US Open]]]]
In the [[2010 Hopman Cup]] in early January, Cîrstea played with [[Victor Hănescu]]. Romania were seeded sixth and finished last in their group, winning against the Australian team of Sam Stosur and [[Lleyton Hewitt]], but losing to the Spain's [[María José Martínez Sánchez]] and [[Tommy Robredo]], the eventual champions, and the American team of [[Melanie Oudin]] and [[John Isner]]. Cîrstea posted a three-set win over Stosur and, with Hanescu, a 7–5, 6–1 win over Stosur and Hewitt. She lost in straight sets to Martínez Sánchez and Oudin.<ref name=Stats />

She easily lost in the first round of the [[2010 Moorilla Hobart International|Hobart tournament]] to [[Peng Shuai]]. At the [[2010 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], she defeated [[Olivia Rogowska]], and lost in the second round to [[Alisa Kleybanova]]. She partnered [[Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova]] in the doubles tournament, losing to Kirilenko/Radwańska in the first round 0–6, 7–6, 2–6.

Her next tournament was the [[2010 Open GDF Suez|Open GdF Suez]] in Paris, where she fell to Oudin 3–6, 0–6. She lost in straight sets in the first rounds of the [[2010 Dubai Tennis Championships – Women's Singles|Dubai]], [[2010 Abierto Mexicano Telcel|Acapulco]], and [[2010 Monterrey Open – Singles|Monterrey]] tournaments, to [[Francesca Schiavone]], [[Sharon Fichman]], and [[Sara Errani]], respectively. At [[2010 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]] she reached the second round, first defeating Kaia Kanepi, before losing to [[Zheng Jie]]. In Miami two weeks later at the [[2010 Sony Ericsson Open|Sony Ericsson Open]], Cîrstea defeated [[Michelle Larcher de Brito]] before losing in the second round to the third seed [[Venus Williams]].

In the clay-court season, Cîrstea prevailed over the sixth seed Maria Kirilenko in the first round of the [[2010 Andalucia Tennis Experience|Andalucia Tennis Experience]]. She then lost in the second round to her fellow Romanian [[Simona Halep]]. At the [[2010 Barcelona Ladies Open|Barcelona Open]], she defeated [[Tamira Paszek]], but lost in the second round to [[Iveta Benešová]], in straight sets. Cîrstea was seeded second at the [[2010 Estoril Open|Estoril Open]] and defeated her compatriot Ioana Olaru in the first round and Larcher de Brito in the second. In the quarterfinals, she beat [[Arantxa Rus]] 6–4, 6–1, but fell to [[Arantxa Parra Santonja]] in the semifinals. In the doubles, she partnered [[Anabel Medina Garrigues]] to win the tournament, beating Peng/Zhang, Manasieva/Olaru and Diatchenko/Vedy. Cîrstea lost to Flavia Pennetta in three sets in the first round of the [[2010 Mutua Madrileña Madrid Open – Women's Singles|Madrid Open]]. Two weeks later, she qualified for the [[2010 Internationaux de Strasbourg|Strasbourg]] tournament, losing to [[Elena Baltacha]] in the first round.

At the [[2010 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]], she lost in the first round to the defending champion [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]]. At [[2010 Aegon International|Eastbourne]], she defeated Francesca Schiavone, losing in the second round to Kuznetsova in three sets. At [[2010 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], she was defeated by [[Petra Kvitová]] in the first round. At the [[2010 GDF Suez Grand Prix|GdF Suez Grand Prix]] she lost in the first round to the qualifier [[Zuzana Ondrášková]]. She lost in the quarterfinals at the [[2010 İstanbul Cup|İstanbul Cup]] toPavlyuchenkova and the [[2010 e-Boks Danish Open|Danish Open]] to [[Klára Zakopalová]]. At the [[2010 Western & Southern Financial Group Masters and Women's Open|Western & Southern Open]], she lost in the first round to [[Sybille Bammer]].<ref name=Stats /> At the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], Cîrstea lost to [[Sofia Arvidsson]] in the first round.

She ended the year placed 93rd in the WTA rankings.

===2011: Re-entering top 60===
[[File:Sorana Cîrstea signing autographs for ball boys at the 2011 BCR Open Romania Ladies.jpg|thumb|left|Cîrstea at the [[2011 BCR Open Romania Ladies]]]]
At the [[2011 Australian Open|Australian Open]], Cîrstea defeated [[Mirjana Lučić]] before she was beaten by [[Shahar Pe'er]] in the second round.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.signonsandiego.com/news/2011/jan/20/relaxed-nadal-moves-toward-another-grand-slam-win/ |title=Relaxed Nadal moves toward another Grand Slam win |last=Passa |first=Dennis |date=20 January 2011 |work=San Diego Union Tribune |access-date=14 May 2011}}</ref> In the doubles tournament, she played with [[Lucie Šafářová]] and defeated the team of [[Sarah Borwell]] and [[Marie-Ève Pelletier]], in three sets. In the second round they lost to [[Cara Black]] and [[Anastasia Rodionova]].<ref name="dtwelve">{{cite web |url=http://www.wtatennis.com/page/Player/Activity/0,,12781~11720,00.html |title=WTA players; Sorana Cirstea; Doubles results, 2011 |publisher=Women's Tennis Association |access-date=14 May 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110325024125/http://www.wtatennis.com/page/Player/Activity/0%2C%2C12781~11720%2C00.html |archive-date=25 March 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

In the [[Cellular South Cup]], Cîrstea defeated [[Catherine Harrison (tennis)|Catherine Harrison]] in the first round, before she lost to Shahar Pe'er in the second. In doubles, she and her partner [[Anastasia Pivovarova]] lost in the first round to [[Andrea Hlaváčková]]/[[Lucie Hradecká]]. At the 2011 [[Abierto Mexicano Telcel]], Cîrstea defeated [[Patricia Mayr-Achleitner]] before she lost to [[Laura Pous Tió]]. Sorana played doubles again now with [[Andreja Klepač]] as a partner. They won the first round by defeated [[Eleni Daniilidou]]/[[Jasmin Wöhr]]. They lost in the second round to [[Irina-Camelia Begu]]/[[Alexandra Panova]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://abiertomextenis.com.mx/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/19981202123043/http://www.abiertomextenis.com.mx/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2 December 1998 |title=Abierto Mexicano de Tenis 2011 |publisher=Abiertomextenis.com.mx |access-date=13 October 2011 }}</ref> Cîrstea qualified in singles for the [[2011 BNP Paribas Open|BNP Paribas Open]]. In the first round she lost to [[Alla Kudryavtseva]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bnpparibasopen.com/ |title=Home |publisher=BNP Paribas Open |access-date=13 October 2011}}</ref> In the Sony Ericsson Open, Sorana received a wildcard in singles, but lost in the first round to Zheng Jie.

At the Andalucia Tennis Experience, she lost in the first round to Sara Errani, in three sets. She paired with [[Maria Elena Camerin]] for the doubles tournament defeating [[Alberta Brianti]]/[[Aurélie Védy]]. They lost in the second round to Errani/Vinci.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.andaluciatennis.com |title=Andalucia Tennis Experience / Marbella |publisher=Andaluciatennis.com |access-date=13 October 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111013234348/http://www.andaluciatennis.com/ |archive-date=13 October 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In the following months, Sorana didn't have good results. But she proved she is back in the form as she won [[2011 Open GDF Suez de Cagnes-sur-Mer Alpes-Maritimes|Open GDF Suez]]. It was a $100k tournament with a lot of top-100 players. She beat [[Patty Schnyder]] in the first round and compatriot [[Alexandra Dulgheru]] in the second in the [[2011 French Open|French Open]] before losing to [[Li Na]], in straight sets.

She then made it into the main draw of the [[2011 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon Championships]], where she lost in straight sets to [[Pauline Parmentier]]. Sorana played doubles again with [[Ayumi Morita]] of Japan defeating [[Chuang Chia-jung]]/[[Hsieh Su-wei]]. They won their second round against [[Sophie Lefèvre]]/[[Evgeniya Rodina]]. They lost in third round to Sabine Lisicki/Sam Stosur.

In Båstad at the Swedish Open, Cîrstea defeated [[Mirjana Lučić]] in the first round. She lost in the second round to [[Vesna Dolonc]]. At the XXIV SNAI Open Internationali Femminili di Tennis di Palermo, Sorana won her first round match against Andrea Hlaváčková. She lost then to [[Tsvetana Pironkova]]. At the ITF Bucharest, Cîrstea beat her compatriots [[Diana Enache]] and [[Mădălina Gojnea]], before losing in the third round to [[Laura Pous Tió]]. She then played in Carlsbad, California, at the [[Mercury Insurance Open]]. She was beaten by [[CoCo Vandeweghe]] in a close match in the first round. Next tournament in Cincinnati, she qualified in singles for the main draw. In the first round she defeated [[Ksenia Pervak]], but lost in the second to [[Jill Craybas]] in the third set tiebreaker.

In Dallas, the inaugural tournament of [[Texas Tennis Open]], Cîrstea beat [[Jarmila Gajdošová]] in the first round, in the second she lost to [[Aravane Rezaï]]. She played doubles for the first time with [[Alberta Brianti]] of Italy. ===2013: First Premier 5 final and career-best ranking at No. 21===
{{BLP unsourced section|date=March 2015}}
[[File:Sorana Cirstea 2013 Indian Wells.jpg|thumb|left|Cîrstea at the 2013 BNP Paribas Open]]
Sorana began the year at the [[2013 ASB Classic]] where she lost her opener to [[Heather Watson]]. The following week, she defeated [[Nina Bratchikova]] before losing to [[Lauren Davis]] in the second round at [[2013 Moorilla Hobart International|Hobart]]. Cîrstea reached the third round of the [[2013 Australian Open|Australian Open]] losing to Li Na. She then followed it up with a semifinal showing at the [[2013 PTT Pattaya Open|Pattaya Open]] losing to eventual champion [[Maria Kirilenko]]. She then reached the third round at [[2013 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]] where she lost to Agnieszka Radwańska. At the [[2013 Sony Open Tennis|Sony Open]] in Miami, Cîrstea made the 4th round after defeating Angelique Kerber in straight sets. She fell to Jelena Janković (0–6, 4–6) in the next round. At the [[2013 French Open|French Open]], she reached the third round losing to world No. 1 and eventual champion, Serena Williams. She then reached the quarterfinal of the [[2013 Aegon Classic|Aegon Classic]] falling to [[Donna Vekić]]. Cîrstea lost to [[Camila Giorgi]] in the second round at [[2013 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]].

She began the [[2013 US Open Series|US Open Series]] with a semifinal at the [[2013 Bank of the West Classic|Bank of the West Classic]] losing to Dominika Cibulková and a quarterfinal at the [[2013 Citi Open|Citi Open]] losing to Alizé Cornet. In the [[2013 Rogers Cup – Women's Singles|Rogers Cup]] en route to reaching her first final in five years, Cîrstea beat two former number-one players, Caroline Wozniacki (5–7, 7–6, 6–4) and Jelena Janković (6–3, 6–4), and former Grand Slam champions Petra Kvitová (4–6, 7–5, 6–2) and Li Na (6–1, 7–6). She lost to top-seeded Serena Williams in the final, 2–6, 0–6. She then withdrew from the [[2013 Western & Southern Open|Western & Southern Open]] citing a back injury, and later retired from her first round match at the [[2013 New Haven Open at Yale|New Haven Open at Yale]] while trailing 3–0 against Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova. She had reached now the 21st place in the WTA rankings.

At the [[2013 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], she defeated qualifier [[Sharon Fichman]] before then being upset by qualifier Kurumi Nara 7–5, 6–1 in the second round. Her next tournament was the [[2013 Guangzhou International Women's Open|Guangzhou International Women's Open]] where she suffered another early-round loss, this time to Bojana Jovanovski 2–6, 1–6. She advanced to the second round of the [[2013 Toray Pan Pacific Open|Pan Pacific Open]] after beating Julia Görges. She then defeated [[Misaki Doi]] before losing to Svetlana Kuznetsova in the third round. She then lost her opening matches at [[2013 China Open (tennis)|Beijing]] and [[2013 Generali Ladies Linz|Linz]] to Bojana Jovanovski and Patricia Mayr-Achleitner.

By the end of the year, she had lost her No. 1 Romanian position to rising star Simona Halep.

===2014: Injuries and Fed Cup success===
{{BLP unsourced section|date=March 2015}}
[[File:Cirstea MA14 (15) (14424955762).jpg|thumb|Sorana Cirstea at the 2014 Mutua Madrid Open]]
Being ranked 22nd in the WTA rankings, Cîrstea began 2014 by losing her openers at [[2014 ASB Classic|Auckland]] to qualifier Sharon Fichman and at the [[2014 Apia International Sydney|Apia International Sydney]] to qualifier and eventual champion [[Tsvetana Pironkova]]. At the [[2014 Australian Open|Australian Open]], Cîrstea was the 21st seed, but she was defeated in the first round by [[Marina Erakovic]]. Cîrstea then reached her first quarterfinal of the year at the [[2014 PTT Pattaya Open|Pattaya Open]] where she was seeded third. She beat [[Anna Karolína Schmiedlová]] and [[Alison Riske]] before losing to eventual finalist [[Karolína Plíšková]] in three sets.

At the [[2014 Qatar Total Open]], she defeated qualifier Alla Kudryavtseva in the first round. Then, she faced second seed Agnieszka Radwańska in the second round and lost. The following week at [[2014 Dubai Tennis Championships|Dubai]], she defeated Roberta Vinci in the first round and fourth seed Sara Errani in the second round for a place in her second quarterfinal of the year. She subsequently lost to eighth seed Caroline Wozniacki in the quarterfinal (1–6, 2–6). Cîrstea was the 25th seed at both the [[2014 BNP Paribas Open|BNP Paribas Open]] and the [[2014 Sony Open Tennis|Sony Open Tennis]]. She lost in the second round at both tournaments to qualifier [[Camila Giorgi]] and Tsvetana Pironkova. Cîrstea had received first-round byes from both tournaments. She was the eighth seed at the [[2014 Family Circle Cup|Family Circle Cup]], but she lost in the second round to [[Teliana Pereira]]. Cîrstea had a first-round bye there as well.

Despite the poor form in WTA tournaments, in April, Cîrstea made a big contribution in [[2014 Fed Cup World Group II Play-offs]], helping Romania promote to World Group II after defeating Serbia 4–1, with Cîrstea winning both her ties against [[Ana Ivanovic]] and Bojana Jovanovski.

At the [[2014 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix|Porsche Tennis Grand Prix]], Cîrstea lost in the first round to German wildcard [[Julia Görges]]. She took fifth seed Petra Kvitová to three sets in their opener at the [[2014 Mutua Madrid Open|Madrid Open]] but still lost 1–6, 7–5, 6–7. Then, Cîrstea succumbed to American qualifier Christina McHale in the first round at the [[2014 Internazionali BNL d'Italia|Internazionali d'Italia]]. Cîrstea managed to end her losing streak at the [[2014 French Open|French Open]] where she was the 26th seed. In the first round, she beat qualifier [[Aleksandra Wozniak]] and then [[Teliana Pereira]]. Cîrstea lost in the third round to sixth seed Jelena Janković, 1–6, 2–6.

At the [[2014 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon Championships]], Cîrstea was the 29th seed. In the first round, she lost to young American qualifier [[Victoria Duval]]. Cîrstea was the top seed at [[2014 Baku Cup|Baku Cup]] and defeated Tunisian wildcard [[Ons Jabeur]] in the first round but then lost to eventual semifinalist Stefanie Vögele 1–6, 1–6. Cîrstea was the eighth seed at the [[2014 Citi Open|Citi Open]]. She defeated [[Kiki Bertens]] in the first round but then lost to Bojana Jovanovski. At the [[2014 Rogers Cup|Rogers Cup]], Cîrstea fell to 15th seed Lucie Šafářová in the first round failing to defend her finalist points from the year before. At the [[2014 Western & Southern Open]], Cîrstea lost in the 1st round to ninth seed and eventual finalist Ana Ivanovic. After Cincinnati, Cîrstea got a wildcard for qualifying at the [[2014 Connecticut Open|Connecticut Open]]. In the first round of qualifying, Cîrstea beat Alison Van Uytvanck. In the second round of qualifying, Cîrstea lost to seventh seed Kaia Kanepi, in three sets.

At the [[2014 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]], Cîrstea easily beat [[Heather Watson]] in the first round 6–1, 6–1. In the second round, Cîrstea lost to seventh seed [[Eugenie Bouchard]]. At the [[2014 Guangzhou International Women's Open|Guangzhou International Open]], she lost in the first round to qualifier [[Petra Martić]] 6–7, 1–6. At the [[2014 Wuhan Open|Wuhan Open]], Cîrstea lost in the first round of qualifying to top seed [[Zarina Diyas]]. Cîrstea's final tournament of the year was the [[2014 China Open (tennis)|China Open]] where she fell in the first round of qualifying to ninth seed [[Ajla Tomljanović]].

At the end of the year, Cîrstea claimed that she had persistent small injuries particularly afflicting her shoulder.

Cîrstea ended 2014 ranked 93 in her seventh straight top-100 season.

===2016: Comeback to top 100 and first Premier Mandatory quarterfinals===
Cîrstea began the year as world No. 244. In January, she played at $25k tournaments in Guaruja and Bertioga, both Brazil. In Guaruja, she beat [[Oleksandra Korashvili]], [[Rebecca Šramková]], [[Jil Teichmann]], and third seed [[Beatriz Haddad Maia]] before losing to [[Montserrat Gonzalez]] in three sets in the final.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gao |first1=Max |title=ITF Roundup: American Rising Stars Sofia Kenin, Tommy Paul Victorious This Week |url=https://www.vavel.com/en-us/tennis-usa/2016/01/25/595879-itf-roundup-american-rising-stars-sofia-kenin-tommy-paul-victorious-this-week.html |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=24 January 2016}}</ref> At Bertioga, she beat [[Sandra Zaniewska]], [[Victoria Bosio]], Jil Teichmann and Rebecca Šramková before beating [[Catalina Pella]] in the final.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gao |first1=Max |title=ITF Roundup: Christina McHale, Sorana Cirstea, Nils Langer Claim Championships |url=https://www.vavel.com/en-us/tennis-usa/2016/02/01/599185-itf-roundup-christina-mchale-christina-mchale-sorana-cirstea-nils-langer-claim-championships.html |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=31 January 2016}}</ref> It was the first tournament win after a period of almost four years.

In February, Cîrstea returned to the WTA Tour by playing at the [[2016 Rio Open – Women's Singles|Rio Open]]. As a wildcard, she reached the semifinals with straight victories against Brazilian wildcard Beatriz Haddad Maia, fifth seed Polona Hercog, and third seed [[Danka Kovinić]]. Cîrstea lost in the semifinals to [[Shelby Rogers]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Beck |first1=James |title=Rogers advances to Rio Open final |url=https://www.postandcourier.com/sports/rogers-advances-to-rio-open-final/article_80017a30-92b9-5965-9c13-efcc66c917b4.html |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=19 February 2016}}</ref> Her performance moved her ranking from 199 to 153. Also in February, she played at a $25k tournament in São Paulo. She defeated Yvonne Cavalle-Reimers, [[Martina Caregaro]], and Jil Teichmann en route to the semifinals where she lost to [[Sara Sorribes Tormo]].

In March, Cîrstea traveled to Miami to compete at the [[2016 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami Masters]]. Receiving a wildcard for qualifying, she lost in the final round of qualifying to 17th seed Francesca Schiavone. Also in March, Sorana played at the [[2016 Engie Open de Seine-et-Marne – Singles|Engie Open de Seine-et-Marne]], an $50k event in [[Croissy-Beaubourg]], France. She defeated [[Elitsa Kostova]], Josephine Boualem, and Andreea Mitu en route to the semifinals, where she lost to second seed [[Pauline Parmentier]].<ref>{{cite news |title=A Franco-Serbian duel on the menu |url=http://www.leparisien.fr/seine-et-marne-77/un-duel-franco-serbe-au-menu-02-04-2016-5679879.php |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=2 April 2016}}</ref>

Cîrstea kicked off her clay-court season at the [[2016 İstanbul Cup – Singles|İstanbul Cup]]. Seeded fifth for qualifying, she made it to the main draw beating Turkish wildcards [[Pemra Özgen]] and [[Başak Eraydın]]. In the main draw, she upset third seed and defending champion [[Lesia Tsurenko]] in the first round.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sakkari delighted to oust struggling Schmiedlova |url=https://sports.yahoo.com/news/sakkari-delighted-oust-struggling-schmiedlova-192000948.html |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=19 April 2016}}</ref> In the second round, she lost to eventual champion [[Çağla Büyükakçay]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Çağla Büyükakçay Rises to the Quarterfinals at the Istanbul Cup |url=https://www.haberler.com/cagla-buyukakcay-istanbul-cup-ta-ceyrek-finale-8376031-haberi/ |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=21 April 2016}}</ref> Cîrstea successfully qualified for the [[2016 J&T Banka Prague Open – Singles|Prague Open]] defeating Karolína Beránková, [[Anastasiya Komardina]], and [[Amandine Hesse]]. She lost in the first round in three sets to the fourth seed and eventual finalist Sam Stosur.<ref>{{cite news |title=WTA PRAGUE&RABAT- Kuznetsova, Safarova, Pliskova and Stosur move into the second round |url=https://www.tennisworldusa.org/tennis/news/On_the_WTA_results_with/32216/wta-praguerabat-kuznetsova-safarova-pliskova-and-stosur-move-into-the-second-round/ |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=26 April 2016}}</ref> Receiving a wild card for the [[2016 Mutua Madrid Open – Women's Singles|Madrid Open]], Cîrstea reached the quarterfinals beating Jelena Jankovic, Danka Kovinić and [[Laura Siegemund]] en route. In the quarterfinals, she faced Dominika Cibulková and lost in three sets.<ref>{{cite news |title=Harrison's Chirico advances to Madrid Open semifinals |url=https://www.lohud.com/story/sports/2016/05/05/chirico-advances-madrid-open-semifinals/83984888/ |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=5 May 2016}}</ref> Cîrstea qualified for the [[2016 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]] defeating [[Zhang Yuxuan]], Jana Čepelová, and Elise Mertens. She lost in the first round to 18th seed [[Elina Svitolina]].<ref>{{cite news |title=ELINA ADVANCED TO THE SECOND ROUND OF THE FRENCH OPEN |url=http://svitolina.com/en/news/233-news-25-05.html |access-date=2 February 2020 |date=25 May 2016}}</ref> 

Cîrstea started her grass-court season at the first edition of the [[2016 Mallorca Open – Singles|Mallorca Open]]. Seeded second for qualifying, she won all of her matches to advance to the main draw beating [[Valentyna Ivakhnenko]], Sesil Karatantcheva, and [[Mandy Minella]]. She advanced to the quarterfinals defeating fifth seed Yulia Putintseva and wild card Daniela Hantuchová. She lost in the quarterfinals to second seed Jelena Janković.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Outlaw |first1=Adrianna |title=Garcia to Face Flipkens in Mallorca Semifinals |url=http://www.tennisnow.com/News/2016/June/Garcia-to-Face-Flipkens-in-Mallorca-Semifinals.aspx |access-date=2 February 2020 |date=17 June 2016}}</ref> Cîrstea played in her final tournament before Wimbledon at the [[2016 Aegon International Eastbourne – Singles|International Eastbourne]]. She retired during her first round of qualifying match over seventh seed [[Varvara Lepchenko]]. At the [[2016 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon Championships]], Cîrstea was defeated in the first round by tenth seed and two time Wimbledon Champion Petra Kvitová.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Low |first1=Silas |title=Wimbledon: Petra Kvitova scurries into second round after straight-set demolition of Sorana Cirstea |url=https://www.vavel.com/en-us/tennis-usa/2016/06/29/664305-wimbledon-petra-kvitova-scurries-into-second-round-after-straight-set-demolition-of-sorana-cirstea.html |access-date=2 February 2020 |publisher=VAVEL |date=29 June 2016}}</ref>

After Wimbledon, Cîrstea played at the [[2016 Reinert Open – Singles|Reinert Open]], a $50k event in Versmold, Germany. As the second seed, she made it to the semifinals beating qualifier [[Andrea Gámiz]], [[Nadia Podoroska]], and eighth seed [[Lesley Kerkhove]]. She lost her semifinal match to [[Antonia Lottner]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Reinert Open: Grand victory of Antonia Lottner over Romanian Sorana Cirstea |url=https://www.wtv.de/leistungssport/reinert-open-grandioser-sieg-von-antonia-lottner-%C3%BCber-rum%C3%A4nin-sorana-cirstea |access-date=2 February 2020 |date=9 July 2016}}</ref> Competing at the [[2016 Swedish Open – Women's Singles|Swedish Open]], Cîrstea lost in the first round to fourth seed [[Annika Beck]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Beck and Errani into 2nd round at Bastad tournament |url=https://www.si.com/tennis/2016/07/19/ap-ten-swedish-open |access-date=2 February 2020 |date=19 July 2016}}</ref>

Cîrstea played only two tournaments during the US Open Series. At the [[2016 Western & Southern Open – Women's Singles|Western & Southern Open]], she lost in the final round of qualifying to seventh seed Alizé Cornet. At the [[2016 Connecticut Open – Singles|Connecticut Open]], she lost in the second round of qualifying to [[Louisa Chirico]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Begin |first1=Sean |title=Germany's Beck Advances At Connecticut Open With Win In Qualifying Round |url=https://www.courant.com/sports/hc-conn-open-notebook-0821-20160820-story.html |access-date=3 February 2020 |date=20 August 2016}}</ref> Playing at the [[2016 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]], she lost in the first round to compatriot [[Ana Bogdan]] in three sets.<ref>{{cite news |title=Tennis. Ana Bogdan defeated Sorana Cîrstea in the first round of the US Open |url=https://www.digi24.ro/stiri/sport/tenis/tenis-ana-bogdan-a-invins-o-pe-sorana-cirstea-in-primul-tur-al-us-open-555974 |access-date=3 February 2020 |date=30 August 2016}}</ref>

After the US Open, Cîrstea competed at the [[2016 Engie Open de Biarritz – Singles|Open de Biarritz]], a $100k event in France. As the second seed, she advanced to the semifinals where she lost to eighth seed Rebecca Šramková. During the last week of September, Cîrstea played at the [[2016 Tashkent Open – Singles|Tashkent Open]]. As the seventh seed, she was defeated in the first round by Francesca Schiavone.<ref>{{cite news |title=Flipkens advances, Buyukakcay out at Tashkent Open |url=https://apnews.com/97d2bfb987a94a80b00eae8747a8a67f/Flipkens-advances,-Buyukakcay-out-at-Tashkent-Open |access-date=3 February 2020 |date=27 September 2016}}</ref> Playing at the [[2016 Generali Ladies Linz – Singles|Generali Ladies Linz]] in Austria, Cîrstea upset sixth seed Kiki Bertens in the first round.<ref>{{cite news |title=In Linz, Bertens slumps to 6th straight opening-round defeat |url=https://www.si.com/tennis/2016/10/10/ap-ten-generali-ladies |access-date=3 February 2020 |date=10 October 2016}}</ref> She lost in the second round to French qualifier [[Océane Dodin]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Top-seeded Muguruza wins Linz opener, Dodin reaches quarters |url=https://apnews.com/d19d3f52f2a44d0f98c6ff71645cf745 |access-date=3 February 2020 |date=12 October 2016}}</ref> Her last WTA event of the year was at the [[2016 BGL Luxembourg Open – Singles|BGL Luxembourg Open]] where she lost in the first round to qualifier [[Tereza Smitková]].

Cîrstea's final two tournaments of the season were on the ITF Circuit. At the [[2016 Internationaux Féminins de la Vienne – Singles|Internationaux de la Vienne]] in Poitiers, France, she lost in the first round to [[Anett Kontaveit]]. Playing her last tournament of 2016 at the [[2016 Open de Limoges – Singles|Open de Limoges]], she was defeated in the second round by [[Tamara Korpatsch]].

Cîrstea ended the year ranked 81.

===2017: Australian Open round of 16===
[[File:Cirstea WM17 (8) (36016694002).jpg|thumb|Cîrstea at the [[2017 Wimbledon Championships]]]]
Cîrstea started her 2017 season at the [[2017 WTA Shenzhen Open – Singles|Shenzhen Open]]. She defeated [[Kristína Kučová]] in the first round before losing to top seed and defending champion Agnieszka Radwańska in the second round.<ref>{{cite news |title=AGNIESZKA RADWANSKA REACHES QUARTERFINALS AT SHENZHEN OPEN |url=https://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2017/01/agnieszka-radwanska-reaches-quarterfinals-at-shenzhen-open/63054/ |access-date=4 February 2020 |date=3 January 2017}}</ref> Cîrstea made it to the fourth round of the [[2017 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]] for the first time in her career defeating [[Irina Khromacheva]], tenth seed [[Carla Suárez Navarro]], and Alison Riske in the first three rounds before losing to seventh seed [[Garbiñe Muguruza]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Toderaș |first1=Ramona |title=Australian Open: Garbiñe Muguruza moves past Sorana Cîrstea to reach quarterfinals |url=https://www.vavel.com/en-us/tennis-usa/2017/01/22/745784-garbine-muguruza-moves-past-sorana-cirstea-to-reach-quarterfinals.html |access-date=4 February 2020 |publisher=VAVEL |date=22 January 2017}}</ref> 

Her next tournament was the [[2017 Taiwan Open – Singles|Taiwan Open]]. Cirstea retired midway through her first-round match against [[Risa Ozaki]] due to a left wrist injury.<ref>{{cite news |title=Doi Knocks Out Hibino In All-Japanese Battle In Taipei City |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1417934/doi-knocks-out-hibino-in-all-japanese-battle-in-taipei-city |access-date=4 February 2020 |date=19 January 2017}}</ref> She returned from injury to be a part of the Romanian team at the [[2017 Fed Cup World Group II|Fed Cup World Group II]] against Belgium. She lost the second rubber against Yanina Wickmayer in a close three-setter which went on for 3 hours & 22 minutes.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Milosavljevic |first1=Zoran |title=BELGIUM STUNS ROMANIA TO TAKE CONTROL ON OPENING DAY |url=https://www.fedcup.com/en/news/253497.aspx |access-date=4 February 2020 |publisher=fedcup.com |date=11 February 2017}}</ref> Even though Romania lost the tie against Belgium, she won her doubles match teaming up with Monica Niculescu.

After making first-round exits at the [[2017 Hungarian Ladies Open – Singles|Hungarian Ladies Open]] and at [[2017 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|Indian Wells]], she won her first singles match in nearly two months against [[Monica Puig]] in the first round of the [[2017 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami Open]]. Cîrstea fell to Caroline Wozniacki in the third round. She made the quarterfinals of the [[2017 Mutua Madrid Open – Women's Singles|Mutua Madrid Open]] where she lost to [[Kristina Mladenovic]], and the semifinals of the [[2017 Nürnberger Versicherungscup – Singles|Nuremberg Cup]]. At the [[2017 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]] she lost to 21st seed Carla Suárez Navarro in the second round.

Cîrstea reached the third round of [[2017 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]] before bowing out to Muguruza.
