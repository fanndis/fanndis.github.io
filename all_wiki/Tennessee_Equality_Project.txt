The '''Tennessee Equality Project''' is an [[LGBT]] organization in the United States state of [[Tennessee]].<ref name="green">Frances Green, ''Gayellow Pages USA 2008-2009'', Renaissance House, 2008, p. 400 [https://books.google.com/books?id=24PciB07qbQC&pg=PA400&]</ref>

==Overview==
The Tennessee Equality Project was founded on June 15, 2004, in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref name="about">{{cite web|url=http://tnequalityproject.org/about-us/ |title=About Us |publisher=Tennessee Equality Project |date=2004-06-15 |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref><ref name="cable">''[[Out & About Newspaper]]'':  [http://www.outandaboutnewspaper.com/article/4770 "Tennessee Equality Project wins CABLE award", May 11, 2011], accessed July 4, 2011</ref>

The organization has lobbied against a 2006 [[Tennessee Amendment 1 (2006)|referendum to amend the state constitution to ban same-sex marriage]],<ref name="advocate">''[[The Advocate (LGBT magazine)|The Advocate]]'': [https://books.google.com/books?id=2WMEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA13& "Marriage Ban Momentum: Will 2005 become a repeat of last year's frenzy state constitutional amendments?", February 15, 2005], accessed July 4, 2011</ref> the dismissal of a lesbian soccer coach by [[Belmont University]] in 2010,<ref name="hrc">Human Rights Campaign: [http://www.hrc.org/issues/15142.htm Tennessee Equality Project Call on Belmont University Officials to Halt Anti-LGBT Policies', December 6, 2010], accessed July 4, 2011</ref> the '' Don't Say Gay'' bill sponsored in 2011 by Sen. [[Stacey Campfield]] and Rep. [[Bill Dunn (American politician)|Bill Dunn]],<ref name="brewer">''[[Pink News]]'': [http://www.pinknews.co.uk/2011/03/01/legislation-to-ban-gay-awareness-in-tennessee-schools-proposed/ Steve Brewer, "Tennessee proposes bill to ban gay awareness in schools", March 1, 2011], accessed July 4, 2011</ref><ref name="geen">''Pink News'':  [http://www.pinknews.co.uk/2011/04/28/anger-over-tennessees-dont-say-gay-bill/ Jessica Geen, "Anger over Tennessee's 'donâ€™t say gay' bill", 28 April 2011], accessed July 4, 2011</ref> and other public issues. It endorsed Nashville mayor [[Karl Dean]] for re-election in 2011.<ref name="endorsements">''Out & About Newspaper'': [http://www.outandaboutnewspaper.com/article/4838 "Tennessee Equality Project PA announces endorsements", June 12, 2011], accessed July 4, 2011</ref>

In December 2015, TEP attempted to open an LGBT center in [[Maryville, Tennessee|Maryville]], the county seat of [[Blount County, Tennessee|Blount County]] in partnership with local stakeholders, but this effort was ultimately unsuccessful.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bales-Sherrod|first1=Lesli|title=LGBT community center on its way|url=http://www.thedailytimes.com/news/lgbt-community-center-on-its-way/article_08e01deb-cffb-54d2-8c4d-de2a15b79a5c.html|accessdate=November 30, 2015|work=The Daily Times|location=Maryville, Tennessee|date=November 28, 2015}}</ref>

The Project has received funds from the [[Human Rights Campaign]]<ref name="brewer"/><ref name="hrc2">Human Rights Campaign: [http://www.hrc.org/15331.htm "Human Rights Campaign Makes $10,000 Grant to Tennessee Equality Project", February 17, 2011] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110706160933/http://www.hrc.org/15331.htm |date=July 6, 2011 }}, accessed July 4, 2011</ref> and has partnered with the California-based [[Courage Campaign]] to develop political advertising based on the life story of an LGBT Tennessee resident.<ref name="courage">''Out & About Newspaper'': [http://www.outandaboutnewspaper.com/article/4887 "Courage Campaign seeks video testimonies", June 30, 2011], accessed July 4, 2011</ref> The organization is a member of the [[Equality Federation]].<ref name="outnashville705">{{cite news | url=http://www.outandaboutnashville.com/story/equality-federation-supports-state-level | title=Equality Federation supports state level activists | work=Out & About Nashville | date=July 1, 2005 | accessdate=January 17, 2014 | author=Maples-Bays, Beth}}</ref>

In April 2019, the TEP received a $113,000 donation and a handwritten letter of support from singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]].<ref name="buzzfeedswiftjustdonated">{{cite news |last1=Bate |first1=Ellie |title=Taylor Swift Just Donated $113,000 To An Organisation That Fights Anti-LGBT Laws In Tennessee |url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/eleanorbate/taylor-swift-donated-lgbt-charity |work=Buzzfeed |date=April 9, 2019}}</ref><ref name="huffpostswiftdonates">{{cite news |last1=Saba |first1=Hamedy |title=Taylor Swift Donates $113,000 To LGBTQ Advocacy Group In Tennessee |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/entry/taylor-swift-donation-lgbtq-advocacy-group-tennessee-equality-project_n_5cabffe1e4b01b34503a9025?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvLnVrLw&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAALc1xhW5hRqX_YZTHsiy1vGrkLuxaDKJMkdYFPoffPBAbGMGVNnfezEEDWQ1KuNVt2_9fesTiBfL7ta0cUrdMK6jLAnXFP5oxwT-o5lEprvCRqb6bndLPTKAtuuvoGtYE2D6rKeY_R5290Mxql1J5PhODNwJWoDSR-H3H6RahN_H |accessdate=April 9, 2019 |work=Huffington Post |date=April 9, 2019}}</ref>
