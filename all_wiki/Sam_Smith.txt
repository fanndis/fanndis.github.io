'''Samuel Frederick Smith''' (born 19 May 1992) is an English singer and songwriter. Smith rose to prominence in October 2012 after being featured on [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure's]] breakthrough single "[[Latch (song)|Latch]]", which peaked at number eleven on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. Smith was subsequently featured on [[Naughty Boy]]'s "[[La La La (Naughty Boy song)|La La La]]", which became a number one single in May 2013. In December 2013, Smith was nominated for the [[2014 Brit Awards|2014 Brit Critics' Choice Award]] and the [[BBC]]'s [[Sound of...|Sound of 2014]] poll,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25216850|title=Brits announce Critics' Choice 2014 shortlist|publisher=BBC|date=5 December 2013|accessdate=19 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p01ky6g6/profiles/samsmith|title=Sound of 2014 Profile: Sam Smith|publisher=BBC|accessdate=10 January 2014}}</ref> winning both.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/brit-awards/10511427/Brits-Critics-Choice-Award-2014-Sam-Smith-announced-as-winner.html|title=Brits Critics' Choice Award 2014: Sam Smith announced as winner|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=12 December 2013|accessdate=19 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25635440|title=BBC Sound of 2014: Sam Smith |publisher=BBC|date=10 January 2014 |accessdate=10 January 2014 }}</ref>

Smith's debut studio album, ''[[In the Lonely Hour]]'', was released in May 2014 on [[Capitol Records UK]].<ref name="inthelonelyhour">{{cite web|url=http://www.factmag.com/2013/12/16/sam-smith-announces-debut-album-in-the-lonely-hour-shares-tour-dates/|title=Sam Smith announces debut album ''In the Lonely Hour'', shares tour dates|last=|first=|date=16 December 2013|website=|publisher=Fact|accessdate=19 December 2013}}</ref> The album's lead single, "[[Lay Me Down (Sam Smith song)|Lay Me Down]]", was released prior to "La La La". The album's second single, "[[Money on My Mind]]", became Smith's second number one single in the UK.<ref name="moneyonmymind">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/money-on-my-mind-ep/id775088328|title='Money on My Mind' – EP by Sam Smith|publisher=[[iTunes]] (UK)|accessdate=19 December 2013}}</ref> The third single, "[[Stay with Me (Sam Smith song)|Stay with Me]]", was an international success, reaching number one in the UK and number two on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], while their singles "[[I'm Not the Only One]]" and "[[Like I Can]]" reached the top ten in the UK. The album won four awards, at the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]], including [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album|Best Pop Vocal Album]], [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]], [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]], and nominations for [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance|Best Pop Solo Performance]].

For Smith's and [[Jimmy Napes]]'s song "[[Writing's on the Wall (Sam Smith song)|Writing's on the Wall]]", the theme for the [[James Bond in film|James Bond]] film ''[[Spectre (2015 film)|Spectre]]'' (2015), Smith won the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Golden Globe Award]] and the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]]. Smith's second studio album, ''[[The Thrill of It All (Sam Smith album)|The Thrill of It All]]'', was released in November 2017 and debuted atop the UK and US album charts. The lead single, "[[Too Good at Goodbyes]]", reached number one in the UK and Australia and number four in the US. Smith released the single "[[Promises (Calvin Harris and Sam Smith song)|Promises]]" with [[Calvin Harris]], which peaked at number one in the UK. Smith released "[[Dancing with a Stranger]]" with [[Normani]] in 2019, which reached the top ten in the UK and the US and was nominated for [[Brit Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] at the [[2020 Brit Awards]], and followed it by releasing a string of commercially successful singles "[[How Do You Sleep? (Sam Smith song)|How Do You Sleep?]]", "[[To Die For (Sam Smith song)|To Die For]]", and "[[I'm Ready (Sam Smith and Demi Lovato song)|I'm Ready]]" with [[Demi Lovato]]; all of which feature on Smith's third album, ''[[Love Goes]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sam Smith's new album 'Love Goes' will arrive October 30; listen to "Diamonds" now – Music News – ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/9/17/sam-smiths-new-album-love-goes-will-arrive-october-30-listen.html|access-date=18 September 2020|website=abcnewsradioonline.com|language=en}}</ref>

Smith has won four [[Grammy Award]]s, three [[Brit Awards]], three [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], and an [[American Music Awards|American Music Award]], as well as a [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe]] and an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] through their career. Smith is [[Non-binary gender|non-binary]] and uses [[singular they|they/them]] pronouns.<ref name="pronouns" /><ref name="pronouns 2" />

==Music career==
===2008–2009: Career beginnings with debut singles===
Smith released two singles before 2010, "Bad Day All Week" and "When It's Alright".

===2012–2013: Breakthrough===
Smith was featured on the [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]] song "[[Latch (song)|Latch]]", which was released on 8 October 2012 and peaked at No. 11 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. In February 2013, Smith released the first single from the debut album, "[[Lay Me Down (Sam Smith song)|Lay Me Down]]", and later in the year featured on [[Naughty Boy]]'s single "[[La La La (Naughty Boy song)|La La La]]". It was released on 19 May 2013 and peaked at No. 1 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. Smith's first EP ''[[Nirvana (EP)|Nirvana]]'' was released the following year. The first song on the EP, titled "[[Safe with Me]]", is produced by Two Inch Punch and was first broadcast on [[MistaJam]]'s [[BBC Radio 1Xtra]] show on 24 July 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.crackintheroad.com/music/23303-new-sam-smith-safe-with-me/|title=New: Sam Smith – Safe With Me|publisher=Crack in the Road|date=24 July 2013|accessdate=24 July 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006123254/http://www.crackintheroad.com/music/23303-new-sam-smith-safe-with-me|archive-date=6 October 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> The second song on the EP is titled "Nirvana" and is produced by Craze & Hoax and Jonathan Creek. The EP also includes Smith's acoustic solo version of "Latch" and a live version of "I've Told You Now".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/nirvana-ep/id708375165|title=iTunes – Music – Nirvana – EP by Sam Smith|publisher=iTunes|accessdate=17 September 2013}}</ref> Smith released the Disclosure, [[Nile Rodgers]], and [[Jimmy Napes]] collaboration "Together" on 25 November 2013 as the only single from ''[[Settle: The Remixes]]''.

===2014–2016: ''In the Lonely Hour'' and international success===
[[File:Sam Smith 2 (15425586230).jpg|thumb|left|upright|Smith singing in Glasgow in 2014]]
The second single from Smith's debut album, titled "[[Money on My Mind]]", was released on 16 February 2014. It was announced on 16 December that Smith's debut studio album, titled ''[[In the Lonely Hour]]'', would be released on 26 May 2014 through [[Capitol Records]].<ref name="inthelonelyhour"/> Smith described the album as "all about unrequited love" as a result of personal experience, having never been loved back by any of their love interests.<ref name="Sound of Boston">{{cite web|url=http://soundofboston.com/angel-cambridge-sam-smith/|title=The Angel of Cambridge: Sam Smith|work=Sound of Boston|last=Ollman|first=Jonah|date=28 March 2014|accessdate=29 March 2014}}</ref> The album reached number one in the [[UK Albums Chart]] and number two on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and by 5 November it had become the second biggest selling album of 2014 in the US behind only ''[[1989 (Taylor Swift album)|1989]]'' by [[Taylor Swift]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Keith|last=Caulfield|title=Official: Taylor Swift's '1989' Debuts With 1.287 Million Sold In First Week|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6304536/official-taylor-swifts-1989-debuts-with-1287-million-sold-in|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|date=4 November 2014|accessdate=4 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/sam-smiths-debut-album-on-track-to-knock-coldplay-off-number-1-3042/|title=Sam Smith's debut album on track to knock Coldplay-off Number 1|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]}}</ref> In January 2015, ''In the Lonely Hour'' was named the second best selling album of 2014 in the UK, behind ''[[x (Ed Sheeran album)|x]]'' by [[Ed Sheeran]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-30641372 "UK album chart for 2014 dominated by British artists"]. BBC. Retrieved 1 January 2014.</ref> A live version of album track "I've Told You Now", performed at [[St Pancras Old Church]], was made available as a free download as part of an [[Amazon.com]] promotion on 27 December 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Told-Live-Pancras-Church-London/dp/B00HG1ANVM|title=I've Told You Now (Live at St Pancras Old Church, London): Sam Smith|date=27 December 2013|access-date=11 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160402175656/http://www.amazon.co.uk/Told-Live-Pancras-Church-London/dp/B00HG1ANVM|archive-date=2 April 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album track "Make It To Me", co-written by Howard of Disclosure and Jimmy Napes, was made available as a free download as part of an [[iTunes Store]] promotion on 13 January 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/make-it-to-me-single/id785860884|title=iTunes – Music – Make It To Me – Single by Sam Smith|publisher=[[iTunes]]|date=13 January 2014}}</ref> Smith went on their debut American headlining tour in the second quarter of 2014, with a setlist of primarily new material.<ref name="Sound of Boston"/>

On 20 January 2014, Smith made their American television debut performing "Latch" with Disclosure on ''[[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]]''. Retrieved 5 December 2014.</ref> The fourth single from the album, "[[I'm Not the Only One]]", reached number three in the UK and number five in the US.<ref name="Billboard"/>

In 2015, it came to light that singer [[Tom Petty]], noting similarities between "Stay with Me" and his 1989 hit song "[[I Won't Back Down]]", had negotiated an out-of-court settlement with Smith in October 2014. Retrieved 26 February 2015.</ref> At the [[2015 Brit Awards]] held at [[The O2 Arena]] in London on 25 February, they performed "Lay Me Down", and won the Brit Awards for [[British Breakthrough Act]], and Global Success.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-30839967 "Brit Awards 2015: Winners list"]. BBC. Retrieved 20 March 2015</ref> At the [[2015 Billboard Music Awards]] on 17 May, Smith received three Billboard Awards: Top Male Artist, Top New Artist, and Top Radio Songs Artist.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/bbma/6568580/billboard-music-awards-2015-winners-list|title=Billboard Music Awards 2015: See the Full Winners List|date=17 May 2015|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=22 August 2015}}</ref> They featured on [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]]'s single "[[Omen (Disclosure song)|Omen]]", which was released on 27 July 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2015/07/disclosure-new-song-and-video-omen-f-sam-smith|title=Disclosure and Sam Smith Connect On "Omen"|date=27 July 2015|accessdate=27 July 2015}}</ref>

Not Really |work=[[The New York Times]] |first=Yonette |last=Joseph |date=29 February 2016 |accessdate=2 March 2016}}</ref>

===2016–2018: ''The Thrill of It All''===
Throughout 2016, Smith released footage of themself in the studio working on new music, leading up to the release of their second studio album. In late 2016, Smith was said to be working with [[Adam Lambert]] on new material. In early 2017, it was reported that Smith would be releasing their second album in September 2017 and had recorded with constant collaborator [[Jimmy Napes]] and [[Naughty Boy]]. In April 2017, Smith was said to have been in studio sessions with [[classical crossover]] group [[Clean Bandit]], working on a possible "comeback single". Along with Clean Bandit, record producer [[Timbaland]] and musician [[Frances (musician)|Frances]] said they had worked on songs for Smith's next album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nwitimes.com/entertainment/music/clean-bandit-working-on-comeback-song-with-sam-smith/article_248112b6-c023-5ba5-8f78-8cd829bb6a39.html|title=Clean Bandit working on comeback song with Sam Smith?|publisher=NWI Times}}</ref>

On 8 September 2017, Smith released a new single titled "[[Too Good at Goodbyes]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://360musicng.org/sam-smith-good-goodbyes-official-single-cover/|title=Sam Smith – Too Good at Goodbyes (Official Single Cover)|work=360musicng.org|date=4 September 2017}}</ref> which debuted at number one in the United Kingdom and number five on the US Billboard Hot 100.<ref>{{cite web|last1=White|first1=Jack|title=Sam Smith scoops his sixth UK Number 1 single with Too Good at Goodbyes|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/sam-smith-scoops-his-sixth-uk-number-1-single-with-too-good-at-goodbyes__20436/|publisher=Official Charts Company|accessdate=15 September 2017|date=15 September 2017}}</ref> Their second studio album, ''[[The Thrill of It All (Sam Smith album)|The Thrill of It All]]'', debuted atop the UK Albums Chart and the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], becoming their first number one album in the US.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8038264/sam-smith-billboard-artist-100-the-thrill-of-it-all|title=Sam Smith Hits No. 1 on Billboard Artist 100, Thanks to 'The Thrill of It All' Debut|work=Billboard|access-date=15 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/sam-smith-s-the-thrill-of-it-all-debuts-at-number-1-on-the-official-albums-chart-this-is-a-dream-come-true-__20979|title=Sam Smith's The Thrill Of It All debuts at Number 1 on the Official Albums Chart: "This is a dream come true"|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|author=Rob Copsey|date=10 November 2017|accessdate=10 November 2017}}</ref> On 20 March 2018, Smith started The Thrill of It All Tour, which visited Europe, North America, Asia, New Zealand and Australia. On 27 March 2018, they released "[[Pray (Sam Smith song)|Pray]]" featuring American rapper [[Logic (musician)|Logic]]. On 17 August 2018, the singer collaborated as the main vocalist on [[Calvin Harris]]'s new track "[[Promises (Calvin Harris and Sam Smith song)|Promises]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thenocturnaltimes.com/calvin-harris-sam-smith-debut-promises/|title=Calvin Harris & Sam Smith Debut "Promises" {{!}} The Nocturnal Times|website=www.thenocturnaltimes.com|language=en-US|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref> The song became Smith's seventh number one hit on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and their first top song on ''Billboard'''s [[Dance/Mix Show Airplay]] chart.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8478334/calvin-harris-ties-rihanna-dance-mix-show-airplay-chart-toppers|title=Calvin Harris Ties Rihanna for Most Dance/Mix Show Airplay Chart No. 1s With 'Promises'|work=Billboard|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref> They also released the song "[[Fire on Fire]]" as the ''[[Watership Down (miniseries)|Watership Down]]'' soundtrack.

===2019–present: ''Love Goes''===
In January 2019, Smith released the song "[[Dancing with a Stranger]]" with [[Normani]] as a single and it reached the top ten in the UK, the US and other countries. On 6 July 2019, [[New 102.7]] radio host Mike Adam announced the song "[[How Do You Sleep? (Sam Smith song)|How Do You Sleep?]]" on their Twitter account.<ref name="Mike Adam">{{cite tweet|user=MikeAdamOnAir|number=1147316780173529089|title=New music from @samsmith is on the way!!! Sam will drop "HOW DO YOU SLEEP?" on July 19th!!! The upcoming #SamSmith single was written with Savan, IIya and Max Martin... if that isn't a recipe for a great song, I don't know what is!|date=6 July 2019|accessdate=10 July 2019}}</ref> On 9 July 2019, Smith posted on social media that an announcement would be made the following day.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=samsmith|number=1148592325398007809|title=🔥🧡 sound on 💛🔥|date=9 July 2019|accessdate=10 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://portalpopline.com.br/sam-smith-prepara-anuncio-especial-para-esta-quarta-feira-10/|title=Sam Smith prepara anúncio especial para esta quarta-feira (10)|website=Portalpopline|language=Portuguese|last=Medeiros|first=Kavad|date=9 July 2019|accessdate=10 July 2019}}</ref> The pre-order link became active on 10 July,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://samsmith.world/HDYS|title=Sam Smith – How Do You Sleep?|website=samsmith.world|accessdate=10 July 2019}}</ref> and later the same day, they formally announced and shared a clip of the song.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=samsmith|number=1148921364377735169|title=HOW DO YOU SLEEP? July 19th|date=10 July 2019|accessdate=10 July 2019}}</ref>

In October 2019, in a new interview with ''The Zach Sang Show'', Smith expressed excitement about their upcoming record, which will land sometime in 2020. The singer also confirmed that their newest offering will feature fewer ballads and plenty of poppier tracks in the vein of their recent two previous singles. "I feel like I have recently shown a side of me which I normally keep to myself or for my family and friends," Smith told Sang. "I showed everyone it and everyone loved it. It's almost given me permission to kinda do what I've always dreamed of doing but I was always scared to do, which is pop music." The singer also described having been spurred on to write new music following a 2018 collaboration with Calvin Harris, "Promises". "I just naturally started getting back into the studio and started to write," they said. "I caught this wind and started riding this wave and I haven't stopped writing since."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/sam-smith-confirms-new-album-coming-2020-2555740|title=Sam Smith confirms new pop-driven album is coming in 2020|date=10 October 2019}}</ref>

On 13 February 2020, Smith revealed that a third studio album, then titled ''To Die For'', was scheduled to be released on 1 May 2020.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[MTV]]|author=Trey Alston|date=13 February 2020|accessdate=13 February 2020|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3156531/sam-smith-to-die-for-album-announcement/|title=SAM SMITH TEASES NEW ALBUM WITH A (LITERALLY) GRABBY PHOTO}}</ref> Later on in the promotion, the album's release date was pushed back to June 2020.

On 30 March 2020, Smith announced that, due to the ongoing effects of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], they would be delaying the release of their third album and would include a number of changes to the album track list and the title, as they would continue to work on it. However, they did state that it would still be released sometime within 2020.<ref name="Fix">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/pop/9413785/sam-smith-releases-cover-coldplay-fix-you|title=Sam Smith Finally Released Their Emotional Cover of Coldplay's 'Fix You'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Kaufamn|first=Gil|date=6 July 2020|accessdate=9 July 2020}}</ref> <br>
"[[I'm Ready (Sam Smith and Demi Lovato song)|I'm Ready]]", a collaboration with American singer [[Demi Lovato]] was released on 16 April and charted well in the top 40 of several countries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9356689/sam-smith-demi-lovato-tease-upcoming-song-collaboration|title=Sam Smith & Demi Lovato Announce New Collaboration|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Peters|first=Mitchell|accessdate=13 April 2020|date=12 April 2020}}</ref>
On 6 July, Smith released their cover of [[Coldplay]]'s "[[Fix You]]". They had originally performed the song earlier in May for the [[iHeartRadio]] Living Room Series.<ref name="Fix" />
On 17 September, they released a new single, "[[Diamonds (Sam Smith song)|Diamonds]]", and announced the new title for their third album, ''[[Love Goes]]'', which was released on 30 October.<ref>https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/sam-smith-diamonds-video-love-goes-1061100/</ref>
