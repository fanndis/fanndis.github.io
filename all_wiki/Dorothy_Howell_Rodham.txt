'''Dorothy Emma Rodham''' (née '''Howell'''; June 4, 1919&nbsp;– November 1, 2011)<ref>1920 Cook Co., IL, U.S. Federal Census, Chicago, 2956 Michigan Ave., Jan. 8 & 9, Enumeration Dist. 66, sheet 4 A, page 73 A, line 34, Dorothy Howell, 7 mons. old.</ref><ref>In daughter Hillary Rodham Clinton's campaign appearance in New York City on June 3rd 2008, following the final primaries of her presidential campaign, she said her mother would be turning 89 the following day.</ref><ref name=Tribune>{{cite news|title=Dorothy Rodham, mother of Hillary Clinton, dies at 92|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/local/breaking/chi-dorothy-rodham-mother-of-hillary-clinton-dies-at-92-20111101,0,3403284.story|newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]]|date=November 1, 2011| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111103004751/http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/local/breaking/chi-dorothy-rodham-mother-of-hillary-clinton-dies-at-92-20111101,0,3403284.story | archivedate=November 3, 2011}}</ref> was an American [[homemaker]] and the mother of former [[First Lady of the United States|First Lady]], [[U.S. Senator]], [[United States Secretary of State]], and 2016 Democratic presidential nominee [[Hillary Clinton]].

==Early life==
Dorothy Howell was born in Chicago, the daughter of Edwin John Howell, Jr. (1897–1946), a Chicago [[firefighter]],<ref name="lh">{{cite book|authorlink=Hillary Clinton|first=Hillary Rodham|last=Clinton|title=[[Living History (book)|Living History]]|publisher=[[Simon & Schuster]]|location=New York|year=2003|isbn=0-7432-2224-5|pages=2–11}}</ref> and Della Murray (1902–1960).<ref name="wargs">{{cite web|authorlink=William Addams Reitwiesner|url=http://www.wargs.com/political/rodham.html|title=The Ancestors of Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton|publisher=wargs.com|accessdate=July 8, 2007|first=William Addams|last=Reitwiesner}}</ref><ref name="nyt-obit"/> She had a younger sister, Isabelle (born 1924).<ref name="lh"/> Her ancestry consisted of Welsh, English, Scottish, French, and distant Dutch heritage; her paternal grandfather was an immigrant from [[Bedminster, Bristol]] in England, and many of her recent forebears had lived in Canada.<ref name="wargs"/>

Her childhood has been described as [[Dickensian]].<ref name="nyt-obit">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/11/02/us/dorothy-rodham-mother-of-hillary-clinton-dies-at-92.html?_r=1&pagewanted=all|title=Dorothy Rodham, Mother and Mentor Of Hillary Clinton, Is Dead at 92|authorlink=Robert D. McFadden|last=McFadden|first=Robert D.|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=November 1, 2011}}</ref><ref name="wapo092307"/><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[NBC Nightly News]]|authorlink=Brian Williams|first=Brian|last=Williams|date=November 1, 2011|title=Nightly broadcast}}</ref> The family lived as boarders in a crowded house.<ref name="nyt-obit"/> The parents were dysfunctional and unhappy<ref name="wapo092307"/> and sometimes prone to violent fights;<ref name="nyt-obit"/> they moved Dorothy around various schools,<ref name=Tribune/> and paid only occasional attention to the children, before divorcing in 1927.<ref name="lh"/>

The children were then sent on a train by themselves, unsupervised (Dorothy was eight years old, Isabelle only three), to live with their paternal grandparents in the [[Los Angeles, California|Los Angeles]] suburb of [[Alhambra, California]].<ref name=Tribune/><ref name="wapo092307"/><ref>1930 Los Angeles Co., CA, U.S. Federal Census, San Gabriel Township, Alhambra City, 320 E. Park St., April 4, Enumeration Dist. 1406, sheet 5 B, page 108 B, line 57.</ref> The sisters endured harsh and unloving treatment from their grandparents.<ref name="wapo092307"/><ref name="pol-hc-dr"/> The grandmother favored black [[Victorian dress]] and punished the girls for trifling acts.<ref name="nyt-kickoff"/> After Dorothy was caught [[trick-or-treating]] one [[Halloween]], an activity the grandparents forbade, she was confined to her room for an entire year except for attending school, and was not even allowed to eat in the kitchen or play in the yard.<ref name="wapo092307"/><ref name="pol-hc-dr">{{cite news|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2014/05/hillary-clinton-mother-memoir-106552.html|title=Hillary Clinton reflects on her mom in memoir|first=Maggie|last=Haberman|newspaper=[[Politico]]|date=May 11, 2014}}</ref>

Having had enough, Dorothy left home at the young age of fourteen in the depths of the [[Great Depression]], working as a housekeeper, cook, and nanny for a [[San Gabriel, California]] family, being paid $3 a week.<ref name="nyt-obit"/><ref name="wapo092307"/><ref name="pol-hc-dr"/> Encouraged by her employer to read and go to school, Dorothy attended [[Alhambra High School (Alhambra, California)|Alhambra High School]], where she joined several clubs and benefited from two teachers.<ref name="nyt-obit"/> After graduating from Alhambra in 1937,<ref>Alhambra High School Yearbook, ''The Alhambran'', 1937.</ref> she moved to Chicago for a failed reunion with her mother,<ref name="lh"/><ref name="wapo092307"/> who by then had married Max Rosenberg.<ref name="bernstein-book"/> Subsequently, she moved into her own apartment there and took office jobs to support herself.<ref name=Tribune/><ref name="lh"/> She later said, "I'd hoped so hard that my mother would love me that I had to take the chance and find out. When she didn't, I had nowhere else to go."<ref name="nyt-obit"/> Hillary Rodham Clinton later attributed her interest in children's welfare to her mother's life as well as her belief that caring adults outside of family can fill a child's emotional voids.<ref name="nyt-obit"/>

==Marriage and family==
While applying for a job as a clerk typist at a textile company, she met a traveling salesman named [[Hugh Rodham (born 1911)|Hugh Ellsworth Rodham]],<ref name="lh"/> eight years her senior, in 1937.<ref name="ttqa">{{cite news|url=http://www.thetimes-tribune.com/site/news.cfm?newsid=18491410&BRD=2185&PAG=461&dept_id=416046&rfi=6|title=Reader Q&A|newspaper=[[The Times-Tribune (Scranton)|The Times-Tribune]]|location=Scranton|date=June 19, 2007}}</ref> After a lengthy courtship, they married in early 1942.<ref name="lh"/>

Their first child and only daughter, [[Hillary Clinton|Hillary]], was born on Sunday October 26, 1947. At the time of her birth, they were living in a one-bedroom apartment in the [[Edgewater, Chicago|Edgewater]] neighborhood of Chicago.<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article">{{cite magazine|last=Hanson|first=Cynthia| title=I Was a Teenage Republican|magazine=[[Chicago (magazine)|Chicago Magazine]]| date=September 1994|url=http://www.chicagomag.com/Chicago-Magazine/September-1994/I-Was-a-Teenage-Republican/index.php?cparticle=1&siarticle=0#artanc}}</ref> (Dorothy read an article about explorer [[Edmund Hillary]]'s name while pregnant, and used his surname's spelling for her name.)<ref name="Two-Ls">{{cite news |last1=Purdum |first1=Todd S. |title=Hillary Clinton Meets Man Who Gave Her 2 L's |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1995/04/03/world/hillary-clinton-meets-man-who-gave-her-2-l-s.html |accessdate=27 July 2020 |work=The New York Times |date=3 April 1995 |location=New York City NY |page=A6}}</ref> The second child, a son named [[Hugh Edwin Rodham|Hugh]], was born in 1950 and during that year, the growing Rodham family moved into a two-story, three-bedroom house in suburban [[Park Ridge, Illinois]].<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/> The couple's third child, a son named [[Tony Rodham|Tony]], was born in 1954. Dorothy was a full-time [[homemaker]].<ref name="bernstein-book"/><ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/>

Dorothy encouraged Hillary to have a love for learning and to pursue an education and a career, though she had never done so herself.<ref name="wapo092307"/> As she later recalled, "I never saw any difference in gender, as far as capabilities or aspirations were concerned. Just because [Hillary] was a girl didn't mean she should be limited."<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/> In contrast to her husband's staunch [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] views,<ref>{{cite book|last=Brock|first=David|authorlink=David Brock|title=[[The Seduction of Hillary Rodham]]|publisher=[[Free Press (publisher)|The Free Press]]|location=New York|year=1996|isbn=0-684-83451-0|page=5}}</ref> Dorothy Rodham was, as her daughter later wrote, essentially a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]], "although she kept it quiet in Republican Park Ridge."<ref name="lh"/> She taught [[Sunday school]] at the First United Methodist Church of Park Ridge.<ref name="bernstein-book"/>

During the 1970s, once her children were grown up, Rodham took courses at [[Oakton Community College]] in a variety of subjects, receiving high grades and earning an [[associate's degree]] in [[liberal arts]].<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/><ref name="wapo-exh"/> She was among the first mothers of that generation to return to school.<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/> In 1987, Rodham and her husband moved to [[Little Rock, Arkansas]], to be closer to their daughter and help care for their young granddaughter, [[Chelsea Clinton|Chelsea]].<ref name="nyt-kickoff"/><ref name="bernstein-book">{{cite book|last=Bernstein|first=Carl|authorlink=Carl Bernstein|title=A Woman in Charge: The Life of Hillary Rodham Clinton|url=https://archive.org/details/womaninchargel00bern|url-access=registration|year=2007|publisher=[[Alfred A. Knopf]]|location=New York|isbn=0-375-40766-9|pages=[https://archive.org/details/womaninchargel00bern/page/14 14], 23–25, 34}}</ref> She took courses in subjects that happened to interest her, focusing on [[psychology]] but including [[logic]] and [[child development]], although she never gained a further degree.<ref name="lh"/><ref name="bernstein-book"/> Her daughter later wrote in her 2003 memoir ''[[Living History (book)|Living History]]'', "I'm still amazed at how my mother emerged from her lonely early life as such an affectionate and levelheaded woman."<ref name=Tribune/>

==Subsequent life==
[[File:Secy of State Hillary Clinton Swearing In.jpg|thumb|right|At Hillary Rodham Clinton's swearing in as U.S. Secretary of State on January 21, 2009]]
Her husband Hugh Rodham died in 1993, shortly after their daughter became First Lady of the United States. Dorothy Rodham remained active but valued her privacy and almost never spoke to the media.<ref name="wapo092307">{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/09/22/AR2007092201175.html|title=A Mother's Strength, a Candidate's Ambition|authorlink=Anne E. Kornblut|first=Anne E.|last=Kornblut|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=September 23, 2007}}</ref> She spent more time at the [[White House]] and accompanied Hillary and Chelsea on visits to France, India, and China; she also enjoyed life in [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref name="nyt-kickoff"/>

At the [[1996 Democratic National Convention]], when [[Bill Clinton]] was nominated for re-election, she appeared in a video message, saying "Everybody knows there is only one person in the world who can really tell the truth about a man, and that's his mother-in-law."<ref name="nyt-kickoff"/> Following the [[Lewinsky scandal]] she was reportedly angry at Bill, but encouraged Hillary to seek her own political career.<ref name="nyt-kickoff"/>

When her daughter was elected to the U.S. Senate in 2000, she moved to Washington, D.C.,<ref name="wapo092307"/> living along [[Connecticut Avenue]].<ref name="hrc-vf-hc-exc"/> She appeared on ''[[The Oprah Winfrey Show]]'' in 2004.<ref name="nyt101106">{{cite news|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9D0DE7D61330F932A25753C1A9609C8B63|title=New Resident at Clinton Home, And She Has a Familiar Name|first=Danny|last=Hakim|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=October 11, 2006}}</ref> Once living alone became too much for her,<ref name="hrc-vf-hc-exc"/> in 2006, she moved into the Clintons' large [[Whitehaven (house)|Whitehaven house]] in the [[Kalorama, Washington, D.C.|Kalorama]] neighborhood of Washington, D.C.<ref name="wapo092307"/><ref name="nyt101106"/><ref name="gerth-vannatta-book">{{cite book|last=Gerth|first=Jeff|authorlink=Jeff Gerth|first2=Don|last2=Van Natta, Jr.|authorlink2=Don Van Natta, Jr.|title=[[Her Way|Her Way: The Hopes and Ambitions of Hillary Rodham Clinton]]|year=2007|publisher=[[Little, Brown and Company]]|location=New York|isbn=0-316-01742-6|page=[https://archive.org/details/herwayhopesambit00gert/page/221 221]}}</ref> There she would often sit and discuss the day when her daughter came home from work.<ref name="nyt-kickoff"/>

Starting in December 2007, she made a rare public appearance in Iowa and other early primary states to campaign for [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2008|Hillary Clinton's 2008 presidential campaign]].<ref name=Tribune/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/politics/2007/12/08/2007-12-08_chelsea_clinton_and_hillarys_mom_join_th.html|title=Chelsea Clinton and Hillary's mom join the campaign in Iowa|first=Michael|last=McAuliff|newspaper=[[New York Daily News]]|date=December 9, 2007}}</ref> She appeared at some events concerning women's issues and also appeared in a Clinton campaign television advertisement.<ref name=Tribune/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://thecaucus.blogs.nytimes.com/2007/12/13/clinton-ad-dorothy-speaks|title=Clinton Ad: Dorothy Speaks|first=Kate|last=Phillips|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=December 13, 2007}}</ref> She was in attendance when her daughter was sworn in as Secretary of State on January 21, 2009.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/45118815/ns/politics-more_politics/t/clintons-mother-dorothy-rodham-dies/#.U3Sgzig-dWs|title=Clinton's mother, Dorothy Rodham, dies at 92|first=Douglass K.|last=Daniel|first2=Matthew|last2=Lee|agency=[[Associated Press]]|publisher=[[NBC News]]|date=November 1, 2011}}</ref>

In her final years, her health began to fail due to heart problems.<ref name="hrc-vf-hc-exc">{{cite news|url=http://www.vogue.com/magazine/article/hillary-clinton-book-hard-choices/#1|title=An Exclusive Excerpt from Hillary Clinton's Upcoming Book, ''Hard Choices''|authorlink=Hillary Clinton|first=Hillary Rodham|last=Clinton|magazine=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|date=May 11, 2014|access-date=May 15, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140515173807/http://www.vogue.com/magazine/article/hillary-clinton-book-hard-choices/#1|archive-date=May 15, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Rodham died at [[George Washington University Hospital]] on November 1, 2011, in Washington, D.C., with Secretary Clinton cancelling a trip overseas, to be by her side; no cause was given.<ref name=Tribune/> Other family members were present as well. A small memorial service was held for her at Whitehaven.<ref name="hrc-vf-hc-exc"/>

==Legacy==
The [[William J. Clinton Presidential Center]] held an exhibit featuring Dorothy Howell Rodham and [[Virginia Dell Kelley]], the mother of Bill Clinton, in 2012.<ref name="wapo-exh"/> It was introduced by a video from Chelsea Clinton in which she talked about the influence her grandmothers had had on her.<ref name="wapo-exh">{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/she-the-people/post/arkansas-exhibit-highlights-bill-and-hillary-clintons-mothers/2012/07/19/gJQATIwtvW_blog.html|title=Arkansas Exhibit Highlights Bill and Hillary Clinton's Mothers|first=Suzi|last=Parker|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=July 19, 2012}}</ref>

In her 2014 memoir ''[[Hard Choices]]'', Hillary Rodham Clinton wrote of Dorothy Howell Rodham, "No one had a bigger influence on my life or did more to shape the person I became."<ref name="pol-hc-dr"/> The struggles that she went through became a major theme of the June 2015 kickoff event to [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2016|Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign]].<ref name="nyt-kickoff">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/06/13/us/politics/story-of-hillary-clintons-mother-forms-emotional-core-of-campaign.html|title=Clinton Embraces Her Mother's Emotional Tale|first=Amy|last=Chozick|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=June 13, 2015|page=A1}}</ref> They were repeated when Clinton gave a victory speech upon becoming the Democratic nominee in early June 2016, saying, "I wish she could see her daughter become the Democratic nominee for President of the United States."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2016/06/08/politics/hillary-clinton-nomination-mother|title=The hero of Clinton's speech: Her mother|first=MJ|last=Lee|first2=Dan|last2=Merica|publisher=[[CNN]]|date=June 8, 2016}}</ref>
