The women's doubles [[tennis]] tournament at the [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Olympic Games]] in [[London]] was held from 28 July to 5 August on the [[grass court]]s of the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in Wimbledon.

All matches were the best of three sets, with [[Tennis score#Scoring a tiebreak game|tie-breaks]] used for the first two sets of each match.<ref name="about">{{cite web|title=Tennis – About|url=http://www.london2012.com/tennis/about/|publisher=Official site of the London 2012 Olympic and Paralympic Games|accessdate=7 July 2012}}</ref>

[[Serena Williams|Serena]] and [[Venus Williams]], from the United States, were the defending champions, having won gold in [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's doubles|Beijing in 2008]]. They successfully defended their title, beating the Czech Republic's [[Andrea Hlaváčková]] and [[Lucie Hradecká]] 6–4, 6–4. With the victory, the [[Williams sisters]] claimed their third gold medal in doubles and also became the first players, male or female, in tennis history to win four Olympic golds, with the pairing winning the doubles gold in 2000, 2008, and 2012, and Venus winning the 2000 women's singles and Serena winning the 2012 women's singles tournaments.<ref name="williams">{{cite web|title=Day 9: Williams sisters make history with doubles gold|url=http://2012.itftennis.com/olympics/news/articles/london-2012-day-9-williams-sisters-make-history-with-doubles-gold.aspx|publisher=Official site of the London 2012 Olympic and Paralympic Games|accessdate=5 August 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120806182859/http://2012.itftennis.com/olympics/news/articles/london-2012-day-9-williams-sisters-make-history-with-doubles-gold.aspx|archive-date=6 August 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> [[Maria Kirilenko]] and [[Nadia Petrova]] of Russia won the bronze medal, defeating [[Liezel Huber]] and [[Lisa Raymond]], also of the United States, in the bronze medal match 4–6, 6–4, 6–1.
