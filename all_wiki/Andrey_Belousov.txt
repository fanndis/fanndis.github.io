'''Andrey  Removich Belousov''' ({{lang-ru|Андре́й Рэ́мович Белоу́сов}}; born 17 March 1959) is a Russian economist and politician, serving as [[First Deputy Prime Minister of Russia|First Deputy Prime Minister]] since 21 January 2020.<ref name=FDPM>[http://www.kremlin.ru/acts/news/62623 Подписаны указы о Первом заместителе и заместителях Председателя Правительства]. [[Kremlin.ru]]. 21 January 2020. Retrieved 1 May 2020.</ref> Previously, he was an Assistant to the [[President of Russia]] and [[Ministry of Economic Development (Russia)|Minister of Economic Development]]. 

Belousov briefly served as Acting [[Prime Minister of Russia]] from April to May 2020, while Mikhail Mishustin was on sick leave.<ref name=APM>[https://russian.rt.com/russia/news/742494-putin-belousov-ukaz?utm_source=yxnews&utm_medium=desktop&utm_referrer=https%3A%2F%2Fyandex.ru%2Fnews Путин подписал указ о назначении Белоусова и. о. главы правительства]. [[RT (TV network)|RT]]. 30 April 2020. Retrieved 1 May 2020.</ref>

==Early life and education==
Belousov was born in Moscow on 17 March 1959.<ref name=dub2012jun>{{cite news|last=Dubien|first=Arnaud|title=The composition of Russia’s new Cabinet and Presidential Administration, and its significance|url=http://www.europarl.europa.eu/committees/es/AFET/studiesdownload.html?languageDocument=EN&file=74731|accessdate=28 March 2013|newspaper=Policy Department DG External Policies|date=June 2012}}</ref><ref name=vor24may>{{cite news|title=Andrei Belousov, the head of the Ministry of Economic Development|url=http://english.ruvr.ru/2012_05_24/75878842/|accessdate=28 March 2013|newspaper=The Voice of Russia|date=24 May 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120704071541/http://english.ruvr.ru/2012_05_24/75878842/|archive-date=4 July 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> He studied economics at [[Moscow State University]] and graduated with honors in 1981.<ref name=dub2012jun/>

==Career==
From 1981 to 1986, Belousov was probationer-researcher and then junior researcher in the simulation laboratory of human-machine systems of the [[Central Economic Mathematical Institute]]. From 1991 to 2006, he was head of laboratory in the Institute of Economic Forecasting in the [[Russian Academy of Science]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ria.ru/spravka/20120521/654335328.html|title=Биография Андрея Белоусова|language=ru|trans-title=Biography of Andrey Belousov|publisher=[[RIA Novosti]]|date=21 May 2012|access-date=1 May 2020}}</ref> He was external advisor to prime minister from 2000 to 2006.<ref name=dub2012jun/> Then he served as deputy minister of economic development and trade for two years from 2006 to 2008.<ref name=dub2012jun/>

From 2008 to 2012, he was director of the finances and economic department in the [[Prime Minister of Russia|Russian Prime Minister]]'s office.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.consultant.ru/online/base/?req=doc;base=EXP;n=424823|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130416162524/http://www.consultant.ru/online/base/?req=doc;base=EXP;n=424823|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 April 2013|title=Распоряжение Правительства РФ от 17.07.2008 N 1019-р "О Белоусове А.Р."|language=ru|trans-title=Order of the Government of Russia frp, 17 July 2008 № 1019-р «On Belousov»|website=Consultant.ru|access-date=1 May 2020}}</ref>

On 21 May 2012, he was appointed minister of economic development to [[Dmitry Medvedev's First Cabinet|the cabinet]] led by prime minister [[Dimitri Medvedev]].<ref name=dub2012jun/> Belousov succeeded [[Elvira Nabiullina]] as minister of economic development.<ref>{{cite news|title=Andrei Belousov appointed Economic Development Minister |url=http://www.interfax.com/newsinf.asp?id=333482 |accessdate=28 March 2013 |newspaper=Interfax |date=21 May 2012 |location=Moscow |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140219033024/http://www.interfax.com/newsinf.asp?id=333482 |archivedate=19 February 2014 |df=dmy }}</ref>

On 24 June 2013, he was appointed as Presidential Assistant in Economic Affairs.

On 21 January 2020, Belousov was appointed as [[First Deputy Prime Minister of Russia]] in the [[Mikhail Mishustin's Cabinet]].<ref name=FDPM/> On 30 April 2020, Belousov was appointed by [[Vladimir Putin]] as Acting [[Prime Minister of Russia]], temporarily replacing Mikhail Mishustin, after the latter was diagnosed with [[Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2|coronavirus]].<ref name=APM/> On 19 May, President Putin has signed the decree about cancellation of the decree on the appointment Belousov as Acting Prime Minister.<ref>[http://www.kremlin.ru/events/president/news/63366 О признании утратившим силу Указа об исполнении обязанностей Председателя Правительства]</ref>
