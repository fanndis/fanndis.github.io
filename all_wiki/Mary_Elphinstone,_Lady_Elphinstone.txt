'''Mary Frances Buller-Fullerton-Elphinstone, Lady Elphinstone''' [[Royal Victorian Order|DCVO]] (née '''Lady Mary Frances Bowes-Lyon'''; 30 August 1883 – 8 February 1961), was a maternal aunt and godparent of [[Elizabeth II]].

==Early life==
Lady Elphinstone was born '''Lady Mary Frances Bowes-Lyon''' on 30 August 1883 in [[Angus, Scotland|Angus]], Scotland, to [[Claude Bowes-Lyon, 14th Earl of Strathmore and Kinghorne]] and [[Cecilia Bowes-Lyon, Countess of Strathmore and Kinghorne|Cecilia Cavendish-Bentinck]]. She was an elder sister of Elizabeth Bowes-Lyon known as [[Elizabeth Bowes-Lyon|Queen Elizabeth of the United Kingdom]] (later The Queen Mother).<ref name="1961NYTObit"/> 

==Personal life==
Lady Mary married [[Sidney Elphinstone, 16th Lord Elphinstone]] (1869–1955) on 24 July 1910 in [[Westminster]]. Lord Elphinstone inherited extensive estates, some of which had been in the family since the reign of King [[David II of Scotland]].  He served as a governor of the [[Bank of Scotland]], [[Lord Clerk Register|Lord Clerk Register of Scotland]] and [[Keeper of the Signet]] and Lord High Commissioner of the [[Church of Scotland]].<ref name="1955ObitNYT"/> The couple had five children:

* The Hon. Mary Elizabeth Elphinstone (1911–1980), who was a bridesmaid at the [[wedding of Prince Albert, Duke of York, and Lady Elizabeth Bowes-Lyon]] in 1923.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/picturegalleries/royalty/9176069/The-Queen-Mother-in-pictures.html?frame=2181538|title=The Queen Mother in pictures|publisher=The Daily Telegraph|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref>
* [[John Elphinstone, 17th Lord Elphinstone]] (1914–1975)
* The Hon. Jean Constance Elphinstone (1915–1999), who married Captain John Wills. Their daughter Marilyn is a god-daughter of [[Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon|Princess Margaret]] and was a bridesmaid at her 1960 wedding.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/godchildren.htm#Margaret |title=Yvonne's Royalty Home Page: Royal Godchildren<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=17 March 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120502185803/http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/godchildren.htm#Margaret |archive-date=2 May 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
* The Rev. The Hon. Andrew Charles Victor Elphinstone (1918–1975), who married Jean Hambro and had issue. His wife was a Lady-in-Waiting to [[Queen Elizabeth II]] <ref>{{Cite web |url=http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/qecoronation.htm |title=Yvonne's Royalty Home Page: Queen Elizabeth II's Coronation Attendants<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=17 March 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120502183750/http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/qecoronation.htm |archive-date=2 May 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
* [[Margaret Rhodes|The Hon. Margaret Elphinstone]] (1925–2016), who married Denys Rhodes and had issue. She was a bridesmaid at the wedding in 1947 of The Princess Elizabeth (now Queen Elizabeth II) and [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh|Prince Philip]] (now the Duke of Edinburgh).

In 1937, she attended her sister and brother-in-law's coronation whereby she sat behind her niece, [[Queen Elizabeth II|Princess Elizabeth]], who would be the future queen, in the royal box.{{fact|date=April 2020}}

Lord Elphinstone died at their home [[Carberry Tower]], [[Musselburgh]], on 28 November 1955.<ref name="1955ObitNYT">{{cite news |last1=Times |first1=Special to The New York |title=ELPHINSTONE DIES; SCOTTISH LORD, 86; Brother-in-Law of the Queen Mother, Sixteenth Holder of Title, Was Sportsman |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1955/11/29/archives/elphinstone-dies-sottish-lord-86-ibrotherinlaw-of-the-gueen-mother.html?searchResultPosition=2 |accessdate=17 April 2020 |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=29 November 1955}}</ref> Lady Elphinstone, who served as president of the Midlothian branch of the [[British Red Cross]], died on 8 February 1961, aged 77, also at Carberry Tower in [[Inveresk]], Scotland.<ref name="1961NYTObit">{{cite news |last1=Times |first1=Special to The New York |title=Lady Elphinstone |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1961/02/09/archives/lady-elphinstone.html?searchResultPosition=1 |accessdate=17 April 2020 |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=9 February 1961}}</ref>
