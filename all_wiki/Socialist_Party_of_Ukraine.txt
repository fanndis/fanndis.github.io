The '''Socialist Party of Ukraine''' ({{lang-uk|Соціалістична Партія України}}, ''Sotsialistychna Partiya Ukrainy'', '''SPU''') is a [[social-democratic]]<ref>[http://www.europeanforum.net/country/ukraine#social_democratic_parties European Forum for Democracy and Solidarity]</ref> [[Political parties in Ukraine|political party in Ukraine]]. It is one of the oldest parties and was created by the former members of the [[Soviet Union|Soviet]]-era [[Communist Party of Ukraine (Soviet Union)|Communist Party of Ukraine]] in late 1991 when the Communist Party was banned.<ref name=ZN.UAbio>{{in lang|uk}} [http://gazeta.dt.ua/POLITICS/ukrayina_partiyna_chastina_v_sotsialistichna_partiya_ukrayini.html УКРАЇНА ПАРТІЙНА. ЧАСТИНА V. СОЦІАЛІСТИЧНА ПАРТІЯ УКРАЇНИ ''SOCIALIST PARTY OF UKRAINE''], ZN.UA (7 March 2002)</ref> It was part of the [[Verkhovna Rada]] (Ukraine's parliament) from 1994 to 2007 and was long the fourth biggest party of Ukraine.<ref name=LigabioSPU/> Since 2007, the election results of the party have been extremely marginal.<ref name=LigabioSPU/> [[Oleksandr Moroz]] led the party for more than twenty years.<ref name=exitMoroz412/><ref name=flipflopTsushko/><ref name=Moroz/>

== History ==
=== Creation ===
[[File:Головний логотип Соціалістичної партії України.jpg|thumb|160px|First logo of the SPU]]
After [[Declaration of Independence of Ukraine|Ukraine gained independence on 24 August 1991]],<ref name=Magocsi>[https://books.google.com/books?id=BNUtdVrw6lIC&pg=PA722 A History of Ukraine: The Land and Its Peoples] by [[Paul Robert Magocsi]], [[University of Toronto Press]], 2010, {{ISBN|1442610212}} (page 722/723)</ref> [[Leonid Kravchuk]] as the [[Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada]] (Ukraine's parliament) signed several important documents among which was the disbandment (26 August) and later the prohibition (30 August) of [[Communist Party of Ukraine (Soviet Union)|communist parties]].<ref name=ZN.UAbio/> This led to the collapse of the communist majority faction, informally known as the "[[group of 239]]".<ref name=ZN.UAbio/><ref>{{cite book|title=Ukraine: A History|author=Subtelny, Orest|publisher=[[University of Toronto Press]]|year=2000|isbn=0-8020-8390-0|pages=[https://archive.org/details/ukrainehistory00subt_0/page/577 577]|authorlink=Orest Subtelny|url=https://archive.org/details/ukrainehistory00subt_0/page/577}}</ref> Four days after the prohibition of communist parties, [[Oleksandr Moroz]], the former leader of Group 239, called on communists to unite in a new left-wing party.<ref name=ZN.UAbio/> In September in several major cities (particularly in [[Donetsk]], [[Dnipropetrovsk]] and [[Kharkiv]]) local subsidiaries of the new party were founded.<ref name=ZN.UAbio/> The founding congress of the party was held in [[Kyiv]] on 26 October 1991 and the first leader of the party became Moroz.<ref name=ZN.UAbio/> The Socialist Party was registered at the [[Ukrainian Ministry of Justice]] on 25 November 1991 under registration number 157.<ref name=sdnet>{{in lang|uk}} [http://sd.net.ua/2009/09/04/socalstichna_partja_ukrani.html Соціалістична партія України], sd.net.ua (September 4, 2009)</ref>

On 19 June 1993, a constituent congress of the recreated [[Communist Party of Ukraine]] took place in [[Donetsk]] that proclaimed itself a direct inheritor of the Communist Party of Ukraine. After the recreation of the Communists a substantial number of the former Communist Party of Ukraine members left the Socialist Party. The Communist Party, however, finally registered in October 1993. In December 1993 the Socialists proclaimed to be in the opposition to the government of [[Leonid Kuchma]] and the President Leonid Kravchuk.<ref name=LigabioSPU>{{in lang|ru}} [http://file.liga.net/party/2944-socialisticheskaya_partiya_ykraini.html Short bio], Liga.net</ref> In the [[1994 Ukrainian presidential election|1994 presidential election]], the Socialists leader Moroz was supported by both his party and the Communist Party. The Socialist party became known for its support in the central regions of Ukraine in the 1990s and 2000s.

=== 1994 parliamentary election ===
{{main|1994 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
In the rounds of the 1994 parliamentary election, the party won 14 seats.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=fp4VUlztcMUC&pg=PA277 Atlas of Eastern Europe in the Twentieth Century] by Richard Crampton and Ben Crampton, 1997, [[Routledge]], {{ISBN|978-0-415-16461-0}}, page 277</ref> In May 1994, Moroz became [[Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada]] (speaker of parliament).<ref name=LigabioSPU/> By mid-1994, the party controlled a parliamentary faction of 25 deputies.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?client=firefox-a&spell=1&q=parliamentary+elections+results+Ukraine+1994+25&btnG=Boeken+zoeken 	 Political parties of the world] by Alan J. Day and Henry W. Degenhardt, 2002, John Harper Publishing, {{ISBN|978-0-9536278-7-5}}, Page 479</ref> In October 1995, some members headed by [[Nataliya Vitrenko]] split to form (in April 1996) the new [[Progressive Socialist Party of Ukraine]].<ref name=ZN.UAbio/>

=== 1998 parliamentary election ===
{{main|1998 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
The party stood for election in 1998 in the [[Socialist Party – Peasant Party]] electoral bloc with the [[Peasant Party of Ukraine]].<ref name=sdnet/> Attempts to form a coalition with the [[Communist Party of Ukraine|Communist Party]] had failed.<ref name=ZN.UAbio/> The block was named Left Center won 8,55% of the votes and 29 [[Proportional representation|proportional seats]] and 5 [[Electoral district|individual seats]] out of 450 seats in the [[Verkhovna Rada]].<ref name=ZN.UAbio/> The bloc gained the post [[Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada]] (speaker of parliament) with the election of [[Oleksandr Tkachenko (politician)|Oleksandr Tkachenko]] on this post.<ref name=LigabioSPU/> The Peasant Party of Ukraine started its own parliamentary faction (containing 15 deputies) in the autumn of 1998 but in the spring of 2000 this factions was disbanded for lack of member.<ref name=UUPAnie>[http://www.ditext.com/danieri/politics.html Understanding Ukrainian Politics: Power, Politics, and Institutional Design] by [[Paul D'Anieri]], [[M.E. Sharpe]], 2006, {{ISBN|978-0-7656-1811-5}}</ref><ref name=PSTRSol>[https://books.google.com/books?id=LNvTSDQXFXgC&pg=PA127 Ukraine and Russia: The Post-Soviet Transition] by Roman Solchanyk, [[Rowman & Littlefield Publishers]], 2001 {{ISBN|0742510174}}</ref> After the creation of the new parliamentary faction [[Solidarity (Ukraine)|Solidarity]] in the spring of 2000 a lot of deputies of Peasant Party moved to this new faction.<ref name=TKSolPor>[http://www.taraskuzio.net/legal_files/Frishberg_Feb.08.pdf Ukrainian Political Update] by [[Taras Kuzio]] and Alex Frishberg, Frishberg & Partners, 21 February 2008 (page 22)</ref> In June 2002, the Left Center faction had 17 members.<ref name=UUPAnie/>

After the election, a group of former SPU members led by Ivan Chizh who were in opposition to Moroz founded the [[Justice Party (Ukraine)|Justice Party]] in 2000.<ref name=ZN.UAbio/><ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://da-ta.com.ua/mon_mainnews/875.htm Партія „Справедливість“], Database DATA</ref>

=== 2002 parliamentary election ===
{{main|2002 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
The party was heavily involved in the [[Ukraine without Kuchma]] campaign.<ref name=LigabioSPU/>

At the [[2002 Ukrainian parliamentary election|parliamentary election]] on 30 March 2002, the party won 6.9% of the popular vote, and 24 out of 450 seats in the [[Verkhovna Rada]]. The party had limited access to media in the campaign.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/1898223.stm Ukraine's election frontrunners], [[BBC News]] (28 March 2002)</ref> The youth wing of the party had left it and had endorsed [[Social Democratic Party of Ukraine (united)]] in the election.<ref name=LigabioSPU/>

In late 2002, Moroz, [[Viktor Yushchenko]] ([[Our Ukraine (political party)|Our Ukraine]]), [[Petro Symonenko]] ([[Communist Party of Ukraine]]) and [[Yulia Tymoshenko]] ([[Yulia Tymoshenko Bloc]]) issued a joint statement concerning "the beginning of a state [[revolution]] in Ukraine". The communists stepped out of the alliance, Symonenko was against a single candidate from the alliance in the [[2004 Ukrainian presidential election|Ukrainian presidential election 2004]], but the other three party's remained allies<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=Wp7VKL4p7kQC&pg=PA117&dq=Tymoshenko+Moroz+Presidential+candidate+2004&client=firefox-a#v=onepage&q=Tymoshenko%20Moroz%20Presidential%20candidate%202004&f=false Understanding Ukrainian Politics: Power, Politics, and Institutional Design] by [[Paul D'Anieri]], [[M.E. Sharpe]], 2006, {{ISBN|978-0-7656-1811-5}}, page 117</ref> (until July 2006).<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/5169068.stm Ukraine coalition born in chaos], [[BBC News]] (July 11, 2006)</ref> The Socialists were active participants in the [[Orange Revolution]].<ref name=LigabioSPU/> The party was a coalition member in the [[First Tymoshenko Government]] and the [[Yekhanurov Government]].<ref name=LigabioSPU/>

In 2005, the party was joined by the Ukrainian Party of Justice - Union of veterans, handicapped, Chornobyl liquidators, and Afghan warriors (former Ukrainian Party of Justice).

=== 2006 parliamentary election ===
{{main|2006 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
[[File:Ukrainian parliamentary election 2006 (SPU)v.PNG|thumb|240px|A map showing the results of the SPU (percentage of total national vote) per region for the 2006 parliamentary elections]] 
The Socialist Party received 5.67% of the national vote during the [[2006 Ukrainian parliamentary election|parliamentary election]] held on 26 March 2006, securing 33 seats in Parliament.<ref name=LigabioSPU/> 
 
The Socialist Party of Ukraine was expected to form a governing coalition with [[Bloc Yulia Tymoshenko|Yulia Tymoshenko]] and [[Our Ukraine bloc|Our Ukraine]].<ref name=LigabioSPU/> However, after 3 months of negotiation agreement could not be finalized with Our Ukraine challenging Moroz's appointment as [[Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada]] (chairman of parliament).<ref name=LigabioSPU/> The Socialist Party then agreed to the formation of an "Anti Crisis" coalition with [[Party of Regions]] and the [[Communist Party of Ukraine|Communist Party]] following the election of [[Oleksandr Moroz]] as Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada in July 2006.<ref name=LigabioSPU/> The newly formed governing coalition elected [[Viktor Yanukovych]] as [[Prime minister of Ukraine]] and was later renamed the [[Alliance of National Unity]].<ref name=LigabioSPU/> Several high up members left the party because of it becoming a partner in the new coalition, influential former member [[Yuriy Lutsenko]] created [[Yuriy Lutsenko's People's Self-Defense|People's Self-Defense]].<ref name=LigabioSPU/> [[President of Ukraine]] [[Viktor Yushchenko|Yushchenko]] dissolved parliament on 2 April 2007 because he believed the government was acting illegally  during the [[2007 Ukrainian political crisis]].<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/7015947.stm Q&A: Ukrainian parliamentary poll ], [[BBC News]] (1 October 2007)</ref>{{#tag:ref|In a press conference in November 2009, Moroz stated he had no regrets about joining the Anti Crisis coalition, saying: "I'm not ashamed but proud of the fact that I managed to halt the crisis of power. The economy operated normal and, the parliament adopted 80% of the laws [it considered] by a constitutional majority of votes. We were close to the decentralization of power. That's why [[Yulia Tymoshenko|Tymoshenko]] and Yushchenko's supporters forced the president to dismiss the parliament and remove me and my political forces illegally".<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/53958/ Moroz says he was responsible for formation of anti-crisis coalition with Regions Party and Communist Party], [[Kyiv Post]] (November 30, 2009)</ref>|group=nb}}

=== 2007 parliamentary election ===
{{main|2007 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
[[File:Ukrainian parliamentary election 2007 (SPU)v.PNG|thumb|240px|A map showing the results of the SPU (percentage of total national vote) per region for the 2007 parliamentary elections]]
In the [[2007 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2007 parliamentary election]], the party's vote share collapsed.<ref name=LigabioSPU/> The Socialist Party of Ukraine failed to secure parliamentary representation, having received 2.86% of the total national vote (0.14% short of the required minimum 3% representation threshold). This led to more high-ranking members leaving the party and the creation of the offspring [[Union of Leftists]].<ref name=LigabioSPU/>

After having led the party for 20 years, Oleksander Moroz in July 2010 was succeeded by [[Vasyl Tsushko]].<ref name=flipflopTsushko>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/75359/ Economy minister appointed Socialist Party head], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (July 26, 2010)</ref> However, Moroz was again elected as party leader in August 2011.<ref name=Moroz>[http://www.nrcu.gov.ua/index.php?id=148&listid=150540 Oleksandr Moroz elected Chairman of Socialist Party of Ukraine] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110817060147/http://www.nrcu.gov.ua/index.php?id=148&listid=150540 |date=2011-08-17 }}, National Radio Company of Ukraine (August 15, 2011)</ref>

=== 2012 parliamentary election ===
{{main|2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
A March 2010 poll predicted that the party would get 0.2% of the vote at the [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election]].<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/63737/ Party Of Regions, Tymoshenko bloc, Strong Ukraine, Front for Change and Communist Party would get into parliament], [[Kyiv Post]] (April 12, 2010)</ref> In the [[2010 Ukrainian local elections|2010 local elections]], the parties electoral misfortunes continued, winning few votes and securing little to no representatives in regional parliaments across Ukraine (winning representatives in 11 [[Ukrainian Oblasts]] parliaments in total), except in the [[Chernihiv Oblast]] and [[Poltava Oblast]] where they won 11% and 5,8% of the votes.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/articles/2010/11/8/5552584/ Results of the elections, preliminary data, on interactive maps] by [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (8 November 2010)</ref>

In July 2011, the party was expelled from the [[Socialist International]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2011/07/3/6352402 Партію Мороза виключили з Соцінтерну], [[Ukrayinska 2012)</ref> In December 2011, the [[Peasant Party of Ukraine]] and 4 other small parties merged with the party (Socialist Ukraine, Children of War, Children of War of the People's Party of Ukraine, Cossack Glory).<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2011/12/18/6847147/ Партія Мороза "проковтнула" п'ять партій], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (18 December  2011)</ref> Plans to merge 11 parties including the Socialist Party of Ukraine into [[United Left and Peasants]] were stopped by the parties council.<ref name=gazeta.ua>{{in lang|uk}} [http://gazeta.ua/articles/politics-newspaper/_socpartiji-ne-spodobalasya-nazva-ob-ednani-livi-i-selyani/415031 Соцпартії не сподобалася назва "Об'єднані ліві і селяни"], Gazeta.ua (16 December  2011)</ref> On 28 January 2012, the merger with the Peasant Party of Ukraine was declared illegal by the [[Ministry of Justice (Ukraine)|Justice Ministry]].<ref>[http://www.ukrainebusiness.com.ua/news/4494.html Ukraine Business Online]</ref> In the election the party won 0.46% of the national votes and no [[constituencies]] (it had competed in 58 constituencies)<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://apk.rbc.ua/ukr/vyboru2012/party/p08/okrug Candidates], [[RBC Ukraine]]</ref> and thus failed to win parliamentary representation.<ref Constituency seats] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121105073259/http://www.cvk.gov.ua/vnd2012/wp039pt001f01=900.html |date=2012-11-05 }}, [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref>

=== 2014 parliamentary elections followed by a leadership crisis ===
{{main|2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election}}
[[File:СПУ (логотип).png|thumb|Party logo in 2015]]
The party did not participate in the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election]].<ref>[http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2014/wp501e?PT001F01=910 Alphabetical Index of parties in 2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election], [[Central Election Commission (Ukraine)|Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref>

In 2017 [[Serhiy Kaplin]], at the time a member of the [[Verkhovna Rada|Ukrainian parliamentary]] faction of [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc]], claimed to be the party's chairman.<ref name="interfax.com.ua">http://interfax.com.ua/news/general/452084.html</ref> Kaplin intended to take the party to elections with [[Party of Pensioners of Ukraine]] under the label "For ordinary people".<ref name="interfax.com.ua"/> But {{illm|Illia Kyva|uk|Кива Ілля Володимирович|ru|Кива, Илья Владимирович}} also claimed to headed the Socialist Party of Ukraine.<ref name=UP7197183SP>{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2018/11/3/7197183/ The Socialists held a congress and sent Kivu to the presidency] by [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (3 November 2018)</ref> In January 2018, during a "joint meeting of the political council and the central control commission of the Socialist Party of Ukraine" Kyva was  expelled from the party.<ref name=UP7197183SP/> Kyva stated this exclusion was  illegitimate.<ref name=UP7197183SP/> According to the official registration of the party Illia Kyva is the chairman of the Socialist Party.<ref name=UP7197183SP/><ref>https://www.unian.ua/m/politics/2370072-ediniy-legitimniy-kiva-prokomentuvav-povidomlennya-pro-svoe-viklyuchennya-iz-sotspartiji.html</ref> Kyva left the party in June 2019 to join [[Opposition Platform — For life]].<ref>https://m.glavcom.ua/country/politics/kiva-rozpoviv-chomu-vin-pishov-v-partiyu-medvedchuka-600129.html</ref>

In the [[2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] the party had 2 candidates in constituencies, but both did not win a parliamentary seat.<ref name="URK.VOTEPP140">[https://ukr.vote/en/parties/140 Socialist Party of Ukraine], [[URK.VOTE]]</ref>

== Socialist Party in presidential elections ==
The party's candidate for the [[1999 Ukrainian presidential election|1999 presidential election]], [[Oleksander Moroz]], came third, with 11.3% of the vote in the first round. [[Oleksander Moroz]] also participated in the [[2004 Ukrainian presidential election|2004 presidential election]]'s first-round ballot where he again came in third place, receiving 5.82% of the vote, and subsequently endorsed [[Viktor Yuschenko]] in the final run-off ballots.

=== 2010 Ukrainian presidential election ===
{{main|2010 Ukrainian presidential election}}
The Socialist Party chose [[Oleksandr Moroz]] as their presidential candidate for the next [[2010 Ukrainian presidential election|presidential election]], scheduled to be held on 17 January 2010. 268 out of 422 party congress delegates registered supported the Moroz's nomination.<ref name="Moroz_nomination">{{cite web|url=http://kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/51285/|title=Socialist Party nominates Moroz for president|date=2009-10-25}}</ref>

==== Opinion polls ====
Public opinion polls have not rated the Socialist Party of Ukraine or its leader Olexandr Moroz as they were undecided as to their participation in the [[2010 Ukrainian presidential election|Ukrainian presidential election]]. In 2005, Moroz received 5.8% of the national vote. An opinion poll conducted by [[FOM-Ukraine]] in April 2009 showed Moroz with less than 1% support, with most analysts considering Moroz not a serious contender as he would not win sufficient votes in the first-round presidential ballot, scheduled for 17 January 2010.
