'''Julia Görges''' (born 2 November 1988) is a German former professional [[tennis]] player. A former top-ten singles player, she was ranked as high as No. 9 in the world on 20 August 2018, and was ranked inside the top 15 in doubles, peaking at world No. 12 on 22 August 2016. She won seven singles and five doubles titles on the [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA Tour]] (her biggest title coming at the year-end [[2017 WTA Elite Trophy]]), as well as six singles and six doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit|ITF Circuit]].

Görges turned professional in 2005, and first broke into the world's top 100 in June 2008. Prior to 2018, her best result in a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles tournament was reaching the fourth round five times. She broke into the top ten for the first time in February 2018, before going on to reach the semifinals at the [[2018 Wimbledon Championships]]. She was also a two-time semifinalist in women's doubles at the Australian Open, and reached the finals in mixed doubles with [[Nenad Zimonjić]] at the [[2014 French Open – Mixed Doubles|2014 French Open]].

She announced her retirement from professional tennis on 21 October 2020, two weeks before her 32nd birthday.

==Personal life==
Julia Görges was born in [[Bad Oldesloe]] to Klaus and Inge Görges, both of whom work in insurance.<ref name="wtatour">{{cite web |url=http://www.wtatour.com/page/GettingToKnow/Read/0,,12781~1833262,00.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120805211652/http://www.wtatour.com/page/GettingToKnow/Read/0,,12781~1833262,00.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-08-05 |title="Getting to Know...Julia Goerges" |website=wtatour.com }}</ref> She has one elder maternal half-sister named Maike, who also works in insurance.<ref name="wtatour"/> She attended the Klaus-Groth-Schule and Theodor-Mommsen-Schule in [[Bad Oldesloe]] from 1995 to 2005, and completed the [[Mittlere Reife]] (middle-school diploma).<ref>{{cite web|title=Julia Görges: "Habe mich so gefreut" Ich habe mir meine Schultüte selbst ausgesucht", erinnert sich die Tennisspielerin Julia Görges an ihre Einschulung 1995 in die Klaus-Groth-Schule...|work=Hamburger Abendblatt|date=4 September 2008|url=http://www.abendblatt.de/region/stormarn/article559991/Julia-Goerges-Habe-mich-so-gefreut.html|accessdate=8 February 2011|language=de}}</ref><ref name=Wahlstedt>{{cite web|last=Dietrich|first=Janina|title=Interview mit Julia Görges|work=Tennis Wahlstedt Aktuell|date=January 2008|url=http://www.tennis-wahlstedt.de/html/img/pool/413_1–2008.pdf|format=PDF|accessdate=8 February 2011|language=de}}{{Dead link|date=April 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

She began playing tennis around the age of five.<ref name="wtatour"/><ref name=Wahlstedt/> Her tennis idol growing up was [[Martina Hingis]], and she is also a fan of [[Roger Federer]].<ref name="wtatour"/> She was coached by [[Sascha Nensel]], former coach of fellow German player [[Nicolas Kiefer]],<ref name="wtatour"/> until 2015. She preferred hard and grass courts, and her favorite tournament was the [[Australian Open]].<ref name="wtatour"/>

==Career==
===2005–2008===
In 2005, she began her career on the ITF Women's Circuit, playing in seven tournaments and losing in the first round in five of them. In 2006 and 2007 she continued to play mostly [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] tournaments. In 2006, she won the [[Wahlstedt]] and [[Bielefeld]] tournaments. In 2007, she won tournaments in [[Antalya]] and [[Bucharest]] and made her first [[WTA Tour]] main draw appearances, the highlight of which was a semifinal in [[2007 Nordea Nordic Light Open|Stockholm]], where she lost to [[Vera Dushevina]] in three sets. Görges made her [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] main draw debut at the [[2007 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], losing to [[Justine Henin]] in the first round in straight sets. In 2008 Görges continued to play in a mix of ITF and WTA events. Her best performances were reaching the semifinals of the [[2008 Banka Koper Slovenia Open|Slovenia Open]], where she lost to [[Anabel Medina Garrigues]], and a quarterfinal-loss to [[Olga Govortsova]] in the [[2008 Cellular South Cup|Cellular South Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Julia Goerges Stats 2008|work=[[ESPN]]|accessdate=7 May 2011|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/sports/tennis/players/profile?playerId=675&year=2008}}</ref>

She made her top-100 debut in the rankings after the [[2008 French Open|French Open]]. She won her first Grand Slam main-draw match at [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]], where she upset the 23rd seed [[Katarina Srebotnik]] in a three-hour, 41-minute first-round match.<ref name="wtatour"/> However, she bowed out in the second round to [[Marina Erakovic]] in straight sets.

===2009: Consistent top-100 ranking===
In 2009, Görges began to play in WTA Tour events more regularly. It was the first season that she played in the main draw of all four Grand Slam tournaments. She began her season at the [[2009 Brisbane International|Brisbane International]], where she lost in qualifying to [[Anna-Lena Grönefeld]].<ref name=nine>{{cite news|title=Julia Goerges Stats 2009|work=[[ESPN]]|accessdate=7 May 2011|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/sports/tennis/players/profile?playerId=675&year=2009}}</ref>

Görges competed at the [[2009 Australian Open|Australian Open]], the [[2009 Open GDF Suez|Open GdF Suez]], and the [[2009 Warsaw Open|Warsaw Open]], losing before the third round in each. She retired in the first round of the [[2009 French Open|French Open]] in a match against [[Iveta Benešová]] due to heat exhaustion.<ref name=nine/>

Görges reached the third round of the [[2009 Aegon Classic|Aegon Classic]], falling to [[Urszula Radwańska]]. She went on to play against [[Jelena Janković]] in the first round of [[2009 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], losing in straight sets. She lost prior to the third round at the [[2009 Banka Koper Slovenia Open|Slovenia Open]], the [[2009 İstanbul Cup|İstanbul Cup]], and the [[2009 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], where she faced [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]].<ref name=nine/>

At the [[2009 Bell Challenge|Bell Challenge]], she managed to reach the semifinals, before losing to [[Lucie Šafářová]]. She was defeated by [[Raluca Olaru]] in the second round of the [[2009 Generali Ladies Linz|Generali Ladies Linz]].<ref name=nine/>

===2010: First WTA title===
Görges started the 2010 season at the [[2010 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]], losing in the first round to [[Yanina Wickmayer]]. She went on to play at the [[2010 Australian Open|Australian Open]], where she beat [[Tamira Paszek]], but then lost to [[Caroline Wozniacki]] in the second round.<ref name=ten>{{cite news|url=http://www.tennismagazine.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Julia+Goerges|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111009002458/http://www.tennismagazine.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Julia+Goerges|url-status=dead|archive-date=9 October 2011|title=Julia Goerges Stats 2010|work=TennisMagazine.com|accessdate=7 May 2011}}</ref>

[[File:Julia Goerges San Diego.jpg|thumb|left|Görges practicing in San Diego in 2011]]
Görges reached the quarterfinals of [[2010 Internationaux de Strasbourg|Strasbourg]], losing to [[Maria Sharapova]]. Following a second round loss at the [[2010 French Open|French Open]] and a first round loss at [[2010 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], she reached the semifinals of [[2010 Internazionali Femminili di Palermo|Palermo]], losing to [[Flavia Pennetta]].<ref name=ten/>

At the [[2010 Gastein Ladies|Gastein Ladies]], Görges won her first career WTA singles title by defeating [[Timea Bacsinszky]] in the final in straight sets. She made her top-50 debut in the rankings afterward. At the [[2010 e-Boks Danish Open|Danish Open]], she reached the quarterfinals, but was unable to take advantage of a 5–3 third-set lead over the top seed Wozniacki, eventually falling in a tiebreak.<ref name=ten/>

Görges defeated [[Romina Oprandi]] in the first round of the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], before falling to the 15th seed Wickmayer in the second round. At the Japan Open, she scored her first win over a former No. 1 player by defeating [[Dinara Safina]] in the first round. In the second, she defeated the fourth seed [[Samantha Stosur]] for her first win over a current top-10 player, before losing to [[CoCo Vandeweghe]] in the quarterfinals.<ref name=ten/>

At the [[2010 Generali Ladies Linz|Generali Ladies Linz]], she reached the quarterfinals, but fell to the eventual champion [[Ana Ivanovic]]. In her final tournament of the season, the [[2010 BGL Luxembourg Open|Luxembourg Open]], she won a quarterfinal rematch with Ivanovic, defeating her in straight sets. She eventually reached her second WTA career final, losing to [[Roberta Vinci]].<ref name=ten/>

===2011: Second career title, breakthrough into top 25===
Beginning her season at the [[2011 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]], Görges lost in the semifinals to the eventual champion [[Gréta Arn]]. In the second round of the [[2011 Australian Open|Australian Open]], she upset the No. 20 seed [[Kaia Kanepi]]. Her third round match against the [[2008 Australian Open|2008]] champion Maria Sharapova was a three-set battle, which Görges eventually lost.<ref name=eleven>{{cite news|url=http://www.tennismagazine.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Julia+Goerges|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111009002458/http://www.tennismagazine.com/players/player_stats.aspx?player_name=Julia+Goerges|url-status=dead|archive-date=9 October 2011|title=Julia Goerges Stats 2011|work=TennisMagazine.com|accessdate=7 May 2011}}</ref> This marked her best performance in a Grand Slam to date, and she was rewarded by achieving a career-high No. 34 singles ranking on 31 January 2011.

[[File:Julia Görges at the Aegon Championships in 2011.jpg|thumb|right|Görges at the [[2011 Aegon International]]]]
In February, she helped [[Germany Fed Cup team|Germany]] defeat [[Slovenia Fed Cup team|Slovenia]] in the [[Fed Cup]] competition by clinching the tie with a straight-sets win over [[Maša Zec Peškirič]], her first win in the Fed Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fedcup.com/en/news/articles/germany-overpowers-slovenia-in-maribor.aspx|title=Germany overpowers Slovenia in Maribor|first=Gorazd|last=Jukovic|work=[[Fed Cup]]|date=6 February 2011|accessdate=20 January 2014}}</ref> Switching to the hard court, she then lost in the first round of [[2011 Monterrey Open|Monterrey]] to [[Ksenia Pervak]], and then continued to struggle at [[2011 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]] and [[2011 Sony Ericsson Open|Miami]], losing in the second and first rounds respectively. At the [[2011 Family Circle Cup|Family Circle Cup]] in Charleston, however, she made a quarterfinal appearance on the green clay, losing to [[Elena Vesnina]] in three sets.<ref name=eleven/>

In the Fed Cup in Stuttgart, Görges won a rubber for Germany against [[Melanie Oudin]], who had defeated her in Miami. Staying in Stuttgart for the [[2011 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix – Singles|Porsche Tennis Grand Prix]], she won her first Premier-level tournament and second WTA tournament, upsetting Samantha Stosur and benefitting from a retirement by [[Victoria Azarenka]]. In the final, Görges scored the biggest win of her career by defeating world No. 1 Caroline Wozniacki in two sets, to become the first German to win the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix since [[Anke Huber]] in 1994.<ref name=eleven/>

Less than two weeks after winning in Stuttgart, Görges defeated Wozniacki once again at the [[Madrid Open (tennis)|Madrid Open]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/sports/tennis/news/story?id=6484023|title=Julia Goerges stuns Caroline Wozniacki|work=[[ESPN]]|date=5 May 2011|accessdate=7 May 2011}}</ref> She reached the semifinals, where she lost to Azarenka.

Görges was the 17th seed at the [[2011 French Open|French Open]], but fell to the 11th seed [[Marion Bartoli]] in three sets in the third round.

After a first round exit to [[Ana Ivanovic]] at the [[2011 Aegon International|Aegon International]], she reached the third round of [[2011 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] where she fell to the 24th seed [[Dominika Cibulková]] in three sets.

Görges failed to advance past the second round at [[2011 Gastein Ladies|Bad Gastein]], [[2011 Bank of the West Classic|Stanford]], [[2011 Mercury Insurance Open|Carlsbad]], [[2011 Rogers Cup|Toronto]], [[2011 Western & Southern Open|Cincinnati]] or [[2011 Texas Tennis Open|Dallas]]. As the 24th seed at the [[2011 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], she fell to the 13th seed [[Peng Shuai]] in two close sets in the third round.

Taking part in the Asian part of the WTA tour, she fell in the quarterfinals of the [[2011 Hansol Korea Open|Korea Open]], the third round of the [[2011 Toray Pan Pacific Open|Pan Pacific Open]], and the first round of the [[2011 China Open (tennis)|China Open]]. She finished her 2011 season with a semifinal loss to Azarenka at the [[2011 BGL Luxembourg Open|BGL Luxembourg Open]]. She finished the year ranked 21st, accumulating a 38–25 singles record and a 22–18 doubles record.

===2012: Top-20 ranking, consistent form===
Seeded fifth at the [[2012 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]], she defeated the defending champion Gréta Arn, before falling to her compatriot [[Angelique Kerber]] in straight sets. Görges experienced more success in doubles, reaching the final with Flavia Pennetta before falling to the Czech duo of [[Andrea Hlaváčková]] and [[Lucie Hradecká]] in a third-set super tiebreak. After retiring in the first round of the [[2012 Apia International Sydney|Apia International Sydney]] against Jelena Janković, she achieved her best Australian Open performance to date by reaching the fourth round. Görges defeated [[Polona Hercog]], [[Eleni Daniilidou]], and [[Romina Oprandi]], before being dominated by the eighth seed [[Agnieszka Radwańska]]. Playing in the Fed Cup for Germany, she lost to [[Petra Kvitová]] in an extremely tight three-setter. Partnering Anna-Lena Grönefeld, she lost to [[Iveta Benešová]] and [[Barbora Záhlavová-Strýcová]] as the Czech Republic won the tie 4–1.

Ranked 21st and seeded sixth, she reached the quarterfinals of the [[2012 Open GDF Suez|Open GdF Suez]], falling to [[Klára Zakopalová]] in three sets. Görges then lost in the second round in [[2012 Qatar Total Open|Qatar]] to [[Varvara Lepchenko]], before reaching the final in [[2012 Dubai Tennis Championships|Dubai]]. She defeated Svetlana Kuznetsova, [[Casey Dellacqua]] and [[Daniela Hantuchová]], scored a two-set win over the third seed Caroline Wozniacki, but then fell to Agnieszka Radwańska again. Her good result at Dubai helped her to achieve a career-high ranking of 15.

Seeded 14th at the [[2012 BNP Paribas Open|BNP Paribas Open]], she advanced to the fourth round in straight sets, before becoming another victim of Azarenka. After receiving another bye into the second round in [[2012 Sony Ericsson Open|Miami]], Görges was defeated by the four-time Grand Slam champion [[Kim Clijsters]].

At [[2012 French Open – Women's Singles|Roland Garros]], Görges was seeded 25th. She reached the third round, beating Lucie Hradecká and [[Heather Watson]] along the way. She lost in the third round to [[Arantxa Rus]], ending the match with two double faults. At the end of the third set Görges complained about the light, which was dismissed by the head supervisor. After this she asked for a medical time-out and asked again to suspend the match, which again was denied by the umpire. In the women's doubles, she lost in the first round with her partner Samantha Stosur.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rolandgarros.com/en_FR/news/articles/2012-06-02/201206021338626339192.html|title=News & Photos / Articles / Saturday 2 June: As it happened|first=Drew|last=Lilley|work=RolandGarros.com|date=2 June 2012|accessdate=14 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sportsmole.co.uk/tennis/french-open/live-commentary/live-commentary-french-open--day-seven-as-it-happened_29429.html|title=Live Commentary: French Open – Day seven as it happened|first=Matt|last=Law|work=sportsmole.co.uk|date=2 June 2012|accessdate=14 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/18306846|title=French Open day seven|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=3 June 2012|accessdate=14 March 2013}}</ref>

She was the No. 1 seed in [[2012 Gastein Ladies|Bad Gastein]] but lost in three sets to the Dutch qualifier [[Richèl Hogenkamp]], ranked 211, who won her first WTA match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/2809888/title/qualifier-stuns-goerges-in-main-draw-debut|title=Qualifier Stuns Goerges In Main Draw Debut|work=[[Women's Tennis Association]]|date=12 June 2012|accessdate=14 March 2013}}</ref> At the same tournament, she won the doubles competition, partnering the American [[Jill Craybas]]. In the final they defeated Grönefeld and [[Petra Martić]].

At [[2012 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] she lost in the third round to Ana Ivanovic, after defeating [[Shahar Pe'er]] and [[Anastasiya Yakimova]] in the first two rounds. In [[2012 Internazionali Femminili di Palermo|Palermo]], Görges was defeated in the quarterfinals by Záhlavová-Strýcová. In the second round of the [[2012 Swedish Open|Swedish Open]], she lost to the eventual champion Hercog in straight sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/20120718/hercog-bounces-goerges-from-bastad_2256076_2853180|title=Hercog Bounces Goerges From Bastad|accessdate=22 July 2012}}</ref>

At the [[Tennis at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|London Olympics]], Görges surprisingly defeated second seed Agnieszka Radwańska in the first round. She also beat Lepchenko in round two, but in the third round lost to [[Maria Kirilenko]] in straight sets. In the women's doubles, she teamed with Grönefeld, and reached the second round.<ref>{{cite Sports-Reference |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/go/julia-gorges-1.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200417221625/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/go/julia-gorges-1.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2020-04-17|title=Julia Görges|access-date=2017-09-27}}</ref>

In August she lost in the first round at [[2012 Rogers Cup|Montreal]] and reached the second round in [[2012 Western & Southern Open|Cincinnati]]. At the [[2012 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] she lost in the first round to [[Kristýna Plíšková]]. In September she lost in the first round in [[2012 Hansol Korea Open|Seoul]]. In [[2012 Toray Pan Pacific Open|Tokyo]] she defeated [[Monica Niculescu]], but lost to Marion Bartoli in the second round.

At the [[2012 China Open (tennis)|China Open]] in Beijing, she defeated [[Vania King]] and eighth seed Stosur. In the third round, she again lost to the ninth seed Bartoli. She then played a fairly successful tournament in [[2012 Generali Ladies Linz|Linz]], reaching the finals of both singles and doubles. In the singles final she lost to Azarenka, the world No. 1. In doubles she played alongside Záhlavová-Strýcová and they were defeated by another German-Czech pair, Grönefeld and [[Květa Peschke]]. In [[2012 BGL Luxembourg Open|Luxembourg]], Görges reached the second round, where she lost to Niculescu, who went on to be defeated in the final by [[Venus Williams]]. She ended 2012 ranked as the world No. 18.

===2013: Loss of form===
[[File:Goerges WM13-008 (9485967921).jpg|thumb|Görges at the [[2013 Wimbledon Championships]]]]
Görges began her season at the [[2013 ASB Classic – Singles|ASB Classic]]. Seeded second, she lost in the second round to Johanna Larsson.<ref>{{cite news |title=Agnieszka Radwanska reaches quarterfinals in Auckland |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2013/01/02/asb-classic-auckland-agnieszka-radwanksa-advances/1803641/ |accessdate=14 April 2020 |date=2 January 2013}}</ref> In doubles, she and her partner, [[Yaroslava Shvedova]], reached the final where they lost to [[Cara Black]]/[[Anastasia Rodionova]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gudris |first1=Erik |title=Radwanska Claims Auckland Title Over Wickmayer |url=http://www.tennisnow.com/News/Radwanska-Claims-Auckland-Title-Over-Wickmayer.aspx |accessdate=14 April 2020 |publisher=www.tennisnow.com |date=5 January 2013}}</ref> In Sydney at the [[2013 Apia International Sydney – Women's Singles|Apia International Sydney]], Görges was defeated in the first round by qualifier Svetlana Kuznetsova. Seeded eighteenth at the [[2013 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], Görges advanced to the fourth round after wins over Vera Dushevina, Romina Oprandi, and Jie Zheng. She lost in her fourth-round match to sixth seed and eventual finalist [[Li Na]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gudris |first1=Erik |title=Sharapova Achieves Record as Li, Radwanska Advance at AO |url=http://www.tennisnow.com/News/Headlines/Sharapova-Earns-Record-as-Li,-Radwanska-Advance-at.aspx |accessdate=14 April 2020 |publisher=www.tennisnow.com |date=20 January 2013}}</ref>

Seeded seventh at the [[2013 Open GDF Suez – Singles|Open GdF Suez]], Görges was stunned in the first round by French wildcard [[Kristina Mladenovic]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Open GDF Suez: Julia Goerges upset by Kristina Mladenovic |url=https://www.si.com/tennis/2013/01/29/open-gdf-suez-paris |accessdate=14 April 2020 |date=29 January 2013}}</ref> During the [[2013 Fed Cup World Group II|Fed Cup]] tie against France, Görges won both her matches defeating Kristina Mladenovic and Pauline Parmentier. Germany won 3-1 over France.<ref>{{cite news |title=Goerges defeats Parmentier as Germany beats France 3-1 in Fed Cup |url=https://www.timescolonist.com/sports/tennis/goerges-defeats-parmentier-as-germany-beats-france-3-1-in-fed-cup-1.70942 |accessdate=14 April 2020 |date=10 February 2013}}</ref> At the [[2013 Dubai Tennis Championships – Women's Singles|Dubai Tennis Championships]], Görges lost in the first round to fifth seed and eventual finalist Sara Errani.<ref>{{cite news |title=Errani, Kvitova advance; Azarenka pulls out of Dubai |url=https://www.wtsp.com/article/sports/tennis/errani-kvitova-advance-azarenka-pulls-out-of-dubai/67-326756444 |accessdate=14 April 2020 |date=19 February 2013}}</ref> In March, Görges played at the [[2013 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|BNP Paribas Open]]. As the twenty-first seed, she received a first-round bye. She defeated Sofia Arvidsson in the second round. She lost her third-round match to tenth seed [[Nadia Petrova]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Coach's words help Kerber fight back at Indian Wells |url=https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/sports/2013-03/12/content_16300857.htm |accessdate=14 April 2020 |date=12 March 2013}}</ref> At the [[2013 Sony Open Tennis – Women's Singles|Sony Open Tennis]] in Miami, Görges was the twenty-fourth seed, and she received a first-round bye. She was defeated in the second round by [[Ajla Tomljanović]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Serena, Radwanska cruise in Miami openers |url=https://www.foxnews.com/sports/serena-radwanska-cruise-in-miami-openers |accessdate=14 April 2020 |date=21 March 2013}}</ref>

Görges began her clay-court season at the [[2013 Family Circle Cup – Singles|Family Circle Cup]]. Seeded tenth, she reached the third round after wins over [[Yulia Putintseva]] and Olga Govortsova. In [[2013 Brussels Open|Brussels]] she reached the second round after defeating CoCo Vandeweghe, but retired the match against Romina Oprandi due to a right wrist injury.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://sport.uk.msn.com/tennis/wozniacki-crashes-out |title=Archived copy |access-date=30 December 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131230234936/http://sport.uk.msn.com/tennis/wozniacki-crashes-out |archive-date=30 December 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

At [[2013 French Open|Roland Garros]] she surprisingly lost in the first round to [[Zuzana Kučová]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Kučová senzačne zdolala Görgesovú, no rozhodnutie nemení|url=http://sport.sme.sk/c/6815527/kucova-senzacne-zdolala-gorgesovu-no-rozhodnutie-nemeni-konci-karieru.html|accessdate=18 August 2013|newspaper=SME|language=sk}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Kucova makes some noise in her last hurra|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2013/05/27/zuzana-kucova-beats-julia-goerges-french-open/2363453/ | work=USA Today | first1=Douglas|last1=Robson|date=27 May 2013}}</ref> She reached the second round in [[2013 Nürnberger Versicherungscup|Nuremberg]], losing to [[Andrea Petkovic]]. She was more successful in doubles during this part of season. Alongside [[Darija Jurak]], she reached the final in Stanford, where they lost to the American pair [[Raquel Kops-Jones]] and [[Abigail Spears]]. In Cincinnati she paired with Barbora Záhlavová-Strýcová and they lost in the semifinals to the other Czech-German pairing of Grönefeld and Peschke. In [[2013 New Haven Open at Yale|New Haven]], Görges won her first-round match against Bojana Jovanovski, but lost in the second round to the sixth seed [[Sloane Stephens]]. Görges then participated in [[2013 KDB Korea Open|Seoul]] where she was the sixth seed. She defeated [[Misaki Doi]] before losing to [[Irina-Camelia Begu]] in the second round. She lost her openers in her final three tournaments of 2013; against [[Sorana Cîrstea]] in [[2013 Toray Pan Pacific Open|Tokyo]], against Kaia Kanepi in [[2013 China Open (tennis)|Beijing]] and against the qualifier [[Camila Giorgi]] in [[2013 Generali Ladies Linz|Linz]]. She ended the year at No. 73.

===2014: Out of top 100, downfall===
To start the year, Görges played the [[2014 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]], making it to the second round before losing to [[Sachie Ishizu]] in three sets. In [[2014 Apia International Sydney|Sydney]], Görges retired in the final round of qualifying but was awarded a lucky loser spot in the main draw, where she lost to Caroline Wozniacki. At the [[2014 Australian Open|Australian Open]], Görges upset seventh seed Sara Errani in the first round, but lost to [[Lauren Davis]] in round two. In [[2014 PTT Pattaya Open|Pattaya]], Görges reached the semifinals, losing to [[Karolína Plíšková]]. Having fallen out of the top 100 for one week, her success in Pattaya moved her back to world No. 88.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.welt.de/newsticker/dpa_nt/regioline_nt/hamburgschleswigholstein_nt/article124479822/Goerges-in-Tennis-Weltrangliste-wieder-in-Top-100.html|title=Görges in Tennis-Weltrangliste wieder in Top 100|work=[[Die Welt]]|date=3 February 2014|accessdate=4 February 2014|language=de}}</ref>

The following week, Görges competed in [[2014 Abierto Mexicano Telcel|Acapulco]] where she defeated the Mexican wildcard [[Marcela Zacarías]] in the first round before losing to Kaia Kanepi in three sets. She reached the second round at [[2014 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]], where she lost to Maria Sharapova. She did not qualify for the [[2014 Sony Open Tennis|Sony Open]] and lost to [[Virginie Razzano]] in the first round at the [[2014 Family Circle Cup|Family Circle Cup]]. In [[2014 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix|Stuttgart]], she defeated Sorana Cîrstea before falling to Ana Ivanovic in three sets. Then she qualified for the [[2014 Mutua Madrid Open|Madrid Open]], but lost her opener to fourth seed [[Simona Halep]]. She did not qualify for [[2014 Internazionali BNL d'Italia|Rome]] but managed to reach the quarterfinals at [[2014 Internationaux de Strasbourg|Strasbourg]] the week before the French Open. She defeated the top seed Sloane Stephens and Lauren Davis, but lost to [[Madison Keys]].

At the [[2014 French Open|French Open]], Görges lost in the second round to [[Eugenie Bouchard]] of Canada in three sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tennisnet.com/de/de/damen/frenchopen/3813305/French-Open_Julia-Gorges-nach-TurboStart-gegen-Eugenie-Bouchard|title=Julia Görges nach Turbo-Start gegen Eugenie Bouchard chancenlos|work=tennisnet.com|date=28 May 2014|accessdate=5 June 2014|language=de}}</ref> Partnering Anna-Lena Grönefeld in the doubles, she lost in the first round to the unseeded pair Dominika Cibulková and Kirsten Flipkens. However, in the mixed doubles partnering [[Nenad Zimonjić]], Görges had a more successful run, reaching her first Grand Slam final. The German-Serbian pair lost to the unseeded Grönefeld and [[Jean-Julien Rojer]] in three sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spiegel.de/sport/sonst/tennis-french-open-groenefeld-gewinnt-mixed-finale-gegen-goerges-a-973617.html|title=French Open: Grönefeld gewinnt Mixed-Titel gegen Görges|work=[[Der Spiegel]]|date=5 June 2014|accessdate=5 June 2014|language=de}}</ref>

[[File:2014 US Open (Tennis) - Tournament - Julia Goerges (14902719440).jpg|thumb|right|Görges at the 2014 US Open]]
At [[2014 Wimbledon Championships - Women's Singles|Wimbledon]], Görges lost in the first round to the eventual semifinalist Lucie Šafářová in two tie-breaks. In the doubles event, she partnered her fellow German, Grönefeld. The pair fell in the quarterfinals to Andrea Petkovic and Magdaléna Rybáriková. The following week, she competed at the [[2014 Gastein Ladies|Gastein Ladies]] where she lost to Stefanie Vögele in the first round. At the [[2014 Swedish Open|Swedish Open]], Görges defeated the seventh seed [[María Teresa Torró Flor]] in the first round to book a second round clash with another Spaniard, [[Sílvia Soler Espinosa]], losing in three sets. In doubles, Görges was the top seed alongside [[Katarzyna Piter]], but they could only make the quarterfinals, losing to the British pairing of [[Jocelyn Rae]] and [[Anna Smith (tennis)|Anna Smith]].

At the [[2014 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] in New York, Görges fought a spirited first round match against the 11th-seeded Flavia Pennetta, losing in a three-setter. She then competed at the [[2014 Coupe Banque Nationale|Coupe Banque Nationale]] in Québec City where she was seeded fifth. She reached the quarterfinals after defeating [[Stéphanie Dubois]] and Melanie Oudin. She then defeated Andrea Hlaváčková to face [[Mirjana Lučić-Baroni]] in the last four, but lost to the eventual tournament champion. Partnering Hlaváčková in the doubles, she made it to the final, but lost in straight sets to Lucie Hradecká and Lučić-Baroni.

[[File:Goerges RG15 (4) (19120298088).jpg|thumb|left|200px|Görges at 2015 French Open]]

===2017: Late career rapid rise===
[[File:Julia Görges at Wimbledon 2017.jpg|left|300px|thumb|Julia Görges at Wimbledon 2017]]
Görges started the 2017 season in [[2017 ASB Classic|Auckland]], defeating the third seed and former world No. 1, Caroline Wozniacki<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-completes-wozniacki-upset-auckland | title = Goerges Completes Wozniacki Upset In Auckland | accessdate = 25 July 2017}}</ref> before losing in the semifinals to [[Ana Konjuh]].

At the [[2017 Australian Open|Australian Open]] she won her opening match against [[Kateřina Siniaková]] but then lost to Jelena Janković. At the [[2017 French Open|French Open]], Görges lost her opening match in a very tight third set to the American [[Madison Brengle]]. Preparing for Wimbledon, Görges reached her first final on grass at the [[2017 Mallorca Open|Mallorca Open]]. She lost to the second seed [[Anastasija Sevastova]]. At [[2017 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], she lost in three sets to [[Lesia Tsurenko]]. She reached her second final of the year on clay, where she lost to the home favourite Irina-Camelia Begu at the [[2017 BRD Bucharest Open|Bucharest Open]].

In August, Görges reached her third final of the year at the [[2017 Citi Open|Citi Open]] in Washington, where she lost to Makarova.<ref>{{cite web| title=Makarova returns to winner's circle, triumphs at Citi Open | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/makarova-returns-winners-circle-rallies-past-goerges-win-citi-open | date = 6 August 2017 | accessdate = 14 August 2017}}</ref> A week later, at [[2017 Western & Southern Open|Cincinnati]] she knocked out the world No. 10 Agnieszka Radwanska in the first round and the world No. 5, [[Elina Svitolina]], in the third round,<ref>{{cite web| title=Goerges snaps Svitolina streak to reach Cincinnati QF | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-snaps-svitolina-streak-reach-cincinnati-qf | date = 18 August 2017 | accessdate = 18 August 2017}}</ref> but lost to Sloane Stephens in two sets.<ref>{{cite web| title=Stephens surge continues in Cincinnati | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/stephens-comeback-western-southern-open-cincinnati-goerges-semifinals | date = 18 August 2017 | accessdate = 20 August 2017}}</ref>

Görges was seeded 30th at the [[2017 US Open (tennis)|US Open]]. She defeated her compatriot [[Annika Beck]] in the first round, [[Zheng Saisai]] in the second and [[Aleksandra Krunić]] in the third to reach the fourth round for the first time. Görges could not avenge her loss against Stephens, and lost in three sets.<ref>{{cite web| title=Sloane Stephens steps into US Open quarterfinals | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/sloane-stephens-steps-us-open-quarterfinals | date = 3 September 2017 | accessdate = 4 September 2017}}</ref>

In October, Görges won the [[2017 Kremlin Cup|Kremlin Cup]] by defeating [[Daria Kasatkina]] in the final. It was her first singles title since 2011.<ref>{{cite web| title=Goerges cruises past Kasatkina to win VTB Kremlin Cup title | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-cruises-past-kasatkina-win-vtb-kremlin-cup-title |date = 21 October 2017 | accessdate = 22 October 2017}}</ref>

The win in Moscow propelled her back into the top 20, and as a result guaranteed her qualification for the [[WTA Elite Trophy]] in Zhuhai. Görges was drawn into the Azalea Group, alongside the top-seeded Kristina Mladenovic and the Wimbledon semifinalist Magdaléna Rybáriková. She defeated them both in straight sets to advance to the semifinal stage, where she faced [[Anastasija Sevastova]], whom she defeated in straight sets as well. She then defeated CoCo Vandeweghe to win the biggest title of her career. She finished the year with a nine-match winning streak and a career-high ranking of 14.

===2018: Top 10 debut, Wimbledon semifinal, two WTA titles===
[[File:Goerges WM18 (1) (30063296738).jpg|thumb|Görges at the 2018 Wimbledon Championships]]
Entering 2018 with the confidence and form of the last year, Görges won the [[2018 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]] at Auckland. This gave her back to back WTA titles after a previous drought of six years, and extended her winning run to 14 matches, only two of which went to the third set.<ref>{{cite web| title=Goerges extends streak, ousts Wozniacki for Auckland crown | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-extends-streak-ousts-wozniacki-auckland-crown |date = 7 January 2018 | accessdate = 7 January 2018}}</ref>

At the [[2018 Australian Open|Australian Open]], she lost in the second round to [[Alizé Cornet]].<ref>{{cite web| title=Cornet puts Goerges winning streak on ice in Melbourne | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/cornet-puts-goerges-winning-streak-ice-melbourne | date = 17 January 2018 | accessdate = 21 January 2018}}</ref> Görges then played in [[2018 St. Petersburg Ladies' Trophy|St. Petersburg]], where she lost in the semifinals to Petra Kvitová.<ref>{{cite web| title=Kvitova grounds Goerges to reach St. Petersburg final | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/kvitova-grounds-goerges-reach-st-petersburg-final | date = 3 February 2018 | accessdate = 3 February 2018}}</ref> Despite the loss, Görges made her top 10 debut following the tournament, as Kristina Mladenovic failed to defend the title.<ref>{{cite web| title=Goerges to make Top 10 debut | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-make-top-10-debut | date = 4 February 2018 | accessdate = 4 February 2018}}</ref>

In [[2018 Qatar Total Open|Doha]], she reached the quarterfinals but retired in the match against Kvitová during the second set because of a hip injury.<ref>{{cite web| title=Kvitova advances following Goerges retirement in Doha | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/kvitova-advances-following-goerges-retirement-doha | date = 16 February 2018 | accessdate = 17 February 2018}}</ref>

In April, she reached final of Premier tournament in [[2018 Volvo Car Open|Charleston]], defeating [[Naomi Osaka]], Daria Kasatkina and Anastasija Sevastova. In the final she lost to Kiki Bertens.<ref>{{cite web| title=Bertens blasts past Goerges for biggest title in Charleston | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/bertens-blasts-past-goerges-biggest-title-charleston | date = 8 April 2018 | accessdate = 12 April 2018}}</ref>

Görges played for Germany in [[2018 Fed Cup World Group|Fed Cup]] semifinals against Czech Republic. At the [[2018 French Open|French Open]], she played as the 11th seed. She defeated Dominika Cibulková and [[Alison Van Uytvanck]] before losing to [[Serena Williams]] in the third round.<ref>{{cite web| title=Serena powers past Goerges, sets Sharapova clash in Paris | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/serena-powers-past-goerges-sets-sharapova-clash-paris | date = 22 June 2018 | accessdate = 23 June 2018}}</ref>

She started the grass-court season in [[2018 Birmingham Classic|Birmingham]], where she lost in the quarterfinals to the defending champion Petra Kvitová.<ref>{{cite web| title=Kvitova races past Goerges to move into Birmingham semis | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/kvitova-races-past-goerges-move-birmingham-semis | date = 22 June 2018 | accessdate = 23 June 2018}}</ref> In [[2018 Eastbourne International|Eastbourne]], Görges received bye in the first round and in the second round she lost to [[Aryna Sabalenka]].

At [[2018 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Görges reached her first Grand Slam semifinal, defeating Monica Puig, [[Vera Lapko]], Barbora Strýcová, [[Donna Vekić]] and Kiki Bertens en route.<ref>{{cite web| title=Goerges ends Bertens’ run, gets Serena next at Wimbledon | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-ends-bertens%E2%80%99-run-gets-serena-next-wimbledon | date = 10 July 2018 | accessdate = 13 July 2018}}</ref> She lost in the semifinal to Serena Williams.<ref>{{cite web| title=Serena books Kerber rematch to reach 10th Wimbledon final | url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/serena-books-kerber-rematch-reach-10th-wimbledon-final | date = 12 July 2018 | accessdate = 12 July 2018}}</ref>

In October, she won her sixth career singles title at the [[2018 BGL Luxembourg Open|Luxembourg Open]], defeating [[Belinda Bencic]] in the final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-grounds-bencic-win-luxembourg|title=Goerges grounds Bencic to win in Luxembourg|publisher=WTA|date=20 October 2018}}</ref>

===2019: Defending Auckland Open title===
Görges won her second [[WTA Auckland Open|Auckland Open]] title by beating Canadian [[Bianca Andreescu]] in the final.<ref>{{cite web| title=Goerges seals Auckland double, ends Andreescu’s fairytale run | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/goerges-seals-auckland-double-ends-andreescu%E2%80%99s-fairytale-run | date = 6 January 2019 | accessdate = 7 January 2019}}</ref> She then headed to [[2019 Australian Open - Women's Singles|Melbourne]] where, as 14th seed, she was beaten in the first round of the Australian Open by [[Danielle Collins]].

In June, Görges reached the final in [[2019 Birmingham Classic|Birmingham]] where she lost to [[Ashleigh Barty]] who became the new No. 1 after this tournament.<ref>{{cite web| title='It's been a whirlwind year' - Barty seals Goerges, secures No.1 ranking with Birmingham crown | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/its-been-whirlwind-year-barty-seals-goerges-secures-no1-ranking-birmingham-crown | work=WTA Tennis | date = 23 June 2019 | accessdate = 24 June 2019}}</ref> At [[2019 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Görges defeated [[Elena-Gabriela Ruse]] and [[Varvara Flink]] before losing in the third round to Serena Williams, in a rematch of the previous year's semifinal.<ref>{{cite web| title=‘Each match counts for me’: Serena extends Goerges mastery to reach Wimbledon fourth round | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/%E2%80%98each-match-counts-me%E2%80%99-serena-extends-goerges-mastery-reach-wimbledon-fourth-round | work=WTA Tennis | date = 6 July 2019 | accessdate = 15 July 2019}}</ref> At the [[2019 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]], Görges defeated [[Natalia Vikhlyantseva]], [[Francesca Di Lorenzo]], and Kiki Bertens to reach the fourth round, where she lost to Donna Vekić, despite having a match point in the second set.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vekic tops Goerges to reach quarter-finals|url=https://www.eurosport.com/tennis/us-open/2019/vekic-tops-goerges-to-reach-u.s.-open-quarter-finals_sto7439609/story.shtml|work=Eurosport|date=2 September 2019|accessdate=4 September 2019}}</ref>

Görges attempted to defend her title at [[2019 BGL Luxembourg Open – Singles|Luxembourg]], and reached the final after defeating [[Misaki Doi]], [[Sorana Cîrstea]], [[Monica Puig]], and [[Elena Rybakina]]. However, she fell to an in form [[Jeļena Ostapenko]] in the final in straight sets.

===2020: Lowest ranking since 2016, end of career===
Görges defeated [[Greetje Minnen]] and [[Jil Teichmann]] to reach the quarterfinals in her [[2020 ASB Classic – Women's Singles|Auckland Open]] title defence, but she lost to Caroline Wozniacki in the quarterfinals in straight sets.<ref>{{cite web| title=Wozniacki dethrones Goerges to return to Auckland semis | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1565278/wozniacki-dethrones-goerges-to-return-to-auckland-semis | work=WTA Tennis | date = 10 January 2019 | accessdate = 11 January 2020}}</ref> Following this loss, she fell to No. 38. She partnered [[Caroline Garcia]] in doubles, and reached the quarterfinals, where they fell to eventual champions [[Asia Muhammad]] and [[Taylor Townsend (tennis)|Taylor Townsend]]. At [[2020 Adelaide International – Women's Singles|Adelaide]], she defeated [[Priscilla Hon]] before falling to [[Belinda Bencic]] in two tight sets. At [[2020 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Melbourne]], she defeated [[Viktória Kužmová]] and 13th seed [[Petra Martić]], before being defeated by [[Alison Riske]] in three sets. Following the Australian Open, Görges' ranking rose to No. 31. After failing to qualify for [[2020 Dubai Tennis Championships – Women's Singles|Dubai]], losing to [[Sorana Cîrstea]] in the second qualifying round, Görges participated in [[2020 Qatar Total Open – Singles|Doha]], where she lost in the first round to [[Maria Sakkari]].

Görges opted to withdraw from the US Open season, citing safety concerns due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. Her first tournament following the suspension of the WTA Tour was at [[2020 Italian Open – Women's Singles|Rome]], where she lost to [[Danka Kovinić]] in the first round, winning just one game. She next participated at the [[2020 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]], where she defeated Riske in the first round, before losing to [[Laura Siegemund]] in the second round. Following these tournaments, Görges' ranking fell to No. 45, her lowest ranking since 2016. On 21 October, Görges announced her immediate retirement from the tour on her website.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1870741/-time-to-say-goodbye-goerges-bids-farewell-to-tennis|title='Time to say goodbye' - Goerges bids farewell to tennis|website=WTA|date=21 October 2020}}</ref>
