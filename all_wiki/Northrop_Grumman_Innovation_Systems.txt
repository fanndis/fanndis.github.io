'''Northrop Grumman Innovation Systems''' ('''NGIS''') was a sector (business segment) of [[Northrop Grumman]] from 2018 to 2020. It was formed out of '''Orbital ATK Inc.''' a company which resulted from the merger of [[Orbital Sciences Corporation]] and parts of [[Alliant Techsystems]] in 2015.  Orbital ATK was purchased by Northrop Grumman in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://spacenews.com/acquisition-of-orbital-atk-approved-company-renamed-northrop-grumman-innovation-systems/|title= Orbital ATK will become Northrop Grumman's fourth business sector|date=2018-06-06|access-date=2018-08-16|archive-date=2020-08-23|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20200823012333/https://spacenews.com/acquisition-of-orbital-atk-approved-company-renamed-northrop-grumman-innovation-systems/|url-status=live}}</ref> Northrop Grumman Innovation Systems designed, built, and delivered space, defense, and aviation-related systems to customers around the world both as a prime contractor and as a merchant supplier. It had a workforce of approximately 12,000 employees dedicated to aerospace and defense including about 4,000 engineers and scientists; 7,000 manufacturing and operations specialists; and 1,000 management and administration personnel.<ref name=overview>{{cite web|title=Company Overview|url=https://www.orbitalatk.com/about/company-overview/|access-date=25 February 2017|archive-date=5 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170705121634/http://www.orbitalatk.com/about/company-overview/|url-status=dead}}</ref> With Northrop Grumman's reorganization of its divisions effective 1 January 2020, NGIS was split, with most of the sector merging with other Northrop Grumman businesses into a new Space Systems sector.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.defensenews.com/industry/2019/09/20/northrop-launches-new-divisions-focused-on-space-cyber-unmanned-tech/|title=Northrop launches new divisions focused on space, cyber, unmanned tech|access-date=2020-04-17|archive-date=2020-08-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823012333/https://www.defensenews.com/industry/2019/09/20/northrop-launches-new-divisions-focused-on-space-cyber-unmanned-tech/|url-status=live}}</ref>

== History ==
[[File:Logo_for_Orbital_ATK.png|thumb|Former logo of Orbital ATK, the company's original name following the merger of [[Orbital Sciences Corporation]] and the supoff aerospace division of [[Alliant Techsystems]].]]

A merger of [[Orbital Sciences Corporation]] and the defense and aerospace divisions of [[Alliant Techsystems]] (ATK) was announced on 29 April 2014. The two companies had collaborated on several previous projects, including the use of 400 ATK rocket motors in Orbital's launch vehicles.<ref>{{cite news|last=Aitoro|first=Jill R.|date=April 29, 2014|newspaper=Washington Business Journal|title=Why the merger of ATK and Orbital Sciences makes sense|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/washington/blog/fedbiz_daily/2014/04/why-the-merger-of-atk-and-orbital-sciences-makes.html?page=all|access-date=February 19, 2015|archive-date=February 20, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150220023916/http://www.bizjournals.com/washington/blog/fedbiz_daily/2014/04/why-the-merger-of-atk-and-orbital-sciences-makes.html?page=all|url-status=live}}</ref> The deal officially closed on 9 February 2015. ATK's sporting-goods division spun off to form [[Vista Outdoor]] on the same day.<ref name=wall>{{cite web|last=Wall|first=Mike|date=February 10, 2015|work=Space.com|title=Orbital ATK, Merger of Orbital Sciences and ATK, Begins Operations|url=http://www.space.com/28515-orbital-atk-merger-private-spaceflight.html|access-date=February 19, 2015|archive-date=April 19, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190419172604/https://www.space.com/28515-orbital-atk-merger-private-spaceflight.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Organization ==
=== Flight Systems Group ===
Based in [[Chandler, Arizona]],<ref name=wall/> the Flight Systems Group includes the [[Northrop Grumman Pegasus|Pegasus]], [[Minotaur (rocket family)|Minotaur]], and [[Antares (rocket)|Antares]] launch vehicles as well as solid-propulsion and aerostructures programs.<ref name=berger>{{cite news|author=Berger, Brian|newspaper=SpaceNews|date=February 12, 2015|title=Orbital ATK Org Charts Detail the Newly Merged Company|url=http://spacenews.com/orbital-atk-org-charts-detail-the-newly-merged-company/|access-date=February 19, 2015|archive-date=August 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823012334/https://spacenews.com/orbital-atk-org-charts-detail-the-newly-merged-company/|url-status=live}}</ref> The company also operates a [[Lockheed L-1011 TriStar]] wide body jetliner, which is named [[Stargazer (aircraft)|Stargazer]] and is used to air launch Pegasus rockets carrying payloads into space.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.orbitalatk.com|title=Orbital ATK |website=www.orbitalatk.com|access-date=2020-08-23|archive-date=2018-06-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180601010814/http://www.orbitalatk.com/|url-status=live}}</ref> The [[Stargazer (aircraft) |Stargazer]] aircraft is also used for testing under specific programs. The Flight Systems Group became part of Northrop Grumman Space Systems on 1 January 2020.

=== Defense Systems Group ===
The Defense Systems Group, based in the [[Baltimore, Maryland]]<ref name=wall/> area, produces tactical missiles, defense electronics, and medium- and large-caliber ammunition. The division also produces fuzing and warheads for both tactical missiles and munitions; precision metal and composite structures for medium and large-caliber ammunition, military aircraft, ground vehicles, and missile systems; load, assembly, and pack (LAP) of medium caliber munitions; and propellants and powders for the canister and commercial markets. The Defense Systems Group became part of Northrop Grumman Defense Systems on 1 January 2020.

=== Space Systems Group ===
Orbital ATK's Space Systems Group provides satellites for [[Commerce|commercial]], scientific, and security purposes.<ref name=berger/> This group also produces the [[Cygnus (spacecraft)|Cygnus]] spacecraft, which delivers cargo to the [[International Space Station]]. The group is based at the company's headquarters on Warp Drive in [[Dulles, Virginia]].<ref name=wall/> The Space Systems Group became part of Northrop Grumman Space Systems on 1 January 2020.
