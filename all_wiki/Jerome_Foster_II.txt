'''Jerome Foster II''' (born 9 May 2002) is an American [[Climate activism|climate change activist]], voting rights advocate, and [[virtual reality]] developer.<ref name="Wash Leadership Acad YouTube">{{Cite AV media |title=Washington Leadership Academy|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ov-jBUb9l20|language=en|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> Foster is the executive director of OneMillionOfUs; an international youth voting and advocacy organization. Foster is known for delivering passionate speeches advocating for the use of intergovernmental unity and [[climate justice]] as a means to help mitigate [[climate change]] in accordance to the [[Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change|IPCC Special Report on Global Warming]] and the [[Green New Deal|Green New Deal Resolution]]. He is also known for wearing a bright-blue earth shirt that says "Think Globally, Bike Locally" on the front and "One Less Car" on the back made by the company, [[Specialized Bicycle Components|Specialized Inc]]. Foster has led three social impact and technology ventures: [[Jerome Foster II#TAU VR|TAU VR]], [[Jerome Foster II#The Climate Reporter|The Climate Reporter]], and [[Jerome Foster II#OneMillionOfUs|OneMillionOfUs]].

Foster has helped to organize three of the top ten largest climate marches across the [[Washington, D.C. area]] and has spoken at the United Nations High Commission on Human Rights in April 2019 and the United Nations Youth Climate Summit in September 2019. He gained international recognition after being the first consecutive [[School strike for climate|climate striker]] in Washington DC<ref>{{cite news|last1=Li|first1=Ang|date=14 March 2019|title='It Will Be Too Late for My Generation.' Meet the Young People Organizing a Massive Climate Change Protest|publisher=TIME Magazine|url=https://time.com/5550823/climate-change-protest-schoolchildren/}}</ref> and being interviewed by [[Al Gore|Former Vice President Al Gore]] at the Atlanta [[The Climate Reality Project|Climate Reality]] Leadership Training in March 2019.<ref>{{cite web|date=6 April 2019|title=Three Great Moments From our Atlanta Training|url=https://www.climaterealityproject.org/blog/three-great-moments-our-atlanta-training|website=Climate Reality}}</ref>

== Activism and entrepreneurship ==
=== TAU VR ===
When Foster was 14 years old, he founded an immersive technology company, TAU VR. In September 2017, TAU VR was featured on XQ Super School Live,<ref name="XQ Super School Youtube">{{Cite AV media |title=XQ Super School Live Jerome Foster II|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7FiHJy0d6ZA|language=en|access-date=2020-03-23}}</ref> which was aired on ABC, CBS, NBC, and FOX.<ref name="XQ Super School Youtube" />

=== The Climate Reporter ===
Foster, in November 2017, founded The Climate Reporter,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rainforest-alliance.org/articles/global-climate-strike-5-youth-activists-who-are-leading-the-charge-on-climate-action|title=Global Climate Strike: 5 Youth Activists Who Are Leading the Charge on Climate Action|publisher=Rainforest Alliance|language=en|access-date=2020-03-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nationofchange.org/2019/09/20/four-climate-scientists-on-how-to-take-on-climate-change-today/|title=Four Climate Scientists on How to Take on Climate Change Today|last=Hansen|first=Terri|date=2019-09-20|website=NationofChange|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-06}}</ref> an international youth-led climate-focused [[News media|news outlet]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://peoplesclimate.org/actions/the-climate-reporter/|title=The Climate Reporter|publisher=Peoples Climate Movement|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-06}}</ref> on writing articles written by teenagers young adults and front-line communities. The Climate Reporter has writers in 11 countries and 3 indigenous nations, according to their archived contributors list.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://medium.com/the-climate-reporter/about|title=About The Climate Reporter|website=Medium|access-date=2020-03-06}}</ref>

=== White House Climate Strikes ===
As a part of [[Greta Thunberg]]'s [[School strike for climate|School Strike for Climate]] protest, Foster holds weekly climate strikes<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wjla.com/news/local/dc-hs-student-climate-change-rally-white-house|title=DC HS student has held climate change rally in front of White House for 57 weeks in a row|last=Rodriguez/ABC7|first=Ashlie|date=2020-03-06|publisher=WJLA|access-date=2020-03-23}}</ref> in front of the White House in [[Lafayette Square, Washington, D.C.|Lafayette Square]].<ref name="Yes taking to Capitol">{{Cite web|url=https://www.yesmagazine.org/environment/2019/05/24/climate-strike-american-capitol-student|title=This 16-Year-Old Is Taking the School Climate Strike to the U.S. Capitol|website=Yes! Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> On September 13, 2019, Thunberg as a part of her trans-American voyage to [[2019 United Nations Climate Change Conference|COP 25]] in Santiago, Chile,<ref>{{Cite news|last=correspondent|first=Fiona Harvey Environment|url=https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2019/nov/01/madrid-to-host-un-climate-summit-after-chile-pulls-out|title=Madrid to host UN climate summit after Chile pulls out|date=2019-11-01|work=The Guardian|access-date=2020-03-23|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> joined Foster's White House climate strikes which drew thousands to the site.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.democracynow.org/2019/9/20/youth_climate_lawsuit_juliana_v_united|title=Kelsey Juliana v. U.S.: Meet the Young Woman Suing the Federal Government over the Climate Crisis|website=Democracy Now!|language=en|access-date=2020-03-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.earthday.org/our-future-is-a-right-youth-climate-activists-strike-outside-white-house/|title="Our future is a right". Youth climate activists strike outside White House|date=2019-09-13|publisher=Earth Day|language=en|access-date=2020-03-24}}</ref>  During his time at Harvard University, he held weekly climate strikes on the steps in front of [[Widener Library]] with Divest Harvard organizers who called on the university to divest their endowments in the fossil fuel industry. He helped get 186 additional signatures<ref>{{Cite web|title=Give Climate Warning ⚠ to Harvard University: Harvard Stop Investing in Fossil Fuel Corporations|url=https://campaigns.wedonthavetime.org/harvard-stop-investing-in-fossil-fuel-corporations-96fa9591-bbc5-4f4c-9441-20ab6f6cb3f3|access-date=2020-07-29|website=Climate Warning to Harvard University|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/wedonthavetime/status/1152858196341592064|url=https://twitter.com/wedonthavetime/status/1152858196341592064|access-date=2020-07-29|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> for their petition to Harvard University during his 3 months that summer. After Foster return from Harvard University in late 2019, he was approached by Jane Fonda where he was asked to help found and construct Fire Drill Fridays which was a weekly climate strike held in front of the U.S. Capitol Building.

=== OneMillionOfUs ===
Foster founded [[OneMillionOfUs]] in early 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.c-span.org/organization/?135483/OneMillionOfUs|title=OneMillionOfUs |publisher=C-SPAN |access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.earthday.org/19-youth-climate-activists-you-should-follow-on-social-media/|title=19 youth climate activists you should be following on social media|date=2019-06-14|publisher=Earth Day|language=en|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.c-span.org/person/?123171|title=Jerome Foster |publisher=C-SPAN |access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> It is an international non-profit youth voting and advocacy organization that provides resources and training to young adults.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/news/annals-of-a-warming-planet/what-can-the-coronavirus-teach-us|title=OneMillionOfUs' Plan to Revolutionize the Youth Vote in America|last=McKibben|first=Bill|website=The New Yorker|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=2020-03-24}}</ref> This organization also created a "Uniting Youth Coalition"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/news/annals-of-a-warming-planet/what-can-the-coronavirus-teach-us|title=What Can the Coronavirus Teach Us?|last=McKibben|first=Bill|website=The New Yorker|language=en|access-date=2020-03-06}}</ref> representing 5 youth social movements: [[Gun violence in the United States|gun violence]], [[Global warming|climate change]], [[Immigration reform in the United States|immigration reform]], [[gender equality]], and [[racial equality]] to have a space on both the local and international level to coordinate events and campaigns between movements. It is often misspelled as "one million of us".
