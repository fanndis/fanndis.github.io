'''Tom King''' (born July 15, 1978) is an American [[author]], [[comic book writer]], and ex-CIA officer. He is best known for writing the novel ''A Once Crowded Sky'', ''[[Vision (Marvel Comics)|The Vision]]'' for [[Marvel Comics]], ''[[The Sheriff of Babylon]]'' for the [[DC Comics]] imprint [[Vertigo Comics|Vertigo]], and ''[[Batman (comic book)|Batman]]'' and ''[[Mister Miracle]]'' for DC Comics.

== Early life ==
King primarily grew up in Southern California. His mother worked for the film industry which inspired his love of storytelling. He interned at both [[DC Comics|DC]] and [[Marvel Comics]] during the late 1990s. He studied both philosophy and history at [[Columbia University]], graduating in 2000. He identifies as "half-Jewish, half-midwestern".<ref>Polo, Susana (25 September 2018) [https://www.polygon.com/comics/2018/9/25/17895756/batman-tom-king-superman-brian-bendis-interview "The World’s Finest: Batman’s Tom King and Superman’s Brian Bendis in conversation"]. [[Polygon (website)|Polygon]].</ref>

== Career ==
King interned both at [[DC Comics]] and [[Marvel Comics]], where he was an assistant to ''[[X-Men]]'' writer [[Chris Claremont]], before joining the [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]] counterterrorism unit after [[September 11 attacks|9/11]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/06/24/the-cia-spook-turned-comic-book-scribe-robin-grabs-a-gun-in-grayson.html|title=The CIA Spook Turned Comic Book Scribe: Robin Grabs a Gun in ‘Grayson’|last=Goldstein|first=Rich|date=2014-06-24|website=The Daily Beast|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=56655|title=Tom King Explores the Undercover History of "Grayson"|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref> King spent seven years as a counterterrorism operations officer for the CIA before quitting to write his debut novel, ''A Once Crowded Sky'', after the birth of his first child.<ref name=WashingtonPost>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/magazine/holy-dc-connection-a-local-comic-book-writer-used-to-work-for-the-cia/2016/01/27/3a4679a0-a98e-11e5-8058-480b572b4aae_story.html|title=Holy DC connection! A local comic book writer used to work for the CIA!|last=Ottesen|first=K. K.|date=2016-01-27|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|language=en-US|issn=0190-8286|access-date=April 20, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.militarytimes.com/story/entertainment/2015/08/02/cia-comics-former-agent-makes-career-splash/30930687/|title=From CIA to comics: Former agent makes career splash|website=MilitaryTimes|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref>

''A Once Crowded Sky'', King's debut superhero novel with comics pages illustrated by [[Tom Fowler (cartoonist)|Tom Fowler]], was published on July 10, 2012 by Touchstone, an imprint of [[Simon & Schuster]], to positive reception.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.avclub.com/review/tom-king-ema-once-crowded-skyem-82788|title=Tom King: A Once Crowded Sky|website=www.avclub.com|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wired.com/2012/07/a-once-crowded-sky/|title=Heroes and Villains Are Gone From A Once Crowded Sky|website=WIRED|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pages.simonandschuster.com/once-crowded-sky|title=A Once Crowded Sky|last=Archipelago|first=World|website=pages.simonandschuster.com|access-date=2016-03-10|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150929074814/http://pages.simonandschuster.com/once-crowded-sky|archivedate=2015-09-29}}</ref>

King was chosen to co-write ''[[Grayson (comic book)|Grayson]]'' for DC Comics, along with [[Tim Seeley]] and Mikel Janin on art. After penning ''[[Nightwing]]'' #30, King, Seeley, and Janin launched ''Grayson'' in May 2014, featuring [[Dick Grayson]] leaving behind his Nightwing persona at age 22 to become Agent 37, a Spyral spy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/2014/04/14/grayson-comic-book-series/7480853/|title=Batman's sidekick leaves the cave for 'Grayson' comic|website=USA TODAY|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=53116|title=Seeley, King Enter the DCU's Espionage World in "Grayson"|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref> King and Seeley plotted the series together and traded issues to script separately, with King providing additional authenticity through his background with the [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://batman-news.com/2015/06/05/interview-graysons-tim-seeley-tom-king/|title=Interview: Grayson’s Tim Seeley and Tom King|last=Yaws|first=Jay|website=Batman News|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/cbr-tv-grayson-co-writer-king-connects-cia-past-to-dc-comics-future|title=CBR TV: "Grayson" Co-Writer King Connects CIA Past to DC Comics Future|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref>

A relaunch of classic DC Comics series ''[[Omega Men|The Omega Men]]'' was published in June 2015 by King and debut artist Barnaby Bagenda, as part of the publisher's relaunch DC You.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2015/06/03/the-omega-men-are-reborn-in-the-new-dc-universe|title=The Omega Men Are Reborn in the New DC Universe|last=Yehl|first=By Joshua|last2=Schedeen|first2=Jesse|website=IGN|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/decoding-dc-entertainments-dc-you-798928|title=Decoding DC Entertainment's 'DC You' Comic Book Relaunch|website=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref> The series follows a group of rebels fighting an oppressive galactic empire, and feature White Lantern [[Kyle Rayner]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/tom-king-hasnt-decided-if-dcs-omega-men-are-good-or-bad-guys|title=Tom King Hasn't Decided if DC's "Omega Men" Are Good or Bad Guys|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref> The Omega Men, created in 1981, are DC's cosmic equivalent to Marvel's [[Guardians of the Galaxy]], though significantly more obscure.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/omega-men-tom-king-2015-6|title=This new comic series is the 'Star Wars' meets 'Game of Thrones' mashup you didn't know you wanted|website=Business Insider|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref> King's and Bagenda's use of the nine-panel grid, popularized by [[Alan Moore|Alan Moore's]] and [[Dave Gibbons|Dave Gibbons']] ''[[Watchmen]]'', has been praised by reviewers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://comicsalliance.com/omega-men-nine-panel-grid/ |title=Restriction & Revolution: 'Omega Men' And The Nine-Panel Grid |website=Comics Alliance |access-date=2016-03-10 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305194248/http://comicsalliance.com/omega-men-nine-panel-grid/ |archivedate=2016-03-05 }}</ref>

In San Diego Comic-Con 2015, [[Vertigo (DC Comics)|Vertigo]] revealed a new creator-owned project written by King with art by Mitch Gerads titled ''The Sheriff of Baghdad''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dailydot.com/geek/vertigo-comics-sdcc-2015-12-new-series-announcement/|title=Vertigo comes back from the dead by announcing 12 new titles at Comic-Con|website=The Daily Dot|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> The project, a crime series in the vein of Vertigo titles like ''[[Preacher (comics)|Preacher]]'' and ''[[Scalped]]'', was set to launch in late 2015, and was inspired by King's time in Iraq as part of the CIA.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/sdcc-vertigo-finishes-2015-by-launching-12-new-series|title=SDCC: Vertigo Finishes 2015 by Launching 12 New Series|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> Initially an eight-issue miniseries, it was later re-titled ''The Sheriff of Babylon'' and expanded into an ongoing series.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.vertigocomics.com/comics/the-sheriff-of-babylon-2015/the-sheriff-of-babylon-1|title=THE SHERIFF OF BABYLON #1|website=www.vertigocomics.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bleedingcool.com/2015/08/18/dc-comics-sheriff-of-baghdad-changes-name-because-of-john-mcphee/|title=DC Comics' Sheriff Of Baghdad Changes Name Because Of John McPhee|website=Bleeding Cool Comic Book, Movie, TV News|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> The first issue launched in December 2015 to critical acclaim, with reviewers praising its "deeply personal" storytelling and the "intriguing" and "captivating" personalities of its characters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/two-new-releases-spotlight-tom-kings-sharp-sophist-229267|title=Two new releases spotlight Tom King’s sharp, sophisticated storytelling|website=www.avclub.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/27022-best-shots-rapid-fire-reviews-all-new-all-different-avengers-2-midnighter-7-star-wars-13-more.html|title=Best Shots Rapid-Fire Reviews: ALL-NEW ALL-DIFFERENT AVENGERS #2, MIDNIGHTER #7, STAR WARS #13, More|website=Newsarama.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> That same year, DC announced "[[Robin War]]", a crossover storyline set for December that would run for five week through titles ''Grayson'', ''Detective Comics'', ''We Are Robin'', and ''Robin: Son of Batman''; King was set to orchestrate the crossover's story-line and pen two one-shots to open and close the series.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/dc-announces-robin-war-crossover-weekly-batman-robin-eternal|title=SDCC: DC Announces "Robin War" Crossover, Weekly "Batman & Robin Eternal"|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref>

As part of [[Marvel Comics]]' [[All-New, All-Different Marvel|All-New, All-Different]] relaunch, King was announced as the writer of ''The Vision'', a new ongoing following the titular character and his newly created family, with artist Gabriel Hernández Walta, colorist [[Jordie Bellaire]], and covers by [[Mike del Mundo]], launching in November 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/king-walta-to-launch-the-vision-ongoingthis-october|title=King & Walta To Launch "The Vision" Ongoing This October|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/tom-king-shares-his-familial-vision-for-marvels-synthezoid-avenger-2015|title=Tom King Shares His Familial "Vision" for Marvel's Synthezoid Avenger|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://comicbooknoise.com/loikamania/2016/02/23/loikamania-244-tom-king/|title=Loikamania 244: Tom King|website=Loikamania|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> ''The Vision'' has been well received by the public, with reviewers calling the series one of Marvel's "biggest surprises" and praising the narration, art, and colors.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://io9.gizmodo.com/marvels-the-vision-is-telling-a-story-unlike-any-superh-1756995647|title=Marvel's The Vision Is Telling a Story Unlike Any Superhero Comic I've Ever Read|last=Whitbrook|first=James|website=io9|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://io9.gizmodo.com/the-vision-is-one-of-the-most-unsettling-comics-ive-rea-1740684807|title=The Vision Is One Of The Most Unsettling Comics I've Read This Year|last=Whitbrook|first=James|website=io9|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref>

In September 2015, DC cancelled King's ''The Omega Men'', along with four other titles, with the series ending with issue seven.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/five-dc-titles-to-end-in-december-including-justice-league-united-and-lobo|title=Five DC Titles Set to End, Including "Justice League United" and "Lobo"|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> After negative fan response to the cancellation, [[Jim Lee]], DC's co-publisher, announced that they would be bringing back ''The Omega Men'' through at least issue 12.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/25973-dc-brings-back-omega-men-due-to-fan-response-at-cancellation.html|title=DC Brings Back OMEGA MEN Due To Fan Response At Cancellation|website=Newsarama.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> Lee described the decision to cancel the series as "a bit hasty," crediting the book's critical acclaim and fan social media reactions as the reason the title would go on for the planned 12-issue run.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2015/09/27/jim-lee-talks-batman-noir-hush-un-canceling-the-omega-men-and-potential-supergirl-comic|title=Jim Lee Talks Batman Noir: Hush, Un-Canceling The Omega Men, and Potential Supergirl Comic|last=Yehl|first=By Joshua|website=IGN|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref>

King penned a ''[[Green Lantern]]'' one-shot that ties into the "Darkseid War" [[Justice League]] storyline, titled "Will You Be My God?", which James Whitbrook of [[io9]] praised as "one of the best" Green Lantern stories.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://io9.gizmodo.com/the-fallout-of-darkseid-war-gives-us-the-best-green-lan-1742132602|title=The Fallout of Darkseid War Gives Us The Best Green Lantern Story In Years|last=Whitbrook|first=James|website=io9|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-10}}</ref>

King and co-writer Tim Seeley announced they would leave ''Grayson'' after issue #18, with King clarifying on Twitter that they were working on something "big and cool" and needed time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicsbeat.com/seeley-and-king-leave-grayson-for-something-big-and-cool/|title=Seeley and King Leave Grayson for "something big and cool"|website=The Beat|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> King and Seeley officially left the series in February with issue #17, with Jackson Lanzing and Collin Kelly taking over for its last three issues with issue #18 in March.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/28185-grayson-writers-on-finale-building-towards-nightwing-s-rebirth.html|title=GRAYSON Writers On Finale & Building Towards NIGHTWING's REBIRTH|website=Newsarama.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref>

DC Comics announced in February 2016 that King had signed an exclusivity deal with the publisher, which would see him writing exclusively for DC and Vertigo.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://comicsalliance.com/tom-king-clay-mann-john-timms-dc-exclusive/ |title=Tom King, Clay Mann, and John Timms Sign Exclusive Deals at DC |website=Comics Alliance |access-date=2016-03-09 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160322071848/http://comicsalliance.com/tom-king-clay-mann-john-timms-dc-exclusive/ |archivedate=2016-03-22 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dccomics.com/blog/2016/02/18/dc-entertainment-signs-top-talent|title=DC ENTERTAINMENT SIGNS TOP TALENT|website=DC Comics|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> King revealed via his Twitter account that he would stay on ''The Vision'' as writer through issue 12, finishing the story arc he had planned from the beginning.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/28042-tom-king-reveals-vision-s-future.html|title=DC-Bound TOM KING Reveals His Last Issue of MARVEL's THE VISION|website=Newsarama.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/axel-in-charge-investigating-black-widow-future-of-the-vision|title=Axel-In-Charge: Investigating "Black Widow," the Future of "The Vision"|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref>

In March 2016, it was announced that King would be writing the main bi-weekly ''[[Batman]]'' series beginning with a new #1, replacing long-time writer [[Scott Snyder]], as part of DC's Rebirth relaunch that June.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/article/wondercon-dc-comics-reveals-rebirth-creative-teams-more-new-details|title=WONDERCON: DC COMICS REVEALS "REBIRTH" CREATIVE TEAMS|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=2016-05-29}}</ref> King has stated that his run would be 100 issues total, with the entirety being released twice-monthly, though this was later curtailed to 85 issues and 3 annuals, with a 12 issue followup maxiseries ''Batman/Catwoman'' to finish the story.<ref>https://www.bleedingcool.com/2017/09/08/tom-king-planning-batman-100-issues/</ref>

In August 2017, King and regular collaborator Mitch Gerads launched the first issue of their ''Mister Miracle'' series, with a planned total run of twelve issues.<ref>https://www.dccomics.com/comics/mister-miracle-2017/mister-miracle-1</ref> In June 2018 DC Comics announced King would be writing ''[[Heroes in Crisis]]'', a limited series centering around a concept he introduced in ''Batman''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=McMillan |first1=Graeme |title=DC's 'Heroes in Crisis' to Explore Superhero Trauma |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/heroes-crisis-comic-focuses-dc-superhero-trauma-1119410 |website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |accessdate=June 14, 2018 |date=June 12, 2018}}</ref>

In July 2018, he received the [[Eisner Award]] for Best Writer, shared with [[Marjorie Liu]].<ref>{{Cite news|author=Ducharme, Jamie|date=21 July 2018|url=http://time.com/5345155/marjorie-liu-eisner-award/|title=A Woman Has Finally Won the Top Writing Award in Comic Books|journal=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref>

In May and June 2019, King, DC Co-Publisher Jim Lee, and [[The CW|CW]] series actresses [[Nafessa Williams]], [[Candice Patton]], and [[Danielle Panabaker]] toured five U.S. military bases in Kuwait with the [[United Service Organizations]] (USO), where they visited the approximately 12,000 U.S. military personnel stationed in that country as part of DC's 80th anniversary of Batman celebration.<ref>{{cite web|author=Arrant, Chris|date=June 2, 2019|url=https://www.newsarama.com/45412-jim-lee-tom-king-dc-cw-stars-make-surprise-uso-trip-to-kuwait.html|publisher=Newsarama|title=JIM LEE, TOM KING, DC-CW Stars Make Surprise USO Trip To KUWAIT|accessdate=June 3, 2019|archivedate=June 3, 2019|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/78rNGSl9v}}</ref>

In September 2020, [[DC Comics]] announced that King would be among the creators of a revived ''Batman: Black and White'' anthology series to debut on December 8, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|author=Adams, Tim|url=https://www.cbr.com/batman-black-and-white-anthology-december-2020/ |title=DC's Batman: Black and White Anthology Series Returns in Late 2020 |date=September 9, 2020 |website=[[CBR.com]] |access-date=September 18, 2020|archivedate=September 9, 2020|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20200918084055/https://www.cbr.com/batman-black-and-white-anthology-december-2020/}}</ref>
