{{main article|2019 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Bianca Andreescu]] won her first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] title, defeating [[Serena Williams]] in the final, 6–3, 7–5 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2019 WTA Tour|2019]] [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2019/sep/07/us-open-women-final-bianca-andreescu-serena-williams-live|title =Bianca Andreescu beats Serena Williams in US Open women's final – as it happened|date=7 September 2019|work=Guardian|accessdate=10 September 2019}}</ref>	
Andreescu became the first [[Canadians|Canadian]] player, as well as the first player born in the 2000s, to win a Grand Slam singles title.<ref name="Andreescu">{{cite web |url=http://baseline.tennis.com/article/84829/bianca-andreescu-serena-williams-us-open-new-york|title=Baseline: The top fast facts from Andreescu's US Open win|author=Nina Pantic|date= 7 September 2019 |accessdate=8 September 2019}}</ref> In addition, Andreescu was the youngest person since [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] in the [[2004 US Open (tennis)|2004 US Open]] to win a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] title, the first teenager to win a Grand Slam singles title since [[Maria Sharapova]] at the [[2006 US Open (tennis)|2006 US Open]] and the first woman to win the US Open on her main draw debut. With the victory, Andreescu entered [[WTA Rankings|the top 5 of the rankings]] for the first time in her career.<ref name="Andreescu" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/tennis/news/32833/11804649/bianca-andreescu-defeats-serena-williams-to-win-us-open-women8217s-singles-title|title= Bianca Andreescu defeats Serena Williams to win US Open women's singles title |author=Mathieu Wood|publisher=[[Sky Sports]]|date=7 September 2019|accessdate=8 September 2019}}</ref>

[[Naomi Osaka]] was the defending champion, but lost to [[Belinda Bencic]] in the fourth round. 

By making the final, Williams set a new record for the longest gap between a player's first and most recent major final appearances, her first being at the [[1999 US Open – Women's Singles|1999 US Open]]. She also equalled the record for most match wins at the US Open (101) held by [[Chris Evert]]. For the fourth time in her career, Williams was attempting to equal [[Margaret Court]]'s [[All-time tennis records – women's singles#Grand Slam singles totals|all-time record of 24 Grand Slam singles titles]] and she was also attempting to win an [[History of tennis|Open Era]] record seventh US Open title.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2019-09-07/2019-09-06_by_the_numbers_us_open_mens_and_womens_singles_finals.html|title=By the numbers: US Open men's and women's singles finals|author=Chris Oddo|date=7 September 2019|accessdate=8 September 2019}}</ref>

Despite losing in the fourth round, [[Ashleigh Barty]] regained the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players#Singles|WTA No. 1 singles ranking]] at the end of the tournament.<ref name="Number">{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/rankings-update-barty-return-no1-major-milestones-us-open-standouts|title= Rankings Update: Barty to return to No.1, major milestones for US Open standouts|publisher=WTA Insider|author=Courtney Nguyen|date=4 September 2019|accessdate=8 September 2019}}</ref> Besides Barty and Osaka, [[Karolína Plíšková]] and [[Simona Halep]] were also in contention for the top ranking at the start of the tournament.<ref name="Number" />

==Championship match ratings==
3.219 million watched on [[ESPN]] in the US.<ref>{{cite web|title=US Open – Women's Singles ratings|url=http://www.showbuzzdaily.com/articles/skedball-weekly-sports-tv-ratings-9-2-9-8-2019.html|website=ShowBuzzDaily|publisher=[[Mitch Metcalf]]|accessdate=10 September 2019}}</ref>

In Canada, preliminary data from [[Numeris]] showed a combined average audience of 3.4 million viewers on [[The Sports Network|TSN]] and [[Réseau des sports|RDS]], in English and French, respectively. Canadian viewership peaked at 5.3 million viewers at 5:59pm during the second set.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bianca's US Open win attracts 3.4M viewers on TSN and RDS|url=https://www.tsn.ca/bianca-andreescu-s-historic-us-open-championship-attracts-record-3-4-million-viewers-on-tsn-and-rds-1.1362975#:~:text=Bianca's%20US%20Open%20win%20attracts%203.4M%20viewers%20on%20TSN%20and%20RDS|website=TSN.ca|publisher=[[TSN.ca Staff]]|accessdate=30 May 2020}}</ref>
