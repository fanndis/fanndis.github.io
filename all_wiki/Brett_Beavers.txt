'''Brett Beavers''' (born in [[Waco, Texas]]), is an American [[country music]] [[songwriter]] and [[record producer|producer]] and the co-author of the book ''Something Worth Leaving Behind''.

==Background==
Beavers attended [[Baylor University]], where he earned a bachelor of science degree in secondary education<ref name="Crossan">{{cite web|url=http://www.songwriteruniverse.com/beavers.htm|title=Bret Beavers Co-Writes & Produces Top Hits For Country Star Dierks Bentley And Other Artists|last=Crossan|first=Jeff|publisher=Songwriter Universe|accessdate=21 September 2010}}</ref> in 1985.<ref name="Rea">{{cite web|url=https://www.nashvillemusicguide.com/nmg_074.htm |title=Producer News From The Row Spotlight on Brett Beavers |last=Rea |first=James |date=October 2009 |work=Nashville Music Guide |accessdate=21 September 2010 }}{{dead link|date=November 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He spent the next four years playing [[bass guitar]] with a small country band throughout [[Central Texas]], honing his skill as a songwriter.<ref name="Rea" /> After a year away from the music business, when he married and taught high school science in [[Tyler, Texas]],<ref name="Rea" /> he moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] to join a band with [[Deryl Dodd]].<ref name="Tucker2003">{{cite book|last=Tucker|first=Susan|title=The secrets of songwriting: leading songwriters reveal how to find inspiration & success|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=d7lVL2X5SJ0C&pg=PA2|accessdate=21 September 2010|date=1 July 2003|publisher=Allworth Communications, Inc.|isbn=978-1-58115-278-4|page=2}}</ref> Shortly after arriving in Nashville, Beavers began touring with [[Martina McBride]] as bass player and bandleader, from 1992–1996, and then with [[Lee Ann Womack]] from 1997–2005 in the same capacity.<ref name="Rea" /> During this time period he started a [[Music publisher (popular music)|publishing company]] and began getting his songs recorded by such artists as [[Tim McGraw]] and [[Billy Ray Cyrus]].<ref name="Tucker2003" /> In 2005, he stopped performing and touring to pursue songwriting and producing on a full-time basis.<ref name="Rea" /> His brother, [[Jim Beavers|Jim]], is also a Nashville songwriter.<ref name="Evans Price">{{cite magazine|last=Evans Price|first=Deborah|date=1 October 2011|title=He's a Business, Man|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|volume=123|issue=35|page=37|issn=0006-2510|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jim-YANOn5AC&q=jim+beavers+brett&pg=RA4-PA37|accessdate=6 May 2016}}</ref>

==Author==
Along with [[Tom Douglas (songwriter)|Tom Douglas]], Beavers wrote the [[Inspirational fiction|inspirational]] book ''Something Worth Leaving Behind'', based on the song of the same name that they co-wrote for Lee Ann Womack.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thomasnelson.com/consumer/product_detail.asp?sku=1401600328&title=&author=Brett_Beavers|title=Something Worth Leaving Behind|publisher=[[Thomas Nelson (publisher)|Thomas Nelson]]|accessdate=21 September 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716144043/http://www.thomasnelson.com/consumer/product_detail.asp?sku=1401600328&title=&author=Brett_Beavers|archive-date=16 July 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>
