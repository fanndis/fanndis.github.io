'''Barbara Pierce Bush''' (born November 25, 1981) is an American activist. She co-founded and is the chair of the board of the non-profit organization [[Global Health Corps]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://ghcorps.org/why-were-here/board-members/ |website=[[Global Health Corps]] official website |title=Board of Directors |access-date=August 7, 2019}}</ref> She and her [[sororal twin|fraternal twin]] sister [[Jenna Bush Hager|Jenna]] are the daughters of the 43rd [[U.S. President]] [[George W. Bush]] and former [[First Lady of the United States|First Lady]] [[Laura Bush]]. She is also a granddaughter of former President [[George H. W. Bush]] and [[Barbara Bush]], after whom she is named.

==Early life==
[[Image:Bush daughters.png|thumb|left|Twins [[Jenna Bush Hager|Jenna]] and Barbara (right) in 1990 with their parents George and Laura]]

Barbara Pierce Bush was born at [[Baylor University Medical Center]] in [[Dallas, Texas]].<ref>{{cite journal|pmc=1200659|title=Neonatal care at Baylor University Medical Center: You've come a long way, baby!|journal=Proceedings (Baylor University. Medical Center)|volume=17|issue=3|pages=251–254|date=July 17, 2004|last=Whitfield|first=Jonathan M.|pmid=16200107}}</ref> When the family lived in the [[Preston Hollow, Dallas, Texas|Preston Hollow]] section of Dallas, she and her twin sister, [[Jenna Bush Hager|Jenna]], attended [[Preston Hollow Elementary School]]; Laura Bush served on Preston Hollow's Parent-Teacher Association at that time.<ref>{{cite news|last=Pulle|first=Matt|title=Split Decision|url=http://www.dallasobserver.com/news/split-decision-6377250|newspaper=[[Dallas Observer]]|date=January 11, 2007|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref> Later, Barbara and Jenna attended [[The Hockaday School]] in Dallas. When her father became [[Governor of Texas]] in 1994, Barbara attended [[St. Andrew's Episcopal School (Texas)|St. Andrew's Episcopal School]] in [[Austin, Texas]]. She began [[Austin High School (Austin, Texas)|Austin High School]] in 1996, graduating with the class of 2000.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://quest.cjonline.com/stories/040400/gen_private.shtml|title=Bush used private school option|agency=[[Associated Press]]|date=April 4, 2000|accessdate=August 22, 2006|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090805010842/http://quest.cjonline.com/stories/040400/gen_private.shtml|archivedate=August 5, 2009}}</ref>

Bush attended [[Yale University]], where she was a legacy member of [[Kappa Alpha Theta]]. She lives in the [[Greenwich Village]] neighborhood of [[New York City]].<ref name=nypost>{{cite news|date=November 9, 2006|work=[[New York Post]] Online Edition|url=http://www.nypost.com/seven/11092006/gossip/pagesix/secure_location_pagesix_.htm|title=Secure location|accessdate=January 24, 2007 |url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070121114302/http://www.nypost.com/seven/11092006/gossip/pagesix/secure_location_pagesix_.htm|archivedate=January 21, 2007}}</ref>

In 2001, Bush was charged with possession of alcohol as a minor.<ref>{{cite web |title=USATODAY.com - Bush daughters cited in Texas |url=https://usatoday30.usatoday.com/news/washington/2001-05-31-bushdaughters.htm |website=usatoday30.usatoday.com}}</ref>

==Political activity==
[[File:Barbara Pierce Bush 2004 RNC P42830-094.jpg|thumb|left|Bush speaking at the 2004 Republican National Convention]]
[[File:Laura and Barbara Bush meet Pope Benedict XVI (cropped).jpg|right|thumb|Bush (left) and her mother in the [[Vatican City]] with [[Pope Benedict XVI]] in 2006]]
In 2011, Bush released a video with the [[Human Rights Campaign]], the nation's largest lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender ([[LGBT]]) civil rights organization, calling on [[New York State]] to legalize [[same-sex marriage]].<ref>[//www.youtube.com/watch?v=-iKqr230U9M Barbara Bush for HRC's NYers for Marriage Equality], HRCMedia on [[YouTube]]</ref><ref name=NYT>{{cite news|last=Barbaro|first=Michael|date=January 31, 2011|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/02/01/us/politics/01bush.html?_r=1&hpw|title=Bush's Daughter, in a Break, Endorses Gay Marriage |newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref> "'I am Barbara Bush, and I am a New Yorker for marriage equality,' she says in the brief message, sponsored by an advocacy group. 'New York is about fairness and equality. And everyone should have the right to marry the person that they love.'"<ref name=NYT/> Bush joined other children of prominent [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] politicians—including [[Meghan McCain]] and [[Mary Cheney]]—in endorsing gay marriage.<ref name=NYT/>

Bush's graduation from Yale in May 2004 was given heavy media coverage. She and Jenna made several media appearances that summer prior to the [[2004 US Presidential election|2004 U.S. Presidential election]], including giving a speech to the [[2004 Republican National Convention|Republican Convention]] on August 31.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A51005-2004Aug31.html|work=The Washington Post|title=Remarks by Barbara and Jenna Bush to the 2004 Republican National Convention|date=August 31, 2004|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref> The two took turns traveling to [[swing state]]s with their father and also gave a seven-page interview and photo shoot in [[Vogue magazine|''Vogue'']].<ref name=vogue>{{cite journal|url=http://www.style.com/vogue/feature/071404/page2.html|title=Jenna and Barbara Bush: Sister Act|author=Julia Reed|journal=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|date=August 2004|url-status=dead|format=&ndash; <sup>[https://scholar.google.co.uk/scholar?hl=en&lr=&q=intitle%3AJenna+and+Barbara+Bush%3A+Sister+Act&as_publication=%5B%5BVogue+%28magazine%29%7CVogue%5D%5D&as_ylo=2004&as_yhi=2004&btnG=Search Scholar search]</sup>|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20051220131812/http://www.style.com/vogue/feature/071404/page2.html |archivedate=December 20, 2005}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[CBS News]]|date=July 16, 2004|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/the-bush-twins-coming-out-party/|title=The Bush Twins' Coming Out Party|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref> Bush joined her mother on diplomatic trips to [[Liberia]] in January 2006 to attend the inauguration of President [[Ellen Johnson Sirleaf]]<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.star-telegram.com/living/family/moms/article3829705.html|author=Sandra Baker|date=December 9, 2011|newspaper=[[Fort Worth Star-Telegram]] |title=Three companies honored as top workplaces for women in Fort Worth|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref> and to [[Vatican City]] to meet with [[Pope Benedict XVI]] in February 2006.

Unlike most of her relatives (but like her twin sister Jenna), Bush is not a member of the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]]. In 2010,  Bush and her sister told ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' that they preferred not to identify with any political party, stating, "We're both very independent thinkers."<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://people.com/archive/the-bush-twins-on-their-own-vol-73-no-19/|title=The Bush Twins On Their Own|author=Sobieraj Westfall, Sandra|magazine=People Magazine|date=May 17, 2010|volume=73|number=19}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/george-w-bushs-daughters-are-not-republicans-2014-9|title=George W. Bush's Daughters Are Not Republicans|author=Campbell, Colin|work=Business Insider|date=September 4, 2014|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2014/sep/5/bush-daughters-decline-republican-label-were-both-/|title=Bush daughters decline Republican label: ‘We’re both very independent’|author=Chumley, Cheryl K.|newspaper=The Washington Times|date=September 5, 2014|accessdate=April 21, 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Bush and her sister authored the joint memoir ''Sisters First: Stories from Our Wild and Wonderful Life'', published in 2017. On October 7, 2018, she married screenwriter Craig Coyne in a private ceremony at the [[Bush compound|Bush family compound]] in [[Kennebunkport, Maine]], with only 20 people attending.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://abc13.com/4440023/|title=Former first daughter Barbara Bush marries Craig Coyne|date=October 8, 2018|website=ABC13 Houston|language=en|access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.today.com/news/barbara-bush-shares-why-her-wedding-was-everything-we-wanted-t140517|title=Barbara Bush shares why her wedding was 'everything we wanted'|website=[[TODAY.com|Today]]|language=en|access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref> They held an additional wedding reception six months later in April 2019 with 100 guests.<ref>{{cite web |work=[[Town & Country (magazine)|Town & Country]] |url=https://www.townandcountrymag.com/society/politics/a27154502/barbara-bush-second-wedding-craig-coyne-george-bush-ranch/ |date=April 15, 2019 |first=Caroline |last=Hallemann |access-date=August 7, 2019 |title=George W. Bush's Daughter Barbara Had a Second Wedding at Her Family's Ranch This Weekend}}</ref>
