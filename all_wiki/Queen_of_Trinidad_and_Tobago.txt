[[Elizabeth II]] was '''Queen of Trinidad and Tobago''' from 1962 to 1976. She was also the [[States headed by Elizabeth II|monarch of other sovereign states]], including the [[United Kingdom]]. The monarch's constitutional roles in [[Dominion of Trinidad and Tobago|Trinidad and Tobago]] were delegated to a [[List of Governors-General of Trinidad and Tobago|Governor-General]], who acted on the advice of Trinidadian ministers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://unctt.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Constitutional-Reform-Forum-Report-2013.pdf|title=National Consultation on Constitutional Reform: Report|author=Trinidad and Tobago Constitution Reform Commission|publisher=Ministry of Legal Affairs|date=27 December 2013|page=6|format=pdf|accessdate=10 November 2015}}</ref>

Trinidad and Tobago became an independent monarchy on 31 August 1962.<ref name=nalis/> In 1974, a constitutional reform commission led by [[Chief Justice of Trinidad and Tobago|Chief Justice]] [[Hugh Wooding]] recommended that the country become a republic, in line with almost universal national opinion.<ref>{{cite book|title=The Constitutional Systems of the Commonwealth Caribbean: A Contextual Analysis|author=Derek O'Brien|date=2014|publisher=Bloomsbury|at=2. The Head of State. Part II: The Crown as Head of State|isbn=978-1-84946-152-8}}</ref> A new constitution was adopted on 1 August 1976, when Trinidad and Tobago became a [[Republics in the Commonwealth of Nations|republic within the Commonwealth]] with the [[President of Trinidad and Tobago]] as [[head of state]].<ref name=nalis>{{cite web|url=http://www.nalis.gov.tt/Research/SubjectGuide/RepublicDay/tabid/250/Default.aspx?PageContentID=346|title=Republic Day|author=Ministry of Public Administration and Information|publisher=National Library of Trinidad and Tobago|accessdate=10 November 2015}}</ref>

Elizabeth II visited Trinidad and Tobago on 7–9 February 1966 and 1–3 November 1985 as part of Caribbean Commonwealth tours,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royal.gov.uk/MonarchAndCommonwealth/QueenandCommonwealth/Commonwealthvisitssince19521.aspx|title=Commonwealth visits since 1952|work=Official website of the British monarchy|publisher=Royal Household|accessdate=10 November 2015}}</ref> and 26–28 November 2009 to attend the [[Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting 2009]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royal.gov.uk/LatestNewsandDiary/Pressreleases/2009/AnnouncementofavisitbyTheQueenandTheDukeofEdinburg.aspx|title=Visit by The Queen and The Duke of Edinburgh to Bermuda, and Trinidad and Tobago|date=2009|type=Press release|publisher=Royal Household|work=Official website of the British monarchy|accessdate=10 November 2015}}</ref>
