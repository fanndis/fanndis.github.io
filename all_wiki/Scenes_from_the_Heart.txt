'''''Scenes from the heart''''' ({{lang-sv|Scener ur hjärtat}}) is a 2018 book by Swedish opera singer [[Malena Ernman]],<ref name="livewire">{{cite web |last1=Lambeck |first1=Petra |title='Scenes From the Heart': Backstory of 16-Year-Old Climate Activist Greta Thunberg |url=https://livewire.thewire.in/politics/greta-thunberg-film-childhood8491/ |website=Live Wire |accessdate=1 July 2019 |ref=livewire}}</ref> her husband [[Svante Thunberg]], and their daughters, [[climate activist]] [[Greta Thunberg]], and Beata Ernman.<ref>{{cite book|title=Scener ur hjärtat|url=http://libris.kb.se/bib/blv1zg9k8m8np5k3|date=2018|accessdate=22 June 2019|isbn=9789177951339|author=Malena Ernman}}</ref><ref name="Scenes from the heart">{{cite book |last1=Ernman |first1=Malena |last2=Thunberg |first2=Svante |last3=Thunberg |first3=Beata |last4=Thunberg |first4=Greta |title=Scenes from the heart |date=2019 |publisher=Particular Books |location=London}}</ref> It consists of three main chapters, divided into several subchapters, and opens with the poem ''[[Elegi (Werner Aspenström poem)|Elegi]]'' from the poetry collection ''[[Ty (poetry collection)|Ty]]'' by [[Werner Aspenström]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Scener ur hjärtat|url=https://www.smakprov.se/bok/pocket/scener-ur-hjartat-9789177951339/|accessdate=22 June 2019|language=Swedish}}</ref> The book is written as an [[autobiography]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Familjen Ernman: Utbrända barn på lika utbränd planet|url=https://www.svd.se/familjen-ernman-utbranda-barn-pa-en-lika-utbrand-planet|publisher=Svenska Dagbladet|date=29 December 2018|accessdate=22 June 2019|issn=1101-2412|language=Swedish|author=Gunilla Kindstrand}}</ref>

The reader follows Malena Ernman and her family as they tour Europe for Ernman's singing career.<ref name="livewire" /> The book details elder daughter Greta's background and family life before she started her [[school strike]] climate campaign, as well as her diagnosis of [[Asperger's syndrome]]. It also addresses Greta's younger sister Beata's [[attention deficit hyperactivity disorder]] (ADHD), and the family's struggle to deal with their daughters' diagnoses.<ref name="livewire" /> In the book, the girls' mother concludes that Asperger's and ADHD are "not a handicap" so much as a "superpower."<ref name="livewire" />

The book has been translated into German. In the original Swedish edition, only Malena Ernman and Svante Thunberg are credited as authors.<ref name=":0" /> Greta has been given top credit in new editions, and her photo is on the cover of the German edition. The book was translated into English by Paul Norlen and [[Saskia Vogel]]<ref name="Penguin Copyright"/> and published under the title '''''Our House Is on Fire: Scenes of a Family and a Planet in Crisis''''' by [[Penguin Books]] on 5{{nbsp}}March 2020.<ref name="livewire" /><ref name="ernman-etal-2020">
