'''Neal Marlens''' (born c. 1955/1956)<ref name=LATimes1988>{{cite newspaper|url= https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1988-11-30-ca-630-story.html|title=TV's '60s: War and Remembrance : Success Turns Into Mixed Blessing for Creators of 'Wonder Years'|date=November 30, 1988|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|first=Diane|last=Haitman|accessdate=July 13, 2020|archivedate=January 27, 2013|archiveurl= http://archive.is/0rTjm|url-status=live|quote=...the husband and wife executive-producing team of Marlens, 32, and Black, 30.}}</ref> is an American television producer and writer. He is known for work on the television series ''[[Growing Pains]]'', ''[[The Wonder Years]]'' and ''[[Ellen (TV series)|Ellen]]'', all with his wife,<ref name=ChicagoTribune1989>{{cite newspaper|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1989-03-16-8903270203-story.html|title='Wonder' Creators Run Out of Yeast|first=Jim |last=Benson|newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]]|date=March 16, 1989|accessdate=July 13, 2020|archivedate=July 13, 2020|archiveurl=http://archive.is/JxLZK |url-status= live|quote=Neal Marlens and Carol Black, the husband-and-wife team who created the popular ABC series ''The Wonder Years'' and now are its writers and executive producers, are about to leave the program to avoid getting burned out, they say, by a demanding work schedule.}}</ref> fellow television producer/writer [[Carol Black (filmmaker)|Carol Black]]

==Early life==
Neal Marlens is one of two sons, with brother Steve, of Al and Hanna Marlens, respectively a ''[[Newsday]]'' managing editor and later an editor at ''[[The New York Times]]'', and a [[Long Island]] school psychologist born in [[Vienna, Austria]], in 1928 and who escaped [[The Holocaust]] by moving first to Cuba and then New York City, and who died in 2008.<ref name=HannaNewsdayObit>{{cite newspaper|url=https://www.newsday.com/long-island/obituary-school-psychologist-hanna-marlens-79-1.880897|title=Obituary: School psychologist Hanna Marlens, 79|first=Rhoda| last=Amon|newspaper=[[Newsday]]|location= New York City / Long Island|date=February 20, 2008|accessdate=July 13, 2020|archivedate=July 13, 2020|archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20200713155411/https://www.newsday.com/long-island/obituary-school-psychologist-hanna-marlens-79-1.880897|url-status=live}}</ref> Neal Marlens was raised in the Audubon Woods section of [[Huntington, New York]], and graduated from Stimson Junior High and [[Walt Whitman High School (New York)|Walt Whitman High School]], both in nearby [[Huntington Station, New York]].Neal attended Swarthmore College in the late 1970's where he competed successfully on the men's tennis team. He added good humor and a friendly personality to a campus that sometimes lacked both.<ref name=Newsday5Connections>{{cite newspaper|url= https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/tv/the-wonder-years-long-island-1.16427670|title=5 LI connections in 'The Wonder Years'|first=Andy|last=Edelstein|newspaper=Newsday|location= New York City / Long Island|date=January 29, 2018|accessdate=July 13, 2020|archivedate=January 30, 2018|archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20180130033850/https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/tv/the-wonder-years-long-island-1.16427670|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Awards==
Marlens won a 1988 [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series]] for ''The Wonder Years'', as well as an additional nomination in that category for 1989, and for comedy-series writing in 1988.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.emmys.com/bios/neal-marlens|title=Neal Marlens|publisher=[[Academy of Television Arts & Sciences|Television Academy]]|accessdate=July 13, 2020 |archivedate=May 12, 2019|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190512145730/https://www.emmys.com/bios/neal-marlens|url-status=live}}</ref>
