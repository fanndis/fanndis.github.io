{{main article|2013 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Serena Williams]] was the defending champion, and she successfully defended her title, defeating [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the final, 7–5, 6–7<sup>(6–8)</sup>, 6–1, to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2013 US Open (tennis)|2013 US Open]] in a rematch of the previous year's final. Just like the previous year, Williams only dropped one set the entire tournament, to Azarenka in the final. By winning the tournament, Williams won her fifth US open crown and her seventeenth grand slam singles title.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/sep/09/serena-williams-victoria-azarenka-us-open-final1|title=Serena Williams crowns 14-year reign with fifth US Open title|date=8 September 2013|work=Guardian UK|accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/usopen/10295021/Serena-Williams-wins-17th-grand-slam-title-with-epic-US-Open-victory-over-Victoria-Azarenka.html|title=Serena Williams wins 17th grand slam title with epic US Open victory over Victoria Azarenka|date=8 September 2013|work=Daily Telegraph|accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref>

This was the last Grand Slam main draw appearance for former World No. 3 and two-time French Open semifinalist [[Nadia Petrova]], who lost to qualifier [[Julia Glushko]] in the first round.
