The '''Super Bowl LIV halftime show''', which is officially known as the '''Pepsi Super Bowl LIV Halftime Show''', took place on February 2, 2020, at [[Hard Rock Stadium]] in [[Miami Gardens, Florida]], as part of [[Super Bowl LIV]]. It was televised in the U.S. by [[Fox NFL|Fox]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nbcsports.com/washington/other-sports/beyond-scoreboard-super-bowl-halftime-performers-announced-plus-nhl-social-media |last=Horrow |first=Rick |last2=Simkins |first2=Tanner |date=October 4, 2019 |title='Beyond the Scoreboard': Super Bowl halftime performers announced, plus NHl social media development |work=[[NBC Sports Washington]] |accessdate=December 18, 2019}}</ref> It was co-headlined by [[Shakira]] and [[Jennifer Lopez]], and included guest appearances by [[Bad Bunny]], [[J Balvin]], and Lopez's daughter Emme Muñiz.<ref name="SBLIVHalftime">{{cite news|title=Jennifer Lopez, Shakira to perform at Pepsi SB halftime show|url=http://www.nfl.com/news/story/0ap3000001059129/article/jennifer-lopez-shakira-to-perform-at-pepsi-sb-halftime-show|publisher=NFL Enterprises|website=NFL.com|date=September 26, 2019|access-date=September 27, 2019}}</ref><ref name=usatoday1>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/music/2019/09/26/super-bowl-jennifer-lopez-shakira-perform-2020-halftime-show/3778236002/ |last=Reed |first=Anika |date=September 26, 2019 |title=Jennifer Lopez, Shakira to perform at 2020 Super Bowl halftime show |work=[[USA Today]] |accessdate=December 18, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/entertainment|title=Entertainment News - Celebrities, Movies, TV, Music|website=CNN|access-date=January 31, 2020}}</ref>

==Background==
In August 2019, it was announced [[Jay-Z]]'s company [[Roc Nation]] had entered a deal with the NFL for Jay-Z and his company to produce the halftime show.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Crucchiola |first1=Jordan |title=An NFL Partnership Makes Jay-Z a Producer for the Super Bowl Halftime Show |url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/08/jay-z-signs-nfl-deal-super-bowl-halftime-show.html |website=Vulture |accessdate=February 1, 2020 |language=en-us |date=August 13, 2019}}</ref> 

On September 26, 2019, the selection of Shakira and Jennifer Lopez to headline the show was announced;<ref name=usatoday1/> according to reports, their selection was intended to reflect the [[Latin American culture|Latin]] culture of the host city Miami.<ref name=billboard1>{{cite web |last1=Flores |first1=Griselda |title=Inside This Year's Super Bowl Halftime Show |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/super-bowl/8549652/jennifer-lopez-shakira-super-bowl-halftime-show-preview |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=February 2, 2020 |date=January 31, 2020}}</ref> This was the third time [[Latin music]] artists headlined the Super Bowl halftime show; [[Gloria Estefan]] headlined both the [[Super Bowl XXVI#Halftime show|Super Bowl XXVI halftime show]] in 1992 and the [[Super Bowl XXXIII#Halftime show|Super Bowl XXXIII halftime show]] in 1999.<ref name=billboard1/> For months, rumors Lopez might be chosen as a halftime show act had circulated; she had expressed her interest in doing so in July 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bueno |first1=Antoinette |title=Jennifer Lopez Talks Possibility of Playing the 2020 Super Bowl |url=https://www.etonline.com/jennifer-lopez-talks-possibility-of-playing-the-2020-super-bowl-halftime-show-exclusive-128646 |website=[[Entertainment Tonight]] |accessdate=February 2, 2020 |date=July 16, 2019}}</ref>

==Development== 
[[File:200127-H-PX819-0092.jpg|thumb|left|[[Hard Rock Stadium]]]]
[[Ricky Kirshner]] served as the show's executive producer.<ref name=detroitsportsnation>{{cite web |title=Super Bowl LIV Halftime Show performers revealed |url=https://detroitsportsnation.com/super-bowl-liv-halftime-show-performers-revealed/ddrysdale/detroit-lions-news/02/02/2020/218666/ |publisher=Detroit Sports Nation |accessdate=7 February 2020 |date=2 February 2020}}</ref><ref name="livedesign">{{cite web |title=Super Bowl LIV Halftime Show Credits List |url=https://www.livedesignonline.com/concerts/super-bowl-liv-halftime-show-credits-list |publisher=Live Design |accessdate=7 February 2020 |language=en |date=6 February 2020}}</ref> Roc Nation served as producers and creative directors of the show.<ref name=detroitsportsnation/> Roc Nation's producers included Dan Parise,<ref name="reuters13">{{cite web |last1=Brown |first1=Nick |title=13 minutes, $13 million: The logistics of pulling off a Super Bowl halftime show |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-football-nfl-superbowl-halftime/13-minutes-13-million-the-logistics-of-pulling-off-a-super-bowl-halftime-show-idUSKBN1ZV3TC |website=[[Reuters]] |accessdate=February 1, 2020 |language=en |date=February 1, 2020}}</ref> and [[Hamish Hamilton (director)|Hamish Hamilton]] served as director.<ref name=detroitsportsnation/><ref name="livedesign"/>

[[Adam Blackstone]], the musical director of the show noted that the show intends to cement what both women represent. Calling Shakira a "global phenomenon" he says he had to think about the singer's global fan base. For Jennifer, he says the intentions were to represent her as an icon in acting, music, and dance. His vision and mission were to make the two acts feel cohesive and a part of the same show.<ref>{{cite web |title=Musical Director Adam Blackstone Discusses His Historic Work On the Shakira/J. Lo Super Bowl Halftime Show|url=https://www.awardsdaily.com/2020/08/19/musical-director-adam-blackstone-discusses-his-historic-work-on-the-shakira-j-lo-super-bowl-halftime-show/|accessdate=19 August 2020 |language=en |date=19 August 2020}}</ref>

[[Tabitha and Napoleon D'umo]] served as the creative directors and choreographers for Lopez's section of the show,<ref name="livedesign"/><ref name="ET 1">{{cite web |last1=Murphy |first1=Desiree |title=Jennifer Lopez's Creative Directors Tease What to Expect From 'Epic' Super Bowl Halftime Show (Exclusive) |url=https://www.etonline.com/jennifer-lopezs-creative-directors-tease-what-to-expect-from-epic-super-bowl-halftime-show |website=[[Entertainment Tonight]] |accessdate=January 29, 2020 |date=January 28, 2020}}</ref> which included approximately 130 dancers.<ref name="ET 1"/> which was choreographed by Tabitha D'umo and [[Parris Goebel]].<ref name="livedesign"/><ref name="ET 1" /> Jaquel Knight, Nadine Eliya, and Maite Marcos were the creative directors and choreographers of Shakira's section of the show.<ref name="livedesign"/> In mid-January, the producers announced they would recruit approximately 600 field team members to assist with the performance.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Canty |first1=Johari |title=600 team members needed to help with Super Bowl Halftime show |url=https://wsvn.com/entertainment/600-team-members-needed-to-help-with-super-bowl-halftime-show/ |publisher=[[WSVN]] |accessdate=February 1, 2020 |language=en |date=January 16, 2020}}</ref> Ultimately, 670 team field members were enlisted.<ref name="livedesign"/> 

Among the equipment used for the show were 565 ROE CB5 tiles, 8 Brompton SX40 4K LED processors, 22 Brompton Tessera XD data distro boxes using 8x optical fiber runs, 4 4K video feeds—two main and two for backup—and 8 LED techs.<ref name="livedesign"/> The [[pyrotechnics]] for the show used 5,350 pieces of product, which were launched from 32 roof locations and 16 special locations, using 80 firing modules. A FireOne control system was used.<ref name="livedesign2">{{cite web |title=Super Bowl LIV Halftime Show Pyro Lights The Sky Over Miami |url=https://www.livedesignonline.com/concerts/super-bowl-liv-halftime-show-pyro-lights-sky-over-miami |publisher=Live Design |accessdate=February 7, 2020 |language=en |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> Twenty-four 30W Arctos Systems lasers were used in the show.<ref name="livedesign2"/>

===Fashion=== 
Shakira's set had three costume changes; each piece was custom-made by Norwegian designer Peter Dundas.<ref name=hrdundas>{{cite web |last1=Gracie |first1=Bianca |title=Super Bowl: All the Details on Shakira's Peter Dundas Outfits |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/super-bowl-all-details-shakiras-peter-dundas-halftime-outfits-1276047 |website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |accessdate=February 6, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> Her first costume was a red, cropped [[bustier]] with a cross-straps top that was paired with a removable corset and fringed skirt, each of which were covered with 123,000 [[Swarovski]] crystals in three shades of red.<ref name=hrdundas/><ref name=latimesfashion>{{cite web |last1=Tschorn |first1=Adam |title=Super Bowl fashion: A rundown of those standout looks from the festive halftime show |url=https://www.latimes.com/lifestyle/story/2020-02-03/super-bowl-fashion-rundown-standout-looks-festive-halftime-show |website=Los Angeles Times |accessdate=February 6, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> This was paired with crystal-accented leather cuffs and knee-high boots that were made by the designer Daniel Jacob.<ref name=latimesfashion/> The boots were covered with 30,000 Swarovski crystals and took ten days to create.<ref name=hrdundas/> Shakira then made a mid-performance costume change to reveal a fringed, feathered skirt that was previously hidden under a [[sarong]].<ref name=hrdundas/> Her final costume was an entirely new ensemble consisting of a bomber jacket covered in gold [[sequin]] embroidery and gold-and-white Swarovski crystals on a matching gold sequin [[crop top]], which she wore with matching high-waisted [[hot pants]] and Dundas-customized [[Adidas Superstar]] sneakers.<ref name=latimesfashion/> 

Lopez's stylists were Rob Zangardi and Mariel Haenn, her makeup artist was Scott Barnes, and her hairstylist was Chris Appleton.<ref name="Vogue J.Lo" /> Her set involved five costume changes; all of her costumes and those of her dancers were custom-designed by [[Versace]] and approved by [[Donatella Versace]].<ref name="Vogue J.Lo">{{cite web |last1=Okwodu |first1=Janelle |title=The Story Behind Jennifer Lopez’s Epic Versace Super Bowl Wardrobe |url=https://www.vogue.com/vogueworld/article/jennifer-lopez-superbowl-2020-versace-wardrobe-rob-and-mariel-interview |website=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]] |accessdate=February 6, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> Lopez was the face of Versace's Spring 2020 campaign.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Calvario |first1=Liz |title=Jennifer Lopez is Jaw-Dropping in New Versace Campaign -- See the Pics |url=https://www.etonline.com/jennifer-lopez-is-jaw-dropping-in-new-versace-campaign-see-the-sizzling-pics-139302 |website=Entertainment Tonight |accessdate=February 6, 2020 |date=January 11, 2020}}</ref> Haenn noted; "Every look has to be layered one on top of the other because she never really leaves the stage".<ref name="Vogue J.Lo" /> Lopez's first outfit was a Swarovski crystal-and-gold-studded, biker-inspired black leather Medusa [[bodysuit]] paired with a pink satin ballgown skirt.<ref name=latimesfashion/> Her backup dancers also wore black leather biker jackets paired with pleated miniskirts that were trimmed with black lace.<ref name=latimesfashion/> Lopez's skirt was torn off to reveal black [[leather]] [[chaps]].<ref name=latimesfashion/> Lopez later took off this outfit to reveal a custom-made body-hugging silver catsuit that was coated in crystals and tiny mirrored panels.<ref name=latimesfashion/> She next changed into a metal-mesh-and-crystal-fringed bustier with bondage-harness details, which was topped by a feather cape around her shoulders that depicted the [[Flag of the United States|U.S. flag]] on the outside and the [[Flag of Puerto Rico|Puerto Rican flag]] on the inside.<ref name=latimesfashion/> Lopez later revealed that the Puerto Rican flag's inclusion in the show was a secret, "The flag — I didn't even show it to anybody until the last minute because I didn't want anybody telling me I couldn't do it", adding: "I just had the American flag on the outside and during rehearsals".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rice |first1=Nicholas |title=Jennifer Lopez Reveals Behind-the-Scenes Secrets from Her 2020 Super Bowl Halftime Show |url=https://people.com/music/jennifer-lopez-shares-secrets-from-2020-super-bowl-halftime-show/ |website=[[People (magazine)|People]] |accessdate=October 16, 2020 |date=October 15, 2020}}</ref>

[[Shakira]], standing on a platform on the stage and wearing a "tiny red sequin dress", opened the show dancing "[[Dare (La La La)]]".<ref name="Billboard Analysis">{{cite web |last1=Cobo |first1=Leila |title=Jennifer Lopez & Shakira Get the Whole Country Dancing With First All-Latin Super Bowl Halftime Show: Analysis |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/super-bowl/8549894/jennifer-lopez-shakira-super-bowl-54-halftime-show-analysis-recap |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 2, 2020}}</ref> She started her set with a performance of "[[She Wolf (Shakira song)|She Wolf]]", surrounded by a group of dancers, the segued into a performance of "[[Empire (Shakira song)|Empire]]", during which she played an electric guitar and included elements of "[[Inevitable (Shakira song)|Inevitable]]" and [[Led Zeppelin]]'s "[[Kashmir (song)|Kashmir]]".<ref name="Billboard Analysis" /> She then wrapped a rope around her wrists, sang part of the first verse of "[[Ojos Así]]", and then did a [[Belly dance|belly dancing]] using part of "Move" by Said Mrad with added strings.<ref name="CNN 1">{{cite web |last1=Respers France |first1=Lisa |title=Jennifer Lopez and Shakira's halftime show was deeper than you thought |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2020/02/02/entertainment/jennifer-lopez-shakira-super-bowl-half-time/index.html |website=[[CNN]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> This was followed by a performance of "[[Whenever, Wherever]]",<ref name="Billboard Analysis" /> after which guest artist [[Bad Bunny]] joined her on stage. Shakira and Bad Bunny performed a [[cover version|cover]] of his song "[[I Like It (Cardi B, Bad Bunny and J Balvin song)|I Like It]]" then they sang a mash up of Shakira's "[[Chantaje]]" and Bunny's "[[Callaíta]]" together.<ref name="Time Recap">{{cite web |last1=Bruner |first1=Raisa |title=The Biggest Moments of the Super Bowl 2020 Halftime Show |url=https://time.com/5774180/super-bowl-2020-halftime-show-recap/ |website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> In the final section of Shakira's set, she performed "[[Hips Don't Lie]]", during which she [[Crowd surfing|crowd surfed]],<ref name="Time Recap" /> danced [[mapalé]], and wagged her tongue in an Arabic tradition known as [[Ululation|zaghrouta]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Respers France |first1=Lisa |title=Shakira's tongue was the meme of Super Bowl night |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2020/02/03/entertainment/shakira-tongue-meme-super-bowl/index.html |website=[[CNN]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref>

[[Jennifer Lopez]] then appeared for the beginning of her set wearing a black leather bodysuit.<ref name="Time Recap" /> After rising from a "skyscraper-turned-stripper pole",<ref name="Rolling Stone Recap">{{cite web |last1=Exposito |first1=Suzy |title=Let’s Get Loud: Jennifer Lopez, Shakira Power Through Cultural Minefield During Super Bowl Halftime Show |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/super-bowl-2020-halftime-jennifer-lopez-shakira-946734/ |website=[[Rolling Stone]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 2, 2020}}</ref> she performed "[[Jenny from the Block]]" then "[[Ain't It Funny (Murder Remix)]]", while backed by dancers.<ref name="Billboard Analysis" /> Following a performance of "[[Get Right (Jennifer Lopez song)|Get Right]]", Lopez changed her costume for a "sparkling" catsuit and sang "[[Waiting for Tonight]]" while [[Pole dance|pole dancing]];<ref name="Time Recap" /> her pole-dance routine included a "tabletop move with the dancers writhing underneath".<ref name="Billboard Analysis" /> She was then joined on stage by [[J Balvin]], who performed "[[Que Calor]]" and "[[Mi Gente (J Balvin and Willy William song)|Mi Gente]]", which were [[Mashup (music)|mashed up]] with Lopez's songs "[[Booty (song)|Booty]]", "[[El Anillo]]" and "[[Love Don't Cost a Thing (song)|Love Don't Cost a Thing]]" as she danced above.<ref name="Rolling Stone Recap" /><ref name="Billboard Analysis" /> Lopez then performed "[[On the Floor]]".

Shakira returned to play drums while Lopez's daughter Emme Muñiz appeared on stage to sing "[[Let's Get Loud]]" with an excerpt from [[Bruce Springsteen]]'s "[[Born in the U.S.A. (song)|Born in the U.S.A.]]" as Lopez was in front of an all-female choir.<ref name="Rolling Stone Recap" /> During this section of Lopez's set, she wore a feather cape depicting both the Puerto Rican and American flags<ref name="Rolling Stone Recap" /> while children on the field appeared in "cage like structures",<ref name="CNN 1" /> which was interpreted as a statement on the [[U.S.-Mexico border crisis]].<ref name="CNN 1" /> 

Shakira, who was now clothed in a "short golden outfit", subsequently performed "[[Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)]]" during a dance routine of [[champeta]].<ref name="Billboard Analysis" /> Lopez then returned to the stage to perform "Aguanile" by [[Héctor Lavoe]] as a "[[Las Vegas|Vegas]]-style salsa number" and was joined by Colombian salsa dance group [[Swing Latino]].<ref name="Billboard Analysis" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Valera |first1=Willy |title=Shakira y J.Lo: un Super Bowl con sabor currambero |url=https://www.elheraldo.co/entretenimiento/shakira-y-jlo-un-super-bowl-con-sabor-currambero-698604 |website=El Heraldo |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |language=es |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> Both of the headlining acts ended their performance with "synchronized shimmies".<ref name="Time Recap" />

The halftime show was critically acclaimed. [[Jon Pareles]] of ''[[The New York Times]]'' gave the performance a positive review, calling it an improvement over the [[Super Bowl LIII halftime show|previous year{{'}}s halftime]]. He called the show "a no-nonsense affirmation of Latin pride and cultural diversity in a political climate where immigrants and American Latinos have been widely demonized", called Shakira and Lopez "Latina superwomen, smiling pop conquistadoras backed by phalanxes of dancers", and calling the show "euphoria with a purpose".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pareles |first1=Jon |title=Jennifer Lopez and Shakira Restore Sparkle to Super Bowl Halftime |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/02/02/arts/music/super-bowl-halftime-review.html |website=[[The New York Times]] |accessdate=February 3, 2020 |date=February 2, 2020}}</ref> David Bauder of the [[Associated Press]] wrote Lopez and Shakira "infused the Super Bowl halftime show with an exuberance and joy that celebrated their Latina heritage". Bauder also said, "Their breathless athleticism matched that of the football players waiting in the locker room" but called J Balvin's and Bad Bunny's guest appearances "superfluous".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bajuder |first1=David |title=Jennifer Lopez, Shakira in joyful, exuberant Super Bowl LIV halftime show |url=https://www.denverpost.com/2020/02/02/jennifer-lopez-shakira-super-bowl-liv-halftime-show-review/ |website=[[The Denver Post]] |agency=The Associated Press |accessdate=February 3, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> [[Greg Kot]] of the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' wrote, "[[Prince (musician)|Prince]]'s [[Super Bowl XLI halftime show|2007 Super Bowl performance]] is still king of halftime, but Shakira and J Lo were certainly the most undeniable performance since then".<ref name=gkchitribreview>{{cite web |last1=Kot |first1=Greg |title=Super Bowl music review: Jennifer Lopez, Shakira conquer, make it Latin pop night in America |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/sc-ent-super-bowl-music-jennifer-lopez-shakira-0202-20200202-aygcoihh5zgdxhplv7w6uta4n4-story.html |website=[[Chicago Tribune]] |accessdate=February 3, 2020 |date=February 2, 2020}}</ref> Bobby Olivier of [[NJ.com]] described the halftime show as "high-octane, relentless Latin pop fun", adding it was "a blazing performance that should challenge [[Lady Gaga]] and [[Beyoncé]] as some of the best halftime sets of the past decade or so".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Olivier |first1=Bobby |title=Super Bowl 2020 halftime show review: JLo and Shakira let loose in blazing performance |url=https://www.nj.com/entertainment/2020/02/super-bowl-2020-halftime-show-review-jlo-and-shakira-let-loose-in-blazing-performance.html |publisher=NJ.com |language=en |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref>

==Commercial reception==
In the United States, the Super Bowl LIV halftime show attracted 103 million viewers, an increase of 4% on [[Super Bowl LIII halftime show|the previous year]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Andreeva |first1=Nellie |last2=Hipes |first2=Patrick |title=Super Bowl Ratings: Viewership For Game, Halftime Show, Lead-Out ‘Masked Singer’ Tick Up – Update |url=https://deadline.com/2020/02/super-bowl-2020-ratings-fox-the-masked-singer-season-3-premiere-1202849673/ |website=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Thorne |first1=Will |title=Jennifer Lopez and Shakira Provide Super Bowl Halftime Show Ratings Boost |url=https://variety.com/2020/tv/news/jennifer-lopez-shakira-super-bowl-halftime-show-ratings-boost-1203491139/ |website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> The viewership for the halftime show was slightly higher than the game itself, which was viewed by an average of 99.9 million television viewers.<ref>{{cite web |title=Here's How Many People Tuned In for Jennifer Lopez & Shakira's Super Bowl Halftime |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/super-bowl/8550023/super-bowl-tv-ratings-jennifer-lopez-shakira-halftime-show |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> Social media activity relating to the show showed a significant spike; over one million [[Twitter|tweets]] mentioned Lopez and Shakira, a 431% increase from the previous year's 127,576 mentions of [[Maroon 5]].<ref name="Forbes Twitter">{{cite web |last1=Berg |first1=Madeline |title=Jennifer Lopez and Shakira Provide Super Bowl Halftime Show Ratings Boost |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/maddieberg/2020/02/02/super-bowl-halftime-show-2019-twitter-reacts-to-jennifer-lopez/#297a9ce6cc2e |website=[[Forbes]] |accessdate=February 4, 2020 |date=February 2, 2020}}</ref> Of the 1,114,545 tweets mentioning the halftime show, 69% of them expressed a positive sentiment; 38% of tweets expressed joy and 8% expressed love.<ref name="Forbes Twitter" /> Shakira's name generated over 2.6 million tweets which accumulated more tweets than the Super Bowl as a whole (1.85 million) placing her at number one on trending topics.<ref>{{cite web|title=Shakira and Jennifer Lopez deliver Super Bowl half-time performance packed with Latino and Arabic influences|url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2020-02-03/shakira-jennifer-lopez-super-bowl-half-time-performance/11924858|website=[[ABC News (Australia)]] |accessdate=October 26, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> Shakira's use of [[Ululation|zaghrouta]] during the show resulted in a viral [[internet meme]].<ref>https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/02/9345832/shakira-tongue-meme-super-bowl-2020</ref>

Both Lopez's and Shakira's music experienced increased sales.<ref name="billboardsales">{{cite web |last1=Caulfield |first1=Keith |title=Jennifer Lopez & Shakira's Super Bowl Halftime Show Yields Big Sales Gains |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8549942/super-bowl-2020-halftime-show-jennifer-lopez-shakira-gains |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=February 3, 2020 |date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> According to an initial sales report by [[Nielsen Corporation|Nielsen]] Music, the songs performed in the show experienced a 1,013% sales increase in the United States on February 2, increasing to 16,000 digital downloads—up from just over 1,000 the previous day.<ref name=billboardsales/> According to [[Alpha Data]], the spike in songs sales was 1,000% and the songs performed in the show increased 1,374% in sales over the previous day, selling more than 17,000 copies.<ref name="rolingstonesales1">{{cite web |last1=Blake |first1=Emily |title=Shakira's 'She Wolf' Won the Super Bowl |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/shakira-jennifer-lopez-super-bowl-streams-sales-948049/ |publisher=Rolling Stone |accessdate=6 February 2020 |date=5 February 2020}}</ref> According to [[Nielsen Music]], of the songs performed in the Super Bowl LIV halftime show, "Whenever, Wherever" was the best-selling, garnering 4,000 downloads, a 1,194% increase over the prior day. The next-best-selling song was "Hips Don't Lie", which garnered 2,000 downloads, an increase 1,126% over the previous day.<ref name=billboardsales/> Alpha Data also said "Whenever, Wherever" was the biggest-selling of the show's songs in sales on the day of the performance, with 4,000 downloads, a 1,349% increase over the previous day.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/> Alpha Data also found in regards to all industry-wide song sales on the day of the performance, "Wherever, Whenever" was the third-best-selling song; "Hips Don't Lie" was the fourth-best selling song; and "Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)" was the tenth-best-selling song.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/> Alpha Data reported "She Wolf" had a 3,000% increase in sales on the day of the show, the greatest percentage increase of any of the songs performed.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/>  It also reported Lopez saw 6,000 in sales of her songs that were included in the show on the day of the performance.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/> "On the Floor" sold 1,600 copies on the day of the performance.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/>

The artists' music also experienced a spike on [[Streaming media|streaming]] platforms.<ref name=marketwatch1>{{cite web |last1=Pesce |first1=Nicole Lyn |title=Jennifer Lopez and Shakira’s music sales spike after their viral Super Bowl halftime show |url=https://www.marketwatch.com/story/jennifer-lopez-and-shakiras-music-sales-spike-after-their-viral-super-bowl-halftime-show-2020-02-03 |publisher=MarketWatch |accessdate=6 February 2020 |date=4 February 2020}}</ref> Amazon reported [[Amazon Alexa]] requests for Lopez's music on [[Amazon Music]] rose 426% and that requests for Shakira rose 303%.<ref name=marketwatch1/> Compared with the previous Sunday, streams of Lopez's tracks on Amazon Music rose 432% while streams of Shakira's tracks rose 150%.<ref name=marketwatch1/> On [[Spotify]], streams for Lopez’s music rose more than 335%, and Shakira’s music rose by approximately 230%.<ref name=marketwatch1/> The song with the greatest increase in streams on Spotify was Shakira's "Empire", with an increase of 2,135%.<ref name=marketwatch1/> Alpha Data found that "Hips Don't Lie was the most streamed of the songs included in the show, with 1.8 million on-demand audio streams on the day of and the day following the performance.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/> "She Wolf" saw a 500% increase in sales in the 24 hours after the show.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/> Alpha Data reported that Lopez's "On the Floor" was streamed 830,000 within 24 hours of the show.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/> As for overall streams [[Billboard (magazine)| Billboard]] reported that the song catalogs of the two performers earned 31.1 million on-demand streams (audio and video combined) on Feb. 2 and Feb. 3 -- up 193% as compared to their streaming sum on Jan. 31 and Feb. 1 (10.6 million). Lopez’s songs amassed 10.1 million streams on Feb. 2-3, up 149% versus the 4.06 million they collected on Jan. 31 and  Feb. 1. Shakira's songs earned 20.98 million streams on Feb. 2 and Feb. 3, up 221% as compared to the 6.53 million they earned on Jan. 31 and Feb. 1.<ref>https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/news/super-bowl/8550364/jennifer-lopez-shakira-streaming-bump-super-bowl-halftime-show</ref>

''Rolling Stone'' and ''[[Forbes]]'' reported after the broadcast, Shakira's catalogue benefited the most across music charts.<ref name="rolingstonesales1"/><ref>https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2020/02/13/shakira-is-the-real-super-bowl-winner-at-least-when-it-comes-to-the-charts/#a8c0375495da</ref> The official video of the show on [[YouTube]] had 30 million views in its first 24 hours, entering the top 20 of most viewed videos and topping YouTube's "trending" charts in many countries. Within four days, it had over 100 million views and became the seventh-fastest-growing music-related video of all time, and also became the most-watched Super Bowl halftime performance of all time on Youtube, overtaking the [[Super Bowl 50 halftime show]], which had 82 million views.<ref name=marketwatch1/> As of June 2020, the performance has accumulated more than 170 million views.<ref>https://www.infobae.com/america/colombia/2020/02/09/champeta-el-dialogo-entre-africa-y-el-caribe-que-shakira-le-mostro-al-mundo-en-el-super-bowl/</ref>
