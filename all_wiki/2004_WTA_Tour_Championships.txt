The '''2004 [[WTA Tour Championships]]''' was a women's round robin [[tennis]] tournament played on indoor hard courts at the [[Staples Center]] in [[Los Angeles]], United States. It was the 34th edition of the year-end singles championships, the 29th edition of the year-end doubles championships, and was part of the [[2004 WTA Tour]]. The tournament was held between November 10 and November 15, 2004. First-seeded [[Maria Sharapova]] won the singles event, the first, and so far only, Russian to win the tournament, and earned $1,000,000 first-prize money as well as 485 ranking points.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sharapova Edges S. Williams|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A52522-2004Nov15.html|work=The Washington Post|date=November 16, 2004|page=D02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author1=Paul Levine|title=Sharapova defeats Serena in WTA Tour Championship final|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/espn/wire?section=tennis&id=1923738|website=sports.espn.go.com|publisher=ESPN|date=December 8, 2004}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sharapova wins season's finale|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/4015111.stm|website=news.bbc.co.uk|publisher=BBC|date=November 16, 2004}}</ref>

[[Justine Henin-Hardenne]] had qualified for the tournament but withdrew due to illness ([[cytomegalovirus]]).<ref>{{cite web|title=Henin-Hardenne's season ends because of virus|url=http://articles.philly.com/2004-10-12/sports/25388926_1_field-hockey-energy-sapping-virus-rodrigo-pessoa|website=articles.philly.com|publisher=Philly.com|date=October 12, 2004}}</ref>
