'''Muhammad Hatta Rajasa''' (born 18 December 1953 in [[Palembang]], [[South Sumatra]]) was the former Coordinating Minister for Economic Affairs in the [[Second United Indonesia Cabinet]] and the vice presidential candidate with [[Prabowo Subianto]] in the [[2014 Indonesian presidential election]].<ref name="thejakartapost.com">{{Cite web|title=Prabowo-Hatta eyes education reform, HR quality|url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2014/05/22/prabowo-hatta-eyes-education-reform-hr-quality.html|access-date=2020-11-09|website=The Jakarta Post|language=en}}</ref> Previously, he was the [[State Secretary#Indonesia|State Secretary]], Minister of Transport, and Minister for Research and Technology in the [[Mutual Assistance Cabinet]] (2001-2004).<ref>{{Cite news |title= United Indonesia Cabinet 2009-2014|newspaper= [[The Jakarta Post]]|date= 2009-10-22|page= 3}}</ref><ref name="SIMANJUNTAK5254">Simanjuntak (2003) p 463</ref>

==Personal life==
Hatta Rajasa is a [[Malay people|Palembang Malay]]. Siti Ruby Aliya Rajasa, Hatta's daughter, is married to the youngest son of [[Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono]], Edhie "Ibas" Baskoro.<ref name=tele-uk-wedding>{{cite news |last1=Pearlman|first1=Jonathan |title=Indonesian wedding sets the course of presidential succession |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/asia/indonesia/8914776/Indonesian-wedding-sets-the-course-of-presidential-succession.html |accessdate=April 15, 2015 |work=[[The Telegraph (UK)|The Telegraph]] |publisher=[[Telegraph Media Group Limited]] |date=November 25, 2011}}</ref><ref name=jp />

==Politics==
He is member of the [[National Mandate Party]] (PAN),<ref name=jp>{{Cite web
  | last=Simamora
  | first=Adianto P.
  | title=Ibas and Aliya get engaged
  | publisher=[[The Jakarta Post]]
  | date=April 27, 2011
  | url=http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2011/04/27/ibas-and-aliya-get-engaged.html}}</ref> a moderate Islamic party. Since 2010 he has been chairman of the party.<ref name=jp />

On May 19, 2014, Hatta registered to run for vice president with [[Prabowo Subianto]] running for president in the election on July 9, 2014. The two were supported by 6 parties – PAN, [[Great Indonesia Movement Party]] (Gerindra),  [[Prosperous Justice Party]] (PKS), [[United Development Party]] (PPP), [[Crescent Star Party (Indonesia)|Crescent Star Party]] (PBB) and [[Golkar]].<ref name="thejakartapost.com"/>

==Education==
* [[Bandung Technological Institute]] – Petroleum Engineering

==Career==
*2009-2014: Coordinating Minister for Economic Affairs
*2004-2009: Minister of Transport ([[United Indonesia Cabinet]])
*2001-2004: Minister of Research and Technology ([[Mutual Assistance Cabinet]])
*2000-current: Secretary General of [[National Mandate Party]]
*1999-2000: Chairman of Reformation Faction on People's Representative Council.
*1982-2000: President Director Arthindo
*1980-1983: Vice-Technical Manager PT. Meta Epsi
*1977-1978: Field Technician PT. Bina Patra Jaya
