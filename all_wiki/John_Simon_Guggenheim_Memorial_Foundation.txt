The '''John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation''' was founded in 1925<ref name="bloomberg">{{cite web|url = http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=4932668 | accessdate = 18 November 2014 | publisher = [[Bloomberg Businessweek]] | title = Company Overview of John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation}}</ref> by Olga and [[Simon Guggenheim]] in memory of their son, who died on April 26, 1922.<ref>{{cite news|accessdate=2018-12-04|title=Mrs. Simon Guggenheim Dead; Philanthropist and Arts Patron|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1970/02/16/archives/mrs-simon-guggenheim-dead-philanthropist-and-arts-patron.html|newspaper=The New York Times}}</ref> The organization awards [[Guggenheim Fellowship]]s to professionals who have demonstrated exceptional ability by publishing a significant body of work in the fields of natural sciences, social sciences, humanities, and the creative arts, excluding the performing arts.<ref name="bloomberg" />
