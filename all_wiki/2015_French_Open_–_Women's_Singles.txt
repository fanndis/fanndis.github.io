{{main article|2015 French Open}}

[[Serena Williams]] defeated first-time Grand Slam finalist [[Lucie Šafářová]] in the final, 6–3, 6–7<sup>(2–7)</sup>, 6–2 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2015 French Open]]. This win gave Williams her third [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Career Grand Slam|Career Grand Slam]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/tennis/33037371|title=French Open: Serena Williams wins after 'nightmare 48 hours'|date=6 June 2015|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=8 June 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2015/jun/06/serena-williams-v-lucie-safarova-french-open-2015-womens-final-live|title=Serena Williams wins French Open after beating Lucie Safarova in final – as it happened|date=6 June 2015|work=Guardian|accessdate=8 June 2015}}</ref> Williams also became the first woman since [[Jennifer Capriati]] in [[2001 French Open – Women's Singles|2001]] to win the Australian Open and the French Open in the same year, and the first to win the US Open, Australian Open and French Open titles consecutively since [[Monica Seles]] (1991&ndash;92).<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.espn.co.uk/tennis/french15/story/_/id/13023322/serena-williams-wins-2015-french-open-20th-grand-slam-title|title=Serena Williams wins French Open for 20th Grand Slam title|date=7 June 2015|work=ESPN Tennis|accessdate=3 July 2017}}</ref> This was Williams' third French Open title and 20th major overall. [[Maria Sharapova]] was the defending champion, but was defeated in the fourth round by the eventual finalist, Šafářová.

[[2008 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|2008]] champion [[Ana Ivanovic]] reached her first Major semi-final since winning the title seven years previously. It was also her last Major semi-final before retiring at the end of 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2015/06/the-latest-quarterfinals-underway-at-french-open/55124/|title=French Open Day 10: Ivanovic into first Grand Slam semifinal since 2008|publisher=tennis.com|work=Associated Press|date=2 June 2015|accessdate=13 February 2019}}</ref>

Šafářová became the first Czech woman to reach the French Open final since [[Hana Mandlíková]] in [[1981 French Open – Women's Singles|1981]].
