The '''Digital Songs''' or '''Digital Song Sales''' chart (previously named '''Hot Digital Songs''')<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/5862620/pitbull-kesha-take-timber-to-top-of-hot-100|work=Billboard|title=Pitbull, Ke$ha Take 'Timber' to Top of Hot 100|first=Gary|last=Trust|date=2014-01-08|accessdate=2014-01-09}}</ref> ranks the best-selling [[Music download|digital]] songs in the United States, as compiled by [[Nielsen SoundScan]] and published by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine. Although it originally started tracking song sales the week of October 30, 2004,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/digital-song-sales|title=Digital Song Sales - Week of October 30, 2004|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-05-28}}</ref> it officially debuted in the issue dated January 22, 2005, and merged all versions of a song sold from digital music distributors.<ref>{{cite journal|date=January 22, 2005|title=New Mix for Digital Rankings|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dRQEAAAAMBAJ|journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|page=6|volume=117|issue=4|accessdate=March 22, 2013}}</ref> Its data was incorporated in the [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] three weeks later. Since October 2004, digital sales have been incorporated into many of ''Billboard''{{'}}s music singles charts.<ref>{{cite news|first=Paul|last=Grein|title=Week Ending March 17, 2013. Songs: The Great Chart Robbery Of 2013|url=http://music.yahoo.com/blogs/chart-watch/week-ending-march-17-2013-songs-great-chart-220437064.html|work=Yahoo!|publisher=Yahoo!|date=2013-03-20|accessdate=2013-03-21}}</ref> The decision was based on the dramatic increase of the digital market while commercial single sales in a physical format were becoming negligible.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=Billboard updated album charts |magazine=Billboard |url= http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/charts/chart_display.jsp?g=Singles&f=Hot+Digital+Tracks&page=2 |accessdate=2008-06-02}}</ref>

The first number one song on the Digital Songs chart was "[[Just Lose It]]" by [[Eminem]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Digital Song Sales Chart - October 30, 2004|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/digital-song-sales/2004-10-30|website=Billboard|accessdate=2020-05-18}}</ref>

The chart's current number one as of the issue dated December 5, 2020, is "[[Life Goes On (BTS song)|Life Goes On]]" by [[BTS]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9491302/bts-life-goes-on-number-one-hot-100|title=BTS' 'Life Goes On' Launches as Historic No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100|work=Billboard|date=November 30, 2020|accessdate=November 30, 2020}}</ref>

== Album records==
* ''[[Teenage Dream (Katy Perry album)|Teenage Dream]]'' by [[Katy Perry]] holds the record for most digital number-ones from one album, with five. "California Gurls," "[[Teenage Dream (Katy Perry song)|Teenage Dream]]," "Firework," "E.T." and "[[Last Friday Night (T.G.I.F.)]]" have all topped the chart.
* ''[[1989 (Taylor Swift album)|1989]]'' and ''[[Reputation (Taylor Swift album)|Reputation]]''  by [[Taylor Swift]] each have four number ones on the chart. "[[Shake It Off]]", "[[Blank Space]]", "[[Out of the Woods (song)|Out of the Woods]]" and "[[Bad Blood (Taylor Swift song)|Bad Blood]]" reached number one from ''1989'' while "[[Look What You Made Me Do]]", "[[...Ready for It?]]", "[[Gorgeous (Taylor Swift song)|Gorgeous]]" and "[[Call It What You Want (Taylor Swift song)|Call It What You Want]]" topped the chart from ''Reputation''.

===Artist achievements===
* [[Katy Perry]] is the first artist in digital history to top 300,000 downloads in weekly sales with eight different songs. She has done it with "[[Hot n Cold]]", "[[California Gurls]]", "[[Firework (song)|Firework]]", "[[E.T. (song)|E.T.]]", "[[The One That Got Away (Katy Perry song)|The One That Got Away]]", "[[Part of Me (Katy Perry song)|Part of Me]]", "[[Roar (song)|Roar]]", and "[[Dark Horse (Katy Perry song)|Dark Horse]]".<ref>[http://new.music.yahoo.com/blogs/chart_watch/74352/week-ending-april-10-2011-songs-its-katys-world/;_ylt=AmaYREm2JEwiMphdilsGRoMPwiUv Week Ending April 10, 2011. Songs: It’s Katy’s World | Chart Watch - Yahoo! Music<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>
*[[Rihanna]] was named the Digital Songs Artist of the 2000s decade.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts-decade-end#/charts-decade-end/digital-songs-artists?year=2009|title=Digital Songs Artist of the decade|magazine=Billboard.com|accessdate=2011-02-05}}</ref>
* [[David Cook (singer)|David Cook]] holds the record for most debuts and total songs charting in one week with 14.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045261/david-cook-sizzles-with-record-chart-debuts|title=David Cook Sizzles With Record Chart Debuts|work=Billboard|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|accessdate=2008-05-28}}</ref>
* [[Miley Cyrus]] is the youngest female artist to debut at number one with 226,000 downloads with her song "[[Party in the U.S.A.]]". "Party" is also tied with [[Psy (entertainer)|Psy]]'s "[[Gangnam Style]]" for the record of most weeks at #1 without topping the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], with six weeks each.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/958016/ushers-omg-stands-pat-atop-hot-100-miley-tames-top-10 |title=Usher's 'OMG' Stands Pat Atop Hot 100, Miley Tames Top 10 |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=2013-11-09}}</ref>
* [[Taylor Swift]] is the only artist to top 500,000 downloads in weekly sales with four different songs. She has done it with "[[We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together]]", "[[I Knew You Were Trouble]]", "[[Shake It Off]]", and "[[Blank Space]]". She also holds the record for the most number one debuts with 19 songs. <ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8015123/taylor-swift-gorgeous-rihanna-digital-song-sales-number-one|title=Taylor Swift Ties Rihanna for Most Digital Song Sales No. 1s With Debut of 'Gorgeous'|accessdate=October 30, 2017}}</ref>
* [[Adele]] is the first and only artist to top 1 million downloads in weekly sales with a song. She has done it with "[[Hello (Adele song)|Hello]]".
* [[Ed Sheeran]] is the first artist to debut two songs at the top two spots for the same week. He has done it with "[[Shape of You]]" and "[[Castle on the Hill (Ed Sheeran song)|Castle on the Hill]]".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7655347/hot-100-ed-sheeran-shape-of-you-debuts-number-one|title=Ed Sheeran Debuts Atop Hot 100 With 'Shape of You' & in Top 10 With 'Castle on the Hill'|magazine=Billboard.com|date=January 17, 2017}}</ref>
*[[Lauren Daigle]] holds the record for the highest-ever debut by a [[Contemporary Christian music|contemporary Christian artist]]. She achieved this when "[[You Say]]" debuted at #5 on the chart dated July 28, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8467018/lauren-daigle-you-say-no-1-hot-christian-songs-chart|title=Lauren Daigle's 'You Say' Soars to No. 1 on Hot Christian Songs Chart|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref>
* [[Lady Gaga]] is the first artist to debut three songs in the top 10 and the first artist to occupy the top four spots for the same week. She has done it with "[[Shallow (Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper song)|Shallow]]", "[[I'll Never Love Again]]", "[[Always Remember Us This Way]]" and "[[Is That Alright?]]" on the week dated October 20, 2018. <ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ladygagamedia.net/lady-gaga-and-bradley-cooper-hold-top-4-spots-on-digital-songs-chart/|title=Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper hold Top 4 spots on Digital Songs Chart|date=2018-10-15|work=Gaga Media|access-date=2018-10-20|language=en-US}}</ref>
*[[Lady Gaga]] is the first and only artist to simultaneously occupy the top three spots in two consecutive weeks. She achieved this on the week dated October 20, 2018 (see above) and on the chart dated October 27, 2018 with "[[Shallow (Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper song)|Shallow]]", "[[Always Remember Us This Way]]" and "[[I'll Never Love Again]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/digital-song-sales/2018-10-27|title=Digital Song Sales – October 27, 2018|accessdate=November 6, 2018}}</ref>
*[[BTS]] is the first act to debut six songs in the top 10 and the first act to occupy the top six spots for the same week. They achieved this with "[[Life Goes On (BTS song)|Life Goes On]]", "Blue & Grey", "Stay", "Telepathy", "Dis-ease" and "Fly To My Room" on the week dated December 5, 2020.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/digital-song-sales/2020-12-05|title=Digital Song Sales – December 5, 2020|accessdate=December 1, 2020}}</ref>
