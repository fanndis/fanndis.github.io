'''Blackpink''' ({{lang-ko|블랙핑크}}; commonly stylized as '''BLACKPINK''' or '''BLΛƆKPIИK''') is a South Korean [[girl group]] formed by [[YG Entertainment]], consisting of members [[Jisoo]], [[Jennie (singer)|Jennie]], [[Rosé (singer)|Rosé]], and [[Lisa (rapper)|Lisa]]. The group debuted in August 2016 with their single album ''[[Square One (Blackpink album)|Square One]]'', which featured "[[Whistle (Blackpink song)|Whistle]]" and "[[Boombayah]]", their first number-one entries on South Korea's [[Gaon Digital Chart]] and the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[World Digital Song Sales]] chart, respectively.

Blackpink has broken numerous online records throughout their career. Blackpink's other [[List of awards and nominations received by Blackpink|accolades]] include the New Artist of the Year Awards at the 31st [[Golden Disc Awards]] and the 26th [[Seoul Music Awards]] as well as recognition as the most powerful celebrities in South Korea in 2019 and the third most powerful celebrities in 2020 by ''[[Forbes Korea Power Celebrity|Forbes]]'' [[Forbes Korea Power Celebrity|Korea]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jmagazine.joins.com/forbes/view/325767|title=포브스코리아 선정 2019 파워 셀러브리티 40 |website=[[JoongAng Ilbo]]|access-date=July 6, 2020|date=April 23, 2019|first=Oh|last=Seung-il}}</ref> and as the first female Korean group on ''Forbes''{{'}} [[Forbes 30 Under 30|30 Under 30 Asia]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ramirez|first=Elaine|title=BlackPink: The All-Girl K-Pop Group Following BTS's Footsteps And Taking Over The U.S.|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/elaineramirez/2019/04/01/blackpink-the-all-girl-k-pop-group-following-btss-footsteps-and-taking-over-the-u-s/|access-date=July 14, 2020|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref> They were also the first K-pop girl group to win an [[MTV Music Video Award]].

== Career ==
=== 2010–2016: Formation and pre-debut activities ===
Blackpink began forming when [[YG Entertainment]] held tryouts worldwide for preteen or teenage recruits to create a new girl group after launching its first major girl group, [[2NE1]], in 2009. According to the members, joining the label as [[Korean idol#Trainee system|trainees]] was similar to enrolling in a full-time pop-star academy, with Jennie calling it "more strict than school" and Rosé comparing it to ''[[The X Factor]]'' with dorm rooms. For members who had left behind life outside of South Korea, the pace of training alongside the culture shock was tough.<ref name=bbcover>{{cite web|first=Nolan|last=Feeney|date=February 28, 2019|title=Inside Blackpink's U.S. Takeover: How the K-Pop Queens Are Changing the Game|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/cover-story/8500312/blackpink-billboard-cover-story-2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 11, 2020}}</ref> Preparations for Blackpink's debut began as early as 2011 when YG Entertainment revealed on November 14, 2011 that the label's new girl group would debut in the early half of 2012 featuring at least seven members.<ref>{{cite web|date=November 11, 2011|title=Super Star K2's Kim Eun-bi To Debut As YG Entertainment Group|url=http://world.kbs.co.kr/english/program/program_musicnews_frame.htm?No=16752&current_page=165|website=[[KBS World]]|access-date=July 11, 2020}}</ref> From then, there were numerous news and rumors surrounding the new girl group about their debut being delayed but there was no official information.<ref>{{cite web|date=September 3, 2012|title=G-Dragon turns producer for YG's new girl group|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20120903001229|website=[[The Korea Herald]]|access-date=July 11, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=February 26, 2013|title=YG Entertainment Delays New Girl Group Debut Yet Again|url=https://www.popdust.com/nazi-chic-2629432175.html|website=Popdust|access-date=July 11, 2020}}</ref> It was only until May 18, 2016 that YG Entertainment confirmed the new girl group, stating that the members were selected through years of stiff competition and confirming that the debut would be in July.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003320557|title=[단독]2NE1 데뷔 후 7년, YG 걸그룹 자매의 탄생|date=May 18, 2016|website=Naver|publisher=Osen|language=ko|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref> The label later confirmed that Jang Hanna and Moon Sua, who were introduced to the public as potential members of the new girl group, were not included in the debut lineup.<ref>{{Cite web|title=[단독] YG 신인 걸그룹..'1순위'라던 장한나 없다 '확인'|url=https://entertain.v.daum.net/v/20160519114704566|date=May 19, 2016|website=Daum|publisher=Ilgan Sports|language=ko|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title='언프리티' 문수아도 YG 新걸그룹 NO "새 인물多"|url=https://entertain.v.daum.net/v/20160520093438960|date=May 20, 2016|website=Daum|publisher=Star News|language=ko|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref>

Jennie was the first member revealed to be in the group on June 1.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://osen.mt.co.kr/article/G1110426941|title=YG 걸그룹, 첫 멤버 제니 공개..실력+비주얼 다 갖췄다|date=June 1, 2016|website=Osen|language=ko|access-date=July 9, 2019}}</ref> Jennie joined YG Entertainment as a trainee in 2010 after moving back to South Korea from New Zealand.<ref name=trainee>{{cite news|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=052&aid=0001163639|title=[Y기획②]멤버를 알면 '진짜' 블랙핑크가 보인다|language=ko|work=YTN|date=July 7, 2018|accessdate=July 11, 2019|last=Ji|first=Seung-hoon}}</ref> In 2012, she was introduced to the public for the first time through a photo titled "Who's that girl?" via the official blog of YG Entertainment on April 10.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=014&aid=0002628723|title=YG 미스테리걸 등장, 눈에 띄는 미모 新걸그룹 멤버(?)|work=Financial News|publisher=Naver|language=ko|date=April 20, 2012|access-date=August 2, 2020}}</ref> Jennie continued to be promoted as a member of the new girl group with multiple collaborations, such as starring in [[G-Dragon]]'s 2012 music video "[[That XX]]" from ''[[One of a Kind (EP)|One of a Kind]]'' [[extended play|EP]] and featuring in G-Dragon's "Black" from 2013 album [[Coup d'etat (G-Dragon album)|''Coup d'etat'']], and [[Lee Hi]]'s "Special" from 2013 album ''[[First Love (Lee Hi album)|First Love]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://star.mt.co.kr/stview.php?no=2012082915564047813|title=YG새걸그룹' 김제니, GD 뮤비 여주인공 전격발탁|date=August 29, 2012|website=Star News|language=ko|access-date=June 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/g-dragon-coup-detat-making-of/black-jennie-kim|title=The Making of G-Dragon's "Coup D'Etat" "Black" feat. Jennie Kim|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=June 26, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.edaily.co.kr/news/Read?newsId=01167686602740040&mediaCodeNo=258|title=YG 새 걸그룹 김제니, 이하이 첫 솔로앨범 참여 '눈길'|date=March 4, 2013|website=EDaily|language=ko|access-date=June 10, 2019}}</ref> Lisa was revealed to be the second member on June 8, 2016.<ref name=lisa>{{cite web|date=June 8, 2016|title=[뮤직톡톡]제니 이어 리사..YG 걸그룹=걸크러시?|url=http://osen.mt.co.kr/article/G1110432048|website=Osen|access-date=August 1, 2020|language=ko}}</ref> Lisa was the only individual among 4,000 applicants to pass the 2010 YG Entertainment audition in her native Thailand, becoming the first foreign trainee to join the label in 2011.<ref name=trainee /> She was first introduced in 2012 with a video that was posted on YG Entertainment's official YouTube channel titled "Who's That Girl???".<ref name=lisa /> Lisa also appeared in the music video for [[Taeyang]]'s "[[Ringa Linga]]" in 2013. She then took on her first modelling job in 2015 for street-wear brand [[YG Entertainment#Nona9on|Nona9on]], following by modelling for cosmetics brand [[YG Entertainment#Moonshot|Moonshot]] in 2016.<ref name=predebut>{{cite web|url=http://news.donga.com/3/07/20160811/79708918/4|title=[블랙핑크 VS 투애니원③] 블랙핑크는 누구?|author=|date=August 12, 2016|website=Donga News|access-date=June 4, 2019}}</ref>

On June 15, Jisoo was revealed to be the third member. Jisoo joined YG Entertainment as a trainee in July 2011. She appeared in several advertisements and music videos in her pre-debut years, including "Spoiler + Happy Ending" (2014) from [[Epik High]]'s studio album ''[[Shoebox (album)|Shoebox]]'' and [[Hi Suhyun]]'s music video "[[I'm Different (Hi Suhyun song)|I'm Different]]" (2014). She also made a [[cameo appearance]] in 2015 drama ''[[The Producers (TV series)|The Producers]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jeon|first=Won|title=YG 걸그룹 지수 티저공개, 센터 차지할 꽃미모|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003343019|date=June 15, 2016|website=Naver|publisher=Osen|language=ko|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref> Rosé was revealed to be a member on June 22, 2016. She ranked first among 700 applicants in the 2012 YG Entertainment audition in Australia, which then resulted in a trainee contract with the label and a move to [[Seoul]] to begin her training.<ref name=trainee /> She was featured in G-Dragon's track "Without You" from ''One of a Kind'' EP in 2012, then credited as "? from YG New Girl Group". However, her featuring vocals and identity was kept secret for almost four years until her first official public introduction when it was revealed that she participated in the song.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sun|first=Mi-kyung|title=YG, 새 걸그룹 네번째 멤버 로제 공개..호주 출신 실력파..'강렬'|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003343019|date=June 22, 2016|website=Naver|publisher=Osen|language=ko|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref>

{{Main|List of awards and nominations received by Blackpink}}
Blackpink's accolades include three [[People's Choice Awards]], a [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]], an [[MTV Video Music Awards|MTV Video Music Award]], five [[Gaon Chart Music Awards]], four [[Golden Disc Awards]], five [[Melon Music Awards]], four [[Mnet Asian Music Awards]], two [[Seoul Music Awards]], and five [[Guinness World Records]].

Their music video for "[[Ddu-Du Ddu-Du]]" became the most-watched music video by a South Korean group in January 2019 and became the first K-pop group music video to surpass one billion views in November 2019.<ref>{{cite web|last=Herman|first=Tamar|date=January 23, 2019|title=Blackpink's 'Ddu-Du Ddu-Du' Becomes Most-Viewed Music Video From a K-Pop Group on YouTube|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8494591/blackpink-ddu-du-ddu-du-most-viewed-music-video-kpop-youtube|accessdate=November 13, 2019|work=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Rolli|first=Bryan|date=November 11, 2019|title=Blackpink's 'Ddu-Du Ddu-Du' Becomes First K-Pop Group Video To Earn 1 Billion YouTube Views|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/bryanrolli/2019/11/11/blackpinks-ddu-du-ddu-du-becomes-first-k-pop-group-video-to-earn-1-billion-youtube-views/|accessdate=November 13, 2019|work=Forbes}}</ref> Following the release of "[[How You Like That]]" and its music video on June 26, 2020, Blackpink broke five [[Guinness World Records]], including those for the most-viewed YouTube video in the first 24 hours of release (with 86.3&nbsp;million views) and the most viewers for a video premiere on YouTube (reaching 1.66&nbsp;million peak concurrent viewers for the live premiere).<ref>{{Cite news|date=July 1, 2020|title=Blackpink break multiple world records with 'How You Like That' video|work=[[NME]]|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/blackpink-break-multiple-world-records-with-how-you-like-that-video-2699364?amp|access-date=July 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Punt|first=Dominic|date=June 30, 2020|title=Blackpink's return sets new YouTube records with How You Like That|work=[[Guinness World Records]]|url=https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/2020/6/blackpinks-return-sets-new-youtube-records-with-how-you-like-that-622453|access-date=July 4, 2020}}</ref>
