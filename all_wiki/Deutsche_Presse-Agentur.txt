'''Deutsche Presse-Agentur GmbH (dpa)''' is a [[Germany|German]] [[news agency]] founded in 1949.<ref name="dpa facts">[http://www.dpa.de/Facts-and-Figures.371.0.html ″Facts and Figures″] on the dpa website</ref> Based in [[Hamburg]], it has grown to be a major worldwide operation serving print media, radio, television, online, mobile phones, and national news agencies. News is available in [[German language|German]], [[English language|English]], [[Spanish language|Spanish]], and [[Arabic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dpa.com/en/products-services/media-newsrooms/#international-services|title=Media & Newsrooms|publisher=dpa|access-date=27 April 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

The dpa is the largest press agency in Germany.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dw-world.de/dw/article/0,,4579908,00.html |title=Germany's biggest press agency turns 60 |date=18 August 2009 |accessdate=13 October 2009 |publisher=[[Deutsche Welle]] }}</ref> Along with the main office in Hamburg, there is a central news office in [[Berlin]]. The dpa has offices in some hundred countries, including twelve regional German bureaus and fifty-five additional offices in Germany.<ref name="dpa facts" />

==History==
The dpa was founded as a co-operative in [[Goslar]] on 18 August 1949, succeeding the [[Deutsche Nachrichtenagentur]] and Süddeutsche News Agency (Südena), and became a [[limited liability company]] in 1951. [[Fritz Singer|Fritz Sänger]] was the first [[editor-in-chief]]. He served as [[managing director]] until 1955 and as [[managing editor]] until 1959. The first transmission occurred at 6 a.m. on 1 September 1949.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dpa.de/fileadmin/media/dpa_downloads_en_es/dpa_history.pdf |title=The history of the German Press Agency dpa  |accessdate=9 September 2014 |publisher=Deutsche Presse-Agentur }}</ref>

In 1986, the dpa founded Global Media Services (GMS), which bought its competitor Cartography Service GmbH in 1988.{{Citation needed|date=March 2019}}

In 2010, the editorial headquarters moved to the historical newspaper district of Berlin, location of the former newsroom for [[Hamburg]], [[Frankfurt]], and [[Berlin]]. The corporate headquarters remain in Hamburg, along with subsidiaries news aktuell GmbH, dpa-media technology GmbH, and dpa-infocom GmbH.{{Citation needed|date=March 2019}}

== Services ==
The dpa Basic Service produces over eight hundred daily reports that appear in almost every German newspaper and news service. The dpa Photo Service releases about twelve hundred photos daily.{{Citation needed|date=March 2019}}

The dpa World News Service releases German news stories in English from Berlin, Spanish from [[Buenos Aires]] and [[Madrid]], and Arabic from [[Cairo]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2019}} In 2009 the dpa launched a dual-language service in [[Turkish language|Turkish]] and German but abandoned it after only nine months.{{Citation needed|date=March 2019}}

The dpa cooperates with other news agencies, including [[Austria Presse-Agentur]], [[DPA-AFX Business News]], and [[Schweizerische Depeschenagentur]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2019}}
