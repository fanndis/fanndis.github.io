'''Jennifer Leigh Warren''' is an American stage, television, film, and [[voice-over]] actress/singer who first came to the world's attention for her work in professional [[musical theater]]. She is best known for originating the role of Crystal in the [[Howard Ashman]] /[[Alan Menken]] hit musical ''[[Little Shop of Horrors (musical)|Little Shop of Horrors]]'',<ref name="broadwayworld">{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/shows/cast.php?showid=5677#.U2amwcZfc4Q|title=Little Shop of Horrors Original Off-Broadway Cast - 1982 Off-Broadway - BroadwayWorld|publisher=broadwayworld.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref> for her performance in the original Broadway cast of the [[Michael John LaChiusa]] musical ''[[Marie Christine]]''<ref name="playbillvault">{{cite web|url=http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/11230/Marie-Christine|title=Marie Christine on Broadway - Information, Cast, Crew, Synopsis and Photos - Playbill Vault|publisher=playbillvault.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref> and for her show stopping performance in the role of Aliceâ€™s Daughter in the original Broadway musical "Big River" with the song "How Blest We Are" written especially for her by [[Roger Miller]].<ref name="therep">{{cite web|url=http://www.therep.org/attend/productions/actor/default.aspx?id=225|title=Jennifer Leigh Warren*|publisher=therep.org|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref>

She is a [[NAACP Theatre Awards|NAACP Theatre Award]] nominee,<ref name="naacptheatreawards">{{cite web|url=http://www.naacptheatreawards.com/PR-2_Nominees.html|title=2012 NAACP Theatre Awards|author=NAACP Beverly Hills-Hollywood Chapter|publisher=naacptheatreawards.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref> ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' [[Ovation Awards|Ovation Award]] winner<ref name="playbill">{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/63622-Ovation-Winner-Jennifer-Leigh-Warren-Sings-for-LAs-Blank-Theatre-Dec-3|title=Ovation Winner Jennifer Leigh Warren Sings for L.A.'s Blank Theatre Dec. 3 - Playbill.com|publisher=playbill.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref> (and three-time nominee<ref name="playbill2">{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/48831-LAs-Ovation-Awards-Announced-Nov-8-David-Hyde-Pierce-Hosts|title=L.A.'s Ovation Awards Announced Nov. 8; David Hyde Pierce Hosts - Playbill.com|publisher=playbill.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref>), [[Back Stage Garland Awards|Backstage Garland]] award winner, and won two Broadway World awards in the same season<ref name="broadwayworld2">{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/search/?q=BWW+2011+Los+Angeles+Awards+Winners+Announced+-+Jennifer+Leigh+Warren+Scores+Double-Win%2C+Ripley%2C+Maroulis+%26+More!&cx=003281041504712504013%3Ama8kxnaa1hu&cof=FORID%3A11&ie=UTF-8&search_type%5B%5D=site|title=Broadway World.com - #1 Site for Broadway Shows, Theatre, Live Entertainment, Tickets &amp; More!|publisher=broadwayworld.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref> for her portrayal of the fairy godmother in the first US [[pantomime]] production ''[[A Cinderella Christmas]]''<ref name="playbill3">{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/143442-Jerry-Mathers-Jennifer-Leigh-Warren-Freddie-Stroma-Cast-in-Lythgoe-Familys-Panto-Cinderella-in-CA|title=Jerry Mathers, Jennifer Leigh Warren, Freddie Stroma Cast in Lythgoe Family's Panto Cinderella in CA - Playbill.com|publisher=playbill.com|accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref> directed by [[Bonnie Lythgoe]] and for her one-woman concert event Diamonds Are Forever:The Songs of Dame [[Shirley Bassey]],<ref name="stagescenela">{{cite web|url=http://www.stagescenela.com/2011/06/diamonds-are-forever-the-songs-of-dame-shirley-bassey/|title=StageSceneLA|publisher=stagescenela.com|accessdate=2014-05-05|date=2011-06-16}}</ref> that premiered in Los Angeles at the Renberg Theater, directed by [[Richard Jay-Alexander]].
