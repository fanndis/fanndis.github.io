'''John Charles Pritzlaff Jr.''' (May 10, 1925 – May 2, 2005) was an American businessman, politician, and diplomat who served in both chambers of the [[Arizona State Legislature]]. He also served as the [[US Ambassador to Malta|United States Ambassador to Malta]] from 1969 to 1972.

==Early life and education==
Pritzlaff was born in [[Milwaukee]], [[Wisconsin]]. In 1958, Pritzlaff moved to [[Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix]], Arizona, with his family.<ref name="Obituary" /> Pritlaff served in the [[United States Army]] during [[World War II]]. In 1947, he received his bachelor's degree in history and political science from [[Princeton University]].

== Career ==
Prior to his political career, he was the Vice-President and General Manager of the [[John Pritzlaff Hardware Company]] in Milwaukee, founded by [[John C. Pritzlaff]]. In Phoenix, Pritzlaff owned the Rockmount Real Estate Investments. He was involved with the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] in Wisconsin and Arizona.<ref name="Obituary">{{Cite web | last = | first = | author-link = | last2 = | first2 = | author2-link = | title = John Charles Pritzlaff Jr. - Obituary| publisher = The Arizona Republic| pages = | date = 5 May 2005| url = http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/azcentral/obituary.aspx?n=john-charles-pritzlaff&pid=3506544| accessdate = 15 July 2014 }}</ref>

He was a member of the [[Arizona House of Representatives]] from 1963 to 1969 and of the [[Arizona Senate]] from 1975 to 1982.<ref>{{cite web|last=|first=|author-link=|date=8 May 2009|title=SG 6 - Members - Senator John C. == Personal life ==
On February 10, 1951, Pritzlaff married Mary Dell Olin,<ref name="Obituary"/><ref name="Olin"/> the daughter of [[Spencer Truman Olin]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/705939/|title=Alton Evening Telegraph from Alton, Illinois on September 11, 1950 · Page 10|publisher=}}</ref> an executive at [[Olin Corporation]]. The family lived in Milwaukee, Wisconsin before moving to Phoenix, Arizona, in 1958. Their daughter Anne, married former Arizona Governor, [[Fife Symington]].<ref>{{Cite web| title =John Pritzlaff, Former US ambassador to Malta, dies| publisher =Malta Independent| date =8 May 2005| url =http://www.independent.com.mt/mobile/2005-05-08/news/john-pritzlaff-former-us-ambassador-to-malta-dies-75176/| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20140714184556/http://www.independent.com.mt/mobile/2005-05-08/news/john-pritzlaff-former-us-ambassador-to-malta-dies-75176/| archive-date =2014-07-14| accessdate =15 July 2014| url-status =dead}}</ref>

Pritzlaff died on May 2, 2005 in [[Arizona]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=John Pritzlaff - Obituary|url=https://www.legacy.com/amp/obituaries/azcentral/3506544|access-date=2020-10-11|website=www.legacy.com|language=en}}</ref>
