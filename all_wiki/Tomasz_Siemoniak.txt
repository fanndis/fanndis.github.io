'''Tomasz Siemoniak''' (born 2 July 1967) is a [[Poles|Polish]] politician, [[Ministry of National Defence (Poland)|Minister of National Defence]] from 2 August 2011 to 16 November 2015 and [[Deputy Prime Minister of Poland]] from 22 September 2014 to 16 November 2015.

==Early life and education==
Siemoniak was born on 2 July 1967.<ref name=MON/> He graduated from the Foreign Trade Faculty of [[Warsaw School of Economics|the Warsaw School of Economics]].<ref name=MON>{{cite web|title=Minister of National Defence|url=http://www.mon.gov.pl/en/strona/186/LG_81_85|work=Ministry of National Defence|accessdate=8 September 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120610092222/http://mon.gov.pl/en/strona/186/LG_81_85#|archive-date=10 June 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> During his university education, he was the head of the [[Independent Students’ Association]] (NZS).<ref name=MON/>

==Career==
Siemoniak began his career at the Polish public broadcasting unit, [[Telewizja Polska]], as the director of the office for field branches and general director of Channel 1. His tenure lasted from 1994 to 1996.<ref name=MON/> He served as the director of the press and information office at the ministry of national defence from 1998 to 2000. During this period he was also a member of the Warsaw city centre district council and deputy chairman of the culture committee of the council. He was appointed deputy chairman of the supervisory board of the [[Polish News Agency]] in 1998, and his tenure ended in 2002.<ref name=MON/> From December 2000 to July 2002 he was also deputy mayor of [[Warsaw]]. He was named as a board member of [[Polskie Radio|Polskie Radio SA]] (public radio company) in 2002 and served there until 2006.<ref name=defnews/> Then he was appointed deputy marshal in the marshal’s office of [[Mazowieckie Voivodeship|the Mazowieckie Voivodeship]] (November 2006 - November 2007).<ref name=kprm>{{cite web|title=Composition of Government|url=http://www.premier.gov.pl/en/government/composition_of_the_government/tomasz_siemoniak,7332/|publisher=KPRM|accessdate=8 September 2012}}{{Dead link|date=May 2019|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref> From November 2007 to August 2011, he held the post of secretary of state in [[Ministry of Interior and Administration (Poland)|the ministry of interior and administration]]. Siemoniak is a member of [[Civic Platform|the Civic Platform]] headed by [[Donald Tusk]].<ref name=and19nov>{{cite news|last=Gardner|first=Andrew|title=Poland's new government wins support|url=http://www.europeanvoice.com/article/2011/november/poland-s-new-government-wins-support/72688.aspx|accessdate=6 July 2013|newspaper=European Voice|date=19 November 2011}}</ref>

On 2 August 2011, he was appointed minister of national defense in [[First Cabinet of Donald Tusk|the cabinet]] led by the prime minister [[Donald Tusk]].<ref name=defnews>{{cite news|title=Poland Names New Defense Minister|url=http://www.defensenews.com/article/20110802/DEFSECT04/108020304/Poland-Names-New-Defense-Minister|accessdate=6 July 2013|newspaper=Defense News|date=2 August 2011}}</ref> Siemoniak replaced [[Bogdan Klich]] in the post.<ref name=premier>{{cite web|title=New minister of national defence appointed|url=http://www.premier.gov.pl/en/press_centre/news/new_minister_of_national_defen,7327/|publisher=Polish Premier|accessdate=8 September 2012|date=2 August 2011}}{{Dead link|date=July 2018|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=no}}</ref> After the elections in [[October 2011 Polish parliamentary elections|October 2011]], Tusk reformed [[Second Cabinet of Donald Tusk|the cabinet]] and Siemoniak retained his post as defense minister.<ref name=and19nov/>

===Activities and views===
In August 2012, Siemoniak declared that Poland is planning to form its own [[Missile Defense System|missile defense system]] with an estimated budget of between $3 billion and $6 billion.<ref name=acus>{{cite web|last=Benites|first=Jorge|title=Poland wants to build missile defense system with France, Germany|url=http://www.acus.org/natosource/poland-wants-build-missile-defense-system-france-germany|publisher=Atlantic Council|accessdate=8 September 2012|date=12 August 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120815113044/http://www.acus.org/natosource/poland-wants-build-missile-defense-system-france-germany#|archive-date=15 August 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
