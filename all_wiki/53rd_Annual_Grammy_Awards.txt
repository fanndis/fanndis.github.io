The '''53rd Annual Grammy Awards''' were held on February 13, 2011, at the [[Staples Center]] in [[Los Angeles]]. They were broadcast on CBS with a rating of 26.6 million viewers. [[Barbra Streisand]] was honored as the [[MusiCares Person of the Year]] two nights prior to the telecast on February 11. Nominations were announced on December 1, 2010 and a total of 109 awards were presented. Most of the awards were presented during the pre-telecast, which took place at the [[Los Angeles Convention Center]] next to the Staples Center, where the main telecast took place. The eligibility period was September 1, 2009 to September 30, 2010.<ref>{{cite web |date=13 April 2010 |title=53rd GRAMMY Awards Slated For Feb. 13, 2011 |website=Grammy.com |url=http://www.grammy.com/news/53rd-grammy-awards-slated-for-feb-13-2011 |accessdate=1 August 2017}}</ref>

For the third year, nominations were announced on prime-time television as part of "The GRAMMY Nominations Concert Live! – Countdown to Music's Biggest Night" a one-hour special broadcast live on CBS from Club Nokia at L.A. Live.

[[Arcade Fire]] won [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] for ''[[The Suburbs (Arcade Fire album)|The Suburbs]]'' becoming the first [[Indie (music)|indie]] act to do so,<ref>{{cite web |date=16 February 2011 |title=The GRAMMYs' Indie Jones |website=Grammy.com |url=http://www.grammy.com/news/the-grammys-indie-jones |accessdate=1 August 2017}}</ref> and surprising many viewers and critics.<ref>{{cite web |title=Arcade Fire Win the Album of the Year Grammy - Pitchfork |website=pitchfork.com |url=http://pitchfork.com/news/41559-arcade-fire-win-the-album-of-the-year-grammy/ |accessdate=1 August 2017}}</ref> [[Baba Yetu]] composed and arranged by [[Christopher Tin]] won [[Best Instrumental Arrangement Accompanying Vocalist(s)]], is the first Grammy given to a piece of music written for a Video Game.<ref>{{YouTube|TM_WzFnmtac}}</ref> [[Esperanza Spalding]] was awarded [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]]. [[Lady Antebellum]] won five awards including [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] for [[Need You Now (Lady Antebellum song)|"Need You Now"]]. Other multiple winners include: [[David Frost (producer)|David Frost]], [[John Legend]], [[Lady Gaga]], [[Jay-Z]], and [[Jeff Beck]] with three awards each. [[The Black Keys]], [[Eminem]], [[Herbie Hancock]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[The Roots]], [[Usher (singer)|Usher]], [[Christopher Tin]] and [[BeBe Winans]] won two awards each.<ref>{{cite web |date=13 February 2011 |title=Ladies Antebellum And Gaga, Jeff Beck, David Frost, John Leg |website=Grammy.com |url=http://www.grammy.com/news/ladies-antebellum-and-gaga-jeff-beck-david-frost-john-legend-win-three-grammys-each |accessdate=1 August 2017}}</ref>

The [[television broadcast]] of the 53rd Grammy Awards marked the last awards show for the Grammy's [[executive producer]], [[John Cossette]], before his death on April 26, 2011.<ref name=billboard>{{cite news |last=Gallo |first=Phill |date=2011-04-27 |title=Grammy Awards producer John Cossette dies at 54 |work=[[Billboard Magazine]] |publisher=Reuters |url=http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/42783136/ns/today-entertainment/ |accessdate=2011-05-01 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110430164419/http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/42783136/ns/today-entertainment |archive-date=2011-04-30}}</ref>
