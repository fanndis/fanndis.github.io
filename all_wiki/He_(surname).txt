'''He''' or '''Ho''' is the [[Romanisation|romanised]] transliteration of several [[Chinese family names]]. 
Of these family names, [[wiktionary:何|何]] ([[Pinyin]]: {{Audio|Zh-hé.ogg|Hé|help=no}}; [[International Phonetic Alphabet|IPA]]: {{IPAc-cmn|h|e|2}}) is the most common at 17th (14 million, according to 2012 survey)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cdn.theatlantic.com/newsroom/img/posts/2013/10/chinassurnames/0886ab335.jpg |title=China's Surnames |publisher=Cdn.theatlantic.com |accessdate=2013-11-05}}</ref> In 2019 Hé was again the 17th most common surname in Mainland China.<ref>http://www.bjnews.com.cn/news/2020/01/20/676822.html</ref>  

A common alternative spelling of this surname is '''Ho''', which is the [[Standard Mandarin|Mandarin]] [[Wade-Giles]] [[romanization]] and the [[Standard Cantonese|Cantonese]] romanization of several [[Chinese surname|Chinese family names]].

In Vietnam, the equivalent surname is ''Hà'' or Ha. Not be confused with the Vietnamese surname ''Hồ'', which is the equivalent of the Chinese surname [[Hu (surname)|Hu]]. In Korea, the equivalent surname is Ha.

The surname originates from the [[Ji (surname)|Ji]] clan of the [[Zhou Dynasty]], and the [[Jiang (surname)|Jiang]] clan of [[Yan Emperor|Yandi]].

It is 21st on the ''[[Hundred Family Surnames]]'', contained in the verse 何呂施張 (He Lü Shi Zhang).

It is considered one of the "[[Nine Sogdian Surnames]]."<ref>https://sogdians.si.edu/sidebars/the-nine-sogdian-surnames/</ref>

Other less common family names romanized He include [[wiktionary:河|河]] (Pinyin: Hé), [[wiktionary:佫|佫]] (Pinyin: Hè), [[wiktionary:赫|赫]] (Pinyin: Hè), and 和 (the 97th name on the ''[[Hundred Family Surnames]]'' poem.<ref name="KS">K. S. Tom. [1989] (1989). Echoes from Old China: Life, Legends and Lore of the Middle Kingdom. [[University of Hawaii Press]]. {{ISBN|0-8248-1285-9}}.</ref>)
