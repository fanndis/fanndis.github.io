{{main|2019 French Open}}

[[Ashleigh Barty]] won her first Grand Slam singles title, defeating [[Markéta Vondroušová]] in the final, 6–1, 6–3 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2019 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2019/jun/08/ashleigh-barty-storms-to-french-open-glory-win-over-marketa-vondrousova|title =Ashleigh Barty storms to French Open glory with win over Vondrousova|date=8 June 2019|work=Guardian|accessdate=11 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/48567958|title =French Open 2019: Ashleigh Barty beats Marketa Vondrousova to win title|date=8 June 2019|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=11 June 2019}}</ref>						
Barty was the first Australian player, male or female, to win a French Open singles title since [[Margaret Court]] in [[1973 French Open – Women's Singles|1973]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rolandgarros.com/en-us/article/barty-vondrousova-womens-singles-final-roland-garros|title=Ashleigh Barty: Roland-Garros champion|publisher=rolandgarros.com|date=8 June 2019|accessdate=8 June 2019}}</ref> as well as the first Australian player to win a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles title since [[Samantha Stosur]] at the [[2011 US Open – Women's Singles|2011 US Open]]. Vondroušová was the first teenager to reach a French Open women's singles final since [[Ana Ivanovic]] in [[2007 French Open – Women's Singles|2007]], as well as the first teenager to reach a Grand Slam women's singles final since [[Caroline Wozniacki]] at the [[2009 US Open – Women's Singles|2009 US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2019/06/johanna-konta-marketa-vondrousova-roland-garros-semifinals-french-open/82396/|title=Vondrousova first teen to reach Roland Garros final since 2007|publisher=tennis.com|date=7 June 2019|accessdate=7 June 2019}}</ref>

[[Simona Halep]] was the defending champion, but lost to [[Amanda Anisimova]] in the quarterfinals. Anisimova was the first player, male or female, born in the 21st century to reach the quarterfinals and semifinals of a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/%E2%80%98i-cant-describe-how-excited-i-am%E2%80%99-anisimova-ends-bolsova%E2%80%99s-french-open-dream-gets-halep-maiden|title='I can't describe how excited I am': Anisimova ends Bolsova's French Open dream, gets Halep in maiden quarterfinal|publisher=WTA Tennis|first=Stephanie|last=Livaudais|date=3 June 2019|accessdate=4 June 2019}}</ref>

[[Naomi Osaka]] retained the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players#Singles|WTA no. 1 singles ranking]] after [[Karolína Plíšková]] lost in the third round.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/no1-scenarios-pliskova-bertens-kvitovas-tough-road-unseat-osaka-roland-garros|title=No.1 Scenarios: Pliskova, Bertens, Kvitova's tough road to unseat Osaka at Roland Garros|author=Courtney Nguyen|publisher=WTA|date=23 May 2019|accessdate=25 May 2019}}</ref> [[Angelique Kerber]], [[Kiki Bertens]] and [[Petra Kvitová]] were also in contention for the top ranking at the start of the tournament. Osaka's streak of 16 consecutive wins at Grand Slam level was broken when she lost to [[Kateřina Siniaková]] in the third round.

For the first time since the [[1978 Australian Open – Women's Singles|1978 Australian Open]], none of the four semifinalists had previously reached a Grand Slam singles final. Of the four, [[Johanna Konta]] was the only one who had previously reached a Grand Slam singles semifinal.

This was the final main Grand Slam draw appearance of former World No. 4, [[2014 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2014 Australian Open]] runner-up, and [[2016 WTA Finals – Singles|2016 WTA Finals]] champion [[Dominika Cibulková]], as she went on to retire from professional tennis after the end of the 2019 season. She lost to [[Aryna Sabalenka]] in the first round. It was also the first French Open main draw appearance of future French Open champion (and Poland's first Grand Slam winner) [[Iga Świątek]]. She made it to the fourth round before losing to Halep in straight sets. Świątek would go on to win the French Open title [[2020 French Open – Women's Singles|the following year]] (coincidentally, she would end up beating Halep in the fourth round in straight sets en route to her title).
