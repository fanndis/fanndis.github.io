'''Julia Klöckner''' (born 16 December 1972) is a German [[politician]] of the [[Christian Democratic Union (Germany)|Christian Democratic Union]] (CDU) who has been serving as Federal Minister of Food, Agriculture and Consumer Protection in the government of [[Chancellor of Germany|Chancellor]] [[Angela Merkel]] since 2018. Since 2012, she has also been part of the CDU leadership.

Klöckner first became known in 1995 when she was chosen as the [[German Wine Queen]] (''Deutsche Weinkönigin''). From 2002 to 2011 she was a member of parliament in the German [[Bundestag]] and, from 2009 to February 2011, she was a [[Secretary of State#Germany|parliamentary undersecretary]] in the [[Federal Ministry of Food, Agriculture and Consumer Protection (Germany)|Federal Ministry of Food, Agriculture and Consumer Protection]]. Since 25 September 2010 she has chaired the [[Rhineland-Palatinate]] CDU party<ref>[https://twitter.com/JuliaKloeckner/statuses/25492596366 Danke für 96,9 % zu meiner Wahl als Landesvorsitzende!] dated 25 September 2010</ref> and, since March 2011, the CDU group in the [[Landtag of Rhineland-Palatinate]], the state parliament. Since 15&nbsp;November 2010 she has been on the CDU's national ''Präsidium'' (executive committee) and on 4 December 2012 she was elected as one of the Deputy Federal Chairmen and -women of the CDU. In recent years, she has regularly been mentioned by the media as potential successor to [[Angela Merkel]].

== Early life and education ==
Klöckner was born on 16 December 1972 in the German [[spa town]] of [[Bad Kreuznach]]. She grew up in [[Guldental]] as the youngest child of a [[vintner]]'s family.<ref>weingut-aloys-kloeckner.de: [http://www.weingut-aloys-kloeckner.de/wp/?page_id=2 ''Familie – Reben brauchen feste Wurzeln ...  Menschen auch.''] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921055555/http://www.weingut-aloys-kloeckner.de/wp/?page_id=2 |date=2013-09-21 }}</ref> After taking her ''[[Abitur]]'' in 1992 at the ''Gymnasium an der Stadtmauer'' [[grammar school]] in Bad Kreuznach, Klöckner studied for a degree in [[political science]], [[Catholic theology]] and [[education]], passing her first ''[[Staatsexamen]]'' in 1998 in  Social Studies and Religion and receiving her [[Master of Arts]] (MA) at the [[Johannes Gutenberg University]] in [[Mainz]].

== Professional career ==
After her intermediate examination, Klöckner worked, with special permission, from 1994 to 1998 as a [[religious education]] teacher at a [[primary school]] in [[Wiesbaden]], and then began her practical training as a journalist. In 1994 she was chosen as the "Wine Queen of the [[Nahe (wine region)|Nahe]] valley" (''Nahe Weinkönigin'') and, in 1995, as the [[German Wine Queen]].<ref>deutscheweinkoenigin.de: [http://www.deutscheweinkoenigin.de/icc/Weinkoenigin/nav/166/16660196-401e-9a21-1cf8-efb104c41ed8&c_page=2&picID=99919640-1e9a-211c-f8ef-b104c41ed8b2 Bildergalerie der Majestäten – Julia Klöckner, 47. Deutsche Weinkönigin] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131027152329/http://www.deutscheweinkoenigin.de/icc/Weinkoenigin/nav/166/16660196-401e-9a21-1cf8-efb104c41ed8%26c_page%3D2%26picID%3D99919640-1e9a-211c-f8ef-b104c41ed8b2 |date=2013-10-27 }}</ref><ref>Sven Astheimer: [https://www.faz.net/s/Rub2309A3DB4F3C4474B93AA8610A24AE0A/Doc~E9A8D9CBB52F7472F8FB90B0C45E281A1~ATpl~Ecommon~Scontent.html „Zwischen Glamour und Gelände“], in: [[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung|FAZ.net]], 17 November 2009 (current version of a text dated 3 January 2009 in the ''Frankfurter Allgemeinen Zeitung'')</ref> In this capacity, she presented Pope [[John Paul II]] with a bottle of [[Riesling]].<ref>Lucian Kim (March 9, 2016), [https://foreignpolicy.com/2016/03/09/could-this-be-angela-merkels-successor-julia-kloeckner-germany-refugees Is This the Next Woman to Run Germany?] ''[[Foreign Policy]]''.</ref>

From 1998 to 2002, Klöckner worked in the department of regional culture at [[Südwestrundfunk|SWR]] television in Mainz as a [[freelancer]] and, from 2000 to 2002, she was [[editor]] of the magazine, ''weinwelt'' ("Wine World").<ref name=JK>[http://juliakloeckner.de/ueber_mich/lebenslauf.php ''Lebenslauf''] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131023190900/http://juliakloeckner.de/ueber_mich/lebenslauf.php |date=2013-10-23 }} at juliakloeckner.de. == Political career ==
[[File:Cdu parteitag dezember 2012 kloeckner.JPG|thumb|Klöckner at the CDU party conference in 2012]]

=== Early beginnings ===
In 1996, Klöckner joined the [[Junge Union]] (JU), the [[Frauen Union]] (FU, the German "Women's Union"), the [[Christian Democratic Union (Germany)|Christian Democratic Union]] party, the [[Europa-Union Deutschland|Europa Union]] and the [[Young European Federalists]]. In 2001 she became a member of the JU's local board in Bad Kreuznach, an office she held until 2007. In the same year she also became a member of the local (county-level) CDU board in Bad Kreuznach.<ref>cdu-kv-badkreuznach.de: [http://www.cdu-kv-badkreuznach.de/7.html ''CDU-Kreisvorstand''] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921060020/http://www.cdu-kv-badkreuznach.de/7.html# |date=2013-09-21 }}</ref> In 2002, Klöckner became a member of the Regional Committee on Security Policy and the Armed Forces<ref>juliawaehlen.de: [http://cms.juliawaehlen.de/_media/Kloeckners-Kurier1.pdf Klöckners Kurier] (pdf: 324&nbsp;kB)</ref> and the JU Regional Executive of [[Rhineland-Palatinate]], where she stayed until 2007.

=== Member of the Bundestag, 2002-2011 ===

'''First term, 2002-2005'''

In October 2002, following the [[German federal election in 2002|German federal election that year]], Klöckner entered the German parliament, the [[Bundestag]], via the Rhineland-Palatinate list of state candidates, having scored 7% less than her SPD rival [[Fritz-Rudolf Körper]] in the first round of voting.

On 14 November 2002 Klöckner was elected as one of the secretaries of the Bundestag's plenary sessions.<ref>bundestag.de: [http://dipbt.bundestag.de/doc/btp/15/15010.pdf Deutscher Bundestag Stenografischer Bericht 10. Sitzung Berlin, Thu 14 Nov 2002] (pdf: 883&nbsp;kB)</ref><ref>bundestag.de: [http://dipbt.bundestag.de/doc/btd/15/000/1500050.pdf Wahlvorschlag für die Wahl der Schriftführer gemäß § 3 der Geschäftsordnung] (pdf: 178&nbsp;kB)</ref> In addition she joined the board of trustees of the [[Federal Agency for Civic Education]].<ref>bpb.de: [http://www.bpb.de/files/CQ0HX2.pdf Bundeszentrale für politische Bildung Jahresbericht 2002/2003] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090304105151/http://www.bpb.de/files/CQ0HX2.pdf |date=2009-03-04 }} (''PDF'')</ref> Later she became a member of the Committee for Consumer Protection, Food and Agriculture.<ref>ausschusslisten.de: [http://www.ausschusslisten.de/webfm_send/181 Ausschuss für Verbraucherschutz, Ernährung und Landwirtschaft, Stand 17. Dezember 2002] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130922083353/http://www.ausschusslisten.de/webfm_send/181 |date=2013-09-22 }} (''PDF'')</ref> and alternate member of the Committee for Human Rights and Humanitarian Aid.<ref>ausschusslisten.de: [http://www.ausschusslisten.de/webfm_send/302 Ausschuss für Menschenrechte und Humanitäre Hilfe, Stand 30. März 2003] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130922092432/http://www.ausschusslisten.de/webfm_send/302 |date=2013-09-22 }} (''PDF'')</ref> In 2003 she joined the [[commission of inquiry]] into "Ethics and Rights in Modern Medicine" and was a member of its sub-groups: "Ethics in Bioscientific and Medical Research" and "Transplantation Medicine".<ref>[http://webarchiv.bundestag.de/cgi/showsearchresult.php?filetoload=/srv/www/htdocs/archive/2005/0113/parlament/kommissionen/ethik_med/infobrosch__re/broschuere.pdf&id=12 Deutscher Bundestag ENQUETE-KOMMISSION ETHIK UND RECHT DER MODERNEN MEDIZIN] ''(PDF)''</ref> She also founded a parliamentary wine forum<ref>reisetravel.eu: [http://reisetravel.eu/no_cache/hotels-restaurants/wein-genuss/das-parlamentarische-weinforum.html Das Parlamentarische Weinforum]{{dead link|date=April 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> and belonged to the all-party discussion group on hospice and palliative care.

Klöckner became a member of the "Young Group" (''Junge Gruppe'') of the [[CDU/CSU]] parliamentary group in the Bundestag, where she served as deputy chairwoman.<ref>pressrelations.de: [http://www.pressrelations.de/new/standard/result_main.cfm?aktion=jour_pm&r=106929 Krings ist neuer Sprecher der Jungen Gruppe] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921055926/http://www.pressrelations.de/new/standard/result_main.cfm?aktion=jour_pm&r=106929 |date=2013-09-21 }}</ref>

'''Second term, 2005-2009'''

In the [[2005 German federal election|federal elections in 2005]] Klöckner secured the [[Kreuznach (electoral district)|constituency of Kreuznach]] for the CDU for the first time in around 50 years with 43.0% of voters placing her first. On 15 December 2005 she was re-elected as secretary to the Bundestag.<ref>bundestag.de: [http://www.bundestag.de/dokumente/protokolle/amtlicheprotokolle/2005/ap168.html 8. In July 2008 Klöckner began a two-year stint as chairwoman of the newly constituted consumer advisory body of Schufa established to represent the interests of consumer protection.

In the [[2009 German federal election|2009 federal elections]] Klöckner was confirmed as the directly elected member of parliament for the constituency of Kreuznach/Birkenfeld with 47.0% of first votes and an 18% lead over her SPD rival.<ref>[[Allgemeine Zeitung (Mainz)|Allgemeine Zeitung]]: [http://www.allgemeine-zeitung.de/region/bad-kreuznach-bad-sobernheim-kirn/stadt-bad-kreuznach/7562779.htm Julia Klöckner (CDU) verteidigt Direktmandat im Wahlkreis Bad Kreuznach]</ref> In the negotiations to form a [[coalition government]] of the Christian Democrats (CDU together with the Bavarian [[Christian Social Union of Bavaria|CSU]]) and the [[Free Democratic Party (Germany)|Free Democratic Party]] (FDP), she was part of the CDU/CSU delegation in the working group on environment, agriculture and consumer protection policies, led by [[Ilse Aigner]] and [[Michael Kauch]].

On 29 October 2009, Klöckner was appointed as a Parliamentary State Secretary of the [[Federal Ministry of Food and Agriculture]] under Minister [[Ilse Aigner]].<ref>bmelv.de: [http://www.bmelv.de/SharedDocs/Pressemitteilungen/2009/257-Vereidigung-Kloeckner.html Julia Klöckner als neue Parlamentarische Staatssekretärin vereidigt] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131021213439/http://www.bmelv.de/SharedDocs/Pressemitteilungen/2009/257-Vereidigung-Kloeckner.html# |date=2013-10-21 }}</ref> In February 2011 she resigned from this office in order to run the CDU in Rhineland-Palatinate. Her successor in this office is [[Peter Bleser]] (CDU).<ref>[http://www.peter-bleser.de/neuigkeit/peter-bleser-ist-neuer-parlamentarischer-staatssekretaer ''Peter Bleser ist neuer Parlamentarischer Staatssekretär''] at www.peter-bleser.de. Retrieved 21 October 2013</ref>

=== State politics in Rhineland-Palatinate ===
[[File:Julia Klöckner (MdL RLP 2014-01-18).jpg|thumbnail|right|Klöckner attending parliament in 2014.]]

Within the Rhineland-Palatinate CDU, Klöckner moved up to the party's state executive committee in 2003. On 13 June 2004 Julia Klöckner was elected to the county council (''[[Kreistag]]'') of [[Landkreis Bad Kreuznach|Bad Kreuznach]] with 42,888 votes, the highest number of votes of the 50 CDU candidates.<ref>cdu-kv-badkreuznach.de: [http://www.cdu-kv-badkreuznach.de/5.html Kreistagswahlergebnis 2004] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131003044458/http://www.cdu-kv-badkreuznach.de/5.html# |date=2013-10-03 }}</ref> In the next council elections on 7 June 2009 she was again elected with the most votes (this time 46,759 votes).<ref>[[Allgemeine Zeitung (Mainz)|Allgemeine Zeitung]]: [http://www.allgemeine-zeitung.de/region/bad-kreuznach-bad-sobernheim-kirn/landkreis-bad-kreuznach/6957226.htm Die neuen Kreistagsmitglieder – STATISTIK Julia Klöckner ist die Häufelkönigin]</ref><ref>kreisbadkreuznach.de: [https://archive.is/20120803170410/http://www.kreisbadkreuznach.de/Mitglieder-des-Kreistages-2009.463.0.html Mitglieder des Kreistages Legislaturperiode 2009–2014]</ref>

On 8 July 2006 Klöckner was elected as Deputy State Chairwoman of the Rhineland-Palatinate CDU with 91.5% of the vote on the CDU Party's 59th Regional Day.<ref>ju-rlp.de: [http://www.ju-rlp.de/webspace/mod_blackdm/black_9_2006.pdf BLACK DAS MAGAZIN der JUNGEN UNION RHEINLAND-PFALZ 09/10 • 3. Jahrgang • Sep./Okt. 2006] (''PDF'')</ref>

On 17 November 2009, Klöckner was proposed by the CDU chairman for Rhineland-Palatinate, [[Christian Baldauf]], as the lead candidate for the 2011 state elections.<ref>[https://www.faz.net/s/Rub594835B672714A1DB1A121534F010EE1/Doc~EEE06B4F9526E4E8E96931DF047185CE8~ATpl~Ecommon~Scontent.html?rss_googlenews faz.net: Die gut vernetzte Weinkönigin], retrieved 17 November 2009</ref> On 17 April 2010 she was duly elected as the CDU's front runner at the CDU state party conference in [[Bingen am Rhein]] with 400 out of 402 votes cast (99.5%).<ref>[[Allgemeine Zeitung (Mainz)|Allgemeine Zeitung]]: [http://www.allgemeine-zeitung.de/nachrichten/politik/rheinland-pfalz/8772232.htm Klöckner wird Spitzenkandidatin mit Traumergebnis – Baldauf gibt CDU-Vorsitz ab]</ref> On 25 September 2010 at the state party conference in [[Mainz]], she was elected as leader of the Rhineland-Palatinate CDU with 96.9% of the vote.<ref>[[Allgemeine Zeitung (Mainz)|Allgemeine Zeitung]]: [http://www.allgemeine-zeitung.de/nachrichten/politik/rheinland-pfalz/9444065.htm Julia Klöckner in Mainz zur CDU-Landeschefin gewählt – Merkels Watschn für Beck]</ref><ref>FAZ.NET: [https://www.faz.net/s/Rub594835B672714A1DB1A121534F010EE1/Doc~E940D0DF48DB4400C916DE92B99E1F7B4~ATpl~Ecommon~Scontent.html Julia Klöckner neue CDU-Landesvorsitzende] dated 26 September 2010</ref> On 4 December 2010 she achieved first place in the state list for the 2011 elections at the party conference of the Rhineland-Palatinate CDU Association in [[Ramstein-Miesenbach]], gaining 99.6% of the votes.<ref>[[Allgemeine Zeitung (Mainz)|Allgemeine Zeitung]]: [http://www.allgemeine-zeitung.de/nachrichten/politik/rheinland-pfalz/9700295.htm Rheinland-Pfalz: CDU wählt Klöckner mit Traumergebnis – Grüne: Ziel zweistellig]</ref>

In the [[2011 Rhineland-Palatinate state election|state elections on 27 March 2011]], voters gave Klöckner the direct mandate for the constituency of [[Bad Kreuznach]] with 44.4% of the votes.<ref>wahlen.rlp.de: [http://www.wahlen.rlp.de/ltw/wahlen/2011/ergebnisse/2170000000000.html?mode=HideMenu Landeswahlleiter Rheinland-Pfalz - Endgültiges Ergebnis Landtagswahl 2011 Wahlkreis Bad Kreuznach] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150209002721/http://wahlen.rlp.de/ltw/wahlen/2011/ergebnisse/2170000000000.html?mode=HideMenu |date=2015-02-09 }}</ref> After scoring their lowest result in the 2006 elections with only 32.8% of the vote, the CDU's performance state level was 2.5% higher than the national trend and just 0.5% behind the SDP, who suffered a historic loss of 9.9% of the vote.

Following the state elections, Klöckner was unanimously elected as the CDU's party leader in the Rhineland-Palatinate parliament on 30 March 2011.<ref>[http://www.cdu-rpk.de/index.php?ka=1&ska=2&idn=171 ''Julia Klöckner neue Landeschefin der CDU Rheinland-Pfalz''] at www.cdu-rpk.de, retrieved 27 September 2013.</ref>

In the [[2016 Rhineland-Palatinate state election|2016 state elections]], Klöckner again ran for the office of [[Minister-President]] of [[List of Ministers-President of Rhineland-Palatinate|Rhineland-Palatinate]]. With the [[European migrant crisis]] emerging as the dominating campaign issue, Klöckner responded by trying to harden her line on migrants while carefully avoiding any whiff of disloyalty to Chancellor Angela Merkel. In a joint statement with CDU [[Baden-Württemberg]] leading candidate [[Guido Wolf (politician)|Guido Wolf]] Klöckner proposed in February 2016 flexible daily quotas for refugee inflows into Germany, which was a step beyond Merkel's “open-doors” policy but not so far as the [[Christian Social Union in Bavaria|CSU]], the CDU's Bavaria-based sister party, which backed fixed annual limits.<ref>Stefan Wagstyl (March 8, 2016), [http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/942dc376-e532-11e5-ac45-5c039e797d1c.html Merkel protégé faces election struggle as migration backlash bites] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref> No one should be allowed to enter Germany without a reason for asylum or a protection status.<ref>[https://www.tagesschau.de/inland/fluechtlingspolitik-195.html CDU-Wahlkämpfer erhöhen Druck auf Merkel], [[Tagesschau (Germany)|Tagesschau]], in German</ref> She eventually lost against incumbent Malu Dreyer.

=== Role in national politics ===
In 2003, Klöckner was elected to the federal board of the ''Frauen Union'', a national organization for CDU women. In addition she became a deputy member in the board of the [[European People's Party]]. On 14 May 2007 she became the deputy chairwoman of the CDU Commission on the "Preservation of the Creation: Climatic, Environmental and Consumer Protection", where she led the Sub-Commission on Consumer Protection.

At the 23rd national CDU party conference on 15 November 2010, Klöckner became a member of the CDU ''Präsidium'' or steering committee with 94.43% of the votes, the highest number of votes out of all 40 candidates.<ref>karlsruhe2010.cdu.de: [http://www.karlsruhe2010.cdu.de/wahlen.html Elections at the 23rd party conference of the CDU in Germany]</ref> On 4 December 2012 she was elected as one of five deputy chairpersons of the national CDU party with 92.92% of the votes (once again the best result),<ref>hannover2012.cdu.de: [http://www.hannover2012.cdu.de/wahlen Elections at the 25th party conference of the CDU in Germany]</ref> serving alongside [[Volker Bouffier]], [[Armin Laschet]], [[Thomas Strobl]] and [[Ursula von der Leyen]].

Following the [[2013 German federal election|2013 federal elections]], Klöckner was part of the CDU/CSU team in the negotiations with the SPD on a coalition agreement for the [[Third Merkel cabinet|third government]] of [[Chancellor of Germany|Chancellor]] [[Angela Merkel]]. By 2016, she was widely seen by commentators as having quietly positioned herself as a leading candidate to replace Merkel.<ref>Paul Carrel and Thorsten Severin (January 24, 2016), [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-europe-migrants-germany-idUSKCN0V20O5 Merkel's party, sliding in polls, weighs German 'border centres'] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref>

== Political positions ==

===Social policy===
Klöckner is a declared opponent of [[abortion]] and also favours a ban on [[stem cell research]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.spiegel.de/politik/deutschland/abtreibungsgegner-im-europa-wahlkampf-konservativer-populismus-a-964402.html |title=EU-Bürgerbegehren: Kulturkampf um den Keim des Lebens |author=Annett Meiritz und Gregor Peter Schmitz |publisher=spiegel.de |date=17 April 2014 |access-date=2014-04-17}}</ref>

Klöckner herself has benefited from a CDU gender quorum introduced in 1996,  as it provided her a good position on the party's candidate list in for the 2002 federal elections. Without the quota, Klöckner has acknowledged in the past, she would not have made it as far within her party.<ref>Peter Müller and Merlind Theile, 21 November 2012, [http://www.spiegel.de/international/germany/passive-gender-equality-policy-could-backfire-for-angela-merkel-a-868343.html Let Down By the Chancellor: Merkel's Passive Gender Equality Policy Could Backfire] ''[[Der Spiegel]]''.</ref> In 2013, she demanded a legally enforced quota calling on publicly traded companies to have women hold a minimum of 30 percent of the seats on their supervisory boards, starting in 2020.<ref>[http://www.dw.de/government-seeks-boardroom-gender-quota-only-in-2020/a-16745742 Government seeks boardroom gender quota only in 2020] ''[[Deutsche Welle]]'', 15 April 2014.</ref>

===Human rights===
In May 2014, Klöckner urged Turks living in Germany to boycott a speech by [[Turkey]]'s [[Prime Minister of Turkey|Prime Minister]] [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan|Erdogan]], as a protest against his government's crackdown on protests that followed the [[Soma mine disaster]], which had killed 301 people.<ref>Alexandra Hudson, Andreas Rinke and Hans-Edzard Busemann, 19 May 2014, [https://www.reuters.com/article/2014/05/19/us-germany-turkey-idUSBREA4I0G220140519 Germany says Turkey's Erdogan welcome despite mounting criticism] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref><ref>Harriet Torry, 19 May 2014, [https://www.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304652804579572191019313818 Berlin Urges Erdogan to Show Restraint at German Rally] ''[[Wall Street Journal]]''.</ref>

When news media in 2014 revealed images purporting to show security guards abusing asylum seekers at a shelter in western Germany, Klöckner said the incident should prompt a review of the country's refugee policy and infrastructure.<ref>Harriet Torry (29 September 2014), [https://www.wsj.com/articles/images-said-to-show-abuse-of-asylum-seekers-in-germany-1412022009 Images Said to Show Abuse of Asylum Seekers in Germany] ''[[Wall Street Journal]]''.</ref> She also called for Germany to support [[Italy]], [[Greece]], and [[Turkey]] in processing asylum applications at registration centres there.<ref>Paul Carrel and Thorsten Severin (24 January 2016), [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-europe-migrants-germany-idUSKCN0V20O5 Merkel's party, sliding in polls, weighs German 'border centres'] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref>

In late 2014, Klöckner spoke out in favour of banning the [[burqa|burka]], arguing that the [[Basic Law for the Federal Republic of Germany|German constitution]] emphasized that women and men were of equal value and that "looking at people's faces" also belonged to the culture of an open society.<ref>[http://www.dw.de/cdu-state-politican-calls-for-banning-burkas-in-germany/a-18104454 CDU state politician calls for banning burkas in Germany] ''[[Deutsche Welle]]'', 1 December 2014.</ref> In 2019, Klöckner repeated her calls for banning the burka.<ref>https://www.dw.com/en/german-conservatives-renew-calls-for-a-burqa-ban/a-49861694</ref>

===Foreign policy===
Speaking on the bilateral relations between Germany and [[Israel]] in 2013, Klöckner held that while "Germany has a historical responsibility," this should not be understood as "a blank check to be uncritical in foreign policy."<ref>Christiane Hoffmann and René Pfister, 14 January 2013, [http://www.spiegel.de/international/germany/germany-debates-the-meaning-of-anti-semitism-a-877414.html Germany and Israel: A Relationship Full of Misunderstandings Germany and Israel: A Relationship Full of Misunderstandings] ''[[Der Spiegel]]''.</ref>

== Controversies ==

=== 2009 presidential election tweet ===
During the [[2009 German presidential election|German presidential election]], Klöckner prematurely reported on [[Twitter]] the results of the vote by the Federal Assembly from the official electoral polling commission (''Zählkommission'').<ref>[http://www.heise.de/newsticker/Bundespraesidenten-Wahl-Ergebnis-per-SMS-und-Twitter-verkuendet--/meldung/138331 heise online: Bundespräsidenten-Wahl: Ergebnis per SMS und Twitter verkündet], retrieved 26 May 2009</ref><ref>[https://www.faz.net/s/RubFC06D389EE76479E9E76425072B196C3/Doc~E66BAB565DE5C4E838B7AA44E6DEF51ED~ATpl~Ecommon~Scontent.html faz.net: Die Weinkönigin und der Bundeshotte], retrieved 25 July 2009</ref> About 15 minutes before the official announcement of the election result, she tweeted a reference to the last day of the [[2008–09 Bundesliga|German football premier league season]]:  ''#Bundesversammlung Leute, Ihr könnt in Ruhe Fußball gucke. Wahlgang hat geklappt!'' ("Federal assembly, people, you can watch football in peace. Election a success!"). Other members of parliament also announced the result via SMS and Twitter prematurely, but justified themselves later, by arguing that they had not participated in the vote counting.

Following criticism of her premature announcement of the result, Klöckner resigned from her post as secretary to the Bundestag.<ref>[http://www.heise.de/newsticker/Twitter-Affaere-beschaeftigt-Bundestagspraesidium--/meldung/139412 heise online: „Twitter-Affäre“ beschäftigt Bundestagspräsidium], retrieved 27 May 2009</ref>

===2019 lobbying accusations===
In June 2019, Klöckner received criticism over a video that could be interpreted as an "advertisement for Swiss food conglomerate [[Nestlé]]". In the clip, Klöckner congratulates Nestle Germany leader [[Marc-Aurel Boersch]] for Nestlé's contributions in the wider context of a nation-wide initiative for [[Voluntary compliance|voluntarily commitments]] of the [[food industry]] to reduce fat, sugar and salt in their products.<ref name="DW.COM">{{cite web | title=Nestle video leads to lobbying accusations against German minister - 06.06.2019 | website=DW.COM | url=https://www.dw.com/en/nestle-video-leads-to-lobbying-accusations-against-german-minister/a-49078899 | access-date=May 26, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Since 2019, Klöckner has been married to an [[antique car]]s dealer.<ref>[https://www.bild.de/politik/inland/politik-inland/julia-kloeckner-bundesministerin-spricht-ueber-ihren-lebensgefaehrten-57077254.bild.html  Bundeslandwirtschaftsministerin Klöckner über ihren Lebensgefährten: Er macht mir jeden Morgen Kaffee, auch um 5 Uhr] ''[[Bild]]'', September 5, 2018.</ref>
