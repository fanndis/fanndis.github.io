'''Charity Tillemann-Dick''' (July 22, 1983 – April 23, 2019<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-48043765 |title=Archived copy |access-date=April 24, 2019 |archive-date=August 23, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030156/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-48043765 |url-status=live }}</ref>), known professionally as '''Charity Sunshine Tillemann-Dick''', was an American soprano and presenter. A recipient of two bilateral (double) lung transplants, she spoke and performed frequently at concerts, conferences and events around the United States.

==Early life==
Tillemann-Dick was born in [[Denver, Colorado]], the fifth of eleven children, including siblings, diplomat and technologist [[Tomicah Tillemann]] and author [[Levi Tillemann]].<ref>[http://www.westword.com/2008-07-10/news/denver-s-own-royal-tannenbaums Denver's Own Royal Tenenbaums - Page 1] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100608123710/http://www.westword.com/2008-07-10/news/denver-s-own-royal-tannenbaums/ |date=June 8, 2010 }}, - Westword.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref> She received a bachelor's degree with high honors from [[Regis University]] and studied music at the [[Peabody Institute]] at Johns Hopkins University and the [[Liszt Academy|Franz Liszt Academy of Music]] in Budapest, where she was also a Fulbright Scholar.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Langer|first=Emily|date=April 24, 2019|title=Charity Tillemann-Dick, operatic soprano who drew life and music from transplanted lungs, dies at 35|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/obituaries/charity-tillemann-dick-operatic-soprano-who-drew-life-and-music-from-transplanted-lungs-dies-at-35/2019/04/24/f5468f5e-6602-11e9-a1b6-b29b90efa879_story.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030220/https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/obituaries/charity-tillemann-dick-operatic-soprano-who-drew-life-and-music-from-transplanted-lungs-dies-at-35/2019/04/24/f5468f5e-6602-11e9-a1b6-b29b90efa879_story.html|archive-date=August 23, 2020|access-date=2020-08-23|website=[[Washington Post]]|language=en}}</ref>

==Music==
A full lyric coloratura soprano, Tillemann-Dick performed across the United States, Europe, and Asia in venues as diverse as The Rose Theater at Lincoln Center in New York City; The Kennedy Center in Washington, DC; Severance Hall in Cleveland, Ohio; The National Symphony Hall in Budapest, Hungary; the American Embassy in Beijing, China; and the National Statuary Hall in the United States Capitol. She collaborated or performed with noted conductors and musicians including Bruno Rigacci, Joella Jones, [[Marvin Hamlisch]], [[Bono]], [[Zoltán Kocsis]], Joan Dornemann, [[Éva Marton]], and former [[Condoleezza Rice|Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice]].<ref>[http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/world/us_and_americas/article532770.ece Profile], timesonline.co.uk; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sfgate.com/health/article/Secretary-of-state-takes-stage-for-Lantos-2663207.php|title=Secretary of state takes stage for Lantos' granddaughter / Rice accompanies young soprano battling rare disease|first1=Edward|last1=Epstein|first2=Chronicle Staff|last2=Writer|date=12 June 2005|website=SFGate|access-date=April 25, 2019|archive-date=August 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030155/https://www.sfgate.com/health/article/Secretary-of-state-takes-stage-for-Lantos-2663207.php|url-status=live}}</ref> Some of her opera roles included Titania in ''[[A Midsummer Night's Dream (Mendelssohn)|A Midsummer's Night Dream]]'', Gilda in ''[[Rigoletto]]'', and Violetta in ''[[La traviata]]''.

Tillemann-Dick performed for numerous presidents, prime ministers, members of Congress and world dignitaries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hXKxlS4vlGA|title=President Sarkozy diner Appeal of Conscience Foundation|first=|last=Ron Agam|date=30 September 2008|publisher=|via=YouTube|access-date=November 26, 2013|archive-date=August 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030210/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hXKxlS4vlGA|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Health and advocacy==
After receiving a diagnosis of idiopathic [[pulmonary hypertension]] in 2004, Tillemann-Dick served as the national spokesperson for the [[Pulmonary Hypertension Association]]. In December 2005, she testified about pulmonary hypertension before the U.S. Congress' Committee on Energy and Commerce.<ref>[http://ftp.resource.org/gpo.gov/hearings/109h/25704.txt Testimony before U.S. Congress] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120407030805/http://ftp.resource.org/gpo.gov/hearings/109h/25704.txt |date=April 7, 2012 }}, ftp.resource.org/gpo.gov; accessed November 8, 2014.</ref>

In September 2009, Tillemann-Dick underwent a double lung transplant at the Cleveland Clinic. Just eight months later, she performed before the doctors, nurses, and support staff at the Clinic.<ref>[http://www.cleveland.com/healthfit/index.ssf/2010/05/double-lung_transplant_patient.html Double-lung transplant patient to perform opera at Cleveland Clinic summit (video)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100529041501/http://www.cleveland.com/healthfit/index.ssf/2010/05/double-lung_transplant_patient.html |date=May 29, 2010 }}, cleveland.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref><ref>[http://www.newsnet5.com/dpp/news/health/young-opera-singer-diagnosed-with-life-threatening-lung-disorder-sings-for-her-doctors- "Young opera singer diagnosed with life-threatening lung disorder sings for her doctors"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101101045823/http://www.newsnet5.com/dpp/news/health/young-opera-singer-diagnosed-with-life-threatening-lung-disorder-sings-for-her-doctors- |date=November 1, 2010 }}, newsnet5.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref><ref>[http://www.fox8.com/news/wjw-opera-singer-txt,0,3752698.story Profile]  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100529051653/http://www.fox8.com/news/wjw-opera-singer-txt%2C0%2C3752698.story |date=May 29, 2010 }}, fox8.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref><ref>[http://www.cnn.com/2010/HEALTH/06/23/soprano.lung.transplant/index.html "Soprano serenades doctors after lung transplant"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030155/http://www.cnn.com/2010/HEALTH/06/23/soprano.lung.transplant/index.html |date=August 23, 2020 }}, CNN.com; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref>

Tillemann-Dick then became a vocal advocate for organ donation and transplantation research in the United States. After complications from rejection, she received a second double lung transplant in January 2012.

== Personal life ==
Tillemann-Dick was the maternal granddaughter of Annette (Tillemann) and [[Tom Lantos|Congressman Tom Lantos]], the former Chairman of the U.S. House Committee on Foreign Affairs and the only Holocaust survivor ever elected to the U.S. Congress. Her paternal grandmother, [[Nancy E. Dick]], was the first female Lieutenant Governor of [[Colorado]]. On April 10, 2008, Tillemann-Dick's father, Timber Dick, a successful inventor and businessman from Denver, died from injuries received in a car accident.<ref>[http://www.denverpost.com/obituaries/ci_8891919 "Denver inventor Timber Dick dies after crash"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303191312/http://www.denverpost.com/obituaries/ci_8891919 |date=March 3, 2016 }}, ''The Denver Post''; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref>

She was married to Yonatan Doron; they lived in Baltimore, Maryland.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-48043765 |title=Charity 'Sunshine' Tillemann-Dick: Opera singer with transplanted lungs dies |publisher=BBC News |date=April 24, 2019 |access-date=April 24, 2019 |archive-date=August 23, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030156/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-48043765 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Tillemann-Dick was a member of [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]].<ref>[http://www.getreligion.org/2009/07/mormons-dont-eat-meat "Mormons don't eat meat?"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030307/https://www.getreligion.org/2009/07/mormons-dont-eat-meat |date=August 23, 2020 }}, getreligion.org, July 2009; accessed November 9, 2014.</ref>

== Death ==
In August 2018, she announced that she had cancer.<ref>{{cite web|title=Opera Singer Battles Cancer after 2 Double Lung Transplants|url=https://my.clevelandclinic.org/podcasts/the-comeback/opera-singer-battles-cancer-after-2-double-lung-transplants|website=Cleveland Clinic|access-date=April 24, 2019|archive-date=August 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030236/https://my.clevelandclinic.org/podcasts/the-comeback/opera-singer-battles-cancer-after-2-double-lung-transplants|url-status=live}}</ref> On April 23, 2019, Tillemann-Dick died at the age of 35, from long-term [[immunosuppression]] from the transplants.<ref>{{cite web|date=24 April 2019|title=Opera singer with transplanted lungs dies|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-48043765|publisher=|via=www.bbc.co.uk|access-date=April 24, 2019|archive-date=August 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823030156/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-48043765|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":0" />
