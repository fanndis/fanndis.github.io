[[Angelique Kerber]] defeated defending champion [[Serena Williams]] in the final, 6–4, 3–6, 6–4 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2016 Australian Open]]. Kerber became the first woman to win a singles major title after having [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records#Won a title after saving match points|saved a match point in the first round]], which she did against [[Misaki Doi]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.vavel.com/en-us/tennis-usa/593255-australian-open-angelique-kerber-advances-despite-scare.html | title=Kerber saves match point | accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref> She is also the first German of either sex to win a major since Graf at the [[1999 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|1999 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-01-30/australian-open3a-serena-williams-beats-angelique-kerber-to-wi/7127362?section=sport|title=Australian Open: Angelique Kerber stuns Serena Williams to win women's final|publisher=ABC News ([[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]])|date=30 January 2016|accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/jan/30/serena-williams-angelque-kerber-australian-open-final-tennis|title=Angelique Kerber stuns Serena Williams to clinch Australian Open title|date=30 January 2016|work=Guardian|accessdate=1 February 2016}}</ref> Serena Williams was attempting for the second time to equal [[Steffi Graf]]'s [[WTA Tour records#Player totals|career record]] of 22 major singles titles.

Serena Williams' loss to Angelique Kerber in the final ended Serena's 8 match winning streak in Grand Slam finals.

The top three seeds (Williams, [[Simona Halep]] and [[Garbiñe Muguruza]]) were in contention for the world [[Women's Tennis Association#WTA Rankings|No. 1 ranking]] at the beginning of the tournament. However, Halep and Muguruza were eliminated in the first and third rounds, respectively, with Williams retaining the top spot at the end of the tournament.	

The twelve seeds that lost in the opening round was the most in the opening round of any women's [[Grand Slam (tennis)|major]] tournament since the 32-seed draw was adopted at the [[2001 Wimbledon Championships]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/5285581/title/insider-notebook-upsets-and-azarenka|title=INSIDER NOTEBOOK: UPSETS AND AZARENKA|publisher=Women's Tennis Association|first=Courtney|last=Nguyen|date=19 January 2016|accessdate=19 January 2016}}</ref>

[[Johanna Konta]] became the first British woman to reach the semi-final of a major since [[Jo Durie]] in 1983.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/tennis/news/12110/10145293/johanna-konta-reaches-australian-open-semis-for-first-time|title=Johanna Konta reaches Australian Open semis for first time|publisher=Sky Sports|first=Andrew|last=Dickson|date=27 January 2016|accessdate=27 January 2016}}</ref> [[Zhang Shuai (tennis)|Zhang Shuai]] became the first qualifier to reach the Australian Open quarter-finals since [[Angélica Gavaldón]] in [[1990 Australian Open – Women's Singles|1990]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://au.eurosport.com/tennis/australian-open-women/2016/zhang-shuai-overcomes-injured-madison-keys-who-ends-match-in-tears_sto5086192/story.shtml|title= Qualifier Zhang Shuai overcomes injured Madison Keys, who ends match in tears|publisher=au.eurosport.com|work=EuroSport |date= 26 January 2016|accessdate=26 January 2016}}</ref>

This was the first Grand Slam appearance for Japan's first ever Grand Slam winner, [[Naomi Osaka]]. She lost to former World No. 1 [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the third round. Three years later, Osaka would go on to claim the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|World Number 1]] ranking after winning the [[2019 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2019]] title.

This was the final event in which 2008 champion and former world No. 1 [[Maria Sharapova]] competed in until the [[2017 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix – Singles|2017 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix]]. Sharapova failed a drug test at this tournament, testing positive for the banned substance [[meldonium]], and was suspended from competitive tennis for 15 months as a result. She lost in the quarterfinals to Serena Williams.
