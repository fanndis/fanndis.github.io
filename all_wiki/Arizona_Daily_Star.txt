The '''''Arizona Daily Star''''' is the major morning [[daily newspaper]] that serves [[Tucson, Arizona|Tucson]] and surrounding districts of southern [[Arizona]] in the [[United States]]. 

==History==
[[L. C. Hughes]] was the [[List of governors of Arizona|Arizona Territory governor]] and founder of the ''Arizona Star'', in 1877. The first issue was published on March 29, 1877.<ref>{{cite web |title=About The Arizona star. [volume] (Tucson, Pima County, Ariz.) 1877-1877 |url=https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn84021914/ |website=Chronicling America |publisher=Library of Congress |accessdate=20 October 2020}}</ref> The newspaper became the ''Arizona Daily Star'' in June 1879.<ref>{{cite web |title=About Arizona daily star. [volume] (Tucson, A.T. [Ariz.]) 1879-current |url=https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn84020668/ |website=Chronicling America |publisher=Library of Congress |accessdate=20 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last=Wagoner |first=Jay J. |title=Arizona Territory 1863&ndash;1912: A Political history |publisher=University of Arizona Press |location=Tucson |year=1970 |isbn=0-8165-0176-9 |url-access=registration |url=https://archive.org/details/arizonaterritory00wago }}</ref>

The paper was purchased by [[Pulitzer, Inc.|Pulitzer]] in 1971; [[Lee Enterprises]] bought Pulitzer in 2005.

==Awards==
In [[1981 Pulitzer Prize|1981]], ''Star'' reporters Clark Hallas and Robert B. Lowe won the [[Pulitzer Prize for Local Investigative Specialized Reporting]] for their stories about recruiting violations by [[University of Arizona]] [[college football|football]] coach, [[Tony Mason (football coach)|Tony Mason]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pulitzer.org/winners/clark-hallas-and-robert-b-lowe|title=The Pulitzer Prizes|accessdate=September 5, 2019}}</ref>
