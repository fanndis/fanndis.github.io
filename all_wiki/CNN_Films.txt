'''CNN Films''' is a motion picture [[division (business)|division]] of [[CNN]], originally launched in 2012.<ref>{{cite news|title=CNN To Start Documentary Film Unit|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/10/08/cnn-documentary-film-unit_n_1948217.html|work=The Huffington Post|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=October 8, 2012}}</ref> Its first film, ''[[Girl Rising (film)|Girl Rising]]'' premiered in spring 2013 in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|last=Benzine|first=Adam|title=CNN launches feature docs division with "Girl Rising"|url=http://realscreen.com/2012/10/08/cnn-launches-feature-docs-division-with-girl-rising/|publisher=Real Screen|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=October 8, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=CNN Announces Creation Of CNN Films|url=http://www.foxtel.com.au/whats-on/foxtel-insider/cnn-announces-creation-of-cnn-films-201771.htm|publisher=Foxtel|accessdate=April 30, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130519055227/http://www.foxtel.com.au/whats-on/foxtel-insider/cnn-announces-creation-of-cnn-films-201771.htm|archive-date=May 19, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==History==
On October 8, 2012, CNN announced the creation of CNN Films. CNN says that it will acquire and commission original feature-length documentaries that will "examine an array of political, social, and economic subject matters." It also signed development deals with documentary directors [[Alex Gibney]] and [[Andrew Rossi]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Lieberman|first=David|title=CNN Creates Unit To Acquire Documentary Films For Theaters And TV|url=https://www.deadline.com/2012/10/cnn-documentary-films/|publisher=Deadline.com|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=October 8, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Flint|first=Joe|title=CNN's creation of film unit is step in right direction|url=https://articles.latimes.com/2012/oct/08/entertainment/la-et-ct-cnn-films-20121008|work=Los Angeles Times|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=October 8, 2012}}</ref> Since its creation, it already acquired the rights for ''Girl Rising'', a 100-minute documentary narrated by [[Meryl Streep]], [[Anne Hathaway]], [[Kerry Washington]], and [[Selena Gomez]].<ref name=Girl>{{cite web|last=Raftery|first=Liz|title=Girl Rising to Help Launch CNN Films|url=https://www.tvguide.com/News/Girl-Rising-Help-1054348.aspx|work=TV Guide|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=October 8, 2012}}</ref>

While the documentaries will initially air on CNN, the network reportedly plans to enter them in film festivals and distribute them to theaters as well.

At the [[2013 Sundance Film Festival]], CNN announced the acquisition of three documentaries. It acquired the rights to air ''[[Life Itself (2014 documentary film)|Life Itself]]'', a film adaptation of [[Roger Ebert]]'s 2011 memoir, in television. The project will be directed by [[Steve James (producer)|Steve James]] and executive produced by [[Martin Scorsese]] and [[Steven Zaillian]]. It is scheduled to air in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Patten|first=Dominic|title=Sundance: CNN Films Makes Buying Debut; Announces Trio Of Documentaries|url=https://www.deadline.com/2013/01/sundance-film-festival-cnn-films-martin-scorsese-roger-ebert-documentaries/|publisher=Deadline.com|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=January 18, 2013}}</ref> The two other untitled projects are expected to air on CNN in 2013. One, from directors [[Michael Tucker (director)|Michael Tucker]] and Petra Epperleini, centers on [[September 11 attacks|9/11]] and reconstructs the events at [[World Trade Center site|Ground Zero]]; the other, from director [[Andrew Rossi]], focuses on the transformation of higher education, examining the costs and relevance of college.<ref>{{cite news|last=Miller|first=Daniel|title=Sundance 2013: CNN Films Announces Three Documentaries|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/sundance-2013-cnn-films-announces-413673|work=The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=January 18, 2013}}</ref>

CNN Films bought the television rights of ''[[Escape Fire: The Fight to Rescue American Healthcare]]'' which premiered on March 10, 2013 on CNN. The film was directed by [[Matthew Heineman]] and [[Susan Froemke]]. The film premiered on January 19, 2012 at the 2013 Sundance Film Festival and was theatrically released on October 5, 2012.<ref name=Escape>{{cite web|last=Patten|first=Dominic|title=CNN Films Acquires ‘Escape Fire’ U.S. TV Rights; Healthcare Doc To Air In March|url=https://www.deadline.com/2013/03/cnn-films-acquires-escape-fire-the-fight-to-rescue-american-healthcare-cnn-jeff-zucker-sundance-film-festival/|publisher=Deadline.com|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=March 1, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=CNN Films: ‘Escape Fire’ Debuts on CNN on March 10 at 8pm ET|url=http://cnnpressroom.blogs.cnn.com/2013/03/01/cnn-launches-escape-fire-on-cable-television-in-march/|publisher=CNN|accessdate=May 1, 2013|date=March 1, 2013}}</ref>

It recently acquired Penny Lane's ''Our Nixon''. CNN plans to premiere the film in August, while [[Cinedigm]] will release it theatrically.<ref name=Nixon>{{cite news|last=Guthrie|first=Marisa|title=CNN Films Snaps Up Richard Nixon Documentary (Exclusive)|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/cnn-films-snaps-up-richard-444766|work=The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=April 24, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Fleischer|first=Matthew|title=CNN Films acquires SXSW doc 'Our Nixon' to air on the network in August|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/cotown/la-et-ct-cnn-films-acquires-our-nixon-cinedigm-sxsw-20130424,0,6743114.story|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=April 24, 2013|work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=CNN Films and Cinedigm Acquire ‘Our Nixon’|url=http://cnnpressroom.blogs.cnn.com/2013/04/24/cnn-films-and-cinedigm-acquire-our-nixon-featuring-newly-released-personal-footage-shot-by-richard-nixons-closest-aides/|publisher=CNN|accessdate=April 30, 2013|date=April 24, 2013}}</ref> The network has also then acquired the domestic television broadcast rights of the Sundance film selection [[Robert Stone (director)|Robert Stone]]'s ''Pandora's Promise'' and is slated to air on CNN on November while it will be theatrically released on June 12.<ref name=Pandora>{{cite news|last=Fleischer|first=Matthew|title=CNN continues acquisition spree with 'Pandora's Promise'|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/cotown/la-et-ct-cnn-continues-acquisition-spree-with-pandoras-promise-20130430,0,1937497.story|work=Los Angeles Times|accessdate=May 1, 2013|date=April 30, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=CNN Films Announces Acquisition of ‘Pandora’s Promise’ for Fall 2013 Broadcast|url=http://cnnpressroom.blogs.cnn.com/2013/04/30/cnn-films-announces-acquisition-of-pandoras-promise-for-fall-2013-broadcast/?iref=allsearch|publisher=CNN|accessdate=May 1, 2013|date=April 30, 2013}}</ref>
