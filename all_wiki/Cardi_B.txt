'''Belcalis Marlenis Almánzar''' (born October 11, 1992), known professionally as '''Cardi B''', is an American rapper, songwriter, and actress.<ref name="Cobo">{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8463929/-i-like-it-hitting-no-1-means-to-latin-music-billboard-chart-cardi-b-j-balvin-bad-bunny |title=The Times Have Changed: What 'I Like It' Hitting No. 1 Means to Latin Music |last=Cobo |first=Leila |date=July 3, 2018 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=July 6, 2018}}</ref> Born in [[Manhattan]] and raised in [[the Bronx]], New York City, she became an [[Internet celebrity]] after several of her posts and videos became popular on [[Vine (service)|Vine]] and [[Instagram]].<ref name="fader1">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/06/22/cardi-b-cover-story-interview|title=Cardi B Did It Her Way|author=Kameir, Rawiwa|date=June 22, 2017|magazine=The Fader|accessdate=July 17, 2017}}</ref> From 2015 to 2017, she appeared as a regular cast member on the [[VH1]] [[reality television]] series ''[[Love & Hip Hop: New York]]'', which depicted her pursuit of her music aspirations. She released two [[mixtape]]s—''[[Gangsta Bitch Music, Vol. 1]]'' and ''[[Gangsta Bitch Music, Vol. 2|Vol. 2]]'', before signing with label [[Atlantic Records]] in early 2017.

Her debut studio album, ''[[Invasion of Privacy (album)|Invasion of Privacy]]'' (2018), debuted at number one on the [[Billboard 200|'' Billboard'' 200]], broke several [[streaming media|streaming]] records, was certified [[RIAA certification|triple platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] and named by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' the top female rap album of the 2010s. Critically acclaimed, it won the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album]], making Cardi B the only woman to win the award as a solo artist, as well as the first female rap artist in 15 years to be nominated for [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]]. It spawned two number-one singles on the [[Billboard Hot 100|'' Billboard'' Hot 100]]; "[[Bodak Yellow]]", which made her the second female rapper to top the chart with a solo output—following [[Lauryn Hill]] in 1998—and "[[I Like It (Cardi B, Bad Bunny and J Balvin song)|I Like It]]", which made her the first female rapper to attain multiple number-one songs on the chart. Her [[Maroon 5]] collaboration "[[Girls Like You]]" made her the only female rapper to top the Hot 100 three times. "[[WAP (song)|WAP]]", the lead single of her second album, expanded her record as [[List of Billboard Hot 100 chart achievements and milestones|the female rapper with the most Hot 100 number-one singles]] as her fourth leader, and made her the only female rap artist to achieve chart-topping singles in two decades (2010s and 2020s).

Recognized by ''[[Forbes]]'' as one of the most influential female rappers of all time, Cardi B is known for her aggressive flow and candid lyrics, which have received widespread media coverage. She is the highest-certified female rapper of all time on the [[List of highest-certified music artists in the United States|RIAA's Top Artists (Digital Singles)]] ranking, also appearing among the ten highest-certified female artists and having [[Cardi B discography|the two top certified songs]] by a female rap artist. She is the only female rapper with [[List of most-streamed songs on Spotify|multiple billion-streamers]] on [[Spotify]]. [[List of awards and nominations received by Cardi B|Her accolades]] include a [[Grammy Award]], eight [[Billboard Music Awards|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], five [[Guinness World Records]], five [[American Music Awards]], eleven [[BET Hip Hop Awards]] and two [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers#Awards|Songwriter of the Year]] awards. In 2018 ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine included her on their [[Time 100|annual list]] of the 100 most influential people in the world, and in 2020, ''Billboard'' honored her as [[Billboard Women in Music#Woman of the Year Award|Woman of the Year]].

==Achievements==
[[File:Cardi_b_(48642557132).jpg|thumb|Cardi B at [[Pinnacle Bank Arena]] in 2019]]
{{Main|List of awards and nominations received by Cardi B}}

Cardi B is the recipient of numerous accolades, including a [[Grammy Award]], eight [[Billboard Music Awards|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], five [[Guinness World Records]], five [[American Music Awards]], four [[MTV Video Music Awards]], four [[BET Awards]], and eleven [[BET Hip Hop Awards]]. ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' included her on their [[Time 100|annual list]] of the 100 most influential people in the world in 2018.<ref name="time 100"/> She received the [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] award for [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers#Awards|Songwriter of the Year]] in 2019, becoming the first female rapper to win the award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/ogdenpayne/2019/06/21/cardi-b-takes-home-ascap-songwriter-of-the-year-award/|title=Cardi B Takes Home ASCAP Songwriter Of The Year Award|first=Ogden|last=Payne|website=Forbes}}</ref> She received the honor for the second time in 2020, making her the first female songwriter to win the award twice.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/9418384/cardi-b-first-woman-repeat-songwriter-of-the-year-ascap-rhythm-soul-awards|title=Cardi B Is First Woman to Repeat as Songwriter of the Year at ASCAP's Rhythm & Soul Awards|work=Billboard}}</ref> In 2020, Cardi B became the first female rapper to be named [[Billboard Women in Music#Woman of the Year Award|Woman of the Year]] at the [[Billboard Women in Music|''Billboard'' Women in Music]] Awards. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/women-in-music/9485074/2020-women-in-music-event-cardi-b-jennifer-lopez/ |title=Cardi B, Jennifer Lopez, Dolly Parton, Dua Lipa & More to Be Honored at Billboard's 2020 Women in Music Event|work=Billboard |year=2020|accessdate=November 18, 2020}}</ref>

Cardi B is the female rapper with the most [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] number one singles (4)<ref name="I like it"/> and the one with the most total weeks on the top position (15).<ref name="hot 100 11"/> "Bodak Yellow" and "I Like It"—both certified [[RIAA certification|nonuple platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA)—became the highest-certified singles by a female rapper.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://allhiphop.com/news/cardi-b-s-bodak-yellow-becomes-highest-riaa-certified-single-by-a-female-rapper-YJbs3a2s-UCJyR-w0WtMDA|title=Cardi B's "Bodak Yellow" Becomes Highest RIAA-Certified Single By A Female Rapper|website=All Hip Hop}}</ref> "I Like It" became the first song led by a female rapper to surpass [[List of most-streamed songs on Spotify|a billion streams]] on [[Spotify]], also making her the first woman in hip hop with multiple billion-streamers on the service, with a total of three so far.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2020/02/13/cardi-b-has-made-history-on-spotify/|title=Cardi B Has Made History On Spotify|first=Hugh|last=McIntyre|website=Forbes}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/number-day-dj-snakes-taki-taki-amasses-billion-123020865.html|title=Number of the day: DJ Snake's 'Taki Taki' amasses a billion plays on Spotify|work=Yahoo!|author=[[Agence France-Presse]]|date=June 9, 2020}}</ref> ''Invasion of Privacy'' was the top female rap album of the 2010s, according to the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] decade-end chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/decade-end/billboard-200|title=Billboard 200 - Decade-End|website=Billboard}}</ref> It also became the longest-charting album by a female rapper on the ''Billboard'' 200,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://allhiphop.com/news/cardi-b-s-invasion-of-privacy-becomes-longest-charting-album-by-a-female-rapper-pjQSZksRi0OLPWzaXgsfgw|title=Cardi B's 'Invasion Of Privacy' Becomes Longest-Charting Album By A Female Rapper|website=All Hip Hop}}</ref> and the most-streamed album by a female rapper on Spotify.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bet.com/music/2019/12/30/cardi-b-invasion-of-privacy-spotify.html?cid=facebook|title=Cardi B's 'Invasion Of Privacy' Is The Most-Streamed Album By A Female Rapper Ever On Spotify|website=BET.com}}</ref> ''Invasion of Privacy''—which made her the first female rapper to win the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album]] as a solo artist—became the first female rap album [[Under Construction (Missy Elliott album)|in fifteen years]] to be nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year]].<ref name="iop aoty nom"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/feb/11/diversity-wins-as-female-artists-and-hip-hop-triumph-at-grammys|title=Diversity wins as female artists and hip-hop triumph at Grammys|work=The Guardian}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' and ''Billboard'' ranked her debut album number 13 and 34 on their critics' lists of best albums of the 2010s respectively, both the highest rank for a female rapper for the decade.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/best-albums-2010s-ranked-913997/|title=The 100 Best Albums of the 2010s|first1=Jon Dolan,Brittany Spanos,Simon ==Personal life==

Cardi B is Catholic;<ref>{{cite web|title=Cardi B on Her Kicking Baby and Pearl-Covered Dress|url=https://video.vogue.com/watch/met-gala-cardi-b-on-her-kicking-baby-and-pearl-covered-dress|website=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|publisher=[[Condé Nast]]|accessdate=May 29, 2018}}</ref> she has mentioned her "strong relationship" with God in interviews, often saying that she directly communicates with God.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UZhsgC0FIQk |title=Cardi B – God Brought Her To The Strip Club To Save Her Life |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=February 21, 2017 |accessdate=July 17, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3E1WnX_U8wA |title=CARDI B ON GOD : Explains Why She Speaks to God – Says It's A 50/50 (VIDEO) |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=February 7, 2017 |accessdate=July 17, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://rickeysmileymorningshow.com/1758012/cardi-b-strip-club/ |title=Getting To Know Cardi B Interview Part Two |publisher=The Rickey Smiley Morning Show |date=September 16, 2015 |accessdate=July 17, 2017}}</ref>

Cardi B's younger sister, Hennessy Carolina, also has a strong following on social media and has accompanied her to award shows, such as the [[2017 Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8252791/cardi-b-on-metoo-video-vixens|title=Cardi B Says #MeToo Movement Has Ignored Video Vixens: 'Nobody Gives a F---'|work=Billboard|date=March 19, 2018|accessdate=April 3, 2018}}</ref>

When my father taught me about Caribbean countries, he told me that these Europeans took over our lands. Because we know that there's African roots inside of us...<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.crfashionbook.com/celebrity/a15956294/card-b-zendaya-cr-fashion-book-interview/|title=Cardi B Opens up to Zendaya in the New Issue of CR Fashion Book|work=[[CR Fashion Book]]|date=December 21, 2018|accessdate=January 19, 2019}}</ref>}}

Cardi has opened up about the [[Me Too movement|#MeToo]] movement and being sexually assaulted.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/celebrity/latest/a19486392/cardi-b-metoo-story-women-hiphop/|title=Cardi B Shares Her Own #MeToo Story and Stands Up for Women in Hip-Hop|first=Erica|last=Gonzales|website=www.harpersbazaar.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/celebs/a18930050/cardi-b-cosmopolitan-cover-interview/|title=Cardi B Gives Her Most Explicit Interview Yet|first=Jazmine|last=Hughes|date=March 19, 2018|website=Cosmopolitan}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/cardi-b-wants-metoo-movement-inclusive-2268950|title=Cardi B wants the #MeToo movement to be more inclusive - NME|first=Tom|last=Connick|date=March 19, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=SzlDn_8Odv8|title=How Did Cardi B Handle Her #Metoo Incident?}}</ref>

===Relationships===
As of early 2017, Cardi B began publicly dating fellow American rapper [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], of the [[southern hip hop]] group [[Migos]].<ref name=NYT/> When speaking on her relationship with Offset, Cardi B told ''The Fader,'' "It's been a blessing, me meeting him and meeting his friends. I see how hard they work. And that motivated me to work even harder. And I see how good things are going for them and how popping it is to be number one. And I'm like, I want that. A lot of people just see they jewelry and they money, but I don't think a lot of people see how hard they work for that shit every single day."<ref name=fader1/> Cardi B and Offset became engaged on October 27, 2017, after Offset proposed to her at the [[Wells Fargo Center (Philadelphia)|Wells Fargo Center]] in Philadelphia, during the [[WUSL|Power 99]] Powerhouse concert.<ref>{{cite web|author=Ballin, Sofiya|date=October 28, 2017|accessdate=October 28, 2017|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/entertainment/cardi-b-gets-engaged-at-power-99s-powerhouse-concert-in-philly-20171028.html|title=Cardi B gets engaged at Power 99's Powerhouse Concert in Philly|publisher=philly.com}}</ref> On April 7, 2018, during her second performance on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'', Cardi B wore a stunning, white [[Christian Siriano]] evening gown which, along with several deliberate camera side shots, revealed her pregnancy. She was about 6 months (24 weeks) pregnant at the time.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8297568/cardi-b-pregnant-snl |title=Cardi B Reveals Pregnancy in 'SNL' Performance |last=DiMeglio |first=Mary J. |date=April 8, 2018 |work=Billboard |access-date=April 8, 2018}}</ref> On June 25, 2018, [[TMZ]] found a marriage license revealing Cardi B and Offset had actually secretly married in September 2017, doing so one month before the public proposal. Cardi B later went on to confirm this revelation in a social media post.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8462548/cardi-b-offset-married |title=Cardi B Confirms Secret Marriage to Offset: 'There Are Some Moments I Want to Keep for Myself' |last=Whittum |first=Connor |date=June 25, 2018 |work=Billboard |accessdate=June 25, 2018}}</ref> In July 2018, Cardi B gave birth to her first child, a daughter named Kulture Kiari Cephus.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/amp/Entertainment/wireStory/cardi-announces-birth-daughter-instagram-56511854|title=Cardi B announces birth of daughter on Instagram|publisher=ABC News|accessdate=July 11, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713233411/https://abcnews.go.com/amp/Entertainment/wireStory/cardi-announces-birth-daughter-instagram-56511854|archive-date=July 13, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> In December 2018 she announced on [[Instagram]] that she and Offset had broken up, though the pair later reunited.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Offset reveals how he and Cardi B got back together|url=https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/offset-reveals-how-he-and-cardi-b-got-back-together|last=Sager|first=Jessica|date=February 28, 2019|website=Fox News|language=en-US|access-date=May 10, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Merrett |first1=Robyn |last2=Sands |first2=Nicole |title=Cardi B Says She and Husband Offset Are Not Together Anymore: 'I Guess We Fell Out of Love' |url=https://people.com/music/cardi-b-offset-split/ |website=People |accessdate=December 5, 2018}}</ref> In February 2019, the couple made a public appearance for the Grammys.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.eonline.com/news/1013374/cardi-b-and-offset-share-major-pda-moment-on-the-2019-grammys-red-carpet|title=Cardi B and Offset Share Major PDA Moment on the 2019 Grammys Red Carpet/|work=E! Online|date=February 11, 2019}}</ref> He accompanied her on the stage during her acceptance speech for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/grammys-2019-cardi-b-wins-best-rap-album/|title=Grammys 2019: Cardi B Wins Best Rap Album|website=Pitchfork|accessdate=February 17, 2019}}</ref> In September 2020, it was reported that Cardi B had filed for divorce,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/au/news/1188154/cardi-b-files-for-divorce-from-offset-after-3-years-of-marriage|title=Cardi B files for divorce from Offset after 3 years of marriage|website=eonline.com|date=September 15, 2020}}</ref><ref>https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-54174437</ref> but the next month it was revealed they were back together.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/music/cardi-b-says-back-with-offset-amid-divorce/|title=Cardi B Reveals She's Back with Offset Amid Divorce: 'It's Hard Not to Talk to Your Best Friend'|website=People.com}}</ref>

===Legal issues===
On October 1, 2018, Cardi B agreed to meet with investigators at a [[Queens]] police station in connection with an alleged assault. She denied involvement through her attorney.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.apnews.com/821226955a3f433f99b2edb5614b0759/|title=Cardi B gets assault summons but lawyer say she did no harm|date=October 1, 2018|work=[[AP News|AP]]}}</ref> She was charged with two [[misdemeanor]]s: assault and reckless endangerment.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://people.com/music/cardi-b-surrendered-police-ordering-attack-strip-club-bartenders/|title=Cardi B Turns Herself in to Police, Arrested and Charged for Alleged Attack on Bartenders|first1=Maria|last1=Pasquini|first2=Sarah|last2=Michaud|first3=Janine|last3=Rubenstein|date=October 1, 2018|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref> Cardi B appeared in court for her arraignment on December 7, 2018, after she failed to show up for the originally scheduled date due to a scheduling conflict, according to her attorney. She was ordered by the judge to avoid having any contact with the two bartenders. She was released by the judge despite prosecutors requesting bail to be set at $2,500.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://amp.cnn.com/cnn/2018/12/07/entertainment/cardi-b-court-hearing/index.html|title=Cardi B shows up to court and avoids potential arrest|last=Melas|first=Chloe|date=December 7, 2018|work=[[CNN]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/cardi-b-strip-club-assault-hearing-766243/amp/|title=Cardi B Appears in Court for Hearing Over Strip Club Assault Charges|last=Kreps|first=Daniel|date=December 7, 2018|work=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> On June 21, 2019, a [[grand jury]] indicted Cardi B on 14 charges, including two counts of felony assault with intent to cause serious physical injury, stemming from the incident.<ref name="nbcncharge">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/cardi-b-indicted-unspecified-charges-alleged-strip-club-brawl-n1020271|title=Cardi B indicted on felony charges in alleged strip-club brawl|last=Fieldstadt|first=Elisha|date=June 21, 2019|work=[[NBC]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-48723275|title=Cardi B charged with strip club assault|date=June 21, 2019|work=[[BBC]]}}</ref> She was arraigned on June 25, 2019 and pleaded not guilty on all charges.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/cardi-b-pleads-not-guilty-strip-club-fight-charges-n1021591|title=Cardi B pleads not guilty to strip club fight charges|work=NBC News}}</ref>
