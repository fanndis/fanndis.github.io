''' Edward Crosby Johnson II''' (1898–1984) was an American businessman and lawyer who founded [[Fidelity Investments]]. 

Johnson came from a family of New England [[Puritan]] ancestry.<ref>Alex Taylor III, "WHY FIDELITY IS THE MASTER OF MUTUAL FUNDS" (1986) archive.fortune.com/magazines/fortune/fortune_archive/1986/09/01/67986/index.htm</ref> He graduated from [[Milton Academy]], [[Harvard College]] and [[Harvard Law School]]<ref name="hbsbio">{{cite web|title=Edward C. Johnson II|url=http://www.hbs.edu/leadership/20th-century-leaders/Pages/details.aspx?profile=edward_c_johnson_ii|website=Harvard Business School|accessdate=March 4, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bostonmagazine.com/news/2018/08/07/abby-johnson-fidelity/|title=Who's Afraid of Abby Johnson?|date=2018-08-07|website=Boston Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-15}}</ref> and became involved in stock market research in 1924.<ref name="nytimesobit">{{cite news|title=Edward Johnson 2d, Was 86; Began Investment Company|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1984/04/05/obituaries/edward-johnson-2d-was-86-began-investment-company.html|accessdate=September 20, 2018|work=The New York Times|date=October 5, 1984}}</ref> 

Johnson II applied to have the "Fidelity Fund" approved in May 1930; [[John C. Hull (politician)|John C. Hull]] (Securities Director of Massachusetts)  approved the "Fidelity Fund" - (FIDELITY FUND, INC. Incorporated in Massachusetts May 1, 1930. as an investment trust of the general man agement type. Officers: E. C. Johnson, 2d., Pres.,  E. C. Johnson, 2nd — Vice-Pres. & Treas.) It became [[Fidelity Investments]]. During the [[Great Depression in the United States]] the "Fidelity Fund" was the only fund approved by Hull.

In 1946, he founded [[Fidelity Investments|Fidelity Management and Research]], and he served as its chairman.<ref name="nytimesobit">{{cite news|title=Edward Johnson 2d, Was 86; Began Investment Company|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1984/04/05/obituaries/edward-johnson-2d-was-86-began-investment-company.html|accessdate=September 20, 2018|work=The New York Times|date=October 5, 1984}}</ref> He died in [[Cataumet, Massachusetts]] of Alzheimer's disease in 1984, and his funeral was held at Milton's Universalist First Parish Church.<ref name="nytimesobit">{{cite news|title=Edward Johnson 2d, Was 86; Began Investment Company|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1984/04/05/obituaries/edward-johnson-2d-was-86-began-investment-company.html|accessdate=September 20, 2018|work=The New York Times|date=October 5, 1984}}</ref>
