'''Montgomery Securities''' was an [[investment bank]] based in [[San Francisco, California]], that specialized in high technology and health care sectors. The firm was founded in 1978 by [[Thom Weisel]].

The bank was acquired by [[NationsBank Corporation]] on June 30, 1997.<ref>{{cite web|author=PETER TRUELLPublished: June 30, 1997 |url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9502E3DE1F31F933A05755C0A961958260 |title=Nationsbank Deal Expected For Montgomery Securities |work=New York Times |date=1997-06-30 |accessdate=2013-12-21}}</ref>  It competed in the same geographic region and market sectors as [[Robertson Stephens]] (acquired by [[BankAmerica Corporation]]) and [[Hambrecht & Quist]] (now part of [[JPMorganChase]]).

The merger of BankAmerica Corporation and NationsBank Corporation, prompted NationsBank CEO [[Hugh McColl]] to propose to put the together the two investment banks, BancAmerica Robertson Stephens and NationsBanc Montgomery Securities.<ref>{{cite web|author=5: 47 PM |url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/1998/04/13/archive/main7175.shtml?source=search_story |title=Merger Hits 'Wall Street West' |publisher=CBS News |date=1998-04-13 |accessdate=2013-12-21}}</ref>

Ultimately the combination did not happen as Robertson Stephens was sold off in 1998 to [[FleetBoston]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/sanfrancisco/stories/2000/09/04/story2.html |title=Robertson Stephens digs in - San Francisco Business Times |publisher=Bizjournals.com |date=2000-09-03 |accessdate=2013-12-21}}</ref>  Though, FleetBoston was later acquired by Bank of America in 2004, Robertson Stephens was closed in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4189/is_20041008/ai_n10171227/ |title=BofA Merges with FleetBoston |publisher=Findarticles.com |date= |accessdate=2013-12-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/sanfrancisco/stories/2002/07/08/daily64.html |title=Robertson Stephens to Close |publisher=Bizjournals.com |date=2002-07-12 |accessdate=2013-12-21}}</ref>

Montgomery Securities is the predecessor of [[Banc of America Securities]].<ref>[http://www.salon.com/money/feature/1998/11/25feature.html ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080515101414/http://www.salon.com/money/feature/1998/11/25feature.html |date=May 15, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://securities.stanford.edu/1037/FEET00_01/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-09-22 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070609184352/http://securities.stanford.edu/1037/FEET00_01/ |archivedate=2007-06-09 }}</ref>
