A '''convention bounce''' or '''convention bump''' refers to an increase in support that U.S. presidential candidates in the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] or [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] party typically enjoy after the televised [[U.S. presidential nominating convention|national convention]] of their party.<ref>{{cite web |title=U.S. Elections Glossary & Terms |url=http://www.allamericanpatriots.com/elections_u_s_elections_glossary_terms |work=All American Patriots |date=September 2004 |accessdate=2009-06-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081201215011/http://www.allamericanpatriots.com/elections_u_s_elections_glossary_terms |archive-date=2008-12-01 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  A [[presumptive nominee]] for president may also be said to experience a "VP bounce" after announcing his or her pick for vice president prior to the convention. The size and impact of convention bumps vary, but presidential candidates usually see at least a small uptick in their polling numbers coming out of their conventions.<ref>[http://election08data.blogspot.com/2008/08/convention-bumps.html Tom Holbrook: Convention Bumps]</ref>

==History of convention bounces==
===1992===
[[Bill Clinton]] benefited from one of the largest bumps in history after the [[1992 Democratic National Convention|Democratic National Convention]] in 1992, climbing by as many as 30 points in the polls, however this was assisted by Independent Ross Perot, who at the time was polling at 20%, withdrawing from the race during the Democratic convention.<ref>[http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5iZ98mObN7ahlFvymY3L42gTv2a2QD92RJHI80 AP: Post-convention bounce averages 10 points] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080902205018/http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5iZ98mObN7ahlFvymY3L42gTv2a2QD92RJHI80 |date=September 2, 2008 }}</ref> Incumbent President [[George H.W. Bush]]'s convention bounce was weak by comparison. Some party leaders blaming former [[presidential primary|primary]] challenger [[Pat Buchanan]]'s fiery and divisive speech, which aired in [[primetime]] due to a scheduling delay.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}}

===1996===
Four years later, [[Bob Dole]] got a big bounce after the [[1996 Republican National Convention|Republican convention]], but quickly fizzled.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}}

===2000===
[[Al Gore]]'s 2000 bounce endured for weeks. Prior to the [[2000 Democratic National Convention|Democratic convention]], Gore was behind [[Texas]] Gov. [[George W. Bush]] by as many as 16 points,<ref name="NYT Gore" /> but was in a statistical tie with the Republican the weekend after his acceptance speech.<ref name="NYT Gore">[https://partners.nytimes.com/library/national/082400poll-watch.html Gore Gets Convention Bounce, but Will Fickle Figures Last?]. New York Times. 2000-08-23.</ref><ref name="ABC Gore">[https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/story?id=123060&page=1 Convention Bounce Gives Gore 5-Point Lead]. ABC News. 2000-08-21.</ref> 

===2004===
To the bafflement of political pundits, Democratic candidate [[John Kerry]] did not get a convention bounce in 2004,<ref>[http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2004/08/02/politics/main633561.shtml CBS News: No Bounce for Kerry]</ref> despite the lukewarm popularity of incumbent George W. Bush.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}}

===2008===
Polls indicated a small bounce for 2008 Democratic presidential nominee [[Barack Obama]] after [[2008 Democratic National Convention|his party's convention]] in August,<ref>[http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0908/13068.html Politico: Obama's bounce smaller than others]</ref> but as expected, much of it evaporated during and after the [[2008 Republican National Convention|Republican convention]] the week following, yielding GOP candidate [[John McCain]] a small lead in several polls.<ref>[http://www.gallup.com/poll/110110/Gallup-Daily-McCains-Bounce-Gives-Him-5Point-Lead.aspx Gallup: McCain’s Bounce Gives Him 5-Point Lead]</ref><ref>[https://www.usatoday.com/news/politics/election2008/2008-09-07-poll_N.htm USA Today Poll: Convention lifts McCain over Obama]</ref> [[Nielsen ratings]] revealed that year's party conventions to be the most-watched ever, with the Republican convention narrowly trumping the Democrats'.<ref>Associated Press: McCain, Obama tied in TV audiences</ref> 

===2012===
The [[RealClearPolitics]] polling index revealed the 2012 convention bounces for President Obama and GOP challenger [[Mitt Romney]] to be comparably smaller than in past elections.<ref name="rcp 2012">[http://www.realclearpolitics.com/epolls/2012/president/us/general_election_romney_vs_obama-1171.html General Election: Romney vs. Obama]. RealClearPolitics. Retrieved 2012-10-08.</ref> Though Romney pulled even with Obama during that year's [[2012 Republican National Convention|Republican convention]] at the end of August, Obama opened up a three- or four-point lead during the [[2012 Democratic National Convention|Democratic convention]] the week after.<ref name="rcp 2012" />

===2016===
In 2016, news sources and polls differed greatly on the size of the convention bounces received by Republican [[Donald Trump]] and Democrat [[Hillary Clinton]] after their respective conventions;<ref>{{cite news|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2016/07/25/politics/donald-trump-hillary-clinton-poll/index.html|title=Donald Trump bounces into the lead|last=Agiesta|first=Jennifer|date=July 25, 2016|publisher=[[CNN]] |accessdate=July 25, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Hartig|first1=Hannah|title=Poll: No Post-Convention Bounce for Donald Trump|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2016-election/poll-no-post-convention-bounce-donald-trump-n616426|publisher=NBC|accessdate=1 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Rappeport|first1=Alan|title=New Poll Reflects a Post-Convention Bounce for Hillary Clinton|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/02/us/politics/clinton-convetion-poll.html|website=The New York Times|accessdate=1 August 2016|date=1 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=CNN/ORC Poll|url=http://i2.cdn.turner.com/cnn/2016/images/08/01/2016.post-dem.convention.pdf|publisher=CNN|accessdate=2 August 2016|date=1 August 2016}}</ref> however, the RealClearPolitics polling index showed both candidates trending upward leading up to and after their acceptance speeches.<ref name="rcp 2016">[https://www.realclearpolitics.com/epolls/2016/president/us/general_election_trump_vs_clinton-5491.html General Election: Trump vs. Clinton]. RealClearPolitics. Retrieved 2019-02-05.</ref> Clinton's bounce lasted somewhat longer than Trump's.<ref name="rcp 2016" />

===2020===
Democratic candidate [[Joe Biden]], who held the lead over incumbent Republican Trump entering the [[2020 Democratic National Convention|Democratic convention]], did not get a convention bounce,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.politico.com/news/2020/08/24/biden-polling-dnc-401081|title=Poll: No convention bounce for Biden, who maintains big lead over Trump|last=Shepard|first=Steven|date=August 24, 2020|publisher=[[Politico]] |accessdate=August 27, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-election-poll/biden-gets-no-convention-bounce-after-democratic-gathering-reuters-ipsos-poll-idUSKBN25M156|title=Biden gets no convention bounce after Democratic gathering: Reuters/Ipsos poll|last=Kahn|first=Chris|date=August 26, 2020|publisher=[[Reuters]] |accessdate=August 27, 2020}}</ref> with a [[CNBC]]-[[Change Research]] poll also indicating that the race had instead tightened in the [[swing state]]s.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/08/26/coronavirus-concerns-fall-and-trump-approval-rises-in-2020-swing-states.html|title=Coronavirus concerns fall and Trump approval ticks higher in swing states, CNBC/Change Research poll finds|first=Jacob|last=Pramuk|work=CNBC|date=August 26, 2020|accessdate=August 27, 2020}}</ref>  Republican candidate [[Donald Trump]] similarly did not receive a convention bounce after the [[2020 Republican National Convention|Republican convention]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=Editor-at-large|first=Analysis by Chris Cillizza, CNN|title=Analysis: It's official: There's no Trump bounce out of the conventions|url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/09/03/politics/dnc-rnc-donald-trump-joe-biden-2020-election/index.html|access-date=2020-09-03|website=CNN}}</ref> <ref>{{Cite web|last=Peters|first=Cameron|date=2020-08-30|title=Poll: Biden’s approval rating got a convention bounce. Trump’s didn’t.|url=https://www.vox.com/2020/8/30/21407646/trump-approval-poll-rnc-abc-news-ipsos|access-date=2020-09-03|website=Vox|language=en}}</ref> with a [[CNN]] poll indicating that the race remained unchanged after both conventions.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=CNN / SSRS Poll (2020-09-02)|url=https://cdn.cnn.com/cnn/2020/images/09/02/rel9a.-.2020.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>
