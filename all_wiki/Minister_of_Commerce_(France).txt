The '''Minister of Commerce''' was a cabinet member in the [[Government of France]]. The position sometimes included responsibility for other government departments such as [[Minister of Public Works (France)|Public Works]], [[Minister of the Interior (France)|Interior]], [[Ministry of Agriculture (France)|Agriculture]] and [[Minister of Posts, Telegraphs, and Telephones|Posts, Telegraphs and Telephones]].  
The position has largely been merged today into the expanded [[Minister of the Economy, Finances and Industry (France)|Ministry of the Economy, Finance and Industry]].

==Officeholders==

===Ministers of Commerce and Manufacturing===

In 1812 [[Napoleon]] created a Ministry of Commerce and Manufacturing (''Ministère du Commerce et des Manufactures''), which he assigned to [[Jean-Baptiste Collin de Sussy]]. That ministry was suppressed in 1814.<ref name="Todisco1969">{{cite book|last=Todisco|first=Umberto|title=Le personnel de la Cour des comptes (1807-1830).|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fPOh4FbZdB4C&pg=PA84|year=1969|publisher=Librairie Droz|isbn=978-2-600-03361-9|pages=84}}</ref>

* 16 January 1812 – 1 April 1814 : [[Jean-Baptiste Collin de Sussy]]

A royal ordinance of 22 January 1828 recreated the Ministry of Commerce and Manufacturing, which covered manufacturing and interior and exterior commerce, which were detached from the [[Minister of the Interior (France)|Ministry of the Interior]]. The ministry was suppressed by ordinance of 8 August 1829, and these services were again made part of the department of the interior.<ref name="France1877">{{cite book|last=France|first=Académie d'agriculture de|title=Mëmoires|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=S9k5AAAAIAAJ&pg=RA1-PA385|year=1877|pages=385ff}}</ref>

* 29 January 1828 – 8 August 1829 : [[Pierre de Saint-Cricq]]

===Ministers of Commerce and Public works ===

An ordinance of 17 March 1831 created the Ministry of Commerce and Public works (''Ministère du Commerce et des Travaux publics''), to which the minister had been named on 13 March 1831. This ministry included agriculture, subsistence, stud farms, interior and exterior commerce and statistics, detached from the department of the Interior.<ref name="France1877"/>

* 13 March 1831 – 31 December 1832 : [[Antoine, comte d'Argout]]
* 31 December 1832 – 4 April 1834 : [[Adolphe Thiers]]

===Ministers of Commerce===
A royal ordinance of 6 April 1834 created the Ministry of Commerce (''Ministère du Commerce''), with the same functions as the Ministry of Commerce and Public works .<ref name="France1877"/>

* 4 April 1834 – 10 November 1834 : [[Charles Marie Tanneguy Duchâtel]]
* 10 November 1834 – 18 November 1834 : [[Jean-Baptiste Teste]]
* 18 November 1834 – 22 February 1836 : [[Charles Marie Tanneguy Duchâtel]]

===Ministers of Commerce and Public works ===
An ordinance of 2 March 1836 recreated the Ministry of Commerce and Public works, with the added responsibilities of bridges, roads and mines.<ref name="France1877"/>

* 22 February 1836 – 6 September 1836 : [[Hippolyte Passy]]
* 6–19 September 1836 : Comte Duchatel (interim)

===Ministers of Public Works, Agriculture and Commerce===

* 19 September 1836 – 31 March 1839 : [[Nicolas Martin du Nord]]
* 31 March 1839 – 12 May 1839 : [[Adrien de Gasparin]]
