The '''70th annual [[Venice Film Festival|Venice International Film Festival]]''' took place in Venice, Italy from 28 August to 7 September 2013.<ref name="labiennale.org">{{cite web |url=http://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/70th-festival/line-up/index.html |title=70th Venice International Film Festival Line-up |work=labiennale.org |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131013114658/http://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/70th-festival/line-up/index.html |archivedate=13 October 2013 }}</ref> American film director [[William Friedkin]] was presented with a lifetime achievement award.<ref name="Friedkin">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-22387152 |title=William Friedkin to receive Venice honour|work=BBC News | date=2 May 2013}}</ref> Italian film director [[Bernardo Bertolucci]] was the President of the Jury. He was previously the President of the Jury at the 40th edition in 1983.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/news/09-05.html |title=Bernardo Bertolucci President of the international Jury of the Venezia 70 Competition |work=labiennale.org |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131004201251/http://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/news/09-05.html |archivedate=4 October 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/bernardo-bertulocci-to-head-venice-jury |title=Bernardo Bertolucci To Head 70th Venice Film Festival Jury |work=Indiewire}}</ref> ''[[Gravity (2013 film)|Gravity]]'', directed by [[Alfonso Cuarón]], was the opening film of the festival.<ref name="Gravity">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-23158374 |title=George Clooney and Sandra Bullock to open Venice film festival |work=BBC News | date=3 July 2013}}</ref> Italian actress [[Eva Riccobono]] hosted the opening and closing nights of the festival.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.redcarpet-fashionawards.com/2013/08/28/eva-riccobono-in-armani-prive-70th-venice-international-film-festival-photocall/ |title=Eva Riccobono In Armani Privé – 70th Venice International Film Festival Photocall |work=redcarpet-fashionawards.com |accessdate=30 July 2013}}</ref>

The [[Golden Lion]] was awarded to the Italian documentary film ''[[Sacro GRA]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Vince l'Italia: Leone d'oro a "Sacro GRA"|url=http://www.repubblica.it/speciali/cinema/venezia/edizione2013/2013/09/07/news/venezia_vincitore-66075620/?ref=HRER3-1|accessdate=7 September 2013|newspaper=La Repubblica|date=7 September 2013}}</ref> It was the first documentary film to win the award at the Venice Festival.<ref>{{cite news|last=Franck Nouchi|title=Pour la première fois, le Lion d'or à Venise récompense un documentaire|url=http://www.lemonde.fr/culture/article/2013/09/08/un-lion-d-or-venitien-pour-un-documentaire-romain_3473005_3246.html|accessdate=8 September 2013|newspaper=Le Monde|date=8 September 2013}}</ref><ref name="BBCDoc">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-24006969 |title=Italian documentary Sacro GRA wins Golden Lion |date=8 September 2013 |work=BBC News}}</ref>

==''Venice 70 - Future Reloaded'' ==
Under the project title ''Venice 70 - Future Reloaded'', 70 top film directors were each invited to make a 60-90 second short film, as "both a collective tribute to the Festival (the first festival in the world to reach the milestone of 70 editions) and a reflection on the future of cinema".<ref name="future">{{cite web|title=Venice Film Festival 70 - Future Reloaded|url=http://en.venezia.net/26/08/2013/venice-film-festival-2013-future-reloaded.html|publisher=Venezia.net|accessdate=5 March 2018}}</ref><ref name="aguilar">{{cite web|last1=Aguilar|first1=Carlos|title=Venezia 70 Future Reloaded Shorts|url=http://www.indiewire.com/2013/10/venezia-70-future-reloaded-shorts-169358/|publisher=Indiewire|accessdate=5 March 2018|date=7 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3064438/ |title=Venice 70: Future Reloaded (2013) |work=IMDb |accessdate=5 April 2018}}</ref>
