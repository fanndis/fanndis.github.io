'''Michael C. Moynihan''' (born August 24, 1974) is an American [[journalist]], National Correspondent for ''[[Vice News]]'' and co-host of ''The Fifth Column'' podcast. He was previously the cultural news editor for ''[[The Daily Beast]]/[[Newsweek]]'', the managing editor of [[Vice (magazine)|''Vice'' magazine]], and a [[senior editor]] of the [[Libertarianism|libertarian]] magazine ''[[Reason (magazine)|Reason]]''. Moynihan was also a resident fellow of the [[free-market]] [[think tank]] [[Timbro]] in Sweden, where he lived and wrote articles about politics in the country, contributing to Swedish-language publications, including ''[[Expressen]]'', ''[[Aftonbladet]]'', ''[[Sveriges Television]]'', ''[[Neo (magazine)|Neo]]'' and ''[[Göteborgs-Tidningen]]''. According to ''[[Media Bistro]]'', "Moynihan is perhaps best known for breaking the story on [[Jonah Lehrer]]'s fabrications."<ref name=bistro>{{cite web| last =O'Shea| first =Chris| title =Michael Moynihan Joins Newsweek/The Daily Beast| work =Media Bistro| date =Sep 26, 2012| url = http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowlny/michael-moynihan-joins-newsweekthe-daily-beast_b68723}}</ref>

Moynihan founded the ''Stockholm Spectator'', an English-language website based in [[Stockholm, Sweden]].<ref name="uddenfeldt">{{Cite news| last = Uddenfeldt  | first = Therese | title = Stockholm kan få tidning på engelska | work = [[Sveriges Radio]] | publisher = sverigesradio.se | date =July 27, 2004  | url = http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=478&artikel=449066| accessdate = 2010-05-06 }}</ref> According to ''[[Sveriges Radio]]'', the site was originally intended to be a print publication modeled on ''[[The Village Voice]]''.<ref name="uddenfeldt" /> Writers were mainly English-speaking [[expatriate]]s living in Sweden.<ref name="uddenfeldt" /> "Despite the fact that so many Swedes speak and read in English there were almost no English-language newspapers in Sweden," said Moynihan to ''Sveriges Radio'' in 2004.<ref name="uddenfeldt" /> It maintained a focus on criticism of the media, but also dealt with current topics in politics and music.<ref name="uddenfeldt" /> Moynihan began serving on the [[editorial board]] of the Swedish magazine ''Neo'' in 2006 along with Peter Wolodarski and Theodore Paues. Swedish politician [[Carl Bildt]] sat on the board of the publication.<ref>{{Cite news | last = Ekström | first = Andreas | title = Borgerlighet för "grästoppar" | work = Sydsvenskan | publisher = www.sydsvenskan.se | date = January 18, 2006 | url = http://www.sydsvenskan.se/samtidigt/article136723/Borgerlighet-for-grastoppar.html | accessdate = 2010-05-06 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110726043716/http://www.sydsvenskan.se/samtidigt/article136723/Borgerlighet-for-grastoppar.html | archive-date = July 26, 2011 }}</ref>

During a controversy in 2006 where the website [[SD-Kuriren]] was criticized by the [[Swedish Minister of Foreign Affairs]] [[Laila Freivalds]] for publishing satires of the Islamic prophet, [[Muhammad]], the website was taken down by its provider.<ref name="goldenberg">{{Cite news| last = Goldenberg | first = David | title =Condom Nation: Gelf highlights overlooked coverage from local media around the world. Why the pope was a moral failure|first=Michael|last=Moynihan|date=12 February 2013|via=www.thedailybeast.com}}</ref> after [[Bill de Blasio]] was elected as Mayor of [[New York City|New York]], Moynihan criticized the nostalgia for the crime-ridden New York of the 1970s that was on display at the inauguration.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/01/06/weren-t-those-the-bad-old-days-the-poison-of-new-york-city-nostalgia|title=Weren’t Those the Bad Old Days? The Poison of New York City Nostalgia|first=Michael|last=Moynihan|date=6 January 2014|via=www.thedailybeast.com}}</ref> Following Hugo Chavez's death, Moynihan described his regime as "extralegal, vindictive, and interested in the short-term gesture rather than the more difficult, long-term solution,"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/03/05/hugo-chavez-dead-at-58-good-riddance|title=Hugo Chavez Dead at 58: Good Riddance!|first=Michael|last=Moynihan|date=5 March 2013|via=www.thedailybeast.com}}</ref> and said that "Chávez's lesson for future authoritarians" is to "make a mockery of democratic institutions, rewrite the Constitution, and persecute—and prosecute—your political enemies. But when you do so, make sure to mutter the appropriate things about poverty, 'the empire,' and the scourge of 'neoliberalism.' All will be forgiven."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/03/07/the-stupidest-hugo-chavez-hagiographies-from-the-yanquis-who-loved-him|title=The Stupidest Hugo Chavez Hagiographies From the Yanquis Who Loved Him|first=Michael|last=Moynihan|date=7 March 2013|via=www.thedailybeast.com}}</ref>
