'''Anna Yuryevna Netrebko''' ({{lang-ru|Анна Юрьевна Нетребко}}; born 18 September 1971) is a [[Russians in Austria|Russian-Austrian]] operatic [[soprano]]. Discovered and promoted by [[Valery Gergiev]], she began her career at the [[Mariinsky Theatre]], collaborating with the conductor in the theater and performances elsewhere. She was noticed globally after playing Donna Anna in Mozart's ''[[Don Giovanni]]'' at the 2002 [[Salzburg Festival]]. She had been known for her rendition of [[Lyric soprano|lyric]] and [[coloratura soprano]] roles, most notably Donna Anna and Violetta in Verdi's ''[[La traviata]]'', and proceeded into heavier 19th-century romantic roles, such as Leonora in ''[[Il trovatore]]'' and the role of Lady Macbeth in ''[[Macbeth (opera)|Macbeth]]''. Since 2016, she has turned her focus to [[Verismo (music)|Verismo]] repertoire.

She has received multiple awards and honors and has substantial discography made primarily under [[Deutsche Grammophon]].

==Early life and training==
Netrebko was born in [[Krasnodar]], in a family of [[Kuban Cossack]] background.<ref>[http://www.rg.ru/2006/05/19/netrebko.html Интервью с Анной Нетребко-Анна Нетребко: Я никогда не ходила по струнке. Татьяна Павловская, Краснодар.] in ''[[Rossiyskaya Gazeta]]'' (in Russian), issue no. 4070 dated 19 May 2006.</ref> While studying at the [[Saint Petersburg Conservatory]], Netrebko worked as a janitor at Saint Petersburg's [[Mariinsky Theatre]].<ref name=telegraph>{{cite news|author=Melissa Whitworth|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/3650296/Cinderella-Soprano.html|title=Cinderella Soprano|work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|date=2006-02-19|accessdate=2013-09-11}}</ref> Later when she auditioned for the theatre, conductor [[Valery Gergiev]], recognizing her from her prior work, subsequently became her vocal mentor.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/anna-netrebko-a-rare-jewel-from-the-east-423103.html|author=Jessica Duchen|title=Anna Netrebko: A rare jewel from the East|work=[[The Independent]]|date=2006-11-06|accessdate=2013-09-11}}</ref> She won first prize at the 1993 International Glinka Vocalists Competition and third prize at the 1996 International Rimsky-Korsakov Young Opera Singers Competition.

==Career==
===1990s===
Under Gergiev's guidance, in 1994, Netrebko made her operatic stage debut at the Mariinsky at age 22 as Susanna in ''[[The Marriage of Figaro]]'' despite initially being billed as Barbarina.<ref name=telegraph /> In the same year, she also performed as the Queen of the Night in ''[[The Magic Flute]]'' with the Riga Independent Opera Avangarda Akadēmija under conductor [[David Milnes]].<ref>Agelet de Saracibar, Carlos; [https://www.scribd.com/doc/63918608/ANPD-Sep-2011 "Anna Netrebko Performance Database"]. ''ANPD''. September 2011.</ref><ref>[http://www.classissima.com/en/news/326476-anna-netrebko-and-erwin-schrott-will-give-a-concert-tour-to-ukraine,-latvia-and-russia/ "Anna Netrebko and Erwin Schrott will give a concert tour to Ukraine..."]. {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140713215928/http://www.classissima.com/en/news/326476-anna-netrebko-and-erwin-schrott-will-give-a-concert-tour-to-ukraine,-latvia-and-russia |date=2014-07-13 }}. ''Classissima''. 2010-10-29.</ref> She subsequently became associated with the Mariinsky Theatre, portraying roles including Lyudmila in Glinka's ''[[Ruslan and Lyudmila (opera)|Ruslan and Lyudmila]]'', Pamina in ''The Magic Flute'', Rosina in ''[[The Barber of Seville]]'', a flower maiden in ''[[Parsifal]]'', and Lucia in ''[[Lucia di Lammermoor]]''. Her collaboration with Gergiev also extended to opera and concert performances at other venues, including Mussorgsky's ''[[The Nursery]]'' at [[The Proms]], ''[[Carmen]]'' at the [[Tokyo Bunka Kaikan]], ''[[Betrothal in a Monastery]]'' at his [[Mikkeli Music Festival]], ''[[Boris Godunov (opera)|Boris Godunov]]'' at the [[Ravenna Festival]], ''[[The Love for Three Oranges]]'' at the [[Concertgebouw]].

In autumn 1995, Netrebko made her American debut as Lyudmila at the [[San Francisco Opera]]. Following this successful performance, she was enrolled in the [[Merola Opera Program]] the next year and then became a frequent guest singer in San Francisco, performing roles including Adina in ''[[L'elisir d'amore]]'', Louisa in ''Betrothal in a Monastery'', Ilia in ''[[Idomeneo]]'', Musetta in ''[[La bohème]]'', Marfa in ''[[The Tsar's Bride (opera)|The Tsar's Bride]]'', Zerlina in ''[[Don Giovanni]]''.<ref>{{cite news |date=1998-01-19 |first=Joshua |last=Kosman |url=https://www.sfgate.com/entertainment/article/Russian-Soprano-s-Star-Rose-Quickly-Netrebko-3016494.php |title=Russian Soprano's Star Rose Quickly |work=San Francisco Chronicle}}</ref> In 1998, she performed as Lyudmila when the Mariinsky production of the opera was presented at the [[Metropolitan Opera]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/news/bs-xpm-1998-05-11-1998131002-story.html |title='Ruslan' given its due by the Kirov Review |date=1998-05-11 |first=Stephen |last=Wigler |work=The Baltimore Sun}}</ref> and debuted at the [[Salzburg Festival]] in ''Parsifal'' conducted by Gergiev. She debuted in the role of Violetta in Verdi's ''[[La traviata]]'' in the same year at the Mariinsky Theatre, and debuted as Amina in ''[[La sonnambula]]'' the following year. In October 1999, Netrebko performed Gilda in ''[[Rigoletto]]'' at the [[Washington National Opera]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Opera singer guards future; Soprano: Anna Netrebko takes things one step at a time |date=1999-10-23 |url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/news/bs-xpm-1999-10-23-9910230314-story.html |first=Stephen |last=Wigler |work=The Baltimore Sun}}</ref>

===2000s===
In 2002, Netrebko made her debut with the [[Metropolitan Opera]] company as Natasha in the Met premiere of Prokofiev's ''[[War and Peace (opera)|War and Peace]]'',<ref>{{Cite news |first=Anthony |last=Tommasini |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/02/16/arts/opera-review-war-and-peace-opens-mishap-raises-concerns.html |title=OPERA REVIEW; 'War and Peace' Opens; Mishap Raises Concerns |work=The New York Times |date=2002-02-16|accessdate=2020-09-12}}</ref> and performed as Giulietta in ''[[I Capuleti e i Montecchi]]'' at the [[Opera Company of Philadelphia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zh589x6MHgQ |title=The Capulets and the Montagues, Philadelphia 2002 |website=YouTube |date=2009-08-25}}</ref> Debuting in the role of Donna Anna in ''[[Don Giovanni]]'' at the Mariinsky Theatre in May, she performed the same role conducted by [[Nikolaus Harnoncourt]] at the [[Salzburg Festival]], which prompted her to international fame. She returned to the Festival next year in the same role, and was featured in subsequent productions including ''War and Peace'', ''I Capuleti e i Montecchi'', [[Willy Decker]]'s new staging of ''La traviata'',<ref>{{cite news |first=George |last=Loomis |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/08/24/arts/traviata-soars-at-salzburg.html |title='Traviata' soars at Salzburg |date=2005-08-24 |work=The New York Times}}</ref> and ''The Marriage of Figaro''.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/08/08/arts/music/08figa.html |title='Figaro,' With Nikolaus Harnoncourt Conducting, at the Salzburg Festival |date=2006-08-08 |work=The New York Times |first=Anthony |last=Tommasini}}</ref>

In 2003, Netrebko performed as Violetta in Verdi's ''[[La traviata]]'' in Munich, the title role in ''[[Lucia di Lammermoor]]'' at the [[Los Angeles Opera]], and Donna Anna at the Covent Garden [[Royal Opera House]]. Her second album, ''Sempre Libera'', was released the following year. She later appeared as Violetta in ''La traviata'' at the Salzburg Festival, conducted by Carlo Rizzi and in 2008 she performed the same role at the [[Royal Opera House]], Covent Garden to triumphant acclaim on the opening night,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2008/apr/26/classicalmusicandopera.russia  |author=Stuart Jeffries |title=I conquered the critics |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=2008-04-25 |accessdate=2015-03-01}}</ref> opposite [[Jonas Kaufmann]] and [[Dmitri Hvorostovsky]] in performances conducted by [[Maurizio Benini]]. However, she cancelled three subsequent performances due to suffering a bronchial condition. This was the second time she had cancelled her performances at the Royal Opera House, having withdrawn from some performances of ''Don Giovanni'' the previous summer due to illness.

She sang Elvira in ''[[I puritani]]'' at the Metropolitan Opera in January 2007, and on 30 May 2007, Netrebko made her [[Carnegie Hall]] debut with [[Dmitri Hvorostovsky]] and the [[Orchestra of St. Luke's]], which was originally scheduled on 2 March 2006 but she postponed due to not feeling artistically ready.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/n/a/2007/05/30/entertainment/e232217D99.DTL |agency=[[Associated Press]] |work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |title=Russian Soprano Debuts at Carnegie Hall |date=2007-05-30 |archivedate=10 December 2008 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081210082301/http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=%2Fn%2Fa%2F2007%2F05%2F30%2Fentertainment%2Fe232217D99.DTL |url-status=live |access-date=6 July 2017 }}</ref> She appeared at the [[Last Night of the Proms]] on 8 September of that year where she performed excerpts from ''[[La sonnambula]]'' and ''[[Giuditta]]'', and the lied "[[Morgen!]]" by [[Richard Strauss]] with [[Joshua Bell]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/proms/2007/whatson/0809.shtml|title=Last Night of the Proms: program|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2013-09-11}}</ref> In the fall of 2007 she reprised her role as Juliette in ''[[Roméo et Juliette]]'' at the Metropolitan Opera.

In May 2008, she made her Paris Opera debut in [[Vincenzo Bellini|Bellini]]'s ''[[I Capuleti e i Montecchi]]'' at the [[Opéra Bastille]], with [[Joyce DiDonato]] as Romeo. In her first performance after her maternity leave, Netrebko sang Lucia in ''Lucia di Lammermoor'' when it opened at the Mariinsky Theatre in Saint Petersburg on 14 January 2009, in a production from the [[Scottish Opera]] led by John Doyle.<ref>{{cite news |last=Stolyarova |first=Galina |url=http://www.times.spb.ru/index.php?action_id=2&story_id=28012 |title=Mariinsky Diva Back With New Production |work=The St. Petersburg Times |date=2009-01-16 |accessdate=2009-02-09}}</ref> She then sang the same role in January and February 2009 at the Metropolitan Opera. Netrebko appeared as Giulietta in ''I Capuleti e i Montecchi'' at the [[Royal Opera House]] in Spring 2009, and as Violetta in ''La traviata'' in June 2009 at the [[San Francisco Opera]].

In 2010, she collaborated with [[Philipp Kirkorov]] on a cover of "[[La voix (song)|La Voix]]", where she sang the chorus.<ref>{{cite news |lang=ru |website=kp.ru |date=2010-05-06 |url=https://www.kp.ru/daily/24485.4/641890/ |title=Анна Нетребко - Филиппу Киркорову: "Спасибо за голос!" |first=Мария |last=Ремизова}}</ref>

==Other activities==
[[File:Anna Netrebko 2008.jpg|thumb|200 px|upright|Netrebko at the [[Mariinsky Theatre]] after receiving the honorary title of [[People's Artist of Russia]] in 2008]]
Netrebko serves as an honorary director of the [[Russian Children's Welfare Society]] and has featured in several editions of "Petroushka Ball", the major fund raiser of the charity.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.maximumdownforce.com/interactive/rcws/rcws_b/events.html |title=Petroushka – Russian Children's Welfare Society |accessdate=2019-10-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rcws.org/about/the-board |title=About – The Board |website=RCWS |accessdate=2019-10-24}}</ref> In 2007, she was announced to be an ambassador for [[SOS Children's Villages]] in Austria, and a sponsor for the Tomilino village in Russia.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sos-childrensvillages.org/news/opera-singer-anna-netrebko-is-ambassador-for-sos-c |title=Opera singer Anna Netrebko is ambassador for SOS Children's Villages Austria |website=SOS Children's Village International |date=2007-03-16 |accessdate=2019-10-24}}</ref> She has been supporting the association "projekt Anna - Kinderhilfe [[Kaliningrad]] e.V." since 2005, and became its patron in 2008.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.wochenanzeiger.de/article/55489.html |title=Hilfe für Waisenkinder - Opernstar unterstützt elternlose Kinder in Russland |date=2005-12-20 |publisher=Münchner Wochenanzeiger |accessdate=2020-11-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.evangelisch.de/inhalte/97204/30-11-2009/projekt-anna-fuer-die-kinder-kaliningrad |title="projekt Anna": Für die Kinder in Kaliningrad |date=2009-11-30 |first=Bettina |last=Scriba |website=evangelisch.de |accessdate=2020-11-07}}</ref> In May 2012, she and her then-partner Erwin Schrott jointly founded the charitable foundation "Anna Netrebko and Erwin Schrott 4 Kids", aiming to promote education, art, culture and youth welfare.<ref>{{cite web |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131213184836/http://www.annaanderwin4kids.com/ |archive-date=2013-12-13 |url=http://www.annaanderwin4kids.com/ |accessdate=2019-10-24 |title=Anna and Erwin Foundation |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vdoper.de/nachrichten/neue-stiftung-kinder-jugendliche-anna-netrebko-erwin-schrott |title=Neue Stiftung für Kinder und Jugendliche von Anna Netrebko und Erwin Schrott |date=2012-05-10 |website=Vdoper.de}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.classical-music.com/news/anna-netrebko-and-erwin-schrott-set-charity |title=Anna Netrebko and Erwin Schrott set up charity |date=2012-06-14 |work=BBC Music Magazine}}</ref>

In December 2014, she gave a ₽1,000,000 cheque to [[Oleg Tsaryov]] saying she was donating to the [[Donetsk Opera and Ballet Theatre]], and posed alongside Tsarov with a flag of [[Novorossiya (confederation)|Novorossiya]], a [[self-proclaimed]] confederation in Ukraine.<ref name=BBC141208>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-30380979|title=Russian soprano Netrebko helps theatre in rebel Ukraine|work=BBC News|date=2014-12-08|accessdate=2014-12-09}}</ref><ref name=AFP141208>{{cite news|url=http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/news/afp/141208/russias-netrebko-sparks-controversy-donation-separatist-region |title=Russia's Netrebko sparks controversy with donation to separatist region |agency=[[Agence France-Presse]] |work=[[GlobalPost]] |date=2014-12-08 |accessdate=2014-12-09 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141209035111/http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/news/afp/141208/russias-netrebko-sparks-controversy-donation-separatist-region |archivedate=2014-12-09}}</ref> Tsarov is one of the [[International sanctions during the 2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine|individuals sanctioned]] by the European Union for his role in the [[2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine]].<ref name=NDN91214/> Netrebko said in a statement "I want to make clear, however, that this donation is not a political act".<ref name=NDN91214>{{cite news|last=Bigg|first=Claire|url=http://www.rferl.org/content/opera-star-under-fire-for-backing-ukraine-separatists/26733809.html|title=Opera Star Under Fire For Backing Ukraine Separatists|agency=[[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]]|date=2014-12-09|accessdate=2014-12-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/annanetrebko/posts/10153448496577627 |title=Original statement on Facebook |date=2014-12-09}}</ref>

She is an ambassador of [[Chopard]], having attended several of its occasions and publicly worn its designs.

==Personal life==
In March 2006, Netrebko applied to become an Austrian citizen, receiving her citizenship in late July.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://wien.orf.at/stories/125089/ |publisher=ORF.at |date=2006-07-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110725175329/http://wien.orf.at/stories/125089/ |archive-date=2011-07-25 |title=Opernstar Anna Netrebko: "Ich liebe Österreich" |lang=de}}</ref> According to an interview in the Austrian weekly news, she will live in Vienna and [[Salzburg]]. Netrebko cites the cumbersome and humiliating process of obtaining visas as a Russian citizen for her many performances abroad as the main reason for obtaining Austrian citizenship.<ref>{{cite news |date=2006-05-26 |url=http://www.themoscowtimes.com/arts_n_ideas/article/national-treasure/357122.html |first=Galina |last=Stolyarova |title=National Treasure|work=[[The Moscow Times]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160112052340/http://www.themoscowtimes.com/arts_n_ideas/article/national-treasure/357122.html |archive-date=2016-01-12}}</ref>

Netrebko started a relationship with Italian bass-baritone [[Simone Alberghini]] when they met each other during performances of ''Rigoletto'' at the Washington National Opera in 1999.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/news/bs-xpm-2004-09-12-0409110045-story.html |title=That voice! That face! That cursing! It's Anna Netrebko |date=2004-09-12 |work=[[The Baltimore Sun]] |first=Tim |last=Smith}}</ref> She announced their engagement but did not consider marrying due to a busy schedule.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.n-tv.de/archiv/Keine-Zeit-zum-Heiraten-article148268.html |date=2005-04-19 |title=Opernstar Anna Netrebko: Keine Zeit zum Heiraten |publisher=n-tv |language=de}}</ref> In May 2007 their relationship was confirmed as ended.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.wienerzeitung.at/meinung/glossen/102410_Das-Buehnentraumpaar-in-Zeiten-der-Krise.html |title=Das Bühnentraumpaar in Zeiten der Krise |date=2007-05-09 |work=Wiener Zeitung |first=Edwin |last=Baumgartner |trans-title=The Stage Dream Couple in Times of Crisis}}</ref>

In December 2007 Netrebko became engaged to Uruguayan bass-baritone [[Erwin Schrott]], whom she first met during a collaboration in 2003.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.stern.de/lifestyle/leute/anna-netrebko-liebe-am-arbeitsplatz-3219678.html |date=2007-12-17 |title=Anna Netrebko: Liebe am Arbeitsplatz |work=Stern |language=de}}</ref> In April 2008, she announced their marriage,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2008/apr/26/classicalmusicandopera.russia |last=Jeffries |first=Stuart |title=I conquered the critics |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=2008-04-26 |accessdate=2009-02-09}}</ref> but their wedding never in fact took place.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/01/16/nyregion/16routine.html |last=Finn |first=Robin |title=Breakfast Eggs (15 Ways) by a Soprano |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=2011-01-14 |accessdate=2012-08-24}}</ref> Their son, Tiago Aruã, was born on 5 September 2008 in Vienna.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://people.com/parents/anna-netrebko/ |title=Opera Singer Anna Netrebko and Erwin Schrott Welcome a Son |date=2008-09-05 |work=People Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.focus.de/panorama/boulevard/anna-netrebko-sohn-heisst-friedlicher-jakob_aid_331972.html |date=2008-09-08 |work=Focus |title=Anna Netrebko: Sohn heißt "friedlicher Jakob"}}</ref> On 25 November 2013, the couple announced their separation,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://news.yahoo.com/netrebko-says-she-split-schrott-203817038.html |title=Netrebko says she has split with Schrott |work=Yahoo! News |date=2013-11-25 |agency=Associated Press |accessdate=2019-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.welt.de/newsticker/dpa_nt/infoline_nt/boulevard_nt/article122238273/Anna-Netrebko-und-Erwin-Schrott-haben-sich-getrennt.html |title=Anna Netrebko und Erwin Schrott haben sich getrennt |date=2013-11-25 |work=Die Welt |agency=dpa |accessdate=2019-09-29}}</ref> after several months of largely separate lives. Tiago, who is mildly autistic, lives exclusively with her.

In February 2014, during rehearsals for a staging of ''[[Manon Lescaut (Puccini)|Manon Lescaut]]'' in Rome, Netrebko began a relationship with [[Azerbaijan]]i tenor {{Interlanguage link multi|Yusif Eyvazov|de}}, her co-star in that opera. Five months later, the couple announced their engagement.<ref>[https://www.facebook.com/annanetrebko/photos/a.170799047626.155509.10480012626/10153038884137627/ Netrebko/Eyvazov announcement]. Facebook. 10 July 2014.
