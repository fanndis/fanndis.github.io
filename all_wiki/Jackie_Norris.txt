'''Jackie Norris''' (born 1970) is an American political aide, educator and corporate strategist, she once served as the assistant to President Obama and Chief of Staff to First Lady Michelle Obama. Jackie is the current CEO and president of Goodwill of Central Iowa. <ref>{{Cite web|title=You Only Want Me for My Money! Tips for Nonprofits looking for Charity Sponsorship|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/shar-mcbee/you-only-want-me-for-my-m_b_3007052.html|website=The Huffington Post|accessdate=2015-04-11|date=2013-06-03|first=Shar|last=McBee}}</ref>

==Biography==
{{more citations needed|section|date=March 2017}}
Norris was born in [[Ossining (town), New York|Ossining, New York]] and earned a degree in political science from the [[State University of New York at Geneseo]] in 1992.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelamron.com/posts/2009/02/05/geneseo-alumna-92-appointed-chief-of-staff-for-first-lady|title=Geneseo alumna '92 appointed chief of staff for First Lady|last=Susmann|first=Herb|date=2009-02-04|accessdate=2017-06-06|website=The Lamron}}</ref> She received her secondary teaching certification in 2001 from Iowa State University and a Masters in Political Science from [[Iowa State University]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.news.iastate.edu/news/2010/feb/NorrisLecture|title=Jackie Norris will speak on women, leadership and service at ISU March 1 - News Service - Iowa State University|website=www.news.iastate.edu|language=en-us|access-date=2018-11-13}}</ref> in 2008.

Norris began her career in the office of Congresswoman [[Louise M. Slaughter]] (D-NY) in 1992. She later served as Vice President [[Al Gore]]’s scheduler and events planner and as Director of Scheduling and Advance for HUD Secretary [[Andrew Cuomo]]. In 1998, Norris served as finance director for Governor [[Tom Vilsack]]’s gubernatorial campaign in Iowa and as Iowa state political director on Al Gore’s 2000 Presidential campaign.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/jackie-norris/gIQASXac9O_topic.html|title=Jackie Norris|date=2012-07-25|website=Washington Post|language=EN|access-date=2018-11-13}}</ref>

In 2002, Norris became a classroom teacher for history and government at three high schools in Perry, Ames and Johnston, Iowa, after receiving her secondary education teacher certification from Iowa State University. She is the 2005 award recipient of the James Madison Fellowship, which is awarded to individuals with a desire to become outstanding teachers of the American Constitution at the secondary level. Norris recently stepped down from her 2-year tenure as an adjunct professor at [[American University]].

During the 2008 presidential campaign, Norris was President [[Barack Obama]]'s Iowa State Director<ref>{{Cite web|title=Obama makes final campaign stop in Des Moines|url=http://www.kcci.com/politics/Obama-makes-final-campaign-stop-in-Des-Moines/17281332|accessdate=2015-04-11|date=2012-11-06|website=KCCI News 8}}</ref> after being his Iowa Senior Advisor during the caucus campaign. Norris served as First Lady [[Michelle Obama]]'s White House Chief of Staff.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Michelle's staff shake-up explained|url=http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0609/23417.html|website=politico.com|accessdate=2015-04-11|first=Nia-Malika|last=Allen|date=2009-06-05}}</ref>

Norris later served as Senior Advisor to the CEO at the Corporation for National and Community Service, to advance the Administration’s work on national and community service and support the implementation of the Serve America Act. Norris recently stepped down as the founding Executive Director of the Points of Light Corporate Institute.{{citation needed|date=March 2017}}

==Family==
Norris is married to John R. Norris, former Commissioner at the [[Federal Energy Regulatory Commission]] (FERC) and now Minister Counselor to the USUN in Rome, Italy. Norris has three sons; Hunter, Cole and Sam.<ref>{{Cite web|title=NEWSMAKER: Once indispensable to powerful pols, FERC commissioner navigates power politics|url=http://www.eenews.net/stories/1059979485|website=eenews.net|accessdate=2015-04-11}}</ref>
