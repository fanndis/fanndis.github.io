'''''Love Is''''' (stylized as '''''Love Is ___''''') is an American drama television series created and produced by [[Mara Brock Akil]] and Salim Akil. Based on the Akils’ real-life relationship, the series follows a modern-day power couple in Black Hollywood balancing successful careers and family over three decades. The series premiered on June 19, 2018, on the [[Oprah Winfrey Network]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2018/05/love-is___-premiere-date-own-mara-brock-akil-salim-akil-1202385171/|title=‘Love Is___’ Premiere Date Set On OWN|last=Petski|first=Denise|website=Deadline Hollywood|date=May 8, 2018|accessdate=May 8, 2018}}</ref> On July 31, 2018, it was originally renewed for a second season,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2018/07/love-is-___-renewed-own-season-2-1202437335/|title=‘Love Is ___’ Renewed By OWN For Season 2|last=Petski|first=Denise|website=Deadline Hollywood|date=July 31, 2018|accessdate=July 31, 2018}}</ref> but on December 19, 2018, OWN reversed the decision and canceled the series after a woman accused Salim Akil of domestic violence in their extramarital affair, as well as copyright infringement by using her screenplay as the basis for the series.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2018/12/love-is-canceled-salim-akil-lawsuit-controversy-own-1202523087/|title=OWN Breaks Up With ‘Love Is _’ Amidst EP Salim Akil Domestic Violence Claims|first=Dominic|last=Patten|date=December 19, 2018|publisher=}}</ref>

==Plot==
In 2017, a Black power couple tell the story of how they found love in one another and of their rise to fame in Hollywood starting in the 1990s. When they meet, Nuri is a comedy sitcom staff writer who longs to work in drama and Yasir is an aspiring but out of work writer/director.

==Production==

===Development===
The series originated under the title ''Documenting Love'', a multi-camera comedy project at [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] with a pilot production commitment in August, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/08/mara-brock-akil-salim-akil-romantic-comedy-documenting-love-abc-pilot-production-commitment-1201809588/|title=ABC Nabs Mara Brock Akil & Salim Akil Romantic Comedy ‘Documenting Love’ With Pilot Production Commitment|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva|date=August 26, 2016|website=Deadline Hollywood|accessdate=February 18, 2018}}</ref> The project, which did not go to production at ABC network, later was rewritten as a one-hour single-camera dramedy. On July 18, 2017, it was announced that the project, now titled ''Love Is'', was ordered direct-to-series on [[Oprah Winfrey Network]] for airing in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2017/07/own-relationship-dramedy-series-mara-brock-akil-salim-akil-1202130795/|title=OWN Orders Relationship Dramedy Series From Mara Brock Akil & Salim Akil|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva|date=July 18, 2017|website=Deadline Hollywood|accessdate=February 18, 2018}}</ref>

==Reception==

===Critical response===
On the [[review aggregator]] website [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the series has an approval rating of 80% based on 10 reviews, with an average rating of 6.7/10.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/tv/love_is_/s01/ |title=''Love Is ____'': Season 1 (2018) |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |accessdate=August 23, 2018}}</ref> [[Metacritic]], which uses a weighted average, assigned a score of 66 out of 100 based on 6 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/tv/love-is|title=''Love Is ____'' Reviews|publisher=[[Metacritic]]|accessdate=August 1, 2018}}</ref>
