{{main|2001 French Open}}

[[Jennifer Capriati]] defeated [[Kim Clijsters]] 1–6, 6–4, 12–10 in the final to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2001 French Open]]. This would be the last time until [[2014 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|2014]] in which the women's final would be decided in three sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/27748785|title=Maria Sharapova beats Simona Halep to win French Open|publisher=BBC Sport|first=Piers|last=Newbury|date=7 June 2014|accessdate=23 January 2016}}</ref> Clijsters became the first Belgian woman to reach the final of a Grand Slam, having won an all-Belgian semifinal against [[Justine Henin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://a.espncdn.com/tennis/french01/news/2001/0607/1210698.html|title=Capriati takes down Hingis in straight sets|publisher=ESPN|date=7 June 2001}}</ref>

[[Mary Pierce]] was the defending champion, but she did not compete this year due to a back injury.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/tennis/hingis-seeded-first-pierce-withdraws-from-french-open-1.260457|title=
Hingis seeded first, Pierce withdraws from French Open|publisher=CBC Sports|date=23 May 2001}}</ref>

This is the first Grand Slam tournament in which future Grand Slam champion [[Marion Bartoli]] competed in the main draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/08/16/how-will-marion-bartoli-be-remembered/|title=How will Marion Bartoli be remembered?|publisher=The Roar|date=16 August 2013|accessdate=24 January 2016}}</ref> It was also the French Open debut of [[2010 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|2010]] champion [[Francesca Schiavone]]; she reached the quarterfinals, which was her best result at the tournament until her title nine years later.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/sports/tennis/french10/news/story?id=5246231|title=Dementieva retires after first-set loss|publisher=ESPN|date=4 June 2010}}</ref>

This is also the last Grand Slam tournament to date to feature only 16 seeds.
