'''St. Luke's Medical Center – Global City''' is a hospital at the [[Bonifacio Global City]] in [[Taguig]], [[Metro Manila]], [[Philippines]]. It is the sister facility of the [[St. Luke's Medical Center - Quezon City|hospital of the same name]] in [[Quezon City]]. Both are affiliated with the Episcopal Church of the Philippines.<ref>official site of the Episcopal Church of the Philippines - Institutitions</ref>

==History==
The hospital at the Bonifacio Global City is an offshoot of the hospital of the same name in Quezon City which was established in 1903. On January 16, 2010 the hospital was inaugurated by then President [[Gloria Macapagal Arroyo]].<ref name=rises>{{cite news|last1=Torrevillas|first1=Domini|title=St. Luke's rises on Global City|url=http://www.philstar.com/opinion/540805/st-lukes-rises-global-city|accessdate=3 December 2016|publisher=The Philippine Star|date=16 January 2010}}</ref>

==Architecture and design==
The hospital was designed by architecture firm RR Payumo and Associates and the construction costed around {{Philippine peso|9 billion}}.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Subido|first1=Joy Angelica|title=St. Luke's Medical Center: A look at the future of healthcare|url=http://www.philstar.com/health-and-family/555771/st-lukes-medical-center-look-future-healthcare|accessdate=3 August 2016|publisher=The Philippine Star|date=3 December 2016}}</ref> A joint venture between First Balfour and Makati Development Corporation was involved in the construction of the hospital.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://firstbalfour.com/project/st-lukes-medical-center-bonifacio-global-city/|title=St. Luke’s Medical Center – Bonifacio Global City – First Balfour|website=firstbalfour.com|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref><ref name=mdc>{{cite web|title=St. Luke's Medical Center - Global City|url=http://www.mdc.com.ph/our-projects/st-lukes-medical-center-global-city|accessdate=3 December 2016|publisher=Makati Development Corporation}}</ref>

Upon its inauguration the health facility hosts a 14-storey nursing tower with a capacity of 600 beds, a helipad and a podium, along with an 11-storey medical arts building which can house clinics for 374 doctors and a ground floor with lobbies described as "hotel-like", and a multi-level parking with at least 1,000 parking slots.<ref name=rises/> The hospital's gross floor area is about {{convert|154000|sqm|sqft|sp=us}}.<ref name=mdc/>

==Facilities==
The hospital hosts 10 institutes dedicated to cancer, digestive and liver diseases, eyes, neurosciences, orthopedics and sports medicine, pathology, pediatrics and child care, pulmonary medicines, and radiology. It also has 18 operating rooms, five delivery rooms, imaging suites, ancillary outpatient services, critical care units, catheterization laboratory, OB-gynecology complex and post anesthetic care units.<ref name=rises/>
The center in Global City houses 10 institutes for the heart, cancer, neurosciences, digestive and liver diseases, eyes, orthopedics and sports medicine, pathology, pediatrics and child care, pulmonary medicines and radiology.<ref name=rises/>

==Reception==
United States based magazine, ''Healthcare Management News and Insights'', list St. Lukes Medical Center - Global City as part of its "World’s Most Beautiful Hospitals" on its March 2012 issue where the hospital was ranked 11th out of the 25 listed. The hospitals were rated by the magazine's editors according to their  interior and exterior features, as well as their "health-promoting" features. St. Lukes was only one of the three Asian hospitals on the list.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carvajal|first1=Nancy|title=St. Luke’s rated among world’s most beautiful hospitals|url=https://globalnation.inquirer.net/32207/st-luke%E2%80%99s-rated-among-world%E2%80%99s-most-beautiful-hospitals|accessdate=3 December 2016|publisher=Philippine Daily Inquirer|date=10 April 2012}}</ref>
