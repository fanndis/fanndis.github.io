'''Bruton Street''' is a street in London's [[Mayfair]] district.

It runs from [[Berkeley Square]] in the south-west to [[New Bond Street]] in the north-east, where it continues as [[Conduit Street]].

Notable residents have included Field Marshal [[John Campbell, 2nd Duke of Argyll]], and [[Richard Brinsley Sheridan]].<ref name="BHO">Edward Walford, 'Berkeley Square and its neighbourhood', in Old and New London: Volume 4 (London, 1878), pp. 326-338. British History Online http://www.british-history.ac.uk/old-new-london/vol4/pp326-338 [accessed 19 September 2020].</ref>

On 21 April 1926, the Queen was born at no.17, the London home of her maternal grandparents, the Earl and Countess of Strathmore and Kinghorne.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://royalcentral.co.uk/uk/queen/the-queens-london-birthplace-17-bruton-st-59298/|title=The Queenâ€™s London Birthplace: 17 Bruton St|date=April 20, 2017}}</ref> The house was damaged in [[the Blitz]], and was later demolished.<ref name="auto"/>

The fashion designer [[Norman Hartnell]] lived and worked at no.26 from 1935 until his death in 1979.
