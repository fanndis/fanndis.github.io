'''General elections''' were held in [[Denmark]] on 5 June 2019 to elect all 179 members of the [[Folketing]];<ref>[https://www.reuters.com/article/us-denmark-election/denmarks-prime-minister-calls-election-to-be-held-on-june-5-idUSKCN1SD180 Denmark's prime minister calls election to be held on June 5] Reuters, 7 May 2019</ref> 175 in [[Geography of Denmark|Denmark]] proper, two in the [[Faroe Islands]] and two in [[Greenland]]. The elections took place ten days after the [[2019 European Parliament election in Denmark|European Parliament elections]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/ep-valg/ep-spidskandidater-uenige-er-folketingsvalgkamp-godt-eller-skidt-eu-debatten|title=EP-spidskandidater uenige: Er Folketingsvalgkamp godt eller skidt for EU-debatten?|last=Lindqvist|first=Andreas|date=7 May 2019|work=DR|access-date=7 May 2019|language=da}}</ref>

The elections resulted in a victory for the "red bloc", comprising parties that supported the [[Social Democrats (Denmark)|Social Democrats]]' leader [[Mette Frederiksen]] as candidate for Prime Minister.  The "red bloc", consisting of the Social Democrats, the [[Danish Social Liberal Party|Social Liberals]], [[Socialist People's Party (Denmark)|Socialist People's Party]], the [[Red–Green Alliance (Denmark)|Red–Green Alliance]], the [[Faroe Islands|Faroese]] [[Social Democratic Party (Faroe Islands)|Social Democratic Party]] and the Greenlandic [[Siumut]],<ref name=":10">{{Cite news|url=https://knr.gl/da/nyheder/fakta-nordatlanten-har-f%C3%B8r-afgjort-dansk-valg|title= FACTS: The North Atlantic has previously decided Danish elections|date=5 June 2019|work=KNR|access-date=7 June 2019}}</ref> won 93 of the 179 seats, securing a parliamentary majority. Meanwhile, the incumbent governing coalition, consisting of [[Venstre (Denmark)|Venstre]], the [[Liberal Alliance (Denmark)|Liberal Alliance]] and the [[Conservative People's Party (Denmark)|Conservative People's Party]] whilst receiving parliamentary support from the [[Danish People's Party]] and [[Nunatta Qitornai]], was reduced to 76 seats (including the [[Venstre (Denmark)|Venstre]]-affiliated [[Union Party (Faroe Islands)|Union Party]] in the [[Faroe Islands]]).

On 6 June, incumbent Prime Minister [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen]] of the [[centre-right]] [[liberalism|liberal]] [[Venstre (Denmark)|Venstre]] party tendered his resignation, and Frederiksen was tasked with forming a new government. On 25 June, Frederiksen reached an agreement with the red bloc, and on 27 June she was appointed Prime Minister and [[Frederiksen Cabinet|her government]], a single-party Social Democratic government, took office.

== Background ==
At the [[2015 Danish general election|2015 general election]], a narrow majority was won by the [[Danish People's Party]], [[Venstre (Denmark)|Venstre]], [[Liberal Alliance (Denmark)|Liberal Alliance]] and the [[Conservative People's Party (Denmark)|Conservative People's Party]], colloquially known as the "blue bloc". They won 90 seats in the Folketing versus 89 seats for the remaining parties, all belonging to the "red bloc". Ten days later, [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen]], the leader of Venstre, became [[Prime Minister of Denmark|Prime Minister]], when Venstre formed a [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen II Cabinet|single-party government]] supported by the remaining parties in the "blue bloc".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jyllands-posten.dk/politik/ECE7834142/Her-er-hele-Lars-L%C3%B8kkes-ministerhold/|title=Her er hele Lars Løkkes ministerhold|publisher=[[Jyllands-Posten]]|date=28 June 2015|language=da|access-date=28 June 2015}}</ref> In November 2016, Rasmussen formed a [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen III Cabinet|new government]], now a coalition with Liberal Alliance, and the Conservative People's Party.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-denmark-government-idUSKBN13N0NW|title=Danish PM names new coalition ministers, reducing risk of snap poll|last=PultzNielsen|first=Annabella|date=28 November 2016|work=Reuters|access-date=6 June 2019|language=en}}</ref>

== Electoral system ==
Of the 179 members of the Folketing, 175 are elected in Denmark proper, two in [[Faroe Islands]] and two in [[Greenland]]. In Denmark there are ten multi-member constituencies containing a total of 135 seats directly elected by [[proportional representation]], with seats allocated using a modified version of the [[Sainte-Laguë method]] and [[Hare quota]]. An additional 40 [[leveling seat]]s are used to address any imbalance in the distribution of the constituency seats, and are distributed among all parties that cross the 2% [[election threshold]], according to their national vote share.<ref>{{cite web|title=Folketinget (The Danish Parliament)|url=http://www.ipu.org/parline-e/reports/2087_B.htm|publisher=[[Inter-Parliamentary Union]]|access-date=24 June 2015|date=10 April 1991}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/valg2015/saadan-taelles-stemmerne-op-forstaa-det-danske-valgsystem-paa-fem-minutter|title=Sådan tælles stemmerne op – forstå det danske valgsystem på fem minutter|last=Madsen|first=Katrine|date=18 June 2015|work=DR|access-date=7 May 2019|language=da}}</ref>

Voters could choose between casting a personal vote for a candidate, or voting for a political party. The votes given to political parties are distributed among the candidates for that party. This can either be done in proportion to their personal votes, or by giving them to candidates in a predetermined order. All parties except the [[Red–Green Alliance (Denmark)|Red–Green Alliance]] make use of the first option.<ref name=":0" />

According to the [[Constitution of Denmark|Danish Constitution]], the 2019 election was required to be held no later than 17 June 2019, as the [[2015 Danish general election|previous elections]] were held on 18 June 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thedanishparliament.dk/en/news/2019/05/general-election-2019|title=General election in Denmark 2019|website=The Danish Parliament|language=da|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/print_da.html|title=Europe :: Denmark — The World Factbook – Central Intelligence Agency|website=www.cia.gov|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref> The Prime Minister is able to call the election at any date,<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|url=https://denmark.dk/society-and-business/government-and-politics|title=Government and politics|website=Denmark.dk|language=en|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref> provided that date is no later than four years from the previous election,<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Faerkel|first=Jens|date=1982|title=Some aspects of the constitution of denmark|journal=Irish Jurist|volume=17|issue=1|pages=8|issn=0021-1273|quote=If, however, the four-year period expires without an election, the prime minister incurs responsibility (Art. 32, para. 3).|jstor=44026921}}</ref> and this is cited as a tactical advantage for the sitting government.<ref name=":8" />

For a new party to become eligible to participate in the election, they must be supported by a number of voters corresponding to 1/175 of all valid votes cast in the previous election. A new party registering to contest the 2019 elections required 20,109 voter declarations to participate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://valg.oim.dk/partier-og-kandidater/hvordan-danner-jeg-et-nyt-parti/|title=Hvordan danner jeg et nyt parti?|date=2 May 2019|website=Økonomi- og indenrigsministeriet|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref>

== Participating parties ==
=== Denmark ===
{{See also|Opinion polling for the 2019 Danish general election}}
All nine parties that held seats in the Folketing contested the elections.<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|url=http://www.dst.dk/valg/Valg1487635/valgopg/valgopgHL.htm|title=Folketingsvalg torsdag 18. juni 2015|date=20 June 2015|publisher=[[Statistics Denmark]]|access-date=7 May 2019}}</ref> Four other parties also gained ballot access: three new parties on the right and the [[Christian Democrats (Denmark)|Christian Democrats]], who lost representation in the [[2005 Danish general election|2005 election]]. In October 2016, [[The New Right (Denmark)|The New Right]], a new right-wing party, became eligible to run in the election,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/nye-borgerlige-kan-stille-op-til-naeste-valg|title=Nye Borgerlige kan stille op til næste valg|date=6 October 2016|work=DR Nyheder|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> and a year later, in October 2017, the Christian Democrats did likewise. The latter had participated in every election from 1971 to 2005.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/indland/kristendemokraterne-melder-sig-klar-til-naeste-folketingsvalg|title=Kristendemokraterne melder sig klar til næste folketingsvalg|last=Batchelor|first=Oliver|date=14 October 2017|website=DR Nyheder|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref>

In February 2019, the party [[Klaus Riskær Pedersen (party)|Klaus Riskær Pedersen]], named after [[Klaus Riskær Pedersen|its founder]], collected the necessary voter declarations and became eligible too, despite, breaking the rules for collection of declarations. Riskær Pedersen found a way to circumvent a 7-day "thinking period" between a voter noting their support for a party and then confirming their signature in the online collection system.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/20109-stiller-sig-bag-klaus-riskaer-har-underskrifter-i-hus-paa-lyntid|title=20.109 stiller sig bag Klaus Riskær: Har underskrifter i hus på lyntid|last=Jørgensen|first=Anna Sol|date=18 February 2019|work=DR Nyheder|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> Even though the rules did not allow this, they contained no possibility of sanctions.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/riskaer-og-stram-kurs-har-brudt-reglerne-flertal-vil-lukke-smutvej-til|title=Riskær og 'Stram Kurs' har brudt reglerne: Flertal vil lukke smutvej til vælgererklæringer|date=15 February 2019|work=DR|access-date=3 June 2019|agency=Ritzau}}</ref> Following this, all political parties in the Folketing agreed to close the loophole and build a new portal for declarations, expected to be available in the end 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-03-22-efter-riskaers-regelbrud-nu-aendres-indsamlingen-af-vaelgererklaeringer|title=Efter Riskærs regelbrud: Nu ændres indsamlingen af vælgererklæringer|last=Nielsen|first=Morten|date=22 March 2019|work=TV2|access-date=3 May 2019|last2=Agger|first2=Simone}}</ref>

In April 2019, following unrest at [[Nørrebro]] caused by demonstrations by [[anti-Islamist]] politician [[Rasmus Paludan (politician)|Rasmus Paludan]], his party [[Hard Line (party)|Hard Line]] managed to collect the required signatures.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/stram-kurs-er-nu-officielt-klar-til-folketingsvalg|title=Stram Kurs er nu officielt klar til folketingsvalg|date=6 May 2019|work=DR|access-date=7 May 2019|language=da}}</ref> Paludan and his party have been surrounded with controversy, with demonstrations containing activities like throwing the [[Quran]] around, burning the Quran and harassing Muslims. In April 2019, Paludan was sentenced for violating a paragraph in the [[Danish Penal Code]] colloquially known as the "racism paragraph".<ref>{{cite news |last=Wandrup|first=Katrine Frederikke|title=Rasmus Paludan dømt|date=5 April 2019|url=https://www.bt.dk/content/item/1282224|language=da|access-date=6 June 2019|last2=Ritzau}}</ref> He had also circumvented the "thinking period" in the same manner as Riskær Pedersen.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-04-27-stram-kurs-har-faaet-underskrifter-nok-til-at-stille-op-til-folketinget|title=Stram Kurs har fået underskrifter nok til at stille op til Folketinget|last=Bagge|first=Christoffer Løvstrup|date=27 April 2019|work=TV2|access-date=2 May 2019|last2=Biener|first2=Mads}}</ref>

== Campaign ==
=== Early statements ===
In October 2017, [[The New Right (Denmark)|The New Right]], a new right-wing political party that became eligible to run in October 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/nye-borgerlige-kan-stille-op-til-naeste-valg|title=Nye Borgerlige kan stille op til næste valg|website=DR|language=da-DK|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> listed three demands for a candidate for Prime Minister to receive their support. All three demands were tightenings of the immigration policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://borsen.dk/nyheder/politik/artikel/1/352546/nye_borgerlige_har_tre_ultimative_krav_til_ny_statsminister.html|title=Nye Borgerlige har tre ultimative krav til ny statsminister|last=Ritzau|date=14 October 2017|website=borsen.dk|language=da|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref>

On 4 June 2018, the [[Social Democrats (Denmark)|Social Democrats]], the largest opposition party, stated that if they were to win the election, they wished to form a single-party government led by their leader [[Mette Frederiksen]], i.e. not as a coalition government with the [[Social Liberal Party (Denmark)|Social Liberal Party]]. This was done in order to both pursue traditional centre-left issues, and to have a strict immigration policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/mette-frederiksen-gaar-til-valg-paa-socialdemokratisk-et-parti-regering|title=Mette Frederiksen går til valg på socialdemokratisk et-parti-regering|website=DR|language=da-DK|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> [[Morten Østergaard]], the leader of the Social Liberal Party, responded by saying that if the Social Democrats wanted their support, they would also need to give them concessions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/oestergaard-efter-s-melding-man-kan-ikke-baade-vaelge-vores-politik-fra-og-taelle|title=Østergaard efter S-melding: Man kan ikke både vælge vores politik fra og tælle vores mandater med|website=DR|language=da-DK|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> The message was welcomed by the anti-immigration [[Danish People's Party]], which supported the centre-right party Venstre in the election. Their leader [[Kristian Thulesen Dahl]], said that this would ensure that they could get through with their immigration policy, no matter which party won the election.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/thulesen-takker-mette-frederiksens-melding-men-vil-hellere-i-regering-med-loekke|title=Thulesen takker for Mette Frederiksens melding, men vil hellere i regering med Løkke|website=DR|language=da-DK|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref>

On 26 June 2018, [[The Alternative (Denmark)|The Alternative]], which traditionally is regarded as belonging to the "red bloc", stated that they no longer would support Mette Frederiksen as candidate to become Prime Minister. Instead, they would support their own political leader, [[Uffe Elbæk]], as a way to "pull their seats from the equation" after the election.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://politiken.dk/indland/politik/art6604931/Uffe-Elb%C3%A6k-vil-selv-v%C3%A6re-statsminister|title=Vil ikke støtte Mette Frederiksen: Uffe Elbæk vil selv være statsminister|date=26 June 2018|website=Politiken|language=da-DK|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> This was done because they did not regard the other parties' ambitions concerning [[climate change]] to be sufficient.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.berlingske.dk/content/item/57134|title=Alternativet opretter egen grøn blok men går med i energiaftale|date=29 June 2018|website=Berlingske.dk|language=da|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> The move was met with criticism, as Elbæk's chances are very slim, and it could risk keeping Lars Løkke Rasmussen as Prime Minister.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.berlingske.dk/content/item/96741|title=Det er helt deprimerende at opleve et parti være så desperat, som jeg oplever, Alternativet er her|date=27 June 2018|website=Berlingske.dk|language=da|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref>

=== Campaign begins ===
The election campaign started on 7 May 2019, when [[Prime Minister of Denmark|Prime Minister]] Lars Løkke Rasmussen announced that the election would be held on 5 June, which is [[Constitution Day (Denmark)|Constitution Day]]. At the time of announcement, [[Mette Frederiksen]], leader of the Social Democrats and contender to the office of Prime Minister, was sick and unable to participate in the televised debates between all party leaders held on the same evening. Instead, former minister [[Nicolai Wammen]] represented the Social Democrats in the debates.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/2019-05-07-folketingsvalget-er-udskrevet-lav-din-egen-regering-thomas-borgen-sigtet|title=Folketingsvalget er udskrevet {{!}} Lav din egen regering {{!}} Thomas Borgen sigtet|last=Christoffersen|first=Philip|date=7 May 2019|work=TV2|access-date=13 May 2019|language=da}}</ref> Frederiksen started campaigning on 10 May.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.kristeligt-dagblad.dk/danmark/mette-frederiksen-er-rask-igen|title=Mette Frederiksen er rask igen og klar til valgkamp|date=9 May 2019|work=Kristeligt Dagblad|access-date=13 May 2019|agency=Ritzau|language=da}}</ref>

=== Talks about an SV coalition government ===
On 16 May, Løkke Rasmussen published a book, in which he was open to a possible coalition government between Venstre and the Social Democrats (an SV-government). He said that he offered to be the "grown up", as a [[Grand coalition|coalition across the political middle]] would be better than a government depending on the outermost political wings, but stressed that he and Venstre still campaigned for a centre-right government.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-05-16-loekke-laegger-op-til-sv-regering-i-ny-bog-jeg-aabner-doeren-paa-klem|title=Løkke lægger op til SV-regering i ny bog: - Jeg åbner døren på klem|last=Braagaard|first=Natali|date=19 May 2019|work=TV2|access-date=25 May 2019|language=da}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://politiken.dk/indland/politik/FV19/art7204805/%C2%BBJeg-tilbyder-at-v%C3%A6re-den-voksne-i-lokalet%C2%AB|title=Løkke om SV-åbning: "Jeg tilbyder at være den voksne i lokalet"|date=16 May 2019|work=Politiken|access-date=25 May 2019|agency=Ritzau|language=da}}</ref> The announcement was remarkable and regarded as a gamble; Løkke Rasmussen called it a "game changer".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.berlingske.dk/politik/loekke-smider-bombe-midt-i-valgkampen-aabner-for-regering-med-venstre-og|title=Løkke smider bombe midt i valgkampen: Åbner for regering med Venstre og Socialdemokratiet|last=Rohde|first=Thomas|date=16 May 2019|work=Berlingske|access-date=25 May 2019|language=da}}</ref> Prominent figures in Venstre, among those deputy chairman and Minister of Finance [[Kristian Jensen]] and Minister for Immigration [[Inger Støjberg]], were deeply critical of the idea<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.berlingske.dk/politik/paa-et-lukket-moede-i-statsministeriet-praesenterede-loekke-en|title=På et lukket møde i Statsministeriet præsenterede Løkke en opsigtsvækkende idé – og gjorde Kristian Jensen urolig|last=Kildegaard|first=Kasper|date=16 May 2019|work=Berlingske|access-date=25 May 2019|last2=Domino|first2=Søren|language=da|last3=Bloch|first3=Cathrine|last4=Holst|first4=Helene Kristine|url-access=subscription}}</ref> while other supported the idea.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/folketingsvalg/fremtraedende-venstrefolk-bakker-op-om-loekkes-tanker-om-sv-regering|title=Fremtrædende Venstrefolk bakker op om Løkkes tanker om SV-regering|last=Toft|first=Emma|date=16 May 2019|work=DR|access-date=25 May 2019|last2=Jakobsen|first2=Jakob Skov|language=da}}</ref> Denmark had an SV-government between 1978 and 1979. [[Jørgensen III Cabinet|That government]] under Prime Minister [[Anker Jørgensen]] of the Social Democrats was widely regarded as a fiasco.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://danmarkshistorien.dk/leksikon-og-kilder/vis/materiale/ministeriet-anker-joergensen-iii-1978-1979/|title=Ministeriet Anker Jørgensen III, 1978–1979|last=Farbøl|first=Farbøl|date=15 November 2017|work=danmarkshistorien.dk|access-date=25 May 2019|publisher=Aarhus University|last2=Olesen|first2=Thorsten Borring|language=da}}</ref>

The day prior, the Social Liberals' vice chairman, [[Martin Lidegaard]], had said that a government with both Venstre, the Social Democrats and the Social Liberals would be the "dream scenario".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-06-04-oestergaard-ingen-redningsplanke-til-loekke-vil-soege-aftale-med-mette-f|title=Østergaard: Ingen redningsplanke til Løkke – vil søge aftale med Mette F.|last=Mortensen|first=Mikkel Walentin|date=4 June 2019|work=TV2|access-date=4 June 2019|language=da}}</ref>

== Results ==
Overall the election was a win for the "red bloc" – the parties that supported Mette Frederiksen, leader of the Social Democrats, as Prime Minister. In total, the Social Democrats, the Social Liberals, Socialist People's Party and the Red–Green Alliance won 91 seats. Green party The Alternative chose to go into opposition as a "green bloc".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.berlingske.dk/politik/paa-en-varm-dag-i-juni-blev-danmark-malet-roedt-nu-venter-benhaarde|title=På en varm dag i juni blev Danmark malet rødt: Nu venter benhårde forhandlinger|last=Kildegaard|first=Kasper|date=6 June 2019|work=Berlingske|access-date=6 June 2019|language=da|url-access=subscription}}</ref>

The Social Democrats defended their position as the largest party, and won an additional seat despite a slightly reduced voter share. They were closely followed by Venstre, who saw the largest gains in seats, picking up an extra nine. In the "blue bloc", only Venstre and the Conservative People's Party saw gains, the latter doubling their seats. The Danish People's Party's vote share fell by 12.4 [[percentage point]]s (pp), well over half of their support. Leader [[Kristian Thulesen Dahl]] speculated that the bad result was due to an extraordinary good election in 2015, and that some voters felt they could "gain [their] policy elsewhere".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://politiken.dk/indland/politik/FV19/art7241627/Thulesen-Vi-har-f%C3%A5et-en-v%C3%A6lgerlussing|title=Thulesen: Vi har fået en vælgerlussing|date=5 June 2019|work=Politiken|access-date=6 June 2019|language=da}}</ref> The Liberal Alliance saw their vote share fall by over two-thirds and became the smallest party in the Folketing, only 0.3pp above the 2% [[election threshold]]. Their leader [[Anders Samuelsen]] was not reelected and he subsequently resigned as leader, succeeded by [[Alex Vanopslagh]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/folketingsvalg/alex-vanopslagh-bliver-liberal-alliances-nye-politiske-leder|title=Alex Vanopslagh bliver Liberal Alliances nye politiske leder|last=Ingvorsen|first=Emil Søndergård|date=9 June 2019|work=DR|access-date=9 June 2019|last2=Nielsen|first2=Kevin Ahrens|language=da}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/folketingsvalg/katastrofevalg-til-liberal-alliance-samuelsen-er-ude-af-folketinget|title=Katastrofevalg til Liberal Alliance: Samuelsen er ude af Folketinget|last=Thomsen|first=Per Bang|date=5 June 2019|work=DR|access-date=6 June 2019|last2=Toft|first2=Emma|language=da}}</ref>

Of the new parties, only the New Right won seats, with Hard Line, the Christian Democrats and Klaus Riskær Pedersen failing to cross the national 2% threshold, although the Christian Democrats were within 200 votes of winning a direct seat in the western Jutland constituency.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/2019-06-05-kristendemokraterne-under-200-stemmer-fra-at-komme-i-folketinget|title=Kristendemokraterne under 200 stemmer fra at komme i Folketinget|last=Søe|first=Carl-Emil|date=5 June 2019|work=TV2|access-date=6 June 2019|language=da}}</ref> On election night, [[Klaus Riskær Pedersen]] announced that he would dissolve his party.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-06-05-klaus-riskaer-pedersen-oploeser-sit-parti|title=Klaus Riskær Pedersen opløser sit parti|last=Josevski|first=Aleksandar|date=5 June 2019|work=TV2|access-date=6 June 2019|language=da}}</ref>

In the Faroe Islands, Republic (which had finished first in the 2015 elections)<ref>[http://kvf.fo/greinar/2015/06/18/tjodveldi-og-javnadarflokkurin-storstir Tjóðveldi og Javnaðarflokkurin størstir] KVF, 18 June 2015</ref> dropped to fourth place and lost their seat. The Union Party replaced them as the first party while the Social Democratic Party finished in second place again, retaining their seat.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altinget.dk/artikel/loesrivelsespartier-ryger-ud-af-folketinget|title=Løsrivelsespartier ryger ud af Folketinget|last=Af Andreas Krog {{!}} 6. juni 2019 kl. 9:05 {{!}} Print|date=6 June 2019|website=Altinget.dk|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref>
In Greenland, the result was a repeat of the 2015 elections, with Inuit Ataqatigiit and Siumut winning the two seats. Siumut regained parliamentary representation after their previous MP, Aleqa Hammond, was expelled from the party in 2016.<ref name=":7">{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/omstridte-aleqa-hammond-smides-ud-af-roedt-valgforbund|title=Omstridte Aleqa Hammond smides ud af rødt valgforbund|date=19 December 2016|work=DR|access-date=21 May 2019|language=da}}</ref><ref name=":11">{{Cite web|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/samfund/2019-06-06-rode-partier-vinder-valget-pa-gronland|title=Røde partier vinder valget på Grønland|date=6 June 2019|website=TV 2|language=da-DK|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref> Hammond later joined Nunatta Qitornai,<ref name="HammondRef2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bt.dk/politik/groenlandsk-loesrivelsesparti-er-loekkes-sikkerhedsnet|title=Grønlandsk løsrivelsesparti er Løkkes sikkerhedsnet|date=26 April 2018|work=BT|access-date=3 May 2019|agency=Ritzau}}</ref> which finished fourth and failed to win a seat.<ref name=":11" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.valg.gl/resultater|title=Kalaallit Nunaanni Qinersinerit – Valg i Greenland|website=Valg.gl|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref>

== Government formation ==
On election night, Prime Minister [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen]] announced that [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen III Cabinet|his government]] would resign the following day.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-06-05-regeringen-gaar-af-men-loekke-vil-fortsaette-som-statsminister|title=Regeringen går af - men Løkke vil fortsætte som statsminister|last=Christoffersen|first=Philip|date=6 June 2019|work=TV 2|access-date=29 June 2019|last2=Secher|first2=Mikkel|language=da}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/world/denmark-election-fallout-1.5164534|title=Denmark's PM Lars Loekke Rasmussen resigns after election disappointment|date=6 June 2019|work=CBC|access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref> Following consultations with the political parties known as a "Queen's round" (Danish: ''Dronningerunde''), Queen [[Margrethe II of Denmark|Margrethe II]] tasked [[Mette Frederiksen]] with forming a new government. At the Queen's round, the Social Liberals, the Socialist People's Party and the Red–Green Alliance supported the Social Democratic leader. Government negotiations started on 7 June.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://jyllands-posten.dk/politik/ECE11430815/mette-frederiksen-er-valgt-som-kongelig-undersoeger/|title=Mette Frederiksen er valgt som kongelig undersøger|date=6 June 2019|work=Jyllands-Posten|access-date=29 June 2019|agency=Ritzau|language=da}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/ligetil/indland/mette-frederiksen-skal-forsoege-danne-ny-regering|title=Mette Frederiksen skal forsøge at danne ny regering|last=Bollerslev|first=Anne|date=7 June 2019|work=DR|access-date=29 June 2019|language=da}}</ref>

On 19 June, the Social Democrats, the Socialist People's Party and the Red–Green Alliance announced an agreement on [[global warming]], committing to reduce Denmark's emission of [[Carbon dioxide|CO<sub>2</sub>]] by 70% in 2030. The Social Liberals decided to stay away from the meeting, as they were dissatisfied that parts were agreed before the negotiations was complete.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-06-19-derfor-meldte-oestergaard-afbud-til-klimamoede-med-frederiksen|title=Derfor meldte Østergaard afbud til klimamøde med Frederiksen|last=Bagge|first=Christoffer Løvstrup|date=19 June 2019|work=TV 2|access-date=29 June 2019|language=da}}</ref>

On 25 June, the four parties announced that they had reached an agreement, allowing Frederiksen to become Prime Minister as leader of a single-party Social Democratic government.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://jyllands-posten.dk/politik/ECE11466474/mette-frederiksen-har-landet-en-aftale-om-ny-regering/|title=Mette Frederiksen har landet en aftale om ny regering|last=Jensen|first=Signe From|date=25 June 2019|work=Jyllands-Posten|access-date=29 June 2019|language=da}}</ref> Completed 20 days after the general election, the negotiations was the longest since [[1988 Danish general election|1988]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://politiken.dk/indland/politik/FV19/art7269460/Mette-F.-overg%C3%A5r-Thornings-forhandlingsmaraton-i-2011|title=Mette F. overgår Thornings forhandlingsmaraton i 2011|last=Beim|first=Jakob Hvide|date=24 June 2019|work=Politiken|access-date=29 June 2019|language=da|url-access=subscription}}</ref> Frederiksen decided not to formulate a government basis [[white paper]], as is otherwise tradition, saying that it was sufficient with the 18-page "political understanding" she had agreed with her parliamentary support.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://politiken.dk/indland/politik/FV19/art7271370/Mette-Frederiksen-dropper-et-regeringsgrundlag|title=Mette Frederiksen dropper et regeringsgrundlag|date=26 June 2019|work=Politiken|access-date=29 June 2019|agency=Ritzau|language=da}}</ref> On 27 June, the [[Frederiksen Cabinet|new cabinet]] was announced and took office the same day.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/viser-stolt-sine-nye-ministre-frem-vores-socialdemokratiske-kompas-skal-lede-os|title=Viser stolt sine nye ministre frem: Vores socialdemokratiske kompas skal lede os|last=Toft|first=Emma|date=27 June 2019|work=DR|access-date=29 June 2019|language=da}}</ref> The average age of ministers were 41.8 years, and Frederiksen herself became the youngest person to hold the office of Prime Minister.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nyheder.tv2.dk/politik/2019-06-28-danmark-skal-nu-styres-af-den-yngste-statsminister-nogensinde|title=Danmark skal nu styres af den yngste statsminister nogensinde|last=Nielsen|first=Nanna Jakobsgaard|date=28 June 2019|work=TV 2|access-date=29 June 2019|language=da}}</ref>
