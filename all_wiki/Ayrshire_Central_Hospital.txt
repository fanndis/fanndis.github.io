'''Ayrshire Central Hospital''', also known as '''Irvine Central Hospital''', is an [[NHS Scotland|NHS]] hospital in [[Irvine, North Ayrshire|Irvine]], [[North Ayrshire]], [[Scotland]]. It is managed by [[NHS Ayrshire and Arran]].

==History==
===Formation===
The hospital is constructed on land which was formerly part of the southern portions of the [[Eglinton Castle|Eglinton Castle Estate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ryden.co.uk/files/public/000/002/880/2880/Statutory%20Listing.pdf|title=Statutory Listing|publisher=Historic Scotland|date=3 December 1992|accessdate=24 December 2018}}</ref> The hospital, which was designed by William Reid and commissioned by the now defunct [[Ayr County Council]], was built from 1936 and was opened in stages from 1941 and became fully operational by 1944.<ref name=hh>{{cite web|url=https://historic-hospitals.com/2015/04/30/ayrshire-central-hospital/|title=Ayrshire Central Hospital|accessdate=24 December 2018}}</ref> The hospital was constructed as a series of pavilions.<ref name=hh/> It joined the [[National Health Service]] in 1948.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhsaaa.net/news/latest-news/70-years-of-care-let-s-celebrate-our-nhs/|title=70 years of care, let’s celebrate our NHS|date=6 July 2018|publisher=NHS Ayrshire and Arran|accessdate=24 December 2018}}</ref>

===Flooding incident===
On 1 December 2005 the maternity building was [[flood]]ed by a burst water tank. The flooding threatened the [[Neonatal intensive care unit|special care baby unit]] and took fire crews from [[Dreghorn]], [[Kilwinning]] and [[Kilmarnock]] 3 hours to control.<ref name="flood">{{cite web|url=http://www.north-ayrshire.gov.uk/chiefexec/comRA.nsf/a35a7276035b6d7e8025707d004fcff5/d818149a2dea235480257116004d7fc9/$FILE/Strathclyde%20Fire%20and%20Rescue%20report.doc |date=February 2006 |title=Strathclyde Fire and Rescue Report |accessdate=2007-10-15 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110608010324/http://www.north-ayrshire.gov.uk/chiefexec/comRA.nsf/a35a7276035b6d7e8025707d004fcff5/d818149a2dea235480257116004d7fc9/%24FILE/Strathclyde%20Fire%20and%20Rescue%20report.doc |archivedate=8 June 2011 }}</ref>

===Maternity and neonatal units===
Until the 1970's uncomplicated births were usually at home or at the nearby Buckredden Maternity Home in [[Kilwinning]]<ref name="births"/> while births at the hospital were often those that could involve complications.<ref name="births">{{cite web|url=http://archiver.rootsweb.com/th/read/AYRSHIRE/2004-05/1085958235 |title=RootsWeb: Ayrshire-L Re: Irvine Central Hospital |publisher=Archiver.rootsweb.com |date=2004-05-31 |accessdate=2009-03-10}}</ref> Subsequently most local births took place in the hospital. However Ayrshire paediatric services were centralised at Crosshouse in July 2006<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nhsayrshireandarran.com/view_item.aspx?item_id=3327&list_id=list1-3324&list_index=0 |title=Children's inpatient services are on the move |date=2006-07-10 |publisher=NHS Ayrshire and Arran |accessdate=2007-10-15 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714180629/http://www.nhsayrshireandarran.com/view_item.aspx?item_id=3327&list_id=list1-3324&list_index=0 |archivedate=14 July 2011 |df=dmy }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nhsayrshireandarran.com/uploads/1957/childrenA5.pdf |title=The future of inpatient services for Ayrshire & Arran’ s children |publisher=NHS Ayrshire and Arran |accessdate=2007-10-15 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714180543/http://www.nhsayrshireandarran.com/uploads/1957/childrenA5.pdf |archivedate=14 July 2011 |df=dmy }}</ref> while maternity services moved to the new Ayrshire Maternity Unit at [[University Hospital Crosshouse|Crosshouse Hospital]] in August 2006.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.nhsayrshireandarran.com/uploads/3402/openD050806.pdf| title=Ayrshire Maternity Unit at Crosshouse| publisher=NHS Ayrshire and Arran| accessdate=2007-10-15| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20071106210736/http://www.nhsayrshireandarran.com/uploads/3402/openD050806.pdf| archivedate= 6 November 2007 | url-status= live}}</ref>

==Services==
The hospital provides young disabled services and rehabilitation services, and has a number of assessment beds for the Elderly Mental Health Service.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nhsaaa.net/hospitals-and-health-centres/ayrshire-central-hospital.aspx |title=Ayrshire Central Hospital |publisher=[[NHS Ayrshire and Arran]] |accessdate=13 July 2014}}</ref>
