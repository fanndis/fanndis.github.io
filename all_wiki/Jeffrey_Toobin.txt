'''Jeffrey Ross Toobin'''<ref name="NYTimes1986">{{cite news |url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9A0DE1DD103EF932A35755C0A960948260 |title=J.R. Toobin Weds Amy B. McIntosh |date=June 1, 1986 |access-date=July 11, 2008 |work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref> ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|t|uː|b|ɪ|n}}; born May 21, 1960) is an American lawyer, blogger, author and legal analyst for [[CNN]].<ref name="ContribNY" /> During the [[Iran–Contra affair]], he served as an associate counsel in the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]. He moved from government into writing during the 1990s, and wrote for ''[[The New Yorker]]'' from 1993 to 2020.

Toobin has written several books, including an account of the [[O. J. Simpson murder case]]. That book was adapted as a TV series, ''[[The People v. O. J. Simpson: American Crime Story]],'' which aired in 2016 as the first season of [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] ''[[American Crime Story]]''; it won nine [[Emmy Award]]s.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.emmys.com/shows/people-v-oj-simpson-american-crime-story
| title = The People vs O.J.Simpson Emmy Awards| date = December 10, 2016| accessdate = October 6, 2019| website = Emmys.com}}</ref> In October of 2020, Toobin took a leave from CNN and was suspended and then was fired from ''[[The New Yorker]]'' after accidentally [[Indecent exposure|exposing]] himself and [[Masturbation|masturbating]] while on a [[Zoom (software)|Zoom]] video call.<ref name=expo/><ref name=":0" />

==Early life and education==
Toobin was born to a [[American Jews|Jewish]] family<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sher |first=Cindy |title= Interview with CNN's Jeffrey Toobin, one of three best-selling authors to headline JUF Trade Dinner season |publisher=[[Jewish United Fund|Jewish United Fund of Metropolitan Chicago]]|date=May 2, 2017 |url= https://www.juf.org/news/local.aspx?id=442290 |quote=Toobin, who is Jewish, is a CNN Senior Analyst, a judicial expert, a staff writer for ''The New Yorker'', and a bestselling author.}}</ref> in [[New York City]] in 1960,<ref>{{cite web|last=Clehane|first=Diane |url=http://www.mediabistro.com/articles/cache/a9909.asp |title=So What Do You Do, Jeffrey Toobin, Author? |website=[[Mediabistro (website)|Mediabistro]]|date=October 10, 2007 |access-date=July 11, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071012094947/http://www.mediabistro.com/articles/cache/a9909.asp|archive-date=2007-10-12}}</ref> the son of [[Marlene Sanders]], former [[ABC News]] and [[CBS News]] correspondent, and [[Jerome Toobin]], a [[news broadcasting]] producer.<ref name="Jewish Ledger">{{cite news|last=Mindell|first=Cindy |url=http://www.jewishledger.com/2010/08/q-a-with-marlene-sanders/ |title=Q & A with... Marlene Sanders|work=[[Jewish Ledger]]|date=August 20, 2010|access-date=2020-10-23}}</ref>

Toobin attended [[Columbia Grammar & Preparatory School]], and then [[Harvard College]] for undergraduate studies. He covered sports for ''[[The Harvard Crimson]]'',<ref name="Jacobs">{{Cite news|last=Jacobs|first=Samuel P.|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2007/6/4/jeffrey-r-toobin-as-an-undergraduate/|title=Jeffrey R. Toobin |work=[[The Harvard Crimson]]|date=June 4, 2007|access-date=February 10, 2017}}</ref> where his column was titled "Inner Toobin". Toobin graduated ''[[Latin honors#Types|magna cum laude]]'' with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in American History and Literature, and was awarded a [[Harry S. Truman Scholarship]]. He then attended [[Harvard Law School]], where he was classmates with [[Elena Kagan]] and graduated ''magna cum laude'' with a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] in 1986. While there, he was an editor of the ''[[Harvard Law Review]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://today.law.harvard.edu/author-and-legal-analyst-jeffrey-toobin-86-named-this-years-class-day-speaker/|title=Author and legal analyst Jeffrey Toobin '86 named this year's Class Day speaker |work=Harvard Law Today|date=May 23, 2013|access-date=February 10, 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Jeffrey toobin 2007.jpg|thumb|Toobin promoting his book ''The Nine: Inside the Secret World of the Supreme Court'' at the 2007 [[Texas Book Festival]]]] 
Toobin began freelancing for ''[[The New Republic]]'' while a law student. After passing the bar, he worked as a law clerk to a federal judge and then as an associate counsel for [[United States Department of Justice Office of Special Counsel|Independent Counsel]] [[Lawrence Walsh]] during the [[Iran–Contra affair]] and [[Oliver North]]'s criminal trial. He next served as an Assistant [[United States Attorney for the Eastern District of New York]] in Brooklyn.<ref name=CommentaryMag>{{cite magazine|last=Eastland|first=Terry |url=http://www.commentarymagazine.com/viewarticle.cfm/opening-arguments--by-jeffrey-toobin-7838 |title=Above the Constitution? |magazine=[[Commentary (magazine)|Commentary]] |date=May 1991 |volume=91 |number=5 |pages=60–62|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130119161725/http://www.commentarymagazine.com/viewarticle.cfm/opening-arguments--by-jeffrey-toobin-7838 |url-status=dead |archive-date=January 19, 2013 |access-date=August 7, 2010}}</ref>

Toobin wrote a book about his work in the Office of Independent Counsel, to which Walsh objected. Toobin went to court to affirm his right to publish. Judge [[John F. Keenan]] of the [[United States District Court for the Southern District of New York]] wrote an opinion that Toobin and his publisher had the right to release this book. Walsh's appeal of the case was dismissed by the court.<ref>{{cite court | litigants=Penguin Books USA, Inc.; Jeffrey R. Toobin v. Lawrence E. Walsh; Office of Independent Counsel | vol=929 | reporter=F.2d | opinion=69 | court=United States Court of Appeals, Second Circuit. | date=March 1991 | url=https://scholar.google.com/scholar_case?case=6604426050094990825&hl=en&as_sdt=6&as_vis=1&oi=scholarr|title=Penguin Books USA, Inc. v. Walsh, 929 F. 2d 69 - Court of Appeals, 2nd Circuit 1991 | via=Google Scholar | accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref>

After three years as an Assistant U.S. Attorney, Toobin resigned from the [[United States Attorney for the Eastern District of New York|U.S. Attorney's office in Brooklyn]], where he had gone to work after working for Walsh, and abandoned "the practice of law."{{Citation needed|date=April 2019}} He started working in 1993 at ''[[The New Yorker]]'' and became a television legal analyst for [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] in 1996.

Toobin has provided broadcast legal analysis on many high-profile cases. In 1994, Toobin broke the story in ''The New Yorker'' that the legal team in [[O. J. Simpson]]'s criminal trial planned to accuse [[Mark Fuhrman]] of planting evidence.<ref name="CNNBio" /> Toobin provided analysis of [[Michael Jackson]]'s [[trial of Michael Jackson|2005 child molestation trial]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2004/LAW/01/16/otsc.toobin/|title=Toobin: Jackson courtroom 'like nothing I've ever seen' |date=January 16, 2004 |publisher=CNN|access-date=September 19, 2016}}</ref> the [[O. J. Simpson murder case#Civil trial|O. J. Simpson civil case]], and prosecutor [[Kenneth Starr]]'s investigation of President [[Bill Clinton]]. He received a 2000 [[News & Documentary Emmy Award|Emmy Award]] for his coverage of the [[Elián González]] custody saga.

Toobin joined [[CNN]] in 2002;<ref name="CNNBio" /> he is now the chief legal analyst. In 2003, he secured the first interview with [[Martha Stewart]] about the [[insider trading]] charges against her.<ref name="ContribNY">{{cite web|title=Contributors: Jeffrey Toobin|url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/bios/jeffrey_toobin/search?contributorName=jeffrey%20toobin|work=[[The New Yorker]]|access-date=August 6, 2010}}</ref>

[[File:Jeffrey Toobin by Gage Skidmore 2.jpg|right|thumb|Toobin speaking about the Supreme Court at the John J. Rhodes Lecture in Tempe, Arizona]]
Toobin is the author of seven books. His book ''[[The Nine (book)|The Nine: Inside the Secret World of the Supreme Court]]'' (2007) received awards from the [[Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism]] and the [[Nieman Foundation for Journalism]] at [[Harvard University]].<ref name="CNNBio">{{cite news |url=http://www.cnn.com/CNN/anchors_reporters/toobin.jeffrey.html |title=Anchors & Reporters – Jeffrey Toobin |access-date=August 6, 2010 |publisher=[[CNN]]}}</ref>

His next book, ''[[The Oath: The Obama White House and the Supreme Court]]'', was published in 2012. ''American Heiress: The Kidnapping, Crimes and Trial of [[Patty Hearst]]'' came out in 2016. All were [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''New York Times'' Best Sellers]]. In 2020, he authored ''True Crimes and Misdemeanors, the Investigation of Donald Trump'', which is described as a condensation of evidence against the character and presidency of [[Donald Trump]] as if he were on trial.<ref>{{cite web|first=Ron|last=Elving|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/07/31/897055924/in-true-crimes-toobin-presents-a-summation-for-the-jury-in-the-case-against-trum|title=In 'True Crimes,' Toobin Presents A Summation For The Jury In The Case Against Trump|website=[[NPR]]|date=July 31, 2020|access-date=2020-10-22}}</ref>

On October 19, 2020, Toobin was suspended from ''The New Yorker'' after he [[Indecent exposure|exposed]] himself and [[Masturbation|masturbated]] during an election scenario exercise conducted via [[Zoom (software)|Zoom]] video call between ''New Yorker'' and [[WNYC]] radio staffers.<ref name=expo>{{Cite news|date=October 20, 2020|title=Jeffrey Toobin suspended by The New Yorker and is temporarily stepping away from CNN following report he exposed himself on Zoom|work=[[CBS News]]|agency=[[Associated Press]]|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/jeffrey-toobin-new-yorker-reporter-suspended-the-new-yorker-leave-cnn-exposed-zoom/|access-date=2020-10-22}}</ref> CNN said Toobin "has asked for some time off while he deals with a personal issue, which we have granted." Toobin, who contends the incident was unintentional, issued a statement: "I made an embarrassingly stupid mistake, believing I was off-camera. I apologize to my wife, family, friends and co-workers."<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Diaz|first1=Johnny|author-link1=Johnny Diaz|last2=Paybarah|first2=Azi|author-link2=Azi Paybarah|date=October 19, 2020|title=New Yorker Suspends Jeffrey Toobin After Zoom Incident|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/19/business/media/jeffrey-toobin-new-yorker-suspended.html|access-date=2020-10-20}}</ref> The next month, he was fired from ''The New Yorker'', after an internal investigation by the parent organization, [[Condé Nast]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |last=Robertson |first=Katie |date=November 11, 2020 |title=Jeffrey Toobin Is Fired by The New Yorker |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/11/11/business/media/jeffrey-toobin-fired-new-yorker.html |access-date=November 11, 2020 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

==Personal life ==
[[File:Jeffrey Toobin (33025184302).jpg|thumb|right|Toobin in 2017]]
In 1986, Toobin married Amy Bennett McIntosh, whom he met in college while they worked at ''The Harvard Crimson''. She is a 1980 Harvard graduate, holds an [[Master of Business Administration|MBA]] degree from [[Harvard Business School]],<ref name="NYTimes1986" /><ref name="Jacobs" /> and has held executive positions at [[Verizon Communications]] and [[Zagat|Zagat Survey]].<ref name="Rush">{{cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/cnn-legal-eagle-jeffrey-toobin-baby-mama-drama-daughter-cbs-news-jeff-greenfield-article-1.194924|date=February 17, 2010|access-date=February 23, 2014|first=George|last=Rush|title=CNN legal eagle Jeffrey Toobin in baby mama drama – with daughter of CBS News' Jeff Greenfield|work=[[New York Daily News]]}}</ref> They have two adult children, a daughter and son.<ref name="Rush" />

Toobin had a longtime off-and-on extramarital affair with attorney Casey Greenfield,<ref name="NYDailyNews">{{Cite news |url=https://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/baby-drama-cnn-star-jeffrey-toobin-offered-casey-greenfield-money-abortion-sources-article-1.446944|title=Baby drama! CNN star Jeffrey Toobin offered Casey Greenfield money for abortion: sources|work=New York Daily News|date=May 8, 2010|access-date=2020-10-22}}</ref> who is the daughter of American television journalist and author [[Jeff Greenfield]] and the ex-wife of screenwriter [[Matt Manfredi]].<ref name="NYTimes2004">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2004/11/21/fashion/weddings/21CGRE.html|work=The New York Times|title=Casey Greenfield, Matt Manfredi|date=November 21, 2004|access-date=2020-10-22}}</ref> They had a child in 2009, which Toobin initially resisted acknowledging.<ref name="Rush" /><ref>{{cite news|first=Robin|last=Finn|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/02/19/nyregion/in-casey-greenfields-personal-custody-fight-the-makings-of-a-public-expert.html|work=The New York Times|title=Casey Greenfield v. the World|date=February 17, 2012|access-date=2020-10-22}}</ref> Ultimately, Toobin's paternity was confirmed with a [[DNA paternity testing|DNA test]] and a Manhattan Family Court judge ordered Toobin to pay child support.<ref name="NYDailyNews" />
