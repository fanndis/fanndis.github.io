'''Amazon Studios''' is an American television and film producer and distributor that is a subsidiary of [[Amazon (company)|Amazon]]. It specializes in developing television series and distributing and producing films. It was started in late 2010.<ref name=fritz>{{cite news |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/cotown/la-et-ct-amazon-studios-comic-20120912,0,5127701.story |title=Amazon Studios going into comics |author=Fritz, Ben  |date=September 12, 2012 |work=[[Los Angeles Times]] |accessdate=September 12, 2012}}</ref> Content is distributed through theaters and [[Prime Video]], Amazon's digital video streaming service, and is a competitor to services like [[Netflix]] and [[Disney+]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://techcrunch.com/2012/05/02/amazon-studios-now-funding-original-content-series-for-amazon-instant-video-service/ |title=Amazon Studios Now Funding Original Content Series For Amazon Video Service |author=Perez, Sarah  |date=May 2, 2012 |website=[[TechCrunch]] |accessdate=May 2, 2012}}</ref>

==Overview==
Scripts for television and films used to be submitted online to Amazon<ref>{{cite web|website=Studios.Amazon.com|url=https://studios.amazon.com/help/faq|title=FAQ}}</ref> and read by staff; however, the website states they no longer accept submissions. Amazon aimed to review submitted scripts within 90 days (although the process may be longer). If a project was chosen for development, the writer was paid $10,000.<ref name=moyer/> If a developed script was selected for distribution as a full-budget movie, the creator was paid $200,000; if it was selected for distribution as a full-budget series, the creator was paid $55,000 as well as "up to 5 percent of Amazon's net receipts from toy and t-shirt licensing, and other royalties and bonuses."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cinemablend.com/television/Amazon-Studios-Invites-TV-Writers-Submit-Comedy-Children-Series-Ideas-42088.html |title=Amazon Studios Invites TV Writers To Submit Comedy And Children's Series Ideas |author=West, Kelly  |date=May 2, 2012 |website=Cinema Blend |accessdate=May 2, 2012}}</ref>

In 2008, Amazon expanded into [[filmmaking|film production]], producing the film ''The Stolen Child'' with [[20th Century Fox]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117981288.html?categoryid=13&cs=1 |title=Amazon, Fox nursing 'Stolen ' |author=Graser, Marc  |date=February 21, 2008 |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |accessdate=February 21, 2008}}</ref> In July 2015, Amazon announced it had acquired [[Spike Lee]]'s new film, ''[[Chi-Raq]]'', as its first Amazon Original Movie.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.denverpost.com/ci_28487111/amazon-studios-acquiring-spike-lee-film-its-1st |title=Amazon Studios acquiring Spike Lee film as its 1st release |agency=[[Associated Press|The Associated Press]] |date=July 15, 2015 |work=The Denver Post|accessdate=July 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/jul/16/spike-lee-chiraq-amazon-first-original-movie|title=Spike Lee's Chiraq gets Amazon release – and Oscars push|last=Barnes|first=Henry|date=2015-07-16|website=The Guardian|language=en|access-date=2018-02-27}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2015/07/16/amazon-movie-chiraq-spike-lee-directs.html|title=Spike Lee directs Amazon's first ever movie|last=Kharpal|first=Arjun|date=2015-07-16|work=CNBC|access-date=2018-02-27}}</ref>

Amazon Studio has also released its first and only comic book series, ''Blackburn Burrow'' in 2012 as a free download.<ref name=fritz/> It contained a survey allowing Amazon to collect feedback to determine whether or not it was worthwhile to make the comic into a film.<ref name=fritz/>

Amazon Studios had received more than 10,000 feature screenplay submissions as of September 2012<ref name=fritz/> and 2,700 television pilots as of March 2013;<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wired.com/underwire/2013/03/nielsen-family-is-dead/ |title=The Nielsen Family Is Dead |author= Vanderbilt, Tom|date=March 28, 2013 |website=[[Wired (website)|Wired]] |accessdate=March 28, 2013}}</ref> 23 films and 26 television series were in active development as of March 2013.<ref name=fritz/><ref name=moyer>{{cite web |url=http://news.cnet.com/8301-1023_3-57459387-93/amazons-studios-effort-picks-first-tv-shows-to-develop/ |title=Amazon's 'Studios' effort picks first TV shows to develop |author=Moyer, Edward  |date=June 23, 2012 |website=[[CNET]] |accessdate=June 23, 2012}}</ref> In late 2016, it reorganized its film division into Prime Movies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/10/amazon-studios-joe-lewis-adds-drama-morgan-wandell-international-productions-1201837722|title=Amazon Studios Comedy Chief Joe Lewis Adds Drama Oversight, Morgan Wandell To Head International Productions|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva|date=17 October 2016|website=Deadline}}</ref>

On July 27, 2017, it was announced that, starting with the December 2017 release ''[[Wonder Wheel (film)|Wonder Wheel]]'', Amazon Studios would be its own self-distributing company. Previously, Amazon Studios had relied on multiple external studios to distribute their projects.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/markets-festivals/amazon-self-distribution-woody-allen-wonder-wheel-1202508413/amp/|title=Amazon Moves Into Self-Distribution With Woody Allen's 'Wonder Wheel' (EXCLUSIVE)|date=27 July 2017|work=Variety|accessdate=29 September 2017}}</ref> The company also acquired global TV rights to ''[[The Lord of the Rings]]'' for $250 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.theguardian.com/media/2017/nov/21/amazon-lord-of-the-rings-tv-netflix-disney-apple|title=Amazon's $1bn bet on Lord of the Rings shows scale of its TV ambition|date=November 21, 2017|website=The Guardian}}</ref> However, Amazon still has external distribution clients outside of the United States, such as [[Elevation Pictures]] in Canada, as well as [[Warner Bros.]] and [[StudioCanal]] in the UK and France.

In April 2018, Amazon Studios announced that they will no longer accept open submissions of screenplays.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2018/digital/news/amazon-studios-shuts-down-open-script-submissions-1202753480/|title=Amazon Studios Shuts Down Open Script-Submission Program|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|first=Todd|last=Spangler|date=April 14, 2018|accessdate=November 8, 2018}}</ref> In September 2020, Amazon signed an overall deal with Francesa Sloane.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=2020-09-23|title=Writer-Producer Francesca Sloane Inks Overall Deal With Amazon Studios; Sets First Project With Legendary Based On Works Of Anaïs Nin|url=https://deadline.com/2020/09/writer-producer-francesca-sloane-inks-overall-deal-with-amazon-studios-sets-first-project-with-legendary-based-on-works-of-anais-nin-1234582813/|access-date=2020-09-23|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Accolades===
In 2015, ''[[Transparent (TV series)|Transparent]]'' was the first show produced by Amazon Studios to win a major award and the first show produced by a [[streaming media]] service to win the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Television Series – Musical or Comedy]] (aka "Golden Globe for Best Series").<ref name='xbrlus_1'>{{cite web|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000101872415000004/amzn-20141231xex991.htm|title=Amazon.com Announces Fourth Quarter Sales Up 15% to $29.33 Billion|website=United States Securities and Exchange Commission|format=XBRL|date=January 29, 2015}}</ref> In 2017, for ''[[Manchester by the Sea (film)|Manchester by the Sea]]'', Amazon Studios became the first streaming media service to be nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Picture]];<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2017/01/oscar-nominations-2017-the-snubs-and-surprises.html|title=The Snubs and Surprises of the 2017 Oscar Nominations|date=24 January 2017|website=Vulture}}</ref> the film was nominated for a total of six [[Academy Awards]], winning two: [[Academy Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]] for [[Casey Affleck]] and [[Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay|Best Original Screenplay]] for [[Kenneth Lonergan]]. The film ''[[The Salesman (2016 film)|The Salesman]]'' (2016) won the [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film]]; it was directed by [[Asghar Farhadi]] and distributed in the US by Amazon Studios.<ref name="AcquiresSalesman">{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/05/amazon-acquires-asghar-farhadi-the-salesman-cohen-media-group-cannes-1201758704/|title=Amazon Acquires Asghar Farhadi's 'The Salesman', Partnering With Cohen Media Group On Domestic – Cannes|website=Deadline Hollywood|first1=Ali|last1=Jaafar|first2=Mike Jr.|last2=Fleming|date=May 18, 2016|accessdate=August 10, 2019}}</ref> In 2018, the [[historical drama|period]] [[comedy-drama]] television series, ''[[The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel]]'', starring [[Rachel Brosnahan]] won two [[Golden Globe Award]]s ([[Golden Globe Award for Best Television Series – Musical or Comedy|Best Television Series – Musical or Comedy]] and [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy|Best Actress – Musical or Comedy]] for Brosnahan) and five [[Primetime Emmy Awards]], including [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series|Outstanding Comedy Series]] and [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series|Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series]] for Brosnahan.
