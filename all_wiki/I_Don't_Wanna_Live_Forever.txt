"'''I Don't Wanna Live Forever'''", alternatively titled "'''I Don't Wanna Live Forever (Fifty Shades Darker)'''", is a song recorded by English singer [[Zayn Malik|Zayn]] and American singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]] for the [[Fifty Shades Darker: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack|soundtrack]] to the 2017 film ''[[Fifty Shades Darker (film)|Fifty Shades Darker]]''. It was written by Swift, [[Sam Dew]], and its producer [[Jack Antonoff]]. The single was released on December 9, 2016 by [[Republic Records]] to international commercial success. "I Don't Wanna Live Forever" is an [[Electro (music)|electro]]-[[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] [[ballad]] that sees Zayn and Swift exchanging romantic overtures. Its accompanying music video was released on January 27, 2017.

Upon release, "I Don't Wanna Live Forever" received positive reviews from [[music critics]], who praised the duo's vocal performances. Debuting at number six on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], the song eventually rose to its peak of number two, becoming Zayn's second top-five hit and Swift's eleventh on the chart, and the highest-peaking song from the ''Fifty Shades'' franchise. It topped the charts in Sweden and Spain, while reaching the top-five region in several other countries. In the US, the song became one of the top-ten best selling singles of 2016. As of June 2018, it is certified [[RIAA certification|4× Platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] for selling over 4 million units in the country. "I Don't Wanna Live Forever" is the [[List of best-selling albums in China#Digital Single/EP (4 tracks or less)|best-selling western single in Chinese history]]. The song is certified Diamond in Brazil and France, and has received several platinum and multi-platinum certifications globally.

At the [[60th Annual Grammy Awards]], "I Don't Wanna Live Forever" received a nomination for [[Best song written for visual media|Best Song Written for Visual Media]]. It won the [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Collaboration|Best Collaboration]] at the [[2017 MTV Video Music Awards]], Best Collaboration of the Year at the [[2017 MTV Millennial Awards]] and an [[IHeartRadio]] Titanium Award for amassing one billion spins.

== Composition ==
"I Don't Wanna Live Forever" is an [[Electro (music)|electro]]-[[Contemporary R&B|R&B]]<ref name="ew">{{Cite news|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/12/09/taylor-swift-zayn-i-dont-wanna-live-forever-ew-review|title=Zayn & Taylor Swift's 'I Don't Wanna Live Forever': EW Review|newspaper=Entertainment Weekly|access-date=December 10, 2016}}</ref> ballad.<ref name="rs">{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/zayn-taylor-swift-team-for-sultry-fifty-shades-darker-song-w454824|title=Taylor Swift, Zayn Team Up for Sultry 'Fifty Shades Darker' Song|newspaper=Rolling Stone|access-date=December 10, 2016}}</ref> On the track, the pair trade "romantic overtures", while Zayn uses a [[falsetto]] on parts of the song, and Swift ponders what the relationship means.<ref name="rs"/>

"I Don't Wanna Live Forever" is written in the key of [[A minor]] and moves at a [[tempo]] of 118 beats per minute in [[Time signature|common time]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://songbpm.com/zayn/i-don't-wanna|title=BPM for 'i don't wanna' by zayn {{!}} songbpm.com|publisher=songbpm.com|access-date=2016-12-23}}</ref>  The song follows a chord progression of F–C–G–Am–F–C–G–G{{Sharp}}{{Small|dim}}–Am, and the vocals span two octaves, from E<sub>3</sub> to E<sub>5</sub>.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.musicnotes.com/sheetmusic/mtd.asp?ppn=MN0170410|title=ZAYN & Taylor Swift "I Don't Wanna Live Forever" Sheet Music in A Minor (transposable) - Download & Print|last=Swift|first=Taylor|publisher=Musicnotes.com|access-date=2016-12-23}}</ref>

== Critical reception ==
Nolan Feenery of ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' gave it a B+ rating: "Welcome to Taylor Swift's Sexy Side." He also praised the vocals of both artists.<ref name="ew"/> Lucas Villa of ''[[AXS (ticket merchant)|AXS]]'' thought the song was not as sexy as [[Ellie Goulding]]'s "[[Love Me like You Do]]" or [[The Weeknd]]'s "[[Earned It]]", writing, "Despite the neutered lyricism, Zayn saves the day and manages to maneuver his most magnificent performance to date."<ref name=axs>{{cite web|last1=Villa|first1=Lucas|title=Review: Zayn saves tame Taylor Swift collaboration 'I Don't Wanna Live Forever'|url=http://www.axs.com/review-zayn-saves-tame-taylor-swift-collaboration-i-don-t-wanna-live-f-111439|website=AXS|date=December 11, 2016}}</ref>

== Commercial performance ==
The song upon release was available only on [[iTunes]] and [[Tidal (service)|Tidal]]. It was not serviced on [[Spotify]] upon launch but was later added the following week. In the United States, it sold 188,000 in its first week, in addition to a 25 million radio audience and 3 million streams. It debuted at number six on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7625602/hot-100-rae-sremmurd-sixth-week-zayn-taylor-swift-j-cole|title=Rae Sremmurd Tops Hot 100, Zayn & Taylor Swift, J. Cole Debut in Top 10|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=December 21, 2016}}</ref> It is Zayn's second top 10 solo single and Taylor Swift's 20th top 10, making her the 16th artist and sixth woman to achieve this.<ref name="hot100">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7625602/hot-100-rae-sremmurd-sixth-week-zayn-taylor-swift-j-cole |title=Rae Sremmurd Tops Hot 100, Zayn & Taylor Swift, J. Cole Debut in Top 10 |date=December 19, 2016 |last=Trust |first=Gary |work=Billboard |access-date=January 7, 2017}}</ref> It debuted at number one on the [[Digital Songs|''Billboard'' Digital Song Sales]] chart, as Zayn's fourth song (second solo song) and Swift's eleventh song to top the chart, tying Swift with [[Katy Perry]] for the second-most in the chart's history, behind [[Rihanna]].<ref name="hot100"/> It is also Zayn's second number-one on the Digital Songs chart in 2016, making him one of only two artists to top the chart with two songs that year, along with [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/music/chart-watch-j-cole-is-bigger-than-you-think-221351031.html|title=Chart Watch: J. Cole 'Eyez' the Top Spot}}</ref> The song later reached a new peak of number two on the Hot 100, but was held off the top spot by [[Ed Sheeran]]'s "[[Shape of You]]" with 109 million in radio audience<ref name="no2"/> and 25.7 million streams,<ref name="Hot 100 3">{{cite No. 3}}</ref> becoming Zayn's second top-five single (as solo artist), Swift's 11th top-five single,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7670081/hot-100-migos-bad-and-boujee-alessia-cara|title=Migos Hold Atop Hot 100, Alessia Cara Hits Top 10}}</ref> and the highest-charting ''Fifty Shades'' single.<ref name="no2"/>  It reached over a million in sales by July 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7857070/nielsen-midyear-2017-charts-kendrick-lamar-ed-sheeran|title=Kendrick Lamar's 'DAMN.' & Ed Sheeran's 'Shape of You' Lead Nielsen States.<ref name=us_sales>{{cite web|url = https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8046939/taylor-swift-career-album-song-sales-ask-billboard| title=Ask Billboard: Taylor Swift's Career copies in that year.<ref name="us sales">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8085932/ed-sheeran-divide-nielsen-music-top-album-2017|title=Ed Sheeran's 'Divide' Is Across European charts, the song debuted at number one in Spain and Sweden, becoming Swift's first number-one single in both countries, while becoming Zayn's first number-one in Spain and his second number-one in Sweden. It was certified platinum there. It reached the top 10 in Austria, Belgium, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Scotland, Switzerland, and the United Kingdom.

The song also saw much commercial success in China. Upon release, it sold over 1.56 million units and became the [[List of best-selling albums in China|best-selling western digital single in Chinese history]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=I Don't Wanna Live Forever (Fifty Shades Darker)|url=http://y.saoju.net/szzj/album/117/|access-date=2020-07-05|website=y.saoju.net}}</ref> It also became the second best-selling international single overall, after [[Big Bang (band)|Big Bang]]’s "[[Flower Road]]", and the best-selling international single by a female lead artist.

==Music video==
The official music video of the song was released on January 27, 2017 through both Zayn and Taylor Swift's official [[YouTube]] account at the same time, and was later uploaded through the official Fifty Shades [[Vevo]] account as well. It was directed by Grant Singer and was produced by Saul Germaine. The music video uploaded on Malik's Vevo channel has more than 110 million views on YouTube as of January 2020. The music video uploaded on Swift's Vevo channel has more than 575 million views on YouTube as of January 2020.<ref>{{YouTube|7F37r50VUTQ|ZAYN, Taylor Swift - I Don’t Wanna Live Forever (TaylorSwiftVEVO)}}</ref> It was named the fourth best music video of 2017 by [[Vevo]].

===Synopsis===
The video starts with Malik emerging from his car on a rainy night in [[London]] at the [[St. Pancras Renaissance London Hotel]] as the paparazzi take photos. As he enters the hotel, he starts singing his part,  and as the chorus begins he reaches the elevator with red lights, and then we pan over to another elevator with Swift singing her part and proceed to her hotel room. Malik in his room sings the chorus and Swift pours some champagne in a glass. The rest of the video is Malik and Swift throwing objects like glasses, lamps and pillows across their rooms in anger with flashing blue and red lights. The pair are seen standing together,  facing backs and singing the chorus together (Malik singing the chorus and Swift lip-syncing) and looking at each other. The video ends with Swift on her bed singing the last part of the song.<ref name="youtube">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AY9blLYMKnI|title=ZAYN, Taylor Swift - I Don't Wanna Live Forever (Fifty Shades Darker)|work=YouTube|date=January 26, 2017|access-date=February 9, 2017}}</ref>
