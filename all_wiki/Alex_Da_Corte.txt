'''Alex Da Corte''' (born 1980) is an American [[conceptual artist]] who works in painting, sculpture, installation, and video. Da Corte often uses surreal imagery and everyday objects in his practice and explores ideas of consumerism, pop culture, mythology, and literature.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newmuseum.org/exhibitions/view/alex-da-corte-harvest-moon|title=Alex Da Corte: Harvest Moon|website=www.newmuseum.org|language=en|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

He has shown internationally at Bodega, Gió Marconi, Josh Lilley Gallery, [[Maccarone art gallery|Maccarone]], [[Herning Museum of Contemporary Art]], [[Massachusetts Museum of Contemporary Art]], and [[Institute of Contemporary Art, Philadelphia|Institute of Contemporary Art]]. Da Corte has worked on a number of collaborative projects with other visual artists, writers, and musicians including [[Jayson Musson]], [[Dev Hynes]], [[Sam McKinniss|Sam Mckinniss]], and [[St. Vincent (musician)|Annie Clark]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/alex-da-corte-c-a-t-spells-murder-karma-r-l-stine-fear-street|title=Artist Alex Da Corte Gets Spooky at Karma|work=Vogue|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Da Corte was born in [[Camden, New Jersey]], in 1980. He spent his formative years growing up in Venezuela.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/129093028|title=Alex Da Corte: Dancing Around Delusion|publisher=|date=2015-05-28}}</ref> In 2001, he studied Film/Animation and Fine Arts at the [[School of Visual Arts]] in New York City and then went on to receive his BFA in Printmaking/Fine Arts, from [[The University of the Arts]] in Philadelphia in 2004. He graduated from [[Yale University]] with an MFA in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://frieze.com/project/alex-da-corte|title=Alex Da Corte|website=Frieze}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/art/alex-da-corte/|title=Alex Da Corte|website=Interview Magazine}}</ref>

== Work ==
Since 2013, Alex Da Corte has mounted shows from New York commercial galleries like Luxembourg & Dayan to international museums like The [[Louisiana Museum of Modern Art]] in Denmark. In 2014, Da Corte had a solo exhibition at the [[Jay Jopling#career|White Cube Gallery]] in London titled "White Rain".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://whitecube.com/exhibitions/alex_da_corte_inside_the_white_cube_2014/|title=Alex Da Corte Inside the White Cube 2014 – White Cube|first=White|last=Cube|publisher=}}</ref>

In 2015, Da Corte's solo show ''Die Hexe'' at Luxembourg & Dayan Gallery (NYC) was reviewed by art critic [[Roberta Smith]] of ''[[The New York Times]]''. In a piece on the exhibition she wrote of Da Corte's previous work, "last year's show dazzled at every turn, weaving confounding narratives about innocence and decadence, mass production and eccentricity..."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/04/03/arts/design/alex-da-corte-die-hexe.html|title=Alex Da Corte: 'Die Hexe'|date=3 April 2015|work=The New York Times}}</ref>

In 2016, Da Corte's opus was the subject of an exhibition entitled ''Free Roses'' at [[Mass MOCA]] in [[North Adams, Massachusetts]] which is the largest of his career so far.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://massmoca.org/event/alex-da-corte/|title=Upcoming Events Alex Da CorteFree Roses|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/28/arts/design/alex-da-cortes-free-roses-puts-his-eccentricities-on-view.html|title=Alex Da Corte's 'Free Roses' Puts His Eccentricities on View|date=28 March 2016|work=The New York Times}}</ref>

In the arena of [[hip hop]], Da Corte directed the 2013 video for the track "Hush BB" by the rapper [[Le1f]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/exclusive-video-premiere-hush-bb-le1f/|title=Exclusive Video Premiere: 'Hush Bb,' Le1f|publisher=}}</ref> He also contributed the cover art for the second studio album by the hip hop group [[Spank Rock]] 2013's "[[Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar]]".

In the recent past Da Corte has taken to taking on the persona of the rapper [[Eminem]] which was the crux of his solo exhibition "Bad Land" at the Josh Lilley gallery in London which ran from November 2017 until February 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/eminem-impersonator-alex-da-corte|title=An Eminem Impersonator Is Smoking Bongs on Loop at a Gallery in London|first=Stephanie|last=Eckardt|date=|website=W Magazine|accessdate=12 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://joshlilley.com/|title=Josh Lilley|author=|date=|website=joshlilley.com|accessdate=12 May 2019}}</ref>

Da Corte's work is included in the 2019 58th edition of the [[Venice Biennale]] "May You Live in Interesting Times" curated by [[Ralph Rugoff]] and appears in both main sections with ''57 Varieties'' in the arsenale and ''The Decorated Shed'' in the giardini.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.artnews.com/2019/05/08/venice-biennale-slideshow-giardini/#jp-carousel-108165 |title = Touring the Venice Biennale, Part 2: The Giardini|date = 2019-05-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.culturedmag.com/venice-biennale-artists/ |title = 10 Artists at the Venice Biennale We Know & Love|date = 2019-05-07}}</ref>

== Collaborations ==
Throughout Da Corte's career, he has collaborated with other artists, including Borna Sammak, Sean Fitzgerald, [[Jayson Musson]] and [[Dev Hynes]]. Da Corte's collaborative installation, ''Easternsports''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://artforum.com/video/mode=large&id=48290|title=Excerpt from Alex Da Corte and Jayson Musson's Easternsports, 2014|publisher=}}</ref> (2014)'','' is two and a half hours of atmospheric video on four channels, and a disjointed essay-poem of tens of thousands of words running through the subtitles.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.artinamericamagazine.com/reviews/alex-da-corte-and-jayson-musson/|title=Alex Da Corte and Jayson Musson – Reviews – Art in America|website=artinamericamagazine.com|access-date=2016-08-05|date=2015-02-19}}</ref> The work was created with Jayson Musson and scored by Dev Hynes. After the completion of ''Easternsports'' Da Corte and Hynes worked together to create a video for [[The Gap, Inc.|GAP]]'s "Play Your Stripes" campaign.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/113309790|title=The GAP: "Play Your Stripes" w/ Blood Orange (Dir. Alex DaCorte)|website=Vimeo|access-date=2016-08-05|date=December 2014}}</ref> Alex Da Corte's most recent collaboration opened on September 3, 2016 at the [[Herning Museum of Contemporary Art]] in Herning, Denmark. The exhibition titled, ''50 Wigs'' showcases original sculptures by Da Corte alongside a collection of objects from [[Andy Warhol]]'s personal estate. Working closely with the [[The Andy Warhol Museum|Andy Warhol Museum]] to bring the show to life, Da Corte "transforms Warhol's personal belongings to art objects"<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.heartmus.dk/besog/udstillinger/kommende-udstillinger/alex-da-corte.html|title=Alex Da Corte – 50 wigs – HEART – Museum of Contemporary Art|website=heartmus.dk|access-date=2016-09-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160821140400/http://www.heartmus.dk/besog/udstillinger/kommende-udstillinger/alex-da-corte.html|archive-date=2016-08-21|url-status=dead|df=}}</ref>

In 2017, Da Corte directed the music video for the musician [[St. Vincent (musician)|St. Vincent]]'s song "[[New York (St. Vincent song)|New York]]". In 2018, the duo reunited for a second pairing, whereby the artist/director employed the artist/musician in another video this time as part his exhibition "C-A-T Spells Murder" at the Karma gallery in New York City (February 18 – March 18, 2018); where in an 11-minute video Da Corte shows St. Vincent holding a one eyed feline while the performer makes a series of facial expressions ending in terror while this film looped was played within an instillation which also featured an eleven foot high sculpture of a cat.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/16/t-magazine/alex-da-corte-st-vincent.html|title=Basking in the Day-Glo of St. Vincent and Alex Da Corte|first=Janelle|last=Zara|date=16 February 2018|publisher=|accessdate=12 May 2019|newspaper=The New York Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://karmakarma.org/exhibitions/alex-da-corte-c-a-t-spells-murder/|title=Alex Da Corte: C-A-T Spells Murder|author=|date=|website=Karma|accessdate=12 May 2019}}</ref>
