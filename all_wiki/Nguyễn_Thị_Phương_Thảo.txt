'''Nguyễn Thị Phương Thảo''' (born June 7, 1970) is a Vietnamese businesswoman, and the President and CEO of [[VietJet Air]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2016/05/24/nguyen-thi-phuong-thao-takes-vietjet-from-bikini-flights-to-ipo-in-5-years.html|title=Nguyen Thi Phuong Thao takes VietJet from 'bikini flights' to IPO in 5 years|last=|first=|date=|website=Cnbc.com|language=Vietnamese|access-date=|accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref> 

==Biography==
Nguyễn Thị Phương Thảo met with early success while studying finance and economics in [[Moscow]], where she began distributing goods from Japan, Hong Kong and South Korea in the then [[Soviet Union]]. Since then she has developed a portfolio of business interests, including VietJet and Sovico Holdings, that itself holds a 90 per cent stake in [[Ho Chi Minh City]]'s Dragon City development.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-03-23/how-bikini-airline-helped-to-create-vietnam-s-first-woman-billionaire |title=How Bikini Airline Helped to Create Vietnam's First Woman Billionaire |publisher=Bloomberg |date=2016-03-23 |accessdate=2017-03-20}}</ref> She holds two bachelor's degrees in financial credit management and labor economics at the [[Plekhanov Russian University of Economics]] and a doctoral degree in economic management at the [[D. Mendeleev University of Chemical Technology of Russia]].

She ranked 62nd in the list of Power Women 2016.<ref>{{cite web|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/#tab:overall|website=Forbes.com|accessdate=8 June 2016}}</ref> She is listed in Asia's 50 Power Businesswomen 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbesasia/2016/04/06/asias-50-power-businesswomen-2016/#5726eac8f487|title=Asia's 50 Power Businesswomen 2016|website=Forbes.com|access-date=2016-07-30}}</ref>

She is Vietnam's first self-made woman billionaire, after she took her budget airline, VietJet Air, public in February 2017.<ref name="Forbes"/> She also has investments in bank and three resorts.<ref name="Forbes"/>
