'''Vance Elliott DeGeneres''' (born September 2, 1954)<ref name="Betty">{{cite book |last=DeGeneres |first=Betty |author-link=Betty DeGeneres |date=1999 |title=Love, Ellen: A Mother/Daughter Journey |url= |location=New York |publisher=Rob Weisbach Books |edition=1st |page=66, 70 |isbn=9780688162740 |oclc=40489091}}</ref><ref name="Almanac">{{cite book |editor-last=Mallegg |editor-first=Kristin |date=2011 |title=Almanac of Famous People |url= |volume=1 |edition=10th |location=Farmington Hills, Michigan |publisher=Gale/Cengage Learning |page= |isbn=9781414445489 |oclc=659753779 |quote=About this person: Born: 1954 in New Orleans, Louisiana, United States}}</ref> is an American actor, comedian, musician, film producer and screenwriter, known for his work in television and movies.

==Early life==
DeGeneres was born at [[Touro Infirmary]] in [[New Orleans]], [[Louisiana]] to [[Betty DeGeneres|Betty]] and Elliott DeGeneres.<ref name="Betty"/> He is the older brother of comedian [[Ellen DeGeneres]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.comedycentral.com/dailyshow/bio_degeneres.tin |title=The Daily Show with Jon Stewart: Vance DeGeneres, Correspondent |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=n.d. |website=Comedy Central |access-date=September 8, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/19990901022015/http://www.comedycentral.com/dailyshow/bio_degeneres.tin |archive-date=September 1, 1999 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
{{More citations needed section|date=September 2020}}
After a two-year stint in the [[Marine Corps]], during which he reached the rank of [[corporal]], DeGeneres hosted a radio program called ''New Wave New Orleans'' in the late 1970s, broadcast on [[WQUE-FM]].

He played bass guitar and was a songwriter in the popular band The Cold, a [[New wave music|new-wave]] band founded in New Orleans in 1979, and was inaugurated into the [[Louisiana Music Hall of Fame]] in 2018.

He originated the role of "Mr. Hands" in "The Mr. Bill Show."

In 1991, DeGeneres was a staff writer on ''Eerie, Indiana'', on NBC.

In 1993, DeGeneres co-created, with Karl Schaefer, a pilot script for ABC called ''Lost Angels''.

In 1994/95, DeGeneres wrote and produced ''9 Line''.

In 1995, he and Emily Cutler co-created and co-hosted ''Movies Schmoovies'', a movie review pilot.

In 1995, DeGeneres and Alex Herschlag co-created and co-hosted a comedy show called ''The 4th Floor Show'', a mock talk show.  The pilot episode aired on E!

In 1996/97, DeGeneres was a writer on ''Ellen''.

In 1998, DeGeneres was a writer on the UPN sitcom ''Hits''.

DeGeneres has been a writer on The Academy Awards, The Emmy Awards, and The Grammy Awards.

In the mid-1980s, DeGeneres co-founded the rock band House of Schock with [[The Go-Go's]] drummer [[Gina Schock]] on Capitol Records.

In 1998, he played keyboards and guitar for the [[New Orleans]]-based band [[Cowboy Mouth]]. In March 2006, he appeared on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'' with Cowboy Mouth to perform their song "The Avenue".

DeGeneres was a correspondent on ''[[The Daily Show with Jon Stewart]]'' from 1999 to 2001.<ref>{{cite news|last=Yahr|first=Emily|title=How 'Indecision 2000' changed everything for Jon Stewart and 'The Daily Show'|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/arts-and-entertainment/wp/2015/08/05/how-indecision-2000-changed-everything-for-jon-stewart-and-the-daily-show/|accessdate=31 July 2017|work=Washington Post|date=5 August 2015}}</ref>

In 2002, he co-created, with Andy Lassner at Fox, a comedy pilot called ''Your Local News''. DeGeneres also hosted.

DeGeneres was co-president of Carousel Productions at Warner Bros. for six years, [[Steve Carell]]'s production company, developing comedy movies, including ''Crazy Stupid Love'' and ''The Incredible Burt Wonderstone''.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Fleming|first=Michael |date=January 13, 2009 |title=Steve Carell's Carousel forms TV arm |url=https://variety.com/2009/scene/markets-festivals/steve-carell-s-carousel-forms-tv-arm-1117998469/ |magazine=Variety|access-date=June 27, 2019}}</ref> He was an executive producer on both films.

DeGeneres was an executive producer on the Showtime series ''Inside Comedy'', hosted by David Steinberg, for four seasons.

He currently has a television development deal at Telepictures Productions, a division of Warner Bros.{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}}

During the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States|coronavirus pandemic]] of 2020, DeGeneres and [[Rick Springfield]] created the online series, ''Rick Springfield & Vance DeGeneres Present the Miniseries: the 60 Second Guide to Songwriting with a Partner''. The comedic series resulted in two songs: "The Wall Will Fall" and "Welcome to Your Bright New World", with proceeds benefitting [[Feeding America]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Spera |first=Keith |date=May 2, 2020 |title=Vance DeGeneres, Rick Springfield's 'The Wall Will Fall': a goof that grew into a fundraiser |url=https://www.nola.com/entertainment_life/keith_spera/article_755561be-939f-11ea-a25a-8b055a708e39.html |work=The Times-Picayune/The New Orleans Advocate |location=New Orleans, Louisiana |access-date=July 21, 2020}}</ref>

He was an executive producer on ''Ellen's Game of Games'', which aired on NBC in 2020.
