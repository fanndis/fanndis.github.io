== Notes ==
* On June 16, 2017, American singer [[Katy Perry]] became the first to exceed 100&nbsp;million followers.<ref>{{cite web|last=Cirisano|first=Tatiana|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/katy-perry-becomes-first-hit-100-million-twitter-followers-1014285|title=Katy Perry Becomes First to Hit 100 Million Twitter Followers|date=June 16, 2017|accessdate=October 30, 2017|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]}}</ref>
* On July 12, 2018, Twitter purged locked accounts—those disabled due to suspicious activity—from follower-count tallies.<ref>{{cite web|last=Spangler|first=Todd|url=https://variety.com/2018/digital/news/twitter-locked-accounts-removed-followers-1202870341/|title=Twitter to Purge Locked Accounts From Follower Counts, Warns of Potential 'Significant Drop' in Followers|date=July 11, 2018|accessdate=August 27, 2018|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}}</ref> Consequently, Twitter's own account took the fastest and greatest plunge out of all the top 50 accounts, from 63 million to 55&nbsp;million followers, and in ranking, from 11th to 16th place.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Simani|first1=Ellis|last2=Schleuss|first2=Jon|url=http://www.latimes.com/projects/la-g-twitter-follower-purge/|title=Here are the biggest losers from Twitter's purge|date=July 13, 2018|accessdate=August 27, 2018|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref>
* On February 3, 2019, American singer [[Demi Lovato]] deleted her Twitter account after being involved in a controversy. At the time, she was ranked 14th and had over 57 million followers.<ref>{{cite web|last=Miller|first=Hayley|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.ca/entry/demi-lovato-twitter-21-savage_us_5c5848e9e4b00187b5531b27|title=Demi Lovato Deletes Twitter Account After Hateful Backlash Over 21 Savage Meme|date=February 4, 2019|accessdate=February 4, 2019|work=[[HuffPost]]}}</ref> On January 14, 2020, she had reactivated her account and started tweeting again under the new name, "Team Demi."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bleznak|first=Becca|date=2020-08-06|title=Demi Lovato Returns to Twitter Amid 7th Album Rumors, But She's Only Following 1 Person|url=https://www.cheatsheet.com/entertainment/demi-lovato-returns-twitter-seventh-album-rumors.html/|access-date=2020-10-23|website=Showbiz Cheat Sheet|language=en-US}}</ref> In July, 2020 her Twitter account was changed back to "@ddlovato".
