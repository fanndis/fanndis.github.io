The '''2014 Australian Open''' was a [[List of tennis tournaments|tennis tournament]] that took place at [[Melbourne Park]] between 13 and 26 January 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ticketliquidator.com/tix/australian-open-tickets.aspx|title=Australian Open Tickets|publisher=Ticketliquidator.com|accessdate=13 November 2013}}</ref> It was the 102nd edition of the [[Australian Open]], and the first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament of the year. The tournament consisted of events for professional players in singles, doubles and mixed doubles play. Junior and wheelchair players competed in singles and doubles tournaments.

[[Li Na]] won the women's singles, beating [[Dominika Cibulková]] in the final. [[Stan Wawrinka|Stanislas Wawrinka]] defeated [[Rafael Nadal]] in the men's singles final to win his first grand slam title. [[Sara Errani]] and [[Roberta Vinci]] defended their women's doubles title with a victory over [[Ekaterina Makarova]] and [[Elena Vesnina]]. [[Łukasz Kubot]] and [[Robert Lindstedt]] took the men's doubles title with a victory over [[Eric Butorac]] and [[Raven Klaasen]]. The mixed doubles were won by [[Kristina Mladenovic]] and [[Daniel Nestor]], with [[Sania Mirza]] and [[Horia Tecău]] the runners-up.

Both defending singles champions lost in the quarterfinals, the first time in the open era.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/25838267|title=Australian Open 2014: Defending champion Victoria Azarenka loses|author=|date=2 April 2018|publisher=|accessdate=2 April 2018|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> [[Novak Djokovic]] was the three-time defending champion in the men's singles, but failed to defend his title, losing to eventual champion Wawrinka. Two-time defending champion [[Victoria Azarenka]] also failed to defend her title in the women's singles, losing to [[Agnieszka Radwańska]]. In addition, the men's doubles defending champions [[The Bryan brothers|Bob & Mike Bryan]] also failed to defend their title, while Errani and Vinci managed to retain their title. As in previous years, this tournament's title sponsor was [[Kia Motors|Kia]].

==Tournament==
[[File:Rod Laver Arena (8984015851).jpg|thumb|left|Rod Laver Arena where the Finals of the Australian Open take place]]
The 2014 Australian Open was the 102nd edition of the tournament and was held at [[Melbourne Park]] in [[Melbourne]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]], Australia.

The tournament was run by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) and was part of the [[2014 ATP World Tour]] and the [[2014 WTA Tour]] calendars under the Grand Slam category. The tournament consisted of both men's and women's singles and doubles draws as well as a mixed doubles event. There were singles and doubles events for both boys and girls (players under 18), which is part of the Grade A category of tournaments, and also singles, doubles and quad events for men's and women's [[wheelchair tennis]] players as part of the NEC tour under the Grand Slam category.

The tournament was played on hard courts and took place over a series of 16 courts, including the three main showcourts: [[Rod Laver Arena]], [[Melbourne Multi Purpose Venue|Hisense Arena]] and [[Margaret Court Arena]]. The latter was undergoing refurbishment, as part of the Melbourne Park Redevelopment project.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com.au/news/2013/12/08/first-glimpse-of-new-look-margaret-court-arena|title=First Glimpse of new-look Margaret Court Arena|publisher=Tennis.com.au|accessdate=6 January 2014}}</ref>

==Broadcast==
In Australia, all matches were broadcast live by the [[Seven Network]]. The majority of matches were shown on the network's primary channel [[Channel 7 Australia|Channel Seven]], however during news programming nationwide and most night matches in [[Perth]], coverage shifted to [[7Two]]. Coverage was presented by [[Johanna Griggs]], [[Jim Wilson (sports journalist)|Jim Wilson]], [[Matthew White (journalist)|Matt White]], [[Hamish McLachlan]] and [[Basil Zempilas]], with commentary from [[Bruce McAvaney]], [[Jim Courier]], [[Samantha Smith (tennis)|Sam Smith]], [[Todd Woodbridge]], [[John Newcombe]], [[Rennae Stubbs]], [[Henri Leconte]] and [[John Fitzgerald (tennis)|John Fitzgerald]]. [[Lleyton Hewitt]], who was competing in the tournament, would become a commentator if he is knocked out.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvtonight.com.au/2014/01/australian-open-2014-guide.html|title=Australian Open 2014: guide|publisher=tvtonight|accessdate=11 January 2014}}</ref> Some outside court matches were shown on [[Fox Sports (Australia)|Fox Sports]] on [[Foxtel]].

====Women's Singles====
{{main|2014 Australian Open – Women's Singles}}

:<small>• It was Li's 2nd and last career Major title and her 1st and only title in Australian Open.</small>

====Men's Doubles====
{{main|2014 Australian Open – Men's Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|POL}} '''[[Łukasz Kubot]]''' / {{flagicon|SWE}} '''[[Robert Lindstedt]]''' defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Eric Butorac]] / {{flagicon|RSA}} [[Raven Klaasen]], 6–3, 6–3

====Women's Doubles====
{{main|2014 Australian Open – Women's Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Sara Errani]]''' / {{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Roberta Vinci]]''' defeated {{flagicon|RUS}} [[Ekaterina Makarova]] / {{flagicon|RUS}} [[Elena Vesnina]], 6–4, 3–6, 7–5
:<small>• It was Errani and Vinci's 4th career Major doubles title and their 2nd respective title in Australian Open.</small>

====Mixed Doubles====
{{main|2014 Australian Open – Mixed Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|FRA}} '''[[Kristina Mladenovic]]''' / {{flagicon|CAN}} '''[[Daniel Nestor]]''' defeated {{flagicon|IND}} [[Sania Mirza]] / {{flagicon|ROU}} [[Horia Tecău]], 6–3, 6–2
:<small>• It was Nestor's 8th career Major mixed doubles title and his 4th title in Australian Open.</small>
:<small>• It was Mladenovic's 3rd career Major mixed doubles title and her 1st title in Australian Open.</small>
