'''Robert Quentin Crane''' (March 21, 1926 – January 5, 2018) was an American politician who served as [[Treasurer and Receiver-General of Massachusetts]] from 1965–1991. He was credited with starting the [[Massachusetts Lottery]].<ref name=park>{{cite news |first= Brian R.|last= Ballou|title=Former treasurer honored with park|url=http://www.boston.com/news/local/massachusetts/articles/2008/10/22/former_treasurer_honored_with_park |quote=A who's who of local and state politicians turned out at Boston College yesterday morning to dedicate a small campus park to former state treasurer Robert Q. Crane, who served in that position for 27 years and is credited with bringing the lottery to the Commonwealth.|work=[[Boston Globe]]|date=October 22, 2008|accessdate=December 9, 2009 }}</ref>

==Early life==
Crane was born in 1926 in [[Providence, Rhode Island]]. He received his education at [[English High School of Boston]] and [[Boston College]]. Crane served in the [[United States Marine Corps]] during [[World War II]].<ref name=link>{{cite web|url=https://archive.org/stream/publicofficersof19891990bost/publicofficersof19891990bost_djvu.txt|title=Public officers of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts|accessdate=December 9, 2009|quote=Robert Quentin Crane; 7 Mountview Road, Wellesley, Democrat. Born: Providence, R.I., March 21, 1926. Education: Boston English High School; Boston College. Organizations: Veterans of Foreign Wars; American Legion; Knights of Columbus; Catholic Alumnae Sodality; Boston College Alumni. Public office: Mass. House 1957-64; Treasurer and Receiver General 1964 (to fill vacancy), 1965-90.|year=1989–90|publisher=[[Massachusetts House of Representatives]]}}</ref>

==Career==
Crane served in the [[Massachusetts House of Representatives]] from 1957-64. He served as the chairman of the Massachusetts Democratic Party. Crane was appointed state treasurer in 1964.<ref name=link/> He defeated [[Louise Day Hicks]], [[John Francis Kennedy (politician)|John Francis Kennedy]], and [[John J. Buckley]] for the Treasurer nomination in 1964.<ref name=park/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=557785|title=Our Campaigns - MA Treasurer - D Primary Race|date=September 10, 1964|website=ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=January 13, 2018}}</ref> He went on to easily defeat Republican [[Robert C. Hahn]] in the general election.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=558838|title=Our Campaigns - MA Treasurer Race|date=November 3, 1964|website=ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=January 13, 2018}}</ref>

In 1971, became the founding chairman of the Massachusetts State Lottery.<ref name=park/> The closest Crane came to losing his office was in 1974 when he defeated Charles Mark Furcolo, a Boston attorney and son of former governor [[Foster Furcolo|John Foster Furcolo]], 51%-49% in the Democratic primary.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=558134|title=Our Campaigns - MA Treasurer - D Primary Race|date=September 10, 1974|website=ourcampaigns.com|accessdate=January 13, 2018}}</ref> He retired from politics in 1991, amid accusations of patronage and nepotism.<ref name=park/>

==Death==
Crane died on January 5, 2018, aged 91.<ref name=death>{{cite news|title=Former state treasurer Robert Crane dies at 91|url=http://www.tauntongazette.com/news/20180106/former-state-treasurer-robert-crane-dies-at-91|work=[[Taunton Daily Gazette]]|date=January 7, 2018|accessdate=January 7, 2018}}</ref>
