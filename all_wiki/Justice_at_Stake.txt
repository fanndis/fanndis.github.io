'''Justice at Stake''' was a judicial advocacy organization active in the United States from 2000 to 2017. The group advocated for an end to judicial elections, and for stricter regulations regarding campaign finance for state-level judicial races.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Oliphant|first1=James|title=When Judges Go Courting|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/magazine/when-cash-turns-judges-into-politicians-20141017|accessdate=29 May 2015|publisher=National Journal|date=October 18, 2014}}</ref> [[George Soros]] was one of the organization's primary donors.<ref>{{cite news|title=Judges, Politics and George Soros|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424127887323415304578368351828159918|accessdate=29 May 2015|publisher=Wall Street Journal|date=March 31, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Pero|first1=Dan|title=George Soros vs. judicial elections|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2010/sep/10/george-soros-vs-judicial-elections/|accessdate=29 May 2015|publisher=Washington Times|date=September 10, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Newlin Carney|first1=Eliza|title=The Perils Of Big Money In Judicial Elections|url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/columns/rules-of-the-game/the-perils-of-big-money-in-judicial-elections-20101004|accessdate=29 May 2015|publisher=National Journal|date=October 4, 2010}}</ref>

The organization's stated mission was to "help Americans keep courts fair and impartial."<ref>{{cite web|title=Our Mission|url=http://www.justiceatstake.org/about/|publisher=Justice at Stake|accessdate=29 May 2015}}</ref>

==Background==
Founded in 2000, Justice at Stake was a [[501(c)|501(c)(3)]] organization governed by a [[board of directors]]. The chair of the board was Mark I. Harrison. The organization announced its closure on June 16, 2017.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Liss|first1=Susan|title=Justice at Stake Closing Its Doors After 16 Years|url=http://www.justiceatstake.org/newsroom/press-releases-16824/?justice_at_stake_closing_its_doors_after_16_years&show=news&newsID=23257|accessdate=19 June 2017|publisher=Justice at Stake|date=June 16, 2017}}</ref>

Justice at Stake advocated for judicial appointments rather than judicial elections. It also advocated for reforms such as public financing of judicial elections and stricter campaign finance regulations regarding state judicial races.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Johnson|first1=Carrie|title=Report: Too Much Money Going To State Court Races|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=129178835|accessdate=29 May 2015|publisher=NPR|date=August 16, 2010}}</ref>
