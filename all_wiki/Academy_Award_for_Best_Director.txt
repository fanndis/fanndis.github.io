The '''Academy Award for Best Director''' (officially known as the '''Academy Award of Merit for Directing''') is an award presented annually by the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS). It is given in honor of a [[film director]] who has exhibited outstanding directing while working in the [[film industry]].

The [[1st Academy Awards]] ceremony was held in 1929 with the award being split into "Dramatic" and "Comedy" categories; [[Frank Borzage]] and [[Lewis Milestone]] won for ''[[7th Heaven (1927 film)|7th Heaven]]'' and ''[[Two Arabian Knights]]'', respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmsite.org/aa27.html|title=1927–28 Academy Awards Winners|publisher=[[AMC Networks|Rainbow Media]]|last=Dirks|first=Tim|accessdate=November 20, 2015|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100724204258/http://www.filmsite.org/aa27.html|archivedate=July 24, 2010}}</ref> However, these categories were merged for all subsequent ceremonies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmsite.org/oscars30.html|title=Academy Awards Winners (1927/8 – 1939)|publisher=[[AMC Networks|Rainbow Media]]|last=Dirks|first=Tim|accessdate=November 20, 2015|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160114184753/http://www.filmsite.org/oscars30.html|archivedate=January 14, 2016}}</ref> Nominees are determined by [[single transferable vote]] within the directors branch of AMPAS; winners are selected by a [[plurality (voting)|plurality vote]] from the entire eligible voting members of the Academy.<ref>{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141021054540/http://www.oscars.org/sites/default/files/87aa_rules.pdf |archivedate=October 21, 2014|url=http://www.oscars.org/sites/default/files/87aa_rules.pdf|title=87th Annual Academy Awards of Merit Rules|publisher=Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences|accessdate=February 8, 2016 |pages=Rule 5.5 & Rule 10 |nopp=y}}</ref><ref name="TheEnvelope">
{{cite news|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061017160343/http://theenvelope.latimes.com/awards/oscars/env-oscarvote7jan07%2C0%2C6919642%2Cprint.story?coll=env-home-headlines |archivedate=17 October 2006 |url=http://theenvelope.latimes.com/awards/oscars/env-oscarvote7jan07,0,6919642,print.story?coll=env-home-headlines |title=Eight things every voter (and fan) should know about Oscar's decidedly unique nomination process. |first=Steve |last=Pond |accessdate=2007-01-27 |date=January 7, 2006 |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ew.com/article/2011/01/27/oscar-voter-ballot-reader-poll-results |title=Oscars: The wacky way the Academy counts votes, and the results of our 'If You Were an Oscar Voter' poll |last=Young |first=John |date=27 January 2011 |work=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |accessdate=29 February 2016 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160506042043/http://www.ew.com/article/2011/01/27/oscar-voter-ballot-reader-poll-results |archivedate=6 May 2016}}</ref>

For the first eleven years of the Academy Awards, directors were allowed to be nominated for multiple films in the same year. However, after the nomination of [[Michael Curtiz]] for two films, ''[[Angels with Dirty Faces]]'' and ''[[Four Daughters]]'', at the [[11th Academy Awards]], the rules were revised so that an individual could only be nominated for one film at each ceremony.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.filmsite.org/aa38.html|title=1938 Academy Awards Winners and History|publisher=[[AMC Networks|Rainbow Media]]|last=Dirks|first=Tim|accessdate=November 20, 2015|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151121060632/http://www.filmsite.org/aa38.html|archivedate=November 21, 2015}}</ref> That rule has since been amended, although the only director who has received multiple nominations in the same year was [[Steven Soderbergh]] for ''[[Erin Brockovich (film)|Erin Brockovich]]'' and ''[[Traffic (2000 film)|Traffic]]'' in 2000, winning the award for the latter. The Academy Awards for Best Director and [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]] have been very closely linked throughout their history. Of the 92 films that have been awarded Best Picture, 66 have also been awarded Best Director.<ref name="awardsdatabase.oscars.org">{{cite web|url=http://awardsdatabase.oscars.org/ampas_awards/help/statistics/bestpixdirdiff.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120113073946/http://awardsdatabase.oscars.org/ampas_awards/help/statistics/bestpixdirdiff.html|title=Picture/Director Not from Same Film|publisher=Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences|date=March 11, 2011|archivedate=January 13, 2012|accessdate=February 8, 2016}}</ref>

Since its inception, the award has been given to 72 directors or directing teams. [[John Ford]] has received the most awards in this category with four. [[William Wyler]] was nominated on twelve occasions, more than any other individual. [[Damien Chazelle]] became the youngest director in history to receive this award, at the age of 32 for his work on ''[[La La Land (film)|La La Land]]''. [[John Singleton]] became the youngest director to be nominated for this award, at age 24 for his work on ''[[Boyz n the Hood]]''. Two directing teams have shared the award; [[Robert Wise]] and [[Jerome Robbins]] for ''[[West Side Story (1961 film)|West Side Story]]'' in 1961 and [[Coen brothers|Joel and Ethan Coen]] for ''[[No Country for Old Men (film)|No Country for Old Men]]'' in 2007. The Coen brothers are the only siblings to have won the award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.today.com/id/23329024/ns/today-today_entertainment/t/coen-brothers-make-oscar-history/|publisher=Today.com|title=Coen brothers make Oscar history|accessdate=July 1, 2016|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160207001713/http://www.today.com/id/23329024/ns/today-today_entertainment/t/coen-brothers-make-oscar-history/|archivedate=February 7, 2016}}</ref> [[Kathryn Bigelow]] is the only woman to have won the award, for 2009's ''[[The Hurt Locker]]''. As of the [[92nd Academy Awards|2020 ceremony]], [[Bong Joon-ho]] is the most recent winner in this category for his work on ''[[Parasite (2019 film)|Parasite]]''.

==Winners and nominees==
In the following table, the years are listed as per Academy convention, and generally correspond to the year of film release in [[Los Angeles County, California|Los Angeles County]], [[California]]; the ceremonies are always held the following year.<ref>{{harvnb|Crouse|2005|p=257}}</ref> For the first five ceremonies, the eligibility period spanned twelve months from August 1 to July 31.<ref name="Oscar eligibility">[[#Levy|Levy 2003]], page 52</ref> For the 6th ceremony held in 1934, the eligibility period lasted from August 1, 1932, to December 31, 1933.<ref name="Oscar eligibility" /> Since the [[7th Academy Awards|7th ceremony]] held in 1935, the period of eligibility became the full previous calendar year from January 1 to December 31.<ref name="Oscar eligibility" />

==Diversity of nominees/winners==

===Asian nominees/winners===
Five Asian directors (one Asian-American) have been nominated a total of seven times in this category, and two individuals have won the award (with Ang Lee winning twice).

* 1965 – (JPN) [[Hiroshi Teshigahara]] for ''[[Woman in the Dunes]]'' 
* 1985 – (JPN) [[Akira Kurosawa]] for ''[[Ran (film)|Ran]]'' 
* 1999 – (IND/USA) [[M. Night Shyamalan]] for ''[[The Sixth Sense]]'' †
* 2000 – (TWN) [[Ang Lee]] for ''[[Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon]]'' †
* 2005 – (TWN) '''{{sort|Lee|[[Ang Lee]]}}''' for '''{{sort|Brokeback|''[[Brokeback Mountain]]''}}''' †
* 2012 – (TWN) '''{{sort|Lee|[[Ang Lee]]}}''' for '''{{sort|Life|''[[Life of Pi (film)|Life of Pi]]''}}''' †
* 2019 – (KOR) '''[[Bong Joon-ho]]''' for '''{{sort|Parasite|''[[Parasite (2019 film)|Parasite]]''}}''' ‡

===Black nominees===
Six black directors have been nominated a total of six times in this category, and none have won the award.<ref>{{cite news|first=Jacob|last=Stolworthy|date=January 23, 2018|title=Oscar nominations 2018: Jordan Peele and Greta Gerwig's Best Director nods are a win for diversity and creativity|work=The Independent|accessdate=January 23, 2018|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/news/oscars-2018-greta-gerwig-jordan-peele-get-out-lady-bird-best-director-christopher-nolan-best-picture-a8174161.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180123184954/http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/news/oscars-2018-greta-gerwig-jordan-peele-get-out-lady-bird-best-director-christopher-nolan-best-picture-a8174161.html|archive-date=January 23, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

* 1991 – (USA) [[John Singleton]] for ''[[Boyz n the Hood]]'' §
* 2009 – (USA) [[Lee Daniels]] for ''[[Precious (film)|Precious]]'' †
* 2013 – (UK) [[Steve McQueen (director)|Steve McQueen]] for ''[[12 Years a Slave (film)|12 Years a Slave]]'' ‡
* 2016 – (USA) [[Barry Jenkins]] for ''[[Moonlight (2016 film)|Moonlight]]'' ‡
* 2017 – (USA) [[Jordan Peele]] for ''[[Get Out]]'' §†
* 2018 – (USA) [[Spike Lee]] for ''[[BlacKkKlansman]]'' †

===Latin American nominees/winners===
Five Latin American directors have been nominated a total of eight times in this category, and three have won the award five times.

* 1985 – (ARG) [[Héctor Babenco]] for ''[[Kiss of the Spider Woman (film)|Kiss of the Spider Woman]]'' †
* 2003 – (BRA) [[Fernando Meirelles]] for ''[[City of God (2002 film)|City of God]]'' 
* 2006 – (MEX) [[Alejandro González Iñárritu|Alejandro G. Iñárritu]] for ''[[Babel (film)|Babel]]'' †
* 2013 – (MEX) '''[[Alfonso Cuarón]]''' for '''''[[Gravity (2013 film)|Gravity]]''''' †
* 2014 – (MEX) '''[[Alejandro González Iñárritu|Alejandro G. Iñárritu]]''' for '''''[[Birdman (film)|Birdman]]''''' ‡
* 2015 – (MEX) '''[[Alejandro González Iñárritu|Alejandro G. Iñárritu]]''' for '''''[[The Revenant (2015 film)|The Revenant]]''''' †
* 2017 – (MEX) '''[[Guillermo del Toro]]''' for '''''[[The Shape of Water]]''''' ‡
* 2018 – (MEX) '''[[Alfonso Cuarón]]''' for '''''[[Roma (2018 film)|Roma]]''''' †

===Oceanic nominees/winners===
Seven Oceanic directors have been nominated a total of eleven times in this category, and one has won the award.

* 1942 – (AUS) [[John Farrow]] for ''[[Wake Island (film)|Wake Island]]'' †
* 1983 – (AUS) [[Bruce Beresford]] for ''[[Tender Mercies]]'' †
* 1985 – (AUS) [[Peter Weir]] for ''[[Witness (1985 film)|Witness]]'' †
* 1989 – (AUS) [[Peter Weir]] for ''[[Dead Poets Society]]'' †
* 1993 – (NZ) {{sort|Campion|[[Jane Campion]]}} for {{sort|Piano|''[[The Piano]]''}} †
* 1995 – (AUS) [[Chris Noonan]] for ''[[Babe (film)|Babe]]'' †
* 1998 – (AUS) [[Peter Weir]] for ''[[The Truman Show]]''
* 2001 – (NZ) [[Peter Jackson]] for ''[[The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring]]'' †
* 2003 – (NZ) '''[[Peter Jackson]]''' for '''''[[The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King]]''''' ‡
* 2003 – (AUS) [[Peter Weir]] for ''[[Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World]]'' †
* 2015 – (AUS) [[George Miller (director)|George Miller]] for ''[[Mad Max: Fury Road]]'' †
