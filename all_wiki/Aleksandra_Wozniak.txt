'''Aleksandra Wozniak''' ({{lang-pl|Woźniak}}; born September 7, 1987) is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] former tennis player. She turned professional in November 2005, and achieved a career-best ranking of No. 21 in June 2009, making her the fifth highest-ranked Canadian singles player of all time.<ref name="Woz1">{{cite web|url=http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-3.asp|title=Bio p.3|publisher=AleksandraWozniak.com|accessdate=October 31, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090607023920/http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-3.asp|archive-date=2009-06-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> She won one [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] and eleven [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] tournaments. At the [[2008 Bank of the West Classic|Bank of the West Classic]] in Stanford in 2008, she became the first Canadian in 20 years to capture a WTA singles title and the first [[Quebec]]er in history to have accomplished such a feat. She reached a career-high ITF junior ranking of No. 3 on January 31, 2005. Wozniak was named Female Player of the Year by ''Tennis Canada'' five times (2004, 2006, 2008, 2009, 2012).

==Early life==
Wozniak's family immigrated to Canada from Poland in 1983, before she was born. She speaks Polish, English and French fluently. She has an elder sister Dorota who also played tennis.<ref name="WTA2">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/player/dorota-wozniak_2257889_8966|title=Dorota Wozniak's WTA profile|publisher=WTA|accessdate=October 31, 2008}}</ref> Aleksandra started playing tennis at the age of three. She was inspired to pick up a racquet by her sister and [[Monica Seles]], her idol growing up, and was coached by her father Antoni.<ref name="WTAQC1">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtaquebec.com/nouvelle.asp?article=1&id=1412|title=Wozniak puts Canada on world court|publisher=WTA Québec|accessdate=May 30, 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081220022615/http://www.wtaquebec.com/nouvelle.asp?article=1|archive-date=December 20, 2008|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

==Tennis career==
===2002–2007: Early years===
In 2002, as a 14-year-old, Wozniak won the Canadian Indoors Under-16 and Under-18 championships.<ref name="Woz2">{{cite web|url=http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-6.asp|title=Bio p.6|publisher=AleksandraWozniak.com|accessdate=October 31, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090607024003/http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-6.asp|archive-date=2009-06-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> She won the [[Kentucky]] International Junior Tennis Derby in 2004,<ref name="Woz3">{{cite web|url=http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-5.asp|title=Bio p.5|publisher=AleksandraWozniak.com|accessdate=October 31, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090607023937/http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-5.asp|archive-date=2009-06-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> and in 2005, she reached No. 3 in the juniors' ranking.<ref name="WTAQC2">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtaquebec.com/nouvelle.asp?article=0&id=142|title=Chasing a tennis dream|publisher=WTA Québec|accessdate=May 30, 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180620101756/http://www.wtaquebec.com/nouvelle.asp?article=0&id=142|archive-date=June 20, 2018|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> She also won the Tevlin Challenger 25k tournament in Toronto, the Hamilton Challenger 25k in Canada, the Victoria Challenger 25k and the Junior Casablanca Cup (as well as the doubles) in Mexico, and the Junior Del Cafe Cup (as well as the doubles) in Costa Rica.<ref name="Woz3"/>

In 2006, Wozniak won the Pittsburgh Challenger (defeating Belarusian [[Victoria Azarenka]]),<ref name="Google1">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=aFkwyOIHsr8C&q=Aleksandra+Wozniak+jewish&pg=PA317|title=Day by Day in Jewish Sports History|isbn=9780881259698|accessdate=October 31, 2008|last1=Wechsler|first1=Bob|year=2008}}</ref> and the Ashland Challenger (defeating Hungarian [[Ágnes Szávay]]).<ref name="Google1"/> Wozniak also won the Challenger in Hamilton the same year.<ref name="Woz4">{{cite web|url=http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-4.asp|title=Bio p.4|publisher=AleksandraWozniak.com|accessdate=October 31, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090607023932/http://www.aleksandrawozniak.com/en/bio-4.asp|archive-date=2009-06-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> In February she beat her first top-100 player, world No. 63 [[Li Na]], in Thailand. In November 2006, she defeated her first top-50 player, world No. 40, [[Olga Puchkova]], in Pittsburgh.

===2008: Breakthrough and first WTA title===
In the first round of the [[2008 French Open|French Open]] in June, Wozniak made it to the third round, before losing to 11th seed [[Vera Zvonareva]]. It was by far her best performance at any of the Grand Slam events, and she became the first Québécoise to reach the third round of a Grand Slam since 2002.<ref name="MG">{{cite web|url=http://blogs.montrealgazette.com/2008/05/31/wozniaks-french-open-run-ends/|title=Wozniak's French Open run ends|work=Montreal Gazette|accessdate=November 23, 2012}}</ref>

In July, Wozniak won her first WTA singles title at the [[2008 Bank of the West Classic|Bank of the West Classic]] at [[Stanford]], [[California]]. During the tournament she beat world No. 20, [[Francesca Schiavone]] of Italy, world No. 29 Sybille Bammer, and world No. 5, [[Serena Williams]] of the U.S. (who had to retire in the match), before defeating sixth seed [[Marion Bartoli]] of France in the final. She was a qualifier to the tournament, so she had to win three qualifying matches and then five main-draw matches in nine days. Wozniak became the first Canadian in 20 years to win a WTA singles title.<ref name="G&M">{{cite news|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/more-sports/wozniak-advances-to-fourth-round/article1161584|title=Canada's Wozniak wins first career WTA title|work=The Globe and Mail|accessdate=October 31, 2008|location=Toronto}}</ref> The victory vaulted her WTA rankings from No. 85 to No. 45 in the world. In August 2008, she was presented with [[key to the city]] in Blainville, Québec, by the mayor; they renamed it "Wozniakville" for 24 hours because for the first time a woman from Québec won a Sony Ericsson WTA Tour singles title (when she won Stanford in July).<ref name="WTA3">{{cite web|url=http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/2/players/playerprofiles/PlayerBio2.asp?PlayerID=310494|title=Aleksandra Wozniak's WTA profile|publisher=WTA|accessdate=October 31, 2008}}</ref>

She also received an award from the [[National Assembly of Québec]] in October 2008 for her career-high ranking of No. 37 and first WTA Tour singles title.<ref name="WTA3"/>

===2009: Continued good form and career-high ranking of No. 21===
[[File:Wozniak 2009 US Open 01.jpg|thumb|left|Wozniak in her first round match against Granville at the 2009 US Open]]
Wozniak was upset by German [[Sabine Lisicki]], also of Polish heritage, in the first round of the [[2009 Australian Open|Australian Open]] where Wozniak was the 30th seed. Wozniak joined up with compatriot [[Daniel Nestor]] in the mixed doubles, where they made it to the quarterfinals before losing to [[Sania Mirza]] and [[Mahesh Bhupathi]].

In March, she defeated world No. 48, [[Lucie Šafářová]], in [[2009 Indian Wells Masters|Indian Wells]]. That month she moved up to a career-best world No. 29. She reached her third singles final in April in the [[2009 MPS Group Championships|Ponte Vedra Beach]], beating world No. 10, [[Nadia Petrova]], in the semifinals, but was then defeated by world No. 12, [[Caroline Wozniacki]]. In May, she upset world No. 13, Marion Bartoli, at the [[2009 Mutua Madrileña Masters Madrid|Madrid Open]].<ref name="WTAQC3">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtaquebec.com/wozniak-a-le-numero-de-bartoli|title=Wozniak a le numéro de Bartoli|publisher=WTA Québec|accessdate=November 23, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140225155722/http://www.wtaquebec.com/wozniak-a-le-numero-de-bartoli/|archive-date=February 25, 2014|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

At the [[2009 French Open|French Open]], Wozniak was the 24th seed and became the first Québécoise to ever be seeded at Roland-Garros. Wozniak made it to the round of 16, before losing to [[Serena Williams]].<ref name="Guardian">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2009/jun/01/serena-williams-aleksandra-wozniak-french-open|title=Williams brushes aside Wozniak to reach the last eight|work=The Guardian|accessdate=November 23, 2012|location=London}}</ref> With her French Open success, Wozniak became Canada's first representative in the fourth round of the French Open women's draw in 17 years, and the first Canadian woman to survive into the second week at the French Open since [[Patricia Hy-Boulais]] in 1992. Wozniak was also the first Canadian to reach the fourth round of a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event since [[Maureen Drake]] qualified for the round of 16 at the [[1999 Australian Open]]. "That's awesome for Canada and I hope to keep going", said Wozniak.<ref name="Google2">{{cite web|url=https://www.google.com/hostednews/canadianpress/article/ALeqM5gD7CuTS2tAR3BX9bknot_KWC6Wtw|title=Wozniak's French Open|work=Canadian Press|accessdate=May 30, 2009}}</ref>

She debuted her grass season in June, at the [[2009 Aegon International|Aegon International]] in Eastbourne, Wozniak made it to the semifinals, before losing to Caroline Wozniacki. After that tournament, Wozniak's ranking rose two spots, to a career-high of No. 21.<ref name="CA">{{cite web|url=http://www.canada.com/Entertainment/Nadal+storylines+Wimbledon/1719069/story.html|title=Nadal and Wimbledon storylines|publisher=Canada.com|accessdate=May 30, 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090625180826/http://www.canada.com/Entertainment/Nadal+storylines+Wimbledon/1719069/story.html|archive-date=June 25, 2009|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> At [[2009 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], she was the first Canadian to be seeded in singles in 20 years at No. 23. However, she fell in the first round to [[Francesca Schiavone]].<ref name="TennisX">{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis-x.com/xblog/2009-06-23/1679.php|title=Schnyder, Wozniak Seeds Out at Wimbledon; Day 2 Schedule|work=TennisX|accessdate=November 23, 2012}}</ref>

At the [[2009 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], she advanced to the third round before losing to tenth seed [[Flavia Pennetta]]. Wozniak entered the [[2009 Toray Pan Pacific Open|Pan Pacific Open]] in Tokyo and made it into the third round before losing in three sets to [[Magdaléna Rybáriková]].

Wozniak was named ''Athlete of the Year'' for the second time in three years at the Tennis Quebec Excellence Awards. Her father Antoni and fitness trainer André Parent were joint winners of the International Coach of the Year Award for Quebec athletes.<ref name="LP">{{cite web|url=http://www.lapresse.ca/sports/tennis/200912/29/01-934813-aleksandra-wozniak-nommee-athlete-feminine-de-lannee.php|title=Aleksandra Wozniak nommée athlète féminine de l'année|work=La Presse|accessdate=November 23, 2012}}</ref> She was also named Female Athlete of the Year by the Canadian Press in recognition of her outstanding season.

===2010–11: Struggle with form and injuries===
[[File:Aleksandra Wozniak 2011.jpg|thumb|right|At the 2011 French Open, her first Slam since coming back from injury]]
In January at the [[2010 Australian Open|Australian Open]], [[Caroline Wozniacki]] eliminated Wozniak for the sixth time in straight sets. After early losses in [[2010 Sony Ericsson Open|Miami]] and [[2010 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]], she went to defend her last year final in [[2010 MPS Group Championships|Ponte Vedra Beach]]. However, she lost in the quarterfinals to [[Dominika Cibulková]].

At the [[2010 French Open|French Open]], she lost in the third round to fifth seed [[Elena Dementieva]] in a match that lasted more than three hours. At [[2010 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Wozniak made it to the second round before losing to fourth seed [[Jelena Janković]]. After losing in the first round of the [[2010 Rogers Cup|Rogers Cup]] in her native [[Quebec]] against [[Timea Bacsinszky]], she lost again in the first round at the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] against world No. 202, [[Sally Peers]], in a match that took just 48 minutes. She was out for the remainder of the season due to a forearm injury.<ref name="VancouverSun1">{{cite web |url=https://vancouversun.com/sports/Tennis+Canada+Aleksandra+Wozniak+season+over+thanks+forearm+injury/3470367/story.html |title=Canada's Aleksandra Wozniak's season over thanks to forearm injury |work=Vancouver Sun |accessdate=October 12, 2010 |archiveurl=https://archive.today/20101115133118/http://www.vancouversun.com/sports/Tennis+Canada+Aleksandra+Wozniak+season+over+thanks+forearm+injury/3470367/story.html |archivedate=2010-11-15 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In her first Grand Slam appearance since coming back from injury, Wozniak qualified for the [[2011 French Open]]. She won her first round match against [[Junri Namigata]] before losing in the second round to world No. 1, [[Caroline Wozniacki]]. She also qualified for [[2011 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], but lost in the first round against [[Barbora Záhlavová-Strýcová]].

She won in early August the second biggest tournament of her career at the $100k [[2011 Odlum Brown Vancouver Open|Vancouver Open]], where she defeated [[Jamie Hampton]] in the final. Wozniak qualified for her third straight Grand Slam at the [[2011 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], but lost in the first round to young American [[Christina McHale]].

===2012: Return to form and first Olympic experience===
[[File:Aleksandra Wozniak at the 2012 US Open 2.jpg|thumb|left|Wozniak at the US Open in 2012]]
At the first Grand Slam of the season, the [[2012 Australian Open|Australian Open]], Wozniak defeated [[Zhang Shuai (tennis)|Zhang Shuai]] in the first round. She made it through to the second round for the first time of her career. She was defeated by 27th seed [[Maria Kirilenko]] in the next round. In March, she won the $100k [[2012 The Bahamas Women's Open|Bahamas Open]], beating [[Alizé Cornet]] in the final. Wozniak reached the third round of the [[2012 Sony Ericsson Open|Sony Ericsson Open]] with wins over [[Eleni Daniilidou]] and world No. 28, [[Monica Niculescu]], in first and second round respectively, but lost to [[Venus Williams]] after having a match point in the third set.

At the [[2012 French Open|French Open]], she reached the third round for the fourth time in five years, but lost to world No. 1, [[Victoria Azarenka]]. She was eliminated in the second round at [[2012 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] by world No. 27, [[Zheng Jie]]. Wozniak qualified for the [[2012 Summer Olympic Games|Summer Olympics]] in London, but lost to Venus Williams in the second round. At the [[2012 Rogers Cup|Rogers Cup]], she reached the quarterfinals at a Premier 5 tournament for the first time of her career, where she lost to Caroline Wozniacki. She became the first Canadian in 20 years (since [[Patricia Hy-Boulais]] in 1992) to reach the quarterfinals there.<ref name="G&M2">{{cite news|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/more-sports/wozniak-downs-mchale-to-reach-rogers-cup-quarter-finals/article4476609 |title=Wozniak downs McHale to reach Rogers Cup quarter-finals |work=The Globe and Mail |accessdate=August 24, 2012 |location=Toronto |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120816063520/http://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/more-sports/wozniak-downs-mchale-to-reach-rogers-cup-quarter-finals/article4476609/ |archivedate=August 16, 2012 }}</ref> Wozniak lost in the second round of the [[2012 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] to world No. 17, [[Lucie Šafářová]].

She was forced to end her season prematurely after suffering a joint sprain in her right shoulder at the [[2012 Challenge Bell|Challenge Bell]] in September.<ref name="G&M3">{{cite news|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/sports/more-sports/shoulder-injury-sidelines-wozniak-for-remainder-of-tennis-season/article4566488|title=Shoulder injury sidelines Wozniak for remainder of tennis season|work=The Globe and Mail|accessdate=September 27, 2012|location=Toronto|date=September 25, 2012}}</ref>

===2013: More injuries===
Wozniak made a first return following her injury at the [[2013 Sony Open Tennis|Sony Open]] in March, but fell to [[Kristina Mladenovic]] in the first round. She was then forced to take a second break of three months to make sure her shoulder is completely healed.<ref name="NP">{{cite web|url=http://sports.nationalpost.com/2013/03/27/canadas-aleksandra-wozniak-out-three-months-with-shoulder-injury|title=Canada's Aleksandra Wozniak out three months with shoulder injury|work=National Post|accessdate=August 15, 2013|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130815204310/http://sports.nationalpost.com/2013/03/27/canadas-aleksandra-wozniak-out-three-months-with-shoulder-injury|archive-date=2013-08-15|url-status=dead}}</ref> She made a second return at the [[2013 New Haven Open at Yale|New Haven Open]] in mid-August, but was defeated in three sets by world No. 26, [[Ekaterina Makarova]], in her opening match.<ref name="OC">{{cite web|url=https://ottawacitizen.com/sports/Wozniak+miss+Rogers/8739943/story.html|title=Wozniak to miss Rogers Cup|work=Ottawa Citizen|accessdate=August 15, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130930235937/http://www.ottawacitizen.com/sports/Wozniak+miss+Rogers/8739943/story.html|archive-date=September 30, 2013|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name="Canoe">{{cite web|url=http://fr.canoe.ca/sports/nouvelles/tennis/archives/2013/08/20130819-161053.html|title=Aleksandra Wozniak s'incline à son retour|publisher=Canoe.ca|accessdate=August 19, 2013}}</ref> Her next tournament was the [[2013 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] where she was eliminated by world No. 2, Victoria Azarenka, in the second round.<ref name="TC">{{cite web|url=http://www.timescolonist.com/sports/no-2-victoria-azarenka-beats-wozniak-6-3-6-1-in-2nd-round-of-us-open-1.606293|title=No. 2 Victoria Azarenka beats Wozniak 6-3, 6-1 in 2nd round of US Open|work=Times Colonist|accessdate=August 29, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927234036/http://www.timescolonist.com/sports/no-2-victoria-azarenka-beats-wozniak-6-3-6-1-in-2nd-round-of-us-open-1.606293|archive-date=2013-09-27|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In September, Wozniak lost in the first round of the [[2013 Challenge Bell|Challenge Bell]] to the eighth seed [[Caroline Garcia]].<ref name="Canoe2">{{cite web|url=http://fr.canoe.ca/sports/nouvelles/tennis/archives/2013/09/20130909-231739.html|title=Aleksandra Wozniak tombe au premier tour|publisher=Canoe.ca|accessdate=September 14, 2013}}</ref> At the Premier-5 [[2013 Toray Pan Pacific Open|Pan Pacific Open]] in late September, Wozniak made it to the second round using her protected ranking. She defeated world No. 47, [[Francesca Schiavone]], in the opening round, but fell to the second seed [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] in the next one.<ref name="SL">{{cite web|url=http://www.sportinglife.com/tennis/news/article/2/8939194/venus-williams-ousts-azarenka|title=Venus Williams ousts Azarenka|publisher=SportingLife.com|accessdate=September 24, 2013}}</ref> The next week, she lost in the first round of the Premier Mandatory [[2013 China Open (tennis)|China Open]] to world No. 13, [[Sloane Stephens]].<ref name="SS">{{cite news|url=http://www1.skysports.com/tennis/news/12110/8945716/wta-china-open-heather-watson-loses-to-varvara-lepchenko-in-three-sets|title=WTA China Open: Heather Watson loses to Varvara Lepchenko in three sets|publisher=SkySports.com|accessdate=September 30, 2013}}</ref> At the last tournament of her season in early October, the [[2013 HP Open|HP Open]], she was defeated by Kristina Mladenovic in the first round.<ref name="TSN">{{cite web|url=https://www.tsn.ca/tennis/story/?id=433662|title=Lisicki moves on in Osaka|publisher=TSN.ca|accessdate=October 9, 2013}}</ref>

She was eliminated by Zhang Shuai in the next round.<ref name="CBC2">{{cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/tennis/eugenie-bouchard-advances-to-mexico-open-quarters-1.2553128|title=Eugenie Bouchard advances to Mexico Open quarters|publisher=CBC Sports|accessdate=February 27, 2014}}</ref>

round.<ref name="CBC4">{{cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/tennis/aleksandra-wozniak-set-aside-at-sony-ericsson-open-1.2577423|title=Aleksandra Wozniak set aside at Sony Ericsson In April, Wozniak won a crucial match over world No. 52, [[Jana Čepelová]], at the [[2014 Fed Cup World Group Play-offs|Fed Cup World Group Play-offs]] to help Canada get its place in the World Group I, the first time ever for the country since the introduction of the new World Group format in 1995.<ref name="CBC5">{{cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/tennis/eugenie-bouchard-canada-clinch-fed-cup-playoff-tie-1.2616200|title=Eugenie Bouchard, Canada clinch Fed Cup playoff tie|publisher=CBC Sports|accessdate=April 20, 2014}}</ref> At the [[2014 French Open|French Open]] in May, Wozniak qualified for the main draw but lost in three sets to world No. 26, [[Sorana Cîrstea]], in the first round, despite having a match point in the second set.<ref name="TSN2">{{cite news|url=https://www.tsn.ca/tennis/story/?id=453228|title=Canadians Fichman, Wozniak fall in first round in Paris|publisher=TSN.ca|accessdate=May 27, 2014}}</ref> At a warm up tournament for Wimbledon, the Premier-level [[2014 Aegon Classic|Aegon Classic]], she qualified for the main draw and reached the third round. ===2015–17: New start===
Wozniak made her return in late August 2015 at the 25k in [[Winnipeg]] after an 11-month layoff following her shoulder surgery.<ref name="CBC6">{{cite news|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/tennis/aleksandra-wozniak-returning-to-tennis-after-long-absence-1.3180572|title=Aleksandra Wozniak returning to tennis after long absence|publisher=CBC Sports|accessdate=December 29, 2015}}</ref> She advanced to the quarterfinals, but was defeated by [[Michaëlla Krajicek]].<ref name="ITF">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/men's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100034560|title=Drawsheet: $25,000 Winnipeg|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=December 29, 2015}}</ref> In September 2015 at the [[2015 Coupe Banque Nationale|Coupe Banque Nationale]], she was awarded a wildcard into the qualifying draw where she lost in the first round to [[Mandy Minella]].<ref name="CBN">{{cite web|url=http://www.coupebanquenationale.ca/en/results/qualifications|title=Results - Qualifications|publisher=CoupeBanqueNationale.ca|accessdate=December 29, 2015}}</ref> She was also unable to qualify for her next tournament, this time in the final round of the $75k [[2015 Coleman Vision Tennis Championships|Coleman Vision Championships]] one week later.<ref name="ITF2">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/men's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100034550|title=Drawsheet: $75,000 Albuquerque, NM|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=December 29, 2015}}</ref> In October 2015, Wozniak lost in the first round of her final two tournaments of the season, the 50ks in [[2015 Abierto Tampico|Tampico]] and [[2015 Challenger Banque Nationale de Saguenay|Saguenay]].<ref name="ITF3">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100035812|title=Drawsheet: $50,000+H Tampico|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=December 29, 2015}}</ref><ref name="ITF4">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100035867|title=Drawsheet: $50,000 Saguenay|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=December 29, 2015}}</ref>

In February 2016, Wozniak advanced to the quarterfinals of the $25k in Surprise, Arizona.<ref name="ITF5">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100036450|title=Drawsheet: $25,000 Surprise, AZ|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> She used her protected ranking to enter the main draw of the [[2016 French Open|French Open]], her first Grand Slam main draw since the 2014 US Open, but was defeated by [[Yulia Putintseva]] in the opening round.<ref name="VCB">{{cite web|url=http://www.vancitybuzz.com/2016/05/french-open-day-5-genie-bouchard|title=French Open roundup: Genie Bouchard faces tough test on Day 5|publisher=Vancity Buzz|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> At the end of July, using again her protected ranking, she entered the singles main draw of the [[2016 Citi Open|Citi Open]] where she lost to [[Jessica Pegula]] in the first round.<ref name="NP2">{{cite web|url=http://news.nationalpost.com/sports/tennis/vasek-pospisil-falls-in-first-round-with-three-set-loss-at-citi-open|title=Vasek Pospisil falls in first round with three-set loss at Citi Open|work=National Post|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> The next week, she received a wildcard into the main draw of the [[2016 Rogers Cup|Rogers Cup]] and lost in the first round to [[Sara Errani]].<ref name="TS">{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/sports/tennis/2016/07/26/eugenie-bouchard-dispatches-safarova-in-rogers-cup-opener.html|title=Eugenie Bouchard dispatches Safarova in Rogers Cup opener|work=The Star|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> She next played the [[2016 Challenger Banque Nationale de Granby|Challenger de Granby]], a $50k event, and reached the semifinals.<ref name="ITF6">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100037562|title=Drawsheet: $50,000 Granby|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref>

At the [[2016 Coupe Banque Nationale|Coupe Banque Nationale]] in September, she was awarded a wildcard to play in the main draw, but she lost to Pegula in her opener for the second time in two months.<ref name="MG2">{{cite news|url=https://montrealgazette.com/sports/tennis/wozniak-loses-in-first-round-of-coupe-banque-nationale-in-quebec-city|title=Wozniak loses in first round of Coupe Banque Nationale in Quebec City|work=Montreal Gazette|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> At the $75k [[2016 Coleman Vision Tennis Championships|Coleman Vision Championships]] the next week, Wozniak advanced to the semifinals where she lost to [[Mandy Minella]] in three sets.<ref name="ITF7">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100037735|title=Drawsheet: $75,000 Albuquerque, NM|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> Also in September, she reached the quarterfinals of the 25k in [[Stillwater, Oklahoma|Stillwater]].<ref name="ITF8">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100038216|title=Drawsheet: $25,000 Stillwater, OK|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=September 30, 2016}}</ref> At the [[2016 Challenger Banque Nationale de Saguenay|Challenger de Saguenay]] in October, Wozniak made it to the quarterfinals, losing to first seed [[CiCi Bellis]].<ref name="ITF9">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/tournaments/women's-tournament/info.aspx?tournamentid=1100037564|title=Drawsheet: $50,000 Saguenay|publisher=ITFTennis.com|accessdate=October 21, 2016}}</ref>

==Fed Cup==
Wozniak won her first Fed Cup match in 2004, defeating Swiss [[Timea Bacsinszky]], and boasts a 40–12 record through April 2016. Her 40 total victories are a [[Canada Fed Cup team|Canadian Fed Cup]] record, as are her 32 wins in singles. She has appeared in 36 ties in the Fed Cup, also a record.<ref name="FC">{{cite web|url=http://www.fedcup.com/en/players/player/profile.aspx?playerid=35013643|title=Aleksandra Wozniak's Fed Cup profile|publisher=Fed Cup|accessdate=November 23, 2012}}</ref>

==Style of play==
Wozniak had an all court game that was anchored by an effective first serve and a strong backhand. She also possessed a good overhead.<ref name="Yahoo2">{{cite web|url=http://uk.eurosport.yahoo.com/25022009/58/tennis-spy-aleksandra-wozniak.html|title=Tennis Spy : Aleksandra Wozniak|publisher=Yahoo Sport UK|accessdate=October 31, 2008}}</ref> Her favourite surface is clay.

'''Notes'''
*<sup>{{note label|Doha/Dubai|1|1}}</sup> The first [[WTA Premier tournaments|Premier 5]] event of the year has switched back and forth between the [[Dubai Tennis Championships]] and the [[Qatar Ladies Open]] since 2009. Dubai was classified as a Premier 5 event from 2009–2011 before being succeeded by Doha for the 2012–2014 period. Since 2015, the two tournaments alternate between Premier 5 and Premier status every year.
*
*<sup>{{note label|Tokyo/Wuhan|2|2}}</sup> In 2014, the [[Pan Pacific Open]] was downgraded to a Premier event and replaced by the [[Wuhan Open]].
