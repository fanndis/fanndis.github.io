'''Jana Čepelová''' ({{IPA-sk|ˈjana ˈtʃɛpɛlɔʋaː}}; born 29 May 1993) is a Slovak [[tennis]] player.

She has won five singles and three doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]]. On 12 May 2014, she reached her best singles ranking of world No. 50. On 18 May 2015, she peaked at No. 158 in the doubles rankings.

Čepelová's best result at a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|major event]] was the third round of the [[2012 Wimbledon Championships|2012]] and [[2016 Wimbledon Championships]]. As a junior, she won the [[2010 Australian Open – Girls' Doubles|girls' doubles]] at the 2010 Australian Open with [[Chantal Škamlová]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.whtimes.co.uk/sport/teen_gosling_tennis_star_babos_sure_of_success_in_2010_1_57052?ot=archant.PrintFriendlyPageLayout.ot|title=Teen Gosling tennis star Babos sure of success in 2010|first=Mark|last=Hemmings|work=Welwyn Hatfield Times|date=2 February 2010|accessdate=18 August 2010}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Čepelová was born in [[Košice]] to Peter and Jarmila and is currently coached by Martin Zathurecký.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/getting-to-know/article/3749101/title/getting-to-know-jana-cepelova|title=Getting to know... Jana Cepelova|work=[[Women's Tennis Association]]|date=5 April 2014|accessdate=6 April 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
===Junior career===
Jana Čepelová and [[Chantal Škamlová]] won girls' doubles at [[2010 Australian Open]]. They also won silver medal in doubles at [[Tennis at the 2010 Summer Youth Olympics|2010 Summer Youth Olympics]]. At the same event, Čepelová won bronze medal in singles.

===2012: Coming onto the WTA Tour===
Čepelová spent most of her 2012-year on the ITF circuit. Her best achievement on the [[WTA Tour]] was at the [[2012 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon Championships]]. She qualified for the main draw by defeating [[Alla Kudryavtseva]], [[Chanel Simmonds]] and [[Ekaterina Bychkova]]. In round one, she beat fellow qualifier [[Kristina Mladenovic]] in three sets. In the second round, she upset 26th seed [[Anabel Medina Garrigues]] before she lost to world No. 2 [[Victoria Azarenka]].

===2014: First WTA final===
At the [[Family Circle Cup]] in Charleston, Čepelová scored the biggest win of her career, defeating world No. 1 [[Serena Williams]] in the second round. Čepelová would go on to reach her first WTA final with victories over [[Elena Vesnina]], [[Daniela Hantuchová]], and [[Belinda Bencic]]. Her run ended there as she lost to [[Andrea Petkovic]].

===2015===
[[File:Jana Cepelova 1, 2015 Wimbledon Qualifying - Diliff.jpg|thumb|Čepelová at the [[2015 Wimbledon Championships]]]]
Čepelová started the year at the [[2015 ASB Classic – Singles|ASB Classic]]. She lost in the first round to third seed, last year finalist, and eventual champion Venus Williams.<ref>{{cite news |title=As it happened: Kiwi, superstars win on Day Two at ASB Classic |url=https://www.tvnz.co.nz/one-news/sport/tennis/as-it-happened-kiwi-superstars-win-on-day-two-at-asb-classic-6215088 |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.tvnz.co.nz |date=6 January 2015}}</ref> In Hobart at the [[2015 Hobart International – Singles|Hobart International]], Čepelová was defeated in the first round by Annika Beck.<ref>{{cite news |title=Annika Beck bites through, Mona Barthel loses with a match ball |url=https://www.tennisnet.com/news/wta-hobart-annika-beck-beisst-sich-durch-mona-barthel-verliert-mit-matchball-33597 |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.tennisnet.com |date=12 January 2015}}</ref>

Playing in Indian Wells at the [[2015 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|BNP Paribas Open]], Čepelová lost in the first round to qualifier [[Lucie Hradecká]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=PISANI |first1=SACHA |title=Americans shine at Indian Wells |url=https://www.sportingnews.com/au/other-sports/news/americans-shine-at-indian-wells/1va9b0ftjr2xj1tw47xh17uhn3 |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.sportingnews.com |date=13 March 2015}}</ref> At the [[2015 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami Open]], Čepelová was defeated in the first round by Julia Görges.<ref>{{cite news |title=Azarenka advances in Miami, Vaidisova claims first win since retirement |url=https://www.beinsports.com/en/tennis/news/victoria-azarenka-advances-at-miami-open-nico/19763 |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.beinsports.com |date=26 March 2015}}</ref> Seeded second at the [[2015 Wilde Lexus Women's USTA Pro Circuit Event – Singles|Wilde Lexus Women's USTA Pro Circuit Event]], Čepelová fell in the first round to [[Laura Siegemund]].

Last year finalist at the [[2015 Family Circle Cup – Singles|Family Circle Cup]], Čepelová lost in the second round to fourth seed Sara Errani.<ref>{{cite news |title=Bouchard falls at Family Circle Cup |url=https://www.tsn.ca/bouchard-falls-at-family-circle-cup-1.251704?tsn-amp |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.tsn.ca |date=8 April 2015}}</ref> At the [[2015 J&T Banka Prague Open – Singles|J&T Banka Prague Open]], Čepelová was defeated in the first round by Elena Vesnina. Competing at the [[2015 Empire Slovak Open – Singles|Empire Slovak Open]] in Slovakia, Čepelová lost in the first round to [[Tereza Smitková]].<ref>{{cite news |title=The Slovaks were not successful, the Czechs were |url=https://www.empireslovakopen.sk/en/slovaks-were-not-successful-czechs-were |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.empireslovakopen.sk |date=7 May 2015}}</ref> Seeded eighth at the [[2015 Open Engie Saint-Gaudens Midi-Pyrénées – Singles|Open Engie Saint-Gaudens Midi-Pyrénées]], Čepelová reached the final where she was defeated by María Teresa Torró Flor.<ref>{{cite news |title=TITA TORRO WINS THE SAINT-GAUDENS TOURNAMENT IN FRANCE |url=http://www.deportevillena.com/2015/05/tita-torro-gana-el-torneo-de-saint.html |accessdate=27 May 2020 |publisher=www.deportevillena.com}}</ref> At the [[2015 French Open – Women's Singles Qualifying|French Open]], Čepelová lost in the second round of qualifying to Kateryna Bondarenko. In [[Marseille]] at the [[2015 Open Féminin de Marseille – Singles|Open Féminin de Marseille]], Čepelová was defeated in the second round by sixth seed [[Denisa Allertová]].

At the [[2015 Aegon Open Nottingham – Women's Singles|Aegon Open Nottingham]], Čepelová's first grass-court tournament of the season, she lost in the first round of qualifying to [[Donna Vekić]]. In Birmingham at the [[2015 Aegon Classic – Singles|Aegon Classic]], Čepelová was defeated in the first round of qualifying by [[Mariana Duque Mariño]]. Coming into [[2015 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]] ranked 106 in the world, Čepelová upset third seed Simona Halep in the first round.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cambers |first1=Simon |title=Wimbledon No3 seed Simona Halep loses to world No106 Jana Cepelova |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/jun/30/wimbledon-simona-halep-loses-jana-cepelova |accessdate=28 May 2020 |publisher=www.theguardian.com |date=30 June 2015}}</ref> She lost in the second round to Monica Niculescu.<ref>{{cite news |title=Petra Kvitova and Agnieszka Radwanska in ruthless form to reach Wimbledon third round |url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/tennis/petra-kvitova-and-agnieszka-radwanska-in-ruthless-form-to-reach-wimbledon-third-round-1.31980 |accessdate=28 May 2020 |publisher=www.thenational.ae |date=2 July 2015}}</ref>

===2016===
Next year at [[2016 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], she defeated [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the second round before losing to [[Lucie Šafářová]] in an epic three-set match. Muguruza was the third top-3 player whom Jana has defeated in her career to this date.

===2020===
Beginning her season at the [[2020 Australian Open – Women's Singles Qualifying|Australian Open]], Čepelová withdrew from her first round of qualifying match against Kurumi Nara.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Williams |first1=Peter |title=Australian Open: Qualifying Round 1 – Novak advances to Round 2 as rain interrupts Open |url=https://tennis.draftcentral.com.au/australian-open-qualifying-round-1-novak-advances-to-round-2-as-rain-interrupts-open/ |accessdate=15 May 2020 |publisher=tennis.draftcentral.com.au |date=16 January 2020}}</ref>

At the [[2020 Qatar Total Open – Singles|Qatar Total Open]], Čepelová lost in the final round of qualifying to [[Jil Teichmann]]. Playing at the first edition of the [[2020 Lyon Open (WTA) – Singles|Lyon Open]], Čepelová was defeated in the first round of qualifying by [[Margot Yerolymos]].
