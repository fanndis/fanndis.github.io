{{Scottish Politics}}
The [[First Minister of Scotland]] is the leader of the [[Scottish Government]], [[Scotland]]'s devolved administration. The First Minister is responsible for the exercise of functions by the Cabinet of the Scottish Government; policy development and coordination; relationships with the rest of the [[United Kingdom]], Europe and the wider world. The First Minister is a [[Member of the Scottish Parliament]] (MSP), and is nominated by the [[Scottish Parliament]] before being officially appointed by the [[Monarchy of the United Kingdom|Monarch]]. 

Currently, the fifth [[First Minister of Scotland]] is [[Nicola Sturgeon]] of the [[Scottish National Party]] (SNP), who succeeded Alex Salmond in November 2014 following his resignation after the [[2014 Scottish independence referendum]]. Sturgeon has led the [[Government of the 5th Scottish Parliament]] since 2016. Sturgeon, and before that, [[Alex Salmond]], previously led the governments of the [[Government of the 3rd Scottish Parliament|3rd]] and [[Government of the 4th Scottish Parliament|4th Scottish Parliaments]] which was first elected in 2007 as a minority government, and re-elected in 2011, where they formed the first majority government in the Scottish Parliament.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2007/may/04/scotland.devolution | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Patrick | last=Wintour | title=SNP wins historic victory | date=4 May 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/scottishelections2007 | location=London | work=The Guardian | title=Scottish elections 2007 | date=15 January 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2011/may/06/snp-election-victory-scottish-independence | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Severin | last=Carrell | title=Stunning SNP election victory throws spotlight on Scottish independence | date=6 May 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-13305522 | work=BBC News | title=Scottish election: SNP wins election | date=6 May 2011}}</ref>

The longest-serving First Minister is Alex Salmond, who surpassed [[Jack McConnell]] on 7 November 2012 and spent a total of 7 and a half years in the role.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-20181668 |title=Longest-serving first minister Salmond will not 'go on and on' |date=7 November 2012 |work= |publisher=BBC News |accessdate=1 February 2013}}</ref> [[Donald Dewar]] was the first person to hold the position.
