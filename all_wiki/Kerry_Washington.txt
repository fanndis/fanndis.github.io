She gained wide public recognition for starring as [[Spin (public relations)|crisis management expert]] [[Olivia Pope]] in the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] drama series ''[[Scandal (TV series)|Scandal]]'' (2012–2018).<ref>{{cite web |last=Bricker |first=Tierney |url=http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/05/abc-picks-up-charlies-angels-good-christian-belles-and-ten-more.html |title=ABC picks up 'Charlie's Angels,' 'Good Christian Belles' and ten more |date=May 13, 2011 |access-date=October 1, 2012 |url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120505020628/http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/05/abc-picks-up-charlies-angels-good-christian-belles-and-ten-more.html |archive-date=May 5, 2012}}</ref> For her role, she was twice nominated for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series]] and once for the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Drama]]. Her portrayal of [[Anita Hill]] in the [[HBO]] television political thriller film,  ''[[Confirmation (film)|Confirmation]]'' (2016), earned her a nomination for both the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie]] and the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Miniseries or Television Film]]. And for her role as Mia Warren in the [[Hulu]] miniseries ''[[Little Fires Everywhere (miniseries)|Little Fires Everywhere]]'' (2020) she was again nominated for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie]].

In film, Washington is known for her roles as Della Bea Robinson in ''[[Ray (film)|Ray]]'' (2004), as Kay in ''[[The Last King of Scotland (film)|The Last King of Scotland]]'' (2006), as [[Alicia Masters]] in the live-action ''[[Fantastic Four]]'' films of [[Fantastic Four (2005 film)|2005]] and [[Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer|2007]], and as Broomhilda von Shaft in [[Quentin Tarantino]]'s ''[[Django Unchained]]'' (2012). She has also starred in the independent films ''[[Our Song (film)|Our Song]]'' (2000), ''[[The Dead Girl]]'' (2006), ''[[Mother and Child (2009 film)|Mother and Child]]'' (2009), ''[[Night Catches Us]]'' (2010), and ''[[American Son (2019 film)|American Son]]'' (2019).

''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine included Washington in its [[Time 100|''Time'' 100]] list of most influential people in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://time.com/70800/kerry-washington-2014-time-100/ |title=Kerry Washington 2014 Time |accessdate=April 26, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140425150724/http://time.com/70800/kerry-washington-2014-time-100/|archive-date=April 25, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2018, ''[[Forbes]]'' named her the eighth highest-paid television actress.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/natalierobehmed/2018/10/25/highest-paid-tv-actresses-2018-sofia-vergara-tops-ranking-again-with-42-5-million/#7f921d4c4799 |title=Highest-Paid TV Actresses 2018: Sofia Vergara Tops Ranking Again With $42.5 Million |first=Natalie |last=Robehmed |work=Forbes |date=October 25, 2018 |accessdate=June 20, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181025173903/https://www.forbes.com/sites/natalierobehmed/2018/10/25/highest-paid-tv-actresses-2018-sofia-vergara-tops-ranking-again-with-42-5-million/#7f921d4c4799|archive-date=October 25, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Washington has won a [[Primetime Emmy Award]] and five [[NAACP Image Awards]], including The President's Award.

== Early life and education ==
Washington was born in [[The Bronx]], New York City, the daughter of Valerie, a professor and educational consultant, and Earl Washington, a [[real estate broker]].<ref name="filmr" /><ref name=elc>{{Cite news |last=Stein |first=Ruthe |title=Washington's 'Mother' instinct |date=May 9, 2010 |url=http://articles.sfgate.com/2010-05-09/entertainment/20889631_1_sundance-film-festival-first-feature-film-kerry-washington |accessdate=September 21, 2010 |work=The San Francisco Chronicle|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100625043439/http://articles.sfgate.com/2010-05-09/entertainment/20889631_1_sundance-film-festival-first-feature-film-kerry-washington|archive-date=June 25, 2010|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Collins |first=Lauren |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2004/10/24/fashion/24NITE.html |title=Kerry Washington: Politics and Shabu Shabu |work=[[New York Times]] |date=October 24, 2004 |accessdate=November 16, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121026092658/http://www.nytimes.com/2004/10/24/fashion/24NITE.html|archive-date=October 26, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> Her father's family is of [[African American]] origin, having moved from [[South Carolina]] to [[Brooklyn]]. Her mother's family is from [[Manhattan]], and Washington has said that her mother is from a "mixed-race background and from Jamaica, so she is partly English and Scottish and Native American, but also descended from enslaved Africans in the Caribbean."<ref>{{cite news |last=Curtis |first=Nick |title=Kerry Washington on making Quentin Tarantino's Django Unchained |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/kerry-washington-on-making-quentin-tarantinos-django-unchained-8453865.html |accessdate=May 17, 2013 |newspaper=Evening Standard |date=January 16, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130301074812/http://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/kerry-washington-on-making-quentin-tarantinos-django-unchained-8453865.html|archive-date=March 1, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/lifestyle/-SO-Who-Knew--Washington-Connects-The-Dots_12733464 |title=So Who Knew? == Career ==

===1994–2009: Beginnings and breakthrough===
[[File:6.24.04KerryWashingtonByLuigiNovi.jpg|thumb|upright|Washington at the New York premiere of ''[[She Hate Me]]'' in 2004]]
Washington got her [[Screen Actors Guild]] (SAG) card as a requirement for a commercial that she starred in.<ref name=TVGuide>"How Did You Get Your SAG-AFTRA Card?" ''[[TV Guide]]''. January 13, 2014. p. 10.</ref> Washington made her screen debut in the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] [[television movie|telefilm]] ''Magical Make-Over'' (1994).<ref name="Kerry"/> She was in the cast of the 1996 [[Public Broadcasting Service|PBS]] [[sketch comedy]]-style educational series ''[[Standard Deviants]]'',<ref name="Kerry"/> and she appeared in the short "3D" and the feature film ''[[Our Song (film)|Our Song]]'' in 2000.<ref name="Kerry"/> She went on to appear in several movies, including ''[[Save the Last Dance]]'' (2001) and ''[[The Human Stain (film)|The Human Stain]]'' (2003). In 2002 she played [[Chris Rock]]'s love interest in the spy thriller ''[[Bad Company (2002 film)|Bad Company]]'', a film that represented a turning point for her, in that it was the first time in her career that she had made enough money annually to qualify for health insurance under SAG.<ref name=TVGuide/>

In 2004, she played the female lead in [[Spike Lee]]'s ''[[She Hate Me]]'', and she received strong reviews for her performance. After 2004, she held parts in ''[[Mr. & Mrs. Smith (2005 film)|Mr. & Mrs. Smith]]'' (2005), ''[[Little Man (2006 film)|Little Man]]'' (2006), ''[[I Think I Love My Wife]]'' (2007), and as a wife of 1970s [[Uganda]]n [[dictator]] [[Idi Amin]] in the [[United Kingdom|UK]] historical drama ''[[The Last King of Scotland (film)|The Last King of Scotland]]'' (2006).<ref name="Kerry"/> Washington has also appeared in the recurring role of Chelina Hall on the ABC [[television program|television series]] ''[[Boston Legal]]'',<ref name="Kerry"/> and in several episodes of the [[A&E Network|A&E]] [[cable television|cable-TV]] series ''[[100 Centre Street]]''.<ref name="Kerry"/> In 2007, she co-directed and appeared in the music video for hip-hop artist [[Common (rapper)|Common]]'s song, "[[I Want You (Common song)|I Want You]]", the fourth single from his album ''[[Finding Forever]]''<ref name="Bio">{{cite web |title=Kerry Washington Bio |url=http://www.buddytv.com/info/kerry-washington-info.aspx |work=[[BuddyTV]] |accessdate=October 1, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170329025955/http://www.buddytv.com/info/kerry-washington-info.aspx|archive-date=March 29, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>
and became a spokesperson for [[L'Oréal]], appearing in commercials and ads alongside fellow actresses, [[Scarlett Johansson]] and [[Eva Longoria]], [[Gong Li]], [[Michelle Yeoh]], [[Dian Sastrowardoyo]], [[Aishwarya Rai]], [[Maya Karin]] and model [[Doutzen Kroes]].<ref name="Washington">{{cite web |title=Kerry Washington |url=http://matineeclassics.com/celebrities/actors/kerry_washington/details/ |accessdate=October 1, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121122151451/http://matineeclassics.com/celebrities/actors/kerry_washington/details/|archive-date=November 22, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>

Washington narrated the critically acclaimed documentary about the New Orleans-based teenage [[TBC Brass Band]], [https://web.archive.org/web/20080501072907/http://fromthemouthpieceonback.com/ ''From the Mouthpiece on Back'']. She also appears in [[Maxwell (musician)|Maxwell]]'s "Bad Habits" video. In 2009, Washington performed in ''[[The People Speak (film)|The People Speak]]'', a documentary feature film that uses dramatic and musical performances of the letters, diaries, and speeches of everyday Americans, based on historian [[Howard Zinn]]'s ''[[A People's History of the United States]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thepeoplespeak.com/pages/credits |title=the people speak |date=October 1, 2012|access-date=May 26, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100513003536/http://www.thepeoplespeak.com/pages/credits/|archive-date=May 13, 2010|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2010–2018: ''Scandal'' and critical acclaim===
In 2010, Washington made her [[Broadway theater|Broadway]] debut in the original production of [[David Mamet]]'s play ''[[Race (play)|Race]]'', alongside [[James Spader]] (with whom she worked on ''Boston Legal''), [[David Alan Grier]], and [[Richard Thomas (actor)|Richard Thomas]]. She also appeared as a part of the ensemble in [[Tyler Perry]]'s 2010 drama film ''[[For Colored Girls]]''.<ref name="Kerry"/>

She starred in [[Quentin Tarantino]]'s film ''[[Django Unchained]]'' (2012), which received widespread critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web |title=Django Unchained |url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/django-unchained |work=[[Metacritic]] |accessdate=May 15, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130102072136/http://www.metacritic.com/movie/django-unchained|archive-date=January 2, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://reservoirwatchdogs.com/2011/10/26/confirmed-kerry-washington-finally-joins-django-unchained-as-djangos-wife/ |title=Kerry Washington finally joins Django Unchained as Django's wife |date=October 26, 2011 |accessdate=October 1, 2012|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330120836/http://reservoirwatchdogs.com/2011/10/26/confirmed-kerry-washington-finally-joins-django-unchained-as-djangos-wife/ |archive-date=March 30, 2012}}</ref> She was invited to join the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] in June 2012 along with 175 other individuals.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.oscars.org/press/pressreleases/2012/20120629a.html |title=Academy Invites 176 to Membership |publisher=The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences |date=June 29, 2012 |accessdate=July 19, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120702000236/http://www.oscars.org/press/pressreleases/2012/20120629a.html|archive-date=July 2, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Activism===
In 2007, Washington and other celebrities joined for the 2007 [[Lee National Denim Day]], supporting the Women's Cancer Programs of the [[Entertainment Industry Foundation]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Reel Sistas Join the Fight Against Breast Cancer |url=http://www.reelsistas.com/2007/09/05/reel-sistas-join-the-fight-against-breast-cancer/ |accessdate=January 24, 2013 |date=September 5, 2007 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201234851/http://www.reelsistas.com/2007/09/05/reel-sistas-join-the-fight-against-breast-cancer/ |archive-date=February 1, 2014}}</ref> 
In September 2012, Washington spoke at the [[2012 Democratic National Convention|Democratic National Convention]] in favor of re-electing [[Barack Obama]], with her speech focusing on addressing voter apathy.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/09/06/kerry-washington-dnc-speech_n_1862985.html |title=Kerry Washington DNC Speech: Read The Democratic National Convention Remarks |work=Huffington Post |date=September 6, 2012 |accessdate=January 15, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120911025834/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/09/06/kerry-washington-dnc-speech_n_1862985.html|archive-date=September 11, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>  Washington has also used her celebrity to support voter registration drives<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-22|title=Kerry Washington teases ‘Scandal’ movie, but is it really happening?|url=https://www.radio.com/music/entertainment-news/kerry-washington-teases-scandal-movie-for-a-good-cause|access-date=2020-10-03|website=www.radio.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-22|title=Sorry, other celebrities.
