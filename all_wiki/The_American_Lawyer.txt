'''''The American Lawyer''''' is a monthly legal [[magazine]] and website published by [[ALM (company)|ALM]] Media. It was founded in 1979 by [[Steven Brill (journalist)|Steven Brill]].

In 1986, ''The American Lawyer'' purchased Review Business Publications of Miami, which included four southern Florida newspapers: ''Broward Review'', ''Palm Beach Review'', ''Miami Review'', and ''South Florida Medical Review''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sun-sentinel.com/news/fl-xpm-1986-01-10-8601020860-story.html |title=4 area journals sold to N.Y. firm |last=Altaner |first=David |date=January 10, 1986 |website=[[South Florida Sun-Sentinel]] |access-date=February 6, 2019}}</ref>

The publication also sponsors annual The American Lawyer Industry Awards.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.tampabaynewswire.com/2020/01/09/greenberg-traurig-named-florida-regional-litigation-department-of-the-year-by-the-american-lawyer-83290|title=Greenberg Traurig Named Florida Regional Litigation Department of the Year by The American Lawyer|website=www.tampabaynewswire.com|access-date=2020-02-13}}</ref>

==Publications==
The website of ''The American Lawyer'', Law.com, publishes daily headline news pertinent to the business of law.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.americanlawyer.com|title=The American Lawyer|work=americanlawyer.com}}</ref> Publications include:

* The "Am Law 100" and "Am Law 200" surveys rank United States [[law firm]]s by revenue per lawyer, compensation, profits per lawyer, profitability index, value per lawyer, profits per equity [[Partner (business rank)|partner]], and overall [[revenue]]<ref name=blumenthal2012PBJ>{{Citation |last=Blumenthal |first=Jeff |publication-date=May 4, 2012 |title=Per-lawyer revenue up |periodical=[[Philadelphia Business Journal]] |at=bizjournals.com |url=http://www.bizjournals.com/philadelphia/print-edition/2012/05/04/per-lawyer-revenue-up.html |accessdate=May 16, 2012 }}</ref>
*The Global 100 — annual ranking of the world's 100 largest law firms across the globe by overall revenue, by number of lawyers per firm, and by profits per equity partner
*The A-List — annual ranking of the most well-rounded, elite law firms in the United States. The A-List has recognized firms based on a combination of factors, both financial and cultural: revenue per lawyer, pro bono commitment, associate satisfaction and racial diversity, with RPL and pro bono given double weight. Also included since 2017 are scores for gender diversity — specifically, percentage of women equity partners
*Diversity Scorecard — an annual record of the average number of full-time-equivalent minority attorneys
* "The View From the Top" — annual poll of law firm [[chairperson]]s
* "Corporate Scorecard" — performance reports of U.S. law firms with the most active corporate finance and capital markets practices<ref>AmericanLawyer.com: [http://www.americanlawyer.com/aboutus.shtml About us]</ref>
