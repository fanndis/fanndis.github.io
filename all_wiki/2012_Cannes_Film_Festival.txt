The '''65th [[Cannes Film Festival]]''' was held from 16 to 27 May 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en.html |title=Festival de Cannes 2012 |accessdate=11 December 2011 |work=festival-cannes.com |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151016162039/http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/archives/2012/allSelections.html |archivedate=16 October 2015 }}</ref> Italian film director [[Nanni Moretti]] was the President of the Jury for the main competition<ref name="President">{{cite web |url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/movies/2012/01/cannes-jury-president-nanni-moretti.html |title=Nanni Moretti named 2012 Cannes jury president |accessdate=21 January 2012|work=LA Times}}</ref> and British actor [[Tim Roth]] was the President of the Jury for the [[Un Certain Regard]] section.<ref name="RothRegard">{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-17699698 |title=Tim Roth to lead Cannes Un Certain Regard jury |accessdate=13 April 2012|work=BBC News}}</ref> French actress [[Bérénice Bejo]] hosted the opening and closing ceremonies.<ref name="Bejo">{{cite web |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/cannes-film-festival/9211616/The-Artist-star-to-host-Cannes-film-ceremonies.html |title=''The Artist'' star to host Cannes film ceremonies |accessdate=19 April 2012 |work=The Telegraph}}</ref>

The festival opened with the US film ''[[Moonrise Kingdom]]'', directed by [[Wes Anderson]] and closed with the late [[Claude Miller]]'s final film ''[[Thérèse Desqueyroux (2012 film)|Thérèse Desqueyroux]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=18 April 2012 |url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/article/58882.html |title=Claude Miller's last film to close the Festival de Cannes |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120805002846/http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/article/58882.html |archivedate=5 August 2012  }}</ref> The main announcement of the line-up took place on 19 April. The official poster of the festival features [[Marilyn Monroe]], to mark the 50th anniversary of her death.<ref name="hollywoodreporter">{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cannes-poster-marilyn-monroe-film-festival-295738 |title=The iconic actress and sex symbol was selected in tribute to the 50th anniversary of her death. |accessdate=3 March 2012 |work=hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref>

The [[Palme d'Or]] was awarded to Austrian director [[Michael Haneke]] for his film ''[[Amour (2012 film)|Amour]]''. Haneke previously won the Palme d'Or in 2009 for ''[[The White Ribbon]]''. The jury gave the [[Grand Prix (Cannes Film Festival)|Grand Prize]] to [[Matteo Garrone]]'s ''[[Reality (2012 film)|Reality]]'', while [[Ken Loach]]'s ''[[The Angels' Share]]'' was awarded the [[Jury Prize (Cannes Film Festival)|Jury Prize]].

==Official selection==
The official selection was announced on 19 April at Grand Hôtel in Paris. Among comments after the announcement, journalists noted the unusually high number of Hollywood films in the line-up, the absence of any female director in the main competition, as well as the absence of competing first-time feature film directors.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bradshaw |first=Peter|authorlink=Peter Bradshaw |date=19 April 2012 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/filmblog/2012/apr/19/cannes-film-festival-2012-lineup |title=Cannes film festival 2012 lineup: the competition's still a man's world |work=guardian.co.uk |publisher=The Guardian|accessdate=20 April 2012}}</ref><ref name=fremauxthr>{{Cite web |last=Leffler |first=Rebecca |date=19 April 2012 |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cannes-film-festival-thierry-fremaux-qa-314227 |title=Cannes 2012: Thierry Fremaux on Increased Star Power and the Festival's Mission (Q&A) |work=The Hollywood Reporter |accessdate=20 April 2012}}</ref> The festival's artistic leader Thierry Frémaux responded that people should not focus only on the competition films: "The selection is an ensemble; you have to consider the whole package."<ref name=fremauxthr />

===Official awards===
The [[Palme d'Or]] was won by the French-language film ''[[Amour (2012 film)|Amour]]'' directed by [[Michael Haneke]]. Haneke previously won the award for ''[[The White Ribbon]]'' in [[2009 Cannes Film Festival|2009]].<ref name="LoveWins">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/cannes-film-festival/9293702/Cannes-2012-Michael-Hanekes-Love-wins-Palme-dOr.html|title=Cannes 2012: Michael Haneke's 'Love' wins Palme d'Or|date=27 May 2012|work=The Daily Telegraph |publisher=Telegraph Media Group |accessdate=27 May 2012}}</ref> ''Love'' tells the story of an elderly couple preparing for death.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/filmblog/2012/may/27/cannes-2012-michael-haneke-amour?newsfeed=true|title=Cannes 2012: Amour's love is justified, but why cold-shoulder Cosmopolis? |last=Brooks |first=Xan |date=27 May 2012 |work=The Guardian |publisher=Guardian Media Group |accessdate=27 May 2012}}</ref> During his acceptance speech, the director said "A very, very big thanks to my actors who have made this film. It's their film. They are the essence of this film."<ref name="LoveWins"/>

Moretti said that none of the winners had been selected unanimously, and described such an outcome as "a middle ground that would have pleased no one". He revealed that ''Holy Motors'', ''Paradise: Love'' and ''Post Tenebras Lux'' were the entries that most had divided the jury.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cineuropa.org/2011/nw.aspx?t=newsdetail&l=en&did=221697 |title=Nanni Moretti: 'No prize was agreed on unanimously' |last=Lemercier |first=Fabien |date=28 May 2012  |work=cineuropa.org |publisher=Cineuropa |accessdate=28 May 2012}}</ref>
