'''''The World's Billionaires''''' is an annual ranking by documented [[net worth]] of the wealthiest [[billionaire]]s in the world, compiled and published in March annually by the [[United States|American]] business magazine ''[[Forbes]]''. The list was first published in March 1987.<ref name=MW2014 /> The total net worth of each individual on the list is estimated and is cited in [[United States dollar]]s, based on their documented assets and accounting for debt and other factors. [[Royal family|Royalty]] and [[dictator]]s whose wealth comes from their positions are excluded from these lists.<ref name=AL2014/> This ranking is an index of the wealthiest documented individuals, excluding and ranking against those with wealth that is not able to be completely ascertained.<ref name="Why Isn’t on ‘Forbes’ List">{{cite web|url=http://www.newsweek.com/why-putin-isnt-forbes-billionaires-list-310818|title=Why Putin Isn't on 'Forbes' Billionaires List |work=[[Forbes]]}}</ref>

In 2018, there was a record of 2,208 people on the list, that included 259 newcomers mostly from [[China]] and the U.S.; there were 63 people under 40 and it had a record number of 256 women.<ref name="Forbes2018"/> The [[mean (average)|average]] net worth of the list came in at {{US$|4.1{{nbsp}}billion|link=yes}}, up {{US$|350{{nbsp}}million}} from 2017. Added together, the total net worth for 2018's billionaires was {{US$|9.1{{nbsp}}trillion}}, up from {{US$|7.67{{nbsp}}trillion}} in 2017.<ref name="Forbes2018"/><ref name="Forbes-2017" /><ref name="Forbes-list-2015" /> {{As of|2018}}, [[Microsoft]] founder [[Bill Gates]] had topped the list 18 of the past 24 years, while [[Amazon (company)|Amazon]] founder [[Jeff Bezos]] is ranked at the top for the first time and he became the first centibillionaire included in the ranking.<ref name="Forbes-2017">{{cite news|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/money/savingandinvesting/forbes-2017-billionaires-list-meet-the-richest-people-on-the-planet/ar-BByqKfX |title=Forbes 2017 billionaires list: Meet the richest people on the planet |last=Kroll|first=Luisa |work=Forbes |access-date=21 March 2017}}</ref> In 2017, [[Mark Zuckerberg]] was the only person in the top 10 billionaires list who is under the age of 50, and the only one in the top 20 billionaires list who is under the age of 40.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2017/08/01/mark-zuckerberg-is-less-than-half-the-age-of-his-super-wealthy-peers.html|title=Mark Zuckerberg is less than half the age of his super-wealthy peers|last=Shinal|first=John|date=2017-08-01|website=www.cnbc.com|access-date=2019-01-15}}</ref>

In 2017, 500 of the richest people in the world became richer by {{US$|1{{nbsp}}trillion}}, according to a report by [[Bloomberg News]].<ref name="BN-20171227">{{cite news |last1=Metcalf |first1=Tom |last2=Witzig |first2=Jack |title=World's Wealthiest Became $1 Trillion Richer in 2017 |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-27/world-s-wealthiest-gain-1-trillion-in-17-on-market-exuberance |date=27 December 2017 |work=[[Bloomberg News]] |access-date=27 December 2017 }}</ref><ref name="WP-20171227">{{cite news |last=Erickson |first=Amanda |title=The world's 500 wealthiest people got $1 trillion richer in 2017 |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/worldviews/wp/2017/12/27/the-worlds-500-wealthiest-people-got-1-trillion-richer-in-2017/ |date=27 December 2017 |work=[[The Washington Post]] |access-date=27 December 2017 }}</ref> According to a 2017 [[Oxfam]] report, the top eight billionaires own as much combined wealth as "the poorest half of the human race".<ref name="OXF-20170116">{{cite news |last=Ratcliff |first=Anna |title=Just 8 men own same wealth as half the world |url=https://www.oxfam.org/en/pressroom/pressreleases/2017-01-16/just-8-men-own-same-wealth-half-world |date=16 January 2017 |work=[[Oxfam]] |access-date=16 January 2017 }}</ref><ref name="NYT-20170116">{{cite news |last=Mullany |first=Gerry |title=World's 8 Richest Have as Much Wealth as Bottom Half of Global Population |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/16/world/eight-richest-wealth-oxfam.html |date=16 January 2017 |work=[[New York Times]] |access-date=16 January 2017 }}</ref>

==Methodology==
Each year, ''Forbes'' employs a team of over 50 reporters from a variety of countries to track the activity of the world's wealthiest individuals<ref name=methodology /> and sometimes groups or families - who share wealth. Preliminary surveys are sent to those who may qualify for the list. According to ''Forbes'', they received three types of responses&nbsp;– some people try to inflate their wealth, others cooperate but leave out details, and some refuse to answer any questions.<ref name=rferl2010 /> Business deals are then scrutinized and estimates of valuable assets&nbsp;– land, homes, vehicles, artwork, etc.&nbsp;– are made. Interviews are conducted to vet the figures and improve the estimate of an individual's holdings. Finally, positions in a publicly traded stock are priced to market on a date roughly a month before publication. Privately held companies are priced by the prevailing [[Price–sales ratio|price-to-sales]] or [[Price–earnings ratio|price-to-earnings]] ratios. Known debt is subtracted from assets to get a final estimate of an individual's estimated worth in United States dollars. Since stock prices fluctuate rapidly, an individual's true wealth and ranking at the time of publication may vary from their situation when the list was compiled.<ref name=methodology>{{cite journal |last=Dolan |first=Kerry A. |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/kerryadolan/2012/03/07/methodology-how-we-crunch-the-numbers/ |title=Methodology: How We Crunch The Numbers |journal=Forbes |date=7 March 2012 |access-date=4 January 2013}}</ref>

Family fortunes dispersed over a large number of relatives are included only if those individuals' holdings are worth more than a billion dollars. However, when a living individual has dispersed his or her wealth to immediate family members, it is included under a single listing provided that individual is still living.<ref name=methodology /> [[Royal family|Royal families]] and [[dictator]]s that have their wealth contingent on a position are always excluded from these lists.<ref>[https://www.forbes.com/2006/05/04/rich-kings-dictators_cz_lk_0504royals.html Fortunes Of Kings, Queens And Dictators] Forbes, 5 May 2006</ref>

==Annual rankings==
<span id="Annual listings"></span>
The rankings are published annually in March, so the net worths listed are snapshots taken at that time. These lists only show the top 10 wealthiest billionaires for each year.

===2018===
In the 32nd annual ''Forbes'' list of the world's billionaires, the aggregate wealth of the top 20 richest people on Earth amounted to about 13 percent of all billionaires' fortunes combined.<ref name="Forbes2018">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/luisakroll/2018/03/06/forbes-billionaires-2018-meet-the-richest-people-on-the-planet/|title= Forbes Billionaires 2018: Meet The Richest People On The Planet|first=Luisa|last=Kroll|work=Forbes|access-date=6 March 2018|date=6 March 2018}}</ref> A record of 2,208 billionaires were in the ranking and the total wealth was $9.1 trillion, up 18% since 2017. For the first time, [[Jeff Bezos]] was listed as the top billionaire due to Amazon's rising stock price that resulted in one person's biggest one-year gain in wealth ($35 billion) since ''Forbes'' started tracking in 1987.<ref name="Forbes2018"/> The U.S. had the most billionaires in the world, with 585, while China was catching up with 476 when including Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan; it had 372 when excluding those three places.<ref name="Forbes2018"/> Forbes excluded [[Al-Walid bin Talal]] and all other Saudi billionaires due to the absence of accurate wealth estimations as a result of the [[2017–19 Saudi Arabian purge]].<ref>[https://www.forbes.com/sites/kerryadolan/2018/03/06/no-saudi-arabian-billionaires-forbes-list-2018-alwaleed-alamoudi/#7ffdea9a1d42 Why No Saudi Arabians Made The Forbes Billionaires List This Year]</ref>

===2017===
On the 30th anniversary of the ''Forbes'' list of the world's billionaires, for the fourth year in a row, [[Bill Gates]] was named the richest man in the world.<ref name="Forbes-2017"/> In 2017, there was a record of 2,043 people on the list, which is the first time over 2,000 people were listed. This included 195 newcomers of whom 76 were from China and 25 from the U.S.; there were 56 people under 40 and it had a record of 227 women.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.forbes.com/billionaires/list/#version:static_tab:women |title=The World's Billionaires List |work=[[Forbes]] |date=23 March 2017 |access-date=21 March 2017}}</ref> The number of billionaires increased 13% to 2,043 from 1,810 in 2016; this was the biggest change in over 30 years of tracking billionaires globally.<ref name="Forbes-2017"/> Added together, the total net worth for 2017's billionaires was {{US$|7.67{{nbsp}}trillion}}, up from {{US$|7.1{{nbsp}}trillion}} in 2015.<ref name="Forbes-2017" /><ref name="Forbes-list-2015" /> This was the first time after 12 years that [[Carlos Slim]] was not within the top five. The U.S. had the most billionaires in the world, with a record of 565. China had 319 (not including Hong Kong, Taiwan or Macau), Germany had 114, and India had the fourth most with 101; India reached over 100 billionaires for its first time.

===2016===
For the third year in a row, Bill Gates was named the richest man in the world by ''Forbes''{{'}} 2016 list of the world's billionaires.<ref name="net worth">{{cite web|title=#1 Bill Gates|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/bill-gates/?list=billionaires|work=Forbes|access-date=2 January 2016}}</ref> This is the 17th time that the founder of Microsoft had claimed the top spot. [[Amancio Ortega]] rose from last year's position of number four to second.<ref name="forbes">{{cite web | url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/amancio-ortega/?list=billionaires | title=Amancio Ortega | work=[[Forbes]]| date=27 January 2016 | access-date=27 January 2016}}</ref> [[Warren Buffett]] of [[Berkshire Hathaway]] came in third for the second consecutive time,<ref>{{cite magazine| url = https://www.forbes.com/profile/warren-buffett/ | title = The World's Billionaires : Warren Buffett | magazine = Forbes | access-date = 3 November 2015}}</ref> while Mexican telecommunication mogul [[Carlos Slim]] slipped  from last year's second position to fourth. [[Jeff Bezos]] of [[Amazon (company)|Amazon]],<ref name="forbes.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/jeff-bezos/ |title=Jeff Bezos |work=Forbes |access-date=27 October 2015}}</ref> [[Mark Zuckerberg]] of [[Facebook]]<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/mark-zuckerberg/?list=billionaires| title=The World's Billionaires - Mark Zuckerberg|magazine=Forbes|date= 1 March 2016|access-date=21 March 2016}}</ref> and [[Michael Bloomberg]] of [[Bloomberg L.P.]],<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/michael-bloomberg/?list=billionaires| title=The World's Billionaires - Michael Bloomberg|magazine=Forbes|date= 1 March 2016|access-date=21 March 2016}}</ref> appeared for the first time on the ''Forbes'' top 10 billionaires list, coming at fifth, sixth and eighth positions, respectively. Zuckerberg became the youngest top 10 billionaire this year at the age of 31. [[Larry Ellison]], [[Charles Koch]] and [[David Koch]] also slipped from their previous year's positions, with Ellison dropping to seventh from fifth, and the Kochs falling to ninth position from sixth.

===2015===
{{main|The World's Billionaires 2015}}

In the 29th annual ''Forbes'' list of global billionaires, a record 1,826 billionaires were named with an aggregated net worth of $7.1 trillion compared to $6.4 trillion in the previous year. 46 of the billionaires in this list were under the age of 40. A record number of 290 people joined the list for the first time, of whom 25 percent hailed from China, which produced a world-leading 71 newcomers. The United States came in second, with 57; followed by India, with 28; and Germany, with 23.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Alexander|first=Dan|title=Record 290 Newcomers Join Forbes Billionaires List, Including Michael Jordan|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/danalexander/2015/03/02/record-290-newcomers-join-forbes-billionaires-list-including-michael-jordan|journal=[[Forbes]]|date=2 March 2015}}</ref> The United States had the largest number of billionaires with 526. [[Russia]] went down to 88 from 111 in 2014. Russia was placed behind [[China]], [[Germany]] and [[India]] by the number of billionaires. Self-made billionaires made up the largest number of people on the list with 1,191 positions (over 65 percent), while just 230 (under 13 percent) had wealth through inheritance. The number of billionaires who inherited a portion but were still working to increase their fortunes is 405.<ref>{{cite journal|title=The World's Billionaires|first=Kerry A.|last=Dolan|journal=[[Forbes]]|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/kerryadolan/2015/03/02/inside-the-2015-forbes-billionaires-list-facts-and-figures|access-date=2 March 2015}}</ref>

[[Bill Gates]] was named the richest man in the world by ''Forbes''<nowiki>'</nowiki> annual list of the world's billionaires. This was the 16th time that the founder of [[Microsoft]] claimed the top spot. [[Carlos Slim]] came in second for the second consecutive time. [[Warren Buffett]] of [[Berkshire Hathaway]] placed third, while [[Amancio Ortega]] of Spain, slipped down a position from the previous year to number four. [[Larry Ellison]], the founder of [[Oracle Corporation|Oracle]], rounded off the top five. [[Christy Walton]] was the highest-ranking female at number eight. America's [[Evan Spiegel]], co-founder of photo messaging app [[Snapchat]], became the youngest billionaire this year at age 24. At age 99, [[David Rockefeller]] maintained his position as the oldest billionaire included in the list.<ref>{{cite journal|title=The World's Billionaires|journal=[[Forbes]]|url=https://www.forbes.com/billionaires/list/#version:static|date=3 March 2015|access-date=2 March 2015}}</ref> [[Mark Zuckerberg]], the founder of Facebook, rose to number 16 with $33.4 billion. [[Iceland]] had a billionaire, [[Thor Bjorgolfsson]], in the list after a gap of five years. [[Guatemala]] had a billionaire, [[Mario Lopez Estrada]], for the first time in its history.<ref name=Forbes>{{cite web|title=Mario Lopez Estrada|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/mario-lopez-estrada/|website=Forbes: The World's Billionaires|access-date=19 August 2015}}</ref>

===2014===
{{main|The World's Billionaires 2014}}

Gates added $9 billion to his fortune since 2013 and topped the ''Forbes'' 2014 billionaire list. He had topped the list in 15 of the previous 20 years, but his previous number one ranking was in 2009.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Inside The 2014 Forbes Billionaires List: Facts And Figures |date=3 March 2014|journal=Forbes |first1=Kerry A.|last1=Dolan |first2=Luisa|last2=Kroll |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/luisakroll/2014/03/03/inside-the-2014-forbes-billionaires-list-facts-and-figures/ |access-date=2 June 2014}}</ref> Mexican telecommunication mogul [[Carlos Slim]] came in second place after being number one the previous four years. [[Zara (retailer)|Zara]] founder [[Amancio Ortega]] placed third for the second consecutive year. American investor [[Warren Buffett]] was in the top five for the 20th consecutive year, placing fourth.<ref name=CSM2014>{{cite news|title=Forbes' richest people: number of billionaires up significantly|first=Elizabeth|last=Barber|date=4 March 2014|work=Christian Science Monitor|url=http://www.csmonitor.com/USA/USA-Update/2014/0303/Forbes-richest-people-number-of-billionaires-up-significantly-video|access-date=4 March 2014}}</ref> America's [[Christy Walton]] was the highest ranking woman, placing ninth overall.<ref name=MW2014>{{cite news |title=Bill Gates back on top, WhatsApp founders and more women in Forbes' billionaire rankings |newspaper=Market Watch, [[The Wall Street Journal]] |date=4 March 2014 |first=Barbara|last=Kollmeyer |url=http://blogs.marketwatch.com/thetell/2014/03/04/bill-gates-back-on-top-whatsapp-founders-and-more-women-in-forbes-billionaire-rankings/ |access-date=5 March 2014}}</ref> [[Aliko Dangote]] of Nigeria became the first African to enter the top 25, with an estimated net worth of $25 billion.<ref name=AL2014>{{cite news|title=Youngest, oldest and one who can body slam you: More on Forbes list of the ultra-rich|first=Leada|last=Gore|date=4 March 2014|publisher=Alabama Media Group|website=AL.com|url=http://www.al.com/business/index.ssf/2014/03/youngest_oldest_and_one_who_ca.html|access-date=5 March 2014}}</ref>

A total of 1,645 people made the 2014 billionaire list, representing a combined wealth of $6.4 trillion.<ref name=CSM2014 /> Of those, a record 268 were newcomers, surpassing 2008's 226 newcomers.<ref name=MW2014 /> 100 people listed in 2013 failed to make the list.<ref name=CSM2014 /> The number of women on the list rose to a record 172 in 2014.<ref name=MW2014 /> Approximately 66 percent of the list were self-made, 13 percent achieved their wealth through inheritance alone, and 21 percent through a mixture of the two.<ref name=AL2014 />

The United States had 492 billionaires on the list, the most of any country.<ref name=CSM2014 /> It also had the most newcomers with 50, and women with 54.<ref name=MW2014 /> China had the second most billionaires with 152, while Russia was third with 111.<ref name=CSM2014 /> [[Algeria]], [[Lithuania]], [[Tanzania]], and [[Uganda]] were all represented on the list for the first time. [[Turkey]] saw the most people drop off the list, 19, due to a period of high inflation in the country.<ref name=AL2014 />

===2013===
{{main|The World's Billionaires 2013}}

Carlos Slim topped the 2013 billionaire list, marking his fourth consecutive year at the top. Gates remained in second, while Amancio Ortega moved up to third. Ortega's gain of $19.5 billion was the largest of anyone on the list.<ref name=Ind2013 /> Warren Buffett failed to make the top three for the first time since 2000, placing fourth. [[Diesel (brand)|Diesel]] founder [[Renzo Rosso]] was among the top newcomers, debuting with an estimate net worth of $3 billion.<ref name=MW2013 />

A global rise in asset prices led ''Forbes'' editor Randall Lane to declare "It [was] a very good year to be a billionaire". However, it was not a good year to be [[Eike Batista]], who fell from seventh to 100th, suffering the largest net loss of anyone on the list.<ref name=Ind2013 /> Overall, net gainers outnumbered net losers by 4:1.<ref name=MW2013>{{cite news|title=Forbes billionaire list 2013: Mr. Slim wins it again|date=4 March 2013|newspaper=Market Watch, [[Wall Street Journal]]|first=Barbara|last=Kollmeyer|url=http://blogs.marketwatch.com/thetell/2013/03/04/forbes-billionaire-list-2013-mr-slim-wins-it-again/|access-date=5 March 2014}}</ref>

A record total of 1,426 people made the 2013 list, representing $5.4 trillion of assets.<ref name=Reuters2013 /> Of those, 442 billionaires hailed from the United States.<ref name=Ind2013>{{cite news|title=2013 Forbes Billionaires list: Record number of new entries appear on rich list, but Carlos Slim and Bill Gates still top the charts|work=The Independent|date=4 March 2013|first=Martin|last=Hickman|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/business/news/2013-forbes-billionaires-list-record-number-of-new-entries-appear-on-rich-list-but-carlos-slim-and-bill-gates-still-top-the-charts-8519568.html|access-date=5 March 2014}}</ref> The Asian-Pacific region had 386 billionaires and Europe 366. The list also featured a record number of newcomers, 210, representing 42 countries. 60 people from the 2012 list fell below a billion dollars of assets in 2013, and eight others from the 2012 list died. The Asia-Pacific region had the most drop-offs, with 29, followed by the United States with 16. The 2013 list featured 138 women, of which 50 came from the United States. A majority of the list (961 individuals, 67 percent) were entirely self-made; 184 (13 percent) inherited their wealth, and 281 (20 percent) achieved their fortune through a combination of inheritance and business acumen.<ref name=Reuters2013>{{cite news|title=Factbox: Record number of billionaires make Forbes 2013 list|date=4 March 2013|agency=Reuters|first=Patricia|last=Reaney|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2013/03/04/us-billionaires-list-factbox-idUSBRE9230EJ20130304|access-date=5 March 2014}}</ref> [[Vietnam]]'s [[Phạm Nhật Vượng]] was the first person from that country to be included in this list.

===2012===
{{main|The World's Billionaires 2012}}

Carlos Slim topped the 2012 list, marking this third consecutive year at the top. Gates placed second but narrowed the gap from 2011 as Slim's fortune fell $5 billion while Gates' rose $5 billion. Warren Buffett remained in third place. [[Bernard Arnault]] of France was the top-ranking European on the list, placing fourth.<ref name=Telegraph2012 /> [[Ricardo Salinas Pliego]] was the greatest gainer in terms of dollars, adding $9.2 billion to his fortune and moving up to number 37 overall.<ref name=Telegraph2012 /> Making her debut on the list at age 27, [[Spanx]] founder [[Sara Blakely]] became the youngest self-made female billionaire ever.<ref>{{cite news|title=Spanx creator Sara Blakely becomes youngest woman on Forbes Billionaires List|work=[[Fox News Channel]]|date=8 March 2012|url=http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2012/03/08/spanx-creator-sara-blakely-becomes-youngest-woman-on-forbes-billionaires-list/|access-date=8 March 2014}}</ref> Colombia's [[Alejandro Santo Domingo]] was the highest-ranked newcomer, inheriting a $9.5 billion stake in [[Santo Domingo Group]] from his father.<ref name=Telegraph2012>{{cite news|title=Forbes rich list 2012: Carlos Slim tops rich list for third year|work=The Telegraph|date=8 March 2012|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/9130301/Forbes-rich-list-2012-Carlos-Slim-tops-rich-list-for-third-year.html|access-date=8 March 2014}}</ref> India's [[Lakshmi Mittal]] was the largest loser as his fortune dropped from $31.1 billion to $20.7 billion as the price of steelmaker [[ArcelorMittal]] fell sharply.<ref name=Telegraph2012 /> As a result, he failed to make the top 10 for the first time since 2004 and lost his title of richest Asian to Hong Kong's [[Li Ka-shing]].<ref name=Forbes2012 />

A record total of 1,226 people made the 2012 list, representing 58 countries. Of those, 126 were newcomers to the list and 104 were women. The United States had the greatest number of billionaires with 425. Russia had 96 people on the list, while China had 95.<ref name=Telegraph2012 /> Georgia, [[List of Moroccan billionaires by net worth|Morocco]], and Peru were newly represented on the list. Falling stock prices in Asia contributed to 117 former billionaires falling from the list worldwide. Twelve others listed in 2011 died. Overall, net gainers (460) barely outnumbered net losers (441).<ref name=Forbes2012>{{cite journal|title=Forbes World's Billionaires 2012|journal=Forbes|first=Luisa|last=Kroll|date=7 March 2014|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/luisakroll/2012/03/07/forbes-worlds-billionaires-2012/|access-date=8 March 2014}}</ref>

To coincide with the release of the 2012 list, ''Forbes'' announced a then-new "Billionaire Real-Time Ticker" updating the wealth of the world's top 50 billionaires in real time.<ref name=Telegraph2012 />

===2011===
{{main|The World's Billionaires 2011}}

In the 25th annual ''Forbes'' list of global billionaires, Slim added $20.5 billion to his fortune, the most of anyone, and retained his number one ranking with a total fortune of $74 billion. Gates remained in second place with $56 billion, while Warren Buffett was third with $50 billion. The top 10 had a combined wealth of $406 billion, up from $342 billion in 2010.{{citation needed|date=February 2020}} According to ''Forbes'' editor Kerry Dolan, "media and technology billionaires definitely benefited from a stronger stock market and a growing enthusiasm for all things social" since the 2010 list.<ref name=Hollywood2011 /> However, Nigerian commodity mogul Aliko Dangote was the greatest gainer on a percentage basis as his fortune increased 557 percent to $13.5 billion. Mark Zuckerberg was one of seven Facebook-related billionaires on the list, as he added $9.5 billion to his net worth to move up to 52nd. Facebook co-founder [[Dustin Moskovitz]] was the youngest person on the list. Aged 26, eight days younger than Zuckerberg, he debuted at number 420 with an estimated fortune of $2.7 billion. [[IKEA]] founder [[Ingvar Kamprad]] was the largest loser as he saw his fortune plummet from $23 billion to $6 billion, dropping him from 11th to 162nd overall.{{citation needed|date=February 2020}}

A record 1,210 billionaires made the 2011 list, representing a combined wealth of $4.5 trillion, up from $3.6 trillion the previous year.<ref name=Syracuse2011>{{cite news|title=Forbes' 2011 list of 'World's Billionaires' is biggest ever|work=Syracuse Post-Standard|first=Geoff|last=Herbert|date=10 March 2011|url=http://www.syracuse.com/news/index.ssf/2011/03/forbes_2011_list_of_worlds_bil.html|access-date=15 March 2014}}</ref> One third of the world's billionaires, 413, came from the United States. China had the second most billionaires with 115, while Russia was third with 101. Asia moved up to 332 billionaires, passing Europe as a region for the first time since the 1990s. ===2010===
{{main|The World's Billionaires 2010}}

Slim narrowly eclipsed Gates to top the billionaire list for the first time. Slim saw his estimated worth surge $18.5 billion to $53.5 billion as shares of [[America Movil]] rose 35 percent. Gates' estimated wealth rose $13 billion to $53 billion, placing him second. Warren Buffett was third with $47 billion.<ref name=Forbes2010>{{cite journal|title=Bill Gates No Longer World's Richest Man|first1=Matthew|last1=Miller|first2=Luisa|last2=Kroll |date=10 March 2010|journal=Forbes|url=https://www.forbes.com/2010/03/09/worlds-richest-people-slim-gates-buffett-billionaires-2010-intro.html|access-date=25 March 2014}}</ref> [[Christy Walton]] was the highest-ranking woman, placing 12th overall, with an inherited fortune of $22.5 billion.<ref name=ChinaDaily2010>{{cite news|title=China ranks No 2 on Forbes billionaires list|date=11 March 2010|work=China Daily|url=http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/business/2010-03/11/content_9575101.htm|access-date=17 March 2014}}</ref> At age 25, Mark Zuckerberg continued to be the world's youngest self-made billionaire.<ref name=rferl2010 /> American [[Isaac Perlmutter]] was among the newcomers with an estimated fortune of $4 billion largely acquired in his sale of [[Marvel Entertainment]] to [[Disney]].<ref name=Forbes2010 />

A total of 1,011 people made the 2010 list. The United States accounted for 403 billionaires, followed by China with 89 and Russia with 62.<ref name=ChinaDaily2010 /> It was the first time China, while including Hong Kong, placed second.<ref name=Forbes2010 /> A total of 55 countries were represented on the 2010 list, including Finland and Pakistan which claimed their first billionaires.<ref name=ChinaDaily2010 /> Eighty-nine women made the list, but only 14 of them were self-made.<ref name=ChinaDaily2010 /> The combined net worth of the list was $3.6 trillion, up 50 percent from 2009's $2.4 trillion, while the average net worth was $3.5 billion.<ref name=rferl2010>{{cite news|title=Forbes' Rich List: Number Of New Billionaires Reflects Global Recovery|work=Radio Free Europe Radio Liberty|first=Nikola|last=Krastev|date=11 March 2010|url=http://www.rferl.org/content/Forbes_Rich_List_Number_Of_New_Billionaires_Reflects_Global_Recovery/1980413.html|access-date=25 March 2014}}</ref>

The 2010 list featured 164 re-entries and 97 true newcomers.<ref name=rferl2010 /> Asia accounted for more than 100 of the new entrants. Overall, just 12 percent of the list lost wealth since 2009, and 30 people fell off the list. 13 others died.<ref name=Forbes2010 /> Of the 89 women, 12 were newcomers in 2010. [[Steve Forbes]] said the growing number of billionaires was a clear sign that the world's economy was recovering from 2009's global financial crisis.<ref name=rferl2010 />

In June 2010, Gates and Buffett announced [[the Giving Pledge]], a promise to give the majority of their wealth to [[philanthropy|philanthropic causes]]. As of 2017, the pledge had 158 signatories, but some of the signatories have since died. Most of the signers of the pledge are billionaires, and their pledges total over $365 billion.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.insatiablefox.com/data-blog/the-giving-pledge-list|title=The Giving Pledge List Reaches $365 Billion|website=Insatiable Fox|access-date=23 April 2016}}</ref>

===2000===
Gates became the first American to take the top spot of the world's billionaires in 1995 with a net worth of $12.5 billion, and he remained there during the [[dot-com bubble]]'s height in 1999 when his fortune peaked at $90 billion. After the dot-com bubble started to collapse in 2000, his wealth dropped to $60 billion, although he remained at the top of the list.

==Statistics==
The [[dot-com bubble]] created the most [[paper wealth]] for some billionaires. However, once the dotcom bubble burst the new rich saw their fortunes disappear.<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.economist.com/node/654053 |title=Special Report on The New Rich |journal=[[The Economist]] |date=14 June 2001 }}</ref> Billionaires' fortunes were hit even harder by the [[Late-2000s financial crisis|global financial crisis]]; 2009 was the first time in five years that the world had a net loss in the number of billionaires.<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.economist.com/node/13356686 |title=Special Report on The Rich |journal=[[The Economist]] |date=4 April 2009 }}</ref> The strong performance of the [[financial market]]s and global economic recovery have erased [[financial asset]]s losses.
