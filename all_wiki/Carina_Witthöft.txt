'''Carina Witthöft''' (also spelled Witthoeft, born 16 February 1995<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.welt.de/print/wams/vermischtes/article13803813/Mit-Talent-und-Willen-nach-oben.html|title=Mit Talent und Willen nach oben|first=Christian|last=Görtzen|work=[[Welt am Sonntag]]|date=8 January 2012|accessdate=11 March 2013|language=German}}</ref>) is a German professional [[tennis]] player. Witthöft has won one [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] singles title whereas on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]], she has won eleven singles titles and one doubles title. On 8 January 2018, she reached her best singles ranking of world No. 48.

==Career==
Witthöft made her [[WTA Tour]] debut at the [[2012 Swedish Open]]. Having defeated [[Marina Shamayko]], [[Akgul Amanmuradova]] and [[Jill Craybas]] to qualify, she lost in the first round of the main draw against [[Kateryna Bondarenko]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/posting/2012/1048/MDS.pdf|title=2012 Sony Swedish Open – main draw singles|work=[[Women's Tennis Association]]|date=22 July 2012|accessdate=11 March 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121023025518/http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/posting/2012/1048/MDS.pdf#|archive-date=2012-10-23|url-status=dead}}</ref>

She won her maiden WTA title at the [[2017 BGL Luxembourg Open|2017 Luxembourg Open]], defeating [[Monica Puig]] in straight sets in the final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rp-online.de/sport/tennis/carina-witthoeft-feiert-in-luxemburg-ersten-sieg-auf-wta-tour-aid-1.7158927|title=Witthöft feiert ersten Sieg auf WTA-Tour|work=RP Online|date=21 October 2017|language=German}}</ref>

She was coached by [[Torben Beltz]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abendblatt.de/sport/welt-des-sports/article125731367/Carina-Witthoeft-greift-mit-Ex-Kerber-Trainer-Beltz-neu-an.html|title=Carina Witthöft greift mit Ex-Kerber-Trainer Beltz neu an|work=[[Hamburger Abendblatt]]|date=12 March 2014|accessdate=24 March 2014|language=German}}</ref> Her current coach is Philip Lang.

===2018===
Witthöft started her 2018 season at the [[2018 Brisbane International – Women's Singles|Brisbane International]]. She lost in the first round to Aleksandra Krunić.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sevastova stuns Stosur in Brisbane opener |url=https://www.dailytrust.com.ng/sevastova-stuns-stosur-in-brisbane-opener.html |accessdate=13 July 2020 |publisher=www.dailytrust.com.ng |date=1 January 2018}}</ref> Despite being defeated in the final round of qualifying at the [[2018 Sydney International – Women's Singles|Sydney International]] by Camila Giorgi, Witthöft entered the main draw as a lucky loser.<ref>{{cite news |title=Julia Goerges Withdraws From Sydney International After Auckland Win |url=https://sports.ndtv.com/tennis/julia-goerges-withdraws-from-sydney-international-after-auckland-win-1796926 |accessdate=14 July 2020 |publisher=sports.ndtv.com |date=7 January 2018}}</ref> She lost in the first round to Australian wildcard [[Samantha Stosur|Sam Stosur]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Walton |first1=Darren |title=Sam Stosur wins tough opener at Sydney International |url=https://www.smh.com.au/sport/tennis/sam-stosur-wins-tough-opener-at-sydney-international-20180109-h0fnlb.html |accessdate=14 July 2020 |publisher=www.smh.com.au |date=9 January 2018}}</ref> At the [[2018 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], Witthöft was defeated in the first round by eighth seed Caroline Garcia.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kane |first1=David |title=Garcia grinds past Witthoeft at Australian Open |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1444900/garcia-grinds-past-witthoeft-at-australian-open |accessdate=14 July 2020 |publisher=www.wtatennis.com |date=16 January 2018}}</ref>

In Qatar at the [[2018 Qatar Total Open – Singles|Qatar Total Open]], Witthöft lost in the second round to top seed Caroline Wozniacki.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Livaudais |first1=Stephanie |title=Wozniacki impresses against Witthoeft in Doha |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1444346/wozniacki-impresses-against-witthoeft-in-doha |accessdate=14 July 2020 |publisher=www.wtatennis.com |date=14 February 2018}}</ref>
