==Biography==
Serhiy Taruta was born in [[Mariupol]] in 1955 and was a successful cyclist in his youth; he later claimed that he rode an average of 120 kilometres a day.<ref name="forbes.net.ua">{{cite web|title = Сергей Тарута. История бизнесмена-мечтателя|url = http://forbes.net.ua/magazine/forbes/1345023-sergej-taruta-istoriya-biznesmena-mechtatelya|website = forbes.net.ua|access-date = 2016-01-11}}</ref> He studied at [[State Higher Education Institution “Pryazovskyi State Technical University”|Pryazovskyi State Technical University]]<ref>{{cite web|title = Тарута Сергей|url = http://file.liga.net/person/289-sergei-taryta.html|website = ЛІГА.Досье|access-date = 2016-01-11}}</ref> before going on to work at the large Ukrainian metal plant, [[Azovstal iron and steel works|Azovstal]].<ref name="forbes.net.ua"/>

==Business career==
In 1995, Serhiy Taruta left Azovstal for "Azovintex", a foreign trade company he co-founded. In December, 1995 he was appointed the CEO of the [[Industrial Union of Donbass Corporation]] (ISD Corporation), which he co-founded. Between 2001 and 2014, Taruta served as the Chairman of the Board of ISD Corporation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://forbes.net.ua/magazine/forbes/1345023-sergej-taruta-istoriya-biznesmena-mechtatelya |title=Сергей Тарута. История бизнесмена-мечтателя |publisher= |website=forbes.net.ua |date= |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref>{{Failed verification|date=August 2017}}

==Political career==
In 1998 and 2002 Serhiy Taruta was elected a member of the Donetsk Regional City Council for [[Telmanove Raion|Telmanovsky]] electoral district, Donetsk Region.{{Citation needed|date=August 2017}}

On 2 March 2014, amidst the [[2014 pro-Russian conflict in Ukraine]], acting President [[Oleksandr Turchynov]] appointed Taruta Governor of [[Donetsk Oblast]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/03/world/europe/ukraine-turns-to-its-oligarchs-for-political-help.html|author=Andrew E. Kramer|title=Ukraine Turns to Its Oligarchs for Political Help|newspaper=The New York Times|date=March 2, 2014|accessdate=March 11, 2014|archivedate=March 3, 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140303113756/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/03/world/europe/ukraine-turns-to-its-oligarchs-for-political-help.html|url-status=live}}</ref> On 10 October 2014, President [[Petro Poroshenko]] removed him from office,<ref>{{cite web|author=Login |url=http://www.kyivpost.com/content/ukraine/poroshenko-fires-taruta-as-donetsk-oblast-governor-367625.html |title=Sources: Poroshenko fires Taruta as Donetsk Oblast governor |publisher=KyivPost |date=2014-10-10 |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref><ref>[http://www.dw.de/putin-and-poroshenko-to-meet-in-milan/a-17988694 Putin and Poroshenko to meet in Milan] - [[Deutsche Welle]], 11 October 2014</ref> replacing him with [[Oleksandr Kikhtenko]].<ref>[http://www.president.gov.ua/en/news/31371.html Petro Poroshenko presented new Head of Donetsk RSA Oleksandr Kikhtenko] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150127152853/http://www.president.gov.ua/en/news/31371.html |date=2015-01-27 }} - Press office of President of Ukraine, 10 October 2014</ref><ref>[http://www.businessinsider.com/afp-ukraine-president-replaces-donetsk-governor-with-hardline-general-2014-10 Ukraine president replaces Donetsk governor with hardline general]  - Business Insider, 10 October 2014</ref>

In the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election]], as an independent candidate Taruta won a [[single-member district]] in [[Mariupol]] with 60.00% of the vote and is thus a member of the [[Ukrainian parliament]] since 27 November 2014.<ref>[http://www.nrcu.gov.ua/en/148/583500/ CEC registers 357 newly elected deputies of 422] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141126114422/http://www.nrcu.gov.ua/en/148/583500/ |date=2014-11-26 }}, [[National Radio Company of Ukraine]] (25 November 2014)<br />[http://www.unian.info/politics/1014078-parliament-to-form-leadership-and-coalition-on-november-27.html Parliament to form leadership and coalition on November 27], [[UNIAN]] (26 November 2014)</ref><ref name="winner for the seat in constituency 58">{{in lang|uk}} [http://vibori2014.rbc.ua/ukr/okrug/donetskaya-oblast/odnomandatny-58 Candidates and winner for the seat in constituency 58 in the 2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election], [[RBK Ukraine]]</ref>

Taruta leads the Group for Inter-parliamentary Ties between Ukraine and [[Germany]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://w1.c1.rada.gov.ua/pls/mpz2/organizations.dep_group_members?grp=8 |title=Офіційний портал Верховної Ради України |publisher=w1.c1.rada.gov.ua |date= |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref>

He is also the Head of the Sub-Committee for Protection of Historic and Cultural Heritage of the Parliamentary Committee for Culture and Spiritual Affairs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://w1.c1.rada.gov.ua/pls/site2/p_komity?pidid=2631 |title=Офіційний портал Верховної Ради України |publisher= |website=w1.c1.rada.gov.ua |date= |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref>

Taruta is the co-chair of the Parliamentary Platform for the Future of Donbass, a body that aims to develop new legislative initiatives to help solve problems related to the reintegration of occupied territories of the region.<ref>{{cite web|author=2016-10-31T18:27+02:00 18:27 31.10.2016 |url=http://interfax.com.ua/news/political/380522.html |title=Тарута: парламентская платформа "Будущее Донбасса" - это шаг к реинтеграции региона |publisher=Interfax.com.ua |date=2016-10-31 |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref>

He is the author of the Three Pillars peace plan for Donbass,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://zn.ua/internal/mirnyy-plan-dlya-donbassa-tri-osnovy-legitimnost-bezopasnost-doverie-_.html |title=Мирный план для Донбасса — Три основы: Легитимность — Безопасность — Доверие - Внутренняя политика Украины – аналитика, статьи, эксклюзивы |publisher= |website=zn.ua |date=2017-02-10 |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref> and also proposed the creation of the Vienna Format for Donbass peace talks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://newsone.ua/ru/taruta-predlozhil-perejti-s-minskogo-na-venskij-format/ |title=Тарута предложил альтернативу Минскому формату &#124; NewsONE |publisher= |website=newsone.ua |date=2017-06-19 |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref>

Taruta led the group of developers of Sustainable Development Doctrine Ukraine 2030.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kpi.ua/en/node/14455 |title=Presentation of Sustainable Development Doctrine "Ukraine 2030" &#124; Igor Sikorsky Kyiv Polytechnic Institute |publisher= |website=kpi.ua |date=2017-05-25 |accessdate=2017-09-06}}</ref>

Serhiy Taruta is a founder of the [[political party "Osnova"]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/202104 |title=Osnova: Taruta's political foundation |publisher= |website=[[The Ukrainian Week]] |date=18 October 2017 |accessdate=22 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pravda.com.ua/rus/news/2017/04/9/7140733/ |script-title=ru:Тарута заявил о создании новой партии &#124; Украинска правда |publisher= |website=[[Ukrainska Pravda]] |date=2017-04-09 |accessdate=2017-09-06|language=ru}}</ref>

On 22 September 2018, [[Osnova (political party)|Osnova]] nominated Taruta as the party's candidate for the [[2019 Ukrainian presidential election]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2018/09/22/7192904/ Taruta's party sent him to the presidency], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (22 September 2018)</ref> On 16 March (2 weeks and one day before the election) Taruta pledged his campaign-team would support fellow  candidate [[Yulia Tymoshenko]]'s campaign efforts.<ref name="Taruta573095ST" /> However, his name was not taken of the ballot.<ref name="Taruta573095ST">{{Cite web|url=https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/573095.html|title=Taruta announces support for Tymoshenko at presidential elections|website=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|access-date=2019-03-16}}</ref> (7 March was the last day when candidates could withdraw their names from the ballot.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2019/03/7/7208642/ Another candidate appeared in favor of Gritsenko], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (7 March 2019)</ref>)

In the July [[2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] Taruta was placed second on the party list of [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"|Fatherland]].<ref>https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2019/06/10/7217645/</ref> He was elected to parliament.<ref>[https://www.ukrinform.net/rubric-elections/2748306-cec-counts-100-percent-of-vote-in-ukraines-parliamentary-elections.html CEC counts 100 percent of vote in Ukraine's parliamentary elections], [[Ukrinform]] (26 July 2019)<br />{{in lang|ru}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/rus/articles/2019/07/21/7221526/ Results of the extraordinary elections of the People's Deputies of Ukraine 2019], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (21 July 2019)</ref>
