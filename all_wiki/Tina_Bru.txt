'''Tina Bru''' (born 18 April 1986) is a Norwegian politician for the [[Conservative Party (Norway)|Conservative Party]]. Since 2020, she has served as the Minister of Petroleum and Energy.<ref name=[[NRK]]>{{cite web |url=https://www.nrk.no/rogaland/tina-bru-blir-klappa-inn-av-oljenaeringa_-_-har-god-innsikt-1.14874236 | publisher= [[NRK]] | title=Tina Bru Is Clapped In By The Petroleum Industry | date= 24 January 2020| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20200613124341/https://www.nrk.no/rogaland/tina-bru-blir-klappa-inn-av-oljenaeringa_-_-har-god-innsikt-1.14874236 | archive-date= 13 June 2020}}</ref> She was elected to the [[Storting]] from [[Rogaland]] in 2013 and became a member of the [[Standing Committee on Energy and the Environment]].<ref name =storting>{{cite web |url=https://www.stortinget.no/no/Representanter-og-komiteer/Representantene/Representantfordeling/Representant/?perid=TIB | title=Bru, Tina ( 1986-) |publisher= Stortinget.no. |language=Norwegian |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20200613124946/https://www.stortinget.no/no/Representanter-og-komiteer/Representantene/Representant/?perid=TIB |archive-date= 13 June 2020 }}</ref> She was reelected to the Storting for the period 2017&ndash;2021, and continued as a member of the Standing Committee on Energy and the Environment.<ref name =storting/>

==Career==
===Minister===
Bru was appointed Minister of Petroleum and Energy following the [[Progress Party (Norway)|Progress Party]]'s withdrawal from government in January 2020. There had been huge expectations for her appointment and it was well received in the petroleum and energy branch.<ref>{{cite web | url= https://www.tk.no/nyheter/oslo/regjeringen/bade-oljenaring-og-miljoorganisasjoner-positive-til-ny-statsrad/s/5-51-759079 | title= Both petroleum sector and environmental organisations positive for the new minister | language= Norwegian | date= 24 January 2020 | accessdate= 10 September 2020 | publisher= Tidens Krav}}</ref>

While attending an opening for offshore wind, she was hassled by protesters and blocked from entering a meeting in [[Rogaland]]. To the press, she expressed discomfort about the entire situation. She took to [[Facebook]] to express what she had been called during the opening. The general secretary for ''Motvind Norge'', Rune Haaland, expressed shock for the way protesters had treated Bru. He stated that most protesters only use their free will of democracy to protst, while also pointing out that the protesters in [[Haugesund]] where not a part of his organisation.<ref>{{cite web | url= https://www.nrk.no/rogaland/vindkraftmotstandere-gikk-til-verbalt-angrep-mot-olje--og-energiminister-tina-bru-1.15052315 | title= Tina Bru: I was called a b***h | language= Norwegian | publisher= NRK | date= 13 June 2020 | accessdate= 15 June 2020}}</ref>

===Party politics===
In February 2020, after being suggested as new deputy leader of the party, she was subsequently installed as second deputy leader, succeeding outgoing [[Bent Høie]] who was nominated to be county governor of Rogaland commencing in 2021.<ref>{{cite web | url= https://hoyre.no/aktuelt/nyheter/2020/tina-bru-foreslas-som-ny-nestleder/ | title= Tina Bru is suggested as new Conservative deputy leader | language= Norwegian | date= 5 February 2020 | accessdate= 10 September 2020 | publisher= hoyre.no}}</ref> She was subsequently elected second deputy leader in September 2020 unopposed.<ref>{{cite web | url= https://www.aftenbladet.no/lokalt/i/aP27gE/tina-bru-klappet-inn-som-bent-hies-arvtaker-i-hyre-ledelsen | title= Tina Bru clapped in as Bent Høie's heir in the Conservative leadership | language= Norwegian | publisher= [[Stavanger Aftenblad]] | date= 11 September 2020 | accessdate= 11 September 2020}}</ref>

==Early and personal life==
Bru was born in [[Moss, Norway|Moss]] on 18 April 1986, a daughter of Sigve Bru and Sissel Skalstad Bru. She has a [[bachelor's degree]] in management, with further specialisation in [[change management]].<ref name =storting/>
