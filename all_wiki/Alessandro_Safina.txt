'''Alessandro Safina''' ({{IPA-it|alesˈsandro saˈfina}}) is an Italian [[operatic pop]] [[tenor]].

Born in [[Siena]], Italy, Safina has sought to combine his interests in [[opera]] and modern [[pop music]].{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}

== Music education ==
Safina began to study music when he was nine years old and attended a music academy at the age of 12. At 17, he was accepted to the Conservatorio di Musica "Luigi Cherubini" in Florence, Italy.

In 1989, he won the "Concorso Lirico Internazionale" in Manatova, Italy, and this led to his opera debut. In 1990 he appeared with soprano [[Katia Ricciarelli]] as [[Rodolfo]] in [[Giacomo Puccini|Puccini]]'s ''[[La bohème]]''.{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}

== Career ==

In the '90s, Safina began recording with Italian pianist/composer Romano Musumarra and his self-titled debut was issued in September 2001. Safina's single "Luna", released in 1999, reached number 2 in the Dutch charts.<ref name="Luna">{{cite web |url=http://www.dutchcharts.nl/showitem.asp?interpret=Alessandro+Safina&titel=Luna&cat=s |title=Alessandro Safina – "Luna" |accessdate=4 October 2009}}</ref>

In 2001, Safina contributed to the score of [[Baz Luhrmann]]'s film ''[[Moulin Rouge!]]'' where he performed [[Elton John]]'s "[[Your Song]]" with [[Ewan McGregor]], and a part of the Elephant Love Medley, sung by Ewan McGregor and [[Nicole Kidman]].{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} That same year, his song Luna was part of the soundtrack of the soap opera ''[[O Clone]]'' from Rede Globo, Safina, also made a guest appearance in the soap opera. In November 2001, Safina sang [[Andrew Lloyd Webber]]'s "[[Music of the Night]]"<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://us.hellomagazine.com/profiles/alessandro-safina|title=Alessandro Safina. Biography, news, photos and videos|website=us.hellomagazine.com|language=English|access-date=2017-08-30}}</ref> for [[Queen Elizabeth II]] during the [[Royal Variety Show]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.royalvarietycharity.org/royal-variety-performance/archive/detail/2001-london-dominion|title=Performances :: 2001, London Dominion Variety|website=www.royalvarietycharity.org|language=en|access-date=2017-08-30}}</ref>

By 2002, Safina's album ''Insieme A Te'' sold 700,000 copies worldwide.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.encyclopedia.com/education/news-wires-white-papers-and-books/safina-alessandro|title=Safina, Alessandro - Dictionary definition of Safina, Alessandro {{!}} Encyclopedia.com|website=www.encyclopedia.com|language=en|access-date=2017-08-30}}</ref> 
In March, 2002, he performed "Del Perduto Amore" at the [[San Remo Music Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.operaandballet.com/?perfomance=3689&page=catalog%20San%20Remo|title=Alessandro Safina (tenor) (Concert) - OperaAndBallet.com|website=www.operaandballet.com|access-date=2017-08-30}}</ref> He also recorded "[[Your Song]]" with [[Elton John]] for [[Sport Relief]], a charity venture to help children.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.advocate.com/arts-entertainment/entertainment-news/2002/05/02/elton-john-rerecords-quotyour-songquot-charity-4131|title=Elton John rerecords &quot;Your Song&quot; for charity (4131)|date=2002-05-02|access-date=2017-08-30|language=en}}</ref>
Safina's concert "Only You", featuring [[Patricia Manterola]], was taped in the amphitheatre El Greco in [[Taormina]], [[Sicily]] in 2001, and was later broadcast in the series ''[[Great Performances]]'' by American public channel [[PBS]] and released in 2003 on [[DVD]]. Also in 2003, Safina performed the English and Korean vocals for a Korean song called "Hamangyeon" (하망연(何茫然)), part of the soundtrack for ''[[Dae Jang Geum]]'', and he appeared as [[Mario Cavaradossi]] in ''Tosca e Altre Due'', a film based on the opera ''[[Tosca]]'' by Puccini.

In 2007, he recorded a duet with the British soprano [[Sarah Brightman]] for her album, ''Symphony''. He then joined her for the Symphony World Tour in 2008 where he sang "[[Canto della Terra]]" and "[[Sarai Qui]]" with Brightman.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.purepeople.com/article/sarah-brightman-et-alessandro-safina-quel-couple-magnifique_a27017/1|title=Sarah Brightman et Alessandro Safina : quel couple magnifique !|access-date=2017-08-30|language=fr}}</ref>

In June 2017, Safina performed "[[O Sole Mio]]" with Italian tenor [[Andrea Bocelli]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ut.uz/en/culture/for-the-first-time-andrea-bocelli-and-alessandro-safina-performed-in-one-concert-and-all-of-it-held-/|title=For the first time, Andrea Bocelli and Alessandro Safina performed in one concert. == Personal life ==
Safina was married to Italian dancer [[Lorenza Mario]] from 2001 to 2011, with whom he has a son Pietro (born 2002). 
Currently, Safina and partner Laura Maria Calafeteanu, a marketing consultant, live together in [[Bucharest]], [[Romania]] with their son, Christian, born in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.libertatea.ro/monden/exclusiv-o-bomba-sexy-fosta-fata-de-la-pagina-5-a-dat-lovitura-1158048 |language=Romanian |title=Tenorul italian Alessandro Safina şi-a botezat zilele trecute, la Târgovişte, copilul pe care îl are cu o româncă|work=[[Libertatea]]|accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://revistatango.ro/alessandro-safina-am-ales-romania-si-romania-m-a-adoptat-83955|title=Alessandro Safina: Am ales România și România m-a adoptat – Marea Dragoste|work=Marea Dragoste|access-date=2017-08-30|language=ro-RO}}</ref>
