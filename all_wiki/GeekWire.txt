'''GeekWire''' is an American technology news website that covers [[startup company|startups]] and established technology companies. The site launched in March 2011<ref>{{cite web|title=Seattle tech news mainstays launch GeekWire website|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/thebigblog/2011/03/07/seattle-tech-news-mainstays-launch-geekwire-website/|website=SeattlePI|accessdate=28 March 2015}}</ref> and is based in [[Seattle]]. It was founded by veteran journalists Todd Bishop and John Cook with investment from [[Jonathan Sposato]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Sposato Invests in GeekWire|url=http://www.xconomy.com/seattle/2011/03/29/sposato-invests-in-geekwire/|website=Xconomy|accessdate=29 March 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=GeekWire profitable after only two years|url=http://www.journalismthatmatters.net/geekwire_profitable_after_only_two_years|website=Jornalism that matters|accessdate=29 March 2015}}</ref>

GeekWire founders John Cook and Todd Bishop were former technology reporters at the [[Seattle Post-Intelligencer]] and the [[Puget Sound Business Journal]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Gone in a Flash: Seattle Tech Bloggers Abruptly Leave Business Journal for All-New GeekWire|url=http://allthingsd.com/20110307/gone-in-a-flash-seattle-tech-bloggers-abruptly-leave-business-journal-for-all-new-geekwire/|website=AllThingsD|accessdate=28 March 2015}}</ref> Bishop and Cook joined the Puget Sound Business Journal to create TechFlash in September 2008, leaving to start GeekWire on March 7, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=TechFlash Duo, Todd Bishop and John Cook, Quit PSBJ to Start New Site, GeekWire|url=http://www.xconomy.com/seattle/2011/03/07/techflash-duo-todd-bishop-and-john-cook-quit-psbj-to-start-new-site-geekwire/|website=Xconomy|accessdate=29 March 2015}}</ref>

GeekWire is regularly featured on the [[Techmeme]] leaderboard as one of the sources most frequently posted to that site.<ref>{{cite web|title=Techmeme Leaderboard|url=http://www.techmeme.com/lb|website=Techmeme|accessdate=28 March 2015}}</ref>
