'''Pierre Nanterme''' (7 September 1959<ref>[https://www.google.com/search?q=Pierre+Nanterme+7+septembre+1959&client=ms-android-samsung-ss&prmd=inv&source=lnms&tbm=bks&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwii6ei4t5jgAhUQM-wKHcioBZ4Q_AUoBHoECAwQBA&cshid=1548951907719&biw=412&bih=652]</ref> – 31 January 2019) was a French business executive. He was the chairman and [[chief executive officer]] (CEO) of [[Accenture]], a global [[management consulting]] and professional services firm.

==Early life==
Pierre Nanterme was born in [[France]] in 1959.<ref name=Teo/> He attended the [[ESSEC Business School]] (''École Supérieure des Sciences Économiques et Commerciales'') in Paris and received a master's in management degree in 1981.<ref name=Teo/> After graduation, he completed his military service in France.<ref name=Ganguly>{{cite news |title=How Accenture's new CEO Pierre Nanterme wants to make the company truly global |author=Dibyendu Ganguly |url=http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2011-06-17/news/29670008_1_ceo-pierre-nanterme-accenture-outsourcing |work=[[The Economic Times]] |date=17 June 2011 |accessdate=30 June 2013}}</ref>

==Career at Accenture==
In 1983, Nanterme began his career at the consulting firm [[Accenture]] (then known as Andersen Consulting).<ref name=Ganguly/><ref name=Ruello/> Roles during his early career at the company included acting as head of the banking and finance practice in France.<ref name=Ruello/> He became a [[Partner (business rank)|partner]] at the firm in 1993.<ref name=Bloomberg>{{cite web |url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=27630476&ticker=ACN |title=Pierre Nanterme |work=[[Bloomberg BusinessWeek]] |publisher=Bloomberg |accessdate=29 April 2013}}</ref>  Between 1993 and 2005, Nanterme held a number of positions in the firm's financial services practice, including managing director for [[Europe]], [[Africa]], and [[Latin America]], as well as global managing director of the insurance industry group.<ref name=Teo/>

In November 2005, Nanterme was appointed the national managing director for Accenture in France.<ref name=Bloomberg/> The next year, he joined Accenture's global leadership team and became the company's chief leadership officer, managing its leadership development.<ref name=Teo/><ref name=Bloomberg/><ref name=B20Summit2011>{{cite news |title=B20  Working Groups’ Contributors |url=http://www.b20businesssummit.com/uploads/Bio_Participants%20B20-2011.pdf |work=B20 Business Summit |date=November 2011 |accessdate=29 June 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426051252/http://www.b20businesssummit.com/uploads/Bio_Participants%20B20-2011.pdf |archive-date=26 April 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Other professional roles and awards==
[[File:Nanterme.jpg|thumb|Pierre Nanterme in Berlin at Fjord with the studio dog ‘Gravity’]]
Nanterme was involved with the [[Mouvement des Entreprises de France]] (MEDEF), the largest French employers' association, and served as president of the association's Commission for Economic Affairs and Public Finance from 2005 to 2013.<ref name=Bloomberg/> He also served on MEDEF's executive board.<ref name=Bloomberg/>

Between 2007 and 2011, Nanterme was chairman of the French consulting association SYNTEC, which has member companies from the engineering, information technology, research, and consulting sectors.<ref name=Chausson /><ref name=Bellemare>{{cite news |title=Hélène Pelosse: a French Agency for Renewable Energy |author=Carole Bellemare |url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/societes/2009/07/01/04015-20090701ARTFIG00551-helene-pelosse-une-francaise-pour-l-agence-des-energies-renouvelables-.php |work=[[Le Figaro]] |date=1 July 2009 |accessdate=30 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release |title=Federation Syntec elects Jean-Luc Placet president |url=http://w3.nexis.com/new/docview/getDocForCuiReq?lni=53D9-KRS1-JD3Y-Y0S8&csi=159583&oc=00240&perma=true |publisher=Corporate IT Update |date=26 July 2011 |accessdate=5 August 2013}}</ref>

In 2010, Nanterme was awarded the insignia ''Chevalier'' of the [[Legion of Honour]] for his work as a French business leader.<ref name=Teo/>

Nanterme served on a number of task forces for the B20 Summit, and was a member of the executive board of the B20 Green Growth Action Alliance, which was launched by the [[World Economic Forum]] to work on the growing need for private funding in sustainability.<ref name=Bloomberg/><ref name=Fidenza>{{cite news |title=Accelerating investment in green growth |author=Brindusa Fidenza |url=http://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/news/nation/2012/07/370_114076.html |work=[[Korea Times]] |date=28 June 2013 |accessdate=30 June 2013}}</ref> He also co-chaired the Alliance's Energy Efficiency working group and served as a member of the Economic Policy working group.<ref name=Bloomberg/>

In addition to the above roles, Nanterme served on the steering board of the [[European Commission]]'s European Cloud Partnership, which aims to encourage the public sector to use [[cloud computing]] services to create economic growth in Europe.<ref name=Teo/><ref name="Malhère">{{cite news |title=INFORMATION SOCIETY : NEW PARTNERSHIP TO BOOST PUBLIC SECTOR USE OF CLOUD SERVICES |author=Manon Malhère |url=http://w3.nexis.com/new/docview/getDocForCuiReq?lni=576G-Y841-JDJ2-V273&csi=340455&oc=00240&perma=true |work=[[Europolitics]]|date=3 December 2012 |accessdate=30 June 2013}}{{subscription required}}</ref><ref name=ECP>{{cite web |url=https://ec.europa.eu/digital-agenda/en/european-cloud-partnership |title=European Cloud Partnership |work=Digital Agenda for Europe |accessdate=29 July 2013}}</ref> He also acted as a board member of the TransAtlantic Business Dialogue, a group of CEOs who form policies to encourage trade between Europe and America.<ref name=Teo/>

==Health concerns and death==

Nanterme was diagnosed with colon cancer in 2016. Twenty days after stepping down as CEO, on 31 January 2019, Nanterme died in [[Paris]], France at the age of 59.<ref name=death>{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-accenture-ceo-death/accentures-former-ceo-nanterme-dies-idUSKCN1PP1QB?il=0 |title=Accenture's former CEO Nanterme dies |work=Reuters |accessdate=31 January 2019}}</ref>
