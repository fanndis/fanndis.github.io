The '''David Lynch Foundation for Consciousness-Based Education and World Peace''' (DLF) is a global [[Foundation (charity)|charitable foundation]] with offices in New York City, Los Angeles, and Fairfield, Iowa.<ref name="Schreiber"/><ref>[http://www.sos.state.ia.us/search/corp/(S(zrt0cabvn3qu3oi14f5slk45))/corp_summary.aspx David Lynch Foundation for Consciousness-Based Education and World Peace Iowa Secretary of State] (Requires a data search for business #314711) {{dead link|date=January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Contact |publisher=David Lynch Foundation |url=http://www.davidlynchfoundation.org/contact.html |accessdate=5 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first=Claire |last=Hoffman |title=David Lynch Is Back ... As a Guru of Transcendental Meditation |newspaper=The New York Times|date=22 February 2013 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/02/24/magazine/david-lynch-transcendental-meditation.html |accessdate=5 March 2013}}</ref> It was founded by film director and [[Transcendental Meditation technique|Transcendental Meditation]] (TM) practitioner [[David Lynch]] in 2005 to fund the teaching of TM in schools.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ocregister.com/ocr/2005/08/10/sections/life/life/article_628247.php |title=Tapping into TM: Filmmaker David Lynch wants to share with schools the benefits he says he's reaped from Transcendental Meditation |author=Larsen, Peter |work=Orange County Register |date=10 August 2005 |page=Cover |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060525061510/http://www.ocregister.com/ocr/2005/08/10/sections/life/life/article_628247.php |archivedate=2006-05-25 |accessdate=6 September 2013}}</ref> Over the years it has expanded its focus to include other "at-risk" populations such as the homeless, U.S. military veterans, African war refugees<ref name=about>[http://www.davidlynchfoundation.org/about-us.html David Lynch Foundation] About Us. Retrieved 22 February 2012.</ref> and prison inmates.<ref name="Details">{{cite web |url=http://www.details.com/culture-trends/critical-eye/201109/transcendental-meditation-pure-consciousness#/ixzz1V2ChzEJb |title=Meditation Nation |publisher=Details Magazine |date=September 2011 |author=Hooper, Joseph |accessdate=29 August 2013}}</ref>

The Foundation is reported to have sponsored between 70,000 and 150,000 students in 350 schools throughout the U.S. and South America. It has held several benefits to inspire charitable contributions. A 2009 benefit concert featured [[Paul McCartney]] and [[Ringo Starr]] and a 2013 event featured [[Mehmet Oz|Dr. Oz]] and [[George Stephanopoulos]]. The Foundation also sponsors research on the TM program.

==Description==
According to the DLF web site, David Lynch Foundation for [[Consciousness-Based Education]] is a non-profit organization, established to "fund the implementation of scientifically proven stress-reducing modalities" for "at-risk populations", including U.S. veterans and African war refugees with [[post-traumatic stress disorder]] (PTSD), inner city students, American Indians, homeless and incarcerated men.<ref name="DLFhomepage">{{cite web|url=http://www.davidlynchfoundation.org/|title=David Lynch Foundation Healing Traumatic Stress and Raising Performance in At-risk Populations|accessdate=15 November 2013}}</ref> The DLF also funds research to "assess the effects of the program on academic performance, [[ADHD]] and other learning disorders, anxiety, depression, substance abuse, cardiovascular disease, post-traumatic stress disorder, and diabetes".<ref name="about"/> In 2005, the DLF announced the long-term goal of raising $7 billion in order to establish seven affiliated "Universities of World Peace" in seven different countries, to train students to practice Transcendental Meditation and become "professional peacemakers".<ref>[http://www.thecrimson.com/article.aspx?ref=508719 Wasserstein, Scoop, "David Lynch meditates on peace", ''Harvard Crimson'' (September 30, 2005)].</ref><ref>[http://download.tmnews.org/2005_07_21_ChronicleHE_ltr.pdf Hoover, Eric, "Filmmaker starts foundation to help students chill out with Transcendental Meditation", ''Chronicle of Higher Education'' (July 21, 2005)].</ref> DLF.TV is the Foundation's production wing and online television channel that provides video content and web casts.<ref name=HBO>{{cite news |last=Yourse |first=Robin-Denise |title=HBO pic on Clintons|newspaper=The Washington Times|date=26 March 2009}}<!--|accessdate=22 February 2013--></ref>

==History==
Concerned about school violence and increasing stress and problems for students,<ref name = "Magic">Springen, Karen, 26 April 2005, "The Magic of Meditation", ''Newsweek''.</ref><ref>Pierce, Elise, March 2006, "Are the Kids All Right?”, ''United Hemispheres''.</ref> David Lynch founded the DLF in July 2005 "to provide the [TM] program to schools".<ref>Larsen, Peter (10 August 2005) ''Orange County Register'', "Tapping into TM".</ref> That year, Lynch, DLF president John Hagelin, and [[Maharishi University of Management]] researcher Fred Travis went on a lecture tour titled "Consciousness, Creativity, and the Brain". Tour dates included the [[University of Southern California]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Yogi Bearer; Dark Films Aside, David Lynch Brims With the Light of Transcendental Meditation|first=William |last=Booth|work=The Washington Post|date=2 December 2005|page=C.01}}</ref> [[UC Berkeley]],<ref>{{cite news|title=FIVE QUESTIONS FOR: David Lynch / Bliss and world peace, one campus at a time|first=Reyhan |last=Harmanci|work=San Francisco Chronicle|date=6 November 2005|page=D.4}}</ref> the [[University of Oregon]] in Eugene,<ref>{{cite news|title=Movie director takes cues from Transcendental Meditation|first=Bob |last=Keefer |work=The Register-Guard|location=Eugene, Or.|date=6 November 2005|page=G.1}}</ref> the [[University of Washington]],<ref>{{cite news|title=DAVID LYNCH IS SPREADING THE GOSPEL OF MEDITATION|first=CECELIA |last=GOODNOW|work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|date=5 November 2005|page=D.1}}</ref> [[Emerson College]],<ref name="Morrow">{{cite news|url=https://www.google.com/search?q=david+Lynch+emerson+college&oq=david+&aqs=chrome.4.69i57j69i60l3j69i59j69i60.5235j0j7&sourceid=chrome&espv=210&es_sm=122&ie=UTF-8#es_sm=122&espv=210&q=david+Lynch+emerson+college&start=50|title=Transcending it all: David Lynch is happy, and wants you to know it |last=Morrow |first=Jennifer |date=11 October 2005 |quote=updated 31 May 2011 |work=The Justice.org |accessdate=11 November 2013}}</ref> [[Yale University]], and [[Brown University]].<ref>{{cite news|title=From Twin Peaks to inner peace|first=Simon |last=Houpt|work=The Globe and Mail|location=Toronto, Ont.|date=10 October 2005|page=R.1}}</ref> In 2005, Lynch offered to fund (for-credit) peace studies courses at several universities with TM instruction being included in each course.<ref>Wilson, Charles, 11 December 2005, "Accredited Bliss", ''New York Times Magazine''.</ref>

By 2006, six public schools in the U.S. had been awarded $25,000 by the David Lynch Foundation.<ref>(May 2066) ''NEA Today'', [[National Education Association]], "Clear Your Mind"</ref> ''[[National Education Association|NEA Today]]'' described the DLF as an organization "which provides funding for students in grades 4 to 12 to learn Transcendental Meditation" and reported that DLF had "helped approximately 500 students and 50 teachers learn how to meditate and "about 1,500 more" were scheduled to learn in the fall.<ref name = "Teacher Mag">Kirsten Wells, Denise (Sept 1 2006) Teacher Magazine, Take Your Meditation.</ref> In October the Foundation withdrew a $175,000 pledge to a [[San Rafael, California|San Rafael]], California, high school after the anti-separation [[Pacific Justice Institute]] threatened to sue for violating the [[First Amendment to the United States Constitution|First Amendment]]'s [[Establishment Clause]].<ref>[http://www.buddhistchannel.tv/index.php?id=65,4611,0,0,1,0 Californian Catholics against teaching of meditation in public schools], The Buddhist Channel, 6 August 2007.</ref> That year Lynch assigned all of his proceeds from the sale of his book ''[[Catching the Big Fish]]'' to the DLF<ref>Lynch, David, (2006) [[The Penguin Group]]) About the Author (last page of the book).</ref> and the Foundation sponsored a presentation on the benefits of TM in education at the [[Harvard Club of Boston]].<ref name="Lindberg">{{cite journal|url=http://www.pulsarnet.com/IdealAcademyArticle.pdf |author1=Cynthia E. Johnson |author2=Lisa Lindberg |quote=Winter 2009|title=Taking Care of The Student – The Forgotten Element in Education|journal=originally printed in Pathways Magazine, Washington, DC.}}</ref>

''Natural Health'' magazine reported in 2007 that the DLF had given $3 million in sponsorship to 20 U.S. schools located in the city of Washington D.C. and various states including Arizona, New York, California, Colorado, Connecticut, and Michigan.<ref name="Nat Health">{{cite news |url=http://www.eurekalert.org/pub_releases/2010-12/muom-eal120810.php |last=Linda |first=Marsa |title=Eastwood and Lynch launch Operation Warrior Wellness to teach 10,000... ==Sponsored programs==
According to the DLF website, the Foundation's sponsorship encompasses programs for schools, military, homeless shelters, prisons and global outreach.<ref name=about/>

===Schools===
[[File:1590-MT-au-Peru-2011-Consciousness-Based-Education.JPG|thumb|DLF funded program in a Peruvian school]]
The Foundation's web site says it focuses on school-wide instruction in Transcendental Meditation with the aim of improving educational and health outcomes. Its school programs, dubbed "Quiet Time", are [[wikt:voluntary|voluntary]], and require [[Parental consent|parental permission]].<ref name="Nobori">{{cite news|url=http://www.edutopia.org/stw-student-stress-meditation-overview|title=A Bold Approach to Reducing Student Stress|last=Nobori|first=Mariko|work=Edutopia|accessdate=14 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131020090157/http://www.edutopia.org/stw-student-stress-meditation-overview|archive-date=20 October 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> According to the Foundation, the programs are offered to students and faculty at no cost to the parents or school.<ref>David Lynch Foundation [http://www.davidlynchfoundation.org/ "Transforming Lives: The David Lynch Foundation"]. Retrieved 17 July 2009.</ref> The Foundation has reportedly funded school programs in the U.S. cities of Washington D.C, New York City, Hartford CT, Detroit MI, San Francisco CA and in the countries of Brazil, Peru, Bolivia, Vietnam, Nepal, Northern Ireland, Ghana, Kenya, Uganda, South Africa and Israel.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=David Lynch Foundation |url=http://www.dlfprojects.org/stress-free-urban-schools.html |title=Stress-free urban schools |archivedate=15 September 2010 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5slwFYdj7?url=http://www.dlfprojects.org/stress-free-urban-schools.html |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dlfprojects.org/at-risk-children.html |publisher=David Lynch Foundation |title=At-risk children in developing countries |archivedate=15 September 2010 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5slwM7Jc4?url=http://www.dlfprojects.org/at-risk-children.html |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>Ehud Zion Waldoks, "School crisis? Send the kids to the corner – to count to 10 cross their legs and hum..." ''The Jerusalem Post'' 22 November 2007.</ref> In 2007, DLF began sponsoring Quiet Time at Visitacion Valley Middle School, providing an annual grant that covers most of the $175,000 cost of dedicated staff to run the program,<ref name="Schreiber"/> as well as supporting three other schools in San Francisco.{{citation needed|date=November 2013}} As of 2007 another ten schools in the same San Francisco district were waiting to start the program. After studying the program, Carlos Garcia, school district superintendent, concluded that Quiet Time was a secular program that calms students and allows them to learn.<ref>States News Service, 22 February 2012, "Risking peace at a troubled school", San Rafael, CA.</ref><ref>[http://cwae.org/quiet_time_program.php] Center for Wellness and Achievement in Education official website. Retrieved 16 February 2013.</ref>

DLF funded Transcendental Meditation courses for students at the Lowell Whiteman Primary School in Boulder, Colorado in 2008.<ref>Fridell, Zach, 14 December 2008, "Meditation soothes Lowell Whiteman students", Steamboat Pilot and Today.</ref> In 2009, ''Church and State'' magazine reported that the [[Americans United for the Separation of Church and State]] had characterized the David Lynch Foundation's funding of Transcendental Meditation for public school students as undermining the constitutional [[separation of church and state]].<ref>[http://www.au.org/media/church-and-state/archives/2009/06/levitating-over-the.html Levitating Over the Church-State Wall?] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101019052414/http://www.au.org/media/church-and-state/archives/2009/06/levitating-over-the.html |date=19 October 2010 }} ''Church & State'' magazine, June 2009.</ref> In 2011, music mogul [[Russell Simmons]] announced plans to provide financial support to the David Lynch Foundation to teach TM at [[Hillhouse High School]] in New Haven, Connecticut.<ref>[http://www.newhavenindependent.org/index.php/archives/entry/russell_simmons_brings_tm_to_hillhouse/id_34743] New Haven Independent, 16 March 2011, Russell Simmons Brings TM to Hillhouse, Michelle Turner.</ref> In 2009 the DLF provided $120,000 to fund a Transcendental Meditation Club at the Tucson Magnet High School in Arizona, U.S. which had offered TM courses to 160 students, staff, and faculty as of 2010.<ref name="Arizona Rep">{{cite news|last=Kossan|first=Pat|title=Transcendental Meditation Club draws student|url=https://www.azcentral.com/arizonarepublic/news/articles/2010/04/08/20100408tucson-transcendental-meditation.html |accessdate=21 February 2013|newspaper=The Arizona Republic|date=8 April 2010}}</ref>

===Military===
In 2006 Lynch made a personal donation of $100,000 and the DLF inaugurated Operation Warrior Wellness (a DLF national initiative) whose goal is to teach the TM technique to 10,000 veterans with post-traumatic stress disorder and other war related ailments. Since 2010 DLF has contributed almost $1 million to sponsor TM courses for veterans and their families.<ref name=veterans/><ref>Bender, Bryan, 26 December 2012, "Training cadets for war and (inner) peace: Study suggests meditation may help prevent PTSD", The Boston Globe.</ref><ref>[http://www.operationwarriorwellness.org/our-direction.html] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130110062952/http://www.operationwarriorwellness.org/our-direction.html |date=10 January 2013 }} Operation Warrior Wellness official website. Retrieved 17 February 2013.</ref> The Transcendental Meditation technique's effect on post-traumatic stress syndrome (PTSD) is the topic of research at the University of Colorado<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.moaa.org/momStory.aspx?pagename=pubs_mom_070601_ptsd |title=Military Officer Association of America, Promise for PTSD |author=Don Vaughn |accessdate=6 September 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111115071731/http://www.moaa.org/momStory.aspx?pagename=pubs_mom_070601_ptsd |archive-date=15 November 2011 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |pmc=1810367 |title=CAM and Post Traumatic Stress Disorder, Pub Med. |pmid=17342251 |doi=10.1093/ecam/nel041 |volume=4 |issue=1 |date=March 2007 |journal=Evid Based Complement Alternat Med |pages=131–2 | last1 = Hankey | first1 = A}}</ref> and was the topic of a study published in ''Military Medicine''<ref name="News Medical">{{cite news |last=Unknown author|title=Transcendental Meditation technique reduces PTSD symptoms in U.S. Marines|url=http://www.news-medical.net/news/20130129/Transcendental-Meditation-technique-reduces-PTSD-symptoms-in-US-Marines.aspx |accessdate=7 February 2013 |newspaper=News Medical |date=29 January 2013}}</ref> in 2011. Other initiatives to teach the TM technique to war veterans who are at risk for PTSD are ongoing.<ref>[https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748704638304575636911988306800] Wall Street Journal, Film Maker Introduces Veterans to Meditation, 26 November 2010.</ref><ref>[https://www.smh.com.au/lifestyle/wellbeing/stars-hum-praises-of-meditations-healing-power-20101214-18wxg.html] ''Sydney Morning Herald'', "Stars Hum Praises of Meditation's Healing Power", 15 December 2010.</ref> The DLF sponsored<ref name=BloombergBW/> TM technique instruction for students at [[Norwich University]], a private military academy, as part of a long-term study on meditation and military performance.<ref>Bender, Bryan (2 December 2012) Training cadets for war and (inner) peace; Study suggests meditation may help prevent PTSD, ''The Boston Globe'' (Boston, MA).</ref><ref>Vogel, Steve (4 May 2012) VA tests use of TM for vets with PTSD, ''The Washington Post''.</ref> The DLF raises funds to cover the $500 per person cost of TM training for military personnel.<ref name=BloombergBW>{{cite news|last=Winter|first=Caroline|title=Transcendental Meditation May Help Stressed Vets|url=http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2013-02-06/transcendental-meditation-may-help-stressed-vets|accessdate=22 February 2013|newspaper=BloombergBusinessweek|date=6 February 2013}}</ref> According to [[Bob Roth]], executive director of the David Lynch Foundation, the US Department of Defense is examining potential advantages of TM for veterans of Iraq and Afghanistan.  In order to procure support from the US government, the Foundation is striving to teach 10,000 veterans, active soldiers, trainees, and their families.<ref>"Filmmaker David Lynch touts meditation for PTSD". ''AFP''. Retrieved 13 April 2013.</ref>

Recent research shows that regular practice of [[Transcendental Meditation technique]] enables some active duty service members battling [[post-traumatic stress]] disorder to reduce or even eliminate their [[psychotropic medication]] and get better control of their often-debilitating symptoms, researchers report in the journal Military Medicine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://neurosciencenews.com/transcendental-meditation-ptsd-3397/|title=PTSD Symptoms May Be Reduced With Transcendental Meditation|last=News|first=Neuroscience|date=2016-01-11|website=Neuroscience News|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-29}}</ref> [[Transcendental Meditation technique]] shows marked efficacy in treating [[anxiety disorders]] says a [[meta-analysis]] of [[randomized controlled trials]], "''TM practice is more effective than treatment as usual and most alternative treatments, with greatest effects observed in individuals with high anxiety."''<ref>{{cite journal |language=en |author= David W. Orme-Johnson, Vernon A. Barnes |title= Effects of the Transcendental Meditation Technique on Trait Anxiety: A Meta-Analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials |journal= [[Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine]]|volume= 20 |number= 5|pages= 330–341 |year= 2014 |pmid= 24107199|doi= 10.1089/acm.2013.0204 |url= http://online.liebertpub.com/doi/pdfplus/10.1089/acm.2013.0204}}</ref>
And a [[meta-analysis]] says: ''"Differential effects of [[relaxation techniques]] on [[trait anxiety]]: a [[meta-analysis]]. Effect sizes for the different treatments (e.g., Progressive Relaxation, EMG Biofeedback, various forms of meditation, etc.) were calculated. Most of the treatments produced similar effect sizes except that Transcendental Meditation had significantly larger effect size (p less than .005)"''<ref>{{cite journal|vauthors= Eppley K|display-authors=etal |title=  Differential effects of relaxation techniques on trait anxiety: a meta-analysis |journal= Journal of Clinical Psychology |volume=  45 |pages= 957–974 |year= 1989 |doi= 10.1002/1097-4679(198911)45:6<957::aid-jclp2270450622>3.0.co;2-q|pmid=2693491}}</ref>

===Social programs===
The DLF began a program in 2010, at a Van Nuys, California, youth shelter called Children of the Night.<ref name="Daily News"/> The David Lynch Foundation also produced the video "Saving the Disposable Ones", about the work of Father Gabriel Mejia and the Fundación Hogares Claret, dedicated to rescuing and rehabilitating homeless children in Colombia.<ref name="BlackTie">{{cite news|url=http://www.blacktiemagazine.com/society_2011_may/South_south_news_hosts_father_gabriel_majia_fundacion_hogares_claret.htm|title=South - South News Father Gabriel Mejia Fundacion Hogares Claret|date=May 2011|work=Black Tie International Magazine|accessdate=17 November 2013}}</ref> According to the DLF web site, Father Mejia has founded 47 shelters for homeless children where thousands of young people practice the Transcendental Meditation technique.<ref>[http://www.davidlynchfoundation.org/global-outreach.html] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101228005030/http://www.davidlynchfoundation.org/global-outreach.html |date=28 December 2010 }} DLF official webs site, Global Outreach page, see the "Support the David Lynch Foundation—Center de Fundación Hogares Claret Project" article in lower right hand corner. Retrieved 11 April 2011.</ref> The Foundation now teaches TM to prisoners.<ref>Stein, Jeannine, 17 March 2012, "TM directs Lynch", Los Angeles Times.</ref>
