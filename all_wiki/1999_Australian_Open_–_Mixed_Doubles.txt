{{main|1999 Australian Open}}
The '''1999 Australian Open – Mixed Doubles''' was the [[Mixed doubles (tennis)|mixed doubles]] event of the eighty-seventh edition of the Australian Open, the first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] of the year. [[Venus Williams]] and [[Justin Gimelstob]] were the defending champions but did not compete that year.

[[Mariaan de Swardt]] and [[David Adams (tennis)|David Adams]] won in the final 6–4, 4–6, 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>, against [[Serena Williams]] and [[Max Mirnyi]].

One of the surprises of this tournament was that over half of the seeds fell in the first round, and none of the seeds made it past the second round.
