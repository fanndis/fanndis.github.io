The '''2003 Lower Saxony state election''' was held on 2 February 2003 to elect the members of the 15th [[Landtag of Lower Saxony]]. The incumbent [[Social Democratic Party of Germany|Social Democratic Party]] (SPD) majority government led by [[Minister President of Lower Saxony|Minister-President]] [[Sigmar Gabriel]] was defeated. The [[Christian Democratic Union of Germany|Christian Democratic Union]] (CDU) came up one seat short of a majority, and formed a coalition with the [[Free Democratic Party (Germany)|Free Democratic Party]] (FDP). CDU leader [[Christian Wulff]] was subsequently elected Minister-President.

==Background==
The election in Lower Saxony had larger significance than just in the state itself.<ref name=joke>{{cite news | first = Allan | last = Hall | title = The joke's on Schroder as Germans seek serious answers to economic crisis | newspaper = [[Scotland on Sunday]] | page = 21 | date = 2002-12-01 }}</ref> Defeat for the Social Democrats in the election would mean they lost their slim majority in the upper house of the German parliament, the [[Bundesrat of Germany|Bundesrat]].<ref name=joke/> It was thought this might cause the collapse of [[Gerhard Schröder]]'s national coalition government between the Social Democrats and the [[Alliance '90/The Greens|Green Party]].<ref name=joke/>

==Campaign and issues==
The economy was seen as the most important issue in the election, with the Social Democrats suffering as a result.<ref name=reject>{{cite web| url = http://edition.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/europe/02/02/germany.elex/index.html | title = State voters reject Schroeder | accessdate = 2009-09-20 | date = 2003-02-03 | publisher = [[CNN]]}}</ref>

[[Opinion poll]]s in December 2002 showed the Christian Democrats in the lead with 43% as against 34% for the Social Democrats.<ref name=desperately>{{cite news | first = Allan | last = Hall | title = Germany is desperately seeking Schroder as chancellor loses his grip | newspaper = [[The Scotsman]] | page = 9 | date = 2002-12-26 }}</ref>
