{{main article|2012 US Open (tennis)}}

[[Serena Williams]] defeated world No. 1 [[Victoria Azarenka]] 6–2, 2–6, 7–5 in the final, to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2012 US Open (tennis)|2012 US Open]], despite Azarenka serving for the match and leading 5–3 in the third set. It was the first time since [[1995 US Open – Women's Singles|1995]] that the final went to three sets. With the victory, Williams became only the third woman in history to win [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]], [[Tennis at the Summer Olympics|Olympics]], and the US Open in the same season after [[Steffi Graf]] and her sister, [[Venus Williams]]. Williams dropped just one set the entire tournament, to Azarenka in the final.

[[Samantha Stosur]] was the defending champion, but she was defeated in the quarterfinals by Victoria Azarenka.

Three-time US Open champion [[Kim Clijsters]], who was on a 22-match winning streak at [[US Open (tennis)|Flushing Meadows]], lost to [[Laura Robson]] in the second round, her earliest exit at the tournament since 2000 and her first defeat since losing to compatriot [[Justine Henin]] in the final in [[2003 US Open – Women's Singles|2003]]. This was also Clijster's last tournament before her second retirement (first was in 2007), until she came back to tour in 2020 at [[2020 Dubai Tennis Championships – Women's Singles|Dubai Tennis Championships]].

This tournament served as the first Grand Slam main draw appearance for future two-time Grand Slam champion and [[List of WTA number 1 ranked players|world No. 1]] [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] who lost to [[Sara Errani]] in the first round. It was also the first Grand Slam main draw appearance for future [[WTA Finals]] champion [[Elina Svitolina]] where she was defeated by former world No. 1 [[Ana Ivanovic]] in the first round. This also marked the first time Ivanovic had reached the quarterfinals of a major since winning the [[2008 French Open – Women's Singles|2008 French Open]].
