The '''first inauguration of George W. Bush''' as the [[List of Presidents of the United States|43rd]] [[President of the United States]] took place on Saturday, January 20, 2001, at the West Front of the [[United States Capitol]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] This was the 54th [[United States presidential inauguration|inauguration]] and marked the commencement of the first term of [[George W. Bush]] as President and [[Dick Cheney]] as [[Vice President of the United States|Vice President]]. [[Chief Justice of the United States|Chief Justice]] [[William Rehnquist]] administered the [[Oath of office of the President of the United States|presidential oath of office]] at 12:01&nbsp;p.m., after he administered the [[Oath of office of the Vice President of the United States|vice-presidential oath of office]] as well.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.inaugural.senate.gov/about/past-inaugural-ceremonies/54th-inaugural-ceremonies/|title=The 54th Presidential Inauguration: George W. Bush, January 20, 2001|publisher=United States Senate|access-date=April 8, 2020}}</ref><ref name=cnn>[http://archives.cnn.com/2001/ALLPOLITICS/stories/01/20/inaugural.wrap/ "Bush gets keys to White House, flexes first presidential muscles"], ''Cable News Network'', January 20, 2001. Last accessed January 22, 2009.</ref> An estimated 300,000 people attended the swearing-in ceremony.<ref>Angela Greiling Keane and Chris Dolmetsch, [https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601087&sid=aJg4GOozGSK4&refer=home "Washington Braces for Chaos, Gridlock at Inaugural (Update1)"] Bloomberg.com, January 18, 2009. Last accessed January 22, 2009.</ref><ref>Jemal R. Brinson, [http://www.ajc.com/services/content/printedition/2009/01/18/inaughistorygraph0118.html "INAUGURATION 2009: Where history will be made"], The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, January 18, 2009. Last accessed January 22, 2009.</ref> This was the first presidential inauguration to take place in the 21st century, and the first in the 3rd millennium.

==Pre-inaugural events==
On the eve of the inauguration, there was a celebration for U.S. authors hosted by [[Laura Bush]] at [[DAR Constitution Hall]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Bush parties on eve of inauguration |url=http://archives.cnn.com/2001/ALLPOLITICS/stories/01/19/inaugural.wrap/index.html |work=[[CNN|Cable News Network]] |date=January 19, 2001 |accessdate=22 January 2009}}</ref> [[Wayne Newton]], [[Brooks & Dunn]], and [[Ricky Martin]] performed as the pre-inaugural entertainment.<ref>{{cite news |first= Jon|last= Pareles|title=Music for Many Firsts at Inauguration Events  |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/01/22/arts/music/22conc.html?_r=1 |quote=Wayne Newton, the country duo Brooks & Dunn and the Latin pop singer Ricky Martin performed at his pre-inaugural event |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=January 21, 2009  |accessdate=2009-01-22 }}</ref>

Thousands of demonstrators attended the inaugural ceremonies in [[Washington, D.C.]], to protest the outcome and controversial circumstances of the [[2000 United States presidential election|2000 presidential election]].<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/1127937.stm "President Bush sworn in"], BBC News, January 20, 2001. Last accessed January 22, 2009.</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20061206144300/http://archives.cnn.com/2001/ALLPOLITICS/stories/01/20/protest.wrap/index.html "Protesters line inaugural parade route"] ''Cable News Network'' (via archive.org), January 20, 2001. Last accessed January 22, 2009.</ref> Four protesters were arrested and Bush's [[limousine]] was hit by a tennis ball and an egg thrown from the crowd during the inaugural parade.<ref name="cnn"/> Details of the parade were famously derided in the opening chapter of the 2001 #1 bestseller ''[[Stupid White Men]]'' on pages 14–15.
