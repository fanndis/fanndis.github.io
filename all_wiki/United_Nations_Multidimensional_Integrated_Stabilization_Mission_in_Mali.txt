The '''United Nations Multidimensional Integrated Stabilization Mission in Mali''' ({{Lang-fr|Mission multidimensionnelle intégrée des Nations unies pour la stabilisation au Mali}}, '''MINUSMA''')<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.un.org/fr/peacekeeping/missions/minusma/index.shtml |title=Mission multidimensionnelle intégrée des Nations Unies pour la stabilisation au Mali (MINUSMA), French language webpage on UN website for the mission |publisher=United Nations |work=www.un.org |date=25 April 2013 |accessdate=27 October 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029191346/http://www.un.org/fr/peacekeeping/missions/minusma/index.shtml |archive-date=29 October 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> is a [[United Nations]] peacekeeping mission in [[Mali]]. MINUSMA was established on 25 April 2013 by [[United Nations Security Council Resolution 2100]] to stabilise the country after the [[Tuareg rebellion (2012)|Tuareg rebellion of 2012]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.un.org/en/peacekeeping/missions/minusma/index.shtml |title=United Nations Multidimensional Integrated Stabilization Mission in Mali (MINUSMA) |publisher=United Nations |work=www.un.org |date=25 April 2013 |accessdate=3 July 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130712205745/http://www.un.org/en/peacekeeping/missions/minusma/index.shtml |archive-date=12 July 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> It was officially deployed on 1 July, and has become the UN's most dangerous peacekeeping mission, with 209 peacekeepers killed out of a force of about 15,200.<ref name=fact>{{Cite web|url=https://peacekeeping.un.org/en/mission/minusma|title=MINUSMA|website=United Nations Peacekeeping}}</ref>

Apart from MINUSMA, there currently are two further international peace operations in Mali. These are the [[European Union]] missions [[EUCAP Sahel Mali]] and [[EUTM Mali]].

==History==
{{Main|Northern Mali conflict}}
In 2012, [[Tuareg]] and other peoples in northern Mali's [[Azawad]] region started an insurgency in the north under the banner of the [[National Movement for the Liberation of Azawad]]. After some initial successes and complaints from the [[Malian army|Malian Army]] that it was ill-equipped to fight the insurgents, who had benefited from an influx of heavy weaponry from the [[2011 Libyan civil war]] as well as other sources, elements of the army [[2012 Malian coup d'état|staged a military coup d'état]] on 21 March 2012.<ref>{{cite web |first=Anne |last=Look |url=http://www.voanews.com/english/news/Uncertainty-Reigns-in-Mali-144018866.html |title=Uncertainty Reigns in Mali |publisher=Voice of America |date=25 March 2012 |access-date=1 July 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425155107/http://www.voanews.com/english/news/Uncertainty-Reigns-in-Mali-144018866.html |archive-date=25 April 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> Following the coup, the rebels made further advances to capture the three biggest cities in the north: [[Gao]], [[Timbuktu]] and [[Kidal]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/world-news/Africa/Timbuktu-bombarded-as-Mali-constitution-restored/Article1-833959.aspx |title=Timbuktu bombarded as Mali constitution 'restored' |work=Hindustan Times |date=1 April 2012 |access-date=1 July 2013 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6COhRabq3?url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/world-news/Africa/Timbuktu-bombarded-as-Mali-constitution-restored/Article1-833959.aspx |archive-date=23 November 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> Following economic sanctions and a blockade by the [[Economic Community of West African States]] (ECOWAS) on the country, a deal, brokered in [[Burkina Faso]] by President [[Blaise Compaoré]] under the auspices of ECOWAS, was signed that would see [[Amadou Sanogo]] cede power to [[Dioncounda Traoré]] to assume the presidency in an [[interim]] capacity until an election is held.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2012/04/201246232416740914.html |title=Mali junta says power transfer 'within days' |publisher=Al Jazeera |access-date=1 July 2013 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6FBSKTIXW?url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2012/04/201246232416740914.html |archive-date=17 March 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 1 July 2013, {{formatnum:6000}} of a future total of {{formatnum:12600}} UN peacekeeping troops officially took over responsibility for patrolling the country's north from [[France]] and the [[ECOWAS]]' [[International Support Mission to Mali]] (AFISMA).<ref>{{in lang|it}} Pineschi Laura, ''TUTELA INTERNAZIONALE DEL PATRIMONIO CULTURALE E MISSIONI DI PACE DELLE NAZIONI UNITE: UN BINOMIO POSSIBILE? IL CASO MINUSMA'', Rivista di Diritto Internazionale 2018 , pag. 5 , fasc. 1</ref> The group was expected to play a role in the [[Malian presidential election, 2013|2013 Malian presidential election]].<ref name="aljaz">{{cite web |url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/07/20137182846984106.html |title=UN takes over Mali peacekeeping mission |publisher=Al Jazeera |access-date=1 July 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130701130212/http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/07/20137182846984106.html |archive-date=1 July 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> The force is the third largest UN peacekeeping force in operation in the world.<ref>[https://www.economist.com/news/middle-east-and-africa/21580472-pursued-one-place-west-africa-extremists-pop-up-somewhere-nearby-first The UN and Mali: Secure the Sahara, if you can] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180306083310/https://www.economist.com/news/middle-east-and-africa/21580472-pursued-one-place-west-africa-extremists-pop-up-somewhere-nearby-first |date=6 March 2018 }}, ''The Economist'' (6 July 2013).</ref>

==Incidents==
In October 2013, a suicide bomber attacked the Chadian soldiers resulting in the death of two soldiers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/10/un-troops-killed-mali-suicide-attack-20131023144437588851.html|title=UN troops killed in Mali suicide attack|accessdate=16 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140923145104/http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/10/un-troops-killed-mali-suicide-attack-20131023144437588851.html|archive-date=23 September 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 13 December, two Senegalese peacekeepers were killed at a bombing outside the [[Malian Solidarity Bank]] in [[Kidal]] a day before the second round of the [[Malian parliamentary election, 2013]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/12/car-bomb-kills-2-un-peacekeepers-mali-20131214144813908884.html|title=Car bomb kills UN peacekeepers in Mali|accessdate=16 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140923185124/http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/12/car-bomb-kills-2-un-peacekeepers-mali-20131214144813908884.html|archive-date=23 September 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> In October 2014, 10 soldiers were killed—nine from [[Niger]] and one from Senegal near Gao and Kidal, respectively—bringing the total number of dead soldiers from the mission to 21 with dozens more wounded. It also preceded Malian Foreign Minister [[Abdoulaye Diop]] calling for the [[UNSC]] to send a rapid deployment force to the country claiming that there was an increase in [[drug traffickers]] and [[Islamic extremism|Islamist fighters]]. U.N. Peacekeeping chief [[Hervé Ladsous]] also spoke to the UNSC from [[Bamako]], where he attend a memorial service for the dead soldiers. He added that a combination of factors has led to the increase in attacks on U.N. troops, including the drawdown of French forces and a perceived lack of Malian security forces, as such MINUSMA, being the main international presence in the area, was a target. He further noted that the UN was no longer working in a [[peacekeeping]] environment, but sought to increase protection of the mission's staff, equipment and bases.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.voanews.com/content/un-condemns-deadly-attack-on-peacekeepers-in-mali/2476252.html|title=Mali Calls for Rapid Reaction Force to Quell Unrest|work=VOA|accessdate=16 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141202013202/http://www.voanews.com/content/un-condemns-deadly-attack-on-peacekeepers-in-mali/2476252.html|archive-date=2 December 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 20 January 2019 the MINUSMA base at [[Aguelhok]] was attacked by militants.  The attack was repelled but 10 Chadian UN peacekeepers were killed and a further 25 injured.<ref>{{cite news |title=UN peacekeepers killed in Mali attack |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-46941711 |accessdate=21 January 2019 |date=20 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190121020026/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-46941711 |archive-date=21 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Mali: Ten UN peacekeeper killed in attack in Mali |url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/01/20/africa/un-peacekeepers-killed-mali/index.html |accessdate=28 January 2019 |work=Cable News Network |agency=CNN |date=21 January 2019}}</ref> The militants had arrived on board a number of armed vehicles.  Several of the attackers are said to have been killed.<ref name=dpost/>  Responsibility was claimed by [[Al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb]] who stated that it was a retaliatory attack for the recent visit to Chad by Israeli prime minister [[Benjamin Netanyahu]] and the subsequent revival of [[Chad–Israel relations|Chad–Israel diplomatic relations]].<ref name=dpost>{{cite news |title=Mali: 10 UN peacekeepers killed in attack on Aguelhok base |url=https://thedefensepost.com/2019/01/20/mali-8-un-peacekeepers-killed-aguelhok/ |accessdate=21 January 2019 |work=The Defense Post |date=20 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Peacekeepers killed in attack on UN northern Mali base |url=https://www.france24.com/en/20190120-un-peacekeepers-killed-attack-northern-mali-base-islamist-militants-united-nations |accessdate=21 January 2019 |work=France 24 |date=20 January 2019 |language=en |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190121071814/https://www.france24.com/en/20190120-un-peacekeepers-killed-attack-northern-mali-base-islamist-militants-united-nations |archive-date=21 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>  UN Secretary-General [[António Guterres]] condemned the attack.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mali: Al-Qaeda claims responsibility for the Aguelhok attack {{!}} The North Africa Journal |url=http://north-africa.com/mali-al-qaeda-claims-responsibility-for-the-aguelhok-attack/ |website=North Africa Journal |accessdate=21 January 2019}}</ref>

On January 25th 2019 Three members of Sri Lankan Peacekeeping troops deployed in Mali were killed, while three Corporals were injured, when their armoured vehicle came under an Improvised Explosive Device (IED) attack, in the general area of Douentza in Mali.<ref>http://www.ft.lk/news/Three-Sri-Lankan-soldiers-die-in-Mali-attack/56-671690</ref> 

The [[United Nations]] reported that attacks in the northern [[Mali]] against a U.N. convoy killed three peacekeepers from Chad and injured four others. Secretary-General [[António Guterres]] and UN spokesman [[Stephane Dujarric]] criticized the attacks, and said that they could account to [[war crimes]] as per the international law.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Multidimensional_Integrated_Stabilization_Mission_in_Mali#Incidents|title=UN Says 3 Peacekeepers Killed, 4 Wounded in Mali Attacks|accessdate=10 May 2020|website= The New York Times}}</ref>{{Circular reference|date=October 2020}}

As of March 31, 2020, 209 MINUSMA troops lost their lives<ref name=fact/>.
