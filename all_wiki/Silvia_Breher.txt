'''Silvia Breher''' ([[Given name#Name%20at%20birth|née]] '''Lucke'''; born 23 July 1973) is a German lawyer and politician of the [[Christian Democratic Union of Germany|Christian Democratic Union]] (CDU). She has been Member of the [[Bundestag]] for the constituency of [[Cloppenburg – Vechta|Cloppenburg - Vechta]] since the [[2017 German federal election|2017 federal election]]. At the CDU conference in November 2019 she was elected as one of the deputy leaders of her party;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www-spiegel-de.cdn.ampproject.org/v/s/www.spiegel.de/politik/deutschland/cdu-silvia-breher-auf-parteitag-zur-neuen-vizevorsitzenden-gewaehlt-a-1297880-amp.html?usqp=mq331AQCKAE=&amp_js_v=0.1|title=CDU: Silvia Breher auf Parteitag zur neuen Vizevorsitzenden gewählt - SPIEGEL ONLINE|website=www-spiegel-de.cdn.ampproject.org|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref> she succeeded [[Ursula von der Leyen]] who had been elected to the [[President of the European Commission|Presidency of the European Commission]].<ref>Robert Roßmann (November 21, 2019), [https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/silvia-breher-cdu-parteitag-stellvertretende-vorsitzende-1.4691510 CDU-Parteitag: Vom Bauernhof an die Parteispitze] ''[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]]''.</ref>

== Early life and family ==
Breher was born in [[Löningen]] and grew up on a farm in [[Lindern]].<ref>Robert Roßmann (November 21, 2019), [https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/silvia-breher-cdu-parteitag-stellvertretende-vorsitzende-1.4691510 CDU-Parteitag: Vom Bauernhof an die Parteispitze] ''[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]]''.</ref> After gaining her Abitur at Copernicus Gymnasium in Löningen she studied Law at the [[Osnabrück University]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.silvia-breher.de/|title=Silvia Breher {{!}} Mitglied des Deutschen Bundestages|language=de-DE|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref> At the end of 2000 she began practicing at a self employed lawyer. From 2011 till 2017 she was the Chief Executive of the "Kreislandsvolkverbandes Vechta", the local farmers' union.<ref name="auto"/>

== Political career ==
Breher is a member of the Christian Democratic Union and of her local CDU organisation in [[Cloppenburg]]. Between 2014 and 2015 she was a member of the CDU Commission ''Nachhaltig leben – Lebensqualität bewahren''<ref name="auto"/>''.'' Since 2018 Breher has been the leader of the Cloppenburg CDU district association<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nwzonline.de/cloppenburg/politik/kreis-cloppenburg-loeningen-parteitag-i-cdu-setzt-auf-frischen-wind_a_50,1,2272041086.html|title=Parteitag I Kreis Cloppenburg/Löningen: CDU setzt auf frischen Wind|last=Nordwest-Zeitung|date=2018-05-07|website=www.nwzonline.de|language=de|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref> and the Löningen CDU association. In March 2019 she was elected leader of the Oldenburg CDU state association<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nwzonline.de/cloppenburg/politik/friesoythe-cdu-landesparteitag-in-friesoythe-silvia-breher-ist-neue-landesvorsitzende_a_50,4,1176849777.html|title=Cdu-Landesparteitag In Friesoythe:|last=Nordwest-Zeitung|date=2019-03-24|website=www.nwzonline.de|language=de|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref> and thus member of the State Executive of the CDU in Lower Saxony, under the leadership of chairman [[Bernd Althusmann]]. 

As successor of [[Franz-Josef Holzenkamp]],<ref>Reinhard Bingener (November 22, 2019), [https://www.faz.net/aktuell/politik/inland/stellvertretende-cdu-vorsitzende-die-erstaunliche-karriere-der-silvia-breher-16499009.html Die erstaunliche Karriere der Silvia Breher] ''[[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung]]''.</ref> Breher was selected as the CDU candidate for Cloppenburg - Vechta for the [[2017 German federal election|2017 federal elections]]. She subsequently won the election with the highest vote share in the country, 57.7 percent.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.welt.de/politik/bundestagswahl/article168296753/Ergebnis-und-Wahlsieger-im-Wahlkreis-32.html|title=Wahlergebnis Cloppenburg – Vechta: Das Ergebnis im Wahlkreis 32 - Bundestagswahl 2017|last=WELT|date=2017-09-24|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref> Her constituency is seen as a CDU safe seat, with her party winning the constituency uninterrupted since 1953. In parliament, she is a member of the Committee on Food and Agriculture as well as a member of the Committee on Family, Senior Citizens, Women and Youth.<ref name="auto"/>

== Political positions ==
In September 2017, Breher supported [[LGBT rights in Germany|same-sex marriages]].<ref>[https://www.kirche-und-leben.de/artikel/silvia-breher-vom-ambo-in-den-bundestag Kirche+Leben: Silvia Breher – Vom Ambo in den Bundesta], September 2017</ref> In April 2020, Breher co-signed – alongside around 50 other members of her parliamentary group – a letter to [[President of the European Commission]] [[Ursula von der Leyen]] which called on the European Union to take in children who were living in migrant camps across Greece.<ref>Charlotte Raskopf (April 6, 2020), [https://www.handelsblatt.com/politik/deutschland/brief-an-von-der-leyen-50-cdu-abgeordnete-fordern-aufnahme-von-fluechtlingskindern-aus-griechischen-lagern/25718598.html 50 CDU-Abgeordnete fordern Aufnahme von Flüchtlingskindern aus griechischen Lagern] ''[[Handelsblatt]]''.</ref><ref>Robert Roßmann (April 6, 2020), [https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/unionsabgeordnete-fluechtlingskinder-coronavirus-eu-kommission-1.4869437 Mehr als 50 Unionsabgeordnete fordern Aufnahme von Flüchtlingskindern] ''[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]]''.</ref>
