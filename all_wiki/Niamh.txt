'''Niamh''' ({{IPA-ga|n̠ʲiəvˠ}}, from  [[Old Irish]] ''Niaṁ'') is an [[Irish language|Irish]] feminine [[given name]] (meaning "bright" or "radiant"),<ref>[http://www.teanglann.ie/en/fgb/niamh Niamh] in Ó Dónaill, ''Foclóir Gaeilge–Béarla'' (1977), accessed 8 August 2020</ref>  [[Anglicisation|anglicised]] as  '''Neve''', '''Nieve''', or '''Neave'''  {{IPAc-en|ˈ|n|iː|v}}.<ref>[http://www.behindthename.com/name/niamh Niamh (behindthename.com)] (accessed 8 August 2020),
[http://www.babynamesofireland.com/neave Neave (babynamesofireland.com)],
[http://www.mfnames.com/fnames/n/origin-and-meaning-of-neve.htm MFnames.com - Origin and Meaning of Neve (mfnames.com)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120304161820/http://www.mfnames.com/fnames/n/origin-and-meaning-of-neve.htm |date=2012-03-04 |accessdate=}}</ref>

In [[Irish mythology]], [[Niamh (mythology)|Niamh]] is the daughter of the god of the sea, [[Manannán mac Lir]] and one of the queens of [[Tír na nÓg]], the land of eternal youth. She was the lover of the poet-hero [[Oisín]]. The first recorded use of Niamh (that spelling) as a given name in modern Ireland was in 1911, when two children were registered with the name and when a Niamh was listed in that year's [[Census of Ireland, 1911|census.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://search.ancestry.com/cgi-bin/sse.dll?_phsrc=BXB21&_phstart=successSource&usePUBJs=true&indiv=1&qh=VQi%2ByoMjKBRZVUWMFiR0PQ%3D%3D&db=FSIrelandCivRegBirth&gss=angs-d&new=1&rank=1&gsfn=Niamh&gsfn_x=1&msbdy=1911&MSAV=1&uidh=e64&pcat=34&fh=0&h=6741875&recoff=&ml_rpos=1|title=Niamh Maire Fitzgerald in the Ireland, Civil Registration Births Index, 1864-1958|website=Ancestry.com|accessdate=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://search.ancestry.com/cgi-bin/sse.dll?_phsrc=BXB22&_phstart=successSource&usePUBJs=true&indiv=1&qh=VQi%2ByoMjKBRZVUWMFiR0PQ%3D%3D&db=FSIrelandCivRegBirth&gss=angs-d&new=1&rank=1&gsfn=Niamh&gsfn_x=1&msbdy=1911&MSAV=1&uidh=e64&pcat=34&fh=1&h=6830314&recoff=&ml_rpos=2|title=Niamh O'Connell in the Ireland, Civil Registration Births Index, 1864-1958|website=Ancestry.com |accessdate= }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://search.ancestry.com/cgi-bin/sse.dll?_phsrc=vxa17&_phstart=successSource&usePUBJs=true&indiv=1&db=websearch-4050&gsfn=Niamh&gsfn_x=1&new=1&rank=1&redir=false&uidh=e64&gss=angs-d&pcat=35&fh=0&h=14833194&recoff=&ml_rpos=1|title=Niamh Máire Nic Ghearailt in the Web: Ireland, Census, 1911 |date=1911 |website=Ancestry.com|access-date=26 December 2018 |accessdate= }}</ref>

''Neve'' is also a Dutch and Flemish surname. The given name of Canadian actress [[Neve Campbell]] is derived from the Dutch surname, her mother's maiden name.
