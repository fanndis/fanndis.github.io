'''''Al Bawaba''''' (البوابة, Arabic for "the portal" or "the gate") is a news, blogging<ref>Slackman, Michael. "[https://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/22/world/middleeast/22dubai.html?bl&ex=1222228800&en=007cf9e8faaaf52a&ei=5087%0A Generation Faithful Young and Arab in Land of Mosques and Bars]." ''[[The New York Times]]''. 21 September 2008.</ref> and media website headquartered in [[Amman]], [[Jordan]] with an office in [[Dubai]], [[United Arab Emirates]].<ref name="Barnhurst2007">{{cite book|author=Kevin G. Barnhurst|title=Media Q: Media/queered : Visibility and Its Discontents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GSKsujbaGG4C&pg=PA14|accessdate=20 September 2014|year=2007|publisher=Peter Lang|isbn=978-0-8204-9532-3|page=14}}</ref> Since 2001 it publishes the [[Mena Report]], which covers the business and economics in Arab world.<ref>{{cite news|author=Massoud Derhally|title=MENA Report launched by Al Bawaba|url=http://www.arabianbusiness.com/mena-report-launched-by-al-bawaba-142212.html|date=30 May 2001|accessdate=20 September 2014|work=Arabian Business}}</ref> Al Bawaba bills itself as "the largest independent producer and distributor of content in the Middle East... [with] a full-time staff of journalists and editors covering the Middle East and North Africa region's events and news".<ref name="Website">{{cite web|title=The Al Bawaba Group corporate page|url=http://www.albawaba.com/corporate|work=Al Bawaba}}</ref>

==History and content==
''Al Bawaba'' was launched in 2000,<ref>{{cite web|title=Al Bawaba|url=http://www.mediame.com/en/country/pan_arab/al_bawaba|publisher=MediaME|accessdate=20 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140924114127/http://www.mediame.com/en/country/pan_arab/al_bawaba|archive-date=2014-09-24|url-status=dead}}</ref> and is owned by Al Bawaba Middle East Limited based in Amman, Jordan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=110282|title=Company Overview of Al Bawaba Middle East Limited|work=[[Bloomberg L.P.|Bloomberg]]|access-date=22 January 2018}}</ref> It had an office in [[Kuwait]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2002/feb/05/world-news-guide-middle-east|title=World news guide: Middle East|date=5 February 2002|work=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=22 January 2018}}</ref>

The network consists of several web portals and websites, besides Al Bawaba including Al Bawaba Blogs, Al Bawaba Music (music site), Al Bawaba Games (Later Gaming Zone),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://albawaba.com/games/index.php3?lang=e|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20010813084230/http://albawaba.com/games/index.php3?lang=e|url-status=dead|archive-date=2001-08-13|title=Archived Al Bawaba Games homepage from August 2001|date=2001-08-13|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref> Sharekna (online photo and video management service), SyndiGate (digital content syndication service).<ref name="Website"/> It publishes news stories related to the [[Middle East]]. It has separate [[English language|English]] and [[Arabic language|Arabic]] sections.
