'''Jane Carol Ginsburg''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|size=small|sep=,|FBA}} (born July 21, 1955) is an American attorney. She is the [[Morton L. Janklow]] Professor of Literary and Artistic Property Law at the [[Columbia Law School]]. She also directs the law school's Kernochan Center for Law, Media and the Arts.<ref name="ColLaw">{{cite web|url=http://www.law.columbia.edu/fac/Jane_Ginsburg|title=Jane C Ginsburg|publisher=[[Columbia Law School]]|accessdate=January 22, 2010}}</ref> In 2011, Ginsburg was elected to the [[British Academy]].<ref>{{cite news|title=GINSBURG GARNERS HONORS IN BRITAIN, U.S.|url=http://www.law.columbia.edu/magazine/62256/ginsburg-garners-honors-in-britain,-u-s-|publisher=[[Columbia Law School|Columbia Law School Magazine]]|date=Fall 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
An expert on [[copyright]], Ginsburg has written various treatises and law review articles. She received her B.A. and M.A. degrees from the [[University of Chicago]], her J.D. degree from [[Harvard Law School]], a [[Master of Advanced Studies|DEA]] with a [[Fulbright Program|Fulbright grant]] (1985), and a [[Doctor of Law]] degree (1995) from [[Panthéon-Assas University]]. At Harvard, she served as an editor of the ''[[Harvard Law Review]]''.<ref name="ColLaw"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.americanbar.org/digital-asset-abstract/|title=Digital Asset Abstract|website=www.americanbar.org}}</ref> After law school, she clerked for Judge [[John Joseph Gibbons|John Gibbons]] of the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit]].

She is the daughter of [[United States Supreme Court]] Justice [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]] and law professor [[Martin Ginsburg]], both of whom formerly served on the Columbia Law School faculty. Justice Ginsburg and Jane are the first mother–daughter pair ever to serve on the same law faculty in the United States.<ref name="obit">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/06/28/us/28ginsburg.html|title=M.D. Ginsburg, 78, Dies; Lawyer and Tax Expert|work=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=June 28, 2010 | first=Gardiner | last=Harris | date=June 27, 2010}}</ref> Her brother is founder of [[Cedille Records]], [[James Steven Ginsburg]].

==In popular culture==
In the 2018, feature film ''[[On the Basis of Sex]]'', a biography of her mother Ruth, a teenage Jane is portrayed by [[Cailee Spaeny]].

==Personal life==
In 1981, Ginsburg married George T. Spera Jr. of [[Mays Landing]], [[New Jersey]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=  |first=  |authorlink=   |title= Jane C. Ginsburg Engaged to Wed  |work=[[New York Times]]|date=July 5, 1981 |url= https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/05/style/jane-c-ginsburg-engaged-to-wed.html |accessdate=}}</ref> Her husband works for the law firm [[Shearman & Sterling]]. They have two children. Their son, Paul Spera, an actor, graduated from Yale in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Geselowitz|first1=Gabriela|title=The Notorious RBG's Grandson is a Total Babe|url=http://jewcy.com/jewish-arts-and-culture/notorious-rbgs-grandson-total-babe|website=Jewcy.com|accessdate=24 July 2017|date=July 27, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Tabio  |first=Nick  |authorlink=   |title=Paul Spera: A Homecoming   |publisher=[[Yale News]]|date=April 27, 2018 |url=https://yaledailynews.com/blog/2018/04/27/paul-spera-a-homecoming/  |accessdate=}}</ref> Their daughter, Clara Spera, graduated from Harvard Law School in 2017. She is married to Scottish actor Rory Boyd.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.roryandclara.com/|title=Clara & Rory}}{{dead link|date=September 2020}}</ref>
