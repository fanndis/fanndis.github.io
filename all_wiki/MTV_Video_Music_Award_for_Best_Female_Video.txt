The '''MTV Video Music Award for Best Female Video''' is one of the original general awards that has been handed out every year since the first annual [[MTV Video Music Award]]s in 1984.  In 2007, however, the award was briefly renamed '''Female Artist of the Year''', and it awarded the artist's whole body of work for that year rather than a specific video.  In 2008, though, the award returned to its original name. The category would become defunct beginning with the [[2017 MTV Video Music Awards|2017]] ceremony after the gender specific categories would be merged into the Artist of the Year category.

[[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]], [[Taylor Swift]] and [[Beyoncé]] are the biggest winners with three wins each, while the former also holds the record for most nominations with 12. Meanwhile, [[Beyoncé]], [[Kelly Clarkson]] and [[Lady Gaga]] are the only artists to win the award for two consecutive years.
