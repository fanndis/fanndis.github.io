[[YouTube]] is an American video-sharing website headquartered in [[San Bruno, California]]. Although the most-viewed videos were initially [[viral video]]s, such as "[[Judson Laipply#"Evolution of Dance"|Evolution of Dance]]" and "[[Charlie Bit My Finger]]", the most-viewed videos were increasingly related to [[music videos]]. In fact, since [[Lady Gaga]]'s "[[Bad Romance]]" in 2009, every video that has reached the top of the "most-viewed YouTube videos" list has been a music video. Although some of the previously most-viewed videos are no longer listed on the site, reaching the top of the list is still considered a tremendous feat.

In November 2005, a [[Nike, Inc.|Nike]] advertisement featuring Brazilian football player [[Ronaldinho]] became the first video to reach one million views.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thedrum.com/news/2016/03/31/2005-nike-video-featuring-ronaldinho-becomes-first-youtube-film-reach-one-million|title=2005: Nike video featuring Ronaldinho becomes first YouTube film to reach one million views}}</ref>

==Billion View Club==
An early metric of a video's popularity was the so-called Billion View Club, videos which had succeeded in reaching over 1 billion views since their initial upload.<ref name="RollingStone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/the-billion-view-club-youtubes-most-watched-music-videos-162074/|title=The Billion View Club: YouTube's Most Watched Music Videos|first=Brittany|last=Spanos|date=October 13, 2015|accessdate=October 15, 2019|work=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref>

In December 2012, "[[Gangnam Style (music video)|Gangnam Style]]" became the first video to reach one billion views.<ref name="billboardbillion">{{cite web|last=Gruger|first=William|title=PSY's 'Gangnam Style' Video Hits 1 Billion Views, Unprecedented Milestone|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1483733/psys-gangnam-style-video-hits-1-billion-views-unprecedented-milestone|date=December 21, 2012|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=December 21, 2012}}</ref> By June 2015, only "[[Baby (Justin Bieber song)#Music video|Baby]]" had also managed to pass this threshold, but, by October 2015, a total of ten videos had done so,<ref name="RollingStone"/> and the number grew further to 100 in February 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2018/02/21/there-are-now-over-100-youtube-videos-with-at-least-a-billion-views/|title=There Are Now Over 100 YouTube Videos With At Least A Billion Views}}</ref> Considering older videos that pre-dated YouTube's creation in 2005 but were added later:
* "[[November Rain]]" by [[Guns N' Roses]] became the first video made prior to YouTube's creation to reach this threshold by July 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://loudwire.com/guns-n-roses-november-rain-first-90s-video-1-billion-views-youtube/|title=Guns N' Roses' 'November Rain' Is First '90s Video to Top 1 Billion YouTube Views|first=Michael|last=Christopher|date=July 16, 2018|accessdate=October 15, 2019|work=[[Loudwire]]}}</ref>
* "[[Numb (Linkin Park song)|Numb]]" by [[Linkin Park]] was the first 2000s video predating YouTube to reach 1 billion views in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/linkin-parks-numb-video-has-reached-1-billion-views-on-youtube|title=Linkin Park's 'Numb' video has reached 1 billion views on YouTube}}</ref>
* "[[Bohemian Rhapsody]]" by [[Queen (band)|Queen]] was the first 1970s video (and pre-1990s video) to reach 1 billion views in July 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2019/07/22/bohemian-rhapsody-becomes-first-pre-1990s-song-rack-billion/|title=Bohemian Rhapsody becomes first pre-1990s song to rack up billion hits on YouTube, site announces|first=Jessica|last=Carpani|date=July 22, 2019|accessdate=October 15, 2019|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]}}</ref>
* Guns N' Roses' "[[Sweet Child o' Mine]]" was the first 1980s video to reach 1 billion views in October 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/digital/news/guns-n-roses-sweet-child-o-mine-1-billion-views-1203371701/|title=Guns N' Roses' 'Sweet Child O' Mine' Becomes First '80s Music Video's to Hit 1 Billion YouTube Views|first=Breanna|last=Bell|date=October 15, 2019|accessdate=October 15, 2019|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}}</ref>

{{As of|2020|October}}, the fastest videos to reach one billion views are "[[Hello (Adele song)#Music video|Hello]]" (87 days), "Despacito" (96 days) and "Shape of You" (97 days);<ref>{{cite news|title=Fastest to 1 billion|url=https://kworb.net/youtube/milestones_1000.html|accessdate=April 12, 2020|work=Kworb}}</ref> the fastest videos to reach two billion views are "Despacito" (154 days), "Shape of You" (187 days) and "[[Girls Like You#Music videos|Girls Like You]]" (293 days);<ref>{{cite news|title=Fastest to 2 billion|url=https://kworb.net/youtube/milestones_2000.html|accessdate=April 12, 2020|work=Kworb}}</ref> the fastest videos to reach three billion views are "Despacito" (203 days) and "Shape of You" (342 days);<ref>{{cite news|title=Fastest to 3 billion|url=https://kworb.net/youtube/milestones_3000.html|accessdate=April 12, 2020|work=Kworb}}</ref> the fastest videos to reach four billion views are "Despacito" (271 days) and "Shape of You" (708 days).<ref>{{cite news|title=Fastest to 4 billion|url=https://kworb.net/youtube/milestones_4000.html|accessdate=April 12, 2020|work=Kworb}}</ref>

The majority of these videos in the Billion View Club have been commercial [[music video]]s by popular artists, but the list has included oddities, typically programs aimed at children. Such videos include two episodes of the Russian animated cartoon ''[[Masha and the Bear]]'', a version of "[[The Wheels on the Bus]]" by the British animation studio [[Little Baby Bum]], and "[[Johny Johny Yes Papa]]" from Indian children's stations LooLoo Kids and [[ChuChu TV]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newstatesman.com/2017/10/rise-and-rise-billion-club-list-youtube-videos-over-one-billion-views|title=The rise and rise of the Billion Club: the list of YouTube videos with over one billion views|first=Anna|last=Leszkiewicz|date=October 11, 2017|accessdate=March 12, 2020|work=[[New Statesman]]}}</ref> Various versions of the song "[[Baby Shark]]" in total amassed more than five billion views by January 2019, with the original version posted by [[Pinkfong]] having exceeded two billion views previously.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.polygon.com/2019/1/30/18203918/baby-shark-song-youtube-video-education-trend|title=Baby Shark videos have amassed a record-breaking 5 billion views|first=Petrana|last=Radulovic|date=January 30, 2019|accessdate=October 15, 2019|work=[[Polygon (website)|Polygon]]}}</ref> The original video by Pinkfong is now the most viewed video on the site. On October 29, 2020, Baby Shark surpassed 7 billion views, and on November 2, it passed Despacito to become the most viewed video on YouTube.
