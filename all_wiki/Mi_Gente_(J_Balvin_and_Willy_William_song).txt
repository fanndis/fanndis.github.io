"'''Mi Gente'''" ({{lang-en|My People}}) is a song recorded by Colombian singer [[J Balvin]] and French singer and producer [[Willy William]]. It was released on 30 June 2017 through [[Scorpio Music]] and [[Universal Music Latin Entertainment|Universal Music Latin]]. It is a remix of Willy William's song "Voodoo Song". The single was serviced to United States [[contemporary hit radio]] on 18 July 2017.<ref name="us radio">{{cite web|url=https://www.allaccess.com/top40-mainstream/future-releases|title=Top 40/M Future Releases|publisher=All Access|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170717194516/https://www.allaccess.com/top40-mainstream/future-releases|archivedate=July 17, 2017|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref> A remix version featuring [[Beyoncé]] was released on 28 September 2017. J Balvin and Willy William released six more "Mi Gente" remixes on 20 October 2017 with [[Steve Aoki]], [[Alesso]], [[Cedric Gervais]], [[Dillon Francis]], [[Sunnery James & Ryan Marciano]], and Henry Fong.

==Composition==
"Mi Gente" is a reworking of Willy William's previous single "Voodoo Song", where tune is taken from Bangla song "Heila Duila Nach."

==Commercial performance==
The original version topped the charts in most Latin American countries.

In the United States, the original version of the song peaked at number two on the [[Hot Latin Songs]] chart and at number 19 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].

In Canada, the remix version of the song featuring Beyonce peaked at number 2 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] and number 3 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]] in the US.

[[J Balvin]] and [[Willy William]] dropped six additional "Mi Gente" remixes with [[Steve Aoki]], [[Alesso]], [[Cedric Gervais]], [[Dillon Francis]], [[Sunnery James & Ryan Marciano]], and Henry Fong.<ref name = "Mi Gente remixes">{{cite web|url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8006819/j-balvin-mi-gente-remixes-steve-aoki-cedric-gervais-dillon-francis|title= J Balvin Drops Six New 'Mi Gente' Remixes With Steve Aoki, Cedric Gervais, Dillon Francis & More|work= billboard.com|accessdate=October 25, 2017}}</ref>

==Music video==
Directed by 36 Grados, the accompanying [[music video]] premiered on [[Vevo]] the same day of the single release. [[Leila Cobo]] from ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine described the video as "an explosion of color and dance" which features people from around the globe and that is all about unifying through music. It has many cameo appearances, including Italian millionaire Gianluca Vacchi.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7850080/j-balvin-video-mi-gente|title=Watch J Balvin's New (Fantastic) Video 'Mi Gente'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Leila|last=Cobo|date=30 June 2017|accessdate=7 July 2017}}</ref>
 
The music video has over 2.7 billion views on YouTube as of July 2020,<ref>{{cite web|title=J. ==Controversy==

Shortly after the song's release, many listeners noticed a very similar resemblance to [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]]'s [[grito]], first appearing at 0:52 on the record, and twice more throughout the duration of the 3:09 track. The issue became very mainstream, with many reporters falsely believing that the grito was of Pitbull, which it was indeed not. Pitbull's first action was releasing a remix to the record, including his own grito, titling the track "Mi Gente [Worldwide Remix].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lyrics.com/lyric-lf/1379568/Willy+William/Mi+Gente+%5BWorldwide+Remix%5D|title=Mi Gente [Worldwide Remix]|date=2017-08-10|work=Noticias RCN|access-date=2020-04-18|language=es}}</ref> After being released, many fans saw the striking resemblance between the two gritos being used. The Pitbull remix intentionally used both Pitbull's and that of the original track to show the similarity.

On October 8, 2019, Pitbull's legal team applied for a trademark on the popular "EEEEEEYOOOOOO!" grito, most popularly brought to market by Pitbull. On April 17, 2020, Pitbull was able to acquire a full trademark to the grito, thus becoming one of the less than 250 owners of a trademark for solely a sound.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.miamiherald.com/miami-com/miami-com-news/article242059686.html|title=‘EEEEEEEYOOOOOO’: Pitbull trademarks his yell. The music industry may never be the same|date=2020-04-17|work=Miami Herald|access-date=2020-04-18|language=es}}</ref> With this, the "EEEEEEYOOOOOO!" found in "Mi Gente" can no longer be copied or parodied in anyway without legal permission from Pitbull. This prevents the rerelease of the song, or any remix for that matter, to be published with that sound, unless it is removed, or permission is granted from Pitbull.

On September 28, 2017, a remix version featuring vocals by American singer [[Beyoncé]] was released on digital stores and streaming services. The remix was released as a single by Universal Music Latin, [[Republic Records]], [[Columbia Records]], and [[Parkwood Entertainment]]. Beyoncé donated all proceeds from the song to hurricane relief charities in Mexico, Puerto Rico, and other Caribbean islands. Balvin and William stated Beyoncé was the "top choice" for the remix, and had no expectation of her agreeing to the song when they reached out to her.

Beyoncé would later perform the song in her [[Beyoncé 2018 Coachella performance|set]] in [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] in 2018. For the second weekend, Balvin joined her on stage.

This song is also part of the set list for the [[OTR II]] tour. The song was added to the set list at the show in [[Manchester]] on June 13, 2018 at [[City of Manchester Stadium|Etihad Stadium]].

===Commercial performance===
Upon the release of the remixed version of the song featuring [[Beyoncé]], the song reached number one on the Hot Latin Songs chart, ending the 35-week reign led by "[[Despacito]]", becoming Balvin's fourth number one on the Hot Latin Songs chart, as well as Beyoncé's highest-charting song on the chart after "[[Irreplaceable]]" peaked at number four and "[[Beautiful Liar]]" peaked at number 10 in 2007. On the Hot 100, the remix moved from number 21 to number three after a full tracking week, giving both Balvin and William their first top 10 single and Beyoncé her 17th top 10 single (27th with Destiny's Child) on the chart. The remix also reached number one the US [[Digital Songs]] chart with 79,000 copies sold during that week (combined sales with the original version), becoming both Balvin and William's first number one song on that respective chart, and Beyoncé's sixth, as well as just the second mostly-Spanish sung song to top the chart after "Despacito".<ref name="Hot 100 peak">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7990506/j-balvin-willy-william-beyonce-cardi-b-hot-100|title=J Balvin, Willy William & Beyonce Blast to No. 3 on Hot 100 With 'Mi Gente,' Cardi B No. 1 for Third Week|work=Billboard|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=October 9, 2017|accessdate=October 10, 2017}}</ref> This made "Mi Gente", alongside "Despacito", as the first time in the Hot 100 chart history for two non-English singles to appear in the top 10 simultaneously.

In Canada, the remix reached a new peak of number two on the Canadian Hot 100, behind [[Post Malone]] and [[21 Savage]]'s "[[Rockstar (Post Malone song)|Rockstar]]", becoming both Balvin and William's highest charting single at the time, and Beyoncé's 13th top 10 single. It also moved from number 17 to number one on the Hot Digital Songs chart.<ref>http://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2017/10/10/shania-hedley-and-tom-petty-loom-large-weeks-chart</ref>

The remix also peaked at number one in many European and Latin American countries, including Argentina, Hungary, Mexico, the Netherlands, Bolivia, Panama, Paraguay, Costa Rica, and Australia.

===Music video===
A music video was also released on YouTube featuring American producer [[Diplo]], French DJ [[David Guetta]], Dutch DJ [[Martin Garrix]], Dutch DJ [[Tiësto]], Venezuelan YouTuber [[Lele Pons]], Brazilian soccer player [[Neymar]], Brazilian singer [[Anitta (singer)|Anitta]], Colombian singer [[Piso 21|El Llane]],  [[Portuguese people|Portuguese]] footballer [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] and several fan made videos.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7981833/the-video-for-the-beyonce-remix-of-mi-gente-is-jam-packed-with|title=The Video for the Beyoncé Remix of 'Mi Gente' Is Jam-Packed With Recognizable Faces|publisher=|via=Billboard}}</ref>

===Critical reception===
The version featuring Beyoncé was named "Best New Track" by ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' with Matthew Ismael Ruiz stating that the song "is about a shared cultural pride, one that transcends borders and race, from J Balvin's native Colombia to Puerto Rico and across all of Latinidad. It's a declaration that "El mundo nos quiere" (The world wants us) set to a head-nod bop. Beyoncé gets this, and with this remix, she backs up her words with action. This is what solidarity sounds like."<ref>https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/j-balvin-and-willy-williams-mi-gente-ft-beyonce-remix/</ref> Randall Roberts from ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' wrote that: "To say Beyoncé lends a hand in the remix is an understatement. Unlike guests who limit their contributions to a single late-track verse or hook, Beyoncé upends "Mi Gente" from the first verse and proceeds to bilingually maneuver through the rhythmic, up-tempo jam like the queen she is."<ref>https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/la-et-entertainment-news-updates-listen-beyonce-remix-j-balvin-1506699503-htmlstory.html</ref>

In 2019, ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' listed ''Mi Gente (Remix)'' at number 189 on their greatest songs of the decade (2010s) list.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/the-200-best-songs-of-the-2010s/|title=The 200 Best Songs of the 2010s|website=Pitchfork|date=October 7, 2019|accessdate=October 8, 2019}}</ref>
