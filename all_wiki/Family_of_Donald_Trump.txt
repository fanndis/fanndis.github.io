The family of [[Donald Trump]], the owner of [[The Trump Organization]] and 45th [[president of the United States]], is a prominent American family active in real estate, entertainment, business, and politics. Trump's immediate family circle has been the [[First Family of the United States|first family of the United States]] since [[Inauguration of Donald Trump|his inauguration]] on January 20, 2017. They are part of the broader [[Trump family]] [[German Americans|originating from]] [[Germany]]. Donald Trump's mother, [[Mary Anne MacLeod Trump|Mary Anne MacLeod]], came from the Hebridean [[Isle of Lewis]], off the west coast of [[Scotland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.scottishroots.com/people/donald.php |title=Scottish Roots – Donald Trump |website=Scottish Roots |access-date=January 29, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180126235639/https://www.scottishroots.com/people/donald.php |archive-date=January 26, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> Trump has five children from three wives, and ten grandchildren.

==Immediate family==
===Wives===
====Ivana Trump====

{{Main|Ivana Trump}}

[[Ivana Trump|Ivana Marie Trump]] (née Zelníčková), the first wife of Donald Trump, was born on February 20, 1949, in [[Zlín]], [[Czechoslovakia]] (now the [[Czech Republic]]). She is a former fashion model and a businesswoman. They were married from 1977 until 1992.

Ivana Trump took a major role in [[the Trump Organization]]. She became the vice president of interior design for the company, leading the signature design of [[Trump Tower]]. Afterwards, her then-husband appointed her to head up the Trump Castle Hotel and Casino as president. She became a naturalized U.S. citizen in 1988.<ref>{{cite news|agency=Associated Press|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1899&dat=19880527&id=LiEgAAAAIBAJ&pg=5053,3823442|title=Ivana Trump becomes U.S. citizen|date=May 27, 1988|accessdate=August 21, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190418083356/https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1899&dat=19880527&id=LiEgAAAAIBAJ&sjid=YmYFAAAAIBAJ&pg=5053,3823442|archive-date=April 18, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

====Marla Maples====

{{Main|Marla Maples}}

[[Marla Maples|Marla Ann Maples]], the second wife of Donald Trump, was born on October 27, 1963, in [[Dalton, Georgia]], making her Donald Trump's only wife who was an American citizen at the time of their marriage. She is an actress and television personality. They were married from 1993 to 1999.

====Melania Trump====

{{Main|Melania Trump}}

[[Melania Trump]] (née Knavs), the third wife of Donald Trump, was born on April 26, 1970, in [[Novo Mesto]], [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|Yugoslavia]] (present-day [[Slovenia]]). She had a lengthy modeling career and is the second foreign-born [[First Lady of the United States|first lady of the United States]], the first being [[Louisa Adams]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/05/09/who-is-melania-trump |title=The Model American |newspaper=[[The New Yorker]] |date=May 9, 2016 |accessdate=November 10, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180726045037/https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/05/09/who-is-melania-trump |archive-date=July 26, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> They were married in 2005.

===Children===

{{See also|List of children of the Presidents of the United States#Donald Trump}}

Trump has five children from three marriages: [[Donald Trump Jr.|Don{{nbs}}Jr.]], [[Ivanka Trump|Ivanka]], and [[Eric Trump]] with Ivana Trump; [[Tiffany Trump]] with Marla Maples; and Barron Trump with First Lady Melania Trump.

====Children with Ivana====

{{Main|Donald Trump Jr.|Ivanka Trump|Eric Trump}}

Donald Jr., Ivanka, and Eric are Trump's three eldest children, from his first marriage with Ivana Trump.

Prior to the election, each of the siblings held the title of executive vice president at [[the Trump Organization]]. During the campaign, they served as surrogates for their father on national news programs. Following Trump's election victory, all three were named to the [[presidential transition team]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2016/07/21/politics/donald-trump-children-republican-convention/ |title=Donald Trump's kids might have saved the convention |newspaper=[[CNN]] |date=July 22, 2016 |accessdate=November 12, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180401003657/https://edition.cnn.com/2016/07/21/politics/donald-trump-children-republican-convention/ |archive-date=April 1, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Following the inauguration, Donald{{nbs}}Jr. and Eric took charge of the family's real estate empire. Ivanka moved to Washington, D.C., with her husband [[Jared Kushner]], who was appointed to a [[Senior Advisor to the President of the United States|senior White House advisory position]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/trumps-eldest-children-handling-white-house-transition/story?id=44897926 |title=How Trump's Eldest Children Have Been Handling the White House Transition |work=[[ABC News]] |first1=Lauren |last1=Effron |first2=John |last2=Santucci |date=January 19, 2017 |access-date=January 20, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170120032718/http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/trumps-eldest-children-handling-white-house-transition/story?id=44897926 |archive-date=January 20, 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref>

{{Main|Tiffany Trump}}

==Ancestry==

{{See|Trump family}}

Donald Trump's paternal ancestry is traceable to [[Bobenheim am Berg]], a village in the [[Palatinate (region)|Palatinate]], Germany, in the 18th century. (Blair 2001:26) cites ''Hanns Drumpf'', recorded in 1608.</ref> This German heritage was long concealed by Donald Trump's father, [[Fred Trump]], who had grown up in a mainly German-speaking environment until he was ten years old;<ref>Blair, ''The Trumps'', p. 115.</ref> after [[World War II]] and until the 1980s, he told people he was of Swedish ancestry.<ref>{{cite news |last=Rozhon |first=Tracie |date=June 26, 1999 |title=Fred C. ===Grandparents===
====Frederick Trump====

{{Main|Frederick Trump}}

In 1885, Donald Trump's grandfather, [[Frederick Trump|Friedrich Trump]], emigrated from Kallstadt, Palatinate (then part of the [[Kingdom of Bavaria]]), to the United States at age 16. He [[anglicized]] his name to ''Frederick'' in 1892 when he became a U.S. citizen.<ref name=dad>{{cite news |last=Blair |first=Gwenda |date=August 24, 2015 |title=The Man Who Made Trump Who He Is |url=http://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2015/08/the-man-who-made-trump-who-he-is-121647 |newspaper=[[Politico]] |access-date=July 24, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190428214907/https://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2015/08/the-man-who-made-trump-who-he-is-121647 |archive-date=April 28, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> During the [[Klondike Gold Rush]], he amassed a fortune by opening a restaurant and hotel in [[Bennett, British Columbia|Bennett]] and later [[Whitehorse, Yukon|Whitehorse]], serving [[Prospecting#The gold rushes|gold seekers]] on their way to the region; one biographer wrote that the business included a brothel, a portrayal Donald Trump has said was "totally false".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/features/2016-trump-family-fortune/ |title=Trump's Family Fortune Originated in a Canadian Gold-Rush Brothel |website=Bloomberg |date=October 26, 2016 |access-date=January 12, 2018 |first=Natalie Obiko |last=Pearson |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171227230616/https://www.bloomberg.com/features/2016-trump-family-fortune/ |archive-date=December 27, 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> Frederick Trump died in the first wave of the [[Spanish flu]] pandemic. After his death, his fortune was passed on to his wife and son.

Frederick Trump was a second cousin of [[Henry J. Heinz]], founder of [[Heinz|H. J. Heinz Company]], whose father also came from Kallstadt.

====Elizabeth Christ Trump====

{{See|Christ (surname)|Hartung}}

Donald Trump's grandmother, [[Elizabeth Christ Trump]], ''née'' Christ, was born in 1880 and died on June 6, 1966. She married Frederick Trump in 1902 and moved to the United States with him. Like her husband, she was a native of Kallstadt, born as the daughter of Philipp and Marie Christ. Philipp Christ was descended from Johannes Christ (1626–1688/9) of [[Flörsheim]], [[Hesse]]. Elizabeth Christ Trump was a descendant of [[Organ building|organ builder]] [[Johann Michael Hartung]] (1708–1763) through her paternal grandmother Sabina Christ.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://gedbas.genealogy.net/person/ancestors/1178251526|title=GEDBAS: Vorfahren von Frederick Christ TRUMP|publisher=|access-date=November 10, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161104075957/https://gedbas.genealogy.net/person/ancestors/1178251526|archive-date=November 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>
