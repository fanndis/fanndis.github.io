'''Expedia.com''' is an online [[travel agency]] and [[metasearch engine]] owned by [[Expedia Group]], an American online travel shopping company based in [[Seattle]].{{check |reason=Not Bellevue? |date=June 2020}} The [[website]] and [[mobile app]] can be used to book [[airline]] tickets, [[hotel]] reservations, [[car rental]]s, [[cruise ship]]s, and vacation packages.

It is owned and operated by [[Expedia Group]], ranked first on the [[list of top earning travel companies]].

==History==
Expedia.com launched on October 22, 1996 as a division of [[Microsoft]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.rewindandcapture.com/why-is-expedia-called-expedia/ |title=Why is Expedia Called Expedia? {{!}} Rewind & Capture|website=www.rewindandcapture.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-12-04}}</ref> In 1999, [[Microsoft]] spun off the company as a [[public company]].<ref>[http://couponasaurus.com/microsoft-announces-expedia-online-travel-service-initial-public-offering/ Microsoft Announces Expedia Online Travel Service Initial Public Offering]</ref> [[Rich Barton]] became Expedia's [[CEO]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/2013/06/rich-barton-empowers-people-and-picks-fights-very-profitably/|title=The Man Who Escaped Microsoft and Took a Whole Company With Him|work=WIRED|access-date=2017-12-04|language=en-US}}</ref>

In July 2001, [[IAC (company)|USA Networks, Inc.]] bought Expedia from Microsoft.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://viewfinder.expedia.com/news/expedia-com-usa-networks-inc-announces-agreement-to-purchase-controlling-stake-in-expedia-becoming-a-leader-in-interactive-travel/|title=Expedia.com USA Networks, Inc., Announces Agreement to Purchase Controlling Stake in Expedia, Becoming a Leader in Interactive Travel (press release)|publisher=Expedia Viewfinder|date=2001-07-16|accessdate=2016-03-24}}</ref>

In December 2010, listings for [[AMR Corporation]], the parent company of [[American Airlines]] and [[Envoy Air|American Eagle Airlines]], were deleted from Expedia's site. The decision resulted from a dispute over the degree of access to the site's customers.<ref>{{cite news|author=The CNN Wire Staff|title=Online travel site Expedia removes American Airlines flights|url=http://www.cnn.com/2011/TRAVEL/01/02/american.expedia/index.html|publisher=[[CNN]]|date=January 3, 2011|accessdate=14 May 2014}}</ref> AMR reversed its decision in April 2011, allowing tickets to once again be sold through the aggregate site.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://money.cnn.com/2011/04/05/news/companies/american_airlines_expedia/index.htm|title=American Airlines reforms partnership with Expedia – Apr. 5, 2011|last=writer|first=By Aaron Smith, staff|website=money.cnn.com|access-date=2017-12-27}}</ref>

In June 2014, Expedia started accepting [[bitcoin]]s.<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2014/06/11/expedia-now-accepts-bitcoin-for-your-crypto-vacations/ Expedia Now Accepts Bitcoin For Your Crypto-Vacations]</ref>

In September 2014, [[Expedia Inc.]] partnered with [[Citigroup]] and created the Expedia+ card whose members can earn bonus points and earn benefits and priority customer service through the website.<ref>[http://www.creditcards.com/credit-cards/expedia-card-from-citi.php Expedia+ Card From Citi.]</ref>

In 2015, the company announced it would be moving its headquarters to [[Seattle]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.geekwire.com/2015/expedia-confirms-seattle-move/|title=Expedia confirms Seattle move, will buy Amgen campus for $229M, relocate from Bellevue by 2018|date=2015-02-05|work=GeekWire|access-date=2018-06-30|language=en-US}}</ref> The relocation to Seattle is currently scheduled for 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.geekwire.com/2015/expedia-confirms-seattle-move/|title=Expedia clears major planning milestone in preparation for 2019 HQ move to Seattle|date=2017-06-14|work=GeekWire|access-date=2018-06-30|language=en-US}}</ref>
