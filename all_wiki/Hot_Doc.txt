'''''Hot Doc''''' is a [[Greece|Greek]] [[news magazine]], launched in April 2012 by its owner and editor [[Kostas Vaxevanis]]. 

In October 2012, it published a special issue containing a list of names claimed to be the contents of the [[Lagarde list]]. On 28 October 2012, Vaxevanis was arrested in relation to its publication.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-20116548|title=Greece arrests journalist over 'Lagarde List' banks leak|accessdate=28 October 2012|work=BBC News|date=28 October 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Greek magazine editor in court for naming alleged tax evaders|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2012/oct/28/greek-editor-court-tax-evaders|work=The Guardian|accessdate=29 October 2012|author=Julian Borger|date=28 October 2012|location=London}}</ref> Vaxevanis' trial began on 1 November<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.cnn.com/2012/11/01/world/europe/greece-journalist-trial/index.html |title=Greek journalist on trial over printing Swiss bank account list |author=Diana Magnay |date=1 November 2012 |work=CNN |accessdate=1 November 2012 |archivedate=1 November 2012 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6Br1fWM90?url=http://www.cnn.com/2012/11/01/world/europe/greece-journalist-trial/index.html |url-status=live }}</ref> and ended the same day with an acquittal.<ref>{{cite news|title=Greek bank list editor Costas Vaxevanis acquitted |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-20172516|work=BBC News|date=1 November 2012}}</ref>
