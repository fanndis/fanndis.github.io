'''Brendan O'Neill''' is a British columnist. He is the editor of [[Spiked (magazine)|''Spiked'']] and has been a columnist for ''[[The Australian]]'' and ''[[The Big Issue]]''.

Once a [[Trotskyism|Trotskyist]] [[Marxism|Marxist]], O'Neill was formerly a member of the [[Revolutionary Communist Party (UK, 1978)|Revolutionary Communist Party]] and wrote for the party's journal, ''[[Living Marxism]]''. O'Neill self identifies as a [[libertarian Marxism|Libertarian Marxist]]<ref>{{cite web |title=The Rubin Report 'What is a Marxist Libertarian?|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lHUjZd63z9U |accessdate=14 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Marsh |first1=Natasha |title=Brendan O’Neill, atheist blogger and the Church’s biggest defender |url=https://www.catholicweekly.com.au/brendan-oneill-atheist-blogger-and-the-churchs-biggest-defender/ |website=The Catholic Weekly |publisher=The Catholic Weekly (Australian)}}</ref> and writes for a range of publications.

==Career==
He began his career at ''Spiked'''s predecessor, ''[[Living Marxism]]'', the journal of the [[Revolutionary Communist Party (UK, 1978)|Revolutionary Communist Party]], which ceased publication after [[ITN]] won their [[Penny Marshall (UK journalist)|libel action]] against it.<ref>Well, Matt (31 March 2000). [https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2000/mar/31/medialaw.media LM closes after losing libel action]. ''[[The Guardian]]''.</ref>

Since then, O'Neill has contributed articles to publications in the United Kingdom, the United States and Australia including ''[[The Spectator]]'', the ''[[New Statesman]]'', [[BBC News Online]], ''[[The Christian Science Monitor]]'', ''[[The American Conservative]]'', ''[[Salon (website)|Salon]]'', [[Rising East]] and occasionally blogged for ''[[The Guardian]]'',<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/global/2008/jun/03/brendanoneill|title=Brendan O'Neill Profile|publisher=guardian.co.uk|accessdate=2009-03-02 | location=London | date=3 June 2008}}</ref> before moving to ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blogs.telegraph.co.uk/news/author/brendanoneill2/|title=Brendan O'Neill|publisher=Telegraph Media Group|accessdate=2012-07-01|url-status=bot: unknown|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110109070250/http://blogs.telegraph.co.uk/news/author/brendanoneill2/|archivedate=9 January 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> He writes a column for ''[[The Big Issue]]'' in London and ''[[The Australian]]'' in Sydney. He also writes articles for ''The Sun''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://muckrack.com/brendan-oneill/articles|title=Articles by Brendan O'Neill &#124; The Spectator Journalist &#124; Muck Rack|website=muckrack.com}}</ref>

O'Neill has served as a visiting fellow and columnist with the Australian libertarian think-tank, the [[Centre for Independent Studies]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cis.org.au/research-scholars/contributors/author/656-oneill-brendan|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150108163459/http://www.cis.org.au/research-scholars/contributors/author/656-oneill-brendan|url-status=dead|title=Centre for Independent Studies - Brendan O'Neill|archivedate=8 January 2015}}</ref> as well as being a keynote speaker for the pro-Israel advocacy organisation [[StandWithUs]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.standwithus.co.il/student-conference/|title=StandWithUs UK 5th Annual Student Conference}}</ref>

Writing as the fictional character "Ethan Greenhart", O'Neill is the author of ''Can I Recycle My Granny?'', a satire of the green movement published by Hodder & Stoughton in 2008.<ref>[https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=i9rtAAAAMAAJ&dq=ethan%20greenhart%20brendan%20o%27neill&source=gbs_book_other_versions ''Can I Recycle My Granny?: And 39 Other Eco-Dilemmas''], Ethan Greenhart, Hodder & Stoughton, 2008</ref>

==Views==
{{Primary sources|section|date=June 2020}}

O'Neill has opposed efforts to combat [[climate change]] through reductions in carbon emissions, and instead advocates for "technological progress".<ref name="spiked">{{ cite news | title='Apocalypse, my arse' | url=http://www.spiked-online.com/index.php?/site/article/2948/|date = 9 March 2007|work = Spiked-online.com}}</ref> He criticised the environmentalist activist [[Greta Thunberg]] in his 2019 article "The Cult of Greta Thunberg"<ref name="O'Neill2019">{{cite news|last=O'Neill|first=Brendan|url=https://www.spiked-online.com/2019/04/22/the-cult-of-greta-thunberg/|title=The cult of Greta Thunberg|work=Spiked|date=22 April 2019|accessdate=25 April 2019}}</ref> in which he describes her as a "[[millenarian]] weirdo" and criticises the allegedly "monotone voice" speech patterns<ref name="Hancock">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2019/apr/25/greta-thunberg-autism-spectrum-critics|author=Charlie Hancock|title=Like Greta Thunberg, I am on the autism spectrum. She gives me hope|work=[[The Guardian]]|date= 25 April 2019|accessdate=23 August 2019}}</ref><ref name="Moran">{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/greta-thunberg-parliament-meeting-brendan-oneill-a8882961.html|first=Layla|last=Moran|authorlink=Layla Moran|title=Greta Thunberg has changed the course of history – what has Brendan O’Neill achieved?|work=[[The Independent]]|date=23 April 2019|accessdate=23 August 2019}}</ref> of the Swedish environmentalist who speaks [[English as a second or foreign language|English as a second language]]. O'Neill has described warnings over [[overpopulation]] as a "[[Malthusian]]" interference in women's right to reproductive freedom.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Revkin |first1=Andrew C. |authorlink1=Andrew Revkin |title=Deconstructing a Bestiary of Malthusian 'Miserabilists' |url=https://dotearth.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/01/19/deconstructing-a-bestiary-of-malthusian-miserabilists/ |accessdate=20 September 2020 |work=Dot Earth |publisher=The New York Times Blog |date=19 January 2011}}</ref>

In a 2012 ''[[HuffPost|Huffington Post]]'' article ,<ref name="O'Neill12">{{cite news|last=O'Neill|first=Brendan|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/brendan-oneill/jimmy-savile-child-abuse_b_2017326.html|title=If You Were Abused By Jimmy Savile, Maybe You Should Keep It to Yourself|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|date=26 December 2012|accessdate=20 October 2017}}</ref> O'Neill argued against victims of sexual abuse by high-profile individuals  coming forward publicly, stating: "I think there is more virtue in keeping the abuse as a firm part of your past, rather than offering it up to a scandal-hungry media and abuse-obsessed society that are desperate for more episodes of perversion to pore over".<ref name="O'Neill12"/>

He considers efforts to combat racism in football to be "a [[class warfare|class war]]" driven by "elites' utter incomprehension of the mass passions that get aired at football matches".<ref name="football">{{cite web |url=http://www.spiked-online.com/index.php/site/article/11963/ |title=This isn’t anti-racism – it’s the policing of passion |work=Spiked-online.com |date=10 January 2012 |accessdate=8 November 2013|url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109003301/http://www.spiked-online.com/index.php/site/article/11963/ |archivedate=9 November 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Referring to high-profile cases of racial abuse and alleged racial abuse, he argued, "these incidents and alleged incidents are not racism at all, in the true meaning of the word", due to the levels of passion involved, describing anti-racism efforts as "a pretty poisonous desire to police the ... working classes".<ref name="football" />

O'Neill has described himself as "an [[atheist]]ic libertarian". He is opposed to the legalization of [[same-sex marriage]] in Australia, arguing that it has been "attended by [[authoritarianism]] wherever it’s been introduced"<ref>{{ cite news | title='Gay marriage and the death of freedom' | url=https://www.spectator.com.au/2014/12/gay-marriage-and-the-death-of-freedom/|date = 6 December 2014|work = The Spectator}}</ref> and criticised opposition to [[Pope Benedict XVI's visit to the United Kingdom]] as intolerant and fearmongering.<ref>{{cite news

In September 2019, he said on the BBC's ''[[Politics Live]]'' that British people should be rioting about delays to [[Brexit]].<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Newsweek |date=27 September 2019 |url=https://www.newsweek.com/brexit-riots-bbc-guest-eu-protests-1461795 |title=BBC guest says people should riot over Brexit delays: 'Why have the British people been so patient?'}}</ref> He said: "I'm amazed that there haven't been riots yet." When asked by guest presenter [[Adam Fleming (journalist)|Adam Fleming]]: "Do you think there will be riots?", O'Neill responded: "I think there should be." In October 2019, 585 complaints about him calling for riots were dismissed by the BBC's executive complaints unit.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tobbitt |first1=Charlotte |title=BBC dismisses 600 complaints over Brendan O'Neill's Brexit riots claim on Politics LIve |url=https://www.pressgazette.co.uk/bbc-dismisses-600-complaints-brendan-oneill-spiked-brexit-leavers-riot-politics-live/ |website=Press Gazette |publisher=Press Gazette |accessdate=15 October 2019}}</ref>

O'Neill and others associated with the [[Revolutionary Communist Party (UK, 1978)|Revolutionary Communist Party]] (RCP), ''Living Marxism'' and ''Spiked''—including [[Frank Furedi]], [[Mick Hume]] and [[Claire Fox]]—are often seen by commenters such as [[Nick Cohen]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.newhumanist.org.uk/2007/01/nick-cohen-vs-institute-of-ideas.html |title=Nick Cohen vs the Institute of Ideas - New Humanist Blog (Rationalist Association) |publisher=Blog.newhumanist.org.uk |date=2007-01-30 |accessdate=2013-11-08}}</ref> as having shifted from a far left position to an extreme stance on the libertarian right. Although O'Neill says he is part of the left,<ref>{{cite web |last=O'Neill |first=Brendan |url=http://blogs.telegraph.co.uk/news/brendanoneill2/100169490/why-its-now-safe-to-say-i-love-marx/ |title=Why it’s now safe to say I love Marx – Telegraph Blogs |publisher=Blogs.telegraph.co.uk |date=6 July 2012|accessdate=8 November 2013|url-status=bot: unknown |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120709112535/http://blogs.telegraph.co.uk/news/brendanoneill2/100169490/why-its-now-safe-to-say-i-love-marx/ |archivedate=9 July 2012 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> critics such as [[George Monbiot]] have suggested that this is typical as a ploy adopted by those associated with the Revolutionary Communist Party (RCP) to split and discredit consensus upon the left<ref>{{cite web|author=George Monbiot |url=https://www.theguardian.com/education/2003/dec/09/highereducation.uk2 |title=George Monbiot: Invasion of the entryists &#124; Education |publisher=The Guardian |date=2003-12-09 |accessdate=2013-11-08}}</ref> and to cause impediments for such movements as environmentalism and the reduction of carbon emissions.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.monbiot.com/2009/01/13/flying-over-the-cuckoos-nest/ |title=Flying Over the Cuckoo’s Nest &#124; George Monbiot |website=Monbiot.com |date=January 13, 2009|accessdate=November 8, 2013}}</ref>
