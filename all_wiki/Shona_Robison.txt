'''Shona McRory Robison''' (born 26 May 1966) is a [[Scottish people|Scottish]] [[politician]] who is the [[Scottish National Party]] [[Member of the Scottish Parliament]] for [[Dundee City East (Scottish Parliament constituency)|Dundee City East]] since 2011 and was [[Cabinet Secretary for Health and Sport]] 2014–2018. She was elected as a [[Scottish National Party]] candidate in the [[2003 Scottish Parliament election|2003 election]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/2994527.stm | title=Labour suffers election casualties | date=2 May 2003 | work=[[BBC News]] | accessdate=3 May 2008 }}</ref> Previously she had represented the SNP as an [[Additional Members System]] member for [[North East Scotland (Scottish Parliament electoral region)|North East Scotland]], having been elected in the [[1999 Scottish Parliament election|1999 election]].<ref name="session1">{{cite web |url=http://www.parliament.scot/msps/32108.aspx |title=Previous MSPs: Session 1 (1999-2003): Shona Robison MSP |publisher=[[Scottish Parliament]] |accessdate=14 May 2016}}</ref> She first became a member of the Cabinet in April 2014.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life and career==

Born in [[Redcar]], England, she went to [[Alva Academy]], and then gained an MA in Social Sciences at [[Glasgow University]] in 1989, and gained a Postgraduate Cert in Community Education, obtained in 1990 at [[Jordanhill College]]. She was active in the [[Young Scots for Independence|SNP Youth Wing]] along with figures such as [[Nicola Sturgeon]] and [[Fiona Hyslop]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-43988436|title=Profile: Shona Robison|date=2018-06-26|access-date=2019-08-19|language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Political career==
In 2003 she became the SNP's Party Spokesperson on Health, remaining in this position until 2007.<ref name="session2">{{cite web |url=http://www.parliament.scot/msps/30574.aspx |title=Previous MSPs: Session 2 (2003-2007): Shona Robison MSP |publisher=Scottish Parliament |accessdate=14 May 2016}}</ref>

After the SNP's victory in the [[2007 Scottish Parliament election|2007 Scottish Parliament Election]], Robison was appointed as the [[Minister for Public Health (Scotland)|Minister for Public Health]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/the-scottish-executive-1.842205 | title=The Scottish Executive | date=17 May 2007 | work=[[The Herald (Glasgow)]] | accessdate=27 September 2014}}</ref> In 2009, she was made [[Minister for Public Health and Sport]] in a Ministerial reshuffle.<ref name="session3">{{cite web|url=http://www.parliament.scot/msps/26650.aspx |title=Previous MSPs: Session 3 (2007-2011): Shona Robison MSP |publisher=Scottish Parliament |accessdate=14 May 2016}}</ref> Following the 2011 election, she was made [[Minister for Commonwealth Games and Sport]], with [[Michael Matheson (politician)|Michael Matheson]] taking on the [[Minister for Public Health]] portfolio.  In April 2014, [[Alex Salmond]] promoted her to the full Cabinet position of [[Cabinet Secretary for Commonwealth Games, Sport, Equalities and Pensioners' Rights]]. Robison was moved to the Health brief, as [[Cabinet Secretary for Health, Wellbeing and Sport]], in [[Nicola Sturgeon]]'s first Ministerial reshuffle in November 2014. On 18 May 2016 Sturgeon reshuffled her cabinet with Robison remaining, with her position now being called Cabinet Secretary for Health and Sport.<ref name="BBC 18May2016">{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-36323276 | title=Scottish cabinet reshuffle: John Swinney becomes education secretary | work=BBC News | date=18 May 2016 | accessdate=21 March 2017}}</ref>

On 26 June 2018 her resignation from the Cabinet was announced, immediately ahead of a cabinet reshuffle.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-44612923 |title=Shona Robison quits as Nicola Sturgeon reshuffles cabinet |work=BBC News |date=26 June 2018 |accessdate=11 November 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==

She was married to [[Stewart Hosie]],<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.parliament.the-stationery-office.co.uk/pa/cm200708/cmhansrd/cm080306/debtext/80306-0018.htm | title=International Women’s Day Debate | date=6 March 2008 | work=House of Commons Hansard Debates | accessdate=3 May 2008 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110605021451/http://www.parliament.the-stationery-office.co.uk/pa/cm200708/cmhansrd/cm080306/debtext/80306-0018.htm | archivedate=5 June 2011 | df=dmy-all }}</ref> who is the Westminster MP for Dundee East and was previously the SNP's Depute Leader. They have one daughter. It was announced on 15 May 2016 that the couple have separated.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.scotsman.com/news/snp-husband-and-wife-stewart-hosie-and-shona-robison-separate-1-4129072|title=SNP husband and wife Stewart Hosie and Shona Robison separate|work=The Scotsman|date=16 May 2016|accessdate=11 November 2018}}</ref> This was followed by reports that Hosie had had an affair with Westminster-based freelance journalist [[Serena Cowdy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/05/16/snps-stewart-hosie-and-angus-macneil-split-from-wives-over-alleg/|title=SNP MPs Stewart Hosie and Angus MacNeil split from wives over alleged affairs with same woman|first=Telegraph|last=Reporters|date=17 May 2016|publisher=|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}}</ref>
