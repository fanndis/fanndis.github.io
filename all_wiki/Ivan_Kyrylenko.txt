'''Ivan Hryhorovych Kyrylenko''' ({{lang-uk|Іван Григорович Кириленко}}; born on October 2, 1956 in [[Berestove]] ([[Zaporizhia Oblast]]), [[Ukrainian SSR]])<ref name=bio>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.vlada.kiev.ua/fcontent.php?pacode=311&topic=87924 Biography] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090302105756/http://www.vlada.kiev.ua/fcontent.php?pacode=311 |date=2009-03-02 }}, Довідники про сучасну Україну</ref> is a [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] politician and from 2007 till December 2011 [[Parliamentary group leader|faction leader]] of [[Yulia Tymoshenko Bloc]] in the [[Ukrainian Parliament]].<ref name=UnianFL/><ref name=nflBYuTAndriy>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/118331/ BYT-Batkivschyna replaces its leader], [[Kyiv Post]] (7 December 2011)</ref><ref name=Kyrygo>[http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/118352/ Tymoshenko aware of change in leadership of BYT-Batkivschyna faction], [[Kyiv Post]] (7 December 2011)</ref>

==Education==
In 1978 he graduated from the Dnepropetrovsk Agricultural Institute, specializing in agricultural scientist. In 1991, Kirilenko was a graduate of the Academy of Social Sciences in Moscow, the specialty analyst.

Doctor of Economics, Ph.D. in History. He defended his thesis entitled "Social development of village: Experience, Problems, Prospects (for example Prydniprovia USSR)" in 1991 at the Academy of Social Sciences (Moscow), and in 1997 ibid - doctoral thesis "The formation and development of the agricultural economy in the form of a market transformation."

== Labor and political activity ==

Before becoming a politician Kyrylenko worked as head of a [[collective farm]] (kolkhoz) and as a civil servant in the Ministry of Agriculture of the [[Dnipropetrovsk Oblast]].<ref name=bio/>

He was first elected into Parliament on an independent candidate on in December 1995 he then joined the faction [[Unity (Ukraine)|Unity]].<ref name=bio/> At the [[1998 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] Kyrylenko was elected into Parliament on a [[Hromada (political party)|Hromada]] ticket.<ref name=bio/><ref>[http://pdc.ceu.hu/archive/00001360/01/14.pdf Summing up some conclusions from the election process], Policy Documentation Center ([[Central European University]]); April 6, 1998</ref> When [[Yulia Tymoshenko]] set up the breakaway [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"]] faction Kyrylenko joined her.<ref name=bio/>

Kyrylenko left Parliament 2001<ref>[http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vd2002/WEBPROC66e?kodvib=1 People's deputies, who have left], [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref> to become Minister of Agriculture (in the [[Kinakh Government]]) and one of the founding members of (the now defunct electoral bloc) [[For United Ukraine]] in 2001.<ref>http://www.jamestown.org/single/?no_cache=1&tx_ttnews[tt_news]=23581&tx_ttnews[backPid]=215</ref> At the time of [[2002 Ukrainian parliamentary election|the next elections]] he was a member of the [[People's Party (Ukraine)|Agrarian Party]] (a part of For United Ukraine).<ref name=bio/> Kyrylenko was Deputy Prime Minister in the [[First Yanukovych Government]] (2002-January 2005) cabinet of [[Viktor Yanukovych]].<ref>[http://www.unhcr.org/refworld/pdfid/3f4f25143.pdf UKRAINE COUNTRY ASSESSMENT April 2003], [[UNHCR]] (April 2003)</ref><ref>[http://www2.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2003/3/6/2614.htm Rada strips moonlighting MPs of powers] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110617071651/http://www.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2003/3/6/2614.htm/ |date=2011-06-17 }}, [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (6-3-2003)</ref><ref>[http://www2.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2002/11/26/2233.htm KUCHMA HAILS NEW AZAROV'S POST] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110617071742/http://www.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2002/11/26/2233.htm/ |date=2011-06-17 }}, [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (26-11-2002)</ref><ref>[http://www2.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2006/4/3/5083.htm Yulia Tymoshenko’s orbits] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090601060900/http://www2.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2006/4/3/5083.htm |date=2009-06-01 }}, [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (20-3-2006)</ref>

In [[2006 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2006]] and [[2007 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2007]] he was elected into Parliament on an [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"]] ticket.<ref name=data>[http://da-ta.com.ua/effort_authority/1663.htm Biography], D A T A</ref>  According to [[Yulia Tymoshenko]], Kyrylenko is her “godfather in politics”.<ref>[http://www.pravda.com.ua/en/news/2007/12/11/9571.htm The Coming Government of Yulia Tymoshenko], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (11-12-2007)</ref> After the 2007 election he was elected [[Parliamentary group leader|faction leader]] of [[Yulia Tymoshenko Bloc]] in the [[Ukrainian Parliament]].<ref name=UnianFL>[http://unian.net/eng/news/news-227294.html Ivan Kyrylenko elected on post of chairman of BYUT faction], [[UNIAN]] (19-12-2007)</ref> The faction re-elected as its faction leader [[Andriy Kozhemiakin]].<ref name=nflBYuTAndriy/><ref name=Kyrygo/>

Kyrylenko was placed at number 15 on the electoral list of [[Batkivshchina]] during the [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election]]; he was re-elected into parliament.<ref name="electedintoVRUK111112">{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/articles/2012/11/11/6977259/ Список депутатів нової Верховної Ради], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (11 November 2012)</ref><ref>[http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/58995 They Call Themselves the Opposition], [[The Ukrainian Week]] (31 August 2012)</ref> He served on the [[Committee of the Verkhovna Rada on issues of European integration]].

In the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] he was again re-elected into parliament; this time after placing 14th on the electoral list of Batkivshchina.<ref name="allcountedCECIU81114">[http://www.ukrinform.ua/eng/news/poroshenko_bloc_to_have_greatest_number_of_seats_in_parliament_327072 Poroshenko Bloc to have greatest number of seats in parliament] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141112000000/http://www.ukrinform.ua/eng/news/poroshenko_bloc_to_have_greatest_number_of_seats_in_parliament_327072 |date=2014-11-12 }}, [[Ukrinform]] (8 November 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233404.html People's Front 0.33% ahead of Poroshenko Bloc with all ballots counted in Ukraine elections - CEC] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141112000000/http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233404.html |date=2014-11-12 }}, [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (8 November 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233426.html Poroshenko Bloc to get 132 seats in parliament - CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (8 November 2014)</ref><ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://tvi.ua/new/2014/09/15/povnyy_vyborchyy_spysok_partiyi_batkivschyna Full electoral list of "Fatherland"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140915212430/http://tvi.ua/new/2014/09/15/povnyy_vyborchyy_spysok_partiyi_batkivschyna |date=2014-09-15 }}, [[TVi (TV channel)|TVi]] (15 September 2014)</ref>

== Scientific activity ==

Published more than 100 scientific works, including some 10 books, including five in collaboration and 2 monographs.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://uaan.gov.ua/content/kirilenko-ivan-grigorovich |title=Website of the National Academy of Agrarian Sciences of Ukraine |access-date=2013-04-21 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130706184834/http://uaan.gov.ua/content/kirilenko-ivan-grigorovich |archive-date=2013-07-06 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Trained Doctors 2 and 3 candidates.

Elected in 2002, a corresponding member of Academy of Agrarian Sciences Research Office of Transfer of innovation.
