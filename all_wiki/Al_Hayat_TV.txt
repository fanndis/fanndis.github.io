'''Alhayat TV''', also known as '''Life TV''' (قناة الحياة), is an [[evangelicalism|evangelical]] [[Christianity|Christian]] [[Arabic language|Arabic]]-language  television channel that airs in countries in [[North Africa]], [[West Asia]], the [[Middle East]], [[United States|America]], [[Canada]], [[Australia]] and some of [[Europe]].<ref name="AU">[http://www.hayatv.tv/home/about-us/3069.html Al Haya TV] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091228001053/http://www.hayatv.tv/home/about-us/3069.html |date=December 28, 2009 }}</ref>

==History==
The channel was founded in [[Cyprus]] in 2003 by Al Hayat Ministries, an evangelical organization.<ref>Al Hayat Ministries, [http://alhayat.org/en-us/ouroutreach/oursupporters.aspx Our Supporters] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150405233638/http://alhayat.org/en-us/ouroutreach/oursupporters.aspx |date=2015-04-05 }}, Official Website, USA, retrieved August 1, 2017</ref><ref> Naomi Sakr, ''Arab Television Today'', I.B.Tauris, UK, 2007, p. 152</ref> 

==Programs==
Al Hayat TV broadcasts programs designed by different Christian ministries.<ref> Al Hayat Ministries, [https://alhayat.org/programs/ Programs], alhayat.org, USA, retrieved November 14, 2020</ref> One of his most famous programs is ''Daring Question'', hosted by [[Brother Rachid]] and broadcast live<ref name="OW">[http://www.hayatv.tv/%D8%A7%D8%B3%D9%84%D8%A7%D9%85%D9%8A%D8%A7%D8%AA/3006.html Official Website] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100117050615/http://www.hayatv.tv/%D8%A7%D8%B3%D9%84%D8%A7%D9%85%D9%8A%D8%A7%D8%AA/3006.html |date=January 17, 2010 }}</ref> [[Joyce Meyer]] has programs translated into Arabic.<ref> Omar Abdel-Razak, bbc.co.uk, [http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00t7g8z Conversion Wars], UK, August 9, 2010</ref>

==Controversies==
Alhayat TV became controversial for content that is heavily critical of [[Islam]] and the [[Prophet Mohammed]]. Its programs are much debated and sometimes the subject of angry criticism from Muslims who claim that Alhayat's content is biased and inaccurate.<ref name="IEN1">{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalreview.com/article/223965/islams-public-enemy-1-raymond-ibrahim|title=Islam's Public Enemy #1|date=March 25, 2008|author=[[Raymond Ibrahim]]|publisher=National Review}}</ref> 

[[Zakaria Botros]], an [[orthodox Christianity|orthodox]] Egyptian-born priest who was twice arrested by Arab authorities, is often cited for his bold tone and exposition of contradictions he perceives in Islam.<ref name="AU" /><ref name="IEN1" /><ref name="OW" /> In 2010, he was expelled from al-Hayat due to threats of violence from radical Muslims.<ref name="BBC">Omar Abdel-Razek, [https://www.bbc.com/arabic/artandculture/2010/05/100517_malayuqal_christians_bbc_tc2 ما لا يقال: هل توقف قناة "الحياة" برامج القمص زكريا بطرس المثيرة للجدل؟], bbc.com, UK, May 18, 2010</ref>

[[Grand Imam of al-Azhar|Al-Azhar]] expressed concerns over the broadcasting of Alhayat TV, saying that the Coptic channel was offensive to Muslims and Islam.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.egyptindependent.com/news/al-azhar-condemns-christian-channel-promoting-sedition|title=Al-Azhar condemns Christian channel for promoting sedition {{!}} Egypt Independent|access-date=April 15, 2017|language=en}}</ref>
