{{main|2001 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Venus Williams]] successfully defended her title, defeating [[Justine Henin]] in the final, 6–1, 3–6, 6–0 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2001 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref>

This tournament saw a second first-round loss for World No. 1 [[Martina Hingis]] at Wimbledon, who previously fell in the first round in [[1999 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1999]]. This time she fell to World No. 83 [[Virginia Ruano Pascual]]. The top seed would not lose in the first round of a Grand Slam again until the [[2017 French Open – Women's Singles|2017 French Open]], a span of 62 Grand Slam majors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/makarova-scores-kerber-upset-roland-garros|title=Makarova scores Kerber upset at Roland Garros|publisher=Women's Tennis Association|date=28 May 2017}}</ref>
