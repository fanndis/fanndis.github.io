[[Lady Gaga]] is an American singer, songwriter, and actress who has received many awards and nominations. She rose to prominence with the release of her debut album ''[[The Fame]]'' in 2008. The album won several awards and was nominated for six [[Grammy Award]]s, including [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]]. The album and its single "[[Poker Face (Lady Gaga song)|Poker Face]]" won [[Grammy Award for Best Electronic/Dance Album|Best Electronic/Dance Album]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording|Best Dance Recording]], respectively, at the [[52nd Annual Grammy Awards|52nd Grammy Awards]]. The album also won [[Brit Award for International Album|International Album]] at the [[2010 BRIT Awards]]. A follow-up EP, titled ''[[The Fame Monster]]'', was released in 2009, and included the singles "[[Bad Romance]]" and "[[Telephone (song)|Telephone]]". The music videos of the songs won eight accolades from thirteen nominations at the [[2010 MTV Video Music Awards]] (VMA), making Gaga the most nominated artist in VMA history for a single year and the first female artist to receive two nominations for [[MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]] in one single night.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lady Gaga's 13 VMA Nominations: How Do They Measure Up?|first=Gil|last=Kaufman|publisher=[[MTV News]]|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1644983/20100803/lady_gaga.jhtml|date=August 3, 2010|accessdate=September 18, 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100905062626/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1644983/20100803/lady_gaga.jhtml|archivedate=September 5, 2010}}</ref> In 2011, Gaga was nominated for six Grammy Awards, and won three—[[Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album|Best Pop Vocal Album]] for ''The Fame Monster'', and [[Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance|Best Female Pop Vocal Performance]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Short Form Music Video|Best Short Form Music Video]] for "Bad Romance".

''[[Born This Way (album)|Born This Way]]'' (2011), Gaga's second studio album, accrued three nominations at the [[54th Annual Grammy Awards]], including her third consecutive nomination for Album of the Year.{{efn|Gaga, [[Beyoncé]] and [[Mariah Carey]] are the only female artists to have lost the [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year]] three times, and they all hold the record for second-most Album of the Year nominations (three) for a female artist after [[Barbra Streisand]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://goldderby.latimes.com/awards_goldderby/2009/06/can-mariah-carey-finally-win-record-of-year-grammy-by-dissing-eminem-1357986-news.html|title=Can Mariah Carey finally win top Grammy Awards by dissing Eminem?|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=June 18, 2009|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120117063116/http://goldderby.latimes.com/awards_goldderby/2009/06/can-mariah-carey-finally-win-record-of-year-grammy-by-dissing-eminem-1357986-news.html|archivedate=January 17, 2012}}</ref>}} It won the [[People's Choice Awards|People's Choice Award]] for Album of the Year the following year, and the music video for the [[Born This Way (song)|title track]] won two VMAs, including [[Best Female Video]]. Her third album, ''[[Artpop]]'' (2013), was nominated for [[Billboard Music Award for Top Dance/Electronic Album|Top Dance/Electronic Album]] at the [[2014 Billboard Music Awards|2014 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]]. In other musical ventures, Gaga released a collaborative jazz album with [[Tony Bennett]], titled ''[[Cheek to Cheek (album)|Cheek to Cheek]]'' (2014), which received the [[Grammy Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album]].

In 2015, Gaga released a song "[[Til It Happens to You]]", for the documentary film, ''[[The Hunting Ground]]''. The song won a [[Satellite Award for Best Original Song]], and was nominated for an [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Academy Award]] and a [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Song]], and a [[Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media]]. In the same year she was named [[Billboard Women in Music#Woman of the Year award|Woman of the Year]] by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. She also became the first woman to receive the Digital Diamond Award from [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/newsitem.php?content_selector=riaa-news-gold-and-platinum&news_month_filter=5&news_year_filter=2014&id=D7373738-7694-E23B-E24A-FF43FF9E7444|title=Lady Gaga Becomes RIAA's First Female Digital Diamond Award Recipient|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|accessdate=May 6, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150711234634/http://www.riaa.com/newsitem.php?content_selector=riaa-news-gold-and-platinum&news_month_filter=5&news_year_filter=2014&id=D7373738-7694-E23B-E24A-FF43FF9E7444|archivedate=July 11, 2015}}</ref> and the first artist to win the [[Songwriters Hall of Fame]]'s [[Songwriters Hall of Fame#Contemporary Icon Award|Contemporary Icon Award]] for "attaining an iconic status in [[popular culture|pop culture]]". Gaga received a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Miniseries or Television Film|Golden Globe Award for Best Actress]] and nominations at the [[42nd People's Choice Awards]] and [[20th Satellite Awards]] for her role in the fifth season of ''[[American Horror Story]]'', entitled ''[[American Horror Story: Hotel|Hotel]]''. She received two nominations at the [[Primetime Emmy Award|Emmy Awards]] for her concerts television specials ''[[Lady Gaga Presents the Monster Ball Tour: At Madison Square Garden]]'' and ''[[Tony Bennett and Lady Gaga: Cheek to Cheek Live!]]''. In addition to music awards, Gaga has earned several awards and nominations for her artistic and philanthropic efforts to society, such as a [[Do Something|Do Something! Award]]. In 2013, she finished second on ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'s}} readers' poll about the most influential people of the past ten years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://time100.time.com/2013/04/11/face-off-whos-the-most-influential/?iid=time100-page-lead|title=Who's the Most Influential?|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|accessdate=October 30, 2015|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022165524/http://time100.time.com/2013/04/11/face-off-whos-the-most-influential/?iid=time100-page-lead|archivedate=October 22, 2015}}</ref>

In 2018, Gaga starred in the musical romance ''[[A Star Is Born (2018 film)|A Star Is Born]]'', a remake film, which garnered her the [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Actress]], and nominations for an [[Academy Award for Best Actress|Academy Award]], a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Drama|Golden Globe]], and a [[BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role|BAFTA Award]]. She also contributed to the [[A Star Is Born (2018 soundtrack)|film's soundtrack]], which earned her the [[BAFTA Award for Best Film Music]]. For its lead single "[[Shallow (Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper song)|Shallow]]", she received an Academy Award and a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song]], and four nominations at the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards]], winning for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance|Best Pop Duo/Group Performance]] and Best Song Written for Visual Media. As a result, Gaga became the first person to win an Academy Award, Grammy, BAFTA and Golden Globe in one year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2019-02-24-with-her-first-oscar-win-lady-gaga-sets-an-incredible-awards-record/|title=With Her First Oscar Win, Lady Gaga Sets An Incredible Awards Record|last=Close|first=Paris|date=February 24, 2019|accessdate=February 25, 2019|website=[[iHeart Radio]]}}</ref> The following year, at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards]], Gaga won two more awards, bringing her total Grammy wins to eleven.
