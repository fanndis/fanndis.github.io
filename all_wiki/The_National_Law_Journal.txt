'''''The National Law Journal''''', a [[United States|U.S.]] [[periodical]] founded in 1978 by [[Jerry Finkelstein]], as a "sibling newspaper" of the ''[[New York Law Journal]]'', that itself was founded in 1888.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alm.com/about/history|title=History|work=alm.com|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140104032207/http://www.alm.com/about/history|archive-date=2014-01-04|df=}}</ref> ''The National Law Journal'' reports legal information of national importance to attorneys, including federal circuit court decisions, verdicts, practitioners' columns, coverage of legislative issues and legal news for the business and private sectors. The Journal releases its list of the "''100 Most Influential Lawyers in America''" once every few years.<ref>{{cite web |title=Who Are the 100 Most Influential Lawyers in America? |date=March 25, 2013 |publisher=Above the Law |url=http://abovethelaw.com/2013/03/who-are-the-100-most-influential-lawyers-in-america/}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=The 100 Most Influential Lawyers in America |publisher=The National Law Journal |url=http://www.nationallawjournal.com/id=1202593197565&The__Most_Influential_Lawyers}}</ref>

The once tabloid-sized weekly newspaper is now a monthly magazine that publishes online daily and is owned by American Lawyer Media, [[ALM (company)|ALM]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alm.com/|title=ALM - Insights. Innovation. Connected.|work=alm.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.alm.com/press_release/the-national-law-journal-unveils-monthly-print-magazine/|title=The National Law Journal Unveils Monthly Print Magazine}}</ref>

In September 2017, Lisa Helem was promoted to editor in chief.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.alm.com/press_release/alm-promotes-lisa-helem-to-editor-in-chief-of-the-national-law-journal//|title=ALM Promotes Lisa Helem to Editor-in-Chief of The National Law Journal}}</ref>
