'''Shamrock Holdings''', or '''Shamrock''', is a private equity firm founded as the [[Roy E. Disney]] family's investment firm; the Disney family remains its sole investor. Shamrock is a private corporation, and is fully owned by the estate of Roy E. Disney.

Disney was the chairman of Shamrock, and [[Stanley Gold]] was its president and [[CEO]]. Shamrock assets include hotels and radio and television stations. Shamrock often takes an [[Aggressiveness strategy|aggressive strategy]], launching hostile takeovers.

==History==
Shamrock Holdings Inc. (SHI) was founded in 1978 as an investment company by Roy E. Disney.<ref name=lat1>Johnson, Greg. (September 28, 2005). [http://articles.latimes.com/2005/sep/28/business/fi-globetrotters28 Roy Disney-Led Fund Buys 80% of Harlem Globetrotters]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref> Shamrock then started buying television and radio stations.  In the mid-1980s, SH moved into other industries.<ref name=lat>Apodaca, Patrice. (October 20, 1992). [http://articles.latimes.com/1992-10-20/business/fi-704_1_radio-station Shamrock to Expand Presence in Radio]. [[Los Angeles Times]].</ref> According to Reuters, in 1984 Shamrock held 1,428,061 (or just over 4%) of all Disney stock.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F05E0D71738F931A25757C0A962948260&n=Top%2fReference%2fTimes%20Topics%2fPeople%2fD%2fDisney%2c%20Roy%20E%2e |title=Shamrock holding 4.1% of Disney Stock, Reuters, April 12, 1984 | work=The New York Times | date=April 12, 1984 | accessdate=May 23, 2010}}</ref>

Shamrock Capital Advisors is a successor company to SHI.<ref name=SCA>[https://shamrockcap.com/ Shamrock Capital web page]. Retrieved 2019-03-27.</ref>

After revealing that the company held 4.7% of [[Central Soya]] and agreements with two investment groups for another 5.9% in March 1985, the company offered to purchase the remaining shares with equity partners.<ref>Harris, Kathryn. (March 15, 1985). [http://articles.latimes.com/1985-03-15/business/fi-35125_1_shamrock-spokesman-clifford-miller Disney Family Offers to Acquire Central Soya]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref> SH closed on the purchase of Central Soya. In November, Shamrock began construction on Shamrock Plaza office building in Agoura Hills estimated to cost $5.1 million.<ref name=lat2>{{cite news|title=Roy E. Disney Firm Building Agoura Offices|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1985-11-17/realestate/re-6800_1_office-building|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=November 17, 1985}}</ref>

In March 1986, Shamrock sold 1.6 million shares of the Walt Disney Co. on the open market dropping its share from 4% to 3% for tax planning and further business needs.<ref>(March 12, 1986). [http://articles.latimes.com/1986-03-12/business/fi-18592_1_shamrock-spokesman Shamrock Holdings reduced its stake in Disney]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In April 1987 its subsidiary, Shamrock Holdings of California purchased controlling interest in DBMS Inc. of Naperville, Ill., a software systems and services company.<ref>(April 22, 1987). [http://articles.latimes.com/1987-04-22/business/fi-49_1_california-shamrock-holdings Industry Notes: California]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref> After the management team of Holly Sugar submitted a plan to take the company private, two share holders Shamrock (5.5%) and Brookehill Equities (11.8%) filed separately with the SEC with the intent to possible make a counter offer.<ref>Bates, James. (January 20, 1987). [http://articles.latimes.com/1987-01-20/business/fi-5880_1_sugar-prices Burbank Investors May Bid on Sugar Company]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In fall 1987, Central Soya, a soybean processor, was sold by Shamrock to [[Ferruzzi Group]] of Italy making a profit of $125 million. In December 1987, the corporation attempted a buy out of [[Wherehouse Entertainment]], who avoid the take over by agreeing to be bought by Adler & Shaykin, a New York investment firm. Instead Shamrock purchased [[Music Plus]] from [[Show Industries]] in April 1988 to begin its move into home entertainment retailing.<ref>Groves, Martha. (April 8, 1988). [http://articles.latimes.com/1988-04-08/business/fi-1211_1_shamrock-holdings Shamrock Holdings Agrees to Buy 54-Store Music Plus Chain]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In 1988 and 1989, it pursued [[Polaroid Corporation|Polaroid]], ultimately failing to take over the company but winning concessions such as an advertising deal for its television stations.<ref name="lat polaroid">{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/1989/03/28/business/polaroid-payout-plan-helps-it-reach-shamrock-accord.html | work=The New York Times | title=Polaroid Payout Plan Helps It Reach Shamrock Accord | first=Robert J. | last=Cole | date=March 28, 1989 | accessdate=May 23, 2010}}</ref>

SH agreed in January 1990 to sell radio station [[KMGC-FM]] to [[Cooke Inlet Radio Partners]]  for $22 million.<ref>{{cite news|title=Shamrock Holdings Sells Dallas Radio Station|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1990-01-16/business/fi-53_1_radio-station|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=January 16, 1990}}</ref> 
In September 1991, Shamrock sold its remaining 11.8% share in [[Enterra]] for about $46.8 million.<ref>(September 16, 1991). [http://articles.latimes.com/1991-09-16/business/fi-1673_1_price-shamrock-s-stake Shamrock to Sell Enterra Stake]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In October 1992, the corporation agreed to sell to [[Blockbuster LLC|Blockbuster Entertainment Corporation]] its Music Plus and [[Sound Warehouse]] music and video chains for $185 million. SH indicated that purchase of additional radio stations were planned given depreciated values and changes in FCC regulations allowing more stations could be owned by a single owner.<ref name=lat/>

In August 1995, Shamrock agreed to sell Shamrock Broadcasting, a 19 radio stations group, to [[Chancellor Broadcasting]] Co. for $395 million.<ref name=lat3>{{cite news|last=Mulligan|first=Thomas S.|title=Company Town: Entertainment Upheaval|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1995-08-04/business/fi-31544_1_radio-stations|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles|date=August 4, 1995}}</ref>

Shamrock sold a 20% share of Koor Industries Ltd. to [[Claridge Israel]] Ltd. in 1997. In June 1999, Shamrock and First Israel Mezzanine Investors Ltd. purchased Tadiran Communications Ltd., an Israel defense communication contractor, from [[Tadiran]] Ltd., a subsidiary of Koor as of 1998.<ref>(June 15, 1999). [http://articles.latimes.com/1999/jun/15/business/fi-46576 Disney Family Buys Defense Contractor]. Bloomberg News. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In 1999, Shamrock Holdings of California Inc. (SH/CA) finished building its Manhattan Beach Studios at estimated cost of $82 million. The studios included 14 sound stages, eight production office buildings and Media Center, a 60,000-square-foot office building. Raleigh Enterprises was hired to manage the studio.<ref name=lat0>Vincent, Roger. (November 23, 2004). [http://articles.latimes.com/2004/nov/23/business/fi-studio23 Manhattan Beach Studios Sold]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref> Ronald Flesch, a real estate developer, and Alice Neuhauser, a motion picture financier, sued SH over the studio claiming that it was their idea they brought to Shamrock in a joint venture deal and that Shamrock cut them out of the project.<ref>(October 21, 2000). [http://articles.latimes.com/2000/oct/21/business/fi-39871 Studio Developers Sue Investment Firm]. Bloomberg News. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In November 2003, SH/CA sold its Manhattan Beach Studios to Oak Tree Capital Management Co. for about $100 million.<ref name=lat0/>

From 2003 to 2005, Shamrock was an instrumental force in the SaveDisney campaign and the ouster of [[Michael Eisner]] as CEO of [[The Walt Disney Company]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sec.gov/rules/proposed/s71903/shamrock032304.htm |title=Shamrock's Comments to SEC}}</ref> As of 2003 [[Roy E. Disney]] held about 1% of Disney stock through Shamrock Holdings. This made him the third largest individual stakeholder of Disney stock.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mywire.com/pubs/USATODAY/2003/12/02/373895?extID=10051 |title=Shamrock holding 1% of Disney Stock, USA Today Article, Dec 2003}}</ref>

Shamrock Holdings' Capital Growth Fund purchased an 80% ownership stake in the [[Harlem Globetrotters]] in September 2005 with the intent to increase merchandising and other income.<ref name=lat1/> By November, Shamrock Capital Advisors raised $125 million towards possible investments in Israeli companies from Israeli and U.S. pension funds, insurance companies and commercial banks.<ref>(November 03, 2005). [http://articles.latimes.com/2005/nov/03/business/fi-wrap3.1 Shamrock to Make Investments in Israel]. Bloomberg News. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In July 2006 one of Roy E. Disney's daughters, Abigail, became Vice Chairman of Shamrock. {{citation needed|date=April 2014}} In October, Shamrock Activist Value Fund sued [[iPass Inc.]], a SH holding, to turn over board room records over failure to fire their CEO and mismanagement of a take over.<ref>(October 12, 2006). [http://articles.latimes.com/2006/oct/12/business/fi-wrap12.1 Roy Disney's Investment Fund Sues Software Firm]. Bloomberg News. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In March 2007, the corporation's Capital Growth Fund invested $50 million into Real D company, a 3D movie equipment manufacturer.<ref>Menn, Joseph. (March 20, 2007). [http://articles.latimes.com/2007/mar/20/business/fi-reald20 Shamrock sees money in 3-D]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

Shamrock Holdings bought a 5.5% stake in [[Texas Industries]] in 2008.<ref>[http://www.dallasnews.com/sharedcontent/dws/bus/stories/111108dnbustibrf.19e9078c3.html  Disney family firm buys 5.5% of TXI]. Dallas News.</ref> Shamrock revealed in December 2008 that it held a 5.7% share of [[Arris Group]]. In October 2009, SH won a shareholders proxy fight at [[Texas Industries]], of which SH own 10% of the company, over management performance. The corporation took a stake in West Pharmaceutical Service by December 2009. Other holding in 2009 were: [[Coinstar]], [[Websense]], and [[Magellan Health Services]]. Roy E. Disney died on December 16, 2009.<ref>Altucher, James. (December 17, 2009). [http://www.dailyfinance.com/2009/12/17/finding-value-in-roy-disneys-shamrock-holdings/ Finding Value in Roy Disney's Shamrock Holdings] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131026193207/http://www.dailyfinance.com/2009/12/17/finding-value-in-roy-disneys-shamrock-holdings/ |date=2013-10-26 }}. Daily Finance (AOL Money & Finance). Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

In October 2018, Shamrock Capital acquired the songwriting catalogue of music producers [[Stargate (music producers)|Stargate]]. [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing]] will continue to administer the catalogue.<ref>Christman, Ed, [https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/8480173/shamrock-capital-acquires-stargate-publishing-catalog-including-katy-perry "Shamrock Capital Acquires Stargate Publishing Catalog, Including Katy Perry, Rihanna & Beyonce Hits"], ''Billboard'', October 16, 2018.</ref>

Shamrock's music catalogue includes the catalogue of dance label [[Om Records]], and the [[Bass Brothers]]' production royalties for [[Eminem]].

On February 12, 2019, Shamrock sold its assets on [[INgrooves]] to [[Universal Music Group]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

In June 2020, Shamrock acquired the advertising trade publication [[Adweek]] from Canadian private equity firm Beringer Capital.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff{{!}}June 2|first=Adweek|last2=2020|title=Adweek Acquired by Shamrock Capital|url=https://www.adweek.com/brand-marketing/adweek-acquired-by-shamrock-capital/|access-date=2020-07-24|website=www.adweek.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

In November 2020, Shamrock acquired the rights to [[Taylor Swift]]'s [[Taylor Swift albums discography|first six studio albums]] from [[Big Machine Records]] (2006-2017), which is owned by media manager [[Scooter Braun]]'s Ithaca Ventures.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Taylor Swift's Masters Sold Again: Why She Refused to Negotiate With Scooter Braun|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9485144/taylor-swift-masters-sold-again-scooter-braun-shamrock/|access-date=2020-11-17|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref> https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/confirmed-taylor-swift-masters-sold-by-scooter-braun-to-shamrock-capital-but-shes-re-recording-them-anyway</ref>

Shamrock Broadcasting was a broadcasting station operating company owned by Shamrock Holdings and Trefoil Capital Investors.

Shamrock Broadcasting was incorporated on July 3, 1979.<ref>{{cite web|title=SHAMROCK BROADCASTING COMPANY, INC. (C0923602)|url=http://kepler.sos.ca.gov/|work=Business Entity Detail|publisher=State of California|accessdate=April 22, 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150315010639/http://kepler.sos.ca.gov/|archivedate=March 15, 2015}}</ref>

Shamrock Broadcasting (SB) agreed to purchase Marlite Communications Group radio operations in March 1993 via a stock swap worth more than $300 million pushing SB's ownership to 21 radio station. [[#Trefoil Investors|Trefoil Capital Investors]] was the primary financier of the deal.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bates|first=James|title=Radio Play : Shamrock Will Acquire Marlite's 9 Stations|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1993-03-12/business/fi-10119_1_radio-station|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=March 12, 1993}}</ref> In July, Shamrock Broadcasting purchased [[KLLI (FM)|KZLA]] radio station.<ref>{{cite news|last=Puig|first=Claudia|title=The Last Word in Lonesome Is Country|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1993-12-02/entertainment/ca-63306_1_country-stations-radio|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angelese Times|date=December 2, 1993}}</ref> In August, the Marlite radio deal was closed making Marlite owner a minority share holder and board director of Shamrock Holding.<ref>{{cite news|title=Roy Disney Completes Purchase of Malrite Unit|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1993-08-03/business/fi-19775_1_roy-disney|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=August 3, 1993}}</ref>

Shamrock Broadcasting Inc. purchased in March 1995 from [[Premiere Radio Networks]] its Denver FM radio station for $5.5 million.<ref>{{cite news|title=Radio Networks Inc Premiere Sells Denver Radio Station for $5.5 Million|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1995-03-28/business/fi-48076_1_radio-station|accessdate=April 22, 2014|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=March 28, 1995}}</ref>

In August 1995, Shamrock agreed to sell Shamrock Broadcasting, a 19 radio stations group, to [[Chancellor Broadcasting]] Co. for $395 million.<ref name=lat3/>

==Trefoil Investors==
Trefoil Capital Investors (TCI) is a partnership formed by Shamrock Holdings with the raising of $450 million from investors by July 1990 that looked to invest in distressed companies. TCI agreed in July to purchase [[Child World]] in July 1990 in a two-stage process, by taking on some of its parent company CNC Holding Corp.'s debt then purchase the 18% shares outstanding on the public market.<ref>Peltz, James F. (July 31, 1990). [http://articles.latimes.com/1990-07-31/business/fi-1138_1_child-world-s-stock Disney Group Agrees to Buy Child World]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref> Conditions that had to be met for the deal to go through were raising $250 in working capital by Trefoil & Child World remaining stable. With a down turn in the Northwest region where Child World is based lead Trefoil to terminate the deal in November 1990.<ref>(November 30, 1990). [https://www.nytimes.com/1990/11/30/business/company-news-child-world-deal-ended-by-trefoil.html COMPANY NEWS; Child World Deal Ended by Trefoil ]. [[New York Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>

Trefoil Investors II is an investment company of Roy Disney and other senior executives of  Shamrock Holdings Inc. In October 1994, purchased a significant stake in Fantastic Foods Inc., a natural foods company with annual revenues nearing $30 million on Nature's Burger hamburger alternative and instant soups and mixes.<ref>Times Staff and Wire Reports. (October 20, 1994). [http://articles.latimes.com/1994-10-20/business/fi-52645_1_natural-foods Trefoil Investors II Buys Into Food Firm]. [[Los Angeles Times]]. Retrieved on April 21, 2014.</ref>
