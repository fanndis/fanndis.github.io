'''Zorana Mihajlović,''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Зорана Михајловић}}, {{IPA-sh|zǒrana mixǎjloʋitɕ|pron}}, born 5 May 1970) is a Serbian politician. She has served as the [[Deputy Prime Minister of Serbia]], and the [[Ministry of Construction, Transportation and Infrastructure (Serbia)|Minister of Construction, Transport and Infrastructure]] in the [[Government of Serbia]] since 27 April 2014.<ref>[http://www.rts.rs/page/stories/sr/story/9/Politika/1143778/Naprednjaci+izabrali+kandidate+za+ministre.html Naprednjaci izabrali kandidate za ministre] ''RTS'' 22 July 2012. Retrieved 27 July 2012. (in Serbian)</ref>

==Early life and education==
Mihajlović was born in May 1970 in [[Tuzla]], [[Socialist Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnia and Herzegovina]], but she finished her elementary and secondary school in [[Belgrade]], [[Serbia]]. She graduated from the [[University of Belgrade]] [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Economics|Faculty of Economics]]<ref>[http://www.srbija.gov.rs/vlada/ministri.php#74032 Biography of Zorana Mihajlović] ''Serbian Government'' Retrieved 27 July 2012. (in Serbian)</ref> in 1993 with a degree in Foreign and Domestic Trade.  Her thesis was titled ''Energy Resources in European Economic Community''.

She received her M.A. degree in 1998 at [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Economics|Faculty of Economics]] with the thesis ''Energy Sector of Serbia and Selected European Countries - [[France]], [[Germany]] and [[Sweden]]''.

Mihajlović obtained a Ph.D. degree in 2001 from the [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Economics|Faculty of Economics]] with the thesis ''Energy and Economic Development and  - Analyses of Interdependence between Serbia and Countries of [[European Union]]''. In 2006, she became research associate at [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Economics|Faculty of Economics]], and since 2008 she has been an associate professor at [[Megatrend University]].<ref>[https://archive.today/20121222145001/http://www.megatrend.edu.rs/fbio/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=587&Itemid=93 Biography from Megatrend University]</ref>

She has published three books in Serbian and several scientific papers in a national journal (also in Serbian).<ref>[http://www.megatrend.edu.rs/fbio/uploaded/radovi/zmihajlovic.pdf List of published books and papers] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120616212754/http://www.megatrend.edu.rs/fbio/uploaded/radovi/zmihajlovic.pdf |date=June 16, 2012 }}</ref>

==Professional career==
Mihajlović worked for nearly 15 years in her profession,  first as a Professor of Accounting and Business Economy at First High School in Economics in Belgrade. From 1996 to 2006, she worked at the Electric Power Industry of Serbia (EPS), in the Elektroistok public company for electric power transport.<ref>[http://www.ekapija.com/website/sr/page/606523 Zorana Mihajlović, Economist - biography]</ref> She was a member of the EPS’s Board of Management from 2004 to 2007. Since 2008, she has worked as an associate professor at [[Megatrend University]] in [[Belgrade]]. 

==Political career==
Mihajlović started her political career as a member of [[G17 Plus]] party. , During that time she worked as adviser for Energy and Environmental Protection to [[Miroljub Labus]], deputy prime minister in the [[Cabinet of Serbia (2004–2007)|Government of Serbia]].<ref>[http://www.srbija.gov.rs/vlada/ministri.php#74032]</ref> She was head of the Sector for Energy and Environmental Protection Politics, and from 2005 to 2006 she was also head of the Cabinet.

Mihajlović joined the [[Serbian Progressive Party]] in April 2010.<ref>[http://istinomer.rs/akter/zorana-mihajlovic-milanovic/ Istinomer:Zorana Mihajlović Milanović] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120608023946/http://istinomer.rs/akter/zorana-mihajlovic-milanovic/ |date=June 8, 2012 }}</ref> She soon became a member of the Executive Board of the [[Serbian Progressive Party]]. During the 2012 campaign for [[2012 Serbian parliamentary election|Serbian general elections]], Zorana Mihajlović was a leader in the campaign for her [[Serbian Progressive Party]].

On 27 July 2012, Mihajlović was elected Minister of Energy, Development and Environmental Protection in the [[Government of Serbia]].<ref>[http://www.b92.net/eng/news/politics-article.php?yyyy=2012&mm=07&dd=27&nav_id=81476 New cabinet elected; PM, ministers take oath of office] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131113223750/http://www.b92.net/eng/news/politics-article.php?yyyy=2012&mm=07&dd=27&nav_id=81476 |date=November 13, 2013 }} ''B92'' 27 July 2012. Retrieved 27 July 2012.</ref>

In February 2017, the [[Prime Minister of Serbia]] [[Aleksandar Vučić]] decided to run for the [[2017 Serbian presidential election]]s.<ref>{{cite news|title=SNS: Jednoglasno - Vučić kandidat za predsednika|url=http://www.b92.net/info/vesti/index.php?yyyy=2017&mm=02&dd=14&nav_category=11&nav_id=1230658|accessdate=29 June 2017|work=b92.net|agency=Tanjug|date=14 February 2017|language=Serbian}}</ref> He won the elections in the first round and was sworn as the [[President of Serbia]] on 31 May 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=Vucic Sworn in as Serbia's New President Amid Protests|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2017-05-31/vucic-sworn-in-as-serbias-new-president-amid-protests|accessdate=29 June 2017|work=usnews.com|date=31 May 2017}}</ref> Weeks later, he gave mandate to [[Ana Brnabić]] to form the governmental cabinet.<ref>{{cite news|title=Vučić: Kandidat za predsednika Vlade je Ana Brnabić|url=http://www.b92.net/info/vesti/index.php?yyyy=2017&mm=06&dd=15&nav_category=11&nav_id=1272498|accessdate=29 June 2017|work=b92.net|date=15 June 2017|language=Serbian}}</ref> On 29 June 2017, the cabinet of Ana Brnabić was formed,<ref>{{cite news|title=Kabinet Ane Brnabić položio zakletvu|url=http://www.danas.rs/politika.56.html?news_id=349757&title=Kabinet+Ane+Brnabi%C4%87+polo%C5%BEio+zakletvu|accessdate=29 June 2017|work=danas.rs|publisher=Danas Online|language=Serbian}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Surk|first1=Barbara|title=Serbia Gets Its First Female, and First Openly Gay, Premier|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/28/world/europe/serbia-ana-brnabic-prime-minister.html|accessdate=29 June 2017|work=nytimes.com|publisher=The New York Times}}</ref> with Mihajlović keeping her office.

==Personal life==
Mihajlović is fluent in English and has working knowledge of French and Italian. She has one son.
