The [[Prime Minister of New Zealand|prime minister]] is the [[head of government]] of New Zealand, and the leader of the [[Cabinet of New Zealand]], with various powers and responsibilities defined by [[Constitutional convention (political custom)|convention]]. Officially, the prime minister is appointed by the [[governor-general of New Zealand]], but by convention, the prime minister must have the confidence of the elected [[New Zealand House of Representatives]]. The prime minister is always a [[member of Parliament]], and is usually the leader of the largest [[List of political parties in New Zealand|political party]] in the House.

This list includes individuals who held the titles of [[Colonial Secretary (New Zealand)|colonial secretary]] and premier, the direct predecessors to the modern office. The title of the office was formally changed to "premier" in 1869, and then to "prime minister" in 1907 when New Zealand was granted [[Dominion of New Zealand|Dominion status]] in the [[British Empire]]. Forty individuals have so far held the premiership,<ref name="biographies">{{cite web|title=Biographies – Premiers and Prime Ministers |url=https://nzhistory.govt.nz/politics/premiers-and-prime-ministers|website=nzhistory.govt.nz|publisher=[[Ministry for Culture and Heritage]]|accessdate=14 December 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Prime Ministers of New Zealand since 1856 |url=https://www.parliament.nz/en/visit-and-learn/mps-and-parliaments-1854-onwards/prime-ministers-of-new-zealand-since-1856/ |publisher=New Zealand Parliament |accessdate=30 April 2019 |language=en |date=18 January 2019}}</ref> not including [[acting prime minister]]s. [[Henry Sewell]] is regarded as New Zealand's first premier, serving in May 1856.<ref>{{cite web |author-link=W. David McIntyre |last1=McIntyre |first1=W. David |title=Sewell, Henry |url=https://teara.govt.nz/en/biographies/1s8/sewell-henry |publisher=[[Dictionary of New Zealand Biography]] |accessdate=18 March 2019 |date=1990}}</ref> Nine prime ministers have held the position for more than one discrete term in office. The longest single term in office was that of [[Richard Seddon]], who held the position for thirteen years between 1893 and 1906.<ref>{{cite web |title='King Dick' Seddon becomes premier |url=https://nzhistory.govt.nz/richard-seddon-becomes-premier |website=nzhistory.govt.nz |publisher=Ministry for Culture and Heritage |accessdate=18 March 2019 |date=14 December 2016}}</ref> The incumbent Prime Minister is [[Jacinda Ardern]], who assumed office on 26 October 2017.<ref name="biographies"/>
