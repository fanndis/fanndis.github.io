'''Gareth Thomas Llewelyn Hughes'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.parliament.nz/en-NZ/PB/Debates/Debates/8/5/d/49HansD_20100216_00000011-List-Member-Vacancy.htm|title=List Member Vacancy|author=[[Speaker of the New Zealand House of Representatives|Speaker of the House]], [[Lockwood Smith|Dr Lockwood Smith]]|publisher=[[New Zealand Parliament]]|date=16 February 2010|accessdate=26 February 2010}}</ref> (born 31 October 1981) is a New Zealand politician and member of the [[Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand|Green Party]]. He took a seat in Parliament as the next person on the Green [[party list]] following the retirement of [[Jeanette Fitzsimons]] in February 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10622767 |title=Fitzsimons steps down as MP - effective immediately |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] | date=28 January 2010|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref>

Hughes did not stand for re-election in the [[2020 New Zealand general election]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |title=Gareth Hughes to stand down at next election|url= https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/political/403473/gareth-hughes-to-stand-down-at-next-election |date=2019-11-17|website=RNZ|language=en-nz|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref>

==Early life==
Hughes grew up in [[Gisborne, New Zealand|Gisborne]],<ref name="odt">{{cite web|url=http://www.odt.co.nz/tags/gareth-hughes/96327/introducing-nz039s-youngest-mp|title=Introducing NZ's youngest MP|work=[[Otago Daily Times]]|first=Emily|last=Menkes|date=5 March 2010|accessdate=22 May 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110608215825/http://www.odt.co.nz/tags/gareth-hughes/96327/introducing-nz039s-youngest-mp|archive-date=8 June 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> and, after attending [[Gisborne Boys' High School]],<ref>{{Cite web | title =Gareth Hughes - List member, Green Party | work =Current MPs | publisher =New Zealand Parliament | url =http://www.parliament.nz/en-NZ/MPP/MPs/MPs/c/3/3/50MP133701-Hughes-Gareth.htm | accessdate =3 November 2010 }}</ref> studied religious studies, history and politics at [[Victoria University of Wellington]].<ref name=partybio>{{cite web |url=http://www.greens.org.nz/people/garethhughes |title=Gareth Hughes |publisher=Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand |accessdate=24 February 2010}}</ref> He became a [[vegetarianism|vegetarian]] while a student.<ref>[http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/3271914/Meet-the-Green-Partys-new-MP "Meet the Green Party's new MP"], [[Stuff.co.nz]], 28 January 2010</ref>  He worked for [[Greenpeace]] in Australia and New Zealand from 2000–2005, and then worked for the Green Party on climate change issues. He is married with two children, Arlo and Zoe.<ref name=candbio>{{cite web |url=http://www.greens.org.nz/people/candidates |title=Election08 Green Party Candidates |work=[[Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand]] |publisher=www.greens.org.nz |accessdate=28 January 2010}}</ref>

In May 2004, Hughes was arrested after dressing as [[Ronald McDonald]] and chaining himself to the gates of [[McDonald's]] New Zealand distribution centre in Wiri, Auckland, protesting against the use of genetically modified chicken feed.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://tvnz.co.nz/politics-news/young-blood-greens-3344755 |title=Young blood for Green |work=[[New Zealand Press Association]]| date= 28 January 2010}}</ref>

In 2009 Hughes co-ordinated [[Greenpeace New Zealand|Greenpeace New Zealand's]] 'Sign On' campaign, which called for stronger action on [[climate change]].<ref name="partybio" /><ref name="young blood">{{cite web|title=Young blood for Greens|url=http://tvnz.co.nz/politics-news/young-blood-greens-3344755|date=28 January 2010|work=[[New Zealand Press Association]]|publisher=[[Television New Zealand]]|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref>

Hughes contested the [[2008 New Zealand general election|2008 election]] for the Green Party. Placed at 11 on the [[party list]] and campaigning mostly for the [[party vote]],<ref name="young blood" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Meet the Green Party's new MP|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/3271914/Meet-the-Green-Partys-new-MP|date=28 January 2010|work=[[New Zealand Press Association]]|publisher=[[Stuff.co.nz]]|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref> Hughes finished fourth in the [[Ōhāriu (New Zealand electorate)|Ōhariu electorate]]. The party did not poll sufficiently high for Hughes to be elected immediately, but he was declared elected after the retirement of [[list MP]] and former co-leader [[Jeanette Fitzsimons]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.elections.org.nz/news/new-list-mp-for-green-party.html |title=New list MP for Green Party |publisher=Chief Electoral Office |date=12 February 2010 |accessdate=24 February 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.salient.org.nz/news/politics-gareth-hughes-interview|title=Politics: Gareth Hughes Interview|first=Jackson|last=Wood|work=[[Salient (magazine)|Salient]]|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref> Hughes was sworn in as an MP on 16 February 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.parliament.nz/en-NZ/PB/Debates/Journals/b/b/6/00HOH201002161-Journals-of-the-House-for-the-week-beginning-February.htm |title=Journals of the House for the week beginning Tuesday, 16 February 2010 |work=[[New Zealand Parliament]] |accessdate=24 February 2010}}</ref> He was the youngest MP in Parliament at the time of his election.<ref name="odt"/>

In his maiden speech on 24 February 2010, Hughes declared his support for a [[Republicanism in New Zealand|New Zealand republic]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.voxy.co.nz/politics/gareth-hughes-maiden-speech-green-party-media-release/5/39602 |title=Gareth Hughes: Maiden Speech |publisher=voxy.co.nz |date=24 February 2010 |accessdate=24 February 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/PA1002/S00348.htm|title=Gareth Hughes Maiden Speech|publisher=[[Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand]]|author=Gareth Hughes|date=25 February 2010|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref> In his first term, Hughes was the Green Party's spokesperson on housing, ICT, libraries and archives, tertiary education, GE, food, oceans,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guide2.co.nz/politics/news/bethune-needs-report-for-his-defence-greens-say/11/16238|title=Bethune needs report for his defence, Greens say|work=[[New Zealand Press Association]]|publisher=Guide2.co.nz|date=11 May 2010|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref> transport,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvnz.co.nz/politics-news/kiwirail-spending-brings-mixed-reaction-3556912|title=KiwiRail focus off the track, says expert|work=[[One News (New Zealand)|ONE News]] and [[New Zealand Press Association]]|publisher=[[Television New Zealand]]|date=18 May 2010|accessdate=22 May 2010}}</ref> and youth.<ref name="partybio" />

[[File:23º Foro Parlamentario Asia Pacífico - Gareth Hughes (NUEVA ZELANDA) (16249473736).jpg|thumb|Hughes at the [[Asia Pacific Parliamentary Forum]], 2015]]
In the [[2011 New Zealand general election|2011 general election]] he stood in the Ōhariu electorate again and was ranked seventh on the Green party list, and was re-elected as a list MP. In the [[2014 New Zealand general election|2014 general election]] he stood only on the party list, ranked fifth, and was elected for a third time. During this term of Parliament, the male co-leader of the Party, [[Russel Norman]], resigned and Hughes [[2015 Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand male co-leadership election|stood for the vacant co-leadership role]] as the self-described "underdog."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gareth Hughes 'underdog' for Green Party leadership|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/66988403/gareth-hughes-underdog-for-green-party-leadership|website=Stuff|language=en|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref> Ultimately, the contest was won by first-term MP [[James Shaw (New Zealand politician)|James Shaw]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2015-05-30|title=James Shaw named new Greens co-leader|language=en-NZ|work=NZ Herald|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/|access-date=2020-05-14|issn=1170-0777}}</ref>

Hughes, along with the rest of the Green Party, voted in support of [[Marriage (Definition of Marriage) Amendment Act 2013]], allowing same-sex couples to legally marry in New Zealand.<ref>{{cite news|title=Marriage equality bill - How MPs voted|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/data/8564068/Marriage-equality-bill-How-MPs-voted|accessdate=3 December 2016|work=[[Stuff.co.nz]]|date=17 April 2013|author=Harkanwal Singh|author2=Andy Ball}}</ref>

In November 2016, it was announced that Hughes would be running for the East Coast electorate against National Party MP [[Anne Tolley]]. This electorate is the one he was raised in.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://gisborneherald.co.nz/localnews/2562925-135/hughes-gets-greens-light-to-take |title=Hughes gets Greens light to take on Tolley |first=Andrew |last=Ashton |work=The Gisborne Herald |date=22 November 2016}}</ref> He was returned to Parliament as the Greens' fifth-ranked and longest serving list MP.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Longest-serving Greens MP Gareth Hughes plans to 'reconnect with activist roots' after retirement|language=en-NZ|work=TVNZ|url=https://www.tvnz.co.nz/one-news/new-zealand/longest-serving-greens-mp-gareth-hughes-plans-reconnect-activist-roots-after-retirement|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref> When the Greens agreed to support a [[Sixth Labour Government of New Zealand|Labour-New Zealand First coalition Government]], Hughes was not appointed to a Ministerial position and instead continued as the party [[Whip (politics)|musterer]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Green Party announces ministers|url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/political/342074/green-party-announces-ministers|date=2017-10-21|website=RNZ|language=en-nz|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref> In 2019, he announced his intention to retire at the next general election.<ref name=":0" /> Ahead of his retirement, and for family reasons, he relocated himself to be the Green Party's member of Parliament based in [[Dunedin]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Veteran Greens MP Hughes happy to represent Dunedin|url=https://www.odt.co.nz/news/dunedin/veteran-greens-mp-hughes-happy-represent-dunedin|last=Houlahan|first=Mike|date=2020-03-17|website=Otago Daily Times Online News|language=en|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref>
