'''Dame Patricia Lee Reddy''' {{post-nominals|country=NZL|GNZMf|QSO|DStJ}} (born 17 May 1954) is a New Zealand lawyer and businesswoman serving as the [[Governor-General of New Zealand]], in office since 2016.

Before becoming governor-general, Reddy was a [[Partner (business rank)|partner]] of a law firm, headed a major review of intelligence agencies, held multiple directorships, chaired the [[New Zealand Film Commission]] and worked as a chief negotiator on [[Treaty of Waitangi settlements]]. Prime Minister [[John Key]] recommended her to succeed [[Jerry Mateparae|Sir Jerry Mateparae]] as the Queen's representative; Reddy was sworn in on 28 September 2016.

==Early life and education==
Born in [[Matamata]], New Zealand, on 17 May 1954,<ref>{{cite book |editor-last=Taylor |editor-first=Alister |title=New Zealand Who's Who Aotearoa 2001 |year=2001 |publisher=Alister Taylor Publishers |location=Auckland |issn=1172-9813 | page=739}}</ref> Reddy is the daughter of Neil William and Catherine Marjorie "Kay" Reddy, both of whom were schoolteachers.<ref name="NZ Herald 22 Mar 2016"/> Three of her forebears left Ireland and went to Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. A distant cousin, singer [[Helen Reddy]], is descended from the Australian forebear.<ref name="quiet achiever">{{cite news|last1=Young|first1=Audrey|title=Spotlight falls on quiet achiever|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11610102|accessdate=23 March 2016|work=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|date=23 March 2016}}</ref> Reddy was raised in the small [[Waikato]] towns of [[Te Akau]] and [[Minginui]] until her family moved to [[Hamilton, New Zealand|Hamilton]] when she was six years old. There, she attended Hillcrest Primary School, [[Peachgrove Intermediate|Peachgrove Intermediate School]] and [[Hamilton Girls' High School]].<ref name="NZ Herald 22 Mar 2016"/>

Reddy completed tertiary study at [[Victoria University of Wellington]], graduating with a [[Bachelor of Laws]] in 1976 and a [[Master of Laws]] with [[first-class honours]] in 1979.<ref name="NZ Herald 22 Mar 2016">{{cite web|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11609882|title=New Governor-General: who is Dame Patsy Reddy?|work=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|date=22 March 2016|accessdate=1 April 2016}}</ref><ref name="Biographical notes">{{cite web|url=https://www.beehive.govt.nz/sites/all/files/Dame%20Patsy%20Reddy%20-%20Biographical%20Note.pdf|title=Dame Patsy Reddy – biographical notes|website=beehive.govt.nz|accessdate=23 March 2016|url-status = dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160407004042/https://www.beehive.govt.nz/sites/all/files/Dame%20Patsy%20Reddy%20-%20Biographical%20Note.pdf|archivedate=7 April 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Career==
Reddy was a junior lecturer and then lecturer at Victoria University's Faculty of Law.<ref name="Biographical notes"/> In 1982 she joined the Wellington firm Watts and Patterson (now Minter Ellison Rudd Watts), becoming their first female partner in 1983. She specialised in tax, corporate and film law. She later took up a position at [[Brierley Investments]], where she was employed for 11 years, and worked on large acquisition negotiations such as the [[privatisation of Air New Zealand]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/78141902/explainer-who-is-dame-patsy-reddy-and-what-will-she-do-as-governorgeneral|title=Who is Dame Patsy Reddy, and what will she do as Governor-General?|work=Stuff|accessdate=1 April 2016|date=22 March 2016|first=Sam|last=Sachdeva}}</ref>

Reddy served as chair of the [[New Zealand Film Commission]] and [[Education Payroll Ltd]] and was a director of Payments NZ Ltd and Active Equity Holdings Ltd. She was a chief Crown negotiator for [[Treaty of Waitangi]] settlements and a lead reviewer for the Performance Improvement Framework for the State Services Commission. She was also the deputy chair of the [[NZ Transport Agency|New Zealand Transport Agency]]. Other directorships included [[Telecom Corporation of New Zealand Limited|Telecom Corporation of New Zealand Ltd]], [[SKYCITY Entertainment Group]], [[New Zealand Post]] and [[Air New Zealand]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nzta.govt.nz/about-us/about-the-nz-transport-agency/our-board|title=NZTA: Our Board|year=2016|publisher=NZ Transport Agency|accessdate=1 April 2016}}</ref>

In 2016, Reddy and [[Michael Cullen (politician)|Sir Michael Cullen]] collaborated on an independent report to the New Zealand government reviewing legislation covering the country's [[intelligence agencies]]. Their report was released on 9 March 2016, two weeks before Reddy's appointment as governor-general was publicly announced.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/77687989/spy-agencies-explainer-what-you-need-to-know-about-the-report-into-spying-laws|title=Spy agencies explainer: what you need to know about the report into spying laws|work=Stuff|accessdate=22 March 2016|date=9 March 2016|first=Sam|last=Sachdeva}}</ref> The report recommended expanding the [[Government Communications Security Bureau]]'s rights to monitor the personal communications of New Zealanders, and was met with some controversy.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://wellington.scoop.co.nz/?p=87376|title=Greens "surprised" by Reddy appointment after controversial spy review|work=Wellington.Scoop|date=March 22, 2016|accessdate=22 March 2016}}</ref>

Reddy was also involved in a number of non-governmental organisations, particularly in the arts and gender equality.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11609509|title=Dame Patsy Reddy to be Governor-General|date=22 March 2016|work=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|accessdate=22 March 2016|first=Audrey|last=Young}}</ref> She was one of the founding members in 2009 of Global Women New Zealand, a group of prominent women who advocate for inclusion and diversity in leadership.<ref name="NZ Herald 22 Mar 2016"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.globalwomen.org.nz|title=Global Women|website=globalwomen.org.nz|accessdate=22 March 2016}}</ref>

==Governor-General of New Zealand==
[[File:Dame Patsy Reddy and Chief Justice, Dame Sian Elias.jpg|thumb|Dame Patsy Reddy with Chief Justice Dame Sian Elias at the swearing-in ceremony, 28 September 2016]]
In March 2016, it was announced that [[Elizabeth II|Queen Elizabeth II]], [[Monarchy of New Zealand|Queen of New Zealand]], had approved the appointment of Reddy as the next [[Governor-General of New Zealand]], for a five-year term starting in September 2016, on the [[Advice (constitutional)|advice]] of then Prime Minister [[John Key]].<ref>{{cite press release|url=https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/pm-welcomes-dame-patsy-reddy-next-governor-general|title=PM welcomes Dame Patsy Reddy as the next Governor-General|website=beehive.govt.nz|date=22 March 2016|first=John|last=Key}}</ref> She was officially [[swearing-in|sworn in]] as the 21st governor-general by Chief Justice [[Sian Elias|Dame Sian Elias]] on 28 September. The swearing-in ceremony included a Māori [[pōwhiri]], a [[21-gun salute]], and music from the [[Royal New Zealand Air Force]] Band and the New Zealand Opera Chorus; the ceremony was attended by hundreds of observers, including film-makers [[Peter Jackson|Sir Peter Jackson]] and [[James Cameron]] (a former neighbour), with an address by Prime Minister John Key.<ref>{{cite press release |title=Swearing-In Ceremony |url=https://gg.govt.nz/publications/swearing-ceremony |publisher=Government House |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en-NZ |date=7 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Weeks |first1=John |title=Hollywood big guns among crowd at swearing in of new Governor-General Dame Patsy Reddy |url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/84733199/new-governorgeneral-dame-patsy-reddy-being-sworn-in-at-parliament |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[Stuff (website)|Stuff]] |date=28 September 2016 |language=en}}</ref> Reddy became the third woman to hold the position, after [[Dame Catherine Tizard]] and [[Dame Silvia Cartwright]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/78130827/dame-patsy-reddy-was-stunned-when-offered-governorgeneral-role|title=Dame Patsy Reddy was 'stunned' when offered Governor-General role|work=Stuff|date=22 March 2016|first1=Rosanna|last1=Price|first2=Jo|last2=Moir|accessdate=22 March 2016}}</ref>

[[File:Dame Patsy Reddy gave her first Royal assent. This is the final step a bill needs to go through before it becomes law.jpg|thumb|left|Dame Patsy Reddy gives assent to a bill, 18 October 2016]]
Reddy gave her first [[Royal assent]] as governor-general on 18 October 2016.<ref>{{cite web |title=New Governor-General gives her first Royal assent |url=https://www.parliament.nz/en/get-involved/features/new-governor-general-gives-her-first-royal-assent/ |publisher=New Zealand Parliament |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en |date=18 October 2016}}</ref>

On 7 November 2016, Reddy welcomed King [[Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands]] and [[Queen Máxima of the Netherlands|Queen Maxima]] to New Zealand. She hosted a state banquet.<ref>{{cite web |title=Warm Wellington welcome for visiting King and Queen of Netherlands |url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11743911 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |language=en-NZ |date=7 November 2016}}</ref>

On 12 December 2016, subsequent to the resignation of John Key, Reddy swore in [[Bill English]] as Prime Minister and [[Paula Bennett]] as Deputy Prime Minister.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Trevett |first1=Claire |title=What Bill English promises to deliver to New Zealand |url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11765337 |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |date=12 December 2016 |language=en-NZ}}</ref>

On 7 February 2017, Reddy delivered her first annual [[Waitangi Day]] Bledisloe Address at the Bledisloe Garden reception at [[Government House, Wellington]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Trevett |first1=Claire |title=Governor-General Dame Patsy Reddy pulls no punches on Treaty of Waitangi |url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11795650 |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |date=6 February 2017 |language=en-NZ}}</ref>

In her first overseas trip, Reddy visited [[Niue]] and the [[Cook Islands]], the associated states of New Zealand, on 21 and 22 March 2017 respectively.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Round |first1=Sally |title=NZ Governor General arrives in Niue |url=https://www.radionz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/327112/nz-governor-general-arrives-in-niue |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[Radio New Zealand]] |date=21 March 2017 |language=en-nz}}</ref> Reddy was welcomed by [[Tom Marsters]], the [[Queen's Representative]] in the Cook Islands.<ref>{{cite news |last=Moore |first=Richard|title=New Zealand Governor General Visits Cook Islands {{!}} Pacific Islands Report |url=http://www.pireport.org/articles/2017/03/22/new-zealand-governor-general-visits-cook-islands |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=www.pireport.org |date=22 March 2017 |language=en}}</ref>

On 6 May 2017, Reddy travelled to Italy, where she visited various cultural events in Rome and Venice. On 14 May, she visited [[Barbados]], where she met with the Governor-General of Barbados, [[Elliott Belgrave]], and the prime minister, [[Freundel Stuart]].<ref>{{cite press release|title=Governor-General to Visit Italy and Barbados |url=https://www.gg.govt.nz/news/governor-general-visit-italy-and-barbados |publisher=Government House |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en |date=5 May 2018}}</ref>

[[File:Reuven Rivlin host the New Zealand Governor General, October 2017 (7043).jpg|thumb|Reddy with Israeli President [[Reuven Rivlin]] during a visit to Israel, 27 October 2017]]
On 30 September 2017, Reddy travelled to Israel for a two-day trip, in which she represented New Zealand at official commemorations to mark the centennial of the [[Battle of Beersheba (1917)|Battle of Beersheba]] during the [[First World War]].<ref>{{cite press release |title=Governor-General to visit Israel |url=https://gg.govt.nz/publications/governor-general-visit-israel |publisher=Government House |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en-NZ |date=27 September 2017}}</ref> She was received by Israeli President [[Reuven Rivlin]] and later met with Prime Minister [[Benjamin Netenyahu]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sedley |first1=David |title=In first, top New Zealander makes official visit to Israel |url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/in-first-top-new-zealander-makes-official-visit-to-israel/ |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[Times of Israel]] |date=30 October 2017}}</ref>

[[File:GGNZ Swearing of new Cabinet - Jacinda Ardern & Winston Peters.jpg|thumb|left|Dame Patsy Reddy with PM Jacinda Ardern and Deputy PM [[Winston Peters]] at swearing-in of the Executive Council, 26 October 2017]]
On 26 October 2017, Reddy presided at the swearing-in of the new [[Executive Council of New Zealand|Executive Council]]. She signed warrants appointing the new prime minister, [[Jacinda Ardern]], and other [[Ministers of the New Zealand Government|ministers]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Jacinda Ardern sworn in as new Prime Minister|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11936791|accessdate=18 December 2017|work= [[The New Zealand Herald]]|date=26 October 2017|language=en-NZ}}</ref> On 8 November, Reddy attended the State Opening of Parliament where she [[Speech from the throne|addressed MPs from the throne]].<ref>{{cite news|title=As it happened: State Opening of Parliament|url=http://www.newshub.co.nz/home/politics/2017/11/live-updates-state-opening-of-parliament.html|accessdate=18 December 2017|work=Newshub|date=11 August 2017}}</ref>

On 24 October 2017, Reddy hosted a state welcome for the President of Ireland, [[Michael D. Higgins]], at Government House.<ref>{{cite web |title=State Welcome for the President of Ireland |url=https://gg.govt.nz/image-galleries/state-welcome-president-ireland-1 |publisher=Government House |accessdate=2 June 2018 |date=24 October 2017 |language=en}}</ref> A couple of weeks later, Reddy hosted German President [[Frank-Walter Steinmeier]] at Government House from 5 to 7 November 2017. The visit started with a wreath-laying ceremony at [[Pukeahu National War Memorial Park]].<ref>{{cite web |title=German President Steinmeier visits New Zealand |url=https://eeas.europa.eu/delegations/new-zealand_en/35000/%20German%20President%20Steinmeier%20visits%20New%20Zealand |publisher=[[European External Action Service]] |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en |date=2 November 2017}}</ref>

On 5 December 2017, Reddy began a three-day trip to Malaysia.<ref>{{cite press release |title=Governor-General's State Visit to Malaysia |url=https://gg.govt.nz/publications/governor-generals-state-visit-malaysia |publisher=Government House |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en-NZ |date=4 December 2017}}</ref> The state visit marked the sixtieth year of diplomatic relations between New Zealand and Malaysia. Among several engagements, Reddy had an audience with [[Muhammad V of Kelantan|Sultan Muhammad V]], and attended a state banquet.<ref>{{cite news |title=NZ governor-general on maiden visit to Malaysia |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2017/12/07/nz-governorgeneral-on-maiden-visit-to-malaysia/ |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]] |date=7 December 2017}}</ref>

On 1 January 2018, Reddy issued her [[New Year]] video message. She focused on gender equality and respect for women; Reddy also noted the 125th anniversary of [[women's suffrage in New Zealand]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Governor-General uses New Year's message to call for gender equality |url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/100276705/governorgeneral-uses-new-years-message-to-call-for-gender-equality |work =Stuff.co.nz |accessdate=2 June 2018 |language=en |date=1 January 2018}}</ref>

Reddy welcomed former US President [[Barack Obama]] to Government House on 22 March 2018.<ref>{{cite news |title=First images from historic meeting between Barack Obama and Jacinda Ardern |url=https://www.tvnz.co.nz/one-news/new-zealand/first-images-historic-meeting-between-barack-obama-and-jacinda-ardern |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=[[1 News]] |date=22 March 2018 |language=en-NZ}}</ref> On 23 April, Reddy travelled to Turkey to represent New Zealand at commemorative events associated with the [[Gallipoli campaign]]; on 26 April, she met Turkish President [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]] at the Presidential Palace in [[Ankara]].<ref>{{cite press release |title=2018 Visit to Turkey for Anzac Day commemorations |url=https://gg.govt.nz/governor-general/blog/2018/05/2018-visit-turkey-anzac-day-commemorations |publisher=Government House |accessdate=3 June 2018 |language=en |date=3 May 2018}}</ref>

[[File:Ceremony of Welcome for TRH The Duke and Duchess of Sussex (18).jpg|thumb|Reddy, with the Duke and Duchess of Sussex, greets crowds at Government House, 28 October 2018]]
On 28 October 2018, Reddy welcomed members of the Royal Family, [[Prince Harry, Duke of Sussex]], and [[Meghan, Duchess of Sussex]], to Wellington. She also hosted a reception at Government House honouring the 125th anniversary of women's suffrage.<ref>{{cite news |title=Governor-General Dame Patsy Reddy hosts reception honouring 125 years since women's suffrage |url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/life-style/life/108174128/governorgeneral-dame-patsy-reddy-hosts-reception-honouring-125-years-since-womens-suffrage |accessdate=29 October 2018 |work=Stuff.co.nz |date=28 October 2018 |language=en}}</ref> The following month, on 19 November, Reddy hosted the President of Chile [[Sebastian Pinera]].<ref>{{cite press release |title=State Welcome for President Sebastian Pinera of Chile |url=https://gg.govt.nz/governor-general/blog/2018/11/state-welcome-president-sebastian-pinera-chile |publisher=Government House |accessdate=15 December 2018 |language=en-NZ |date=19 November 2018}}</ref> On 3 December she welcomed the President of the Republic of Korea, [[Moon Jae-in]] and his wife [[Kim Jung-sook]] to New Zealand during a ceremony at Auckland Government House.<ref>{{cite press release |title=State Welcome for the President of the Republic of Korea |url=https://gg.govt.nz/governor-general/blog/2018/12/state-welcome-president-republic-korea |publisher=Government House |accessdate=15 December 2018 |language=en-NZ |date=3 December 2018}}</ref>

On 15 March 2019, Reddy released a message expressing condolences to the victims of the [[Christchurch mosque shootings]]. She remarked "Now more than ever is the time to affirm the values that we hold dear – compassion, kindness and tolerance.".<ref>{{cite web |title=Message from the Governor-General |url=https://gg.govt.nz/governor-general/blog/2019/03/message-governor-general |website=gg.govt.nz |accessdate=16 March 2019 |language=en-NZ |date=15 March 2019}}</ref>

On 7 June 2019, Reddy represented New Zealand at the commemorations for the 75th anniversary of the [[D-Day landings]] at Portsmouth in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web |title=D-Day 75th anniversary commemorations |url=https://gg.govt.nz/governor-general/blog |website=gg.govt.nz |accessdate=14 July 2019 |date=7 June 2019}}</ref>

In October 2019 she visited Japan on an official visit.<ref>{{cite web |title=Official visit to Japan |url=https://gg.govt.nz/image-galleries/official-visit-japan |website=gg.govt.nz |accessdate=2 January 2020 |language=en-NZ |date=24 October 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Dame Patsy Reddy and Sir David Gascoigne 2016.jpg|thumb|left|Dame Patsy Reddy and Sir David Gascoigne sign the Visitors' Book at [[Parliament House, Wellington]], 2016]] 
Reddy is married to the former New Zealand Judicial Conduct Commissioner, [[David Gascoigne|Sir David Gascoigne]].<ref name="NZ Herald 22 Mar 2016"/> They married one week before her appointment as governor-general was announced. She and her first husband, Geoff Harley, a tax barrister, divorced in 1988.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lawfuel.com/newzealand/16-things-didnt-know-dame-patsy-reddy/ |title=16 Things You Didn’t Know About Dame Patsy Reddy |date=22 March 2016 |publisher=LawFuel |access-date=29 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160927032331/http://www.lawfuel.com/newzealand/16-things-didnt-know-dame-patsy-reddy/ |archive-date=27 September 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=New Governor-General Patsy Reddy ties the knot with long-term partner|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/79094938/New-Governor-General-Patsy-Reddy-ties-the-knot-with-long-term-partner|website=stuff|publisher=stuff|accessdate=31 Aug 2016}}</ref> Both husbands were her associates at Rudd Watts & Stone in Wellington in the 1980s.<ref>{{cite web|title=Five minutes with… Lloyd Kavanagh, Minter Ellison Rudd Watts|url=http://www.nzlawyermagazine.co.nz/news/five-minutes-with-lloyd-kavanagh-minter-ellison-rudd-watts-193566.aspx|website=NZ Lawyer|publisher=NZ Lawyer|accessdate=31 Aug 2016}}</ref>

Reddy is the first [[vegan]] governor-general. Accordingly, the banquet at her swearing-in ceremony was entirely vegan.<ref>{{cite news |title=No animals harmed in the making of new Governor-General's first banquet |url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/life-style/food-wine/84777958/No-animals-harmed-in-the-making-of-new-Governor-Generals-first-banquet |accessdate=2 June 2018 |work=Stuff.co.nz |date=30 December 2016 |language=en}}</ref>

In the [[2014 Birthday Honours (New Zealand)|2014 Queen's Birthday Honours]], Reddy was appointed a [[Dame Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit]] for services to the arts and business.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/publications/queens-birthday-honours-list-2014 |title=Queen's Birthday honours list 2014 |date=2 June 2014 |publisher=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet |accessdate=28 April 2018}}</ref> In June 2016, she was made a [[Most Venerable Order of the Hospital of Saint John of Jerusalem|Dame of the Most Venerable Order of the Hospital of Saint John of Jerusalem]].<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=61619 |date=15 June 2016 |page=13264 }}</ref>

Reddy was promoted as an [[Additional Dame Grand Companion of The New Zealand Order of Merit]] and appointed an [[Queen's Service Order|Additional Companion of the Queen's Service Order]] on 27 June 2016 in preparation for becoming governor-general.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.beehive.govt.nz/release/announcement-honours-dame-patsy-reddy|title=Announcement of honours for Dame Patsy Reddy|publisher=|accessdate=27 June 2016}}</ref> As governor-general, Reddy is entitled to be styled "[[Excellency|Her Excellency]]" while in office and "[[The Right Honourable]]" for life.<ref>{{cite web |title=Protocols |url=https://www.parliament.nz/resource/en-nz/QWA_00124_2015/3f65c9ef7d40ad53c7f9a5eeb7caa172a7afb513 |pages=5, 9 |publisher=New Zealand Parliament |accessdate=8 June 2018 |year=2015 |format=PDF}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title="The Right Honourable" |url=https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/our-programmes/new-zealand-royal-honours/new-zealand-royal-honours-system/types-new-zealand-royal-honours/the-right-honourable |publisher=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet |accessdate=8 June 2018 |language=en-NZ |date=1 April 2011}}</ref>

Victoria University of Wellington conferred an [[honorary degree|honorary]] [[Doctor of Laws]] degree on Reddy on 13 December 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.victoria.ac.nz/news/2017/11/governor-general-to-receive-honorary-doctorate|title=Governor-General to receive honorary doctorate|publisher=Victoria University of Wellington|date=1 November 2017}}</ref>

As the wife of Sir David, Reddy is entitled to the style Lady Gascoigne, but this is never used because of the honours she holds in her own right.<ref>[https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/honours/overview/titles-and-styles DPMC — Titles and styles of knights and dames]. Retrieved on 29 December 2016.</ref>
