The '''Decoration of Honour for Services to the Republic of Austria''' ({{lang-de|'''Ehrenzeichen für Verdienste um die Republik Österreich'''}}) is a [[state decoration|national honour]] awarded by the [[Republic of Austria]].

The decoration is awarded in 15 grades and is Austria's highest [[honours system in the Republic of Austria|national honour]].

==History==
The "Decoration of Honour for Services to the Republic of Austria" was established by the [[National Council of Austria|National Council]] in 1952.<ref name=aaa>{{Cite web |url=http://www.hofburg.at/show_content.php?sid=48&s2id=0&language=en |title=Archived copy |access-date=2010-02-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090216055404/http://hofburg.at/show_content.php?sid=48&s2id=0&language=en |archive-date=2009-02-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref> It is conferred by the Republic of Austria to honour people (from Austria and abroad) who have rendered meritorious services to the country. Recipients are selected by the government. The awards are made by the [[President of Austria|President]] in accordance with the respective laws.<ref name=aaa/> The [[President of Austria|State President of Austria]] automatically receives the "Grand Star of the Decoration for Services to the Republic of Austria" by being elected to the office and holds this honour for life. The decoration should not be confused with other decorations in the Austrian honours system such as the "[[Austrian Decoration for Science and Art]]".
