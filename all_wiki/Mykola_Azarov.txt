'''Mykola Yanovych Azarov''' ({{lang-uk|Мико́ла Я́нович Аза́ров}}; {{lang-ru|Никола́й Я́нович Азaров|Nikolay Yanovich Azarov}}; né '''Pakhlo'''<!-- please provide source for suggested IPA |pɐˈxlo-->; [[Cyrillic]]:  Пахло; born 17 December 1947) is a [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] politician who was the [[Prime Minister of Ukraine]] from 11 March 2010 to 27 January 2014. He was the First Vice Prime Minister and Finance Minister from 2002 to 2005 and again from 2006 to 2007. Azarov also served [[ex officio]] as an acting Prime Minister in the [[First Yanukovych Government]] when [[Viktor Yanukovych]] ran for president at first and then upon resignation of his government.

==Early life, education and career==
Azarov was born in [[Kaluga]]<ref name=Rian/> on 17 December 1947 in the [[Russian SFSR]], [[Soviet Union]],<ref name="Познер">{{cite news|accessdate=29 June 2012|url=http://www.1tv.ru/videoarchive/48590&p=371|title=Познер. Гость в студии – Николай Азаров|publisher=1 канал|archivedate=28 June 2012|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120628231337/http://www.1tv.ru/videoarchive/48590%26p%3D371|df=dmy-all}}<br/>{{cite news|url=http://tsn.ua/ukrayina/azarov-viyavivsya-napolovinu-estoncem.html|script-title=uk:Азаров виявився наполовину естонцем|trans-title=Azarov, as it turns out, is half Estonian|agency=[[1+1 (channel)|TSN]]|date=6 October 2010|accessdate=23 January 2012|archivedate=19 October 2013|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131019111459/http://tsn.ua/ukrayina/azarov-viyavivsya-napolovinu-estoncem.html|url-status=live|language=uk|df=dmy-all}}</ref> to an [[Estonian people|Estonian]] father, Jaan Pahlo, and a [[Russians|Russian]] mother, Yekaterina Pavlovna Kvasnikova, as Nikolay Pakhlo.<ref name="wife name">{{cite news|agency=Gazeta.ua|url=http://gazeta.ua/post/330779|script-title=uk:Микола Азаров став прем’єр-міністром|trans-title=Mykola Azarov became prime minister|date=12 March 2010|accessdate=31 August 2010|archivedate=9 August 2011|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110809135451/http://gazeta.ua/post/330779|url-status=live|language=uk|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref name=tapes/> When he married his wife, Lyudmila Azarova, he took her name.<ref name="wife name"/><ref name=tapes/> Azarov attended the [[Moscow State University]] where he earned his doctorate in geology and [[mineralogy]] in 1973.<ref name=Rian/> He worked at the [[Tulaugol]] coal enterprise until 1976.<ref name=Rian/> Azarov moved to [[Donetsk]] permanently in 1984 to become deputy director of the Ukrainian State Geological Institute, that he went on to head.<ref name=tapes/> In 1984–1995 he was a deputy director and director of Ukraine's State Research and Design Institute of Mining Geology and Geomechanics.<ref name=Rian/> In 1991 he was hired as a professor at [[Donetsk National Technical University]].

==Political career==
{{see also|Party of Labor (Ukraine)}}

===Parliamentary career===
In 1994 Azarov was elected member of the [[Verkhovna Rada]] (the Ukrainian parliament) for the first time, representing the Petroskiy electoral district, located in the city of [[Donetsk]]. In 1995–1997 he served as head of the parliament's Committee on Budgets, while also sitting on the Verkhovna Rada's presidium. In parliament, he belonged to an inter regional group of MPs supporting then-[[President of Ukraine]] [[Leonid Kuchma]]. In 1995, while carrying on as an MP, Azarov was appointed an adviser to the currency council of the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine. In 1996 he became Chairman of the State Tax Administration of Ukraine.

===Resignation as Prime Minister===
Azarov resigned 28 January 2014, amid [[2014 Hrushevskoho Street riots|heavy riots]] and the [[Euromaidan]] protests.<ref name=BBCNews2014a/> According to his cabinet, Azarov was quoted saying that "In order to create additional opportunities for socio-political compromise, for the sake of the peaceful settlement of the conflict, I have made a personal decision to ask the Ukrainian president to accept my resignation from the post of Ukrainian prime minister".<ref>{{cite news|date=28 January 2014|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/187663.html|title=Ukrainian Prime Minister Azarov resigns|agency=Interfax-Ukraine|archivedate=2 February 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202103625/http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/187663.html|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Azarov flew to [[Austria]] to join family members in a private jet hours after quitting.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ukraine ex-PM Azarov jets to Austria|date=31 January 2014|url=http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/news/afp/140131/ukraine-ex-pm-azarov-jets-austria|newspaper=[[GlobalPost]]|agency=[[Agence France-Presse]]|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140203011517/http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/news/afp/140131/ukraine-ex-pm-azarov-jets-austria|archivedate=3 February 2014|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> As of 23 February 2014 Azarov is based in the [[Russian Federation]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/192150.html|title=Azarov left for Russia|agency=Interfax-Ukraine|date=23 February 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140227194551/http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/192150.html|archivedate=27 February ===Post-resignation===
On August 3, 2015, Azarov announced in [[Moscow]] the creation of a "[[Ukraine Salvation Committee]]," (Комитет спасения Украины) set up outside [[Ukraine]], seeking to have "all citizens, political parties, labor union and social movements to unite and restore order in our home by joint efforts" <ref>{{cite news|title=Ukraine's former prime minister Azarov announces establishing Ukraine Salvation Committee|url=http://tass.ru/en/world/812210|accessdate=3 August 2015|agency=[[Russian News Agency TASS]]|date=3 August 2015}}</ref> and to change Ukraine's leadership.<ref name=AzcomsU/><ref>{{cite news|title=Экс-премьер Азаров заявил о создании "Комитета спасения Украины"|url=http://ria.ru/world/20150803/1160170696.html|accessdate=3 August 2015|agency=[[RIA Novosti]]|date=3 August 2015}}</ref> The committee's chairman and its choice for [[President of Ukraine]] is [[Volodymyr Oliynyk]].<ref name=AzcomsU/> Azarov claimed he could not name all members of the committee because some lived in Ukraine and it would be dangerous to disclose their names.<ref name=AzcomsU>[http://www.rferl.org/content/ukraine-ex-pm-azarov-forms-salvation-committee/27167032.html Ex-PM Azarov, In Moscow, Proclaims 'Salvation Committee' For Ukraine], [[Radio Free Europe]] (3 August 2015)</ref>

==Interpol Red Notice==
As of 12 January 2015, Azarov has had an Interpol Red Notice issued,<ref>{{cite web|title=AZAROV, MYKOLA|url=http://www.interpol.int/notice/search/wanted/2014-40791|website=interpol.int|accessdate=3 August 2015}}</ref> along with ex President [[Viktor Yanukovych]] and former Minister of Finance [[Yuriy Kolobov]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interpol.int/notice/search/wanted/2014-64176|title= INTERPOL|publisher=}}</ref> on charges of 'Misappropriation, embezzlement or conversion of property by malversation, if committed in respect of an especially gross amount, or by an organized group.'<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interpol.int/notice/search/wanted/2014-40791|title= INTERPOL|publisher=}}</ref>

Since 3 July 2014 Azarov is in the international wanted list for abuse of power.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/07/3/7030915|newspaper=[[Ukrayinska Pravda]]|date=3 July 2014|title=Азаров знаходиться у міждержавному розшуку – МВС|trans-title=Azarov is on the international wanted list – MIA|accessdate=3 July 2014}}</ref><ref name="en.interfax.com.ua">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/243934.html Ukraine accuses Russia of breaking CIS agreements over Yanukovych extradition], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (12 January 2015)</ref>

==Cultural and political image==
Azarov speaks [[Ukrainian language|Ukrainian]] poorly.<ref name=AdInterim/><ref name=news.bbc.co.uk>{{cite news|work=BBC News|date=26 April 2012|accessdate=9 June 2012|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/country_profiles/1102303.stm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120609073220/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/country_profiles/1102303.stm|title=Ukraine country profile|archivedate=9 June 2012|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref name=Ukrlang>{{cite news|work=[[Kyiv Post]]|date=11 March 2010|url=http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/61440/|title=Azarov of Party of Regions swears speaking Ukrainian if appointed prime minister|accessdate=11 March 2010|url-status=live|archivedate=5 June 2011|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110605061452/http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/61440/|df=dmy-all}}<br/>{{YouTube|9zDq9jVj_Ng|Уроки куртульной речи от министра б'Азарова.}}</ref> Nevertheless, he assured his constituents in early March 2010 that [[First Azarov Government|his government]] will be speaking Ukrainian.<ref name=Ukrlang/> In April 2011 he also stated: "I feel one hundred percent Ukrainian".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/102180/|title=Azarov: I feel one hundred percent Ukrainian|date=12 April 2011|accessdate=12 April 2011|newspaper=Kyiv Post|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110605061452/http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/61440/|archivedate=5 June 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> In an 11 March 2010 article the UK daily ''[[The Guardian]]'' labelled him the most [[Russophilia|Russophile]] member of the new cabinet.
