'''SVMK Inc.,''' doing business as '''SurveyMonkey,''' is an [[Survey data collection#Online surveys|online survey]]<ref>{{cite news|title=How to Use Online Tools for Customer Surveys|url=http://www.inc.com/guides/2010/07/how-to-use-online-tools-for-customer-surveys.html|accessdate=20 February 2013|newspaper=Inc.|date=30 July 2010}}</ref> development [[Cloud computing|cloud-based]] [[software as a service]] company. It was founded in 1999 by [[Ryan Finley (businessman)|Ryan Finley]] and Chris Finley. The company provides  surveys, and a suite of paid back-end programs.{{citation needed|date=April 2020}}

The company went public in September 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/05c52a50-c123-11e8-95b1-d36dfef1b89a|title=SurveyMonkey shares jump by 60% after Wall Street IPO|last=Waters|first=Richard|date=26 September 2018|access-date=28 September 2018}}</ref>

==History==
===Founding===
The company was owned by Ryan and Chris Finley until [[Spectrum Equity]] and [[Bain Capital]] acquired a majority interest in the company in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|title=SurveyMonkey Receives Investment From Spectrum Equity |url=https://www.spectrumequity.com/news/surveymonkey-receives-investment-from-spectrum-equity|publisher=Spectrum Equity|accessdate=January 15, 2019}}</ref>

[[Dave Goldberg]] joined SurveyMonkey in 2009.<ref name=GoldbergDeathCNNMoney>{{cite web | url=https://money.cnn.com/2015/05/02/technology/dave-goldberg-sheryl-sandberg-husband/ | title=Dave Goldberg, husband of Facebook exec Sheryl Sandberg, dies suddenly | publisher=CNN Money | date=May 2, 2015 | accessdate=May 2, 2015 | author=King, Hope}}</ref>

[[File:SurveyMonkeyNewHQ.jpg|thumb|SurveyMonkey Headquarters in San Mateo, CA]]

===Expansion===
In 2010, the company received [[United States dollar|US]]$100 million in debt financing from [[Bank of America Merrill Lynch]] and [[SunTrust Robinson Humphrey]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Matthew |last=Lynley |publisher=Venturebeat.com|url=https://venturebeat.com/2010/11/03/surveymonkey-debt-financing/ |title=SurveyMonkey bucks the trend, picks up $100M in debt financing |date= November 3, 2010 }}</ref>

In 2013, the company raised $800 million in debt and equity valuing the company at $1.35 billion.<ref>{{cite news|last=Lashinsky |first=Adam |url=http://tech.fortune.cnn.com/2013/01/17/surveymonkey-ipo/ |title=Why SurveyMonkey is holding off on an IPO - Fortune Tech |publisher=Tech.fortune.cnn.com |date=2013-01-17 |accessdate=2014-03-03}}</ref> In September 2013, the company announced [[HIPAA]]-compliant features for premium subscription holders.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marketwatch.com/story/surveymonkey-protects-patient-privacy-with-new-hipaa-features-2013-09-10 |title=SurveyMonkey Protects Patient Privacy with new HIPAA Features |publisher=MarketWatch |accessdate=2014-03-03}}</ref> In November 2013, the company announced a new enterprise product with additional management capabilities.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/alexkonrad/2013/11/18/surveymonkey-launches-enterprise-product/ |title=SurveyMonkey Jumps Into Enterprise Market With New Product For Businesses |work=Forbes |date=2013-11-18 |accessdate=2014-03-03 |first=Alex |last=Konrad}}</ref>

In 2014, the company raised $250 million in equity financing from [[Google Capital]] (now CapitalG), Tiger Global Management, Baillie Gifford, T. Rowe Price and Morgan Stanley.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fortune.com/2014/12/15/surveymonkey-is-worth-2-billion-after-latest-fundraising-round/|title=SurveyMonkey is worth $2 billion after new $250 million fundraising round|date=15 December 2014|accessdate=24 July 2016}}</ref>

Dave Goldberg died in May 2015.<ref name=GoldbergDeathCNNMoney />

[[Zander Lurie]] was named chairman of the board in July 2015.<ref>{{cite news |title= Sheryl Sandberg Joins SurveyMonkey's Board |author=Quentin Hardy |url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/07/06/sheryl-sandberg-joins-surveymonkeys-board/?_r=0 |newspaper=NY Times |date=July 6, 2015}}</ref>

Starting August 3, 2015, Bill Veghte replaced Goldberg as SurveyMonkey CEO. Veghte held top posts at HP and Microsoft.<ref name=VeghteForbes>{{cite web | url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/mzhang/2015/07/07/hps-bill-veghte-joins-survey-monkey-as-new-ceo/#cb8221b5ef87 | title=HP's Bill Veghte Joins SurveyMonkey As New CEO | publisher=Forbes Tech | date=July 7, 2015 | accessdate=October 7, 2017 | author=Zhang, Maggie}}</ref> Veghte left the role after fewer than 6 months due to strategic differences with investors.<ref name=VeghteExit>{{cite web | url=https://www.research-live.com/article/news/veghte-out-and-lurie-in-as-surveymonkey-ceo-/id/5001462 | title=Veghte out and Lurie in as SurveyMonkey CEO  | publisher=ResearchLive | date=15 January 2016 | accessdate=October 7, 2017 }}</ref> Veghte was replaced by Zander Lurie in January 2016.

In 2017, the company rolled out "SurveyMonkey Genius" which estimates survey performance and makes actionable suggestions to increase survey effectiveness.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.destinationcrm.com/Articles/ReadArticle.aspx?ArticleID=119389|title=SurveyMonkey Announces People Powered Data Platform|date=17 July 2017|publisher=DestinationCRM |accessdate=5 February 2019}}</ref> In August 2017, the company launched "SurveyMonkey CX" which assists organizations in managing their customer experience programs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://customerthink.com/can-surveymonkey-and-zoho-disrupt-enterprise-software/|title=Can SurveyMonkey and Zoho Disrupt Enterprise Software?|date=10 August 2017|publisher=CustomerThink |accessdate=5 February 2019}}</ref>

On September 26, 2018, SurveyMonkey (SVMK) made its debut on the [[NASDAQ]] Exchange. <reF>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/09/26/surveymonkey-svmk-ipo-stock-starts-trading-on-the-nasdaq.html|title=SurveyMonkey surges 42% in market debut, ends trading at $17 a share |publisher=CNBC |accessdate=December 21, 2018}}</ref> The stock launched at $12 per share and rose 43% on the first day of trading.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestreet.com/markets/ipos/survey-monkey-ipo-14724704|title=Survey Monkey Pops 43% on IPO |publisher=The Street |accessdate=December 21, 2018}}</ref>

Emory University Goizueta Business School dean Erika James joined the SurveyMonkey board of directors in 2018, creating Gender Parity on the company's board.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/90218542/surveymonkeys-board-is-now-50-women|title=SurveyMonkey's board is now 50% women |publisher=Fast Company |accessdate=29 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/atlanta/news/2018/08/14/surveymonkey-adds-emory-dean-to-board-of-directors.html|title=SurveyMonkey adds Emory dean to board of directors, balancing its gender representation|last=Mandel|first=Eric|date=14 August 2018|website=www.bizjournals.com|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref>
