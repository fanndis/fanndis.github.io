A '''White House conference''' is a national meeting sponsored by the [[Executive Office of the President of the United States]] with the purpose of discussing an issue or topic of importance to the American public.  Some last for one day while others last for several.<ref name="cwla">{{cite web | url=http://www.cwla.org/advocacy/whitehouseconf10.ppt  | title=White House Conference on Children and Youth: What’s It All About? | publisher=[[Child Welfare League of America]] | year=2008 | accessdate=2008-06-18}}</ref>  Typical attendees of a White House conference include experts in the particular field, community leaders and citizens with an interest in the issue.<ref name="hsw">{{cite web | url=http://reference.howstuffworks.com/white-house-conference-encyclopedia.htm | title=White House conference | publisher=[[HowStuffWorks]] | accessdate=2008-06-18}}</ref>  The President usually speaks to a general session of the conference; the conference concludes by issuing a report to the President summarizing issues and making recommendations for executive or legislative action.<ref name="hsw"/>  The [[First Lady of the United States]] also sometimes hosts White House conferences.<ref name="nfll">{{cite web | title = First Lady Biography: Hillary Clinton | url = http://www.firstladies.org/biographies/firstladies.aspx?biography=43 | publisher = National First Ladies' Library | accessdate = 2006-08-22 }}</ref><ref name="nfll-44">{{cite web | title = First Lady Biography: Laura Bush | url = http://www.firstladies.org/biographies/firstladies.aspx?biography=44 | publisher = National First Ladies' Library | accessdate = 2008-06-18 }}</ref>  White House conferences are typically created by specific legislation.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.reagan.utexas.edu/archives/speeches/1987/050587e.htm | title=Executive Order 12595 — White House Conference for a Drug Free America | publisher=[[Office of the Federal Register]] | date=1987-05-06 | accessdate=2008-06-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.whcoa.gov/about/history.asp | title=2005 White House Conference on Aging: History | publisher=[[The White House]] | date=2004-09-27 | accessdate=2008-06-18 |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080517165640/http://www.whcoa.gov/about/history.asp <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = 2008-05-17}}</ref>

The first White House conference was the Conference on the Care of Dependent Children held in 1909 under President [[Theodore Roosevelt]]; it later became the long-running [[White House Conference on Children and Youth]].<ref name="cwla"/>  The first iteration's recommendations called for the end of routine institutionalization of neglected or otherwise dependent children and the creation of the Federal Children's Bureau.<ref name="cwla"/> The most well-known White House conference is the [[White House Conference on Aging]], which has occurred once a decade since the 1950s.<ref name="cwla"/>  Other well-known conferences include the 1966 [[White House Conference on Civil Rights]] and the 1971 [[White House Conference on Youth]].  Other topics have included drug abuse, education, family life, nutrition, disabled people and other issues.<ref name="hsw"/>
