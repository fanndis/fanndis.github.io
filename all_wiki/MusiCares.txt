'''MusiCares Foundation''' is a non-profit organization established in 1989 and incorporated in 1993<ref name= corp/> by the [[National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences]]. Meant for musicians to have a place to turn in times of financial, personal, or medical crisis, its primary purpose is to focus the resources and attention of the music industry on human service issues which directly impact the health and welfare of the [[music community]]. The foundation's programs include emergency financial assistance, addiction recovery, outreach and leadership activities, and senior housing.<ref>{{cite web|title=MusiCares - Mission|url=https://www.grammy.com/musicares/about|website=Grammy.com|accessdate=August 15, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Addiction Recovery Resources|url=https://www.grammy.com/musicares/programs/addiction-recovery-resources|website=Grammy|accessdate=August 15, 2017}}</ref> MusiCares also offers hearing clinics backstage at several major festivals around the U.S. to help musicians protect their ears.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.grammy.com/musicares/news/hearing-clinics-protect-your-ears-musicares |title=Hearing clinics: Protect your ears with MusiCares |date=July 19, 2017 |website=Grammy.com}}</ref> Since 1989, MusiCares has distributed more than $48 million to artists in need.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/stevebaltin/2017/02/08/why-the-biggest-musicians-from-springsteen-and-dylan-to-neil-diamond-love-musicares/#370e2a94712e |title=Why The Biggest Musicians, From Springsteen and Dylan To Neil Diamond, Love MusiCares |last=Baltin |first=Steve |date=February 8, 2017 |website=Forbes}}</ref>

In September 2004, MusiCares acquired the '''Musicians Assistance Program''', a similar program assisting musicians in need, including [[drug rehabilitation]].<ref name="merge">{{cite web |url=http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20040921005401/en/MusiCares-Foundation-Acquires-Musicians-Assistance-Program-Creating |title=MusiCares Foundation Acquires Musicians' Assistance Program, Creating Preeminent Assistance Resource for Musicians; Agreement Unites Leadership, Experience to Help Artists in Need |website=[[Business Wire]] |date=September 21, 2004}}</ref>

MusiCares awards recording artists with the "[[MusiCares Person of the Year|Person of the Year Award]]", to commend musicians for their artistic achievement in the [[music industry]] and dedication to [[philanthropy]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/content_display/genre/e3i7da84139be87c4e2c9fefd2e064ffdb2|title=Neil Young Named MusiCares' Person of the Year |last=Donahue|first=Ann|date=January 30, 2010|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> and with the "[[Stevie Ray Vaughan|Stevie Ray Vaughan Award]]", to honor musicians on their commitment to helping others in the addiction recovery process.

==MusiCares MAP Fund Benefit Concert==
To help raise funding for the program, a MusiCares MAP Fund Benefit Concert is held annually since 2005.<ref name="amc">{{Cite web |url=https://www.amcnetworks.com/press-releases/musicares-map-fund-benefit-concert-to-honor-dave-navarro-and-goldenvoice/ |title=Musicares® Map Fund Benefit Concert to Honor Dave Navarro and Goldenvoice |date=April 21, 2005 |website=AMC Networks}}</ref> Among the awards given at the event is the '''Stevie Ray Vaughan Award''', named after the late guitarist [[Stevie Ray Vaughan]], which recognizes musicians for their devotion to helping other addicts struggling with the recovery process.<ref name="SRVA">{{cite web|title=12 recovery icons: Alice Cooper to Smokey Robinson|url=https://www.grammy.com/musicares/news/12-recovery-icons-alice-cooper-smokey-robinson|website=Grammy|accessdate=August 15, 2017|date=June 26, 2017}}</ref> 

Founded by longtime musician and addiction recovery activist [[Buddy Arnold]],<ref name="amc"/> the event was originally called ''Musicians' Assistance Program (MAP) Awards''.<ref name="SRV">{{cite web|url=http://www.stevieray.com/awards.htm|title=SRV Latest Awards & Achievements|website=www.stevieray.com}}</ref> [[Eric Clapton]] was honored with the Musicians' Assistance Program's (MAP) first Stevie Ray Vaughan Award on November 2, 1999.<ref name="SRV"/> In 2004, MusiCares acquired MAP and merged the two programs under the MusiCares banner.<ref name="merge"/><ref name="amc"/>

The 2010 Musicares MAP Fund benefit concert celebrated Women In Recovery and honored former U.S. first lady [[Betty Ford]] and the [[Betty Ford Center]]. [[Susan Ford Bales]] accepted the MusiCares MAP Fund award on behalf of her mother.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/los-angeles/article/MusiCares-MAP-Fund-Benefits-Women-In-Recovery-57-page3 |title=MusiCares MAP Fund Benefits Women In Recovery 5/7 |date=March 30, 2010 |website=Broadway World}}</ref>
