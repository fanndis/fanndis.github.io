'''Khalifa University''' (جامعة خليفة, also known as Khalifa University of Science, Technology & Research, or KUSTAR) is a science-focused [[university]] located in [[Abu Dhabi]], [[United Arab Emirates]] with a [[satellite campus]] in [[Sharjah (emirate)|Sharjah]]. In 2017 it is ranked as the 401st best university in the world by QS rankings.<ref>http://www.topuniversities.com/universities/khalifa-university</ref>

Founded in 2007 by a decree issued by UAE President His Highness Sheikh [[Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan]], Khalifa University was established in an effort to support a knowledge-based economy that will contribute to the state’s post-oil future.<ref name="sasaki.com">{{Cite web |url=http://www.sasaki.com/project/143/sustainability-creates-vibrant-community/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2012-10-17 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130202044147/http://www.sasaki.com/project/143/sustainability-creates-vibrant-community/ |archive-date=2013-02-02 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Currently, it is merging with  [[The Petroleum Institute]] and [[Masdar Institute of Science and Technology]].<ref>{{cite web|author=The National|url=https://www.thenational.ae/uae/government/abu-dhabi-approves-merger-of-universities-and-billions-in-projects-1.192240 |title=Abu Dhabi approves merger of universities and billions in projects |publisher=Thenational.ae |date=2016-10-13 |accessdate=2016-10-13}}</ref>

The university has a student population of approximately 1300.<ref name="kustar.ac.ae"/>

In the 2019 Times Higher Education Asia University Rankings, Khalifa University placed as the 28th best university in Asia. In September 2018, Khalifa University was ranked 1st in the UAE and 2nd in the Arab region by the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2019.<ref>{{cite web|author=The National|url=https://www.timeshighereducation.com/world-university-rankings/2019/regional-ranking#!/page/1/length/25/sort_by/rank/sort_order/asc/cols/stats |title=Asia University Rankings 2019 |publisher=Timeshighereducation}}</ref> The AI Institute has a funding outlay of AED 160 million for a five-year period.<ref>{{cite web|author=Khaleej Times|url=https://www.khaleejtimes.com/technology/new-uae-based-institute-to-boost-students-artificial-intelligence-skills |title=New UAE-based institute to boost students' Artificial Intelligence skills|publisher=Khaleej Times}}</ref>

== History ==
Originally established in 1989 as the Etisalat College of Engineering (ECE), the school’s main campus was built in Sharjah, and aimed to supply the Emirates Telecommunications Corporation ([[Etisalat]]) with technologically trained workers.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.etisalat.com/html/corporate/history.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2012-10-21 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130122161407/http://www.etisalat.com/html/corporate/history.html |archive-date=2013-01-22 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Khalifa University’s current president, Tod A. Laursen, was appointed in August 2010. As of 2013, KU faculty and staff come from over 40 countries, and its student body is internationally diverse and co-educational.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kustar.ac.ae/pages/ku-at-a-glance#tab3|title=Employees at Khalifa University|publisher=kustar.ac.ae|access-date=2019-08-06|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140619095532/http://www.kustar.ac.ae/pages/ku-at-a-glance%23tab3#tab3|archive-date=2014-06-19|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== International & Civil Security ===
Distinct in the Gulf,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.albawaba.com/region%E2%80%99s-first-graduate-program-international-and-civil-security-be-showcased-khalifa-university-ide |title=Region’s first graduate program in International and Civil Security to be showcased by Khalifa University at IDEX 2011 |publisher=Al Bawaba |date=2011-02-16 |accessdate=2012-10-17}}</ref> Khalifa University's Institute for International and Civil Security (IICS) seeks to "produce research which will strengthen the security of the UAE in a way that is responsive to the needs of national and global security decision-makers ... enabling the programme to make major contributions that enhance regional, international and human security."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.khaleejtimes.com/displayarticle.asp?xfile=/data/theuae/2011/July/theuae_July689.xml&section=theuae |title=KU launches Master’s in International, Civil Security |publisher=Khaleejtimes.com |date=2011-07-27 |accessdate=2012-10-17}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.albawaba.com/khalifa-university-master%E2%80%99s-international-and-civil-security-receives-accreditation-385468 |title=Khalifa University Master’s in International and Civil Security receives accreditation |publisher=Al Bawaba |date=2011-07-26 |accessdate=2012-10-17}}</ref> The IICS's director is Professor [[Joel Hayward]], "a world authority on international conflict and strategy".<ref name=Al-Khaleej>{{cite web|url= http://www.alkhaleej.ae/alkhaleej/page/d797498c-4e4a-45d9-91fc-97473b742b6a|title= Professor at Khalifa University publishes Book on the Effects of Airpower on the Environment (Arabic)|accessdate=23  April 2014|author= |publisher= Al-Khaleej (Arabic language), 11 February 2014}}</ref>

=== Academic partnership ===
In recent years, KU has established a number of international academic and research partnerships. They include associations with the [[Georgia Institute of Technology]],<ref name="ReferenceA">http://www.kustar.ac.ae/pages/strategic-partnerships</ref> [[Texas A&M University]] Nuclear Security Science & Policy Institute,<ref name="ReferenceA"/> and [[KAIST]]. In April 2011, Khalifa University signed a [[memorandum of understanding]] with the [[University of Bristol]] to establish and create the Visual Signal Analysis and Processing (VSAP) research centre at the university’s Abu Dhabi campus.

=== Campuses ===
'''Campus Expansion'''

The University has plans to build a new campus near the current campus of Abu Dhabi. The new campus would be 80 hectares (198 acres) in size and allow enrollment to increase up to 6,000 students.<ref name="sasaki.com"/>  The new campus will house a medical school, state of the art labs, a student hub, and sporting facilities.<ref name="sasaki.com"/>

=== Student Life ===
Khalifa University’s campus student newsletter is ''The Student Voice'', which is published once a semester. Students can also participate in various clubs and academic organizations ranging from student government to the sports club.<ref>http://www.kustar.ac.ae/pages/campus-life</ref>
