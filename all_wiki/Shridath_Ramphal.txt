'''Sir Shridath Surendranath Ramphal''' {{post-nominals|size=100%|country=GBR-cats|GCMG|AC|ONZ|OE|OCC|QC}} (born 3 October 1928), often known as '''Sir Sonny Ramphal''', is a [[Guyana|Guyanese]] politician who was the second [[Commonwealth Secretary-General]], holding the position from 1975 to 1990. He was also the [[foreign minister]] of [[Guyana]] from 1972 to 1975, and assistant attorney general of the [[West Indies Federation]] from 1958 to 1962. 

Shridath Ramphal is an [[Earth Charter]] International Commission member.

==Biography==
Ramphal was born in [[New Amsterdam, Guyana|New Amsterdam]], [[British Guiana]], to an [[Indo-Guyanese]] family. After attending schools in [[Georgetown, Guyana|Georgetown]], Ramphal studied law at [[King's College London]], graduating with [[LL.B]] and [[LL.M]] degrees. He was [[called to the bar]] at [[Gray's Inn]] in London in 1951. As a [[pupil barrister]] he worked with the British politician and lawyer [[Dingle Foot]]. He continued studying law for a year at [[Harvard Law School]] in the US on a 1962 [[Guggenheim Fellowship]].<ref name="Integrationist">{{cite web|title=Shridath Ramphal|url=http://www.theintegrationistcaribbean.org/integration/speecheswritings/shridath-ramphal/|website=The Integrationist Caribbean|accessdate=9 November 2017}}</ref><ref name="JTT">{{cite web|title=Sir Shridath Ramphal|url=http://www.ttlawcourts.org/index.php/component/attachments/download/2380|website=The Judiciary of Trinidad and Tobago|accessdate=9 November 2017}}</ref>

Ramphal started his legal career as a Crown Counsel in the Attorney-General’s Office in 1953, becoming Solicitor-General and then Assistant Attorney-General of the short-lived West Indies Federation. After a period in private practice in Jamaica he returned to Guyana in 1965 to be the [[Attorney General of Guyana|Attorney General]]. Two years later he was also appointed Minister of State in the Ministry of External Affairs, later becoming Minister of Justice (from 1973) and Minister of Foreign Affairs (from 1972). In 1975 he left Guyana to be Commonwealth Secretary-General.<ref> {{cite web|url=http://www.stabroeknews.com/2008/features/10/03/tributes-to-sir-shridath-ramphal-on-his-eightieth-birthday/|title= Tributes to Sir Shridath Ramphal on his eightieth birthday|work= [[Stabroek News]]|date=3 October 2008 |accessdate = 27 July 2015}} </ref>

He also served as the Chancellor of the [[University of Warwick]] from 1989 to 2002, at the [[University of the West Indies]] until 2003, and as Chancellor of the [[University of Guyana]]. 

During Ramphal's time as [[Commonwealth of Nations|Commonwealth]] Secretary-General, the [[United Kingdom]] represented by [[Margaret Thatcher]] was found to be in a minority of one on the issue of [[economic sanctions]] against [[History of South Africa in the apartheid era|apartheid]] [[South Africa]].

With [[Ingvar Carlsson]], he was in 1995 one of the co-chairs of the [[Commission on Global Governance]], which reported on issues of [[international development]], [[international security]], [[globalization]] and [[global governance]].

==Honours and awards==
Sir Shridath was appointed a Companion of the [[Order of St Michael and St George]] (CMG) in the [[1966 Birthday Honours]] (the list was dated to 25 May of that year).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/44004/supplement/6533 |title=London Gazette, Supplement: 44004 |publisher=London-gazette.co.uk |page=6533|date=3 June 1966 |accessdate=19 July 2014}}</ref> He was [[Knight Bachelor|knighted]] in the [[1970 New Year Honours]], and invested with his knighthood by the Queen at Buckingham Palace on 3 February.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/45004/supplement/49 |title=London Gazette, Supplement: 45004 |publisher=London-gazette.co.uk |page=49|date=30 December 1969 |accessdate=19 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/45036/supplement/1551 |title=London Gazette, Supplement: 45036 |publisher=London-gazette.co.uk |page=1551|date=6 February 1970 |accessdate=19 July 2014}}</ref> He was appointed a Knight Grand Cross of the Order of St. Michael and St. George (GCMG) in 1990.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.barbadosadvocate.com/newsitem.asp?more=local&NewsID=20498 |title=Chancellor’s choice: Sir Shridath awarded Chancellor’s Medal |first=Tanya |last=Lightbourne|newspaper=Barbados Advocate |date=22 October 2011 |accessdate=19 July 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140728224712/http://www.barbadosadvocate.com/newsitem.asp?more=local&NewsID=20498 |archivedate=28 July 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

On 26 February 1982, Sir Shridath was appointed an honorary Companion of the [[Order of Australia]] (AC).<ref>[http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=882139&search_type=advanced&showInd=true Australia: It's an Honour]</ref>On 6 February 1990, Ramphal was the 19th appointee to the [[Order of New Zealand]],<ref>"Honours and Awards" (15 February 1990), 23 ''[[New Zealand Gazette]]'' 445 at 446.</ref> New Zealand's highest civil honour. He was decorated as a Member of the [[Order of the Caribbean Community]] (OCC) in the first conferment in 1992.<ref name="JTT"/> In May 2006 Ramphal was appointed an Honorary Fellow of [[Royal Society of Arts]]. He is a Vice-President of the [[Royal Commonwealth Society]]. The Ramphal Building at the [[University of Warwick]] was named in his honour.
