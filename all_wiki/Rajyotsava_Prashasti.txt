The '''Rajyotsava Prashasti''' or ''' Rajyotsava Awards''',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.karnataka.com/profile/rajyotsava/|title=Karnataka Rajyotsava &#124; Karnataka Formation Day|date=29 October 2017|accessdate=23 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ibnlive.in.com/news/karnataka-harish-hande-among-50-state-awardees/197777-60-115.html|title=News18.com: CNN-News18 Breaking News India, Latest News Headlines, Live News Updates|website=News18|accessdate=23 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deccanherald.com/content/109120/rajyotsava-awards-162.html|title=Rajyotsava awards for 162|date=30 October 2010|website=Deccan Herald|accessdate=23 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Archive |url=http://www.hindu.com/2010/10/31/stories/2010103162631000.htm |title=National : 159 chosen for Karnataka Rajyotsava Award |publisher=The Hindu |accessdate=2019-10-23}}</ref> the second highest civilian honor of the [[Karnataka]] state of India are conferred annually by the [[Government of Karnataka|Karnataka Government]] on the occasion of the establishment of the state on 1 November celebrated as the [[Kannada Rajyotsava]].<ref name="rajyotsava">{{cite web|url=http://www.mphasis.com/newsroom/RajyotsavaAward.htm|title=Rajyotsava Award for Jerry Rao|work=Online webpage of Mphasis|publisher=Mphasis|accessdate=2007-07-08}}</ref>

The awards are presented in [[Bengaluru]] by the [[Chief Minister of Karnataka]] on 1 November of every year. Each award carries an amount of {{INR}}100,000, a 20-gram gold medal and a citation.<ref>{{cite news|title=60 persons to get Rajyotsava award|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/bangalore/60-persons-to-get-rajyotsava-award/article7807734.ece|newspaper=The Hindu|accessdate=2 April 2017|date=27 October 2015}}</ref> In addition to that, the government has in the past, allotted commercial land for eligible awardees.<ref name="award">{{cite web|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/2004/10/24/stories/2004102406660400.htm|title= 1,000 applications received|work=Online webpage of The Hindu|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=2007-07-08}}</ref>

==History==
Karnataka Rajyotsava Award was instituted in 1966 to be awarded to citizens of Karnataka in recognition of their distinguished contribution in various spheres of activity including the [[Arts]], [[Education]], Industry, [[Literature]], [[Science]], [[Sports]], [[Medicine]], [[Social Service]] and [[Public affairs (broadcasting)|Public Affairs]]. It has also been awarded to some distinguished individuals who were not citizens of Karnataka but did contribute in various ways to Karnataka. The award was not conferred in 1977, 1978, 1979, 1980 and 2009.<ref>{{cite web|title=ರಾಜ್ಯೋತ್ಸವ ಪ್ರಶಸ್ತಿ ಸಂಪೂರ್ಣ ಪಟ್ಟಿ 1966 ರಿಂದ  - 2015 ರವರೆಗೆ|url=http://siri.kannadasiri.co.in/kannadasiri/downloads/Rajothsava_List.pdf|website=Kannada Siri|accessdate=24 June 2017}}</ref>

===2019 awards===
{{Main|Rajyotsava Awards (2019)}}

The [[Government of Karnataka]] announced 64 names for the year 2019 on the occasion of the 64th anniversary of the awards. The awards were given away at [[Ravindra Kalakshetra]] in Bengaluru on 1 November 2019 by Chief Minister [[B. S. Yeddyurappa]].

The awardees for 2019 include [[K. Chidananda Gowda]] (former Vice Chancellor, Kuvempur University), V. A. Deshpande (sculptor), Mohan H Sitnoor (artist), Chandrakanth Karadalli (Children's writer), H. Kushi (young Yoga exponent), Muddu Mohan (Hindustani musician), among others.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thenewsminute.com/article/karnataka-cm-yediyurappa-present-rajyotsava-awards-nov-1-full-list-111346 |title=Karnataka CM Yediyurappa to present Rajyotsava awards on Nov 1 |date=29 October 2019 |work=The News Minute |access-date=5 November 2019}}{{Cite news |url=https://www.thenewsminute.com/article/karnataka-cm-yediyurappa-present-rajyotsava-awards-nov-1-full-list-111346 |title=Karnataka CM Yediyurappa to present Rajyotsava awards on Nov 1 |date=29 October 2019 |work=The News Minute |access-date=5 November 2019}}</ref>

===2014 awards===
{{Main|Rajyotsava Awards (2014)}}
The [[Government of Karnataka]] announced 59 names for the year 2014<ref>{{cite web | url=http://karnatakavarthe.org/en/rajyotsava-award-2014/ | title=Rajyotsava Award – 2014 | publisher=Government of Karnataka, Department of Information and Public relations | date=30 October  2014 | accessdate=2 November 2014}}</ref> on the occasion of the 59th anniversary of the awards. The 2014 awards saw 1924 nominations<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.deccanherald.com/content/438794/list-rajyotsava-award-winners.html | title=9 to get Rajyotsava award | date=30 October 2014 | agency=Deccan Herald | accessdate=2 November 2014 | location=Bangalore}}</ref> that were screened by a panel. The award winners for 2014 include [[S. Janaki]] (playback singer), [[Manepalli Narayana Rao Venkatachaliah|
Justice M N Venkatachalaih]] (former [[Chief Justice of India]]), [[Krishnaswamy Kasturirangan|Dr. K. Kasturirangan]] (former [[ISRO]] head), Dr. B.N. Suresh (ISRO scientist), [[M. R. Poovamma]] (athlete), [[Mamatha Poojary|Mamatha Poojari]] ([[kabaddi]] player),  [[M. S. Rajashekar]] (Kannada movie director), [[Vaijanath Biradar|Vaijanath Biradar Patil]] (veteran Kannada actor), D Kumardas (Hindustani vocal singer), amongst others.
