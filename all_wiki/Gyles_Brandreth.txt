'''Gyles Daubeney Brandreth''' (born 8 March 1948) is an English writer, broadcaster, actor, and former [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] Member of Parliament.

==Early life==
Brandreth was born in [[Wuppertal]], Germany, where his father, Charles Brandreth, was serving as a legal officer with the [[Allied Control Council|Allied Control Commission]].<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|title=Gyles Brandreth Biography|url=http://www.bookbrowse.com/biographies/index.cfm/author_number/1519/gyles-brandreth|work=bookbrowse.com|accessdate=10 August 2013}}</ref> After moving to London with his parents at the age of three, Brandreth was educated at the [[Lycée Français Charles de Gaulle]] (as it is called today) in [[South Kensington]], [[Bedales School]] in [[Petersfield]], [[Hampshire]], where he met his friend [[Simon Cadell]], and [[New College, Oxford]], where he met [[Rick Stein]].<ref name="Bio"/>

He was President of the [[Oxford Union]] in [[Michaelmas term]], 1969, and edited the university magazine ''[[Isis (magazine)|Isis]]''. He was described in a contemporaneous publication as "Oxford's [[Pooh-Bah|Lord High Everything Else]]".<ref>''Cherwell Guide 1972''</ref> [[Christopher Hitchens]] suggested that Brandreth "set out to make himself into a [[Kenneth Tynan|Ken Tynan]]. Wore a cloak."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/books/7778346/An-audience-with-Christopher-Hitchens.html|location=London, UK|work=The Daily Telegraph|first=Nigel|last=Farndale|title=An audience with Christopher Hitchens|date=2 June 2010}}</ref> He became a theatre producer, politician, journalist, author and publisher as well as, later, a TV presenter.

==Television==
In the 1970s Brandreth hosted the [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] children's show ''Puzzle Party''.

He has appeared on ''[[Countdown (game show)|Countdown]]'' more than 300 times, in Dictionary Corner, including [[Carol Vorderman]]'s final episode in 2008, making more appearances than any other guest. He also appeared on [[TV-am]]. He was known for his collection of jumpers, of which some were sold in a charity auction in 1993.

Brandreth hosted the short-lived game show ''[[Public Opinion (TV series)|Public Opinion]]'' in 2004.<ref>{{cite web |title=Public Opinion |url=http://www.ukgameshows.com/ukgs/Public_Opinion |website=UK Game Shows |accessdate=18 March 2020}}</ref> In 2006 he appeared on the television series ''[[That Mitchell and Webb Look]]'', on the fictional game show "[[Numberwang]]", satirising his appearances in ''Countdown'''s Dictionary Corner. In 2007 he guest-starred in the ''[[Doctor Who]]'' audio play ''[[I.D. (Doctor Who audio)|I.D.]]''. From July to August 2009 he hosted the game show ''[[Knowitalls]]'' on [[BBC Two]]. In April 2010 he appeared on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s ''Vote Now Show''. He also made a cameo appearance as himself in Channel 4 sitcom ''[[The IT Crowd]]'', in the episode "The Final Countdown".

A frequent guest on BBC television panel shows, he has appeared on five episodes of ''[[QI]]'' and six episodes of ''[[Have I Got News for You]]''. He has also appeared in episodes of Channel 5's ''[[The Gadget Show]]'', and is a contributor to the BBC's early evening programme ''[[The One Show]]''. He has appeared in two episodes of the TV adaptation of ''[[Just A Minute]]'', as part of the show's 45th anniversary. In 2013 he was a guest on the Matt Lucas Awards.

He appeared on ''[[Room 101 (British TV series)|Room 101]]'' in 2005, while [[Paul Merton]] was host, successfully banishing the [[Royal Variety Performance]] and the [[British honours system]] into Room 101, saying that he would never accept an honour himself.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iYsX0fHV9aE|work=BBC|title=Room 101 with Gyles Brandreth|date=5 October 2005}}</ref> In 2013 he clarified that position, stating that he had "no fundamental objection to the honours system", and that he selected the honours system for Room 101 because he could "tell funny stories about it".<ref>{{Citation|last=ChatPolitics|title=Gyles Brandreth on Tony Blair, the monarchy, and 'Just A Minute'|date=2013-08-30|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=alK-9YFep9c|accessdate=2016-08-07}}</ref>

In 2019, Brandreth appeared on series 1 of [[Celebrity Gogglebox]] alongside [[Sheila Hancock]]. In 2020, it was announced Brandreth would be returning for Series 2, however this time alongside [[Maureen Lipman]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Curtis Brown|url=https://www.curtisbrown.co.uk/news/gyles-brandreth-joins-celebrity-gogglebox|access-date=2020-07-13|website=www.curtisbrown.co.uk}}</ref> <ref>{{Cite web|title=Lloyds Bank sponsors Channel 4's Celebrity Gogglebox in first ever TV sponsorship {{!}} Channel 4|url=https://www.channel4.com/press/news/lloyds-bank-sponsors-channel-4s-celebrity-gogglebox-first-ever-tv-sponsorship|access-date=2020-07-13|website=www.channel4.com}}</ref>

==Radio==
Brandreth has presented programmes on London's [[LBC]] radio at various times since 1973, such as ''Star Quality''. He frequently appears on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s comedy panel game ''[[Just a Minute]]''.<ref name="Bio"/> He has appeared on several episodes of Radio 4's political programme ''[[The Westminster Hour]]'', explaining his thoughts on how to make the most of being a government minister.
From 2003 to 2005 Brandreth hosted the Radio 4 comedy panel game ''[[Whispers (radio series)|Whispers]]''.

In 2006, Brandreth appeared in the Radio 4 comedy programme ''[[Living with the Enemy (radio programme)|Living with the Enemy]]'' which he co-wrote with comedian [[Nick Revell]], in which they appear as a former Conservative government minister and a former comedian. In 2010 he broadcast a Radio 4 documentary about his great-great-grandfather, [[Benjamin Brandreth]], the inventor of a medicine called "Brandreth's Pills". He is the host of the Radio 4 comedy [[panel show]] ''[[Wordaholics]]'', first aired on 20 February 2012. He appeared on the Radio 4 programme ''[[The Museum of Curiosity]]'' in August 2017, to which he donated a button that was once owned by a famous actor.

In April 2019, Brandreth began co-hosting a podcast titled ''Something Rhymes With Purple'' alongside friend and colleague [[Susie Dent]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://play.acast.com/s/somethingrhymeswithpurple|title=Something Rhymes With Purple podcast archive.|website=acast.com|access-date=16 August 2019}}</ref> The podcast discusses aspects of the English language such as historic or unusual words and their origins, as well as the origins of popular phrases and sayings.

==Writing==
Since the 1970s Brandreth has written various books about [[Scrabble]], words, puzzles and jokes, for adults and children and co-founded the ''[[Games & Puzzles]]'' magazine.
He wrote an authorised biography of actor [[John Gielgud]], as well as [[lipogram]]mic reworks of Shakespeare. In the 1980s, Brandreth wrote scripts for ''[[Dear Ladies]]'', the television programme featuring [[Hinge and Bracket]]. Brandreth is also the creator of a stage show called ''Zipp!'' which enjoyed success at the [[Edinburgh Festival]] and had a short run in the West End.<ref>{{cite web|last1=London Theatre|title=Zipp! The Musical with Gyles Brandreth at Duchess 23 Jan 03|url=https://www.londontheatre.co.uk/theatre-news/news/zipp-the-musical-with-gyles-brandreth-at-duchess-23-jan-03|website=London Theatre|accessdate=20 April 2018}}</ref>

Brandreth has kept a diary.  In 1999, he published his diaries between 1990 and 1997, written during his days as a politician, called ''Breaking the Code.''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gylesbrandreth.net/main/main.php?page=books|title=Gyles Brandreth: Author, Broadcaster, Actor, Entertainer|website=www.gylesbrandreth.net|access-date=2016-03-19}}</ref>

In September 2004, Brandreth's book on the marriage of [[Elizabeth II|the Queen]] and the [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh|Duke of Edinburgh]], ''Philip and Elizabeth: Portrait of a Marriage'' was published.<ref name="Bio" /> In July 2005, he published a second book on the Royal Family, entitled ''Charles and Camilla: Portrait of a Love Affair'', which concerns the three-decade love affair between [[Charles, Prince of Wales]] and [[Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall]].<ref name="Bio" />

Brandreth has written a series of seven works of historical fiction called ''The Oscar Wilde Murder Mysteries'', in which [[Oscar Wilde]] works with both [[Robert Sherard]] and [[Arthur Conan Doyle]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscarwildemurdermysteries.com/|title=The Oscar Wilde Murder Mysteries by Gyles Brandreth|website=www.oscarwildemurdermysteries.com|access-date=2016-03-19}}</ref>

Over the years he has written and appeared in a number of comedic one-man shows and toured in a number of venues. Shows have included ''The One-to-One Show'' in 2010–2011, ''Looking for Happiness'' in 2013–2014 and ''Word Power'' in 2015–2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.waterstones.com/author/gyles-brandreth/42359|title=Gyles Brandreth books and biography {{!}} Waterstones|website=www.waterstones.com|language=en|access-date=2018-09-14}}</ref>

Brandreth has also written a book entitled ''Have You Eaten Grandma?'' which is about the English language and correct grammar.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.penguin.co.uk/authors/gyles-brandreth/9837/|title=Gyles Brandreth|website=www.penguin.co.uk|language=en|access-date=2018-09-14}}</ref>

==Politics==
Brandreth was a [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] Member of Parliament (MP), representing the [[City of Chester (constituency)|City of Chester]], from 1992 to 1997. He proposed a Private Member's Bill which became law as the [[Marriage Act 1994]]. In 1995, he was appointed to a [[junior minister]]ial position as a [[Lord of the Treasury]], with his role being essentially that of a [[whip (politics)|whip]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/programmes/the_westminster_hour/2317053.stm|work=BBC News|title=The Brandreth Rules|date=10 January 2006}}</ref><ref>[http://www.jla.co.uk/conference-speakers/gyles-brandreth] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090602062705/http://www.jla.co.uk/conference-speakers/gyles-brandreth |date=2 June 2009 }}</ref>

He later published a book of his diaries from his time as a whip, ''Breaking the Code''. After his parliamentary career, he broadcast some of his reminiscences on [[BBC]] radio as ''Brandreth on Office'' and ''The Brandreth Rules'' in 2001, 2003 and 2005.

In August 2014, Brandreth was one of 200 public figures who were signatories to a letter to ''[[The Guardian]]'' opposing [[Scottish independence]] in the run-up to September's [[2014 Scottish independence referendum|referendum on that issue]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/aug/07/celebrities-open-letter-scotland-independence-full-text|title=Celebrities' open letter to Scotland – full text and list of signatories|work=The Guardian|date=7 August 2014|accessdate=26 August 2014}}</ref>

==Other activities==
Brandreth is a former European [[Monopoly (game)|Monopoly]] champion,<ref name=portrait>{{cite news| url = https://www.telegraph.co.uk/motoring/2721641/Portrait-of-a-driver-Gyles-Brandreth.html | title = Portrait of a driver: Gyles Brandreth | accessdate = 21 January 2009 | date = 7 April 2003 | newspaper = [[The Daily Telegraph]] | location=London | first=Harry | last=Mount}}</ref> and president of the [[Association of British Scrabble Players]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absp.org.uk/absp/gyles.shtml |title=Gyles Brandreth |publisher=ABSP |accessdate=9 June 2012}}</ref> having organised the first [[National Scrabble Championship (UK)|British National Scrabble Championship]] in 1971.

He is also the president of the [[Oscar Wilde]] Society. The society was founded in September 1990 by a group of fans of Wilde and his work. It is a non-profit organisation that aims to increase knowledge, enjoyment and study of Wilde's life, personality and works. It organises lectures, readings and discussions, as well as visits to places connected with him.<ref name="royalcentral.co.uk">{{cite web|url=http://royalcentral.co.uk/uk/charlesandcamilla/camilla-to-join-gyles-brandreth-to-mark-161st-anniversary-of-oscar-wildes-birth-55042|title=Camilla to join Gyles Brandreth to mark 161st anniversary of Oscar Wilde's birth|website=Royal Central|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-03-19|date=16 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://oscarwildesociety.co.uk/ |title=Welcome to the website for |publisher=The Oscar Wilde Society |accessdate=2017-03-12}}</ref>

Brandreth hosts an annual Oscar Wilde party to celebrate the writer's birth. Guests have included [[Stephen Fry]], [[Joanna Lumley]], [[Derek Jacobi]], [[Julian Fellowes]] and [[Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall]]<ref name="royalcentral.co.uk"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tatler.com/bystander/events/2014/october/oscar-wilde-party-at-the-langham#!/15526/image/1|title=Oscar Wilde party at The Langham|website=Tatler|access-date=2016-03-19}}</ref> The venues are often places of interest in Wilde's life, for example the [[Langham Hotel, London|Langham Hotel]] where ''[[A Picture of Dorian Gray]]'' was commissioned.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://oscarwildesociety.co.uk/events/|title=Events|website=Oscar Wilde Society|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-19}}</ref> In August 2005, he appeared in a production of Shakespeare's ''[[Twelfth Night]]'' at the [[Edinburgh Festival]].

He is an [[after-dinner speaker]] and held the world record for the longest continuous after-dinner speech,  twelve-and-a-half hours, done as a charity stunt. With his wife, he founded a [[Teddy bear museum]]. Located in [[Stratford-upon-Avon]] for 18 years, it was relocated to the Polka Theatre in [[Wimbledon, London]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.polkatheatre.com/editorial/teddy-bear-collection|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111015040432/http://www.polkatheatre.com/editorial/teddy-bear-collection|url-status=dead|archive-date=15 October 2011|title=Polka Theatre - World-class theatre for children|website=www.polkatheatre.com|access-date=2016-03-19}}</ref> In 2016, the museum moved to [[Newby Hall]] in Yorkshire.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-york-north-yorkshire-36372330 |title=Gyles Brandreth's 1,000 teddies move to Newby Hall |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=24 May 2016 |website=BBC |access-date=28 August 2019 }}</ref> He is a patron of the [[National Piers Society]] and vice-president of charity [[Fields in Trust]] (formerly the [[National Playing Fields Association]]).

[[File:Shaheel Graduation felicitation.jpg|thumb|Brandreth awarding a degree to a student at a 2017 graduation ceremony at the University of Chester]]
In 2014, Brandreth was awarded the [[honorary degree]] of [[Doctor of Letters]] (DLitt) from the [[University of Chester]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://alumni.chester.ac.uk/Pages/honorary-graduates-2014.aspx|title= Honorary Graduates 2014|accessdate=12 March 2019|work=University of Chester website}}</ref> and was appointed the university's [[Chancellor (education)|chancellor]] in December 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chester.ac.uk/node/39096|title=University's new Chancellor to be Gyles Brandreth.|publisher=[[University of Chester]]|date=8 December 2016|accessdate=12 June 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Brandreth married Michèle Brown, a writer and publisher, in [[Westminster]] in 1973.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=DceauGrhRZKbZo%2BSgQQ1MA&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=9 February 2017|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=3vPGdqJ44LGnLYxvwCs8ow&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=9 February 2017|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref> They reside in [[Barnes, London|Barnes, Southwest London]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://richmonduponthamesnotables.tumblr.com/search/gyles |title=Famous Residents of Richmond-upon-Thames |publisher=Richmonduponthamesnotables.tumblr.com |date=2015-12-18 |accessdate=2017-03-12}}</ref> and have three grown-up children: Benet, a barrister; Saethryd, a journalist; and Aphra, a former government economist and now financial director of a veterinary business,<ref name=Aphra>{{cite web|url=http://www.thegoodlifesurbiton.co.uk/|title=The Good Life, Surbiton's Community Newspaper.|work=Issue 45, March 2019, page 8.}}</ref> and who is mother of their first grandchild, Kiyo.<ref>{{cite news|author=Lynn Barber |url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2005/aug/07/edinburghfestival2005.edinburghfestival |title=Turned out nice again &#124; Culture |newspaper=The Guardian |accessdate=2017-03-12}}</ref> Aphra was the Conservative candidate in the [[2019 United Kingdom general election]] for the constituency of [[Kingston and Surbiton (UK Parliament constituency)|Kingston and Surbiton]],<ref name=Aphra/> but did not win the seat.
