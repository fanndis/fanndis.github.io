Reported in outlets including {{cite web |url= https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2020/0527/1142943-octavia-spencer-revises-her-age-upwards-on-her-birthday/ |title=Octavia Spencer revises her age upwards for her birthday |publisher=[[RTÉ]] |accessdate= May 27, 2020 |archivedate=May 27, 2020 |archiveurl =https://web.archive.org/web/20200527162732/https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2020/0527/1142943-octavia-spencer-revises-her-age-upwards-on-her-birthday/ |url-status=live|quote=The big 5-0. She is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Octavia Spencer|several accolades]], including an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]], a [[Golden Globe Award]], and three [[Screen Actors Guild Awards]]. She is one of two women of color to have received three Oscar nominations and the first black actress to receive two consecutive nominations.<ref name="ew"/>

Spencer made her film debut in the 1996 drama ''[[A Time to Kill (1996 film)|A Time to Kill]]''. Following a decade of brief roles in film and television, her breakthrough came in 2011, when she played a maid in 1960s America in ''[[The Help (film)|The Help]]'', for which she won several awards, including the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]]. For her performance in [[Ryan Coogler]]'s biopic ''[[Fruitvale Station]]'' (2013), she won the [[National Board of Review Award for Best Supporting Actress]].

Spencer received further Academy Award nominations for Best Supporting Actress for playing other women in 1960s America, the mathematician [[Dorothy Vaughan]] in the biographical drama ''[[Hidden Figures]]'' (2016), and a cleaning woman in the fantasy drama ''[[The Shape of Water]]'' (2017). She also appeared in the films ''[[Smashed (film)|Smashed]]'' (2012), ''[[Snowpiercer]]'' (2013), ''[[Get on Up (film)|Get on Up]]'' (2014), ''[[The Divergent Series]]'' (2015{{ndash}}16), ''[[Gifted (2017 film)|Gifted]]'' (2017), ''[[Instant Family]]'' (2018), and ''[[Onward (film)|Onward]]'' (2020); and had starring roles in ''[[The Shack (2017 film)|The Shack]]'' (2017), ''[[Luce (film)|Luce]]'' (2019), and ''[[Ma (film)|Ma]]'' (2019).

Spencer also had a recurring role in the [[CBS]] sitcom ''[[Mom (TV series)|Mom]]'' (2013{{En dash}}15) and a starring role in the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] teen drama series ''[[Red Band Society]]'' (2014{{En dash}}15). She portrays the lead role in the [[Apple TV+]] drama series ''[[Truth Be Told (2019 TV series)|Truth Be Told]]'' (2019–present), and received a nomination for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie]] for her portrayal [[Madam C. J. Walker]] in the [[Netflix]] limited series ''[[Self Made (TV series)|Self Made]]'' (2020). As an author, Spencer created the children's book series ''Randi Rhodes, Ninja Detective''. She has published two books in the series, titled ''The Case of the Time-Capsule Bandit'' (2013) and ''The Sweetest Heist in History'' (2015).<ref>{{cite news |title=Cover reveal: Octavia Spencer's first book, 'Randi Rhodes, Ninja Detective' |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/books/2013/01/16/octavia-spencer-book-cover-reveal/1841193/ |first=Lindsay |last=Deutsch |date=January 16, 2013 |newspaper=[[USA Today]] |accessdate=February 21, 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
===1996–2009===
Spencer worked as an intern on the set of ''[[The Long Walk Home]],'' a film starring [[Whoopi Goldberg]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Dreier |first=Peter |title=Will The Help's Oscar Revive Interest in The Long Walk Home? |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/peter-dreier/oscars-2012-the-help_b_1303171.html |website=Huffington Post |accessdate=May 25, 2012 |date=February 27, 2012}}</ref> In 1997, she moved to Los Angeles on the advice of her friend [[Tate Taylor]], the future director of ''[[The Help (film)|The Help]]'', in which Spencer would later star.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/06/movies/octavia-spencer-hidden-figures.html |title=Octavia Spencer Cracks a Few Hollywood Equations |last=Onstad |first=Katrina |date=November 3, 2016 |newspaper=The New York Times |access-date=April 3, 2017 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

Spencer made her film debut as a nurse in [[Joel Schumacher]]'s ''[[A Time to Kill (1996 film)|A Time to Kill]],'' based on the book by [[John Grisham]]. She was originally hired to work on casting, but asked Schumacher if she could audition for a part.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allbusiness.com/services/amusement-recreation-services/4725950-1.html |title=Octavia Spencer: The Quip Queen |first=Jenelle |last=Riley |date=May 4, 2005 |website=AllBusiness.com |accessdate=August 8, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071116083658/http://www.allbusiness.com/services/amusement-recreation-services/4725950-1.html |archive-date=November 16, 2007}}</ref> Other film credits include: ''[[Never Been Kissed]],'' ''[[Big Momma's House]]'', ''[[Bad Santa]]'', ''[[Spider-Man (2002 film)|Spider-Man]]'', ''[[Coach Carter]]'', ''[[Win a Date with Tad Hamilton!]]'' and ''Pretty Ugly People''. She has made a number of guest appearances on television series, including ''[[Raising the Bar (2008 TV series)|Raising the Bar]]'', ''[[CSI: Crime Scene Investigation]]'', ''[[The Big Bang Theory]]'', ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place]]'', ''[[Grounded for Life]]'', ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'', ''[[Titus (TV series)|Titus]]'', ''[[Becker (TV series)|Becker]]'', ''[[30 Rock (TV series)|30 Rock]]'' and ''[[Dharma & Greg]],'' plus a recurring role on the sitcom ''[[Mom (TV series)|Mom]]''. She is best known for her starring roles as Serenity Johnson on [[Comedy Central]]'s ''[[Halfway Home (TV series)|Halfway Home]],'' and Constance Grady, the amorous INS caseworker on ''[[Ugly Betty]]''.

In 2003, Spencer made her stage debut in Los Angeles, in [[Del Shores]]' play, ''The Trials and Tribulations of a Trailer Trash Housewife,'' starring opposite veteran actress [[Beth Grant]]. It was her first and only play, as, she once explained, she suffers from what she called "intense [[stage fright]]".<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.backstage.com/bso/news-and-features-features/how-octavia-spencer-landed-the-role-of-a-1005312262.story |title=How Octavia Spencer Landed the Role of a Lifetime |first=Jenelle |last=Riley |magazine=[[Backstage (magazine)|Backstage]] |date=August 8, 2011 |accessdate=August 8, 2011 |url-status=dead |archivedate=March 16, 2016 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160316131953/http://www.backstage.com/interview/how-octavia-spencer-landed-the-role-of-a-lifetime/}}</ref> Later that year, she starred opposite [[Allison Janney]] in [[Tate Taylor]]'s short feature ''Chicken Party.''

In 2008, Spencer's brief appearance in ''[[Seven Pounds]]'' as Kate, [[Rosario Dawson]]'s home care nurse, garnered her high praise and media attention.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://theenvelope.latimes.com/galleries/la-en-smallroles4-2009feb04,0,5692496.htmlstory |title=Big impressions in brief roles |first=Lisa |last=Rosen |date=February 4, 2009 |newspaper=Los Angeles Times |accessdate=June 27, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090207025132/http://theenvelope.latimes.com/galleries/la-en-smallroles4-2009feb04,0,5692496.htmlstory |archive-date=February 7, 2009}}</ref> In April 2009, ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' listed Spencer as among its "25 Funniest Actresses in Hollywood."<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20271251,00.html#20605962 |title=The 25 Funniest Actresses in Hollywood: Octavia Spencer |magazine=Entertainment Weekly |date=April 13, 2009 |accessdate=June 27, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090413043203/http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20271251_9,00.html |archive-date=April 13, 2009}}</ref>

In August 2009, Spencer appeared in [[Rob Zombie]]'s ''[[Halloween II (2009 film)|Halloween II]].'' She also had a role in the American remake of the Danish classic ''[[Love at First Hiccup]],'' opposite Scout Taylor-Compton. Spencer starred in the feature film ''Herpes Boy,'' alongside Beth Grant, [[Ahna O'Reilly]] and Byron Lane. She played the voice of "Minny" on the audio version of novel ''The Help,'' by Kathryn Stockett. Later that year, Spencer's short film ''The Captain'' was honored by the CICFF as a finalist for the REEL Poetry Award.

===2010–2019===
In August 2010, Spencer joined [[Viola Davis]], [[Emma Stone]] and [[Bryce Dallas Howard]] in the period drama film ''[[The Help (film)|The Help]],'' an adaptation of the novel of the same name. She played the feisty and unflappable maid Minny Jackson. The film was written, produced and directed by [[Tate Taylor]], and produced by Brunson Green, [[Chris Columbus (filmmaker)|Chris Columbus]], [[Michael Barnathan]], and Mark Radcliffe. She won the [[69th Golden Globe Awards|2012 Golden Globe Award]] for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture]] for her work in ''The Help.''<ref name=ivillage>{{cite web |url=http://www.ivillage.com/emma-stone-viola-davis-octavia-spencer-oscar-nods-help/1-a-373040 |title=Could Viola Davis, Emma Stone & Octavia Spencer Get Oscar Nods for 'The Help'?  |last=O'Malley |first=Sheila |date=August 15, 2011 |website=[[iVillage]] |access-date=January 27, 2019 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110928110643/http://www.ivillage.com/emma-stone-viola-davis-octavia-spencer-oscar-nods-help/1-a-373040 |archive-date=September 28, 2011}}</ref> On February 12, 2012, Spencer won a [[BAFTA]] for Best Supporting Actress for her Performance in ''The Help'', and on February 26 she won the [[Academy Award]] for Best Supporting Actress for the same performance; it was her first Oscar nomination and first win. Spencer was given a standing ovation at the ceremony, and was moved to tears during her acceptance speech. In June 2012, Spencer was invited to join the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Thompson |first=Arienne |title=McConaughey, Spencer invited to join Academy |url=http://content.usatoday.com/communities/entertainment/post/2012/06/mcconaughey-chastain-invited-to-join-academy-/1#.UemMNtJM-So |newspaper=[[USA Today]] |accessdate=July 19, 2013 |date=June 29, 2012}}</ref>

The film was released in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |title=Tate Taylor Reunites With ‘The Help’s Viola Davis, Octavia Spencer On James Brown Pic |url=https://www.deadline.com/2013/09/tate-taylor-reunites-with-the-helps-viola-davis-octavia-spencer-on-james-brown-pic/ |first=Mike |last=Fleming Jr |date=September 30, 2013 |website=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |accessdate=October 23, 2013}}</ref> From September 2014 until February 2015, she starred in [[Steven Spielberg]]'s [[Fox Television Network|Fox]] drama television series ''[[Red Band Society]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Red Band Society Stars Octavia Spencer and Dave Annable on Looking for Life and Laughs in Sickness and Tragedy |url=http://www.eonline.com/news/575129/red-band-society-stars-octavia-spencer-and-dave-annable-on-looking-for-life-and-laughs-in-sickness-and-tragedy |first=Chris |last=Harnick |date=September 17, 2014 |website=[[E! Spencer co-starred alongside [[Kevin Costner]] in the drama film ''[[Black or White (film)|Black or White]]'' (2014)<ref>{{cite web |title=Octavia Spencer Will Battle Kevin Costner For Custody In Drama 'Black And White' |url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/octavia-spencer-will-battle-kevin-costner-for-custody-in-drama-black-and-white |first=Tambay A. |last=Obenson |date=May 8, 2013  |website=[[IndieWire]] |accessdate=July 18, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130525175733/http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/octavia-spencer-will-battle-kevin-costner-for-custody-in-drama-black-and-white |archive-date=May 25, 2013}}</ref> and co-starred as Johanna Reyes in the second installment of the ''Divergent'' series, ''[[The Divergent Series: Insurgent]]'' (2015).<ref>{{cite web |title=Casting News: Octavia Spencer To Play Johanna In 'Insurgent' |url=http://www.pagetopremiere.com/2014/05/casting-news-octavia-spencer-to-play-johanna-in-insurgent/ |first=Yaunna |last=Sommersby |date=May 12, 2014 |website=PageToPremiere.com |accessdate=September 18, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141113140709/http://www.pagetopremiere.com/2014/05/casting-news-octavia-spencer-to-play-johanna-in-insurgent/ |archivedate=November 13, 2014 |df=mdy-all}}</ref> She reprised the role in ''[[The Divergent Series: Allegiant]]'' (2016). She voiced Mrs. Otterton in Disney's ''[[Zootopia]]'', which marked her animated film debut.

In 2016, she starred alongside [[Taraji P. Henson]] and [[Janelle Monáe]] in ''[[Hidden Figures]],'' a film about African-American mathematicians at NASA who were critical to its success in the 1960s in the [[Space Race]], and who each had careers there. She played mathematician and human computer [[Dorothy Vaughan]]. The role garnered her a [[Golden Globe Award]] and [[Screen Actors Guild Award]] for her performance and earned Spencer a second nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]], making her the first black actress to follow up an Oscar victory with another nomination, having previously won for ''The Help''.<ref name="ew">{{cite magazine |url=https://ew.com/awards/2018/01/23/oscars-octavia-spencer-makes-history-shape-of-water-nomination/ |title=Oscars: Octavia Spencer makes history with The Shape of Water nomination |first=Joey |last=Nolfi |date=January 23, 2018 |magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |accessdate=January 23, 2018}}</ref> In honor of [[Martin Luther King Jr. Day]], Spencer bought out a Los Angeles screening of ''Hidden Figures'' to treat low-income families that would have been unable to afford to see the film otherwise.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.etonline.com/movies/207658_hidden_figures_stars_share_the_love_after_another_big_box_office_weekend/ |title='Hidden Figures' Stars Octavia Spencer & Taraji P. In 2017, Spencer co-starred with [[Sally Hawkins]] in [[Guillermo del Toro]]'s  [[dark fantasy]] [[Drama (film and television)|drama film]] ''[[The Shape of Water]]'', which follows a mute custodian at a high-security government laboratory who befriends a captured humanoid-[[amphibian]] creature. The film was screened in the main competition section of the [[74th Venice International Film Festival]], where it premiered to positive reviews on August 31, 2017,<ref name="InComp">{{cite web |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/venice-film-festival-unveils-lineup-74th-edition-1024415 |title=Venice Competition Includes Films From George Clooney, Guillermo del Toro, Darren Aronofsky |last=Anderson |first=Ariston |date=July 27, 2017 |website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |access-date=July 27, 2017}}</ref> and was awarded the [[Golden Lion]] for best film in the competition.<ref>{{cite news |title=Guillermo del Toro's 'The Shape of Water' wins Venice Golden Lion |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2017/sep/09/guillermo-del-toro-the-shape-of-water-golden-lion-venice-2017 |date=September 9, 2017 |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |accessdate=September 9, 2017}}</ref> It also screened at the [[2017 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref name="TIFF">{{cite web |url=https://deadline.com/2017/07/toronto-film-festival-2017-margot-robbie-gary-oldman-jake-gyllenhaal-emma-stone-christian-bale-1202135285/ |title=Toronto Film Festival 2017 Unveils Strong Slate |first=Mike |last=Fleming Jr |date=July 25, 2017 |website=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |publisher=[[Penske Media Corporation|Penske Business Media, LLC]] |accessdate=July 25, 2017}}</ref> Spencer earned Golden Globe and BAFTA Award nominations for her portrayal, while receiving her third [[Academy Award]] nomination in the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Best Supporting Actress]] category, making her the first black actress to receive two consecutive Oscar nods in back-to-back years as well as the most nominated black actress to date, along with ''The Help'' co-star Viola Davis.<ref name="ew"/>

In 2018, Spencer has starring roles as a preschool principal in the drama film ''[[A Kid Like Jake]]'' and a social worker in the comedy film ''[[Instant Family]]''. She also executive produced the biographical comedy-drama film ''[[Green Book (film)|Green Book]]'', which went on to receive the [[Academy Award for Best Picture]].

In 2019, she starred as Harriet Wilson, a history teacher who makes an alarming discovery about an all-star student, in the drama film ''[[Luce (film)|Luce]]''. The film premiered at the [[2019 Sundance Film Festival]] and earned critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/luce#contentReviews|title=Luce (2019)|website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=October 10, 2019}}</ref> She then received praise for her starring role as Sue Ann "Ma" Ellington in the psychological horror film ''[[Ma (film)|Ma]]'', which was directed by frequent collaborator Tate Taylor. For the latter, she received a nomination for the [[Saturn Award for Best Actress]]. Also in 2019, Spencer returned to television by executive producing and starring in the [[Apple TV+]] drama series ''[[Truth Be Told (2019 TV series)|Truth Be Told]]''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |url=https://deadline.com/2018/05/are-you-sleeping-drama-octavia-spencer-star-apple-series-order-reese-witherspoon-peter-chernin-1202381254/ |title='Are You Sleeping' Drama Starring Octavia Spencer Gets Apple Series Order; Reese Witherspoon & Peter Chernin Producing |last=Andreeva |first=Nellie |date=May 2, 2018 |website=Deadline Hollywood |access-date=January 12, 2019}}</ref>

===2020–present===
In January 2020, Spencer had a voice role as Dab-Dab, an enthusiastic duck with a metal leg, in the fantasy comedy film ''[[Dolittle (film)|Dolittle]]'', starring [[Robert Downey Jr.]] as the [[Doctor Dolittle|titular character]]. She also voiced a [[manticore]] restaurant owner in the [[Pixar]] animated fantasy film ''[[Onward (film)|Onward]]'', which was released in March 2020 to positive reviews.

Spencer executive produced and starred as [[Madam C. J. Walker]] in the [[Netflix]] biographical limited series ''[[Self Made (TV series)|Self Made]]'', which was released on March 20, 2020.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |url=https://deadline.com/2018/07/netflix-orders-madam-c-j-walker-limited-series-starring-octavia-spencer-produced-by-lebron-james-tca-1202435971/ |title=Netflix Orders 'Madam C.J. ==Awards and nominations==
{{main|List of awards and nominations received by Octavia Spencer}}

Spencer has received three nominations for the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]], including one win. She is the first black actress to receive two consecutive Academy Award nominations in back-to-back years, the first black actress to receive two Academy Award nominations after a win, and is tied with [[Viola Davis]] as the most nominated black actress to date.<ref name="ew"/>

She has also won three [[Screen Actors Guild Awards]], three [[National Board of Review|National Board of Review Awards]], two [[Satellite Award for Best Cast – Motion Picture|Satellite Awards]], two [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards]], a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Golden Globe Award]], and a [[BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role|BAFTA Award]].
