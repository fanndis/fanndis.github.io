'''Christopher James<ref name="sg-elect06"/> de Souza''' ([[Chinese language|Chinese]]: 迪舒沙, [[Tamil language|Tamil]]: கிறிஸ்தபர் டி சூசா; born 21 January 1976) is a [[Singaporean]] politician. He is currently the [[Speaker of the Parliament of Singapore|Deputy Speaker of the Parliament of Singapore]] with [[Jessica Tan]].

He has served as the  [[Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Holland–Bukit Timah Group Representation Constituency|Holland–Bukit Timah GRC]] for Ulu Pandan since May 2006 and is a practising litigation partner at the [[Singapore]]-based law firm Lee & Lee.<ref>[http://www.parliament.gov.sg/mp/christopher-de-souza?viewcv=Christopher%20De%20Souza Christopher de Souza Parliament of Singapore] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120320110956/http://www.parliament.gov.sg/mp/christopher-de-souza?viewcv=Christopher%20De%20Souza |date=2012-03-20 }}, accessed on 22 October 2015.</ref><ref>[http://www.leenlee.com.sg/our-people/partners/de-souza-christopher/ Lee & Lee - Partners - Christopher de Souza] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160530150540/http://www.leenlee.com.sg/our-people/partners/de-souza-christopher/ |date=2016-05-30 }}, accessed on 22 October 2015.</ref>

==Early life==
de Souza first served as a [[law clerk|Justice's Law Clerk]] to then [[Chief Justice of Singapore|Chief Justice]] [[Yong Pung How]], then as Deputy Public Prosecutor, followed by State Counsel, Magistrate, and finally Assistant Registrar at the [[High Court of Singapore]]. He graduated from his LLB Class at [[King's College London]] with First Class Honours in 2000 and then pursued his post-graduate studies as a Shell Scholar at the [[University of Oxford]] where he graduated with a [[Bachelor of Civil Law]] with distinction in 2001.<ref name="pap"/> During [[National service in Singapore|National Service]] in the [[Singapore Armed Forces]], he was assigned to a combat squadron and was conferred the Sword of Honour.<ref name="straits">[http://www.stomp.com.sg/elections2010/sliced/dyk_profile_Christopher.html The Straits Times - General Elections 2011]{{dead link|date=August 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}, accessed on 16 September 2011</ref> He completed his National Service as an [[Officer Cadet School (Singapore)|officer]].<ref name="leenlee">[http://www.leenlee.com.sg/en/our-people/details.html?tx_smemployeelist_pi1%5BempID%5D=177 Lee & Lee - Partners - Christopher de Souza] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150105113723/http://www.leenlee.com.sg/en/our-people/details.html?tx_smemployeelist_pi1%5BempID%5D=177 |date=2015-01-05 }}, accessed on 31 August 2015.</ref> He represented Singapore in [[hockey]] at various tournaments up to 2005.<ref name="leenlee"/>

==Political career==
de Souza is a representative of the [[People's Action Party]] and a part of the team which won the [[2011 Singapore general election|2011 general elections]] for the [[Holland–Bukit Timah GRC]] with a margin of 60.08% of the votes.<ref name="yahoo">[http://sg.news.yahoo.com/blogs/singaporescene/final-tally-votes-cast-ge2011-released-171354318.html Final tally of votes cast for GE2011 released] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110514180849/http://sg.news.yahoo.com/blogs/singaporescene/final-tally-votes-cast-ge2011-released-171354318.html |date=2011-05-14 }}, Fann Sim, SingaporeScene, Yahoo! News Singapore, 12 May 2011, accessed on 16 September 2011.</ref> He is specifically in charge of [[Ulu Pandan]] ward where he has been the Parliamentary representative since 2006.<ref name="pap">[http://ge.pap.org.sg/candidate/chrisdesouza People's Action Party - MP-Elect Profile - Christopher de Souza] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110504142005/http://ge.pap.org.sg/candidate/chrisdesouza |date=2011-05-04 }}, accessed on 16 September 2011.</ref>

He is currently serving on [[Parliament of Singapore#Government Parliamentary Committees|Government Parliamentary Committees]] on Manpower (as the Deputy Chairman), and Home Affairs and Law. In 2009, he was nominated as a Young Global Leader at the [[World Economic Forum]].<ref name="wef">[http://www.weforum.org/young-global-leaders/christopher-de-souza/index.html World Economic Forum - Christopher De Souza] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110814084632/http://www.weforum.org/young-global-leaders/christopher-de-souza/index.html |date=2011-08-14 }}, accessed on 16 September 2011.</ref>

de Souza was elected Deputy Speaker in the 14th Parliament.

==Personal life==
de Souza is a [[Roman Catholic]] and is married.
