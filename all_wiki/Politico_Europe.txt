'''''Politico Europe''''' (stylized as '''''POLITICO Europe''''') is the European edition of the American news organization ''[[Politico]]'' reporting and supporting the [[European Union]]. Its headquarters are located in [[Brussels]] with additional offices in London, Berlin, Warsaw, Paris, and Frankfurt.<ref name="auto">{{Cite news|url=https://digiday.com/media/politico-sets-sights-london-growth-playbook-extension/|title=Politico is expanding Playbook to London - Digiday|date=2017-06-02|work=Digiday|access-date=2018-10-01|language=en-US}}</ref>
 
In September 2014, ''Politico'' formed a joint venture with German publisher [[Axel Springer SE]] to launch its European edition.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://money.cnn.com/2014/09/09/media/politico-europe-expansion/|title=Politico's next battleground: Europe|work=CNN|author=Pallota, Frank|date=September 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/politico-axel-springer-to-start-european-news-venture-1410287813|title=Politico, Axel Springer to Start European News Venture|last=Hagey|first=Keach|date=2014-09-09|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2018-09-04|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> In December 2014, the joint venture announced its acquisition of ''Development Institute International'', a leading French conference business, and ''European Voice'', a European political newspaper previously part of the [[Economist Group]], to be relaunched under the ''Politico'' brand.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/politico-axel-springer-to-buy-european-voice-1418213001|title=Politico, Axel Springer Buy European Voice|work=The Wall Street Journal|author=Emmerentze Jervell, Ellen|date=December 10, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/3c5ad074-805e-11e4-9907-00144feabdc0|title=Subscribe to read|website=Financial Times|language=en-GB|access-date=2018-09-04}}</ref> Among the participants of the launch event on April 21, 2015, was [[President of the European Council]] [[Donald Tusk]] and then [[President of the European Parliament]] [[Martin Schulz]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/politico-the-us-news-site-launches-in-europe-10210972.html|title=Politico: the US news site launches in Europe|work=Evening Standard|author=Jarvis, Alice-Azania|date=April 30, 2015}}</ref>

''Politico Europe'' debuted with its first print issue two days later, on April 23, 2015.<ref>{{cite web |website=Professional.co.uk|url= http://edition.pagesuite-professional.co.uk/Launch.aspx?PBID=926f0c8c-7f9b-4c0c-a74d-25af040856bf |title= ''Politico Europe''}}</ref>

The main sources of revenue are advertising, event sponsorship and paid subscriptions with nearly half coming from the [[Subscription business model|subscription business]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://digiday.com/media/half-politico-europes-revenues-come-paid-subscriptions/|title=Half of Politico Europe's revenues come from paid subscriptions|date=2018-04-12|work=[[Digiday]]|access-date=2018-05-10|language=en-US}}</ref>

In June 2018, the second year in a row, the annual [[ComRes]]/[[Burson-Marsteller]] survey among EU experts named ''Politico Europe'' as the most influential publication on European affairs. Despite its relative newness to the Brussels media landscape, ''Politico Europe'' has ranked above established publications such as the ''[[Financial Times]]'', ''[[The Economist]]'', [[BBC]] and the ''[[The Wall Street Journal|Wall Street Journal]]'', as well as [[Twitter]] and [[Facebook]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018|title=2018 EU Media Survey|url=http://www.comresglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Final_Presentation_BM.pdf|publisher=ComRes/Burson·Marsteller|language=en}}</ref>

== ''Politico Pro'' ==
''Politico Pro'' is a premium politics and policy news service that launched in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pressgazette.co.uk/politico-introduces-paid-professional-subscription-services-europe-five-months-after-launch/|title=Politico introduces paid-for 'professional' subscription services to Europe five months after launch – Press Gazette|website=www.pressgazette.co.uk|language=en-US|access-date=2018-09-04}}</ref> The newsletter based subscriptions cover different policy areas such as Agriculture and Food, Financial Services, Healthcare, Trade, Energy and Climate, Technology and Transportation. There are also products that are less industry focused but instead provide information on a certain topic like [[Brexit]], [[sustainability]], [[data]] and [[digitization]], the [[Budget of the European Union|EU Budget]] or competition. In April 2018, ''Politico Europe'' added DataPoint, a research platform that gives subscribers access to presentations and analysis about topics from various policy areas, to its subscription portfolio.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://digiday.com/media/half-politico-europes-revenues-come-paid-subscriptions/|title=Half of Politico Europe's revenues come from paid subscriptions - Digiday|date=2018-04-12|work=Digiday|access-date=2018-09-04|language=en-US}}</ref> ''Politico Pro''’s paid offerings reach 45.000 subscribers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lecho.be/actualite/archive/politico-se-renforce-en-europe-via-une-acquisition/10027916.html|title=Politico se renforce en Europe via une acquisition|website=www.lecho.be|language=fr|access-date=2018-09-05}}</ref> Subscription prices start at 7000 euros a year, but can also be in the high five-digit range.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sueddeutsche.de/medien/online-magazin-alles-fuer-die-blase-1.3935976|title=Alles für die Blase|website=www.sueddeutsche.de|language=de|access-date=2018-09-05}}</ref>

In July 2018, ''Politico Europe'' announced the acquisition of technology from Statehill Inc, and the subsequent launch of a data-driven information platform, Pro Intelligence.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lecho.be/actualite/archive/politico-se-renforce-en-europe-via-une-acquisition/10027916.html|title=Politico se renforce en Europe via une acquisition|date=2018-07-03|work=L'Echo|access-date=2018-10-01|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.journalism.co.uk/news/politico-europe-launches-pro-intelligence-platform-to-empower-reporting-on-eu-policies/s2/a729100/|title=POLITICO Europe launches Pro Intelligence platform to empower reporting on EU policies|date=2018-10-17|access-date=2018-10-29}}</ref>

== People ==
Stephen Brown was named editor-in-chief in September 2019, having already taken over as executive editor from [[Matthew Kaminski]] who is now ''Politico''’s editor-in-chief, based in Washington.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.politico.eu/politico-europe-names-stephen-brown-editor-in-chief/|title=POLITICO EUROPE NAMES STEPHEN BROWN EDITOR IN CHIEF|website=POLITICO|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.turi2.de/aktuell/springer-macht-stephen-brown-zum-europaeischen-politico-chef/|title=Springer macht Stephen Brown zum europäischen Politico-Redaktionschef.|website=turi2|language=de-DE|access-date=2018-12-10}}</ref> [[Shéhérazade Semsar de Boisséson|Shéhérazade Semsar de-Boisséson]] is the CEO.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2015/jun/18/can-politico-make-brussels-sexy|title=Can Politico make Brussels sexy? {{!}} Gideon Lewis-Kraus|last=Lewis-Kraus|first=Gideon|date=2015-06-18|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=2018-10-01}}</ref>

In March 2018, Florian Eder took over the flagship morning newsletter ''Brussels Playbook'' from Ryan Heath, who authored the ''Brussels Playbook'' for three years.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.politico.eu/newsletter/brussels-playbook/politico-brussels-playbook-presented-by-epp-group-selmayr-day-in-strasbourg-euro-future-new-old-national-front/|title=POLITICO Brussels Playbook, presented by EPP Group: Selmayr day in Strasbourg — Euro future — New old National Front|date=2018-03-12|work=Politico|access-date=2018-10-01|language=en-US}}</ref> In September 2017, ''Politico Europe'' launched the ''London Playbook'' morning newsletter with Jack Blanchard as the lead writer.<ref name="auto"/> ''Brussels Playbook'' has now around 90.000 subscribers, ''London Playbook'' has 40.000 subscribers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lecho.be/entreprises/media-marketing/politico-prevoit-le-break-even-cette-annee/10153301.html|title=Politico prévoit le break-even cette année - L'Echos|date=2019-08-13|work=L'Echo|language=Fr}}</ref>

== Profile ==
The majority of the publication's articles cover the day-to-day business of the [[European Commission]], the [[European Parliament]], the [[Council of the European Union]] and the EU's interactions in domestic and international affairs. ''Politico Europe'' also publishes profiles of important or influential public figures within the European Union.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2015/jun/18/can-politico-make-brussels-sexy|title=Can Politico make Brussels sexy? {{!}} Gideon Lewis-Kraus|last=Lewis-Kraus|first=Gideon|date=2015-06-18|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=2018-10-02}}</ref> ''Politico Europe'' also organizes and hosts EU-related conferences, seminars, and debates.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sueddeutsche.de/medien/online-magazin-alles-fuer-die-blase-1.3935976|title=Alles für die Blase|last=Mühlauer|first=Alexander|date=2018-04-08|work=sueddeutsche.de|access-date=2018-12-12|language=de|issn=0174-4917}}</ref>
