'''Joan Biskupic''' ({{lang-hr|Biskupić}}; born {{Circa| 1956|lk=yes}}<ref>{{cite web|title=Talent Magnet|url=https://ajrarchive.org/Article.asp?id=1015&id=1015|publisher=[[American Journalism Review]]|date=June 2000|quote=Joan Biskupic, the Post's 44-year-old U.S. Supreme Court reporter|accessdate=February 26, 2010}}</ref> is an American journalist, author, and lawyer who has covered the [[Supreme Court of the United States|United States Supreme Court]] since 1989. She is a full time Supreme Court analyst at CNN.<ref name="CNN">{{cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/profiles/joan-biskupic|title=Joan Biskupic profile|publisher=[[CNN]]|accessdate=October 9, 2018}}</ref> She was previously Editor in Charge, Legal Affairs for ''[[Reuters]]'' from 2012 to 2016. For the 2016–17 academic year, she was a visiting professor at the [[University of California, Irvine]]'s School of Law. From 2000 to 2012 she was the Legal Affairs Correspondent for ''[[USA Today]]''.{{citation needed|date=December 2016}}

From 1992 to 2000, she was the Supreme Court reporter for ''[[The Washington Post]]'', and from 1989 to 1992 she was a legal affairs writer for ''[[Congressional Quarterly]]''.<ref name="pbs">{{cite web|url=https://www.pbs.org/weta/washingtonweek/panelist/Joan-Biskupic|title=Joan Biskupic profile|publisher=[[Public Broadcasting Service]]|accessdate=February 25, 2010}}</ref><ref name="usa-today">{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/opinion/bios/biskupic.htm|title=Joan Biskupic profile|work=USA Today|accessdate=February 5, 2010|date=July 24, 2000}}</ref> Her work was a finalist for the [[Pulitzer Prize for Explanatory Reporting]] in 2015. She was awarded the 1991 [[Everett McKinley Dirksen]] Award for Distinguished Reporting on Congress for her coverage of the [[Clarence Thomas Supreme Court nomination|Clarence Thomas hearings]] for ''Congressional Quarterly''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalpress.org/info-url3520/info-url_show.htm?doc_id=118438 |title=Everett McKinley Dirksen Awards for Distinguished Reporting of Congress |publisher=[[National Press Foundation]] |accessdate=February 26, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090127031453/http://www.nationalpress.org/info-url3520/info-url_show.htm?doc_id=118438 |archivedate=January 27, 2009 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wilsoncenter.org/index.cfm?fuseaction=news.item&news_id=63772|title=Sandra Day O'Connor and the Supreme Court|first=Peter|last=Bean|date=March 15, 2004|publisher=[[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]]|accessdate=February 26, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110605062436/http://www.wilsoncenter.org/index.cfm?fuseaction=news.item&news_id=63772|archive-date=June 5, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> Prior to that, she covered government and politics for the ''[[Milwaukee Journal]]''<ref name="MilwaukeeJournal">{{cite news|title= Making case for case-by-case justice|first=Biskupic|last=Joan|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1683&dat=19960911&id=1kscAAAAIBAJ&pg=6789,197655|newspaper=Milwaukee Journal Sentinel|date=September 11, 1996|page=3E|accessdate=February 26, 2010|quote=Joan Biskupic, a former Milwaukee Journal reporter, is the Supreme Court reporter for ''The Washington Post''}}</ref> and the ''[[Tulsa Tribune]]''.{{citation needed|date=December 2016}}

Biskupic has written a number of books on the Supreme Court, including biographies of Supreme Court Justices [[Sandra Day O'Connor]], [[Antonin Scalia]], and [[Sonia Sotomayor]], and Chief Justice [[John Roberts]]. She was awarded three residential fellowships at the [[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]], in 2003, 2004 and 2008, for work on these biographies.<ref name="jb-education"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wilsoncenter.org/index.cfm?fuseaction=sf.profile&person_id=61029|title=Joan Biskupic, Woodrow Wilson Center Public Policy Scholar|publisher=[[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]]|accessdate=February 26, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110605062452/http://www.wilsoncenter.org/index.cfm?fuseaction=sf.profile&person_id=61029|archive-date=June 5, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wilsoncenter.org/about/WWC_AR0708.pdf|title=Annual Report October 1, 2007–September 30, 2008|publisher=[[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]]|accessdate=February 26, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100709203951/http://www.wilsoncenter.org/about/WWC_AR0708.pdf|archive-date=July 9, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> She is also a commentator who appears regularly on television and radio programs. She is a regular panelist on ''[[Washington Week]]'' and has appeared on ''[[The Diane Rehm Show]]'', ''[[Today (NBC program)|The Today Show]]'', ''[[Good Morning America]]'', ''[[Nightline (U.S. news program)|Nightline]]'', ''[[Face the Nation]]'', and Stay Tuned with [[Preet Bharara|Preet]].<ref name="jb-journalism">{{cite web|url=http://joanbiskupic.com/biskupic-journalism.htm|work=joanbiskupic.com|title=About Joan Biskupik (sic)|accessdate=February 25, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101015143451/http://www.joanbiskupic.com/biskupic-journalism.htm|archive-date=October 15, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Education==
Biskupic is one of nine siblings born to a  Catholic family of [[Croatian American|Croatian]] and [[Irish American|Irish]] descent. She received her high school diploma from [[Benet Academy]],<ref>{{Cite news|last=Broz|first=Joan|title=Former students fondly remember 'tough' Mr. White|newspaper=The Daily Herald|date=September 22, 2008|url=https://www.dailyherald.com/story/?id=236151|accessdate=August 24, 2010}}</ref> a B.A. degree in Journalism from [[Marquette University]], an [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] degree in English from the [[University of Oklahoma]], and a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] degree from [[Georgetown University Law Center]]. One of her brothers, [[Steven M. Biskupic]] (born 1961), is a former U.S. Attorney for the Eastern District of Wisconsin, now in private practice. Another brother, Vince Biskupic, is a judge on the [[Outagamie County, Wisconsin|Outagamie Circuit Court]].

She has been a member of the [[District of Columbia]] bar since 1994.<ref name="jb-education">{{cite web|url=http://joanbiskupic.com/biskupic-education.htm|work=joanbiskupic.com|title=About Joan Biskupik (sic)|accessdate=February 25, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101014101644/http://www.joanbiskupic.com/biskupic-education.htm|archive-date=October 14, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> Biskupic received Honorary Doctors of Laws from [[Georgetown University]] in May 2014<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.georgetown.edu/hoyas2014/speakers.html |title=Georgetown Announces Speakers for 2014 Commencement |work=Georgetown University |accessdate=May 1, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140502033259/http://www.georgetown.edu/hoyas2014/speakers.html |archivedate=May 2, 2014 }}</ref> and [[Marquette University]] in May 2010.{{Citation needed|date=May 2014}}
