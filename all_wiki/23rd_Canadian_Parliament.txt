The '''23rd Canadian Parliament''' was in session from October 14, 1957, until February 1, 1958. The membership was set by the [[1957 Canadian federal election|1957 federal election]] on June 10, 1957, and it changed only somewhat due to resignations and [[by-elections]] until it was dissolved prior to the [[1958 Canadian federal election|1958 election]].

It was the only parliament formally opened by Queen [[Elizabeth II]] herself, rather than her formal representative, the [[Governor General of Canada|Governor General]].

It was controlled by a [[Progressive Conservative Party of Canada|Progressive Conservative Party]] minority under [[Prime Minister of Canada|Prime Minister]] [[John Diefenbaker]] and the [[18th Canadian Ministry]].  The [[Official Opposition (Canada)|Official Opposition]] was the [[Liberal Party of Canada|Liberal Party]], led first by [[Louis St. Laurent]], and then by [[Lester B. Pearson]].

It was the second shortest parliament in Canadian history.

The [[Speaker of the House of Commons of Canada|Speaker]] was [[Roland Michener]].  See also [[List of Canadian electoral districts 1952–1966]] for a list of the ridings in this parliament.
