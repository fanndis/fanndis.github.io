{{main article|2016 French Open}}

[[Garbiñe Muguruza]] defeated defending champion [[Serena Williams]] 7–5, 6–4, to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2016 French Open]]. Williams was attempting, for the third time, to equal [[Steffi Graf]]'s [[WTA Tour records#Player totals|Open Era record]] of 22 major singles titles. She was also attempting to become only the second player, after Steffi Graf, to complete a quadruple [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Multiple Career Grand Slams|Career Grand Slam]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2016/jun/04/french-open-2016-womens-final-serena-williams-v-garbine-muguruza-live|title= Garbiñe Muguruza beats Serena Williams to win French Open title – as it happened|date=4 June 2016|work=Guardian|accessdate=7 June 2016}}</ref>	
Muguruza became just the second Spanish woman in history, after three-time champion [[Arantxa Sánchez Vicario]], to win the French Open.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/36452385|title= French Open 2016: Garbine Muguruza upsets Serena Williams to win title|date=4 June 2016|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=7 June 2016}}</ref> Muguruza dropped only one set the entire tournament, to [[Anna Karolína Schmiedlová]] in the first round.

Williams retained the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|WTA no. 1 singles ranking]] after [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] and [[Angelique Kerber]] lost in fourth and first rounds, respectively.

[[Kiki Bertens]] became the first Dutch woman to reach the semi-final of a Grand Slam event since [[Betty Stöve]] reached the same stage, at the [[1977 US Open (tennis)|1977 US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dutchnews.nl/news/archives/2016/06/91391-2/|title=Kiki Bertens reaches Roland Garros semi-finals}}</ref>

The tournament marked the first time that former world No. 1 [[Caroline Wozniacki]] did not play in the main draw of a Grand Slam singles event (being forced to withdraw due to an ankle injury)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2016/05/17/former-no-1-wozniacki-withdraws-from-french-open/84494342/|title=Caroline Wozniacki withdraws from French Open due to injured ankle|work=[[USA Today]]|date=17 May 2016|accessdate=25 May 2018}}</ref> since her major debut at the [[2007 French Open – Women's Singles|2007 French Open]], ending a streak of 36 consecutive Grand Slam appearances. It also marked the first French Open main draw appearance (and Latvia's first Grand Slam champion) for [[Jeļena Ostapenko]], who would go on to win the event the following year. She was beaten by [[Naomi Osaka]] in the first round.
