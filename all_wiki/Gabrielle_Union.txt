'''Gabrielle Monique Union-Wade''' (born October 29, 1972)<ref name="tvg">{{cite magazine |url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/gabrielle-union/bio/151123/ |title=Gabrielle Union Biography |work=[[TV Guide]] |accessdate=November 24, 2019 |archivedate=September 27, 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150927010434/http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/gabrielle-union/bio/151123/}}</ref><!--TV Guide citation, which also verifies middle name Monique, is live, but birthdate appears only in archived version--> is an American actress, voice artist, activist, and author. She began her career in the 1990s, appearing on television [[sitcom]]s, before landing supporting roles in teenage comedy films ''[[She's All That]]'' and ''[[10 Things I Hate About You]]'' (1999). Her breakthrough role was in the 2000 film ''[[Bring It On (film)|Bring It On]]''.

Union is known for her performances in the romantic comedy films ''[[The Brothers (2001 film)|The Brothers]]'' (2001), ''[[Deliver Us from Eva]]'' (2003), ''[[Daddy's Little Girls]]'' (2007), ''[[Think Like a Man]]'' (2012) and ''[[Think Like a Man Too]]'' (2014). She also had starring roles in the [[CBS]] medical drama series ''[[City of Angels (2000 TV series)|City of Angels]]'' (2000) and films ''[[Bad Boys II]]'' (2003), ''[[Cradle 2 the Grave]]'' (2003), ''[[Neo Ned]]'' (2005), ''[[Cadillac Records]]'' (2008), ''[[Top Five]]'' (2014) and ''[[Breaking In (2018 film)|Breaking In]]'' (2018). In 2013, Union began starring as lead character in the [[BET]] drama series ''[[Being Mary Jane]]'', for which she has received an [[NAACP Image Award]]. She co-starred in the 2016 film ''[[The Birth of a Nation (2016 film)|The Birth of a Nation]]'', and next appeared in ''[[Almost Christmas (film)|Almost Christmas]]'' (2016) and ''[[Sleepless (2017 film)|Sleepless]]'' (2017).

Outside of acting, Union has written two books: a memoir, entitled ''We're Going to Need More Wine'' (2017), and a children's book, entitled ''Welcome to the Party'' (2020).<ref>[https://www.harpercollins.com/9780062999184/welcome-to-the-party/ ''Welcome to the Party'']. HarperCollins.</ref>  She has also been an outspoken advocate for issues involving women's health and [[violence against women]]. Union was included on [[Time (magazine)|''Time'']]'s list of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gabrielle Union: The 100 Most Influential People of 2020|url=https://time.com/collection/100-most-influential-people-2020/5888441/gabrielle-union/|access-date=2020-09-23|website=Time}}</ref>

==Early life==
Union was born in [[Omaha, Nebraska]], the daughter of Theresa ({{née}} Glass), a phone company manager and social worker, and Sylvester E. Union, a military sergeant.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.notablebiographies.com/newsmakers2/2004-Q-Z/Union-Gabrielle.html |title=Gabrielle Union |website=NotableBiographies.com |accessdate=March 13, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thereader.com/comments/being_gabby/ |location=Omaha, Nebraska |title=Being Gabby |publisher=The Reader |accessdate=January 9, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160128025618/http://www.thereader.com/comments/being_gabby/ |archive-date=January 28, 2016}}</ref> She was raised [[Catholic Church|Roman Catholic]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/12/10/AR2007121002026.html |work=The Washington Post |first=Teresa |last=Wiltz |title=Gabrielle Union, Relishing Her Latest 'Holiday' Treat |date=December 11, 2007}}</ref> During her childhood, she was taught to be "an independent woman, standing on my own two feet, and that's the road I opted to take."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.voice-online.co.uk/article/gabrielle-union-opens-about-being-raped-teenager |date=February 27, 2014 |title=Gabrielle Union Opens Up About Being Raped As Teenager |first=Mary |last=Isokariari |publisher=The Voice Online}}</ref> According to Union, her mother taught her to have a "world perspective" and took her to a [[gay pride]] parade at the age of eight after the family moved to [[Pleasanton, California]],<ref name="2007TheWashingtonPost" /> where she attended [[Foothill High School (Pleasanton, California)|Foothill High School]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.nbcbayarea.com/news/local/Dwyane-Wade-Asks-Pleasantons-Gabrielle-Union-to-Keep-It-Down-148310425.html |title=Dwyane Wade to Gabrielle Union: Keep It Down |last=Farooq |first=Sajid |date=April 20, 2012 |work=NBC Bay Area |access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref>

Her parents divorced after thirty years of marriage. Union had said, "They handled their divorce and our subsequent transition into a [[blended family]] with grace, dignity and respect. They always put us first and didn't involve us. I'm lucky that I can just mirror what my parents did and always put the kids first. They're pretty awesome. I'm lucky."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://madamenoire.com/404408/gabrielle-union-opens-raped-teen-suing-payless-result/ |title=Gabrielle Union Opens Up About Suing Former Employer After Being Raped at Work |first=Jazmine Denise |work=Madame Noire |last=Rodgers |date=February 25, 2014}}</ref>

Union grew up with self-esteem issues relating to her appearance, as she believed growing up that "blonde was the ideal of beauty, and if I looked nothing like that, then I must be ugly."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/ageless-gabrielle-union-uses-her-star-power-for-good-92665931259.html |title=Ageless Gabrielle Union Uses Her Star Power for Good |website=Yahoo.com |accessdate=March 24, 2019}}</ref> On her college football memories, Union reflected, "In my family if you couldn't talk [[Nebraska Cornhuskers|Cornhusker]] football—that means knowing the black shirt defense, knowing the I-back formation—then you don't get to have an opinion. When I first toured the Nebraska campus and I saw [[Turner Gill]] walk, I freaked out. That was like the biggest star-struck moment I've probably ever had in my life. But it's because I grew up in a household that always talked specifically Cornhusker football and [[Big Eight Conference|Big 8]] sports at the time."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://espn.go.com/blog/playbook/trending/post/_/id/111/gabrielle-union-talks-dating-sports |title=Gabrielle Union talks dating, sports |date=April 17, 2012 |first=Kelley |last=Carter |website=[[ESPN]]}}</ref>

Union has said her personality as a young person was "mean" until her trainer A.J. Johnson confronted her over the behavior during a party the two were attending.<ref>{{cite news |first=Lisa |last=Capretto |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/20/gabrielle-union-oprah-mean-girl_n_3462333.html |title=Gabrielle Union Tells Oprah Why She Stopped Being A 'Mean Girl' (VIDEO) |date=June 20, 2013 |website=[[Huffington Post]]}}</ref>

In 1991, at the age of 19, Union was attacked and raped at her part-time job at a [[Payless ShoeSource|Payless]] shoe store by a robber.<ref>{{cite web |first=Melanie |last=Eversly |url=https://thegrio.com/2018/04/28/gabrielle-union-recounts-rape-experience-to-shut-down-cosby-apologists/ |title=Gabrielle Union recounts rape experience to shut down Cosby apologists |website=[[The Grio]] |date=April 28, 2018 |accessdate=May 11, 2018}}</ref> Union has stated that she would not have survived the attack had it not been for lessons she learned by watching [[Oprah Winfrey]]'s talk show.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.hollywood.com/news/celebrities/3489594/gabrielle-union-thanks-oprah-winfrey-for-rape-survival |title=Gabrielle Union Thanks Oprah Winfrey for Rape Survival |date=April 7, 2006 |website=Hollywood.com}}</ref> She later successfully sued Payless for negligence, alleging that the store failed to warn employees about the assailant, who had been positively identified robbing another Payless location prior to her rape.<ref>{{cite news |first=Nardine |last=Saad |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/la-et-entertainment-news-updates-gabrielle-union-miscarriages-rape-memoir-1507152688-htmlstory.html |title=Gabrielle Union recounts infertility and a college rape |work=[[Los Angeles Times]] |publisher=[[Tronc]] |location=Los Angeles, California |date=October 4, 2017 |accessdate=May 11, 2018}}</ref>

Union has a bachelor's degree in sociology from [[UCLA]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sun-sentinel.com/entertainment/events/sf-being-gabrielle-union-15-things-you-didnt-know-about-mrs-dwyane-wade-20140903-story.html |title=Being Gabrielle Union: 15 things you didn't know about Mrs. Dwyane Wade |last=Hood |first=Micaela |date=3 September 2014 |website=sun-sentinel.com |url-status=live |access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/celebs/a32351/reasons-you-should-love-gabrielle-union/ |title=7 Reasons You Should Love Gabrielle Union |last=Hurwitz |first=Gabrielle |date=2014-10-23 |website=Cosmopolitan |access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref>

==Career==
===Early roles===
Union started her acting career with minor roles. Her first audition was for ''[[Saved by the Bell]]''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |url=https://www.eonline.com/news/1040033/how-gabrielle-union-made-all-of-her-wildest-dreams-come-true |title=How Gabrielle Union Made All of Her Wildest Dreams Come True |last=Grossbart |first=Sarah |date=7 November 2019 |website=E! Online |url-status=live |access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> In 1997, Union appeared in the sixth-season episode of ''[[Star Trek: Deep Space Nine]]''—[[Sons and Daughters (Star Trek: Deep Space Nine)|"Sons and Daughters"]] as the [[Klingon]] N'Garen. She also appeared in ''[[Sister, Sister (TV series)|Sister, Sister]]'' as Vanessa, in ''[[Smart Guy]]'' as Denise, and in five episodes of ''[[7th Heaven (TV series)|7th Heaven]]'' as Keesha Hamilton. She also appeared on an episode of ''[[Friends (TV series)|Friends]]'', "The One with the Cheap Wedding Dress", as Kristen, a love interest to both Ross & Joey. Other earlier roles included teen movies such as ''[[10 Things I Hate About You]]'' and ''[[She's All That]]''.<ref name=":0" />

=== 2000–09 ===
[[File:GabrielleUnionFeb09 cropped.jpg|thumb|left|Union in 2009]]
Union appeared in the 2000 film ''[[Love & Basketball]]''. In the same year, she performed the role of Isis in the cheerleading movie ''[[Bring It On (film)|Bring It On]]''. ''Bring It On'' helped push Union into the mainstream.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gabrielle Union |url=http://www.bet.com/topics/g/gabrielle-union.html |publisher=BET |accessdate=February 26, 2014}}</ref> Union has said that of all her films, ''Bring It On'' gave her the "biggest boost".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://newsblaze.com/story/20071210122608tsop.nb/topstory.html |title=Gabrielle Union 'The Perfect Holiday' Interview with Kam Williams |date=December 10, 2007 |publisher=News Blaze}}</ref> It led to Union being cast in the [[CBS]] television drama ''[[City of Angels (2000 TV series)|City of Angels]]'' as Dr. Courtney Ellis. Union was featured in ''[[The Brothers (2001 film)|The Brothers]]'' and was seen as having a "beguiling sincerity, even when she's fudging the truth."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2001-03-23/entertainment/0103230336_1_brothers-gary-hardwick-marriage |title=These 'Brothers' Search for the Fast Breaks of Life |first=Michael |last=Wilmington |date=March 23, 2001 |work=Chicago Tribune}}</ref> Union was cast in her first leading role in the 2003 film ''[[Deliver Us from Eva]]''. When casting Eva, director Gary Hardwick was looking for an actress capable of instantly changing "from funny to caustic and dramatic." Hardwick had previously worked with Union in ''The Brothers'' and believed she was perfect for the role of Eva.<ref name="About.com" /> Union's role in the film was met with praise, with Dustin Putman of ''All-Reviews.com'' writing that she was "the star attraction, and the number-one reason to even consider seeing the film."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.all-reviews.com/videos-5/deliver-us-from-eva.htm |title=All-Reviews.com Movie/Video Review Deliver Us From Eva |website=All-Reviews.com |first=Dustin |last=Putman}}</ref> Union drew influence from her father for the "stern" look she had in the film, admitting that she had stolen it from him.<ref name="About.com">{{cite news |url=http://movies.about.com/library/weekly/aadeliverusfromevainta.htm |title=Gabrielle Union Talks About "Deliver Us From Eva" |website=About.com}}</ref>

In 2003, Union landed the role of the main character Mike Lowrey's girlfriend, Syd, in the film ''[[Bad Boys II]]'', a box-office success grossing more than $273{{Nbsp}}million worldwide. Union felt that she had been "blessed" with her role in the film, feeling it elevated her career.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1456163/bad-boys-2-co-star-gabrielle-union-says-flick-will-be-crazy-action/ |title='Bad Boys 2′ Co-Star Gabrielle Union Says Flick Will Be 'Crazy Action' She also had an intriguing role of Dorothy, a complex and damaged psychiatric patient, in the film Running With Scissors, in 2003. |date=July 19, 2002 |first=Ryan J |last=Downey |publisher=MTV}}</ref> The following year she appeared in ''[[Something the Lord Made]]''. Union starred in the film ''[[Breakin' All the Rules]]'' in 2004. The film was unpopular with critics.<ref>{{cite web |title=Breakin' All the Rules |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/breakin_all_the_rules/ |website=Rotten Tomatoes |accessdate=July 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Breakin' All the Rules |url=https://boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=breakinalltherules.htm |website=Box Office Mojo |accessdate=July 28, 2014}}</ref> She appeared in the 2005 film ''[[Neo Ned]]'', portraying an African-American woman with delusions that she is [[Adolf Hitler]]. She and co-star [[Jeremy Renner]] were noted by Mark Olsen of the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' as having "a strange, offbeat chemistry that drives the film".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://articles.latimes.com/2006/sep/08/entertainment/et-neo8 |title=Leads make ''Neo Ned'' engaging |date=September 8, 2006 |work=Los Angeles Times |first=Mark |last=Olsen}}</ref> She won an award for Best Actress in ''Neo Ned'' at the [[Palm Beach International Film Festival]], and the film received awards at several festivals.

[[File:GabrielleUnionMay10.jpg|thumb|Union in May 2010]]
She starred in the 2005 remake of ''[[The Honeymooners (2005 film)|The Honeymooners]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://sports.espn.go.com/espn/page3/story?page=turner/union/050613 |title=The state of a Union |date=June 16, 2005 |first=Miki |last=Turner |website=ESPN.com}}</ref> Union starred in the short-lived [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] series ''[[Night Stalker (TV series)|Night Stalker]]''. She called the series a "reworking" rather than a remake. Union admitted that at the time of getting the script she was turned off, but she became interested after reading the script at her agent's insistence. She then met with series creator [[Frank Spotnitz]] and executive producer [[Daniel Sackheim]], who told her they thought of her anytime they thought of the character.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://onlineathens.com/stories/092905/ent_20050929035.shtml |title=Gabrielle Union returns to TV in ABC's 'Night Stalker' |date=September 29, 2005 |first=Janice Rhoshalle |last=Littlejohn |publisher=Associated Press}}</ref> In 2006, she starred in the music video for [[Busta Rhymes]]' "[[I Love My Bitch]]".{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}

Union starred in the 2007 film ''[[Daddy's Little Girls]]'' by [[Tyler Perry]]. She played Julia Rossmore, a romantically challenged attorney. The role was written with her in mind.<ref name="CBSFebruary2007">{{cite news |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/gabrielle-union-a-part-all-her-own/ |title=Gabrielle Union: A Part All Her Own |publisher=CBS News |date=February 19, 2007 |first=Ellen |last=Crean}}</ref> Before working with Perry, she went to see his stage show to both understand him and his audience.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://movies.about.com/od/uniongabrielle/a/daddysgu021207.htm |title=Gabrielle Union Discusses "Tyler Perry's Daddy's Little Girls" |website=About.com |first=Rebecca |last=Murray}}</ref> She filmed ''Daddy's Little Girls'' in mid-2006 in Atlanta, Georgia.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cinemablend.com/new/Gabrielle-Union-Is-A-Daddy-s-Girl-2725.html |title=Gabrielle Union Is A Daddy's Girl |website=CinemaBlend.com |first=Lexi |last=Feinberg |date=May 23, 2006}}</ref> Union was praised for her character's portrayal and for having a "great sense of comedic timing".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cinemablend.com/reviews/Tyler-Perry-s-Daddy-s-Little-Girls-2130.html |title=Tyler Perry's Daddy's Little Girls |first=Rafe |last=Telsch |website=CinemaBlend.com}}</ref> Union's character also drew comparisons to Eva, her role in ''Deliver Us from Eva''. According to Union, Perry had specifically approached her over not turning the role into the same character.<ref name="Dating">{{cite news |url=http://www.hollywood.com/static/daddys-little-girls-star-gabrielle-union-dating-and-can-afford-her-own-diamonds |title='DADDY'S LITTLE GIRLS' STAR GABRIELLE UNION: DATING AND CAN AFFORD HER |website=Hollywood.com}}</ref> Union made an appearance in the 2007 Christmas film ''[[The Perfect Holiday]]''. Since she had no children of her own, Union drew on her familiarity with her mother and sister to portray the divorced single mother of three in the film.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://movies.about.com/od/theperfectholiday/a/perfect120307.htm |title=Gabrielle Union Talks The Perfect Holiday |first=Rebecca |last=Murray |website=Movies.about.com}}</ref> Union initially turned down the role, as she did not want to get typecast for playing a mother until she was told of other actresses that had played similar roles and still found success in their careers.<ref name="2007TheWashingtonPost">{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/12/10/AR2007121002026.html |title=Gabrielle Union, Relishing Her Latest 'Holiday' Treat |date=December 11, 2007 |work=The Washington Post |first=Teresa |last=Wiltz}}</ref>

In an interview with ''[[Art Nouveau Magazine]]'', Union complained about the lack of roles for Black actresses and actors in [[Hollywood]]: "There used to be [roles] specifically written black, if you knew [[Denzel Washington|Denzel]] was doing a movie you knew his wife, girl or love interest was going to be black [but] that's not necessarily the case anymore. You're in that room with every amazingly talented actress of every hue, and it's a dogfight, it's hard."<ref>[http://artnouveaumagazine.com/JanuaryFebruary/blacklisted.html | Art Nouveau Magazine | Art, Fashion, Music, Culture<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081121052316/http://artnouveaumagazine.com/JanuaryFebruary/blacklisted.html |date=November 21, 2008}}</ref> Moreover, although she often plays weak, insecure characters, Union believes that "Hollywood needs to recognize all shades of African-American beauty."{{citation needed|date=September 2014}}

=== 2010s ===
[[File:Gabrielle Union - Flickr - nick step (2).jpg|thumb|right|Union in September 2010]]

==Personal life==
Union met [[National Football League|NFL]] player [[Chris Howard (American football)|Chris Howard]] at a party in 1999. They married on May 5, 2001, and separated in October 2005.<ref>{{cite news |title=Gabrielle Union, Husband Split |url=https://people.com/celebrity/gabrielle-union-husband-split/ |accessdate=November 23, 2019 |work=People |date=November 1, 2005}}</ref> The divorce was finalized in 2006.{{citation needed|date=December 2013}} Union said that when she got divorced she "sort of realized that she hadn't been making sound choices which were the best for her to pursue her hopes and dreams and aspirations and passions."<ref name="aalbc.com" /> In a 2014 interview, Union stated that she may have rushed into the relationship for the wrong reasons, noting that, "In my 20s, I was all about getting the ring".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.wetpaint.com/urban/articles/2014-01-06-gabrielle-union-ex-husband-chris-howard |title=Gabrielle Union Looks Back on Her First Marriage: "I Was All About Getting the Ring" |date=January 6, 2014 |first=Lindsay |last=Dreyer |website=Wetpaint}}</ref> Union had earlier reflected that she spent much of the marriage "upset", and from the relationship she realized that the men in her life were "just human".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://hollywoodlife.com/2010/03/18/gabrielle-union-i-spent-so-much-of-my-marriage-upset/ |date=March 18, 2010 |first=Chloe |last=Melas |work=Hollywood Life |title=Gabrielle Union: 'I Spent So Much Of My Marriage Upset'}}</ref>
