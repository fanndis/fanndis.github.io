'''''Ashes and Embers''''' is a 1982 [[drama film]] directed by [[Haile Gerima]] and starring John Anderson.

==Premise==
''Ashes and Embers'' is a two-hour film about the travails of black urban life. It is the story of a moody and disillusioned black veteran of the [[Vietnam War]].<ref>Maslin, Janet, ''New York Times'', "[https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9D0CEED81038F934A25752C1A964948260Movie Review: Ashes and Embers]", November 17, 1982.</ref>

==Cast==
* John Anderson as Ned Charles
* Evelyn A. Blackwell as Grandma
* Norman Blalock as Jim
* Kathy Flewellen as Liza Jane
* Uwezo Flewellen as Kimathi
* Barry Wiggins as Randolph

Fellow filmmakers [[Billy Woodberry]] and Bernard Nicolas make brief cameo appearances in the film.

==Awards and honors==
In 1983, director Haile Gerima won the FIPRESCI Prize for Forum of New Cinema at the [[Berlin International Film Festival]] for ''Ashes and Embers''.
