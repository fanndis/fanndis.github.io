{{Politics of the Philippines}}
The '''legislative districts of Pampanga''' are the representations of the [[Provinces of the Philippines|province]] of [[Pampanga]] and the [[Cities of the Philippines#Independent cities|highly urbanized city]] of [[Angeles, Philippines|Angeles]] in the [[List of legislatures of the Philippines|various national legislatures]] of the [[Philippines]]. The province and the city are currently represented in the [[House of Representatives of the Philippines|lower house]] of the [[Congress of the Philippines]] through their [[#1st District|first]], [[#2nd District|second]], [[#3rd District|third]], and [[#4th District|fourth]] congressional districts.

== History ==
The province was divided into two [[House of Representatives of the Philippines#District representation|legislative districts]] until 1972. The province and the chartered city of Angeles were represented in the [[Interim Batasang Pambansa]] as part of [[Interim Batasang Pambansa#Regional Representatives|Region III]] from 1978 to 1984, and together elected [[Regular Batasang Pambansa#Members|four representatives]] at-large to the [[Regular Batasang Pambansa]] from 1984 to 1986. Pampanga (with the highly urbanized city of Angeles) was redistricted into four [[House of Representatives of the Philippines#District representation|congressional districts]] under the new  Constitution<ref>http://www.lawphil.net/consti/cons1987.html</ref> which took effect on February 7, 1987, and elected members to the restored House of Representatives starting [[1987 Philippine legislative election|that same year]].
