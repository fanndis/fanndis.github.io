This page provides the '''party lists''' for '''New Zealand's [[2011 New Zealand general election|2011 general election]]'''. Party lists determine (in the light of country-wide proportional voting) the appointment of list MPs under the [[Mixed-member proportional representation|mixed-member proportional]] (MMP) representation electoral system. The Electoral Commission issued a deadline of noon on 1 November for submitting party lists.<ref>[http://www.elections.org.nz/elections/2011-general-election-and-referendum/election-date-announcement-and-key-dates.html General election key dates] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111014115011/http://www.elections.org.nz/elections/2011-general-election-and-referendum/election-date-announcement-and-key-dates.html |date=14 October 2011 }}</ref>

== Successful parties ==
Parties are ordered according to their share of the vote.

=== National Party ===
The National Party released a party list of 75 candidates in early September.<ref name="Stuff.co.nz_5561512">{{cite news |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/5561512/National-announce-party-list |title=National announce party list |author=Chapman, Kate |date=4 September 2011 |work=[[Stuff.co.nz]] |accessdate=1 November 2011}}</ref> The list was revised when [[Allan Peachey]], ranked 48th, withdrew due to ill health.<ref name=retirement>{{cite web |url=http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/PO1110/S00063/allan-peachey-announces-retirement.htm |title=Allan Peachey Announces Retirement |publisher=Press Release: National Party (via Scoop.co.nz) |date=5 October 2011}}</ref> Simon O'Connor, who replaced Peachey as candidate for the Tāmaki electorate, was then added to the list, albeit in a lower position than Peachey had held.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.elections.org.nz/events/past-events-0/2011-general-election/parties-candidates-and-promoters-2011-general-election--2 | title=National Party List 2011 | publisher=[[Electoral Commission (New Zealand)|Electoral Commission]] |date=15 November 2012 | accessdate=15 February 2015}}</ref>

=== Labour Party ===
The [[New Zealand Labour Party|Labour Party]] announced a party list of 70 candidates.<ref name=labourlist>{{cite press release |title=Labour Party List 2011 |url= http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/PA1104/S00194/labour-party-list-2011.htm |publisher=[[New Zealand Labour Party]] |agency=[[Scoop (website)|Scoop]] |date=10 April 2011 |accessdate=10 April 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.elections.org.nz/events/past-events-0/2011-general-election/parties-candidates-and-promoters-2011-general-election--6 | title=Labour Party List 2011 | publisher=[[Electoral Commission (New Zealand)|Electoral Commission]] |date=15 November 2012 | accessdate=15 February 2015}}</ref>

Four sitting MPs were not placed on the list. [[Damien O'Connor]], a list MP contesting [[West Coast-Tasman]], and [[Lianne Dalziel]], MP for Christchurch East, both declined a place on the list stating they only wanted to represent the electorate they held candidacy for.<ref>http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/PA1104/S00194/labour-party-list-2011.htm</ref><ref name="NZ_Herald_10717865">{{cite news |url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10717865 |title=I'll be an electorate MP or nothing, says Dalziel |author=Claire Trevett and Derek Cheng |date=8 April 2011 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |accessdate=1 November 2011}}</ref> [[Louisa Wall]], who had twice entered Parliament as a list MP following another list member's resignation, also stood as an electorate-only candidate in [[Manurewa (New Zealand electorate)|Manurewa]]. [[Manukau East]] MP [[Ross Robertson]] has never accepted a list position.
