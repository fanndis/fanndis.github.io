'''Malala Fund''' is an international, [[non-profit organization|non-profit]] organization that advocates for [[female education|girls' education]]. It was co-founded by [[Malala Yousafzai]], the Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest [[Nobel Prize]] laureate and her father, [[Ziauddin Yousafzai|Ziauddin]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://thelede.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/04/05/malala-yousafzai-announces-grant-for-girls-education/|title = Malala Yousafzai Announces Grant for Girls' Education|date = 5 April 2013|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = The New York Times|last = Hauser|first = Christine}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = Education|date = 4 February 2013|accessdate = 29 August 2018|website = ABC News|last = Whitcraft|first = Teri}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.fastcompany.com/3050372/how-teenage-activist-malala-yousafzai-is-turning-her-fame-into-a-movement|title = How Teenage Activist Malala Yousafzai Is Turning Her Fame Into A Movement|date = 1 September 2015|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = Fast Company|last = Leber|first = Jessica}}</ref> The stated goal of the organization is to ensure 12 years of free, safe and quality [[education]] for every girl.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.theverge.com/2018/1/22/16919156/apple-malala-yousafzai-malala-fund-girls-education-tim-cook|title = Apple partners with Malala Yousafzai's Malala Fund ==History==
The first contribution to Malala Fund in 2013 came from [[Angelina Jolie]] who gave a $200,000 personal donation, which was used to fund girls' education where Malala is from in [[Pakistan]]’s [[Swat District|Swat Valley]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/04/05/angelina-jolie-malala-charity_n_3019303.html|title = Angelina Jolie Donates $200,000 To Malala Fund|date = 5 April 2013|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = Huffington Post|last = Frith|first = Maxine}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.itv.com/news/2013-04-05/malala-announces-first-grant-from-fund-set-up-in-her-name/|title = Malala announces first grant from fund set up in her name|date = 5 April 2013|accessdate = 29 August 2018|website = ITV News|last = Barker|first = Faye}}</ref>

In 2014, Malala Fund helped build an all-girls secondary school in rural Kenya<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20140705/education/Malala-takes-education-bid-to-Kenya.526423|title = Malala takes education bid to Kenya|date = 5 July 2014|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = Times of Malta|last = Smith|first = Hayden}}</ref> and provided school supplies and continued [[education in Pakistan]] for children fleeing [[War in North-West Pakistan|conflict in North Waziristan]] and [[2014 India–Pakistan floods|the floods of 2014]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://blog.malala.org/malala-fund-giving-2014-who-you-helped-support-43b8095cf22d|title = Malala Fund Giving 2014: Who You Helped Support|date = 14 January 2015|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = Malala Fund}}</ref>

In 2015, when the government of [[Sierra Leone]] closed schools due to the [[West African Ebola virus epidemic|Ebola epidemic]], Malala Fund bought radios and created classrooms for 1,200 marginalized girls to continue their education.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.npr.org/sections/goatsandsoda/2015/02/18/387027766/now-this-is-an-example-of-truly-educational-radio|title = Now This Is An Example Of Truly Educational Radio|date = 18 February 2015|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = National Public Radio|last = Poon|first = Linda}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://blog.malala.org/world-radio-day-fa0ee75f7aa1|title = World Radio Day|date = 9 February 2015|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = Malala Fund}}</ref> Building on Malala's advocacy for girls in [[Nigeria]],<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2017/07/19/malala-boko-haram/491004001/|title = Malala speaks out against Boko Haram in Nigeria|date = 19 July 2017|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = USA Today|last = Umar|first = Haruna}}</ref> Malala Fund pledged full scholarships to [[Chibok schoolgirls kidnapping|Chibok schoolgirls]] freed from the abduction by [[Boko Haram]] to complete their secondary education.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.cnn.com/2015/04/13/world/malala-nigeria-schoolgirls-abducted-boko-haram/index.html|title = Malala's letter to Nigeria's abducted schoolgirls: 'solidarity, love, and hope'|date = 13 April 2015|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = CNN|last = Levs|first = Josh}}</ref> On 12 July 2015, her 18th birthday, Malala announced funding through Malala Fund of a secondary school in the [[Bekaa Valley]], [[Lebanon]], near the Syrian border, for Syrian refugees.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.reuters.com/article/lebanon-malala/nobel-winner-malala-opens-school-for-syrian-refugees-idUSKCN0PM0L320150713|title = Nobel winner Malala opens school for Syrian refugees|date = 13 July 2015|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = Reuters|last = Westall|first = Sylvia}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.csmonitor.com/World/Global-News/2015/0713/Malala-Yousafzai-s-birthday-request-investment-in-books-not-bullets|title = Malala Yousafzai's birthday request: investment in 'books, not bullets'|date = 13 July 2015|accessdate = 29 August 2018|website = The Christian Science Monitor|last = Mendoza|first = Jessica}}</ref>

In 2016, Malala visited [[Dadaab|Dadaab Refugee Camp]] for her birthday and attended the graduation of refugee girls from a mentorship program on leadership and life skills supported by Malala Fund.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.unhcr.org/ke/1408-malala-celebrates-her-19th-birthday-with-refugees-in-dadaab.html|title = Malala Celebrates her 19th Birthday with Refugees in Dadaab|date = 12 July 2016|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = UNHCR|last = Opile|first = Caroline}}</ref> In December 2016, the [[Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation]] committed $4 million to help Malala Fund launch the Education Champion Network to support education champions in developing countries.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.gatesfoundation.org/How-We-Work/Quick-Links/Grants-Database/Grants/2016/12/OPP1166109|title = The Malala Fund&nbsp;– Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation|date = December 2016|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.malala.org/partners|title = Malala Fund Partners|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = Malala Fund}}</ref>

In 2017, Malala Fund significantly expanded investment projects that Newsweek described as, "education advocacy programs run by local people&nbsp;— the kind Yousafzai and her father led when they lived in Pakistan&nbsp;— and will disburse up to $10 million a year over the next decade."<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.newsweek.com/2017/01/20/exclusive-malala-yousafzai-interview-davos-540978.html|title = Malala Yousafzai's New Mission: Can She Still Inspire as an Adult?|date = 1 October 2017|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = Newsweek|last = Gidda|first = Mirren}}</ref> New grants included a project in [[Education in Afghanistan|Afghanistan]] to support the recruitment and training of teachers to fill spots in the country's overcrowded classrooms<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.bbc.com/news/business-40250219|title = Tough school? War, illiteracy and hope in Afghanistan|date = 14 June 2017|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = BBC|last = Hicks|first = Bill}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.theguardian.com/teacher-network/2017/oct/09/teachers-are-nation-builders-developing-countries-must-invest-in-them-properly|title = Teachers are nation-builders. Developing countries must invest in them properly|date = 9 October 2017|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = The Guardian|last = Yousafzai|first = Ziauddin}}</ref> and supporting local activists in Nigeria to campaign for increasing public education from 9 years to 12 years.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://news.un.org/en/story/2017/10/567872-interview-fighting-girls-education-un-advocate-malala-yousafzai-finds-her#.WdbwjWiCyUk|title = INTERVIEW: In fighting for girls’ education, UN advocate Malala Yousafzai finds her purpose|date = 5 October 2017|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = UN News}}</ref>

In 2018, [[Apple Inc.]] partnered with Malala Fund to fund expansion to India and Latin America and provide technology, curriculum assistance and policy research with a goal of educating more than 100,000 girls.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://time.com/5112439/apple-malala-fund/|title = Apple Is Partnering With Malala's Non-Profit to Educate More Than 100,000 Girls|date = 22 January 2018|accessdate ==Programs==
===Education Champion Network===
Malala Fund supports local advocates and programs to advance girls’ secondary education around the world.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.refinery29.com/2017/05/153232/malala-yousafzai-exclusive-refugee-interview-college-acceptance|title = What Happens When The World's Most Famous Teen Activist Grows Up?|date = 9 May 2017|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = Refinery29|last = Van Oot|first = Torey}}</ref> The current priority countries for Malala Fund are [[Afghanistan]], [[Brazil]], [[Ethiopia]], [[India]], [[Lebanon]], [[Nigeria]], [[Pakistan]] and [[Turkey]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malala.org/gulmakai-network|title=Education Champion Network {{!}} Malala Fund|website=www.malala.org|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://g1.globo.com/educacao/noticia/a-estrategia-de-malala-para-colocar-130-milhoes-de-meninas-na-escola.ghtml|title = A estratégia de Malala para colocar 130 milhões de meninas na escola|date = 16 July 2018|accessdate = 10 September 2018|website = O Globo|last = Carolina Moreno|first = Ana}}</ref> One of the champions for Pakistan is [[Gulalai Ismail]], chairperson of [[Aware Girls]] an organisation with which Yousafzai trained in 2011.<ref name="im2018">{{cite web |last1=Plackis-Cheng |first1=Paksy |title=Aware Girls |url=http://www.impactmania.com/article/aware-girls-building-peace-pakistan/ |website=Impactmania |accessdate=14 December 2018}}</ref><ref name="guar2018">{{cite web |last1=Briggs |first1=Billy |title=The Peshawar women fighting the Taliban: 'We cannot trust anyone' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/cities/2015/oct/13/the-peshawar-women-fighting-the-taliban-we-cannot-trust-anyone |website=The Guardian |accessdate=13 December 2018}}</ref>

===Research and Advocacy===
Malala, Ziauddin, Malala Fund staff, members of the Education Champion Network and young education activists participate in conferences and meet with political leaders to advocate for girls' education.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.malala.org/advocacy|title = Malala Fund Advocacy|accessdate = 27 August 2018|website = Malala Fund}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/reliable-source/wp/2015/06/23/malala-yousafzai-visits-capitol-hill-to-advocate-for-girls-education/|title = Malala Yousafzai visits Capitol Hill to advocate for girls’ education|date = 23 June 2015|accessdate = 29 August 2018|website = The Washington Post|last = Heil|first = Emily}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.globalpartnership.org/blog/15-women-speak-power-education|title = 15 women speak up on the power of education|date = 9 March 2018|accessdate = 29 August 2018|website = Global Partnership for Education}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.stkate.edu/news-and-events/news/from-yemen-to-un-stkates-student-advocates-for-womens-education|title = From Yemen to the United Nations: St. Kate’s student advocates for women’s education|date = 3 October 2016|accessdate = 29 August 2018|website = St. Catherine University News|last = Rolenc|first = Sharon}}</ref> The advocacy goals are to increase funding for girls' education<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.newsdeeply.com/womensadvancement/community/2017/06/21/g20-will-never-get-women-to-work-without-investing-in-girls-education|title = G20 Will Never Get Women to Work Without Investing in Girls’ Education|date = 21 June 2017|accessdate = 10 September 2018|website = News Deeply|last1 = Royle|first1 = Taylor|last2 = Johnston|first2 discrimination]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.vogue.com.au/culture/features/emma-watson-and-malala-yousafzai-two-activists-on-how-empowering-women-begins-with-education/news-story/e391c6d1861508c3819a82ea26ea61ed|title = Emma Watson and Malala Yousafzai: two activists on how empowering women begins with education|date = 8 March 2018|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = Vogue Australia|last = Watson|first = Emma}}</ref> Malala Fund has conducted research on the impact of girls' secondary education in collaboration with [[Brookings Institution]], [[World Bank]] and [[Results for Development]].

In June 2018, Malala Fund helped secure a $2.9 billion commitment for girls' education from [[Group of Seven|G7]] countries and the [[World Bank]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/international/world-news/3billion-pledged-for-girls-education-at-g7-delighting-malala/articleshow/64526609.cms|title = $3 billion pledged for girls education at G7, delighting Malala|date = 10 June 2018|accessdate = 28 August 2018|website = The Economic Times}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.g7g20summits.org/opinions/the-key-to-unlocking-girls-potential/|title = MALALA FUND ADVOCACY: The key to unlocking girls’ potential|accessdate = 10 September 2018|website = The Global Governance Project|last = Mohamed|first = Farah}}</ref>
