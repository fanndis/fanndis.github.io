The wedding of [[Prince Charles]] and [[Lady Diana Spencer]] took place on Wednesday 29 July 1981<ref name="Live">{{cite web|title=The Royal Wedding of HRH The Prince of Wales and the Lady Diana Spencer|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00fj1gx|publisher=BBC One|date=29 July 1981|access-date=25 December 2019|archive-date=3 August 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190803143639/https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00fj1gx|url-status=live}}</ref> at [[St Paul's Cathedral]] in [[London]], United Kingdom. The groom was the [[heir apparent]] to the British throne, and the bride was a member of the [[Spencer family]].

The ceremony was a traditional [[Church of England]] wedding service. The [[dean of St Paul's Cathedral]], [[Alan Webster (priest)|Alan Webster]], presided at the service, and the [[archbishop of Canterbury]], [[Robert Runcie]], conducted the marriage. Notable figures in attendance included many members of other royal families, republican heads of state, and members of the bride's and groom's families. After the ceremony, the couple made the traditional appearance on the balcony of [[Buckingham Palace]]. The United Kingdom had a national holiday on that day to mark the wedding.<ref name="bbcWeddingDay"/> The ceremony featured many ceremonial aspects, including use of the [[Royal Mews|state carriages]] and roles for the [[Foot Guards]] and [[Household Cavalry]].

Their marriage was widely billed as a "fairytale wedding" and the "wedding of the century". It was watched by an estimated global TV audience of 750&nbsp;million people.<ref name="bbcWeddingDay">{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/onthisday/hi/dates/stories/july/29/newsid_2494000/2494949.stm|title=1981: Charles and Diana marry|date=29 July 1982|accessdate=13 October 2008|publisher=BBC News|work=On This Day|archive-date=10 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180110135139/http://news.bbc.co.uk/onthisday/hi/dates/stories/july/29/newsid_2494000/2494949.stm|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=WashPostEngagement>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/inatl/longterm/diana/stories/glamor0901.htm|title=International Special Report: Princess Diana, 1961–1997|date=30 January 1999|accessdate=13 October 2008|work=The Washington Post|archive-date=19 August 2000|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20000819020342/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/inatl/longterm/diana/stories/glamor0901.htm|url-status=live}}{{registration}}</ref> Events were held around the [[Commonwealth of Nations|Commonwealth]] to mark the wedding. Many street parties were held throughout the United Kingdom to celebrate the occasion. The couple separated in 1992 and divorced in 1996 after fifteen years of marriage.

==Engagement==
[[Prince Charles]] had known [[Lady Diana Spencer]] for several years. They first met in 1977 while Charles was dating her elder sister [[Lady Sarah McCorquodale|Lady Sarah]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/history/events/prince_charles_and_lady_diana_spencers_wedding|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110321120400/http://www.bbc.co.uk/history/events/prince_charles_and_lady_diana_spencers_wedding|url-status=dead|archive-date=21 March 2011|title=Prince Charles and Lady Diana Spencer's wedding|publisher=BBC History|accessdate=12 May 2018}}</ref> He took serious interest in her as a potential bride in 1980 when they were guests at a country weekend, where she watched him play [[polo]]. He invited her for a sailing weekend to [[Cowes]] aboard the royal yacht ''[[HMY Britannia|Britannia]]'' as their relationship began to develop. This was followed by an invitation to [[Balmoral Castle]], the Royal family's Scottish home, to meet his family.<ref name=age17nov>{{cite news|title=Royal weekend fuels rumours|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=DPRUAAAAIBAJ&pg=4597,379407&dq=diana+spencer&hl=en|accessdate=22 July 2013|newspaper=The Age|date=17 November 1980|via=Google News|archive-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126085349/https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=DPRUAAAAIBAJ&sjid=a5IDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4597,379407&dq=diana+spencer&hl=en|url-status=live}}</ref>{{sfn|Dimbleby|p=279|1994}} Diana was well received at Balmoral by [[Queen Elizabeth II]], [[Prince Philip]], and [[Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother]]. The couple then had several dates in London. Diana and Charles had been seeing each other for about six months when he proposed on 3 February 1981 in the nursery at Windsor Castle. Diana had planned a holiday for the next week, and Charles hoped she would use the time to consider her answer.<ref name="A"/> Diana accepted, but their engagement was kept secret for the next few weeks.{{sfn|Morton|1997|p=118}} Diana later claimed that the couple had met only 13 times in total before the announcement of their engagement.<ref name="VF-Wedding">{{cite web|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/style/2018/04/princess-diana-royal-wedding|title=Inside Princess Diana's Royal Wedding Fairy Tale|publisher=Vanity Fair|date=17 April 2018|accessdate=11 May 2018|first=Julie|last=Miller|archive-date=5 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180605194316/https://www.vanityfair.com/style/2018/04/princess-diana-royal-wedding|url-status=live}}</ref>

Two nights before the wedding, a gala ball was held at [[Buckingham Palace]], and the Queen subsequently hosted a dinner for a crowd of 90 individuals.<ref name="NT-Wedding">{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/28/world/charles-and-lady-diana-rehearse-the-wedding.html|title=Charles and Lady Diana Rehearse the Wedding|work=The New York Times|first=RW|last=Apple Jr|date=28 July 1981|accessdate=11 May 2018|archive-date=12 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180512113000/https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/28/world/charles-and-lady-diana-rehearse-the-wedding.html|url-status=live}}</ref> A reception with dancing for 1,500 people was also held. ==Wedding==
[[File:Combined Coat of Arms of Charles and Diana, the Prince and Princess of Wales.svg|thumb|Combined [[Coat of arms of the Prince of Wales|coat of arms of Charles]] and Diana, the Prince and Princess of Wales]]
3,500 guests made up the congregation at St Paul's Cathedral.<ref name="A">{{cite web|publisher=DotDash|url=https://www.thoughtco.com/princess-dianas-wedding-3528741|title=Princess Diana's Wedding|author=Jone Johnson Lewis|work=ThoughtCo|access-date=25 March 2020|archive-date=22 June 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190622163029/https://www.thoughtco.com/princess-dianas-wedding-3528741|url-status=live}}</ref> Charles and Diana selected St Paul's over [[Westminster Abbey]], the traditional site of royal weddings, because St. Paul's offered more seating<ref name="VF-Wedding"/> and permitted a longer procession through London.

The ceremony was a traditional [[Church of England]] wedding service, presided over by the [[Most Reverend]] [[Robert Runcie]], [[Archbishop of Canterbury]],<ref name="bbcWeddingDay"/> and the [[Very Reverend]] [[Alan Brunskill Webster|Alan Webster]], [[Dean of St Paul's|Dean]] of [[St. Paul's Cathedral]]. Two&nbsp;million spectators lined the route of Diana's procession from [[Clarence House]], with 4,000 police and 2,200 military officers to manage the crowds.<ref name="A"/> The security increased and sharpshooters were stationed due to the potential threat of an attack by the Irish Republican guerrillas.<ref name="VF-Wedding"/><ref name="NT-Wedding"/><ref>{{cite increased.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/archives/entry/policing-the-1981-royal-wedding|title=Policing the 1981 royal wedding|publisher=CBC|date=27 July 1981|accessdate=11 May Regiments from the [[Commonwealth realm]]s participated in the procession, including [[the Royal Regiment of Canada]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.army.forces.gc.ca/rrc/command/rsm.html|title= Command: Regimental Sergeant Major|publisher=Queen's Printer for Canada|accessdate=8 April 2011}}{{deadlink|date=March 2020}}</ref>

At 10:22 BST the Queen and the royal family were taken to the cathedral in eight carriages. The Prince of Wales in the [[1902 State Landau]] which was later used following the ceremony to take the couple back to Buckingham Palace.<ref name="WP-Planning"/> Lady Diana arrived at the cathedral in the [[Glass Coach]] with her father, [[John Spencer, 8th Earl Spencer|John Spencer]]; she was escorted by six mounted Metropolitan Police officers.<ref name="A"/> She arrived almost on time for the 11:20 BST ceremony.<ref name=bbcWeddingDay/> The carriage was too small to hold the two of them comfortably due to her voluminous dress and train.<ref name="VF-Wedding"/> As the orchestra played [[Trumpet voluntary]], an anthem by [[Jeremiah Clarke]], the bride made the three-and-a-half minute walk up the aisle.<ref name="bbcWeddingDay"/><ref name="NTimes-Wedding"/>

Diana accidentally changed the order of Charles's names during her vows, saying "Philip Charles Arthur George" instead of the correct "Charles Philip Arthur George".<ref name=bbcWeddingDay/> She did not promise to "obey" him as part of the traditional vows. That word was eliminated at the couple's request, which caused a sensation at the time.<ref name="'70s 98">{{cite book|title=How We Got Here: The '70s|last=Frum|first=David|year=2000|publisher=Basic Books|location=New York City|isbn=0-465-04195-7|page=98|url=https://archive.org/details/howwegothere70sd00frum/page/98}}</ref> Charles also made an error. He said he would offer her "thy goods" instead of "my worldly goods".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marieclaire.co.uk/entertainment/people/princess-diana-prince-charles-wedding-mistake-500552|title=Here's the big mistake that happened on Princess Diana and Prince Charles' wedding day|publisher=TI Media|work=Marie Claire|first=Jenny|last=Proudfoot|date=26 April 2017|accessdate=9 May 2018|archive-date=8 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180508211950/http://www.marieclaire.co.uk/entertainment/people/princess-diana-prince-charles-wedding-mistake-500552|url-status=live}}</ref> In keeping with tradition, the couple's wedding rings were crafted from [[Welsh gold]] from the [[Clogau]] [[St David]]'s mine in [[Bontddu]].<ref name="NTimes-Wedding">{{cite web|url=https://archive.nytimes.com/www.nytimes.com/learning/general/onthisday/big/0729.html|title=Amid Splendor, Charles Weds Diana|publisher=The New York Times|first=RW|last=Apple Jr|date=29 July 1981|accessdate=12 May 2018|work=On this Day|archive-date=22 April 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180422103725/https://archive.nytimes.com/www.nytimes.com/learning/general/onthisday/big/0729.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The tradition of using Welsh gold within the wedding rings of the Royal Family dates back to 1923.<ref name="WP-Planning"/> Upon marriage Diana automatically acquired the title of [[Princess of Wales]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/sns-bc-eu--ap-was-there-diana-wedding-20170825-story.html|title=AP Was There: Prince Charles and Princess Diana's wedding|publisher=Chicago Tribune|agency=Associated Press|first=Hugh|last=Mulligan|date=25 August 2017|accessdate=12 May 2018|archive-date=12 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180512181535/http://www.chicagotribune.com/sns-bc-eu--ap-was-there-diana-wedding-20170825-story.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

Other church representatives present who gave prayers after the service were a former Archbishop of Canterbury, [[Donald Coggan]], [[Basil Hume|Cardinal Basil Hume]], the Right Reverend Andrew Doig and the [[Harry Williams (priest)|Reverend Harry Williams]] CR.<ref name="royal wedding">[http://archive.timesonline.co.uk/tol/viewArticle.arc?articleId=ARCHIVE-The_Times-1981-07-29-15-001&pageId=ARCHIVE-The_Times-1981-07-29-15 Royal Wedding]{{deadlink|date=March 2020}} ''The Times'', 29 July 1981, page 15</ref><ref name="NTimes-Wedding"/>

===Music===
Three choirs, three orchestras and a fanfare ensemble played the music for the service. These were the [[Bach Choir]], the Choir of St. Paul's Cathedral, the Choir of the Chapel Royal, the Orchestra of the Royal Opera House, the [[Philharmonia Orchestra]] and the [[English Chamber Orchestra]] and a fanfare ensemble from the Royal Military School.<ref name="nytimes">{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/22/arts/anthem-is-composed-for-royal-bridal.html|title=Anthem is Composed for Royal Bridal|last=Rothstein|first=Edward|date=22 July 1981|work=The New York Times|accessdate=1 September 2016|archive-date=7 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160307012823/http://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/22/arts/anthem-is-composed-for-royal-bridal.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The choirs were conducted by [[Barry Rose]], the choirmaster at St. Paul's Cathedral. The cathedral's organist, [[Christopher Dearnley]]; and its sub-organist, [[John Scott (organist)|John Scott]]; played the organ. The Orchestra of the Royal Opera House, the Philharmonia Orchestra and the English Chamber Orchestra were conducted by [[David Willcocks|Sir David Willcocks]], who was the director of the Royal College of Music and of the Bach Choir;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thebachchoir.org.uk/news/sir-david-willcocks-1919-2015|title=Sir David Willcocks (1919-2015) Musical Director of The Bach Choir for 38 Years|publisher=The Bach Choir|accessdate=29 November 2020}}</ref> [[Richard Popplewell]], the organist at Chapel Royal; and [[Colin Davis|Sir Colin Davis]], who was the musical director of Covent Garden.<ref name="nytimes"/><ref name="royal wedding"/> Music and songs used during the wedding included the "[[Prince of Denmark's March]]", "[[I Vow to Thee, My Country]]", "[[Pomp and Circumstance Marches#March No. 4 in G|Pomp and Circumstance No.4]]" and the [[God Save the Queen|British National Anthem ("God Save the Queen")]].<ref name="royal wedding"/> New Zealand soprano, [[Kiri Te Kanawa]] sang "Let The Bright Seraphim" from [[G. F. Handel]]'s [[Samson (Handel)|Samson]].<ref name="WP-Planning">{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1981/07/26/the-royal-wedding/131f3274-e84b-4f31-b7bc-9a4be062b605/?noredirect=on|title=The Royal Wedding|work=The Washington Post|first=Leonard|last=Downie Jr|date=26 July 1981|accessdate=31 July 2018|archive-date=19 May 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200519095313/https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1981/07/26/the-royal-wedding/131f3274-e84b-4f31-b7bc-9a4be062b605/?noredirect=on|url-status=live}}</ref>

Diana chose the floral scent Quelques Fleurs, which featured "notes of tuberose, jasmine and rose".<ref name="People-Perfume">{{cite web|url=https://people.com/royals/princess-dianas-wedding-day-perfume/|title=All About Princess Diana's Wedding Day Perfume — and How She Accidentally Spilled It on Her Dress!|publisher=Meredith Corporation|work=People|first=Erin|last=Hill|date=29 July 2018|accessdate=23 February 2019|archive-date=24 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190224062414/https://people.com/royals/princess-dianas-wedding-day-perfume/|url-status=live}}</ref> She was reported to have accidentally spilled perfume over a part of her dress which she later covered with her hand during the ceremony.<ref name="VF-Wedding"/> The bride also had a pair of slippers made out of hand-made ivory silk with pearl and sequin embroidery.<ref name="RoyalDress">{{cite web|url=https://www.royal.uk/wedding-dresses|title=Royal Wedding Dresses throughout history|publisher=UK Royal Family|accessdate=9 October 2018|archive-date=10 October 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181010095306/https://www.royal.uk/wedding-dresses|url-status=live}}</ref> Barbara Daly did the bride's make-up for the ceremony.<ref name="People-Perfume"/>

Per the Queen's orders, two similar bouquets were prepared for the bride by David Longman which contained "[[gardenias]], [[stephanotis]], odontolglossum orchid, [[lily of the valley]], Earl Mountbatten roses, [[freesia]], [[Veronica (plant)|veronica]], [[ivy]], [[Myrtus|myrtle]] and trasdescantia".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marieclaire.co.uk/entertainment/people/princess-diana-two-wedding-bouquets-593991|title=This is why the Queen made Princess Diana have two wedding bouquets|publisher=TI Media|work=Marie Claire|first=Jenny|last=Proudfoot|date=2 May 2018|accessdate=3 May 2018|archive-date=2 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180502211002/http://www.marieclaire.co.uk/entertainment/people/princess-diana-two-wedding-bouquets-593991|url-status=live}}</ref>

Charles wore his full dress naval commander uniform.<ref name=leo20jul>{{cite news|last=Downie Jr.|first=Leonard|title=Britain Celebrates, Charles Takes a Bride|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/inatl/longterm/diana/background/wedding1.htm|accessdate=23 July 2013|work=Washington Post|date=29 July 1981|page=A01|archive-date=11 November 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121111035259/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/inatl/longterm/diana/background/wedding1.htm|url-status=live}}{{registration}}</ref> He also wore stars of the orders of the Garter and the Thistle, the Queen's silver jubilee medal, and "the royal cipher of the Prince of Wales in gold on epaulettes on both shoulders."<ref name="WP-Planning"/> He carried a "full dress sword tassled in gold."<ref name="WP-Planning"/>

===Attendants===
The royal couple had seven bridal attendants. Eleven-year-old [[Lord Nicholas Windsor]], son of the Duke and Duchess of Kent, and eight-year-old Edward van Cutsem, godsons of the Prince of Wales, were [[Page boy (wedding attendant)|page boy]]s. Diana's [[bridesmaids]] were seventeen-year-old [[Lady Sarah Armstrong-Jones]], daughter of the Earl of Snowdon and [[Princess Margaret]];<ref name=leo20jul/> thirteen-year-old [[India Hicks]], the granddaughter of the [[Louis Mountbatten, 1st Earl Mountbatten of Burma|Earl Mountbatten of Burma]] and daughter of [[David Nightingale Hicks|David]] and [[Lady Pamela Hicks]]; six-year-old Catherine Cameron, daughter of Donald and Lady Cecil Cameron and granddaughter of the [[Peter Kerr, 12th Marquess of Lothian|Marquess of Lothian]]; eleven-year-old Sarah-Jane Gaselee, daughter of Nick Gaselee and his wife; and five-year-old Clementine Hambro, daughter of Rupert Hambro and the Hon Mrs Hambro and granddaughter of [[Christopher Soames, Baron Soames|Lord]] and [[Mary Soames, Baroness Soames|Lady Soames]] and great-granddaughter of [[Winston Churchill]].<ref name="NT-Wedding"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/royals/link-between-harry-meghan-wedding-charles-diana/|title=The Surprising Link Between Harry and Meghan's Royal Wedding and Charles and Diana's|publisher=Meredith Corporation|work=People|first=Simon|last=Perry|date=3 May 2018|accessdate=9 May 2018|archive-date=8 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180508223321/http://people.com/royals/link-between-harry-meghan-wedding-charles-diana/|url-status=live}}</ref> Princes [[Prince Andrew, Duke of York|Andrew]] and [[Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex|Edward]] were the Prince of Wales's supporters (the equivalent of "best man" for a royal wedding).<ref name="NT-Wedding"/>

===Guests===
{{main|List of wedding guests of Prince Charles and Lady Diana Spencer}}
[[File:Prime_Minister_Robert_Muldoon_and_his_wife_Thea_attending_the_royal_wedding,_1981_(28335997832).jpg|thumb|[[Robert Muldoon]] and his wife [[Thea Muldoon]] attending the royal wedding]]
All of the Queen's [[Governor-General|governors-general]], as well as Europe's crowned heads, attended, with the exception of [[Juan Carlos I of Spain|King Juan Carlos]] and [[Queen Sofía of Spain]]. (The Spanish king was "advised" not to attend by his government because the newlyweds' honeymoon included a stopover in the disputed territory of [[Gibraltar]]).<ref name = "NYT Guest List">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/25/world/prince-s-guest-list-embraces-kings-and-charwomen.html|title=Prince's Guest List embraces Kings and Charwomen|work=New York Times|first=RW|last=Apple Jr|date=25 July 1981|access-date=31 October 2017|archive-date=23 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170823121805/http://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/25/world/prince-s-guest-list-embraces-kings-and-charwomen.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Most of Europe's elected heads of state were among the guests, with the exceptions of the [[President of Greece]], [[Constantine Karamanlis]] (who declined because Greece's exiled monarch, [[Constantine II of Greece|Constantine II]], a kinsman and friend of the bridegroom, had been invited as "King of the Hellenes"), and the [[President of Ireland]], [[Patrick Hillery]] (who was advised by [[Taoiseach]] [[Charles Haughey]] not to attend because of the dispute over the status of [[Northern Ireland]]).<ref group=fn>The period when the advice was given coincided with a change of government. Traditionally Irish presidents and British royalty did not meet publicly because of the Northern Ireland issue.</ref> [[First Lady]] [[Nancy Reagan]] represented the United States at the wedding.<ref name="Huffpost-Wedding">{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/07/29/princess-diana-wedding_n_855296.html|title=A Look Back At Princess Diana & Prince Charles' Wedding|publisher=Huffington Post|first=Hilary|last=Moss|date=29 April 2011|accessdate=10 April 2018|archive-date=18 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160918014151/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/07/29/princess-diana-wedding_n_855296.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Among other invitees were the couple's friends and the bride invited the staff of the nursery school in which she had worked to the wedding.<ref name="WP-Planning"/> [[Spike Milligan]] and [[Harry Secombe]] were among the entertainers who were invited to the ceremony by the Prince of Wales.<ref name="WP-Planning"/>

===Reception===
The couple and 120 guests went to [[Buckingham Palace]] for a wedding breakfast following the ceremony.<ref name="A"/> Diana and Charles made a traditional appearance on a balcony of Buckingham Palace at 13:10 BST, and delighted the crowd when they kissed,<ref name=bbcWeddingDay/><ref name="A"/> initiating the tradition of kissing the bride on the balcony.<ref name="Huffpost-Wedding"/> Over the night, fireworks were displayed above Hyde Park and 100 beacons were lit up across the country to celebrate the royal wedding.<ref name="WP-Planning"/>

The couple had 27 [[wedding cakes]].<ref name="VF-Wedding"/> The Naval Armed Forces supplied the official wedding cake. David Avery, head baker at the Royal Naval cooking school in Chatham Kent, made the cake over 14 weeks. They made two identical cakes in case one was damaged. The Prince of Wales's coat of arms and the Spencer family's crest were used in the decoration of the five-foot-tall layered fruitcake which weighed 225 pounds.<ref name="VF-Wedding"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/diana-prince-charles-wedding-2011-4|title=Flashback: Diana's Wedding Was Four Times As Expensive, And Her Train Was 17-Feet Longer|publisher=Insider|work=Business Insider|first=Leah|last=Goldman|date=29 April 2011|accessdate=11 May 2018|archive-date=12 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180512112302/http://www.businessinsider.com/diana-prince-charles-wedding-2011-4|url-status=live}}</ref> The couple's other wedding cake was created by [[Belgium|Belgian]] [[pastry chef]] [[S. G. Sender|SG Sender]], who was known as the "cakemaker to the kings".<ref name=expatica>{{cite news|title=Belgian "cakemaker to the kings" dies|url=http://www.expatica.com/be/news/belgian-news/Belgian-_cakemaker-to-the-kings_-dies_54655.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090727162835/http://www.expatica.com/be/news/belgian-news/Belgian-_cakemaker-to-the-kings_-dies_54655.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=27 July 2009|work=Expatica|date=20 July 2009|accessdate=25 July 2009}}</ref> Another wedding cake was created by [[Nicholas Lodge|Chef Nicholas Lodge]]; Chef Nicholas had previously made the Queen Mother's 80th Birthday Cake and also commissioned to create a Christening Cake for Prince Harry.<ref>{{cite web|title=About Nicholas Lodge|url=https://www.nicholaslodge.com/about-us|work=International Sugar Art Collection|publisher=Nicholas Lodge|access-date=25 March 2020|archive-date=25 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200325173629/https://www.nicholaslodge.com/about-us|url-status=live}}</ref> A slice of the couple's wedding cake was later auctioned off by [[Julien's Auctions]] in 2018 and was estimated to sell between $800–$1,200.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://time.com/5265520/diana-royal-wedding-cake/|title=Endlessly Appetizing 37-Year-Old Royal Wedding Cake Up For Auction|publisher=Time|first=Kate|last=Samuelson|date=4 May 2018|accessdate=9 May 2018|archive-date=7 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180507183041/http://time.com/5265520/diana-royal-wedding-cake/|url-status=live}}</ref>

An estimated 750&nbsp;million people watched the ceremony worldwide,<ref name=bbcWeddingDay/> and this figure allegedly rose to a billion when the radio audience is added in, although there are no means of verifying these figures.<ref name="A"/> The event was broadcast in 50 countries with near 100 television companies covering it.<ref name="WP-Planning"/> The wedding ceremony was positively received by the public,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/article/remembering-the-wedding-of-the-prince-of-wales-and-lady-diana-spencer|title=From The Archive: Remembering The Prince Of Wales to ''[[The New York Times]]'' symbolised "the continuity of the monarchy" in the UK.<ref name="NTimes-Wedding"/> A number of ceremonies and parties were held at different places by the public to celebrate the occasion across the United Kingdom.<ref name="Guardian-Wedding">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2011/apr/24/royal-wedding-charles-diana-1981|title='The stuff of web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00fnhtx|title=Scotland celebrates as Prince Charles marries Diana|publisher=BBC One|work=BBC Midday News|date=22 January 2011|accessdate=11 May in honour of the couple.<ref name="Guardian-Wedding"/>

A group of people left London and travelled to France and Ireland in protest to the wedding, while some released black balloons over London amidst the wedding procession.<ref name="NTimes-Wedding"/>

===Gifts===
[[File:1981_Royal_Wedding_Stamp.jpg|thumb|1981 Royal Wedding Stamp (NZ)|335x335px]]
The couple received gifts from foreign officials including "an engraved Steuben glass bowl and a handmade porcelain centerpiece by Boehm" from the US, a set of antique furniture and "a watercolor of loons" by Canadian Robert Bateman for Prince Charles, together with "a large brooch of gold, diamonds and platinum" for Diana from Canada, handcrafted silver platters from Australia, an "all-wool broadloom carpet" from New Zealand, "a matching diamond and sapphire watch, bracelet, pendant, ring, and earrings" from the [[Fahd of Saudi Arabia|Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia]], and a "small oil painting by the American artist Henry Kohler of Prince Charles playing polo", and a clock in Art Deco style by [[Cartier (jeweler)|Cartier]]'s chief designer, Daniel Ciacquinot.<ref name="VF-Wedding"/><ref name="NYT-Gifts">{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/27/style/royal-wedding-gifts-extraordinary-and-ordinary-diana.html|title=Royal Wedding Gifts: Extraordinary and Ordinary Diana|work=The New York Times|first=Susan|last=Goodman|date=27 July 1981|accessdate=20 February 2019|archive-date=21 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190221000653/https://www.nytimes.com/1981/07/27/style/royal-wedding-gifts-extraordinary-and-ordinary-diana.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The [[Edinburgh District Council]] was among the organisations that made a charitable donation in honour of the couple's wedding and donated $92,500 to the Thistle Fund, "a charity for the disabled".<ref name="NYT-Gifts"/> The [[Greater Manchester Council]] offered engineering apprenticeships for a small number of unemployed young people, and [[Cambridge University]] sent "a spare copy of ''The Complete English Traveller''" by [[Robert Sanders (writer)|Robert Sanders]].<ref name="NYT-Gifts"/> The Worshipful Company of Glovers of London presented the couple with gloves made out of leather, silks and cotton. A number of these gifts were displayed at St. James's Palace from 5 August to 4 October 1981.<ref name="NYT-Gifts"/>

==Honeymoon==
A "just married" sign was attached to the [[Landau (carriage)|landau]] by Princes Andrew and Edward.<ref name="NTimes-Wedding"/> The couple was driven over [[Westminster Bridge]] to catch the train from [[London Waterloo railway station|Waterloo station]] to Romsey in Hampshire to begin their honeymoon.<ref name=bbcWeddingDay/> The couple left from Waterloo station in the [[British Royal Train]] + [[975025 Caroline|975025 ''Caroline'']]. They travelled to [[Broadlands]], where Prince Charles's parents had spent their wedding night in 1947.<ref name=leo20jul/> They stayed there for three days,<ref name=leo20jul/> then flew to [[Gibraltar]], where they boarded the Royal Yacht ''[[HMY Britannia|Britannia]]'' for an eleven-day cruise of the [[Mediterranean Sea|Mediterranean]], visiting Tunisia, [[Sardinia]], Greece and Egypt.<ref name="NTimes-Wedding"/> Then they flew to Scotland, where the rest of the royal family had gathered at [[Balmoral Castle]], and spent time in a hunting lodge on the estate. During that time, the press was given an arranged opportunity to take pictures.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1992-07-01/news/vw-1329_1_royal-couple|title=Charles and Diana's Short Honeymoon|work=Los Angeles Times|date=1 July 1992|accessdate=31 July 2018|archive-date=12 November 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161112035209/http://articles.latimes.com/1992-07-01/news/vw-1329_1_royal-couple|url-status=live}}</ref> Despite their happy appearance, Diana's suspicion over Charles having an enduring affection for his former lover Camilla grew as Camilla's photographs fell out of his diary and Diana discovered that he was wearing cufflinks that were given to him by Camilla.<ref name="Time-2020"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/the-crown-why-charles-and-camilla-may-not-be-amused-6fmbs80l3|title=The Crown: why Charles and Camilla may not be amused|work=The Times|first=Hilary|last=Rose|date=9 November 2020|accessdate=19 November 2020}}</ref> By the time the couple returned from their honeymoon, their wedding gifts were displayed at [[St James's Palace]].<ref name="WP-Planning"/>
