The '''Center for Public Leadership''' is an academic research center that provides teaching, research and training in the practical skills of leadership for people in government, nonprofits, and business. It was established in 2000, through a gift from the [[Wexner Foundation]], at [[Harvard Kennedy School]], [[Harvard University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/about|title=About|website=cpl.hks.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref>

In January 2008 the center moved from its offices at 124 Mt. Auburn Street to its current location in the Taubman Building on the main campus of Harvard Kennedy School in Cambridge, Massachusetts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/visiting|title=Visiting CPL|website=cpl.hks.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref>

== Behavioral Insights Group ==
Founded in 2013 and led by faculty director [[Todd Rogers (behavioral scientist)]], the Behavioral Insights Group (BIG) is a center for behavioral science and behavioral economics research at Harvard Kennedy School.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/behavioral-insights-group|title=Behavioral Insights Group|website=cpl.hks.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref> BIG faculty engage in research and teach courses that apply behavioral economics to areas including public policy, global health, and law.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/big-courses-2018-19|title=BIG Courses|website=cpl.hks.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref> BIG also houses the Behavioral Insights Student Group (BISG).

==Hauser Leaders Program==
The Hauser Leaders Program allows high-profile leaders from across sectors to join CPL for one-to-two semesters to advise students and lead events.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/hauser-leaders-program|title=Hauser Leaders Program|website=cpl.hks.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref>

== Hauser Institute for Civil Society ==
Established through a grant by [[Rita Hauser|Rita]] and Gustave Hauser in 1997, the Hauser Institute for Civil Society (formerly the [[Hauser Center for Nonprofit Organizations]]) joined CPL in 2013. The Hauser Institute supports research and programming related to civil society and non-profit organizations. Hauser Institute initiatives include the Gleitsman Program in Leadership for Social Change, the Initiative for Responsible Investment, and the New World Social Innovation Fellowship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/hauser-institute|title=Hauser Institute|website=cpl.hks.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-05-23}}</ref>

===Gleitsman Program in Leadership for Social Change===
Through a gift of $20 million from the estate of Alan Gleitsman, the center endowed the [https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/gleitsman-program-leadership-social-change Gleitsman Program in Leadership for Social Change] in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.centerforpublicleadership.org/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=95&Itemid=88|title=Center for Public Leadership - Gleitsman Program in Leadership for Social Change|work=centerforpublicleadership.org|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100910035628/http://www.centerforpublicleadership.org/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=95&Itemid=88|archivedate=2010-09-10}}</ref> The program supports the annual [https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/gleitsman-international-activist-award Gleitsman International Activist Award] and [https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/gleitsman-citizen-activist-award Gleitsman Citizen Activist Award], Gleitsman Leadership Fellows,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://cpl.hks.harvard.edu/gleitsman-leadership-fellowship|title=Gleitsman Leadership Fellowship|access-date=2017-11-20|language=en}}</ref> and social change scholarship.
