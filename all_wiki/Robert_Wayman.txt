'''Robert P. Wayman''' is the former [[chief financial officer]] (CFO) of the [[Hewlett-Packard|Hewlett-Packard Company]] from 1984 to 2006. He served as the company's interim [[chief executive officer|CEO]] in 2005, and was an executive vice president, as well as a member of the H-P board of directors.

==Early life and education==

Wayman is a Chicago native.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB113867092150660454|title=Hewlett-Packard's Wayman to Retire|date=Jan 31, 2006|website=WSJ|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> He attended [[Northwestern University]] in [[Evanston, Illinois]], where he graduated with a bachelor's degree in science engineering and a [[Master of Business Administration]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB110795493376449938|title= H-P's Executive Shake Up|date=Feb 9, 2005|website=WSJ|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref>

==Career==

Robert Wayman joined the Hewlett-Packard Company in 1969 as a cost accountant at its [[Loveland, Colorado]] office, after a few years he moved to the company's headquarters at [[Palo Alto, California]], where he held a number of finance positions before he was appointed as the company's CFO in 1984, he then reported to former CEO [[John A. Young|John Young]]. He served on the company's board of directors from 1993 to 2002, and rejoined the board in 2005.<ref name=hp>{{cite web|url=https://www8.hp.com/us/en/hp-news/press-release.html?id=169052|title=HP CFO Bob Wayman to Retire After 37 Years at HP, to be Succeeded by Treasurer Cathie Lesjak|date=Dec 11, 2006 |website=hp.com|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref>

In February 2005, he was appointed an interim CEO, following [[Carly Fiorina]] departure as a result of pressures from the board of directors;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://money.cnn.com/2005/03/29/technology/hp_ceo_search/index.htm|title= It's official: Hurd new CEO at HP|date=March 29, 2005|website=CNN|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> Wayman retained his finance responsibilities,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cfo.com/strategy/2005/02/hps-fiorina-ousted-cfo-steps-in/|title=HP's Fiorina Ousted; CFO Steps In|date=February 9, 2005|website=cfo.com|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> and served in the top executive role until [[Mark Hurd]] assumed the position in March 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748703309704575413863250301470|title=Hurd's Reign as Hewlett CEO Is Bookended by Scandal|date=Aug 6, 2010|website=WSJ|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> The board of directors approved a $3 million bonus<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marketwatch.com/story/h-p-approves-3-million-award-for-cfo-bob-wayman|title=H-P approves $3 million award for CFO Bob Wayman|date=April 5, 2005|website=MarketWatch|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> for his 52-day service as CEO, an unusual payout,<ref name=bloomberg>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2005-04-06/hps-58-000-a-day-interim-ceo|title=HP's $58,000-a-Day Interim CEO|date=April 7, 2005|website=Bloomberg|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> that led to questions by some corporate governance experts and employees,<ref name=bloomberg /><ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=ShE1DQAAQBAJ&printsec=frontcover&dq=Becoming+Hewlett+Packard:+Why+Strategic+Leadership+Matters&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjk_ZWUkrvmAhWRdd8KHYeAC1MQ6AEwAHoECAUQAQ Becoming Hewlett Packard: Why Strategic Leadership Matters]. p.262, {{ISBN|978-0190640446}}</ref> but was defended by the company.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2006-dec-12-fi-hp12-story.html|title= HP finance chief to exit after 37 years at firm|date=Dec 12, 2006|website=LATimes|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnet.com/news/hp-earmarks-3-million-bonus-for-interim-chief/|title=HP earmarks $3 million bonus for interim chief|date=April 5, 2005|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref>

On December 11, 2006, after 37 years with the company, Robert Wayman announced his retirement, which became effective on December 31.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/12/12/technology/12hewlett.html|title=Hewlett-Packard's Longtime Financial Chief Is Set to Retire|date=Dec 12, 2006|website=The New York Times|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref><ref name=hp /> He was succeeded by [[Catherine A. Lesjak|Cathie Lesjak]]. In a Financial Times interview, Wayman said he began to plan for retirement since 2002 but reiterated due to the company's controversial deal to acquire [[Compaq]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/eb446df0-8924-11db-a876-0000779e2340|title= HP's Wayman to leave group after 37 years|date=December 11, 2006|website=Financial Times|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref>

Wayman joined the [[Santa Clara, California|Santa Clara]]-based [[Affymetrix]] board of directors on March 19, 2007,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20070319005433/en/Affymetrix-Appoints-Robert-Wayman-Board-Directors|title=Affymetrix Appoints Robert Wayman to Board of Directors|date=March 19, 2007|website=BusinessWire|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> until it was acquired by [[Thermo Fisher Scientific]] in 2016. He was a board member of [[Sybase]], until it was sold in 2010.<ref name=financialexec>{{cite web|url=https://nf.financialexecutives.org/eweb/dynamicpage.aspx?site=_hof&webcode=member_bio&name=Wayman|title=Robert P. Wayman|website=financialexecutives.org|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref> He also served as director in other corporation boards, including [[Con-Way|Con-Way Inc.]],<ref name=financialexec /> and [[CareFusion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cardinalhealth.mediaroom.com/newsreleasearchive?item=122650|title=CareFusion Announces Board of Directors, Progress on Alaris Consent Decree|date=June 9, 2009|website=Cardinal Health|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref>

He currently serves on the board of the nonprofit V Foundation for Cancer Research.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.v.org/people/robert-p-wayman|title=Robert P. Wayman|website=V Foundation for Cancer Research|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref> He is a member of the Advisory Board to [[Kellogg School of Management|Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kellogg.northwestern.edu/about/leadership/our-global-advisory-board.aspx|title=Global Advisory Board|website=Kellogg School of Management}}</ref>
