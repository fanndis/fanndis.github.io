'''''Billboard'' Women in Music''' is an annual event held by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Its main award is titled Woman of the Year, established to recognize "women in the music industry who have made significant contributions to the business and who, through their work and continued success, inspire generations of women to take on increasing responsibilities within the field", according to the magazine.<ref name="bb"/> [[Taylor Swift]] is the most awarded woman of the event, with three awards (two Woman of the Year awards and the Woman of the Decade award).

==Woman of the Decade Award==
[[Taylor Swift]] was honored with the first-ever Woman of the Decade Award, for being "one of the most accomplished musical artists of all time over the course of the 2010s".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8543996/taylor-swift-first-ever-woman-of-the-decade-award |title=Taylor Swift Will Receive First-Ever Woman of the Decade Honor at Billboard's Women in Music |work=Billboard |year=2019|accessdate=November 18, 2019}}</ref>
* 2010s: [[Taylor Swift]] (2019)

==Woman of the Year Award==
In 2007, [[Reba McEntire]] was honored with ''Billboard''{{'}}s first Woman of the Year Award because of "her success as a recording artist, contributions to the business, and leadership in embracing the changing music business."<ref name="bb">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1049244/mcentire-named-billboards-woman-of-the-year |title=McEntire Named Billboard's Woman Of The Year |work=Billboard |year=2007|accessdate=September 16, 2013}}</ref> Every year since then, ''Billboard'' has honored a female artist with the award. [[Taylor Swift]] is the first and only woman to be honored more than once. [[Billie Eilish]] is the youngest artist to ever be honored with the award. The most recent winner is [[Cardi B]], the first female rapper to receive the accolade.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/women-in-music/9485074/2020-women-in-music-event-cardi-b-jennifer-lopez/ |title=Cardi B, Jennifer Lopez, Dolly Parton, Dua Lipa & More to Be Honored at Billboard's 2020 Women in Music Event|work=Billboard |year=2020|accessdate=November 18, 2020}}</ref>
