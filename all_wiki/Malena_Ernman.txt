'''Sara Magdalena Ernman''' (born {{#dateformat:4 November 1970}}) is a Swedish opera singer. Besides operas and operettas, she has also performed [[chanson]]s, cabaret, jazz, and appeared in [[Musical theatre|musicals]]. She is a member of The [[Royal Swedish Academy of Music]]. Ernman represented Sweden at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009]] in Moscow.

== Life and career ==
[[File:Sveriges nationaldag 3518 (27446746831).jpg|thumb|upright|Ernman performing at [[Skansen]] in Stockholm during the Swedish National Day in June 2016]]

=== Early life ===
Ernman was born in [[Uppsala Municipality|Uppsala]], Sweden, spent her childhood and school years in [[Sandviken]], and was educated at the [[Royal College of Music in Stockholm]], the Music Conservatory in [[Orléans]], France, and the school of the [[Royal Swedish Opera]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ernman|first1=Malena|title=Bio|url=http://www.malenaernman.com/about/|website=Mezzo-Soprano Malena Ernman|accessdate=11 August 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150928232128/http://www.malenaernman.com/about/|archive-date=28 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> She is married to actor Svante Thunberg and together they have two daughters: singer Beata Ernman <ref>https://www.dw.com/en/greta-thunbergs-sister-launches-her-singing-career/a-52419740</ref> and climate activist [[Greta Thunberg]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.classicfm.com/music-news/greta-thunberg-mother-opera-singer-malena-ernman/|title=Greta Thunberg’s mother is actually an incredible opera singer|last=Macdonald|first=Kyle|date=2 October 2019|website=Classic FM|accessdate=28 December 2019}}</ref>

=== Operas ===
In 1997, Ernman sang in the premiere of [[Ivar Hallström]]'s 1897 opera ''Liten Karin'' in [[Vadstena]]; [[Opera (British magazine)|Opera]] magazine noted that "the mezzo Malena Ernman was very expressive as Princess Cecilia, King Erik XIV’s sister".<ref>Guillaume Maijeur. Vadstena - Wider horizons. ''Opera'', 1997 Annual Festival Issue, p127.</ref> In 1998 her Rosina in ''[[Il barbiere di Siviglia]]'' at the [[Royal Swedish Opera|Royal Opera]] in Stockholm was described as "displaying impressive technique" and "shaping the character with mocking good humour".<ref>Åsa Mälhammar. Sweden - Stud-farm 'Figaro'. ''Opera'', April 1999, Vol.50 No.4, p459.</ref> The same year, she sang Kaja in the premiere of [[Sven-David Sandström]]'s ''Staden'' under [[Leif Segerstam]] also at the Royal Opera in Stockholm, where one reviewer commented that "in vocal focus and expression, her full, rich voice is not that far behind [[Cecilia Bartoli|Bartoli]]".<ref>Claes Wahlin. Sweden - Big city blues. ''Opera'', February 1999, Vol.50 No.2, p213-214.</ref> In July 1999 Ernman sang the trouser role of Ziöberg in the premiere of Jonas Forssell's ''Trädgården'' (The Garden) at the [[Drottningholm Palace Theatre]] in Stockholm, conducted by [[Roy Goodman]], the first new opera to be premiered at the theatre in modern times.<ref>Allison, John. Sweden: Down 'The Garden' path - Drottningholm. ''Opera'', November 1999, Vol.50 No.11, p1345-46.</ref>

In Brussels in 2000, her Nerone in Handel's ''[[Agrippina (opera)|Agrippina]]'', alongside [[Rosemary Joshua]]'s Poppea and [[Anna Caterina Antonacci]]'s Aggripina was described as "the most convincingly brattish young man imaginable".<ref>John McCann. Belgium - Handel to perfection. ''Opera'', September 2000, Vol. 51, No.9, p1083-84.</ref> In 2013 she returned to this role at the [[Gran Teatre del Liceu]] in [[Barcelona]] in a production by [[David McVicar]] conducted by [[Harry Bicket]].<ref>Xavier Cester. Report from Barcelona. ''Opera'', March 2014, Vol 65 No.3, p324.</ref>

Ernman has sung several major roles with the [[Staatsoper Berlin]], including Cherubino in ''[[Le nozze di Figaro]]'' and Zerlina in ''[[Don Giovanni]]'', both under conductor [[Daniel Barenboim]]. She also performed Rosina in [[Rossini]]'s ''[[Il barbiere di Siviglia]]'' with Staatsoper Berlin and the [[Finnish National Opera]]. With the Royal Opera Stockholm she has also sung the title role in ''[[Carmen]]''.<ref name=Philpedia>{{cite web|title=About the Performer: Malena Ernman|url=http://www.laphil.com/philpedia/malena-ernman|website=LA Phil|accessdate=11 August 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180129035558/https://www.laphil.com/philpedia/malena-ernman|archive-date=29 January 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Ernman worked with conductor [[René Jacobs]] in the roles of Nerone in ''[[Agrippina (opera)|Agrippina]]'', Roberto in Scarlatti’s ''[[Griselda (A. Scarlatti)|Griselda]]'' and Diana in [[Francesco Cavalli|Cavalli]]’s ''[[La Calisto]]''.<ref name=Philpedia />

In 2001, she sang Sesto in Handel’s ''[[Giulio Cesare]]'' at the Drottningholm Festival. She appeared at the [[Glyndebourne Festival]] in 2002 singing Nancy in ''[[Albert Herring]]''<ref>[[Andrew Porter (music critic)|Andrew Porter]]. Report from Glyndebourne. ''Opera'', October 2002, Vol.53 No.10 p1254.</ref> and in 2003 singing Prince Orlovsky in [[Johann Strauss II|Johann Strauss]]'s ''[[Die Fledermaus]]'', which was also performed and broadcast at the [[BBC Proms]] that year.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/ecwc8g Proms Archive -  Prom 23 Monday 4 Aug 2003 Royal Albert Hall Die Fledermaus], accessed 1 August 2019.</ref> During the 2003/2004 season Ernman sang Donna Elvira in ''[[Don Giovanni]]'' at [[La Monnaie]] in Brussels. At the [[Aix-en-Provence Festival]] that year, she performed as Lichas in ''[[Hercules (Handel)|Hercules]]'' with [[Les Arts Florissants (ensemble)|Les Arts Florissants]] under conductor [[William Christie (musician)|William Christie]], a role that was later revived at the [[Paris Opera]] and the [[Vienna Festival]]. During the spring and summer of 2005, she performed the title role in [[Philippe Boesmans]]’s ''[[Julie (opera)|Julie]]'' at la Monnaie, the Vienna Festival and Aix-en-Provence. In spring of 2006 she sang the title role of ''[[Dido and Aeneas]]'' with William Christie at the Vienna Festival.<ref>Christopher Norton-Welsh. Report from Vienna. ''Opera'', September 2006, Vol.57 No.9, p1062-63. The production was later mounted at the Opéra-Comique in Paris and filmed.</ref>

In 2006, Ernman made her debut at the [[Salzburg Festival]] as Annio in ''[[La clemenza di Tito]]'' under conductor [[Nikolaus Harnoncourt]]. In 2007 her roles included Sesto in ''Giulio Cesare'' with René Jacobs in Vienna, Cherubino in ''[[Le nozze di Figaro]]'' with [[Daniel Harding]] in Aix-en-Provence, and Nerone in ''[[L'incoronazione di Poppea]]'' in Amsterdam. In 2008 she sang Angelina in ''[[La Cenerentola]]'' with the Royal Swedish Opera and ''Dido and Aeneas'' with Christie and the [[Opéra-Comique]] in Paris. In 2009 she reprised Angelina in ''La Cenerentola'' with Oper Frankfurt and the Swedish Royal Opera, and Dido in ''Dido and Aeneas'' with Christie in Vienna and Amsterdam. In 2010, she sang the castrato role of Idamante in ''[[Idomeneo]]'' under [[Jérémie Rhorer]] at the [[Theatre de la Monnaie]] in Brussels, where her "feisty" portrayal of the prince was "as if to the gender born, her efforts rewarded by the inclusion of the usually cut aria 'No, la morte'.<ref>John McCann. Report from Brussels. ''Opera'', July 2010, Vol. 61, No.7, p827.</ref> Vienna saw her in the title role of ''[[Serse]]'' by Handel in October 2011 at the [[Theater an der Wien]],<ref>Christopher Norton-Welsh. Report from Vienna. ''Opera'', January 2012, Vol.63 No.1, p34.</ref> and the following season she sang Eduige in the Nicolas Harnoncourt-led production of Handel's ''[[Rodelinda (opera)|Rodelinda]]'' at the same house, later released on DVD. Back in the city as Elena in ''[[La donna del lago]]'' in August 2012, she was "impressive... dealing with the vocal difficulties with aplomb and managing the extra dramatic demands made on her with genuine expressivity".<ref>Christopher Norton-Welsh. Report from Vienna. ''Opera'', January 2013, Vol 64 No.1, p42-43.</ref> She added Béatrice to her repertoire in 2013 in performances at Theater an der Wien of Berlioz's [[Béatrice et Bénédict|late opéra-comique]].<ref>Gerhard Persché. Reports from Vienna. ''Opera'', July 2013 Vol.64 No.7, p869.</ref>

She sang Gabriella in the Swedish musical ''Så som i himmelen'', based on a 2004 film of the [[Så som i himmelen|same name]], with words by Kay Pollak and Carin Pollak and the score by Fredrik Kempe, which premiered at the [[Oscarsteatern]] in September 2018.<ref>[https://www.expressen.se/noje/malena-ernman-har-publiken-i-sin-hand/ Expressen report 'Malena Ernman har publiken i sin hand' mentioning the musical], accessed 18 April 2019.</ref>

=== Concerts ===
Early recitals on Swedish Radio included Rachmaninov in 1994, ''The airconditioned nightmare'' by Olov Olofsson, songs by [[Gunnar de Frumerie]], and an eclectic mix of Fauré, Debussy, Jolivet, Ravel, Bizet, Barber, Ives and Lehrer in 1996, Brahms lieder, and works by Carlid, Mahler and Berio in 1998.<ref>[https://smdb.kb.se/catalog/search?q=malena+ernman+year%3A1990-1999+typ%3Aradio&sort=OLDEST Svensk Mediadatabas for Malena Ernman, 1990-99] accessed 28 June 2019.</ref>
Ernman has performed several concert pieces as well. At the Salzburg Festival she sang Mozart’s "[[Mass in C minor, K. 139 "Waisenhaus"|Waisenhausmesse]]" with conductor [[Frans Brüggen]]. She performed [[Berio]]’s "Folksongs" with the Stockholm Royal Philharmonic Orchestra under [[Carlo Rizzi (conductor)|Carlo Rizzi]], and at the Verbier Festival with [[Gustavo Dudamel]]. She sang the world premiere of "Nachtgesänge" by [[Fabian Müller (composer)|Fabian Müller]] with the [[Zurich Tonhalle Orchestra]]. In Minneapolis she sang [[Mozart]]’s "[[Requiem (Mozart)|Requiem]]" with [[Arnold Östman]].<ref name=Philpedia />

=== 2009 Melodifestivalen and Eurovision ===
On 28 November 2008, it was announced that Ernman would enter [[Melodifestivalen 2009]] for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009]] with the song "[[La voix (song)|La voix]]," written by [[Fredrik Kempe]]. On 28 February 2009 Ernman competed in the 4th semi-final of Melodifestivalen in [[Malmö]] and became a finalist.<ref>{{cite web|author=Clas Svahn |url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/agnes-och-malena-ernman-till-globen-1.810401 |title=Agnes och Malena Ernman till Globen |publisher=DN.SE |date=2009-02-28 |accessdate=2012-08-12 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090611035140/http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/agnes-och-malena-ernman-till-globen-1.810401 |archivedate=June 11, 2009 }}</ref> She went on to win the final on 14 March at the [[Globe Arena]] in Stockholm, and to represent Sweden in the Eurovision Song Contest in [[Moscow]]. She qualified as a finalist on 12 May and performed in the finals on 16 May, where she finished 21st with 33 points.<ref name="eurovision.tv">{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=2001 |title=Sweden: Malena Ernman to Moscow! &#124; News &#124; Eurovision Song Contest - Baku 2012 |publisher=Eurovision.tv |date=2009-03-14 |accessdate=2012-08-12}}</ref> "La voix" was the first Swedish entry to contain a substantial amount of French lyrics; it was written by Ernman herself, who speaks French fluently.<ref name="eurovision.tv"/>

Ernman revealed that the dress for her Eurovision performance cost 400,000 [[Swedish krona|kronor]] ([[euro|€]]37,471) and was made by designer [[Camilla Thulin]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.expressen.se/noje/melodifestivalen/1.1552411/har-ar-malenas-finalklanning |title=Här är Malenas finalklänning &#124; Melodifestivalen &#124; Expressen Nöje &#124; Nöjesnyheter Musiknyheter Filmnyheter |publisher=Expressen.se |date=2009-04-30 |accessdate=2012-08-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110920220007/http://www.expressen.se/noje/melodifestivalen/1.1552411/har-ar-malenas-finalklanning |archive-date=2011-09-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Singer [[Dea Norberg]] joined Ernman as one of the choirgirls. Ernman later participated in the Second Chance round of [[Melodifestivalen 2015]] as a guest singer for [[Behrang Miri]]s entry.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/behrang-miris-kamp-darfor-staller-malena-ernman-upp-i-melodifestivalen-2015|title=Behrang Miris kamp – därför ställer Malena Ernman upp i Melodifestivalen 2015|work=svt.se|accessdate=7 March 2015}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Ernman is married to Swedish actor Svante Thunberg.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/malena-ernman-vi-branner-ut-oss-pa-en-utbrand-planet/|title=Malena Ernman om familjekrisen och klimatkrisen|date=23 August 2018|website=[[Dagens Nyheter]]|accessdate=30 April 2019}}</ref> Their daughter [[Greta Thunberg|Greta]] rose to worldwide prominence when she initiated the [[school strike for climate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/women/greta-thunberg-mother-eurovision-malena-ernman-sweden-twitter-a8884781.html|title=Greta Thunberg's mother is a former Eurovision contestant and Twitter is ecstatic|last=O'Malley|first=Katie|date=24 April 2019|website=The Independent|accessdate=30 April 2019}}</ref> In August 2014, 11-year-old Greta suddenly stopped eating, talking, reading, or wanting to do anything. This condition lasted for several months until she was finally diagnosed with [[Asperger syndrome]]. The acute period of her daughter’s condition affected Ernman and her family to such an extent that she had three breakdowns during her professional activity and five performances had to be cancelled. After the crisis was overcome, she turned to the nationwide daily newspaper ''[[Expressen]]'', which reported it in detail, because she wanted to help other families in a similar situation.<ref>Christer Olsson: [https://www.expressen.se/halsoliv/halsa/det-har-varit-ett-helvetiskt-ar "Det har varit ett helvetiskt år (That has been a hell of a year)"], ''[[Expressen]]'', Stockholm, May 20, 2015.</ref>

Ernman has been politically active in support of the [[Paris Agreement|Paris Agreement against climate change]]; she wrote a collaborative debate piece in ''[[Dagens Nyheter]]''.<ref>{{cite news|author=Ernman, Malena |display-authors=et al | date=June 3, 2017 | title= I den akuta klimatkrisen väljer vi nu bort flyget |trans-title=In the acute climate crisis, we are now opting out of flight | work=[[Dagens Nyheter]] | location = Stockholm, Sweden | language = Swedish | url=https://www.dn.se/debatt/i-den-akuta-klimatkrisen-valjer-vi-nu-bort-flyget/ | accessdate=July 30, 2019 |url-access=subscription}}</ref> With her husband she co-wrote the book ''Scener ur hjärtat'' (''[[Scenes from the Heart]]'') about her family, the environment, and sustainability.<ref>Published in August 2018 by Bokförlaget Polaris, Sweden.</ref>
