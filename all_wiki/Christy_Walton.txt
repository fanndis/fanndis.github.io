'''Christy Ruth Walton''' (née '''Tallant''';<ref name="Billionaires 2013" /><ref name="Forbes400" /> born February 8, 1949) is the widow of [[John T. Walton]], one of the sons of [[Sam Walton]], the founder of [[Walmart]].

In June 2005, her husband died in a plane crash,<ref>{{cite news |title=WalMart heir dies in plane crash |url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/2005/06/28/wal-mart-heir-dies-in-plane-crash/ |publisher=[[Fox News]] |agency=[[Associated Press]] |date=June 28, 2005 |access-date=December 1, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130719035023/https://www.foxnews.com/story/2005/06/28/wal-mart-heir-dies-in-plane-crash/ |archive-date=July 19, 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> making her the main heir to his fortune of {{Currency|18.2|USD}} billion. ''Forbes'' listed Christy Walton as the [[richest woman]] in the world for several years.<ref name="Forbes400"/><ref name=forbes-2015>{{cite news |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/kerryadolan/2015/03/02/the-richest-women-in-the-world-2015/ |title=The Richest Women In The World 2015 |first=Kerry A. |last=Dolan |work=[[Forbes]] |date=March 2, 2015 |access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref> Her net worth was estimated to $41.7 billion in March 2015,<ref name=forbes-2015 /> the bulk of which came from her shares in Walmart, but also from [[First Solar]], in which her husband had invested. In November 2015, an analysis of court documents by [[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]] revealed that the bulk of her husband's wealth had been transferred to her son, [[Lukas Walton]], so that her own net worth was "only" about {{Currency|5|USD}} billion.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-11-06/a-wal-mart-heir-is-27-billion-poorer-than-everyone-calculated |title=A Wal-Mart Heir Is $27 Billion Poorer Than Everyone Thought |publisher=[[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]] |date=November 6, 2015 |access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
Profiled by [[Condé Nast]] Portfolio magazine in The Giving Index, she is ranked as the highest female philanthropist according to the amount she gives as a percentage of her wealth. At a then estimated $16.3 billion net worth, she contributed a total of $3.5 billion cumulatively between 2002 and 2006.<ref name="FOP">[http://www.facesofphilanthropy.com/christy-walton Christy Walton] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200324173904/http://www.facesofphilanthropy.com/christy-walton |date=March 24, 2020 }}. Faces of Philanthropy, accessed December 15, 2010.</ref>

[[Non-profit organization]]s in which Walton is actively serving include the national association of trustees and staff, corporate giving officers, and individual donors of [[Philanthropy Roundtable|The Philanthropy Roundtable]]. [[San Diego Natural History Museum|The San Diego Natural History Museum]], where she is a board member, as well as [[Zoological Society of San Diego|the San Diego Zoological Society]] and the [[Mingei International Museum]] are also institutions to which she makes donations.<ref name="FOP"/> In 2006, Walton donated her own old [[Victorian architecture|Victorian]] home which was built in 1896 for former [[National City, California|National City]], [[California]] postmaster Oliver Noyes and is of historical significance, to the [http://www.icfdn.org International Community Foundation - Center for Cross-Border Philanthropy]. Since her donation, she has endowed $4&nbsp;million towards the edifice's preservation.<ref name="FOP"/>

Additionally, she supports her family's own charitable foundation, the Walton Family Charitable Support Foundation, which prioritizes education and benefits institutions of higher education such as the [[Sam M. Walton College of Business]] at the [[University of Arkansas]], and several other colleges, community trusts, universities and foundations. In 2007, her family's foundation donated as much as $1.6&nbsp;billion.<ref name="FOP"/>

''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' reported on March 2, 2009<ref>Wal-Mart's Walton books film debut [https://www.variety.com/article/VR1118000735?refCatId=13] retrieved December 30, 2011</ref> that Christy Walton had set up a production company called Tenaja Productions to finance a film adaptation of ''[[Bless Me, Ultima]],'' a popular Chicano coming of age novel. Filming wrapped in Santa Fe, New Mexico in late 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://talent.filmindependent.org/filmmakers/sarah-dileo/|title=Film Independent TALENT|website=talent.filmindependent.org|access-date=April 5, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171117122834/http://talent.filmindependent.org/filmmakers/sarah-dileo/|archive-date=November 17, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> The film premiered at the [[Plaza Theatre (El Paso)|Plaza Theatre]] in [[El Paso, Texas]]  on September 17, 2012,<ref>María Cortés González, [https://www.abqjournal.com/115377/ultima-movie-slated-to-premiere-in-el-paso.html "'Bless Me, Ultima' premiere in El Paso is Plaza Theatre's first in 63 years"], ''[[El Paso Times]]'', September 18, 2012.</ref> and received a general release in February 2013.<ref>Ana Gershanik, [http://blog.nola.com/new_orleans/2013/02/sarah_dileo_of_new_orleans_pro.html "Sarah DiLeo of New Orleans produces first film, 'Bless Me, Ultima,' opening Feb. 22"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130225024332/http://blog.nola.com/new_orleans/2013/02/sarah_dileo_of_new_orleans_pro.html |date=February 25, 2013 }}, ''[[Times-Picayune]]'', February 21, 2013.</ref>

==Politics==
Walton backed [[The Lincoln Project]], an anti-Trump super-PAC, which ran ads in battleground states.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/christy-walton/?sh=446456e72a60 |title=Christy Walton |date=11 November 2020 |work=Forbes}}</ref> She donated $20,000 to ''The Lincoln Project'' in January 2020 and a further $10,000 in May 2020.<ref>{{Cite news |url= https://www.forbes.com/sites/michelatindera/2020/07/15/six-billionaires-join-christy-walton-to-support-anti-trump-republican-group-that-sparked-trump-twitter-tirades/?sh=7bc678ea6ef0 |title= Six More Billionaires Donate To The Anti-Trump Lincoln Project |date=15 July 2020 |work=Forbes}}</ref>
