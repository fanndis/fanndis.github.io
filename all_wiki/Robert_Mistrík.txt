'''Robert Mistrík''' ({{IPA-sk|'rɔbɛrt 'mistriːk}}, born 13 August 1966) is a [[Slovaks|Slovak]] chemist, scientist, businessman and politician.

==Early life==

Robert Mistrík was born on 13 August 1966 in [[Banská Bystrica]], where he spent his childhood. He attended the Gymnázium Jozefa Gregora Tajovského in Banská Bystrica and in 1991 graduated with a degree in Analytical Chemistry from the Faculty of Chemical Technology of the [[Slovak Technical University]] in [[Bratislava]]. He completed a PhD at the [[University of Vienna]] in 1994.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mistrík, Robert |url=https://usearch.univie.ac.at/primo_library/libweb/action/dlDisplay.do?vid=UWI&docId=UWI_alma21205454570003332&fn=permalink |website=University of Vienna |accessdate=6 February 2019}}</ref> He continued his scientific career as a visiting scholar at the [[National Institute of Standards and Technology]] in [[Gaithersburg, Maryland]].

==Career==

In 1998 Mistrík founded the mass spectrometry, metabolomics and chemical analysis firm HighChem, which he still runs today.<ref>{{cite web |title=HighChem chce zmapovat chemiu človeka|url=https://www.etrend.sk/trend-archiv/rok-2015/cislo-37/highchem-chce-zmapovat-chemiu-cloveka.html |website=HighChem |accessdate=6 February 2019}}</ref> He served as a member of the scientific committee at the METACancer consortium, aimed at investigating biomarkers for breast cancer.<ref>{{cite web |title=info|url=http://www.metacancer-fp7.eu/index.php?id=363 |website=METACancer |accessdate=6 February 2019}}</ref> He serves on the board of directors of the Metabolomics Society.<ref>{{cite web |title=12TH ANNUAL CONFERENCE OF THE METABOLOMICS SOCIETY |url=http://metabolomics2016.org/images/2017-metabolomics-programme.pdf |website=Metabolomics Society |accessdate=6 February 2019}}</ref>

==Political career==

Robert Mistrík was one of the co-founders of the centre-right [[Freedom and Solidarity]] party,<ref>{{cite web |title=Register of Political Parties |url=http://ives.minv.sk/rez/registre/pages/detailps.aspx?id=153180 |website=Ministry of the Interior of the Slovak Republic |accessdate=6 February 2019}}</ref> of which he was a member until 2012. On 15 May 2018 he announced his candidacy for the [[2019 Slovak presidential election]],<ref>{{cite news |title=Researcher Robert Mistrík will run for president |url=https://spectator.sme.sk/c/20827038/researcher-robert-mistrik-will-run-for-president.html |accessdate=6 February 2019 |work=The Slovak Spectator |date=16 May 2018}}</ref> but later withdrew it.
