"'''Watch n' Learn'''" is a song recorded by [[Barbadian people|Barbadian]] recording artist [[Rihanna]], for her sixth studio album ''[[Talk That Talk (Rihanna album)|Talk That Talk]]'' (2011). It was written by [[Priscilla Renea]], [[Hit-Boy|Chauncey Hollis]], Rihanna and Alja Jackson. The production was done by Hollis under his stage-name [[Hit-Boy]]. When Renea came with an idea and concept for the song, Hollis had already started working on the composition, without having in mind any particular artist. With the work on the track being finished, it was forwarded to Rihanna and her label, which eagerly accepted it.

"Watch n' Learn" is a [[dancehall]] song with prominent [[Reggae music|reggae]] characteristics. The song's instrumentation uses finger-snapping, [[percussions]], background clicks, [[synths]] and "island-flavored" [[drum beat]].<ref name="time"/><ref name="popdust"/><ref name="allmusic"/> Lyrically, it is about a woman who teaches her partner how to love her in the right way, throughout which she uses [[Sexual intercourse|sexual]] references. "Watch n' Learn" received predominantly positive reviews from music critics, with many of them praising its composition and sound. Upon the release of ''Talk That Talk'', the song debuted at number 80 on the singles chart in South Korea.

== Background and development ==
[[File:Vista aérea de Times Square desde el Empire State Building.jpg|thumb|left|upright|One of the recording locations of the song were Roc the Mic Studios in [[New York City]] (''pictured'').]]
"Watch n' Learn" was written by [[Priscilla Renea]], [[Hit-Boy|Chauncey Hollis]], [[Rihanna]] and Alja Jackson. The production of the song was helmed by Hollis under his stage-name Hit-Boy.<ref name="Notes">{{cite AV media notes|others=Rihanna|title=[[Talk That Talk (Rihanna album)|Talk That Talk]]|year=2011|type= inlay cover|publisher= [[The Island Def Jam Music Group]]|page=9}}</ref> Hollis had previously worked with music artists like [[Lil Wayne]], [[The Throne]], [[Eminem]] and [[Jennifer Lopez]].<ref name="hitboy interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1674237/rihanna-talk-that-talk-watch-learn-hit-boy.jhtml|title=Rihanna 'Killed It' On Talk That Talk's 'Watch N' Learn'|publisher=[[MTV News]]. [[Viacom (2005–present)|Viacom]]|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|date=November 11, 2011|accessdate=March 28, 2012}}</ref> "Watch n' Learn" wasn't originally meant for any particular artist when Hit-Boy started to work on it. In an interview with Jocelyn Vena from [[MTV News]] he explained: "I was at the studio with this amazing [songwriter] Priscilla Renea, and I was going to tell her to go home, but I was like, 'Stay here and see if you could come up with an idea for this [the song]'".<ref name="hitboy interview"/> Renea previously penned Rihanna's 2011 single "[[California King Bed]]" (''[[Loud (Rihanna album)|Loud]]'', 2010).<ref name="buzzworthy">{{cite web|url=http://buzzworthy.mtv.com/2011/11/21/rihanna-talk-that-talk/|title=Rihanna's 'Talk That Talk': A Track-By-Track Guide To Her New Album|publisher=MTV. Viacom|last=Lansky|first=Sam|date=November 21, 2011|accessdate=March 28, 2012}}</ref> Hollis further explained that by the time he left the studio and came home, Renea already sent him the idea and concept for the song through [[IChat]]. He was eventually satisfied with it and sent the song to Rihanna, in which her team "went crazy" when they heard it.<ref name="hitboy interview"/> Rihanna then recorded the song for inclusion on her sixth studio album ''[[Talk That Talk (Rihanna album)|Talk That Talk]]'' (2011).<ref name="Notes"/>

In the same interview for the publication, Hollis explained the title of the song: "It's called 'Watch n' Learn,' and people will understand what it's about once it comes out. It's a very sexy record, but it's fun. It's [got] great melodies; it's catchy; the beat knocks. It's so many different elements to it."<ref name="hitboy interview"/> When asked how is he satisfied with Rihanna's vocals and his prediction on the song he further stated: "She definitely murdered it vocally. I was just at the studio last week listening to a mix of it, and man, she killed it. I really feel like, this song, it's going to be big. I'm really excited about it."<ref name="hitboy interview"/> "Watch n' Learn" was recorded at Roc the Mic Studios in [[New York City]] and at Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg in [[Paris]]. The song was mixed by Marcos Tovar. Its vocal production was completed by [[Kuk Harrell]] and assisted by TT. and Jennifer Rosales.<ref name="Notes"/>

== Critical reception ==
[[File:BEYONCE CONCERT IN CENTRAL PARK 2011 Good Morning America's Summer Concert Series - Central Park, Manhattan NYC - 070111 cropped.jpg|thumb|left|upright|alt=A picture of a blonde woman wearing a yellow outfit performing.|Jon Caramanica from ''[[The New York Times]]'' linked "Watch n' Learn" to [[Beyoncé]]'s 2011 single "[[Party (Beyoncé song)|Party]]"]]
Chris Coplan of Consequence of Sound called "Watch n' Learn" a "reggae jam" and further concluded that "low-key Rihanna, without heaps of easily identifiable help or loads of over-saturated gimmicks, is still a knockout".<ref name="consequence"/> Matthew Horton of [[Virgin Media]] called the song a "wonderfully sunny tribal disco" track with a thematics regarding [[oral sex]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.virginmedia.com/music/reviews/rihanna-talk-that-talk.php|title=Talk That Talk&nbsp;— Rihanna|publisher=[[Virgin Media]]|last=Horton|first=Matthew|date=November 21, 2011|accessdate=March 29, 2012}}</ref> In a review of ''Talk That Talk'', Mesfin Fekadu of ''[[The Boston Globe]]'' stated that Rihanna is "raunchy" on the new album and it works. Regarding the song he further commented that she's [Rihanna] schooling her man in the bedroom on the fun "Watch N' Learn.".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2011/11/21/review_rihanna_successfully_borrows_from_herself/|title=Review: Rihanna successfully borrows from herself|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|publisher=[[The New York Times Company]]|last=Fekadu|first=Mesfin|date=November 21, 2011|accessdate=March 29, 2012}}</ref> Giovanny Caquais of CultureBlues concluded that the track "strips away" from the [[Techno music|techno]] and [[David Guetta]] influences which according to him the album is "drowning in". He further thought that "sassy women will absolutely love to sing [the song] in their cars."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cultureblues.com/2011/11/album-review-talk-that-talk-rihanna/|title=Album Review: 'Talk That Talk'&nbsp;– Rihanna|publisher=CultureBlues|last=Caquais|first=Giovanny|date=November 30, 2011|accessdate=March 29, 2012}}</ref> Maz Hallima of Flavour Magazine positively reviewed "Watch n' Learn", considering her favorite song from the album together with "[[Cockiness (Love It)]]" and "[[You da One]]". Hallima thought that the song has Rihanna's signature sound and wrote she "really enjoyed the contrast between the light beat and her husky voice&nbsp;– pure catchiness."<ref name="flavour"/>

Glenn Gamboa of ''[[Newsweek]]'' considered "Watch n' Learn" an "answer to [[Janet Jackson]]'s "[[Doesn't Really Matter]]" for its similar production and "playful" nature.<ref>{{cite web 2011|accessdate=November 15, 2014}}</ref> ''[[Los Angeles Times]]''’ Randal Roberts while reviewing the song stated: Rihanna "reels off her carnal intentions with an impressive though not entirely believable candor."<ref name="LATIMES"/> Julianne Shepherd of ''Spin'' called the track Rihanna's game in which there is a very little chance for winning&nbsp;— "but she'd love it if you tried".<ref name="Spin"/> Andy Kellman of [[Allmusic]] commended the melody of the song and noted that "Watch n' Learn" is more unique than Hit-Boy's work on Kanye West and [[Jay-Z]]'s 2011 single "[[Niggas in
