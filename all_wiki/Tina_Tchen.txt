'''Christina M. "Tina" Tchen''' (born January 25, 1956) is an [[United States|American]] [[lawyer]] and CEO of [[Time's Up (movement)|Time's Up]]. Her work centers on issues related to [[gender inequity]], [[sexual harassment]], and lack of diversity in the workplace.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buckleyfirm.com/people/tina-tchen|title=Tina Tchen|date=2017-09-11|website=Buckley LLP|language=en|access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Tchen was born in [[Columbus, Ohio|Columbus]], [[Ohio]]<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.americanbar.org/news/abanews/aba-news-archives/2018/07/chicago_lawyer_tina/|title=Chicago lawyer Tina M. Tchen to receive ABA Margaret Brent Award|website=www.americanbar.org|language=en|access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref> on January 25, 1956 to Chinese immigrants who fled the [[People's Republic of China]] in 1949. Her father worked as a [[psychiatrist]] and her mother a scientist.<ref name="profiles">Who Runs Gov. [http://www.whorunsgov.com/Profiles/Christina_M._Tchen "Profiles: Christina M. Tchen"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090820090158/http://www.whorunsgov.com/Profiles/Christina_M._Tchen |date=2009-08-20 }}, WhoRunsGov.com, accessed August 2, 2009.</ref> She grew up in [[Beachwood, Ohio]], graduating from [[Beachwood High School]]. She graduated from [[Radcliffe College]] of [[Harvard University]] in 1978,<ref name="Skiba" /> and received her [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] degree in 1984 from [[Northwestern University]] School of Law.

==Career==
Tchen worked for several years{{how many|date=October 2019}} for the [[Illinois]] Bureau of the Budget where she served as the Governor's budget analyst for the Department of Children and Family Services. In 1988, Tchen began as an associate at [[Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom]], later becoming a partner at the firm in 1992, where she specialized in litigation in the [[United States federal courts|federal courts]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/christina-m-tchen/gIQAEgiS9O_topic.html|title=Christina M. Tchen|date=2012-11-16|website=Washington Post|language=EN|access-date=2018-11-13}}</ref><ref name="profiles"/><ref name="chief">{{cite web|url=http://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2011/01/05/tina-tchen-join-office-first-lady-chief-staff|title=Tina Tchen to Join Office of the First Lady as Chief of Staff|date=2011-01-05|publisher=The White House Office of the First Lady|location=Washington, DC|accessdate=2011-01-05}}</ref> In 1992, she argued on behalf of the State of Illinois in front of the [[Supreme Court of the United States]] in ''Artist M. v. Suter'', which helped reform the state's foster care program by closing a [[loophole]].<ref name="profiles" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.skadden.com/index.cfm?contentID=45&bioID=721|title=Christina M Tchen - Skadden, Arps|date=2008-12-31|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081231013953/http://www.skadden.com/index.cfm?contentID=45&bioID=721|access-date=2019-10-24|archive-date=2008-12-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oyez.org/cases/1991/90-1488|title=Suter v. Artist M.|last=|first=|date=|website=Oyez|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref>

During President Obama's campaign in 2008, Tchen was one of his biggest fundraisers, raising $200,000.<ref name="profiles" /> From [[First inauguration of Barack Obama|Obama's inauguration]] in 2009 until January 5, 2011, she was the Director of the [[White House Office of Public Engagement and Intergovernmental Affairs|White House Office of Public Engagement]], previously known as the Office of Public Liaison.<ref name="chief" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2009/05/18/engagement-women-health-care-and-yarn|title=Engagement, Women, Health Care, and Yarn|date=2009-05-18|publisher=The White House Office of Public Engagement|location=Washington, DC|accessdate=2011-09-15}}</ref> From 2011 until 2017, she served as Assistant to [[President of the United States|President]] [[Barack Obama]]; Chief of Staff to [[First Lady of the United States|First Lady]] [[Michelle Obama]]; and Executive Director of the [[White House Council on Women and Girls]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aaa-fund.com/?p=6571|title=Tina Tchen to become Michelle Obama's Chief of Staff|date=2011-01-05|publisher=Asian American Action Fund|location=Washington, DC|accessdate=2011-01-05}}</ref><ref name="chief" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/eop/cwg/who/leadership|title=Council on Women and Girls: Leadership|date=2009-10-01|publisher=The White House Council on Women and Girls|location=Washington, DC|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111001194414/http://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/eop/cwg/who/leadership|archivedate=2011-10-01|accessdate=2011-09-15}}</ref>

In 2017, she became a partner in the law firm [[Buckley Sandler|Buckley Sander]] where she led the workplace culture practice and was head of the firm's Chicago office.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/06/arts/music/grammys-diversity-task-force-tina-tchen.html|title=Time's Up Co-Founder Will Head Recording Academy Task Force on Diversity|last=Sisario|first=Ben|date=2018-03-06|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-10-24|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref name="Zraick 2019" /><ref name=":0" /> In 2018, she was one of the co-founders of [[Time's Up (movement)|Time's Up]] and lead its [[legal defense fund]] that connected victims of sexual harassment with lawyers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/01/01/movies/times-up-hollywood-women-sexual-harassment.html|title=Powerful Hollywood Women Unveil Anti-Harassment Action Plan|last=Buckley|first=Cara|date=2018-01-01|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-10-24|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> The fund has raised more than $24 million by 2019 and has connected more than 3,600 workers in various industries to legal support for sexual harassment cases.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2019/10/07/tina-tchen-will-become-president-and-chief-executive-of-times-up.html|title=Tina Tchen will become president and chief executive of Time's Up|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|accessdate=November 1, 2019}}</ref>

In March 2019, Tchen was hired as an adviser to investigate the [[Southern Poverty Law Center|Southern Poverty Law Center's]] (SPLC) "workplace culture" after allegations of sexual and racial harassment led to the firing of the SPLC's co-founder and resignation of its president.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/03/25/us/morris-dees-leaves-splc.html|title=Roiled by Staff Uproar, Civil Rights Group Looks at Intolerance Within|last1=Burch|first1=Audra D. S.|date=2019-03-25|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-03-27|last2=Blinder|first2=Alan|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331|last3=Eligon|first3=John}}</ref><ref name="Zraick 2019" /> Also that year, she was named chair of the [[The Recording Academy|Recording Academy's]] new task force for inclusion and diversity following [[Neil Portnow]] and [[Kenneth Ehrlich|Ken Ehrlich's]] disparaging comments about women in the music industry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/therecord/2018/03/06/591213252/times-up-leader-tina-tchen-to-head-recording-academy-s-diversity-task-force|title=Time's Up Leader Tina Tchen To Head Recording Academy's Diversity Task Force|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref>

On October 7, 2019, Tchen was appointed chief executive officer of [[Time's Up (movement)|Time's Up]].<ref name="Zraick 2019">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/10/07/arts/tina-tchen-times-up.html|title=Tina Tchen, Ex-Obama Aide Will Take Over Time's Up|last=Zraick|first=Karen|date=October 7, 2019|website=The New York Times|language=en}}</ref>
