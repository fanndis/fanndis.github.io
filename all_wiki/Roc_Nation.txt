'''Roc Nation, LLC''' is an entertainment agency founded by rapper [[Jay-Z]] in 2008. The company has offices in [[New York City]], [[London]], [[Nashville]] and [[Los Angeles]]. The full-service entertainment company houses a record label, talent agency, philanthropy, school, apparel, touring and concerts production company.

==History ==
[[File:Jay-Z @ Shawn 'Jay-Z' Carter Foundation Carnival 02.jpg|thumb|right|[[Jay-Z]], founder of Roc Nation]]
In 2008, Roc Nation was founded by [[Jay-Z]]. The firm has a multi-year partnership with [[Live Nation]].

In June 2009, Roc Nation signed a four-year distribution deal with [[Sony Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/268241/jay-z-confirms-roc-nation-deals-with-sony-atlantic|title=Jay-Z confirms Roc Nation deal with Sony |publisher=Universal Music |date=2013-04-08 |accessdate=2014-09-03}}</ref> 

In 2010, Roc Nation partnered with London-based entertainment company Three Six Zero Group.<ref>{{cite web|title=Three Six Zero signs Roc Nation deal|url=http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/three-six-zero-signs-roc-nation-deal/044666|work=Music Week|publisher=Music Week|accessdate=14 March 2013}}</ref> 

In February 2013, Roc Nation signed a worldwide music publishing administration deal with [[Warner Chappell Music|Warner/Chappell Music]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/global/1549317/jay-z-roc-nation-sign-global-publishing-administration-deals-with |title=Roc Nation signs publishing administration deal with Warner/Chappell |publisher=Billboard |date=2013-02-19|accessdate=2014-04-02}}</ref> 

In April 2013, Roc Nation formed a new sports management division, [[Roc Nation Sports]], a subsidiary dedicated to sports representation for professional athletes.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/04/03/sports/new-jay-z-lyrics-for-athletes-please-let-me-represent-you.html?hp&_r=0 |title=With New Move, Jay-Z Enters a Sports Agent State of Mind |work=The New York Times |date=2013-04-02|accessdate=2013-04-02}}</ref> Shortly thereafter, Roc Nation Sports also launched a boxing promotion division.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://sportsagentblog.com/2015/02/12/roc-nation-sports-signs-6-new-boxers/ |title=Roc Nation Sports Signs 6 New Boxers |publisher=Sports Agent Blog |date=2015-02-12|accessdate=2015-04-17}}</ref> After the Sony deal expired, in April 2013, Roc Nation signed a multi-year partnership with [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.universalmusic.com/corporate/detail/2471 |title=Universal Music Group strikes global partnership with Roc Nation |publisher=Universal Music |date=2013-04-08 |accessdate=2014-09-03}}</ref> 

In February 2015, Roc Nation and Three Six Zero Group announced the formation of Three Six Zero Entertainment, a division representing clients in film, television and the literary arts.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6472674/jay-z-roc-nation-three-six-zero-entertainment-film-and-television |title=Jay Z's Roc Nation and Three Six Zero Partner to Launch Film and Television Management Arm |publisher=Billboard |date=2015-02-12|accessdate=2015-04-17}}</ref>

In November 2016, the administrator of [[Prince (musician)|Prince]]'s estate authorized a copyright lawsuit against Roc Nation for putting 15 of Prince's albums up on their affiliated streaming service [[Tidal (service)|Tidal]].<ref>{{cite magazine | url = http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/thr-esq/jay-zs-roc-nation-sued-streaming-prince-albums-tidal-947794 | title = Jay Z's Roc Nation Sued for Streaming Prince Albums on Tidal | date=16 November 2016 | accessdate = 17 November 2016 | magazine = The Hollywood Reporter | author = Eriq Gardner}}</ref>

In beginning of March 2017, Roc Nation announced the formation of Arrive, a new division of the company that will focus on investing and nurturing promising startups.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jay-z-announces-new-startup-company-arrive-124700/|title=Jay Z Announces New Startup Company, Arrive|first1=Daniel|last1=Kreps|date=6 March 2017|publisher=|accessdate=9 February 2019}}</ref>

In July 2017, Roc Nation collaborated with art collective MSFTSrep.

In 2018, Roc Nation briefly supported Arliss Network which is its competitor that later released www.arlissnet.blogspot.com .

In August 2019, Roc Nation announced a long-term partnership with the National Football League. The company will be the live music entertainment strategist for the league and will focus on enhancing the league's live game day experience, as well as augment social justice efforts through the Inspire Change initiative.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nfl.com/news/story/0ap3000001041162/article/jayzs-roc-nation-entering-partnership-with-nfl|title=Jay-Z's Roc Nation entering partnership with NFL|website=NFL.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-14}}</ref>

In the year of 2019, it was reported that Jay-Z worked out a music deal for former mentor [[Jaz-O]]'s Kingz Kounty Media Group to distributed through Roc Nation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://onsmash.com/music/jay-z-signs-jaz-o-to-roc-nation/|title=JAY-Z Signs Jaz-O to Roc Nation after Ending Beef|website=onsmashmusic.com|author=VIRAL MVP|date=July 9, 2019}}</ref>

Roc Nation produced a television docuseries ''[[Free Meek]]'' about [[Meek Mill]]'s ongoing battle with the U.S. justice system following a disputed conviction in 2007. The five-part series premiered August 9, 2019, on [[Amazon Prime Video]],<ref>{{cite news |last1=Langmann |first1=Brady |title=Inside the Relentless Private Investigation That Granted Meek Mill a New Trial |url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/music/a28646949/free-meek-mill-amazon-documentary-true-story/ |accessdate=August 14, 2019 |work=Esquire |date=August 11, 2019}}</ref> and was executive produced by Meek Mill and Jay-Z.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Berman |first1=Judy |title='Free Meek' Brings Meek Mill's Long Legal Nightmare to Life |url=https://time.com/5638254/free-meek-mill-amazon-review/ |accessdate=August 14, 2019 |work=Time |date=July 31, 2019 |language=en}}</ref> In 2020, Roc Nation also produced the [[Super Bowl LIV halftime show]]<ref>{{cite web |last1=Crucchiola |first1=Jordan |title=An NFL Partnership Makes Jay-Z a Producer for the Super Bowl Halftime Show |url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/08/jay-z-signs-nfl-deal-super-bowl-halftime-show.html |website=Vulture |accessdate=February 1, 2020 |language=en-us |date=August 13, 2019}}</ref> and co-produced the [[BBC]] television drama series ''[[Noughts + Crosses]]'' with [[Participant (company)|Participant]] and [[ITV Studios]]' [[Mammoth Screen]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nme.com/news/tv/jay-zs-roc-nation-produce-tv-adaptation-malorie-blackmans-noughts-crosses-2386967|title=Jay Z's Roc Nation to produce TV adaptation of Malorie Blackman's 'Noughts and Crosses'|date=5 October 2018|work=NME|access-date=9 October 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2020, Roc Nation partnered with Long Island University in Brooklyn to begin a program called, Roc Nation School For Music, Sports & Entertainment.

In October 2020, Roc Nation released a compilation album titled ''Reprise'', benefiting various social justice initiatives across the US.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/roc-nation-reprise-compilation-album|title=Listen to Roc Nation's 'Reprise' Compilation f/ Ty Dolla Sign, Jorja Smith, Vic Mensa, Chronixx, and More|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Price|first=Joe|date=October 19, 2020|accessdate=October 11, 2020}}</ref>
