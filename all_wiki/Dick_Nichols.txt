'''Richard Dale Nichols''' (April 29, 1926 – March 7, 2019) was an American banker and politician who served [[one-term congressmen|one-term]] as the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. Representative]] from [[Kansas's 5th congressional district | the 5th Congressional District of Kansas]] .

==Life and career==
Born in [[Fort Scott, Kansas]], Nichols attended the public schools. He earned his B.S. from [[Kansas State University]] in 1951, after serving as an ensign in the [[United States Navy]] from 1944 to 1947.

Nichols was informational counsel to the Kansas State Board of Agriculture, served as associate farm director of radio and television stations in [[Topeka, Kansas]], and was agricultural representative of a bank in [[Hutchinson, Kansas]]. From 1969 until he resigned in 1990 after being elected to Congress, Nichols served as president and chairman of the board of Home State Bank in [[McPherson, Kansas]].<ref>https://rotarydistrict5670.org/stories/sen--moran-pays-tribute-to-congressman-dick-nichols</ref>

He served as a member of the Kansas State Republican Executive Committee, was a delegate to the 1988 Republican National Convention, and was the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] chair for the Fifth Congressional District from 1986 to 1990.

In July 1986, Nichols and his wife were stabbed by an insane man aboard the [[Staten Island Ferry]] while a touring [[New York City]]. He fully recovered from his wounds and was visited by Mayor [[Ed Koch]] in the hospital.<ref>{{cite web|title=MAN WITH SWORD KILLS 2 AND WOUNDS 9 ON S.I. FERRY|date=July 8, 1986|last=McFadden|first=Robert D.|work=New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1986/07/08/nyregion/man-with-sword-kills-2-and-wounds-9-on-si-ferry.html?pagewanted=all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Nichols first speaker in series|date=October 24, 2011|work=McPherson Sentinel|url=https://www.mcphersonsentinel.com/article/20111024/NEWS/310249974}}</ref><ref name="Wichita_Eagle"/>

Nichols was elected as a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] to the [[102nd United States Congress|One Hundred Second Congress]] (January 3, 1991 – January 3, 1993), representing [[Kansas's 5th congressional district]]. He narrowly beat future [[Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|FDIC]] Chairwoman [[Sheila Bair]] in a 6-way Republican primary.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=412575|title=KS District 5 - R Primary - August 7, 1990|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=March 9, 2019}}</ref> In the reapportionment following the [[United States Census, 1990|1990 Census]], the size of Kansas' congressional delegation was reduced from 5 to 4, eliminating the 5th district. In 1992, Rep. Nichols ran for the Republican nomination to challenge [[Dan Glickman]] in the [[Kansas's 4th congressional district|4th congressional district]], but lost in the primary to state Senator Eric R. Yost, who lost to Glickman in the general election.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=362267|title=KS District 4 - R Primary - August 4, 1992|publisher=Our Campaigns|accessdate=March 9, 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Nichols married Connie Weinbrenner in 1951, and together would have three children. Connie earned four degrees and was a professor at [[McPherson College]] before her death from cancer in 1994. Two years later, Nichols married his second wife, Linda.<ref name="Wichita_Eagle">{{cite web|title=Dick Nichols, Kansas' last Fifth District congressman, 'put service to others above self'|date=March 9, 2019|last=Finger|first=Stan|work=Wichita Eagle|accessdate=March 9, 2019|url=https://www.kansas.com/news/politics-government/article227341179.html}}</ref>

Dick Nichols died at his home in [[McPherson, Kansas]] on March 7, 2019 at the age of 92.<ref>{{cite web |title=Richard D. Nichols |url=https://www.salina.com/obituaries/20190308/richard-d-nichols |website=Salina Journal |accessdate=9 March 2019 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="Wichita_Eagle"/>
