'''Liane Moriarty''' (born 1966) is an [[Australian people|Australian]] [[author]]. She has written eight novels, including the ''[[New York Times]]'' best seller ''[[Big Little Lies (novel)|Big Little Lies]]'', which was adapted into a television series for HBO.   

==Career==
After leaving school, Moriarty worked in advertising and marketing at a legal publishing company. She then ran her own company for a while before taking work as a freelance advertising copywriter. In 2004, after obtaining a master's degree at Macquarie University in Sydney her first novel ''Three Wishes'', written as part of the degree, was published.<ref name="LianeMoriarty">{{cite web |url=http://lianemoriarty.com.au/index.php |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-07-17 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711140359/http://lianemoriarty.com.au/index.php |archivedate=2011-07-11 }}</ref>

She has published seven further novels.

Her novel ''[[Big Little Lies (novel)|Big Little Lies]]'' has been adapted into [[Big Little Lies (TV series)|a television series]] by [[HBO]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/news/reese-witherspoon-nicole-kidman-hbo-big-little-lies-1201490902/|title=HBO Orders Reese Witherspoon-Nicole Kidman Limited Series ‘Big Little Lies’|last=Littleton|first=Cynthia|date=2015-05-08|work=Variety|access-date=2017-09-19|language=en-US}}</ref> and stars [[Reese Witherspoon]], [[Nicole Kidman]], [[Shailene Woodley]], [[Laura Dern]], [[Zoë Kravitz]], and [[Alexander Skarsgård]]. The series premiered on 19 February 2017, with the first season concluding on 2 April 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://deadline.com/2016/11/big-little-lies-premiere-date-hbo-liane-moriarty-david-e-kelley-reese-witherspoon-1201860563/|title=‘Big Little Lies’ From David E. Kelley Gets Premiere Date On HBO|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=2016-11-28|work=Deadline|access-date=2017-09-19|language=en-US}}</ref> A second season was announced in December 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/12/08/arts/television/big-little-lies-second-season-hbo.html|title=‘Big Little Lies’ to Return for a Second Season|last=Koblin|first=John|date=2017-12-08|work=The New York Times|access-date=2017-12-30|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Season two premiered in June 2019.

A previous novel, ''[[The Husband's Secret]]'', is being adapted into a film. In September 2013, [[CBS Films]] acquired the rights to the novel.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cbs-films-adapt-husbands-secret-620748|title=CBS Films to Adapt 'The Husband's Secret' for Big Screen|last=Ng|first=Philiana|date=2013-09-04|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en}}</ref> In May 2017, it was announced that the film will star [[Blake Lively]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/blake-lively-husbands-secret-liane-moriarty-1202444822/ |title=Blake Lively to Star in Thriller ‘The Husband’s Secret’ From ‘Big Little Lies’ Author |last=Kroll |first=Justin |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=2017-05-25 |access-date=2018-07-16}}</ref>

Her novel ''[[Nine Perfect Strangers]]'' is being adapted into a television series by Hulu, starring and produced by [[Nicole Kidman]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/nicole-kidman-drama-nine-perfect-strangers-big-little-lies-team-lands-at-hulu-1206234|title=Nicole Kidman, 'Big Little Lies' Team Set Drama Series at Hulu|last=Porter|first=Rick|date=2019-05-01|work=Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2019-07-03|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Moriarty lives in [[Sydney]] with her husband, Adam, a former farmer from [[Tasmania]] who worked in agricultural marketing, and two children, George and Anna.<ref name="Bearup2014">{{cite news|last1=Bearup|first1=Greg|title=Big little lives|url=https://www.theaustralian.com.au/arts/books/liane-moriarty-the-bestselling-novelist-who-flies-under-the-radar/news-story/36223d405130a023efc125b8ea8c9565?nk=010006b349b78e3a05d70b2939623848-1491256696|accessdate=3 April 2017|work=[[The Australian]]|date=12 December 2014}}</ref><ref name="Hooton2016">{{cite news|last1=Hooton|first1=Amanda|title=How Sydney author Liane Moriarty sold six million books and inspired an HBO series|url=https://www.smh.com.au/good-weekend/how-sydney-author-liane-moriarty-sold-six-million-books-and-inspired-an-hbo-series-20160701-gpw7ul.html|accessdate=3 April 2017|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=15 July 2016}}</ref> She is the older sister of authors [[Jaclyn Moriarty]] and [[Nicola Moriarty]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.penguin.com.au/authors/nicola-moriarty|title=Nicola Moriarty|website=www.penguin.com.au}}</ref><ref name="LianeMoriarty" />
