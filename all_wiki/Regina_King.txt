'''Regina Rene King''' (born January 15, 1971)<ref name=tvg>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/regina-king/bio/171160|title=Regina King Biography at|magazine=[[TV Guide]]|accessdate=March 21, 2013|archivedate=September 10, 2015 |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20150910172033/https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/regina-king/bio/171160/|url-status=dead}}</ref><!--site is live but birthdate appears only in archived version--> is an American actress and director. She is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Regina King|several awards]], including an [[Academy Award]], a [[Golden Globe Award]], and four acting [[Primetime Emmy Award]]s, the most for an African-American performer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sep. 20|first=Tracy Brown Digital Editor|last2=Pm|first2=20206:15|date=2020-09-21|title=Regina King ties record for most acting Emmys won by a Black performer|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/tv/story/2020-09-20/emmys-2020-regina-king-most-wins-black-actor-alfre-woodard|access-date=2020-09-21|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref> ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine named her one of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] in 2019.<ref name="Time100">{{cite news|first=Viola|last=Davis |title=Regina King |url=http://time.com/collection/100-most-influential-people-2019/5567860/regina-king |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|date=April 17, 2019| accessdate=April 17, 2019 }}</ref>

King first gained attention for her role in the television series ''[[227 (TV series)|227]]'' from 1985 to 1990. She rose to prominence with roles in the films ''[[Friday (1995 film)|Friday]]'' (1995) and ''[[Jerry Maguire]]'' (1996), and the crime television series ''[[Southland (TV series)|Southland]]'' (2009–2013). From 2015 to 2017, King starred in the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] anthology series ''[[American Crime (TV series)|American Crime]]'', for which she won two [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie|Primetime Emmy Awards]], and in 2018, she starred in the [[Netflix]] miniseries ''[[Seven Seconds (TV series)|Seven Seconds]]'', for which she won her third Emmy Award. Her role as a troubled mother in the 2018 film ''[[If Beale Street Could Talk (film)|If Beale Street Could Talk]]'' won her the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]] and the [[Golden Globe Award]] for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Best Supporting Actress]]. King won her fourth Emmy award for starring in the 2019 superhero television series ''[[Watchmen (TV series)|Watchmen]].''

King has also played supporting roles in the films ''[[Boyz n the Hood]]'' (1991), ''[[Poetic Justice (film)|Poetic Justice]]'' (1993), ''[[Legally Blonde 2: Red, White & Blonde]]'' (2003), ''[[Ray (film)|Ray]]'' (2004), and ''[[Miss Congeniality 2: Armed & Fabulous]]'' (2005). Her other television roles include the drama series ''[[The Leftovers (TV series)|The Leftovers]]'' from 2015 to 2017 and the sitcom ''[[The Big Bang Theory]]'' from 2013 to 2019.

King has directed episodes for several television shows, including ''[[Scandal (TV series)|Scandal]]'' in 2015 and 2016 and ''[[This Is Us]]'' in 2017. She has also directed the music video for the 2010 song "[[Finding My Way Back]]" by [[Jaheim]]. Her feature film directorial debut came with the drama ''[[One Night in Miami (film)|One Night in Miami]]'' (2020).

==Early life and education==
King was born  in [[Los Angeles]] and grew up in [[View Park-Windsor Hills, California|View Park-Windsor Hills]]. She is the eldest daughter of Gloria (Cain), a special education teacher, and Thomas King, an electrician.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/64/Regina-King.html|title=Regina King Biography (1971-)|website=www.filmreference.com|accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>Stated on ''[[Who Do You Think You Are? (U.S. TV series)|Who Do You Think You Are?]]'', December 17, 2018</ref> King's parents divorced in 1979.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800021532/bio|title=Regina King profile at Yahoo! Movies|publisher=|accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref> King's younger sister is former actress [[Reina King]], who appeared on ''[[What's Happening Now!!]]'' King attended [[Westchester High School (Los Angeles)|Westchester High School]]; graduating in 1988<ref>[http://www.classmates.com/siteui/yearbooks/2845?page=35 1988 Westchester High School Yearbook (Los Angeles, California)]</ref> and later attended the [[University of Southern California]].<ref>{{cite news |title=The scene stealer |url=https://www.vulture.com/2015/12/regina-king-hollywood-scene-stealer.html |accessdate=July 31, 2019 |publisher=Vulture}}</ref>

==Career==
King began her acting career in 1985 playing the role of Brenda Jenkins on the [[television]] series ''[[227 (TV series)|227]]'',<ref name=VanityFair>"It's Evening in America". ''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]''. May 2012. p. 155.</ref> a role she played until the show ended in 1990. She went on to appear in the [[John Singleton]] films ''[[Boyz n the Hood]]'', ''[[Poetic Justice (film)|Poetic Justice]]'' and ''[[Higher Learning]]''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.essence.com/2011/01/17/regina-kings-career-in-pictures|title=Regina King's Most Memorable Roles|work=Essence.com|access-date=October 24, 2017|language=en}}</ref> In 1995, she was featured in the hit comedy film ''[[Friday (1995 film)|Friday]]''.<ref name=":0" />  In 1996, she starred in the [[Martin Lawrence]] dark comedy-romance ''[[A Thin Line Between Love and Hate]]'' as Mia.<ref>{{cite news |title=A Thin Line Between Love and Hate |url=https://variety.com/1996/film/reviews/a-thin-line-between-love-and-hate-1200445703/ |accessdate=July 31, 2019 |work=Variety |date=March 31, 1996}}</ref> King gained fame starring in the 1996 blockbuster romantic comedy film ''[[Jerry Maguire]]'' as Marcee Tidwell.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web |title=Regina King Has So Many Stories to Tell |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/06/arts/television/regina-king-profile.html |first=Wesley|last=Morris|newspaper=The New York Times |date=March 6, 2016|accessdate=July 31, 2019}}</ref> She had a role in ''[[Enemy of the State (film)|Enemy of the State]]''<ref>{{cite news |title=Enemy of the State |url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/events/film/1998-11-20/142042/ |accessdate=July 31, 2019 |publisher=austinchronicle.com |date=November 20, 1998}}</ref> and was also featured in ''[[How Stella Got Her Groove Back]]'', ''[[Mighty Joe Young (1998 film)|Mighty Joe Young]]'', ''[[Down to Earth (2001 film)|Down to Earth]]'', ''[[Daddy Day Care]]'', ''[[Legally Blonde 2: Red, White & Blonde]]'', ''[[A Cinderella Story]]'', ''[[Ray (film)|Ray]]'' and ''[[Miss Congeniality 2: Armed and Fabulous]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Review: Miss Congeniality 2: Armed and Fabulous |url=https://www.slantmagazine.com/film/miss-congeniality-2-armed-and-fabulous/ |work=Slant magazine |accessdate=July 31, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0005093/|title=Regina King|website=IMDb|access-date=2018-10-15}}</ref>

Beginning in the mid-2000s, she had ongoing roles on several TV series, including acclaimed work as the voice of [[Huey Freeman|Huey]] and [[Riley Freeman]] for the animated series ''[[The Boondocks (TV series)|The Boondocks]]'' and Detective Lydia Adams on the [[TNT (U.S. TV network)|TNT]] [[police procedural|police drama]] ''[[Southland (TV series)|Southland]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title=Regina King is the queen of 'Southland' |url=https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/celebrities/regina-king-is-the-queen-of-southland-1.1781074 |accessdate=July 31, 2019 |work=Newsday |date=February 25, 2010}}</ref> She also appeared in roles on ''[[24 (TV series)|24]]'', ''[[The Big Bang Theory]]'', and ''[[Shameless (U.S. TV series)|Shameless]]''. In 2007 she played Lisa Moore in ''[[This Christmas (film)|This Christmas]]''. She was a cast member on [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s [[John Ridley]]-penned ensemble drama ''[[American Crime (TV series)|American Crime]],'' and played a devout member of the [[Nation of Islam]] and sister to a drug addict accused of murder.<ref name="American crime" /> 

In September 2015 and September 2016, King won the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie|Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or a Movie]] for ''American Crime''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/regina-king-if-beale-street-could-talk-1202598448/|title=Film News Roundup: Regina King Joins Barry Jenkins' 'If Beale Street Could Talk'|last=McNary|first=Dave|date=October 25, 2017|work=Variety|access-date=November 10, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="American crime">{{cite web |title=Aliyah Shadeed played by Regina King |url=https://abc.go.com/shows/american-crime/cast/aliyah-shadeed |publisher=ABC |accessdate=July 31, 2019}}</ref> In 2018, she played the role of the mother of a murdered black teenager in Netflix original series ''[[Seven Seconds (TV series)|Seven Seconds]]'', winning her third [[Primetime Emmy Award]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Regina King Lands Her Third Emmy; Portrays Mom Whose Son Is Killed By Police |first=Denise|last=Petski|url=https://deadline.com/2018/09/regina-wins-emmy-best-actress-limited-series-seven-seconds-1202465623/ |website=Deadline |date=September 17, 2018|accessdate=July 31, 2019}}</ref> Her performance in the 2018 film ''[[If Beale Street Could Talk (film)|If Beale Street Could Talk]]'', directed by [[Barry Jenkins]], garnered critical acclaim and earned her the [[Golden Globe Award]] and [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Regina King wins supporting actress Oscar for 'Beale Street' |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-awards-oscars-supportingactress/regina-king-wins-supporting-actress-oscar-for-beale-street-idUSKCN1QE02X |accessdate=July 31, 2019 |publisher=Reuters |date=February 24, 2019}}</ref>

In July 2019, it was announced King would direct ''[[One Night in Miami (film)|One Night in Miami]]'' based upon the [[One Night in Miami|play of the same name]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/07/regina-king-direct-adaptation-of-one-night-in-miami-kemp-powers-1202643921/|title=Regina King To Direct Adaptation Of ''One Night In Miami''|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|first=Dino|last=Day-Ramos|date=July 9, 2019|accessdate=January 7, 2020}}</ref> [[Principal photography]] began in January 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2020/01/regina-king-directing-debut-one-night-in-miami-kingsley-ben-adir-malcolm-x-eli-goree-cassius-clay-aldis-hodge-jim-brown-leslie-odom-jr-sam-cooke-1202823324/|title=Regina King Directing Debut ''One Night In Miami'' Underway With Kingsley Ben-Adir, Eli Goree, Aldis Hodge & Leslie Odom Jr As '60s Icons|website=Mike|last=Fleming Jr|date=January 7, 2020|accessdate=January 7, 2020}}</ref> The film had its world premiere at the [[Venice Film Festival]] on September 7, 2020, the first film directed by an African-American woman to be selected in the festival's history.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2020/sep/07/regina-king-makes-history-at-venice-film-festival-one-night-in-miami|title=Regina King makes history at Venice film festival with One Night in Miami|work=[[The Guardian]]|first=Lanre|last=Bakare|location=Venice|date=7 September 2020|accessdate=7 September 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1997, King married Ian Alexander, but they divorced in 2007.{{Cn|date=September 2020}} They have one son, born in 1996.{{Cn|date=September 2020}}
