'''Elizabeth Curtis Sittenfeld''' (born 1975) is an American writer. She is the author of a collection of short stories as well as six novels: ''Prep'', the story of students at a [[Massachusetts]] [[University-preparatory school|prep school]]; ''The Man of My Dreams'', a [[Bildungsroman|coming-of-age novel]] and an examination of romantic love; ''American Wife,'' a fictional story loosely based on the life of [[First Lady]] [[Laura Bush]]; ''Sisterland'', which tells the story of identical twins with psychic powers, ''[[Eligible (novel)|Eligible]]'', a modern-day retelling of ''[[Pride and Prejudice]]'', and ''Rodham'', an alternate history political novel about the life of [[Hillary Clinton]].

==Early life and education==
Elizabeth Curtis Sittenfeld was born August 23, 1975, in [[Cincinnati, Ohio]]. She is the second of four children (three girls and a boy) born to Mr. and Mrs. Paul George Sittenfeld. Her mother, the former Elizabeth “Betsy” Curtis Bascom, is an [[art history]] teacher and librarian at [[Seven Hills School]], a private school in Cincinnati, and her father is an investment adviser.<ref>[https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/61658400 Newspapers.com]</ref> Her younger brother, [[P. G. Sittenfeld]], is a member of the [[Cincinnati City Council]]. She is of "half-Catholic, half-Jewish" background.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/confessions-of-a-prep-school-feminist|title=Confessions of a Prep-School Feminist|author=Curtis Sittenfeld|date=May 19, 2014|work=[[The New Yorker]]|accessdate=July 2, 2015}}</ref>

She attended Seven Hills School through the eighth grade, then attended high school at [[Groton School]], a [[boarding school]] in [[Groton, Massachusetts]], graduating in 1993. In 1992, the summer before her senior year, she won ''[[Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen]]'' magazine's fiction contest.

She attended [[Vassar College]] in [[Poughkeepsie (town), New York|Poughkeepsie, New York]] before transferring to [[Stanford University]] in [[Palo Alto, California]]. At Stanford, she studied creative writing, wrote articles for the college newspaper, and edited that paper's weekly arts magazine. At the time, she was also chosen as one of ''[[Glamour magazine|Glamour]]''ʼs College Women of the Year.<ref name="Guardian-interview">{{cite web|last1=Wood|first1=Gaby|title=The old school ties that dominate America|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2005/sep/25/fiction.features3|website=Guardian|accessdate=12 November 2016}}</ref><ref>"Glamour's top 10 college women 1996". Glamour. v. 94, n. 10. October 1996. 108.</ref><ref>Reon Carter.  "Local college senior makes Glamour's top 10".  ''The [[Cincinnati Enquirer]]''.  September 21, 1996.  C2.</ref> She went on to earn an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] from the [[Iowa Writers' Workshop]] at the [[University of Iowa]].<ref name="Wapo1">{{cite web|last1=Stuever|first1=Hank|title=Move Over, Holden|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A45628-2005Feb22.html|website=Washington Post|accessdate=12 November 2016}}</ref>

==Career==

===''Prep''===
Her first novel, ''Prep'', which took her three years to write, was published in 2005 and concerns a girl, Lee Fiora, from [[South Bend, Indiana]], who goes to Ault School, an elite [[boarding school]] near [[Boston, Massachusetts]].<ref>[http://curtissittenfeld.com/about-curtis-sittenfeld.html Curtissittenfeld.com] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091001020718/http://www.curtissittenfeld.com/about-curtis-sittenfeld.html |date=October 1, 2009 }}</ref> 

[[Elissa Schappell]], who wrote in the ''[[New York Times Review of Books]]'': "Sittenfeld's dialogue is so convincing that one wonders if she didn't wear a wire under her hockey kilt." ''[[The New York Times]]'' named ''Prep'' one of their top five works of fiction for 2005.<ref>''The Ten Best Books of 2005," ''The New York Times Book Review'', December 11, 2005.</ref> ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' labelled ''Prep'' a "cult-classic" in a 2018 reassessment.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The untold story of 'Prep': Secrets of the cult-classic campus novel|url=https://ew.com/books/2018/08/28/prep-curtis-sittenfeld/|website=EW.com|language=EN|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref>

===''The Man of My Dreams''===
Sittenfeld's second novel, called ''The Man of My Dreams'', was published in May 2006 by [[Random House]]. It follows a girl named Hannah from the end of her 8th grade year through her college years at [[Tufts]] and into her late twenties. 

===''American Wife''===
Sittenfeld's third novel, called ''American Wife'' (2008), is the tale of Alice Blackwell, a fictional character who shares many similarities with former First Lady [[Laura Bush]].<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Kakutani|first1=Michiko|title=First Lady, Second Version|journal=The New York Times|date=28 August 2008|issue=New York Edition|page=E21 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/08/29/books/29book.html |accessdate=21 March 2017}}</ref> In November 2011, it was announced that Red Crown Productions had begun work on a film version, with the adaptation written by Academy Award-nominated screenwriter [[Ron Nyswaner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deadline.com/2011/11/ron-nyswaner-to-script-curtis-sittenfeld-novel-american-wife/|title=Ron Nyswaner To Script Curtis Sittenfeld Novel 'American Wife'|author=Mike Fleming Jr|work=Deadline|accessdate=July 2, 2015}}</ref> 

===''Sisterland''===
''Sisterland'' was published on June 25, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bookpage.com/the-book-case/2013/01/28/sittenfelds-sisterland-coming-this-summer/|title=Bookpage.com|date=2013}}</ref> The book's protagonist Kate is an identical twin with psychic powers.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Groskop|first1=Viv|title=Sisterland by Curtis Sittenfeld - A Review|journal=The Guardian|date=2 June 2013|issue=The Observer - US Edition|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/jun/02/sisterland-curtis-sittenfeld-fiction-review|accessdate=21 March 2017}}</ref>

===''Eligible''===
''[[Eligible (novel)|Eligible]]'' was published on April 19, 2016 by [[Random House]]. It is a modern retelling of ''[[Pride and Prejudice]]'' set in [[Cincinnati, Ohio]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cincinnati.com/story/entertainment/books/2015/04/14/curtis-sittenfeld-cincy-novel/25774599/|title=Will Cincinnati's Mr. Darcy dine at Boca?|author=Carol Motsinger|accessdate=September 3, 2015}}</ref> In September 2017, [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] announced its commitment to make a TV pilot of ''Eligible'' with Sherri Cooper and Jennifer Levin to write it.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/entertainment/2017/09/08/report-curtis-sittenfelds-book-eligible-headed-tv/647042001/|title=Report: Curtis Sittenfeld's book 'Eligible' headed to TV|work=Cincinnati.com|access-date=2018-03-31|language=en}}</ref>

===''You Think It, I'll Say It''===
''You Think It, I'll Say It'' is a collection of Sittenfeld's stories that [[Random House]] published on 24 April 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=You Think It, I'll Say It|url=https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/558519/you-think-it-ill-say-it-by-curtis-sittenfeld/9780399592867/|website=Penguin Random House|accessdate=27 April 2018}}</ref>

===''Rodham''===
''Rodham'' is an [[alternate history]] political novel about the life of [[Hillary Clinton]], published in 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Sittenfeld|first=Curtis|date=2020-05-15|title=What if Hillary had become president? Curtis Sittenfeld on rewriting Clinton's life|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2020/may/15/what-if-hillary-had-become-president-curtis-sittenfeld-on-rewriting-clintons-life|access-date=2020-05-27|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> The novel diverges from reality at the point where Hillary chooses not to marry [[Bill Clinton]] and enters political life as a single woman.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Brockes|first=Emma|date=2020-05-24|title=Rodham by Curtis Sittenfeld review – Hill minus Bill|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2020/may/24/rodham-by-curtis-sittenfeld-review-hill-minus-bill|access-date=2020-05-27|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>
