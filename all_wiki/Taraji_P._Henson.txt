'''Taraji Penda Henson'''<ref name="bio">{{cite web|title=Taraji P. Henson - Biography |url=http://www.biography.com/people/taraji-henson-21192695#synopsis|publisher=[[Biography.com]] ([[FYI (TV channel)|FYI]] / [[A&E Networks]]) |access-date=September 18, 2014}}</ref><ref name=tvg>{{cite magazine | url = https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/taraji-p-henson-191266/ |title=Taraji Penda. Henson | magazine= [[TV Guide|TVGuide.com]] | access-date= September 18, 2014 |archive-date=March 5, 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305215321/http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/taraji-p-henson/191266/ | url-status=live}}</ref> ({{IPAc-en|t|ə|'|r|ɑː|dʒ|i}} {{respell|tə|RAH|jee}}; born September 11, 1970) is an American actress. She studied acting at [[Howard University]] and began her Hollywood career in guest roles on several television shows before making her breakthrough in ''[[Baby Boy (film)|Baby Boy]]'' (2001). She received praise for her performances as a prostitute in ''[[Hustle & Flow]]'' (2005), for which she received a [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]] nomination; and as a single mother of a disabled child in [[David Fincher]]'s ''[[The Curious Case of Benjamin Button (film)|The Curious Case of Benjamin Button]]'' (2008), for which she received [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Academy Award]], [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Supporting Role|SAG Award]] and [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Supporting Actress|Critics Choice Award]] nominations for Best Supporting Actress. In 2010, she appeared in the action comedy ''[[Date Night]]'', and co-starred in the remake of ''[[The Karate Kid (2010 film)|The Karate Kid]]''.

Henson has also had an extensive and successful career in television, including series such as ''[[The Division]]'', ''[[Boston Legal]]'' and ''[[Eli Stone]]''. In 2011, she starred in the [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]] [[Television film]] ''[[Taken from Me: The Tiffany Rubin Story]]'', which brought her a nomination for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited Series or Movie]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abc7.com/archive/8359122/|title=Emmys 2011 winners: Full list}}</ref> From 2011 to 2013, she co-starred as Detective Jocelyn Carter in the [[CBS]] drama ''[[Person of Interest (TV series)|Person of Interest]]'', for which she won an [[NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series|NAACP Image Award]]. She starred in the ensemble films ''[[Think Like a Man]]'' (2012) and [[Think Like a Man Too|its 2014 sequel]]. In 2015 she began starring as [[Cookie Lyon]] in the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] drama series ''[[Empire (2015 TV series)|Empire]]'', for which she became the first [[African-American]] woman to win a [[Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Drama Series]]. She also won a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Drama|Golden Globe Award]]; and was nominated for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series|Emmy Awards]] in 2015 and 2016.

In 2016, ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' named Henson one of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]]. That year, she released a [[New York Times Best Seller List|''New York Times'' best selling]] autobiography titled ''Around the Way Girl''.<ref>[http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/around-the-way-girl-taraji-p-henson/1123565092 Around The Way Girl] accessed 11/3/2016</ref> Also that year, she was praised for her starring role as [[Katherine Johnson]] in the critically acclaimed drama film ''[[Hidden Figures]]'', for which she won a [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2017/01/sag-awards-2017-winners/amp?client=safari|title= SAG Awards 2017: See the Complete Winners List|magazine=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|date=January 29, 2017}}</ref>

==Film career==

=== Early career (2001–2014) ===
Henson received her [[Screen Actors Guild|SAG]] membership card in the early 1990s for doing three roles as a [[Extra (acting)|background performer]] (extra). Her first prominent role was in the 2001 comedy-drama film ''[[Baby Boy (film)|Baby Boy]]'', where she portrayed Yvette, alongside singer [[Tyrese Gibson]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Taraji P. Henson Talks Cookie Taking Over Her Life, Kerry Washington, Baby Boy Sequel, Season 2 Of Empire + More |url=http://www.power1051fm.com/onair/angie-martinez-54909/taraji-p-henson-talks-cookie-taking-13412740|publisher=power1051fm.com|access-date=March 18, 2015}}</ref>

In 2005, Henson was in the independent film ''[[Hustle & Flow]]'' as Shug, the love interest of [[Terrence Howard]], who portrayed the male lead DJay. As detailed below, she made her singing debut in the film, which was nominated for two [[Academy awards]] and won one. In 2008 she appeared with [[Brad Pitt]] in ''[[The Curious Case of Benjamin Button (film)|The Curious Case of Benjamin Button]],''<ref name="kam">{{cite news |first=Kam |last=Williams |title=Taraji P. Henson: The Curious Case of Benjamin Button Interview |date=December 14, 2008 | publisher=KamWilliams.com |url=http://www.kamwilliams.com/2008/12/taraji-p-henson-curious-case-of.html |access-date=June 30, 2009}}</ref> where she played Queenie, Benjamin's mother, and for which she received an Academy Award nomination for [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Best Supporting Actress]].<ref>{{Citation | last = MacDonald |first = Gayle| title=Benjamin Button Leads Race for What Pitt Calls -- Our Highest Honour| newspaper=Globe & Mail |location=Toronto |page= R13|date= January 23, 2009}}</ref> In an interview with Lauren Viera of the Chicago ''Tribune,'' she noted that "Queenie is the embodiment of unconditional love."<ref>Viera, Lauren (December 21, 2008). [http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2008-12-21/news/0812190173_1_curious-case-queenie-fincher "The curious case of Taraji P. Henson"]. ''[[Chicago Tribune]]''.</ref>[[File:Taraji P. Henson 2011.jpg|thumb|Henson in 2011]]

Henson acted in two [[Tyler Perry]] films, ''[[The Family That Preys]]'' in 2008 and ''[[I Can Do Bad All By Myself (film)|I Can Do Bad All By Myself]]'' in 2009. In 2010 she appeared in the remake of ''[[The Karate Kid (2010 film)|The Karate Kid]]'' with [[Jaden Smith]]. Though it did not receive a majority of positive reviews, the film was a commercial success.<ref>{{cite news|title=Taraji P. Henson Interview: The Karate Kid, Beijing Culture and Working for Will Smith|url=http://www.pr.com/article/1154|access-date=March 18, 2015|publisher=pr.com|date=June 9, 2010}}</ref>

In 2011 Henson starred as Tiffany Rubin in the [[Lifetime Movie Network]] film ''[[Taken from Me: The Tiffany Rubin Story]].'' It was based on true events in the life of a New York woman whose son, Kobe, was abducted by his biological father to [[South Korea]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Taken from Me: The Tiffany Rubin Story: TV review [Lifetime] |url=http://entertainmentrealm.com/2011/01/30/taken-from-me-the-tiffany-rubin-story-tv-review-lifetime-movie/|access-date=March 19, 2015|publisher=entertainmentrealm.com}}</ref> Her portrayal of Rubin received positive reviews<ref>{{cite web|title=Taken from Me: The Tiffany Rubin Story|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/taken_from_me_the_tiffany_rubin_story_2010/|website=Rotten Tomatoes|access-date=March 19, 2015}}</ref> and earned her several award nominations, including a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Taraji P. Henson|url=http://www.emmys.com/bios/taraji-p-henson |publisher=Emmys|access-date=March 19, 2015}}</ref>

In 2012, Henson was in the large ensemble cast film ''[[Think Like A Man]],'' based on [[Steve Harvey]]'s 2009 book ''[[Act Like a Lady, Think Like a Man]].'' She reprised the role in the film's sequel, ''[[Think Like a Man Too]],'' released in June 2014.<ref name="think">{{cite news|title=Production starts on 'Think Like a Man Too'| url= http://www.upi.com/Entertainment_News/Movies/2013/05/28/Production-starts-on-Think-Like-a-Man-Too/UPI-48371369775541/|access-date=September 19, 2013|newspaper=upi.com|date=May 28, 2013}}</ref>

=== ''Hidden Figures'' and beyond (2015–present) ===
In 2016, Henson starred in the film ''[[Hidden Figures]],'' a major box-office success nominated for numerous awards, including three [[Academy Award|Oscars]] ([[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]], [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay|Best Adapted Screenplay]], and [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Best Supporting Actress]] for [[Octavia Spencer]]) and two [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globes]] ([[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Best Supporting Actress]] for Spencer and [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score|Best Original Score]]). It won the [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]].

In January 2018, she starred in [[Screen Gems|Sony Screen Gems]]'s thriller-drama film ''[[Proud Mary (film)|Proud Mary]]'', as a [[Contract killing|hit woman]] whose life is turned around when she meets a young boy who awakens the maternal instinct she never knew she had.<ref name="Fleming Jr">{{cite magazine |url=https://deadline.com/2017/01/taraji-p-henson-proud-mary-screen-gems-hidden-figures-empire-1201883396/#respond |title=Hidden Figures Taraji P. On March 16, 2015, she was a guest co-host on ''[[Live! with Kelly and Michael]],'' filling in for [[Kelly Ripa]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Scoop: ''Live with Kelly and Michael'' Week of March 16, 2015|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwtv/article/Scoop-LIVE-WITH-KELLY-AND-MICHAEL-Week-of-March-16-2015-20150313#|publisher=broadwayworld.com|access-date=March 18, 2015}}</ref>

Henson collaborated with MAC Cosmetics to create the Taraji P. Henson makeup collection in late August 2016. The #MACTaraji collection debuted the following month.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://mic.com/articles/152478/mac-cosmetics-continues-its-long-history-of-diversity-with-taraji-p-henson-collaboration#.QBv96INYe |title=MAC Cosmetics continues its long history of diversity with Taraji P. Henson collaboration |author=Hayley Wilbur| publisher=Mic|date=24 August 2016| access-date= August 31, 2016}}</ref> In November 2016, she again collaborated with MAC as the spokesperson for their Viva Glam Campaign, along with [[Jussie Smollett]], to benefit the MAC HIV/AIDS fund. Their collection debuted in February, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.essence.com/beauty/makeup/taraji-p-henson-viva-glam-collection |title=Taraji P. Henson's MAC Viva Glam Collection Is Finally Here |author=Nikki Brown| publisher=Essence|date=3 February 2017| access-date= February 3, 2017}}</ref>

Henson has had a small stage career, appearing in [[August Wilson|August Wilson's]] ''[[Joe Turner's Come and Gone]]'' and the [[Pasadena Playhouse|Pasadena Playhouse's]] ''Above the Fold''.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}}
She started The Boris Lawrence Henson Foundation.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}} Named after her father, it deals with cultural mental illness.

==Personal life==
In 1994, Henson gave birth to her son Marcell.<ref>{{cite news |url = http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/taraji-p-henson-sends-son-marcel-to-howard-after-racial-profiling-2015233 |title= Taraji P. In 2014, Henson said that her son had been racially profiled by police and that his car had been illegally searched during a traffic stop in [[Glendale, California]]. Hashish oil and marijuana were found inside the car.<ref name=latimes-march2015>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/local/lanow/la-me-ln-actress-taraji-p-henson-son-racial-profile-20150327-story.html#navtype=outfit|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|title=Actress Taraji Henson apologizes to Glendale police for racial profile claims| first= Veronica| last= Rocha| date= March 27, 2015|access-date=March 28, 2015| archive-date= April 9, 2015| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150409093239/http://www.latimes.com/local/lanow/la-me-ln-actress-taraji-p-henson-son-racial-profile-20150327-story.html | url-status=live}}</ref> Forty minutes after the video was made public,<ref name=latimes-march2015 /> Henson said in an [[Instagram]] message, "I would like to publicly apologize to the officer and the Glendale Police Department. A mother's job is not easy, and neither is a police officer's."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://instagram.com/p/0v0KjQOuFM/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402153245/https://instagram.com/p/0v0KjQOuFM/|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 2, 2015|title=#TurningANegativeIntoAPositive #LoveTarajiPHenson | publisher= Taraji P.
