'''Michael Len Williams II''' (born March 23, 1989), known professionally as '''Mike Will Made It''' (often stylized as '''Mike WiLL Made-It''')<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5747924/mike-will-made-it|title=Mike Will Made-It|publisher=|accessdate=1 July 2016}}</ref> or simply '''Mike Will''', is an American record producer, DJ, rapper, and songwriter. He is best known for [[Hip hop production|producing]] [[Trap music (hip hop)|trap beats]] for several [[Southern hip hop]] artists as well as for producing several singles, such as "[[Black Beatles]]" and "[[Powerglide (song)|Powerglide]]" by [[Rae Sremmurd]], "[[Mercy (GOOD Music song)|Mercy]]" by [[GOOD Music]], "[[No Lie (2 Chainz song)|No Lie]]" by [[2 Chainz]], "[[Bandz a Make Her Dance]]" by [[Juicy J]], "[[Pour It Up]]" by [[Rihanna]], "[[Love Me (Lil Wayne song)|Love Me]]" by [[Lil Wayne]], "[[Body Party]]" by [[Ciara]], "[[We Can't Stop]]" by [[Miley Cyrus]], "[[Formation (song)|Formation]]" by [[Beyoncé]], and "[[Humble (song)|Humble]]" by [[Kendrick Lamar]]. He has released six [[mixtape]]s and one studio album, ''[[Ransom 2]]''.

==Early life==
Williams was born in [[Marietta, Georgia]], the youngest of three children; he has two older sisters.<ref name=faderconstruction>{{cite web|last=Noz|first=Andrew|title=Beat Construction: Mike WiLL Made It|url=http://www.thefader.com/2012/04/30/beat-construction-mike-will-made-it/|publisher=The Fader|date=April 30, 2012}}</ref> His father, Michael Williams Sr., is a former [[IBM]] executive who worked as a club DJ in the 1970s. His mother,  Shirley Williams, a former bank loan officer, was once in a gospel group, singing for [[Dottie Peoples]]. He grew up in a musical family as his uncle was an accomplished guitar player and one of his older sisters was a drum major in the Olympics.<ref name=faderconstruction /> While growing up, Mike Will was athletic, participating in a number of sports, including basketball, baseball, and football, with dreams of becoming a professional athlete.<ref name=complex3-5>{{cite web|last=Pereira|first=Julian|title=Beyond the Beats: Mike WiLL|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/03/behind-the-boards-mike-will/|publisher=Complex Magazine|date=March 5, 2012}}</ref>

His life also centered around a love of hip hop music. Mike Will first developed his talent for music by re-playing popular instrumentals that he heard on the radio while he and his friends would [[Freestyle rap|freestyle]] to them.<ref name=faderconstruction /> In a number of interviews, Will has mentioned, in particular, re-playing the instrumental for "[[Still Fly]]", a popular song by the southern rap group [[Big Tymers]], on a [[Casio]] brand keyboard, and has also mentioned re-playing "[[Young'n (Holla Back)]]" by New York rapper [[Fabolous]] on production equipment at a local music store.<ref name=hhtobias>{{cite web|last=Tobias|first=Jonathan|title=Mike Will Made It Explains Convincing Young Jeezy Of A "TM103" Track, Working With Gucci Mane As A Teenager|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/dxnext/id.226/title.mike-will-made-it-explains-convincing-young-jeezy-of-a-tm103-track-working-with-gucci-mane-as-a-teenager|publisher=Hip Hop DX|date=December 16, 2011}}</ref>

At age 14, Mike Will began to develop his own beats on a [[Korg]] ES1 beat machine, which his father bought for him as a Christmas present from the local music store [[Mars Music]]. As Mike Will became more accomplished, he also began to use production equipment, including the Korg Triton, the Akai MPC1000, the [[Yamaha Motif]], and the Roland Fantom.<ref name=faderconstruction /> By the time Mike Will was 16, he was spending time at local recording studios in Atlanta, trying to shop his beats to established artists.<ref name="Devin Leonard">{{cite web | url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-08-07/mike-will-made-it-hip-hops-hottest-producer-is-crossover-king | title=Hip-Hop Producer Mike WiLL's Makeover of Miley Cyrus Was Only the Beginning | date=August 7, 2014 | accessdate=16 March 2016 | author=Devin Leonard}}</ref> He was initially ignored, but eventually one of his beat tapes made its way into the hands of [[Gucci Mane]], who then invited Mike Will to Patchwerk studios, an Atlanta recording studio.<ref name="Devin Leonard"/>

Upon graduating high school, Mike Will enrolled at [[Georgia State University]] to pursue undergraduate studies mainly due to pressure from his parents, but chose to take a hiatus and eventually dropped out after several semesters,  with a 3.1 GPA, to focus on his music career.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2012/04/30/beat-construction-mike-will-made-it/|title=Beat Construction: Mike Will Made It|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-05-10|language=en}}</ref> During this hiatus, he produced his first hit. Upon hearing one of his produced singles "[[Tupac Back]]" began to enjoy success on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' rap and Hot 100 charts, Mike Will decided to quit school and focus on his music career full-time.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://theburtonwire.com/2015/02/23/entertainment/mike-will-made-it-debuts-ear-drummers-act-rae-sremmurd/ | title=Mike WiLL Made-It Debuts Ear Drummers' Act Rae Sremmurd | publisher=The Burton Wire | date=February 23, 2015 | accessdate=16 March 2016}}</ref> He has since advised aspiring music producers not to drop out of college if they are not sure of their own future career outcome or lacking a defined plan before entering the industry.<ref name=faderconstruction />

===2011–2012: Production and mixtapes===
In an interview with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Mike Will said, "Gucci Mane was the first [major artist] to ever [[Rapping|rap]] on my beat."<ref name=xxlbristout>{{cite web|last=Bristout|first=Ralph|title=Production Credit: Mike Will Speaks on Working With Rick Ross, Young Jeezy & 50 Cent|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/features/2012/01/mike-will-speaks-on-working-with-rick-ross-young-jeezy-50-cent-production-credit/|publisher=XXL Magazine|date=January 26, 2012}}</ref>  Mike Will first met Gucci Mane at Atlanta recording studio, [[PatchWerk Recording Studios]], and upon introducing himself and giving Gucci Mane a CD of beats, Gucci Mane proceeded to [[freestyle rap|freestyle]] over each of the instrumentals. He then offered Mike Will $1,000 for one of his beats.<ref name="billboard1">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5645388/mike-will-made-it-the-billboard-cover-story |title=Mike Will Made It: The Billboard Cover Story |publisher=Billboard |date=2013-08-09 |accessdate=2013-10-16}}</ref> The two artists began releasing songs together, such as "East Atlanta 6", and a number of songs from Gucci Mane's mixtape, ''No Pad, No Pencil''. After developing this close relationship with Gucci Mane, he began working with other big-name Atlanta rappers such as [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Waka Flocka Flame]], [[Rocko (rapper)|Rocko]], and 2 Chainz.<ref name="billboard1"/>

In 2011, Mike Will released his first [[Single (music)|single]], "[[Tupac Back]]", performed by [[Meek Mill]] and [[Rick Ross]], from the compilation album ''[[Self Made Vol. 1]]''.<ref name=complextupac>{{cite web|last=Pereira|first=Julian|title=Beyond the Beats: Mike WiLL|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/03/behind-the-boards-mike-will/meek-mill-tupac-back|date=March 5, 2012}}</ref> The single was released on April 5, 2011 and peaked at No. 31 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=R&B/Hip Hop Songs|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2011-07-09/r-b-hip-hop-songs?order=drop|publisher=Billboard Magazine|date=July 9, 2011}}</ref> This came after Mike Will submitted some of his beats to an A&R for Rick Ross' [[Maybach Music Group]] label.<ref name=complextupac />

Also in 2011, Mike Will worked with Atlanta rapper Future, producing several popular and noteworthy songs together, including "[[Ain't No Way Around It (DJ Drama song)|Ain't No Way Around It]]",<ref name=complexfuture>{{cite web|last=Pereira|first=Julian|title=Beyond the Beats: Mike WiLL|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/03/behind-the-boards-mike-will/future-aint-no-way-around-it|publisher=Complex Magazine|date=March 5, 2012}}</ref> "Itchin", and three songs from Future's 2012 major label debut album ''[[Pluto (Future album)|Pluto]]'': "Neva End", "Truth Gonna Hurt You", and "[[Turn On the Lights (song)|Turn On the Lights]]".<ref name=faderconstruction /> "Turn On the Lights" was promoted as a single, and to date has peaked at No. 2 on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs ''Billboard chart''.<ref name=billboardfuture>{{cite web|title=R&B/Hip Hop Songs|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2012-09-29/r-b-hip-hop-songs|publisher=Billboard Magazine|date=September 24, 2012}}</ref>

===2013–present: Debut studio album and Miley Cyrus collaborations===
Mike Will executive produced Miley Cyrus's fourth studio album ''[[Bangerz]]'' (2013), including its lead single "[[We Can't Stop]]".  He produced eight tracks on ''Bangerz'' and six tracks on Cyrus' other project ''Miley Cyrus & Her Dead Petz''.

On September 9, 2013, Mike Will premiered his commercial debut single "[[23 (song)|23]]", featuring [[Wiz Khalifa]], Juicy J and Miley Cyrus. He revealed that he had signed with [[Interscope Records]] to release his debut album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/mike-will-made-it-f-miley-cyrus-wif-khalifa-juicy-j-23 |title=Mike WiLL Made It, Miley Cyrus, Wiz Khalifa, Juicy J "23" |publisher=Complex |date=2013-09-09 |accessdate=2013-10-16}}</ref> His debut album will also feature Beyoncé, Future, [[Kendrick Lamar]], and 2 Chainz.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/video-mike-will-made-it-f-miley-cyrus-wiz-khalifa-juicy-j-23 |title=Mike WiLL Made It f/ Miley Cyrus, Wiz Khalifa & Juicy J "23" Video |publisher=Complex |date=2013-09-24 |accessdate=2013-10-16}}</ref>

He was named producer of the year by [[HipHopDX]] on December 18, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/editorials/id.2241/title.the-2013-hiphopdx-year-end-awards|title=The 2013 HipHopDX Year End Awards|first=HipHopDX -|last=http://hiphopdx.com|publisher=|accessdate=1 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140122060241/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/editorials/id.2241/title.the-2013-hiphopdx-year-end-awards|archive-date=22 January 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On June 17, 2014, Mike WIll Made It released the first single from his mixtape, "Buy the World", featuring [[Future (rapper)|Future]], Lil Wayne and Kendrick Lamar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/buy-world-feat.-future-lil/id888908768|title=Buy the World (feat. Lil Wayne, Kendrick Lamar & Future) - Single by Mike Will Made-It on iTunes|publisher=|accessdate=1 July 2016}}</ref> On December 15, 2014, he released his fifth mixtape, ''Ransom''. Featured artists include Big Sean, Juicy J, 2 Chainz, Lil Wayne and Kendrick Lamar.

On March 24, 2017, he released ''Ransom 2''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7727761/mike-will-made-it-ransom-2-release-date|title=Mike Will Made-It Reveals 'Ransom 2' Release Date|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-01-04}}</ref> The bigger names showcase a quick flip through Mike Will's contact list: Rihanna, Kendrick Lamar, Future, Pharrell, Lil Wayne, Big Sean, Migos, YG, and more.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/23087-ransom-2/|title=Mike WiLL Made-It: Ransom 2 Album Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-01-04}}</ref>

On August 21, 2020, Mike Will released the single "Bang Bang" with rapper [[Chief Keef]]. He also announced that his upcoming project is "74%" done.<ref name="BangBang" />

==Influences==
In interviews, Mike Will has named a number of musical influences on his own production work. In a "Behind the Beats" interview with ''Complex'' magazine, he says that he "always admired [[Dr. Dre]]'s drums", and calls [[Timbaland]] "the first crazy pop producer... He brought so many artists to the game."<ref name=complexinfl>{{cite web|last=Pereira|first=Julian|title=Beyond the Beats: Mike WiLL|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/03/behind-the-boards-mike-will/influences|publisher=Complex Magazine|date=March 5, 2012}}</ref> As a southern producer, he says, he "was also looking up to [[Shawty Redd]]. He was the first producer to really bring that dark trap energy and sound."<ref name=complexinfl /> He also refers to [[DJ Toomp]] as "my big brother or my mentor", saying, "I love what he does."<ref name=complexinfl /> In an interview, with [[AOL]]'s The Boombox, Mike WiLL also cites [[Pharrell Williams|Pharrell]] as a producer that he respects.

==Production equipment==
Mike Will uses a variety of production equipment to create beats. His gear includes the Korg Triton, Akai MPC 1000, [[Logic Pro]], [[FL Studio]], [[Yamaha Motif]], and the [[Roland Fantom-X]].<ref name=faderconstruction /><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.thefader.com/2012/12/12/drum-majors-four-producers-to-watch | title=Drum Majors: Four Producers to Watch | publisher=The Fader | date=12 December 2012 | accessdate=10 March 2015 |author1=Matthew Schnipper |author2=Naomi Zeichner |author3=JORDAN SARGENT |author4=Olivia Graham }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://v103.cbslocal.com/2012/01/31/producers-week-day-2-mike-will-made-it-and-m-sixteen/ | title="Producers Week" Day 2: Mike Will Made It And M-Sixteen! | publisher=V-103 | date=January 31, 2012 | accessdate=10 March 2015}}</ref>
