"'''Diamonds'''" is a song recorded by [[Barbadians|Barbadian]] singer [[Rihanna]] for her seventh studio album, ''[[Unapologetic]]'' (2012). It was written by [[Sia (musician)|Sia Furler]] together with its producers, [[Benny Blanco]] and [[Stargate (production team)|StarGate]]. The song premiered on September 26, 2012, during the ''[[Elvis Duran and the Morning Show]]'' and was [[music download|digitally]] released the following day as the [[lead single]] from ''Unapologetic''. "Diamonds" is a mid-tempo [[Pop music|pop]], [[Electronic music|electronic]] and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] [[ballad]] that features heavy [[synthesizer]]s, orchestral sounds and electronic rhythms. The song's lyrics serve as a departure from the themes of unhealthy relationships that were on Rihanna's previous singles and contain a prominent concept of love.

"Diamonds" topped music charts in over twenty countries, including the United States, where it became Rihanna's twelfth number-one single on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and [[List of Billboard Hot 100 chart achievements and milestones#Most number-one singles|tied her]] with [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]] and [[The Supremes]] for the fifth-most number-one singles in the chart's history. "Diamonds" was certified [[RIAA certification|sextuple platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) and sold over 3.5 million digital copies in the country. It also peaked at number one on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and became Rihanna's seventh number one song in the country; it was certified double platinum by the [[British Phonographic Industry]] (BPI). By May 2013, it had sold over 7.5 million copies worldwide and became [[List of best-selling singles|one of the best-selling singles of all-time]].

The song's [[music video]] was shot by director [[Anthony Mandler]], a frequent collaborator of Rihanna's, and depicts her in four environments that represent the elements of earth, air, water, and fire. The video received positive reviews and was praised for its imagery. Some critics believe that the heavily tattooed man intertwined with Rihanna's arm in the video resembles [[Chris Brown]]. The singer performed "Diamonds" on television shows such as ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' and ''[[The X Factor (UK TV series)|The X Factor]]'' and included it on the [[777 Tour|777]], [[Diamonds World Tour|Diamonds]], [[The Monster Tour (Eminem and Rihanna)|Monster Tour]] and the [[Anti World Tour]] [[set list]]s. The [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP) recognized it as one of the most performed songs of 2013 and 2014. The official remix of "Diamonds" featured rapper [[Kanye West]] and was released on November 16, 2012. The song has been covered by various recording artists, including [[Josef Salvat]], who released his own cover of "Diamonds" as a single.

==Writing and production==
[[File:Sia Furler in concert.jpg|alt=A blonde woman wearing a white dress is performing|thumb|upright=0.7|left|[[Sia (musician)|Sia]] (''pictured'') wrote the lyrics, while producer [[Benny Blanco]] co-wrote and co-produced "Diamonds" with [[Stargate (production team)|StarGate]].]]

In 2012, American songwriter and record producer [[Benny Blanco]] met with Norwegian production duo [[Stargate (production team)|StarGate]] at a recording studio in [[New York City]] to write new songs, including one for Rihanna.<ref name="the source">{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2012/10/07/sourcetv-benny-blanco-talks-producing-rihannas-diamonds/|title=SourceTV: Benny Blanco Talks Producing Rihanna's 'Diamonds'|newspaper=[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]|last=Lynch|first=Sean|date=October 7, 2012|accessdate=October 7, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130427093611/http://thesource.com/2012/10/07/sourcetv-benny-blanco-talks-producing-rihannas-diamonds/|archive-date=April 27, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> StarGate had previously produced her singles "[[Don't Stop the Music (Rihanna song)|Don't Stop the Music]]" (2007) and "[[Only Girl (In the World)]]" (2010).<ref name="daily news">{{cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/rihanna-debuts-new-single-diamonds-a-great-feeling-i-listen-article-1.1168728|title=Rihanna debuts new single 'Diamonds': Ballad is grand and obvious but not as haunting as it longs to be|newspaper=[[Daily News (New York)|Daily News]]|location=New York|last=Farber|first=Jim|date=September 26, 2012|accessdate=September 26, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121001042329/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/rihanna-debuts-new-single-diamonds-a-great-feeling-i-listen-article-1.1168728|archive-date=October 1, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> According to Blanco, he and StarGate wanted to deviate from Rihanna's usual sound and produce as though the song would be for a rapper such as [[Kanye West]]: "It's the one that we weren't thinking Rihanna ... that turned into the Rihanna record ... But that's how it always happens like with me".<ref name="the source"/> They produced the song's drum machine beat after the other instrumental music was recorded.<ref name="the source"/>

StarGate's Mikkel Eriksen told ''[[The New York Times]]'' that Blanco took a recorded snippet of Eriksen's singing, altered it electronically, and made it sound "dirtier".<ref name="ny times benny"/> He then applied timbre and used audio software to create ghostly accompaniment lines. Eriksen described Blanco's style as "unorthodox, as he almost never plays the keyboards but throws in weird samples and alters them to the right pitch to go with the song."<ref name="ny times benny">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/01/06/arts/music/benny-blanco-hit-maker-for-rihanna-and-maroon-5.html?pagewanted=all&_r=1&|title=Only 24, and at Home in the Top 10|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|last=McKinley Jr.|first=James|date=January 4, 2013|accessdate=January 9, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131034123/http://www.nytimes.com/2013/01/06/arts/music/benny-blanco-hit-maker-for-rihanna-and-maroon-5.html?pagewanted=all&_r=1&|archive-date=January 31, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Australian singer-songwriter [[Sia (musician)|Sia]] later joined them and wrote the lyrics for "Diamonds" in 14 minutes.<ref name="the source"/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://6thfloor.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/04/21/how-a-song-written-by-sia-furler-becomes-a-hit/?_php=true&_type=blogs&_r=0 |title=How a Song Written by Sia Furler Becomes a Hit |work=The New York Times |last=Knopper |first=Steve |date=April 21, 2014 |accessdate=April 23, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141205003032/http://6thfloor.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/04/21/how-a-song-written-by-sia-furler-becomes-a-hit/?_php=true&_type=blogs&_r=0 |archivedate=December 5, 2014 }}</ref>

After the song was completed, they wanted to play it to Rihanna, but Blanco was skeptical about the reaction towards the song because of its slow sound. After StarGate played it to her, they called Blanco from [[London]] and told him that she liked the song: "She's flippin' out. She played it like seven times in a row. It's her favorite song."<ref name="the source"/> [[Phil Tan]] and the assistant Daniela Rivera, mixed and mastered "Diamonds".<ref name="Notes"/> Recalling it, in an interview with ''[[The Huffington Post]]'', Blanco commented, "We needed to have it recorded, the beat finished, mixed and mastered in 24 hours. She was recording in a separate part of the world, sending back the files, we're finishing the music and then we're mixing and mastering it, and then it was out in a few days. It's fucking amazing and incredible."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/11/08/benny-blanco-diamonds-rihanna_n_2094534.html|title=Benny Blanco On 'Diamonds,' Writing For Women & The Secret Sauce To Making A Hit|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|last=Makarechi|first=Kia|date=November 8, 2012|accessdate=August 9, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131209171420/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/11/08/benny-blanco-diamonds-rihanna_n_2094534.html|archive-date=December 9, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Kuk Harrell]] produced Rihanna's vocals on the song and recorded them together with Marcos Tovar.<ref name="Notes"/>

==Music video==

===Background and concept===
{{Quote box|width=30%|align=left|quote="With every song it's a different story, so the visuals are very specific to that story and that world. With 'Diamonds,' it was just a series of vignettes that we put together to help get the emotion across throughout the song 'cause the song changes and it builds, and there's no real way that you could do that."|source=—Rihanna explaining the concept for the music video.<ref name="MTVVena">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1697054/rihanna-diamonds-video-imagery.jhtml|title=Rihanna Wanted 'Diamonds' Video To Give Fans The 'Right Impression'|publisher=MTV News|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|date=November 9, 2012|accessdate=November 9, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109154826/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1697054/rihanna-diamonds-video-imagery.jhtml|archive-date=November 9, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>}}
Rihanna began to film the [[music video]] for "Diamonds" on October 21, 2012, at the [[Mojave Desert]] in [[Lancaster, California]].<ref name="mtv1">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1695937/rihanna-diamonds-music-video.jhtml|title=Rihanna Sets The World On Fire In 'Diamonds' Video Tease|publisher=MTV News|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|date=October 22, 2012|accessdate=October 22, 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ETXBXn6S?url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1695937/rihanna-diamonds-music-video.jhtml|archive-date=February 16, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> It was directed by her regular collaborator [[Anthony Mandler]], who previously directed her videos for "[[Russian Roulette (Rihanna song)|Russian Roulette]]" (2009) and "[[Man Down (song)|Man Down]]" (2011).<ref name="mtv1"/> Photographs of the production leaked to the press the same day and showed Rihanna in a white and black dress in front of a fire. Ethan Sacks of the ''New York Daily News'' remarked that she "looked like a gem on the set of her video shoot."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/rihanna-glitters-set-diamonds-video-shoot-article-1.1189369|title=Rihanna glitters on set of 'Diamonds' video shoot|work=Daily News|location=New York|date=October 22, 2012|accessdate=October 22, 2012|last=Sacks|first=Ethan|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121025022132/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/rihanna-glitters-set-diamonds-video-shoot-article-1.1189369|archive-date=October 25, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> Rihanna said that she wanted the video's abstract scenes to capture the appropriate emotion for the audience.<ref name="MTVVena" /> Mandler sought to create a "wider landscape" by suggesting and transfiguring ideas for viewers through the video: "[T]hey needed to feel like dream vignettes, like, when you wake up from a dream, you realize what you were dreaming about is not really what it was about, it was about something else."<ref name="Mandler"/> He also tried to relate the video's concept to Rihanna's personal life: "What's truth or fiction? You know 'Is she drowning, or is she in complete ecstasy? Are those hands coming apart, or are they finding each other?' ... we tried to bring up these deeper questions that relate to the song and her life and finding beauty in chaos and finding beauty in pain and finding pain in beauty."<ref name="Mandler">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1697489/rihanna-diamonds-music-video-director-secrets.jhtml|title=Does Rihanna's Director Know Something About 'Diamonds' That We Don't?|publisher=MTV News|last=Montgomery|first=James|date=November 15, 2012|accessdate=November 16, 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ETXC9ueN?url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1697489/rihanna-diamonds-music-video-director-secrets.jhtml|archive-date=February 16, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> On November 7, a behind-the-scenes video was posted on [[MTV]]'s official website.<ref name="premiere">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1696915/rihanna-diamonds-video-clip.jhtml|title=Rihanna To Premiere 'Diamonds' Video Tomorrow On MTV: Watch A Sneak Peak!|publisher=MTV News|last=Warner|first=Kara|date=November 7, 2012|accessdate=November 7, 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ETXCyvv9?url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1696915/rihanna-diamonds-video-clip.jhtml|archive-date=February 16, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> It featured Rihanna in a "leopard-print" robe as she emerged from a trailer and headed to the shooting set. ===Synopsis===
[[File:Diamonds video shoot.png|thumbnail|right|280px|alt=A screenshot of two tattooed hands intertwining|A screenshot from the music video showing Rihanna's hand intertwined with that of a heavily tattooed man. Some critics believed the arm of the man resembled that of [[Chris Brown]].<ref name="nbc review"/>]]

The music video opens with a view of Rihanna lying in open waters, as other scenes of diamonds being rolled in a manner suggestive of making a joint are intercut&nbsp;– a scene reminiscent of the single's artwork. As the video progresses Rihanna is shown running on a highway. In a [[black-and-white]] scene, Rihanna licks the joint and begins smoking it. The image fades to black as the song begins. Rihanna performs in the same black-and-white setting while in a black corset. Images of Rihanna on a bed in a room while singing are shown, with objects breaking, curtains falling and roses burning in [[Backwards masking|reverse effect]]. Rihanna is shown again on the highway, running away from what appears to be the headlights of a chasing car. She then stops as she looks up to the night sky at an [[Aurora (astronomy)|aurora]] view. Throughout the video, Rihanna's hand is shown intertwined with that of a heavily tattooed man, whose face is not shown in the video. As the second verse starts, Rihanna is shown in a desert where horses run freely. Scenes of a street battle are later shown, depicting burning cars and broken glass around the environment. As the video continues, the singer and her lover's hands are shown slowly slipping apart, with only their fingertips touching towards the conclusion of the video. The video ends with Rihanna once again floating alone in the open waters.
