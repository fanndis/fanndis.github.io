'''David Samuel Rosenthal''' is an [[United States|American]] writer and [[Television producer|TV producer]], best known as the executive producer of season seven of the popular [[comedy-drama]] ''[[Gilmore Girls]]'' and co-creator of the original [[Ellen (TV series)|''Ellen'']] TV series.

== Early life ==
Rosenthal is from [[Lawrenceville, New Jersey]], to a family of [[Jewish]] background. His father is [[Anti-Defamation League]] Latin American Affairs director and [[Human rights activists|human rights activist]] rabbi Morton Rosenthal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://margueritefeitlowitz.com/2013/02/rabbi-morton-rosenthal-human-rights-hero/|title=Marguerite Feitlowitz {{!}} Rabbi Morton Rosenthal – Human Rights Hero|website=margueritefeitlowitz.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-09-27}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.clevelandjewishnews.com/community/lifecycles/obituaries/morton-rosenthal/article_e64758b8-659f-11e2-9c25-0019bb2963f4.html|title=MORTON ROSENTHAL|work=Cleveland Jewish News|access-date=2017-09-27|language=en}}</ref>

Rosenthal graduated from the [[University of Pennsylvania]] (Penn).

== Career ==
Shortly after moving to Wisconsin, Rosenthal was hired as a writer's assistant on the ABC sitcom ''[[Anything but Love]]''.  He began to write on his own and got a job on the show [[Nurses (American TV series)|''Nurses'']].  Rosenthal was later hired as a staff writer on ''Anything but Love''.  When that ended, he wrote for [[Laurie Hill (TV series)|''Laurie Hill'']], a show created by [[Neal Marlens|Neil Marlens]] and [[Carol L. Black|Carol Black]], the people behind ''[[The Wonder Years]]''.  With Marlens and Black, Rosenthal helped develop a sitcom for Laurie Hill co-star [[Ellen DeGeneres]]. The show, entitled ''These Friends of Mine'', became [[Ellen (TV series)|''Ellen'']].  Pretty soon, Rosenthal was running it. He was just 24.{{Citation needed|date=November 2017}}

After a ten year-long stint developing sitcoms for [[Jeffrey Katzenberg]], he was hired as a writer on the sitcom ''[[Spin City]]'' and was quickly promoted to [[showrunner]]. After ''Spin City'', Rosenthal landed a development contract with [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox Television]] for $2.5 million per year.{{Citation needed|date=November 2017}}

In April 2006, it was announced that ''Gilmore Girls'' executive producers [[Amy Sherman-Palladino]] and her husband [[Daniel Palladino|Daniel]] could not come to an agreement with [[The CW]] and would be leaving the show when their contracts expired that summer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/team-palladino-says-8524/|title=Team Palladino Says "Goodbye, Girls"|date=2006-04-20|work=TVGuide.com|access-date=2017-09-27|language=en}}</ref>  Rosenthal, who worked on the show as a writer and producer for season six, was selected by Sherman-Palladino to replace her as showrunner.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.tvguide.com/news/finally-gilmores-new-8380/|title=Finally: Gilmore's New Boss Speaks|date=2006-07-18|work=TVGuide.com|access-date=2017-09-27|language=en}}</ref> Though the season was noted as uneven compared to the previous six seasons, it is noted as having some good notes and moments overall from Rosenthal's writing and direction.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.vulture.com/2015/11/gilmore-girls-season-seven-defense.html|title=In Defense of Gilmore Girls’ Seventh Season|date=2015-11-06|work=Vulture|access-date=2017-09-27|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://decider.com/2016/06/20/unpopular-opinion-season-7-of-gilmore-girls-is-not-that-bad/|title=Unpopular Opinion: Season 7 of ‘Gilmore Girls’ Is Very Underrated|date=2016-06-20|work=Decider {{!}} Where To Stream Movies & Shows on Netflix, Hulu, Amazon Instant, HBO Go|access-date=2017-09-27|language=en-US}}</ref>

Rosenthal was uninvolved with the 2016 Netflix revival ''[[Gilmore Girls: A Year in the Life]]''.

== Personal life ==
Rosenthal was married to fellow ''Spin City'' writer, Sarah Dunn, they divorced in 2001.  He later quit his job on ''Spin City'' to focus on writing a controversial play about supermodel [[Heidi Klum]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2002/may/05/magazine/tm-41437|title=The Loneliness of the Long-Distance Ranter|last=REITMAN|first=JANET|date=2002-05-05|work=Los Angeles Times|access-date=2017-09-27|language=en-US|issn=0458-3035}}</ref> He married comedy writer Gracie Glassmeyer in 2015.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.greylikesweddings.com/real-weddings/gracie-davids-intimate-wedding-cafe-amelie/|title=Gracie and David's Intimate wedding at Café Amelie|date=2016-07-12|work=Grey Likes Weddings {{!}} Wedding Fashion & Inspiration {{!}} Best Wedding Blog|access-date=2017-11-16|language=en-US}}</ref>

Rosenthal is an avid tennis fan.
