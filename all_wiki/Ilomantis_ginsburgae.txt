'''''Ilomantis ginsburgae''''' is a species of leaf-dwelling [[praying mantis]] from [[Madagascar]].<ref name="BrannochSvenson2016">{{cite journal |last1=Brannoch |first1=Sydney K. |last2=Svenson |first2=Gavin J. |title=Leveraging female genitalic characters for generic and species delimitation in ''Nilomantis'' Werner, 1907 and ''Ilomantis'' Giglio-Tos, 1915 (Mantodea, Nilomantinae) |journal=Insect Systematics & Evolution |year=2016 |volume=47 |issue=3 |pages=209â€“244 |doi=10.1163/1876312X-47032141}}</ref> It is the first praying mantis species characterised by its female [[genitalia]]. It was named after [[United States Supreme Court Justice]] [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]] for her "commitment to women's rights and gender equality" and for her custom of wearing a [[Jabot (neckwear)|jabot]], a frilly neckwear which resembles the insect's neck plate.<ref name="Politico">{{cite web|last1=Nelson|first1=Louis|title=Researchers name new praying mantis species after Ruth Bader Ginsburg |url=http://www.politico.com/story/2016/06/insect-ruth-bader-ginsburg-223791|website=Politico|publisher=Politico|date=1 June 2016|accessdate=1 June 2016}}</ref><ref name="Science">{{cite web|last1=Panko|first1=Ben|title=Mantis named after Ruth Bader Ginsburg may usher in new way to classify insects|url=http://www.sciencemag.org/news/2016/06/mantis-named-after-ruth-bader-ginsburg-may-usher-new-way-classify-insects|website=Science|publisher=Science|date=1 June 2016|accessdate=1 June 2016}}</ref>
