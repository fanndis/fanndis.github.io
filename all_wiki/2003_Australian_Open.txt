The '''2003 Australian Open''' was a [[tennis]] tournament held in 2003. It was the first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event of the [[2003 ATP Tour]] and the [[2003 WTA Tour]]. It was the 91st edition of the event and attracted 512,225 spectators.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clearinghouseforsport.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0006/87036/Tennis_Australia_Annual_Report_2002-2003.pdf|title=Tennis Australia Annual Report 2002-2003|website=Clearinghouse for Sports|publisher=Tennis Australia|access-date=20 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160404025301/https://www.clearinghouseforsport.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0006/87036/Tennis_Australia_Annual_Report_2002-2003.pdf|archive-date=2016-04-04|url-status=dead}}</ref>

[[Thomas Johansson]] could not defend his 2002 title due to an injury which would rule him out for all of 2003. [[Jennifer Capriati]] was unsuccessful in her title defence, being defeated in the first round by German [[Marlene Weingärtner]]. [[Andre Agassi]] won his fourth Australian Open and final Grand Slam title, defeating [[Rainer Schüttler]] in a lopsided final. [[Serena Williams]] defeated her sister [[Venus Williams|Venus]] in the final in three sets, to win her fourth consecutive Grand Slam title to hold all four Grand Slam titles at once.

==Seniors==
===Men's Singles===
{{main|2003 Australian Open – Men's Singles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Andre Agassi]]''' defeated {{flagicon|GER}} [[Rainer Schüttler]], 6–2, 6–2, 6–1
*It was Agassi's 8th (and last) career Grand Slam title, and his 4th Australian Open title (an Open Era record until it was broken by Novak Djokovic in 2015).

===Women's Singles===
{{main|2003 Australian Open – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]'''<ref>Serena completed the "[[Serena Slam]]", winning all four Grand Slam tournaments in a row.</ref> defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Venus Williams]], 7–6<sup>(7–4)</sup>, 3–6, 6–4
*It was Serena's 5th career Grand Slam title, her 4th in a row, and her 1st Australian Open title. this also marks Serena claiming a Career [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] and first of two [[Serena Slam]]s.
