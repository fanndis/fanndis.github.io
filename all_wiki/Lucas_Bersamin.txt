'''Lucas Purugganan Bersamin''' (born October 18, 1949) is the incumbent chairperson of the [[Government Service Insurance System]] and a member of its board of trustees.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Gita-Carlos|first=Ruth Abbey|url=https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1093829|title=Ex-Chief Justice Bersamin named GSIS chair|date=February 14, 2020|work=[[Philippine News Agency]]|access-date=February 14, 2020}}</ref>
He was the [[Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines|25th Chief Justice]] of the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines|Philippine Supreme Court]], the highest court in the [[Philippines]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://leagueonlinenews.com/news/bersamin-is-the-new-cj/|title=Metro News Today: Bersamin Is The New Chief Justice|date=November 28, 2018|work=League Online News}}</ref> He was named by then [[President of the Philippines|President]] [[Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo]] to the High Court as an Associate Justice on April 2, 2009. Prior to becoming an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court, he was a member of the [[Philippine Court of Appeals|Court of Appeals]].<ref name=gman>{{cite news|title=Arroyo appoints CA Justice Bersamin to High Court |url=http://www.gmanews.tv/story/155266/Arroyo-appoints-CA-Justice-Bersamin-to-high-court |publisher=GMANews.TV |date=2009-04-02 |accessdate=2009-04-02 }}</ref>

==Biography==
Justice Bersamin earned his undergraduate degree from the [[University of the Philippines]] and graduated from the [[University of the East]] [[University of the East College of Law|College of Law]] in 1973. He placed 9th in the 1973 Bar Examinations with an average of 86.3%. Justice Bersamin was then named Commonwealth Judicial Education Institute in [[Dalhousie University]] in [[Halifax Regional Municipality|Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://sc.judiciary.gov.ph/jbersamin.php |title=Justice Lucas P. Bersamin |website=Supreme Court of the Philippines |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120123143738/http://sc.judiciary.gov.ph/jbersamin.php |archive-date=2012-01-23 |url-status=dead |access-date=2012-11-26}}</ref>

He was in private practice from 1974 until 1986, when he was appointed a trial court judge in Quezon City by President [[Corazon Aquino]]. Justice Bersamin was a professor at the [[Ateneo Law School]], the [[University of the East|University of the East College of Law]], and the [[University of Santo Tomas|University of Santo Tomas Faculty of Civil Law]]. He was special lecturer at the College of Law, University of Cebu in 2006. He continues to lecture for the Philippine Judicial Academy.

In 2003, Bersamin was elevated to the Court of Appeals by President Arroyo. In April 2009, through a midnight appointment, Bersamin was elevated by President [[Gloria Macapagal Arroyo]] to Associate Justice of the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines]], a few months before she stepped down from the presidency amid corruption cases that were going to be filed against her.

He was one of the associate justices who voted in favor of the [[Quo warranto petition against Maria Lourdes Sereno|''quo warranto'' petition against Maria Lourdes Sereno]],<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2018/05/11/1814263/sc-votes-oust-sereno |title=SC votes to oust Sereno |last=Patag |first=Kristine Joy |date=May 11, 2018 |work=Philstar.com}}</ref> which led to the appointment of Teresita de Castro as new Chief Justice, replacing [[Maria Lourdes Sereno]]. President [[Rodrigo Duterte]] appointed Bersamin as the new [[Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines|Chief Justice of the Supreme Court]] on November 26, 2018, succeeding [[Teresita de Castro|Teresita Leonardo-De Castro]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.gmanetwork.com/news/news/nation/676359/sc-associate-justice-bersamin-named-new-chief-justice/story/ |title=SC Associate Justice Bersamin named new Chief Justice |last=Lopez |first=Virgil |date=November 28, 2018 |work=GMA News Online |access-date=2019-06-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/news/11/28/18/lucas-bersamin-is-the-new-chief-justice-source |title=Lucas Bersamin is the new Chief Justice: source |date=November 28, 2018 |work=ABS-CBN News}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2018/11/28/1872454/bersamin-dutertes-new-chief-justice |title=Bersamin is Duterte’s new chief justice |last=Patag |first=Kristine Joy |date=November 28, 2018 |work=Philstar.com}}</ref>

On February 6, 2020, Bersamin was appointed by President Duterte as the chairperson of the [[Government Service Insurance System]] and a member of the agency's board of trustees. He will serve the unexpired term of former GSIS President Jesus Clint Aranas that will end on June 30, 2020.<ref name=":0" />

He is the brother of former congressman Luis "Chito" P. Bersamin, Jr. of Abra, who was [[2006 in the Philippines#December|gunned down]] during a wedding ceremony<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gmanews.tv/story/81265/Bersamins-brother-elated-over-murder-raps-filed-vs-Valera|title=Bersamin’s brother elated over murder raps filed vs Valera|date=February 19, 2008}}</ref> and brother of Abra former provincial governor Eustaquio P. Bersamin.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Romero|first=Purple S.|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/nation/04/02/09/update-bersamin-new-sc-justice|title=(Update) Bersamin is new SC justice|date=April 2, 2009|work=abs-cbnNEWS.com/Newsbreak}}</ref>
