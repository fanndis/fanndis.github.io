The 2019 [[Nobel Peace Prize]] was awarded to the [[Prime Minister of Ethiopia]] [[Abiy Ahmed Ali]] "for his efforts to achieve peace and international cooperation, and in particular for his decisive initiative to resolve the [[Eritrean–Ethiopian War|border conflict with neighbouring Eritrea]]."<ref>{{cite web |title=The Nobel Peace Prize 2019 |url=https://www.nobelpeaceprize.org/Announcements/The-Nobel-Peace-Prize-2019 |website=The Nobel Peace Prize |date=11 October 2019 |accessdate=11 October 2019}}</ref> The award was announced by the [[Norwegian Nobel Committee]] on 11 October 2019.

== Candidates ==
The 16-year old Swedish climate activist [[Greta Thunberg]] was considered the favorite.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bunyan |first1=Rachael |title=Here Are the Favorites to Win the 2019 Nobel Peace Prize |url=https://time.com/5691777/nobel-peace-prize-predictions-2019/ |website=Time |date=7 October 2019 |accessdate=11 October 2019 |language=en}}</ref> Other candidates included the Ethiopian Prime Minister [[Abiy Ahmed]] for a peace treaty in the [[Eritrean–Ethiopian War]], the Brazilian environmentalist [[Raoni Metuktire]], and the organization [[Reporters Without Borders]] which advocates for freedom of information and freedom of the press.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Sullivan |first1=Rory |title=A schoolgirl, a prime minister and an indigenous leader: Meet the Nobel Peace Prize favorites| url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/10/10/world/nobel-peace-prize-2019-favorites-scli-intl/index.html |publisher=CNN |date=10 October 2019| accessdate=11 October 2019}}</ref>

== Nobel Committee ==
Tasked with reviewing nominations from September of the previous year through February 1 and ultimately selecting the Prize winners,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://mainichi.jp/english/articles/20181204/p2a/00m/0na/004000c |title=News Navigator: Why is the Nobel Peace Prize awarded in Norway? | author=<!--Not stated--> |date=4 December 2018 |newspaper=Mainichi Daily News |accessdate=7 October 2019}}</ref> the members of the Norwegian Nobel Committee for 2019 were:<ref>{{cite web |title=The Norwegian Nobel Committee |url=https://www.nobelpeaceprize.org/Organization/Nobel-Committee |website=The Nobel Peace Prize |accessdate=7 October 2019}}</ref>

*[[Berit Reiss-Andersen]] (chair, born 1954), [[advocate]] (barrister) and President of the [[Norwegian Bar Association]], former [[State Secretary (Norway)|state secretary]] for the [[Minister of Justice and the Police (Norway)|Minister of Justice and the Police]] (representing the [[Norwegian Labour Party|Labour Party]]). Member of the Norwegian Nobel Committee since 2012, reappointed for the period 2018–2023.
*[[Henrik Syse]] (vice-chair, born 1966), Research Professor at the [[Peace Research Institute Oslo]]. Member of the Committee since 2015, appointed for the period 2015–2020.
*[[Thorbjørn Jagland]] (born 1950), former Member of Parliament and [[President of the Storting]] and former Prime Minister for the [[Norwegian Labour Party|Labour Party]], current Secretary-General of the [[Council of Europe]]. Chair of the Norwegian Nobel Committee from 2009 to 2015. Currently regular member. Member of the Committee since 2009, reappointed for the period 2015–2020.
*[[Anne Enger]] (born 1949), former Leader of the [[Centre Party (Norway)|Centre Party]] and [[Minister of Culture and Church Affairs|Minister of Culture]]. Appointed for the period 2018–2020.
*[[Asle Toje]] (born 1974), foreign policy scholar. Appointed for the period 2018–2023.
