{{short description|Musical note}}
'''C{{music|sharp}}''' ('''C-sharp''') is a musical note lying a [[chromatic semitone]] above [[C (musical note)|C]] and a [[diatonic semitone]] below [[D (musical note)|D]]. C-sharp is thus [[enharmonic]] to [[D♭ (musical note)|D{{music|flat}}]]. It is the second semitone in the French [[solfège]] and is known there as ''do dièse''. In some European notations, it is known as '''Cis'''. In equal temperament it is also enharmonic with B{{music|double sharp}} (Hisis).

When calculated in [[equal temperament]] with a reference of [[A (musical note)|A]] above [[middle C]] as 440 [[hertz|Hz]], the [[frequency]] of C{{music|sharp}}<sub>4</sub> (the C{{music|sharp}} above middle C) is about 277.18&nbsp;Hz. See [[pitch (music)]] for a discussion of historical variations in frequency.
