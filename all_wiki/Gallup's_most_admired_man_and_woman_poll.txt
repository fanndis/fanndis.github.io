'''Gallup's most admired man and woman poll''' is an annual poll that [[The Gallup Organization|Gallup]] has conducted at the end of most years since 1948.<ref>{{cite web|last=Panetta|first=Grace|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/donald-trump-barack-obama-tie-2019-most-admired-man-gallup-2019-12|title=Donald Trump and Barack Obama are tied for 2019's most admired man in the US|work=[[Business Insider]] |date=December 30, 2019|access-date=July 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Seiger|first=Theresa|url=https://www.kiro7.com/news/trending/trump-obama-share-title-most-admired-man-annual-gallup-poll/QZAJKUAJDNCB5BUKI3AMDF6X5I/|title=Trump, Obama share title as most admired man in annual Gallup poll|work=[[KIRO-TV]]|date=December 30, 2019|access-date=July 24, 2020}}</ref> Americans are asked, without prompting, to say what man and woman "living today in any part of the world, do [they] admire most?"<ref name="g07">{{cite web|url=http://www.gallup.com/poll/103462/hillary-edges-oprah-most-admired-woman-07.aspx |title=Hillary Edges Out Oprah as Most Admired Woman in ‘07 |publisher=Gallup |date=December 26, 2007 |accessdate=December 28, 2010}}</ref> The result is published as a top ten list. Most years the most admired man is the sitting [[President of the United States]] and the most admired woman is or has been the [[First Lady of the United States]].<ref>{{cite book | title=Social Research for Consumers | first=Earl R. | last=Babbie | publisher=[[Wadsworth Publishing Company]] | date=1982 | page=14}}</ref>

Gallup's poll is announced during the last week of each year, and the results typically gets mainstream media coverage at the time.<ref>See for example {{cite news | url=https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/michelle-obama-voted-most-admired-woman-bumps-hillary-clinton-top-n952391 | title=Michelle Obama voted most admired woman, bumps Hillary Clinton from top spot: Gallup | first=Elisha | last=Fieldstadt | publisher=[[NBC News]] | date=December 27, 2018}}; {{cite news | url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-27/obama-tops-trump-as-most-admired-gallup-poll-of-americans-finds | title=Obama Tops Trump as Most Admired, Gallup Poll of Americans Finds | first= Terrence | last=Dopp | publisher=[[Bloomberg News]] | date=December 27, 2017}}; {{cite news | url=https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/2016-12-28/gallup-barack-obama-beats-donald-trump-for-most-admired-man | title=Gallup: Obama Is Most-Admired Man in the World | first=Gabrielle | last=Levy | newspaper=[[U.S. News & World Report]] | date=December 28, 2016}}; and so forth.</ref>  The survey has been considered, in part, a test of [[name recognition]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://people.com/politics/michelle-obama-most-admired-woman-gallup-poll-hillary-clinton-melania-trump/ | title=Michelle Obama Tops List of Most Admired Women As Hillary Clinton and Melania Trump Tie for Third | first=Chris | last=Harris | magazine=[[People (magazine)|People]] | date=December 27, 2018}}</ref>  While places at the top of the list are often predictable, scholars have found appearances further down in the top ten to be illuminating, such as in 1958 when segregationist governor [[Orval Faubus]] made the list in the wake of the [[Little Rock Nine]] civil rights episode.<ref>{{cite book | title=The Hollow Hope: Can Courts Bring About Social Change? | url=https://archive.org/details/hollowhopecancou00rose_735 | url-access=limited | edition=Second | first=Gerald N. | last=Rosenberg | publisher=[[University of Chicago Press]] | date=2008 | page=[https://archive.org/details/hollowhopecancou00rose_735/page/n141 129]}}</ref>

==Trends and top finishers==
Sitting U.S. presidents have captured the top spot for men in 60 out of 73 years.<ref name="g18">{{cite news | url=https://news.gallup.com/poll/245669/michelle-obama-ends-hillary-clinton-run-admired.aspx | title=Michelle Obama Ends Hillary Clinton's Run as Most Admired | first=Jeffrey M. | last=Jones | publisher=Gallup | date=December 27, 2018}}</ref>
In his lifetime, evangelist [[Billy Graham]] had 61 appearances in the top 10, the most of anyone, with eight second-place finishes.<ref name="g18"/> The second most is [[Ronald Reagan]] with 31, and [[Jimmy Carter]] with 28 and [[Pope John Paul II]] with 27 follow.<ref name="g13">{{cite press release |url= http://www.gallup.com/poll/166646/obama-clinton-continue-reign-admired-man-woman.aspx |title=Obama, Clinton Continue Reign as Most Admired |publisher=Gallup |date=December 30, 2013 |accessdate=December 30, 2012}}</ref> The sitting pope has finished in the top ten in every year since 1977.<ref name="g13"/>

Among women, the poll has shown [[Eleanor Roosevelt]], [[Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis]], and [[Hillary Rodham Clinton]] as U.S. First Ladies with the most appearances on the list. The high mark for top 10 appearances belongs to [[Queen Elizabeth II]] with 50 through 2018.<ref name="g18"/> Although never winning, broadcaster [[Oprah Winfrey]] has finished in the top 10 a total of 31 times through 2018, including finishing second 14 times.<ref name="g18"/>  The high mark for most appearances at number 1, the most admired woman in the world, belongs to Hillary Clinton, who served as First Lady of Arkansas from 1983 to 1992, [[First Lady of the United States]] during the presidency of [[Bill Clinton]] from 1993 to 2001, [[United States Senator]] representing [[New York (state)|New York]] from 2001 to 2009, and [[United States Secretary of State]] from 2009 to 2013. She ranked first 22 times out of 25 during the years 1993–2017, including 16 in a row from 2002 to 2017, before her dominance was broken in 2018 by [[Michelle Obama]].<ref name="g18"/> In 1980, [[Mother Teresa]] and [[Rosalynn Carter]] tied for most admired woman, and in 2019, [[Barack Obama]] and [[Donald Trump]] shared the title of most admired man.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.gallup.com/poll/273125/obama-trump-tie-admired-man-2019.aspx|title=Obama, Trump Tie as Most Admired Man in 2019|date=December 30, 2019}}</ref>
