"'''Rehab'''" is a song recorded by [[Barbadian people|Barbadian]] singer [[Rihanna]] for her third studio album, ''[[Good Girl Gone Bad (Rihanna album)|Good Girl Gone Bad]]'' (2007). [[Def Jam Recordings]] serviced the song to [[contemporary hit radio]] in the United States on October 6,&nbsp;2008, as the eighth and final single from the album. It was released in the United Kingdom as a [[CD single]] on December 8, 2008.

Development of "Rehab" began while Rihanna was accompanying [[Timbaland]] on [[Justin Timberlake]]'s [[FutureSex/LoveShow]] tour in 2007. Timberlake wrote the song in collaboration with his producers, Hannon Lane and Timbaland, and provided additional vocals. "Rehab" is a mid-paced [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] song with an emotional, melancholy chorus; the lyrics are about the protagonist's painful memories of her former lover, who is portrayed metaphorically as a disease.

"Rehab" reached top-ten positions on the singles charts in Austria, Germany, the Netherlands and Norway. It reached number 16 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and number 18 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine's [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]]. It was certified double platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). [[Anthony Mandler]] directed the accompanying music video, which was shot in [[Vasquez Rocks Park]], near [[Los Angeles]]. It won the [[Urban Music Awards|Urban Music Award]] for Best Music Video. Rihanna performed "Rehab" on the [[Good Girl Gone Bad Tour]] (2007–09), and occasionally on the [[Last Girl on Earth]] (2010–11).

==Composition==
"Rehab" is a mid-[[tempo]] [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] [[sentimental ballad|ballad]] with a subtle [[backbeat]].<ref name="EW"/><ref name="IGN"/> According to the digital sheet music published by [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing]], it is written in the key of [[G minor]] and set in [[common time]], with a moderately slow [[Groove (music)|groove]].<ref name="Musicnotes"/> The vocal range spans nearly an octave and a half from [[F (musical note)|F<sub>3</sub>]] to [[B♭ (musical note)|B{{music|flat}}<sub>4</sub>]].<ref name="Musicnotes">{{cite web|url= http://www.musicnotes.com/sheetmusic/mtdVPE.asp?ppn=MN0071889|title=Digital Sheet Music – Rihanna – Rehab|publisher= [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing]]|accessdate= July 26, 2007}}</ref> "Rehab" opens with a string section, played by Stevie Blacke, and a [[violin]], [[cello]], and [[tambourine]] instrumental groove with Hannon Lane's keyboard melody.<ref name="Notes"/><ref name="IGN"/> Rihanna then sings the opening lines, "Baby, baby, when we first met, I never felt something so strong".<ref name="Musicnotes"/>

Critics noted similarities among the structures of "Rehab" and some of Timberlake's songs, such as "[[What Goes Around... Comes Around]]" and "[[Cry Me a River (Justin Timberlake song)|Cry Me a River]]".<ref name="PopMatters"/><ref name="Metro">{{cite news|url=http://www.metroweekly.com/arts_entertainment/music.php?ak=2843|title=Bad Girl: With her new album, Rihanna proves herself to be the new bad girl of our good-pop dreams|work=[[Metro Weekly]]|publisher=Jansi LLC|last=Rule|first=Doug|date=July 5, 2007|accessdate=September 9, 2010|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.metroweekly.com%2Farts_entertainment%2Fmusic.php%3Fak%3D2843&date=2011-09-29|archivedate=September 29, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> Spence D. of [[IGN]] wrote that the song has an "R&B swoon", similar to Rihanna's previous singles.<ref name="IGN"/> In an interview with Margeaux Watson for ''Entertainment Weekly'', Rihanna explained the meaning of the song's lyrics: "'Rehab' is a metaphorical song. Rehab really just means we have to get over the guy. So we talk about checking ourselves into rehab, meaning we have to get over him. And we compare the guy to a disease or an addiction."<ref name="Lyrics">{{cite web|url=https://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20043289_20043293_20043298,00.html|title=Caribbean Queen|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|last=Watson|first=Margeaux|date=June 21, 2007|accessdate=September 9, 2010|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ew.com%2Few%2Farticle%2F0%2C%2C20043289_20043293_20043298%2C00.html&date=2011-09-29|archivedate=September 29, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> Watson further called the song a "lovesick ballad".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music-mix.ew.com/2008/11/13/rihanna-goes-to/|title=Rihanna goes to 'Rehab'|work=Entertainment Weekly|last=Watson|first=Margeaux|date=November 12, 2008|accessdate=September 8, 2009|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fmusic-mix.ew.com%2F2008%2F11%2F13%2Frihanna-goes-to%2F&date=2011-09-29|archivedate=September 29, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Chart performance==
"Rehab" entered the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart at number 91 on November 22,&nbsp;2008,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/2008-11-22/hot-100?order=gainer|title=Billboard Hot 100|work=Billboard|accessdate=October 6, 2011|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.billboard.com%2F%23%2Fcharts%2Fhot-100%3FchartDate%3D2008-11-22%26order%3Dgainer&date=2011-12-10|archivedate=December 10, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> and peaked at number 18, making it Rihanna's twelfth US top-twenty single.<ref name="US"/> It also peaked at number 17 on ''Billboard'''s [[Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)|Pop Songs]] chart<ref name="POP"/> and at number 52 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.<ref name="R&B"/> The song rose to number 19 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] chart, having started at number 56 on December 6, 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/2008-12-06/canadian-hot-100?order=gainer|title=Canadian Hot 100|work=Billboard|accessdate=October 6, 2011|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.billboard.com%2F%23%2Fcharts%2Fhot-100%3FchartDate%3D2008-11-22%26order%3Dgainer&date=2011-12-10|archivedate=December 10, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> It entered the [[ARIA Charts|Australian ARIA Singles Chart]] at number 37 on November 17,&nbsp;2008 and eventually reached number 26, thus becoming Rihanna's twelfth consecutive top-thirty single in Australia.<ref name="AUS1"/> In New Zealand, it first entered the singles chart at number 24 on October 27,&nbsp;2008, and rose to number 12.<ref name="NZ"/> On January 11,&nbsp;2009, it was certified gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of New Zealand]] (RIANZ), 12 weeks after it first charted.<ref name="nzcert"/>

"Rehab" first appeared in the [[UK Singles Chart]] at number 51 on November 22,&nbsp;2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/singles/Rehab|title=Rihanna - Rehab|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=December 5, 2011|df=}}</ref> After two weeks, it became the greatest gainer on the chart, climbing twenty-seven places to number 24,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20081130/7501/|title=2008-12-06 Top 40 Official UK Singles Archive|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=November 24, 2011}}</ref> and in the week after that, it reached its peak of 16.<ref name="UKs"/> More than 160,000 copies of the song have been sold in the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/music/charts/official-uk-countdowns/rihanna-official-top-20|title=Rihanna – Official Top 20 Selling Singles|publisher=MTV.
