==Events==
* [[475]] &ndash; The [[Roman Empire|Roman]] general [[Orestes (father of Romulus Augustulus)|Orestes]] forces western [[Roman Emperors|Roman Emperor]] [[Julius Nepos]] to flee his [[capital city]], [[Ravenna]].
* [[489]] &ndash; [[Theodoric the Great|Theodoric]], king of the [[Ostrogoths]], defeats [[Odoacer]] at the [[Battle of Isonzo (489)|Battle of Isonzo]], forcing his way into Italy.
* [[632]] &ndash; [[Fatimah]], daughter of the Islamic prophet [[Muhammad]], dies, with her cause of death being a controversial topic among the [[Sunni]] Muslims and [[Shia]] Muslims.
* [[663]] &ndash; [[Silla]]–[[Tang dynasty|Tang]] armies crush the [[Baekje]] restoration attempt and force [[Yamato period|Yamato Japan]] to withdraw from Korea in the [[Battle of Baekgang]].
*[[1189]] &ndash; [[Third Crusade]]: The Crusaders begin the [[Siege of Acre (1189–91)|Siege of Acre]] under [[Guy of Lusignan]].
*[[1521]] &ndash; The [[Ottoman Turks]] occupy [[Belgrade]].
*[[1524]] &ndash; The [[Kaqchikel people|Kaqchikel]] [[Maya civilization|Maya]] rebel against their former Spanish allies during the [[Spanish conquest of Guatemala]].
*[[1542]] &ndash; [[Ottoman–Portuguese conflicts (1538–59)|Turkish–Portuguese War]]: [[Battle of Wofla]]: The Portuguese are scattered, their leader [[Christovão da Gama]] is captured and later executed.
*[[1565]] &ndash; [[Pedro Menéndez de Avilés]] sights land near [[St. Augustine, Florida]] and founds the oldest continuously occupied European-established city in the [[Contiguous United States|continental United States]].
*[[1609]] &ndash; [[Henry Hudson]] discovers [[Delaware Bay]].
*[[1619]] &ndash; Election of [[Ferdinand II, Holy Roman Emperor]].
*[[1640]] &ndash; [[Bishops' Wars#Second Bishops' War (1640)|Second Bishop's War]]: King [[Charles I of England|Charles I]]'s English army loses to a [[Covenanter|Scottish Covenanter]] force at the [[Battle of Newburn]].
*[[1648]] &ndash; The [[Siege of Colchester]] ends when Royalists Forces surrender to the Parliamentary Forces after eleven weeks, during the [[Second English Civil War]].
*[[1709]] &ndash; [[Pamheiba|Meidingnu Pamheiba]] is crowned King of [[Manipur]].
*[[1789]] &ndash; [[William Herschel]] discovers a new moon of Saturn: [[Enceladus]].
*[[1810]] &ndash; [[Battle of Grand Port]]: The French accept the surrender of a [[British Navy]] fleet.
*[[1830]] &ndash; The [[Baltimore and Ohio Railroad]]'s new ''[[Tom Thumb (locomotive)|Tom Thumb]]'' steam locomotive races a horse-drawn car, presaging steam's role in U.S. railroads.
*[[1845]] &ndash; The first issue of ''[[Scientific American]]'' magazine is published.
*[[1849]] &ndash; After a month-long siege, [[Venice]], which had declared itself independent as the [[Republic of San Marco]], surrenders to Austria.
*[[1859]] &ndash; The [[Solar storm of 1859|Carrington event]] is the strongest [[geomagnetic storm]] on record to strike the Earth. [[Electrical telegraph]] service is widely disrupted.
*[[1861]] &ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] forces attack [[Cape Hatteras]], [[North Carolina]] in the [[Battle of Hatteras Inlet Batteries]] which lasts for two days.
*[[1862]] &ndash; American Civil War: [[Second Battle of Bull Run]], also known as the Battle of Second Manassas.  The battle ends on August 30.  
*[[1867]] &ndash; The United States takes possession of the (at this point unoccupied) [[Midway Atoll]].
*[[1879]] &ndash; [[Cetshwayo]], last king of the [[Zulu people|Zulu]]s, is captured by the [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|British]].
*[[1898]] &ndash; [[Caleb Bradham]]'s beverage "Brad's Drink" is renamed "[[Pepsi|Pepsi-Cola]]".
*[[1901]] &ndash; [[Silliman University]] is founded in the [[Philippines]]. It is the first American [[private school]] in the country.
*[[1909]] &ndash; A group of mid-level [[Greek Army]] officers launches the [[Goudi coup]], seeking wide-ranging reforms.
*[[1913]] &ndash; [[Wilhelmina of the Netherlands|Queen Wilhelmina]] opens the [[Peace Palace]] in [[The Hague]].
*[[1914]] &ndash; [[World War I]]: The [[Royal Navy]] defeats the [[German Empire|German]] fleet in the [[Battle of Heligoland Bight (1914)|Battle of Heligoland Bight]].
*[[1916]] &ndash; World War I: Germany declares war on [[Kingdom of Romania|Romania]].
*  1916   &ndash; World War I: [[Kingdom of Italy|Italy]] declares war on Germany.
*[[1917]] &ndash; Ten [[Suffragettes]] are arrested while picketing the [[White House]].
*[[1921]] &ndash; The [[Red Army]] dissolved the [[Free Territory]], after driving the [[Revolutionary Insurrectionary Army of Ukraine|Black Army]] out of [[Ukrainian War of Independence|Ukraine]].
*[[1924]] &ndash; The [[Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic|Georgian]] opposition stages the [[August Uprising]] against the [[Soviet Union]].
*[[1937]] &ndash; [[Toyota Motors]] becomes an independent company.
*[[1943]] &ndash; [[Denmark in World War II]]: German authorities demand that Danish authorities crack down on acts of resistance. The next day, [[martial law]] is imposed on Denmark.
*[[1944]] &ndash; World War II: [[Marseille]] and [[Toulon]] are liberated.
*[[1955]] &ndash; [[African Americans|Black]] teenager [[Emmett Till]] is brutally murdered in [[Mississippi]], galvanizing the nascent [[civil rights movement]].
*[[1957]] &ndash; [[United States Senate|U.S. Senator]] [[Strom Thurmond]] begins [[Strom Thurmond filibuster of the Civil Rights Act of 1957|a filibuster]] to prevent the Senate from voting on the [[Civil Rights Act of 1957]]; he stopped speaking 24 hours and 18 minutes later, the longest filibuster ever conducted by a single Senator.
*[[1963]] &ndash; [[March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom]]: Rev. Dr. [[Martin Luther King Jr.]] gives his ''[[I Have a Dream]]'' speech.
*[[1964]] &ndash; The [[1964 Philadelphia race riot|Philadelphia race riot]] begins.
*[[1968]] &ndash; Police and protesters clash during [[1968 Democratic National Convention protest activity]].<ref>{{cite news|newspaper=The Guardian|location=London |accessdate=July 29, 2019|title=The whole world is watching|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2018/aug/19/the-whole-world-is-watching-chicago-police-riot-vietnam-war-regan}}</ref>
*[[1988]] &ndash; [[Ramstein air show disaster]]: Three aircraft of the [[Frecce Tricolori]] demonstration team collide and the wreckage falls into the crowd. Seventy-five are killed and 346 seriously injured.
*[[1990]] &ndash; [[Iraq]] declares [[Kuwait]] to be its newest [[Governorates of Iraq|province]].
* 1990 &ndash; [[1990 Plainfield tornado|An F5 tornado]] strikes the [[Illinois]] cities of [[Plainfield, Illinois|Plainfield]] and [[Joliet, Illinois|Joliet]], killing 29 people.
*[[1993]] &ndash; NASA's [[Galileo (spacecraft)|''Galileo'']] probe performs a [[243 Ida#Galileo flyby|flyby of the asteroid 243 Ida]]. Astronomers later discover a moon, the first known [[Minor-planet moon|asteroid moon]], in pictures from the flyby and name it Dactyl.
*[[1998]] &ndash; [[Pakistan]]'s [[National Assembly of Pakistan|National Assembly]] passes a [[Constitution of Pakistan|constitutional]] amendment to make the "[[Qur'an]] and [[Sunnah]]" the "supreme law" but the bill is defeated in the [[Senate of Pakistan|Senate]].
*  1998   &ndash; [[Second Congo War]]: [[Armed Forces of the Democratic Republic of the Congo|Loyalist troops]] backed by [[Angola]]n and [[Zimbabwean]] forces repulse the [[Rally for Congolese Democracy|RCD]] and [[Rwanda]]n offensive on [[Kinshasa]].
*[[2003]] &ndash; In "one of the most complicated and bizarre crimes in the annals of the [[FBI]]", [[Murder of Brian Wells|Brian Wells]] dies after becoming involved in a complex plot involving a [[bank robbery]], a [[scavenger hunt]], and a [[Improvised explosive device|homemade explosive device]].<ref>Drew Griffin and David Fitzpatrick (August 8, 2011) [http://www.cnn.com/2011/CRIME/08/05/pizza.bomb/ New details revealed in 'pizza collar bomb' heist], CNN.com, accessed 13 May 2018</ref>
