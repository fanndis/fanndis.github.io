'''Qai Qai''' ({{IPAc-en||k|w|aɪ|k|w|aɪ}}), is a doll belonging to Alexis Olympia Ohanian, the daughter of American professional tennis player and winner of 23 [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] titles, [[Serena Williams]], and co-founder of [[Reddit]] and [[Initialized Capital]], [[Alexis Ohanian|Alexis Ohanian Sr]]. She has become popular on social media as of 2018, with many pictures of her face digitized and animated to have various expressions.

==Background==
Alexis Ohanian and Serena Williams' daughter, Olympia, was born on September 1, 2017 in [[West Palm Beach, Florida]].<ref name="uwlupohautman">{{cite web|first=Nicholas|last=Hautman|first2=Marc|last2=Lupo|date=1 September 2017|title=Serena Williams Welcomes Her First Child!|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-moms/news/serena-williams-gives-birth-welcomes-first-child-with-alexis-ohanian-w492788|website=Us Weekly|accessdate=17 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Elise Solé |url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/serena-williams-baby-girl-meaning-behind-name-173808990.html |title=Serena Williams's baby girl and the meaning behind her name |publisher=Yahoo! |date=13 September 2017 |accessdate=4 January 2019}}</ref> When Williams bought the doll for Olympia, she indicated that she wanted her daughter's first doll to be black because growing up, she did not have many opportunities to own a black doll.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/sports/tennis/daughters-celebrity-doll-qai-qai-holds-special-place-in-serena-williams-family/ar-BBSgrfg|title=Daughter's celebrity doll 'Qai Qai' holds special place in Serena Williams' family|author=|date=|website=www.msn.com|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://atlantablackstar.com/2019/01/18/serena-williams-explains-why-it-was-important-for-her-daughters-first-doll-to-be-black/ |title=Serena Williams Explains Why It Was Important for Her Daughter’s First Doll To Be Black |website=Atlanta Black Star |first=Kiersten |last=Willis |date=18 January 2019 |accessdate=15 October 2020}}</ref> Qai Qai got her name from Williams's nephew.<ref name="oprahmag whois">{{cite web|url=https://www.oprahmag.com/life/a25905219/serena-williams-daughter-doll-qai-qai/|title=Who Is Qai Qai? Meet the Special Member of Serena Williams's Family|first=Michelle|last=Darrisaw|date=15 January 2019|website=Oprah Magazine|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref>

==Social media presence==
Qai Qai's first post on her own Instagram was on August 22, 2018 where she is lying on the floor.<ref name="the cut">{{cite web|url=https://www.thecut.com/2018/09/qai-qai-granddaughter-doll-of-serena-williams-who-is-she.html|title=Meet Serena Williams's Daughter's Doll, Instagram Influencer|first=Kelly|last=Conaboy|date=14 September 2018|website=The Cut|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref><!-- The Cut, fashion blog of New York magazine -->  She also joined Twitter that month, although the account has not been officially verified.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://twitter.com/RealQaiQai|title=Qai Qai (@RealQaiQai) | work=Twitter | accessdate=8 November 2019}}</ref>

Popularity and fandom has increased for Qai Qai.  While likely run by Olympia's parents, [[Serena Williams]] and [[Alexis Ohanian]], it is unknown who administers the doll's account.<ref name="w magazine">{{cite web|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/serena-williams-alexis-ohanian-qai-qai-instagram-account|title=Who Is the Mastermind Behind @RealQaiQai's Instagram Account?|first=Brooke|last=Marine|date=|website=W Magazine|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref>
Some images show Qai Qai being neglected. This spurred public concern where social media fans used the hashtag #QQPS (Qai Qai Protective Services).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.lipstickalley.com/threads/qaiqai-has-been-found-safe-well-sorta.1974256/ |title=QaiQai has been found safe! Well sorta |date=23 November 2018}}</ref>

Qai Qai has been featured in several ''[[USA Today]]'' articles,<ref name="usa today venus">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/aus/2019/01/17/australian-open-venus-williams-love-qai-qai-doll/2601310002/|title=Venus Williams loves her niece's Qai Qai doll as much as social media does|author=|date=|website=USA TODAY|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref><ref name="usa today">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2019/01/14/serena-williams-daughters-special-doll-qai-qai-has-become-celebrity/2578058002/|title=Daughter's celebrity doll 'Qai Qai' holds special place in Serena Williams' family|website=USA TODAY|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref> ''[[The Today Show|Today]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.today.com/video/qai-qai-serena-williams-daughter-s-doll-becomes-instagram-star-1428836931938|title=Qai Qai, Serena Williams' daughter's doll, becomes Instagram star|author=|date=|website=TODAY.com|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref> and [[Oprah (magazine)|''Oprah'' magazine]], the last of which she did an exclusive interview.<ref name="oprah interview">{{cite web|url=https://www.oprahmag.com/entertainment/a26521970/serena-williams-alexis-olympia-qai-qai-doll/|title=Serena Williams's Doll Qai Qai Just Gave Her First Exclusive Interview|first=Arianna|last=Davis|date=28 February 2019|website=Oprah Magazine|accessdate=19 June 2019}}</ref>

On August 29, 2019, Alexis Ohanian Sr. wore a shirt with Qai Qai's likeness to the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|2019 US Open]] match with Serena Williams vs. [[Caty McNally]].<ref name="US open match">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/mrsmaryallen/status/1166916138053197824|title=Tweet from @mrsmaryallen}}</ref> This same shirt was worn by Ohanian Sr. in an Instagram post on December 14, 2018.<ref name="Instagram Post from Dec 14, 2018">{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bsj-kxFlsKy/ |title=Alexis Ohanian Sr. on Instagram |date=14 December 2018}} </ref>  Another Qai Qai shirt was featured on Ohanian Sr.'s Instagram post.<ref name="Instagram Post from January 16, 2019">{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BsumiTPlZ0e/ |title=Alexis Ohanian Sr. on Instagram |date=16 January 2019}}</ref> This selfie shirt was part of a limited run fundraiser for the non-profit organization Zoe’s Dolls, which was founded by an eleven year old with the mission of giving brown dolls away to brown kids.<ref name="Twitter Post from Jan 12, 2018">{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/RealQaiQai/status/1072911212684476416 |title=Qai Qai on Twitter |date=12 January 2018}}</ref>

On December 2, 2019, @realqaiqai announced on Twitter that additional merchandise is available for Cyber Monday 2019 with a portion of proceeds going to Zoe's Dolls.<ref name="Twitter post from Qai Qai">{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/RealQaiQai/status/1201586242032480262 |title=Qai Qai on Twitter |date=2 December 2019}}</ref>
