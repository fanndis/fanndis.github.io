'''Alexandra Corinne Pelosi''' (born October 5, 1970) is an American journalist, documentary filmmaker, and writer. She is a daughter of the [[Speaker of the United States House of Representatives]] [[Nancy Pelosi]] and [[Paul Pelosi]].<ref name = USAToday>{{cite news | url = https://www.usatoday.com/life/television/news/2009-02-15-pelosi-right-america_N.htm  | title = 'Alexandra Pelosi takes a hard 'Right' turn in documentary'  | first = Gary | last = Strauss | date = February 15, 2009 | work=USA Today}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Pelosi was born and raised in [[San Francisco, California]]. The youngest of five children, she earned a B.A. from [[Loyola Marymount University]]. In 1993, she received a master's degree from the [[USC Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/24/|title=Alexandra Pelosi Plunges Into Trump Country|date=October 24, 2018|work=The New York Times}}</ref>

==Career==
Pelosi has filmed, produced and directed 14 documentary films, 13 with [[Sheila Nevins]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/12/16/style/sheila-nevins-leaving-hbo-documentary-films.html|title=The Grande Dame of Documentary Is Leaving Her Home at HBO|last=Dowd|first=Maureen|work=The New York Times|date=December 16, 2017|accessdate = February 3, 2018}}</ref> Her most recent film, ''[[American Selfie: One Nation Shoots Itself]]'' was released on [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]]. This was their 13th film together. <ref>{{cite web|url=
https://variety.com/2020/film/news/showtime-mtv-documentary-films-alexandra-pelosi-american-selfie-1234790702/ |title=Showtime, MTV Documentary Films Partner on Alexandra Pelosi’s ‘American Selfie’ (EXCLUSIVE). |last=Lang|first=Brent|work= Variety|date=October 2, 2020|accessdate = October 28, 2020}}</ref>

Before making documentaries, Pelosi spent a decade as a field producer at NBC News. In 2000, while working as a producer for NBC covering [[George W. Bush]]'s presidential campaign.<ref name = "New York Observer">{{cite news | url = http://observer.com/2002/11/getting-high-on-george/|title = ' Getting High on George|first   =Jason | last = Gay |   date = November 4, 2002 | work=New York Observer}}</ref> She brought along a handheld camcorder documenting 18 months of her experience on the campaign trail; the footage was used to create ''[[Journeys with George]]'',<ref name = NYTimes>{{cite news | url = https://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/05/movies/television-review-food-jokes-and-few-issues-on-the-bush-2000-campaign.html | title = ' Food, Jokes and Few Issues on the Bush 2000 Campaign | first   =Caryn | last = James | page = C01 | date = November 5, 2002 | work=The New York Times}}</ref> a documentary that earned her six [[Emmy]] nominations.

In 2001, [[Sheila Nevins]] convinced Pelosi to leave network news to work exclusively for HBO. During the [[2004 Democratic primary|2004 Democratic primaries]], Pelosi returned to the campaign trail, this time following the Democratic candidates. Her HBO documentary, ''Diary of a Political Tourist'', was accompanied by her first book ''Sneaking into the Flying Circus: How the Media Turn Our Presidential Campaigns into Freak Shows'', about the process of selecting candidates for President of the United States. She stated that her conversations with [[Candy Crowley]] of CNN, [[Howard Dean]], and [[Wesley Clark]] inspired her to write a book.

In 2006, Pelosi created the documentary about evangelical Christians called ''[[Friends of God: A Road Trip with Alexandra Pelosi]]'' which featured former pastor [[Ted Haggard]]. After it aired on HBO in 2007, she made a follow-up film, ''The Trials of Ted Haggard'', chronicling the exile of Ted Haggard from New Life Church after his sex and drug scandal, which ''The New York Times'' called "strangely intriguing".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/01/29/arts/television/29tria.html
|title= Exiled Preacher Joins the Crowd in the Virtual Confession Box
|newspaper=The New York Times|date= January 28, 2009
|last1= Stanley

|work=Los Angeles Times|date=January 29, 2009

Pelosi went back on the campaign trail in 2008 to document the birth of the [[Tea Party movement]] at Republican campaign events for her film, ''[[Right America: Feeling Wronged - Some Voices from the Campaign Trail]]'', which premiered on HBO on President's Day 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://topdocumentaryfilms.com/right-america-feeling-wronged/|title=Right America Feeling Wronged|work=TopDocumentaryFilms}}</ref> CNN reported in July 2010 that Pelosi was no longer making political documentaries.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Alexandra Pelosi discusses film on homeless in the OC|url=http://www.cnn.com/2010/SHOWBIZ/TV/07/24/pelosi.homeless/index.html|date=July 24, 2010|first=Pamela|last=Sellers|work=[[CNN]]|mode=cs2|accessdate=July 25, 2010}}</ref> Her 2010 HBO film, ''Homeless: The Motel Kids of Orange County'' follows the children of the working poor in Orange County, California.

''The New York Times'' praised the film for "advancing a theme of the failed American dream."<ref>{{Cite news|title=Growing up paupers in a land of privilege|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/07/26/arts/television/26motel.html|date=July 25, 2010|first= Mike|last=Hale|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref>

On July 4, 2011, HBO debuted Pelosi's next film ''[[Citizen USA: A 50 State Road Trip|Citizen U.S.A.: A 50 State Road Trip]].'' Pelosi traveled to all 50 states to attend naturalization ceremonies and interviewed immigrants as they became legalized American citizens. The film was released with a coffee table book titled ''[[Citizen USA: A 50 State Road Trip]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11737142-citizen-u-s-a|title=Citizen U.S.A. A 50 State Roadtrip|work=Good Reads}}</ref>

Pelosi was at the 2013 [[Sundance Film Festival]] with her film ''[[Fall to Grace (documentary)|Fall to Grace]]'',
<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E5K3ZVsULiU|title= Alexandra Pelosi talks Sundance|work=The Hollywood Reporter}}</ref> about disgraced former New Jersey governor [[Jim McGreevey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2013/01/alexandra-pelosi-sundance-debut-has-political-angle-086449|title=Alexandra Pelosi Sundance debut has political angle|work=Politico}}</ref> She was spotted around Park City with fellow headliner [[Kenneth Cole (designer)|Kenneth Cole]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zimbio.com/photos/Kenneth+Cole/Alexandra+Pelosi|title=Brunch hosted by Kenneth Cole|work=Zimbio}}</ref>

In 2015, Pelosi returned to San Francisco to make a film about the tech boom's impact on the city. According to [[Recode]], it's "a clear-eyed, sober recap of what's been going on...Pelosi's tale is also deeply personal; she grew up in San Francisco, but she has lived in New York for a long time. A key theme of the documentary is that the San Francisco to which she's returning is very different from the one she left." <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.recode.net/2015/9/27/11618910/alexandra-pelosis-new-hbo-documentary-isnt-the-anti-tech-movie-you|title =Alexandra Pelosi's new documentary isn't the anti-tech movie you think it is|work = Recode|last = Kulwin |first = Noah|date =September 27, 2015}}</ref> Variety called ''[[San Francisco 2.0]]'' "one of her finest."<ref>{{cite web|title = TV Review: HBO's 'Fixing the System' With President Obama, 'San Francisco 2.0'|url = https://variety.com/2015/tv/reviews/fixing-the-system-vice-special-report-president-obama-san-francisco-2-0-alexandra-pelosi-hbo-documentary-review-1201598084/|website = Variety|accessdate = 2015-10-31|last = Lowry|first = Brian|date = 2015-09-24}}</ref> San Francisco 2.0 was nominated for an Emmy for best business reporting.<ref>{{cite web|title = Emmy Nominations|url = http://emmyonline.com/download/news_37th_nominations_Revised_10.04.16.pdf|website = Emmysonline}}</ref>

In 2016, Pelosi made ''[[Meet the Donors: Does Money Talk?]]'' about money's influence in politics.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2016/08/01/entertainment/meet-the-donors-alexandra-pelosi-documentary-review/|author=Lowry, Brian|title=Alexandra Pelosi's 'Meet the Donors' exposes billionaires who buy into politics|date=August 1, 2016|accessdate=August 6, 2016}}</ref> In a profile in ''Vogue'', Pelosi calls her film a "light romp into the road map of the people and places that are funding our elections." The film drops in on a handful of folks who rank on the [[OpenSecrets.org]] list of top donors.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/meet-the-donors-hbo-alexandra-pelosi-interview|title= Alexandra Pelosi Romps Through the World of Mega Donors|date=August 1, 2016}}</ref>

[[Uproxx]] described it as watching "Pelosi meet with an assortment of billionaire donors, asking them why they give millions to candidates, how this funding affects campaigns, and all the access these hefty donations can get you." On the press tour for the film, Pelosi talked about everything she has learned in her lifetime on the political fundraising circuit<ref>{{cite news|url=https://uproxx.com/tv/alexandra-pelosi-interview-meet-the-donors/|title=Alexandra Pelosi interview|date=August 1, 2016}}</ref>

''[[The Words That Built America]]'', Pelosi's eleventh film, premiered on July 4, 2017, narrated by Pulitzer Prize winning [[David McCullough]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehill.com/blogs/in-the-know/in-the-know/339738-new-alexandra-pelosi-documentary-brings-together-gop-dem/|title=New Alexandra Pelosi documentary brings together GOP, Dem members|last=Kurtz|first=Judy|work=The Hill|date = June 28, 2017|accessdate = February 3, 2018}}</ref> is a reading of the U.S. Constitution read by all the living presidents, vice presidents, 50 US senators of both parties, Supreme Court justices, and others. It includes a reading of the Declaration of Independence read by [[Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson|The Rock]], [[Meryl Streep]], [[Robert De Niro]], [[Robert Redford]], [[Sean Hannity]], [[Kid Rock]], and other celebrities.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hbo.com/content/hboweb/en/documentaries/the-words-that-built-america/the-words-that-built-america.html|title=The Words That Built America|work=HBO.com|accessdate=February 4, 2018}}</ref> It closes with middle-school children from the [[United Nations International School]] reading the [[United States Bill of Rights|Bill of Rights]] and summaries of the other amendments. On [[Anderson Cooper 360]], Anderson Cooper talked about hanging out with Pelosi's two sons on the set.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/celebs-politicians-unite-for-hbos-the-words-that-built-america-w490815/|title= Celebrities and Politicians unite for HBO's 4th of July Documentary "The Words that built America"  |last=Hautman|first=Nicholas|work=UsWeekly|accessdate=March 1, 2018|date= July 3, 2017 }}</ref>

According to ''The Port Arthur News'', Pelosi was spotted in Port Arthur filming a new HBO documentary about Trump's America.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.panews.com/2017/12/29/acclaimed-director-films-harvey-recovery-in-port-arthur/|title=Acclaimed director films Harvey recovery in Port Arthur|last=Ball|first=David|work=The Port Arthur News|date=December 29, 2017|accessdate=February 4, 2018}}</ref>

''Outside the Bubble: A Roadtrip'' with Alexandra Pelosi aired on HBO in October 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hbo.com/documentaries/outside-the-bubble-on-the-road-with-alexandra-pelosi|work=HBO|title= Outside The Bubble: On the Road with Alexandra Pelosi |accessdate=November 8, 2018}}</ref> According to IMDB, "Pelosi sets out on a cross-country trip to engage in conversations with fellow Americans in an effort to gain an unfiltered understanding of other perspectives." <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9108266/|work=IMDB|title= Outside The Bubble:On the Road with Alexandra Pelosi 2018 |accessdate=December 1, 2018}}</ref> According to ''The New York Times'',"It's not just another episode of the learned cosmopolitan descending from the ivory tower to produce anthropological discourses on that strange creature known as the Trump voter and make it back to the big city in time for a martini. Though she is Democratic royalty, Ms. Pelosi has spent much of her career dissecting, with compassion, the psyche of the political right in America."
<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/24/style/alexandra-pelosi-outside-the-bubble.html|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|title = Alexandra Pelosi Plunges Into Trump Country|accessdate=October 27, 2018|date=2018-10-24|last1=McCreesh|first1=Shawn}}</ref>

In January 2019, Pelosi debuted "Goodbye Congress" on HBO's ''[[Vice News Tonight]]'' - the film that features exit interviews with 14 retiring members of Congress, including Speaker Paul Ryan and 7 other Republicans who explain how Washington works.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YIGwqUBvuqM|title = Goodbye, Congress: A VICE News Tonight Special Report| publisher = Youtube}}</ref>

In October 2020, Pelosi released [[American Selfie: One Nation Shoots Itself]] on Showtime. <ref name =  IndieWire>{{cite news | url = https://www.indiewire.com/2020/10/american-selfie-alexandra-pelosi-documentary-trailer-1234591363/| title = 'American Selfie’ Trailer: Alexandra Pelosi’s New Doc Takes a Snapshot of a Divided America| first   =Tyler | last = Hersko | date = October 8, 2020 | work=IndieWire}}</ref> On [[The Daily Show]], Pelosi talked about filming the National Guard's use of chemical agents at the protest outside the White House on June 1, 2020.  <ref name = YouTube>{{cite news | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8YYVXHhQWV0| title = 'Alexandra Pelosi - How America Weaponized Phones {{!}} The Daily Social Distancing Show'| first   =Trevor | last = Noah | date = October 7, 2020 | work=The Daily Show with Trevor Noah}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On June 18, 2005, in [[Greenwich Village]], she married Dutch journalist, lawyer, and United States correspondent [[Michiel Vos]] (born on December 19, 1970 in Groningen, the Netherlands).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/06/19/fashion/weddings/alexandra-pelosi-and-michiel-vos.html| title =Alexandra Pelosi and Michiel Vos| work=The New York Times|date=June 19, 2006}}</ref>

In 2006, Pelosi gave birth to their first child, a boy named Paul,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blog.sfgate.com/politics/2006/11/13/baby-announcement/|title=Baby Announcement|date=November 13, 2006}}</ref> named after his grandfather [[Paul Pelosi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,1559031,00.html|title=Nancy Pelosi a Grandma for the Sixth Time|last=Podesta|first=Jane|work=People.com|date=November 14, 2006|accessdate=February 4, 2018}}</ref> Pelosi had a second son in 2007, named Thomas, after his great grandfather [[Thomas D'Alesandro Jr.]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.democraticleader.gov/newsroom/speaker-pelosi-announces-birth-of-seventh-grandchild/|title=Speaker Pelosi Announces Birth of Seventh Grandchild|date=December 8, 2007|accessdate=February 4, 2018|work=Democraticleader.gov}}</ref> [[Maureen Dowd]] wrote in ''[[The New York Times]]'' about the charity lemonade stand that Ms. Pelosi's son Thomas runs in Manhattan. In previous years, the proceeds have gone to Hurricane Harvey and California wildfire victims.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/01/12/opinion/sunday/dowd-nancy-pelosi-donald-trump.html |work= The New York Times| title= Nancy Pelosi Spanks the First Brat|date= January 12, 2019}}</ref>

Pelosi lives in Greenwich Village. Her bathroom was featured in Home & Garden section of ''The New York Times''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/05/09/garden/a-memoir-disguised-as-a-bath.html|title=A Memoir disguised as a Bath|date=May 9, 2002|work=The New York Times}}.</ref>

In the "In My Library" column for ''The New York Post'', Pelosi cites her two favorite political books: ''[[Political Fictions]]'' by [[Joan Didion]] and ''[[The Journalist and the Murderer]]'' by [[Janet Malcolm]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2016/07/30/in-my-library-alexandra-pelosi/|title=In My Library:Alexandra Pelosi
|date=July 30, 2016|work=New York Post}}</ref>

Pelosi's children are often seen with her on the red carpet.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.zimbio.com/photos/Thomas+Vos/Fall+to+Grace+Premieres+in+NYC/1UntnK1SYfh|title='Fall to Grace' Premieres in NYC|date= March 20, 2013}}</ref> They are often photographed with their grandmother [[Nancy Pelosi]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/logos-trailblazer-honors-nancy-pelosi-activist-cleve-jones-urge-people-keep-fighting-lgbtq-rights-1016469 |title= Logos Trailblazer Honors Nancy Pelosi |date= June 23, 2017 }}</ref> Nancy Pelosi often mentions them in speeches.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.sfgate.com/politics/amp/Pelosi-No-boots-on-the-ground-in-Iraq-5684288.php |title= No Boots on the Ground |date= August 12, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.zimbio.com/photos/Thomas+Vos/House+Democrats+Hold+News+Conference+Working/jIdesP6RQ1P |title= House Democrats Hold News Conference on Working Families Day of Action |date= October 26, 2015 }}</ref> Pelosi says, 'public service runs in the family'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sfgate.com/news/amp/Public-service-runs-in-the-family-5920532.php|title=Public service runs in the family|date=November 26, 2014}}</ref> According to Politico, during the battle to repeal Obamacare, Pelosi's 9-year-old grandson Thomas ambled over to reporters in slacks and a blazer and announced, "It's not going to pass," "He [Paul Ryan] doesn't have the votes!"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2017/11/02/nancy-pelosi-house-democratic-leader-profile-feature-2017-215759|title=Nancy Pelosi has Trump right where she wants him|date= November 2, 2017}}</ref> In a joint interview on CNN, [[Paul Ryan]] and [[Nancy Pelosi]] discussed Ryan's friendship with Pelosi's children. "I thought you were going to brag about how much my grandchildren are your big fans," Pelosi said. "That's right, her grandkids actually like me, go figure," Ryan replied.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thehill.com/homenews/house/338075-ryan-pelosis-grandkids-actually-like-me |work=The Hill| title=Ryan: Pelosi's grandkids 'actually like me'|date= June 15, 2017}}</ref>

At Spotlight on Alexandra Pelosi at Lincoln Center, Pelosi talked about the solitary life of a documentary filmmaker and how being naive is the best prescription for a documentary filmmaker: "It feels like every documentary has been made, but it's not true. The next great documentary film is going to be made by someone whose name you don't even know yet." <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zON1UjQlv3Y|title=Spotlight On...Alexandra Pelosi|date=October 22, 2013|work=YouTube}}</ref>
