'''Shailagh Jane Murray''' (born October 9, 1965) is an American university administrator, former political aide and [[journalist]] who served as a [[Senior Advisor to the President of the United States|Senior Advisor]] to [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Barack Obama]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Dovere |first=Edward-Isaac |authorlink=Edward-Isaac Dovere|url=http://www.politico.com/story/2015/03/barack-obama-names-two-new-top-aides-116349.html |title=Barack Obama names two new top aides |date=24 March 2015 |accessdate=27 March 2015 | work= [[Politico]] }}</ref> She previously served as Deputy Chief of Staff and Communications Director for [[Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] [[Joe Biden]].<ref name=Comms>{{cite news|last=Reinhard |first=Beth |title= Shailagh Murray, Communications Director, Office of the Vice President |url=http://www.nationaljournal.com/decision-makers/white-house/shailagh-murray-communications-director-20130716 |date=16 July 2013 |accessdate=23 May 2015 |work=[[National Journal]] |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150524061541/http://www.nationaljournal.com/decision-makers/white-house/shailagh-murray-communications-director-20130716 |archivedate=24 May 2015 }}</ref>

==Career==
Murray worked for ''The Wall Street Journal'' from 1999 to 2005.<ref name=Comms /> She then became Capitol Hill correspondent for ''[[The Washington Post]]'', covering the elections of 2006, 2008 and 2010.<ref name=Comms />

She joined the [[Office of the Vice President of the United States|Office of the Vice President]] in 2011 as Communications Director and was later promoted to Deputy Chief of Staff.<ref>{{cite news|last=Memoli |first=Michael A. | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160927104950/http://articles.latimes.com/2011/mar/18/news/la-pn-biden-hire-reporter-20110319 |archivedate= 27 September 2016|url= http://articles.latimes.com/2011/mar/18/news/la-pn-biden-hire-reporter-20110319 |title= Biden names reporter as communications director |date= 18 March 2011 |accessdate= 27 March 2015 |newspaper= [[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref>

In March 2015, she was named as the successor to [[Dan Pfeiffer]] as [[Senior Advisor to the President of the United States|Senior Advisor to the President]] with a communications portfolio.<ref name="Dana Milbank">{{cite news|last=Milbank|first=Dana |title=Is this the end of the White House's insufferable insularity?|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/is-this-the-end-of-the-white-houses-insufferable-insularity/2015/03/27/0bf3ce24-d485-11e4-a62f-ee745911a4ff_story.html|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=27 March 2015|accessdate=19 May 2015}}</ref>

In September 2018, she was appointed Executive Vice President for Public Affairs of [[Columbia University]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=September 5, 2018|title=Shailagh Murray Appointed Executive Vice President for Public Affairs|url=https://gca.columbia.edu/news/shailagh-murray-appointed-executive-vice-president-public-affairs|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=June 18, 2020|website=Columbia University}}</ref>
