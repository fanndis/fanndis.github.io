'''Hilda Cathy Heine''' (born April 6, 1951) is a Marshallese educator and politician, who served as the [[List of Presidents of the Marshall Islands|eighth]] [[President of the Marshall Islands]]. Prior to assuming office, she served as the Minister of Education. She was the first individual in the [[Marshall Islands]] to earn a [[doctorate degree]], and the founder of the women's rights group Women United Together Marshall Islands (WUTMI).<ref name="Johnson">{{cite news|url=http://www.mvariety.com/regional-news/83419-marshalls-leadership-battles-end-for-now|title=Marshalls leadership battles end, for now|last=Johnson|first=Giff|date=29 Jan 2016|work=[[Marianas Variety]]|accessdate=31 January 2016|location=Majuro}}</ref><ref name="SallinenSiamomua2005">{{cite book|last1=Sallinen|first1=Maarit|last2=Siamomua|first2=Amelia Kinahoi|title=Profiles of Pacific Women|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=EV_aAAAAMAAJ|year=2005|publisher=UNIFEM Pacific Regional Office|page=13}}</ref>

Heine is the first woman to hold the presidency of the Marshall Islands.<ref name=mijournal2>{{cite news |first=Giff |last=Johnson |title=Hilda emerges as RMI President |url=http://marshallislandsjournal.com/Journal_WP/?p=2921 |work=[[Marshall Islands Journal]] |date=2016-01-28 |accessdate=2016-01-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160201023226/http://marshallislandsjournal.com/Journal_WP/?p=2921 |archive-date=2016-02-01 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She is also the first female president of any [[Micronesia]]n country, and only the fourth woman to serve as [[head of government]] for any independent nation in [[Oceania]] (following [[Jenny Shipley]] and [[Helen Clark]] of [[New Zealand]] and [[Julia Gillard]] of [[Australia]]).<ref name=easrwest>{{cite news|title=Hilda Heine Becomes First Women President Of The RMI  |url=http://pidp.eastwestcenter.org/pireport/2016/January/01-27-01.htm |work=[[East-West Center]] ([[Radio New Zealand International]]) |date=2016-01-27 |accessdate=2016-01-31}}</ref><ref name=rnzi>{{cite news|title=Hilda Heine elected Marshalls president |url=http://www.radionz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/295053/hilda-heine-elected-marshalls-president |work=[[Radio New Zealand International]] |date=2016-01-28 |accessdate=2016-01-31}}</ref><ref name=pina>{{cite news|title=First female President Hilda Hine elected in the Marshall Islands |url=http://www.pina.com.fj/?p=pacnews&m=read&o=96226186656a836ae74ce00ffa8cd5 |work=PACNews (Pacific Islands News Association) |date=2016-01-27 |accessdate=2016-01-31}}</ref> She is one of only three women currently serving in the [[Legislature of the Marshall Islands|Nitijeļā]], the country's legislature, as of January 2016.<ref name=rnzi/>

==Early life and education==
Heine was born on April 6, 1951, in [[Majuro|Majuro Atoll]], Marshall Islands.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rmiparliament.org/cms/members.html#1-president-of-the-republic-of-the-marshall-islands-her-excellency-president-hilda-c-heine|title=Members: President of the Republic of the Marshall Islands - Her Excellency President Hilda C. Heine|publisher=Republic of the Marshall Islands Parliament (Nitijela)|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191103092457/https://rmiparliament.org/cms/members.html#1-president-of-the-republic-of-the-marshall-islands-her-excellency-president-hilda-c-heine|archive-date=2019-11-03|access-date=2019-11-03}}</ref> She attended college in the [[United States]] where she earned her undergraduate degree at the [[University of Oregon]] in 1970.  She earned a master's degree at the [[University of Hawaii]] in 1975, and an educational doctorate at the [[University of Southern California]] in 2004.<ref name="pacwip.org">{{cite web|title=Marshall Islands|url=http://www.pacwip.org/future-elections/marshall-is/?viewdetails&candidate=10|publisher=Pacific Women in Politics|accessdate=5 June 2015}}</ref>

She received her honorary doctorate in philosophy from [[Fu Jen Catholic University]] in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fju.edu.tw/focusDetail.jsp?focusID=1460&focusClassID=3|title=輔仁大學全球資訊網|website=www.fju.edu.tw}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Hilda Heine.jpg|thumb|President of the Republic of the [[Marshall Islands]], H.E. Hilda C. Heine walking through the Memorial Amphitheater at [[Arlington National Cemetery]] Sept. 12, 2017.]]
Heine worked at [[Marshall Islands High School]] in [[Majuro]] from 1975 through 1982, serving both as a classroom teacher and as a counselor.<ref name="pacwip.org" /> In 2000, Heine founded Women United Together Marshall Islands (WUTMI), a women's rights group.<ref name="RNZI">{{cite news|url=http://news.pngfacts.com/2016/01/first-female-president-hilda-hine.html|title=First Female President Hilda Hine Elected In The Marshall Islands|date=28 Jan 2016|work=RNZI|publisher=Papua New Guinea Today|accessdate=31 January 2016}}</ref><ref name="AVI">{{cite web|url=http://www.australianvolunteers.com/volunteer/australian-volunteers-for-international-development-avid/why-volunteer-with-avid/host-organisation-profiles/women-united-together-marshall-islands-wutmi/|title=Australian Volunteers International&nbsp;— Women United Together Marshall Islands (WUTMI)|work=Australian Volunteers for International Development&nbsp;— Australian Government|publisher=Australian Volunteers International|accessdate=31 January 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160307132254/http://www.australianvolunteers.com/volunteer/australian-volunteers-for-international-development-avid/why-volunteer-with-avid/host-organisation-profiles/women-united-together-marshall-islands-wutmi/|archive-date=7 March 2016}}</ref> Since 2005, she had been [[Pacific Resources for Education and Learning]]'s (PREL) Director at the Pacific Comprehensive Assistance Center.<ref name="pacwip.org" /> Heine participated in the 2009 with the Pacific Islands Climate Change Education Partnership. She has also been associated with the Leadership Pacific Advisory Board, the Commission on Education in Micronesia, and the Human Resources in Health Task Force.<ref name="pacwip.org" />

Representing [[Aur Atoll]] in the [[Nitijeļā]] (Legislature), she became Minister of Education.<ref name="Inc.2013">{{cite book|title=Marshall Islands Land Ownership and Agricultural Laws Handbook - Strategic Information and Basic Laws|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Mfb_BgAAQBAJ&pg=PA22|date=4 April 2013|publisher=Lulu.com|isbn=978-1-4387-5952-4|page=22}}</ref>

In January 2016 Aelon Kein Ad member [[Casten Nemra]] was elected as [[President of the Marshall Islands]]. Shortly afterwards Heine along with former Minister [[Thomas Heine]] and [[Wilbur Heine]] withdrew their support of Nemra and defected to the opposition. The decision was made after Thomas was not offered a positioned in the cabinet of Nemra.<ref name=mijournal2/> Nemra was shortly afterwards removed from office in a vote of no confidence, having been in office for only two weeks. Heine was chosen as the replacement candidate by the opposition. On January 27, 2016, as sole candidate, she received 24 votes with six abstaining and three absent from the 33 members of the Nitijeļā.<ref name=abc>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-01-27/hilda-heine-elected-first-ever-female-pacific-leader/7118664 |title=Hilda Heine elected first female Pacific leader as president of Marshall Islands|work=[[ABC News (Australia)|ABC News]] |date=January 27, 2016 |accessdate=January 27, 2016}}</ref><ref name=rnz>{{cite news|url=http://www.radionz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/295053/hilda-heine-elected-marshalls-president|title=Hilda Heine elected Marshalls president|work=[[Radio New Zealand]]|date=January 27, 2016 |accessdate=January 27, 2016}}</ref> Heine was sworn into office as President of the Marshall Islands on January 28, 2016.<ref name=mijournal2/> She became the first woman to hold the position.<ref name="EU">{{cite press release|title=Statement by the Spokesperson on the election of the President of the Republic of Marshall Islands|publisher=European Union (EEAS)|date=28 Jan 2016|url=http://eeas.europa.eu/statements-eeas/2016/160128_01_en.htm|accessdate=31 January 2016}}</ref> 

On November 12, 2018 Heine survived a [[Motion of no confidence|vote of no confidence]] with the outcome in votes being 16-16, falling short of the 17 votes needed.<ref>{{cite web|last=Johnson|first=Giff|url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/375726/marshall-islands-president-narrowly-survives-no-confidence-vote |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200917183812/https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/375726/marshall-islands-president-narrowly-survives-no-confidence-vote |title=Marshall Islands president narrowly survives no confidence vote |publisher=Radio New Zealand |date=12 November 2018 |archive-date=17 September 2020}}</ref> Heine and [[Kitlang Kabua]], were the only two women elected in the [[2019 Marshallese general election]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Johnson |first=Giff |url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/407600/marshalls-president-kabua-s-inauguration-set-for-monday |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201018060514/https://www.rnz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/407600/marshalls-president-kabua-s-inauguration-set-for-monday |title=Marshalls' President Kabua's inauguration set for Monday |publisher=Radio New Zealand |date=20 January 2020 |archive-date=18 October 2020}}</ref> On January 6, 2020 she lost her bid for re-election in a 12-20 vote against [[David Kabua]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Losinio |first=Louella |url=https://www.pncguam.com/rmi-elects-new-president/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200107014208/https://www.pncguam.com/rmi-elects-new-president/ |title=RMI elects new president |publisher=PNC Guam |date=6 January 2020 |archive-date=7 January 2020}}</ref>
