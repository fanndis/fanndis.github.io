The '''University of Kansas School of Law''' is a public [[law school]] located on the main campus of the [[University of Kansas]] in [[Lawrence, Kansas]]. The University of Kansas Law School was founded in 1893, replacing the earlier Department of Law, which had existed since 1878.<ref>[http://www.law.ku.edu About KU Law]</ref> The school has more than 60 faculty members and approximately 332 students. The school is accredited by the [[American Bar Association]] and is a member of the Association of American Law Schools.<ref>[http://www.law.ku.edu/glance KU Law at a glance]</ref>

With over 370,000 volumes, the Wheat Law Library at the University of Kansas School of Law is the second largest and oldest law library in the state of Kansas.<ref>[http://www.ku.edu/libraries/ Wheat Law Library]</ref>

== Curriculum ==
The first-year curriculum includes [[Civil Procedure]], [[Constitutional Law]], [[Contracts]], [[Criminal Law]], [[Torts]], and [[Property law|Property]].<ref>[http://www.law.ku.edu/academics/course_descriptions.shtml Course descriptions]</ref> In addition, students take Lawyering I and II, [[legal writing]] classes designed to teach [[legal research]] and writing in a context that emphasizes professionalism and practical skills. All first-year students have one of their classes in a small section of approximately 20 students, providing an informal learning atmosphere.

== Employment ==
According to The University of Kansas official 2020 ABA-required disclosures, 72% of the Class of 2019 obtained full-time, long-term, bar passage-required employment 10 months after graduation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://law.ku.edu/sites/law.ku.edu/files/docs/standard_509/aba-employment-summary-2019-graduates-update.pdf|title=ABA Employment Summary Class of 2019|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|accessdate=11 June 2019}}</ref>

== Costs ==
The total cost of attendance (indicating the cost of tuition, fees, and living expenses) at the University of Kansas for the 2018-2019 academic year was $39,274 for residents of Kansas and $55,438 for residents of other states.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://law.ku.edu/apply#tuition|title=Tuition and Expenses}}</ref> 86 percent of students received grants for the 2017-2018 academic year.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://law.ku.edu/sites/law.ku.edu/files/docs/standard_509/aba-standard-509-report-2018.pdf|title= KU Law Official Guide}}</ref>
