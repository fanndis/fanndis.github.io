The '''2019 G20 Osaka summit''' was the  [[List of G20 summits|fourteenth]] meeting of the [[G20]], a forum of 19 countries and the [[EU]] that together represent most of the world economy. It was held on 28–29 June 2019 at the [[Intex Osaka|International Exhibition Center]] in [[Osaka]].<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2018/02/20/national/government-picks-osaka-venue-g-20-summit-2019-sources/#.Wov_9kxuKDs| title=Government picks Osaka as venue for G-20 summit in 2019: sources| date=February 20, 2018| work=[[The Japan Times]]}}</ref><ref name="intex">{{cite web| url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2019/05/27/national/politics-diplomacy/concern-grows-g20-security-preparations-will-impact-osaka/| title=Concern grows over how G20 security preparations will impact Osaka| date=27 May 2019| first=Eric| last=Johnston| website=japantimes.co.jp}}</ref> It was the first G20 summit to be hosted by [[Japan]].<ref>{{cite web| url=http://asia.nikkei.com/Spotlight/G-20-summit/Japan-to-host-G-20-summit-in-2019-for-1st-time| title=Japan to host G-20 summit in 2019 for 1st time| date=July 9, 2017| work=[[The Nikkei]]| access-date=July 12, 2017| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170807155420/https://asia.nikkei.com/Spotlight/G-20-summit/Japan-to-host-G-20-summit-in-2019-for-1st-time| archive-date=August 7, 2017| url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Issues==
[[File:Press conference EU-Mercosul on June 26, 2019 (VII).jpg|thumb|The [[European Union–Mercosur Free Trade Agreement]] would form one of the world's largest free trade areas]]
The 2019 G20 Summit discussed eight themes to Ensure Global Sustainable Development.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dinesh |first1=Singh |title=G20 Summit 2019 Themes Ensures Sustainable Development |url=http://abclive.in/g20-summit-2019-themes/ |accessdate=27 June 2019 |agency=ABC Live India}}</ref> The eight themes were "Global Economy", "Trade and Investment", "Innovation", "Environment and Energy", "Employment", "Women's Empowerment", "Development" and "Health".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/g20_summit/osaka19/en/summit/theme/ |title=Main Themes |website=G20 Osaka Summit 2019 |publisher=[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)]] |access-date=August 6, 2020 |archive-url= }}</ref>

Regards to "Trade and Investment", support for the necessary reform of the [[World Trade Organization|World Trade Organization (WTO)]] was agreed.<ref name="declaration" /> WHO Director-General [[Roberto Azevêdo]] had been participating in the summit, welcomed the communique.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wto.org/english/news_e/news19_e/dgra_29jun19_e.htm |date=June 29, 2019 |title=Azevêdo welcomes G20 leaders’ reaffirmation of commitment to WTO reform |publisher=[[World Trade Organization|WTO]] |access-date=September 6, 2020 }}</ref>

Regards to "Innovation", necessity of respected and interoperable frameworks on Data Free Flow with Trust, both domestic and international, was discussed.<ref name="declaration" />

Regards to "Environment and Energy", a common global vision, the "Osaka Blue Ocean Vision" which is aiming to reduce additional pollution by marine plastic litter to zero by 2050 through a comprehensive life-cycle approach was shared.<ref name="declaration">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/g20_summit/osaka19/en/documents/final_g20_osaka_leaders_declaration.html |title=G20 Osaka Leaders' Declaration |website=G20 Osaka Summit 2019 |publisher=[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)]] |access-date=August 2, 2020 }}</ref>

Leaders' Special Event was also held, and "Digital Economy" and "Women’s Empowerment" were discussed. During the former event, "Osaka Declaration on Digital Economy" was issued,<ref name="digital">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/g20_summit/osaka19/pdf/special_event/en/special_event_01.pdf |title= Osaka Declaration on Digital Economy |format=PDF |website=G20 Osaka Summit 2019 |publisher=[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)]] |access-date=August 4, 2020 }}</ref> in which those leaders declared the launch of the "Osaka Track", a process which demonstrates their commitment to promote efforts on international rule-making on digital economy, especially on data flow and electronic commerce.<ref name="special">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/g20_summit/osaka19/en/special_event/ |title=Leaders' Special Event |website=G20 Osaka Summit 2019 |publisher=[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)]] |access-date=August 2, 2020 }}</ref><ref name="wef_dfft">{{Cite web|url=https://www.weforum.org/whitepapers/data-free-flow-with-trust-dfft-paths-towards-free-and-trusted-data-flows |date=June 10, 2020 |title=Data Free Flow with Trust (DFFT): Paths towards Free and Trusted Data Flows |website=[[World Economic Forum]] |publisher=World Economic Forum |access-date=August 3, 2020 }}</ref>
Regards to the latter event, a press release compiling the messages from the leaders on their national measures and commitment regarding women's empowerment was issued after the event.<ref name="woman">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/g20_summit/osaka19/pdf/special_event/en/special_event_03.pdf |title=Press Release: Leaders' Special Event on Women’s Empowerment at G20 Osaka Summit |format=PDF |website=G20 Osaka Summit 2019 |publisher=[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)]] |access-date=August 5, 2020 }}</ref>

==Related topics==
[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China]] has issued a statement considering the [[2019–20 Hong Kong protests|Hong Kong Protests]] as China's internal affairs on June 24. The Chinese government wanted to delist the Hong Kong issue from the summit's agenda for the year, to avoid possible political and security confrontations between all G20 leaders (from outside China) and China. China has threatened to attack G20 nations suspected for “wrongfully” accusing China.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.mingpao.com/pns/要聞/article/20190625/s00001/1561399810603/外交部-中方不允g20討論香港| title=外交部：中方不允G20討論香港| date=June 25, 2019| work=[[Ming Pao]]}}</ref> However, Japanese Prime Minister Abe had raised the issue to President Xi just before the official summit, while some Hong Kong citizens protested in places around the summit venue. Pro-independence leader [[Chan Ho-tin]] demonstrated with people from Chinese ethnic minorities like [[Rebiya Kadeer]] for Hong Kong, as well as [[Xinjiang]] issues.<ref>{{cite news |last1=INC |first1=SANKEI DIGITAL |script-title=ja:亡命ウイグル人ら、中国の弾圧訴え抗議デモ |url=https://www.sankei.com/west/news/190628/wst1906280042-n1.html |accessdate=2019-06-28 |work={{lang|ja|産経ニュース}} |date=2019-06-28 |language=ja}}</ref>

On June 29, [[President of the United States]] [[Donald Trump]] offered [[North Korea]]'s [[Kim Jong-un]] weekend meeting in demilitarized zone,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lee |first1=Joyce |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-northkorea-usa-trump/trump-offers-north-koreas-kim-weekend-meeting-in-demilitarized-zone-idUSKCN1TT354 |title=Trump offers North Korea's Kim weekend meeting in demilitarized zone |date=June 29, 2019 |accessdate=August 4, 2020 |work=[[Reuters]] }}</ref> and [[2019 Koreas–United States DMZ Summit]] was realized.
