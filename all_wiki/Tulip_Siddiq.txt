'''Tulip Rizwana Siddiq''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|FRSA}} ({{lang-bn|টিউলিপ রেজওয়ানা সিদ্দীক}}; born 16 September 1982) is a British politician serving as the [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Hampstead and Kilburn (UK Parliament constituency)|Hampstead and Kilburn]] since [[2015 United Kingdom general election|2015]]. A member of the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]], she previously was the [[Camden London Borough Council]]lor for [[Regent's Park (Camden ward)|Regent's Park]] from 2010 until 2014.

==Early political career==
At the age of 16, Siddiq joined the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]].<ref name="standard1"/> Siddiq's father suffered a stroke, which left him disabled<ref name="independent2"/> and unable to speak for five years.<ref name="standard4"/><ref name="independent2"/> She has cited the [[National Health Service|NHS]] and the care her disabled father received as the reason why she joined.<ref name="kilburntimes1"/> She identified [[Barbara Castle, Baroness Castle of Blackburn|Barbara Castle]] as her political heroine.<ref name="huffingtonpost"/> and has described her mother and maternal aunt as "two very strong feminists".<ref name="independent3">{{cite news |last=Siddiq|first=Tulip|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/tulip-siddiq-the-north-london-labour-candidate-trying-to-improve-the-woeful-diversity-of-westminster-10157378.html|title=Why do we still cling to the belief that most rapes are committed by strangers in dark alleyways?|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|date=26 November 2015|accessdate=1 January 2016}}</ref>

She has worked for [[Amnesty International]],<ref name="standard4"/> the [[Greater London Authority]],<ref name="labourcouncillor">{{cite news|url=http://www.labourcouncillor.org.uk/tulip_siddiq|title=Tulip Siddiq – Camden candidate for 2010 elections|publisher=Labour Councillor|accessdate=18 September 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111203011400/http://www.labourcouncillor.org.uk/tulip_siddiq|archive-date=3 December 2011}}</ref> at Philip Gould Associates, the political consultancy firm run by [[New Labour]] strategist [[Philip Gould, Baron Gould of Brookwood|Philip Gould]],<ref name="hamhigh3"/><ref name="standard4"/> [[Save the Children]], and Brunswick, where she worked on corporate social responsibilities initiatives for major British manufacturers, as well as for MPs [[Oona King]], [[Sadiq Khan]] and<ref name="bbwhoswho10"/> [[Harry Cohen]]. Siddiq worked on [[Ed Miliband]]'s campaign to be leader of the Labour Party,<ref name="labourcouncillor"/> and as a special advisor to [[Tessa Jowell]].<ref name="standard1"/> She has campaigned for political parties internationally and, in 2008, campaigned for [[Barack Obama]] in the U.S.<ref name="bbwhoswho10"/>

In a 2006 by-election, Siddiq stood unsuccessfully for Camden Council.<ref name="huffingtonpost"/> In the [[2010 United Kingdom local elections|2010 local government elections]], Siddiq became the first female Bangladeshi councillor for Camden Council,<ref name="telegraph1">{{cite news |last=Wilkinson|first=Michael|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/general-election-2015/11377416/General-Election-2015-the-seats-that-will-decide-who-wins.html|title=9 seats that will decide who wins the General Election|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=30 January 2015|accessdate=4 March 2015}}</ref> where she was Cabinet Member for Culture and Communities until [[2014 Camden London Borough Council election|May 2014]].<ref name="theguardian1">{{cite news |last=Siddiq|first=Tulip|url=https://www.theguardian.com/local-government-network/2013/dec/12/library-cuts-volunteers-saved-camdens-libraries|title=How a team of volunteers saved Camden's at risk libraries|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=13 December 2013|accessdate=10 January 2015}}</ref>

==Other activities==
Siddiq was a board member of West Euston Partnership and is governor of the Camden and Islington [[NHS Foundation Trust]].<ref name="sylhettimesdigital">{{cite news |url=http://sylhettimesdigital.co.uk/miliband-praises-young-british-bangladeshi-labour-hopefuls/|title=Miliband praises young British Bangladeshi Labour hopefuls|publisher=The Sylhet Times|date=20 January 2010|accessdate=18 September 2011}}</ref> She served as national BAME (Black, Asian and Minority Ethnic) Officer for [[Young Labour (United Kingdom)|Young Labour]] and Women's Officer for [[London Labour Party|London]] Young Labour. She is an executive board member of [[Unite the Union]], a member of the [[Co-operative Party]], a fellow of the [[Royal Society of Arts]] and is also a member of the Commonwealth Journalists Association (UK).<ref name="bbwhoswho10"/> She also oversaw Camden's engagement with the [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012 London Olympics]], which saw the launch of three legacy schemes to encourage more physical activities, Camden Sports Academy, School and Community Games, and Pro-Active Ambassadors.<ref name="camdennewjournal1">{{cite news |last=Barnett|first=Steve|url=http://www.camdennewjournal.com/news/2011/jan/2012-sports-scheme-olympic-hopefuls-support-gold-challenge-initiative-swiss-cottage-le|title=2012 sports scheme – Olympic hopefuls support Gold Challenge initiative at Swiss Cottage Leisure Centre|location=Camden|publisher=[[Camden New Journal]]|date=27 January 2011|accessdate=10 January 2015}}</ref>

Siddiq served two years as a [[school governor]] at Beckford Primary School and Richard Cobden Primary School<ref name="rcobden">{{cite web |url=http://www.rcobden.camden.sch.uk/governors_lists.html|title=List of Governors|publisher=Richard Cobden Primary School|accessdate=5 February 2013}}</ref> and is a current governor at the Working Men's College in Camden.<ref name="wmcollege">{{cite web |url=http://www.wmcollege.ac.uk/pages/working-mens-college-governors.aspx|title=Working Men's College Governors|publisher=Working Men's College|accessdate=10 January 2015}}</ref> As of January 2014, Siddiq supports a number of organisations in Hampstead and Kilburn, including school governor roles at Emmanuel Primary School<ref name="emmanuel">{{cite web |url= http://www.emmanuel.camden.sch.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Governors-of-Emmanuel-School.pdf|title=Membership of the Governing Body|publisher=Emmanual Church of England Primary School|accessdate=10 January 2015}}</ref> and Granville Plus Nursery<ref name="granvilleplus">{{cite web |url=http://www.granvilleplus.org.uk/Governors/|title=Governors|publisher=Granville Plus Nursery School|accessdate=10 January 2015}}</ref> as well as being a trustee of the Camden Arts Centre.<ref name="camdenartscentre">{{cite web |url=http://www.camdenartscentre.org/contact|title=Contact|publisher=Camden Arts Centre|accessdate=10 January 2015}}</ref> She has also written for ''[[Hampstead and Highgate Express]]''<ref name="bbwhoswho10"/> as a foreign correspondent, primarily covering the [[Elections in the United States|U.S. elections]].
