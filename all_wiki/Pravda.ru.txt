'''Pravda.ru''' ({{lang-ru|Правда.Ру|lit=truth}}) formerly '''Pravda Online''', is a Russian internet news website established in 1999 and owned by Pravda.ru Holding headed by Vadim Gorshenin.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Russia: Pravda Versus Pravda: Which 'Truth' Will Prevail?|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/1104890.html|access-date=2020-10-16|website=RadioFreeEurope/RadioLiberty|language=en}}</ref>

==History==
After the collapse of the [[Soviet Union]], the oldest Soviet paper founded in 1912, ''[[Pravda]]'', split into two different papers. Significant members of the main editorial staff (Viktor Afanasiev, Gennady Seleznev, Yuri Zhukov, Vera Tkachenko and Vadim Gorshenin) left Pravda to form the online news & opinion website Pravda.ru.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://english.pravda.ru/opinion/columnists/16-09-2013/125664-pravda_mccain-0/ |title=There is no Pravda. There is Pravda.Ru |last1=Gorshenin |first1=Vadim |date=September 16, 2013 |work=english.pravda.ru |publisher= |accessdate=}}</ref> Following a court case Pravda name was allowed to be used by both the newspaper owned by the [[Communist Party of the Russian Federation|Communist Party of Russia]] and the pro-Putin Pravda.ru ran by journalists associated with the defunct soviet Pravda.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Dear Senator McCain: Pravda.ru is not Pravda|url=https://www.nationalreview.com/media-blog/dear-senator-mccain-pravdaru-not-pravda-greg-pollowitz/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=ENCYCLOPÆDIA BRITANNICA, Pravda: SOVIET NEWSPAPER|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Pravda|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>   

According to politologist [[Stanislav Belkovsky]], Pravda.ru is controlled by [[Konstantin Kostin (politician)|Konstantin Kostin]] and his wife Olga Kostina.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.gazeta.ru/politics/2011/03/16_a_3556453.shtml |script-title=ru:Называем Ходорковского серийным убийцей |last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date=March 16, 2011 |work=gazeta.ru |language=Russian |accessdate=}}</ref> The Russian politician, [[Sergey Veremeenko]] also holds interests in Pravda.ru and Pravda International.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/Fringes/portrait-of-a-russian-oligarch |title=Portrait of a Russian Oligarch |last1=Duffy |first1=Ryan |date=July 8, 2013 |work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |publisher= |accessdate=March 31, 2014}}</ref> Pravda.ru was registered in November 1999 and has been published since January 27, 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|title="Правда.ру" впервые изменила логотип|url=https://www.sostav.ru/news/2010/12/03/r11/|access-date=2020-10-16|website=www.sostav.ru}}</ref> Pravda.ru also launched an English version (pravdareport.com), a Portuguese version, as well as an Italian version.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=ESI MANUAL, THE RUSSIAN DEBATE ON THE SOUTH CAUCASUS: WHO IS WHO?|url=https://www.esiweb.org/pdf/esi_-_russia_manual_-_part_1_-_print_media.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref> The printed version was registered by the Ministry of the Russian Federation for Press, TV and Radio Broadcasting and Mass Communications on 17 November 2003.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ретро-новости: Microsoft Office 2000, "Правда.ру", Международный день без интернета|url=https://3dnews.ru/news/556988|access-date=2020-10-16|website=3DNews - Daily Digital Digest|language=ru}}</ref>

In 2007 Pravda.ru was classified as a popular leftist, nationalist news website by the [[Federation of American Scientists]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Russia -- Source Descriptors of Key Russian Media|url=https://fas.org/irp/dni/osc/russian-media.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

The former URL english.pravda.ru, giving access to the English language part of the web site, now redirects to the domain pravdareport.com.

==Controversy==
{{original research|section|date= April 2020}}
The organisation has been accused of supporting the Russian government's position on various subjects both within and outside the country and also of producing [[Neo-Sovietism|neo-Soviet]], [[Russian nationalism|Russian nationalist]] news and outright [[Conspiracy theory|conspiracy theories]]. The media outlet which is a part of a larger pro-Kremlin media holding is said to have created an algorithm for vetting media outlets in terms of their pro-government ideological standing.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=The 20 Russian News Outlets You Need To Read Before They Get The Ax|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/twenty-russian-news-outlets-you-need-to-read-before-they-get-the-axe/25317371.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>
