'''Patrick Galbraith''' (born April 16, 1967) is a former professional [[tennis]] player from the United States.

A doubles specialist, Galbraith reached the World No. 1 doubles ranking in 1993. During his career he won 38 top-level doubles titles. He was a mixed doubles champion at the [[U.S. Open (tennis)|US Open]] in 1994 (partnering [[Elna Reinach]]) and 1996 (partnering [[Lisa Raymond]]). He also won the men's doubles title at the [[Tennis Masters Cup|ATP Tour World Championships]] in 1995 (partnering [[Grant Connell]]). He was a men's doubles runner-up at [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]] in both 1993 and 1994, and a mixed doubles runner-up at [[French Open]] in 1997. He retired from the professional tour in 1999, having won prize money totalling US$2,684,136.

Prior to turning professional, Galbraith played tennis for [[University of California, Los Angeles|UCLA]] from 1986–1989, where he was a three-time [[All-America]]n and an [[National Collegiate Athletic Association|NCAA]] doubles champion in 1988.

In November 2018 Gabraith was elected as chairman of the board and president of the [[United States Tennis Association]] (USTA), succeeding [[Katrina Adams]].<ref>{{cite web |title=USTA Names Patrick Galbraith as Chairman of Board and President |url=http://www.tennisnow.com/Blogs/NET-POSTS/November-2018-(1)/USTA-Names-Patrick-Galbraith-as-Chairman-of-Board.aspx |website=Tennisnow |date=November 27, 2018}}</ref>
