The '''Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond''' is the headquarters of the Fifth District of the [[Federal Reserve]] located in [[Richmond, Virginia]]. It covers the [[District of Columbia]], [[Maryland]], [[North Carolina]], [[South Carolina]], [[Virginia]], and most of [[West Virginia]] excluding the [[Northern Panhandle of West Virginia|Northern Panhandle]]. Branch offices are located in [[Baltimore, Maryland]] and [[Charlotte, North Carolina]]. [[Thomas Barkin]] became president of the Richmond Fed following the retirement of [[Jeffrey M. Lacker]] in April 2017. <ref>{{cite web|title=Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond Presidential Search|url=https://www.richmondfed.org/about_us/our_governance/presidential_search|website=www.richmondfed.org|accessdate=24 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170425114221/https://www.richmondfed.org/about_us/our_governance/presidential_search|archive-date=25 April 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
The previous president, [[J. Alfred Broaddus]], retired in 2004.<ref>{{cite web|title=Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond|url=https://www.richmondfed.org/|website=www.richmondfed.org|accessdate=12 January 2017}}</ref>

==Building==
[[File:Richmond Fed map.png|250px|thumb|right|Fifth District of the Federal Reserve]]
The Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond is located in [[Downtown Richmond, Virginia]]. It has an aluminum facade and was designed by [[Minoru Yamasaki]], who also designed the former [[World Trade Center (1973–2001)|World Trade Center]].<ref name=emporis>{{cite web|url=http://www.emporis.com/application/?nav=building&id=130661|title=Federal Reserve Bank Building|publisher=[[Emporis]]}}</ref> Despite being one of the [[List of tallest buildings in Virginia|tallest buildings in the state]], 49% of the building's total floor area is located underground.<ref name=emporis/> The building was proposed in 1972, and built from 1975-1978.<ref name=emporis/>

==Economy==
The Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond is the fourth-largest Federal Reserve Bank by assets held, after [[Federal Reserve Bank of New York|New York]], [[Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco|San Francisco]], and [[Federal Reserve Bank of Atlanta|Atlanta]], as of December 2018.<ref>[https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h41/20181206/]</ref>[[File:Federal Reserve Note Seal (Richmond).tif|thumb|right|Federal Reserve Note Seal (Richmond)]][[File:FRS RICH cent grp 121613 0556 02838 (14079997691).jpg|thumb|right|Former presidents of the Richmond Fed (Left to Right: J. Alfred Broaddus Jr.; Robert P. Black; Jeffrey M. Lacker)]]
