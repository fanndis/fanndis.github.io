'''''Outlook''''' is a weekly general interest [[Indian English|English]] and [[Hindi]] news magazine published in India.<ref>[http://www.worldpress.org/newspapers/ASIA/India.cfm India: Newspapers and Magazines Online] ''worldpress.org''. Retrieved 31 March 2013</ref><ref>[http://www.livemint.com/Opinion/b0OBLCrqIzns2v5HJTL7eO/Vinod-Mehta--Politicians-and-journalists-should-never-be-f.html Politicians, journalists should never be friends:Vinod Mehta] ''livemint.com''. Retrieved 31 March 2013</ref>

==History and profile==
''Outlook'' was first issued in October 1995 with [[Vinod Mehta]] as the [[editor in chief]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Vinod Mehta, editor of India's Outlook magazine, dies at 73|url=http://www.arabnews.com/world/news/715421|accessdate=20 November 2016|work=Arab News|agency=AP|date=8 March 2015}}</ref> It is owned by the [[Rajan Raheja Group]].<ref name="reddif">{{cite web|title=2. Which business family owns the Outlook Publishing group?|url=http://www.rediff.com/money/2004/feb/20quiz.htm|accessdate=6 January 2015}}</ref> The publisher is Outlook Publishing (India) Pvt. Ltd.<ref>{{cite news|title=Outlook group to relaunch Newsweek in India by Apr|url=http://www.business-standard.com/article/companies/outlook-group-to-relaunch-newsweek-in-india-by-apr-106022401017_1.html|accessdate=20 November 2016|work=Business Standard|date=24 February 2006|location=New Delhi}}</ref> It features contents from politics, sports, cinema, and stories of broad interests. By December 2018, ''Outlook'' magazine's [[Facebook]] following had grown to over 12 [[lakh]] (1.2 million).
