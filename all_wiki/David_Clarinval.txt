'''David Clarinval''' (born 10 January 1976) is a Belgian politician of the [[Mouvement Réformateur|Reformist Movement]] who has been serving as Minister of the Middle Class, [[Small and medium-sized enterprises|SME]]s, Self-employed, [[Minister of Agriculture (Belgium)|Agriculture]], Social Integration and Urban Policy in the [[government]] of [[Prime Minister of Belgium|Prime Minister]] [[Alexander De Croo]] since 2020.

==Early life==
Clarinval was born on January 10, 1976 in the town of [[Dinant]].<ref name="sud">{{cite news |title=Wilmès Première ministre: le député MR David Clarinval devrait devenir ministre du Budget |url=https://www.sudinfo.be/id148601/article/2019-10-26/wilmes-premiere-ministre-le-depute-mr-david-clarinval-devrait-devenir-ministre |accessdate=21 January 2020 |work=sudinfo.be |date=26 October 2019 |language=fr}}</ref> He studied political science at [[Université catholique de Louvain]].<ref name="sud"/> After graduation, he served as an assistant to the [[Mouvement Réformateur]] in the [[Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)|Chamber of Representatives]].<ref name="sud"/> 

==Political career==
In the [[2000 Belgian local elections]], at age 24, Clarinval was elected Mayor of the town of [[Bièvre, Belgium|Bièvre]].<ref name="sud"/> At the time, he was the youngest mayor in the country.<ref name="sud"/>

Clarinval has been a member of the Chamber of Representatives of [[Belgium]] since 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.david-clarinval.be/|title=David Clarinval : Ministre du Budget et de la Fonction publique|website=www.david-clarinval.be|access-date=2020-01-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.david-clarinval.be/page/david-clarinval-qui-suis-je.html|title=David Clarinval|website=www.david-clarinval.be|access-date=2020-01-21}}</ref> On 27 October 2019, he was appointed by Prime Minister [[Sophie Wilmès]] as the Minister of Budget and Public Service and Minister for Science Policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.belgium.be/fr/la_belgique/pouvoirs_publics/autorites_federales/gouvernement_federal/composition_gouvernement/index_david_clarinval|title=Monsieur David Clarinval|date=2015-09-24|website=Belgium.be|language=fr|access-date=2020-01-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brusselstimes.com/all-news/belgium-all-news/90682/european-space-agency-creates-150-new-jobs-in-belgium-esa-ariane-6-vega-launchers-sabca-safran-aeroboosters-thales-alenia-space-site-spacebel-david-clarinval/|title=European Space Agency creates 150 new jobs in Belgium|date=2020-01-21|website=The Brussels Times|language=en|access-date=2020-01-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brusselstimes.com/belgium/88574/several-envelops-with-white-powder-sent-to-federal-ministers-in-brussels/|title=Federal ministries on lockdown after envelopes with white powder discovered|date=2020-01-10|website=The Brussels Times|language=en|access-date=2020-01-21}}</ref> In November, he was appointed as one of three Deputy Prime Ministers, replacing [[Didier Reynders]].
