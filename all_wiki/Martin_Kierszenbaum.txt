'''Martin Kierszenbaum,''' also known by his [[pseudonym]] '''Cherry Cherry Boom Boom''', is an American music persona. As a songwriter and producer he worked for [[Lady Gaga]], [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]], [[Mylene Farmer]], [[Keane (band)|Keane]], [[Tokio Hotel]], [[Far East Movement]], [[Robyn]], [[Feist (singer)|Feist]], Flipsyde, Ivy Levan, [[Ai (singer)|Ai]] [[Colette Carr]], [[Alexandra Burke]] and [[Natalia Kills]].<ref name="corner">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6319848/corner-office-martin-kierszenbaum-cherrytree|title=Corner Office: Martin Kierszenbaum, Chairman of Cherrytree, on Working with Lady Gaga, A&R'ing Sting and the Streaming Debate|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-09-18}}</ref> He owns The Cherrytree Music Company which developed the careers of Lady Gaga, [[LMFAO]], [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]], [[Ellie Goulding]], [[La Roux]] and [[t.A.T.u.]]. Under the alias Cherry Cherry Boom Boom he releases electronic music and remixes.

== Biography ==
Kierszenbaum grew up studying classical piano. As a kid he lived with his family in Argentina, London, South America and Europe, before eventually settling in 
near New Haven, Connecticut when he was eight years old. He then moved to Michigan.<ref name="keyboard">{{cite news |last1=Regen |first1=Jon |title=Martin Kierszenbaum |url=https://www.keyboardmag.com/artists/martin-kierszenbaum |accessdate=17 December 2019 |work=KeyboardMag |language=en-us}}</ref>

He graduated from [[University of Michigan]] with a [[B.A.]] in music theory, Spanish, and communication. He then went on to the Annenberg School of [[University of Southern California]] to complete his Masters degree in communications management.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://arbrecordings.com/maroon/maroon.pdf |title=Maroon |accessdate=17 December 2019}}</ref>

His sister is a classical violinist.<ref name="corner" />

== Career ==
Kierszenbaum co-founded independent record label Arb Recordings which released the hip-hop duo Maroon which he also co-founded under the moniker Mk Chilly Dog. The band existed 1985â€“1994.<ref>{{cite web |title=Maroon, Lost Classics of Hip Hop |url=http://arbrecordings.com/maroon/home.html |website=arbrecordings.com |accessdate=17 December 2019}}</ref>

Kierszenbaum then got a job at the [[PolyGram|PolyGram Music Group]] mailroom. During 1989-1991 he worked as an international publicity manager at [[Warner Bros Records]].<ref name="keyboard" /> Later, he worked as publicist in the international division of [[A&M Records]] and by 1998 he was working for its new owner [[Interscope Records]] (Universal's unit) as the head of international marketing. Kierszenbaum was promoted to head of international operations and was responsible for discovering and signing non-American artists. His idea to promote t.A.T.u's single "[[All the Things She Said]]", whose lyrics he wrote, in Japan through karaoke bars led to 1.7 mil copies sold in the country.<ref name="nytimes">{{cite web |last1=Leeds |first1=Jeff |title=Cherrytree Records - Martin Kierszenbaum - Pop Marketing |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/12/05/arts/music/05cher.html |website=The New York Times |accessdate=17 December 2019 |date=5 December 2007}}</ref><ref name="musicweek" /><ref name="laweekly" /> He promoted [[Eminem]] and [[Limp Bizkit]] on the European market.

In 2005 he founded the music management record label and publishing firm Cherrytree Music Company.<ref name="laweekly">{{cite news |last1=Holslin |first1=Peter |title=What's Next for the Former Interscope Affiliate That Helped Launch Lady Gaga and Feist? |url=https://www.laweekly.com/whats-next-for-the-former-interscope-affiliate-that-helped-launch-lady-gaga-and-feist/ |accessdate=17 December 2019 |work=[[LA Weekly]] |date=2 February 2016}}</ref>

He was also working as President of [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] Pop/Rock at [[Interscope Records]] and head of international operations at [[Interscope Geffen A&M Records]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.songwriteruniverse.com/martin-kierszenbaum-123.htm|title=Interview With Martin Kierszenbaum, Chairman Of Cherrytree Records, President Of A&R Pop/Rock At Interscope Records And Head Of International Operations For Interscope-Geffen-A&M|last=Kawashima|first=Dale|work=Songwriter's Universe|accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/12/05/arts/music/05cher.html?pagewanted=all|title=As U.S. Pop Wanes Abroad, Talent Scout Looks Wide|last=Leeds|first=Jeff|date=5 December 2007|work=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=24 August 2011}}</ref>
There he signed Lady Gaga, [[LMFAO]], [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]] and [[t.A.T.u.]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Martin Kierszenbaum on Signing t.A.T.u. to Interscope, Launching Cherrytree |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8545426/martin-kierszenbaum-tatu-interscope-cherrytree-billboard-moment |accessdate=17 December 2019 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

He parted his way with Interscope in 2015.<ref name="musicweek">{{cite news |title=Cherrytree boss Martin Kierszenbaum on streaming, Sting & Shaggy and the future of the record biz |url=https://www.musicweek.com/management/read/i-m-super-gung-ho-cherrytree-boss-martin-kierszenbaum-on-streaming-sting-shaggy-and-the-future-of-the-record-biz/076245 |accessdate=17 December 2019 |work=www.musicweek.com |language=en}}</ref>

== Music ==
Kierszenbaum wrote and produced songs for ''[[200 km/h in the Wrong Lane]]'' by t.A.T.u. He also wrote and produced on Lady Gaga's debut album ''[[The Fame]]''.

His [[pseudonym]], ''Cherry Cherry Boom Boom'', is referred to in the [[Lady Gaga]] songs "[[Christmas Tree (Lady Gaga song)|Christmas Tree]]", "[[Eh, Eh (Nothing Else I Can Say)]]", "The Fame", "I Like It Rough" and "Starstruck". Also in the song "I Wanna Touch You" by [[Colby O'Donis]] and "White Flag" by [[Far East Movement]] featuring Kayla Kai, from their album ''[[Free Wired]]''.{{citation needed|date=October 2016}} It is also referenced in some of [[Space Cowboy (musician)|Space Cowboy's]] music such as his singles "[[Falling Down (Space Cowboy song)|Falling Down]]", "[[I Came 2 Party]]" (that featured [[Cinema Bizarre]]), and the first part of his pseudonym "cherry cherry" is referred to in "Not in Love" by [[Natalia Kills]].{{citation needed|date=October 2016}}

Martin featured in a remix of Far East Movement's "[[Like a G6]]" with Colette Carr called "G6 (V6 Reflip)". He also features in backing vocals on "Not In Love", a track from [[Natalia Kills]]'s debut album, ''[[Perfectionist (Natalia Kills album)|Perfectionist]]''.{{citation needed|date=October 2016}}
