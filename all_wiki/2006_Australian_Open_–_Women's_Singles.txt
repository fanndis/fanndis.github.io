{{main|2006 Australian Open}}
Third seed [[Amélie Mauresmo]] defeated [[Justine Henin|Justine Henin-Hardenne]] in the final 6–1, 2–0 ret., to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2006 Australian Open]]. Henin was suffering from stomach cramps resulting from the accidental misuse of anti-inflammatories for a chronic shoulder injury.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2006/jan/28/tennis.australianopen2007  Mauresmo's wait for a grand slam finally ends]</ref> This was the third match Mauresmo played during the tournament where her opponent retired.<ref>[http://www.espn.com/sports/tennis/aus06/news/story?id=2308903 Mauresmo wins first Slam when opponent retires]</ref> Mauresmo and Henin-Hardenne would have a rematch in the final of [[2006 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]] later that year, where Mauresmo would win in three sets.

[[Serena Williams]] was the defending champion, but she was defeated in the third round by [[Daniela Hantuchová]].<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/4631036.stm Hantuchova stuns champion Serena]</ref>

This tournament saw three-time Australian Open champion [[Martina Hingis]] make her Grand Slam comeback since [[2002 US Open – Women's Singles|2002 US Open]] after a three-year absence from the WTA Tour,<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2006/jan/25/tennis.uk Clijsters halts Hingis comeback | Sport | guardian.co.uk]</ref> and was also the first Grand Slam in which future World No. 1 and two-time Australian Open champion [[Victoria Azarenka]] competed in the main draw.<ref>[http://tennisstat.blogspot.com.au/2013/01/victoria-azarenka-and-australian-open.html Tennis Statistics: Victoria Azarenka and Australian Open 2013]</ref>
