'''Stewart Hosie''' (born 3 January 1963) is a [[Scottish National Party]] (SNP) politician serving as [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Dundee East (UK Parliament constituency)|Dundee East]] since [[2005 United Kingdom general election|2005]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/vote2005/html/688.stm|title=BBC NEWS – Election 2005 – Results – Dundee East}}</ref> He served as Deputy Leader of the SNP to [[Nicola Sturgeon]] from November 2014 to October 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Angus Robertson named as SNP deputy leader|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-37640935|accessdate=13 October 2016|work=BBC News|date=13 October 2016}}</ref> He was also the SNP [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|Deputy Westminster Leader]] and the SNP [[Shadow Chancellor of the Exchequer]] from May 2015, until he was succeeded in both positions by [[Kirsty Blackman]] in June 2017.

==Background==
Born in [[Dundee]], Stewart Hosie was educated at Brackens Primary School, Invertay Primary School Monifieth and [[Carnoustie High School]]. He then attended [[Dundee Institute of Technology]] where he gained a [[Higher Diploma]] in [[Computer Studies]].<ref name="stewarthosie.com">{{cite web|url=http://stewarthosie.com/?page_id=8|title=stewarthosie|website=stewarthosie}}</ref> He worked in IT for 20 years and ran his own business.<ref name="stewarthosie.com"/> From 1986 to 1989 he was the SNP's first Youth Convener.<ref name="auto">{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/democracylive/hi/representatives/profiles/38317.stm|title=Democracy Live – Your representatives – Stewart Hosie}}</ref> From 1999, he spent four years as the Party's National Secretary before being elected as the Organisation Convener in 2003.<ref name="auto"/>

==Political career==
After his election to the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] as the Member for Dundee East in 2005, Hosie was appointed the SNP Spokesperson for [[Home Affairs]] and Women, positions which he held until 2007.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=http://www.parliament.uk/biographies/Commons/Stewart-Hosie/1514|title=Stewart Hosie MP|work=UK Parliament}}</ref> He was also appointed SNP Spokesperson for the [[Treasury]]. In 2010 he was appointed [[Deputy Leader]] and [[Chief Whip]] of the SNP Westminster Group.<ref name="auto1"/> 
In the Commons, Hosie has become known for his outspoken criticism of the [[Welfare Reform Act 2012]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ghVWVG3OA5A|title=Stewart Hosie SNP 'This Bedroom tax only works if the policy fails!'|work=YouTube}}</ref> as well as his interest in the [[Prudential Regulation Authority (United Kingdom)|Prudential Regulation Authority]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://publications.parliament.uk/pa/cm201314/cmselect/cmtreasy/458/458.pdf |title=Unknown |accessdate=2020-02-18}}</ref> He sits on the Commons [[Treasury Select Committee]].<ref name="auto1"/> 
Hosie is known for his support and representation of charities, most notably anti-sectarian charity [[Nil by Mouth (charity)|Nil By Mouth]],<ref name="auto1"/> children's charity the Smart Play Network,<ref name="auto1"/> and humanitarian organisation [[Islamic Relief]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://stewarthosie.com/?p=692|title=Stewart Hosie – Depute Leader of the SNP}}</ref> He is also a member of the All Party Parliamentary Friends of the [[Baháʼí Faith|Baháʼí]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://publications.parliament.uk/pa/cm/cmallparty/register/bahais.htm|title=House of Commons – Register Of All-Party Groups as at 5 December 2013: Baháʼís}}</ref>

===SNP Deputy leadership bid, 2014===
{{further|Scottish National Party leadership election, 2014}}
Following defeat in the [[2014 Scottish independence referendum]], [[Scottish National Party]] leader and [[First Minister of Scotland]] [[Alex Salmond]] announced his resignation as SNP leader and First Minister of Scotland.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/sep/19/alex-salmond-resigns-first-minister-for-scotland|title=Alex Salmond resigns as first minister after Scotland rejects independence|author=Severin Carrell|work=the Guardian}}</ref> In the aftermath of his resignation, a leadership bid was launched, and SNP Deputy leader [[Nicola Sturgeon]] stood down to stand for leader, triggering a Deputy leader contest.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/scottish-independence/scottish-referendum-nicola-sturgeon-edges-closer-to-snp-leadership-9746536.html|title=Scottish referendum: Nicola Sturgeon edges closer to SNP leadership following Alex Salmond's resignation|work=The Independent}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-29297949|title=BBC News – Scottish ministers 'back Sturgeon as next first minister'|work=BBC News}}</ref>

The results of the election were announced at the SNP's Autumn conference on 14 November 2014, with Hosie polling 42.2%, [[Keith Brown (Scottish politician)|Keith Brown]] on 34.2% and [[Angela Constance]] on 23.5% in the first round of the [[single transferable vote]] election. In the second round, Hosie was elected after getting 55.5% of votes following the elimination of Constance. Turnout was 55% of SNP members.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-30052173|title=BBC News – SNP conference: Hosie elected SNP deputy leader|work=BBC News}}</ref>

==Electoral history==
First standing for election to the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] at the [[1992 United Kingdom general election|1992 general election]] in [[Kirkcaldy (UK Parliament constituency)|Kirkcaldy]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politicsresources.net/area/uk/ge92/ge92index.htm|title=UK General election results April 9th 1992 [Archive]}}</ref> he later stood for both the [[Westminster]] and [[Scottish parliament]]. He contested Kirkcaldy again at the [[1997 United Kingdom general election|1997 general election]],<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|url=http://www.politicsresources.net/area/uk/constit/641.htm|title=Kirkcaldy}}</ref> and also contested [[Kirkcaldy (Scottish Parliament constituency)]] at the [[1999 Scottish Parliament election]].<ref name="parliament.uk">{{cite web|url=http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/msps/25290.aspx|title=Falkirk East to Livingston|date=31 March 2011|website=www.scottish.parliament.uk}}</ref> At the [[2001 United Kingdom general election|2001 general election]], he contested [[Dundee East (UK Parliament constituency)|Dundee East]] and reduced [[Scottish Labour Party|Labour]]'s majority from 9,961 votes at the previous election to 4,466 votes.<ref name="auto2"/> and was eventually elected on [[2005 United Kingdom general election|2005 general election]], gaining the seat from Labour.<ref name="auto3">{{cite web|url=http://www.politicsresources.net/area/uk/ge05/ge05index.htm|title=UK General election results 2005 [Archive]}}</ref> Hosie retained the seat with an increased majority at the [[2010 United Kingdom general election|2010 general election]].<ref name="auto4">{{cite web|url=http://www.politicsresources.net/area/uk/ge10/ge10index.htm|title=UK General election results 2010 [Archive]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hosie has 2 siblings. Hosie was married to [[Shona Robison]], [[Member of the Scottish Parliament|MSP]] for [[Dundee City East (Scottish Parliament constituency)]] and [[Cabinet Secretary for Health, Wellbeing and Sport]]. The couple have a daughter.<ref>http://stewarthosie.com/about/{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In 2012, he suffered from a [[transient ischaemic attack]] (minor stroke), and was treated in [[Ninewells Hospital]] in Dundee.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-20528212|title=BBC News – SNP MP Stewart Hosie recovering after stroke|work=BBC News}}</ref> He is a supporter of [[Dundee United F.C.]].<ref name="auto"/>

In May 2016, Hosie and Robison announced that they had separated.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecourier.co.uk/fp/news/politics/scottish-politics/173252/snp-power-couple-hosie-robison-split-nearly-20-years-marriage/|title=SNP power couple Hosie and Robison split after nearly 20 years of marriage - The Courier}}</ref> This was followed by reports that Hosie and his colleague [[Angus Macneil]] had both had an affair with Westminster-based freelance journalist [[Serena Cowdy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/05/16/snps-stewart-hosie-and-angus-macneil-split-from-wives-over-alleg/|title=SNP MPs Stewart Hosie and Angus MacNeil split from wives over alleged affairs with same woman|first=Telegraph|last=Reporters|date=17 May 2016|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}}</ref> On 22 May, Hosie announced his intention not to stand for re-election as Deputy Leader, due to "intense scrutiny" by the media of his private life, meaning that his term of office would end at the SNP's annual conference later in the year.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-36354430|title=SNP's Stewart Hosie to quit as deputy leader |work=[[BBC News]] |date=22 May 2016}}</ref>

In August 2018, Hosie married [[Serena Cowdy]] in Scotland.
