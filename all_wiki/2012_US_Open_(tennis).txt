The '''2012 [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]]''' was a [[tennis]] tournament played on the [[Tennis court#Hard courts|outdoor hard courts]] at the [[USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center]] in [[Flushing Meadows]] Park, of [[Queens]], New York City, United States. It was played from August 27 to September 10.<ref>{{cite web|title=Federer, Djokovic, Nadal headline men's field|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2012-08-08/201208081344455811922.html?promo=home|accessdate=August 14, 2012|publisher=www.usopen.org|date=August 8, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120815150133/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2012-08-08/201208081344455811922.html?promo=home|archive-date=August 15, 2012|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> As a result of adverse weather conditions on September 8, which included a full evacuation of the National Tennis Center because of an upcoming tornado, another day was added to the schedule for the fifth straight year,<ref>{{cite web|title= U.S. Open: For Fifth Straight Year, Men’s Final Pushed to Monday|url=http://www.sportsmediawatch.com/2012/09/u-s-open-for-fifth-straight-year-mens-final-pushed-to-monday/|accessdate=September 9, 2012|publisher= Sports Media Watch |date=September 8, 2012}}</ref> with the women's final postponed to the afternoon of Sunday, September 9 rather than the previous evening, the men's semi-final between [[Novak Djokovic]] and [[David Ferrer]] suspended on September 8 and completed on September 9, and the men's final postponed to the afternoon of Monday, September 10.

Djokovic and [[Samantha Stosur]] were the defending men's and women's singles champions, respectively. Both were unsuccessful in their title defences; Stosur being narrowly defeated by World No. 1 [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the quarter-finals, and Djokovic defeated in the final by [[Andy Murray]]. Murray became the first British man since [[Fred Perry]] in 1936 to win a Grand Slam singles title, and the first man to win both the US Open and the [[Tennis at the 2012 Summer Olympics|Olympic men's singles gold medal]] in the same year.<ref>{{cite web|last=Fisher|first=Stewart|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/mobile/news/home-news/andy-murray-wins-first-grand-slam-title-with-us-open-triumph.1347344961?_=27cfac7148112eaa048ea53bbeb10cacab631742|title=Andy Murray wins first Grand Slam title with US Open triumph|work=[[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]]|date=September 11, 2012|accessdate=September 12, 2012}}</ref> In the women's draw, [[Serena Williams]] won her fourth US Open title, and first since [[2008 US Open (tennis)|2008]], by defeating [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the final.

==Notable Events==
[[File:Sara errani babolat 2012 us open cropped.jpg|thumb|[[Sara Errani]] – Winner of Women's Doubles and Semifinalist of Women's Singles]]
* [[2010 US Open (tennis)|2010]] champion and [[2011 US Open (tennis)|2011]] finalist [[Rafael Nadal]] withdrew from the tournament because of a knee injury.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/08/16/sports/tennis/rafael-nadal-withdraws-from-us-open.html | work=The New York Times | first=Ben | last=Rothenberg | title=Nadal, Still Recovering From Knee Problems, Will Miss United States Open | date=August 15, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/aug/15/rafael-nadal-us-open-knee-injury
|title=Rafael Nadal out of US Open after failing to recover from knee injury|date=August 15, 2012|work=Guardian UK|accessdate=August 27, 2012|location=London}}</ref>
*2010 finalist [[Vera Zvonareva]] also withdrew from the tournament due to illness.
*Two former World No. 1s and US Open champions ended their careers after this year's US Open. Three-time women's champion [[Kim Clijsters]] suffered her first defeat at Flushing Meadows since losing the [[2003 US Open (tennis)|2003]] final to her [[Clijsters–Henin rivalry|compatriot and rival]] [[Justine Henin]], when she was defeated in the second round by [[Laura Robson]]. This loss marked the end of Clijsters' singles career. American [[Andy Roddick]], who won in 2003, retired from professional tennis with his loss against [[2009 US Open (tennis)|2009]] champion [[Juan Martín del Potro]] in the fourth round.
* Robson followed up her upset of Clijsters with a third round victory against [[Li Na]], making the fourth round of a Grand Slam tournament for the first time, and this was also the first British woman to reach the fourth round in any Grand Slam since [[Samantha Smith (tennis)|Samantha Smith]] did so in [[1998 Wimbledon Championships|1998 Wimbledon]].
*On September 3, [[John Isner]] and [[Philipp Kohlschreiber]] finished play at 2:26 am, tying the [[1993 US Open (tennis)|1993]] [[Mats Wilander]]/[[Mikael Pernfors]] record for the latest-ever finish to a matchday at the tournament.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/sep/03/us-open-2012-kohlschreiber-isner | location=London | work=The Guardian | title=US Open 2012: Philipp Kohlschreiber and John Isner equal record finish | date=September 3, 2012}}</ref>
* [[Serena Williams]] won 23 consecutive games from 4&ndash;4 in the first set against [[Ekaterina Makarova]] in the third round through to 3&ndash;0 in the first set against [[Ana Ivanovic]] in the quarter-finals.
*After 17 consecutive Grand Slam tournaments without a quarter-final appearance, Ivanovic reached that stage for the first time since winning the [[2008 French Open]].<ref>[http://www.wtatennis.com/news/20120905/stats-corner-majors-between-titles-qfs_2256076_2911617 WTA | News | Latest News | Stats Corner: Majors Between Titles & QFs]</ref>
*Ivanovic, [[Marion Bartoli]] and [[Roberta Vinci]] all advanced to the US Open quarter-finals for the first time. For Vinci, this was her first Grand Slam quarter-final, eleven years and 31 Grand Slam tournaments after debuting at the [[2001 US Open (tennis)|2001 US Open]].<ref>[http://espn.go.com/espnw/more-sports/8334639/2012-us-open-no-2-agnieszka-radwanska-labors-loses-roberta-vinci 2012 U.S. Open – No. 2 Agnieszka Radwanska labors, loses to Roberta Vinci – espnW]</ref>
* [[Maria Sharapova]] returned to the semi-finals for the first time since 2006, and [[Sara Errani]] advanced to her first ever US Open semi-final.
* [[Serena Williams]] advanced to her second straight US Open final, and her sixth overall. She defeated [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the first three-set final to be decided since 1995.{{citation needed|date=September 2012}}
*In the men's draw, [[Tomáš Berdych]] advanced to his first US Open quarter-final by virtue of his three-set win against [[Nicolás Almagro]] in the fourth round. He then advanced to the semi-finals after upsetting five-time US Open champion [[Roger Federer]] in the quarter-finals.
**With Federer's defeat, this meant that for the first time since the [[2004 French Open]], neither Federer nor Nadal featured in a Grand Slam semi-final.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2012-09-07/ferrer-reaches-us-open-semi-final/4248286?section=sport Ferrer reaches US Open semi-final - ABC Grandstand Sport - ABC News (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)]</ref>
* With their victory in the men's doubles final, [[Bob and Mike Bryan]] took sole possession of the Open-era record for most Grand Slam men's doubles titles. Their 12th Grand Slam title took them past [[The Woodies]] ([[Todd Woodbridge]] and [[Mark Woodforde]]). The win was also their fourth at the US Open, tying the Open-era record of [[Bob Lutz (tennis)|Bob Lutz]] and [[Stan Smith]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://espn.go.com/tennis/usopen12/story/_/id/8348728/2012-us-open-bob-bryan-mike-bryan-win-men-doubles-set-slam-record |title=Bryan brothers set Slam record |agency=Associated Press |publisher=ESPN.com |date=September 7, 2012 |accessdate=September 11, 2012}}</ref>
*The women's final between [[Victoria Azarenka]] and [[Serena Williams]] was postponed to Sunday, due to inclement weather, for the fourth time in five years.{{citation needed|date=September 2012}}
*The second men's semi-final between [[David Ferrer]] and [[Novak Djokovic]] was also postponed to Sunday, also due to inclement weather, with the men's final to be played on Monday for the fifth year in a row.{{citation needed|date=September 2012}}
* [[Andy Murray]] became the first British winner of a Grand Slam singles title since 1977, and the first British man to do so since 1936, by defeating the defending champion Djokovic in the final. Lasting 4:54, it was the equal-longest US Open final by duration in history, and the equal-second longest Grand Slam final in the Open Era, only behind the [[2012 Australian Open &ndash; Men's Singles final|2012 Australian Open final]].<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2012-09-11/murray-downs-djokovic-to-win-us-open/4254534?section=sport Murray downs Djokovic to win US Open - ABC Grandstand Sport - ABC News (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)]</ref>
*For the first time since 2003, the four Grand Slam Men's Singles titles were won by different players.{{citation needed|date=September 2012}}

===Seniors===

====Men's singles====
{{main|2012 US Open – Men's Singles}}

:<small>• It was Murray's 1st career Grand Slam singles title.</small>

====Women's singles====
{{main|2012 US Open – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]''' defeated {{flagicon|BLR}} [[Victoria Azarenka]], 6–2, 2–6, 7–5
:<small>• It was Williams' 15th career Grand Slam singles title and her 4th at the US Open.</small>

====Men's doubles====
{{main|2012 US Open – Men's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Bob Bryan]]''' / {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Mike Bryan]]''' defeated {{flagicon|IND}} [[Leander Paes]] / {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Radek Štěpánek]], 6–3, 6–4
:<small>• It was Bob and Mike's 12th career Grand Slam doubles title and their 4th at the US Open.</small>

====Women's doubles====
{{main|2012 US Open – Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Sara Errani]]''' / {{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Roberta Vinci]]''' defeated {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Andrea Hlaváčková]] / {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Lucie Hradecká]], 6–4, 6–2
:<small>• It was Errani's 2nd career Grand Slam doubles title and her 1st at the US Open.</small>
:<small>• It was Vinci's 2nd career Grand Slam doubles title and her 1st at the US Open.</small>

====Mixed doubles====
{{main|2012 US Open – Mixed Doubles}}
{{flagicon|RUS}} '''[[Ekaterina Makarova]]''' / {{flagicon|BRA}} '''[[Bruno Soares]]''' defeated {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Květa Peschke]] / {{flagicon|POL}} [[Marcin Matkowski]], 6–7<sup>(8–10)</sup>, 6–1, [12–10]
:<small>• It was Makarova's 1st career Grand Slam mixed doubles title.</small>
:<small>• It was Soares' 1st career Grand Slam mixed doubles title.</small>
