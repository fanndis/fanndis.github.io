'''Premier Tower''' is mixed-use skyscraper currently under construction on the corner of Bourke Street and [[Spencer Street, Melbourne|Spencer Street]], in [[Melbourne]], Australia.<ref>[https://urban.melbourne/projects/cbd-west-end/134-160-spencer-street-melbourne 134-160 Spencer Street, Melbourne]. ''UrbanMelbourne.info''. Retrieved 21 May 2015</ref>

Designed by [[Elenberg Fraser]], plans for a 294-metre (965&nbsp;ft) tall building with 90-storeys were initially proposed in 2014;<ref>Lucas, Clay. (4 September 2014). [http://www.theage.com.au/victoria/90storey-tower-to-replace-savoy-tavern-on-spencer-street-20140904-10c98b.html "90-storey tower to replace Savoy Tavern on Spencer Street"]. ''[[The Age]]''. Retrieved 21 May 2015</ref><ref>Lenaghan, Nick. (14 May 2015). [http://www.afr.com/real-estate/commercial/investment/singaporelisted-fragrance-wins-approval-for-savoy-tower-20150514-gh1o5l "Singapore-listed Fragrance wins approval for Savoy tower"]. ''[[The Australian Financial Review]]''. Retrieved 21 May 2015</ref> however, in order not to cast a shadow on the [[Yarra River]] to the south, both the height and the number of levels of the project were reduced in later plans, which were submitted to the Department of Planning. In May 2015, Planning Minister [[Richard Wynne]] approved the development, which now consists of a 245.9–metre high residential and hotel skyscraper of 78 levels. When built, Premier Tower will become one of the [[List of tallest buildings in Melbourne|tallest buildings in Melbourne]].<ref name="premctbuh">[http://skyscrapercenter.com/building/134-160-spencer-street/17613 Premier Tower – The Skyscraper Center]. ''[[Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat]]''. Retrieved 27 January 2016</ref><ref name="approved">Preiss, Benjamin. (14 May 2015). [http://www.theage.com.au/victoria/approved-68floor-tower-for-savoy-tavern-site-on-corner-of-bourke-and-spencer-streets-20150514-gh1bwy.html "Approved: 68-floor tower for Savoy Tavern site on corner of Bourke and Spencer streets"]. ''[[The Age]]''. Retrieved 21 May 2015</ref> According to Elenberg Fraser, the design of the building was in part inspired by the music video of "[[Haunted (Beyoncé song)|Ghost]]" by [[Beyoncé]].<ref>Miller, Ross. (6 July 2014). [https://www.theverge.com/tldr/2015/7/6/8900107/beyonce-skyscraper-melbourne-premiere-tower "Let's talk about this Beyoncé-inspired skyscraper being built in Australia"]. ''The Verge''. Retrieved 13 March 2016</ref>

Early construction on the project commenced in January 2017.<ref name="premctbuh"/> The skyscraper is developed by the Fragrance Group, and will cost [[Australian dollar|AUD]]$350 million to construct.<ref>(19 May 2015).
