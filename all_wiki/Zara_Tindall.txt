'''Zara Anne Elizabeth Tindall''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|MBE}} (née '''Phillips'''; born 15 May 1981) is a granddaughter of [[Queen Elizabeth II]], an [[equestrianism|equestrian]], and an [[Olympic Games|Olympian]]. She is second child and only daughter of [[Anne, Princess Royal]] and [[Mark Phillips]]. At birth, she was sixth in the [[line of succession to the British throne]]; she is currently 18th in line.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.royal.uk/succession|title=Succession of the Royal Family|publisher=[[Royal Household]]|accessdate=30 July 2020}}</ref>

Tindall won the [[Eventing World Championship]] in [[Aachen]] in 2006. That same year, she was voted 2006 [[BBC Sports Personality of the Year Award|BBC Sports Personality of the Year]] by the public. She was appointed [[Order of the British Empire|Member of the Order of the British Empire]] (MBE) in the [[2007 New Year Honours]] for her services to equestrianism.<ref name=gazette/> In 2012, she carried an Olympic flame at [[Cheltenham Racecourse]] on her horse Toytown. As a member of the [[Team GB|Great Britain Eventing Team]], she won a [[Equestrian at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Team eventing|silver medal at the 2012 Summer Olympics]], presented to her by her mother.

She married [[rugby union]] player [[Mike Tindall]] in 2011. The couple have two children.

==Early life and education==

Zara Anne Elizabeth Phillips was born on 15 May 1981 at 8:15 pm in the Lindo Wing of [[St Mary's Hospital, London]]. She was christened on 27 July 1981, at Windsor Castle; her first name was suggested by her uncle, the [[Charles, Prince of Wales|Prince of Wales]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Zara Phillips| url=http://www.hellomagazine.com/profiles/zara-phillips/|accessdate=24 August 2013}}</ref> Her godparents are her maternal uncle, the [[Prince Andrew|Duke of York]]; the [[Leonora Anson, Countess of Lichfield|Countess of Lichfield]]; Helen, Lady Stewart, the wife of [[Jackie Stewart|Sir Jackie Stewart]]; [[Andrew Parker Bowles]]; and [[Hugh Thomas (equestrian)|Hugh Thomas]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Zara Phillips|url=http://www.equestrianlife.com/mw/Zara_Phillips|work=Equestrian Life|accessdate=11 August 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131016081800/http://www.equestrianlife.com/mw/Zara_Phillips|archive-date=16 October 2013}}</ref> She has an elder brother, [[Peter Phillips|Peter]], and two younger half-sisters, Felicity Wade (née Tonkin;<ref name="NZHerald">{{cite web|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/lifestyle/news/article.cfm?c_id=6&objectid=11907630|title=Zara Tindall's 'secret' nephew born in NZ|publisher=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|date=20 August 2017|access-date=12 August 2020|quote=Wade, former Tonkin, is the daughter of Mark Phillips - also father to Queen Elizabeth's grandchildren Zara Tindall and her brother Peter Phillips.}}</ref> from her father's affair with Heather Tonkin) and Stephanie Phillips, from her father's second marriage to [[Sandy Pflueger]].

Phillips attended Beaudesert Park School in [[Stroud (district)|Stroud]], [[Gloucestershire]], and [[Port Regis School]] in [[Shaftesbury]], [[Dorset]], before following other members of the royal family in attending [[Gordonstoun|Gordonstoun School]] in [[Moray]], Scotland. During her schooldays, Phillips excelled at many sporting activities, representing her schools in hockey, athletics and gymnastics. She later studied at [[University of Exeter]] and qualified as a [[Physical therapy|physiotherapist]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Tyzack|first1=Anna|title=Zara Phillips interview|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/theroyalfamily/7928346/Zara-Phillips-interview.html|accessdate=6 November 2015|work=The Telegraph|date=6 August 2010}}</ref><ref name="Hello_Magazine">{{cite news|url=http://www.hellomagazine.com/profiles/zara-phillips/|title=Latest news and profile of Zara Phillips|newspaper=Hello|accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref>

==Equestrianism==
[[File:Zara Phillips High Kingdom cross country Olympics 2012.jpg|thumb|left|Zara Tindall competing at the 2012 Summer Olympics in London.]]

After university, she began to pursue an equestrian career, in the footsteps of her parents. In June 2003, she announced that she had secured a sponsorship deal with [[Cantor Fitzgerald|Cantor Index]], a leading company in [[spread betting]], to help cover the costs of her equestrian career.<ref name="Gloucestershire">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/gloucestershire/content/articles/2005/05/03/zara_phillips_feature.shtml|title=The young royals: Zara Phillips|publisher=BBC|date=28 October 2014|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> She finished runner up at [[Burghley Horse Trials]] in 2003 in her first four-star event.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrylife.co.uk/out-and-about/sporting-country-pursuits/zara-tindall-riding-falling-off-high-kingdom-dude-164550?_ga=2.261478797.123065091.1526531722-832846644.1526531722|title=Zara Tindall on riding, falling off, and why High Kingdom is ‘such a dude’|publisher=Country Life|first=Kate|last=Green|date=30 August 2017|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> Tindall missed the [[2004 Summer Olympics]] in Athens, when her horse suffered an injury during training.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/equestrian/7448590.stm |title=Phillips out of GB Olympic team|work=BBC News |date=11 June 2008 |accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref>

Riding her horse [[Toytown (horse)|Toytown]], Tindall collected individual and team gold medals at the 2005 [[European Eventing Championship]] in Blenheim,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://inside.fei.org/news/2005-fei-european-eventing-championship|title=2005 FEI European Eventing Championship|publisher=Fédération Equestre Internationale|date=10 September 2005|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref> and individual gold and team silver medals at the [[2006 FEI World Equestrian Games]] in [[Aachen]], Germany, making her the reigning [[Eventing World Championship|Eventing World Champion]] until 2010.<ref name="Germany-Champ">{{cite web|url=https://inside.fei.org/fei/fei-weg/2006|title=2006 Aachen (GER)|publisher=Fédération Equestre Internationale|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/olympics/equestrianism/7506603/Zara-Phillips-and-HRH-Princess-Royal-offer-unique-insight-into-sport-of-equestrianism.html|title=Zara Phillips and HRH Princess Royal offer unique insight into sport of equestrianism|newspaper=The Daily Telegraph|first=Pippa|last=Cuckson|date=23 March 2010|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref> The same year after her win in Germany, she was voted [[BBC Sports Personality of the Year Award|BBC Sports Personality of the Year]] by the British viewing public (an award her mother won in 1971).<ref name="Germany-Champ"/> She was also appointed [[Order of the British Empire|Member of the Order of the British Empire]] (MBE) in the [[2007 New Year Honours]] for her services to equestrianism.<ref name=gazette>{{London Gazette|issue=58196 |supp=y|pages=13–21|date=30 December 2006}}</ref> Despite winning team gold at the 2007 European Eventing Championships in Italy, she failed to defend her individual title after a problem in the show-jumping phase of the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.horseandhound.co.uk/eventingleague/britishteam/European_eventing_championships_show_jumping_news_143540.html |title=Horses for sale, Equestrian news|publisher=Horse & Hound|accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref>

The [[British Olympic Association]] announced that Tindall would ride Toytown for the British equestrian team at the Beijing Olympic Games 2008 in Hong Kong.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/equestrian/7392233.stm |title=Phillips heads GB eventing team|work=BBC News|date=9 May 2008|accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref> However Toytown was injured during training and she was forced to withdraw from the team. <ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/equestrian/7448590.stm |title=Phillips out of GB Olympic team|work=BBC News |date=11 June 2008 |accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref>

On 25 October 2008, Tindall fell from her horse, Tsunami II, at the 15th fence of a cross-country event at [[Pau, Pyrénées-Atlantiques|Pau]], France, and broke her right [[collarbone]]. The horse broke its neck after it tipped over the hedge and was [[Animal euthanasia|put down]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/olympic_games/7691920.stm |title=Rider Phillips breaks collarbone|work=BBC News|date=26 October 2008|accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref> In 2009, Tindall announced she was designing her own range of equestrian clothing for [[Musto (company)|Musto Outdoor Clothing]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.horseandhound.co.uk/news/397/293313.html|title=Zara Phillips designs equestrian clothing line for Musto|publisher=Horse & Hound|accessdate=1 December 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110610075047/http://www.horseandhound.co.uk/news/397/293313.html|archive-date=10 June 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Moore |first=Matthew |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/newstopics/theroyalfamily/6850109/Zara-Phillips-designs-outdoor-clothing-range.html |title=Zara Phillips designs outdoor clothing range|newspaper=Telegraph|date= 20 December 2009|accessdate=1 December 2011 |location=London}}</ref> The range is named ZP176 after the team number she was given when she first represented her country. The range was officially launched in July 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.musto.com/fcp/content/zara_video/content |title=Content |publisher=Musto |accessdate=1 December 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111007185759/http://www.musto.com/fcp/content/zara_video/content |archive-date=7 October 2011  }}</ref>

On 11 June 2012, Tindall was announced as a member of the British Equestrian team for the [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012 London Olympic Games]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/jun/11/zara-phillips-compete-2012-olympics |title=Phillips in GB Olympic team|newspaper=The Guardian|date=11 June 2012|accessdate=12 June 2012}}</ref> She competed in the Olympics on High Kingdom and won a silver medal in the team event. Her mother, Princess Anne, presented her medal.<ref>[http://www.london2012.com/news/articles/women-shine-cross-country-course.html Women Shine Cross Country Course] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120801094114/http://www.london2012.com/news/articles/women-shine-cross-country-course.html |date=1 August 2012 }} London 2012</ref> Tindall finished second at [[Luhmühlen Horse Trials]] 2013, on her top horse High Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.horseandhound.co.uk/news/andrew-nicholson-wins-luhmuhlen-horse-trials-cci4-395164|title=Andrew Nicholson wins Luhmühlen horse trials CCI4* — Zara Phillips second|publisher=Horse & Hound|date=16 June 2013|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref> At the World Equestrian Games in 2014, Tindall and High Kingdom were part of the British team that won team silver.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/equestrian/27921329|title=Zara Phillips in Great Britain squad for World Equestrian Games|publisher=BBC|date=1 July 2014|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref>

Tindall stopped using her maiden name, Phillips, in March 2016. She competed for the first time as Zara Tindall during her unsuccessful attempt to qualify for the [[2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Rio Olympic Games]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/36592142|title=Rio 2016: Zara Tindall misses out on Olympics selection|publisher=BBC|date=21 June 2016|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref> In August 2020, Tindall was selected for her first appearance on the British Team competing at the prestigious [[FEI Nations Cup]] in Le Pin au Haras, [[France]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Murray |first1=Becky |title=Zara Tindall’s Tokyo hopeful selected for first Nations Cup of the season |url=https://www.horseandhound.co.uk/news/zara-tindalls-tokyo-hopeful-selected-for-first-british-team-nations-cup-721539 |website=Horse & Hound |access-date=3 December 2020}}</ref>

==Charity work==
Tindall frequently supports and attends events for various charitable causes, mainly revolving around spinal injuries, equestrianism, and children's causes. In 2005, she auctioned one of her evening gowns, worn at the London premiere of the film ''[[Seabiscuit (film)|Seabiscuit]]'', to raise money for [[tsunami]] relief. She also undertook a visit to New Zealand in her role as patron of The Catwalk Trust.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://catwalk.org.nz/project/zara-phillips-mbe/|title=Zara Phillips MBE|publisher=Catwalk|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/lifestyle/news/article.cfm?c_id=6&objectid=11464443|title=Charity welcomes royal Zara Phillips|publisher=NZ Herald|date=13 June 2015|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> From 1998 to 2005, she served as the president of Club 16–24, a group which encourages young people to take an interest in racing.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/1352330/Zara-Phillips-to-lead-junior-club-at-Cheltenham.html|title=Zara Phillips to lead junior club at Cheltenham|newspaper=The Daily Telegraph|first=Robert|last=Hardman|date=11 August 2000|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> She is associated with Inspire, a Salisbury-based medical research charity which helps to improve the quality of life of people with spinal cord injuries, and [[Sargent Cancer Care for Children]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.scotsman.com/lifestyle/profile-zara-phillips-royal-with-a-common-touch-1-1371100|title=Profile: Zara Phillips – Royal with a common touch|newspaper=The Scotsman|date=24 June 2010|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> the UK's leading charity for children's cancer.  She is patron of Lucy Air Ambulance for Children,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lucyairambulance.org.uk/donate/all-news/our-patron-zara-philips-speaks-out-in-support |title=Our Patron Zara Phillips, Speaks out in support |accessdate=7 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120110202109/http://www.lucyairambulance.org.uk/donate/all-news/our-patron-zara-philips-speaks-out-in-support |archive-date=10 January 2012 }}</ref> which is the UK's first dedicated air transfer service to fly critically ill infants and children to hospitals for urgent care. 

Tindall has appeared at events for for [[John Caudwell|The Caudwell Charitable Trust]], which targets children with special needs, disabilities and serious illnesses.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/stoke/features/2004/03/caudwell_ball.shtml|title=A night full of sweetness and light|publisher=BBC|date=March 2004|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> In 2006, she took part in a special charity day for [[Cantor Index]], whose staff were killed in the [[11 September 2001 attacks]]. In 2007, she became patron of the Mark Davies Injured Riders Fund.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.horseandhound.co.uk/eventingleague/article.php?id=143648/|title=Horses for sale|publisher=Horse & Hound|accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref> To help with [[Sport Relief|Sport Relief 2008]], she posed for a portrait by artist [[Jack Vettriano]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/showbiz/portrait-of-zara-the-royal-vamp-by-jack-vettriano-7243526.html |title=Portrait of Zara, the royal vamp – by Jack Vettriano |date=27 February 2008 |work=standard.co.uk}}</ref> In 2009, she attended a celebrity poker tournament in [[Monaco]] in aid of [[Darfur]], Sudan.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allheadlinenews.com/articles/7014972286|title=News|publisher=AHN|accessdate=1 December 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519170814/http://www.allheadlinenews.com/articles/7014972286|archive-date=19 May 2010}}</ref> In October 2010, she attended a celebrity poker tournament in London, in aid of [[Cancer Research UK]], of which she is patron. In 2011, she auctioned another evening gown in aide of the [[2011 Christchurch earthquake|Christchurch earthquake]] appeal, raising £22,000. In 2013, she visited the Stroud Maternity Ward to celebrate their 60th anniversary. In 2014, Tindall lent her support to the #bringbackourgirls campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sel.com.au/client/zara-phillips-mbe/|title=Zara Phillips, MBE|publisher=Sports and Entertainment Limited|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref>
