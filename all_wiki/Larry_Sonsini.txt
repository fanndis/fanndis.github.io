'''Larry Sonsini''' (born c. 1941 in [[Rome, New York]]) is an American [[lawyer]] specializing all aspects of [[corporate law]]. He is senior partner and founder<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsgr.com/WSGR/Display.aspx?SectionName=news/1017-martin.htm|title=Press Release: Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati Elects Katharine Martin as Chair of the Board - Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati|publisher=}}</ref> of [[Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati]], a [[law firm]] in [[business law|business]] and [[intellectual property law]]. Sonsini has been described by ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'' as "the most influential and well-connected lawyer in the industry."<ref name=fortune/>

Sonsini studied law at the [[University of California, Berkeley School of Law]] where he graduated and received his J.D. degree from in 1966.<ref>{{cite news|last=Rivlin|first=Gary|title=A Counselor Pulled From the Shadows|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/07/30/business/yourmoney/30sonsini.html?_r=1&pagewanted=all|newspaper=The New York Times|date=July 30, 2006}}</ref> He also received his B.A. degree in political science from the [[University of California, Berkeley]] in 1963.<ref>WSGR, Larry Sonsini, Biography, https://www.wsgr.com/WSGR/DBIndex.aspx?SectionName=attorneys/BIOS/113.htm</ref>

Sonsini started out counseling semiconductor companies including [[Cypress Semiconductor]], [[National Semiconductor]] and [[LSI Logic]]. He later counseled hardware firms such as [[Sun Microsystems]], [[Apple Computer]], [[Silicon Graphics]] and [[Seagate Technology]] and software companies such as [[Novell]], [[Sybase]], [[WordPerfect]] and [[VMware]].  He has also worked for [[Google]], [[Netscape]] and [[YouTube]].<ref name=fortune>{{cite news|last=Parloff|first=Roger|title=Scandals rock Silicon Valley's top legal ace|url=https://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/fortune_archive/2006/11/27/8394382/index.htm|newspaper=Fortune|date=November 17, 2006}}</ref>  He has served as an advisor to many of the key figures in Silicon Valley history, including Steve Jobs, and the principals of Google and Hewlett-Packard.

Sonsini was recognized for his commitment to education in Silicon Valley at the [[Silicon Valley Education Foundation]]'s September 2012 Pioneers & Purpose event. He was honored with the Pioneer Business Leader Award, which is awarded to individuals who have achieved outstanding accomplishments in business and education.

His son Pete Sonsini joined venture capital firm [[New Enterprise Associates]] in 2005, and now is a [[General partnership|general partner]] of the firm and head of its enterprise practice group.<ref>{{Cite news |title= Pete Sonsini Brings Tech Heritage To NEA |work= Venture Capital Dispatch |publisher= Wall Street Journal |date= October 17, 2011 |author= Scott Denne |url= https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2011/10/17/pete-sonsini-brings-tech-heritage-to-nea/s |accessdate= October 6, 2016 }}</ref><ref>http://www.nea.com/team/peter-sonsini</ref> Another son Matthew W. Sonsini is married to Lisa Sobrato Sonsini, daughter of billionaire real estate developer [[John A. Sobrato]].<ref>https://www.mercurynews.com/2008/04/02/sobrato-family-ties-get-tighter/</ref>
