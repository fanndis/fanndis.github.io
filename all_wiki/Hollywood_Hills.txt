'''Hollywood Hills''' is a residential neighborhood in the [[Central Los Angeles|Central LA]] region of the [[City of Los Angeles]], [[California]].

__TOC__

==Geography==
[[File:Los Angeles from Hollywood Hills.jpg|thumb|left|Los Angeles from Hollywood Hills]]
The Hollywood Hills straddle the [[Cahuenga Pass]] within the [[Santa Monica Mountains]].

The neighborhood touches [[Studio City]], [[Universal City, California|Universal City]] and [[Burbank, California|Burbank]] on the north, [[Griffith Park]] on the north and east, [[Los Feliz, Los Angeles|Los Feliz]] on the southeast, [[Hollywood]] on the south and [[Hollywood Hills West]] on the west. It includes [[Forest Lawn Memorial Park (Hollywood Hills)|Forest Lawn Memorial Park]], the [[Hollywood Reservoir]], the [[Hollywood Sign]], the [[Hollywood Bowl]] and the [[John Anson Ford Amphitheatre|John Anson Ford Theater]].<ref name=MappingLACentralLA>{{Cite web|url=http://maps.latimes.com/neighborhoods/region/central-la/|title=Central L.A.|website=Mapping L.A.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maps.latimes.com/neighborhoods/|title=Mapping L.A. - Los Angeles Times|website=maps.latimes.com}}</ref><ref name=MappingLASanFernandoValley>{{Cite web|url=http://maps.latimes.com/neighborhoods/region/san-fernando-valley/|title=San Fernando Valley|website=Mapping L.A.}}</ref><ref name=ThomasGuideHollywoodHills>''The Thomas Guide,'' 2006, pages 563 and 593</ref>

Hollywood Hills is bisected southeast-northwest by [[U.S. Route 101 in California|US 101]]. The neighborhood is bounded on the northwest and north by the Los Angeles [[city limits|city line]], on the east by a [[fireroad]] through Griffith Park, continuing on [[Western Avenue (Los Angeles)|Western Avenue]], on the south by [[Franklin Avenue (Los Angeles)|Franklin Avenue]] and on the west by an irregular line that includes Outpost Drive.<ref name=ThomasGuideHollywoodHills/><ref name=MappingLAHollywoodHills/>

Bedrock of the Hills is a complex association of [[granitic]] and [[metamorphic]] rock plus interbedded [[sandstone]] and [[shale]] on which brown loamy soil, often shallow and with abundant stones, has developed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://casoilresource.lawr.ucdavis.edu/gmap/|title=SoilWeb: An Online Soil Survey Browser {{!}} California Soil Resource Lab|website=casoilresource.lawr.ucdavis.edu|access-date=2020-01-28}}</ref>

===Population===

21,588 people lived in the neighborhood's 7.05 square miles, according to the 2000 U.S. census—averaging 3,063 people per square mile, among the lowest [[population density|population densities]] in the city or the county. The population was estimated at 22,988 in 2008. The median age for residents was 37, considered old for the city and [[Los Angeles County|the county]]. The percentages of residents aged 19 through 64 were among the county's highest.<ref name=MappingLAHollywoodHills/>

The neighborhood has a [[diversity index]] of 0.433, and the percentage of [[Non-Hispanic Whites]] is 74.1%. [[Hispanic and Latino Americans|Latinos]] make up 9.4%, [[Asian American|Asians]] are at 6.7%, [[African American]] at 4.6% and others at 5.3%. In 2000, [[Mexico]] (7.9%) and the [[United Kingdom]] (7.8%) were the most common places of birth for the 22.8% of the residents who were born abroad, which was considered a low percentage of foreign-born when compared with the city or county as a whole.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://maps.latimes.com/neighborhoods/diversity/neighborhood/list/|title=Diversity Ranking - Mapping L.A. - Los Angeles Times|website=maps.latimes.com}}</ref>

The median household income in 2008 dollars was $69,277, considered high for the city but about average for the county. The percentage of households earning $125,000 or more was high, compared to the county at large. The average household size of 1.8 people was relatively low. Renters occupied 56.5% of the housing units, and homeowners the rest.<ref name=MappingLAHollywoodHills/>

In 2000, there were 270 families headed by single parents, or 6.9%, a rate that was low in both the county and the city.<ref name=MappingLAHollywoodHills/>

===Education===

In 2000, 54.8% of residents aged 25 and older held a four-year degree, considered high when compared with the city and the county as a whole.<ref name=MappingLAHollywoodHills/>

There are five secondary or elementary schools within the neighborhood's boundaries:<ref name="MappingLAHollywoodHills"/>

* Immaculate Heart High and Middle School, private, 5515 Franklin Avenue
* Valley View Elementary School, [[LAUSD]], 6921 Woodrow Wilson Drive
* The Neilson Academy, private, 2528 Canyon Drive
* Cheremoya Avenue Elementary School, LAUSD, 6017 Franklin Avenue
* The Oaks, private elementary, 6817 Franklin Avenue

The [[American Film Institute]] is at 2021 North [[Western Avenue (Los Angeles)|Western Avenue]]<ref name="MappingLAHollywoodHills"/>
