'''Dušan Vujović''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Душан Вујовић}}; born 22 July 1951) is a Serbian economist and politician. He served as the [[Ministry of Finance (Serbia)|Minister of Finance]] in the [[Government of Serbia]] from 2014 to 2018. He also had short terms as acting [[Ministry of Economy (Serbia)|Minister of Economy]] in 2014 and as acting [[Ministry of Defence (Serbia)|Minister of Defence]] in 2016.

==Education and career==
Vujović was born in [[Požarevac]] on 22 July 1951. He finished primary and secondary school in hometown and later graduated from the [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Economics]] in 1974. He received his Master’s degrees in 1977 and a PhD in 1984 at the Belgrade Faculty of Economics. He did his post-doctoral studies at the University of California, Berkeley.<ref name="bio29June">{{cite news|title=Biografije Ane Brnabić i ministara|url=http://rs.n1info.com/a279654/Vesti/Vesti/Biografija-Ane-Brnabic-i-ministara.html|accessdate=2 September 2017|work=n1info.com|date=29 June 2017|language=Serbian}}</ref>

He has worked as an assistant professor at the Institute of International Politics and Economics in Belgrade and as an assistant at the Belgrade Faculty of Economics, and then as an assistant professor and associate professor at this faculty.
He is a long-time associate of the World Bank. He led the program of advanced training of the staff of ministries of economy of countries in transition of eastern and southern Europe, the former Soviet Union, China and Vietnam. He worked as representative of Serbia and Montenegro in the World Bank’s Board of Governors, led a Bank’s program in Ukraine and was the Bank’s chief economist for Europe and Central Asia.

Since 2011, he has worked as a consultant of the World Bank and USAID in the field of innovation, budget reform and public sector reform.<ref name="bio29June"/>

He has published a number of papers in international journals and is also a professor for economics at the [[Singidunum University]] in Belgrade.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fefa.edu.rs/index.php?option=com_content&view=category&layout=blog&id=117&Itemid=373&lang=en |title=Archived copy |access-date=2015-06-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140514191108/http://www.fefa.edu.rs/index.php?option=com_content&view=category&layout=blog&id=117&Itemid=373&lang=en |archive-date=2014-05-14 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Political career==
[[File:Westbalkankonferenz Wien 2015 (20303009083).jpg|thumb|right|Vujović at the [[2015 Western Balkans Summit, Vienna|2015 Western Balkans Summit]] in Vienna, Austria.]]
On 27 April 2014, following the [[2014 Serbian parliamentary election|2014 parliamentary elections]], Vujović was elected as the [[Ministry of Finance (Serbia)|Minister of Finance of Serbia]] in the [[Government of Serbia]], on nomination by the leading government party [[Serbian Progressive Party]] (SNS).<ref name="bio29June"/> He also had short stints as acting [[Ministry of Economy (Serbia)|Minister of Economy]] in 2014 and as acting [[Ministry of Defence (Serbia)|Minister of Defence]] in 2016.

On 7 May 2018, he resigned from the position of Minister of Finance, due to personal reasons.<ref name="resignationMay18">{{cite news|title=Vujović podneo ostavku|url=https://www.b92.net/biz/vesti/srbija.php?yyyy=2018&mm=05&dd=07&nav_id=1389939|accessdate=7 May 2018|work=b92.net|agency=Tanjug|date=7 May 2018|language=Serbian}}</ref> He stated that during his tenure: "measurable results were achieved, among which are macroeconomic stabilization, fiscal consolidation and the definition of structural reforms necessary to ensure that these results are sustained".<ref name="resignationMay18" /> On 16 May, the [[National Assembly (Serbia)|National Assembly]] ratified his resignation and [[Ana Brnabić]], at the time [[Prime Minister of Serbia]]  temporarily took over his duties.<ref name="BrnabicHandover">{{cite news|title=Brnabićeva ministar finansija, do imenovanja novog|url=https://www.b92.net/biz/vesti/srbija.php?yyyy=2018&mm=05&dd=16&nav_id=1393433|accessdate=16 May 2018|work=b92.net|agency=Tanjug|date=16 May 2018|language=Serbian}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Vujović is married and has two children. He speaks English and Russian.<ref name="bio29June"/> He is one of the richest ministers in the Government of Serbia, having real estates mainly in [[Washington, D.C.]] and [[Belgrade]], which have an estimated worth of nearly [[USD|$]]3 million.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dušan Vujović|url=https://imovinapoliticara.krik.rs/display/profil.php?disp_id=18|website=KRIK.rs|accessdate=2 September 2017|language=Serbian}}</ref>
