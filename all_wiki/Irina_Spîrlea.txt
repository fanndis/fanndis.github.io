'''Irina Spîrlea''' (born 26 March 1974) is a retired tennis player from Romania who turned professional in 1990. She won four singles and six doubles titles during her career. Spîrlea reached her highest individual ranking on the [[WTA Tour]] on 13 October 1997, when she became number 7 in the world. She retired in 2000.

==Personal life==
Spîrlea married Massimiliano Pace, her former coach, in 2001, and has a son, Tommaso, born in 2002, as well as a younger daughter, Francesca.<ref>[http://www.adevarul.ro/articole/irina-spirlea-a-nascut-un-baietel/10508 Article in Romanian] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928022853/http://www.adevarul.ro/articole/irina-spirlea-a-nascut-un-baietel/10508 |date=28 September 2007 }}</ref><ref>[http://www.prosport.ro/alte-sporturi/tenis/sparlea-sfarleaza-gospodina-2581018 Article in Romanian]</ref>

==Career==
Irina Spîrlea is one of the more successful tennis players from Romania, being one of only three women to have reached the top 10 (the others being Virginia Ruzici and Simona Halep.)<ref>[http://www.clarosports.com/news_stories/wta-announces-bucharest-open-for-2014-11168207 Article in Romanian] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140512231839/http://www.clarosports.com/news_stories/wta-announces-bucharest-open-for-2014-11168207 |date=12 May 2014 }}</ref> Her best performance at a Grand Slam tournament was a semi-final at the US Open in 1997. She won the WTA's Newcomer of the Year award in 1994.<ref>[http://www.wtatennis.com/scontent/article/3010014/title/wta-awards]</ref>

In 1996 Spîrlea became the first player in the history of the [[Women's Tennis Association]] to receive a match default for conduct when she swore at an official in Italian during a match played in Palermo, Italy.<ref>[http://news.cincinnati.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20070718/SPT/707180354/-1/CINCI The Enquirer – This article is no longer available<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref>[http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/tennis/events/1997/usopen/profiles/profiles14.html CNN/SI – US Open Player Profiles<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

Spîrlea was involved in a bumping incident with [[Venus Williams]] during a changeover in the semifinals of the [[1997 US Open (tennis)|1997 US Open]]. Spîrlea collided with Williams near the net post while changing ends, and did not move sideways. Spîrlea went on to lose the match 6–7, 6–4, 6–7 in a third-set tiebreak, after holding two match points, at 6–4 and 6–5 in the tie breaker. [[Richard Williams (tennis coach)|Williams' father]] accused Spîrlea of racism, and later called her "an ugly white turkey".<ref name="sportsillustrated.cnn.com">[http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/tennis/news/1997/12/19/news.yetennis.html sportsillustrated.cnn.com]</ref> Spîrlea accused Williams of arrogance, saying in a press conference following the match, "I'm not going to move. She never tries to turn (...) She thinks she's the fucking Venus Williams."<ref>[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/main.jhtml?xml=/sport/1997/09/08/sthing08.xml telegraph.co.uk]</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wh-_DwAAQBAJ&pg=PA33&lpg=PA33&dq=%22she+thinks+she's+the+fucking+venus+williams%22#q=%22she%20thinks%20she's%20the%20fucking%20venus%20williams%22|title=Different Strokes: Serena, Venus, and the Unfinished Black Tennis Revolution|last=Harris|first=Cecil|date=2020-02-01|publisher=U of Nebraska Press|isbn=978-1-4962-1465-2|language=en}}</ref><ref name="sportsillustrated.cnn.com"/> Williams, however, shrugged off the confrontation saying "I thought we both weren't looking. I'm sorry she feels that way. It's not a big thing to me. No one said 'Excuse me'.<ref>[http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m1355/is_n19_v92/ai_19813584 Father of tennis star Venus Williams charges racism during recent U.S. Open – Richard Williams|Jet|Find Articles at BNET.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>
