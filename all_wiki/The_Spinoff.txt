'''''The Spinoff''''' is a [[New Zealand]] online commentary and opinion magazine started by Duncan Greive in 2014. The site began as a blog about TV shows that was sponsored by the streaming platform, Lightbox.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Grieve and the rise of The Spinoff|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/83588346/duncan-greive-and-the-rise-of-the-spinoff|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=stuff.co.nz}}</ref> It quickly broadened its scope and now covers a range of topics including [[New Zealand politics]], pop culture and social issues, often from a [[politically progressive]] or [[left-wing]] perspective.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Olds|first1=Jeremy|title=Duncan Greive and the rise of The Spinoff|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/83588346/duncan-greive-and-the-rise-of-the-spinoff|website=Stuff|accessdate=19 July 2017}}</ref>

==''The Spinoff TV''==

''The Spinoff TV'' is a TV show that covered current affairs, pop culture, and media and was created as a collaboration between The Spinoff and [[MediaWorks New Zealand|MediaWorks]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/media/12-12-2017/holy-heck-the-spinoff-tv-is-coming-to-three-in-2018/ |title=Holy heck: The Spinoff TV is coming to Three in 2018! |date=12 December 2017 |work=The Spinoff |access-date=10 July 2019}}</ref> It is hosted by Alex Casey and Leonie Hayden, and aired on [[Three (TV channel)|Three]]. It premiered on 22 June 2018,<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/media/06-06-2018/huge-and-true-the-spinoff-tv-is-coming-to-three-on-june-22/ |title=Huge and true: The Spinoff TV is coming to Three on June 22 |last=Spinoff |first=The |date=6 June 2018 |access-date=10 July 2019}}</ref> and aired its final episode on 5 October 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.threenow.co.nz/shows/the-spinoff-tv/S1668-531 |title=The Spinoff TV |website=www.threenow.co.nz |access-date=10 July 2019}}</ref> Its first season had 16 episodes; it was not renewed for a second season.

==Arts coverage==

In 2019, ''The Spinoff'' received funding from [[Creative New Zealand]] to commission articles on contemporary New Zealand art and artists.<ref>{{cite web |title=Arts Grants Round 1 2019 |url=https://www.creativenz.govt.nz/results-of-our-work/who-got-funded/funding-rounds/arts-grants-round-1-2019 |website=Creative New Zealand |accessdate=25 October 2019}}</ref> The art section is edited by New Zealand critics Mark Amery and Megan Dunn.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dunn |first1=Megan |title=Introducing The Spinoff Art |url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/art/03-07-2019/introducing-the-spinoff-art/ |website=The Spinoff |accessdate=25 October 2019 |date=3 July 2019}}</ref>
