"'''Perfect'''" is a song by English singer-songwriter [[Ed Sheeran]] from his third studio album, ''[[÷ (album)|÷]]'' (2017).<ref name="Independent">{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/divide-ed-sheeran-album-review-spotify-how-to-listen-tour-dates-a7608826.html|title=Ed Sheeran, Divide, album review: Singer-songwriter's third record is impressive for ambition alone|work=[[The Independent]]|last=O'Connor|first=Roisin|date=3 March 2017|accessdate=18 March 2017}}</ref> After the album's release, it charted at number four on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name="Telegraph">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/artists/divide-conquer-ed-sheeran-smashes-uk-chart-records-new-album/|title=Divide and conquer: Ed Sheeran smashes UK chart records with new album and dominates singles rankings|work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|date=10 March 2017|accessdate=18 March 2017}}</ref> On 21 August 2017, ''Billboard'' announced that "Perfect" would be the fourth single from the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7934255/despacito-hot-100-number-one-15-weeks|title='Despacito' on Hot 100: Second Song Ever to Lead for at Least 15 Weeks|work=Billboard|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=21 August 2017|accessdate=21 August 2017}}</ref> The song was serviced to [[Contemporary hit radio|pop radio]] on 26 September 2017 as the third single from the album in the United States (fourth overall).<ref name="usachr"/> The song eventually reached number one on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in December 2017. "Perfect" became the UK [[List of UK Singles Chart Christmas number ones|Christmas number-one]] song for 2017 and also peaked at number one in sixteen other countries, including [[Australia]], [[Canada]], [[Ireland ]], and [[New Zealand]].

The second version of the single, titled "Perfect Duet", with American singer [[Beyoncé]] was released on 1 December 2017.<ref name="OCC">{{cite news|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ed-sheerans-perfect-remix-features-beyonce__21134/|title=Ed Sheeran's Perfect remix features Beyonce|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|last=White|first=Jack|date=30 November 2017|accessdate=30 November 2017}}</ref> Another duet with Italian singer [[Andrea Bocelli]], titled "Perfect Symphony", was released on 15 December 2017.<ref name="remix5">{{cite web|title=Perfect Symphony (with Andrea Bocelli) – Single by Ed Sheeran|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/perfect-symphony-with-andrea-bocelli-single/1323176986|publisher=iTunes Store|date=15 December 2017|accessdate=15 December 2017}}</ref> The song and its official music video received three nominations at the [[2018 MTV Video Music Awards]].

==Composition and production==
"Perfect" was the first track Sheeran wrote for his third studio album ''[[÷ (album)|÷]]''.<ref name="axs">{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/ed-sheeran-talks-collaborating-with-his-brother-on-perfect-122702 |title=Ed Sheeran talks collaborating with his brother on 'Perfect'|last=Melton|first=Lori|work=AXS|date=5 September 2017|accessdate=20 November 2017}}</ref> The song was recorded in 2016 and first appeared online in early March 2017. The song is a romantic ballad written about his wife-to-be Cherry Seaborn, whom he knew from school and then reconnected with when she was working in New York.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-39113501|title=Ed Sheeran: 'I've got a song that's better than Thinking Out Loud'|work=BBC News|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=1 March 2017|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/ed-sheeran-how-i-won-over-my-wonderful-girlfriend-cherry-seaborn-w471580/ |title=Ed Sheeran: How I Won Over My ‘Wonderful’ Girlfriend |first= Ian |last=Drewe |date=14 March 2017|work=US Magazine |accessdate=20 November 2017}}</ref> Sheeran revealed that the inspiration for the lyrics came after visiting [[James Blunt]]'s house in [[Ibiza]], where the two singers had listened to the rapper [[Future (rapper)|Future]]'s music at six in the morning.<ref name="zane"/> He said: "Barefoot on the grass, listening to our favorite song, which happened to be Future's "[[March Madness (song)|March Madness]]"... I booked the studio for the day, and I had that and I was like, right, let me just flesh that out. And the song happened and was sort of finished that day. I knew it was special."<ref name="zane"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2989825/ed-sheeran-perfect-best-love-song/|title=Hear the Ballad Ed Sheeran Calls 'The Best Love Song' of His Career|work=MTV News|last=Roth|first=Madeline|date=3 March 2017|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref>

In an interview with [[Zane Lowe]], Sheeran said that he needed to write the best love song of his career with "Perfect"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.guitaa.com/chords/ed-sheeran-perfect-official-music-video|title=Ed Sheeran - Perfect (Official Music Video)|website=guitaa.com/|access-date=2020-06-06}}</ref>. He said: "I need to do it 100% me, so everyone will listen to that will be like, "Ah! He can actually do it!" ... I wanted to beat "[[Thinking Out Loud]]", 'cause I know that song was going to define me."<ref name="zane">{{cite AV media |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/post/idsa.38f32af0-ff6c-11e6-ba14-08153ff3e5e4 |title=Zane Lowe and Ed Sheeran, Pt 2 |date=3 February 2017|time=15:50–23:20 |first=Zane|last=Lowe|work=iTunes}}</ref>

Sheeran produced the song himself with help from [[Will Hicks (Record producer)|Will Hicks]]. It was recorded with strings orchestration from his brother Matthew Sheeran.<ref name="zane"/> This is the first time the brothers had collaborated on a song as it was the final wish of their grandmother to see the brothers working together before she died. The full string orchestration was later used for the recording with [[Andrea Bocelli]].<ref name=bocelli />

The song is written in the key of [[A-flat major|A{{flat}} major]] ([[G major]] in live performances) with a [[tempo]] of 63.5 beats per minute in [[Quadruple meter|{{music|time|12|8}} time (if not using triplets)]] or [[Quadruple meter|{{music|time|4|4}} time (if using triplets)]]. "Perfect" moves at a chord progression of A{{flat}}{{sup|5}}–Fm{{sup|7}}–D{{flat}}{{sup|sus2}}–E{{flat}}. The vocals span from E{{flat}}<sub>3</sub> to A{{flat}}<sub>4</sub> in the song.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sheeran|first1=Ed|title=Ed Sheeran "Perfect" Sheet Music in Ab Major (transposable) – Download & Print|url=https://www.musicnotes.com/sheetmusic/mtd.asp?ppn=MN0172836&intcmp=Recommended|website=Musicnotes.com|accessdate=8 December 2017}}</ref>

==Commercial performance==
In March 2017, Sheeran broke the record set by [[Frankie Laine]] in 1953, occupying all of the top five positions in the United Kingdom, and placing nine songs in the top ten of the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name="musiceek1">{{cite web|url=http://www.musicweek.com/analysis/read/official-charts-analysis-ed-sheeran-dominates-singles-chart-with-nine-of-top-10-songs/067791|title=Official Charts Analysis: Ed Sheeran dominates singles chart with nine of Top 10 songs|work=[[Music Week]]|author=Alan Jones|date=10 March 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> Also, every single one of the sixteen tracks from his new album ''[[÷ (album)|÷]]'' entered the top twenty. "Perfect" debuted at number four, selling 62,599 copies.<ref name="musiceek1"/> After Sheeran's performance of "Perfect" on ''[[The X Factor (UK series 14)|The X Factor]]'' on 26 November 2017, the song reached a new peak in the UK, advancing to number three and selling 32,507 units.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicweek.com/analysis/read/charts-analysis-camila-cabello-makes-it-five-weeks-at-the-top/070706|title=OCharts Analysis: Camila Cabello makes it five weeks at the top|work=Music Week|author=Alan Jones|date=1 December 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> The [[Beyoncé]] version, released on 1 December 2017, drove consumption of the track to 89,359 sales (including 45,460 from sales-equivalent streams) as it became Sheeran's second number-one song from ''÷'', following "[[Shape of You]]" – and his fourth number-one single in total.<ref name="musiceek2">{{cite web|url=https://www.musicweek.com/analysis/read/charts-analysis-ed-sheeran-s-perfect-result-on-singles-chart/070779|title=Charts Analysis: Ed Sheeran's Perfect result on singles chart|work=Music Week|author=Alan Jones|date=8 December 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> With UK sales of 1,048,313, "Perfect" became Sheeran's tenth million-selling single.<ref name="musiceek2"/> It was the [[List of UK Singles Chart Christmas number ones|Christmas number-one song]] for 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ed-sheeran-s-perfect-claims-the-uk-s-official-christmas-number-1-2017-its-an-actual-dream__21338/|title=Ed Sheeran claims Christmas Number 1: "It's an actual dream"|publisher=Official Charts Company|last=Copsey|first=Rob|date=22 December 2017|accessdate=23 December 2017}}</ref> and held the summit for six consecutive weeks before being displaced by [[Eminem]]'s "[[River (Eminem song)|River]]", which features Sheeran as a guest artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/eminem-claims-his-ninth-number-1-with-river-on-the-official-singles-chart__21514/|title=Eminem claims his ninth Number 1 with River on the Official Singles Chart|publisher=Official Charts Company|last=Copsey|first=Rob|date=19 January 2018|accessdate=19 January 2018}}</ref>  As of 2020, it is the 2nd longest runner on the UK Singles top 75, with 121 weeks spent on that chart, bettered only by "My Way" by [[Frank Sinatra]] (124 weeks).

In the United States, ten songs from ''÷'' debuted on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in March 2017, including "Perfect" at number thirty-seven.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7720931/hot-100-ed-sheeran-divide-clean-bandit-top-ten|title=Ed Sheeran Stays Atop Hot 100, Clean Bandit Bounds to Top 10|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Gary |last=Trust|date=13 March 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> In October, the song ascended to number eighteen and became the third song from ''÷'' to reach the country's top twenty.<ref name="billboard1">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8013874/gucci-mane-pink-ed-sheeran-camila-cabello-hot-100-chart-moves|title=Hot 100 Chart Moves: Gucci Mane, P!nk, Ed Sheeran & Camila Cabello Hit the Top 20|work=Billboard|first=Gary|last=Trust|date=24 October 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> It also lifted 4–3 on [[Digital Songs]] (50,000 downloads sold), 34–30 on [[Hot 100 Airplay (Radio Songs)|Radio Songs]] (45 million) and 46–35 on [[Streaming Songs]] (11.5 million).<ref name="billboard1"/> On 30 October 2017, "Perfect" soared to the top ten on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name="billboard2">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8015110/post-malone-rockstar-21-savage-hot-100-camila-cabello-havana-ed-sheeran-perfect|title=Post Malone & 21 Savage Lead Hot 100, Camila Cabello & Ed Sheeran Hit Top 10|work=Billboard|first=Gary|last=Trust|date=30 October 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> The ballad gained in all metrics, holding at number three on Digital Song Sales (53,000, up 7 percent) and climbing 30-22 on Radio Songs (53 million, up 16 percent) and 35–27 on Streaming Songs (13.1 million, up 14 percent).<ref name="billboard2"/> On 20 November 2017, "Perfect" pushed to a new ''Billboard'' Hot 100 high (8–7) and reached the Radio Songs top ten (17–10; 73 million, up 16 percent).<ref name="billboard3">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8039635/hot-100-post-malone-rockstar-camila-cabello-havana-imagine-dragons-thunder|title=Post Malone's 'Rockstar' Tops Hot 100, Camila Cabello's 'Havana' Is Top Seller & Imagine Dragons' 'Thunder' Rules Radio|work=Billboard|author=Gary Trust|date=20 November 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> Sheeran scored his seventh Radio Songs top ten and the third from his album ''÷'', following "Shape of You" and "[[Castle on the Hill]]".<ref name="billboard3"/> The next week, "Perfect" hit top five on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, becoming the second-highest-charting hit from his album ''÷'', following "Shape of You".<ref name="billboard4">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/8047011/post-malones-rockstar-rules-billboard-hot-100-ed-sheerans-perfect-hits-top-five|title=Post Malone's 'Rockstar' Rules Billboard Hot 100, Ed Sheeran's 'Perfect' Hits Top Five|work=Billboard|author=Gary Trust|date=27 November 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> "Perfect" became Sheeran's third top five hit overall; his first, "[[Thinking Out Loud]]", rose to number two in 2015.<ref name="billboard4"/> "Perfect" remained at number three on Digital Song Sales (60,000, down 6 percent) and powered 10–6 on Radio Songs (80 million, up 10 percent) and 38–15 on Streaming Songs (18.9 million, up 61 percent).<ref name="billboard4"/>

On 4 December 2017, "Perfect" pushed to a new number three ''Billboard'' Hot 100 high, and became Sheeran's third number one on Digital Song Sales (69,000, down 1 percent).<ref name="billboard5">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8055045/hot-100-post-malone-rockstar-ed-sheeran-g-eazy|title=Post Malone's 'Rockstar' Leads Billboard Hot 100 for Eighth Week, G-Eazy's 'No Limit' Lifts to Top 10|work=Billboard|author=Gary Trust|date=4 December 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> He previously topped the chart for a week in 2015 with "Thinking Out Loud" and for ten weeks earlier in 2017 with "Shape of You".<ref name="billboard5"/> "Perfect" reached number one on Digital Song Sales, aided by the first few hours of tracking for its duet version, released before the end of the sales (and streaming) tracking week at 7 p.m. ET on 30 November.<ref name="billboard5"/> For the full week, the Beyoncé version accounted for 18 percent of the song's sales.<ref name="billboard5"/> On 11 December 2017, after a full tracking week, the single topped the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart, becoming Sheeran's second number-one song in the United States and Beyoncé's sixth as a solo act.<ref name="Hot 100 peak">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8063597/ed-sheeran-beyonce-perfect-billboard-hot-100-number-one|title=Ed Sheeran & Beyonce's 'Perfect' Tops Billboard Hot 100|magazine=Billboard|author=Gary Trust|date=11 December 2017|accessdate=11 December 2017}}</ref> "Perfect" remained at number-one on the Digital Songs chart with sales of 181,000 copies (up 202 percent).<ref name="Hot 100 peak"/> The duet version accounted for 63 percent of the song's total sales for the week.<ref name="Hot 100 peak"/> "Perfect" also rose 11–3 on the Streaming Songs chart with 34.9 million US streams, up 87 percent, while on Radio Songs, it pushed 4–3 (102 million in audience, up 14 percent).<ref name="Hot 100 peak"/> The duet topped the Hot 100 for five weeks. The original version of the song would later take over at the top on the week ending 20 January 2018. After six total weeks at number one, the song was dethroned by "Havana" by Camila Cabello featuring Young Thug.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8095345/camila-cabello-havana-young-thug-hot-100-number-1|title=Camila Cabello Earns First Billboard Hot 100 No. 1 With 'Havana,' Featuring Young Thug|last=Trust|first=Gary|magazine=Billboard|date=22 January 2018|accessdate=23 January 2018}}</ref> "Perfect" was the seventh best-selling song of 2017 in the US, with 1,340,000 copies sold that year.<ref name="us sales">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8085932/ed-sheeran-divide-nielsen-music-top-album-2017|title=Ed Sheeran's 'Divide' Is Nielsen Music's Top Album of 2017 in U.S.|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|magazine=Billboard|date=3 January 2018|accessdate=4 January 2018}}</ref> It was the best-selling song of 2018, with 1,300,000 copies sold.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8492663/nielsen-music-top-album-2018-drake-scorpion |title=Drake's 'Scorpion' Is Nielsen Music's Top Album Of 2018 in U.S., 'God's Plan' Most-Streamed Song|date=8 January 2019 |first= Keith |last=Caulfield|work=Billboard}}</ref>

The original version peaked at number one in Austria, Belgium, France, Luxembourg, Malaysia, Netherlands, Philippines, Poland, Scotland, Slovakia, Slovenia, and Switzerland. After the Beyoncé duet release, "Perfect" also reached number one in Australia, Denmark, Germany, Ireland, Italy, New Zealand, Sweden, the United Kingdom, and the United States.<ref name="NZ"/><ref name="Sweden">{{cite web|url=http://www.sverigetopplistan.se/|title=Sverigetopplistan – Sveriges Officiella Topplista|publisher=[[Sverigetopplistan]]|accessdate=9 December 2017}}</ref> The song is Sheeran's fourth to reach number one in Australia,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ariacharts.com.au/news/2017/ed-sheeran-s-perfect-hits-1|title=Ed Sheeran's Perfect Hits #1|publisher=Australian Recording Industry Association|date=9 December 2017|accessdate=20 January 2018}}</ref> and held the country's top spot for eight consecutive weeks before "[[God's Plan (song)|God's Plan]]" by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] displaced it.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/australian-singles-drake-gods-plan-is-the-first-new-no-1-of-2018-20180203|title=Australian Singles : Drake 'God's Plan' Is The First New No 1 of 2018|first=Gavin|last=Ryan|publisher=Noise11.com|date=3 February 2018|accessdate=10 February 2018}}</ref> It went to number one as well in Canada, becoming Sheeran's second number one there and Beyoncé's first.<ref name="SheeranCanada"/><ref name="BeyonceCanada"/> As of 3 January 2019, the song has sold 207,000 digital copies in Canada.<ref>{{cite web |title=Total Album Equivalent Audio Consumption in Canada Increases 21% in 2018 |url=https://www.nielsen.com/ca/en/insights/news/2019/total-album-equivalent-audio-consumption-in-canada-increases-21-percent-in-2018.html |publisher=[[Nielsen Media Research]] |accessdate=16 April 2019 |date=8 January 2019}}</ref>

==Music video==
[[File:Hintertux - panoramio (1).jpg|thumb|The video for "Perfect" narrates a romantic story that happened in the [[Hintertux|Hintertux Mountains]], Austria]]
On 22 September 2017, a lyric video for "Perfect" was released on Sheeran's [[YouTube]] channel.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/watch-ed-sheerans-lyric-video-for-new-single-perfect-2143767|title=Watch Ed Sheeran's lyric video for new single 'Perfect'|website=NME|accessdate=25 September 2017}}</ref> The music video for "Perfect" was released on Sheeran's YouTube channel on 9 November 2017. The video stars [[Zoey Deutch]] and was directed by Jason Koenig, who also directed the video for "[[Shape of You]]". The video was filmed at the Austrian ski resort of [[Hintertux]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8031042/ed-sheeran-music-videos-divide-destinations|title=Around The World With Ed Sheeran: 5 of His 'Divide' Destination Music Videos|work=Billboard|accessdate=23 November 2017}}</ref> and shows Sheeran and Deutch going on a ski trip with friends, with the two dancing in the snow and ending up in a cabin together.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/zoey-deutch-ed-sheeran-music-video|title=Zoey Deutch Stars in Ed Sheeran's New Music Video, "Perfect"|last=Logan|first=Elizabeth|work=W Magazine|date=9 November 2017|accessdate=14 November 2017}}</ref> Ross McNeilage who writes for [[MTV UK]] called the video a "Christmas dream" for its wintery visual. While praising the simple story and the video's cinematography, McNeilage noted that Zoey Deutch acts opposite to the superstar as his love interest and we watch as they "coyly flirt until they realise their love for one another and slow dance in the snow together."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/ed-sheeran/news/ed-sheeran-releases-heartbreakingly-sweet-perfect-video#wttdFBqhRLwbOHKC.99|title=Ed Sheeran Releases Heartbreakingly Sweet 'Perfect' Video|publisher=[[MTV UK]]|date=9 November 2017|access-date=6 January 2017}}</ref> As of August 2020, the music video has received over 2.5 billion views on YouTube,<ref name="2Vv-BfVoq4g">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Vv-BfVoq4g|title=Ed Sheeran – Perfect (Official Music Video)|date=9 November 2017|accessdate=26 April 2018}}</ref> making it the 31st most viewed video on the site.<ref>{{cite web|title=Top 1000 Most Viewed YouTube Videos of All Time|url=https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL11E57E1166929B60 |publisher=YouTube|accessdate=28 February 2020}}</ref> It received nominations for Song of the Year, [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Pop Video|Best Pop Video]] and [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Direction|Best Direction]] at the [[2018 MTV Video Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/cardi-b-beyonce-jay-z-lead-2018-mtv-vma-video-music-awards-nominations-1202874001/|title=Cardi B, Beyonce, Jay-Z Lead 2018 MTV VMA Nominations|last=Staff|first=Variety|date=2018-07-16|work=Variety|access-date=2018-07-16|language=en-US}}</ref>

On 1 December 2017, a duet by American singer [[Beyoncé]] titled "Perfect Duet" was made available worldwide.<ref name="OCC"/><ref name="remix4"/><ref name="Xyr">{{cite web|url=https://tidal.com/track/81825679|title=Perfect Duet (with Beyoncé) / Ed Sheeran TIDAL|website=Tidal|access-date=10 December 2018}}</ref>

The song is a stripped down, acoustic version of the original, with Beyoncé singing the second verse from a female perspective. Sheeran stated the song was Beyoncé's favorite from the album, and reached out to her to re-record the song. Beyoncé agreed, and the two recorded the song in May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8054748/ed-sheeran-beyonce-perfect-duet-stream|title=Ed Sheeran & Beyonce Team for 'Perfect' Duet|work=Billboard|last=Stutz|first=Colin|date=30 November 2017|accessdate=12 December 2017}}</ref>

===Live performances===
Beyoncé performed her verse as part of the setlist during the [[On the Run II Tour]] in 2018. On 2 December 2018, almost exactly a year since the release of the duet version, Ed Sheeran and Beyoncé performed the first-ever live rendition of "Perfect Duet" at the [[Global Citizen Festival#2018|2018 Global Citizen Festival]] in Johannesburg, South Africa.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8487720/beyonce-jay-z-ed-sheeran-chris-martin-nelson-mandela-global-citizen-festival-johannesburg|title=Beyonce, JAY-Z, Ed Sheeran, Chris Martin & More Pay Homage to Nelson Mandela at Global Citizen Festival|website=Billboard|access-date=2018-12-31}}</ref>

===Composition===
A fully orchestral version of the song was recorded at [[Abbey Road Studios]] although only parts of it were used in the original release of the song.<ref name="axs"/><ref name="zane"/> The full orchestral version was used in the duet with [[Andrea Bocelli]].<ref name=bocelli>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8070692/ed-sheerans-perfect-symphony-andrea-bocelli |title=Ed Sheeran Delivers 'Perfect Symphony' Featuring Andrea Bocelli|work=Billboard|last=Brandle|first=Lars|date=15 December 2017}}</ref>
