'''Carla Suárez Navarro''' ({{IPA-es|ˈkaɾla ˈswaɾeθ naˈβaro}}; born 3 September 1988 in [[Las Palmas de Gran Canaria]]) is a Spanish former professional [[tennis]] player. A former top 10 singles player, she reached her career-high singles ranking of world No. 6 on 29 February 2016, her career-high doubles ranking of world No. 11 on 27 April 2015, and won two singles and three doubles titles on the WTA tour.

She first came to prominence by reaching the quarterfinals of the [[2008 French Open – Women's Singles|2008 French Open]] as a qualifier, in what was her first appearance in the main draw of a Grand Slam tournament. That year she also began representing [[Spain Fed Cup team|Spain]] in the [[2008 Fed Cup]]. She reached another six Grand Slam quarterfinals in her career (at the [[2009 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2009 Australian Open]], defeating [[Venus Williams]] en route, the [[2013 US Open – Women's Singles|2013 US Open]], where she lost to eventual champion [[Serena Williams]], the [[2014 French Open – Women's Singles|2014 French Open]], where she lost in three sets to [[Eugenie Bouchard]], the [[2016 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2016 Australian Open]], where she lost to [[Agnieszka Radwańska]], the [[2018 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2018 Australian Open]], where she was defeated by eventual champion [[Caroline Wozniacki]], and the [[2018 US Open – Women's Singles|2018 US Open]], where she lost to [[Madison Keys]].)

Suárez Navarro was born in the [[Canary Islands]] to handball player José Luís Suárez and former gymnast Lali Navarro. She started playing tennis when she was nine and moved to [[Barcelona]] in 2007 in order to train at the Pro-Ab Team Tennis Academy. She admires [[Steffi Graf]], [[Lleyton Hewitt]], [[Justine Henin]] and [[Michael Jordan]].

In September 2020, she was diagnosed with [[Hodgkin Lymphoma]] just months after announcing her retirement from tennis.<ref>[https://www.cnn.com/2020/09/01/tennis/carla-surez-navarro-cancer-diagnosis-tennis-spt-intl/index.html Carla Suárez Navarro 'willing to face whatever comes' after cancer diagnosis]</ref>

==Career==
===2008–2009===
[[File:Csn wim08 1.JPG|thumb|left|200px|Suárez playing at the 2008 Wimbledon Championships]]
At the [[2008 French Open|French Open]], Suárez Navarro reached the quarterfinals of her first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] main draw tournament. After she won three qualifying matches, she defeated former world No. 1 [[Amélie Mauresmo]] of France in the second round 6–3, 6–4, Australian [[Casey Dellacqua]] in the third round, and 26th-seeded Italian [[Flavia Pennetta]] in the fourth round 6–3, 6–2. Suárez Navarro then lost to third-seeded [[Jelena Janković]] in the quarterfinals 3–6, 2–6. At [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]], she lost to second-seeded Janković in the second round 1–6, 3–6.

Suárez Navarro reached the second [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] quarterfinal of her career at the [[2009 Australian Open]] before being defeated by [[Elena Dementieva]] 6–2, 6–2. She upset [[Venus Williams]] in the second round 2–6, 6–3, 7–5. This was her first win over a top-10 player.

In March, Suárez Navarro reached her first final on the [[WTA Tour]] at the [[Andalucia Tennis Experience]], a [[clay court]] event in [[Marbella, Spain]]. She lost to Jelena Janković in the final 3–6, 6–3, 3–6.

At [[French Open|Roland Garros]] seeded 22, she defeated [[Edina Gallovits]] 6–1, 6–4 and [[Lucie Hradecká]] 6–2, 6–4, before losing to [[Victoria Azarenka]] in the third round 7–5, 5–7, 2–6.

At [[2009 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Suárez Navarro defeated No. 25 [[Kaia Kanepi]] in the first round and [[Ekaterina Makarova]] in the second. She faced defending champion Venus Williams for the first time since the 2009 Australian Open but was unable to repeat the upset win, losing 0–6, 4–6.

===2010===
Suárez Navarro had a good start to 2010, making the second round of the [[2010 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]] in Auckland before losing to top seed Flavia Pennetta in straight sets. She then made the quarterfinal of the [[2010 Moorilla Hobart International|Hobart International]] before losing to No. 2 seed [[Shahar Pe'er]] 6–4, 6–7, 5–7.

At the [[2010 Australian Open|Australian Open]], Suárez Navarro made it to the third round before losing to top seed, the defending (and eventual) champion [[Serena Williams]], 0–6, 3–6.

She was upset in the first round of the [[2010 Copa BBVA-Colsanitas|Copa BBVA-Colsanitas]] by world No. 198 [[Kristina Antoniychuk]] 1–6, 4–6, then made it to the semifinals of the [[2010 Abierto Mexicano Telcel|Abierto Mexicano Telcel]] in Acapulco before losing to [[Polona Hercog]] 3–6, 5–7.

Unseeded at the [[2010 BNP Paribas Open|BNP Paribas Open]] at Indian Wells, California, Suárez Navarro reached the fourth round, losing to [[Alisa Kleybanova]] 6–2, 6–7, 4–6. Along the way, she gained one of the biggest wins of her career by defeating world No. 3 and top seed [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]], 6–4, 4–6, 6–1 in the second round.

She reached the final of the Andalucia Tennis Experience in Marbella for the second year in a row, losing to Flavia Pennetta 2–6, 6–4, 3–6.

Suarez Navarro suffered an ankle injury at the tournament in Fes, Morocco. She returned to play in the [[2010 French Open|French Open]], losing her first-round match to [[Olga Govortsova]] 6–7, 1–6, she remained sidelined until the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|US Open]].

===2011===
Carla began the [[2011 WTA Tour|season]] at the [[2011 ASB Classic|ASB Classic]] in Auckland. As the ninth seed, she was upset in round two by [[Heather Watson]]. She also lost early in [[2011 Moorilla Hobart International|Hobart]] to [[Alberta Brianti]].

Going into the Australian Open, she defeated American [[Christina McHale]] by 4–6, 6–3, 6–4; but lost to eventual titlist [[Kim Clijsters]] of Belgium in the following round. Next she represented Spain at the [[Fed Cup]] against [[Estonia Fed Cup team|Estonia]] in [[Tallinn]], where she had a 1–1 record; getting past [[Anett Kontaveit]] but falling to [[Kaia Kanepi]].

Then, Suárez followed an early loss in [[2011 Open GDF Suez|Paris]] with a semifinal and a quarterfinal, in [[2011 Copa Sony Ericsson Colsanitas|Bogotá]] and [[2011 Abierto Mexicano Telcel|Acapulco]], respectively. An elbow injury caused her to stop playing for two and a half months and withdraw from Roland Garros.

She returned to the competition at the [[2011 Open GDF Suez de Marseille|Open GDF Suez de Marseille]], where she beat [[Sun Shengnan]] and [[Aleksandra Wozniak]] to make the quarterfinals, but was beaten by home crowd favourite [[Pauline Parmentier]].

She failed to qualify for Wimbledon, losing to [[Ekaterina Ivanova (tennis)|Ekaterina Ivanova]] in round two.

In early September, Suárez reached round four of the US Open for the first time, defeating [[Mathilde Johansson]], [[Simona Halep]] and compatriot [[Silvia Soler Espinosa]]. However, her run was ended by [[Andrea Petkovic]].

===2012===
Carla Suárez Navarro was impressive in the [[2012 French Open|French Open]], reaching the third round. In the first round, Suarez Navarro comfortably beat [[Tamarine Tanasugarn]], 6–0, 6–2. In the second round she faced [[Sesil Karatantcheva]] of [[Kazakhstan]], and beat her 4–6, 6–4, 6–1. In the third round she lost to [[Yaroslava Shvedova]], also from Kazakhstan, 6–4, 7–5, who had just returned from an injury.

At [[2012 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] Carla Suárez Navarro only made it to the first round, but it was against fifth seed [[Samantha Stosur]] of Australia. She was well beaten, losing 1–6, 3–6.

At Italiacom Open, she reached the quarterfinals, losing to Laura Robson 4–6, 6–2, 3–6.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/18838886|title=Laura Robson into semi-finals after win at Italiacom Open|accessdate=2012-07-14}}</ref>

In the [[2012 Summer Olympics|London Olympics]], she beat [[Samantha Stosur]] in a thrilling first-round match, 3–6, 7–5, 10–8,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.london2012.com/tennis/event/women-singles/index.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-07-31 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120731234343/http://www.london2012.com/tennis/event/women-singles/index.html |archive-date=2012-07-31 }}</ref> avenging her first round defeat to the Australian at Wimbledon the previous month.

===2015: Top 10 ranking===
At the [[2015 Australian Open|Australian Open]] she lost in the first round to [[Carina Witthöft]] in straight sets. At the [[2015 BNP Paribas Open|BNP Paribas Open]] she reached the quarterfinals before losing to [[Simona Halep]] in three sets. Her good form on the U.S. hardcourts continued with a run to her first [[WTA Premier tournaments|Premier Mandatory]] final at the [[2015 Miami Open|Miami Open]]. She defeated Agnieszka Radwańska, Venus Williams and Andrea Petkovic before losing, 2–6, 0–6, in the final to Serena Williams. Despite this defeat she entered the top ten of the world rankings for the first time in her career. Another good run at the [[2015 Mutua Madrid Open|Mutua Madrid Open]] resulted in a quarterfinal defeat to Williams, again in straight sets. In Rome at the [[2015 Internazionali BNL d'Italia|Internazionali BNL d'Italia]] she reached her first final at [[WTA Premier tournaments|Premier 5]] level. She recorded three victories over top ten players at the same event for the first time, with victories over [[Eugenie Bouchard]], [[Petra Kvitová]] and [[Simona Halep]] before losing to [[Maria Sharapova]] in three sets.

At the [[2015 French Open|French Open]] she was seeded eighth. She lost to [[Jelena Ostapenko]] in the first round of Wimbledon that year, and her results for the rest of the year were inauspicious apart from reaching the quarterfinals in [[2015 Kremlin Cup|Moscow]] in October and a crushing 6–0, 6–0 victory over an injured Andrea Petkovic at [[2015 WTA Elite Trophy|Zhuhai]] in November. Carla ended the year ranked 13th.

===2016: First Premier 5 title===
Suárez Navarro made a good start to the year. She reached the semifinals at [[2016 Brisbane International|Brisbane]], losing to [[Angelique Kerber]], and the quarterfinals of the [[2016 Australian Open|Australian Open]], losing to Agnieszka Radwańska. As a result, she moved back up to world No. 8 on February 1. She then defeated Jelena Janković in Spain's Fed Cup tie against Serbia.

At the [[Dubai Tennis Championships]], she received a first round bye but fell to eventual semifinalist [[Caroline Garcia]]. Her next tournament was the [[WTA Qatar Open|Qatar Open]] where she also received a first-round bye and defeated [[Donna Vekić]], [[Timea Bacsinszky]], [[Elena Vesnina]] and Agnieszka Radwańska en route to the final, ensuring a new career-high ranking of world No. 6. She then avenged her first round loss to Jelena Ostapenko in the previous year's Wimbledon with a three set win over Ostapenko, earning her first Premier title and her biggest title to date.

At the [[2016 Summer Olympics]], she reached the third round, beating [[Ana Ivanovic]] and [[Ana Konjuh]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rio.itftennis.com/media/otedrawsheets/1100288096.pdf|title=2016 Olympic Tennis|date=13 August 2016|website=www.rio.itftennis.com|access-date=7 September 2017}}</ref>

==Playing style==
Suárez Navarro uses a single-handed backhand, unlike most female players of her era.<ref>{{cite news|title=One-handed backhand now a rarity in post-Henin era|url=https://www.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/2008-06-23-tennis-backhands_N.htm|work=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=5 June 2010|date=June 24, 2008}}</ref> She has said in interviews that her favorite shot is her cross-court backhand and that her favourite surfaces are [[clay court|clay]] and [[Tennis court#Hard courts|hard]]. [[Frew McMillan]] has said, "There's  something of [[Justine Henin]] about her game. She has a great variety of shots."<ref>Speaking on British Eurosport after Suárez Navarro's defeat of Garrigues in the fourth round of the 2009 Australian Open</ref>

==Apparel and equipment==
Suárez Navarro wears [[Lotto Sport Italia|Lotto]] clothing and uses the Six.One racquet made by [[Wilson Sporting Goods|Wilson]].
