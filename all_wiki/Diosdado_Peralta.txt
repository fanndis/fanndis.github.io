'''Diosdado "Dado" Madarang Peralta''' (born March 27, 1952) is a [[Philippines|Filipino]] attorney and jurist who serves as the [[Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines|Chief Justice of the Philippines]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Gavilan|first=Jodesz|date=October 24, 2019|title=FAST FACTS: Who is new Supreme Court Chief Justice Diosdado Peralta?|work=Rappler|url=https://www.rappler.com/newsbreak/iq/243235-things-to-know-supreme-court-chief-justice-diosdado-peralta|access-date=June 15, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Santos|first=Elmor|date=October 23, 2019|title=Diosdado Peralta is new Chief Justice|work=[[CNN Philippines]]|url=https://cnnphilippines.com/news/2019/10/23/chief-justice-diosdado-peralta-.html|access-date=June 15, 2020}}</ref> He was appointed by [[President of the Philippines|President]] [[Rodrigo Duterte]] on October 23, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/1179001/diosdado-peralta-is-new-chief-justice|title=Diosdado Peralta is new Chief Justice|last=Corrales|first=Nestor|date=October 23, 2019|website=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer|Inquirer.net]]|language=en|access-date=2019-10-23}}</ref> He previously served as [[Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines|Associate Justice]] of the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines|Supreme Court]] from January 13, 2009 to October 22, 2019.<ref name=gman>{{cite news|title=Sandiganbayan justice is new SC magistrate |url=http://www.gmanews.tv/story/144165/Sandiganbayan-justice-is-new-SC-magistrate |publisher=GMANews.TV |date=2009-01-14 |accessdate=2009-01-14 }}</ref> He is the third [[Sandiganbayan]] Presiding Justice to be appointed to the High Tribunal.<ref name=Peralta>http://sc.judiciary.gov.ph/aboutsc/justices/j-peralta.php</ref>

== Personal life and education ==
[[File:Peraltajf.JPG|thumb|left|150px|[[Chambers (law)|Chambers]] of  Diosdado M. Peralta (new [[Supreme Court of the Philippines|Supreme Court]] building).]]

Justice Peralta finished his Bachelor of Science degree at the [[Colegio de San Juan de Letran]] in 1974 before pursuing law at the [[University of Santo Tomas Faculty of Civil Law]], where he graduated in 1979.<ref name=Peralta /> He is married to Fernanda Lampas-Peralta, an Associate Justice of the [[Philippine Court of Appeals]].<ref name="gman" /><ref>{{Cite news|last=Torres-Tupas|first=Tetch|date=October 23, 2019|title=Who is newly appointed Chief Justice Diosdado Peralta?|work=Inquirer|url=https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/1179059/who-is-newly-appointed-chief-justice-diosdado-peralta|access-date=June 15, 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Legal career ===
He worked in the private sector before joining government service as an assistant city prosecutor in [[Laoag City]] and [[Manila]].<ref name="gman" /> 

=== Jurist ===
On September 22, 1994, he was appointed Judge of the Regional Trial Court, Branch 95, Quezon City, which was designated as Special Criminal Court on Heinous Crimes and, later, Drugs Cases,<ref name="Peralta" /> then named to the Sandiganbayan in 2002 by President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo.<ref name="gman" /> He was designated Presiding Justice of the Sandiganbayan in March 2008,<ref>{{cite news|title=Peralta new Sandiganbayan presiding justice |url=http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/story/86748/news/nation/update-peralta-new-sandiganbayan-presiding-justice |publisher=GMANews.TV |date=2008-03-29 |accessdate=2009-03-29 }}</ref> and served in that capacity until his appointment to the Supreme Court. Justice Peralta is the Chairperson of the High Court's Committee on the Revision of the Benchbook on the Application, Computation, and Graduation of Penalties.  He presently sits as a Member of the House of Representative Electoral Tribunal (HET).<ref name="Peralta" /> Justice Peralta was the Chairman of the 2014 [[Philippine Bar Examination]] committee.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite news|date=2014-04-29|title=Bar exams set on October|publisher=Manila Bulletin Online|url=http://devsite.mb.com.ph/bar-exams-set-on-october/|url-status=dead|accessdate=2009-01-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150128132918/http://devsite.mb.com.ph/bar-exams-set-on-october/|archive-date=2015-01-28}}</ref>

== Awards ==
He was the recipient of several commendations, two of which were the Special Centennial Awards in the Field of [[Criminal Law]], given by the [[Integrated Bar of the Philippines]] and the Supreme Court during its Centennial Celebration on June 6, 2001, and the Judicial Excellence Awards 2002 (Chief Justice Ramon Avanceña Award for Outstanding Regional Trial Court Judge).<ref name="Peralta" /> In recognition of his vast contribution in the field of law, he was also the recipient of the Outstanding Thomasian Alumni Awards for Law (TOTAL Awardee in Law/Justice) on August 2, 2008, which was the highest award bestowed by the [[University of Santo Tomas]] to an alumnus.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sandigan Justice leads TOTAL|url=http://www.varsitarian.net/news/sandigan_justice_leads_total |publisher=The Varsitarian |date=2008-09-07 |accessdate=2008-09-07 }}</ref>
