'''Barbara Dale Underwood''' (born August 16, 1944) is an American lawyer currently serving as the [[Solicitor General]] of [[New York (state)|New York]]. She was first appointed to the position in January 2007 by [[Andrew Cuomo]], who was then serving as the state's [[Attorney General]]. Underwood was reappointed in 2011 by Attorney General [[Eric Schneiderman]].

Underwood herself served briefly as the [[Attorney General of New York]] upon Schneiderman's resignation from the position on May 8, 2018. She declined to seek the office in the [[2018 New York state elections]], and resumed the position of Solicitor General after [[Letitia James]] was sworn in to succeed her on January 1, 2019. Underwood was the first woman to serve as Attorney General of New York.

==Early life==
Underwood was born in [[Evansville, Indiana]], to Dr. Robert Underwood and Mildred Kinstein Underwood. She grew up in [[Belleville, New Jersey]], and is the eldest of three daughters.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1998/04/09/nyregion/public-lives-smart-lawyer-for-a-rich-querulous-uncle.html|title=Public Lives; Smart Lawyer for a Rich, Querulous Uncle|last=Hoffman|first=Jan|date=April 9, 1998|newspaper=The New York Times|access-date=February 9, 2017|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Underwood earned an [[Bachelor of arts|A.B.]]  ''[[magna cum laude]]'' from [[Radcliffe College]] of [[Harvard University]] in 1966 and received her [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from [[Georgetown University Law Center]] in 1969.

While Underwood was a student at Radcliffe in 1963, she worked at WJRZ as news editor and got engaged to Howard Allen Cohen, a second year student at Rutgers Law School, who was also working at WJRZ.<ref name="FirstMarriage">{{cite news |title=Barbara Underwood Is Planning Nuptials |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1964/01/26/archives/barbara-underwood-is-planning-nuptials.html |accessdate=June 14, 2018 |newspaper=New York Times |date=January 16, 1964}}</ref>

==Legal career==
After graduation, Underwood clerked for Chief Judge [[David L. Bazelon]] of the [[United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit|U.S. Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit]] and then [[Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States|Associate Justice]] [[Thurgood Marshall]] of the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] from 1971 to 1972.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ag.ny.gov/senior-staff|title=Senior Staff {{!}} www.ag.ny.gov|website=ag.ny.gov|language=en|access-date=February 9, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170211075033/https://ag.ny.gov/senior-staff|archive-date=February 11, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> After leaving the Court, Underwood was a [[law professor]] for 10 years at the [[Yale Law School]], from 1972 to 1982. She also has taught at [[Brooklyn Law School]] and at [[New York University School of Law]].

Underwood left academia to work in the [[Kings County District Attorney]]'s Office.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kurtz|first1=Howard|title=The Private Public Prosecutor|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1987/10/27/the-private-public-prosecutor/543dcb63-ce18-417e-99b6-acb154b6b921/|accessdate=September 28, 2017|work=Washington Post|date=October 27, 1987}}</ref> She later served in the District Attorneys for three different counties in [[New York City]]:  she was an Assistant District Attorney in [[New York County District Attorney|New York County]], Chief of Appeals and Counsel to the District Attorney in [[Kings County, New York|Kings County]] (Brooklyn), and Senior Executive Assistant District Attorney for Legal Affairs in [[Queens County, New York|Queens County]]. She then served as Chief [[Assistant United States Attorney]] and later Counsel to the [[United States Attorney for the Eastern District of New York]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/attorney-general-elect-eric-schneiderman-appointments-blog-entry-1.1682292|title=Here's Attorney General-Elect Eric Schneiderman's First Round Of Appointments|newspaper=NY Daily News|date=December 22, 2010|access-date=February 9, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Mustain|first1=Gene|title=Court Opens Spy Heaing|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/archives/news/court-opens-spy-hearing-article-1.716904|accessdate=May 10, 2017|work=New York Daily News|date=June 9, 1996}}</ref>

In 1998, Underwood was appointed by [[United States Attorney General]] [[Janet Reno]] to be Principal Deputy Solicitor General under [[United States Solicitor General|Solicitor General]] [[Seth P. Waxman]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite press release |url = http://www.usdoj.gov/opa/pr/1998/March/111.htm.html | title = Underwood Appointed Principal Deputy Solicitor General | publisher = [[United States Department of Justice]] | date= March 11, 1998 | accessdate = May 8, 2018 }}</ref> Underwood held that position for three years, and then served as [[Solicitor General of the United States|Acting Solicitor General]] from January to June 2001, making her the first woman to act as Solicitor General.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2009/1/5/obama-names-law-school-dean-solicitor/|title=Obama Names Law School Dean Solicitor General|last1=Groll|first1=Elias J.|last2=Jiang|first2=Athena Y.|date=January 5, 2009|website=www.thecrimson.com|language=en|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=February 9, 2017}}</ref> After working in the U.S. Attorney's Office in New York for several years, in 2007 she was appointed Solicitor General of the State of New York, a position she held under two state Attorneys General.<ref>{{cite news|title=Attorney General Andrew Cuomo appoints top legal team to Martin Tankleff case|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/crime/attorney-general-andrew-cuomo-appoints-top-legal-team-martin-tankleff-case-article-1.343638|accessdate=May 9, 2017|work=New York Daily News|agency=Associated Press|date=January 18, 2008}}</ref> She has argued twenty cases before the U.S. Supreme Court, and many cases before the federal and state appellate courts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oyez.org/advocates/barbara_d_underwood|title=Cases argued by Barbara Underwood|publisher=Oyez!|accessdate=April 4, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://nypost.com/2008/03/06/a-flight-of-fancy/|title=A Flight of Fancy|last1=Fermino|first1=Jennifer|date=March 6, 2008|work=New York Post|accessdate=September 28, 2017}}</ref>

She became Acting Attorney General on May 8, 2018, following the resignation of Eric Schneiderman.<ref>{{cite news | accessdate = May 8, 2018 | url = https://www.newsday.com/news/region-state/attorney-general-schneiderman-assault-resign-1.18447687 | work = Newsday | date= May 8, 2018 | title= Acting AG named as Dems mull replacement | first= Yancey | last= Roy }}</ref> The State Legislature picked Underwood as the replacement to fill the remainder of Schneiderman's term on May 22, 2018.<ref>Colby Hamilton. "[https://www.law.com/newyorklawjournal/2018/05/22/underwood-set-to-be-chosen-as-interim-attorney-general/?slreturn=20180422162933 Lawmakers Select Underwood as Interim Attorney General]." ''[[New York Law Journal]]''. May 22, 2018.</ref>  Underwood pledged not to seek elected office and fill out the remainder of Schneiderman's term until an elected successor is sworn in.<ref>Karen Dewitt "[http://wxxinews.org/post/barbara-underwood-named-interim-nys-attorney-general Barbara Underwood named interim NYS Attorney General]" [[WXXI (AM)|WXXI News]] May 22, 2018</ref> As Attorney General, Underwood successfully sued [[Donald Trump]] and his foundation, [[Donald J. Trump Foundation]], for "a pattern of persistent illegal conduct."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/06/14/new-york-attorney-general-sues-trump-and-family-over-charity.html|title=New York attorney general sues Trump and family over charity, claiming 'illegal conduct'|last=Mangan|first=Dan|date=June 14, 2018|work=CNBC|access-date=June 14, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vox.com/2018/6/14/17464010/trump-foundation-new-york-lawsuit|title=Read: New York attorney general's new lawsuit against Trump Foundation, President Trump, and his children|last=Prokop|first=Andrew|date=June 14, 2018|work=Vox|access-date=June 14, 2018}}</ref>

The Attorney General's office passed to [[Letitia James]] following her victory in the [[2018 New York state elections]].<ref name="APjan2019">{{cite news |publisher=The Associated Press |title=New York's 1st female attorney general closes surprise tenure as leading Trump antagonist |date=January 1, 2019 |accessdate=January 1, 2019 |url=https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/politics-news/new-york-s-1st-female-attorney-general-closes-surprise-tenure-n953536}}</ref> Underwood, who had never sought or held elective office, professed contentment when she returned to the Solicitor General's office in January 2019.<ref name="APjan2019"/> "I like that role, and so I'm happy to go back to doing it," she told an [[Associated Press|AP]] interviewer at the time. "I have come to like this too. It's not that I'm eager to leave this, but I'm very happy to be going on to something that I know I like."<ref name="APjan2019"/> Underwood left the office having made history, as the first woman to serve as Attorney General of New York.<ref>{{cite news|last1=McKinley|first1=Jesse|title=Barbara Underwood approved as state attorney general; first woman to hold post|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/05/22/nyregion/barbara-underwood-approved-as-state-attorney-general-first-woman-to-hold-post.html|accessdate=May 24, 2018|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=May 22, 2018}}</ref>

On November 30, 2020, as Solicitor General of New York, Underwood argued the case [[Trump v. New York]] on behalf of New York State.<ref>https://www.c-span.org/video/?477428-1/trump-v-york-oral-argument&live</ref>

==Family==
Underwood has lived in [[New York City]] since 1982. She is married to Martin Halpern, former [[Brandeis University]] professor of playwriting and dramatic literature.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite news|title=Barbara Underwood Marries a Playwright|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1985/08/05/style/barbara-underwood-marries-a-playwright.html?mcubz=1|accessdate=September 28, 2017|work=New York Times|date=August 5, 1985}}</ref> They live in [[Brooklyn]] and have one son, Robert, born in 1986.<ref name=":0" /> Underwood had one previous marriage which ended in divorce.<ref name=":0" />
Underwood is Jewish.<ref>{{cite tweet |last=Underwood   |first=Barbara  |author-link= |user=newyorkstateag |number=897580156734361600 |date=15 August 2017 |title=I am Jewish. There are New Yorkers still alive today who fought against Nazis, or survived the concentration camps. |language=en |retweet= |link=https://twitter.com/newyorkstateag/status/897580156734361600 |access-date=18 December 2018 }}</ref>
