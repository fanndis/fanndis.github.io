'''St. Luke's Medical Center''' ('''SLMC''') is a [[private company|private]] [[non-profit company|non-profit]]<ref>{{cite web|last1=Calonge|first1=Emilia|title=Medical and Health Archives|url=http://ncca.gov.ph/subcommissions/subcommission-on-cultural-heritagesch/archives/medical-and-health-archives/|publisher=National Commission for Culture and the Arts|accessdate=3 December 2016|date=16 April 2014}}</ref> health care institution based in [[Quezon City]], [[Metro Manila]], [[Philippines]] which operates two hospitals of the same name in Quezon City and Taguig.<ref>http://www.temos-worldwide.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=blogcategory&id=2&Itemid=44&limit=5&limitstart=10</ref><ref>http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/story/344939/cbb/st-luke-s-medical-center-among-40-best-hospitals-in-the-world-think-tank-says</ref><ref>http://www.diplomatic-council.org/download/Teilnahmeprogramme/DC-Temos-Conference-Program.pdf</ref> 

Founded by the American missionary Charles Henry Brent under the [[Protestant]] [[Episcopal Church (United States)|Episcopal Church in the United States]] (Protestant Episcopal Church in the United States of America) in 1903, it is the third [[United States|American]] and [[Protestant]] founded hospital in the Philippines after [[Iloilo Mission Hospital|CPU–Iloilo Mission Hospital]] and [[Silliman University Medical Center]].

The St. Luke's Medical Center in Quezon City serves as the university hospital of the [[Trinity University of Asia]], the first Protestant Episcopalian university in Asia.

==History==
[[File:St. Luke's Hospital, Manila (1923).jpg|thumb|St. Luke's Hospital in [[Manila]] in 1923]]
St. Luke’s was established in 1903 by [[American Colonial Period (Philippines)|American]] [[Episcopal Church of the United States of America|Episcopalian]] missionaries as a charity ward and [[dispensary]] hospital. St. Luke's started out as fully free outpatient clinic for the poor in Calle Magdalena, Tondo. It also supports and conducts medical, dental and surgical missions in rural areas. It was transformed into an independent, non-sectarian, non-stock, non-profit corporation in the 1970s.

==Facilities==
===Hospitals===
[[File:St Lukes Medical Center BGC.JPG|thumb|St. Luke's Medical Center - Global City]]
{{main|St. Luke's Medical Center - Quezon City|St. Luke's Medical Center - Global City}}
St. Luke's Medical Center Inc. maintains two hospitals, one in Quezon City and another at the [[Bonifacio Global City]] in [[Taguig]]. SLMC is based in the Quezon City Hospital while the Taguig hospital is a wholly owned corporation of the Quezon City-based SLMC.<ref>{{cite news|title=Frederick Dy is new chair of St. Luke’s|url=https://business.inquirer.net/15071/frederick-dy-is-new-chair-of-st-luke%E2%80%99s|accessdate=3 December 2016|publisher=Philippine Daily Inquirer|date=26 August 2011}}</ref> The institution also plans to establish a third hospital in [[Davao City]], the construction of which is set to begin mid-2019.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Galang |first1=Vincent Mariel |title=Construction on St. Luke’s Davao to start mid-2019 |url=https://www.bworldonline.com/construction-on-st-lukes-davao-to-start-mid-2019/ |accessdate=13 March 2019 |work=Business World |date=12 March 2019}}</ref>

===Extension clinic===
There is also an extension clinic located in [[Ermita, Manila]]. The extension clinic caters primarily to those patients seeking medical examination requirements for Visa application for US, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand.

==International affiliates==
On 1 February 2005, St. Luke's signed an Affiliation Agreement with [[Memorial-Sloan Kettering Cancer Center]] in [[New York City]]. SLMC also have affiliations with New York- Presbyterian Hospital, Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons and Weill Cornell Medical College of Cornell University
