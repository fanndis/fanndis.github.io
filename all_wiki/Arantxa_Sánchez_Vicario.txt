{{family name hatnote|Sánchez|Vicario|lang=Spanish}}
'''Aránzazu Isabel María''' "'''Arantxa'''" '''Sánchez Vicario'''<ref>Vicario is not her married name. It is her mother's maiden name. In the [[Spanish naming customs|Spanish naming system]], every person has two surnames: the first one comes from the father, the second from the mother. A woman never changes surnames, regardless of whether she marries or divorces.</ref> ({{IPA-es|aˈɾanθaθu isaˈβel maˈɾi.a aˈɾantʃa ˈsantʃeθ βiˈkaɾjo}};{{efn|In isolation, ''Vicario'' is pronounced {{IPA-es|biˈkaɾjo|}}.}} born 18 December 1971) is a Spanish former world No. 1 retired [[tennis]] player. She won 14 [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] titles: four in singles, six in women's doubles, and four in mixed doubles. She also won four Olympic medals. In 1994, she was crowned the ITF World Champion for the year.

==Career==
Arantxa Sánchez Vicario started playing tennis at the age of four, when she followed her older brothers [[Emilio Sánchez]] and [[Javier Sánchez (tennis)|Javier Sánchez]] (both of whom became professional players) to the court and hit balls against the wall with her first racquet. As a 17-year-old, she became the youngest winner of the women's singles title at the 1989 French Open, defeating World No. 1 [[Steffi Graf]] in the final. ([[Monica Seles]] broke the record the following year when she won the title at age 16.)

Sánchez Vicario quickly developed a reputation on the tour for her tenacity and refusal to concede a point. Commentator [[Bud Collins]] described her as "unceasing in determined pursuit of tennis balls, none seeming too distant to be retrieved in some manner and returned again and again to demoralize opponents" and nicknamed her the "Barcelona Bumblebee".<ref>{{cite book | first=Bud | last=Collins | author2=Xander Hollander | year=1996 | title=Bud Collins' Tennis Encyclopedia | pages=[https://archive.org/details/budcollinstennis0000unse/page/434 434] | publisher=Visible Ink Press | isbn=1-57859-000-0 | url=https://archive.org/details/budcollinstennis0000unse/page/434 }}</ref>

She won six women's doubles Grand Slam titles, including the US Open in 1993 (with [[Helena Suková]]) and [[Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] in 1995 (with [[Jana Novotná]]). She also won four Grand Slam mixed doubles titles. In 1991, she helped Spain win its first-ever [[Fed Cup]] title, and helped Spain win the Fed Cup in 1993, 1994, 1995, and 1998. Sanchez Vicario holds the records for the most matches won by a player in Fed Cup competition (72) and for most ties played (58). She was ITF world champion in 1994 in singles.<ref>[http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/2/players/playerprofiles/PlayerBio2.asp?PlayerID=190008 Aranxta Sanchez Vicario WTA Bio Page]</ref>

Sánchez Vicario was also a member of the Spanish teams that won the [[Hopman Cup]] in 1990 and 2002.

Over the course of her career, Sánchez Vicario won 29 singles titles and 69 doubles titles before retiring in November 2002.<ref>{{cite news |title=TENNIS; Sánchez-Vicario Ends Her Career|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/13/sports/plus-tennis-sanchez-vicario-ends-her-career.html|accessdate=12 May 2009|date=13 November 2002 |work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref> She came out of retirement in 2004 to play doubles in a few select tournaments as well as the [[2004 Summer Olympics]], where she became the only tennis player to play in five [[Olympics]] in the [[Olympics Games|Games]] history.<ref>{{cite news|title=Notebook: Rower makes history|url=https://www.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/2004-07-01-notebook-rowing_x.htm|accessdate=12 May 2009 |date=1 July 2004 |work=[[USA Today]]}}</ref> Sanchez Vicario is the most decorated Olympian in Spanish history with four medals – two silver and two bronze.<ref>{{cite web|title=Arantxa sanchez Vicario |url=http://www.tennisfame.com/famer.aspx?pgID=867&hof_id=244 |accessdate=12 May 2009 |publisher=[[International Tennis Hall of Fame]] |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927183742/http://www.tennisfame.com/famer.aspx?pgID=867&hof_id=244 |archivedate=27 September 2007 }}</ref>

In 2005, ''TENNIS ''Magazine ranked her in 27th place in its list of [[Tennis (magazine)#"The 40 Greatest Players of the Tennis Era" (2005)|40 Greatest Players of the ''TENNIS'' era]] and in 2007, she was inducted into the [[International Tennis Hall of Fame]]. She was only the third Spanish player (and the first Spanish woman) to be inducted.

In 2009, Sánchez Vicario was present at the opening ceremony of [[Madrid]]'s [[Caja Mágica]], the new venue for the [[Madrid Open (tennis)|Madrid Masters]]. The second show court is named Court Arantxa Sánchez Vicario in her honour.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/09/sports/tennis/09tennis.html|title=Tennis Stadium Opens (and Closes) in Madrid | work=The New York Times | first=Christopher | last=Clarey | date=9 May 2009 | accessdate=25 April 2010}}</ref>

In 2015 Sanchez Vicario went into professional coaching. She got involved in training Danish player [[Caroline Wozniacki]].

==Personal life==
She has been married twice: her first marriage to the sports writer Juan Vehils in July 2000 ended in 2001. She then married businessman Josep Santacana in September 2008, with whom she has a daughter named Arantxa (born February 2009) and son named named Leo (born October 2011).<ref>[http://www.tennis.com/backcourt/general/backcourt.aspx?id=146044 Wedding Bells: Sanchez-Vicario Gets Married!] Tennis.com, 21 September 2008</ref><ref>[http://www.zimbio.com/Arantxa+Sanchez-vicario/articles/5/Arantxa+Sanchez+Vicario+gave+birth+baby+girl Arantxa Sanchez Vicario gave birth to a baby girl] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090927152346/http://www.zimbio.com/Arantxa+Sanchez-vicario/articles/5/Arantxa+Sanchez+Vicario+gave+birth+baby+girl |date=27 September 2009 }} Zimbio.com, 3 March 2009</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/gente/arantxa-sanchez-vicario-alumbra-segundo-hijo-1203900|title=Arantxa Sánchez Vicario alumbra a su segundo hijo|work=El Periódico de Catalunya|date=3 November 2011|accessdate=8 November 2011}}</ref> In 2019, Sánchez Vicario and Santacana divorced.<ref name="Divorce">{{cite news | last1=Guerra |first1=Andres | title=Arantxa Sánchez Vicario ya está divorciada | url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/gente/20190321/461155672423/arantxa-sanchez-vicario-josep-santacana-divorcio-sentencia.html |accessdate=18 July 2020 | work=La Vanguardia | date=21 March 2019}}</ref>

In 2012, Sánchez Vicario published an autobiography in which she claimed that, despite having earned $60 million over the course of her career, her parents had exerted almost total control over her finances and lost all of her money.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sanchez Vicario says $60M in career earnings gone|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/story/2012-02-08/arantxa-sanchez-vicario-blames-parents-for-lost-money/53007004/1|work='USA Today]|accessdate=3 July 2015|date=8 February 2012}}</ref> The same year, Sánchez Vicario sued her father and older brother [[Javier Sanchez (tennis)|Javier]] for the alleged mishandling of her career earnings. The court case continued over three years, and in 2015 concluded in a private settlement.<ref name="Sue">{{cite news | last1=Lynch |first1=Donal | title=Game, set and... cash troubles: how Wimbledon stars like Becker, Borg and Sanchez courted controversies | url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/life/weekend/game-set-and-cash-troubles-how-wimbledon-stars-like-becker-borg-and-sanchez-courted-controversies-37055915.html |accessdate=18 July 2020 | work=Belfast Telegraph| date=30 June 2018}}</ref>

She has faced multiple court proceedings relating to charges of tax evasion and fraud. In 2009, Sánchez Vicario was found guilty of tax evasion and ordered to repay €3.5 million.<ref name="Evasion">{{cite news | last1=Galaz |first1=Mabel | title=Naming and shaming the celebrity tax dodgers | url=https://english.elpais.com/elpais/2014/05/06/inenglish/1399378385_063821.html | accessdate=18 July 2020 | work=La Vanguardia | date=6 May 2014}}</ref> In 2015, Banque de Luxembourg successfully filed complaint against her for credit and property fraud amounting to $5.2 million, however they were unable to recoup it. In 2018, Sánchez Vicario was once again charged with fraud, for deliberately misleading the courts on her financial set-up during the previous case.<ref name="Fraud">{{cite news | last1=Adicott |first1=Adam | title=Former No.1 Arantxa Sanchez Vicario Facing Financial And Personal Upheaval | url=https://www.ubitennis.net/2018/02/former-no-1-arantxa-sanchez-vicario-facing-financial-personal-upheaval/ |accessdate=18 July 2020 | work=La Vanguardia | date=25 February 2002}}</ref>

As well as tennis-playing siblings Javier and Emilio, Sanchez Vicario also has an older sister - Marisa - who briefly played professional tennis, peaking at world no. 368 in 1990.<ref name="Sister">{{cite news | title=ATP Marisa Sanchez Vicario Bio | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/players/190010/marisa-s-nchez-vicario |accessdate=18 July 2020 | work=WTA Website}}</ref><ref name="Older Sister">{{cite news | last1=Taules |first1=Silvia | title=Marisa Sánchez Vicario, la silenciosa hermana del clan: viuda, empresaria, tenista | url=https://www.vanitatis.elconfidencial.com/famosos/2019-03-23/marisa-sanchez-vicario-silenciosa-hermana-clan-viuda-empresaria-tenista_1889038/|accessdate=18 July 2020 | work=El Confidencial | date=23 March 2019}}</ref>
