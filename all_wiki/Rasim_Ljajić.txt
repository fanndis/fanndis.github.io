'''Rasim Ljajić''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Расим Љајић}}; {{IPA-sh|rǎːsim ʎǎːjitɕ|sr}}; born 28 January 1964) is a Serbian politician of [[Bosniaks of Serbia|Bosniak]] ethnicity. He has served as the [[Deputy Prime Minister of Serbia]] and the [[Ministry of Trade, Tourism and Telecommunications (Serbia)|Minister of Trade, Tourism and Telecommunications]] since 27 July 2012. He is the president of the [[Social Democratic Party of Serbia]], [[2007 Serbian parliamentary election|elected]] from 21 January 2007. Ljajić was also the president of the National Council for Cooperation with the [[ICTY|Hague Tribunal]].

==Education==
Ljajić graduated from the [[University of Sarajevo]] School of Medicine.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}}

==Political career==
In 1990, Ljajić was elected Secretary General of the [[Party of Democratic Action of Sandžak]] as one of its founders, a branch of the SDA in the [[Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina]], aimed at gathering Bosniaks in Serbia.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Bugajski |first1=Janusz |title=Political Parties of Eastern Europe: A Guide to Politics in the Post-communist Era: A Guide to Politics in the Post-communist Era |date=2020 |publisher=Routledge |isbn=978-1-00016-135-9 |page=471 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LRb8DwAAQBAJ&pg=PT471}}</ref> In 1993 he left the party and with dissidents formed the [[Sandžak Democratic Party]], criticizing [[Sulejman Ugljanin]] for being an extremist and endorsing separatism from Yugoslavia in an effort to join an enlarged Bosnia dominated by Bosnian Muslims.

One of the [[Democratic Opposition of Serbia]] leaders, he became Minister of Human and Minority Rights in 2000 after the fall of [[Slobodan Milošević]], and his mandate as a minister was extended in the rump DS-led 2001 government.<ref>{{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/*/http://www.humanrights.gov.yu/english/ministarstvo/ministar.htm|title=Rasim Ljajić at the Ministry of Human and Minority Rights website|accessdate=17 May 2018}}</ref>

Ljajić is the long-term Head of the Coordination Team with the [[International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia|Hague Tribunal]]. In the [[2003 Serbian parliamentary election|2003 parliamentary election]] he unsuccessfully led a "Together for Tolerance" coalition, along with [[Nenad Čanak]] of the [[League of Social Democrats of Vojvodina]] and [[Jožef Kasa]] of the [[Alliance of Vojvodina Hungarians]]. The coalition received 4.2% of the vote and did not pass the 5% threshold.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Muller |first1=Tom |title=Political Handbook of the World 2012 |date=2012 |publisher=SAGE |isbn=978-1-60871-995-2 |page=1258 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=d33MfEFXvW8C&pg=PA1258}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Ljajić is an [[Bosniaks in Serbia|ethnic Bosniak]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.b92.net/eng/news/politics.php?yyyy=2011&mm=06&dd=17&nav_id=74971|title=Muslim Academy "part of election campaign" - English - on B92.net|publisher=}}</ref> His relative and close friend Dževad Ljajić died in the military helicopter crash in Serbia on the night of 14 March 2015, which also claimed six other lives.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telegraf.rs/vesti/1477773-ministar-ljajic-poginuo-mi-je-rodjak-brinucu-o-njegovoj-porodici-dok-sam-ziv|title=MINISTAR LJAJIĆ: Poginuo mi je rođak, brinuću o njegovoj porodici dok sam živ!|website=www.telegraf.rs}}</ref>
