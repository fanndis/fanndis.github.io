'''Stephen Samuel Roach''' (born September 16, 1945) is an American economist and serves as senior fellow at Yale University’s [[Jackson Institute for Global Affairs]] and a senior lecturer at [[Yale School of Management]].  He was formerly chairman of Morgan Stanley Asia and chief economist at [[Morgan Stanley]], the New York-based investment bank.

==Career==
Roach holds a [[Ph.D.]] in economics from [[New York University]]<ref name=SOM/> and a bachelor's degree in economics from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]].

After earning his Ph.D., Roach was a research fellow at the [[Brookings Institution]] in Washington, D.C. Roach served on the research staff of the [[Federal Reserve Board]] in Washington, D.C.<ref name=SOM/> from 1972 until 1979, supervising the preparation of the official Federal Reserve projections of the U.S. economy.  From 1979 until joining Morgan Stanley, Roach was Vice President for Economic Analysis for the [[Morgan Guaranty Trust Company]] in New York.{{Citation needed|date=March 2013}}

Roach was with Morgan Stanley for 30-plus years and was the investment bank's chief [[economist]]<ref name=SOM>[http://mba.yale.edu/faculty/profiles/roach.shtml Stephen Roach], Yale School of Management web bio. Retrieved 2013-03-27.</ref> since 1991, serving as head of the firm's global team of economists in New York, London, Tokyo, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Paris{{Citation needed|date=August 2010}}.

He also writes monthly columns for international media organization [[Project Syndicate]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.project-syndicate.org/columnist/stephen-s--roach|title=Stephen S. Roach - Project Syndicate|website=Project Syndicate|language=en|access-date=2017-10-19}}</ref>
