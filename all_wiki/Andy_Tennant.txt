'''Andrew Wellman Tennant''' (born June 15, 1955) is an American screenwriter, film and television director, actor, and dancer.

==Early life==
Tennant was born June 15, 1955 in [[Chicago, Illinois]] and was raised in [[Flossmoor, Illinois]], a suburb of Chicago. His father was Don Tennant, a legendary creative advertising talent with Leo Burnett Agency in Chicago. As a boy, he spent his summers on Old Mission Peninsula in northern Michigan and at Camp Minocqua in northern Wisconsin.  He graduated from Homewood-Flossmoor High School in 1973.  He studied theater under John Houseman at [[University of Southern California]].

== Career ==

=== Dancing/acting and directorial debut ===
In 1978 he was cast as an extra of a dancing role in the musical adaption film ''[[Grease (film)|Grease]]'' starring actor [[John Travolta]] and singer/actress [[Olivia Newton-John]]. That same year he was cast as an extra in a dancing role in the musical comedy film adaption of ''[[Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (film)|Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Heart's Club Band]]''. His next role as an actor was in the 1979 period comedy film ''[[1941 (film)|1941]]'' as Babyface directed by [[Steven Spielberg]] featuring the ensemble cast of [[Dan Aykroyd]], [[Ned Beatty]], [[John Belushi]], [[John Candy]], [[Christopher Lee|Chrisopher Lee]], [[Toshiro Mifune]] and [[Robert Stack]]. He was cast in the comedy film ''[[Midnight Madness (1980 film)|Midnight Madness]]'' as Melio - Blue Team in 1980. He was cast as Boy Greaser in the sequel musical romantic-comedy ''[[Grease 2]]'' starring actor [[Maxwell Caulfield]] and actress [[Michelle Pfeiffer]] in 1982. Tennant quit acting and began writing and producing with his first project being the [[NBC]] made-for-television movie ''[[Moving Target (1988 American film)|Moving Target]]'' in 1988 starring actor/comedian [[Jason Bateman]], singer/actress [[Chynna Phillips]] and actor [[Jack Wagner (actor)|Jack Wagner]]. He branched out to directing, making his directorial debut in 1989 for the [[ABC Studios|ABC]] coming-of-age comedy-drama series ''[[The Wonder Years]]'' directing the season 3 episode "Math Class" starring actor [[Fred Savage]]. He worked as a director and producer for two episodes of the NBC adaption of the 1980s [[John Hughes (filmmaker)|John Hughes]] film ''[[Ferris Bueller (TV series)|Ferris Bueller]]'' starring actor [[Charlie Schlatter]] in 1990. He directed four episodes from season 1 of the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] sitcom ''[[Parker Lewis Can't Lose]]'' starring actor [[Corin Nemec|Corin Nemac]] (1990). He directed the [[TNT (U.S. TV network)|TNT]] made-for-television drama movie ''[[Keep the Change (film)|Keep the Change]]'' starring actor [[William Petersen]] in 1992. He directed the unaired pilot episode of the Fox comedy series ''[[Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventures (1992 TV series)|Bill and Ted's Excellent Adventures]]'' a spin-off of the 1980s film series originally starring actor [[Keanu Reeves]] in 1992. He directed the made-for-television movie ''[[Desperate Choices: To Save My Child]]'' starring actress [[Joanna Kerns]] and actor [[Bruce Davison]] in 1992. He directed an episode of the Fox sitcom ''[[Great Scott! (TV series)|Great Scott!]]'' starring actor [[Tobey Maguire]] in 1992. He directed the made-for-television movie ''What She Doesn't Know'' in 1992. He directed the ABC made-for-television drama movie ''[[The Amy Fisher Story]]'' starring actress [[Drew Barrymore]] in 1993. He directed two episodes of the Fox weird western series ''[[The Adventures of Brisco County, Jr.]]'' starring actor [[Bruce Campbell]] in 1993. He directed an episode of the CBS crime/drama series ''[[South of Sunset]]'' starring actor [[Glenn Frey]] in 1993. He directed an episode of the ABC series ''[[The Byrds of Paradise]]'' starring actor [[Timothy Busfield]] in 1994. He directed an episode of the Fox science fiction/fantasy series ''[[Sliders]]'' starring actor [[Jerry O'Connell]] in 1995.

=== Feature film director ===
He made his feature film directorial debut in 1995 with the comedy/family film ''[[It Takes Two (1995 film)|It Takes Two]]'' starring actress/comedienne [[Kirstie Alley]], actor/comedian [[Steve Guttenberg]] and actresses [[Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen]]. He directed the romantic comedy film ''[[Fools Rush In (1997 film)|Fools Rush In]]'' starring actor/comedian [[Matthew Perry]] and actress [[Salma Hayek|Selma Hayek]] in (1997). He directed and wrote the romantic/drama film [[Ever After|''Ever After: A Cinderella Story'']] re-teaming with Drew Barrymore in (1998). He directed the biographical drama film ''[[Anna and the King]]'' loosely based on the 1944 novel Anna and the King of Siam, which give a fictionalized account of the diaries of [[Anna Leonowens]] starring Oscar-winning actress [[Jodie Foster]] and actor [[Chow Yun-fat]] in (1999). He directed the romantic/comedy film ''[[Sweet Home Alabama (film)|Sweet Home Alabama]]'' starring actress [[Reese Witherspoon]] in (2002). He returned to television to directed as well as co-produce an episode of the Fox drama series ''[[The American Embassy]]'' starring actress [[Arija Bareikis]] in (2002). He returned to direct the romantic comedy film ''[[Hitch (film)|Hitch]]'' starring [[Will Smith]] and [[Eva Mendes]] in (2005). He directed the made-for-television Fox movie ''[[The Wedding Album (TV series)|The Wedding Album]]'' starring actor [[Bruno Campos]] and actress [[Tara Summers]] in (2006). He directed the romantic comedy/adventure film ''[[Fool's Gold (2008 film)|Fool's Gold]]'' starring actor [[Matthew McConaughey]] and actress [[Kate Hudson]] in (2008). He directed the made-for-television movie ''Operating Instructions'' in (2009). He directed the action/romantic-comedy film ''[[The Bounty Hunter (2010 film)|The Bounty Hunter]]'' starring actor [[Gerard Butler]] and actress [[Jennifer Aniston]] in (2010). He directed the made-for-television movie ''Thunderballs'' in (2011). He served as an executive producer for the ESPN sports newsmagazine series ''[[E:60]]'' earning three Emmy nominations and one win from (2011-2016). He directed an episode of the [[Amazon Video]] web series ''[[Betas]]'' starring actor [[Joe Dinicol]] in (2013). He directed the comedy film ''[[Wild Oats (film)|Wild Oats]]'' starring actresses [[Shirley MacLaine]] and [[Jessica Lange]] in (2016). He directed two episodes of the up-coming [[Netflix]] web series ''[[The Kominsky Method]]'' starring actor [[Michael Douglas]] in (2018).

== Personal life ==
Tennant married Fine Art and Cultural Photographer, Sharon Kay Johnson February 13, 1993.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1993/02/14/style/weddings-sharon-johnson-andrew-tennant.html|title=WEDDINGS; Sharon Johnson, Andrew Tennant|access-date=2018-04-18|language=en}}</ref> The couple have four children, three of whom are triplets. They currently reside in Los Angeles, California.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0855035/bio|title=Andy Tennant|website=IMDb|access-date=2018-04-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.robertbermangallery.com/artists/sharon-johnson-tennant|title=Sharon Johnson-Tennant - Artists - Robert Berman Gallery|website=www.robertbermangallery.com|language=en|access-date=2018-04-18}}</ref>
