[[Nicola Sturgeon]] formed the '''second Sturgeon government''' following her [[Scottish National Party]]'s victory in the [[2016 Scottish Parliament election]]. Sturgeon was nominated by a vote of the [[5th Scottish Parliament]] for appointment to the post of [[First Minister of Scotland|First Minister]] on 17 May 2016.<ref name="BBC17MAY">{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-36300892 | title=Nicola Sturgeon wins Scottish first minister vote | work=BBC News |publisher=BBC | date=17 May 2016 | accessdate=17 May 2016}}</ref> She was subsequently appointed by [[Queen Elizabeth II]] on 18 May, and announced formation of a new [[Scottish National Party]] [[minority government]].<ref name="BBC18M2">{{cite news | url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-36323276 | title=Scottish cabinet reshuffle: John Swinney becomes education secretary | work=BBC News | publisher=BBC| date=18 May 2016 | accessdate=18 May 2016}}</ref>

==History==
In the [[2016 Scottish Parliament election|May 2016 Scottish Parliament election]], the Scottish National Party (SNP) won 63 of the 129 seats contested. Incumbent First Minister [[Nicola Sturgeon]] soon afterwards announced her intention to form a minority government. She was nominated for the post of [[First Minister of Scotland|First Minister]] by a vote of the [[Scottish Parliament]] on 17 May, defeating [[Scottish Liberal Democrats|Scottish Liberal Democrat]] leader, Willie Rennie by 63 votes to 5, with 59 abstentions.<ref name="BBC17MAY"/> Ahead of the formation of the new government, long-serving ministers [[Alex Neil (politician)|Alex Neil]] and [[Richard Lochhead]] announced their resignations from the cabinet.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-36316534 |title=Alex Neil and Richard Lochhead step down from Holyrood cabinet |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=18 May 2016 |accessdate=18 May 2016}}</ref> Sturgeon recommended the appointment of [[James Wolffe]] as [[Lord Advocate]] and [[Alison Di Rollo]] as [[Solicitor General for Scotland|Solicitor General]] on 31 May 2016. Their recommendation was confirmed by the Scottish Parliament on the same day.<ref name="SG245"/>

Nicola Sturgeon announced a cabinet reshuffle on 26 June 2018.<ref name="adda2">{{Cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2018/jun/26/nicola-sturgeon-reshuffle-scottish-cabinet-shona-robinson-health-education |title=Nicola Sturgeon carries out major reshuffle of Scottish cabinet |last=Carrell |first=Severin  |date=26 June 2018 |work=The Guardian}}</ref>
