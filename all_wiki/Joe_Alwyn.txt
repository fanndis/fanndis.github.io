'''Joseph Matthew Alwyn''' (born 21 February 1991)<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/person/joe-alwyn |title=Joe Alwyn|work= [[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]]|location=UK|accessdate=13 September 2017|archive-date= 8 January 2018|archive-url=https://archive.today/20180108224236/http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/person/joe-alwyn|url-status=live}}</ref> is an English actor. He has starred in films such as ''[[Billy Lynn's Long Halftime Walk (film)|Billy Lynn's Long Halftime Walk]]'' (2016), ''[[The Favourite]]'' (2018), and ''[[Harriet (film)|Harriet]]'' (2019).

== Early life ==
Alwyn was born in Tunbridge Wells, Kent, England.<ref>https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:QV45-1RKS</ref> Raised in [[Tufnell Park]] and [[Crouch End]], North London,<ref name="standard">{{cite web|last1=McLean|first1=Craig|title=Joe Alwyn: the Crouch End boy taking Hollywood by storm|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/esmagazine/joe-alwyn-the-crouch-end-boy-taking-hollywood-by-storm-a3459576.html|work=[[London Evening Standard]]| location =UK|accessdate=16 May 2017|date=9 February 2017|quote=...26-year-old Crouch Ender...| archive-date=9 February 2017| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170209142602/http://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/esmagazine/joe-alwyn-the-crouch-end-boy-taking-hollywood-by-storm-a3459576.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="i-D">{{cite web|last1=Flynn|first1=Paul|title=Joe Alwyn Was Destined To Be a Star|website=i-D| publisher=I-D.Vice.com|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/joe-alwyn-was-destined-to-be-a-star|accessdate=19 May 2017|date=4 November 2016| archive-date= 26 February 2017| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170226134727/https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/joe-alwyn-was-destined-to-be-a-star|url-status=live}}</ref> Alwyn is the son of a psychotherapist mother and a documentary filmmaker father.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.co.uk/article/joe-alwyn-interview-2018|title=Joe Alwyn On Fame, Romance, And His Blockbuster-Filled Autumn|first=Giles|last=Hattersley|website=British Vogue |date=27 September 2018}}</ref> He is the great-grandson of composer [[William Alwyn]].

== Personal life ==
Since 2016, Alwyn has been dating American singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]].<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/movies/a25133806/joe-alwyn-mary-queen-of-scots-interview-2018/|title=Prepare to See Joe Alwyn in, Well, Everything|first=Emily|last=Poenisch|date=7 December 2018|website=Esquire}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Boucher  |first=Ashley |date=24 May 2019  |title=Taylor Swift and Boyfriend Joe Alwyn Hold Hands as They Stroll the Streets of Paris |url=https://people.com/music/taylor-swift-joe-alwyn-hold-hands-paris/ |work=People }} </ref> He has been referred to in much of Swift's recent music, most notably in ''[[Reputation (Taylor Swift album)|Reputation]]'' (2017), ''[[Lover (album)|Lover]]'' (2019), and ''[[Folklore (Taylor Swift album)|Folklore]]'' (2020).<ref>{{Cite web|last=Singh|first=Olivia|last2=Samshian|first2=Jacob|date=October 31, 2019|title=Everything we know about Taylor Swift's mysterious boyfriend and muse, Joe Alwyn|url=https://www.insider.com/who-is-joe-alwyn-taylor-swift-2017-11|access-date=November 7, 2020|website=[[Insider Inc.|Insider]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hearon|first=Sarah|date=October 8, 2020|title=7 Times Taylor Swift May Have Referenced Joe Alwyn on ‘Folklore’|url=https://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/pictures/taylor-swift-may-have-referenced-joe-alwyn-on-folklore-lyrics/|access-date=November 7, 2020|website=[[US Weekly]]|language=en-US}}</ref> Alwyn has stated he finds it "flattering" that Swift writes songs about him.<ref>{{cite news|last=Dresdale|first=Andrea|date=May 24, 2019|title=Joe Alwyn finds it 'flattering' that Taylor Swift writes songs about him|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|url=https://www.goodmorningamerica.com/culture/story/joe-alwyn-finds-flattering-taylor-swift-writes-songs-67856948|access-date=November 7, 2020}}</ref> Two songs on her album ''Folklore'', "[[Exile (Taylor Swift song)|Exile]]" and "[[Betty (Taylor Swift song)|Betty]]" were credited to a co-writer named William Bowery, which led to speculation that it was a pseudonym for Alwyn. Swift confirmed that Bowery was indeed an alias for Alwyn in her concert film ''[[Folklore: The Long Pond Studio Sessions]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/taylor-swift-confirms-the-identity-of-folklore-song-co-writer-william-bowery-2825041|title=Taylor Swift confirms the identity of 'Folklore' song co-writer William Bowery|first=Elizabeth|last=Aubrey|magazine=NME|date=November 25, 2020|accessdate=November 25, 2020}}</ref>
