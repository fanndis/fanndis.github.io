'''Sophie Wilmès''' ({{IPA-fr|sɔfi wilmɛs}}; born 15 January 1975) is a [[Belgium|Belgian]] politician who has served as [[List of Foreign Ministers of Belgium|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] in the [[De Croo Government|government]] of [[Prime Minister of Belgium|Prime Minister]] [[Alexander De Croo]] since October 2020. She is the country's first female foreign minister, and was over the year from October 2019 the first female prime minister in Belgian history.<ref name=msr>Marine Strauss (October 1, 2020),[https://www.reuters.com/article/idUSKBN26M75Y Finally, a government after 652 days: New Belgian PM debuts at EU summit] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref> She is a member of the [[Mouvement Réformateur|Reformist Movement]].{{cn|date=October 2020}}

Wilmès was elected to the [[Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)|Chamber of Representatives]] in 2014, and served as [[Minister of Budget (Belgium)|budget minister]] in the [[Michel I Government|first]] and [[Michel II Government|second]] governments of [[Charles Michel]] from 2015 to 2019.{{cn|date=October 2020}} In the aftermath of the [[2019 Belgian federal election]], [[Philippe of Belgium]] appointed Wilmès to lead a caretaker government (the [[Wilmès I Government]]) before she formed an executive government (the [[Wilmès II Government]]) in March 2020 to handle the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Belgium|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name=psw>{{cite web|title=Premier Sophie Wilmès (MR) vraagt morgen het vertrouwen in de Kamer|url=https://vrtnws.be/p.NqGMjWNNk|author=VRT NWS|work=vrtnws.be|date=2020-03-16|accessdate=2020-03-17|language=nl}}</ref> 

In October 2020, she joined the new De Croo government as foreign minister<ref name=msr/> and [[deputy prime minister]].{{cn|date=October 2020}} She was criticized for her handling while Premier of the coronavirus pandemic by De Croo when he said on day one of his new administration: "Our country, our economy, our companies cannot afford new generalised lockdowns."<ref name=msr/>

== Early life ==
Sophie Wilmès was born in [[Ixelles]], [[Brussels]] on 15 January 1975.<ref name=Sudinfo1>{{cite news |last1=Nizet |first1=Pierre |title=Qui est vraiment Sophie Wilmès, notre nouvelle Première |language=fr}}</ref> He father, [[Philippe Wilmès]], was a banker and economics professor at the [[Université catholique de Louvain]] who had been active in liberal politics and had served as ''[[chef de cabinet]]'' to [[Jean Gol]] of the [[Liberal Reformist Party (Belgium)|Liberal Reformist Party]] (''Parti Réformateur Libéral'', PRL).<ref name=LF1>{{cite news |last1=Figaro |first1=Madame |title=Sophie Wilmès : une ministre modèle devient la première femme à diriger la Belgique |date=27 October 2019}}</ref> Her paternal grandparents were killed in the bombing of [[Limal]] during World War II.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lecho.be/entreprises/services-financiers-assurances/deces-de-philippe-wilmes/8919713.html|title=Décès de Philippe Wilmès|newspaper=[[L'Echo]]|language=French|date=25 May Wilmès has a degree in applied communication from [[IHECS]] and a degree in financial management ([[Saint-Louis University, Brussels]]).<ref name="whoami">{{cite web |url=https://www.sophiewilmes.be/en/who-am-i/ |work=Sophie Wilmes |accessdate=17 March 2020 |title=Who Am I?}}</ref>

For a time, she worked for the [[European Commission]] as a financial officer, and then as an economic and financial adviser in a law firm.<ref name="whoami"/>

== Political career ==
In 2000, Wilmès became a councillor in [[Uccle]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brusselstimes.com/belgium/75966/british-immigration-officials-deployed-to-zeebrugge-in-crackdown-against-organised-smuggling/|title=UK immigration officials sent to Zeebrugge in crackdown against organised smuggling|date=29 October 2019|website=The Brussels Times|language=en|access-date=29 October 2019}}</ref> From 2007 to 2014 Wilmès was First Alderman in charge of Finance, Budget, Francophone Education, Communication and Local Businesses for the town of [[Sint-Genesius-Rode]]. From 2014 to 2015 she was a provincial councillor for the province of [[Flemish Brabant]].

In October 2014 she was elected to the [[Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)|Chamber of Representatives]].<ref name="whoami"/> 

In September 2015, minister of the budget [[Hervé Jamar]] announced that he would resign on 1 October 2015 because he was selected as the governor of the province of [[Liège (province)|Liège]]. Wilmès was selected to succeed him in the [[Michel I Government]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2019/oct/28/belgium-first-female-pm-sophie-wilmes|title=Belgium gets first female PM as Sophie Wilmès takes office|last=Rankin|first=Jennifer|date=28 October 2019|work=The Guardian|access-date=29 October 2019|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> In December 2018 she became Minister of Budget, Civil Service, National Lottery and Scientific Policy in the [[Michel II Government]].{{cn|date=October 2020}}

On 27 October 2019, Wilmès became the first female [[Prime Minister of Belgium]], succeeding [[Charles Michel]]. She led a [[Wilmès I Government|caretaker government]] while negotiations proceeded to form a new coalition government.<ref name=":0" /> On 16 March 2020, with negotiations still underway after 15 months, all major parties agreed to grant full legislative powers to the Wilmès government in order to fight the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Belgium|COVID-19 pandemic]]. Under the terms of the agreement, Wilmès was granted special powers to deal with the pandemic's economic and social impact. These powers were to last for three months, though they could be renewed once for an additional three months. Wilmès was officially nominated as prime minister by King Philippe later on 16 March, <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/mar/16/belgium-hands-powers-to-caretaker-pm-sophie-wilmes-to-fight-covid-19 |title=Belgium hands powers to caretaker PM to fight Covid-19 after 15-month stalemate|date=16 March 2020|website=The Guardian|language=en|access-date=18 March 2020}}</ref> and her reshuffled [[Wilmès II Government|executive government]] was sworn in the day after.

After the failure of her Covid lock-down policy,<ref name=msr/> on 1 October 2020 Wilmès was appointed [[deputy prime minister]] and [[List of Foreign Ministers of Belgium|foreign minister]] in the [[De Croo Government|new government]] formed under [[Alexander De Croo]], becoming the first female foreign minister in Belgian history.<ref name=msr/> For a time beginning on 22 October 2020, she managed the country's foreign relations from her intensive care hospital bed as she suffered from [[COVID-19]].<ref name=swc/> It was noted by ''[[Deutsche Welle]]'' that "Wilmes tested positive for coronavirus" prior to 17 October "after attending an EU summit with her counterparts"<ref name="dwsw">{{cite news |title=Belgian Foreign Minister Sophie Wilmes in intensive care with COVID-19 |url=https://www.dw.com/en/belgian-foreign-minister-sophie-wilmes-in-intensive-care-with-covid-19/a-55358381 |publisher=Deutsche Welle |date=October 21, 2020}}</ref> at the [[Europa building]] in [[Luxembourg]] on 12 October<ref name="ecfac12">{{cite news |title=FOREIGN AFFAIRS COUNCIL Luxembourg, 12 October 2020 |url=https://www.consilium.europa.eu/media/46075/12-fac-participants.pdf |publisher=European Council |date=12 October 2020}}</ref> and 13 October.<ref name="ecgac13">{{cite news |title=GENERAL AFFAIRS COUNCIL Luxembourg, 13 Octobre 2020 |url=https://www.consilium.europa.eu/media/46134/20201013-gac-presslist.pdf |publisher=European Council |date=13 October 2020}}</ref> Her Austrian counterpart, [[Alexander Schallenberg]], has also since had a positive test.<ref name=dwsw/>

==Personal life==
In May 2009, Wilmès married [[Chris Stone (footballer)|Chris Stone]], an [[Australia|Australian]] businessman and former [[Australian rules football|footballer]]. They have three daughters: Victoria, Charlotte, and Elizabeth. Stone has a son, Jonathan, from a previous relationship.<ref>{{Cite web

Shortly after the end of her term as Prime Minister, on 17 October 2020, she tweeted that she was [[COVID-19]]-positive.<ref>[https://twitter.com/Sophie_Wilmes/status/1317447193381437440 twitter.com]</ref> On 22 October, she was admitted to intensive care in stable condition.<ref name=swc>{{cite news |last1=Boffey |first1=Daniel |title=Belgium's deputy PM in intensive care with coronavirus |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/oct/22/belgium-deputy-pm-sophie-wilmes-in-intensive-care-with-coronavirus |accessdate=22 October 2020 |work=The Guardian |date=22 October 2020}}</ref> The [[COVID-19 pandemic in Belgium]] is one of the worst in Europe. She was released from hospital on 30 October.<ref>[[La Libre]] 30-10-20 à 12h19 "Sophie Wilmès est sortie de l'hôpital: elle poursuivra sa convalescence à son domicile"</ref>
