'''Mahesh Shrinivas Bhupathi''' (born 7 June 1974) is a retired Indian professional [[tennis player]]. In 1997, he became the first Indian to win a Grand Slam tournament (with [[Rika Hiraki]]).{{#tag:ref|Hiraki was the first Japanese woman to win a Grand Slam tournament, while Bhupathi was the first Indian of any kind to win a Grand Slam in mixed doubles.|group=lower-alpha}} With his win at the Australian Open mixed doubles in 2006, he joined the elite group of eight tennis players who have achieved a career Grand Slam in mixed doubles. He is also the founder of [[International Premier Tennis League]]. In December 2016, Bhupathi was appointed as India's next non-playing [[Davis Cup]] captain and took over the reins from [[Anand Amritraj]] in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Indian Express|title=Mahesh Bhupathi named India's non-playing Davis Cup captain|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/sports/tennis/mahesh-bhupathi-india-davis-cup-captain-anand-amritraj-440013/|website=Indian Express|accessdate=21 June 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170821172305/http://indianexpress.com/article/sports/tennis/mahesh-bhupathi-india-davis-cup-captain-anand-amritraj-440013/|archive-date=21 August 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==

===1995–2006===
Mahesh Bhupathi is considered one of the top doubles players of the 1990s and 2000s. In 1999, Bhupathi won three doubles titles with [[Leander Paes]], including the [[French Open]] and [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]]. He and Paes became the first doubles team to reach the finals of all four [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]]s, the first time such a feat has been achieved in the open era and the first time since 1952. On 26 April of that year, they became the world no. 1 doubles team. Bhupathi also won the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]] [[Mixed doubles (tennis)|mixed doubles]] with [[Ai Sugiyama]] of Japan.

In 2006, Bhupathi teamed with [[Martina Hingis]] in the Australian Open mixed doubles competition. Entering the tournament unseeded and as wildcards,<ref>[http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/3/global/includes/TrackIt.asp?file=http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/3/global/Pdfs/events/2006/melbourne_draws.pdf Source] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071001073233/http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/3/global/includes/TrackIt.asp?file=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.sonyericssonwtatour.com%2F3%2Fglobal%2FPdfs%2Fevents%2F2006%2Fmelbourne_draws.pdf |date=1 October 2007 }}</ref> the first-time pair defeated four seeded opponents along the way, while only dropping a single set throughout. Bhupathi and Hingis defeated the sixth-seeded team of [[Daniel Nestor]] and [[Elena Likhovtseva]] in straight sets, 6–3, 6–3, to capture the championship. It was the sixth mixed doubles Grand Slam for Bhupathi, and the first one for Hingis. By winning the Australian Open, Bhupathi completed a career Grand Slam in mixed doubles.

===2007–2008===
In 2007, Bhupathi and [[Radek Štěpánek]] reached the [[2007 Australian Open]] [[2007 Australian Open – Men's Doubles|men's doubles event]]'s quarterfinals. Bhupathi teamed with Štěpánek at the [[2007 French Open]] to make the [[2007 French Open – Men's Doubles|doubles]] semifinals, defeating two-year defending champions [[Jonas Björkman]] and [[Max Mirnyi]] in the quarterfinals. The team lost to the eventual champions [[Mark Knowles]] and [[Daniel Nestor]]. After [[2007 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]], Bhupathi teamed with [[Pavel Vízner]] to win the [[2007 Canada Masters]], defeating the top-ranked doubles team [[Bob Bryan|Bob]] and [[Mike Bryan]] en route. After this victory, he won a tournament in [[2007 Pilot Pen Tennis|New Haven]] with [[Nenad Zimonjić]]. At the [[2007 US Open (tennis)|2007 US Open]], he and Zimonjić paired in [[2007 US Open – Men's Doubles|doubles]]. After the US Open, the team that beat Bhupathi and Štěpánek in the French Open semifinals, Knowles and Nestor, split up. Bhupathi became Knowles' partner,<ref>[http://www.atptennis.com/3/en/players/doubles/profiles/bhupathiknowles.asp Team profile]{{dead link|date=September 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> while Zimonjić became Nestor's, but back surgery meant he was out until the end of the year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/sports/2007/oct/02bhup.htm|title=Bhupathi to undergo back surgery|website=www.rediff.com|access-date=12 October 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071018063330/http://www.rediff.com/sports/2007/oct/02bhup.htm|archive-date=18 October 2007|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2009–2012===
In 2009, Bhupathi and compatriot [[Sania Mirza]] won the mixed doubles title at the Australian Open, beating [[Nathalie Dechy]] and [[Andy Ram]], 6–3, 6–1, in the final. The Indian pair thus made up for the disappointment of the previous year's final when they were beaten by [[Sun Tiantian]] and [[Nenad Zimonjić]]. With this win, Bhupathi's count in mixed doubles Grand Slam titles increased to seven.

Bhupathi broke up his partnership with Knowles and began playing once again with [[Max Mirnyi]], with whom he played to win the [[2002 US Open (tennis)|2002 US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/sports/2002/sep/07bhup.htm|title=Bhupathi-Mirnyi win US Open doubles|accessdate=7 September 2002|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20021217151541/http://www.rediff.com/sports/2002/sep/07bhup.htm|archive-date=17 December 2002|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2011, Bhupathi reunited with former playing partner [[Leander Paes]] for the [[2011 Australian Open]]. The team reached the final, but lost 3–6, 4–6 to the Bryan brothers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.oneindia.in/2011/01/29/sports-leander-paes-bhupathi-lose-australian-open-aid0033.html|title= Leander Paes, Mahesh Bhupathi lose Australian Open|accessdate=29 January 2011}}</ref>On 7 June 2012, Bhupathi and [[Sania Mirza]] won the French Open mixed doubles.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tennis/french-open-2012/indian-challenge/Mahesh-Bhupathi-and-Sania-Mirza-win-French-Open-mixed-doubles-crown/articleshow/13909650.cms|title=Mahesh Bhupathi and Sania Mirza win French Open mixed doubles crown|date=8 June 2012|work=The Times Of India|access-date=8 June 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120608111125/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tennis/french-open-2012/indian-challenge/Mahesh-Bhupathi-and-Sania-Mirza-win-French-Open-mixed-doubles-crown/articleshow/13909650.cms|archive-date=8 June 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>
On 4 November 2012, Bhupathi and partner [[Rohan Bopanna]] won the Paris Masters cup.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-11-04/top-stories/34907719_1_bhupathi-and-bopanna-indian-pair-paris-masters|title=Mahesh Bhupathi-Rohan Bopanna win Paris Masters|accessdate=4 November 2012|work=The Times Of India|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130710004109/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-11-04/top-stories/34907719_1_bhupathi-and-bopanna-indian-pair-paris-masters|archive-date=10 July 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> In spite of suffering a setback with their loss against [[Jonathan Marray]] and [[Frederik Nielsen]] in the ATP Tour Finals opener,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tennis/top-stories/Bhupathi-Bopanna-beaten-in-Tour-Finals-opener/articleshow/17117489.cms|title=Bhupathi-Bopanna beaten in Tour Finals opener|accessdate=6 November 2012 | work=The Times Of India}}</ref> the Indian duo reached the final round of the ATP Tour Finals, but suffered a defeat at the hands of [[Marcel Granollers]] and [[Marc López]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tennis/top-stories/Bhupathi-Bopanna-defeated-in-final-of-ATP-Tour-Finals/articleshow/17199682.cms|title=Bhupathi-Bopanna defeated in final of ATP Tour Finals|accessdate=13 November 2012|work=The Times Of India|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121113122021/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tennis/top-stories/Bhupathi-Bopanna-defeated-in-final-of-ATP-Tour-Finals/articleshow/17199682.cms|archive-date=13 November 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2013===
Bhupathi and Bopanna played with different partners for the first three months of 2013, Bhupathi winning the tournament in Dubai in March with [[Michaël Llodra]], but rejoined starting with the Monte-Carlo Masters.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sports.ndtv.com/tennis/news/205686-mahesh-bhupathi-rohan-bopanna-back-together|title=Mahesh Bhupathi-Rohan Bopanna back together – NDTV Sports|last=NDTVSports.com|publisher=|access-date=1 April 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130404001105/http://sports.ndtv.com/tennis/news/205686-mahesh-bhupathi-rohan-bopanna-back-together|archive-date=4 April 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Playing Style==
Mahesh Bhupathi is known for his big serve. According to Nadal, his strong backhand makes him the best for an Ad Court player. Roger Federer acclaims him as one of the best players of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=imdi0_BLz40|title=TWTP - DOUBLES SPECIALIST (Roger Federer talks about Mahesh Bhupathi)|last=twtpofficial|date=29 June 2012|publisher=|via=YouTube|access-date=30 November 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160610143117/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=imdi0_BLz40|archive-date=10 June 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> He often discusses strategies between the serves with his partner during the match and also communicates using finger-at-the-back signals.

==Year-end finals<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Players/Top-Players/Mahesh-Bhupathi.aspx|title=Mahesh Bhupathi - Overview - ATP World Tour - Tennis|publisher=|access-date=23 November 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111116074225/http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Players/Top-Players/Mahesh-Bhupathi.aspx|archive-date=16 November 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>==
Bhupathi appeared with Paes in six season finales. In 2011, they appeared, for the first time since 2002, after securing qualification in mid-October.

Bhupathi played at the year-end championships with Paes from 1997 to 2000 and in 2002, reaching three finals. In 1997, they lost the final to [[Rick Leach]] and [[Jonathan Stark (tennis)|Jonathan Stark]]. They lost the 1999 final to [[Sébastien Lareau]] and [[Alex O’Brien]]. In 2000, they lost the final to [[Donald Johnson]] and [[Pieter Norval]].

Bhupathi also qualified with [[Max Mirnyi]] in 2003, 2004, and 2010, when they finished runners-up to [[Daniel Nestor]] and [[Nenad Zimonjić]].

He appeared at the finals with [[Mark Knowles]] in 2008 and 2009. 

In 2012, he and [[Rohan Bopanna]] made it to the final, where they lost to [[Marcel Granollers]] and [[Marc López]].

==Davis Cup and Asian Games==
Bhupathi has donned Indian colours numerous times for the [[Davis Cup]] as well as other international tournaments, including the [[Asian Games]].

Bhupathi has played 55 matches for India in the Davis Cup (from 1995 to 2011), winning 35 and losing 20. Out of the 35 matches that he won, 27 of his victories came in doubles matches.

In 2006, Bhupathi won the doubles championship with [[Leander Paes]] at the Asian Games in Doha.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/sports/2006/dec/13dohalead02.htm|title=Paes-Bhupathi win men's doubles|accessdate=13 December 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070104151206/http://www.rediff.com/sports/2006/dec/13dohalead02.htm|archive-date=4 January 2007|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Personal life==

In 2001, he was awarded the [[Padma Shri]], one of India's highest civilian awards. Bhupathi is an alumnus of the [[University of Mississippi]] in the United States. He is the founder of Globosport India private Limited which he started in 2002 as a sports and entertainment agency.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=44300383|title=Globosport India Private Limited: Private Company Information - Bloomberg|website=www.bloomberg.com|access-date=2018-06-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180626164221/https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=44300383|archive-date=26 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

He married model [[Shvetha Jaishankar]] in 2002 but the couple got divorced in 2009 after seven years of marriage.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sinhl|first1=Gauri|title=Shvetha & Mahesh? Why knot!|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/delhi-times/Shvetha-Mahesh-Why-knot/articleshow/20992167.cms|accessdate=21 August 2017|agency=TNN|publisher=Times Of India|date=2 September 2002|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170212043706/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/delhi-times/Shvetha-Mahesh-Why-knot/articleshow/20992167.cms|archive-date=12 February 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> He then married  [[Miss Universe 2000]] [[Lara Dutta]] in a civil ceremony on 16 February 2011 at [[Bandra]], [[Mumbai]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.samaylive.com/entertainment-news/676482721/lara-dutta-mahesh-bhupathi-church-wedding-on-saturday.html|title=Mahesh Bhupathi-Lara Dutta exchange wedding vows|publisher=English.samaylive.com|date=19 February 2011|accessdate=24 June 2011|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715234626/http://english.samaylive.com/entertainment-news/676482721/lara-dutta-mahesh-bhupathi-church-wedding-on-saturday.html|archivedate=15 July 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> It was followed by a Christian ceremony on 20 February 2011 at Sunset Point in Goa.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-02-20/off-the-field/28625396_1|work=The Times of India|title=Mahesh Bhupathi and Lara Dutta exchange wedding vows|date=20 February 2011|access-date=29 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105131059/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-02-20/off-the-field/28625396_1|archive-date=5 November 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 1 August 2011, Dutta confirmed that she was pregnant with their first child. Their daughter Saira was born on 20 January 2012.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lara Dutta is pregnant!|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-08-01/news-interviews/29838384_1_lara-dutta-marriage-front-miss-universe|work=Times of India|accessdate=2011-08-01|date=1 August 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105094741/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-08-01/news-interviews/29838384_1_lara-dutta-marriage-front-miss-universe|archive-date=5 November 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2010, the couple started a film production company, Big Daddy Productions.<ref>{{cite news|last=Ganguly|first=Prithwish|title=Lara, Mahesh start working together|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2010-12-21/news-interviews/28261659_1_lara-dutta-mahesh-and-lara-film|accessdate=15 March 2012|newspaper=Times of India|date=21 December 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110823144855/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2010-12-21/news-interviews/28261659_1_lara-dutta-mahesh-and-lara-film|archive-date=23 August 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2014, Mahesh Bhupathi launched an authentic Indian sports brand, ZEVEN. The company currently endorses [[Ravindra Jadeja]], [[Rohan Bopanna]], [[Shikhar Dhawan]] and Mary Kom, amongst others.

==Partnership with Leander Paes==
[[File:LeeAndHesh1.jpg|thumb|240px|right|Bhupathi (right) and Leander Paes]]
Bhupathi and Leander Paes partnered in the men's doubles event at the [[2008 Summer Olympics]] in Beijing, but lost the quarterfinals,<ref>[http://www.atptennis.com/en/common/TrackIt.asp?file=http://www.atptennis.com/1/posting/2008/440/mdd.pdf 2008 Ordina Open Men's Doubles Draw]{{dead link|date=May 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> to [[Roger Federer]] and [[Stanislas Wawrinka]] of Switzerland, who went on to win the gold medal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/sports/2008/aug/16swiss.htm|title=Federer leads Swiss to doubles gold|access-date=17 August 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080819002855/http://www.rediff.com/sports/2008/aug/16swiss.htm|archive-date=19 August 2008|url-status=live}}</ref>

Paes and Bhupathi decided to team up again at the Australian Open 2011, ending a nine-year separation on the ATP circuit. They reached the finals of the event,<ref>{{cite web|title = Doubles pair Paes, Bhupathi to reunite|publisher = Australian Broadcasting Corporation|url = http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2010/11/21/3072309.htm|accessdate = 29 October 2010|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20101126015156/http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2010/11/21/3072309.htm|archive-date = 26 November 2010|url-status = live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Bhupathi-Paes To Reunite in 2011 in Bid To Complete Career Grand Slam|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/News/Tennis/2010/11/46/Finals-Paes-Bhupathi-To-Reunite-In–2011.aspx|date=20 November 2010|accessdate=9 December 2010|first=James|last=Buddell|publisher=ATPWorldTour.com}}</ref> but lost to American twins [[Bob and Mike Bryan]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/01/30/sports/tennis/30doubles.html|title= Bryans Win Doubles Again|accessdate=29 January 2011 | work=The New York Times|first=Christopher|last=Clarey|date=29 January 2011}}</ref> Paes stated at the time that the best thing has been to have their friendship back.<ref>ATP World Tour Finals interview</ref>

The Indian duo has a 303–103 career record together. They have a Davis Cup record of longest winning streak in doubles, with 23 straight wins.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.daviscup.com/en/history/records.aspx|title=Davis Cup - Page Not Found|website=www.daviscup.com|access-date=2 February 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120208132452/http://www.daviscup.com/en/history/records.aspx|archive-date=8 February 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>

Leander Paes wanted to play with Mahesh Bhupathi in the men's doubles event of the [[London Olympics]], to be held July–August 2012.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tournaments/london-olympics/Mahesh-Bhupathi-declines-to-play-with-Leander-Paes/articleshow/14235780.cms|title= Mahesh Bhupathi declines to play with Leander Paes|date= 18 June 2012|work= The Times Of India|access-date= 18 June 2012|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20120618101854/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tournaments/london-olympics/Mahesh-Bhupathi-declines-to-play-with-Leander-Paes/articleshow/14235780.cms|archive-date= 18 June 2012|url-status= live}}</ref>
On 19 June 2012, the All India Tennis association relented to the demands of Bhupathi and Bopanna of not playing along Paes. Two teams were sent for the London Olympics- 2012, with Mahesh Bhupathi and Rohan Bopanna as one team and the other team consisting of Leander Paes and Vishnu Vardhan.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tournaments/london-olympics/AITA-may-send-two-teams-to-London-Olympics/articleshow/14265577.cms|title= AITA may send two teams to London Olympics|date= 19 June 2012|work= The Times Of India|access-date= 19 June 2012|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20120619100354/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/tournaments/london-olympics/AITA-may-send-two-teams-to-London-Olympics/articleshow/14265577.cms|archive-date= 19 June 2012|url-status= live}}</ref> Bhupathi also accused AITA of using Sania Mirza as bait for Leander's participation in the Olympics.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-06-27/news/32440491_1_mahesh-bhupathi-doubles-sania-mirza | work=The Times Of India | title=Sania Mirza was used without even being consulted: Bhupathi – The Times of India | access-date=3 August 2012 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105052558/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-06-27/news/32440491_1_mahesh-bhupathi-doubles-sania-mirza | archive-date=5 November 2013 | url-status=live }}</ref> When AITA relented to the wishes of Bhupathi and Bopanna and permitted them to play together, they lost in the second round to the unseeded French pairing of [[Richard Gasquet]] and [[Julien Benneteau]].

== Sports management and sports-based e-commerce==
Bhupathi has also been involved in developing tennis facilities in India and, along with his company Globosport, has played a key rôle in developing and managing the careers of many Indian athletes, including [[Sania Mirza]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/articleshow/46367823.cms|title= Sania Mirza signs up with Globosport|work= [[Times of India]]|date= 14 May 2003|access-date= 9 September 2008|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20121021165228/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/articleshow/46367823.cms|archive-date= 21 October 2012|url-status= live}}</ref>

==International Premier Tennis League==
Mahesh Bhupathi announced the founding of the International Premier Tennis League on 25 May 2013, in Paris. The initial plan was to start the [[sports league|league]] with six charter franchises in Asia with the inaugural [[season (sports)|season]] commencing in November 2014. Bhupathi said the league would be modeled after the [[Indian Premier League]], a [[cricket]] league in India. [[Justin Gimelstob]] said that the league would be star-driven as [[World Team Tennis]] was in the 1970s.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mahesh Bhupathi Unveils Plan for IPL-Style Tennis League|url=http://sports.ndtv.com/french-open-2013/news/208302-mahesh-bhupathi-unveils-plan-for-international-premier-tennis-league|date=25 May 2013|website=NDTV Sports|accessdate=17 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222102338/http://sports.ndtv.com/french-open-2013/news/208302-mahesh-bhupathi-unveils-plan-for-international-premier-tennis-league|archive-date=22 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>
