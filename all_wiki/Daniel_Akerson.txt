'''Daniel Francis "Dan" Akerson''' (born October 21, 1948) is the former [[Chairman]] and [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] of [[General Motors]], serving from 2010 to 2014. Akerson succeeded [[Edward Whitacre, Jr.|Edward Whitacre]] as CEO on September 1, 2010, and became Chairman of the Board on January 1, 2011. He was succeeded by [[General Motors]] CEO [[Mary Barra]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2013/12/10/gm-names-mary-barra-as-new-ceo.html|title=GM caps busy week by naming Mary Barra as new chief|last=David|first=Javier E.|date=2013-12-10|website=CNBC|language=en|access-date=2020-02-21}}</ref> Akerson was a Managing Director of [[The Carlyle Group]] and head of global buyout prior to joining [[General Motors]].  He joined the [[General Motors]] board of directors on July 24, 2009. Akerson also serves on the boards of [[American Express]] and the [[United States Naval Academy|U.S. Naval Academy]] Foundation,<ref name="Daniel Akerson Bio1">{{cite news

==Personal life==
Akerson was born in [[Oakland, California]], grew up in [[Mankato, Minnesota|Mankato]], [[Minnesota]], and currently resides in [[McLean, Virginia]]. He holds a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in [[engineering]] from the [[United States Naval Academy]] (Class of 1970) and a [[Master of Science]] degree in [[economics]] from the [[London School of Economics]].<ref name="BW1">{{cite web
  | title = The Carlyle Group 
  | publisher = Investing.businessweek.com
  | url = http://investing.businessweek.com/businessweek/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=93549&privcapId=23333&previousCapId=92001&previousTitle=American%20Express%20Company|accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref> Akerson served as an [[military officer|officer]] on a [[United States Navy|Naval]] destroyer from 1970-1975.

Akerson's maternal grandparents are [[German People|German]] and his paternal grandparents are [[Swedes|Swedish]].<ref name=HourDetroit>{{cite web|last=James|first=Sheryl|title=Navigating Troubled Waters|url=http://www.hourdetroit.com/Hour-Detroit/October-2013/Navigating-Troubled-Waters/|work=Hour Detroit magazine|accessdate=10 December 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
Akerson joined [[MCI Communications|MCI Inc.]] in 1983 where he served as the [[CFO]] for several years as well as President and Chief Operating Officer.<ref name="usna.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.usna.com/page.aspx?pid=467 |title=Home - News16_10 - Lockheed Martin |website=Usna.com |date= |accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref> He left MCI in 1993 to become chairman and chief executive of [[General Instrument]], where he succeeded former [[United States Secretary of Defense]] [[Donald Rumsfeld]].<ref name="NYT2">{{cite news
  | title = MCI's President Quits in a Surprise Move 
  | publisher = The New York Times, EDMUND L. ANDREWS, August 12, 1993
  | url = https://www.nytimes.com/1993/08/12/business/mci-s-president-quits-in-a-surprise-move.html
| first=Edmund L.
| last=Andrews
| date=August 12, 1993
| accessdate=April 30, 2010}}</ref>

In 1996, Akerson was hired to be the chief executive of [[Nextel]]. During his tenure as CEO, Nextel's revenues grew from $171.7 million in the year before his arrival to more than $3.3 billion in 1998. Shortly after stepping down as CEO of Nextel in July 1999, Akerson was brought in by [[Craig McCaw]] to run Nextlink Communications, later rebranded as [[XO Communications]].<ref name="WAPO1">{{cite news
  | title = Nextlink CEO Is on a Hot Streak 
  | publisher = The Washington Post, Rob Garretson, June 26, 2000
  | url = https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/WPcap/2000-06/26/004r-062600-idx.html
| date=June 26, 2000
| accessdate=April 30, 2010}}</ref> XO Communications entered bankruptcy in June 2002, and Akerson resigned as CEO in December 2002.<ref name="WAPO2">{{cite news

| date=December 28, 2002}}</ref> Akerson joined [[The Carlyle Group]] in 2003.<ref name="WAPO3">{{cite news

While at The Carlyle Group, Akerson ran the Company's largest private equity fund.<ref>{{cite web|author=David Welch |url=http://www.businessweek.com/magazine/content/10_36/b4193064985274.htm |title=Dan Akerson Takes the Wheel at GM - Bloomberg |website=Businessweek.com |date=2010-08-26 |accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref>

In July 2009, Akerson was named to the board of directors of [[General Motors]] as a representative of the [[U.S. Treasury]], which at the time owned a 61% stake in GM.<ref name="WAPO4">{{cite news
  | title = New GM Board Members Named 
  | publisher = The Washington Post, Terri Rupar, July 23, 2009
  | url = http://voices.washingtonpost.com/economy-watch/2009/07/new_gm_board_members_named.html
| accessdate=April 30, 2010}}</ref> On August 12, 2010 it was announced that Akerson would be the successor of Ed Whitacre as CEO of General Motors, starting September 1, 2010 and would also assume the Chairman of the Board position on January 1, 2011.
General Motors, during Akerson's first year of tenure in 2011, earned a record $7.6 billion in profit off of $150.3 billion in sales.<ref name="Bunkley">{{cite news|title=G.M. Reports Big Profit; Europe Lags|work=[[NY Times]]|date=February 16, 2012|accessdate=February 16, 2012|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/02/17/business/gm-reports-its-largest-annual-profit.html?_r=1&hpw|first=Nick|last=Bunkley}}</ref>  In December 2013, the U.S. Treasury sold its final remaining shares of GM common stock.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.treasury.gov/press-center/press-releases/Pages/jl2236.aspx|title=Treasury Sells Final Shares of GM Common Stock|website=www.treasury.gov|access-date=2019-08-21}}</ref>

In April 2013, investors began to speculate that the 64-year-old executive may be considering retirement. The speculation was based solely on changes to Akerson's compensation plan at GM.<ref>{{cite web|author=Zach Bowman |url=http://www.autoblog.com/2013/04/28/gm-alters-akersons-pay-mix-because-he-may-retire/ |title=GM alters Akerson's pay mix because he may retire |website=Autoblog.com |date=2013-04-28 |accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref> 

On December 10, 2013, GM announced that Akerson will be replaced as CEO of GM by [[Mary Barra]], effective January 15, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|author=Javier E. David |url=https://www.cnbc.com/id/101232970 |title=GM names Mary Barra as new CEO |website=Cnbc.com |date=2013-12-10 |accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref>  It is reported that Akerson's retirement was expedited by his wife's advanced stage cancer,<ref>{{cite news| url=https://money.cnn.com/2013/12/10/news/companies/gm-ceo-mary-barra/index.html?hpt=hp_t2 | work=CNN | first=Aaron | last=Smith | title=GM names Mary Barra as new CEO | date=December 10, 2013}}</ref> however on March 1, 2014, The Carlyle Group announced that Akerson rejoined the firm as Vice Chairman and Special Advisor to the Board of Directors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.carlyle.com/news-room/news-release-archive/carlyle-group-names-daniel-akerson-vice-chairman-and-special-advisor |title=News Release Archive &#124; The Carlyle Group |website=Carlyle.com |date= |accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref> As of February 2014 to current Akerson serves on the board of directors at [http://www.Lockheedmartin.com Lockheed Martin].

==Public citizenship==
In August 2016, Akerson announced that he would—for the first time in his life—not vote for the Republican presidential candidate.<ref name="washingtonpost1">{{cite news|last=Akerson |first=Daniel |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/ive-always-voted-republican-until-now/2016/08/17/03a1b970-622d-11e6-96c0-37533479f3f5_story.html |title=I've always voted Republican. Until now. |newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] |date=2016-08-17 |accessdate=2017-07-11}}</ref>  In an [[op-ed]] piece for [[The Washington Post]], Akerson excoriated [[Donald Trump]] on a wide range of issues, while praising [[Hillary Clinton]].<ref name="washingtonpost1"/>
