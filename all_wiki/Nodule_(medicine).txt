In [[medicine]], '''nodules''' are small nodes i.e., small, rounded masses.<ref name = Mosbys2013p1299>{{vcite book | author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |chapter= | title= Mosbyâ€™s Medical Dictionary - E-Book, Edition 9 | publisher= Elsevier Health Sciences | date= 2013| isbn= 9780323112581| pages= 1299 | url =https://www.google.com/search?tbm=bks&q=isbn:9780323112581}}</ref> They can be elevated areas of tissue or fluid inside or under the skin with a diameter greater than 0.5 centimeters. Nodules may form on [[tendon]]s and [[muscle]]s in response to injury. The [[Vocal folds|vocal cords]] may also develop nodules. Nodules are normally [[benign]] and often painless, although they can affect the functioning of the [[organ (anatomy)|organ]]<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Britton|first1=K. E.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bpQpBAAAQBAJ&q=%22Nodule%22+medicine+-wikipedia&pg=PA205|title=Clinical Nuclear Medicine|last2=Gilday|first2=David L.|last3=Maisey|first3=Michael|date=2013-12-11|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4899-3358-4|language=en}}</ref> or be painful as in [[endometriosis]].<ref name= Nisenblat2016ppn>{{cite journal | vauthors = Nisenblat V, Bossuyt PM, Farquhar C, Johnson N, Hull ML | title = Imaging modalities for the non-invasive diagnosis of endometriosis | journal = The Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews | volume = 2 | pages = | quote = painful palpable nodules| date = February 2016 | pmid = 26919512 | doi = 10.1002/14651858.cd009591.pub2 | pmc = 7100540 }}</ref>

[[Vocal fold nodule]]s, [[thyroid nodule]]s and [[rheumatoid nodule]]s are examples. [[Furuncle]]s and  [[Kaposi's sarcoma]]ta are known to cause dermatological nodules.

The sexually transmitted disease (STD) [[gonorrhea]] is also known for its cause of nodules on the genitalia and mouth for those who are victim to the disease.

Smaller (less than 0.5 cm) elevated soft tissue lesions may be termed [[Papule|papules]].<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Potter|first1=Patricia A.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=obPwAwAAQBAJ&q=Smaller+(less+than+0.5+cm)+elevated+soft+tissue+lesions+may+be+termed+papules.&pg=PA329|title=Essentials for Nursing Practice - E-Book|last2=Perry|first2=Anne Griffin|last3=Stockert|first3=Patricia|last4=Hall|first4=Amy|date=2014-03-25|publisher=Elsevier Health Sciences|isbn=978-0-323-18881-4|language=en}}</ref>
