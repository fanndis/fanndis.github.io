'''Catherine''' "'''Caty'''" '''McNally''' (born November 20, 2001) is an [[United States|American]] [[tennis]] player. She is the 2018 [[2018 French Open|French Open]] juniors doubles and 2018 French Open girls' singles runner-up and [[2018 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] junior doubles champion.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gauff edges McNally in all-American girls' final in Paris |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1441829/gauff-edges-mcnally-in-all-american-girls-final-in-paris |website=[[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=GAUFF, MCNALLY WIN US OPEN GIRLS' DOUBLES |url=https://www.usta.com/en/home/pro/pro-media---news/coco-gauff--caty-mcnally-win-us-open-junior-doubles-titles.html |website=USTA |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref> She has won two doubles titles on the [[WTA Tour]], as well as two singles titles and five doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Caty McNally WTA & ITF titles |url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/caty-mcnally/800459512/usa/wt/d/titles |website=ITF |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref> On September 9, 2019, she reached her best singles ranking of world No. 105. On March 9, 2020, she achieved her highest doubles ranking of world No. 38.<ref name=WTA>{{cite web |title=Caty McNally best ranking at WTA |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/players/325725/catherine-mcnally |website=WTA |accessdate=January 13, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life and background==
McNally was born in [[Madeira, Ohio|Madeira]], a suburb of [[Cincinnati]], [[Ohio]],<ref name=Baum>{{cite news |last1=Baum |first1=Adam |title=Cincinnati's Caty McNally awarded wild card for the 2019 Western & Southern Open |url=https://eu.cincinnati.com/story/sports/Ohio/2019/08/01/cincys-caty-mcnally-earns-wild-card-2019-western-southern-open/1815316001/ |accessdate=August 10, 2019 |work=Cincinnati Enquirer |date=August 1, 2019}}</ref> to John McNally and Lynn Nabors-McNally. Her mother was briefly a professional tennis player who had a career best doubles ranking inside the top 250. Her older brother [[John McNally (tennis)|John]] is also a professional and was a high-ranked junior player. Both are coached by their mother.<ref>{{cite web |title=Williams sisters, Bryan brothers and now ... Meet the McNallys |url=https://www.desertsun.com/story/sports/columnists/shad-powers/2016/04/08/williams-sisters-bryan-brothers-and-now-meet-mcnallys/82822896/ |website=Desert Sun |accessdate=August 1, 2019}}</ref>

==Junior career==
McNally finished runner-up at the Wimbledon junior doubles tournament in [[2016 Wimbledon Championships – Girls' Doubles|2016]], [[2017 Wimbledon Championships – Girls' Doubles|2017]] and [[2018 Wimbledon Championships – Girls' Doubles|2018]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Madeira teen Caty McNally ousted in Wimbledon first round |url=https://www.wlwt.com/article/madeira-teen-caty-mcnally-ousted-in-wimbledon-first-round/28241923 |accessdate=August 1, 2019 |work=[[WLWT]] |date=July 1, 2019}}</ref> She won her first Grand Slam junior title at the [[2018 French Open]] doubles event at the age of 16, partnering with [[Iga Świątek]].<ref name=Cincinnati>{{cite news |last1=Springer |first1=Scott |title=Madeira's Caty McNally qualifies for Wimbledon |url=https://eu.cincinnati.com/story/sports/high-school/ohio-high-school/2019/06/27/madeiras-caty-mcnally-qualifies-wimbledon-tennis/1582446001/ |accessdate=August 1, 2019 |work=Cincinnati Enquirer |date=June 28, 2019}}</ref> At the same tournament she reached the final of the girls singles, where she lost to [[Coco Gauff]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Record-breaking Gauff and Tseng rule juniors |url=https://www.rolandgarros.com/en-us/article/roland-garros-2018-record-breaking-gauff-and-tseng-rule-junior-ranks |accessdate=1 August 2019 |work=Roland Garros |date=June 9, 2018}}</ref> In September 2018, she partnered Gauff to win the girls doubles title at the [[2018 US Open – Girls' Doubles|US Open]].<ref name=MacPherson>{{cite news |last1=MacPherson |first1=Alex |title=Getting to know Wimbledon 2019 debutantes Gauff, McNally and Flink |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/getting-know-wimbledon-2019-debutantes-gauff-mcnally-and-flink |accessdate=1 August 2019 |work=WTA |date=June 28, 2019}}</ref>

In 2017, McNally was on the United States team that won the [[Junior Davis Cup and Junior Fed Cup|Junior Fed Cup]], having previously been a losing finalist.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Moran |first1=Tom |title=USA crowned Junior Fed Cup champions |url=https://www.itftennis.com/juniors/news/articles/usa-crowned-junior-fed-cup-champions.aspx |accessdate=August 4, 2019 |work=ITF |date=September 24, 2017}}</ref>

==Professional career==
===2017–18: WTA doubles debut===
McNally made her WTA main-draw debut at the [[2017 Western & Southern Open]] in the doubles draw, partnering with [[Alexa Glatch]].<ref name=Cincinnati/>

In February 2019, McNally won the $100K [[2019 Dow Tennis Classic – Singles|Dow Tennis Classic]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kildee |first1=Katy |title=Caty McNally vs. Jessica Pegula in Dow Tennis Classic |url=https://www.ourmidland.com/photos/slideshow/Caty-McNally-vs-Jessica-Pegula-in-Dow-Tennis-189632.php |accessdate=6 March 2019 |work=Midland Daily News |date=February 3, 2019}}</ref> Later that month, she reached the third round of the [[2019 Oracle Challenger Series – Indian Wells – Women's Singles|Indian Wells Challenger]], losing to eventual winner [[Viktorija Golubic]].<ref name=Tennisexplorer>{{cite web |title=McNally, Caty – Profile |url=https://www.tennisexplorer.com/player/mcnally-c14af/ |website=Tennisexplorer.com |accessdate=6 March 2019}}</ref> A week after that, she qualified for the [[2019 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|BNP Paribas Open]], also in Indian Wells, beating [[Kristýna Plíšková]] and [[Timea Bacsinszky]] in the qualifiers.<ref name=Tennisexplorer/> She earned a wildcard for the [[2019 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami Open]], where she was again beaten by Coco Gauff.<ref name=MacPherson/> In July, she qualified for her first Grand Slam main-draw tournament at [[2019 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]].<ref name=MacPherson/>

===2020===
In her first tournament of 2020, the [[2020 ASB Classic – Women's Singles|Auckland Open]], McNally was knocked out in the first round of the singles after qualifying as a lucky loser, but she and Gauff reached the semifinals of the [[2020 ASB Classic – Women's Doubles|doubles]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Laura Siegemund - Caty McNally |url=https://www.eurosport.co.uk/tennis/wta-auckland/2020/live-laura-siegemund-caty-mcnally_mtc1169982/live.shtml |accessdate=13 January 2020 |work=Eurosport |date=January 7, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Long |first1=David |title=Coco Gauff, Caty McNally into doubles semifinals at ASB Classic |url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/sport/tennis/118669631/coco-gauff-caty-mcnally-into-doubles-semifinals-at-asb-classic |accessdate=January 13, 2020 |work=[[Stuff (website)|Stuff]] |date=January 8, 2020}}</ref> At the [[2020 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], she won her qualifying matches, entering the main draw, where she defeated [[Sam Stosur]] in the first round, before losing to [[Zhang Shuai (tennis)|Zhang Shuai]]. In doubles, McNally and Gauff recorded their best result at a Grand Slam tournament, reaching the quarterfinals before falling to second seeded [[Kristina Mladenovic]] and [[Tímea Babos]] in two sets.<ref name="2020 Australian Open Doubles">{{Cite news |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1590901/babos-mladenovic-prove-too-good-for-gauff-mcnally-in-melbourne-doubles |title=Babos, Mladenovic prove too good for Gauff, McNally in Melbourne doubles |work=WTA Tennis |access-date=January 28, 2020}}</ref> As a result, McNally broke into the top 40 in the WTA doubles rankings. In March, she lost to [[Sachia Vickery]] in the first round of the [[2020 Oracle Challenger Series – Indian Wells – Women's Singles|Indian Wells Challenger]] tournament,<ref>{{cite web |title=Oracle Challenger Tournament: Sacia Vickery v Catherine McNally |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/tournament/1103/indian-wells-125k/2020/scores/LS033 |website=WTA |accessdate=9 September 2020}}</ref> but teamed up with [[Jessica Pegula]] to reach the final of the [[2020 Oracle Challenger Series – Indian Wells – Women's Doubles|doubles]], beating third seeds Stosur and [[Yanina Wickmayer]] in the semifinal before falling to [[Asia Muhammad]] and [[Taylor Townsend]] in the final.<ref>{{cite web |title=Asia Muhammad/Taylor Townsend vs. Caty McNally/Jessica Pegula 07.03.2020 - Oracle Challenger Series - Indian Wells |url=http://www.tennislive.net/wta/match/asia-muhammed-taylor-townsend-VS-caty-mcnally-jessica-pegula/oracle-challenger-series-indian-wells-2020/ |website=Tennislive.net |accessdate=9 September 2020}}</ref>

Following the break caused by the [[COVID-19]] pandemic, McNally took part in the [[2020 Western & Southern Open – Women's Singles|Western & Southern Open]], which was moved from her home town of Cincinnati to New York, losing in the first round to [[Alizé Cornet]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Alize Cornet vs. Caty McNally 22.08.2020 - Western & Southern Open - New York |url=http://www.tennislive.us/wta/match/alize-cornet-VS-caty-mcnally/western-southern-open-cincinnati-2020/ |website=Tennislive.us |accessdate=9 September 2020}}</ref> The following week, she reached the third round of a Grand Slam tournament for the first time when she beat 21st seed [[Ekaterina Alexandrova]] in the second round of the [[2020 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sode |first1=Scott |title=Getting to Know: Caty McNally |url=https://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2020-09-04/andy_murray_reflects_on_really_positive_us_open_return.html |accessdate=9 September 2020 |work=WTA |date=September 4, 2020}}</ref>
