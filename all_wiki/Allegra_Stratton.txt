'''Allegra Elizabeth Jane Stratton'''<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/10.1093/ww/9780199540884.001.0001/ww-9780199540884-e-284230|title=Stratton, Allegra Elizabeth Jane, (born 10 April 1980), National Editor, ITV News, since 2016|website=WHO'S WHO & WHO WAS WHO|doi=10.1093/ww/9780199540884.001.0001/ww-9780199540884-e-284230}}</ref> (born 10 April 1980)<ref name="auto"/> is a British political aide, writer, and former journalist serving as the [[Downing Street Press Secretary]] under Prime Minister [[Boris Johnson]] since November 2020.

Stratton worked for ''[[The Guardian]]'' as a political correspondent until joining the [[BBC]] in 2012. Stratton was the [[political editor]] of [[BBC Two]]'s ''[[Newsnight]]'' from 2012 to 2015. She worked for [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] as national editor of [[ITV News]] from 2015 to 2018 and co-presenter of ''[[Peston on Sunday]]'' from 2016 to 2018. 

After leaving her journalism career, Stratton became a [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] political advisor. She was [[Chancellor of the Exchequer|Chancellor]] [[Rishi Sunak]]'s director of strategic communications at [[HM Treasury|the Treasury]] from April 2020 to October 2020. She was announced as the new press secretary for [[10 Downing Street]] on 8 October 2020.

==Early life==
Stratton was born in [[Chiswick]], [[West London]] on 10 April 1980 as one of four children of a translator father and textile artist mother.<ref name="telegraph">{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/women/politics/allegra-stratton-boriss-answer-kellyanne-conway/|title=Who is Allegra Stratton â€“ the woman set to be Boris's press secretary?|work=The Daily Telegraph|last=Silverman|first=Rosa|date=9 October 2020|access-date=10 October 2020}}</ref> She was named after [[Allegra Byron]].<ref name=telegraph-20160220>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/tv/2016/02/19/allegra-stratton-the-real-reason-that-jeremy-paxman-quit-newsnig/ |title=Allegra Stratton: The real reason that Jeremy Paxman quit Newsnight |last=Woods |first=Judith |newspaper=The Sunday Telegraph |url-access=subscription |date=20 February 2016 |access-date=9 October 2020 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://www.pressreader.com/uk/the-sunday-telegraph/20160221/282303909202080 |archive-date=21 February 2016 }}</ref> Stratton attended [[Chiswick Community School]] and [[Latymer Upper School]], an independent school in West London. She then transferred to [[Emmanuel College, Cambridge]], where she read [[archaeology]] and [[anthropology]].<ref name=independent-20201008>{{Cite news|last=Stone|first=Jon|date=8 October 2020|title=Who is Allegra Stratton, Boris Johnson's new spokesperson?|work=The Independent|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/allegra-stratton-boris-johnson-new-spokesperson-press-conference-b889549.html|access-date=9 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Bloxham|first=Andy|date=22 November 2011|title=Allegra Stratton appointed political editor of BBC's Newsnight|work=The Telegraph|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/bbc/8905825/Allegra-Stratton-appointed-political-editor-of-BBCs-Newsnight.html|access-date=9 October 2020}}</ref> 

==Career==
Stratton worked as a producer for the [[BBC]],<ref name=":0" /> on the foreign desk at ''[[The Times]]'' and wrote for ''[[The Independent]]'' and the ''[[New Statesman]]''.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}} She then joined ''[[The Guardian]]'' as a political correspondent, presenting the newspaper's "Politics Weekly" podcast with journalist Tom Clark.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/series/politicsweekly/podcast.xml |title=Politics Weekly |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=21 February 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130731023620/http://www.theguardian.com/politics/series/politicsweekly/podcast.xml |archive-date=31 July 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

During this period she wrote the novel ''Muhajababes'', which explores the youth culture of the Middle East and the contradictions of the modern life of young adults in [[Muslim]] societies.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.salon.com/books/review/2008/07/10/young_islam/index.html |title=Here come the muhajababes!: How sex, booze and heavy metal fit into the world of hip young Arabs today. |last=Miller |first=Laura |work=[[Salon (website)|Salon]] |date=10 July 2008 |access-date=13 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080710133914/http://www.salon.com/books/review/2008/07/10/young_islam/index.html |archive-date=10 July 2008 |page=1-3 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.humanevents.com/article.php?id=21171 |first=Susan |last=Easton |title=Hijabs and Muhajababes |newspaper=[[Human Events]] |date=19 June 2007 |access-date=13 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070619144107/http://www.humanevents.com/article.php?id=21171 |archive-date=19 June 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The book was based on Stratton's experiences travelling in the region in 2005.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Aspden|first=Rachel|date=23 July 2006|title=Islam and the porno devils|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2006/jul/23/islam.religion|access-date=9 October 2020}}</ref>

Stratton returned to the BBC on 20 February 2012,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/newsnight/fromthewebteam/2012/02/monday_20_february_2012.html |title=Newsnight: From the web team: Monday 20 February 2012 |date=20 February 2012 |access-date=21 February 2012 |first=Verity |last=Murphy |publisher=[[BBC]]}}</ref> as [[political editor]] of ''[[Newsnight]]'', replacing [[Michael Crick]] who left to become a political correspondent for [[Channel 4]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2011/jul/19/channel-4-michael-crick |title=Channel 4 News appoints Michael Crick as political correspondent |first=John |last=Plunkett |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |location=London |date=30 November 2015 |access-date=10 January 2016 }}</ref> In May the same year, she faced criticism for a ''Newsnight'' interview with a single mother who was claiming housing benefit. The interviewee described feeling "humiliated" by Stratton, who misrepresented her as unemployed.<ref name=newstatesman-20120529>{{cite news |url=https://www.newstatesman.com/blogs/broadcast/2012/05/how-newsnight-humilated-single-mother-shanene-thorpe |title=How Newsnight humiliated single mother Shanene Thorpe |magazine=New Statesman |date=29 May 2012 |access-date=9 October 2020}}</ref> ''[[Private Eye]]'' magazine reported that Stratton had chosen the single mother over several other interviewees offered, including a couple with four children who had lost their jobs and faced homelessness.<ref name=independent-20201008/> This incident led to a 20,000-signature petition soliciting an apology from Stratton and ''Newsnight''.<ref name="auto1">{{cite news|url=http://www.theguardian.com/media/2012/dec/12/rts-boss-accuses-bbc-news|title=Outgoing RTS boss accuses BBC News heads of 'culture of denial'|date=12 December 2012|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> Following an official complaint to the BBC's Editorial Complaints Unit, a correction and apology was issued in August.<ref name="auto1" /><ref name=bbc-20120831>{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/complaints/complaint/newsnight_23052012 |title=Complaints - Newsnight, BBC Two, Wednesday 23 May 2012 |last=Rippon |first=Peter |publisher=BBC |date=31 August 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120610071849/http://www.bbc.co.uk/complaints/complaint/newsnight_23052012 |archive-date=10 June 2012}}</ref>

In November 2015, Stratton left the BBC to join [[ITV News]] as its national editor.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-34962285 |title=Newsnight political editor Stratton follows Peston to ITV |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=30 November 2015 |access-date=13 August 2019 }}</ref> She made her first appearance on ITV's ''[[ITV News at Ten|News at Ten]]'' in January 2016 and co-presented ''[[Peston on Sunday]]'' with [[Robert Peston]] until April 2018, when she departed to spend weekends with her children.<ref>{{Cite news|date=16 April 2018|title=The Londoner: Co-anchor Allegra Stratton departs Peston|work=Evening Standard|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/londoners-diary/the-londoner-coanchor-allegra-stratton-departs-peston-a3815116.html|access-date=9 October 2020}}</ref>

In April 2020, she quit ITV News to become director of strategic communications at [[HM Treasury|the Treasury]] under [[Chancellor of the Exchequer|Chancellor]] [[Rishi Sunak]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.prweek.com/article/1681521/chancellor-picks-top-political-journalist-comms-chief|title=Chancellor picks top political journalist as comms chief|last=Griggs|first=Ian|date=27 April 2020|work=[[PR Week]]|accessdate=6 May 2020}}</ref> Six months later, in October 2020, she was appointed the new [[Downing Street Press Secretary]], and as such will be fronting the new televised press briefings<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|date=8 October 2020|title=Ex-Journalist Stratton to lead No 10 TV briefings|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-politics-54468229|access-date=8 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Stewart|first=Heather|date=9 October 2020|title=Could being the face of Boris Johnson be the worst job in politics?|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2020/oct/09/could-being-the-face-of-boris-johnson-be-the-worst-job-in-politics|access-date=12 October 2020}}</ref> currently<!---25 November 2020---> scheduled for launch in January 2021.<ref name="Peston">{{Cite tweet|last=Peston|first=Robert|user=Peston|number=1331309697106907142|date=24 November 2020|title=The revolution of televised briefings by the PM's press secretary, Allegra Stratton, is scheduled to start 11 Jan and will be every Monday, Tuesday and Thursday when Commons is sitting. Interesting that the briefing won't take place on #PMQs day. I guess on Wednesdays...}}</ref> The briefings will begin on 11 January and will take place when the House of Commons will sit on Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays.<ref name="Peston"/>

==Personal life==
Stratton is married to [[James Forsyth (journalist)|James Forsyth]], [[political editor]] of ''[[The Spectator]]'' magazine.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/theguardian/2011/aug/13/simon-hoggart-week-riots-economy |title=Simon Hoggart's week: sailing through dire economic straits |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |first=Simon |last=Hoggart |author-link=Simon Hoggart |date=13 August 2011 |access-date=13 August 2019 }}</ref> The couple have two children and live in [[Canonbury]], [[North London]].<ref name="standard">{{cite news|date=28 March 2013|title=Allegra Stratton&#039;s My London|newspaper=[[Evening Standard]]|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/esmagazine/allegra-strattons-my-london-8552134.html|access-date=31 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|date=16 April 2018|title=The Londoner: Co-anchor Allegra Stratton departs Peston|work=Evening Standard|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/londoners-diary/the-londoner-coanchor-allegra-stratton-departs-peston-a3815116.html}}</ref> Future [[Chancellor of the Exchequer|Chancellor]] [[Rishi Sunak]] was [[best man]] at their wedding in 2011,<ref name="telegraph"/> and they and Sunak are godparents to each other's children.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/meet-the-chancellor-the-real-rishi-sunak-by-the-people-who-know-him-best-q5lk3qf8l|work=The Times|title=Meet the chancellor: the real Rishi Sunak, by the people who know him best|last=Edwardes|first=Charlotte|date=1 August 2020|access-date=11 September 2020|archive-date=11 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200911224110/https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/meet-the-chancellor-the-real-rishi-sunak-by-the-people-who-know-him-best-q5lk3qf8l|url-status=live|url-access=subscription}}</ref>

In November 2020, Stratton told ''[[The Sunday Telegraph]]'' that despite voting for the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]], the [[Green Party of England and Wales|Green Party]] and the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]] in the past, she voted for [[Brexit]] and describes herself as "a Johnson Tory".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/politics/2020/11/14/johnson-tory-voted-brexit-insists-new-face-no-10/|title=Allegra Stratton exclusive: 'I'm a Johnson Tory and voted for Brexit' insists new face of No 10|last=Malnick|first=Edward|date=15 November 2020|website=www.telegraph.co.uk|access-date=15 November 2020|url-access=limited}}</ref>
