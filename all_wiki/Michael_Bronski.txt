'''Michael Bronski''' (born May 12, 1949) is an American academic and writer, best known for his 2011 book ''A Queer History of the United States''.<ref>[http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/books/2011/05/how_gays_helped_make_and_remake_america.html "How Gays Helped Make and Remake America"]. ''[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]'', May 23, 2011.</ref> He has been involved with LGBT politics since 1969 as an activist and organizer. He has won numerous awards for LGBTQ activism and scholarship, including the prestigious Publishing Triangle's [[Bill Whitehead Award]] for Lifetime Achievement. Bronski is a Professor of Practice in Media and Activism at [[Harvard University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wgs.fas.harvard.edu/people/michael-bronski|title=Michael Bronski|website=wgs.fas.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref>

==Career==
Since 1970, Bronski has written extensively on culture, politics, film, theater, books, sexuality, LGBT culture, and current events. As a journalist, cultural critic and political commentator he has been published in a wide array of venues including ''The Village Voice'', ''The Boston Globe'', ''GLQ'', ''The Los Angeles Times'', ''The Boston Phoenix'', ''Cineaste'', ''Contemporary Women's Writing'', ''TIME,'', '' The Nation'', and the ''Boston Review'' <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.encyclopedia.com/arts/educational-magazines/bronski-michael-1945|title=Bronski, Michael 1945- {{!}} Encyclopedia.com|website=www.encyclopedia.com|access-date=2019-06-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Authors|url=http://bostonreview.net/author/michael-bronski |website=Boston Review |publisher=Boston Review |accessdate=2 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Authors |url=https://www.thenation.com/authors/michael-bronski/ |website=The Nation |publisher=The Nation |accessdate=2 May 2020}}</ref> His scholarship includes over 50 essays in anthologies on LGBTQ culture and politics. 

He was an original member of [[Fag Rag|''Fag Rag'' Collective]] from 1971 to 1998 and the Good Gay Poets Collective.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thecambridgeroom.wordpress.com/tag/good-gay-poets-collective/|title=Good Gay Poets Collective {{!}} The Cambridge Room|language=en|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref> He was a founding member of the ''Boston Gay Review''. He acted as program coordinator for OutWrite: Lesbian and Gay Literary Conference for five years in the 1990s.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jZwUC3CJq0IC&q=OutWrite%3A+Lesbian+and+Gay+Literary+Conference+%22bronski%22&pg=PT162|title=Who's Who in Contemporary Gay and Lesbian History Vol.2: From World War II to the Present Day|last1=Aldrich|first1=Robert|last2=Wotherspoon|first2=Garry|date=2005-07-25|publisher=Routledge|isbn=9781134583133|language=en}}</ref>

Bronski was awarded the 1995 AIDS Action Committee Community Recognition Award for 20 years of journalism on gay and [[HIV/AIDS|AIDS]]-related topics.<ref name="wgs.fas.harvard.edu">{{Cite web|url=https://wgs.fas.harvard.edu/news/michael-bronski-receives-bill-whitehead-lifetime-achievement-award|title=Michael Bronski Receives Bill Whitehead Lifetime Achievement Award|website=wgs.fas.harvard.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref> In 1996, he received the Cambridge Lavender Alliance Lifetime Achievement Award for journalism and political organizing.

Bronski was featured in the [[BBC|BBC's]] ''Stage Struck: Gay Theater in the Twentieth Century'' (1999), [[Public Broadcasting Services|PBS's]] ''[[After Stonewall]]'' (1999)'','' [[Cinemax|Cinemax's]] ''The Hidden Führer: Debating the Enigma of Hitler's Sexuality'' (2004), and ''[[Wrangler: Anatomy of an Icon]]'' (2008).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1131290/|title=Michael Bronski|website=IMDb|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref>

In 1999, the Anderson Prize Foundation granted Bronski the Stonewall Award in recognition for "helping improve the lives and LGBT people in the United States."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dgala.org/2011/08/|title=August 2011 – DGALA {{!}} Dartmouth LGBTQIA+ Alum Association|language=en|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref>

''A Queer History of the United States'' won both a [[Lambda Literary Award]] and a [[Stonewall Book Award]] in 2012.<ref name="Harvard">{{cite web|url=https://wgs.fas.harvard.edu/people/michael-bronski|title=Harvard}}.</ref> He also previously won two Lambda Literary Awards as an editor of [[anthology|anthologies]], in 1997 for ''Taking Liberties: Gay Men's Essays on Politics, Culture, & Sex'' and in 2004 for ''Pulp Friction: Uncovering the Golden Age of Gay Male Pulps''.

Bronski consulted on LGBT content and analyzed focus group results for [[MTV]]/[[Logo TV|Logo]] in 2014 and wrote ten biographies of noted LGBT historical figures for the MTV/Logo June [[Pride Month]] programming in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/pride-lgbtq-icons-logo_n_5932d126e4b075bff0f3e6bb|title=Celebrate Pride Month With Logo's Beautiful Animations Of Historic LGBTQ Icons|last=Nichols|first=James Michael|date=2017-06-03|website=HuffPost|language=en|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref>

In 2017, he was the recipient of the [[Publishing Triangle|Publishing Triangle's]] prestigious [[Bill Whitehead Award]] for Lifetime Achievement.<ref name="wgs.fas.harvard.edu"/> Previous awardees included Audre Lorde, Martin Duberman, and Alison Bechdel.

He was a senior lecturer in Women’s and Gender Studies and in Jewish Studies at [[Dartmouth College]], where he was granted the 2008 Distinguished Lecturer Award and 2004 Leadership Award from the Dartmouth Gay and Lesbian Alumni Association.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dartmouthalumnimagazine.com/articles/america-queer|title=America Is Queer|last=Oct 2011|first=Michael Bronski {{!}} Sept-|website=Dartmouth Alumni Magazine|language=en|access-date=2019-06-08}}</ref> He is currently Professor of the Practice in Media and Activism in the Studies of Women, Gender, and Sexuality at [[Harvard University]].<ref name="Harvard" />  

==Personal life==
Bronski has resided in [[Cambridge, Massachusetts|Cambridge]], Massachusetts since 1971. He was the partner of American poet [[Walta Borawski]], who died in 1994.<ref>{{cite web|title=Michael Bronski and Walta Borawski. Cambridge, MA.|url=https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/5e66b3e8-7c93-d471-e040-e00a180654d7|accessdate=22 September 2017}}</ref>
