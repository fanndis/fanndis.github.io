'''Avril''' is an international French [[Agribusiness|agro-industrial group]], specialising in human nutrition, animal nutrition, renewable [[Oleochemistry|chemistry]], and renewable [[Biodiesel|energy]]. The group was founded in 1983, by producers of [[oilseeds]] and [[protein crop]]s, to assure market growth. Avril's primary purpose is the economic development of oilseed products – [[rapeseed]], [[sunflower]], [[soybean]] – and protein products – [[pea]]s, [[field bean]]s, [[lupin]]. In 2014, Avril processed half of the rapeseed and sunflower grains collected in France.<ref name=RA42>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=42}}</ref>

Formerly known as Sofiproteol, the group adopted the name of Avril in 2015, as a result of a new structure of its governance and its legal framework. As a limited partnership with share capital (''société en commandite par actions''), this operation was intended to ensure the group's independence and to have the agricultural sector represented as the focal point of its growth strategy. Thus, Avril enables a distinction between industrial and financial activities. The financial activities are clustered around Sofiprotéol<ref name=landrieu>{{Harvsp|Landrieu|2015}}</ref><ref>{{Harvsp|Cougard : Sofiprotéol se rebaptise Avril et change de gouvernance|2015}}</ref>

Avril operates on a model of integrated sectors, which is unique in the French and European agro-industrial business world. The group systematically reinvests profits within agricultural sectors. Its goal is to ensure a fair distribution of added value for the entire chain, from producers to consumers.<ref name=RA2>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=2}}</ref>

Avril has a presence in 21 countries and employs 7,600 employees.<ref name= accueil>[http://www.groupeavril.com/fr Avril, ''Page d'accueil'', consultée le 7 février 2019]</ref> In 2017, its turnover amounted to 6.2 billion euros.

==History==

===1962 -1983: From Plan proteins to CNTA, the genesis of Sofiprotéol===
In 1962, the [[Dillon Round]] world trade negotiations led to a massive influx of US soybeans, exempt from European customs.<ref name=SH360>{{Harvsp|Schneider & Huyghe|2015|p=360}}</ref> Soy, rich in protein, is needed as livestock feed. In June 1973, following an exceptional drought, the US government decreed an embargo on the export of seeds and [[Press cake|oilcake]]. This caused prices to soar, reflecting an interdependence of French and European farmers.<ref name=SH361>{{Harvsp|Schneider & Huyghe|2015|p=361}}</ref>

In 1974, a Protein Plan is launched in France under the leadership of a young farmer and unionist, [[Jean-Claude Sabin]], backed by the public authorities.<ref name=CNCA>{{Harvsp|CNCA|1975|p=81}}</ref> The aim of the plan was to increase the production of oilseed rape, sunflower, peas, flax and field beans through creating a chain of French vegetable oil and protein. Two interprofessional organizations emerged : the ''National Interbranch Union of Protein-rich plants'' (UNIP) in 1976 and the ''National Interbranch Organisation of Oilseed and Oil fruits'' (ONIDOL) in 1978.<ref name=MHV>{{Harvsp|Vincent|2002}}</ref> These organizations merged in 2015, giving birth to [[Terres Univia]].<ref name=I.E.>{{Harvsp|I.E.|2015}}</ref>

In the 1970s, the ''National Syndicate of Agricultural Techniques'' (CNTA), a key player of the Protein Plan, became the industrial tool of the French oilseed sector. Its bankruptcy in 1983 undermined the sector most tied to production. To refloat, the interprofessional organizations decided to create a financial fund: Sofiprotéol.<ref name=Azouvi>{{Harvsp|Azouvi|1984|p=27}}</ref>

===1983: Birth of Sofiprotéol===
The new company has been considered as a financial player in the French sector of vegetable oils and proteins. Its shareholders have included many agricultural stakeholders: UNIP, ONIDOL, and the ''French Federation of Oilseed Producers'' (FOP), bringing together nearly 100,000 producers.<ref name=Duboelle>{{Harvsp|Duboelle|2014}}</ref>

Sofiprotéol had five employees in 1983.<ref name=gpeavril73>{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1973-1983|title=" 11 May 1983: Creation of Sofiprotéol " In ''History : 1973 – 1983''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 18 September 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022130034/http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1973-1983|archive-date=22 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> It was chaired by Jean-Claude Sabin, with general management entrusted to [[Philippe Tillous-Borde]], an agricultural engineer specialized in industrial investments. Together, they ran the company for nearly 20 years.<ref name=Delanglade>{{Harvsp|Delanglade|2013}}</ref>

From the beginning, the objectives of Sofiprotéol were to financially secure production for the various players in the sector and to ensure an industrial outlet for oilseeds and protein crops.<ref name=Ballerini44>{{Harvsp|Ballerini|2006|p=44}}</ref> It first played the role of a financial institution as development bank in the sector<ref name=Boucly/>{{,}}<ref name=Ballerini44/>{{,}}.<ref name=Boemare>{{Harvsp|Boemare|2001|p=236}}</ref> Then, in the following years, the company took over assets in the field of [[Trituration|crushing seeds]], seeds, and packaging of edible oils.<ref name=LeDouarin>{{Harvsp|Le Douarin|2014}}</ref> This was the beginning of extending the agricultural sector with the construction of an industrial chain, from farmers, to seed storage agencies and seed producers.<ref name=Boucly>{{Harvsp|Boucly|2014}}</ref>

===1987 – 1993: Development of a French biofuel sector===
In search of French rapeseed markets, ONIDOL with the [[French Institute of Petroleum]] (IFP) launched the first studies establishing a biofuel based on methyl rapeseed esters in 1987.<ref name=Jamet>{{Harvsp|Jamet|1993|p=140}}</ref> After three years of experimentation, Diester® was launched in 1990.<ref name=Perthuis>{{Harvsp|Perthuis|1993|p=182}}</ref>

From 1991 to 1995, the EU allocated FF300&nbsp;million to support innovative projects in non-food applications of farming products, including biofuels.<ref name=gpeavril>{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1983-1993|title=" Launch of the Diester brand " In ''History : 1983 – 1993''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 18 September 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022130039/http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1983-1993|archive-date=22 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 1992, France authorized the incorporation of biofuels in diesel up to 5% and to 30% for company vehicles.<ref name=gpeavril/> Sofiprotéol developed industrial activity through involvement in the management of biodiesel production facilities. This was the beginning of the biodiesel industry, marked by the construction of the first plant in [[Compiègne]] in 1988.<ref name=AM>{{Harvsp|Mathieu|1998|p=35}}</ref>

===1993 – 2006: Strengthening co-products strategy===
In 1993, Sofiprotéol opened a large rapeseed and sunflower seed crushing factory in [[Grand-Couronne]], near [[Rouen]]. With a capacity of 360,000 tons, the site supplied a new factory dedicated to the production of biodiesel in 1995.<ref name=gpeavrilpac>{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1983-1993|title="  Reform of the CAP (Common Agricultural Policy)  " In ''History : 1983 – 1993''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 21 September 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022130039/http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1983-1993|archive-date=22 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> During this period, Sofiprotéol augmented and extended its sector strategy by developing its work in [[oleochemistry]], [[biotechnologies]], animal feed, and nutrition.

In 1996, Sofiprotéol created [[Novance]] in partnership with [[Rhône-Poulenc|Rhône-Poulenc Chimie]].<ref name=UN96>{{Harvsp|L'Usine nouvelle n°2553|1996}}</ref> Novance produced products derived from vegetable oils for non-food applications, such as [[glycerin]]. In 1998, the Group took stake in [[Glon]] [[Sanders (firm)|Sanders]], a leader in the French animal feed sector and a major outlet for oilseed<ref name=Guerny>{{Harvsp|Guerny|2007}}</ref>{{,}}.<ref name=LFA07>{{Harvsp|La France Agricole|2007}}</ref> Glon was fully acquired in 2012. With the 2003 acquisition of [[Lesieur]]<ref name=FA2936>{{Harvsp|La France Agricole n°2963|2002}}</ref> and [[Puget, Vaucluse|Puget]] in 2004,<ref name=Moal>{{Harvsp|Moal|2004}}</ref> the group became the primary manufacturer of edible oils in France.<ref name=gpeavril9306>{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1993-2006|title="  Lesieur: a key name in oil  » In ''History : 1993 – 2006''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 20 September 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022130044/http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/1993-2006|archive-date=22 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Between 2007 and 2008, six new units tripled the production of biodiesel, thanks to the Biofuel Plan launched by French public authorities.<ref name=OESTV>{{Harvsp|OESTV|2006|p=22}}</ref>

===2007 – 2015: Restructuring and internationalization===
The 2000s were marked by a Sofiprotéol surge. The group changed the development of its businesses. In 2007, Sofiprotéol created two major business divisions: ''Oilseeds'', responsible for enhancing vegetable oils, and ''Animal Products'', developing the group's activities in the field of nutrition and livestock production with related expertise : biosafety, nutritional specialties, etc...<ref name="gpeavrilpôles">{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/2006-2015|title=" The creation of two major divisions of activities " In ''History : 2006 – 2015''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 21 September 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022130049/http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/2006-2015|archive-date=22 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The group has strengthened its international position. With the 2008 acquisition of the [[Belgian]] company [[Oleon]], the group has been the European leader in oleochemistry.<ref name=LFA08>{{Harvsp|La France Agricole|2008}}</ref> With the 2010 acquisition of [[Expur]] in Romania, the Group gained a firm foothold in the oilseed production basin of Eastern Europe and the [[Black Sea]].<ref name=gpeavrilroumanie>{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/2006-2015|title=" Conquering the Romanian market " In ''History : 2006 – 2015''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 21 September 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151022130049/http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/history/2006-2015|archive-date=22 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2011, [[Lesieur Cristal]] was acquired, [[Morocco]]'s leader in not only oils and seeds, but also soaps. Through the [[Green Morocco Plan]], Sofiprotéol supported a strategy to develop Moroccan industries based on rapeseed, sunflower, and olive oil.<ref name=Cougard>{{Harvsp|Cougard|2015}}</ref>

In 2012, [[Jean-Philippe Puig]] succeeded [[Philippe Tillous-Borde]] as head of group.<ref name=Delanglade/> In 2015, the group adopted new governance and a new identity.<ref name="action" /> Industrial activities were assigned to the limited partnership with share capital (SCA), Avril. [[Sofiprotéol]] remains as an investment company under the control of Avril Group.<ref name=Cahuzac>{{Harvsp|Cahuzac|2015}}</ref>

==Activities==

===Oilseeds Division===
In 2014, the Oilseeds Division of Avril Group generated a turnover of 4.58&nbsp;billion euros.<ref name=RA58>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=58}}</ref>

====Human nutrition====
Avril Group is the largest producer of table oils in France ([[Lesieur (oil)|Lesieur]], [[Puget (oil)|Puget]], [[Fruit d'Or]], [[Soléou]], [[Guénard]]), [[Morocco]] ([[Lesieur Cristal]]) and [[Romania]] ([[Bunica (oil)|Bunica]]).<ref name="Cahuzac" /> By contributing to 1.2&nbsp;million tons of oil in 2014, Avril participated in the daily supply of 81 million individuals.<ref name=RA29>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=29}}</ref> The group also manufactures oil and vegetable based sauces and condiments.<ref name=RA36>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=36}}</ref>

====Renewable energies====
Avril produces under the brand [[Diester]], a fifth of 11 million tons of biodiesel consumed annually by the [[European Union]].<ref name="EurObserv’ER">{{Harvsp|EurObserv'ER|2014|p=4}}</ref> It is the leading European producer of biodiesel.<ref name=RA29/> Diester is incorporated at a rate of 8% in the fuel used by all French diesel vehicles.<ref name=RA29/> Diester is a [[first-generation biofuel]], made from rapeseed and sunflower vegetable oil.

Avril invests in research to develop [[second-generation biofuels]]. The target being to market biofuels from the inedible parts of plants and agricultural waste ([[lignin]], [[cellulose]]) in order to produce food and energy. In 2013, The group launched a production of biodiesel based on animal fats and waste oils. The group participates in ''BioTfueL'',<ref name=Monnier>{{Harvsp|Monnier|2014}}</ref> a program that develops [[biodiesel]] and [[biokerosene]] from forestry waste (straw, wood, etc.). Avril also initiated Institute PIVERT, in [[Picardy]]. Institute PIVERT is the first European center developing plant chemistry based on oilseed biomass, collaborating with hundreds of researchers.<ref name=Roussange>{{Harvsp|Roussange|2014}}</ref>

====Oleochemicals and renewable chemistry====
Through the 1996 creation of [[Novance]] and the 2008 acquisition of [[Oleon]], Avril has dominated the European market of [[oleochemistry]].<ref name=RA41>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=41}}</ref> Oleochemistry is a branch of "plant chemistry" developing chemical molecules, commonly referred to as bio-based molecules.<ref name=EV>{{Harvsp|Entrepreneur Vert|2010}}</ref> Avril manufactures and markets a range of bio-based molecules from vegetable oils (rapeseed, sunflower, palm, soy, coconut). Among other molecules, Avril produces glycerol, a biodiesel byproduct. Glycerol is a non-toxic liquid, with a sweet taste, that is used in many pharmaceutical, cosmetic, and food products.<ref name=MEDDE>{{Harvsp|MEDDE|2011}}</ref>

Through Lesieur Cristal, Avril produces soaps in Morocco, marketed under the brands ''Taous'' and ''El Kef''.<ref name="Cougard" />

===Animal Products Division===
In 2014, the Animal Products Division of Avril Group generated a turnover of 1.82&nbsp;billion euros.<ref name=RA58/>

====Animal nutrition and production====

Through [[Sanders (firm)|Sanders]], [[Adonial]], and others companies, Avril produces and sells rapeseed and sunflower [[press cake]]s.<ref name=Cougard12>{{Harvsp|Garnier|2012}}</ref> Extracted from oilseeds, these solid residues are rich in protein. Crushed and mixed with other ingredients ([[protein]], [[Food grain|grains]], [[vitamins]], etc.), they are intended to feed farm animals such as pigs, cattle, poultry, and rabbits. One hundred million chickens are fed annually by Avril.<ref name=poulet>{{Cite web|url=http://www.groupeavril.com/en/group/organisation/animal-product-division|title=" Partner livestock farmers " In ''Animal product division''|website=http://www.groupeavril.com, consulted 21 September 2015}}</ref> European rapeseed and sunflower press cakes are a substitute for American soybean press cakes.<ref name=SH361/>

====Biosafety and nutritional specialties====

[[Biosecurity]] covers all preventive hygiene methods to minimize the infection and spread of diseases in livestock. In 2014, the group created [[Mixscience]] and [[Theseo]], both having developed a reputation in France and abroad, around products and services for the hygiene of animal shelters, breeding material and treatment of water, air and surfaces.<ref name=Vergonjeanne>{{Harvsp|Vergonjeanne|2014}}</ref>

====Processing and distribution of food products====

Since 2005, Avril has produced and marketed eggs known as [[Matines]].<ref name=Cougard14>{{Harvsp|Cougard|2014}}</ref> Through [[Ovoteam]] and [[3 Vallées]], Avril supplies eggs for the agribusiness industry.<ref name=Cougard05>{{Harvsp|Cougard|2005}}</ref>

With [[Abera (firm)|Abera]] and [[Porcgros]], Avril is also engaged in the pig industry, through slaughtering and meat cutting.<ref name=LE10>{{Harvsp|Les Echos|2010}}</ref>

===Investor Profession: Sofiprotéol===
[[Sofiprotéol]] assists with loans and equity investments for over a hundred companies, representing approximately 80,000 employees<ref>{{Harvsp|Ouest-France Entreprises|2015}}</ref> in the agro-industry and in related sectors such as [[Dairy product|dairy processing]].<ref name=laurent>{{Harvsp|Laurent|2015}}</ref> Sofiprotéol is committed to its partners on a permanent basis. The company strengthens the equity of companies, supporting them in their strategic decisions and modernization.<ref name=Boucly/>

Following restructuring ratified in 2015, the shareholders of Sofiprotéol – [[Crédit Agricole]], [[Natixis]], [[Unigrains]] – became more involved. With a capital base from 70 to 330 million euros,<ref name=RA13>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=13}}</ref> Sofiprotéol intends to expand its scope of action to new sectors of agro-industry.<ref name=laurent/>

Sofiprotéol is committed in the following activities:<ref name=RA53>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=53}}</ref>
* Collection processing of agricultural products
* Upsteam plant production
* Upsteam animal production
* Investment funds
* Food products and consumer goods
* Sofiproteol dette privée (debt fund)
* Sustainable innovation, other

==Governance==

===Shareholders===

The historical shareholders of the group are:
* [[Interprofessional Development Fund for the Oilseeds and Proteins]] (FIDOP)
* [[French Federation of Oilseed and Protein Crop Producers]] (FOP)

With the restructuring, a new player entered into the group's capital in 2014: Fondation Avril.<ref name= action/>

===Group organization in Avril===

Avril Group is a limited partnership with share capital (SCA) comprising two categories of partners:
* Limited partners, who represent the shareholders, exercise control through the Supervisory Board.
*  The General Partner, who acts as manager of the Group through ''Avril Gestion''. The Board of ''Avril Gestion'' appoints the manager of the SCA. The manager is supported by an executive committee.<ref name=RA9/>

By adopting the status of a limited partnership with share capital (SCA), historical shareholders of Sofiprotéol, the oilseed producers intend to remain at the heart of the project led by Avril.<ref name=landrieu/>

====Supervisory Board====

The Supervisory Board is composed of eight members, six of which represent FIDOP, FOP, and Fondation Avril. Within this council also sit two elected staff members of the group. The Supervisory Board is currently chaired by [[Jacques Siret]], President of [[Terre Univia]].<ref name=RA9>{{Harvsp|Avril Group : Activity Report|2014|p=9}}</ref>

==== Management Board ====
The Management Board of Avril Gestion has nine members:

* Sébastien Windsor : Vice-Chairman of the FOP, Chairman of Terres Innovia
* Alain Renoux, Vice-President of the FOP
* Benjamin Lammert, Member of the Board of the FOP

=== Fondation Avril ===

Avril Group is committed to pay 35% of its [[dividends]]<ref name=L&dlc>{{Harvsp|Lentschner|La Chesnais|2015}}</ref> to a foundation bearing its name and recognized as a [[public service]] by decree on 11 December 2014.<ref name="RA9" />

The foundation supports causes of general interest and is attached to three [[sustainable development]] goals:<ref name=LeBourdonnec>{{Harvsp|Le Bourdonnec|Tillous-Borde|2015|p=147}}</ref>

* to contribute to preserving a high-quality environment in different territories and ensuring collective development of rural communities ;
* to aid developing regions of the world, notably in Africa, in working to protect biodiversity and anticipating the impact of climate change on agricultural productions ;
* to promote a healthy and [[sustainable diet]] through high quality products that are environmentally friendly and accessible to all.

The foundation is chaired by [[Philippe Tillous-Borde]], co-founder of the Group in 1983.<ref name=Monteil>{{Harvsp|Monteil|2015}}</ref> Appointed July 2015, Philippe Leroux is the director.<ref>{{Harvsp|Le Syndicat Agricole|2015}}</ref> Former director of the [[French Development Agency]] (AFD), [[Jean-Michel Severino]] is treasurer.<ref name=D&P>{{Harvsp| Delfortrie|Plagnol|2015}}</ref>
