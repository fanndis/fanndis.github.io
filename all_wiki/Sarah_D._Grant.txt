'''Sarah Dickinson Grant''' (November 10, 1942 &ndash; May 3, 2016) was an American judge.

Born in [[El Dorado, Kansas]], Grant lived in [[Austin, Texas]] and graduated from [[McCallum High School]]. She received her bachelor's degree from [[University of California, Los Angeles]]. in 1966, and was a medical case worker at Harbor General Hospital in [[Los Angeles, California]]. In 1970, Grant received her law degree from Arizona  State University School of Law and was one of the first women in the first graduating class of the law school. Grant practiced law in [[Phoenix, Arizona]]. From 1979 to 1982, Grant served as judge of the [[Maricopa County, Arizona]] Superior Court. In 1982, Grant was appointed judge of the [[Arizona Court of Appeals]], Division One, replacing [[Sandra Day O'Connor]] and served until her retirement in 1999.
