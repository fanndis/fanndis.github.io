'''Mukesh Wadhumal "Micky" Jagtiani''' (born 15 August 1952) is an Indian [[billionaire]] businessman, based in the [[UAE]], and is the chairman and owner of the [[Dubai]] based [[Landmark Group]].

==Early life and education==
Jagtiani is of [[Sindhis|Sindhi]] heritage.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dailytimes.com.pk/341601/most-billionaires-in-india-today-once-resided-in-pakistans-sindh/|title=Most billionaires in India today once resided in Pakistanâ€™s Sindh|work=Daily Times|first=Waseem|last=Palijo|date=8 January 2019|accessdate=7 May 2020|archive-date=7 May 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200507172647/https://dailytimes.com.pk/341601/most-billionaires-in-india-today-once-resided-in-pakistans-sindh/}}</ref>
He did his schooling in [[Chennai]], [[Mumbai]] and [[Beirut]], before moving to London, where he joined an accounting school from which he eventually dropped out.<ref name=Businessweek />

==Career==
Jagtiani returned to Bahrain and took over his deceased brother's shop that he turned into a baby products shop (''Babyshop''). After 10 years working in the shop, Jagtiani started to expand by opening 6 new shops. When the [[Gulf War]] struck, he moved to Dubai where he founded the Landmark Group. Over the years, the Landmark Group expanded into fashion, electronics, furniture and budget hotels in Middle East and Southeast Asia.<ref name=Forbes /> It employs around 45000+ people and has more than 1000 stores across the [[Persian Gulf]] region, Middle East and India.<ref>[http://www.arabianbusiness.com/expat-powerlist/profile/1231?clr=2&sort=citizenship 19# Mukesh Jagtiani:50 most influential expatriates]{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} Arab Business.</ref>

In 2008, Jagtiani now a billionaire mall developer and the chairman, Landmark International, Dubai-based retail group, bought a 6% stake in the UK high-street retailer [[Debenhams]], and entered the ''[[Forbes]]'' list of billionaires,<ref name=Businessweek>{{cite news |title= CEOs Without College Degrees. |url=http://images.businessweek.com/ss/09/05/0522_no_college_ceos/11.htm |publisher=[[Business Week]] |date=April 5, 2009 }}</ref> and the 16th [[List of Indians by net worth|richest Indian]] with a net worth of USD 2 billion.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mukesh Ambani richest Indian in Forbes list |url=http://www.indianexpress.com/news/mukesh-ambani-richest-indian-in-forbes-list/385210/2 |publisher=[[Indian Express]]|date=Nov 13, 2008 }}</ref>

In 2014, he launched his [[family office]] with assets worth $5 billion.<ref>Reeba Zachariah, Boby Kurian, [https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-business/JM-Financial-top-gun-quits-to-launch-family-office-of-Micky-Jagtiani/articleshow/33257425.cms JM Financial top gun quits to launch family office of Micky Jagtiani], ''Indiatimes.com'', 5 April 2014</ref>

In May 2020, he had a net worth of US$2.1 billion, according to ''[[Forbes]]''.<ref name=Forbes/>

==Philanthropy==
In 2000, he established LIFE (Landmark International Foundation of Empowerment), to support underprivileged children in India, through school and healthcare development programmes, homes for destitute children, vocational and non-formal schools, community clinics and medical camps for slum-dwellers and an old age home [[Chennai]].<ref name=Forbes /><ref>{{cite news |title=Talking shop |url=http://www.busmanagementme.com/article/Talking-shop/ |publisher=Business Management }}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is married, with three children, and lives in Dubai, United Arab Emirates.<ref name=Forbes/>
