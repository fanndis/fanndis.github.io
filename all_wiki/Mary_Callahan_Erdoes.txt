'''Mary Callahan Erdoes''' (born August 13, 1967) is Chief Executive Officer of J.P. Morgan Asset & Wealth Management (a division of [[JPMorgan Chase]]), a global leader in investment management and private banking with $2.8 trillion<ref>https://seekingalpha.com/article/4187177-jpmorgan-chase-and-co-2018-q2-results-earnings-call-slides</ref> in client assets. She is also a member of JPMorgan Chase & Co.'s Operating Committee.

==Early life and education==
Mary Callahan was born on 13 August 1967 to Patricia and Patrick Callahan, Jr.. Patrick Callahan was a former partner at investment banking firm [[Lazard Freres]].<ref name="nysun.com">{{Cite news|url=http://www.nysun.com/business/difference-between-rich-and-wealthy/25541/|title=The Difference Between Rich and Wealthy|work=The New York Sun|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref> Callahan Erdoes was raised in [[Winnetka, Illinois]], an affluent [[North Shore (Chicago)|North Shore]] suburb of [[Chicago]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2005/07/24/business/yourmoney/she-does-the-math.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160313054054/http://www.nytimes.com/2005/07/24/business/yourmoney/she-does-the-math.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-03-13|title=She Does the Math - The New York Times|date=2016-03-13|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref><ref name="web.archive.org">{{Cite web|url=http://www.forbes.com/sites/nathanvardi/2011/08/24/the-1-trillion-woman/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151018184723/http://www.forbes.com/sites/nathanvardi/2011/08/24/the-1-trillion-woman/|url-status=dead|archive-date=2015-10-18|title=Mary Callahan Erdoes: Wall Street's $1 Trillion Woman|date=2015-10-18|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref> She was raised in a [[Roman Catholic]] family of [[Irish American|Irish]] descent.<ref name="web.archive.org"/> Callahan Erdoes is an alumna of the all-girls Roman Catholic [[Woodlands Academy of the Sacred Heart]] in [[Lake Forest, Illinois]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.woodlandsacademy.org/news/item/index.aspx?linkid=842&moduleid=52|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160313055114/http://www.woodlandsacademy.org/news/item/index.aspx?linkid=842&moduleid=52|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-03-13|title=Woodlands Academy: Woodlands Alumna Mary Callahan Erdoes '85 Once Again a Forbes Most Powerful Honoree|date=2016-03-13|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/suburbs/lake-county-news-sun/community/chi-ugc-article-woodlands-academy-awards-new-alumna-achieveme-2-2015-10-05-story.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160314000728/http://www.chicagotribune.com/suburbs/lake-county-news-sun/community/chi-ugc-article-woodlands-academy-awards-new-alumna-achieveme-2-2015-10-05-story.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-03-14|title=Woodlands Academy Awards New Alumna Achievement Honor - Lake County News-Sun|date=2016-03-14|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref> Erdoes completed her bachelor's degree at [[Georgetown University]], majoring in Mathematics. She was the only woman to complete a mathematics major at Georgetown at that time. She earned her MBA at [[Harvard Business School]].<ref name="nysun.com"/>

==Career==
Callahan Erdoes started her career with Stein Roe & Farnham, and described her maternal grandmother as instrumental for helping her get that job during college. She described her position there as a "glorified mailroom job".<ref name=nytimes/> She then moved on to [[Bankers Trust]], where she worked in corporate finance, merchant banking, and high-yield debt underwriting. Prior to joining J.P. Morgan, she was employed at Meredith, Martin & Kaye, a fixed-income specialty advisory firm, where she was responsible for credit research, trading, and individual portfolio management. In 1996, she joined J.P. Morgan Asset Management as head of fixed income for [[high-net-worth individual]]s, foundations, and endowments.<ref name=crains/> In March 2005, she was appointed CEO of [[JPMorgan Chase|J.P. Morgan Private Bank]].<ref name="nysun.com"/> She assumed her current post in September 2009. She has been mentioned as a potential successor to JPMorgan Chase & Co. CEO [[Jamie Dimon]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Keenan |first=Charles |url=http://www.americanbanker.com/usb_issues/120_10/mary-callahan-erdoes-1026130-1.html |title=#6 Mary Callahan Erdoes |publisher=American Banker and SourceMedia, Inc. |date=October 2010 |accessdate=23 March 2011}}</ref>

In 2012, Callahan Erdoes was included in the [[50 Most Influential (Bloomberg Markets ranking)|50 Most Influential]] list of ''[[Bloomberg Markets]]''. In March 2013, ''[[Business Insider]]'' included Callahan Erdoes on its list of the 25 most powerful women on Wall Street.<ref name="The 25 Most Powerful Women on Wall Street">{{cite web|title=The 25 Most Powerful Women on Wall Street|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/the-most-powerful-women-on-wall-street-2013-3 |accessdate=20 March 2013}}</ref> In 2016, Callahan Erdoes was named the 60th most powerful woman in the world by ''Forbes''.<ref name="Forbes14">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/2/#tab:overall|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|website=Forbes|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=3 June 2017}}</ref>

She is a board member of Robin Hood,<ref>https://www.robinhood.org/about-us/governance/</ref> the U.S. Fund for [[UNICEF]].<ref name=unicef/> and the U.S.-China Business Council.<ref>https://www.uschina.org/about/board-of-directors</ref> She also serves on the Federal Reserve Bank of New York's Investor Advisory Committee on Financial Markets.<ref>https://www.newyorkfed.org/aboutthefed/ag_financial_markets.html</ref>

==Personal life==
Callahan Erdoes met her husband Philip Erdoes at [[Harvard Business School]].  While Mrs Erdoes is Catholic, her husband is Jewish.  They live in [[New York City]] with their three daughters.<ref name=unicef>{{cite web |url=http://www.unicefusa.org/about/leadership/board/mary-callahan-erdoes.html |title=Mary Callahan Erdoes |accessdate=23 March 2011}}</ref>

Callahan Erdoes is a major [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] donor and fundraiser. She contributed to the presidential campaigns of [[John McCain]] and [[Mitt Romney]] in 2008 and 2012, respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2011-11-29/romney-best-chance-to-beat-obama-jpmorgan-fundraiser|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160314000457/https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2011-11-29/romney-best-chance-to-beat-obama-jpmorgan-fundraiser|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-03-14|title=JPMorgan Executives Plan Romney New York Fundraiser Next Month - Bloomberg Business|date=2016-03-14|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref>
