{{main|2016 Wimbledon Championships}}

[[Serena Williams]] was the defending champion and successfully defended her title, defeating [[Angelique Kerber]] in the final, 7–5, 6–3 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2016 Wimbledon Championships. By winning her seventh Wimbledon title, Williams equaled [[Steffi Graf]]'s [[Open Era]] record of 22 major singles titles.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/36755299|title=Wimbledon 2016: Serena Williams beats Angelique Kerber to win 22nd Grand Slam|date=9 July 2016|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=11 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title= Wimbledon 2016: Serena Williams beats Kerber in final to equal Graf record |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/jul/09/serena-williams-angelique-kerber-wimbledon-womens-final-report|date=9 July 2016|accessdate=9 July 2016|first=Simon|last=Cambers|work=The Guardian}}</ref> This was also the first time two women contested two major finals against one another in a single season since [[Amélie Mauresmo]] and [[Justine Henin|Justine Henin-Hardenne]] met in the 2006 [[2006 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]] and [[2006 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]] finals.<ref>{{cite news|title= Serena Williams-Angelique Kerber Final Is Grand Slam Rematch |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/07/08/sports/tennis/serena-williams-angelique-kerber-final-is-grand-slam-rematch.html?ref=sports&_r=0|date=7 July 2016|accessdate=9 July 2016|first=Christopher|last=Clarey|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2016/jul/09/serena-williams-v-angelique-kerber-wimbledon-womens-final-live|title=Serena Williams beats Kerber in Wimbledon women's final – as it happened|date=9 July 2016|work=Guardian|accessdate=11 July 2016}}</ref> Williams dropped just one set the entire tournament, to [[Christina McHale]] in the second round.
 
In addition to Williams and Kerber, [[Garbiñe Muguruza]], [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] and [[Simona Halep]] were also in contention for the world [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|WTA no. 1 ranking]] at the beginning of the tournament. Williams retained the top spot by reaching the fourth round and eventually winning the title. She also spent her 300th week at the top of the WTA rankings during the second week of the tournament, and won her 300th Slam match at the tournament by defeating [[Annika Beck]] in the third round.

At 36 years old, [[Venus Williams]] became the oldest woman to reach a Grand Slam semifinal since [[Martina Navratilova]] at the [[1994 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1994 Wimbledon Championships]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Wimbledon 2016: Venus & Serena Williams through to semi-finals|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/36716595|date=5 July 2016|accessdate=5 July 2016|first=Piers|last=Newbery|work=BBC Sport|publisher=BBC}}</ref>
