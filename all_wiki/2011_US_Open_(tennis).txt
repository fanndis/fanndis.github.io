The '''2011 US Open''' was a [[tennis]] tournament played on the [[Tennis court#Hard courts|outdoor hard courts]] at the [[Billie Jean King National Tennis Center]] in [[Flushing Meadows]] Park, of [[Queens]], New York City, United States. It began on 29 August and was originally scheduled to end on 11 September, but the men's final was postponed to 12 September due to rain.

[[Rafael Nadal]] and [[Kim Clijsters]] were the defending champions. Due to an abdominal muscle injury, Clijsters opted not to defend her title.

In the women's singles, Australia's [[Samantha Stosur]] defeated [[Serena Williams]] in straight sets 6–2, 6–3 for her first Grand Slam title. Stosur thus became the first Australian female player to win a Grand Slam since [[Evonne Goolagong Cawley]] in 1980.

In the men's singles, both Nadal and [[Novak Djokovic]] played the final for the second consecutive year. This time, Djokovic won 6–2, 6–4, 6–7<sup>(3–7)</sup>, 6–1 for his first US Open title.

===Prize money===
The USTA announced that the 2011 US Open purse has increased by more than one million dollars to reach a record $23.7 million. In addition to the base purse of $23.7 million, the top three men's and top three women's finishers in the Olympus US Open Series may earn up to an additional $2.6 million in bonus prize money at the US Open, providing a potential total payout of $26.3 million. ==Player(s) of the Day==
*Day 1: {{flagicon|USA}} [[Madison Keys]] – The 16-year-old Keys defeated her compatriot and elder by 21 years, [[Jill Craybas]] on the Grandstand with a 6–2, 6–4 score in their first-round encounter, giving the Florida native her first-ever win at the US Open.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 1 |work=usopen.org |url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |date=2011-08-29 |accessdate=2011-08-29 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110817142056/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |archivedate=2011-08-17 }}</ref>
*Day 2: {{flagicon|ROU}} [[Simona Halep]] – Halep, the 19-year-old world number 53, defeated the reigning [[2011 French Open|French Open]] champion and sixth seed [[Li Na]] at Louis Armstrong Stadium with a 6–2, 7–5 score in their first round encounter.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 2|work=usopen.org|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/2011-08-30/201108301314748232544.html|date=2011-08-30|accessdate=2011-08-31|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110913191523/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/2011-08-30/201108301314748232544.html|archive-date=2011-09-13|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*Day 3: {{flagicon|FRA}} [[Julien Benneteau]] – The French wild-card upset tenth-seeded Spaniard [[Nicolás Almagro]] in straight sets.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 3|work=usopen.org|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/2011-08-31/201108311314832542892.html|date=2011-08-31|accessdate=2011-09-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110913191526/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/2011-08-31/201108311314832542892.html|archive-date=2011-09-13|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*Day 4: {{flagicon|ESP}} [[Juan Carlos Ferrero]] – The unseeded Ferrero, an experienced ATP Tour player and the second oldest man in the draw at 31 years old, defeated seventh-seeded [[Gaël Monfils]] in an extraordinary five-set match to advance into the third round.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 4 |work=usopen.org |url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |date=2011-09-01 |accessdate=2011-09-02 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110913190920/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |archivedate=2011-09-13 }}</ref>
*Day 5: {{flagicon|ITA}} [[Flavia Pennetta]] – The twenty-sixth seed, two-time quarterfinalist, and former world number 10 upset third seed, 2006 US Open champion, and former world number 1 [[Maria Sharapova]] in three sets, 6–3, 3–6, 6–4, to advance to the fourth round.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 5 |work=usopen.org |url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |accessdate=2011-09-03 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110913190920/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |archivedate=2011-09-13 }}</ref>
*Day 6: {{flagicon|ITA}} [[Francesca Schiavone]] – The 31-year-old seventh seed saved a match point in her battle against world number 81 [[Chanelle Scheepers]], and went on to win, 5–7, 7–6, 6–3, advancing to the fourth round.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 6 |work=usopen.org |url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |accessdate=2011-09-04 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110913190920/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/index.html?promo=subnav |archivedate=2011-09-13 }}</ref>
*Day 7: {{flagicon|USA}} [[Donald Young (tennis)|Donald Young]] – The 22-year-old [[United States of America|American]] wildcard upset twenty-fourth seeded veteran [[Juan Ignacio Chela]] in the third round in straight sets with a score of 7–5, 6–4, 6–3. He made it to the fourth round of a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] for the first time in his career after upsetting Chela and the fourteenth seeded [[Stanislas Wawrinka]] in the second round.<ref>{{cite web|title=Player of the Day: Day 7|work=usopen.org|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/2011-09-04/201109051315181342813.html|accessdate=2011-09-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110913191528/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/playerday/2011-09-04/201109051315181342813.html|archive-date=2011-09-13|url-status=dead}}</ref>
