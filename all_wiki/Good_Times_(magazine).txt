'''''Good Times Magazine'''''<ref name=ArbitraryNameForSource1>{{cite web |url=http://www.manta.com/c/mmcvwnh/good-times-magazine |title=Good Times Magazine Company Profile|accessdate=February 14, 2013}}</ref> is a music and entertainment newspaper in [[Long Island]], New York, USA. Founded in 1969 by [[Richard Branciforte]]<ref name=ArbitraryNameForSource2>{{cite web |url=http://libn.com/2009/04/17/print-first-model-keeps-good-times-magazine-humming |title=Print-first model keeps Good Times magazine humming|date=April 17, 2009}}</ref> in an effort to get free tickets to [[Woodstock]], the paper became the Long Island musician's bible in the 1970s and 1980s, publishing interviews with [[Bruce Springsteen]] and [[Duane Allman]] among others. Good Times Magazine is America's oldest regional entertainment newspaper.<ref name=ArbitraryNameForSource4>{{cite web |url=http://blog.cubreporters.org/2011/02/writer-jobs-long-island-entertainment.html |title=Long Island entertainment publication |date=February 7, 2011}}</ref>

Good Times has primarily focused on the Long Island<ref name=ArbitraryNameForSource3>{{cite web |url=http://www.longislandexchange.com/business/magazines.html |title=Long Island Magazines |accessdate=February 14, 2013}}</ref> scene, publishing interviews and reviews of local musicians.

Good Times alumni include writers such as MTV's [[Kurt Loder]], [[Rolling Stone]]'s [[David Fricke]] and [[Entertainment Weekly]]'s [[Leonard Maltin]]. Their current columns and columnists are The Sports Beat with Lloyd Carroll, The Dungeon (Heavy Metal) with Dave Wolff and Rhythm Tracking with Jimi LaLumia.

In 2006, Good Times re-launched their website and an interactive discussion board known as the Good Times Music Blog.
