'''Harmondsworth Immigration Removal Centre''' is an [[immigration detention]] facility in [[Harmondsworth]], [[London Borough of Hillingdon]], near [[London Heathrow Airport]] run by  [[Mitie]]. Harmondsworth, which neighbours the [[Colnbrook Immigration Removal Centre]], holds around 620 men.<ref>"[http://www.bia.homeoffice.gov.uk/managingborders/immigrationremovalcentres/harmondsworth Harmondsworth Immigration Removal Centre] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081211023713/http://www.bia.homeoffice.gov.uk/managingborders/immigrationremovalcentres/harmondsworth |date=11 December 2008 }}." [[UK Border Agency]]. ==Inspections==
In 2006, [[Anne Owers]] HM Inspector of Prisons (HMIP) reported that Harmondsworth "had been allowed to slip into a culture and approach which was wholly at odds with its stated purpose" further "It is essentially a problem of management, and it is of some concern that this had not been fully identified and resolved earlier by the contractor and the [[Immigration and Nationality Directorate]]."{{Citation needed|date=February 2010}}

In January 2008, the Chief Inspector and her team conducted an unannounced inspection and found significant improvements had been implemented since the last inspection. The Inspection of January 2008 indicated the change programme that was taking place. An extract from the executive summary of the Chief Inspector of Prisons states: ‘’All those met – both staff and detainees – recognised the change that had taken place under the new management. This was confirmed by the evidence of the inspection, which showed improvement in all of our four key areas – safety, respect, purposeful activity and resettlement – though there was still some way to go to ensure that those changes were firmly embedded and could withstand the expansion of the centre to full capacity. [..]  We observed a different attitude and approach from staff. Security in the centre was much less intrusive, and support for detainees in the early days had improved noticeably [...]"{{Citation needed|date=February 2010}}

Overall, this inspection recorded significant improvement in a centre that has long been of concern to us and the detention authorities.  The build-up to reopening had provided space to work with staff to change the culture and approach of the centre.  However, managers were aware, and this inspection confirmed, that there was still more to do before the centre expanded to full capacity.  Negative staff attitudes had been effectively challenged…{{Citation needed|date=February 2010}}

==Deaths and hunger-strikes==
Several detainees have committed [[suicide]] while held in Harmondsworth. Robertas Grabys was found hanged in 2000,<ref>[http://www.freemovement.org.uk/Resources/selfharm.html 58 asylum seekers/migrants in the UK, have taken their own lives]{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} freemovement.org.uk, 2010.</ref><ref name="irr">[http://www.irr.org.uk/2006/january/ha000020.html Detainees punished for protesting after suicide] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101102231854/http://www.irr.org.uk/2006/january/ha000020.html |date=2 November 2010 }} independent race and refugee news network, 2006.</ref> Sergey Barnuyck was found hanged in 2004,<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070928152722/http://www.ncadc.org.uk/archives/filed%20newszines/oldnewszines/newszine52/sergy.html Remember Sergey Baranuyck 1973-2004] ncadc.org, 2007. Hosted on [[Wayback machine]].</ref><ref>[http://www.indymedia.org.uk/en/2010/04/449438.html Remember the dead - Justice for the Living] INDYMEDIA UK, 2010.</ref> and Bereket Yohannes was found hanged in 2006.<ref name="irr"/><ref>[http://www.ncadc.org.uk/archives/filed%20newszines/oldnewszines/Old%2051-100/newszine66/bereket.html In Memory of Bereket Yohannes]{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} ncadc.org, 2009.</ref> An 84-year-old Canadian with [[Alzheimer's disease]], Alois Dvorzac, died there in January 2014.<ref>Paraic O'Brien, [http://www.channel4.com/news/left-to-die-in-british-detention-who-was-alois-dvorzac Left to die in British detention: who was Alois Dvorzac?], ''[[Channel 4 News]]'', 18 March 2014.</ref>

The Nigerian asylum-seeker [[Ifa Muaza]] went on [[hunger strike]] in 2013.<ref name="guardian">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2013/nov/19/asylum-seeker-hunger-strike-stay-custody-death-nigeria-muaza|title=Dying asylum seeker on hunger strike must stay in custody, says high court|date=19 November 2013|accessdate=21 November 2013|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> In April 2015, over 200 detainees joined in a collective hunger-strike, which subsequently spread to [[Morton Hall Immigration Removal Centre]].<ref>Chris Green, [https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/harmondsworth-asylum-seekers-hunger-strike-spreads-to-second-centre-10099111.html Harmondsworth: Asylum seekers' hunger strike spreads to second centre], ''The Independent'', 7 April 2015.</ref>
