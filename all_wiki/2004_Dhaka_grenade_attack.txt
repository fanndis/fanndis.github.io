The '''2004 Dhaka grenade attack''' took place at an anti-terrorism rally organised by [[Bangladesh Awami League|Awami League]] on Bangabandhu Avenue on 21 August 2004. The attack left 24 dead and more than 300 injured. The attack was carried out at 5.22 PM after [[Sheikh Hasina]] the leader of opposition finished addressing a crowd of 20,000 people from the back of a truck.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bhattacharjee |first1=Partha |date=21 August 2014 |title=Back from death, only to suffer |url=http://www.thedailystar.net/back-from-death-only-to-suffer-38058 |newspaper=The Daily Star |access-date=20 September 2014}}</ref> The attacks targeted Awami League president Sheikh Hasina. Hasina was injured in the attack.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sultan |first1=Tipu |last2=Karmakar |first2=Prasanta |title=10 years of 21 August grenade attack |url=http://en.prothom-alo.com/bangladesh/news/52307/Ten-years-since-the-21-August-grenade-atta  |url-status=dead|newspaper=Prothom Alo |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141114145452/http://en.prothom-alo.com/bangladesh/news/52307/Ten-years-since-the-21-August-grenade-atta |archive-date=14 November 2014 |access-date=9 July 2015}}</ref>

==Events and casualties==
Awami League chief Sheikh Hasina had been speaking at a public meeting on Bangabandhu Avenue, protesting blasts against the party's workers in [[Sylhet]]. The rally drew a crowd of 20,000 people.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Adiga |first=Aravind |date=30 August 2004 |title=Bangladesh: A democracy is shaken |url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,501040906-689488,00.html |magazine=Time |access-date=20 March 2013}}</ref> As Hasina finished her speech, a total of 13 grenades were thrown into the crowd from the rooftops of nearby buildings, killing at least 16 people on spot, later the death toll reached 24. The blast left more than 500 injured. Among the dead were Hasina's bodyguard, Mahbubur Rahman and Awami League Women's Affairs Secretary [[Ivy Rahman]], who died from her injuries three days later.<ref>{{cite news |title=End of a fight - Ivy dies from her wounds after 58 hours |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2004/08/25/d4082501011.htm |newspaper=The Daily Star |date=5 August 2004}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2006/08/21/d60821011310.htm |title=Hasina says govt didn't carry out proper probe |work=The Daily Star |date= |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref>

=== Reaction ===
The Awami League called for a nationwide [[hartal]] on 23 and 24 August 2004 following the incident. Khaleda Zia, then [[Prime Minister of Bangladesh]] condemned the attacks, and also vowed a thorough probe to catch the culprits.<ref>{{cite news |title=Bangladesh awakes in shock as blast toll hits 18 |url=http://www.theage.com.au/articles/2004/08/22/1093113031418.html?from=storylhs |newspaper=The Age}}</ref> An intercity train was burned down by Awami League activists on strike in [[Bhairab Upazila|Bhairab]]. Awami Leagues activists also organized protests in Chittagong and furled black flags at the sight of the attack. A funeral service for the victims at [[Baitul Mukarram National Mosque|Baitul Mokarram National Mosque]] was attended by 20 thousand people.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2004/08/23/d4082301011.htm |title=Passenger train torched at Bhairab; AL workers fight with cops |work=The Daily Star |date= |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref> Protests in Dhaka were attacked by members of Bangladesh Police and [[Jatiyatabadi Sramik Dal]], the workers wing of Bangladesh Nationalist Party. The Jatiyatabadi Sramik Dal activists also attacked and injured six members of the press.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2004/08/24/d4082401011.htm |title=Cops, BNP men attack protest processions |work=The Daily Star |date= |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref>

President of the United States, [[George W. Bush]], expressed "shock" at the attack and conveyed his message to Prime Minister Khaleda Zia and Opposition leader Sheikh Hasina through the Secretary of State of the United States, [[Colin Powell]]. The attack was also condemned by the United Kingdom, Royal Netherlands, Denmark, France, Germany, Italy, and Sweden.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2004/08/24/d4082401022.htm |title=Powell phones Khaleda,Hasina; Straw slates attack; envoys visit AL president |work=The Daily Star |date= |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref>

==Investigation==

===Initial investigation===
Bangladesh Police refused to register any criminal case filed by Bangladesh Awami League over the attack and only registered a general diary.<ref name=":1" /> The government initially refused to hand over the bodies of the victims.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2004/08/23/d4082301022.htm |title='I heard a bang and blood splashed on my body' |work=The Daily Star |date= |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref> Investigators from the Federal Bureau of Investigations and Interpol made repeated visits to Bangladesh to provide technical support.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news |url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2005/08/21/d5082101033.htm |title=One Year into Aug 21 Carnage |last=Manik |first=Julfikar Ali |last2=Ashraf |first2=Shamim |date= |work=The Daily Star |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref> The Government also tried to implicate Mokhlesur Rahman, an Awami League activists, and Shaibal Saha Partha. They were arrested by Bangladesh Police.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.voabangla.com/a/a-16-a-2004-09-01-1-fbi-94349234/1385284.html |title=FBI in Dhaka to probe grenade attacks - 2004-09-01 |website=VoA |language=bn |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref> Shaibal Saha Partha and Joj Miah were tortured in custody and forced to give a false confessional statement.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thedailystar.net/frontpage/testimony-terror-sponsored-state-130054 |title=Testimony to terror sponsored by state |date=21 August 2015 |work=The Daily Star |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref> An investigation by the [[Supreme Court Bar Association (Bangladesh)|Supreme Court Bar Association]] accused the government of destroying evidence. The government was also criticised for hurriedly burying two unidentified dead bodies from the terror attack in the middle of the night.<ref name=":2" />

In 2004, the Bangladesh Nationalist Party-led alliance government assigned Crime Investigation Department of Police to foresee the investigation. They came up with a story that some Joj Miah, also known as Jamal Ahmed from Noakhali district, along with 14 other criminals of Seven Star terrorist group of Subrata Bain attacked the Awami League rally. They met at Moghbazar before the attack, and rehearsed at a remote island before the attack.<ref>{{cite news |last=|first=|title=Joj Mia gives deposition|url=https://en.prothomalo.com/bangladesh/news/54443/Joj-Mia-gives-deposition |work=Prothom Alo |access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref> The government of Bangladesh formed a one-man judicial probe led by Justice Joynul Abedin. The Awami League rejected the commission which blamed the attack on a neighboring country. ''The Daily Star'' described Abedin as a "shame" for the judiciary in Bangladesh.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.thedailystar.net/frontpage/news/one-man-shame-judiciary-1788154 |title=One man shame for judiciary |date=21 August 2019 |work=The Daily Star |access-date=22 August 2019}}</ref>

On 26 June 2005 Joj Mia, a petty criminal, confessed his involvement in the crime under section 164, to the magistrate. The story collapsed following investigative journalism who discovered holes in the official story.<ref name=":0" />

===Fresh investigation===
In 2007, after the military-backed government assumed office, many of the BNP and Awami League leaders were rounded up by the government agencies, and fresh investigation into the case was launched.

After almost one year, in November 2007, Mufti Hannan, a militant leader from [[Gopalganj District, Bangladesh|Gopalganj]] who was arrested by the BNP-led government in 2005, revealed that the attack was operated by the militant outfit Harkat-ul-Jihad-al-Islami of which he was a leader. He also admitted that he got support from Maulana Tazuddin, brother of BNP leader and former deputy minister Abdus Salam Pintu while coordinating the attack.<ref name="bangladeshnews.com.bd">{{cite news|url=http://www.bangladeshnews.com.bd/2007/11/02/huji-boss-hannan-admits-to-aug-21-grenade-attack/ |url-status=dead |title=Huji boss Hannan admits to Aug 21 grenade attack|newspaper=Bangladesh News|date=2 November 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130316064037/http://www.bangladeshnews.com.bd/2007/11/02/huji-boss-hannan-admits-to-aug-21-grenade-attack/ |archive-date=16 March 2013 |access-date=20 March 2013}}</ref>

According to his statement, Abdus Salam Pintu had knowledge of the attack.

In 2008, after the detailed investigation, the then CID high official [[Mohammad Javed Patwary]] concluded that the attack was aimed at killing [[Sheikh Hasina]] and was guided by the common grievance of both Mufti Hannan and [[Abdus Salam Pintu]] against [[Sheikh Hasina]] for her role in "subduing" Islam. The investigation report mentioned that, Abdus Salam Pintu was personally responsible for the attack.

===Further investigation===

In 2009, Awami League came to office and decided to launch a further investigation into the incident and appointed a retired CID official Abdul Kahar Akhand as the person in charge.

In the same year, Abdul Majed Bhat alias Yusuf Bhat gave a confessional statement with the details regarding the source of grenade used in the attack. He claimed that Muzaffar Ahmad Shah of Tehrik-e-Jihad Islami (TEJI) gave the grenades to Maulana Tajuddin to send those to Indian militant groups. Tajuddin, instead of sending those to India, kept those with him. According to Yusuf Bhat, these grenades were later handed over to Mufti Hannan to carry out the attack.<ref>{{cite news |last=Dipu |first=Sarwar |title=Pakistani militant Yusuf Bhat's 'Bangladesh Mission' |url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/militancy/2018/08/23/pakistani-militant-yusuf-bhat-s-bangladesh-mission |work=Dhaka Tribune |access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

After two years, in 2011, Mufti Hannan gave another confessional statement implicating many big names, mostly BNP leaders and some former officials of the government including the son of opposition leader and former prime minister [[Khaleda Zia]], [[Tarique Rahman]], former deputy minister [[Abdus Salam Pintu]], former member of parliament [[Kazi Shah Mofazzal Hossain Kaikobad]] and some officials of the Home Ministry, [[Bangladesh Police|police]], [[Directorate General of Forces Intelligence]] (DGFI), [[National Security Intelligence]] (NSI) and Prime Minister's Office (PMO) with involvement in the planning of the bombing.

In the statement, Mufti Hannan claimed that the attack was aimed at destroying the top leadership of Awami League including Sheikh Hasina, and BNP leader Tarique Rahman along with Jamaat leader Ali Ahsan Mohammad Mujahid and the then Home Minister Lutfuzzaman Babor assured them with government support.

==Perpetrators==
[[Harkat-ul-Jihad-al-Islami]] (HUJI) leader [[Mufti Abdul Hannan]] was arrested on 30 September 2005 for the grenade attacks,<ref>{{cite news |title=Mufti Hannan arrested |url=http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2005/09/30/mufti-hannan-arrested-1st-ld |newspaper=bdnews24.com |access-date=21 June 2016}}</ref> and was later charged in connection with it. He reportedly confessed to the attacks in November 2007.<ref name="bangladeshnews.com.bd"/> He was sentenced to death in December 2008 for attempting to kill [[Anwar Choudhury]] in 2004.<ref>{{cite news|author=Uttam Khan|url=http://news.theage.com.au/world/three-bangladeshis-to-hang-for-attack-on-british-envoy-20081223-74dm.html|title=Three Bangladeshis to hang for attack on British envoy|newspaper=The Age|date=23 December 2008|access-date=20 March 2013}}</ref> In March 2012, the son of opposition leader and former prime minister [[Khaleda Zia]], [[Tarique Rahman]], and 28 others were tried in absentia for their alleged involvement in the attack.<ref name=deccan>{{cite news |title=Dhaka court indicts ex-premier's son for 2004 grenade attack |url=http://www.deccanherald.com/content/235430/dhaka-court-indicts-ex-premiers.html |newspaper=Deccan Herald |date=18 March 2012 |access-date=22 March 2013}}</ref> The supplementary charge sheets charges [[HuJI|Huji]], influential leaders of the [[Bangladesh Nationalist Party]] (BNP) and the [[Jamaat-e-Islami Bangladesh|Jamaat]], including former deputy minister [[Abdus Salam Pintu]], former member of parliament [[Kazi Shah Mofazzal Hossain Kaikobad]] and some officials of the Home Ministry, police, DGFI, NSI and PMO with involvement in the planning of the bombing.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tipu |first1=Md Sanaul Islam |title=Prosecutor: AL grenade attack cases may see verdict by December |url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/2015/jul/04/prosecutor-al-grenade-attack-cases-may-see-verdict-december |work=Dhaka Tribune |access-date=9 July 2015}}</ref>

Jamal Ahmed, also known as Joj Mia was coerced into giving a false confession. He was forced to implicate Seven-Star Group, led by Subrata Bain through torture by security forces during BNP rule.<ref>{{cite news |title=Joj Mia gives deposition |url=http://en.prothom-alo.com/bangladesh/news/54443/Joj-Mia-gives-deposition |newspaper=Prothom Alo |access-date=9 July 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141205025820/http://en.prothom-alo.com/bangladesh/news/54443/Joj-Mia-gives-deposition |archive-date=5 December 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Charges and punishments==
On 10  October 2018, a special court, Speedy Trial Tribunal-1, delivered verdicts in relevant cases and accused 49 persons in total.<ref name=unb>{{Cite news |url=http://www.unb.com.bd/category/Bangladesh/verdict-in-aug-21-grenade-attack-cases-babar-salam-among-31-accused-taken-to-court/3929|title=Verdict in Aug 21 grenade attack cases: Babar, Salam among 31 accused taken to court|work=United News of Bangladesh|access-date=15 October 2018 |date=10 October 2018}}</ref><ref name=verdict>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thedailystar.net/august-21-carnage/21-august-grenade-attack-verdict-tarique-rahman-awarded-life-1645090|title=August 21 attack: 'State-backed crime' punished|date=11 October 2018|work=The Daily Star|access-date=14 October 2018 |author1=Shakhawat Liton|author2=Chaitanya Chandra Halder|author3=Wasim Bin Habib|author4=Tuhin Shubhra Adhikary}}</ref> It ruled the grenade attack "was a well-orchestrated plan, executed through abuse of state power".<ref name=verdict/> Judge Shahed Nuruddin said, "The specialised deadly Arges grenades that are used in wars were blasted at the Awami League's central office on 23 Bangabandhu Avenue in broad daylight with the help of the then state machinery".<ref name=verdict/> On charges of killing through common intention, planning and criminal conspiracy, 38 persons were found guilty.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2018/10/10/grenade-attack-in-2004-was-designed-to-obliterate-awami-league-says-judge|title=Grenade attack in 2004 was designed to 'obliterate' Awami League, says judge|access-date=14 October 2018|date=10 October 2018|work=bdnews24.com}}</ref> 19 of them were sentenced to death:
