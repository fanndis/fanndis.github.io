The '''National Order of Merit''' (Spanish: '''''Orden Nacional al Mérito''''') of [[Paraguay]] is an award given by the government of Paraguay. The [[award]] was first established in 1865 and can be given to any person or organization that has contributed in some way to the benefit of Paraguay.

== About ==
The National Order of Merit is given to a person or an organization who has contributed to the benefit of [[Paraguay]] in various fields of endeavor.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=21 November 2019|title=JICA receives a National Order of Merit from the Government of Paraguay: Contribution to the development of Paraguay spans over 40 years|url=https://www.jica.go.jp/english/news/press/2019/20191121_31.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-06-24|website=JICA}}</ref> This can include through [[culture]], leadership, and science, among others.<ref name=":1" />

The medal was designed as a five-pointed star with the words "''honor et gloria''" on the front and "''premium meriti''" on the reverse.<ref name=":0" /> 

== History ==
The National Order of Merit was established on April 8, 1865 by [[Francisco Solano López]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last=Gillingham|first=Harrold E.|url=https://archive.org/details/southamericandec0000harr/page/n5/mode/2up?q=national+order+of+merit|title=South American Decorations and War Medals|publisher=The American Numismatic Society|others=Lenard L. Babin|year=1970|isbn=|edition=Reprint|location=New York|pages=136-137|via=Internet Archive}}</ref> In 1867, López declared that women were eligible to earn the award.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Saeger|first=James Schofield|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ajLpDyIbcdIC&newbks=0&printsec=frontcover&pg=PA132&dq=%22national+order+of+merit%22+paraguay&hl=en|title=Francisco Solano López and the Ruination of Paraguay: Honor and Egocentrism|publisher=Rowman & Littlefield|year=2007|isbn=978-0-7425-3755-2|location=Lanham|pages=161|language=en}}</ref> The award was based on the [[Legion of Honour|French Legion of Honor]] and had several different levels.<ref name=":2">{{Cite book|last=Washburn|first=Charles Ames|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=h3cYAAAAYAAJ&newbks=0&printsec=frontcover&pg=PA45&dq=%22national+order+of+merit%22+paraguay&hl=en|title=The History of Paraguay: With Notes of Personal Observations, and Reminiscences of Diplomacy Under Difficulties|publisher=Lee & Shepard, Publishishers|year=1871|isbn=|volume=2|location=Boston|pages=44-45|language=en}}</ref> The highest level was the Grand Cross.<ref name=":2" /> The other levels were Knight, Official, Commander and Grand Official.<ref name=":2" />

On September 7, 1956, Law No. 394 was passed with several provisions regarding the awarding of the National Order of Merit.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=16 January 2016|title=Insfrán recibirá en Paraguay la condecoración de la “Orden Nacional al Mérito” por apoyo a inundados – NF|url=https://www.noticiasformosa.com.ar/2016/01/16/insfran-recibira-en-paraguay-la-condecoracion-de-la-orden-nacional-al-merito-por-apoyo-a-inundados/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-06-24|website=Noticias Formosa|language=es}}</ref>
