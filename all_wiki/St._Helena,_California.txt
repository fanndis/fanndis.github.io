'''St. Helena''' ({{IPAc-en|h|ə|ˈ|l|iː|n|ə}} {{respell|hə|LEE|nə}}; [[Wappo language|Wappo]]: '''Anakotanoma''', "Bull Snake Village")<ref name="AutoY4-7"/> is a city in [[Napa County, California|Napa County]], in [[California]]'s [[Wine Country (California)|Wine Country]], part of the [[North Bay (San Francisco Bay Area)|North Bay region]] of the [[San Francisco Bay Area]]. The population was 5,814 at the [[2010 United States Census|2010 census]].

The city is the center of [[St. Helena AVA|the eponymous American Viticultural Area]] which includes 416 vineyards encompassing {{convert|6800|acre}} of vineyards. St. Helena is the location of [[The Culinary Institute of America at Greystone]] and a campus of [[Napa Valley College]].

==History==
[[Ellen G. White|Ellen White]], co-founder of the [[Seventh-day Adventist Church]], had a home called [[Elmshaven]] near St. Helena, beginning in 1900. She died there in 1915, and the site is now a [[National Historic Landmark]]. Both the [[Beringer Vineyards]] and the [[Charles Krug Winery]] are [[California Historical Landmark]]s.<ref name="CHL"/>

St. Helena's community center was built as a [[Carnegie library]]; it served as the city library from 1908 to 1978.<ref name="AutoY4-8"/>

==Geography==
St. Helena has a total area of {{convert|5.03|sqmi|km2|1|abbr=on}}, of which {{convert|4.99|sqmi|km2|1|abbr=on}} is land and {{convert|0.11|sqmi|km2|1|abbr=on}} (0.81%) is water.

===Climate===
The [[National Weather Service]] has a cooperative weather station in St. Helena. Winters are cool and wet, while summers have hot days and cool nights with little precipitation. Average temperatures in December, the coldest month, range from {{convert|57|°F|°C|abbr=on}} to {{convert|38|°F|°C|abbr=on}}. Average temperatures in July and August, the warmest months, range from {{convert|89|°F|°C|abbr=on}} to {{convert|55|°F|°C|abbr=on}}. There are an average of 55.7 days with highs of 90&nbsp;°F (32&nbsp;°C) or higher and an average of 34.7 days with lows of 32&nbsp;°F (0&nbsp;°C) or lower. The record high temperature was {{convert|115|°F|°C|abbr=on}} on July 13, 1972, and the record low temperature was {{convert|11|°F|°C|abbr=on}} on December 11, 1932.

Average annual precipitation is {{convert|36.64|in|mm}}. There are an average of 68 days with measurable precipitation. The wettest year was 1983 with {{convert|75.40|in|mm}} and the driest year was 1976 with {{convert|10.41|in|mm}}. The wettest month on record was February 1986 with {{convert|25.60|in|mm}}. The most rainfall in 24 hours was {{convert|6.83|in|mm}} on January 21, 1967. Although snow rarely falls in St. Helena, there is an annual average snowfall of {{convert|0.2|in|mm|adj=on}}. The most snowfall in one month was {{convert|4.0|in|mm}}, recorded in January 1974 and again in March 1976. The most snowfall in 24 hours was {{convert|4.0|in|mm}} on March 2, 1976.<ref name="AutoY4-9"/>

The [[2010 United States Census]]<ref name="AutoY4-11"/> reported that St. Helena had a population of 5,814. The [[population density]] was 1,156.7 people per square mile (446.6/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of St. Helena was 4,525 (77.8%) [[White (U.S. Census)|White]], 25 (0.4%) [[African American (U.S. Census)|African American]], 35 (0.6%) [[Native American (U.S. Census)|Native American]], 98 (1.7%) [[Asian (U.S. Census)|Asian]], 9 (0.2%) [[Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)|Pacific Islander]], 978 (16.8%) from [[Race (United States Census)|other races]], and 144 (2.5%) from two or more races. [[Hispanic (U.S. Census)|Hispanic]] or [[Latino (U.S. Census)|Latino]] of any race were 1,914 persons (32.9%).

The Census reported that 98.3% of the population lived in households and 1.7% lived in non-institutionalized group quarters.

There were 2,401 households, out of which 694 (28.9%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 1,118 (46.6%) were [[marriage|opposite-sex married couples]] living together, 223 (9.3%) had a female householder with no husband present, 99 (4.1%) had a male householder with no wife present. There were 96 (4.0%) [[POSSLQ|unmarried opposite-sex partnerships]], and 20 (0.8%) [[same-sex partnerships|same-sex married couples or partnerships]]. 805 households (33.5%) were made up of individuals, and 411 (17.1%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.38. There were 1,440 [[family (U.S. Census)|families]] (60.0% of all households); the average family size was 3.03.

The population was spread out, with 1,280 people (22.0%) under the age of 18, 453 people (7.8%) aged 18 to 24, 1,333 people (22.9%) aged 25 to 44, 1,627 people (28.0%) aged 45 to 64, and 1,121 people (19.3%) who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 42.9 years. For every 100 females, there were 88.2 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 84.9 males.

There were 2,776 housing units at an average density of 552.3 per square mile (213.2/km<sup>2</sup>), of which 55.4% were owner-occupied and 44.6% were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 2.7%; the rental vacancy rate was 5.8%. 51.4% of the population lived in owner-occupied housing units and 46.9% lived in rental housing units.

==Government==
St. Helena is a [[general law city]] which lacks its own charter. It operates under a [[Council–manager government|council–manager]] form of government.<ref name=about/> In the [[California State Legislature]], St. Helena is in {{Representative|casd|3|fmt=sdistrict}},<ref name="AutoY4-12"/> and in {{Representative|caad|4|fmt=adistrict}}.<ref name="AutoY4-13"/> In the [[United States House of Representatives]], St. Helena is in {{Representative|cacd|5|fmt=district}}.<ref name="AutoY4-14"/>

==Education==
[[Saint Helena Unified School District]] is the local school district. [[St. Helena High School]] serves students in grades 9-12.

The city has two tertiary campuses, one is the Upper Valley Campus of [[Napa Valley College]]; the other is [[The Culinary Institute of America at Greystone]], a branch campus of the main institution in [[Hyde Park, New York]].

==Economy==
Major employers in St. Helena include [[Trinchero Family Estates]], [[Beringer Vineyards]], and [[The Culinary Institute of America at Greystone|The Culinary Institute of America]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cityofnapa.org/670/Economic-Reports|title=Economic Reports &#124; Napa, CA|website=www.cityofnapa.org}}</ref> The city is distinct in its regulation against chain restaurants; only one exists in the city - an [[A&W Restaurants|A&W]] - established before the legislation was enacted.<ref name="AutoY4-16"/> The [[St. Helena AVA]] is in the region, and [[Duckhorn Vineyards]], [[Newton Vineyard]], [[Charles Krug Winery]], and numerous other vineyards and wineries exist near St. Helena. [[Adventist Health St. Helena]] is located in neighboring [[Deer Park, California]].
