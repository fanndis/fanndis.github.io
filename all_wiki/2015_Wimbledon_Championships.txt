The '''2015 Wimbledon Championships''' was a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] [[tennis]] tournament which took place  at the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in [[Wimbledon, London]], United Kingdom, from 29 June to 12 July 2015.

It was the 129th edition of the championships, the 48th in the [[Open Era (tennis)|Open Era]] and the third [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament of the year, played on grass courts and part of the [[ATP World Tour]], the [[WTA Tour]], the ITF Junior Tour and the NEC Tour. They were organised by the [[All England Lawn Tennis Club]] and the [[International Tennis Federation]]. The tournament was held one week later than in previous seasons, giving a three-week gap from the end of the [[2015 French Open]]. The change, announced in 2012, is intended to provide players more time for recuperation and preparatory grass-court tournaments.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/tennis-wimbledon-idINDEE86I0KE20120719|title=Wimbledon to move back a week from 2015|last=Fallon|first=Clare|date=19 July 2012|work=[[Reuters]]|accessdate=26 June 2017}}</ref>

[[Novak Djokovic]] of [[Serbia]] won his third Wimbledon title in men's singles, defending his championship from 2014. [[Petra Kvitová]] of the [[Czech Republic]] was the defending champion in women's singles, but she lost to [[Jelena Janković]] in the third round.

[[Serena Williams]] won her sixth Wimbledon and 21st [[Grand Slam (tennis)|major]] title, defeating first-time finalist [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the final, 6–4, 6–4. She also achieved her second [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Non-calendar year Grand Slam|non-calendar year Grand Slam]] after winning the [[2014 US Open (tennis)|2014 US Open]], [[2015 Australian Open]] and [[2015 French Open]].  With this win, Williams also became the oldest woman to win a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles title in the [[Open Era]] at 33 years and 289 days old,<ref>289 - 263 = 26</ref> beating [[Martina Navratilova]] by 26 days, who won the [[1990 Wimbledon Championships]] at 33 years and 263 days old.<ref name="Age">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/4854556/title/10-things-to-know-serena-vs-muguruza|title=News – WTA Tennis English|work=Women's Tennis Association}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/blog/2015/jul/13/wimbledon-2015-review-of-the-tournament-guardian-sport|title= Wimbledon 2015 review: the best and worst of this year’s tournament|date=12 July 2015|work=Guardian |accessdate=14 July 2015}}</ref>

== Tournament ==
[[File:Centre Court roof.jpg|200px|thumb|right|Centre Court where the Finals of Wimbledon take place.]]
The 2015 Wimbledon Championships was the 129th edition of the tournament and was held at [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in London.

The tournament was an event run by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) and is part of the [[2015 ATP World Tour]] and the [[2015 WTA Tour]] calendars under the Grand Slam category. The tournament consisted of both men's and women's singles and doubles draws as well as a mixed doubles event. There were singles and doubles events for both boys and girls (players under 18), which were part of the Grade A category of tournaments, and doubles events for men's and women's [[wheelchair tennis]] players as part of the NEC tour under the Grand Slam category. The tournament was played on grass courts and took place over a series of 19 courts, including the four main showcourts, [[Centre Court]], [[No. 1 Court (Wimbledon)|No. 1 Court]], [[No. 2 Court (Wimbledon)|No. 2 Court]] and [[No. 3 Court (Wimbledon)|No. 3 Court]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://2015.wimbledon.com/pdf/easy-access-map.pdf |title=The Championships 2015 Map |publisher=Wimbledon.com |date=29 June 2015}}</ref>

=== Prize money ===

The Wimbledon total prize money for 2015 has been increased by 7% to £26.75m. The winners of the men's and women's singles titles will earn £1.88m, up £120,000 from the previous year. The figures for doubles events are per pair.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wimbledon.com/pdf/Wimbledon_Prize_Money_2015.pdf |title=The Championships, Wimbledon – Prize Money |publisher=Wimbledon.com }}</ref>

== Singles seeds ==
Seedings were announced on Wednesday, 24 June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://2015.wimbledon.com/en_GB/news/articles/2015-06-24/seeding_for_the_championships_2015.html |title=Seeding for The Championships 2015 |publisher=Wimbledon.com |date=24 June 2015}}</ref>

=== [[2015 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|Gentlemen's Singles]] ===
Seeds are adjusted on a surface-based system to reflect more accurately the individual player's grass court achievement as per the following formula, which applies to the top 32 players according to the ATP Rankings on 22 June 2015:
* Take Entry System Position points at 22 June 2015.
* Add 100% points earned for all grass court tournaments in the past 12 months (16 June 2014 – 21 June 2015).
* Add 75% points earned for best grass court tournament in the 12 months before that (17 June 2013 – 15 June 2014).

Rank and points before in the following table are as of 29 June 2015.<br />
Because the tournament takes place one week later than in 2014, points defending includes results from both the [[2014 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|2014 Wimbledon Championships]] and tournaments from the week of 7 July 2014 ([[2014 Hall of Fame Tennis Championships – Singles|Newport]], [[2014 Swedish Open – Men's Singles|Båstad]] and [[2014 MercedesCup – Singles|Stuttgart]]).

==== Gentlemen's Singles ====
{{Main article|2015 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles}}
* {{flagicon|SRB}} '''[[Novak Djokovic]]''' def. {{flagicon|SUI}} [[Roger Federer]], 7–6<sup>(7–1)</sup>, 6–7<sup>(10–12)</sup>, 6–4, 6–3

Prior to the finals, the two had faced off 39 times, with Federer having won the most matches, [[Djokovic–Federer rivalry|20–19]]. At the time of the finals Djokovic was ranked No. 1 and Federer at No. 2. This encounter was their third meeting in a Grand Slam final, when the last two previous were split between the two at the [[2007 US Open (tennis)|2007 US Open]] and [[2014 Wimbledon Championships]]. Federer got the first break of serve in the match, during the first set, yet Djokovic quickly broke back leveling the match.<ref name="USA Today Men">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2015/07/12/novak-djokovic-roger-federer-wimbledon/30046229/|title=Novak Djokovic beats Roger Federer for third Wimbledon title|first=Nick|last=Marvel|journal=[[USA Today]]|date=12 July 2015|accessdate=13 July 2015}}</ref><ref name="BBC Sport Men">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/tennis/33499557|title=Wimbledon 2015: Novak Djokovic beats Roger Federer in final|first=Piers|last=Newbery|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=12 July 2015|accessdate=13 July 2015}}</ref> When Djokovic was serving to remain in the first set, he had to fend off two set points from Federer, which he eventually got the set into a tiebreak, and it was a lopsided tiebreak that sent Djokovic up one set to none.<ref name="USA Today Men" /><ref name="BBC Sport Men" /> The second set was a closely fought affair, but was decided to Federer's edge in the tiebreak.<ref name="USA Today Men" /><ref name="BBC Sport Men" /> The last two sets were rather uneventful in the spectrum of the match because Djokovic got the breaks of serve, allowing him to win his third Wimbledon title, and second in a row.<ref name="USA Today Men" /><ref name="BBC Sport Men" /> This put Djokovic eighth on the all-time list of [[List of Grand Slam men's singles champions#Most Grand Slam singles titles (85 or more)|Men's Grand Slam singles champions]], and putting him fifth during the Open Era.<ref name="USA Today Men" /><ref name="BBC Sport Men" /> He now possesses as many [[List of Wimbledon gentlemen's singles champions#Multiple champions|Wimbledon singles titles]], as his coach [[Boris Becker]] won in his career.<ref name="USA Today Men" /><ref name="BBC Sport Men" />
