'''Viktor Pavlovych Pshonka''' ({{lang-uk|Віктор Павлович Пшонка}}; born 6 February 1954, [[:uk:Сергіївка (Слов'янський район)|Serhiyivtsi]], [[Sloviansk Raion|Sloviansk district]], [[Donetsk oblast]], ({{lang-uk|Сергіївці Слов’янського району Донецької області}}) [[Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic|Ukraine]], [[USSR]]) is a former [[Prosecutor General of Ukraine]] (from 4 November 2010<ref name=update>{{cite news |title=Update: Yanukovych signs law on new prosecutor general |work=[[Kyiv Post]] |agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]] |url=http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/88698/ |date=4 November 2010| access-date=26 November 2019 |language=en |archive-date=5 November 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101105120424/http://www.kyivpost.com/news/politics/detail/88698/}}</ref> until 22 February 2014), State Counselor of Justice of Ukraine and member of the [[Supreme Council of Justice|High Council of Justice of Ukraine]].<ref name="vru.gov.ua">{{cite web |url=http://www.vru.gov.ua/member/15 |title=ПШОНКА Віктор Павлович. General Prosecutor Ukraine |work=[[Supreme Council of Justice|Ukraine High Council of Justice]] website |date=14 October 2013 |access-date=25 November 2019 |archive-date=14 October 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131014014317/http://www.vru.gov.ua/member/15 |language=uk}}</ref><ref name="guidance_gpu">{{cite web |url=http://www.gp.gov.ua/ua/guidance_gpu.html |title=Генеральний прокурор України, державний радник юстиції України: Пшонка Віктор Павлович |trans-title=Prosecutor General of Ukraine, State Advisor of Justice for Ukraine: Pshonka Viktor Pavlovych |work=[[Prosecutor General of Ukraine|General Prosecutor of Ukraine]] website |access-date=25 November 2019 |archive-date=4 December 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121204045042/http://www.gp.gov.ua/ua/guidance_gpu.html |language=uk}}</ref> He holds a Doctor of Laws degree, and is a member of the International Association of Prosecutors.<ref name="guidance_gpu"/>

On 22 February 2014, as a part of the "[[2014 Ukrainian revolution|Maidan revolution]]", the [[Verkhovna Rada]] ousted Pshonka in a no-confidence vote.<ref name=PsoutI>{{cite news |url=http://en.trend.az/news/politics/2245153.html |title=Ukrainian parliament expresses no-confidence to Viktor Pshonka |agency=[[Trend News Agency]] |date=22 February 2014 |access-date=26 November 2019 |language=en |archive-date=27 December 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141227195059/https://en.trend.az/azerbaijan/politics/2245153.html}}</ref> On 23 February 2014 an arrest warrant was issued to arrest Pshonka.<ref name=BBCRLLout>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-26312008 |title=Ukraine: Speaker Oleksandr Turchynov named interim president] |work=[[BBC News]] |date=23 February 2014 |access-date=26 November 2019 |archive-date=3 March 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140303223143/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-26312008}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Viktor Pshonka was born on 6 February 1954 in Serhiyivka, Slovyansk District of [[Donetsk Oblast]]. After graduating from high school he was called up for military service in the Soviet Army. Having discharged from army, he got a job at one of the enterprises in [[Donetsk Oblast]].<ref name=guidance_gpu />

In 1975, Viktor Pshonka enrolled at the Kharkiv Law Institute.

==Career==
{{more footnotes|section|date=February 2014}}
In 1980, he began work at [[Kramatorsk]] Prosecutor's Office in [[Donetsk Oblast]], where he served as investigator, and eventually as an assistant to prosecutor of the city of Kramatorsk.

From 1986 till 1997, Viktor Pshonka served as prosecutor of [[Kramatorsk]] City.

In 1997, he was appointed first deputy prosecutor of [[Donetsk Oblast]].

In 1998–2003, he was prosecutor of [[Donetsk Oblast]], one of the biggest regions in [[Ukraine]].

In November 2003, Viktor P. Pshonka was appointed Deputy Prosecutor General of Ukraine. He was in charge of prosecutor's supervision over observance of laws by the agencies of the [[Ministry of Internal Affairs (Ukraine)|Ministry of Interior]], the [[Security Service of Ukraine]] (SBU), tax police and special units combating organized crime and corruption. He was also responsible for supervision over state customs service and border guard.<ref name=guidance_gpu />

He resigned voluntarily from his office in December 2004, but returned to the Prosecutor General's Office of Ukraine<ref>[http://www.gp.gov.ua/ Prosecutor General’s Office of Ukraine]</ref> at the end of 2006.<ref name=guidance_gpu />

In November 2010, members of Ukraine's Parliament voted in favor (292 votes) of Viktor Pshonka's appointment as the [[General Prosecutor of Ukraine]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2010/11/4/5542069/ |title=В Україні новий генпрокурор |trans-title=New General Prosecutor in Ukraine |work=[[Ukrayinska Pravda]] |date=4 November 2010 |access-date=26 November 2019 |archive-date=29 December 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171229052258/http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2010/11/4/5542069/ |language=uk}}</ref>

=== Criminal prosecution ===
On 22 February 2014, as a part of the [[2014 Ukrainian revolution|Euromaidan Revolution]], the [[Verkhovna Rada]] ousted Pshonka in a no-confidence vote.<ref name=PsoutI/> On 23 February 2014, an arrest warrant was issued to arrest Pshonka.<ref name=BBCRLLout/>

But, on 5 December 2015, Interpol denied a request to put former Prosecutor General of Ukraine Viktor Pshonka on the wanted list allegedly due to the weakness of the evidence submitted by the competent authorities of Ukraine.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/309185.html|title = Interpol denies request for putting Pshonka on wanted list quoting evidence as unconvincing – Ukraine's PGO|agency = [[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date = 5 December 2015 |access-date = 2016-03-16 |archive-date=12 December 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151212193406/http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/309185.html}}</ref>

==Awards==
In view of special accomplishments in strengthening of the due course of law in Ukraine, V. Pshonka was awarded a rank of ''State Counselor of Justice of Ukraine'', ''Honored Jurist of Ukraine'' and ''Third Class Order of Merit ''by the Decrees of the President of Ukraine.<ref name=guidance_gpu />

In 2000, he was named ''Jurist of the Year'' in the nomination among prosecutors.<ref name=guidance_gpu />

In 2002, he was awarded the ''Certificate of Honor of the ''[[Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine]].<ref name=vru.gov.ua />

Viktor Pshonka was awarded lapel badges of the "''Honorable Member of Prosecution Service of Ukraine”'', ''“Statehood. Fairness. Honesty”'' of 2nd Degree.<ref name=guidance_gpu />

He also received ''“Acknowledgement of Honest Service in Prosecution Service of Ukraine” ''of 1st Degree, as well as awards from the Ukrainian Orthodox Church.<ref name=guidance_gpu />
