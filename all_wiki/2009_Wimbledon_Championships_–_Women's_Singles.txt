{{main|2009 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Serena Williams]] defeated her [[Williams sisters|sister]], the two-time defending champion [[Venus Williams]], in the final, 7–6<sup>(7–3)</sup>, 6–2 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2009 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> This was Serena's third Wimbledon singles title and eleventh Grand Slam singles title overall.<ref name="final">{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/wimbledon/5742576/Wimbledon-2009-Serena-Williams-beats-sister-Venus-in-womens-singles-final.html|title=Wimbledon 2009: Serena Williams beats sister Venus in women’s singles final|date=4 July 2009|work=The Telegraph|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> It was also the third time she had won a Grand Slam title after saving a match point against her, which she did in her semifinal match against [[Elena Dementieva]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/05/sports/tennis/05wimbledon.html|title=A Less Familiar Ending to a Final Between Sisters|date=4 July 2009|work=The New York Times|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> Serena dropped only one set the entire tournament, to Dementieva in the semifinals.

Venus Williams was attempting to become the first player to win the women's singles tournament three consecutive times since [[Steffi Graf]] was champion in [[1991 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1991]], [[1992 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1992]] and [[1993 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1993]].<ref name="final" />

This edition of the tournament saw the top four seeds all reach the semifinals, for the second time in four years after [[2006 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|2006]]. As of 2020, this remains the last Grand Slam event to feature the top four seeds on the women's side in the semifinals. The match between Serena Williams and Dementieva was the longest women's semifinal at Wimbledon in the [[Open Era]], lasting two hours and 49 minutes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2009/jul/02/serena-williams-beats-elena-dementieva|title=Spirited Serena Williams inches past Elena Dementieva |date=2 July 2009|work=The Guardian|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref>
