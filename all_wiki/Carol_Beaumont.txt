'''Carol Ann Beaumont'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.parliament.nz/en-NZ/PB/Debates/Debates/5/c/4/49HansD_20081208_00000010-Members-Sworn.htm|title=New Zealand Hansard - Members Sworn &#91;Volume:651;Page:2&#93;|publisher=[[New Zealand Parliament]]|accessdate=2017-03-15}}</ref> (born 6 October 1960) is a politician from New Zealand, first elected to represent the [[New Zealand Labour Party|Labour Party]] through the party list vote in the {{NZ election link|2008}}. Beaumont stood in the {{NZ electorate link|Maungakiekie}} electorate, finishing second in both 2008<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.electionresults.govt.nz/electionresults_2008/electorate-25.html |title=Official Count Results - Maungakiekie |website=Electionresults.govt.nz |date=2008-11-22 |accessdate=2017-03-15}}</ref> and 2011. In the {{NZ election link|2011}} she initially missed joining the [[50th New Zealand Parliament]], her list ranking (22) sitting just above the cut-off due to Labour's reduced party vote. In March 2013 [[Charles Chauvel (politician)|Charles Chauvel]]'s resignation saw her return to Parliament until her defeat at the {{NZ election link|2014}}.<ref name="2013return">{{cite news |title=Beaumont returns to Parliament after Chauvel's resignation |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10870793 |accessdate=12 March 2013|work=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|date=12 March 2013}}</ref>

==Professional life==
[[File:Mining Protest-4 cropped.jpg|thumb|left|Beaumont (right), with [[Jacinda Ardern]] and [[Phil Goff]]]]
In May 2003 Beaumont was elected Secretary of the [[New Zealand Council of Trade Unions]].<ref name="Department of Labour">{{cite web|url=http://www.dol.govt.nz/services/PartnershipResourceCentre/Carol-Beaumont-Advisory-Board.asp |title=Carol Beaumont |publisher=[[Department of Labour (New Zealand)|Department of Labour]] |accessdate=2008-12-11 |url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081015114613/http://dol.govt.nz/services/PartnershipResourceCentre/Carol-Beaumont-Advisory-Board.asp |archivedate=15 October 2008 }}</ref> This followed 20 years involvement in the union movement.

==Personal life==
Beaumont was heavily involved during her years at University in the Student Associations around New Zealand. She worked as a Research Officer and Tertiary Union Delegate during this time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.parliament.nz/en-nz/mpp/mps/former/50PlibMpsFormerBeaumontCarol1/beaumont-carol |title=Beaumont, Carol - New Zealand Parliament |website=Parliament.nz |date=2014-10-06 |accessdate=2017-03-15}}</ref> Beaumont's partner, Robert Gallagher, was one of the [[New Zealand Labour Party|Labour Party]] campaign team strategists and in January 2015 announced his intention to stand for the [[New Zealand Labour Party#List of presidents|Labour Party Presidency]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Nominees announced for Labour Party presidency | url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11392042| publisher= NZ Herald| accessdate= 31 January 2015}}</ref>

Beaumont stood for Labour in the [[Maungakiekie (New Zealand electorate)|Maungakiekie]] seat at the [[2008 New Zealand general election|2008 general election]], finishing second to National's [[Sam Lotu-Iiga]]. The seat had previously been held by Labour's [[Mark Gosche]]. She was ranked 28th on Labour's party list and was elected to Parliament as a List MP.

In the 49th Parliament Beaumont was appointed Labour's spokesperson for Consumer Affairs, and associate spokesperson for Labour by Labour leader [[Phil Goff]].<ref name="labour shadow cabinet">{{Cite web |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/vote08/4767140a28435.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=11 December 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081225120056/http://www.stuff.co.nz/vote08/4767140a28435.html |archive-date=25 December 2008 |url-status=dead|df=dmy-all }}</ref> In early 2010 she took over responsibility for [[Charles Chauvel (politician)|Charles Chauvel]]'s Credit Reforms (Responsible Lending) Bill, which had been drawn from the ballot in August 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url= |title=Credit Reforms (Responsible Lending) Bill |publisher=Parliament of New Zealand }}</ref> The bill was defeated at its first reading in July 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tvnz.co.nz/politics-news/loan-shark-bill-killed-first-reading-3665374 |title=Loan sharks "not going away" |publisher=TVNZ |date=2010-07-21 |accessdate=2010-07-22}}</ref>

At the [[2011 New Zealand general election|2011 general election]] Beaumont again contested [[Maungakiekie (New Zealand electorate)|Maungakiekie]] finishing second to incumbent [[Sam Lotu-Iiga]]. She was ranked 22 on Labour's party list but this was not high enough to be reelected. Following the resignation of list MP [[Charles Chauvel (politician)|Charles Chauvel]], Beaumont was declared elected to parliament on 12 March 2013.<ref name=2013return /><ref>[http://www.elections.org.nz/news-media/new-list-mp-new-zealand-labour-party-1 New List MP for New Zealand Labour Party] elections.org.nz, 12 March 2013</ref>

At the [[2014 New Zealand general election|2014 election]] Beaumont stood again for the seat of {{NZ electorate link|Maungakiekie}} with a potential boost in numbers due to boundary changes moving several thousand new voters into the area whom were cited as potential Labour voters by media sources.<ref>{{cite web |title= Sam Lotu IIga Claims Maungakiekie| url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/10524978/Sam-Lotu-Iiga-claims-Maungakiekie| publisher= Stuff.co.nz |accessdate= 31 January 2015}}</ref> Beaumont failed to take the seat back for Labour and was not elected on the List again due to her position being several places below the Party Vote entitlement. Since the 2014 election Beaumont has been working still in the public service; currently she has undertaken campaigning to highlight [[loan shark]] issues<ref>{{cite web |title=CAROL BEAUMONT, FORMER LABOUR CONSUMER AFFAIRS SPOKESPERSON| url=http://www.radiolive.co.nz/Carol-Beaumont-former-Labour-Consumer-Affairs-Spokesperson/tabid/506/articleID/67248/Default.aspx|publisher= Radiolive.co.nz|accessdate= 31 January 2015 }}</ref> as well as sitting as an Advisory Member of the Work Research Institute.
