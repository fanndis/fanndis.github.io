'''Karlie Elizabeth Kloss''' (born August 3, 1992) is an American fashion model. ''[[Vogue Paris]]'' declared her one of the "top 30 models of the 2000s" when she was 17.<ref>{{cite web |title=Les 30 mannequins des années 2000 |url=http://www.vogue.fr/mode/en-vogue/diaporama/les-30-mannequins-des-annees-2000/1662#!091218-les-mannequins-des-annees-2000-aspx72577image-jpg |publisher=[[Condé Nast]] |work=[[Vogue Paris]] |language=fr |date=December 18, 2009 |access-date=January 20, 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6JATskx4b?url=http://www.vogue.fr/mode/en-vogue/diaporama/les-30-mannequins-des-annees-2000/1662#!091218-les-mannequins-des-annees-2000-aspx72577image-jpg |archive-date=August 26, 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> On the runway, she is known for her "panther"-like walk.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/best-model-runway-walks-of-all-time|title=The Fiercest (and Funniest) Model Runway Walks of All Time|work=Vogue|publisher=Condé Nast|last=Satenstein|first=Liana}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/at-18-model-karlie-kloss-conquers-the-runways-at-new-yorks-fashion-week/2011/02/15/ABnUgoQ_story.html|title=At 18, model Karlie Kloss conquers the runways at New York's Fashion Week|newspaper=The Washington Post|last=Kaufman|first=Sarah|date=February 15, 2011}}</ref>

Kloss was a [[Victoria's Secret Angel]] from 2013 until 2015; she resigned to study at [[New York University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.co.uk/article/karlie-kloss-on-modelling-faith-philanthropy-business|title=Karlie Kloss: "Only Now Do I Have The Confidence To Stand Tall & Know The Power Of My Voice"|work=[[British Vogue]]|date=July 1, 2019}}</ref> Models.com has said that Kloss "represents the gold standard of modeling—a girl with the look, the poise, and the drive to take things to the next level," and she ranks on their "New Supers" and "Money Girl" lists.<ref>{{cite web |title=New Supers|url=https://models.com/rankings/ui/NewSupers/2529#2529 |publisher=models.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://models.com/rankings/ui/MoneyGirls/2529#2529|title=THE MONEY GIRLS|publisher=models.com|quote=Just when you think you've got Karlie Kloss figured out the beauty from St. Louis surprises you. When you thought she was just a high fashion princess Karlie comes along and scoops up every money client. When you think she's the cute girl next door she opens up and shows Vogue Italia her sexy, powerful side. Karlie's versatility and endless drive have impressed everyone in the business - you gotta love a girl who can make Anna Wintour smile - and given that clients like Dior, Donna Karan and Oscar de la Renta are clamoring for Ms. Kloss' attention, we're sure to be seeing her around for a long time coming.}}</ref> By 2019, she had appeared on 40 international ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' covers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/careers/career-advice/2019/03/05/project-runway-coding-supermodel-karlie-kloss-entrepreneur/3028618002/|title=From 'Project Runway' to coding: How supermodel Karlie Kloss became a tech entrepreneur|work=[[USA Today]]|publisher=Gannett Satellite Information Network, LLC|last=Hutcheson|first=Susannah}}</ref>

Outside of modeling, Kloss has an interest in technology and has founded the "Kode with Klossy" camp, which aims to get young girls interested in [[Science, technology, engineering, and mathematics|STEM]] fields.<ref name="KodeBI"/> In 2019, Kloss became the host of the [[reality competition]] television series ''[[Project Runway]]''.<ref name="project-runway"/>

==Early life and education==
Kloss was born in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]],<ref name="NYMag.com profile">{{cite news |title=Karlie Kloss: Model Profile |work=[[New York Magazine]] |url=http://nymag.com/fashion/models/kkloss/karliekloss/ |access-date=August 8, 2016 |archive-date=July 8, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160708184602/http://nymag.com/fashion/models/kkloss/karliekloss/ |url-status=live}}</ref> to Tracy, a freelance director, and Kurt Kloss, an emergency physician.<ref name="Blasberg 2013">{{cite news |last=Blasberg |first=Derek |author-link=Derek Blasberg |title=The Klosses|date=February 16, 2013 |work=[[The Wall Street Journal]] |url=https://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424127887323375204578272163876557122 |access-date=January 14, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131127000925/http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424127887323375204578272163876557122 |archive-date=November 27, 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Her family has Danish, German and Polish ancestry.<ref name="Blasberg 2013"/> She has three sisters.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/gallery/karlie-kloss-and-family|title=At Home with Supermodel Karlie Kloss and Her Sisters|first=Arthur Elgort, Jane Keltner de|last=Valle|access-date=May 3, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180504011411/https://www.teenvogue.com/gallery/karlie-kloss-and-family|archive-date=May 4, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> She moved to [[St. Louis]] with her family in 1994.<ref name="NYMag.com profile"/> In 2013, the family moved to [[Goshen, New York]] to supplement her modeling career.<ref name="Blasberg 2013"/> Kloss has called her classical ballet training "a beautiful thing" that taught her how to move in the modeling world and was a great training ground for her runway walk.<ref name="EOnline July 2011">{{cite web |title=Five Things to Know About Joe Jonas' New Cuddle Partner Karlie Kloss |url=http://www.eonline.com/uberblog/b251750_five_things_know_about_joe_jonas_new.html |publisher=[[E! She graduated from [[Webster Groves High School]] in [[Webster Groves, Missouri]], where she was a cheerleader, in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stltoday.com/news/local/education/from-webster-groves-cheerleader-to-supermodel-what-s-next-for/article_5ad528d2-ecdd-5ffc-9ae0-bba04895035d.html|title=From Webster Groves cheerleader to supermodel, what's next for Karlie Kloss?|work=St. Louis Post-Dispatch|last=Sultan|first=Aisha|publisher=STLtoday.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Top fashion model Karlie Kloss goes to the Webster Groves High School prom |url=http://www.stltoday.com/news/local/columns/deb-peterson/top-fashion-model-karlie-kloss-goes-to-the-webster-groves/article_3fb07562-7680-11e0-9b4b-0019bb30f31a.html |publisher=Kevin Mowbray |newspaper=[[St. Louis Post-Dispatch]] |last=Peterson |first=Deb |date=May 4, 2011 |access-date=September 30, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140830092326/http://www.stltoday.com/news/local/columns/deb-peterson/top-fashion-model-karlie-kloss-goes-to-the-webster-groves/article_3fb07562-7680-11e0-9b4b-0019bb30f31a.html |archive-date=August 30, 2014 |url-status=live}}</ref> In September 2015, Kloss enrolled in the [[Gallatin School of Individualized Study]] of [[New York University]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/karlie-kloss-shares-day-student-nyu/story?id=33513759 |title=Karlie Kloss Shares First Day As Student At NYU|publisher=[[ABC News]]}}</ref>

In collaboration with [[Momofuku (restaurants)|Momofuku]] [[Milk Bar (bakery)|Milk Bar]], Kloss created a special recipe called Karlie's Kookies sold in [[DKNY]] Soho during Fashion's Night Out in [[New York City]], with proceeds benefiting hungry children around the world (through [[FEED Projects]]). Kloss called it a "Perfect 10 Kookie" because for every tin of cookies sold, 10 meals were donated to starving children all over the world.<ref>{{cite web |title=Karlie's Kookies: A Perfect 10 |url=http://www.style.com/stylefile/2012/09/karlies-kookies-a-perfect-10 |publisher=style.com |date=September 5, 2012 |access-date=November 12, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121017072904/http://www.style.com/stylefile/2012/09/karlies-kookies-a-perfect-10/ |archive-date=October 17, 2012 |url-status=live}}</ref>

On August 14, 2020, ''[[Daily Front Row]]'' reported Kloss was one of a group of high profile investors who purchased ''[[W magazine]]'', a troubled fashion magazine.<ref name=fashionweekdaily2020-08-14/>  The new owners chose to retain the current editor-in-chief, [[Sara Moonves]], who was credited with introducing the new investors, and arousing their interest in the purchase.  Kloss was reported to have been trusted by the other investors to play a hands-on role in working with Moonves in the magazine's operation.
