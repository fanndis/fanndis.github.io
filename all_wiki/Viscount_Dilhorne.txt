'''Viscount Dilhorne''', of [[Greens Norton]] in the [[County of Northampton]], is a title in the [[Peerage of the United Kingdom]].<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=43511 |date=8 December 1964 |page=10447}}</ref> It was created on 7 December 1964 for the lawyer, [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] politician and former [[Lord Chancellor]], [[Reginald Manningham-Buller, 1st Viscount Dilhorne|Reginald Manningham-Buller, 1st Baron Dilhorne]]. He had already succeeded his father as fourth Baronet of Dilhorne and been created '''Baron Dilhorne''', ''of [[Towcester]] in the County of Northampton'' on 17 July 1962, also in the Peerage of the United Kingdom.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=42736 |date=20 July 1962 |page=5807}}</ref> The '''Baronetcy''', of [[Dilhorne]] in the [[County of Stafford]], was created in the [[Baronetage of the United Kingdom]] on 20 January 1866<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=23059 |date=12 January 1866 |page=209}}</ref> for the first Viscount's great-grandfather [[Sir Edward Manningham-Buller, 1st Baronet|Edward Manningham-Buller]]. He represented [[Staffordshire North (UK Parliament constituency)|Staffordshire North]] and [[Stafford (UK Parliament constituency)|Stafford]] in Parliament. Manningham-Buller was the third son of Sir Francis Buller, 2nd Baronet, of Churston Court, whose eldest son, the third Baronet, was created [[Baron Churston]] in 1858. His grandson, the third Baronet, sat as a Conservative [[Member of Parliament]] for [[Kettering (UK Parliament constituency)|Kettering]] and [[Northampton (UK Parliament constituency)|Northampton]]. He was succeeded by his eldest son, the aforementioned fourth Baronet, who was elevated to the peerage as Viscount Dilhorne. {{As of|2017}} the titles are held by the latter's son, the second Viscount, who succeeded in 1980. 

[[Eliza Manningham-Buller]], former [[Director general of MI5]], is the second daughter of the first Viscount.

The family seat is The Dower House, near [[Dorchester, Dorset]].
