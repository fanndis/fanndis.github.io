[[Elizabeth II]] was '''Queen of Sierra Leone''' from 1961 to 1971, when [[Sierra Leone]] was an independent [[constitutional monarchy]]. The Queen was also monarch of the other [[Commonwealth realm]]s, including the [[United Kingdom]].

Sierra Leone became an independent realm by the Sierra Leone Independence Act 1961, which transformed the [[British Crown Colony of Sierra Leone]] into an independent member of the [[Commonwealth of Nations]].<ref>{{cite hansard|url=http://hansard.millbanksystems.com/lords/1961/mar/27/sierra-leone-independence-bill|title=Sierra Leone Independence Bill|date=27 March 1961|volume=230|column=23–40|house=Lords}}</ref> The Queen was the ceremonial [[head of state]], represented by the [[List of Governors-General of Sierra Leone|Governor-General of Sierra Leone]], who resided in [[State House (Sierra Leone)|State House]], which flew the [[Union Jack]].<ref>{{cite book|title=British Foreign Policy and the Conflict in Sierra Leone, 1991–2001|author=Michael S. Kargbo|year=2006|publisher=Peter Lang|pages=70–71|isbn=0-8204-7506-8}}</ref> Her formal title was ''Queen of Sierra Leone and of Her other Realms and Territories, Head of the Commonwealth''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.archontology.org/nations/sierra_leone/00_1961_1971_s.php|title=Sierra Leone: Heads of State: 1961–1971|publisher=archontology.org|accessdate=8 November 2015}}</ref> The monarchy was abolished in 1971, when Sierra Leone became a [[Republics in the Commonwealth of Nations|republic within the Commonwealth]] with the [[President of Sierra Leone]] as head of state.

The Queen and her husband, [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh]], visited Sierra Leone on the royal yacht ''[[HMY Britannia|Britannia]]''<ref name=guardian>{{cite journal|title=Queen causes a frenzy in Freetown: A royal visit to Sierra Leone creates colourful boating chaos|date=26 November 1961|journal=The Observer|url=https://www.theguardian.com/news/2013/nov/24/queen-causes-frenzy-sierra-leone-freetown|accessdate=8 November 2015}}</ref> from 25 November to 1 December 1961.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royal.gov.uk/MonarchUK/RoyalVisits/Commonwealthvisitssince1952.aspx|title=Commonwealth visits since 1952|publisher=Royal Household|work=Official website of the British monarchy|accessdate=8 November 2015}}</ref> [[Queen Elizabeth II Quay]] in [[Freetown]] is named after her.
