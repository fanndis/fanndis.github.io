'''Maia Sandu''' ({{IPA-ro|ˈmaja ˈsandu}}, born 24 May 1972) is a [[Moldova]]n politician and the [[President of Moldova|president-elect of Moldova]]. She is the current leader of the [[Party of Action and Solidarity]] (PAS) and former [[Prime Minister of Moldova]] from 8 June 2019 until 14 November 2019.<ref name="Maia Sandu new Moldova Prime-minister">{{cite web|url=https://protv.md/actualitate/maia-sandu-este-noul-prim-ministru-al-republicii-moldova-igor-dodon-a-semnat-decretul-foto---2494900.html|title=Maia Sandu este noul prim-ministru al Republicii Moldova.|date=8 June 2019|website=protv.md|language=ro}}</ref> On 12 November 2019, Sandu's government fell after a vote of no-confidence, with 63 (deputies from PSRM and PDM) of the 101 MPs having voted on the motion submitted by the PSRM.<ref name="DW.COM_2020-02-05">{{Citation | url=https://www.dw.com/ro/guvernul-condus-de-maia-sandu-a-fost-demis-dodon-se-apuc%C4%83-s%C4%83-%C8%99i-fac%C4%83-propriul-cabinet/a-51212876| title=Guvernul condus de Maia Sandu a fost demis. Dodon se apucă să-și facă propriul cabinet|newspaper=DW.COM|access-date=5 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-moldova-politics/moldovas-fledgling-government-brought-down-by-no-confidence-vote-idUSKBN1XM1G4|title=Moldova's fledgling government felled by no-confidence vote|date=12 November 2019|website=Reuters}}</ref> 

Sandu was [[Ministry of Education of Moldova|Minister of Education]] from 2012 to 2015 and member of the [[Parliament of the Republic of Moldova|Parliament of Moldova]] from 2014 to 2015, and again in 2019.<ref name="ACUM secure 26 out of 101 seats">{{cite web|url=http://www.constcourt.md/libview.php?l=ro&idc=7&id=1417&t=/Media/Noutati/Confirmarea-rezultatelor-alegerilor-parlamentare-din-24-februarie-2019-si-validarea-mandatelor-de-deputat-de-catre-Curtea-Constitutionala-pentru-Parlamentul-de-legislatura-a-X-a|title=Confirmarea rezultatelor alegerilor parlamentare din 24 februarie 2019 şi validarea mandatelor de deputat de către Curtea Constituţională pentru Parlamentul de legislatura a X-a|date=9 March 2019|website=constcourt.md|language=ro}}</ref><ref name="Maia Sandu Minister of Education 2012-2015">{{cite web|url=https://www.timpul.md/articol/maia-sandu-a-preluat-atributiile-functiei-de-ministru-al-educatiei-35771.html|title=Maia Sandu a preluat atribuţiile funcţiei de ministru al Educaţiei|date=26 July 2012|website=timpul.md|language=ro}}</ref><ref name="Maia Sandu și-a dat demisia din funcția de deputat">{{cite web|url=https://moldova.europalibera.org/a/30042829.html|title=Maia Sandu și-a dat demisia din funcția de deputat|date=8 July 2019|website=europalibera.org|language=ro}}</ref> She was selected as the joint candidate of the [[pro-Europeanism|pro-European]] [[Dignity and Truth Platform Party|PPDA]] and [[Party of Action and Solidarity|PAS]] parties for [[President of the Republic of Moldova|President of Moldova]] in the [[2016 Moldovan presidential election|2016 election]]. However, she was defeated in the subsequent runoff by the pro-Russian [[Party of Socialists of the Republic of Moldova|PSRM]] candidate, [[Igor Dodon]], losing the popular vote by a margin of 48% to 52%. In a rematch between Dodon and Sandu in the [[2020 Moldovan presidential election|2020 election]], she won the subsequent runoff, 58% to 42%. She will be the first female president of Moldova.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.g4media.ro/maia-sandu-aleasa-presedinte-al-republicii-moldova-victorie-detasata-in-fata-prorusului-dodon-la-peste-15-puncte-procentuale.html|title=Maia Sandu, aleasă președinte al Republicii Moldova. Victorie detașată în fața prorusului Dodon, la peste 15 puncte procentuale|newspaper=G4Media.ro|date=16 November 2020|language=ro}}</ref>

==Early life and professional career==
Sandu was born on 24 May 1972 in the commune of [[Risipeni]], located in the [[Fălești District]] in the [[Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic]] (under the [[USSR]]). She was born to of Grigorie, a veterinarian and Emilia Sandu<ref>https://agora.md/stiri/24397/video--maia-sandu-apare-pentru-prima-oara-in-public-alaturi-de-mama-sa</ref>, a teacher.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Presidential elections Republic of Moldova. Who is Maia Sandu, the woman who writes history in Chisinau|url=https://tv6.news/presidential-elections-republic-of-moldova-who-is-maia-sandu-the-woman-who-writes-history-in-chisinau/|access-date=2020-11-21|website=TV6 News|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=admin|date=2016-10-15|title=CV-ul şi averea Maiei Sandu|url=https://www.zdg.md/investigatii/ancheta/cv-ul-si-averea-maiei-sandu/|access-date=2020-11-21|website=Ziarul de Gardă|language=ro-RO}}</ref> From 1989 to 1994, she majored in management at the [[Academy of Economic Studies of Moldova]] (ASEM). From 1995 to 1998, she majored in international relations at the [http://www.aap.gov.md/ Academy of Public Administration] (AAP) in [[Chișinău]]. In 2010, she graduated from the [[John F. Kennedy School of Government]] at [[Harvard University]]. From 2010 to 2012, Sandu worked as Adviser to the Executive Director at the [[World Bank]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]. Sandu speaks [[Russian language|Russian]], [[Spanish language|Spanish]] and [[English language|English]] in addition to her native [[Romanian language|Romanian]].

==Political career==
From 2012 to 2015 she served as [[Ministry of Education of Moldova|Minister of Education of Moldova]]. She was considered on 23 July 2015 by the Liberal Democratic Party as a nominee to be the next [[Prime Minister of Moldova]], succeeding [[Natalia Gherman]] and [[Chiril Gaburici]].<ref name="Deutsche Welle">{{cite web|url=http://www.dw.com/en/ex-world-bank-economist-set-to-become-prime-minister-in-moldova/a-18604667|title=Ex-World Bank economist set to become prime minister in Moldova|publisher=www.dw.com/en|access-date=24 July 2015}}</ref> 
[[File:Maia Sandu at a meeting with Ovidiu Raețchi, delegate of Romanian diaspora in the Romanian Parliament, discussing political situation in Moldova.jpg|left|thumb|Sandu at a meeting with [[Ovidiu Raețchi]] in January 2016]]
A day after being proposed by a renewed pro-European coalition, Sandu set the departure of the Head of the [[National Bank of Moldova]], [[Dorin Drăguțanu]] and the [[State Prosecutor of Moldova|State Prosecutor]] Corneliu Gurin as conditions for her acceptance of the office.<ref name="Reuters">{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2015/07/24/moldova-premier-idUSL5N10437820150724|title=Moldova PM nominee pushes tough demands for taking top job|publisher=www.reuters.com/|access-date=25 July 2015}}</ref> Ultimately, [[Valeriu Streleț]] was nominated over Sandu by the [[President of Moldova]].

On 23 December 2015 she launched a platform „În /pas/ cu Maia Sandu” ("In step with Maia Sandu"{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}<!--"keeping up" is more accurate to the original meaning, but that may get some associations perceived as negative with the Kardashians show; in any case if there is an official translation on one of the party's channels it should be used – until then, we are doing original research-->) that later became a political party called [[Party of Action and Solidarity|"Partidul Acțiune și Solidaritate" ("Party of Action and Solidarity")]].<ref name="Maia Sandu elected Leader of PAS">{{cite web|url=https://www.realitatea.md/decis-maia-sandu-aleasa-in-calitate-de-pre-edinte-al-partidului-ac-iune-i-solidaritate-pe-un-termen-de-zece-luni_39134.html|title=Maia Sandu, aleasă în calitate de președinte al PAS pe un termen de zece luni|date=15 May 2016|website=realitatea.md|language=ro}}</ref><ref name="Maia Sandu re-elected Leader of PAS">{{cite web|url=http://tv8.md/2017/09/10/ultima-ora-maia-sandu-a-fost-realeasa-in-functia-de-presedinte-al-pas/|title=Maia Sandu a fost realeasă în funcția de președinte al PAS|date=10 September 2017|website=tv8.md|language=ro}}</ref>

In 2016, Sandu was the pro-European candidate in the [[2016 Moldovan presidential election|Moldovan presidential election]]. Running on a pro-EU action platform, she was one of the two candidates that reached the runoff of the election.<ref name="Reuters"/>

According to some polls from 2019, Sandu ranks among the most trusted three politicians in Moldova.<ref name="protv.md_2019-02-13">{{Citation | url=http://protv.md/stiri/actualitate/partidele-care-ar-ajunge-in-legislativ-daca-duminica-viitoare---2466841.html| title=Partidele care ar ajunge in legislativ daca duminica viitoare ar avea loc alegeri parlamentare. Top trei politicieni care se bucura de cea mai mare incredere. Sondaj iData| newspaper=protv.md| access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="stiri.md_2019-02-13">{{Citation | url=https://stiri.md/article/politica/sondaj-cine-sunt-politicienii-in-care-moldovenii-au-cea-mai-mare-incredere| title=Sondaj: Cine sunt politicienii în care moldovenii au cea mai mare încredere| newspaper=stiri.md| access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="TVR MOLDOVA_2019-02-13">{{Citation | url=http://tvrmoldova.md/alegeri-parlamentare/sondaj-igor-dodon-si-pavel-filip-se-bucura-de-cea-mai-mare-incredere-din-partea-populatiei/| title=SONDAJ: Igor Dodon şi Pavel Filip se bucură de cea mai mare încredere din partea populaţiei| newspaper=TVR MOLDOVA| access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref> The most recent available poll, conducted by Public Opinion Fund, shows that Sandu is the second most trusted political personality, polling at 24%, closely following Igor Dodon, who polls at 26%.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ipn.md/en/igor-dodon-is-most-trusted-political-personality-survey-7965_1070086.html|title=Igor Dodon is most trusted political personality, survey|date=5 December 2019|website=IPN|publisher=IPN.com|language=en|access-date=8 December 2019}}</ref> Other older polls, however, place her lower, in the 6th place.<ref name="UNIMEDIA_2019-02-13">{{Citation | url=https://unimedia.info/ro/news/d8648c3c2edd695a/sondaj-bop-cei-mai-apreciati-politicieni-din-republica-moldova.html| title=Sondaj BOP: Cei mai apreciați politicieni din Republica Moldova| newspaper=UNIMEDIA| access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref>

===As Prime Minister===
{{Main|Sandu Cabinet}}
[[File:Pence meets with New Moldova PM Maia Sandu (1).jpg|thumb|Sandu meeting with Vice President [[Mike Pence]] at the [[White House]], September 2019.]]
In the [[2019 Moldovan parliamentary election|2019 parliamentary election]], Sandu's [[Party of Action and Solidarity|PAS]] together with its ally, [[Dignity and Truth Platform Party|PPDA]] led by [[Andrei Năstase]] formed the [[NOW Platform DA and PAS|ACUM Electoral Bloc]] and secured 26 of the 101 seats in the [[Parliament of the Republic of Moldova|Parliament of Moldova]].<ref name="Moldova 2019 Election">{{cite web|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/moldova-constitutional-court-confirms-parliament-elections/29812890.html|title=Moldova's Constitutional Court Confirms February 24 Vote Result|date=10 March 2019|website=RadioFreeEuropeRadioLiberty|language=en}}</ref> On 8 June 2019, Maia Sandu was elected [[Prime Minister of Moldova]] in a coalition government with [[PSRM]].<ref name="Maia Sandu 2019 new Moldova PM">{{Citation | url=https://unimedia.info/ro/news/b502345d0d7f9866/ultima-ora-candidatura-maiei-sandu-in-functie-de-prim-ministru-votata-de-parlament.html| title=Candidatura Maiei Sandu, în funcția de prim-ministru, votată de Parlament| newspaper=Pro TV| access-date=8 June 2019|language=ro}}</ref> On the same day, the [[Constitutional Court of Moldova]] declared unconstitutional her designation for this position as well as the appointment of the Government of the Republic of Moldova, which sparked the [[2019 Moldovan constitutional crisis|2019 constitutional crisis]].<ref name="Constitutional Court Initial Decision">{{Citation | url=http://www.constcourt.md/libview.php?l=ro&idc=7&id=1497&t=/Media/Noutati/Curtea-Constitutionala-a-examinat-decretele-Preedintelui-Republicii-Moldova-privind-desemnarea-in-functia-de-Prim-Ministru-si-numirea-Guvernului| title=Curtea Constituţională a examinat decretele Președintelui Republicii Moldova privind desemnarea în funcţia de Prim-Ministru şi numirea Guvernului| newspaper=constcourt.md| access-date=8 June 2019|language=ro}}</ref> However, on 15 June 2019, the Constitutional Court revised and repealed its previous decisions declaring the [[Sandu Cabinet]] to have been constitutionally created.<ref name="Constitutional Court decision revised">{{Citation | url=http://www.constcourt.md/libview.php?l=en&id=1509&idc=7&t=/Media/News//| title=Constitutional Court Revised Acts Delivered on 7-9 June 2019| newspaper=constcourt.md| access-date=15 June 2019|language=en}}</ref>

The next day, she called for the restoration of public order, discouraging citizens from attending local rallies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gov.md/en/content/prime-minister-directs-message-citizens-moldova|title={{!}} Government of Republic of Moldova|website=gov.md|access-date=27 September 2019}}</ref> In June 2019, she lifted a March 2017 ban by former Prime Minister Filip of official visits by government officials to [[Russia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tass.com/politics/1068807/amp|title=Moldovan PM plans to visit Russia|website=tass.com|access-date=27 September 2019}}</ref> In one of her first interviews to foreign media, she announced her intention to request that the [[United States Treasury]] add [[Vlad Plahotniuc]] to the [[Magnitsky List]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/odr/maia-sandu-the-politician-at-the-heart-of-moldovas-quiet-revolution/|title=An interview with Maia Sandu, the politician at the heart of Moldova's quiet revolution|website=openDemocracy|access-date=27 September 2019}}</ref> In August, Sandu asked the State Chancellery to prepare a draft decree where 23 August was declared to be the [[European Day of Remembrance for Victims of Stalinism and Nazism]] instead of the regular [[Liberation from Fascist Occupation Day|Liberation Day]]. The decree was opposed by her coalition partner, the [[PSRM]], with Moldova's president and ex-PSRM leader [[Igor Dodon]] announcing that he will celebrate the date in the old style, rejecting Sandu's proposal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://balkaninsight.com/2019/08/15/pms-plan-to-commemorate-victims-of-totalitarianism-divides-moldova/|title=PM's Plan to Commemorate Victims of Totalitarianism Divides Moldova|date=15 August 2019|website=Balkan Insight|language=en-US|access-date=27 September 2019}}</ref>

Under Maia Sandu, Moldova began taking steps towards the [[European Union]] as Sandu herself is pro-E.U. Maia Sandu was ousted as prime minister on 12 November 2019, following a vote of [[no confidence]]. She remained as a caretaker of the office until the formation of a new government.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.realitatea.md/ce-urmeaza-dupa-demiterea-guvernului-sandu--prevederile-constitutiei_102362.html|title=Ce urmeaza dupa demiterea Guvernului Sandu. Prevederile Constituției|date=12 November 2019|website=realitatea.md}}</ref>

== Political views ==

=== Transnistria ===
She declared that [[Operational Group of Russian Forces|Operational Group of Russian Forces (OGRF)]] should withdraw from the breakaway [[Transnistria]], saying to the [[RBK TV|RBK]] that although they guard ammunition depots, "there are no bilateral agreements on the OGRF and on the weapons depots". She also stated that its her position that the "mission should be transformed into an OSCE civilian observer mission".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Times|first=The Moscow|date=2020-11-30|title=Pull Russian Troops Out of Moldova, New President Says|url=https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2020/11/30/pull-russian-troops-out-of-moldova-new-president-says-a72184|access-date=2020-12-04|website=The Moscow Times|language=en}}</ref>

==Controversies==

=== Politics ===
In September 2016, Sandu instituted proceedings against the State Chancellery, requesting to be presented the shorthand from the Cabinet meeting where the state guarantees for the three bankrupted banks (Banca Socială, Unibank and Banca de Economii) had been approved.<ref name="Crimemoldova.com_2019-10-01">{{Citation | url=http://crimemoldova.com/news/politika/cancelaria-de-stat-ac-ionat-n-judecat-de-maia-sandu-pentru-refuzul-de-a-prezenta-informa-iile-despre/?sphrase_id=1483| title=Cancelaria de Stat acționată în judecată de Maia Sandu| newspaper=Crimemoldova.com| access-date=1 October 2019}}</ref> Prime Minister [[Pavel Filip]] published on his Facebook page, the shorthand of the last Cabinet meeting, when the decision on granting the emergency credit for the Banca de Economii was adopted. The shorthand included the speeches of former NBM governor Dorin Drăguțanu, former Prime Minister [[Chiril Gaburici]], and Sandu's own speeches from the time as minister of education. It is mentioned that at the end the decision was voted unanimously. The shorthand was not signed.<ref name="Crimemoldova.com_2019/10/01">{{Citation | url=https://crimemoldova.com/news/politika/premierul-pavel-filip-a-publicat-stenograma-ultimei-edin-e-de-guvern-privind-acordarea-creditului-de/| title=Și Maia Sandu a votat pentru acordarea creditului de urgenţă în cazul BEM, Filip a desecretizat stenograma ședinței de guvern| newspaper=Crimemoldova.com| access-date=1 October 2019}}</ref>

In 2016, within the debates for the presidential elections, Sandu was asked by the socialist [[Ion Ceban]] if she voted for the airport concession at the Cabinet meeting of 29 May 2013. In reply, Sandu said that she did not attend the Government meeting in which the airport concession was voted. However, the socialists lifted the archive and obtained the video recording showing that Sandu participated in the meeting and voted for the concession of [[Chișinău International Airport]].<ref name="Actualitati.md_2016-10-19">{{Citation | url=https://actualitati.md/md/video-dovada-minciunii-maia-sandu-a-votat-pentru-concesionarea-aeroportului| title=(video) Dovada minciunii: Maia Sandu a votat pentru concesionarea aeroportului| newspaper=Actualitati.md| date= 19 October 2016| access-date= 1 October 2019}}</ref>

=== Historical ===
Regarding former leader of Romania [[Ion Antonescu]], Sandu said in 2018 that he was "a historical figure about whom we may say both good and bad things". Her statements were sharply criticized by the Jewish Community of Moldova (CERM), who issued an open letter stating: "The lack of sanctions for... Holocaust denial and glorification of fascism in Moldovan legislation allows some opinion leaders and political leaders to not be held accountable for such acts, and lets them create their public image by distorting and revising historical facts and fueling inter-ethnic and inter-religious discrimination and hate".<ref name="Jcm.md/ru/all-news/scrisoare-cerm-cu-privire-lider-pas-maja-sandu_2019-02-07">{{Citation|title=Scrisoare deschisă a Comunității Evreiești din Republica Moldova cu privire la declarația liderului PAS Maia Sandu|url=https://www.jcm.md/ru/all-news/scrisoare-cerm-cu-privire-lider-pas-maja-sandu|newspaper=Jcm.md/ru/all-news/scrisoare-cerm-cu-privire-lider-pas-maja-sandu|access-date=8 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="Radio Europa Liberă_2019-07-11">{{Citation | url=https://moldova.europalibera.org/a/anti-semitismul-%C3%AEn-cre%C8%99tere-%C3%AEn-2018-raport-anual-maia-sandu-men%C8%9Bionata-pentru-o-declaratie-despre-antonescu/29914340.html| title=Antisemitismul, în creștere în 2018: raport anual (VIDEO)| newspaper=Radio Europa Liberă| access-date=11 July 2019}}</ref> Sandu replied to this accusation in later interviews by stating "I regret that my words about the dictator Ion Antonescu were made an object of interpretation... My attitude towards any criminal regime of the 20th century, whether Nazi or communist, which have millions of lives on their consciences, is well known and unequivocally negative. Ion Antonescu was a war criminal, rightly condemned by the international community for war crimes against Jewish and Roma people".<ref>{{Cite web|title=«Мое отношение к любым преступным режимам отрицательно».
