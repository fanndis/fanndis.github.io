'''Karla Camila Cabello Estrabao''' ({{IPAc-en|k|ə|ˈ|m|iː|l|ə|_|k|ə|ˈ|b|eɪ|oʊ}}; {{IPA-es|kaˈmila kaˈβeʎo|lang}}; born March 3, 1997)<ref>{{cite web |last=Braidwood |first=Ella |title=21 geeky facts about Camila Cabello |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/camila-cabello-facts-2252388 |website=[[NME]] |accessdate=February 6, 2019 |date=March 6, 2018}}</ref> is a Cuban-American <!-- Please discuss any changes to her nationality -->singer and songwriter. She rose to prominence as a member of the girl group [[Fifth Harmony]], formed on [[The X Factor (American TV series)|''The X Factor USA'']] in 2012, signing a joint record deal with [[Syco Music]] and [[Epic Records]]. While in Fifth Harmony, Cabello began to establish herself as a solo artist with the release of the collaborations "[[I Know What You Did Last Summer (song)|I Know What You Did Last Summer]]" with [[Shawn Mendes]], and "[[Bad Things (Machine Gun Kelly and Camila Cabello song)|Bad Things]]" with [[Machine Gun Kelly (rapper)|Machine Gun Kelly]], the latter reaching number four on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. After leaving the group in late 2016, Cabello released several other collaborations, including "[[Hey Ma (Pitbull and J Balvin song)|Hey Ma]]" by [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]] and [[J Balvin]] for [[The Fate of the Furious: The Album|''The Fate of the Furious'' soundtrack]], and her debut solo single "[[Crying in the Club]]".

Cabello's debut studio album, ''[[Camila (album)|Camila]]'' (2018), reached number one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. The [[Latin music]]-influenced pop album was critically well-received, and received a Platinum certification by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). Its lead single "[[Havana (Camila Cabello song)|Havana]]" topped the charts in several countries including the US and UK, and follow-up single "[[Never Be the Same (Camila Cabello song)|Never Be the Same]]" reached the top ten in multiple countries. Cabello's 2019 duet with Mendes, "[[Señorita (Shawn Mendes and Camila Cabello song)|Señorita]]", became her second single to top the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. Her second studio album, ''[[Romance (Camila Cabello album)|Romance]]'' (2019), peaked at number three on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart and number one in Canada. Cabello is set to star in an upcoming film adaptation of ''[[Cinderella (2021 film)|Cinderella]]'' (2021), for [[Sony Pictures]].

Cabello has amassed billions of streams on music platforms. She has the [[List of best-selling singles|best-selling digital single]] of 2018 according to [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] (IFPI). Cabello has won many awards throughout her career, including two [[Latin Grammy Awards]], five [[American Music Awards]], and one [[Billboard Music Award]]. She has also received three [[Grammy Award]] nominations.

==Early life==
Cabello was born in [[Havana]], Cuba<ref name=popsugar /><ref name="All Music Bio">{{cite web |title=Biography & History |publisher=AllMusic |first=Neil Z. |last=Yeung |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/camila-cabello-mn0003392996/biography | accessdate=May 1, 2017}}</ref> to Sinuhe Estrabao and Alejandro Cabello, and grew up in the town of [[Cojímar]] in [[Habana del Este|East Havana]]. Her father was born in [[Mexico City]] and is a [[Mexican immigration to Cuba|Mexican who moved to Cuba]]. She has a younger sister named Sofia.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.capitalfm.com/artists/camila-cabello/empowering-message-instagram/|title=Camila Cabello's Empowering Message About Her Sister Is So Important|last=Marti|first=Diana|work=Capital FM|accessdate=2018-09-06|language=en-UK}}</ref> For most of her early life, Cabello and her family moved back and forth between Havana and Mexico City. When Cabello was five, she relocated to [[Miami, Florida]], in the United States, with her mother; her father was unable to obtain a visa at the time, and joined the family approximately 18 months later.<ref name=popsugar>{{cite web|url=http://www.popsugar.com/latina/Camila-Cabello-Her-Cuban-Background-42239921 |title=Camila Cabello: 'Our Dreams Were Bigger Than Our Fears' |publisher=PopSugar.com |date=October 5, 2016 |first=Camila |last=Cabello |archivedate=November 30, 2016 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161130123537/http://www.popsugar.com/latina/Camila-Cabello-Her-Cuban-Background-42239921 |url-status=live |quote=...my mom and I immigrated to America. I was almost 7 at the time, born in Havana, Cuba. My papá is puro Mexicano... |df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://ew.com/article/2016/09/16/camila-cabello-immigration-story/amp/|title=Camila Cabello shares immigration story|date=September 16, 2016|accessdate=June 26, 2019|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://time.com/collection/american-voices-2017/4927699/american-voices-camila-cabello/|title=Cabello Going Solo|website=Time.com|date=2017|accessdate=June 26, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/pop/7751677/camila-cabello-writing-music-immigrant-cuba-to-america|title=Camila Cabello Is Writing Music About Her Family's Journey From Cuba: 'I Want to Make a Love Song for Immigrants'|website=Billboard.com|date=April 5, 2017|accessdate=June 26, 2019}}</ref> Cabello acquired American citizenship in 2008.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://time.com/collection/american-voices-2017/4927699/american-voices-camila-cabello/ |title=Cabello Going Solo |last=Bacle |first=Ariana |date=2017 |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |accessdate=May 8, 2018}}</ref> She attended [[Miami Palmetto High School]] but left in the 2012–2013 school year, while she was in 9th grade, to pursue her singing career. She later earned her high school diploma.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thepalmettopanther.com/qa-with-palmetto-student-camila-cabello/|title=Q&A with Palmetto student Camila Cabello|last=Arguelles|first=Victoria|work=The Panther|accessdate=2017-10-17|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Career==
===2012–2016: ''The X Factor'' and Fifth Harmony===

Camila Cabello auditioned for ''The X Factor'' in [[Greensboro, North Carolina]] with [[Aretha Franklin]]'s "[[Respect (song)|Respect]]",<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.nrj.fr/artistes/camila-cabello/actus/camila-cabello-de-candidate-a-the-x-factor-a-superstar-355912 |title=Camila Cabello: de candidate à "The X Factor" à superstar |first=De La Forterie |last=Sonia |date=March 15, 2018 |work=[[NRJ]] |accessdate=March 18, 2018 |language=French}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/music/fifth-harmony-member-camila-cabello-quits-group-1.12776718 |title=Fifth Harmony member Camila Cabello quits group |last=Lovece |first=Frank |date=December 19, 2016 |work=Newsday |accessdate=March 18, 2018}}</ref> however, her audition was not aired because the series did not get the rights for the song. After elimination during the "bootcamp" portion of the process in Miami, Florida, Cabello was called back to the stage along with other contestants [[Ally Brooke]], [[Normani]], [[Lauren Jauregui]], and [[Dinah Jane]] to form the girl group that would later become known as [[Fifth Harmony]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fifthharmonyofficial.com/bio/|title=Bio: Fifth Harmony |publisher=Fifth Harmony Official|accessdate=January 14, 2015| archivedate= January 3, 2016 |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20160103050655/http://fifthharmonyofficial.com/bio}}</ref> After finishing in third place on the show they signed a joint deal with [[Syco Music]], owned by [[Simon Cowell]], and [[Epic Records]], [[L.A. Reid]]'s record label.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Phull|first1=Hardeep|title=How losing 'X-Factor' made Fifth Harmony the ultimate winners |url=https://nypost.com/2015/08/22/how-losing-x-factor-made-fifth-harmony-the-ultimate-winners/|website=New York Post|accessdate=January 3, 2017|date=August 22, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Gomez|first1=Patrick|title=Simon Cowell Signs Fifth Harmony|url=http://people.com/tv/simon-cowell-signs-fifth-harmony/|website=People|accessdate=January 3, 2017|date=January 17, 2013}}</ref>

The group released the EP ''[[Better Together (EP)|Better Together]]'' (2013) along with the studio albums ''[[Reflection (Fifth Harmony album)|Reflection]]'' (2015) and ''[[7/27]]'' (2016). The latter two generated the singles "[[Worth It (Fifth Harmony song)|Worth It]]" and "[[Work from Home (song)|Work from Home]]", respectively, which reached the top 10 in several international charts.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5665538/fifth-harmony/biography |title=Fifth Harmony Biography |date=December 19, 2016 |work=Billboard}}</ref> From 2013 through the end of 2016, Cabello performed in various [[Fifth Harmony#Tours|Fifth Harmony tours]].
