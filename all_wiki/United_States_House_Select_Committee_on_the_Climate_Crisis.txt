The '''House Select Committee on the [[Climate crisis|Climate Crisis]]''' is a [[select or special committee (United States)|select committee]] established in the [[116th United States Congress]] in 2019 when [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrats]] regained the majority of the [[United States House of Representatives]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/science/archive/2018/12/house-democrats-form-new-committee-climate-crisis/579109/|title=Democrats Establish a New House 'Climate Crisis' Committee|work=The Atlantic|date=December 28, 2018|accessdate=December 28, 2018}}</ref> The Chair is Congresswoman [[Kathy Castor]] of Florida.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2018/dec/28/karen-castor-house-committee-climate-change|title=Karen Castor named to lead restored House panel on climate change|work=The Guardian|date=December 28, 2018|accessdate=December 28, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.loe.org/shows/segments.html?programID=19-P13-00002&segmentID=1|title=Living on Earth: Capitol Hill Panel on Climate Crisis|last=Curwood|first=Steve|date=January 11, 2019|website=Living on Earth|publisher=World Media Foundation|language=en|access-date=January 15, 2019}}</ref> The committee has no mandate or subpoena power to compel witnesses to testify.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://psmag.com/environment/democrats-would-be-crazy-not-to-embrace-the-green-new-deal|title=Democrats Would Be Foolish Not to Embrace the Green New Deal|last=Zhang|first=Sharon|date=January 15, 2019|website=Pacific Standard|language=en|access-date=January 15, 2019}}</ref> 

Its predecessor was the [[United States House Select Committee on Energy Independence and Global Warming]], which existed from 2007 to 2011, and was not renewed when the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republicans]] gained control of the House for the [[112th Congress]].<ref>{{USBill|112|hres|5|which=1}}</ref>

[[Nancy Pelosi]], in her then-role as [[House Minority Leader]], called for the Select Committee a week prior to Election Day 2018,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eenews.net/stories/1060104843|title=HOUSE: Pelosi wants to resurrect select climate panel|website=[[Environment & Energy Publishing]] |language=en|access-date=2019-03-11}}</ref> telling the ''New York Times'' she wanted it to "'prepare the way with evidence' for energy conservation and other climate change mitigation legislation...Pelosi said it was clearly still needed to educate the public about the impact of more frequent [[extreme weather]] events."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/31/us/politics/democrats-midterm-elections.html|title=First Up if Democrats Win: Campaign and Ethics Changes, Infrastructure and Drug Prices|last=Fandos|first=Nicholas|date=2018-10-31|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-03-11|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> In November and December of 2018, youth climate activists with the [[Sunrise Movement]] pushed House Democrats to form a select committee with the mandate to draft "[[Green New Deal]]" legislation, working with incoming freshman Rep. [[Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez]], who proposed language for the committee's authorization.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://docs.google.com/document/d/1jxUzp9SZ6-VB-4wSm8sselVMsqWZrSrYpYC9slHKLzo/edit|title=FINAL Select Committee for a Green New Deal|last=Ocasio-Cortez|first=Alexandria}}</ref> The activists staged a series of sit-ins in the offices of [[Nancy Pelosi]], [[Steny Hoyer]], and [[Jim McGovern (American politician)|Jim McGovern]], the incoming Speaker, Majority Leader, and Rules Committee chair. About two dozen Democratic members of Congress supported their proposal, but the incoming chairs of the Energy & Commerce and Natural Resources Committees, Reps. [[Frank Pallone]] and [[Raul Grijalva]], opposed it.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.thenation.com/article/can-the-blue-wave-deliver-a-green-new-deal/|title=Can the Blue Wave Deliver a Green New Deal?|last=Cook|first=Christopher D.|magazine=The Nation|date=2018-11-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://thehill.com/policy/energy-environment/420588-second-round-of-capitol-protests-demand-climate-change-action|title=More protesters storm Pelosi's office demanding climate change action|date=2018-12-10|newspaper=The Hill|last1=Green|first1=Miranda|last2=Cama|first2=Timothy}}</ref>

The committee held its first field hearing on August 1, 2019 at the [[University of Colorado Boulder|University of Colorado at Boulder, Colorado]]. The witnesses started with Colorado Governor [[Jared Polis]], followed by a panel that included the mayors of [[Boulder, Colorado|Boulder]] and [[Ft. Collins]], an expert in rural agricultural energy issues from [[Colorado State University]], a representative of the oil and gas industry, and the director of the Boulder university's chief sustainability officer.
