'''Peter Klein''' is an American businessman mostly known for holding the chief financial officer position at [[Microsoft|Microsoft Corporation]]. On April 18, 2013, Microsoft announced Klein would be leaving the company at the end of Microsoft's current fiscal year.<ref name="vbresign">{{cite web |title= Microsoft CFO Peter Klein to depart after 4 years on the job |url= https://venturebeat.com/2013/04/18/microsoft-cfo-peter-klein-leaving/ |work= VentureBeat |last=Ludwig | first=Sean |date= April 18, 2013}}</ref>

He started at Microsoft in 2002 as the CFO of Microsoft's Server & Tools Business Group. He then served as the corporate vice president and CFO of Microsoft's Business Division (MBD), overseeing the financial performance of the division which includes the [[Microsoft Office]] System, Unified Communications, Microsoft Business Solutions, among others. After the departure of [[Chris Liddell]] in 2009, he was appointed the company's [[Chief financial officer|CFO]], a position he held for 4 years, before resigning in 2013.<ref>{{cite web |title= Microsoft CFO Peter Klein Resigns |url= http://www.tomshardware.com/news/CFO-Chief-Financial-Officer-Peter-Klein-Stgeve-Ballmer-Resigns,22177.html |work= Tom's Hardware |last=Parrish | first=Kevin |date= April 23, 2013}}</ref> He was succeeded by [[Amy Hood]].

Klein holds a bachelor's degree from [[Yale University]] and a Master of Business Administration from the [[University of Washington]].
