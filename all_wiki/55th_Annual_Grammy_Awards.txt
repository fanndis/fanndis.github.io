The '''55th Annual [[Grammy Award]]s''' were held on February 10, 2013, at the [[Staples Center]] in [[Los Angeles]] honoring the best in music for the recording year beginning October 1, 2011 through September 30, 2012. The show was broadcast on [[CBS]] at 8 p.m. ET/PT and was hosted for the second time by [[LL Cool J]]. The "Pre-Telecast Ceremony" was streamed live from LA's Nokia Theater at the official Grammy website. Nominations were announced on December 5, 2012 on prime-time television as part of "The GRAMMY Nominations Concert Live! – Countdown to Music's Biggest Night", a one-hour special co-hosted by LL Cool J & [[Taylor Swift]] and broadcast live on CBS from the [[Bridgestone Arena]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/news/grammy-awards/370372-grammy-awards-2013-nominations-frank-ocean-fun|title= 2013 Grammy Nominations|date=December 6, 2012 |work=MTV|accessdate=December 6, 2012 }}</ref> <!--Per [[WP:NCM]], do not replicate stylized typography of band names.-->[[Fun (band)|Fun]], [[Frank Ocean]], [[Mumford & Sons]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Kanye West]] and [[Dan Auerbach]] received the most nominations with six each.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/dec/06/grammys-male-artists-dominate |title= Grammys 2013: male artists lead nominations|date=December 6, 2012 |work=Guardian UK|accessdate=December 6, 2012|location=London}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/music/la-et-ms-grammy-awards-2013,0,4507240.story
|title= Grammy Awards 2013: Mumford & Sons' 'Babel' wins album of the year|date=February 11, 2012|work= Los Angeles Times|accessdate=February 11, 2013|first=Randy|last=Lewis}}</ref>
 
[[Gotye]] and [[Kimbra]] won the [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] for "[[Somebody That I Used to Know]]", becoming the second Australian and first New Zealand act to win the award. [[Mumford & Sons]] won the [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] for ''[[Babel (Mumford & Sons album)|Babel]]'', and [[Fun (band)|Fun]] won the [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] (with [[Jeff Bhasker]]) for "[[We Are Young]]" as well as the [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]].<ref name="AP">{{cite news|url=https://www.google.com/hostednews/ap/article/ALeqM5j9DitZBWTyQDuCSInkoLIKzqvl8Q?docId=496f29a430ac4935ac57244e581bd3e5 |title=Grammy Awards voters spread the love all around |date=February 11, 2013 |agency=Associated Press |accessdate=February 11, 2013 }}</ref> [[Kelly Clarkson]] won the [[Best Pop Vocal Album]] for ''[[Stronger (Kelly Clarkson album)|Stronger]]'', becoming the first and only artist to win the award twice.<ref name="AP" /> Dan Auerbach won the most number of awards during the ceremony, with five (including three as part of [[The Black Keys]]); followed by The Black Keys, Gotye, [[Jay-Z]], [[Skrillex]], [[Kanye West]], with three each.<ref name="AP" /> Other multiple winners include: [[Chick Corea]], Fun, Kimbra, Mumford & Sons, [[Frank Ocean]], [[Matt Redman]] and [[Esperanza Spalding]] with two awards each.<ref name="AP" /><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/feb/10/grammys-2013-live-blog-coverage?intcmp=239|title= The Grammys 2013 – live coverage of the 55th annual music awards|date=February 11, 2012|work= Guardian UK|accessdate=February 11, 2013|location=London|first1=Emma G|last1=Keller|first2=Brian|last2=Braiker}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/9861257/Grammys-2013-Mumford-and-Sons-steal-Americas-thunder.html|title= Grammys 2013: Mumford & Sons steal America’s thunder|date=February 11, 2012|work= Daily Telegraph|accessdate=February 11, 2013|location=London|first=Neil|last=McCormick}}</ref>

The Recording Academy introduced three new categories to the 78 awards previously presented at the [[2012 Grammy Awards|54th ceremony]]—[[Best Classical Compendium]], [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Jazz Album|Best Latin Jazz Album]], and [[Best Urban Contemporary Album]], bringing it to a total of 81 awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news|title=News|date=April 30, 2017|website=GRAMMY.com}}</ref> 70 of them were presented at the pre-telecast at the [[Nokia Theatre L.A. Live|Nokia Theatre]], with the remaining 11 were presented at the main ceremony.<ref name="Pre-telecast">{{cite news|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/music/la-et-ms-grammys-2013-pretelecast-stream-live-nokia-theatre-20130205,0,4179719.story|title=Grammys 2013: Pre-telecast to stream live from Nokia Theatre|date=February 5, 2013|accessdate=February 9, 2013|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|publisher=[[Tribune Company]]|first=Gerrick D.|last=Kennedy}}</ref> [[Bruce Springsteen]] received the [[MusiCares Person of the Year]] award on February 8, 2013 at the 23rd Grammy Benefit Gala at the [[Los Angeles Convention Center]], two nights prior to the main ceremony.<ref name="MusiCares">{{Cite web|url=http://www.grammy.org/recording-academy/press-release/jun-28-2012-400-am|title=Twenty-time Grammy® Winner Bruce Springsteen to be honored as the 2013 MusiCares® Person of the Year}}</ref> The official poster was designed by Artist Erika Iris Simmons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/news/artist-erika-iris-simmons-creates-official-artwork-for-55th-grammy-awards |title=Artist Erika Iris Simmons Creates Official Artwork For 55th GRAMMY Awards|publisher=[[National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences|The Recording Academy]]|date=November 30, 2012}}</ref> The program producer is AEG Ehrlich Ventures, with Ken Ehrlich serving as executive producer, Louis J. Horvitz as director and David Wild and Ken Ehrlich as writers.<ref name="JT">[http://www.grammy.com/news/justin-timberlake-to-perform-on-musics-biggest-night Justin Timberlake To Perform On Music's Biggest Night], Grammy.com, January 30, 2013</ref>

==Most cumulative Grammy Awards==
For many winners, this year's Grammy Award wasn't their first. In fact, over 40 winners now have achieved three or more wins.
These artists have received the most cumulative Grammy Awards:

*20th Grammy: [[Pat Metheny]]
*19th, 20th and 21st Grammy: [[Kanye West]]
*19th and 20th Grammy: [[Chick Corea]]
*17th Grammy: [[Yo-Yo Ma]]
*15th, 16th and 17th Grammy: [[Jay Z]]
*15th Grammy: [[Alicia Keys]] 
*14th Grammy: [[Beyoncé]] (''plus three as member of [[Destiny's Child]]'')
*14th Grammy: [[Steve Epstein]]
*13th Grammy: [[T-Bone Burnett]]
*12th Grammy: [[Eminem]]
*12th Grammy: [[Jay David Saks]]
*11th Grammy: [[Michael Tilson Thomas]]
*10th Grammy: [[James Levine]]
*10th Grammy: [[Bonnie Raitt]]
*9th Grammy: [[Paul McCartney]] (''plus 7 as member of [[The Beatles]]'')
*9th Grammy: [[Adele]]
*9th Grammy: Richard King
* 7th Grammy: [[Taylor Swift]]
* 6th Grammy: [[Rihanna]]
* 6th Grammy : Carrie Underwood

The winner with the longest span of Grammy wins this year was [[Gil Evans]], whose first win was in 1960. This year he won a posthumous award. The longest span for any artist who is still active was for [[Paul McCartney]], who first won an award in 1965 (as a member of [[The Beatles]]).
[[Janis Ian]] won her first Grammy in 37 years, having last won in 1976 in the [[Best Female Pop Vocal Performance]] category.
