'''American City Business Journals''' ('''ACBJ''') is an American newspaper chain based in [[Charlotte, North Carolina]]. ACBJ publishes under six brands, which together reach four million readers: [[The Business Journals]], which publishes local business news for 44 markets in the United States, [[Hemmings Motor News]], [[Street & Smith's]] Sports Business Daily, [[Inside Lacrosse]], AmericanInno, and Bizwomen.com. The company is owned by [[Advance Publications]].<ref name=acbj/>{{Failed verification|date=March 2020|reason=The target page, and the off-linked 'more info' page, do not mention ownership of the chain ... at least the present version does not.}}

==History==
The company began in 1982 with the Kansas City Business Journal.<ref name="postdispatch" />  As of 1986, Mike Russell was [[chairman]] of the firm.<ref name="postdispatch" />

ACBJ [[went public]] in 1985 and was traded as an [[Over-the-counter (finance)|over the counter stock]].<ref name="postdispatch" />

In 1986, ACBJ bought out the full complement of publications from Business Journal Publications, which included the St. Louis Business Journal, and a number of other business journals and legal publications.<ref name="postdispatch" />

[[Ray Shaw (journalist)|Ray Shaw]] joined ACBJ in 1989 and served as the company's chairman and [[chief executive officer]] for two decades, ending with his death in 2009.<ref name="Jackson" />  Under Ray Shaw's leadership, the company moved from [[Kansas City, Missouri]], to [[Charlotte, North Carolina]] and greatly increased the number of its publications.<ref name="Jackson">{{cite news|last=Shaw|first=Whitney|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/jacksonville/stories/2009/07/20/daily2.html|title=ACBJ Chairman Ray Shaw dies at 75|date=July 20, 2009|work=[[Jacksonville Business Journal]]|access-date=July 20, 2009|publisher=American City Business Journals|location=Jacksonville, Florida|url-access=registration<!--3 article limit without free registration-->}}</ref>

In 2009, ACBJ acquired [[Portfolio.com]], a website formerly associated with ''[[Condé Nast Portfolio]]'', and redirected the URL to the ACBJ website.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://talkingbiznews.com/they-talk-biz-news/more-on-the-portfoliocom-relaunch/|title=More on the Portfolio.com relaunch|date=December 8, 2009|website=Talking Biz News|publisher=[[Chris Roush]]|type=Blog|access-date=March 3, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite press release|title=Portfolio.com Returns as National Voice for Strategic Business Leaders|date=December 7, 2009|publisher=ACBJ|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/portfoliocom-returns-as-national-voice-for-strategic-business-leaders-78714897.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160601060605/http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/portfoliocom-returns-as-national-voice-for-strategic-business-leaders-78714897.html|archive-date=June 1, 2016}}{{Self-published source}}</ref>

==The Business Journals==
<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:Bizflag.gif|frame|Bizjournals logo|{{deletable image-caption|1=Monday, February 25, 2008}}]] -->
''The Business Journals'' is the online media division of American City Business Journals which operates websites for each of the company's 40 print business journals, as well as its web-only local business news and information sites for [[Chicago]], Illinois, [[Los Angeles]], California and [[New York City]], New York.

The site contains local business news from around the nation, updated throughout the day, along with an archive that contains more than 5 million business news articles published since 1996. The Business Journals' sites have more than four million unique visitors each month.

[[File:Best Places To Work awards.jpg|thumb|The Minneapolis-St. Paul Business Journal's Best Places To Work awards]]

===Forty under 40 Award===
''The Business Journal's Forty Under 40'' is an annual list compiled and published by the ''Business Journal'' newspapers since 1992.<ref>{{cite news|last=Kass|first=Mark|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/milwaukee/blog/2012/01/business-journal-names-forty-under-40.html|title=Business Journal names Forty under 40 winners|date=January 5, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/columbus/search/results?q=Forty+Under+40|title=Columbus Business First 40 under 40 search|date=January 20, 2012}}</ref> The purpose is to list the 40 most successful entrepreneurs under the age of 40. The 40 are usually nominated from within individual areas in range of the local business journal's publication.

===Best Places to Work Award===
''The Business Journal's Best Places to Work'' ranks top businesses in local areas for best employee experience. Rankings are determined based on surveys uncovering such variables as leadership, corporate culture, and communications. Some variation exists between different cities. For example, [[Minneapolis–Saint Paul|Minneapolis-St. Paul]] recognized 59 companies and 4 individuals in 2018,<ref>{{cite news|last=Kuehn|first=Nancy|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/twincities/news/2018/08/16/all-of-mspbjs-2018-best-places-to-work-honorees.html#i/11013046|title=Best Places to Work|date=August 16, 2018}}</ref> whereas Los Angeles will honor its top 100 in 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.eventbrite.com/e/los-angeles-business-journal-best-places-to-work-2019-tickets-55186403017|title=Los Angeles Business Journal Best Places to Work 2019|date=2019}}</ref>
