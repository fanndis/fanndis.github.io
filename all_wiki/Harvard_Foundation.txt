'''The Harvard Foundation for Intercultural and Race Relations''' was founded in 1981 by the [[President of Harvard University|President]] and Deans of [[Harvard University]] to improve relations among racial and ethnic groups at the University.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Keller |first1=Morton |last2=Keller |first2=Phyllis |title=Making Harvard Modern: The Rise of America's University |publisher=Oxford University Press |date=November 15, 2001 |page=477 |isbn=978-0-195-14457-4}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2006/9/29/harvard-foundation-celebrates-25th-the-harvard |title=Harvard Foundation Celebrates 25th |publisher=The Harvard Crimson |accessdate=26 November 2013}}</ref><ref name="HarvardFoundation">{{cite web |url=http://www.harvardfoundation.fas.harvard.edu/icb/icb.do |title=The Harvard Foundation for Intercultural and Race Relations |publisher=The Harvard Foundation |accessdate=26 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131222000201/http://www.harvardfoundation.fas.harvard.edu/icb/icb.do |archive-date=22 December 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The Harvard Foundation sponsors activities to promote interracial and intercultural awareness in the Harvard community that range from student-led discussions on issues regarding [[Cultural diversity|diversity]] to ceremonies that highlight the [[Multiculturalism|multicultural]] contributions of people within and beyond [[Harvard College|Harvard]].<ref name="HarvardFoundation" /><ref>{{cite book |last=Fox |first=Dov |title=The Truth About Harvard: A Behind the Scenes Look at Admissions and Life on Campus |publisher=Princeton Review |date=September 14, 2004 |page=41 |isbn=978-0-375-76435-6}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/1997/04.24/CultivatingFrie.html |title=Harvard Foundation: 16 Years of Promoting Cultural Understanding and Harmony |publisher=The Harvard Gazette |accessdate=26 November 2013}}</ref> The founding director of the Foundation was Dr. [[S. Allen Counter]].
