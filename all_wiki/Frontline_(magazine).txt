'''''Frontline''''' is a [[fortnightly]] [[English language]] magazine published by [[The Hindu Group]] of publications from [[Chennai]], [[India]]. [[R Vijaya Sankar]] is the editor-in-chief of the magazine. As a current affairs magazine, it covers domestic and International news. ''Frontline'' gives a prominent place to various issues of development and hindrances in the Indian states. Apart from topics of politics and [[political economy]], it also covers a wide range of topics including Arts, books, cinema, Science and English language.

==History==
''Frontline'' was first published in December 1984.<ref>{{cite news |title=The Hindu relaunches Frontline |url=http://www.afaqs.com/news/story/35408_The-Hindu-relaunches-Frontline |accessdate=9 December 2015 |work=Afaqs |date=21 September 2012}}</ref> It was originally intended to be a newspaper when it was started by the founders, they later had differences in opinion regarding the content and intent of the publication and the magazine was sold to [[PL Investments]] Ltd, which later sold it to [[The Hindu Group]].

==Contributors and perspective==
Its regular contributors include known [[Progressivism|progressive]] writers such as C. P. Chandrasekhar, [[Praful Bidwai]], R.K. Raghavan, [[Jayati Ghosh]] and Bhaskar Ghose. [[Aijaz Ahmad]], a [[Marxist]] [[literary]] critic and political analyst, also contributes occasional essays on various topics. [[Vijay Prashad]], the Marxist historian, writes a regular "Letter from America" for the magazine, as well as reports on American issues.

Frontline is noted for its serious coverage of issues.<ref name="The Invisible Intellectual">{{cite web |url=http://www.zmag.org/sustainers/content/2004-03/11deshpande.cfm |title=The Invisible Intellectual |date=11 March 2004 |work=ZNET |accessdate=27 July 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929135538/http://www.zmag.org/sustainers/content/2004-03/11deshpande.cfm |archive-date=29 September 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The magazine has made it a point to analyse issues related to the [[working class]]es,<ref name="Weavers in Distress">{{cite web |url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl1917/19171250.htm |title=Weavers in Distress |date=7 August 2002 |work=Frontline |accessdate=16 August 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070917015050/http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl1917/19171250.htm |archive-date=17 September 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> the unorganized sectors, [[tribe|tribal]] regions,<ref name="Tribal Issues">{{cite web |url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2211/stories/20050603001508800.htm |title=Tribal Issues |date=3 June 2005 |work=Frontline |accessdate=16 August 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070812053118/http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2211/stories/20050603001508800.htm |archive-date=12 August 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> etc. in India.

Since, 1991, the magazine has opposed the economic reforms of successive governments comprising disinvestment<ref name="Disinvestment Designs">{{cite web |url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2212/stories/20050617004901000.htm |title=Disinvestment Designs |date=4 June 2005 |work=Frontline |accessdate=16 August 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071022110220/http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2212/stories/20050617004901000.htm |archive-date=22 October 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> in state owned corporations, opening of areas like telecom and insurance to private and foreign players among others.

==Awards==
Regular correspondent, [[Dionne Bunsha]] received the [[Ramnath Goenka]] in 2007 for outstanding [[Journalism|reporting]] in the field of environment and books.<ref name="'Awards for Bunsha">{{cite web |url=http://www.hindu.com/2007/07/18/stories/2007071856131500.htm |title=Award for Bunsha |date=18 July 2007 |work=The Hindu |accessdate=1 August 2007}}</ref> Journalist, [[P.Sainath]], a frequent contributor to Frontline won the 2007 [[Ramon Magsaysay Award]] in the fields of creative arts, journalism and communication.<ref name="Sainath">{{cite web |url=http://www.hindu.com/2007/08/01/stories/2007080156480100.htm |title=Award for Sainath |date=1 August 2007 |work=The Hindu |accessdate=1 August 2007}}</ref>

Other journalists such as [[Asha Krishnakumar]] and [[Praveen Swami]] are also recipients of prestigious awards in journalism, such as the Lorenzo Natali prize.<ref name="Awards">{{cite web |url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2017/stories/20030829008513400.htm |title=Awards for journalists |date=29 August 2003 |work=Frontline |accessdate=1 August 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090603075850/http://www.hinduonnet.com/fline/fl2017/stories/20030829008513400.htm |archive-date=3 June 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
