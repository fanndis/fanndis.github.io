'''Moshe Lax''' is an American businessman from [[New York City|New York]], known for his legal problems and his business association with [[Ivanka Trump]].

==Personal life and education==
Lax grew up in [[Brooklyn]]<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017">{{cite news |last1=Schreckinger |first1=Ben |title=The Trouble With Ivanka's Business Partner |url=https://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2017/08/27/ivanka-trump-business-partner-215544 |accessdate=19 August 2018 |work=[[POLITICO]] Magazine |date=27 August 2017 |language=en}}</ref> as the only son of the noted diamond dealer, real estate developer, and philanthropist [[Chaim Lax]]<ref name="Mishpacha 5 April 2017">{{cite news |last1=Besser |first1=Yisroel |title=Polish Your Diamond |url=http://www.mishpacha.com/Browse/Article/7641/Polish-Your-Diamond |accessdate=19 August 2018 |work=[[Mishpacha]] |date=5 April 2017}}</ref>  who partnered with Brooklyn developer [[Isaac Hager]] in gentrifying [[Williamsburg, Brooklyn]].<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Mairer |first1= Mark |last2=Small  |first2= Eddie |title= Isaac Hager's higher power - The developer is gaining outer borough market share, despite a trail of foreclosures and lawsuits from his early real estate days  |publisher=[[The Real Deal (magazine)|The Real Deal]]|date=May 1, 2018 |url= https://therealdeal.com/issues_articles/isaac-hagers-higher-power/ |accessdate=}}</ref> He attended local [[Hasidic]] schools and the religious school ([[yeshiva]]) of Rabbi [[Shaul Brus]].<ref name="Mishpacha 5 April 2017" />

After his marriage to Shaindy Lax,<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" /> he ended his religious studies ([[kollel]])<ref name="Mishpacha 5 April 2017" /> to found the '''Dynamic Diamonds Corp.''', a diamond sales business, together with his father.<ref name="Diamonds.net 25 July 2017">{{cite news |title=Fifth Avenue Diamond Gallery Set to Open |url=https://www.diamonds.net/News/NewsItem.aspx?ArticleID=59118&ArticleTitle=Fifth+Avenue+Diamond+Gallery+Set+to+Open |accessdate=19 August 2018 |work=Diamonds.net |agency=[[Rapaport News]] |date=25 July 2017}}</ref> He resides in a mansion in [[Riverdale, Bronx|Riverdale]] with his wife.<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" />

==Business career==
When his father died in 2008, Lax inherited his vast debts.<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" /> Lax was sued by the estate's creditors, who alleged in court that Lax had used shell companies to hide the estate's assets, and that he attempted to extort them and other members of the Orthodox Jewish community into giving up on the debts.<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" /> The lawsuit was eventually settled.<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" /> Lax was also the subject of several other lawsuits, many of which regarded unpaid debts and legal fees.<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" />

In 2017, Lax opened a two-story fashion gallery on [[Fifth Avenue]] in New York.<ref name="Diamonds.net 25 July 2017" /> The opening was attended by [[Tiffany Trump]], [[Kimberly Guilfoyle]] and Lax's landlord [[Eliot Spitzer]].<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" />

In August 2018, the U.S. [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] (DOJ) sued Lax for the payment of $60 million worth of taxes. The DOJ alleged that Lax, his father and his sister Zlaty Schwartz conducted complex fake transactions in order to fraudulently evade taxes.<ref name="Politico 19 August 2018">{{cite news |last1=Schreckinger |first1=Ben |title=DOJ Sues Ivanka's Ex-Business Partner for Massive Fraud |url=https://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2018/08/17/ivanka-trump-business-partner-justice-department-lawsuit-moshe-lax-219369 |accessdate=19 August 2018 |work=POLITICO Magazine |date=17 August 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

===Association with Ivanka and Donald Trump===
Lax was [[Ivanka Trump]]'s business partner for licensing her name in jewelry, and he helped her open an [[Upper East Side]] boutique in 2007.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Abrams |first1=Rachel |title=Ivanka Trump to End Fine Jewelry Line in Favor of Mass-Market One |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/13/business/ivanka-trump-jewelry.html |accessdate=19 August 2018 |work=The New York Times |date=13 March 2017 |language=en}}</ref> Lax became a friend of Ivanka Trump, her father [[Donald Trump]] and their family, and was invited to attend Donald Trump's 2016 [[2016 United States presidential election|election victory]] party and [[Inauguration of Donald Trump|inauguration]].<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" />

The business relationship between Lax and Ivanka Trump continued even after Lax's company defaulted on several payments in 2011, entangled Ivanka Trump in a legal dispute with another diamond dealer (which Lax lost in 2015), and continued to have other legal problems.<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" /> Lax was listed as chairman of Ivanka Trump Fine Jewelry until 2017,<ref name="Politico 27 August 2017" /> and she at one point owned part of his company.<ref name="Politico 19 August 2018" />

==Academia==
Moshe Lax is a [[Talmudic scholar]] of Jewish law and philosophy and has published a two-volume book titled "Derech Nesher".<ref>{{cite book |title=HebrewBooks.org Sefer Detail: דרך נשר - כרך ב -- משה לאקס |url=http://www.hebrewbooks.org/53494 |accessdate=25 September 2019}}</ref><ref>http://www.hebrewbooks.org/pdfpager.aspx?req=57757&st=&pgnum=5</ref>

Lax co-authored with Hillel E. Broder, PhD, "If I am here, then all is here: Towards a Phenomenological Existentialism in the Rabbinic Law of Beit Hillel".<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Broder |first1=Hillel |last2=Lax |first2=Moshe |title=“If I am here, then all is here”: Towards a Phenomenological Existentialism in the Rabbinic Law of Beit Hillel |journal=Hakirah: The Flatbush Journal of Jewish Law and Thought |date=Summer 2017 |pages=135-160 |url=http://hakirah.org/Vol23BroderLax.pdf |accessdate=25 September 2019}}</ref> He also co-authored and published research on the self with neuropsychologist Stan Klein, UC Santa Barbara.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Klein |first1=SB |last2=Lax |first2=ML |title=The unanticipated resilience of trait self-knowledge in the face of neural damage. |journal=Memory (Hove, England) |date=November 2010 |volume=18 |issue=8 |pages=918-48 |doi=10.1080/09658211.2010.524651 |pmid=21108109}}</ref>
