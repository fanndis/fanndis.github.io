'''Salar Kamangar''' ({{lang-fa|سالار کمانگر}};<ref>{{IPA-fa|sɒːˌlɒːɾe kæmɒnˈɡæɾ}}</ref> born 1977 in [[Piranshahr]])<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stanford.edu/group/psa/organization/vote/election1997.utf8.html |title=PSA Elections 1997 |publisher=Stanford.edu |date= |accessdate=2011-01-20}}</ref><ref name="Payvand.com">{{cite web|url=http://payvand.com/blog/blog/2010/11/15/salar-kamangar-iranian-american-leading-youtube/ |title=Salar Kamangar, Iranian-American Leading YouTube |publisher=Payvand.com |date=2010-11-15 |accessdate=2011-01-20}}</ref> is an Iranian-American senior executive at [[Google]] and former CEO of Google's [[YouTube]] brand.<ref name="tmn">{{cite news|title=Salar Kamangar, YouTube CEO, talks about the video site's future|url=http://www.mercurynews.com/2011/03/03/salar-kamangar-youtube-ceo-talks-about-the-video-sites-future/|accessdate=4 September 2017|work=The Mercury News|publisher=Digital First Media|date=3 March 2011|ref=tmn}}</ref>

==Early childhood and education==
Salar Kamangar (born in [[Piranshahr]], [[Pahlavi dynasty|Imperial State of Iran]]) holds a [[bachelor's degree]] in Biological Sciences with honors from [[Stanford University]] and was the 9th employee to join Google.<ref name="tmn" /> He joined after graduating from Stanford in 1998.<ref name="tmn" />

==Google==
On October 29, 2010, it was announced that Salar "SK" Kamangar, who was in charge of day-to-day activities, would replace [[Chad Hurley]] as [[CEO]] of [[YouTube]].<ref name="tmn" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.myfoxdfw.com/dpps/news/youtube-co-founder-steps-down-as-ceo-dpgapx-20101029-gc_10366544 |title=YouTube Co-Founder Steps Down as CEO |publisher=Myfoxdfw.com |date=2010-10-29 |access-date=2011-01-20 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101113070144/http://www.myfoxdfw.com/dpps/news/youtube-co-founder-steps-down-as-ceo-dpgapx-20101029-gc_10366544 |archive-date=2010-11-13 |df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.parsacf.org/Page/74 |title=Salar Kamangar Parsa Community Foundation |publisher=Parsacf.org |date= |accessdate=2011-01-20}}</ref>  He was replaced as CEO of YouTube on February 5, 2014. His successor at [[YouTube]] is [[Susan Wojcicki]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://in.reuters.com/article/2014/02/05/us-google-youtube-idINBREA141Y420140205 |publisher=Reuters |date= |accessdate=2013-02-05}}</ref>

Prior to that, Kamangar created the company's first [[business plan]] and was responsible for its legal and finance functions.{{citation needed|date=December 2012}} From there, he became a founding member of Google's product team,{{citation needed|date=December 2012}} where he worked on consumer projects including the acquisition of [[DejaNews]] and the subsequent launch of [[Google Groups]].
