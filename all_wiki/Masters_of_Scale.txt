'''''Masters of Scale''''' is a business and finance podcast hosted by [[Reid Hoffman]], cofounder of [[LinkedIn]]. The show is created and owned by WaitWhat.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/293164|title=Check Out a New Podcast Hosted by Reid Hoffman|last=Huspeni|first=Andrea|date=April 25, 2017|work=Entrepreneur|access-date=January 1, 2019}}</ref> In each episode, Hoffman introduces a theory on how successful businesses scale, and tests its validity by interviewing founders about their path to scale. Guests have included [[Facebook]] founder & CEO [[Mark Zuckerberg]], [[Starbucks]] founder and former CEO [[Howard Schultz]], [[Netflix]] founder and CEO [[Reed Hastings]], [[Fiat Chrysler Automobiles|FCA]] and [[Exor (company)|Exor]] Chairman [[John Elkann]] and others. Episodes also feature brief "cameo" appearances from other founders and experts in different industries who build on Hoffman's theories.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.othervalleys.net/home/mastersofscale|title=Masters of Scale: Reid Hoffman Tests His Theories of Success|last=Ramachandran|first=Anjali|date=June 8, 2017|work=Other Valleys|access-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.recode.net/2017/7/18/15992344/transcript-greylock-partner-reid-hoffman-linkedin-win-future-recode-decode|title=Greylock Partner Reid Hoffman on Recode|last=Swisher|first=Kara|date=July 18, 2017|work=Recode|access-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref>

''Masters of Scale'' was the first American media program to commit to a 50/50 [[gender balance]] for guests.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://qz.com/work/1408421/reid-hoffman-wishes-he-could-go-back-to-stanford-and-befriend-more-women/|title=Reid Hoffman Wishes He Could Go Back to Stanford and Befriend More Women|last=Fessler|first=Leah|date=Oct 30, 2018|work=Quartz|access-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/susanmcpherson/2018/02/07/media-mastermind-june-cohen-on-why-gender-balance-matters-in-storytelling-and-in-business/#524fbc68dcbf|title=Media Mastermind June Cohen On Why Gender Balance Matters In Storytelling And In Business|last=McPherson|first=Susan|date=Feb 7, 2018|work=Forbes|access-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref> Hoffman told Quartz: "Silicon Valley prides itself on being a meritocracy, but the data shows it's not. [''Masters of Scale''] launched with and maintained our commitment to a 50/50 gender balance of guests on each season. It's important to not just talk about this topic, but to take action ... set an example."<ref name=":2" />

== History ==
Executive producers Deron Triff and June Cohen approached Hoffman with the idea for the podcast, pitching it as a way of "sharing his ideas on scale in a way that could, well, scale."<ref name=":1" /> The first episode of ''Masters of Scale,'' featuring [[Airbnb]] CEO and cofounder [[Brian Chesky]], aired on May 3, 2017.<ref name=":0" /> In March 2018, the show completed its second season. In July 2018, the show launched its third season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/masters-of-scale-with-reid-hoffman/id1227971746?mt=2|title=Masters of Scale with Reid Hoffman — on iTunes|website=iTunes|access-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref> In April 2020, a spinoff series ''Masters of Scale: Rapid Response'' was launched to cover the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the business community.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/masters-of-scale-rapid-response/id1507293907|title=Podcast: Masters of Scale: Rapid Response|last=|first=|date=April 18, 2020|website=Podcast: Masters of Scale: Rapid Response|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

== Credits ==
According to the show credits, the show's executive producers are June Cohen and Deron Triff. The supervising producer is Jai Punjabi. The producers are Chris McLeod, Adam Skuse, Dan Kedmey, Jordan McLeod and Ben Manila. The show is produced with support from Jennie Cataldo. Original music and sound design are by Ryan Holladay.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mastersofscale.com/about-us/|title=About Us - Masters of Scale|website=Masters of Scale|access-date=June 3, 2019}}</ref>

== Format ==
Each episode of ''Masters of Scale'' is based around one of host Reid Hoffman's theories on how companies scale, which he sets out to prove, using the guest's life and experience, as well as other additional guests. Each episode is scored with an original soundtrack.

== Guests ==
A typical episode of ''Masters of Scale'' features one primary guest, whose story is told in detail, and who helps explain the theory, and several cameo appearances by other guests. These guests have been drawn from a wide spectrum of backgrounds, and have included [[Nobel Prize]]-winning economist [[Daniel Kahneman]], Hall of Fame sportscaster [[Dick Stockton]], National Geographic explorer [[Andrés Ruzo]] and Olympic runner [[Natasha Hastings]].

== Awards ==
In April 2018, ''Masters of Scale'' won the [[Webby Award|Webby]] People's Voice Award for Best Business Podcast.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.webbyawards.com/winners/2018/podcasts-digital-audio/features/best-series/|title=Best Series|language=en-US|access-date=December 22, 2018}}</ref> In 2020, ''Masters of Scale'' was short-listed for the [[Webby Award|Webby]] for both Best Business Podcast and Best Live Podcast Recording.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://winners.webbyawards.com/winners/podcasts/individual-episodes-mini-series-specials/live-podcast-recording|title=Best Live Podcast Recording|language=en-US|access-date=October 13, 2020}}</ref>
