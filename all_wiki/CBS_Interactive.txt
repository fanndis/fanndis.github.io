'''CBS Interactive Inc.''' (formerly '''CBS Digital Media Group''') is an American media company and is a subsidiary of the CBS Entertainment Group division of [[ViacomCBS]]. It is an online content network for information and entertainment. Its websites cover [[news]], [[sports]], [[entertainment]], [[technology]], and [[business]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbsinteractive.com/our_brands.php?tag=main_wrap;mainnav|title=All Brands - CBS Interactive|work=cbsinteractive.com|accessdate=September 2, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120616031451/https://www.cbsinteractive.com/our_brands.php?tag=main_wrap%3Bmainnav|archive-date=June 16, 2012}}</ref>  It is headed by Marc DeBevoise.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/digital/news/jim-lanzone-marc-debevoise-cbs-interactive-ceo-1203391660/|title=Marc DeBevoise Named CEO of CBS Interactive as Jim Lanzone Joins Benchmark Capital|author=Cynthia Littleton|publisher=[[Penske Business Media]]|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=December 9, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/dawnchmielewski/2019/11/04/cbs-interactive-chief-jim-lanzone-exits-marc-debevoise-named-ceo/#790a452f745a|title=CBS Interactive Chief Jim Lanzone To Exit, Marc DeBevoise Named New CEO|publisher=Forbes|work=[[Forbes Media LLC]]|accessdate=December 9, 2019}}</ref>

CBS Interactive coordinates with CBS Network Sales to bring together ad sales operations, as well as the corporation's television, sports, and news groups, who will continue to be involved in program development.

== History ==
On May 30, 2007, CBS Interactive acquired [[Last.fm]] for Â£140 million (US$280 million).<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/technology/6701863.stm | work=[[BBC News]] | title=Music site Last.fm bought by CBS | date=May 30, 2007 | accessdate=April 28, 2010}}</ref>

On June 30, 2008, [[CNET|CNET Networks]] was acquired by CBS and the assets were merged into CBS Interactive, including [[Metacritic]], [[GameSpot]], [[TV.com]], and [[Movietome]].<ref name="cbs ">{{cite web|url=http://www.cbscorporation.com/news/prdetails.php?id=3503 |title=CBS CORPORATION COMPLETES ACQUISITION OF CNET NETWORKS; MERGES OPERATIONS INTO NEW, EXPANDED CBS INTERACTIVE BUSINESS UNIT |publisher=CBS Corporation |date=June 30, 2008 |accessdate=June 30, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080829001159/http://www.cbscorporation.com/news/prdetails.php?id=3503 |archive-date=August 29, 2008 }}</ref>

On March 15, 2012, it was announced that CBS Interactive acquired [[video game]]-based website [[Giant Bomb]] and [[comic book]]-based website [[Comic Vine]] from [[Whiskey Media]], who sold off their other remaining websites to [[BermanBraun]]. This occasion marked the return of [[video game journalism|video game journalist]] [[Jeff Gerstmann]] to the CBS Interactive division of video game websites, which includes [[GameSpot]] and [[GameFAQs]], and has Gerstmann once again working directly with some of his former peers at GameSpot within the same building at the CBS Interactive headquarters.<ref name="giantbomb">[http://www.gamespot.com/news/giant-bomb-comic-vine-join-cbs-interactive-6366357 Giant Bomb, Comic Vine join CBS Interactive] GameSpot.com</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://allthingsd.com/20120315/exclusive-bermanbraun-buys-most-of-shelby-bonnies-whiskey-media/|title=BermanBraun Buys Most of Shelby Bonnie's Whiskey Media | author= Kara Swisher|work=[[AllThingsD]]|accessdate=September 2, 2015}}</ref>

On April 17, 2012, it was announced that [[Major League Gaming]] and CBS Interactive would be entering a partnership alongside [[Twitch (service)|Twitch]] to be the only exclusive online broadcaster of their Pro Circuit competitions, as well as for advertising representation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.majorleaguegaming.com/news/major-league-gaming-partners-with-cbs-interactive|title=Major League Gaming Partners with CBS Interactive|work=Major League Gaming|accessdate=September 2, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120419124307/http://www.majorleaguegaming.com/news/major-league-gaming-partners-with-cbs-interactive|archive-date=April 19, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://thenextweb.com/insider/2012/04/17/cbs-interactive-partners-with-twitch-tv-and-major-league-gaming-to-form-esports-behemoth/|title=CBS Takes Esports Big with Twitch.tv and MLG|author=Alex Wilhelm|date=April 17, 2012|work=The Next Web|accessdate=September 2, 2015}}</ref>

On November 4, 2019, [[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] reported that Lanzone would leave CBSi after 9 years to become an executive in residence at [[Benchmark (venture capital firm)|Benchmark Capital]], and would be succeeded by Marc DeBevoise.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/tv/news/jim-lanzone-marc-debevoise-cbs-interactive-ceo-1203391660/|title=Marc DeBevoise Named CEO of CBS Interactive as Jim Lanzone Joins Benchmark Capital|last1=Littleton|first1=Cynthia|last2=Littleton|first2=Cynthia|date=2019-11-04|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2019-11-04}}</ref>

On September 14, 2020, CBS announced that it would divest the CNET Media Group to [[Red Ventures]] for $500 million, which occurred on October 30, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Red Ventures acquires CNET Media Group from ViacomCBS for $500M|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2020/09/14/red-ventures-acquires-cnet-media-group-from-viacomcbs-for-500m/|access-date=2020-09-14|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Spangler|first=Todd|date=2020-09-14|title=ViacomCBS Reaches Deal to Sell CNET for $500 Million to Marketing Firm Red Ventures|url=https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/cnet-viacomcbs-sells-red-ventures-1234768810/|access-date=2020-09-14|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Red Ventures Announces Closing of Acquisition of CNET Media Group|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/red-ventures-announces-closing-of-acquisition-of-cnet-media-group-301163922.html|date=2020-10-30|access-date=2020-11-06|website=PR Newswire|language=en}}</ref>

=== General entertainment ===
The CBS Interactive entertainment brands are CBS.com, the online home of [[CBS]], an American network television channel, and the [[CBS All Access]] streaming service.

=== General news ===
CBS Interactive's news brands are CBSNews.com, the online home of [[CBS News]],<ref name=cbs/> the news division of CBS; and [[CBSN]],<ref name="cbsn">{{cite web |last1=Weprin |first1=Alex |title=CBS News readies 24-hour 'CBSN' digital network |url=https://www.politico.com/media/story/2014/10/cbs-news-readies-24-hour-cbsn-digital-network-003000 |website=POLITICO Media |accessdate=18 February 2019 |language=en}}</ref> a 24-hour news channel based completely online and on smart devices, a division of CBS News.

=== Sports ===
CBS Interactive's sports brands are [[CBSSports.com]], the online home of [[CBS Sports]], with news, scores, video, and discussion about [[professional sports]] along with the streaming sports network [[CBS Sports HQ]]; CBSSports.com College Network, the online home of [[CBS Sports Network|CBS Sports College Network]] with news, scores, video, and discussion about [[college athletics|collegiate sports]]; CBS Sports Fantasy, a website featuring [[fantasy sports]]; and [[MaxPreps]], a website including information, recruiting, news, video, and discussion about [[high school sports]]. [[CBS Sports]] also manages an advance media division that hosts college and high school sports digital presence to both design the websites and manage the ads on each of those 250+ properties.<ref>{{cite web|title=CBS Interactive|url=http://www.cbsiam.com|accessdate=January 19, 2017}}</ref>

=== Music ===
As of 2020, CBS Interactive owns [[Last.fm]],<ref name=cbs/> the music discovery service and listening service.

=== mySimon ===
is a [[Price comparison service|comparison shopping website]] owned by [[CBS Interactive]]. The site offers shopping recommendations, buying advice, and side by side price comparisons for various products. Founded in 1998, mySimon was acquired in 2000 for $700 million.<ref>{{cite news | first = Clint | last = Boulton | title = CNET Acquires mySimon | date = 2000-01-20 | url = http://www.internetnews.com/ec-news/article.php/289951/CNET+Acquires+mySimon.htm | work = InternetNews.com | accessdate = 2013-10-15}}</ref> mySimon was also nominated for a Webby award in 2001.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://winners.webbyawards.com/2001/web/general-website/commerce | title = Commerce / The Webby Awards / 2001 | accessdate = 2013-10-15}}</ref> The site directs users to other shopping sites such as [[Amazon (company)|Amazon.com]] when searching for products. It is not affiliated with [[Simon Property Group]], a mall operator. The website is also unrelated to corporate sibling and major book publisher, [[Simon & Schuster]].

== Former properties ==
CBS Interactive owned several websites in the past, most of them are sold to Red Ventures in 2020. Its former websites are:
* [[BNET]], founded in 2007, was an [[online magazine]] dedicated to issues of [[business management]]. It merged into [[CBS MoneyWatch]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/bnet-is-now-cbs-moneywatchcom-27-03-2012/|title=BNET is now CBS Moneywatch.com|author1=Jack Otter|date=March 27, 2012|work=CBS News|accessdate=January 15, 2017}}</ref>
* [[CNET]], an American media website that publishes reviews, news, articles, blogs, podcasts, and videos on technology and consumer electronics globally; CNET Content Solutions, content solutions related to technology and consumer electronics.
* [[Download.com]], a website for free software.
* [[TechRepublic]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Heim |first1=Sarah J. |title=CNET Acquires TechRepublic |url=https://www.adweek.com/brand-marketing/cnet-acquires-techrepublic-48104/ |website=www.adweek.com |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> contains news, information, resources, and forums for [[Information technology|IT]] professionals.

* [[GameFAQs]],<ref>{{cite web |title=GameFAQs is moving to a new domain in February |url=https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/boards/6-gamefaqs-announcements/76238326 |website=gamefaqs.gamespot.com |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> a community driven website with guides and forums about [[video games]]
* [[GameSpot]],<ref name=cbs/> contains news, reviews, and guides about video games.

* [[onGamers]], contains news analyses, and columns about the [[eSports]] scene.
* [[TV Guide|TVGuide.com]], the ultimate listings guide for any Service Provider.
* [[Chowhound]], the website that contains recipes, discussions, reviews, videos, and stories all related to [[food]]
