'''Jan Kenneth Eliasson''' (born 17 September 1940) is a [[Sweden|Swedish]] [[diplomat]] who was [[Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations]] from July 2012 to December 2016.<ref name=toppjobb>{{cite news |title=Eliasson får FN-toppjobb |first=Staffan |last=Kihlström |url=http://www.dn.se/nyheter/varlden/eliasson-far-fn-toppjobb |language=Swedish |newspaper=[[Dagens Nyheter]] |date=2 March 2012 |access-date=2 March 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.expressen.se/nyheter/har-svar-jan-eliasson-eden---blir-ny-fn-topp/|title=Här svär Jan Eliasson eden - blir ny FN-topp|newspaper=[[Expressen]] |first=Lars |last=Näslund |date=2 July 2012 |access-date=17 September 2015 |language=Swedish}}</ref> A member of the [[Swedish Social Democratic Party]], Eliasson served as [[Minister for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)|Minister for Foreign Affairs]] from 24 April to 6 October 2006. Eliasson was appointed as Governing Board Chair of the [[Stockholm International Peace Research Institute]] in April 2017 and assumed his role as of 1 June 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sipri.org/media/2017/sipri-welcomes-ambassador-jan-eliasson-new-governing-board-chair|title=SIPRI welcomes Ambassador Jan Eliasson as new Governing Board Chair {{!}} SIPRI|website=www.sipri.org|language=en|access-date=2018-01-23}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Jan Eliasson was born in a [[working-class]] family in [[Gothenburg]] in [[Sweden]]. He was an [[AFS Intercultural Programs|AFS]] exchange student in [[Indiana]], United States, from 1957 to 1958 and was commissioned a naval officer in the reserve after training at the [[Royal Swedish Naval Academy]] in 1962. In 1965 he earned a [[master's degree]] in [[economics]] from the [[School of Business, Economics and Law]] at the [[University of Gothenburg]] where he was also a president of the local [[AIESEC]] committee. He also holds [[honorary degree]]s from [[American University]] (1994), University of Gothenburg (2001) and [[Uppsala University]] (2005).

Eliasson has authored and co-authored numerous books and articles and is a frequent lecturer on foreign policy and diplomacy. Since 1988 he has been a visiting lecturer on mediation, conflict resolution and UN reform at Uppsala University.

Eliasson started his diplomatic career in 1965, when he was employed at the [[Ministry for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)|Swedish Ministry for Foreign Affairs]]. From 1982 to 1983 he served as Diplomatic Advisor to the Swedish Prime Minister [[Olof Palme]], and from 1983 to 1987 as Director General for Political Affairs in the Ministry for Foreign Affairs.

From 1980 to 1986, Eliasson was part of the U.N. mission, mediating in the [[Iran–Iraq War]]. From 1988 to 1992 he served as Sweden's [[Permanent Representative]] to the [[United Nations]] in [[New York City|New York]], where he also served as the [[United Nations Secretary-General|Secretary-General]]'s Personal Representative on Iran/Iraq.

In 1991, Eliasson was chairman of the [[United Nations General Assembly|U.N. General Assembly]]'s working group on emergency relief and Vice President of the [[United Nations Economic and Social Council|U.N. Economic and Social Council]] (ECOSOC) from 1991 to 1992. In 1992 he was appointed as the first [[Under-Secretary-General for Humanitarian Affairs and Emergency Relief Coordinator|U.N. Under-Secretary-General for Humanitarian Affairs]]. He was involved in operations in [[Somalia]], [[Sudan]], [[Mozambique]] and the [[Balkans]]. He also took initiatives on issues such as land mines, prevention and humanitarian action.

[[Image:Bush Eliasson.jpg|thumb|250px|left|Jan Eliasson (right) meeting with [[George W. Bush]] in 2005.]]

In 1993–94 Eliasson served as mediator in the Nagorno-Karabakh conflict for the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE).<ref name="unitednationswebservicessection">{{cite web|url=https://www.un.org/sg/management/senstaff_details.asp?smgID=163|title=Jan  Eliasson, Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations|author=United Nations Web Services Section|work=un.org|access-date=17 September 2015}}</ref>

From 1994 to 1999, Eliasson served as Swedish [[State Secretary for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)|State Secretary for Foreign Affairs]]. From 2000 to 2005 he served as Sweden's Ambassador to [[Washington, D.C.]], United States. On 13 June 2005 he was unanimously elected [[President of the United Nations General Assembly]], for its sixtieth session. He served as president from 13 September 2005 to 11 September 2006.<ref name="unitednationswebservicessection"/>

From 24 April 2006 to 6 October 2006 he also served as [[Minister for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)|Swedish Minister of Foreign Affairs]] in the [[Swedish Social Democratic Party|Social Democratic]] cabinet of [[Göran Persson]]. Following the [[2006 Swedish general election]] when his party lost the election, he announced that he will teach at Uppsala University.<ref name="unitednationswebservicessection"/>

[[File:Humanitarian and Cultural crisis in the Middle East Jan Eliasson Press Briefing.webm|thumb|Humanitarian and Cultural crisis in the Middle East Jan Eliasson, 7 September 2015]]
Eliasson condemned the [[2006 Lebanon War]]: "The military offensive of [[Israel]] is an extremely dangerous escalation of the situation in the region". He also condemned the attacks of [[Hezbollah]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Skarp kritik från den svenska regeringen (Sharp criticism from the Swedish government) |date=13 July 2006 |work=[[Dagens Nyheter]] |url=http://www.dn.se/DNet/jsp/polopoly.jsp?d=148&a=559274 |language=sv |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120204060100/http://www.dn.se/DNet/jsp/polopoly.jsp?d=148&a=559274 |archivedate=February 4, 2012 }}</ref>

In December 2006, then U.N. Secretary-General [[Kofi Annan]] announced Eliasson as [[Special Envoy]] to [[War in Darfur|Darfur]], [[Sudan]]. He left this mission in June 2008.<ref name="unitednationswebservicessection"/>

In March 2009, Eliasson gave a lecture entitled "Armed Conflict: The Cost to Civilians" at the University of San Diego's Joan B. Kroc Institute for Peace & Justice Distinguished Lecture Series.

Eliasson is an Eminent Member of the [[Sergio Vieira de Mello Foundation]] and he currently sits on the advisory board of the [[Alliance for Peacebuilding]]. Eliasson is Chair of WaterAid Sweden. Since 2010 he serves in the UN Secretary-General's Advocates Group for the achievement of the Millennium Development Goals. Eliasson is a former Board member of [[DARA (international organization)|DARA]].

On 2 March 2012, Jan Eliasson was appointed Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations by Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon. He took office as Deputy Secretary-General on 1 July 2012.<ref name="unitednationswebservicessection"/>
He always carries in his pocket a print of the UN charter.

On 23 October 2017, the Tallberg Foundation announced that its Global Leadership Prize will henceforth be named after Jan Eliasson.<ref>http://www.tallbergfoundation.org/2017/10/23/honoring-jan-eliasson/</ref>

On 27 April 2017, the Swedish Government appointed Jan Eliasson as Chair of the [[Stockholm International Peace Research Institute|SIPRI]] Governing Board. Eliasson has formally assumed the position as of 1 June 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.regeringen.se/pressmeddelanden/2017/04/jan-eliasson-ny-styrelseordforande-for-sipri/|title=Jan Eliasson ny styrelseordförande för SIPRI|last=Regeringskansliet|first=Regeringen och|date=2017-04-27|website=Regeringskansliet|language=sv|access-date=2018-01-23}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Eliasson is married to Kerstin Eliasson, former Swedish State Secretary for Education and Science. They have three children: Anna, Emilie and Johan. He is a [[GAIS]] supporter.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.gp.se/sport/fotboll/1.586870-gais-tar-till-diplomathjalp-for-wanderson?articleRenderMode=image&image=bigTop|title=Gais tar till diplomathjälp för Wanderson|newspaper=[[Göteborgs-Posten]] |first=Emma |last=Lukins |date=29 March 2011 |access-date=17 September 2015 |language=Swedish}}</ref>
