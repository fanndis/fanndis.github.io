The '''2016 Australian Open''' was a [[List of tennis tournaments|tennis tournament]] that took place at [[Melbourne Park]] between 18 and 31 January 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ticketliquidator.com/tix/australian-open-tickets.aspx|title=Australian Open Tickets|publisher=Ticketliquidator.com|accessdate=13 November 2013}}</ref> It was the 104th edition of the [[Australian Open]], and the first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament of the year. The tournament consisted of events for professional players in singles, doubles and mixed doubles play. Junior and wheelchair players competed in singles and doubles tournaments.

[[Novak Djokovic]] successfully defended the men's singles title and thus won a record-equaling sixth Australian Open title. [[Serena Williams]] was the defending champion in the women's singles but failed to defend her title, losing to [[Angelique Kerber]] in the final; by winning, Kerber became the first German player of any gender to win a Grand Slam title since [[Steffi Graf]] won her last such title at the [[1999 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-01-30/australian-open3a-serena-williams-beats-angelique-kerber-to-wi/7127362?section=sport|title=Australian Open: Angelique Kerber stuns Serena Williams to win women's final|publisher=ABC News ([[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]])|date=30 January 2016|accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref>

As in previous years, this year's tournament's title sponsor was [[Kia Motors|Kia]]. This edition set a new attendance record for the tournament of 720,363.<ref>{{cite web|title=What We Learned from the Australian Open|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2016/02/what-we-learned-from-the-australian-open/57489/|publisher=Tennis.com|date=1 February 2016}}</ref>

==Tournament==
[[File:Rod Laver Arena (8984015851).jpg|thumb|left|Rod Laver Arena where the Finals of the Australian Open take place]]
The 2016 Australian Open was the 104th edition of the tournament and was held at [[Melbourne Park]] in [[Melbourne]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]], Australia.

The tournament was run by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) and was part of the [[2016 ATP World Tour]] and the [[2016 WTA Tour]] calendars under the Grand Slam category. The tournament consisted of both men's and women's singles and doubles draws as well as a mixed doubles event. There were singles and doubles events for both boys and girls (players under 18), which was part of the Grade A category of tournaments, and also singles, doubles and quad events for men's and women's [[wheelchair tennis]] players as part of the NEC tour under the Grand Slam category.

The tournament was played on hard courts and take place over a series of 25 courts, including the three main show courts: [[Rod Laver Arena]], [[Melbourne Multi Purpose Venue|Hisense Arena]] and [[Margaret Court Arena]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com.au/news/2013/12/08/first-glimpse-of-new-look-margaret-court-arena|title=First Glimpse of new-look Margaret Court Arena|publisher=Tennis.com.au|accessdate=6 January 2014}}</ref>

==Broadcast==
In Australia, selected key matches were broadcast live by the [[Seven Network]]. The majority of matches were shown on the network's primary channel [[Seven Network|Channel Seven]], however during news programming nationwide and most night matches in [[Perth]], coverage shifted to either [[7Two]] or [[7mate]]. Additionally, every match was also available to be [[Streaming media|streamed]] live through a free 7Tennis [[mobile app]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[TV Tonight]]|first=David|last=Knox|url=http://www.tvtonight.com.au/2015/12/seven-tennis-2016-summer-guide.html|title=Seven Tennis 2016: summer guide |date=17 December 2015|accessdate=9 January 2016}}</ref>

Internationally, [[ESPN]] held the rights for America and Central America, broadcasting matches on [[ESPN2]] and [[ESPN3]] in the [[United States]] as well as regionally on [[ESPN International]].  ESPN also sub-licenses matches to [[Tennis Channel]].<ref name="broadcasters">{{cite web|url=http://event.ausopen.com/event-guide/broadcasting|title=Broadcasting|work=Australian Open|accessdate=9 January 2016|archive-url=https://archive.is/20160117170300/http://event.ausopen.com/event-guide/broadcasting|archive-date=2016-01-17|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.multichannel.com/news/internet-video/espn-aces-10-year-renewal-australian-open/298556|title=ESPN Aces 10-Year Renewal With Australian Open|work=Multichannel News|date=10 September 2013|accessdate=9 January 2016|first=Mike|last=Reynolds}}</ref> Other broadcasters included [[beIN Sports]] in the Middle East, [[SuperSport (South African TV channel)|SuperSport]] in Africa, [[Eurosport]] through Europe (plus [[Nederlandse Omroep Stichting|NOS]] Netherlands and [[SRG SSR]] in Switzerland), [[China Central Television|CCTV]], [[iQiyi]] and [[Shanghai Media Group|SMG]] in China, [[Fiji One]] in Fiji, [[Sony ESPN]] in India, both [[WOWOW]] and [[NHK]] in Japan, [[Sky Television (New Zealand)|Sky]] in New Zealand and [[Fox Sports Asia]] in selected markets in the Asia Pacific region.<ref name="broadcasters"/> In Canada, [[The Sports Network|TSN]] broadcast matches across multiple channels.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadcastermagazine.com/news/tsn-secures-10-year-australian-open-extension/1000820923/|title=TSN Secures 10-Year Australian Open Extension|accessdate=August 6, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140221225438/http://www.broadcastermagazine.com/news/tsn-secures-10-year-australian-open-extension/1000820923/|archive-date=February 21, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Events==
===Spectator safety===
Spectator safety became a major issue during the tournament, with up to four separate cases reported:
*On Day 2, play was suspended during the fourth set of [[Bernard Tomic]]'s first round match against [[Denis Istomin]] for 20 minutes after an elderly spectator collapsed due to heat stress; she was subsequently treated with an [[Epinephrine autoinjector|EpiPen]] and taken away from Hisense Arena.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/tennis/australian-open-2016-bernard-tomic-rattled-after-spectators-medical-emergency-20160119-gm9ihd.html|title=Australian Open 2016: Bernard Tomic rattled after spectator's medical emergency|publisher=[[Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=20 January 2016|accessdate=16 March 2016}}</ref>
*On Day 4, [[Ana Ivanovic]]'s second round match against [[Anastasija Sevastova]] was interrupted in the first set when another elderly spectator fell down a set of stairs, delaying play by 25 minutes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/tennis/australian-open-2016-woman-collapses-in-the-stands-at-ivanovicsevastova-match-20160121-gmap4w.html|title=Australian Open 2016: Ana Ivanovic left shaken after woman falls mid-match|publisher=[[Sydney Morning Herald]]|first=Larissa|last=Nicholson|date=21 January 2016|accessdate=16 March 2016}}</ref>
*On Day 6, in the most serious case, Ivanovic was again involved in a match that had to be suspended, after her coach [[Nigel Sears]] suffered a heart attack during the second set of her match against [[Madison Keys]]. Sears, who is the father-in-law of [[Andy Murray]], had to be stretchered out of the stands and play on Rod Laver Arena was suspended for an hour. Having led by a set and a break at the time, Ivanovic proceeded to lose the match in three sets. Sears was later taken to hospital where he eventually made a full recovery.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-01-23/ana-ivanovic-coach-nigel-sears-falls-stairs-australian-open/7109912|title=Australian Open: Ana Ivanovic's coach Nigel Sears collapses in stand, play resumes after suspension|publisher=ABC News ([[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]])|date=24 January 2016|accessdate=16 March 2016}}</ref>
*On Day 7, [[Sam Groth]]'s mother fell down a set of stairs on Hisense Arena during the second set of her son and [[Lleyton Hewitt]]'s doubles match against [[Jack Sock]] and [[Vasek Pospisil]], causing play to be suspended by 20 minutes. She was later able to walk out of the court unassisted.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/sport/tennis/australian-open-2016-sam-groths-mother-falls-down-stairs-fourth-spectator-emergency/news-story/23657ed9f1cf74b98845563223d6f320|title=Australian Open 2016: Sam Groth’s mother falls down stairs, fourth spectator emergency|publisher=The Australian|date=24 January 2016|accessdate=16 March 2016}}</ref>

===Maria Sharapova doping controversy===
On 7 March 2016, five weeks after the conclusion of the tournament, world number seven [[Maria Sharapova]] announced at a press conference in Los Angeles that she had failed a drug test following her quarter-final defeat by [[Serena Williams]] on 26 January. Sharapova confessed to taking the substance [[meldonium]], which was placed on the [[World Anti-Doping Agency]]'s list of banned substances on 1 January; she was later suspended for two years (later reduced to fifteen months on appeal), backdated to 26 January, and was subsequently docked the $A375,000 she earned for reaching the quarter-finals.<ref name="Sharapova">{{cite web|url=http://www.skysports.com/tennis/news/12110/10196392/maria-sharapova-reveals-failed-drug-test-at-australian-open|title=Maria Sharapova reveals failed drug test at Australian Open|publisher=Sky Sports|first=Jefferson|last=Lake|date=8 March 2016|accessdate=8 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2016/06/sharapova-receives-two-year-ban-backdated-january-2016/58910/|title=SHARAPOVA RECEIVES TWO-YEAR BAN, BACKDATED TO JANUARY 2016|publisher=tennis.com|first=Ed|last=McGrogan|date=8 June 2016|accessdate=13 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-10-05/maria-sharapova-doping-ban-cas/7900714|title=Maria Sharapova has doping ban reduced to 15 months by Court of Arbitration for Sport|publisher=ABC News ([[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]])|date=5 October 2016|accessdate=13 January 2018}}</ref>

==Champions==

===Seniors===

====Men's Singles====
{{main|2016 Australian Open – Men's Singles}}
* {{flagicon|SRB}} '''[[Novak Djokovic]]''' defeated {{flagicon|GBR}} [[Andy Murray]], 6–1, 7–5, 7–6<sup>(7–3)</sup>
Djokovic and Murray had faced one another 30 times prior to the final, with Djokovic victorious on 21 occasions.  Murray had lost four Australian Open finals, three times to Djokovic, while the Serb had won the title five times.  After an even first game, Djokovic broke Murray twice to lead 5&ndash;0, before Murray held.  Djokovic took the winning game to secure the first set 6&ndash;1 in 30 minutes.  The second set went with serve until Djokovic broke Murray to lead 4&ndash;3.  The Scot broke back immediately and held his serve, but Djokovic broke in the eleventh game, then went on to hold serve, taking the second set 7&ndash;5.  Djokovic broke the Murray serve in the first game of the third set, but Murray broke back to restore parity in the set at 3&ndash;3.  The subsequent games went with serve and sent the set to a tie-break.  Djokovic led 3&ndash;0 and 6&ndash;1 before finally securing the championship victory by three sets to love, with a 7&ndash;3 tie-break victory.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2016/jan/31/novak-djokovic-v-andy-murray-australian-open-final-live | work = The Guardian| title = Andy Murray beaten by Novak Djokovic in Australian Open final – as it happened | first = Jacob | last = Steinberg | date= 31 January 2016 | accessdate = 1 February 2016}}</ref>

====Women's Singles====
{{main|2016 Australian Open – Women's Singles}}
* {{flagicon|GER}} '''[[Angelique Kerber]]''' defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Serena Williams]], 6–4, 3–6, 6–4

Going into the final, Kerber and Williams had faced each other six times with Williams holding a 5&ndash;1 advantage.  Kerber broke Williams in the third game of the first set with Williams breaking back to make it 3&ndash;3.   Kerber immediately broke back and held serve to win the first set 6&ndash;4.  Williams took advantage of the third of three break points in the fourth game of the second set, the remainder of the set going with serve, leveling the match at one set all.  Kerber broke Williams in the second game of the final set, but Williams immediately broke back and held her own serve to level the deciding set at 2&ndash;2.   Another break for Kerber saw her leading 5&ndash;2 but Williams broke back once again, taking the set to 5&ndash;4 to Kerber.  A cross-court exchange described as "breathtaking" saw Williams hit the ball long, securing the title for Kerber.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2016/jan/30/serena-williams-v-angelique-kerber-australian-open-final-live| title = Angelique Kerber stuns Serena Williams to claim Australian Open title – as it happened | work = The Guardian | date = 30 January 2016 | accessdate = 1 February 2016 |first = Katy | last = Murrells}}</ref>
