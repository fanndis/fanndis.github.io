'''''Business Insider''''' is an American financial and business news website founded in 2007 and owned by the German publishing house [[Axel Springer SE|Axel Springer]]. It operates 14 national editions and an international edition. Several national editions are published in local languages.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/welcome-business-insider-japan-2017-1|title=Welcome, Business Insider Japan!|last=|first=|date=January 16, 2017|access-date=July 15, 2017}}</ref>

== History ==
''Business Insider'' was launched in 2007 and is based in New York City. Founded by [[DoubleClick]]'s former CEO [[Kevin P. Ryan]], [[Dwight Merriman]], and [[Henry Blodget]],<ref name="Springer Acquisition">{{Cite web|url=http://www.axelspringer.de/en/presse/Leading-Digital-Publisher-Axel-Springer-Acquires-Business-Insider_24619096.html|title=Leading Digital Publisher Axel Springer Acquires Business Insider|date=September 29, 2015|accessdate=September 29, 2015}}</ref> the site began as a consolidation of [[vertical market|industry vertical]] blogs, the first of them being ''Silicon Alley Insider'' (began May 16, 2007) and ''Clusterstock'' (started March 20, 2008).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/about|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130423112900/http://www.businessinsider.com/about|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 23, 2013|title=Welcome To Business Insider|date=April 23, 2013|website=Business Insider|accessdate=November 18, 2016}}</ref> In addition to providing and analyzing business news, the site aggregates news stories on various subjects from around the web. It first reported a profit in the fourth quarter of 2010.<ref name="post">{{cite news |title=Business Insider Turns A$2,127 Profit On $4.8 Million in Revenue |work=[[TechCrunch]] |date=March 7, 2011 |url=https://techcrunch.com/2011/03/07/business-insider-4-8-million-profit/ }}</ref> In June 2012, it had 5.4 million unique visitors.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Hagey|first1=Keach|title=Henry Blodget's Second Act|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10000872396390444840104577555180608254796|accessdate=November 9, 2015|work=The Wall Street Journal|date=July 29, 2012}}</ref> In 2015 [[Axel Springer SE]] acquired 88% of the stake in [[Business Insider Inc.]] for $343 million (€306 million).<ref>{{cite news |last=Spangler |first=Todd |url=https://variety.com/2015/digital/global/axel-springer-buys-business-insider-343-million-1201604768/ |title=Germany's Axel Springer Buys Business Insider in $343 Million Deal |date=September 29, 2015 |work=Variety |accessdate=July 20, 2018}}</ref>

[[Forgeglobal.com]] lists [[Allen & Company|Allen & Company LLC]], RRE Ventures,<ref>[https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/rre-ventures#section-overview RRE Ventures]</ref> [[Robert_Pittman_(media_executive)#Pilot_Group|Pilot Group]], [[Kohlberg & Company|Kohlberg Ventures]], [[Andreessen Horowitz]], Matthew Luckett,<ref>[https://www.crunchbase.com/person/matthew-luckett#section-overview Matthew Luckett]</ref> [[Allen Morgan]], [[Marc Andreessen]], [[HuffPost]], [[Institutional Venture Partners]], [[Bezos Expeditions]], [[Jeff Bezos]], and [[Gordon Crovitz]] as significant early holders of the stock.<ref name="fgbi">{{cite news |title=Business Insider |url=https://forgeglobal.com/company/business-insider |accessdate=29 April 2020 |publisher=Forge Global, Inc}}</ref>

''Business Insider'' also hosts industry conferences, such as IGNITION,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/event/ignition-2012|title=IGNITION 2012|work=Business Insider}}</ref> which explores the emerging [[business models]] of [[digital media]]. In January 2015, ''Business Insider'' launched ''BI Intelligence'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://intelligence.businessinsider.com/welcome|title=BI Intelligence|website=Business Insider}}</ref> a subscription-based research service that provides data and analysis on the mobile, payments, eCommerce, social, and digital media industries. The site publishes numerous annual editorial franchises, such as the "Digital 100: The World's Most Valuable Private Tech Companies".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100-revised-the-most-valuable-private-companies-in-the-world-2012-11 |title=Digital 100: The World's Most Valuable Private Tech Companies|work=Business Insider|date=November 2012}}</ref>

=== ''Tech Insider'' ===
In July 2015, ''Business Insider'' began the technology website ''Tech Insider'', with a staff of 40 people working primarily from the company's existing New York headquarters, but originally separated from the main ''Business Insider'' newsroom.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/cmo/2015/07/27/business-insider-broadens-ambitions-with-new-tech-site/|title=Business Insider Broadens Ambitions With New Tech Site|date=July 27, 2015|newspaper=[[The Wall Street Journal]]}}</ref> However, ''Tech Insider'' was eventually folded into the ''Business Insider'' website.<ref name="Mullin WSJ">{{cite news |last=Mullin |first=Benjamin |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/business-insider-drops-business-from-its-name-as-company-broadens-coverage-distribution-1513249201 |title=Business Insider Inc. Drops 'Business' From Its Name as Company Broadens Coverage, Distribution |date=December 14, 2017 |work=The Wall Street Journal |accessdate=July 20, 2018}}</ref>

=== ''Markets Insider'' ===
In October 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/business-insider-launches-markets-data-site-with-help-from-axel-springer-1477303201|title=Business Insider Launches Markets Data Site With Help From Axel Springer|last=Alpert|first=Lukas I.|website=WSJ|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-01}}</ref> ''Business Insider'' started ''Markets Insider'' as a joint venture with Finanzen.net, another Axel Springer company. The markets data site covers stocks<ref>{{Cite web|last=GmbH|first=finanzen net|title=BOND SCREENER & FINDER {{!}} ETF DATABASE {{!}} Markets Insider|url=https://markets.businessinsider.com/bonds/finder|access-date=2020-09-06|website=markets.businessinsider.com|language=en-us}}</ref> and the macro economy, as well as other financial assets.{{Citation needed|date=August 2019}}

=== ''BI Sub-Saharan Africa'' ===
In January 2017, ''Business Insider'' announced that it would be launching a new Sub-Saharan site in partnership with [[Ringier]] Africa Digital Publishing.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://uk.news.yahoo.com/welcome-business-insider-sub-saharan-231003242.html | title = Welcome Business Insider Sub-Saharan Africa! | date= January 23, 2017 | accessdate = January 25, 2017 | publisher = Yahoo News UK | author = Roddy Salazar }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.africa-newsroom.com/press/ringier-launches-business-insider-sub-saharan-africa?lang=en | title = 
Ringier Launches Business Insider Sub Saharan Africa | date= January 20, 2017 | accessdate = January 25, 2017 | publisher = Africa Newsroom }}</ref> [[Henry Blodget]]—co-founder, CEO, and global editor-in-chief of ''Business Insider''—said of the partnership, "Ringier ... is the ideal partner to help us bring to sub-saharan Africa [our] unique voice and attitude."<ref>{{cite magazine |url= http://www.adweek.com/fishbowlny/business-insider-launches-sub-saharan-site/395156 | title = Business Insider Launches Sub-Saharan Site | date= January 20, 2017 | accessdate = January 25, 2017 | magazine = AdWeek | author = Chris O'Shea }}</ref> Former Ventures Africa journalist David Adeleke is the editor for Business Insider SSA.{{Citation needed|date=July 2018}}

== Reception ==
In January 2009, the ''Clusterstock'' section appeared in ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s "Best 25 Financial Blogs",<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.time.com/time/business/article/0,8599,1873144,00.html |title=Best 25 Financial Blogs | work=[[Time (magazine)|TIME]] |location =New York |date= January 22, 2009 |accessdate =June 1, 2010 |first1=Douglas A. |last1=McIntyre |first2=Ashley C. |last2=Allen}}</ref> and the ''Silicon Alley Insider'' section was listed in ''[[PC Magazine]]''{{'}}s "Our Favorite Blogs 2009."<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.pcmag.com/print_article2/0,1217,a=246136,00.asp?hidPrint=true |title=Our Favorite Blogs 2009 |work=PC Magazine |location=New York |date=November 23, 2009 |accessdate=June 1, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606165855/http://www.pcmag.com/print_article2/0%2C1217%2Ca%3D246136%2C00.asp?hidPrint=true |archive-date=June 6, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> 2009 also saw ''Business Insider''{{'}}s selection as an official [[Webby Award|Webby]] honoree for Best Business Blog.<ref name=webby>{{cite web|title=Blog-Business: Official Honoree|url=http://www.webbyawards.com/winners/2010/web/general-website-categories/blog-business/the-business-insider/|website=Webby Awards}}</ref>

In 2012, ''Business Insider'' was named to the [[Inc. 500]]. In 2013, the publication was once again nominated in the Blog-Business category at the Webby Awards.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://webbyawards.com/winners/2013/web/general-website/blog-business/business-insider/|title=Business Insider {{!}} The Webby Awards|access-date=February 9, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> In January 2014, ''[[The New York Times]]'' reported that ''Business Insider''{{'s}} web traffic was comparable to that of ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]''.<ref name="NYT: joining Vox">{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/27/business/media/ezra-klein-joining-vox-media-as-web-journalism-asserts-itself.html |accessdate=December 26, 2014 |title=Ezra Klein Is Joining Vox Media as Web Journalism Asserts Itself |last1=Carr |first1=David |date=January 26, 2014 |work=[[The New York Times]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150216173600/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/01/27/business/media/ezra-klein-joining-vox-media-as-web-journalism-asserts-itself.html?_r=0 |archivedate=February 16, 2015 |url-status=live  }}</ref> In 2017, ''[[Digiday]]'' included imprint ''Insider'' as a candidate in two separate categories—"Best New Vertical" and "Best Use of Instagram"—at their annual Publishing Awards.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://digiday.com/awards/business-insiders-social-first-insider-best-new-vertical-years-digiday-publishing-awards/|title=Business Insider's social-first Insider is up for Best New Vertical for this year's Digiday Publishing Awards – Digiday|last=Awards|first=Digiday|date=February 7, 2017|newspaper=[[Digiday]]|access-date=February 9, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>
==Tabloid clickbait==
The website has faced criticism for what critics consider its [[clickbait]]-style headlines.<ref name="wsj._Henr">{{Cite news |title = Henry Blodget's Second Act |last = Hagey |first = Keach|date = July 29, 2012 |work=Wall Street Journal|accessdate = August 13, 2015 |url = https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10000872396390444840104577555180608254796}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url =https://techcrunch.com/2012/05/22/henry-blodget-linkbait-slideshows-aggregation/ |title =Business Insider's Henry Blodget Defends Linkbait, Slideshows, And Aggregation |last =Ha |first = Anthony |date =May 22, 2012 |publisher =[[TechCrunch]] |access-date =January 23, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url =http://www.bloombergview.com/articles/2015-09-29/can-business-insider-make-money- |title =Can Business Insider Make Money? |last =Bershidsky |first =Leonid |date =September 29, 2015 |website =[[Bloomberg News]] |access-date =January 23, 2016}}</ref> In 2013, ''[[The New Yorker]]'' criticized the website for prioritizing publishing speed over accuracy.<ref>{{cite news |url =https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2013/04/08/business-outsider |title =Business Outsider |last = Auletta |first = Ken |date =April 8, 2013 |work =The New Yorker |access-date =January 23, 2016}}</ref> In 2018, the website received criticism from some media outlets after deleting a controversial column about [[Scarlett Johansson]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sfchronicle.com/opinion/article/Waving-the-white-flag-in-Scarlett-Johansson-flap-13071611.php|title=Waving the white flag in Scarlett Johansson flap|date=July 13, 2018|work=San Francisco Chronicle|access-date=July 13, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://nymag.com/daily/intelligencer/2018/07/bi-retracted-a-bad-piece-and-set-a-terrible-precedent.html |title=Business Insider Retracted a Bad Piece — and Set a Terrible Precedent|last=Singal|first=Jesse|work=Daily Intelligencer|access-date=July 13, 2018|language=en}}</ref>
