==Early life==
Braun was born in [[New York City]] to [[Conservative Judaism|Conservative Jewish]] parents, Ervin and Susan (née Schlussel) Braun.<ref>{{cite news|first=David|last=Lev|work=[[Arutz 7]]|title=Young Bieber Gets a Firsthand Lesson in Israeli Politics|url=http://www.israelnationalnews.com/News/News.aspx/143531|access-date=January 7, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170422041603/http://israelnationalnews.com/News/News.aspx/143531|archive-date=April 22, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=May 10, 2006|first=Coley|last=Ward|title=Scooter Braun is the Hustla|url=https://creativeloafing.com/content-165779-scooter-braun-is-the-hustla|access-date=2020-10-10|website=Creative Loafing|language=en|archive-date=May 13, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200513080234/https://creativeloafing.com/content-165779-Scooter-Braun-is-the-Hustla|url-status=live}}</ref> Ervin's parents "had barely escaped" the [[Holocaust]], and lived in [[Hungary]] until 1956, when they fled to the United States. Ervin grew up in [[Queens]], and became a [[Dentistry|dentist]]; Susan Schlussel Braun was an  [[Orthodontics|orthodontist]]. After the couple married, they settled in [[Cos Cob, Connecticut]].<ref name=mofflymedia>{{cite web|url=http://mofflymedia.com/Moffly-Publications/Greenwich-Magazine/December-2010/Brains-amp-Braun|last=Hodenfield|first=Chris|title=Brains & Braun|work=Greenwich Magazine|date=December 2010|access-date=December 7, 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120503024157/http://greenwichmag.com/g/December-2010/Brains-amp-Braun|archive-date=May 3, 2012}}</ref><ref name="Bringing Up Bieber">{{cite web|url=http://www.nypost.com/p/entertainment/music/bringing_up_bieber_XCn8bmwOLrbGJZazZI3CAN|last=Schuster|first=Dana|title=Bringing Up Bieber|date=August 15, 2010|work=[[New York Post]]|access-date=January 11, 2011|archive-date=November 5, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121105121947/http://www.nypost.com/p/entertainment/music/bringing_up_bieber_XCn8bmwOLrbGJZazZI3CAN|url-status=live}}</ref>

Braun has four siblings, including [[Adam Braun]]. He grew up in [[Cos Cob, Connecticut]] and attended [[Greenwich High School]] where he was elected class president.<ref name=clatl>{{cite web|url=http://clatl.com/atlanta/the-hustla/Content?oid=1258376|title=Scooter Braun is the Hustla: How a white kid from the North became a power player in Atlanta hip-hop|last=Ward|first=Coley|work=clatl.com|date=May 10, 2006|access-date=December 13, 2010|archive-date=September 24, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100924140651/http://clatl.com/atlanta/the-hustla/Content?oid=1258376|url-status=live}}</ref>

Braun attended [[Emory University]] in [[Atlanta]], where he played [[college basketball]] until his sophomore year.<ref name=mofflymedia /> After [[Jermaine Dupri]] asked him to become the head of marketing at his label, [[So So Def]], Braun reportedly dropped out of college before completing his degree.<ref name="newyorker"/><ref>[http://www.jpost.com/Features/In-Thespotlight/Braun-and-brains-behind-Bieber-313141 Jerusalem Post: "Braun and Brains Behind Bieber" by David Brinn and Amy Spiro] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181120123115/https://www.jpost.com//Features/In-Thespotlight/Braun-and-brains-behind-Bieber-313141 |date=November 20, 2018 }} May 14, 2013</ref><ref name=hollywoodreporter>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/brains-bieber-conversation-scooter-braun-48088|last=Halperin|first=Shirley|title=The Brains Behind Bieber: A Conversation with Scooter Braun|work=Hollywood Reporter|date=November 22, 2010|access-date=December 12, 2010|archive-date=March 31, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331042551/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/brains-bieber-conversation-scooter-braun-48088|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
Braun began organizing parties while studying at [[Emory University]] in [[Atlanta]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Brains Behind Bieber: A Conversation With Scooter Braun {{!}} Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/brains-bieber-conversation-scooter-braun-48088|access-date=2020-10-10|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com|archive-date=March 31, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331042551/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/brains-bieber-conversation-scooter-braun-48088|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2002, Braun was hired to plan [[Party#After-party|after-parties]] in each of the five cities on the [[Anger Management Tour]], featuring [[Ludacris]] and [[Eminem]].<ref name=clatl/> This launch into the world of [[hip-hop]] led Braun to producer [[Jermaine Dupri]], the director of [[So So Def Recordings|So So Def Records]]. Braun was 19 years old when Dupri asked him to join So So Def in a marketing position, and 20 when Dupri named him So So Def's executive director for marketing.<ref>{{cite journal|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BKrMkpe3hGgC&q=scooter+braun+billboard+30+under+30+2009&pg=PT22|title=30 Under Thirty 2009|last=Duffy|first=Thom|journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publication-date=August 22, 2009|access-date=May 11, 2015|page=23|volume=121|number=33|issn=0006-2510|date=August 22, 2009}}</ref> Still in his sophomore year at Emory, Braun was working at So So Def and operating his party promotion business. Some of his larger events included parties for the [[2003 NBA All-Star Game]] and after-parties on [[Britney Spears]]' [[The Onyx Hotel Tour|Onyx Hotel Tour]].<ref name=clatl/> Braun departed So So Def to start a private venture including a marketing business, music label, and artist representation.<ref name="newyorker">{{cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2012/09/03/120903fa_fact_widdicombe?currentPage=all|last=Widdycombe|first=Lizzy|title=Teen Titan|date=September 3, 2012|work=[[The New Yorker]]|access-date=June 27, 2013|archive-date=May 13, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130513181231/http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2012/09/03/120903fa_fact_widdicombe?currentPage=all|url-status=live}}</ref> He started his own marketing business by brokering a $12 million campaign deal between Ludacris and [[Pontiac (automobile)|Pontiac]]; the [[music video]] for Ludacris' "[[The Red Light District#Samples and interpolations|Two Miles an Hour]]" would feature a Pontiac while Pontiac's commercials would feature the song.<ref name=clatl/>

Braun first encountered [[Justin Bieber]] when he saw a video of a 12-year-old Bieber on [[YouTube]], performing a song by [[Ne-Yo]]. Braun contacted Bieber's mother, [[Pattie Mallette]], who agreed to bring her son to Atlanta for a no-strings-attached trial period. Eventually, Braun convinced them to move permanently from Canada to the United States. After further online success, Braun pitched Bieber to two successful artists, [[Usher (singer)|Usher]] and [[Justin Timberlake]]; both expressed interest. Eventually Usher's mentor, music executive [[L. A. Reid]], signed Bieber to a deal with [[The Island Def Jam Music Group|Island Def Jam]] in partnership with [[RBMG Records|Raymond-Braun Media Group (RBMG)]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/justin-bieber-cover-team-strategy-97658|title=Justin Bieber Cover: The Team and Strategy Behind Making Him a Star|first=Shirley|last=Halperin|date=February 9, 2011|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|access-date=May 11, 2015|quote=The resulting joint venture, which took six months to come together, is a straightforward 50-50 split between IDJ and the newly formed Raymond Braun Media Group.|archive-date=March 31, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331042601/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/justin-bieber-cover-team-strategy-97658|url-status=live}}</ref> He then signed a deal with Spotify.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Spangler|first1=Todd|last2=Spangler|first2=Todd|date=2020-10-21|title=Spotify Inks Podcast First-Look Deal With Scooter Braun's Ithaca, Kicking Off With Graham Bunn Country Music Series|url=https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/spotify-scooter-braun-podcast-graham-bunn-country-music-1234811781/|access-date=2020-10-25|website=Variety|language=en-US|archive-date=October 21, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201021144315/https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/spotify-scooter-braun-podcast-graham-bunn-country-music-1234811781/|url-status=live}}</ref>
