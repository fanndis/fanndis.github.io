'''King Fahd Medical City (KFMC)''' is a medical facility in [[Riyadh]], [[Saudi Arabia]]. With four hospitals, KFMC is the largest medical facility in Saudi Arabia. It was built at a cost of $633 million. KFMC’s yearly operating budget is estimated at $150 million for medical and non-medical maintenance and catering contracts.<ref name="KFMC2"/> According to the Minister of Health, Dr. Hamad ibn Abdullah Al-Manie in his inaugural address, it is expected to treat more than 50,000 patients in the hospital and more than 2,000,000 patients in outpatient clinics annually.<ref name="KFMC2">{{cite web|url=http://www.arabnews.com/node/256188|title=Abdullah Opens Fahd Medical City|last=Rasooldeen|first=Mohammed|date=2004-10-06|work=[[Arab News]]|accessdate=May 6, 2017}}</ref> The complex consists of four hospitals:  The Obstetrics & Gynecology Hospital, The Specialist Hospital, The Rehabilitation Hospital, and The Pediatric Hospital.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Inc|first=International Hospitals Recruitment|title=International Hospitals Recruitment Inc.|url=https://ihrcanada.com/|access-date=2020-07-29|website=International Hospitals Recruitment Inc.}}</ref>

== Children Specialized Hospital ==
The Children Specialized Hospital was the first hospital to operate in KFMC, starting in June 2003.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Home|url=https://www.kfmc.med.sa/EN/ChildrenSpecializedHospital/Pages/default.aspx|access-date=2020-07-29|website=www.kfmc.med.sa}}</ref> The hospital contains several facilities such as the medical library and central records, in-service training facilities, pharmacy, operating theaters, and additional laboratories.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Inc|first=International Hospitals Recruitment|title=International Hospitals Recruitment Inc.|url=https://ihrcanada.com/|access-date=2020-07-29|website=International Hospitals Recruitment Inc.}}</ref>
