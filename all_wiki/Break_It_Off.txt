"'''Break It Off'''" is a song by Barbadian singer [[Rihanna]] from her second studio album ''[[A Girl Like Me (Rihanna album)|A Girl like Me]]'' (2006), and features guest vocals from [[Sean Paul]]. It was written by Donovan Bennett, Paul, K. Ford and Rihanna, while production was handled by Don Corleon. The song was released on 13 November 2006, as the album's fourth and final single. "Break It Off" is a futuristic [[Pop music|pop]]-[[dancehall]] and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] song, which is layered over an [[Electro music|electro]]-[[reggae]] [[Beat (music)|beat]].

Critical reception of the song was positive, with reviewers praising the collaboration between Rihanna and Paul and as a return to the former's roots. "Break It Off" reached number eight in Portugal, number nine in the US, and number ten in Flanders (Belgium). Rihanna performed the song at [[Radio 1's Big Weekend|Radio One Big Weekend]] in 2007 and it was included on the [[set list]] of her [[Good Girl Gone Bad Tour]] (2007–09), and was subsequently included on the accompanying DVD release, entitled ''[[Good Girl Gone Bad Live]]''.

==Background and release==
[[File:SeanPaulIRAWA.jpg|thumb|left|upright=0.75|alt=A picture of a man wearing a beige jacket and holding a silver award statue|[[Sean Paul]] co-wrote and provided vocals on "Break It Off".]]
"Break It Off" was recorded at 2 Hard Studios in [[Kingston, Jamaica]].<ref name="Notes"/> It was written by Donovan Bennett, Sean Paul Henriques (better known as [[Sean Paul]]), K. Ford and [[Rihanna]], who is credited as Robyn Fenty.<ref name="Notes"/> Production of the song was handled by Don Corleon.<ref name="Notes"/> It was [[Music engineer|engineered]] by Jeremy Harding and Bennett, who also [[Mixing (music production)|mixed]] the song.<ref name="Notes"/> All [[instrumentation]] was provided by Bennett.<ref name="Notes"/> Paul explained how he and Rihanna met in a 2011 interview with ''[[Rap-Up]]''. During her visit to Jamaica, Rihanna was taken on a tour around the island by Paul, where he took her to some of the beaches and experienced the nightlife.<ref name="2RapUp2011">{{cite web|title=Rap-Up TV: Sean Paul gets 'Deep' with Kelly Rowland, reminisces about Rihanna, & praises Nicki Minaj|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2011/10/13/rap-up-tv-sean-paul-gets-deep-with-kelly-rowland-reminisces-about-rihanna-praises-nicki-minaj/|work=[[Rap-Up]]|date=5 August 2012|accessdate=5 August 2012}}</ref> He continued to explain that they spent a few days together and that he took her to the [[Bob Marley Museum]], which was something she had "always wanted to do."<ref name="2RapUp2011"/> Reminiscing on "Break It Off" and Rihanna, Paul stated that working with the singer and creating the song was "his most memorable collaboration."<ref name="2RapUp2011"/>

"Break It Off" was released as the fourth and final single from Rihanna's second studio album, ''[[A Girl like Me (Rihanna album)|A Girl like Me]]'' (2006). It was released to US [[Contemporary hit radio]] station playlists on 13 November 2006, by [[Def Jam Recordings]].<ref name="USRadioRelease">{{cite magazine |url=http://www.fmqb.com/Article.asp?id=69239 |title=Available for aiplay |magazine=[[FMQB]] |date=8 August 2006 |accessdate=11 December 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130602195200/http://fmqb.com/Article.asp?id=69239 |archivedate=2 June 2013 }}</ref> It was also made available to [[Music download|download digitally]] via [[iTunes Store|iTunes]] in various countries around the world on 27 February 2007, including Australia,<ref name="AustraliaiTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/break-it-off-single/id218285244|title=Break It Off – Single – Rihanna & Sean Paul|work=[[iTunes Store]] (Australia)|publisher=[[Apple Inc.|Apple]]|date=27 February 2007|accessdate=5 August 2012}}</ref> Austria,<ref name="AustriaTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/at/album/break-it-off-single/id218285244|title=Break It Off – Single – Rihanna & Sean Paul|work=iTunes Store (Austria)|publisher=Apple|date=27 February 2007|accessdate=5 August 2012|language=German}}</ref> Belgium<ref name="BelgiumiTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/be/album/break-it-off-single/id218285244|title=Break It Off – Single – Rihanna & Sean Paul|work=iTunes Store (Belgium)|publisher=Apple|date=27 February 2007|accessdate=5 August 2012}}</ref> and Spain.<ref name="SpainiTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/es/album/break-it-off-single/id218285244|title=Break It Off – Single – Rihanna & Sean Paul|work=iTunes Store (Spain)|publisher=Apple|date=27 February 2007|accessdate=5 August 2012|language=Spanish}}</ref>

==Composition and lyrics==
"Break It Off" is a "futuristic"<ref name="About.com"/> [[Pop music|pop]]-[[dancehall]] and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] song, which is layered over an [[Electro music|electro]]-[[reggae]] [[Beat (music)|beat]].<ref name="NYTReview"/> It received a comparison by Ruth Jamieson for ''[[The Guardian]]'' to Rihanna's debut single "[[Pon de Replay]]" from her first studio album, ''[[Music of the Sun]]''.<ref name="The Guardian">{{cite news|last=Jamieson|first=Ruth|title=Rihanna, A Girl Like Me|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2006/apr/23/34|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=23 April 2006|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> According to Dan Charnas for ''[[The Washington Post]]'', "Break It Off" showcases Rihanna's [[Caribbean]] roots.<ref name="TWPReview">{{cite news|last=Charnas|first=Dan|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/04/25/AR2006042501896.html|title=Quick Spin|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=25 April 2006|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> "Break It Off" is written in the [[key (music)|key]] of [[D-flat major]] and is set in [[common time]] with a moderate dance [[tempo|groove]] with 130 [[Beats per minute#Beats per minute|beats per minute]] (BPM).<ref name="Digital Sheet Music, Rihanna Break It Off">{{cite journal|title=Digital Sheet Music, Rihanna 'Break It Off'|publisher=Musicnotes.com|year=2006}}</ref> Rihanna's [[vocal range]] in the song spans one [[octave]] from the lower note of [[A (musical note)|A<sub>3</sub>]] to the higher note of [[A (musical note)|A<sub>4</sub>]].<ref name="Digital Sheet Music, Rihanna Break It Off"/> The song includes [[piano]] keys and [[guitar]] strings as part of its instrumental composition.<ref name="Notes"/><ref name="Digital Sheet Music, Rihanna Break It Off"/>

==Critical reception==
"Break It Off" garnered a positive response from [[Music journalism|music critics]]. David Jeffries for [[AllMusic]] was complimentary of "Break It Off", writing that it is "totally juiced" and that she provides good competition for Paul.<ref name="AllmusicReview">{{cite web|last=Jeffries|first=David|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/a-girl-like-me-r828728/review|title=Review: A Girl Like Me|website=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> Kelefa Sanneh for ''[[The New York Times]]'' praised the song, and described it as Rihanna's "triumphant return to her old formula."<ref name="NYTReview">{{cite news|last=Sanneh|first=Kelefa|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/04/24/arts/critics-choice-new-cds-642568.html|title=Critic's Choice: New CD's|work=[[The New York Times]]|publisher=[[Arthur Ochs Sulzberger, Jr.]]|date=24 April 2006|accessdate=5 August 2012}}</ref> Quentin B. Huff for [[PopMatters]] lauded Sean Paul for his contribution to the song while praising Rihanna's hook, writing that "[Rihanna's] hook is so infectious that hers is the voice you ultimately remember. That's another dancehall-flavored song and another winner."<ref name="PopMattersReview">{{cite web|last=Huff|first=Quentin B.|url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/rihanna-a-girl-like-me/|title=Review: A Girl Like Me|work=[[PopMatters]]|date=25 April 2006|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> Bill Lamb for [[About.com]] called "Break It Off", along with "Kisses Don't Lie", "candidates for widespread pop radio airplay".<ref name="About.com">{{cite web|last=Lamb|first=Bill|url=http://top40.about.com/od/albums/fr/rihannagirllike.htm|title=Rihanna – A Girl Like Me|publisher=[[About.com]]|accessdate=6 May 2012}}</ref>
