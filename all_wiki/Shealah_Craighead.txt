'''Shealah Craighead''' (born September 25, 1976) is an American government photographer currently serving as the [[Chief Official White House Photographer]] for President [[Donald Trump]]. Craighead previously served as official photographer to former First Lady [[Laura Bush]] in the [[Presidency of George W. Bush|administration of George W. Bush]].<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.aol.com/article/news/2017/08/08/trumps-official-white-house-photographer-reveals-how-she-gained/23070968/ | title = Trump's Official White House Photographer reveals how she gained his trust | publisher =AOL | date = August 8, 2017 | first = Sarah | last = Jacobs|access-date = February 12, 2018}}</ref>

==Early life and family==
Craighead was born in [[Connecticut]] where her parents owned a photo lab. As she grew up she "had always wanted to do something that involved traveling the world and living in hotels. And that involved photography." She wanted to work in Washington, D.C., where she ended up living.<ref name=Flock>{{cite news|url=https://www.pbs.org/newshour/art/like-president-trumps-white-house-photographer/|author=Flock, Elizabeth|date=August 7, 2017|accessdate=August 13, 2017|title=What it's like to be President Trump's White House photographer|publisher=PBS (Newshour Productions)}}</ref> She became engaged to a British [[Royal Marine]] and member of the [[Special Air Service|SAS]], Christian Craighead after he proposed to her in February 2020. The couple announced this jointly on their social media pages in July after first going public in Las Vegas earlier in the year as "Mr and Mrs Smith".<ref>https://www.instagram.com/shealahdcphoto/?hl=en</ref> <ref> https://imgur.com/gallery/HWZq2uP </ref>

==Career==
[[File:Shealah Craighead Marine One 2017-05-04.jpg|thumb|left|Craighead photographing [[Marine One]] in 2017]]
She attended college, doing freelance photography work with the ''Boston Globe'', Associated Press, and Getty Images afterwards. In 2005, she worked under David Bohrer, who was the official photographer for Vice President [[Dick Cheney]]. She photographed "weddings, sports, events, news, portraits," and spot news.<ref name=Flock /> She uses a stool while taking photos as she is only {{convert|5|ft|2|in}} tall.<ref name=Flock />

When another photographer decided to not return from maternity leave, Craighead applied for their job and became the official photographer to First Lady [[Laura Bush]] in the administration of [[George W. Bush]].<ref name=Flock /><ref name="CNN1">{{cite web|last1=Stelter|first1=Brian|title=Trump team closing in on White House photographer who worked with Bushes|url=https://money.cnn.com/2017/01/21/media/donald-trump-white-house-photographer/|website=CNN.com|accessdate=27 January 2017}}</ref> She was the photographer of [[Jenna Bush Hager]]'s wedding in 2008 as well.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.marieclaire.com/politics/a15338513/shealah-craighead-chief-white-house-photographer-interview/|title=The Woman Behind the Lens: Meet White House Photographer Shealah Craighead|last=Epstein|first=Rachel|date=2018-01-18|website=Marie Claire|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-20}}</ref> In 2008, she was campaign photographer for [[Sarah Palin]],<ref>{{cite book|title=Newsweek|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=mUnuAAAAMAAJ|year=2008|publisher=Newsweek, Incorporated}}</ref><ref name="ConroyWalshe2010">{{cite book|author1=Scott Conroy|author2=Shushannah Walshe|title=Sarah from Alaska: The Sudden Rise and Brutal Education of a New Conservative Superstar|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zjn9AgAAQBAJ&pg=PA218|date=November 2010|publisher=PublicAffairs|isbn=978-1-58648-904-5|pages=218â€“}}</ref> and then for Texas Senator [[Kay Bailey Hutchison]] and later for Florida Senator [[Marco Rubio]] through his Senate career and presidential campaign.<ref name=Flock />

In 2017, Craighead was named as the official [[White House]] photographer for the presidency of [[Donald Trump]].<ref name="Time1">{{cite web|last1=Laurent|first1=Olivia|title=Meet President Trump's Official White House Photographer|url=http://time.com/4642160/donald-trump-official-photographer/|website=Time|accessdate=27 January 2017}}</ref><ref name="ParMatch">{{cite web|last1=Sekkai|first1=Kahina|title=Shealah Craighead, la photographe officielle de Donald Trump|url=http://www.parismatch.com/Actu/International/Shealah-Craighead-la-photographe-officielle-de-Donald-Trump-1174412|website=Paris Match|accessdate=27 January 2017}}</ref> The Trump administration employs a total of four photographers with different specialties including fashion, military, and administration.<ref name=Flock /> Craighead photographed [[Nancy Pelosi]] standing, to address a seated Donald Trump in an October 2019 meeting on Turkey.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-50079639|title=Whose meltdown?
