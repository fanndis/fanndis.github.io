'''Olivier Rousteing''' (born September 13, 1985)<ref>in french : https://www.elle.fr/Personnalites/Olivier-Rousteing</ref> is a [[French people|French]] [[fashion designer]]. He has been the creative director of [[Balmain (fashion house)|Balmain]] since 2011.

== Early life and education ==
Rousteing was adopted by his parents at the age of 1.<ref name="Van Meter">{{cite web|url=http://www.out.com/fashion/2015/4/02/olivier-rousteing-selfie-made-man|title=Olivier Rousteing: A Self(ie)-Made Man|last1=Van Meter|first1=William|date=2 April 2015|website=Out|publisher=LPI Media|accessdate=11 July 2015}}</ref> Rousteing's mother is an optician, while his father is a seaport manager.<ref name="Van Meter" /> He grew up in Bordeaux and moved to [[Paris]] to study at [[ESMOD]] (Ecole Supérieure des Arts et Techniques de la Mode). Like [[Simon Porte Jacquemus|Jacquemus]], he dropped out in his first year citing lack of creative freedom.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}} After completing internships in Italy, in 2003, Rousteing began his career as a designer at [[Roberto Cavalli]] and went on to become the head of the label's women's division.

==Career==
In 2003, he graduated and began his career as a designer at Roberto Cavalli, where he was promoted to creative director of the Italian label's women's ready-to-wear collection, serving for five years in that position.<ref name="en.vogue.fr">{{Cite web |url=http://en.vogue.fr/vogue-list/thevoguelist/olivier-rousteing/988 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2015-10-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180626192639/https://en.vogue.fr/vogue-list/thevoguelist/olivier-rousteing/988 |archive-date=2018-06-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Rousteing joined Balmain in 2009.<ref>“Olivier Rousteing Named New Designer at Balmain.” T Magazine. Accessed January 26, 2014. http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/04/26/olivier-rousteing-named-new-designer-at-balmain/</ref> During his early time at Balmain he worked closely with [[Christophe Decarnin]], then the French fashion house's creative director.<ref name="en.vogue.fr"/>

On 26 April 2011, at 25 years old, Olivier Rousteing replaced Christophe Decarnin as the [[creative director]] of [[Balmain (fashion house)|Balmain]].<ref>[http://en.vogue.fr/thevoguelist/olivier-rousteing-1/344 Olivier Rousteing] ''Vogue''</ref> While he liked Decarnin's aesthetic, he wanted to orient the label towards the finer aspects of French couture. At the time of his appointment, Rousteing was a relatively unknown designer, and brought a much-needed fresh take on the brand's aesthetic that remains to this day. He has been credited with adding an Asian influence to the clothing, as Asia comprises a huge part of the brand's buyers.<ref>admin. "Exclusive Interview with Balmain's Olivier Rousteing." The D'Vine. Accessed January 26, 2014. http://www.the-dvine.com/2012/11/exclusive-interview-with-balmains-olivier-rousteing/.</ref> Rousteing says his age, initial anonymity, and especially race led to grumblings amongst the fashion establishment.<ref name="Van Meter" /> "People were like, 'Oh my God, he's a minority taking over a French house!'" Rousteing told ''Out'' magazine in 2015.<ref name="Van Meter" />

Since his arrival, menswear now accounts for 40 percent of Balmain's revenue.<ref name="Van Meter" /><ref name=Schneier /> While the company did not release figures, it was estimated that Balmain's revenue increased 15 to 20 percent between 2012 and 2015.<ref name="Van Meter" /> Rousteing opened a Balmain store in [[London]], Balmain's stand-alone boutique outside of Paris.<ref name=Schneier>{{cite web|last1=Schneier|first1=Matthew|title=Olivier Rousteing Puts Balmain Men’s Wear on the Runway|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/06/30/fashion/paris-mens-fashion-spring-2015-balmain-olivier-rousteing.html?ribbon-ad-idx=5&rref=style&module=Ribbon&version=context&region=Header&action=click&contentCollection=Style&pgtype=article|website=The New York Times|accessdate=11 July 2015|date=29 June 2015}}</ref> A [[New York City]] store opened in SoHo in April 2016.<ref>http://www.papermag.com/balmain-opens-flagship-store-1710103151.html</ref> Other stores are in the planning stage.<ref name=Schneier />

With the help of A-List celebrity friends like [[Kim Kardashian]], [[Kelly Rowland]], [[Jennifer Lopez]], [[Rihanna]], [[Björk]], [[Beyoncé]], [[Justin Bieber]], [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Chris Brown]], EMFAM and various models, including those from [[Victoria's Secret]], Rousteing helped Balmain become the first French label to pass the million-follower plateau on [[Instagram]].<ref name=Schneier /><ref name=Vingan>{{cite web |last1=Vingan |first1=Alyssa |title=Balmain's Olivier Rousteing Opens Up About Industry Racism and Instagram in 'Out' |url=http://fashionista.com/2015/04/olivier-rousteing-out-magazine |website=[[Fashionista (website)|Fashionista]] |publisher=Breaking Media, Inc. |accessdate=11 July 2015|date=3 April 2015}}</ref>

He designed the costumes for the opera, ''Renaissance'' – a 27-minute ballet with 22 dancers,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/gallery/balmain-olivier-rousteing-designs-costumes-for-paris-opera-ballet|title=Balmain's Ballet: First Look At Olivier Rousteing's Designs For The Paris Opéra|last=Crisell|first=Hattie|website=www.vogue.co.uk|access-date=2017-11-27}}</ref> which was choreographed by Sébastien Bertaud and opened at Opéra de Paris on June 13, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.operadeparis.fr/en/season-16-17/ballet/bertaud-bouche-paul-valastro|title=Bertaud / Bouché / Paul / Valastro - Ballet - Season 16/17 Programming|website=Opéra national de Paris|language=en|access-date=2017-11-27}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Rousteing was adopted and is openly gay.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/article/olivier-rousteing-balmain-interview-vogue-september-issue|title=Olivier Rousteing's Childhood Memories|last=Weir|first=Laura|access-date=2017-11-27}}</ref> Though he had previously believed he was of [[mixed race]], in 2019 he discovered that his birth mother is [[Somalis|Somali]] from [[Djibouti]] and his birth father [[Ethiopian]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/09/24/style/olivier-rousteing-adoption-film-balmain.html|title=The Truth About Olivier Rousteing|last=Friedman|first=Vanessa|access-date=2019-09-25}}</ref>
