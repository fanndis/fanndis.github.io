'''Tally Technologies, Inc.''' (or simply '''Tally''') is a [[San Francisco]], California-based American [[financial services]] company founded by Jason Brown and Jasper Platz in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/tally-raises-25-million-series-b-led-by-kleiner-perkins-300686153.html|title=Tally Raises $25 Million Series B Led By Kleiner Perkins|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en|access-date=2018-08-09}}</ref>

The company's [[smartphone]] app helps its users pay down their [[credit card]] debt, based on an analysis of their personal financial profiles and a new line of credit it provides with a lower interest rate.<ref name="NYTimes">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/21/your-money/a-service-to-pay-off-high-interest-credit-cards-but-a-bad-time-to-start.html|title=A Service to Pay Off High-Interest Credit Cards, but a Bad Time to Start|last1=Lieber|first1=Ron|date=19 May 2016|work=New York Times|accessdate=22 May 2016|publisher=New York Times|ref=Ny Times}}</ref> The app also manages credit card payments, allowing its users to avoid credit card late fees.<ref name="Mashable">{{cite news|url=http://mashable.com/2016/05/29/tally-credit-card-app|title=A new app wants to help you beat the credit card companies|last1=Abbruzzese|first1=Jason|date=29 May 2016|work=Mashable|accessdate=31 May 2016|publisher=Mashable}}</ref>

Tally earns revenue by charging interest when a user carries a balance.<ref name="NYTimes" /> The company does not charge annual, balance transfer, late, prepayment or insufficient funds fees, and generates revenue only when it charges a lower APR than its users' credit cards.<ref name="SVBJ" /><ref name="Credit.com" /> 

==Founders and Investors==
[[File:JasperPlatz JasonBrown.jpg|thumb|Jasper Platz and Jason Brown in San Francisco]]
Jason Brown and Jasper Platz, who previously founded [[REPOWER by Solar Universe|Gen110, Inc.]], a [[solar energy]] company funded by [[Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers]],<ref name="KPCB">{{cite web|last1=Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers|title=Gen110|url=http://www.kpcb.com/companies/gen110|website=KPCB|accessdate=31 May 2016}}</ref><ref name="Venture Beat">{{cite news|last1=Mitroff|first1=Sarah|title=Gen110 grabs funding from Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers|url=https://venturebeat.com/2012/05/17/gen110-grabs-funding/|accessdate=31 May 2016|work=Venture Beat|publisher=Venture Beat|date=17 May 2012}}</ref> started Tally in 2015.<ref name="Mashable" /> Brown and Platz realized that since credit card [[annual percentage rate]]s (APRs) are typically 15 to 20 percent<ref name="NYTimes" /> and [[late fee]]s are common,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/201512_cfpb_report-the-consumer-credit-card-market.pdf|title=The Consumer Credit Card Market|last=|first=|date=December 2015|website=CFPB|publisher=CFPB|access-date=31 May 2016}}</ref> there was an opportunity to make credit cards "less expensive and easier to manage."

In June 2015, Tally received $2 million in [[Seed money|seed funding]] led by [[Aileen Lee]] at Cowboy Ventures<ref name="Mashable" />, with participation from Accelerate IT Ventures (AITV) and Sherpalo Ventures. 

In May 2016, the company secured a $15 million [[Series A round]] led by Sean Flynn at [[Shasta Ventures]], with participation from existing investors, as well as [[Silicon Valley Bank]] ({{NASDAQ|SIVB}}).<ref name="SVBJ">{{Cite news|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/sanjose/blog/techflash/2016/05/credit-card-manager-tally-raises-15m-to-cut.html|title=Credit card manager Tally raises $15M to cut consumer debt|last=Hall|first=Gina|date=20 May 2016|work=Silicon Valley Business Journal|access-date=31 May 2016|via=}}</ref> 

In July 2018, Tally announced it raised an additional $25 million in [[venture capital]] funding from Kleiner Perkins and existing investors Shasta Ventures, Cowboy Ventures and Sway Ventures.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cheddar.com/videos/credit-card-payoff-app-tally-raises-25-million|title=Credit Card Payoff App Tally Raises $25 Million|website=Cheddar|language=en|access-date=2018-08-09}}</ref> 

==Features==
Tally pays its users' credit card debt with a [[line of credit]] provided by the company. The user makes one monthly minimum payment to Tally.<ref name="NYTimes" /> To determine eligibility, the user scans their credit cards through the app and agrees to a soft credit check.<ref name="Mashable" /> .The new credit line is a [[revolving account]] and is available to customers with at least a 660 [[FICO]] [[Credit score in the United States|credit score]].<ref name="Credit.com">{{cite news|last1=Krasny|first1=Jill|title=A Simple Way to Pay Off Your Most Expensive Credit Card Debt|url=http://blog.credit.com/2016/05/a-simple-way-to-pay-off-your-most-expensive-credit-card-debt-145396/|accessdate=31 May 2016|work=Credit.com|publisher=Credit.com|date=25 May 2016}}</ref> Tally also offers the feature of sending payments automatically, so that users can pay their bills on time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/08/28/robo-advisor-for-the-99-percent-aims-to-help-you-pay-off-debt-faster.html|title=This app wants to make your credit cards cheaper so you can pay off debt faster|last=Elkins|first=Kathleen|date=2018-08-28|website=CNBC|language=en|access-date=2019-08-22}}</ref>
