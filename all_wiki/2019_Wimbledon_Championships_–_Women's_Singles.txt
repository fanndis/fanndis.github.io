{{main|2019 Wimbledon Championships}}

[[Simona Halep]] defeated [[Serena Williams]] in the final, 6–2, 6–2 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2019 Wimbledon Championships. It took just 56 minutes for Halep to secure victory where she committed only 3 unforced errors, the fewest on record in a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] final.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://amp.cnn.com/cnn/2019/07/13/tennis/simona-halep-serena-williams-wimbledon-final-tennis-intl-spt/index.html|title=Flawless Simona Halep beats Serena Williams to win first Wimbledon|website=CNN|date=14 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.foxsportsasia.com/tennis/wimbledon/1139918/wimbledon-2019-simona-halep-creates-all-time-grand-slam-record-for-lowest-unforced-errors/amp/|title=Wimbledon 2019: Simona Halep creates all-time Grand Slam record for lowest unforced errors|website=Fox Sports Asia|date=14 July 2019}}</ref> In the entire tournament, she dropped only one set, against compatriot [[Mihaela Buzărnescu]]. Williams was attempting to equal [[Margaret Court]]'s [[List of Grand Slam women's singles champions#Most Grand Slam tournament singles titles|all-time record]] of 24 Grand Slam singles titles for the third time. At 37 years and 291 days, Williams became the oldest Grand Slam women's singles finalist to compete since the start of the [[History of tennis|Open Era]] in 1968.

[[Angelique Kerber]] was the defending champion, but lost in the second round to [[Lauren Davis]]. This was the first time in Open Era history that a defending Major champion lost to a [[lucky loser]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/wta-rankings-update-2019-halep-returns-top-5-gauff-soars | title=WTA rankings update 2019: Halep returns to Top 5, Gauff soars | accessdate=15 July 2019}}</ref>

Halep became the first [[Romania]]n female player to reach and win the Wimbledon final. Romanian players previously won [[List of Wimbledon Open Era champions|8 Open Era titles]] at Wimbledon, but none in senior singles. [[Ilie Năstase]] lost two senior singles finals in [[1972 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1972]] and [[1976 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|1976]]. The only other Romanian woman to have previously held a trophy here is [[Ioana Gașpar]], back in [[2000 Wimbledon Championships|2000]] when she was only a junior.

This was the first Major tournament in which [[Ashleigh Barty]] competed as the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players#singles|WTA world No. 1]]. She retained this ranking following the end of the tournament despite being upset by [[Alison Riske]] in the fourth round. [[Naomi Osaka]], [[Karolína Plíšková]], [[Kiki Bertens]] and [[Petra Kvitová]] were also in contention for the top ranking at the start of the tournament. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/wimbledon-2019-whos-chasing-ashleigh-barty-world-no1|title=Wimbledon 2019: Who's chasing Ashleigh Barty for World No.1?|author=Courtney Nguyen|publisher=WTA|date=30 June 2019|accessdate=30 June 2019}}</ref> 

15-year old [[Coco Gauff]] became the youngest player to win a main draw singles match at Wimbledon since [[Jennifer Capriati]] in [[1991 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1991]], defeating the oldest player in the main draw, [[Venus Williams]] at 39, in the first round. Gauff was also the youngest qualifier in Wimbledon history.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2019/07/01/wimbledon-cori-gauff-venus-williams/1620561001/|title=Who is the 15-year-old who beat 39-year-old Venus Williams at Wimbledon?|author=Scott Gleeson|publisher=USA Today|date=1 July 2019|accessdate=1 July 2019}}</ref> She was defeated by eventual champion Halep in the fourth round.

[[Karolína Muchová]] became the first player to reach the quarterfinals of Wimbledon in their debut since [[Li Na]] in [[2006 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|2006]], after defeating Plíšková in the fourth round.

This was the first Wimbledon where a final set [[Tie break (tennis)|tie break]] rule was introduced. Upon reaching 12–12 in the third set, a classic tie break would be played. No women's singles match required the use of the final set tie break.
