'''Ashley Robin Bloomfield'''<ref name="Wai2575">{{cite web |url=https://forms.justice.govt.nz/search/Documents/WT/wt_DOC_142224591/Wai%202575%2C%20A059.pdf |title=Wai 2575, #A59 |date=7 September 2018 |publisher=Waitangi Tribunal, Department of Justice |access-date=25 April 2020}}</ref> (born {{Birth based on age as of date|54|2020|04|11|noage=1}}) is a New Zealand public health official. He is the chief executive of the [[Ministry of Health (New Zealand)|Ministry of Health]] and the country's Director-General of Health. He has been the public-facing health specialist liaising with the media during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand]] on behalf of the government, since the first press conference on 27 January 2020.

==Early life and family==
Bloomfield was born in [[Napier, New Zealand|Napier]]<ref>{{cite news |first=Sahiban |last=Hyde |url= https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=12333498 |work=[[Hawke's Bay Today]] |title=Covid 19 coronavirus: Ashley Bloomfield stuns boy, 6, with handwritten response to letter |date=20 May 2020 |access-date=20 May 2020}}</ref> in 1965 or 1966, one of three children of Allan Olaf Bloomfield and Myreine Alice Bloomfield (née Osborne).<ref name="Fisher"/><ref name="Death notice"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://knowledgebank.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/node/35428/images/1439429289_PN029Apr1961-14_0.jpg |title=Bloomfield–Osborne |work=Hawke's Bay Photo News |date=April 1961| volume=3 |number=5 |page=15 |access-date=12 April 2020}}</ref> His mother was a schoolteacher, while his father was a [[lieutenant colonel]] in the [[Royal New Zealand Infantry Regiment]] (Territorial Force) and a manager at Mitsubishi Motors in [[Porirua]], and was appointed a [[Member of the Order of the British Empire]] in the [[1974 New Year Honours (New Zealand)|1974 New Year Honours]].<ref name="Fisher"/><ref name="Death notice">{{cite news |url=https://notices.nzherald.co.nz/obituaries/nzherald-nz/obituary.aspx?n=allan-olaf-bloomfield&pid=184129599 |title=Allan Bloomfield death notice |work=New Zealand Herald |date=15 February 2017 |access-date=12 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{London Gazette |issue=46163 |date=1 January 1974 |page=36 |supp=2}}</ref>

Bloomfield grew up in [[Tawa, New Zealand|Tawa]], a suburb of [[Wellington]], and was educated at [[Scots College, Wellington|Scots College]], where he was head prefect,<ref name="Fisher">{{cite news |url= https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=12324032 |title= Ashley Bloomfield's rise to the top – the inside story |work=New Zealand Herald |date=11 April 2020 |access-date=12 April 2020 |first=David |last=Fisher}}</ref> [[Dux#Education|dux]], played 1st XV rugby<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nzherald.co.nz/lifestyle/news/article.cfm?c_id=6&objectid=12330408 |title=Covid-19 coronavirus: Inside Dr Ashley Bloomfield's work and family bubble |work=New Zealand Herald |date=7 May 2020 |access-date=7 May 2020 }}</ref> and was a member of the cast of the college's production of ''[[Oklahoma!]]''<ref>{{Cite news |title=Hilary Barry shares photo of teen Ashley Bloomfield performing in 1983 school production of Oklahoma! |date=31 August 2020 |first=Monika |last=Barton |work=Newshub |url=https://www.newshub.co.nz/home/entertainment/2020/08/hilary-barry-shares-photo-of-teen-ashley-bloomfield-performing-in-1983-school-production-of-oklahoma.html |access-date=15 November 2020}}</ref>

Bloomfield graduated [[Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery]] from the [[University of Auckland]] in 1990.<ref name="Wai2575"/><ref name="Fisher"/> About the same time, he married his wife, Libby, also a doctor, and the couple went on to have three children.<ref name="Fisher"/>

==Career==
Bloomfield completed several years of clinical work and from 1996<ref name="Stuff bio" /> specialised in public health medicine,<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://www.odt.co.nz/opinion/editorial/cometh-hour%E2%80%A6|title=Cometh the hour…|date=21 March 2020|work=Otago Daily Times|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref> concentrating on non-communicable diseases.<ref name="MoH introduction" /><ref>{{Cite news |last=Thomas |first=Rachel |url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/science/28-03-2020/the-face-of-the-covid-19-response-who-is-ashley-bloomfield/ |title=The face of the Covid-19 response: Who is Ashley Bloomfield? |date=28 March 2020 |work=The Spinoff |access-date=28 March 2020 }}</ref> In 1997, he graduated from the University of Auckland with a Master of Public Health degree, with first-class honours.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/for/current-students/cs-academic-information/cs-graduation-information/cs-graduation-search-and-confirmation/cs-graduation-search-results.html |title=Graduation search results |publisher=University of Auckland |access-date=21 August 2020}}</ref>

Between 2004 and 2006, Bloomfield was the Ministry of Health's acting director of public health.<ref>{{cite news |title=Check for Sars symptoms, doctors told |url= https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=3563153 |access-date=18 March 2020 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |date=28 April 2004 | first=Renee |last=Kiriona}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Bird flu campaign to target 1.4m households |url= https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10364209 |access-date=18 March 2020 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |date=18 January 2006 |first1=Kiong |last1=Errol |first2=Martin |last2= Johnston }}</ref> From 2006 to 2010, he was the ministry's chief public health adviser.<ref name="Stuff bio" />

[[File:Ashley Bloomfield elbow bump.jpg|thumb|right|Bloomfield (right) [[elbow bump]]s with Michael Holdsworth at [[Government House, Wellington]], in October 2020, while [[Patsy Reddy|Dame Patsy Reddy]] and [[David Gascoigne|Sir David Gascoigne]] look on]]
From late 2010 to late 2011, based at the [[World Health Organization]] in [[Geneva]], Bloomfield worked on non-communicable disease prevention and control with a global focus.<ref name="MoH introduction">{{cite web |title=Introducing Ashley Bloomfield |url= https://www.health.govt.nz/news-media/news-items/introducing-ashley-bloomfield |publisher=[[Ministry of Health (New Zealand)|Ministry of Health]] |access-date=18 March 2020 |date=13 June 2018}}</ref> From 2012 to 2015, he held leading positions across district health boards with [[Capital & Coast District Health Board|Capital & Coast District]], [[Hutt Valley District Health Board|Hutt Valley]], and [[Wairarapa District Health Board|Wairarapa]]. From 2015 to 2018, Bloomfield was chief executive at Hutt Valley District Health Board.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.health.govt.nz/about-ministry/leadership-ministry/executive-leadership-team#ashley|title=Dr Ashley Bloomfield: Director-General of Health and Chief Executive|date=25 November 2019|website=Ministry of Health Manatū Hauoa|publisher=New Zealand Government|access-date=18 March 2020}}</ref> In the first half of 2018, Bloomfield was seconded to the [[Capital & Coast District Health Board]] where he was interim chief executive. Since 11 June 2018, he has been the chief executive of the country's Ministry of Health and the country's Director-General of Health.<ref name="Wai2575"/><ref name="Fisher"/><ref name="MoH introduction" /> Since he started as the ministry's Chief Executive he has attended a [[leadership]] programme at [[Saïd Business School]] at the [[University of Oxford]].<ref name="Stuff bio">{{cite news |last1=Macdonald |first1=Nikki |title=Coronavirus: Ashley Bloomfield is the face of NZ's virus defence |url= https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/health/120071359/coronavirus-ashley-bloomfield-is-the-face-of-nzs-pandemic-response |access-date=18 March 2020 |work=[[Stuff (website)|Stuff]] |date=7 March 2020 | quote = After taking the job – and after attending a leadership programme at Oxford University – Bloomfield said he subscribed to a 'convene and collaborate' and 'ensuring direction not giving direction' model of leadership, rather than command and control. }}</ref>

Colleagues describe Bloomfield as "measured, methodical, calm and sensible".<ref name="Stuff bio" /> Former health reporter Tess Nichol, writing for the American online magazine ''[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]'', described him as "New Zealand’s current obsession, an unlikely heartthrob, a mild-mannered health care hero".<ref>

Bloomfield chose the nickname "The Eliminator" for the match, in which he opened the scoring with the first [[Try (rugby)|try]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chumko|first=Andre|date=2020-07-25|title=Dr Ashley Bloomfield helps his Centurions XV team win the parliamentary rugby match|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/sport/rugby/122247888/dr-ashley-bloomfield-helps-his-centurions-xv-team-win-the-parliamentary-rugby-match|access-date=2020-08-14|website=Stuff|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Porter|first=Joe|date=2020-07-24|title=Dr Bloomfield the 'Eliminator' to star alongside ex All Blacks|url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/sport/421902/dr-bloomfield-the-eliminator-to-star-alongside-ex-all-blacks|access-date=2020-08-14|website=RNZ|language=en-nz}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-07-25|title=Bloomfa!
