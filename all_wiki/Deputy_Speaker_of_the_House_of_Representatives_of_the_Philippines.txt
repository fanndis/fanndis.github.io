{{Politics of the Philippines}}
The '''Deputy Speakers of the House of Representatives of the Philippines''' are the second highest-ranking officials of the [[Philippine House of Representatives]]. During the absence of the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|House Speaker]], one of the House Deputy Speakers will preside over the [[Philippine House of Representatives|House of Representatives]].

Usually, the House just names six people as deputy speakers, but House Majority Leader [[Rodolfo Fariñas]] said in August 2016 that they will increase the numbers to cater to the supposed move to federalism.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.rappler.com/nation/141961-house-more-deputy-speakers-federalism |title=House to appoint more deputy speakers to prepare for federalism |publisher=[[Rappler]] |author=Mara Cepeda |date=August 4, 2016 |accessdate=September 27, 2016}}</ref>

{{As of|2020|10|15|df=US}}, there are 22 Deputy Speakers of the House of Representatives.<ref name="hordeputy">{{Cite web |url=http://www.congress.gov.ph/leaders/?l=deputy |title=HOUSE LEADERS INFORMATION - DEPUTY SPEAKERS |publisher=[[House of Representatives of the Philippines]] |accessdate=October 15, 2020}}</ref>

==Duties and powers==
The duties and powers of the House Deputy Speakers, as contained in the House Rules, are the following:<ref name="hordeputy"/>

*to assume the duties and powers of the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]] when so chosen by a majority vote or by lot among themselves, as the case may be, when the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]] is absent or temporarily incapacitated, until such time that the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]] returns to the performance of his/her duties; and, in case of resignation, removal, permanent incapacity or death of the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]], until such time that a new [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]] is elected and qualified;
*to preside over the session when, even if present, the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]] does not preside, or has not designated any other member as temporary presiding officer as provided in Section 14(h) of Rule IV;
*to monitor, coordinate and facilitate action on measures filed, requests, and other concerns of members representing constituencies in any of the island groups namely [[Luzon]], [[Visayas]] and [[Mindanao]], to which they may be assigned by the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]];
*to recommend to the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]] appropriate policies, strategies and programs of action to improve the pace and quality of legislation and to effectively address issues and concerns of members on matters affecting them, their constituencies, and the overall operations and integrity of the [[Philippine House of Representatives|House]];
*to appoint personnel of the [[Philippine House of Representatives|House]] when so authorized by the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]]; and
*to perform such other duties and functions as may be assigned and/or delegated by the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives|Speaker]].
