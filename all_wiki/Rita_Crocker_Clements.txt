'''Rita Crocker Clements''' (October 30, 1931 – January 6, 2018) was an American [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] organizer, an activist in historic preservation, and a First Lady of the [[U.S. state]] of [[Texas]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.statesman.com/news/state--regional/rita-clements-former-first-lady-texas-dies/MJYdAOzZYPsuGan2wEr6dP/|title=Rita Clements, former first lady of Texas, dies at 86|work=statesman|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref> 

==Early life==

She was born in [[Newton, Kansas|Newton]], [[Kansas]], on October 30, 1931, to rancher and political activist Mason Crocker and his wife Florabel.<ref name=":1" /> The family relocated to [[Brady, Texas|Brady]], [[Texas]], when she was 10 years old.<ref name=":1" /> She attended [[Hockaday School|The Hockaday School]] in Texas, graduating in 1949.<ref name=":1" /> She then studied at [[Wellesley College]], [[Massachusetts]], before completing her undergraduate degree in 1953 at the [[University of Texas at Austin]].<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":4" /> She graduated with honors in Spanish and also minored in history and government.<ref name=":4" /> Her 1952 marriage to [[Richard Bass]] produced four children. They divorced in 1974, and in 1975 she married [[Bill Clements]].<ref name="UT"/>

Beginning her political career as a volunteer for [[Presidency of Dwight D. Eisenhower|Dwight D. Eisenhower]]'s 1952 campaign for President of the United States,<ref name="UT">{{cite web|title=Rita Crocker Clements|url=http://www.utsystem.edu/board-of-regents/former-regents/rita-crocker-clements|publisher=University of Texas|accessdate=August 22, 2013}}</ref> by 1958, she had risen to [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] precinct chairman in [[Dallas County, Texas|Dallas County]].<ref name=":1" /> Her continued involvement in party politics helped elect [[John Tower]] to the [[United States Senate]] in 1961.<ref name=":1" /> She served as state co-chair of the 1964 committee for the [[Barry Goldwater presidential campaign, 1964|Barry Goldwater presidential campaign]].<ref name="TAMU">{{cite web|title=Rita Crocker Clements Personal Papers 1932–2001|url=http://libraryasp.tamu.edu/Cushing/collectn/modpol/rcc/bio.htm|publisher=Texas A & M University|accessdate=August 22, 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20121211095124/http://libraryasp.tamu.edu/Cushing/collectn/modpol/rcc/bio.htm|archivedate=December 11, 2012|df=mdy-all}}</ref> Clements was appointed to the [[Republican National Committee]] in 1973.<ref>{{cite journal|title=1991 Distinguished Alumni|journal=The Alcalde|date=Sep–Oct 1991|page=30|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_NYDAAAAMBAJ&q=%22rita+clements%22&pg=PA30}}</ref> From 1972 to 1975 she was a member of the National Advisory Council for Economic Opportunity.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.tshmf.org/mm-clements.html|title=M/M Clements|website=Texas State History Museum Foundation|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref> 

Serving as First Lady of Texas during the two non-consecutive terms (1979–1983 and 1987–1991) of her husband Governor Bill Clements,<ref>{{cite web|title=Governors and First Ladies of Texas |url=http://www.txfgm.org/governors_ladies.php |publisher=Governor's Mansion of Texas. |accessdate=August 22, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130911081857/http://www.txfgm.org/governors_ladies.php |archivedate=September 11, 2013 }}</ref> she was a key strategist in each of her husband's election campaigns.<ref name=":0" />

Her leadership of the Texas Main Street program, part of the Texas Historic Commission, put her in the forefront of preserving the state's history.<ref name=":0" />'' ''The program aimed to maintain local town centers through the preservation of landmarks and the revival of sustaining businesses.<ref name=":1" /> During 1979–1982, she also spearheaded the renovation of the [[Texas Governor's Mansion]].  She and the Governor founded the non-profit organization Friends of the Governor's Mansion to raise private money for the project, as well as to fund future maintenance of the mansion. The non-profit organization initially raised $3 million. The state legislature appropriated an additional $1 million for the restoration.<ref>{{cite web|last=Smyrl|first=Vivian Elizabeth|title=Governor's Mansion|url=https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/online/articles/ccg03|work=Handbook of Texas Online|publisher=Texas State Historical Association|accessdate=August 22, 2013}}{{cite journal|last=Sharpe|first=Patricia|title=Scaffold, Please|journal=Texas Monthly|date=January 1983|pages=61, 62, 64|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KS4EAAAAMBAJ&q=%22rita+clements%22&pg=PA64|accessdate=August 22, 2013}}{{cite web|title=Friends of the Governors Mansion|url=http://governor.state.tx.us/mansion/friends_ofthe_mansion/|publisher=State of Texas, Office of the Governor|accessdate=August 22, 2013}}</ref> During her husband's second term in office, Clements contributed to the campaign to have the 1888 Texas Capitol restored.<ref name=":1" />

==Later life==

In her post-First Lady of Texas life, she continued to be active in business and civic organizations on heritage preservation, education, volunteerism and advocacy of women's issues. She served on the boards of La Quinta Motor Inns, Team Bank, Bank One, Texas, [[Dr Pepper Snapple Group|Dr Pepper]], the [[Dallas Historical Society]] and the educational foundation The O'Donnell Foundation of Dallas, which she had co-founded in 1957.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.chron.com/news/politics/article/Former-Texas-First-Lady-Rita-Clements-86-dies-12479071.php|title=Former Texas First Lady Rita Clements, 86, dies in Dallas|work=Houston Chronicle|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":4" />  In 1996, she was appointed to the University of Texas Board of Regents by Governor [[George W. Bush]], and re-appointed by Governor [[Rick Perry]], serving until November 2007.<ref name="UT" /><ref name="TAMU" />

==Death==
Clements died in Dallas on January 6, 2018, from complications of [[Alzheimer's disease]] at the age of 86.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Ward, Mike|title=Former Texas First Lady Rita Clements, 86, dies in Dallas|url=https://www.chron.com/news/politics/article/Former-Texas-First-Lady-Rita-Clements-86-dies-12479071.php|publisher=Chron|date=7 January 2018}}</ref><ref name=":4">{{cite news|first1=Joe|last1=Simacher|first2=Claire|last2=Ballor|title=Rita Clements, civic leader, political trailblazer and Texas' former first lady, dies at 86|url=https://www.dallasnews.com/obituaries/obituaries/2018/01/06/rita-clements-civic-leader-political-trailblazer-texas-former-first-lady-dies-86|accessdate=January 6, 2018|work=Dallas News|date=January 6, 2018|language=en}}</ref> On news of her death, President [[George W. Bush]] and First Lady [[Laura Bush]] released a statement of condolence, acknowledging Clements' strength and service to the state of Texas.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nbcdfw.com/news/local/Rita-Crocker-Clements-Widow-of-Late-Texas-Governor-Bill-Clements-Dies-at-86-468225643.html|title=Widow of Late Texas Governor Bill Clements, Dies at 86|work=NBC 5 Dallas-Fort Worth|access-date=January 7, 2018|language=en}}</ref>

==Honors and awards ==
Clements received the Miss [[Ima Hogg]] Special Award from the Winedale Society and the Ruth B. Lester Award from the Texas Historical Commission for her work on heritage preservation.<ref name=":3" /> In 1991, she was named a Distinguished Alumna of the University of Texas.<ref name=":3" /> In 1996, Clements was elected to the [[Texas Women's Hall of Fame]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rita Crocker Clements|url=http://www.twu.edu/twhf/tw-clements.asp|work=Texas Women's Hall of Fame|publisher=Texas Woman's University|accessdate=August 22, 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130808034802/http://www.twu.edu/twhf/tw-clements.asp|archivedate=August 8, 2013|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

In 2007, the Texas State History Museum Foundation bestowed former Governor and Rita Clements with the History-Making Texan Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tshmf.org/about-the-history-making-texan-award.html|title=About the History-Making Texan Award|website=Texas State History Museum Foundation|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref> In 2009 the couple were recipients of the Santa Rita Award for their contributions to the University of Texas System.<ref name=":4" />
