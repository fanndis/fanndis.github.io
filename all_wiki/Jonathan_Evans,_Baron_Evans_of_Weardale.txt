'''Jonathan Douglas Evans, Baron Evans of Weardale''', [[Order of the Bath|KCB]], [[Deputy Lieutenant|DL]] (born 1958<ref>EVANS Jonathan, ''World Who's Who'' (Europa Biographical Reference)</ref>) was [[Director General of MI5|Director General]] of the British [[MI5|Security Service]], the United Kingdom's domestic security and [[counter-intelligence]] service.  He took over the role on the retirement of his predecessor [[Eliza Manningham-Buller|Dame Eliza Manningham-Buller]] on 21 April 2007. Evans was succeeded by [[Andrew Parker (MI5 officer)|Andrew Parker]] on 22 April 2013.

==Biography==
He was educated at [[Sevenoaks School]] and [[Bristol University]] where he read [[classical studies]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://irisonline.org.uk/index.php/interviews/interview-archive/20-an-interview-with-mi5s-jonathan-evans|title=Iris Online|author=Administrator|work=irisonline.org.uk|accessdate=16 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425081156/http://irisonline.org.uk/index.php/interviews/interview-archive/20-an-interview-with-mi5s-jonathan-evans|archive-date=25 April 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |title=The Defence of the Realm |first=Christopher |last=Andrew |page=[https://archive.org/details/defenceofrealmau0000andr/page/806 806] |publisher=Allen Lane |year=2009 |ISBN=978-0-7139-9885-6 |url=https://archive.org/details/defenceofrealmau0000andr/page/806 }}</ref> Evans joined the Security Service in 1980, and initially worked in counter espionage.<ref name="bbcbio">{{cite news |author=[[Gordon Corera]] |title= From the Cold War to al-Qaeda |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/6428477.stm |publisher=BBC News |date=7 March 2007 |accessdate=7 March 2007 }}</ref> In 1985 he moved to the protective security function, dealing with internal and personnel security, before switching to domestic [[counter-terrorism]] in the late 1980s.<ref name="bbcbio"/> For more than a decade he was involved with the effort to combat the domestic threat of groups such as the [[Provisional Irish Republican Army|Provisional IRA]] during [[The Troubles]]. In 1999, with the violence in Northern Ireland greatly reduced due to the [[Good Friday Agreement]], Evans moved to G-Branch,<ref name="times">{{cite news|title=New MI5 boss is top expert on Al-Qaeda|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/uk/article756272.ece|work=The Sunday Times|date=17 December 2006|accessdate=7 March 2007|location=London | first=David | last=Leppard}}</ref> the section of MI5 which deals with international terrorism.  There he became an expert on [[al-Qaeda]]<ref name=guardian>{{cite news|title=MI5 told MPs on eve of 7/7: no imminent terror threat|url=https://www.theguardian.com/attackonlondon/story/0,,1985972,00.html|work=The Guardian|date=9 January 2007|accessdate=7 March 2007 | location=London | first=Ian | last=Cobain}}</ref> and other branches of [[Islamic terrorism]].  He rose to head the section in 2001 (only a few days before the [[September 11, 2001 attacks]]), a position which put him on the service's board of management. In 2005, he became Deputy Director General before being promoted to head the organisation in 2007.<ref name="bbcnews">{{cite news|title= MI5 deputy to take over as head|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/6427993.stm|publisher=BBC News|date=7 March 2007|accessdate=7 March 2007}}</ref>

In November 2007, Evans talked publicly about the threat the UK faces from digital espionage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/africa/7123970.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Business - MI5 warns over China spy threat|work=bbc.co.uk|accessdate=16 September 2015}}</ref>  He spoke at RUSI on [[National Security]] in February 2008. He has a Certificate in Company Direction from the [[Institute of Directors]]. In July 2010, the government revealed Evans received an annual salary of £159,999.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/politics/10482482.stm|title=Quango chiefs' salaries revealed|date=2 July 2010|publisher=BBC News|accessdate=2 July 2010}}</ref> In September 2010, Evans said that [[Anwar al-Awlaki]] was the West's Public Enemy No 1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/terrorism-in-the-uk/8009819/Anwar-al-Awlaki-the-new-Osama-bin-Laden.html|author=Peter Johnston |title=Anwar al Awlaki: the new Osama bin Laden? |work=The Telegraph |date=17 September 2010 |accessdate=30 October 2010}}</ref> Al-Awlaki would be killed by a U.S. [[drone strike]] on 30 September 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Islamist cleric Anwar al-Awlaki 'killed in Yemen'|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-15121879|work=BBC News|accessdate=September 30, 2011|date=September 30, 2011}}</ref>

Evans was appointed [[Knight Commander of the Order of the Bath]] (KCB) in the [[2013 New Year Honours]] for services to defence.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=60367 |date=29 December 2012 |page=2 |supp=y}}</ref> Evans was succeeded by [[Andrew Parker (MI5 officer)|Andrew Parker]] on 22 April 2013.<ref name="homeoffice">{{cite news|title= Appointment of the new Director General of the Security Service|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/appointment-of-the-new-director-general-of-the-security-service|publisher=Home Office|date=28 March 2013|accessdate=24 April 2013}}</ref><ref name="MI5">{{cite news|title= Director General|url=https://www.mi5.gov.uk/home/about-us/who-we-are/staff-and-management/director-general.html|publisher=MI5|accessdate=24 April 2013}}</ref> After retiring as Director General, Evans joined the Board of [[HSBC Holdings]] as a Non-Executive Director.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-22737606 |title=BBC News - HSBC appoints ex-MI5 chief Sir Jonathan Evans to board |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=2013-05-31 |accessdate=2014-03-19}}</ref> He is also a non-executive director of Ark Data Centres,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.datacentres.com/dc-news/ark-data-centres-announces-appointment-jonathan-evans |title=Ark Data Centres announces appointment of Jonathan Evans |work=DataCentres.com |accessdate=16 September 2015 }}{{Dead link|date=February 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref name=st-20191229>{{cite news |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/boeing-sues-art-data-centres-where-former-mi5-boss-jonathan-evans-is-a-director-lcx635n8q |title=Boeing sues Ark Data Centres, where former MI5 boss is a director |last=Evans |first=Peter |newspaper=Sunday Times |location=London |url-access=subscription |date=29 December 2019 |access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref> a senior Associate Fellow at the Royal United Services Institute<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rusi.org/go.php?structureID=associates&ref=B51F12DB3180B4 |title=Sir Jonathan Evans KCB |publisher=RUSI |date=2001-09-01 |accessdate=2014-03-19 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141213093539/https://www.rusi.org/go.php?structureID=associates&ref=B51F12DB3180B4 |archive-date=2014-12-13 }}</ref> and an Honorary Professor at the University of St Andrews.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.st-andrews.ac.uk/~cstpv/staff/jde.html |title=CSTPV - Jonathan Evans |publisher=St-andrews.ac.uk |accessdate=2014-03-19}}</ref> From 2014 to 2015 he was a non-executive director of the National Crime Agency.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalcrimeagency.gov.uk/news/news-listings/563-resignation-of-non-executive-director|title=National Crime Agency - Resignation of non-executive director|work=nationalcrimeagency.gov.uk|accessdate=16 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150905200445/http://www.nationalcrimeagency.gov.uk/news/news-listings/563-resignation-of-non-executive-director|archive-date=5 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> He has written occasionally in the Sunday Times on classic cars.<ref>{{cite web|author=Richard Kerbaj and Nick Rufford |url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/news/uk_news/People/article1252002.ece |title=The one thing Q wouldn’t give me |publisher=The Sunday Times |date=2013-04-28 |accessdate=2014-03-19}}</ref>

On 21 October 2014, it was announced that he was to become a [[Crossbench]] life peer, having been nominated personally by the Prime Minister.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/peerages-conferred/ |title=Press Notice: Peerages conferred |date=21 October 2014 |website=www.gov.uk |publisher=Prime Minister's Office |accessdate=22 October 2014}}</ref> He was created '''Baron Evans of Weardale''', ''of [[Toys Hill]] in the [[County of Kent]]'', on 3 December 2014.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=61068 |date=8 December 2014 |page=23622}}</ref> In January 2015 he was appointed a Deputy Lieutenant of Kent.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=61120 |date=6 February 2015 |page=1094}}</ref> and on 1 November 2018 he was appointed Chair of the [[Committee on Standards in Public Life]] for a 5-year term.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/prime-minister-appoints-new-committee-chairs|title=Prime Minister appoints new committee chairs|date=25 October 2018|publisher=Cabinet Office|accessdate=13 November 2018}}</ref> In December 2018, following controversy about his paid business portfolio in light of his appointment to the Committee, it was announced that he would step down from his position as a Non-Executive Director with HSBC Holdings in early 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.sky.com/story/former-mi5-chief-lord-evans-to-step-down-from-hsbc-board-11594861|title=Former MI5 chief Lord Evans to step down from HSBC board|website=Sky News|language=en|access-date=2019-01-12}}</ref>
