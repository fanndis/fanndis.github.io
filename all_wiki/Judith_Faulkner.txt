'''Judith "Judy" R. Faulkner''' (born August 1943) is an American [[billionaire]], and the CEO and founder of [[Epic Systems]], a healthcare software company located in [[Wisconsin]].<ref name="Isthmus">{{cite news |last=Eisen |first=Marc |title=Epic Systems Corporation: An Epic timeline |work=Isthmus |date=June 20, 2008 |accessdate=2009-02-20 |url=http://www.thedailypage.com/isthmus/article.php?article=22984}}</ref> Faulkner founded Epic Systems in 1979, with the original name of Human Services Computing.<ref name="Zina">{{cite news |first=Zina |last=Moukheiber |title=Judy Faulkner: Health Care's Low-Key Billionaire |date=March 7, 2012 |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zinamoukheiber/2012/03/07/judy-faulkner-health-cares-low-key-billionaire/print/ |work=Forbes |accessdate=2013-12-12}}</ref> In 2013, ''[[Forbes]]'' called her "the most powerful woman in healthcare",<ref>{{cite news |last=Moukheiber |first=Zina |title=An Interview With The Most Powerful Woman In Health Care |work=Forbes |date=May 15, 2013 |accessdate=2013-12-06 |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zinamoukheiber/2013/05/15/a-chat-with-epic-systems-ceo-judy-faulkner/}}</ref> and on the magazine's 2020 list of the world's billionaires she was ranked #836 and, as one of the wealthiest self-made women, she had a net worth of [[United States dollar|US$]]5.5 billion.<ref name="Forbes profile" />

== Early life and education ==
Faulkner was born in August 1943<ref name="Wealth-X">{{cite web |url=http://www.wealthx.com/articles/2015/judith-r-faulkner/ |title=Billionaires: Judith R. FAULKNER |publisher=Wealth-X |accessdate=2017-01-03}}</ref><ref name=check-business>{{cite web |title=Judith R. Faulkner |url=https://www.check-business.co.uk/director/178163650001/Judith%20R.%20Faulkner |website=check-business |accessdate=5 September 2015 }}{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> to Louis and Del Greenfield.<ref name="Ferral, 2017">{{cite news |first1= Katelyn |last1= Ferral |first2= Erik |last2= Lorenzsonn |url= http://host.madison.com/ct/news/local/her-way-epic-systems-ceo-judy-faulkner-talks-about-trusting/article_7fafd560-d5fd-5a7f-8ef8-59d6bd4cf452.html |title= Her way: Epic Systems CEO Judy Faulkner talks about trusting her vision |publisher= The Capital Times|date= April 12, 2017}}</ref> Faulkner's parents inspired her early interest in health care; her father, Louis, was a pharmacist and her mother, Del, was the director of Oregon Physicians for Social Responsibility.<ref name="Ferral, 2017" /> She was raised in the [[Erlton, New Jersey|Erlton]] neighborhood of [[Cherry Hill, New Jersey]] and graduated from [[Moorestown Friends School]] in 1961.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.mfriends.org/camden-scholars-gifts/|title=Nearly $3 Million in Transformative Gifts Kickstart Campaign To Permanently Endow Camden Scholars Program - Moorestown Friends School|date=2016-03-28|work=Moorestown Friends School|access-date=2018-01-01|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first= Tim |last= Ronaldson |url= https://moorestownsun.com/an-honor-of-epic-proportions-53533c699e68 |title= An honor of 'Epic' proportions |newspaper= Moorestown Sun |date= May 6, 2011}}</ref> She received a bachelor's degree in mathematics from [[Dickinson College]] and a master's degree in computer science from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]].<ref name="Ferral, 2017" />

== Career ==
In 1979, shortly after receiving her master's degree, Faulkner co-founded Human Services Computing, with Dr. John Greist.<ref name="Ferral, 2017" />
Human Services Computing, which later became [[Epic Systems]], began in a basement at 2020 University Avenue in Madison, WI.<ref name="Ferral, 2017" /> The company was started with a $70,000 investment from friends and family, but has never taken investment from venture capital or private equity and remains a privately held company.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Moukheiber |first1=Zina |title=Epic Systems' Tough Billionaire |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zinamoukheiber/2012/04/18/epic-systems-tough-billionaire/#263a102858d9 |accessdate=17 February 2019 |work=Forbes |date=18 April 2012}}</ref> In fact, Faulkner prides herself in the fact that Epic is homegrown; they have never acquired another company<ref name="Ferral, 2017" /> and Faulkner has stated they will never go public.<ref>{{cite news |title=Epic CEO Judy Faulkner Say Would Never Consider Buyout Offer From Apple |url=https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/stocks/epic-ceo-judy-faulkner-say-would-never-consider-buyout-offer-from-apple-1027957794 |accessdate=17 February 2019 |agency=Business Insider |issue=15 February 2019}}</ref> Epic Systems now holds the medical records of over 200 million people.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.epic.com/about|title=About Us – Epic|author=|date=|website=www.epic.com|accessdate=26 February 2019}}</ref> Faulkner and her family currently own 43% of Epic Systems.<ref>{{cite news |last=Boulton |first=Guy |url=http://archive.jsonline.com/business/epic-systems-founder-judy-faulkner-plans-to-give-billions-to-charity-b99646453z1-366647741.html |title=Epic Systems founder Judy Faulkner plans to give billions to charity |work=Milwaukee Journal Sentinel |publisher=Gannett Co/Elizabeth Brenner |date=2016-01-26 |accessdate=2017-03-14 }}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Faulkner lives in [[Madison, Wisconsin]].<ref name="Forbes profile"/><ref name="Zina" /> She is married to Dr. Gordon Faulkner, a pediatrician.<ref name="modernhealthcare.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.modernhealthcare.com/article/20150310/NEWS/303109971|title=Faulkner funds foundation to keep Epic private|author=|date=|website=modernhealthcare.com|accessdate=16 March 2018}}</ref> They have three children.<ref name="Forbes profile"/><ref name="modernhealthcare.com"/> 

In 2016, Faulkner signed [[The Giving Pledge]], committing 99% of her assets to philanthropy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jsonline.com/business/epic-systems-founder-judy-faulkner-plans-to-give-billions-to-charity-b99646453z1-366647741.html|title=Epic Systems founder plans to give billions to charity|website=www.jsonline.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-20}}</ref>
