The '''''Los Angeles Sentinel''''' is a weekly [[African-American_businesses#Newspapers_and_magazines|African-American owned]] newspaper published in [[Los Angeles, California]]. The paper boasts of reaching 125,000 readers {{As of|2004|lc=on}}, making it one of the oldest, largest and most influential [[African-American newspapers]] in the [[U.S. West|Western United States]]. The ''Sentinel'' was also noted for their coverage of the changing [[African-American]] daily life experience in the post-1992 Los Angeles Riots era.<ref> Thornton, Michael C. “Meaningful Dialogue? The Los Angeles Sentinel's Depiction of Black and Asian American Relations, 1993-2000.” Journal of Black Studies, vol. 42, no. 8, 2011, pp. 1275–1298. JSTOR, JSTOR, www.jstor.org/stable/41304584.</ref>

The ''Sentinel'' was founded in 1933 by [[Leon H. Washington Jr.]] for black readers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blackpast.org/african-american-history/washington-jr-leon-h-1907-1974/|title=Leon H. Washington Jr. (1907-1974)|last=Hodder|first=Kevin|date=2011-12-18|website=The Black Past: Remembered and Reclaimed|language=en|access-date=2019-12-25}}</ref> Since that time, the newspaper has been considered a staple of black life in Los Angeles.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Carr|first=Elston|date=1993-06-13|title=CRENSHAW : Los Angeles Sentinel to Relocate Offices|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1993-06-13-ci-2743-story.html|access-date=2020-09-02|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref> The paper mainly focuses on and thus enjoys most of its [[newspaper circulation|circulation]] in the predominantly [[African-American neighborhood]]s of [[South Los Angeles]], [[Inglewood, California|Inglewood]] and [[Compton, California|Compton]]. The office is on [[Crenshaw Boulevard]] with commercial corridor in the [[Hyde Park, Los Angeles|Hyde Park neighborhood]] which is known as "the heart of African American commerce in Los Angeles".

On March 17, 2004, the ''Sentinel'' was purchased and came under the direction of [[real estate developer]] and [[activist|community activist]] [[Danny Bakewell]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Danny Bakewell takes over Sentinel|url=http://www.laobserved.com/archive/001598.html|date=2005-11-25|website=L.A. Observed|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20051125114359/http://www.laobserved.com/archive/001598.html|archive-date=2005-11-25|access-date=2020-05-31}}</ref> He planned to provide the diversity of thought that exists among African-Americans with opinion columns and editorials. He also wanted to cover business and reach young people.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Life & Times Transcripts: 05/17/04 - Includes interview with Danny Bakewell|url=http://www.kcet.org/lifeandtimes/archives/200405/20040517.php|last=Zavala|first=Val|date=2005-01-01|website=KCET Life and Times|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050101001328/http://www.kcet.org/lifeandtimes/archives/200405/20040517.php|archive-date=2005-01-01|access-date=2020-05-31}}</ref>
