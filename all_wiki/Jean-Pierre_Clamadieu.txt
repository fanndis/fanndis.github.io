'''Jean-Pierre Clamadieu<ref>[http://www.whoswho.fr/bio/jean-pierre-clamadieu_48760 Jean-Pierre Clamadieu: Who's Who in France]</ref>''' born August 15, 1958, is a French businessman.

Clamadieu has been the Chairman of [[Engie]] since May 2018<ref>David Keohane (March 8, 2018), [https://www.ft.com/content/ba63e404-22a6-11e8-9a70-08f715791301 Engie lifts dividend as energy group nears end of transformation] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref> and a former chief executive officer of Solvay and member of the board of directors from May 2012 to March 2019. He served chairman of the board and chief executive officer of [[Rhodia (company)|Rhodia]],<ref>[http://www.rhodia.com/en/news_center/news_releases/jean-pierre_clamadieu_appointment_180308.tcm Jean-Pierre Clamadieu as chairman & CEO of Rhodia]</ref> a French chemical company acquired and integrated into the Belgian group [[Solvay (company)|Solvay]] in September 2011.

Clamadieu is also member of the board of ''[[AXA]]'' (a French insurer) and ''[[Airbus]]''. Between 2014 and 2016, he served as president of the [[European Chemical Industry Council]] (''Cefic'').<ref>[http://www.cefic.org/About-us/How-Cefic-is-organised/Executive-Committee--Board/ CEFIC's Board and Executive Committee]</ref> From 2007 to February 2014, he was the chairman of the Sustainable Development Commission of [[Mouvement des Entreprises de France|MEDEF]] and from 2010 to 2015, chairman of the Franco-Brazilian Business Council of ''MEDEF International''.

== Early life and education ==
Clamadieu graduated from [[Mines ParisTech|Ecole Nationale Superieure des Mines de Paris]] before becoming chief engineer of the [[Corps of Mines (France)|Corps of Mines]] (class of 1983).

== Career ==
For nine years, Clamadieu worked mainly for the [[Ministry of the Economy and Finance (France)|French Ministry of Industry]] and as technical advisor to the [[Minister of Labour (France)|Minister of Labor]].

In 1993, Clamadieu joined [[Rhône-Poulenc]] to develop new activities in the field of automobile pollution control. In the following years, he held several executive positions in the [[Rhodia (company)|Rhodia]] group, as president of Rhodia's Chemicals in Latin America, president of Rhodia Eco Services, senior vice-president for Rhodia corporate purchasing, president of Rhodia Organic Fine Chemicals Division and president of Rhodia Pharmaceuticals & Agrochemicals Division.

In October 2003 Clamadieu was appointed CEO of [[Rhodia (company)|Rhodia]], and became its chairman and CEO in March 2008. In October 2003, while the group was on the edge of bankruptcy, he saved the company and transformed it into a modern and profitable group, well known for its transparency of financial information<ref>[http://www.rhodia.com/en/news_center/news_releases/Prix_financial_transparency_051011.tcm Rhodia awarded "Grand Prix for Financial Transparency"]</ref> and its responsible governance.<ref>[http://www.rhodia.com/en/news_center/news_releases/Award_responsable_governance_141011.tcm Rhodia wins "Responsible Governance Award"]</ref>

In April 2011, the Belgian chemical group [[Solvay (company)|Solvay]] started a tender offer on Rhodia<ref>[https://www.reuters.com/article/2011/04/04/solvay-idUSLDE66P0SY20110404 Solvay bids 3,4 billions € for Rhodia]</ref> and achieved it successfully the same year in September.

In May 2012, Clamadieu was appointed CEO of the new Solvay Group. Four months later, Solvay joined the [[CAC 40]] index replacing [[PSA Peugeot Citroën]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-19516327 Solvay replaced PSA Peugeot Citroën in CAC 40 index]</ref>

Since his appointment, Clamadieu led the integration of the new group and its transformation into a major player in the field of speciality chemicals and advanced materials, which combines industrial competitiveness with the quest for sustainable solutions for its clients. Clamadieu also promotes an ambitious a coordinated European energy policy. In May 2018, Clamadieu was appointed chairman of the board of the [[ENGIE]] group. He remained chairman and CEO of Solvay until February 2019.
