'''Michael Maurice Cashman, Baron Cashman''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%|CBE}} (born 17 December 1950) is a British politician and former actor. He was a [[Member of the European Parliament]] for the [[West Midlands (European Parliament constituency)|West Midlands]] [[Electoral district|constituency]] from 1999 until he stood down in [[2014 European Parliament election in the United Kingdom|2014]]. He has since then been appointed to the [[House of Lords]].<ref>{{Cite news|url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-28703150|title = Karren Brady and Sir Stuart Rose among new life peers|date = 8 August 2014|publisher = BBC}}</ref> On 23 September 2014, Cashman was appointed the Labour Party's special envoy on LGBT issues worldwide. He resigned from the Labour Party on 22 May 2019 in opposition to [[Brexit]], and offered his support to the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/black-country/michael-cashman-sensationally-quits-labour-16311445|title=Labour peer sensationally QUITS party - to vote for Lib Dems|last=Rodger|first=James|date=2019-05-22|website=birminghammail|access-date=2019-09-25}}</ref>

== Acting ==
A former child actor, Cashman had a long career principally on television in supporting roles. His first television appearance was in the 14th episode "The Tin God" of the [[ITC Entertainment|ITC]] series ''[[Gideon's Way]]'' filmed in 1964 and aired in 1965. He appeared with the [[National Youth Theatre]] in ''[[Zigger Zagger]]'' at the [[Novello Theatre|Strand Theatre]] in the [[West End of London|West End]] in March 1968. He had been in the business for more than 20 years when he landed the role of [[Colin Russell (EastEnders)|Colin Russell]] in [[British Broadcasting Corporation|BBC]] TV's ''[[EastEnders]]'', a character who was a participant in the first gay kiss in a British [[soap opera]]. In 1980, Cashman was a series regular in series 2 and 3 of [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]]'s ''[[The Sandbaggers]]'' as Sandbagger 2 (initially Sandbagger 3). Cashman also appeared in the ''[[Doctor Who]]'' serial ''[[Time-Flight]]'' in March 1982. It was announced on 25 August 2016 that Cashman would reprise his role as Colin Russell in ''EastEnders'' for two episodes in September.<ref>{{cite news|title=Lord Cashman to reprise EastEnders role after 30 years|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2016/aug/25/lord-cashman-eastenders-first-gay-kiss|accessdate=25 August 2016|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=24 August 2016}}</ref>

He is the treasurer of [[British Actors' Equity Association|Equity]] and led the campaign to obtain pension rights for members.{{citation needed|date=October 2018}}

== Politics ==
Cashman is a founder of [[Stonewall (UK)|Stonewall]],<ref>{{cite news|last1=Godfrey|first1=Chris|title=Section 28 protesters 30 years on|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/mar/27/section-28-protesters-30-years-on-we-were-arrested-and-put-in-a-cell-up-by-big-ben|accessdate=31 March 2018|newspaper=The Guardian|date=27 March 2018}}</ref> an Honorary Associate of the [[National Secular Society]], a Patron of [[The Food Chain]], a London-based HIV charity and a patron of [[LGBT Labour]].

In the [[European Parliament]] he was Labour spokesman on human rights.

A member of the [[European Parliament Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs|Civil Liberties Committee]], Cashman has been a [[rapporteur]] on several key initiatives including an initiative on funerals of those who die in third countries, an issue he was motivated to act on after the death of his own father.{{citation needed|date=October 2018}}

He is a trenchant critic of discrimination against minorities within the European Union. He has been leading a cross-party coalition to tackle the rise in homophobia throughout Europe. He has in the past supported the [[Gay rights in Poland|gay pride march]] in [[Warsaw]], which he attended.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/5068318.stm | work=BBC News | title=Fears of Poland's gay community | date=10 June 2006 | accessdate=12 May 2010}}</ref> He is also the President of the European Parliament's Intergroup on gay and lesbian issues. Cashman is a member of the [[ACP–EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly]]. In 2014, he criticised [[McDonald's]], [[Coca-Cola]] and [[Visa Inc.]] during a session of parliament in protest at their sponsorship of the [[2014 Winter Olympics]] taking place in [[Sochi]], Russia, despite [[Vladimir Putin]] having recently introduced legislation against the [[LGBT]] community. Cashman cut up his Visa card during the session.<ref>{{cite web | url= http://www.ibtimes.com/european-parliament-member-michael-cashman-cuts-his-visa-card-protest-sochi-video-1554069 | title=European Parliament Member Michael Cashman Cuts His Visa Card To Protest Sochi| work=International Business Times | date=7 February 2014 | accessdate=10 February 2014}}</ref>

In 2007 he was awarded an [[honorary doctorate]] from the [[University of Staffordshire]] for his human rights work.<ref>{{cite web|title=University Disclosure Log: The names of those people given honorary degrees by the University in the academic years of 2007/08, 2008/09 and 2009/10.|url=http://www.staffs.ac.uk/legal/foi/disclosurelog/?year=2010&month=10|website=Staffordshire University}}</ref> That same year he was elected MEP of the Year for Justice and Fundamental Rights by his peers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ilga-europe.org/europe/news/michael_cashman_receives_mep_award |title=ILGA-Europe |accessdate=25 August 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090108115441/http://www.ilga-europe.org/europe/news/michael_cashman_receives_mep_award |archive-date=8 January 2009 |df=dmy }}</ref>

Cashman was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) in the 2013 New Year Honours for public and political service.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=60367 |date=29 December 2012 |page=7 |supp=y}}</ref>

It was reported on 8 August 2014 Cashman was to be among 22 new life peers announced by the government.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-28703150|title=Karren Brady and Sir Stuart Rose among new life peers|work=BBC News|accessdate=25 August 2016}}</ref> On 23 September 2014 he was created a [[life peer]] taking the title '''Baron Cashman''', of [[Limehouse]] in the [[London Borough of Tower Hamlets]],<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=61001 |date=29 September 2014 |page=18838}}</ref> which is also his birthplace.

In June 2015, Cashman announced his involvement and investment in SuitLink Ltd., a global LGBT and ally professional social network.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://twitter.com/mcashmancbe/status/610733556806098945
|title=Michael Cashman's Twitter Update
|publisher=Twitter}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.suitlink.com/
|title=SuitLink – LGBT and Ally Professional Social Network}}</ref>

In 2007, Cashman supported [[Alan Johnson]] for the deputy leadership of the Labour Party. In 2010, he supported [[David Miliband]] for the leadership of the party. In 2015, Cashman supported [[Yvette Cooper]] for the leadership and gave his second preference to [[Liz Kendall]].{{citation needed|date=October 2018}}

Cashman released his memoir 'One of Them: From Albert Square to Parliament Square' with [[Bloomsbury Publishing]] in February 2020.<ref>https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/one-of-them-9781526612328/</ref>

== Personal life ==
Cashman registered a [[Civil partnerships in the United Kingdom|civil partnership]] with Paul Cottingham, his partner of over twenty years, on 11 March 2006. They met in 1983 and were together for 31 years until Cottingham died. A fellow actor and humanitarian activist, Cottingham who also worked for the Labour Party as High Value fundraiser and for Britain in Europe, became Cashman's researcher during Cashman's time in the European Parliament. In March 2011 he was diagnosed with a very rare cancer, [[angiosarcoma]], and died on 23 October 2014 at the [[Royal Marsden Hospital]], London. He was cremated in a humanist service at the City of London Cemetery on 7 November 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gaystarnews.com/article/paul-cottingham-love-michael-cashmans-life-dies241014|title=Paul Cottingham, 'the love of Michael Cashman's life', dies - Gay Star News|date=24 October 2014|accessdate=25 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pinknews.co.uk/2014/10/24/ed-miliband-pays-tribute-to-paul-cottingham-activist-and-partner-of-michael-cashman|title=Ed Miliband pays tribute to Paul Cottingham, activist and partner of Michael Cashman|accessdate=25 August 2016|date=24 October 2014}}</ref>
