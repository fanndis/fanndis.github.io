'''Yulia Antonovna Putintseva''' ({{lang-ru|Ю́лия Анто́новна Пути́нцева}}; {{IPA-ru|pʊˈtʲintsɨvə}}, ''puh-TEEN-tseh-vah'';<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tph7EBDTTCA|title=Юлия Путинцева WTA St Petersburg Ladies Trophy 2017 4.02 - YouTube|date=2020-02-04}}</ref> born 7 January 1995) is a [[Kazakhstan|Kazakh]] [[tennis]] player of Russian origin and descent. She is a three-time Grand Slam quarterfinalist (twice at the [[French Open]] and once at the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]]), and she achieved her career-high singles ranking of world No. 27 in February 2017. So far, she has won one WTA singles title.<ref name="WTA profile">{{WTA|id=317683|name=Yulia Putintseva}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Yulia Putintseva was born to Anton Putintsev and Anna Putintseva, and has a brother named Ilya. Born in [[Moscow]], she currently resides in Boca Raton, Florida. She was introduced to sport by her father. She liked it and was soon practicing at Spartak Club in Moscow. Later, she moved to Paris to attend Mouratouglou Academy after winning a big Under 14s event. Her favourite surface is clay, while her favourite tournaments are Australian Open and US Open. Tennis idols were Martina Hingis and Justine Henin. She also enjoys dancing, music, singing, Sudoku, playing cards and chess. As of the start of June 2012, she represents [[Kazakhstan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/players/317683/yulia-putintseva/bio|title=Yulia Putintseva's Bio|website=WTA}}</ref>

==Tennis career==
===Junior years===
In 2009, she played the second round of [[2009 US Open - Girls' Singles|US Open in girls' singles]], and the first round of the [[2009 US Open - Girls' Doubles|girls' doubles event]], partnering [[Tamara Čurović]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/us-open-junior-tennis-championships/usa/2009/j-ga-usa-01a-2009/draws-and-results/|title=2009 US Open Girl's Singles & Doubles}}</ref> Putintseva also won three junior tournaments: the 17th International junior tournament Città di Prato 2009, the 31st International junior tournament Città di Santa Croce, both in Italy, and the 15th ITF Junior Open in Austria.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/yulia-putintseva/800302719/kaz/jt/s/titles/#pprofile-info-tabs|title=2009 ITF Junior Titles}}</ref>

In 2010, she lost in the second round of the [[2010 Australian Open - Girls' Singles|girls' singles event]] at the [[2010 Australian Open|Australian Open]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/australian-open-junior-championships/aus/2010/j-ga-aus-01a-2010/draws-and-results/|title=2010 Australian Open Girl's Singles}}</ref> Putintseva advanced to the semifinal at [[2010 Wimbledon Championships – Girls' Singles|Wimbledon]] and represented [[Russia at the 2010 Summer Youth Olympics|Russia]] in the [[2010 Summer Youth Olympics|Youth Olympic Games]] in August, where she lost in the semifinal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/the-junior-championships-wimbledon/gbr/2010/j-ga-gbr-01a-2010/draws-and-results/|title=2010 Wimbledon Girl's Singles}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/youth-olympic-games-(yog)/sgp/2010/j-ga-sgp-01a-2010/draws-and-results/|title=2010 Youth Olympic Games Girl's Singles}}</ref> In the last junior Grand Slam event of this season, the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], she reached the final but lost to [[Daria Gavrilova]] in straight sets.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/us-open-junior-tennis-championships/usa/2010/j-ga-usa-01a-2010/draws-and-results/|title=2010 US Open Girl's Singles}}</ref>

===2012–2015===
In 2012, she won another ITF tournament in Australia, and also received a wildcard to the [[2012 e-Boks Open|e-Boks Open]] where she won her first-round match, but then lost to former world No. 1, [[Jelena Janković]], in straight sets. In May, Putintseva won as a qualifier the [[2012 Open GDF SUEZ de Cagnes-sur-Mer Alpes-Maritimes|Open de Cagnes-sur-Mer]], a $100k event in France. As a result, she jumped to world No. 122.

At the [[2013 Australian Open]], Putintseva defeated [[Christina McHale]] in three sets. She then lost to [[Carla Suárez Navarro]] in three tight sets. At the [[2013 French Open|French Open]], she stomped the world No. 44 [[Ayumi Morita]]. Her next opponent was the 2012 French Open finalist [[Sara Errani]] who defeated her in straight sets.

At the [[2014 PTT Pattaya Open]], Putintseva was defeated in the first round by qualifier [[Alexandra Dulgheru]] in three sets. Putintseva reached the quarterfinals of the [[Swedish Open]] losing to [[Jana Čepelová]], and of the [[Japan Women's Open]] losing to Samantha Stosur.

She reached the second round at the [[2015 French Open]] and [[2015 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] losing to eventual quarterfinalist [[Elina Svitolina]] and to [[Venus Williams]], respectively.

===2016: First Grand Slam quarterfinal===
[[File:Putintseva US16 (15) (29827576296).jpg|thumb|Putintseva at the [[2016 US Open (tennis)|2016 US Open]]]]
At the [[2016 Australian Open|Australian Open]], she upset former world No. 1 [[Caroline Wozniacki]] in the first round, winning in three sets.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/5281895/title/putintseva-rallies-past-wozniacki|title=Putintseva Rallies Past Wozniacki|publisher=[[Women's Tennis Association]] (WTA)|website=www.wtatennis.com|date=18 January 2016|accessdate= 18 January 2016}}</ref> She lost in the third round to [[Margarita Gasparyan]]. Putintseva reached semifinals in [[2016 Taiwan Open|Kaohsiung]], where she lost to the eventual winner Venus Williams. At her next tournament, the [[WTA Qatar Open|Qatar Open]], Putintseva earned a straight-sets win over [[Anna Karolína Schmiedlová]], but lost to [[Timea Bacsinszky]] in the following match. At [[Indian Wells Masters|Indian Wells]], Putintseva defeated [[Peng Shuai]] and earned another upset in 2016 by defeating world No. 27, [[Kristina Mladenovic]] in straight sets. She then lost to world No. 1, [[Serena Williams]].

At the [[2016 French Open|French Open]], Putinseva beat [[Aleksandra Wozniak]] in the first round in straight sets, before going on to beat the No. 28 seed [[Andrea Petkovic]] and [[Karin Knapp]] en route to the fourth round, where she upset world No. 14, [[Carla Suárez Navarro]], before losing to eventual finalist Serena Williams in three sets in her first Grand Slam quarterfinal.

Prior to Wimbledon, she played at the inaugural [[Mallorca Open]] and the [[Eastbourne International]], losing both in the first round. At the third Grand Slam event of the year, Putintseva was defeated in round two by the hard hitting [[Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova]] in straight sets. The following week, she was chosen as part of the Kazakhstan Olympic Tennis team for the [[2016 Summer Olympics|Rio Summer Olympics]].

To start off the US Open Series tournaments, Putintseva started with a semifinal run at the [[2016 Citi Open – Women's Singles|Citi Open]]. Seeded sixth, she lost in the semifinals to eventual champion [[Yanina Wickmayer]]. She lost to Pavlyuchenkova in the first round of the [[2016 Rogers Cup – Women's Singles|Rogers Open]]. She was scheduled to compete at the [[2016 Brasil Tennis Cup|Brasil Tennis Cup]], but withdrew because of injury. This would also cause her to miss the [[Tennis at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Women's Singles|Olympics]].

After returning from injury, Putintseva lost in the first round of the [[2016 Western & Southern Open – Women's Singles|Western & Southern Open]] to qualifier [[Annika Beck]]. As a result of her injury before the Olympics, Putintseva was forced to play in the qualifying tournament before the [[2016 Connecticut Open – Singles|Connecticut Open]]. Seeded No. 1 in qualifying, she was stunned in the first round by [[Carina Witthöft]]. Putintseva next competed at the [[2016 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]]. She had an easy first-round win over [[Sabine Lisicki]]. However, for the second week in a row, she was beaten by Witthöft, in three sets. She had early-round losses in many tournaments after the US Open, and lost in the second round of both the [[2016 Toray Pan Pacific Open – Singles|Toray Pan Open]] and the [[2016 China Open – Women's Singles|China Open]]. She finished the season with another second-round loss to Elina Svitolina at the [[2016 Kremlin Cup – Women's Singles|Kremlin Cup]].

===2017: First WTA final===
[[File:Putintseva WM17 (8) (36183418645).jpg|thumb|Putintseva at the [[2017 Wimbledon Championships]]]]
Putintseva started the 2017 season at the [[2017 Brisbane International – Women's Singles|Brisbane International]]. She lost in the first round to third seed and eventual champion, Karolína Plíšková.<ref>{{cite news |title=Pliskova breezes into Round 2 |url=https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/archive/sports/pliskova-breezes-into-round-2-344901 |accessdate=20 April 2020 |date=2 January 2017}}</ref> In Sydney at the [[2017 Apia International Sydney – Women's Singles|Apia International Sydney]], Putintseva was defeated in the second round by tenth seed Caroline Wozniacki.<ref>{{cite news |title=Johanna Konta into Sydney quarters as world No1 Angelique Kerber crashes out |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2017/jan/10/johanna-konta-sydney-angelique-kerber-tennis |accessdate=20 April 2020 |date=10 January 2017}}</ref> At the [[2017 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], Putintseva was the 31st seed; it was the first time she was seeded at a Grand Slam tournament. She lost in the second round to Jeļena Ostapenko.<ref>{{cite news |title=Third Round Awaits After 2 Years for Former No. 1 Caroline Wozniacki |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/18/sports/karolina-pliskova-australian-open.html |accessdate=20 April 2020 |publisher=The New York Times |date=18 January 2017}}</ref>

She then competed at the [[2017 St. Petersburg Ladies' Trophy – Singles|St. Petersburg Open]], beating [[Johanna Larsson]] (who retired in the second set) and Annika Beck before beating No. 3 and No. 2 seeds, [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] and [[Dominika Cibulková]], en route to her first WTA Tour final. Her win over Cibulková was her first over a top-5 player.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2017/02/dominika-cibulkova-surprised-yulia-putintseva-st-petersburg/63956/|title=Dominika Cibulkova surprised by Yulia Putintseva in St. Petersburg|newspaper=Tennis.com|access-date=2017-02-05}}</ref> But she then lost to Kristina Mladenovic in three sets. However, her campaign in St. Petersburg ensured a career-high ranking of world No. 27.
