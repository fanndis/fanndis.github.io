'''Melissa Victoria Harris-Perry''' (born October 2, 1973), formerly known as '''Melissa Victoria Harris-Lacewell''', is an American writer, professor, television host, and political commentator with a focus on [[African-American]] politics. Harris-Perry hosted the ''[[Melissa Harris-Perry (TV program)|Melissa Harris-Perry]]'' weekend news and opinion television show on [[MSNBC]] from 2012 to February 27, 2016.

==Early life==
Harris-Perry was born to a white mother and black father.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Harris|first1=Aisha|title=When White Parents Adopt Black Children|url=http://www.slate.com/blogs/browbeat/2014/01/24/closure_documentary_and_melissa_harris_perry_add_a_personal_voice_to_transracial.html|accessdate=August 29, 2015|work=browbeat|date=January 24, 2014}}</ref> She was born in [[Seattle]] and grew up in [[Chesterfield County, Virginia]], one of the counties adjoining the independent city of [[Richmond, Virginia]], where she attended [[Thomas Dale High School]]. Her father was the first dean of African-American Affairs at the [[University of Virginia]].<ref name="RTD2011">{{cite news | url = http://www2.timesdispatch.com/news/2011/feb/06/tdmet02-chesterfield-native-now-msnbc-commentator--ar-823398/ | title = Chesterfield native, now MSNBC commentator, speaking at VCU | work = [[Richmond Times-Dispatch]] | first = Michael Paul | last = Williams | date = February 6, 2011 | accessdate = June 19, 2011 | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://archive.is/20130204210259/http://www2.timesdispatch.com/news/2011/feb/06/tdmet02-chesterfield-native-now-msnbc-commentator--ar-823398/ | archivedate = February 4, 2013 | df = mdy-all }}</ref> Harris-Perry's mother, Diana Gray, taught at a community college and was working on her doctorate when they met. She worked for non-profit organizations that provided services such as [[day-care]] centers, health care for people in rural communities, and access to [[Reproductive health|reproductive care]] for poor women.<ref>{{cite news|last=Pope|first=John|title=New Orleans transplant has a life rich in politics, pedagogy|url=http://www.nola.com/politics/index.ssf/2011/10/new_orleans_transplant_has_a_l.html|newspaper=[[The Times-Picayune]]|date=October 2, 2011|accessdate=September 7, 2015}}</ref>

Harris-Perry graduated from [[Wake Forest University]] with a [[bachelor's degree]] in English and earned a PhD in [[political science]] from [[Duke University]]. She received an [[honorary degree|honorary doctorate]] from [[Meadville Lombard Theological School]], and is studying toward a [[Master of Divinity]] in theology at [[Union Theological Seminary (Manhattan)|Union Theological Seminary]] of [[Columbia University]].<ref name="About Harris-Perry">{{cite web |url=http://www.melissaharrisperry.com/about.html |title=About Melissa Harris-Perry |website=MelissaHarrisPerry.com |year=2011 |accessdate=April 8, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101023090345/http://melissaharrisperry.com/about.html |archivedate=October 23, 2010 |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref name="U.S. 1">{{cite news | url = http://princetoninfo.com/index.php?option=com_us1more&Itemid=6&key=10-10-2007_c_01 | title = From House to Home | work = [[U.S. 1 (newspaper)|U.S. 1 Newspaper]] | first = Anne | last = Levin | date = October 10, 2007 | accessdate = June 19, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
Harris-Perry joined the [[political science]] faculty of the [[University of Chicago]] in 1999 and taught there for seven years, until 2006, when she accepted a tenured appointment at [[Princeton University]] as an Associate Professor of [[Political Science]] and [[African-American Studies]]. Harris-Perry left Princeton in 2011 after being denied a full professorship<ref>{{cite web|last1=Glickel|first1=Jen|title=Uncommon Interview – Melissa Harris-Lacewell |url=http://chicagomaroon.com/2005/02/12/uncommon-interview-melissa-harris-lacewell/|work=[[The Chicago Maroon]]|date=February 12, 2005|accessdate=February 7, 2015}}</ref><ref name="Leaving Princeton">{{cite news | url = http://www.nj.com/mercer/index.ssf/2012/02/princeton_center_for_african_a.html | title = Princeton Center for African American Studies loses two high-profile figures, but gains renewed sense of purpose | work = [[The Times of Trenton]] | first = Wendy | last = Plump | date = February 12, 2012 | accessdate = October 8, 2012}}</ref> for [[Tulane University]], where she was Founding Director of the Anna Julia Cooper Project, a center for the study of race, gender, and politics in the South.

On July 1, 2014, Harris-Perry returned to [[Wake Forest University|Wake Forest]] as the [[Maya Angelou]] Presidential Chair Professor of Politics and International Affairs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.wfu.edu/2014/04/11/melissa-harris-perry-to-join-faculty/ |title=Melissa Harris-Perry to join faculty |author=Neal, Katie |date=April 11, 2014 |website=WFU.edu |publisher=[[Wake Forest University]] News Center |accessdate=September 7, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://politics.wfu.edu/faculty-and-staff/melissa-harris-perry/|title=Melissa Harris-Perry – Politics and International Affairs|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref> The Anna Julia Cooper Project is now resident at Wake Forest.

She is a regular columnist for the magazine ''[[The Nation]]'', the co-host of the magazine’s podcast System Check<ref name="System Check">{{cite web |url=https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/system-check/id1536830138 |title=System Check |work=[[The Nation]] |accessdate=October 26, 2020}}</ref> with Dorian Warren, and the author of two books (one published under the name Melissa Victoria Harris-Lacewell).

=== MSNBC television series ===
{{main|Melissa Harris-Perry (TV series)}}
On February 18, 2012 Harris-Perry began hosting an [[MSNBC]] weekend morning show [[eponymous]]ly titled ''Melissa Harris-Perry''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mediaite.com/tv/melissa-harris-perry-to-host-msnbc-weekend-show-starting-in-february/ |title=Melissa Harris-Perry To Host MSNBC Weekend Show Starting in February|work= [[Mediaite]]|accessdate=January 5, 2012|author= Christopher, Tommy |date= January 5, 2012 }}</ref>

In early 2013, Harris-Perry was criticized by some political commentators for statements she made on her program related to collective parenting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/04/11/melissa-harris-perry-and-the-firestorm-over-collective-parenting.html|title=Melissa Harris-Perry and the Firestorm Over 'Collective' Parenting|last=Freedlander|first=David|date=April 11, 2013|work=[[The Daily Beast]]|accessdate=December 10, 2013}}</ref> On December 31, 2013, she apologized for a "photos of the year" segment on December 28, 2013, that included jokes about a family picture featuring former Republican presidential candidate [[Mitt Romney]]'s family, including his [[transracial adoption|adopted black]] grandson.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Elections/Vox-News/2013/1231/Melissa-Harris-Perry-apologizes-for-Romney-grandchild-jokes-Sincere/ |title=Melissa Harris-Perry Apologizes for Romney Grandchild Jokes|work= [[The Christian Science Monitor]]|accessdate=December 31, 2013|author= Grier, Peter |date= December 31, 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Brett |last=LoGiurato |url=http://www.sfgate.com/technology/businessinsider/article/MSNBC-Host-Apologizes-To-Romney-Family-After-5104455.php |title=Here's Melissa Harris-Perry's Tearful Apology for the Controversial Segment on the Romneys' Black Grandchild |work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |date=January 2, 2014 |accessdate=January 6, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Harris-Perry, Melissa |url=http://www.msnbc.com/melissa-harris-perry/apology-melissa-harris-perry |title=An apology from Melissa Harris-Perry |publisher=MSNBC |date=December 31, 2013 |accessdate=January 6, 2014}}</ref>

==== Departure ====
On February 26, 2016, Harris-Perry sent an email to co-workers that she would not host her show on MSNBC for the coming weekend, stating: "Our show was taken—without comment or discussion or notice—in the midst of an election season [...] I will not be used as a tool for [management's] purposes [...] I am not a token, mammy, or little brown bobble head." Her show was scheduled to air as usual on Saturday, but Harris-Perry chose not to return, saying: "I am only willing to return when that return happens under certain terms."<ref name=":0">{{Cite news| url = https://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/27/business/media/melissa-harris-perry-walks-off-her-msnbc-show-after-pre-emptions.html| title = Melissa Harris-Perry Walks Off Her MSNBC Show After Pre-emptions| last = Koblin| first = John| date =February 26, 2016| newspaper = The New York Times| issn = 0362-4331| access-date =February 28, 2016}}</ref> She said she would only return when she could do "substantive, meaningful and autonomous work." NBC responded that "many of our daytime programs have been temporarily upended by breaking political coverage, including M.H.P."<ref name=":0"/> The public dispute led to discussions between the network and her representatives about ending her relationship with MSNBC.<ref>{{Cite news| url = https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/msnbc-will-cut-ties-with-show-host-who-wrote-critical-email-to-colleagues/2016/02/27/bce30c8e-dd82-11e5-891a-4ed04f4213e8_story.html| title = MSNBC to sever ties with Melissa Harris-Perry after host's critical email| last = Farhi| first = Paul| date =February 27, 2016| newspaper = The Washington Post| language = en-US| issn = 0190-8286| access-date =February 28, 2016}}</ref> On February 28, 2016, the network confirmed that Harris-Perry was leaving the network.<ref>{{Cite news| url = https://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/29/business/media/melissa-harris-perry-is-out-at-msnbc-cable-network-confirms.html| title = Melissa Harris-Perry Is Out at MSNBC, Network Confirms| last = Koblin| first = John| date =February 28, 2016| newspaper = The New York Times| issn = 0362-4331| access-date =February 29, 2016}}</ref>

=== ELLE.com editor-at-large ===
On April 18, 2016, it was announced that Harris-Perry joined [[Elle (magazine)|Elle.com]] as [[editor-at-large]]. In the role, Harris-Perry is stated to focus on areas of race, gender, politics, and fashion, "telling the often-overlooked stories of women and girls of color".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Chernikoff|first1=Leah|title=Melissa Harris-Perry Joins ELLE.com as Editor-at-Large|url=http://www.elle.com/culture/career-politics/news/a35760/melissa-harris-perry-joins-ellecom-as-editor-at-large/|website=Elle|date=April 18, 2016|accessdate=April 19, 2016}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
In April 2015, the ''[[Winston-Salem Journal]]'' reported that the [[IRS]] had placed a [[tax lien]] on the property of Harris-Perry and her husband for about $70,000 in delinquent taxes. Harris-Perry said she and her husband paid $21,721 on April 15, 2015, and have a payment plan with the IRS.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.journalnow.com/news/local/irs-files-k-tax-lien-against-harris-perry-husband/article_7e39a096-e3c9-11e4-9c3a-2feeea1356be.html |title=IRS files $70K tax lien against Harris-Perry, husband |work=[[Winston-Salem Journal]] |author=Hewlett, Michael| date=April 15, 2015 |accessdate=September 7, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.politico.com/blogs/media/2015/04/msnbcs-melissa-harris-perry-owes-70-000-in-delinquent-taxes-irs-says-205646|title=MSNBC's Melissa Harris-Perry owes $70,000 in delinquent taxes, IRS says|newspaper=POLITICO|access-date=October 31, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/erik-wemple/wp/2015/04/22/msnbc-fails-to-address-tax-problems-of-hosts/|title=MSNBC fails to address tax problems of hosts|last=Wemple|first=Erik|date=April 22, 2015|newspaper=The Washington Post|language=en-US|issn=0190-8286|access-date=October 31, 2016}}</ref>
