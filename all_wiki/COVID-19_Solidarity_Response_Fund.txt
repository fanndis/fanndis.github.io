'''COVID-19 Solidarity Response Fund''' is a global fund for supporting the work of the [[World Health Organization]] (WHO) in containing the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name=WHO>{{cite news |title=Coronavirus update: new WHO fund, Guterres calls for 'prudence, not panic' |url=https://news.un.org/en/story/2020/03/1059421 |accessdate=16 March 2020 |work=UN News |publisher=UNO |date=13 March 2020 |language=en}}</ref> It was launched on March 13, 2020 by the Director-General of WHO in Geneva, Switzerland.<ref name=WHO/> The purpose of the response fund is to "support WHOâ€™s work to track and understand the spread of the virus; to ensure patients get the care they need and frontline workers get essential supplies and information; and to accelerate efforts to develop vaccines, tests, and treatments."<ref>{{cite web |title=WHO, UN Foundation and partners launch first-of-its-kind COVID-19 Solidarity Response Fund |url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/13-03-2020-who-un-foundation-and-partners-launch-first-of-its-kind-covid-19-solidarity-response-fund |website=www.who.int |publisher=World Health Organization |accessdate=16 March 2020 |language=en}}</ref> Major companies, including [[Facebook]], [[H&M]] and [[Google]] have donated to the Solidarity Response Fund, in addition to several private individuals.<ref name=WHO/><ref>{{cite news |last1=Turk |first1=Robyn |title=H&M Foundation donates to COVID-19 Solidarity Response Fund |url=https://fashionunited.uk/news/fashion/h-m-foundation-donates-to-covid-19-solidarity-response-fund/2020031648002 |accessdate=16 March 2020 |work=fashionunited.uk |date=16 March 2020 |language=en-gb}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Together we can raise $7.5 million to help stop COVID-19 |url=https://www.google.org/crisis/coronavirus-relief/ |website=Google.org |accessdate=16 March 2020 |language=en |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200316193353/https://www.google.org/crisis/coronavirus-relief/ |archive-date=16 March 2020 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Facebook Commits $20 Million in Matching Funds for COVID-19 Response |url=https://philanthropynewsdigest.org/news/facebook-commits-20-million-in-matching-funds-for-covid-19-response |website=Philanthropy News Digest (PND) |accessdate=16 March 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

[[UNICEF]] joined the Solidarity Response Fund on April 3, 2020 to work together with [[World Health Organization|WHO]] on [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] response.<ref>{{Cite web|title=WHO and UNICEF to partner on pandemic response through COVID-19 Solidarity Response Fund|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/03-04-2020-who-and-unicef-to-partner-on-pandemic-response-through-covid-19-solidarity-response-fund|website=www.who.int|language=en|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref>

According to WHO's estimations, the requirement to respond to [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] until the end of 2020 is US$1.7 billion. As of May 15, 2020, 36.2% (US$624.5 m) of required amount has been collected.<ref>{{Cite web|title=COVID-19 contributions tracker|url=https://www.who.int/emergencies/diseases/novel-coronavirus-2019/donors-and-partners/funding|website=www.who.int|language=en|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref>
