'''INSEAD''' is a graduate [[business school]] with locations in Europe ([[France]]), Asia ([[Singapore]]), the Middle East ([[Abu Dhabi]]), and North America ([[San Francisco]]). The name "INSEAD" originated as an acronym of '''''Ins'''titut '''E'''uropéen d'<nowiki/>'''Ad'''ministration des Affaires'' ({{Langnf||French|"European Institute of Business Administration"}}).

INSEAD offers a full-time MBA program, an [[Master of business administration#Programmes|Executive MBA]] (EMBA) program, a [[Master of Finance|Master in Finance]] program, a [[PhD in management]] program, a [[Master of Management|Master in Management]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessbecause.com/news/masters-in-management/6120/insead-master-in-management|title=What's Different About INSEAD's Master In Management?|date=May 30, 2019|website=businessbecause.com}}</ref> program and a variety of [[executive education]] programs.

==History==
[[File:Chateau Fontainebleau.jpg|thumb|right|Château de Fontainebleau]]
INSEAD was founded in 1957 by venture capitalist [[Georges Doriot]] (the "father of venture capitalism")<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/theymadeamerica/whomade/doriot_hi.html|title=Who Made America? &#124; Innovators &#124; Georges Doriot|website=pbs.org}}</ref> along with Claude Janssen and [[Olivier Giscard d'Estaing]]. Original seed money was provided by the [[Paris Chamber of Commerce]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://knowledge.insead.edu/entrepreneurship-innovation/insead-at-50-the-defining-years-1356|title=INSEAD at 50: The defining years|publisher=INSEAD|accessdate=19 May 2014}}</ref> The school was originally based in the [[Palace of Fontainebleau|Château de Fontainebleau]], before moving to its current Europe campus in 1967.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://about.insead.edu/who_we_are/history.cfm|title=Our History|publisher=INSEAD|accessdate=19 May 2014}}</ref>

==Campuses==

The original campus (Europe campus) is located in [[Fontainebleau]], near [[Paris]], [[France]]. INSEAD's second campus (Asia campus) is in the [[one-north]] district of the city-state of [[Singapore]] next to [[one-north MRT station]]. The third and newest campus (Middle East campus) is located in [[Abu Dhabi]]. Although located in [[Europe]] and [[Asia]], INSEAD pursues the US model of a [[business school]].<ref>{{Cite journal | title=Andreas Kaplan, 2018, A school is "a building that has four walls…with tomorrow inside": Toward the reinvention of the business school, Business Horizons|journal = Business Horizons|volume = 61|issue = 4|pages = 599–608|doi = 10.1016/j.bushor.2018.03.010|year = 2018|last1 = Kaplan|first1 = Andreas}}</ref> INSEAD has been a pioneer in setting up a multi-campus business school as a way to increase the global presence and nature of its faculty and curriculum.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.iedp.com/magazine/2014issue17/index.html#6 | title=A Force for Good - A conversation with INSEAD Dean Ilian Mihov | publisher=Developing Leaders, Issue 17 - Autumn/Fall 2014 | accessdate=23 January 2015 | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150123125848/http://www.iedp.com/magazine/2014issue17/index.html#6 | archive-date=23 January 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.between-us.com/1097/insead.htm|title= INSEAD - Leading Business School in Europe, Asia and Abu Dhabi|publisher= Between-Us|accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref> A [[Harvard Business School]] case study, for instance, explores its approach to business education in a global context and how it functions with a multi-campus setting.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=35546 |title=INSEAD |first1=Srikant M. |last1=Datar |author-link1=Srikant Datar |first2=David A. |last2=Garvin |first3=Carin-Isabel |last3=Knoop |date=May 2009 |website=[[Harvard Business School]] Case 308-009}}</ref>

==Degree programmes==

===Business Foundations Certificate Programme===

INSEAD in collaboration with Sorbonne Université offers the Business Foundations Certificate (BFC) programme tailored for recent master or PhD graduates from the sciences, medicine, humanities, engineering and law. The programme equips participants with a broad set of business and economics skills that optimally complement their graduate training. It also supports participants' transition to a business career and helps aspiring entrepreneurs to fulfil the goal of their own start-up by complementing their ideas with a solid foundation in business and leadership [https://www.insead.edu/business-foundations-certificate-programme].

===MBA programme===

INSEAD's MBA participants can take the MBA's core courses at either  or both of its Europe and Asia campuses<ref>{{cite web| url=http://mba.insead.edu/the-insead-mba/campus-exchange.cfm|title=MBA Programme - Campus Exchange |website= insead.edu/|accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref> (as well as an MBA period at its Middle East campus). They follow the same core courses in parallel regardless of   campus, and there are faculty who teach on both the Europe and Asia campuses as well as permanent faculty at each of the three campuses who live and work in the respective regions.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://mba.insead.edu/the-insead-mba/campus-exchange.cfm|title= MBA Programme - Campus Exchange |website=mba.insead.edu/ |accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref> Approximately 20% of the class entered the MBA program with other graduate or professional degrees; including medical doctors, lawyers, and Ph.Ds.

INSEAD offers two MBA schedules per year: one starting in September which takes ten months to complete, and a 12-month promotion starting in January for students who want  to complete a summer internship.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/report-on-business/careers/business-education/is-a-summer-internship-necessary-in-a-one-year-mba/article4201494/|title=Is a summer internship necessary in a one-year MBA?|publisher=The Globe and Mail|accessdate=26 January 2015}}</ref>

The INSEAD MBA curriculum comprises  required core courses and electives. The core   covers traditional [[management]] disciplines including [[finance]], [[economics]], [[organizational behavior]], [[accounting]], [[ethics]], [[marketing]], [[statistics]], [[operations management]], international political environment, public policy, [[supply chain management]], [[leadership]] and [[strategic management|corporate strategy]]. There are 75 electives on offer<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mba.insead.edu/the-insead-mba|title=MBA Programme - Ten reasons to choose the INSEAD MBA |website= mba.insead.edu/|accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref> in areas such as accounting and control, decision sciences, economics and [[political science]], [[entrepreneurship]] and [[family business|family enterprise]], finance, and organisational behavior, strategy, marketing, technology and [[operations management]]. Students are required  to speak two languages upon entry and a third by graduation.

===Executive MBA programme===

INSEAD has two EMBA [[Master of Business Administration#Programmes|Executive MBA]] programmes. The Global Executive MBA (GEMBA),<ref>{{cite web| url=http://global.emba.insead.edu/|title= Welcome to INSEAD's Executive MBA programme|website= global.emba.insead.edu/|accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref> and the Tsinghua INSEAD EMBA (TIEMBA).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tsinghua.insead.edu/|title=Tsinghua-INSEAD EMBA Programme (TIEMBA) - INSEAD|date=19 April 2017|website=insead.edu}}</ref> Both EMBA programmes are master's-level degree programmes that take place on a part-time, modular basis.

The programmes offer experienced business executives an intensive 14–17-month modular course that takes place in modular periods (approximately every six to seven weeks). Each period on campus is between one and two weeks' duration. For the GEMBA programme the physical time on campus represents 12 weeks in total with participants going to all three campuses (Fontainebleau, France, Abu Dhabi, and Singapore). For the TIEMBA programme the physical time on campus represents 12 weeks in total with participants alternating between Tsinghua's campus in Beijing, China,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tsinghua.edu.cn/|title=清华大学|website=tsinghua.edu.cn}}</ref> and INSEAD's campus in Singapore.

Both the GEMBA and TIEMBA programmes include a schedule of group coaching, 360-degree assessments and team activities designed to develop a leadership style, called the Leadership Development Programme.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.topmba.com/institution/insead/insead-executive-mba|title= TOP MBA - INSEAD Executive MBA|publisher= Quacquarelli Symonds|accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref>

===Executive Master in Coaching and Consulting for Change===

The Executive Master in Coaching and Consulting for Change is a specialized master's degree. It provides a grounding in basic drivers of human behavior and the hidden dynamics of organizations. Integrating business education with a range of psychological disciplines, the programme enables participants to understand themselves and others at a fundamental level, which prepares them to assume  roles in organisations, individual and organizational development and change management.<ref>{{cite web|title=The INSEAD Executive Master in Consulting and Coaching for Change Programme|url=http://tune.pk/video/2322582/the-insead-executive-master-in-consulting-and-coaching-for-change-programme/|accessdate=26 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402131459/http://tune.pk/video/2322582/the-insead-executive-master-in-consulting-and-coaching-for-change-programme/|archive-date=2 April 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Executive Master in Consulting and Coaching for Change - Creating Reflective Change Agents|url=http://specialised-degrees.insead.edu/executive-master-in-consulting-and-coaching-for-change/|accessdate=26 January 2015}}</ref>

===Master in Finance===

INSEAD's Master in Finance (MFin) teaches participants finance and accounting skills on a par with those taught in an MBA programme, and also offers leadership and management perspectives. The program   is offered in a modular format over a 20-month period to allow professionals to study while continuing to work. Participants take time off from work for each of the six modules (2–3 weeks each) to take classes on campus, and continue working in between.<ref>{{cite web|title=The INSEAD MFin - Preparing Financial Leaders|url=http://specialised-degrees.insead.edu/master-in-finance/|accessdate=26 January 2015}}</ref>

===PhD programme===

The INSEAD PhD in Management is a doctoral degree in business to prepare students for a career in academia. It requires four to five years of full-time study - the first two years devoted to coursework, while from the third and fourth (or sometimes fifth) years  dedicated to research and dissertation.<ref>{{cite web|title=INSEAD PhD in Management Overview|url=http://phd.insead.edu/programme/|accessdate=31 March 2014}}</ref> Students have the option to start their studies on either the Asia (Singapore) or Europe (France) campus,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://phd.insead.edu/programme/ |title=INSEAD PhD in Management Programme Overview |publisher=INSEAD |accessdate=2014-01-31}}</ref> and do an exchange in North America (USA) through the INSEAD-Wharton Alliance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://phd.insead.edu/programme/wharton_alliance/ | title=INSEAD-Wharton Alliance}}</ref> There are eight areas of specialization: Accounting, Decision Sciences, Entrepreneurship, Finance, Marketing, Organisational Behavior, Strategy, and Technology and Operations Management.<ref>{{cite web|title=INSEAD PhD in Management Areas of Specialisation|url=http://phd.insead.edu/programme/areas_of_specialisation/|accessdate=31 March 2014}}</ref> INSEAD offers fellowships, whereby students receive full tuition fee waiver, annual stipend and research support funding.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://phd.insead.edu/financing/ | title=INSEAD PhD in Management Fellowship and Financing}}</ref>

=== Master in Management (MIM) ===
This 14 to 16-month programme has an innovative learning approach with an applied problem-solving orientation. It aims to empower the next generation of well-rounded, agile-thinking and innovative individuals who are ready to make a positive impact in today's society.

==Executive education==

INSEAD holds both company/firm specific and open enrolment executive education programmes at its locations in Europe, Asia, the Middle East and North America, as well as in partnership with corporate universities. Participants usually come from senior or top management, with many years of experience within their company or industry and younger 'high-potentials' identified as being key in succession strategies within their companies. Approximately 12,000 executives from over 125 countries undertake courses or programmes at INSEAD each year.<ref>{{cite web|title=INSEAD at a Glance |url=http://insead.edu/media_relations/rightnav/documents/insead-at-a-glance-october2013.pdf |publisher=INSEAD |accessdate=19 November 2013}}</ref>

In 2011, INSEAD launched an ''Executive Certificate in Global Management'' which is a formal recognition awarded to participants who complete at least three INSEAD global management and leadership programmes within a four-year period.<ref>{{cite web|title=INSEAD at a Glance |url=http://executive-education.insead.edu/executive_certificate_global_management/ |publisher=INSEAD |date=October 2013 |accessdate=23 January 2015}}</ref>

== Teaching methods ==
Teaching methods at INSEAD include [[case method|case studies]], lectures, peer-to-peer learning, tutorials, group work, [[Business simulation|simulations]] and role-plays.

===Case studies===

The [[case method]] is largely used in the classroom as a teaching method. Business case studies authored by INSEAD professors are the second most widely used in classrooms by [[business school]]s globally, after Harvard Business School's case studies. Together with Harvard Business School and ICFAI Business School Hyderabad, INSEAD makes up the 'big three' in case publishing globally.<ref>{{Cite web|last=BLoC|first=Team|date=2020-04-27|title=ICFAI Business School among world's most successful case publishers|url=https://bloncampus.thehindubusinessline.com/b-school-corner/icfai-business-school-among-worlds-most-successful-case-publishers/article31447853.ece|access-date=2020-06-11|website=@businessline|language=en}}</ref>

The business cases that have been designed at INSEAD have received many awards,<ref name="2008 European Case Awards">{{cite web | url=https://www.insead.edu/media_relations/press_release/2008_european-case-awards.cfm | title=News - INSEAD | date=6 February 2015 | website=insead.edu}}</ref>{{Better source|date=June 2020}} are made available in case clearing houses, and are used by many other business schools.

===Innovation in education===
A number of researches and initiatives are conducted at INSEAD to incorporate innovative learning approaches.

Centers conduct research in learning technologies and approaches such as:
* INSEAD CALT (the Centre of Advanced Learning Technologies)<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20010720161827/http://www.calt.insead.edu/ INSEAD CALT] (Centre for Advanced Learning Technologies)</ref> has been involved in many research projects, and in particular projects funded by the research programmes from the [[European Commission]] on approaches such as [[business simulation]]s, or [[learning communities]].
* INSEAD Learning Innovation Centre<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.insead.edu/facultyresearch/centres/learning_innovation_centre/ |title=INSEAD Learning Innovation Centre |publisher=INSEAD |accessdate=2012-06-25}}</ref> was funded to managing innovation in INSEAD programme design and delivery. For instance, INSEAD Learning Innovation Centre has introduced the use of the ''[[Second Life]]'' virtual world as an education tool.<ref>{{Cite news | last=Murray| first=Sarah | date=October 27, 2008 | title=Technology: Networking widens EMBA net | newspaper=[[Financial Times]] | url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/23b7b568-a15e-11dd-82fd-000077b07658.html }}</ref>
* INSEAD eLab<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.insead.edu/facultyresearch/centres/elab/ |title=INSEAD elab |publisher=INSEAD |accessdate=2013-11-18}}</ref> is the INSEAD center that connects research sponsors and external collaborators interested in the area of big data and data analytics with INSEAD's expertise in this broad area. INSEAD eLab conducts research and supports teaching focused on data analytics business solutions, tools, frameworks, and research insights that can help academics and practitioners better capitalise on the vast opportunities the "world of data" creates. To achieve this, the centre involves INSEAD faculty with specific domain expertise (e.g. in marketing, economics, operations, or finance), senior researchers from different fields, engineers and data scientists, and collaborators from other academic institutions as well as the private sector. The centre also uses state-of-the-art data analytics and collaborative software development platforms, which enable both advanced data analytics approaches and seamless collaboration within INSEAD as well as with other institutions and sponsors.
