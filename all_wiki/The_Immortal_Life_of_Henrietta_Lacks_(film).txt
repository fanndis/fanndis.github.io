'''''The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks''''' is a [[drama]] [[television film]] directed by [[George C. Wolfe]] and starring [[Oprah Winfrey]] and [[Rose Byrne]]. It is based on the book [[The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks|of the same name]] by [[Rebecca Skloot]] and documents the story of [[Henrietta Lacks]], who was diagnosed with [[cervical cancer]] in the 1950s, and whose cancer cells (later known as [[HeLa]]) would change the course of cancer treatment. The film premiered on [[HBO]] on April 22, 2017.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Agard|first1=Chancellor|title=Oprah Winfrey's Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks gets HBO premiere date|url=https://ew.com/tv/2017/02/14/oprah-winfreys-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks-gets-hbo-premiere-date/|date=February 14, 2017|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|publisher=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|accessdate=June 14, 2018}}</ref>

== Production ==
The film was announced on May 2, 2016 with [[Alan Ball (screenwriter)|Alan Ball]] and [[Oprah Winfrey]] as executive producers. [[George C. Wolfe]] would direct the film, with Winfrey taking the role of Lacks' daughter Deborah.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/05/oprah-winfrey-star-in-the-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks-hbo-films-1201747653/|title=Oprah Winfrey To Star In HBO Films’ ‘The Immortal Life Of Henrietta Lacks’|last=Andreeva|first=Nellie|date=May 2, 2016|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|publisher=[[Penske Business Media]]|access-date=September 29, 2016}}</ref> In July, [[Rose Byrne]] was cast as [[Rebecca Skloot]], the author of the book about Henrietta, who befriends Deborah while reporting on her mother's life,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/07/rose-byrne-cast-oprah-winfrey-hbo-films-the-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks-1201787103/|title=Rose Byrne Joins Oprah Winfrey In HBO Films’ ‘The Immortal Life Of Henrietta Lacks’|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=July 15, 2016|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|publisher=[[Penske Business Media]]|access-date=September 29, 2016}}</ref> and [[Renée Elise Goldsberry]] was cast as the titular Henrietta Lacks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/07/renee-elise-goldsberry-cast-hbo-films-the-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks-oprah-winfrey-1201792421/|title=Renée Elise Goldsberry Cast In HBO Films’ ‘The Immortal Life Of Henrietta Lacks’|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=July 25, 2016|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|publisher=[[Penske Business Media]]|access-date=September 29, 2016}}</ref> The core supporting cast was rounded out in August 2016, with [[Courtney B. Vance]], [[Ruben Santiago-Hudson]], [[Reg E. Cathey]] and [[Leslie Uggams]] amongst them.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/08/courtney-b-vance-leslie-uggams-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks-oprah-winfrey-hbo-1201801925/|title=Courtney B. Vance, Leslie Uggams, 7 Others Join Oprah Winfrey In HBO’s ‘Immortal Life Of Henrietta Lacks’|last=Pedersen|first=Erik|date=August 11, 2016|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|publisher=[[Penske Business Media]]|access-date=September 29, 2016}}</ref> After several weeks of shooting in [[Atlanta]], ''The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks'' began filming in [[Baltimore]] in September, 2016.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Mayhugh|first1=Jess|title=Oprah's "The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks" Films in Baltimore This Week|url=http://www.baltimoremagazine.net/2016/9/21/oprah-winfreys-the-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks-films-in-baltimore-this-week#|date=September 21, 2016|work=[[Baltimore Magazine]]|publisher=[[Rosebud Entertainment]]|accessdate=September 29, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Britto|first1=Brittany|title=Oprah Winfrey spotted in Baltimore as 'Henrietta Lacks' movie films in city|url=http://www.baltimoresun.com/features/baltimore-insider-blog/bal-oprah-winfrey-sightings-in-baltimore-20160921-story.html|date=September 21, 2016|work=[[The Baltimore Sun]]|publisher=Baltimore Sun Media Group|accessdate=September 29, 2016}}</ref> Two of [[Henrietta Lacks]]'s sons, Zakariyya and Sonny, were consultants on the film.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://scienceandfilm.org/articles/2888/lydia-pilcher-on-the-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks|title=Sloan Science & Film|website=Sloan Science & Film|access-date=April 14, 2017|first=Sonia|last=Shechet Epstein|publisher=[[Museum of the Moving Image]]}}</ref>

==Reception==
===Critical reception===
''The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks'' received mixed to positive reviews from critics, with Winfrey's performance gaining high praise. The [[review aggregator]] website [[Rotten Tomatoes]] gave the film an approval rating of 65%, based on 31 reviews, with an average rating of 6.5/10.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_immortal_life_of_henrietta_lacks |title=The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks (2017) |publisher=[[Fandango Media]]|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |accessdate=June 14, 2018}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]] the film has a score of 64 out of 100, based on 22 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<ref name=Metacritic>{{cite web |title=The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks |publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|work=[[Metacritic]] |accessdate=April 27, 2017 |url=https://www.metacritic.com/tv/the-immortal-life-of-henrietta-lacks }}</ref>

===Accolades===
The film was nominated for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Television Movie]] at the [[69th Primetime Emmy Awards]] held September 17, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vox.com/culture/2017/7/13/15964344/emmy-nominees-2017-livestream-list-emmys|title=Emmy nominations 2017: the full list|date=July 13, 2017|work=[[Vox (website)|Vox]]|accessdate=July 14, 2017|first=Caroline|last=Framke|publisher=[[Vox Media]]}}</ref>
