'''Cecilia Elena Rouse''' (born December 18, 1963) is an American [[economist]] and dean of the [[Princeton School of Public and International Affairs]] at [[Princeton University]].<ref name="longtimefocus">{{Cite news|last1=Tankersley|first1=Jim|last2=Smialek|first2=Jeanna|date=2020-12-02|title=Biden's New Top Economist Has a Longtime Focus on Workers|language=en-US|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/01/business/cecilia-rouse-biden-economist.html|access-date=2020-12-02|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> [[Joe Biden]] nominated Rouse to be chair of the [[Council of Economic Advisers]] in November 2020.<ref name="BidenSite1">{{cite web |title=Economy Nominees and Appointees |url=https://buildbackbetter.gov/nominees-and-appointees/economy/ |website=President-Elect Joe Biden |access-date=30 November 2020 |date=30 November 2020}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Cecilia grew up in [[Del Mar, California]] and graduated from [[Torrey Pines High School]] in 1981.<ref>{{Cite news|last=[[Associated Press]]|date=2020-12-01|title=Del Mar native nominated for post in Biden administration|url=https://www.delmartimes.net/news/story/2020-12-01/del-mar-native-nominated-for-post-in-biden-administration|access-date=2020-12-04|work=[[The San Diego Union-Tribune|Del Mar Times]]|language=en-US}}</ref> She has two siblings: Forest Rouse, a physicist; and [[Carolyn Rouse]], an anthropologist and professor at Princeton University. Her father is a research physicist who received his PhD from the [[California Institute of Technology]] in 1956. Her mother worked as a school psychologist.

Rouse received a Bachelor of Arts in economics from [[Harvard University]] in 1986 and a PhD in economics in 1992.<ref name="longtimefocus"/><ref>{{Cite web|title=Cecilia Rouse|url=https://www.bsg.ox.ac.uk/people/cecilia-rouse|access-date=2020-12-02|publisher=[[Blavatnik School of Government]]|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
After earning her doctorate, Rouse joined the faculty at [[Princeton University]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}}

Rouse served as a member of President [[Barack Obama]]'s [[Council of Economic Advisers]] from 2009 to 2011.<ref name="obamacea">{{Cite web|title=Former Members of the Council|url=https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/administration/eop/cea/about/Former-Members|access-date=2020-12-02|publisher=[[Presidency of Barack Obama|Obama White House]]|language=en}}</ref> Prior to serving in the Obama Administration, Rouse served in the [[United States National Economic Council|National Economic Council]] under President [[Bill Clinton]] from 1998 to 1999.<ref name="obamacea"/>

Rouse is the dean of the Princeton School of Public and International Affairs and the Lawrence and Shirley Katzman and Lewis and Anna Ernst Professor in the Economics of Education.{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}} She is the founding director of the Princeton University Education Research Section, is a member of the [[National Academy of Education]] and a research associate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}} Her primary research interests are in [[Labour economics|labor economics]]<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Davidson|first1=Kate|last2=Thomas|first2=Ken|date=2020-11-30|title=Joe Biden Fills Out His Economic Team|language=en-US|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/biden-to-name-rouse-tanden-to-economic-team-11606684256|access-date=2020-12-02|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> with a focus on the economics of education.{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}} Rouse has served as an editor of the ''[[Journal of Labor Economics]]'' and as a senior editor of ''[[The Future of Children]]''. {{Citation needed|date=December 2020}}She is a member of the board of directors of [[MDRC]], and a director of the [[T. Rowe Price]] Equity Mutual Funds and an advisory board member of the T. Rowe Price Fixed Income Mutual Funds.{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}}

Rouse signed a 2018 ''amici curiae'' brief that expressed support for Harvard University in the ''[[Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard]]'' lawsuit. Other signatories included [[Alan Krueger|Alan B. Krueger]], [[Robert Solow|Robert M. Solow]], [[George Akerlof|George A. Akerlof]], [[Janet Yellen]], as well as numerous others.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Exhibit A |url=https://admissionscase.harvard.edu/files/adm-case/files/economists_amended_brief_dkt._527-1.pdf|website=admissionscase.harvard.edu|access-date=2018-12-30}}</ref>

President-elect [[Joe Biden]] intends to nominate Rouse to become Chair of the [[Council of Economic Advisers]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Thomas|first=Ken|date=2020-11-29|title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} Biden to Name Rouse, Tanden to Economic Team|language=en-US|work=Wall Street Journal|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/biden-to-name-rouse-tanden-to-economic-team-11606684256|access-date=2020-11-29|issn=0099-9660}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
She has two daughters. Her husband is Ford Morrison, son of author [[Toni Morrison]].<ref>{{cite web|date=2016-01-21|title=Cecilia Rouse named Wilson School dean|url=https://paw.princeton.edu/article/cecilia-rouse-named-wilson-school-dean}}</ref>
