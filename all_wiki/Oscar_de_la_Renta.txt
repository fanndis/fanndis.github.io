'''Óscar Arístides Renta Fiallo''' (22 July 1932 – 20 October 2014), known professionally as '''Oscar de la Renta''', was a [[People of the Dominican Republic|Dominican]] fashion designer. Born in [[Santo Domingo]], he was trained by [[Cristóbal Balenciaga]] and [[Antonio Castillo (costume designer)|Antonio del Castillo]]. De la Renta became internationally known in the 1960s as one of the [[couturier]]s who dressed [[Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis|Jacqueline Kennedy]]. He worked for [[Lanvin (clothing)|Lanvin]] and [[Balmain (fashion house)|Balmain]]. His [[eponym]]ous fashion house has boutiques around the world including in [[Harrods]] of London and Madison Avenue in New York.

==Early life==
De la Renta, the youngest of seven children and the only boy in his family,<ref name="nyt10-20" /> was born in [[Santo Domingo]], [[Dominican Republic]], to a Dominican mother, Carmen María Antonia Fiallo, and a Puerto Rican father,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elmundo.es/america/2014/10/21/5445c23d268e3eb7138b4574.html|title=Muere el diseñador de moda Oscar de la Renta|website=elmundo.es|accessdate=1 May 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171013120305/http://www.elmundo.es/america/2014/10/21/5445c23d268e3eb7138b4574.html|archive-date=13 October 2017}}</ref> Óscar Avelino De La Renta, owner of an [[insurance]] company. The Fiallos, De la Renta's mother's family, were so embedded in Dominican society that they could count poets, scholars, and businessmen, as well as top army brass among their members.<ref>{{cite book |last=Mower |first=Sarah |title=Oscar: The Style, Inspiration and Life of Oscar De La Renta |year=2002 |publisher=Assouline |location=New York |page=11}}</ref> Their origin in the island can be traced back to the foundation of [[San Carlos de Tenerife]] in 1685 by [[Isleños|Canarian]] settlers.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Espinal Hernández |first1=Edwin Rafael |title=Oscar de la Renta: entorno genealógico |url=http://www.idg.org.do/capsulas/noviembre2014/noviembre201401.htm |website=Instituto Dominicano de Genealogía |publisher=Hoy |accessdate=17 November 2014 |location=Santo Domingo |language=es |date=1 November 2013 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129021125/http://www.idg.org.do/capsulas/noviembre2014/noviembre201401.htm |archive-date=29 November 2014 }}</ref>

A maternal uncle, [[Luis Arístides Fiallo Cabral]], was a doctor, lawyer, and architect, who received every degree the [[Universidad Autónoma de Santo Domingo|University of Santo Domingo]] could offer.<ref name="mower12">{{cite book |last=Mower |first=Sarah |title=Oscar: The Style, Inspiration and Life of Oscar De La Renta |year=2002 |publisher=Assouline |location=New York |page=12}}</ref> Another maternal uncle, [[Fabio Fiallo]], was a diplomat and poet.<ref name="mower12" /> On his father's side, De la Renta's great-great grandfather [[José Ortíz de la Renta]] was the first mayor of [[Ponce, Puerto Rico]], elected by popular vote and who had the distinction of serving as mayor eight times, the most ever for the city.<ref name="mower2002">{{cite book|title=Oscar: The Style, Inspiration and Life of Oscar De La Renta|last=Mower|first=Sarah|publisher=Assouline|year=2002|location=New York}}</ref>

De la Renta was raised [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] in a protective family.<ref name="nymag02-10" /><ref name="nymag2005" /> His mother died from complications of [[multiple sclerosis]] when he was 18.<ref name="nymag02-10">{{cite magazine |url=http://nymag.com/thecut/2013/02/oscar-de-la-renta-william-norwich-interview.html |title=Oscar de la Renta on Taking in Galliano, the Women He's Known and Loved, and Why He Once Tried to Hit Cecil Beaton |magazine=New York |date=10 February 2013 |first=William |last=Norwich |accessdate=21 October 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023145657/http://nymag.com/thecut/2013/02/oscar-de-la-renta-william-norwich-interview.html |archive-date=23 October 2014 }}</ref>

At the age of 18, he went to study painting in Spain at the [[Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando|Royal Academy of San Fernando]] in [[Madrid]].<ref name="nyt10-20" /><ref name="nymag02-10" /><ref name="cnn10-20">{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2014/10/20/living/oscar-de-la-renta-death/index.html?hpt=hp_t1|title=Oscar de la Renta, legendary fashion designer, dies at 82|last=Ahmed|first=Saeed|date=20 October 2014|work=CNN|accessdate=21 October 2014|last2=Ford|first2=Dana|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141021032432/http://www.cnn.com/2014/10/20/living/oscar-de-la-renta-death/index.html?hpt=hp_t1|archive-date=21 October 2014}}</ref> For extra money, he drew clothes for newspapers and fashion houses.<ref name="nyt10-20" /> After [[Francesca Braggiotti|Francesca Lodge]], the wife of [[John Davis Lodge]], the [[United States Ambassador to Spain|U.S. Ambassador to Spain]], saw some of his dress sketches, she commissioned de la Renta to design a gown for her daughter.<ref name="nyt10-20" /> The dress appeared on the cover of ''[[Life (magazine)|Life]]'' magazine that fall.<ref name="cnn10-20" /> He quickly became interested in the world of fashion design and began sketching for leading Spanish fashion houses, which soon led to an apprenticeship with Spain's most renowned [[Haute couture|couturier]], [[Cristóbal Balenciaga]]. He considered Cristóbal Balenciaga his mentor.<ref name="Oscar de la Renta Sits for Q&A at the Design Leadership Summit">{{cite web |url=http://www.wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-scoops/make-it-a-melba-7266429?src=nl/mornReport/20131107 |title=Oscar de la Renta Sits for Q&A at the Design Leadership Summit |publisher=WWD |date=7 November 2013 |accessdate=7 November 2013 |author=Feitelberg, Rosemary |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141104171826/http://www.wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-scoops/make-it-a-melba-7266429?src=nl%2FmornReport%2F20131107 |archive-date=4 November 2014 }}</ref> In 1961, de la Renta left Spain to join [[Antonio Castillo (costume designer)|Antonio del Castillo]] as a couture assistant at [[Lanvin (clothing)|Lanvin]] in [[Paris]].<ref name="Óscar de la Renta">{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/voguepedia/Oscar_de_la_Renta |title=Óscar de la Renta |accessdate=10 July 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120615054251/http://www.vogue.com/voguepedia/Oscar_de_la_Renta |archive-date=15 June 2012 }}</ref>

==Career==
In 1963, de la Renta turned to [[Diana Vreeland]], the editor-in-chief of ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'', for advice, saying that what he really wanted was to "get into ready to wear, because that's where the money is".<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1963">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=3& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1963 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=3& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> Vreeland replied, "Then go to [[Elizabeth Arden|Arden]] because you will make your reputation faster. She is not a designer, so she will promote you. At the other place, you will always be eclipsed by the name of [[Christian Dior|Dior]]."<ref>Morris, Bernadine, "Diana Vreeland, Editor, Dies; Voice of Fashion for Decades", ''The New York Times'', 23 August 1989, page A1</ref> De la Renta proceeded to work for Arden for two years in [[New York City]] before he went to work for [[Jane Derby]], an American fashion house.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1965">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=4& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1965 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=4& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> When Derby died in August 1965, de la Renta took over the label.<ref>[http://dept.kent.edu/museum/exhibit/oscar/main.htm Kent University Museum] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090327174232/http://dept.kent.edu/museum/exhibit/oscar/main.htm |date=2009-03-27 }}</ref>

From 1993 to 2002, de la Renta designed the [[haute couture]] collection for the house of [[Balmain (fashion house)|Balmain]], becoming the first Dominican to design for a French couture house.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1993">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=10& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1993 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=10& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://nymag.com/thecut/2014/09/oscar-de-la-renta-loved-powerful-women.html |title=Oscar de la Renta Loved Powerful Women |magazine=New York |first=Véronique |last=Hyland |date=20 October 2014 |accessdate=21 October 2014 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141022050516/http://nymag.com/thecut/2014/09/oscar-de-la-renta-loved-powerful-women.html |archive-date=22 October 2014 }}</ref> In 2006, the Oscar de la Renta label diversified into bridal wear.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 2006">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=15& |title=Óscar de la Renta 2006 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=15& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref>

De la Renta's designs have been worn by a diverse group of distinguished women and celebrities.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta" /> De la Renta's brand saw international [[wholesale]] growth beginning in 2003, under the direction of CEO Alex Bolen, from five to seventy-five locations.<ref name="CEO Talk by Imran Amed, Business of Fashion">{{cite web |url=http://www.businessoffashion.com/2009/01/ceo-talk-alex-bolen-chief-executive-officer-oscar-de-la-renta.html |title=Business of Fashion 2009 |accessdate=26 September 2013 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130929034751/http://www.businessoffashion.com/2009/01/ceo-talk-alex-bolen-chief-executive-officer-oscar-de-la-renta.html |archive-date=29 September 2013 }}</ref> De la Renta's ready-to-wear designs are available in his retail stores, online, and with select wholesale partners worldwide.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 2004">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=13& |title=Óscar de la Renta 2004 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=13& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref>

In 2014, the [[George W. Bush Presidential Center]] hosted an exhibit entitled "Oscar de la Renta: Five Decades of Style" which shared the designer's creations for Mrs. Bush and America's First Ladies.{{Citation needed|date=September 2017}}

==Other enterprises==
In 1977, de la Renta launched his fragrance, ''OSCAR'',<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1977">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=7& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1977 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=7& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> followed by an accessories line in 2001<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 2001">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=11& |title=Óscar de la Renta 2001 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=11& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> and a homewares line in 2002.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 2002">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=12& |title=Óscar de la Renta 2002 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=12& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> The new business venture included 100 home furnishings for Century Furniture featuring dining tables, upholstered chairs, and couches. In 2004, he added a less expensive line of clothing called O Oscar. De la Renta said he wanted to attract new customers whom he could not reach before.<ref name="Biography.com">>{{cite web |url=http://www.biography.com/people/oscar-de-la-renta-9270239 |title=Biography.com |accessdate=26 Sep 2013 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928105717/http://www.biography.com/people/oscar-de-la-renta-9270239 |archive-date=2013-09-28 }}</ref>

In 2006, de la Renta designed Tortuga Bay, a boutique hotel at [[Puntacana Resort and Club]]. The hotel is part of the luxury hotel collection, The Leading Hotels of the World.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lhw.com/hotel/Tortuga-Bay-Punta-Cana-Dominican-Republic|title=Tortuga Bay : Punta Cana, Dominican Republic : The Leading Hotels of the World|website=www.lhw.com|language=en|access-date=2017-06-28|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170513113540/http://www.lhw.com/hotel/Tortuga-Bay-Punta-Cana-Dominican-Republic|archive-date=2017-05-13}}</ref>

==Awards, honors, and philanthropic endeavors==

===Design awards===
In 1967 and 1968, de la Renta won the [[Coty Award]] (the U.S. fashion industry "[[Academy Awards|Oscars]]") and in 1973 was inducted into the Coty Hall of Fame.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1967">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=5& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1967 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=5& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref>

From 1973 to 1976, and from 1986 to 1988, he served as President of the [[Council of Fashion Designers of America|CFDA]].<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1973">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=6& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1973 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=6& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> He is also a two-time winner of the American Fashion Critic's Award and was inducted into the Fame in 1973.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta" />

De la Renta's talents received continual international recognition. Among them, he received the ''Council of Fashion Designers Designer of the Year Award'' in 2000 and in 2007 (tied with [[Proenza Schouler]]). In February 1990, he was honored with the [[Council of Fashion Designers of America|CFDA Lifetime Achievement Award]].<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1990">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=9& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1990 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=9& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> King [[Juan Carlos I of Spain|Juan Carlos of Spain]] bestowed de la Renta with two awards, the Gold Medal of Bellas Artes and the [[Order of Civil Merit|La Gran Cruz de la Orden del Mérito Civil]].<ref name="Óscar de la Renta" /> He was recognized by the French government with the [[Legion of Honour|Légion d'honneur]] as a Commandeur.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta" />

===Other awards===
Oscar de la Renta was named to the [[International Best Dressed List]] Hall of Fame in 1973.<ref>[http://www.vanityfair.com/style/2014/09/international-best-dressed-hall-of-fame Profile] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160413042555/http://www.vanityfair.com/style/2014/09/international-best-dressed-hall-of-fame |date=2016-04-13 }}, VanityFair.com; accessed 20 October 2014.</ref>

The Dominican Republic honored him with the [[Order of Merit of Duarte, Sánchez and Mella]] and the [[Order of Christopher Columbus]]. De la Renta founded the Casa del Niño orphanage in [[La Romana, La Romana|La Romana]]<ref name="Óscar de la Renta 1982">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=8& |title=Óscar de la Renta 1982 |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=8& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref> He contributed extensively in the construction of a much needed school near his home at the ''Punta Cana Resort and Club'' in [[Punta Cana]]. {{citation needed|date=December 2011}}

De la Renta held dual citizenship in the Dominican Republic and the United States.<ref name="mower12" /> He was an [[Ambassador-at-Large]] of the Dominican Republic.<ref name="mower12" />

De la Renta served as a board member of the [[Metropolitan Opera]], [[Carnegie Hall]] and [[WNET]]. He served on the boards of several charitable institutions such as New Yorkers for Children, the America's Society. He was chairman of the [[Queen Sofía Spanish Institute]].<ref name="Óscar de la Renta" /> He received an honorary degree from [[Hamilton College (New York)]] on 26 May 2013.

In February 2014, Oscar de la Renta recreated his entire Spring presentation, ''Designed for A Cure 2014 collection'', to raise money for the Sylvester Comprehensive Cancer Center at the [[Leonard M. Miller School of Medicine|University of Miami Leonard M. Miller School of Medicine]].<ref name="Oscar de la Renta Takes Collection to Miami">{{cite web |url=http://www.wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-scoops/oscar-heads-south-7481123?src=nl/mornReport/20140219 |title=Oscar de la Renta Takes Collection to Miami |publisher=WWD |date=19 February 2014 |accessdate=19 February 2014 |author=Kleinman, Rebecca |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140321055925/http://www.wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-scoops/oscar-heads-south-7481123?src=nl%2FmornReport%2F20140219 |archive-date=21 March 2014 }}</ref>

===Honors===
In  1991, de la Renta was the recipient of the Golden Plate Award of the [[Academy of Achievement|American Academy of Achievement]].<ref>{{cite web|title= Golden Plate Awardees of the American Academy of Achievement |website=www.achievement.org|publisher=[[American Academy of Achievement]]|url=https://achievement.org/our-history/golden-plate-awards/#business}}</ref> In 2014, de la Renta was the recipient of the Carnegie Hall Medal of Excellence.<ref name="Vogue-carnegie-medal">{{cite web |last1=Malle |first1=Chloe |title=Oscar de la Renta Receives Medal of Excellence at Carnegie Hall |url=http://www.vogue.com/969650/carnegie-hall-gala-oscar-de-la-renta/ |website=Vogue |accessdate=26 June 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160812134418/http://www.vogue.com/969650/carnegie-hall-gala-oscar-de-la-renta/ |archive-date=12 August 2016 }}</ref>
In 2017, de la Renta was honored by the United States Postal Service with an eleven stamp series, featuring a black and white photo of him and ten details from his fashion designs. <ref>https://stamps.org/US-New-Issues-2017</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1966, de la Renta became the third husband of [[Françoise de Langlade]] (1921–1983),<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/1983/06/18/obituaries/francoise-de-la-rente-62-a-leader-in-fashion.html ''Francoise de la Rente, 62, a Leader in Fashion''] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161115073004/http://www.nytimes.com/1983/06/18/obituaries/francoise-de-la-rente-62-a-leader-in-fashion.html |date=2016-11-15 }} from ''[[The New York Times]]'' 18 June 1983</ref> an editor-in-chief of ''[[Vogue (magazine)|French Vogue]]'' who once worked for the fashion house of [[Elsa Schiaparelli]]. They were married until she died of cancer in 1983.<ref name="nymag2005" /> After her death, de la Renta adopted a boy from the Dominican Republic and named him Moisés.<ref name="nymag2005">{{cite magazine |url=http://nymag.com/nymetro/shopping/fashion/spring05/11016 |title=Dynatsty |magazine=[[New York (magazine)|New York]] |date=21 May 2005 |first=David |last=Amsden |accessdate=21 October 2014 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023142827/http://nymag.com/nymetro/shopping/fashion/spring05/11016/ |archive-date=23 October 2014 }}</ref>

In 1990, the designer married [[Annette de la Renta|Annette Engelhard]] (born 1939), daughter of [[Fritz Mannheimer]] and his wife [[Jane Engelhard|Jane]] née Reiss, and adoptive daughter of her mother's second husband, [[Charles W. Engelhard, Jr.]] De la Renta had stepchildren from both marriages. His son-in-law Alex Bolen currently operates as Chief Executive Officer, and stepdaughter Eliza Bolen serves as Vice President of Licensing at Oscar de la Renta, LLC.<ref name="Óscar de la Renta Today">{{cite web |url=http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=/thehouse/#timelineId=17& |title=Óscar de la Renta Today |url-status=dead |accessdate=10 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722070743/http://www.oscardelarenta.com/?folderId=%2Fthehouse%2F#timelineId=17& |archive-date=22 July 2012 }}</ref><ref>[http://www.billionaire.com/interviews/823/alex-bolen-oscar-de-la-renta-ceo-interview Billionaire Magazine: "Oscar de la Renta: Hard Work and Good Fortune"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141103135823/http://www.billionaire.com/interviews/823/alex-bolen-oscar-de-la-renta-ceo-interview |date=2014-11-03 }} by Norman Tan, 2 June 2014.</ref>

De la Renta was regarded as an unofficial ambassador of the [[Dominican Republic]], his home country, and held a diplomatic passport. He had homes there in [[Casa de Campo, Dominican Republic|Casa de Campo]] and [[Punta Cana]], in addition to his residence in [[Kent, Connecticut]].<ref name="nyt10-20" />

==Health==
De la Renta was diagnosed with [[cancer]] in 2006.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/fashion-icon-oscar-de-la-renta-dies-82/story?id=26332287 |title=Iconic Fashion Designer Oscar de la Renta Dies at 82 |publisher=ABC |date=20 October 2014 |accessdate=20 October 2014}}</ref> A year later at the [[Council of Fashion Designers of America|CFDA]] "Fashion Talks" event, Executive Director [[Fern Mallis]] called him "The Sultan of Suave". At that event, he spoke of his cancer, saying:
