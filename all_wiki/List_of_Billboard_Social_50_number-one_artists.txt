The '''''Billboard'' Social 50''' is a popularity chart which ranks the most active musical artists on the world's leading [[social networking service]]s.<ref name="Social 50">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/p/billboard-charts-legend|title=Billboard Charts Legend – General Information|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190807055754/https://www.billboard.com/p/billboard-charts-legend|archive-date=August 7, 2019|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> Its data, published by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine and compiled by [[Next Big Sound]], is based collectively on each artist's weekly additions of [[Friending|friends]], fans and followers, along with artist website views and [[streaming media]].<ref name="Social 50"/> Bill Werde, the former editorial director of ''Billboard'', called the Social&nbsp;50 "yet another step" in the evolution of the magazine and an "important response to our changing times".<ref name="Mashable.com">{{cite web|url=http://mashable.com/2010/12/02/billboard-social-50|title=Billboard Launches 'Social 50' Chart to Rate Artists by Online Popularity|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|date=December 2, 2010|work=[[Mashable.com|Mashable]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101203141902/https://mashable.com/2010/12/02/billboard-social-50/|archive-date=December 3, 2010|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> The chart initially only retrieved its data from [[YouTube]], [[Vevo]], [[Facebook]], [[Twitter]], [[Myspace]], and [[iLike]] to create its ranking, but in November 2012 was expanded to include [[SoundCloud]] and [[Instagram]].<ref name="New Chart Based on Social Networking"/><ref name="Changes">{{cite web|url=http://billboard.com/articles/news/474220/one-direction-rita-ora-benefit-from-social-50-formula-shake-up|title=One Direction, Rita Ora Benefit From Social 50 Formula Shake-Up|last=Gruger|first=William|date=November 12, 2012|work=Billboard|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130213044608/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/474220/one-direction-rita-ora-benefit-from-social-50-formula-shake-up|archive-date=February 13, 2013|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> Data from [[Vine (service)|Vine]] and [[Tumblr]] were added to the chart in June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6605110/vine-tumblr-billboard-social-50-chart|title=Vine & Tumblr Join the Billboard Social 50 Chart|last=Gruger|first=William|date=June 18, 2015|work=Billboard|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150621001949/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6605110/vine-tumblr-billboard-social-50-chart|archive-date=June 21, 2015|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref>

The ''Billboard'' Social&nbsp;50 was launched on December 11, 2010. The first artist to reach number one on the chart was Barbadian singer [[Rihanna]].<ref name="New Chart Based on Social Networking">{{cite web|url=http://rollingstone.com/music/news/new-chart-based-on-social-networking-data-debuts-20101202|title=New Chart Based on Social Networking Data Debuts|last=Johnston|first=Maura|date=December 2, 2010|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180721043919/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/new-chart-based-on-social-networking-data-debuts-250551/|archive-date=July 21, 2018|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Top Billboard's Social 50 Chart">{{cite web|url=http://mtv.com/news/articles/1653499/rihanna-justin-bieber-top-billboards-social-50-chart.jhtml|title=Rihanna, Justin Bieber Top Billboard's Social 50 Chart|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|date=December 3, 2010|work=[[MTV News]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714220135/http://www.mtv.com/news/1653499/rihanna-justin-bieber-top-billboards-social-50-chart/|archive-date=July 14, 2014|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> Since debuting, she has spent a total of 21 weeks at the top of the chart. [[BTS]] holds the record for the most weeks at number one, with 206.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8549956/bts-break-social-50-chart-record|title=BTS Break Record For Most Weeks at No. 1 on Social 50 Chart|last=Rutherford|first=Kevin|date=February 3, 2020|work=Billboard|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200204012015/https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8549956/bts-break-social-50-chart-record|archive-date=February 4, 2020|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> Canadian singer-songwriter [[Justin Bieber]] follows with 163 weeks at number one.

On October 2016, the South Korean boy band [[BTS]] landed the number one spot on the Social 50 chart, becoming the second K-Pop act, after Psy, to reach first place on the chart. They currently also hold the record for the most consecutive weeks at number one, with 177. Following her death in December 2012, American singer [[Jenni Rivera]] became the first and only artist to top the chart [[wiktionary:posthumously|posthumously]]. Since its launch, 20&nbsp;artists have reached the top spot on the ''Billboard'' Social&nbsp;50. Three of these artists—Rivera, [[Skrillex]] and [[Justin Timberlake]]—have only reached the spot for a single week.
