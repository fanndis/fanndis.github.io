'''Rajnath Singh''' (born 10 July 1951) is an Indian politician serving as the [[Minister of Defence (India)|Defence Minister of India]]. He is the former [[List of presidents of the Bharatiya Janata Party|President]] of [[Bharatiya Janata Party]]. He has previously served as the [[List of Chief Ministers of Uttar Pradesh|Chief Minister]] of [[Uttar Pradesh]] and as a [[Union Council of Ministers|Cabinet Minister]] in the [[Vajpayee Government]]. He was the [[Minister of Home Affairs (India)|Home Minister]] in the [[First Modi ministry|First Modi Ministry]]. He has also served as the [[List of Presidents of the Bharatiya Janata Party|President]] of the BJP twice i.e. 2005 to 2009 and 2013 to 2014. He is a veteran leader of the [[BJP]] who started his career as a [[Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh|RSS]] Swayamsevak.<ref name="Meet the leaders">{{Cite news |title=Meet the men and women who will run India for the next 5 years - Ministry of utmost prowess|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/elections/lok-sabha/india/meet-the-men-and-women-who-will-run-india-for-the-next-5-years/rajnath-singh-defence-minister/slideshow/69594872.cms |website=The Economic Times |access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref> He is an advocate of the party's [[Hindutva]] ideology.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Rajnath Singh {{!}} Biography & Facts|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Rajnath-Singh |website=Encyclopedia Britannica |language=en |access-date=20 August 2020}}</ref>

He has been [[Member of parliament, Lok Sabha]]  two times from [[Lucknow (Lok Sabha constituency)]] and once from [[Ghaziabad (Lok Sabha constituency)]].<ref name="Rajnath Singh elections 2020">{{Cite news |title=Rajnath Singh: Rajnath Singh BJP from LUCKNOW in Lok Sabha Elections {{!}} Rajnath Singh News, images and videos|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/rajnath-singh/candidates/candidateid-11464.cms |website=The Economic Times |access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref> He was also active in State Politics and remained MLA from [[Haidergarh (assembly constituency)]] twice being [[Chief minister (India)|Chief minister]].<ref name=":1" />

==Early life==
Singh was born in Bhabhaura village of [[Chandauli district]], [[Uttar Pradesh]] to father Ram Badan Singh and mother Gujarati Devi.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kaushal |first1=Pradeep |title=Jaswant is sacked without show-cause notice, but Vasundhara could defy directive to resign |url=http://archive.indianexpress.com/news/-jaswant-is-sacked-without-showcause-notice-but-vasundhara-could-defy-directive-to-resign-/517003 |work=Indian Express |date=15 September 2009 |access-date=21 September 2020}}</ref><ref name="Bio">{{Cite web|title=Biography – Rajnath Singh|url=https://www.rajnathsingh.in/biography/ |website=rajnathsingh.in |access-date=25 July 2020}}</ref> He was born into a family of farmers. He received his primary education from a local school of his village and went on to secure a master's degree in physics, acquiring first division results from the [[Gorakhpur University]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=10 April 2019|agency=PTI|title=Rajnath Singh's biography tracing 5 decades in politics to hit stands in May|url=https://www.indiatoday.in/india/story/rajnath-singh-biography-rajneeti-1498533-2019-04-10 |work=India Today |language=en |access-date=21 August 2020}}</ref> From childhood he was inspired by the ideology of [[Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=11 March 2011|title=Shri Rajnath Singh|url=http://censusindia.gov.in/2011-Documents/ShriRajnathSingh.pdf|url-status=live|archive-date=10 August 2020|access-date=14 August 2020|website=[[Government of India]]}}</ref> He worked as a lecturer of physics at K.B. Post-Graduate College Mirzapur, UP.<ref name="Bio" /> He has also one brother, Jaipal Singh.<ref>{{Cite news |date=29 August 2015|title=Rajnath Singh celebrates Raksha Bandhan with children at his residence|url=https://www.financialexpress.com/india-news/rajnath-singh-celebrates-raksha-bandhan-with-children-at-his-residence/127212/ |website=The Financial Express |language=en-US |access-date=21 September 2020}}</ref>

== Early political career ==
=== Early career in RSS ===
He had been associated with the [[Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh]] since 1964, at the age of 13 and remained connected with the organisation. He also became [[Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh|Shakha Karavah]] (General Secretary) of [[Mirzapur]] in the year 1972.<ref name="Bio" /> After 2 years in the year 1974, he joined the politics.<ref name="Rajnath Singh elections 2020" /> Between 1969 and 1971 he was the organizational secretary of the [[Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad]] (the student wing of the RSS) in [[Gorakhpur]]. He became the general secretary of the RSS's [[Mirzapur]] branch in 1972.<ref name=":2" />

=== Entry into politics ===
In 1974, he was appointed secretary for the Mirzapur unit of the [[Bharatiya Jana Sangh]], predecessor of [[Bharatiya Janata Party]].<ref name="profile">{{cite news|title=Profile: Rajnath Singh |publisher=[[Zee News]] |url=http://www.zeenews.com/znnew/articles.asp?rep=2&aid=264537&sid=ARC |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070930185718/http://www.zeenews.com/znnew/articles.asp?rep=2&aid=264537&sid=ARC |archive-date=30 September 2007 }}</ref>

In 1975, aged 24, Singh was appointed District President of the [[Jana Sangh]]. In 1977, he was elected [[Member of the Legislative Assembly (India)|Member of Legislative Assembly]] from the [[Mirzapur]]. At that time he was influence by the [[JP Movement]] of [[Jayaprakash Narayan]] and joined the [[Janata Party]] and was elected as [[Member of the Legislative Assembly (India)|Member of Legislative Assembly]] from Mirzapur.<ref>{{Cite web |date=24 April 2020|title=Rajnath Singh Political Career,Age,Birthday,Wiki,Family,Biography & More|url=https://medium.com/@lelebhikari123/rajnath-singh-political-career-age-birthday-wiki-family-biography-more-70265e236029 |website=Medium |language=en |access-date=20 August 2020}}</ref> He was also arrested in the year 1975 during the state of [[National Emergency]] for associating with [[JP Movement]] and was detained for a time period of 2 years and when he was released, he was re-elected as [[Member Of Legislative Assembly]].<ref name="Did you know?">{{Cite news |title=Did you know⁠? India's Defence Minister Rajnath Singh has a master's degree in physics⁠ — and other interesting facts about his life|url=https://www.businessinsider.in/thelife/personalities/news/rajnath-singh-profile-interesting-facts-all-you-need-to-know-about-indias-minister-of-defence/slidelist/76888231.cms |website=Business Insider |access-date=22 August 2020}}</ref>

At that time he gained the popularity in [[State (politics)]] and the joined [[BJP]] in the year 1980 and was one of the initial members of the Party.{{Sfn|Chintamani|2019|p=35}} He became the State President of the BJP youth wing in 1984, the National general secretary in 1986 and the National President in 1988. He was also elected into the [[Uttar Pradesh]] [[Uttar Pradesh Legislative Council|legislative council.]]<ref name="profile" />

== Education Minister (1991–1992) ==
In 1991, when [[Bharatiya Janata Party]] made its government first time in [[Uttar Pradesh]], he was appointed as the Education Minister. He remained minister for a tenure of two years.<ref name="DDMod" /> Major highlights of his tenure as Education Minister included [[Anti-Copying Act, 1992]], which made copying a non-bailable offence,<ref name="rediff1">[http://www.rediff.com/news/report/how-rajnath-singh-rose-through-the-ranks/20130131.htm "How Rajnath Singh rose through the ranks"]. Rediff.com. 31 January 2013</ref> modernising science texts and incorporating [[Indian mathematics#Vedic period|vedic mathematics]] into the [[syllabus]].<ref name=":1">{{cite news |url=http://indiatoday.intoday.in/story/who-is-rajnath-singh/1/247240.html |title=Who is Rajnath Singh? |work=India Today |date=23 January 2013 |access-date=28 January 2013}}</ref>

=== Modernising of science texts and Vedic Mathematics ===
In 1991, he rewrote history texts and incorporated [[Vedic Mathematics]] into the syllabus.<ref name=":1" /> He was a loyal man of [[Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh|RSS]], which is a [[Culturalism|culturalist]] organisation and was considered as a move by the Sangh.{{Sfn|Singh|2019|p=2}} He also focused to revive our modern tongue language instead of foreign languages, but also states and appreciates knowledge of modern languages.<ref name=":13">{{Cite news|title=Need to change present education system: Home Minister Rajnath Singh|work=The Economic Times|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/politics-and-nation/need-to-change-present-education-system-home-minister-rajnath-singh/articleshow/45004996.cms?from=mdr|access-date=26 August 2020}}</ref>

== Union Surface Transport Minister (1999–2000) ==
In April 1994, he was elected into the [[Rajya Sabha]] (Upper House of the [[Parliament of India|Parliament]]) and he became involved with the Advisory committee on Industry (1994–96), Consultative Committee for the Ministry of Agriculture, Business Advisory Committee, House Committee and the Committee on Human Resource Development.<ref name="profile"/> On 25 March 1997, he became the President of the BJP's unit in Uttar Pradesh and in 1999 he became the Union Cabinet Minister for Surface Transport.<ref name="profile" />

=== Resignation ===
In the year 2002, he resigned from the position of [[Chief Minister of U.P.]] within the time of 2 years because at that time during the ground test, the [[BJP]] Government was at minority status. Because of the following reason there was imposition of [[President's rule]] in the State, then after that in the 14th Legislative Assembly, [[Mayawati]] became the Chief Minister for the 3rd time.<ref name="Rajnath Singh elections 2020" />

== Union Agriculture Minister (2003–04) ==
In 2003, Singh was appointed as the [[Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare|Minister of Agriculture]] and subsequently for Food Processing in the [[National Democratic Alliance (India)|NDA]] Government led by [[Atal Bihari Vajpayee]], and was faced with the difficult task of maintaining one of the most volatile areas of India's economy.<ref name=":6">{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/2003/06/11/stories/2003061100100800.htm|title=Courage, Mr Rajnath Singh|work=[[The Hindu]]|date=11 June 2003}}</ref> During this period he initiated a few epoch-making projects including the Kisan Call Centre and Farm Income Insurance Scheme.<ref name=":7">[http://wikimapia.org/16811689/Shri-Rajnath-Singh-MP-Loksabh-Ghaziabad-Ex-CM-Uattar-pardesh-1-30-Raj-Nagra "Shri Rajnath Singh, MP (Ghaziabad)"]. wikimapia.org</ref> He brought down interest rates on Agriculture loans and also established Farmer Commission and initiated Farms Income Insurance Scheme.<ref name=achieve/>

=== Second time (2013–2014) ===
On 24 January 2013, following the resignation of [[Nitin Gadkari]] due to corruption charges, Singh was re-elected as the BJP's National President.<ref>{{cite news |agency=Press Trust of India |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2009-12-19/india/28067924_1_rajnath-singh-nitin-gadkari-bjp-president |title=Rajnath steps down, Gadkari takes over as BJP president |work=The Times of India |date=19 December 2009 |access-date=28 January 2013}}</ref>

Singh is on record shortly after the law [[Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code]] was re-instated in 2013, claiming that his party is "unambiguously" in favour of the law, also claiming that "We will state (at an all-party meeting if it is called) that we support Section 377 because we believe that homosexuality is an unnatural act and cannot be supported."<ref name=":4">{{Cite news |url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/1131214/jsp/nation/story_17679913.jsp |title=BJP comes out, vows to oppose homosexuality|last=Rameshan|first=Radhika|date=13 December 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131216103934/https://www.telegraphindia.com/1131214/jsp/nation/story_17679913.jsp|archive-date=16 December 2012|website=The Telegraph}}</ref> Singh was elected President for his second term after Gadkari stepped down in 2013. Singh played a large role in the BJP's campaign for the [[2014 Indian general election]], including declaring [[Narendra Modi]] the party's Prime Ministerial candidate despite opposition from within the BJP. After the party's landslide victory, Singh resigned the party presidency to assume the position of Home Minister.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Desk|first=India TV News|date=9 July 2014|title=Rajnath Singh - from 'Physics lecturer' to 'Union Home Minister'|url=https://www.indiatvnews.com/politics/national/rajnath-singh-home-minister-of-india-bjp-modi-government-latest-18798.html|access-date=13 October 2020|website=www.indiatvnews.com|language=en}}</ref>

He contested the 2014 Lok Sabha elections from [[Lucknow (Lok Sabha constituency)|Lucknow]] constituency and was subsequently elected as a [[Member of Parliament (India)|Member of the Parliament]].<ref name="the hindu">[http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-otherstates/rajnath-singh-surpasses-vajpayees-victory-margin-in-lucknow/article6021382.ece "Rajnath Singh surpasses Vajpayee's victory margin in Lucknow"].
