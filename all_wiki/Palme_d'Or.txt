The '''Palme d'Or''' ({{IPA-fr|palm(ə) dɔʁ}}; {{lang-en|'''Golden Palm'''}}) is the highest prize awarded at the [[Cannes Film Festival]].<ref name="Brief History">{{cite web|url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/69-editions/palme/a-brief-history-of-the-palme-d-or |title=A brief history of the Palme d'or |publisher=Festival-cannes.com |accessdate=28 May 2017}}</ref> It was introduced in 1955 by the festival's organizing committee. Previously, from 1939 to 1954, the highest prize at the festival was the '''Grand Prix du Festival International du Film'''.<ref name="Brief History" /> In 1964, The Palme d'Or was replaced again by the Grand Prix, before being reintroduced in 1975.<ref name="Brief History" />

The Palme d'Or is widely considered to be one of the most prestigious awards in the [[film industry]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vox.com/culture/2017/5/16/15508284/cannes-film-festival-how-to-pronounce-high-heels-netflix-jury|title=Why the Cannes Film Festival matters (and how to pronounce it)|work=Vox|access-date=2018-01-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.france24.com/en/20170528-cannes-2017-swedish-film-square-ruben-ostlund-wins-top-prize-palme-d-or|title=Cannes 2017: Sweden's Ruben Östlund wins Palme d’Or for 'The Square' - France 24|date=2017-05-28|work=France 24|access-date=2018-01-21|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://deadline.com/2016/05/cannes-film-festival-academy-awards-pete-hammond-commentary-1201751722/|title=Cannes Vs Oscar: Why The Palme d’Or And Best Picture Academy Award Don’t Make A Perfect Match|last=Hammond|first=Pete|date=2016-05-11|work=Deadline|access-date=2018-01-21|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lists/cannes-palme-dor-winners-ranked-891143/item/scarecrow-palme-dor-winners-891097|title='Scarecrow' (1973) - Cannes: All the Palme d'Or Winners, Ranked|website=The Hollywood Reporter}}</ref>

== History ==
[[File:Blason ville fr Cannes (Alpes-Maritimes).svg|thumb|left|upright=0.75|The Commune of Cannes coat of arms]]
In 1954, the festival decided to present an award annually, titled the Grand Prix of the International Film Festival, with a new design each year from a contemporary artist. The festival's board of directors invited several jewellers to submit designs for a [[Palm (plant)|palm]], in tribute to the coat of arms of the city of [[Cannes]] evoking the famous legend of [[Honoratus|Saint Honorat]] and the palm trees lining the famous [[Promenade de la Croisette]].<ref name="Brief History" /> The original design by Parisian jeweller Lucienne Lazon, who took inspiration in a sketch done by legendary director [[Jean Cocteau]], had the [[bevel]]led lower extremity of the [[stalk (botany)|stalk]] forming a heart, and the pedestal a sculpture in [[terracotta]] by the artist [[Sébastien]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.journaldesfemmes.com/luxe/magazine/1575795-histoire-de-la-palme-d-or-cannes-chopard/|title=Histoire de la Palme d'or, de Lucienne Lazon à Chopard|access-date=2017-05-31|language=fr}}</ref>

In 1955, the first Palme d'Or was awarded to [[Delbert Mann]] for the film ''[[Marty (film)|Marty]]''. From 1964 to 1974, the Festival temporarily resumed a Grand Prix.<ref name="Brief History" /> In 1975, the Palme d'Or was reintroduced and has since remained the symbol of the Cannes Film Festival, awarded every year to the director of the winning film, and was then presented in a case of pure red [[Morocco leather]] lined with white [[suede]].<ref name="Brief History" />

As of 2019, [[Jane Campion]] is the only female director to have won the Palme d'Or, for her work on ''[[The Piano]]''. However, in 2013, when ''[[Blue Is the Warmest Colour|Blue Is the Warmest Color]]'' won the Palme d'Or, the [[Steven Spielberg]]-headed jury awarded it to the film's director [[Abdellatif Kechiche]], as well as the film's actresses [[Adèle Exarchopoulos]] and [[Léa Seydoux]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/showbiz/article/cannes-5-unforgettable-palme-dor-winners#Tr3TF0fe42zMAXKU.97|title=Cannes: 5 unforgettable Palme d’Or winners|date=2015-05-24|access-date=2018-01-21}}</ref> This marks the first time multiple Palme d'Or trophies were given out in the festival's history.<ref name="The Hollywood Reporter">{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/director-abdellatif-kechiche-why-im-selling-my-palme-dor-1014637|title=Director Abdellatif Kechiche: Why I'm Selling My Palme d'Or (Exclusive)|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-01-21|language=en}}</ref> [[File:Palmed'or.jpg|thumb|upright=1.05|Palme d'Or awarded to ''[[Apocalypse Now]]'' at the [[1979 Cannes Film Festival]]]]

The jury decided to award the actresses alongside the director due to a Cannes policy that forbids the Palme d'Or-winning film from receiving any additional awards, thereby preventing the jury from rewarding both the film and the film's actresses separately.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/participer/rules?id=2 |title=Festival de Cannes - Regulations |publisher=Festival-cannes.fr |accessdate=28 May 2017}}</ref> Of the unorthodox decision, Spielberg said that "had the casting been 3% wrong, it wouldn't have worked like it did for us".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.festival-cannes.fr/en/mediaPlayer/13503.html |title=Conference of the Jury of 66th Festival de Cannes | work=Festival de Cannes |year=2013 |accessdate=27 May 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131230235647/http://www.festival-cannes.fr/en/mediaPlayer/13503.html |archivedate=30 December 2013 |df=dmy}}(video unavailable)</ref> Kechiche later auctioned off his Palme d'Or trophy to fund his new feature film, and expressed dissatisfaction about the festival having given out multiple trophies in an interview with ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', saying that he felt they had "publicly insulted" him by doing it, and that "liberating myself from this Palme d’Or is a way of washing my hands of this sorry affair".<ref name="The Hollywood Reporter"/>

Since its reintroduction, the prize has been redesigned several times. At the beginning of the 1980s, the rounded shape of the pedestal, bearing the palm, gradually transformed to become pyramidal in 1984. In 1992, Thierry de Bourqueney redesigned the Palme and its pedestal in hand-cut [[crystal]]. In 1997, [[Caroline Scheufele]] redesigned the statuette and since then, has been manufactured by Swiss jewellery [[Chopard]]. The palm is made from 4.16 oz (118 grams) of [[Carat (purity)|18-carat yellow gold]] while the base of the branch forms a small heart. The Palme d’or rests on a dainty rock crystal cushion shaped like an emerald-cut diamond.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chopard.com/intl/palme-d-or|title=THE PALME D'OR|website=Chopard|access-date=2020-03-14|language=en}}</ref> A single piece of cut crystal forms a cushion for the palm, which was hand-cast into a wax mould and now presented in a case of blue [[Morocco leather]]. In 1998, Theo Angelopoulos was the first director to win the Palme d'or as we now know it today for his film ''[[Eternity and a Day]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.festival-cannes.com/en/69-editions/palme/a-brief-history-of-the-palme-dor |title=A brief history of the Palme d'Or - Festival de Cannes 2019 (International Film Festival) |work=Festival-cannes.com |accessdate=4 March 2020}}</ref>

The winner of the 2014 Palme d'Or, ''[[Winter Sleep (film)|Winter Sleep]]''—a Turkish film by [[Nuri Bilge Ceylan]]—occurred during the same year as the 100th anniversary of Turkish cinema. Upon receiving the award, Ceylan dedicated the prize to both the "young people" involved in the ongoing political unrest in Turkey and the workers who were killed in the [[Soma mine disaster]], which occurred on the day prior to the commencement of the awards event.<ref>{{cite news|author1=Xan Brooks|title=Cannes festival ready for shut-eye after Winter Sleep wins Palme d'Or|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/may/25/cannes-2014-winter-sleep-palme-d-or-timothy-spall?CMP=EMCNEWEML6619I2|accessdate=1 June 2014|work=The Guardian|agency=Guardian News and Media Limited|date=25 May 2014}}</ref>

In 2017, the award was re-designed to celebrate the festival's [[2017 Cannes Film Festival|70th anniversary]].<ref name="Brief History" /> The diamonds were provided by an ethical supplier certified by the Responsible Jewellery Council.<ref name="Brief History" />

The [[2020 Cannes Film Festival]] was cancelled due to the ongoing [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. 56 films were announced as official selections by the festival, but no awards were given this year.<ref name="LA Times">{{Cite web|date=2020-06-03|title=What Cannes 2020 line-up says about the cancelled festival|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/movies/story/2020-06-03/cannes-2020-canceled-lineup-wes-anderson-pixar|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en}}</ref>

There have been only three winners of both the Palme d’Or and the Oscar for the Best Picture: ''[[The Lost Weekend (film)|The Lost Weekend]]'' (1945), ''[[Marty (film)|Marty]]'' (1955), and ''[[Parasite (2019 film)|Parasite]]'' (2019).
: <sup>‡</sup> The Palme d'Or for ''[[Union Pacific (film)|Union Pacific]]'' was awarded in retrospect at the 2002 festival. The festival's debut was to take place in 1939, but it was cancelled due to [[World War II]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2002/film/markets-festivals/pianist-tickles-cannes-1117867622/ |title=‘Pianist’ tickles Cannes |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |last=McCarthy |first=Todd |date=May 26, 2002 |accessdate=May 24, 2018 }}</ref> The organisers of the 2002 festival presented part of the original 1939 selection to a professional jury of six members. The films were: ''[[Goodbye, Mr. Chips (1939 film)|Goodbye Mr. Chips]]'', ''[[La Piste du Nord]]'', ''[[Lenin in 1918]]'', ''[[The Four Feathers]]'', ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]'', ''[[Union Pacific (film)|Union Pacific]]'', and ''[[Wilton's Zoo|Boefje]]''.
