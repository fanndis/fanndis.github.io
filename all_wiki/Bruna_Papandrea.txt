'''Bruna Papandrea''' (born 1971) is an Australian [[Film producer|film]] and [[television producer]] and the founder of production company [[Made Up Stories (company)|Made Up Stories]]. Prior to [[Made Up Stories (company)|Made Up Stories]], Papandrea co-founded the production company [[Hello Sunshine (company)|Pacific Standard]] with [[Reese Witherspoon]].

==Early life and education==
Papandrea was raised in [[Adelaide]], [[South Australia]],<ref name=smith2020/> by a [[single parent|single mother]].<ref name=ausinfilm/>

==Career==
She moved to [[New York City]] in the 1990s after establishing a career in film production. She briefly returned to Australia to produce the 2000 film ''[[Better Than Sex (film)|Better Than Sex]]'', which was nominated for the [[AACTA Awards|AFI Award]] for [[AACTA Award for Best Film|Best Film]], before moving to London in 2001. She served as a production executive at the film studio [[Mirage Enterprises]] and later returned to New York to work for GreeneStreet Films, a production company for independent films, as a creative director. At GreeneStreet she executive produced the 2006 romantic comedy ''[[Wedding Daze]]'' before joining another independent production company, Groundswell Productions, in Los Angeles in February 2006. There she was responsible for producing ''[[Smart People]]'' (2008), ''[[Milk (2008 American film)|Milk]]'' (2008), ''[[The Marc Pease Experience]]'' (2009), and ''[[All Good Things (film)|All Good Things]]'' (2010).<ref name=focus>{{cite web|url=http://www.focusfeatures.com/milk/castncrew?member=bruna_papandrea|title=Bruna Papandrea|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160421133541/http://www.focusfeatures.com/milk/castncrew?member=bruna_papandrea|archive-date=21 April 2016|publisher=[[Focus Features]]|accessdate=9 February 2015}}</ref>

In 2011, Papandrea produced ''[[Warm Bodies (film)|Warm Bodies]]'', a [[zombie comedy]] film directed by [[Jonathan Levine]] and released in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://collider.com/bruna-papandrea-warm-bodies-interview/|title=Producer Bruna Papandrea Talks Working with Director Jonathan Levine, Aiming for a PG-13 Rating, Zombie Sex, and More on the Set of WARM BODIES|date=4 January 2013|first=Matt|last = Goldberg|publisher=Collider.com|accessdate=9 February 2015}}</ref>

In 2012, she and actress [[Reese Witherspoon]] co-founded Pacific Standard, a [[Beverly Hills]]-based production company focusing on creating films made by and about women.<ref name=variety>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/features/reese-witherspoon-production-company-female-driven-material-1201323117/|title=Reese Witherspoon, Bruna Papandrea Push for Female-Driven Material With Pacific Standard|date=7 October 2014|first=Jenelle|last=Riley|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=9 February 2015}}</ref><ref name=smith2020/> Their first two projects at Pacific Standard were ''[[Gone Girl (film)|Gone Girl]]'' and ''[[Wild (2014 film)|Wild]]'', both adapted from books whose rights were acquired by Papandrea and Witherspoon before publication. Both films were released in 2014, by which time they had also completed production on the 2015 comedy film ''[[Hot Pursuit (2015 film)|Hot Pursuit]]''.<ref name=variety/> In 2016, they announced that they were ending their partnership, though they would continue to work on the projects that were in the middle of production, including the [[HBO]] series ''[[Big Little Lies (miniseries)|Big Little Lies]]'' and a film adaptation of the novel ''[[Luckiest Girl Alive]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2016/film/news/reese-witherspoon-bruna-papandrea-split-1201850426/|title=Reese Witherspoon Parts Ways With Producing Partner Bruna Papandrea|date=1 September 2016|first=Justin|last=Kroll|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=16 March 2017}}</ref>

→ ''See also: [[List of accolades received by Gone Girl (film)]], [[Wild (2014 film)#Accolades|Wild (2014 film) § Accolades]],'' and ''[[Big Little Lies (TV series)#Accolades|Big Little Lies & Accolades]]''

Papandrea launched her current production company, [[Made Up Stories (company)|Made Up Stories]], in January 2017 to carry on her mission in championing female filmmakers, adapting female-written novels and creating stories featuring multi-faceted female characters. The company is also committed to inclusivity in less-acknowledged departments behind-the-scenes such as transportation teams and gaffers. Papandrea has expressed intent to start a foundation, Made Up Solutions, to provide opportunities for women from low socio-economic and diverse backgrounds.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Producer Bruna Papandrea Didn’t See A Space For Women In Hollywood, So She Created One Herself|url=https://www.bustle.com/p/producer-bruna-papandrea-didnt-see-a-space-for-women-in-hollywood-so-she-created-one-herself-15959699|access-date=2020-10-29|website=Bustle|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Bruna Papandrea: From Adelaide to Hollywood|url=http://www.australiaunlimited.com/culture/brunapapandrea|access-date=2020-10-29|website=www.australiaunlimited.com|language=en}}</ref>

In April 2020, Papandrea and [[Gregg Fienberg]] founded the "It Takes Our Village" initiative, along with numerous A-list Hollywood producers to help raise money for below-the-line crews affected by COVID-19.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mark Ruffalo, David E. Kelley and More Launch COVID-19 Relief Fund for Film Crews {{!}} Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/mark-ruffalo-david-e-kelley-more-launch-covid-19-relief-fund-film-crews-1289297|access-date=2020-10-29|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref> As she explained, "“The below-the-line crews are the backbone of our industry,” she said, “They are our community – and our community is in need. This initiative is personal to me as I’m a working-class girl who grew up with unions protecting my family. I want to help provide that protection to those who are a vital part in creating stories; the stories that are providing escapism and comfort to people around the world."<ref>{{Cite web|last=McNary|first=Dave|date=2020-04-08|title=Producers Launch ‘It Takes Our Village’ Relief Fund for Crews Impacted By Coronavirus|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/news/coronavirus-producers-relief-fund-1234574869/|access-date=2020-10-29|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref>

{{as of|October 2020}} Papandrea is working on TV miniseries ''[[Nine Perfect Strangers (miniseries)|Nine Perfect Strangers]]''  on the northern coast of [[New South Wales]], and is planning to stay in Australia for the foreseeable future.<ref name=smith2020/>

==Personal life==
Papandrea is married to producer Steve Hutensky and gave birth to twins in 2012. She has [[systemic lupus erythematosus]] (lupus), diagnosed in August 2010, which causes her chronic joint pain.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2014/scene/vpage/reese-witherspoon-honors-bruna-papandrea-at-lupus-la-luncheon-1201362898/|title=Reese Witherspoon Honors Bruna Papandrea at Lupus LA Luncheon|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=9 February 2015|first=Janine|last=Lew|date=23 November 2014}}</ref>
