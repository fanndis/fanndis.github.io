'''Zen Yoga''' refers to a blend of [[yoga]] and [[Zen]] practices, as taught by several modern teachers. 

Aaron Hoopes combines the different philosophies of various Eastern health and fitness traditions, creating this form of yoga to increase accessibility to people who are less athletic.<ref name=reuters>{{cite web| title=Zen Yoga founder adds Japanese twist to ancient practice| publisher=Reuters News Agency| last=Hardach| first=Sophie| date=2008-02-25| accessdate=2012-03-10| url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2008/02/25/us-japan-zen-yoga-idUST1251120080225}}</ref> The philosophies of shanti yoga, [[qigong]], and [[taijiquan|tai-chi]] are combined to help increase flexibility, improve the flow of breathing, and open joints. The philosophy of Zen is incorporated through being mindfully aware of the present moment.<ref name=reuters/> The basic principle of Zen Yoga is that simple breathing, movement and stretching exercises are available to anyone regardless of age, fitness, or health.<ref name=reuters/>

Oki-do yoga, which was developed by Japanese master Oki Masahiro (1921–1985) based on "Indian Yoga, the Chinese Yin-Yang principles and the Japanese Zen tradition," is also referred to as Zen Yoga.<ref>[http://www.okidoyoga.org.uk/ ''Okido Yoga UK''</ref>

The Zenway Sangha, a lay Rinzai organisation founded by [[Shinzan Miyamae Roshi|Shinzan Miyamae]], has integrated yoga practices in their teachings.<ref>Open Way Zen Brisbane, [http://www.openway.org.au/teachers.html#Hogen ''Zen Master Hogen-San'']</ref><ref>{{cite book|author=Daido Hogen Yamahata |editor= K. Carne|title=The Other Shore|date=1986 |location=Oxford |publisher=Parchment Press|page=40}}</ref> According to Zenway teacher [[Julian Daizan Skinner Roshi|Julian Skinner]], Zen practice in general, and Zen Yoga in particular, emphasize three intertwined areas – physical alignment, the flow of energy in the body and awareness or mindfulness (Japanese “nen” 念).<ref name="skinner">{{cite journal|last1=Skinner|first1=Julian Daizan|title=A View on Zen and Yoga|journal=The Middle Way|date=May 2014|volume=89|issue=1|page=31}}</ref>

The Zenyoga association in Hungary offers a combination of Zen and yoga.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.yogaalliance.org/SchoolPublicProfile?sid=1325&lid=1325|title=School Profile {{!}} Yoga Alliance|website=www.yogaalliance.org|language=en-US|access-date=2017-10-02}}</ref> International affiliates include groups in Celje (Slovenia), Berlin (Germany) and Cape Cod (USA).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://zenyoga.hr/vjezbanje/gdje-vjezbati/|title=Zenyoga|website=zenyoga.hr|access-date=2017-10-02}}</ref>
