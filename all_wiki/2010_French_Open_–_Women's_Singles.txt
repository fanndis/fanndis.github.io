{{main article|2010 French Open}}

[[Francesca Schiavone]] won her first and only Grand Slam singles title, defeating first-time Grand Slam finalist [[Samantha Stosur]],  6–4, 7–6<sup>(7–2)</sup>, to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2010 French Open]], in a repeat of their last year's first round match. Schiavone thus became the first Italian woman to win a [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament, and the first woman in the [[Open Era]] to win the French Open while seeded outside the top 10. The final was the first since the [[2004 French Open – Women's Singles|2004 tournament]] where both players were appearing in their first Grand Slam singles final. Schiavone remains the last woman with a one-handed backhand to win a grand slam tournament on the woman's tour. In virtue of her victory, Schiavone also made the top 10 for the first time, debuting at No. 6.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/8723949.stm|title=Italy's Francesca Schiavone wins French Open title|publisher=BBC Sport|date=5 June 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/frenchopen/7805844/French-Open-2010-Francesca-Schiavone-beats-Sam-Stosur-to-win-womens-final.html|title=French Open 2010: Francesca Schiavone beats Sam Stosur to win women's final|publisher=The Telegraph|date=6 June 2010|last1=Ramsay|first1=Alix}}</ref>

[[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] was the defending champion, but lost in the third round to [[Maria Kirilenko]].

This was the last French Open appearance of four-time champion [[Justine Henin]] following her comeback. Prior to her defeat to Stosur in the fourth round, Henin had a streak of 24 match wins in the tournament (and had won 40 consecutive sets at Roland Garros before losing the second set of her third round match against [[Maria Sharapova]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/06/01/sports/tennis/01tennis.html|title=Stosur Ends Justine Henin's French Open Streak|work=The New York Times|date=31 May 2010}}</ref> It was also the Grand Slam tournament debut of future world No. 1 and two-time Grand Slam champion [[Simona Halep]]. She was defeated by Stosur in the first round.

This was the first Grand Slam tournament since the [[1979 Australian Open – Women's Singles|1979 Australian Open]] where none of the semifinalists had previously won a Grand Slam title.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/06/04/sports/tennis/04tennis.html|title=Schiavone in Final After Dementieva Retires|work=The New York Times|date=3 June 2010}}</ref>
