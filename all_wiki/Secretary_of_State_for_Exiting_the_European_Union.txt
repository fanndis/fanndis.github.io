'''Her Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for Exiting the European Union''' or, informally, '''Brexit Secretary''',<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/politics/brexit-secretary-david-davis-we-will-quit-the-eu-in-december-2018-a3295716.html | title=Brexit Secretary David Davis says UK 'will quit the EU in December 2018' | work=[[Evening Standard]] | date=14 July 2016 | accessdate=14 July 2016 | author=Cecil, Nicholas}}</ref> was the [[Secretary of State (United Kingdom)|Secretary of State]] responsible for the [[United Kingdom]]'s withdrawal from the [[European Union]], informally referred to as "[[Brexit]]". The secretary was overseeing [[Brexit negotiations]] following a [[2016 United Kingdom European Union membership referendum|nationwide referendum on 23 June 2016]], in which a majority voted in favour of exiting the EU.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-britain-eu-davis-idUKKCN0ZT2IH |title='Charming Bastard' David Davis to lead Brexit talks|work=[[Reuters]]|authors= James, William; Holden, Michael|date=13 July 2016|access-date=9 July 2018}}.</ref><ref name="CSW">{{cite news|author=Foster, Matt|url=https://www.civilserviceworld.com/articles/news/new-department-business-energy-and-industrial-strategy-swallows-decc-and-bis-%E2%80%94-full|title= New Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy swallows up DECC and BIS — full details and reaction|work= Civil Service World|date=14 July 2016|access-date=9 July 2018}}</ref> The office-holder was a member of the [[Cabinet of the United Kingdom|Cabinet]].

The position was created at the outset of the [[First May ministry|premiership]] of [[Theresa May]], who became [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]] on 13 July 2016.<ref name="Gov.UK">{{Cite press release|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/new-ministerial-appointment-july-2016-secretary-of-state-for-exiting-the-european-union|title=New ministerial appointment July 2016: Secretary of State for Exiting the European Union|date=13 July 2016|publisher=Prime Minister's Office, [[10 Downing Street]]|access-date= 9 July 2018}}</ref> May reportedly ordered civil servants to find a building to house a new [[Department for Exiting the European Union]], to be headed by the Secretary of State.<ref>{{cite news|author=Taylor, Adam|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/worldviews/wp/2016/07/14/david-davis-the-man-in-charge-of-getting-britain-out-of-the-e-u/ |title=David Davis: The man in charge of getting Britain out of the E.U.|work= [[Washington Post]]|date=14 July 2016|access-date= 9 July 2018}}</ref> The headquarters of the [[Department of Energy and Climate Change]] (DECC) at Whitehall Place, which was to be vacated once the DECC was merged into the newly created [[Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy]], was viewed as a potential site for the department.<ref name="CSW"/> The department now occupies [[9 Downing Street]].

The inaugural holder was [[David Davis (British politician)|David Davis]] MP,<ref name="Gov.UK" /> a longtime [[Euroscepticism in the United Kingdom|Eurosceptic]] who campaigned for the UK to leave the EU.<ref>{{cite news|author= Crace, John|url= https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2016/feb/04/david-davis-spells-out-his-eu-strategy-be-more-like-canada|title= David Davis spells out his EU strategy: be more like Canada|work= [[The Guardian]]|date= 4 February 2016|access-date= 9 July 2018}}</ref> Davis is a former [[chairman of the Conservative Party]] who served  in the [[Second Major ministry|government of John Major]] as [[Minister of State for Europe]] (1994–97) and in the [[Shadow Cabinet of David Cameron]] as [[shadow home secretary|Shadow Home Secretary]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-politics-36785814|title= Theresa May's cabinet: Who's in and who's out?|work=[[BBC News]]|date=13 July 2016|access-date= 9 July 2018}}</ref>

Davis resigned on 8 July 2018 shortly before midnight; [[Dominic Raab]] was appointed on 9 July as his replacement and resigned on 15 November 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-46219495|title=Brexit Secretary Dominic Raab resigns over EU agreement|last=|first=|work=[[BBC News]]| date= 15 November 2018|access-date=15 November 2018}}</ref> [[Stephen Barclay]], who had been serving as [[Minister of State for Health]], was selected as Raab's successor on 16 November 2018.<ref name="Barclay appointed">{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-46241693 |title=Steve Barclay named new Brexit Secretary |date=16 November 2018 |work=[[BBC News]] |access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref>
This page also includes the portfolios of '''Chief Negotiator for Exiting the European Union''', '''Minister of State for Exiting the European Union''' and '''Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Exiting the European Union'''.

The position was shadowed in [[Parliament of the United Kingdom|Parliament]] by the [[Shadow Secretary of State for Exiting the European Union]] who was also a full member of [[Jeremy Corbyn|Jeremy Corbyn's]] [[Shadow Cabinet of Jeremy Corbyn|Shadow Cabinet]].

The office was abolished on 31 January 2020, as the United Kingdom left the European Union.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/department-for-exiting-the-european-union|title=Department for Exiting the European Union}}</ref>
