{{Distinguish|World Tennis Championship|World Championship Tennis}}
The [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) designates a '''World Champion''' each year based on performances throughout the year, emphasising the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments,<ref name="slampriority">ITF Constitution, p.26, Rule 2.2(a)(iii)</ref> and also considering team events such as the [[Davis Cup]] and [[Fed Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/1687794.stm|title=ITF opt for Hewitt and Capriati|date=2 December 2001|work=[[BBC Online]]|accessdate=20 December 2013}}</ref> Men's and women's singles champions were first named in 1978; the title is now also awarded for doubles, wheelchair, and junior players.<ref>ITF Constitution, p.53 Appendix E</ref> It is sometimes named the "ITF Player of the Year" award, alluding to similar other year-end awards in tennis.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/tennis/story/_/id/21711645|title=Rafael Nadal, Garbine Muguruza win ITF Player of the Year awards|last=|first=|date=2017-12-08|website=ESPN.com|language=en|archive-url=|archive-date=|url-status=|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref>

==Rules and procedures==
The ITF's constitution states that no tennis [[tournament]] can be designated the "World Championships" without unanimous consent of the ITF Council.<ref>ITF Constitution, p.29: Article 29(a)</ref> There is currently no such tournament. The constitution also states:<ref>ITF Constitution, p.38 Regulation 3.1</ref>
:The ITF may award the title of World Champion to players who, in the opinion of the Board of Directors, are the most outstanding players in any one-year. The names of players who have been awarded this title shall be listed in the Roll of Honour.
It also states:<ref name="slampriority"/>
:Official Tennis Championships [i.e. the Grand Slam events] shall be the decisive factor in the determination of the ITF World Champions for each year.

For singles, ITF appoints a panel of former top players at the start of the season, which votes on the champion at the end of the season.

The boys' and girls' singles and doubles titles prior to 2003 were awarded based on world ranking. Since then singles and doubles rankings have been combined in a single award each for boys and for girls.<ref>ITF Constitution, Appendix E, pp.53, 60</ref>

The world champion accolade has been extended by the ITF to wheelchair tennis players of the Men's and Women's division since 1991. In November 2017, the ITF announced that the quad wheelchair tennis division is to be recognised in its annual list of ITF World Champions.<ref>http://www.itftennis.com/276840?channel=itfwheelchairtennisnews</ref>

In 1996, the [[Philippe Chatrier Award]] was introduced, honouring individuals or organisations who have made outstanding contributions to tennis globally, both on and off the court. The award is considered to be the ITF's highest accolade and is named after the former French tennis player Philippe Chatrier, who was President of the governing body between 1977-1991.<ref name="Philippe">{{cite news | last1=Gillen |first1=Nancy | title=Santana and Stolle receive ITF Philippe Chatrier Award | url=https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1092706/santana-stolle-philippe-chatrier-award |accessdate=15 July 2020 | work=Inside the Games | date=1 April 2020}}</ref>

The ITF World Champions' Dinner takes place in Paris during the [[French Open (tennis)|French Open]], to honour the previous year's champions,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/about/world-champions/champions-dinner.aspx|title=World Champions Dinner|year=2013|publisher=ITF|accessdate=20 December 2013}}</ref> who are presented with a trophy, but not any monetary prize.<ref name="stp19790117"/>

name="age19860121">{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=j8wzAAAAIBAJ&sjid=U5UDAAAAIBAJ&pg=1953%2C3476736|title=All the awards, plus a rebuke|last=Fogarty|first=Mark|date=21 January News Archive]]|page=4D|accessdate=20 December 2013}}</ref>

The 1990 designation of Lendl as champion was a surprise.<ref name="ap19901218"/>  That year, the [[Association of Tennis Professionals]] named [[Stefan Edberg]] its "Player of The Year", in accordance with the [[ATP Rankings]], while ''Tennis Magazine'' (France) ranked Edberg first, [[Andre Agassi]] second, and Lendl third.<ref name="tennisfr1991"/> ''Tennis'' also suggested the ITF was punishing Edberg for denigrating the [[Grand Slam Cup]] tournament it had introduced.<ref name="tennisfr1991">{{cite journal |journal=Tennis |location=France |date=February 1991 |issue=179 |quote=On' a choisi de sanctionner un champion qui n'avait pas craint d'avouer publiquement le peu d'importance qu'il accordait à la Coupe du Grand Chelem, la fameuse invention de la FIT pour 'casser' l'ATP Tour. (It was decided to punish a champion who was not afraid to admit publicly how little he thought of the Grand Slam Cup, the famous ITF invention to 'break' the ATP Tour) |language=French}}</ref> The ITF panel, of Perry, Trabert, and [[Frank Sedgman]], called it "the toughest decision any of us can remember having to make", and stated it was Lendl's better average performance in the Grand Slams that made the difference.<ref name="ap19901218">{{cite news |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=V5MrAAAAIBAJ&sjid=stAFAAAAIBAJ&pg=3224%2C1916947 |title=Lendl, not Edberg, named tennis world champion |agency=Associated Press |date=18 December 1990 |work=[[Moscow-Pullman Daily News]] |publisher=Google News Archive |page=1C |accessdate=20 December 2013}}</ref>

The choice to award Djokovic the ITF World Champion of 2013 over Nadal was unexpected. Nadal finished the year ranked #1 and with more slams (2 to 1), more Masters titles (5 to 3), and more tournament titles (10 to 7).  Similar to the situation with Edberg in 1990, the ITF cited Nadal's failure to win a match at 2 of the 4 slams (DNP the Australian Open, 1st round loss at Wimbledon) to justify their decision and Djokovic’s consistent results across all four Slams (1 title, 2 runner-ups, 1 SF), Davis Cup (led Serbia to final, won 7/7 singles rubbers) and the ATP World Tour Finals (won title).<ref name="2013champions">{{cite news |url=https://www.itftennis.com/news/163361.aspx |title=Williams and Djokovic named 2013 ITF World Champion |date=18 December 2013  |publisher= ITF }}</ref>

Other instances when the ITF choices differed from the ATP rankings are 1978 ([[Jimmy Connors]]), 1982 (McEnroe), 1989 (Lendl).   None of these, however, were controversial and were generally agreed upon, with the 1978 and 1982 choices being particularly clear cut in favor of Borg (1978) and Connors (1982).
