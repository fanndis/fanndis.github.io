{{main|2002 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Serena Williams]] defeated the two-time defending champion, her sister [[Venus Williams|Venus]], in the final, 7–6<sup>(7–4)</sup>, 6–3 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2002 Wimbledon Championships. <ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> Serena claimed the World No. 1 ranking for the first time after winning this tournament. She also became the first player since [[Steffi Graf]] in 1996 to win the 'Channel Slam' (winning the French Open and Wimbledon in the same year), a feat which she accomplished again in [[2015 Wimbledon Championships - Women's Singles|2015]]. Serena did not drop a set throughout the entire tournament.
