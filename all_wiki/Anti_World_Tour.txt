The tour was announced on 15th November 2015 and began on March 12, 2016, in [[Jacksonville, Florida|Jacksonville]] and ended on November 27, 2016, in [[Abu Dhabi]], [[United Arab Emirates]]. It took place in North America, Europe and Asia with a total of.

It was the twenty-second-highest-grossing tour of 2016 in North America, according to ''[[Pollstar]]''{{'}}s annual Year End Top 200 North American Tours chart, grossing $40.3 million. The tour in the US sold 454,955 tickets in 41 shows. The tour earned a total of $110 million worldwide.

== Background ==
After the release of her seventh studio album, ''[[Unapologetic]]'', (2012) and her fifth concert tour, the [[Diamonds World Tour]], Rihanna went on a hiatus. Between 2005 and 2012, Rihanna released or re-released at least one album a year. However, in 2013, Rihanna took a step back from music and did not release a new album that year or the following.<ref name=nmeinterview>{{cite web|title=Rihanna Exclusive: I Break the Rules Even When I Don't Intend To|url=http://www.nme.com/features/rihanna-exclusive-interview-i-break-the-rules-even-when-i-dont-intend-to|work=[[NME]]|date=October 7, 2015|accessdate=October 8, 2015}}</ref> Instead Rihanna opted to take part in other endeavors, starring in a 3D animated film entitled ''[[Home (2015 animated film)|Home]]'', alongside [[Jim Parsons]], [[Steve Martin]] and [[Jennifer Lopez]], as well as executively producing its accompanying soundtrack.<ref>{{cite news|last=Graser|first=Marc|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/news/rihanna-to-make-concept-album-for-dreamworks-animations-home-1201120819/|title=Rihanna to Make Concept Album for Upcoming Animated Movie|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=February 25, 2014|accessdate=April 23, 2014}}</ref>

In November 2015, it was announced that Rihanna had signed a $25 million contract with Samsung to not only promote Samsung's Galaxy line of products, but to also sponsor the release of ''Anti'' and its supporting tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2015/10/29/rihanna-inks-25m-sponsorship-deal-with-samsung/|title=Rihanna inks $25M sponsorship deal with Samsung|work=New York Post|accessdate=November 17, 2015}}</ref> On November 23, 2015, Rihanna announced she would be embarking on "The Anti World Tour".<ref name="Tour dates">{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/rihanna-details-massive-anti-world-tour-20151123 |title=Rihanna Details Massive 'Anti' World Tour |last=Kreps |first=Daniel |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=November 23, 2015 |accessdate=November 23, 2015}}</ref> The [[Samsung]]-sponsored tour began in March 2016, with [[Travis Scott]] supporting in North America, and [[Big Sean]] and [[DJ Mustard]] supporting at selected European dates.<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=Rihanna |number=668855691612958720 |date=November 23, 2015 |title=Rihanna: Anti World Tour|accessdate=November 26, 2015 }}</ref>

==Critical reception==
[[File:Rihanna.png|Rihanna in Warsaw on August 5, 2016|thumb]]

===North America===
In a review for ''[[The New York Times]]'', Jon Caramanica stated that Rihanna was at her "most confident" and "the most present she's ever been onstage", calling her dancing "casual" and that she "appeared to be finding joy in singing — when she was doing it."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/29/arts/music/rihanna-anti-tour.html?_r=0 |title=Review: Rihanna on Tour, Part Preacher, Part Dominatrix, All Human |last=Caramanica|first=Jon|date= March 28, 2016|work=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=April 1, 2017}}</ref> Reviewing the Los Angeles concert, Latifah Muhammad from ''[[VIBE]]'' described the tour as a "randomly epic road trip."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2016/05/review-rihanna-anti-world-tour-los-angeles/ |title=Review: Rihanna's 'ANTI' World Tour Is Something You Have To See To Believe |last=Muhammad |first=Latifah|date= May 5, 2016|work=[[VIBE]]|accessdate=April 1, 2017}}</ref>

===Europe===
Ed Power from ''[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]'' gave Dublin concert four stars, writing that the tour "didn't waste time on embellishments and instead delivered full-strength shots of sass and escapism."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/what-to-listen-to/rihanna-anti-world-tour-dublin-review--a-slick-sleek-arena-block/ |title=Rihanna, Anti World Tour, Dublin, review: 'A slick, sleek arena blockbuster delivered full-strength shots of sass and escapism' |last=Power |first=Ed |date= 22 June 2016|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|accessdate=April 1, 2017}}</ref> The London concert also received four stars from ''[[The Guardian]]'', where Michael Cragg praised the singer's "unshakeable Rihanna-ness; that perfect pop voice, the undeniable presence, that couldn't-give-a-shit attitude."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jun/24/rihanna-review-wembley-stadium-anti |title=Rihanna review – like watching a different artist}}</ref>
