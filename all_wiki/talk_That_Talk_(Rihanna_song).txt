"'''Talk That Talk'''" is a song recorded by Barbadian singer [[Rihanna]] for her 2011 [[Talk That Talk|studio album of the same name]]. It features a [[Rapping|rap verse]] by American [[rapping|rapper]] [[Jay-Z]], who had previously collaborated with Rihanna on her song "[[Umbrella (song)|Umbrella]]" in 2007 and "[[Run This Town]]" in 2009. The song was written by Jay-Z, [[Ester Dean]], [[The Notorious B.I.G.|Christopher Wallace]], [[Buckwild|Anthony Best]], [[Sean Combs]], and [[Chucky Thompson]] together with the Norwegian production duo [[Stargate (production team)|StarGate]]. [[Def Jam Recordings]] serviced the track to [[urban radio]] in the United States on January 17, 2012, as the third single from ''Talk That Talk''. It was released in France as a [[CD single]] on March 26. "Talk That Talk" is a [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] song with [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] beats, rough drums and unrefined [[Synthesizer|synths]], and has a similar style to Rihanna's 2010 single "[[Rude Boy (Rihanna song)|Rude Boy]]".

The single was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration]] at the [[2013 Grammy Awards|2013 ceremony]]. The song appeared on several charts worldwide; it reached number 31 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], number 25 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], and the top ten in Israel, Norway, and South Korea. It was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), denoting [[Music download|digital downloads]] of over one million copies in the US. Rihanna performed "Talk That Talk" on television shows such as ''[[The Jonathan Ross Show]]'' and ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'', and included it on the [[set list]]s of the 2013 [[Diamonds World Tour|Diamonds]] and the 2014 [[The Monster Tour (Eminem and Rihanna)|The Monster Tour]] with [[Eminem]].

==Background==
[[File:Jay-Z-02-mika.jpg|left|thumb|upright|alt=A black-and-white picture of an African-American man wearing a white shirt, trousers, a cap, and a wristwatch|"Talk That Talk" features rapper [[Jay-Z]], who also co-wrote the song.]]

Before her sixth studio album ''[[Talk That Talk]]'' was released in November 2011, Rihanna announced on her [[Twitter]] account that, apart from the song "[[We Found Love]]" with [[Calvin Harris]], the album would have only one other featured artist, although she did not mention the artist's name.<ref name="RapUp1">{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2011/11/07/jay-z-reunites-with-rihanna-on-talk-that-talk/#more-103469|title=Jay-Z Reunites with Rihanna on 'Talk That Talk'|work=[[Rap-Up]]|date=November 7, 2011|accessdate=November 14, 2011}}</ref> Jay Brown, Rihanna's manager, explained that they preferred to record independently rather than collaborate with other artists.<ref name="Spy">{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/thesound/a350264/rihannas-new-album-talk-that-talk-first-listen.html|title=Rihanna's new album 'Talk That Talk': First listen|work= [[Digital Spy]]|last=Copsey|first=Robert|date=November 10, 2011|accessdate=November 14, 2011}}</ref> On November 8, Rihanna confirmed on Twitter that her mentor, American rapper [[Jay-Z]], would appear as a featured artist on the album's title track.<ref name="MTV1">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1673976/rihanna-jay-z-talk-that-talk.jhtml|title=Rihanna Confirms Jay-Z Talk That Talk Cameo|publisher= [[MTV News]]|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|date=November 8, 2011|accessdate=November 14, 2011}}</ref> Brown said that the collaboration "happened organically".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/news/rihanna/337117-rihanna-talk-that-talk-first-listen-jay-z-calvin-harris|title=Rihanna – 'Talk That Talk' First Listen! |publisher= [[MTV UK]]|last=Dorken|first=Joanne|date=November 10, 2011|accessdate=November 16, 2011}}</ref>

"Talk That Talk" is the third major collaboration between Rihanna and Jay-Z, who had worked together on "[[Umbrella (song)|Umbrella]]" in 2007 and "[[Run This Town]]" in 2009.<ref name="MTV1"/> "Umbrella" was the lead single from Rihanna's 2007 album ''[[Good Girl Gone Bad (Rihanna album)|Good Girl Gone Bad]]'' and topped the charts in more than ten countries, including the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], on which it spent seven consecutive weeks at number one.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1050760/plain-white-ts-finally-unseats-rihanna-on-hot-100|title=Plain White T's Finally Unseats Rihanna On Hot 100|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate= November 14, 2011}}</ref> "Run This Town", which also featured rapper [[Kanye West]], was released as the second single from Jay-Z's 2009 album ''[[The Blueprint 3]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1616871/20090724/jay_z.jhtml|title=Jay-Z's 'Run This Town' Makes Radio Debut|publisher=MTV News|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=July 24, 2009|accessdate=November 14, 2011}}</ref>

==Production and release==
"Talk That Talk" was written by [[Ester Dean]], Jay-Z, [[Stargate (production team)|StarGate]], [[Buckwild|Anthony Best]], [[Sean Combs]], and [[Chucky Thompson]], and produced by StarGate.<ref name="Booklet"/> They had produced Rihanna's 2010 hit singles "[[Only Girl (In the World)]]" and "[[What's My Name? (Rihanna song)|What's My Name?]]" for her fifth album ''[[Loud (Rihanna album)|Loud]]''.<ref>{{cite AV media notes |title=''[[Loud (Rihanna album)|Loud]]'' |others=[[Rihanna]] |year=2010 |type=liner notes |publisher=Def Jam, SRP}}</ref> StarGate told Norwegian website 730.no that it was their first collaboration with Jay-Z and said that they were very satisfied with both the song and each artist's contribution.<ref name="RapUp1"/> "Talk That Talk" was recorded at Roc the Mic Studios and The Jungle City Studios in New York City, [[Westlake Recording Studios]] in Los Angeles, and The Hide Out Studios in London. StarGate, Miles Walker, and Mike Anderson served as the song's [[Audio engineer|recording engineer]]s. Rihanna's vocals were recorded by Marcos Tovar and [[Kuk Harrell]], who additionally produced them, while Jordan "DJ Swivel" Young recorded Jay-Z's verses. Additional recording of the song was done in Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg and [[Savoy Hotel|Savoy London]] hotels. "Talk That Talk" was [[Mixing (music production)|mixed]] by [[Phil Tan]] and assistant Daniela Rivera at Ninja Beat Club Studios in [[Atlanta]]. Eriksen and Hermansen recorded the song's instrumentation, and Tim Blacksmith and Danny D. were assigned as its [[executive producer]]s.<ref name="Booklet"/>

==Commercial performance==
After the release of the album, the song charted in many countries owing to strong digital sales. It debuted and peaked at number 31 on the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and sold 73,000 digital copies, which was the highest debut on the chart for that week.<ref name="US debut"/> In the week of January 28, 2012, "Talk That Talk" re-entered the chart at number 100.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2012-01-28?rank=100|title=The Hot 100: Week of January 28, 2012|work=Billboard|accessdate=May 19, 2020}}</ref> It reached number 36 in the week of March 24.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2012-03-24?rank=36|title=The Hot 100: Week of March 24, 2012|work=Billboard|accessdate=May 19, 2020}}</ref> The song also appeared on the [[R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart at number 63 in the issue dated January 28.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2012-01-28/r-b-hip-hop-songs?order=gainer|title=Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs: Jan 28, 2012 – (Biggest Jump)|work=Billboard|accessdate=August 21, 2013}}</ref> It peaked of number 12 and stayed on the chart for 21 weeks.<ref name="US R&B"/> It was ranked number 73 on the [[Billboard Year-End|''Billboard''{{'}}s year-end]] R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart.<ref name="year end chart"/> On the [[Pop Songs]] chart, the single debuted and peaked at number 26 in the week of April 7.<ref name="pop"/> It was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), denoting digital downloads of 1 million copies in the United States.<ref name="riaa"/> "Talk That Talk" debuted and peaked at number 30 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] chart.<ref name="Canada and Japan"/>

In Europe, after the release of the album, the song appeared on ten national charts. In the United Kingdom, it debuted at number 25 on the [[UK Singles Chart|Singles Chart]],<ref name="UK"/> and reached number seven on the [[UK R&B Chart|R&B Chart]].<ref name="UK R&B"/> The song made a top-ten debut on the [[VG-lista|Norwegian Singles Chart]] and charted at number 11 on the [[Swiss Hitparade|Swiss Singles Chart]].<ref name="NOR"/> It also made top-forty appearances in the Republic of Ireland,<ref name="IRE"/> New Zealand,<ref name="NZ"/> and Spain, and also entered the top thirty in Scotland,<ref name="SCO"/> France,<ref name="FRA"/> and Denmark.<ref name="DEN"/> After its release as a single, "Talk That Talk" entered the [[ARIA Charts|Australian Singles Chart]] at number 42 and peaked at number 28 on February 19.<ref name="AUS"/> It was certified platinum by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA) for selling 70,000 digital copies.<ref name="ausgold"/> Following the release of the album, the song debuted in South Korea on the [[Gaon Chart|Gaon International Chart]] at number 22 with sales of 14,207 digital copies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/digital_chart/download.php?nationGbn=E&current_week=49&current_year=2011&chart_Time=week |title=South Korea Gaon International Chart (Week: November 20, 2011 to November 26, 2011) |publisher=[[Gaon Chart]] |accessdate=August 21, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140104204252/http://gaonchart.co.kr/digital_chart/download.php?nationGbn=E |archivedate=January 4, 2014 }}</ref> The following week, it sold 27,169 more copies and peaked at number 9 on the chart.<ref name="korea"/>

She is wearing silky black outfit and has a golden chain on her neck.|Rihanna performing "Talk That Talk" on the [[Diamonds World Tour]] (2013)]]
Rihanna first performed "Talk That Talk" on the British talk show ''[[The Jonathan Ross Show]]'', which aired on March 3, 2012. She performed without Jay-Z and was also interviewed.<ref name="Jonathan">{{cite web|url=http://www.capitalfm.com/artists/rihanna/news/talk-that-talk-live-video/|title=Rihanna Performs 'Talk That Talk' Live On Jonathan Ross Show – Video|publisher=Capital FM|date=March 4, 2012|accessdate=August 29, 2013}}</ref> On May 5, Rihanna performed the song on the American comedy show ''Saturday Night Live'' as part of a medley that included the original interlude version of "[[Birthday Cake (song)|Birthday Cake]]".<ref name="SNL2012">{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2012/05/05/rihanna-performs-on-snl/|title=Rihanna performs on 'SNL'|work=Rap-Up|accessdate=June 12, 2012}}</ref> In her performance, she wore an all-black outfit with a giant spider web as her backdrop.<ref name="SNL2012"/> She sang a short section of "Birthday Cake", which transitioned into "Talk That Talk", of which the latter was performed in its entirety.<ref name="SNL2012"/> Rihanna and Jay-Z performed "Talk That Talk" together at the 2012 BBC Radio 1's [[Hackney Weekend]] in London. The performance also featured their previous collaborations "Run This Town" and "Umbrella".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/18575052|title=Rihanna and Jay-Z make 'history' at R1 Hackney Weekend|publisher=BBC|last=Cochrane|first=Greg|date=June 25, 2012|accessdate=September 18, 2012}}</ref> In November, she performed the song on the [[set list]] of her [[Unapologetic#777 Tour|777 Tour]], a seven-day long promotional tour that supported the release of her 2012 album ''[[Unapologetic]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ramirez|first=Erika|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/474107/rihannas-777-tour-diary-day-four-paris|title=Rihanna's 777 Tour Diary: Day Four, Paris|work=Billboard|date=November 18, 2012|accessdate=August 19, 2013}}</ref> "Talk That Talk" was also included on the set list of her 2013 [[Diamonds World Tour]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mercurynews.com/music/ci_22974451/setlist-rihanna-san-jose|title=Setlist: Rihanna in San Jose|newspaper=[[Mercury News]]|last=Harrington|first=Jim|date=April 6, 2013|accessdate=May 2, 2013}}</ref> Rihanna performed the song on her joint tour with rapper [[Eminem]], titled [[The Monster Tour (Eminem and Rihanna)|The Monster Tour]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6214202/eminem-rihanna-monster-tour-rose-bowl-concert-review|title=Eminem & Rihanna's Monster Tour Roars to Life at Rose Bowl: Concert Review|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Steve|last=Baltin|date=August 8, 2014|accessdate=August 8, 2014}}</ref>
