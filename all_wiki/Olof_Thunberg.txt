'''Fritz-Olof Thunberg''' (21 May 1925&nbsp;– 24 February 2020) was a [[Sweden|Swedish]] actor, voice actor and director, perhaps best known as the voice of the cartoon character [[Bamse]].

==Life and career==
Thunberg was born Fritz-Olof Thunberg in [[Västerås]], where he formed a theatre club called Scenklubben which also included [[Lars Ekborg]] and the poet [[Bo Setterlind]]. He studied at Calle Flygare's drama school in [[Stockholm]] and then from 1950 to 1952 at [[Dramatens elevskola]]; between the two, in the late 1940s, he performed in public parks.<ref name=film>[http://www.sfi.se/sv/svensk-filmdatabas/Item/?type=PERSON&itemid=64238 "Olof Thunberg"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170925051918/http://www.sfi.se/sv/svensk-filmdatabas/Item/?type=PERSON&itemid=64238 |date=25 September 2017 }}, [[Swedish Film Database]], 2004, updated 16 December 2013 {{in lang|sv}}.</ref>

After finishing drama school, Thunberg joined the [[Östgötateatern]] regional theatre, where he directed in addition to acting, then became a freelance actor.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/BRE1Vw|title=Olof Thunberg död – gjorde rösten till Bamse|website=Aftonbladet|accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> In the 1950s he became known for his work on the radio programme ''Mannen i svart'' ("The Man in Black"), where he presented all kinds of stories of horror and the supernatural in his deep voice, accompanied by a variety of sound effects.<ref name=film /> In 1963, he had a supporting role in [[Ingmar Bergman]]'s drama ''[[Winter Light]]'' as the church organist Fredrik.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/skadespelaren-olof-thunberg-ar-dod/|title=Skådespelaren Olof Thunberg är död|date=25 February 2020|website=Dagens Nyheter|accessdate=25 February 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200227172412/https://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/skadespelaren-olof-thunberg-ar-dod/|archive-date=27 February 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

Thunberg appeared in a variety of films, often in a supporting role as a confident charmer, and is the voice of both [[Bamse]] and [[Agaton Sax]] in children's films and recordings.<ref name=film /> He also voiced [[Shere Khan]] in the Swedish-dubbed versions of both the 1967 [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]] ''[[The Jungle Book (1967 film)|The Jungle Book]]'' and its 2003 sequel, ''[[The Jungle Book 2]]''.<ref name="auto" />

In 2006, Thunberg was a host of ''[[Sommar (radio program)|Sommar]]'' on [[Sveriges Radio P1]].<ref>[http://sverigesradio.se/sida/avsnitt/364603?programid=2071 Olof Thunberg] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150327064316/http://sverigesradio.se/sida/avsnitt/364603?programid=2071 |date=27 March 2015 }}, Sommer & Vinter i P1, 6 August 2006 {{in lang|sv}}.</ref>

Thunberg also directed for television.<ref name=film />

===Personal life===
[[File:Familjen Thunberg i Allas 1967.jpg|thumb|right|Olof Thunberg with Mona Andersson and their first-born daughter Amanda at their home in [[Värmdö (island)|Värmdö]], 1967.]]
Thunberg was previously married to Ingrid Johansson.<ref name="SVDB" /> He had a later [[cohabitation]] with actress {{Interlanguage link|Mona Andersson|sv|Mona Andersson}}, whom he got to know in connection with a radio play.<ref name="ALLSVNG">{{Cite magazine|title=Allas Veckotidning|trans-title=All Weekly|publisher=Allers förlag|location=Stockholm|date=1967|page=11|language=sv|issue=46}}</ref> He was the father of actor Svante Thunberg<ref name="SVDB1">{{cite news|title=Svenska Dagbladet: "Svensk" Shakespeare i parkerna|trans-title=Swedish Daily News: "Swedish" Shakespeare in the parks|url=https://www.svd.se/arkiv/1983-06-08/11|first=Gaby|last=Wigardt|newspaper=[[Svenska Dagbladet]]|location=Stockholm|date=8 June 1983|page=11|language=sv|access-date=6 October 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191006214936/https://www.svd.se/arkiv/1983-06-08/11|archive-date=6 October 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="ALLSVNG1">{{cite news|title=Expressen: 4 × Thunberg = Shakespeare|trans-title=The Express: 4 × Thunberg = Shakespeare|first=Stefan|last=Sjöström|newspaper=[[Expressen]]|location=Stockholm|date=11 July 1983|page=24|language=sv}}</ref> and father-in-law of opera singer [[Malena Ernman]], from whom his granddaughter is the climate activist [[Greta Thunberg]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.expressen.se/noje/olof-thunberg-ar-dod-gjorde-rosten-till-bamse/|title=Olof Thunberg är död – gjorde rösten till Bamse|website=www.expressen.se|accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref>

In 1959, Thunberg became engaged to actress [[Lena Granhagen]], whom he had met while directing a play in which she acted in at [[Intiman]].<ref name="DV 1961:17 s 20">{{Harvnb|Myrberg|1961|p=20}}.</ref>{{Full citation needed|date=May 2020}} They lived together into the 1960s, and they acted together as a couple in the television series ''Villervalle i Söderhavet'' in 1963.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dt.se/artikel/skadespelaren-olof-thunberg-ar-dod-gav-rost-at-bamse-och-blev-farfar-at-greta-thunberg-1|title=Skådespelaren Olof Thunberg är död – gav röst åt Bamse och blev farfar åt Greta Thunberg|date=25 February 2020|website=dt.se|accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref>

Thunberg died on 24 February 2020 at the age of 94.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.svt.se/kultur/skadespelaren-olof-thunberg-ar-dod|title=Skådespelaren Olof Thunberg är död|date=25 February 2020|website=SVT Nyheter|accessdate=25 February 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200225224702/https://www.svt.se/kultur/skadespelaren-olof-thunberg-ar-dod|archive-date=25 February 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dw.com/en/greta-thunbergs-grandfather-veteran-swedish-actor-olof-thunberg-dies/a-52534367|title=Greta Thunberg's grandfather, veteran Swedish actor Olof Thunberg, dies|date=25 February 2020|publisher=dw.com|accessdate=2 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200227001809/https://www.dw.com/en/greta-thunbergs-grandfather-veteran-swedish-actor-olof-thunberg-dies/a-52534367|archive-date=27 February 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>
