{{main article|2014 US Open (tennis)}}
[[Serena Williams]] was the two-time defending champion and successfully defended her title, defeating [[Caroline Wozniacki]] in the final, 6–3, 6–3 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2014 US Open (tennis)|2014 US Open]]. Williams did not lose a set in the entire tournament and did not lose more than three games in any set. This was Williams' 18th [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles title, tying the number won by [[Martina Navratilova]] and [[Chris Evert]]. With the victory, Serena tied the [[Open Era]] record of six US Open titles held by Evert. This was Williams' third US Open championship that she competed in [[List_of_Grand_Slam_related_tennis_records#Won_a_title_without_losing_a_set|without losing a set]], also tying Evert.<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2014/sep/07/serena-williams-caroline-wozniacki-us-open-2014-sixth-title</ref>

This tournament was the Grand Slam debut of [[CiCi Bellis]], who became the youngest player to win a match at the US Open since 1996 (and the youngest American to do so since 1986) when she defeated the twelfth seed [[Dominika Cibulková]] in the first round at the age of 15.<ref>{{cite web|title=American Bellis, 15, upsets Cibulkova at US Open|url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/2014/08/26/3347582_american-bellis-15-upsets-cibulkova.html?rh=1|publisher=The News Tribune|accessdate=27 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140903053055/http://www.thenewstribune.com/2014/08/26/3347582_american-bellis-15-upsets-cibulkova.html?rh=1|archive-date=2014-09-03|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.si.com/tennis/2014/08/26/what-you-need-know-about-catherine-cici-bellis|title=What you need to know about Catherine 'CiCi' Bellis|accessdate=2014-08-29|date=2014-08-26|author=Nguyen, Courtney|work=[[Sports Illustrated]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2014/sep/07/serena-williams-caroline-wozniacki-us-open-2014-sixth-title|title= Serena Williams powers past Caroline Wozniacki for sixth title|date=9 September 2014|work=Guardian |accessdate=10 September 2014}}</ref>

All three top seeds (Williams, [[Simona Halep]] and [[Petra Kvitová]]) were in contention for the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked players|World No. 1 ranking]] at the start of the tournament. Williams retained the top position after Halep and Kvitová both lost in the third round.

This was the last time when 8 different women contested in the four grand slam finals of a calendar year.
