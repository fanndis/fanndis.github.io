'''Celeste Ng''' ({{zh|t=伍綺詩}}) (born July 30, 1980)<ref>{{cite web |title=Celeste Ng - Knowledge & Wisdom |url=https://newengland.com/yankee-magazine/living/profiles/celeste-ng-knowledge-wisdom/ |website=newengland.com |publisher=Yankee Publishing, Inc. |accessdate=2020-03-18}}</ref> is an American writer and novelist.<ref name="Hoby">{{cite web|title=Amazon book of the year winner Celeste Ng: ‘Writing’s like shouting into the world’|first=Hermione|last=Hoby|date=November 17, 2014|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2014/nov/17/amazon-book-of-the-year-winner-celeste-ng-writing-is-like-shouting-into-the-world|work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|work=[[NPR]]|title='Everything I Never Told You' Exposed in Biracial Family's Loss|date=June 28, 2014|author=NPR Staff|url=http://www.npr.org/blogs/codeswitch/2014/06/28/325927002/everything-i-never-told-you-exposed-in-biracial-familys-loss}}</ref><ref name="Connors">{{cite web|work=[[The Plain Dealer]]|title=Writer Celeste Ng talks about growing up in Shaker Heights and her buzz novel of the summer, ''Everything I Never Told You''|first=Joanna|last=Connors|date=July 9, 2014|url=http://www.cleveland.com/books/index.ssf/2014/07/writer_celeste_ng_talks_about.html|publisher=Cleveland.com}}</ref> She has released many short stories that have been published in a variety of literary journals.<ref name=celeste /> Ng's first novel, ''[[Everything I Never Told You]]'', released on June 26, 2014, won the Amazon Book of the Year award,<ref name=Hoby /> as well as praise from critics. Ng's short story ''Girls at Play'' won a Pushcart Prize in 2012, and was a 2015 recipient of the [[Alex Awards]]. Her most recent novel is ''[[Little Fires Everywhere (novel)|Little Fires Everywhere]]''. She received a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gf.org/fellows/all-fellows/celeste-ng/|title=Guggenheim Fellowship in 2020|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Celeste Ng was born in [[Pittsburgh]], [[Pennsylvania]].<ref name="Hoby"/> Her parents moved from [[Hong Kong]] in the late 1960s.<ref name="Hoby"/> Her father, who died in 2004, was a physicist at [[NASA]] in the [[John H. Glenn Research Center]] (formerly known as the NASA Lewis Research Center).<ref name="Connors"/> Her mother was a chemist who taught at [[Cleveland State University]].<ref name="Connors"/>

When Ng was ten, she moved from Pittsburgh to [[Shaker Heights, Ohio]]<ref name="Connors"/> with her parents and sister.<ref name="Politics and Prose"/> She attended the schools in the [[Shaker Heights City School District]], from Woodbury Elementary all the way up to [[Shaker Heights High School]].<ref name="Connors"/> At Shaker Heights High School, Ng was involved with the student group on race relations for three years and a co-editor of the school's literary magazine, ''Semanteme''.<ref name="Connors"/> She graduated from high school in 1998.

After graduating from high school, Ng studied English at [[Harvard University]]. She then attended graduate school at [[University of Michigan]], where she earned her [[Master of Fine Arts]] in writing, now the Helen Zell Writers’ Program. At the University of Michigan, Ng won the [[Hopwood Award]] for her short story "What Passes Over".<ref name="Connors"/><ref>{{cite web|title="What Passes Over" by Celeste Ng|url=http://www.one-story.com/index.php?page=stories&story_id=86|website=One story|accessdate=20 August 2015}}</ref>

Ng lives in [[Cambridge, Massachusetts]], with her husband and son.<ref name="Connors"/> While on a book tour for ''Everything I Never Told You'', Ng said her favorite book when she was little was ''[[Harriet the Spy]]''.<ref name="Politics and Prose"/> Her favorite book is now ''[[The God of Small Things]]'' by [[Arundhati Roy]].<ref name="Connors"/>

==Career==
Ng received the [[Pushcart Prize]] in 2012 for her story "Girls, At Play".<ref>{{cite web|title=Girls, At Play|first=Ronna|last=Wineberg|url=http://blr.med.nyu.edu/content/editors-picks/girlsatplay|work=[[Bellevue Literary Review]]}}</ref> Her fiction has appeared in ''[[One Story]]'', ''[[TriQuarterly]]'', and ''[[Subtropics (journal)|Subtropics]]''. Her essays have appeared in ''Kenyon Review Online'', ''[[The Millions]]'', and elsewhere. Ng taught writing at the University of Michigan and at [[Grub Street, Inc.|Grub Street]] in [[Boston]].<ref name=Dreifus>{{cite web|last1=Dreifus|first1=Erika|title=Everything She Graciously Told Me: An Interview with Celeste Ng|url=http://www.erikadreifus.com/resources/interviews/everything-she-graciously-told-me-an-interview-with-celeste-ng/|website=erika dreifus|accessdate=6 August 2015}}</ref> Ng also was an editor of blogs at the website [[Fiction Writers Review]] for three years.<ref name="Dreifus"/>

Her debut novel, ''Everything I Never Told You: A Novel'', is a literary thriller that focuses on an American family in 1970s Ohio.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sunday Book Review, The Leftovers 'Everything I Never Told You,' by Celeste Ng|authorlink=Alexander Chee|first=Alexander|last=Chee|date=August 15, 2014|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2014/08/17/books/review/everything-i-never-told-you-by-celeste-ng.html|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Book Review 'Everything I Never Told You' by Celeste Ng|first=Clea|last=Simon|date=July 1, 2014|url=http://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/books/2014/07/01/book-review-everything-never-told-you-celeste/NegiY6oApUZG71YIRq6olM/story.html|work=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref> The novel had four drafts and one revision before completion, which took 6 years. In working on it, Ng said she drew on her own experiences of racism as well as her family and friends.<ref name=Schlupe>{{cite web|last1=Schluep|first1=Chris|title=CelesteNgBEA|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DzSw7GfYhbo|website=youtube}}</ref> The book, which the [[Los Angeles Times]] called an "excellent first novel about family, love, and ambition," won Amazon book of the year in 2014<ref name="Hoby"/><ref name=LAT>{{cite web|title=Review 'Everything I Never Told You' a moving tale of a dysfunctional family|first=Hector|last=Tobar|url=http://touch.latimes.com/#section/-1/article/p2p-80707087/|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=July 4, 2014}}</ref> and was a ''New York Times'' Notable Book of 2014.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ng |first1=Celeste |title=PRAISE FOR EVERYTHING I NEVER TOLD YOU |url=https://www.celesteng.com/praise-einty |website=Celeste Ng}}</ref> It has been translated into 15 languages.<ref name=celeste />

Ng's second novel, ''Little Fires Everywhere'', is set in Shaker Heights, Ohio, and follows two families, one a mother and daughter, that challenge the boundaries in the town.<ref name="Politics and Prose">{{cite web|author=Politics and Prose|title=Celeste Ng, "Everything I Never Told You"|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AQUttMxMjqo|website=youtube|accessdate=6 August 2015}}</ref><ref name="Schlupe"/> The novel was developed into a 2020 [[Hulu]] [[Little Fires Everywhere (miniseries)|miniseries of the same name]] starring and executive produced by [[Reese Witherspoon]] and [[Kerry Washington]]; Ng also serves as one of the show's producers.
