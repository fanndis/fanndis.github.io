'''Samsung Galaxy''' (stylised as '''SΛMSUNG Galaxy''' since 2015,<ref name="rebrand">{{Cite web |url=https://www.androidauthority.com/long-live-galaxy-brand-japan-604346/ |title=''Samsung's rebranding to Galaxy is total in Japan'' – AndroidAuthority on April 27th 2015 |access-date=25 June 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200627201355/https://www.androidauthority.com/long-live-galaxy-brand-japan-604346/ |archive-date=27 June 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref> previously stylised as '''Samsung GALAXY'''; abbreviated as '''SG''') is a series of [[computing]] and [[mobile computing]] devices that are designed, manufactured and marketed by [[Samsung Electronics]]. The product line includes the [[Samsung Galaxy S series]] of high-end [[smartphones]], the [[Samsung Galaxy Tab series]] of [[Tablet computer|tablets]], the [[Samsung Galaxy Note series]] of tablets and [[phablet]]s with the added functionality of a stylus, the foldable [[Samsung Galaxy Z series]], and [[smartwatch]]es including the first version of the [[Samsung Galaxy Gear]], with later versions dropping the Galaxy branding, until the release of the [[Samsung Galaxy Watch]] in 2018.

Samsung Galaxy devices use the [[Android (operating system)|Android operating system]] produced by [[Google]], usually with a custom user interface called [[One UI]] (with previous versions being known as [[Samsung Experience]] and [[TouchWiz]]). However, the [[Samsung Galaxy TabPro S|Galaxy TabPro S]] is the first Galaxy-branded [[Windows 10]] device that was announced in [[CES 2016]]. The Galaxy Watch is the first Galaxy-branded smartwatch since the release of later iterations of the Gear smartwatch from 2014 to 2017. In April 2019, the company announced that it would postpone the release of its folding smartphone, the [[Samsung Galaxy Fold]], days after several early reviewers said the screens on their devices had broken;<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-48013395|title=Broken screens delay Samsung Galaxy Fold launch|date=22 April 2019|access-date=23 April 2019|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190423114107/https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-48013395|archive-date=23 April 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> the Fold was rereleased in September 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.androidauthority.com/samsung-galaxy-fold-price-release-date-availability-956698/|title=Samsung Galaxy Fold: Price (it's a lot!), release date, availability (Updated)|date=5 September 2019|website=Android Authority|access-date=23 September 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190923051322/https://www.androidauthority.com/samsung-galaxy-fold-price-release-date-availability-956698/|archive-date=23 September 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Definitions ==

=== Categories ===

==== Smartphones ====
The flagship Galaxy smartphones are the [[Samsung Galaxy S series|Galaxy S]] and the [[Samsung Galaxy Note series |Galaxy Note]] series. The [[Samsung Galaxy Z series|Galaxy Z series]] are foldable smartphones. The [[Samsung Galaxy A series|Galaxy A series]] and [[Samsung Galaxy M series|Galaxy M series]] are mid-range and low-end/budget models. Other lines are discontinued.

=== Model numbers ===
Since September 2013,  model numbers of devices in the Samsung Galaxy series are in the "SM-xxxx" format (excluding the Galaxy J SC-02F&SGH-N075T). Previously, from 2009 until September 2013, the model numbers were in the "GT-xxxx" format.

* GT-Nnnn0 – mainstream Note model (Old type of model number)
* SM-Nnn0 – mainstream Note model (New type of model number)
* GT-Pnn00/10 – mainstream Tab model (Old type of model number)
* SM-Tnn0/1 – mainstream Tab model (New type of model number)
* GT-Snnn0 – mainstream model
* GT-Snnn2/ SM-Gnnn/DS <!---To avoid confusion, spaces added between S---> / SM-Gnnn/DD SM-Gnnn2 [[Samsung Galaxy Duos|Dual-SIM "Duos" model]]
* GT-Snnn5/GT-Nnnn5/GT-Pnnn5/GT-Innn5/SM-NnnnF/SM-Tnn5/SM-GnnnF – 4G/LTE model
* SM-Wnnn – [[Microsoft Windows|Windows]] model (i.e., [[Galaxy Book]])
* SM-Fnnn – Z series model

===Phones===

==== Samsung Galaxy Note Series  ====
{{main|Samsung Galaxy Note series}}
[[File:Galaxy Note 10 & Note 10+.jpg|thumb|250px|Samsung smartphones (front)]]
[[File:Samsung Galaxy Note 10.jpg|thumb|250px|Samsung smartphones (rear)]]
The Galaxy Note smartphones have been considered the first commercially successful examples of "phablets"  line is primarily oriented towards pen computing; all Galaxy Note models ship with a stylus pen and incorporate a pressure-sensitive Wacom digitizer. The [[Samsung Galaxy Note 20|Samsung Galaxy Note 20 and Note 20 Ultra]] are the latest smartphones under in this series, released in August 2020.

==== Samsung Galaxy S Series ====
{{main|Samsung Galaxy S series}}
The Galaxy S series is a line of high-end smartphones that has co-existed with the Galaxy Note line as being Samsung's flagship smartphones. The latest models are the [[Samsung Galaxy S20|Samsung Galaxy S20, S20+, and S20 Ultra]], released in February 2020.

==== Samsung Galaxy Z Series ====
{{main|Samsung Galaxy Z series}}
The Galaxy Z series is a line of high-end, foldable smartphones. The line started in 2019 with the [[Samsung Galaxy Fold]]. The latest phones from this series are the [[Samsung Galaxy Z Flip]] and the [[Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 2]], both released in 2020.

==== Samsung Galaxy A (Alpha) Series ====
{{main|Samsung Galaxy A series}}
The Galaxy A series (meaning Alpha) is a line of mid-range Android smartphones manufactured by [[Samsung Electronics]]. The Galaxy A series is similar to the high-end Galaxy S series, but with lower specifications and features.

==== Samsung Galaxy M (Millennial) Series ====
{{Main|Samsung Galaxy M series}}
The Galaxy M series is a line of online-exclusive, low-end phones; considered as the successor to the Galaxy J and Galaxy On series.

The [[Samsung Galaxy Tab S7|Samsung Galaxy Tab S7 and S7+]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techtalkhome.com/samsung-galaxy-tab-s7-price-specifications-and-feat/|title=Samsung Galaxy Tab S7 Price and Galaxy Tab S7 + Price}}</ref> are the latest devices, released in 5, August 2020. The Galaxy Tab S7+ also has 5G models that start at EUR 1,099 for the 6GB RAM + 128GB storage variant on the other hand, the 8GB RAM + 256GB storage is priced at EUR 1,179

{{main|Samsung Gear|Samsung Galaxy Watch series}}
Samsung announced the [[Samsung Galaxy Gear]], a smartwatch running Android 4.3, on 4 September 2013. The Galaxy Gear was Samsung's only smartwatch to feature "Galaxy" branding; later Samsung smartwatches use the [[Samsung Gear]] branding. The Gear series was later succeeded by the [[Samsung Galaxy Watch series]].

=="Over the Horizon"==
"Over the Horizon" is the trademark sound for Samsung smartphone devices. It was composed in 2011 by Joong-sam Yun and is the only music stored in the music library of any new Samsung smartphone. The sound appears as the default [[ringtone]], as well as the sound when the phone turns on or off, and as a notification sound. The [[Galaxy SII]] is the first phone to feature "Over the Horizon". While the basic composition of the six-note tune has not changed since its inception, various versions of different genres have been introduced as the product line evolved. Before the "Over the Horizon" slogan, "Beyond Samsung" was the only music file stored in the library, while "Samsung Tune" was used as the default ringtone. The sound has been covered by various popular artists who have released their own arrangements and remixes of the song, such as [[Quincy Jones]], [[Icona Pop]], and various [[K-Pop]] artists.<ref>{{cite news|work=Samsung Newsroom|title=Over the Horizon: The Evolution of the Samsung Galaxy Brand Sound|date=18 April 2015|url=https://news.samsung.com/global/over-the-horizon-the-evolution-of-the-samsung-galaxy-brand-sound|access-date=1 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160613140151/https://news.samsung.com/global/over-the-horizon-the-evolution-of-the-samsung-galaxy-brand-sound|archive-date=13 June 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> In Samsung's U.S. registration of the trademark for the sound, it is described as "the sound of a bell playing a B4 dotted eighth note, a B4 sixteenth note, an F#5 sixteenth note, a B5 sixteenth note, an A#5 eighth note, and an F#5 half note."<ref>Non-visual mark, ser. no. 85/376,579  (filed 20 July 2011), reg. no. [http://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=85376579&caseType=SERIAL_NO&searchType=statusSearch 4,361,853] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130511081924/http://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=85376579&caseType=SERIAL_NO&searchType=statusSearch |date=11 May 2013 }} (2 July 2013).
