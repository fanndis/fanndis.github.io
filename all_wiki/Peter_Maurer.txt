'''Peter Maurer''' (born 1956) is the President of the [[International Committee of the Red Cross]] (ICRC),<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.icrc.org/en/document/president-and-vice-president-icrc |title=The president and vice-president of the ICRC |accessdate=2015-05-08 |year=2015}}</ref> appointed 1 July 2012. Maurer was born in [[Thun]], [[Switzerland]].

==Education==
Maurer studied [[History]] and [[International law|International Law]] in [[Bern]], and was awarded a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]].

==Career==
In 1987, Maurer entered the Swiss Diplomatic Service ([[Federal Department of Foreign Affairs]]), where he held various positions in Bern and [[Pretoria]], [[South Africa]], before being transferred to [[New York City|New York]] in 1996 as Deputy Permanent Observer at the Swiss Mission to the [[United Nations]]. In 2000, he was appointed Ambassador and head of the division in charge of human security at the headquarters of [[Switzerland|Switzerland's]] Foreign Ministry in Berne.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.icrc.org/eng/resources/documents/news-release/2011/geneva-news-2011-10-19.htm |title=ICRC names new president |accessdate=2015-05-08 |year=2011}}</ref>

In 2004, Maurer was appointed Ambassador and Permanent Representative of Switzerland to the United Nations in New York. In this position, he endeavoured to integrate Switzerland, which had only recently joined the United Nations, into multilateral networks. In June 2009, the [[United Nations General Assembly]] elected Maurer Chairman of the [[United Nations General Assembly Fifth Committee|Fifth Committee]], in charge of United Nations administrative and budgetary affairs.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.un.org/press/en/2009/bio4129.doc.htm |title=Peter Maurer of Switzerland Chair of Fifth Committee |accessdate=2015-05-08 |year=2009}}</ref> In addition, he was elected Chairman of the [[Burundi]] Configuration of the [[Peacebuilding Commission|UN Peacebuilding Commission]]. In January 2010, Maurer became Switzerland's State Secretary for Foreign Affairs,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Peter Maurer neuer Staatssekretär im EDA|url=https://www.admin.ch/gov/de/start/dokumentation/medienmitteilungen.msg-id-31071.html|access-date=2020-10-26|website=www.admin.ch}}</ref> a position he held until he was elected President of the ICRC, succeeding [[Jakob Kellenberger]] in June 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Erfolge und Schwierigkeiten|url=https://www.swissinfo.ch/ger/geschichte-des-ikrk_erfolge-und-schwierigkeiten/32991846|access-date=2020-10-26|website=SWI swissinfo.ch|language=de}}</ref>

Maurer led the ICRC through a historic budget increase, from 1.1 billion CHF in 2011 to over 1.6 billion CHF in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.icrc.org/en/who-we-are/finances |title=ICRC Finances |accessdate=2015-05-08 |year=2015}}</ref> In addition, he launched the world’s first “humanitarian impact bond” scheme, in which private investors lend €22 million of five-year funding and their repayments depend on what difference the project makes to its clients’ lives.<ref>Kate Allen (September 8, 2017) [https://www.ft.com/content/8d82241d-096b-313e-91d8-786ba99d2073 Red Cross launches “humanitarian impact bond”] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref> Since 2019, Maurer has been co-chairing the [[World Economic Forum]] High-Level Group on Humanitarian Investing, alongside [[Børge Brende]] and [[Kristalina Georgieva]].<ref>[https://www.weforum.org/press/2019/01/world-economic-forum-2019-annual-meeting-launching-a-new-humanitarian-investing-initiative/ World Economic Forum 2019 Annual Meeting launching a new Humanitarian Investing Initiative] [[World Economic Forum]], press release of January 18, 2019.</ref> In September 2019, he re-elected as the president of the ICRC for an additional term of 4 years.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Peter Maurer für weitere Amtsperiode als IKRK-Präsident bestätigt|url=https://www.aargauerzeitung.ch/schweiz/peter-maurer-fuer-weitere-amtsperiode-als-ikrk-praesident-bestaetigt-136043009|access-date=2020-10-26|website=Aargauer Zeitung|language=de-ch}}</ref>
