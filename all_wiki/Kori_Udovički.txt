'''Kori Udovički''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Кори Удовички}}, {{IPA-sh|kôri udoʋǐtʃkiː|sr}}; born 4 December 1961) is a [[Serbia]]n politician, former Deputy Prime Minister of Serbia,<ref>[http://www.b92.net/eng/news/politics.php?yyyy=2016&mm=07&dd=26&nav_id=98720 New government by August 4, five ministers are out - report] ''B92.net'' 26 July 2016. Retrieved 12 August 2016.</ref> and the Minister of Public Administration and Local Self-Government in the [[Government of Serbia]].<ref>[http://inserbia.info/today/2014/04/udovicki-public-policy-secretariat-to-be-introduced/ Udovicki: Public policy secretariat to be introduced] ''INSerbia.info'' 28 April 2014. Retrieved 29 April 2014.</ref>

Prior to that position, she served as an Assistant Secretary-General of the [[United Nations]], Assistant Administrator of the [[United Nations Development Programme|UNDP]] and Director of the Regional Bureau of [[United Nations Development Programme|UNDP]] for Europe and [[Commonwealth of Independent States]] (RBEC) from 2007 to 2012.<ref>{{cite news |title= Turkish Minister Meets Senior UN Official |url= http://www.turkishweekly.net/news/101311/turkish-minister-meets-senior-un-official.html |newspaper= Turkish Weekly |date= 16 April 2010 |accessdate= 14 January 2011 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100418120429/http://www.turkishweekly.net/news/101311/turkish-minister-meets-senior-un-official.html |archive-date= 18 April 2010 |url-status= dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= Kori Udovicki joins UNDP as Director of Bureau for Europe and the Commonwealth of Independent States |url= http://content.undp.org/go/newsroom/2007/february/kori-udovicki-20070201.en |newspaper= UNDP Newsroom |date= 1 February 2007 |accessdate= 27 February 2012 |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20111202195114/http://content.undp.org/go/newsroom/2007/february/kori-udovicki-20070201.en |archive-date= 2 December 2011 }}</ref>

Previously she was the founder and the President of the Center for Advanced Economic Studies (CEVES), a Belgrade [[Non-governmental organization|NGO]] that works for the advancement of economic research and education in South East Europe. She was also President of the Board of Directors of the Foundation for the Advancement of Economics (''FREN''). CEVES's main publication is Quarterly Monitor of Economic Trends and Policies in Serbia, a publication that systematically monitors macroeconomic, corporate and financial trends and policies in Serbia. It comes out in Serbian and English and is also posted on the CEVES and FREN websites. Kori Udovički was the Editor in Chief until February 2007.

==Education and political career==
She graduated from the [[University of Belgrade]] [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Economics|Faculty of Economics]] in 1984 she obtained an MA (1988) and a PhD (1999) in Economics from [[Yale University]]. She researched inter-regional trade and integration between the republics of the former [[Yugoslavia]]. More recently, she has studied the sustainability of Serbia's macroeconomic framework. From 1993 to 2001, she worked at the [[International Monetary Fund|IMF]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] and then returned to Belgrade as Special Advisor to the Serbian Minister of Finance and Economic Affairs. In 2002, she became Minister of Energy and Mining in [[Government of Serbia|Serbian Government]]. She was appointed [[List of governors of national banks of Serbia and Yugoslavia|Governor]] of the [[National Bank of Serbia]]<ref>{{cite web |url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/3084599.stm |title= Serbia seeks new top banker |date= 21 July 2003 |publisher= BBC News |accessdate=14 January 2011}}</ref> on July 23, 2003, a position she held until February 25, 2004, when her appointment was annulled due to the illegal use of a proxy vote in the Serbian parliament {{Citation needed|date=December 2010}}.

==Personal life==
She is married and has three children. Her maternal uncle is former [[President of Bolivia]] [[Gonzalo Sánchez de Lozada]]. Her father {{Interlanguage link multi|Lazar Udovički|sr|3=Лазар Удовички|lt=Lazar Udovički}} was a politician in [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|SFR Yugoslavia]] who served as ambassador in Bolivia. Kori is the sister-in-law of actor [[Rade Šerbedžija]] as he is married to her sister [[Lenka Udovički|Lenka]].
