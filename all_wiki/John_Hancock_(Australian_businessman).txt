'''John Hancock''' (born 1976 as John Langley Hayward) is an Australian businessman. He is the son of [[Gina Rinehart]] and grandson of the late mining magnate [[Lang Hancock]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gina Rinehart misses wedding of son John Hancock|url = http://www.theaustralian.com.au/news/nation/gina-rinehart-misses-wedding-of-son-john-hancock/story-e6frg6nf-1226924398777|website = www.theaustralian.com.au|accessdate = 2015-05-29|last = Garvey|first = Paul|publisher = The Australian|date = 2014-05-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Australian Story - House Of Hancock - Transcript|url = http://www.abc.net.au/austory/content/2012/s3468959.htm|website = www.abc.net.au|accessdate = 2015-05-29}}</ref>

== Early childhood and education ==
John Hancock was born John Langley Hayward.<ref name="JLH1976">{{cite news |url = http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article44524735|title = Master John Langley Hayward Australia's richest baby|newspaper = The Australian Women's Weekly|date = 11 February 1976|accessdate = 14 January 2011|page = 13|publisher = National Library of Australia}}</ref> He has a younger sister Bianca Rinehart and two half sisters Ginia Rinehart and Hope Welker. He changed his surname to Hancock,<ref name=":0" /> tribute to his grandfather after a dispute with his mining magnate mother Gina Rinehart. His father is Englishman Greg Milton.<ref>{{Cite web|title = 'I'm doing it for Lang': John Hancock|url = http://www.smh.com.au/business/im-doing-it-for-lang-john-hancock-20130913-2tqfj.html|accessdate = 2015-05-29}}</ref> who later changed his name to Greg Hayward.<ref name="JLH1976"/> Hancock was educated abroad at the Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts in the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|title = The mother of all feuds|url = http://www.smh.com.au/business/the-mother-of-all-feuds-20130913-2tesa.html|accessdate = 2015-05-29}}</ref>

== Trust fund case ==
In 2010, Hancock and his sisters Hope Welker and Bianca Rinehart launched supreme court action on their mother in relation to the operation of  family trust fund setup by the late grandfather.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = John Hancock’s ‘conundrum’ with mum Gina Rinehart|url = http://www.afr.com/markets/commodities/metals/john-hancocks-conundrum-with-mum-gina-rinehart-20141203-11ytbd|accessdate = 2015-05-29|date = 2014-12-03|website = John Hancock’s ‘conundrum’ with mum Gina Rinehart|publisher = AFR}}</ref>

John quoted as responding to a question about living off the family trust fund:<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gina Rinehart - The Power of One - Four Corners|url = http://www.abc.net.au/4corners/stories/2012/06/20/3529598.htm|website = www.abc.net.au|accessdate = 2015-05-29}}</ref> <blockquote>“Well it'd be nice if I was, but I have all the bad things about having money and none of the good things.”</blockquote>Despite his difference with his mother, he still loves her very much<ref>{{Cite web|title = No Cookies {{!}}  Perth Now|url = http://www.perthnow.com.au/news/western-australia/gina-rineharts-son-john-hancock-disputes-tv-portrayal-says-he-still-loves-his-mother/story-fnhocxo3-1227211385965|website = www.perthnow.com.au|accessdate = 2015-05-29}}</ref> and have indicated they agree 90 percent of the time.<ref name=":0" /> It is the 10 percent which causes the difficulties.<ref name=":0" />

The NSW Supreme Court handed down its decision by appointing Bianca Rinehart as trustee of the Hope Margaret Hancock Trust.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title = Rinehart's eldest daughter handed control of family's $4b trust|url = http://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-05-28/gina-rinehart-court-decision-family-trust/6504410|website = ABC News|accessdate = 2015-05-29}}</ref> Their mother already agreed to step aside as trustee and wanted consultation on who should replace her.<ref name=":1" />
