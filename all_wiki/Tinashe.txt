'''Tinashe Jorgensen Kachingwe'''<ref name="AMreview"/> ({{IPAc-en|t|iː|ˈ|n|ɑː|ʃ|eɪ}};<ref>{{cite web|title=How You REALLY Pronounce Tinashe|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7E95ATbuHXY|website=YouTube|accessdate=March 1, 2017}}</ref> born February 6, 1993<ref name="AMreview"/>), known mononymously as '''Tinashe''', is an American singer, songwriter, dancer, record producer, and actress.<!--Occupations subject is most notable for per reliable sources--><ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/es/blog/tinashe-black-water-interview|title=Tinashe: Are You Ready?|work=NOISEY|accessdate=April 13, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304085229/http://noisey.vice.com/es/blog/tinashe-black-water-interview|archive-date=March 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Born in [[Lexington, Kentucky]],<ref name="AMreview"/> Tinashe moved to [[Los Angeles]] as a child to pursue a career in entertainment. Her notable roles included as a motion-capture model in the animated film ''[[The Polar Express (film)|The Polar Express]]'' (2004), Robin Wheeler in the [[Cartoon Network]] television series ''[[Out of Jimmy's Head]]'' (2007–2008), and a recurring role in the [[CBS]] series ''[[Two and a Half Men]]'' (2008–2009).

Between 2007 and 2011, Tinashe was a member of the girl group [[the Stunners]]. Following their disbandment, she released the positively-reviewed self-recorded [[mixtape]]s ''[[In Case We Die (Tinashe album)|In Case We Die]]'' (2012) and ''[[Reverie (Tinashe album)|Reverie]]'' (2012). Following their success, Tinashe signed with [[RCA Records]] and released her third mixtape, ''[[Black Water (Tinashe album)|Black Water]]'' (2013). Her debut single, "[[2 On]]", reached number one on the [[Rhythmic (chart)|Rhythmic]] airplay chart, and peaked at number 24 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/music/tinashe-feat-schoolboy-q-2-on/|title=Tinashe feat. Schoolboy Q – 2 On Music Video|work=What's Good?|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140324191119/http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/music/tinashe-feat-schoolboy-q-2-on/|archivedate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> Her debut studio album, ''[[Aquarius (Tinashe album)|Aquarius]]'' (2014), was noted by music critics as one of the most “solid” debuts by a new female artist in years. The album garnered her nominations for [[Soul Train Award|Soul Train]] and [[BET Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Lewis|first=Brittany|url=http://globalgrind.com/music/tinashe-debut-album-reverie-mixtape-video|title=Tinashe Reveals Details About Her Debut Album|work=Global Grind|date=December 13, 2012|accessdate=December 26, 2012}}</ref> Her collaboration with [[Snakehips (duo)|Snakehips]] and [[Chance the Rapper]], "[[All My Friends (Snakehips song)|All My Friends]]", won the Ivor Novello Award for Best Contemporary Song in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2016/05/20/adele-wins-big-again-at-the-2016-ivor-novello-awards/#c785ddd69468|title= Adele Wins Big (Again) At The 2016 Ivor Novello Awards |work=Forbes|accessdate=October 17, 2017}}</ref> The same year, Tinashe appeared on [[Britney Spears]]' single "[[Slumber Party (song)|Slumber Party]]", which topped [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s [[Dance Club Songs]] chart. Tinashe's second studio album ''[[Nightride]]'' (2016) reached number 8 on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart in the US. ''[[Joyride (Tinashe album)|Joyride]]'' (2018), her third studio album, peaked at number 58 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart and reached number 6 on the [[UK R&B Singles and Albums Charts|UK R&B Albums]] chart. After departing RCA, she independently released her fourth studio album, ''[[Songs for You]]'' in November 2019.

Tinashe describes her music as "rhythmic pop" that explores the genres of [[alternative R&B]], [[Pop music|pop]], and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]]. 

==Life and career==

===1993–2006: Early life===
Tinashe Kachingwe was born on February 6, 1993, in [[Lexington, Kentucky]], and is the eldest child of college professors Michael and Aimie Kachingwe. Her father is a professor who teaches acting at [[California State Polytechnic University, Pomona]] and a first generation immigrant of the [[Shona people]] of [[Zimbabwe]], while her mother, who is of [[Danes|Danish]], [[Norwegians|Norwegian]], and [[Irish people|Irish]] descent, teaches physical therapy at [[California State University, Northridge]]. Her parents met each other on a blind date during their time as undergrads at the [[University of Iowa]].<ref name="Tinashe">{{cite web | url=http://ca.complex.com/music/tinashe-interview-2016-cover-story | title=Tinashe | publisher=Complex | date=February 2, 2016 | accessdate=March 16, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Tinashe/status/28694064811479040|title=I am.. Zimbabwean and Norwegian/Danish/Irish...|date=January 21, 2011}}</ref> Tinashe's name means "We have God (or God is with us)" in the [[Shona language]]. She has two younger brothers, Thulani and Kudzai.<ref name=ZAA>{{cite web|title=Tinashe brings touch of glamour to ZAA|url=http://www.newzimbabwe.com/SHOWBIZ-10716-Tinashe+adds+touch+of+glamour+to+ZAA/SHOWBIZ.aspx|website=New Zimbabwe|accessdate=March 14, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402160448/http://www.newzimbabwe.com/SHOWBIZ-10716-Tinashe+adds+touch+of+glamour+to+ZAA/SHOWBIZ.aspx|archivedate=April 2, 2015}}</ref> She and her family moved to Los Angeles, California when she was eight years old.<ref name=Biography>{{cite web|title=About Tinashe|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/tinashe/biography/|website=MTV|publisher=Viacom|accessdate=March 15, 2015}}</ref> She attended [[Crescenta Valley High School]] for a year before finishing early to pursue a career in music full-time.<ref>{{cite web|last=Inocencio|first=Marc|url=http://www.at40.com/news-article/tinashe-and-ryan-call-radio-stations-to-correct-pronunciation-of-her-name/33395|title=Tinashe and Ryan Call Radio Stations to Correct Pronunciation of Her Name|work=American Top 40|date=October 15, 2014|accessdate=March 14, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402164527/http://www.at40.com/news-article/tinashe-and-ryan-call-radio-stations-to-correct-pronunciation-of-her-name/33395|archivedate=April 2, 2015}}</ref> She began studying ballet, tap, and jazz dancing at the age of 4, and continued to compete in various styles as a part of a dance company until she was 18.<ref>{{cite web|last=Carlin|first=Shannon|title=New Music To Know: Tinashe is an R&B Singer on the Verge|url=http://radio.com/2014/09/03/new-music-to-know-tinashe-interview/|website=Radio|accessdate=March 14, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150212210732/http://radio.com/2014/09/03/new-music-to-know-tinashe-interview/|archivedate=February 12, 2015}}</ref>

===2007–2011: ''The Stunners'' and acting roles===
{{Main|The Stunners}}
Tinashe scored roles in a number of TV shows, movies, and children's videos in the early 2000s, including the movies ''[[Cora Unashamed]]'' (2000), ''[[Call Me Claus]]'' (2001), ''[[Masked and Anonymous]]'' (2003), and two TV series: ''[[Rocket Power]]'' (2004) and ''[[Avatar: The Last Airbender]]'' (2007). These led to a regular role in the [[Cartoon Network]] TV series ''[[Out of Jimmy's Head]]'' in 2007–2008 and a recurring role on ''[[Two and a Half Men]]'' in 2008–2009.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kearse |first1=Kerri |title=What Viewers Need to Know About the Cast of Fox's Upcoming Rent: Live |url=http://www.playbill.com/article/what-viewers-need-to-know-about-the-cast-of-foxs-upcoming-rent-live |accessdate=December 17, 2018 |work=Playbill |date=December 8, 2018 |language=en}}</ref> Although she was uncredited in the poorly received ''Masked and Anonymous'', [[Roger Ebert]] observed at the film's [[2003 Sundance Film Festival]] premiere "the one performer in the movie whose performance actually was applauded; that was a young black girl named Tinashe Kachingwe, who sings '[[The Times They Are a-Changin' (song)|The Times They Are A-Changin']]' with such sweetness and conviction that she is like a master class." He concluded his review of the movie: "If I had been asked to serve as consultant on this picture, my advice would have amounted to three words: more Tinashe Kachingwe."<ref>{{cite news|last=Ebert|first=Roger|authorlink=Roger Ebert|date=August 15, 2003|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/masked-and-anonymous-2003|title=Masked and Anonymous|newspaper=[[Chicago Sun-Times]]|accessdate=August 28, 2018}}</ref>

In 2007, Tinashe joined [[girl group]] [[the Stunners]], founded by [[Vitamin C (singer)|Vitamin C]]. Her bandmates were friends Marisol Esparza, [[Allie Gonino]], [[Hayley Kiyoko]], and [[Kelsey Sanders]].<ref name="Stunners Bio">{{cite web|title=The Stunners|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/the-stunners/#biographyEnd|website=MTV|publisher=Viacom|accessdate=March 15, 2015}}</ref> Six months after their formation, the group signed with [[Columbia Records]], and later signed a production deal with [[Lionsgate Entertainment]] for scripted television show for MTV.<ref name=comet/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.jsyk.com/2009/10/20/listen-to-the-stunners-new-self-titled-ep/|title=Listen to The Stunners' New Self-Titled EP|work=[[American Online|AOL]]|publisher=Nadine Cheung|date=September 20, 2009|accessdate=October 22, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100729050225/http://www.jsyk.com/2009/10/20/listen-to-the-stunners-new-self-titled-ep/|archive-date=July 29, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> On March 18, 2009, they released a single and video, "Bubblegum." In October, the group released a five-song [[Extended play|EP]], influenced by artists such as [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]], [[Gwen Stefani]], and [[Rihanna]].<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-stunners-mn0002126388|title=The Stunners: Biography|publisher=Allmusic|date=March 22, 2012}}</ref> The music video for their [[promotional single|promo single]], "We Got It", was released on February 22, 2010,<ref name=comet>{{cite news|last=Lindell |first=Karen |url=http://www.vcstar.com/news/2010/jul/16/hayleys-comet-ever-since-she-joined-the-stunners/ |title=Hayley's Comet |work=[[Ventura County Star]] |date=July 16, 2010 |accessdate=October 23, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110907012005/http://www.vcstar.com/news/2010/jul/16/hayleys-comet-ever-since-she-joined-the-stunners/ |archivedate=September 7, 2011 }}</ref> and they performed the song on ''[[The Today Show]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thestunnersmusic.com/|title=HELLO! Meet the Stunners!|accessdate=September 15, 2011}}</ref> and ''[[The Wendy Williams Show]]''.<ref name=comet/><ref name=bandblog>{{cite web|url=http://bandweblogs.com/blog/2009/12/08/the-stunners-santa-bring-my-soldier-home-wendy-williams-show-performance/ |title=The Stunners – "Santa Bring My Soldier Home" + Wendy Williams Show performance |work=BandWebLogs |date=December 9, 2009 |accessdate=October 23, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111105174624/http://bandweblogs.com/blog/2009/12/08/the-stunners-santa-bring-my-soldier-home-wendy-williams-show-performance/ |archivedate=November 5, 2011 }}</ref> After signing with [[Universal Republic Records]]<ref name=comet/> in 2010 they released a single, "Dancin' Around the Truth."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.allaccess.com/top40-mainstream/future-releases|title=Top 40/M Future Releases|work=All Access Music Group|accessdate=April 25, 2010}}</ref> The music video premiered on June 2, 2010, just before the group was announced as an opening act on [[Justin Bieber]]'s [[My World Tour]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://concertsandsports.wordpress.com/2010/06/03/justin-bieber-presents-the-stunners/|title=Justin Bieber presents The Stunners!|date=June 3, 2010|accessdate=October 23, 2011}}</ref>

After the Bieber tour the Stunners returned to the recording studio, but disbanded in 2011, and Tinashe began pursuing a solo career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tommy2.net/content/?p=24183|title=Tommy2.net Entry for April 6, 2011|date=April 6, 2011|accessdate=December 27, 2011}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=December 2012}} After the Stunners disbanded, Tinashe "bought a bunch of equipment, a camera, and microphones", and began to teach herself how to record and mix music. She wrote and recorded songs in her room studio. She produced beats with [[Logic Pro]], and also filmed and edited her own music videos with [[Pro Tools]] and [[Final Cut Pro]]. Tinashe cites YouTube as her "teacher".<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/pictures/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-july-2014-20140718/tinashe-0867410|title=Tinashe Photo - 10 New Artists You Need to Know: July 2014 |website=Rolling Stone|accessdate=November 10, 2014|date=July 18, 2014 }}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
{{Quote box
|quote = I write a lot of it in my car, that's just a very inspiring place to listen to music. I think it puts me in the zone. I just think of melodies usually and then lyrics come after. Sometimes really easily, and then sometimes I'll have a melody for a week before I come up with any lyrics so it just kind of depends.|source = — Tinashe, speaking on her writing style.<ref name="noisey.vice.com">{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/blog/tinashe-black-water-interview|title=Tinashe: Are You Ready? |website=NOISEY|publisher=noisey.vice.com|accessdate=November 10, 2014|date=2013-12-06 }}</ref>

Tinashe's musical style has been descibed as [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]],<ref name="AMreview">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/tinashe-mn0003236942/Biography|title=Tinashe Biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|last=Kellmen|first=Andy}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/arts-culture/american-r-b-singer-tinashe-on-her-secret-to-success-make-things-happen-for-yourself-1.96076|title=American R&B singer Tinashe on her secret to success: 'Make things happen for yourself'|work=[[The National (Abu Dhabi)|The National]]|date=June 17, 2015|accessdate=January 30, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2018/10/30/tinashe-rent-live-version-fox/|title=R&B Sensation Tinashe Takes A Leading Role In The Live Version Of 'Rent'|work=[[The Source]]|date=October 30, 2018|accessdate=January 30, 2019}}</ref> [[Pop music|pop]],<ref name="latimes">{{cite web |last=Kennedy |first=Gerrick D. |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-tinashe-company-20170107-story.html |title=Tinashe's dance heavy 'Company' is the first must-see music video of 2017 |work=Los Angeles Times |date=January 7, 2017 |accessdate=January 30, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8094152/tinashe-new-album-singles-joyride|title=Tinashe Announces Three New Singles 'Coming Soon'|work=Billboard|date=January 12, 2018|accessdate=January 20, 2019}}</ref> and [[alternative R&B]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Why Tinashe Is One of 2017's Most Daring Artists |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8062757/why-tinashe-is-one-of-2017s-most-daring-artists |work=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Nguyen |first1=Dean Van |title=Tinashe - 'Aquarius' |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/reviews-various-artists-15695 |accessdate=January 30, 2019 |work=NME |date=October 7, 2014}}</ref> Tinashe said she writes her songs and is open to writing collaborations with other artists.<ref name="noisey.vice.com"/> Her writing consists of "things that are relative to me and my life; whether that's relationships I've had or relationships that people I know are in, because I can put myself into other people's mindstates and write from that point of view too".<ref name="vice">{{cite web|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/nebbdm/combine-aaliyah-james-blake-and-janet-jackson-and-you-might-just-get-a-tinashe|title=Combine Aaliyah, James Blake and Janet Jackson and you might just get a Tinashe |work=[[i-D]]|accessdate=November 10, 2014}}</ref> She also likes to write about society; "different things that affect me day to day from more of a philosophical standpoint rather than just the obvious stuff."<ref name="vice"/> Her mixtapes have been more experimental, incorporating elements of traditional R&B, electro-hop<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stern |first1=Bradley |title=Required Listening: Tinashe, 'Black Water' (Mixtape) |url=https://muumuse.com/2013/11/tinashe-black-water-mixtape.html/ |website=MuuMuse |accessdate=November 23, 2019 |date=November 27, 2013}}</ref> and dark pop.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Smith |first1=Da’Shan |title=A comprehensive breakdown of the musicality in Tinashe's discography |url=https://www.revolt.tv/2018/6/20/20825023/a-comprehensive-breakdown-of-the-musicality-in-tinashe-s-discography |accessdate=November 23, 2019 |work=REVOLT |date=June 20, 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

Regarding her musical style, she is "really inspired by R&B, hip-hop, and alternative music".<ref name="noisey.vice.com"/> 
{{quote|I just find darkness interesting in a way. I just love songs that sound like a little crazy, I think it creates an interesting juxtaposition between my voice because it is so bright, and soft, and like pretty, and sweet that I don't want my music to come across sweet or nice ... so I think of something that's dark and heavy and then I put like sweet and light on top.<ref name="noisey.vice.com"/>}}

Tinashe said in 2016 she feels she does not "fit into any particular genre."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Me + My Selves: Tinashe on Tinashe on Tinashe|publisher=NYLON Video|via=Youtube|date=October 5, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WQ22jwQkqM4|accessdate=November 2, 2016}}</ref> She later said she considers herself "a pop artist who makes R&B-tinged pop music".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/jun/12/tinashe-if-black-singer-either-beyonce-rihanna|title=Tinashe: 'If you're a black singer, you're either Beyoncé or Rihanna'|date=June 12, 2017|work=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=January 20, 2019}}</ref> Her style has been compared to [[Janet Jackson]], [[Aaliyah]], and [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]].<ref name="vice"/> Tinashe was inspired by the music her parents would play in the house when she was young.<ref name="mainstream">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6386058/tinashe-video-interview-aquarius|title=Tinashe Video Interview: On Bringing More Females Into the Mainstream & The Success of '2 On'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=April 13, 2016}}</ref> She considers [[Britney Spears]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Janet Jackson]], [[Sade (singer)|Sade]], [[Christina Aguilera]], [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]], [[the xx]], and [[SBTRKT]] as significant influences on her.<ref name="vice"/><ref name="mainstream"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/7513629/popping-up-tinashe|title=Popping Up: Tinashe|work=Music News, Reviews, and Gossip on Idolator.com|accessdate=April 13, 2016}}</ref><ref name="openingceremony">{{cite web|url=http://www.openingceremony.us/entry.asp?pid=9890|title=The Look: Tinashe – OPENING CEREMONY|publisher=openingceremony.us|accessdate=November 10, 2014}}</ref>
{{quote|I think that [Michael Jackson, Janet Jackson, Christina Aguilera, and Sade] are all iconic in some type of way. They're all amazing singers, performers and that live aspect is something I admire. I always want to put on a great live show.<ref name="openingceremony"/>}}

==Personal life==
As a child, Tinashe faced [[bullying]] from her peers:

{{quote|I actually did get bullied a lot in school. I went to [[State school|public school]] until 9th grade and had a pretty bad experience. I missed out on prom and graduation and the whole [[college]] experience but I don't feel bad about it because everything else I was doing was what I actually wanted.<ref name="bullettmedia">{{cite web|url=http://bullettmedia.com/article/how-tinashe-became-rbs-next-breakout-startinashe/|title=How Tinashe Became R&B's Next Breakout Star|publisher=bullettmedia.com|accessdate=November 10, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141110233842/http://bullettmedia.com/article/how-tinashe-became-rbs-next-breakout-startinashe/|archive-date=November 10, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>}}
Tinashe resides in Los Angeles, has a bedroom studio, and has a [[black belt (martial arts)|black belt]] in [[Taekwondo]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-11-11|title=Tinashe Has a Black Belt in Taekwondo|url=https://www.gq.com.au/gq-women/women-of-the-week/tinashe-has-a-black-belt-in-taekwondo/image-gallery/6b249c7479faf2ad02e0634e871780a9|access-date=2020-08-11|website=GQ|language=en}}</ref>

On July 8, 2012, Tinashe initially came out as bisexual on ''[[Tumblr]]''. <ref>https://jano4tinashe.tumblr.com/post/26785430714/tinashe-im-very-proud-of-frank-ocean-coming-out</ref>

On February 2, 2016, Tinashe posed for ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' and invited them to her home to do a feature about her family. At the time, Tinashe lived at home with her family in La Crescenta, a suburb 20 miles north of Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/tinashe-interview-2016-cover-story|title=Tinashe and Family on Racism, Self Doubt, and Nobody Wanting To Date Her|last=Scarano|first=Ross|work=Complex|accessdate=April 27, 2018}}</ref> In June 2020, she attended the [[George Floyd protests]] in Los Angeles.<ref name="Floyd"/>

On August 28, 2020, Tinashe speaks on her sexuality in ''[[Gay Times]]''. Initially refusing to label herself to hinder from the public to avoid misconceptions from others, she publicly opened about it in the same article. <ref>https://www.pride.com/comingout/2020/8/28/tinashe-opens-about-her-bisexuality</ref>

In October 2020, she posted a message on social media in support of [[Armenians]] in regards to the [[2020 Nagorno-Karabakh conflict|Nagorno-Karabakh war]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Tinashe Twitter|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/Tinashe/status/1313991236550787077|accessdate=October 16, 2020}}</ref>
