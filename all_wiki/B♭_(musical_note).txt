'''B{{music|flat}}''' ('''B-flat'''; also called '''si bémol''') is the eleventh step of the Western [[chromatic scale]] (starting from [[C (musical note)|C]]).
It lies a [[diatonic semitone]] above [[A (musical note)|A]] and a [[chromatic semitone]] below B, thus being [[enharmonic]] to [[A♯ (musical note)|A{{music|♯}}]], even though in some [[musical tuning]]s, B{{music|flat}} will have a different sounding pitch than A{{music|sharp}}. B-flat is also enharmonic to C{{music|bb}}.

When calculated in [[equal temperament]] with a reference of [[A (musical note)|A]] above [[middle C]] as 440 [[hertz|Hz]], the [[frequency]] of the B{{music|flat}} above middle C is approximately 466.164&nbsp;Hz. See [[pitch (music)]] for a discussion of historical variations in frequency.

While [[orchestra]]s tune to an A provided by the [[oboe|oboist]], [[wind ensembles]] usually tune to a B-flat provided by a [[tuba]], [[Horn (instrument)|horn]], or [[clarinet]].

In Germany, Russia, Poland and Scandinavia, this pitch is designated B, with 'H' used to designate the B-[[Natural (music)|natural]]. Since the 1990s, B-flat is often denoted Bb or "Bess" instead of B in Swedish music textbooks. Natural B is called B by Swedish jazz and pop musicians, but still denoted H in classical music. See [[B (musical note)#Variation of meaning by geographical region|B (musical note)]] and [[Musical note#Note_names_and_their_history|Musical note: Note names and their history]] for explanations.
