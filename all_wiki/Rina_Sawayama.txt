{{nihongo|'''Rina Sawayama'''|„É™„Éä„Éª„Çµ„ÉØ„É§„Éû||born 16 August 1990}} is a [[Japanese in the United Kingdom|Japanese-British]] singer-songwriter and model. While studying politics, psychology and sociology at [[Magdalene College, Cambridge|Magdalene College]], [[University of Cambridge]], Sawayama was a member of the [[hip hop music|hip-hop]] group Lazy Lion with musician [[Wolf Alice|Theo Ellis]]. Sawayama made her solo debut in 2013 and released her debut extended-play, [[Rina (EP)|Rina]], in 2017. Her debut studio album, ''[[Sawayama]]'', was released in April 2020 to critical acclaim.

==Early life==
Sawayama was born on 16 August 1990<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/rinasawayama|title=Rina Sawayama's Twitter profile|access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref> in [[Niigata (city)|Niigata]], Japan.<ref name="VoS">{{cite web |url=https://vibesofsilence.com/introducing-rina-sawayama |title=Introducing: Rina Sawayama |work=Vibes of Silence |date=4 November 2017 |access-date=18 November 2017 |archive-date=19 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180619035731/https://vibesofsilence.com/introducing-rina-sawayama/ |url-status=live}}</ref> Sawayama lived there until the age of five, when her family decided to move to [[London]], England, where she was raised and currently resides.<ref name="Vogue">{{cite news |url=http://www.vogue.com/article/rina-sawayama-model-singer-japanese-beauty-standards-orange-hair |title=Meet the Tangerine-Haired Model and Singer Who's Taking On Asian Beauty Standards |last=Kim |first=Monica |date=17 October 2016 |magazine=Vogue |access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref> She holds an [[indefinite leave to remain]] (ILR) visa in the UK.<ref name=":7"/>

While studying politics, psychology and sociology at [[Magdalene College, Cambridge|Magdalene College, University of Cambridge]],<ref name="Pixelated People">{{cite web |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/pixelated-people-rina-sawayama-interviewed |title=Pixelated People: Rina Sawayama Interviewed|date=8 February 2018 |access-date=19 April 2020 |archive-date=9 May 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200509174702/https://www.clashmusic.com/features/pixelated-people-rina-sawayama-interviewed |url-status=live}}</ref> Sawayama decided to pursue music and modelling. During her time at university, she performed in a [[hip hop music|hip-hop]] group called Lazy Lion with Theo Ellis from the [[indie rock]] band [[Wolf Alice]].<ref name="VoS"/> She graduated from the university with a degree in political science. In May 2020, Sawayama said that she was doing an online course at the [[University of Oxford]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-05-20 |title=Welcome to Rina's world: Rina Sawayama on Charli XCX, Cambridge and her pop icons |url=https://www.thelovemagazine.co.uk/article/welcome-to-rinas-world-rina-sawayama-on-charli-xcx-cambridge-and-her-pop-icons |access-date=2020-08-19 |website=LOVE |archive-date=21 August 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200821041623/https://www.thelovemagazine.co.uk/article/welcome-to-rinas-world-rina-sawayama-on-charli-xcx-cambridge-and-her-pop-icons |url-status=live}}</ref>

===Collaborations===
In 2016, Sawayama collaborated with Taiwanese visual artist, [[John Yuyi]], on a visual series critiquing Asian, including Japanese, beauty standards. Subsequently, she was profiled in ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]''. In the interview, Sawayama described the genesis of the idea and collaboration: "For a lot of women in Japan, these are the expectations people put on them, from anime culture, kawaii culture‚Ä¶ that can really put women at a disadvantage, objectifying and infantilising them."<ref name="Vogue" /> Sawayama has worked with Nicola Formichetti for MAC x Nicopanda. ==Personal life==
In August 2018, Sawayama [[coming out|came out]] during an interview with [[Vice (magazine)|''Broadly'']], declaring: "I've always written songs about girls. I don't think I've ever mentioned a guy in my songs, and that's why I wanted to talk about it." She identified as both [[bisexuality|bisexual]] and [[pansexuality|pansexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://broadly.vice.com/en_us/article/zmkgyx/rina-sawayama-comes-out-pansexual-interview|title=Singer Rina Sawayama Is Ready to Rep Her Pansexuality|last=Tsjeng|first=Zing|website=Broadly|access-date=17 August 2018|archive-date=14 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180814170214/https://broadly.vice.com/en_us/article/zmkgyx/rina-sawayama-comes-out-pansexual-interview|url-status=live}}</ref>
