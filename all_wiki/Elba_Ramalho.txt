'''Elba Ramalho''' (born August 17, 1951 in [[Conceição, Paraíba|Conceição]], [[Paraíba]]) is a [[Brazil]]ian songwriter, performer, poet and actress. She is sometimes called "The Queen of [[Forró]]".

In addition to her successful solo career, Elba has collaborated with a number of well-established Brazilian acts, including [[Alceu Valença]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-04|title=Elba Ramalho, Alceu Valença e Geraldo Azevedo fazem 1ª live juntos neste domingo|url=https://f5.folha.uol.com.br/musica/2020/07/elba-ramalho-alceu-valenca-e-geraldo-azevedo-fazem-1a-live-juntos-neste-domingo.shtml|access-date=2020-07-26|website=F5|language=pt-BR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Alceu Valença, Elba Ramalho e Geraldo Azevedo renovam repertório para primeira live do projeto 'O grande encontro'|url=https://g1.globo.com/pop-arte/musica/blog/mauro-ferreira/post/2020/06/30/alceu-valenca-elba-ramalho-e-geraldo-azevedo-renovam-repertorio-para-primeira-live-do-projeto-o-grande-encontro.ghtml|access-date=2020-07-26|website=G1|language=pt-br}}</ref> and her first cousin [[Zé Ramalho]].

In [[20th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|2019]], her album ''[[O Ouro do Pó da Estrada]]'' was nominated for the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Portuguese Language Roots Album]].<ref name="billboardLG19">{{cite web |last1=Cabo |first1=Leila |title=Latin Grammys 2019 Winners: Complete List |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8543699/latin-grammys-2019-winners-list |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[MRC (company)|MRC]] |accessdate=9 September 2020 |date=14 November 2019}}</ref>
