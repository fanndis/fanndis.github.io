'''Ivo Vajgl''' (born 3 March 1943) is a [[Slovenia]]n politician and [[Member of the European Parliament]] (MEP) from [[Slovenia]]. He is a member of the [http://desus.si/ DeSUS] party (Democratic party of Pensioneers of Slovenia) and individual member of European Democratic Party ([http://www.democrats.eu/ EDP]), in the European Parliament he is a member of [https://alde.eu/en/ ALDE] Group - Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe.

==Political career==
Born in [[Maribor]], he attended the [[University of Ljubljana]]. He was appointed foreign minister by [[Prime Minister of Slovenia|Prime Minister]] [[Anton Rop]] on 6 July 2004, replacing [[Dimitrij Rupel]], who left the [[Liberal Democracy of Slovenia]] (LDS) party to join the opposition [[Slovenian Democratic Party]]. Vajgl's tenure ended on 3 December 2004 with Rupel's reappointment to the position in the new administration of [[Janez Janša]], formed after the [[2004 Slovenian parliamentary election|2004 elections]] to the [[National Assembly (Slovenia)|National Assembly]], which were held on 3 October.

As foreign minister, Vajgl joined Rop in [[signatories to the Treaty establishing a Constitution for Europe|signing]] the [[Treaty establishing a Constitution for Europe]] in [[Rome]] on 29 October 2004. Between 2004 and 2007, Vajgl worked as the advisor for foreign policy in the cabinet of [[President of Slovenia]] [[Janez Drnovšek]]. From the mid-1990s to 2007, Vajgl was a member of the LDS party. In 2007, Vajgl left the LDS party and joined the newly formed party ''[[Zares]]''. In 2008, as a member of the Zares party, he was elected to the National Assembly of the Republic of Slovenia and became the Chairman of the Foreign Policy Committee.

From 1992 to 1996, he was the Slovenian Ambassador to Sweden as well as all the Nordic and Baltic countries. From 1998 to 2002, he was Slovenian Ambassador to Austria and head of the Slovenian Mission to the OSCE in Vienna. His last post as Slovenian Ambassador took him again to the German Capital, this time Berlin, from 2002 to 2004.

In the 2009 European Parliament [http://www.dvk-rs.si/arhivi/ep2009/ elections], Ivo Vajgl headed the list of seven candidates for the party Zares. On 7 June 2009 he was elected a Member of the European Parliament with 9.77% of the vote and 21,031 preferential votes.

In 2014  European Parliament [http://www.volitve.gov.si/ep2014/ elections], Ivo Vajgl was elected on the list of the DeSUS party with 8.12% of the vote and 21,569 preferential votes.

Vajgl is an art lover. He speaks English, German, Italian, and French, as well as the languages of the peoples of former Yugoslavia{{Citation needed|date=February 2019}}.{{clarify|date=February 2019}}
