The '''2010 French Open''' was a tennis tournament played on [[Tennis court#Clay courts|outdoor clay courts]]. It was the 114th edition of the [[French Open]], and the second [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event of the year. It took place at the [[Stade Roland Garros]] in Paris, France, from 23 May through 6 June 2010.<ref>{{Cite web
  | title = Official Site of the French Open | publisher = [[Association of Tennis Professionals]] | url =  http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Tournaments/Roland-Garros.aspx | accessdate =22 April 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100527120404/http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Tournaments/Roland-Garros.aspx| archivedate= 27 May 2010 | url-status= live}}</ref>

[[Roger Federer]] and [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] were the defending champions. Federer lost to [[Robin Söderling]] in the quarterfinals, while Kuznetsova lost to [[Maria Kirilenko]] in the third round.

The 2010 French Open also featured the return of four-time champion [[Justine Henin]], who retired immediately before the [[2008 French Open]], where she was the 3-time defending champion.

==Day-by-day summaries==
{{main|2010 French Open – Day-by-day Summaries}}

==Seniors==

===Men's singles===
{{main|2010 French Open – Men's Singles}}
{{flagicon|ESP}} '''[[Rafael Nadal]]''' defeated {{flagicon|SWE}} [[Robin Söderling]], 6–4, 6–2, 6–4
*It was Nadal's 4th title of this year and the 40th of his career. It was his fifth win in six years at Roland Garros and his seventh Grand Slam men's singles victory.
*Nadal reclaimed the No. 1 ATP ranking with this victory.
*Nadal's victory also completed a historic 'Clay Slam' for Nadal, seeing him become the first person in history to win all [[ATP Tour Masters 1000|Masters 1000]] tournaments on clay (Monte Carlo, Rome and Madrid), as well as the French Open, in the same calendar year.
*This was the second time Nadal had won the French Open without dropping a set.

===Women's singles===
{{main|2010 French Open – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Francesca Schiavone]]''' defeated {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Samantha Stosur]], 6–4, 7–6<sup>(7–2)</sup>
*Both Schiavone and Stosur were first-time Grand Slam finalists.
*It was Schiavone's second title of the year, the fourth of her career, and her first major title.

===Men's doubles===
{{main|2010 French Open – Men's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|CAN}} '''[[Daniel Nestor]]''' / {{flagicon|SRB|2004}} '''[[Nenad Zimonjić]]''' defeated {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Lukáš Dlouhý]] / {{flagicon|IND}} [[Leander Paes]], 7–5, 6–2

===Women's doubles===
{{main|2010 French Open – Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]''' / {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Venus Williams]]''' defeated {{flagicon|CZE}} [[Květa Peschke]] / {{flagicon|SLO}} [[Katarina Srebotnik]], 6–2, 6–3
* The Williams sisters won their 12th Grand Slam doubles title and 2nd at the French Open. With this, they hold all Grand Slam doubles titles simultaneously.
* Also, they have won the career women's doubles golden slam for the second time in their respective careers.

===Mixed doubles===
{{main|2010 French Open – Mixed Doubles}}
{{flagicon|SVN}} '''[[Katarina Srebotnik]]''' / {{flagicon|SRB|2004}} '''[[Nenad Zimonjić]]''' defeated {{flagicon|KAZ}} [[Yaroslava Shvedova]] / {{flagicon|AUT}} [[Julian Knowle]], 4–6, 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>, [11–9]
* Srebotnik and Zimonjić both won their fourth Grand Slam mixed doubles title.

===Wheelchair men's doubles===

{{flagicon|FRA}} '''[[Stéphane Houdet]]''' / {{flagicon|JPN}} '''[[Shingo Kunieda]]''' defeated {{flagicon|NED}} [[Robin Ammerlaan]] / {{flagicon|SWE}} [[Stefan Olsson]], 6–0, 5–7, [10–8]
* Houdet wins his fourth wheelchair Grand Slam doubles title and the first at French Open, and Kunieda wins his ninth wheelchair Grand Slam doubles title and second at French.

===Wheelchair women's doubles===
{{main|2010 French Open – Wheelchair Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|AUS}} '''[[Daniela Di Toro]]''' / {{flagicon|NED}} '''[[Aniek van Koot]]''' defeated {{flagicon|NED}} [[Esther Vergeer]] / {{flagicon|NED}} [[Sharon Walraven]], 3–6, 6–3, [10–4]
* Di Toro and van Koot win their first wheelchair Grand Slam title in doubles.
