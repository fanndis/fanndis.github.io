'''Boston.com''' is a regional website that offers news and information about the [[Boston, Massachusetts]], region.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/industry-us-bostonglobe/boston-globe-launches-subscription-web-site-idUSTRE78B4LM20110912|title=Boston Globe launches subscription web site|date=2011-09-12|publisher=Reuters|accessdate=2018-02-20}}</ref> It is owned and operated by [[Boston Globe Media Partners]], the publisher of ''[[The Boston Globe]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bostonglobemedia.com/|title=Boston Globe Media|website=Boston Globe Media|accessdate=2018-02-20}}</ref>

Boston.com was one of the first news websites on the public web, launched in late October 1995 by Boston Globe Electronic Publishing Inc. The domain name was purchased from the Boston-area cafe chain [[Au Bon Pain]] in exchange for print advertisements for charities chosen by Au Bon Pain's CEO.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.boston.com/news/innovation/2013/06/20/with-buyers-eyeing-globe-real-estate-how-much-is-in-a-domain-name|title=With buyers eyeing Globe real estate, how much is in a (domain) name?|date=2013-06-20|work=Boston.com|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

Since its inception, Boston.com has covered a wide range of stories of interest to people in the region. It was the primary website of ''The Boston Globe'' until late 2011.{{cn|date=July 2019}}

Boston.com currently{{when?|date=July 2019}} covers local, national, and international news, professional sports, weather, traffic, entertainment, and lifestyle.{{cn|date=July 2019}}

The site also maintains a mobile application for iPhone and Android devices. It connects readers with stories featured on the website.<ref>{{Citation|last=LLC|first=Boston Globe Media Partners|title=Boston.com|date=2017-12-12|url=https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.thoughtbot.boston_com_news&hl=en|publisher=Boston Globe Media Partners, LLC|accessdate=2018-02-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/boston-com/id361786318?mt=8|title=Boston.com on the App Store|website=App Store|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

== Relation to ''The Boston Globe'' ==
On September 12, 2011, ''The Boston Globe'' launched a separate site at BostonGlobe.com that put most content from its newsroom behind a paywall.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.adweek.com/digital/boston-globe-paywall-site/|title=''The Boston Globe'' Launches A Beautiful, Brand New Paywall Website|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> Since that time, Boston.com has been a separate, standalone entity providing coverage of local news, sports, weather, and leisure on a free, advertising-supported platform. The two media outlets share office space at 1 Exchange Place in downtown Boston.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/metro/2017/06/21/years-after-leaving-newspaper-row-globe-returns-downtown/AcjrianLw3gvX3e1YFrqAL/story.html|title=59 years after leaving Newspaper Row, Globe returns to downtown |work=[[The Boston Globe]]|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>
