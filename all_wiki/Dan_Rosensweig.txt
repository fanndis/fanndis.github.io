'''Dan Rosensweig''' is an American business executive who is chairman, president and [[chief executive officer]] (CEO) of student services company [[Chegg]].<ref name="Moving Beyond Textbook Rentals to 'Student Hub'">{{cite web|url=http://chronicle.com/article/From-Textbook-Rentals-to/138793/| title=Moving Beyond Textbook Rentals to 'Student Hub'|last=New|first=Jake|website=The Chronicle of Higher Education|date=April 29, 2013}}</ref> Previously, Rosensweig served as president and CEO of ''[[Guitar Hero]]''; COO at [[Yahoo!]]; president of [[CNET]], and president and CEO of [[ZDNet]].

==Early life and education==
Rosensweig was born in [[Dobbs Ferry, New York]] and raised in [[Scarsdale, New York]], where he graduated from [[Scarsdale High School]]. He played [[offensive lineman]] on the school's football team.<ref name="Crunchbase">{{cite web|url=http://www.crunchbase.com/person/dan-rosensweig| title=Dan Rosensweig's Profile|publisher=Crunchbase}}</ref>

Rosensweig enrolled at [[Hobart and William Smith]], graduating in 1983 with a Bachelor of Arts in [[political science]]. There, Rosensweig was a member of ''[[Kappa Sigma]]'' fraternity and the [[Little Theatre Movement|Little Theatre]], and studied abroad in [[London]].<ref name="President's Forum">{{cite web|url=http://www.crunchbase.com/person/dan-rosensweig| title=Hobart and William Smith Colleges President's Forum}}</ref>

==Career==
Following college, Rosensweig's first job was at [[Dictaphone]], then an independent [[subsidiary]] of [[Pitney Bowes]], selling word processors [[door-to-door]] in [[Manhattan]]. Within three hours on the job, he was informed that the company had to [[lay off]] nearly 1,000 people, including his division.<ref name="Moving Beyond Textbook Rentals to 'Student Hub'" />

In 1983, Rosensweig began working at [[Ziff Davis]] as a [[cold caller]] selling magazines to [[mom-and-pop]] computer retail stores, then worked his way up through the circulation department, to classified ad sales and display ad sales, and then to associate publisher of ''[[PC Magazine]]''.<ref name="Moving Beyond Textbook Rentals to 'Student Hub'" /> Under Rosensweig's leadership, ''PC Magazine'' became the leading computer magazine in both audience reach and revenue.<ref name="Moving Beyond Textbook Rentals to 'Student Hub'"/>

In 1996, Rosensweig led the Ziff Davis launch of a series of [[Internet]] print magazines, including, by license, the acclaimed ''[[Yahoo! Internet Life]]''.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sKj3AwAAQBAJ&pg=PT47|title=Marissa Mayer and the Fight to Save Yahoo!|last=Carlson|first=Nicholas|publisher=Grand Central Publishing|page=47|date=2015}}</ref> In 1998, he took over Ziff Davis' [[Internet]] Operation as president and CEO of [[ZiffNet]], now known as [[ZDNet]], where he took the company public and sold it to [[CNET]], in 2001.<ref name="Crunchbase" /> Rosensweig played a critical role in the successful merger with CNET.<ref name=Berman>{{cite web|url=https://moneyinc.com/10-things-didnt-know-chegg-ceo-dan-rosensweig/| last=Berman|first=Nat|title=10 Things You Didn’t Know about Chegg CEO Dan Rosensweig|website=[[Money, Inc.]]|date=}}</ref> During his tenure at CNET, Rosensweig served as a key participant in company-wide efforts to develop and introduce innovative new Internet advertising formats, such as interactive messaging.<ref name="Daniel L. Rosensweig">{{cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=231300&ticker=ATVI&previousCapId=22903&previousTitle=Sequoia%20Capital| title=Daniel L. Rosensweig's Executive Profile|publisher=[[Businessweek]]}}</ref>

In 2002, Rosensweig joined [[Yahoo!]] as COO, where he oversaw global operations through 2006,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-yahoo-workers-idUSKCN1010Q5 |title=Silicon Valley's Yahoo diaspora mourns company's decline|last1=Todd|first1=Deborah M.|last2=Dastin|first2=Jeffrey|publisher=[[Reuters]]|date=July 21, 2016}}</ref><ref name=Cisco>{{cite web|url=https://newsroom.cisco.com/dlls/dan_rosensweig.pdf| title=Dan Rosensweig Chief Operating Officer Yahoo!}}</ref> and developed a mentoring program.

In 2007, Rosensweig became a partner of private investment firm [[Quadrangle Group]], founded by [[Steven Rattner]], opening an office in [[Silicon Valley]].<ref name="Chegg's Management Team">{{cite web|url=http://www.chegg.com/managementteam| title=Chegg's Management Team}}</ref>

In March 2009, Rosensweig became president and CEO of ''[[Guitar Hero]]''; under his management, the company launched ''[[Band Hero]]'' and ''[[DJ Hero]]''.<ref name="Chegg's Management Team" />

In February 2010, Rosensweig joined [[Chegg]] as president and CEO,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/07/11/business/11corner.html|title=Remember to Thank Your Star Players|publisher=[[The New York Times]]|date=July 10, 2010}}</ref> where he has overseen expansion and multiple acquisitions, and heads operations and executive management. He launched the company's IPO in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://jewishbusinessnews.com/2013/11/04/dan-rosensweig-adds-48-4-million-to-original-funding-target-of-150-million-on-cheggs-ipo/| last=Hecht|first=Albert|title=Dan Rosensweig Adds $48.4 Million To Original Funding Target Of $150 Million On Chegg’s IPO|website=[[Jewish Business News]]|date=November 4, 2013}}</ref> Chegg expanded from textbook rental into a portfolio of student services under his leadership,<ref name="Moving Beyond Textbook Rentals to 'Student Hub'" /><ref name="Chegg's Management Team" /> transforming Chegg's digital assets to meet student needs.

Rosensweig is a proponent of [[mentoring]],<ref name=Forbes>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/kymmcnicholas/2011/08/18/dan-rosensweig-his-journey-from-yahoo-to-guitar-hero-then-chegg/#17b0c1ff46bd| last=McNicholas|first=Kym|title=Dan Rosensweig: His Journey From Yahoo To Guitar Hero Then Chegg|website=[[Forbes]]|date=August 18, 2011}}</ref> and of workplace equality.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fortune.com/2013/03/15/advice-from-a-male-ceo-we-all-need-to-lean-in/| last=Sellers|first=Patricia|title=Advice from a male CEO: "We all need to Lean In"|website=Fortune|date=November 15, 2013}}</ref> He is an investor in many [[Silicon Valley]] companies, including [[Rent The Runway]] and [[Reputation.com]].<ref name="Chegg's Management Team" />

==Personal life==
Rosensweig is married and has two daughters. He is a noted fan of [[Bruce Springsteen]].<ref name="Chegg's Management Team" />

==Boards==
Rosensweig participates on the [[advisory board]] of [[non-profit organization|NPO]] [[DonorsChoose.org]], has served as a Executives in Residence for the mentoring program of The Media and Technology Program at [[Columbia University]]; and sits on the [[board of directors|boards of directors]] of [[Adobe, Inc.]], [[VOX Media]], [[Katalyst Media]], [[Fender Musical Instruments Corporation|Fender]], and [[OZY Media]], among others.

He also sits on the board at [[Hobart and William Smith Colleges]], where the Rosensweig Learning Commons, Warren Hunting Smith Library is named for him. He is a [[Colgate University]] ''emeritus'' trustee.<ref name="Chegg's Management Team" /> He also works with the [[Boys & Girls Clubs of America|Boys and Girls Club]].
