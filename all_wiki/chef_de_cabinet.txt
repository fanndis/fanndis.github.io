In several French-speaking countries and international organisations, a '''''chef de cabinet''''' ([[French language|French]]; literally "Head of Office") is a senior [[civil servant]] or official who acts as an [[Personal assistant|aide]] or [[private secretary]] to a government figure, typically a [[Minister (government)|minister]]. They typically work in the ministerial office (''cabinet'').

The title is used by the head of an office in the [[United Nations Secretariat]], appointed by the [[United Nations Secretary-General|Secretary-General]], or in the [[European Commission]], appointed by an individual [[European Commissioner]] for his or her [[Cabinet (European Commission)|personal cabinet]]. The position's rank and responsibilities are equivalent to a [[Chief of staff (politics)|chief of staff]]. The current ''chef de cabinet'' to the United Nations [[Secretary-General of the United Nations|Secretary&#8209;General]] is [[Maria Luiza Ribeiro Viotti]].<ref>https://www.un.org/sg/en/content/profiles/maria-luiza-ribeiro-viotti</ref>
