'''Heiko Echter von der Leyen''' (born 2 June 1955) is a German physician, professor of medicine, aristocrat and the husband of politician [[Ursula von der Leyen]]. He is a member of the [[Von der Leyen (family from Krefeld)|von der Leyen]] noble family.

He was born the son of physician Ulrich von der Leyen (1918–1992) and his wife Cornelia Maria née Groth (1922–2014). After finishing his medical education at the [[University of Hamburg]] and [[Hannover Medical School|Hanover Medical School]], he was on faculty at [[Stanford University]] from 1992 to 1996.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spiegel.de/wissenschaft/mensch/streit-ueber-stammzellgesetz-kleines-forschungsgebiet-sorgt-fuer-grosses-brimborium-a-481808.html|title=Streit über Stammzellgesetz: Kleines Forschungsgebiet sorgt für großes Brimborium - DER SPIEGEL - Wissenschaft|first=Franziska Badenschier, DER|last=SPIEGEL|website=www.spiegel.de}}</ref> He earned his [[Habilitation]] at Hanover Medical School in 1998<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forschung-und-lehre.de/fehler-404/|title=Fehler 404|date=November 4, 2020|website=www.forschung-und-lehre.de}}</ref> and became an adjunct full professor of internal medicine and experimental cardiology at Hanover Medical School in 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.apv-mainz.de/pharma-verfahrenstechnik/|title=APV|date=September 25, 2020|website=APV Mainz}}</ref>

He became the Director of Hanover Clinical Trial Center GmbH in 2005. Hanover Clinical Trial Center (HCTC) is an academic clinical research organization located at the campus of Hanover Medical School.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mhh.de/institute-zentren-forschungseinrichtungen/koordinationszentrum-fuer-klinische-studien-hctc-kks|title=Medizinische Hochschule Hannover : Koordinationszentrum für klinische Studien HCTC - KKS|website=www.mhh.de}}</ref>

Heiko and Ursula von der Leyen have seven children.
