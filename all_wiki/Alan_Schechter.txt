'''Alan Schechter''' (born 1936) is a political scientist. He is [[Professor Emeritus]] of [[Political Science]] at [[Wellesley College]] in [[Massachusetts]].<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=2019-10-10|title=Alan H. Schechter|url=http://www.wellesley.edu/politicalscience/faculty/schechter|website=Wellesley College}}</ref>

He was educated at [[Amherst College]], where he received his AB, and at [[Columbia University]], where he earned his [[PhD]].

Schechter was [[Hillary Rodham]]'s advisor during her years at Wellesley College and supervised [[Hillary Rodham senior thesis|her senior thesis]];<ref>{{cite web|first1=Bill Dedman Investigative reporter|last1=msnbc.com|accessdate=2019-10-10|title=How the Clintons wrapped up Hillary's thesis|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/17388394/ns/politics-decision_08/t/how-clintons-wrapped-hillarys-thesis/|date=7 September 2007|website=msnbc.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|first1=Aaron|last1=Blake|first2=Frances Stead|last2=Sellers|accessdate=2019-10-10|title=Hillary Clinton, Saul Alinsky and Lucifer, explained|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2016/07/20/hillary-clinton-saul-alinsky-and-lucifer-explained/|newspaper=Washington Post|date=20 July 2016|issn=0190-8286|via=www.washingtonpost.com}}</ref> [[Susan Estrich]]'s book ''[[The Case for Hillary Clinton]]'' mentions her experience also writing an honors thesis for Schechter (at a different time). He remains involved with the college, running the Wellesley in Washington internship program,<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=2019-10-10|title=Wellesley in Washington Summer Internship Program|url=http://www.wellesley.edu/politicalscience/washington|website=Wellesley College}}</ref> in which Rodham participated as a student and which continues to send approximately twenty women to [[Washington, D.C.|Washington]] for internships each summer.

Schechter is the former Chairman,<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=2019-10-10|title=President Clinton Names Alan Schechter to the J. William Fulbright Foreign Scholarship Board (11/21/00)|url=https://clintonwhitehouse4.archives.gov/textonly/library/hot_releases/November_21_2000_1.html|website=clintonwhitehouse4.archives.gov}}</ref> Vice-Chairman,<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=2019-10-10|title=WELLESLEY COLLEGE PROFESSOR ALAN H. SCHECHTER ELECTED VICE CHAIR OF FULBRIGHT FOREIGN SCHOLARSHIP BOARD|url=http://web.wellesley.edu/PublicAffairs/PAhomepage/schechter.html|website=web.wellesley.edu}}</ref> and member of the J. William Fulbright Foreign Scholarship Board ([[Fulbright Program]]), a Presidential appointment.
