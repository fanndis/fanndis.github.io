'''Bailey Bryan''' is an American singer-songwriter based in [[Nashville]]. Her single "Own It" debuted at number 60 on the U.S. Country Airplay chart.<ref name="own it chart">{{cite web|title=Bailey Bryan Own It Chart History|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/bailey-bryan/chart-history/country-airplay/song/996430|website=Billboard|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref>

==Early life==
Bailey Bryan was born in [[Sequim, Washington]].<ref name="npr bio">{{cite web|last1=Hight|first1=Jewly|title=At 19, Bailey Bryan Is Ready To 'Own It'|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2017/06/23/534074433/world-cafe-nashville-bailey-bryan|website=NPR.org|access-date=24 April 2018|language=en}}</ref> She began writing songs at age 12<ref name="red light">{{cite web|title=Bailey Bryan • Red Light Management|url=http://www.redlightmanagement.com/artists/bailey-bryan/|website=Red Light Management|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref> and [[busking]]<ref name="billboard bio">{{cite web|last1=Stark|first1=Phyllis|title=Chartbreaker: Bailey Bryan on Taylor Swift's Influence and How Spinal Surgery Inspired Her Breakout Single|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/magazine-feature/7809497/bailey-bryan-chartbreaker-taylor-swift-influence|website=Billboard|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref> locally before signing a publishing deal and relocating to Nashville.<ref name="rolling stone">{{cite web|last1=Hodge|first1=Will|last2=Menconi|first2=David|last3=Hudak|first3=Joseph|last4=Moss|first4=Marissa R.|last5=McKenna|first5=Brittney|title=10 New Country Artists You Need to Know: January 2017|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/lists/10-new-country-artists-you-need-to-know-january-2017-w461425/bailey-bryan-w461435|website=Rolling Stone|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2016, Bryan was nominated by [[Hillary Scott]] as an Artist of Tomorrow by the [[Grammy Award|Grammys]].<ref name="so far">{{cite web|last1=Dauphin|first1=Chuck|title=Bailey Bryan Premieres Breakup-Inspired 'Hard Drive Home,' Talks Mentor Hillary Scott|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/7759650/bailey-bryan-so-far-hillary-scott-mentor|website=Billboard|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref><ref name="CBS">{{cite web|title=GRAMMY Awards Cast: Bailey Bryan|url=https://www.cbs.com/shows/grammys/artist-of-tomorrow/nominee/bailey-bryan/|website=CBS|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref> In 2017, Bryan released "Own It" as her first single, which debuted at number 60 on the [[Country Airplay|U.S. Country Airplay]] chart. In April 2017, she released her debut [[extended play]] ''So Far''.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Next 10 Under 21: Which Young Artists Are Coming Up Next?|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/list/7981778/next-21-under-21-bhad-bhabie-finn-wolfhard|website=Billboard|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref> In October 2017, she released a cover of [[Drake (musician)|Drake's]] "[[Too Good]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2017/music/news/bailey-bryan-covers-drake-too-good-video-watch-1202593352/|title=Video Premiere: Bailey Bryan’s Sultry Country Cover of Drake’s ‘Too Good’|last=Willman|first=Chris|date=2017-10-18|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2019-08-01}}</ref> In early 2018, ''Billboard'' named her one of "15 Country Artists to Watch in 2018".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Dauphin|first1=Chuck|last2=Weatherby|first2=Taylor|title=15 Country Artists to Watch in 2018|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/8085893/country-artists-to-watch-2018|website=Billboard|access-date=24 April 2018}}</ref> She released the single "Songbird" in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dukes |first1=Billy |title=Bailey Bryan's Honest 'Songbird' Is All of Us |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/bailey-bryas-songbird/ |website=Taste of Country |access-date=30 November 2018 |language=en}}</ref> In May 2019, she released another Drake cover of "[[One Dance]]" with UK artist Kyla, who Drake originally sampled the song from. The song premiered through [[Clash (magazine)|''Clash'' magazine]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/watch-kyla-x-bailey-bryan-perform-one-dance|title=Watch Kyla x Bailey Bryan Perform 'One Dance'|website=Clash Magazine|language=en|access-date=2019-08-01}}</ref> That same month, she released the single "Perspective".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Morin |first1=Natalie |title=The Drop: Bailey Bryan Wrote A Love Song During A Breakup — Here's Her "Perspective" |url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2019/05/233073/the-drop-bailey-bryan-perspective-music-video-exclusive-premiere |website=www.refinery29.com |access-date=1 August 2019 |language=en}}</ref> In October 2019, she released the EP ''Perspective,''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/bailey-bryan-perspective-pop-music-894035/|title=Bailey Bryan Dives Headfirst into Pop on New 'Perspective' EP|last=Hudak|first=Joseph|date=2019-10-03|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> as well as a music video for the song "Where We Started" from the EP, which starred [[Mike Johnson (television personality)|Mike Johnson]] from ''[[The Bachelorette]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bailey Bryan Premieres New Music Video Starring Bachelorette's Mike Johnson as Her Love Interest|url=https://people.com/music/bailey-bryan-where-we-started-music-video-mike-johnson/|access-date=2020-07-31|website=PEOPLE.com|language=EN}}</ref> On June 26, she released the song "play w/ me".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bailey Bryan estrena "Play with Me"|url=http://revistakuadro.com/bailey-bryan-estrena-play-with-me/|access-date=2020-06-29|language=es-ES}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Bryan dubbed her songwriting as primarily deriving from [[Pop music|pop]], [[R&B]], [[Country music|country]], and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] styles,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ellwood-Hughes |first1=Pip |title=Watch: Bailey Bryan covers Drake's Too Good |url=https://www.entertainment-focus.com/music-section/ef-country/watch-bailey-bryan-covers-drakes-too-good/ |website=Entertainment Focus |access-date=1 August 2019 |date=24 October 2017}}</ref> and stated that her music is influenced by [[Taylor Swift]] and [[The Chicks]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=At 19, Bailey Bryan Is Ready To 'Own It'|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2017/06/23/534074433/world-cafe-nashville-bailey-bryan|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-09-11|website=[[NPR]]|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Crae|first=Ross|title=Rising country star Bailey Bryan on bringing her blend of styles to Scotland for the first time|url=https://www.sundaypost.com/fp/rising-country-star-bailey-bryan-on-bringing-her-blend-of-styles-to-scotland-for-the-first-time/|access-date=2020-09-11|website=The Sunday Post|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Stark|first=Phyllis|date=May 26, 2020|title=Chartbreaker: Bailey Bryan on Taylor Swift's Influence and How Spinal Surgery Inspired Her Breakout Single|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/magazine-feature/7809497/bailey-bryan-chartbreaker-taylor-swift-influence|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=September 11, 2020|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>
