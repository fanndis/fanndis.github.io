Several polling firms have conducted [[opinion polls]] during the term of the [[52nd New Zealand Parliament]] in the lead up to the [[2020 New Zealand general election|2020 general election]], which elects the [[53rd New Zealand Parliament|53rd Parliament]]. The 52nd Parliament was elected on 23 September 2017 and dissolved on 6 September 2020. The 2020 election was originally due to take place on Saturday 19 September 2020, but due to a second [[COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand|COVID-19 outbreak]] it was delayed until Saturday 17 October 2020.<ref name="Stuff: delayed"/>

Very few polls have been conducted compared to previous electoral cycles.<ref>{{cite news |author=Michael Appleton |date=5 November 2018 |title=Why the drought in New Zealand opinion polling matters |work=The Spinoff |url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/politics/05-11-2018/why-the-drought-in-new-zealand-opinion-polling-matters/ |access-date=5 November 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181105075529/https://thespinoff.co.nz/politics/05-11-2018/why-the-drought-in-new-zealand-opinion-polling-matters/ |archive-date=5 November 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> The two regular polls are [[Television New Zealand]] (''[[1 News (New Zealand)|1 News]]''), conducted by [[Colmar Brunton]], and [[Roy Morgan Research]], with less frequent polls from [[MediaWorks New Zealand]] ([[Newshub]]) Reid Research. The sample size, margin of error and confidence interval of each poll varies by organisation and date.

Poll results are listed in the table below in reverse chronological order. The highest percentage figure in each polling survey is displayed in bold, and the background shaded in the leading party's colour. The 'party lead' column shows the percentage-point difference between the two parties with the highest figures. In the instance of a tie, both figures are shaded and displayed in bold. Percentages may not add to 100 percent due to polls not reporting figures for all minor parties and due to rounding. Refusals are generally excluded from the party vote percentages, while question wording and the treatment of "don't know" responses and those not intending to vote may vary between survey organisations.

The parties shown in the table are [[New Zealand National Party|National]] (NAT), [[New Zealand Labour Party|Labour]] (LAB), [[New Zealand First]] (NZF), [[Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand|Green]] (GRN), [[ACT New Zealand|ACT]], [[The Opportunities Party|Opportunities]] (TOP), [[Māori Party|Māori]] (MRI), [[New Conservative Party (New Zealand)|New Conservative]] (NCP) and [[Advance New Zealand]] (ANZ). Other parties have also registered in some polls, but are not listed in this table.

===UMR and Curia polls===
These polls are typically unpublished and are used internally for Labour (UMR) and National (Curia). Although these polls are sometimes leaked or partially leaked, their details are not publicly available for viewing and scrutinising. Because not all of their polls are made public, it is likely that those that are released are cherry-picked and therefore may not truly indicate ongoing trends.
