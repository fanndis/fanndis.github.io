'''Catherine Lloyd Burns''' (born April 19, 1961)<ref name="filmreference">{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/57/Catherine-Lloyd-Burns.html|title=Catherine Lloyd Burns Biography (1961-)|publisher=filmreference|accessdate=2009-08-18}}</ref> is an actress and writer.<ref name="filmreference" /><ref name="catherinelloydburns.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.catherinelloydburns.com/|title=Catherine Lloyd Burns |accessdate=2009-08-18}}</ref>

==Acting==
She has appeared in multiple television shows, and was a regular on ''[[Partners (1995 TV series)|Partners]]'' and ''[[LateLine]]''.<ref name="WVAH-TV Fox 11">{{cite web|url=http://www.wvah.com/programs/malcolminthemiddle/catherinelloydburns.shtml|title=Catherine Lloyd Burns|publisher=WVAH-TV Fox 11|accessdate=2009-12-04|location=Charleston-Huntington, WV|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20001012131320/http://www.wvah.com/programs/malcolminthemiddle/catherinelloydburns.shtml|archivedate=2000-10-12}}</ref> She played Caroline Miller, Malcolm's "overly earnest" teacher, in ''[[Malcolm in the Middle]]''.<ref name="LA Weekly 2000-02-24">{{cite news|url=http://www.laweekly.com/2000-02-24/film-tv/kids-in-the-hall/|title=Kids in the Hall|last=Lloyd|first=Robert|date=2000-02-24|work=LA Weekly|accessdate=2009-12-04}}</ref> Burns has appeared in numerous feature films and the independent film ''Everything Put Together''. Along with her role, she was a cowriter of the independent film ''[[Everything Put Together]]''.<ref name="NY Times 2001-11-09">{{cite news|url=https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9905E6D91F39F93AA35752C1A9679C8B63&pagewanted=print|title=Film Review; Losing Her Child to a Mysterious Killer|last=Holden|first=Stephen|date=2001-11-09|work=The New York Times|accessdate=2009-12-03}}</ref><ref name="Village Voice 2001-11-03">{{cite news|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2001-11-13/film/requiem-suite/|title=Requiem Suite  Marc Forster's Symphonies of Sorrow|last=Fuller|first=Graham|date=2001-11-13|work=The Village Voice|accessdate=2009-12-03}}</ref> She has also worked in theater with New York's [[Naked Angels (theater company)|Naked Angels]].<ref name="WVAH-TV Fox 11"/>

==Writing==
The film ''Everything Put Together'' was written by a number of authors, including Catherine and her husband. It was nominated for the [[Grand Jury Prize]] at the Sundance Film Festivals in 2000. In 2001, it was nominated for an [[Independent Spirit Award]] as the best feature made for under $500,000. Director [[Marc Forster]] won Independent Spirit's Someone to Watch Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/story?id=107779&page=1#.UDEAIlJOiVo|title=Tiger Takes 3 Spirit Awards|date=2001-03-24|work=ABC News|accessdate=2012-08-19}}</ref>

Her first<ref name="catherinelloydburns.com" /> book is ''It Hit Me Like a Ton of Bricks'', which was published by [[Farrar, Straus and Giroux]] in 2007. The book is a [[biography]], from the point of view of the author. It centers around personal [[memoirs]], and [[family relationships]].<ref name="Boston Globe">{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/books/articles/2006/06/04/on_families_happy_and_unhappy_in_their_own_ways/|title=On families, happy and unhappy in their own ways|last=Rand|first=Naomi|date=2006-06-04|work=The Boston Globe 2006-06-04|accessdate=2009-12-03}}</ref><ref name="GoogleBooks">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=58KBliR-gk0C&dq=It+Hit+Me+Like+a+Ton+of+Bricks|title=It Hit Me Like a Ton of Bricks: A Memoir of a Mother and Daughter |publisher=Google Books|accessdate=2009-08-18}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Burns is Jewish and was born in New York City. She is married to Adam Forgash, with one daughter.<ref name="filmreference" /><ref name="catherinelloydburns.com" /> They both live in New York. Her late father, Lloyd Burns, worked in entertainment, and her Canadian-born mother, [[Red Burns]] (née Gennis), founded the Interactive Telecommunications Program (NYU). Her husband is a writer and producer.<ref name="filmreference" />
