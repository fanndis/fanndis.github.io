'''Villa Guardamangia''' ([[Italian language|Italian]] – 'look' and 'eat'), formerly known as '''Casa Medina'''<ref name="Connell">{{cite book |last=Connell |first=Brian |title=Manifest Destiny: A Study in Five Profiles of the Rise and Influence of the Mountbatten Family |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qVAZAAAAIAAJ&q=medina+guardamangia&dq=medina+guardamangia |date=1953 |publisher=Cassell}}</ref><ref name=gaullaumier1987>{{cite book |last1=Guillaumier |first1=Alfie |title=Bliet u Rħula Maltin |date=1987 |publisher=Valletta Publishing & Promotion Co. Ltd. |location=[[Valletta]] |page=268 |language=Maltese}}</ref> and sometimes referred to as ''Casa Guardamangia'',<ref>{{cite news |last1=De Marco |first1=Mario |authorlink1=Mario de Marco |title=Sharing some palatial thoughts |url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150502/opinion/sharing-some-palatial-thoughts.566404 |newspaper=[[Times of Malta]] |date=2 May 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126110036/http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150502/opinion/sharing-some-palatial-thoughts.566404 |archivedate=26 November 2015}}</ref> is a {{convert|16791|sqft|m2}} [[townhouse]] in [[Gwardamanġa]], [[Pietà, Malta|Pietà]], [[Malta]], which served as the residence of Princess Elizabeth, Duchess of Edinburgh (later Queen [[Elizabeth II]]), and [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh]], between 1949 and 1951, while Philip was stationed in Malta as a naval officer.

==History==
Villa Guardamangia was built in 1900<ref name=godfreydykes/><ref>{{cite web |title=Scheduled Property - Gwardamanġa |website=[[Malta Environment and Planning Authority]] |url=http://www.mepa.org.mt/npi/pieta/pieta_se/listed/101.htm |accessdate=February 14, 2006 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927072717/http://www.mepa.org.mt/npi/pieta/pieta_se/listed/101.htm |archivedate=September 27, 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.grechclaudio.com/kampanja-elettorali/l-ewwel-distrett/gwardamangia |title=Gwardamangia |website=Grech Claudio |language=Maltese |accessdate=December 1, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208083937/http://www.grechclaudio.com/kampanja-elettorali/l-ewwel-distrett/gwardamangia |archivedate=December 8, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Martin |first1=Ivan |title=Queen Elizabeth's Malta home in disrepair and must be restored before the royal visit |url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150426/local/queen-elizabeths-malta-home-in-disrepair-and-must-be-restored-before.565512 |newspaper=Times of Malta |date=April 26, 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126212359/http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150426/local/queen-elizabeths-malta-home-in-disrepair-and-must-be-restored-before.565512 |archivedate=November 26, 2015}}</ref> by Sir [[Augusto Bartolo]]<ref name=auto>{{cite magazine |title=Villa Guardamangia |magazine=The Crown Colonist |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=C5AeAQAAMAAJ&q=Villa+Guardamangia&dq=Villa+Guardamangia |date=1950 |volume=20 |page=455}}</ref> and was called ''Casa Medina''.{{sfnp|Hicks|2014|p=27}}<ref>{{cite book |last=Hatch |first=Alden |authorlink=Alden Hatch |title=The Mountbattens |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bYByAAAAMAAJ&q=casa+medina+malta&dq=casa+medina+malta |date=1966 |publisher=W.H. Allen |page=208}}</ref> It was originally a farmhouse.<ref>{{cite book |last=Christopher |first=Peter H. |date=2017 |title=The Spanish Knight's Secret |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=TkxDDgAAQBAJ&pg=PT61 |publisher=[[FriesenPress]] |page=61 |isbn=978-1-4602-9709-4}}</ref> It consists of 18 rooms in the living quarters, stables for the animals, a large garden area with a walk-path and a war shelter.<ref>https://timesofmalta.com/articles/view/glimpse-of-a-villas-glorious-past.811278</ref>

In around 1929, the villa was first leased to [[Louis Mountbatten, 1st Earl Mountbatten of Burma|Louis Mountbatten]],<ref name=godfreydykes/> who had interest in it because of its proximity to [[Marsa, Malta|Marsa]], which has a [[horse racing]] track and a [[golf course]] that suited his lavish lifestyle.<ref name=godfreydykes/> The villa was in a bad state and divided into apartments,<ref name="Connell"/> leading the Mountbattens to reside in two rooms at the [[Hotel Phoenicia]] in [[Floriana]]<ref name="Connell"/> while the house was being renovated.{{sfnp|Morgan|1991|p=439}} Mountbatten bought the villa after some time<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20121216/life-features/maltese-seaman-who-survived-sinking-of-mountbatten-s-ship.449833 |title=Maltese seaman who survived sinking of Mountbatten's ship |first=Alfred Conti |last=Borda |date=December 16, 2012 |newspaper=Times of Malta |accessdate=1 January 2018}}</ref> and frequented it while stationed in Malta as Commander-in-Chief of the [[Mediterranean Fleet]] in the 1950s.<ref name=godfreydykes>{{cite web |title=The Malta home of TRH's The Duke and Duchess of Edinburgh at the Villa Guardamangia, Pieta during the 1949 to 1951 period |url=http://www.godfreydykes.info/villa_guardamangia.html |website=godfreydykes.info |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126112322/http://www.godfreydykes.info/villa_guardamangia.html |archivedate=26 November 2015}}</ref>

When the Duke and Duchess of Edinburgh came to Malta at first they lodged at [[San Anton Palace]], hosted by [[Gerald Strickland]] and his wife.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20151115/life-features/Queen-Elizabeth-s-stays-in-Malta-over-the-years.592305 |title=Queen Elizabeth's stays in Malta over the years |first=Michael |last=Galea |date=November 15, 2015 |newspaper=Times of Malta |accessdate=1 January 2018}}</ref> Princess Elizabeth (later Queen [[Elizabeth II]]) and her then-fiancé, Philip Mountbatten (later [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh]]), first stayed at Guardamangia in 1946.<ref>{{cite book |last=Dexter |first=Margaret |title=Malta Remembered |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LTq0kWshOb4C&pg=PR11&dq=elizabeth+in+malta |date=13 March 2009 |publisher=iUniverse |isbn=978-0-595-89670-7 |page=11}}</ref> The couple returned a number of times between then and 1952, while Philip was [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh#Marriage|stationed in Malta as a Royal Navy officer]]<ref name=telegraph2015-04-30>{{cite news |last1=Alexander |first1=Adam |title=Row in Malta over Queen's crumbling former home |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/europe/malta/11572954/Row-in-Malta-over-Queens-crumbling-former-home.html |newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |date=30 April 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151115123341/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/europe/malta/11572954/Row-in-Malta-over-Queens-crumbling-former-home.html |archivedate=15 November 2015}}</ref> and Elizabeth worked with the [[SSAFA|Soldiers, Sailors, Airmen Families Association]] (SSAFA) at [[Auberge de Castile]].{{sfnp|Hicks|2014|p=171}} Mountbatten eventually passed the villa to the royal couple<ref>{{cite book|last=Morrow |first=Ann |title=The Queen |url=https://archive.org/details/queenmorr00morr |url-access=registration |date=1 August 1983 |publisher=W. Morrow |isbn=978-0-688-02136-8}}</ref>{{sfnp|Edwards|1990}} and they resided there continuously between 1949 and 1951. It has been suggested that their eldest child, [[Charles, Prince of Wales|Prince Charles]], was conceived at the villa.<ref name=telegraph2015-04-30/> The Queen has described her stay on Malta as one of the best periods of her life, as it was the only time she was able to live "normally".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Castillo |first1=Ruth |title="They were proud that she lived here!" – G'Mangia residents on the Queen |url=http://www.tvm.com.mt/en/news/they-were-proud-that-she-lived-here-gmangia-residents-on-the-queen/ |website=[[TVM (Malta)|TVM]] |date=26 November 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126214748/http://www.tvm.com.mt/en/news/they-were-proud-that-she-lived-here-gmangia-residents-on-the-queen/ |archivedate=26 November 2015}}</ref>

Queen Elizabeth II visited Villa Guardamangia during her state visit to Malta in 1992 and, in 2007, she and the Duke of Edinburgh celebrated their 60th anniversary there.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.independent.com.mt/articles/2015-10-26/football/Milan-16-year-old-Donnarumma-youngest-keeper-to-start-Serie-A-match-6736144214 |title='It looks rather sad now': Queen's forlorn response on villa where she once lived |date=28 November 2015 |newspaper=[[The Malta Independent]] |accessdate=1 January 2018}}</ref> The Queen was given a painting of Villa Guardamangia by the Maltese High Commissioner in London, Norman Hamilton, in 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://netnews.com.mt/news/villa-guardamangia-ghal-darba-darbtejn/ |title=Guardamangia...ghal darba darbtejn! |website=Net News |date=28 November 2015 |language=Maltese}}</ref> When the Queen was in Malta for the [[Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting 2015]], she asked to see the villa, but was reportedly refused by its owners,<ref name=telegraph2015-04-30/> the family of Ġużè Schembri,<ref name=gaullaumier1987/> as it was in a poor state of repair and subject of a dispute between its owners and the government.<ref name=telegraph2015-11-25/> President [[Marie Louise Coleiro Preca]] presented the Queen with another painting of the villa's façade.<ref>{{cite news |title=Painting of Villa Guardamangia presented to the Queen |url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20151126/local/painting-of-villa-guardamangia-presented-to-the-queen.593619 |newspaper=Times of Malta |date=26 November 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126185550/http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20151126/local/painting-of-villa-guardamangia-presented-to-the-queen.593619 |archivedate=26 November 2015}}</ref>

The building was for sale in 2019 with the asking price at €5,900,000,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofmalta.com/articles/view/historic-potential-of-queens-old-home.715809 |title=Historic potential of Queen's old home |publisher=Timesofmalta.com |date=1940-08-22 |accessdate=2019-10-23}}</ref> but if it had to be bought from an agent it would have been sold for around €6,500,000 or more.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cheatsheet.com/entertainment/queen-elizabeths-former-home-in-malta-is-on-the-market-for-6-7-million.html/|title=Queen Elizabeth's Former Home in Malta Is on the Market for $6.7 Million|first1=Megan|last1=Elliott|first2=More|last2=Articles|first3=2019|last3=June 14|date=June 14, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.homesofquality.com.mt/en/property/914956/Gwardamangia-Villa|title=Gwardamangia - Villa|website=www.homesofquality.com.mt}}</ref> Public request has asked the [[Maltese Government]] to purchase the building as state property, restore it and open it to the public.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maltatoday.com.mt/business/property/95590/malta_palazzo_that_served_as_queen_elizabeths_home_on_sale_for_6_million|title=Malta palazzo that served as Queen Elizabeth's home on sale for €6 million|website=MaltaToday.com.mt}}</ref> Significant movable property were sold off in a public auction in September 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofmalta.com/articles/view/contents-of-queens-malta-home-to-be-sold-at-auction.734545|title=Contents of Queen's Malta home to be sold at auction|website=Times of Malta}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://belgraviaauctions.com/auctions/13th-14th-september-2019-auction-antiques-works-of-art/|title=September 13th & 14th, 2019: Antiques & Works of Art|first=Joe|last=Sammut|website=Belgravia}}</ref> [[Word of mouth]] has it that the British monarchy was interested in purchasing the building.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofmalta.com/articles/view/inside-the-maltese-house-the-queen-called-home.736952 |title=Inside the Maltese house the Queen called home |publisher=Timesofmalta.com |date=1940-08-22 |accessdate=2019-10-23}}</ref>

==Building and gardens==

[[File:Villa_Guardamangia_Malta.jpeg|thumb|Front façade with plaque reading "Villa Guardamangia".]]

[[File:Villa_Guardamangia_-_Pietà.jpeg|thumb|The Villa is in a dilapidated state.]]

The villa is found just outside the outskirts<ref>{{cite book |last=Campbell |first=Judith |title=Elizabeth and Philip |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=D1EKAQAAMAAJ&q=Villa+Guardamangia+roof&dq=Villa+Guardamangia+roof |date=14 September 1972 |publisher=Barker |page=61}}</ref> and suburb<ref name="bbc.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/magazine-34932651 |title=A legacy of fry-ups and red postboxes |date=29 November 2015 |website=BBC News |accessdate=1 January 2018}}</ref> of [[Valletta]] in the hamlet of [[Guardamangia]]<ref>{{cite book |author=DrGoogelberg |title=English Princesses |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=UQf1AwAAQBAJ&pg=PA172&dq=Villa+Guardamangia+street |date=2012 |publisher=[[Lulu.com]] |isbn=978-1-291-07969-2|page=172}}</ref> set just at the crest<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pieta-apartments.com/#!explore-hotel/c1nt3 |title=Apartments For Sale |work=Pieta Apartments |access-date=2015-12-01 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208101723/http://www.pieta-apartments.com/#!explore-hotel/c1nt3 |archive-date=2015-12-08 |url-status=dead }}</ref> in a quiet residential area<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.express.co.uk/travel/articles/620956/Malta-royal-reign-Explore-distinctly-British-isle-regal-heritage-Queen |title=Malta: Explore this distinctly British isle's regal heritage |first=Juliet |last=Rix |date=November 20, 2015 |newspaper=[[Daily Express]] |accessdate=1 January 2018}}</ref> in a narrow street.<ref>{{cite book |last=Cathcart |first=Helen |title=The Queen Herself |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Ea1MX5Qj98AC&q=Villa+Guardamangia+street&dq=Villa+Guardamangia+street |date=5 February 1982 |publisher=W.H. Allen |isbn=978-0-491-02755-7 |page=125}}</ref> The [[Gwardamanġa#Overview|villa is described]] as built in the form of a palace complemented with sea views over [[Marsamxett Harbour]].{{sfnp|Edwards|1990|p=214}} The Queen describes it as a "town house".{{sfnp|Hicks|2014|p=27}} It is a typical traditional Maltese residence.<ref name="auto"/> The building is built with [[limestone]], known as sandstone and described by the Queen as "yellow stone",{{sfnp|Hicks|2014|p=27}} and designed with spacious interiors.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Bedell Smith |first=Sally |date=December 14, 2011 |url=http://www.vanityfair.com/style/society/2012/01/queen-elizabeth-201201 |title=Love and Majesty |magazine=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]}}</ref> The house has two entrances with one set at street level and another set after going up a flight of stairs under an elaborate front porch.{{sfnp|Hicks|2014|p=27}} The royal family had taken their own personal belongings from Britain when they lived at the villa<ref>{{cite book |last=Fisher |first=Graham |title=The Queen's Family |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wcRyE6l4X_UC&q=Villa+Guardamangia&dq=Villa+Guardamangia |date=1982 |publisher=W.H. Allen |isbn=978-0-491-02867-7}}</ref> allowing them to live in a lavish residence,<ref>{{cite book |last1=Higham |first1=Charles |last2=Moseley |first2=Roy |title=Elizabeth and Philip: The Untold Story of the Queen of England and her Prince |url=https://archive.org/details/elizabethphilipu00high |url-access=registration |date=March 1993 |publisher=Berkley Books |isbn=978-0-425-13757-4}}</ref> in a once-elegant home.<ref name="bbc.com"/> The royal family had British servants at the villa.<ref>{{cite book |title=The Malta Year Book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=cbNOAAAAMAAJ&q=queen+guardamangia&dq=queen+guardamangia |date=1968 |publisher=St. Michael's College Publications}}</ref>

The gardens of the villa are secluded.<ref>{{cite book |last=Holden |first=Anthony |title=Charles, Prince of Wales |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=33yJAQvfMU4C&q=garden+villa+guardamangia&dq=garden+villa+guardamangia |date=1980 |publisher=Pan |isbn=978-0-330-26167-8 |page=73}}</ref> The Queen has described it simply as the "small garden at Villa Guardamangia".{{sfnp|Hicks|2014|p=171}} The Queen herself had decorated the gardens and the surroundings according to her tastes and lifestyle,{{sfnp|Morgan|1991|p=432}} however most garden-related work was done by a gardener.<ref name="Dean1954">{{cite book |last=Dean |first=John |title=H. R. H. Price Philip, Duke of Edinburgh: A Portrait |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LrvTAAAAMAAJ&q=Villa+Guardamangia&dq=Villa+Guardamangia |date=1954 |publisher=R. Hale}}</ref> The main outdoor feature in the garden is the long terrace taking from the building of the villa to the other side of the garden. In the middle of the terrace is where a bench stood that is the place where most known published photos of the royal couple and guests were taken.<ref>{{cite book |last=Flamini |first=Roland |title=Sovereign: Elizabeth II and the Windsor Dynasty |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zu4kit93X_oC&q=garden+villa+guardamangia&dq=garden+villa+guardamangia |date=4 July 1992 |publisher=Random House Publishing Group |isbn=978-0-440-21190-7 |page=114}}</ref> Other photos were taken on the roof terrace of the villa,<ref name=godfreydykes/> while some were taken by the press back then at the front of the villa while the couple walked in on the flight of stairs.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.flickr.com/photos/britishmonarchy/15091692269 |title=Returning to Villa Guardamangia after State Drive [Royal Visit to Malta, 1949] |author=British Monarchy |website=[[Flickr]] |access-date=2015-12-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208144850/https://www.flickr.com/photos/britishmonarchy/15091692269 |archive-date=2015-12-08 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The garden had a function to entertain and also to cultivate flowers, which Prince Philip enjoyed to have in his cabin and wardroom.<ref name="Dean1954"/>

==Current==
The building is scheduled as a Grade 2 monument by the [[Malta Environment and Planning Authority]] and it is in a dilapidated state.<ref>{{cite news |title=Development Planning Act, 1992 (Section 46) – Scheduling of Property |url=http://www.mepa.org.mt/lpgdocuments/GNs/gn349_01.pdf |work=Malta Government Gazette |date=20 April 2001 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126112453/http://www.mepa.org.mt/lpgdocuments/GNs/gn349_01.pdf |archivedate=26 November 2015 |page=4036}}</ref> The NGO [[Flimkien għal Ambjent Aħjar]] has called for its restoration and the government was then in process to expropriate and restore the villa,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Martin |first1=Ivan |title=Appeal for Queen Elizabeth's former home to be restored |url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150425/local/updated-appeal-for-queen-elizabeths-former-home-to-be-restored.565455 |newspaper=Times of Malta |date=25 April 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150818172722/http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20150425/local/updated-appeal-for-queen-elizabeths-former-home-to-be-restored.565455 |archivedate=18 August 2015}}</ref> accusing its then owners of allowing the villa to deteriorate in order to justify demolishing it so that the site can be sold and redeveloped.<ref name="auto1">{{cite news |title=Row in Malta over Queen's crumbling former home |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/europe/malta/11572954/Row-in-Malta-over-Queens-crumbling-former-home.html |accessdate=29 November 2015 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=30 April 2015}}</ref>

Villa Guardamangia is a potential [[tourism in Malta|tourist attraction]] once restored. In a non-scientific 2015 online poll, 84% of respondents stated that they would visit the villa if it were restored and opened to the public.<ref>{{cite news |title=Telegraph runs poll asking people if they would visit the Queen's old home in Malta if restored |url=http://www.independent.com.mt/articles/2015-05-01/local-news/Telegraph-runs-poll-asking-people-if-they-would-visit-the-Queen-s-old-home-in-Malta-if-restored-6736134780 |newspaper=[[The Malta Independent]] |date=1 May 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150806145438/http://www.independent.com.mt/articles/2015-05-01/local-news/Telegraph-runs-poll-asking-people-if-they-would-visit-the-Queen-s-old-home-in-Malta-if-restored-6736134780 |archivedate=6 August 2015}}</ref><ref name="Propertyshowrooms.com">{{cite news |last1=James |first1=Roxanne |title=Restoration Row over Queen's Former Home in Malta |url=https://www.propertyshowrooms.com/malta/property/news/restoration-row-over-queen-s-former-home-malta_313417.html |website=Propertyshowrooms.com |date=20 May 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150525075611/http://www.propertyshowrooms.com/malta/property/news/restoration-row-over-queen-s-former-home-malta_313417.html |archivedate=25 May 2015}}</ref> The villa is a common [[landmark]] associated with the royal family.<ref>{{cite book|last=Matthews |first=Jodie |title=Islands and Britishness: A Global Perspective |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RyssBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA62&dq=Villa+Guardamangia |date=15 November 2011 |publisher=Cambridge Scholars Publishing |isbn=978-1-4438-3543-5 |page=62}}</ref> ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' has mistakenly portrayed the front façade of [[Villa Luginsland]] in [[Rabat, Malta|Rabat]] as the back of Villa Guardamangia.<ref name="auto1"/> 

Villa Guardamangia was privately owned by Marika Schembri and her siblings.<ref name=telegraph2015-11-25>{{cite news |last1=Rayner |first1=Gordon |title=Queen makes emotional visit to Malta for what may be her last Commonwealth summit |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/queen-elizabeth-II/12012163/Queen-makes-emotional-visit-to-Malta-for-what-may-be-her-last-Commonwealth-summit.html |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=25 November 2015 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126011050/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/queen-elizabeth-II/12012163/Queen-makes-emotional-visit-to-Malta-for-what-may-be-her-last-Commonwealth-summit.html |archivedate=26 November 2015}}</ref> In June 2019, the Villa was put up for sale for €6 million (£5.3 million).<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-48602842|title=Queen's former Malta home Villa Guardamangia on sale|publisher=BBC News|date=11 June 2019|accessdate=11 June 2019}}</ref> However, it was bought for €5 million.<ref>https://timesofmalta.com/articles/view/uk-royal-family-to-be-invited-for-reopening-of-queens-former-home.796662</ref> Since October 2019, the villa belongs exclusively to the [[Government of Malta]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofmalta.com/articles/view/government-buys-villa-guardamangia-the-queens-former-malta-residence.744069 |title=Queen's former Malta home to be restored after being bought by government |publisher=Timesofmalta.com |date=2019-10-22 |accessdate=2019-10-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.one.com.mt/news/2019/10/22/il-pm-ihabbar-li-l-gvern-xtara-l-unika-dar-li-kienet-tghix-fiha-r-regina-2/ |title=Il-PM ħabbar li l-Gvern xtara l-unika dar li kienet tgħix fiha r-Reġina barra r-Renju Unit – One News |publisher=One.com.mt |date=2019-10-22 |accessdate=2019-10-23}}</ref>
