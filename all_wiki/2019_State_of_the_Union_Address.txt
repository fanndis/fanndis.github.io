The '''2019 State of the Union Address''' was given by the 45th [[President of the United States]], [[Donald Trump]], on Tuesday, February 5, 2019, at 9 p.m. [[Eastern Time Zone|EST]], in the chamber of the [[United States House of Representatives]] to the [[116th United States Congress]]. It was Trump's second [[State of the Union|State of the Union Address]] and his [[February 2017 Donald Trump speech to joint session of Congress|third speech]] to a [[joint session of the United States Congress]]. Presiding over this joint session was the [[Speaker of the United States House of Representatives|House Speaker]], [[Nancy Pelosi]], accompanied by [[Mike Pence]], the [[Vice President of the United States]].

It was the first address to a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]]-controlled House of Representatives since 2010. 

The Democratic Response was given by [[2018 Georgia gubernatorial election|2018 Georgia Gubernatorial Nominee]] [[Stacey Abrams]] and the Spanish-language response was given by [[California Attorney General]] and former U.S. Representative [[Xavier Becerra]].<ref name="Levine">{{Cite news|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2019/01/29/stacey-abrams-to-give-democratic-response-to-state-of-the-union-1134068|title=Stacey Abrams to give Democratic response to State of the Union|last=Levine|first=Marianne|date=January 29, 2019|work=[[Politico]]|publisher=[[Capitol News Company]]|location=[[Arlington County, Virginia]]|access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref><ref name="RollCall">{{cite news|url=http://www.rollcall.com/news/campaigns/stacey-abrams-to-give-democratic-response-to-sotu|title=Stacey Abrams to give Democratic Response to State of The Union Address|first=Simone|last=Pathé|work=[[Roll Call]]|publisher=[[FiscalNote]]|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]|date=January 29, 2019|access-date=January 29, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Spanish">{{cite news|url=https://thehill.com/latino/427528-california-ag-becerra-to-give-spanish-language-sotu-response|title=California AG Becerra to give Spanish-language SOTU Response|first=Rafael|last=Bernal|work=[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]|publisher=Capital Hill Publishing Corp.|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]|date=January 29, 2019|access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref>

The Address was watched by 46.8 million viewers, and aired live on 12 major television networks. Viewership statistics do not include views from online [[Live streaming|live streams]]. There were also 15.2 million interactions regarding the Address on social media.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nielsen.com/us/en/insights/news/2019/nearly-47-million-viewers-watch-president-trumps-state-of-the-union-address.html|title=Nearly 47 Million Viewers Watch President Trump's State of the Union Address|website=www.nielsen.com|access-date=2019-02-21}}</ref>

==Background==
[[Article Two of the United States Constitution#Clause 1: State of the Union|Article II, Section 3, Clause 1]] of the [[United States Constitution]] states that the president "shall from time to time give to the Congress Information of the State of the Union, and recommend to their Consideration such measures as he shall judge necessary and expedient."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.senate.gov/civics/constitution_item/constitution.htm|title=Constitution of the United States|website=[[United States Senate]]|publisher=[[Library of Congress]]|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]|access-date=January 6, 2019}}</ref>

Speaker of the House Nancy Pelosi sent an invitation to President [[Donald Trump]] on January 3, 2019, to deliver a "State of the Union address before a Joint Session of Congress on Tuesday, January 29, 2019 in the House Chamber." The invitation was sent just hours after her election to Speaker of the House.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2019/01/03/trump-state-of-the-union-address-invitation-1080818|title=Pelosi invites Trump to give State of the Union on Jan. 29|last=Griffiths|first=Brent D.|date=January 3, 2019|work=[[Politico]]|publisher=[[Capitol News Company]]|location=[[Arlington County, Virginia]]|access-date=January 6, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/01/03/politics/nancy-pelosi-donald-trump-state-of-the-union/index.html|title=Pelosi invites Trump to deliver State of the Union on January 29|last=Watkins|first=Eli|date=January 3, 2019|work=[[CNN]]|publisher=[[Turner Broadcasting System]]|location=[[Atlanta]]|access-date=January 6, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thehill.com/homenews/house/423800-pelosi-invites-trump-to-give-state-of-the-union-on-january-29|title=Pelosi invites Trump to give State of the Union on January 29|last=Marcos|first=Christina|date=January 3, 2019|work=[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]|publisher=Capitol Hill Publishing Corp|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]|access-date=January 6, 2019}}</ref> On January 6, President Trump commented to reporters that "I will be making the State of the Union on January 29. And I look forward to speaking, really, before the world.&nbsp;We have a lot of great things to say."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Office of the Press Secretary|url=https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-marine-one-departure-29/|title=Remarks by President Trump Before Marine One Departure|date=January 6, 2019|work=[[whitehouse.gov]]|publisher=[[White House]]|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]|access-date=January 7, 2019|author1-link=White House Office of the Press Secretary}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/world/us/trump-says-look-forward-to-sotu-address-to-speak-before-world/articleshow/67416490.cms|title=Trump says look forward to State of the Union address to speak before world|date=January 7, 2019|work=[[The Times of India]]|publisher=[[The Times Group]]|location=[[Mumbai]]|access-date=January 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.newsmax.com/john-gizzi/trump-will-deliver-state-of-union/2019/01/06/id/897022/|title=Trump to Newsmax: "I Will Be Giving the State of the Union Address"|last=Gizzi|first=John|date=January 6, 2019|work=[[Newsmax]]|publisher=[[Newsmax Media]]|location=United States|access-date=January 7, 2019}}</ref>

== Address ==
[[File:2019 State of the Union (46282653814).jpg|thumb|Photo taken during the 2019 State of the Union address]]
The State of the Union Address was given at 9:00&nbsp;p.m. [[Eastern Time Zone|EST]] on February 5, 2019. President Trump began the address without an introduction from Pelosi, breaking with a SOTU custom.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://time.com/5522266/donald-trump-no-pelosi-introduction-state-of-the-union/|title=Donald Trump Did Not Wait for Nancy Pelosi to Introduce Him During the State of the Union|last=Abrams|first=Abigail|date=February 5, 2019|work=Time|access-date=February 5, 2019}}</ref> During the speech, Vice President Mike Pence and Speaker of the House Nancy Pelosi sat behind Trump. [[United States Secretary of Energy|U.S. Secretary of Energy]] [[Rick Perry]] was named the [[designated survivor]] and was at an undisclosed location during the address so that, in case of a catastrophe, the [[continuity of government]] would be upheld.<ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2019/02/05/donald-trumps-designated-survivor-rick-perry/2783329002/|title=Designated survivor for State of the Union address: Rick Perry|last1=Fritze|first1=John|date=February 5, 2019|work=[[USA Today]]|access-date=February 5, 2019|last2=Hayes|first2=Christal}}</ref>

The speech lasted 82 minutes, making it the third longest State of the Union Address.<ref name="duration" /> During the speech, Trump discussed [[bipartisanship]], the [[Economy of the United States|economy]], [[job]]s, [[Unemployment in the United States|unemployment]], [[tax reform]], [[Energy in the United States|energy production]], unity, the [[Opioid epidemic|opioid crisis]], [[prison reform]], [[Immigration to the United States|immigration]], [[United States border security concerns|border security]], the [[Mexico–United States barrier|border wall]], [[Foreign trade of the United States|trade]], [[infrastructure]], [[prescription drug]]s, [[HIV/AIDS]], [[cancer]], [[family leave]], [[Abortion in the United States|abortion]], [[National security of the United States|national security]], [[North Korea]], [[Venezuela]], the [[Middle East]], [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|ISIS]], [[Afghanistan]], [[Iran]], [[antisemitism]], [[veteran]]s, and opportunity. He also discussed the [[Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty]], [[socialism]], and the [[War in Afghanistan (2001–present)|War in Afghanistan]].

During the address, President Trump announced his [[2019 North Korea–United States Vietnam Summit|next summit]] with North Korean leader [[Kim Jong-un]]. The summit took place on February 27–28, 2019 in [[Vietnam]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2019/02/trump-kim-summit-held-vietnam-february-27-28-190206032947647.html|title=Second Trump-Kim summit to be held in Vietnam on February 27–28|website=www.aljazeera.com|access-date=2019-02-06}}</ref>

In his Address, Trump asserted that "The savage gang [[MS-13]] now operates in at least 20 different American states and they almost all come through our southern border... Just yesterday, an MS-13 gang member was taken into custody for a fatal shooting on a subway platform in New York City. We are removing these gang members by the thousands, but until we secure our border, they are going to keep streaming right back in."<ref name="ABCsought">{{cite news |title=MS-13 gang member arrested in Queens subway shooting, 2 others sought |url=https://abc7ny.com/ms-13-gang-member-arrested-2-sought-in-subway-shooting/5119077/ |access-date=6 February 2019 |publisher=[[WABC-TV]] |date=6 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="LindMurder">{{cite news |last1=Lind |first1=Dara |title=Trump has a long history of fearmongering about immigrant murder |url=https://www.vox.com/2019/2/5/18213077/state-of-the-union-2019-trump-david-killed-immigrant-family |access-date=6 February 2019 |publisher=Vox |date=5 February 2019}}</ref> ''[[The Washington Post]]'' fact checker found that MS-13 is active primarily in [[Los Angeles]], [[Long Island (New York)]], and the [[Washington metropolitan area|Washington, D.C. metro area]]. ''The Washington Post'' also concluded that 1,332 illegal alien members of MS-13 were deported in fiscal year 2018.<ref name="KesslerKellyRizzo">{{cite news |last1=Kessler |first1=Glenn |last2=Rizzo |first2=Salvador |last3=Kelly |first3=Kelly |title=Fact-checking President Trump's 2019 State of the Union address |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/02/06/fact-checking-president-trumps-state-union-address/ |access-date=6 February 2019 |work=Washington Post |date=6 February 2019}}</ref>

== Reactions ==
Polls conducted by [[CBS]] gauged approval of speech, viewership, among other things. A poll by CBS found 76% of people approved of the speech.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/state-of-the-union-2019-most-viewers-approved-of-trumps-sotu-address-cbs-news-poll/|title=Most viewers approved of Trump's second State of the Union address|date=6 February 2019|website=www.cbsnews.com|language=en|access-date=6 February 2019}}</ref> Another poll by CBS based on party identification found that 43% of Republicans, 24% of Democrats, and 30% of Independents watched the address.<ref name=":1" /> Another poll based on party identification found that 97% of Republicans, 30% of Democrats, and 82% of Independents approved the message of the speech.<ref name=":1" />

Polls conducted by [[CNN]] gauged approval of speech, policy approval, among other things.<ref name=":3">{{cite web |title=Speech audience was most partisan since 2001 |url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/02/06/politics/state-of-the-union-poll/index.html |website=CNN |access-date=6 February 2019 |language=en |date=6 February 2019}}</ref> 59% of viewers approved the President's address.<ref name=":3" /> 71% of viewers thought that the President's policies were moving the country in the right direction; 76% on economy, 70% on trade and national security, 68% on immigration, and 65% on taxes.<ref name=":3" />

==Fundraising by Trump's 2020 campaign==
A fundraising effort by Trump's [[Donald Trump 2020 presidential campaign|2020 campaign]] on the days leading to the address and on the day of the address raised $2.4 million from 76,000 donors. His campaign displayed names of donors on a live streaming broadcast of the event on Donald Trump's Facebook page. This is comparable to fundraising for Trump's 2020 campaign that occurred during the 2018 address that similarly included the display of names of donors on a streaming broadcast online.<ref>{{cite news |last=Bedard |first=Paul |date=February 5, 2019 |title=Trump raises $2.4 million from 76,000 in SOTU pitch, a new record |url=https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/washington-secrets/trump-raises-2-4-million-from-76-000-in-sotu-pitch-a-new-record |work=[[Washington Examiner]] |access-date=February 5, 2019 }}</ref>
