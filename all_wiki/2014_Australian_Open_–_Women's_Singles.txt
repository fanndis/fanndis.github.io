{{main|2014 Australian Open}}

[[Li Na]] defeated [[Dominika Cibulková]] in the final, 7–6<sup>(7–3)</sup>, 6–0 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2014 Australian Open]]. Li became the first Asian Australian Open champion and [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records|sixth woman to win the title after being match point down]] (in the third round against [[Lucie Šafářová]]).<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/tennis/25875113
|title= Li Na beats Dominika Cibulkova to win Australian Open final|date=25 January 2014|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=28 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2014/jan/25/li-na-v-dominika-cibulkova-live|title= Li Na wins Australian Open final against Dominika Cibulkova – as it happened|date=25 January 2014|work=Guardian|accessdate=28 January 2014}}</ref>

[[Victoria Azarenka]] was the two-time defending champion, but was defeated by [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] in the quarterfinals, thus ending her 18-match winning streak at the Australian Open.<ref>https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/25838267</ref>

By winning her third round match, women's top seed [[Serena Williams]] broke [[Margaret Court]]'s record of 60 match victories at the Australian Open.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/tennis/25771715|title= Novak Djokovic into round four|date=17 January 2014|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=28 January 2014}}</ref>

[[Eugenie Bouchard]] became the first Canadian to reach the semi-finals in the Major since [[Carling Bassett-Seguso]] in the [[1984 US Open – Women's Singles|1984 US Open]]. This was her fourth Major appearance in the main draw (and the first time as a seeded player in the tournament).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ctvnews.ca/sports/eugenie-bouchard-first-canadian-to-reach-australian-open-semi-finals-1.1648926|title=Eugenie Bouchard: First Canadian to reach Australian Open semi-finals|publisher=[[CTV News]]|date=21 January 2014|accessdate=16 May 2018}}</ref> This was also the first Grand Slam tournament where future World No. 1 and two-time Grand Slam winner [[Simona Halep]] reached the quarterfinals.
