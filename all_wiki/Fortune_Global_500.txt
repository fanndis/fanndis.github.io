The '''''Fortune'' Global 500''', also known as '''Global 500''', is an annual ranking of the top 500 [[corporation]]s worldwide as measured by [[revenue]].  The list is compiled and published annually by ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'' magazine.

== Methodology ==
Until 1989, it listed only non-[[United States]] industrial corporations under the title "International 500" while the [[Fortune 500|''Fortune'' 500]] contained and still contains exclusively United States corporations. In 1990, United States companies were added to compile a truly global list of top industrial corporations as ranked by sales. Since 1995, the list has had its current form, listing also top financial corporations and service providers by revenue.

=== Controversy ===
Several inconsistencies exist in ''Fortune''{{'s}} ranking of cities with the most ''Fortune'' 500 headquarters. On June 3, 2011, the ''[[Atlanta Business Chronicle]]'' stated examples of ''Fortune'' including regional headquarters for some cities, excluding regional headquarters for other cities and in some cases excluding headquarters that are physically located inside a city limit.<ref>{{cite web|title=Some cities rankings are overstated|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/atlanta/print-edition/2011/06/03/fortune-500-list-overstates-atlantas.html|author=Maria Saporta|publisher=Atlanta Business Chronicle|accessdate=28 October 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130714234502/http://www.bizjournals.com/atlanta/print-edition/2011/06/03/fortune-500-list-overstates-atlantas.html|archive-date=14 July 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Geographical distribution ==
Since 2001, there has been a significant change in the geographical distribution of the companies in the Global 500 rankings. The number of [[North America]]n-based companies decreased from 215 in 2001 to 143 in 2017{{Citation needed|date=July 2018}} and the contribution of [[Asia]]n-based companies increased rapidly from 116 in 2001 to 197 in 2017{{Citation needed|date=July 2018}}. Most of this growth is accounted for by the rapid increase in the number of Chinese Global 500 companies, of which there were 109 by 2017, increasing from only 10 in 2001. The share of [[Europe]]an-based companies also declined, from 158 to 143, over the same period.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/global500/|title=A New Perspective on the Corporate World|access-date=July 8, 2013|publisher=CNN Money, Fortune Magazine|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190629173859/https://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/global500/|archive-date=June 29, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://beta.fortune.com/global500/visualizations/|title=Visualize The Global 500|website=Fortune|access-date=2016-09-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160907062205/http://beta.fortune.com/global500/visualizations|archive-date=2016-09-07|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-business/40-of-fortune-500-companies-asian-india-has-7-in-list/articleshow/59707630.cms|title=40% of Fortune 500 companies Asian, India has 7 in list - Times of India â–º|author=|date=|website=The Times of India|access-date=January 28, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190217031820/https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-business/40-of-fortune-500-companies-asian-india-has-7-in-list/articleshow/59707630.cms|archive-date=February 17, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>
