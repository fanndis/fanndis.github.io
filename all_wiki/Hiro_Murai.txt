'''Hiro Murai''' (born 1983)<ref name="JPNews">{{cite web |title=Music video by Tokyo-born filmmaker wins Grammy |work=Japan News |date=February 11, 2019 |url=http://the-japan-news.com/news/article/0005537649}}</ref> is a Japanese-born American filmmaker based in [[Los Angeles]]. His most notable works include internationally successful music videos for artists such as [[Donald Glover|Childish Gambino]], [[Earl Sweatshirt]], [[Nick Murphy (musician)|Chet Faker]], [[Flying Lotus]], [[David Guetta]], [[St. Vincent (musician)|St. Vincent]], [[The Shins]], [[The Fray]], [[Bloc Party]] and [[Queens of the Stone Age]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiromurai.com/music-videos/|title=Music Videos|publisher=www.hiromurai.com|access-date=2016-08-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160728124253/http://hiromurai.com/music-videos/|archive-date=2016-07-28|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2013, he directed the short film ''[[Clapping for the Wrong Reasons]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiromurai.com/commercials/|title=Other Films|publisher=www.hiromurai.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Music Video Director Hiro Murai Is Donald Glover’s Secret Weapon - DJBooth Article|url=http://djbooth.net/news/entry/2016-08-24-hiro-murai-donald-glover-secret-weapon|publisher=Djbooth.net|accessdate=31 August 2016}}</ref> a companion piece for Childish Gambino's second studio album, ''[[Because the Internet]]'', and its attached screenplay. In the same year, he created a multi-panel video set for [[Frank Ocean|Frank Ocean's]] performance at the Grammys.<ref>{{Citation|last=Murai|first=Hiro|title=Frank Ocean &quot;Forrest Gump&quot; Grammy Visuals|date=2013-02-27|url=https://vimeo.com/60699138|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2013/02/12/behind-the-scenes-of-frank-oceans-grammys-performance-with-director-hiro-murai/|title=Behind the Scenes of Frank Ocean’s Grammys Performance with Director Hiro Murai|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-11-26|language=en}}</ref>

In 2016, he directed several episodes of the comedy-drama ''[[Atlanta (TV series)|Atlanta]]'', collaborating again with Donald Glover aka Childish Gambino.<ref name="indiewire_1201726188">{{Cite web|url=http://www.indiewire.com/2016/09/atlanta-hiro-murai-fx-donald-glover-1201726188/|title=‘Atlanta’: Hiro Murai Directs FX’s Beautiful and Baffling Series {{!}} IndieWire|last=Nguyen|first=Hanh|website=www.indiewire.com|access-date=2016-09-29}}</ref> For his work on the show, he has received three nominations for the [[Primetime Emmy Award|Emmy Award]] for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series|Outstanding Comedy Series]] and [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Directing for a Comedy Series|Outstanding Directing for a Comedy Series]].<ref name="emmy">{{cite web|url=http://www.emmys.com/bios/hiro-murai|title=Hiro Murai|website=emmys.com|publisher=[[Academy of Television Arts & Sciences]]|accessdate=September 12, 2018}}</ref> In the same year, he directed a [[Nike, Inc.|Nike]] campaign starring [[Chance the Rapper|Chance The Rapper]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Murai|first=Hiro|title=Nike x Chance The Rapper - We The People|date=2016-08-06|url=https://vimeo.com/177833573|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref>

On February 10, 2019, the music video for [[Donald Glover|Childish Gambino]]'s "[[This Is America (song)|This Is America]]", directed by Murai, won the [[Grammy Award for Best Music Video]].

In November 2018, the trailer for his debut feature film ''[[Guava Island]]'' starring Donald Glover and [[Rihanna]] premiered at the Pharos Festival in New Zealand as he collaborated with Donald Glover once again.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2023724/rihanna-childish-gambino-guava-island-trailer-pharos/video/|title='Guava Island' Trailer: Watch Donald Glover & Rihanna's New Movie Trailer|date=2018-11-25|work=Stereogum|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref> ''Guava Island'' was released on April 13, 2019, by [[Amazon Studios]] through [[Amazon Prime Video]]. ==Early life and education==
Murai was born to Japanese popular music composer Kunihiko Murai.<ref name="JPNews"/> He moved to Los Angeles when he was nine years old and later graduated with a degree from the [[USC School of Cinematic Arts]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.booooooom.com/2014/11/19/interview-director-hiro-murai/|title=An Interview with Director Hiro Murai|publisher=www.booooooom.com}}</ref>

After graduation, Murai turned to freelance work as the director of photography for numerous music videos, as well as [[Visual effect|VFX]] and [[storyboard]]ing, most notably for [[Ace Norton]]. After some time Murai began to direct low budget films.
