'''''Lover''''' is the seventh studio album by American singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]], released on August 23, 2019, through [[Republic Records]]. As the [[executive producer]], Swift enlisted producers [[Jack Antonoff]], [[Louis Bell]], [[Frank Dukes]], and [[Joel Little]] for the project. 

Inspired by the connections felt with fans during her [[Taylor Swift's Reputation Stadium Tour|Reputation Stadium Tour]] (2018), Swift sought to make an album with bright and lighter tones by delving deep into her personal life, departing from the dark sound of its predecessor, ''[[Reputation (Taylor Swift album)|Reputation]]'' (2017). She defined ''Lover'' as a "love letter to love itself" that celebrates the full spectrum of love. It also incorporates politically charged themes, such as [[feminism]], disillusionment and [[LGBT rights]]. ''Lover'' is primarily a 1980s [[retro style|retro]]-styled [[Pop music|pop]] record that combines [[synth-pop]], [[electropop]] and [[pop rock]] with influences from [[Country music|country]], [[funk]], [[Folk music|folk]] and [[contemporary R&B|R&B]]. The album's artwork and visuals use a [[summer]] theme consisting of [[pastel (color)|pastel colors]].

Contemporary critics received ''Lover'' with mostly positive reviews. Praise centered on Swift's songwriting conveying emotional maturity, while a few critics found the album sprawling and inconsistent. The album contains collaborations with [[Brendon Urie]] and [[the Dixie Chicks]], and was supported by four singles: "[[Me!]]", "[[You Need to Calm Down]]", "[[Lover (Taylor Swift song)|Lover]]", and "[[The Man (Taylor Swift song)|The Man]]", the first three of which reached the top 10 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. In the United States, ''Lover'' was Swift's sixth number-one album on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and was certified [[RIAA certification|double platinum]]. All of its 18 tracks charted simultaneously on the Hot 100, breaking the all-time record for most simultaneous entries. The album topped the charts in several other countries, including Canada, Australia, Ireland, Norway, the Netherlands, New Zealand, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, and the United Kingdom. Selling 3.2&nbsp;million copies in 2019, it was the world's best-selling studio album of the year.

At the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards]], the album received a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album|Best Pop Vocal Album]], and its singles "You Need to Calm Down" and "Lover" were nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance|Best Pop Solo Performance]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]], respectively. ''Lover'' won [[American Music Award for Favorite Pop/Rock Album|Favorite Pop/Rock Album]] at the [[American Music Awards of 2019|American Music Awards]], and out of 17 nominations at [[MTV Video Music Awards]], its music videos won four. ''Lover'' was listed as one of the best albums of 2019 by numerous publications.

== Conception ==
Inspired by the "positive energy" she felt during her [[Reputation Stadium Tour]], ''Lover'' was conceived by the "symbiotic relationship" Swift has with her fans, who led her to feel a "tonal shift", and helped her "dig out of the darker era of ''Reputation''". Swift stated she had an [[Epiphany (feeling)|epiphany]] during the tour, that "despite the caricature that she thought had been created of her, there were many people who saw what others had simply refused to"; she added: "I would look out into the audience and I'd see these amazing, thoughtful, caring, wonderful, empathetic people". Swift explained, "so often with our takedown culture, talking shit about a celebrity is basically the same as talking shit about the new [[iPhone]]. So when I go and I meet fans, I see that they actually see me as a flesh-and-blood human being. Thatâ€”as contrived as it may soundâ€”changed [me] completely, assigning humanity to my life". At the tour's end, Swift channeled this positive energy into the making of ''Lover''.<ref name="EWMay" />

Swift described ''Lover'' as a [[Romance (love)|romantic]] one, stating that it was "not just simply thematically, like it's all [[love song]]s or something. The idea of something being romantic, it doesn't have to be a happy song. You can find romance in loneliness or sadness or going through things in your life... it just looks at those things through a romantic gaze".<ref name="release" /> In a ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' interview published in August 2019, Swift described the album as a "[[love letter]] to love, in all of its maddening, passionate, exciting, enchanting, horrific, tragic, wonderful glory".<ref name="Vogue">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/taylor-swift-cover-september-2019|title=Taylor Swift on Sexism, Scrutiny, and Standing Up for Herself|first=Abby|last=Aguirre|magazine=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|date=August 8, 2019|access-date=August 16, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190810081937/https://www.vogue.com/article/taylor-swift-cover-september-2019?verso=true|archive-date=August 10, 2019}}</ref> On August 23, Swift described the album as "a celebration of love, in all its complexity, coziness, and chaos".<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B1fgxY-DsvJ/|title=Taylor Swift on Instagram|date=August 23, 2019|website=Instagram|access-date=March 25, 2020}}</ref> In an [[Instagram]] post dated September 17, 2019, Swift defined ''Lover'' as an album that feels like "fields, sunsets, + SUMMER".<ref name="IG2019-09-172">{{Cite web|date=September 17, 2019|title=Taylor Swift on Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B2hAIecjv5J/|access-date=March 8, 2020|website=Instagram}}</ref> In an interview with [[Zane Lowe]] for [[Beats 1]] on October 30, 2019, Swift said she considered ''Lover'' a return to "singing about my life in the way I actually experience it", unlike "through a filter of extremes" as is the case with ''Reputation''. She added the album was the first time she wrote about "love that was very real", compared to earlier songs such as "[[Love Story (Taylor Swift song)|Love Story]]" (2008).<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Aniftos|first=Rania|date=October 30, 2019|title=Taylor Swift Compares 'Lover' to 'Reputation', Talks #MeToo Movement With Zane Lowe For Beats 1 Interview|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8541404/taylor-swift-zane-lowe-beats-1-interview|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 14, 2019}}</ref> Swift told ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' that, compared to the "theatricality" of ''Reputation'' where she "said everything [she] needed to say", ''Lover'' turned out to be the album where she was no longer "answering to something".<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Chiu|first=Melody|date=December 4, 2019|title=Inside Taylor Swift's Life-Changing Year: 'I'm Proud of the Things I've Withstood'|url=https://people.com/music/taylor-swift-people-of-the-year-life-changing-year/|magazine=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=December 5, 2019}}</ref> She explained to ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'', that the true distinction between the two albums is the delivery: "This time around I feel more comfortable being brave enough to be vulnerable, because my fans are brave enough to be vulnerable with me. === Title ===

While promoting the album in a YouTube livestream on August 22, Swift revealed that the album was originally titled ''Daylight'' before she wrote the song "[[Lover (Taylor Swift song)|Lover]]" which changed her mind; she stated: "I decide an album title based on something that has a nice theme to it and a ring; it's very [[mood board]] how I go about [it]. The only time I ever started with a title was ''[[Reputation (Taylor Swift album)|Reputation]]''. That was the only time I started an album with a title so I began writing songs around that word. For this album, I actually thought the title of this album was going to be ''Daylight'' for a couple months. Then I wrote 'Lover' and I was like 'That's the title'".<ref name="YouTube Livestream22">{{cite magazine|last=Bailey|first=Alyssa|date=August 22, 2019|title=What Taylor Swift Revealed During Her YouTube Live: Her Lover Song Meanings, Old Diary Entries, and More|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/celebrities/a28789479/taylor-swift-lover-youtube-live-recap/|magazine=Elle|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190823085153/https://www.elle.com/culture/celebrities/a28789479/taylor-swift-lover-youtube-live-recap/|archive-date=August 23, 2019|access-date=August 24, 2019}}</ref> In the voice memos for Lover, which are found on the deluxe editions of the album, Swift further explained that after she wrote the song, she knew it would be the title and that it would "depict the tone for the record, and it's been a real catalyst for what this album has become."

=== Writing and production ===
Swift wrote ''Lover'' from an "open, free, romantic, whimsical place"; she added that the album felt "aesthetically very daytime", whereas its predecessor ''Reputation'' (2017) was "all [[cityscape]], darkness, full [[Swamp Witch]]".<ref name=":110">{{Cite web|last=Huff|first=Lauren|date=August 22, 2019|title=Taylor Swift talks all things 'Lover,' reveals album's original title|url=https://ew.com/music/2019/08/22/taylor-swift-lover-livestream-details-original-album-title/|access-date=March 22, 2020|website=Entertainment Weekly}}</ref> Swift stated that she tried "not to go into making an album with any expectation"; she started to "write so much" that she "knew immediately it [''Lover''] would probably be bigger" than her other studio albums.<ref name="EWMay"/> Swift was the sole writer of three tracksâ€”"[[Lover (Taylor Swift song)|Lover]]", "Cornelia Street" and "Daylight"; the rest were co-written with the producers. She co-produced every song and served as the sole executive producer of the album. Swift also highlighted that every song on ''Lover'' were written specifically for the album, devoid of leftovers from other albums; citing "This Love" as an example, which she had written closer to ''[[Red (Taylor Swift album)|Red]]'' (2012), but ended up being on ''[[1989 (Taylor Swift album)|1989]]'' (2014). Her percussion credits for the track "Paper Rings" made ''Lover'' her first album since ''1989'' that she actively participated in instrumental recordings.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dominick|first=Nora|date=August 26, 2019|title=Here Are 19 Behind-The-Scenes Facts About Taylor Swift's "Lover"|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/noradominick/taylor-swift-lover-behind-the-scenes-facts|access-date=June 8, 2020|website=[[BuzzFeed]]|language=en}}</ref> [[File:Antonoff in Tucson (cropped).jpg|thumb|[[Jack Antonoff]], Swift's frequent collaborator since 2013, co-produced the majority of ''Lover''.|alt=Man performing with a guitar|right|261x261px]]
Swift produced ''Lover'' with frequent collaborator [[Jack Antonoff]], as well as first-time collaborators [[Joel Little]], [[Louis Bell]], [[Frank Dukes]], and [[Sounwave]].<ref name="LinerNotes" /> Swift and Antonoff first worked together on the song "[[Sweeter than Fiction]]" for the 2013 film ''[[One Chance (film)|One Chance]]''; Antonoff went on to write and produce on two of Swift's albumsâ€”''[[1989 (Taylor Swift album)|1989]]'' (2014) and ''[[Reputation (Taylor Swift album)|Reputation]]'' (2017), as well as the song "[[I Don't Wanna Live Forever]]" for the 2017 film ''[[Fifty Shades Darker (film)|Fifty Shades Darker]]''. Antonoff co-wrote eight and co-produced eleven songs in the album. Little, known for being the lead singer of the punk band [[Goodnight Nurse]] and for his work with [[Lorde]], co-wrote and co-produced four songs, including the singles "[[Me!]]" and "[[You Need to Calm Down]]". Bell and Dukes, who worked with artists including [[Camila Cabello]] and [[Post Malone]], co-wrote and co-produced three songs. Swift co-produced tracks with Bell, Dukes, and Little from November to December 2018. Meanwhile, Swift worked with Antonoff on the album from January to February 2019. Sounwave has co-writing and co-producing credits on the song "London Boy"; other artists with writing credits include [[St. Vincent (musician)|St. Vincent]], [[Cautious Clay]], and [[Brendon Urie]].

"Lover", the title track, was written by Swift on piano, late at night at her home in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]; Swift messaged a voice memo of the song to Antonoff. === Lyrics and themes ===
Dubbed as Swift's most romantic and free-spirited work yet,<ref name=":63">{{Cite web|last=Bruner|first=Raisa|date=August 24, 2019|title=Let's Analyze the Lyrics to Every Song on Taylor Swift's Lover|url=https://time.com/5651207/taylor-swift-lover-songs-explained/|access-date=April 16, 2020|website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref><ref name=":1" /> ''Lover'' has been described by the singer as "a celebration of love, in all its complexity, coziness, and chaos"<ref name=":32">{{Cite web|title=Taylor Swift on Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B1fgxY-DsvJ/|date=August 23, 2019|website=Instagram|access-date=March 25, 2020}}</ref> and "a [[love letter]] to love, in all of its maddening, passionate, exciting, enchanting, horrific, tragic, wonderful glory",<ref name=":63"/> presenting her most mature understanding of love yet.<ref name="Pitchforkreview"/><ref name="Variety review"/> The album is characterized by themes of contentment, emotional evolution, and moving-on from the past, with forward-thinking songs,<ref name="NYTreview"/><ref name="rsreview"/> and thus, is designated as Swift's recalibration from the themes of ''Reputation''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Murphy|first=John|date=August 23, 2019|title=Taylor Swift â€“ Lover {{!}} Album Reviews|url=https://www.musicomh.com/reviews/albums/taylor-swift-lover|access-date=June 8, 2020|website=[[musicOMH]]}}</ref><ref name="NYTreview"/> Hope, new beginnings and flourishment were highlighted as the album's principle mood;<ref name=":16"/> however, the lyrical scope covers diverse themes such as [[self-love]], individuality, romantic devotion, [[feminism]], political criticism, grief, [[mental health]], familial love, friendship, and [[LGBT rights by country or territory|LGBT rights]], weaving them into a single concept.<ref name="Billboardreview"/><ref name="MTV"/> [[Stephen Erlewine]] of [[AllMusic]] stated that ''Lover'' comprises the full range of human emotion, in which Swift embraces "all aspects of her personality".<ref name="AllMusic"/>

The highly detailed lyrics have been found reminiscent to that of Swift's old-school work, where characters had novelistic aspects,<ref name=":13"/> especially the personal lyricism of ''[[Speak Now]]'' (2010)<ref name=":15"/> and the confessional songwriting of ''[[Red (Taylor Swift album)|Red]]'' (2012).<ref name="TheObserverreview"/> Deborah Krieger of ''[[PopMatters]]'' observed that the songs on ''Lover'' largely stem from contentment and satisfaction "in the middle of a strong relationship", rather than chronicling its downfall, which she cited as the main difference between ''Lover'' and its predecessors like ''Speak Now'', ''Red'' and ''1989.''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Krieger|first=Deborah|date=September 3, 2019|title=Taylor Swift's 'Lover' Finds Her in Romantic Bliss and at Peak Creativity|url=https://www.popmatters.com/taylor-swift-lover-review-2640081922.html|access-date=June 8, 2020|website=[[PopMatters]]}}</ref> In agreement, Larry Fitzmaurice saw Swift reaching a point of contentment in her art and self-perception In ''Lover''.<ref name="EWreview"/> The album has been found to be saturated in Swift's personal stories, packed with autobiographical details, emotional clarity and wisdom;<ref name=":14"/><ref name="telegraphreview"/><ref name="avclub"/><ref name="LATimesreview"/> ''Variety''<nowiki/>'s Chris Wilman highlighted "the minor war between past doubts and current happiness" which adds sophisticated layers to the lyrics.<ref name="Variety review"/> Critics further noticed how ''Lover'' provides glimpses into Swift's inner life using specific lyrics; Dana Schwartz of ''Time'' described it as "lifting a curtain to reveal the three-dimensional interiority behind two-dimensional paparazzi photos", while ''The A.V. Club''<nowiki/>'s Annie Zaleski thought that it delves into "love's day-to-day joys" that "drop just enough breadcrumb hints into reality to make songs more believable".<ref name=":15"/><ref name="avclub"/>

Open-hearted [[love song]]s dominate ''Lover,'' and examines all aspects of love. The songs consist of diaristic lyrics, emotional [[Bridge (music)|bridges]], ebullience, witty [[couplet]]s and turns of [[phrase]]; while also celebrating Swift's sense of humor, departing from the serious ''Reputation'' persona. As seen in "[[The Man (Taylor Swift song)|The Man]]", "[[Miss Americana & the Heartbreak Prince]]" and "[[You Need to Calm Down]]", the album incorporates politically charged lyrics, a previously less explored territory for Swift, while also embodying [[Americana (music)|Americana]]-inspired songwriting.<ref name=":15" /><ref name="NMEreview" /><ref name="telegraphreview" /><ref name=":16" /><ref name="Variety review" /> ''The Daily Telegraph'' attributed the "romantic zippiness" of "Paper Rings" and "I Think He Knows" to that of [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] musicals.<ref name="telegraphreview" /> Many songs on ''Lover'' continue Swift's self-assertive attitude and a sense of authority that has been observed in her preceding works.<ref name=":20">{{Cite web|title=The many sounds of Taylor Swift's new album "Lover"|url=http://www.dailynebraskan.com/culture/the-many-sounds-of-taylor-swifts-new-album-lover/article_fe34ae40-c7ad-11e9-9034-070a1eac9e9d.html|last=Schilz|first=Libby|date=August 26, 2019|website=[[The Daily Nebraskan]]|access-date=June 1, 2020}}</ref> 

The album has also been theorized as a part of a trilogy. [[MTV]] opined that if''1989'' is Swift's "pop magnum opus, a high-pitched trumpeting into a newfound universe of freedom and independence", and ''Reputation'' "reflects the scorn felt when the reality of the world's cruelty drives her to distress", then ''Lover'' completes the [[trilogy]]â€”"a pastel-hued compromise between the good and the bad, not in spite of who she's been, but because of who she's been".<ref name="MTV" /> ''[[The Quietus]]'' argued ''Lover'' could be the finale to a ''Red'' and ''1989'' trilogy (considering ''Reputation'' as an odd standalone), or the first part to a new trilogy constituting Swift's future projects.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Thorpe-Tracey|first=CJ|date=September 3, 2019|title=Taylor Swift â€“ Lover |url=https://thequietus.com/articles/27028-taylor-swift-lover-review|access-date=June 8, 2020|website=The Quietus|language=en-us}}</ref>

Jason Lipshutz of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' defined ''Lover'' as a distillation of all of Swift's accomplishments, condensing different components of her music to reflect her current interests and strengths as a singer-songwriter.<ref name="Billboardreview2">{{cite magazine|last=Lipshutz|first=Jason|date=August 23, 2019|title=Taylor Swift's 'Lover' is Free-Spirited, Full of Passion & A Snapshot That Will Endure|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8528162/taylor-swift-lover-review-first-listen-analysis|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190826112101/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8528162/taylor-swift-lover-review-first-listen-analysis|archive-date=August 26, 2019|access-date=August 25, 2019}}</ref> MTV's Terron Moore called the album a "dazzling, bursting compendium of pompous hope, modest love, and sobering grief, as well as a journey to make peace with the past".<ref name="MTV2">{{cite web|last=Moore|first=Terron|date=December 16, 2019|title=Albums of the Year: On Lover Taylor Swift Reckons With Her Own Genius|url=https://www.mtv.com/news/3147966/taylor-swift-lover-albums-of-the-year/|access-date=December 16, 2019|website=[[MTV]]}}</ref> [[Rob Sheffield]] of ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' compared the album's introspection to that of [[David Bowie]]'s ''[[Low (David Bowie album)|Low]]'', [[Joni Mitchell]]'s ''[[Hejira (album)|Hejira]]'', [[Al Green]]'s ''[[The Belle Album]]'' and [[Carole King]]'s ''[[Tapestry (Carole King album)|Tapestry]]'', and opined that ''Lover'' also has many sharp "therein lies the issue" moments like ''Reputation''; however, Swift diminishes the "Therein Factor" to make room for "a whole avalanche of emotion".<ref name=":14" />

Detailing the emotional weight of the lyrics, Craig Jenkins of''[[Vulture magazine|Vulture]]'' called ''Lover'' a [[song cycle]] that celebrates the feeling of "meeting someone you'd happily spend the rest of your life with".<ref name=":13" /> Zaleski opined that ''Lover'' finds Swift in a confident headspace, with the knowledge she is not required to prove herself to anybody.<ref name="avclub" /> Moore broke down the album into three different parts: Swift and love, Swift and fame, Swift and her audience, with its 18 songs traversing "a hard road to the freedom that exists in surrendering to the best and worst this world has to offer" with peaceful grace.<ref name="MTV" /> ''[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]'' writer Rebecca Lewis outlined that all 18 tracks of ''Lover'' work together, with lyrics bouncing between happiness, anger, lust, infatuation and heartache, enabling a showcase of the "full spectrum of love".<ref name=":16" />

===Marketing===
Swift teased her seventh studio album through cryptic hint-dropping and "[[Easter egg (media)|easters eggs]]" on [[social media]]. ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' wrote, Swift "has perfected the [[pop culture]] feedback loop: She shares updates about her life and drops hints about new music, which fans then gobble up and re-promote with their own theories, which Swift then re-shares on her [[Tumblr]] or incorporates into future clues. It's like a T-Swift-built [[Penrose stairs|Escher staircase]] of personal memories and moments that tease what's next". Swift commented that she loves "that they [fans] like the cryptic hint-dropping. Because as long as they like it, I'll keep doing it. It's fun. It feels mischievous and playful". ===Tour===
{{Main|Lover Fest}}

On September 17, 2019, Swift announced the [[Lover Fest]], a concert tour set to begin in the summer of 2020, starting on June 20, 2020, in [[Werchter|Werchter, Belgium]] and concluding on August 1, 2020, in [[Foxborough, Massachusetts]]. Swift intended to play two shows at [[SoFi Stadium]] in Los Angeles as part of the "Lover Fest West", and two shows at [[Gillette Stadium]] in Foxborough as part of the "Lover Fest East", in addition to ten festival dates in [[Europe]] and two shows in [[Brazil]]. Swift explained on social media that "The Lover album is open fields, sunsets, + SUMMER. I want to perform it in a way that feels authentic. Nora Princiotti of ''[[The Boston Globe]]'' labelled ''Lover'' as "satisfying pop" that dips back into many of Swift's "creative wells", with songs that "snatch elements from all over her past", instead of "going for a clear, new sound like she did on her previous two albums"; she added that the result is a "dreamy record that makes good use of its stylistic freedom".<ref name="BostonGlobe"/> Nick Levine from ''[[NME]]'' considered ''Lover'' to be "more sprawling and further from flawless" than ''1989'' (2014), but it succeeds due to Swift's "frequently dazzling" melodies, and the "loved-up lyrics are ultimately quite touching." He concluded that despite "the odd dud", the album is a "welcome reminder of her songwriting skills and ability to craft sonically inviting pop music".<ref name="NMEreview"/> Alexandra Pollard from ''[[The Independent]]'' awarded the album four stars out of five, writing "there is a brilliant album among the 18 songs, if only it had been pruned a little".<ref name="TheIndependentreview">{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/taylor-swift-review-lover-album-tracklist-stream-lyrics-joe-alwyn-london-boy-a9075376.html|title=Taylor Swift, Lover review: The sound of an artist excited to be earnest again|last=Pollard|first=Alexandra|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|date=August 23, 2019|access-date=August 25, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190824083403/https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/taylor-swift-review-lover-album-tracklist-stream-lyrics-joe-alwyn-london-boy-a9075376.html|archive-date=August 24, 2019}}</ref> Reviewing in his [[Substack]]-published [[Robert Christgau#Consumer Guide|Consumer Guide]] column, [[Robert Christgau]] preferred ''Lover'' over ''Reputation''{{'}}s celebrity concept and admired Swift for focusing her talents on songs about love, which he said is a more relatable theme "for female pop fans with their own lives, not just unfortunates ensnared by the vicarious vagaries of [[celebrity culture]]".<ref name="xgau">{{cite web|last=Christgau|first=Robert|author-link=Robert Christgau|date=September 18, 2019|url=https://robertchristgau.substack.com/p/consumer-guide-september-2019|title=Consumer Guide: September 2019|work=And It Don't Stop|publisher=Substack|access-date=September 29, 2019}}</ref>

In a less favorable review, Sal Cinquemani from ''[[Slant Magazine]]'' wrote the album "attempts to be something to everyone" but "lacks a unified sonic aesthetic".<ref name="Slantreview">{{cite web|last=Cinquemani|first=Sal|title=Review: Taylor Swift's Lover Course Corrects in Multiple Directions|url=https://www.slantmagazine.com/music/review-taylor-swift-lover-course-corrects-in-multiple-directions/|website=[[Slant Magazine]]|date=August 23, 2019|access-date=August 30, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190823203812/https://www.slantmagazine.com/music/review-taylor-swift-lover-course-corrects-in-multiple-directions/|archive-date=August 23, 2019}}</ref> [[Alexis Petridis]] from ''[[The Guardian]]'' called ''Lover'' "too long" and appears to be an attempt by Swift to "reassert her commercial dominance". However, he remarked Swift's songwriting as better "than any of her competitors" and praised "False God" and "It's Nice to Have a Friend" for being "more satisfying".<ref name="guardianreview"/> Writing for ''[[The Observer]]'', [[Kitty Empire]] summarized the album as a "[[kitsch]]-leaning festival of humour, pastels, butterflies and the desire not to be defined by negatives", but also wonders if it might be "a partial retrenchment until Swift decides what to do next", giving it three stars out of five.<ref name="TheObserverreview">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/24/taylor-swift-lover-album-review|title=Taylor Swift: Lover review â€“ a return to past glories|last=Empire|first=Kitty|author-link=Kitty Empire|newspaper=[[The Observer]]|date=August 24, 2019|access-date=August 26, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190826033045/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/24/taylor-swift-lover-album-review|archive-date=August 26, 2019}}</ref>

==Accolades==
''Lover'' and its singles received three nominations at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|62nd Grammy Awards]]. The album was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album|Best Pop Vocal Album]], which is Swift's third consecutive nomination in the category, following ''1989'' (2014) and ''Reputation'' (2017). "[[You Need to Calm Down]]" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance|Best Pop Solo Performance]] while the title track "[[Lover (Taylor Swift song)|Lover]]" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]].

Swift scored wins in all her five nominations at the [[American Music Awards of 2019]], becoming the most awarded artist of the night and the most awarded artist in the [[American Music Awards|AMAs]] history (29 wins), extending her record in the categories of [[American Music Award for Artist of the Year|Artist of the Year]], [[American Music Award for Favorite Pop/Rock Female Artist|Favorite Pop/Rock Female Artist]] and [[American Music Award for Favorite Pop/Rock Album|Favorite Pop/Rock Album]]. In the US, ''Lover'' sold around 450,000 copies in its first day, earning the biggest sales week of 2019, breaking the first-week sales record of [[Jonas Brothers]]' ''[[Happiness Begins]]'' (357,000) in a day.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8528285/taylor-swift-lover-us-biggest-sales-week-first-day|title=Taylor Swift's 'Lover' Lands 2019's Biggest Sales Week in U.S. After First Day of Release|first=Keith|last=Caulfield|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=August 24, 2019|access-date=August 24, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190824233622/https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8528285/taylor-swift-lover-us-biggest-sales-week-first-day|archive-date=August 24, 2019}}</ref> ''Lover'' debuted at number-one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, generating more than 867,000 units with around 679,000 pure sales, immediately becoming the country's best-selling album of 2019, and the biggest sales week since Swift's own ''Reputation.'' In Europe, ''Lover'' gave Swift her highest debuts on many charts thus far in her career. In the United Kingdom, it debuted at number one on the [[UK Albums Chart]] with 53,000 units, consisting of 35,000 traditional sales. It made Swift the first female artist to have four chart-topping albums in the 2010s decade.<ref>{{cite news|last=Copsey|first=Rob|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/taylor-swift-scores-fourth-uk-number-1-album-with-lover__27164/|title=Taylor Swift scores fourth UK Number 1 album with Lover|date=August 30, 2019|access-date=August 30, 2019|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190830155749/https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/taylor-swift-scores-fourth-uk-number-1-album-with-lover__27164/|archive-date=August 30, 2019|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]}}</ref> It also was the year's fastest-selling female digital album in the UK, surpassing [[Ariana Grande]]'s ''[[Thank U, Next]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/50-incredible-facts-about-the-uks-2019-official-singles-and-albums-charts__28019/|title=50 incredible facts about UK's 2019 Official Singles and Official Charts|last=White|first=Jack|date=December 27, 2019|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=December 28, 2019}}</ref> The album is certified Gold by the [[British Phonographic Industry]] for selling over 100,000 copies in the country. In Ireland, the album debuted at number one on the [[Irish Albums Chart]], making Swift the only female artist with four chart-topping albums in the country in the 2010s decade.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/taylor-swift-becomes-the-female-artist-with-the-most-irish-number-1-albums-this-decade-with-lover__27159/|title=Taylor Swift becomes the female artist with the most Irish Number 1 albums this decade with Lover|last=White|first=Jack|date=August 30, 2019|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190904085349/https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/taylor-swift-becomes-the-female-artist-with-the-most-irish-number-1-albums-this-decade-with-lover__27159/|archive-date=September 4, 2019|access-date=September 4, 2019}}</ref> ''Lover'' also went number-one in Belgium, Estonia, Norway, Portugal, Scotland, Spain and Sweden.<ref name=":21"/>

In Australia, ''Lover'' debuted at number one on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]], becoming Swift's fifth consecutive chart-topper in the country. It also posted the biggest first-week sales of 2019 in the country and all of the 18 tracks from the album appeared in the top 75 of the ARIA Top 100 Singles Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ariacharts.com.au/news/2019/lover-lands-taylor-swift-fifth-1-album|title=Lover lands Taylor Swift fifth #1 album|publisher=[[Australian Recording Industry Association]]|date=August 31, 2019|access-date=September 2, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190902065634/https://www.ariacharts.com.au/news/2019/lover-lands-taylor-swift-fifth-1-album|archive-date=September 2, 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8528884/taylor-swift-tones-and-i-australia-charts|title=Taylor Swift Blasts to No. 1 on Australia's Albums Chart With 'Lover'|last=Brandle|first=Lars|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=September 2, 2019|access-date=September 4, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190903205142/https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8528884/taylor-swift-tones-and-i-australia-charts|archive-date=September 3, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The album was certified 2Ã— Platinum in the country, denoting over 140,000 copies earned. ''Lover'' also debuted atop the [[Official New Zealand Music Chart|albums chart in New Zealand]], becoming Swift's sixth number-one album in the country.<ref name=":21"/>

In China, ''Lover'' was a massive success, topping the best-selling digital albums lists of all major Chinese music platforms. It became the first international album in Chinese history to surpass 1 million units within a week of its release, setting the record for the biggest sales week for an international artist in the country.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Taylor Swift's Lover tops one million album sale equivalents in China â€“ breaking international record|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/taylor-swifts-lover-tops-one-million-album-sale-equivalents-in-china-breaking-international-record/|date=August 30, 2019|website=Music Business Worldwide|access-date=May 16, 2020}}</ref> By the end of 2019, it became the best-selling and most consumed album of the year in China.<ref name=":11" /> ''Lover'' has been certified 3Ã— Diamond by [[QQ Music]].<ref name="QQMusic" /> It is one of the [[List of best-selling albums in China#Best-selling digital albums|best-selling digital albums of all time in China]]. Following ''1989'' and ''Reputation'', ''Lover'' is Swift's third album to rank in the all-time top 10, making her the first non-Chinese act to achieve this; all the three releases have exceeded 1 million digital units in the country.<ref name=":11">{{cite news|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-music-taylorswift-china/taylor-swifts-lover-album-breaks-new-record-in-china-idUKKCN1VK17J|publisher=[[Reuters]]|first=Tara|last=Oakes|editor-first=Gareth|editor-last=Jones|title=Taylor Swift's 'Lover' album breaks new record in China|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190830105858/https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-music-taylorswift-china/taylor-swifts-lover-album-breaks-new-record-in-china-idUKKCN1VK17J|archive-date=August 30, 2019|date=August 30, 2019|access-date=August 30, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Guinness"/> In Japan, ''Lover'' debuted at the third spot on the [[Oricon Albums Chart]] with only three days of tracking, moving over 27,500 units.<ref name=":21"/>

=== Worldwide ===
''Lover'' sold more than 3.2&nbsp;million pure copies worldwide in 2019.<ref name="2019sales">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/9338380/ifpi-best-selling-albums-list-2019|title=Arashi Best-Of Tops Taylor Swift for IFPI's Best-Selling Album of 2019|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 19, 2020|access-date=March 21, 2020}}</ref> [[Republic Records]] reported that the album earned 3 million units in global consumption in its first week.<ref name=":21"/> The strong global sales of ''Lover'' led the [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] (IFPI) to name Swift the number-one best selling artist of 2019, earning the honor for the second time, her first being in 2014 after the release of ''1989,'' making Swift the first female artist to do so.<ref name=":10">{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/taylor-swift-named-top-global-music-seller-2019-1203521492/|title=Taylor Swift Named Top Global Music Seller of 2019; Universal Claims 8 of Top 10 Artists|last=Willman|first=Chris|date=March 2, 2020|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|access-date=March 3, 2020}}</ref>

''Lover'' was the best-selling studio album of 2019, setting the [[Guinness World Records|''Guinness World'' ''record'']] for the biggest-selling album worldwide for a solo artist, marking Swift's seventh ''Guinness'' record.<ref name="Guinness">{{Cite web|title=Biggest-selling album worldwide for a solo artist (current)|url=https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/613283-biggest-selling-album-worldwide-for-a-solo-artist-current|access-date=2020-06-21|website=Guinness World Records|language=en-GB}}</ref> ''Lover'' was also the best-selling album by a female artist, second overall, behind Japanese band [[Arashi]]'s greatest hits album ''[[5x20 All the Best!! 1999â€“2019]]''.<ref name="2019sales"/> It is Swift's fourth consecutive album following ''Red'' (2012), ''1989'' (2014), and ''Reputation'' (2017) to place second on IFPI's annual chart, which has been tracking worldwide pure album sales since 2012. In February 2020, [[Universal Music Publishing Group]] reported that ''Lover'' has exceeded 5 million units in global consumption.<ref>{{Cite web|date=February 6, 2020|title=Taylor Swift Signs Exclusive Global Publishing Agreement With Universal Music Publishing Group|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/taylor-swift-signs-exclusive-global-publishing-agreement-with-universal-music-publishing-group-301000419.html|website=[[PR Newswire]]|publisher=Universal Music Publishing Group}}</ref>
