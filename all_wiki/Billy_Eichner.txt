'''Billy Eichner''' (born September 18, 1978) is an American comedian, actor, and producer. He is the star, executive producer, and creator of [[Funny Or Die]]'s ''[[Billy on the Street]]'', a comedy game show that aired on [[truTV]]. Eichner was nominated for a Daytime Emmy Award for "Outstanding Game Show Host" in 2013. He is also known for playing [[Craig Middlebrooks]] on the sitcom ''[[Parks and Recreation]]'' and [[Timon and Pumbaa|Timon]] in the [[The Lion King (2019 film)|2019 remake]] of ''[[The Lion King (1994 film)|The Lion King]]''.

==Career==
Eichner gained attention as the host and writer of ''Creation Nation: A Live Talk Show'', a critically acclaimed stage show in New York.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/05/15/theater/newsandfeatures/15calh.html|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|title=Hey Gang, Let's Put On a Twisted Talk Show|accessdate=August 21, 2014|date=May 15, 2005|first=Ada|last=Calhoun}}</ref> He also appeared on ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' as a special correspondent in original video shorts<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teamcoco.com/content/team-coco-heads-superbowl|title=Team Coco Heads To The Superbowl! [sic]|accessdate=August 21, 2014|date=February 3, 2012|publisher=teamcoco.com}}</ref> and as himself on [[Bravo (U.S. TV channel)|Bravo]]'s ''[[Watch What Happens: Live]]'' with [[Andy Cohen (television personality)|Andy Cohen]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bravotv.com/watch-what-happens-live/season-6/photos/show-shots/martha-plimpton-and-billy-eichner|title= Watch What Happens Live - Martha Plimpton and Billy Eichner|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131015050023/http://www.bravotv.com/watch-what-happens-live/season-6/photos/show-shots/martha-plimpton-and-billy-eichner|archive-date=October 15, 2013 |accessdate=August 1, 2012|publisher=[[Bravo (U.S. TV channel)|BravoTV]]}}</ref> ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbc.com/last-call-with-carson-daly/episode-guide/12192011.shtml|title=Last Call with Carson Daly |publisher= [[NBC]]|accessdate=August 21, 2014}}</ref> ''[[The Wendy Williams Show]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wendyshow.com/2012/02/24/latoya-jackson-90210-the-cw |title=LaToya Jackson and Billy Eichner |accessdate=August 21, 2014 |publisher=[[The Wendy Williams Show]] |date=February 24, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141228103445/http://www.wendyshow.com/2012/02/24/latoya-jackson-90210-the-cw/ |archive-date=December 28, 2014 }}</ref> and ''[[Fashion Police]]'' with [[Joan Rivers]], among others.

Eichner has been a [[voiceover]] guest star in several episodes of the [[Cartoon series|animated TV series]] ''[[Bob's Burgers]]'' as Mr. Ambrose, the Librarian. He has been a regular performer at New York City's [[Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ucbcomedy.com/talent/view/1826/billy-eichner|title=Billy Eichner|accessdate=August 1, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101022060111/http://www.ucbcomedy.com/talent/view/1826/billy-eichner|archive-date=October 22, 2010|publisher=ucbcomedy.com}}</ref>

On August 5, 2013, Eichner guest starred in the [[Parks and Recreation (season 6)|sixth season]] of ''[[Parks and Recreation]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://splitsider.com/2013/08/billy-eichner-to-play-donna|title=Billy Eichner to Play Donna's Doppelganger on 'Parks and Rec'|publisher=[[Splitsider]]|accessdate=August 21, 2014|first=Bradford|last=Evans|date=August 5, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170930035833/http://splitsider.com/2013/08/billy-eichner-to-play-donna/|archive-date=September 30, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Eichner's first episode aired on October 10, 2013. He stars as [[Donna Meagle|Donna's]] Eagleton counterpart, Craig Middlebrooks, who joins the [[Pawnee (Parks and Recreation)|Pawnee]] Parks and Recreation Department when Pawnee absorbs Eagleton.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/10/10/billy-eichner-parks-and-rec_n_4073919.html?ir=Comedy|title= Billy Eichner On 'Parks And Recreation' ‚Äì And Every Other Show Ever, Please (PHOTOS)|accessdate=August 21, 2014|newspaper=[[The Huffington Post]]|date=October 10, 2013|first=Maggie|last=Furlong}}</ref> He became a series regular in episode four of the seventh season of the show.

During the [[66th Primetime Emmy Awards]] show in 2014, Eichner appeared with show host [[Seth Meyers]] on the streets of New York, interviewing random people.<ref>{{cite web|first=Eric|last=Shorey|title=Billy Eichner and Seth Meyers Harass New Yorkers About The Emmy Awards|url=http://www.thebacklot.com/billy-eichner-and-seth-meyers-harass-new-yorkers-about-the-emmy-awards/08/2014|publisher=thebacklot.com|accessdate=December 27, 2014}}</ref>

Eichner starred in a [[Hulu]] original series titled ''[[Difficult People]]'' with Julie Klausner, and executive produced by [[Amy Poehler]], The project was originally set up at [[USA Network]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.hulu.com/2014/11/18/hulu-orders-amy-poehlers-difficult-people-starring-julie-klausner-and-billy-eichner/|title=Hulu Orders Amy Poehler's Difficult People Starring Julie Klausner and Billy Eichner|work=[[Hulu]]|access-date=August 23, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150831081926/http://blog.hulu.com/2014/11/18/hulu-orders-amy-poehlers-difficult-people-starring-julie-klausner-and-billy-eichner/|archive-date=August 31, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> The series began airing on August 5, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2015/06/watch-the-first-difficult-people-teaser.html|title=Watch the First Difficult People Teaser -- Vulture|work=Vulture}}</ref> In 2016, Eichner appeared in ''[[Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising]]'', released on May 20.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kroll|first1=Justin|title=Billy Eichner Joins Cast of 'Neighbors 2'|url=https://variety.com/2015/film/news/billy-eichner-neighbors-2-1201601900/|accessdate=October 30, 2015}}</ref> In 2017, he joined the cast of ''[[American Horror Story]]'' for recurring roles in its seventh and eighth seasons.<ref>{{cite web |last= Stack|first= Tim|title='American Horror Story': Billy Eichner Cast in Season 7|url=https://ew.com/tv/2017/03/28/american-horror-story-billy-eichner-season-7/|website=EW.com|accessdate=March 30, 2017|date=March 28, 2017}}</ref>

In 2019, Eichner provided the voice of [[Timon and Pumbaa|Timon]] in Disney's [[The Lion King (2019 film)|live action remake]] of ''[[The Lion King]]'', from director [[Jon Favreau]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/lion-king-cast-seth-rogen-billy-eichner-talks-voice-timon-pumba-997310|title=Seth Rogen, Billy Eichner in Talks to Voice Timon and Pumbaa in New 'Lion King'|last=Galuppo|first=Mia|date=April 25, 2017|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|accessdate=26 April 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=Disney|author-link=Disney|number=925853092255551488|date= November 1, 2017|title=#TheLionKing. 2019. ü¶Åüëë|accessdate=November 3, 2017 }}</ref> Eichner said that Timon "[is] such a great role that allows you to do so much. ===Politics===
Eichner is a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]]. On February 1, 2018, Eichner announced a new initiative called Glam Up the Midterms, during that night's episode of ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]'' in conjunction with Funny or Die and with the support of several late night hosts including [[Sarah Silverman]], [[James Corden]], [[Seth Meyers]], [[Jimmy Kimmel]], [[Conan O'Brien]], [[John Oliver]], [[Chelsea Handler]], [[Robin Thede]], and [[Andy Cohen]]. Glam Up the Midterm's purpose was getting people to vote in the November 6, 2018, midterm elections, especially millennials, of whom only 12% voted in the previous midterm election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/glam-up-the-midterms-2018-billy-eichner-john-oliver-andy-cohen-millennial-voting-campaign|title=Billy Eichner, John Oliver, Andy Cohen, and More Are Joining Forces to Glam Up the Midterms|work=Vogue|access-date=2018-03-01 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://mashable.com/2018/02/02/billy-eichner-launches-glam-up-the-mid-terms/ |title=Billy Eichner is on a mission to make the mid-terms glam again|last=Hamilton|first=Isobel|work=Mashable|access-date=2018-03-01 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/billy-eichner-launches-star-studded-campaign-get-millennials-vote-midterm-elections-1081342|title=Billy Eichner Launches Star-Studded Campaign to Get Millennials to Vote in Midterm Elections|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-03-01 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://glamupthemidterms.com/|title=Glam Up The Midterms|website=glamupthemidterms.com|access-date=2018-03-01}}</ref> Eichner joined the progressive political organization [[Swing Left]] as senior advisor starting in 2019<ref>{{Cite web|title=Billy Eichner Hits the Street With a New Goal: "Flipping the Senate" {{!}} Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/billy-eichner-hits-street-a-new-goal-flipping-senate-1255928|access-date=2020-11-03|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref> and endorsed [[Elizabeth Warren]] in the [[2020 Democratic Party presidential primaries]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/billyeichner/status/1232146620407377921|title=Because I love her and think she has ALL the qualities of a great leader, as well as a healing way of communicating that this country so desperately needs, I'm voting for @ewarren. Do with this info what you will. I'm not a perfect person & I once lied about seeing Wicked.|last=eichner|first=billy|date=2020-02-24|website=@billyeichner|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref>
