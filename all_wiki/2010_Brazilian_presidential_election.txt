The '''Brazilian presidential election''' was held in 2010 with [[Two-round system|two rounds]] of balloting. The election determined the successor to [[President of Brazil|President]] [[Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva]], known as Lula, of the left-wing [[Workers' Party (Brazil)|Workers' Party]],<ref>Colitt, Raymond. [https://www.reuters.com/article/idUSN3021637720100111?type=marketsNews "Key dates in Brazil's 2010 presidential race"]. [[Reuters]]. January 11, 2010.</ref> who was constitutionally ineligible to run for a third term  as he has already served two terms after winning the [[2002 Brazilian general election|elections in 2002]] and being [[2006 Brazilian general election|re-elected in 2006]].<ref name="NYT">Barrionuevo, Alexei. [https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/24/world/americas/24brazil.html?_r=1&scp=1&sq=Dilma%20Rousseff&st=cse "The Health of a Likely Presidential Candidate Comes Under Brazil's Microscope"]. ''[[The New York Times]]'', May 23, 2009. Retrieved June 14, 2009.</ref> 

With the support of Lula, the ruling Workers' Party nominated [[Dilma Rousseff]], a former member and co-founder of the [[Democratic Labour Party (Brazil)|Democratic Labour Party]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mares nunca dantes navegados|url=https://piaui.folha.uol.com.br/materia/mares-nunca-dantes-navegados/|access-date=2020-11-13|website=revista piauí|language=pt-BR}}</ref> who joined Lula's administration as [[Ministry of Mines and Energy (Brazil)|Ministry of Mines and Energy]] and later served as presidential [[Chief of Staff of the Presidency (Brazil)|Chief of Staff]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=NEWS IN ENGLISH - Dilma Rousseff biography|url=https://agencia-brasil.jusbrasil.com.br/noticias/2319394/news-in-english-dilma-rousseff-biography|access-date=2020-11-13|website=Jusbrasil|language=pt-br}}</ref> For her vice presidential [[Vice President of Brazil|running mate]], Dilma chose [[Michel Temer]], a member of the centre-right [[Brazilian Democratic Movement]], who served as [[President of the Chamber of Deputies (Brazil)|President of the Chamber of Deputies]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Temer é aprovado vice na chapa de Dilma à Presidência - Política|url=https://politica.estadao.com.br/noticias/geral,temer-e-aprovado-vice-na-chapa-de-dilma-a-presidencia,565622|access-date=2020-11-13|website=Estadão|language=pt-BR}}</ref> and previously considered a presidential run in his own right.

The centre-right [[Brazilian Social Democracy Party]] (PSDB) nominated [[José Serra]], who resigned as [[List of Governors of São Paulo|Governor of São Paulo]] to mount his presidential campaign. A former [[List of mayors of São Paulo|Mayor of São Paulo]] who had served as [[Ministry of Health (Brazil)|Minister of Health]] during the [[Fernando Henrique Cardoso|Cardoso Administration]], Serra had previously been the presidential nominee of his party in [[2002 Brazilian general election|2002]].<ref>{{cite news|date=25 September 2010|title=Editorial: O mal a evitar|language=Portuguese|newspaper=[[O Estado de S. Paulo]]|url=http://www.estadao.com.br/noticias/geral,editorial-o-mal-a-evitar,615255,0.htm|accessdate=27 October 2010}}</ref> For his vice presidential running mate, Serra chose [[Antônio Pedro de Siqueira Indio da Costa|Indio da Costa]], a conservative [[Federal Deputy of Brazil|Federal Deputy]] from [[Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro]] who was a member of the right-wing [[Democrats (Brazil)|Democrats]] party. Da Costa, who became embroiled in controversy over his suggestion that the Workers' Party was linked to the international drug trade, received international comparisons to American politician [[Sarah Palin]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=No Twitter, vice de Serra é comparado a Sarah Palin - 19/07/2010 - Poder - Folha de S.Paulo|url=https://m.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2010/07/769088-no-twitter-vice-de-serra-e-comparado-a-sarah-palin.shtml|access-date=2020-11-14|website=m.folha.uol.com.br}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Barrionuevo|first=Alexei|date=2010-07-26|title=Brazil’s President Works to Lend Popularity to a Protégée (Published 2010)|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/07/26/world/americas/26brazil.html|access-date=2020-11-14|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

Additionally, [[Marina Silva]], a [[Brazilian Senate|Senator]] from the northwestern state of [[Acre (state)|Acre]] and former [[Ministry of the Environment (Brazil)|Minister of the Environment]] under Lula, left the ruling Workers Party to mount a candidacy as part of the [[Green Party (Brazil)|Green Party]]. Silva criticized the environmental policies of the Lula Administration and ran a campaign in support of [[sustainable development]], ending corruption, and decriminalizing marijuana.<ref name="Maciel2">{{cite web|author=David Maciel|title=De Lula à Dilma Roussef: crise econômica, hegemonia neoliberal e regressão política|url=http://www.ifch.unicamp.br/formulario_cemarx/selecao/2012/trabalhos/7355_Maciel_David.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111224232/http://www.ifch.unicamp.br/formulario_cemarx/selecao/2012/trabalhos/7355_Maciel_David.pdf|archive-date=11 November 2013|accessdate=1 August 2012|language=Portuguese}}</ref> Silva, who would've been the first [[Afro-Brazilians|Black]] woman to serve as President,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2011-07-16|title=Marina Silva diz querer ser primeira mulher negra a ser presidente - Abril.com|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716023826/http://www.abril.com.br/noticias/brasil/marina-silva-diz-querer-ser-primeira-mulher-negra-ser-presidente-568637.shtml|access-date=2020-11-14|website=web.archive.org}}</ref> saw support from younger voters and managed to win almost 20% of the vote in the first round, well exceeding initial expectations.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2010-10-03|title=Marina Silva surpreende na reta final e pode gerar segundo turno, diz Instituto Análise {{!}} Guilherme Barros|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101003225834/http://colunistas.ig.com.br/guilhermebarros/2010/09/28/marina-silva-surpreende-na-reta-final-e-pode-gerar-segundo-turno-diz-instituto-analise/|access-date=2020-11-14|website=web.archive.org}}</ref>

Dilma defeated Serra in the second round, becoming the first and only female President of Brazil. The election was noted as being the first time since [[1989 Brazilian presidential election|the inaugural presidential election]] after the [[Brazilian military dictatorship|military dictatorship]] that Lula did not run for president.<ref name="Ray">Colitt, Raymond. [https://www.reuters.com/article/idUSN3013312120100111?loomia_ow=t0:s0:a49:g43:r1:c0.500000:b29738878:z0 "Positions of Brazil's leading candidates"]. Reuters. January 11, 2010.</ref>

=== Lula's potential successors ===
As Lula was ineligible to run for a third consecutive term due to term limits established by the [[Constitution of Brazil|Brazilian constitution]], speculation mounted in the years prior to the election around who might take the mantle of the [[Workers' Party (Brazil)|Workers' Party]] in his absence. Though Lula was widely popular, many commentators speculated that his likely successors in the PT would fail to generate his populist touch.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Colitt|first=Raymond|date=2008-04-09|title=Brazil President Lula hunting for right successor|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/brazil-politics-idUKN0317542320080409|access-date=2020-12-01}}</ref> Polling conducted in Lula's second term found that a slim majority favored amending the constitution to allow Lula to run for a third term, but he opposed such efforts.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Costa|first=Ana Nicolaci da|date=2008-04-28|title=Many in Brazil want third Lula term but he says no|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-brazil-politics-idUSN2848213920080428|access-date=2020-12-01}}</ref> Two of Lula's key allies tipped as potential successors, former [[Ministry of Finance (Brazil)|Minister of Finance]] [[Antonio Palocci]] and former presidential [[Chief of Staff of the Presidency (Brazil)|Chief of Staff]] [[José Dirceu]], were harmed by their involvement in the 2005 [[Mensalão scandal]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Line|first=A. TARDE On|title=Lula já planeja seu sucessor para 2010|url=http://www.atarde.com.br/politica/noticias/1129903-lula-ja-planeja-seu-sucessor-para-2010|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Portal A TARDE|language=pt-br}}</ref>

TARDE On|title=Lula já planeja seu sucessor para 2010|url=http://www.atarde.com.br/politica/noticias/1129903-lula-ja-planeja-seu-sucessor-para-2010|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Portal A TARDE|language=pt-br}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www2.senado.leg.br/bdsf/bitstream/handle/id/474262/noticia.htm?sequence=1|access-date=2020-12-01|website=www2.senado.leg.br}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Folha de S.Paulo - Presidente já pensa em Marta Suplicy ou Ciro Gomes para a disputa de 2010 - 03/09/2006|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/brasil/fc0309200611.htm|access-date=2020-12-01|website=www1.folha.uol.com.br}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Colitt|first=Raymond|date=2008-04-09|title=Brazil President Lula hunting for right successor|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/brazil-politics-idUKN0317542320080409|access-date=2020-12-01}}</ref> Polling conducted in 2008 found her to be a more popular potential presidential candidate than Dilma.<ref>{{Cite web|title=José Serra é o favorito à sucessão de Lula em 2010 - 31/03/2008 - Eleições - Datafolha|url=http://datafolha.folha.uol.com.br/eleicoes/2008/03/1197602-jose-serra-e-o-favorito-a-sucessao-de-lula-em-2010.shtml|access-date=2020-12-01|website=datafolha.folha.uol.com.br}}</ref> After losing her bid for reelection in 2004,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Folha de S.Paulo - ELEIÇÕES 2004Serra vence em São Paulo; PT sofre derrota no país - 01/11/2004|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/brasil/fc0111200415.htm|access-date=2020-12-01|website=www1.folha.uol.com.br}}</ref> Suplicy was appointed by Lula to the position of [[Ministry of Tourism (Brazil)|Minister of Tourism]], which was speculated to be an attempt at elevating her profile for a 2010 presidential bid.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Marta Suplicy aceita ser a nova ministra do Turismo {{!}} - Guia da Saúde - SC|url=http://clicrbs.com.br/especial/sc/qualidade-de-vida-sc/19,0,1451700,|access-date=2020-12-01|website=clicrbs.com.br}}</ref> In 2008, Suplicy mounted another bid was for Mayor of São Paulo, which was seen as a precursor to a potential presidential bid in 2010;<ref>{{Cite web|last=LR|first=Redacción|date=2008-10-04|title=Hoy se sabrá quién podría ser el sucesor de Lula|url=https://larepublica.pe/mundo/373070-hoy-se-sabra-quien-podria-ser-el-sucesor-de-lula/|access-date=2020-12-01|website=larepublica.pe|language=es-PE}}</ref> Suplicy lost the election, damaging her political fortunes.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mal presagio para Lula en ballotage del domingo|url=https://www.ambito.com/mundo/mal-presagio-lula-ballotage-del-domingo-n3523673|access-date=2020-12-01|website=www.ambito.com}}</ref>

In addition to Dilma, Suplicy, and Gomes, other PT members or allies were mentioned as being potentially tapped by Lula to run in his absence. [[Brazilian Senate|Senator]] [[Aloízio Mercadante]] of [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]], a key power-player in the PT who helped found the party<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reuters|title=Brazil's Rousseff shifts Cabinet into election gear|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-2014-02-03-sns-rt-brazil-rousseffcabinet-20140203-story.html|access-date=2020-12-01|website=chicagotribune.com|language=en-US}}</ref> and served as Lula's vice presidential running-mate in the [[1994 Brazilian general election|1994 presidential election]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Folha de S.Paulo - PT quer Mercadante para o lugar de Bisol - 27/7/1994|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/1994/7/27/brasil/11.html|access-date=2020-12-01|website=www1.folha.uol.com.br}}</ref> was also mentioned as a possible successor.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Line|first=A. === PSDB nomination ===
[[José Serra]], the [[List of Governors of São Paulo|Governors of São Paulo]], resigned from the position to mount a bid for the presidency as a member of the dominant centre-right [[Brazilian Social Democracy Party|Brazilian Social Democratic Party]] (PSDB). Serra previously served as the party's candidate in the [[2002 Brazilian general election|2002 presidential election]], where he lost to Lula. [[Aécio Neves]], the popular [[Governor of Minas Gerais]], was considered another potential candidate for the party, and later served as the party's nominee in the [[2014 Brazilian general election|2014 presidential election]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=PSDB 'está longe' de decidir nome para 2010, diz Aécio - Política|url=https://politica.estadao.com.br/noticias/eleicoes,psdb-esta-longe-de-decidir-nome-para-2010-diz-aecio,291140|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Estadão|language=pt-BR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=G1 > Política - NOTÍCIAS - Aécio Neves desiste de disputar a Presidência em 2010|url=http://g1.globo.com/Noticias/Politica/0,,MUL1419120-5601,00-AECIO+NEVES+DESISTE+DE+DISPUTAR+A+PRESIDENCIA+EM.html|access-date=2020-12-01|website=g1.globo.com}}</ref> During the campaign, Serra sought to challenge the perception of the PSDB as an elitist party.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Line|first=A. TARDE On|title=Serra quer mudar suposta imagem elitista do PSDB|url=http://www.atarde.com.br/politica/eleicoes/noticias/1296890-serra-quer-mudar-suposta-imagem-elitista-do-psdb|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Portal A TARDE|language=pt-br}}</ref> Serra's moderate positions on social issues such as [[Abortion in Brazil|abortion]] and religious issues, where he took a [[Secularism|secularist]] approach compared to many conservative politicians, was noted.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2012-10-30|title=As vidas possíveis de José Serra|url=https://www.cartacapital.com.br/politica/as-vidas-possiveis-de-jose-serra/|access-date=2020-12-01|website=CartaCapital|language=pt-BR}}</ref>

=== Efforts by PSOL ===
[[Heloísa Helena (politician)|Heloísa Helena]], a prominent former [[Brazilian Senate|Senator]] from [[Alagoas]], considered a presidential candidacy on the [[Socialism and Liberty Party]] (PSOL) ticket.<ref name="Col" /> Formerly a member of the dominant Workers' Party (PT), Helena was expelled from the PT in 2003 for criticizing the party's move to the centre under the Lula presidency. In the run-up to the election, Helena was considered a serious prospective candidate, being described as the only viable candidate who could potentially abandon the country's [[free market|market-friendly]] economic policies.<ref name="Col" /> However, she declined to run for president in order to win back her Senate seat; she lost her race for Senate.<ref name="US">{{in lang|pt}} Faria, Tales. == Issues ==
Both candidates offered little threat to the economic stability of the country, but they differed significantly on issues such as [[Balanced budget|fiscal discipline]], [[foreign policy]] and [[Interventionism (politics)|state intervention]].<ref name="Ray" /> They were both likely to maintain a primary budget surplus to make [[public debt]] payments and reduce the ratio of debt to [[GDP]]. Some analysts believe Serra would have contained expenditure more effectively.<ref name="Ray" /> Rousseff, in the other hand, favors a bigger role for state enterprises in the economy, which could reduce participation by private firms in sectors such as banking, oil and gas.<ref name="Ray" /> Serra, who authorized the sale of [[Banco Nossa Caixa|Nossa Caixa bank]] in 2008, is seen as more open to [[privatization]], as well as cuts in the [[public sector]] payroll.<ref name="Col" /> While past elections brought economic instability, in 2010 neither candidate was expected to stray far from current economic policies.<ref name="WSJ">Prada, Paulo. [https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748704743404575127913634823670?mod=WSJ_hpp_MIDDLENexttoWhatsNewsTop "For Brazil, It's Finally Tomorrow"]. ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]''. March 29, 2010.</ref>

Rousseff was expected to continue Lula's foreign policy, boosting ties with developing nations, pushing for reform of multilateral bodies and lobbying for a permanent seat on the [[U.N. Security Council]].<ref name="Ray" /> Serra would likely have cooled ties with Lula's left-wing allies in [[Latin America]], which could affect energy investments in both [[Bolivia]] and [[Venezuela]].<ref name="Ray" /> He could also take a harder line in trade disputes with Argentina and [[Mercosur]].<ref name="Ray" /> According to [[Mark Weisbrot]], in an [[op-ed]] published by ''[[The Guardian Unlimited]]'' on January 29, 2010, if the centre-right candidate wins the race, it "would really be a huge win for the [U.S.] [[U.S. State Department|State Department]]." He argues that "while U.S. officials under both [[George W. Bush|Bush]] and [[Barack Obama|Obama]] have maintained a friendly posture toward Brazil, it is obvious that they deeply resent the changes in Brazilian foreign policy [...] and its independent stances with regard to the [[Middle East]], [[Iran]] and elsewhere."<ref>Weisbrot, Mark. [http://www.huffingtonpost.com/mark-weisbrot/why-washington-cares-abou_b_444459.html "Why Washington Cares About Countries Like Haiti And Honduras"]. ''[[The Huffington Post]]''. February 1, 2010. (Originally published by ''[[The Guardian Unlimited]]'' on January 29, 2010).</ref>

==Campaign==
The official campaign began on July 6, 2010.<ref name="Glob">{{in lang|pt}} [http://g1.globo.com/jornal-da-globo/noticia/2010/07/comeca-campanha-eleitoral-dos-9-candidatos-que-concorrem-presidencia.html "Começa campanha eleitoral dos 9 candidatos que concorrem à Presidência"]. ''Jornal da Globo''. July 7, 2010.</ref> The Superior Electoral Court has accepted the candidacies of all nine applicant candidates. According to the Superior Electoral Court's guidelines, once the official campaign began the candidates are allowed to participate on marches, motorcades, and use sound trucks to ask for votes and distributing leaflets.<ref name="Glob" /> But they are forbidden to distribute shirts, hats, and gifts such as keyrings and pens.<ref name="Glob" /> Rallies are allowed, but music concerts are prohibited.<ref name="Glob" /> The candidates are not allowed to advertise on streetlights, bridges, clubs and other places of common use.<ref name="Glob" /> Billboard ads are also prohibited, as well as attendance in inauguration of public premises.<ref name="Glob" />

===Issues===

==== Public health ====
One of the main public health issues debated so far in the election, raised by Rousseff's campaign, is the addiction to [[crack cocaine]].<ref>{{in lang|pt}} Mendonça, Ricardo. [http://colunas.epoca.globo.com/bocadeurna/2010/05/12/dilma-e-o-crack/ "Boca de Urna: Dilma e o crack"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110813210311/http://colunas.epoca.globo.com/bocadeurna/2010/05/12/dilma-e-o-crack/ |date=August 13, 2011 }}. ''[[Época (Brazilian magazine)|Época]]''. May 12, 2010.</ref> As a response to her campaign, Serra said he will establish clinics to treat addicts.<ref name="Prop">{{in lang|pt}} [http://noticias.r7.com/brasil/noticias/veja-as-principais-propostas-dos-presidenciaveis-dilma-marina-e-serra-para-cinco-areas-20100707.html "Veja as principais propostas dos presidenciáveis Dilma, Marina e Serra para cinco áreas"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100710130447/http://noticias.r7.com/brasil/noticias/veja-as-principais-propostas-dos-presidenciaveis-dilma-marina-e-serra-para-cinco-areas-20100707.html |date=2010-07-10 }}. R7. July 7, 2010.</ref> He also said he will hand over 150 medical specialties clinics in two years.<ref name="Prop" /> Rousseff said she will expand measures currently implemented by the Lula government.<ref name="Prop" /> She has also advocated the need for national production and distribution of medicines, through increased public investment.<ref name="Prop" /> Silva has advocated the focus on disease prevention.<ref name="Prop" />

==== Education ====
Serra has pledged to invest in the infrastructure of [[primary school|primary]] public schools, while Rousseff said that eradicating [[illiteracy]] is her top priority.<ref name="Prop" /> She has also proposed the creation of a National Articulated System of Education to redesign the mechanisms employed in managing the sector.<ref name="Prop" /> Silva said her focus is to invest intensively in all levels of formal education. She has also advocated the expansion of technologies access and the adoption of central lines to be addressed by educators.<ref name="Prop" />

==== Welfare ====
Serra has compromised to retain ''[[Bolsa Família]]'', claiming it will be expanded through aids to young people that take [[vocational education]] courses.<ref name="Prop" /> Dilma also said that she will expand the program, defending the "institutional strengthening" of the Ministry of Social Development and Action Against Hunger, which means that the ministry will be responsible for integrating all social policies of the government.<ref name="Prop" /> Silva defends a "third generation of social welfare", which would be achieved through partnerships with the private sector and the structuring of more educational projects.<ref name="Prop" />

==== Employment ====
Serra has committed to expand technical schools in order to create more jobs.<ref name="Prop" /> He has also said that improving the infrastructure of public services will be a tool for creating new jobs.<ref name="Prop" /> Rousseff has defended the maintenance of the economic policies of the Lula government, but has also promised to hold a tax reform in order to alleviate the expenses of workers.<ref name="Prop" /> Silva has proposed the creation of [[green job]]s through tax incentives for environmentally friendly businesses, in order to reduce the emission and consumption of [[carbon dioxide]].<ref name="Prop" />

==== Public safety ====
Although it was not included on his government plan, Serra's main proposal for public safety is the creation of a Ministry of Public Safety.<ref name="Prop" /> On the other hand, Rousseff has promised to expand the current National Public Security and Citizenship Program to the whole country.<ref name="Prop" /> She has also proposed the creation of a Constitutional Fund for Public Security, which would give aids in the wages of police officers nationwide.<ref name="Prop" /> Silva defended the creation of a "new institutional structure for public safety", which would combine the police work with investments in preventive policies.<ref name="Prop" />

=== Debates ===
{{main|Brazilian presidential election debates, 2010}}

For the 2010 presidential election, the Superior Electoral Court has approved three televised debates, in addition to an unprecedented internet debate, which will be held by [[Universo Online|UOL]] and ''[[Folha de S.Paulo]]'' on August 18.<ref name="EFE">{{in lang|pt}} EFE. [https://www.google.com/hostednews/epa/article/ALeqM5jRjL4zIDP0cZdV7iVxsj2zUNx5wg "Final da Libertadores teve audiência maior que debate dos presidenciáveis"]. European Pressphoto Agency. August 6, 2010.</ref>

According to the Superior Electoral Court's guidelines, the candidates whose parties are not represented in the [[Chamber of Deputies of Brazil|lower chamber of the National Congress]] are not able to participate in televised debates.<ref name="deb" /> Such candidates are challenging this decision in order to be able to participate on the debates.<ref name="deb" />

The first presidential debate took place on August 5, held by [[Rede Bandeirantes]].<ref name="EFE" /> The second debate was held on August 18, 2010 by internet portal UOL and newspaper ''[[Folha de S.Paulo]]''. It was the first presidential debate broadcast exclusively through internet in the history of the country.

=== Electoral programme ===
[[File:Debate presidencia 2010 CNI.jpg|thumb|right|Presidential debate.]]
According to the electoral law, all free-to-air television and radio networks must carry two 50-minute time slots a day<ref name="BBC">[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-latin-america-11008240 "Brazil candidates begin daily election broadcasts"]. BBC. August 18, 2010.</ref> from August 17 until September 30, 2010.<ref name="Dau">Dáu, Priscila. [http://www.correio24horas.com.br/noticias/detalhes/detalhes-1/artigo/comeca-amanha-horario-eleitoral-gratuito-no-radio-e-na-televisao/ "Começa amanhã horário eleitoral gratuito no rádio e na televisão"]. ''Correio''. September 16, 2010.</ref> The time allocated to each candidate is based on the number of seats held by the parties comprising their coalition in the Chamber of Deputies.<ref name="Dau" /> The electoral programs are considered a key campaign tool in Brazil, where TV and radio are the main sources of information for most voters.<ref name="BBC" /> The free air time on radio and TV also includes candidates contesting races for Governors, Legislative Assemblies, and both houses of the Congress.<ref name="BBC" /> Parties are also allowed to run six 30-second advertisements per day.<ref name="BBC" />

==Opinion polling==
{{Main|Opinion polling for the 2010 Brazilian presidential election}}
From January 1, 2010 up to the day preceding the election, all polls had to be registered with the [[Superior Electoral Court (Brazil)|Superior Electoral Court]].<ref>{{in lang|pt}} [http://noticias.terra.com.br/eleicoes/2010/noticias/0,,OI4392823-EI15311,00-Confira+o+calendario+eleitoral.html "Confira o calendário eleitoral 2010"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100827143415/http://noticias.terra.com.br/eleicoes/2010/noticias/0,,OI4392823-EI15311,00-Confira+o+calendario+eleitoral.html |date=2010-08-27 }}. Terra.</ref>

[[File:Graph-umfragen-wahlen-brasilien-2010.gif|thumb|Opinion polling in 2010]]
According to polls, Rousseff was most likely to have won the race in the first round with over 50% of the valid voting intentions. She had a rapid increase in her popularity since mid-2009,{{Why?|date=January 2011}} and thus consolidated her lead against Serra, who had led the polls for over two years. She was given a further boost when incumbent President Lula campaigned on her behalf on her television programmes.<ref name="Col" /> Marina Silva was not able to reach more than 10% in the polls, but at the end of the campaign she experienced a rapid boost,{{Why?|date=January 2011}} and achieved 14% at the last poll. The other candidates had been unable to reach more than 2%. The number of undecided voters and those who declared an intention to vote blank or null was at about 12% according to the last poll.

| {{EU}} || President of the European Commission [[José Manuel Barroso]] congratulated Rousseff for her victory in the presidential elections, and highlighted the historical significance of a woman to assume these functions for the first time in Brazil. "Allow me to highlight and congratulate you with the historical significance of the first election of a woman as President of the Federative Republic of Brazil," read the message sent by José Manuel Barroso to the new President of Brazil. The European leader recalled that "Brazil is a strategic partner of prime importance for the EU, sharing values and common strategic objectives" and advocated the continuation of Brazil's role in world affairs, a country "stronger, able to contribute to the resolution of global problems, to the stability and prosperity of Latin America and the intensification of bilateral relations with the European Union". According to Barroso, "The European Commission is confident that it will continue to work with Your Excellency and your Government in deepening the strategic partnership between the EU and Brazil, established in July 2007, and that it will further strengthen in the coming years".<ref>[http://arquivo.pt/wayback/20101101164048/http://jn.sapo.pt/PaginaInicial/Mundo/Interior.aspx?content_id=1700362 Durão Barroso felicita vitória de Dilma Rousseff e quer continuação de parceria estratégica] Jornal de Notícias.
