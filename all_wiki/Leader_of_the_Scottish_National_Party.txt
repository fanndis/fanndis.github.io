The Leader of the [[Scottish National Party]] is the head of the SNP. The incumbent is [[Nicola Sturgeon]] who was elected in November 2014, succeeding [[Alex Salmond]] as party leader and [[First Minister of Scotland]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nicola Sturgeon elected SNP leader|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/av/uk-scotland-30052051/nicola-sturgeon-elected-snp-leader|website=BBC News|language=en-GB|date=14 November 2014|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref>

[[Alex Salmond]] announced his intention to resign as the leader of the [[Scottish National Party]] on 19 September 2014, following the defeat of the [[Yes Scotland|Yes Scotland campaign]] in the [[2014 Scottish independence referendum]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Carrell|first=Severin|last2=Wintour|first2=Patrick|last3=Mason|first3=Rowena|date=2014-09-19|title=Alex Salmond resigns as first minister after Scotland rejects independence|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/sep/19/alex-salmond-resigns-first-minister-for-scotland|access-date=2020-05-10|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> On 14 November 2014, he was replaced by [[Nicola Sturgeon]], who won the [[2014 Scottish National Party leadership election]]. Sturgeon was officially sworn-in as [[First Minister of Scotland|First Minister]] on 20 November 2014. The following day, [[John Swinney]] was appointed as the [[Deputy First Minister of Scotland]] by Sturgeon.

== History ==
{{Expand section|date=May 2020}}The post of Leader of the Scottish National Party was officially created on 7 April 1934; the first leader was [[Alexander MacEwen]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Scotland Back in the Day: The origins of the SNP – from Burns to MacEwen|url=https://www.thenational.scot/news/14862061.scotland-back-in-the-day-the-origins-of-the-snp-from-burns-to-macewen/|website=The National|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref>The [[Scottish National Party]] first came into power on 16 May 2007; they were led by [[Alex Salmond]], where the [[Scottish Labour|Scottish Labour Party]] lost their 50 year rule of [[Scotland]] by one seat.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Wintour|first=Patrick|last2=editor|first2=political|date=2007-05-04|title=SNP wins historic victory|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2007/may/04/scotland.devolution|access-date=2020-05-09|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

== Roles ==
Currently, the leader [[Nicola Sturgeon]], the [[First Minister of Scotland|First Minister]] and Leader of the [[Scottish National Party]], is responsible for the overall growth. They usually advocate for [[Scottish independence]] and [[Pro-Europeanism|Pro Europeanism]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=SNP calls for opposition parties to back two-year Brexit transition extension due to coronavirus crisis|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/coronavirus-brexit-transition-period-snp-demand-two-year-delay-a9499211.html|date=2020-05-05|website=The Independent|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref>

Keith Brown is the [[Deputy Leader|Depute Leader]] of the [[Scottish National Party]]; however, he is not the [[Deputy First Minister of Scotland]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Keith Brown|url=https://www.parliament.scot/msps/currentmsps/keith-brown-msp.aspx|date=2019-10-09|website=www.parliament.scot|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=2018-06-08 |title=Keith Brown elected SNP deputy leader |language=en-GB |work=BBC News |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-44397652 |access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref>

[[Ian Blackford]] is the Leader of the [[Scottish National Party]] in Westminster, along with [[Kirsty Blackman]], who is Deputy Leader.<ref>{{Cite web|title=New appointments this week in UK politics, the civil service and public affairs|url=https://www.politicshome.com/members/article/new-appointments-this-week-in-uk-politics-the-civil-service-and-public-affairs-65458|date=2020-02-25|website=Politics Home|language=en|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Scottish National Party press release|url=https://www.snp.org/the-real-opposition-meet-your-new-snp-westminster-frontbench-team/|last=|first=|date=|website=Scottish National Party|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

== Independence vote ==
In 2007 [[Alex Salmond]] announced on the manifesto that "The SNP believe Scotland and England should be equal nations – friends, and partners - both free to make our own choices"<ref>{{Cite news|last=Tempest|first=Matthew|last2=correspondent|first2=political|date=2007-04-12|title=SNP manifesto: key points|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2007/apr/12/scotland.devolution1|access-date=2020-05-10|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> In 2014, the SNP-led Scottish Government ran a vote to get the nation out of the [[United Kingdom]]; it was unsuccessful. Later that day, [[Alex Salmond|Salmond]] resigned.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Salmond resigns following defeat in Scottish independence referendum|url=http://theconversation.com/alex-salmond-resigns-following-defeat-in-scottish-independence-referendum-31816|last=Leith|first=Murray|website=The Conversation|language=en|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref>He previously said "This is a once in a generation opportunity for Scotland" He pledged that; there would be no more [[Scottish independence|independence]] referendums.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Salmond: 'This is a once in a generation opportunity for Scotland'|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/scottish-independence/11095210/Alex-Salmond-This-is-a-once-in-a-generation-opportunity-for-Scotland.html|last=Lockhart|first=Keely|last2=PA|date=2014-09-14|website=www.telegraph.co.uk|language=en|access-date=2020-05-11|last3=Sep 2014|first3=video source BBC One 1:07PM BST 14}}</ref>

Nicola Sturgeon later said, "she could think of no greater privilege than to lead the party":<ref>{{Cite news|date=2014-09-19|title=Politicians react to Salmond exit|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-29287380|access-date=2020-05-12}}</ref> she was later unopposed in the leadership election.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Castle|first=Stephen|date=2014-11-14|title=Nicola Sturgeon Becomes Leader of Scottish National Party|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/11/15/world/europe/nicola-sturgeon-scottish-national-party-leader.html|access-date=2020-05-12|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>
