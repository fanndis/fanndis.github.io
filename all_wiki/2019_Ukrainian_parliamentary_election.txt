'''[[Snap election]]s to the [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] parliament'''  were held on 21 July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.president.gov.ua/documents/3032019-27121|script-title=uk:Указ Президента України №303/2019|trans-title=Decree of the President of Ukraine No. 303/2019|language=uk|website=Official internet site of the President of Ukraine|date=21 May 2019|accessdate=6 July 2019}}</ref> Originally scheduled to be held at the end of October, these elections were brought forward after newly inaugurated [[President of Ukraine|President]] [[Volodymyr Zelensky]] dissolved parliament on 21 May 2019, during his inauguration.<ref name="poll/30010735"/> The election result was the one-party majority, a novelty in Ukraine, for President Zelensky's [[Servant of the People (political party)|Servant of the People]] party with 254 seats.<ref name=CEUp79905/>

About 80 percent of the elected candidates were new to parliament; 83 deputies managed to get reelected from [[8th_Ukrainian_Verkhovna_Rada|the previous parliament]] and 13 deputies from earlier convocations.<ref name=CEUp79905/> All deputies from Servant of the People were political newcomers.<ref name=CEUp79905/> 61 percent of the new MPs had never before been engaged in politics.<ref name=CEUp79905/>

Out of 225 constituencies, 26 were suspended due to the [[Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation|March 2014 annexation]] of [[Crimea]] by [[Russia]] and the [[War in Donbass|ongoing occupation]] of parts of [[Donetsk Oblast]] and [[Luhansk Oblast]] by separatist forces of the self-declared [[Donetsk People's Republic]] and [[Luhansk People's Republic]] ([[Timeline of the war in Donbass (April–June 2014)|since April 2014]]).

==Background==
Originally scheduled to be held at the end of October 2019, the 2019 Ukrainian parliamentary elections were brought forward after newly inaugurated [[President of Ukraine|President]] [[Volodymyr Zelensky]] dissolved parliament early on 21 May 2019 (a day after his inauguration), despite claims that he did not have the legal grounds to do this. After Zelensky issued the decree (calling early elections), a lawsuit was filed to the [[Constitutional Court of Ukraine]], which sought to declare the decree unconstitutional and therefore illegal.<ref name="Noelectionsglavcom">{{cite web|last=Vuyets|first=Pavlo|url=https://glavcom.ua/publications/viboriv-ne-bude-nad-chim-chakluje-konstituciyniy-sud-600915.html|script-title=uk:Виборів не буде? Над чим чаклує Конституційний суд|trans-title=There will be no elections? What the Constitutional Court is conjuring|language=uk|website=glavcom.ua|date=11 June 2019|accessdate=13 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Sazonov|first=Kyrylo|url=https://glavcom.ua/columns/kyrylosazonov/konstituciyniy-sud-do-kincya-chervnya-virishit-chi-buv-zakonnim-ukaz-zelenskogo-pro-rozpusk-radi-599444.html|script-title=uk:Конституційний суд до кінця червня вирішить чи був законним указ Зеленського про розпуск Ради|trans-title=By the end of June, the Constitutional Court will decide whether Zelensky's decree on the dissolution of the Council was lawful|language=uk|website=glavcom.ua|date=5 June 2019|accessdate=13 July 2019|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190713235257/https://glavcom.ua/columns/kyrylosazonov/konstituciyniy-sud-do-kincya-chervnya-virishit-chi-buv-zakonnim-ukaz-zelenskogo-pro-rozpusk-radi-599444.html|archivedate=13 July 2019}}</ref> The court declared the decree to be legal on 20 June 2019.<ref name="poll/30010735">{{cite news|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/ukrainian-constitutional-court-oks-parliament-s-dissolution-early-poll/30010735.html|title=Ukrainian Constitutional Court OKs Parliament's Dissolution, Early Elections|publisher=[[Radio Free Europe]]|date=20 June 2019|accessdate=6 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.unian.ua/elections/10581924-ksu-viznav-konstituciynim-ukaz-zelenskogo-pro-rozpusk-radi.html|script-title=uk:КСУ визнав конституційним указ Зеленського про розпуск Ради|trans-title=The Constitutional Court recognised Zelensky's constitutional decree to dissolve the Council|language=uk|publisher=www.unian.ua|date=20 June 2019|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> The official reason why Zelensky dissolved parliament was "a lack of a [[government coalition]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.unian.info/politics/10587333-presidential-administration-in-ukraine-says-move-to-dissolve-rada-legally-correct.html|title=Presidential Administration in Ukraine says move to dissolve Rada legally correct|publisher=[[UNIAN]]|date=16 June 2019|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> 

Following the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2014 parliamentary elections]], the [[European solidarity|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]] (PPB) party became the largest party, after securing 132 seats. On 21 November 2014, the Petro Poroshenko Bloc, People's Front, Self Reliance, Fatherland and the Radical Party all signed a coalition agreement.<ref name=YG2wb>{{cite news|url=https://focus.ua/politics/302623|title=Five political forces sign coalition agreement|publisher=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date=21 November 2014|accessdate=14 July 2019}}<br>{{cite news|url=https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/235527.html|title=Ukraine's parliamentary parties initial coalition agreement|publisher=Interfax-Ukraine|date=21 November 2014|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> [[Arseniy Yatsenyuk]] became [[Prime Minister of Ukraine|Prime Minister]] on 2 December 2014. The Radical Party left the coalition on 1 September 2015 in protest over a vote in parliament involving a change to the [[Constitution of Ukraine|Ukrainian Constitution]] that would lead to [[Minsk II|decentralization and greater powers]] for [[War in Donbass|areas held by separatists]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Krasnolutska|first=Daryna|last2=Verbyany|first2=Volodymyr|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-09-01/ukraine-radical-party-quits-ruling-coalition-after-deadly-clash|title=Ukraine Radical Party Quits Ruling Coalition After Deadly Clash|publisher=[[Bloomberg News]]|url-access=subscription|date=1 September 2015|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> February 2016 saw the start of the fall of the Yatsenyuk cabinet after the economy minister [[Aivaras Abromavičius]] announced his resignation claiming the government did not have real commitment to fight corruption.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.unian.info/economics/1253799-ukraine-finance-minister-decides-to-resign.html|title=Ukraine Economy Minister decides to resign|publisher=[[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency|UNIAN]]|date=3 February 2016|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> On 17 and 18 February 2016, the Fatherland and Self Reliance parties left the coalition; meaning that the coalition became 5 deputies short of the 226 needed.<ref >{{cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/325715.html|title=Samopomich pulls out from ruling coalition in parliament|publisher=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date=18 February 2016|accessdate=14 July 2019}}<br>{{cite news|url=http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7099477/|script-title=uk:"Самопоміч" виходить із коаліції|trans-title="Self-help" emerges from the coalition|language=uk|publisher=[[Ukrayinska Pravda]]|date=18 February 2016|accessdate=14 July 2019}}<br>{{cite news|url=https://www.unian.info/politics/1267671-batkivschyna-faction-pulls-out-of-coalition.html|title=Batkivschyna faction pulls out of coalition|publisher=[[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency|UNIAN]]|date=17 February 2016|accessdate=14 July 2019}}<br>{{cite news|last=Goncharova|first=Olena|url=http://www.kyivpost.com/article/content/ukraine-politics/batkivshchyna-faction-leaves-ruling-coalition-408285.html|title=Batkivshchyna faction leaves ruling coalition|publisher=[[Kyiv Post]]|date=17 February 2016|accessdate=14 July 2019|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160217142355/http://www.kyivpost.com/article/content/ukraine-politics/batkivshchyna-faction-leaves-ruling-coalition-408285.html|archivedate=17 February 2016}}</ref> On 14 April 2016, [[Volodymyr Groysman]] became the new Prime Minister and the [[Groysman government]] began with a new cabinet of ministers.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-36043967|title=Ukraine MPs approve Volodymyr Groysman as new PM|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=14 April 2016|accessdate=14 April 2016}}</ref>
Due to the short period of time available to organize the 2019 parliamentary election, current Ukrainian public procurement laws were not followed and to bypass this, local election commissions will work under deferred payment.<ref name="Noelectionsglavcom"/>

==Electoral system==
Under current law 225 members of the Verkhovna Rada are elected by nationwide [[closed list|closed]] [[party-list proportional representation]] with 5% electoral [[election threshold]] and the other 225 seats elected in [[Electoral district|constituencies]] with a [[First-past-the-post voting|first-past-the-post electoral system]] in one round (candidate with the highest vote total wins).<ref name=Unian29916>{{cite news|last=Pryshliak|first=Nadia|url=http://www.unian.info/politics/1545927-electoral-dead-end-for-rada.html|title=Electoral dead-end for Rada|publisher=[[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency|UNIAN]]|date=29 September 2016|accessdate=6 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Parliament passes law on parliamentary elections|url=https://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/parliament-passes-law-on-parliamentary-elections-117151.html|agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|publisher=[[Kyiv Post]]|date=17 November Out of 225 constituencies, 26 are suspended due to the [[Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation|March 2014 annexation]] of [[Crimea]] by [[Russia]] and the occupation of parts of [[Donetsk Oblast]] and [[Luhansk Oblast]] [[War in Donbass|by separatists]] ([[Timeline of the war in Donbass (April–June 2014)|since April 2014]]).{{fact|date=June 2020}}

Candidates had until 20 June to submit documents to the [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]] to register as candidates for the position of deputy of the Verkhovna Rada.<ref>{{cite web|last=Fesenko|first=Volodymyr|url=https://blogs.pravda.com.ua/authors/fesenko/5cfec861d04c0/|script-title=ru:Дневник парламентских выборов-2019 (30 мая – 10 июня 2019 г.)|trans-title=Diary of the parliamentary elections-2019 (30 May – 10 June 2019)|language=ru|publisher=[[Ukrayinska Pravda]]|date=11 June 2019|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> On 25 June 2019, the Central Election Commission ended its registration process.<ref name="kandidativ-604913">{{cite web|url=https://glavcom.ua/news/do-verhovnoji-radi-balotuyutsya-5845-kandidativ-604913.html|script-title=uk:До Верховної Ради балотуються 5845 кандидатів|trans-title=5845 candidates are running for the Verkhovna Rada|language=uk|website=glavcom.ua|date=26 June 2019|access-date=7 July 2019}}</ref> It registered 5,845 candidates for the elections:<ref name="kandidativ-604913"/> 3,171 candidates in the single-member constituencies and 2,674 candidates in the single nationwide constituency with 22 parties.<ref name="kandidativ-604913"/><ref name="partiv">{{cite web|url=https://glavcom.ua/news/do-verhovnoji-radi-balotuyutsya-5845-kandidativ-604913.html|script-title=uk:Выборы в Верховную Раду 2019|trans-title=Elections to Verkhovna Rada|language=uk|website=fakty.com.ua|date=12 July 2019|access-date=12 July 2019}}</ref>

Since 2014, various politicians have proposed to reform the electoral system to 100% party-list proportional representation with open lists.<ref name=Unian29916/> President Zelensky is the main proponent.<ref name="Zelenskyhromadske">{{cite news|url=https://hromadske.ua/ru/posts/zelenskij-ekstrenno-sobiraet-radu-22-maya|script-title=ru:Зеленский экстренно собирает Раду 22 мая|trans-title=Zelensky urgently assembles Rada on 22 May|language=ru|publisher=hromadske.ua|date=22 May 2019|accessdate=14 July 2019|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190522134923/https://hromadske.ua/ru/posts/zelenskij-ekstrenno-sobiraet-radu-22-maya|archivedate=22 May 2019}}</ref> The proposal is opposed by [[Yulia Tymoshenko]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://focus.ua/politics/302623|script-title=uk:Тимошенко выступила против выборов с открытыми списками|trans-title=Tymoshenko has opposed open-list elections|language=uk|website=focus.ua|date=7 April 2014|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref> A vote on the proposal (authored by the president) was supposed to take place on 22 May 2019, but members of parliament voted against including it in the agenda.<ref name="Zelenskyhromadske"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.unian.info/politics/10559205-ukraine-s-rada-fails-to-vote-for-election-law-amendments.html|title=Ukraine's Rada fails to vote for election law amendments|website=www.unian.info|date=22 May 2019|accessdate=14 July 2019}}</ref>
