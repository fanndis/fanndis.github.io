'''Jan Tore Sanner''' (born 6 May 1965) is a Norwegian politician for the [[Conservative Party of Norway|Conservative Party]] who serves as Minister of Finance since 2020.<ref name=[[NRK]]>{{cite web | url= https://www.nrk.no/norge/slik-er-solberg-regjeringen-4.0-1.14873946 | publisher= [[NRK]] | title= This Is Solberg’s Cabinet 4.0 | date= 24 January 2020 | accessdate= 24 January 2020}};</ref> He previously served as Minister of Education from 2018-2020 and Minister of Local Government from 2013-2018.

==Early life and education==
Jan Tore Sanner was born in [[Bærum]] as a son of biochemist Tore Sanner and local politician Nina Sanner. He grew up at [[Nadderud]] and finished his secondary education at [[Nadderud Upper Secondary School]] in 1984. Sanner has education in [[marketing]] and [[market economy]].<ref name=bio>{{stortingetbio|JTS}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Litt (fest) og integrering hver dag|last=Mejlbo|first=Kaja|date=20 January 2018|page=Budstikka}}</ref> He has never worked in private sector.

==Political career==
On the local level he was a deputy member of Bærum [[municipal council (Norway)|municipal council]] from 1983 to 1989. He was elected to the [[Storting|Norwegian Parliament]] from [[Akershus]] in 1993. He had previously served as a deputy representative during the term 1989&ndash;1993. From 1989 to 1990 he moved up as a regular representative, filling in for [[Kaci Kullmann Five]] who was appointed to the [[cabinet Syse]].<ref name=bio/> Sanner and Syse went to the same school and were both leaders of the Young Conservatives.

In the [[Norwegian Young Conservatives]], the youth wing of the Conservative Party, he chaired the local chapter from 1985 to 1986, the regional chapter from 1987 to 1988, became deputy leader nationwide in 1988 and leader from 1990 to 1994. In the Conservative Party he was a member of the central party board from 1988 to 1994, served as first deputy leader of the regional branch from 2001 to 2005. He was second deputy leader of the nationwide party from 2004 to 2008, and rose to first deputy leader in 2008.<ref name=bio/>

As a result of the parliamentary election in the fall of 2013, Sanner holds the position as Minister of Local Government and Modernisation in the coalition government led by Prime Minister [[Erna Solberg]]. In 2018 he became Minister of Education in a cabinet reshuffle.<ref name=bio/>
