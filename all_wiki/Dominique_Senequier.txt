'''Dominique Senequier''' (born 1953) is a French businesswoman.  She is president of [[Ardian (company)|Ardian]] (formerly Axa Private Equity), a [[private equity]] firm she founded in 1996.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ardian-investment.com/en/team|title=website of Ardian, "Management"|author=|date=|website=ardian-investment.com|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

==Early life and education==

Dominique Senequier attended high school at Lycée Thiers in [[Marseille]]. She holds a postgraduate degree in Banking and Monetary Economics from the [[University of Paris|University of Sorbonne]].

She was one of the first seven women admitted to the [[École Polytechnique]] in 1972, the year of its first female intake.<ref name="Forbes" /><ref name="Wharton">Knowledge@Wharton: ''[http://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article.cfm?articleid=2976 Axa's Dominque Senequier]'', published 6 April 2012, retrieved 18 August 2012.</ref>

==Career==

Dominique Senequier began her career as [[insurance commissioner]] for the [[French Ministry of Finance]], where she worked from 1975 until 1980.

She worked in [[reinsurance]], international development, and private equity at GAN, a subsidiary of [[Groupama]] from 1980 to 1996. She also created and developed the subsidiary GAN Participations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.europeanpwn.net/index.php?article_id=1186|title=Home|author=|date=|website=www.europeanpwn.net|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

In 1996, she joined the [[Axa|Axa Group]] and founded [[Axa Private Equity]], which became the largest private equity company in Europe with $50 billion of assets under management in 2014.<ref>[https://www.altassets.net/private-equity-news/by-news-type/firm-news/ardian-celebrates-first-year-of-independence-by-hitting-50bn-aum.html Alt Assets, Ardian celebrates first year of independence by hitting $50bn AUM, 2 October 2014]</ref> In 2013, she announced that the firm was separating from Axa Group; it became [[Ardian (company)|Ardian]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pehub.com/2013/09/axa-private-equity-completes-spin-out-now-renamed-ardian/|title=Axa Private Equity completes spin-out, now renamed Ardian - PE Hub|author=|date=30 September 2013|website=pehub.com|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

Senequier has more than 410 employees running more than 50 active funds in 12 offices around the world.<ref name="businessweek">Business week: ''[http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=99862 Dominique Senequier]'', retrieved 18 August 2012.</ref><ref name="bloomberg">Hymowitz, Carol: ''[https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2011-09-22/hewlett-packard-director-senequier-will-depart-amid-turmoil-over-strategy.html Hewlett-Packard Director Senequier Will Depart Amid Turmoil Over Strategy]'', Bloomberg, published 22 September 2011, retrieved 18 August 2012.</ref><ref name="businessweek2">Chassany, Anne-Sylvaine:  ''[http://www.businessweek.com/magazine/content/10_29/b4187042974799.htm Axa's Senequier Prepares to Go Shopping]'', Business Week, published 8 July 2010, retrieved 19 August 2012.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Ardian opens second U.S. office in San Francisco|url = https://www.pehub.com/2015/10/ardian-opens-second-u-s-office-in-san-francisco/|website = www.pehub.com|accessdate = 2015-12-03}}</ref>

She is a former director of [[Hewlett-Packard]], having resigned in March 2012, and a former non-executive member of the board of directors of the Italian group [[Compagnie Industriali Riunite]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cirgroup.com/governance/board-of-directors/board-of-directors/biography/dominique-senequier.html|title=CIR Compagnie Industriali Riunite: Board of Directors|author=|date=|website=www.cirgroup.com|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

She is vice-president of the [[Supervisory Board]] of [[Hermès]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://finance.hermes.com/en/Corporate-Governance/Management-Bodies/The-Supervisory-Board|title=The Supervisory Board - Management Bodies - Corporate Governance - Hermès financial information|author=|date=|website=finance.hermes.com|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

==Influence==
Senequier was ranked number 50 on [[Forbes list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women]] in 2009. In 2011, she was number 98. She ranked 16th in 2013, and 12th in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fortune.com/2014/09/18/most-powerful-women-emea/|title=The Most Powerful Women of Europe, the Middle East and Africa|author=|date=|website=fortune.com|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

In 2012 she was made a ''chevalier'' (knight) of the [[Legion of Honour]].<ref name="Forbes">[[Forbes]]: ''[https://www.forbes.com/profile/dominique-senequier/ Dominique Senequier]'', retrieved 18 August 2012.</ref><ref name="Forbes2">[[Forbes]]: ''[https://www.forbes.com/lists/2009/11/power-women-09_Dominique-Senequier_YKD0.html Power Women 2009: Dominique Senequier]'', retrieved 18 August 2012.</ref>

She is a member of the [[International Actuarial Association]].

In October 2013, she was named in the [[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]] Top 50 Most Influential People in the world in Money Managers category.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2013-09-03/most-influential-50-s-new-names-show-shakeup-bloomberg-markets.html|title=Bloomberg, Most Influential 50s New Names Show Shakeup in Finance, 4 September 2013|author=|date=|website=bloomberg.com|accessdate=16 August 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==

She is a concert-standard pianist who enjoys opera, sometimes travelling to Venice and Salzburg to hear it.<ref name="businessweek2" /><ref name="efinancial">Hodkinson, Paul: ''[http://www.efinancialnews.com/story/2010-09-06/axa-chief-dominique-senequier-talks-tough Axa Chief Dominique Senequier talks tough]'', efinancial news, published 6 September 2010, retrieved 18 August 2012.</ref>
