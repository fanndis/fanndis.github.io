'''Søren Pind''' (born 20 November 1969)<ref name=kraks>{{cite encyclopedia | title = Pind, Søren | encyclopedia = [[Kraks Blå Bog]] | year = 2005 | page = 946 | isbn = 87-7225-861-6}}</ref> is a Danish lawyer and politician, a member of the center-right party [[Venstre (Denmark)|Venstre]]. He served as [[Denmark|Danish]] [[Justice Minister of Denmark|Minister of Justice]] from 2015 to 2016, and has served as [[Government of Denmark|Minister of Science, Technology, Information and Higher Education]] from 2016 to May 2018, where he resigned because he was tired of politics.

Pind was born in [[Herning]]. He served as [[councillor]] in the [[Copenhagen Municipality|Copenhagen City Council]] from 1994 to 2005,<ref name=kraks /> and has taught at the [[University of Copenhagen]].

Pind is considered more liberal than most other members of his party. In 2003, he was part of a group of party members who issued the "ten liberal theses", a document unsuccessfully challenging the direction the party was taking under Prime Minister [[Anders Fogh Rasmussen]].<ref>{{cite news | title = Pind trækker i land | work = Politiken | date = 7 August 2003 | url = http://politiken.dk/indland/ECE65008/pind-traekker-i-land/}}</ref>

He was appointed to the [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen I Cabinet]] during a cabinet reshuffle in February 2010, becoming [[Minister for Development Cooperation of Denmark|Minister for Development Cooperation]].<ref>{{cite news | title = Søren Pind bliver minister for udvikling og bistand | work = Politiken | date = 22 February 2010 | url = http://politiken.dk/politik/ECE908151/soeren-pind-bliver-minister-for-udvikling-og-bistand/}}</ref> He was appointed [[Minister for Refugees, Immigrants and Integration (Denmark)|Minister for Refugees, Immigrants and Integration]] on 8 March 2011, thus holding double ministerial office.<ref>{{cite news | title = Søren Pind bliver ny integrationsminister | work = [[Politiken]] | date = 8 March 2011 | url = http://politiken.dk/politik/article1216859.ece}}</ref>

Pind's first term as minister ended on October 3. 2011, after the center-left coalition prevailed in the [[2011 Danish parliamentary election|2011 parliamentary election]].

On 28 June 2015 he was appointed as Minister of Justice in the [[Cabinet of Lars Løkke Rasmussen II]].

On 28 November 2016 he was appointed as Minister of Science, Technology, Information and Higher Education in Prime Minister [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen III Cabinet|Lars Løkke Rasmussen's Cabinet III.]]
