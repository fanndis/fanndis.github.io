'''State University of New York at Purchase''' (commonly '''Purchase College''' or '''SUNY Purchase''') is a public college in [[Purchase, New York]]. It is one of 13 comprehensive colleges in the [[State University of New York]] (SUNY) system. It was founded by Governor [[Nelson Rockefeller]] in 1967 as "the cultural gem of the SUNY system."

Purchase College confers the following degrees: [[Bachelor of Arts]] (BA), [[Bachelor of Science]] (BS), [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] (BFA), [[Bachelor of Music]] (MusB), [[Master of Arts]] (MA), [[Master of Fine Arts]] (MFA), and the [[Master of Music]] (MM).<ref name=fastfacts>{{cite web |title=Fast Facts|publisher=Purchase College|url=https://www.purchase.edu/about/facts-and-stats/ | website= purchase.edu| access-date= }}</ref> As a requirement for the BA and BS degree, students undertake a senior project in which they devote two semesters to an in-depth, original, and creative study under the close supervision of a faculty mentor. Similarly, the BFA and MusB studies culminate in a senior exhibition, film, or recital.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.purchase.edu/academics/the-undergraduate-experience/senior-project/|title=Senior Project|publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu | access-date= }}</ref> Master's degree programs culminate in a thesis and the MFA and MM culminate in an exhibition, recital, or related presentation.

==History==
[[File:The original farm, Purchase College.jpg|200px|right|thumb|Purchase College was originally a 500-acre estate and working farm in the 18th century.]]
The land that would become Purchase College was first settled by the Thomas family in 1734.<ref name=campushistory>{{cite web |title= Campus history to 1900|publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/PurchaseHistory/CampusHistory1900.aspx|accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref> John Thomas served as an assemblyman in [[Province of New York|colonial New York]] from 1743 to 1776. He served as a judge for the Court of Common Pleas in Westchester and a Muster-Master. Judge Thomas was an early supporter of American independence. Robert Bolton wrote in ''History of Westchester County'' that Thomas was "a warm Whig" who gave the first public reading of the [[Declaration of Independence]] in New York at the White Plains courthouse on July 11, 1776.<ref name=campushistory /> On March 22, 1777, Thomas was imprisoned by the British and died on May 2, 1777.<ref name=campushistory />

John Thomas' sons, John Thomas, Jr. and Thomas Thomas, also fought for American independence.<ref name=campushistory /> Thomas Thomas was later appointed a General. He is buried at the Thomas family graveyard, which is located behind the [[Neuberger Museum of Art]] on the campus of Purchase College.<ref name=campushistory /> A tall, white stone obelisk commemorates General Thomas and his family.<ref name=campushistory />

In 2019, Thomas J. Schwarz announced that he was stepping down from his role as President after 18 years of service.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.westchestermagazine.com/Blogs/914INC-Incoming/June-2019/Purchase-College-President-Thomas-Schwarz/|title=The Exit Interview|publisher=Westchester Magazine}}</ref> State University of New York Board of Trustees has appointed Dennis Craig as interim president of Purchase College effective on August 1, 2019.<ref name="College Interim President">{{cite web|url=https://www.suny.edu/suny-news/press-releases/04-2019/4-30-19-purchase/purchase-interim-president.html|title=College Interim President|publisher=SUNY}}</ref>

==Academics==
[[File:Summer orientation, Purchase College, 2013.jpg|220px|left|thumb|Summer orientation 2013]]
As of 2017, Purchase College had 4,121 undergraduate students with freshman enrollment of 763.<ref name=usnewsoverview /><ref name=princetonoverview>{{cite web |title=State University of New York--Purchase College|publisher=[[The Princeton Review]]|url=https://www.princetonreview.com/schools/1024042/college/state-university-new-york--purchase-college#!studentbody}}</ref><ref name=fastfacts/> 57% of Purchase's student body is female. 15% of the college's students come from outside of New York state and 2.4% of its students are international.<ref name=princetonoverview /> Purchase has an acceptance rate of 44% and a student-teacher ratio of 14:1.<ref name=kiplingeroverview>{{cite web |title=Best Values in Colleges Purchase College|date=December 2015|publisher=[[Kiplinger]]|url=https://www.kiplinger.com/tool/college/T014-S001-find-best-colleges-value-rankings/end_page.php?school=8844|accessdate=February 24, 2016}}</ref> 62% of Purchase students receive need-based financial aid and the college has an endowment of $61.1 million.<ref name=usnewsoverview />

Purchase College offers majors from three schools: the School for Liberal Arts and Sciences, the School of the Arts, and the School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education.<ref name=areasofstudy>{{cite web |title=Areas of Study|publisher=Purchase College|url=https://www.purchase.edu/academics/}}</ref> According to U.S. News & World Report, the five most popular majors for 2016 graduates at Purchase College were Visual and Performing Arts (48%); General Studies and Humanities (16%); Social Sciences (8%); Psychology (6%); and Communication, Journalism and related programs (5%).<ref name=popularmajors>{{cite web |title=Purchase College -- SUNY Academic Life|publisher=[[US News and World Report]]|url=https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/suny-purchase-6791/academics}}</ref>

===Conservatory of Dance===
[[File:Inside the Dance Building, Purchase College.jpg|220px|right|thumb|Inside the dance building]]
The Conservatory of Dance houses both bachelor's and master's programs.<ref name=dance>{{cite web |title=Arts - Dance |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/Arts/Dance/|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> It is one of the most highly regarded conservatories of dance in the United States.<ref name=nytmespdc>{{cite news |title=Purchase Dance Company|work= [[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/events/dance/purchase-dance-company-12453.html|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> Undergraduates may major in [[contemporary ballet|modern]] or performance [[ballet]], and [[Choreography (dance)|dance composition]] and dance production.<ref name=dance /> The conservatory confers master's degrees in dance choreography and performance teaching.<ref name=dance /> The Conservatory of Dance is housed in the Purchase College Dance Building, which was the first facility constructed in the United States solely for the study and performance of dance.<ref name=nytimespdc2>{{cite news |title=From College to the World, by Way of Dance| first= Laura Joseph |last= Mogil|date=April 13, 2008|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/04/13/nyregion/nyregionspecial2/13dancewe.html?_r=0|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref>

It is also home to the Purchase Dance Company, the college's student dance company.<ref name=dancecompany>{{cite web |title= Dance - Dance Company|publisher=Purchase College |url= http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/Arts/Dance/DanceCompany.aspx|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> The Purchase Dance company presents ''[[The Nutcracker]]'' every December and a balanced repertory during the spring semester.<ref name= dancemagazine>{{cite news|title=Learning Curves: College Dance Companies| first= Leslie |last= Holleran|date=February 2014| work= Dance Magazine|url=http://www.dancemagazine.com/issues/February-2014/Learning-Curves-College-Dance-Companies| accessdate= August 24, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140912033902/http://dancemagazine.com/issues/February-2014/Learning-Curves-College-Dance-Companies|archive-date=September 12, 2014|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The dance company also tours throughout the United States and internationally during the college's summer break.<ref name=dancecompany /> Purchase College students must audition for inclusion in the dance company, and the cast for individual shows is based on the technical competencies of members of the company.<ref name=dancemagazine /> Students may earn college credit for their participation in the company.<ref name=dancemagazine />

===Conservatory of Music=== 

[[File:SUNY JazzOrch Performs at Lincoln Center.jpg|250px|right|thumb|Purchase Jazz Orchestra performs at Lincoln Center in New York City.]] 

Purchase College's Conservatory of Music houses the college's bachelor's and master's programs in music.<ref name= music>{{cite web |title=Music - Programs |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/departments/AcademicPrograms/Arts/music/programs.aspx| access-date= August 24, 2014}}</ref> Undergraduates may study [[classical music]] instrumentation with a concentration in one of several types of instruments; voice and opera; classical composition; [[jazz]]; studio composition; or studio production.<ref name=music /> The Conservatory of Music also offers master's programs in all of these areas, except studio production.<ref name=musicmm>{{cite web |title=Music - Graduate Programs |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/departments/AcademicPrograms/Arts/music/GraduatePrograms.aspx| access-date= August 24, 2014}}</ref> The enrollment in the conservatory is limited to 400 undergraduate and graduate students.<ref name=music /> It is one of the few conservatories in the United States that produces full opera productions predominately for undergraduates.<ref name= collegeguide>{{cite book |last=Everette |first=Carole J. |title=College Guide for Performing Arts Majors |publisher=Peterson's}}</ref> The conservatory's Music Building has two recital halls, 75 practice rooms, 80 [[Steinway & Sons]] pianos, and professional [[recording studios]]. 

The Purchase Opera, the school's student opera company, was founded in 1998 and has won nine first-place honors from the National Opera Association.<ref name=spring2012>{{cite web |title=Purchase College Magazine| date=Spring–Summer 2012 |publisher= Purchase College |url= http://issuu.com/suny.purchase.college/docs/purchase_college_spring_magazine_20| access-date=August 24, 2014}}</ref> During the 2012–13 season, the opera won first place in the [[National Opera Association]]'s Division II for its production of [[Die Fledermaus]] and second place in Division III for its production of ''[[Hansel and Gretel (opera)|Hansel and Gretel]]''.<ref name=noa>{{cite web |title=2012-2013 Video Competition Winners| website= NOA.org| publisher=National Opera Association| url= http://www.noa.org/competitions/opera-production/2012-2013-winners.html| access-date=August 24, 2014}}</ref> 

The Purchase Jazz Orchestra is a 17-piece big band composed of students from the conservatory's jazz studies program. Each year the orchestra performs at jazz venues such as [[Blue Note Jazz Club]] and Dizzy's in New York City.<ref name=bluenote>{{cite web |title=Blue Note New York Performance Schedule |website= bluenote.net |publisher=Blue Note| url= http://www.bluenote.net/newyork/schedule/moreinfo.cgi?id=10875| access-date=August 24, 2014}}</ref><ref name=dizzy>{{cite web |title=Purchase Jazz Orchestra| publisher= Dizzy's, Jazz at Lincoln Center | url = http://jalc.org/events/av-146235| website= jalc.org| accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref>

===Conservatory of Theatre Arts===

The Conservatory of Theatre Arts confers four undergraduate degrees: acting; playwriting and screenwriting; theatre design/technology; and theatre and performance.<ref name=theatredegrees>{{cite web |title=Arts - Theatre Arts |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/departments/AcademicPrograms/Arts/taf/default.aspx|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> It also offers a master's in theatre design/technology.<ref name=theatredegrees /> The conservatory is among the top theatre schools in the nation, according to the [[Princeton Review]].<ref name=princetheater>{{cite web |title=Best College Theater|publisher=Princeton Review|url=https://www.princetonreview.com/college-rankings?rankings=best-college-theater|accessdate=January 17, 2018}}</ref> The conservatory was ranked 20 in Hollywood Reporter's list of Best Drama Schools in 2014.<ref name=hollywoodreporter>{{cite news |title=The 25 Best Drama Schools in 2014|date=May 30, 2014| first= Tim | last= Appelo|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |url= http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/25-best-drama-schools-2014-706880|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> It has a total enrollment of around 400 students.<ref name=stagedirections>{{cite news |title=School Spotlight: The Sopranos of Theatre|publisher=Stage Direction|url=http://www.stage-directions.com/586-the-sopranos-of-theatre.html|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref>

The conservatory's training focuses on the needs and strengths of individual students, instead of a one-size-fits-all training approach.<ref name=newsday>{{cite news |title=Wesley Snipes, Edie Falco, Stanley Tucci among stars launched at Purchase College|date=May 16, 2013| first= Chris |last= Serico |work= [[Newsday]] |url=http://long-island.newsday.com/search/wesley-snipes-edie-falco-stanley-tucci-among-stars-launched-at-purchase-college-1.5274577|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> Students participate in showcases and exhibitions in New York City, Los Angeles, and on-campus at the school's [[blackbox theater]].<ref name=newsday /> Conservatory students can also work on Purchase Repertory Theatre productions. The theatre's productions are held at the Purchase Arts Center and are student-led shows that feature both acting and design/technology students.<ref name=stagedirections /> Notable acting faculty include [[Christopher McCann]].

The Broadway Technical Theatre History Project at Purchase College presents the annual "Backstage Legends and Masters Award" to distinguished professionals who represent a variety of Broadway production specialties.<ref name=backstage>{{cite web|title=PRG's Fred Gallo Receives the Third Annual 'Backstage Legends and Masters' Award: PRG|date=April 24, 2014|publisher=PRG|url=http://www.prg.com/about-us/news/prgs-fred-gallo-receives-third-annual-backstage-legends-masters-award/|accessdate=August 24, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140826115819/http://www.prg.com/about-us/news/prgs-fred-gallo-receives-third-annual-backstage-legends-masters-award/|archive-date=August 26, 2014|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

==School of Liberal Arts and Sciences==
Purchase College's School of Liberal Arts and Sciences houses the college's School of Film and Media Studies; School of Humanities; School of Natural and Social Sciences and interdisciplinary studies.<ref name=purchaselas>{{cite web |title=Academic Programs - LAS |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu | url= http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/las/|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> Students can choose from 23 separate majors or they can design an interdisciplinary major from several courses of study.<ref name=purchaselas />

The annual Durst Lecture Series, supported by an endowment from the Durst family, brings in celebrated writers to the campus. Past lecturers include authors Tim O'Brien, Hettie Jones, Claudia Rankine and Manohla Dargis.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://patch.com/new-york/harrison/purchase-college-announces-poet-claudia-rankine-roy-shirley-durst-distinguished | title = Purchase College Announces Poet Claudia Rankine as Roy and Shirley Durst Distinguished Chair in Literature | publisher = Harrison Patch | date = 9 September 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.crainsnewyork.com/events-calendar/details/4/3388097 | title = Purchase College Presents NY Times Film Critic Manohla Dargis and Filmmaker Arthur Jafa at Schomburg Center | publisher = Crain's New York Business | date = 28 February 2016 }}</ref>

===School of Humanities===
The School of Humanities houses the college's undergraduate programs in [[art history]]; [[creative writing]]; history; [[journalism]]; language and culture; [[literature]]; and [[philosophy]].<ref name=humanities>{{cite web |title=LAS - Humanities |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/LAS/Humanities/|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> It also offers a master's in art history.<ref name=humanities />
[[File:Claudiarankine.png|thumb|Poet Claudia Rankine, whose work has been featured in Harper's and The Kenyon Review, speaks to audience members after a lecture.]]
In addition to its curriculum, the School of Humanities frequently features renowned authors and scholars who provide lectures, known as the Durst Lecture Series. These lectures are supported by the Roy and Shirley Durst Distinguished Chair in Literature.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.purchase.edu/academics/school-of-humanities/lectures/durst-distinguished-lectures/|title=Durst Distinguished Lectures |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |language=en|access-date=2018-02-07}}</ref> Past lecturers include Rachel Kushner, Claudia Rankine, Kirstin Valdez Quade, and Alexander Chee. Lectures are open to the public and provide an open forum for student feedback and interaction.

===School of Natural and Social Sciences===
[[File:Gloved student in lab (Purchase College).jpg|220px|right|thumb|Purchase College Student in lab facility of the Natural Sciences Building.]]
Purchase's School of Natural and Social Sciences houses the college's undergraduate programs in [[anthropology]]; [[biochemistry]]; [[biology]]; [[chemistry]]; [[economics]]; [[environmental studies]]; [[mathematics]]/[[computer science]]; [[political science]]; [[psychology]]; and [[sociology]].<ref name=sciences>{{cite web |title=LAS - School of Natural & Social Sciences |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/LAS/Sciences/|accessdate=August 24, 2014}}</ref> The school also presents an annual Natural and Social Sciences Symposium, which exhibits original research conducted by students; and a lecture series funded by Con Edison.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://patch.com/new-york/harrison/calendar/event/20160913/20895/science-in-the-modern-world-paul-thibodeau | title = Science in the Modern World: Paul Thibodeau | publisher = Harrison Patch | date = 13 September 2016 }}</ref>

===Interdisciplinary Studies===
The School of Liberal Arts and Sciences offers the Liberal Arts Individualized Program of Study (informally called the Bachelor of Arts in Liberal Arts),<ref name=spring2013 /> which is open to students who want to pursue an individualized course of study that is not accommodated by an existing major.<ref name=spring2013 /> Students work with two faculty members representing their study disciplines to create an individualized curriculum.<ref name=spring2013 /> It also encompasses undergraduate programs in [[gender studies]], [[Asian studies]], and [[Latin American studies]].<ref name= interdisciplinary>{{cite news|title= LAS - Interdisciplinary Studies| website= purchase.edu| publisher= Purchase College |url= http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/LAS/Interdisciplinary/|accessdate=August 24, 2014|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20141006132939/http://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/LAS/Interdisciplinary/|archive-date=October 6, 2014|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

==School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education==
The School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education at Purchase College allows community residents and students to complete their bachelor's degree and to take both credit and noncredit courses at the college.<ref name=continuinged>{{cite web|title=School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu| url= http://www.purchase.edu/sharedmedia/coursecatalog/liberal%20studies%20and%20continuing%20education.pdf|accessdate=August 24, 2014 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20150924083400/http://www.purchase.edu/sharedmedia/coursecatalog/liberal%20studies%20and%20continuing%20education.pdf |archive-date= September 24, 2015|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The school confers the bachelor's degree in [[liberal arts education|liberal studies]], which is designed for students with some undergraduate credit who want complete their degree within a tight time-frame and are looking for a flexible schedule. Up to 90 transfer credits are accepted in this program.<ref name=continuinged /> It also offers [[continuing education]] and certificate programs; an online winter session; and the college's summer session.<ref name=continuinged />

=== Noncredit Professional Certificate Programs ===
The School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education (LSCE) offers noncredit professional certificate courses in [[Real estate appraisal|appraisal studies]] (summer only), [[arts management]], [[drawing]] and [[painting]], [[geographic information system]]s (GIS) (fall and spring only), [[home staging]], [[interior design]] (fall and spring only), [[Museology|museum studies]] (fall and spring only), and [[social media marketing]] (fall and spring only). Students may take individual courses without commitment to an entire program, or complete the program requirements and earn a certificate.

The School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education also partners with specific online providers to increase the flexibility and breadth of certificate offerings. Students can take online courses in [[Nonprofit Management|nonprofit management]], [[Legal management|paralegal studies]], and receive a CEU certificate upon completion of the health coach training program at the [[Institute for Integrative Nutrition]] (IIN).<ref>{{Cite web|title = Purchase College - Academic Programs - CE - Professional Certificate Programs|url = https://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/CE/Certificate/ |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu|access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref>

=== Noncredit Personal Enrichment Courses ===
The School of Liberal Studies & Continuing Education offers noncredit personal enrichment courses that are open to the general public and allow participants to explore personal interests. Students may take courses in [[woodworking]], [[Woodturning|wood turning]], [[Tattoo|tattoo illustration]], [[photography]], [[creative writing]], and [[filmmaking]], with courses in other programs offered throughout the year. The personal enrichment program also offers students who are not enrolled in a degree program at Purchase College the opportunity to take selected undergraduate credit courses on a noncredit basis at a lower noncredit tuition rate. Additionally, students may take individual courses in any of the noncredit professional certificate programs without making a commitment to the entire program.<ref>{{Cite web|title = General Information - Noncredit Personal Enrichment Courses|url = https://www.purchase.edu/departments/AcademicPrograms/ce/GeneralInformation/NoncreditGeneralInfo.aspx |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref>

=== Youth and Precollege Programs ===
For more than 38 years, the School of Liberal Arts & Continuing Education has offered the Summer Youth and Precollege Program in the Arts to provide precollege students a forum to experience enriching learning opportunities. Courses are offered in areas such as [[Songwriter|songwriting]], [[acting]], [[architecture]], [[visual arts]], [[creative writing]], [[filmmaking]], [[video game]] and app creation, voice, [[fashion]], [[musical theatre]], and more. Programs are offered in two- and four-week sessions over a six-week period, with full-day and commuter options.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Purchase College - Academic Programs - Continuing Education - Youth & Precollege Programs|url = https://www.purchase.edu/Departments/AcademicPrograms/CE/Youth/ |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref>

==Student life==

===Purchase Student Government Association===
The Purchase Student Government Association (PSGA) is a [[nonprofit corporation]] responsible for managing the money collected from [[Purchase College]] students' [[Student fee|Mandatory Student Activity Fee]]. The PSGA is divided into three branches: executive, legislative, and judicial. These three branches are subdivided into six bodies: the Executive Board, the Senate, the Judicial Board, the Council of Clubs & Organizations, the Student Activities Board, and Services Board. In addition to advocating on the student body's behalf, the PSGA runs the college's [[Student Center]], and most non-academic activities on campus, including numerous student-run services, and all clubs and organizations.<ref name=psga>{{cite web|title=Student Handbook - PSGA |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url= http://www.purchase.edu/CampusLifeAndAthletics/StudentHandbook/psga.aspx|accessdate=1 September 2014|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20131022014927/http://www.purchase.edu/CampusLifeAndAthletics/StudentHandbook/psga.aspx|archive-date= October 22, 2013 |url-status= dead |df= mdy-all}}</ref>

=== Events ===
In addition to its clubs, Purchase College holds several events throughout the year, accommodating the diverse musical and artistic interests of its student body. Most notably, these events include:
* Culture Shock: Culture Shock is an annual two-day music and carnival festival sponsored by the PSGA. Typically held in April, the weekend festival has featured dozens of renowned performers, including alumni students who have recently graduated from Purchase's Music Conservatory. Some notable performers include: [[Iggy Azalea]], [[Flatbush Zombies]], [[MF DOOM]], [[Lil B]], [[Ween]], [[Jay Electronica]], SZA, [[Dan Deacon]], [[Regina Spektor]], [[Deerhoof]], [[Pissed Jeans]], [[Animal Collective]], [[GZA]], [[Cat Power]], [[Blonde Redhead]], [[Bouncing Souls]], [[Ghostface Killah]], [[Ted Leo]], [[Biz Markie]], [[Kool Keith]], [[Slick Rick]], [[Destiny's Child]], Solange, [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]], [[Tycho (musician)|Tycho]], [[Beach Fossils]], [[Dead Prez]], The Front Bottoms, and [[Big Freedia]].
* Fall Fest: An "appetizer" for Culture Shock, Fall Fest is the first of the two major music festivals at Purchase College, typically held in October. Like Culture Shock, Fall Fest features of number of bands and Purchase Music Conservatory Alumni.
* Zombie Prom: Zombie Prom is a [[prom]]-type event held annually in the spring that features live music, a DJ, and of course - students dressed up as zombies.[[File:ZombieProm.jpg|thumb|Purchase College hosts an annual "Zombie Prom" for its students where they can dance, jam to some music, and dress up as zombies.]]
Purchase College does not officially recognize fraternities or sororities on its campus. However, upon request, the college will allow such organizations to use space on campus, as available, to the same extent it provides space to other student organizations. [[Sigma Alpha Mu]] has a chapter on campus, however, they're not recognized by SUNY Purchase. They disbanded in the Fall 2019 semester.

==Athletics==
SUNY Purchase teams, the '''Purchase Panthers''', participate as a member of the [[National Collegiate Athletic Association]]'s [[Division III (NCAA)|Division III]]. The Panthers are a member of the [[Skyline Conference]]. Men's sports include baseball, basketball, cross country, golf, soccer, swimming & diving, tennis and volleyball. Women's sports include basketball, cross country, lacrosse, soccer, softball, swimming & diving, tennis and volleyball.

The Purchase College Athletic Department also houses non-varsity and intramural teams and clubs. Intramural teams include [[basketball]], [[flag football]], [[floor hockey]], [[indoor soccer]], racquet sports, co-ed [[softball]], Water Polo, Quidditch and [[volleyball]].<ref name=intramuralteams>{{cite web |title=Purchase Panthers - Intramurals|publisher=Purchase College|url=http://www.purchasecollegeathletics.com/sports/2013/11/21/GEN_1121131455.aspx|accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref> Intramural clubs include Men's Lacrosse, [[fencing]] club, [[stage combat]], [[Tae Kwan Do]], [[Ultimate Frisbee]] club, [[Nerf]] club, Outdoors Adventure, [[field hockey]], [[Chung Do Kwan]], [[equestrianism|equestrian]], [[Zumba]] and [[PiYo]].<ref name=intramuralclubs>{{cite web |title=Purchase Panthers - Club Sports|publisher=Purchase College| url=http://www.purchasecollegeathletics.com/sports/2013/11/21/GEN_1121131326.aspx|accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref>

In 2014, SUNY Purchase Men's Soccer Program won its first Skyline Conference Championship, defeating St. Joe's (LI) in overtime by the score of 2–1.

==Campus==
[[File:Outback-Fortawesome.jpg|thumb|A side view of residence halls Outback and Fort Awesome (far left) from the parking lot of Purchase College.]]
[[File:SUNY Purchase College.jpg|thumb|Photograph of Student Services at Purchase College]]
Purchase College is located on approximately {{cvt|500|acre|km2}} in [[Westchester County, New York]] on the former Strathglass farm.<ref name=nytimesexperiment>{{cite news |title=Purchase College, the 25-Year-Old Experiment| first= Roberta |last= Hershenson |date=September 22, 1996|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1996/09/22/nyregion/purchase-college-the-25-year-old-experiment.html?src=pm&pagewanted=1|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> The property was originally owned by Thomas Thomas, an [[American Revolutionary war]] soldier, whose family-and-servant cemetery remains on the campus between the south end of the Humanities and Visual Arts buildings.<ref name=purchasehistory>{{cite web |title=Purchase College History |publisher= SUNY Purchase| website= purchase.edu |url=http://www.purchase.edu/AboutPurchase/Hi story.aspx|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> The college is adjacent to the [[Westchester County Airport]], and is across the street from [[PepsiCo]]'s corporate headquarters.

==Architecture==
The college's master architectural plan was created by architect [[Edward Larrabee Barnes]] and reflected the belief that "[[modern architecture]] might be able to reshape the world."<ref name=nytimearchitecture>{{cite news |title= Architecture: SUNY Purchase Campus Reflects Design Innocence of the 60's |date=March 2, 1981| first= Paul | last= Goldberger| work= The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1981/03/02/arts/architecture-suny-purchase-campus-reflects-design-innocence-of-the-60-s.html |accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> It has been described as a "period piece of the 1960s" and the architects who designed and built the campus include Philip Johnson and John Burgee, Paul Rudolph, Venturi & Rauch, Gwathmey Siegel & Henderson, The Architects Collaborative, Giovanni Pasanella, and Gunnar Birkerts.<ref name=nytimearchitecture /> The campus' original buildings were placed close together to allow the surrounding fields to remain open.<ref name=nytimearchitecture /> The college grounds are also home to many sculptures.<ref name=visitorsguide/>

==Campus sustainability==
The college consistently ranks as one of the top sustainable colleges in the nation having ranked 72nd by the [[Sierra Club]]'s America's [[Environmentally friendly|greenest]] colleges. This ranking factors in the college's energy use, waste, water, food, and purchasing policies.<ref name=sierraclub>{{cite web |title=Complete rankings|publisher=[[Sierra Club]]| website= sierraclub.org| url= http://vault.sierraclub.org/sierra/201209/coolschools/complete-rankings-cool-schools.aspx|accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref> The college is also included in the 2014 Princeton Review Guide to Green Colleges.<ref name= greencolleges>{{cite web |title=Green Guide Colleges (Full List)|publisher=[[The Princeton Review]]| website= princetonreview.com| url= http://www.princetonreview.com/green-schools-full-list.aspx |accessdate=1 September 2014}}</ref>

In 2014, the college unveiled the "Rocket" composting system, which has the capacity to handle 460 gallons of [[food waste]] every week.<ref name=rocket>{{cite news |title=Purchase College To Introduce Heavy Duty Food-Waste Composter| first= Tom | last= Renner |date= February 3, 2014|url= http://rye.dailyvoice.com/schools/purchase-college-introduce-heavy-duty-food-waste-composter| website= Rye [[The Daily Voice (American hyperlocal news)|Daily Voice]] | via= rye.dailyvoice.com| publisher= | accessdate= 1 September 2014}}</ref>

==Performing Arts Center==

Situated on the campus is the college's Performing Arts Center. It is a four-theatre complex that is the largest performing arts center in the SUNY system.<ref name=artscenter>{{cite web |title=About the Center - The Performing Arts Center, Purchase College |publisher=Purchase College|url=http://www.artscenter.org/about-the-center/|accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref> The center's performance spaces include the 1400-seat, three-tiered Concert Hall with hydraulic lifts for orchestra; the 600-seat Recital Hall with rear-screen projection bay; the 700-seat PepsiCo Theatre designed by Ming Cho Lee; and the Repertory Theatre, a "black box" with flexible stage and seating configurations.<ref name=artscenterthreatres>{{cite web |title=Theatres - The Performing Arts Center, Purchase College|publisher=Purchase College|url=http://www.artscenter.org/facility-rentals/theatres/#recital| accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref> Each theatre is specifically designed for the presentation of a different type of performance and many types of events.

The Performing Arts Center presents a broad range of performances – offering music, dance, theatre, comedy, and cinema. The Performing Arts center is also home to Conservatory of Theatre Arts' Purchase Repertory Theatre. The Center's ongoing initiatives include artist partnerships, residency activities, and commissions.

==Neuberger Museum of Art==
[[File:Staircase at Neuberger Museum, Purchase College.jpg|thumb|A look inside Purchase College's Neuberger Museum, revamped in 2012.]]
The college also houses the [[Neuberger Museum of Art]], which is among the ten largest museums in New York and the eighth-largest university museum in the nation.<ref name=visitorsguide/> The museum opened in 1972.<ref name=nytimesexperiment/> It holds a permanent collection of more than 7,000 works of art<ref name=museum>{{cite web |title=About Purchase - Neuberger Museum of Art|publisher=Purchase College|url=http://www.purchase.edu/AboutPurchase/NeubergerMuseumOfArt.aspx|accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref> and features a full schedule of exhibitions, lectures, films, and multimedia events. The museum presents more than a dozen exhibitions each year in addition to ongoing exhibitions from its permanent collections. The Neuberger Museum of Art has works from 20th-century masters, midcareer and emerging artists, and is well known for its permanent exhibition of [[African art]].<ref name="ny timesafrican">{{cite news |title=Neuberger Museum Gets a New Director| first= Felicia R.| last= Lee|date=April 25, 2012| work= The New York Times |url=http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/04/25/neuberger-museum-gets-a-new-director/ |accessdate=August 16, 2014}}</ref>
