'''Bonnie Hammer''' (born 1950) is an American network and studio executive. {{Asof|2019}}, her title within the [[Comcast]] hierarchy is ''Chairman, NBCUniversal Content Studios'', overseeing [[Universal Content Productions]], [[Universal Television]] and NBCUniversal International Studios.

== Early life ==
Born to a [[American Jews|Jewish]] family<ref>{{cite news|author=Ruth Eglash|title=Jewish women who rule! (according to Forbes)|url=http://www.jpost.com/International/Jewish-women-who-rule-according-to-Forbes|accessdate=10 September 2013|newspaper=[[Jpost]]|date=August 23, 2012}}</ref><ref name=VarietyADL>[https://variety.com/2008/tv/news/bonnie-hammer-r-20277/# Variety: "Bonnie Hammer reins in the rhetoric for the ADL"] June 3, 2008</ref> in 1950,<ref name="forbes.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/bonnie-hammer/ |title=Bonnie Hammer |work=Forbes |date=1970-01-01 |accessdate=2015-09-18}}</ref> Hammer was raised in Queens, New York, the youngest of three children. Hammer's mother was a full-time mom; her dad, a Russian immigrant, started his own pen company. Intending to become a photojournalist, Hammer enrolled at [[Boston University College of Communication]], earning a bachelor's degree in communications in 1971 and later a master's degree in Media Technology from the [[Boston University School of Education]] in 1975.

==Career==
Hammer began her career in television at [[WGBH-TV]], the public television station in Boston, where she produced ''[[This Old House]]'', Infinity Factory and ''[[ZOOM (1972 TV series)|ZOOM]]'' for PBS. She later executive-produced ''Good Day!'' for [[Boston]]'s [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] affiliate, [[WCVB-TV]].

Hammer first established herself professionally in [[New York City|New York]] as an original programming executive at [[Lifetime Television]] Network, where she executive produced several award-winning documentaries for the network's acclaimed Signature Series; she was honored with the [[The Dorothy and Lillian Gish Prize|Lillian Gish Award]], several [[CINE Golden Eagle|Cine Golden Eagles]]<ref>{{cite web|title=CINE|url=http://www.cine.org/}}</ref> and the National Association for Youth's Mentor Award.

In 1989 Hammer joined [[Universal Television]] as a programming executive. Here, Hammer partnered with [[Vince McMahon]] to transform the [[WWE|WWF]] franchise into a cultural phenomenon.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Carter |first1=Bill |title=The Queen of Cable TV |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/12/19/business/media/bonnie-hammer-the-woman-who-saves-cable-networks.html |website=The New York Times |accessdate=9 April 2020}}</ref>  Hammer also spearheaded the launch of the successful “Sci Fi Prime,” the channel's first full night of original programming, as well as the “I am Sci-Fi” messaging effort which redefined how viewers related to the channel and its programming.{{Citation needed|date=January 2016}}

When Hammer took on the role of Sci-Fi president,<ref name="books.google.com">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=mTEOrAa2g5EC&q=bonnie+hammer&pg=PA274 |title=Vault Guide to the Top Media & Entertainment Employers - Laurie Pasiuk - Google Books |date= September 2005|isbn=9781581313376 |accessdate=2015-09-18|last1=Pasiuk |first1=Laurie }}</ref> she brought to network a mini-series in partnership with Steven Spielberg called, ''[[Steven Spielberg Presents: Taken]]''. The project, a twenty-hour miniseries about alien abductions, garnered the best ratings Sci-Fi had seen to date and earned the channel its first major [[Emmy]]. During the six years Hammer presided over Sci-Fi, the channel's audience doubled and Sci-Fi ranked in cable's top 10 among adults 25 to 54 and 18 to 49.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news.aspx?date=12/17/08&id=20081217scifi01|title=Sci Fi Has Best year Ever! #5 in Adults 25 - 54 |publisher=The Futon Critic, Sci Fi Channel|date=December 17, 2008|accessdate=July 7, 2009 }}</ref>

In 2004 Universal Television merged with NBC and Hammer became president of [[USA Network|USA]] in addition to Sci-Fi.<ref name="books.google.com"/> As she had done at Sci-Fi, Hammer rebranded USA with “Characters Welcome,” – conveying the message that people, and “wacky but memorable characters” were at the heart of the channel's programming.<ref name=number3>{{cite web|title=She Made It: Bonnie Hammer|url=http://www.shemadeit.org/meet/biography.aspx?m=130|publisher=The Paley Center for Media}}</ref>  USA has ranked #1 among cable networks for a record-setting eight years. After her success at Sci-Fi and USA, Hammer was named the most influential woman in cable by ''CableWorld'' magazine.<ref name=number4>{{cite web|title=2007 Most Powerful Women in Cable |url=http://www.shemadeit.org/meet/biography.aspx?m=130|publisher=CableFAX Magazine}}</ref>

In March 2008, Hammer took leadership at the new studio [[Universal Cable Productions]]. At the same time she became head of the digital networks [[Cloo]], [[Chiller]] and [[Universal HD]]. Adding to the list in 2011, she assumed responsibility for [[E!]], [[G4 (U.S. TV channel)|G4]] and Wilshire Studios. In July 2012, under the leadership of Hammer, E! unveiled a major brand evolution of the network and relaunch of its market-leading E! Online website, both of which allowed E! to become the global destination for pop culture.<ref name=number5>{{cite web|title=Bonnie Hammer|url=http://www.nbcuni.com/corporate/management/senior-corporate-executives/nbc-universal/bonnie-hammer/|publisher=NBC Universal|access-date=2014-02-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131031101502/http://www.nbcuni.com/corporate/management/senior-corporate-executives/nbc-universal/bonnie-hammer/|archive-date=2013-10-31|url-status=dead}}</ref> In September 2013, she oversaw the launch of Esquire Network, a lifestyle and entertainment network that replaced Style Network (Variety).

In 2019, Hammer lead the creation of the management team of [[Peacock (streaming service)|Peacock]], a streaming service to be launched in July 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lynch |first1=Jason |title=NBCUniversal Will Roll Out an Ad-Supported OTT Service Next Year, Overseen by Bonnie Hammer |url=https://www.adweek.com/tv-video/nbcuniversal-will-roll-out-an-ad-supported-ott-service-next-year-overseen-by-bonnie-hammer/ |website=Adweek |accessdate=9 April 2020}}</ref>

Outside of NBCUniversal, Bonnie Hammer serves on the Board of Directors of eBay and IAC/InteractiveCorp. <ref>{{cite web |last1=Staff |first1=Ebay Inc. |title=NBCUniversal Executive Bonnie Hammer Joins Our Board |url=https://www.ebayinc.com/stories/news/nbcuniversal-executive-bonnie-hammer-joins-our-board/ |website=eBay |accessdate=9 April 2020}}</ref> <ref>{{cite web |last1=Lieberman |first1=David |title=Bonnie Hammer Re-Teams With Mentor Barry Diller On IAC Board |url=https://deadline.com/2014/09/bonnie-hammer-barry-diller-iac-board-834782/ |website=Deadline |accessdate=9 April 2020}}</ref> <ref>{{cite web |title=AFI Board of Trustees |url=https://www.afi.com/board-of-trustees/ |website=AFI: American Film Institute |accessdate=9 April 2020}}</ref>

==Social activism==

While at USA Network, Hammer spearheaded the “Erase the Hate” pro-social campaign, which earned a National Emmy Governor's Award.<ref name=number7>{{cite web|title=Bonnie Hammer and James Dolan Top List of NCTA's 2010 Vanguard Winners|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/bonnie-hammer-and-james-dolan-top-list-of-nctas-2010-vanguard-winners-89131857.html|publisher=The Cable Show via PR Newswire}}</ref> She later extended it to create “Characters Unite,” a public service program to combat hate and discrimination and promoting tolerance and acceptance. Under Hammer's leadership, Characters Unite has grown into an award-winning, multi-platform initiative that includes on-air programming such as documentaries and themed episodes of USA series, [[public service announcement]]s, digital content through the website and social media outlets, and community outreach and high school education programs, featuring a partnership with storytelling group [[The Moth]].<ref name=number5 /> In 2018, Erase The Hate<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.erasethehate.org/about|title=About {{!}} What Is Erase The Hate?|website=Erase the Hate|language=en|access-date=2019-05-21}}</ref> was relaunched in partnership with Civic Nation,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://civicnation.org/|title=Civic Nation! Empowering people to take action can change culture.|website=Civic Nation|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-21}}</ref> a non-partisan non-profit organization, to support and celebrate those taking action in the fight against hate across America.

Hammer also created the “Visions for Tomorrow” campaign, which is an effort to spark America's leading thinkers, organizations, and policymakers into searching for ways to solve society's most pressing issues.<ref name=number3 />
