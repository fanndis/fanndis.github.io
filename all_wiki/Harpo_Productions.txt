'''Harpo Productions''' (also referred to as '''Harpo Studios''') is an American [[multimedia]] [[Film production|production]] [[company (law)|company]] founded by [[Oprah Winfrey]] and based in [[West Hollywood, California]].<ref name="Promethe l Media" /><ref name="Tribune Publishing"/> It is the sole subsidiary of her media and entertainment company '''Harpo, Inc'''. The name "Harpo" is "Oprah" spelled backwards, and it was also the name of her on-screen husband in ''[[The Color Purple (film)|The Color Purple]]''.

Harpo Productions' subsidiaries consist of Harpo Print, the company's publishing house, and a minority interest in cable network [[Oprah Winfrey Network]] (OWN) — in 2017, [[Discovery Communications]] purchased 24.5% of OWN for $70 million,<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> leaving Harpo with 25.5% ownership. Harpo previously controlled 50% of OWN.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/oprah-winfrey-sells-part-of-stake-in-own-network-to-discovery-1512414120|title=Oprah Winfrey Sells Part of Stake in OWN Network to Discovery; Discovery will control 70% of OWN after purchase of stake for $70 million|last=Flint|first=Joe|date=December 4, 2017|work=The Wall Street Journal Online|access-date=December 5, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-04/oprah-winfrey-gets-70-million-as-discovery-boosts-stake-in-own|title=Discovery Boosts Its Stake in Oprah Winfrey Network|last=Smith|first=Gerry|date=December 4, 2017|work=Bloomberg|access-date=December 5, 2017}}</ref>

Past subsidiaries include Harpo Films, the company's film studio which shut down in early 2013, and [[Oprah Radio|Harpo Radio]], the company's radio broadcasting division that shuttered on January 1, 2015.<ref name=harporadio/> The company was previously based in [[Chicago]], with Harpo Studios situated in the West Loop of the [[Near West Side, Chicago|Near West Side]] neighborhood. The building was demolished in 2016, and new headquarters for [[McDonald's]] opened on the site in 2018.<ref>https://chicago.curbed.com/2016/8/2/12353448/west-loop-development-mcdonalds-harpo-studios-demolition</ref>

==Divisions==

===Oprah Winfrey Network (OWN)===
[[Image:OWN 2011 logo.svg|thumb|left|Oprah Winfrey Network (OWN) logo]]
On January 1, 2011, the [[Oprah Winfrey Network]] (OWN) launched.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/oprah-2011-1|title=Oprah's OWN Network Launch Draws Solid Reviews Even From The Cynics|work=Business Insider|access-date=2017-09-07|language=en}}</ref> It is co-owned by Harpo Studios (25.5%) and [[Discovery, Inc.|Discovery Communications]], Inc (74.5%).<ref name=":0" /> The network replaced the [[Discovery Health Channel]], a cable channel previously owned by Discovery Communications, Inc. The network initially had a variety of new shows, including some hosted by the stable of experts Winfrey has cultivated on her daytime talk show including: ''The [[Gayle King]] Show'', ''[[Our America with Lisa Ling]]'', ''In The Bedroom with [[Dr. Laura Berman]]'' and ''[[Enough Already! with Peter Walsh]]''.

===Harpo Print, LLC===
Together with Hearst Magazines, Harpo Print, LLC publishes ''[[O, The Oprah Magazine]]''.<ref name="uoiiaoo">[http://www.oprah.com/pressroom/Harpo-Studios-Announces-the-Formation-of-Harpo-Creative-Works "Harpo Studios Announces the Formation of Harpo Creative Works"], Oprah.com, June 22, 2010.</ref> The company also published ''O at Home'', which Hearst officially folded in 2008 after a four-year run.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/11/08/business/media/08hearst.html|title=Hearst to Close Its Spinoff of O, the Oprah Magazine|last=Pérez-Peña|first=Richard|date=2008-11-08|work=The New York Times}}</ref> In July 2020, it was announced that O, The Oprah Magazine will end its regular print edition after the December 2020 issue.<ref>https://apnews.com/de998fe469fcfb2c28aa89ee7445f050</ref><ref>https://www.chron.com/business/article/Oprah-s-O-Mag-to-end-regular-print-editions-15439762.php</ref>

Harpo Studios was the home of ''The Oprah Winfrey Show,'' from January 15, 1990.  located in [[Near West Side, Chicago|Near West Side]] neighborhood of Chicago. The set also housed ''[[The Rosie Show]]'', an American evening television talk show which was hosted and produced by actress and comedian Rosie O'Donnell, airing on the [[Oprah Winfrey Network]]. Harpo Studios has [[Emmy Award]]-winning teams in production [[designs]], [[lighting]], [[camera]] [[Camera operator|work]], [[editing]], [[Sound recording and reproduction|audio]] direction and [[graphic design]]. It also served as the location for the set of ''[[The Women of Brewster Place (1989 television)|The Women of Brewster Place]]''. Scenes from ''[[Beloved (1998 film)|Beloved]]'' were also filmed on a set in Harpo Studios.  The studio was sold to developer Sterling Bay in 2014 and was demolished in 2016.<ref>https://www.chicagotribune.com/business/ct-harpo-studios-demolition-20160726-story.html</ref>  The site would be home to the new global headquarters of [[McDonald's]].

The land where the production studio formerly sat once housed the 2nd Regiment Armory that was used as a makeshift morgue for victims of the capsizing of the steamer ''[[SS Eastland]]''.<ref>[http://www.oprah.com/article/oprahshow/tows_trivia "The Oprah Winfrey Show trivia"], Oprah.com. Retrieved 2008-07-28.</ref> The {{convert|88000|sqft|m2|adj=on}} facility was renovated and opened in the late 1980s for her show.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=950DE3DC1038F931A25750C0A96F948260|title=Oprah Winfrey's Odyssey: Talk-Show Host to Mogul|author=Colander, Pat|date=1989-03-12|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref>

===Harpo Films, Inc.===
Founded in 1993, Harpo Films, Inc. was the biggest division of Harpo Productions, run by [[Kate Forte]] for 18 years. It was an active supplier of [[motion pictures]], developing and producing award-winning features and [[Wikipedia:Reference desk/Archives/Entertainment/2010 September 17#Long-form television|long-form]] television programs, including the "''Oprah Winfrey Presents''" telefilms for the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC television network]]. Harpo Films was based in [[Los Angeles, California]].

===Harpo Radio, Inc.===
[[Image:Sm oprah.png|thumb|left|Former [[XM]] 156 / [[Sirius Satellite Radio|SR]] 195  Logo as Oprah and Friends]]
Harpo Radio, Inc. was the holding company for the [[Oprah Radio|Oprah & Friends]] channel (156 on [[XM Satellite Radio]]). Oprah & Friends featured a broad range of daily and weekly programming on a variety of topics including self-improvement, [[nutrition]], [[Physical fitness|fitness]], [[parenting]], [[health]], [[home]], [[finance]] and current events hosted by personalities from ''The Oprah Winfrey Show'' and ''[[O, The Oprah Magazine]]''.

Regular presenters included specialists from a variety of fields, including [[Maya Angelou|Dr. Maya Angelou]], Robin Smith, [[Marianne Williamson]], [[Dr. Mehmet Oz]], [[Bob Greene]], [[Nate Berkus]], Jean Chatzky, [[Gayle King]], Rabbi Shumley Boteach, [[Holly Robinson Peete]] and Rodney Peete, and Michael Losier. Oprah, herself, also personally interviewed some of the most influential voices in the spiritual realm on her weekly program, ''Oprah's Soul Series''.

Harpo Radio, Inc. produced and broadcast the Oprah & Friends channel from an [[XM]] studio in [[Chicago, Illinois]], from [[New York, New York]]. It shut down on January 1, 2015.<ref name="harporadio">http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/oprah-winfrey-channel-siriusxm-air-article-1.2070226</ref>
