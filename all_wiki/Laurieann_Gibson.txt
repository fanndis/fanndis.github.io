'''Laurieann Gibson''' is a Canadian choreographer, director, television personality, singer, actress and dancer. She has choreographed dance numbers for musical artists such as [[Michael Jackson]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[Lady Gaga]] and [[Beyoncé]]. Her music video directing credits include "[[Judas (Lady Gaga song)|Judas]]", "[[You and I (Lady Gaga song)|You and I]]" and "Love to My Cobain".

In 2005, Gibson rose to prominence on the [[MTV]] reality series, ''[[Making the Band]]'', and later worked as a judge on ''[[Little Talent Show]]'', ''[[Skating with the Stars]]'' and ''[[So You Think You Can Dance]]''. Gibson also had a brief musical career, in which she released two albums.

==Life and career==
Gibson was born in [[Toronto]], Ontario, Canada.<ref name="DanceInforma">{{cite news|last1=Searle|first1=Deborah|title=LaurieAnn Gibson – A Passion and a Calling|url=http://dancemagazine.com.au/2010/04/laurieann-gibson-%E2%80%93-a-passion-and-a-calling/|accessdate=October 17, 2017|work=Dance Informa|date=April 6, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180830005127/http://dancemagazine.com.au/2010/04/laurieann-gibson-%e2%80%93-a-passion-and-a-calling/|archive-date=August 30, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> She rose to public prominence as the star of [[MTV]]'s ''[[Making the Band]]'' and P. Diddy and Mark Burnett's ''[[P. Diddy's Starmaker|Starmaker]]'' series.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hip-Hop Choreographer Laurie Ann Gibson Does Ballet, Battles Diddy|url=http://www.vulture.com/2007/11/hiphop_choreographer_laura_ann.html|accessdate=October 17, 2017|work=[[Vulture.com|Vulture]]|date=November 21, 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180723093220/http://www.vulture.com/2007/11/hiphop_choreographer_laura_ann.html|archive-date=July 23, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Kaufman|first1=Gil|title=Lady Gaga Cuts Ties With Laurieann Gibson|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1674286/lady-gaga-creative-director-laurieann-gibson/|accessdate=October 17, 2017|work=MTV News|date=November 14, 2011}}</ref> Gibson was also a "Fly Girl" on the popular TV series ''[[In Living Color]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Laurieann Gibson: 'Nobody Who Has a Hit Record Should Be Twerking'|url=https://www.bet.com/news/celebrities/2015/06/10/laurieann-gibson-nobody-who-has-a-hit-record-should-be-twerking.html|accessdate=October 17, 2017|work=[[BET]]|date=June 10, 2015}}</ref>

Following her training at the [[Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater]], Gibson went from theatre dance to hip hop, becoming director of choreography for [[Motown Records]] and [[Bad Boy Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dance-teacher.com/technique-laurieann-gibson-2392323601.html|title=Technique: Laurieann Gibson|last=Zar|first=Rachel|date=2011-05-23|website=Dance Teacher|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref> She has worked with [[Michael Jackson]], [[Alicia Keys]], and [[Beyoncé]].<ref name="DanceInforma"/> She has also done choreography for the movies, ''[[Alfie (2004 film)|Alfie]]'' and ''[[Honey (2003 film)|Honey]]'', in which she also appeared as star [[Jessica Alba]]'s dance nemesis.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Moss|first1=Corey|title=Lil' Romeo Finds Big Sis In Co-Star Jessica Alba|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1479198/lil-romeo-finds-big-sis-in-co-star-jessica-alba/|accessdate=October 17, 2017|work=MTV News|date=September 22, 2003}}</ref>

In November 2011, Lady Gaga ended her professional relationship with Gibson due to "creative differences".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/music_blog/2011/11/lady-gaga-dismisses-longtime-creative-director-laurieann-gibson.html|first=Gerrick|last=Kennedy|title=Lady Gaga dismisses longtime creative director Laurieann Gibson|work=Los Angeles Times|date=November 14, 2011|accessdate=November 15, 2011}}</ref>

In 2012, Gibson directed [[Big Bang (South Korean band)|BIGBANG]]'s World Tour.<ref>{{cite web|author=Chrissy Mahlmeister|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2516200/big-bang-tour/|title=Big Bang Pairs Up With Laurieann Gibson|publisher=MTV|date=March 14, 2012}}</ref>

Gibson was the choreographer for [[Gugu Mbatha-Raw]] in ''[[Beyond the Lights]]'', training her for 6 months in order to perfect her pop-star persona.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Gaffney|first1=Adrienne|title='Beyond the Lights' Star Gugu Mbatha-Raw on Rihanna, Lauryn Hill and Judy Garland Inspiring Her Big-Screen Role|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/6319878/gugu-mbatha-raw-on-beyond-the-lights-rihanna-lauryn-hill-and-judy-garland|accessdate=10 March 2018|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=17 November 2014}}</ref> 

In 2017, Gibson was featured on Lifetime's ''Dance Moms'' as a choreographer for Abby Lee Miller's Elite Competition Team.

In 2019, Gibson became a judge on the sixteenth season of ''So You Think You Can Dance'' alongside, Nigel Lythgoe, Mary Murphy, and fellow newcomer Dominic "D-Trix" Sandoval.

In 2020, Gibson was awarded the inaugural Willie Dunn Award by the [[Prism Prize]] committee, to honour her work as a choreographer and director of music videos.<ref name=2020special>[https://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2020/07/22/2020-prism-prize-special-award-winners-announced "2020 Prism Prize Special Award Winners Announced"]. ''FYI Music News'', July 22, 2020.</ref>
