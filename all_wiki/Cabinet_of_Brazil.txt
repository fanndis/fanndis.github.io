{{Politics of Brazil}}
The '''Cabinet of Brazil''' ({{lang-pt|Gabinete Ministerial do Brasil}}) is composed of the [[Minister of State|Ministers of State]] and [[senior advisor]]s of the [[Executive (government)|executive branch]] of the [[Government of Brazil|federal government]] of [[Brazil]]. Cabinet officers are appointed and dismissed by the [[President of Brazil|President]]. There are currently twenty-three [[Ministry (government department)|Ministries]], including six Ministry-level offices: the Chief of Staff, Secretary of Government, General-Secretary of the Presidency, Institutional Security, Attorney General, and President of the Central Bank.

==Responsibilities==
Ministers assist the President in the exercise of executive power. Each minister is responsible for the general administration of a government portfolio, and heads the corresponding [[Ministry (government department)|government ministry]]. Ministers prepare standards, monitor and evaluate federal programs, and formulate and implement policies for the sectors they represent. They are also responsible for establishing strategies, policies and priorities in the application of public resources. Generally, the  minister considered to be the highest-ranking is the [[Chief of Staff of Brazil|Chief of Staff]], while other high-profile ministers include [[Ministry of Finance (Brazil)|Finance]], [[Ministry of Justice (Brazil)|Justice]], [[Ministry of External Relations (Brazil)|Foreign Affairs]] and [[Ministry of Defence (Brazil)|Defense]].
