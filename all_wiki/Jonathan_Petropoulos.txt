'''Jonathan Petropoulos''' (born January 10, 1961) is an American historian who writes about [[National Socialism]] and, in particular, the fate of [[Nazi Plunder|art looted]] during [[World War II]]. He is John V. Croul Professor of European History at [[Claremont McKenna College]] in [[Claremont, California]].<ref name="LATimes" /> Before his 1999 appointment to Claremont McKenna College, Petropoulos taught at [[Loyola College in Maryland]].<ref name="PetropoulosCV" />

From 1998 to 2000, Petropoulos served as Research Director for the Presidential Advisory Commission on Holocaust Assets, chaired by [[Edgar Bronfman, Sr.]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Panel on Nazi Art Theft Fell Short, Experts Say|first=Ralph|last=Blumenthal|date=2003-03-03|accessdate=2008-07-27|work=[[New York Times]]|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9804EFD71E3CF930A35750C0A9659C8B63}}</ref> Since 2000, Petropoulos has served as an expert witness in several legal cases concerning Nazi-looted assets, including ''Altmann v. Austria'' (six paintings by [[Gustav Klimt|Klimt]], including [[Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I]]),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bslaw.com/altmann/Klimt/Petropoulos.pdf|title=Report of Professor Jonathan Petropoulos, Claremont McKenna College|last=Petropolous|first=Jonathan|date=July 2005|website=Burris, Schoenberg & Walden, LLP}}</ref> ''Cassirer v. [[Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum]]'' (painting by [[Pissarro]]), ''[[Alphonse Kann|Kann]] v. [[Daniel Wildenstein|Wildenstein]]'' (medieval manuscripts), and ''Rosner et al. v. U.S.A.'' (the [[Hungarian Gold Train]] case).<ref name="PetropoulosCV">{{cite web|url=http://www.claremontmckenna.edu/hist/CV/jpetropoulos-cv.pdf|title=Jonathan Petropoulos ''curriculum vitae''|date=June 2006|accessdate=29 Dec 2008|publisher=[[Claremont McKenna College]]|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606033127/http://www.claremontmckenna.edu/hist/CV/jpetropoulos-cv.pdf|archivedate=2011-06-06}}</ref>

Petropoulos was featured in ''[[The Rape of Europa (book)|The Rape of Europa]]'', a 2006 documentary on Nazi art looting.<ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Petropoulos Featured in Documentary About Nazi Art Thefts|url=http://www.claremontmckenna.edu/news/pressreleases/article.asp?article_id=904|date=2007-10-18|accessdate=2008-07-27|publisher=[[Claremont McKenna College]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080123204442/http://www.claremontmckenna.edu/news/pressreleases/article.asp?article_id=904|archive-date=2008-01-23|url-status=dead}}</ref> He is the author of four books: ''Art as Politics in the Third Reich'' (1996),<ref>Petropoulos, Jonathan. ''Art As Politics in the Third Reich'', University of North Carolina Press, 1999. {{ISBN|0-8078-4809-3}}</ref> ''The Faustian Bargain'' (2000),<ref>Petropoulos, Jonathan. ''The Faustian Bargain: The Art World In Nazy Germany'', Oxford University Press, 2000. {{ISBN|0-19-512964-4}}</ref> ''Royals and the Reich'' (2006)<ref>Petropoulos, Jonathan. ''Royals and the Reich'', Oxford University Press, 2006. {{ISBN|0-19-516133-5}} </ref> and ''Artists Under Hitler: Collaboration and Survival in Nazi Germany'' (2014). With John Roth, he is the co-editor of ''Gray Zones: Ambiguity and Compromise in the Holocaust and Aftermath'' (2005).<ref>Petropoulos, Jonathan and Roth, John, editors. ''Gray Zones: Ambiguity and Compromise in the Holocaust and Aftermath'', Berghahn Books, 2005. {{ISBN|1-84545-302-6}}</ref>
