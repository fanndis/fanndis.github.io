"'''Top Off'''" is a song recorded by American record producer [[DJ Khaled]], featuring guest vocals from American rappers [[Jay-Z]] and [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and singer [[Beyoncé]] (credited as "B"). The artists co-wrote the song with Joe Zarrillo, who co-produced it with Khaled and Beyoncé. It was released on March 2, 2018, as the lead single from Khaled's eleventh studio album, ''[[Father of Asahd]]''. [[Complex (magazine)|''Complex'']] magazine ranked Beyoncé's verse on the song as the second best on the album.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/05/ranking-the-features-dj-khaled-father-of-asahd/|title=Ranking the Features on DJ Khaled's Album 'Father of Asahd'|last=Frazier|first=Tharpe|date=May 17, 2019|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|language=en|access-date=November 25, 2019}}</ref>

==Release and promotion==
On February 28, 2018, DJ Khaled announced that he would reveal the title of his upcoming album and release its lead single on March 1, 2018.<ref>{{cite news|last=Aniftos|first=Rania|title=DJ Khaled Teases New 'Father Of Asahd' Album With First Single|url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/03/dj-khaled-teases-father-of-asahd-album-top-off-song-teaser/|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]|date=March 1, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Sterling|first=Scott|title=DJ Khaled Set to Kick Off Summer 2018 with New Song & Album Title|url=http://news.radio.com/2018/02/28/dj-khaled-summer-2018-new-song-album-title/|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=[[CBS Radio]]|date=February 28, 2018}}</ref> Prior to the release, Khaled posted a video of him playing the song for comedian Kevin Hart, who reacted by saying: "The world flatlined."<ref>{{cite news|last=Zidel|first=Alex|title=DJ Khaled Played Kevin Hart His New Single & The Comedian Flipped Out|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dj-khaled-played-kevin-hart-his-new-single-and-the-comedian-absolutely-flipped-out-news.44828.html|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=HotNewHipHop|date=February 28, 2018}}</ref>

==Critical reception==
"Top Off" received mixed reviews from critics.<ref>{{cite news|last=Findlay|first=Mitch|title=DJ Khaled, Jay-Z, Beyonce, & Future's "Top Off" Yields Mixed Reviews|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dj-khaled-jay-z-beyonce-and-futures-top-off-yields-mixed-reviews-news.44923.html|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=HopNewHipHop|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> According to ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s Gil Kaufman, "Top Off" is "a breathless outlaw track about outrunning the police and enjoying the finer things".<ref>{{cite news|last=Kaufman|first=Gil|title=Stream DJ Khaled's 'Top Off' Featuring JAY-Z, Future & Beyonce|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8224110/dj-khaled-top-off-jay-z-future-beyonce|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Billboard|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Jon Caramanica of ''[[The New York Times]]'' disliked Future's verse, writing: "Once you swipe away the numbing hook and pseudo-verse from Future, there, plain as day, is an impressive Jay-Z/Beyoncé duet."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Pareles|first1=Jon|last2=Caramanica|first2=Jon|last3=Russonello|first3=Giovanni|title=The Playlist: Beyoncé and Jay-Z Unite, and 12 More New Songs|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/02/arts/music/playlist-jay-z-beyonce-khaled-rae-sremmurd.html|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=The New York Times|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Larry Fitzmaurice of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' gave a negative review of the song, writing that it is "more bland than it is bizarrely successful, proof that Khaled's blockbuster-stuffed style often suffocates under the heft of its own bravado". He opined that the artists' personalities "are simply too distinct at this point to produce something compelling when shoved together on the same track".<ref>{{cite news|last=Fitzmaurice|first=Larry|title="Top Off" [ft. JAY-Z, Future and Beyoncé] by DJ Khaled Review|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/dj-khaled-top-off-ft-jay-z-future-and-beyonce/|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Pitchfork|date=March 3, 2018}}</ref> Pat King of ''[[Metro (U.S. newspaper)|Metro]]'' named it "one of the catchiest songs in recent memory about outrunning the cops".<ref>{{cite news|last=King|first=Pat|title=The best new Spotify singles for your Monday commute|url=https://www.metro.us/entertainment/new-releases-on-spotify|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Metro|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Adreon Patterson of ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]'' felt that "the track is a little underwhelming considering the collaborators' musical status". Serving as a follow-up to "[[Shining (song)|Shining]]" and "[[I Got the Keys]]", he found the song "disappointing and anti-climactic".<ref>{{cite news|last=Patterson|first=Adreon|title=Beyonce, Jay Z and Future Go Off on DJ Khaled's New Single "Top Off"|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2018/03/beyonce-jay-z-and-future-go-in-on-dj-khaleds-new-s.html|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Paste|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Tom Breihan of [[Stereogum]] praised Khaled for making the song sounds like both a "fame-flexing celebrity event" and an "actual song".<ref>{{cite news|last=Breihan|first=Tom|title=DJ Khaled – "Top Off" (Feat. Jay-Z, Beyoncé, & Future)|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1985484/dj-khaled-top-off-feat-jay-z-beyonce-future/music/|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Stereogum|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Lauren O'Neill of ''[[Vice (magazine)|Noisey]]'' complimented Beyoncé's ability "to knock every other rapper out of the park with her skill and flow".<ref>{{cite news|last=O'Neill|first=Lauren|title=Beyoncé is the Best Rapper On DJ Khaled's New Track "Top Off"|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/gy8nd9/beyonce-is-the-best-rapper-on-dj-khaleds-new-track-top-off|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Noisey|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Bianca Gracie of [[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse]] declared that the song lacks innovation and fails to be memorable. "Jay-Z and Beyoncé deserve a harder beat underneath their flows; the juvenile SoundCloud production didn't do them any favors especially on Beyoncé's opening part."<ref>{{cite news|last=Gracie|first=Bianca|title=Beyoncé Carries the Weight on DJ Khaled's 'Top Off' Song With Jay-Z & Future|url=https://www.fuse.tv/2018/03/beyonce-top-off-song-jay-z-future-dj-khaled|accessdate=March 3, 2018|work=Fuse|date=March 2, 2018}}</ref> Following the release of ''[[Father of Asahd]]'', Frazier Tharpe of [[Complex (magazine)|''Complex'']] magazine ranked the features on the album, with Beyoncé's verse on "Top Off" coming in 2nd, behind [[Nipsey Hussle]]'s verse on "[[Higher (DJ Khaled song)|Higher]]". Tharpe commented that "if  there's any benefit to keeping this old dog of a song on the album, it's giving this blackout verse from B a proper home".<ref name=":0" />
