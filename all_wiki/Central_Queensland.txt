'''Central Queensland''' is an ambiguous [[geography|geographical]] division of [[Queensland]] ([[States and territories of Australia|a state in Australia]]) that centres on the eastern coast, around the [[Tropic of Capricorn]]. Its major regional centre is [[Rockhampton, Queensland|Rockhampton]]. The region extends from the  [[Capricorn Coast]] west to the Central Highlands at [[Emerald, Queensland|Emerald]], north to the [[Mackay Regional Council]] southern boundary, and south to [[Gladstone, Queensland|Gladstone]]. The region is also known as '''Capricornia'''.  It is one of Australia's main coal exporting regions.

At the 2011 Australian Census the region recorded a total population from the six local government areas of 233,931.<ref name="ceda"/>

==Industry==
[[File:StateLibQld 2 232853 Processing plant of Mount Morgan Mines next to the Dee River.jpg|thumb|left|Gold processing plant at [[Mount Morgan, Queensland|Mount Morgan]], 1903]]
Economically, Central Queensland is an important centre of [[primary sector]] [[Industry (economics)|industries]], particularly for food and fibre production.<ref name="aoofaf">{{cite web |url=http://www.rdafcw.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/CQ-Food-and-Fibre-Industries-RDAFCW.pdf |title=An Overview of Food and Fibre Industries in Central Queensland |publisher=Regional Development Australia |access-date=20 June 2014 }}{{dead link|date=August 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Central Queensland includes the [[Bowen Basin]] which is rich in high quality coking coal, the [[Port of Gladstone]] produces 40% of the state's export earnings, the [[Fitzroy River (Queensland)|Fitzroy River]] is the second-largest river system in Australia and commands significant water resources such as [[Fairbairn Dam]]. Gladstone has a significant aluminium smelter.

===Beef===
Rockhampton is claimed to be the beef capital of Australia, a title which is disputed by [[Casino, New South Wales|Casino]] in New South Wales.<ref name="aorhe">{{Cite news |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2014-04-17/beef-capital-fight/5397492 |title=An ownership row has erupted over who holds Australia's Beef Capital title |author=Paul Robinson, Elloise Farrow-Smith & Miranda Saunders |access-date=19 June 2014 |date= 17 April 2014 |work=ABC News |publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation }}</ref> Beef production in the region is centered on Brigalow and speargrass land types.<ref name="teobicq">{{cite web |url=http://www.futurebeef.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/CQBEEF_economics_book.pdf |title=The economics of beef in Central Queensland |publisher=Department of Primary Industries and Fisheries |access-date=20 June 2014 }}</ref> There are three [[abattoir]]s in the region.<ref name="aoofaf"/>  Every three years, Rockhampton holds the national [[Beef Australia]] exposition to celebrate the cattle industry as well as to facilitate trade opportunities for Australian beef producers.<ref>[http://beefaustralia.com.au/about About Beef Australia], [[Beef Australia]] website.  Accessed 10 December 2017.</ref>

===Mining===
[[File:The Big Sapphire Ring.jpg|thumb|upright|The Big Sapphire Ring, [[Sapphire, Queensland|Sapphire]], 2012]]
Central Queensland is one of the world's leading producers and exporters of [[Bituminous coal|black coal]].<ref name="cqiam">{{cite web |url=http://mines.industry.qld.gov.au/mining/central-qld-info-maps.htm |title=Central Queensland information and maps |date=27 February 2014 |work=Department of Natural Resources and Mines |publisher=The State of Queensland |access-date=19 June 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140704020458/http://mines.industry.qld.gov.au/mining/central-qld-info-maps.htm |archive-date=4 July 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> [[Ludwig Leichhardt]] was the first European to discover coal deposits in the region in 1845.<ref name="cmib">{{cite web|url=http://www.bicc.com.au/coal-mining-in-blackwater/ |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130729080545/http://www.bicc.com.au/coal-mining-in-blackwater/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=29 July 2013 |title=Coal Mining in Blackwater |publisher=Blackwater International Coal Centre |access-date=3 August 2014 }}</ref>  In the 2011-2012 financial year the region produced 40% of the state's total coal production.<ref name="cqrp">{{cite web |url=http://www.dlg.qld.gov.au/planning/regional-planning/central-queensland-regional-plan.html |title=Central Queensland Regional Plan |date=24 April 2015 |publisher=Department of Infrastructure, Local Government and Planning |access-date=26 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150507214902/http://www.dlg.qld.gov.au/planning/regional-planning/central-queensland-regional-plan.html |archive-date=7 May 2015 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Coal is extracted from the [[Bowen Basin]] and transported to port facilities at [[Port of Gladstone]] via the [[Blackwater railway system]] or to both [[Hay Point, Queensland|Hay Point]] and [[Abbot Point]] via the [[Goonyella railway line]]. Coal mining is expanding west into the [[Galilee Basin]] and requires an extension of the Goonyella line to transport coal to port. Many mines in the region, particularly those within the [[Fitzroy River (Queensland)|Fitzroy River]] basin, were severely impacted by flooding during the [[2010â€“11 Queensland floods]].<ref name="rtfp">{{cite web |url=http://www.qldreconstruction.org.au/case-studies/central-queensland-mines |title=Return to full production for flood affected mines in Central Queensland |work=Queensland Reconstruction Authority |publisher=Queensland Government |access-date=19 June 2014 }}</ref>

Gold, silver, limestone, coal seam gas, magnesite and gemstones are also mined.<ref name="cqiam"/>  Sapphires were discovered here in 1875.<ref name="hoftsi">{{cite web |url=http://www.australiansapphire.com/history_central_queensland.htm |title=History of the Sapphire Industry - Central Queensland |publisher=Australian Sapphire |access-date=19 June 2014 }}</ref>  Gold was discovered in the Mount Morgan region around 1865.  [[Mount Morgan Mine]] has since gone on to become one of Australia's richest mines.<ref>{{cite QHR|15526|Mount Morgan Mine Site|600751|accessdate=19 June 2014}}</ref>

Purpose built mining towns in Central Queensland include [[Dysart, Queensland|Dysart]], [[Middlemount, Queensland|Middlemount]], [[Moranbah, Queensland|Moranbah]], [[Mount Morgan, Queensland|Mount Morgan]] and [[Moura, Queensland|Moura]]. Three mining disaster have occurred at Moura since 1975, resulting in the loss of 36 lives.<ref name="moin">{{cite web |url=http://mines.industry.qld.gov.au/safety-and-health/moura-inquiries.htm |title=Moura inquiries |publisher=The State of Queensland |access-date=3 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140321211754/http://mines.industry.qld.gov.au/safety-and-health/moura-inquiries.htm |archive-date=21 March 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

===Citrus canker outbreak===

In 2004, an [[orchard]] on Evergreen farm was the site of the first detection of [[citrus canker]] in Central Queensland. A significant part of the citrus growing industry was devastated when a total of {{convert|6000|acre|km2}} of crop had to be destroyed so the disease would not spread across the country.<ref name="ll">{{cite episode | title=Spot of Trouble  | series=[[Landline (TV series)|Landline]]  | airdate=29 March 2009 }}</ref> In 2005 several fresh [[outbreak]]s were reported so the eradication expanded to include private backyard trees.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/newsitems/200509/s1455151.htm |title=Citrus canker outbreak to impact on residents |access-date=29 March 2008 |date=7 September 2005 |work=ABC News Online |publisher=[[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]] }}{{Dead link|date=June 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>  The outbreak's cause has not been fully explained despite a federal inquiry.<ref name="ll"/> In 2009 authorities from the [[Government of Queensland]] declared the eradication program complete.

==Protected areas==
[[File:Carnarvon Gorge from Boolimba Bluff 01.07.2010.jpg|thumb|[[Carnarvon Gorge]] from Boolimba Bluff, 2010]]
[[File:Mt Castletower from Lake Awoonga.jpg|thumb|Mount Castletower and [[Lake Awoonga]]]]
The region contain 33 national parks.  [[Great Keppel Island]] has been an island tourist attraction since the 1960s.  It and several other islands in the area are surrounded by coral reefs.<ref name="kbaisma">{{cite web |url=http://www.nprsr.qld.gov.au/parks/keppel-bay-islands/about.html |title=About Keppel Bay Islands |date=6 January 2014 |publisher=Department of National Parks, Recreation, Sport and Racing |access-date=19 June 2014 }}</ref>  In the west of the region is Queensland's central highlands and the [[Carnarvon Gorge]], which is protected within the [[Carnarvon National Park]].  Carnarvon Gorge features white sandstone cliffs, steep-sided gorges a diverse range of significant plant and animal species and many walking tracks.<ref name="acg">{{cite web |url=http://www.nprsr.qld.gov.au/parks/carnarvon-gorge/about.html |title=About Carnarvon Gorge |publisher=Department of National Parks, Recreation, Sport and Racing |access-date=19 June 2014 }}</ref>  [[Kroombit Tops National Park]] provides habitat for the endemic [[Kroombit tinker frog]].  [[Deepwater National Park]] is good place for turtle watching. From November to March three species of turtle lay their eggs on beaches protected within the park.<ref name="nch">{{cite web |url=http://www.nprsr.qld.gov.au/parks/deepwater/culture.html |title=Nature, culture and history |publisher=Department of National Parks, Recreation, Sport and Racing |access-date=19 June 2014 }}</ref>

==Communities==
For this purpose the area of Central Queensland was restricted to the areas encircled by the [[Dawson Highway]] between Gladstone and Springsure; the [[Gregory Highway]] between Springsure and Clermont, and the [[Peak Downs Highway]] between Clermont and enters North Queensland via Mackay - extended right to the eastern coastline.

Major cities in the region are [[Emerald, Queensland|Emerald]], [[Gladstone, Queensland|Gladstone]] and [[Rockhampton, Queensland|Rockhampton]]. Some communities on the [[Capricorn Coast]] include [[Byfield, Queensland|Byfield]], [[Yeppoon, Queensland|Yeppoon]], [[Great Keppel Island]], [[Emu Park, Queensland|Emu Park]] and [[Cawarral, Queensland|Cawarral]].

== History ==
''[[Gungabula language|Gungabula]]'' (also known as ''Kongabula'' and ''Khungabula'') is an [[Australian Aboriginal languages|Australian Aboriginal language]] of the headwaters of the [[Dawson River (Queensland)|Dawson River]] in Central Queensland. The language region includes areas within the local government area of [[Maranoa Region]], particularly the towns of [[Charleville, Queensland|Charleville]], [[Augathella]] and [[Blackall, Queensland|Blackall]] and as well as the [[Carnarvon Range]].<ref>{{Cite SLQ-CC-BY|url=https://maps.slq.qld.gov.au/iyil/view/67|title=Gungabula|author=|date=|website=Queensland Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander languages map|access-date=28 January 2020}}</ref>

''[[Wadja language|Wadja]]'' (also known as ''Wadjigu'', ''Wadya'', ''Wadjainngo'', ''Mandalgu'', and ''Wadjigun)'' is an Australian Aboriginal language in Central Queensland. The language region includes  the local government areas of the [[Aboriginal Shire of Woorabinda]] and [[Central Highlands Region]], including the [[Blackdown Tableland National Park|Blackdown Tablelands]]. the [[Comet River]], and the [[Expedition Range]], and the towns of [[Woorabinda, Queensland|Woorabinda]], [[Springsure]] and [[Rolleston, Queensland|Rolleston]].<ref>{{Cite SLQ-CC-BY|url=https://maps.slq.qld.gov.au/iyil/view/137|title=Wadja|author=|date=|website=Queensland Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander languages map|access-date=5 February 2020}}</ref>

''[[Yagalingu language|Yagalingu]]'' (also known as ''Jagalingu'', ''Auanbura'', ''Kokleburra'', ''Owanburra'', ''Kowanburra'', ''Wagalbara'', and ''Djagalingu'') is an Australian Aboriginal language of Central Queensland. Its traditional language region was within the local government area of [[Isaac Region]], from the headwaters of the [[Belyando River]] south to Avoca, north to [[Laglan, Queensland|Laglan]], west to the [[Great Dividing Range]], and east and south to [[Drummond Range]].<ref>{{Cite SLQ-CC-BY|url=https://maps.slq.qld.gov.au/iyil/view/157|title=Yagalingu|author=|date=|website=Queensland Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander languages map|access-date=30 January 2020}}</ref>

''[[Yambina language|Yambina]]'' (also known as ''Jambina'' and ''Jambeena'') is an [[Australian Aboriginal languages|Australian Aboriginal language]] of Central Queensland. Its traditional language region is the local government area of [[Central Highlands Region]], including [[Shire of Peak Downs|Peak Downs]], Logan Creek, south to Avon Downs, east to [[Denham Range]] and Logan Downs, west to Elgin Downs and at Solferino.<ref>{{Cite SLQ-CC-BY|url=https://maps.slq.qld.gov.au/iyil/view/160|title=Yambina|author=|date=|website=Queensland Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander languages map|access-date=30 January 2020}}</ref>

''[[Yetimarala language|Yetimarala]]'' (also known as ''Jetimarala'', ''Yetimaralla'', and ''Bayali'') is an [[Australian Aboriginal languages|Australian Aboriginal language]] of Central Queensland. Its traditional language region is within the local government areas of [[Central Highlands Region]], on the [[Boomer Range]] and [[Broadsound Range]] and the [[Fitzroy River (Queensland)|Fitzroy River]], Killarney Station, [[Mackenzie River (Queensland)|Mackenzie River]] and [[Isaac River]].<ref>{{Cite SLQ-CC-BY|url=https://maps.slq.qld.gov.au/iyil/view/162|title=Yetimarala|author=|date=|website=Queensland Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander languages map|access-date=30 January 2020}}</ref>
