'''''Tennis''''' is a U.S. [[sport]]s [[magazine]] devoted to the sport of [[tennis]]. The print magazine is published eight months per-year, while the magazine also operates a website, '''Tennis.com'''.

==History==
The magazine was established in May 1965, published out of [[Chicago]] with a regional focus.<ref name="beginnings"/> The tennis boom of the 1970s resulted in a rapid expansion of the magazine, both in scope and circulation. In addition to top tennis stars, celebrities like [[Johnny Carson]] and [[Farrah Fawcett]] appeared on the cover.<ref name="beginnings"/> It was owned by Golf Digest / Tennis Magazine and sold to the New York Times Company.

[[Miller Publishing Company|Miller Publishing]] bought the magazine in 1997 from [[The New York Times Company]].<ref name="ct">{{cite news|last1=lazarus|first1=George|title=Tennis Magazine Hopes Seles Sells New Cover Look|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/1998-12-09/business/9812090254_1_newsstand-tennis-cover|accessdate=March 1, 2017|work=Chicago Tribune|publisher=Tribune Publishing|date=December 9, 1998|language=en}}</ref> It brought on two retired champions as part owners and contributors: first [[Chris Evert]] in 2000 then [[Pete Sampras]] in 2003.<ref>{{cite news|last=Thomaselli|first=Rich|title=Pete Sampras Becomes Part Owner of "Tennis" Magazine |url=http://adage.com/article/mediaworks/pete-sampras-part-owner-tennis-magazine/38518|accessdate=19 September 2011|newspaper=Advertising Age|date=October 6, 2003}}</ref> In the early 2010s the circulation was 600,000 subscriptions, the majority of which were purchased by the [[United States Tennis Association]] (USTA) for its members.<ref name=12circ/><ref name=sbj/>

In 2014, publisher and USTA board member Jeff Willams purchased controlling interest in Tennis Media Company, owner of the magazine and its offshoot website.<ref name=sbj>{{cite news|last1=Kaplan|first1=Daniel|title=Tennis’ publisher buys firm’s controlling stake|url=http://www.sportsbusinessdaily.com/Journal/Issues/2014/08/18/Media/Tennis-magazine.aspx|accessdate=March 1, 2017|work=Sports Business Journal|publisher=American City Business Journals|date=August 18, 2014}}</ref> In 2017, [[Sinclair Broadcast Group]], owner of [[Tennis Channel]], acquired Tennis Media Company for $8 million, seeking to build synergies between the properties.<ref name=bc>{{cite news|last1=Marszalek|first1=Diana|title=Sinclair Buys 'Tennis' Magazine|url=http://www.broadcastingcable.com/news/currency/sinclair-buys-tennis-magazine/163734|accessdate=March 8, 2017|work=Broadcasting & Cable|publisher=NewBay Media|date=March 1, 2017}}</ref>

==Content==
Aside from articles about the most recent events and most active [[List of tennis players|players]], the magazine also includes the recent ranking for both [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP]] and [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]], as well as brief summaries of the future [[List of tennis tournaments|tournaments]], their participants and the past winners.

Chris Evert has her own personal section in the magazine—usually the first page—which is called "Chrissie's Page". Aside from Evert, other famous players and coaches also contribute to the magazine, Pete Sampras, [[Paul Annacone]], former coach of Sampras, is the Senior Instruction Editor and [[Brad Gilbert]], former coach of [[Andre Agassi]] and [[Andy Roddick]], is Touring Instruction Editor.
