'''''Fade to Black''''' is a 2004 [[documentary film]] about the career of American [[Rapping|rapper]] [[Jay-Z]]. It also features many other famous names in [[hip hop music]]. This live concert at [[Madison Square Garden]] was meant to be Jay-Z's final performance, as he announced his intentions to retire from the industry.<ref name=wapo>{{cite news|title=Jay-Z's 'Fade' Gets an A|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A25216-2004Nov4.html| last=Harrington| first=Richard| newspaper=Washington Post| date=November 5, 2004| page=36|access-date=October 9, 2017}}</ref><ref name=videohound>{{cite book| chapter=Fade to Black 2004| title=VideoHound's Golden Movie Retriever| publisher=Gale| year=2008| via=HighBeam Research| url-access=subscription| url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-2505908191.html| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201040241/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G2-2505908191.html| url-status=dead| archive-date=2017-12-01}}</ref>

''Fade to Black'' runs through some of the major parts of Jay-Z's Madison Square Garden performance while cutting to Jay-Z and his exploits, as well as insights into the making of ''[[The Black Album (Jay-Z album)|The Black Album]]''.<ref name=wapo/><ref name=videohound/>
