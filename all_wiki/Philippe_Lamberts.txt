'''Philippe Lamberts''' (born 16 April 1963) is a Belgian politician and [[Member of the European Parliament]] (MEP) from [[Belgium]].

==Education and early career==
Lamberts graduated as an engineer at the [[Université catholique de Louvain|University of Louvain]] (UCLouvain) in 1986. From 1987 to 2009 he worked at IBM in a variety of positions, also as a manager. Carrying out the function of councillor between 1994 and 2006, Lamberts represented the [[French language|French]]-speaking Green Party [[Ecolo]] in the local council of [[Anderlecht]]. Between 1999 and 2003 he was an adviser of the Vice-Prime Minister [[Isabelle Durant]] on foreign affairs and defence.
==Member of the European Parliament, 2009–present==
[[File:Brexit debate - Philippe Lamberts (Greens EFA, Belgium) (48753084208).jpg|thumb|Lamberts speaks at the European Parliament in 2019]]
Lamberts was the co-spokesperson of the [[European Green Party]] between 2006 and 2012. (first with [[Ulrike Lunacek]], then with [[Monica Frassoni]]) and has been a [[Member of the European Parliament]] (MEP) for the [[Ecolo]] party since 2009.<ref>{{cite web |title=Philippe LAMBERTS (articles on the EGP website) |url=http://europeangreens.eu/search/site/lamberts |publisher=[[European Green Party]] |accessdate=1 July 2009}}</ref> He has been leading the [[The Greens–European Free Alliance|Greens–European Free Alliance]] in the European Parliament since 2014, alongside co-chair [[Rebecca Harms]]<ref>Dave Keating (June 11, 2014), [http://www.politico.eu/article/greens-pick-up-new-members-and-elect-presidents/ Greens pick up new members and elect presidents] ''[[European Voice]]''.</ref> and later [[Ska Keller]].<ref>Greens-EFA Press Release (December 13, 2016), [https://www.greens-efa.eu/en/article/new-bureau-of-the-greens-efa-group/ New Bureau of the Greens/EFA Group]</ref> He is a member of the [[European Parliament]]'s [[Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs]] (ECON) and the [[Committee for Industry, Research and Energy]].

As a member of ECON, Lamberts was credited with an amendment to the Fourth [[Capital Requirements Directive]] that capped bonus payments in the financial services to no more than 100% of their salary, or 200% with shareholder approval.<ref>[http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/c1ea897c-9a55-11e3-8e06-00144feab7de.html#axzz2u5ASVJX6 Clout of European Parliament is growing], ''[[Financial Times]]'', 20 February 2014</ref> In 2015, he led calls for a special committee of inquiry into how EU Member States give special tax treatment to “national champions;”<ref>Carla Main (February 3, 2015), [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-02-03/eu-tax-inquiry-bnp-ma-on-counterfeits-compliance EU Tax Inquiry, BNP, Ma on Counterfeits: Compliance] ''[[Bloomberg Business]]''.</ref> he later became a member of the parliament's Special Committee on Tax Rulings and Other Measures Similar in Nature or Effect.

Since 2017, Lamberts has also been serving on the Parliament's so-called Brexit Steering Group, which works under the aegis of the [[Conference of Presidents]] and to coordinates Parliament's deliberations, considerations and resolutions on the [[Brexit|UK's withdrawal from the EU]].<ref>[http://www.epgenpro.europarl.europa.eu/static/brexit-steering-group/en/home.html Brexit Steering Group] [[European Parliament]].</ref>
