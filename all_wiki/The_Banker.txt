'''''The Banker''''' is a British [[English language|English]]-language monthly international financial affairs publication owned by [[The Financial Times]] Ltd. and edited in London, United Kingdom. The magazine was first published in January 1926 through founding Editor, [[Brendan Bracken]] of the ''[[Financial News (1884–1945)|Financial News]]'', who went on to become the chairman of the ''[[Financial Times]]'' from 1945-1958.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/News/Window-on-the-world|title=The Banker History: Window on the World|work=The Banker|accessdate=4 August 2011}}</ref>

Since its founding, the magazine has claimed a dedication to the international perspective through features, interviews, multi-media applications, and events. The Banker is the world’s premier banking and finance resource, read in over 180 countries and is the key source of data and analysis for the industry. It combines in-depth regional and country coverage with reports on global financial markets, regulation and policy, cash management and securities services, commodities and carbon finance, infrastructure and project finance, trading and technology, clearing and settlement, and management and governance issues.<ref>[http://www.thebanker.com/Information/About-Us "About Us"]. ''The Banker''.</ref>

''The Banker'' is read most widely in banks, financial institutions, multilateral corporations, central banks, and finance ministries around the world. Approximately 60% of its readers are CEO/President and CFO/Treasurers of their organizations.<ref>[http://www.thebanker.com/Information/Advertise Media Pack] The Banker</ref>

==Features and awards==
''The Banker''{{'}}s primary focus is global financial comment and insight, featuring opinion pieces, profiles, and interviews with leading banking and finance figures. The Bracken Column <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Comment/Bracken |title=The Bracken Column |publisher=Thebanker.com |date= |accessdate=2011-08-04}}</ref> is named after Brendan Bracken, the founding editor of The Banker and serves as a think-tank for financial sector participants.

The Bank of the Year Awards<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Awards/The-Bank-of-The-Year-Awards|title=The Bank of the Year Awards|work=The Banker|accessdate=4 August 2011}}</ref> is an annual awards event recognising the top financial institutions in the world. The most recent event took place in December 2012, and 149 countries were acknowledged for their achievements, with [[Scotiabank]] winning the global award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Awards/Bank-of-The-Year-Awards/The-Banker-Awards-2012-Global-and-regional-winners |title=Bank of the Year Awards 2012 |work=The Banker|accessdate=29 November 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scotiabank.com/vg/en/0,,7371,00.html|title=Scotiabank named Global Bank of the Year|work=Scotiabank|date=1 December 2012}}</ref>

Top 1000 World Banks<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Top-1000-World-Banks|title=Top 1000 World Banks|work=The Banker|accessdate=4 August 2011}}</ref> is published annually in July based on the findings of The Banker Database.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebankerdatabase.com|title=The Banker Database|publisher=The Banker Database|accessdate=4 August 2011}}</ref> This ranking serves to recognise global leaders in the industry for their achievements, ranking the world's banks by Tier 1 capital globally, as well as by individual country. The awards are the industry’s most widely used index of global banking, and are internationally recognised as the definitive guide to the soundness, strength, and profitability of banks.<ref>[http://www.thebanker.com/Media/Files/The-Banker-Media-Pack-2011 Media Pack] The Banker</ref> The banks are assessed by Tier 1 capital, with secondary rankings by assets, capital/asset ratio, real profit growth, profit on average capital, and return on assets. In 2013 [[ICBC]] was ranked in top place,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Banker-Data/Banker-Rankings/Top-1000-World-Banks-2013|title=The Top 1000 World Banks 2013|work=The Banker|date=1 July 2013|accessdate=8 July 2013}}</ref> the first time ever for a Chinese bank<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/2013/07/01/uk-banks-rankings-icbc-idUKBRE9600IH20130701 |title=China's ICC tops global bank rankings for first time|work=Reuters|date=1 July 2013|accessdate=8 July 2013}}</ref> and retained the top position in the 2014 ranking.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Top-1000-World-Banks/Top-1000-World-Banks-2014-Back-on-track|title=Top 1000 World Banks 2014: Back on track? |work=The Banker |date=30 June 2014|accessdate=17 July 2014}}</ref>

As well as The Bank of the Year Awards, the Banker also conducts awards for Deals of the Year, Innovation in Banking Technology, Investment Banking, Finance Minister of the Year, Central Bank Governor of the Year, Private Banking, and Islamic Bank of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/Awards|title=The Banker awards|work=The Banker|accessdate=4 August 2011}}</ref>

How to Run a Bank<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebanker.com/How-To-Run-A-Bank|title=How to Run a Bank|work=The Banker|accessdate=4 August 2011}}</ref> is an annual guide to international banking in the new financial environment, combining contributions from bankers, regulators, academics, and economists.

Top Islamic Financial Institutions<ref>{{cite web|url=http://islamicbanks.thebanker.com |title=Top Islamic Institutions|publisher=The Banker|accessdate=7 July 2013}}</ref> annual listing assesses the global state of the Islamic banking and finance market today. Since 2007, it has aimed to help estimate the size and growth rate of the emerging Islamic financial marketplace.

On 8 January 2014, it was announced that Ireland's [[Michael Noonan (Fine Gael politician)|Michael Noonan]] was awarded "Best finance minister in Europe".
