The '''Home Secretary''' {{efn|group=note|name=a|Officially: '''Her Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for the Home Department'''}}, is a senior [[Minister of the Crown]] within the [[Government of the United Kingdom]], and head of the [[Home Office]]. As one of the four [[Great Offices of State]], the Home Secretary is a senior member of the [[Cabinet of the United Kingdom|British Cabinet]].

The current Secretary of State for the Home Department is [[Priti Patel]], [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|MP]], who was appointed by Prime Minister Boris Johnson in July 2019.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-britain-eu-leader-patel-idUSKCN1UJ2HW|title=Priti Patel appointed UK interior minister: statement|date=24 July 2019|via=www.reuters.com}}</ref>

==Responsibilities==
Corresponding to what is generally known as an [[Interior minister|interior minister]] in many other countries, the Home Secretary's remit includes:
* [[Law enforcement in the United Kingdom|Law enforcement]] in [[England and Wales]]
* Matters of [[national security]]
* Issues concerning [[Modern immigration to the United Kingdom|immigration]]
* Oversight of the [[MI5|Security Service (MI5)]].<ref name="gov.uk">{{cite web |url=https://www.gov.uk/government/ministers/secretary-of-state-for-the-home-department |title=Secretary of State for the Home Department |website=[[gov.uk]] |publisher=Government of the United Kingdom |accessdate=18 July 2016}}</ref> 

Formerly, the Home Secretary was the minister responsible for prisons and probation in England and Wales; however in 2007 those responsibilities were transferred to the [[Ministry of Justice (United Kingdom)|Ministry of Justice]] under the [[Lord Chancellor]].
