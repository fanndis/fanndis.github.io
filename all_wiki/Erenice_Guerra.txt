'''Erenice Alves Guerra''' ({{IPA-pt|eɾeˈnjsj ˈawvjs ˈgɛʁɐ|br}} or [[Help:IPA for Portuguese|[eɾẽˈnjsj ˈawvjs ˈgɛʁɐ]]]; born February 15, 1959) was the 41st [[Chief of Staff of Brazil]]. She is best known for resigning from the post of Chief of Staff due to credible [http://g1.globo.com/politica/noticia/2010/09/ministra-erenice-guerra-deixa-o-governo.html allegations] of corruption and nepotism for which she was publicly reprimanded by the [http://etica.planalto.gov.br/ President's Public Ethics Committee]. She was born in [[Brasília]] and is a law graduate, specialized in [[Health Law]].

She was Chief of Staff of the Secretariat of Public Security of the [[Brazilian Federal District|Federal District]] Government (GDF) and general manager of the [[Brasília Metro]]. She was also head of the legal department of the Brasília Society of Collective Transport (TCB).

At the federal administration level, she was manager of [[Eletronorte]], Attorney General of the National School of Public Administration (ENAP), Legal Adviser of the Ministry of Mines and Energy (MME) and consultant of the [[United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization]] (UNESCO) at the [[Ministry of Health (Brazil)|Ministry of Health]]. She also worked in the council of the [[Chamber of Deputies of Brazil|Chamber of Deputies]] of Brazil.

Erenice was an advisor at [[Petrobras]] and the [[CHESF|São Francisco Hydroelectric Company]] (CHESF). Currently she is member of the [[board of directors]] of the [[Brazilian Development Bank]] (BNDES).

From 2005 to 2010 she held the position of Executive Secretary of the Presidential Staff Office.
