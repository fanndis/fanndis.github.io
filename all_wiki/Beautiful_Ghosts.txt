"'''Beautiful Ghosts'''", alternatively titled "'''Beautiful Ghosts (From the Motion Picture ''Cats'')'''", is a song by American singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]] for the 2019 ''[[Cats (2019 film)|Cats]]'' film adaptation, in which Swift played [[Bombalurina (cat)|Bombalurina]]. The song was written by Swift and ''Cats'' creator [[Andrew Lloyd Webber]], and produced by [[Greg Wells]], Lloyd Webber, and ''Cats'' director [[Tom Hooper]]. It was released on November 15, 2019. The song is performed in the film by the principal character [[Victoria the White Cat|Victoria]], portrayed by [[Francesca Hayward]]. A 30-second reprise is also sung by [[Judi Dench]] as [[Old Deuteronomy]].<ref name="VarietyOct2019"/> Swift performs the official single version that is played over the ending credits.<ref name="HR">{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/andrew-lloyd-webber-talks-working-taylor-swift-new-cats-song-1249724|title=Andrew Lloyd Webber Talks Working With Taylor Swift on New 'Cats' Song|last=Vlessing|first=Etan|date=October 24, 2019|website=The Hollywood Reporter|url-status=live|access-date=October 24, 2019}}</ref>

Upon release, the song received positive reviews from music critics. It received a nomination for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] at the [[77th Golden Globe Awards]], and is nominated for the [[Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media]] at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]]—Swift's third nomination for the latter, following "[[Safe & Sound (Taylor Swift song)|Safe & Sound]]" (2011) and "[[I Don't Wanna Live Forever]]" (2016).

== Background and release ==
As a concept, the then-untitled song was first reported in early 2018, when the film adaptation was in early stages of production.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/andrew-lloyd-webber-pens-new-song-for-possible-cats-film|title=Andrew Lloyd Webber Pens New Song for Possible Cats Film|last=Gans|first=Andrew|date=January 5, 2018|website=Playbill|url-status=live|access-date=October 24, 2019}}</ref> The song was designed to provide a way for Victoria to introduce her character to the audience, as she communicates solely through gestures and dance in the original stage show.<ref name="HR" /> Swift described the song as such:

{{quote|"Beautiful Ghosts" is sung from a young voice who is wondering if she will ever have glory days. Longing for the sense of belonging she sees everyone finding. Reaching for it, desperately afraid of never having beautiful ghosts of days gone by to cling to in her older years.<ref name="SwiftBillboard">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/movies/8543618/taylor-swift-cats-beautiful-ghosts-open-letter|title=The Importance of 'Cats,' in Taylor Swift's Own Words|last=Swift|first=Taylor|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=November 16, 2019}}</ref>}}The song was released on digital platforms and to streaming services on November 15, 2019, one month before the film's theatrical release.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Aniftos|first=Rania|date=November 15, 2019|title=Taylor Swift Releases 'Beautiful Ghosts,' Co-Written With Andrew Lloyd Webber for 'Cats' Film|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8543715/taylor-swift-beautiful-ghosts-cat|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=November 15, 2019}}</ref>

== Composition ==
The song has a running time of four minutes and twenty-one seconds. It is in the key of E, [[modulation (music)|modulating]] from [[E minor|minor]] key in the verses to a [[E major|major]] key in the chorus with a vocal range spanning from [[G♯ (musical note)|G#{{sub|3}}]] to [[C♯ (musical note)|C#{{sub|5}}]]. It has a [[tempo]] of 60 beats per minute. The song is in a [[Metre (music)|compound meter]] that changes between {{music|time|6|8}} and {{music|time|9|8}} time signatures throughout most of the song, with the ending in compound septuple meter {{music|time|21|8}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicnotes.com/sheetmusic/mtd.asp?ppn=MN0204531|title=Taylor Swift "Beautiful Ghosts"|website=www.musicnotes.com|access-date=2019-11-18|date=November 15, 2019}}</ref> The orchestral ballad started as an acoustic melody composed by Lloyd Webber.<ref name="HR" /> During rehearsals in December 2018 at his London studio, he played Swift the melody on the piano, and Swift immediately started improvising lyrics.<ref name="VarietyOct2019">{{cite magazine|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/taylor-swift-andrew-lloyd-webber-co-write-new-song-cats-film-1203382162/|title=Taylor Swift and Andrew Lloyd Webber Have Co-Written a New Song for 'Cats'|last=Burlingame|first=Jon|magazine=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=October 24, 2019|accessdate=October 25, 2019}}</ref><ref name="SwiftBillboard"/> According to Lloyd Webber, the two "wrote 90 percent of it pretty much over an afternoon."<ref name="VarietyOct2019"/> He described the collaboration with Swift as "a joy", the collaboration as "one of the finest of [his] 50-year career",<ref>{{cite tweet|user=OfficialALW|last=Lloyd Webber|first=Andrew|author-link=Andrew Lloyd Webber|number=1196375543912042496|title=Thrilled at the reaction to "Beautiful Ghosts". This collaboration with @taylorswift13 has been a joy, one of the finest of my 50-year career.|date=November 18, 2019|accessdate=November 23, 2019}}</ref> Swift's lyrics as "brilliant", and her performance of the song as "emotional" and "among the very best [he's] ever had".
<ref>{{cite tweet|title=Taylor's lyric for Beautiful Ghosts is brilliant and her emotional performance of our song must be among the very best I've ever had.|user=OfficialALW|last=Lloyd Webber|first=Andrew|author-link=Andrew Lloyd Webber|number=1201202735833649154|date=December 1, 2019|accessdate=December 2, 2019}}</ref> [[Tom Hooper]], director of the film and a producer of the song, complimented the "extraordinary beauty" of Swift's lyrics and praised Swift for her "profound understanding of what we're trying to do with the movie."<ref name="PeopleNov2019">{{cite magazine|url=https://people.com/music/taylor-swift-cats-song-beautiful-ghosts/|title=Taylor Swift Shows Off Her Impressive Vocals in New Song 'Beautiful Ghosts' from Cats Musical Film|last1=Aderoju|first1=Darlene|last2=Goldstein|first2=Joelle|website=[[People (magazine)|People]]|language=en|date=November 15, 2019|access-date=November 15, 2019}}</ref>

== Critical reception ==

The song received positive reviews from music critics, who praised Swift's vocal performance. Darlene Aderoju and Joelle Goldstein of ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' described the song as "chillingly-beautiful" and a "haunting melody" that "speaks to a feeling of longing to be wanted and reminiscing on better memories." They also praised Swift's singing, describing it as "impressive pipes beyond her typical country-pop style", especially the final belted note.<ref name="PeopleNov2019"/> Writing for ''[[HuffPost]]'', Ron Dicker complimented Swift's vocals, referring to the song as "hauntingly gorgeous" and an "absolute showstopper".<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/taylor-swift-cats-beautiful-ghosts_n_5dcea458e4b029474815a10a |title=Taylor Swift's New Song For 'Cats' Is An Absolute Showstopper |last=Dicker |first=Ron |magazine=[[HuffPost]] |date=November 15, 2019 |accessdate=November 15, 2019}}</ref> ''[[Elite Daily]]'''s Jessica Bolaños opined that the song is "nothing short of perfection" and stated that the lyrics are "dripping with passion".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/taylor-swifts-beautiful-ghosts-lyrics-will-make-you-so-emotional-19352653|title=Taylor Swift's "Beautiful Ghosts" Lyrics Will Make You So Emotional|last=Bolaños|first=Jessica|magazine=[[Elite Daily]]|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=November 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/taylor-swifts-beautiful-ghosts-from-cats-shows-off-her-english-accent-19352088|title=Taylor Swift's "Beautiful Ghosts" From 'Cats' Shows Off Her English Accent|last=Bolaños|first=Jessica|magazine=[[Elite Daily]]|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=November 15, 2019}}</ref> Chris Willman of ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' wrote that the song is a return to the "youthful tonality" in Swift's voice, typical of "her ''[[Fearless (Taylor Swift album)|Fearless]]'' days".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/taylor-swift-releases-beautiful-ghosts-andrew-lloyd-webber-cats-1203406052/|title=Taylor Swift Releases 'Beautiful Ghosts,' Her Andrew Lloyd Webber Collaboration for 'Cats'|last=Willman|first=Chris|magazine=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=November 16, 2019}}</ref> Brittany Spanos and Ryan Reeds of ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' described the song as "cinematic" and "wistful". They also opined that the song "builds to a rousing climax with the singer's voice in a full roar".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/taylor-swift-beautiful-ghosts-cats-movie-new-song-911988/|title=Hear Taylor Swift's New 'Cats' Soundtrack Song 'Beautiful Ghosts'|last1=Spanos|first1=Brittany|last2=Reeds|first2=Ryan|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=November 16, 2019}}</ref> ''[[MTV News|MTV]]''<nowiki/>'s Madeline Roth praised Swift's vocal performance and labeled the final high note of the song as "more powerful than anything we've heard from T. Swift in recent memory".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3146069/taylor-swift-beautiful-ghosts-cats/|title=Taylor Swift Drops Her Pitch-Purrfect Cats Ballad 'Beautiful Ghosts'|last=Roth|first=Madeline|website=[[MTV News|MTV]]|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=November 16, 2019}}</ref> Carolyn Droke of ''[[Uproxx]]'' wrote that the song is about "the melancholy feeling that comes with reminiscing on old memories" and appreciated Swift for showing off her vocal range.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://uproxx.com/pop/best-new-pop-taylor-swift-camila-cabello-billie-eilish/|title=All The Best New Pop Music From This Week|last=Droke|first=Carolyn|website=[[Uproxx]]|date=November 19, 2019|accessdate=November 20, 2019}}</ref>

Others gave the song less favourable reviews. Adam Feldman wrote for ''[[Time Out (magazine)|Time Out]]'' that "Beautiful Ghosts" is "terrible", saying that "Swift's lyrics clank with banality at nearly every turn of phrase".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.timeout.com/newyork/news/the-new-taylor-swift-song-from-cats-has-dropped-and-its-terrible-111519|title=The Taylor Swift song from Cats has dropped and it's terrible|last=Feldman|first=Adam|website=Time Out New York|date=November 15, 2019|access-date=December 10, 2019}}</ref> Johnny Oleksinski of the ''[[New York Post]]'' agreed, calling the song "a boring '[[Memory (Cats song)|Memory]]' copycat", "an awkward fit" and "a desperate attempt to be affably plain".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2019/11/15/taylor-swifts-beautiful-ghosts-is-a-boring-memory-copycat/|title=Taylor Swift's 'Beautiful Ghosts' is a boring 'Memory' copycat|last=Oleksinski|first=Johnny|website=New York Post|date=November 15, 2019|access-date=December 10, 2019}}</ref> Leah Marilla Thomas of ''[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]'' praised the final chorus, but described the song as "a little bonkers" and said that listeners need to lower their expectations before listening.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/music/a29833498/taylor-swift-cats-song-lyrics-explained/|title=Taylor Swift's 'Cats' Song Is Here and There's Sooo Much to Unpack|last=Thomas|first=Leah Marilla|website=Cosmopolitan|date=November 18, 2019|access-date=December 10, 2019}}</ref> Various ''[[Vulture magazine|Vulture]]'' staff also criticized the song, describing the lyrics as nonsensical and corny; Swift's vocal performance was criticised, with Rebecca Alter summarizing the song as Swift "leaping for that note and falling off a cliff".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/12/taylor-swift-beautiful-ghosts-cats-explained.html|title=Let Us Gather to Discuss Taylor Swift and Andrew Lloyd Webber's Cats Song|date=December 9, 2019|website=Vulture|language=en-us|access-date=December 11, 2019}}</ref>

The song received a nomination from the [[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]] in the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] category at the [[77th Golden Globe Awards]], giving Swift her third career Golden Globe nomination.<ref name="deadline golden globes">{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/12/golden-globes-nominations-announced-updating-live-1202804135/|title=Golden Globes Nominations|last=Hipes|first=Patrick|website=Deadline|date=December 9, 2019|access-date=December 10, 2019}}</ref>

== Lyric video ==
On November 15, 2019, a lyric video for the song was released on [[YouTube]]. The lyric video has over 6 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{Citation|author=Swift, Taylor|title=Taylor Swift - Beautiful Ghosts (From The Motion Picture "Cats" / Lyric Video)|website=[[YouTube]]|date=November 15, 2019|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=trIjpVH8h88|accessdate=November 30, 2019|author-link=Taylor Swift}}</ref>

==Accolades==
The song was nominated for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] at the [[77th Golden Globe Awards]], becoming Swift's third nomination in the category, following "[[Safe & Sound (Taylor Swift song)|Safe & Sound]]" (2013) and "[[Sweeter than Fiction]]" (2014). At the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]], "Beautiful Ghosts" is nominated for [[Best song written for visual media|Best Song Written for Visual Media]], marking Swift's third nomination in the category, following "[[Safe & Sound (Taylor Swift song)|Safe & Sound]]" (2011) and "[[I Don't Wanna Live Forever]]" (2016).
