'''ActBlue''' is an American nonprofit technology organization established in June 2004 that enables left-leaning nonprofits, [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] candidates, and [[Progressivism in the United States|progressive]] groups to raise money from individual donors on the Internet by providing them with online fundraising software. Its stated mission is to "empower small-dollar donors".<ref name=Somerville>{{cite news|last1=Pindell|first1=James|title=How a Somerville nonprofit revolutionized American politics|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/magazine/2017/05/09/how-somerville-actblue-revolutionized-american-politics/Vev2sgidKycWKePKcmg65N/story.html|work=The Boston Globe|date=10 May 2017|access-date=11 April 2018|archive-date=12 April 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180412001140/https://www.bostonglobe.com/magazine/2017/05/09/how-somerville-actblue-revolutionized-american-politics/Vev2sgidKycWKePKcmg65N/story.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Activities==
[[File:Mark Warner and Ciro Rodriguez at ActBlue DC Fundraiser (170505792).jpg|thumb|Co-founder Benjamin Rahn at a fundraiser in 2006]]
ActBlue does not endorse individual candidates.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Willis |first1=Derek |title=How ActBlue Became a Powerful Force in Fund-Raising |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/10/09/upshot/how-actblue-became-a-powerful-force-in-fund-raising.html |accessdate=23 March 2020 |work=The New York Times |date=9 October 2014 |archive-date=4 March 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200304154359/https://www.nytimes.com/2014/10/09/upshot/how-actblue-became-a-powerful-force-in-fund-raising.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The organization is open to Democratic campaigns, candidates, committees, and progressive [[501(c) organization|501(c)4 organizations]]. Groups that use ActBlue pay a 3.95% credit card processing fee. As a nonprofit, ActBlue runs its own, separate fundraising program and accepts tips on contributions to pay for its expenses.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kroll|first1=Andy|title=The $2 Billion Powerhouse Behind Jon Ossoff|url=https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2017/06/the-2-billion-powerhouse-behind-jon-ossoff/|issue=July/August 2017|work=Mother Jones|access-date=2018-04-11|archive-date=2018-04-11|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180411174725/https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2017/06/the-2-billion-powerhouse-behind-jon-ossoff/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=Somerville /><ref>{{cite web|title=Pricing|url=https://secure.actblue.com/pricing|website=ActBlue.com|publisher=ActBlue|access-date=2017-07-24|archive-date=2017-07-12|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170712160439/https://secure.actblue.com/pricing|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Federal Election Commission reporting==
ActBlue reports to the [[Federal Election Commission]] all contributors to Federal campaigns, regardless of the amount.<ref>{{Cite web |title=What happens to my money when I donate? |url=https://support.actblue.com/donors/contributions/what-happens-to-my-money-when-i-donate/ |access-date=2020-07-14 |quote=As required by federal law, ActBlue reports and itemizes (that means list the donor name & information) for every single federal donation that comes through our platform, including donations under $200. |archive-date=2020-07-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200714065809/https://support.actblue.com/donors/contributions/what-happens-to-my-money-when-i-donate/ |url-status=live }}</ref> When a candidate for a Federal election raises money through ActBlue, ActBlue serves as a ''conduit'' for election law purposes.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Earmarked contributions|url=https://www.fec.gov/updates/earmarked-contributions/|access-date=2020-07-14|website=FEC.gov|language=en|archive-date=2020-07-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200714053849/https://www.fec.gov/updates/earmarked-contributions/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Why is ActBlue considered a PAC? {{!}} ActBlue Support|url=https://support.actblue.com/donors/about-actblue/why-is-actblue-considered-a-pac/|access-date=2020-07-14|archive-date=2020-07-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200724225021/https://support.actblue.com/donors/about-actblue/why-is-actblue-considered-a-pac/|url-status=live}}</ref> All conduit contributions are itemized and reported. By contrast, there is a $200 threshold for reporting individuals who contribute directly to a candidate committee. Many small donors, whose names would ordinarily be shielded, are thus exposed to the public.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Primo|first=David M.|date=18 August 2019|title=Personal Data About Small-Donor Democrats Is All Over the Internet|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/08/18/opinion/democrats-donor-data.html|access-date=2020-07-14|issn=0362-4331|archive-date=2020-07-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200716215850/https://www.nytimes.com/2019/08/18/opinion/democrats-donor-data.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Fundraising==

ActBlue raised $19 million in its first three years, from 2004 to 2007.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Mosk|first1=Matthew|date=11 March 2007|title=Donations Pooled Online Are Getting Candidates' Attention|work=[[Washington Post]]|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/03/10/AR2007031001185_pf.html|access-date=20 September 2017|archive-date=13 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713170352/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/03/10/AR2007031001185_pf.html|url-status=live}}</ref> In the 2005-2006 campaign, the site raised $17 million for 1500 Democratic candidates, with $15.5 million going to congressional campaigns. By August 2007, the site had raised $25.5 million.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Helman|first1=Scott|date=7 August 2007|title=Internet-based PAC driving Democratic push|work=The Boston Globe|url=http://archive.boston.com/news/local/massachusetts/articles/2007/08/07/internet_based_pac_driving_democratic_push/|access-date=23 July 2020|archive-date=21 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200921135910/http://archive.boston.com/news/local/massachusetts/articles/2007/08/07/internet_based_pac_driving_democratic_push/|url-status=live}}</ref>

In the 2018 midterms elections, ActBlue raised $1.6 billion for Democratic candidates.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Isenstadt |first1=Alex |title=GOP to launch new fundraising site as Dems crush the online money game |url=https://www.politico.com/story/2019/06/23/republicans-win-red-2020-1377058 |accessdate=23 March 2020 |work=POLITICO |date=23 June 2019 |language=en |archive-date=16 March 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200316194742/https://www.politico.com/story/2019/06/23/republicans-win-red-2020-1377058 |url-status=live }}</ref>  According to FEC data, from January 2017 to October 2018 [[Beto Oâ€™Rourke]] had raised $45 million through ActBlue for his unsuccessful run against [[Ted Cruz]], 48% of which came from outside Texas.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lavine |first1=Carrie |last2=Zubak-Skess |first2=Chris |others=Graphics by Rachael Dottle |title=How ActBlue Is Trying To Turn Small Donations Into A Blue Wave |url=https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/how-actblue-is-trying-to-turn-small-donations-into-a-blue-wave/ |website=[[Fivethirtyeight]] |publisher=[[ABC News]] |date=October 25, 2018 |access-date=May 30, 2019 |archive-date=May 30, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190530195918/https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/how-actblue-is-trying-to-turn-small-donations-into-a-blue-wave/ |url-status=live }}</ref>  

In 2019, ActBlue raised roughly $1 billion for a wide variety of campaigns.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hakim |first1=Danny |last2=Thrush |first2=Glenn |title=How the Trump Campaign Took Over the G.O.P. |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/09/us/trump-campaign-brad-parscale.html |accessdate=28 March 2020 |work=The New York Times |date=9 March 2020 |archive-date=31 March 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200331093500/https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/09/us/trump-campaign-brad-parscale.html |url-status=live }}</ref> ''The Daily Beast'' notes that between January and mid-July 2019, ActBlue brought in $420 million, and that "According to the organization, that total came from 3.3 million unique donors and was dispersed to almost 9,000 Democratic campaigns and organizations, with $246 million coming in the second quarter alone."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Resnick |first1=Gideon |title=ActBlue Has Brought in a Whopping $420 Million This Year |url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/actblue-the-democratic-small-dollar-fundraising-platform-has-brought-in-a-whopping-dollar420-million-this-year |accessdate=24 April 2020 |work=The Daily Beast |date=17 July 2019 |language=en |archive-date=24 July 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200724231511/https://www.thedailybeast.com/actblue-the-democratic-small-dollar-fundraising-platform-has-brought-in-a-whopping-dollar420-million-this-year |url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2020, several fundraising records were broken. In the week following the [[killing of George Floyd]], on May 31st, over $19 million was raised, the highest single-day total so far that year. On June 1st, that yearly record was again broken with $20 million in donations. Over half of donations in the week following the killing went to charitable (non-political) causes, including one ActBlue page devoted to a [[bail fund]] which raised over $1.5 million from over 20,000 donors.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Goldmacher|first1=Shane|date=1 June 2020|title=Protests Spur Surge in Donations, Giving ActBlue Its Biggest Day of the Year|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/06/01/us/politics/donations-protests-actblue-democrats.html|accessdate=2 June 2020|archive-date=2 June 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200602005649/https://www.nytimes.com/2020/06/01/us/politics/donations-protests-actblue-democrats.html|url-status=live}}</ref> In the day following [[Death and state funeral of Ruth Bader Ginsburg|the death]] of Supreme Court Justice [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]], over $30 million was donated through ActBlue, again breaking the single-day fundraising record.<ref>{{Cite web|title=ActBlue shatters donation record in hours after RBG's death|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/politics/actblue-shatters-donation-record-in-hours-after-rbgs-death/ar-BB19deT4?ocid=uxbndlbing|access-date=2020-09-20|website=www.msn.com}}</ref>
