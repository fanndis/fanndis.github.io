'''Lady Louise Alice Elizabeth Mary Mountbatten-Windsor'''<ref name=huf>{{cite web |title=Mountbatten-Windsor? |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/will-bower/prince-george-last-name_b_3667750.html |accessdate=2 August 2014 |date=29 July 2013}}</ref> (born 8 November 2003) is a member of the [[British royal family]]. She is the elder child and only daughter of [[Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex]], and [[Sophie, Countess of Wessex]]. Louise is the youngest<!--Charlotte is a great-grandaughter--> granddaughter of [[Queen Elizabeth II]] and [[Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh]]. She is 13th in the [[line of succession to the British throne]].

==Birth and baptism==
Lady Louise Windsor was born prematurely on 8 November 2003 at 23:32 GMT at [[Frimley Park Hospital]] in [[Frimley]], [[Surrey]], after her mother was rushed there by ambulance from the Wessex home at [[Bagshot Park]], Surrey. Prince Edward was not present for the birth because he was in [[Mauritius]] and because it occurred so suddenly.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/onthisday/hi/dates/stories/november/8/newsid_3724000/3724342.stm |title=Royal baby born prematurely |work=[[BBC News]] |date=8 November 2003 |accessdate=11 May 2010}}</ref> Louise was delivered by emergency [[Caesarean section]] due to [[placental abruption]], which caused severe blood loss to both child and mother. Louise was transferred to a neo-natal unit in [[St George's Hospital]], [[Tooting]], London, as a precaution. Meanwhile, her mother remained at Frimley Park until she was well enough to be discharged, on 23 November 2003. Louise's name was announced on 27 November.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/3240966.stm |title=Royal Wessex baby finally named |work=BBC News |date=27 November 2003 |accessdate=7 May 2010}}</ref>

She was baptised in the Private Chapel of [[Windsor Castle]] on 24 April 2004 by [[David Conner (bishop)|David Conner]], the [[Dean of Windsor]]; her godparents were [[Lady Sarah Chatto]], [[Lord Ivar Mountbatten]], Lady Alexandra Etherington, [[Urs Schwarzenbach|Francesca Schwarzenbach]] and Rupert Elliott.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.royal.uk/announcement-christening-lady-louise-windsor |title=Announcement of the christening of Lady Louise Windsor |publisher=royal.uk |accessdate=8 April 2004 |date=7 April 2004}}</ref><ref name="Yvonne">{{cite web |url=http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/christenings.htm#Christenings |title=Royal Christenings |publisher=Yvonne's Royalty |accessdate=15 October 2011 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/61Fn0l7vK?url=http://users.uniserve.com/~canyon/christenings.htm#Christenings |archive-date=27 August 2011 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Louise was the last child to wear the original [[royal christening gown]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/prince-george/10975852/Prince-Georges-christening-gown-the-true-story.html |title=Prince George's christening gown: the true story |work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]] |accessdate=19 July 2014 |date=19 July 2014 |last1=Ranscombe |first1=Siân}}</ref>

Born with [[esotropia]], Louise underwent an operation in an unsuccessful attempt to correct the problem in January 2006.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.debretts.com/people/royal-family/royal-portraits/louise-windsor.aspx |title=Lady Louise Windsor |publisher=Debrett's |accessdate=2 January 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717050159/http://www.debretts.com/people/royal-family/royal-portraits/louise-windsor.aspx |archivedate=17 July 2011 |df=dmy-all}}</ref> She had further treatment in late 2013 that corrected her vision.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/theroyalfamily/10570166/Prince-Edwards-daughter-has-successful-operation-to-correct-squint.html |title=Prince Edward's daughter has successful operation to correct squint |date=14 January 2014 |work=The Telegraph}}</ref>

==Education==
Louise attended [[St George's School, Windsor Castle]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ladymollyparker.tumblr.com/post/121669168621/the-lady-louise-mountbatten-windsor-watches-and |title=The Lady Louise Mountbatten-Windsor |publisher=ladymollyparker.tumblr.com |accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref> before moving to [[St Mary's School Ascot]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.st-marys-ascot.co.uk/gallery/default.asp?pid=165&gcatid=18&albumid=73 |title=Year 9 Team-building day |publisher=st-marys-ascot.co.uk |accessdate=12 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612211103/http://www.st-marys-ascot.co.uk/gallery/default.asp?pid=165&gcatid=18&albumid=73 |archive-date=12 June 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref> in 2017 for her [[year 9]] school year.

==Personal interests==
Louise is a member of [[Girlguiding]], of which her grandmother is patron and her mother is president.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://guidingmanual.guk.org.uk/supporting_info/roles_in_guiding/national_roles.aspx |title= Roles in Guiding |publisher=The Guide Association}}</ref> Her mother was a [[Brownies (Scouting)|Brownie]] and a Guide when she was a child.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sophie celebrates 100 years of the Brownies with Lady Louise |url=http://royalcentral.co.uk/wessexes/sophie-celebrates-100-years-of-the-brownies-with-lady-louise-33520 |accessdate=2 November 2014 |date=27 June 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201035030/http://royalcentral.co.uk/wessexes/sophie-celebrates-100-years-of-the-brownies-with-lady-louise-33520 |archive-date=1 December 2017 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

She was taught to ride at a young age, and joined her father on horseback as part of the Queen's 90th birthday celebrations in Windsor in 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hellomagazine.com/royalty/2016051631434/queen-family-birthday-pageant/ |title=The Queen's delight as family take part in birthday pageant |work=Hello! |accessdate=16 May 2016 |date=16 May 2016}}</ref> She has taken up [[Driving (horse)|carriage driving]], a sport popularised in Britain by her grandfather, Prince Philip.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hellomagazine.com/royalty/2017062640135/lady-louise-windsor-carriage-driving/ |title=Lady Louise Windsor, 13, takes after grandfather Prince Philip carriage driving |work=Hello! |accessdate=26 June 2017 |date=26 June 2017}}</ref> In May 2017, she was responsible for leading the carriages of the Champagne Laurent-Perrier Meet of the British Driving Society at the [[Royal Windsor Horse Show]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.horseandhound.co.uk/news/lady-louise-queens-grandaughter-royal-windsor-carriage-driving-620806 |title=Queen's granddaughter makes Royal Windsor driving debut |work=[[Horse & Hound]] |accessdate=17 May 2017 |date=17 May 2017}}</ref>

In May 2019, Louise took part in the Private Driving Singles carriage drive at the [[Royal Windsor Horse Show]] and achieved third place.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.express.co.uk/life-style/style/1124974/sophie-countess-of-wessex-lady-louise-windsor-children-prince-edward-rhys-jones|title=Sophie, Countess of Wessex cuts casual figure as she supports daughter Lady Louise Windsor|date=9 May 2019|publisher=express.co.uk|accessdate=9 May 2019}}</ref>

==Titles, styles, and honours==
===Titles and styles===
{{see|Mountbatten-Windsor}}
She is styled as "Lady Louise Mountbatten-Windsor",<ref name="Official Statement">[http://www.royal.gov.uk/LatestNewsandDiary/Pressreleases/2004/FamilyphotographsofTheEarlandCountessofWessexwitht.aspx Statement issued by the Press Secretary to the Queen: Announcement of the christening of Lady Louise Windsor] – The official website of The British Monarchy</ref> although [[letters patent]] issued in 1917, and still in force,  assign a princely status and the style of ''[[Royal Highness]]'' to all children of a monarch's sons.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=30428 |page=13086 |date=14 December 1917 }}</ref> Consequently she is entitled to the style and title of "Her Royal Highness Princess Louise of Wessex". However, when her parents married, the Queen, via a [[Buckingham Palace]] press release, announced that their children would be styled as the children of an earl, rather than as prince or princess.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20140201141036/http://www.gov-news.org/gov/uk/news/title_of_hrh_the_prince_edward/40309.html UK Government News – 19th June, 1999: TITLE OF HRH THE PRINCE EDWARD] (Accessed 18 January 2014)</ref> Thus, court communications refer to her as ''Lady Louise Windsor''.<ref name="debrett">{{cite book |title=Debrett's Peerage & Baronetage 2008 |year=2008 |isbn=978-1870520805 |publisher=Debrett's Peerage Limited |last1=Kidd |first1=Charles |last2=Shaw |first2=Christine}}</ref> In 2020, the Countess of Wessex stated that Louise retained her royal title and style and will make a choice on whether to use it when she turns 18.<ref>{{cite web|title=Queen Elizabeth's Grandchildren Will 'Work for a Living' and Won't Use HRH, Says Mom Sophie Wessex|url=https://people.com/royals/queen-elizabeths-grandchildren-will-work-for-a-living-mom-sophie-wessex-says/|access-date=2020-06-09|website=PEOPLE.com}}</ref>
