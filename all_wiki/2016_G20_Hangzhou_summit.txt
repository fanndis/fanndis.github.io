The '''2016 G20 Hangzhou summit''' was the [[List of G20 summits|eleventh]] meeting of the [[Group of Twenty]] (G20). It was held on 4–5 September 2016 in the city of [[Hangzhou]], [[Zhejiang]]. It was the first ever G20 summit to be hosted in China<ref name="Lelièvre"/> and the second in an [[Asian countries|Asian country]] after [[2010 G20 Seoul summit]] was hosted in South Korea.

=== Pollution in China ===

In the days before the G20 Hangzhou summit, only some [[vehicle]]s were allowed through the city, construction sites were stopped, inhabitants received a week-long holidays (and were encouraged to leave the city) and [[Industry of China|factories]] in the region (including more than two hundred [[steel mill]]s) were asked to stop their production (in order to temporarily reduce air [[Pollution in China|pollution]]).<ref name="Lelièvre">{{in lang|fr}} Frédéric Lelièvre, [https://www.letemps.ch/economie/2016/08/29/chine-championne-monde-finance-verte "La Chine championne du monde de la finance verte"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160914142904/https://www.letemps.ch/economie/2016/08/29/chine-championne-monde-finance-verte |date=14 September 2016 }}, ''[[Le temps]]'', 29 August 2016 (page visited on 2 September 2016).</ref><ref>Tom Phillips, [https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/sep/05/ghost-town-how-china-emptied-hangzhou-to-guarantee-perfect-g20 "Ghost town: how China emptied Hangzhou to guarantee 'perfect' G20"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160924013139/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/sep/05/ghost-town-how-china-emptied-hangzhou-to-guarantee-perfect-g20 |date=24 September 2016 }}, ''[[The Guardian]]'', Monday 5 September 2016 (page visited on 5 September 2016).</ref><ref>[[Reuters]], [https://www.nytimes.com/reuters/2016/09/03/world/asia/03reuters-g20-china-hangzhou.html?_r=0 "China's Hangzhou Turns Ghost Town as G20 Leaders Arrive"], ''[[The New York Times]]'', 3 September 2016 (page visited on 4 September 2016).</ref><ref>A similar approach was used for the [[2008 Summer Olympics]] in [[Beijing]], see [[Concerns and controversies at the 2008 Summer Olympics#Air pollution|Air pollution at the 2008 S15 China Victory Day Parade]].</ref>

=== Economic statements ===

The main themes of final communiqué of the summit are:<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.lemonde.fr/economie-mondiale/article/2016/09/05/accord-du-g20-pour-s-opposer-au-protectionnisme-et-lutter-contre-l-evasion-fiscale_4992783_1656941.html "Accord du G20 pour « s’opposer au protectionnisme » et lutter contre l’évasion fiscale"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160908220216/http://www.lemonde.fr//economie-mondiale/article/2016/09/05/accord-du-g20-pour-s-opposer-au-protectionnisme-et-lutter-contre-l-evasion-fiscale_4992783_1656941.html |date=8 September 2016 }}, ''[[Le monde]]'', 5 September 2016 (page visited on 5 September 2016).</ref>
* Fight against [[tax evasion]] (asking the [[Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development]] for a [[black list]] of [[tax haven]]s).
* Favour [[international trade]] and investments and opposition to [[protectionism]].
* [[Stimulus (economics)|Fiscal stimulus]] and innovation to boost [[economic growth]].
* Combating "[[populist]] attacks" against [[globalisation]].
* Strengthen support for [[European migrant crisis|refugees]].

== Controversy ==
=== Internet censorship ===
Xi Jinping in the speech quoted "[[Guoyu (book)|Guoyu]]", Traditional Chinese history books, in Chapter 9 of the "{{lang|zh-hans|{{linktext|通商|宽|农}}}}" ({{transl|zh|tōng shāng kuān nóng}}, meaning reducing taxes and make road easy to walk, promote commercialization, and relax agriculture<ref>{{cite web|title=Language Log » Annals of literary vs. vernacular, part 2|url=http://languagelog.ldc.upenn.edu/nll/?p=27882|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170617204754/http://languagelog.ldc.upenn.edu/nll/?p=27882|archivedate=17 June 2017|df=dmy-all}}</ref>), but he mistakenly pronounced it as "{{lang|zh-hans|通商宽衣}}" ("{{lang|zh-hans|{{linktext|宽|衣}}}}" {{transl|zh|kuān yī}} means Undress), the [[Central Propaganda Department]] ordered media and social platforms to prohibit this discussion.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=zh:中国网络审查员狂删习近平“宽衣”口误议论帖|url=http://www.voachinese.com/a/news-president-xi-speech-slip-up-keeps-china-censors-busy-20160905/3494215.html|accessdate=1 September 2017|publisher=[[Voice of America]]|date=6 September 2016|language=zh|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170828185412/https://www.voachinese.com/a/news-president-xi-speech-slip-up-keeps-china-censors-busy-20160905/3494215.html|archivedate=28 August 2017|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
