[[Serena Williams]] defeated her sister [[Venus Williams]] in the final 7–5, 6–3 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2002 French Open]]. This was also her first step towards completing her first [[Serena Williams#2002–03: "Serena Slam"|"Serena Slam"]]. [[Jennifer Capriati]] was the defending champion, but she lost to Serena Williams in the semifinals, in what was a rematch of a match from [[2001 French Open – Women's Singles|the previous year's]] quarterfinals.

This tournament was notable for being the first Grand Slam in which future world No. 2 [[Vera Zvonareva]] competed in the main draw.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.tennisrulz.com/players/zvonareva/biography.php |title=Welcome to this Vera Zvonareva webpage! |access-date=2013-03-05 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130411221022/http://www.tennisrulz.com/players/zvonareva/biography.php |archive-date=2013-04-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She drew Serena Williams in the fourth round, and was defeated.
