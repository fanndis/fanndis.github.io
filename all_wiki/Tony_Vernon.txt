'''W. Anthony "Tony" Vernon''' is a business executive who was the first [[chief executive officer]] of [[Kraft Foods]] after it was spun off from the [[Mondelēz International]] in 2012. Prior to his time at Kraft, he had a 23-year career at [[Johnson & Johnson]] and spent three years with [[Ripplewood Holdings]]. When he took on this position his salary increased to $1m per annum.  Vernon’s overall compensation was $9.19 million for 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Wohl|first1=Jessica|title=Kraft CEO pulls in more than $9 million in pay in 2013|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2014-03-14/business/chi-kraft-ceo-pulls-in-more-than-9-million-in-pay-in-2013-20140314_1_kraft-ceo-oscar-mayer-chief-executive-tony-vernon|publisher=Chicago Tribune|accessdate=22 March 2015}}</ref>

==Education==
Vernon holds a [[bachelor's degree]] from [[Lawrence University]] in history and a [[Master of Business Administration]] from the [[Kellogg Graduate School of Management]] at [[Northwestern University]].<ref name=KFNTVPKFN>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=aDUqG0ZWY77A|title=Kraft Foods Names Tony Vernon President, Kraft Foods North |accessdate=2014-12-19|date=2009-06-22|publisher=[[Bloomberg News]]}}</ref>

==Career==
During his 23-year career at Johnson & Johnson, Vernon ran independent business units and led consumer brands such as [[Tylenol (brand)|Tylenol]], [[Motrin]], [[Pepcid AC]], [[Imodium]] and
[[Splenda]].<ref name=KFNTVPKFN/> He then served as healthcare industry partner of Ripplewood Holdings for three years before joining Kraft in 2009.<ref name=KFNTVPKFN/> In June 2009, Kraft Foods Inc. announced that Vernon would become President of Kraft Foods North America that August, replacing Rick Searer.<ref name=KFNTVPKFN/> In December 2011, when the company announced that it would reorganize in 2012, Vernon was announced as the CEO of the grocery business that became the current incarnation of Kraft.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2011-12-05/business/chi-kraft-chooses-leaders-for-separate-companies-20111205_1_ceo-irene-rosenfeld-kraft-chairman-kraft-foods-north-america|title=Kraft chooses leaders for separate companies|accessdate=2014-12-19|date=2011-12-05|work=[[Chicago Tribune]]|author=York, Emily Bryson}}</ref> After [[Mondelēz International]] spun off Kraft on October 1, 2012, Vernon became CEO.<ref name=KNGCPMBiSoR>{{cite web|url=http://adage.com/article/news/kraft-s-grocery-company-plans-marketing-boost/237084/|title=Kraft's New Grocery Company Plans Marketing Boost in Search of 'Renaissance': CEO Tony Vernon Outlines Plans in Advance of Oct. 1 Split|accessdate=2014-12-19|date=2012-09-07|work=[[Advertising Age]]|author=Schultz, E. J.}}</ref> At the time of the split, Kraft Foods (which included brands such as [[Oscar Mayer]], [[Planters]], [[Velveeta]], [[Kool-Aid]], [[Miracle Whip]], [[Capri Sun]], [[A1 Steak Sauce|A1]], [[Cool Whip]] and [[Jell-O]]) was the fourth-largest food and beverage company in North America, trailing [[PepsiCo]], [[Nestle]] and [[Coca-Cola]].<ref name=KNGCPMBiSoR/> His salary jumped by 49% during his first year as CEO to $6.83 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-04-05/business/chi-krafts-vernon-sees-pay-rise-49-as-ceo-20130405_1_kraft-macaroni-cheese-vernon-pay-increase|title=Kraft's Vernon sees pay rise 49% as CEO|accessdate=2014-12-26|date=2013-04-05|work=[[Chicago Tribune]]|author=York, Emily Bryson}}</ref> On December 18, 2014, the company announced that Vernon intended to retire as CEO effective from December 28, and that the company planned to replace him with Chairman John Cahill.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/kraft-foods-group-ceo-to-retire-names-chairman-successor-1418912458|title=Kraft Foods Announces CEO Change: Chairman Cahill Set to Replace Vernon, Spur Changes at Food Company|accessdate=2014-12-19|date=2014-12-18|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|author= Gasparro, Annie and Joann S. Lublin}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/w-vernon/ |title=W Vernon: Profile |work=Forbes |accessdate=2015-01-05}}</ref>
