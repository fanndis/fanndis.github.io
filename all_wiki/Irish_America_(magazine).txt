'''''Irish America''''' is a bi-monthly periodical that aims to cover topics relevant to the Irish in North America including a range of political, economic, social, and cultural themes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/who-we-are/about-the-magazine/|title=About the Magazine {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> The magazine’s inaugural issue was published in October 1985.<ref>[https://issuu.com/irishamerica/docs/october_1985.r Irish American October 1985]</ref> ''Irish America'' focuses on political and business leaders, organizations, artists, writers and community figures among the Irish in America through its annual lists, awards, and events including the Wall Street 50, Business 100, and Stars of the South.

''Irish America''{{'s}} primary goals are to emphasize the achievements of [[Irish American]]s in each bi-monthly issue, such as former U.S. president [[Bill Clinton]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2016/03/bill-clinton-the-peacemaker/|title=Bill Clinton: The Peacemaker {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> former U.S. Secretary of State [[Hillary Clinton]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2015/03/hillary-clinton-inducted-to-irish-america-hall-of-fame-for-work-on-n-i-peace/|title=Weekly Comment:Hillary Clinton Inducted to Irish America Hall of Fame {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> former president of [[Coca-Cola]] and chairman of [[Allen & Company]] [[Donald R. Keough]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2005/10/20-great-interviewsdonald-keough/|title=20 Great Interviews:Donald Keough {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> comedian [[Kathy Griffin]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2011/12/kathy-griffin/|title=Kathy Griffin {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> film actress [[Maureen O'Hara]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2014/12/what-are-you-like-maureen-ohara/|title=What Are You Like? Maureen O’Hara {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> political commentators [[Chris Matthews]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2014/03/chris-matthews-political-commentator-author/|title=Chris Matthews: Political Commentator, Author {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> and [[Bill O'Reilly (political commentator)|Bill O'Reilly]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2014/03/bill-oreilly-broadcaster-author/|title=Bill O’Reilly: Broadcaster, Author {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref> and California governor [[Jerry Brown]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/2018/02/hall-of-fame-jerry-brown/|title=Hall of Fame: Jerry Brown {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref>

== History ==

[[Niall O'Dowd]] and [[Patricia Harty]] co-founded ''Irish America'' in 1985. Since then, it reports to have increased circulation to over 100,000 in the [[United States]], [[Ireland]] and [[Northern Ireland]]. Its writers and correspondents aim to cover news events relevant to the Irish American community, through news stories, features, and interviews. Irish America has celebrated Irish Americans from the worlds of politics, music, movies, theater, business, dance, books and academia to appear on the covers of the magazine over the years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://irishamerica.com/archives/2018-archive/april-may-2018/|title=April / May 2018 {{!}} Irish America|website=irishamerica.com|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2018}}</ref>

==Awards==
Many awards are given to Irish Americans by the magazine. The top Irish American people of the year are recognized in the selection of the [http://irishamerica.com/top-100/ Top 100]. The annual Wall Street 50<ref>[http://irishamerica.com/wall-street-50/ Wall Street 50]</ref> highlights the accomplishments of Irish Americans in the financial community, and there has been a Guest of Honor at every Wall Street 50 event.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.irishcentral.com/news/community/major-milestone-ahead-as-irish-america-magazine-celebrates-20th-wall-street-50-awards|title=Irish America magazine celebrates 20th Wall Street 50 awards|date=2017-09-29|work=IrishCentral.com|access-date=April 12, 2018|language=en}}</ref> The Business 100, meanwhile, features 100 honorees and one guest of honor at a luncheon that happens annually.<ref>[http://irishamerica.com/business-100/ The Business 100]</ref>

From 2006 to 2011, ''Irish America'' magazine also honored the southern United States with the [http://irishamerica.com/stars-of-the-south/ Stars of the South award]. The honorees were feted at an annual celebratory dinner in Atlanta.

In 2010, ''Irish America'' magazine began inducting members into the [[Irish America Hall of Fame]].
