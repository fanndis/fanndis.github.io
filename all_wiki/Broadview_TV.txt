'''Broadview TV GmbH''' is a German documentary film production company based in Cologne.

BROADVIEW produces documentaries for broadcast and cable networks in Germany and worldwide, including [[ZDF]], [[ARD (broadcaster)|ARD]], [[arte]], [[HBO]], [[A&E (TV channel)|A&E]], [[NHK]], [[RTL Television|RTL]], as well as for national and international Institutions including the [[Foreign Office (Germany)|German Foreign Office]] and the [[Commission of the European Union]]. The films have gone on to win the [[Emmy Award|International Emmy® Award]], the [[German Television Award]], the Magnolia Award and Banff Television Award. 

== History ==
Since its founding in 1999 by [[Leopold Hoesch (film producer)|Leopold Hoesch]], BROADVIEW TV is one of Germany's leading production companies in the field of historical and cultural documentaries. BROADVIEW TV has produced numerous documentaries for TV and cinema including the [[ARD (broadcaster)|ARD]]-series 'German Dynasties', the [[arte]]-series 'TOO young TO DIE' and the [[ZDF]]-series 'Theatre Portraits' with [[Esther Schweins]]. Under the label Broadview Pictures, the company produced the cinematic documentaries '[[:de:Klitschko (Film)|Klitschko]]' (2011) and '[[:de:Nowitzki. Der perfekte Wurf|Nowitzki. The Perfect Shot]]' (2014). Furthermore, Broadview TV shoots imagefilms for companies and institutions including the [[Foreign Office (Germany)|German Foreign Office]] and the [[European Union]].

Broadview TV is managed by [[Leopold Hoesch (film producer)|Leopold Hoesch]]. He represents [[Emmy Award|The International Academy Of Television Arts & Industry]] as Ambassador to Germany. Together with [[Guido Knopp]] and Sebastian Dehnhardt, Hoesch won the Emmy Award for Best Documentary for 'The Drama of Dresden' (2005).
