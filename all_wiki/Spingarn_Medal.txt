{{short description|Award for the highest achievement of an American of African descent}}
The '''Spingarn Medal''' is awarded annually by the [[NAACP|National Association for the Advancement of Colored People]] (NAACP) for outstanding achievement by an [[African Americans|African American]]. The award was created in 1914 by [[Joel Elias Spingarn]], chairman of the board of directors of the NAACP.<ref name="Britannica">{{cite encyclopedia|title=Spingarn Medal|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Spingarn-Medal|accessdate=November 11, 2017}}</ref> It was first awarded to biologist [[Ernest Everett Just|Ernest E. Just]] in 1915, and has been given most years thereafter.

At its annual convention, the NAACP presents the award after deciding from open nominations.<!--General ref.--> Should the organization end, it would be managed by [[Howard University|Howard]] or [[Fisk University|Fisk]] Universities.<ref name="Britannica"/> The gold medal is valued at $100, and Spingarn left $20,000 ({{Inflation|US|20000|1939|r=-3|fmt=eq}}) in his will for the NAACP to continue giving it indefinitely.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aaregistry.org/historic_events/view/award-excellence-spingarn-medal|title=An award of excellence, the Spingarn Medal|work=African American Registry|accessdate=November 11, 2017}}</ref>
