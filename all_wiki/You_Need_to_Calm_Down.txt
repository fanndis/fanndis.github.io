"'''You Need to Calm Down'''" is a song recorded by American singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]], released on June 14, 2019, as the second single from her seventh studio album, ''[[Lover (album)|Lover]]'' (2019). As the fourteenth track on the album, Swift wrote and produced the song with [[Joel Little]], who also co-wrote and co-produced her previous single "[[Me!]]". "You Need to Calm Down" is an upbeat, summery [[synth-pop]] and [[electropop]] song, with a chorus of plunging echoes. It sees Swift addressing [[Internet troll|internet trolls]] and [[homophobia|homophobes]], voicing her support for the [[LGBTQ+]] community.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/taylor-swift-releases-pro-glaad-single-you-need-to-calm-down-1203243277/|title=Taylor Swift Releases GLAAD-Boosting, Homophobia-Bashing 'You Need to Calm Down'|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=June 13, 2019|accessdate=June 13, 2019|first=Chris|last=Willman|archive-date=June 14, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190614054419/https://variety.com/2019/music/news/taylor-swift-releases-pro-glaad-single-you-need-to-calm-down-1203243277/|url-status=live}}</ref> An accompanying lyric video was released on June 14, 2019. The music video was released on June 17, featuring an [[ensemble cast]], directed by Swift and Drew Kirsch, and executive-produced by Swift and [[Todrick Hall]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2047760/taylor-swift-new-album-lover/news/|title=Taylor Swift Announces New Album Lover, Out In August|first=James|last=Rettig|website=[[Stereogum]]|date=June 13, 2019|accessdate=June 13, 2019|archive-date=June 13, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190613231606/https://www.stereogum.com/2047760/taylor-swift-new-album-lover/news/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="eonline">{{cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/news/1049245/taylor-swift-announces-new-album-lover-and-song-you-need-to-calm-down|title=Taylor Swift Announces New Album Lover and Song "You Need to Calm Down"|first=Jess|last=Cohen|website=[[E! "You Need to Calm Down" divided music critics; some praised the song for its catchiness and pro-LGBT message, while others criticized its message as confusing and cynical. The song debuted at number one in Scotland, while reaching the top five in Australia, Canada, Ireland, Malaysia, New Zealand and the United Kingdom. It debuted at the second spot on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], becoming the second consecutive top-two single from ''Lover'', thus tying Swift with [[Madonna]] for the most number-two hits in the chart's history; it is also Swift's record-extending sixteenth top-ten debut on the chart.   

At the [[2019 MTV Video Music Awards]], the "You Need to Calm Down" music video received nine nominations, winning two: [[MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]]â€”Swift's second win in the category, following "[[Bad Blood (Taylor Swift song)|Bad Blood]]" (2015), and the [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Video with a Social Message|Video for Good]]. The song also received a nomination at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|62nd Grammy Awards]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance|Best Pop Solo Performance]]. On August 20, 2019, a [[Clean Bandit]] remix of the song was released.

==Commercial performance==
[[File:Madonna Rebel Heart Tour 2015 - Stockholm (23051472299) (cropped).jpg|thumb|upright|Swift tied [[Madonna's]] record of most number-two hits in [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] history; both of them have scored six number-two hits on the chart.]]

In the United States, "You Need to Calm Down" debuted at number two on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], becoming the second top two hit from the album ''Lover''.<ref name="US Billboard 100"/> Like its predecessor "[[Me!]]", "[[Old Town Road]]" by [[Lil Nas X]] featuring [[Billy Ray Cyrus]] blocked it from reaching the top spot. "You Need to Calm Down" garnered several Hot 100 chart records for Swift:<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2019/07/03/4-ways-taylor-swifts-you-need-to-calm-down-helped-her-make-history-inside-the-top-10/|title=4 Ways Taylor Swift's 'You Need To Calm Down' Helped Her Make History Inside The Top 10|last=McIntyre|first=Hugh|date=July 3, 2019|website=[[Forbes]]|language=|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191129121809/https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2019/07/03/4-ways-taylor-swifts-you-need-to-calm-down-helped-her-make-history-inside-the-top-10/|archive-date=November 29, 2019|access-date=2020-04-20}}</ref> With six songs reaching the number-two spot, Swift tied [[Madonna]] as the artist with the most number two hits on the chart, having previously reaching the spot with ''Lover'''s lead single "Me!", "[[I Don't Wanna Live Forever]]" (2017), "[[I Knew You Were Trouble]]" (2013), "[[Today Was a Fairytale]]" (2010) and "[[You Belong with Me]]" (2009); Madonna achieved the feat with "[[Material Girl]]", "[[I'll Remember]]", "[[Frozen (Madonna song)|Frozen]]", "[[Causing a Commotion|Causing A Commotion]]", "[[Express Yourself (Madonna song)|Express Yourself]]" and "[[Cherish (Madonna song)|Cherish]]". The song became Swift's twenty-fourth top 10 hit, which is the fifth-most among female artists in the chart's history, behind Madonna (38), [[Rihanna]] (31), [[Mariah Carey]] (28) and [[Janet Jackson]] (27). The single is also Swift's sixteenth song to debut inside the top 10 of the chart, which is the second most number of top-ten debuts in the chart's history, only behind [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], who has 20 top-ten debuts, making Swift the first and only female artist with 16 top-ten debuts in the Hot 100 history.   

The song debuted at number-one on the [[Digital Songs|''Billboard'' Digital Song Sales]] charts, selling 79,000 [[Music download|downloads]], becoming Swift's record-extending seventeenth number-one hit on that chart. The song dropped to number thirteen in its second week on the Hot 100, eventually rising back up to number four following the release of the parent album.<ref name="Hot 100">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8517224/lil-nas-x-old-town-road-number-one-hot-100-twelve-weeks|title=Lil Nas X's 'Old Town Road' Leads Billboard Hot 100 for 12th Week, New Taylor Swift & Drake Songs Debut in Top 10|work=Billboard|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=June 24, 2019|accessdate=June 25, 2019|archive-date=June 24, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190624182611/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8517224/lil-nas-x-old-town-road-number-one-hot-100-twelve-weeks|url-status=live}}</ref> With 12 consecutive weeks spent in the top 20 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], "You Need to Calm Down" is Swift's song with the longest duration spent in the top 20 since "[[I Don't Wanna Live Forever]]" with [[Zayn Malik]] in 2017. The track started at number 33 on the [[Mainstream Top 40]] chart, based on three days of airplay, and has since reached number 9.<ref name="Adult Top 40" /><ref name="Mainstream Top 40" /> It also debuted at number 35 on the [[Adult Top 40]], peaking at number 3. As of August 2020, the song has sold over 3 million units in the U.S.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=chartdata|url=https://twitter.com/chartdata/status/1297367961338961921|number=1297367961338961921|date=August 23, 2020|accessdate=August 23, 2020|title=.@taylorswift13's "You Need to Calm Down" has now sold over 3 million units in the US.}}</ref>

In Canada, the song entered the [[Billboard charts|Hot AC]] chart at number 40 based on three days of radio tracking.<ref name="Canada Hot AC"/> It debuted at number four on the [[Canadian Hot 100]], becoming the second top five hit from the album in the country.

In the United Kingdom, the song debuted at number five of the [[UK Singles Chart]], becoming Swift's thirteenth top 10 in the UK.<ref name="UK"/> In Scotland, the song debuted at number one on the singles chart, becoming Swift's fifth chart topper in the country, as well as her second consecutive number-one hit in Scotland from ''Lover''.<ref name="Scotland"/> In Ireland, the song debuted at number five, tying Swift's previous single, "Me!", which peaked in the week ending May 3, 2019.<ref name="Ireland"/> In Germany, the song debuted at number 37 on the [[GfK Entertainment charts|Official German Charts]] later peaking at number 36.<ref name="Germany"/> In the Netherlands, the song debuted at number 28 on the [[Single Top 100]] chart.<ref name="Dutch100"/> In Belgium, the song arrived at number 11 on the Belgian [[Ultratip]] chart later peaking at number three.<ref name="Flanders"/> In Sweden, the song entered at number 55 on the [[Sverigetopplistan]] chart later peaking at number 35.<ref name="Sweden"/>

In Australia, the song debuted at number 3 in the [[ARIA Charts]], becoming the second top three hit from the album in the country.<ref name="Australia"/> In New Zealand, the song entered the [[Recorded Music NZ]] singles chart at number 5, becoming Swift's fifteenth top 10 single in the country.<ref name="NZ"/> The song also topped New Zealand Hot Singles chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nztop40.co.nz/chart/hotsingles?chart=4654|title=Hot Singles Chart|publisher=[[Recorded Music NZ]]|accessdate=June 22, 2019|archive-date=June 21, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190621061335/https://nztop40.co.nz/chart/hotsingles?chart=4654|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Synopsis===
[[File:Taylor Swift - You Need to Calm Down (music video screenshot of pop queen pageant).png|thumb|[[Drag queen]]s impersonating female music artists for a "pop queen pageant" in the music video. From left to right, [[Tatianna]] as [[Ariana Grande]], [[Trinity Taylor]] as [[Lady Gaga]], [[Delta Work]] as [[Adele]], [[Trinity K. The video is set in a colorful [[trailer park]]. Swift awakens in her trailer, wearing a bathrobe over a swimsuit, with an embroidery containing the [[Cher]] quote "Mom, I am a Rich Man" hanging on a wall.<ref name="VultureJG">{{cite news|url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/06/taylor-swift-you-need-to-calm-down-video-easter-eggs.html|title=Every Easter Egg in Taylor Swift's 'You Need to Calm Down' Music Video|first=Jill|last=Gutowitz|website=Vulture|date=June 18, 2019|accessdate=June 18, 2019|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190618032518/https://www.vulture.com/2019/06/taylor-swift-you-need-to-calm-down-video-easter-eggs.html|archivedate=June 18, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> Swift throws her phone onto her bed, which then gives off sparks and starts a fire in the trailer. She nonchalantly walks to a pool, ignoring her burning trailer. She goes into the pool, lying on a float, while looking at the camera and singing.The camera then switches to other residents in the trailer park and their activities, including Mayfield dancing, Hart weightlifting a [[boombox]] and Cox watering her yard of [[plastic flamingo]]es and greeting Lockhart, who promptly faints.

The scene cuts to Swift walking and dancing down a street with Hall, interspersed with scenes of Kiyoko shooting an arrow into a target with the number "5" (a clue to Swift's next promotional single "[[The Archer (song)|The Archer]]", the fifth track on the album), protestors holding placards with anti-gay slogans (a reference to a real-life religious group that picketed Swift's concerts),<ref name="VogueCover2019"/> Ciara officiating a wedding between Ferguson and Mikita, and Rippon serving [[snow cone]]s to customers from a stall. Swift also holds a tea party with the Fab Five and Hall. In another trailer, Lambert tattoos the words "Cruel Summer" (later revealed to be a song on the album) onto DeGeneres' right arm. In the next scene, Swift and other residents [[sun tanning|sun tan]] while ignoring the heckling protestors, followed by Porter walking down the middle of the two crowds wearing a dress.

The scene shifts to a "pop queen pageant" with the contestants dressed as numerous female singers. RuPaul walks down the lineup with a crown decorated with [[emerald]]s and [[fleur-de-lis]] motifs,<ref name="VultureJG"/> but instead of crowning a winner, he throws it in the air. A food fight begins, with Swift appearing in a french fries costume and Perry in a hamburger suit. The two see and walk towards each other. Elsewhere, Reynolds is portraying [[Norman Rockwell]] working on a painting of the [[Stonewall Inn]].<ref name="VultureJG"/><ref name="IndiewireJD"/> Swift and Perry smile, dance and share a hug. At the end of the video, a message appears urging viewers to sign Swift's Change.org petition for the United States Senate to pass the Equality Act.

== Impact ==
On June 1, 2019, Swift initiated a petition on [[Change.org]], titled "Support the Equality Act", in favour of the [[United States Senate]]'s support in passing the pro-[[LGBT]] "[[Equality Act (United States)|Equality Act]]" in the country's [[United States Congress|Congress]]; the act bans [[Discrimination against LGBT people|discrimination]] "on the basis of the [[sex]], [[sexual orientation]], [[gender identity]], or [[Pregnancy discrimination|pregnancy, childbirth, or a related medical condition]] of an individual, as well as because of [[Sex-based discrimination|sex-based stereotypes]]".<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://mashable.com/article/taylor-swift-vmas-equality-act-petition-speech/|title=Taylor Swift called out Trump for ignoring her Equality Act petition at the VMAs|last=Welsh|first=Caitlin|date=|website=[[Mashable]]|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412121259/https://mashable.com/article/taylor-swift-vmas-equality-act-petition-speech/|archive-date=2020-04-12|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref> A message urging the viewers to sign the petition appears at the end of the "You Need to Calm Down" music video; it states: "Let's show our pride by demanding that, on a national level, our laws truly treat all of our citizens equally. Please sign my petition for Senate support of the Equality Act on Change.org".<ref name="Elite Daily">{{Cite web|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/heres-how-to-sign-taylor-swifts-petition-supporting-the-equality-act-18700857|title=Here's How To Sign Taylor Swift's Petition Supporting The Equality Act|last=Boyle|first=Kelli|date=August 27, 2019|website=[[Elite Daily]]|language=|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412115326/https://www.elitedaily.com/p/heres-how-to-sign-taylor-swifts-petition-supporting-the-equality-act-18700857|archive-date=April 12, 2020|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref> As of April 12, 2020, the petition has over 704,000 signatures, including those from [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] [[US senator|senators]] and [[2020 United States presidential election|presidential candidates]] such as [[Elizabeth Warren]], [[Amy Klobuchar]], [[Ed Markey]], [[Kirsten Gillibrand]], [[Tim Kaine]], [[Cory Booker|Cory Brooker]] and [[Beto O'Rourke]]. Swift also addressed a letter to [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] senator [[Lamar Alexander]] of [[Tennessee]], her home state, asking him and the other senators to support the act;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2019-08-27/taylor-swift-white-house-equality-act|title=Taylor Swift called out the White House at the VMAs. Here's her Equality Act petition, explained|last=DÃ­az|first=Alexa|date=2019-08-27|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]|language=|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412115329/https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2019-08-27/taylor-swift-white-house-equality-act|archive-date=2020-04-12|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref> writing that, "for American citizens to be denied jobs or housing based on who they love or how they identify is un-American and cruel".<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2019/06/25/taylor-swifts-new-song-drives-donations-to-this-lgbtq-advocacy-group.html|title=Taylor Swift's new song 'You need to calm down' drives donations to LGBTQ advocacy group, but not without critics|last=Mitra|first=Mallika|date=2019-06-25|website=[[CNBC]]|language=|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200505195952/https://www.cnbc.com/2019/06/25/taylor-swifts-new-song-drives-donations-to-this-lgbtq-advocacy-group.html|archive-date=2020-05-05|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref>

The release of "You Need to Calm Down" further led to a spike in individual donations to the LGBT [[non-governmental organization]], [[GLAAD]], as the lyrics of the song namechecks the organization: "why are you mad when you could be GLAAD?". In response, GLAAD started a [[Facebook]] fundraiser for fans to support the advocacy work for [[Pride Month|Pride month]], with $1300 as a goal since 13 is Swift's lucky number.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8516079/taylor-swift-you-need-to-calm-down-glaad-donations|title=Taylor Swift's 'You Need to Calm Down' Leads to Spike in GLAAD Donations|date=2019-06-14|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-12|archive-date=2020-05-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200505013534/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8516079/taylor-swift-you-need-to-calm-down-glaad-donations|url-status=live}}</ref> Anthony Ramos, Director of Talent Management at GLAAD, stated that Swift "is one of the world's biggest pop stars; the fact that she continues to use her platform and music to support the LGBTQ community and the Equality Act is a true sign of being an [[Straight ally|ally]]. 'You Need to Calm Down' is the perfect [[Gay pride|Pride]] anthem, and we're thrilled to see Taylor standing with the LGBTQ community to promote [[Inclusivism|inclusivity]], equality, and acceptance this Pride month". Ramos also highlighted that many of the individual donations were made in the amount of $13.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3" /> [[Sarah Kate Ellis|Sarah Kate]], the CEO and President of GLAAD, stated: "Taylor Swift continues to use her platform to speak out against [[discrimination]] and create a world where everyone can live the life they love. GLAAD is so thankful for her donation to support our advocacy efforts and for her ongoing work to speak out for what is fair, just, and LGBTQ inclusive. In today's divisive political and cultural climate, we need more allies like Taylor, who send positive and uplifting messages to LGBTQ people everywhere".<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.glaad.org/blog/fans-celebrate-twitter-after-taylor-swift-shouts-out-glaad-and-lgbtq-people-new-pride-themed|title=Fans Celebrate on Twitter After Taylor Swift Shouts Out GLAAD and LGBTQ People in New Pride-Themed Anthem 'You Need to Calm Down'|last=Harvey|first=Spencer|date=2019-06-14|website=GLAAD|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412133535/https://www.glaad.org/blog/fans-celebrate-twitter-after-taylor-swift-shouts-out-glaad-and-lgbtq-people-new-pride-themed|archive-date=2020-04-12|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref>

{{Quote|text=I just want to say that this is a fan-voted award, so I first want to say thank you to the fans, because in this video several points were made, so you voting for this video means that you want a world, where we're all treated equally under the law, regardless of who we love, regardless of how we identify. At the end of this video, there was a petition and there still is a petition for the [[Equality Act (United States)|Equality Act]], which basically just says we all deserve equal rights under the law. I want to thank everyone who signed that petition because it now has a half a million signatures, which is five times the amount that it would need to warrant a response from the [[White House]].|author=Swift|title=in her [[MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]] speech at the [[2019 MTV Video Music Awards]].<ref name="Elite Daily"/>|source=}}

On August 26, 2019, Swift won the [[MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]] award for the "You Need to Calm Down" music video at the [[2019 MTV Video Music Awards]]; in her speech, Swift urged the audience and the viewers to sign her petition, and pointed out that the [[White House]] has not responded to the petition despite having "five times the amount of signatures that it would need to warrant a response".<ref name=":1" /> The televised speech led to a surge in the number of signatures on the petition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://time.com/5662540/taylor-swift-vma-2019-equality-act/|title=Taylor Swift Used Her 2019 VMAs Speech to Advocate for the Equality Act|last=McCluskey|first=Megan|date=August 27, 2019|website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|language=|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200603181020/https://time.com/5662540/taylor-swift-vma-2019-equality-act/|archive-date=June 3, 2020|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref> The next day, the White House issued a statement; [[Judd Deere]], the [[White House deputy press secretary|White House deputy Press Secretary]], stated that "the [[Presidency of Donald Trump|Trump administration]] absolutely opposes discrimination of any kind and supports the equal treatment of all; however, the [[United States House of Representatives|House]]-passed bill in its current form is filled with poison pills that threaten to undermine [[Parental rights|parental and conscience rights]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8528552/taylor-swifts-equality-act-petition-white-house-response|title=White House Responds to Taylor Swift's Equality Act Petition After VMAs|date=2019-08-27|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-12|archive-date=2020-04-12|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412122046/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8528552/taylor-swifts-equality-act-petition-white-house-response|url-status=live}}</ref>
