The [[United States|American]]-based international [[Automotive industry|automotive]] [[Conglomerate (company)|conglomerate]] [[General Motors]] (GM) underpins its many [[vehicle]] [[Car model|models]] with various [[Car platform|platforms]]. These platforms are established sets of [[axle]]s, [[Car suspension|suspensions]], and [[Steering|steering mechanisms]] which fit various [[Car body configurations|bodies]] and [[powertrain]]s from various [[Brand|marques]] that GM owns.

From the early [[20th century|twentieth century]], a [[Latin script|Latin letter]]-based naming scheme was used to designate platforms,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blog.1aauto.com/letter-general-motors-body-styles-mean/|title=What does the letter in GM's Body Styles actually mean? {{!}} 1AAuto Blog|date=2017-02-24|website=1A Auto Blog|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref> which were aimed at vehicles under different brands that served similar [[Niche market|niches]] of the market. For example, the [[GM B platform|B platform]] was the base for [[Full-size car|fullsize]], [[rear-wheel drive]] (RWD) [[Sedan (automobile)|sedans]] and [[Station wagon|wagons]] from 1926 to 1996. This platform underpinned vehicles made by [[Buick]], [[Cadillac]], [[Chevrolet]], [[Marquette (automobile)|Marquette]], [[Pontiac (automobile)|Pontiac]], and [[Oldsmobile]]. During the 1970s and 1980s, GM introduced many new [[front-wheel drive]] (FWD) platforms for the first time, such as the [[GM C platform (1985)|FWD C platform]] introduced in 1985. Despite being mechanically very new and different, it kept the same name as the [[GM C platform (1936)|RWD C platform]] for the sake of consistency, as most of the models remained the same, such as the [[Oldsmobile 98]]. For most of these platforms, the platform name is the fourth character of a vehicle's [[Vehicle identification number|VIN]], with a notable exception being [[truck]]s, for which the it is the fifth character.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Standard catalog of American cars, 1946-1975|url=https://archive.org/details/standardcatalogo00beve|url-access=registration|date=1997|publisher=Krause Publications|others=Kowalke, Ron.|isbn=0-87341-521-3|edition=4th|location=Iola, WI|oclc=38115432}}</ref>

At the outset of the [[21st century|twenty-first century]], General Motors' approach to platforms changed,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-gm-productplan-idUSKCN0V72LP|title=GM pitches new product strategy to skeptical investors|date=2016-01-29|work=Reuters|access-date=2020-04-27|language=en}}</ref> and so did the nomenclature they use. Platforms themselves are now referred to by GM as "architectures",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gminsidenews.com/index.php?page=platform_guide|title=GM Inside News Forum - Platform Guide|website=www.gminsidenews.com|access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref> and are now named according to the [[English language|English-language]] names of letters from the [[Greek alphabet]], such as the [[Subcompact car|subcompact]] [[GM Gamma platform|Gamma platform]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jalopnik.com/jalopnik-guide-to-gm-global-platforms-and-college-greek-348074|title=Jalopnik Guide To GM Global Platforms And College Greek Organizations|website=Jalopnik|language=en-us|access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref> Today, many of the since-discontinued Latin letter platforms are informally called "bodies", such as "J-body", which refers to the [[GM J platform|J platform]]. In the [[2010s]], GM once again began to change platform nomenclature, this time to a four-character format: platform-generation-XX. An example of this is the D2XX, from the ''second generation'' of the [[GM Delta platform|''Delta platform'']], hence the "D" and "2".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.carblogindia.com/general-motor-d2xx-platform/|title=General Motor D2XX Platform To Be Used In Many Cars Of GM Group|date=2012-09-05|website=Car Blog India|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref>

All but three platforms listed here use a front-mounted engine, and those exceptions are noted in the 'layout' column.

== In production ==
As of April 2020, GM produces [[car]]s, trucks, and [[sport utility vehicle]]s (SUVs) of multiple different sizes on 19 different platforms: 7 of which are inherently RWD, with the rest being FWD. All but 5 of these have [[four-wheel drive]] variants as well.

{{collapse-top|GM platform nomenclature guide}}
The GM nomenclature works as follows: 
<br />
1st position is the platform:
* A – Alpha
* C – Chi
* D – Delta
* G – Gamma
* E – Epsilon
* P – Premium Epsilon (XTS)
* Y – Corvette (Y-body)
* L – Lambda
* K – Trucks
<br />
2nd position is the platform generation.
<br />
3rd position is the body style:
* A – Convertible
* B – Coupe
* S – Sedan
* J – Hatchback
* K – CUV?
* L – Long Wheel Base Sedan
* M – Minispace
* U – Crossover/CUV (5 seater)
* Y – SUV/Truck (7 seater)
<br />
4th position is the Brand:
* B – Buick	
* C – Chevrolet
* G – GMC
* H – Holden
* L – Cadillac
* M – Citroën (partnership between GM & PSA)
* O – Opel/Vauxhall
<br />
5th position is an optional qualifier: for example the Sales market area: 
* S – sales market China
* N – sales market North-America
* I – electric/hybrid
<br />
For example, E2UB-N is the Crossover Buick for the North-American market in the second generation of the Epsilon platform.

==Historical applications==
As of April 2020, GM has produced [[car]]s, trucks, and SUVs of multiple different sizes on 107 different platforms: 55 of these with Latin letters, 12 with English spellings of Greek letters, and 40 others. Also, 64 of these platforms are inherently RWD, while the rest are primarily FWD. Furthermore, 50 of these have four-wheel drive variants as well.

=== EV platforms ===
[[Electric vehicle]] platforms are the only other exception to the forthcoming VSS consolidation.

==== BT1XX ====
BT1XX, standing for ''first'' generation ''battery''-electric ''truck'', is an upcoming electric body-on-frame truck and SUV platform derived from the GMT T1XX platform with RWD and AWD capabilities.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-autos-labor-evs-exclusive-idUSKBN1WX2HZ|title=Exclusive: Electric Hummer could be part of GM's move into EV trucks, SUVs - sources|date=2019-10-19|work=Reuters|access-date=2020-04-27|language=en}}</ref> It is currently only slated to underpin the 2021 [[GMC Hummer EV|GMC Hummer]], though it has not been clarified as to whether it is the only model for which the platform is to be used.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jalopnik.com/gms-bt1-program-might-include-electric-hummers-trucks-1839177244|title=GM's 'BT1' Program Might Include Electric Hummers, Trucks, Cadillac SUVs: Report|website=Jalopnik|language=en-us|access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref>

==== BEV3 ====
BEV3, standing for Battery Electric Vehicle 3 and also known as Ultium, is to be the replacement for the current BEV2 platform.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gmauthority.com/blog/2020/02/gm-electric-vehicle-plan-for-bev3-platform-comes-into-focus/|title=GM Electric Vehicle Plan For BEV3 Platform Comes Into Focus|website=GM Authority|access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref> Little is known about the nature of this platform, though it has been confirmed that it will underpin, among other models, a future Cadillac crossover.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.electrive.com/2020/02/07/first-gm-model-on-new-e-platform-is-a-cadillac/|title=First GM Model on New e-Platform is a Cadillac|date=7 February 2020|website=Electrive|access-date=April 27, 2020}}</ref>
