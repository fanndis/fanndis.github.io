The '''2016 [[MTV Video Music Awards]]''' were held on August 28, 2016<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2357372/2016-vma-dates/ | title=Mark Your Calendars — The 2016 VMAs Will Air On … | work=MTV News | date=October 21, 2015 | accessdate=February 3, 2016}}</ref> at [[Madison Square Garden]] in [[Manhattan]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7340813/mtv-vmas-madison-square-garden-new-york | title=MTV VMAs to Be Held at Madison Square Garden for the First Time | work=Billboard | date=April 21, 2016 | accessdate=April 21, 2016 | author=Greenwald, Morgan}}</ref> [[Adele]]'s "[[Hello (Adele song)|Hello]]" was the most nominated video with seven categories. This marked the 33rd edition of the live broadcast. [[Beyoncé]] led the nominations with a total of eleven, followed by Adele, who had eight.<ref name="noms"/> [[Rihanna]] received the [[Michael Jackson Video Vanguard Award]] after performing several medley numbers during the ceremony.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2910480/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-2016-vmas/ | title=Rihanna Is Your 2016 VMA Video Vanguard | work=MTV News | date=August 11, 2016 | accessdate=August 11, 2016}}</ref> [[Britney Spears]] marked her first performance at the awards show since the heavily criticized [[2007 MTV Video Music Awards|2007 show]] nine years prior.<ref>http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/britney-spears-is-ready-to-make-history-at-the-vmas-9-years-after-disastrous-performance_us_57b33b2ae4b0863b0284f09a</ref> Beyoncé won eight awards to bring her career total of wins to 26 VMAs, overtaking [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]]'s previous record of 20 awards, making her the artist with the most wins in the history of the award show.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/live/2016/aug/28/vma-2016-mtv-video-music-awards-live-kanye-britney-beyonce|title= MTV VMAs 2016: the MTV Video Music Awards – as it happened|date=29 August 2016|work=Guardian|accessdate=29 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-mtv-vmas-20160826-snap-story.html|title= Britney can't come close to matching Beyoncé's fire at MTV VMAs|date=29 August 2016|work=Los Angeles Times|accessdate=29 August 2016}}</ref>

The ceremony was shown on multiple [[Viacom (2005–present)|Viacom]] cable networks and through [[smart TV]] and mobile devices which allow access to MTV's [[TV Everywhere]]-authenticated live stream within their app (dependent upon provider), along with MTV's website and [[List of Facebook features#Live streaming|Facebook Live]].<ref>https://variety.com/2016/digital/news/vma-2016-live-stream-watch-online-mtv-video-music-awards-1201846193/</ref>

Compared to the previous year's show which had a lesser amount of Viacom networks simulcasting the ceremony, the 2016 ceremony's numbers showed a 35% drop across the measured networks carrying the ceremony, making it the lowest rated ceremony in MTV's 32-year history beating out 1996 and 2015, totalling a cumulative 6.5 million viewers (being later beaten by the 2017 edition), though the network also claimed substantial additional streaming viewership across MTV apps and Facebook Live.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7488058/mtv-vmas-ratings-drop-65-million-viewers-across-nets | title=MTV VMAs Ratings Drop Again, Draw 6.5 Million Viewers Across Nets. The highest peaks of rating were during the three presentations of Rihanna and Britney Spears | work=Billboard | date=August 29, 2016 | accessdate=August 29, 2016 | author=O'Conell, Michael}}</ref> 3.3 million viewers saw the show via MTV.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7494646/mtv-considers-vma-changes-audience-shift | title=MTV Considers VMA Changes Because of Audience Shift | work=Billboard | date=August 30, 2016 | accessdate=August 30, 2016}}</ref>
