'''Period of mobilization for the suppression of Communist rebellion''' ({{zh|t=動員戡亂時期|p=Dòngyuán Kānluàn Shíqí|poj=Tōng-oân Kham-loān sî-kî}}) is a political term used by the [[Kuomintang]]-led [[government of the Republic of China]] to indicate the country's entering into a [[state of emergency]] with the raising [[Chinese Civil War]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.mac.gov.tw/ct.asp?xItem=49748&ctNode=5842&mp=4 |title=Relations Across The Taiwan Straits |accessdate=2010-04-25 |last= |first= |date= |publisher=Mainland Affairs Information and Research Center, Mainland Affairs Council, Taiwan}}</ref> The term aimed to mobilize the people and resources under [[Kuomintang]]'s control to fight with the [[Communist Party of China]] rebellion.

The term was announced in July 1947 by [[Chiang Kai-shek]], the chairman of the [[Nationalist Government]], as an administrative order. As the situations worsen by time, it was then turned into a [[constitutional amendment]] named ''[[Temporary Provisions against the Communist Rebellion]]'' ({{lang|zh-tw|動員戡亂時期臨時條款}}) on May 10, 1948. With the progression [[Chinese Communist Revolution]], the ''Temporary Provisions'' were no longer valid in most of the [[China|Chinese]] territory after the [[Communist Party of China|Communist]]'s [[People's Liberation Army|armed forces]] expelled the [[Kuomintang]]'s [[Republic of China Armed Forces|armed forces]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.twcenter.org.tw/e02/20011224.htm |title=Constitution Day and Constitutional Government |accessdate=2010-04-25 |last=Hsueh |first= Hua-yuen |year=2001 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718134843/http://www.twcenter.org.tw/e02/20011224.htm |archivedate=2011-07-18 }}</ref> However, it was still enforced by the [[Kuomintang]]-led [[government of the Republic of China]] regime in [[Taiwan]] until the early 1990s.

== The temporary provisions ==
{{Wikisource|Temporary Provisions Effective During the Period of Communist Rebellion}}

In 1946, the [[Chinese Civil War]] between the [[Communist Party of China]] (CCP) and the Government of the Republic of China had resumed, in response of the war, the first session of the [[National Assembly of the Republic of China|National Assembly]] convened in 1948 enacted the "Mobilization for the Suppression of Communist Rebellion Provisional Act". The Provisional Act is provided to the then President [[Chiang Kai-shek]] extended powers to mobilize against the CCP. After the central government of the Republic of China had relocated to Taiwan in 1949, the Provisional Act provided the government ways to suppress its opponents.

== Enforcement of the temporary provisions ==
=== Mainland China ===
{{See also|Chinese Communist Revolution|Republic of China retreat to Taiwan}}
This ''Temporary Provisions'' extended the power of the [[President of the Republic of China|President]] and limits the civil liberties. The [[Kuomintang]]-led government also released two ''Declaration of Nationwide Martial Law'' in 1948 and 1949. With the progression [[Chinese Communist Revolution]], the ''Temporary Provisions'' were no longer valid in most of the [[China|Chinese]] territory after the [[Communist Party of China|Communist]]'s [[People's Liberation Army|armed forces]] expelled the [[Kuomintang]]'s [[Republic of China Armed Forces|armed forces]]. By the end of 1949, the [[Communist Party of China]] have founded the People's Republic of [[China]] and controlled almost the whole [[mainland China]]. Thus ''Temporary Provisions'' is no longer enforced except some small piece of territory that is still controlled by the [[Kuomintang]], notably the following territories that is transferred after 1949

* [[Hainan]]: ROC government lost control after the [[Landing Operation on Hainan Island]] in May 1950.
* Some offshore islands of [[Chekiang Province, Republic of China|Chekiang]]: ROC government lost control after the [[Battle of Yijiangshan Islands]] in January 1955.

=== Kinmen and Matsu ===
{{See also|Fujian Province, Republic of China|Kinmen|Matsu Islands}}
[[Kinmen]] and [[Matsu Islands]] is part of the traditional [[Fujian]] and was declared as ''Alert Zone'' in the first ''Declaration of Nationwide Martial Law'' on December 10, 1948. Then, they were moved to the ''War Zone'' in the second ''Declaration of Nationwide Martial Law'' on July 7, 1949. With the [[government of the Republic of China]]'s [[Republic of China retreat to Taiwan|retreat to Taiwan]], the islands became the frontier between the administration of [[Taiwan]] and [[China]]. Several [[List of battles in Kinmen|battles]] happened in [[Kinmen]] in the 1950s.

The [[government of the Republic of China]] in [[Taipei]] enforced the ''War Zone Administration'' in [[Kinmen]] and [[Matsu Islands]] since 1956. Under this special decree, the civil [[Fujian Provincial Government]] was replaced by the military defense commands in [[Kinmen]] and [[Matsu Islands]]. [[Kinmen County Government]], [[Lienchiang County Government]] and other field offices of the [[Executive Yuan|central government]] were all placed under the control of the military defense commands in the two places.

The War Zone Administrations existed even after the temporary provisions was repealed in May 1991. [[Kinmen]] and [[Matsu Islands|Matsu]] experienced the longest period of military control under the period of mobilization for the suppression of Communist rebellion from 1948 to November 1992.

=== Taiwan and Penghu ===
{{See also|White Terror (Taiwan)|Martial law in Taiwan}}
Taiwan was [[Taiwan under Japanese rule|under Japanese rule]] before 1945. After the [[World War II]], the [[Republic of China (1912–1949)|Republic of China]] occupied Taiwan on behalf of the [[Allies of World War II|Allies]]. However, with the fast changing situation of the [[Chinese Civil War]]. Taiwan hosts the lost [[Kuomintang]]-led [[government of the Republic of China]] after December 1949. The ''Temporary Provisions'' were brought to Taiwan along with the government. In Taiwan the term "[[White Terror (Taiwan)|White Terror]]" is often used to describe the era.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2005/05/20/2003255840|title=White Terror exhibit unveils part of the truth|accessdate=2010-04-25 |last=Huang |first= Tai-lin|date=20 May 2005|quote=Pages full of despair and fear could be written about the era known as the White Terror in Taiwan.|work= Taipei Times}}</ref>

==Termination==
The period was formally ended by [[President of the Republic of China|President]] [[Lee Teng-hui]] on 1 May 1991 by repealing the ''[[Temporary Provisions against the Communist Rebellion]]'' and replaced with the ''[[Additional Articles of the Constitution of the Republic of China|Additional Articles of the Constitution]]''.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Van Vranken Hickey|first=Dennis|url=https://www.questia.com/read/105513012/the-armies-of-east-asia-china-taiwan-japan-and|title=The Armies of East Asia: China, Taiwan, Japan, and the Koreas|location=Boulder, CO|year=2001|publisher=[[Lynne Rienner Publishers]]|page=120|isbn=9781555879921|accessdate=9 August 2015 |url-access=subscription |via=[[Questia]]}}</ref> All the declarations of martial laws based on ''[[Temporary Provisions against the Communist Rebellion]]'' were nullified when the Temporary Provisions repealed on May 1, 1991. However, the [[Ministry of National Defense (Taiwan)|Ministry of National Defense]] then issued a temporary declaration of martial law effective in the frontier region including [[Fujian Province, Republic of China|Fukien Province]] ([[Kinmen]] and [[Matsu Islands|Matsu]]) and [[South China Sea Islands]] ([[Pratas Islands|Tungsha]], and [[Taiping Island]] in [[Spratly Islands|Nansha]]). The temporary martial law was lifted on November 7, 1992, this marked all the [[Free area of the Republic of China]] has turned into constitutional democracy.
