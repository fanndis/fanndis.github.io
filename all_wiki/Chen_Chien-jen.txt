'''Chen Chien-jen'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://english.president.gov.tw/Page/41|title=Vice President Chen|accessdate=12 May 2020|website=Office of President Republic of China (Taiwan)|quote=Mr. Chen Chien-jen was born in Cishan Township, Kaohsiung County (now merged into Kaohsiung City) in 1951.}}</ref> {{small|[[Order of Dr. Sun Yat-sen|OS]] [[Order of St. Gregory the Great|KSG]] [[Order of the Holy Sepulchre|KHS]]}} ({{zh|t=陳建仁}}, born 6 June 1951) is a Taiwanese [[epidemiologist]] and politician. He joined the [[Chen Shui-bian]] presidential administration in 2003 as leader of the [[Ministry of Health and Welfare (Taiwan)|Department of Health]], serving through 2005. He later headed the [[Ministry of Science and Technology (Taiwan)|National Science Council]] between 2006 and 2008. Chen then served as a vice president of [[Academia Sinica]] from 2011 to 2015. Later that year, Chen joined [[Tsai Ing-wen]] on the [[Democratic Progressive Party]] presidential ticket. He served one term as [[Vice President of the Republic of China]], from 2016 to 2020. 

He was a member of the Board of Trustees of [[Fu Jen Catholic University]] before running for the presidential election and served as Fu Jen's Robert J. Ronald Chair Professor after leaving office.<ref>[http://www.trust.fju.edu.tw/aboutFju.jsp?labelID=1 The18th Session of the Board of Trustees]</ref><ref>[http://www.trust.fju.edu.tw/DownloadNewsFileServlet?file=2 輔仁大學學校財團法人董事會第 18 屆第 16 次會議摘要]</ref><ref>[https://www.fju.edu.tw/focusDetail.jsp?focusID=1579&focusClassID=3 輔仁大學醫學院聘請中研院陳建仁院士 擔任第一屆劉建仁神父紀念講座教授]</ref>

==Early and personal life==
Sir Chen Chien-jen was born at his family home in [[Cishan District|Cishan]], Kaohsiung County, in 1951,<ref name="vpc">{{cite web|url=https://english.president.gov.tw/Page/41|title=Vice President Chen|accessdate=7 September 2019|website=Office of the President Republic of China (Taiwan)|quote=Mr. Chen Chien-jen was born in Cishan Township, Kaohsiung County (now merged into Kaohsiung City) in 1951.{...}The Vatican has invited Mr. Chen and his wife Ms. Lo Fong-ping to visit several times, where they have been received by Pope John Paul II, Pope Benedict XVI, and Pope Francis.}}</ref> as one of eight children.<ref name="taidayprofile">{{cite news|title=Chen Chien-jen: Vice President of the Republic of China|url=https://www.taiwantoday.tw/images/ufile/file20160929111114748.pdf|accessdate=12 March 2018|work=Taiwan Today|date=29 September 2016}}{{Dead link|date=November 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> His father, [[Chen Hsin-an (politician)|Chen Hsin-an]], served as [[List of county magistrates of Kaohsiung|Kaohsiung County Magistrate]] from 1954 to 1957.<ref name=cnaprofile>{{cite news|last1=Hsu|first1=Elizabeth|title=Chen Chien-jen vows to be more than just figurehead vice president|url=http://focustaiwan.tw/news/aftr/201601160035.aspx|accessdate=18 January 2016|agency=Central News Agency|date=16 January 2016}}</ref> Chen's mother Chen Wei Lien-chih managed a daycare.<ref name="taidayprofile"/> Chen is married to Lo Fong-ping,<ref>{{cite news|last1=Lu|first1=Hsin-hui|last2=Hou|first2=Elaine|title=Taiwan's VP to attend Dominican Republic's presidential inauguration|url=http://m.focustaiwan.tw/news/aipl/201608050011.aspx|accessdate=5 August 2016|agency=Central News Agency|date=5 August 2016}}</ref> whose family is from Nanjing.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Strong |first1=Matthew |title=Chen calls for end to party polarization |url=https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/en/news/2858217 |accessdate=31 August 2018 |work=Taiwan News |date=26 December 2015}}</ref>

Chen Chien-jen is a devout [[Catholic Church|Catholic]]. Chen and his wife were invited to visit the Vatican several times by Popes [[John Paul II]], [[Benedict XVI]], and [[Pope Francis|Francis]]. He has been invested as a [[Order of the Holy Sepulchre|Knight of the Equestrian of the Holy Sepulchre of Jerusalem]] (2010)<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.amdgchinese.org/big5/news_view.php?cat_id=N&news_id=998|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151117021534/http://www.amdgchinese.org/big5/news_view.php?cat_id=N&news_id=998|url-status=dead|archive-date=2015-11-17|title=【禮儀】11/14 耶路撒冷聖墓騎士冊封大典|accessdate=2015-11-13|publisher=耶穌會中華省}}</ref> and a [[Order of St. Gregory the Great|Knight of the Pontifical Equestrian Order of St. Gregory the Great]] (2013).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.catholic.org.tw/catholicweekly/253-01.pdf|title=八位新封聖大額我略爵士|accessdate=2015-11-13|publisher=天主教會台灣地區主教團|date=2013-08-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170516050310/http://catholicweekly.catholic.org.tw/253-01.pdf#|archive-date=2017-05-16|url-status=dead}}</ref> He served on the board of trustees of [[Fu Jen Catholic University]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gaetan |first1=Victor |title=Taiwan's Catholic Church: Quest for National Identity |url=https://www.ncregister.com/daily-news/taiwans-catholic-church-quest-for-national-identity |accessdate=19 May 2020 |work=National Catholic Register |date=19 December 2017}}</ref>

==Political career==
Chen served as [[Ministry of Health and Welfare (Taiwan)#Minister of Department of Health|Minister of Health]] from 2003 to 2005.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Wu |first1=Debby |title=New chief takes over at the DOH |url=https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2003/05/18/0000211032 |accessdate=18 May 2020 |work=Taipei Times |date=18 May 2003}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Wu |first1=Debby |title=Tough times lie ahead for health chief |url=https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2003/05/19/0000211166 |accessdate=18 May 2020 |work=Taipei Times |date=19 May 2003}}</ref> As health minister, he was praised for effectively managing the [[SARS epidemic]] through quarantine and screening procedures,<ref name=natureprofile/> despite Taiwan's non-membership in the [[World Health Organization]] complicating the coordination of research efforts.<ref name=naturesars>{{cite journal|last1=Cyranoski|first1=David|title=Taiwan left isolated in fight against SARS|journal=Nature|date=17 April 2003|volume=422|issue=652|pages=652|doi=10.1038/422652a|pmid=12700727|pmc=7095487|bibcode=2003Natur.422Q.652C|doi-access=free}}</ref> His successor [[Hou Sheng-mao]] credited Chen with reforming the [[National Health Insurance (Taiwan)|National Health Insurance]] program.<ref>{{cite news |title=Officials receive awards |url=https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2005/03/29/2003248203 |accessdate=18 May 2020 |work=Taipei Times |date=29 March 2003}}</ref> Chen led the [[Ministry of Science and Technology (Taiwan)|National Science Council]] from 2006 to 2008.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Chiu |first1=Yu-Tzu |title=Lu offers some advice to new Cabinet team |url=https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2006/01/23/2003290354 |accessdate=18 May 2020 |work=Taipei Times |date=23 January 2006}}</ref>

===Vice presidency===
On 16 November 2015, Chen was confirmed as the running mate for [[Tsai Ing-wen]] in the [[2016 Taiwanese presidential election]]<ref>{{cite news |last=Hsu |first=Stacy |date=17 November 2015 |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/front/archives/2015/11/17/2003632640 |title=DPP's Tsai picks Chen Chien-jen |work=Taipei Times |page=1}}</ref> after media speculation earlier in the month.<ref>{{citation|url=http://udn.com/news/story/1/1311950|title=副手是陳建仁？蔡英文：宣布了就知道|publisher=[[United Daily News]]|language=Chinese}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Academia Sinica VP confirmed as running mate of Tsai Ing-wen|url=http://focustaiwan.tw/news/aipl/201511140012.aspx|accessdate=15 November 2015|work=Focus Taiwan|agency=Central News Agency|date=14 November 2015}}</ref> During the campaign, Chen became known by the nickname Brother Da-jen (大仁哥), after a character portrayed by [[Chen Bolin]] on the romantic drama ''[[In Time with You]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tseng|first1=Wei-chen |title=Reporter's Notebook: DPP's Chen in demand, KMT's Wang shunned |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2015/12/20/2003635231 |work=Taipei Times|date=20 December 2015 |page=3}}</ref> Chen is the first Catholic vice presidential nominee in Taiwan.<ref>{{cite news |title=Taiwan elects first Catholic vice president |url=https://www.ucanews.com/news/taiwan-elects-first-catholic-vice-president/74990 |accessdate=19 May 2020 |publisher=Union of Catholic Asian News |date=18 January 2016}}</ref> On 16 January 2016, Tsai and Chen won the presidential election in a landslide.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Loa |first1=Iok-sin |last2=Hsu |first2=Stacy |last3=Gerber |first3=Abraham |title=ELECTIONS: Madam President |url=https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/front/archives/2016/01/17/2003637385 |accessdate=18 May 2020 |work=Taipei Times |date=17 January 2016}}</ref> Chen took up his post as Vice President on 20 May 2016.<ref>Austin Ramzy: [https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/20/world/asia/tsai-ing-wen-sworn-in-as-taiwans-president-as-china-watches-closely.html ''Tsai Ing-wen Sworn In as Taiwan’s President, as China In March 2019, Chen announced that he would not seek a second term as vice president alongside Tsai.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Yeh |first1=Su-ping |last2=Su |first2=Lung-chi |last3=Wang |first3=Flor |title=Vice president declares he won't run for second term with Tsai |url=http://focustaiwan.tw/news/aipl/201903290018.aspx |accessdate=29 March 2019 |agency=Central News Agency |date=29 March 2019}}</ref> Chen received international attention for his role in leading Taiwan's response to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Taiwan|COVID-19 pandemic]] due to his unique position as both vice president and his epidemiologist background.<ref>{{cite news |title=Taiwan's weapon against coronavirus: An epidemiologist as vice president|url=https://www.todayonline.com/world/taiwans-weapon-against-coronavirus-epidemiologist-vice-president |accessdate=10 May 2020 |agency=Today |date=10 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Hernández |first1=Javier C. |last2=Horton |first2=Chris |title=Taiwan's Weapon Against Coronavirus: An Epidemiologist as Vice President |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/05/09/world/asia/taiwan-vice-president-coronavirus.html |accessdate=10 May 2020 |work=New York Times |date=9 May 2020}}</ref> Days before he stepped down from the vice presidency, Chen stated that he would return to the Academia Sinica as a research fellow and thus forgo the pension connected to his political office.<ref>{{cite news |title=VP to give up pension, return to research |url=https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2020/05/15/2003736444 |accessdate=15 May 2020 |work=Taipei Times |date=15 May 2020}}</ref>
