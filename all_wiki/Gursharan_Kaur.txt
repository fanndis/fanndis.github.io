'''Gursharan Kaur ''' (born 13 September 1937) is a history professor and author. She is the wife of the [[List of Prime Ministers of India|13th]]<ref>{{cite web | url=http://pmindia.nic.in/pm_manmohan.html | title=Prime Minister Manmohan Singh - PMO Profile | publisher=Prime Minister's Office, India | date=27 May 2014 | accessdate=28 May 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140527212158/http://pmindia.nic.in/pm_manmohan.html | archivedate=27 May 2014}}</ref> [[Prime Minister of India]], [[Manmohan Singh]].

== Early life ==
Gurusharan was born to Sardar Chattar Singh Kohli,<ref>Strictly Personal Book by Daman Singh</ref> an engineer of [[Burmah Oil|Burmah-Shell]], and Sardarni Bhagwanti Kaur in [[Jalandhar]] on 13 September 1937 in [[British Raj|British India]]. Her ancestral village was Dhakkam in [[Jhelum district]] (now in [[Punjab, Pakistan]]). She has four sisters and a younger brother.

Mrs. Singh is known in the Sikh community of [[Delhi]] for her ''[[kirtan]]'' singing, and has also appeared on Jalandhar Radio.<ref>[http://www.hindustantimes.com/StoryPage/StoryPage.aspx?sectionName=HomePage&id=1bc46240-ef03-40b2-83f6-5fe6cbd772d2&&Headline=A+first+lady+for+all+seasons First Lady for all seasons]</ref>

== Personal life ==
Since her husband became Prime Minister in 2004, she has accompanied him abroad on state visits. However, the family has largely stayed out of the limelight. Their three daughters - [[Upinder Kaur|Upinder]], [[Daman Kaur|Daman]] and Amrit, have successful, non-political, careers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pmindia.nic.in/meet.htm|title=Dr. Manmohan Singh: Personal Profile|publisher=Prime Minister's Office, Government of India|accessdate=2009-04-04|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090303150424/http://pmindia.nic.in/meet.htm|archivedate=3 March 2009}}</ref> [[Upinder Kaur]] is a professor of history at [[Delhi University]]. She has written six books, including ''Ancient Delhi'' (1999) and ''A History of Ancient and Early Medieval India'' (2008).<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.business-standard.com/india/storypage.php?autono=330920&chkFlg= |title= This Singh is King of History |accessdate=2009-04-04|last= Raote |first= Rrishi |date= 2008-10-10 |newspaper= [[Business Standard]]}}</ref> Daman Singh is a graduate of [[St. Stephen's College, Delhi|St. Stephen's College]], Delhi and [[Institute of Rural Management, Anand|Institute of Rural Management]], [[Anand, Gujarat]], and author of ''The Last Frontier: People and Forests in Mizoram'' and a novel ''Nine by Nine''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://specials.rediff.com/news/2009/jan/28slid1-dr-manmohan-singhs-daughter-daman-singh-turns-author.htm|title=Meet Dr. Singh's daughter|date=2009-01-28|publisher=Rediff.com|accessdate=2009-04-04}}</ref> Amrit Singh is a staff attorney at the [[American Civil Liberties Union|ACLU]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/articleshow/msid-2639327,prtpage-1.cms |title= PM's daughter puts White House in the dock|accessdate=2008-10-13 |last= Rajghatta|first= Chidanand|date= 2007-12-21 |publisher= [[ToI]]}}</ref>
