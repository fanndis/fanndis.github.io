'''Henry Schacht''' (born 1933) is an [[United States|American]] businessman, a former chairman and [[chief executive officer]] of [[Cummins]] Diesel (1973â€“1994), and later CEO of [[Lucent Technologies]].

Previously he was on the boards of [[CBS]], [[Chase (bank)|Chase Manhattan]], and [[Alcoa]].<ref>{{cite book| last = Endlich| first = Lisa| title = Optical Illusions, Lucent and the crash of Telecoms| place = New York| publisher = Simon & Schuster| year = 2004| isbn = 0-7432-2667-4| url-access = registration| url = https://archive.org/details/opticalillusions0000endl}}</ref>

He assumed the Lucent CEO role in a transitory capacity upon Lucent's spinoff from [[AT&T Corporation]], and served from 1995 to 1997. Mr. Schacht was brought back in 2001 to replace [[Richard McGinn]], who had served as Lucent's CEO during the intervening years. 
He is currently the managing director and senior advisor of the  [[private equity]] firm [[Warburg Pincus]] LLC [[New York City|New York]].
