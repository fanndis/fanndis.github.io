The world average of '''female top executives''' is 8 percent. [[Thailand]] has the highest proportion of female CEOs in the world, with 30 percent of companies employing female CEOs, followed by the [[People's Republic of China]], with 19 percent.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=http://business.asiaone.com/Business/News/Office/Story/A1Story20110308-267058.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-04-16 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110311124526/http://business.asiaone.com/Business/News/Office/Story/A1Story20110308-267058.html |archivedate=2011-03-11 }}</ref> In the [[European Union]] the figure is 9 percent and in the [[United States]] it's 5 percent.<ref name=autogenerated1 /> Only 14.2% of the top five leadership positions at the companies in the [[S&P 500 Index|S&P '''500''']] are held by women, according to a recent CNNMoney analysis,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://money.cnn.com/2015/03/24/investing/female-ceo-pipeline-leadership/index.html|title=Why only 14% of top execs are women|last=Egan|first=Matt|date=2015-03-24|work=[[CNN]]}}</ref> out of those '''500''' companies, there are only '''24''' female CEOs.
