'''Eva Asderaki''' ({{lang-el|Εύα Ασδεράκη}}, born 27 January 1982),<ref name=Ellines/> also known by her married name '''Eva Asderaki-Moore''', is a Greek [[Official (tennis)|tennis umpire]], who has umpired international tennis matches since 2001. She has umpired at all four [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments, and in 2015, she became the first woman to umpire a men's [[US Open (tennis)|US Open tennis]] final.

==Personal life==
Asderaki was born on 27 January 1982 in [[Chalcis]], Greece.<ref name=Ellines>{{cite news| url= https://www.ellines.com/en/achievements/24017-first-woman-ever-to-umpire-mens-us-open-final/| title= First woman ever to umpire men’s US Open final| work=Ellines.com| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref><ref name=GR>{{cite news| url= https://usa.greekreporter.com/2015/09/14/us-open-greek-chair-umpire-eva-asderaki-makes-history/| title= US OPEN: Greek Chair Umpire Eva Asderaki Makes History| work=[[Greek Reporter]]| date=14 September 2015| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> As a youngster, she started playing tennis.<ref name=SB>{{cite web| url= https://www.sbnation.com/tennis/2011/8/1/2310565/sitting-down-with-chair-umpire-eva-asderaki| title= Sitting Down With Chair Umpire Eva Asderaki| work=[[SB Nation]]| date=1 August 2011 | accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> She was once ranked the seventh best under-16 tennis player in Greece.<ref name=PTF>{{cite web| url= https://www.protennisfan.com/2019/02/life-in-the-chair-an-interview-with-itf-umpire-eva-asderaki-moore.html| title= Life In The Chair: An Interview With ITF Umpire Eva Asderaki Moore| publisher=Pro Tennis Fan| date=23 February 2019| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref>

Asderaki has lived in England and now lives in Australia with her husband Paul Moore.<ref name=SMH>{{cite news| url= https://www.smh.com.au/sport/tennis/australian-open-2016-meet-the-melbourne-umpire-john-mcenroe-respects-20160112-gm3xh1.html| title= Australian Open 2016: Meet the Melbourne umpire John McEnroe respects| work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]| date=12 January 2016| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> The couple married in 2012 in London,<ref name=Gazz>{{cite news| url= https://www.gazzetta.gr/weekend-journal/article/805815/eya-asderaki-koryfaia-kai-ellinida| title= Εύα Ασδεράκη: Kορυφαία και Ελληνίδα!| language=el| trans-title=Eva Asderaki: Top and Greek!| work=Gazzetta.gr| date=2015|accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> and they had their first child in July 2018.<ref name=PTF/>

==Career==
Asderaki has a gold umpiring accreditation from the [[International Tennis Federation]].<ref name=Ellines/><ref name=SMH/> She started as a line judge at her local tennis club in 1997,<ref name=Ellines/> and she earned her first ITF umpiring badge in [[Thessaloniki]] in 2000.<ref name=GR/><ref name=Efi>{{cite news| url= https://www.iefimerida.gr/news/18454/%CF%80%CE%BF%CE%B9%CE%B1-%CE%B5%CE%AF%CE%BD%CE%B1%CE%B9-%CE%B7-%CE%B5%CE%BB%CE%BB%CE%B7%CE%BD%CE%AF%CE%B4%CE%B1-%CF%80%CE%BF%CF%85-%CE%B5%CE%BA%CE%BD%CE%B5%CF%8D%CF%81%CE%B9%CF%83%CE%B5-%CF%84%CE%B7%CE%BD-%CF%83%CE%B5%CF%81%CE%AD%CE%BD%CE%B1-%CE%B3%CE%BF%CF%85%CE%AF%CE%BB%CE%B9%CE%B1%CE%BC%CF%82| title= Ποια είνι η ελληνίδα που έκανε την Σερένα Γουίλιαμς να φωνάζει| trans-title=Who is the Greek woman who made Serena Williams shout| work=[[Efimerida ton Syntakton]]| date=12 September 2011| accessdate=9 May 2020}}</ref> From 2000 to 2008, she studied umpiring in Luxembourg.<ref name=GR/> She is the only current international Greek tennis umpire.<ref name=Efi/>

Asderaki's first professional event was in [[Athens]] in 2000. The event had a prize equivalent to around 10,000[[Euro|€]] for the winner.<ref name=Gazz/><ref name=PT>{{cite news| url= https://www.protothema.gr/sports/article/508764/eua-asderaki-i-ellinida-pou-egrapse-istoria-ston-teliko-andron-tou-us-open-2015-/| title= Εύα Ασδεράκη, η Ελληνίδα που έγραψε ιστορία στον τελικό ανδρών του US Open 2015| language=el| trans-title=Eva Asderaki, the Greek woman who made history in the men's final of the US Open 2015| work=[[Proto Thema]]| date=14 September 2015| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> Asderaki started umpiring internationally in 2001,<ref name=TB>{{cite news| url= https://web.archive.org/web/20150916021817/http://tennisbest.com/news-link/12580-la-serata-magica-di-eva-asderaki#| title= La serata magica di Eva Asderaki| language=it| trans-title=The magical evening of Eva Asderaki|work=Tennis Best| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> and her first international event was in Israel.<ref name=Gazz/> Asderaki umpired at the [[2004 Summer Olympics]] in Athens, Greece.<ref name=GR/> In 2007, she started regularly umpiring in [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] tour events.<ref name=TW>{{cite web| url= https://www.tennisworldusa.org/tennis/news/Roger_Federer/74319/federer-v-djokovic-2015-us-open-final-is-my-career-highlight-asderaki/| title= Federer v Djokovic 2015 US Open final is my career highlight - Asderaki| publisher=Tennis World USA| date=22 July 2019| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref>

In 2011, Asderaki umpired in the [[2011 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|women's singles]] events at [[2011 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]],<ref name=GR/> and the [[2011 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|US Open]].<ref name=Ellines/> During the US Open final, she had a verbal disagreement with [[Serena Williams]], after overturning a point as Williams had shouted during the rally.<ref name=Ellines/><ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.nytimes.com/2011/09/12/sports/tennis/stosur-wins-us-open-womens-title.html| title= Stosur Captures the Title After a Williams Outburst| work=[[The New York Times]]| date=11 September 2011| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref><ref name=Post>{{cite news| url= https://www.ilpost.it/2011/09/13/serene-williams-litiga-giudice-tennis/| title= Sei una brutta persona| language=it| trans-title=You are a bad person|date=13 September 2011| accessdate=3 May 2020}}</ref> Williams later said that Asderaki was "ugly on the inside,"<ref name=SMH/> although Williams later claimed that she had mistaken Asderaki for another umpire that she had disagreed with on a previous occasion.<ref name=Gazz/>
