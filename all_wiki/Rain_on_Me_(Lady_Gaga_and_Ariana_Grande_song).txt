"'''Rain on Me'''" is a song by American singers [[Lady Gaga]] and [[Ariana Grande]], from Gaga's sixth studio album, ''[[Chromatica]]'' (2020). It was written by Gaga, Grande, [[Nija Charles]], [[Rami Yacoub]], [[Tchami]], [[Boys Noize]] and its producers [[BloodPop]] and [[Burns (musician)|Burns]]. An upbeat [[House music|house]], [[dance-pop]] and [[disco]] song, the song features a synth-disco beat and funk guitars. Described by Gaga as a "celebration of tears," it is about being able to keep going despite the hardships of life. 

The track was released by [[Interscope Records]] as the album's second single on May 22, 2020. Many critics <!--do not use "critically acclaimed" without citing a source saying that per WP:SYNT-->lauded the singers' vocals as well as the song's message. With the song's debut at the top of the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], it became Gaga's fifth and Grande's fourth US number-one single in the country. It marked the first female collaboration to debut atop the chart, and made Grande the first artist to have four songs debut at the top spot. On [[Spotify]], "Rain on Me" was the seventh most streamed song of summer 2020 and the most streamed song by a female artist globally during the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/spotify-reveals-the-most-streamed-songs-of-the-summer-2737199|title=Spotify reveals the most streamed songs of the summer|first=Tom|last=Skinner|website=[[NME]]|date=August 25, 2020|accessdate=September 3, 2020}}</ref> Additionally, "Rain on Me" topped the charts in twelve other countries, and also charted within the top ten in more than forty territories. 

The accompanying music video was directed by filmmaker [[Robert Rodriguez]], and features Gaga and Grande dancing inside a giant arena during a rainstorm, with [[daggers]] falling from the sky. It received acclaim from critics and was nominated for seven [[MTV Video Music Awards]], including [[MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]], ultimately winning three awards, including Song of the Year. The duo also performed "Rain on Me" at the [[2020 MTV Video Music Awards|awards show]]. "Rain on Me" was further promoted with the release of mock news broadcasts in partnership with [[The Weather Channel]] in which Gaga and Grande both appeared. The song received a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance|Best Pop Duo/Group Performance]] at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards|63rd Grammy Awards]].

==Commercial performance==
[[File:Dangerous Woman Tour in Manchester4.jpg|thumb|With "Rain on Me", Grande broke the record for the most number one debuts on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], with four songs.]]
"Rain on Me" debuted at number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]], becoming Gaga's fifth and Grande's fourth number one single as well as Gaga's 17th top 10 entry and Grande's 16th. It additionally became the first female-collaboration song to debut at number one on the chart. "Rain on Me" is Gaga's second song to debut at the top following "[[Born This Way (song)|Born This Way]]", making her the fourth female artist to have two songs debut at number one, following [[Mariah Carey]], [[Britney Spears]], and Ariana Grande, and seventh overall, following [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Justin Bieber]], and [[Travis Scott]]. Gaga is also the third artist following Carey and [[Beyonc√©]] to accumulate a number one single in the 2000s, 2010s, and 2020s decades. Grande became the first artist to have four number one debut singles, surpassing Carey, Drake, and Bieber. Gaga also earns her eighth Digital Song Sales No. 1 and Grande adds her seventh. The song was driven by 31.5 million streams, 73,000 downloads and 11.1 million radio airplay impressions in the week ending May 28, 2020. On August 28, 2020, "Rain on Me" was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) for moving 1 million units in the United States. The song's sales were aided by four physical/digital combination offerings during the tracking week; consumers could also purchase [[Cassette tape|cassette]], CD and [[vinyl single]]s, each with a digital download.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lady Gaga & Ariana Grande's 'Rain on Me' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9394719/rain-on-me-debuts-atop-hot-100-lady-gaga-ariana-grande?utm_medium=social&utm_source=twitter |work=Billboard |accessdate=June 1, 2020}}</ref> 

In the United Kingdom, "Rain on Me" debuted at number one on the [[UK Singles Chart]] becoming both Gaga and Grande's sixth UK number one single and Gaga's first single to debut atop. Notching up 70,000 chart sales to seize the top spot from the previous week's leader, [[DaBaby]]'s "[[Rockstar (DaBaby song)|Rockstar]]", "Rain on Me" claimed the biggest ever opening week streaming numbers by an all-female collaboration in the UK, with 8.1 million plays in seven days. At six number-one singles each, Gaga and Grande match [[Britney Spears]] and [[Rod Stewart]] with the tenth-most number-ones on the UK Singles Chart.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lady Gaga & Ariana Grande's Rain On Me debuts at Number 1 on the Official Singles Chart |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/lady-gaga-ariana-grandes-rain-on-me-debuts-at-number-1-on-the-official-singles-chart-thank-you-for-celebrating-this-song-__29876/ |publisher=Official Charts Company |accessdate=May 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200530134911/https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/lady-gaga-ariana-grandes-rain-on-me-debuts-at-number-1-on-the-official-singles-chart-thank-you-for-celebrating-this-song-__29876/|archive-date=May 30, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> With 515,000 chart sales during the three-month period, "Rain on Me" was the third best selling song of the summer 2020 in the UK, being the biggest song by a female artist in the country.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The UK's Official Top 40 Songs of the Summer 2020|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-uks-official-top-40-songs-of-the-summer-2020__30882/|publisher=Official Charts Company |accessdate=September 3, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

In Ireland, "Rain on Me" debuted atop the [[Irish Singles Chart]], becoming Grande's sixth and Gaga's seventh number one single in the country. "Rain on Me" also scored the biggest opening week of 2020, surpassing the previous best set by [[Billie Eilish]]'s "[[No Time to Die (song)|No Time to Die]]". "Rain on Me" is Gaga's first song to debut in pole position in over nine years since  "[[Born This Way (song)|Born This Way]]" in 2011 and Grande's sixth to lead the top 50 in Ireland, and in doing so, she matched [[Rihanna]] as the female artists with the most number-ones of the past ten years.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lady Gaga and Ariana Grande claim Number 1 on Official Irish Singles Chart, score biggest opening week of 2020 with Rain On Me |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/lady-gaga-and-ariana-grande-claim-number-1-on-official-irish-singles-chart-score-biggest-opening-week-of-2020-with-rain-on-me__29880/ |publisher=Official Charts Company |accessdate=May 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200531024309/https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/lady-gaga-and-ariana-grande-claim-number-1-on-official-irish-singles-chart-score-biggest-opening-week-of-2020-with-rain-on-me__29880/|archive-date=May 31, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Live performance==
On August 30, 2020, the duo performed the song at the [[2020 MTV Video Music Awards]], marking its first live performance. The track was part of a medley of songs from ''Chromatica''. After performing "911", Gaga changed into a purple outift with a spiky shoulder-piece and started singing "Rain on Me". Grande joined her on stage, sporting long pigtails and high, white platform shoes. Accompanied by dancers, they performed the choreography as seen in the music video of the song, which included their synchronized jumping.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Shaffer|first1=Claire|last2=Spanos|first2=Brittany|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/lady-gaga-ariana-grande-rain-on-me-vmas-1050834/|title=Lady Gaga Brings 'Chromatica' to the VMAs With a Dizzying, Futuristic Medley|date=August 30, 2020|accessdate=August 30, 2020|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|archiveurl=http://archive.today/2020.08.31-013951/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/lady-gaga-ariana-grande-rain-on-me-vmas-1050834/ |archivedate=August 31, 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Hosken|first1=Patrick|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3169497/lady-gaga-ariana-granda-rain-on-me-vmas/|title=Lady Gaga's Mechanized Mask and Ariana Grande Sparkle in 'Rain on Me' Debut|date=August 30, 2020|accessdate=September 6, 2020|work=[[MTV]]|archiveurl=http://archive.today/2020.09.06-134221/http://www.mtv.com/news/3169497/lady-gaga-ariana-granda-rain-on-me-vmas/|archivedate=September 6, 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref> Joey Nolfi from ''Entertainment Weekly'' called the performance "epic", as "the two singers hit high notes and performed exuberant choreography, wearing futuristic [[Face masks during the COVID-19 pandemic|masks]] the entire time."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Nolfi|first1=Joey|url=https://ew.com/awards/lady-gaga-ariana-grande-2020-mtv-vmas-performance/|title=Lady Gaga, Ariana Grande rain excellence on VMAs with epic performance of 'Rain on Me'|date=August 30, 2020|accessdate=September 6, 2020|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|archiveurl=http://archive.today/2020.09.06-130652/https://ew.com/awards/lady-gaga-ariana-grande-2020-mtv-vmas-performance/|archivedate=September 6, 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref>
