The '''Port of Lisbon''' ([[Portuguese language|Portuguese]]: ''Porto de Lisboa'') is the third-largest [[port]] in [[Portugal]], mainly on the north sides of the [[River Tagus|Tagus]]'s large natural harbour that opens west, through a short strait, onto the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. Each part lies against central parts of the Portuguese capital [[Lisbon]]. Due to its strategic site between Europe, Africa, and the Atlantic, it is one of the most accessed and used in [[Europe]]. For [[container ship]]s it begins with a 1080-metre mooring, with cranes, south of a thin, rectangular, pleasure boat marina. It is north-east of the centre of the strait-spanning suspension bridge, the [[Ponte 25 de Abril]]. Continuations are to the north-east. These are a bank-side [[cruise ship]] terminal next to the old [[Alfama]] district, followed by multi-use harbour-side terminals at Xabregas, Grilo, [[Beato (Lisbon)|Beato]] ({{IPA-pt|ˈbjatu|}})) and Braço do Prata, [[Marvila (Lisbon)]]. 

Denser military docks are beyond a headland to the south-east &ndash; [[Lisbon Naval Base|Lisbon Naval Base, long colloquially synonymous with Alfeite]], a slightly wider, once royally-owned, district.

==History==
{{expand-section|date=October 2020}}
There are data on human presence in the Tagus estuary since prehistory. Probably the [[Phoenicians]] were in this area in the 12th Century BC and they would create a commercial port in the north margin of the [[River Tagus]].{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} In 205 BC, the city (known as ''Olissippo'') was conquered by the [[Ancient Rome|Romans]]. In the 5th Century the [[Suebi]] conquered the area, followed by the [[Visigoths]]. In AD 714, the [[Moors]] conquered Lisbon, expanding the port with their [[Mediterranean]] and Atlantic trades.

[[Manuel Frasquilho|Manuel Antunes Frasquilho]] was President of the Port of Lisbon Administration (APL), from 2005 to 2009, during which he implemented the ''Plano Estratégico do Porto de Lisboa'' (Strategic Plan for the Port of Lisbon), a massive long term modernization and development plan, aimed at increasing port traffic, efficiency, and accessibility.<ref>[https://www.jornaldenegocios.pt/empresas/detalhe/porto_de_lisboa_vai_reforcar_trafego_de_contentores_por_via_ferrea_e_fluvial Jornal de Negócios - Porto de Lisboa vai reforçar tráfego de contentores por via férrea e fluvial]</ref><ref>[http://docplayer.com.br/14141272-Manuel-frasquilho-presidente-da-apl-o-porto-de-lisboa-e-para-continuar.html Transportes em Revista - Manuel Frasquilho: Presidente da APL «O Porto de Lisboa é para continuar»]</ref> Frasquilh was elected President of the RETE – Association for the Collaboration between Ports and Cities in 2005.<ref>[http://www.portodelisboa.pt/portal/page/portal/PORTAL_PORTO_LISBOA/PORTO_LISBOA/ESTUDOS_PROMOCAO_COMERCIAL/PROMOCAO_COMERCIAL/ACCOES_PROMOCAO_COMERCIAL/ASSOCIACOES_APL_PERTENCE_TRABALHO Porto de Lisboa -  Associações a que a APL pertence e trabalho desenvolvido]</ref><ref>[http://www.portodelisboa.pt/portal/page/portal/PORTAL_PORTO_LISBOA/GESTAO_PORTAL/CRIAR_DESTAQUES/CRIAR_DESTAQUES/Anteriores/Presidente%20do%20Porto%20de%20Lisboa%20eleito%20para%20a%20presid%EAncia%20de%20o Porto de Lisboa - Presidente do Porto de Lisboa eleito para a presidência de organização internacional de colaboração entre cidades e portos]</ref><ref>[http://retedigital.com/en/socios/manuel-frasquilho/ RETE Digital - Manuel Frasquilho]</ref><ref>[http://www.rtp.pt/noticias/economia/porto-lisboa-define-plano-estrategico-com-aposta-mercadorias-e-turismo_n80079 RTP - Porto Lisboa define plano estratégico com aposta mercadorias e turismo]</ref>
