==Africa==

===Ghana===
* [[Silverbird Cinemas]] – in 2 locations in [[Accra, Ghana]]

=== Morocco ===
* Megarama – operating in [[Casablanca]], [[Marrakesh]] and [[Fez]], with more cities coming
* [[IMAX]] – inside [[Morocco Mall]], Casablanca
* La Renaissance – independent cinema in Rabat; includes a restaurant and a music venue

===Nigeria===
* [[Silverbird Cinemas]] – As at 2016, the largest cinema chain in West Africa in-terms of screen numbers, with 69 screens. It has 8 theatres located in [[Lagos]], [[Abuja]], [[Port-Harcourt]], [[Ikeja]], [[Uyo]] and [[Accra]]-[[Ghana]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://silverbirdcinemas.com/home/about-silverbird-group |title=About Silverbird Group |publisher=Silverbird Cinemas |work=Silverbird Group |accessdate=23 April 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140427224230/http://silverbirdcinemas.com/home/about-silverbird-group |archivedate=27 April 2014 }} 
</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.silverbirdgroup.com/film-distribution |title=Silverbird Film Distribution}}</ref>
* Filmhouse Cinemas (West Africa) – with 10 cinemas as at 2016, it is the largest cinema chain in West Africa in terms of location numbers. It has 44 screens in Lagos, [[Ibadan]], [[Lekki]] where they have the first [[IMAX]] theatre in West Africa, [[Calabar]], [[Akure]], Port Harcourt, [[Kano]] and [[Asaba, Delta|Asaba]] as at December 2016. The company was established in 2012 and it is aiming to have 25 cinemas in six years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmhouseng.com/about.html |title=Welcome to The Film House Cinemas |publisher=The Film House Cinemas |work=Film House, Nigeria |accessdate=23 April 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140424013103/http://www.filmhouseng.com/about.html |archivedate=24 April 2014 }} 
</ref>
* Genesis Deluxe cinemas – with over 15 screens and 7 theatres, located in Lagos, Lekki, Port-Harcourt, [[Enugu]], [[Effurun]] and [[Owerri]]. It was the first cinema in West Africa to show a 3D movie.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.genesisdeluxecinemas.com/about.html |title=Genesis Deluxe Cinemas: About Us |publisher=Genesis Deluxe Cinemas |work=Genesis Deluxe |accessdate=23 April 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140215073142/http://www.genesisdeluxecinemas.com/about.html |archivedate=15 February 2014 }} 
</ref>
* Viva Cinemas – with 18 screens and 5 theatres in Ilorin, Ibadan, Ota, Enugu, Ikeja. The company started operations in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ibpulse.com/2015/03/what-you-should-know-about-viva-entertainment-cinema-palms-mall-ring-road-ibadan-pictures.html |title=What you should know about Viva Entertainment Cinema |publisher=IB Pulse |date=March 2015 |accessdate=5 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150315114151/http://ibpulse.com/2015/03/what-you-should-know-about-viva-entertainment-cinema-palms-mall-ring-road-ibadan-pictures.html |archivedate=15 March 2015 }}</ref>
* Ozone Cinemas – with 4 screens located in Lagos. It is the first multiplex cinema to be built on the mainland of Lagos.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ozonecinemas.com/about_us.htm | title=Ozone Cinemas – About Us | publisher=Ozone Cinemas | work=Ozone Cinemas | accessdate=23 April 2014}}</ref>

===Canada===
*[[Alliance Cinemas]] – after selling its BC locations, it now operates only one theater in Toronto
*Ciné Entreprise<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cinentreprise.com/|title=Les Cinémas Ciné Entreprise - Horaires at Cinémas|author=|date=|website=www.cinentreprise.com}}</ref> – independent theatre chain based in rural Québec
*[[Cinémas Guzzo]] – 10 locations and 142 screens in the Montreal area
*[[Cineplex Entertainment]] – Canada's largest and North America's fifth-largest [[movie theater]] company, with 162 locations and 1,635 screens
** [[Cineplex Entertainment|Cinema City]] – discount chain in Western Canada, purchased by Cineplex
**[[Cineplex Odeon Cinemas]] – operations in both Canada and the United States. Operations in each country is owned by separate companies. Cineplex Entertainment in Canada and AMC Theatres in the United States.
**Colossus (theatre) – a Famous Players brand, now owned by Cineplex
**[[Famous Players]] – formerly Canada's largest theatre chain; purchased by [[Cineplex Entertainment]] in 2005
**Fortune Cinemas – a movie theatre chain that operated in Canada. Cineplex has sold seven theatres in March 2006 in [[Québec]] ec to Chelsea-based Fortune Cinemas Inc. Cineplex Entertainment later acquired (or in this case, re-acquired) some of Fortune Cinemas theatres after they went bankrupt. 
**[[Galaxy Cinemas]] – mid-sized chain that was the parent company to [[Cineplex Entertainment]].  Galaxy purchased bankrupt Cineplex in 2003.
**Scotiabank Theatres – a Cineplex brand
**[[SilverCity]] – a Famous Players brand, now owned by Cineplex
*[https://cinestarz.ca/ CinéStarz] – small chain that operates 5 theaters: 3 in Quebec and 2 in Ontario
*film.ca Cinemas<ref>{{cite web|url=http://film.ca/|title=Big Movies, Small Prices - Film.Ca Cinemas in Oakville, ON|author=|date=|website=Film.Ca Cinemas in Oakville, ON}}</ref> – a small Canadian operator with one location in Oakville, ON
*[[Imagine Cinemas]] – 14 locations and 90 screens, in Ontario and BC.
*[[Landmark Cinemas]] – Canada's second-largest chain with 45 locations and 317 screens in British Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba, Ontario and the Yukon
*Premier Theatres<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.premiertheatres.ca/|title=Premier Theatres|author=|date=|website=www.premiertheatres.ca}}</ref> – operates 5 drive-in theaters and 4 cinemas with a total of 23 screens in Ontario and Yellowknife NWT
*[[Rainbow and Magic Lantern Cinemas]] – 11 locations and 43 screens operating in Ontario, Alberta and Saskatchewan

====Defunct brands in Canada====
*[[AMC Theatres]] – {{as of|2012|July|lc=y}} AMC divested of its Canadian operations, selling four to [[Cineplex Entertainment|Cineplex]], two to [[Empire Theatres]] which were later sold to Landmark Cinemas in 2013, closing two.
*[[Empire Theatres]] – closed on October 29, 2013, by selling most of their locations to Cineplex Entertainment and Landmark Cinemas and closing 3 others that were not included in the sales. It was formerly Canada's second-largest chain.
