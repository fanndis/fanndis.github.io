'''Bayan Muna''' (literally, "Nation First") is a [[Party-list representation in the House of Representatives of the Philippines|party-list]] in the [[Philippines]], a member of the [[Left-wing politics|leftist]] [[political party]] [[Makabayan]]. The motto of the party is ''"New Politics, the Politics of Change"'', against "traditional, elitist, pro-imperialist politics".<ref>[https://bayanmuna.org/a-homepage-section/about-us/ Bayan Muna. About Bayan Muna.]</ref> Its platform includes the advocation of a government that progressively supports the [[working class]], with meaningful representation of all democratic sectors in the Philippines. Bayan Muna was the second most popular [[Party-list proportional representation|party-list]] party in the 2007 Philippine elections. In 2009, Bayan Muna Representative [[Satur Ocampo]] and former Bayan Muna and incumbent Gabriela Representative [[Liza Maza]] ran for senate seats as guest candidates under the ticket of billionaire real estate magnate Senator [[Manny Villar]]. Ocampo said that their common objective “is to emancipate the people from widespread poverty and social injustice."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2009/12/15/532400/villar-welcomes-ocampo-maza-nps-guest-senatorial-bets|title=Villar welcomes Ocampo, Maza as NP's guest senatorial bets|work=The Philippine Star|access-date=2019-02-12}}</ref>

==2007 Elections==
In the weeks leading up the Philippine national election, Executive Secretary [[Eduardo Ermita]], on behalf of the Armed Forces of the Philippines, admitted "[[Malacañang]]’s leading role in the fabricated charges leveled against Rep. Ocampo and the campaign to crush Bayan Muna."<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Davao Today|url=http://davaotoday.com/2007/04/09/philippines-satur-back-on-campaign-trail-palace-afp-finally-admit-roles-in-anti-bayan-muna-drive|title=Satur back on campaign trail; Palace, AFP finally admit roles in anti-Bayan Muna drive|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928165049/http://davaotoday.com/2007/04/09/philippines-satur-back-on-campaign-trail-palace-afp-finally-admit-roles-in-anti-bayan-muna-drive/|archive-date=September 28, 2007|date=April 9, 2007|via=[[Wayback Machine]]}}</ref> Bayan Muna partylist representative [[Satur Ocampo]] was detained weeks prior in March 2007 on murder charges from an alleged 1984 [[communist]] purge in [[Leyte]]. The arrest was widely condemned by international observers. Rep. Ocampo was ordered released on bail by the Supreme Court.<ref name="arrest3">[[Philippine Inquirer]]. [http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/nation/view_article.php?article_id=56439 "Media group slams MPD media ban on Ocampo"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080501073845/http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/nation/view_article.php?article_id=56439 |date=May 1, 2008 }} 22 March 2007.</ref> In the 14 May 2007 [[2007 Philippine general election|election]], the party won 2 seats in the nationwide party-list vote.
