'''Elizabeth Jane DeGeneres''' ([[née]] '''Pfeffer'''; born May 20, 1930) is an American [[LGBT social movements|LGBT rights]] [[List of LGBT rights activists|activist]] and former speech therapist.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/Mother-of-Comedian-Ellen-DeGeneres-Is-Dream-Mom-2988358.php|title=Mother of Comedian Ellen DeGeneres Is Dream Mom for Gays/Support for daughter leads to activism|work=SFGate|access-date=2017-05-10}}</ref> She is the mother of [[Ellen DeGeneres|Ellen]] and [[Vance DeGeneres]] and the first straight spokeswoman for the [[Human Rights Campaign]]'s National Coming Out Project<ref>"Yep, I'm an activist: Betty DeGeneres, Ellen's mom, agrees to serve as spokeswoman for HRC's coming-out project", [[The Advocate (LGBT magazine)|The Advocate]], October 28, 1997</ref> and an active member of [[Parents, Families and Friends of Lesbians and Gays]] (PFLAG). She gained notability following her lesbian daughter Ellen's highly publicized [[coming out]] in 1997.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.thelavendereffect.org/projects/ohp/betty-degeneres/|title=Betty DeGeneres|date=2015-01-24|work=THE LAVENDER EFFECT®|access-date=2017-05-10|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Life and career==
[[File:Donna & Kris with Betty DeGeneres (255623801).jpg|thumb|left|DeGeneres (right) in 1997]]
The youngest of three daughters, DeGeneres was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, in 1930, during the height of the Great Depression.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://archive.boston.com/lifestyle/blogs/bostonspirit/2012/04/americas_mom_betty_degeneres_d.html|title='America's Mom' Betty DeGeneres Discusses Ellen, Parenting, and Lady Gaga|work=Boston.com|access-date=2017-05-10|language=en}}</ref> Her father was German and her mother was Irish.<ref name=":1" /> Her sisters (Helen and Audrey) were not involved in day-to-day family life while DeGeneres was in her late teens, but the sisters did become close in adulthood.<ref name=":0" /> She attended [[Louisiana State University]] (LSU) for two years, studying speech language pathology to become a speech therapist. While in college she performed in a number of plays with [[Joanne Woodward]], who would go on to be an Academy Award-winning actress.<ref name=":1" />

She married Elliott DeGeneres in 1952. The couple divorced in 1974. Shortly afterwards, DeGeneres married Roy Gruessendorf, who moved the family to Texas. DeGeneres was diagnosed with [[breast cancer]] shortly after her second marriage. Their marriage ended in the early 1980s; Gruessendorf died in 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://heavy.com/entertainment/2019/05/ellen-degeneres-stepdad-roy-gruessendorf/|title=Roy Gruessendorf, Ellen’s Stepfather: 5 Fast Facts You Need to Know|website=Heavy.com|date=2019-05-28|access-date=2019-05-29}}</ref> In 2005, Ellen spoke publicly for the first time about the molestation she had suffered from her stepfather when she was a teenager.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/celebrity/ellen-degeneres-i-was-molested/|title=Ellen DeGeneres: I Was Molested|website=People|date=2005-05-18|access-date=2019-05-29}}</ref> DeGeneres returned to LSU after the age of 50 to complete her master's degree in speech pathology.<ref name=":0" />

DeGeneres is the author of two books: ''Love, Ellen: A Mother Daughter Journey'' and ''Just a Mom''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/40650.Betty_DeGeneres|title=Betty DeGeneres|website=Goodreads|access-date=2017-05-10}}</ref> In ''Love, Ellen: A Mother Daughter Journey'', Betty describes her reaction to her daughter coming out as a lesbian, and her path from passive acceptance to becoming a passionate advocate for LGBT rights.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books/about/Love_Ellen.html?id=pAT6rTEyFuUC&redir_esc=y|title=Love, Ellen: A Mother/Daughter Journey|last=Degeneres|first=Betty|date=2000-04-26|publisher=Harper Collins|isbn=9780688176884|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://people.com/tv/ellen-degeneres-mom-remembers-how-she-came-out-as-gay/|title=Ellen DeGeneres’ Mom Remembers How She Came Out as Gay|date=2015-06-26|work=PEOPLE.com|access-date=2017-08-10|language=en-US}}</ref> She also describes the media scrutiny she received after Ellen came out. Her second book, ''Just a Mom'', continues these themes. She also wrote a column for the (now defunct) website [[PlanetOut]] called "Ask Betty."<ref>{{cite web | title =PlanetOut Gay and Lesbian Internet Site and Betty DeGeneres, Author And Spokesmom, Launch New Online Advice Channel 'Ask Betty' to Celebrate Gay Pride Weekend 1999 |url = http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-54978488.html |publisher = Highbeam.com |accessdate = 2012-02-21 }}{{dead link|date=February 2019|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

DeGeneres appeared as an extra in the episode "[[The Puppy Episode]]" of the television sitcom ''[[Ellen (TV series)|Ellen]]'', in which her daughter Ellen's character comes out. DeGeneres appears in many episodes of ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'', sitting in the audience.<ref name=":0" />

DeGeneres survived [[breast cancer]] and discusses this during public appearances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/people/2007-09-26-ellen-degeneres_N.htm|title=DeGeneres on a mission: Breast cancer awareness - USATODAY.com|website=usatoday30.usatoday.com|access-date=2017-05-10}}</ref>
