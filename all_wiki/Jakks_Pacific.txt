'''Jakks Pacific, Inc.''' is an American company that designs and markets toys and consumer products, with a range of products that feature numerous children's toy licenses. The company is named after its founder, [[Jack Friedman]], who had previously founded [[LJN]] and [[THQ]] and presided over the company until retiring as CEO and chairman after March 31, 2010,<ref name="1stPresident_01">{{cite web|last=Caoili |first=Eric |title=THQ, Jakks Founder Jack Friedman Passes Away At 70 |url=https://www.gamasutra.com/view/news/119315/THQ_Jakks_Founder_Jack_Friedman_Passes_Away_At_70.php |publisher=[[Gamasutra]] |accessdate=22 July 2020 |date=3 May 2010}}</ref> a month before his death on 3 May, 2010.<ref name="1stPresident_02">{{cite web|last=Crowe |first=Deborah |title=Jakks Founder Jack Friedman Dies (LABJ) |url=https://labusinessjournal.com/news/2010/may/03/jakks-founder-jack-friedman-dies/ |publisher=[[Los Angeles Business Journal]]|accessdate=22 July 2020|date=3 May 2010}}</ref><ref name="1stPresident_03">{{cite web|author=IGN staff |title=Jakks Pacific Founder, Chairman Jack Friedman Dies (IGN. Updated 15 Jun 2012.) |url=https://www.ign.com/articles/2010/05/04/jakks-pacific-founder-chairman-jack-friedman-dies |publisher=[[IGN]]|accessdate=22 July 2020|date=5 May 2010}}</ref><ref name="Friedman_dies">{{cite web|last=Nelson |first=Valerie J. |title=Jack Friedman dies at 70; toy maker|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2010/may/06/local/la-me-jack-friedman-20100506|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|accessdate=3 December 2012|date=6 May 2010}}</ref>

Jakks produces action figures, electronics, dolls, dress-up, role play, Halloween costumes, kids' furniture, vehicles, plush, art activity kits, seasonal products, infant/preschool, construction toys, and pet toys sold under various proprietary brands including Jakks Pacific, Creative Designs International, [[Road Champions]], Funnoodle, Go Fly a Kite, Jakks Pets, EyeClops, Plug It In & Play TV Games (which came out in 2007), Girl Gourmet, Kids Only!, Tollytots and Disguise. Jakks is a licensee of several hundred trademarks including [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]], ''[[Star Wars]]'' and [[Nintendo]].

==History==
[[File:Jakks pacific low res.png|thumb|Jakks Pacific's logo, 1995–2015]]
Jakks Pacific, Inc. was founded in 1995. Jakks acquired several companies including [[Remco]], Child Guidance, and [[Road Champions]] in 1997, Berk and Flying Colors in 1999, Pentech in 2000, Toymax, Go Fly A Kite and Funnoodle in 2002, Color Workshop and [[Trendmasters]] in 2003, [[Play Along Toys]] in 2004, Pet Pal Corp. in 2005, Creative Designs International in 2006, and Kids Only Toys, Tollytots, and Disguise in 2008.

In February 2005, Jakks Pacific was instructed to restate the financial statements for fiscal year 2003 to account for the acquisition of Toymax, Trendmasters and P&M Products.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20050222005502/en/JAKKS-Pacific-Reports-Preliminary-Fourth-Quarter-Year-End| title=JAKKS Pacific Reports Preliminary Fourth Quarter and Year-End Results for 2004}}</ref>

In 2010, the company formed Pacific Animation Partners LLC, a joint venture between it and [[Dentsu|Dentsu Entertainment USA]], [[Fremantle (company)|Fremantle]] and [[Topps]], to launch the ''[[Monsuno]]'' property in 2012.<ref>{{ cite web|url=http://toybook.com/jakks-partners-with-dentsu-for-new-boys-action-property/|title=JAKKS Partners with Dentsu for New Boys Action Property|publisher=Toybook|accessdate=2016-10-17|date=2010-01-07}}</ref><ref>{{ cite web|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/tv/nicktoons-debuts-monsuno-feb-23/|title=Nicktoons Debuts 'Monsuno' Feb. 23|publisher=[[Animation Magazine]]|accessdate=2016-10-17|date=2011-02-06}}</ref> 

In 2012, Jakks Pacific announced the successful negotiation of licences for ''[[The Dark Knight Rises]]'' and [[The Amazing Spider-Man (2012 film)|''The Amazing Spider-Man'']], promoting new toy ranges at the New York and other International Toy Fairs.

In 2014, the company launched Jakks Meisheng Trading, a joint venture with Meisheng Culture and Creative Corp., to bring its toys to [[China]].<ref>{{ cite web|last=Brzoznowski|first=Kristin|url=http://en.yibada.com/articles/7937/20141113/jakks-pacific-sets-up-new-joint-venture-in-china.htm|title=Jakks Pacific Sets Up New Joint Venture in China|publisher=[[Yibada]]|accessdate=2016-10-17|date=2014-11-13}}</ref> In 2016, the two created Studio JP, a joint venture to produce animation.<ref>{{ cite web|last=Milligan|first=Mercedes|url=http://worldscreen.com/tvkids/jakks-pacific-sets-up-content-creation-studio/|title=JAKKS Pacific Sets Up Content-Creation Studio|publisher=WorldScreen|accessdate=2016-10-17|date=2016-10-16}}</ref>

==Products==
{{unreferenced|section|date=February 2019}}

The company makes a variety of toys including Plug It In and Play TV games, various electronics, and action figures.  The company also licenses global brand names for manufactured toys.

===Plug It In & Play TV Games===
'''Plug It In & Play TV Games''' interactive products are a series of [[plug-n-play game]] devices produced by Jakks Pacific. When connected to a [[television set]], the user is able to play a pre-defined selection of [[video game]]s.  Some models are collections of ports of games by companies such as [[Atari]] and [[Namco]], while others are collections of original games.  Some versions facilitate the addition of games, via proprietary [[GameKey]] expansion cards, and/or include wireless features.
