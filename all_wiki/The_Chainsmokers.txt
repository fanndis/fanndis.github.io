'''The Chainsmokers''' is<!-- NOTE: Do NOT change to "are" group is a singular unit --> an American electronic DJ and production duo consisting of Alexander "Alex" Pall and Andrew "Drew" Taggart (with Matt McGuire on live shows). They started out by releasing remixes of songs by indie artists. The [[electronic dance music|EDM]]-[[Pop music|pop]] duo achieved a breakthrough with their 2014 song "[[Selfie (song)|#Selfie]]", which became a top twenty single in several countries. They have won a Grammy award,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/artist/the-chainsmokers|title=The Chainsmokers|work=Grammy}}</ref> two [[American Music Award]]s,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.theamas.com/winners/2016-winners/ |title= American Music Award Winners 2016 |work=TheAMAs}}</ref> seven [[Billboard Music Awards]] and nine [[iHeartRadio Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.iheart.com/news/heres-the-complete-list-of-iheartradio-15616268/ |title= Complete List of #iHeartAwards Winners |work= iHeart |access-date= March 24, 2017 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170409080744/https://www.iheart.com/news/heres-the-complete-list-of-iheartradio-15616268/ |archive-date= April 9, 2017 |url-status= dead |df= mdy-all }}</ref> According to ''[[Forbes]]'' 2019 list of highest paid celebrities, the Chainsmokers were the highest paid DJs, dethroning [[Calvin Harris]] after six years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://weraveyou.com/2019/07/calvin-harris-forbes-dethroned/|title=Calvin Harris dethroned from his highest paid DJ spot on Forbes after 6 years|last=Potrc|first=About the Author / Barbara|date=July 13, 2019|website=We Rave You|access-date=July 15, 2019}}</ref>

Their debut EP, ''[[Bouquet (EP)|Bouquet]]'', was released in October 2015 and featured the single "Roses" which reached the top 10 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. "Don't Let Me Down", featuring American singer [[Daya (singer)|Daya]], became their first top-five single on the Billboard chart and won the [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording]] at the [[59th Grammy Awards|59th awards ceremony]]. Their single "[[Closer (Chainsmokers song)|Closer]]", featuring American singer-songwriter [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]], became their first number-one single on the Billboard  chart. The duo's second EP, ''[[Collage (EP)|Collage]]'', was released in November 2016. Their debut studio album, ''[[Memories...Do Not Open]]'', was released in April 2017 and topped the US Billboard 200 chart. Their second album, ''[[Sick Boy (album)|Sick Boy]]'', was released in December 2018.

==Career==
===2012: Formation===
The Chainsmokers were initially made up of Pall and former DJ Rhett Bixler.<ref name="wireimage">{{cite web|last1=Steve Mack / Stringer|title=The Chainsmokers DJs Rhett Bixler and Alex Pall perform at the Grand... {{!}} WireImage {{!}} 131499892 |url= http://www.wireimage.com/celebrity-pictures/The-Chainsmokers-DJs-Rhett-Bixler-and-Alex-Pall-perform-at-the-Grand/131499892 |website= www.wireimage.com |publisher=Getty Images |accessdate=April 28, 2017 |location= New York, NY |date=November 4, 2011 }}</ref><ref name="catalog" /> The Chainsmokers were re-formed as an EDM DJ duo in 2012 under the management of Adam Alpert in [[New York City]]. Pall attended [[New York University]] for art history and music business<ref name="raver">{{cite web |url= http://raverrafting.com/the-chainsmokers-interview/2013/05/16/ |title=The Chainsmokers Talk Early Beginnings, Favorite Gigs To Play, Love Of Music Blogs And Yoga Pants (Exclusive Interview) |last= Alvodomar |first=Tyler |website= Raver Rafting |accessdate= October 16, 2016}}</ref> while Taggart had been attending [[Syracuse University]] and was an [[intern]] at [[Interscope Records]] before the two met. Taggart had taken an interest in being a DJ and released some original songs on the website [[SoundCloud]]. Taggart was informed by someone working for Alpert that a duo, which was being managed by Alpert, had an open spot after Bixler left, which prompted Taggart to leave [[Maine]] in order to go to [[New York City]].<ref name="interview">{{cite web|url= http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/the-chainsmokers#_ |title=The Chainsmokers |last= Rosenzweig |first= Mathias |website= Interview Magazine |accessdate= October 12, 2016}}</ref> Pall, who had grown up as a DJ and had been working at an [[art gallery]] in [[Chelsea, Manhattan|Chelsea]], [[Manhattan]] at the time, was introduced to Taggart by Alpert.<ref name="catalog">{{cite web |url= http://thoughtcatalog.com/thought-catalog/2010/08/alex-bixler-chainsmokers-new-york-city-stadiumred-rhett-pall/ |title= The Chainsmokers: Party People |website= Thought Catalog |date=August 25, 2010 |accessdate= October 16, 2016}}</ref> In April 2013, The Chainsmokers released a remix of "Medicine" by [[Daughter (band)|Daughter]] and it reached number 1 on [[HypeMachine]].<ref name="YGM-20130404">{{cite web |last1=Brakatselos |first1=Nick |title=Daughter – Medicine (The Chainsmokers Remix) |url=http://yesgoodmusic.com/daughter-medicine-the-chainsmokers-remix |website=YesGoodMusic.com |accessdate=March 15, 2019 |date=April 4, 2013}}</ref>

The duo started out by making remixes of indie bands.<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/music/american-dj-duo-the-chainsmokers-move-from-indie-remixes-to-topping-charts/news-story/e32bff34a373e6f7346d00a5f979986a |title= Rise of The Chainsmokers: How Andrew Taggart and Alex Pall went from remixes to chart toppers |accessdate= August 27, 2016}}</ref> In 2012, they collaborated with Indian actress and recording artist [[Priyanka Chopra]] on the single "Erase" which was followed by "The Rookie" in early 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.hindustantimes.com/brunch/priyanka-chopra-the-girl-who-leapt-through-time/story-Rt4Vwx9Nb8c9z0Damv9N6K.html |title= Priyanka Chopra: The girl who leapt through time |date= October 10, 2015 |publisher= Hindustan Times}}</ref>

During an October 2016 television interview, ABC News Nightline reporter Nick Watt asked Pall and Taggart "What's with the (duo's) name?" Pall replied that, "At the time of conception, it was... it was totally just like I was in college. You know I enjoyed smoking weed and you know it was just like such a 'yeah the domain's open'. I don't have to have any, like, underscores." Taggart added "It's just a name."<ref>{{cite web|title=The Chainsmokers {{!}} Inside the Studio with Alex Pall, Drew Taggart|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D1Hc7xMd1Jo|publisher=ABC News {{!}} Nightline|accessdate=April 28, 2017|date=October 6, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2013–2014: First live performance and breakthrough ===
[[File:The Chainsmokers - Logo 2013.jpg|thumb|right|Logo before 2015.]]
The duo's first live performance was opening for [[Timeflies]] at [[Terminal 5 (venue)|Terminal 5]] in September 2014.<ref name="raver" /> Their single "[[Selfie (song)|#Selfie]]", released for free in December 2013, was picked up by [[Dim Mak Records]] who re-released it in January 2014 and eventually streamed it to [[Republic Records]].<ref name="daya" /> The duo achieved breakthrough success as the single charted internationally and peaked on the [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/5794072/The+Chainsmokers/chart?f=1234|title=The Chainsmokers – Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|accessdate=August 27, 2016}}</ref> Pall has described the song as "life-changing" for the pair.<ref name="askmen">{{cite web|url=http://www.askmen.com/recess/interviews/chainsmokers-interview.html|title=What's It Like To Be An EDM Star & Trash Hotel Rooms? The Chainsmokers Know|last=Morris|first=David|website=AskMen|accessdate=October 14, 2016}}</ref>

On August 5, 2014, the Chainsmokers released "Kanye", featuring sirenXX, the follow-up to "#Selfie". Seven months later, they released "Let You Go", featuring the American synthpop group [[Great Good Fine Ok]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/6873941/miguel-chainsmokers-grammy-party-recap-dance|title=The Chainsmokers and Miguel Delight During Grammy Week|work=Billboard}}</ref> They signed with Disruptor Records, a joint venture label with [[Sony Music Entertainment]] by their manager [[Adam Alpert]], in April 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/legal-and-management/6243757/exclusive-sony-music-forms-disruptor-with|title=Exclusive: Sony Music Forms Disruptor With Chainsmokers Manager|work=Billboard}}</ref>

=== 2015–2016: ''Bouquet'' and ''Collage'' ===
{{See also|Bouquet (EP)|Collage (EP)}}
[[File:The Chainsmokers - Logo.jpg|thumb|right|Logo from 2015 to 2018.]]
On May 18, 2015, they released the single "Good Intentions".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.beatport.com/release/good-intentions-extended-mix/1523732|title=Good Intentions (Extended Mix) from Disruptor Records/Columbia on Beatport|website=www.beatport.com|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref> Their follow-up single, "[[Roses (The Chainsmokers song)|Roses]]", was released a month later on June 16.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://play.google.com/store/music/album/The_Chainsmokers_Roses?id=Brwvoerlo7ucelacz74frvgr7cq&hl=en_US|title=The Chainsmokers: Roses – Music on Google Play|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref> "Roses" became a commercial success and has been certified multiplatinum in several countries, including the duo's native United States where it reached the top 10 of the Billboard chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6859556/justin-bieber-replaces-himself-number-1-hot-100|title=Justin Bieber Replaces Himself at No. 1 on Hot 100 With 'Love Yourself'|work=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=The+Chainsmokers&ti=Roses#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum – RIAA|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref> Further singles included "Waterbed", a collaboration with Dutch DJ [[Tiësto]] entitled "Split (Only U)", and "[[Until You Were Gone (The Chainsmokers and Tritonal song)|Until You Were Gone]]" which was released on September 18, 2015. These songs, except "Split (Only U)", were featured on the group's debut EP, ''[[Bouquet (EP)|Bouquet]]'', which was released on October 23, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/7615803/chainsmokers-interview-roses-bouquet-ep|title=The Chainsmokers On Rising Hit "Roses," Debut EP 'Bouquet' & Leaving "#Selfie" Behind: Idolator Interview – Idolator|date=November 23, 2015|publisher=Idolator}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/bouquet-ep/1050203612|title=Bouquet – EP by The Chainsmokers|date=October 23, 2015|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref> A week later, the song "[[New York City (The Chainsmokers song)|New York City]]" was released as the fifth and final single from the EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rateyourmusic.com/release/single/the-chainsmokers/new-york-city/|title=New York City by The Chainsmokers|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref>

====Safe & Sound charity concert====
On July 14, 2020, after having largely isolated themselves since the social media hiatus and the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], the duo announced plans to hold a "[[Drive-in theater|drive-in]]" charity concert, "Safe & Sound", on July 25, as their first in-person concert since January 2020 prior to [[Super Bowl LIV]]. The concert was presented by [[Fuckjerry|FuckJerry]]'s [[Tequila]] brand JAJA, which is co-owned by The Chainsmokers. The concert was stated to be a "socially-distanced drive-in show to benefit the charities [[No Kid Hungry]], Southampton Fresh Air Home, and the Children's Medical Fund of New York" and was meant to be a future example of [[COVID-19 pandemic]] concert audience safety. On 25 July 2020, The Chainsmokers and [[Goldman Sachs|Goldman Sachs']] [[Chief executive officer|CEO]], [[David M. Solomon|David Solomon]], who produces [[electronic dance music]] under the [[stage name]] DJ D-Sol, headlined the [[Benefit concert|charity concert]] in [[Southampton, New York]]. The event was stated to follow guidelines issued by the [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|CDC]] in order to prevent the spread of [[Coronavirus disease 2019]] (COVID-19), including attendees being required to take temperature checks for 14 days prior to the event and when entering, being provided with complimentary face masks, and instructed to remain within the vicinity of their parked vehicle unless using a restroom. At the time of the concert, the state of New York prohibited non-essential gatherings of more than 50 people.<ref name=":1" /> However, footage from the event on social media seemingly showed a large crowd of attendees in a [[mosh pit]], allegedly in violation of [[social distancing]] recommendations.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-14|title=Here's Everything We Know About the Chainsmokers' Upcoming Drive-In Concert|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/9418214/the-chainsmokers-drive-in-concert-everything-we-know|access-date=2020-07-28|website=Billboard|publisher=[[MRC (company)|MRC Media & Info]]}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Moayeri|first=Lilydate=2020-07-28|title=Chainsmokers Draw Heat for Hamptons Drive-In Concert That NY Health Commissioner Calls 'Public Health Threat'|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/chainsmokers-hamptons-drive-in-crowd-concert-1234717898/|access-date=2020-07-28|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref> The concert received backlash from [[social media]] users who pointed out that images and videos posted by the concert-goers did not promote [[social distancing]] as the concert promoters had initially stated.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Paiella|first=Gabriella|title=Everything to Know About the Ill-Advised Chainsmokers Hamptons Concert feat. the CEO of Goldman Sachs|url=https://www.gq.com/story/chainsmokers-hamptons-concert|access-date=2020-07-29|website=GQ|language=en-us}}</ref> The event was investigated by the state of New York health authorities for "rampant" violations of [[social distancing]] guidelines.<ref>{{cite news |last=de la Merced |first= Michael J. |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/07/28/business/goldman-sachs-ceo-played-an-ill-fated-dj-gig-in-the-hamptons.html |title=Goldman Sachs C.E.O. played an ill-fated DJ gig in the Hamptons. |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=2020-07-28 |accessdate=2020-07-28 }}</ref>

He is the band's primary songwriter, and performs vocals and guitar on some songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/closer-mw0002971877/credits|title=Closer – The Chainsmokers {{!}} Credits {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=June 7, 2018}}</ref> His mother is a teacher and his father sold prosthetics.<ref name="portland press">{{cite news |last1=Routhier |first1= Ray |title= Freeport grad rockets to selfie-made stardom – Portland Press Herald |url= http://www.pressherald.com/2014/10/20/freeport-grad-rockets-to-selfie-made-stardom/ |accessdate= April 28, 2017 |work= Press Herald |issue= Updated December 6, 2016 |publisher= Portland Press Herald |date=October 20, 2014}}</ref> He got into [[electronic dance music]] at the age of 15 while abroad in [[Argentina]], where he was introduced to the music of [[David Guetta]], [[Daft Punk]] and [[Trentemøller]].<ref name="billboard"/>

Matt McGuire serves as the drummer and music director for live shows, and currently performs with them when they go on tour. McGuire also frequently appears alongside the duo in various music videos.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7694356/the-chainsmokers-drummer-matt-mcguire-arena-tour|title=The Chainsmokers Add 'Savage' Aussie Drummer Matt McGuire for Arena Tour|website=Billboard|access-date=May 22, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youredm.com/2018/01/25/chainsmokers-drummer-opens-success-social-media-sick-boy/|title="It's like hitting you in the f*cking face with reality": The Chainsmokers' Drummer Reveals All|last=karsoowuttt|date=January 25, 2018|website=Your EDM|access-date=May 22, 2019}}</ref>

Tony Ann served as the live keyboard player, performing with them on the "Memories...Do Not Open Tour". He also has songwriting credits on their singles "Sick Boy", "Side Effects", and "Call You Mine".<ref>{{Citation|title=The Chainsmokers & Bebe Rexha – Call You Mine|url=https://genius.com/The-chainsmokers-and-bebe-rexha-call-you-mine-lyrics|access-date=June 28, 2019}}</ref>
