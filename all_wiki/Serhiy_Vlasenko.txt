'''Serhiy Vlasenko''' ({{lang-uk|Сергій Володимирович Власенко}}; born on 7 March 1967 in [[Lviv]]<ref name=LIGA>{{in lang|ru}} [http://file.liga.net/person/510-sergei-vlasenko.html Biography], LIGA</ref><ref name=GazetaSV>{{in lang|uk}} [http://gazeta.ua/ru/election/candidat/1697 Biography] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20130419092620/http://gazeta.ua/ru/election/candidat/1697 |date=2013-04-19 }}, [[Gazeta.ua]]</ref>) is a [[Ukraine|Ukrainian]] politician and lawyer.<ref name=LIGA/><ref name=GazetaSV/><ref name=SLlawer1/>

==Early life and career==
In 1992, Vlasenko graduated from the law department of the [[Lviv University]].<ref name=LIGA/> From 1992 till March 2008, he worked for various [[law firm]]s in Ukraine (as a partner of [[Magisters]] from 2006).<ref name=LIGA/> In March 2008 Vlasenko was appointed deputy head of the [[State Tax Administration of Ukraine]].<ref name=LIGA/>

==Political career==
In 2007 Vlasenko became a member of the [[Ukraine – Forward!#Ukrainian Social Democratic Party|Ukrainian Social Democratic Party]].<ref name=LIGA/> in the [[2007 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2007 parliamentary elections]] he was placed at number 170 on the electoral list of  the [[Yulia Tymoshenko Bloc]] and was not elected into the [[Ukrainian Parliament]].<ref name=LIGA/> In June 2008, Vlasenko became [[People's Deputy of Ukraine]] and a member of the faction of the [[Yulia Tymoshenko Bloc]].<ref name=LIGA/> After he left the post of deputy head of the State Tax Administration.<ref name=LIGA/>

Vlasenko is [[Yulia Tymoshenko]]'s lawyer in [[Criminal cases against Yulia Tymoshenko since 2010|the various court cases against her (since 2010)]].<ref name=SLlawer1>[http://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/vlasenko-kozhemiakin-plakhotniuk-to-be-tymoshenkos-defense-lawyers-in-scherban-case-319207.html Vlasenko, Kozhemiakin, Plakhotniuk to be Tymoshenko's defense lawyers in Scherban case], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (22 January 2013)</ref><ref>[http://www.ukrainebusiness.com.ua/news/2331.html Tymoshenko claims new case on gas contracts without merit], [[Ukraine Business Online]] (19 May 2011)<br />[http://www.rferl.org/content/us_law_firm_says_charges_against_tymoshenko_are_not_based_on_facts/24238686.html U.S. Law Firm Says Charges Against Tymoshenko Are Not Based On Facts], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (17 June 2011)</ref><ref name=VRN5312/>

Vlasenko was placed at number 20 on the electoral list of [[Batkivshchina]] during the [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election]].<ref>[http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/58995 They Call Themselves the Opposition], [[The Ukrainian Week]] (31 August 2012)</ref><ref name="electedintoVRUK111112"/> He was re-elected into parliament.<ref name="electedintoVRUK111112">{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/articles/2012/11/11/6977259/ Список депутатів нової Верховної Ради], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (11 November 2012)</ref> Vlasenko is a member of Batkivshchina.<ref name=GazetaSV/>

On 21 January 2013, Vlasenko stated that he stood accused of car theft, robbery and failing to obey a court ruling stemming from his divorce several years ago.<ref name="GuardianVlasenko">[https://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/jan/21/ukraine-tymoshenko-lawyer-criminal-charges Lawyer of former Ukraine PM Yulia Tymoshenko 'faces criminal charges'], [[Guardian.co.uk]] (21 January 2013)</ref> On 5 March 2013, this investigation was "closed for lack of crime".<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2013/03/5/6984874/ ГПУ: провадження проти Власенка закрито ''GPU: proceedings against Vlasenko closed''], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (5 March 2013)</ref> As of 13 March 2013, [[Ukrainian prosecutors]] are investigating allegations of Vlasenko's former wife that he beat her; as a result of this, Vlasenko can not leave Ukraine.<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/tymoshenkos-lawyer-vlasenko-not-let-out-of-ukraine-again-3-321636.html Tymoshenko's lawyer Vlasenko not let out of Ukraine again], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (13 March 2013)</ref>

On 28 February 2013, [[Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada]] [[Volodymyr Vasylyovych Rybak|Volodymyr Rybak]] inquired to the [[Higher Administrative Court of Ukraine]] on depriving Vlasenko of his seat in parliament<ref>[http://www.interfax.co.uk/ukraine-news/batkivschyna-again-blocks-parliaments-work-2/ Batkivschyna again blocks parliament’s work], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (5 March 2013)<br />[http://www.interfax.co.uk/ukraine-news/verkhovna-rada-chair-sends-to-higher-administrative-court-inquiry-on-depriving-vlasenko-of-parliamentary-seat-says-turchynov-3/ Verkhovna Rada Chair sends to Higher Administrative Court inquiry on depriving Vlasenko of parliamentary seat, says Turchynov], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (1 March 2013)</ref> because he continued to engage in other than parliamentary activity.<ref name=VRN5312/> Vlasenko claimed his legal license was annulled and he was no longer engaged in any extra-parliamentary activity.<ref name=VRN5312>[http://en.rian.ru/world/20130305/179829825.html Ukraine Opposition Obstructs Parliament], [[RIA Novosti]] (5 March 2013)</ref> On 5 March 2012 [[European Commissioner for Enlargement and European Neighbourhood Policy|European Union (EU) Commissioner for Enlargement]] [[Štefan Füle]] and [[High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy|EU High Representative for Foreign Affairs]] [[Catherine Ashton]] called on the Ukrainian authorities to address this situation so as to avoid creating any perception of misuse of the judiciary for political purposes.<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/opinion/op-ed/catherine-ashton-and-stefan-fule-concerned-about-political-developments-in-ukraine-321291.html Catherine Ashton and Štefan Füle concerned about political developments in Ukraine], [[Kyiv Post]] (5 March 2013)</ref> The [[U.S. Department of State]] agreed with the EU on this and added the case appeared to be politically motivated due to Vlasenko's connection with Yulia Tymoshenko.<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/us-call-attempt-to-strip-vlasenko-of-his-parliamentary-seat-politically-motivated-321333.html US call attempt to strip Vlasenko of his parliamentary seat politically motivated], [[Kyiv Post]] (6 March 2013)</ref> The Higher Administrative Court annulled the parliamentary mandate of Vlasenko on 6 March 2013.<ref>[http://www.interfax.co.uk/ukraine-news/court-deprives-vlasenko-of-parliamentary-mandate-2/ Court deprives Vlasenko of parliamentary mandate], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (6 March 2013)</ref> Štefan Füle considered this "not European way" the same day.<ref>[http://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/fule-stripping-vlasenko-of-his-mandate-not-european-way-321374.html Fule: Stripping Vlasenko of his mandate not European way], ''[[Kyiv Post]]'' (6 March 2013)</ref>

On 4 March 2014, the Verkhovna Rada returned a deputy mandate to Vlasenko, and Speaker [[Oleksandr Turchynov]] said that "according to a Higher Administrative Court ruling of February 28, 2014, Vlasenko is a people's deputy".<ref name=VN14314/> The Ukrainian Prosecutor General's Office closed criminal proceedings against Vlasenko "due to the absence of a crime in his actions" on 14 March 2014.<ref name=VN14314>http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/195890.html</ref>

In the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] he was again re-elected into parliament; this time after placing 15th on the electoral list of Batkivshchina.<ref name="allcountedCECIU81114">[http://www.ukrinform.ua/eng/news/poroshenko_bloc_to_have_greatest_number_of_seats_in_parliament_327072 Poroshenko Bloc to have greatest number of seats in parliament], [[Ukrinform]] (8 November 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233404.html People's Front 0.33% ahead of Poroshenko Bloc with all ballots counted in Ukraine elections - CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (8 November 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233426.html Poroshenko Bloc to get 132 seats in parliament - CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (8 November 2014)</ref><ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://tvi.ua/new/2014/09/15/povnyy_vyborchyy_spysok_partiyi_batkivschyna Full electoral list of "Fatherland"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140915212430/http://tvi.ua/new/2014/09/15/povnyy_vyborchyy_spysok_partiyi_batkivschyna |date=2014-09-15 }}, [[TVi (TV channel)|TVi]] (15 September 2014)</ref>

In addition to his work in parliament, Vlasenko is a member of the Ukrainian delegation to the [[Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe]] (PACE). He serves on the Assembly's Committee on the Election of [[List of judges of the European Court of Human Rights|Judges]] to the [[European Court of Human Rights]]; the Committee on Legal Affairs and Human Rights; the Committee on Migration, Refugees and Displaced Persons; and the Sub-Committee on the implementation of judgments of the European Court of Human Rights.
