'''Ralf Georg Reuth''' (born 4 June 1952 in [[Oberfranken]]) is a German journalist and historian.<ref name="S1">Enrico Syring: ''Review: Ralf Georg Reuth, Hitler. Eine politische Biographie''. In: ''[[Militärgeschichtliche Zeitschrift]]'' 62 (2003), p. 596.</ref> Reuth studied with [[Andreas Hillgruber]] and wrote his Ph.D. on the German strategy in the Mediterranean from 1940 to 1942.<ref name="S1" /> He published several books dealing with the Nazi era, among them  a biography of [[Joseph Goebbels]] in 1992 and of [[Adolf Hitler|Hitler]] in 2003. Reuth also edited a multivolume selection from the diaries of Joseph Goebbels, which drew criticism notably from Bernd Sösemann.<ref name="S1" /> He also wrote biographies of [[Erwin Rommel]], [[Angela Merkel]] and [[Helmut Kohl]].

Reuth's 2004 book ''Rommel: Das Ende einer Legende'', dedicated to the topic of the [[Rommel myth]], has started the reevaluation of Rommel's role in history.{{sfn|Beckett|2014|pp=1-2}} The book was published in English in 2005 as ''Rommel: The End of a Legend''.
