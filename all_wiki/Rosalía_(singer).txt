== Impact ==
Music in Spain in the 20th century was a huge art. The popularitzation and growing international recognition of Spanish musical legends such as [[Julio Iglesias]] and his son [[Enrique Iglesias|Enrique]], [[Camar√≥n de la Isla]], [[Joan Manuel Serrat]], [[Joaqu√≠n Sabina]], [[Alejandro Sanz]], [[Miguel Bos√©]], [[Roc√≠o Jurado]] or [[Isabel Pantoja]] was very notable and pleasing at the time, making the country reach a satisfying peak of musical projection. Despite its huge projection, Spain's interest in the music industry began to decrease progressively, affecting a huge amount of potential artists. After the [[2008‚Äì2014 Spanish financial crisis|Spanish financial crisis]] began in 2008, the [[music industry]] received zero interest by the population since there was nothing fresh to bet on or to finance. Art in Spain became truly despised and wasted, expanding the thought that studying and/or performing arts is useless. Rosal√≠a, however, stated that she wanted to study musical arts since she was a teenager, something very rare at the time.

Rosal√≠a is the main character in the first tidal wave of Spanish international musicians of the 21st century. Her rise to fame came just when it was needed. Tangana]], [[La Zowi]] or [[Bad Gyal]] started to make fresh urban music influenced by the folklore of regions like [[Puerto Rico]], [[Jamaica]] or [[Colombia]].
