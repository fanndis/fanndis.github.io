"'''Eyes Open'''" is a song written and recorded by American singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]] for ''[[The Hunger Games (film)|The Hunger Games]]'' movie soundtrack, ''[[The Hunger Games: Songs from District 12 and Beyond]]'', though the song is not included in the film itself. Produced by Swift and [[Nathan Chapman (record producer)|Nathan Chapman]], it was released as the second [[single (music)|single]] from the album and was sent to mainstream radio on March 27, 2012. A [[music video|lyric video]] consisting of animation was released on [[Vevo]] in May 2012.

==Background==
The song was one of two songs that Swift wrote and recorded for the [[The Hunger Games: Songs from District 12 and Beyond|soundtrack to the film]], ''[[The Hunger Games (film)|The Hunger Games]]'', the other song being [[Safe & Sound (Taylor Swift song)|"Safe & Sound"]]. Swift first premiered the song during a performance in [[Auckland]] for her [[Speak Now World Tour]], before launching into the song she told the crowd: "I'm really excited about it...but, I mean, you don't think I'd get in trouble if I played it now? Probably not, right?"<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.eonline.com/news/taylor_swift_unveils_hunger_games_song/302181 | title=Taylor Swift Unveils Hunger Games Song "Eyes Open" | work=[[E! Online]] | date=March 19, 2012 | accessdate=March 28, 2012 | last=Josh | first=Grossman}}</ref> The song was leaked before its official release date of March 20, 2012.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/03/15/hunger-games-taylor-swift-kid-cudi_n_1347337.html | title='Hunger Games': Taylor Swift's 'Eyes Open' And Kid Cudi's 'The Ruler And The Killer' Leak (LISTEN) | work=[[The Huffington Post]] | date=March 15, 2012 | accessdate=March 28, 2012 | author=Bell, Crystal}}</ref> A [[music video]] comprised as a lyric video was released on May 17, 2012 on Swift's [[Vevo]] account.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://taylorswift.com/media/videos/15853 |title=Music Videos |publisher=Taylor Swift |accessdate=2012-06-11 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120527200630/http://taylorswift.com/media/videos/15853 |archivedate=2012-05-27 }}</ref><ref name="youtube">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8hsVICl7d8k&ob=av2e|title=Taylor Swift - Eyes Open (Lyric Version)|work=TaylorSwiftVevo|publisher=[[YouTube]]|date=2012-05-17|accessdate=2012-05-21}}

"Eyes Open" is a song of four minutes and four seconds in length. Critics deemed the song reminiscent of [[alternative rock]].<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.entertainmentwise.com/news/71508/Taylor-Swift-Previews-Hunger-Games-Song-Eyes-Open| title=Taylor Swift Previews Hunger Games Song 'Eyes Open'| url-status=dead| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120316020201/http://www.entertainmentwise.com/news/71508/Taylor-Swift-Previews-Hunger-Games-Song-Eyes-Open| archivedate=2012-03-16}}</ref><ref name="Billboard">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/501236/taylor-swift-keeps-eyes-open-on-new-hunger-games-tune|title=Taylor Swift Keeps 'Eyes Open' on New 'Hunger Games' Tune|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|quote=Existing more in the modern rock vein than her usual country-pop oeuvre, "Eyes Open" finds Swift living her own Hunger Games, clinging to survival...|first=Jason|last=Lipshutz|date=15 March 2012}}</ref> According to Swift, she wrote the song about Katniss's "relationship" with the Capitol. Swift describes the song as the opposite of the melancholic "Safe & Sound," stating that "[it is] more frantic and fast-paced, a completely different shade of music."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/taylor-swift-talks-eyes-open-hunger-games-soundtrack/ |title=Taylor Swift Spills About Writing 'Eyes Open' for 'The Hunger Games' Soundtrack |publisher=[[Taste of Country]] |first=Brittany Joy| last=Cooper| date=20 March 2012}}</ref>

==Critical reception==
The song garnered general critical acclaim from contemporary critics - most of whom favorably noted the song's more rock-oriented tone as compared to her previous releases. While reviewing ''[[The Hunger Games: Songs from District 12 and Beyond]]'', Matt Bjorke named "Eyes Open" as one of the "standouts" on the record.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/various-artists-the-hunger-games-soundtrack| title=Various Artists - The Hunger Games Soundtrack| url-status=dead| archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120529114440/http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/various-artists-the-hunger-games-soundtrack| archivedate=2012-05-29}}</ref> Jason Lipshutz of [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] also found the same description of the song - stating that it "[exists] more in the modern rock vein than her usual country-pop oeuvre."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lipshutz |first1=Jason |title=Taylor Swift Keeps 'Eyes Open' on New 'Hunger Games' Tune |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/501236/taylor-swift-keeps-eyes-open-on-new-hunger-games-tune |website=Billboard |accessdate=1 October 2018}}</ref>

==Lyric video==
A lyric video for the song was released on Swift’s YouTube channel on May 17, 2012. {{As of|January 2020}} the video has over 20 million views.<ref>{{cite web|title=Taylor Swift - Eyes Open (Lyric Version)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8hsVICl7d8k|work=YouTube|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref>

==Chart performance==
The song entered the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] as the Hot Shot Debut at number 19 with sales of 176,000 in its first week.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/498734/fun-notch-fourth-week-atop-hot-100 | title=Fun. Notch Fourth Week Atop Hot 100 | publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=March 28, 2012 | accessdate=March 29, 2012 | author=Trust, Gary}}</ref> "Eyes Open" debuted on  ''Billboard'' [[Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)|Pop Songs chart]] at number 28, making it Swift's highest Pop Songs chart debut,<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/chartbeat/498204/justin-bieber-taylor-swift-jennifer-lopez-storm-pop-songs| title=Justin Bieber, Taylor Swift, Jennifer Lopez Storm Pop Songs| work=Billboard.com| date=April 2, 2012}}</ref> later overtaken by "[[We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together]]" which debuted at number 18. The song would later reach number 20 on the said chart on the week ending April 28, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=taylor swift|chart=Pop Songs B}}|title=''Billboard Hot 100 for Taylor Swift''|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|date=May 18, 2012|accessdate=2012-05-18}}</ref>
The song also debuted on ''Billboard'' [[Adult Pop Songs]] at number 24 and later peaked at number 11 on the week ending May 12, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=taylor swift|chart=Adult Pop Songs}}|title=''Billboard Adult Pop Songs for Taylor Swift''|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|date=May 18, 2012|accessdate=2012-05-18}}</ref>
The song became Swift's 16th million-seller and her second million-seller from the soundtrack to ''The Hunger Games''.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://music.yahoo.com/blogs/chart-watch/week-ending-july-15-2012-songs-blow-one-213010349.html;_ylt=A29D5pWNNydQdHMAnwAPwiUv;_ylu=X3oDMTFldWpmbzY4BG1pdANNdXNpYyBCbG9nIEluZGV4BHBvcwM1MARzZWMDTWVkaWFCbG9nSW5kZXg-;_ylg=X3oDMTFpMm9iMzh1BGludGwDdXMEbGFuZwNlbi11cwRwc3RhaWQDBHBzdGNhdANibG9nBHB0A3NlY3Rpb25z;_ylv=3| title=Week Ending July 15, 2012. Songs: Blow Me (One More Hit)| publisher=yahoo.com}}</ref>  The song has sold 1.4 million copies in the US as of November 2017.<ref name=sales>{{cite web|url = https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8046939/taylor-swift-career-album-song-sales-ask-billboard| title=Ask Billboard: Taylor Swift's Career Album & Song Sales |date=November 26, 2017 |accessdate = November 26, 2017 |first=Gary |last=Trust}}</ref>

Outside the U.S., "Eyes Open" achieved moderate success. In Canada, the song entered the [[Canadian Hot 100]] and peaked at number 17 on the week ending April 7, 2012.<ref>{{cite web | url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=taylor swift|chart=Billboard Canadian Hot 100}}| title=Taylor Swift Chart History| publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=March 28, 2012 | accessdate=March 29, 2012}}</ref> In Australia, the song peaked at [[ARIA Charts|Australian Singles Chart]] at number 47 on the week ending March 26, 2012.<ref name="australian-charts1">{{cite web | url=http://australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Taylor+Swift | title=Discography Taylor Swift | publisher=Hung Medien | work=australian-charts.com | accessdate=April 2, 2012}}</ref> On New Zealand, the song peaked at number six, became Swift's third top ten hit there, following "[[Love Story (Taylor Swift song)|Love Story]]"  and "[[You Belong with Me]]".<ref name="charts2">{{cite web | url=https://charts.nz/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Taylor+Swift | title=Discography Taylor Swift | publisher=Hung Medien | work=charts.nz | accessdate=April 2, 2012}}</ref> "Eyes Open" also charted in Ireland and UK, where it peaked at number 65 and number 70 respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://acharts.us/song/69583|title=Taylor Swift - Eyes Open - Music Charts|publisher=aCharts.us|accessdate=May 20, 2012}}</ref>

"Eyes Open" was the highest charting single from all of ''The Hunger Games'' movies, until surpassed by "[[The Hanging Tree (The Hunger Games song)|The Hanging Tree]]" by [[James Newton Howard]] featuring vocals from American actress [[Jennifer Lawrence]], which peaked at number 12 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]].
