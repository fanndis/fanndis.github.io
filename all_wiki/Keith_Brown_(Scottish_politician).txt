'''Keith James Brown''' (born 20 December 1961) is a Scottish politician who is currently Depute Leader of the [[Scottish National Party]]. He is a former [[Royal Marines]] [[commando]] and served as [[Cabinet Secretary for Economy, Jobs and Fair Work]] from May 2016 to June 2018. Brown has been a [[Member of the Scottish Parliament]] (MSP) since 2007, first representing the [[Ochil (Scottish Parliament constituency)|Ochil]] constituency 2007–2011, then the [[Clackmannanshire and Dunblane (Scottish Parliament constituency)|Clackmannanshire and Dunblane]] constituency since 2011.

==Early career==
Born in Edinburgh, Brown attended [[Tynecastle High School]] in the city, before joining the [[Royal Marines]] and serving in the [[Falklands War]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-11987571|title=New minister Keith Brown has bridge toll conviction|publisher=BBC|work=[[BBC News]]|date=13 December 2010|accessdate=13 December 2010}}</ref> Upon leaving the Marines he attended the [[University of Dundee]] where he joined the SNP, graduating in [[Political Science|Politics]] in 1988.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web |url=http://www.snp.org/people/parliamentarians/keith-brown-msp/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2008-01-18 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080116123956/http://www.snp.org/people/parliamentarians/keith-brown-msp/ |archivedate=16 January 2008 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> During his time at the University of Dundee, he studied abroad at the [[University of Prince Edward Island]] between 1985–1986.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.parliament.scot/msps/currentmsps/98302.aspx|title=Personal Information|date=2016-06-05|website=www.parliament.scot|access-date=2017-04-26}}</ref> He then went to work in local government administration in Stirling and was also an active trade union representative with [[UNISON]].<ref name=":0" />

He was the SNP candidate for the [[European Parliament]] for [[Lothians (European Parliament constituency)|Lothians constituency]] in [[1994 European Parliament election in the United Kingdom|1994]], coming second with 53,324 votes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alba.org.uk/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716180807/http://www.alba.org.uk/eecelect/lot.html|url-status=dead|title=Alba Money – Financial news and budgeting advice.|archivedate=16 July 2011}}</ref> Brown was then elected to represent [[Alva, Clackmannanshire|Alva]] on [[Clackmannanshire| Clackmannanshire Council]] in a [[by-election]] in 1996.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alba.org.uk/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716180815/http://www.alba.org.uk/localby/alva.html|url-status=dead|title=Alba Money – Financial news and budgeting advice.|archivedate=16 July 2011}}</ref> He became group leader in 1997, and then Leader of the Council after the SNP took control of Clackmannanshire in [[Local government in Scotland#Election results.2C 2003|the 2003 elections]].<ref name=autogenerated1 />

==Holyrood==
Brown was elected to the Scottish Parliament by Ochil constituency at the [[2007 Scottish Parliament election|2007 elections]], increasing both absolute SNP vote and majority.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/vote2007/scottish_parliment/html/442.stm | work=BBC News | title=BBC NEWS, Scottish elections 2007, Ochil}}</ref>

He was appointed to be Convener of both the Parliament's Standards & Public Appointments Committee and Procedures Committee, and after overseeing their merger now convenes the new Standards, Procedures & Public Appointments Committee.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/s3/committees/stanproc/index.htm|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080101154722/http://www.scottish.parliament.uk/s3/committees/stanproc/index.htm|url-status=dead|title=The Scottish Parliament: – Committees – Standards, Procedures and Public Appointments<!-- Bot generated title -->|archivedate=1 January 2008}}</ref>

In his maiden speech on the [[Forth Road Bridge#Abolition|abolition of bridge tolls]] he declared that he still had an outstanding fine from the [[Skye bridge#Toll controversy|Skye Bridge protests]] of 1994.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thecourier.co.uk/output/2007/06/01/newsstory9795052t0.asp|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081202155241/http://www.thecourier.co.uk/output/2007/06/01/newsstory9795052t0.asp|url-status=dead|title=The Courier: Taking you to the heart of Tayside and Fife<!-- Bot generated title -->|archivedate=2 December 2008}}</ref> He has been an opponent of the proposals for an overhead electricity line from [[Beauly]] to [[Denny, Falkirk|Denny]], arguing for an underground alternative.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://news.scotsman.com/ViewArticle.aspx?articleid=3585950 | location=Edinburgh | work=The Scotsman | title=Scotsman.com News}}</ref> Brown has also been campaigning for [[Scotland football team|Scotland football matches]] to be available on [[terrestrial television]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/scottish-news/show-scotland-games-on-main-tv-966067|title=Show Scotland Games On Main TV Says MSP|date=9 January 2008|website=dailyrecord}}</ref>

On 12 February 2009, Brown was appointed Minister for Schools and Skills. On 8 December 2009, Brown became Minister for Schools and Skills In the first reshuffle of the SNP Government.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/6665051.stm |title=Cabinet and ministers at-a-glance |work=BBC News |date= 8 December 2009}}</ref>  In December 2010, he was appointed as [[Minister for Transport and Infrastructure]] in the Scottish government.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-11978544 |title=Keith Brown named new Scottish transport minister |work=BBC News |date=12 December 2010}}</ref>

Brown won re-election to the Scottish Parliament in [[2011 Scottish Parliament election|2011]], representing the newly created [[Clackmannanshire and Dunblane (Scottish Parliament constituency)|Clackmannanshire and Dunblane]]. On 19 May 2011, Brown was named as the Minister for Housing and Transport. On 5 September 2012, he became Minister for Transport and Veterans.

===SNP Depute leadership bid, 2014===
{{further|Scottish National Party leadership election, 2014}}
Following defeat in the [[2014 Scottish independence referendum]], [[Scottish National Party]] leader and [[First Minister of Scotland]] [[Alex Salmond]] announced his resignation as SNP leader and First Minister of Scotland.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/sep/19/alex-salmond-resigns-first-minister-for-scotland|title=Alex Salmond resigns as first minister after Scotland rejects independence|first1=Severin|last1=Carrell|first2=Patrick|last2=Wintour|first3=Rowena|last3=Mason|date=19 September 2014|via=www.theguardian.com}}</ref> In the aftermath of his resignation, a leadership bid was launched, with [[Deputy First Minister of Scotland]] and SNP Depute leader [[Nicola Sturgeon]] widely tipped to become Salmond's successor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/scottish-independence/scottish-referendum-nicola-sturgeon-edges-closer-to-snp-leadership-9746536.html|title=Scottish referendum: Nicola Sturgeon edges closer to SNP leadership|date=21 September 2014|website=The Independent}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-29297949|title=Scottish ministers 'back Sturgeon'|date=21 September 2014|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

On 25 September 2014, Brown officially launched his bid to become the Depute leader of the Scottish National Party, with the backing of several SNP MSPs, as well as several Scottish Government ministers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/scottish-news/transport-minister-keith-brown-dundee-4324139|title=Transport Minister Keith Brown and Dundee MP Stewart Hosie launch rival bids for SNP Deputy Leadership|date=25 September 2014|website=dailyrecord}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://stv.tv/news/politics/293854-snp-minister-yousaf-supports-keith-brown-deputy-leadership-bid/ |title=SNP minister Yousaf supports Keith Brown deputy leadership bid |work=[[STV News]] |date=29 September 2014 |accessdate=28 August 2016}}</ref> The results of the election were announced at the SNP Autumn Conference on the 14 November, with Brown losing the contest to [[Stewart Hosie]], with 45.5% of the vote in the second round to Hosie's 55.5%.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-30052053 |title=Stewart Hosie elected deputy leader of the SNP |work=BBC News |date=14 November 2014}}</ref>

===Depute leader of the Scottish National Party===
On 8 June 2018, Keith Brown became the depute leader of the Scottish National Party, having contested and won the [[2018 Scottish National Party depute leadership election]] with 55% of votes in the second round.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-44397652 SNP conference: Keith Brown elected deputy leader]. BBC NEWS. Published 8 June 2018. Retrieved 8 June 2018.</ref> At his acceptance speech at the SNP conference in Aberdeen, Brown told fellow SNP members to prepare for a [[Proposed second Scottish independence referendum|second Scottish independence referendum]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.scotsman.com/news/indyref2-ahead-vows-keith-brown-after-elected-snp-depute-leader-1-4751545|title=Indyref2 ahead vows Keith Brown after elected SNP Depute Leader|access-date=2018-06-09|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://inews.co.uk/news/scotland/indyref2-undoubtedly-happen-snp-depute-keith-brown/|title=IndyRef2 will ‘undoubtedly’ happen, says new SNP depute leader Keith Brown|date=2018-06-08|work=iNews|access-date=2018-06-09|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.eveningtimes.co.uk/news/16278701._Get_ready_for_a_second_vote___New_SNP_depute_s_message_to_Scottish_independence_supporters/|title='Get ready for a second vote': New SNP depute's message to Scottish independence supporters|website=Evening Times|language=en|access-date=2018-06-09}}</ref> Brown also announced that he would chair three national assemblies to debate the SNP's 2018 Growth Commission report on an independent Scotland's economic prospects.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.commonspace.scot/articles/12861/new-snp-depute-leader-keith-brown-promises-national-debate-growth-commission-snp18|title=New SNP depute leader Keith Brown promises a national debate on the Growth Commission at #SNP18|work=CommonSpace|access-date=2018-06-09|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.eveningtimes.co.uk/news/16278373.SNP_elects_Keith_Brwon_as_new_Depute_Leader/|title=SNP elects Keith Brown as new Depute Leader|website=Evening Times|language=en|access-date=2018-06-09}}</ref>

Although Brown was elected Depute Leader of the SNP, [[John Swinney]] remained as Sturgeon's Deputy First Minister despite Brown being a member of the [[Scottish Parliament]]. In the June 2018 reshuffle of the [[Scottish Government]], Brown stood down as Economy Secretary to focus on his role as depute leader, He was replaced by [[Derek Mackay]]. He was subsequently given a role as the SNP's [[Campaign Manager]].
