'''Sir Philip John May''' (born 1957) is a British investment manager and [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] supporter who worked for [[Capital Group]] as an [[Investment management|investment relationship manager]]. He is married to [[Theresa May]], former [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]].

==Early life==
May was born in [[Norwich]] in 1957.<ref name="auto"/> His father was a sales representative for a shoe wholesaler, while his mother taught [[French language|French]].<ref name=BBC19>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/36774270|title=Profile: Theresa May's husband: Philip|date=13 July 2016|work=[[BBC News]]|accessdate=10 April 2019}}</ref> He grew up on [[Wirral Peninsula|the Wirral]], attending school in [[Heswall]] and then [[Calday Grange Grammar School]] in [[West Kirby]].<ref name=NS19>{{cite news|url=https://www.newstatesman.com/politics/uk/2019/01/philip-may-prime-minister-s-closest-political-adviser|title=Philip May: the Prime Minister's closest political adviser|date=23 January 2019|work=[[New Statesman]]|accessdate=10 April 2019}}</ref><ref name="ITV News">{{cite web|url=http://www.itv.com/news/2016-07-12/philip-may-the-banker-husband-and-theresas-real-rock/|title=Philip May: The banker husband and Theresa's 'real rock'|website=ITV News|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160713160009/http://www.itv.com/news/2016-07-12/philip-may-the-banker-husband-and-theresas-real-rock/|archivedate=13 July 2016|url-status = live|accessdate=13 July 2016|df=}}</ref> May studied history at [[Lincoln College, Oxford|Lincoln College]], [[University of Oxford|Oxford]],<ref name="INewsJul16">{{cite web |url=https://inews.co.uk/essentials/news/politics/theresa-mays-husband/ |title=Who is Theresa May's husband? |last=Wyatt |first=Daisy |date=3 July 2016 |work=I (newspaper) |accessdate=8 July 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160706221619/https://inews.co.uk/essentials/news/politics/theresa-mays-husband/ |archivedate=6 July 2016 |df= }}</ref> and was elected [[List of Presidents of the Oxford Union|President]] of the [[Oxford Union]] in 1979. He succeeded future Conservative MP [[Sir Alan Duncan]] in the role before handing over the baton to journalist [[Michael Crick]].<ref name="hibbert">{{cite book |title=[[The Encyclopaedia of Oxford]] |publisher=[[Macmillan Publishers|Macmillan]] |chapter=Presidents of the Union since 1900 |year=1988 |pages=527–532 |isbn=0-333-39917-X |editor-last=Hibbert|editor-first=Christopher |editor-link=Christopher Hibbert}}</ref>

==Financial career==
May has worked in [[finance]] since graduating from [[Oxford University]]. In 2005, he joined the financial group [[Capital Group Companies|Capital Group]] as a relationship manager; he had previously been a fund manager for [[Barclays Investment Bank|de Zoete & Bevan]], Prudential Portfolio Managers and [[Deutsche Asset Management]].<ref name="INewsJul16" /><ref name="Guardian Goodley">{{cite web |last1=Goodley |first1=Simon |title=Philip May: the reserved City fixture and husband happy to take a back seat |url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2016/jul/12/philip-may-theresa-may-quiet-husband-city-experience |website=The Guardian |accessdate=13 July 2016 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160712212610/https://www.theguardian.com/business/2016/jul/12/philip-may-theresa-may-quiet-husband-city-experience |archivedate=12 July 2016 |df= }}</ref> His former [[LinkedIn]] profile listed his focuses in work as pension fund and insurance relationship management.<ref name="Levine">{{cite web |last1=Levine |first1=Daniel |title=Philip John May, Theresa May's Husband: 5 Fast Facts You Need to Know |url=http://heavy.com/news/2016/06/philip-john-may-theresa-may-husband-photos-pictures-united-kingdom-brexit-family-g4s/ |website=Heavy |accessdate=12 July 2016 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160713132750/http://heavy.com/news/2016/06/philip-john-may-theresa-may-husband-photos-pictures-united-kingdom-brexit-family-g4s/ |archivedate=13 July 2016 |df= }}</ref><ref name="Makortoff">{{cite web |last1=Makortoff |title=Who is Britain's new 'first husband'? |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2016/07/12/who-is-britains-new-first-husband.html |publisher=CNBC |accessdate=13 July 2016 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160712122842/http://www.cnbc.com/2016/07/12/who-is-britains-new-first-husband.html |archivedate=12 July 2016 |df= }}</ref>

In regards to his persona, one of his colleagues told ''[[The Guardian]]'' that "around the office, he is a fairly head-down type of guy. There is a stereotypical investment manager with a big ego – he's not like that at all."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theweek.co.uk/74495/philip-may-five-things-you-didnt-know|title=Philip May: five facts about the prime minister’s husband|website=The Week UK|language=en|access-date=2019-07-27}}</ref>

After his wife [[Theresa May]]<ref name="Theresa May – what lies beyond the public image?">{{cite web |last1=Day |first1=Elizabeth |title=Theresa May – what lies beyond the public image? |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/jul/27/theresa-may-profile-beyond-the-public-image |website=[[The Guardian]] |accessdate=10 July 2016 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160715192717/http://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/jul/27/theresa-may-profile-beyond-the-public-image |archivedate=15 July 2016 |df= }}</ref> emerged as the remaining candidate for the [[2016 Conservative Party (UK) leadership election|Conservative Party leadership]], May's employer issued a statement saying his current job does not make him responsible for investment decisions: "[May] is not involved with, and doesn't manage, money and is not a portfolio manager. His job is to ensure the clients are happy with the service and that we understand their goals."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-38577336 |title=Theresa May used 'well-established' blind trust mechanism |date=11 January 2017 |publisher=BBC |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170528014306/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-38577336 |archivedate=28 May 2017 |df= }}</ref>

May briefly served as chairman of the [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] [[Wimbledon (UK Parliament constituency)|Wimbledon]] [[constituency]] association before reportedly deciding to concentrate on a career in finance.<ref name="ITV News" /> Nonetheless he remained consistently active and enthusiastic as a Tory supporter, and often described as an "experienced Conservative activist".<ref name="ft.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/6fa69b80-12f5-11e7-b0c1-37e417ee6c76|title=Philip May: Tory activist, City insider . . . and Europhile?|website=Financial Times|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180101030228/https://www.ft.com/content/6fa69b80-12f5-11e7-b0c1-37e417ee6c76|archivedate=1 January 2018|url-status = live|df=}}</ref> He was named in the [[Panama Papers]] in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.businessinsider.com/theresa-may-philip-may-offshore-panama-papers-blind-trust-tax-return-mcdonnell-2017-12?r=US&IR=T|title=May told to prove she has 'nothing to hide' after 10,000 sign petition to declare offshore interests|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180101135357/http://uk.businessinsider.com/theresa-may-philip-may-offshore-panama-papers-blind-trust-tax-return-mcdonnell-2017-12?r=US&IR=T|archivedate=1 January 2018|url-status = live|df=}}</ref>

May did not attend meetings to advise the Prime Minister in any official capacity but was referred to by some as the Prime Minister's 'most trusted adviser',<ref name="auto" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/07/13/meet-the-close-knit-team-of-trusted-advisors-who-will-guide-ther/|title=Meet the close-knit team of trusted advisors who will guide Theresa May as PM|last=Blackburn|first=Virginia|date=2016-07-13|work=The Telegraph|access-date=2018-02-05|language=en-GB|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> following her consultation with him over calling the snap [[2017 United Kingdom general election|general election in 2017]] and her 2016 [[Conservative Party Conference (UK)|Conservative Party Conference]] speech.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.politico.eu/article/philip-may-g20-britain-first-turn-on-the-world-stage/|title=Mr. May makes global debut|date=2017-07-06|work=POLITICO|access-date=2018-02-05|language=en-US}}</ref> He helped to canvass voters ahead of the [[2017 Copeland by-election]] and supports his wife in her [[Maidenhead (UK Parliament constituency)|Maidenhead]] constituency affairs.<ref name="GuardMay17">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2017/may/09/philip-may-the-prime-ministers-husband-steps-out-of-the-shadows|title=Philip May: the prime minister's husband steps out of the shadows|author=Esther Addley|date=9 May 2017|work=The Guardian|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170509235015/https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2017/may/09/philip-may-the-prime-ministers-husband-steps-out-of-the-shadows|archivedate=9 May 2017|url-status = live|accessdate=10 May 2017|df=}}</ref>

May made his first official visit as the [[spouse of the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|British PM's consort]] to the [[G20 2017|G20]] [[summit]] in July 2017 at [[Hamburg|Hamburg, Germany]]. During the visit, he attended [[Gala (festivity)|gala concert]]s and took boat trips with the spouses of other world leaders.<ref name=":0" />

On 27 January 2019, ''[[The Sunday Times]]'' reported Theresa May's chief of staff [[Gavin Barwell|Lord Barwell]] broke ranks to accuse Philip May of "scuppering" plans to offer the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] a permanent customs union with the [[European Union|EU]] to try and push the [[Brexit withdrawal agreement|withdrawal agreement]] through [[British Parliament|parliament]]. May is said to have encouraged his wife to seek changes to the deal by removing the [[Irish backstop]], in order for it to be approved by parliament with the support of the [[Democratic Unionist Party|DUP]] and [[Brexiteer]] [[Member of parliament|MPs]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/politics/brexit-deal-latest-irish-foreign-minister-deals-another-blow-to-theresa-may-by-insisting-backstop-a4049816.html|title=MPs' next recess could be cancelled as PM dealt another backstop blow|date=27 January 2019|website=Evening Standard}}</ref>

In July 2020, he was [[Knight Bachelor|knighted]] for political service in [[Boris Johnson]]'s belated [[2019 Dissolution Honours|(2019) Dissolution Honours]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=31 July 2020|title=Dissolution Peerages and Honours 2019|url=https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/905975/Dissolution_Peerages_2019.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=31 July 2020|website=Gov.uk}}</ref>

==Personal life==
May and his future wife, then Theresa Brasier, met as [[undergraduate]]s at [[Oxford University|Oxford]], being introduced by [[Benazir Bhutto]] at a [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] student event.<ref>{{cite web |last=Mendick |first=Robert |title=The Oxford romance that has guided Theresa May from tragedy to triumph |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/07/09/the-oxford-romance-that-has-guided-theresa-may-from-tragedy-to-t/ |work=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |date=9 July 2016 |accessdate=13 July 2016 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160711080709/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/07/09/the-oxford-romance-that-has-guided-theresa-may-from-tragedy-to-t/ |archivedate=11 July 2016 |df= }}</ref> They further bonded over a shared love of [[cricket]], and were married on 6 September 1980 by Theresa's father, [[Reverend|the Rev]] [[Hubert Brasier]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2009/dec/14/theresa-may-lady-in-waiting |title=Theresa May: David Cameron's lady in waiting |last=Orr |first=Deborah |date=14 December 2009 |work=The Guardian |accessdate=9 June 2011 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131107030719/http://www.theguardian.com/politics/2009/dec/14/theresa-may-lady-in-waiting |archivedate=7 November 2013 |df= }}</ref><ref name="Teleg">{{cite web |title=I was probably goody two-shoes: Theresa May interviewed |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/politics/9762170/Theresa-May-interview-I-probably-was-Goody-Two-Shoes-at-school.html |work=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |accessdate=3 July 2016 |url-status = live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160703135423/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/politics/9762170/Theresa-May-interview-I-probably-was-Goody-Two-Shoes-at-school.html |archivedate=3 July 2016 |df= }}</ref>

As the [[spouse of the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]], May avoided giving interviews or making public statements but did accompany his wife for a joint interview on the [[BBC One]] programme ''[[The One Show]]'' prior to the [[2017 United Kingdom general election|2017 general election]].<ref name="GuardMay17" /> During this interview, Theresa May admitted her sadness, for health reasons, she and Philip have not been able to have children, saying: "You look at families all the time and you see there is something there that you don't have".<ref name="Teleg" /><ref name="Philip May (LinkedIn page)">{{cite web |title=Philip May (LinkedIn page) |url=https://www.linkedin.com/in/philip-may-4b344133 |publisher=[[LinkedIn]] |accessdate=3 July 2016}}{{dead link|date=January 2018}}</ref> Also in the interview, May said: "I get to decide when I take the bins out. Not if I take them out", and commented, "I do the traditional boy jobs by and large."<ref name="auto">{{Cite news|last=Becket|first=Adam|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/who-is-philip-may-theresa-may-husband-closest-advisor-capital-group-paradise-papers-2017-11|title=Who is Philip May? Theresa May's husband and closest advisor|work=Business Insider|date=2017-11-21|access-date=2018-02-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/election-2017-39865991|title=The Mays on love, shoes, and who takes the bins out|last=Hulme|first=Susan|date=2017-05-09|work=BBC News|access-date=2018-02-05|language=en-GB}}</ref> Asked about the downside to being married to the Prime Minister, May insisted it was a privilege, saying: "If you're the kind of man who expects his tea to be on the table at six o'clock every evening, you could be a disappointed man."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/05/09/theresa-philip-may-one-show-sweet-screamingly-dull/|title=Theresa and Philip May on The One Show: sweet, but screamingly dull|last=Deacon|first=Michael|date=2017-05-09|work=The Telegraph|access-date=2018-02-05|language=en-GB|issn=0307-1235}}</ref>
