'''East China''' ({{zh |s=华东 |t=華東 |p=Huádōng |l = China's East }}) is a geographical and a loosely defined cultural [[region]] that covers the eastern coastal area of [[China]].

An abolished concept, for economical purposes the region was defined from 1961 to 1978 by the [[State Council of the People's Republic of China|Chinese Central Government]] to include the [[Provinces of China|provinces]] of (in alphabetical order) [[Anhui]], [[Fujian]], [[Jiangsu]], [[Jiangxi]], [[Shandong]] and [[Zhejiang]], as well as the [[municipality of China|municipality]] of [[Shanghai]]. Since the Chinese government [[political status of Taiwan|claims]] [[Taiwan]] and the few outlying islands of Fujian ([[Kinmen]] and [[Matsu Islands|Matsu]]) governed by the [[Government of the Republic of China|Republic of China]] (Taiwanese government) as [[Free area of the Republic of China|its territory]], the claimed "[[Taiwan Province, People's Republic of China]]" was once classified in this region.
