'''The Republicans''' ({{lang-fr|Les Républicains}}; '''LR''') is a [[Liberal conservatism|liberal-conservative]] and  [[Gaullism|Gaullist]] [[list of political parties in France|political party in France]].

The party was formed on 30 May 2015 by renaming the [[Union for a Popular Movement]] (UMP) party, which had been founded in 2002 under the leadership of former [[President of France]] [[Jacques Chirac]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/europe/boost-for-sarkozy-as-ump-party-changes-its-name-to-the-republicans-10286171.html|title = Nicolas Sarkozy changes UMP party's name to The Republicans ahead of political comeback|date = 30 May 2015|accessdate = 2015-05-30|website = [[The Independent]]|last = Bolton|first = Doug}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2015/05/29/us-france-sarkozy-idUSKBN0OE2J120150529 |title=France's UMP party changes name to The Republicans, boosting Sarkozy |agency=Reuters Editorial |date=2015-05-29 |accessdate=2015-12-06}}</ref> The party used to be one of the two [[Major party|major]] political parties in the [[France|French Fifth Republic]] along with the [[Centre-left politics|centre-left]] [[Socialist Party (France)|Socialist Party]] (PS), and, following the [[2017 French legislative election|2017 legislative election]], it remains the second largest party in the [[National Assembly (France)|National Assembly]] (behind [[President of France|President]] [[Emmanuel Macron|Macron]]'s [[La République En Marche!|LREM]]). LR is a member of the [[European People's Party]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.epp.eu/member-parties |title=EPP - European People's Party - Member Parties |location=50.8398374;4.3671204 |publisher=European People's Party |accessdate=2015-12-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160504032123/http://www.epp.eu/member-parties/ |archive-date=2016-05-04 }}</ref> the [[Centrist Democrat International]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://idc-cdi.com/en/parties/ |title=Partidos Archivo |publisher=idc-cdi |date=2015-11-26 |accessdate=2015-12-06}}</ref> and the [[International Democrat Union]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://idu.org/member-parties/ |title=International Democrat Union » Member Parties |publisher=Idu.org |date=2015-10-27 |accessdate=2015-12-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170224043633/http://idu.org/member-parties/ |archive-date=2017-02-24 }}</ref>

==History==

=== UMP name change ===
The UMP's ([[Union for a Popular Movement]]) change of party name and of party structure was one of the promises made by Nicolas Sarkozy during his campaign for the UMP Presidency in 2014.<ref name="Richebois">{{Cite web|title=Comment l'UMP a changé de nom|url=https://www.lesechos.fr/2015/06/comment-lump-a-change-de-nom-248774|last=Richebois|first=Véronique|date=2015-06-01|website=Les Echos|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> After his election in November 2014, Nicolas Sarkozy, the President of France from 2007 to 2012, put forward the request to the party's general committee to change its name to Les Républicains ("The Republicans") and alter the statutes of the party. The proposed statutes provided for, among other provisions, the election of the presidents of the departmental federations by direct democracy and consulting members on election nominations.<ref>{{cite journal|first=Anne-Laëtitia|last= Béraud |title=L'UMP se dote des statuts du nouveau parti baptisé «Les Républicains |journal=[[20 minutes (France)|20 Minutes]] |date=14 April 2015 |url=http://www.20minutes.fr/politique/1586487-20150414-ump-dote-statuts-future-formation-republicains |accessdate=2015-09-18 }}</ref> Nicolas Sarkozy wanted to change the name of the party in order to showcase the reunification of the different political views, from the social Gaullism of Henri Guaino to the right line of Patrick Buisson, into "one family".<ref name="Clavaud-Mégevand">{{Cite web|title=Les Républicains: Pourquoi le changement de nom coince à l'UMP?|url=https://www.20minutes.fr/politique/1602079-20150505-changement-nom-ump-pourquoi-ca-coince|last=Clavaud-Mégevand|first=Coline|website=www.20minutes.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref><ref name="Richebois"/><ref>{{Cite web|title=NKM : pourquoi l'UMP doit s'appeler "Les Républicains"|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/politique/2015/04/28/01002-20150428ARTFIG00288-nkm-pourquoi-l-ump-doit-s-appeler-les-republicains.php|last=Garat|first=Jean-Baptiste|date=2015-04-28|website=Le Figaro.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> As declared in an interview for the Journal Du Dimanche, Sarkozy also wished to change the name in order to be ahead of his adversaries Alain Juppé et François Fillon (also belonging to the [[Union for a Popular Movement|UMP)]] for the [[2017 French presidential election|2017 presidential elections]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sarkozy au JDD : "Si je réussis, ils ne pourront plus me rattraper"|url=https://www.lejdd.fr/Politique/Sarkozy-au-JDD-Si-je-reussis-ils-ne-pourront-plus-me-rattraper-688506|last=Jeudy|first=Bruno|website=lejdd.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> This desire for changing the name was not received well by all members of the party. In an interview for BFMTV, Alain Juppé mocked the ex-French President for wanting to change the name of the UMP.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Juppé ironise sur le changement nom de l'UMP voulu par Sarkozy|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/politique/le-scan/citations/2014/09/23/25002-20140923ARTFIG00409-juppe-ironise-sur-le-changement-nom-de-l-ump-voulu-par-sarkozy.php|last=Le Scan Politique|date=2014-09-23|website=Le Figaro.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> Additionally, Gilles Boyer, supporter of Francois Fillon, showed his reluctance regarding the change of name by tweeting, "We are republicans. We are not THE republicans."<ref name="Clavaud-Mégevand"/> This change of name was perceived by some journalists as an attempt to make the public forget the judicial problems linked to the UMP, especially the Bygmalion case, case in which some members of the UMP are suspected to have forged documents over the expenses of Nicolas Sarkozy's 2012 presidential campaign.<ref>{{Cite web|title=VIDEO. UMP : "Pourquoi Nicolas Sarkozy change-t-il le nom de son parti ?"|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/societe/video-ump-pourquoi-nicolas-sarkozy-change-t-il-le-nom-de-son-parti_908353.html|date=2015-05-17|website=Franceinfo|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Copé à Sarkozy : "Est-ce bien utile de changer le nom de l'UMP après une telle victoire ?"|url=https://www.nouvelobs.com/politique/20150331.OBS6040/cope-a-sarkozy-est-ce-bien-utile-de-changer-le-nom-de-l-ump-apres-une-telle-victoire.html|website=L'Obs|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Les quatre principales affaires qui menacent toujours Nicolas Sarkozy|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/politique/affaire/financement-de-la-chtmlagne-de-sarkozy/les-quatre-principales-affaires-qui-menacent-toujours-nicolas-sarkozy_3447693.html|date=2019-05-17|website=Franceinfo|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref>

Critics of the name change claimed it was unfair for Sarkozy to name the party "Republicans", because every French person is a republican if they support the values and ideals of the French Republic that emanated from the [[French Revolution]], and as such the term is above party politics.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.theguardian.com/world/2015/may/26/france-sarkozy-les-republicains-ump-socialists|title = France: judges clear way for Sarkozy to rename UMP party Les Républicains |date = 26 May last name, sued the UMP.<ref name="Le Monde.fr">{{Cite news|date=2015-05-26|title=La justice autorise l'UMP à se rebaptiser " Les Républicains "|language=fr|work=Le Monde.fr|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/politique/article/2015/05/26/la-justice-autorise-l-ump-a-se-rebaptiser-les-republicains_4640293_823448.html|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite was in favour of the UMP's change in name, stating that the "manifestly unlawful disturbance" and the "imminent damage" alleged by the complainants have not been demonstrated.<ref name="Le Monde.fr"/> The new name was adopted by the party bureau on 5 May 2015 and approved by the party membership on 28 May by an online "yes" vote of 83.3% on a 45.7% turnout after a court ruling in favour of Sarkozy.<ref name="LP3052015">Pauline Théveniaud (avec Olivier Beaumont), [http://m.leparisien.fr/politique/les-republicains-reunis-a-paris-pour-leur-congres-fondateur-30-05-2015-4817071.php Congrès === Founding congress ===
The change to the name "The Republicans" was confirmed at the party's founding congress on 30 May 2015 at the Paris Event Centre in [[Paris]], attended by 10,000 activists.<ref>Alexandre Lemarié et Matthieu Goar, [http://mobile.lemonde.fr/politique/article/2015/05/30/sarkozy-met-les-republicains-en-ordre-de-bataille-pour-2017_4644145_823448.html?xtref=https://www.google.fr/ Sarkozy met les Républicains en ordre de bataille pour 2017] ''[[Le Monde]]'', 30 mai 2015</ref> [[Angela Merkel]], chairwoman of the centre-right [[Christian Democratic Union of Germany|CDU]], sent a congratulatory message to the congress. The Republicans thus became the legal successor of the UMP and the leading centre-right party in France.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://news.yahoo.com/frances-sarkozy-renames-ump-party-republicans-211542144.html|title = France's Sarkozy renames UMP party 'The Republicans' |date = 29 May 2015|accessdate = 2015-06-01|website = [[Yahoo News]]|last = AFP}}</ref>

The organisation has been declared in the ''préfecture de [[Saône-et-Loire]]'' on 9 April 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.journal-officiel.gouv.fr/publications/assoc/pdf/2015/0017/JOAFE_PDF_Unitaire_20150017_01214.pdf |title=Official journal |date=2015 |website=www.journal-officiel.gouv.fr }}</ref> According to the statement of this declaration, its aim is to "promote ideas of the right and centre, open to every people who wish to be member and debate in the spirit of a political party with republican ideas in France or outside France".{{citation needed|date=June 2015}} This party foundation was published in the ''[[Journal officiel de la République française]]'' on 25 April 2015.<ref>No d'annonce : 1214 Paru le : 25/04/2015 Association : LES REPUBLICAINS. Identification R.N.A. : W715002794 No de parution : 20150017 Département (Région) : Saône-et-Loire (Bourgogne) Lieu parution : Déclaration à la préfecture de Saône-et-Loire. Type d'annonce : ASSOCIATION/CREATION</ref>

=== Since 2016 ===

On 3 July 2016, [[Nicolas Sarkozy]] announced that he would resign as leader that year in order to compete to be the right-wing candidate in the [[2017 French presidential election|2017 presidential election]].<ref name=resign>{{cite news|title=Nicolas Sarkozy confirms return bid for French presidency|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/jul/03/nicolas-sarkozy-confirms-return-bid-for-french-presidency|accessdate=4 July 2016|work=The Guardian|date=3 July 2016}}</ref>

In order to decide which candidate will represent The Republicans for the 2017 presidential elections, a party's primary was organised in November 2016.<ref name="lci.fr">{{Cite web|title=Primaire à droite : les résultats définitifs du premier tour enfin connus|url=https://www.lci.fr/primaire-droite/primaire-a-droite-les-resultats-definitifs-du-premier-tour-enfin-connus-2013991.html|website=LCI|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> The activists of the movement could choose between 7 candidates: François Fillion, Alain Juppé, Nicolas Sarkozy, Jean-François Copé, Nathalie Kosciusko-Morizet, Bruno Le Maire and Jean-Frédéric Poisson. François Fillon, with 44,1% of the votes, and Alain Juppé, with 28,6%, were the two candidates qualified for the second round of the election.<ref name="lci.fr"/> François Fillon won the second turn of the election with 66,5% of the votes and was therefore appointed as The Republicans' candidate for the presidential election in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|title=François Fillon remporte largement la primaire de la droite|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/elections/presidentielles/primaires-droite/2016/11/27/35004-20161127ARTFIG00185-fillon-largement-vainqueur-de-la-primaire-selon-les-premiers-resultats.php|last=Figaro|first=Le|date=2016-11-27|website=Le Figaro.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref>

[[François Fillon]] suffered a historic defeat in the first round of the presidential election, as he was the first right wing candidate in the history of the Fifth Republic who failed to continue to the second round.<ref>{{cite news|author=Olivier Beaumont|title=VIDEO. Une défaite historique pour François Fillon|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/elections/presidentielle/video-une-defaite-historique-pour-francois-fillon-24-04-2017-6882981.php|newspaper=Le Parisien|date=24 April 2017|accessdate=21 October 2017}}</ref> This led to the victory of Emmanuel Macron,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Emmanuel Macron président : les raisons d'une victoire|url=https://www.france24.com/fr/20170507-france-presidentielle-2017-emmanuel-macron-elu-president-raisons-succes-victoire-marche|date=2017-05-07|website=France 24|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> leader of his newly created party ''[[La République En Marche!]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Emmanuel Macron lance "En Marche !", son mouvement politique|url=https://www.lesechos.fr/2016/04/emmanuel-macron-lance-en-marche-son-mouvement-politique-206386|last=Poussielgue|first=Grégoire|date=2016-04-06|website=Les Echos|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> François Fillon finished third in the first round of the presidential election with 20,01% of the vote, behind Emmanuel Macron (24,01%) and Marine le Pen (21,30%).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Résultats France - Présidentielle 2017 - 1er et 2nd tour|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/data/france/presidentielle-2017/|website=Le Monde.fr|language=fr-FR|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> This defeat is mainly due to the [[Fillon affair|Penelopegate scandal]], as François Fillon was considered the favourite candidate by the polls before these revelations.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sondage présidentielle 2017 : Fillon devant Le Pen, Valls et Macron mieux placés que Hollande|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/elections/presidentielle/sondage-presidentielle-2017-fillon-devant-le-pen-valls-et-macron-mieux-places-que-hollande-29-11-2016-6392391.php|date=2016-11-29|website=leparisien.fr|language=fr-FR|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref>

The election victory of Emmanuel Macron in 2017 altered the French political landscape.<ref name="france24.com">{{Cite web|title=Un an après l'élection de Macron, le paysage politique français toujours en recomposition|url=https://www.france24.com/fr/20180507-france-an-election-emmanuel-macron-recomposition-paysage-politique-francais-lfi-ps-lr-fn|date=2018-05-07|website=France 24|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> After [[Emmanuel Macron]] was elected as president, he appointed three right-wing politicians in his government from The Republicans, namely [[Édouard Philippe]] as [[Prime Minister of France|Prime Minister]], [[Bruno Le Maire]] as [[Minister of Economy and Finance (France)|French Minister of the Economy and Finance]], and [[Gérald Darmanin]] as [[Ministry of Budget, Public Accounts and Civil Administration|Minister of Public Action and Accounts]].<ref name="france24.com"/> The fact that three ex-members from The Republicans are now part of the government, has divided the political party based on views of whether or not the republicans should support the incumbent government.<ref name="france24.com"/> Some members of The Republicans, such as Thierry Solère or Sébastien Lecornu, therefore decided to leave the party in order to join [[La République En Marche!]], the new political party created by Emmanuel Macron.<ref name="france24.com"/> Other members, like Franck Riester or Fabienne Keller, decided to create a new political party: "[[Agir (France)|Agir]]".<ref name="france24.com"/> Additionally, a parliamentary group including LR dissidents supportive of the government line, "[[The Constructives: Republicans, UDI, and Independents group|The Constructives]]", was formed in the [[National Assembly (France)|National Assembly]], separate from the [[The Republicans group (National Assembly)|existing group]].<ref>{{cite news|author=Paul Chaulet|title=L'avenir incertain des députés LR "constructifs" à l'Assemblée nationale|url=http://www.lexpress.fr/actualite/politique/lr/l-avenir-incertain-des-deputes-lr-constructifs-a-l-assemblee-nationale_1919506.html|newspaper=L'Express|date=20 June 2017|accessdate=21 October 2017}}</ref>

A month after the Presidential elections, the legislative elections took place in France. In the second round of the [[2017 French legislative election|legislative elections]] in June, The Republicans won 112 seats in parliament, which is 82 less than the number of seats won by the [[Union for a Popular Movement|UMP]] in 2012<ref>{{Cite web|title=Résultats élections législatives 2012 France entière|url=http://www.lexpress.fr/actualite/politique/elections/legislatives-2012/resultats-elections/france.html|website=LExpress.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Résultats élections législatives 2017 France entière|url=https://www.lexpress.fr/actualite/politique/elections/legislatives-2017/resultats-elections/france.html|website=LExpress.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> This result was the worst performance of a major right-wing political party in French history.<ref>{{cite news|author=Marion Mourgue|date=18 June 2017|title=Législatives : un revers historique pour Les Républicains|newspaper=Le Figaro|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/elections/legislatives/2017/06/18/38001-20170618ARTFIG00281-legislatives-un-revers-historique-pour-les-republicains.php|accessdate=21 October 2017}}</ref>

On 11 July, the political bureau of The Republicans agreed to hold a [[2017 The Republicans (France) leadership election|leadership election]] for president of the party on 10 and 17 December;<ref>{{cite news|title=Les Républicains éliront leur nouveau président en décembre 2017|url=http://www.rtl.fr/actu/politique/les-republicains-eliront-leur-nouveau-president-en-decembre-2017-7789300987|agency=Agence France-Presse|publisher=RTL|date=11 July 2017|accessdate=21 October 2017}}</ref> [[Laurent Wauquiez]] was elected in a single round on the 10th of December, winning 74,64 % of the votes.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2017-12-10|title=Laurent Wauquiez élu président du parti Les Républicains|language=fr|work=Le Monde.fr|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/politique/article/2017/12/10/laurent-wauquiez-prend-la-tete-du-parti-les-republicains_5227612_823448.html|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref> [[Laurent Wauquiez|Laurent Wauquiez's]] election for the head of the Party continued to divide The Republicans as 26 elected officials left the party between his election on the 10th of December and the 21st of February 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Depuis l'élection de Laurent Wauquiez, 26 élus ont quitté les Républicains|url=https://www.lejdd.fr/Politique/depuis-lelection-de-laurent-wauquiez-voici-le-nombre-delus-qui-ont-quitte-les-republicains-3580643|last=JDD|first=Le|website=lejdd.fr|language=fr|access-date=2020-05-15}}</ref>

On 2 June 2019, a week after overseeing the worst result for the right in its history in the [[2019 European Parliament election in France|European elections]] with 8.48% of the vote, Wauquiez announced his resignation as president of The Republicans.<ref>{{cite news|title=Laurent Wauquiez démissionne de la présidence des Républicains|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/politique/laurent-wauquiez-demissionne-de-la-presidence-des-republicains-20190602|newspaper=Le Figaro|date=2 June 2019|accessdate=2 June 2019}}</ref>
 
On 13 October 2019, [[Christian Jacob (politician)|Christian Jacob]], former Minister of the [[French Civil Service]], was [[2019 The Republicans (France) leadership election|elected]] as President of the party, taking from interim President [[Jean Leonetti]].<ref>{{cite web |title=France's troubled conservatives elect new leader |url=https://www.france24.com/en/20191013-france-s-troubled-conservatives-elect-new-leader |publisher=[[France 24]] |accessdate=7 February 2020 |date=13 October 2019}}</ref>

In the [[2020 French Senate election]], the Republicans held their majority.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chadwick|first=Lauren|date=2020-09-27|title=French Senate elections: Right and centre parties maintain majority|url=https://www.euronews.com/2020/09/27/french-senate-elections-expected-to-be-dominated-by-right-wing-republicans|access-date=2020-10-03|website=euronews|language=en}}</ref>
