The '''2000 French Open''' was a [[tennis]] tournament that took place on the outdoor [[Clay_court|clay courts]] at the [[Stade Roland Garros]] in [[Paris]], [[France]]. The tournament was held from 29 May until 11 June. It was the 104th staging of the [[French Open]], and the second [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tennis event of 2000.

==Seniors==
===Men's singles===
{{main|2000 French Open – Men's Singles}}
{{flagicon|Brazil}} [[Gustavo Kuerten]] defeated {{flagicon|Sweden}} [[Magnus Norman]], 6–2, 6–3, 2–6, 7–6<sup>(8–6)</sup>
:<small>• It was Kuerten's 2nd career Grand Slam singles title and his 2nd title at the French Open. It was Kuerten's 3rd title of the year, and his 8th overall.</small>

===Women's singles===
{{main|2000 French Open – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|France}} [[Mary Pierce]]<ref>Pierce was the first Frenchwoman to win the Women's Singles title since Françoise Durr won the title in 1967.</ref> defeated {{flagicon|Spain}} [[Conchita Martínez]], 6–2, 7–5
:<small>• It was Pierce's 2nd and last career Grand Slam singles title and her 1st title at the French Open. It was Pierce's 2nd title of the year, and her 15th overall.</small>

===Men's doubles===
{{main|2000 French Open – Men's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|AUS}} [[Todd Woodbridge]] / {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Mark Woodforde]]<ref>With the victory, [[The Woodies]] completed the career Grand Slam in Men's Doubles.</ref> defeated {{flagicon|NED}} [[Paul Haarhuis]] / {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Sandon Stolle]], 7–6<sup>(9–7)</sup>, 6–4 
:<small>• It was Woodbridge's 10th career Grand Slam doubles title and his 1st and only title at the French Open.</small>
:<small>• It was Woodforde's 11th career Grand Slam doubles title and his 1st and only title at the French Open.</small>

===Women's doubles===
{{main|2000 French Open – Women's Doubles}}
{{flagicon|SUI}} [[Martina Hingis]] / {{flagicon|FRA}} [[Mary Pierce]] defeated {{flagicon|ESP}} [[Virginia Ruano Pascual|Virginia Ruano]] / {{flagicon|ARG}} [[Paola Suárez]], 6–2, 6–4
:<small>• It was Hingis' 8th career Grand Slam doubles title and her 2nd and last title at the French Open.</small>
:<small>• It was Pierce's 1st and only career Grand Slam doubles title.</small>

===Mixed doubles===
{{main|2000 French Open – Mixed Doubles}}
{{flagicon|RSA}} [[Mariaan de Swardt]] / {{flagicon|RSA}} [[David Adams (tennis player)|David Adams]] defeated {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Rennae Stubbs]] / {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Todd Woodbridge]], 6–3, 3–6, 6–3
:<small>• It was de Swardt's 2nd and last career Grand Slam mixed doubles title and her 1st title at the French Open.</small>
:<small>• It was Adams's 2nd and last career Grand Slam mixed doubles title and his 1st title at the French Open.</small>

==Juniors==

{{flagicon|FRA}} [[Paul-Henri Mathieu]] defeated {{flagicon|Spain}} [[Tommy Robredo]], 3–6, 7–6<sup>(7–3)</sup>, 6–2

===Girls' singles===
{{flagicon|FRA}} [[Virginie Razzano]] defeated {{flagicon|ARG}} [[María Emilia Salerni]], 5–7, 6–4, 8–6

===Boys' doubles===
{{flagicon|Spain}} [[Marc López]] / {{flagicon|Spain}} [[Tommy Robredo]] defeated {{flagicon|SWE}} [[Joachim Johansson]] / {{flagicon|USA}} [[Andy Roddick]], 7–6<sup>(7–2)</sup>, 6–0

===Girls' doubles===
{{flagicon|Spain}} [[María José Martínez Sánchez|Maria José Martinez]] / {{flagicon|Spain}} [[Anabel Medina Garrigues]] defeated {{flagicon|CRO}} [[Matea Mezak]] / {{flagicon|RUS}} [[Dinara Safina]], 6–0, 6–1
