'''John Swannell''', born 27 December 1946, is a British [[photographer]].

==Career==
Swannell was born in London in 1946.<ref name=guardian>[https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2008/mar/27/bestshot.john.swannell]</ref> After leaving school at the age of 16, Swannell worked first as an assistant at ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' Studios and then assisted [[David Bailey (photographer)|David Bailey]] for four years before setting up his own studio.<ref>{{cite web|title=John Swannell: photographer|url=http://www.johnswannell.com/about.asp|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120208232143/http://www.johnswannell.com/about.asp|archivedate=2012-02-08}}</ref>

He spent the next ten years travelling and working for magazines such as ''Vogue'', ''[[Harpers & Queen]]'', ''[[The Sunday Times]]'' and ''[[Tatler (1901)|Tatler]]''. During this time he developed his very distinctive, individual style in both fashion and beauty photography.

Swannell provided the photograph for the cover of the 1973 album ''[[The Man in the Bowler Hat]]'' and the 1976 ''[[Mr. Mick]]'' album by British rock group [[Stackridge]], and has many other record cover photograph credits to his name.<ref>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/john-swannell-mn0002160803]</ref>

He is known for his [[British Royal Family|royal]] portrait photographs. Other portrait commissions have included the 2004 Christmas card for [[Tony Blair|Tony]] and [[Cherie Blair]]; [[Richard Attenborough]], [[Michael Caine]], [[Christopher Nolan]], Elkie Brooks, [[Bryan Ferry]], [[Norman Foster]], [[Bob Hoskins]], [[Glenda Jackson]], [[Tom Jones (singer)|Tom Jones]], [[Ken Livingstone]], [[Joanna Lumley]].<ref>{{cite web|title=John Swannell, Esq|url=http://www.debretts.com/people/biographies/browse/s/1935/John+SWANNELL.aspx|publisher=Debretts People of Today}}</ref>

In 2019, Clarendon Fine Art hosted a major retrospective of his work.<ref>{{cite web|title=40 Years On|url=https://www.metro.news/my-first-home-john-swannell/1829171/|publisher=Metro}}</ref>


===Royal photographer===
During his career Swannell has photographed all the leading members of the [[British Royal Family]], apart from [[Princess Margaret]].<ref>{{cite web|title=John Swannell interview 2008|url=http://www.f2photo.co.uk/profiles/profiles_f2_johnswannell.html|publisher=Freelance Photographer|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090309031410/http://www.f2photo.co.uk/profiles/profiles_f2_johnswannell.html|archivedate=2009-03-09}}</ref>

In November 1994 [[Diana, Princess of Wales]] personally commissioned Swannell to photograph her together with her sons.

In February 2012 Swannell was commissioned to take the official photographs of [[Queen Elizabeth II]] and the [[Duke of Edinburgh]] to mark the [[Diamond Jubilee]] of her accession to the throne.<ref>{{cite web|title=Official portraits released|url=http://www.thediamondjubilee.org/accession-day-6-february-2012|publisher=The official website of The Queen's Diamond Jubilee|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120209072431/http://www.thediamondjubilee.org/accession-day-6-february-2012|archivedate=2012-02-09}}</ref>

===Awards===
In 1993 Swannell was awarded a Fellowship of the [[Royal Photographic Society]]; he was one of the youngest members to have achieved this status at the time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Masters of photography: John Swannell|url=http://www.professionalphotographer.co.uk/Legends/Interviews/John-Swannell|publisher=Professional Photographer}}</ref>

==Charity work==
On 7 June 2011 Swannell gave an illustrated talk "My world in pictures" at the [[Royal Geographical Society]] in aid of the [[National Autistic Society]].

==Personal life==
Swannell's mother, Lilly McCann, came from just outside [[Enniskillen]], [[County Fermanagh]], Northern Ireland. He has a large family connection still there in the McCann family.

Swannell is married to [[Marianne Lah]] and they have two children, daughter Sophia (b. 1983) and son Charlie (b. 1989). He also has two daughters, Alice and Jane, from his previous marriage.
