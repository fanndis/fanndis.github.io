The '''2005 Australian Open''' was a Grand Slam [[tennis]] tournament held in Melbourne, Australia from 17 until 30 January 2005. [[Roger Federer]] was unsuccessful in defending his 2004 title, being defeated in the semi-finals by eventual champion [[Marat Safin]] in a rematch of the 2004 final. Safin defeated third-seed [[Lleyton Hewitt]] in the final in four sets. [[Justine Henin|Justine Henin-Hardenne]] could not defend her 2004 title due to an injury suffered in the second half of 2004. [[Serena Williams]], the champion in [[2003 Australian Open|2003]], defeated [[Lindsay Davenport]] in the women's final.

==Seniors==

===Men's Singles===
{{main|2005 Australian Open – Men's Singles}}
{{flagicon|RUS}} '''[[Marat Safin]]''' defeated {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Lleyton Hewitt]], 1–6, 6–3, 6–4, 6–4
*It was Safin's 1st title of the year, and his 15th overall. It was his 2nd career Grand Slam title, his 1st Australian Open title and the last championship of his career. Safin became the second Russian player to win the Australian Open men's singles title, following [[Yevgeny Kafelnikov]]'s victory in 1999.

===Women's Singles===
{{main|2005 Australian Open – Women's Singles}}
{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]''' defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Lindsay Davenport]], 2–6, 6–3, 6–0
*It was Williams's 1st title of the year, and her 26th overall. It was her 7th career Grand Slam title, and her 2nd Australian Open title. The final featured a long injury time out for Williams in the second set at 3-3.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://staugustine.com/stories/012905/spo_2856524.shtml | title=Serena Williams overcomes rib injury | accessdate=11 April 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.tennis-x.com/story/2005-01-29/h.php | title=Serena outlasts exhausted Davenport | accessdate=11 April 2016}}</ref>
