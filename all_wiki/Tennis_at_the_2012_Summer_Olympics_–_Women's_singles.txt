The women's singles [[tennis]] tournament at the [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Olympic Games]] in [[London]] was held at the [[All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club]] in [[Wimbledon, London|Wimbledon]] from 28 July to 4 August 2012, and was the first Olympic [[grass court]] tournament since tennis was re-introduced to the Games.<ref name="Official Site of 2012 Games">{{cite web|url=http://www.london2012.com/tennis/event/men-singles/index.html|title=Tennis|publisher=London 2012 Organization Committee|accessdate=7 July 2012}}</ref>

[[Elena Dementieva]] was the reigning champion having [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|won gold]] at the [[2008 Summer Olympic Games|2008 Olympics]] in Beijing.<ref>{{cite news|title=Tennis stars excited for London Olympics|url=https://www.google.com/hostednews/ukpress/article/ALeqM5i9bOhoSHwLotCP5xv59h_6iLk1-g?docId=N0059141340469593340A|accessdate=7 July 2012|date=23 June 2012 |agency=The Press Association}}</ref> She retired from the sport in 2010 and thus did not defend her title. The first round provided some surprising results, with [[Li Na]] and [[Samantha Stosur]] both eliminated in three sets. The biggest upset was provided by [[Julia Görges]] defeating Wimbledon finalist [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] in three close sets. Three British players made it into the second round but all fell in straight sets.

Both finalists, [[Serena Williams]] and [[Maria Sharapova]], were fighting for a Career Golden Slam, and Williams won the gold medal (without losing a set, without losing more than three games in any set, and without losing more than five games in any single match) and became the second woman to win a Career Golden Slam after [[Steffi Graf]]. Williams also became the first tennis player, male or female, to hold a Career Golden Slam in both singles and doubles. Furthermore, Williams' serve was only broken once throughout the entire tournament, by [[Urszula Radwańska]] in the second round. Williams defeated Sharapova 6–0, 6–1 in the final. The bronze medal was won by [[Victoria Azarenka]], who defeated [[Maria Kirilenko]] in the bronze medal match 6–3, 6–4.
