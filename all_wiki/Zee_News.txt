'''Zee News''' is a [[Hindi language|Hindi-language]] [[news channel]] owned by the [[Essel Group]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.esselgroup.com/zee-media-corporation-limited.html |title=Our Companies: Zee Media Corporation Limited |website=esselgroup.com|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> It is associated with several other sister news channels which provide coverage in Hindi, English and several regional languages of India. Zee News was launched on 27 August 1999, as Zee Sports Ltd. The company was [[Certificate of incorporation|reincorporated]] on 27 May 2004, as Zee News Ltd.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.business-standard.com/company/zee-media-27705/information/company-history |title=Zee Media Corporation Ltd. |work=[[Business Standard]]|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> In 2013, Zee News Ltd changed its name to Zee Media Corporation Limited.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/zee-media-corporation-ltd/infocompanyhistory/companyid-18879.cms |title=Zee Media Corporation History {{!}} Zee Media Corporation Information|website=[[The Economic Times]]|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> The Zee News channel is the flagship channel of the company.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.ndtv.com/business/stock/zee-media-corporation-ltd_zeenews/reports-directors-report |title=Director Reports, Company Results of Zee Media Corp |website=[[NDTV Profit]]|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref>

The chairman of the group is [[Subhash Chandra]], who is a [[Bharatiya Janata Party]]-backed member of the [[Rajya Sabha]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Mohan |first=Archis |url=https://www.business-standard.com/article/politics/subhash-chandra-bets-on-bjp-support-for-rajya-sabha-entry-116053101457_1.html |title=Subhash Chandra bets on BJP support for Rajya Sabha entry |date=1 June 2016 |work=Business Standard India|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref> [[Sudhir Chaudhary (journalist)|Sudhir Chaudhary]] is the editor-in-chief of Zee News and the anchor of the prime time show on the channel.<ref name="exch1">{{cite web |url=http://www.exchange4media.mobi/tv/sudhir-chaudhary-now-also-editor-in-chief-of-zee-business_68276.html |title=Sudhir Chaudhary now also editor in Chief of zee business|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20170715163422/http://www.exchange4media.mobi/tv/sudhir-chaudhary-now-also-editor-in-chief-of-zee-business_68276.html|archive-date=15 July 2017|access-date=15 July 2017 }}</ref> The channel has been involved in several controversies and has broadcast fabricated news stories on multiple occasions.<ref name="fake">List of sources:

=== Newspaper ===
The Daily News & Analysis was an English-language [[broadsheet]] newspaper owned by the Zee Media Corporation Limited. It was launched in 2005 and branded under the Daily News & Analysis (DNA) program of Zee News. It was primarily circulated in [[Mumbai]] and discontinued on 9 October 2019 citing losses.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://theprint.in/india/dna-goes-out-of-print-14-years-after-launch-rise-fall-promising-newspaper/304050/ |title=DNA goes out of print 14 years after launch – rise & sudden fall of a promising newspaper |last=Dutta |first=Amrita Nayak |date=11 October 2019 |website=[[ThePrint]] |language=en-US|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.chronosphere.biz/IMO/26/imo.html#dna |title=DNA commissions E&Y to certify circulation |author=Bhupesh Trivedi |date=1 June 2006 |work=Indian Media Observer (issue #26)|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071219110303/http://www.chronosphere.biz/IMO/26/imo.html#dna |archive-date=19 December 2007}}</ref>

===Cases of fabrication===

==== Jawaharlal Nehru University sedition controversy ====
{{main|2016 JNU sedition controversy}}
''Zee News'' reported that students from the Democratic Students' Union (DSU) raised "anti-India" slogans such as ''Bharat ki barbadi'' (Destruction of India) and ''Pakistan Zindabad'' (Long live Pakistan) in an event on the [[Jawaharlal Nehru University]] campus.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://zeenews.india.com/news/india/from-pakistan-zindabad-to-bharat-ki-barbaadi-slogans-that-were-raised-by-anti-nationals-in-jnu_1855562.html |title=From 'Pakistan Zindabad' to 'Bharat ki barbaadi' – Slogans that were raised by 'anti-nationals' in JNU |date=14 February 2016 |newspaper=Zee News}}</ref> In a letter Vishwa Deepak, a journalist working at the channel gave a statement that "our biases made us hear ''Bhartiya Court Zindabad'' (Long live Indian courts) as ''Pakistan Zindabad.''" Vishwa Deepak later resigned from the channel after expressing reservations over its "biased coverage". The footage on the newscast of Zee News had formed the basis of charges filed by the [[Delhi Police]].<ref name="TIE-quit2">{{cite news |url=http://indianexpress.com/article/india/india-news-india/zee-news-producer-quits-video-we-shot-had-no-pakistan-zindabad-slogan/ |title=Zee News producer quits: Video we shot had no Pakistan Zindabad slogan |date=22 February 2016 |newspaper=The Indian Express}}</ref> Sudhir Chaudhary, editor and prime time anchor of the channel however on a telecast made a statement saying "our channel only showed what was happening there, whatever we have shown is 100% authentic."<ref name="newslaundry-VD2">{{cite web |url=http://www.newslaundry.com/2016/02/21/we-speak-with-the-zee-news-journalist-who-resigned-over-the-channels-coverage-of-junrow/ |title=We speak with the Zee News journalist who resigned over the channel's coverage of #JNURow |date=21 February 2016 |newspaper=Newslaundry}}</ref> The forensic report of the Delhi Police however later stated that the footage was doctored.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.indiatoday.in/india/delhi/story/forensic-experts-say-kanhaiya-video-was-doctored-309626-2016-02-19 |title=Forensic experts say Kanhaiya video was doctored |newspaper=India Today|access-date=12 March 2019 |agency=Ist |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.indiatoday.in/india/delhi/story/forensic-experts-say-kanhaiya-video-was-doctored-309626-2016-02-19 |title=JNUSU's Kanhaiya Kumar sedition row: 'Raw footage given by TV channel Doctored, finds Forensic Experts' |date=12 June 2016 |work=India Today |accessdate=5 March 2019 |agency=Express News Service |language=en-IN}}</ref>

==== GPS Chips in ₹2000 currency notes ====

Arun Jaitley Dismisses Rumours |website=NDTV.com|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref> The [[Reserve Bank of India]] has also given a statement that no such chips are present in the currency notes.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Raghavan |first=TCA Sharad |url=https://www.thehindu.com/business/Economy/No-electronic-chip-in-Rs.-2000-notes-RBI/article16440933.ece |title=No electronic chip in Rs. 2000 notes: RBI |date=9 November 2016 |work=The Hindu|access-date=24 February 2020|language=en-IN |issn=0971-751X}}</ref> The presence of "nano GPS" in the currency notes has been classified as a [[hoax]] being spread on social media.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://gadgets.ndtv.com/social-networking/news/2000-note-nano-gps-chip-rumours-1623133 |title=No, New Rs. 2000 Note Will Not Have a 'Nano GPS Chip' |website=NDTV Gadgets 360 |language=en|access-date=9 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.indiatoday.in/technology/features/story/no-rs-2000-note-wont-have-nano-gps-for-tracking-but-it-may-have-rfid-351039-2016-11-10 |title=No, Rs 2000 note doesn't have nano GPS for tracking but it may have RFID |date=10 November 2016 |website=[[India Today]] |language=en|access-date=9 April 2020 |last3=Ist |first3=2016 10:06}}</ref>

==== Coronavirus Cases Misreporting ====

=== Mahua Moitra criminal defamation case ===
Zee News telecasted a show featuring editor-in-chief [[Sudhir Chaudhary (journalist)|Sudhir Chaudhary]] where he claimed that [[Trinamool Congress]] legislator [[Mahua Moitra]] had plagiarised author Martin Longman in her maiden speech after being elected to the [[Lok Sabha]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.altnews.in/zee-news-falsely-claims-mahua-moitra-plagiarised-her-maiden-parliament-speech/ |title=Zee News falsely claims Mahua Moitra plagiarised her maiden Parliament speech |last=Chaudhuri |first=Pooja |date=3 July 2019 |website=Alt News |language=en-GB|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref> Moitra accused the channel of false reporting and submitted a breach of privilege motion against Zee News and Sudhir Chaudhary.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.business-standard.com/article/news-ani/mahua-moitra-submits-breach-of-privilege-motion-against-zee-tv-ls-speaker-disallows-it-119070400464_1.html |title=Mahua Moitra submits breach of privilege motion against Zee TV, LS speaker disallows it |date=4 July 2019 |work=Business Standard|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref> Martin Longman responded and stated that the legislator did not plagiarise him.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.indiatoday.in/india/story/martin-longman-fascism-article-mahua-moitra-1561305-2019-07-03 |title=Trinamool MP Mahua Moitra didn't plagiarise: US writer of fascism article shuts trolls |date=3 July 2019 |website=[[India Today]] |language=en|access-date=24 February 2020 }}</ref> Subsequently, Moitra filed a criminal defamation case against Chaudhary.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |url=https://thewire.in/law/mahua-moitra-criminal-defamation-sudhir-chaudhary |title=Mahua Moitra Files Criminal Defamation Case Against Sudhir Chaudhary |website=The Wire|access-date=24 February 2020}}</ref>

=== Coverage of 2020 Delhi election results ===
The [[2020 Delhi Legislative Assembly election]] were held on 8 February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/elections/assembly-elections/delhi/delhi-election-exit-polls-2020-results-live-news/liveblog/74028057.cms |title=Delhi Election 2020 : Polling held peacefully, counting of votes on February 11 |website=[[The Economic Times]] |language=en|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref> The exit poll results predicted the [[Aam Aadmi Party]] to retain their government in [[National capital territory of Delhi]]. The Zee News telecast of the exit poll results featured Sudhir Chaudhary who indulged in polemic commentary against the voters of the election.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://theprint.in/plugged-in/primetime/evm-tampering-in-delhi-across-channels-zees-sudhir-chaudhury-angry-at-voters/362327/ |title=EVM tampering in Delhi across channels & Zee's Sudhir Chaudhury angry at voters |last=Jha |first=Fiza |date=10 February 2020 |website=[[ThePrint]] |language=en-US|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref> He stated that the people of Delhi had chosen [[Pakistan]] over [[Hindustan]] and that the rule of [[Mughals]] will now return. He further alleged the people of Delhi are lazy and only concerned about "freebies" and that issues like [[Ram Janmabhoomi|Ram Mandir]], [[2019 Balakot airstrike|Balakot airstrike]] and [[Article 370 of the Constitution of India|Revocation of Article 370]] do not matter to them which is why they have rejected the [[Bharatiya Janata Party]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.jansatta.com/national/exit-poll-2020-delhi-election-zee-news-tv-anchor-sudhir-chaudhary-after-exit-poll-out-people-not-worried-about-country/1312191/ |title=वीडियो: एग्जिट पोल में AAP की जीत पर एंकर ने कहा- दिल्ली को देश टूटने की चिंता नहीं |date=9 February 2020 |website=[[Jansatta]] |language=hi|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref> The telecast resulted in backlash and mockery of Zee News and Sudhir Chaudhary on social media.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.freepressjournal.in/india/how-does-this-qualify-as-journalism-twitter-mocks-zee-news-and-sudhir-chaudhary-for-blaming-delhi-voters-after-exit-polls |title='How does this qualify as journalism?': Twitter mocks Zee News and Sudhir Chaudhary for blaming Delhi voters after exit polls |date=9 February 2020 |website=[[Free Press Journal]] |language=en|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref>

===Ban in Nepal===
On 9 July 2020, Nepal's satellite and cable television operators banned Zee News and some other Indian privately owned news channels, citing "propaganda and defamatory report against Nepal government".<ref>{{cite news |title=Cable operators ban Indian news channels in response to 'insulting' video about prime minister |url=https://kathmandupost.com/national/2020/07/09/cable-operators-ban-indian-news-channels-in-response-to-insulting-video-about-prime-minister |accessdate=13 July 2020 |publisher=The Kathmandu Post}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Nepal bans private Indian channels for airing 'false propaganda' |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/07/nepal-bans-private-indian-channels-airing-false-propaganda-200710094837428.html |accessdate=13 July 2020 |publisher=Al Jazeera}}</ref>
