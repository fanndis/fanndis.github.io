'''Paul G. Kengor''' (born December 6, 1966) is an author and professor of political science at [[Grove City College]], a private Christian [[liberal arts college]] in [[Grove City, Pennsylvania]]. He is the executive director of [[Institute for Faith and Freedom]], a Grove City College [[ Conservatism in the United States|conservative]] think tank/policy center. He is also a visiting fellow at the [[Hoover Institution|Hoover Institution on War, Revolution, and Peace]], a conservative think tank at [[Stanford University]].

Kengor has focused much of his work on [[Ronald Reagan]], faith and the presidency, conservative politics, the [[Cold War]], the communist movement, and [[Catholic Church|Catholicism]]. 

==Education==
Kengor received his master's degree from the [[American University School of International Service]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.gcc.edu/Pages/Grove-City-College.aspx |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-05-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140508152438/http://gcc.edu/Pages/Grove-City-College.aspx |archive-date=2014-05-08 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and his doctorate<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gcc.edu/Home/Staff-Directory/Staff-Detail/paul-kengor|title=Paul Kengor|work=Grove City College|access-date=2018-10-28|language=en-US}}</ref> from the [[University of Pittsburgh Graduate School of Public and International Affairs]].

== Career ==
Kengor has done work for the [[Center for Strategic and International Studies]] and the conservative think tanks, the [[Heritage Foundation]] and the [[Allegheny Institute for Public Policy]]; and has served on the editorial board of [[Presidential Studies Quarterly]]. He does a regular commentary for three nationally syndicated radio programs: American Radio Journal, Moody Broadcasting, and [[WDEO (AM)|Ave Maria Radio Network]]/CatholicExchange.com.<ref>http://www.visionandvalues.org/</ref> He also writes for the conservative [[American Spectator]].<ref>[https://spectator.org/author/paul-kengor/ American Spectator Authors. Paul Kengor] American Spectator. 2020. Retrieved November 28, 2020</ref>
