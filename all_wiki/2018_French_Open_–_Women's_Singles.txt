{{main|2018 French Open}}

World No. 1 [[Simona Halep]] won her first Grand Slam title, defeating [[Sloane Stephens]] in the final, 3–6, 6–4, 6–1 to win the Women's Singles tennis title at the [[2018 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2018/jun/09/french-open-2018-sloane-stephens-simona-halep-live-score|title =Simona Halep beats Sloane Stephens to win French Open title – as it happened|date=9 June 2018|work=Guardian|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref> She became the second Romanian woman to win a Grand Slam title in  Women's Singles after the [[1978 French Open]] champion [[Virginia Ruzici]].<ref name="Halep">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/44425403|title=French Open 2018: Simona Halep beats Sloane Stephens in final|author=Jonathan Jurejko|publisher=BBC Sport|date=9 June 2018|accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref> She also became the sixth woman to win both the senior and junior title, having won the latter in [[2008 French Open – Girls' Singles|2008]].<ref name="Halep" />

Halep retained the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|WTA no. 1 singles ranking]] after defeating fellow contender [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the semifinals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eurosport.co.uk/tennis/simona-halep-reaches-french-open-final-and-retains-number-one-ranking_sto6797079/story.shtml|title=Simona Halep reaches French Open final and retains number one ranking|publisher=PA Sport|date=7 June 2018|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref> In addition to Halep and Muguruza, [[Caroline Wozniacki]], [[Elina Svitolina]], [[Karolína Plíšková]] and [[Caroline Garcia]] were also in contention for the top ranking at the start of the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/battle-no1-wozniacki-svitolina-muguruza-aim-unseat-halep-paris|title=Battle for No.1: Wozniacki, Svitolina, Muguruza aim to unseat Halep in Paris|author=Courtney Nguyen|publisher=WTA Insider|date=23 May 2018|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref> 

[[Jeļena Ostapenko]] was the defending champion, but was defeated in the first round by [[Kateryna Kozlova]], making her only the second French Open champion (after [[Anastasia Myskina]] in [[2005 French Open|2005]]) to lose in the first round of her title defense.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/44273662|title=French Open 2018: Venus Williams and Jelena Ostapenko lose in first round|publisher=BBC Sport|date=27 May 2018|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref>

This was the final appearance of the [[2010 French Open – Women's Singles|2010 champion]] [[Francesca Schiavone]] in the final Grand Slam tournament, she lost to [[Viktória Kužmová]] in the first round; she would announce her retirement months later.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.espn.co.uk/tennis/story/_/id/23623110/french-open-2018-unhappy-first-venus-williams-season |title=An unhappy 'first' for Venus Williams this season |author=Bonnie D. Ford |publisher=ESPN |date=27 May 2018 |accessdate=27 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2018-09-05/2018-09-05_francesca_schiavone_announces_retirement_at_us_open.html|title=Francesca Schiavone announces retirement at US Open|publisher=U.S. Open|date=5 September 2018|accessdate=5 Sep 2018}}</ref>

This tournament marked the first time that [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] did not play in the main draw of a Grand Slam singles event since her debut at the [[2006 Wimbledon Championships]], ending a streak of 47 consecutive appearances.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sport.pl/tenis/7,64987,23412513,roland-garros-2018-agnieszka-radwanska-nie-zagra-nie-chcemy.html|title=Roland Garros 2018. Agnieszka Radwańska nie zagra. 'Nie chcemy ryzykować'|work=Sport.pl|access-date=2018-05-17|language=pl-PL}}</ref> 

This was [[Serena Williams]]' first Grand Slam appearance since the [[2017 Australian Open]] and giving birth to her daughter in September 2017. She was unseeded in a Grand Slam singles event for the first time since the [[2007 Australian Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/01/27/sports/27iht-web.0127tennis.4368100.html |title=Williams shocks Sharapova to win Australian Open - Sports - International Herald Tribune|author=Christopher Clarey|work=The New York Times|date=27 January 2007|accessdate=7 June 2018}}</ref> Williams was attempting to equal [[Margaret Court]]'s [[List of Grand Slam women's singles champions#Most Grand Slam tournament singles titles|all-time record]] of 24 Grand Slam singles titles. She was also attempting to become only the second player, after [[Steffi Graf]], to complete a quadruple [[Grand Slam (tennis)#Multiple Career Grand Slams|career Grand Slam]], however she withdrew before her fourth round match against [[Maria Sharapova]] due to a pectoral muscle injury, ending her streak of 10 consecutive major quarterfinals dating back to the [[2014 US Open – Women's Singles|2014 US Open]] (she did not play between the [[2017 French Open – Women's Singles|2017 French Open]] and [[2018 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2018 Australian Open]] due to pregnancy and maternity leave).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/06/04/sports/serena-williams-withdraw-french-open.html|title=Serena Williams Is Out of French Open With Injury|author=Christopher Clarey|work=The New York Times|date=4 June 2018|accessdate=4 June 2018}}</ref>

This was also the final [[French Open]] appearance of [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|former World No. 1]] and two time ([[2012 French Open - Women's Singles|2012]], [[2014 French Open - Women's Singles|2014]]) champion [[Maria Sharapova]]. She lost to former World No. 1 and the [[2016 French Open – Women's Singles|2016]] champion [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the quarterfinals.
