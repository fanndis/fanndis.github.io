'''Ella Mai Howell''' (born 3 November 1994) is an English singer and songwriter. Her singing career began at the [[British and Irish Modern Music Institute]] in London in 2014, during which time she auditioned as part of a trio on the [[The X Factor (British series 11)|11th season]] of ''[[The X Factor (British TV series)|The X Factor]]''. In 2015, she uploaded a four-track solo EP of originals to SoundCloud titled Troubled, and was discovered on social media by American record producer [[Mustard (record producer)|Mustard]] and signed with his record label, 10 Summers Records.

From 2016 to 2018, she released three [[Extended play|EPs]] on the label, including ''Time'', ''Change'', and ''[[Ready (Ella Mai EP)|Ready]]''. Her [[Ella Mai (album)|self-titled debut studio album]] was released in October 2018 and featured the singles "[[Boo'd Up]]" and "[[Trip (Ella Mai song)|Trip]]", which were released on 20 February 2018 and 3 August 2018, respectively. In 2019, "Boo'd Up" was nominated for two [[Grammy Award]]s: [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Song|Best R&B Song]], winning for the latter, as well as Mai herself being nominated for [[Brit Award for British Breakthrough Act|British Breakthrough Act]] at the [[2019 Brit Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/idles-1975-dua-lipa-2019-brit-awards-nominations-2430434|title=IDLES, The 1975, and Dua Lipa amongst stars nominated for 2019 BRIT Awards|last=Daly|first=Rhian|date=12 January 2019|website=NME|accessdate=24 January 2019}}</ref> At the [[2019 Billboard Music Awards|2019 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]], she won three awards, including the award for [[Billboard Music Award for Top R&B Artist|Top R&B Artist]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Lynch|first=Joe|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/8509655/billboard-music-awards-2019-winners-list|title=2019 Billboard Music Awards Winners: The Complete List|website=Billboard|date=1 May 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Ella Mai was born on 3 November 1994 to a Jamaican mother and an Irish father<ref name=BillboardEllaMai>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8461270/ella-mai-chartbreaker-bood-up-interview|title=Chartbreaker: Ella Mai Can't Believe How Huge 'Boo'd Up' Has Become: 'I Have No Words'|website=Billboard.com|accessdate=27 November 2018}}</ref> in [[London]]. According to the singer, her mother, a lover of American [[jazz]] music, named her after [[Ella Fitzgerald]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nylon.com/articles/ella-mai-interview|title=Ella Mai Makes The Music That We Can't Stop Listening To|date=24 March 2017|website=Nylon.com|accessdate=27 November 2018}}</ref> Mai moved from London to [[New York City]] at the age of 12 and graduated from [[Queens High School of Teaching]] in [[Glen Oaks, Queens]], before returning to England.<ref name=BillboardEllaMai/>

==Career==
Ella Mai's singing career began studying at the [[British and Irish Modern Music Institute]] London (BIMM London) in 2014. During that time she competed on [[The X Factor (UK season 11]] of ''[[The X Factor (UK TV series)|The X Factor]]'' as part of a trio, 'Arize', but didn't advance beyond the initial audition for the judges. The group broke up shortly thereafter. 

===2015–19: Self-titled debut album===

In 2015, Mai uploaded a four-track solo EP of original recorded songs to [[SoundCloud]] titled ''Troubled''. Shortly after, the release of ''Troubled'' Mai was discovered on [[Instagram]] and signed to [[DJ Mustard]]'s label, 10 Summers Records and [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.interscope.com/artists/ella-mai|title=Ella Mai|date=24 May 2018|website=Interscope.com}}</ref> In February 2016, she released ''Time'', the first in her EP trilogy. The six-track EP included the single "She Don't", which featured [[Ty Dolla Sign]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ella-mai-time-ep-new-mixtape.116276.html|title=Ella Mai – Time EP – Download & Listen [New Mixtape]|website=Hotnewhiphop.com}}</ref> She released her second EP, ''Change'', in November 2016 and third, ''[[Ready (Ella Mai EP)|Ready]]'', in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2016/11/ella-mai-change-ep/|title=Take Ella Mai's New 'Change' EP For A Spin|date=22 November 2016|website=Vibe.com}}</ref> "[[Boo'd Up]]", which was featured on the EP, rose in popularity on social media as well as in nightclubs over the next few months.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/05/24/ella-mai-bood-up-interview|title=Ella Mai's Slow Burn|last=Howard|first=Jackson|date=2018|website=The Fader|accessdate=16 June 2018}}</ref> Ella Mai toured with [[Kehlani]] on her [[SweetSexySavage World Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://earmilk.com/2017/03/20/dj-mustard-signee-ella-mai-is-ready-to-be-a-household-name-in-rb-interview/|title=DJ Mustard signee Ella Mai is 'Ready' to be a household name in R&B [Interview] – EARMILK|date=20 March 2017|website=Earmilk.com}}</ref> After Mai served as the opening act on [[Kehlani]]'s tour, her music reached a bigger audience and the song grew on radio airplay in the spring of 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/hiphop/ella-mai-bood-up-billboard-hit-blew-up-r-b-comeback/|title=How Ella Mai's Unlikely 'Billboard' Hit 'Boo'd Up' Exploded In Popularity A Year After Its Release|last=Johnson|first=Cherise|date=15 June 2018|website=Uproxx|accessdate=16 June 2018}}</ref>

[[File:Ella Mai 2017.png|200px|right|thumbnail|upright|Ella Mai in 2017.]]

On 26 April 2018, she released a music video for "[[Boo'd Up]]" after it started to gain popularity.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8389217/ella-mai-bood-up-video|title=Ella Mai Premieres 'Boo'd Up' Video and Talks Debut Album: 'You Can Hear Growth'|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> The song became her first top ten song in the US in the following month, deeming it her "breakthrough hit."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8458488/ella-mai-bood-up-top-10-hot-100|title=Ella Mai Scores First Top 10 on Billboard Hot 100 With 'Boo'd Up'|last=Zellner|first=Xander|date=30 May 2018|website=Billboard|accessdate=16 June 2018}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' wrote that the single is "one of the biggest singles by a breakout female R&B singer in the past 10 years."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/ella-mais-bood-up-return-of-female-rb-singers-w520365|title='Boo'd Up': How Ella Mai Is Leading Female R&B Singers Back Onto the Charts|last=Leight|first=Elias|date=23 May 2018|website=Rolling Stone|accessdate=16 June 2018}}</ref> The single peaked at number five on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]],<ref name="Hot 100 peak">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8460299/post-malone-psycho-no-1-hot-100|title=Post Malone's 'Psycho' Hits No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100, Maroon 5's 'Girls Like You' Leaps to Top Five|work=Billboard|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=11 June 2018|accessdate=12 June 2018}}</ref> and broke the record for the most weeks at number one of any song by a woman on the [[R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay]] chart.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ella Mai's 'Boo'd Up' Sets Record For Most Weeks at No. 1 Among Women On R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8478283/ella-mai-bood-up-sets-record-rb-hip-hop-airplay-chart |website=Billboard}}</ref> On 14 August, she joined [[Bruno Mars]] on the [[24K Magic World Tour]] after [[Cardi B]] dropped out.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/music/news/bruno-mars-replaces-cardi-b-with-ciara-boyz-ii-men-and-more-on-24k-magic-tour/ar-BBLWJ9K|title=Bruno Mars Replaces Cardi B with Ciara, Boyz II Men and More on 24K Magic Tour|website=www.msn.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-08-15}}</ref> 

On 3 August, she released the single "[[Trip (Ella Mai song)|Trip]]", with an accompanied music video releasing on 18 September.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ella Mai Is Lovestruck on Her New Single 'Trip' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8468604/ella-mai-new-song-trip |website=Billboard}}</ref> The song peaked at number 11 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. Ella Mai released her [[Ella Mai (album)|self-titled]] debut album, a 16-track set featuring "Boo'd Up", on 12 October 2018, with guest appearances from [[Chris Brown]], [[John Legend]], and [[H.E.R.]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8479631/ella-mai-debut-album-stream-it-now|title=Ella Mai's Debut Album Is Here: Stream It Now|work=Billboard}}</ref> The album sold 69,000 [[album-equivalent units]] with 17,000 coming from pure sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8480932/a-star-is-born-soundtrack-second-week-no-1-billboard-200|title='A Star Is Born' Soundtrack Spends Second Week at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Chart|work=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=21 October 2018}}</ref> On 22 October 2018, Mai announced her debut tour set to begin in January 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8481003/ella-mai-tour-dates|title=Ella Mai Announces The Debut Tour: See the Dates|website=Billboard.com|accessdate=27 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2018/10/23/ella-mai-announces-her-2019-the-debut-tour/|title=Ella Mai Announces Her 2019 'The Debut Tour' - The Source|date=23 October 2018|website=Thesource.com|accessdate=27 November 2018}}</ref>
In November, she made two guest appearances on [[JID]]'s song "Tiiied", along with [[6lack|6LACK]] and on [[Meek Mill]]'s song "[[24/7 (Meek Mill song)|24/7]]", on the albums [[DiCaprio 2|Dicapiro 2]] and [[Championships (album)|Championships]] respectively. On 18 November, she performed on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' with [[Jezebel (website)|''Jezebel'']] describing Mai as "Pure '90s R&B Heartthrob".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/11/ella-mai-performs-trip-bood-up-on-snl-watch/|title=Ella Mai Performs "Trip" and "Boo'd Up" on 'SNL': Watch|date=18 November 2018|website=Spin.com|accessdate=27 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://jezebel.com/on-saturday-night-live-ella-mai-is-pure-90s-r-b-heartt-1830523774|title=On Saturday Night Live, Ella Mai is Pure '90s R&B Heartthrob|first=Emily|last=Alford|website=Jezebel.com|accessdate=27 November 2018}}</ref> 

Mai was also nominated for two [[61st Grammy Awards|Grammy Awards]]: [[Best R&B Song]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] with "[[Boo'd Up]]", winning for the former.<ref>{{cite web |title=ELLA MAI NOMINATED FOR GRAMMY AWARD |url=https://www.bimm.ie/news/ella-mai-nominated-for-grammy-award/ |website=BIMM |accessdate=5 January 2019}}</ref> "Boo'd Up" would go onto to win the 2019 Billboard Music Award for Top R&B Song.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/8509655/billboard-music-awards-2019-winners-list|title=2019 Billboard Music Awards Winners: The Complete List|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref> In 2019, Mai won the [[Billboard Music Award]]s for Top R&B Artist and Top Female R&B Artist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/8509655/billboard-music-awards-2019-winners-list|title=2019 Billboard Music Awards Winners: The Complete List|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref>
Apart from her music, Mai also performed a sketch on [[Nickelodeon (United States)|Nickelodeon]]'s ''[[All That]]'' (which aired on 27 July 2019) called "Boo'd Up", a paranormal parody of ''[[The Dating Game]]'', in which she played herself as a contestant looking for a ghost to haunt her family home, but instead of choosing the three suitors offered, she chose her former ghost who came back and she forgave him.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k3ykhsid2iI Ella Mai Gets 'Boo'd Up' by Ghosts on All That!] from [[YouTube]] (27 July 2019)</ref>

===2020: Second studio album===
In 2020, Mai received her third nomination for the [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Album]] for her self-titled debut at the [[62nd Grammy Awards]].

On 2 October 2020, Mai released the single, "Not Another Love Song".<ref name="Return" />

==Artistry==
In multiple interviews, Ella Mai names [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Chris Brown]], [[Brandy Norwood|Brandy]], [[Destiny's Child]], [[Alicia Keys]], and [[Mariah Carey]] as her biggest influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2017/10/01/chris-brown-previews-ella-mai-collaboration/|title=Chris Brown Previews Ella Mai Collaboration|website=Rap-up.com}}</ref> ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'' said her "perception of the powerful emotion is kindred to R&B heartthrobs of the '90s, who flooded the radio airwaves with soulful ballads, baby-making tunes, and heart-wrenching break-up anthems."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.vibe.com/amp/2018/02/ella-mai-interview-next|title=NEXT: Ella Mai Is Bringing Confidence Back To R&B Love Songs|date=26 February 2018|website=Vibe.com|first=Jessica|last=McKinney|accessdate=29 April 2019 }}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' described her sound as "a British émigré full of self-confidence and an affinity for classic Nineties [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-june-2017-w484147/ella-mai-w484212|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know Now|website=Rolling Stone}}</ref>
