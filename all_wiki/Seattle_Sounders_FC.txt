'''Seattle Sounders FC'''<!--Per the Sounders organization, the club name is FC, not Football Club. Please see talk. Further discussion is always welcomed.--> is an American professional [[association football|soccer]] club based in [[Seattle|Seattle, Washington]]. The Sounders compete as a member of the [[Western Conference (MLS)|Western Conference]] of [[Major League Soccer]] (MLS). The club was established on November 13, 2007, and began play in 2009 as an MLS [[expansion team]]. The Sounders are a [[Phoenix club (association football)|phoenix club]], carrying the same name as the [[Seattle Sounders (1974–1983)|original franchise]] that competed in the [[North American Soccer League (1968–1984)|North American Soccer League]] from 1974 to 1983.

The club's majority owner is [[Adrian Hanauer]], and its minority owners are [[Jody Allen|the estate of Paul Allen]], [[Drew Carey]], and 14 families from the Seattle area. Former [[Seattle Sounders (1994–2008)|USL Sounders]] coach and assistant coach [[Brian Schmetzer]] took over as head coach in July 2016 after the departure of [[Sigi Schmid]]. The Sounders play their home league matches at [[Lumen Field]], with a reduced capacity of 37,722 seats for most matches. Along with several organized groups, a 53-member marching band called 'Sound Wave' supports the club at each home match. Seattle has long-standing rivalries with nearby clubs [[Portland Timbers-Seattle Sounders rivalry|Portland Timbers]] and [[Vancouver Whitecaps FC]], with whom it competes for the [[Cascadia Cup]].

The Sounders played their inaugural match on March 19, 2009, winning 3–0 over the [[New York Red Bulls]]. Seattle has been among the league's most successful teams, winning the [[U.S. Open Cup]] four times, the [[Supporters' Shield]] in [[2014 Major League Soccer season|2014]], and the [[MLS Cup]] in [[MLS Cup 2016|2016]] and [[MLS Cup 2019|2019]]. The team has qualified for the [[MLS Cup Playoffs]] in each of its eleven seasons, making the final three times, and competed in the [[CONCACAF Champions League]] five times, advancing to the semifinal round once. The team set a new MLS record for average attendance in each of its first five seasons. The Sounders are ranked as one of the [[Major League Soccer's most valuable teams|most valuable franchises]] in North America.

The team's players have included joint top goalscorers [[Clint Dempsey]] and [[Fredy Montero]], as well as long-time captain [[Osvaldo Alonso]], and current captain [[Nicolás Lodeiro]]. The Sounders also operate a players' academy and lower division teams that have produced homegrown players, including forward [[Jordan Morris]] and current [[Newcastle United F.C.]] defender [[DeAndre Yedlin]].

==History==
{{main|History of Seattle Sounders FC}}
{{see also|History of professional soccer in Seattle}}
Even before the first cities in the United States were chosen to host [[Major League Soccer]] teams, Seattle was considered a viable location for a professional team.<ref name="1994mls"/> In 1994, as the U.S. was preparing to host the [[1994 FIFA World Cup|FIFA World Cup]], more than 30 cities were pursuing the rights to an MLS team, Seattle being among them.<ref name="bigsoccer">{{cite news|first=Tom|last=Farrey |title=Big Soccer Surprise: 38,000 To See Match|url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19940128&slug=1891994 |newspaper=The Seattle Times|date=January 28, 1994|accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> However, despite the strong soccer fan base in Seattle, the absence of a soccer-only stadium was a drawback to establishing an MLS team.<ref>{{cite news |first=Tom |last=Farrey |title=Key Need For Soccer: A Stadium |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19940204&slug=1893501 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=February 4, 1994 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> Cities seeking consideration for an inaugural MLS team were also expected to secure 10,000 assurances from fans for season tickets.<ref name="bigsoccer"/> By the June 3, 1994 deadline for MLS team bids, Seattle organizers had secured fewer than 1,500 such assurances.<ref>{{cite news |title=Soccer Ticket Pledges Exceed 1,300 |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19940603&slug=1913752 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=June 4, 1994 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> These low numbers were a result of competition between the ticket campaign for the MLS expansion team and for the [[Seattle Sounders (1994–2008)|American Professional Soccer League (APSL) Sounders]] expansion team.<ref name="1994tickets">{{cite news |first=Tom |last=Farrey |title=Soccer League Rejects Sounders' Plan; Two Will Compete To Sell Season Tickets |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19940224&slug=1896933 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=February 24, 1994 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref>

In a June 14, 1994 announcement, Seattle was not included among the first seven cities to be awarded an MLS team.<ref name="1994mls">{{cite news |first=Tom |last=Farrey |title=Seattle Misses First MLS Cut&nbsp;– But Bid Isn't Dead |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19940615&slug=1915706 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=June 15, 1994 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> Five more teams were to be announced later in the year, and to improve their chances this time, Seattle MLS organizers began working with the [[University of Washington]] to secure use of [[Husky Stadium]] as an interim stadium while they pursued the construction of a permanent soccer-specific facility.<ref>{{cite news |first=Tom |last=Farrey |title=Seattle Gets Boost in Bid For Team – Husky Stadium Might Be MLS Site |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19940717&slug=1920841 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=July 17, 1994 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> In November 1994, the start of the first MLS season was postponed until 1996, and it was noted that the absence of an "adequate grass-field facility" in the area and the presence of the new APSL Seattle Sounders team had thwarted Seattle's MLS bid.<ref>{{cite news |title=Major League Soccer Postponed Until 1996 |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19941116&slug=1942150 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=November 16, 1994 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> In the end, Seattle was not among the cities chosen to establish a team during the first season of MLS.<ref>{{cite news |title=U.S. Pro Soccer To Become Reality in March Of '96 |url=http://www.deseretnews.com/article/463166/US-PRO-SOCCER-TO-BECOME-REALITY-IN-MARCH-OF-96.html?s_cid=s10 |newspaper=Deseret News |date=June 7, 1995 |accessdate=February 18, 2012}}</ref>

In 1996, as [[Seattle Seahawks]] owner [[Paul Allen]] worked with the city to build a new football stadium for his team, the potential of an MLS expansion team that could be a co-tenant helped drive public support for the effort.<ref>{{cite news |first=Elliott |last=Almond |title=Want A Stadium For Football? Try A Little Soccer |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19960511&slug=2328650 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=May 11, 1996 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> Many of the state's voters supported the referendum to construct Seahawks Stadium because it was also expected to be a professional soccer venue.<ref name="2000mls">{{cite news |first=Bob |last=Sherwin |title=MLS team in Seattle no sure bet |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=20000510&slug=4020160 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=May 10, 2000 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> While the stadium problem was being resolved, a new issue emerged.  By 2000, MLS was moving away from league-operated teams to investor-operated teams, so wealthy individuals would need to step forward for Seattle to obtain an MLS expansion team.<ref name="2000mls"/>

In 2003, Seattle was again listed as a possibility for an MLS expansion team when the ten-team league announced plans to expand into new markets.<ref>{{cite news |title=Garber says MLS plans to add teams |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=20021019&slug=expand19 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=October 19, 2002 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> In 2004, MLS commissioner [[Don Garber]] indicated that Seattle had been "very close" to receiving the expansion team ultimately awarded to [[Real Salt Lake|Salt Lake]]. [[Adrian Hanauer]], then-owner of the United Soccer League's (USL) Sounders (formerly the APSL Sounders), was in discussions with MLS about an estimated payment of $1&nbsp;million to secure rights to a Seattle franchise for 2006.<ref name="2006mls">{{cite news |first=José Miguel |last=Romero |title=Seattle at 'top of list' for MLS team in 2006 |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=20040715&slug=mls15 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=July 15, 2004 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref> However, when Seattle was passed over again in 2006, Hanauer announced that he would not be able to secure an expansion team without the help of more investors willing to cover the increasing MLS franchise fees which had grown beyond $10&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news |first=Matt |last=Massey |title=Seattle's chances for MLS team take a hit |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=20050416&slug=sounders16 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=April 16, 2005 |accessdate=June 23, 2009}}</ref>

===Expansion and inaugural season===
{{Main|2009 Seattle Sounders FC season}}

In 2007, Hanauer teamed up with Hollywood producer [[Joe Roth]] to make another bid for MLS expansion into Seattle, at a cost of $30&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news |first=Ridge |last=Mahoney |title=Sounders bring MLS success in Seattle |url=https://www.usatoday.com/sports/soccer/mls/united/2009-07-28-seattle-sounders_N.htm |newspaper=USA Today |date=July 28, 2009 |accessdate=December 5, 2009}}</ref> Paul Allen, whose First and Goal company operated Qwest Field (now [[Lumen Field]]), joined the ownership group that same year, making the bid the most promising yet for Seattle.<ref>{{cite news|first=Craig |last=Smith |title=Gooooal? City may soon have franchise |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/soccer/2003941033_sounders11.html |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071012230838/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/soccer/2003941033_sounders11.html |archivedate=October 12, 2007 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=October 11, 2007 |accessdate=March 20, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> During the first week of November 2007, rumors began to build that MLS would be announcing an expansion into Seattle the following week, and that the ownership group had taken on a fourth member, TV personality [[Drew Carey]].<ref name="expansion">{{cite news |first=Larry |last=Stone |title=Big-time soccer coming in 2009 |url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=20071110&slug=mls10 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=November 10, 2007 |accessdate=February 18, 2012}}</ref> In a press conference on November 13, 2007, it was announced that Seattle had been awarded an expansion team. The announcement marked the return of [[United States soccer pyramid|top-level]] soccer to Seattle for the first time since the dissolution of its [[Seattle Sounders (NASL)|North American Soccer League (NASL) team]] in 1983.  The announcement also meant that the Seattle Sounders of the USL First Division would play its final season the year before the new MLS franchise was formed.<ref>{{cite news|title=It's official: Seattle to get MLS team in 2009 |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/soccer/2004011415_webmls13.html |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071114193955/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/soccer/2004011415_webmls13.html |archivedate=November 14, 2007 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=November 14, 2007 |accessdate=March 20, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first=Laurence |last=Moroney |title=MLS announces expansion in Seattle |date=November 13, 2007 |url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/news/article/mls-announces-expansion-seattle |publisher=Major League Soccer |accessdate=March 20, 2012 }}</ref>

"Seattle Sounders FC" was announced as the team name on April 7, 2008, along with the team logo, colors and badge design, in a presentation held at the [[Space Needle]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Matthew |last= Gaschk |title=Sounders FC by fans' acclamation |url=http://www.seattlepi.com/soccer/358051_sounders08.html |newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |date=April 7, 2008 |accessdate=July 19, 2009}}</ref> [[Sigi Schmid]] was introduced as the club's first head coach on December 16, 2008, after leaving the [[Columbus Crew]] following their [[MLS Cup 2008|MLS Cup victory]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2008524337_sounders16.html |title=Sigi Schmid becomes Sounders FC's first coach |newspaper=The Seattle Times |last=Romero |first=José Miguel |date=December 16, 2008 |accessdate=December 16, 2008 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081217050552/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/sounders/2008524337_sounders16.html |archivedate=December 17, 2008 }}</ref>

On September 2, 2009, the Sounders became the second MLS expansion team in league history ([[Chicago Fire S.C.|Chicago]] was the first) to win the [[Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup|U.S. Open Cup]] tournament in its first season.<ref name="opencup">{{cite news|title=Seattle Sounders FC Become Second MLS Expansion Team to Claim U.S. Open Cup Crown |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/News/Lamar-Hunt-U-S-Open-Cup/2009/09/Seattle-Sounders-Become-Second-Expansion-Team-to-Claim-US-Open-Cup-Crown.aspx |publisher=United States Soccer Federation |date=September 2, 2009 |accessdate=September 3, 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090905014508/http://www.ussoccer.com/News/Lamar-Hunt-U-S-Open-Cup/2009/09/Seattle-Sounders-Become-Second-Expansion-Team-to-Claim-US-Open-Cup-Crown.aspx |archivedate=September 5, 2009 }}</ref> They did so by defeating [[D.C. In winning the U.S. Open Cup tournament, they qualified for the preliminary round of the [[2010–11 CONCACAF Champions League]].<ref name="opencup"/>

On October 17, 2009, the Sounders became the third MLS expansion team in league history to qualify for the playoffs in its first season. They clinched a playoff berth with a come-from-behind victory over the [[Kansas City Wizards]] 3–2 at Kansas City.<ref>{{cite news|first=Matt |last=Gaschk |title=Sounders Clinch Playoff Berth With 3–2 Win |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/Matchday/Matches/2009/Season/Game-29-at-Kansas-City.aspx |publisher=Sounders FC |date=October 17, 2009 |accessdate=February 18, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120217215112/http://www.soundersfc.com/Matchday/Matches/2009/Season/Game-29-at-Kansas-City.aspx |archivedate=February 17, 2012 }}</ref> Seattle finished the regular season with a record of 12 wins, 7 losses, and 11 draws.  The club set a new MLS record for average attendance with 30,943 fans per match.<ref>{{cite news |first=Greg |last=Johns |title=Sounders explain why season tickets capped at 32,000 |url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/sounders/2010/01/27/sounders-explain-why-season-tickets-capped-at-32000/ |work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |date=January 27, 2010 |accessdate=February 18, 2012}}</ref> Its inaugural season came to an end in the [[2009 MLS Cup Playoffs]] with a loss in the conference semifinals to the [[Houston Dynamo]] by a 1–0 aggregate score in a [[Two-legged match|two-legged series]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Bernardo |last=Fallas |title=Dynamo advance to Western final vs. Galaxy|url=http://www.chron.com/sports/dynamo/article/Dynamo-advance-to-Western-final-vs-Galaxy-1617599.php |work=Houston Chronicle |date=November 8, 2009 |accessdate=February 18, 2012}}</ref> During the 2009 season, all 15 Sounders MLS regular season home matches, its home playoff match, and its four home U.S. Open Cup matches (played at [[Starfire Sports Complex]]) were sold out.<ref>{{cite news|first=José Miguel |last=Romero |title=Sounders success story, by the numbers |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2010203684_soundersnumbers05.html |newspaper=The Seattle Times |date=November 4, 2009 |accessdate=November 10, 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091108012102/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/sounders/2010203684_soundersnumbers05.html |archivedate=November 8, 2009 }}</ref>

===Early years (2010–2013)===

Before the first match of the Sounders' [[2010 Seattle Sounders FC season|second season]], the club increased the number of season ticket holders to 32,000.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/sounders/archives/192482.asp |title=Sounders explain why season tickets capped at 32,000 |first=Greg |last=Johns |date=January 27, 2010 |newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |accessdate=October 11, 2010}}</ref> The first match of the season was played at Qwest Field, with Seattle hosting a new MLS expansion team, the [[Philadelphia Union]]. The Sounders won 2–0 on goals from Brad Evans and Fredy Montero. However, Seattle followed the win by losing 8 of its next 14 matches. In the latter half of the regular season, Seattle reversed its fortune. The team won 10 of its last 15 matches, and clinched a playoff berth for the second consecutive year with a 2–1 win on October 10, 2010 at Kansas City.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sports/2013121167_sounders10.html |title=Sounders clinch a playoff berth with second-half surge |first=Joshua |last=Mayers |date=October 9, 2010 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=October 11, 2010}}</ref> They finished the season with 14 wins, 10 losses, and 6 ties.  In the playoffs, the Sounders were eliminated in the conference semifinals by the [[Los Angeles Galaxy]] on a 3–1 aggregate score.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2013373934_sounders08.html |title=Sounders FC ousted from MLS playoffs |first=Joshua |last=Mayers |date=November 7, 2010 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=December 15, 2010}}</ref> The club broke its own single-season attendance record, averaging 36,173 fans per match,<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.mlsnet.com/news/article/fans-motivate-seattle-2-1-win-over-chivas |title=Fans motivate Seattle to 2–1 win over Chivas |first=Andrew |last=Winner |date=October 17, 2010 |accessdate=October 18, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.today/20120907142513/http://www.mlsnet.com/news/article/fans-motivate-seattle-2-1-win-over-chivas |archivedate=September 7, 2012  }}</ref> and again sold out every league match.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/soundersfcblog/2013163986_sounders_fc_release_chivas_usa.html |title=Sounders FC release Chivas USA preview & broadcast info |first=Joshua |last=Mayers |date=October 14, 2010 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=October 18, 2010}}</ref>

The Sounders also competed in two additional competitions during the 2010 season&nbsp;– the CONCACAF Champions League and the U.S. Open Cup.  In the Champions League, Seattle progressed through the preliminary round, beating [[A.D. Isidro Metapán|Isidro Metapán]] 2–1 on aggregate, but was eliminated in the group stage.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/localnews/2013032175_apsoccdmarathonsounders1stldwritethru.html |title=Fucito's 2 goals boost Sounders past Marathon, 2–0 |first=Joshua |last=Mayers |date=September 29, 2010 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=October 11, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110622080421/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/localnews/2013032175_apsoccdmarathonsounders1stldwritethru.html |archivedate=June 22, 2011 }}</ref> In the U.S. Open Cup, Seattle won matches at Portland and at home against the Los Angeles Galaxy and [[Chivas USA]] before reaching the final, which they hosted at Qwest Field against the [[Columbus Crew]]. ===First Supporters Shield (2014)===
{{main|2014 Seattle Sounders FC season}}
[[File:Sounders celebrating the third goal vs. Colorado Rapids.jpg|thumb|right|Sounders players celebrating a goal against the Colorado Rapids, 2014]]

After a disappointing 2013 season, the Sounders replaced starting goalkeeper Michael Gspurning with Toronto's [[Stefan Frei]]. Several veteran players, including Eddie Johnson, Patrick Ianni, Jhon Kennedy Hurtado, Mauro Rosales, and Steve Zakuani, left the club as part of a major restructure; [[Brad Evans]] was named as club captain.<ref>{{cite news |last=Pentz |first=Matt |date=November 20, 2014 |title=How Sigi Schmid saved his job and moved the Sounders forward |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2025067300_sounders21xml.html |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> [[Marco Pappa]], an experienced MLS player and Guatemalan international, was added to the team. Homegrown player [[DeAndre Yedlin]] was transferred to [[Tottenham Hotspur]] but was loaned back to Seattle until the end of the MLS season.<ref>{{cite news |date=August 13, 2014 |title=Tottenham confirm signing of DeAndre Yedlin on four-year contract |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/aug/13/deandre-yedlin-signs-tottenham-hotspur-seattle-sounders |work=The Guardian |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>

The Sounders advanced to the [[2014 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup Final]] and defeated the [[Philadelphia Union]] in [[extra time]], to win their fourth trophy of the tournament. On October 25, 2014, the final game of the 2014 regular season, the Sounders defeated the LA Galaxy 2–0 to secure and win their first [[Supporters' Shield]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Bird |first=Liviu |date=October 25, 2014 |title=Pappa's brace carries Sounders to Supporters' Shield win over Galaxy |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/planet-futbol/2014/10/25/marco-pappa-two-goals-sounders-supporters-shield-win-over-galaxy |work=Sports Illustrated |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>

Entering the playoffs as the top seed, Seattle defeated FC Dallas on the [[away goals rule]] and advanced to the Western Conference Championship to face the LA Galaxy once again. The Sounders tied the Galaxy on [[aggregate score]] but lost on the away goals rule after losing 1–0 away and winning 2–1 at home. LA would go on to win the MLS Cup.<ref>{{cite news |last=Lilgenwall |first=Ari |date=December 18, 2014 |title=2014 in Review: Seattle Sounders enjoy historic season, but fall just short of reaching MLS Cup |url=http://www.mlssoccer.com/year-in-review/2014/seattle-sounders-fc |publisher=MLSsoccer.com |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141227050554/http://www.mlssoccer.com/year-in-review/2014/seattle-sounders-fc |archivedate=December 27, 2014 |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>

The injury and suspension of the team's main attacking duo led to a death spiral during the summer, with Seattle winning only one match in nine games. By late August, Martins had recovered and led the team to an 8-match unbeaten streak to secure a playoff spot, finishing fourth in the Western Conference. The Sounders had also successfully topped their group in the [[2015–16 CONCACAF Champions League]], beating the Vancouver Whitecaps and [[Club Deportivo Olimpia]], with two wins, one draw, and one loss.<ref>{{cite news |date=September 23, 2015 |title=Seattle Sounders, LA Galaxy through to CCL knockout round |url=http://www.espnfc.com/concacaf-champions-league/story/2629233/seattle-sounders-la-galaxy-through-to-ccl-knockout-round |work=ESPN FC |agency=Associated Press |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> [[Paraguay]]an international [[Nelson Haedo Valdez]] was signed as the club's newest [[Designated Player Rule|designated player]], and was joined by fellow international signings [[Andreas Ivanschitz]] and [[Román Torres]] in August 2015;<ref>{{cite news |last=Ruiz |first=Don |date=August 15, 2015 |title=Sounders to sport new look against Orlando |url=http://www.thenewstribune.com/sports/soccer/mls/sounders-fc/article31221407.html |work=The News Tribune |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> [[Erik Friberg (footballer)|Erik Friberg]] also returned to the club after his stint in Europe.<ref>{{cite news |last=Pentz |first=Matt |date=July 22, 2015 |title=Sounders happy with immediate impact from Erik Friberg |url=http://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/sounders-happy-with-immediate-impact-from-erik-friberg/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>

==Team colors and crest==
The badge design resembles a [[Escutcheon (heraldry)|heraldic shield]], and consists of two layers which represent "the partnership between the ownership, the community, the players and the fans". The logo incorporates the [[Space Needle]], an internationally recognized Seattle landmark. The official team colors are Sounder Blue, signifying the waters of the [[Puget Sound]]; Rave Green, representing the forests of the [[Pacific Northwest]]; and Cascade Shale, representing the [[Cascade Range]] to the east of Seattle.<ref>{{cite news|title=Badge and Colors Fact Sheet|url=http://www.soundersfc.com/News/Articles/2008/04-April/Badge-and-Colors-Fact-Sheet.aspx|publisher=Seattle Sounders FC|accessdate=April 7, 2008}}</ref> Fans chose a name for the team in an online poll held between March 27 and 31, 2008. The initial list of possibilities&nbsp;– Seattle FC, Seattle Republic and Seattle Alliance&nbsp;– deliberately did not include Seattle Sounders in order to provide a fresh start. Despite the names having been selected through fan research and internal committees, the omission of the traditional Sounders name embittered many in the Seattle community.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/News/Articles/2008/04-April/Sounders-FC-Rings-True.aspx |title=Sounders FC Rings True |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |first=Frank |last=MacDonald |date=April 9, 2008 |accessdate=December 5, 2009}}</ref><ref name=team_name>{{cite news|url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/collegesports/2004296104_regr21.html |title=NW Briefs: List narrows for Seattle Major League Soccer team names |newspaper=The Seattle Times |first=José Miguel |last=Romero |date=March 21, 2008 |accessdate=March 21, 2008 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080324210353/http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/collegesports/2004296104_regr21.html |archivedate=March 24, 2008 }}</ref>

In response to the backlash, the team added a fourth [[Write-in candidate|write-in]] option for the team name, allowing for any name to be suggested on the ballot.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.seattlepi.com/soccer/356474_mls26.html |title=Sounders name isn't dead yet |newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |first=Matthew |last=Gaschk |date=March 25, 2008 |accessdate=March 27, 2008}}</ref> Of the more than 14,500 votes received in choosing the new team name, 49% of the votes included some form of the name "Sounders".<ref>{{cite news |title=Seattle Sounders FC Selected as Team Name |date=April 7, 2008 |url =http://www.soundersfc.com/News/Articles/2008/04-April/Seattle-Sounders-FC-Selected-as-Team-Name.aspx |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=July 19, 2009}}</ref> Upon announcing the name of the club, Hanauer acknowledged the significance of keeping with tradition: "The team playing at the highest level in our region has always been called Sounders. Starting with the NASL and then the USL 1st Division, we now have the chance to create a separate and distinct identity with the new MLS team."<ref>{{cite news |title=Introducing, the Seattle Sounders |date=April 7, 2008 |url=http://goal.blogs.nytimes.com/2008/04/07/introducing-the-seattle-sounders/ |newspaper=The New York Times |first=Jeffrey |last=Marcus |accessdate=November 14, 2009}}</ref> The [[Seattle Sounders (NASL)|NASL team]] was originally named in a public contest, with "Sounders" announced as the winner on January 22, 1974.<ref>{{cite news |date=January 22, 1974 |title=Sounders is the new name, soccer's the name of the game |page=D3 |work=The Seattle Times}}</ref>

==Supporters==
{{details|Seattle Sounders FC supporters}}
[[File:Seattle sounders tifo 2.jpg|thumb|right|alt=Fans waving flags and unfurling a large green and blue tifo behind a goal|[[Emerald City Supporters]] unveil a [[tifo]] prior to the club's inaugural match.]]
The Sounders FC Alliance was established at the request of minority owner [[Drew Carey]]. Based on the fan association at FC Barcelona, members of the Alliance have the ability to vote on the removal of the general manager and on other team decisions.  Season ticket holders become automatic members, while non-season ticket holders may buy into the Alliance for a fee. Membership benefits include voting privileges, an invitation to the annual meeting and other team perks. Members may also be elected to the Sounders FC Alliance Council by receiving at least 25 nominations from other members on an annual basis. The first vote on retaining or replacing Sounders general manager Adrian Hanauer was scheduled to be held between October to December 2012. After 13,775 votes registered, Hanauer was retained by the Alliance.<ref>{{cite web|author=Matt Gaschk |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/news/articles/2012/12-december/hanauer-retained-as-gm.aspx |title=Sounders FC Announce Results of Landmark General Manager Vote &#124; Seattle Sounders FC |publisher=Soundersfc.com |date=December 13, 2012 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> Drew Carey is the chairman of the Sounders FC Alliance.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.seattlepi.com/soccer/363518_sounders17.html |title=Sounders FC owner caters to fans |last=Gaschk |first=Matthew |newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |date=May 16, 2008 |accessdate=May 19, 2008}}</ref>

Carey also requested that the Sounders have their own marching band, the first of its kind in MLS.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/sounders/2008/12/05/keeping_it_official.html |title=Keeping it official |accessdate=December 14, 2009 |last=Romero |first=José |date=December 5, 2009 |work=The Seattle Times |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111116204957/http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/sounders/2008/12/05/keeping_it_official.html |archivedate=November 16, 2011  }}</ref> This led to the creation of the Sound Wave, a 53-member marching band consisting of brass and marching percussion.<ref name="soundwave"/> The band plays music from multiple genres, such as Latin, rock and pop,<ref name="soundwave">{{cite web |title=The Band |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/Team/The-Band.aspx |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=March 1, 2009}}</ref> and sits on the north end of Lumen Field.<ref>{{cite news |first=José Miguel |last=Romero |title=Golden Scarf presentation |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/soundersfcblog/2009281780_golden_scarf_presentation.html |work=The Seattle Times |date=May 30, 2009 |accessdate=November 30, 2009}}</ref> The March to the Match, in which fans march from [[Occidental Park (Seattle)|Occidental Park]] to Lumen Field before each home match, has been accompanied by the Sound Wave.<ref>{{cite web |title=March to the Match |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/Matchday/March-to-the-Match.aspx |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=November 11, 2009}}</ref>

Besides the Alliance, there are currently four recognized, independent [[supporters groups]] for the Sounders. [[Emerald City Supporters]] (ECS), which formed in 2005 to support the USL Sounders, is the largest supporter group and sits in the south end of the stadium in sections 121–123.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.weareecs.com/about/ |title=About ECS |newspaper=Emerald City Supporters |publisher=WeAreECS.com |accessdate=May 13, 2008}}</ref> ''Eastside Supporters'' is a group which can be found in section 150 which they call "The Pod". [[Gorilla FC]] is a Sounders supporters group that sits in the south end of Lumen Field in Sections 119 and 120.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/thebigblog/archives/164426.asp |title=The Sounders and Gorilla FC: New club, old sport |accessdate=December 14, 2009 |work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |first=Monica |last=Guzman |date=March 18, 2009 }}</ref> ''The North End Faithful'' sit in the north end of the stadium beneath the "Hawks Nest" in sections 100 and 144–152.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://northendfaithful.com|title=North End Faithful|work=Members Association|publisher=North End Faithful|accessdate=February 26, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110426125540/http://northendfaithful.com/|archive-date=April 26, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Rivalries==

The [[Portland Timbers–Seattle Sounders rivalry|Seattle–Portland]] and Seattle–Vancouver rivalries formed in the mid-seventies in the NASL and carried on through to the USL and MLS. In 2004, the fan-based [[Cascadia Cup]] was created to formalize the competition between the Seattle, [[Portland Timbers (2001–2010)|Portland]], and [[Vancouver Whitecaps (1986–2010)|Vancouver]] USL teams.<ref>{{cite news |title=Soccer fans creating Cascadia Cup |url=http://www.seattlepi.com/othersports/167070_awest31.html |newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer |date=March 31, 2004 |accessdate=October 15, 2009}}</ref> The trophy is exchanged by the three teams' supporters groups and is based on the results of certain regular season matches.<ref>{{cite news |last=Baker |first=Geoff |date=March 21, 2018 |title=Cascadia Cup representatives say Sounders-Timbers match on May 13 won't count in competition standings |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/cascadia-cup-representatives-say-sounders-timbers-match-on-may-13-wont-count-in-competition-standings/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=September 19, 2018}}</ref> The Cascadia Cup was contested without Seattle for two seasons after the Sounders entered MLS in 2009, but Portland and Vancouver were awarded expansion teams that began play in 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sounders FC get a rival&nbsp;– Portland awarded MLS franchise |url=http://www.komonews.com/sports/41582562.html |publisher=KOMO-TV |date=March 20, 2009 |accessdate=May 25, 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111004044858/http://www.komonews.com/sports/41582562.html |archivedate=October 4, 2011 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first=Grant |last=Wahl |title=A Pacific Passion Play |date=May 23, 2011 |work=Sports Illustrated |url=http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/vault/article/magazine/MAG1186007/index.htm |accessdate=June 11, 2011}}</ref>

The fan-created [[Heritage Cup (MLS)|Heritage Cup]] competition with the [[San Jose Earthquakes]] began in the 2009 MLS season. MLS teams that carry on the names of their NASL predecessors are eligible to compete.  The results of their league matches determine the winner.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Heritage Cup |url=http://www.ssvcf.org/heritagecup.html |publisher=Soccer Silicon Valley Community Foundation |accessdate=July 12, 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090325052023/http://www.ssvcf.org/heritagecup.html |archivedate=March 25, 2009 }}</ref><!--<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/soundersfcblog/2009323835_let_the_heritage_cup_competiti.html |title=Let the Heritage Cup competition begin |first=Jose |last=Romero |date=June 10, 2009 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=February 19, 2011}}</ref>-->

Although there is no official rivalry between the Sounders and [[Los Angeles Galaxy]], the two teams have met several times in the MLS Cup Playoffs.<ref>{{cite news |date=October 26, 2015 |title=Sounders FC, LA Galaxy to add another chapter to postseason history |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/post/2015/10/26/sounders-fc-la-galaxy-add-another-chapter-postseason-history |work=SoundersFC.com |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> The relationship between former head coaches [[Bruce Arena]] and Sigi Schmid, the two most successful in league history, also played a factor in the clubs' rivalry.<ref>{{cite news |last=Parker |first=Graham |date=July 7, 2016 |title=Bruce Arena and Sigi Schmid molded modern MLS coaching landscape |url=http://www.espnfc.us/major-league-soccer/19/blog/post/2908188/bruce-arena-and-sigi-schmid-molded-modern-mls-coaching-landscape |work=ESPN FC |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> Schmid later served as the Galaxy's head coach from 2017 to 2018, shortly after his departure from the Sounders.<ref>{{cite news |last=Baker |first=Geoff |date=July 29, 2017 |title=Sigi Schmid returns to pitch, coaches Galaxy to scoreless draw against Sounders |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/sigi-schmid-returns-to-pitch-coaches-galaxy-to-scoreless-draw-against-sounders/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=September 19, 2018}}</ref>

==Ownership and team management==

The ownership group of the club is composed of three owners and an ownership group composed of 14 families. The majority owner is [[Adrian Hanauer]], the former owner of the now defunct USL-1 team Seattle Sounders. Independent minority owners include the estate of [[Paul Allen]], Microsoft co-founder and owner of the Seattle Seahawks and Portland Trail Blazers, owned by [[Jody Allen]]; and [[Drew Carey]], comedian and game show host.<ref name=ownership>{{cite news |title=Carey signs on to invest in Seattle expansion team |date=November 12, 2007 |publisher=ESPN |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/news/story?id=481717&cc=5901 |accessdate=November 13, 2007}}</ref> Hanauer and his mother Lenore Hanauer also became minority owners of Tacoma-based [[Reign FC]] in the [[National Women's Soccer League]] in January 2019, though there was no affiliation between the two clubs;<ref>{{cite news |last=Hammond |first=Andrew |date=January 30, 2019 |title=Seattle Reign moving to Cheney Stadium, and the S2 team is changing its name |url=https://www.thenewstribune.com/sports/soccer/article225264695.html |work=The News Tribune |accessdate=March 18, 2019}}</ref> their involvement with that club ended when [[Olympique Lyonnais|OL Groupe]] purchased majority ownership of it the following year.<ref>{{cite press release |title=Reign FC Has Entered into Exclusive Negotiations to Sell the Club to OL Groupe |url=https://www.reignfc.com/news/2019/11/25/reign-fc-has-entered-into-exclusive-negotiations-to-sell-the-club-to-ol-groupe |accessdate=November 25, 2019 |publisher=Reign FC |date=November 25, 2019}}</ref> Paul Allen's partnership allowed for the team to share certain resources with the Seahawks, including over half of the team's full-time staff, with merged ticket, marketing, and financial operations.<ref name="McIntyre May 18, 2012">{{cite news | url=http://espn.go.com/sports/soccer/story/_/id/7929299/soccer-seattle-sounders-prove-mls-put-fans-seats-espn-magazine | title=Reigning in Seattle | work=ESPN The Magazine | date=May 16, 2012 | accessdate=May 18, 2012 | author=McIntyre, Doug}}</ref> This arrangement ended on April 30, 2014, with the Sounders becoming a fully independent business operation.<ref>{{cite news |last=Mayers |first=Joshua |date=April 29, 2014 |title=Sounders FC nearly completes independent business transition from Seahawks |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2023489016_sounders30xml.html |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>

[[Joe Roth]] was the majority owner from 2007 to 2015, when he ceded the role to Hanauer, who was replaced as general manager earlier that year.<ref>{{cite news|first=Matt|last=Pentz |title=Adrian Hanauer is the new majority owner of the Seattle Sounders|url=http://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/adrian-hanauer-is-the-new-majority-owner-of-the-seattle-sounders/ |newspaper=The Seattle Times|date=November 12, 2015|accessdate=November 12, 2015}}</ref> Roth departed the club's ownership group in 2019.<ref name="Ownership2019">{{cite press release |date=August 13, 2019 |title=Sounders FC opens new chapter in club history, as 11 families with deep Seattle roots join Rave Green Ownership group |url=https://www.soundersfc.com/post/2019/08/13/eleven-families-with-seattle-roots-join-sounders-fc-ownership-group |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=August 13, 2019}}</ref> He was replaced by Seattle Futbol Club LLC, formed by 11 families with ties to the Seattle area including former [[Microsoft]] executive [[Terry Myerson]] and his wife Katie Myerson, [[Russell Wilson]], [[Ciara]], [[Macklemore]] and Tricia Davis, [[Satya Nadella]] and Anu Nadella, [[Amy Hood]] and Max Kleinman, [[Joe Belfiore]] and Kristina Belfiore, [[S. Somasegar]] and Akila Somasegar, Chee Chew and Christine Chew, David Nathanson and Sabina Nathanson, [[Brian McAndrews]] and Elise Holschuh, and Mark Agne and Tomoko Agne.<ref name="Ownership2019"/><ref name=":0">{{cite news |last=Evans |first=Jayda |date=August 13, 2019 |title=Russell Wilson, Ciara, Macklemore, Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and more join Sounders ownership |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/russell-wilson-ciara-macklemore-microsoft-execs-and-more-join-sounders-ownership-group/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=August 13, 2019}}</ref> Myerson arranged for the investment through his connections and negotiations that took 15 months to complete. The expanded ownership group will have an executive board to decide on club matters.<ref name=":0" /> Peter Tomozawa and Donna LeDuc joined the new minority ownership group shortly afterwards, with Tomozawa taking over as president of business operations.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Evans|first=Jayda|date=August 21, 2019|title=Sounders announce Peter Tomozawa as president of business operations|work=The Seattle Times|url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/sounders-announce-peter-tomozawa-as-president-of-business-operations/|url-status=live|access-date=September 2, 2020}}</ref> In October 2020, [[Seattle Kraken]] team president [[Tod Leiweke]] and his wife, Tara, were announced as additional part-owners of the team.<ref>{{cite news |last=Bakers |first=Geoff |date=October 5, 2020 |title=Kraken CEO Tod Leiweke and his wife, Tara, join Sounders ownership group |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/kraken-ceo-tod-leiweke-and-his-wife-tara-join-sounders-ownership-group/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=October 6, 2020}}</ref> Retired baseball player [[Ken Griffey Jr.]] and his wife, Melissa, also joined the ownership group the following month.<ref>{{cite news |last=Evans |first=Jayda |date=November 17, 2020 |title=Mariners legend Ken Griffey Jr. to join Seattle Sounders ownership group |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/mariners-legend-ken-griffey-jr-to-join-seattle-sounders-ownership-group/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=November 17, 2020}}</ref>

[[Brian Schmetzer]] is the Sounders' current head coach. He was promoted to interim head coach from his role as assistant coach after [[Sigi Schmid]] parted ways with the club on mutual terms in July 2016.<ref name="SigiJul2016"/> Schmetzer was made permanent head coach in November 2016, following the team's qualification for that year's playoffs.<ref>{{cite news |last=Milles |first=Todd |date=November 2, 2016 |title=Sounders waste no more time, make Schmetzer permanent head coach |url=https://www.thenewstribune.com/sports/soccer/mls/sounders-fc/article112220157.html |work=The News Tribune |accessdate=September 19, 2018}}</ref> The club's assistant coaches are [[Gonzalo Pineda]], a retired Liga MX and MLS player,<ref>{{cite news |last=Pentz |first=Matt |date=January 20, 2017 |title=Former Sounders midfielder Gonzalo Pineda rejoins club as assistant coach |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/former-sounders-midfielder-gonzalo-pineda-rejoins-club-as-assistant-coach/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=September 25, 2019}}</ref> and [[Djimi Traoré]], a retired Sounders player who played for [[Liverpool F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news |last=Pentz |first=Matt |date=March 28, 2016 |title=Djimi Traore makes seamless transition to Sounders' coaching staff |url=http://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/djimi-traore-makes-seamless-transition-to-sounders-coaching-staff/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> Tom Dutra was hired in 2008 as the club's goalkeeper coach.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.soundersfc.com/News/Articles/2008/12-December/Tom-Dutra-Named-Goalkeeper-Coach.aspx |title=Tom Dutra Named Goalkeeper Coach |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |date=December 29, 2008 |accessdate=November 11, 2009}}</ref><ref name="Staff">{{cite web |title=Technical Staff |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/team/coaches |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=July 26, 2016|date=February 15, 2014}}</ref>

[[Garth Lagerwey]] was hired from [[Real Salt Lake]] in January 2015 as the club's [[general manager]] and President of Soccer, replacing Adrian Hanauer.<ref name="Lagerwey"/> Former MLS player and [[Everett, Washington]] native [[Chris Henderson]] was named technical director on January 24, 2009.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.soundersfc.com/News/Articles/2008/01-January/MLS-Seattle-Hires-Henderson-as-Technical-Director.aspx |title=Seattle Hires Henderson as Technical Director |publisher=Sounders FC |date=January 24, 2008 |accessdate=February 18, 2012}}</ref> Former Sounders defender [[Taylor Graham]] was hired as the club's Vice President of Business Operations in 2012,<ref>{{cite news |last=Mayers |first=Joshua |date=March 27, 2012 |title=Furlough day (Tuesday links) |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/soundersfcblog/2017848882_furlough_day_tuesday_links_1.html |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref> replacing longtime Seattle Seahawks executive Gary Wright.<ref>{{cite news|title=Longtime Seahawks VP moves to MLS team |date=March 24, 2008 |url=http://www.bizjournals.com/seattle/stories/2008/03/24/daily5.html |newspaper=Puget Sound Business Journal |accessdate=March 30, 2008}}</ref>

SportsBusiness Journal and SportsBusiness Daily recognized Seattle Sounders FC as the Professional Sports Team of the Year in 2009 because of the team's record-setting success in attendance, as well as making the playoffs in its inaugural season.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.bizjournals.com/seattle/blog/2010/05/sounders_named_pro_sports_team_of_the_year.html |title=Sounders named pro sports team of the year |first=George
 |last=Erb |date=May 20, 2010 |newspaper=Puget Sound Business Journal |accessdate=February 6, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/News/Articles/2010/05-May/Sounders-FC-win-Sports-Business-Journals-team-of-the-year.aspx |title=Seattle Sounders FC recognized as the 2010 professional sports team of the year |date=May 20, 2010 |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=February 6, 2011}}</ref> Former Seahawks and Sounders CEO Tod Leiweke was recognized by the Puget Sound Business Journal as the newspaper's 2009 Executive of the Year.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.bizjournals.com/seattle/stories/2009/11/30/daily19.html |title=Leiweke named PSBJ Executive of the Year |first=George |last=Erb |date=December 2, 2009 |newspaper=Puget Sound Business Journal |accessdate=February 6, 2011}}</ref> Gary Wright was named MLS Executive of the Year in 2009.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/othersports/2017250076_sportsstar16.html |title=Sounders' Gary Wright named Seattle sports executive of year |first=Joshua |last=Mayers |date=January 15, 2012 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=January 30, 2012}}</ref> In 2012, he was named Seattle Sports Star Executive of the Year.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/othersports/2017336085_sportsstar26.html |title=Keller, Polk, Vandersloot win Seattle Sports Star of Year awards |first=Bob |last=Condotta |date=January 25, 2012 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=January 30, 2012}}</ref>

A 2015 study by [[Forbes]] ranked the Sounders number one in the league in terms of annual revenues ($50&nbsp;million) and operating income ($10&nbsp;million). Consequently, the Sounders were also ranked as the most valuable franchise ($245&nbsp;million) in MLS&nbsp;– a 717% increase over the expansion fee it paid to join the league.<ref name="forbes.com">{{cite news |last=Smith |first=Chris |date=August 19, 2015 |title=Major League Soccer's Most Valuable Teams |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/chrissmith/2015/08/19/major-league-soccers-most-valuable-teams-2015-2/ |work=Forbes |accessdate=August 22, 2015}}</ref> The financial success of the team is driven in large part by their high attendance figures and local popularity.<ref name="forbes.com"/>

===Affiliated teams===
{{main|Seattle Sounders FC U-23|Seattle Sounders Women}}

In January 2012, the Sounders announced a partnership for the Tacoma Tide, an amateur team in the [[USL Premier Development League]], to rebrand as [[Seattle Sounders FC U-23]] and appoint [[Darren Sawatzky]] as head coach.<ref>{{cite news |date=January 25, 2013 |title=Sounders FC Aligns With U-23 PDL Franchise |url=https://www.soundersfc.com/post/2012/01/25/sounders-fc-aligns-u-23-pdl-franchise |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=November 26, 2017}}</ref> This team would field former academy players during their summers off from university teams.<ref>{{cite news |last=Billings |first=Jon |date=June 12, 2014 |title=Jordan Morris returns, active for Sounders U-23 |url=http://blog.thenewstribune.com/soccer/2014/06/12/jordan-morris-returns-to-town-active-for-sounders-u-23/ |work=The News Tribune |accessdate=November 26, 2017 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170630074911/http://blog.thenewstribune.com/soccer/2014/06/12/jordan-morris-returns-to-town-active-for-sounders-u-23/ |archivedate=June 30, 2017  }}</ref> This branding agreement was terminated by the Sounders in January 2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sounders U23 to re-brand |url=http://soundersu23.com/Team/NewsItem/4263 |accessdate=13 January 2020 |publisher=Seattle Sounders U-23 |date=11 January 2020}}</ref>

The Sounders were also affiliated with an amateur women's team, [[Seattle Sounders Women]], that plays at Starfire Sports Complex.<ref>{{cite news |last=Tesfatsion |first=Master |date=August 9, 2012 |title=U.S. goes for Olympic gold as women's pro soccer fails to catch on at home |page=C1 |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/olympics/us-goes-for-olympic-gold-as-womens-pro-soccer-fails-to-catch-on-at-home/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=November 26, 2017}}</ref> This branding agreement was also terminated by the Sounders in January 2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sounders Women to re-brand |url=http://www.sounderswomen.com/Team/NewsItem/3220 |accessdate=13 January 2020 |publisher=Sounders Women |date=11 January 2020}}</ref>

==Broadcasting==
Since 2014, television rights in English for regionally-broadcast matches are held by [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] [[owned-and-operated station]] [[KCPQ]] and sister station [[KZJO]] within the Seattle market. English television broadcasts are called by [[Keith Costigan]], who works alongside former Sounders goalkeeper [[Kasey Keller]] for color commentary and former Sounders midfielder [[Steve Zakuani]] for analysis.<ref>{{cite web |title=Television Broadcasts |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/matchday/broadcasting-network/television |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=July 26, 2016|date=February 28, 2014 }}</ref> Previously, matches aired on [[KING-TV]] and [[KONG (TV)|KONG]] from 2009 to 2013.<ref>{{cite news |last=Clark |first=Dave |date=December 9, 2013 |title=Sounders moving local broadcasts to Fox's Q13 |url=https://www.sounderatheart.com/2013/12/9/5194346/sounders-moving-local-broadcasts-to-foxs-q13 |work=Sounder at Heart |publisher=SB Nation |accessdate=February 20, 2019}}</ref> Matches are televised in Spanish on [[Univision]] station [[KUNS-TV|KUNS]], anchored by Jaime Mendez with analysts Diego Arrioja and [[Jhon Kennedy Hurtado]].<ref>{{cite press release |date=February 10, 2016 |title=Sounders FC announces new broadcast talent, regional broadcast details |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/post/2016/02/10/sounders-fc-announces-new-broadcast-talent-regional-broadcast-details |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Broadcast19">{{cite press release |date=February 20, 2019 |title=Sounders FC announces complete regional broadcast lineup for 2019 |url=https://www.soundersfc.com/post/2019/02/20/sounders-fc-announces-complete-regional-broadcast-lineup-2019 |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=February 20, 2019}}</ref> In 2018, the team renewed its agreements with KCPQ and KZJO; in addition, the team announced a new streaming deal with [[YouTube TV]] for exclusive rights to stream the team's matches within Washington state. This replaces a previous syndication deal with [[Root Sports Northwest]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/digital/news/youtube-tv-seattle-sounders-exclusive-rights-1202713547/|title=YouTube TV Nabs Exclusive Streaming Rights to Seattle Sounders Games, Its Second MLS Deal|last=Spangler|first=Todd|date=February 28, 2018|work=Variety|access-date=March 25, 2018}}</ref> In 2020, the team switched its streaming rights to [[Prime Video]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.engadget.com/amazon-prime-mls-seattle-sounders-mls-170009304.html|title=Amazon Prime will stream select Sounders matches|last=Holt|first=Kris|date=August 18, 2020|work=Engadget|access-date=August 18, 2020}}</ref>

On radio, Sounders matches are called in English on [[KJR (AM)|950 KJR AM]] by Matt Johnson with analysis from [[Wade Webber]], [[Danny Jackson]], and [[Pete Fewing]]. The station also airs a weekly show dedicated to the Sounders on Tuesdays.<ref name="Broadcast19"/> [[KKMO]] (El Rey 1360 AM) broadcasts matches and a weekly show in Spanish with commentary from Mario Rodriguez, Carlos Tapia, and Felipe Maqueda.<ref name="Broadcast19"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Radio Programming |url=http://www.soundersfc.com/matchday/broadcasting-network/radio |publisher=Seattle Sounders FC |accessdate=July 26, 2016|date=February 14, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Pentz |first=Matt |date=February 10, 2016 |title=Seattle Sounders unveil new play-by-play announcers, release regional broadcast details |url=http://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/seattle-sounders-unveil-new-play-by-play-announcers-release-regional-broadcast-details/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>

Former [[Seattle SuperSonics]] announcer [[Kevin Calabro]] and former U.S. soccer star [[Greg Vanney]] called the play-by-play for the local broadcasts during the Sounders' inaugural season in 2009.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sounders find voice: Kevin Calabro |date=July 22, 2008 |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/jerrybrewer/2008065310_brewer22.html |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 22, 2008 | first=Jerry | last=Brewer}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/sounders/2009/04/27/games_through_june_28_sold_out.html |title=Games through June 28 sold out, and if Sounders FC wins tomorrow |accessdate=November 6, 2009 |last=Romero |first=José Miguel |date=April 27, 2009 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090501144542/http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/sounders/2009/04/27/games_through_june_28_sold_out.html |archivedate=May 1, 2009  }}</ref> However, they were replaced by former [[BBC]] cricket and general sport commentator [[Arlo White]] for the 2010 and 2011 seasons, who called English language broadcasts without a partner.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2011027342_sounders10.html |title=BBC vet Arlo White, 36, to succeed Kevin Calabro as the Sounders play-by-play broadcaster |accessdate=February 10, 2010|last=Romero |first= José Miguel |date=February 9, 2010 |newspaper=The Seattle Times}}</ref> In 2012, White was hired by [[NBC Sports Network]] to be the voice of their soccer coverage. That led to BBC commentator [[Ross Fletcher]] becoming the club's television and radio play-by-play commentator beginning with the 2012 season, working alongside Kasey Keller as the color commentator.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://old.seattletimes.com/html/sounders/2017202849_sounders11.html |title=Sounders hire announcers Ross Fletcher, Kasey Keller |first=Joshua |last=Mayers |date=January 10, 2012 |newspaper=The Seattle Times |accessdate=January 21, 2012}}</ref> Fletcher left the club at the end of the 2015 season and was replaced by Keith Costigan.<ref>{{cite news |last=Pentz |first=Matt |date=October 22, 2015 |title=Seattle Sounders broadcaster Ross Fletcher to leave club at the end of this season |url=http://www.seattletimes.com/sports/sounders/seattle-sounders-broadcaster-ross-fletcher-to-leave-the-club-at-the-end-of-this-season/ |work=The Seattle Times |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Oshan |first=Jeremiah |date=February 12, 2016 |title=Trying to understand the frustration around Sounders new announcers |url=http://www.sounderatheart.com/2016/2/12/10976692/trying-to-understand-the-frustration-around-sounders-new-announcers |work=Sounder at Heart |publisher=SB Nation |accessdate=July 26, 2016}}</ref>
