'''Jennifer Leigh''' (born August 10, 1983 in [[Wilmington, Delaware]]) is an [[United States|American]] professional [[poker]] player.

Leigh built up from playing $5 SNG [[online poker]] tournaments to playing $100/$200 [[betting (poker)|limit]] [[hold'em]] cash games. She also cashed in the $1,000 limit hold'em event of the [[World Championship of Online Poker]].

Leigh's nickname "Jennicide" was her alias on [[Hacker (computer security)|hacking]] [[bulletin board]]s when she was younger. She was at one time involved in [[beige box (phreaking)|beige boxing]].<ref>[http://video.cardplayer.com:8080/video/mp3/thecircuit_022206.mp3 The Circuit]{{Dead link|date=February 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> (Leigh has described herself as a [[nerd]], with knowledge of [[Linux]] and [[C Sharp (programming language)|C#]] programming.)

Whilst Leigh is best known for playing online poker at [[PokerStars]], she was also involved in the ''[[Poker Royale]]: Battle of the Ages'' series. She won two preliminary heats and went on to finish 5th in the Grand Final. She  finished [[poker jargon#I|in the money]] in the [[World Poker Tour]] (WPT) 2006 [[Caribbean Poker Adventure]].

Leigh has blogged about reaching level 70 on ''[[World of Warcraft]]'', previously played the [[MMORPG]] ''[[EverQuest]]'', and is now actively playing ''[[Aion: The Tower of Eternity|Aion]]''.

Leigh has also posed for ''[[FHM]] Online'' in 2007,<ref>[http://www.fhmonline.com/articles-4386.asp?cnl_id=5&stn_id=46&idx_id=4386 "Jennicide's Shoot with FHM"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070504021007/http://www.fhmonline.com/articles-4386.asp?cnl_id=5&stn_id=46&idx_id=4386 |date=May 4, 2007 }}, FHMonline.com, May 2, 2007</ref> and appeared in the May 2008 issue of ''[[Playboy]]''.

As of 2008, her live tournament winnings exceed $61,000.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pokerdb.thehendonmob.com/player.php?a=r&n=54733 |title=Jennifer Leigh: Hendon Mob Poker Database |work=The Hendon Mob Poker Database |accessdate=January 23, 2015}}</ref>
