'''White Rabbits''' is an American six-piece [[indie rock]] band based in [[Brooklyn|Brooklyn, New York]], originally from [[Columbia, Missouri]]. The band released its debut studio album, ''[[Fort Nightly]]'', on May 22, 2007. Their second album, ''[[It's Frightening]]'', was released on May 19, 2009. They released their third album, ''[[Milk Famous]]'', on March 6, 2012. The band is signed to [[TBD Records]].<ref name="WhiteRabbitsTBD">{{cite web|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2008/03/white-rabbits-join-radioheads-label-schedule-many.html|title=White Rabbits Join Radiohead's Label, Schedule Many Dates|last=Ray|first=Austin|date=March 11, 2008|publisher=Pastemagazine.com|accessdate=2009-02-17}}</ref>

==History==
The band formed in Columbia, Missouri. The band's frontmen, Greg Roberts and Stephen Patterson, met at the college in 2004.  Alex Even, [[Adam Russell (musician)|Adam Russell]] and Greg had formerly played in the band ''Texas Chainsaw Mass Choir''. The original White Rabbits lineup consisted of five members, but after relocating to New York City the next year, the band expanded to six members. The sixth member, Jamie Levinson, had grown up on the same street as Roberts in [[Webster Groves, Missouri]]. Roberts and Levinson both played in a ska band (called The Hubcaps) while in high school.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012|reason=Reliable source needed for full paragraph}}

The White Rabbits recorded its first studio album, ''Fort Nightly'', and released it in 2007 to the public through [[Say Hey Records]]. The album was praised by critics.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/artists/whiterabbits/fortnightly |title=Reviews for Fort Nightly by White Rabbits |publisher=Metacritic |date=2007-05-22 |accessdate=2014-06-30}}</ref>

In addition to consistent touring, they have been featured on ''[[NPR]]'s World Cafe'', where the band members discussed how they met and formed in the college town of [[Columbia, Missouri]]. They also talked about their early sound and performed three songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=14239777 |title=White Rabbits: 'Honky-Tonk Calypso' : World Cafe |publisher=NPR |date= |accessdate=2014-06-30}}</ref> They have also performed on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]''.<ref>[http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/download/44383-white-rabbits-the-plot-live-on-late-show-with-david-letterman ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080107084145/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/download/44383-white-rabbits-the-plot-live-on-late-show-with-david-letterman |date=January 7, 2008 }}</ref>

On May 12, 2009, the band released their second album, titled ''[[It's Frightening]]''. The album was produced by [[Britt Daniel]], lead singer and guitarist of [[Spoon (band)|Spoon]] (whom they asked after meeting the band while on tour in Minneapolis).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/149124-spoons-britt-daniel-working-with-jon-brion|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090215184050/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/149124-spoons-britt-daniel-working-with-jon-brion|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 15, 2009|title=Spoon's Britt Daniel Working With Jon Brion &#124; Pitchfork|date=February 15, 2009}}</ref> Retrieved on February 16, 2009<ref>{{cite web|url=http://inflatableferret.com/interviews/white-rabbits-interview-the-inflatable-ferrets-first-interview/ |title=White Rabbits Interview (Taken from the 1st Issue) |publisher=Inflatable Ferret |date= |accessdate=2012-04-01}}</ref> The album was released on [[TBD Records]], which also hosts the bands [[Radiohead]] and [[Underworld (band)|Underworld]].<ref name="WhiteRabbitsTBD"/> The band also worked with Nicholas Vernhes at the Rare Book Room.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nthword.com/issue2/white_rabbits2.php |title=Adjust your Thinking. White Rabbits |publisher=nthWord |date= |accessdate=2014-06-30}}</ref>

Their third album, titled ''Milk Famous'', was released on March 6, 2012. The band entered a hiatus following the end of the tour in support of the album in December 2012. They reunited for a one-off show at Brooklyn's Cameo Gallery on June 15, 2014.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020|reason=Reliable source needed for beginning of paragraph}} Stephen Patterson and Greg Roberts played drums and bass guitar, respectively, in the touring band of former [[The Walkmen]] frontman [[Hamilton Leithauser]] in 2016 and 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://clrvynt.com/hamilton-leithauser-interview/ |title=Avoiding the Rock Fantasy Vacation with Hamilton Leithauser |publisher=CLRVYNT |date=2016-11-28 |accessdate=2020-04-21}}</ref> with Patterson playing drums again for Leithauser in January 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/hamilton-leithauser-previewed-new-solo-lp-covered-big-thief-more-cafe-carlyle-residency-pics/ |title=Hamilton Leithauser previewed new solo LP, covered Big Thief & more @ Cafe Carlyle residency (pics) |publisher=Brooklyn Vegan |date=2020-01-15 |accessdate=2020-04-21}}</ref>

==Live shows==
White Rabbits are known for their energy onstage, their switching of instruments, and [[Cover version|cover song]]s, notably [[Bob Dylan]]'s "[[Maggie's Farm]]", which was also covered by one of the band's main influences, [[The Specials]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.qromag.com/reviews/concert_reviews/white_rabbits_live/ |title=QRO Magazine - Concert Reviews Â» White Rabbits : Live |publisher=Qromag.com |date=2007-06-04 |accessdate=2012-04-01}}</ref> Typically they will put their own twist on cover songs by playing them in a style/genre that is different from the original.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.daytrotter.com/daytrotterSessions/891/free-songs-white-rabbits|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070914142408/http://www.daytrotter.com/daytrotterSessions/891/free-songs-white-rabbits|url-status=dead|archive-date=September 14, 2007|title=Daytrotter &#124; Free Songs: White Rabbits|date=September 14, 2007}}</ref> After playing a particularly energetic show at ''South By Southwest'' in 2008, ''[[NME]]'' named them one of the top three bands performing that year.

They have toured with bands such as [[The Walkmen]], [[Richard Swift (musician)|Richard Swift]], [[Interpol (band)|Interpol]] and [[Spoon (band)|Spoon]]. In 2007, they began a national tour with the [[Kaiser Chiefs]] in support of their album ''[[Fort Nightly]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://prod1.cmj.com/articles/display_article.php?id=44547359|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080105011736/http://prod1.cmj.com/articles/display_article.php?id=44547359|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 5, 2008|date=January 5, 2008}}</ref> This followed a tour through the United Kingdom.  In 2010 the band went on tour with [[Interpol (band)|Interpol]].

White Rabbits also performed at the 2010 [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]] on Saturday, April 17, 2010 on the main stage.  The following day, Stephen Patterson, along with [[Deerhunter (band)|Deerhunter]] frontman [[Bradford Cox]], joined [[Spoon (band)|Spoon]] during their set for "Who Makes Your Money."<ref>{{cite web|last=Ross |first=Dalton |url=http://music-mix.ew.com/2010/04/19/coachella-gorillaz-yorke-pavement/ |title=Coachella 2010 Sunday: Gorillaz, Pavement, and Thom Yorke close out the festival on high notes &#124; The Music Mix &#124; EW.com |publisher=Music-mix.ew.com |date=2010-04-19 |accessdate=2012-04-01}}</ref>
 
White Rabbits played at the 2010 [[Austin City Limits Music Festival]] and the 2010 Voodoo Music Experience in New Orleans Halloween weekend, and opened for [[Muse (band)|Muse]] at London's Wembley Stadium on September 10, 2010. On October 29, 2010 they were the opening act for Interpol at the Verizon Wireless Theatre in Houston, TX.

They played on the BBC show ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'', where they performed "Percussion Gun" and "They Done Wrong/We Done Wrong".

In 2012, they toured with [[The Shins]].
