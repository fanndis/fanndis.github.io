'''Dayana Oleksandrívna Yastremska''' ({{lang-uk|Даяна Олександрівна Ястремська}}; born 15 May 2000) is a Ukrainian professional [[tennis]] player. She has a career-high [[Women's Tennis Association]] (WTA) ranking of No. 21 in the world, and is the second-highest ranked player under 21 years old behind [[Bianca Andreescu]]. Yastremska has won three WTA titles in four finals appearances.

A junior Grand Slam runner-up in both singles and doubles, Yastremska had a quick breakthrough onto the WTA Tour. She made her debut in the top 100 and won two titles when she was 18 years old, including her first at the [[2018 Hong Kong Tennis Open|Hong Kong Tennis Open]] in 2018. She had a successful 2019 that helped her rise from No. 58 at the start of the year up to No. 22 by the end of the season.

Yastremska is one of the most aggressive players on the WTA Tour and has led the tour in highest percentage of shots that end in a winner, an unforced error, or an opponent's forced error.

==Early life and background==
Dayana Yastremska was born on 15 May 2000 to Marina and Alexander Yastremsky in [[Odesa]], the third-largest city in Ukraine.<ref name=wta-profile/><ref name=mom-english/> She has a sister Ivanna who is six years younger.<ref name="sister">{{cite web |last1=Ubha |first1=Ravi |title=Adelaide finalist Dayana Yastremska and Sascha Bajin are dreaming big |url=https://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2020/01/dayana-yastremska-sascha-bajin-wta-adelaide/86885/ |website=Tennis.com |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 16 January 2020}}</ref> Her father had been a volleyball player and also has served on the Odesa City Council. When Yastremska was five years old, her grandfather Ivan introduced her to tennis. After trying other sports such as gymnastics and swimming, she chose to focus on tennis, saying at the age of twelve, "I chose tennis because it is very hard and beautiful. I love work and I want to write my new history in tennis." She entered her first tournament when she was seven and finished in third place, despite the event being open to children who were several years older.<ref name="bio">{{cite web |title=Хто така Даяна Ястремська: історія 18-річної зірки українського тенісу |url=https://24tv.ua/sport/dayana_yastremska_biografiya_foto_tenisistki_ukrayini_yastremskoyi_shho_vidomo_n1049368 |website=24tv.ua |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 3 February 2019 | trans-title = Who is Diana Yastremskaya: history of 18-year-old Ukrainian tennis star | language = Ukrainian | first = Igor | last = Sadienitsa}}</ref> Yastremska credits her parents for her success at tennis, saying, "When I was younger I had to sacrifice a lot of things but now I’m not regretting it... I have to say a big thank you to my parents because if they didn’t push me in the right moment then I don’t think I’d have the life I have right now."<ref name=sister/>

==Junior career==
[[File:Dayana Yastremska Junior Orange Bowl.png|thumb|left|upright=0.8|Yastremska at the 2012 Junior Orange Bowl]]
Yastremska had early success as a junior, finishing runner-up at the 12-and-under [[Junior Orange Bowl (tennis)|Junior Orange Bowl]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Colette |title=Americans Mmoh and Desiatnikov Win Junior Orange Bowl Titles; Lumsden and Oliel Give Great Britain and Israel Historic Wins |url=http://tenniskalamazoo.blogspot.com/2012/12/americans-mmoh-and-desiatnikov-win.html |website=ZooTennis.com |accessdate=18 January 2020 | date = 23 December 2012}}</ref> She made her debut on the [[ITF Junior Circuit]] in March 2014 at the age of 13. Towards the end of the year, she won her first title at a Grade 4 event in November. With this success, she moved up to playing higher-level tournaments more regularly. Yastremska reached a Grade 1 semifinal in July in Austria and won a Grade 2 title in August in Hungary. She made her debut at the highest-level Grade A tournaments in October, reaching the quarterfinals at the [[Osaka Mayor's Cup]] in Japan. She fared better in doubles, finishing runner-up to two Japanese players.<ref name="itf-junior-singles">{{cite web |title=Dayana Yastremska Junior Singles Activity |url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/dayana-yastremska/800357289/ukr/jt/s/activity |website=ITF World Tennis Tour |accessdate=18 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="itf-junior-doubles">{{cite web |title=Dayana Yastremska Junior Doubles Activity |url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/dayana-yastremska/800357289/ukr/jt/d/activity |website=ITF World Tennis Tour |accessdate=18 January 2020}}</ref>

Yastremska made her junior Grand Slam debut at the [[2016 Australian Open]], where she made the singles quarterfinals.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Colette |title=Three Australians Reach Semifinals in AO Junior Championships; Leustian, Ong Advance to Les Petits As Quarterfinals; Kingsley, Tiafoe Reach Quarterfinals in Maui $50Ks |url=http://tenniskalamazoo.blogspot.com/2016/01/three-australians-reach-semifinals-in.html |website=ZooTennis.com |accessdate=18 January 2020 | date = 28 January 2016}}</ref> In doubles, she made it to the final, losing to [[Anna Kalinskaya]] and [[Tereza Mihalíková]] alongside compatriot [[Anastasia Zarycká]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Colette |title=Lapko, Anderson Win Australian Open Junior Titles; Leustian Reaches Les Petits As Final; Kingsley Faces McHale in Maui $50K Final; Midland Qualifying Draw Out |url=http://tenniskalamazoo.blogspot.com/2016/01/lapko-anderson-win-australian-open.html |website=ZooTennis.com |accessdate=18 January 2020 | date = 30 January 2016}}</ref> Yastremska only played Grade A tournaments the rest of the year. At the [[Copa Gerdau]], she lost in the singles semifinals to [[Amanda Anisimova]]. In the doubles event, she partnered with [[Panna Udvardy]] to win the only Grade A title of her career, defeating the American team of [[Caty McNally]] and [[Natasha Subhash]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Colette |title=Arconada Faces Anisimova in all-US Porto Alegre Grade A Final, McNally Plays for Boys Title; Paulus Wins Grade 4 in Puerto Rico; Stewart and Min in Orlando $10K Final |url=http://tenniskalamazoo.blogspot.com/2016/03/arconada-faces-anisimova-in-all-us.html |website=ZooTennis.com |accessdate=18 January 2020 | date =19 March 2016}}</ref> After a third round appearance in singles at the [[2016 French Open]],<ref name="itf-junior-singles"/> Yastremska concluded her junior career at [[2016 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]]. At her last event, she made her only junior Grand Slam singles final. After upsetting top seed [[Olesya Pervushina]] in the semifinals,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Colette |title=Potapova and Yastremska Advance to Saturday's Girls Final at Wimbledon; De Minaur and Shapovalov Reach Boys Championship Match; Three US Girls in Doubles Semifinals |url=http://tenniskalamazoo.blogspot.com/2016/07/potapova-and-yastremska-advance-to.html |website=ZooTennis.com |accessdate=18 January 2020 | date = 8 July 2016}}</ref> she finished runner-up to [[Anastasia Potapova]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Colette |title=Anastasia Potapova Claims Wimbledon Girls Title in Unusual Finish; Americans Arconada, Liu and McNally Reach Girls Doubles Final |url=http://tenniskalamazoo.blogspot.com/2016/07/anastasia-potapova-claims-wimbledon.html |website=ZooTennis.com |accessdate=18 January 2020 | date = 9 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| title=Anastasia Potapova wins dream Wimbledon title | url=https://www.itftennis.com/juniors/news/articles/anastasia-potapova-wins-dream-wimbledon-title.aspx | last=Harwitt | first=Sandra | website=ITF Tennis | date = 9 July 2016 | accessdate = 29 July 2019 | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20190729140905/https://www.itftennis.com/juniors/news/articles/anastasia-potapova-wins-dream-wimbledon-title.aspx | url-status = dead | archivedate = 29 July 2019}}</ref> This helped her reach a career-high junior ranking of {{abbr|No.|world number}} 6 in the world.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dayana Yastremska Junior Singles Overview |url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/dayana-yastremska/800357289/ukr/jt/s/overview |website=ITF World Tennis Tour |accessdate=18 January 2020}}</ref>

===2018: Maiden WTA title and top 100 debut at 18===
Yastremska had a slow start to the 2018 season. She lost in the second round of qualifying at the [[2018 Australian Open|Australian Open]]. After injuring her ankle at the [[2018 Abierto Mexicano Telcel|Abierto Mexicano]] in February,<ref>{{cite web |title=Hanging on the Dream |url=https://abiertomexicanodetenis.com/en/2018/02/27/hanging-on-the-dream/ |website=Abierto Mexicano de Tennis |accessdate=1 March 2020 | date = 27 February 2018}}</ref> she did not enter any tournaments in March. With a ranking well outside of the top 100, she needed to enter qualifying at her first seven tournaments of the year, reaching three main draws.<ref name=itf-singles/> At the last of these events, Yastremska produced her best result, finishing runner-up to [[Rebecca Peterson]] at the $100K [[2018 Open de Cagnes-sur-Mer|Open de Cagnes-sur-Mer]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Petersons stora succé – är nu bäst i Sverige |url=https://www.expressen.se/sport/tennis/petersons-stora-succe-ar-nu-bast-i-sverige/ |website=Expressen |accessdate=19 January 2020 | language = Swedish | date = 13 May 2018 |first= Linus | last = Sunnervik| trans-title = Peterson's great success - is now the best in Sweden}}</ref> This result took her into the top 150 for the first time.<ref name=wta-rankings/> She also reached the $100K final at the [[2018 Fuzion 100 Ilkley Trophy|Ilkley Trophy]], losing to [[Tereza Smitková]] in a third-set tiebreak.<ref>{{cite web |title=Fuzion 100 Ilkley Trophy: Smitkova and Stakhovsky crowned singles champions |url=https://www.lta.org.uk/major-tennis-events/trophy-series/ilkley-trophy/news/2018/fuzion-100-ilkley-trophy-smitkova-and-stakhovsky-crowned-singles-champions/# |website=Lawn Tennis Association |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 24 June 2018}}</ref> She did not enter the [[2018 French Open|French Open]] and lost in the qualifying competition at [[2018 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]].<ref name=itf-singles/> During the second week of Wimbledon, Yastremska won the separate $60K [[2018 Torneo Internazionale Femminile Antico Tiro a Volo|Torneo Internazionale Femminile Antico Tiro a Volo]] in Rome. She defeated Potapova in the final in 45 minutes, only losing one game.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.tennisworldusa.org/tennis/news/Rising_Stars/57510/dayana-yastremska-thumps-anastasia-potapova-in-rome-for-the-third-pro-title/| title=Dayana Yastremska thumps Anastasia Potapova in Rome for the third pro title | website=Tennis World USA | accessdate=25 November 2018 | first = Jovaca | last = Ilic | date = 9 July 2018}}</ref> With this title, she made her debut in the top 100 at the age of 18.<ref name=wta-rankings/><ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/ukrainian-teen-yastremska-first-new-millennium-wta-top-100 | title=Ukrainian teen Yastremska first of new millennium into WTA Top 100 | last = Kane | first = David | website=WTA Tennis | date = 17 July 2018 | accessdate = 17 July 2018}}</ref>

In the second half of the season, Yastremska had more success at the WTA level. She qualified for the Premier-level [[2018 Connecticut Open|Connecticut Open]], where she upset No. 36 [[Danielle Collins]] for her only main draw match win.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fornabaio |first1=Michael |title=Connecticut Open: Yastremska making name for self, tops final American in draw |url=https://www.nhregister.com/sports/article/Connecticut-Open-Yastremska-making-name-for-13169827.php |website=New Haven Register |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 20 August 2018}}</ref> The following week, she made her Grand Slam main draw debut and was upset in her opening round match by qualifier [[Karolína Muchová]].<ref name="wta-matches">{{cite web |title=Dayana Yastremska Matches |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/players/324261/dayana-yastremska/matches |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 January 2020}}</ref> Yastremska made her breakthrough on the WTA Tour in her last two tournaments of the year. In the second half of the season, Yastremska produced another good performance at a Grand Slam at the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|US Open]], losing in the third round to compatriot and world No. 5 [[Elina Svitolina]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kapetanakis |first1=Arthur |title=Svitolina dominates Yastremska in Ukrainian showdown at 2019 US Open |url=https://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2019-08-30/2019-08-30_svitolina_dominates_yastremska_in_ukrainian_showdown_at_2019_us_open.html |website=US Open |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 30 August 2019}}</ref> She then achieved her best Premier 5 result of the year, a quarterfinal at the [[2019 Wuhan Open|Wuhan Open]]. They upset top seeds [[Hsieh Su-wei]] and [[Barbora Strýcová]] in their second match before finishing runner-up to Kenin and [[Bethanie Mattek-Sands]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Carroll |first1=Dave |title=Hsieh, Chan sisters tumble out of the China Open |url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/sport/archives/2019/10/03/2003723311 |website=Taipei Times |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date= 3 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Kenin and Mattek-Sands lift China Open doubles crown |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1485654/kenin-and-mattek-sands-lift-china-open-doubles-crown |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 6 October 2019}}</ref> At the end of season, Yastremska unexpectedly qualified for the [[2019 WTA Elite Trophy|WTA Elite Trophy]], the second-tier year-end championships, after several higher-ranked players withdrew from consideration. She was placed in a group with No. 20 [[Donna Vekić]] and No. 10 [[Kiki Bertens]]. After both her and Bertens defeated Vekić in their opening matches,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Macpherson |first1=Alex |title='I just don't put pressure on myself' - Yastremska strikes out Vekic in superb Zhuhai debut |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1485358/-i-just-don-t-put-pressure-on-myself-yastremska-strikes-out-vekic-in-superb-zhuhai-debut |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 23 October 2019}}</ref> Yastremska lost to Bertens and did not advance out of her group.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Macpherson |first1=Alex |title=Just really proud and happy' - Bertens withstands Yastremska to make Zhuhai semifinals |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1485337/-just-really-proud-and-happy-bertens-withstands-yastremska-to-make-zhuhai-semifinals |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 24 October 2019}}</ref> She finished the season at a career-high of No. 22 in the world.<ref name=wta-rankings/>

===2020: First Premier final===
Yastremska had a strong start to the 2020 season. She reached the final at the Premier-level [[2020 Adelaide International|Adelaide International]]. During the event, she defeated three top 20 players, including No. 12 Aryna Sabalenka, before finishing runner-up to world No. 1 [[Ashleigh Barty]], who won the title in her home country.<ref>{{cite web |title=Days before Australian Open, world No. 1 Barty wins Adelaide title |url=https://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2020/01/top-ranked-barty-wins-1st-title-at-home-ahead-of-aussie-open/86911/ |website=Tennis.com |accessdate=19 January 2020| date=18 January 2020 | agency = Associated Press}}</ref>  In the [[2020 Australian Open - Women's Singles|Australian Open]] she beat [[Kaja Juvan]] in the first round before falling to [[Caroline Wozniacki]] in the second, the Dane's final tournament victory before her retirement.

[[Ukraine Fed Cup team|Ukraine]] played their [[2020 Fed Cup Europe/Africa Zone Group I – Pool A (Tallinn)|Fed Cup]] Group I tournament in [[Tallinn|Estonia]], where Yastremska was victorious in all three of her singles matches.  She then lost in the first round in [[2020 Dubai Tennis Championships - Women's Singles|Dubai]] to [[Veronika Kudermetova]] before falling to [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the third round in [[2020 Qatar Total Open - Singles|Doha]].  This was her last match before international tennis was suspended due to the COVID-19 coronavirus.

==National representation==
Yastremska made her debut for [[Ukraine Fed Cup team]] at the [[2019 Fed Cup]]. The team was playing a tie against the host country [[Poland Fed Cup team|Poland]] in a [[2019 Fed Cup Europe/Africa Zone Group I – Play-offs#5th place play-off|third-place play-off for Europe/Africa Zone Group I]]. Although Yastremska won the second singles rubber against [[Iga Świątek]], Ukraine lost the tie after failing to win either the first singles rubber or the decisive doubles rubber, the latter of which ended in a third-set tiebreak.<ref>{{cite web |title=Group I Europe/Africa Zone A 3rd to 4th Play-off Final |url=https://www.fedcup.com/en/draws-results/tie.aspx?id=W-FC-2019-G1-EPA-A-PP3-4-POL-UKR-01 |website=Fed Cup |accessdate=19 January 2020}}</ref>

==Playing style==
Yastremska's coach [[Sascha Bajin]] described her playing style as, "She’s a great mover and she has incredible power. Her ground strokes are very powerful."<ref name=sister/> Yastremska has an aggressive attacking style and can hit high numbers of winners. She had 33 winners in the finals when she won her first two title matches, and 49 winners when she won her third title. She also can hit a high number of unforced errors while going for winners, totaling 21, 53, and 50 such errors in those three matches.<ref name=first-title/><ref name=second-title/><ref name=third-title/> As of the 2019 Australian Open, Yastremska was ranked as the most aggressive player on the WTA Tour, having led the tour with 28.6 per cent of her shots ending in a winner, an unforced error, or an opponent's forced error, a statistic known as her Aggression Score.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Sackmann |first1=Jeff |title=Dayana Yastremska Hits Harder Than You ==Coaches==
Yastremska's parents have both been a part of her coaching team,<ref name="neva-cup-interview">{{cite web |title=Dayana Yastremska: "If we play with my sister, we will be better than Serena and Venus" |url=http://nevacup.formulatx.com/en/2017/09/22/dayana-yastremskaya-esli-budem-igrat-paru-s-sestroj-budem-luchshe-chem-serena-i-vinus/ |website=Neva Cup |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 22 September 2017}}</ref> with her mother continuing to serve as her mental coach.<ref name=mom-english/> When Yastremska was a junior, she had a variety of different coaches. She worked with former top 100 player [[Viktoriya Kutuzova]] and Kutuzova's father Valery. She later worked with another former top 100 player in [[Jean-René Lisnard]] and then former world No. 2 [[Magnus Norman]].<ref name=bio/> As a professional in 2017, she has trained in Istanbul with Gavin Hopper, a former coach of [[Monica Seles]].<ref name="kane-2018">{{cite web |last1=Kane |first1=David |date= 19 December 2017 |title=2018 Scouting Report: Why young gun Dayana Yastremska is ready for primetime |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1414761/2018-scouting-report-why-young-gun-dayana-yastremska-is-ready-for-primetime |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 January 2020}}</ref> Yastremska later began training at the Justine Henin Academy, with [[Justine Henin|Henin]] becoming her consultant in 2018. She worked with one of the academy's coaches, Oliver Jeunehomme, until September 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dayana Yastremska splits with coach Oliver Jeunehomme |url=https://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2019/09/dayana-yastremska-splits-coach-oliver-jeunehomme-justine-henin-academy/85212/ |website=Tennis.com |accessdate=19 January 2020 | first= Kamakshi | last = Tandon | date = 28 September 2019}}</ref> In the offseason, she hired Sascha Bajin as her new coach.<ref>{{cite web |title=Yastremska announces Bajin as new coach for 2020 |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1498182/yastremska-announces-bajin-as-new-coach-for-2020 |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 January 2020 | date = 21 November 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
At the [[2019 Australian Open]], Yastremska's mother suffered an eye injury after a champagne bottle that she was holding exploded unexpectedly. She had immediate surgery to save her eye, which was coordinated in part by [[Elina Svitolina]]'s agent Stefan Gurov and financially covered by tournament director Craig Tiley. Yastremska dedicated her title at the [[2019 Hua Hin Championships|Hua Hin Championships]] the following month to her mother as a result of this incident.<ref name=mom-english/>

During the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] in 2020, Yastremska began a music career. She released her first single, Thousands of Me, in May, and a dance song, "Favourite Track", in August.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kvitova back on court, Yastremska's debut single |url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1668167/kvitova-back-on-court-yastremska-s-debut-single |website=WTA Tennis |accessdate=19 September 2020 | date = 13 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Savage |first1=Nic |title=Tennis: Dayana Yastremska becomes a star away from the court in the music studio |url=https://www.news.com.au/sport/sports-life/tennis-dayana-yastremska-becomes-a-star-away-from-the-court-in-the-music-studio/news-story/eca9449f2adfe96c4a1eb4bb55dd4815 |website=news.com.au |accessdate=19 September 2020 | date = 2 August 2020}}</ref>
