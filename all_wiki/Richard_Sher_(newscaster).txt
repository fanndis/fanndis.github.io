{{short description|American television journalist}}
'''Richard Sher''' is a longtime newscaster in [[Baltimore, Maryland]], who spent most of his career at [[WJZ-TV]].

== Early life ==

Richard Sher was born in 1941 to Michael and Lillian Sher at the Women's Hospital in [[Bolton Hill, Baltimore|Bolton Hill]].  He grew up in Northwest Baltimore and attended the [[St. Paul's School (Brooklandville, Maryland)|St. Paul's School]] in Baltimore County, where he was "one of the few Jews."<ref>Gabrielle Souza, "Getting Rich," ''Baltimore Magazine'' July, 2015 [http://www.baltimoremagazine.com/2015/7/8/richard-sher-hosts-game-show-at-horseshoe-casino Richard Sher Hosts Gameshow]</ref>  Sher received his [[undergraduate degree]] in 1963 and his [[Master of Arts]] degree in [[Communication]]s in 1965 from the [[University of Maryland, College Park]].

== Broadcasting career ==

Before arriving at WJZ, Sher was news director for WBAL-FM Radio (WBAL-FM "News 98" became [[WIYY]] / 98 ''Rock'' in 1975), where he established an all news format for the [[radio broadcasting|station]]. He has also worked in [[San Francisco]] and [[Washington, D.C.]].

Sher joined WJZ in 1975. From 1988 through February 1995, he co-anchored the weekend [[newscast]]s, and he was then the co-anchor of Eyewitness News at Five until January 1998. He also hosted a local talk show, ''People Are Talking'', with [[Oprah Winfrey]] in the beginning of her career.

Sher played a [[News presenter|broadcaster]] in a [[Cameo appearance|cameo role]] in the 2006 [[Robin Williams]] movie ''[[Man of the Year (2006 film)|Man of the Year]]''.

Sher retired from WJZ on November 24, 2008, after 33 years there.  His retirement announcement was pre-empted by his friend and former colleague Winfrey, who introduced him to her audience and informed them of Sher's impending retirement.<ref>Michael Olesker, "Richard Sher says Goodbye to WJZ on Oprah," ''Washington Examiner'', November 26, 2008 [http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/richard-sher-says-goodbye-to-wjz-on-oprah/article/33762 Richard Sher says goodbye to WJZ on Oprah]</ref>

== After WJZ ==

After retiring from WJZ, Sher's presence on the Baltimore airwaves endured. His Sunday morning public affairs forum ''Square Off'', which he had hosted for many years on WJZ, continued on [[WMAR-TV]].

In 2015, Sher began hosting a [[game show]] at the [[Horseshoe Casino Baltimore|Horseshoe Casino]] in Baltimore.  The show was named ''People Are Winning'', as "a nod to ''People Are Talking'', the long-running morning show that Sher hosted on WJZ-TV."<ref>Richard Gorelick, "Richard Sher to host live game show at Horseshoe Casino," ''Baltimore Sun'', April 2, 2015 [http://www.baltimoresun.com/features/baltimore-insider-blog/bal-richard-sher-horseshoe-20150402-story.html Richard Sher to host live game show at Horseshoe Casino]</ref>  He volunteers weekly at Maryland's [[R Adams Cowley Shock Trauma Center]].<ref>Gabrielle Souza, "Getting Rich," ''Baltimore Magazine'' July, 2015 [http://www.baltimoremagazine.com/2015/7/8/richard-sher-hosts-game-show-at-horseshoe-casino Richard Sher Hosts Gameshow]</ref><ref>Michael Olesker, "Happy Birthday, Richard Sher" ''JMore: Baltimore Jewish Living'', November 16, 2016 [https://www.jmoreliving.com/2016/11/16/happy-birthday-richard-sher/ Happy Birthday Richard Sher]</ref>
