A [[Mass shootings in the United States|mass shooting]] took place on November 7, 2018, in [[Thousand Oaks, California]], United States, at the Borderline Bar and Grill, a country-western bar frequented by college students.<ref name="VCStar">{{cite news |title=Thousand Oaks shooting: 13, including officer, killed at Borderline Bar & Grill |author=Staff |date=November 8, 2018 |newspaper=[[Ventura County Star]] |url=https://www.vcstar.com/story/news/2018/11/07/multiple-victims-reported-shooting-t-o-bar/1927732002/ |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref> Thirteen people were killed, including the perpetrator, who died of a self-inflicted gunshot wound,<ref name=bbc>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-46150847|title=Thousand Oaks: Las Vegas shooting survivor among dead |date=November 9, 2018 |accessdate=November 9, 2018 |publisher=[[BBC]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/gunman-killed-12-died-self-inflicted-gunshot-021507782.html |title=Gunman who killed 12 died from self-inflicted gunshot |date=November 10, 2018 |accessdate=November 10, 2018 |agency=Associated Press |first=Jonathan J. |last=Cooper}}</ref> and a police officer who was shot multiple times, with the fatal wound accidentally being fired by another officer.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vcstar.com/story/news/2019/05/07/shooting-investigation-borderline-bar-ventura-county-sheriffs-office/3641969002/|title='Unprecedented in scope and size': Nothing prepared detectives for Borderline shooting|last=Wilson|first=Kathleen|date=May 7, 2019|newspaper=[[Ventura County Star]]|language=en|access-date=2019-05-08}}</ref> One other person sustained a gunshot wound, while fifteen others were injured by incidental causes.<ref name=FBI/>

Police identified the killer as 28-year-old Ian David Long, a [[United States Marine Corps]] veteran.<ref name="BBC">{{cite news |title=Thousand Oaks: Ex-Marine Ian David Long identified as suspect |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-46135459 |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=November 8, 2018 |accessdate=November 8, 2018}}</ref><ref name="marine0" />

== Events ==
At 11:18{{nbsp}}p.m., a gunman, later identified as Ian David Long, entered the Borderline Bar and Grill and opened fire on the approximately 175 patrons and employees inside.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads>{{cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/local/california/la-me-borderline-shooting-heroism-20181231-htmlstory.html|title=Must Reads: Friends took this photo at their favorite bar in Thousand Oaks. Minutes later, the shooting started|publisher=The Los Angeles Times|last1=Karlamangla|first1=Soumya|last2=Mejia|first2=Brittny|date=December 31, 2018|accessdate=November 8, 2020}}</ref> At the time, the bar was hosting a regularly scheduled ''College Country Night'' event, and it was popular among students in the area, especially those from [[Pepperdine University]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Borderline Bar & Grill - CALENDAR |url=https://www.borderlinebarandgrill.com/calendar |website=California {{!}} Borderline Bar &amp; Grill |access-date=November 8, 2018 |date=November 8, 2018 |quote=College Country Night! Learn to Dance w/Lessons at 9:30 & 10:30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |first=Scott |last=Berson |url=https://www.sacbee.com/latest-news/article221340460.html |title=At least 13 dead in bar shooting, niece of Emmy-winning actress among the victims |newspaper=[[The Sacramento Bee]] |date=November 8, 2018 |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.ndtv.com/world-news/several-injured-in-shooting-at-a-bar-in-southern-california-in-us-say-police-1944358 |title=12 Killed In California Bar Shooting, Gunman Dead |date=November 8, 2018 |publisher=[[NDTV]] |location=New Delhi, India |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref> [[California Lutheran University]], [[California State University Channel Islands]],<ref name=CNN.Shooting>{{Cite news |publisher=[[CNN]] |url=https://www.cnn.com/us/live-news/california-shooting-intl/index.html |title=12 dead in California bar shooting |date=November 8, 2018 |access-date=November 8, 2018 |first=Lauren |last=Said-Moorhouse |first2=Rob |last2=Picheta |first3=Veronica |last3=Rocha |first4=Meg |last4=Wagner |first5=Jessie |last5=Yeung}}</ref> and [[Moorpark College]].<ref name="VCStar" /> Long was armed with a legally purchased [[.45 ACP|.45-caliber]] [[Glock 21SF|Glock 21]] semi-automatic pistol with a [[laser sight]]<ref name="auto"/> and seven [[Gun laws in California#Summary table|banned]] [[High-capacity magazine ban|high-capacity magazines]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.npr.org/2018/11/28/671353612/no-motive-yet-found-for-mass-shooting-at-borderline-bar-and-grill |title=Authorities Describe 'Confusion And Chaos' At Borderline Bar Shooting In California |publisher=[[NPR]] |date=November 28, 2018 |first=Ina |last=Jaffe |accessdate=December 8, 2018}}</ref> carrying a total of 210 rounds, along with a folding knife.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/>

Upon entering through the bar's front door, Long first killed the cashier nearby, then started shooting at the patrons.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/> He fired a total of approximately 60 rounds<ref name="auto"/> and threw [[smoke bomb]]s.<ref name="VCStar" /><ref name="NBC_News" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/world/twelve-killed-after-gunman-opens-fire-in-california-bar/ar-BBPt98U |title=Twelve killed after gunman opens fire in California bar |date=November 8, 2018 |publisher=[[Sky News]] |ref=harv}}</ref> Many of the victims died in the first few minutes of the shooting while they were lying on the floor or trying to charge at Long.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/> Witnesses described the gunman as a heavily tattooed white male dressed entirely in black.<ref name="NBC_News" /> Some people shattered the bar's windows, allowing many to flee, while some others hid in an employee bathroom or the attic.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/> During the shooting, Long answered a phone call made by the mother of a patron who escaped, and he also made several posts on [[Instagram]] expressing his thoughts.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/><ref>{{cite news |title=What Thousand Oaks shooting suspect posted to social media during massacre |publisher=[[KABC-TV]] |location=Los Angeles, California |url=https://abc7chicago.com/thousand-oaks-gunman-posted-to-social-media-during-attack/4656647/|date=November 9, 2018|archive-date=2018-11-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181111133714/https://abc7chicago.com/thousand-oaks-gunman-posted-to-social-media-during-attack/4656647/ |access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref>

Three minutes after the first [[9-1-1]] calls,<ref name="Mercury Shooting calls">{{cite web |last1=Gafni |first1=Mathias |title="We’re making entry": Listen to police encounter Thousand Oaks nightclub shooter |url=https://www.mercurynews.com/2018/11/08/were-making-entry-listen-to-police-encounter-thousand-oaks-nightclub-shooter/ |website=Mercury News |accessdate=24 January 2019}}</ref><ref name="Thousand Oaks Shooting CNBC">{{cite web |last1=Srivastava |first1=Spriha |title=Mass shooting at a bar in Thousand Oaks, California, kills 12; shooter also confirmed dead |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/11/08/multiple-injuries-reported-at-a-shooting-in-thousand-oaks-california-authorities-say.html |website=CNBC |access-date=25 January 2019}}</ref> [[Ventura County Sheriff's Office|Ventura County Sheriff]]'s Sgt. Ron Helus and two [[California Highway Patrol]] (CHP) officers arrived at the scene.<ref name="NBC_News" /> Hearing gunshots coming from the building, Helus and one of the CHP officers ran inside at 11:26{{nbsp}}p.m. and immediately came under fire. In the ensuing gunfight, Helus was shot five times by Long, who used a flashlight with a laser sight on his pistol in the large, darkened, smoke-filled room.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.charlotteobserver.com/news/nation-world/national/article222792655.html |title=In darkness and chaos, deputy killed by friendly fire |newspaper=[[The Charlotte Observer]] |first=Amanda Lee |last=Myers |agency=Associated Press |date=December 7, 2018 |access-date=December 8, 2018}}</ref> Positioned between Long and the CHP officer, Helus was also accidentally struck by a bullet from the officer's rifle that went through his heart and fatally wounded him.<ref name="auto">{{cite news |url=https://www.latimes.com/local/california/la-me-ln-borderline-update-20181207-story.html |title=CHP officer's bullet killed sheriff's deputy who responded to Thousand Oaks bar shooting |last1=Karlamangla |first1=Soumya |last2=Fry |first2=Hannah |date=December 7, 2018 |access-date=December 7, 2018 |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref> Moments later responding [[Ventura County Sheriff's Office|Ventura County Sheriff]]'s deputies, who were securing the perimeter, located and evacuated Helus to safety outside. A [[SWAT]] team and other police officers arrived on the scene shortly afterwards.<ref name="NBC_News" />

Long stopped shooting victims following the exchange of gunfire with police. At 11:39{{nbsp}}p.m., he committed suicide in the bar's office.<ref name=LATimes.MustReads/> Agents from the [[Federal Bureau of Investigation]], the [[Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives]], and the [[Department of Homeland Security]] were deployed to further investigate the scene along with [[EMTs]] and [[paramedics]] from the [[Ventura County Fire Department]] and [[American Medical Response|AMR]] to assist victims.<ref name="VCStar" /><ref name="LA Times 1">{{cite news |url=https://www.latimes.com/local/lanow/la-me-ln-thousand-oaks-20181107-story.html |title=Thousand Oaks shooting leaves 13 people dead, including gunman, and 18 injured |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |date=November 8, 2018 |first=Sean |last=Greene |first2=Alene |last2=Tchekmedyian |first3=Brittny |last3=Mejia |first4=Sarah |last4=Parvini |first5=James |last5=Queally |first6=Richard |last6=Winton |first7=Jaura J. |last7=Nelson |first8=Hannah |last8=Fry |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref>

== Perpetrator ==
[[Ventura County Sheriff's Office|Ventura County Sheriff]] Geoff Dean identified the gunman as 28-year-old Ian David Long, a resident of nearby [[Newbury Park, California|Newbury Park]].<ref name="marine1">{{cite news |first=Jose A. |last=Del Real |first2=Gerry |last2=Mullany |first3=Russell |last3=Goldman |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/11/08/us/shooting-california-thousand-oaks.html|title=California Shooting Kills 12 at Country Music Bar, a Year After Las Vegas |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |date=November 8, 2018 |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref> His gun was reportedly purchased [[Gun law in the United States|legally]].<ref name="marine2">{{Cite news |url=https://abc7.com/thousand-oaks-shooter-used-45-caliber-handgun-with-extended-magazine/4646579/ |title=Thousand Oaks shooter used .45-caliber handgun w/ extended clip |date=November 8, 2018 |publisher=[[KABC-TV]] |location=Los Angeles, California |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://abc13.com/4855337/|title=Sergeant in California mass shooting killed by friendly fire|work=ABC13 Houston| first=Carlos |last=Granda |date=December 7, 2018|access-date=8 December 2018}}</ref>

Long served in the [[United States Marine Corps]] from August 2008 to March 2013, and had gone to [[Afghanistan]] from November 2010 to June 2011. After being discharged, he attended [[California State University, Northridge]] as an [[athletic training]] major from 2013 to 2016, but he did not graduate.<ref name=CNN.Gunman/> While he was previously living with [[roommates]] in [[Reseda, Los Angeles|Reseda]], Long was living with his mother at the time of the shooting.<ref>{{Cite news |first1=Jennifer |last1=Medina |first2=Serge F. |last2=Kovaleski |first3=Matthew |last3=Haag |url=https://abc11.com/thousand-oaks-shooting-suspect-what-we-know/4645948/ |title=What we know about Thousand Oaks shooting suspect, Ian David Long |publisher=[[WTVD]] |location=Durham, North Carolina |date=November 8, 2018 |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref><ref name="LAT Netburn">{{cite news |url=http://www.latimes.com/science/sciencenow/la-sci-sn-ptsd-mass-shootings-20181110-story.html |title=The role of PTSD in mass shootings: Let's separate myth from reality |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |first=Deborah |last=Netburn |date=November 10, 2018 |access-date=November 12, 2018}}</ref> A cousin told the media that Long's father had died from cancer early in his life.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2018/11/08/california-shooter-ian-david-long-was-weird-loner-danced-in-garage/|title=California shooter Ian David Long was ‘weird’ loner, danced in garage|first=Lia|last=Eustachewich|date=November 8, 2018}}</ref> His friends say that Long had been at the bar with them and some considered him a regular patron.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/11/08/us/thousand-oaks-gunman/index.html|title=Thousand Oaks gunman was a Marine veteran who often visited the site of the shooting|author=Eric Levenson, Jose Pagliery and Majlie de Puy Kamp|website=CNN}}</ref>

Police and a mental health crisis team visited Long in 2017 for his irate and irrational behavior, but they decided not to detain him at a psychiatric facility.<ref name=CNN.Gunman>{{cite news |publisher=[[CNN]] |date=November 8, 2018 |first=Eric |last=Levenson |first2=Jose |last2=Pagliery |first3=Majlie de Puy |last3=Kamp |title=Thousand Oaks gunman was a Marine veteran who often visited the site of the shooting |accessdate=December 9, 2018 |url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/11/08/us/thousand-oaks-gunman/index.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |work=[[USA Today]] |date=November 8, 2018 |first=Chris |last=Woodyard |first2=Marco |last2=Della Cava |title=What we know about Thousand Oaks gunman Ian David Long, a Marine veteran |accessdate=December 9, 2018 |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2018/11/08/ian-long-gunman-thousand-oaks-california-borderline-bar-grill/1928231002/}}</ref> A high school teacher raised claims that Long had physically assaulted her as a student but was encouraged not to push the incident so as to not endanger his future in the Marine Corps. In her statement, the teacher alleged that Long had issues long before his military service.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://losangeles.cbslocal.com/2018/11/08/thousand-oaks-gunman-high-school-coach-assault/ |title=Thousand Oaks Gunman's High School Coach Speaks About Sexual Assault |date=November 8, 2018 |publisher=[[KCAL-TV]] |location=Los Angeles, California |access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref> Although he had served overseas in the military, behavioral scientist and clinical psychologist Lisa Jaycox said that it was premature to say whether Long suffered from [[posttraumatic stress disorder]] or if it was a factor in the shooting.<ref name="LAT Netburn"/><ref name="marine0">{{Cite news |first=Caroline |last=Burke |url=https://heavy.com/news/2018/11/ian-david-long-military-background/ |title=Ian David Long's Military Background: The Shooter Was a Former&nbsp;Marine |publisher=[[Heavy.com]] |date=November 8, 2018 |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |first1=Jennifer |last1=Medina |first2=Dave |last2=Philipps |first3=Serge F. |last3=Kovaleski |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/11/08/us/ian-david-long-california-shooter.html |title=Dueling Images: A Smiling Young Marine and a Killer Dressed in Black |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |date=November 8, 2018 |access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref>

== Aftermath ==
Within 24 hours of the shooting, the [[Woolsey Fire]] forced the evacuation of many people in the area, including several survivors.<ref name="NPR 2018/12/15">{{cite news |url=https://www.npr.org/2018/12/15/676521424/after-the-thousand-oaks-shooting-a-community-wide-effort-to-memorialize-and-heal |title=After The Thousand Oaks' Shooting, A Community-Wide Effort To Memorialize And Heal |first=Ina |last=Jaffe |date=December 15, 2018 |publisher=[[NPR]] |access-date=December 17, 2018}}</ref> The mother of a victim who died in the Thousand Oaks shooting after surviving the [[2017 Las Vegas shooting]] called for gun control legislation from legislators despite the high-capacity magazine used by the gunman already being made illegal in California.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://amp.usatoday.com/amp/1928201002|title=Court blocked law that would have made possession of magazine in Thousand Oaks illegal|publisher=USA Today|last=Cummings|first=William|date=November 11, 2018|accessdate=November 8, 2020}}</ref><ref name="KCLU Mother">{{cite news |url=http://www.kclu.org/post/mother-one-borderline-bar-and-grill-victims-turns-pain-anti-gun-activism |title=Mother Of One Of The Borderline Bar And Grill Victims Turns Pain Into Anti-Gun Activism |first=Lance |last=Orozco |date=November 30, 2018 |publisher=[[KCLU-FM]] |location=Thousand Oaks, California |access-date=December 18, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.kron4.com/news/california/mom-of-thousand-oaks-shooting-victim-i-don-t-want-thoughts-i-want-gun-control-/1584289930 |title=Mom of Thousand Oaks shooting victim: 'I don't want thoughts. I want gun control' |agency=Associated Press |date=November 9, 2018 |publisher=[[KRON-TV]] |location=San Francisco, California |access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2018/11/09/thousand-oaks-shooting-mother-pleads-want-gun-control/1940769002/ |title=Thousand Oaks shooting victim's mother pleads, 'I don't want thoughts. I want gun control' |last=Pitofsky |first=Marina |date=November 9, 2018 |work=[[USA Today]] |access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref> The only gun store in the town told reporters that there was an increase in individuals looking to purchase a weapon for protection on the day after the shooting.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2018/11/09/thousand-oaks-following-ian-long-shooting-borderline-bar-and-grill-fearful-residents-buy-guns/1947476002/ |title=At the only gun shop in Thousand Oaks, fearful residents decide it's time to buy a gun |last=Garcia-Roberts |first=Gus |date=November 9, 2018 |work=[[USA Today]] |access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref>

On November 15, 2018, the funeral for Helus was held. Law enforcement officers, local and state leaders including California Governor [[Jerry Brown]] and Governor-Elect [[Gavin Newsom]], as well as local citizens attended his funeral.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://people.com/crime/funeral-ventura-sheriff-sgt-killed-thousand-oaks-shooting/ |title=First Responder Was Killed in Restaurant Mass Shooting at Same Place Where He Proposed to His Wife |website=[[People (magazine)|People]] |date=November 15, 2018 |first=Elaine |last=Aradillas |access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref> A vigil at Calvary Community Church in nearby [[Westlake Village]], for two of their congregation, was attended by around 500 mourners.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation-now/2018/11/09/ventura-hill-woolsey-fire-california-thousand-oaks-shooting/1949030002/ |title='I just wonder what's next': Thousand Oaks residents deal with mass shooting, horrific fire |last=Kisken |first=Tom |date=November 9, 2018 |work=[[USA Today]] |access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref>

Numerous national sports teams highlighted the victims and donated money to those affected by the shooting.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/2018/11/11/rams-andrew-whitworth-donates-game-check-thousand-oaks-shooting-victims-families/ |title=Rams' Andrew Whitworth donates game check to Thousand Oaks shooting victims, families |last=Boren |first=Cindy |date=November 11, 2018 |website=[[The Washington Post]]}}</ref> Victims were provided with these donations immediately after the shooting. Each victim's family was provided with $20,000 for burial costs.<ref name="LAT $3.6 million" /> After a third distribution in March 2019, a total of $3.6 million had been contributed by more than 25,000 people and through more than 200 fundraisers. The funds were distributed to the families of victims, those physically injured, and others present in the establishment.<ref name="LAT $3.6 million ">{{Cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/local/lanow/la-me-ln-borderline-shooting-donations-20190326-story.html|title=$3.6 million in donations distributed to Borderline shooting survivors|last=Reyes-Velarde|first=Alejandra|date=March 27, 2019|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|access-date=27 March 2019}}</ref> A local foundation received and distributed the funds in a cooperative effort without charging any administrative fees.<ref name="VCStar $3.6 million">{{Cite news|url=https://www.vcstar.com/story/news/2019/03/23/3-6-million-sent-families-survivors-borderline-shooting/3248440002/|title=$3.6 million sent to families, survivors in Borderline shooting|last=Wilson|first=Kathleen|date=March 23, 2019|newspaper=[[Ventura County Star]]|language=en|access-date=March 27, 2019}}</ref>
[[File:Susan Orfanos attends 2020 State of the Union, Washington, D.C. 05.jpg|thumb|Susan Orfanos, whose son was killed in the shooting, meets with [[Julia Brownley]] and [[Dianne Feinstein]] about gun violence in 2020.]]
The Ventura county Board of Supervisors formed the Task Force on Mental Health & Safety in response to the shooting. Initially composed of key county officials, the ongoing task force looks for gaps in the systems that protect the public and that provide mental health care and makes recommendations on how to prevent and lessen the damage from mass shootings.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vcstar.com/story/news/2019/03/23/ventura-county-mental-health-task-force-focus-shooting-prevention/3203354002/|title=Task force begins tackling ways to prevent mass shootings|last=Wilson|first=Kathleen|date=March 27, 2019|newspaper=[[Ventura County Star]]|language=en|access-date=March 27, 2019}}</ref>

A memorial was planned be constructed in a community park.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vcstar.com/story/news/local/communities/conejo-valley/2019/06/13/thousand-oaks-borderline-shooting-memorial-to-be-built-conejo-creek-park-north/1445220001/|title=Conejo Creek Park North selected as site for permanent Borderline Memorial|last=Harris|first=Mike|date=June 13, 2019|newspaper=[[Ventura County Star]]|language=en|access-date=2019-06-14}}</ref>  The "Healing Garden" memorial at Conejo Creek Park was dedicated a year after the massacre.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.malibutimes.com/malibu_life/article_d636ce38-39b7-11ea-8aae-f7df41bc664e.html|title=Memorial Garden Opens in Thousand Oaks on Borderline Shooting Anniversary|last=Bravo|first=Samantha|date=November 19, 2019|newspaper=[[The Malibu Times]]|language=en|access-date=2020-10-31}}</ref>

The owners of Borderline Bar & Grill have stated that they plan to renovate and re-open the location.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vcstar.com/story/news/local/2019/11/28/borderline-bar-grill-owners-announce-plans-open-new-location/4327443002/|title=Borderline Bar & Grill owners announce plans to reopen T.O. venue, site of mass shooting|first1=Cheri|last1=Carlson|first2=Mike|last2=Harris|website=Ventura County Star}}</ref> They have also opened a second location in [[Agoura Hills, California]] called BL Dancehall & Saloon.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://abc7.com/5875621/|title=Borderline Bar and Grill opens new location in Agoura Hills|date=January 25, 2020|website=ABC7 Los Angeles}}</ref>
