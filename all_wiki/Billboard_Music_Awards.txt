The '''''Billboard'' Music Awards''' are honors given out annually by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', a publication and [[Billboard charts|music popularity chart]] covering the music business. The ''Billboard'' Music Awards show had been held annually since 1990 and the event was formerly held in December<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/bbma/7386047/top-billboard-music-award-winners-all-time-1990-2016 |title=Top Billboard Music Award Winners of All Time (1990–2016) |last=Caulfield |first=Keith |date=March 31, 2016 |work=Billboard |access-date=April 10, 2017}}</ref> until it went dormant in 2006. The awards returned in 2011 and are now held annually in May. <ref>{{Cite web|title= Who Should Perform at the 2020 Billboard Music Awards? Vote! |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9434787/2020-bbmas-performers-poll|work= Billboard|access-date= 11 August 2020}}</ref>

==Awards process==
Unlike other awards, such as the [[Grammy Award]], which determine nominations as a result of the highest votes received by the [[National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences]], the ''Billboard'' Music Awards finalists are based on album and digital songs sales, streaming, radio airplay, touring, and social engagement. These measurements are tracked year-round by ''Billboard'' and its data partners, including [[Nielsen SoundScan|
Nielsen Music]] and [[Next Big Sound]]. The 2018 awards are based on the reporting period of April 8, 2017 through March 31, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/bbma/8343598/billboard-music-awards-2018-nominations-list |title=Billboard Music Awards 2018 Nominations: See the Full List |date=April 17, 2018 |work=Billboard |access-date=April 17, 2018}}</ref> Awards are given for the top album, artist and single in a number of different [[music genres]].

==Categories==
From 1989 to 2006, the show had the same categories and category names every year. In 2011, for the first time, all of the awards were renamed to "Top [award title]". The "of the year" portion of each category title no longer exists, and many of the awards have been further renamed. Other awards, including both "crossover" awards (No. 1 Classical Crossover Artist and No. 1 Classical Crossover Album) were discontinued. As of 2017, there are two fan-voted categories.

===Current categories===
The general categories are Top Artist, Top Billboard 200 Album, Top Hot 100 Song and Top New Artist. These categories highlighted in each award and other categories are divided by genre.

====Spotlight Award====
In 1988, [[Michael Jackson]] was honored with ''Billboard''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s first [[Billboard Music Award for Spotlight|Spotlight Award]] for being the first artist in music history to have five consecutive number ones singles on [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] from [[Bad (album)|one album]]. This happened before the Awards were televised. In 2012, [[Katy Perry]] was honored with ''Billboard''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s second Spotlight award for being the second and first female artist in music history to have five consecutive number ones singles on ''Billboard'' Hot 100 from [[Teenage Dream (Katy Perry album)|one album]].

==Broadcast==
Since its inception (created by Rick Garson, Paul Flattery & Jim Yukich), the BMAs had been telecast on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] network; however due to contractual expirations and other unforeseen circumstances, the awards were cancelled for 2007. Plans for a new version of the awards in 2008 (in association with [[AEG Live]]) fell through, and the BMAs were not held until 2011.

On February 17, 2011, ''Billboard'' announced that it would bring the BMAs back to television, moving from its original home on Fox to its new network, [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]], on May 22, 2011.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/industry/tv-film/billboard-music-awards-to-air-live-from-1005040022.story|title=Billboard Music Awards To Air Live From Las Vegas On ABC May 22|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-12-02|language=en}}</ref> A new award statuette was created by New York firm [[Society Awards]]. [[Dick Clark Productions]], which is co-owned with ''Billboard'', began producing the ceremony in 2014.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/6084674/the-billboard-music-awards-keep-bubbling|title=The Billboard Music Awards Keep Bubbling|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-12-02|language=en}}</ref> On November 28, 2017, it was announced that the ''Billboard'' Music Awards would be moving from ABC to [[NBC]] beginning in 2018 under a multi-year contract.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2017/music/news/billboard-music-awards-nbc-abc-1202624885/|title=Billboard Music Awards Move From ABC to NBC in Multi-Year Deal|last=Otterson|first=Joe|date=2017-11-28|work=Variety|access-date=2017-12-02|language=en-US}}</ref>

The 2020 ceremony, originally scheduled for April 29, was postponed indefinitely on March 17 due to [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on television|coronavirus-related]] public assembly concerns.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/billboard-music-awards-be-rescheduled-1284816|title=Billboard Music Awards to Be Rescheduled|last=Nordyke|first=Kimberly|date=17 March 2020|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|accessdate=17 March 2020}}</ref> On August 14, 2020, it was announced that the 2020 ceremony had been rescheduled to October 14.
