'''Nicholas David Gordon "Nick" Knight''' [[Order of the British Empire|OBE]] (born 24 November 1958) is a British [[fashion photography|fashion photographer]] and founder and director of SHOWstudio.com. He is an honorary professor at [[University of the Arts London]] and was awarded an honorary Ph.D. by the same university.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nickknight.com/about/|title=About|publisher=}}</ref> He has produced books of his work including retrospectives ''Nicknight'' (1994) and ''Nick Knight'' (2009). In 2016, Knight's 1992 campaign photograph for fashion brand [[Jil Sander]] was sold by [[Phillips (auctioneers)|Phillips]] auction house at the record-breaking price of HKD 2,360,000.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.phillips.com/detail/NICK-KNIGHT/HK010116/48|title=Phillips : HK010116, Nick-Knight}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Knight was born in [[Hammersmith]], [[London]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/article/nick-knight-biography|title=Nick Knight|first=Jo|last=Craven|publisher=}}</ref> He studied photography at [[Arts University Bournemouth|Bournemouth and Poole College of Art and Design]] and published his first book of photographs 'Skinhead' in 1982 when he was still a student at the school. He was then commissioned by ''[[i-D]]'' editor Terry Jones to create a series of portraits for magazine's fifth-anniversary issue. His work caught the attention of art director Marc Ascoli, who commissioned Knight to shoot the 1986 catalog of Japanese designer [[Yohji Yamamoto]] in collaboration with [[Peter Saville (graphic designer)|Peter Saville]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.businessoffashion.com/community/people/nick-knight|title=Nick Knight - #BoF500 - The Business of Fashion|publisher=}}</ref>

In 1992 Knight took a year long break from fashion photography to work on an exhibition at [[Natural History Museum, London]] with the British architect [[David Chipperfield]]. The exhibition was called ''Plant Power'' and was on the theme of the relationship between humans and plants.<ref>"[https://www.nytimes.com/2007/04/15/style/tmagazine/15tquest.html Vision Quest]". Alice Rawsthorn, ''The New York Times,'' 15 April 2007. Accessed 15 May 2017</ref> The exhibition lasted for fifteen years.<ref>http://www.nhm.ac.uk/research-curation/library/archives/catalogue/dserve.exe?dsqServer=placid&dsqIni=Dserve.ini&dsqApp=Archive&dsqDb=Catalog&dsqCmd=show.tcl&dsqSearch=(RefNo==%27DF%20EXH%2F700%2F2%2F30%27)</ref>

In November 2000, Knight launched SHOWstudio.com.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://showstudio.com/about|title=About SHOWstudio - SHOWstudio - The Home of Fashion Film and Live Fashion Broadcasting|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/art/news/brit-insurance-design-awards-shortlist-from-alexander-mcqueen-to-zaha-hadid-1871852.html|title=Brit Insurance Design Awards shortlist: From Alexander McQueen to Zaha|date=18 January 2010|publisher=}}</ref>

He directed his first music video in 2001, for the song "[[Pagan Poetry]]" by [[Björk]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://showstudio.com/project/pagan_poetry|title=Pagan Poetry|publisher=}}</ref> In 2003, he created a film for [[Massive Attack]]'s album ''[[100th Window]].''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://showstudio.com/project/special_cases|title=Special Cases|publisher=}}</ref> In 2011 and 2013, he directed the videos for [[Lady Gaga]]'s single "[[Born This Way (song)|Born This Way]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1658858/lady-gaga-born-this-way-video/|title=Lady Gaga's 'Born This Way' Video Premieres|publisher=}}</ref> and [[Kanye West]]'s "[[Bound 2]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/kanye-west-previews-new-video-bound-2-featuring-kim-kardashian-topless-8951227.html|title=Kanye West debuts Nick Knight-directed Bound 2 video featuring Kim|date=20 November 2013|publisher=}}</ref> and "[[Black Skinhead]]." In 2016, he photographed American rapper/singer [[Travis Scott]] for his sophomore album ''[[Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight]].''

In 2016, he was commissioned to shoot official portraits of Queen Elizabeth and Prince Charles for the Queen's 90th birthday.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-scoops/nick-knight-portrait-queen-elizabeth-prince-charles-10730233/|title=Nick Knight Shoots Portrait of Queen Elizabeth and Prince Charles|first=Natalie|last=Theodosi|date=18 December 2016|publisher=}}</ref>

In 2019, he collaborated with Kanye West again, directing his short film ''[[Jesus Is King (film)|Jesus Is King]].''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://showstudio.com/news/nick-knight-on-directing-jesus-is-king-a-kanye-west-film|title=Nick Knight on Directing ‘Jesus is King: A Kanye West Film’ - SHOWstudio|publisher=}}</ref> Filmed in the summer of 2019, the film brings West's Sunday Service to life in the [[Roden Crater]], artist [[James Turrell]]'s installation in the [[Painted Desert (Arizona)|Painted Desert]]. The film accompanied the release of ''[[Jesus Is King]]'', West's ninth studio album, and was released in [[IMAX]] theaters on October 25, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Haring |first1=Bruce |title=Kanye West Will Unveil New IMAX Film, 'Jesus Is King', Celebrating His Sunday Service |url=https://deadline.com/2019/09/kanye-west-imax-jesus-is-king-sunday-service-1202747654 |website=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |accessdate=September 29, 2019 |language=en |date=September 29, 2019}}</ref>
