The '''2013 [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]]''' was a tennis tournament played on outdoor [[Hardcourt|hard courts]]. It was the 133rd edition of the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]], the fourth and final [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] event of the year. It took place at the [[USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center]], and ran from August 26 to September 9.<ref>[http://www.usopen.org/news/schedule_changes_prize_money_increases_for_2013_us_open/ Schedule changes, prize money increases for 2013 US Open] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130123085826/http://www.usopen.org/news/schedule_changes_prize_money_increases_for_2013_us_open/ |date=January 23, 2013 }}</ref>

[[Andy Murray]] and [[Serena Williams]] were the defending champions in the singles events. Williams successfully defended her title, but Murray was defeated in the quarterfinals by [[Stanislas Wawrinka]]. [[Rafael Nadal]] won the men's singles.

==Tournament==
[[File:Arthur Ashe Stadium 01.JPG|200px|thumb|left|Arthur Ashe Stadium where the Finals of US Open take place]]
The 2013 US Open was the 133rd edition of the tournament and was held at [[USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center]] in New York City, United States.

The tournament was an event run by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) and was part of the [[2013 ATP World Tour]] and the [[2013 WTA Tour]] calendars under the Grand Slam category. The tournament consisted of both men's and women's singles and doubles draws as well as a mixed doubles event. There were singles and doubles events for both boys and girls (players under 18), which was part of the Grade A category of tournaments, and after one-year break due to [[2012 Summer Paralympics|Paralympic Games in London]] singles, doubles and quad events for men's and women's [[wheelchair tennis]] players as part of the NEC tour under the Grand Slam category.

The tournament was played on hard courts and took place over a series of 17 courts, including the three main showcourts, [[Arthur Ashe Stadium]], [[Louis Armstrong Stadium]] and [[Louis Armstrong Stadium|Grandstand]].

==Notable events==
* In 2008–2012, due to inclement weather conditions, the tournament lasted 15 instead of the scheduled 14 days, ending on the third Monday. In 2013, the schedule was extended to 15 days, potentially giving the players one more day to prepare for the final match.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/tennis/20730946 |title=US Open 2013: Men's and women's finals moved |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=December 14, 2012 |accessdate=September 1, 2013}}</ref> 
* [[Maria Sharapova]], 2006 champion, ranked third in the world (formerly first), withdrew from the tournament due to a shoulder injury.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2013/08/21/maria-sharapova-withdraws-us-open/2683231/ |title=Maria Sharapova withdraws from the US Open |publisher=Usatoday.com |date=August 22, 2013 |accessdate=September 1, 2013}}</ref>
* In a second round match which pitted the [[2001 US Open (tennis)|2001]] and [[2009 US Open (tennis)|2009]] men's champions against each other, [[Lleyton Hewitt]] defeated [[Juan Martín del Potro]] in five grueling sets lasting over four hours; this marked the first time since he won the title in 2001 that Hewitt had defeated a top ten opponent at the US Open.<ref>{{cite web|author=Linda Pearce |url=http://www.theage.com.au/sport/tennis/lleyton-hewitt-upsets-sixth-seed-at-us-open-20130831-2sx4g.html |title=Lleyton Hewitt upsets sixth seed at US Open |publisher=Theage.com.au |accessdate=September 1, 2013}}</ref>
* By winning the first set in her fourth round loss to [[Victoria Azarenka]], [[Ana Ivanovic]] won her first set against a top ten opponent at a Grand Slam since she won the [[2008 French Open]], ending a streak of 15 consecutive sets lost against a top ten seed.{{ref|1|[note a]}}<ref>[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/09/05/another-grand-slam-another-serbian-flop/ Another Grand Slam, another Serbian flop], ''The Roar'', September 5, 2013</ref>
* [[Serena Williams]] won 24 consecutive games from 1&ndash;1 in the second set against [[Sloane Stephens]] in the fourth round through to 1&ndash;0 in the second set against [[Li Na]] in the semi-finals, breaking her own record of 23 games at [[2012 US Open (tennis)|last year's US Open]].<ref name="Serena_v_Azarenka">[http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-09-07/williams2c-azarenka-to-face-off-in-us-open-final/4942380?section=sport US Open: Serena Williams, Victoria Azarenka dominate in semi-finals, 2012 final re-match up next], ''ABC Grandstand Sport (Australian Broadcasting Corporation)'', September 7, 2013</ref>
* For the second consecutive year, Serena Williams and [[Victoria Azarenka]] contested the women's final, marking the first time since 2001&ndash;2 in which two consecutive US Open finals were contested between the same two players.<ref name="Serena_v_Azarenka" />
* With [[Novak Djokovic]] and [[Rafael Nadal]] contesting the men's final, this marked the first time since the [[2000 Australian Open]] in which both the men's and women's finals were contested between the top two.<ref>[http://www.theroar.com.au/2013/09/09/2013-us-open-mens-final-preview/ 2013 US Open: Men's final preview], ''The Roar'', September 9, 2013</ref>
* By winning the tournament, both Rafael Nadal and Serena Williams collected the biggest payday in tennis history of $3.6M ($2.6M for the championship and an added $1M bonus for winning the [[2013 US Open Series]]). Also, with the win, Williams and Nadal have now won each Grand Slam together – the Australian Open (2009), the French Open (2013), Wimbledon (2010) and the US Open (2013). They are the first male-female pair in the entire history of tennis, not just in the Open Era, to win all four Grand Slams together.{{ref|1|a}}<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/3453148/title/seventeen-stats-for-seventeen-slams|title=Seventeen Stats For Seventeen Slams|last=admin|date=September 9, 2013|website=wtatennis.com|accessdate=March 21, 2018}}</ref>
* By winning the US Open, [[Rafael Nadal]] became the first player since [[Andy Roddick]] in 2003 to capture the 3 major consecutive US hardcourt season titles, namely the Roger's Cup, Cincinnati Masters and US Open.

===Prize money===
The US Open total prize money for 2013 was increased by almost nine million dollars to tournament record $34,300,000.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2013-08-05/201308051375726586971.html|title=2013 US Open to feature largest purse in tournament history|website=usopen.org|accessdate=March 21, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130923000058/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2013-08-05/201308051375726586971.html|archive-date=September 23, 2013|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

In the 2013 season, the US Open prize money was the highest out of four grand slam tournaments, compared to $30m at the [[2013 Australian Open|Australian Open]], $29m at the [[2013 French Open|French Open]], and $34m at the [[2013 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon Championships]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/sport/tennis/wimbledon-prizemoney-hits-34m-2013-grand-slam-jackpot-at-124m/story-fnbe6xeb-1226628792427 |title=Wimbledon prizemoney hits $34m, 2013 grand slam jackpot at $124m |publisher=Theaustralian.com.au |date=September 28, 2012 |accessdate=June 21, 2013}}</ref>

:<small>• This was Williams' 17th career Grand Slam singles title and her 5th at the US Open</small>

====Men's doubles====
{{main|2013 US Open – Men's Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|IND}} '''[[Leander Paes]]''' / {{flagicon|CZE}} '''[[Radek Štěpánek]]''' defeated {{flagicon|AUT}} [[Alexander Peya]] / {{flagicon|BRA}} [[Bruno Soares]], 6–1, 6–3
:<small>• This was Paes' 8th career Grand Slam doubles title and his 3rd at the US Open</small>
:<small>• This was Štěpánek's 2nd career Grand Slam doubles title and his 1st at the US Open</small>

====Women's doubles====
{{main|2013 US Open – Women's Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|CZE}} '''[[Andrea Hlaváčková]]''' / {{flagicon|CZE}} '''[[Lucie Hradecká]]'''   defeated {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Ashleigh Barty]] / {{flagicon|AUS}} [[Casey Dellacqua]], 6–7<sup>(4–7)</sup>, 6–1, 6–4
:<small>• This was Hlaváčková's 2nd career Grand Slam doubles title and her 1st at the US Open</small>
:<small>• This was Hradecká's 2nd career Grand Slam doubles title and her 1st at the US Open</small>

====Mixed doubles====
{{main|2013 US Open – Mixed Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|CZE}} '''[[Andrea Hlaváčková]]''' / {{flagicon|BLR}} '''[[Max Mirnyi]]''' defeated {{flagicon|USA}} [[Abigail Spears]] / {{flagicon|MEX}} [[Santiago González (tennis)|Santiago González]], 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>, 6–3
:<small>• This was Hlaváčková's 1st career Grand Slam mixed doubles title</small>
:<small>• This was Mirnyi's 4th career Grand Slam mixed doubles title and his 3rd at the US Open</small>
