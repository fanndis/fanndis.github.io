The '''Parliamentary Evangelical Front''' or more simply known as the '''Evangelical Caucus''' (known in [[Brazilian Portuguese|Portuguese]] as ''Frente Parlamentar Evangélica'' and  ''Bancada Evangélica'' respectively) is a loosely organized group of [[Protestantism in Brazil|Protestant]], [[Evangelicalism|Evangelical]], and [[Pentecostalism|Pentecostal]] lawmakers in the Brazilian government and legislature.

If considered a political party, the Evangelical Caucus would be the third largest in the Brazilian government, surpassing the [[Brazilian Democratic Movement]] and the [[Workers' Party (Brazil)|Workers' Party]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Bancada evangélica seria 3.º partido da Câmara|url=https://www.gazetadopovo.com.br/vidapublica/conteudo.phtml/?id=1365176&tit=Bancada-evangelica-seria-3-partido-da-Camara|language=Portuguese|date=21 April 2013|accessdate=18 September 2019|publisher=Gazeta do Povo|author=Chico Marés}}</ref>

==Leadership==
The evangelical caucus has no formalized leadership; however [[João Campos de Araújo]], [[Anthony Garotinho]], [[Eduardo Cunha]], [[Lincoln Portela]], and [[Magno Malta]] were considered the unofficial leaders of the caucus in 2013.<ref name="líderes">{{cite web|title=Principais líderes da bancada evangélica|url=https://ultimosegundo.ig.com.br/politica/2013-04-14/principais-lideres-da-bancada-evangelica.html|date=14 April 2013|accessdate=18 September 2019|language=Portuguese|publisher=iG Brasil|work=Último Segundo}}</ref>

==Membership==
The Evangelical Caucus of Brazil has no defined standard of membership but is generally open to all Protestant lawmakers in the Brazilian legislature, including mainline denominations as well as members of evangelical and Pentecostal movements.

The caucus was formed especially because Brazil's fast growing Protestant population was underrepresented in government, and as is the norm Catholic and non-religious politicians are not allowed to be members.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vinde a mim os eleitores: a força da bancada evangélica no Congresso|url=https://veja.abril.com.br/politica/vinde-a-mim-os-eleitores-a-forca-da-bancada-evangelica-no-congresso/|language=Portuguese|publisher=[[Veja (magazine)|Veja]]|date=23 March 2013|accessdate=18 September 2019|author=Gabriel Castro and Marcela Mattos}}</ref> Converative Catholic politicians are usually not considered to be part of the evangelical caucus, certain politicians such as [[Hugo Leal (politician)|Hugo Leal]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Bancada evangélica tenta garantir controle da reforma do Código Penal na Câmara|publisher=Último Segundo|url=https://ultimosegundo.ig.com.br/politica/2013-09-05/bancada-evangelica-tenta-garantir-controle-da-reforma-do-codigo-penal-na-camara.html|first=Luciana|last=Lima|work=iG Brasília|date=5 September 2013|accessdate=19 March 2019|language=Portuguese}}</ref> and [[Jair Bolsonaro]]<ref name="Bolsonaro">{{Cite news|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/internacional/en/brazil/2018/10/bolsonaro-a-catholic-expanded-his-base-among-evangelicals.shtml|title=Bolsonaro, a Catholic, Expanded His Base Among Evangelicals|date=2018-10-30|work=Folha de S.Paulo|access-date=2018-11-01|language=pt-BR}}</ref> often collaborate with the caucus.

Although the Evangelical Caucus is often perceived by the media, its members, and supporters to be conservative/right wing, it is worth noting that a few members are affiliated with left-wing politics in Brazil, such as [[Benedita da Silva]],<ref name=story>{{Cite book|title = Benedita Da Silva: An Afro-Brazilian Woman's Story of Politics and Love|url = https://books.google.com/books?id=Q2a84KyZtfwC|publisher = Food First Books|date =1 January 1997|isbn = 9780935028706|language = en|first = Benedita da|last = Silva|first2 = Medea|last2 = Benjamin}}</ref> [[Marina Silva]],<ref name="CP World">{{cite web |last1=Blair |first1=Leonardo |title=Devout Evangelical Christian Marina Silva Started Life Illiterate in the Amazon; Now She's Primed to Become Brazil's First Black President |url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/devout-evangelical-christian-marina-silva-started-life-illiterate-in-the-amazon-now-shes-primed-to-become-brazils-first-black-president-125539/ |website=The Christian Post |publisher=The Christian Post, INC |accessdate=6 September 2018 |language=en-US |date=August 28, 2014}}</ref> and [[Walter Pinheiro]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Evangélicos têm atuação heterogênea no Congresso|url=https://ultimosegundo.ig.com.br/politica/2013-04-23/evangelicos-tem-atuacao-heterogenea-no-congresso.html|publisher=Último Segundo|date=23 April 2013|accessdate=5 May 2019|first=Luciana|last=Lima|language=Portuguese}}</ref>

==Influence on politics==
The evangelical front is often seen as part of the [[Evangelical Christian politics in Latin America|rising Evangelical influence]] in politics in Latin America. The evangelical vote was seen as key in the [[Impeachment of Dilma Rousseff]] and the election of Jair Bolsonaro in the [[2018 Brazilian general election|2018 election]].<ref name="Bolsonaro"/>
