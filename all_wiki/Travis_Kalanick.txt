'''Travis Cordell Kalanick''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|k|æ|l|ə|n|ɪ|k}}; born August 6, 1976) is an American businessman. He is the co-founder of [[Scour Inc.|Scour]], a peer-to-peer file sharing application;<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/travis-kalanicks-first-company-got-sued-for-250-billion-so-he-started-a-new-revenge-business-that-made-him-a-millionaire-2015-9|title=Travis Kalanick's first company got sued for $250&nbsp;billion – so he started a new 'revenge business' that made him a millionaire|last=Kosoff|first=Maya|website=[[Business Insider]]|access-date=12 June 2019|archive-date=December 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181214195734/https://www.businessinsider.com/travis-kalanicks-first-company-got-sued-for-250-billion-so-he-started-a-new-revenge-business-that-made-him-a-millionaire-2015-9|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Red Swoosh]], a peer-to-peer content delivery network;<ref name=":2" /> and [[Uber]]. Red Swoosh was sold to [[Akamai Technologies]] in 2007.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2007/04/12/payday-for-red-swoosh-15-million-from-akamai/|title=Payday for Red Swoosh: $15 million from Akamai|last=Arrington|first=Michael|work=TechCrunch|access-date=8 March 2018|archive-date=June 13, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170613021809/https://techcrunch.com/2007/04/12/payday-for-red-swoosh-15-million-from-akamai/|url-status=live}}</ref> Kalanick is the co-founder and former CEO of Uber, a position he held from 2010 to 2017. He resigned from Uber in 2017, after controversy over the company's reported unethical culture, including allegations that he ignored reports of sexual harassment at the company. In the weeks leading up to a December 24, 2019 announcement that he would resign his Board seat effective December 31, Kalanick sold off approximately 90% of his shares in Uber.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/uber-co-founder-travis-kalanick-to-depart-companys-board-11577196747|title=Uber Co-Founder Travis Kalanick Departs Board, Sells All His Shares|first=Eliot|last=Brown|date=December 24, 2019|via=www.wsj.com|access-date=December 24, 2019|archive-date=December 24, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191224144548/https://www.wsj.com/articles/uber-co-founder-travis-kalanick-to-depart-companys-board-11577196747|url-status=live}}</ref>

Kalanick is ranked 238th on the [[Forbes 400]] list of richest Americans, with a net worth of $2.6 billion.<ref name=forbes400>{{cite web | title=Forbes: Travis Kalanick | url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/travis-kalanick/ | work=[[Forbes]] | access-date=July 25, 2017 | archive-date=July 30, 2017 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170730032228/https://www.forbes.com/profile/travis-kalanick/ | url-status=live }}</ref>

===Education===
Kalanick studied computer engineering and business economics at the [[University of California, Los Angeles]].<ref name=Inc/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www2.technologyreview.com/tr35/profile.aspx?TRID=263 |title=Innovator Under 35: Travis Kalanick, 25 – MIT Technology Review |publisher=.technologyreview.com |accessdate=17 January 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://dcweek2012.sched.org/speaker/traviskalanick1 |title=Travis Kalanick – DCWEEK 2012 |publisher=Dcweek2012.sched.org |accessdate=17 January 2013 |archive-date=November 6, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121106025049/http://dcweek2012.sched.org/speaker/traviskalanick1 |url-status=live }}</ref> While studying at UCLA, Kalanick was a member of [[Theta Xi]] fraternity and started his first business, an online file-exchange service called Scour. In 1998, he dropped out of UCLA to work at the start-up full-time.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/travis-kalanick/|title=Travis Kalanick|work=Forbes|access-date=25 July 2017|archive-date=July 30, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170730032228/https://www.forbes.com/profile/travis-kalanick/|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Red Swoosh (2001–2007) ===
In 2001, with Michael Todd, Kalanick started a new company called [[Red Swoosh]], another [[peer-to-peer]] file-sharing company.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-life-rise-photos-2017-6|title=The life and rise of Travis Kalanick, Uber's controversial billionaire CEO|work=Business Insider|access-date=9 December 2017|archive-date=December 10, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171210015622/http://www.businessinsider.com/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-life-rise-photos-2017-6|url-status=live}}</ref> Red Swoosh software took advantage of increased bandwidth efficiency on the Internet to allow users to transfer and trade large media files, including music files and videos. The company also received help from former Scour employees.{{citation needed|date=February 2018}}

=== Uber (2009–2019) ===

In 2009, Kalanick joined [[Garrett Camp]], who he has said has "credit for the original idea"<ref name=BI-2014 /><ref name=UberEventVideo /> of [[Uber]].<ref name="wsj">{{cite news | url=https://blogs.wsj.com/digits/2011/06/17/worth-it-an-app-to-get-a-cab/ | title=Worth It? An App to Get a Cab | work=The Wall Street Journal | date=June 17, 2011 | agency=Dow Jones & Company | last=Goode | first=Lauren | access-date=August 4, 2017 | archive-date=July 6, 2018 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180706104024/https://blogs.wsj.com/digits/2011/06/17/worth-it-an-app-to-get-a-cab/ | url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2012-08-09/travis-kalanick-on-leading-uber-a-car-service |title=Travis Kalanick on Leading Uber, a Car Service |work=Bloomberg BusinessWeek |date=August 9, 2012 |accessdate=17 January 2013 |archive-date=December 29, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121229102426/http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2012-08-09/travis-kalanick-on-leading-uber-a-car-service |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://startupmixology.tech.co/2011-dc/staff/travis-kalanick |title=Travis Kalanick : Startup Mixology Conference – D.C. – June 16, 2011 |publisher=Startupmixology.tech.co |date=June 16, 2011 |accessdate=17 January 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130113125704/http://startupmixology.tech.co/2011-dc/staff/travis-kalanick |archive-date=January 13, 2013  }}</ref> Camp, co-founder of [[StumbleUpon]], spent $800 hiring a private driver with friends and had been mulling over ways to decrease the cost of black car services (meaning, taxis that are dispatched by a central service rather than hailed directly on the street) ever since. He realized that sharing the cost with people could make it affordable, and his idea morphed into Uber.<ref name=BI-2014 /> "Garrett is the guy who invented that", Kalanick said at an early Uber event in San Francisco.<ref name=BI-2014 /><ref name=UberEventVideo>{{cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/19508136|title=Uber Event, SF Video (00:01:45)|publisher=Uber|year=2011|access-date=March 7, 2017|archive-date=February 9, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170209131556/https://vimeo.com/19508136|url-status=live}}</ref> The first prototype was built by Camp, and his friends, Oscar Salazar and Conrad Whelan, with Kalanick being brought on as a "mega advisor" to the company.<ref name=BI-2014 /> In October 2010, Kalanick succeeded [[Ryan Graves]] as CEO,<ref name=BI-2014 /> who had held the position for ten months.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.linkedin.com/in/ragraves|title=Ryan Graves|via=LinkedIn|accessdate=June 2, 2016|archive-date=September 10, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100910220318/http://www.linkedin.com/in/ragraves|url-status=live}}</ref>

On June 13, 2017, it was announced that Kalanick would take an indefinite leave of absence from Uber.  His responsibilities would be assumed by his direct reports in the organization.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wong|first1=Julia Carrie|title=Embattled Uber CEO Travis Kalanick takes indefinite leave of absence|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2017/jun/13/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-leave-absence-scandal|accessdate=June 13, 2017|work=The Guardian|date=June 13, 2017|archive-date=June 13, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170613192810/https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2017/jun/13/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-leave-absence-scandal|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Bensinger|first1=Greg|title=Uber CEO Travis Kalanick to take a leave of absence|url=http://www.marketwatch.com/story/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-to-take-a-leave-of-absence-2017-06-13|accessdate=June 13, 2017|publisher=MarketWatch|date=June 13, 2017|archive-date=June 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170617053141/http://www.marketwatch.com/story/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-to-take-a-leave-of-absence-2017-06-13|url-status=live}}</ref>

On June 20, 2017, Kalanick resigned as CEO after 5 major investors, including Benchmark Capital, reportedly demanded his resignation.<ref name="NY_mobile">{{cite news|last1=Isaac|first1=Mike|title=Uber Founder Travis Kalanick Resigns as C.E.O.|url=https://mobile.nytimes.com/2017/06/21/technology/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick.html|accessdate=June 21, 2017|work=The New York Times|date=June 21, 2017|archive-date=June 21, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170621062340/https://mobile.nytimes.com/2017/06/21/technology/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Despite his resignation, Kalanick was to retain his seat on Uber's board of directors.<ref name="BI">{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-out-2017-6|title=Travis Kalanick resigns as Uber CEO|work=Business Insider|access-date=25 July 2017|archive-date=July 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170717091953/http://www.businessinsider.com/uber-ceo-travis-kalanick-out-2017-6|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Dara Khosrowshahi]] took his position of CEO in August 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://moneyish.com/ish/five-things-you-didnt-know-about-ubers-new-ceo/|title=Five things you didn't know about Uber's new CEO|access-date=28 January 2018|archive-date=December 19, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171219162013/https://moneyish.com/ish/five-things-you-didnt-know-about-ubers-new-ceo/|url-status=live}}</ref>

On December 24, 2019 Kalanick announced his resignation from the Board effective December 31, 2019. In the weeks leading up to this announcement Kalanick sold off more than $2.5 billion of Uber stock holding which was about 90% of his shares. <ref name="auto"/>

=== Benchmark controversy ===
On August 10, 2017, Axios reported that [[Benchmark (venture capital firm)|Benchmark]] was suing Kalanick for "fraud, breach of contract and breach of fiduciary duty." The suit is based on Uber's decision to expand the number of board seats, with Benchmark arguing the decision is invalid due to withholding of material information prior to the vote.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.axios.com/benchmark-capital-sues-travis-kalanick-for-fraud-2471455477.html|title=Scoop: Benchmark Capital sues Travis Kalanick for fraud|date=August 10, 2017|work=Axios|access-date=10 August 2017|archive-date=August 10, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170810212814/https://www.axios.com/benchmark-capital-sues-travis-kalanick-for-fraud-2471455477.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The lawsuit was very controversial in Silicon Valley because of the relationship between the founder and the investor.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/01/25/benchmarks-lawsuit-against-former-uber-ceo-kalanick-dismissed/|title=Benchmark's lawsuit against former Uber CEO Kalanick dismissed|last=Roof|first=Katie|date=January 25, 2018|work=TechCrunch|access-date=31 January 2018|archive-date=February 1, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201075522/https://techcrunch.com/2018/01/25/benchmarks-lawsuit-against-former-uber-ceo-kalanick-dismissed/|url-status=live}}</ref> The court ruled in the favor of Kalanick to move the case to arbitration on August 30.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2017/08/30/pishevar-says-delaware-court-is-sending-benchmark-lawsuit-to-arbitration/|title=Benchmark-Kalanick Uber board suit sent to arbitration|last=Roof|first=Katie|date=August 30, 2017|work=TechCrunch|access-date=31 January 2018|archive-date=February 1, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201075458/https://techcrunch.com/2017/08/30/pishevar-says-delaware-court-is-sending-benchmark-lawsuit-to-arbitration/|url-status=live}}</ref> In response to the court ruling Kalanick released a public statement:

<blockquote>Pleased that the court has ruled in his favor today and remains confident that he will prevail in the arbitration process. Benchmark's false allegations are wholly without merit and have unnecessarily harmed Uber and its shareholders.<ref name=":0" /></blockquote>

In January 2018, Benchmark dropped the lawsuit against Kalanick. The investment firm agreed to drop the lawsuit if Uber completed its transaction with [[SoftBank Group|Softbank]], a prior condition Benchmark had agreed to. Completed in early January, Uber agreed to sell a sizable stake to SoftBank.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-28/uber-investors-are-said-to-agree-to-sell-stake-in-softbank-deal|title=Uber Investors Agree to Sell Stake in SoftBank Deal|date=December 28, 2017|publisher=Bloomberg L.P.|access-date=31 January 2018|archive-date=February 1, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201020854/https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-12-28/uber-investors-are-said-to-agree-to-sell-stake-in-softbank-deal|url-status=live}}</ref> The share purchase valued Uber at $48&nbsp;billion.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/bizcarson/2018/01/18/softbank-closes-uber-deal/|title=It's Official: SoftBank Closes Multibillion-Dollar Deal In Uber|last=Carson|first=Biz|work=Forbes|access-date=31 January 2018|archive-date=February 1, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201020347/https://www.forbes.com/sites/bizcarson/2018/01/18/softbank-closes-uber-deal/|url-status=live}}</ref>
