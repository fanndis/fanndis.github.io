From 1957 to 1960, [[Dominion of Ghana|Ghana]] was an independent [[constitutional monarchy]] with [[Elizabeth II]] as its queen. She was also the Queen of the other [[Commonwealth realm]]s, including the [[United Kingdom]], and her constitutional roles in Ghana were delegated to a [[List of Governors-General of Ghana|Governor-General]].<ref name=judicial>{{cite web|url=http://www.judicial.gov.gh/index.php/history/after-independence|title=After Independence|publisher=Judicial Service of Ghana|accessdate=8 November 2015}}</ref>

The [[Ghana Independence Act 1957]] transformed the [[Gold Coast (British colony)|British Crown Colony of the Gold Coast]] into the independent [[sovereign state]] of Ghana with the Queen as head of state on 6 March 1957.<ref name=judicial/> Ghana adopted a new constitution in 1960 that replaced the Queen and Governor-General on 1 July 1960 with a [[President of Ghana|President]].<ref name=judicial/> Ghana became a [[Republics in the Commonwealth of Nations|republic within the Commonwealth]].

[[File:The National Archives UK - CO 1069-43 03 003.jpg|thumb|left|The Queen meeting Ghanaian government minister [[Komla Agbeli Gbedemah]] and his wife, 1953]]

The Queen visited Ghana on 9–20 November 1961 and 7–9 November 1999.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royal.gov.uk/MonarchUK/RoyalVisits/Commonwealthvisitssince1952.aspx|title=Commonwealth visits since 1952|publisher=Royal Household|work=Official website of the British monarchy|accessdate=8 November 2015}}</ref>
