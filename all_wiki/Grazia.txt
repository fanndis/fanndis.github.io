'''''Grazia''''' ({{IPA-it|ˈɡrattsja|pron}}; Italian for ''Grace'') is a weekly [[women's magazine]] that originated in Italy with international editions printed in [[Albania]], [[Argentina]], [https://graziamagazine.com/ Australia], [[Bahrain]], [[Bulgaria]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Media landscapes. Bulgaria|url=http://ejc.net/media_landscapes/bulgaria|work=European Journalism Center|accessdate=13 June 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171221020936/http://ejc.net/media_landscapes/bulgaria|archive-date=21 December 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[China]], [[Croatia]], [[Colombia]], [[France]], [[Germany]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grazia-magazin.de/|title=Grazia Germany|accessdate=12 March 2010}}</ref> [[Greece]], [[Indonesia]], [[India]], [[Jordan]], [[North Macedonia|Macedonia]],<ref>{{cite web| url=https://dn.sapo.pt/2005/11/10/media/revista_grazia_chega_a_portugal_jane.html|title=Revista 'Grazia' chega a Portugal em Janeiro|author=Paula Brito|date=10 November 2005|work=[[Diário de Notícias]]|accessdate=21 September 2007}}</ref> [[Mexico]], the [[Netherlands]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Partners|url=http://www.fashionweek.nl/en/downtown/support|work=Fashion Week|accessdate=9 May 2015}}</ref> [[Poland]], [[Portugal]], [[Pakistan]],  [[Serbia]], [[Slovenia]], [[South Korea]], [[Spain]], [[Thailand]], and the [[United Kingdom]].

==History and profile==
[[File:Prima copertina Grazia 1938 Mondadori.jpg|thumb|First cover Grazia 1938 Mondadori]]
The Italian edition of ''Grazia'' was first published [[Arnoldo Mondadori Editore|Mondadori]] in November 1938.<ref name=davste>{{cite book|author1=David Forgacs|author2=Stephen Gundle|title=Mass Culture and Italian Society from Fascism to the Cold War|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-rIZzodaZCYC&pg=PR7|accessdate=3 December 2014|year=2007|publisher=Indiana University Press|isbn=0-253-21948-5|page=7}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Arnoldo Mondadori Editore S.p.A. - Company Profile|url=http://www.referenceforbusiness.com/history2/21/Arnoldo-Mondadori-Editore-S-p-A.html|work=Reference for Business|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref> The magazine was modelled on the American magazine ''[[Harper's Bazaar]]''.<ref>{{cite book|author=Adam Arvidsson|title=Marketing Modernity: Italian Advertising from Fascism to Postmodernity|date=2003|publisher=Routledge|location=New York|page=23|url=https://www.questia.com/read/104246488/marketing-modernity-italian-advertising-from-fascism|accessdate=25 April 2015}}{{Subscription required|via=Questia}}</ref> The start of ''Grazia'' was a return in Italy to traditionalist values such as [[cooking]] and [[child-rearing]].<ref name=penm>{{cite journal|author=Penelope Morris|title=A window on the private sphere: Advice columns, marriage, and the evolving family in 1950s Italy|journal=The  Italianist|date=2007|volume=27|url=http://eprints.gla.ac.uk/5137/1/morris27window.pdf|accessdate=27 April 2015}}</ref> During the [[Fascism in Italy|fascist rule]] in the country the magazine followed the [[Fascism|Fascist]] policies and propaganda.<ref name=penm/> Following World War II the magazine was renewed, but its [[Conservativism|conservative]] stance remained.<ref name=penm/>

From its beginning in 1938 to September 1943, [[Bruno Munari]] served as the [[art director]] for the magazine and for another Mondadori title, ''[[Tempo (Italian magazine)|Tempo]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bruno Munari: art director, 1943-1944|url=http://www.domusweb.it/en/from-the-archive/2012/03/24/bruno-munari-art-director-1943-1944.html|work=Domus|accessdate=25 April 2015|date=24 March 2012}}</ref>

In Italy, it is owned by the Mondadori<ref name=davste/><ref>{{cite news|title=Time Inc in Joint Venture to Publish talian Fortune|url=http://www.apnewsarchive.com/1988/Time-Inc-in-Joint-Venture-To-Publish-Italian-Fortune/id-44dc57c4901f9715131c3be18f630ee5|accessdate=17 February 2015|work=Associated Press|date=7 November 1988}}</ref> which later became one of [[Silvio Berlusconi]]'s companies,<ref>{{cite news|author=Eric Lyman|title=Italian publisher unveils magazine dedicated to Pope Francis|url=http://ncronline.org/blogs/francis-chronicles/italian-publisher-unveils-magazine-dedicated-pope-francis|accessdate=2 November 2014|work=National Catholic Reporter|date=5 March 2014|location=Rome}}</ref>

''Grazia'' published an article in July 2015 which promoted the breeding of family pets to make money to buy better furniture. This brought international condemnation from animal lovers owing to the number of unwanted pets which are killed in overcrowded shelters each week. A press statement from the British [[Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals]] (RSPCA) was critical of ''Grazia''.<ref>[http://www.petgazette.biz/7393-grazia-article-sparks-criticism-from-pet-lovers/ ''Grazia'' article sparks criticism from pet lovers] ''Pet Gazette''. Retrieved 8 July 2015</ref>

==International editions==
''Grazia'' has international editions in several countries.<ref name=studi>{{cite web|title=10 Pure Italian Fashion Magazines You can’t Miss|url=http://www.studying.it/10-pure-italian-fashion-magazines-you-cant-miss/|work=Studying It|accessdate=18 June 2015|date=5 July 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150618173647/http://www.studying.it/10-pure-italian-fashion-magazines-you-cant-miss/|archive-date=18 June 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Its first international edition was published in Bulgaria in March 2004.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bulgaria|url=http://www.graziainternational.com/edition/bulgaria/|work=Grazia International|accessdate=13 June 2015}}</ref> The British edition of the magazine began publication in February 2005<ref name=studi/> and is owned in the UK under licence by [[Bauer Verlagsgruppe|Bauer Consumer Media]].{{citation needed|date=August 2014}} The Greek version was launched in April 2005.<ref name=studi/> In November of the same year its edition in the [[United Arab Emirates]] was first issued.<ref name=studi/>

The magazine had an edition in [[Croatia]] from February 2006 and in [[Serbia]] from June 2006.<ref name=studi/> The Russian edition began publication in March 2007,<ref name=studi/> while the [[Netherlands]] followed in August 2007.<ref name=studi/> ''[[Grazia (India)|Grazia India]]'' was launched in May 2008.<ref name=studi/> Its Australian edition began publication in July 2008,<ref name=studi/> but folded in February 2013.<ref>[http://www.theaustralian.com.au/media/grazia-gone-as-magazines-slump/story-e6frg996-1226578125857 "Grazia gone as magazines slump"], ''The Australian''</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Max Mason|title=New editor-in-chief of The Australian Women's Weekly named as Kim Wilson|url=http://www.smh.com.au/business/media-and-marketing/new-editorinchief-of-the-australian-womens-weekly-named-as-kim-wilson-20160210-gmq943.html|accessdate=18 February 2017|work=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=11 February 2016}}</ref> [https://grazia.com.au/ ''Grazia Australia''] reopened in 2015 under new management. In February 2009 the [[Chinese language|Chinese]] version of ''Grazia'' was started, being its twelfth international edition.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Chen Duo Icy|author2=Chen Jiaxi Viola|author3=Liu Wenman Nicole|author4=Wu Ge Mavis|title="Grazia China" Study Report|url=http://comd.hkbu.edu.hk/mm/mmgtproject/image/projects/2013_project/13_group3.pdf|work=Hong Kong Baptist University|accessdate=27 April 2015|date=May 2013}}</ref> The French version of the magazine was first published in August 2009.<ref>{{cite web|title=France|url=http://www.mondadori.com/Group/Magazines/France|work=Mondadori|accessdate=1 November 2014}}</ref>

The inaugural issue of ''Grazia Korea'' was launched on 20 February 2013. It features a photospread of actors [[Lee Byung-hun]], [[Bae Soo-bin]] and Kim Do-hyun for the play based on the film ''[[Masquerade (2012 film)|Masquerade]]'' (2012).<ref>{{cite news|author=So Hyoun An|title=The Three Gwanghaes Lee Byung Hun, Bae Soo Bin and Kim Do Hyun Pose for Grazia|url=http://enewsworld.mnet.com/enews/contents.asp?idx=29793|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130419012604/http://enewsworld.mnet.com/enews/contents.asp?idx=29793|url-status=dead|archive-date=19 April 2013|accessdate=23 February 2013|work=enewsWorld|date=23 February 2013|agency=CJ E&M}}</ref> ''Grazia Pakistan'' was launched in February 2017.[http://www.graziainternational.com/edition/pakistan/] The launch party took place at the HSY Mansion in Krachi on the 6th of February with publisher and Editot-in-Chief of Grazia Pakistan, Zahraa Saifullah and the first issue cover girl [[Mawra Hocane]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hipinpakistan.com/news/1151817|title=Mawra Hocane becomes the first cover girl for Grazia Pakistan|last=Desk|first=HIP|date=2017-02-07|work=HIP|access-date=2017-09-28|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Germany===
Only one and a half years after the start of the German edition of Grazia, [[Mediengruppe Klambt]] brought the title 2011 into a joint venture in which [[Gruner + Jahr]] barely held the majority. in 2017, the women's magazine has been bought back completely. As of January 1, 2018, Mediengruppe Klambt is taking over the 50.1% share, which was previously held by Gruner + Jahr.<ref name="Klambt übernimmt Grazia komplett">{{cite web|last1=Hein|first1=David|title=Klambt übernimmt Grazia komplett|url=http://www.horizont.net/medien/nachrichten/Von-GrunerJahr-Klambt-uebernimmt-Grazia-komplett--162485|website=horizont.net|accessdate=9 November 2017|date=8 November 2017}}</ref>

===Germany===
The fashion magazine's published circulation in the third quarter 2017 was 96,632 copies - a drop of around 20 percent compared to the same period of 2016. Since 2016 ''Grazia'' has come under pressure at the [[kiosk]]s with the magazine ''Olivia'' of [[Bauer Media Group]].<ref name="Klambt übernimmt Grazia komplett"/>
