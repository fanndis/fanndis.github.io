'''Owen Thomas Van Natta'''<ref>''Who's Who in America''. 64th edition, 2010</ref> (born November 12, 1969)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://birthdatabase.com|title=The SeeMe.com Blog » Come see us ! XoXo <3|accessdate=September 15, 2016|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160914020752/http://www.birthdatabase.com/|archivedate=September 14, 2016|df=mdy-all}} {{unreliable source?|date=September 2016}} </ref> is an American businessman who has found success in a variety of tech spaces, including e-tail, social networking, music streaming, gaming, finance and investment. He has held notable positions in various tech companies, including [[Amazon.com]], [[Facebook]], [[Myspace]], [[BOKU mobile payments|BOKU Mobile Payments]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=EquityNet|title=BOKU|url=https://www.equitynet.com/c/boku?s=p?sp=NG|access-date=2020-12-02|website=EquityNet}}</ref>, and [[Zynga]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Shaw|first=Lucas|date=2011-11-17|title=Zynga executive Owen Van Natta resigns|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-media-zynga-idUSTRE7AG2QJ20111117|access-date=2020-12-02}}</ref> Van Natta holds a bachelor's degree in English and [[American literature]] from the [[University of California, Santa Cruz]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2009/04/23/new-myspace-ceo-settles-many-old-scores/|title=Hiring Of New MySpace CEO Settles Many Old Scores|first=Michael|last=Arrington|publisher=[[TechCrunch]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref>

Van Natta is currently a Founder and Managing Director of Prefix Capital <ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Prefix Capital|url=https://www.prefixcapital.com/|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Prefix Capital|language=en-US}}</ref>, as well as a Founder of [[415, LLC]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=415 - Crunchbase Investor Profile & Investments|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/415|access-date=2020-12-02|website=Crunchbase|language=en}}</ref><ref name="geekwire.com"/>

Van Natta married Jennifer Van Natta in 1998 and had two children together<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Exploratorium Board Bios|url=https://www.exploratorium.edu/sites/default/files/press/pdf/New%20Board%20Members%20Press%20Release.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>. The couple divorced in August 2019. <ref>{{Cite web|title=Case Info|url=https://webapps.sftc.org/ci/CaseInfo.dll?CaseNum=FDI17787315&SessionID=5CFEA5219B3C22692AACADE777A8E6126F2F0BCF|access-date=2020-11-24|website=webapps.sftc.org}}</ref>

==Career==
From June 1998 to August 2005, Van Natta was Vice President of Worldwide Business and Corporate Development at [[Amazon.com]], where he managed global marketing programs and strategic partnerships during the period where Amazon was laying the groundwork for the company's historic growth in market capitalization<ref>{{Cite web|last=Molla|first=Rani|date=2017-05-15|title=Amazon’s epic 20-year run as a public company, explained in five charts|url=https://www.vox.com/2017/5/15/15610786/amazon-jeff-bezos-public-company-profit-revenue-explained-five-charts|access-date=2020-12-02|website=Vox|language=en}}</ref>. He was part of the founding team of A9.com, the Amazon.com search engine optimization company, and was responsible for site operations and sponsored-link advertising.<ref name="FastCompany">{{cite web|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/blog/noah-robischon/editors-desk/who-will-run-myspace-after-chris-dewolfe-owen-van-natta-or-jeff-ber|title=Former Facebook COO Owen Van Natta Taking Over at MySpace|first=Noah|last=Robischon|date=April 23, 2009|work=[[FastCompany]]}}</ref>

Van Natta became the Chief Operating Officer at [[Facebook]], where he focused on revenue operations, business development, and strategic partnerships from September 2005 to February 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kara.allthingsd.com/20080219/owen-van-natta-to-leave-facebook/|title=Owen Van Natta to Leave Facebook|publisher=[[All Things Digital|allthingsd.com]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref> At the time of his departure, his title was Chief Revenue Officer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.cnet.com/8301-13577_3-10093541-36.html|title=Ex-Facebook exec takes helm at Project Playlist|publisher=[[cnet.com]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref> He went on to serve as the Chief Executive Officer of [[Project Playlist]] in 2008, a music sharing website allowing users to search for music, create custom playlists, and share the content with friends.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://venturebeat.com/2008/11/11/project-playlist-hires-owen-van-natta-and-raises-20m-but-for-what/|title=Project Playlist hires Owen Van Natta and raises $20M, but for what?|publisher=[[Venture Beat]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref>

In April 2009, Van Natta became Chief Executive Officer of [[MySpace]], where he was responsible for all aspects of the company's strategic vision and the execution of its global business initiatives.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2009/04/24/news-corp-pulls-the-trigger-owen-van-vatta-now-runs-myspace/|title=News Corp Pulls The Trigger: Owen Van Natta Now Runs The Circus. success|first=Emma|last=Barnett|publisher=[[telegraph.co.uk]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref> By the end of 2009, [[NewsCorp]]'s Fox Interactive Media business posted revenue of $226 million and stepped down and joined [[Zynga]] in February 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.engadget.com/2011/11/20/former-myspace-ceo-owen-van-natta-now-former-zynga-executive/|title=Former Myspace CEO Owen Van Natta joined [[Zynga]], an tech company that makes online social games, as Executive Vice President of Business and as a member of the Board of Directors in August 2010.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20101009132546/http://www.kerryonworld.com/business/former-myspace-ceo-owen-van-natta-joins-online-gaming-publisher-zynga KerryOnWorld August 2010]</ref> He was responsible for the company's revenue strategy, corporate development, international expansion and brand. He resigned from Zynga on November 17, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-media-zynga-idUSTRE7AG2QJ20111117|title=Zynga executive Owen Van Natta resigns|date=November 17, 2011|publisher=[[Reuters]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052970203699404577044363493656398|title=Zynga Executive Van Natta  Resigns|first=Shayndi|last=Raice|date=November 19, 2011|publisher=[[Wall Street Journal]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/myspace-ceo-owen-van-natta-263586|title=Former MySpace CEO Owen Van Natta Leaves Zynga Executive Role|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|accessdate=September 15, 2016}}</ref>

In 2013, Van Natta founded 415 LLC, which provides seed funding for and advises (primarily early-stage) companies. 415 assists in the areas of strategy, funding, deals/partnerships, social, e- and m-commerce, operations, user acquisition/engagement, culture and growth.<ref>{{Cite web|title=415 - Crunchbase Investor Profile & Investments|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/415|access-date=2020-12-02|website=Crunchbase|language=en}}</ref>

He is currently a Founder and Managing Director of Prefix Capital <ref name=":0" />, an early stage investment fund that was founded in 2019<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prefix Capital - Crunchbase Investor Profile & Investments|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/prefix-capital|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Crunchbase|language=en}}</ref>.
