The '''Catalan independence referendum of 2017''', also known by the [[numeronym]] '''1-O''' (for "1 October") in Spanish media, was an [[independence referendum]] held on 1 October 2017 in the [[Autonomous communities of Spain|Spanish autonomous community]] of [[Catalonia]], passed by the [[Parliament of Catalonia]] as the [[Law on the Referendum on Self-determination of Catalonia]] and foundation of the Republic|Law of juridical transition and foundation of the Republic of Catalonia]] the following day 7 September, which stated that independence would be binding with a simple majority, without requiring a minimum turnout.<ref name="TheGuardian06092017">{{cite The referendum question, which voters answered with "Yes" or "No", was "Do you want Catalonia to become an independent state in the form of a republic?". The "Yes" side won, with 2,044,038 (92.01%) voting for independence and 177,547 (7.99%) voting against, on a turnout of 43.03%. The Catalan government estimated that up to 770,000 votes were not cast due to polling stations being closed off during the police crackdown,<ref name="Results"/><ref name="Mundo-2Oct17"/><ref name="Independent-2Oct17"/><ref name="CNN-2Oct17"/> although the "universal census" system introduced earlier in the day allowed electors to vote at any given polling station.<ref name="Universal"/><ref name="ElPais03112017">{{cite news|title=El 'sí' obtuvo más votos que personas censadas en 71 municipios|url=https://elpais.com/ccaa/2017/10/03/catalunya/1507048467_326934.html|publisher=[[El País]]|date=3 October 2017|language=es}}</ref> Catalan government officials have argued that the turnout would have been higher were it not for Spanish and Catalan police suppression of the vote.<ref name="TurullSays">{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/10/01/europe/catalonia-spain-independence-referendum-result/index.html |title=Catalonia referendum plunges Spain into political crisis |author= Hilary Clarke, Isa Soares and Vasco Cotovio |agency= [[CNN]] |date=2 October 2017 |access-date= 4 October 2017 |quote= Turnout was about 42% of the 5.3 million eligible voters... ==Background==

The ballot was initially scheduled for no later than 17 September 2017, a result of an election pledge made by pro-independence parties ahead of the [[2015 Catalan regional election|2015 Catalan election]] (during the previous legislature, the Catalan government had held [[2014 Catalan self-determination referendum|a non-binding "citizen participation process"]] about the question).

The election resulted in a [[minority government]] for the [[Junts pel Sí]] coalition (JxSí), which had won a plurality of MPs (62 of the 135 seats), plus conditional support from the 10 [[Popular Unity Candidacy - Constituent Call|CUP-CC]] MPs. Shortly after the government was formed, it resolved to hold a referendum on independence.<ref>{{cite news|title=Catalan Parliament Votes Yes to Independence Referendum Next September|url=http://www.voanews.com/a/catalan-parliament-votes-yes-independence-referendum-september/3539984.html|access-date=30 March 2017|date=6 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Prim|first1=Sara|title=Puigdemont to call a referendum next September if Spain doesn't allow Catalans to vote|url=http://www.catalannewsagency.com/politics/item/puigdemont-during-vote-of-confidence-it-s-either-referendum-or-referendum|agency=Catalan News Agency|access-date=2 October 2016|date=28 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Parliament approves calling a referendum with or without Spain's consent|url=http://www.catalannewsagency.com/politics/item/parliament-approves-calling-a-referendum-with-or-without-spain-s-consent|agency=Catalan News Agency|access-date=8 October 2016|date=7 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Calvo|first1=Amanda|title=Catalan parliament votes yes to independence referendum next September|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-spain-catalonia-idUSKCN126250|agency=Reuters|access-date=8 October 2016|date=6 October 2016}}</ref>

[[File:Demonstration "Som una nació. Nosaltres decidim" (Cataluña, 2010).jpg|thumb|Demonstration for [[2010 Catalan autonomy protest|Catalan autonomy in 2010]]]]
On 24 January 2017, the [[Government of Catalonia]] held a privately organised conference<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldo.es/noticias/nacional/2017/01/23/puigdemont-junqueras-viajan-bruselas-este-martes-para-defender-referendum-pactado-1155132-305.html|title=Puigdemont y Junqueras viajan a Bruselas este martes para defender un referéndum pactado|first=Europa Press.|last=Barcelona|website=heraldo.es|access-date=23 June 2017}}</ref> in one of the rooms of the [[European Parliament]] in its [[Brussels]] headquarters. The event, entitled "The Catalan Referendum", was promoted by [[Carles Puigdemont]], President, [[Oriol Junqueras]], Vice President, and [[Raül Romeva]]. It was attended by 500 people, among whom were MEPs, diplomats and journalists from the international media.<ref>{{cite news|agency= Catalan News Agency|title= Puigdemont: "Europe cannot look the other way"|url=http://www.catalannewsagency.com/politics/item/puigdemont-europe-cannot-look-the-other-way |access-date=25 January 2017|date=25 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|agency= VilaWeb|title= Puigdemont on the referendum: 'Europe cannot look the other way and should be part of the solution' |url=http://www.vilaweb.cat/noticies/puigdemont-on-the-referendum-europe-cannot-look-the-other-way-and-should-be-part-of-the-solution/ |access-date=25 January 2017|date=25 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|agency= VilaWeb|title= MEPs from fourteen countries and all political groups attend hearing with Puigdemont, Junqueras and Romeva in Brussels |url=http://www.vilaweb.cat/noticies/meps-from-fourteen-countries-and-all-political-groups-attend-hearing-with-puigdemont-junqueras-and-romeva-in-brussels/|access-date=25 January 2017|date=25 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|agency= YouTube |title= The Catalan Referendum conference |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=98k8D_2HpCM|access-date=27 March 2017|date=10 February 2017}}</ref>

===Organisation===
[[File:El president i els alcaldes entrant al Palau de la Generalitat.jpg|thumb|Catalan president [[Carles Puigdemont]] and 700 mayors of Catalonia on the meeting at preparation of referendum, 16 September 2017]]
[[File:Inici acte final campanya 1O.jpg|thumb|Final meeting of campaign for referendum in front of [[Palau Nacional]], 29 September 2017]]

The Catalan government's decree officially calling the referendum was expected to be approved in the second half of August<ref name="CatrefOct">{{cite web|url=http://elpais.com/elpais/2017/06/09/inenglish/1496995696_161601.html?rel=mas|title=Catalan independence referendum to be held on October 1: regional premier|first= Javier|last= Casqueiro|website=elpais.com|date=9 June 2017|access-date=29 June 2017}}</ref> but was approved only on 6 September.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/catalan-parliament-paves-independence-vote-49646053|title=Catalan parliament paves the way for independence vote|last=News|first=ABC|work=ABC News|access-date=6 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170906083731/https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/catalan-parliament-paves-independence-vote-49646053|archive-date=6 September 2017|url-status = dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Shortly after the referendum was announced, attention focused on the issue of the [[ballot box]]es, since the government of Spain is in charge of providing them, whereas for this non-state-sanctioned vote, the [[Generalitat de Catalunya|government of Catalonia]] would have to put them in place, potentially risking prosecution for the misuse of public funds. It is still unclear who bought them. The Catalan government opened a bidding process to buy them but no offers were presented. [[Cristóbal Montoro]], the Spanish [[Ministry of Economy, Industry and Competitiveness (Spain)|Minister of the Treasury]], assured that the ballot boxes were not paid for with public money and some media reported they were finally bought by an individual whose identity remains unknown.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bustos |first1=Jorge |date=16 April 2018 |title=Cristóbal Montoro: "Acepto que al PP le pasa algo, pero su problema no es Rajoy" |url=http://www.elmundo.es/espana/2018/04/16/5ad3b150ca4741ec268b460e.html |language=es-ES |work=El Mundo |location=Madrid |access-date=16 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Orriols |first1=Núria |date=7 October 2017 |title=Les urnes: quatre mesos de viatge fins a l'1-O |url=https://www.ara.cat/politica/urnes-quatre-mesos-viatge-fins_0_1883811627.html |language=ca |work=Diari Ara |location=Barcelona |access-date=11 April 2018}}</ref>

Tendering by the Catalan government for materials such as ballot papers and envelopes for a putative regional election in the region were thought by some to be an attempt to covertly organise the referendum.<ref>{{cite news |last=G. Sastre |first=Daniel |date=22 March 2017 |title=El Parlament aprueba los Presupuestos del referéndum al borde del motín |url=http://www.elmundo.es/cataluna/2017/03/22/58d26a11e2704eec288b45b5.html |language=es |work=El Mundo |location=Barcelona |access-date=30 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Cordero |first=Dani |date=23 March 2017 |title=Puigdemont prepara la maquinaria electoral para unas autonómicas |url=http://ccaa.elpais.com/ccaa/2017/03/23/catalunya/1490269577_485283.html |language=es |work=El País |location=Barcelona |access-date=27 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Barrena |first1=Xabi |last2=Masreal |first2=Fidel |date=23 March 2017 |title=El Govern activa los trámites para poder hacer el referéndum |url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/politica/govern-licita-concurso-para-material-electoral-5920396 |language=es |work=El Periódico de Catalunya |location=Barcelona |access-date=27 March 2017}}</ref> On 24 March, the Spanish Public Prosecutor's Office in Catalonia had already announced an inquiry to determine whether a referendum is in the planning.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Barrena |first1=Xabi |last2=Albalat |first2=J.G. |date=24 March 2017 |title=La fiscalía investiga si el Govern está preparando el referéndum |url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/politica/fiscalia-investiga-preparativos-referendum-5923591 |language=es |work=El Periódico de Catalunya |location=Barcelona |access-date=30 March 2017}}</ref>

In terms of its organisation, the [[electoral roll]] is one of the main points in contention, since this is managed by the National Institute of Statistics, an autonomous organisation placed under the jurisdiction of the government of Spain. To access its data, polls must have been authorised by the [[Spanish Congress]].<ref name="LVdatos">{{cite web|url=http://www.lavanguardia.com/politica/20170627/423716998832/referendum-1o-censo-junta-electoral-neutralidad.html|title=Se buscan los datos de 5,5 millones de catalanes para el Referéndum|first= Jaume|last= Aroca|website=lavanguardia.com|language=es|access-date=30 June 2017}}</ref> Without an undisputed access to the electoral roll, the results may be deemed unreliable. Similar difficulties could be met when it comes to the [[electoral commission]] to be formed for monitoring the polling and results.<ref name="LVdatos"/>

An official announcement by the government of Catalonia suggested that Catalan residents overseas willing to vote would have to register. By the end of June 2017, out of 285,000 Catalans living abroad and eligible to vote, 5,000 had registered.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elpais.com/elpais/2017/06/26/inenglish/1498473753_881035.html|title=Catalan government fails to mobilize region's expats in run-up to referendum|first1= Javier|last1= Casqueiro|first2= Dani|last2= Cordero|website=elpais.com|date=26 June 2017|access-date=29 June 2017}}</ref>

===Tensions within the Catalan government===
{{main|2017 Spanish constitutional crisis}}

On 3 July 2017, President Puigdemont sacked his Minister for Enterprise, {{Interlanguage link|Jordi Baiget|ca|Jordi Baiget i Cantons|es|Jordi Baiget}}, who, in the face of the legal challenges, had just expressed doubts regarding the referendum taking place as envisioned by the Catalan government.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://cat.elpais.com/cat/2017/07/03/catalunya/1499107547_465163.html|title=Puigdemont cessa Baiget pels seus dubtes sobre el referèndum|first1= Dani|last1= Cordero|first2= Pere|last2=Rios|website=elpais.com|language=ca|access-date=4 July 2017}}</ref>

Then, on 14 July, Puigdemont proceeded with a [[cabinet reshuffle]], replacing three additional ministers in his cabinet (the ones responsible for presidency, education, and interior) in a move widely seen as a removal of the remaining hesitant voices within his cabinet in regards to the referendum issue.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://cat.elpais.com/cat/2017/07/14/catalunya/1500019036_391705.html|title=Junqueras liderarà el referèndum després d'imposar un Govern de fidels|first=Àngels|last=Piñol|website=elpais.com|language=ca|access-date=14 July 2017}}</ref>

On 17 July, the chief of the [[Mossos d'Esquadra|Catalan police]], called ''Mossos d'Esquadra'', resigned without giving any reason. The Catalan police force is seen as key to enforcing any court orders sought by the central government challenging the secession vote.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-07-17/catalan-police-chief-resigns-as-referendum-on-secession-looms|title=Catalan Police Chief Resigns as Referendum on Secession Looms|first=Esteban|last=Duarte|website=bloomberg.com|access-date=17 July 2017}}</ref>

Additional isolated resignations and dismissals have been noted among some of the high-level civil servants potentially playing a role with the vote's organisation.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lavanguardia.com/politica/20170718/424199640428/fede-adan-dimite-interior-112.html|title=El director del 112 Fede Adan también dimite tras la renuncia de Jané y Batlle|website=lavanguardia.com|language=es|access-date=21 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elmundo.es/cataluna/2017/07/21/5971321c268e3e44528b47cd.html|title=La 'número dos' de Enseñanza dimite a una semana del relevo de la consellera|website=elmundo.es|language=es|access-date=21 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/politica/20170721/tercera-baja-ensenyament-referendum-1o-6182526|title=Tercera baja en la Conselleria d'Ensenyament por el Referéndum del 1-O|first=Fidel|last=Masreal|website=elperiodico.com|language=es|access-date=21 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lavanguardia.com/politica/20170726/43117864576/cargo-erc-dimite-preparativos-1o.html|title=Referéndum: Dimite Ángel Xifré, el primer alto cargo de ERC|website=lavanguardia.com|language=es|access-date=26 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://elpais.com/ccaa/2017/09/13/catalunya/1505285628_302655.html|title=El Govern cesa al presidente del Consorcio de Educación de Barcelona en plena búsqueda de locales|first=Clara|last=Blanchar|website=elpais.com|language=es|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref>

==Administration==
[[File:Papeleta Referendum 2017.png|thumb|Ballot paper that the Catalan government intended to use in the referendum, in [[Catalan language|Catalan]], [[Spanish language|Castilian Spanish]] and [[Aranese dialect|Aranese Occitan]], the three official languages of Catalonia]]

The [[Catalan Government]] announced it planned to hold the referendum on 1 October 2017. The [[Electoral Commission of Catalonia]] was responsible for overseeing the referendum, but it was dissolved on 22 September 2017 after the Constitutional Court announced that otherwise they would be fined between €6,000 and €12,000 per day.<ref name="electoral-commission">{{cite news|last1=Jones|first1=Sam|title=Catalan campaigners hand out a million referendum ballots|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/sep/24/catalan-campaigners-hand-out-million-independence-referendum-ballots|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=24 September 2017}}</ref> The campaign was planned to last 15 days, spanning from 00:00 on 15 September 2017 to 24:00 on 29 September 2017.{{needs update|date=September 2017}}

According to the Catalan government, the following people were entitled to vote in the referendum:<ref name="vilaweb-guide">{{cite news |title=La guia per a votar al referèndum del primer d'octubre |url=https://www.vilaweb.cat/noticies/referendum-1-octubre-1o-votacio-cens-electoral-guia-meses-participacio/ |publisher=[[VilaWeb]] |date=1 October 2017 |access-date=11 April 2018 |language=ca}}</ref>
* Those who have the political condition of [[Catalans|Catalan]], are 18 years of age or older on the voting day, are not under any of the situations that legally deprive the right to vote and are on the electoral roll.
* Those Catalans currently residing abroad and who have their last residence in Catalonia, fulfil all the legal requirements, and have formally applied to take part in the voting process.

===International observers===
[[File:Sinn Féin representatives meeting Catalan President Carles Puigdemont ahead of the referendum.jpg|thumb|[[Sinn Féin]] representatives meeting Catalan President Carles Puigdemont ahead of the referendum.]]
The referendum was attended by several international observers. The first accredited international observers, led by The Hague Center for Strategic Studies, were headed by [[Daan Everts]] and consisted of 20 observers from the [[United States of America]], the [[United Kingdom]], the [[Netherlands]], [[France]] and [[Poland]], among others. The second international mission, called the International Electoral Expert Research Team, was headed by [[Helena Catt]] and consisted of 17 observers from the United Kingdom, France, [[Ireland]] and [[New Zealand]], among others. There was also a delegation of 33 parliamentarians and politicians, called the International Parliamentary Delegation on Catalonia's Referendum on Self-Determination 1 October 2017, from political parties in [[Slovakia]], [[Belgium]], [[Denmark]], [[Estonia]], [[Finland]], France, [[Germany]], [[Greece]], [[Iceland]], Ireland, [[Israel]], [[Latvia]], [[Republic of Macedonia]], [[Monaco]], Spain, [[Sweden]] and United Kingdom.

Of these international observers, those summoned by Diplocat are not recognised by the UN as such in not recognising this Diplocat as an international observer.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://politica.elpais.com/politica/2017/07/25/actualidad/1501008212_310224.html|title=La ONU frena el intento de dar aval internacional al referéndum catalán |access-date= 30 September 2017 |language= es |newspaper=[[El País]] |date=26 July 2017|last1=González |first1=Miguel}}</ref>

Due to some alleged irregularities and to the actions taken by the national police and civil guard, the international observers, invited by the Generalitat, declared that the referendum results could not be considered valid as the process failed to meet the minimum international standards for elections. They also criticized the police violence.<ref name="observadores" /><ref name="regulations" /><ref name="mediaciones" />

==Opinion polls==
===On 1 October 2017 referendum===
[[File:Catalan referendum Oct 1 2017 Angeleta.jpg|thumb|Queues of voters at polling stations for the referendum]]
[[File:Referèndum 1-O, Barcelona - Guinardó.jpg|thumb|A person voting in [[Guinardó]], Barcelona]]
Pollsters generally started using the proposed referendum question ("Do you want Catalonia to be an independent state in the form of a republic?") after it was revealed in early June 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=Tensions grow in Spain as Catalonia independence referendum confirmed|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/06/09/tensions-grow-spain-catalonia-independence-referendum-confirmed/|access-date=10 July 2017|work=The Daily Telegraph|date=9 June 2017}}</ref>

The Centre for Opinion Studies (''Centre d'Estudis d'Opinió'', CEO) polled respondents on their intentions rather than asking them the actual referendum question. In its March 2017 poll, aside from asking respondents whether they would want Catalonia to become an independent state, it asked their intents in the event of a referendum on the independence of Catalonia being called and organised by the Government of Catalonia without agreement from the Spanish Government. In a July 2017 poll a similar question was proposed, with the difference that it asked about the actual 1 October referendum.

=== Alleged irregularities ===
Due in part to the deactivation and repeated blocking by the police of the computer programs used to implement universal census and result reporting,<ref>{{cite news|title=La Guardia Civil gana la guerra tecnológica al tumbar el 'censo universal'|url=http://www.diariodeleon.es/noticias/espana/guardia-civil-gana-guerra-tecnologica-tumbar-censo-universal_1192194.html|access-date=11 October 2017|work=Diario de León|language=es}}</ref> some alleged irregularities were reported by Spanish media during the celebration of the referendum. Among them, people recorded voting more than once, votes made by non-Catalan people not included in the census or images of people voting several times, including journalists.<ref name="LaSexta01102017.2">{{cite web|title=Una periodista de laSexta comprueba cómo era posible votar varias veces en distintos colegios|url=http://www.lasexta.com/noticias/nacional/una-periodista-de-lasexta-comprueba-como-era-posible-votar-varias-veces-en-distintos-colegios_2017100159d143700cf20201565f8898.html|work=[[La Sexta]] |date=1 October 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref name="dabdfca8">{{cite news|title=Irregularidades en el referéndum: gente que vota cuatro veces, votos 'extranjeros'...|url=https://www.elconfidencial.com/espana/cataluna/2017-10-01/irregularidades-votaciones-referendum-cataluna_1453255/|publisher=[[El Confidencial]]|date=1 October 2017|language=es}}</ref> Other media reported the system did not validate the second attempt when voting, but it couldn't be confirmed that this system worked during the whole process.<ref name="ElPais03112017" /><ref name="NacioDigital04112017">{{Cite news|url=http://www.naciodigital.cat/noticia/139623/video/comproven/si/es/pot/votar/dues/vegades/al/referendum|title=VÍDEO Comproven si es pot votar dues vegades al referèndum|publisher=NacióDigital|access-date=2017-11-04|language=ca}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rac1.cat/info-rac1/20171001/431709827961/oriol-de-balanzo-de-la-competencia-prova-de-votar-dos-cops.html|title=Oriol de Balanzó, de 'La competència', intenta votar dos cops|publisher=RAC1|access-date=2018-07-04|language=ca}}</ref>

The Catalan government was not allowed to use the same ballot boxes used in other elections and referendums because they are owned by the Spanish government, so different ballot boxes were used,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.naciodigital.cat/noticia/139404/tsjc/ordena/policies/locals/vigilar/urnes/estat|title=El TSJC ordena a les policies locals vigilar les urnes de l'Estat|date=29 September 2017|website=Nació Digital|language=ca|access-date=11 May 2018}}</ref> those were described as translucent by some media and opaque by some others in contrast to the transparent ballot boxes used in elections.<ref name="regulations" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://es.reuters.com/article/topNews/idESKCN1C610N-OESTP|title=El Gobierno catalán anuncia un censo universal para facilitar la votación|date=1 October 2017|work=Reuters|access-date=11 May 2018|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rac1.cat/programes/el-mon/20170929/431633296099/ja-sabem-com-son-les-urnes-del-referendum.html|title=Ja sabem com són les urnes del referèndum|date=29 September 2017|work=RAC1|access-date=11 May 2018|language=ca}}</ref> Ballots and ballot boxes were transported together, which according to Spanish media raised doubts about whether those ballots were removed or not prior to the vote.<ref name="756e1b4f">{{cite news|title=La caída al suelo de una bolsa con una urna y papeletas de votación crea debate en las redes|url=http://www.europapress.es/nacional/noticia-caida-suelo-bolsa-urna-papeletas-votacion-crea-debate-redes-20171001123440.html|publisher=[[Europa Press (news agency)|Europa Press]]|date=1 October 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref name="fakenewsara">{{Cite news|url=https://www.ara.cat/un-any-1-o/1-octubre-2017-referendum-jornada-procliu-noticies-falses_0_2097990184.html|title=1-O: una jornada procliu a les notícies falses|date=30 September 2018|work=Diari Ara|access-date=7 October 2018|language=ca}}</ref> Another controversial footage shows ballot boxes placed in the street, where any person could submit their vote without census control.<ref name="razón">{{cite web|url=https://www.larazon.es/espana/urnas-opacas-llenas-de-papeletas-llegan-a-los-colegios-electorales-BM16381303|title=Así es el referéndum: voto en plena calle y urnas que llegan llenas a los colegios|date=October 2017|publisher=La Razón}}</ref><ref name="4710d3b0">{{cite news|title=Decenas de personas votan en la calle sin ningún tipo de control 3]]|language=es}}</ref> According to a Catalan newspaper, a volunteer declared that it was "a symbolic vote" in Ramon i Cajal school, Barcelona, after Spanish police removed the ballot boxes at another nearby polling station.<ref name="fakenewsara" />

There was no electoral board as it dissolved itself on 22 September to avoid being fined by the Constitutional Court and the counting system was blocked by the Guardia civil following orders from the Catalan High Court Justice.<ref name="regulations" /><ref name="electoral-commission" /> Guardia Civil also shut down a [[WordPress.com]] blog which alleged to be used as a voting system in the referendum, the Catalan government said they didn't know about its existence.<ref name="regulations" /><ref>{{cite news|title=La Guardia Civil cierra un blog para votar telemáticamente sin controlar los datos|url=https://www.elconfidencial.com/espana/cataluna/2017-10-01/referendum-cataluna-sistema-informatico-censo-universal_1452929/|work=[[El Confidencial]]|date=1 October 2017|access-date=9 November 2017}}</ref>

The Spanish Government denounced that the rules of the referendum that were changed 45 minutes before opening.<ref name="a4b7381e">{{cite news|title=REFERÉNDUM DEL 1-O El Gobierno español da por "desbaratado" el referéndum tras el cambio de las normas a 45 minutos de la votación|url=http://www.deia.com/2017/10/01/politica/estado/el-gobierno-da-por-desbaratado-el-referendum-tras-el-cambio-de-las-normas-a-45-minutos-de-la-votacion-|publisher=Deia|date=1 October 2017|language=es}}</ref> The new rules included the universal census according to which any citizen could vote in any voting center even if it was not the one originally assigned. They also accepted the use of non-official ballots printed at home and made optional the requirement of using envelopes.<ref name="regulations" />

=== Controversy over the results ===
The publication of the results generated controversy both for the lack of basic electoral warranties,<ref name="sin-ratificar">{{cite news|title=El Govern proclama los resultados del referéndum saltándose la ley aprobada en el Parlament|url=http://www.eldiario.es/catalunya/politica/Govern-resultados-referendum-saltandose-Parlament_0_694331311.html|publisher=[[Eldiario.es]]|date=6 October 2017|language=es}}</ref> as for the lack of coherence between the results that were published after 95% of the votes had been tallied and the official results published five days later.<ref>{{cite news|title=Los resultados del referéndum ilegal publicados por el Govern no cuadran|url=http://cadenaser.com/emisora/2017/10/06/sercat/1507302340_737104.html|publisher=SER Catalunya|date=6 October 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=¿Por qué no cuadran los resultados del referéndum del 1-O?|url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/politica/20171006/resultados-referendum-cataluna-diferencias-6337288|publisher=El Periódico|date=6 October 2017|language=es}}</ref>

One analyst said that the large pro-independence vote (90% by official estimates) could actually be a sign that many people did not vote at all, and that the referendum lacked the conditions for fairness.<ref name="PBS NewsHour">{{Cite news|url=http://www.pbs.org/newshour/updates/happened-catalonias-vote-independence-whats-next/|title=What happened with Catalonia's vote for independence -- and what's next|last=Epatko|first=Larisa|date=4 October 2017|work=[[PBS NewsHour]]|access-date=4 October 2017}}</ref>

In 71 municipalities the number of "yes" votes tallied were more than the number of registered voters for those municipalities, which could be partly explained by the "universal census" system introduced earlier in the day allowing people to vote in a different poll station than the one they were assigned.<ref name="ElPais03112017" />

The [[Civil Guard (Spain)|Civil Guard]] delivered a report to the [[Audiencia Nacional (Spain)|Spanish High Court]] with recordings of conversations that allegedly demonstrate that "the results of the referendum were decided in the days leading up to its holding".<ref>{{cite news|title=La Guardia Civil presenta grabaciones que demuestran que el resultado del 1-O estaba decidido previamente|url=http://www.expansion.com/economia/politica/2017/10/12/59df279446163f82308b4663.html|work=[[Expansión (Spanish newspaper)|Expansión]]|date=12 October 2017|access-date=9 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=La estrategia del 1-O: "Si votan dos millones, se tiene que decir que habrían ido tres, pero nos boicotearon"|url=http://www.elmundo.es/espana/2017/10/12/59dfa9ebe2704e82608b464c.html|work=[[El Mundo (Spain)|El Mundo]]|date=12 October 2017|access-date=9 November 2017}}</ref>

In the elections of December 2017 called by Spanish Prime Minister, parties supporting independence got 47.5% of the votes, but due to the high level of participation that was 33,970 more votes than 'Yes' votes were cast during the referendum.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nuvol.com/noticies/els-escacs-i-les-llegendes-electorals-del-21-d/|title=Els escacs i les llegendes electorals del 21-D|last=Vicenç|first=Josep|date=6 January 2018|work=El Núvol|access-date=13 May 2018|last2=Eres|language=ca}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
{{main|Reactions to the 2017 Catalan independence referendum}}
{{see also|2017 Catalan general strike}}
[[File:Demonstration in front of the headquarters of the Spanish National Police in Barcelona.png|thumb|[[2017 Catalan general strike]] against police brutality]]

On 3 October 2017, Carles Puigdemont said that his government intends to act on the result of the referendum "at the end of this week or the beginning of next" and declare independence from Spain. Puigdemont would go before the Catalan Parliament to address them on Monday 9 October 2017, pending the agreement of other political parties.<ref>{{cite web|last1=McKirdy|first1=Euan|last2=Vasco|first2=Cotovio|last3=Smith-Spark|first3=laura|title=Catalonia crisis deepens as independence declaration looms|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2017/10/04/europe/catalonia-independence-puigdemont-king-felipe/index.html|website=CNN.com|publisher=CNN|access-date=4 October 2017}}</ref>

On 4 October 2017, [[Mireia Boya Busquet|Mireia Boya]], a lawmaker of the Popular Unity Candidacy (CUP), announced that a declaration of independence would likely come after the parliamentary session on 9 October.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Berwick|first1=Angus|last2=Dowsett|first2=Sonya|title=Catalonia moves to declare independence from Spain on Monday|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-spain-politics-catalonia/catalonia-moves-to-declare-independence-from-spain-on-monday-idUSKCN1C9109|website=Reuters.com|publisher=Reuters|access-date=4 October 2017}}</ref>

The King of Spain, [[Felipe VI]], called the Catalan referendum "illegal" and appealed to the union [of Spain], calling the situation "extremely serious".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.dn.pt/mundo/interior/catalunha-vai-declarar-a-independencia-dentro-de-dias-8818663.html |title= Catalunha vai declarar a independência "dentro de dias", diz Puigdemont |language= pt |publisher=[[Diário de Notícias]] |date=3 October 2017 |access-date= 4 October 2017}}</ref>

According to [[Switzerland|Swiss]] national radio, the Foreign Ministry of Switzerland has offered to mediate between the two sides in the crisis.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/10/06/world/europe/catalonia-referendum-sedition.html?rref=collection%2Fsectioncollection%2Fworld&action=click&contentCollection=world&region=rank&module=package&version=highlights&contentPlacement=2&pgtype=sectionfront|title=Catalonia Government Declares Overwhelming Vote for Independence|author=Rafael Minder|date=6 October 2017|access-date=6 October 2017}}</ref> However, on 16 October 2017 the Foreign Ministry of Switzerland released a press note declaring that no formal offer was made, also stating that the independent aspirations in Catalonia are an internal affair of Spain and should be resolved within its constitutional order. It also made clear that Switzerland fully respects Spanish Sovereignty and that in any case any facilitation of the process could only take place in case that both sides requested it.<ref>{{cite news|title=Suiza rechaza cualquier papel mediador en Cataluña|url=http://www.abc.es/espana/abci-suiza-rechaza-cualquier-papel-mediador-cataluna-201710161738_noticia.html|work=[[ABC (newspaper)|ABC]]|date=16 October 2017|language=es-ES}}</ref>

===Violence and injuries===

According to the final report by the Catalan Health Service (CatSalut) of the Generalitat, there were 1,066 people attended by the Catalan hospitals in connection with the Catalan Referendum: 966 on 1 October 2017 and 75 during 2–4 October.
According to the severity, the reports indicates that 886 (=823+63) were categorised as mild, 173 (=163+10) as moderate and 7 (=5+2) as severe. On 20 October 2017, the last injured person left the hospital.
Regarding the age distribution: 10,4% of them were elderly people (>65 years old) and 23 of them were over 79 years old. Also, 2.1% were underage, including 2 children under 11 years old.

On 19 February 2018, in the hearing for the injuries in the village of Castellgalí, a Guardia Civil policeman testified before the judge that he only found passive resistance, thus contradicting a Spanish police statement, which claimed that violence, kicks and spitting took place at that location.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sallent|first1=Josep|title=Un guàrdia civil de l'1-O a Castellgalí contradiu l'atestat i admet la resistència passiva dels veïns|url=http://www.regio7.cat/fet-divers/2018/02/19/guardia-civil-l1-castellgali-contradiu/460928.html|access-date=20 February 2018|date=20 February 2018|publisher=Regió7|language=ca}}</ref>

According to a 2020 study, the crackdown by the Spanish state on Catalan activists "increased public sympathy for independence for a short period, and heightened animosity towards actors perceived to be associated or complicit with the Spanish state."<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Balcells|first=Laia|last2=Dorsey|first2=Spencer|last3=Tellez|first3=Juan F.|date=2020|title=Repression and Dissent in Contemporary Catalonia|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/british-journal-of-political-science/article/repression-and-dissent-in-contemporary-catalonia/2C3AD879C0F86AC7E568A3C3A855B1D7|journal=British Journal of Political Science|language=en|pages=1–9|doi=10.1017/S0007123420000307|issn=0007-1234}}</ref> 

===Economic effects===
{{Outdated section|date=October 2017}}
As of August 2017 the [[credit spread (bond)|spread]] between Spanish 10-year government debt and German bonds was close to its narrowest in seven years; however, since the start of July the yield on the Catalan regional government's bonds had jumped by about 50 basis points,<ref name="bloombergthink2">{{cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-08-09/catalans-think-twice-about-risks-of-rupture-as-good-life-returns|title=Catalans Think Twice About Risks of Rupture as Jobs Return|first=Esteban|last=Duarte|website=bloomberg.com|access-date=9 August 2017}}</ref> signaling unease among investors in regards to the referendum issue.

[[Stratfor]] suggested financial market disruption is due to the political upheaval. Predrag Dukic, senior equity sales trader at CM Capital Markets Bolsa, wrote: "The independence movement seeks to paralyze the region with strikes, disobedience, etc., a nightmare scenario for what until yesterday seemed a strong Spanish economic recovery." Markus Schomer, chief global economist at [[Pacific Century Group|PineBridge Investments]], suggested that the uncertainty both in and outside of Spain has made it hard to price the scenarios into final markets so far. Further he commented a strong approval could result in a [[euro]] &minus;0.0255% sell off, just as in the aftermath of the [[2017 German federal election|German federal election the previous week]]. "I don't think there is an immediate change coming from that referendum. It'll take quite a bit longer to assess where this is going and what this will mean, how the EU will react, how the Spanish government will react. So I don't think you'll see people adjusting their portfolios on Monday, but you could get the classic knee-jerk, risk-off reaction."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marketwatch.com/story/5-things-investors-need-to-know-about-catalonias-independence-referendum-2017-09-28|title=5 things investors need to know about Catalonia's independence referendum|first=Barbara|last=Kollmeyer|access-date=30 September 2017}}</ref>

===Political effect===
{{Further| Catalan declaration of independence | 2017 Spanish constitutional crisis}}
[[File:20171010 proclamacio independencia 4694.jpg|thumb|Pro-independence supporters during a rally in Barcelona, 10 October 2017]]
[[File:Carles Puigdemont el 10 d'octubre de 2017.jpg|thumb|Catalan President [[Carles Puigdemont]] gives a speech at the [[Parliament of Catalonia|Catalan Parliament]] in Barcelona on 10 October 2017]]

On 10 October in a speech in front of the Catalan parliament Puigdemont stated that he considered the referendum results to be valid and in consequence used the following wording: "I assume the mandate of the people for Catalonia to become an independent state in the shape of a republic", before adding that he would "ask Parliament to suspend the effects of the declaration of independence". In response the Central government made a formal request for him to answer before the Monday 16 October 2017 if he declared independence asking specifically for yes or no answer clarifying that any answer different than a "no" would be interpreted as a "yes".<ref>{{cite news|last1=Baquero|first1=Camilo S.|title=La CUP exige a Puigdemont que responda "nítidamente" que declaró la independencia|url=https://elpais.com/ccaa/2017/10/14/catalunya/1507982674_623828.html|work=[[El País]]|date=14 October 2017|language=es}}</ref> Along with the formal request there was also an offer from the central government negotiated with the [[Spanish Socialist Workers' Party]] to evaluate the situation of Catalonia within Spain and to study possible reforms, if needed, to the Spanish Constitution.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.lavanguardia.com/politica/20171011/431980233564/pedro-sanchez-rajoy-requerimiento-declaracion-de-independenciac.html|title=Sánchez acuerda con Rajoy abordar la reforma de la Constitución a cambio de apoyarle|author=Juan Carlos Merino, Luis B. García|publisher=[[La Vanguardia]]|date=11 October 2017}}</ref> On 16 October 2017 Puigdemont gave a response that did not address the issue of whether or not there had been a declaration of independence.<ref>{{cite news|title=Catalan president doesn't clarify whether he declared independence|url=http://www.politico.eu/article/catalan-president-doesnt-clarify-whether-he-declared-independence/|work=[[Politico]]|date=16 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Minder|first1=Raphael|title=Catalonia's Leader, Facing Deadline, Won't Say if Region Declared Independence|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/10/16/world/europe/catalonia-spain-independence.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=16 October 2017}}</ref> this triggered a second deadline of 10 am on Thursday 19 October for them to backtrack before direct rule was imposed.<ref>{{cite news|last=Jones|first=Sam|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/oct/16/catalan-president-carles-puigdemont-ignores-madrids-ultimatum|title=Catalan president Carles Puigdemont ignores Madrid's ultimatum|date=16 October 2017|work=The Guardian|location=Madrid|access-date=16 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Couzens|first=Gerard|url=http://www.express.co.uk/news/world/866931/Catalonia-latest-news-independence-Puigdemont-Spain-direct-rule|title=Furious Spain sets Catalonia new deadline as it REFUSES to confirm independence|date=16 October 2017|work=Daily Express|access-date=16 October 2017}}</ref> The Spanish government subsequently offered to abort the suspension of self-rule if the Catalan government called for regional elections.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elconfidencial.com/espana/cataluna/2017-10-18/independencia-cataluna-gobierno-rajoy-carles-puigdemont-elecciones_1462694/|title=El Gobierno podría frenar el artículo 155 en caso de que Puigdemont convoque elecciones. Noticias de Cataluña}}</ref> The response from Puigdemont to the second deadline was again not clear. Since he refused to abandon his independence push, on 21 October the Spanish government initiated the implementation of article 155 of the Spanish constitution.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Jones|first1=Sam|title=Spain to impose direct rule as Catalonia leader refuses to back down|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/oct/18/spain-direct-rule-catalonia-deadline-direct-rule|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=19 October 2017}}</ref>

On 27 October 2017, the [[Parliament of Catalonia]] unilaterally [[Declaration of Independence of Catalonia|declared independence]] from Spain. The proposal presented by the pro-independence political parties [[Junts pel Sí]] and [[Popular Unity Candidacy]] was approved with 70 votes in favor 10 against and 2 blank votes.<ref>{{cite news|title=Catalan independence crisis escalates|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-41780116|work=[[BBC News]]|date=27 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Catalan Parliament Votes for Independence From Spain|url=https://www.nbcwashington.com/news/national-international/Spain-Catalonia-Secession-Vote-453568293.html|work=NBC4 Washington}}</ref> 55 MPs from the opposition refused to be present during the voting after the legal services of the Catalan Parliament advised that the voting could not take place as the law in which it was based had been suspended by the Constitutional Court.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Piñol|first1=Pere Ríos, Àngels|title=El Parlament de Cataluña aprueba la resolución para declarar la independencia|url=https://elpais.com/ccaa/2017/10/27/catalunya/1509105810_557081.html|work=[[El País]]|date=27 October 2017|language=es}}</ref> Within hours, the Spanish Senate approved actions proposed by the Spanish government to invoke Article 155 and assume direct control over some of Catalonia's autonomous powers.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Booth|first1=William|last2=Rolfe|first2=Pamela|title=Spain to impose direct rule over Catalonia after region declares independence|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/a-day-of-fireworks-in-catalonia-both-spanish-and-catalan-parliaments-are-scheduled-to-convene/2017/10/27/09685d34-ba90-11e7-9b93-b97043e57a22_story.html|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=27 October 2017}}</ref> The measure was passed with 214 votes in favour, 47 against and 1 abstention. The measure is intended to be temporary; its claimed objective being to "re-establish the rule of law" and restore autonomy after new elections.<ref>{{cite news|title=Catalonia just declared independence from Spain|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/spain-article-155-catalonia-independence-referendum-2017-10|work=[[Business Insider]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Spain's Senate approves powers to remove Catalan government|url=https://www.thelocal.es/20171027/breaking-spains-senate-approves-powers-to-dissolve-catalan-government|date=27 October 2017}}</ref> The first measures taken by Spanish Prime Minister [[Mariano Rajoy]] after the approval by the senate was to fire the Catalan President [[Carles Puigdemont]] and his cabinet, dissolving the [[Parliament of Catalonia]] and scheduling fresh [[2017 Catalan regional election|Catalan elections on 21 December 2017]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Spanish PM dissolves Catalan parliament|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-41783289|work=[[BBC News]]|date=27 October 2017}}</ref>

On 16 January 2018, the Spanish Constitutional Court issued a temporary restraining order regarding the work of the Catalan Government commission that investigates the violation of fundamental rights in Catalonia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/politica/20180116/4455957636/tribunal-constitucional-suspende-comision-generalitat-violacion-derechos-referendum-1o.html|title=El TC suspende la comisión de la Generalitat para investigar la violación de derechos el 1-O|date=16 January 2018|website=La Vanguardia|access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref>

==Press coverage and Internet==
Different sources describe aspects of the events differently according to their political stance. Notable examples include the coverage of Spanish TV channel TVE ([[Televisión Española]]) and Catalan channel [[TV3 (Catalonia)|TV3]]. TV3 covered the referendum and the police violence regularly while TVE mainly emphasised the Spanish government's position on its legality.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-41517569 |title=Catalonia media reflects polarised Spanish society |publisher=BBC News |date=6 October 2017 |access-date=8 October 2017}}</ref> TVE media coverage was criticised by Televisión Española information council, calling for the resignation of the entire direction.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.publico.es/actualidad/consejo-informativos-tve-pide-dimision-inmediata-direccion-cobertura-1.html |title=El Consejo de Informativos de TVE pide la "dimisión inmediata" de la dirección por la cobertura del 1-O |publisher=Diario Público |date=2 October 2017 |access-date=8 October 2017}}</ref> TV3 has been criticized both within and outside Catalonia as a mouthpiece for the independence movement.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elmundo.es/cataluna/2016/11/05/581e1dea46163f45028b4570.html|title=Denuncian la "manipulación" de TV3 y Catalunya Ràdio en la detención de la alcaldesa de Berga}}</ref>

Various media reported on fake images of police violence against civilians that were posted in social networks.<ref name="TheGuardian08102017">{{cite web|url=https://amp.theguardian.com/world/2017/oct/08/catalonia-demo-injuries-fact-checking|last=Preston|first=Peter|title=Violence in Catalonia needed closer scrutiny in age of fake news|date=8 October 2017|work=The Guardian|access-date=20 October 2017|quote=With fake news apparently leaking from every media orifice, you'd have hoped for a little more diligence over those Catalan referendum demonstration figures and images. Over to the fact-checkers … (...) The reporting of what happened – including the detail of those 893 injured voters – hadn't been independently checked. }}</ref> They included images of people injured in other events, including footage from strikes and anti-austerity protests 4–5 years prior, Turkish police charges and protests by miners.<ref name="LeMonde02102017"/><ref name="ElMundo02102017.2"/><ref name="ElMundo04102017" /> The news sites ''[[Okdiario]]'' and ''Periodista Digital'' were also accused of falsely trying to discredit some of those involved in police violence episodes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eldiario.es/catalunya/mujer-llevo-policia-volandas-Otegi_0_693981008.html|title=No, la mujer a la que se llevó la Guardia Civil en volandas no se hizo una foto con Otegi|date=3 October 2017|publisher=[[El Confidencial]]|access-date=5 October 2017|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.publico.es/tremending/2017/10/05/la-prensa-seria-el-bulo-de-okdiario-y-periodista-digital-sobre-la-pacifica-abuelita-del-1-o/|title=El bulo de 'Okdiario' y 'Periodista Digital' sobre la "pacífica abuelita" del 1-O|date=5 October 2017|publisher=[[Público (Spain)|Público]]|access-date=6 October 2017|language=es}}</ref>

Some non-Spanish media outlets have criticized the [[Mariano Rajoy]]'s government, [[Police brutality|police violence]] against civilians or Spain's media coverage, including ''[[The Guardian]]'',<ref>"[https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/oct/02/ripples-from-catalan-referendum-could-extend-beyond-spain Ripples from Catalan referendum could extend beyond Spain]". ''[[The Guardian]]''. 2 October 2017.</ref> ''[[The Independent]]'',<ref>"[https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/editorials/catalan-catalonia-independence-referendum-make-the-question-harder-a7976946.html Police brutality has made any long-term solution to the Catalan question increasingly difficult]". ''The Independent''. 1 October 2017.</ref> ''[[Al-Jazeera]]'',<ref>"[http://www.aljazeera.com/programmes/listeningpost/2017/10/spain-media-spin-catalonia-171008112952501.html Spain's media spin on Catalonia]". ''Al-Jazeera''. 10 October 2017.</ref> ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'',<ref>"[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/10/02/european-commission-refuses-condemn-spanish-police-violence/ European Commission refuses to condemn Spanish police violence in Catalonia as UN demands investigation ]". ''The Daily Telegraph''. 2 October 2017.</ref> and ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>"[https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2017/10/02/world/europe/ap-eu-spain-catalonia-analysis.html Analysis: Spanish police attacks— mistake or part of a plan?]". ''The New York Times''. 2 October 2017.</ref>

The Spanish newspaper ''[[El País]]'' argued that "the network of fake-news producers that Russia has employed to weaken the United States and the European Union is now operating at full speed on Catalonia",<ref>{{cite news|url=https://politica.elpais.com/politica/2017/09/22/actualidad/1506101626_670033.html|title=La maquinaria de injerencias rusa penetra la crisis catalane|date=22 September 2017|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref> involving a network of Russian media outlets and social network bots which, according to the argument, aimed to influence local and global discussion of events. Later investigations by Medium-DFRL said it found support for some but not all of the arguments made by Spanish outlets.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://medium.com/dfrlab/electionwatch-russia-and-referendums-in-catalonia-192743efcd76|title=#ElectionWatch: Russia and Referendums in Catalonia?|agency=Medium Digital Forensics Research Lab (DFRL)|date=28 September 2017|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref> It is argued that the goal wasn't specifically to support Catalan independence but to "foment divisions to gradually undermine Europe's democracy and institutions"<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.voanews.com/a/analysts-russia-may-be-helping-catalonia-secessionists/4050196.html|title=Analysts: Russia May Be Helping Catalonia Secessionists|date=29 September 2017|agency=VOA|access-date=5 October 2017|quote=""It's not that Russia necessarily wants the independence of Catalonia. What it's principally seeking is to foment divisions to gradually undermine Europe's democracy and institutions," said Brett Schaffer, an analyst of the Alliance to Safeguard Democracy, a project supported by the German Marshall Fund, which monitors pro-Kremlin information networks. "}}</ref> and at discrediting Spanish legal and political authorities,<ref name=Politico>{{cite news|url=http://www.politico.eu/article/russia-catalonia-referendum-fake-news-misinformation/|title=Catalan referendum stokes fears of Russian influence|date=30 September 2017|agency=Politico|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref> while Russian authorities have denied that Russian actors had any involvement.<ref name=Politico/>

== In popular culture ==
[[File:Matthias Laurenz Gräff, "Katalanisches Tryptichon".jpg|thumb|200px|''Katalanisches Tryptichon'', painted by [[Matthias Laurenz Gräff]]]]
Several documentaries have been made about the day of the referendum, the most important ones being {{Interlanguage link|1-O (documentary)|lt=''1-O''|ca|1-O (documental)}} produced by [[Mediapro]] and ''El primer dia d'octubre'' produced by [[La Directa]] and released for free in YouTube.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rac1.cat/info-rac1/20180105/434073791735/dos-documentals-recuperen-els-fets-de-l1-o.html|title=Dos documentals recuperen els fets de l'1-O|date=10 January 2018|work=RAC 1|access-date=16 June 2018|language=ca}}</ref><ref>{{Cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K6i69o2ANJg|title=El primer dia d'octubre|date=13 November 2017|type=Television production|publisher=La Directa}}</ref>

Many books about the topic were published as well, including ''Operació urnes'' ("''Operation Ballot Boxes''"), explaining how the ballot boxes were distributed around Catalonia, and ''Dies que duraran anys'' ("''Days That Will Last for Years''"), a photo book by [[Jordi Borràs]]. Those two being the first and the third top-sellers in the category of non-fiction in Catalan language during the [[Diada de Sant Jordi]] of 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://beteve.cat/sant-jordi-2018-llibres-mes-venuts/|title=Els llibres més venuts del Sant Jordi 2018: Gironell i Dueñas, al capdavant|last=Segura|first=Sergio|date=23 April 2018|work=betevé|access-date=16 June 2018|language=ca}}</ref>

Several Catalan groups also composed songs specially dedicated to that day, including ''Agafant l'horitzó'' (''Catching the horizon'') by [[Txarango]] and ''Rojos y separatistas'' (''Red and separatists'') by [[Lágrimas de Sangre]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vilaweb.cat/noticies/sis-mesos-de-l1-o-deu-cancons-que-reivindiquen-el-referendum-dindependencia-de-catalunya/|title=Sis mesos de l'1-O: deu cançons que reivindiquen el referèndum d'independència de Catalunya|date=1 April 2018|work=VilaWeb|access-date=16 June 2018|language=ca}}</ref> Some international groups used images from the police violence during the day of the referendum in their concerts and music videos, including [[A Sound of Thunder (band)|A Sound of Thunder]] and [[Steven Patrick Morrissey]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.enderrock.cat/noticia/16169/sound/of/thunder/ja/videoclip/versio/segadors|title=A Sound of Thunder ja té videoclip per a la versió d'"Els Segadors"|last=Pujols|first=Marcel|date=4 January 2018|work=Enderrock|access-date=16 June 2018|language=ca}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.elnacional.cat/ca/politica/morrissey-referendum-policia_245433_102.html|title=Morrissey utilitza imatges de les càrregues de l'1-O a la seva gira mundial|date=6 March 2018|work=El Nacional|access-date=16 June 2018|language=ca}}</ref>

On 15 June, the exhibition ''55 Ballot Boxes for Freedom'' opened in Brussels, with artwork inspired by the ballot boxes of the Catalan referendum.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cataloniatoday.cat/article/1395726-brussel-hosts-ballot-boxes-for-freedom.html|title=Brussel hosts ballot boxes for freedom|date=18 May 2018|work=Catalonia Today|access-date=16 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://elmon.cat/politica/brusselles-inaugura-lexposicio-55-urnes-llibertat-sobre-l1|title=Brussel·les inaugura l'exposició "55 Urnes per la llibertat" sobre l'1-O|date=15 June 2018|work=El Món|access-date=16 June 2018|language=ca}}</ref>

[[Netflix]] used images from the police action during the day of the referendum to promote ''[[Black Mirror]]'' series in Spain.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.clarin.com/mundo/netflix-usa-referendum-cataluna-promocionar-cuarta-temporada-black-mirror_0_BkhI7oo4M.html|title=Netflix usa el referéndum de Cataluña para promocionar la cuarta temporada de Black Mirror|date=16 January 2018|work=Clarín|access-date=16 June 2018|language=es}}</ref>

== In 2020 ==
In February 2020, Catalan nationalists and Spanish leaders came to negotiate on Catalonia's political future. Included in Catalan nationalists' two points was a retroactive legalization of the 2017 independence referendum. The terms have not been accepted but it is the beginning of open negotiations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2020-02-26/spain-catalonia-independence-talks|title=Leaders of Spain and Catalonia open long-awaited talks on region's political future|last=Bernhard |first=Meg |date=26 February 2020 |website=Los Angeles Times|access-date=1 November 2020}}</ref> Fifteen representatives began talks with a discussion of the history of the Catalan crisis. Despite disagreement on when it began, the two sides concurred that the [[People's Party (Spain)|People's Party]] bore the overall responsibility in its fight against the Statute of Autonomy.<ref name=cue>{{cite web|url=https://english.elpais.com/politics/catalonia_independence/2020-02-28/catalan-talks-start-with-three-hours-of-therapy-about-the-origins-of-the-crisis.html |title=Catalan talks start with three hours of 'therapy' about the origins of the crisis |date=28 February 2020 |last=Cué |first=Carlos |work=El País |access-date=1 November 2020}}</ref> It was agreed that negotiations would continue, with plans for monthly meetings and plenary sessions every six months.<ref name=cue /> The [[COVID-19 pandemic]] cancelled these plans, but in September 2020, leaders discussed resuming negotiations.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.catalannews.com/politics/item/catalan-and-spanish-presidents-agree-on-preparing-meeting-between-governments |title=Catalan and Spanish presidents agree on preparing meeting between governments |website=Catalan News |date=7 September 2020 |access-date=1 November 2020}}</ref>
