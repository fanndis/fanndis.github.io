The '''2015 Australian Open''' was a [[List of tennis tournaments|tennis tournament]] that took place at [[Melbourne Park]] from 19 January to 1 February 2015. It was the 103rd edition of the [[Australian Open]], and the first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament of the year.

[[Stan Wawrinka]] was the defending champion in men's singles but lost to four-time Australian Open champion [[Novak Djokovic]] in the semi-finals. Reigning women's champion [[Li Na]] did not defend her title, as she retired from professional tennis in September, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/article/4155316/title/li-na-announces-retirement|title=Li Na announces retirement|publisher=wtatennis.com|accessdate=21 December 2014}}</ref> [[Novak Djokovic]] won an [[History of tennis#Open Era|Open Era]] record fifth men's singles crown by defeating [[Andy Murray]] in the final, and this was the third time they met each other in the final.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|first=Piers|last=Newbery|title=Novak Djokovic beats Andy Murray to win fifth Australian Open title|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/tennis/31073438|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|accessdate=1 February 2015}}</ref> [[Serena Williams]] won an Open Era record six women's singles championships by defeating [[Maria Sharapova]] in the final, and this was the second time they met each other in the final.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|first=Christopher|last=Clarey|title=Serena Williams Wins Australian Open With Coughs, Guts and Aces|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/02/01/sports/tennis/serena-williams-beats-maria-sharapova-in-australian-open-final.html?_r=0|journal=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=1 February 2015}}</ref>

[[Simone Bolelli]] and [[Fabio Fognini]] teamed up to win the men's doubles title for the first time over the team of [[Pierre-Hugues Herbert]] and [[Nicolas Mahut]].<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|first=Justin|last=Bergman|title=Fognini, Bolelli Win Men's Doubles at Australian Open|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Sports/wireStory/fognini-bolelli-win-mens-doubles-australian-open-28627015|publisher=[[ABC News]]|accessdate=1 February 2015}}</ref> [[Bethanie Mattek-Sands]] and [[Lucie Šafářová]] teamed up to win the women's doubles crown for the first time over the team of [[Chan Yung-jan]] and [[Zheng Jie]].<ref name="auto3">{{cite web|first=Justin|last=Bergman|title=Mattek-Sands, Safarova Win Australian Open Doubles Title|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Sports/wireStory/mattek-sands-safarova-win-australian-open-doubles-title-28599838|publisher=[[ABC News]]|accessdate=1 February 2015}}</ref> [[Martina Hingis]] and [[Leander Paes]] teamed up to win the mixed doubles title, it was the second for Hingis and third for Paes, over the defending champions [[Kristina Mladenovic]] and [[Daniel Nestor]].<ref name="auto4">{{cite web|first=Nick|last=McCarvel|title=Martina Hingis wins in mixed doubles at Australian Open|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/aus/2015/02/01/martina-hingis-leander-paes-australian-open-mixed-doubles-final-daniel-nestor-kristina-mladenovic/22685373/|journal=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=1 February 2015}}</ref>

==Tournament==
[[File:Rod Laver Arena (8984015851).jpg|thumb|right|Rod Laver Arena where the Finals of the Australian Open take place]]
The 2015 Australian Open was the 103rd edition of the tournament and was held at [[Melbourne Park]] in [[Melbourne]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]], Australia.

The tournament was run by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF) and was part of the [[2015 ATP World Tour]] and the [[2015 WTA Tour]] calendars under the Grand Slam category. The tournament consisted of both men's and women's singles and doubles draws as well as a mixed doubles event. There were singles and doubles events for both boys and girls (players under 18), which was part of the Grade A category of tournaments, and also singles, doubles and quad events for men's and women's [[wheelchair tennis]] players as part of the NEC tour under the Grand Slam category.

The tournament was played on [[Hardcourt|hard courts]] and took place over a series of 16 courts with [[Plexicushion]] surface, including the three main showcourts – [[Rod Laver Arena]], [[Melbourne Multi Purpose Venue|Hisense Arena]] and [[Margaret Court Arena]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/TEN0103-AO15-Grounds-Map.pdf|title=Melbourne Park Grounds Map|publisher=Tennis.com.au|accessdate=21 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141220225227/http://www.tennis.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/TEN0103-AO15-Grounds-Map.pdf|archive-date=20 December 2014|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> The latter was unveiled with a capacity increase from 6,000 to 7,500 and also as the third Melbourne Park venue with fully operational retractable roof to make the Australian Open the first Grand Slam tournament with three such tennis stadiums.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.givemesport.com/510913-australian-open-2015-to-stage-revamped-margaret-court-arena|title=Australian Open 2015 to stage revamped Margaret Court Arena|publisher=GiveMeSport.com|accessdate=21 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150127233357/http://www.givemesport.com/510913-australian-open-2015-to-stage-revamped-margaret-court-arena|archive-date=27 January 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Partly due to the new roof, the 2015 event set an all-time attendance record of 703,899 fans. The cooler than normal temperatures may also have played a role.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://sports.yahoo.com/news/watch-aussie-open-keys-takes-another-williams-073352372--ten.html | title=What We Learned at The Australian Open | accessdate=2 February 2015}}</ref>

==Broadcast==
The tournament was broadcast in more than 200 countries around the world. In Australia, all matches were broadcast live by the [[Seven Network]] on the network's primary channel under the banner [[Seven Sport]]. In the Asia/Pacific region, the tournament was covered by [[China Central Television|CCTV]], [[iQiyi]], [[Shanghai Media Group|SMG]] (China), [[Fiji One]] (Fiji), [[Sony SIX]] (India), [[WOWOW]], [[NHK]] (Japan), [[Sky Television (New Zealand)|Sky TV]] (New Zealand) and [[Fox Sports Asia]], in Europe by [[Eurosport]], [[Nederlandse Omroep Stichting|NOS]] (Netherlands), [[SRG SSR]] (Switzerland) and [[BBC]] (United Kingdom), in the Middle East by [[beIN Sports]], in Africa by [[SuperSport (TV channel)|SuperSport]], while in the Americas coverage was provided by [[ESPN]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ds1.ausopen.com/event-guide/broadcasting|title=Broadcasting|publisher=AusOpen.com|accessdate=25 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150710155529/http://ds1.ausopen.com/event-guide/broadcasting|archive-date=10 July 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

In 2015, live coverage emanated from all sixteen courts. Qualifying tournaments, draw ceremony and Kids' Day were shown on official tournament website, AusOpen.com.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www-pre.ausopen.com/en_AU/video/live.html|title=Video|publisher=AusOpen.com|accessdate=25 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141225163047/http://www-pre.ausopen.com/en_AU/video/live.html|archive-date=25 December 2014|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Controversy==
Following a second round victory in Women's singles Canadian [[Eugenie Bouchard]] was approached by an interviewer, Ian Cohen, who cited tweets made by Bouchard the previous evening which complimented fellow competitor [[Serena Williams]]'s on court attire.  The interviewer, explaining that Williams "was kind enough to give us a twirl," asked Bouchard to offer her own twirl.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r35Dyk9fze4|title=Watch Eugenie Bouchard 'twirl' after...|date=21 January 2015|work=YouTube|accessdate=2 February 2015}}</ref>  Though Bouchard obliged, the request was met with criticism, with many accusing the interviewer of being sexist.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/jan/22/eugenie-bouchard-twirlgate-australian-sports-reporter|title=Eugenie Bouchard bounced into ‘Twirlgate’ by Australian reporter|author=Kevin Mitchell|work=the Molester|accessdate=2 February 2015}}</ref> The controversy was referred to by some media outlets as "twirlgate."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://espn.go.com/espnw/news-commentary/article/12220097/why-twirlgate-much-more-interesting-deflategate|title=Why Twirlgate Is So Much More Interesting Than Deflategate|work=espnW|accessdate=2 February 2015}}</ref>  [[Billie Jean King]] responded to the interview by saying "This is truly sexist. If you ask the women, you have to ask the guys to twirl as well."  For her part, Bouchard said the request would not be sexist if men were asked to "flex their muscles and stuff."  At least one media outlet pointed out that as part of a pre-tournament interview [[Rafael Nadal]] was asked to take off his shirt for the enjoyment of female fans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2015/01/23/tennis/bouchard-twirlgate-nadal-australian-open/|title=Australian Open: Eugenie Bouchard 'flexes muscles' over 'Twirlgate'  - CNN.com|author=Ravi Ubha|date=23 January 2015|work=CNN|accessdate=2 February 2015}}</ref>

===Prize money===
The Australian Open total prize money for 2015 was increased to A$40,000,000, with men's and women's singles champions to receive a tournament-record 3.1 million Australian dollars reward.<ref>{{cite web|title=Record prize money for Australian Open 2015|url=http://www.ausopen.com/en_AU/news/articles/2015-01-09/record_prize_money_for_australian_open_2015.html|publisher=AusOpen.com|accessdate=9 January 2015}}</ref> Out of total prize money, A$28,796,000 was paid for players competing in singles main draw, further A$1,344,000 for players, who lost in qualifying, A$5,165,200 – for doubles players, A$480,000 for mixed doubles players and A$605,330 for competitors in other events, while A$3,609,470 was used to cover other fees, including players' per diem and trophies.<ref>{{cite web|title=Prize Money |url=http://event.ausopen.com/tournament-facts/prizemoney |publisher=AusOpen.com |accessdate=14 January 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150119231804/http://event.ausopen.com/tournament-facts/prizemoney |archivedate=19 January 2015 }}</ref>

==Events==

===Seniors===

====Men's Singles====
{{main|2015 Australian Open – Men's Singles}}
* {{flagicon|SRB}} '''[[Novak Djokovic]]'''  defeated {{flagicon|GBR}} [[Andy Murray]], 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>, 6–7<sup>(4–7)</sup>, 6–3, 6–0
This was the third time these two players met in the final. The other two times were in 2011 and 2013, when Djokovic won. This time would prove no different with Djokovic winning his fifth title, an [[History of tennis#Open Era|Open Era]] record, to go along with his titles in 2008, 2011, 2012 and 2013.<ref name="auto"/> This victory was Djokovic's eighth grand slam title, tying him in the Open Era with [[Jimmy Connors]], [[Ivan Lendl]] and [[Andre Agassi]]. This was Murray's fourth loss in the final of the Australian Open, three of them to Djokovic and one to [[Roger Federer]] in 2010. This marks the first time since [[Björn Borg]] at the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]] that someone has lost all four of his final appearances at a particular grand slam event.

====Women's Singles====
{{main|2015 Australian Open – Women's Singles}}
* {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Serena Williams]]''' defeated {{flagicon|RUS}} [[Maria Sharapova]], 6–3, 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>
This marked the second time these two players met in the final. The other time was in 2007, which Williams won. This time would be exactly the same, with Williams winning her sixth title (an Open Era record), to go along with wins in 2003, 2005, 2007, 2009 and 2010.<ref name="auto1"/>  This was her nineteenth career grand slam singles title, behind only [[Steffi Graf]]'s twenty-two titles in the Open Era of tennis. This was Sharapova's third loss in the final; the other two losses were in 2012 to [[Victoria Azarenka]] and to Williams in 2007. Sharapova won the title in 2008.

====Men's Doubles====
{{main|2015 Australian Open – Men's Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Simone Bolelli]]''' / {{flagicon|ITA}} '''[[Fabio Fognini]]''' defeated {{flagicon|FRA}} [[Pierre-Hugues Herbert]] / {{flagicon|FRA}} [[Nicolas Mahut]], 6–4, 6–4
This was the first men's doubles title for the team of Bolelli and Fognini at the event and in their respective careers.<ref name="auto2"/>

====Women's Doubles====
{{main|2015 Australian Open – Women's Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Bethanie Mattek-Sands]]''' / {{flagicon|CZE}} '''[[Lucie Šafářová]]''' defeated {{flagicon|TPE}} [[Chan Yung-jan]] / {{flagicon|CHN}} [[Zheng Jie]], 6–4, 7–6<sup>(7–5)</sup>
This was the first women's doubles title for the team of Mattek-Sands and Šafářová at the event and in their respective careers.<ref name="auto3"/>  One of their finalist opponents, Zheng Jie won the title in 2006 with [[Yan Zi (tennis)|Yan Zi]].

====Mixed Doubles====
{{main|2015 Australian Open – Mixed Doubles}}
* {{flagicon|SUI}} '''[[Martina Hingis]]''' / {{flagicon|IND}} '''[[Leander Paes]]''' defeated {{flagicon|FRA}} [[Kristina Mladenovic]] / {{flagicon|CAN}} [[Daniel Nestor]], 6–4, 6–3
This was a match of past mixed doubles champions at the event, which Hingis won with [[Mahesh Bhupathi]] in 2006, while her partner Paes won titles in 2003 with [[Martina Navratilova]] and in 2010 with [[Cara Black]].<ref name="auto4"/> Their finalist opponents' won the event last year, but Nestor won titles in 2007 with [[Elena Likhovtseva]] and 2011 with [[Katarina Srebotnik]]. This was Hingis' second mixed doubles title for her career, and for Paes' it is his seventh mixed doubles grand slam crown for his career.

==Main draw wildcard entries==
{{main|2015 Australian Open – Main Draw Wildcard Entries}}
As part of an agreement between [[Tennis Australia]], the [[United States Tennis Association]] (USTA) and the [[Fédération Française de Tennis|French Tennis Federation]] (FFT), one male and one female player from the United States and France received a wild card into the Australian Open singles event. USTA gave it to [[Denis Kudla]] and [[Irina Falconi]], thanks to their positions in 2014 USTA Pro Circuit's Australian Open Wild Card Challenge standing,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usta.com/Pro-Tennis/Pro-Circuit/2015_australian_open_wild_card_challenge_standings/|publisher=USTA.com|title=2015 Australian Open Wild Card Challenge|accessdate=26 December 2014}}</ref> while [[Lucas Pouille]] and [[Océane Dodin]] were chosen by internal FFT selection.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fft.fr/actualites/fil-d-infos/open-daustralie-dodin-et-pouille-invites|publisher=Fédération Française de Tennis|title=Open D'Australie Dodin et Pouille invites|date=12 December 2014|accessdate=12 December 2014}}</ref>

Further four wildcards were awarded at Asia-Pacific Australian Open Wildcard Playoff into the men's and women's singles and doubles main draw events,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com.au/news/2014/12/01/australian-open-wildcards-for-chang-zhang|title=Australian Open wildcards for Chang and Zhang|publisher=Tennis Australia|date=1 December 2014|accessdate=1 December 2014}}</ref> while Tennis Australia organized its own playoff competitions, where [[Jordan Thompson (tennis)|Jordan Thompson]], [[Daria Gavrilova]] and [[Sam Thompson (tennis)|Sam Thompson]] & [[Masa Jovanovic]] mixed doubles team received entries to Australian Open.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tennis.com.au/news-and-events/pro-tournaments/ao-play-off|title=Australian Open Play-off|publisher=Tennis.com.au|accessdate=2014-12-17}}</ref>

Remaining wildcard places were filled by Australian internal selection.
