'''James Jianzhang Liang''' (born December 1969) is a Chinese businessman, and the chairman, former CEO, and co-founder of [[Ctrip]], a Chinese provider of travel services including accommodation reservation, transportation ticketing, packaged tours, and corporate travel management.

==Education==
Liang earned bachelor's degree from [[Fudan University]], and master's degree in computer science from the [[Georgia Institute of Technology]].<ref name="imodules.com">{{cite web|url=http://nyustern.imodules.com/s/1068/GroupBus/3col.aspx?pgid=5496&gid=2|title=James Liang|publisher=|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref><ref name="exechange.com">{{cite web|url=https://exechange.com/6126/ctrip-ceo-james-liang-leaves-post-abruptly-2/6126|title=Ctrip CEO James Liang leaves post abruptly - Push-out Score - Page 6126|website=exechange.com|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref> 
In 2011, Liang earned a PhD from [[Stanford University]].<ref name="imodules.com"/>

==Career==
From 1991 to 1999, Liang worked for [[Oracle Corporation]] in the US and China, in technical and managerial roles with  from 1991 to 1999 in the United States and China, rising to head of Oracle China's ERP consulting division.<ref name="exechange.com"/>

Liang co-founded Ctrip in 1999, with Neil Shen, Min Fan, and [[Ji Qi (entrepreneur)|Ji Qi]]. He  was the CEO from 2000 to January 2006, and from March 2013 to November 2016, and chairman since August 2003.<ref name="ctrip">{{cite web|url=http://ir.ctrip.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=148903&p=irol-govBio&ID=153541|publisher=ir.ctrip.com|title=Ctrip - Investor Relations - Biography|accessdate=2016-11-25}}</ref> In November 2016, [[Jane Jie Sun]] succeeded him as CEO, with Liang remaining as executive chairman.<ref name="exechange.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chinamoneynetwork.com/2016/11/17/ctrip-appoints-jane-jie-sun-as-ceo-james-liang-remains-executive-chairman|title=Ctrip Appoints Jane Jie Sun As CEO, James Liang Remains Executive Chairman â€“ China Money Network|website=www.chinamoneynetwork.com|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref>

In the ''[[Forbes]]'' China Rich List for 2005, Liang had an estimated net worth of $62 million.<ref name="forbes.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/business/lists/2005/74/GIY0.html|title=James Liang Jianzhang, The 400 Richest Chinese -  Forbes.com|website=www.forbes.com|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref>

Liang is a board member of Homeinns Group (NASDAQ: HMIN), Tuniu (NASDAQ: TOUR), eHi (NASDAQ: EHIC),<ref name="exechange.com"/> and SINA Corporation (NASDAQ: SINA).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.streetinsider.com/Corporate+News/Sina+Corp+(SINA)+Names+James+Jianzhang+Liang+to+Board/13609536.html|title=Sina Corp (SINA) Names James Jianzhang Liang to Board|publisher=|accessdate=11 June 2018}}</ref>

Liang is a part-time professor at Peking University's [[Guanghua School of Management]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scmp.com/author/james-liang|title=James Liang|publisher=|accessdate=4 July 2018}}</ref>
