'''Witold Jan Waszczykowski''' ({{IPA-pl|ˈvitɔlt vaʂt͡ʂɨˈkɔfskʲi|lang|Pl-Witold Waszczykowski.ogg}}; born 5 May 1957) is a [[Poland|Polish]] politician.  He was the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Poland)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] between 2015 and 2018. Waszczykowski was a Member of the [[Sejm]] (2011–2019), and has been the Member of the [[European Parliament]] since 2019.

==Life and career==
[[File:Secretary Tillerson Shakes Hands With Polish Foreign Minister Waszczykowski (33982426332).jpg|thumb|left|200px|with U.S. Secretary of State [[Rex Tillerson]] at the U.S. Department of State in Washington (2017)]]
[[File:Secretary Kerry Shakes Hands With Polish Foreign Minister Waszczykowski Before A Bilateral Meeting at the National Stadium in Warsaw (27556969203).jpg|thumb|right|200px|Foreign Minister Witold Waszczykowski with U.S. Secretary of State [[John Kerry]] during [[2016 Warsaw summit]]]]
Waszczykowski was born in [[Piotrków Trybunalski]], [[Poland]] on 5 May 1957. He is a graduate of the [[University of Łódź]], earning a [[Master's degree]] in [[history]], and the [[University of Oregon]], where he received a master's degree in [[international studies]]. Waszczykowski completed advanced studies at the [[Geneva Centre for Security Policy]]. He also holds a [[PhD]] in history from the University of Łódź.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nauka-polska.pl/#/profile/scientist?id=104175&_k=ft7bcr|title=Nowa Nauka Polska|website=nauka-polska.pl|access-date=2019-08-08}}</ref>

He joined the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Poland)|Ministry of Foreign Affairs]] in 1992. Between 1997 and 1999 he was working at the Permanent Representation of Poland to [[NATO]] in [[Brussels]] as deputy chief of mission. From 1999 to 2002, Waszczykowski was the Ambassador of [[Poland]] to [[Iran]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=Sejm Rzeczypospolitej Polskiej. VII kadencja. Przewodnik|last=|first=|publisher=Wydawnictwo Sejmowe|year=2012|isbn=|location=Warszawa|pages=454}}</ref> On 4 November 2005, he became [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Poland)|Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs]]. Waszczykowski served as the chief negotiator with the United States on [[missile defense]]. He served until 11 August 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wprost.pl/kraj/136088/Waszczykowski-odwolany.html|title=Waszczykowski odwołany|date=2008-08-11|website=WPROST.pl|language=pl|access-date=2019-08-08}}</ref>

From 27 August 2008 to 6 July 2010, Waszczykowski was the Deputy Head of the [[National Security Bureau (Poland)|National Security Bureau]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bbn.gov.pl/portal/pl/2/2317/Dymisja_zastepcow_Szefa_BBN.html|title=Biuro Bezpieczeństwa Narodowego - Dymisja zastępców Szefa BBN - Wydarzenia -|date=2010-08-26|website=web.archive.org|language=pl|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100826095244/http://www.bbn.gov.pl/portal/pl/2/2317/Dymisja_zastepcow_Szefa_BBN.html|archive-date=2010-08-26|url-status=|access-date=6 July 2010}}</ref> In the [[2011 Polish parliamentary election|2011 parliamentary elections]], he successfully ran for the [[Sejm]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wybory2011.pkw.gov.pl/wsw/pl/sjm-9.html|title=Wybory 2011|website=wybory2011.pkw.gov.pl|access-date=2019-08-08}}</ref> He was reelected in [[2015 Polish parliamentary election|2015]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://parlament2015.pkw.gov.pl/349_Wyniki_Sejm|title=PKW {{!}} Wybory do Sejmu RP i Senatu RP|website=parlament2015.pkw.gov.pl|access-date=2019-08-08}}</ref>

Since 16 November 2015, Waszczykowski has been the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Poland)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] in the [[Cabinet of Beata Szydło]].

On 10 January 2017, he accidentally referred to the country of [[Saint Kitts and Nevis]] as "[[San Escobar]]" (the mistake reportedly stems from the islands' Spanish name, San Cristóbal y Nieves).<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-38582447 |title=San Escobar: Polish foreign minister's slip invents a country |work=BBC |location=London |publisher=BBC |date=2017-01-11 |accessdate=2017-01-13 |quote=Witold Waszczykowski told reporters he met with representatives of various states for Poland's bid to join the UN security council, "such as Belize or San Escobar". }}</ref> This was immediately picked up by newspapers throughout the world, such as ''[[The Guardian]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/jan/11/polish-minister-mocked-over-meeting-with-fictional-san-escobar|title=Polish minister mocked over meeting with fictional nation of San Escobar|first=Nadia|last=Khomami|date=11 January 2017|publisher=|via=The Guardian}}</ref> ''[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/01/11/polands-foreign-minister-mocked-naming-non-existent-country/|title=Poland's foreign minister mocked after naming non-existent country as supporter in bid for UN Security Council seat|publisher=}}</ref> ''[[The Washington Post]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/worldviews/wp/2017/01/11/san-escobar-how-polands-foreign-minister-helped-create-a-fake-country/|title=San Escobar: How Poland’s foreign minister helped create a fake country|publisher=}}</ref> ''The New York Times'',<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2017/01/11/world/europe/ap-eu-poland-fake-country.html?_r=0</ref> and ''Britské listy''.<ref>http://blisty.cz/art/85254.html</ref>

In 2019, he was elected member to the [[European Parliament]], receiving 168 021 votes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wybory.gov.pl/pe2019/pl/wyniki/pl|title=Wybory do Parlamentu Europejskiego 2019|website=wybory.gov.pl|language=pl|access-date=2019-08-08}}</ref>

Waszczykowski has been also cooperating with the [[Sobieski Institute]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sobieski.org.pl/author/waszczykowski/|title=dr Witold Waszczykowski, Autor w serwisie Instytut Sobieskiego|website=Instytut Sobieskiego|language=pl-PL|access-date=2019-08-08}}</ref>
