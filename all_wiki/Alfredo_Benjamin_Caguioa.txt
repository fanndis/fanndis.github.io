'''Alfredo Benjamin Sabater Caguioa''' (born September 30, 1959)<ref name="appointment">{{cite news|url=https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2013/01/10/appointments-and-designations-january-10-2013/|title=Appointments and Designations: January 10, 2013|publisher=[[Official Gazette (Philippines)|Official Gazette]]|accessdate=July 13, 2016}}</ref> is a [[Filipinos|Filipino]] [[lawyer]] who is an Associate Justice of the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines]].<ref name="pdioath">{{Cite news |last=Torres-Tupas|first=Tetch |title=Justice Secretary Caguioa takes oath as 174th SC justice |newspaper=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]] |date=January 27, 2016 |url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/759290/justice-secretary-caguioa-takes-oath-as-174th-sc-justice |accessdate=April 1, 2016}}</ref><ref name="philstaroath">{{Cite news |title=PNoy appoints DOJ chief as next SC justice |newspaper=[[The Philippine Star]] |date=January 22, 2016 |url=http://www.philstar.com/headlines/2016/01/22/1545231/pnoy-appoints-doj-chief-next-sc-justice |accessdate=April 1, 2016}}</ref> Prior to his appointment as Associate Justice he had served as acting [[Department of Justice (Philippines)|Secretary of the Department of Justice]] and Chief Presidential Legal Counsel under the presidency of [[Benigno Aquino III]].<ref name="actingsoj">{{cite news|last=Gonzales|first=Yuji Vincent|title=Aquino appoints legal counsel Alfredo Caguioa as ad interim DOJ chief|url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/730632/aquino-appoints-legal-counsel-alfredo-caguioa-as-ad-interim-doj-chief|accessdate=14 October 2015|publisher=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]|date=13 October 2015}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Caguioa was born September 30, 1959.<ref name="appointment"/> He is a son of [[Court of Appeals of the Philippines|Court of Appeals]] Justice Eduardo P. Caguioa. He finished elementary in Ateneo in 1973 then high school in 1977. He obtained his Economics degree from [[Ateneo de Manila University]] in 1981, and acquired his Bachelor of Laws from [[Ateneo Law School]] in 1985 where he ranked 5th in his class. On the same year he ranked 15th on the Bar Exam and was admitted to the Philippine Bar the following year.<ref name="rappler">{{Cite news |last=Lim |first=Gerard |title=Who is newly-appointed SC Justice Alfredo Caguioa? |publisher=[[Rappler]] |date=January 23, 2016 |url=http://www.rappler.com/newsbreak/iq/119912-supreme-court-associate-justice-alfredo-benjamin-caguioa |accessdate=July 13, 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==
Caguioa joined SyCip Salazar Hernandez and Gatmaitan in 1986 and was a partner there from 1994 until February 2007. Together with five other lawyers from his previous firm, they founded the Caguioa and Gatmaytan and Associates in February 2007.<ref name="cglaw">{{Cite news |title=Alfredo Benjamin S. Caguioa |publisher=Gatmaytan Yap Patacsil Gutierrez & Protacio |url=http://www.cagatlaw.com/caguioa.html |accessdate=July 13, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817014300/http://www.cagatlaw.com/caguioa.html |archive-date=August 17, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="appointment"/> He was a professor at the [[Ateneo Law School]] and [[San Sebastian College – Recoletos de Manila|San Sebastian College]] College of Law. Since 2015 he's a professor of Civil Law in [[University of Santo Tomas]]. Caguioa was cited as a leading Philippine lawyer in dispute resolution by Chambers & Partners in its 2010 and 2011 Asia-Pacific publications.<ref name="psalm">{{Cite news |title=Secretary's Profile |publisher=Power Sector Assets and Liabilities Management Corporation |url=https://www.psalm.gov.ph/documents/Sec%20Profile/Secretary%20%20Alfredo%20Benjamin%20Caguioa.pdf |accessdate=July 13, 2016}}</ref><ref name="cglaw"/><ref name="bworld">{{Cite news |title=Caguioa appointed to head DoJ |publisher=[[BusinessWorld|Business World Online]] |url=http://www.bworldonline.com/content.php?section=Nation&title=caguioa-appointed-to-head-doj&id=116919 |date=October 14, 2015 |accessdate=July 13, 2016}}</ref>

On January 10, 2013, he was appointed as Chief Presidential Legal Counsel of then President [[Benigno Aquino III]], his classmate since grade school.<ref name="gmacplc">{{Cite news |last=Chiu |first=Patricia Denise |title=PNoy names Ateneo classmate new chief legal counsel |publisher=[[GMA News]] |date=January 10, 2013 |url=http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/story/289674/news/nation/pnoy-names-ateneo-classmate-new-chief-legal-counsel |accessdate=July 13, 2016}}</ref><ref name="abscplc">{{Cite news |last=Cheng |first=Willard|title=PNoy classmate is new chief pres'l legal counsel |publisher=[[ABS-CBN News]] |date=January 10, 2013 |url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/nation/01/10/13/pnoy-classmate-new-chief-presl-legal-counsel |accessdate=July 13, 2016}}</ref> He was part of the official delegation of the country to the [[Permanent Court of Arbitration]] for the [[Philippines v. China|South China Sea Arbitration]]. After the resignation of then Secretary [[Leila de Lima]] last October 2015, he was appointed as acting [[Department of Justice (Philippines)|Secretary of the Department of Justice]].<ref name="actingsoj"/><ref name="rappler"/><ref name="bworld"/> He was appointed as the 174th [[Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines|Associate Justice]] of the [[Supreme Court of the Philippines]] replacing the retired Associate Justice Martin Villarama Jr.<ref name="rappler"/>
