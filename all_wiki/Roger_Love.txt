'''Roger Love''' is an American [[vocal coach]] based in Los Angeles who has worked with many singers, actors and public speakers.

== Career ==
Love took his first singing lessons when he was 13 years old.<ref name="dc">{{cite web |title=Roger Love |url=https://www.theintroverteddoctor.com/rogerlove |website=TheIntrovertedDoctor |accessdate=28 January 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
By 16<ref>{{cite web |title=Roger Love Visits the City of Youngstown - The Jambar |url=https://www.thejambar.com/roger-love-visits-city-youngstown/ |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> or 17<ref name="jew">{{cite web |title=Roger Love: From Bima to Eminem |url=https://jewishjournal.com/news/los_angeles/community/93444/ |website=Jewish Journal |accessdate=28 January 2020 |date=21 June 2011}}</ref> he studied [[vocal pedagogy]] in [[Seth Riggs]]'s studio. He became a junior partner in the studio but soon founded his own studio and took some of Riggs' clients.<ref name=":3">{{cite news |title=Vocal coach Roger Love helps put the sing in Hollywood performances |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/la-et-mn-begin-again-vocal-coach-20140625-story.html |accessdate=28 January 2020 |work=Los Angeles Times |date=25 June 2014}}</ref> He was initially interested in a career in [[opera]], but developed a passion for working with [[pop music]]ians after coaching [[The Beach Boys]], [[Chicago (band)|Chicago]], and [[The 5th Dimension]].<ref name=":4" /> Today he owns Voice Place Inc.

Love is notable for working with [[Gwen Stefani]],<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/raquelbaldelomar/2017/04/19/five-strategies-to-help-you-sound-like-the-leader-you-want-to-be/|title=Five Strategies To Help You Sound Like The Leader You Want To Be|last=Baldelomar|first=Raquel|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref> [[Selena Gomez]],<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Nightline/video/reps-mariah-carey-ear-piece-working-years-eve-44520719|title=Video: Reps for Mariah Carey Say Her 'Earpiece Was Not Working' New Year's Eve|website=ABC News|language=en|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref> [[John Mayer]],<ref name=":1" /> and [[Eminem]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/01/16/arts/music/my-idol-idyll-how-i-trained-to-belt-like-fantasia.html|title=My 'Idol' Idyll: How I Trained to Belt Like Fantasia|last=Grimes|first=William|date=2005-01-16|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-01-25|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

Love has worked as a vocal consultant on major [[Hollywood]] films including ''[[A Star Is Born (2018 film)|A Star Is Born (2018)]]'', ''[[Crazy Heart]]'', ''[[Southland Tales]]'', ''[[Walk the Line]]'', and ''[[Begin Again (film)|Begin Again]]''; coaching actors such as [[Keira Knightley]],<ref name=":3"/> [[Reese Witherspoon]],<ref name=":3" /> [[Joaquin Phoenix]],<ref name=":3" /> and [[Bradley Cooper]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/film-tv/a14326780/lady-gaga-bradley-cooper-star-is-born-photo/|title=Bradley Cooper and Lady Gaga Duet in a New 'A Star Is Born' Photo|last=Gonzales|first=Erica|date=2017-12-06|website=Harper's BAZAAR|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref> Many of them have won [[Grammy Award]], [[Screen Actors Guild Awards]], and [[Academy Awards]] after working with Love.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2010/music/news/coach-helps-actors-find-voice-1118015018/|title=Coach helps actors find voice|last=Morris|first=Chris|date=2010-02-11|website=Variety|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref>

Love has been a featured cast member on TV shows such as ''[[Popstars]]'' ([[The WB|WB]]),<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://www.roland.com/us/artists/1687/|title=Roland - Artists - Vocal Artists - Roger Love|last=Corporation|first=Roland|website=Roland|access-date=2020-02-01}}</ref> ''[[Rock Star: INXS|Rock Star INXS]]'' ([[CBS]]),<ref name=":5" /> ''[[Sing Your Face Off]]'' ([[ABC Studios|ABC]])<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.creators.com/read/hollywood-exclusive/05/14/jon-lovitz-shines-in-sing-your-face-off|title=Jon Lovitz Shines in 'Sing Your Face Off', by Stacy Jenel Smith|date=2014-05-29|website=www.creators.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-01}}</ref> and ''[[Encore! (TV series)|ENCORE]]'' ([[Disney+]]).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mycitymag.com/encore-flint-central-high-school-featured-on-disney/|title=Encore! Flint Central High School Featured on Disney+|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-01}}</ref>

Love has also developed a method for increasing clarity and confidence when speaking,<ref name=":0" /> and has coached executives<ref name=":5" /> and personalities including [[Tony Robbins]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.inc.com/ken-sterling/a-top-vocal-coach-shares-his-personal-tips-for-acing-your-next-presentation.html|title=This Top Vocal Coach Who's Worked With Tony Robbins Shares How to Connect With Your Audience|last=Sterling|first=Ken|date=2019-04-22|website=Inc.com|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref> and [[Tyra Banks]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/more-glee-the-story-behin_b_4461580|title=More Glee: The Story Behind the Magic|date=2013-12-18|website=HuffPost|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref>

== Method ==
Love’s vocal method focuses on [[diaphragmatic breathing]], daily vocal exercises, and bridging the [[head voice]] and [[chest voice]] via the “middle voice”.<ref name=":2" /> Love analyzes his clients’ voices by asking them to sing a [[Scale (music)|musical scale]] that includes very low and very high [[Musical note|notes]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.backstage.com/magazine/article/getting-actors-sing-57341/|title=A Voice Teacher on Getting Actors to Sing|date=2010-03-24|website=www.backstage.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Love was born around 1969-1970 in Los Angeles in a Jewish working-class family. He started singing in a [[Synagogue]] [[Temple Beth Am (Los Angeles, California)|Temple Beth Am]] guided by the [[Cantor]] Allan Michelson.<ref name="jew" /> Later he worked and a fill-in cantor at [[Burbank, California|Burbank]] Temple Emanu El. Love is married to Miyoko Love and has two children [[Madison Love|Madison Emiko Love]], a [[Japanese Americans|Japanese-American]] songwriter and vocalist,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.songwriteruniverse.com/madison-emiko-love-songwriter-2018.htm|title=Singer/Songwriter Madison Emiko Love Co-Writes Pop Hits "Bad Things" And "Him & I" |date=2018-03-12|work=Songwriter Universe |access-date=2020-01-25|language=en-US}}</ref> and Colin Makoto Love. Love lives in [[Los Angeles]] and owns a dog, Gigi.

Love has been featured in the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]''<ref name=":3" /> and ''[[HuffPost]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/how-to-feel-confident-in-any-situation_b_58594a60e4b06ae7ec2a4198|title=How to Feel Confident in Any Situation|last1=McDermott|first1=Cortney|last2=Writer|first2=ContributorAward-winning|date=2016-12-20|website=HuffPost|language=en|access-date=2020-01-25|last3=Speaker|last4=Strategist}}</ref> and on ''[[Larry King Now]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ora.tv/larrykingnow/2016/12/9/roger-love-on-finding-your-best-voice|title=Roger Love on finding your best voice|website=Ora TV|language=en|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref>  [[Rachael Ray (talk show)|''Rachael Ray'']]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rachaelrayshow.com/tips/11390_Only_Online_More_Voice_Tips_from_Roger_Love|title=Only Online: More Voice Tips from Roger Love|date=2009-04-20|website=Rachael Ray Show|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref> and ''[[Nightline]]''.<ref name=":1" />
