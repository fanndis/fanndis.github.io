'''World News Media Limited''' is the publisher of ''World Finance'' magazine which is  tied to the marketing of numerous [[vanity award]]s under the name of the World Finance Awards. It also publishes ''The New Economy'' which organises The New Economy Awards. The company was established in 2004 and trades from London.

==History and financials==
The company was established in July 2004.<ref name=coy>[https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/05186749 World News Media Limited.] Companies House. Retrieved 4 July 2020.</ref> Its registered office is in Judd Street, London, WC, but its offices are at 40 Compton Street in the London Borough of Islington.<ref>[https://www.wnmedia.com/contact Contact.] World News Media. Retrieved 22 August 2017.</ref> The director and principal shareholder is Howard Angel.<ref name=coy/> As at 31 August 2019, the company had net shareholder's funds of £76,193 (2018, negative £37,061). It has issued and paid-up share capital of £100.<ref name=coy/>

==Magazines==
[[File:World Finance branded Goodge Street News.jpg|thumb|A ''World Finance'' branded newsagent in Goodge Street, London.]]
The company publishes the magazines ''World Finance'' and ''The New Economy'',<ref>[https://www.theneweconomy.com/magazine ''The New Economy''.] Retrieved 9 September 2017.</ref> bi-monthly or less frequently, in print and electronically free of charge by [[Issuu]] along with iPad and Android editions. The magazines feature in-house produced copy plus topical non-exclusive agency-produced articles. Editorials are credited to Project Syndicate. Features profile award winners and sponsored supplements are produced such as ''Project Finance Deals of the Year - 2015'', ''Banking in Nigeria'', and ''Sustainable Development in Morocco''.<ref name=world>[https://www.worldfinance.com/supplements Supplements.] ''World Finance''. Retrieved 23 August 2017.</ref>

''Business Destinations'' and ''European CEO'' are published by associated company Tower Business Media Limited on a similar basis and have their own set of awards.<ref>[https://www.businessdestinations.com/magazine/ ''Business Destinations''.] Retrieved 9 September 2017.</ref><ref>[https://www.europeanceo.com/digital-editions/ ''European CEO''.] Retrieved 9 September 2017.</ref>

==World Finance Awards==
[[File:World Finance Award.jpg|thumb|A World Finance award for "Best Non-Life Insurance Company, Kazakhstan", 2013.]]
[[File:Н.Тульчинский в студии Лондонской Фондовой Биржи получает награду World Finance.jpg|thumb|A winner from Kazakhstan receiving their award in 2013.]]
As of 2013, the company provided awards in 16 different categories from banking to telecoms under the name of the World Finance Awards which started in 2007.<ref>[https://www.worldfinance.com/awards World Finance Awards.] World Finance. Retrieved 22 August 2017.</ref> Tim Hunter, writing in ''Stuff'' in 2014, commented, "Each award category has a winner for each country represented and in one single category there were 69 winners from countries including Angola to Vietnam and Pakistan to Peru. In the banking category there were more than 200 winners."<ref name="Stuff">{{cite news|title=What's the worth of a prize with a price tag?|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/business/opinion-analysis/10115577/Whats-the-worth-of-a-prize-with-a-price-tag|accessdate=31 July 2017|work=Stuff|date=3 June 2014}} [https://archive.is/pPG8D Archived here.]</ref> The company's awards have since been extended to include [[Islamic finance]].<ref>[http://pakobserver.net/world-finance-announces-winners-2017-awards/ World Finance announces winners of 2017 awards.] ''[[Pakistan Observer]]''. Retrieved 23 August 2017.</ref>

In January 2017, the consumer watchdog of Botswana was threatened with a legal action for defamation by World News Media for questioning the criteria by which various banks in Botswana had been given a World Finance Award in 2012.<ref>[http://consumerwatchdogbw.blogspot.co.uk/2017/01/our-response-to-threat-from-world-news.html Our response to the threat from World News Media.] Consumer Watchdog, 10 January 2017. Retrieved 22 August 2017.</ref>
