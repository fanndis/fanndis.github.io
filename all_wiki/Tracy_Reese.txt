'''Tracy Reese''' (born February 12, 1964) is an [[United States|American]] [[fashion designer]] who specializes in women's ready-to-wear [[clothing]], [[Fashion accessories|accessories]], and home fashions such as linens. She is a board member of the [[Council of Fashion Designers of America]], having been inducted in 2007.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bing|first=Bonnie|title=Designer stays true to her distinctive look|url=http://www.kansas.com/2011/11/14/2102562/designer-stays-true-to-her-distinctive.html|accessdate=November 22, 2011|newspaper=Wichita Eagle|date=November 14, 2011}}</ref>

==Early life==
Reese is originally from [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]], and graduated from [[Cass Technical High School]]. She recalls making clothing from scratch while growing up, alongside her mother, while they worked sitting side by side at their own sewing machines.<ref>{{cite news|last=Johnson|first=Eunice W.|title=Behind the Seams|newspaper=Ebony|issn=0012-9011|page=89|date=February 2006 |volume=61 |number=4}}</ref> In 1982, she moved to [[New York City]] to pursue her education at [[Parsons School of Design]]. After graduating with an accelerated degree in 1984, Reese worked under [[Martine Sitbon]] at the firm Arlequin. She worked at several top fashion design houses, and eventually became head of the Women's Portfolio for [[Perry Ellis]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Tracy Reese|url=http://www.mbfashionweek.com/designers/tracy_reese|publisher=Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week|accessdate=November 22, 2011}}</ref> before launching her own label.

==Tracy Reese label==
The Tracy Reese fashion label was launched in New York City in 1998. The Tracy Reese label produces both [[ready-to-wear]] and resort wear. Plenty (2000) and Frock! (2006) were launched as mass market lines at a more affordable price range. Plenty has a focus on home fashions. Tracy Reese Black Label launched in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tracy Reese -- Designer Fashion Label|url=http://nymag.com/fashion/fashionshows/designers/bios/tracyreese/|publisher=New York Magazine, New York Media LLC|accessdate=November 22, 2011}}</ref>

Tracy Reese designs are noted for their femininity and retro-influenced style; the textiles themselves make use of bright colors, elaborate graphic patterns, and a playful use of bohemian touches.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bing|first=Bonnie|title=Designer stays true to her distinctive look|url=http://www.kansas.com/2011/11/14/2102562/designer-stays-true-to-her-distinctive.html|accessdate=November 22, 2011|newspaper=Wichita Eagle|date=November 14, 2011}}</ref> Clothing and home fashions by Tracy Reese's design lines are carried by retailers such as [[Bloomingdale's]], [[Bergdorf Goodman]], [[Neiman Marcus]], [[Anthropologie]], [[Modcloth]], and [[Nordstrom]]. The Tracy Reese flagship store in New York City opened in 2006. In a ''New York Times'' interview on the occasion of the store's five-year anniversary, Reese expressed optimism about current trends continuing to support her design vision:{{blockquote|We love pretty, we like beautiful, flattering, color! That’s what people come here for. There was a moment when big stores weren’t into that but our customer always was, and she was coming here".<ref>{{cite news|last=Oliver|first=Simone S.|title=Tracy Reese Celebrates Five Years in the Meatpacking District|url=http://runway.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/06/09/tracy-reese-celebrates-five-years-in-the-flatiron-district/|accessdate=November 22, 2011|newspaper=New York Times|date=June 9, 2011}}</ref>}}

In 2011, a second exclusive Tracy Reese store opened, this one in Tokyo.<ref>{{cite web|last=Edelson|first=Sharon|title=Tracy Reese Opens in Tokyo|url=http://www.wwd.com/retail-news/designer-luxury/tracy-reese-opens-in-tokyo-3484996|publisher=Women's Wear Daily|accessdate=November 22, 2011|date=February 14, 2011}}</ref>

Reese has a number of celebrity clients, including First Lady [[Michelle Obama]], who wore Tracy Reese dresses during a [[First Family]] visit to Hawaii in 2011/2012,<ref>http://mrs-o.com/newdata/2012/1/3/scenes-from-hawaii-part-ii.html</ref> at a ceremony in 2013 commemorating the 50th anniversary of Martin Luther King Jr.’s “I Have a Dream” speech,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hollywoodlife.com/2013/08/28/michelle-obama-march-on-washington-dress-pics-tracy-reese/|title=Michelle Obama Stuns For The March On Washington Anniversary|last=Mitzeliotis|first=Katrina|date=2013-08-28|website=Hollywood Life|language=en|access-date=2020-02-21}}</ref> and on other occasions. The First Lady also wore a custom-made Tracy Reese dress for her 2012 DNC Convention speech.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/2012-democratic-convention-michelle-obamas-tracy-reese-dress-steals-spotlight-368069|title=2012 Democratic Convention: Michelle Obama's Tracy Reese Dress Steals Spotlight|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en|access-date=2020-02-21}}</ref>
