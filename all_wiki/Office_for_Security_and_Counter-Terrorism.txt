The '''Office for Security and Counter-Terrorism''' ('''OSCT''') is an executive directorate of the UK government [[Home Office]], created in 2007,<ref name=guardian-20140608>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/jun/08/infighting-gove-home-office-extremists |title=Infighting between ministers goes back to 7 July London attacks |author=Mark Townsend |newspaper=The Guardian |date=8 June 2014 |accessdate=8 June 2014}}</ref> responsible for leading the work on [[counter-terrorism]] in the [[UK]], working closely with the police and security services. The office reports to the [[Home Secretary]] and [[Minister of State for Security|Minister of State for Security and Counter-Terrorism]] (currently [[James Brokenshire]]). Its current Director General is Tom Hurd,<ref name="sundaytimes">{{cite news|last=Leppard|first=David|title=Chief snooper pops out of the shadows|url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/comment/profiles/article1021573.ece|accessdate=22 April 2012|newspaper=Sunday Times|date=22 April 2012}}</ref> who is described as the senior government official responsible for counter terrorist and organised crime strategy.<ref name=farr-statement1/>

==Responsibilities==
According to its website, the current responsibilities of the OSCT are:<ref name="HomeOffice">{{cite web |url=http://security.homeoffice.gov.uk/about-us/about-the-directorate/?version=1 |title=Office for Security and Counter-Terrorism - About us |publisher=Home Office |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071022054409/http://security.homeoffice.gov.uk/about-us/about-the-directorate/?version=1 |archivedate=2007-10-22}}</ref>

*Exercising the UK's response to a [[terrorist]] incident
*Developing legislation on terrorism here and overseas 
*Providing security measures and protection packages for public figures
*Ensuring that the UK’s critical national [[infrastructure]] is protected from attack (including electronic attack)
*Ensuring the UK is prepared to deal with a chemical, biological, or nuclear release
*Liaising with government and emergency services during terrorist incidents or counter-terrorism operations

==Programmes==
{{main|CONTEST}}
The Preventing Violent Extremism strategy (Prevent),<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.gov.uk/government/policies/protecting-the-uk-against-terrorism/supporting-pages/prevent |title=Protecting the UK against terrorism - Prevent |publisher=gov.uk |date=26 March 2013 |accessdate=9 June 2014}}</ref> is a £140 million{{Clarify|reason=per annum?|date=May 2016}} programme run by OSCT.

In 2013, OSCT stated that 500 people had gone through its Channel deradicalisation programme, including some considering participating in the [[Syrian civil war]], steering some away from [[violent extremism]].<ref name=bbc-20130326>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21940899 |title=500 terror suspects 'deradicalised' by Home Office |publisher=BBC |date=26 March 2013 |accessdate=8 June 2014}}</ref>
