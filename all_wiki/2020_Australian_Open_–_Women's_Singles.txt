{{main|2020 Australian Open}}

[[Sofia Kenin]] won her first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] title, defeating [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the final, 4–6, 6–2, 6–2.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2020/feb/01/australian-open-final-garbine-muguruza-sofia-kenin-report |title = Sofia Kenin battles back to beat Garbiñe Muguruza in Australian Open final|date=1 February 2020|work=Guardian|accessdate=5 February 2020}}</ref>	
Kenin became the first American woman, other than [[Serena Williams]], to win the [[Australian Open]] women's singles title since [[Jennifer Capriati]] in [[2002 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2002]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/51340294 |title = Australian Open: Sofia Kenin beats Garbine Muguruza to win title|date=1 February 2020|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=5 February 2020}}</ref>	
Kenin was the youngest Australian Open finalist since [[Ana Ivanovic]] and the youngest champion since [[Maria Sharapova]], both in [[2008 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2008]]. Muguruza was the first unseeded player, without being awarded a wildcard or entering through a protected ranking, to reach the Australian Open final since Williams in [[2007 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2007]].<ref> Although unseeded when she reached the [[2010 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2010 Australian Open women's singles]] final, [[Justine Henin]] was awarded a wildcard into the event.</ref> This was the first Australian Open final in the [[History of tennis|Open Era]] to be contested between two players ranked outside the top ten, and the first Grand Slam final to feature two players outside the top ten since the [[2018 US Open – Women's Singles final|2018 US Open final]].

[[Naomi Osaka]] was the defending champion, but lost to [[Coco Gauff]] in the third round.

This was the final tournament for the [[2018 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2018 champion]] and former world no. 1 [[Caroline Wozniacki]], who announced her retirement effective at the end of the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cbssports.com/tennis/news/caroline-wozniacki-29-says-she-will-retire-following-2020-australian-open/|title=Caroline Wozniacki, 29, says she will retire following 2020 Australian Open|last=Bengel|first=Chris|date=6 December 2019|work=CBS Sports|access-date=6 December 2019}}</ref> She lost in the third round to [[Ons Jabeur]].

This tournament was also the last event in which five-time Grand Slam champion, [[2008 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2008 champion]], and former World No. 1 Sharapova competed, losing to [[Donna Vekić]] in the first round. She announced her immediate retirement from professional tennis in February.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1625831/maria-sharapova-retires-from-tennis|title=Maria Sharapova retires from tennis|publisher=[[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]]|date=26 February 2020|accessdate=26 February 2020}}</ref>

[[Bianca Andreescu]], the reigning [[2019 US Open – Women's Singles|women's singles champion]] of the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|2019 US Open]], the Grand Slam tournament immediately preceding the 2020 Australian Open, withdrew due to a knee injury. This marked the first time since the [[1996 Australian Open – Women's Singles|1996 Australian Open]] that the defending [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]] champion (in that case, [[Steffi Graf]]) withdrew before the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1566334/andreescu-announces-her-australian-open-absence|title=Andreescu announces her Australian Open absence|date=11 January 2020|work=WTA|accessdate=13 January 2020}}</ref>

[[Serena Williams]] was aiming to become the first woman to win a Grand Slam singles title in four different decades (as well as equal the all-time record for most Grand Slam titles held by [[Margaret Court]]) but she lost to [[Wang Qiang (tennis)|Wang Qiang]] in the third round, her earliest exit in Melbourne since [[2006 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2006]].

The elimination of [[Angelique Kerber]] in the fourth round guaranteed a first-time Australian Open champion for the third year in a row. Jabeur became the first Arabian woman to reach the quarterfinals of a Grand Slam tournament,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://apnews.com/a34770ed822aceb96dbed043b5490455 |title=First for Jabeur as she reaches quarterfinals in Australia |website=[[Associated Press]] |date=26 January 2020 |accessdate=27 January 2020 |first=John |last=Pye}}</ref> as well as the first African woman to reach the quarterfinals of a Grand Slam since [[Amanda Coetzer]] at the [[2001 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2001 Australian Open]].<ref name=":0" />

[[Ashleigh Barty]] became the first Australian woman to reach the women's singles semifinals since [[Wendy Turnbull]] in [[1984 Australian Open – Women's Singles|1984]]. Barty's quarterfinal win over [[Petra Kvitová]] was her first ever top ten singles win at a Grand Slam,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web
 | title = Australian Open 2020: Day 9, Match Points
 | url = https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1590199/australian-open-2020-day-9-match-points
 | publisher = WTA Tennis
 | date = 2020-01-27
 | access-date = 2020-01-28
 | author = Alex Macpherson
}}</ref> having lost her five previous matches. 

[[Anett Kontaveit]] became the first player from Estonia, male or female, to reach the quarterfinals of the Australian Open, and the second to do so at any Grand Slam after [[Kaia Kanepi]]. She would lose to [[Simona Halep]] in straight sets.
