'''Alison Elizabeth Larter''' (born February 28, 1976)<ref>{{cite news |first=Mike|last=Rose |url=http://www.cleveland.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2016/02/jason_aldean_ali_larter_top_li.html |title=Jason Aldean, Ali Larter top list of celebrity birthdays for February 28, 2016 |newspaper=The Plain Dealer |location=Cleveland, Ohio |date=2016-02-28 |accessdate=2017-01-03 }}<!-- 40 years old in 2016 --></ref>{{better source|date=October 2020}} is an American actress and model. She portrayed fictional model [[Allegra Coleman]] in a 1996 ''[[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]]'' magazine hoax and took on [[guest role]]s on several [[television show]]s in the 1990s. She made her film debut in ''[[Varsity Blues (film)|Varsity Blues]]'' (1999), which was followed by the [[horror film]]s ''[[House on Haunted Hill (1999 film)|House on Haunted Hill]]'' (1999), ''[[Final Destination (film)|Final Destination]]'' (2000), and ''[[Final Destination 2]]'' (2003). Her role as [[Clear Rivers]] in the latter two films earned her a reputation as a [[scream queen]].

Larter played major [[supporting role]]s in the comedy ''[[Legally Blonde]]'' (2001) and the romantic comedy ''[[A Lot Like Love]]'', (2005) as well as lead roles in [[Bollywood]] movie ''[[Marigold (2007 film)|Marigold]]'' (2007) and the thriller ''[[Obsessed (2009 film)|Obsessed]]'' (2009). She played the dual roles of [[Niki Sanders]] and [[Tracy Strauss]] on the [[NBC]] [[science fiction]] drama ''[[Heroes (U.S. TV series)|Heroes]]'' (2006–2010).<ref>{{cite web| title= Heroes Cast Members, Tracy Strauss| publisher= NBC| url= http://www.nbc.com/heroes/cast/Tracy-Strauss/| accessdate= 2010-07-24| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100408230844/http://www.nbc.com/heroes/cast/Tracy-Strauss/| archive-date= April 8, 2010| url-status= dead| df= mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| last= Feinburg| first= Daniel| title= NBC's 'Heroes' Fascinates Larter| publisher= Zap2it| date= 2006-07-03| url= http://www.zap2it.com/tv/news/zap-alilarterheroesstory,0,7909542.story | accessdate= 2010-06-27}}</ref> She achieved wider recognition for her portrayal of [[video game]] heroine [[Claire Redfield]] in the action horror film ''[[Resident Evil: Extinction]]'' (2007), ''[[Resident Evil: Afterlife]]'' (2010), and ''[[Resident Evil: The Final Chapter]]'' (2016).<ref name="boxoffice" />
 
Larter's presence in the media is reinforced by her appearances in lists compiled by ''[[Maxim (magazine)|Maxim]]'', ''[[FHM]]'', and ''[[Stuff magazine|Stuff]]'', as well as ''[[People magazine|People]]'' magazine's "Best Dressed List" in 2007.<!--NOTE: Please check section "In the media" for references. Thank you.--> She released her [[cookbook]], ''Kitchen Revelry: A Year of Festive Menus from My Home to Yours'', in September 2013.

==Early life and modeling==
Larter was born in [[Cherry Hill, New Jersey]],<ref name="Cosmo">{{cite news| last= Fahner| first= Molly| title= Fun Fearless Female of the Year: Ali Larter| work= [[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]| url= http://www.cosmopolitan.com/celebrity/exclusive/ali-larter-cover-interview | accessdate= 2010-06-27}}</ref> the daughter of Margaret, a realtor, and Danforth Larter, a trucking executive. She attended Carusi Middle School and later graduated from [[Cherry Hill High School West]] during her time as a model,<ref>{{cite news| last= Longsdorf| first= Amy| title= Tough Larter is looking for action (1 of 3)|newspaper= Courier-Post| date=2010-09-12| url= http://www.courierpostonline.com/article/20100912/LIFE/9120303/Tough-Larter-is-looking-for-action| accessdate= 2010-09-13}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> but she didn't finish her senior year of high school because of modeling. Her mom accompanied her everywhere until she turned 18.<ref name="jam.canoe.ca">http://jam.canoe.ca/Movies/Artists/L/Larter_Ali/2003/01/31/759707.html{{Dead link|date=August 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Her parents have since moved to [[Allentown, Pennsylvania]].<ref name="TV Guide">{{cite web| title= Ali Larter Biography| work= TV Guide | url= https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/ali-larter/bio/139014 | accessdate= 2007-08-27}}</ref>

Larter said she was a tomboy until she turned 13. She began her modeling career at the age of 14 when a modeling scout discovered her on the street and was asked to star in a ''[[Phillies]]'' commercial; this led to a [[Model (person)|model]]ing contract with the prestigious [[Ford Modeling Agency]] in New York.<ref name="canoe">{{cite news| last= B. Hobson| first= Louis| title= Counting herself lucky| publisher= Canoe.ca| url= http://jam.canoe.ca/Movies/Artists/L/Larter_Ali/2003/01/31/759707.html| archive-url= https://archive.is/20130120205649/http://jam.canoe.ca/Movies/Artists/L/Larter_Ali/2003/01/31/759707.html| url-status= dead| archive-date= 2013-01-20| accessdate= 2010-06-27}}</ref> Larter subsequently skipped her senior year to model in Australia, Italy, and Japan,<ref name="canoe" /> the latter a country she would temporarily settle in at the age of seventeen.<ref name="Yahoo">{{cite web| title= Ali Larter Biography| publisher= Yahoo.com| url= https://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800019825/bio| accessdate= 2007-08-27| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20071011074542/http://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800019825/bio| archive-date= 2007-10-11| url-status= live}}</ref>

While modeling in Italy, Larter met fellow model and aspiring actress [[Amy Smart]] and the two "became instant friends", according to Larter.<ref name="People">{{cite web| title= Ali Larter People Biography| work= [[People magazine|People]]| url= http://www.people.com/people/ali_larter/biography| accessdate= 2010-03-30}}</ref> In November 1996, Larter portrayed fictional actress [[Allegra Coleman]] in an ''[[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]]'' magazine hoax.<ref name="salon">{{cite news| last= Futrelle| first= David| title= Esquire's sexy starlet is the hoax with the most| work= Salon| url= http://www.salon.com/media/circus/1996/11/01/media961101| accessdate= 2007-12-18}}</ref> When speaking about the cover and her subsequent fame, Larter said, "When the door opens for you in Hollywood, you need to run with it. You know?"<ref>{{cite news| last= Haber| first= Matt| title= Get Real| newspaper= Entertainment Weekly| date=1998-12-18| url= https://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,286190,00.html| accessdate= 2010-10-17}}</ref> Larter is currently signed to [[IMG Models]].<ref>{{cite news| last= Yotka| first= Steff| title= Monday Model Roundup: Tanya D.'s New Name, DNA's Brits and Marilyn's Margherita | publisher= Fashionista| date=2011-07-25| url= http://fashionista.com/2011/07/monday-model-roundup-tanya-d-s-new-name-dnas-brits-and-marilyns-margherita/| accessdate= 2011-08-02}}</ref>

==Career==
===Early acting credits  (1997–1999)===
Larter landed her first professional acting roles in 1997 when she appeared in several [[television program]]s. She appeared in an episode of the [[Brooke Shields]] television series ''[[Suddenly Susan]]'', and the short-lived series ''Chicago Sons''. These roles were followed by a number of other appearances on ''[[Dawson's Creek]]'', ''[[Chicago Hope]]'', and ''[[Just Shoot Me!]]''

In 1999, Larter made her film debut in the [[coming-of-age film|coming-of-age]] dramedy ''[[Varsity Blues (film)|Varsity Blues]]'', which re-united her with ''Dawson's Creek'' star [[James Van Der Beek]] and close friend [[Amy Smart]]. Smart had persuaded Larter to audition for the movie,<ref name="canoe" /> in which she played Darcy Sears, a love interest for one of the main characters. ''Varsity Blues'' drew a domestic [[box office]] gross of US$53 million.<ref name="boxoffice" /> Also in 1999, she made supporting appearances in the teen comedies ''Giving It Up'' and ''[[Drive Me Crazy]]'', and starred in the remake ''[[House on Haunted Hill (1999 film)|House on Haunted Hill]]'' which was made for around US$20 million. The horror film, following a group of strangers invited to a party at an abandoned asylum, was panned by critics,<ref>{{cite web|title=House on Haunted Hill |publisher=Rotten Tomatoes |url=http://uk.rottentomatoes.com/m/1093881-house_on_haunted_hill/ |accessdate=2010-03-30 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100429045644/http://uk.rottentomatoes.com/m/1093881-house_on_haunted_hill/ |archive-date=April 29, 2010 }}</ref> but grossed US$15 million on its opening weekend and went on to earn over US$40 million overall.<ref name="boxoffice" />

===Breakthrough with ''Final Destination'' (2000–2005)===
Larter starred as Clear Rivers, one of the main characters in the teen supernatural horror film ''[[Final Destination (film)|Final Destination]]'' (2000). Also starring [[Devon Sawa]] and [[Kerr Smith]], the movie's premise followed several teenagers who survive a plane crash but are stalked and killed by death itself. ''Final Destination'' made US$112 million by the end of its theatrical run.<ref>{{cite web| title= Final Destination| publisher= Box Office Mojo| url=https://boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=finaldestination.htm | accessdate= 2010-08-13}}</ref> In 2001, she appeared in the comedy ''[[Legally Blonde]]'' with [[Reese Witherspoon]]. She played Brooke Taylor Windham, a widow accused of her husband's murder.<ref>{{cite news| last= Feiwell| first= Jill| title= Larter goes 'Blonde' in laffer| newspaper= Variety| date= 2000-10-09| url= https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117787445.html?categoryid=13&cs=1&query=ali+larter| accessdate= 2010-08-10}}</ref> The film earned the top spot with US$20 million in its opening weekend<ref>{{cite web| title= Weekend Box Office Results for July 13–15, 2001| publisher= Box Office Mojo| url=https://boxofficemojo.com/weekend/chart/?yr=2001&wknd=28&p=.htm | accessdate= 2010-08-10| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100807075407/http://www.boxofficemojo.com/weekend/chart/?yr=2001&wknd=28&p=.htm| archive-date=2010-08-07| url-status= live}}</ref> and ended up grossing $141 million worldwide.<ref name="boxoffice" /> With US$96 million of it total gross deriving from domestic markets, ''Legally Blonde'' is Larter's highest-grossing film domestically.<ref name="boxoffice" />

Larter next appeared as [[Zerelda Mimms]] in the [[Western (genre)|western]] comedy ''[[American Outlaws]]''.<ref>{{cite news| last= Harris| first= Dana| title= Caan, Larter don 'Jesse' oater roles| newspaper= Variety| date= 2000-04-30| url= https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117781069.html?categoryid=13&cs=1&query=ali+larter| accessdate= 2010-09-05}}</ref> Directed by [[Les Mayfield]] and co-starring [[Colin Farrell]] and [[Scott Caan]], the movie was poorly received by critics<ref>{{cite web| title= American Outlaws| publisher= Rotten Tomatoes| url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/american_outlaws/| accessdate= 2010-09-05}}</ref> and at the box office, garnering US$13 million at the end of its theatrical run.<ref name="boxoffice" /> She also starred in [[Kevin Smith]]'s ''[[Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back]].'' That year, Larter appeared on the cover of ''Maxim'' magazine and performed in the stage play ''[[The Vagina Monologues]]'' in New York City.<ref name="canoe" /> In Spring 2002, Larter moved from [[Los Angeles]] to [[New York City|New York]]. "I was too young and impressionable to handle the pressures of L.A." Larter recalled in an interview, "I'm a woman now. I am no longer the little girl who could be easily influenced."<ref>{{cite news| last= Logan| first= Michael| title= Twisted Sisters: Heroes' Ali Larter Teases a Big Battle| work= TV Guide| date= 2007-03-05| url=https://www.tvguide.com/news/Twisted-Sisters-Heroes-38759.aspx| accessdate= 2010-08-27}}</ref>

Her first project there was to reprise her role as Clear Rivers in the sequel to ''Final Destination'' entitled ''[[Final Destination 2]]''. In an interview with [[IGN]], Larter explained her return to the franchise: "When New Line asked me to come back, I thought it was great. They showed me the script and let me have some input, and it was really terrific."<ref>{{cite news|last=B. |first=Scott |title=An Interview with Ali Larter |publisher=IGN |date=2003-01-30 |url=http://uk.movies.ign.com/articles/384/384146p1.html |accessdate=2010-06-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120311105156/http://uk.movies.ign.com/articles/384/384146p1.html |archive-date=March 11, 2012 }}</ref> The film made US$90 million worldwide,<ref>{{cite web| title= Weekend Box Office Results for January 31 – February 2, 2003| publisher= Box Office Mojo| url= https://boxofficemojo.com/weekend/chart/?yr=2003&wknd=05&p=.htm| accessdate= 2007-08-27}}</ref> to a mixed critical reception.<ref>{{cite web|title=Final Destination 2 |publisher=Rotten Tomotoes |url=http://uk.rottentomatoes.com/m/final_destination_2/ |accessdate=2007-08-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080109121906/http://uk.rottentomatoes.com/m/final_destination_2/ |archive-date=January 9, 2008 }}</ref> A year later, Larter served as an associate producer of and starred in the thriller ''[[Three Way (film)|Three Way]]'', and in 2005, she appeared in the independent political thriller ''[[Confess (film)|Confess]]'', and had a role in the romantic comedy ''[[A Lot Like Love]]'', starring [[Amanda Peet]] and [[Ashton Kutcher]].

===''Heroes''  and ''Resident Evil'' (2006–2010)===
[[File:Ali Larter with Heroes-Poster.jpg|thumb|upright|Larter promoting ''[[Heroes (U.S. TV series)|Heroes]]'' at [[Comic-Con]], July 2006]]
Larter moved back to [[Los Angeles]] in 2005.<ref name="Yahoo"/> Her first audition was for the [[NBC Universal|NBC]] [[science fiction]] [[television drama|drama]] television series ''[[Heroes (U.S. TV series)|Heroes]]''.<ref name="chictrib" /> Larter played the characters of [[Niki Sanders]], who suffered from [[Dissociative identity disorder|DID]], and [[Tracy Strauss]] on the show created by [[Tim Kring]]. Larter's initial character Niki Sanders, was a wife, mother, and a former internet stripper from [[Las Vegas Valley|Las Vegas]] who exhibits superhuman strength and alternate personalities who go by the names of Jessica and Niki. "Ali read for the part and just owned it from the second she walked in," Kring said to the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'', "It was a very impressive audition."<ref name="chictrib" /> As of the third season, Larter began to play the new character of Tracy Strauss, who possessed the ability to freeze objects; and later, turn her body into water.<ref>{{cite news| last= West| first= Abby| title= Ali Larter on her 'Heroes' transformation| newspaper= Entertainment Weekly| date= 2008-08-30| url= http://popwatch.ew.com/2008/09/30/ali-larter-on-h/| accessdate= 2010-07-02}}</ref> 

Larter took on the title role in ''[[Marigold (2007 film)|Marigold]]'' (2007), alongside [[Salman Khan]].<ref name="marigold">{{cite news| last= Groves| first= Don| title= Larter blooms as lead in Indian 'Marigold'| newspaper= Variety| date= 2004-03-17| url= https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117901842.html?categoryid=1237&cs=1&query=ali+larter+marigold| accessdate= 2010-08-31}}</ref> The film was released in August 2007 and revolved around an American actress (Larter) who goes to India and gets caught up in the exotic world of Bollywood. Filming took place in [[North India]] and [[London]], commencing in June 2004.<ref name="marigold" /> In an interview with the [[BBC]], Larter remarked this role was "an opportunity to overcome my fear of singing and dancing because I have no professional training [...] I really focused on the character and loved this journey she went on and the experiences she had."<ref>{{cite news| last= Pandohar| first= Jaspreet| title= Ali Larter 'Marigold' Interview| publisher= BBC News| url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/films/2007/08/13/ali_larter_marigold_2007_interview.shtml| accessdate= 2010-01-18}}</ref> She was paid a seven-figure salary for her part in the film.<ref>{{cite news| last= Ramesh| first= Randeep| title= Kylie does Bollywood: Stars go east to beat the Hollywood crunch| newspaper= The Guardian| date= 2009-03-14| url= https://www.theguardian.com/film/2009/mar/14/bollywood-kylie-minogue| accessdate= 2010-07-24 | location=London}}</ref>

In 2007, she appeared opposite [[Milla Jovovich]] in ''[[Resident Evil: Extinction]]'', portraying the character of [[Claire Redfield]], who in the film, is the leader of a convoy of zombie apocalypse survivors who go to [[Alaska]] in search of a safe haven. Her role sent her to [[Mexicali]], Mexico for filming from May to late July, and required her hair to be dyed a light red.<ref name="BD">{{cite web| title= Resident Evil: Extinction – Interview w/Star Ali Larter| publisher= Bloody Disgusting| url= https://www.bloody-disgusting.com/interview/398| accessdate= 2007-09-16}}</ref> Larter explained her character Claire: "She became the leader of this convoy. She's incredibly strong, patient. I think she serves a role for everyone within this convoy, let it be a mother to someone, a buddy, a best friend."<ref name="BD"/> Despite negative reviews, the film made a total of US$147 million worldwide.<ref name="boxoffice" /> Also in the same year, she appeared with [[Hayes McArthur]] in the comedy ''[[Homo Erectus (film)|Homo Erectus]]'' and appeared as Evelyn Garland in the biographical drama ''[[Crazy (2007 film)|Crazy]]'', based on guitarist [[Hank Garland]].

Larter starred opposite [[Beyoncé]] and [[Idris Elba]] in the [[Screen Gems]]-produced thriller ''[[Obsessed (2009 film)|Obsessed]]'' (2009).<ref>{{cite news| last= Fleming| first= Michael| title= Knowles, Larter, Elba are 'Obsessed'| newspaper= Variety| date= 2008-03-05| url= https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117981913.html?categoryid=1238&cs=1&query=ali+larter| accessdate= 2010-08-09}}</ref> The film follows an office executive (Elba) whose marriage to Knowles' character is threatened by the aggressive interests of a co-worker, portrayed by Larter. In an interview with [[Glam Media|Glam]], Larter said that she "was excited to get the chance to play a femme fatale. I love playing women that are dark and vulnerable and sort of filled with a little bit of crazy emotion." While the film was released to mixed reviews, Larter was critically praised for her performance.<ref>{{cite news|last=Barth |first=Lauren |title=Obsessed with Ali Larter |publisher=Glam |url=http://movies.glam.com/articles/detail/iobsessedi_with_ali_larter/ |accessdate=2009-12-18 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711095644/http://movies.glam.com/articles/detail/iobsessedi_with_ali_larter/ |archive-date=July 11, 2011 }}</ref> [[Derek Malcolm]] of ''[[The London Evening Standard]]'' felt that the movie was a "dim reworking of ''[[Fatal Attraction]]''" and noted: "Larter as the pathological minx is the best thing about it."<ref>{{cite news|last=Malcolm |first=Derek |title=Obsessed is a dim reworking of Fatal Attraction |newspaper=London Evening Standard |date=2009-05-29 |url=http://www.thisislondon.co.uk/film/review-23701403-obsessed-is-a-dim-reworking-of-fatal-attraction.do |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130114021826/http://www.thisislondon.co.uk/film/review-23701403-obsessed-is-a-dim-reworking-of-fatal-attraction.do |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-01-14 |accessdate=2010-08-09 }}</ref> ''Obsessed'' opened at top spot in its opening weekend with US$28 million,<ref>{{cite news| last= Ryan| first= Joel| title= Box Office Totally Obsessed with Beyoncé, Larter | publisher= E! Online| date= 2009-04-26| url=http://uk.eonline.com/uberblog/b120683_box_office_totally_obsessed_with.html| accessdate= 2007-12-18}}</ref> and made a total domestic gross of US$68 million, becoming Larter's second highest-grossing film in North-America.<ref name="boxoffice">{{cite web| title= Ali Larter Movie Box Office Results| publisher= Box Office Mojo| url=https://boxofficemojo.com/people/chart/?id=alilarter.htm | accessdate= 2010-09-25}}</ref> It resulted in Larter's third [[Teen Choice Award]] nomination and an [[MTV Movie Award]] for Best Fight with Beyonce.<ref>{{cite news|last=Neel |first=Julia |title=MTV Movie Awards 2010 |work=Vogue |date=2010-06-07 |url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/celebrity-photos/100607-mtv-movie-awards-2010.aspx |accessdate=2010-06-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100610215249/http://www.vogue.co.uk/celebrity-photos/100607-mtv-movie-awards-2010.aspx |archive-date=June 10, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Larter reprised her role of [[Claire Redfield]] in ''[[Resident Evil: Afterlife]]'' (2010), which was filmed in [[3-D film|3D]] and saw her character ambushed and mentally manipulated by the fictional [[Umbrella Corporation]], before she is rescued by Alice (Milla Jovovich).<ref>{{cite news| title= Ali Larter Returns in 'Resident Evil: Afterlife'| publisher= Bloody Disgusting| date= 2009-09-22| url= https://www.bloody-disgusting.com/news/17445| accessdate= 2010-06-27}}</ref><ref name="wondercon">{{cite news|last=Fienberg |first=Daniel |title=Ali Larter thinks 'Heroes' will be back |publisher=Hitfix.com |date=2010-04-03 |url=http://www.hitfix.com/articles/2010-4-3-wondercon-notes-ali-larter-thinks-heroes-will-be-back |accessdate=2010-06-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100711071908/http://www.hitfix.com/articles/2010-4-3-wondercon-notes-ali-larter-thinks-heroes-will-be-back |archive-date=July 11, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Bentley |first=David |title=Milla Jovovich, Wentworth Miller, Ali Larter talk Resident Evil: Afterlife |newspaper=Coventry Telegraph |date=2010-08-09 |url=http://blogs.coventrytelegraph.net/thegeekfiles/2010/08/milla-jovovich-wentworth-mille.html |accessdate=2010-08-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100818172143/http://blogs.coventrytelegraph.net/thegeekfiles/2010/08/milla-jovovich-wentworth-mille.html |archive-date=August 18, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Like the previous ''Resident Evil'' entries, the film received negative reviews but became a major commercial success, earning US$296 million worldwide.<ref name="boxoffice" /> On returning to the role of Claire, she stated in an interview with [[JoBlo.com]]: "I guess people liked me as her [...] I'm excited that they brought me back [...] To work with the man [Paul W. S. Anderson] who really created this world and this vision was what excited me about joining this next installment".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.joblo.com/movie-news/set-visit-resident-evil-afterlife|title=Set Visit: Resident Evil: Afterlife|website=joblo.com|accessdate=March 26, 2018}}</ref>

===Hiatus and return to acting (2011–present)===
Following the release of ''Afterlife'', Larter took a career hiatus to focus on her family, returning to the screen when she played the love interest of a successful but psychotic man ([[Matt LeBlanc]]) in the comedy ''[[Lovesick (2014 film)|Lovesick]]'' (2014). The film screened at the 15th annual [[Newport Beach Film Festival]] and was released for VOD and selected theaters. She portrayed the "fair-weather" friend of a woman with [[ALS]] in the independent drama ''[[You're Not You]]'' (2014), directed by [[George C.
