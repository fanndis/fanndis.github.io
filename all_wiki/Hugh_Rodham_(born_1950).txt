'''Hugh Edwin Rodham''' (born May 16, 1950)<ref>{{cite book | url=https://archive.org/details/stateofunioninsi00oppe | url-access=registration | quote=Hugh Edwin Rodham 26 May 1950. | title=State of a Union: Inside the Complex Marriage of Bill and Hillary Clinton | first=Jerry | last=Oppenheimer | publisher=HarperCollins | location= | date=2000 | page= }}</ref><ref>[http://www.firstladies.org/biographies/firstladies.aspx?biography=43 "First Lady Biography: Hillary Clinton"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060902082333/http://www.firstladies.org/biographies/firstladies.aspx?biography=43 |date=September 2, 2006 }}.  Accessed via Google cache July 10, 2007.</ref> is an American [[lawyer]] and former [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] [[politician]] who is the only surviving brother of former [[New York (state)|New York]] [[United States Senator|Senator]], [[First Lady of the United States|First Lady]], and [[United States Secretary of State|Secretary of State]], [[Hillary Clinton|Hillary Rodham Clinton]], and the brother-in-law of former [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Bill Clinton]]. 

In 1989 Rodham became Assistant Public Defender for the [[Miami Drug Court]]. Rodham made one run for political office, winning the 1994 Democratic nomination for the United States Senate seat from [[Florida]], but losing the general election to incumbent Senator [[Connie Mack III]]. During the [[Clinton administration]], some of his actions came under public scrutiny. Since then he has been in private practice as a lawyer.

==Early life==
Rodham was raised in a [[United Methodist]] family in suburban [[Park Ridge, Illinois]].<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/> His father, [[Hugh Ellsworth Rodham]] (1911–1993), was of Welsh and English descent.<ref name="nehgs">{{cite web |author=Roberts, Gary Boyd |url=http://www.americanancestors.org/ancestry-of-senator-hillary-rodham-clinton/ |title=Notes on the Ancestry of Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton |publisher=[[New England Historic Genealogical Society]] |accessdate=November 10, 2012 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101203151445/http://www.americanancestors.org/ancestry-of-senator-hillary-rodham-clinton/ |archivedate=December 3, 2010}}</ref> He managed a successful small business in the textile industry.<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/> His mother, [[Dorothy Howell Rodham|Dorothy Emma Howell]] (1919–2011), was a homemaker of English, Scottish, French Canadian, and Welsh descent.<ref name="nehgs"/><ref>{{cite news | author=[[Megan Smolenyak|Smolenyak, Megan]] |url=http://irishamerica.com/2015/03/hillary-clintons-celtic-roots/ |title=Hillary Clinton's Celtic Roots |work=Irish America |date=April–May 2015}}</ref> Hugh has an older sister, [[Hillary Clinton|Hillary]], and younger brother, [[Tony Rodham|Tony]].<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/>  He was often referred to as "Hughie" growing up, to distinguish him from his father, and that name form would persist into adulthood at times.<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article"/><ref name="ch10-mva"/><ref name="wapo-retooling"/>

Attending [[Maine South High School]] in Park Ridge, Rodham was known as what one writer later termed a "fun-loving jock" and was not as academically oriented as his sister had been.<ref name="Spet94 Chicago article">{{cite magazine| last = Hanson| first = Cynthia| title = I Was a Teenage Republican| magazine = [[Chicago (magazine)|Chicago Magazine]]| date = September 1994| url = http://www.chicagomag.com/Chicago-Magazine/September-1994/I-Was-a-Teenage-Republican/index.php?cparticle=1&siarticle=0#artanc| accessdate =20 September 2009| quote = The "boys," as they're still called, are Hughie and Tony ... At Maine South, the boys were known as fun-loving jocks. The Rodhams expected them to perform as well on the football field as Hillary did in the classroom}}</ref>  He graduated from [[Maine South High School]] in 1968, playing on the [[American football|football]], [[wrestling]], and [[baseball]] teams.<ref name="cst022301">{{cite news | url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4155/is_20010223/ai_n13900533 | title=Politics thicker than blood? | author=Lynn Sweet | newspaper=The Chicago Sun-Times | date=2001-02-23 | accessdate=2007-07-08}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref>

Like his father, Rodham attended [[Pennsylvania State University]], graduating in 1972 with a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree from the College of Health and Human Development.  While attending Penn State he was [[substitution (sport)|backup]] [[quarterback]] on the [[Penn State Nittany Lions football]] team.<ref name="cnnbio">{{cite news | url=http://www.cnn.com/ALLPOLITICS/1996/candidates/democrat/clinton/rodham.shtml | title=The Rodham Family Biography | accessdate = 2007-07-08 | publisher=CNN}}</ref>  Rodham was also an active member of the Sigma Triton charge of [[Theta Delta Chi]] fraternity at Penn State.<ref name="pillarsofprospect">{{cite web |url=http://www.sigmatriton.org/v2/wp-content/pdfs/pillars_of_pospect.pdf | title=The Pillars of Prospect: Sigma Phi Sigma/Theta Delta Chi Alumni Association | date=May 2008 | accessdate = 2008-10-20 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081029055444/http://www.sigmatriton.org/v2/wp-content/pdfs/pillars_of_pospect.pdf |archivedate=2008-10-29}}</ref>

He served in the [[Peace Corps]] in [[Colombia]]<ref name="cnnbio"/> for over a year, training [[teacher]]s; he later called it the most rewarding experience of his life.<ref name="cnn022401">[http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0102/24/lklw.00.html "Looking Back on Interviews With the Rodhams and Roger Clinton"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161005211750/http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0102/24/lklw.00.html |date=October 5, 2016 }}, ''[[Larry King Live]]'', February 24, 2001. Accessed July 11, 2007.</ref>  He then gained advanced degrees in [[education studies|education]] (M.A.) and [[law school|law]] ([[Juris Doctor|J.D.]]) at the [[University of Arkansas]],<!-- Fayetteville or Little Rock? don't know --><ref name="cnnbio"/> the latter occurring while [[Bill Clinton]] was governor of the state.<ref name="cst022301"/>  <!-- had to take bar exam multiple times per mh-florida cite ... not sure that needs to be included -->

== Public defender ==
Rodham moved to [[Miami]], where he began to practice [[criminal law]];<ref name="cnnbio"/> from around 1980 he became an investigator for the [[public defender]] in [[Dade County, Florida|Dade County]].<ref name="cnn022401"/><ref name="nyt042397">Barry Meier, [https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9505E2DD133EF930A15757C0A961958260 " First Brother-in-Law Has Tobacco Talks Role"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080124161846/http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9505E2DD133EF930A15757C0A961958260 |date=January 24, 2008 }}, ''[[The New York Times]]'', April 23, 1997. Accessed July 10, 2007.</ref>  For a while there he shared a [[condominium]] with his brother Tony.<ref name="cnnbio"/>  While frequently paired together in commentary, the brothers have distinct personalities; Hugh has been compared by friends and acquaintances to the [[Norm Peterson]] character from the television series ''[[Cheers]]''.<ref name="mh-florida"/>

Rodham married Maria Victoria Arias, a [[Cuba]]n immigrant lawyer whom he met while she was interning at the public defender's office, in 1986.<ref name="cst022301"/><ref name="cnnbio"/>  She had come to the United States from Cuba around 1960, as [[Fidel Castro]] took power, when she was two years old.<ref name="ch10-mva">{{cite news | url=http://www.local10.com/news/politics/who-is-clintons-cuban-american-sister-in-law | title=Who is Clinton's Cuban-American sister-in-law? | first=Glenna | last=Milberg | publisher=[[WPLG]] | location=Miami, Florida | date=July 31, 2015}}</ref>  She graduated from the [[University of Miami Law School]] in 1989 and subsequently became an established [[real estate lawyer]].<ref name="ch10-mva"/><ref name="mh-florida"/>  She switched from being a [[United States Republican Party|Republican]] to a Democrat during the [[Bill Clinton presidential campaign, 1992]], and founded a group known as Cuban-American Women for Clinton.<ref name="ch10-mva"/>  A 1993 ''[[Washington Post]]'' story described her as "a smart, tough attorney, a Cuban version of Hillary."<ref name="wapo-retooling">{{cite news | url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1993/01/13/the-retooling-of-the-political-wife/34247bb9-6199-4279-803d-36117cfcee58/ | title=The Retooling of the Political Wife | first=Martha | last=Sherrill | newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] | date=January 13, 1993}}</ref>  She has given both Bill and Hillary Clinton advice on Cuba matters and an immediate connection to the [[Cuban exile community in Miami]].<ref name="mh-florida"/>  The couple have lived in [[Coral Gables, Florida]], during their married life.<ref name="cnnbio"/>

In 1989 the groundbreaking, much imitated [[Miami Drug Court]] was co-founded by [[State Attorney]] [[Janet Reno]],<ref name="cst022301"/><ref name="cnnbio"/> Chief Judge Gerald Wetherington, and Public Defender Bennett Brummer. Seeking to combat the [[crack epidemic|crack cocaine epidemic]] of the time, it sought to find nonviolent drug users and give addicts structured programs to overcome addiction, often in lieu of a jail sentence. Rodham became Assistant Public Defender in the court.<ref name="cnn022201">[http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0102/22/ip.00.html "Hillary Clinton Addresses Presidential Pardons; Are Clinton Controversies Stifling Bush Agenda?"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161006175116/http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0102/22/ip.00.html |date=October 6, 2016 }}, CNN, February 22, 2001. Accessed July 11, 2007.</ref> In this work Rodham was praised by local officials for effective, tireless work for long hours at low wages;<ref name="cnn022201"/> Rodham himself would later say, "Public Defenders are the last bastion for liberty ... we provide a strong defense for every citizen accused."<ref name="cnn022401"/>  Reno would later credit Rodham for making the Drug Court a success: "That drug court could never have been established without the cooperation of the public defender ... [who] was a fellow by the name of Hugh Rodham, the assistant public defender. And we didn't know who Hugh Rodham was in those days, but one thing he did was get his clients in the back room, and every now and then you could hear him raise his voice and tell them to get with it and let's go. ... He was assiduous in protecting his clients' rights, but he was also extraordinarily helpful in making sure his clients understood that here was a real chance to solve their problems."<ref name="doj">[http://www.usdoj.gov/archive/ag/speeches/1998/0309_agthf.htm "Town Hall Forum with the Attorney General"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090305001739/http://www.usdoj.gov/archive/ag/speeches/1998/0309_agthf.htm |date=March 5, 2009 }}, [[U.S. Department of Justice]], March 9, 1998. Accessed July 11, 2007.</ref>

In 1993, Hugh Rodham and his brother Tony ignited a small controversy when they tried to solicit corporate donations for Clinton's inaugural parties; they dropped the effort after a public outcry.<ref name="time012593">Priscilla Painton, [http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,977542-1,00.html "Guess Who's Paying for Dinner"]  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070930121729/http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0%2C9171%2C977542-1%2C00.html
|date=September 30, 2007 }}, ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'', January 25, 1993. Accessed July 19, 2007.</ref>
Once the Clintons entered the White House, Rodham became a sometime [[golf]]ing buddy of the President<ref name="time022201">{{cite news | url=http://www.time.com/time/nation/article/0,8599,100329,00.html | title=The Rumpled, Ragtag Career of Hugh Rodham | author=Jessica Reaves | magazine=Time  | date=2002-02-22 | accessdate=2006-03-26}}</ref> and he and Maria often joined family get-togethers at [[Camp David]].<ref name="lh">[[Hillary Clinton|Hillary Rodham Clinton]], ''[[Living History (book)|Living History]]'', [[Simon & Schuster]], 2003, {{ISBN|0-7432-2224-5}}, plate B-15.</ref> When Clinton was having trouble with his cabinet nominations, Rodham recommended Reno to the president for appointment as [[U.S. Attorney General]].<ref name="cst022301"/><ref name="cnn110199">Viveca Novak and Jay Branegan, [http://www.cnn.com/ALLPOLITICS/time/1999/10/25/brothers.html "Are Hillary's brothers driving off course?"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304002004/http://www.cnn.com/ALLPOLITICS/time/1999/10/25/brothers.html |date=March 4, 2016 }}, CNN, November 1, 1999. Accessed July 10, 2007.</ref>

==Run for U.S. Senate==
{{seealso|United States Senate election in Florida, 1994}}

Rodham left the public defenders office to run for the [[United States Senate]] in [[Florida]] in 1994. He had some basic problems as a candidate, including having to explain why he had never registered to vote in the state until 1992.<ref name="mh-florida">{{cite news | url=http://www.miamiherald.com/news/politics-government/election/article109171837.html | title=Hillary Clinton's connections to Florida are complicated | first=Adam C. | last=Smith | newspaper=[[Miami Herald]] | agency=[[Tampa Bay Times]] | date=October 19, 2016 | accessdate=April 25, 2018 }}</ref>  In addition, his campaign manager left the campaign after not getting paid for weeks when told there was no money but discovered that funds were in the account he actually set up.<ref name="mh-florida"/> Rodham did not always demonstrate a deep understanding of the political issues of the day and had difficulty being taken seriously by political commentators.<ref name="mh-florida"/>

In the contest for the [[United States Democratic Party|Democratic Party]] nomination, Rodham competed in a four-person primary field and finished first with 34 percent, which meant there would be a [[Two-round system|runoff election]].<ref name="NYT10051994"/> After the first primary, the third-place finisher, flamboyant Miami lawyer and perennial losing candidate [[Ellis Rubin]],<ref name="NewsMax10162000"/> joined forces with Rodham as a "senior executive consultant" and hatchet man.<ref name="mh092294">{{cite news | url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=MH&p_theme=mh&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&s_dispstring=hugh%20rodham%20ellis%20rubin%20mike%20wiley%20AND%20date(all)&p_field_advanced-0=&p_text_advanced-0=(hugh%20rodham%20ellis%20rubin%20mike%20wiley)&xcal_numdocs=20&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&xcal_useweights=no | title=Rubin Joins Rodham Campaign, Rips Wiley | author=Tom Fielder | work=[[The Miami Herald]] | date=1994-09-22 | format=fee required}}</ref>

The second-place finisher and other entrant in the runoff was Mike Wiley, a [[talk radio]] personality and advocate of [[UFO conspiracy theories]].<ref name="time022201"/><ref name="NYT10051994">{{cite news | title = Florida Vote Goes to Brother Of First Lady|newspaper = [[The New York Times]]| date = October 5, 1994| accessdate = 2008-01-29| url = https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9504E6D9153DF936A35753C1A962958260}}</ref> In the presence of Rodham at a press conference, Rubin leveled the accusation that Wiley was hiding his [[Jewish]] faith by changing his name from his birth name, Michael Schreiber,<ref name="NYT10051994"/><ref name="NewsMax10162000">{{cite news|title=More Anti-Semitism in Hillary's Closet |publisher=[[NewsMax]] |date=October 16, 2000 |accessdate=2008-01-29 |url=http://archive.newsmax.com/scripts/showinside.pl?a=2000/10/15/230026 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130104091338/http://archive.newsmax.com/scripts/showinside.pl?a=2000/10/15/230026 |url-status=dead |archive-date=January 4, 2013 }}</ref> and that Wiley "changed his name before the campaign to deceive voters about his Jewish religion."<ref name="NYT10051994"/>  Rodham won the runoff contest by a margin of 58 to 42 percent,<ref name="time022201"/><ref name="NYT10051994">{{cite news | title = Florida Vote Goes to Brother Of First Lady|newspaper = [[The New York Times]]| date = October 5, 1994| accessdate = 2008-01-29| url = https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9504E6D9153DF936A35753C1A962958260}}</ref> but as a result of the friction that had developed, Wiley refused to endorse Rodham afterward.<ref name="NYT10051994"/>

In the general election contest against incumbent Republican Senator [[Connie Mack III]],<ref name="cnnbio"/> Rodham had few funds, only one television commercial, and little support from the Florida Democratic party establishment in [[United States Senate elections, 1994|a year that saw Republican gains everywhere]].<ref name="cst022301"/><ref name="cnnbio"/> Bill and Hillary Clinton both campaigned for Rodham, but his organization was unable to take advantage of their help.<ref name="nyt101694">Michael Wines, [https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9B02EFDE123CF935A25753C1A962958260 "Clinton Finds Few Listeners at Rally in Miami"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080124161923/http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9B02EFDE123CF935A25753C1A962958260 |date=January 24, 2008 }}, ''[[The New York Times]]'', October 16, 1994.  Accessed July 10, 2007.</ref> In one case late in the race, Bill Clinton flew to Miami to speak at a rally for Rodham but only 200 people were in the audience due to poor advance work by the campaign.<ref name="nyt101694"/><ref name="sunsent-hr">{{cite news | url=http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/2001-02-22/news/0102220092_1_hugh-rodham-carlos-vignali-rodham-s-involvement | title=Rodham Returns Pardon Fees | first=Buddy | last=Nevins | first2=Mary | last2=Hladky | newspaper=[[Sun-Sentinel]] | location=Ft. Lauderdale | date=February 22, 2001}}</ref>

In November 1994, Rodham lost by a 70 percent to 30 percent margin to Mack.<ref name="cnnbio"/>  Rodham lost in even normally Democratic areas such as [[Broward County, Florida|Broward County]].<ref name="sunsent-hr"/>
 
After the election, Rubin switched allegiance again and charged Rodham with election law violations in the first primary.<ref name="mh-dis"/> The [[Federal Elections Commission]] eventually dismissed the allegations.<ref name="mh-dis">{{cite news | url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=MH&p_theme=mh&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&s_dispstring=%22hugh%20rodham%22%20%22ellis%20rubin%22%20AND%20date(all)&p_field_advanced-0=&p_text_advanced-0=(%22hugh%20rodham%22%20%22ellis%20rubin%22)&xcal_numdocs=20&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&xcal_useweights=no | title=FEC Dismisses Allegations Against Rodham Campaign | author=Tom Fielder | work=[[The Miami Herald]] | date=1996-04-06 | format=fee required}}</ref>

Rodham then had some involvement in Broward County politics, supporting a candidate for the County Commission who lost.<ref name="sunsent-hr"/> He subsequently tried to unseat the Dade County Democratic Party Chairman; after badly losing that race, he disappeared from the Florida political scene.<ref name="cst022301"/>

==Return to law==
After losing the Senate race, Rodham returned to law, while also trying his hand at hosting a syndicated [[talk radio]] show around 1996.<ref name="cnnbio"/><ref name="cnn022401"/> The radio show failed to find an audience.<ref name="mh-florida"/> During 1995 through 1997 Rodham started working on a very large [[tobacco lawsuit]] with other attorneys; observers were puzzled as to Rodham's involvement, given his limited experience in the area, and speculated that [[influence peddling]] might be the reason.<ref name="cst022301"/><ref name="nyt042397"/>  The massive case eventually failed to gain Congressional approval,<ref name="time022201"/> but by one report a 2002 settlement involved in it made Rodham financially secure.<ref name="mh-florida"/>

In 1999, Hugh and brother [[Tony Rodham]] entered into a $118 million venture to grow and export [[hazelnuts]] from the [[Georgia (country)|Republic of Georgia]].<ref name="time022201"/>  The [[U.S. State Department]] and [[National Security Advisor (United States)|National Security Advisor]] [[Sandy Berger]] became upset, however, when the Rodhams' local business connection in [[Batumi]] turned out to be [[Aslan Abashidze]], a major political opponent of Georgian President [[Eduard Shevardnadze]], then a key U.S. ally in the region.<ref name="time022201"/><ref name="cnn110199"/><ref name="mnt">[http://bestof.miaminewtimes.com/bestof/award.php?award=15376 "Best Local Boy Gone Bad (2001)"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927001615/http://bestof.miaminewtimes.com/bestof/award.php?award=15376 |date=September 27, 2007 }}, ''[[Miami New Times]]''. Accessed July 10, 2007.</ref>  After initial resistance,<ref name="cnn110199"/> Berger and the Clintons prevailed upon the Rodham brothers to drop the deal.<ref name="cst022301"/><ref name="time022201"/>  Hugh Rodham stated that he was only acting as a lawyer for the venture and did not have money invested in it.<ref name="cnn110199"/>

Episodes such as these led Hillary Clinton's White House staff to refer to Hugh and Tony as "the Brothers Rodham",<ref name="nyt022301">Todd S. Purdum, [https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=950DE3DE1339F930A15751C0A9679C8B63 "Siblings Who Often Emerge In an Unflattering Spotlight"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110121141247/http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=950DE3DE1339F930A15751C0A9679C8B63 |date=January 21, 2011 }}, ''[[The New York Times]]'', February 23, 2001. Accessed July 28, 2007.</ref> extending the American tradition of [[List of noted U.S. presidential relatives|troublesome presidential siblings]] to the [[brother-in-law]] category;<ref name="cnn110199"/> one senior White House official would be quoted as saying, "You never wanted to hear their name come up in any context other than playing golf."<ref name="nyt022301"/>

As the [[Clinton administration]] came to a close in early 2001, it was discovered that Hugh Rodham received around $400,000 for legal services regarding gaining the Presidential pardon of businessman [[Glenn Braswell]] who had been convicted of fraud, and the sentence commutation of drug trafficker [[Carlos Vignali]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0102/22/bn.04.html|title=CNN Transcript - Breaking News: Pardon Controversies Deepen With Involvement of Hugh Rodham |date=February 22, 2001|publisher=CNN|accessdate=April 8, 2007}}</ref>  While legal experts said that Rodham may well not have done anything wrong, the appearance of possible impropriety certainly existed.<ref name="cnn022201"/> Moreover, coming while the [[Bill Clinton pardons controversy]] was already in full force, this was a further embarrassment for the former administration and even got the attention of the Congressional [[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]].<ref name="cst022301"/>  Hillary Clinton, now a newly-sworn-in Senator, said, "He's my brother. I love my brother ... I'm just extremely disappointed in this terrible misjudgment that he made ... I knew nothing about my brother's involvement in these pardons. I knew nothing about his taking money for his involvement."<ref name="cst022301"/>  Both Clintons pressured Rodham to return the $400,000, which he promptly did.<ref name="time022201"/><ref name="sunsent-hr"/>  During this time, Rodham additionally collected media criticism for being overweight and a poor dresser.<ref name="time022201"/><ref name="mnt"/>  By 2001 he was working in the law firm of Rodham & Fine in downtown [[Fort Lauderdale]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2001-02-23/news/0102230141_1_clinton-administration-hugh-rodham-pardon | title=Rodham Is A Tough Guy To Pin Down | author=Maya Bell | newspaper=[[Orlando Sentinel]] | date=February 23, 2001}}</ref><ref name="sunsent-hr"/>

Subsequently, Rodham stayed out of the public eye.<ref name="mj-2015"/>  When [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2008|his sister's 2008 presidential campaign]] came to the family summer home of [[Scranton, Pennsylvania]], for the [[Pennsylvania Democratic primary, 2008|state primary]], he hosted a reception for campaign workers.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/03/10/us/politics/10scranton.html | title=Pennsylvania Ties Could Help Clinton | author=Katherine Q. Seelye | newspaper=[[The New York Times]] | date=2008-03-10 | accessdate=2008-03-14}}</ref>  By the mid-2010s, he was still working at Rodham & Fine.<ref name="mj-2015">{{cite news | url=https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2015/04/hillary-clinton-hugh-tony-rodham | title=Hillary Clinton Has a Family Problem – and It's Not Bill | author=Tim Murphy | magazine=[[Mother Jones (magazine)|Mother Jones]] | date=April 8, 2015}}</ref><ref name="mh-florida"/>  During the [[Hillary Clinton presidential campaign, 2016]], he and Tony appeared with her at a rally in Scranton in April 2016.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/politics/first-draft/2016/04/23/hillary-clintons-pennsylvania-visit-is-another-welcome-home/ | title=Hillary Clinton's Pennsylvania Visit Is Another 'Welcome Home' | first=Thomas | last=Kaplan | newspaper=[[The New York Times]] | date=April 23, 2016 | accessdate=April 26, 2018}}</ref>  But generally he kept a low profile during the campaign and declined to speak to the press.<ref name="mh-florida"/>
