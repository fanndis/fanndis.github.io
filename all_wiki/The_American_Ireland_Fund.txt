The American Ireland Fund (DBA '''The Ireland Funds America'''), is a tax-exempt organization incorporated under the laws of the United States and has been determined by the IRS to be a public charity under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, tax ID # is 25-1306992. The organization raises funds for the support of peace and reconciliation, community development, education, and arts and culture in [[Ireland]].
Collectively with The Ireland Funds’ 12 worldwide chapters, it has raised more than $550 million (US), benefiting more than 3,000 Irish charities.<ref name="Fund">[https://irelandfunds.org/ The Ireland Funds], ''The Ireland Funds'', Boston, MA, 2014, Retrieved 5 January 2014.</ref><ref name="Organization">[http://www.charitynavigator.org/index.cfm?bay=search.summary&orgid=4527#.UsnQKr3Tm70 American Ireland Fund], ''Charity Navigator'', 2014, Retrieved 5 January 2014.</ref> 

==History==
Founded by [[Tony O'Reilly]] and [[Dan Rooney]], The Ireland Funds America is part of [[The Ireland Funds]]. The Ireland Funds were founded in 1976.<ref name="Ireland">[https://irelandfunds.org/about-us About Us], ''The Ireland Funds'', Boston, MA, 2014, Retrieved 5 January 2014.</ref> On St. Patrick's Day of 1987, The Ireland Fund merged with the American Irish Foundation. The American Irish Foundation had been founded by US President [[John F. Kennedy]] and Irish President [[Éamon de Valera]]. After the merger, the new organization took the name The American Ireland Fund. The merger was made official at a [[White House]] function.<ref name=Byrne>{{Citation

Today, the global organization is known as The Ireland Funds. Operating in 12 countries with worldwide membership chapters, The Ireland Funds have raised more than $600 million (US) for over 3,200 organizations.<ref name="Ireland" /> ''[[The Irish Times]]'' reported that the organization gave $15.7 million to such Irish organizations in 2012. The organization's net revenue amounted to more than $20 million in both 2011 and 2012.<ref name=Times>{{cite news|last=Hancock|first=Ciaran|title=American Ireland Fund chief Kieran McLoughlin paid $619,000 in 2012|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/business/sectors/american-ireland-fund-chief-kieran-mcloughlin-paid-619-000-in-2012-1.1637860|accessdate=January 7, 2014|newspaper=[[The Irish Times]]|date=December 27, 2013}}</ref>According to the organisation's 2015 Form 990, total unrestricted revenues and support amounted to $29,893,941.<ref name="The Ireland Funds Financials">https://irelandfunds.org/about-us/the-ireland-funds-america/financials/</ref>

The American Ireland Fund donated between $500,000 and $1 million to the [[Clinton Foundation]].<ref name=clinton>{{cite news|last=Carswell|first=Simon|title=Clinton-linked firm touted ability to 'secure' funds from Irish charity|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/world/us/clinton-linked-firm-touted-ability-to-secure-funds-from-irish-charity-1.2844741|newspaper=[[The Irish Times]]|date=October 27, 2016|accessdate=2018-01-14}}</ref>
