''' European Solidarity''' ({{lang-uk|Європейська солідарність}}, ''Yevropeys'ka Solidarnist' '') is a [[List of political parties in Ukraine|political party]] in [[Ukraine]]. It has its roots in a [[parliamentary group]] called '''Solidarity''' dating from 2000<ref name=SDnet/> and has existed since in various forms as a political outlet for [[Petro Poroshenko]].

The party with its then name '''Petro Poroshenko Bloc''' won 132 of the 423 contested seats in the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election]], more than any other party.<ref>{{cite news|author1= Ian Bateson|author2= Olena Goncharova|author3= Kyiv Post staff|url= http://www.kyivpost.com/content/ukraine/poroshenko-and-yatsenyuks-parties-maneuver-for-lead-role-in-coalition-369983.html|title= Poroshenko and Yatsenyuk's parties maneuver for lead role in coalition|work= [[Kyiv Post]]|date= 29 October 2014|accessdate= 5 November 2015}}<br />{{cite news|url= https://www.kyivpost.com/opinion/editorial/new-verkhovna-rada-370121.html|title= New Verkhovna Rada|work= [[Kyiv Post]]|date= 30 October 2014|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20141031101752/http://www.kyivpost.com/opinion/editorial/new-verkhovna-rada-370121.html|url-status= dead|archive-date= 31 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="allcountedCECIU81114">{{cite news|url= http://www.ukrinform.net/rubric-politics/1726373-poroshenko_bloc_to_have_greatest_number_of_seats_in_parliament_327072.html|title= Poroshenko Bloc to have greatest number of seats in parliament|work= [[Ukrinform]]|date= 8 November 2014|accessdate= 5 November 2015|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160109180125/http://www.ukrinform.net/rubric-politics/1726373-poroshenko_bloc_to_have_greatest_number_of_seats_in_parliament_327072.html|archive-date= 9 January 2016|url-status= dead}}<br />{{cite news|url= http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233404.html|title= People's Front 0.33% ahead of Poroshenko Bloc with all ballots counted in Ukraine elections - CEC|agency= [[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date= 8 November 2014|accessdate= 5 November 2015}}<br />{{cite news|url= http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233426.html|title= Poroshenko Bloc to get 132 seats in parliament - CEC|agency= [[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date= 8 November 2014|accessdate= 5 November 2015}}</ref>

In August 2015, the [[Ukrainian Democratic Alliance for Reform]] merged into the party.<ref name="mergeudar">{{cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/286616.html|title=Klitschko becomes leader of Petro Poroshenko Bloc 'Solidarity' party|work=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date=28 August 2015|accessdate=5 November 2015}}</ref> But in May 2019, UDAR undid this merge.<ref name="pravda.com.ua">{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2019/05/18/7215407/ Klitschko said that UDAR will go to the polls], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (May 18, 2019)</ref>

In October 2017, the party had about 30,000 members; former members of [[Party of Regions]] are denied membership.<ref name="members1017">{{cite news|url= http://m.glavcom.ua/publications/bezgolova-partiya-vladi-shcho-vidbuvajetsya-z-bpp-442958.html|title= Headless party power. What happens to the BPP?|work=[[Glavcom]]|date=10 October 2017|accessdate=10 October 2017|language=Ukrainian}}</ref>

In the [[2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election]], the party won 23 seats on the nationwide party list and 2 constituency seats.<ref name="2748306cec100percentES">[https://www.ukrinform.net/rubric-elections/2748306-cec-counts-100-percent-of-vote-in-ukraines-parliamentary-elections.html CEC counts 100 percent of vote in Ukraine's parliamentary elections], [[Ukrinform]] (26 July 2019)<br />{{in lang|ru}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/rus/articles/2019/07/21/7221526/ Results of the extraordinary elections of the People's Deputies of Ukraine 2019], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (21 July 2019)</ref>

==History==

===Solidarity (2000–2013)===
The party started in 2000 as a parliamentary faction called "Solidarity",<ref name=SDnet/> set up by [[Petro Poroshenko]], until then a member of the [[Social Democratic Party of Ukraine (united)]] [[Centre for Eastern Studies]] (7 November 2012)</ref>) and Poroshenko became a Party of Regions deputy.<ref name=PoroWP/>

In 2001, Poroshenko expressed interest in the creation of the [[Our Ukraine Bloc]].<ref name=PoroWP/> However, in order to receive quote in Our Ukraine he had to join the bloc with his whole party.<ref name=PoroWP /><ref name=Twice2713 /> The Party of Ukraine's Solidarity failed to break away from the Party of Regions, therefore Poroshenko decided to create a new [[wikt:phantom|phantom]] party with a similar name, the party "Solidarity".<ref name=PoroWP /> At the [[2002 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2002 parliamentary elections]] Solidarity was able to join Our Ukraine.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=H23Pv4Ik3vMC&pg=PA391&dq=Solidarity+Our+Ukraine+Bloc&hl=nl&sa=X&ei=FycoUb_-C6TL0AWNvoDQAQ&ved=0CDUQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=Solidarity%20Our%20Ukraine%20Bloc&f=false Communist and Post-Communist Parties in Europe] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107173813/https://books.google.com/books?id=H23Pv4Ik3vMC&pg=PA391&dq=Solidarity+Our+Ukraine+Bloc&hl=nl&sa=X&ei=FycoUb_-C6TL0AWNvoDQAQ&ved=0CDUQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=Solidarity%20Our%20Ukraine%20Bloc&f=false |date=January 7, 2014 }}, [[Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht]], 2008, {{ISBN|3-525-36912-3}} (page 391)</ref> Top party members who received a parliamentary mandate on party list of the Our Ukraine electoral bloc in 2002 were [[Volodymyr Plyutynsky]], [[Volodymyr Makeyenko]], [[Eduard Matviychuk]], [[Anatoliy Korchynsky]], while a single constituency in Vinnytsia Oblast was won by [[Petro Poroshenko]].

After 2002, Solidarity stopped participating in elections.<ref name=SDnet>{{Cite web|url=http://sd.net.ua/2009/09/04/partja_soldarnst.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160818172525/http://sd.net.ua/2009/09/04/partja_soldarnst.html|url-status=dead|title=Партія "Солідарність"|archivedate=August 18, 2016|website=ASD-inform Украина}}</ref><ref>{{in lang|uk}} [https://web.archive.org/web/20121127194105/http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2012/wp039?PT001F01=900 Results of voting in single constituencies in 2012] & [https://web.archive.org/web/20120815025015/http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2012/wp400?PT001F01=900 Nationwide list], [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref> In 2004, the party left Our Ukraine, and was represented by 23 deputies in the [[Verkhovna Rada]] (the forming of new factions whose parties were not directly elected into parliament was not unique in Ukraine at the time.){{clarify|date=February 2015}}<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20041201074312/http://www.globalsecurity.org/military/world/ukraine/parties.htm Ukraine Political Parties], GlobalSecurity.org (Archived from [http://www.globalsecurity.org/military/world/ukraine/parties.htm#trud the original] on 17 November 2014)</ref> In March 2013 the [[Ministry of Justice of Ukraine|Ministry of Justice]] asked the [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]] for evidence that Solidarity had not been involved in elections since 2003.<ref name=PoroWP />

On 17 June 2013, [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"|Fatherland]] [[People's Deputy of Ukraine|member of parliament]] [[Yuriy Stets]] became head of the party.<ref name=Twice2713>[http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/83457 United Twice] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160316003111/http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/83457 |date=March 16, 2016 }}, ''[[The Ukrainian Week]]'' (2 July 2013)</ref> Stets was a member of the [[Batkivshchyna#"United Opposition" during the 2012 parliamentary elections|united opposition]]'s political council.<ref name=Twice2713 />

On 16 October 2013, a court cancelled the registration certificate of Solidarity.<ref name=PoroWP /> The party could have challenged this on appeal, but did not<ref name=PoroWP /> and was legally eliminated on 31 December 2013 "due to lack of reporting".<ref name=PoroPMyth /> and because for more than 10 years had not participated in any election.<ref name=PoroWP>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/rus/articles/2014/06/6/7028205/view_print/ Poroshenko goes to work] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303230000/http://www.pravda.com.ua/rus/articles/2014/06/6/7028205/view_print/ |date=March 3, 2016 }}, ''[[Ukrayinska Pravda]]'' (6 June 2014)</ref>

===Petro Poroshenko Bloc (2014–2019)===
[[File:Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity" logo.svg|thumb|Petro Poroshenko Bloc logo]]
Early in 2014 Poroshenko became leader of the '''National Alliance of freedom and Ukrainian patriotism "OFFENSIVE"''', which was renamed "All-Ukrainian Union Solidarity".<ref name=PoroWP/><ref name=PoroPMyth>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/articles/2014/05/16/7025568/ Poroshenko and void] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160514035410/http://www.pravda.com.ua/articles/2014/05/16/7025568/ |date=May 14, 2016 }}, [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (16 May 2014)</ref> By doing so, Poroshenko de facto prolonged the life of Solidarity and de facto merged the National Alliance of freedom and Ukrainian patriotism "OFFENSIVE" into Solidarity<ref name=PoroWP/><ref name=PoroPMyth/> (legally the original party "Solidarity" does not exist anymore<ref name=PoroWP/><ref name=PoroPMyth/>). National Alliance of freedom and Ukrainian patriotism "OFFENSIVE" was registered in May 2000 under the name '''All-Ukrainian Party of Peace and Unity''' ({{lang-uk|Всеукраїнська партія миру i єдності}}, HPEM). It was not allowed to participate in the [[electoral alliance]] "Rainbow" in the [[2002 Ukrainian parliamentary election|Ukrainian 2002 parliamentary elections]].<ref name=DATA/> In the [[2006 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2006 elections]], the party failed as part of the electoral alliance [[Yuriy Karmazin Bloc]] to win parliamentary representation.<ref name=DATA/> In the [[2007 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2007 elections]], the party failed again as part of the [[All-Ukrainian Community]] to win parliamentary representation.<ref name=DATA>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.da-ta.com.ua/mon_mainnews/874.htm Всеукраїнська партія Миру і Єдності], DATA</ref> After this election (the party) "All-Ukrainian Party of Peace and Unity" was renamed National Alliance of Freedom and Ukrainian patriotism "OFFENSIVE".<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.cvk.gov.ua/party/ BASIC INFORMATION ABOUT POLITICAL PARTIES IN UKRAINE] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006074404/http://www.cvk.gov.ua/party/ |date=2014-10-06 }}, [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref> National Alliance of freedom and Ukrainian patriotism "OFFENSIVE" did not participate in the [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2012 parliamentary elections]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2012/wp039?PT001F01=900 Results of voting in single constituencies in 2012] {{Webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20121228133412/www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2012/wp039?PT001F01=900 |date=2012-12-28 }} & [http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2012/wp400?PT001F01=900 Nationwide list] {{Webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20121222055848/www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2012/wp400?PT001F01=900 |date=2012-12-22 }}, [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref>

On 21 November 2014 the party became a member of the coalition supporting the current [[second Yatsenyuk government]] and endorsed nine new ministers for the government.<ref name="FLUYGIU21214">{{cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/237576.html|title=Rada supports coalition-proposed government lineup|agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date=2 December 2014|accessdate=5 November 2015}}<br />{{cite news|author1=Kyiv Post staff writers|author2=Oksana Grytsenko|url=http://www.kyivpost.com/content/ukraine/rada-approves-new-cabinet-with-three-former-foreigners-in-it-373861.html|title=Rada approves new Cabinet with three foreigners|work=[[Kyiv Post]]|date=2 December 2014|accessdate=5 November 2015}}<br />{{cite news|url=http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/12/2/7046214/|script-title=uk:Рада проголосувала новий кабмін|trans-title=Rada voted in a new cabinet|language=uk|work=[[Ukrayinska Pravda]]|date=2 December 2014|accessdate=5 November 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/235649.html|title=Five political forces sign coalition agreement|agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date=21 November 2014|accessdate=5 November 2015}}<br />{{cite news|url=http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/235527.html|title=Ukraine's parliamentary parties initial coalition agreement|agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|date=21 November 2014|accessdate=5 November 2015}}</ref>

In March 2015, "Solidarity" was added to the name "Bloc of Petro Poroshenko". On 28 August 2015 [[UDAR]] and Petro Poroshenko Bloc officially merged into Petro Poroshenko Bloc.<ref name="mergeudar"/> UDAR party leader [[Vitali Klitschko]] became so the new party leader.<ref name=mergeudar/>

Klitschko resigned as Petro Poroshenko Bloc chairman (on 26 May) after a new law barring him as head of the [[Kyiv City State Administration]] to be chairman or a member of a political party took effect on 1 May 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.unian.info/kyiv/1356522-klitschko-resigns-as-bpp-solidarnist-chairman.html |title=Klitschko resigns as BPP Solidarnist chairman|accessdate=2016-06-25|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160807111424/http://www.unian.info/kiev/1356522-klitschko-resigns-as-bpp-solidarnist-chairman.html|archivedate=2016-08-07}}</ref>

===European Solidarity (from 2019)===
[[File:Електоральна підтримка партії Європейська Солідарність на Парламентських виборах в Україні 2019 року.svg|thumb|Results of the 2019 elections]]
The party changed its name to its current form on 24 May 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ukranews.com/en/news/633620-petro-poroshenko-bloc-renames-as-european-solidarity|title=Petro Poroshenko Bloc Renames As European Solidarity &#124; Ukrainian news}}</ref> According to party leader Poroshenko this had to be done in order to bring in a new leadership of the party and to win the [[2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2019/05/24/7216047/|title=Партія Порошенка змінила назву|website=Українська правда}}</ref>

Top 10 politicians on the party list to the Ukrainian parliament in these elections were 1. [[Petro Poroshenko]], 2. [[Andriy Parubiy]], 3. [[Iryna Herashchenko]], 4. [[Mykhailo Zabrodskyi]], 5. [[Sofia Fedyna]], 6. [[Mustafa Dzhemilev]], 7. [[Yana Zinkevych]], 8. [[Oleh Synyutka]], 9. [[Akhtem Chyihoz]], 10. In the 2019 parliamentary election, European Solidarity scored badly, dropping to 8,10% of votes and electing 23 MPs (37% of which were women<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2019/07/23/7221668/ The new Council has increased the number of women deputies], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (23 July 2019)</ref>) on the nationwide party list and winning 2 constituency seats.<ref name="2748306cec100percentES"/> The party voted against the confidence vote in the new [[Honcharuk Government]].

In June 2020 former Secretary of the [[National Security and Defense Council of Ukraine|National Security and Defense Council]] during Poroshenko's presidency [[Oleksandr Turchynov]] became head of the [[2020 Ukrainian local elections]] headquarters of the party.<ref name="7257588TurchynovBB">{{in lang|uk}} [https://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2020/06/30/7257588/ Turchynov will head the Eurosolidarity headquarters and will look for effective managers], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (30 June 2020)</ref> In this election European Solidarity did well in [[West Ukraine|West]] and [[central Ukraine]] and it significantly improved on its 2019 parliamentary election result in all [[Ukrainian oblasts]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [https://blogs.pravda.com.ua/authors/fesenko/5fbb7f2c065f1/ Party measurement of the results of local elections in 2020], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (23 November 2020)</ref><ref>[https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/ukrainealert/winners-and-losers-of-ukraines-local-elections/ Winners and losers of Ukraine’s local elections], [[Atlantic Council]] (2 November 2020)</ref>

==Ideology and positions==

The party officially decries populism and advocates for pragmatism and realism.<ref name="offplat"/> According to [[Oleg Varfolomeyev]] of the [[Eurasia Daily Monitor]] the party is a [[Liberalism|liberal]] party (and [[UDAR]] was as well).<ref name="Oleg Varfolomeyev">{{cite news|author=Oleg Varfolomeyev|url=http://www.ukrweekly.com/uwwp/ukraines-pro-government-parties-join-forces-ahead-of-local-elections/|title=Ukraine's pro-government parties join forces ahead of local elections|publisher=[[Ukrainian Weekly]]|agency=Eurasia Daily Monitor|date=18 September 2015|accessdate=5 November 2015}}</ref> According to [[Bohdan Butkevych]] of [[The Ukrainian Week]], the party does not have an ideological unity.<ref name=messBPP/> Due to the fact the party was created shortly before the [[2014 Ukrainian parliamentary election]] and then its "party list was drawn up by taking almost anyone who was ready and willing to invest their own resources".<ref name=messBPP/> Hence its parliamentary faction consists of people who "have very different interests, methods of getting into parliament and plans".<ref name=messBPP>[http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/160227 Petro Poroshenko Bloc: fragmentation] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160528012628/http://ukrainianweek.com/Politics/160227 |date=May 28, 2016 }}, [[The Ukrainian Week]] (8 March 2016)</ref> (Hence) the party's MPs tend not to vote alike.<ref name=messBPP/>

The party broadly reflects Poroshenko's ideology. On 27 August 2014 newly elected party leader Yuriy Lutsenko stated that the Petro Poroshenko Bloc should help Poroshenko implement his election promises.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/08/27/7035916/ Poroshenko will be honorary leader of "Solidarity" party can head Lutsenko] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303215011/http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/08/27/7035916/ |date=March 3, 2016 }}, [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (27 August 2014)</ref> Official party positions include:<ref name="offplat">{{cite web|url=http://solydarnist.org/?page_id=874|title=ПРОГРАМА ПАРТІЇ|publisher=}}</ref>
* [[Open list]] elections
* [[Decentralization]]
* Creating a [[public television]] network
* Bringing attention to the plight of the [[Crimean Tatars]]
* Ensuring language rights for [[Russian language in Ukraine|Russian speakers]] while maintaining Ukrainian as the sole official language
* [[Ukraine and the European Union|Membership of Ukraine in the European Union]]
* Welfare and social protection for poor citizens
* [[National Police of Ukraine|Law enforcement reform]] and creation of an independent judiciary
* Ending corruption
* Ensuring Ukraine's territorial integrity
* Energy independence for Ukraine
* Abolishing the immunity of senior officials<ref>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231070.html Poroshenko Block ready to vote for scrapping presidential immunity - Lutsenko] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160806150825/http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231070.html |date=August 6, 2016 }}, [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27.10.2014)</ref>
* Privatizing all [[Coal in Ukraine|Ukrainian coal mines]] and liquidate or mothball all mines that cannot be privatized, with social support for the workers of the liquidated or mothballed mines and the population of these territories<ref>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231805.html Bloc of Petro Poroshenko proposes privatization of mines, exchange trading in coal be fixed in coalition agreement] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160806160246/http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231805.html|date=August 6, 2016}}, [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (29 October 2014)</ref>
* Legislation to restrict religions whose leadership reside in aggressor states, e.g. Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://euromaidanpress.com/2017/05/19/ukrainian-legislation-about-religion-will-finalize-divorce-between-kyiv-and-moscow-euromaidan-press/#arvlbdata|title=Ukrainian legislation about religion will finalize divorce between Kyiv and Moscow|date=19 May 2017|accessdate=25 September 2017}}<br />{{cite web|url=https://www.rt.com/news/388751-ukraine-orthodox-church-discriminatory/|title=Russian church leader asks UN, Pope to intervene with Kiev over 'discriminatory' religious laws|date=17 May 2017|accessdate=25 September 2017}}</ref>

As to the [[War in Donbass]], the party advocates an end to the conflict by peaceful means.<ref name="Olszanski"/>
