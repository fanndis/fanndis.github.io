'''[[Snap election|Snap]] elections''' to the [[Verkhovna Rada]] took place on 26 October 2014.

[[Petro Poroshenko]], the [[President of Ukraine]], had pressed for early parliamentary elections since his victory in the [[2014 Ukrainian presidential election|presidential election]] in May.<ref name=IUEEU>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/210172.html Poroshenko hopes for early parliamentary elections in Ukraine this fall - presidential envoy], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (19 June 2014)</ref> The July breakup of the ruling coalition gave him the right to dissolve the parliament, so on 25 August 2014 he announced the early election.<ref name=PsUE25814>[https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-08-25/ukraine-president-poroshenko-calls-snap-general-election.html Ukraine President Poroshenko Calls Snap General Election], [[Bloomberg News]] (25 August 2014)</ref>

The voting was not provided in the [[Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation|annexed]] [[Autonomous Republic of Crimea]] and [[Sevastopol]]. The voting also did not reach significant parts of [[Donetsk Oblast|Donetsk]] and [[Luhansk Oblast|Luhansk]] oblasts because of the ongoing [[War in Donbass]].<ref name=27sIU2510>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/230595.html Parliamentary elections not to be held at nine constituencies in Donetsk region and six constituencies in Luhansk region - CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (25 October 2014)</ref> Because of this, 27 out of the 450 seats in the [[8th Ukrainian Verkhovna Rada|8th Verkhovna Rada]] remained unfilled.

This election can be called a [[realigning election|realignment]]. Ruling from 2010–2014, and taking one of the top 2 spots in elections since 2006, the [[Party of Regions]] did not participate in this election, while its informal successor [[Opposition Bloc]] showed only a modest result with 9.43% of the vote. For the first time since Ukrainian independence, the [[Communist Party of Ukraine]] gained no parliamentary representation. Four newly created parties got the highest support in this election: [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity"|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]] (formed in July 2014 by Poroshenko's supporters), [[People's Front (Ukraine)|People's Front]] (split from [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"|Fatherland]] in August 2014), [[Self Reliance (political party)|Self Reliance]] (registered in 2012) and [[Opposition Bloc]] (formed in September 2014 by a group of the former [[Party of Regions]] members).

Since the [[parallel voting]] system was used, the allocation of seats is not proportional. For example, the winner in party-list voting (22.12%) [[People's Front (Ukraine)|People's Front]] got second faction with 82 seats, while [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity"|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]] took 123 seats with 21.82% of votes.

The work of the [[8th Ukrainian Verkhovna Rada|new parliament]] started on 27 November 2014.<ref name=endRda714>[http://www.unian.info/politics/1014078-parliament-to-form-leadership-and-coalition-on-november-27.html "Parliament to form leadership and coalition on November 27"], [[UNIAN]] (26 November 2014)</ref> On the same day, five factions formed the "European Ukraine" coalition: [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity"|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]], [[People's Front (Ukraine)|People's Front]], [[Self Reliance (political party)|Self Reliance]], [[Radical Party of Oleh Lyashko|Radical Party]] and [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"|Fatherland]].<ref>http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/236635.html</ref> On 2 December the [[second Yatsenyuk government]] was approved.<ref>http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/237576.html</ref>

==Background==
According to the election law of November 2011, elections to the [[Verkhovna Rada]] must take place at least every five years.<ref name=newUKRelectionlawof171111>{{cite news|title=Parliament passes law on parliamentary elections|url=https://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/parliament-passes-law-on-parliamentary-elections-117151.html|accessdate=9 August 2015|agency=[[Interfax-Ukraine]]|publisher=[[Kyiv Post]]|date=17 November 2011}}</ref><ref name=ofloeoPDoU>{{in lang|uk}} [http://zakon4.rada.gov.ua/laws/show/4061-17 Law of Ukraine "On Elections of People's Deputies of Ukraine " dated 17 November 2011], [[Verkhovna Rada|Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine]]</ref> That law came into effect with the [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election]]. If the Rada had sat for the maximum allotted time, the next parliamentary election would have occurred on 29 October 2017.<ref name=newUKRelectionlawof171111/> Despite this, the president-elect [[Petro Poroshenko]] said that he wanted to hold early parliamentary elections following his victory in the [[2014 Ukrainian presidential election|presidential election]] on 25 May 2014.<ref name=IUEEU/> At 26 June session of the [[Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe]], Poroshenko said that he hoped to hold parliamentary elections in October 2014, portraying this as "the most democratic way".<ref name=IUEEUZZ>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/211079.html Poroshenko hopes early parliamentary elections in Ukraine will take place in October], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (26 June 2014)</ref>{{#tag:ref|In a [[Research & Branding Group]] opinion poll held from 26 July until 5 August 72% of respondents supported the call for early elections.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/08/15/7034944/ Більше 70% українців - за переобрання Ради (Over 70% of Ukrainians for the re-election of the Rada)], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (15 August 2014)</ref>|group=nb}}

The parliamentary coalition that supported the [[First Yatsenyuk government|Yatsenyuk Government]], formed in the aftermath of the [[2014 Ukrainian revolution]] and of the [[Euromaidan]] movement, was dissolved on 24 July.<ref name=YGdSOT>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/215293.html "Rada speaker announces dissolution of parliamentary coalition"], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (24 July 2014)</ref> If no new coalition formed within thirty days, President Poroshenko would become entitled to dissolve the Rada and to call early parliamentary elections.<ref name=YGdSOT/> On the same day as the dissolution, the [[People's Will (parliamentary group)|Sovereign European Ukraine]] faction submitted a bill to the Rada that called for elections to take place on 28 September 2014.<ref name=SUEwbne>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/215314.html "Rada registers bill setting early parliamentary elections date for September 28, 2014"], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (24 July 2014)</ref>

In an interview with Ukrainian television channels on 14 August, Poroshenko justified early elections because the Rada refused to recognise the self-proclaimed breakaway [[Donetsk People's Republic|Donetsk]] and [[Luhansk People's Republic|Lugansk]] People's Republics as [[terrorist organisation]]s.<ref name="fifth column PP">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/216712.html "Poroshenko frustrated by Rada refusing to recognize self-proclaimed republics as terrorist organizations"], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (14 August 2014)</ref> The two republics, situated in the eastern Ukrainian region of the [[Donbass]], originated in the [[Donetsk Oblast|Donetsk]] and [[Luhansk Oblast|Luhansk]] oblasts of Ukraine respectively, and have been fighting Ukrainian government forces in the [[War in Donbass]].<ref name="Ukraine crisis timeline BBC">{{cite news|title=Ukraine crisis timeline|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-26248275|publisher=[[BBC News]]|accessdate=29 May 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140527083835/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-26248275|archivedate=27 May 2014|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> President Poroshenko said: "I don't know how to work with a parliament in which a huge number [of deputies], whole factions, make up 'the [[fifth column]]' controlled from abroad [referring to Russia]. And this danger is only increasing".<ref name="fifth column PP"/> He also said that new elections "are the best and the most efficient form of [[lustration]] of not only the parliament but also the political forces".

Poroshenko announced on 25 August that he had called for elections to the Rada to take place on 26 October 2014.<ref name=PsUE25814/><ref name=BBCtsP25814>[https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-28931054 "Ukraine crisis: President calls snap vote amid fighting"], [[BBC News]] (25 August 2014)</ref> In his accompanying television address, he portrayed the elections as necessary to "purify the Rada of the mainstay of [former president] [[Viktor Yanukovych]]". These deputies, Poroshenko said, "clearly do not represent the people who elected them".<ref name=SPne25814/> Poroshenko also painted these Rada deputies as responsible for "the [[Dictatorship laws in Ukraine|[January 2014] Dictatorship laws]] that took the lives of the [[List of people killed during Euromaidan|Heavenly Hundred]]".<ref name=SPne25814/> Poroshenko also stated that many of the (then) current MPs were "direct sponsors and accomplices or at least sympathizers of the militants/separatists".<ref name=SPne25814>[http://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2014/08/25/Ukrainian-President-dissolves-Parliament-announces-early-elections/3261408995210/ "Ukrainian President dissolves Parliament, announces early elections"], [[United Press International]] (25 August 2014)<br />[http://www.themoscowtimes.com/news/article/ukraine-s-petro-poroshenko-announces-new-parliamentary-elections/505837.html Ukraine's Petro Poroshenko Dissolves Parliament, Sets Election Date], [[The Moscow Times]] (26 August 2014)<br />[http://www.president.gov.ua/en/news/31081.html "President's address on the occasion of early parliamentary elections of October 26"], [[Presidential Administration of Ukraine]] (25 August 2014)</ref>

==Electoral system==
The [[Verkhovna Rada]] has 450 members, elected to a five-year term in [[parallel voting]], with 225 members elected in [[single-member]] constituencies using [[FPTP]] system and 225 members elected by [[proportional representation]] ([[closed list]]) in a single nationwide constituency using the [[largest remainder method]] with 5% threshold.<ref>[http://www.ifes.org/election-materials/ukraine-parliamentary-election-law-2011-0 IFES]</ref> Parties are not allowed to form electoral blocs (in contrast to 1998-2007 elections). Attempts to return to [[proportional representation]] with [[open party list]]s and [[electoral bloc]]s were failed.<ref>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/218366.html Rada fails to put on today's agenda three bills on elections of MPs], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (14 August 2014)</ref>

The division into 225 electoral districts was the same as at [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election|the 2012 election]]. The voting was organized only in 198 of them.

===Non-voting areas===
The voting was impossible to provide on the territories that was not under government control. Particularly, because of Russian occupation of Crimea there was no voting in all 10 districts in [[Autonomous Republic of Crimea]] and 2 in [[Sevastopol]]. The voting in [[Donbass]] was provided partially. The democratic watchdog [[OPORA]] estimated that 4.6 million Ukrainians were unable to vote: 1.8 million in Crimea and the city of Sevastopol, 1.6 million in Donetsk Oblast and 1.2 million in Luhansk Oblast.<ref>[http://www.unian.net/politics/1000335-na-vyiborah-ne-smogut-progolosovat-pochti-5-mln-ukraintsev.html На выборах не смогут проголосовать почти 5 млн украинцев Подробности читайте на УНИАН: http://www.unian.net/politics/1000335-na-vyiborah-ne-smogut-progolosovat-pochti-5-mln-ukraintsev.html], [[Ukrainian Independent Information Agency]] (24 October 2014)</ref>

====Crimea====
About 1.8 million of eligible voters live in [[Autonomous Republic of Crimea]] and the city of [[Sevastopol]], that is 5% of the whole number of voters in Ukraine. Ukrainian government lost control over the region in March 2014 during the [[Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation|Crimean crisis]]. Since that time no legitimate elections are provided there. In the 2014 legislative election Crimean voters had ability to vote in any other region for party-lists in the single nationwide constituency, but they were unable to vote for candidates in single-member constituencies.<ref name="CECeWiDChtde2914">{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.5.ua/ukrajina/politika/item/392829-u-tsvk-poiasnyly-iak-holosuvatymut-meshkantsi-okupovanykh-rosiieiu-terytorii The CEC explained how to vote to inhabitants of Russian-occupied territories], [[5 Kanal (Ukraine)|5 Kanal]] (2 September 2014)</ref>

====Donbass====
"[[Donbass]]" is an unofficial name of 2 the most eastern Ukrainian oblasts: [[Donetsk Oblast|Donetsk]] and [[Luhansk Oblast|Luhansk]]. About 5 million of eligible voters live there, that is 14% of the whole number of voters in Ukraine. After an active phase of [[War in Donbass]] in the summer 2014 and September ceasefire, roughly a half of the region remained to be controlled by separatists loyal to Yanukovych. On the day before the election, the CEC stated that there was no ability to provide voting in the captured areas.<ref name=27sIU2510/> Thus, full-fledged voting was provided only in 8 districts of 32. Other 9 districts were split by the front line for controlled and not controlled areas, so voting was provided there only partly. In 2 of them only slight number of polling station was opened: in 53rd district only 9% of voters were able to vote and in 45th district only 2%. In spite of this, the elections in these single-member constituencies were recognized as successful, and the winners (Oleh Nedava and [[Yukhym Zvyahilsky]]) got mandates. To the other 15 districts ballots were not transferred at all.

==Campaign==
In the 225 electoral districts some 3,321 candidates participated, out of which 2,018 were [[Independent politician|independent candidates]].<ref name="Basic electoral statistics 2014 extraordinary parliamentary election CECU">[http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2014/wp001e.html Basic electoral statistics 2014 extraordinary parliamentary election] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141029090732/http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2014/wp001e.html |date=29 October 2014 }}, [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]]</ref> 52 [[Political parties in Ukraine|political parties]] nominated candidates.<ref name="Basic electoral statistics 2014 extraordinary parliamentary election CECU"/> 147 candidates withdrew after the 1 October candidate registration deadline.<ref name="Registration of candidates to run in early parliamentary elections in Ukraine ended">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/226369.html Registration of candidates to run in early parliamentary elections in Ukraine ended], [[Interfax Ukraine]] (01.10.2014)</ref>

In the election campaign the parties positions on foreign relations and the [[War in Donbass]] could be roughly divided into two groups.<ref name=OhoaecU14>{{Citation |first=Tadeusz A. |last=Olszański |title=Ukraine's political parties at the start of the election campaign |publisher=OSW—Centre for Eastern Studies |date=17 September 2014 |url=http://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/analyses/2014-09-17/ukraines-political-parties-start-election-campaign}}</ref> The first group consisted of pro-European parties that advocated to end the War in Donbass by use of force and consisted of [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"|Fatherland Party]], [[Civic Position]], [[Radical Party (Ukraine)|Radical Party]] and [[People's Front (Ukraine)|People's Front]] (this party was ambiguous about use of force).<ref name=OhoaecU14/> [[Svoboda (political party)|Svoboda]] also wanted to end the War in Donbass by use of force.<ref name=OhoaecU14/> The party [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity"|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]] was the only pro-European party that wanted to end the War in Donbass by a peaceful solution.<ref name=OhoaecU14/> The second group was [[Strong Ukraine]] and [[Opposition Bloc]] who were considered pro-Russian and they advocated to end the War in Donbass by a peaceful solution.<ref name=OhoaecU14/> The [[Communist Party of Ukraine]] (according to political scientist Tadeusz A. Olszański) "effectively supports the separatist rebellion".<ref name=OhoaecU14/>

According to Olszański Radical Party and the Communists were the only [[left-wing]] parties.<ref name=OhoaecU14/>

=====Forming of political coalitions=====
On 2 September [[Vitaliy Kovalchuk]] (the parliamentary leader) of [[UDAR]] stated that since his party and [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity"|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]] had agreed to "joint participation in parliamentary elections" on 29 March 2014 the two parties were "in discussion" about "the format" for how to do so in these elections. On 15 September it became clear that 30% of the Petro Poroshenko Bloc election list would be filled by members of UDAR and that UDAR leader [[Vitali Klitschko]] is at the top of this list, Klitschko vowed not to resign as incumbent [[Mayor of Kyiv]].<ref name=UDARisPPB>{{in lang|ru}} [http://korrespondent.net/ukraine/politics/3418992-letchyky-kombaty-zhurnalysty-kto-ydet-v-novuui-verkhovnuui-radu Pilots, combatants, and journalists. Who goes to the new Verkhovna Rada ], [[Korrespondent.net]] (15 September 2014)<br />[http://klichko.org/news/?id=23180&lang=eng Klitschko: I lead my team to Parliament], [[UDAR]] official website (14 September 2014)<br />[http://en.itar-tass.com/world/749643 Deadline for nomination of candidates running in early election to Rada expires], [[ITAR-TASS]] (15 September 2014)</ref><ref name=KsKM2710>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231244.html Klitschko has no plans to leave post of Kyiv mayor], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)</ref>

7 September party congress of [[Civil Position]] decided that the party would participate in the election on a partly list with members of [[Democratic Alliance (Ukraine)|Democratic Alliance]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/09/7/7037006/ The party decided Gritsenko, who will go to Council], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (7 September 2014)</ref>

On 10 September, the [[All-Ukrainian Union "Fatherland"|Fatherland Party]] split because party leaders Yatsenyuk and Turchynov became founding members of the new party [[People's Front (Ukraine)|People's Front]].<ref name=peoples_front>{{in lang|uk}}[http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2014/09/10/7037335/ Yatsenyuk became a leader of the "People's Front" political council, while Turchynov is a head of its headquarters]. [[Ukrayinska Pravda]]. 10 September 2014<br />[http://en.ria.ru/world/20140910/192809518/Ukrainian-PM-Parliament-Speaker-to-Head-Newly-Formed-Popular.html Ukrainian PM, Parliament Speaker to Head Newly Formed Popular Front Party], [[RIA Novosti]] (10 September 2014)</ref>

The biggest party in the previous [[2012 Ukrainian parliamentary election|2012 parliamentary elections]], [[Party of Regions]], chose not to participate in the election because of a perceived lack of legitimacy (of the election), because not every resident of the [[Donbas]] could vote.<ref>[http://en.ria.ru/politics/20140914/192930339/Ukraines-Party-of-Regions-Refuses-to-Participate-in-Rada.html Ukraine's Party of Regions Refuses to Participate in Rada Elections], [[RIA Novosti]] (23 September 2014)</ref> Individual members of the Party of Regions would take part in the election as candidates of the party [[Opposition Bloc]].<ref name=FPoR23914>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/225005.html Opposition Bloc chooses top ten candidates for parliamentary elections], [[Interfax Ukraine]] (23 September 2014)<br />[http://www.kyivpost.com/content/politics/allies-of-yanukovych-trying-for-parliament-365185.html?flavour=mobile Allies of Yanukovych trying for parliament], [[Kyiv Post]] (21 September 2014)<br />[http://un.ua/eng/article/532653.html Party Of Regions Will Not Contest Snap Parliamentary Elections Independently] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140924041429/http://un.ua/eng/article/532653.html |date=24 September 2014 }}, [[Ukrainian News Agency]] (14 September 2014)</ref> According to [[Yuriy Boyko]], who is heading the party's election list, Opposition Bloc does not represent parties, but consists only of individual politicians.<ref name=FPoR23914/>

==Results==
The counting of votes was significantly delayed: Central Electoral Commission announced that all ballots were counted on 10 November only.<ref name="People's Front 0.33%">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/233548.html People's Front 0.33% ahead of Poroshenko Bloc with all ballots counted in Ukraine elections – CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (10 November 2014)</ref> The announcement of the final result for 38th electoral district was delayed till mid-November due to the results being challenged in court.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.radiosvoboda.org/content/article/26696305.html In the 38th district of Dnipropetrovsk stop recount], [[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]] (17 November 2014)</ref>

===Voter turnout===
[[File:Voter turnout - Ukraine - 2014-10-26 20-00.svg|thumb|Voter turnout throughout Ukraine.]]
In the 2014 election the number of participated voters was much less than in 2012 (16,052,228 down from 20,797,206).<ref name="CECReport">[http://www.cvk.gov.ua/info/protokol_bmvo_ndu_26102014.pdf CEC report]</ref> The main reason of this decrease was the impossibility to organize voting in some regions. Because of this,  the official turnout was calculated by division by the number of people who live in areas where voting was provided only (according to the CEC, it was 30,921,218).<ref name="CECReport"/> So, the official voter turnout was 51.91%. In any case this percent is smaller than in 2012, when the turnout was 57.43%.

The turnout level had obvious geographical differences. The highest turnout (60-70%) was registered in western regions (except [[Zakarpattia Oblast|Zakarpattia]] and [[Chernivtsi Oblast]]), the level of turnout in central regions was average (54-58%) and in southern and eastern regions it was quite low (40-48%). The turnout in two oblasts of [[Donbass]] (or more exactly in the parts of these oblasts where the voting was provided) was 32% - the lowest in the country.<ref>[http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2014/WP064E?pt001f01=910&pid100=14 CEC data about turnout in Donetsk region]<br />[http://www.cvk.gov.ua/pls/vnd2014/WP064E?pt001f01=910&pid100=44 CEC data about turnout in Luhansk region]</ref>

Previously, the [[Donbass]] region displayed high turnout for every election. The [[Party of Regions]] that had plenitude of the power over this region until the [[2014 Ukrainian revolution]] artificially increased voter turnout there by use of management reserves and falsifications. The 2014 election was the end of this artificial increase. One more reason of low turnout in [[Donbass]] as well as in other south-eastern regions was a feeling among a significant part of the local population that no party represented their interests.<ref name="Olszanski AE">{{Citation |first=Tadeusz A. |last=Olszański |title=A strong vote for reform: Ukraine after the parliamentary elections |publisher=OSW—Centre for Eastern Studies |date=29 October 2014 |url=http://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/analyses/2014-10-29/a-strong-vote-reform-ukraine-after-parliamentary-elections}}</ref>

==Conduct==
The elections were monitored by 2,321 accredited foreign observers,<ref name=acremIU/> 304 of them on behalf of 21 states and 2,017 from 20 international organisations.<ref name=acremIU>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/229756.html Almost 2,500 foreign observers to watch Ukrainian elections – CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (21 October 2014)</ref>

The Ukrainian democratic watchdog [[PORA|OPORA]] stated about the elections that they were legitimate, but that "the [election] campaign cannot be called fully free within the limits of the country" because of the [[War in Donbass|fighting in the Donbass region]].<ref name=OporaaOV>[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231177.html Ukrainian Rada elections generally without incident – OPORA], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231176.html CVU to ask CEC to declare invalid elections at constituencies Nos. 45 and 102], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231241.html OSCE claims elections in Ukraine held up to democratic standards], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231242.html Voting, tabulation in Rada elections organized properly - OSCE observers], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231268.html OSCE: Ukraine elections a step forward], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231280.html Barroso, Van Rompuy call Ukraine elections a victory of democracy], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231120.html PABSEC speaks positively of Verkhovna Rada elections], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (27 October 2014)<br />[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/231424.html International Republican Institute congratulates Ukraine on elections that meet international standards], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (28 October 2014)<br />[http://www.kyivpost.com/opinion/op-ed/john-kerry-ukraines-parliamentary-elections-369690.html John Kerry: Ukraine's parliamentary elections], [[Kyiv Post]] (28 October 2014)</ref> The [[NGO]] [[Committee of Voters of Ukraine]] asked the [[Central Election Commission of Ukraine]] to declare invalid the results of constituencies 45 and 102 because there "significant irregularities were numerous".<ref name=OporaaOV/>

The [[OSCE]] stated about the elections that they were "in line with international commitments, and were characterized by many positive aspects, including an impartial and efficient Central Election Commission, competitive contests that offered voters real choice, and general respect for fundamental freedoms".<ref name=OporaaOV/> [[European Council President]] [[Herman Van Rompuy]] and [[European Commission President]] [[José Manuel Barroso]] called the elections "a victory of the people of Ukraine and of democracy".<ref name=OporaaOV/> [[US President]] [[Barack Obama]] and his [[US Secretary of State|Secretary of State]] [[John Kerry]] also congratulated Ukraine "on successful parliamentary elections".<ref name=OporaaOV/> The day after the election [[Russia]]n Foreign Minister [[Sergey Lavrov]] stated "I think we will recognize this election because it is very important for us that Ukraine will finally have authorities that do not fight one another, do not drag Ukraine to the [[Western world|West]] or to the East, but that will deal with the real problems facing the country".<ref name=KarasinKoH/> He also hoped that the new Ukrainian government "will be constructive, will not seek to continue escalating confrontational tendencies in society, (in ties) with Russia".<ref name=KarasinKoH/> Lavrov's Deputy Foreign Minister [[Grigory Karasin]] stated "the election is valid in spite of the rather harsh and dirty election campaign".<ref name=KarasinKoH/> Karasin also "welcomed the success of parties supporting a peaceful resolution of [[War in Donbass|the conflict in eastern Ukraine]]" and warned that "nationalistic and chauvinistic forces" in parliament could undermine peace efforts and were "extremely dangerous".<ref (27 October 2014)<br />[http://www.kyivpost.com/content/ukraine/russian-senators-prepared-to-cooperate-with-new-verkhovna-rada-369801.html Russian senators prepared to cooperate with new Verkhovna earlier established between the [[State Duma]] and the Federation Council on the one hand and the [[Verkhovna Rada]] on the other will hopefully be re-launched in some format" although he stated he ==By-elections==
===2015===
On 26 July 2015 mid-term election were held in constituency 205 located in [[Chernihiv]].<ref name=uec26714/> These were necessary after 2014 winner [[Valeriy Kulich]] had left parliament because of his appointment as Governor of [[Chernihiv Oblast]].<ref name=uec26714/> 91 candidates took part in the elections; eight of them for political parties, the others were self-nominated candidates.<ref name=uec26714/> On election day the ballot paper stretched to about 1 meter.<ref name=uec26714/> 36 candidates had withdrawn from participation in the elections.<ref name=uec26714>[http://www.unian.info/politics/1105011-mid-term-elections-start-in-chernihiv-constituency-no205.html Mid-term elections start in Chernihiv (constituency No.205)], [[UNIAN]] (26 July 2015)</ref> During the election campaign top candidates [[Hennadiy Korban]] and [[Serhiy Berezenko]] were repeatedly accused of bribing voters, the use of [[black PR]] and other violations (of the electoral legislation).<ref name="Г">[http://gordonua.com/news/politics/Korban-YA-poteryal-moralnye-pravo-vozglavlyat-politsovet-parii-Ukrop-91321.html Корбан: Я потерял моральное право возглавлять политсовет партии "Укроп"] "Гордон", .07.2015<br />[http://www.globalpost.com/article/6622364/2015/07/27/ukraine-election-district-205 This crazy Ukrainian election shows the country has a ways to go toward reform], [[Global Post]] (28 July 2015)</ref>

The election was won by Berezenko of [[Petro Poroshenko Bloc "Solidarity"|Petro Poroshenko Bloc]] with 35.90% of the vote.<ref name="byud20528715"/> Second most votes were won by Korban of [[UKROP]] who received 14.76%.<ref name="byud20528715">[http://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/280526.html With 100% of ballots counted in Rada by-election, Berezenko gets 35.90% of votes, Korban 14.76% – CEC], [[Interfax-Ukraine]] (28 July 2015)</ref> The official voter turnout was set at 35.3%.<ref>[http://www.unian.info/politics/1105170-additional-rada-election-in-chernihiv-759-of-protocols-processed.html Additional Rada election in Chernihiv: 87.95% of protocols processed], [[UNIAN]] (27 July 2015)</ref>

===2016===
On 17 July 2016 mid-term election were held in 7 single-member districts (constituency 23, 27, 85, 114, 151, 183 and 206) because their representatives had been elected to executive political positions and the death of [[Ihor Yeremeyev]].<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7108026/ CEC appointed elections in four constituencies], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (10 May 2016)<br />{{in lang|uk}} [http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7112314/ Довибори до ВР: У Дніпрі 51 кандидат, є представник КПУ (By-election to the Verkhovna Rada: in Dnipro 51, a candidate is a representative of the Communist Party)], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (19 June 2016)<br />{{in lang|ru}}'''/(website has automatic [[Google Translate]] option)''' [http://file.liga.net/person/366-igor-eremeev.html Short bio of Ihor Yeremeyev], LIGA</ref> Turnout varied from about 50% until less than 20%.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7115093/ CEC: Voter turnout for midterm elections nearly 33%. No data Luhansk], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (17 July 2016)</ref> The elections were monitored by 57 international official observers.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7114469/ CEC registered 43 observers to the Rada by-election], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (12 July 2016)</ref>

Vote counting in constituency 114 (situated in [[Stanytsia Luhanska]]) was disrupted after several members of the election commission refused to count votes.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7115197/ Problem county in the Luhansk region resumed counting votes], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (18 July 2016)</ref> In the same constituency 14 [[criminal case]]s for violating the election were opened.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://pda.pravda.com.ua/news/id_7115138/ In Luhansk opened 14 cases for violating the election], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (18 July 2016)</ref> Constituency 151 was last in announcing its final results when it did so on 21 July 2016.<ref>{{in lang|uk}} [http://www.pravda.com.ua/news/2016/07/21/7115528/ Became known the results of the vote in the last of the 7 districts], [[Ukrayinska Pravda]] (21 July 2016)</ref>
