The '''Constitution of the Federative Republic of Brazil''' ({{lang-pt|Constituição da República Federativa do Brasil}}) is the [[Constitution|supreme law]] of [[Brazil]]. It is the foundation and source of the legal authority underlying the existence of Brazil and the [[federal government of Brazil]]. It provides the framework for the organization of the Brazilian government and for the relationship of the federal government to the states, to citizens, and to all people within Brazil.

==Overview==
The current Brazilian Constitution is the seventh enacted since the country's independence in 1822, and the sixth since the proclamation of the republic in 1889.  It was promulgated on 5 October 1988, after a two-year process in which it was written from scratch.

==History==
{{main|History of the Constitution of Brazil}}
[[File:Constituição 1988 (Capa) 01.tiff|thumb|First version of the current Brazilian Constitution]]
The current Constitution of Brazil was drafted as a reaction to the period of [[Military dictatorship in Brazil|military dictatorship]], and sought to guarantee [[Individual and group rights|individual rights]] and restrict the state's ability to limit freedom, to punish offences and to regulate individual life. Among the new constitutional guarantees are the errand of injunction and the ''[[habeas data]]''. It also anticipated the existence of a Consumers' Defence Code (enacted in 1990), of a Children's and Youth Code (1990) and of a new Civil Code (2002).

It was the first constitution to demand severe punishment for breaches of [[civil liberties]] and rights. Consequently, Brazil later approved a law making the propagation of [[prejudice]] against any [[minority group|minority]] or [[ethnic group]] an unbailable crime. This law provided legal remedy against those who spread [[hate speech]] or those who do not treat all citizens equally. This second aspect helped disabled people to have a reserved percentage of jobs in the public service and large companies, and [[Afro-Brazilians]] to seek reparation for [[Racism in Brazil|racism]] in court.

Breaking with the authoritarian logic of the previous Constitution, it made unbailable crimes those of torture and of actions directed against the [[democracy|democratic state]] and the [[constitutionality|constitutional order]], thus creating constitutional devices to block [[coup d'état|coups d'état]] of any kind.

The Constitution also established many forms of direct popular participation besides regular voting, such as plebiscite, [[referendum]] and the possibility of ordinary citizens proposing new laws. Examples of these democratic mechanisms were the 1993 plebiscite concerning the form of government, where the [[presidential system]] was confirmed, and the [[2005 Brazilian firearms and ammunition referendum|2005 firearms and ammunition referendum]].

The mention of God in the preamble of the Constitution (and later on the Brazilian currency) was opposed by most leftists as incompatible with [[freedom of religion]] because it does not recognise the rights of [[polytheists]] such as some [[indigenous peoples of Brazil|indigenous peoples]] or of [[atheism|atheists]]. The [[Supreme Federal Court]] has ruled that this commission of the protection of God was not [[constitutionality|unconstitutional]] since the preamble of the constitution is simply an indication of principles that serves as an introduction to the constitutional text and reflects the ideological conceptions of the legislator, falling within the scope of political ideology and not of the Law.

== Criticism ==
[[File:Roberto Campos.jpg|thumb|left|250px|[[Roberto Campos]], one of the few voices to rise up against the 1988 Constitution at the time of its creation]]

The Federal Constitution of 1988 is criticized in the doctrine for being very extensive, long-winded and analytical. This characteristic forced the Constitution to be amended several times, in politically costly processes, to adapt to the changes in society<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2004-jun-13/erro_politico_1988_produziu_constituicao_dificil|title=Erro político produziu Constituição difícil até para técnicos|date=13 June 2004|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Conjur}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2006-mai-14/judiciario_deixou_departamento_tecnico|title=Entrevista: Luís Roberto Barroso — Parte 1|date=14 May 2006|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Conjur}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2006-mai-12/marca_inequivoca_cf_88_judicializacao|title=A marca inequívoca da CF de 88 é a da judicialização|date=14 May 2006|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Conjur}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2008/10/451126-constituicao-federal-completa-20-anos-envelhecida-e-remendada.shtml|title=Constituição Federal completa 20 anos envelhecida e remendada|date=4 October 2010|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Conjur}}</ref>

Another criticism is that the 1988 Federal Constitution reproduces a model of [[State capitalism]], expanding state monopolies and regulations, which allowed the Brazilian state, in 2017, to have stakes in more than 650 companies, involved in one third of the national GDP. This model also created restrictions for the performance of foreign companies in several fields with harmful consequences for the country's growth. In the view of some scholars, this economic model favors [[patrimonialism]] and [[corruption]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.oxfordscholarship.com/view/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780190250256.001.0001/acprof-9780190250256-chapter-15 |title=The Case of Brazil |access-date=26 February 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2012-nov-25/embargos-culturais-criticas-roberto-campos-constituicao-ainda-ecoam|title=Críticas de Roberto Campos à Constituição ainda ecoam|date=25 November 2012|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Conjur}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://g1.globo.com/mundo/blog/helio-gurovitz/post/privatizacoes-ainda-e-pouco.html |title=Privatizações: ainda é pouco |access-date=26 February 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/1994/5/29/caderno_especial/22.html|title=Constituição de 1988 pára pesquisa mineral|date=29 May 2004|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Folha}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/1995/3/27/caderno_especial/6.html|title=Governo quer ampliar abertura da economia e flexibilizar monopólios|date=29 May 2004|access-date=3 March 2018|publisher=Folha}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://politica.estadao.com.br/noticias/geral,a-questao-e-havera-mais,70001984641|title=ANÁLISE - A questão é: haverá mais?|date=29 May 2004|access-date=7 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/opiniao/2017/12/1940945-inchaco-estatal.shtml|title=Inchaço estatal|access-date=7 March 2018|publisher=Folha}}</ref>

Since the approval of the 1988 Federal Constitution, the homicide rate has grown 124% in Brazil and more than one million people have been murdered. While in the 1980s there were 11.7 homicides per 100 thousand inhabitants, in 2010 the rate reached 26.2 - an average increase of 2.7% per year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ultimosegundo.ig.com.br/brasil/taxa-de-homicidios-cresceu-124-nos-ultimos-30-anos-no-brasil/n1597408258382.html|title=País está em situação epidêmica, de acordo com a OMS, que limita em 10 o número de assassinatos por 100 mil habitantes; taxa do Brasil é de 26,2%|date=14 December 2011|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Veja}}</ref>

A portion of the doctrine has blamed the Constitution. [[Raul Jungmann]] criticized the fact that the Constitution gave states responsibility for public security, leaving the Union with only a residual role. This would have enabled the growth of factions such as the [[Primeiro Comando da Capital]] and the [[Comando Vermelho]]. In the view of [[Alexandre de Moraes]], Brazil confused the respect for the dignity of the human person with the leniency for criminal leaders.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noticias.r7.com/brasil/raul-jungmann-prendemos-muito-mas-prendemos-mal-27022018|title=Raul Jungmann: "Prendemos muito, mas prendemos mal"|date=27 February 2018|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Folha}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/cotidiano/2017/01/1847985-faccoes-criminosas-se-alimentam-das-falhas-do-estado-diz-pesquisadora.shtml|title=Facções criminosas se alimentam das falhas do Estado, diz pesquisadora|date=27 February 2018|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Folha}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://istoe.com.br/pais-deve-acabar-com-a-hipocrisia-ao-tratar-questoes-penitenciarias-diz-moraes/|title=País deve acabar com a hipocrisia ao tratar questões penitenciárias, diz Moraes|date=30 November 2017|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Istoé}}</ref>

The Constitution is also responsible for leaving a slow procedural system. Brazil has the 30th slowest judiciary among 133 countries, according to the [[World Bank]]. This has caused the judiciary to use provisional arrests as an advance of the sentence. In 2015 more than 40% of prisoners in Brazil were provisional.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.com/portuguese/noticias/2013/09/130920_lentidao_justica_pai_jf|title=Por que a Justiça brasileira é lenta?|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Istoé}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://exame.abril.com.br/brasil/por-que-a-justica-brasileira-e-lenta/|title=Por que a Justiça brasileira é lenta?|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Istoé}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://gazetaweb.globo.com/gazetadealagoas/noticia.php?c=318611|title=Justiça, mesmo tardia|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Istoé}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cartacapital.com.br/sociedade/prende-primeiro-pergunta-depois-2548.html|title=JNo Brasil, 40% dos presos são provisórios|access-date= 5 March 2018|publisher=Carta Capital}}</ref>

A World Bank study criticized the 1988 Federal Constitution for extending the privileges of civil servants, aggravating income inequality in Brazil. [59] Remuneration and retirement are disproportionately high according to studies. In 2015, the federal government's deficit with the retirement of the approximately 1 million government employees was greater than the total registered with 33 million private pensioners. For the World Bank, civil servants are among the richest fifth of the Brazilian population. For [[Roberto Brant]], the Federal Constitution was captured by groups of civil servants in 1988.Philosopher [[Fernando Schüler]] maintains that Brazil went against the grain in the 1980s: "While the world tried to adjust the State to globalization and modernize public management, Brazil bet on a super bureaucratic state in the 1988 Constitution. We offer rigid stability in the employment for civil servants, we mix careers of State with common careers of the public service, we create the law of biddings, we cast the budgets and we eliminate any space for the meritocracy in the public area. " For jurist [[Modesto Carvalhosa]], only a new "principiological" constitution would end the privileges of the 1988 Constitution.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://veja.abril.com.br/revista-veja/assim-nao-da-2/|title=Assim não dá|date=24 November 2017|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Veja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://noblat.oglobo.globo.com/editoriais/noticia/2017/12/os-privilegios-salariais-do-servidor-publico.html|title=Os privilégios salariais do servidor público|date=24 November 2017|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Globo}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.valor.com.br/brasil/4914686/servico-publico-puxa-desigualdade-na-previdencia|title=Serviço público puxa desigualdade na Previdência|date=24 November 2017|access-date= 5 August 2017|publisher=Globo}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://asmetro.org.br/portalsn/2017/11/22/para-o-banco-mundial-brasil-precisa-reduzir-os-privilegios-de-servidores/|title=Para o Banco Mundial, Brasil precisa reduzir os privilégios de servidores|date=24 November 2017|access-date= 5 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.em.com.br/app/noticia/politica/2017/11/29/interna_politica,920462/pobre-paga-privilegios-de-servidores-diz-ex-ministro-da-previdencia.shtml|title="Pobre paga privilégios de servidores", diz ex-ministro da Previdência|date=24 November 2017|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/colunas/fernando-schuler/2019/10/reforma-administrativa-ou-do-estado.shtml|title=Reforma administrativa ou do Estado?|access-date= 10 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.publico.pt/2018/10/27/mundo/entrevista/sociedade-brasileira-hoje-papel-brutal-politicao-eleitorado-brasileiro-eliminar-politicos-partidos-sugam-recursos-1848998|title="Os brasileiros estão a eliminar os políticos que sugam o Estado" |access-date= 10 October 2019}}</ref>

The Federal Constitution of 1988 is also criticized for having adopted one of the broadest Special Forums due to the prerogative of function in the world, jurists understand that this extension encourages corruption. A quarter of the actions with a Privileged Forum take more than ten years to be judged. The [[Supreme Federal Court]] takes 1,300 days to judge criminal actions by persons with privileged jurisdiction. Between 2001 and 2017, 200 actions involving the Privileged Forum expired.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://oglobo.globo.com/brasil/foro-privilegiado-no-brasil-mais-amplo-comparado-outros-20-paises-20973826|publisher=O Globo|date=24 October 2016|access-date=14 December 2016|title=Foro privilegiado no Brasil é mais amplo comparado a outros 20 países}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.valor.com.br/politica/4754425/para-ministro-barroso-foro-privilegiado-e-feito-para-nao-funcionar|publisher=Valor Econômico|date=24 October 2016|access-date=14 December 2016|title=Para ministro Barroso, foro privilegiado 'é feito para não funcionar'|author=Estevão Taiar}}</ref><ref>José Nêumanne (13 Junho 2016).  [http://politica.estadao.com.br/blogs/neumanne/o-privilegio-do-foro/ «O privilégio do foro»].politica.estadao.com.br</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2016/11/1829725-casos-de-foro-privilegiado-se-arrastam-por-ate-18-anos-no-supremo.shtml|title=Casos de foro privilegiado se arrastam por até 18 anos|date=6 November 2016|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://gauchazh.clicrbs.com.br/politica/noticia/2017/03/stf-leva-1-3-mil-dias-para-julgar-acoes-penais-de-pessoas-com-foro-privilegiado-9751641.html|title=STF leva 1,3 mil dias para julgar ações penais de pessoas com foro privilegiado|date=19 March 2017|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.jota.info/justica/o-foro-privilegiado-em-numeros-23112017|title=O foro privilegiado em números|date=23 November 2017|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref>

Also criticized is the requirement of unappealable transit for the execution of the sentence. For Judge [[Sérgio Moro]], waiting for the final judgment will contribute to impunity. According to Minister [[Teori Zavascki]] after confirming a second sentence sentence, one could no longer speak of the principle of non-culpability, since "the exceptional remedies, for the superimposition courts, do not boast the ability to review facts and evidence". In the United Nations, 193 of the 194 countries have a first or second arrest.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2016/10/1819693-aguardar-o-transito-em-julgado-contribui-para-a-impunidade-dos-poderosos-diz-moro.shtml|title=Aguardar o trânsito em julgado contribui para a impunidade, diz Moro|access-date=20 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.emagis.com.br/area-gratuita/noticias/prisao-antes-do-transito-em-julgado-mudanca-de-rumos-no-stf/|title=Prisão antes do trânsito em julgado: mudança de rumos no STF|access-date=20 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://blogs.oglobo.globo.com/merval-pereira/post/na-onu-193-dos-194-paises-tem-prisao-em-1-ou-2-instancia.html|title=Na ONU, 193 dos 194 países têm prisão em 1ª ou 2ª instância|date=20 March 2018|access-date=20 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.folhadelondrina.com.br/opiniao/punicao-em-debate-prisao-em-2-instancia-e-moralizadora-999967.html|title= 'Prisão em 2ª instância é moralizadora'|access-date=20 March 2018}}</ref>

In the electoral aspect, the Constitution adopted the mandatory vote. Among the 15 largest economies in the world, Brazil is the only country in which voting is mandatory. A 2014 survey showed that the mandatory vote is rejected by 61% of Brazilians. Some indoctrinates question whether it is democratic to compel people to vote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://direito.folha.uol.com.br/blog/voto-obrigatrio-no-mundo|title=Voto obrigatório no mundo|date=12 May 2014|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://datafolha.folha.uol.com.br/eleicoes/2014/05/1453158-rejeicao-a-voto-obrigatorio-atinge-61-e-alcanca-taxa-recorde-entre-brasileiros.shtml|title=Rejeição a voto obrigatório atinge 61% e alcança taxa recorde entre brasileiros|date=12 May 2014|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2005-out-24/democracia_voto_direito_nao_obrigacao|title=Na democracia o voto é um direito, não uma obrigação|date=24 May 2005|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2005-nov-09/principal_instrumento_democracia_facultativo|title=Principal instrumento da democracia tem de ser facultativo|date=24 May 2005|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref>

The Constitution adopted the social democratic model of State organization, as defined by the columnist for the newspaper O Estado de S. Paulo [[Luiz Sérgio Henriques]]. For professor and lawyer [[Marco Aurélio Marrafon]], president of the Brazilian Academy of Constitutional Law, the 1988 Brazilian Magna Carta organized the State according to the Welfare State model, in which it is intended to reconcile "the liberal component of preservation of individual rights and limitation of state power, with direct economic intervention and the promotion of public policies, in order to redistribute resources and reduce social inequalities ". In order to finance the Welfare State, it was necessary to raise the tax burden, which went from 23.4% of GDP in 1988, to 33.6% of GDP in 2005, and to link budget revenues. Thus, the Union reached 93% of mandatory spending in 2017, decreasing the room for maneuver by the government and affecting investments. This political option is criticized by some indoctrinators.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://opiniao.estadao.com.br/noticias/geral,a-perspectiva-social-democrata-imp-,716432|title=A perspectiva social-democrata|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.conjur.com.br/2014-nov-10/constituicao-poder-esgotamento-estado-bem-estar-afeta-concretizacao-direitos-sociais|title=Esgotamento do Estado de Bem-Estar afeta concretização de direitos sociais|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.jota.info/opiniao-e-analise/colunas/o-mundo-fora-dos-autos/do-poder-constituinte-lipoaspiracional-08102018|title=Do poder constituinte lipoaspiracional|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nacoesunidas.org/pesquisadores-alertam-para-sistema-tributario-regressivo-no-brasil-mais-pobres-sao-afetados/|title=Pesquisadores alertam para sistema tributário regressivo no Brasil; mais pobres são afetados|access-date= 17 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://epocanegocios.globo.com/Economia/noticia/2018/03/epoca-negocios-uniao-tem-93-de-gastos-obrigatorios.html/|title=União tem 93% de gastos obrigatórios|access-date= 29 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gazetadopovo.com.br/politica/republica/quem-vencer-as-eleicoes-vai-governar-de-maos-amarradas-evl017lsiy64g53id79gzupx6/|title="Quem vencer as eleições vai governar de mãos amarradas"|access-date= 29 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gazetadopovo.com.br/politica/republica/constituicao-de-1988-e-a-causa-do-atraso-do-brasil-diz-descendente-de-d-pedro-ii-0mfem5c1ldu9q9w0klmsibl6z/|title="Constituição de 1988 é a causa do atraso do Brasil, diz descendente de D. Pedro II"|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://opiniao.estadao.com.br/noticias/geral,a-constituicao-de-1988-na-visao-de-roberto-campos,70002057765|title=A Constituição de 1988 na visão de Roberto Campos|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/especial/fj03109807.htm|title=Carta criou despesas sem fonte de recursos |access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.academia.org.br/artigos/uma-visao-critica|title=Uma visão crítica |access-date= 5 January 2020}}</ref>

Doctrines criticize an alleged excessive power granted to the [[Order of Attorneys of Brazil]] by the Constitution. Brazilian philosopher and journalist [[Hélio Schwartsman]] considers that the 1988 Constitution conferred "disproportionate powers" on lawyers "such as appointing judges, writing laws, proposing direct actions of unconstitutionality, defining who can and who cannot become a lawyer". [[Roberto Campos]], economist, ex-senator and Minister of Planning of Brazil in the early years of the military dictatorship noted that "The [[Order of Attorneys of Brazil|OAB]] has achieved the feat of being mentioned three times in what he defines as the "besteirol Constitution" of 1988. According to him, "it's perhaps the only case in the world where a club of professionals has enshrined the constitutional text." <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/colunas/helioschwartsman/2012/08/1145463-negocios-da-oab.shtml|title=Negócios da OAB|access-date= 30 August 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/fsp/1996/5/26/brasil/3.html|title=O pior corporativismo|access-date= 5 March 2018}}</ref>

==Contents==
[[File:Constituição Cosme Coelho Rocha.JPG|thumb|2017 edition.]]
The Constitution of Brazil is composed of nine titles, subsequently divided into chapters and then articles. The articles are in turn divided into short clauses called ''incisos'' (indicated by [[Roman numerals]]) and ''parágrafos'' (indicated by numbers followed by §). The Constitution refers to the country as "the Union".

===Preamble===
The preamble to the Federal Constitution is a brief introductory statement that sets out the guiding purpose and principles of the document. The text reads:<ref>[http://www.servat.unibe.ch/icl/br00000_.html Brazil - Constitution:Preamble] International Constitutional Law. Retrieved 2011-10-13.</ref><ref name="Brazilian Constitution">[http://www.imprensaoficial.com.br/PortalIO/download/pdf/Constituicoes_declaracao.pdf Declaração - Constituição Federal]</ref>
{{quote|We, the representatives of the Brazilian People, assembled in the National Constituent Assembly to institute a Democratic State for the purpose of ensuring the exercise of social and individual rights, liberty, security, well being, development, equality and justice as supreme values of a fraternal, pluralist    and unprejudiced society, based on social harmony and committed, in the internal and international spheres, to the peaceful solution of disputes, promulgate, under the protection of God, this Constitution of the Federative Republic of Brazil.}}

===Title 1===
Title 1 is devoted to the fundamental principles of the Union. It describes the [[states of Brazil|States]], the [[municipalities of Brazil|municipalities]] and the [[Federal District (Brazil)|Federal District]] as the indissoluble constituents of the Union. It also establishes three independent, harmonic government branches: the [[federal government of Brazil|Executive]], the [[National Congress of Brazil|Legislative]] and the [[Judiciary of Brazil|Judiciary]], and lists the nation's main goals.

One of the most important excerpts from this title is in Article 1, single paragraph, stating:

{{quote|All power emanates from the People, who exercise it through elected representatives or directly, under this Constitution.}}

===Title 2===
Title 2 states the Fundamental Safeguards.<ref>{{cite web|title=Brazil 1988 (rev. 2014)|url=https://www.constituteproject.org/constitution/Brazil_2014?lang=en#55|website=Constitute|access-date=9 April 2015}}</ref> It ensures basic rights to all citizens and foreigners residing in the Country, prohibits [[capital punishment]], defines [[Brazilian citizenship|citizenship requirements]], [[political rights]], among other regulations.

===Title 3===
Title 3 regulates the state organization. It establishes [[Brasília]] as the nation's capital, describes the rights and duties of the states, the municipalities, as well rules for the public staff.

===Title 4===
Title 4 is about the branches of government. It describes the attributes for every government branch, and the rules for [[constitutional amendment|amendment]]s to the Constitution as well.

===Title 5===
Title 5 regulates the defense of the State and its democratic institutions. It rules the deployment of the [[Brazilian Armed Forces|armed forces]], the [[national security]] baselines, and declaration of [[state of emergency]].

===Title 6===
Title 6 comprises [[taxation]] and the nation's budget. It disposes on [[distribution (economics)|budget distribution]] among the Union's components and their competencies, and the nation's budget.

===Title 7===
Title 7 rules the economic activities in the country, the agricultural and urban policies, as well the state monopolies.

* The Constitution allows the Brazilian government to "expropriate, for the purpose of [[agrarian reform]], rural property that is not performing its social function." (Article 184)
* According to Article 187, the social function is performed when rural property simultaneously meets the following requirements: 
** rational and adequate use;
** adequate use of available natural resources and preservation of the environment; 
** compliance with the provisions which regulate labor relations; and
** exploitation which favors the well-being of the owners and workers.

===Title 8===
Title 8 is about the social order. It establishes the Social Security system, the Public Health system, the Public Pension system, among regulations concerning education, culture, science and technology, and sports policies.

===Title 9===
Title 9 encompasses general constitutional dispositions. Among those, there are sparse regulations, as well as transitional dispositions.
