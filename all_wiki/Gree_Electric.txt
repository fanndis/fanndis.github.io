'''Gree Electric Appliances Inc. of Zhuhai''' is a [[China|Chinese]] [[major appliance]] manufacturer headquartered in [[Zhuhai]], [[Guangdong]] province. It is the world's largest residential [[air-conditioner]] manufacturer.<ref name="bus1">Businessweek.com. "[https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2009-08-05/guangdong-visions-forging-chinas-future]." ''Guangdong Visions: Forging China's Future.'' Retrieved on 2016-08-08.</ref> The company offers two types of air conditioners: household air conditioners and commercial air conditioners. The company also produces electric fans, water dispensers, heaters, rice cookers, air purifiers, water kettles, humidifiers and induction cookers, among others products. It distributes its products in China and abroad under the [[brand name]] '''Gree'''. The company has two joint-ventures with [[Daikin]], ''Zhuhai Gree Daikin Device Co., Ltd.'', and ''Zhuhai Gree Daikin Precision Mold Co., Ltd.''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Daikin Industries and Gree Electric Appliances Agree to Establish Joint Venture {{!}} News Release {{!}} Daikin Industries, Ltd.|url=https://www.daikin.com/press/2009/090218/|access-date=2020-06-13|website=www.daikin.com}}</ref>

==History==
Gree was established in [[Zhuhai]], [[Guangdong]], in 1989 under its former name of '''Zhuhai City Haili Cooling Engineering Company Limited''' ({{zh|s=珠海市海利冷气工程股份有限公司}}). It was restructured and renamed '''Gree Electric Appliances Inc. of Zhuhai''' in 1994.  The company started as a nameless factory with 200 employees and annual production of less than 20,000 units.<ref name="bus1" />

It was listed on the [[Shenzhen Stock Exchange]] in 1996.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://fingad.duedee.com/2008/7/23/Gree-Electric-Appliances-Inc-of-ZhuHai---Depressing-Performance/37375/ |title=Gree Electric Appliances Inc, of ZhuHai |access-date=2008-11-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170727153758/http://fingad.duedee.com/2008/7/23/Gree-Electric-Appliances-Inc-of-ZhuHai---Depressing-Performance/37375 |archive-date=2017-07-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  The company grew 47% in 2008, despite the [[Financial crisis of 2007–2010|global recession]], booking $23 billion in contract sales.<ref name="bus1" /> The company is a multinational enterprise with 70,000 employees and annual production of 65.5 million units.<ref name="bus1" /> In April 2011, Gree announced that first-quarter net income rose 47 percent from a year earlier to 934.7 million Yuan.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2011-04-21/chalco-beiqi-foton-gree-laiwu-steel-china-equity-preview.html |title=China Stocks: Air China, Chalco, Chengde Lolo, Gree Electric - Bloomberg|work=bloomberg.com |year=2011 |accessdate=19 May 2011}}</ref>

== Management ==
Gree Electric's Chairwoman is [[Dong Mingzhu]], or "Sister Dong" as she is known in China.<ref name=GreeElectric2015AR/> She was appointed CEO in 2009.{{citation needed|date=November 2016}} She joined the company in 1990.{{citation needed|date=November 2016}} Dong was also the Chairwoman of its largest shareholder at the time, [[Gree Group]], until November 2016.<ref>[http://finance.sina.com.cn/review/jcgc/2016-11-14/doc-ifxxsmic6194236.shtml 评论：董明珠股权没获得又丢掉了格力集团董事长] (in Chinese)</ref> Gree Group was owned by [[Zhuhai Municipal People's Government]] (the local government of [[Zhuhai]]).

==Shareholders==
As of 31 December 2015, the largest shareholder of Gree Electric was [[Gree Group]]; it was followed by [[Hebei Jinghai Investment Guarantee]] (for 8.91%), [[China Securities Finance]] (for 2.99%), [[Central Huijin Investment]] (for 1.40%). [[UBS]] (for 1.21%), [[Foresea Life Insurance]] (for 1.14%), [[Yale University]] (for 0.95%), [[Hexie Health Insurance]] (for 0.80%), chairwoman Dong Mingzhu (for 0.73%) and a private equity fund (for 0.71%).<ref name=GreeElectric2015AR/>

In 2001 Gree Group owned a 50.289% stake in Gree Electric.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://disclosure.szse.cn/finalpage/2002-03-15/553010.PDF|title=2001 Annual Report|date=15 March 2002|access-date=10 December 2016|work=Gree Electric|publisher=Shenzhen Stock Exchange|language=Chinese}}</ref> Gree Group was also the largest shareholder of [[Gree Real Estate]] ({{SSE|600185}}) until 2015.

In December 2019, Gree Group sold most of the stake they owned in Gree Electric, to a private equity fund.
