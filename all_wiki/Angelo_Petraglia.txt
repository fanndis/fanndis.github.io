'''Angelo Petraglia''' (born May 5, 1954) is an American record producer and songwriter. He was a member of the new wave band [[Face to Face (new wave band)|Face to Face]] and is best known for his work with [[Kings of Leon]].

==Early life==
He was born in [[the Bronx]] and grew up in [[Pelham, New York]]. His father was a janitor and his mother was a bookkeeper. By the time he’d reached the third grade, he was playing guitar, inspired by [[Ricky Nelson]]’s brief promotional appearances at the end of [[Ozzie and Harriet]]. He studied visual art at [[New England College]].<ref name="nashville1">{{cite web|author=Hank Spuggard |url=http://www.nashville.com/music/2010/07/nashville-com-featured-songwriter-angelo/ |title=Featured Songwriter: Angelo « Nashville Music Scene |publisher=Nashville.com |accessdate=2012-05-19 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131006131344/http://www.nashville.com/music/2010/07/nashville-com-featured-songwriter-angelo/ |archivedate=2013-10-06 }}</ref>

==Songwriting career==
Petraglia has produced albums and co-written songs with [[Kings of Leon]].<ref name="gibson1">{{cite web|url=http://www2.gibson.com/News-Lifestyle/Features/en-us/kings-of-leon-producer-angelo.aspx |title=Kings of Leon Producer Angelo Petraglia on Early KOL and Their Gibsons |publisher=.gibson.com |date=2008-08-18 |accessdate=2012-05-19}}</ref> He, along with [[Larry Gottlieb]] and [[Kim Richey]], received a Grammy nomination as the songwriters for [[Trisha Yearwood]]’s hit "[[Believe Me Baby (I Lied)]]". Petraglia also wrote and produced [[Patty Griffin]]’s song "One Big Love" for her critically acclaimed record ''[[Flaming Red]]'' (1998). "One Big Love" was later recorded by [[Emmylou Harris]] and cut on her Grammy award winning record ''[[Red Dirt Girl]]'' (2000).<ref name="gibson1"/> He also co-wrote a song on [[Taylor Swift]]'s [[Taylor Swift (album)|self-titled debut album]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Angelo+Petraglia#Credits_Writing-Arrangement=&p=2&t=Credits_All |title=Angelo Petraglia Discography at Discogs |publisher=Discogs.com |date= |accessdate=2012-05-19}}</ref> He has written songs for Warren Zanes ([[Del Fuegos]]), [[Peter Wolf]] and [[Kim Richey]].<ref name="nashville1"/> He has also written songs for [[Martina McBride]], [[Tim McGraw]], [[Sara Evans]], [[Lee Ann Womack]], [[Brooks and Dunn]], and [[Jessica Andrews]].<ref name="nashville1"/>

Petraglia performs "[http://abc.go.com/music-lounge/blogs/exclusives/nashville-season-2-premiere Why Can't I Say Goodnight]" with [[Kim Richey]], a song he wrote with Richey for ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]''. The song is performed by [[Sam Palladio]] (Gunnar) and [[Clare Bowen]] (Scarlett) in the show.
