'''Khandaker Mosharraf Hossain''' (born 25 September 1942)<ref>{{Cite web|title=Constituency 213_11th_En|url=http://www.parliament.gov.bd/index.php/en/mps/members-of-parliament/current-mp-s/list-of-11th-parliament-members-english?layout=edit&id=3773|access-date=2020-10-19|website=www.parliament.gov.bd}}</ref> is a Bangladeshi politician. He is the incumbent [[Jatiya Sangsad]] member representing the [[Faridpur-3]] constituency since 2009. He served as a minister of the [[Ministry of Labour and Employment (Bangladesh)|Minister of Labour and Employment]], [[Ministry of Expatriates' Welfare and Overseas Employment|Minister of Expatriates' Welfare and Overseas Employment]] and [[Ministry of Local Government and Rural Development and Co-operative|Minister of Local Government and Rural Development and Co-operative]].<ref name=gov>{{cite web |url=http://www.cabinet.gov.bd/site/page/c3a1d735-abd6-4655-89ff-9e506d7537d7/Hon'ble-Ministers |title=Hon'ble Ministers |publisher=Government of Bangladesh |accessdate=2018-10-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151016223618/https://cabinet.gov.bd/site/page/c3a1d735-abd6-4655-89ff-9e506d7537d7/Hon'ble-Ministers|archive-date=2015-10-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2012-09-16|title=MKA replaces Shahara|url=https://www.thedailystar.net/news-detail-249887|access-date=2020-10-19|website=The Daily Star|language=en}}</ref>

==Education==
Hossain completed his BSc in [[Civil Engineering]] 1963 and MSc in Traffic and Highway Engineering from [[Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology]] (BUET). He also studied at [[Strathclyde University]] and earned the degree of Masters of Engineering.<ref name=probashi>{{cite web |url=http://www.probashi.gov.bd/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=94&Itemid=87 |title=Engineer Khandker Mosharraf Hossain, M.P. |website=Ministry of Expatriates' Welfare & Overseas Employment |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150512180943/http://www.probashi.gov.bd/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=94&Itemid=87 |archive-date=May 12, 2015 |access-date=March 17, 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
Hossain was the first chief engineer of the Rural Workers' Programme in Bangladesh. From 1973-80 he worked as the project director of this program.<ref name=probashi/> He joined BUET as a visiting professor in 1980.<ref name=probashi/> He worked as the chief technical consultant of ILO in Sierra-Leone during 1980-89 and Uganda during 1989–90. He was the president of Bangladesh Chamber of Industries (BCI) for two terms.<ref name=probashi/> Hossain was the President of International Labour Conference from June 2009 to June 2010. He was the president of Colombo Process, a forum of 11 Asian labour sending countries. The 4th Ministerial Consultation of Colombo Process was held in Dhaka from 19–21 April 2011 under his chairmanship.<ref name=probashi/> Hossain received Journalist Association Award for his contribution to the trade and industrial sector of Bangladesh.<ref name=probashi/>

==Personal life==
Hossain has a son, Khandaker Mashrur Hossain, who is married  to [[Saima Wazed]], the daughter of [[Sheikh Hasina]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hasina visits the house of her daughter's in-laws in Faridpur|url=https://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2017/03/30/hasina-visits-the-house-of-her-daughter-s-in-laws-in-faridpur|access-date=2020-10-19|website=bdnews24.com}}</ref>
