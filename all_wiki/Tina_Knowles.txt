==Career==
[[File:Beyoncé Cosmetology Center.jpg|thumbnail|left|Tina Knowles and her daughter Beyoncé Knowles-Carter pictured during the opening of the Beyoncé Cosmetology Center in Brooklyn in March 2010]]
Tina Knowles started her career at 19 when she relocated to [[California]] to work as a makeup artist for [[Shiseido]]. However, she returned home when her parents fell ill.<ref name=TNYT012117/> Knowles worked as a dance choreographer for [[University of Alabama at Birmingham|UAB]] in Birmingham, Alabama before working as a [[beautician]] until 1990 when she opened the salon Headliners, located in Houston.<ref name="texas" /> The salon went on to become one of the most famous hair businesses in Houston.<ref name="ebony" /> Tina's rise to prominence came as costume designer for Destiny's Child. In the early days of her daughter's career, when money was limited, she created outfits that the members wore on-stage and to events.<ref name="ebony">{{cite news|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GtYDAAAAMBAJ&q=tina+knowles+hair+salon&pg=PA166|title=Mother Power! - Beyoncé and Tina Knowles|work=[[Ebony (magazine)|Ebony]]|date=May 2003|volume=58|number=7|page=166|publisher=Johnson Publishing Company|accessdate=September 7, 2014|issn=0012-9011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/theobserver/2001/jun/10/life1.lifemagazine5|title=Date with destiny|last=Dunn|first=Jancee|date=June 10, 2001|work=[[The Observer]]|accessdate=September 7, 2014}}</ref> In 2002, she published a book titled ''Destiny's Style: Bootylicious Fashion, Beauty and Lifestyle Secrets From Destiny's Child'' in which she discussed how fashion affected Destiny's Child's success. The book was published through the company [[HarperCollins]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Book Excerpt: Destiny's Style |url=https://abcnews.go.com/GMA/WinterConcert/story?id=125692|publisher=[[ABC News]]. [[American Broadcasting Company]]|accessdate=September 7, 2014}}</ref>

In 2004, Tina Knowles launched the clothing line [[House of Deréon]] alongside Beyoncé, naming it after her mother Agnes Deréon.<ref name="dereon">{{cite news|url=http://www.chron.com/life/article/Tina-Knowles-offers-high-fashion-at-low-cost-1710274.php|title=Tina Knowles offers high fashion at low cost|work=[[Houston Chronicle]]|first=Joy|last=Sewing|date=October 17, 2010|accessdate=September 7, 2014}}</ref> On November 22, 2010, Knowles appeared with Beyoncé on ''[[The View (talk show)|The View]]'' to promote her clothing line called "Miss Tina by Tina Knowles".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://okmagazine.com/uncategorized/tina-knowles-shows-her-clothing-line-view/|title=Tina Knowles Shows Off Her Clothing Line on 'The View'|work=[[OK!]]|date=November 22, 2010|accessdate=September 7, 2014}}</ref> In 2010, she expanded the line to [[Walmart]] after it was previously being sold on the [[Home Shopping Network]].<ref name="dereon" /> Discussing her style of designing for the line, Knowles revealed she aimed to "hide flaws and create a slimming silhouette", adding, "Everything is created like a foundation garment, even the dresses. The pieces are designed to make the woman look leaner and taller."<ref name="dereon" /> In 2010, Tina Knowles again collaborated with Beyoncé to open the Beyoncé Cosmetology Center at the [[Phoenix House]] in Brooklyn.<ref name="dereon" />

In July 2020, Knowles made a cameo in Beyoncé's [[Disney+]] film ''[[Black Is King]]''. Before the film's release, she revealed she had let her daughter borrow art pieces for the project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/beyonce-tina-knowles-lawson-art-pieces-collection-project-950788/|title=Beyoncé Is Borrowing Some of Her Mother's Art Pieces for a Mysterious New Project|first1=Claire|last1=Shaffer|date=February 11, 2020}}</ref>
