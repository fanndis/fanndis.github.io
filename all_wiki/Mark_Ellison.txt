'''Mark Ellison [[QC]]''' is a British barrister and present head of [[QEB Hollis Whiteman (chambers)|QEB Holllis Whiteman]] chambers.<ref name=Leg500/><ref name=QEBHWBio>{{cite web|url=http://www.qebholliswhiteman.co.uk/queen-s-counsel/mark-ellison-qc.aspx|title=Mark Ellison QC|publisher=QEB Hollis Whiteman|accessdate=7 March 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140308014801/http://www.qebholliswhiteman.co.uk/queen-s-counsel/mark-ellison-qc.aspx|archive-date=8 March 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
Having gained his [[LLB]] at the [[University of Wales]],<ref name=QEBHWBio/> he was called to the bar at [[Gray's Inn]] in 1979. Specialising in fraud, he acted for both defence and prosecution in a number of high-profile serious fraud cases, including the [[Guinness share-trading fraud]] and [[Blue Arrow]]; and [[corruption]] involving [[North Sea oil]] and [[Channel Tunnel]] contracts.

In 1994 he was invited to become an [[HM Treasury]] counsel,<ref name=Leg500/><ref name=QEBHWBio/> and was appointed First Senior Treasury Counsel from 2006 to 2008.<ref name=Leg500/><ref name=QEBHWBio/> During this time he advised and acted in: complex serious fraud; corruption in public and private bodies; third party disclosure; trial secrecy; press freedom issue; admissibility and gathering of foreign evidence, including intercept and official secrets act; advice on prosecuting members of political parties for donation offences; advice on criminal law issues connected to the alleged illegality of the [[Iraq War]].<ref name=Leg500/><ref name=QEBHWBio/> He has also worked for clients with regards professional practise and disciplinary issues in front of the [[General Medical Council]].<ref name=Leg500/><ref name=QEBHWBio/>

==Notable cases==
===Stephen Lawrence===
{{main|Murder of Stephen Lawrence}}
In 2012, Ellison successfully led and secured the first convictions of any of [[Stephen Lawrence]]'s murderers, through the convictions for murder of Gary Dobson and David Norris. Despite inquiries by both [[Scotland Yard]] and the [[Independent Police Complaints Commission]] ruling that there was no new evidence to warrant further investigation, after discussions with [[Doreen Lawrence|Dame Doreen Lawrence]], [[Home Secretary]] [[Theresa May]] commissioned Ellison to review Scotland Yard's investigations into alleged police corruption.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/crime/lawrence-murder-police-corruption-will-be-investigated-7811117.html|title=May defies Met to order inquiry after Independent campaign|author=Andrew Grice & Paul Peachey|publisher=[[The Independent]]|date=2 June 2012|accessdate=7 March 2014}}</ref>

[https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/stephen-lawrence-independent-review The report], was presented to Parliament by May on 6 March 2014. [[Bernard Hogan-Howe|Sir Bernard Hogan-Howe]], [[Commissioner of the Metropolitan Police]] said the report, which has prompted an inquiry into undercover policing, was "devastating".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26485664|title=Hogan-Howe vows to restore trust in Met after new Lawrence row|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=7 March 2014|accessdate=7 March 2014}}</ref> Ellison's report also showed that there was substantial evidence linking an alleged corrupt police officer with involvement in the murder of [[private investigator]] [[Daniel Morgan (private investigator)|Daniel Morgan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-wales-26491279|title=Daniel Morgan murder case 'corruption link' with Lawrence investigation|publisher=[[BBC Wales]]|date=7 March 2014|accessdate=7 March 2014}}</ref>

==Notable appointments==
Ellison was appointed [[Queen's Counsel]] in 2008.<ref name=Leg500>{{cite web|url=http://www.legal500.com/firms/9590/offices/9590/lawyers/72025|title=Mark Ellison QC|publisher=[[The Legal 500]]|accessdate=7 March 2014}}</ref><ref name=QEBHWBio/> Ellison is a past chairman of [[Bar Council]]'s remuneration and terms of work committee, and a Bar Council member of the Criminal Justice Council.<ref name=Leg500/><ref name=QEBHWBio/>
