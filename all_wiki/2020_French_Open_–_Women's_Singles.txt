{{main article|2020 French Open}}

[[Iga Świątek]] defeated [[Sofia Kenin]] in the final, 6–4, 6–1 to win her first [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournament and capture the Women's Singles [[tennis]] title at the 2020 French Open.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2020/oct/10/iga-swiatek-beats-sofia-kenin-french-open-final-history|title=Iga Swiatek demolishes Sofia Kenin in final to make French Open history|date=10 October 2020|work=Guardian|accessdate=12 October 2020}}</ref>

This was also Świątek's first WTA singles title, making her the first player since [[Jeļena Ostapenko]] to win a major tournament as their maiden singles title.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rolandgarros.com/en-us/article/iga-swiatek-interview-still-in-shock-but-ready-to-embrace-new-status|title=Swiatek still in shock but ready to embrace new status|date=11 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020|work=Roland-Garros}}</ref> Ranked world No. 54 in the [[WTA rankings]], Świątek became the lowest-ranked player to win the [[French Open]] since rankings were introduced in 1975 and the lowest-ranked player to win a Grand Slam title since [[Sloane Stephens]] won the [[2017 US Open – Women's Singles|2017 US Open]]. Świątek also became the first Polish player, male or female, to win a Grand Slam singles title. Winning a French Open singles title at 19 years and 4 months of age made Świątek the youngest woman to do so since [[Monica Seles]] in [[1992 French Open – Women's Singles|1992]]. Świątek's [[List of Grand Slam related tennis records#Won a title without losing a set|did not drop a set throughout the entire tournament]], becoming the first player since [[Justine Henin]] in [[2007 French Open – Women's Singles|2007]] to win the French Open without dropping a set.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2020/10/iga-swiateks-unlikely-dominant-historic-win-roland-garros/91360/|title=Igainst all odds: Digging into stat and trend-busting title|work=Tennis|date=10 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref> Additionally, she did not lose more than four games in any set and did not lose more than five games in any match. Świątek dropped only 28 games throughout the tournament in total, the fewest since [[Steffi Graf]] dropped 20 games in [[1988 French Open – Women's Singles|1988]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-tennis-frenchopen-women/teenager-swiatek-dazzles-in-paris-to-claim-first-grand-slam-title-idUKKBN26V0NW|title=Teenager Swiatek dazzles in Paris to claim first Grand Slam title|work=[[Reuters]]|date=10 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref>, meaning that this was the first French Open where both the men's and women's singles champions did not drop a set on their way to the title. Świątek became the first female Polish player since [[Jadwiga Jędrzejowska]] in [[1939 French Championships – Women's Singles|1939]] to reach a French Open final, and the first to reach any Grand Slam final since [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] reached that stage of the [[2012 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|2012 Wimbledon Championships]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sportstar.thehindu.com/tennis/iga-swiatek-poland-celebrations-andrzej-duda-morawiecki-radwanska-french-open-2020/article32825941.ece|title=Poland rejoices after Swiatek's French Open victory|work=[[Sportstar]]|date=11 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref>

Kenin was attempting to become the first player since [[Angelique Kerber]] in [[2016 WTA Tour|2016]] to win their first two Grand Slam singles titles in the same year and the first female player to win more than one Grand Slam singles title in the same year since Kerber in 2016 as well, having won the [[2020 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2020 Australian Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2020/10/09/french-open-final-preview-prediction-iga-swiatek-vs-sofia-kenin-13396671/|title=French Open final preview and prediction: Iga Swiatek vs Sofia Kenin|work=[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]|date=9 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref>

[[Ashleigh Barty]] was the defending champion, but she chose not to participate due to safety concerns resulting from the ongoing [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tennishead.net/ashleigh-barty-withdraws-from-french-open-and-ends-season-saying-health-and-well-being-are-my-priority/|title=Ashleigh Barty withdraws from French Open and ends season: ‘Health and well-being are my priorities’|work=TennisHead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rfi.fr/en/sports/20200908-french-open-champion-ashleigh-barty-withdraws-from-2020-tournament-over-coronavirus-fears|title=French Open champion Ashleigh Barty withdraws from 2020 tournament over coronavirus fears|work=RFI}}</ref> This marked the first time since [[2008 French Open – Women's Singles|2008]] that the defending French Open champion did not participate and the second consecutive Grand Slam tournament where the defending champion did not participate, after [[Bianca Andreescu]] withdrew from the [[2020 US Open – Women's Singles|2020 US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis-tourtalk.com/60949/bianca-andreescu-withdraws-from-us-open|title=Bianca Andreescu Withdraws From US Open|work=Tennis TourTalk}}</ref>

[[Simona Halep]] was in contention for the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players#Singles|WTA No. 1 singles ranking]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.startribune.com/perfect-prep-halep-takes-14-match-win-streak-to-french-open/572546651/|title=Halep takes 14-match win streak to French Open|accessdate=27 September 2020}}</ref> but lost in the fourth round to Świątek. As a result, Barty retained the No. 1 ranking and finished the year as the year-end No. 1 for the second consecutive year.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://7news.com.au/sport/tennis/ashleigh-barty-makes-aussie-tennis-history-without-even-playing-a-match-c-1364594|title=Ashleigh Barty makes Aussie tennis history without even playing a match|date=5 October 2020|work=[[Seven News]]|accessdate=9 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://thenewdaily.com.au/sport/2020/10/05/french-open-ash-barty-no-1/|title=Run of French Open upsets guarantees Ash Barty year-end No.1 ranking|date=5 October 2020|work=[[The New Daily]]|accessdate=9 October 2020}}</ref> Halep's loss in the fourth round guaranteed a new French Open champion for the fifth consecutive year and a maiden Grand Slam finalist from the top half of the draw.

[[Serena Williams]] was attempting to add to her [[Open Era]] record of 23 Grand Slam singles titles and equal [[Margaret Court]]'s all-time record of 24 Grand Slam singles titles, but withdrew before her second round match due to a left Achilles injury she suffered earlier in September at the [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thenewdaily.com.au/sport/tennis/2020/09/26/serena-williams-margaret-court/|title=Serena Williams limps towards another shot at Margaret Court's record|date=26 September 2020|work=[[The New Daily]]|accessdate=27 September 2020}}</ref>

Only 13 of the 32 seeded players advanced to the third round, the fewest at any Grand Slam since [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]] expanded to 32 seeds in [[2001 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|2001]]. Only three seeded players advanced to the quarterfinals, the fewest since the [[1978 Australian Open – Women's Singles|1978 Australian Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2020/oct/02/simona-halep-piles-on-pressure-to-set-up-fourth-round-meeting-with-iga-swiatek|first=Kevin|last=Mitchell|title=Simona Halep piles on pressure to set up fourth round meeting with Iga Swiatek}}</ref> This was the first Grand Slam tournament since the [[1999 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1999 Wimbledon Championships]] that two qualifiers, [[Nadia Podoroska]] and [[Martina Trevisan]], reached the quarterfinals.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennisnow.com/News/2020/October/Qualifying-to-Quarters-Trevisan-and-Podoroska-Stu.aspx|title=Qualifying to quarters: Trevisan and Podoroska stun and run in paris|work=Tennis Now|date=4 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref> The semifinal match between Świątek and Podoroska was also the first semifinal to be contested by two unseeded players at a Grand Slam since the [[1983 French Open – Women's Singles|1983 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2020/oct/08/iga-swiatek-thrashes-nadia-podoroska-to-reach-french-open-final|title=Iga Swiatek thrashes Podoroska to set up French Open final against Kenin|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=8 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref>

Podoroska became the first qualifier in history to reach the semifinals of the French Open and the first to reach the semifinals at any Grand Slam tournament since [[Alexandra Stevenson]] achieved this feat at the aforementioned 1999 Wimbledon Championships.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1857473/podoroska-stuns-svitolina-to-create-roland-garros-history|title=Podoroska stuns Svitolina to create Roland Garros history|work=[[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]]|date=6 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref> She also became the first female Argentine player since [[Paola Suárez]] in 2004 to reach the quarterfinals of a Grand Slam tournament in singles and the lowest-ranked semifinalist (ranked No. 130) at any Grand Slam since Henin reached that stage of the [[2010 Australian Open – Women's Singles|2010 Australian Open]] as an unranked player.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2020/oct/08/french-open-womens-semi-finals-swiatek-podoroska-kenin-kvitova-live-tennis?page=with:block-5f7f17b78f08bbf7a1fff191|title=French Open: Kenin beats Kvitova and will play Swiatek in the final – as it happened|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=8 October 2020|accessdate=1 November 2020}}</ref>
