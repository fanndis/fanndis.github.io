'''Trump''' ({{IPAc-en|us|ˈ|t|r|ʌ|m|p}}; {{IPA-de|ˈtʁʊmp|lang}}; {{IPA-all|ˈdrʊmpʰ|[[Palatine German language|Palatine German]]:}})<ref name="theguardian1">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/jan/29/kallstadt-germany-on-the-trail-of-the-donald-in-the-trump-ancestral-home |title=Kallstadt, Germany: on the trail of 'the Donald' in the Trump ancestral home |first=Kate |last=Connolly |date=January 29, 2016 |location=London |work=The Guardian}}</ref> is a German and German-American family originating in the [[Electoral Palatinate]], [[Holy Roman Empire]], today in modern [[Germany]].

While the [[Trump (surname)|surname Trump]] is found throughout Germany,<!--originated in Bavaria?--> it has been recorded variously as Drumb, Tromb, Tromp, Trum, Trumpff, and Dromb in Kallstadt since the 17th century.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Connolly|first=Kate|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/jan/29/kallstadt-germany-on-the-trail-of-the-donald-in-the-trump-ancestral-home|title=Kallstadt, Germany: on the trail of 'the Donald' in the Trump ancestral home|date=2016-01-29|work=The Guardian|access-date=2020-02-08|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Family members still live in the region in [[southwestern Germany]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.politico.eu/article/donald-trump-ancestry-forefathers-kallstadt/|title=Donald Trump, Germany’s disfavored son|date=September 23, 2016}}</ref>

Members of the family came to the United States in the 19th century, including Charlotte Louisa Trump's son Johann Heinrich Heinz, the father of [[Henry J. Heinz]], the founder of the [[Heinz]] company.

==History==
{{further|Trump (surname)}}
[[File:Kallstadt Hauptstrasse.jpg|thumb|left|[[Kallstadt]], [[Germany]], the Trump family's ancestral home.]]
''[[Trump (surname)|Trump]]'' is a [[German surname]] derived from a word that was used to refer both  to [[:wikt:drum|drum]]s and to  [[:wikt:trumpet|trumpets]].<ref>Bahlow: Deutsches Namenslexikon (1982). MHG ''trumpe, trumbe, trume'' could mean either "trumpet" or "drum". Lexer, ''Mittelhochdeutsches Handwörterbuch'' (1872–1878) records variants in ''d-'' (''drumme, drume, drumpe'') under the meaning "drum"</ref> The name is on record in [[Kallstadt]] from the 17th century.<ref name=gedbas />

[[Gwenda Blair]] suggests the family may be descended from an itinerant lawyer, Hanns Drumpf, who settled in Kallstadt in 1608 and whose descendants changed their name from Drumpf to Trump during the [[Thirty Years' War]] of [[17th century|the early 1600s]].<ref name="BlairBook">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uJifCgAAQBAJ|title=The Trumps: Three Generations of Builders and a Presidential Candidate|date=2015|publisher=Simon and Schuster|pages=26–27|last1=Blair|first1=Gwenda|access-date=March 4, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Donald Drumpf: A Funny Label, but Is It Fair|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/03/us/politics/donald-drumpf-a-funny-label-but-is-it-fair.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|author=Victor, Daniel|date=March 2, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/ideas/2015/09/09/why-donald-trump-better-than-donald-drumpf/7ltKCyRasCa5TY2l1Tr4HP/story.html | title = Why Donald Trump trumps Donald Drumpf | work = [[The Boston Globe]] | access-date = March 3, 2016 }}</ref> However, this is not in accordance with data provided by German genealogists.<ref name=gedbas>Verein für Computergenealogie: Welle}}</ref>

Johannes Trump from the nearby village [[Bobenheim am Berg]] had established himself as a winegrower in Kallstadt by the late 17th century.<ref name=gedbas /> Several of his descendants also were vintners in Kallstadt, one of many villages in the famous wine-growing region of the [[Palatinate (region)|Palatinate]] (''Pfalz'').<ref name="Blair">{{cite book | last = Blair | first = Gwenda | title = The Trumps: Three Generations of Builders and a Presidential Candidate | publisher = Simon & Schuster | edition = 1st | date = 2001 | page = [https://archive.org/details/trumps00gwen/page/26 26] | url = https://archive.org/details/trumps00gwen/page/26 | isbn = 9780743210799 }}</ref> Charlotte Louisa Trump (1789–1833) married Johann Georg Heinz, and their son Johann Heinrich (John Henry) Heinz (1811–1891) emigrated from Kallstadt to the United States in 1840 and was the father of [[Henry J. Heinz]] (1844–1919), the founder of the [[Heinz]] company.

[[Frederick Trump]], grandfather of Donald Trump, was born 1869 in [[Kallstadt]] as Friedrich Trump.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.swr.de/swraktuell/rp/kaiserslautern/wie-donald-trumps-grossvater-bordellbetreiber-wurde/-/id=1632/did=17872442/nid=1632/ve9e7k/index.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=January 15, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180116024701/https://www.swr.de/swraktuell/rp/kaiserslautern/wie-donald-trumps-grossvater-bordellbetreiber-wurde/-/id=1632/did=17872442/nid=1632/ve9e7k/index.html |archive-date=January 16, 2018 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> [[Frederick Trump|Friedrich Trump]] was one of six children of a winegrower and a second cousin of [[Henry J. Heinz]], the founder of [[Heinz]]. Friedrich learned the profession of a barber. In 1885, at the age of 16, Friedrich emigrated from Kallstadt to the United States and [[Anglicisation|anglicized]] his name to ''Frederick'' in 1892 when he became a U.S. citizen.<ref name=dad>{{cite news |last=Blair |first=Gwenda |date=August 24, 2015 |title=The Man Who Made Trump Who He Is |url=http://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2015/08/the-man-who-made-trump-who-he-is-121647 |newspaper=[[Politico]] |access-date=July 24, 2016}}</ref>

In March 1894 Trump moved to the emerging mining town of [[Monte Cristo, Washington]] in [[Snohomish County]] north of Seattle.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/northwest/donald-trumps-grandfather-got-business-start-in-seattle/ |title=Donald Trump's grandfather got business start in Seattle |author=Evan Bush |date= August 25, 2015 |access-date= September 21, 2015 |newspaper=[[The Seattle Times]]}}</ref> After evidence of mineral deposits had been discovered in 1889, Monte Cristo was expected to produce a fortune in gold and silver.<ref name="Blair"/>{{rp|53–58}} During the [[Klondike Gold Rush|Alaska Gold Rush]], he amassed a fortune by catering to the needs of [[Prospecting#The gold rushes|gold seekers]] on their way to the region, providing alcohol, restaurants and hotels with special suites for prostitutes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.swp.de/ulm/nachrichten/vermischtes/trumps-grossvater-war-ein-gluecksritter-aus-der-pfalz-13596007.html|title=Trump's Großvater war ein Glücksritter aus der Pfalz|website=Südwestpresse|date=9 August 2016|access-date=August 25, 2018}}</ref><ref name=brothel>{{cite web|last1=Panetta|first1=Alexander|title=Donald Trump's grandfather ran Canadian brothel during gold rush|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/trump-canada-yukon-1.3235254|website=[[CBC News]]|access-date=December 10, 2015|date=September 19, 2015}}</ref>

During a visit to Kallstadt,{{when|date=January 2020}} Frederick was accused of having left the country to avoid military service and was deprived of his Bavarian citizenship, later being banished from Germany for the same offence.<ref name=dad/><ref>The Guardian,"Historian finds German decree banishing Trump's grandfather," 21 November 2016 [https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/nov/21/trump-grandfather-friedrich-banished-germany-historian-royal-decree]</ref> As he had not served the mandatory military duty of two years in the [[Kingdom of Bavaria]], his emigration was deemed illegal.<ref name="The New York Times Company">{{cite web |last1=Bennhold |first1=Katrin |title=Trump’s Ancestral Village Abounds With His Relatives. Few Admit a Link. |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/07/02/world/europe/trump-germany-family-ancestry-kallstadt.html |website=The New York Times |publisher=The New York Times Company |access-date=22 December 2019}}</ref>

During the visit, Trump fell in love with [[Elizabeth Christ Trump|Elisabeth Christ]] (1880–1966) and married her in 1902. She was also a native of Kallstadt, and born as the daughter of Philipp and Marie Christ (Philipp Christ was descended from Johannes Christ (1626–1688/9) of [[Flörsheim]], [[Hesse]]<ref name=GEDBAS>{{cite web|url=https://gedbas.genealogy.net/person/ancestors/1178251526|title=GEDBAS: Vorfahren von Frederick Christ TRUMP|access-date=November 10, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161104075957/https://gedbas.genealogy.net/person/ancestors/1178251526|archive-date=November 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>). Frederick sold his business and settled with Elizabeth in New York City. Elisabeth soon became homesick, and they returned to Germany. Trump tried unsuccessfully to regain his residency. To avoid deportation he wrote to [[Luitpold, Prince Regent of Bavaria|Prince Luitpold]], but was refused due to his lack of military service.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/letter-donald-trumps-grandfather-wrote-pleading-to-stay-in-germany-surfaces-1479746306|title=Letter Donald Trump’s Grandfather Wrote Pleading to Stay in Germany Surfaces|website=The Wall Street Journal|date=November 21, 2016|access-date=August 25, 2018}}</ref><ref name="The New York Times Company"/> On 1 July 1905 they boarded the [[SS Pennsylvania (1896)|SS ''Pennsylvania'']] and returned to the U.S. Frederick and Elisabeth were the parents of Elizabeth Trump (1904–1961), real estate developer [[Fred Trump]] (1905–1999) and physicist [[John G. Trump]] (1907–1985). Frederick died of the [[Spanish flu]] on May 30, 1918.

==Immediate family of Donald Trump==

President Donald Trump has married three times, to [[Ivana Trump|Ivana Zelníčková]], [[Marla Maples]] and [[Melania Trump|Melania Knauss]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/05/09/who-is-melania-trump |title=The Model American |newspaper=[[The New Yorker]] |date=May 9, 2016 |access-date=November 10, 2016}}</ref>

President Trump has five children of whom four are adult children from two previous marriages: [[Donald Trump Jr.|Donald Jr.]], [[Ivanka Trump|Ivanka]], and [[Eric Trump]] with [[Ivana Trump]], and [[Tiffany Trump]] with [[Marla Maples]]. Trump has one son with Melania Trump, Barron, born on March 20, 2006. He is the first pre-teen son to live in the [[White House]] since [[John F. Kennedy Jr.]] arrived as an infant in 1961.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.miamiherald.com/latest-news/article113805053.html |title=Barron Trump: First young presidential son in the White House since 1963 |newspaper=[[Miami Herald]] |date=November 9, 2016 |first=Luisa |last=Yanez |access-date=November 10, 2016}}</ref> All three of his children with Ivana Trump were executive vice presidents at [[the Trump Organization]] until Trump was elected president. Tiffany Trump is a recent college graduate of the [[University of Pennsylvania]], her father's [[alma mater]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-37924617 |title=US election: Trump children&nbsp;– who is the new first family? |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=November 9, 2016 |access-date=November 10, 2016}}</ref>

==Religious affiliations==
The original Trump family in [[Germany]] are [[Lutheranism|Lutheran]] members<ref>The small town of Kallstadt has only one religious building, the Central Lutheran church. See [[Kallstadt]] for more information.</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=[[The Trumps: Three Generations That Built an Empire]]|date=2000|publisher=Simon and Schuster|isbn=978-0743210799|pages=[https://archive.org/details/trumps00gwen/page/28 28–29]|author1=[[Gwenda Blair]]}}</ref> of the [[Evangelical Church of the Palatinate]], although that region [[Electoral Palatinate|has been]] overwhelmingly [[Calvinist]]<ref>Note that local Calvinists are also members of the [[Evangelical Church of the Palatinate]], because it is a [[United church]].</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oikoumene.org/en/member-churches/ekd-evangelical-church-of-the-palatinate|title=EKD&nbsp;– Evangelical Church of the Palatinate&nbsp;– World Council of Churches |website=www.oikoumene.org}}</ref> since [[Frederick III, Elector Palatine]] introduced the Reformed faith in the mid 16th century.<ref name="Blair28">Blair 2001, pp. 28–29</ref> Members of the family's American branch, beginning with Mary Anne McLeod, tend to be [[Presbyterians]], [[Reformed Church in America|Dutch Reformed]] or adherents of other [[Calvinism|Reformed denominations]]. Donald Trump's parents belonged to the [[Reformed Church in America]], although he was [[Confirmation|confirmed]] at one of the [[Presbyterian Church (USA)]] congregations himself.<ref>Blair, Gwenda (2001). T''he Trumps: Three Generations of Builders and a Presidential Candidate'' (1st ed.). Simon & Schuster. pp. 28–29, 453; {{ISBN|978-0743210799}}.</ref> This matter of membership, like in case of many [[American Protestants]], does not play a big role since he attends services of both denominations and other ones that are part of the [[Reformed tradition]], and sometimes even some that subscribe to other Protestant branches (like the [[Episcopal Church (USA)]], an [[Anglican]] denomination).

In 2016, Donald Trump visited [[Bethesda-by-the-Sea]], an Episcopal Church, for a Christmas service.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://ijr.com/2016/12/763547-church-of-bethesda-by-the-sea-had-a-strong-reaction-to-donald-trumps-attendance-at-their-christmas-service/ |title=When the Trump Family Arrives to Church of Bethesda-by-the-Sea The Crowd Rises For A Standing Ovation |newspaper=Independent Journal Review |date=December 2016 |access-date=March 17, 2017 |first=Jenni |last=Fink }}</ref> Melania Trump is, like most Slovenians, Roman Catholic.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/melania-trump-catholic-white-house_us_59270d0ce4b0265790f5f76f|title=Melania Trump Will Be The First Catholic To Live At The White House Since JFK|website=HuffPost|language=en|access-date=2017-05-25}}</ref> Ivanka Trump practices [[Judaism]], having converted upon marrying [[Jared Kushner]].<ref name="auto2">{{cite web |url=http://www.jta.org/2016/11/06/news-opinion/united-states/ivanka-trump-visits-the-lubavitcher-rebbes-grave-ahead-of-election |title=Ivanka Trump visits the Lubavitcher rebbe's grave ahead of election |date=November 6, 2016 |publisher=Jewish Telegraphic Agency}}</ref><ref name=haaretz>{{Cite news|url= http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/spages/1032908.html| title= Report: Tycoon Donald Trump's daughter converting to Judaism|work=Haaretz|date=October 30, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081102093814/http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/spages/1032908.html|archive-date=November 2, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal| last= Messer| first= Lesley|url= http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20291939,00.html |title= Ivanka Trump Converts to Judaism for Fiancé| work= People| date= July 17, 2009| access-date= February 19, 2010}}</ref>
