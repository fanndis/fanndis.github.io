'''Manohla June Dargis''' ({{IPAc-en|m|ə|ˈ|n|oʊ|l|ə|_|ˈ|d|ɑːr|ɡ|ɪ|s}})<ref>{{cite podcast|url=http://linoleumknife.libsyn.com/manohla-dargis-on-2016|title=Manohla Dargis on 2016|work=Linoleum Knife|date=January 15, 2017|minutes=1 hour and 22|accessdate=December 4, 2019}}</ref> is an American [[film critic]]. She is one of the chief film critics for ''[[The New York Times]]'', along with [[A. O. Scott]].<ref name="NYT Bio">{{cite news|url=https://movies.nytimes.com/ref/movies/bio_dargis.html|title=Film Critic Biography: Manohla Dargis|work=[[The New York Times]]|access-date=September 12, 2010 | date=December 7, 2004}}</ref> She is a five-time finalist for the [[Pulitzer Prize for Criticism]].

==Career==
Before being a film critic for ''[[The New York Times]]'', Dargis was a chief film critic for the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'', the film editor at the ''[[LA Weekly]]'', and a film critic at ''[[The Village Voice]]'', where she had two columns on [[Experimental film|avant-garde cinema]] ("CounterCurrents" and "Shock Corridor"). Her work has been included in a number of books, including ''Women and Film: A Sight and Sound Reader'' and ''American Movie Critics: An Anthology from the Silents Until Now,'' published by the [[Library of America]]. She wrote a monograph on [[Curtis Hanson]]'s film ''[[L.A. Confidential (film)|L.A. Confidential]]'' for the [[British Film Institute]] and served as the president and vice-president of the [[Los Angeles Film Critics Association]].

In 2012, Dargis received the Nelson A. Rockefeller Award from [[State University of New York at Purchase|Purchase College]]; the award is, according to the college, "presented to individuals who have distinguished themselves through their contributions to the arts."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.purchase.edu/soagala/rockefeller.aspx|title=Nelson A. Rockefeller Awards|publisher=Purchase.edu|access-date=April 29, 2016|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160421103610/https://www.purchase.edu/soagala/rockefeller.aspx|archivedate=April 21, 2016|df=mdy-all}}</ref> In 2013, Matt Barone of ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' named her the eighth-greatest film critic of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/pop-culture/2013/02/best-movie-critics-of-all-time/ |title=The 25 Best Movie Critics of All Time |last=Barone |first=Matt |work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=February 8, 2013 |access-date=September 21, 2016}}</ref> She was also a finalist for the [[Pulitzer Prize for Criticism]] in 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pulitzer.org/citation/2013-Criticism|title=The 2013 Pulitzer Prize Winners Criticism|publisher=pulitzer.org|access-date=May 16, 2015}}</ref> 2015,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pulitzer.org/citation/2015-Criticism|title=The 2015 Pulitzer Prize Winners Criticism|publisher=pulitzer.org|access-date=May 16, 2015}}</ref> 2016,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pulitzer.org/citation/2016-Criticism|title=The 2016 Pulitzer Prize Winners Criticism|publisher=pulitzer.org|access-date=April 26, 2016}}</ref> 2018,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pulitzer.org/prize-winners-by-year/2018|title = 2018 Pulitzer Prize Winners & Finalists|publisher=pulitzer.org|accessdate = April 16, 2018}}</ref> and 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pulitzer.org/prize-winners-by-year/2019|title=2019 Pulitzer Prize Winners & Finalists|website=www.pulitzer.org|language=en|access-date=April 20, 2019}}</ref>

Dargis participated in the [[The Sight & Sound Greatest Films of All Time 2012|2012 ''Sight & Sound'' critics' poll]], where she listed her 10 favorite films as follows: ''[[Au Hasard Balthazar]]'', ''[[Barry Lyndon]]'', ''[[Flowers of Shanghai]]'', ''[[The Flowers of St. Francis]]'', ''[[The Godfather Part II]]'', ''Little Stabs at Happiness'', ''[[Masculin Féminin]]'', ''[[There Will Be Blood]]'', ''[[Touch of Evil]]'', and ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]''.<ref>[https://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/sightandsoundpoll2012/voter/372]</ref>

==Personal life==
Dargis grew up in [[Manhattan]]'s [[East Village, Manhattan|East Village]], evincing an early love of film through regular attendance at [[8th Street and St. Mark's Place|St. Mark's Cinema and Theater 80]].<ref name="NYT Bio"/> She graduated from [[Hunter College High School]] and received her BA in literature from [[State University of New York at Purchase]] in January 1985.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.purchase.edu/AboutPurchase/Rockefeller/pastrecipients.aspx |title=Rockefeller Award past recipients |publisher=Purchase.edu |access-date=August 27, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150928234525/http://www.purchase.edu/AboutPurchase/Rockefeller/pastrecipients.aspx |archivedate=September 28, 2015 |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>Purchase College, SUNY Institutional Advancement (914)-251-7909</ref> She received a master of arts in cinema studies in 1988 from the [[New York University Graduate School of Arts and Science]]. Dargis married wine expert Lou Amdur in 1994. They live in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.usc.edu/libraries/partners/laih/fellows/ManohlaDargis.htm |title=Manohla Dargis |publisher=University of Southern California |access-date=November 26, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130708145142/http://www.usc.edu/libraries/partners/laih/fellows/ManohlaDargis.htm |archivedate=July 8, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
