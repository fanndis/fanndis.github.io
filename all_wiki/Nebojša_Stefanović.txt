'''Nebojša Stefanović''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Небојша Стефановић}}, {{IPA-sh|nɛ̌bɔːjʃa stɛfǎːnɔʋitɕ|pron}}; born 20 November 1976) is a Serbian politician. He has served as [[Ministry of Defence (Serbia)|Minister of Defence]] since 2020 and [[Deputy Prime Minister of Serbia|Deputy Prime Minister]] since 2016. He is chairman of the Belgrade board of [[Serbian Progressive Party]] (SNS).<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nebojsastefanovic.rs/nebojsa-stefanovic-biografija/|title=Биографија|date=27 February 2013|work=Небојша Стефановић|access-date=2018-04-17|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180414010428/http://nebojsastefanovic.rs/nebojsa-stefanovic-biografija/|archive-date=14 April 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

His past tenure included the [[President of the National Assembly of Serbia|speaker of the National Assembly of Serbia]] from July 2012 until April 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.parlament.gov.rs/national-assembly/the-assembly-1804-2018/multi-party-national-assembly-of-the-republic-of-serbia-(1991-2018).2174.html|title=National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia {{!}} Multi-party National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia (1991–2018)|website=www.parlament.gov.rs|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref>

He served as [[Ministry of Internal Affairs (Serbia)|Minister of the Interior]] of the Republic of Serbia in the period from April 2014 to October 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=srbija.gov.rs|title=Примопредаја дужности у Министарству одбране и МУП-у|url=https://www.srbija.gov.rs/vest/493792/primopredaja-duznosti-u-ministarstvu-odbrane-i-mup-u.php|access-date=2020-10-30|website=www.srbija.gov.rs|language=sr}}</ref>

He began his political career in the [[Serbian Radical Party]] (SRS), while in 2008, he was one of the founders of the Serbian Progressive Party (SNS). Stefanović holds a disputed PhD in economics, which caused controversy.

== Early life and education ==
Nebojša Stefanović was born on 20 November 1976 in Belgrade. He grew up in [[New Belgrade]], where he finished elementary school and I[[Ninth Belgrade Gymnasium|X Grammar School "Mihajlo Petrović Alas"]] in Belgrade. He graduated from the Graduate School of Business Studies of the [[John Naisbitt University|Megatrend University]]. He received his master's degree in Economics defending his master's thesis ''Contemporary Principles of Management in Local Self-Government'' in 2011. Furthermore, in June 2013, he earned his doctorate in Economics with the thesis ''A New Role of Strategic Management in Managing Local Self-Government''.

He has begun his business career in 2004 in domestic and foreign trade company "Interspeed Ltd." on the position of [[Marketing management|Marketing Manager]]. In 2008, he was appointed on the position of deputy financial director of company "Jabuka."

Nebojša Stefanović speaks English and Russian. He is married to Ana Stefanović and has two children.<ref>{{Cite web|last=srbija.gov.rs|title=Nebojsa Stefanovic|url=https://www.srbija.gov.rs/biografija/en/33975/nebojsa-stefanovic.php|access-date=2020-10-30|website=www.srbija.gov.rs|language=en}}</ref>

== Political career ==
Stefanović has been involved in politics for more than 25 years. He has begun his political career in the [[Serbian Radical Party]] (SRS). He is one of the founders of the [[Serbian Progressive Party]] (SNS), member of the Presidency<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.srbijadanas.com/clanak/skupstina-sns-izabrano-42-clana-predsednistva-stranke-28-05-2016|title=STRANKA IZABRALA: Ovo su novi članovi Predsedništva SNS-a|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr-RS}}</ref> and chairman of the [[Belgrade]] board of Serbian Progressive Party.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nebojsastefanovic.rs/predsednik-gradskog-odbora-sns-stefanovic-nebojsa/|title=Председник Градског одбора Београд Српске напредне странке|date=9 May 2013|work=Небојша Стефановић|access-date=2018-04-17|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180413191600/http://nebojsastefanovic.rs/predsednik-gradskog-odbora-sns-stefanovic-nebojsa/|archive-date=13 April 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> As the chairman of the Belgrade board, he made significant achievements along with his team. In the 2014 [[2014 Belgrade City Assembly election|Belgrade City Assembly election]], SNS came to power with 43.62% votes,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.izbori.live/?electionId=6&resultSourceId=8|title=Избори Уживо|website=www.izbori.live|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref> while in the [[2018 Belgrade City Assembly election|2018 election]], SNS won again with 44.99% votes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/naslovna/politika/aktuelno.289.html:715122-KONACNI-REZULTATI-IZBORA-U-BEOGRADU-Lista-Aleksandar-Vucic---Zato-sto-volimo-Beorgad-osvojila-4499-odsto-glasova|title=KONAČNI REZULTATI IZBORA U BEOGRADU: Lista "Aleksandar Vučić – Zato što volimo Beorgad" osvojila 44,99 odsto glasova|website=www.novosti.rs|language=sr-Latn|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref>

From the establishment of the SNS until the fourth assembly of the SNS (held in 2016), he was a vice president of the main board of SNS.

=== Member of Parliament ===
As a representative of SRS, He was a councillor in the [[City Assembly of Belgrade]] during the 2004–2008 tenure. In the [[2007 Serbian parliamentary election|2007 parliamentary elections]], he was elected for member of the [[National Assembly (Serbia)|National Assembly of Serbia]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.parlament.gov.rs/national-assembly/composition/members-of-parliament/legislature-archive/14-february-2007-legislature.489.html|title=National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia {{!}} 14 February 2007 legislature|website=www.parlament.gov.rs|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref> and in the same time he served as Chair of the Committee for Trade and Tourism of the National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia.

In the [[2012 Serbian parliamentary election|2012 parliamentary elections]], as a representative of SNS, he was reelected for member of the National Assembly of Serbia and councillor in the City Assembly of Belgrade.

=== Speaker of the National Assembly of Serbia ===
From July 2012 until April 2014, Nebojša Stefanović was [[President of the National Assembly of Serbia|Speaker of the National Assembly of Serbia]], as well as the Chair of the Committee for the Rights of the Child. Moreover, he was a Head of Delegation of the National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia in the Inter-Parliamentary Union.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.parlament.gov.rs/national-assembly/composition/delegations/delegations.15.497.html|title=National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia {{!}} Delegations|website=www.parlament.gov.rs|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref>

During his tenure as speaker of the National Assembly, he was remembered for his struggles and commitments to pregnant women and mothers. On his initiative, the Law on Right to Healthcare for children, pregnant women and maternities was adopted.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.paragraf.rs/dnevne-vesti/281113/281113-stampa2.html|title=Paragraf {{!}} Arhiva vesti {{!}} 28 November 2013.|website=www.paragraf.rs|access-date=2018-04-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180413185649/https://www.paragraf.rs/dnevne-vesti/281113/281113-stampa2.html|archive-date=13 April 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> He also launched the notable initiative Priorities for children and pregnant women, which is in force up to this date.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.rts.rs/page/stories/sr/story/125/drustvo/1343728/drustvena-briga-za-decu-i-trudnice-.html|title=Društvena briga za decu i trudnice|last=Serbia|first=RTS, Radio televizija Srbije, Radio Television of|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr}}</ref>

=== Deputy prime minister and minister of the interior ===
After the [[2014 Serbian parliamentary election|2014 parliamentary elections]], he was appointed on the position of the [[Ministry of Internal Affairs (Serbia)|minister of the interior]]. He was reelected on the same position after the [[2016 Serbian parliamentary election|2016 parliamentary elections]], as well as appointed for the [[Deputy Prime Minister of Serbia|deputy prime minister]]. He has remained on the same positions after the [[First cabinet of Ana Brnabić|2017 cabinet reshuffle]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.srbija.gov.rs/vlada/ministri.php#67121|title=Потпредседници и министри|website=www.srbija.gov.rs|access-date=2018-04-13}}</ref>

''During his tenure'' as the minister of the interior, numerous of reforms have been implemented. One, out of his many personal initiatives, was implementation of the program Children Security Basics in the first, fourth and sixth grades of elementary schools in [[Serbia]]. The purpose of this program is to introduce children with all challenges and dangers they can encounter.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.danas.rs/drustvo/stefanovic-uvodi-se-predmet-osnovi-bezbednosti-dece/|title=Stefanović: Uvodi se predmet "Osnovi bezbednosti dece"|date=23 February 2018|work=Dnevni list Danas|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr-RS}}</ref>

He is the first minister of the interior which has launched the initiative for building of the monument for the fallen police officers in Belgrade.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.b92.net/info/vesti/index.php?yyyy=2018&mm=02&dd=27&nav_category=11&nav_id=1363683|title=Poginulim policajcima spomen-obeležje u BG|work=B92.net|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr-Latn-RS}}</ref>

Besides these, he has been holding position of the president of the Council for the fight against human trafficking,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mfin.gov.rs/pages/article.php?id=13468|title=Министарство финансија – Влада републике Србије –  online|website=www.mfin.gov.rs|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref> commander of the Headquarters for emergency situation of the Republic of Serbia,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pravno-informacioni-sistem.rs/SlGlasnikPortal/reg/viewAct/f48b1d3f-dbc0-4a14-8fd9-7b32e0fb605a|title=Odluka o obrazovanju Republičkog štaba za vanredne situacije|website=www.pravno-informacioni-sistem.rs|access-date=2018-04-17}}</ref> the president of the National Council for fight against hazardous occurrences in sports,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.danas.rs/drustvo/konstituisan-nacionalni-savet-za-sprecavanje-negativnih-pojava-u-sportu/|title=Konstituisan "Nacionalni savet za sprečavanje negativnih pojava u sportu"|date=9 December 2017|work=Dnevni list Danas|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr-RS}}</ref> as well as the president of the Coordination team for guiding activities in fight against money laundering and terrorism financing.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://rs.n1info.com/a369767/Vesti/Brnabic-o-crnoj-listi-FATF-a.html|title=Brnabić: Izveštaj FATF-a više ne odražava realnu situaciju|work=N1 Srbija|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr-Latn}}</ref> From 7 November 2017, he was appointed as a Secretary of the National Security Council, and at the same time the Head of the Bureau for Coordination of Security Services.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://rs.n1info.com/a340220/Vesti/Vesti/Stefanovic-sef-Biroa-za-koordinaciju-rada-sluzbi-bezbednosti.html|title=Stefanović je novi šef Biroa za koordinaciju rada službi bezbednosti|work=N1 Srbija|access-date=2018-04-17|language=sr-Latn}}</ref>

=== Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Defense ===
On 28 October 2020, [[National Assembly (Serbia)|the National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia]] elected a new Government of the Republic of Serbia led by the Prime Minister [[Ana Brnabić]]. On 29 October 2020, the newly elected Minister of Defense Nebojša Stefanović, assumed the office from the former Minister, [[Aleksandar Vulin]].

=== Doctorate controversy ===
On 1 June 2014, a group of Serbian academics based in the UK published an article claiming that parts of Mr. Stefanović's doctoral dissertation were plagiarised.<ref>[http://balkanist.net/getting-a-phd-in-serbia-has-never-been-easier-the-case-of-minister-of-internal-affairs-nebojsa-stefanovic/ Getting a PhD in Serbia has Never Been Easier: The Case of Minister of Internal Affairs Nebojša Stefanović]. Balkanist. 1 June 2014.</ref>

In June 2014 the Senate of Megatrend University formed a commission with the agenda of evaluation of merits for initiation of the procedure for his doctorate annulment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cir.megatrend.edu.rs/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Odluka1.pdf|title=Одлука о образовању Комисије|last=|first=|date=5 June 2014|website=|publisher=Мегатренд универзитет|language=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2018-04-16}}</ref> The university Senate then adopted a report from the Commission which stated that there were no grounds for initiation of the procedure for the Nebojša Stefanović's doctorate annulment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cir.megatrend.edu.rs/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Odluka.pdf|title=Усвајање извештаја Комисије|last=|first=|date=11 June 2014|website=|publisher=Мегатренд универзитет|language=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2018-04-16}}</ref> In August 2014 another independent Commission was formed by the Senate which later unanimously ruled that there were no grounds for initiation of the procedure for Nebojša Stefanović's doctorate annulment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://megatrend.edu.rs/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/senatu-mtu.pdf|title=Стаб независне Комисије|last=|first=|date=23 September 2014|website=|publisher=Мегатренд универзитет|language=|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180417023034/http://megatrend.edu.rs/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/senatu-mtu.pdf|archive-date=17 April 2018|access-date=2018-04-16|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Arms trade scandal ===
A GIM letter dated 27 April 2017 to Krusik lists the names of three GIM representatives and three Raniad Al Jazira employees who visited the arms factory in order to inspect and accept the ordered weapons. Among the GIM representatives was Branko Stefanovic.<ref>http://armswatch.com/leaked-arms-dealers-passports-reveal-who-supplies-terrorists-in-yemen-serbia-files-part-3/</ref>

According to documents leaked by whistleblower Aleksandar Obradovic (a Krusik munitions plant employee), the minister’s father Branko Stefanovic was involved in the purchase of mortar shells at privileged prices from Krusik.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://rs.n1info.com/English/NEWS/a542277/Serbian-Anti-Corruption-Agency-tells-N1-it-is-investigating-Minister-Stefanovic.html|title=Serbian Anti-Corruption Agency tells N1 it is investigating Minister Stefanovic|website=N1 Srbija|language=sr-Latn|access-date=2019-12-21}}</ref>
