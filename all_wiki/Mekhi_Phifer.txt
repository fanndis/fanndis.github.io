Jude Law is 39. @MekhiPhifer is 37. Diego Luna is 32.|url=https://twitter.com/bettyb00p00/status/152533332037615616|publisher=Mekhi Phifer Verified Twitter|accessdate=30 June 2014|date=29 Dec 2011|quote=Mekhi Phifer retweeted this to his Verified Twitter Account}}</ref><ref name=tvg>{{cite web | url= http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/mekhi-phifer/bio/165693 |  title =Mekhi Phifer | publisher = [[TV Guide|TVGuide.com]] | accessdate= 2014-05-07 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140507214500/http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/mekhi-phifer/bio/165693 | archive-date = May 7, 2014 | url-status=live}}</ref> is an American actor. He portrayed Dr. [[Greg Pratt]] on [[NBC]]'s long-running medical drama ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'' and had a co-starring role opposite [[Eminem]] in the feature film ''[[8 Mile (film)|8 Mile]]''. He was a recurring cast member on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] crime show ''[[Lie to Me]]'' in the role of Ben Reynolds before season three, and also starred as CIA agent [[Rex Matheson]] in ''[[Torchwood: Miracle Day]]''.

==Early life==
Phifer was born in [[Harlem]], New York City and grew up in a single-parent household with his mother, Rhoda Phifer, a high-school teacher.<ref name="filmr">[http://www.filmreference.com/film/33/Mekhi-Phifer.html Mekhi Phifer Biography (1975–)<!-- Bot generated title -->] from filmreference.com</ref>

==Career==
In 1994, Phifer attended an open-casting call for director [[Spike Lee]]'s ''[[Clockers (film)|Clockers]]'', beating over a thousand others to get the lead role as a narcotics dealer embroiled in a murder cover-up.<ref name=tvg /> He followed that role with another in the comedy spoof feature ''[[High School High]]'' (which also starred his former wife [[Malinda Williams]]) and continued by co-starring in the horror film ''[[I Still Know What You Did Last Summer]]'', starring [[Jennifer Love Hewitt]] and [[Freddie Prinze Jr.]]. He also starred in the film “Paid in Full” in 2003. 

He portrayed Dr. [[Gregory Pratt]] on the medical drama ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'', which he started in April 2002. Phifer left the show in September 2008, in the first episode of [[ER (season 15)|Season 15]]. His character died during the conclusion of the [[ER (season 14)|season 14 finale]] cliffhanger involving an ambulance explosion that was rigged to kill an injured FBI informant ([[Steve Buscemi]]). During his six years in the show, he was nominated twice for an [[NAACP Image Award]].

[[File:US Navy 041211-N-8273J-197 Television and movie actor Mekhi Phifer poses for a photo on the flight deck aboard the aircraft carrier USS Nimitz (CVN 68) during a recent tour of the ship.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Phifer on the flight deck of {{USS|Nimitz|CVN-68|6}} in 2004]]

Phifer's television credits include the movies ''[[The Tuskegee Airmen]]'' (1995), [[Home Box Office|HBO's]] ''[[Subway Stories|Subway Stories: Tales from the Underground]]'' (1997), and ''[[Brian's Song (2001 film)]]'' as former [[Chicago Bears|Chicago Bear]] [[Gale Sayers]]. He received additional notice for his performance opposite singer [[Beyoncé Knowles]] (from [[Destiny's Child]]) on MTV's alternative take on the ''[[Carmen]]'' legend with the movie ''[[Carmen: A Hip Hopera]]'' (2001). Phifer had a recurring guest role in Seasons 5 and 6 of ''[[Homicide: Life on the Street]]'', portraying "Junior Bunk", the troubled nephew of Baltimore drug lord Luther Mahoney, and also guest-starred in ''[[New York Undercover]]''. Likewise, he earned an [[NAACP]] Image Award nomination for another TV movie, ''[[A Lesson Before Dying]]'', opposite [[Don Cheadle]]. In 2009, Phifer began a guest-starring arc on the [[Fox (network)|Fox]] drama ''[[Lie to Me]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.zap2it.com/tv/news/zap-mekhi-phifer-guests-on-lie-to-me,0,7607210.story |title='Lie to Me' recruits Mekhi Phifer |accessdate=May 6, 2009 |publisher=Zap2it }}</ref>

Among Phifer's other big-screen credits are ''[[Soul Food (film)|Soul Food]]'', ''The Biography of [[Spud Webb]]'', ''[[Hell's Kitchen (1998 film)|Hell's Kitchen, NYC]]'', ''Tears of a Clown'', ''[[O (film)|O]]'' (as the titular character Odin a.k.a. O), and the thriller ''[[Uninvited Guest]]'' (as Silk). He appeared in ''[[Impostor (2002 film)|Impostor]]'' as well as ''[[Paid in Full (2002 film)|Paid in Full]]'', an urban classic, that has given him notorious recognition as he portrayed Mitch and director [[Curtis Hanson]]'s ''[[8 Mile (film)|8 Mile]]'', opposite [[Eminem]]. He is mentioned in the [[Grammy]]- and [[Academy Award]]-winning song "[[Lose Yourself]]" by Eminem.

In June 2011, Phifer starred alongside [[John Barrowman]] and [[Eve Myles]] as CIA agent [[Rex Matheson]] in ''[[Torchwood: Miracle Day]]'', the fourth series of the [[BBC Worldwide|BBC]]/[[Starz Entertainment]] television show ''[[Torchwood]]''. In December of that year, he also starred on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in [[Lydia Diamond]]'s ''[[Stick Fly]]''.

Phifer starred as Agent Collins in the fourth season of ''[[White Collar (TV series)|White Collar]]''.

He made a guest appearance on the sitcom series ''[[Husbands (TV series)|Husbands]]'' in its second season.<ref name="Tubefilter">{{citation|url=http://www.tubefilter.com/2012/08/17/husbands-second-season/|title=Hollywood Stars Drop in on Male Newlyweds in 'Husbands'|publisher=Tubefilter.com|accessdate=August 18, 2012}}</ref>

In fall 2016, he joined the TV series ''[[Frequency (TV series)|Frequency]]'' as a series regular.

==Personal life==
Phifer has a son with his former wife, actress [[Malinda Williams]]. His second son was born to Oni Souratha in Los Angeles in 2007.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Saad |first1=Nardine |title=Report: Mekhi Phifer of 'ER,' 'Divergent' files for bankruptcy |url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/gossip/la-et-mg-mekhi-phifer-bankruptcy-20140507-story.html |accessdate=July 18, 2018 |work=Los Angeles Times |date=May 7, 2014}}</ref> On March 30, 2013, Phifer married his longtime girlfriend Reshelet Barnes in Beverly Hills, California.<ref>{{cite news |title=Former 'ER' actor Mekhi Phifer marries Reshelet Barnes |url=https://www.upi.com/Former-ER-actor-Mekhi-Phifer-marries-Reshelet-Barnes/43331364818822/ |accessdate=July 18, 2018 |agency=United Press International |date=April 1, 2013}}</ref>

==Other activities==
Phifer was the 2004 winner of the Celebrity Poker Showdown Championship, defeating [[Neil Patrick Harris]] during the fourth season. Phifer plays on the [[World Poker Tour]] in the Hollywood Home games for the Love Our Children USA charity. Phifer is chairman of the board of trustees of The Vine Group USA.<ref>[http://www.tvgusa.org/ The Vine Group, USA<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> a non-profit organization established in 2000 to provide educational resources to universities in Africa. He has signed up with [[Full Tilt Poker]]. He has his own well on the poker forum 2+2.

Phifer is an owner of a number of [[The Athlete's Foot|Athlete's Foot]] athletic shoe-store franchises in California.<ref name=tvg />

In January 2011, Phifer launched Third Reel Films.<ref>[http://thirdreel.com/about/ Thirdreel],</ref> The stated mission of the company is to provide an environment for filmmakers to showcase their work to entertainment representatives, acquisition executives, and other key-industry professionals.
