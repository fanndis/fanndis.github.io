'''Kelvin Glen Davis''' (born 2 March 1967) is a New Zealand politician and a member of the [[New Zealand House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] who has served as [[Deputy Leader of the New Zealand Labour Party|Deputy Leader]] of the [[New Zealand Labour Party|Labour Party]] since 1 August 2017.

A former teacher, Davis served as a [[list MP]] from [[2008 New Zealand general election|2008]] to 2011 and again in 2014. He won the electorate of [[Te Tai Tokerau]] in the [[2014 New Zealand general election|2014 election]]. Davis was elected as Labour Deputy Leader two months before the [[2017 New Zealand general election|2017 election]], becoming the first deputy of [[Māori people|Māori]] descent in the Labour Party. Currently, the third-ranked member of the [[Sixth Labour Government of New Zealand|Sixth Labour Government]], Davis serves as the [[Minister of Corrections (New Zealand)|Minister of Corrections]], [[Minister of Tourism (New Zealand)|Minister of Tourism]] and Minister for Māori Crown Relations, in addition to an Associate Minister of Education portfolio (Māori Education).

==Early life==
Born in [[Kawakawa, New Zealand|Kawakawa]] on 2 March 1967,<ref>{{cite book |last= Taylor |first= Alister |title= New Zealand Who’s Who Aotearoa |accessdate=  |edition=  |origyear= 1991 |year= 1998 |publisher= Alister Taylor |location= Wellington |isbn= 0-908578-24-5 |oclc= |page= |pages= }}</ref> and raised in the [[Bay of Islands]],<ref name="Labour profile">{{cite web |title=Kelvin Davis |url= http://www.labour.org.nz/kelvindavis |publisher=[[New Zealand Labour Party]] |accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> Davis affiliates to the [[Ngāpuhi]] [[iwi]].<ref name="Who is Kelvin Davis?"/> He received his secondary education at the [[Bay of Islands College]] in Kawakawa from 1980 to 1984.<ref name="Ngati Manu CV">{{cite web |title=Kelvin Davis |url= http://www.naumaiplace.com/site/ngati-manu/home/page/526/kelvin-davis-labour-list-mp2-2014/ |publisher=[[Ngāpuhi|Ngati Manu]] |accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref> He obtained a Diploma of Teaching from [[Auckland College of Education]] (1985–1987) and taught at Koru School in [[Mangere]] (1988–1990) and Bay of Islands Intermediate School in Kawakawa (1991–1993), before becoming principal of [[Karetu]] School near Kawakawa (1994–1998).<ref name="Ngati Manu CV" /> He then worked for the Education Advisory Service (1998–1999) and the education improvement and development project Te Putahitanga Matauranga (2000). He was principal of [[Kaitaia]] Intermediate School from 2001 to 2007.<ref name="Ngati Manu CV" />

In the [[2008 New Zealand general election|2008 general election]] Davis stood for Labour in the [[Te Tai Tokerau]] seat. He was defeated by the incumbent [[Hone Harawira]] of the [[Māori Party]], but was still elected into the [[49th New Zealand Parliament]] by way of the party list.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.parliament.nz/en/pb/research-papers/document/00PLLawrp08051/final-results-for-the-2008-new-zealand-general-election|title=Final Results for the 2008 New Zealand General Election - New Zealand Parliament|website=www.parliament.nz|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref>

He was the Labour Party's candidate in the [[2011 Te Tai Tokerau by-election]] and was again defeated by Harawira. He unsuccessfully contested the seat at the [[2011 New Zealand general election|general election later in the year]].<ref name="Stuff.co.nz_4982854">{{cite news |url= http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/4982854/Labour-contesting-Tai-Tokerau-by-election |title=Labour contesting Tai Tokerau by-election |author=Chapman, Kate |date=10 May 2011 |work=[[Stuff.co.nz]] |accessdate=12 October 2011}}</ref> After placing second to Harawira three times and losing his seat in the 2011 election, Davis announced his retirement from politics, which ended up being temporary.<ref name="Who is Kelvin Davis?">{{cite news|last1=Davison|first1=Isaac|title=Who is Labour's new deputy leader Kelvin Davis?|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11897749|accessdate=9 October 2017|work=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|date=1 August 2017}}</ref>

Davis was selected as Labour's candidate for Te Tai Tokerau in the {{NZ election link|2014}}.<ref name="Who is Kelvin Davis?" /> As a result of Labour's [[Shane Jones]]' resignation from Parliament at the end of May 2014, Davis was eligible to assume Jones' place as he had been the highest-ranking non-MP in [[Party lists in the 2011 New Zealand general election#Labour party|Labour's 2011 party list]].<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/9966660/Shane-Jones-to-quit-Labour |title=Shane Jones 'to quit Labour' |author=Vernon Small and Michael Fox |date=22 April 2014 |work=Stuff}}</ref> He entered the House of Representatives on 23 May 2014.<ref name="Who is Kelvin Davis?" />

The [[Mana Movement]] formed a [[coalition]] with the [[Internet Party (New Zealand)|Internet Party]] prior to the [[2014 New Zealand general election|2014 general election]]. The coalition was registered with the [[Electoral Commission (New Zealand)|Electoral Commission]] as the [[Internet Party and Mana Movement]] in July 2014, allowing it to contest the party vote.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/aug/23/-sp-new-zealand-election-john-key-alleged-dirty-politics|title=The whale that swallowed New Zealand's election campaign|last=Manhire|first=Toby|date=2014-08-23|work=The Guardian|access-date=2020-01-31|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> This strategic coalition resulted in Davis getting endorsements from [[Winston Peters]] of [[New Zealand First]]<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bennett |first1=Adam |title=Election 2014: Winston Peters hits out at National after big poll surge |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=11328639 |accessdate=3 October 2014 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |date=21 September 2014}}</ref> and the Prime Minister, [[John Key]] of the [[New Zealand National Party|National Party]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newstalkzb.co.nz/news/politics/keys-subtle-endorsement-for-kelvin-davis/|title=Key's subtle endorsement for Kelvin Davis|last=McQuillan|first=Laura|website=ZB|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> Even the electorate's candidate for the [[Māori Party]], Te Hira Paenga, reminded voters of the importance of strategic voting.<ref>{{cite news |title=Davis picking up endorsements |url= http://www.waateanews.com/waateanews/x_story_id/NzkxNg==/Davis-picking-up-endorsements |accessdate=3 October 2014 |work=Radio Waatea |date=19 September 2014}}</ref> In this election, Davis ousted Harawira, which ended the representation of the Mana Party in Parliament.<ref name="Ousted Harawira">{{cite news |last1=Smith |first1=Simon |title=Davis' win a critical blow for Harawira, Internet Mana |url= http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/10524510/Davis-win-a-critical-blow-for-Harawira-Internet-Mana |accessdate=30 September 2014 |work=[[Stuff.co.nz]] |date=20 September 2014}}</ref>

Following his election, Davis became Labour's corrections spokesperson.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.labour.org.nz/kelvindavis|title=Hon Kelvin Davis|website=New Zealand Labour Party|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> In 2015, Davis criticised private prison provider [[Serco]]'s management of inmates, alleging 'corruption' at the [[Mount Eden Prisons|Mount Eden remand facility]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.parliament.nz/en/pb/hansard-debates/rhr/document/51HansD_20151209_00000016/urgent-debates-serco-decision-to-end-contract-for-mt|title=Urgent Debates — Serco—Decision to End Contract for Mt Eden Corrections Facility - New Zealand Parliament|website=www.parliament.nz|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> Following an inquiry, Serco lost its contract to run the facility and Minister of Corrections [[Sam Lotu-Iiga]] was relieved of his post.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Moir|first1=Jo|title=Labour's Kelvin Davis says Mt Eden prison guard revelations are 'corruption'|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/70619419/labours-kelvin-davis-says-mt-eden-prison-guard-revelations-are-corruption|accessdate=9 October 2017|agency=[[Stuff.co.nz]]|date=28 July 2016}}</ref><ref name="Who is Kelvin Davis?" /> Davis also criticized the Australian government for its incarceration of [[New Zealand Australians|New Zealand expatriates]] facing deportation.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2015/oct/17/new-zealand-mp-says-deportations-make-a-mockery-of-anzac-relationship|title=New Zealand MP says deportations make a mockery of Anzac relationship|last=Medhora|first=Shalailah|date=2015-10-16|work=The Guardian|access-date=2020-01-31|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Davis has also drawn attention to the disproportionately high number of Māori in the New Zealand prison system; with Māori comprising 50.9% of the prison population despite making up just 15% of New Zealand's population.<ref>{{cite news|last1=White|first1=Di|title=Kelvin Davis is NZ’s best hope for prison reform in decades|url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/politics/08-08-2017/kelvin-davis-is-nzs-best-hope-for-prison-reform-in-decades/|accessdate=9 October 2017|publisher=The Spinoff|date=8 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/nov/30/the-man-on-a-mission-to-get-new-zealands-maori-out-of-prison|title=The man on a mission to get New Zealand's Māori out of prison|last=Ainge Roy|first=Eleanor|date=2018-11-29|work=The Guardian|access-date=2020-01-31|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

===Deputy Leader===
On 1 August 2017, Davis was appointed as the new [[Deputy Leader of the New Zealand Labour Party|Deputy Leader of the Labour Party]], serving under [[Jacinda Ardern]].<ref name="Who is Kelvin Davis?" /> On 1 September 2017, Ardern corrected Davis after he publicly stated that Labour would campaign on a capital gains tax policy during the 2020 general election rather than implementing it mid-term.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kirk|first1=Stacey|title=Jacinda Ardern tells Kelvin Davis off over capital gains tax comments|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/96385172|accessdate=9 October 2017|agency=[[Stuff.co.nz]]|date=1 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Kelvin Davis says he's clearer on the party's policies now|url=http://www.radionz.co.nz/national/programmes/checkpoint/audio/201857035/kelvin-davis-says-he-s-clearer-on-the-party-s-policies-now|accessdate=9 October 2017|publisher=[[Radio New Zealand]]|date=1 September 2017}}</ref> On 19 September, Davis indicated that he was willing to sacrifice his position as Deputy Prime Minister in order for Labour to form a coalition government with either [[New Zealand First]] or the [[Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand|Green Party]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Kelvin Davis will sacrifice top job|url=https://www.maoritelevision.com/news/politics/kelvin-davis-will-sacrifice-top-job|accessdate=9 October 2017|publisher=[[Māori Television]]|date=19 September 2017}}</ref> During the [[2017 New Zealand general election|2017 election]] on 23 September, Davis was re-elected in Te Tai Tokerau and defeated Mana Movement leader Hone Harawira by 4,807 votes.<ref>{{cite web|title=Te Tai Tokerau - Official Result|url=http://www.electionresults.govt.nz/electionresults_2017/electorate-details-69.html|publisher=Election Commission|accessdate=9 October 2017}}</ref>

Following the 2017 election, Davis was appointed Minister for Māori Crown Relations ({{lang|mi|Te Arawhiti}}), [[Minister of Corrections (New Zealand)|Minister of Corrections]], [[Minister of Tourism (New Zealand)|Minister of Tourism]], and Associate Minister of Education in the [[Sixth Labour Government of New Zealand|Sixth Labour Government]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ministerial List|url=https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/our-business-units/cabinet-office/ministers-and-their-portfolios/ministerial-list|publisher=[[Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (New Zealand)|Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet]]|accessdate=26 October 2017}}</ref> As Minister of Corrections, Davis has stressed the need to address the high rates of incarceration of Māori people. In August 2018, he called for a change to "a level of imprisonment that is simply devastating our Māori [[whānau]] and communities".<ref>{{cite news |title=Minister of Corrections Kelvin Davis issues heartfelt call to change the level of Māori imprisonment |url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=12110833 |accessdate=16 May 2020 |work=New Zealand  Herald |date=21 August 2018 |language=en-NZ}}</ref>

During the [[2020 New Zealand general election]] held on 17 October, Davis was re-elected in [[Te Tai Tokerau]] by a final margin of 8,164 votes, defeating the [[Māori Party]]'s candidate [[Mariameno Kapa-Kingi]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Te Tai Tokerau - Official Result |url=https://www.electionresults.govt.nz/electionresults_2020/electorate-details-70.html |publisher=[[Electoral Commission (New Zealand)|Electoral Commission]] |accessdate=13 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>  On the night of Labour's election victory, Davis attracted online criticism after he delivered a victory speech mocking his National Party opponent [[Judith Collins]], with critics describing it as "despicable," "ungracious," and "disgusting."<ref>{{cite news |title=Election 2020: Is Kelvin Davis' election night speech the world's worst political speech? |url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/election-2020-is-kelvin-davis-election-night-speech-the-worlds-worst-political-speech/BBIG3TIAVORROD2LTQ3L4NGH44/ |accessdate=22 October 2020 |work=[[New Zealand Herald]] |date=18 October 2020 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20201022114944/https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/election-2020-is-kelvin-davis-election-night-speech-the-worlds-worst-political-speech/BBIG3TIAVORROD2LTQ3L4NGH44/ |archivedate=22 October 2020}}</ref>

On 2 November 2020, Davis was allocated third place in Prime Minister Ardern's Cabinet, retaining his portfolios as Minister for Māori Crown Relations and Minister of Corrections while picking up the portfolios of [[Ministry for Children|Minister for Children]] and Association Minister of Education with responsibility for Māori Education.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ministerial List for Announcement on Monday |url=https://dpmc.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2020-11/ministerial-list-2-nov-2020.pdf |publisher=[[Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (New Zealand)|Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet]] |accessdate=4 November 2020 |date=2 November 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201103122141/https://dpmc.govt.nz/sites/default/files/2020-11/ministerial-list-2-nov-2020.pdf|archive-date=3 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> That same day, Davis indicated that he would not be seeking the role of [[Deputy Prime Minister of New Zealand|Deputy Prime Minister]], a position usually held by the deputy leader of the senior coalition party or the leader of a coalition partner. Winston Peters, the leader of New Zealand First, had served as Deputy Prime Minister during the first term of the Sixth Labour Government but had lost his seat during the 2020 general election.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Whyte |first1=Anna |title=Kelvin Davis says he won't seek Deputy Prime Minister role |url=https://www.tvnz.co.nz/one-news/new-zealand/kelvin-davis-says-he-wont-seek-deputy-prime-minister-role |access-date=2 December 2020 |work=[[1 News]] |date=2 November 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201106145322/https://www.tvnz.co.nz/one-news/new-zealand/kelvin-davis-says-he-wont-seek-deputy-prime-minister-role |archive-date=2 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Cooke |first1=Henry |title=Kelvin Davis won't seek the role of Deputy Prime Minister, but wants to stay on deputy Labour leader |url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/300147451/kelvin-davis-wont-seek-the-role-of-deputy-prime-minister-but-wants-to-stay-on-deputy-labour-leader |access-date=2 December 2020 |work=[[Stuff (website)|Stuff]] |date=2 November 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201121235610/https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/politics/300147451/kelvin-davis-wont-seek-the-role-of-deputy-prime-minister-but-wants-to-stay-on-deputy-labour-leader |archive-date=21 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>
