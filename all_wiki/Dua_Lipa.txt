'''Dua Lipa''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|d|uː|ə|_|ˈ|l|iː|p|ə}}; {{IPA-sq|ˈdua ˈlipa}}; born 22 August 1995)<ref name=allmusicbio>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/dua-lipa-mn0003426731/biography|title=Dua Lipa Biography|first=Neil Z.|last=Yeung|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> is an English<!-- Do NOT add Albanian as ethnicity does not belong in the lead section per [[MOS:BLPLEAD]] and nor is there a reliable source that confirms she also has Albanian citizenship. --> singer and songwriter. After working as a model, she signed with [[Warner Music Group]] in 2015 and released her [[Dua Lipa (album)|self-titled debut album]] in 2017. The album peaked at number three on the [[UK Albums Chart]], and produced nine singles, including "[[Be the One (Dua Lipa song)|Be the One]]" and "[[IDGAF (Dua Lipa song)|IDGAF]]", and the UK number-one single "[[New Rules]]", which also peaked at number six in the US. In 2018, Lipa won the [[Brit Awards]] for [[Brit Award for British Female Solo Artist|British Female Solo Artist]] and [[Brit Award for British Breakthrough Act|British Breakthrough Act]].

Released in April 2018, the single "[[One Kiss (song)|One Kiss]]" with [[Calvin Harris]] peaked at number one in the UK and became the [[List of UK Singles Chart number ones of the 2010s|longest-running number-one single for a female artist]] in 2018. It won her the 2019 [[Brit Award for Song of the Year]]. In 2019, she was also awarded the [[Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], and "[[Electricity (Silk City and Dua Lipa song)|Electricity]]", a collaboration with [[Silk City (duo)|Silk City]], won her the Grammy Award for [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording|Best Dance Recording]]. The success of the singles helped her self-titled album become one of the [[List of most-streamed songs on Spotify#Most-streamed albums|most-streamed albums]] on [[Spotify]] and achieve platinum certifications. Her second studio album, ''[[Future Nostalgia]]'', was released in March 2020 to critical acclaim, earning Lipa six Grammy nominations, including in the category [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year | Album of the Year]]. It became her first UK number-one album, spending four weeks at number one; its lead single "[[Don't Start Now]]" became one of the most commercially successful songs of 2020; it is one of four songs, and the only song by a female artist, that has spent all of 2020 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. It peaked at number two on both the [[UK Singles Chart]] and the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100. 

Lipa has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Dua Lipa|various accolades]], including two [[Grammy Award]]s, three [[Brit Awards]], two [[MTV Europe Music Award]]s, one [[MTV Video Music Award]], and an [[American Music Award]].

On 21 September 2018, she retweeted commentary that highlighted quotes by [[Jair Bolsonaro]] in which he stated that "he would rather his son die than be homosexual" and told a congresswoman "he would not rape her because she was ugly"; in the retweet Lipa simply added the hashtag [[Ele Não movement#The hashtag|#EleNão]], a social movement that went against Bolsonaro and his [[Jair Bolsonaro presidential campaign|2018 presidential campaign]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Daw|first=Stephen|date=22 September 2018|title=Dua Lipa, Imagine Dragons & More Speak Out Against Anti-LGBTQ Brazilian Presidential Candidate Jair Bolsonaro|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8476435/dua-lipa-imagine-dragons-diplo-anti-bolsonaro-campaign-ele-nao|access-date=28 September 2020|website=Billboard}}</ref> On 27 September 2018, Brazilian model Liziane Gutiérrez said on [[Instagram]] that Lipa had expelled her from the VIP area of her concert in [[Las Vegas]] for wearing a T-shirt with the hashtag #EleSim that symbolized support for Bolsonaro.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=12 October 2018|title=Dua Lipa expulsó del VIP de su concierto a modelo brasileña que apoya a Bolsonaro|url=https://www.cnnchile.com/cultura/dua-lipa-expulso-del-vip-de-su-concierto-a-modelo-brasilena-que-apoya-a-bolsonaro_20181012/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=28 September 2020|website=CNN Chile|language=Spanish}}</ref>

In November 2018, Lipa expressed dissatisfaction with the [[Withdrawal from the European Union|withdrawal]] of [[United Kingdom]] of the [[European Union]] due to [[Brexit]], saying "she understands the importance of belonging to said political community because she is from [[Kosovo]]".<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=15 November 2018|title=Dua Lipa talks new music, refugees and Brexit|url=https://edition.cnn.com/videos/world/2018/11/15/ctw-dua-lipa-new-album-intv.cnn|access-date=27 September 2020|website=CNN}}</ref>

On 2 December 2019, Lipa endorsed the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] in the [[2019 United Kingdom general election|2019 UK general election]], sharing the policies of both Labour and [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservatives]] on various issues in an Instagram post and writing that it was "the most important election in a generation".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Skinner|first=Tom|date=3 December 2019|title=Dua Lipa calls on fanbase to support Labour in general election: "Your vote counts"|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/dua-lipa-calls-on-fanbase-to-support-labour-in-general-election-2583650|access-date=27 September 2020|website=NME}}</ref> On 3 March 2020, she asked US voters to pick [[Bernie Sanders]] for [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] nominee for [[president of the United States]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Diavolo|first=Lucy|date=3 March 2020|title=Dua Lipa Asked Super Tuesday Voters to Pick Bernie Sanders as Their Democratic Nominee|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/dua-lipa-super-tuesday-vote-for-bernie-sanders|access-date=27 September 2020|website=Teen Vogue}}</ref>

In June 2020, Lipa shared, and later deleted, a post depicting Israeli security forces detaining Palestinian minors,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Frot|first=Mathilde|date=2 June 2020|title=Dua Lipa shares anti-Israel post on Instagram|url=https://www.jpost.com/israel-news/culture/pop-star-dua-lipa-shares-anti-israel-post-on-instagram-629955|access-date=20 July 2020|website=[[The Jerusalem Post]]}}</ref> which said "fake" [[Jewish people]] in the Israeli government and "fake [[Christianity|Christians]]" in the United States had created [[Hamas]] for people to "believe that Hamas is the reasons for the decades worth of [[Military occupation|occupation]], [[oppression]], [[ethnic cleansing]], and murder."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Frot|first=Mathilde|date=1 June 2020|title=Dua Lipa criticised over Israel Instagram post referencing 'fake' Jews|url=https://jewishnews.timesofisrael.com/dua-lipa-criticised-over-israel-instagram-post-referencing-fake-jews/|access-date=4 July 2020|website=[[The Times of Israel]]}}</ref>

On 18 July 2020, Lipa shared a post on her [[Instagram]] story saying "[[Kosovo–Serbia relations|Why Kosovo is not & will never be, Serbia]]", asking followers to sign a petition which asked [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] to include [[Kosovo]] in their mapping services.<ref name=BBC_2020>{{Cite web|last=Maričić|first=Slobodan|date=19 July 2020|title=Dua Lipa, Kosovo i Srbija: O Eplu, granicama i tome ko je prvi došao|url=https://www.bbc.com/serbian/lat/balkan-53473793|access-date=20 July 2020|website=[[BBC]]|language=sr}}</ref> The next day, on 19 July 2020, Lipa posted an image of a banner commonly associated with supporters of extreme [[Albanian nationalism]] on her social media accounts,<ref name="BBC">{{Cite web|date=20 July 2020|title=Dua Lipa sparks controversy with 'Greater Albania' map tweet|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-53483451|access-date=20 July 2020|website=[[BBC]]}}</ref> depicting an [[Irredentism|irredentist]] map of [[Greater Albania]], with the caption "[[Indigenous peoples|autochthonous]]" alluding to the [[Illyrians|Illyrian]] theory of the [[origin of the Albanians]]. In response, [[Twitter]] users, many of them [[Macedonians (ethnic group)|Macedonian]], [[Greeks|Greek]], [[Montenegrins|Montenegrin]] and [[Serbs|Serbian]], accused the singer of [[Ethnic nationalism|ethno-nationalism]].<ref name=PinkNews>{{Cite web|last=Milton|first=Josh|date=20 July 2020|title=Absolutely nobody had 'Dua Lipa comes out as an Albanian nationalist' on their 2020 bingo cards, so everyone is confused|url=https://www.pinknews.co.uk/2020/07/20/dua-lipa-albanian-greater-albania-nationalism-flag-tweet/|access-date=20 July 2020|website=[[PinkNews]]}}</ref><ref name=Axios>{{Cite web|last=Savitsky|first=Shane|date=20 July 2020|title=Dua Lipa courts controversy with tweet backing Albanian nationalism|url=https://www.axios.com/dua-lipa-albanian-nationalism-kosovo-tweet-f2dbf0d3-f044-4f52-a147-cabd39df632c.html|access-date=20 July 2020|website=[[Axios (website)|Axios]]}}</ref> In response, Lipa tweeted that her post "was not intended to incite hate" and said it had been "misinterpreted".<ref>{{Cite news|date=21 July 2020|title=Dua Lipa courts controversy with nationalist tweet|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-53483451|access-date=22 July 2020}}</ref>

In October 2020, Lipa expressed support for Democratic candidate [[Joe Biden]] to [[2020 United States presidential election|become US president]] and she partook in an online event hosted by the [[Albanian Americans|Albanian American]] community to fundraise for his campaign.<ref name="Mejdini">{{cite news|last=Mejdini|first=Fatjona|title=Biden Counts on History to Help Win Albanian American Vote|url=https://balkaninsight.com/2020/10/30/biden-counts-on-history-to-help-win-albanian-american-vote/|agency=Balkan Insight|date=30 October 2020|accessdate=30 October 2020|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

=== Controversies  ===
In January 2018, she faced controversy when a cover of the song "[[Smoke, Drink, Break-Up]]" she recorded in 2014 resurfaced on [[YouTube]] where she can be heard saying the [[Nigger|N-word]] with distorted audio: "So I roll me swisha, pour me some liquor before we start to bicker and calling each other out our names like bitches and nig—", with a distorted sound effect causing the word to end halfway. On 4 January, she tweeted that she "never meant to offend or upset anyone" and "shouldn't have gone there at all", and that a different word should have been used.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rice|first=Nicholas|date=5 January 2018|title=Dua Lipa Apologizes For Using Racial Slur in 2014 Cover Song|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8092701/dua-lipa-apologizes-for-using-racial-slur-2014-cover|access-date=28 July 2020|website=Billboard}}</ref>
