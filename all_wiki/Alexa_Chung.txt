'''Alexa Chung''' (born 5 November 1983) is a [[British writer]], [[television presenter]], [[Fashion model|model]], and [[fashion designer]]. She has authored the book ''It'' (2013)<ref>{{cite web|title=It by Alexa Chung – review|url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/sep/09/it-alexa-chung-book-review|website=The Guardian}}</ref> and launched her [[eponymous]] fashion brand in May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.alexachung.com/uk/about/|title=About Alexa Chung|website=Alexa Chung}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Designing It Girl Alexa Chung Finally Has a Line to Call Her Own|url=http://www.vogue.com/article/alexa-chung-collection-launch-interview|website=Vogue}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Alexa Chung Hosts a London Launch Party for Her New Line|url=http://www.vogue.com/article/alexa-chung-daisy-lowe-pixie-geldof-london-launch-party|website=Vogue}}</ref>

==Early life==
Chung was born in [[Privett]], [[Hampshire]], the daughter of Gillian and Phillip Chung. Her father is of mixed [[Chinese people|Chinese]]<ref name=":0" /> and English descent, while her mother is of English descent.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2010/oct/03/alexa-chung-mtv-interview|title=Alexa Chung: 'I'm probably not the best role model'|first=Elizabeth|last=Day|publisher=The Guardian|date=2 October 2010|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref><ref name="lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk" /> Growing up she had a pony and took dancing classes.<ref name="lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk" /> Chung attended secondary state school at the local [[Perins School]] and spent [[sixth form]] at [[Peter Symonds College]], Winchester. She was accepted at [[King's College London]] to study English and [[Chelsea College of Arts]] to study art, but at the age of 16 was scouted by a [[modelling agency]] before attending.<ref name="lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk">{{cite news |last=Barton |first=Laura |url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2008/mar/31/television |title=She's the one |publisher=The Guardian |date=30 March 2008 |accessdate=18 July 2014 |location=London |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20131208235850/http://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2008/mar/31/television |archivedate= 8 December 2013 |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==<!-- Please don't alter this spelling; she's British, so we use British English spelling per [[WP:ENGVAR]] -->

=== Modelling ===
[[File:Alexa Chung 2015 (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|upright|In 2015]]
Chung was scouted after being seen in the comedy tent at the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading Festival]] at the age of 16.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenational.ae/lifestyle/fashion/slavic-models-crowd-the-runways |title=Slavic models crowd the runways |publisher=The National |date=19 July 2009 |accessdate=17 March 2011}}</ref> She modelled for [[teen magazine]]s, such as ''[[Elle Girl]]'' and ''[[CosmoGIRL!]]'', and worked for companies including [[Fanta]], [[Sony Ericsson]], [[Sunsilk]], [[Urban Outfitters]], and [[Tampax]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fashionmodeldirectory.com/models/Alexa_Chung/ |title=Fashion Model Online Directory |publisher=Fashionmodeldirectory.com |accessdate=24 November 2008| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20081202060522/http://www.fashionmodeldirectory.com/models/Alexa_Chung/| archivedate= 2 December 2008 | url-status= live}}</ref> She appeared in [[music video]]s for artists including [[the Streets]], [[Westlife]], [[Delta Goodrem]], [[Reuben (band)|Reuben]] and [[Holly Valance]] and starred as Jake in a [[Teleplay|scripted]] [[Reality television|reality show]] called ''Shoot Me'', broadcast on [[Fashion&nbsp;TV]] in 2005. After four years, Chung quit modelling with the intention of beginning an art [[Foundation (non-profit)|foundation]] or [[fashion journalism]] course, having become disillusioned. She had developed a "distorted [[body image]]" and "low [[self-esteem]]" through modelling.<ref name="timesonline1">{{cite news |last=Spicer |first=Kate |url=http://women.timesonline.co.uk/tol/life_and_style/women/celebrity/article3112005.ece |title=The Times's "Small Screen Goddess" interview |publisher=Women.timesonline.co.uk |date= 6 January 2008 |accessdate=24 November 2008 |location=London}}</ref>

After she became a TV&nbsp;personality, Chung made a return to occasional modelling. In 2008, she was the face of [[Australia]]n fashion label Antipodium's SS&nbsp;2008 collection and [[Oxfam]]'s ethical fashion range, Revamped. In September 2008, Chung walked the catwalk as part of the Vivienne Westwood ''Red Label'' Spring/Summer 2009 show at [[London Fashion Week]]. In early 2009, she became the face of British [[High Street]] giant [[New Look (clothing retailer)|New Look]]. Chung then joined London-based modelling agency [[Select Model Management]] and, in April 2009, she modeled Wren's Holiday 2009 collection, alongside her friend [[Tennessee Thomas]]. In July 2009, Chung moved to [[NEXT Model Management]] and became the face of [[DKNY]] Jeans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.graziadaily.co.uk/fashion/archive/2009/07/30/alexa-chung-scores-dkny-jeans-campaign.htm |title=Grazia Fashion: Alexa Chung scores DKNY Jeans campaign |publisher=Graziadaily.co.uk |date= |accessdate=17 March 2011}}</ref> Chung was the face of [[South Korea]]n brand MOGG's Spring/Summer 2010 campaign. In January 2010, she became the main model of the Spring/Summer 2010 [[Pepe Jeans]] London campaign and reprised her role for the Fall/Winter 2010 campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.graziadaily.co.uk/fashion/archive/2010/01/04/alexa-chung-for-pepe-jeans.htm |title=Grazia Fashion: Alexa Chung for Pepe Jeans |publisher=Graziadaily.co.uk |date= |accessdate=17 March 2011}}</ref> She was announced as the first celebrity face of [[Lacoste]] in June 2010, appearing in both the TV and print advertising campaigns for their "Joy of Pink" fragrance.<ref name="fashion.telegraph.co.uk">[http://fashion.telegraph.co.uk/columns/hilary-alexander/TMG7833164/Alexa-Chung-is-the-face-of-Lacoste.html "Alexa Chung is the face of Lacoste"], ''The Telegraph'', 16 June 2010, accessed 15 January 2011.</ref> In January 2011, Chung was announced as the face of Italian sneaker brand [[Superga (brand)|Superga]], in a campaign to mark their 100th anniversary.<ref name="vogue.co.uk">{{cite web|first=Ella |last=Alexander |url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/news/daily/110121-alexa-chung-for-superga.aspx |title=Alexa Chung for Superga |publisher=Vogue.co.uk |date=21 January 2011 |accessdate=17 March 2011}}</ref> In February 2012, she took part in the [[London Fashion Week]] show for designer [[Stella McCartney]], where she acted as a guest assistant to magician [[Hans Klok]] in a number of illusions including being levitated and [[Sawing a woman in half|sawed in half]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2012/feb/19/stella-mccartney-london-fashion-week |title=Stella McCartney's party will go down in fashion history |publisher=The Guardian |date= 19 February 2012|accessdate=28 March 2012 |location=London |first=Jess |last=Cartner-Morley}}</ref><ref>''LIVE with Gabby'', [[Channel 5 (UK)|Channel&nbsp;5]], 22 March 2012.</ref>

Chung worked with [[Tommy Hilfiger Corporation|Tommy Hilfiger]] as a guest editor for their Fall 2014 collection, and modeled their clothes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wwd.com/globe-news/fashion-memopad/guest-editor-7970070/|title=Tommy Hilfiger group taps Alexa Chung|first=David|last=Yi|publisher=WWD|date=7 October 2014|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref> Chung became the face of [[Longchamp (company)|Longchamp]] in 2013 and by 2015 she had appeared in her fourth campaign for the fashion brand.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/news/2013/11/26/alexa-chung-for-longchamp-campaign-revealed|title=Join Alexa Chung on her Longchamp campaign|first=Ella|last=Alexander|publisher=Vogue|date=26 November 2013|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://wwd.com/eye/parties/alexa-chung-longchamp-campaign-new-york-dinner-10165621/|title=Alexa Chung hosts another Longchamp campaign in New York|first=Maxine|last=Wally|publisher=WWD|date=24 June 2015|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref>

=== Style ===
Chung is a muse to many fashion designers because of her distinctive personal style.<ref>{{cite web|title=The 10 best dressed|url=http://www.matchesfashion.com/womens/the-style-report/top-10-dressed|publisher=Matches Fashion|access-date=31 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130609075214/http://www.matchesfashion.com/womens/the-style-report/top-10-dressed|archive-date=9 June 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> She frequently appears on best-dressed lists, is a regular model for ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'', ''[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]'' and ''[[Harper's Bazaar]]'' and is often seen in the front row at fashion shows.

In January 2010, the designer handbag company [[Mulberry (company)|Mulberry]] debuted the "Alexa," a much sought-after bag named after and inspired by her. According to ''[[The Guardian]]'', 380 "Alexa" bags were sold in one week alone in November 2010, and the company cited the bag's popularity for increased sales at its UK and international stores that year.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2010-11-21|title=Money bags: the story behind Mulberry's Alexa|url=http://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2010/nov/21/alexa-handbag-fashion-mulberry|access-date=2020-08-20|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref>

In February 2010, Chung collaborated with [[J.Crew]]'s Madewell on a womenswear line which was unveiled during New York Fashion Week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elle.com/Runway/Ready-to-Wear/Fall-2010-RTW/ALEXA-CHUNG-FOR-MADEWELL/ALEXA-CHUNG-FOR-MADEWELL |title=Alexa Chung for Madewell 2010 Fall |publisher=Elle |date=17 February 2011 |accessdate=17 March 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110710183358/http://www.elle.com/Runway/Ready-to-Wear/Fall-2010-RTW/ALEXA-CHUNG-FOR-MADEWELL/ALEXA-CHUNG-FOR-MADEWELL |archivedate=10 July 2011 }}</ref> She collaborated with Madewell for a second collection, released in September 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dunne|first=Eleanor|date=2011-08-22|title=Sneak Peak at Alexa Chung for Madewell's Fall 2011 Ad Campaign|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/fashion/fashion-designers/alexa-chung-madewell-campaign-082211|access-date=2020-08-20|website=Harper's BAZAAR|language=en-US}}</ref>

In December 2010, [[Bryan Ferry]], on behalf of the [[British Fashion Council]], presented Chung with the British Style Award which "recognises an individual who embodies the spirit of British fashion and is an international ambassador for the UK as a leading creative hub for fashion" at a ceremony at the [[Savoy Theatre]] in London.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.britishfashionawards.com/content.aspx?CategoryID=781 |title=British Style – British Style 2010 |publisher=British Fashion Awards |accessdate=17 March 2011 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110428142532/http://www.britishfashionawards.com/content.aspx?CategoryID=781 |archivedate=28 April 2011 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> At the British Fashion Awards of 2011, 2012 and 2013, Chung won the British Style Award, for which the public voted.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://fashion.telegraph.co.uk/Article/TMG8921991/705/British-Fashion-Awards-2011-Alexa-Chung-beats-Kate-Middleton-to-style-icon-crown.html | location=London | work=The Daily Telegraph | first=Belinda | last=White | title=Mad Men Style| date=29 November 2011}}</ref> In 2015, Chung collaborated with [[Adriano Goldschmied|AG Jeans]] on two collections.<ref name="observer.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vogue.com/8513153/alexa-chung-ag-jeans-collaboration/|title=Alexa Chung designs a collection for AG jeans|first=Liana|last=Satenstein|publisher=Vogue|date=20 January 2015|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref>

=== Alexachung ===
Chung launched her own fashion label, Alexachung, in May 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Alexa Chung on her new collection: ‘I deliberately went quite weird’ |url=https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2017/jun/03/alexa-chung-new-fashion-collection-deliberately-weird |website=The Guardian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Alexa Chung: The Vogue Interview |url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/article/alexa-chung-interview-vogue-2017 |website=Vogue}}</ref> On its official website, the brand says its vision is "to create clothes that people want to wear. Combining Parisian chic with a New York edge, as well as the very British sense of humour, it is inspired by everything from Jane Birkin’s insouciance to David Hockney’s stripy sweater."<ref>{{Cite web|title=About - Shop Womens About Online - ALEXACHUNG|url=https://www.alexachung.com/row/about|access-date=2020-08-20|website=www.alexachung.com}}</ref>

===TV presenting===
In April 2006, the then-model Chung was offered the job of co‑host on ''[[Popworld]]'' on [[Channel&nbsp;4]], a music show known for its irreverent and awkward style of interviews. Chung and co‑host [[Alex Zane]] also presented a weekly radio show called ''Popworld Radio''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channel4radio.com/show/index.php?Id=410&Referer=&Order=Name&PageLength=5&CategoryId=&ProductCategoryId=57&TypeId=&ProductId=&Desc=&CurrentPage=2 |title=Popworld radio – listen online |publisher=Channel4radio.com |date= |accessdate=24 November 2008 |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20081207004244/http://www.channel4radio.com/show/index.php?Id=410&Referer=&Order=Name&PageLength=5&CategoryId=&ProductCategoryId=57&TypeId=&ProductId=&Desc=&CurrentPage=2| archivedate= 7 December 2008}}</ref> Chung then moved to Channel 4, where she guest presented ''Big Brother's Big Mouth'' and appeared as a panellist on [[quiz show]] ''[[8 out of 10 Cats]]''. She hosted a number of ''T4 Movie Specials'', ''4Music Specials'', ''T4 Holiday Mornings'' along with T4's coverage of many [[music festival]]s.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.independenttalent.com/presenters/alexa-chung |title=Independent – Alexa's talent agency |publisher=Independenttalent.com |accessdate=24 November 2008 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081229121619/http://www.independenttalent.com/presenters/alexa-chung |archivedate=29 December 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In January 2008, Chung became one of the four anchor [[T4 (Channel 4)|T4]] presenters. She hosted T4's ''[[Vanity Lair]]'', a reality show investigating the concept of "beauty". When the show was mentioned in a later interview, "Chung raise[d] her eyebrows, indicating embarrassment".<ref name="Wiseman">{{cite news|last=Wiseman |first=Eva |url=http://lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk/fashion/story/0,,2288542,00.html |title=The Observer – Who's Cool Now |publisher=Lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk |date= 6 July 2008|accessdate=24 November 2008 | location=London}}</ref> As well as her work on T4, she became a long-time host of Channel&nbsp;4's early morning music programme ''[[Freshly Squeezed]]'' beginning in September 2007.<ref name="lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk" /> In addition to her Channel&nbsp;4 commitments, Chung presented the [[ITV1]] series ''[[Get A Grip (TV series)|Get a Grip]]'' (2007) and [[BBC Three]]'s ''[[The Wall (2008 TV series)|The Wall]]'' (2008). In mid-2008, Chung began to host more fashion-oriented programming. She was the "roving reporter" on Channel&nbsp;4's "[[Gok's Fashion Fix]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channel4.com/life/microsites/G/gok_fashion/biogs/gok.html |title=Gok's Fashion Fix – official website |publisher=Channel4.com |accessdate=24 November 2008| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20081118061822/http://www.channel4.com/life/microsites/G/gok_fashion/biogs/gok.html| archivedate= 18 November 2008 | url-status= live}}</ref> On the programme, Chung roadtested the latest fashion trends with members of the public, and interviewed fashion designers such as [[Roberto Cavalli]], [[Karl Lagerfeld]], [[Jean Paul Gaultier]], [[Margherita Missoni]] and [[Christian Lacroix]]. She described it as "pretty much my dream job".<ref name="Wiseman" /> Chung also hosted a [[T4 (Channel 4)|T4]] fashion and music show called ''[[Frock Me (tv series)|Frock Me]]'', with fashion designer [[Henry Holland (fashion designer)|Henry Holland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.channel4.com/entertainment/t4/microsites/F/frockme/index.html |title=Frock Me's official site |publisher=Channel4.com |accessdate=24 November 2008 |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20081220010118/http://www.channel4.com/entertainment/t4/microsites/F/frockme/index.html| archivedate= 20 December 2008 |url-status= live}}</ref> She hosted the ''Diesel U Music Awards'' and the ''Elle'' Style Awards.<ref name="lifeandhealth.guardian.co.uk" /> In 2009, Chung was awarded the ''Elle'' Style Award for "Best TV Presenter" and ''[[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]]''{{’}}s award for Best TV Presenter in 2009. Also that year, she received Glamour's at the magazine's annual award ceremony.

Chung left Channel&nbsp;4 and the United Kingdom in April 2009 to pursue a career in the US. She presented ''[[It's On with Alexa Chung]]'' for [[MTV]]. The live, daily show—billed as TRL's replacement—featured celebrity talk, live music and online interaction with viewers. The show was cancelled in December 2009.<ref name="popcrunch.com">[http://www.popcrunch.com/mtv-its-on-with-alexa-chung-cancelled "It's On with Alexa Chung cancelled"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110601213932/http://www.popcrunch.com/mtv-its-on-with-alexa-chung-cancelled/ |date=1 June 2011 }}, ''Popcruch'', 11 December 2009, accessed 15 January 2011.</ref> She returned to UK TV screens in April 2010 with a second series of ''Frock Me''. In October 2010, she began hosting ''Gonzo With Alexa Chung'', an informal British chat show previously hosted by [[Zane Lowe]], for [[MTV Rocks]].<ref name="digitalspy.co.uk">[http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/tv/news/a273656/alexa-chung-named-new-host-of-mtv-gonzo.html "Alexa Chung named new host of MTV's ''Gonzo''"], ''Digital Spy'', 6 September 2010, accessed 15 January 2011.</ref> In January 2011, she co‑hosted [[NBC]]'s "2011 Golden Globe Arrivals Special" with [[Carson Daly]] and [[Natalie Morales (journalist)|Natalie Morales]]. The live show featured interviews with television and film stars as well as segments devoted to fashion on the [[red carpet]].<ref>[http://www.nbc.com/news/2011/01/13/nbcs-2011-golden-globe-arrivals-special-pre-show-announce-carson-daly-natalie-morales-and-alexa-chun/ "NBC's "2011 Golden Globe Arrivals Special" Pre-Show Announce Carson Daly] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110115175544/http://www.nbc.com/news/2011/01/13/nbcs-2011-golden-globe-arrivals-special-pre-show-announce-carson-daly-natalie-morales-and-alexa-chun/ |date=15 January 2011 }}, [[Natalie Morales (journalist)|Natalie Morales]] and Alexa Chung as Hosts", ''NBC'', 13 January 2011, accessed 15 January 2011.</ref> Chung presented the [[iTunes Festival]] in July 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itv.com/itunesfestival2011/presenters/ |title=Alexa Chung iTunes Festival |publisher=itv.com |accessdate=7 November 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120102113232/http://www.itv.com/itunesfestival2011/presenters/ |archivedate=2 January 2012 }}</ref> Chung has since worked on 24 Hour Catwalk, acting as the host and a judge. In 2012, Chung soon became the founding co-host for the nightly music show Fuse News. In December 2013, it was announced that Chung would be leaving Fuse News to focus on other endeavours.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fuse.tv/2013/12/fuse-news-welcomes-georgie-okell-new-host |title=Georgie Okell Joins 'Fuse News,' Alexa Chung Exits – News – Fuse |publisher=Fuse.tv |date=16 December 2013 |accessdate=5 January 2014}}</ref> From September 2015, Chung will host an online documentary series for ''Vogue UK''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fashionista.com/2015/07/alexa-chung-british-vogue-fashion-documentary|title=Alexa Chung to 'investigate the state of fashion' in new docu-series|first=Chantal|last=Fernandez|publisher=Fashionista|date=14 July 2015|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref>

She launched her own [[YouTube]] channel in May 2019; in a statement, she said: “It’s an incredibly exciting prospect as I can’t wait to share unfiltered original content through a host of videos spanning fashion, art, travel and other random things.”<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alexa Chung Launches YouTube Channel {{!}} Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/alexa-chung-launches-youtube-channel-1207235|access-date=2020-08-20|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref>

Chung joined [[Queer Eye (2018 TV series)|''Queer Eye''<nowiki/>'s]] [[Tan France]] as co-host of the [[Netflix]] original fashion competition show ''[[Next In Fashion]]'', which debuted on 29 January 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Samuel|last=Spencer|date=2020-01-28|title=All you need to know about Netflix's new fashion competition "Next in Fashion"|url=https://www.newsweek.com/next-fashion-netflix-release-date-judges-contestants-trailer-alexa-chung-tan-france-1484363|access-date=2020-08-20|website=Newsweek|language=en}}</ref> In June 2020, Netflix announced it was cancelling the show after one season.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Petski|first=Denise|date=2020-06-04|title=‘Next In Fashion’ Canceled By Netflix After One Season|url=https://deadline.com/2020/06/next-in-fashion-canceled-netflix-after-one-season-1202951483/|access-date=2020-08-20|website=Deadline|language=en}}</ref>

===Writing===
Chung wrote a monthly column for British women's magazine ''[[Company (magazine)|Company]]'' from October 2007 to June 2008. She then wrote a weekly newspaper column for Britain's ''[[The Independent]]'' from November 2008 to June 2009, titled "Girl About Town" and later "New York Doll." In June 2009, she was made a contributing editor of British ''Vogue''. She has interviewed designers such as [[Karl Lagerfeld]] and [[Christopher Kane]] for the publication, and wrote a cover story about [[Kate Upton]].<ref name="observer.com">{{cite web|url=http://observer.com/2015/07/dont-call-alexa-chung-an-it-girl/|title=Don't Call Alexa Chung an 'It' Girl|first=Dena|last=Silver|publisher=Observer|date=28 July 2015|accessdate=29 September 2015}}</ref> Chung's first book, ''It'', was released on 5 September 2013.
