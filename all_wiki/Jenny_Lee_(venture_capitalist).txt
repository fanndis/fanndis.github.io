'''Jenny Lee''' (born 1972) is a Singaporean venture capitalist. A managing partner of [[GGV Capital]] based in Shanghai, Lee was No. 10 and the first woman venture capitalist in the top 10 of [[Forbes]] [[Midas List]] 2015.<ref name=ST2015>{{cite web|first1=May|last1=Chen|title=Jenny Lee: A guiding star in the world of tech start-ups|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/business/invest/jenny-lee-a-guiding-star-in-the-world-of-tech-start-ups|website=[[The Straits Times]]|accessdate=2016-12-01|date=4 July 2015|archive-date=2016-12-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161201213938/http://www.straitstimes.com/business/invest/jenny-lee-a-guiding-star-in-the-world-of-tech-start-ups|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=#100 Jenny Lee|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/jenny-lee/|website=[[Forbes]]|accessdate=2016-12-01|date=2016|archive-date=2016-12-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161217142942/http://www.forbes.com/profile/jenny-lee/|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2016, she ranked 100th in [[Forbes list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|Forbes World's 100 Most Powerful Women list]].<ref>{{cite web|title=She broke $300,000 bond to succeed abroad|url=http://www.tnp.sg/news/singapore-news/she-broke-300000-bond-succeed-abroad|website=[[The New Paper]]|accessdate=2016-12-02|date=28 November 2016|archive-date=2016-12-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161202102421/http://www.tnp.sg/news/singapore-news/she-broke-300000-bond-succeed-abroad|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Early and personal life ==
Born to a Chinese schoolteacher father and a housewife, Lee's brother Hong Meng is an engineer. She was a student of [[CHIJ Saint Nicholas Girls' School]] and [[Hwa Chong Junior College]].<ref name=ST2015/><ref name=ST2014>{{cite web|first1=Grace|last1=Chng|title=S'porean is top woman investor on Forbes list|url=http://www.stjobs.sg/career-resources/personalities/sporean-is-top-woman-investor-on-forbes-list/a/123240|website=[[The Straits Times]]|accessdate=2016-12-01|date=16 June 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161201213307/http://www.stjobs.sg/career-resources/personalities/sporean-is-top-woman-investor-on-forbes-list/a/123240|archive-date=1 December 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

She is married to Vincent Koh.

== Career ==
A [[ST Engineering]] scholar, she studied engineering at [[Cornell University]] in New York from 1991 to 1995 and graduated with a [[Bachelor of Arts and Science]] and a [[Master of Science]]. Upon her return, she joined [[ST Aerospace]] as a jet engineer.<ref name=ST2014/><ref>{{cite web|first1=Mark|last1=Milian|title=Jenny Lee: The VC With an Eye for China's Rising Tech Giants|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-06-13/jenny-lee-of-ggv-capital-has-an-eye-for-chinas-rising-tech-giants|website=[[Bloomberg News]]|accessdate=2016-12-01|date=13 June 2014|archive-date=2016-09-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160914214848/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-06-13/jenny-lee-of-ggv-capital-has-an-eye-for-chinas-rising-tech-giants|url-status=live}}</ref>
   
In 2001, she obtained a [[Master of Business Administration]] from the [[Kellogg School of Management]] in Chicago. It was her two-year stint at Kellogg that opened her eyes to a different career path.

Seeing the US stock market boomed and then busted in 2001 and how the capital markets affected everyone, it made her realised "the big world outside and she wanted to get out there and learn some more."<ref name=ST2014/>

In 2001, she returned to Singapore.

She found a job with [[Morgan Stanley]] in Hong Kong. A year later, she joined Japanese venture-capital firm, JAFCO Asia.

In 2005, she joined [[GGV Capital]] as a managing partner<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnet.com/news/silicon-valley-unicorns-dying-vc-jenny-lee/|title=Are unicorns dying in Silicon Valley?|date=2017-10-18|work=CNET|access-date=2018-07-26|language=en|archive-date=2018-07-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180726201654/https://www.cnet.com/news/silicon-valley-unicorns-dying-vc-jenny-lee/|url-status=live}}</ref> and was involved in setting up GGV presence in China. While working with GGV, the firm invested in startups such as [[Alibaba Group|Alibaba]], [[DiDi (company)|Didi Chuxing]], [[Xiaomi]], [[Toutiao]] and Southeast Asia-based Grab.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/ggv-capital-jenny-lee-vc-101553/|title=What does it take to be a good VC? Patience & resilience, says GGV Capital's Jenny Lee|work=DealStreetAsia|access-date=2018-07-26|language=en-US|archive-date=2018-07-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180726201835/https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/ggv-capital-jenny-lee-vc-101553/|url-status=live}}</ref>
