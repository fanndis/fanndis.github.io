'''Jennifer Michelle Lee'''<ref name=CBChronicleBook>{{cite news|last=Amidi|first=Amid|title=Chronicle Books Is Releasing A Ton Of Cartoon Titles This Fall|url=http://www.cartoonbrew.com/books/chronicle-books-is-releasing-a-ton-of-cartoon-titles-this-fall-61810.html|accessdate=January 5, 2014|newspaper=[[Cartoon Brew]]|date=April 26, 2012}}</ref> ([[née]] '''Rebecchi'''; October 22, 1971)<ref name="Perry">{{cite news|last1=Perry|first1=Valerie A.|title=Legal Notices (Probate Court of the City of East Providence)|work=Providence Journal|publisher=The Providence Journal Company|date=21 January 1995|page=B-14}} This article lists various matters noticed for hearing before the probate court, of which the relevant one is as follows: "REBECCHI, JENNIFER MICHELLE, estate - Change of name to Jennifer Michelle Lee; for hearing January 24, 1995." Available via [[ProQuest NewsStand]].</ref> is an American [[screenwriter]], [[film director]], and [[chief creative officer]] of [[Walt Disney Animation Studios]]. She is best known as the writer and director of ''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]'' and its sequel ''[[Frozen II]]'', the former of which earned her an [[Academy Award for Best Animated Feature]]. Lee is the first female director of a Walt Disney Animation Studios [[feature film]]<ref name=THRBreakSciFi>{{cite news|last=Kit|first=Borys|title='Frozen' Director Wants to Break a Sci-Fi Blockbuster Barrier|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/frozen-jennifer-lee-disney-animation-666372|accessdate=December 31, 2013|newspaper=The Hollywood Reporter|date=December 18, 2013|quote= Lee, 42, is the first woman in the studio's history to act as a director on one of its animated features (she co-directed with Chris Buck).}}</ref> and the first female director of a feature film that earned more than $1 billion in gross box office revenue.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Flores|first1=Terry|title='Frozen's' Jennifer Lee Melts Glass Ceilings|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/awards/frozens-jennifer-lee-melts-ceilings-1201216961/|accessdate=June 16, 2014|work=Variety|publisher=Variety Media LLC|date=June 10, 2014}}</ref> She has won one [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]], [[British Academy Film Awards|BAFTA Award]] and [[Annie Awards|Annie Award]], and has been nominated for one BAFTA Award and two Annie Awards.<ref name=":1" />

==Early life==
Lee was born in October 22, 1971<ref name=THRBreakSciFi /><ref name="LAWQueen">{{cite news|url=https://www.laweekly.com/jennifer-lee-disneys-new-animation-queen/|title=Jennifer Lee: Disney's New Animation Queen|last=Stewart|first=Jill|date=May 15, 2013|newspaper=LA Weekly|accessdate=November 8, 2019|quote=Raised in East Providence, R.I., Lee, 41, was a flute-playing band nerd who "became a cheerleader — a nerd cheerleader."}}</ref> to Linda Lee and Saverio Rebecchi, who were living in [[Barrington, Rhode Island]] at the time;<ref name="Flynn">{{cite news|last=Flynn|first=Sean|title=Is it her time to shine?|url=http://www.newportri.com/newportdailynews/news/page_one/is-it-her-time-to-shine/article_a39a9415-c79a-54ac-bcf8-798ad87e0f9f.html|newspaper=The Newport Daily News|accessdate=April 3, 2014|date=February 17, 2014}}</ref> after their divorce, Lee and her older sister, Amy who is an English teacher, lived with their mother in [[East Providence, Rhode Island]].<ref name=LAWQueen /><ref name="Flynn" />  Both Lee and her older sister graduated from [[East Providence High School]] and the [[University of New Hampshire]].<ref name="Flynn" /> Lee earned a bachelor's degree in English<ref name="AWNDirectorFrozen">{{cite news|url=http://www.awn.com/news/disney-names-jennifer-lee-director-frozen|title=Disney Names Jennifer Lee Director Of 'Frozen'|last=Wolfe|first=Jennifer|date=November 29, 2012|newspaper=Animation World Network|accessdate=January 5, 2014}}</ref> in 1992<ref name="Mackin">{{cite web|last=Mackin|first=Jean|title='Frozen' writer, director says UNH helped pave her way|url=http://www.wmur.com/entertainment/frozen-writer-director-says-unh-helped-pave-her-way/23914756|work=WMUR New Hampshire 9|publisher=Manchester Hearst Properties Inc.|accessdate=April 30, 2014|date=January 14, 2014}}</ref> and went to New York City, where she worked as a graphic artist in publishing; she designed audiobooks for [[Random House]].<ref name=LAWQueen /><ref name="Flynn" />  As an adult, she began using her mother's maiden name, Lee, in a professional capacity<ref name="Flynn" /> and in January 1995, [[Name change|legally changed her last name]] from Rebecchi to Lee.<ref name="Perry" />

==Career==
Lee graduated from [[Columbia University School of the Arts#Film|Columbia University School of the Arts' Film Program]] with an [[Master of Fine Arts|MFA]] in film in 2005.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.caa.com/caaspeakers/jennifer-lee|title=Jennifer Lee {{!}} Speaking Fee, Booking Agent, & Contact Info {{!}} CAA Speakers|website=www.caa.com|access-date=2019-11-08}}</ref> While at Columbia, she won several awards for excellence in screenwriting and gave birth to her daughter, Agatha Lee Monn.<ref name=LAWQueen /><ref name=":0" /> 

Her script for ''The Round Up'' was a quarter-finalist in the [[Nicholl Fellowships in Screenwriting]] competition in 2009 and was subsequently optioned by [[Appian Way Productions]].<ref name=":0" />

In March 2011, [[Phil Johnston (filmmaker)|Phil Johnston]], a former classmate at Columbia, called Lee to ask her to join him at Disney Animation in [[Burbank, California|Burbank]] to help him write ''[[Wreck-It Ralph]]''.<ref name="LAWQueen" /> What was supposed to be a temporary eight-week writing gig eventually turned into a much longer commitment.<ref name="LAWQueen" /> First, she was asked to stay on until ''Ralph'' was finished.<ref name="LAWQueen" /> She then became involved with [[Frozen (2013 film)|''Frozen'']], initially as screenwriter and later as co-director with [[Chris Buck]].<ref name="LAWQueen" /> When Lee was brought on board, she helped transition the film from an action-adventure to "more musical, with more comedy."<ref name="FCReinvented">{{cite web|last=Laporte |first=Nicole |title=How "Frozen" Director Jennifer Lee Reinvented the Story of the Snow Queen |url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3027019/most-creative-people/how-frozen-director-jennifer-lee-reinvented-the-story-of-the-snow-queen |publisher=Fast Company |accessdate=April 29, 2014 |date=February 28, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140314091751/http://www.fastcompany.com/3027019/most-creative-people/how-frozen-director-jennifer-lee-reinvented-the-story-of-the-snow-queen |archivedate=March 14, 2014 }}</ref> She worked closely with the songwriters ([[Robert Lopez]] and [[Kristen Anderson-Lopez]]) in the writing of the script.<ref name="FCReinvented" /> ''Frozen'' gave her the opportunity to celebrate "wild and wonderful" girls like her childhood self, and her daughter, Agatha.<ref name="FCReinvented" /> It was also the highest-earning film with a female director in terms of domestic earnings, until surpassed by [[Warner Bros.]]' ''[[Wonder Woman (2017 film)|Wonder Woman]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://blog.womenandhollywood.com/wonder-woman-is-now-the-top-female-helmed-film-at-the-domestic-box-office-f38eba286f3b |title="Wonder Woman" Is Now the Top Female-Helmed Film at the Domestic Box Office |publisher=Blog.womenandhollywood.com |accessdate=2017-08-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170724160242/http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2017/07/24/wonder-woman-sequel-title-revealed.html |archive-date=July 24, 2017 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

On May 17, 2014, Lee delivered the [[Commencement speech|commencement address]] to the class of 2014 at her alma mater, the University of New Hampshire.<ref>{{cite web|last=Record|first=Jody|title="Frozen" Screenwriter/Director Jennifer Lee '92 is UNH 2014 Commencement Speaker|url=http://www.unh.edu/news/releases/2014/03/jr19commencement.cfm|publisher=University of New Hampshire Media Relations|accessdate=May 17, 2014|date=March 19, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Lawrence">{{cite news|last=Lawrence|first=Mike|title='Frozen' director warms up to speaking at UNH commencement|url=http://www.unionleader.com/article/20140517/NEWS04/140519189|accessdate=May 18, 2014|newspaper=New Hampshire Union-Leader|date=May 17, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Sanborn">{{cite news|last=Sanborn|first=Aaron|title=Oscar-winning director of 'Frozen' inspires UNH grads|url=http://www.seacoastonline.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20140517/NEWS/140519750/-1/NEWSMAP|accessdate=May 18, 2014|newspaper=Seacoastonline.com|date=May 17, 2014}}</ref>  She revealed that she had struggled with self-doubt while growing up,<ref name="UNH">{{cite web|last=Lee|first=Jennifer|title=Jennifer Lee '92 UNH Commencement Speech 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TLwsrVVgeQs|publisher=University of New Hampshire|accessdate=May 17, 2014|format=Video|date=May 17, 2014}}</ref>{{rp|2:00}} and then in April of her junior year of college, her boyfriend was killed in a boating accident, after which she felt "no doubt, only grief ... and for a brief moment ... [knew] better than to waste a second doubting."<ref name="UNH" />{{rp|6:18}}  Years later, that memory would help her overcome her initial doubt over whether she was good enough to apply to Columbia.<ref name="UNH" />{{rp|7:10}} At Columbia, Johnston recognized she was talented but insecure, and one day asked her to "promise ... that you'll leave it out of your work, just know that you're good enough and move on."<ref name="Sanborn" />  She concluded: "If I learned one thing it is that self-doubt is one of the most destructive forces. It makes you defensive instead of open, reactive instead of active. Self-doubt is consuming and cruel and my hope today is that we can all collectively agree to ban it ... Please know, from here on out, you are enough and dare I say, more than enough."<ref name="Sanborn" />  UNH then awarded her the honorary degree of [[Doctor of Humane Letters]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Anonymous|title=Honorary degrees conferred at UNH|url=http://www.unionleader.com/article/20140517/NEWS04/140519174|accessdate=May 18, 2014|newspaper=New Hampshire Union-Leader|date=May 17, 2014}}</ref>

In September 2014, it was announced that Lee and Buck would co-direct a [[short film]] featuring the ''Frozen'' characters called ''[[Frozen Fever]]''. It was released in March 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Disney short 'Frozen Fever' coming in spring 2015, with new song|author=Gettell, O.|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/moviesnow/la-et-mn-disney-short-frozen-fever-coming-in-spring-2015-20140903-story.html|date=September 3, 2014|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|accessdate=September 6, 2014}}</ref> Lee was one of several Disney writers and directors who received credit for "Creative Leadership" on the 2014 film ''[[Big Hero 6 (film)|Big Hero 6]]'' and the 2016 film ''[[Moana (2016 film)|Moana]]'', and received screen credit as one of the writers who developed the story for the 2016 film ''[[Zootopia]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Did a Disney animated film really say that? If it's 'Zootopia,' prepare to be shocked|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/la-et-mn-zootopia-production-20160304-story.html|author=Keegan, Rebecca|date=March 4, 2016|accessdate=2016-03-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Big Hero 6 (2014) Production Credits|work=[[New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/movies/movie/477628/Big-Hero-6/credits|accessdate=2016-03-17}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Moana screen credits|url=http://www.hollywood.com/movies/moana-60594396/credits/|publisher=hollywood.com|accessdate=2016-11-26}}</ref>

In August 2014, ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' reported that Lee's next project after ''Frozen'' would be an [[A Wrinkle in Time (2018 film)|adaptation]] of [[Madeleine L'Engle]]'s 1962 novel ''[[A Wrinkle in Time]]''.<ref name=wrinkle>{{cite web|title='Frozen' Director Jennifer Lee to Adapt 'A Wrinkle in Time' for Disney |publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/news/frozen-director-jennifer-lee-to-adapt-a-wrinkle-in-time-for-disney-exclusive-1201275488/|accessdate=August 6, 2014|date=August 6, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title='Frozen' Scribe Jennifer Lee Signs On To Write Big-Screen Adaptation Of 'A Wrinkle In Time'|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/05/jennifer-lee-a-wrinkle-in-time_n_5652505.html|author=Jacobs, M.|publisher=[[Huffington Post]]|date=August 5, 2014|accessdate=August 6, 2014}}</ref> After the news broke, Lee tweeted: "Been in love with the book for over 30 years. Writing this script means the world to me."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Rivera|first1=Zayda|title='Frozen' director Jennifer Lee set to adapt 'A Wrinkle in Time' for Disney|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/movies/frozen-director-adapt-wrinkle-time-disney-article-1.1892875|accessdate=August 9, 2014|work=New York Daily News|publisher=Daily News, L.P.|date=August 5, 2014}}</ref> Catherine Hand, the executive producer of the [[A Wrinkle in Time (2003 film)|2003 television film version]], and Jim Whitaker produced for Disney,<ref name=wrinkle /> and [[Ava DuVernay]] directed the film, based on Lee's script. ''A Wrinkle in Time'' was released in March 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2016/film/news/ava-duvernay-wrinkle-in-time-movie-1201712895/|title=Ava DuVernay to Direct 'A Wrinkle in Time' Movie Adaptation|author=McNary, Dave|date=February 23, 2016|accessdate=2016-03-14|publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/11/ava-duvernay-a-wrinkle-in-time-release-date-1201854261/|title=Ava DuVernay's 'A Wrinkle In Time' To Hit Theaters In Spring 2018|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|first=Anthony|last=D'Alessandro|date=November 14, 2016|accessdate=November 14, 2016}}</ref>

On March 12, 2015, Disney announced that Lee and Buck would co-direct a [[Frozen II|full length sequel]] to ''Frozen''.<ref name=frozen2>{{cite web|title=Disney Announces 'Frozen 2'|last=Graser|first=Marc|url=https://variety.com/2015/film/news/disney-announces-frozen-2-1201451480/|publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=March 12, 2015|accessdate=2015-03-15}}</ref>

In June 2018, Lee was named the chief creative officer of Walt Disney Animation Studios, following [[John Lasseter]]'s departure from Disney.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jennifer Lee, Pete Docter to Run Disney Animation, Pixar|author=Brent Land|url=https://variety.com/2018/film/news/jennifer-lee-pete-docter-head-disney-animation-pixar-1202851411/|publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=June 19, 2018|accessdate=2018-06-19}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Lee married Robert Joseph Monn<ref name=THRApologizesParents>{{cite news|last1=Wilson Hunt|first1=Stacey|title='Frozen' Director Apologizes to Parents for "Let It Go"|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/frozen-director-apologizes-parents-let-756614|accessdate=July 24, 2016|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=December 12, 2014}}</ref> on May 30, 1999, at the [[Rhode Island Country Club]]<ref>{{cite news|last1=Anonymous|title=Weddings|work=Providence Journal|publisher=A.H. Belo Corporation|date=25 June 1999|page=J-10}} Available via [[ProQuest NewsStand]].</ref> (they later divorced).<ref name="Flynn" /> They have a daughter Agatha Lee Monn<ref name=TWSJListenSongs>{{cite news|last=Chai|first=Barbara|title=Listen to Songs From Disney's 'Frozen' and Hear How They Were Written|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2013/11/27/listen-to-songs-from-disneys-frozen-and-hear-how-they-were-written/|accessdate=January 5, 2014|newspaper=The Wall Street Journal|date=November 27, 2013}}</ref> (born in 2003),<ref name=THRBreakSciFi /><ref name=LAWQueen /> who voices pre-teen Anna for the middle verse of "[[Do You Want to Build a Snowman?]]" in ''Frozen''.<ref name=THRApologizesParents /> Lee and her daughter now live in the [[San Fernando Valley]] in [[Los Angeles County, California]].<ref name=LAWQueen />

In November 2019, Lee confirmed that she is in a relationship with actor [[Alfred Molina]].<ref name="Dowd">{{cite news |last1=Dowd |first1=Maureen |title=Jennifer Lee, Queen of the 'Frozen' Franchise |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/11/07/style/jennifer-lee-frozen.html |accessdate=November 25, 2019 |work=The New York Times |publisher=The New York Times Company |date=November 7, 2019}}</ref>
