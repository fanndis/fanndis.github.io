'''Dulce Quintans-Saguisag''' (September 5, 1943 &ndash; November 8, 2007) was a [[Philippines|Filipino]] politician and former Secretary of the [[Department of Social Welfare and Development]] under the administration of former President [[Joseph Estrada]].<ref name=pdi>{{cite news |first=|last=|title= Dulce Saguisag, 64 
|url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/metro/view_article.php?article_id=99662|work= [[Philippine Daily Inquirer]] |publisher= |date=2007-11-08 |accessdate=2007-11-25}}</ref> Saguisag was one of Estrada's eleven [[Cabinet of the Philippines|cabinet members]] who withdrew support for Estrada on January 19, 2001, following accusations of massive [[Political corruption|corruption]] by the president.<ref name=pdi/> Estrada was ousted from office the next day, which is now known in the Philippines as [[EDSA II]].<ref name=pdi/>

She also served as the secretary-general of the [[Gymnastics Association of the Philippines]].<ref name=pdi2>{{cite news|title=Saguisag wife killed in road mishap|url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/metro/view_article.php?article_id=99542|work=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]|date=2007-11-08|accessdate=2007-11-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080118114720/http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/metro/view_article.php?article_id=99542|archive-date=2008-01-18|url-status=dead}}</ref>
Saguisag was the wife of a former [[Senate of the Philippines|Filipino Senator]] [[Rene Saguisag]],<ref name=pdi2/>  who was in office from 1987 to 1992.<ref name=pdi2/>

== Early life ==
Dulce Saguisag was born '''Dulce Maramba Quintans''' on September 5, 1943.<ref name=pdi/> She received a [[bachelor's degree]] in [[social work]] from the [[University of the Philippines Diliman]] and placed third in the country in the 1964 National Board Examination for Social Workers.<ref name=pdi/>

She initially began her career as in 1964 at the Social Welfare Administration, which is now called the [[Department of Social Welfare and Development]].<ref name=pdi/> She held a position of senior [[social worker]] at the SWA until 1965, when she became a supervising social worker and community center director.<ref name=pdi/>

Saguisag left the Philippines for the [[United States]] in 1967 after winning a [[Research funding|grant]] by the [[US State Department]] to observe social work in the country.<ref name=pdi/> She was also recruited by the [[John F. Kennedy War on Poverty]] program while in the United States.<ref name=pdi/> 
 
Saguisag won a [[scholarship]] to [[Boston College]] where she received a master's degree in social work, with an emphasis on [[community organization]] and [[social planning]], in 1969.<ref name=pdi/>

She met her future husband, [[Rene Saguisag]], a student at [[Harvard Law School]], while studying at Boston College.<ref name=pdi/> The couple had two daughters and three sons during their marriage.<ref name=pdi/> Her hobbies included collecting [[angel]] and [[Santa Claus]] [[Figurine|figurines]] and [[ballroom dancing]] with her husband.<ref name=pdi/>

== Career ==
Upon receiving her master's degree, Saguisag returned to the Philippines.  She took a job with the [[Department of Social Welfare and Development]] in 1971.<ref name=pdi/> She worked as a social welfare program specialist at the department before being promoted to assistant [[Director-general|director]] of the [[Bureau of Family and Child Welfare]].<ref name=pdi/>

She left her position with the bureau in order to become dual director of [[human resources]] development and director of [[management]] training at the [[Development Academy of the Philippines]] in 1976.<ref name=pdi/>

Saguisag once again left to take a new position, this time for an organization outside of the Filipino government. In 1984, she took a job with the Mondragon International Philippines, Inc. as a [[Management|manager]] for [[human resource development]].<ref name=pdi/> She stayed with the company until 1999.<ref name=pdi/> She was serving as Mondragon's vice chairperson and deputy [[chief executive officer]] at the time of her departure.<ref name=pdi/>

== Secretary of the Department of Social Welfare and Development ==
Dulce Saguisag was appointed Secretary of the [[Department of Social Welfare and Development]] (DSWD) by [[President of the Philippines|President]] [[Joseph Estrada]] in October 2000.<ref name=pdi/> Her appointment to Secretary of the DSWD followed the resignation of then [[Vice President of the Philippines|Vice President]] and DSWD Secretary [[Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo]].<ref name=pdi/> Arroyo had left the Estrada administration over the [[jueteng|jueteng scandal]] which had plagued Estrada during his term.<ref name=pdi/> Saguisag accepted the Secretary position despite the objections of her husband, Rene Saguisag.  He worried that the cabinet position would be too stressful to his wife, who had undergone successful [[breast cancer]] surgery in 1997.<ref name=pdi/>

Saguisag became one of eleven of Estrada's cabinet members who withdrew support for the embattled President on January 19, 2001.<ref name=pdi/> Her decision to withdraw her support for the President came after allegations of massive government corruption against Estrada.<ref name=pdi/> Estrada was ousted from office the next day on January 20, 2001, which is now known as [[EDSA II]] in the Philippines.<ref name=pdi/> (Dulce Saguisag's husband, Rene Saguisag, later became Estrada's [[defense lawyer]].<ref name=pdi/>)

== Car accident ==
Dulce Saguisag was killed in a serious [[car accident]] on November 8, 2007, on President Osmeña Highway in [[Makati City]].<ref name=pdi2/> Her husband, [[Rene Saguisag]], was seriously injured in the accident.<ref name=pdi2/> Two other people riding in the same [[Toyota Grandia]] van as the Saguisags, including Filipino dance instructor, [[Rhea Imelda Obong]], and driver, Felipe Calvario, were also seriously injured in the accident.<ref name=pdi2/> All four were rushed to [[Ospital ng Makati]] where Dulce Saguisag was pronounced dead from her injuries.<ref name=pdi2/>

The [[Toyota Hiace|Toyota]] that Ducle Saguisag was riding in, which was travelling on Pasay Road, was struck by a [[Isuzu Giga|dump truck]] at the corner of the President Osmeña Highway in Makati City at about 2 A.M.<ref name=pdi2/> The dump truck had reportedly been speeding when it ran a traffic light and struck the left side of the Saguisags' van, killing Dulce.<ref name=pdi2/> Police arrested the driver of the dump truck at the scene of the accident.<ref name=pdi2/>

Dulce Saguisag's funeral was held on November 16, 2007.<ref name=pdi3>{{cite news|first=Allison Lopez|last=Tarra Quismundo|title=Dulce Saguisag laid to rest|url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/metro/view_article.php?article_id=101297|work=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]|publisher=|date=2007-11-16|accessdate=2007-11-27}}{{dead link|date=September 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The service began with a two-hour [[Roman Catholic]] [[mass (liturgy)|mass]] at the [[chapel]] of [[St. Scholastica’s College]] in [[Manila]], which Saguisag had attended.<ref name=pdi3/> The funeral procession then passed Saguisag's "ancestral home" on [[Bigasan Street]] in Makati City.<ref name=pdi3/> Dulce Saguisag was buried in the Garden of Reverence section of the [[Manila Memorial Park]] cemetery in [[Parañaque City]].<ref name=pdi3/>

Three hundred people attended her funeral.  Prominent mourners included former Filipino Presidents [[Corazon Aquino]] and [[Joseph Estrada]], [[Vice President of the Philippines|Vice President]] [[Noli de Castro]], [[San Juan, Metro Manila|San Juan City]] Mayor [[Joseph Victor Ejercito]], and [[Gina de Venecia]], the wife of the [[Speaker of the Philippine House of Representatives]] [[Jose de Venecia]].<ref name=pdi3/>

After a month, on December 8, 2007, [[Senator]] [[Rene Saguisag]] was released from the hospital, freed from tubes, but “still needs assistance by a hired a private [[nurse]]. He lost lots of weight. He was out of the [[Intensive care unit|ICU]] on November 28, or 20 days from the fatal accident.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/nation/view_article.php?article_id=105676 |title=Inquirer.net, Ex-senator Saguisag leaves hospital |access-date=2007-12-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080125072340/http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/breakingnews/nation/view_article.php?article_id=105676 |archive-date=2008-01-25 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Their youngest daughter 16-year-old [[gymnast]] Kaissa Saguisag's knee injury ended her quest for gold at the 24th [[Southeast Asian Games]] .<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://globalnation.inquirer.net/cebudailynews/news/view_article.php?article_id=106603 |title=Inquirer.net, Knee injury ends gymnast Saguisag's quest for SEAG gold |access-date=2007-12-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716082641/http://globalnation.inquirer.net/cebudailynews/news/view_article.php?article_id=106603 |archive-date=2011-07-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
