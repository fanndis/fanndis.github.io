The '''77th Golden Globe Awards''' honored the best in [[2019 in film|film]] and [[2019 in American television|American television of 2019]], as chosen by the [[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]]. Produced by [[Dick Clark Productions]] and the HFPA, the ceremony was broadcast live on January 5, 2020, from [[The Beverly Hilton]] in [[Beverly Hills, California]] beginning at 5:00&nbsp;p.m. [[Pacific Time Zone|PST]] / 8:00&nbsp;p.m. [[Eastern Time Zone|EST]]. The ceremony aired live on [[NBC]] in the United States. [[Ricky Gervais]] hosted the ceremony for the fifth and "final" time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/11/12/movies/ricky-gervais-golden-globes.html|title=Ricky Gervais Will Return to Host (and Roast) the Golden Globes|last=Buchanan|first=Kyle|date=November 12, 2019|work=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=November 13, 2019|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191113014624/https://www.nytimes.com/2019/11/12/movies/ricky-gervais-golden-globes.html|archivedate=November 13, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2020/jan/05/golden-globes-2020-1917-and-fleabag-lead-british-invasion-with-major-wins|title=Golden Globes 2020: 1917 and Fleabag lead British invasion with major wins|date=5 January 2020|work=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=6 January 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200106061211/https://www.theguardian.com/film/2020/jan/05/golden-globes-2020-1917-and-fleabag-lead-british-invasion-with-major-wins|archivedate=6 January 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>	

The nominees were announced on December 9, 2019, by [[Tim Allen]], [[Dakota Fanning]] and [[Susan Kelechi Watson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ew.com/golden-globes/2019/12/05/tim-allen-dakota-fanning-susan-kelechi-watson-golden-globe/|title=Tim Allen, Dakota Fanning, and Susan Kelechi Watson to announce 2020 Golden Globe nominations|last=Huff|first=Lauren|date=December 5, 2019|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|accessdate=December 9, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191209050051/https://ew.com/golden-globes/2019/12/05/tim-allen-dakota-fanning-susan-kelechi-watson-golden-globe/|archivedate=December 9, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2019/dec/09/golden-globes-2020-full-list-of-nominations|title=Golden Globes 2020: full list of nominations|date=9 December 2019|work=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=10 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191210105001/https://www.theguardian.com/film/2019/dec/09/golden-globes-2020-full-list-of-nominations|archivedate=10 December 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> ''[[Marriage Story]]'' earned a leading six nominations. [[Tom Hanks]] and [[Ellen DeGeneres]] were announced as the recipients of the [[Golden Globe Cecil B. DeMille Award|Cecil B. DeMille Award]] and the [[Carol Burnett Award]], respectively.

''[[Once Upon a Time in Hollywood]]'' won the most awards for the ceremony with three, including [[Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy|Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy]]. ''[[1917 (2019 film)|1917]]'', ''[[Joker (2019 film)|Joker]]'', and ''[[Rocketman (film)|Rocketman]]'' won two awards each, with ''1917'' winning [[Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Drama|Best Motion Picture – Drama]]. In television, ''[[Succession (TV series)|Succession]]'', ''[[Fleabag]]'' and ''[[Chernobyl (miniseries)|Chernobyl]]'' were the most awarded, with two awards each.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lang|first=Brent|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/awards/golden-globes-2019-biggest-wins-netflix-1203457082/|title=Golden Globes: 'Once Upon a Time in Hollywood,' '1917' Win Big|date=5 January 2020|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|accessdate=6 January 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200108034816/https://variety.com/2020/film/awards/golden-globes-2019-biggest-wins-netflix-1203457082/|archivedate=8 January 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

The ceremony was nominated for two [[Primetime Emmy Award]]s, [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Variety Special (Live)|Outstanding Variety Special (Live)]] and [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Production Design for a Variety Special|Outstanding Production Design for a Variety Special]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.emmys.com/shows/77th-annual-golden-globe-awards|title=77th Annual Golden Globe Awards|website=Emmys.com|publisher=[[Academy of Television Arts & Sciences]]|accessdate=September 15, 2020}}</ref>

===Cecil B. DeMille Award===
The [[Golden Globe Cecil B. DeMille Award|Cecil B. DeMille Award]] is an honorary award bestowed for outstanding contributions to the world of entertainment. It is awarded to honorees who have made a significant mark in the film industry and is named after its first recipient, director [[Cecil B. DeMille]].

* [[Tom Hanks]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goldenglobes.com/cecil-b-demille-award-0|title=The Cecil B. deMille Award|publisher= [[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]]|language=en|accessdate=November 13, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191123205901/https://www.goldenglobes.com/cecil-b-demille-award-0|archivedate=November 23, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Carol Burnett Award===
The [[Carol Burnett Award]] is an honorary award given for outstanding and lasting contributions to television on or off the screen. It is named in honor of its first recipient, actress [[Carol Burnett]].

* [[Ellen DeGeneres]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goldenglobes.com/carol-burnett-award-0|title=The Carol Burnett Award|publisher= [[Hollywood Foreign Press Association]]|language=en|accessdate=November 13, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191209213452/https://www.goldenglobes.com/carol-burnett-award-0|archivedate=December 9, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>
