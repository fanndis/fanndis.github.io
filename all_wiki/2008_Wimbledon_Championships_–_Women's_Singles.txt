{{main|2008 Wimbledon Championships}}
[[Venus Williams]] was the defending champion, and successfully defended her title, defeating her [[Williams sisters|sister]] [[Serena Williams]] in the final, 7–5, 6–4 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2008 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> This was Venus's fifth Wimbledon title and her seventh Grand Slam singles title overall.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2008/jul/05/wimbledon.tennis10|title= Venus the afternoon star as defending champion triumphs over Serena  |date=5 July 2008|work=The Guardian|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> This was also the second time she won a Grand Slam tournament without losing a set.

The top four seeds, [[Ana Ivanovic]], [[Jelena Janković]], [[Maria Sharapova]] and [[Svetlana Kuznetsova]] were in contention for the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|WTA No. 1 ranking]]. Ivanovic retained the top ranking despite losing in the third round to [[Zheng Jie]]. Janković, Sharapova and Kuznetsova also lost in the first four rounds, it marked the first time in Wimbledon history that none of the top four women's seeds had advanced to at least the quarterfinals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2008/jun/30/wimbledon.tennis6|title=Wimbledon 2008: Jankovic sent crashing to historic defeat |date=30 June 2008|work=The Guardian|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref>

Zheng's feat of reaching the semifinals made her the first Chinese player of either sex to reach a Grand Slam singles semifinal, and the first [[Wild card (sports)#Professional tennis|wild card]] ever to reach the semifinals of the women's singles event at Wimbledon.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/07/01/sports/01iht-tennisserana1.14136112.html|title=Serena Williams and wild card Zheng Jie also reach semifinals |date=1 July 2008|work=The New York Times|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> Zheng's win against [[Ana Ivanovic]] in the third round also made her the lowest-ranked player (ranked 133rd) to defeat a reigning world No. 1.{{fact|date=September 2019}} This record would be broken in just two months by [[Julie Coin]] at the [[2008 US Open – Women's Singles|2008 US Open]], who also defeated Ivanovic.{{fact|date=September 2019}}
