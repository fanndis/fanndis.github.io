'''David Bruce Goldberg''' (October 2, 1967 – May 1, 2015) was an American management consultant and businessman. He was the founder of [[LAUNCH Media]] and the CEO of [[SurveyMonkey]]. He was married to [[Sheryl Sandberg]], [[Chief operating officer|COO]] of [[Facebook]].

==Early life and education==
Goldberg was born in [[Chicago, Illinois]], on October 2, 1967 and grew up in [[Minneapolis, Minnesota]].<ref name="WP obit">{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/david-goldberg-executive-of-online-questionnaire-service-dies-at-47/2015/05/02/a3ded996-f112-11e4-8abc-d6aa3bad79dd_story.html|title=David Goldberg, 47, SurveyMonkey CEO, Sandberg Husband, Dies|last=Coppola|first=Gabrielle|first2=Sarah|last2=Frier|date=May 2, 2015|work=[[The Washington Post]]|accessdate=November 5, 2015}}</ref> His mother, Paula Goldberg, is co-founder and executive director of the Pacer Center, and his father, Melvin Bert "Mel" Goldberg (1942–1998), was associate dean and professor at the [[William Mitchell College of Law]].<ref name="WP obit" /> He interned at the Minneapolis ''[[Star Tribune]]'' newspaper while in high school.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/david-goldberg-surveymonkey-ceo-dies-at-age-47-1430672009 |title=Web Executive David Goldberg Dies at Age 47 |newspaper=The Wall Street Journal|url-access=subscription  |date=May 3, 2015 |accessdate=May 4, 2015 }}</ref> He graduated from [[Blake School (Minneapolis)|Blake School]] in Minneapolis in 1985,<ref name="Star-Trib">{{cite news|url=http://www.startribune.com/local/302317551.html|title=David Goldberg, Silicon Valley CEO with deep ties to Minneapolis, dies at 47|date=May 3, 2015|work=Minneapolis Star-Tribune|accessdate=May 3, 2015}}</ref> and graduated ''[[magna cum laude]]'' from [[Harvard University]] in 1989, majoring in history and government.<ref name=nytimes/> Goldberg was an enthusiastic [[Minnesota Vikings]] fan throughout his life.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Grant|first1=Adam|title=Remembering Dave Goldberg, the Ultimate Mensch|url=https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/remembering-dave-goldberg-ultimate-mensch-adam-grant|accessdate=May 23, 2015|work=[[Pulse (app)|Pulse]]|publisher=LinkedIn|date=May 10, 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
Goldberg worked for [[Bain & Company]] for two years after graduating from college. He had planned to attend law school but instead joined [[Capitol Records]], where he served as director of marketing strategy and new business development.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Swisher|first1=Kara|title=Former Yahoo Music Exec Dave Goldberg to Head SurveyMonkey|url=http://allthingsd.com/20090420/former-yahoo-music-exec-dave-goldberg-to-head-survey-monkey/|accessdate=May 4, 2015|publisher=All Things D|date=April 20, 2009}}</ref><ref name=businessinsider>{{cite news|last1=Bort|first1=Julie|title=Dave Goldberg had an incredible life — but it wasn't easy|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/the-incredible-life-of-david-goldberg-2015-5|accessdate=May 3, 2015|work=Business Insider|date=May 2, 2015}}</ref> He founded [[LAUNCH Media]] in 1994, and led it through its acquisition by [[Yahoo!|Yahoo Inc.]] in 2001.<ref name=businessinsider /> He quit Yahoo in 2007 and joined [[Benchmark (venture capital firm)|Benchmark Capital]]. He then joined [[SurveyMonkey]] in 2009.<ref name=nytimes/>

==Personal life==
In 2004, Goldberg married [[Sheryl Sandberg]]. The couple had two children and lived in [[Menlo Park, California]].<ref name=nytimes /> Prior to his death, he and Sandberg were active philanthropists, signing [[The Giving Pledge]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://givingpledge.org/|title=Home - The Giving Pledge|website=Giving Pledge}}</ref>

==Death and memorials==
On May 1, 2015, Goldberg died suddenly while vacationing with Sandberg at a private beach-front villa near the [[Four Seasons Hotels and Resorts]] in [[Punta Mita]], Mexico; he was pronounced dead at a hospital in [[Nuevo Vallarta]]. Goldberg's death was initially attributed to falling off a [[treadmill]] while exercising at a gym and suffering head trauma and blood loss.<ref name=nytimes>{{cite web|last1=Goel|first1=Vindu|last2=Hardy|first2=Quentin|title=Dave Goldberg, Head of Web Survey Company and Half of a Silicon Valley Power Couple, Dies at 47|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/03/business/dave-goldberg-47-surveymonkey-chief-and-sheryl-sandbergs-husband-dies.html|work=The New York Times|accessdate=May 2, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Goel|first1=Vindu|title=Dave Goldberg, Silicon Valley Executive, Said to Have Died After Exercising|url=http://nyti.ms/1KHa9Yq|work=The New York Times|accessdate=May 4, 2015|last2=Archibold|first2=Randal C.}}</ref><ref name="BBCaccident">{{cite news  
| url=  https://www.bbc.com/news/business-32586657
| title=  Silicon Valley's Dave Goldberg died after gym accident
| work=  [[BBC News]]
| date=  May 5, 2015
}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nordic.businessinsider.com/sheryl-sandberg-martin-seligmans-3-ps-helped-me-cope-with-my-husbands-death-2016-5/|title=Sheryl Sandberg says the '3 P's' have helped her become a stronger person after her husband's tragic death|date=May 18, 2016|website=nordic.businessinsider.com}}</ref>  Sandberg has subsequently said that her husband's death was due to an [[Heart arrhythmia|arrhythmia]] caused by undiagnosed [[coronary artery disease]], and not due to falling from a treadmill.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kara|first1=Swisher|title=Full transcript: Sheryl Sandberg talks grief, resilience, and "Option B" on Recode Decode|url=https://www.recode.net/2017/5/8/15573660/transcript-sheryl-sandberg-facebook-grief-resilience-option-b-recode-decode|website=www.recode.net|publisher=Vox Media|accessdate=9 May 2017}}</ref>

Goldberg's brother, Robert, described his family's "incredible shock and sadness," in an online post asking well-wishers to post photos and memories to his [[Facebook]] profile page. "In lieu of donations, we want to celebrate his life in a manner that respects the family's privacy as they cope with this tragic, life-changing event," Robert Goldberg wrote. Tributes to Goldberg were posted to social media across the Silicon Valley community. A spokesperson for Facebook said its executives were "heartbroken by this news." [[Mark Zuckerberg]], Facebook's chief executive, said in a post that Goldberg was "an amazing person and I am glad I got to know him." "One of the truly great people on the planet, Dave was of almost unimaginably remarkable character," said [[Dick Costolo]], [[Twitter]]'s chief executive. "One of kindest and most generous friends I've known," said [[Jeff Weiner]], chief executive of [[LinkedIn]].<ref name=nytimes /><ref>{{cite news|last1=Bradshaw|first1=Tim|title=Silicon Valley entrepreneur David Goldberg dies|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/technology/silicon-valley-entrepreneur-david-goldberg-dies/ar-BBj5pYB?ocid=ansfinancialtimes11|accessdate=May 4, 2015|work=Financial Times|via=MSN.com|date=May 2, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304043204/http://www.msn.com/en-us/news/technology/silicon-valley-entrepreneur-david-goldberg-dies/ar-BBj5pYB?ocid=ansfinancialtimes11|archive-date=March 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Professional poker player [[Phil Hellmuth|Phil Helmuth]], who was a friend, donated his historic 14th World Series of Poker bracelet to Goldberg's family.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pokernews.com/news/2015/06/phil-hellmuth-wins-record-14th-wsop-bracelet-21824.htm|title = Phil Hellmuth Wins Record 14th WSOP Bracelet After Capturing $10K Razz Title|last = Holloway|first = Chad|authorlink = Chad Holloway|date = June 9, 2015|accessdate =12 October 2016}}</ref>

A memorial service for Goldberg was held at [[Stanford Memorial Auditorium]] on the [[Stanford University]] campus on May 5, 2015. The 90-minute service was attended by many members of what ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'' described as "the elite of Silicon Valley," as well as [[U2]] frontman [[Bono]], who performed his song "[[One (U2 song)|One]]," and actor [[Ben Affleck]]. Affleck had worked with Goldberg when he served on the Board of Directors of a company he co-founded called Live Planet and the two had also worked together on The Eastern Congo Initiative, for which Goldberg helped Affleck raise funds.<ref name=FortuneStanford>{{cite news|last1=Lashinsky|title=Tears and laughter at a celebration of Dave Goldberg's life|first1=Adam|url=https://fortune.com/2015/05/05/dave-goldberg-memorial-stanford/|accessdate=May 6, 2015|work=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]|date=May 5, 2015}}</ref>

Sheryl Sandberg, Goldberg's wife, wrote a Facebook post with an essay commemorating Goldberg 30 days after his death ([[sheloshim]] period), talking about her own struggle overcoming the grief of his death and the support that her loved ones had given her in this difficult time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10155617891025177&set=a.404308695176.365039.717545176&type=1&permPage=1|title = Today is the end of sheloshim for my beloved husband|last = Sandberg|first = Sheryl|authorlink = Sheryl Sandberg|date = June 3, 2015|accessdate = June 6, 2015}}</ref> The post was liked by more than 900,000 people, shared 400,000 times, and received over 70,000 comments. It was also discussed in ''[[Business Insider]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/sheryl-sandbergs-essay-on-dave-goldbergs-death-and-grief-2015-6|title = Sheryl Sandberg wrote a beautiful essay about the sudden death of her husband and dealing with grief|website = [[Business Insider]]|date = June 3, 2015|accessdate = June 6, 2015|last = Shontell|first = Alyssa}}</ref> and the ''[[New York Times]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/06/04/sheryl-sandbergs-post-on-late-husband-sets-off-meditations-on-grief/?_r=0|title = Sheryl Sandberg's Post on Late Husband Sets Off Meditations on Grief|date = June 4, 2015|accessdate = June 6, 2015|newspaper = [[New York Times]]|last = Goel|first = Vindu}}</ref>
