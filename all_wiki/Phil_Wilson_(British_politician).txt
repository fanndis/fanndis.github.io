'''Philip Wilson'''<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=59418 |date=13 May 2010 |page=8741}}</ref> (born 31 May 1959) is a British [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] politician. He was elected as [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Sedgefield (UK Parliament constituency)|Sedgefield]]<ref name="times">{{cite news|title=Victory for Blair's aide keeps the flame alive |work=[[The Times]]|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/politics/article2107633.ece |date=20 July 2007 |accessdate=20 July 2007|first=Fran|last=Yeoman}}</ref> in a [[2007 Sedgefield by-election|by-election]] that followed the resignation of [[Tony Blair]], former [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]], from the seat.<ref name="guardian">{{cite news|title=Blair turns out to back Sedgefield candidate |work=[[The Guardian]] |url=http://politics.guardian.co.uk/byelections/story/0,,2118232,00.html |date=4 July 2007 |accessdate=4 July 2007 |first=Hélène|last=Mulholland}}</ref><ref name="local man">{{cite news |title=Phil Wilson – Labour's local man. |work=sedgefield-labour.com |url=http://www.sedgefield-labour.com/phil?PageId=1ab18950-598d-fdd4-7926-68156f231b3f |accessdate=6 July 2007 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071008221947/http://www.sedgefield-labour.com/phil?PageId=1ab18950-598d-fdd4-7926-68156f231b3f |archive-date=8 October 2007 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> He lost the seat at the [[2019 United Kingdom general election|2019 general election]] to [[Paul Howell (MP)|Paul Howell]] of the [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]].<ref name="ge2019">{{cite news|title=Sedgefield parliamentary constituency - Election 2019 | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/politics/constituencies/E14000915|publisher=BBC News|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref>

==Early life and career==
The son of a [[Fishburn]] colliery worker, Wilson has lived in the [[Sedgefield (borough)|Sedgefield]] constituency all of his life.<ref name="local man"/> After attending Trimdon Secondary Modern and Sedgefield Comprehensive School, Wilson became a shop assistant before moving on to be a [[clerical worker]] in the [[British Civil Service|civil service]] where he was a [[trade union|lay-trade union]] official.<ref name="local man"/> Wilson later worked as a gambling lobbyist for the [[Gala Coral Group]] in the lead up to the passing of the [[Gambling Act 2005|2005 Gambling Act]], and as a director at London based [[Public affairs industry|public affairs]] consultancy Fellows’ Associates.<ref name=prweek-20070718>{{cite news |url=http://www.prweek.com/article/672225/wilson-bid-hides-pr-credentials |title=Wilson bid hides PR credentials |publisher=PR Week |date=18 July 2007 |accessdate=22 May 2017}}</ref>

Wilson is known for being one of the "Famous Five", a group of local [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] members who helped a young [[Tony Blair]] get selected as the Labour candidate for Sedgefield for the [[1983 United Kingdom general election|1983 general election]].<ref name="guardian"/> He subsequently worked for [[Tony Blair]] in his constituency office, the Labour Party and a PR company.<ref name="about me">{{cite web|title=About Me|url=http://www.philwilson-labour.co.uk/about-me/486|accessdate=17 March 2015}}</ref>

==Parliamentary career==
Wilson was a Labour [[Whip (politics)|Assistant Whip]] from 2010 to 2015.<ref name=hoc-bio>{{cite web |url=http://www.parliament.uk/biographies/commons/phil-wilson/1603 |title=Phil Wilson |publisher=UK Parliament |work=House of Commons |accessdate=22 May 2017}}</ref> In 2012, he was elected to the [[Progress (organisation)|Progress]] strategy board. In his work for Progress, he was criticised for a blog post that he was accused of plagiarizing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://politicalscrapbook.net/2012/07/phil-wilson-mp-accused-of-plagiarism/|title=Labour whip under fire over ‘plagiarised blog post’ claims|website=Political Scrapbook|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-12-29}}</ref>

In 2013, a letter attributed to Conservative MP [[John Glen (politician)|John Glen]] accused Wilson of failing to properly declare donations from [[Hitachi]] before speaking about the matter in a [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] debate. Glen subsequently accepted that Wilson was not at fault and apologised personally to him, saying the letter had been drafted on his behalf and he had not read it before it was issued.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Merrick|first1=Robert|title=Complaint was "cooked up" by Tories, claim|url=http://www.thenorthernecho.co.uk/news/10279522._Sleaze__complaint_against_Sedgefield_MP__Phil_Wilson__collapses/|accessdate=17 March 2015|publisher=Northern Echo|date=11 March 2013}}</ref> In his biography on his personal website, Wilson states that bringing a Hitachi Rail factory project to [[Newton Aycliffe]] in his constituency represents his "proudest political achievement so far".<ref name="about me" />

In his [[2017 United Kingdom general election|2017 general election]] voter leaflet, Wilson stated he was not a supporter of Labour leader [[Jeremy Corbyn]], and suggested Labour would not win the election.<ref name=telegraph-20170522>{{cite news|last=Horton |first=Helena|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/05/22/labour-candidate-disowns-jeremy-corbyn-leaflet-tells-constituents/ |title=Labour candidate disowns Jeremy Corbyn on his leaflet as he tells constituents he is 'no supporter' |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=22 May 2017 |accessdate=22 May 2017}}</ref> He had supported [[Owen Smith]] in the failed attempt to replace [[Jeremy Corbyn]] in the [[2016 Labour Party leadership election (UK)|2016 Labour Party leadership election]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/mps-nominating-owen-smith-labour-8451186|title=Which MPs are nominating Owen Smith in the Labour leadership contest?|last1=Smith|first1=Mikey|date=20 July 2016|work=Mirror|access-date=10 November 2018|last2=Bloom|first2=Dan}}</ref>

Wilson has called for a second referendum on [[Brexit]]. He said "Before we retreat to the past, people should be given another chance to decide whether that is where they want to end up".<ref name=bbc-20180510>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-tees-44066677 |title=North East Labour MPs call for second EU vote |work=BBC News |date=10 May 2018 |accessdate=17 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/news/north-east-news/leave-campaign-cheated-win-eu-14919038|title=Leave campaign 'cheated' its way to victory - now MPs want Brexit to be scrapped|first=Jonathan|last=Walker|date=17 July 2018|website=nechronicle}}</ref> He believes that there were "facts that weren’t put before the British people during the referendum campaign".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenorthernecho.co.uk/news/15907346.Phil_Wilson_on_Brexit_analysis___quot_Facts_weren__39_t_put_before_British_people_quot_/ |title=Phil Wilson on Brexit analysis: "Facts weren't put before British people" |publisher=The Northern Echo |date=30 January 2018 |accessdate=2018-07-30}}</ref> Later he supported the proposal to join the [[European Economic Area]] to mitigate the perceived disadvantages of Brexit.<ref name=labourlist-20180611>{{cite web |url=https://labourlist.org/2018/06/phil-wilson-ill-be-backing-the-eea-the-only-available-brexit-option-to-minimise-risk/ |title=I'll be backing the EEA – the only available Brexit option to minimise risk |last=Wilson |first=Phil |publisher=LabourList |date=11 June 2018 |accessdate=17 September 2018}}</ref> Wilson's constituency of Sedgefield voted to Leave the European Union by 59.4%.<ref name="Sedgefield EU Referendum vote">{{cite web |url=https://inews.co.uk/news/politics/every-leave-constituency-with-a-remain-mp/ |title=Every Leave constituency where the MP voted Remain |last=News |first=I |publisher=I News |date=12 May 2017 |accessdate=10 October 2017}}</ref>
