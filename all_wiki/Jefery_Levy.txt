'''Jefery Levy''' (born May 21, 1958)<ref>{{cite book |last=Roberts |first=Jerry |date=June 5, 2009 |title=Encyclopedia of Television Film Directors |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kW8j6sHvrewC&pg=PA335&dq=jefery+levy+1958&hl=ru&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwib5aSt6IbPAhVKFCwKHR_3BuAQ6AEIIDAA#v=onepage&q=jefery%20levy%201958&f=false |location= |publisher=Scarecrow Press|page=335 |isbn=978-0810861381}}</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[film]] and [[television]] [[film director|director]], [[Film producer|producer]], and [[writer]] based in [[Beverly Hills]], [[California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/home/la-lh-levy-set-piece-20141120-story.html|title=Set Pieces: Jefery Levy's home sets the stage for 'Me' and 'The Key'|author=Los Angeles Times|date=20 November 2014|work=latimes.com}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1985, while a graduate student at UCLA, Levy wrote and produced the low-budget hit [[Ghoulies]], a horror-comedy film featuring small, demonic creatures.<ref name="joshual.weinstein">{{cite web|url=https://www.thewrap.com/ghoulies-producer-writer-jefery-levy-launches-production-company-33208/#.dpuf|title='Ghoulies' Writer-Producer Jefery Levy Launches Production Company|author=Joshua L. Weinstein|date=30 November 2011|work=TheWrap}}</ref> He has produced and directed dozens of TV and film projects over a span of three decades and is most recently known for the 2015 film ''ME'', which he directed, produced, wrote, and starred in.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://whatsuphollywood.com/2014/11/18/exclusive-interview-of-jefery-levy-and-david-arquette-from-the-key-and-me/|title=Exclusive interview of Jefery Levy and David Arquette from "The Key" and "Me" - What's Up Hollywood|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://flaunt.com/art/me-film/|title=Flaunt Magazine - Art: How Should a Film ME?*|work=Flaunt Magazine}}</ref>

In 2012, Levy created the production company X MARKS THE EARTH, a micro budget film studio with the vision of converting small budget films into television shows.<ref name="joshual.weinstein"/><ref name="xmarkstheearth.com">{{cite web|url=http://xmarkstheearth.com/about/|title=About|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150526132019/http://xmarkstheearth.com/about/|archivedate=2015-05-26}}</ref>

===''The Key''===
Levy's 2014 film adaption of the novel ''The Key'' by the japanese writer [[Junichiro Tanizaki]] premiered at the Real Experiment Film Festival at the Laemmle Music Hall Theater in Beverly Hills.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ireport.cnn.com/docs/DOC-1194710|title=Jefery Levy's THE KEY. A review|work=CNN iReport}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://filmcourage.com/2014/11/25/jefery-levy-directs-the-key-starring-david-arquette-and-bai-ling-an-erotic-exploration-of-marriage-and-lust-playing-soon/|title=Jefery Levy Directs THE KEY - Starring David Arquette and Bai Ling - An Erotic Exploration of Marriage and Lust - Playing Soon! - Film Courage|author=Filmcourage|date=25 November 2014|work=Film Courage}}</ref> ''[[The Key (2014 film)|The Key]]'' explores the twisted sexual life and marriage of a [[Los Angeles]] couple through their private journals in an explicit cinematic portrayal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jeferylevy.com/films/the-key/|title=The Key|publisher=}}</ref>

==Teaching appointments==
From 1991 to 1996, Levy served as the youngest Associate Professor at USC School of Cinema/Television. Levy was also a faculty member of [[The American Film Institute]] (where he taught the master directing class from 1995-1996).<ref name="xmarkstheearth.com"/>
