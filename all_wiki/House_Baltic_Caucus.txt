The '''House Baltic Caucus''' is a [[bipartisan]] registered [[Caucuses of the United States Congress|Caucus of the House of Representatives]] since its inception in 1997 and is composed of members from both the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] and [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] Parties. The members of the House Baltic Caucus have a strong interest in promoting opportunities to strengthen the economic, political, and cultural relationships of the [[United States of America]] with [[Estonia]], [[Latvia]], and [[Lithuania]]. [[File:House Baltic Caucus.png|thumb|House Baltic Caucus]]

== Purpose ==
* maintain strong relationships with [[Estonia]], [[Latvia]], and [[Lithuania]];
* promote [[democratic principles]] and [[human rights]];
* assist in strengthening [[Free market|free market economies]] in Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania; 
* work to support legislation bolstering the [[Defense (military)|defense]] of the [[Baltic countries]].

== Membership ==
As of May 21, 2019 the House Baltic Caucus has 68 members<ref>[http://housebalticcaucus.webs.com/members.htm], Members of the House Baltic Caucus</ref> from both the Democratic and Republican Parties. The House Baltic Caucus is led by Congressmen [[John Shimkus]] (R-IL) and [[Adam Schiff]] (D-CA).
