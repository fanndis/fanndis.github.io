'''Vintage Books''' is a trade paperback publishing [[imprint (trade name)|imprint]] established in 1954 by [[Alfred A. Knopf]].

The company was purchased by [[Random House]] publishing in April 1960, and is a subdivision of Random House. In 1990, Vintage UK was set up in the [[United Kingdom]]. After Random House merged with [[Bantam Books|Bantam]] [[Doubleday (publisher)|Doubleday]] [[Dell Publishing|Dell]], Doubleday's Anchor Books trade paperback line was added to the same division as Vintage.<ref>{{Cite news| issn = 0362-4331| last = Carvajal| first = Doreen| title = Bertelsmann Is Reorganizing Random House| work = The New York Times| accessdate = 2019-10-14| date = 1999-05-28| url = https://www.nytimes.com/1999/05/28/business/bertelsmann-is-reorganizing-random-house.html}}</ref>

Vintage began publishing some mass-market paperback books in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Doubleday, Knopf Form Mass Market Line| work = PublishersWeekly.com| accessdate = 2019-10-14| url = https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/print/20031215/36311-doubleday-knopf-form-mass-market-line.html}}</ref>
