<!-- This capitalization is correct, see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Albums#Capitalization -->'''''Liebe ist für alle da''''' ({{IPA-de|ˈliːbə ʔɪst fyːɐ̯ ˈʔalə daː|lang}}, "Love is there for everyone") is the sixth studio album by German [[Neue Deutsche Härte]] band [[Rammstein]]. It was released through [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]] in Europe on 16 October 2009, the United Kingdom on 19 October 2009, and the United States on 20 October 2009.<ref name="officialblog">[http://www.rammstein.de/blog/lang/en/ Rammstein Official Website News Page]. Rammstein.de. Retrieved 15 August 2009.</ref> 

This would be the band's last album for nearly a decade, until the release of [[Untitled Rammstein album|their untitled follow-up album]] in May 2019.

==Promotion and release==
The album title was confirmed on 1 September 2009, in a promotional video<ref>[http://www.thegauntlet.com/article/307/16750/Rammstein-releases-porno.html Promotional video]. Thegauntlet.com</ref> for the new single, "[[Pussy (Rammstein song)|Pussy]]", released on 18 September 2009, in the European Union and 19 September 2009, in the United States. The single's [[B-side]] was "Rammlied".<ref>[http://www.thegauntlet.com/article/307/16941/New-Rammstein-song-posted.html The Gauntlet – New Rammstein song posted] TheGauntlet.com</ref> The video for "Pussy" was released on adult website Visit-X, two days before the single. The video features scenes of explicit male and female nudity as well as the band members (played by body doubles in the nude scenes) engaging in sexual activity with women. In July 2009, a promo version of the title track, <!-- This capitalization is correct, see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Albums#Capitalization -->"Liebe ist für alle da", [[internet leak|leaked]] onto the internet, along with various promotional materials. Pictures taken throughout the recording of the album were later made available on Rammstein's official website. The album was released on 16 October 2009, in Europe and 20 October in the U.S.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Gauntlet - Rammstein unveil new single and release date|url=http://www.thegauntlet.com/article/307/16755/Rammstein-unveil-new-single-and-release-date.html|website=www.thegauntlet.com|access-date=2020-05-07}}</ref>

[[Paul Landers]] stated that tracks which did not make it on the album would appear as B-sides on singles and that there would be a special edition of the album with five bonus tracks
{{citation needed|date=July 2020}} On 8 November 2009, Rammstein played their first show for their "Liebe ist für alle da" tour in [[Lisbon]], Portugal, their first live show in four years. As announced, a video was shot for the album's third single, "[[Haifisch]]", which premiered on the band's MySpace page on 23 April 2010. "Waidmanns Heil" is a playable track in the rhythm video game ''[[Guitar Hero: Warriors of Rock]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.plasticaxe.com/2010/06/04/guitar-hero-warriors-of-rock-the-set-list-so-far/ |title=Guitar Hero: Warriors of Rock setlist so far |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100620010536/http://www.plasticaxe.com/2010/06/04/guitar-hero-warriors-of-rock-the-set-list-so-far/ |archivedate=20 June 2010 }}PlasticAxe.com</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://multiplayerblog.mtv.com/2010/06/04/guitar-hero-warriors-of-rock-track-list-fills-out/|title=Guitar Hero: Warriors of Rock setlist fills out}}Multiplayerblog. MTV</ref> In 2013, the song "Führe mich" was featured in [[Lars von Trier]]'s film ''[[Nymphomaniac (film)|Nymphomaniac]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.empireonline.com/empireblogs/words-from-the-wise/post/p1437|title=Lars Von Trier's Nymphomaniac: First Look}}EmpireOnline.com</ref>

==Commercial performance==
''Liebe ist für alle da'' was certified platinum in Finland before its release, solely from pre-release sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rocksins.com/2009/10/rammstein-liebe-ist-fur-alle-da-platinum-in-finland-975/ |title=Rammstein Liebe Ist Fur Alle Da Platinum in Finland |publisher=Rock Sins |accessdate=8 August 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715200303/http://www.rocksins.com/2009/10/rammstein-liebe-ist-fur-alle-da-platinum-in-finland-975/ |archivedate=15 July 2011 }}</ref> It debuted at number 3 in the French charts,<ref>{{cite web|title=Music News, Reviews, Articles, Information, News Online & Free Music {{!}} ''Billboard''|url=http://www.billboard.com/#/charts/france-albums?chartDate=2009-10-31|work=Billboard|accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref> making it Rammstein's most commercially successful album in France to date.{{Citation needed|date=August 2011}} Furthermore, the album entered at number 13 on the US charts, making it Rammstein's highest-charting album in the United States, although it subsequently fell off the US albums chart after four weeks. In Germany, the album entered the charts at number 1 after the best start of a music album in 2009<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.blogspan.net/5595-rammstein-sturmen-auf-platz-eins-der-album-charts.html |title=Rammstein stürmen auf Platz eins der Album-Charts – Rang, Position, Naidoo, Platz, Michelle, Xavier, Sechs, Alles, Rammstein, Single-Charts, Miley, Humanoid |publisher=Blogspan.Net |date=27 October 2009 |accessdate=8 August 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723032124/http://www.blogspan.net/5595-rammstein-sturmen-auf-platz-eins-der-album-charts.html |archive-date=23 July 2011 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> and beating popular German R&B-singer [[Xavier Naidoo]]'s new album "Alles kann besser werden". It remained high in the charts until it was censored.

==Censorship==
The album was added to the index of the [[Bundesprüfstelle für jugendgefährdende Medien]] (Federal Department for Media Harmful to Minors) in Germany, due to the track "[[Ich tu dir weh]]" and a picture inside the album booklet displaying [[Richard Kruspe]] spanking a nude woman.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://herzeleid.com/en/news |title=Rammstein News &#124; Herzeleid.com &#124; The number one source for Rammstein |publisher=Herzeleid.com |accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref> This meant only adults could purchase the album; additionally, it could not be displayed in stores that are accessible for minors (see [[BPjM#Legal consequences|BPjM]] for details).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.welt.de/kultur/article5111714/Bundespruefstelle-setzt-Rammstein-Album-auf-Index.html |title="Liebe ist für alle da": Bundesprüfstelle setzt Rammstein-Album auf Index – Nachrichten Kultur – WELT ONLINE |language=de |work=Die Welt |accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref> As a consequence, the album was re-released in Germany on 16 November in an edited version without the song and the picture.<ref name="Censored"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.de/dp/B002VWJU5Q |title=Liebe Ist für Alle Da (Zensierte Version): Rammstein: Amazon.de: Musik |accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref> The band still played the [[instrumental]] version song live in Germany until it was also banned from live performances.

Despite the censorship, the video for "Ich tu dir weh" was released on 21 December 2009, on adult website Visit-X, after advertisement on the band's official website. Any references have since been deleted, presumably following German law prohibiting advertisement for media on the "index".

On 31 May 2010 the administrative court in [[Cologne]] has decided to waive the suspensive effect of the censorship.<ref>https://dejure.org/dienste/vernetzung/rechtsprechung?Gericht=VG%20K%F6ln&Datum=31.05.2010&Aktenzeichen=22%20L%201899%2F09</ref> The German department deleted the record from the censorship lists on 1 June.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rammsteinworld.com/actualite-466-suppression-censure-liebe-ist-fur-alle-da |title=Suppression de la censure de Liebe ist für alle da |publisher=Rammstein World |date=16 October 2009 |accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref> On 9 June the band announced on their official website that the original version of the album was available at their shop and that a release of the single "Ich tu dir weh" in Germany was planned in a short period of time.<ref>{{cite web|title="ICH TU DIR WEH" &laquo; Rammstein|url=http://www.rammstein.de/news/ich-tu-dir-weh/|accessdate=9 August 2011|language=German}}</ref> The single was eventually released on 5 February 2010, and in the UK on 15 February 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rammsteinworld.com/actualite-442-single-ich-tu-dir-weh-toutes-les-infos |title=17 January 2010 |publisher=Rammsteinworld.com |date=16 October 2009 |accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref>
