'''Plan B Entertainment, Inc.''',<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.manta.com/c/mtvttgs/plan-b-entertainment-inc | title=Plan B Entertainment, Inc. | publisher=Manta}}</ref> more commonly known as '''Plan B''', is an American [[production company]] founded in November in 2001 by [[Brad Grey]], [[Brad Pitt]] and [[Jennifer Aniston]]. In 2005, after Pitt and Aniston divorced, Grey became the CEO of [[Paramount Pictures]] and Pitt became the sole owner of the company. The president of the company was for many years [[Dede Gardner]], but she and Pitt named [[Jeremy Kleiner]] co-president with Gardner in 2013.<ref>{{cite journal | last=Kroll | first=Justin | url=https://variety.com/2013/film/news/jeremy-kleiner-tapped-as-co-president-of-plan-b-entertainment-1200486686/ | title=Jeremy Kleiner Tapped as Co-President of Plan B Entertainment | journal=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | date=May 22, 2013 }}</ref> Three of the production company's movies, ''[[The Departed]]'', ''[[12 Years a Slave (film)|12 Years a Slave]]'' and ''[[Moonlight (2016 film)|Moonlight]]'', have won the [[Academy Award for Best Picture]]. 

Pitt, Gardner and Kleiner received the [[Producers Guild of America]]'s [[David O. Selznick Achievement Award in Theatrical Motion Pictures]] in 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hipes |first1=Patrick |title=Plan B Trio To Receive PGA’s David O. Selznick Award |url=https://deadline.com/2019/12/plan-b-pga-david-o-selznick-award-brad-pitt-dede-gardner-jeremy-kleiner-1202807788/ |website=Deadline |accessdate=13 February 2020 |date=December 12, 2019}}</ref>
