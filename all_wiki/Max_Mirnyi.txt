'''Maksim''' '''Mikalaevich''' "'''Max'''" '''Mirnyi''' ({{lang-be|Максім Мікалаевіч Мірны}}, {{IPA-be|makˈsʲim ˈmʲirnɨ|pron}}; {{lang-ru|Максим Николаевич Мирный}}, {{IPA-ru|mɐkˈsʲim ˈmʲirnɨj|pron}}; born 6 July 1977)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maxmirnyi.com/bio.html|title=Mrnyi/Nestor beat Bryans for queen’s title; Qureshi/Rojer capture halle trophy – Max Mirnyi}}</ref> is a [[Belarussians|Belarusian]] former professional [[tennis]] player.

Mirnyi became a doubles specialist following his singles career, in which he reached a career-high of World No. 18 (August 2003) and finished in the top 50 in the world for seven straight years despite only winning one ATP singles title (2003 Rotterdam Open). He represented Belarus in Davis Cup competition from April 1994, where he holds a record of 47 wins and 27 losses in 35 ties played. He reached the [[List of ATP number 1 ranked doubles players|World No. 1 doubles ranking]] in June 2003 and won ten Grand Slam titles: Men's Doubles in the 2000 and 2002 [[US Open (tennis)|US Open]] and the 2005, 2006, 2011 and 2012 [[French Open]]; and mixed doubles in the 1998, 2007, and 2013 U.S. Opens and 1998 [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]].

At the [[2012 Summer Olympics]] in London, Mirnyi carried the flag of Belarus at the Opening Ceremony on 27 July 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://the-slice.com/2012/07/mirnyi-carries-belarus-flag-at-london-2012-opening-ceremony/|title=Mirnyi Carries Belarus Flag At London 2012 Opening Ceremony |accessdate=2012-07-27}}</ref> and won the gold medal in the Mixed Doubles with [[Victoria Azarenka]] on 5 August 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/london-olympics-2012/news/London-Olympics-Mirnyi-Azarenka-win-mixed-doubles-gold/articleshow/15367203.cms|title=London Olympics: Mirnyi, Azarenka win mixed doubles gold|accessdate=2012-08-05}}</ref>

==Career==
Mirnyi won the [[1998 US Open (tennis)|1998 US Open]] and [[1998 Wimbledon]] along with [[Serena Williams]], and lost the final of [[1999 Australian Open]]. His best performance at a Grand Slam in singles came when he got to the quarterfinals of the [[2002 US Open – Men's Singles|2002 US Open]], defeating [[Attila Sávolt]], [[Antony Dupuis]], [[Dominik Hrbatý]] and [[Roger Federer]] before losing to [[Andre Agassi]]. In the 2004 [[Davis Cup]], he and [[Vladimir Voltchkov]] defeated Russia 3–2 and [[Argentina]] 5–0, reaching the semifinals, where they lost to the United States. In 2006, he scored an upset against [[James Blake (tennis)|James Blake]] at Wimbledon. That same year, he and [[Jonas Björkman]] defeated the [[Bryan brothers]] in men's doubles at the [[French Open]]. In 2011, he and Daniel Nestor defeated [[Juan Sebastián Cabal]] and [[Eduardo Schwank]] at the [[French Open. In 2012, he and Daniel Nestor defeated the [[Bryan brothers]], both at the Queen's Club, UK,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/business/daniel-nestor-max-mirnyi-win-doubles-title-at-queen-s-club-1.1203909|title=Daniel Nestor, Max Mirnyi win doubles title at Queen's Club|website=CBC News|access-date=2016-06-22}}</ref> and again at the French Open.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.nationalpost.com/sports/daniel-nestor-max-mirnyi-repeat-as-french-open-doubles-champions-2|title=Daniel Nestor, Max Mirnyi repeat as French Open doubles champions|website=National Post|access-date=2016-06-22}}</ref> {{citation needed|date=July 2014}}
