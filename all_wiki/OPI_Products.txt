'''OPI Products''', branded as '''O·P·I''', is an [[United States|American]] [[nail polish]] manufacturer headquartered in [[Calabasas, California|Calabasas]], [[California]] and a subsidiary of [[Coty, Inc.]]

== History ==
OPI, originally named '''Odontorium Products Inc.''', was a small dental supply company purchased by George Schaeffer in 1981.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Behind The Brand: OPI|url=http://www.thebeautyinformer.com/2017/05/opi-brand-history.html|access-date=2020-08-24|website=The Beauty Informer}}</ref>  Shortly after taking over the company, Schaeffer was joined by Hungarian-born<ref>{{cite news|url=https://jewishnews.timesofisrael.com/meet-the-hungarian-jewish-woman-behind-the-worlds-largest-luxury-nail-brand/|title=Meet the Hungarian Jewish woman behind the world’s largest luxury nail brand|date=Aug 29, 2019|newspaper=[[Jewish News]]|accessdate=2019-11-01}}</ref> [[Suzi Weiss-Fischmann]], OPI's Executive Vice President and Artistic Director. Schaeffer and Weiss-Fischmann partnered with R. Eric Montgomery, a biochemist, and created an acrylic system that Schaeffer sold door-to-door to local nail salons. They closed the dental sales and focused entirely on nail products changing the name to OPI Products Inc.

In 1989, OPI expanded its portfolio to nail lacquers and later other products. In 2003, OPI created a ''[[Legally Blonde 2]]'' collection that was also featured in the film.<ref name="Legally">{{cite news|last=Lu-Lien Tan|first=Cheryl|title=Clothes Encounters; As the fashion world grows more aggressive about marketing clothes in movies, some wonder if hot films are beginning to resemble stylish ads|date=2003-07-06|work=The Baltimore Sun|page=1.N}}</ref> Collaborations that have followed include [[Ford Mustang]], the 2010 film [[Alice in Wonderland (2010 film)|''Alice in Wonderland'']],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thegloss.com/beauty/opi-goes-through-the-looking-glass/|title=OPI goes through the looking glass|last=Fierro|first=Dina|date=2009-12-24|publisher=The Gloss|accessdate=2010-12-10}}</ref> and Dell (2009).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/idUS144156+07-Oct-2009+BW20091007/|title=Industry Leaders Dell and OPI Partner to Deliver a New, Polished Laptop Collection|date=2009-10-07|publisher=Reuters|accessdate=2010-12-10}}</ref>

In 2003, OPI launched a nail polish line for dogs called Pawlish that received mixed reviews.<ref name="Pawlish">{{cite news|last=Donaldson-Evans|first=Catherine|url=http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,98000,00.html|title=Pups Panting for "Pawlished" Nails|date=2003-09-23|work=Fox News|accessdate=2010-12-20}}</ref> In 2007, after pressure from the EPA and the organization [[Campaign for Safe Cosmetics]], OPI reformulated its nail lacquers and eliminated chemicals [[Dibutyl phthalate|DBP]] (dibutyl phthalate), formaldehyde and toluene.<ref>/ [http://blog.seattlepi.com/environment/library/OPILetterToAlexandraGormanMarch2007.pdf statement]</ref>

In 2010, OPI was acquired by [[Coty, Inc.]]<ref>[http://www.opi.com/fb/OPIAcquiredPressRelease.pdf joint statement]</ref> In 2014, Coty appointed Mary van Praag as General Manager of OPI.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.modernsalon.com/news/salon-industry-news/OPI-Founder-and-CEO-George-Schaeffer-to-Step-Down-233323821.html|title=OPI Founder and CEO George Schaeffer to Step Down|last1=Moratto|first1=Anne|website=Modern Salon}}</ref> Van Praag  replaced John Heffner, the now CEO of Drybar.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.modernsalon.com/news/salon-industry-news/OPI-Founder-and-CEO-George-Schaeffer-to-Step-Down-233323821.html|title=OPI Founder and CEO George Schaeffer to Step Down|last1=Moratto|first1=Anne|website=Modern Salon}}</ref>
