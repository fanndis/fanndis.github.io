'''New Delhi Television Limited''' ('''NDTV''') is an Indian television media company founded in 1988 by [[Radhika Roy]], a journalist. NDTV started off as a content provider for [[Doordarshan]], producing the show ''The World This Week'' in November 1988. In 1998, NDTV started its own [[24/7 service|24&times;7 news channel]] with STAR and later shifted to its own venture in 2003, launching both the English and Hindi versions of the channel. NDTV's slogan is "Experience. Truth First".

== History ==
NDTV started out as a content provider company for the public broadcaster Doordarshan.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Batabyal|first1=Somnath|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=k6FlAgAAQBAJ&q=ndtv&pg=PT27|title=Indian Mass Media and the Politics of Change|last2=Chowdhry|first2=Angad|last3=Gaur|first3=Meenu|last4=Pohjonen|first4=Matti|date=11 April 2013|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-136-19665-2|language=en}}</ref> It started off in November 1988 by producing the 30-minute weekly news show ''The World This Week'' for [[Doordarshan]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite book|last=Shrivastava|first=K. M.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Tpt8knkqUsoC&q=ndtv&pg=PA36|title=Broadcast Journalism|publisher=Sterling Publishers Pvt. Ltd|isbn=978-81-207-3597-2|pages=36{{ndash}}37|language=en}}</ref> In 1989, NDTV created India's first live coverage and analysis of the [[1989 Indian general election|general elections]]. In 1995 NDTV produced a national news show, "''Tonight''", which was telecast on Doordarshan.<ref name=":0" />

In 1998 NDTV started India's first 24x7 news show in collaboration with [[Rupert Murdoch]]'s STAR News Channel.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":0" /> During the initial years there was always a delay of 10 minutes which later shifted to five minutes between telecast and production of live news due to government restrictions.<ref name=":0" /> In 1999, NDTV launched its website.<ref name=":1" />

==Controversies==
===Corruption and criminal conspiracy===
On 20 January 1998, [[Central Bureau of Investigation]] filed various cases against New Delhi Television (NDTV) managing director Prannoy Roy, former director general of [[Doordarshan]] R. Basu and five other top officials of Doordarshan under Section 120-B of the [[Indian Penal Code]] (IPC) for criminal conspiracy and under the Prevention of Corruption Act. According to the [[Central Bureau of Investigation]] (CBI) charge-sheet, Doordarshan suffered a loss of over {{INRConvert|35.2|m|year=1998|lk=r}} due to the "undue favours" shown to NDTV as its programme ''The World This Week'' (''TWTW'') was put in 'A' category instead of 'special A' category.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://zoomindianmedia.wordpress.com/2011/02/14/ndtv-and-prannoy-roy-once-upon-a-time/ |title=NDTV and Prannoy Roy â€“ Once Upon a Time " Zoom Indian Media |publisher=Zoomindianmedia.wordpress.com |date=14 February 2011 |access-date=26 May 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.outlookindia.com/article.aspx?204285 |title=How Zee Is Shooting Star &#124; ===Allegations of Tax evasion===
NDTV, through its foreign subsidiaries have allegedly violated numerous Indian tax and corporate laws.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sunday-guardian.com/a/1088 |title=NDTV juggles funds, shares abroad, avoids tax|work=[[The Sunday Guardian]]|date=5 December 2010 |access-date=6 December 2010}}</ref> NDTV has denied these allegations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://videos.indiannewsandviews.com/watch_inavXFkd_zNSE6Uinav_NDTV_Frauds_Book_Release_function_at_the_Constitution_Club,_New_Delhi_on_March_27,_2017 |title=NDTV Frauds Book Release function at the Constitution Club, New Delhi on March 27, 2017 |access-date=10 January 2018}} [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XFkd_zNSE6U Video on YouTube (1h 12m)]</ref>

''[[The Sunday Guardian]]'', a centre-left newspaper ran a story which exposed the NDTV's financial misdemeanours and malpractices in connivance with ICICI Bank.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sunday-guardian.com/a/1082|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110202102749/http://www.sunday-guardian.com/a/1082|title=NDTV-ICICI loan chicanery saved Roys|archive-date=2 February 2011|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref> It provides details of how NDTV's major stakeholders raised funds by misdeclaration of the value of shares in NDTV. NDTV CEO claimed<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sunday-guardian.com/a/1083|title=NDTV CEO gives reply, Guardian responds|date=8 December 2010|work=The Sunday Guardian|access-date=5 February 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101208060733/http://www.sunday-guardian.com/a/1083|archive-date=8 December 2010}}</ref> to the ''Sunday Guardian'' along with the threat of "criminal defamation". On 5 June 2017, the house of Prannoy Roy was raided by NIA and CBI.

On 19 November 2015, the [[Enforcement Directorate|ED]] served {{INRConvert|20.3|b}} notice to NDTV for alleged violations under the [[Foreign Exchange Management Act|FEMA]] act,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/ed-serves-rs-2030-crore-notice-on-ndtv-for-fema-violations/article7900384.ece?homepage=true |title=ED serves Rs 2,030 crore notice on NDTV for FEMA violations|newspaper=[[The Hindu]]|date=20 November 2015|last1=Pandey|first1=Devesh K.}}</ref> however the company said it has been advised that the allegations, while true are not "legally tenable".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/india/india-news-india/ndtv-gets-ed-notice-for-alleged-fema-violations/ |title=NDTV gets ED notice for alleged FEMA violations|publisher=[[The Indian Express]]|date=20 November 2015}}</ref>

The "Income Tax Appellate Tribunal" (ITAT) has upheld an income tax department finding that promoters of NDTV used their own shell companies to round-trip investments of {{INRConvert|6.42|b}} during 2009{{ndash}}10, making them liable for recovery of tax and penalty.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/ndtv-round-tripped-rs-642-crore-via-shell-companies-in-2009-2010-tribunal/articleshow/59719501.cms|title=NDTV round-tripped Rs 642 crore via shell companies in 2009-2010: Tribunal|publisher=[[The Times of India]]|date=23 July 2017}}</ref>

Regarding an investment by the US TV network, NBC (an 80% subsidiary of GE at the time) into NDTV Networks of $150&nbsp;million, both NDTV and NBC/GE insist was an absolutely legal and bona fide investment while the IT department claims it is an illegal transaction. NDTV has called it the Indian Authorities variant of "McCarthyism" to suppress the media.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.in/2016/03/29/prannoy-roy-mccarthyism_n_9560526.html|title=NDTV's Prannoy Roy Says India Has Entered An Era Of McCarthyism|date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

===Commonwealth Games contract===
On 5 August 2011, [[Comptroller and Auditor General of India]]'s report on [[XIX Commonwealth Games]] was tabled in [[Parliament of India]]. In section 14.4.2 of the report, CAG alleged that while awarding contracts worth {{INRConvert|37.8|m}} for production and broadcast of commercials to promote CWG-2010 to NDTV and CNN-IBN, the Commonwealth Games Organizing Committee followed an arbitrary approach. Proposals were considered in an ad-hoc manner, as and when a proposal was received; no form of competitive tendering was adopted. The CAG further said in its report that, "We had no assurance about the competitiveness of the rates quoted by these channels and the need and usefulness of these proposals. From March 2010 to June 2010, the entire pre games publicity and sponsorship publicity was done only on NDTV & CNN-IBN."<ref>{{cite web|title=CAG Report on XIX Commonwealth Games|url=http://saiindia.gov.in/english/home/Our_Products/Audit_Report/Government_Wise/union_audit/recent_reports/union_performance/2011_2012/Civil_%20Performance_Audits/Report_No_6_CWG/CWG%20English%20-%20Part-1.pdf|publisher=Comptroller & Auditor General of India (Pdf)|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120131103116/http://saiindia.gov.in/english/home/Our_Products/Audit_Report/Government_Wise/union_audit/recent_reports/union_performance/2011_2012/Civil_%20Performance_Audits/Report_No_6_CWG/CWG%20English%20-%20Part-1.pdf|archive-date=31 January 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=CAG blames top media houses in Commonwealth Games Scam|url=http://www.newsofdelhi.com/featured-post/cag-report-names-ndtvcnn-ibnhindustantimes-in-cwg-scam|newspaper=News of Delhi|access-date=25 January 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120106011632/http://www.newsofdelhi.com/featured-post/cag-report-names-ndtvcnn-ibnhindustantimes-in-cwg-scam|archive-date=6 January 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Games contracts to media houses arbitrary and biased: CAG|url=http://indiatoday.intoday.in/story/games-contracts-to-media-houses-arbitrary-and-biased-cag-report/1/147735.html|newspaper=[[India Today]]}}</ref>

===Suit against TAM India===
{{Importance section|date=March 2018}}
In 2012, NDTV alleged that TV rating measurement company TAM had indulged in malpractice of influencing and generating favourable TV ratings for their channel. They claimed that two TAM representatives had met NDTV staff in April with an offer to fix the TV ratings in 2{{ndash}}3 weeks by bribing viewers in homes (which otherwise are secret) where the measuring meters were installed. NDTV claimed that this malpractice had resulted in enormous losses for the company over several years and filed a case against TAM, TAM India and its global parent firms for $1.39&nbsp;billion in the Supreme Court of New York.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/article3708627.ece|title= NDTV says TV ratings manipulated | newspaper= [[The Hindu]]| date= 1 August 2012}}</ref> Subsequently, in 2014, the New York Supreme Court dismissed NDTV's petition citing that since it was based on activities and impact in India, NDTV needed to pursue the case in India instead of the US.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.firstpost.com/business/ndtv-tam-imbroglio-highlights-of-ny-court-judgment-663121.html|title=NDTV-TAM imbroglio: highlights of NY Court judgment|website=Firstpost|access-date=18 April 2019}}</ref>

===One-day ban===
On 4 November 2016, the Union Ministry of Information and Broadcasting ordered the channel to go off air on 9 November for allegedly divulging 'strategically sensitive' details while covering [[2016 Pathankot attack|January's deadly attack on the Pathankot air base]].<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.ndtv.com/india-news/withdraw-ban-on-ndtv-india-straight-away-say-editors-journalists-1621400|title= Editors say BAN violation of freedom of media | newspaper= NDTV| date= 4 November 2016}}</ref> Government scrapped their decision on the ban for reasons not made clear.

===2008 loan case===
On 5 June 2017, the CBI raided premises of channel promoters Prannoy and Radika Roy, accusing it of "causing an alleged loss" to [[ICICI Bank]] of {{INRConvert|480|m}}.<ref name="tnyt1">{{cite news|last1=Berry|first1=Ellen|title=Raids in India Target Founders of News Outlet Critical of Government|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/05/world/asia/india-ndtv-raids-narendra-modi-prannoy-roy.html|access-date=5 June 2017|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=5 June 2017}}</ref> The complainant, Sanjay Dutt, Director of Quantum Securities Pvt. Ltd., alleged that the Roys took a loan of around {{INRConvert|3.50|b}} from ICICI in 2008{{ndash}}09, putting up their shareholdings in the company at an inflated value as [[collateral (finance)|collateral]]. He added that while the amount was repaid the following year, the accrued interest of {{INRConvert|500|m}} remained unpaid.<ref>{{cite web|title=First Information Report|url=http://cbi.nic.in/firs/2017/2017_pdf/2017_ac2_firs/RC2172017A0009.pdf|work=Central Bureau of Investigation|publisher=cbi.nic.in|access-date=5 June 2017|date=2 June 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180404064042/http://cbi.nic.in/firs/2017/2017_pdf/2017_ac2_firs/RC2172017A0009.pdf|archive-date=4 April 2018}}</ref> In its response, NDTV denied any "default on any loan to ICICI or any other bank", while attaching a picture of the bank's 2009 letter that confirmed that the "entire amount due," amounting to {{INRConvert|3.75|m}}, had "been repaid in full."<ref>{{cite web|title=The NDTV Saga and Indian Journalism|url=http://www.anupamtimes.com/2017/06/the-ndtv-saga-and-indias-journalism.html|publisher=Anupamtimes|access-date=7 June 2017}}</ref> It added that, "No matter how much the politicians attack us - We will not give up the fight for freedom and the independence of media in India."<ref>{{cite web|title=Updated Statement From NDTV On CBI Raids|url=http://www.ndtv.com/communication/updated-statement-from-ndtv-on-cbi-raids-1708164|publisher=NDTV|access-date=5 June 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170605165230/http://www.ndtv.com/communication/updated-statement-from-ndtv-on-cbi-raids-1708164|archive-date=5 June 2017|date=5 June 2017}}</ref>
