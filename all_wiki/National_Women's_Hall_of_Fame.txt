The '''National Women's Hall of Fame''' is an [[United States|American]] institution created in 1969 by a group of people in [[Seneca Falls (town), New York|Seneca Falls, New York]], the location of the [[Seneca Falls Convention|1848 women's rights convention]].<ref name="nwhof_history">{{Cite news|url=https://www.womenofthehall.org/about-the-hall/our-history/|title=Our History - National Women’s Hall of Fame|publisher=National Women's Hall of Fame|access-date=2018-01-22|language=en-US}}</ref>

The National Women's [[Hall of Fame]] inducts distinguished American women through a rigorous national honors selection process involving representatives of the nation's important organizations and areas of expertise.<ref name="nwhof_faq">{{Cite news|url=https://www.womenofthehall.org/about-the-hall/faqs/|title=FAQs - National Women's Hall of Fame|work=National Women’s Hall of Fame|access-date=2018-01-22|language=en-US}}</ref> Nominees are selected on the basis of the changes they created that affect the social, economic or cultural aspects of society; the significant national or global impact and results of change due to their achievement; and the enduring value of their achievements or changes.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.csmonitor.com/1995/0915/15041.html|title=18 Nominees Chosen for National Women's Hall of Fame|date=1995-09-15|work=Christian Science Monitor|access-date=2018-01-22|issn=0882-7729}}</ref> Induction ceremonies are held every odd- numbered year in the fall, with the names of the women to be honored announced earlier in the spring, usually during March, [[Women's History Month]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.womenofthehall.org/event/2017-induction-weekend/|title=2017 Induction Weekend - National Women’s Hall of Fame|work=National Women’s Hall of Fame|access-date=2018-01-22|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://guthyjacksonfoundation.org/events/womenofthehall/|title=Now Streaming Live: The National Women's Hall of Fame Inducts Victoria Jackson - Mother, Entrepreneur, Innovator, Author, and Philanthropist|work=The Guthy-Jackson Charitable Foundation|access-date=2018-01-22|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180122072708/https://guthyjacksonfoundation.org/events/womenofthehall/|archive-date=2018-01-22|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Location==
The National Women's Hall of Fame was hosted by [[Eisenhower College]] until 1979, when the organization rented out a historic bank building in the Seneca Falls Historic District. The historic bank was renovated to house the NWHF's permanent exhibit, historical artifacts, and offices.<ref name="buchanan">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lc9Pzsa2zyUC&pg=PA203|title=The American Women's Rights Movement: A Chronology of Events and of Opportunities from 1600 to 2008|last=Buchanan|first=Paul D.|date=2009|publisher=Branden Books|isbn=9780828321600|language=en}}</ref> In August 2020, the National Women's Hall of Fame opened its door to the third and final home: the historic Seneca Knitting Mill, which resides across the canal of the [[Women's Rights National Historical Park]] where 1848 Convention was held. The site is in view of the Wesleyan Chapel where the 1848 women's rights convention took place.<ref name=finger/> This renovation and move into the historic Seneca Knitting Mill has taken several years to accomplish. In 2014 the organization's board undertook a $20 million capital campaign to fund the development of the 1844 Seneca Knitting Mill, which is associated with the [[Abolitionism|abolitionist movement]] and with the birthplace of women's rights.<ref name=finger>{{cite web |url=http://www.fltimes.com/news/a-conversation-with-jill-tietjen-ceo-of-national-women-s/article_cfb8138e-f27a-11e4-bf46-8761b0f82835.html|title=A Conversation With: Jill Tietjen, CEO of National Women's Hall of Fame|first=David L.|last=Shaw|date=4 May 2015|accessdate=3 June 2018|work=Finger Lakes Times}}</ref> The move and finalization of Phase 1 has currently doubled the size of the National Women's Hall of Fame. Currently, campaigning to work on Phase 2: a elevator, additional staircase, and other renovations is underway. Once the Homecoming Campaign is complete, the historic Seneca Knitting Mill will quadruple the available space to {{convert|16000|sqft}}, including exhibit space, offices, and meeting space for conferences, wedding receptions, and community events.<ref name=finger/>
