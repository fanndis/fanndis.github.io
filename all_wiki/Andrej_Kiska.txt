'''Andrej Kiska''' ({{IPA-sk|ˈandrɛj ˈkiska}}; born 2 February 1963) is a Slovak politician, entrepreneur, writer  and philanthropist who was [[President of Slovakia]] from 2014 to 2019. He ran as an [[independent (politician)|independent candidate]] in the [[2014 Slovak presidential election|2014 presidential election]] and was elected as President in the second round of voting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/2014/03/16/uk-slovakia-election-idUKBREA2F00V20140316|title= Slovak PM Fico, political novice advance to run-off presidential vote|date=16 March 2014|publisher=[[Reuters]]|accessdate=16 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tyden.cz/rubriky/zahranici/evropa/slovaci-voli-prezidenta-fica-ohrozuje-filantrop-kiska_300966.html#.UyV-V84m-t8|title=Slováci volí prezidenta. Fica ohrožuje filantrop Kiska|date=15 March 2014 |publisher=Týden|language=cs|accessdate=16 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.topky.sk/cl/1000735/1375311/Kandidat-na-prezidenta-Andrej-Kiska--Najvyssim-zmyslom-zivota-je-pomahat-druhym- | title=Kandidát na prezidenta Andrej Kiska: Najvyšším zmyslom života je pomáhať druhým! | publisher=Topky.sk | accessdate=March 16, 2014 | language=sk}}</ref> He has written two books about happiness, success and his life.

==Biography==
===Early and personal life===
Kiska was born in [[Poprad]]. He studied [[electrical engineering]]. His father was an active member of the ruling Communist Party of Czechoslovakia. He also applied for Communist party membership, but was rejected.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://domov.sme.sk/c/7154011/podnikatel-filantrop-a-uz-aj-prezident-andrej-kiska.html|title=Podnikateľ, filantrop a už aj prezident. Andrej Kiska|first=Petit Press|last=a.s|website=domov.sme.sk}}</ref>

Kiska was married to {{Interlanguage link|Mária Kisková|sk}}, an educator and politician from 1985 until 2001. They had two children, Andrej Kiska (1986) and Natália Kisková (1990).<ref name=ssp>{{cite news |title=President Kiska has a new baby son |url=https://spectator.sme.sk/c/20578740/president-kiska-has-a-new-baby-son.html |work=The Slovak Spectator |date=2017-07-10 |accessdate=2018-08-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141117161812/http://www.pluska.sk/spravy/z-domova/prva-dama-martina-kiskova-co-odkazuje-vsetkym-neprajnikom.html |archive-date=2014-11-17 |url-status = live}}</ref> The couple divorced in 2001 after 18 years of marriage.

Kiska married his second wife, [[Martina Kisková]], in 2003. The couple have three children- - Veronika (born 2005), Viktor (born 2009), and Martin (born July 2017 during his presidency).<ref name=ssp/>

===Career===
In 1990, after the [[Velvet Revolution]], he moved to the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novinky.cz/zahranicni/evropa/331929-novym-slovenskym-prezidentem-bude-andrej-kiska.html|title=Novým slovenským prezidentem bude Andrej Kiska|date=29 March 2014|publisher=[[novinky.cz]]|language=cs|accessdate=30 March 2014}}</ref> Later he founded ''Triangel'' and ''Quatro'',<ref name="databazeknih">{{cite web | url=http://www.databazeknih.cz/zivotopis/andrej-kiska-23188 | title=Andrej Kiska - životopis | publisher=DatabazeKnih | accessdate=March 17, 2014 | language=sk}}</ref> two Slovakia-based [[hire-purchase]] companies that give the buyer the possibility to pay for goods in several installments over a number of months instead of paying the full price at once.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.investopedia.com/terms/h/hire-purchase.asp | title=Hire-purchase definition | publisher=Investopedia | accessdate=March 17, 2014}}</ref>

Tax authorities accused his co-owned company KTAG (with his brother Jaroslav) of tax non-compliance and under-payments, regarding expenses for his presidential campaign. The company eventually agreed to pay the taxes and a penalty.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://slovensko.hnonline.sk/1025396-kiska-sklamal-transparency-vyhovorky-mal-prijat-zodpovednost-a-ospravedlnit-sa|title=Kiska sklamal Transparency: Výhovorky, mal prijať zodpovednosť a ospravedlniť sa|website=slovensko.hnonline.sk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://domov.sme.sk/c/20647309/kiska-bol-som-presvedceny-ze-firma-kona-spravne.html|title=Kiska: Bol som presvedčený, že firma koná správne|first=Petit Press|last=a.s|website=domov.sme.sk}}</ref>

=== Tax non-compliance and legal problems===

Andrej Kiska's companies were involved in a number of tax-non compliance issues. Improperly reported income from various business activities resulted in a smaller VAT and Income tax payments.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://spravy.pravda.sk/domace/clanok/441545-otazky-a-odpovede-v-com-spociva-trestna-cinnost-v-kauze-kiskovej-firmy/|title=Otázky a odpovede: V čom spočíva trestná činnosť v kauze Kiskovej firmy?|date=September 13, 2017|website=Pravda.sk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nazory.pravda.sk/komentare-a-glosy/clanok/441818-kiska-bez-svatoziary/|title=Kiska bez svätožiary|date=September 16, 2017|website=Pravda.sk}}</ref>

In his personal tax filing he did not disclose personal propagation before elections performed by his company as a non-cash income, but claimed that he paid for it. Eventually tax office confirmed he did not pay, did not report non-cash income, and his company did not report income from propagation activities but fully deducted all VAT. Remaining costs additionally decreased tax base.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://domov.sme.sk/c/20649096/tlak-na-kisku-sa-stupnuje-unikli-dalsie-dokumenty.html|title=Tlak na Kisku sa stupňuje, unikli ďalšie dokumenty|first=Petit Press|last=a.s|website=domov.sme.sk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://slovensko.hnonline.sk/1025081-kisku-vysetrovala-policia-pre-vydavky-na-kampan-prezident-reagoval-na-obvinenie|title=Kisku vyšetrovala polícia pre výdavky na kampaň, prezident reagoval na obvinenie|website=slovensko.hnonline.sk}}</ref>

===Philanthropy===
In 2006, Kiska co-founded a [[non-profit]] [[charitable organization]] called [[Dobrý anjel]] (translated as ''Good Angel''), in which donors help families that have found themselves in a difficult financial situation as a result of a family member contracting a serious disease (such as [[cancer]]).<ref name="databazeknih" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dobryanjel.sk/viac-o-dobrom-anjelovi/ |title=Čo je systém Dobrý anjel | publisher=Dobrý anjel |accessdate=March 17, 2014 |language=sk}}</ref> By 2016, more than 170,000 people have donated to this organization in Slovakia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dobryanjel.sk/zoznam-dobrych-anjelov/|title=Zoznam Dobrých anjelov|date=March 1, 2014|publisher=Dobrý anjel|language=sk|accessdate=August 9, 2016}}</ref> In 2014 Good Angel expanded their activities also to Czech Republic, where 60.000 are paying their monthly contributions in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dobryandel.cz/en/|title=DOBRÝ ANDĚL, nadace|website=DOBRÝ ANDĚL|language=en-US|access-date=2016-08-09}}</ref> In addition to that, Kiska donates his monthly presidential salary to people in need.

==Presidency==
[[File:Secretary Pompeo Meets with Slovak President Kiska (46347493364).jpg|thumb|Kiska (right) with US Secretary of State [[Mike Pompeo]] in February 2019]]
In the first round of the [[2014 Slovak presidential election]], Kiska placed second with 24% of the vote, behind Prime Minister [[Robert Fico]] (28%). As none of candidates got more than 50% of votes, Kiska and Fico progressed to a presidential runoff vote on 29 March 2014. With the support of right-wing parties and other defeated candidates, Kiska won decisively in the second round, receiving nearly 60% of the vote.<ref>[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/slovakia/10732435/Millionaire-Andrej-Kiska-elected-first-Slovak-president-with-no-communist-past.html "Millionaire Andrej Kiska elected first Slovak president with no communist past"], ''[[Telegraph Online]]'', 30 March 2014, accessed 30 March 2014.</ref> He took office on 15 June 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.europeonline-magazine.eu/andrej-kiska-inaugurated-as-slovakian-president_341202.html|title=Andrej Kiska inaugurated as Slovakian president|date=15 June 2014|publisher=Europe Online Magazine|accessdate=15 June 2014}}</ref>

Kiska announced on 15 May 2018 that he will not participate in [[2019 Slovak presidential election|2019 presidential election]], arguing that his departure might end "the era of political confrontation" his country faced and citing a desire to spend more time with his family.<ref name="Kiska nebude kandidovať na prezidenta">{{cite web|title=Kiska nebude kandidovať na prezidenta|url=https://domov.sme.sk/c/20826159/andrej-kiska-prezidentske-volby-2019-oznamenie.html?ref=st-bno-1|website=domov.sme.sk|accessdate=15 May 2018|language=sk}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-05-15/eu-to-lose-key-ally-in-slovakia-as-president-won-t-seek-new-term|title=EU to Lose Key Ally in Slovakia as President Won't Seek New Term|date=2018-05-15|work=Bloomberg.com|access-date=2018-05-15|language=en}}</ref> At the time Kiska made the announcement, polls indicated that he was Slovakia's most trusted politician and that he would have likely been the frontrunner in the election had he chosen to present himself as a candidate.<ref name=":0" />

===Foreign policy===
Kiska supports [[Kosovo independence]] and is in favour of Slovakia diplomatically [[International recognition of Kosovo|recognising]] [[Kosovo]] as an independent sovereign state.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.b92.net/info/vesti/index.php?yyyy=2014&mm=03&dd=30&nav_category=78&nav_id=830326|title=Milioner na čelu Slovačke|date=2014-03-30|accessdate=2014-03-31|publisher=[[B92]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.welt.de/politik/ausland/article126354035/Parteiloser-Millionaer-wird-slowakischer-Praesident.html|title=Parteiloser Millionär wird slowakischer Präsident|accessdate=2014-03-31|publisher=[[Die Welt]]}}</ref>

Kiska has also come out against Russia over the Ukraine conflict and supports [[International sanctions during the Ukrainian crisis|sanctions]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.praguepost.com/eu-news/40949-slovak-president-rejects-pm-s-negative-view-of-russian-sanctions |title=Slovak president rejects PM’s negative view of Russian sanctions |date=2014-08-15 |accessdate=2015-04-02 |publisher=[[The Prague Post]] |url-status = dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402052915/http://praguepost.com/eu-news/40949-slovak-president-rejects-pm-s-negative-view-of-russian-sanctions |archive-date=2015-04-02 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/slovak-president-kiska-eu-must-stand-united-on-russia-1423073916|title=Slovak President Kiska: EU Must Stand United on Russia|date=2015-02-04|accessdate=2015-04-02|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://uatoday.tv/politics/slovakian-president-andrej-kiska-meets-with-nato-secretary-general-jens-stoltenberg-413090.html|title=Slovakian President Andrej Kiska meets with NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg|date=2015-03-03|accessdate=2015-04-02|publisher=[[Ukraine Today]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://spectator.sme.sk/c/20056584/president-will-not-go-to-moscow.html|title=President will not go to Moscow|date=2015-03-18|accessdate=2015-04-02|publisher=[[The Slovak Spectator]]}}</ref>
