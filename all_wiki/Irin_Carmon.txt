'''Irin Carmon''' ({{IPAc-en|lang|ɪ|ˈ|r|ɪ|n|_|k|ɑːr|ˈ|m|oʊ|n}})<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/irincarmon/how-to-pronounce|title=How to pronounce Irin Carmon's name|author=Irin Carmon|accessdate=9 July 2018}}</ref> is an [[Israeli-American]]<ref name=tweet>{{cite web|title=Irin Carmon|url=https://twitter.com/irin/status/745360023312707586|website=Twitter}}</ref> [[journalist]] and [[Pundit|commentator]]. She is a senior correspondent at ''[[New York Magazine]]'',<ref name="nymag.com">{{cite web|url=http://nymag.com/press/2018/07/irin-carmon-joins-new-york-magazine-as-senior-correspondent.html|title=Irin Carmon Joins New York Magazine As Senior Correspondent|website=New York Magazine|accessdate=30 December 2018}}</ref> and a [[CNN]] contributor.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thewrap.com/notorious-rbg-author-irin-carmon-hired-by-cnn-as-contributor/|website=The Wrap|title='Notorious RBG' Author Irin Carmon Hired by CNN as Contributor|accessdate=30 December 2018}}</ref> She is co-author of ''Notorious RBG: The Life and Times of Ruth Bader Ginsburg''. Previously, she was a national reporter at [[MSNBC]], covering women, politics, and culture for the [[website]] and on air. She was a Visiting Fellow in the Program for the Study of Reproductive Justice at [[Yale Law School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://irincarmon.people.msnbc.com/_tps/index#|title=Irin Carmon - MSNBC|website=MSNBC|accessdate=10 May 2017}}</ref>

In 2011, she was named one of ''[[Forbes (magazine)|Forbes]]''' "30 under 30"<ref>{{cite web|last=Bercovici|first=Jeff|title=Media|url=https://www.forbes.com/special-report/2011/30-under30-12/30-under-30-12_media.html|publisher=Forbes Magazine|accessdate=2 January 2012}}</ref>  in media and featured in ''New York Magazine'' as a face of young feminism.<ref>{{cite news|title=The Rebirth of the Feminist Manifesto|url=http://nymag.com/news/features/feminist-blogs-2011-11/index1.html|date=November 2011}}</ref> She received the November 2011 Sidney award from [[The Sidney Hillman Foundation]] recognizing her reporting on the Mississippi Personhood Initiative for Salon.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hillmanfoundation.org/thesidney/backstory/irin-carmon|title=Irin Carmon Wins November Sidney Award - Hillman Foundation<!-- Bot generated title -->|publisher=|accessdate=10 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111221024738/http://www.hillmanfoundation.org/thesidney/backstory/irin-carmon|archive-date=21 December 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[Mediaite]] named her among four in its award for Best TV pundit of 2014.<ref name="auto">{{cite news|title=Mediaite Awards 2014: We Pick the Year's Very BEST in Media|url=http://www.mediaite.com/online/mediaite-awards-2014-we-pick-the-years-very-best-in-media/#4|date=December 17, 2014}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Carmon is Jewish<ref>{{Cite web|title=I'm a Jew|url=https://twitter.com/irin/status/1037034616572522498|access-date=2020-09-21|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> and was born in [[Israel]], the granddaughter of Zionists who lived in [[Palestine (region)|Palestine]] during World War II.<ref name=jezebel>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=Helen Thomas: When An Icon Disappoints|url=http://jezebel.com/5557093/helen-thomas-when-an-icon-disappoints|website=jezebel.com|date=June 7, 2010}}</ref> She grew up on Long Island.<ref name="jezebel.com">{{cite news|last1=Holmes|first1=Anna|title=Good Enough To Eat Meet: Say Hello To Our Newest Ladyblogger|url=http://jezebel.com/5400211/good-enough-to-eat-meet-say-hello-to-our-newest-ladyblogger|website=Jezebel}}</ref> She is a naturalized citizen of the United States.<ref name="Twitter">[https://twitter.com/yaleisp], Twitter.</ref>

A graduate of Waldorf School of Garden City in 2001, Carmon attended [[Harvard College]] and graduated in 2005 with an AB in Literature, ''[[magna cum laude]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last1=La Bella|first1=Jeanenne|title=Alumnae Profile: Irin Carmon Class of 2001|url=http://www.waldorfgarden.org/uploaded/admissions/kathy/Blogs_Media/news-summer2012_2012.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303224822/http://www.waldorfgarden.org/uploaded/admissions/kathy/Blogs_Media/news-summer2012_2012.pdf|archive-date=2016-03-03|agency=The News: The Waldorf School of Garden City|issue=2|date=Summer 2012|volume=65}}</ref>

While at Harvard, Carmon wrote for [[The Harvard Crimson]]<ref>{{cite news|title=Irin Carmon Writer Profile|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/writer/3598/Irin__Carmon/|publisher=The Harvard Crimson}}</ref> and [[Let's Go (book series)]].<ref>{{cite book|url=http://www.worldcat.org/oclc/1035830136 |title=Notorious RBG (2015) : the life and times of Ruth Bader Ginsburg / |website=Worldcat.org |date= 27 October 2015|isbn=9780062415820 |oclc=1035830136 |accessdate=2017-05-17|last1=Carmon |first1=Irin |last2=Knizhnik |first2=Shana }}</ref> Her senior thesis was titled, "Genealogies of Catastrophe: Yehuda Amichai's Lo Me'Achshav, Lo Me'kan and Ricardo Piglia's Respiracion Artificial."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=w5aQNwAACAAJ|title=Genealogies of Catastrophe: Yehuda Amichai's Lo Me'Achshav, Lo Me'kan and Ricardo Piglia's Respiracion Artificial|first=Irin|last=Carmon|date=1 January 2005|publisher=Harvard University|accessdate=10 May 2017|via=Google Books}}</ref>

== Career ==

Early in her career, Carmon wrote regularly for the ''[[Boston Globe]]'',<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=Israel rounds up migrants in deportation campaign|url=http://www.boston.com/news/world/middleeast/articles/2004/07/25/israel_rounds_up_migrants_in_deportation_campaign?pg=full|newspaper=[[The Boston Globe]]|date=July 25, 2004}}</ref> the ''[[Village Voice]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/authors/irin-carmon/|title=Irin Carmon - New York News, Food, Culture and Events - Village Voice|website=Village Voice|accessdate=10 May 2017}}</ref> and ''[[The Anniston Star]]''.<ref name="jezebel.com"/> She was a media reporter for the fashion-industry trade journal ''[[Women's Wear Daily]]'' from 2006 to 2009.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wwd.com/wwd-masthead/irin-carmon-1616643|website=WWD.com|publisher=Women's Wear Daily|title=Irin Carmon}}</ref>

Carmon was a [[Jezebel (website)|Jezebel]] staff writer from 2009 to 2011.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=Irin Carmon|url=http://irin-carmon-old.kinja.com/|website=Jezebel}}</ref> She wrote a post calling ''[[The Daily Show]]'' a "boys' club where women's contributions are often ignored and dismissed”, and opining that then-correspondent [[Olivia Munn]] was only hired on the show because of her status as a [[sex symbol]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=The Daily Show's Woman Problem|url=http://jezebel.com/5570545/comedy-of-errors-behind-the-scenes-of-the--daily-shows-lady-problem|website=[[Jezebel (website)|Jezebel]]|date=June 23, 2010}}</ref> The women of the ''Daily Show'' responded by publishing an open letter defending their workplace.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Itzkoff|first1=Dave|title=In Open Letter, Women of ''The Daily Show'' Respond to Charges of Sexism|url=http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/07/06/in-open-letter-women-of-the-daily-show-respond-to-charges-of-sexism/|work=New York Times|date=July 6, 2010}}</ref> In response to allegations that she had failed to provide adequate time for comment, Carmon posted her email thread with the ''Daily Show'' publicist which took place one week before the story was published.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=My Daily Show Emails|url=http://www.salon.com/2012/07/20/my_daily_show_emails/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120831072512/https://www.salon.com/2012/07/20/my_daily_show_emails/|archive-date=August 31, 2012|website=Salon.com|date=July 20, 2012}}</ref> Two years later, Carmon noted her appreciation for changes at ''The Daily Show'' since the controversy.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=Did I ruin journalism?|url=http://www.salon.com/2012/07/23/did_i_ruin_journalism/|website=Salon.com|date=July 23, 2012}}</ref>

From 2011 to 2013, Carmon was a staff writer for [[Salon (website)|Salon]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.salon.com/writer/irin_carmon/|website=Salon|title=Irin Carmon}}</ref> Her Salon coverage of [[Eden Foods]] drew attention to the organic food company's lawsuit against the contraception mandate of the [[Affordable Care Act]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=Organic Eden Foods' quiet right-wing agenda|url=http://www.salon.com/2013/04/11/organic_eden_foods_quiet_right_wing_agenda/|website=Salon}}</ref> Her piece was used in an Appeals Court ruling as evidence against Eden Foods' claim of a religious freedom motive.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carmon|first1=Irin|title=Birth Control, the Supreme Court and me|url=http://www.msnbc.com/msnbc/birth-control-the-supreme-court-and-me|website=MSNBC.com|date=November 25, 2013}}</ref> In October 2012, she and Jezebel founder [[Anna Holmes]] started the trending #sorryfeminists hashtag that mocked negative stereotypes of feminists.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Marcotte|first1=Amanda|title=Sorry, Feminists|url=http://www.slate.com/blogs/xx_factor/2012/10/08/sorry_feminists_a_hashtag_that_fights_stereotypes_of_feminists_with_dadaist_humor_.html|website=Slate|date=October 8, 2012}}</ref>

In June 2013, Carmon was hired full-time by [[MSNBC]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sterne|first1=Peter|title=MSNBC.com Hires Irin Carmon, Timothy Noah, and Others|url=http://observer.com/2013/06/msnbc-com-hires-irin-carmon-timothy-noah-and-others/|date=June 17, 2013}}</ref> She has written for MSNBC.com and contributed on the shows [[The Reid Report]], [[Melissa Harris-Perry (TV series)|Melissa Harris-Perry]], and [[All In with Chris Hayes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://irincarmon.people.msnbc.com/|title=Irin Carmon - MSNBC|website=MSNBC|accessdate=10 May 2017}}</ref> [[Mediaite]] named her in a four-way tie among the "Best TV Pundits" of 2014 for bringing "a comprehensive understanding to women's health and justice issues that goes beyond the usual talking points."<ref name="auto"/> In January 2015, New York Magazine reported that Carmon would be co-authoring the biography<ref name="Archipelago">{{cite web|title = Notorious RBG - Irin Carmon, Shana Knizhnik - Hardcover|url = http://www.harpercollins.com/9780062415837/notorious-rbg|website = HarperCollins US|access-date = 2016-02-08|first = World|last = Archipelago}}</ref> ''Notorious R.B.G.: The Life and Times of Ruth Bader Ginsburg'' with [[Shana Knizhnik]], the creator of the Notorious R.B.G. blog.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Stoeffel|first1=Kat|title=Notorious R.B.G. Gets Her Own Biography, From the People Who Made Her a Meme|url=http://nymag.com/thecut/2015/01/meme-blogger-will-co-author-rbgs-biography.html|date=January 7, 2015}}</ref>  The book was released in October 2015<ref name="Archipelago"/> and debuted at #7 on the ''New York Times'' [[The New York Times Best Seller list|Best Seller]] list.<ref>{{cite web|title = Best Sellers - The New York Times|url = https://www.nytimes.com/best-sellers-books/2015-11-15/hardcover-nonfiction/list.html|website = www.nytimes.com|access-date = 2016-04-12}}</ref> In February 2015, Carmon conducted an exclusive interview for MSNBC with [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]] for ''[[The Rachel Maddow Show]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Joyella|first1=Mark|publisher=[[Adweek]]|website=TVNewser|title='Notorious RBG': MSNBC's Exclusive with Justice Ginsburg|url=http://www.adweek.com/tvnewser/notorious-rbg-msnbcs-exclusive-with-justice-ginsburg/255834|date=February 12, 2015}}</ref>

In late 2017 and early 2018, Carmon teamed up with the ''[[Washington Post]]'' to break the news of sexual harassment and assault allegations against [[Charlie Rose]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Eight women say Charlie Rose sexually harassed them — with nudity, groping and lewd calls |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/investigations/eight-women-say-charlie-rose-sexually-harassed-them--with-nudity-groping-and-lewd-calls/2017/11/20/9b168de8-caec-11e7-8321-481fd63f174d_story.html |work=The Washington Post |accessdate=30 December 2018}}</ref> as well as [[CBS]]’s knowledge of his conduct.<ref>{{cite web |title=Charlie Rose's misconduct was widespread at CBS and three managers were warned, investigation finds |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/charlie-roses-misconduct-was-widespread-at-cbs-and-three-managers-were-warned-investigation-finds/2018/05/02/80613d24-3228-11e8-94fa-32d48460b955_story.html |work=The Washington Post |accessdate=30 December 2018}}</ref> That work won a 2018 Mirror Award from the Newhouse School at Syracuse University.<ref>{{cite web |title=Presenting the 2018 Mirror Award Winners |url=https://news.syr.edu/blog/2018/06/20/presenting-the-2018-mirror-award-winners/ |website=Syracuse University News |accessdate=30 December 2018}}</ref>

In July 2018, Carmon was hired by New York Magazine,<ref name="nymag.com"/> as a senior correspondent. In November 2018, she was hired by CNN as a contributor.<ref>{{cite web|first=Jon|last=Levine|url=https://www.thewrap.com/notorious-rbg-author-irin-carmon-hired-by-cnn-as-contributor/|title='Notorious RBG' Author Irin Carmon Hired by CNN as Contributor|website=[[TheWrap]]|publisher=TheWrap News, Inc.|location=Los Angeles, California|date=November 14, 2018|accessdate=December 30, 2018}}</ref>
