'''David Charles Meyers''' (born October 19, 1972)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:QJWB-H9ZH|title=United States Public Records, 1970-2009: David Charles Meyers}}</ref> is an American music video, commercial and film director.

==Early life==
Born and raised in [[Berkeley, California]], Dave Meyers developed his love for film while working for a local Landmark cinema at age 17. After graduating from [[Berkeley High School (California)|Berkeley High School]] he attended [[Loyola Marymount University]] where he studied Film Production and Philosophy.{{citation needed|date=December 2015}}

==Career==
After graduating from [[Loyola Marymount University]], Meyers worked his way through the studio systems at Paramount and Fox before filming his first music video with rap crew The Whoridas.

Inspired by a chance meeting with director [[Gus Van Sant]], Meyers steered his focus away from feature film-making to directing music videos.<ref>{{cite news |last=Green |first=Catherine |date=August 11, 2017 |title= The Director Behind Some of the Most Iconic Music Videos of the 2000s|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2017/08/the-art-and-business-of-making-music-videos/535254/ |work=The Atlantic |location=NYC |access-date=August 22, 2019 }}</ref>  He has directed more than 200 videos for some of the music industry’s most popular recording artists. His work brought dozens of nominations and 12 [[MTV Video Music Awards]] including the 2003, 2011, 2017 and 2018 [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Video of the Year]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Sanneh|first=Kelefa|title=A Win for Missy Elliott at Music Video Awards|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2003/08/29/nyregion/a-win-for-missy-elliott-at-music-video-awards.html|accessdate=August 22, 2019|newspaper=New York Times|date=August 29, 2003}}</ref> In 2006, he won the [[Grammy Award for Best Music Video]] for co-directing Missy Elliott's "Lose Control".

In 1999, Dave directed the [[Eddie Griffin]] comedy ''[[Foolish (film)|Foolish]]'' and in 2007 he directed the remake of ''[[The Hitcher (2007 film)|The Hitcher]]'', which was produced by director [[Michael Bay]].<ref>Lemire, Christy (2007) "[https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=6WBWAAAAIBAJ&sjid=tfADAAAAIBAJ&pg=6462,4540864&dq=dave-meyers+video&hl=en 'Hitcher' remake does the original proud]", Associated Press/''[[Eugene Register-Guard]]'', January 22, 2007, p. B5, retrieved 2011-07-11</ref>

Meyers is a frequent collaborator to [[Ariana Grande]], [[Katy Perry]] and [[Missy Elliott]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Drucker |first=Eric |date=November 20, 2015 |title=Talking to Dave Meyers, the director behind Missy Elliott's 'WTF' music video |url=https://www.theverge.com/2015/11/20/9768742/missy-elliott-wtf-music-video-director-interview |work=The Verge |location=Washington, DC |access-date=August 22, 2019}}</ref>
