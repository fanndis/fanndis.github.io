The '''''Financial Review'' Rich List 2020''' is the 37th annual survey of the 200 wealthiest people resident in [[Australia]], published by ''[[The Australian Financial Review]]'' in ''The Australian Financial Review Magazine'' on 30 October 2020.<ref name="afr2020">{{cite web|url=https://www.afr.com/rich-list/the-10-richest-australians-revealed-20201028-p569c7|url-access=subscription|title=The full list: Australia's wealthiest 200 revealed|work=[[The Australian Financial Review]]|publisher=Nine Publishing|date=30 October 2020|author1=Bailey, Michael|author2=Sprague, Julie-anne|accessdate=31 October 2020}}</ref> 

The [[net worth]] of the wealthiest individual, [[Gina Rinehart]], was {{AUD}}28.89&nbsp;[[1,000,000,000|billion]]; while the net worth of the 200th wealthiest individual, [[Nigel Satterley]], was {{AUD}}540&nbsp;million; up from {{AUD}}472&nbsp;million for the 200th individual in 2019.<ref name=nine>{{cite news |author=Marsh, Stuart|title=Gina Rinehart named Australia's richest person with $29 billion personal fortune|url=https://www.9news.com.au/national/australias-richest-people-afr-2020-list-gina-rinehart-personal-net-worth/02a5756f-43e9-4dc6-9ba5-d0e2ea2f2f4b|work=[[Nine News]]|date=29 October 2020|access-date=31 October 2020 }}</ref> The combined wealth of the 200 individuals was calculated as {{AUD}}424&nbsp;bn, an increase of {{AUD}}72&nbsp;bn on the previous year;<ref name=smh/> compared with a combined wealth of {{AUD}}6.4&nbsp;bn in 1984 when the ''BRW'' Rich 200 commenced.<ref name="afr2020"/><ref name=smh>{{cite news |author=Kruger, Colin|url=https://www.smh.com.au/business/the-economy/what-crisis-red-dirt-billionaires-still-rule-the-rich-list-20201030-p56a0v.html|title=What crisis? Red dirt billionaires still rule the rich list|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=30 October 2020|access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref><!-- [check numbers in italics, find reference for same] ''Twenty-six'' women were included on the 2020 Rich List, representing ''13'' percent of the list; up from twenty-six in 2019, or 13 percent. --> The list included nineteen debutants, including three of Rinehart's children.<ref name=debut>{{cite news |author2=Bailey, Michael |author1=Sprague, Julie-anne |title=Meet the debutants storming up the Rich List |url=https://www.afr.com/rich-list/meet-the-debutants-storming-up-the-rich-list-20201027-p568ue|url-access=subscription|work=[[The Australian Financial Review]]|publisher=Nine Publishing|date=28 October 2020|access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref>
