}}'''The Green Papers''' is a website that follows the results of [[United States presidential elections]]. It was created by Richard Berg-Andersson and Tony Roza in 1999. It has become particularly known for covering the results of [[presidential primaries]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.bu.edu/today/2016/the-green-papers-political-website/ |title=The Green Papers Political Website:  A Go-to Source for Journalists  |last=Barlow |first=Rich |date=2016-06-07 |website=BU Today |language=en |access-date=2018-09-10}}</ref> It was one of the first websites to monitor election results.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/8gegng/a-shakeup-this-late-in-the-campaign-is-very-bad-for-team-trump |title=The Latest Campaign Shakeup Does Not Look Good for Donald Trump |last=Pearl |first=Mike |date=2016-08-22 |website=Vice |language=en-us |access-date=2018-09-10}}</ref> During the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 presidential election]], many journalists began paying attention to the site's delegate counts, and Quoctrung Bui of ''[[the New York Times]]'' noted that the site "...does something very few media organizations are willing to do: accurately and independently tabulate delegates in real time."<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/07/upshot/the-secretive-duo-guiding-the-delegate-count.html |title=The Secretive Duo Guiding the Delegate Count |last=Bui |first=Quoctrung |date=2016-05-08 |work=The New York Times |access-date=2018-09-10 |language=en}}</ref>
