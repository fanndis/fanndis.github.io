'''Edmund Munroe Smith''' (December 8, 1854 – April 13, 1926) was an [[United States|American]] [[jurist]] and [[historian]].

==Family and education==
Smith was born in [[Brooklyn, New York]], the son of Horatio Southgate Smith and his wife, Susan Dwight Munroe.<ref name="chapman" /> He received his [[Bachelor of Arts|A.B.]] from [[Amherst College]] in 1874 and his [[Bachelor of Laws|LL.B.]] from [[Columbia Law School]] in 1877.<ref name="brown" />  In 1879, Smith returned to Amherst to earn an [[Master of Arts|A.M.]] degree.<ref name="brown" /> He received the degree of [[Doctor of Canon and Civil Law|J.U.D.]] from the [[University of Göttingen]] in 1880.<ref name="brown" />

In 1890, Smith married Emma Gertrude Huidekoper, daughter of General [[Henry S. Huidekoper]].<ref name="chapman" /> They had one daughter, Gertrude Munroe Smith, born in 1891.<ref name="chapman" /> Smith's brother, [[Henry Smith Munroe|Henry Maynard Smith]] (he changed his name to Henry Smith Munroe), was also a professor at [[Columbia University|Columbia]], and served as Dean of the [[Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science|School of Mines]] from 1891 to 1915.  His great-granddaughter is [[Meg Whitman]], former CEO of [[eBay]] and candidate for [[Governor of California]].

==Career==
Smith filled several posts at [[Columbia University]] from 1880 onwards. He was a lecturer in [[Roman law]] and an instructor in history from 1880 to 1883.<ref name="brown" /> He was promoted to adjunct professor of history in 1883, and became a full professor of Roman law and comparative jurisprudence in 1891.<ref name="brown" /> Smith was one of the founders of the ''[[Political Science Quarterly]],'' and served as its managing editor from 1886 to 1893 and again from 1904 to 1913.<ref name="encyc" />

He retired from teaching in 1924 and died of [[pneumonia]] in 1926 at the age of seventy-seven.<ref name="obit" />
