'''Karsten Braasch''' (born 14 July 1967) is a German former professional [[tennis]] player. His highest ATP singles ranking was World No. 38, which he reached in June 1994. His career-high in doubles was World No. 36, achieved in November 1997.<ref>[http://www.atpworldtour.com/Tennis/Players/Br/K/Karsten-Braasch.aspx?t=rh "Karsten Braasch ATP Rankings History"], ATPWorldTour.com</ref> He was well-noted for his service motion and his habit of smoking during changeovers.

Braasch competed in a "[[Battle of the Sexes (tennis)#1998: Karsten Braasch vs. the Williams sisters|Battle of the Sexes]]" contest against the Williams sisters ([[Venus Williams#1998: Karsten Braasch vs. the Williams sisters|Venus]] and [[Serena Williams#Battle of the Sexes: Karsten Braasch vs the Williams sisters|Serena]]) at the 1998 Australian Open when he was ranked 203. Braasch was described by one journalist as "a man whose training regime centred around a pack of cigarettes and more than a couple bottles of ice cold lager".<ref>[http://www.ontennis.com/news/serena-williams-still-savouring-andy-roddick-%2526%2523039%3Bwin%2526%2523039%3B-16-years "Serena Williams still savouring Andy Roddick 'win' 16 years on"], OnTennis.com, posted 22 January 2009</ref> He nonetheless defeated both sisters, playing a single set against each, beating Serena 6–1 and Venus 6–2.<ref>[https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1298&dat=19980127&id=kqJjAAAAIBAJ&sjid=mggGAAAAIBAJ&pg=3506,5011601 "Sister act falls in Battle of Sexes], The Free Lance-Star – 27 January 1998</ref> Braasch was thirty years old at the time, while Venus and Serena were seventeen and sixteen, respectively.
