'''David Adams''' (born 5 January 1970) is a former [[tennis]] player from South Africa. He turned professional in 1989. In his career, he won 19 doubles titles and finished runner-up an additional 33 times, including at the French Open in 1992. He achieved a career-high doubles ranking of world No. 9 in February 1994.

Adams participated in six [[Davis Cup]] ties for South Africa between 1997 and 2003, posting a 4–2 record, all in doubles.

Adams won two Grand Slam titles in mixed doubles, both with fellow South African [[Mariaan de Swardt]], taking the Australian Open title in 1999 and the French Open title in 2000.<ref>{{cite news|title=Kafelnikov captures Open title|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/268571.stm|work=BBC News|date=31 January 1999}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Djata|first1=Sundiata|title=Blacks at the Net : Black Achievement in the History of Tennis.|date=2008|publisher=Syracuse University Press|location=Syracuse, N.Y.|isbn=978-0815608981|page=82|edition=1}}</ref>
