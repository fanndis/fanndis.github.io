'''Carlos Minc Baumfeld''' (born July 12, 1951) is a Brazilian geographer, professor, environmentalist, politician and Minister of Environment in [[Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva]]'s second term as [[president of Brazil]].

==Personal life==
Minc was born on July 12, 1951. He studied at [[Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro]]. He was a student leader and participated in resistance against the military dictatorship. He was arrested in 1969 and exiled. In 1979, with the help from [[Amnesty]], he returned to Brazil.<ref name="Pt source">{{cite web |url=http://noticias.uol.com.br/ultnot/2008/05/14/ult23u2323.jhtm   |title=Planalto confirma Carlos Minc como novo ministro do Meio Ambiente |accessdate=2008-05-25 |publisher=|language=pt}}</ref>

In 1978, Minc completed his master's degree from the [[Technical University of Lisbon]]. He completed his doctorate from the [[University of Paris]] in 1984.

Minc is married and has two children.<ref name="Pt source"/>

==Political career==
Minc was one of the founding members of the [[Green Party (Brazil)|Green Party]], together with [[Fernando Gabeira]], [[Alfredo Sirkis]], and others.<ref name="Pt source"/>

Minc was elected Member state for the first time in 1986, in coalition with [[Workers' Party (Brazil)|Workers' Party]]. He was re-elected in 1994, 1998, and 2002.<ref name="Pt source"/>

On November 22, 2006, [[Sérgio Cabral Filho]], governor of the state of [[Rio de Janeiro]], appointed Minc for the post of Secretary of Environment.<ref name="Pt source"/>

On May 13, 2008, [[Marina Silva]] resigned from the post of Minister of Environment. Minc became the new Minister of Environment on May 14, 2008.<ref name="Pt source"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Amazon 'needs economic chances' |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/7402254.stm  |format= |work= |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=May 15, 2008  |accessdate=2008-05-25 }}</ref>

On March 31, 2010 resigned from Ministry to run as State Congressman (Deputado Estadual) in Rio de Janeiro by [[Workers' Party (Brazil)|Workers' Party]] and was elected with 87.210 votes.

In January 2011, was invited again to be Secretary of Environment in the Rio de Janeiro State Government where he is until now.

==Awards==
In 1989, Minc received the ''Global Award 500'', granted by the [[United Nations]] to those who stand out in the defence of the environment in the world.<ref name="Pt source"/>

==Books==
Minc is also a writer. He is the author of the following books:
* ''Como Fazer Movimento Ecológico'' (Vozes, 1985)
* ''A Reconquista da Terra'' (Zahar, 1986)
* ''Ecologia e Política no Brasil'' (Espaço e Tempo/Iuperj, 1987), co-authored with [[Fernando Gabeira]] and others
* ''Despoluindo a Política'' (Relume Dumará, 1994)
