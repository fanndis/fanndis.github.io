'''Joseph Blake''' (died 1700), the nephew of British General at Sea [[Robert Blake (admiral)|Robert Blake]],<ref name=" SouCahima">[https://books.google.com/books?id=1Pw7AAAAIAAJ&dq=%22joseph%20blake%22%20carolina&pg=PA163#v=onepage&q=%22joseph%20blake%22%20carolina&f=false ''The South Carolina Historical Magazine'']</ref> was governor of [[Province of South Carolina|colonial South Carolina]] in 1694 (chosen by the council), and from 1696 to his death 1700.

== Biography ==
Joseph Blake was born in 1663,<ref name=" Carojosb">[http://www.carolana.com/Carolina/Governors/jblake.html Carolana: Joseph Blake. President of the Council and Actring Governor of Carolina Province 1694]. Retrieved in September 14, 2015.</ref><ref name=" halsejosb">[http://www.halseymap.com/Flash/gov-detail.asp?polID=88 Preservation Society of Charleston:Joseph Blake] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150911192051/http://www.halseymap.com/flash/gov-detail.asp?polID=88 |date=11 September 2015 }}.</ref> in [[England]].<ref name=" halsejosb"/> He was the son of Benjamin Blake and nephew of [[Lord Proprietor]], [[John Archdale]].<ref name=" Carojosb"/> To early 1680s, he emigrated to Carolina.<ref name=" halsejosb"/> In 1685, Joseph Blake was appointed a Deputy by his Archdale. During a time, he was member of the Executive Councils. Late, he was named as a Deputy of Peter Colleton, 2nd Baronet. In October 29, 1694,<ref name=" Carojosb"/> he was appointed governor of [[Province of South Carolina|colonial South Carolina]] by the council,<ref name=" SouCahima"/><ref name=" Carojosb"/><ref name=" halsejosb"/> and he kept the charge until his death, in September 7, 1700.<ref name=" Carojosb"/><ref name=" halsejosb"/>

Blake married two times: The first of them was with Deborah Morton, who was daughter of Governor [[Joseph Morton (governor)|Joseph Morton]],<ref name=" halsejosb"/><ref name=" Carojosb"/> marrying with him before 1685. The second was Elizabeth Axtell, who had a son, Joseph Blake Jr. He had several lands: "Plainsfield", located on the [[Stono River]]; Newington Plantation, located in [[Dorchester County, South Carolina|Dorchester County]]; and "Pawlet" in [[Colleton County, South Carolina]].<ref name=" Carojosb"/>
