'''''I Will Follow''''' is a 2010 American [[Independent film|independent]] [[Drama (film and television)|drama]] film written and directed by [[Ava DuVernay]], who also produced the film with [[Molly Mayeux]]. It stars [[Salli Richardson|Salli Richardson-Whitfield]], [[Omari Hardwick]], [[Blair Underwood]] and [[Beverly Todd]].

==Plot==

Maye (Salli Richardson-Whitfield) is a successful artist who has taken a leave from work to care for her ill aunt, Amanda (Beverly Todd). As the film begins, Maye is moving out of the home she once shared with Amanda after her death and contemplating her relationships, her career, her past and her future.

==Production==

''I Will Follow'' was shot in 11 days on location in [[Topanga Canyon]], California, on a $50,000 budget. Its title was taken from the [[U2]] song of the same name.<ref name=nyt100512>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/10/07/movies/ava-duvernay-and-middle-of-nowhere.html|title=Sheâ€™s a Graduate of an Unusual Film School|accessdate=September 24, 2016|last=Rickey|first=Carrie|date=October 5, 2012|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref>

==Release==

''I Will Follow'' made its world premiere in New York City at the 2010 Urbanworld Film Festival, where it won audience honors. It also played at the 2010 [[Chicago International Film Festival]] and the 2010 [[AFI Film Festival]].{{citation needed|date=May 2017}} The film had a [[limited release]] on March 11, 2011. Film critic [[Roger Ebert]] gave the film three and a half out of four stars.<ref name=Ebert-IWF-2011>{{cite web|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/i-will-follow-2011|work=[[RogerEbert.com]]|title=I Will Follow|first=Roger|last=Ebert|accessdate=February 4, 2018|date=March 8, 2011}}</ref>
