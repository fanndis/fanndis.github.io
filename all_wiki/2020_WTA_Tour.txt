The '''2020 WTA Tour''' was the elite professional [[tennis]] circuit organised by the [[Women's Tennis Association]] (WTA) for the 2020 tennis season. The 2020 WTA Tour calendar originally comprised the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments (supervised by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF), the [[WTA Premier tournaments]] (Premier Mandatory, Premier 5, and regular Premier), the [[WTA International tournaments]], the [[Fed Cup]] (organized by the ITF), and the year-end championships (the [[WTA Tour Championships]] and the [[WTA Elite Trophy]]).<ref name=WTACalendar2020>{{cite web|work=wtatennis.com|publisher=WTA Tour, Inc.|title=2020 WTA calendar|url=http://wtafiles.wtatennis.com/pdf/calendar/WTA_Calendar_2020.pdf|accessdate=2019-10-15|format=PDF}}</ref>

Many tournaments were cancelled or postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], including the [[Tennis at the 2020 Summer Olympics|Tokyo Summer Olympics]].<ref name=suspension>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/51953480| title=Coronavirus: ATP and WTA Tours extend suspension until 7 June|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=18 March 2020|accessdate=18 March 2020}}</ref><ref name="suspension2">{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1651150/wta-and-atp-announce-further-suspension-of-tours|title=WTA and ATP announce further suspension of tours|date=1 April 2020|website=WTA}}</ref><ref name=Olympics>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/52020134|title=Tokyo 2020: Olympic Games postponed because of coronavirus|website=BBC Sport|date=24 March 2020}}</ref><ref name="suspension3">{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1669332/wta-announces-further-suspension-of-four-tournaments|title=WTA announces further suspension of four tournaments|date=15 May 2020|website=WTA}}</ref><ref name="china swing">{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/1738145/wta-tournaments-in-china-cancelled-for-2020|title=WTA tournaments in China cancelled for 2020|date=24 July 2020|website=WTA}}</ref>

==Statistical information==
These tables present the number of [[Types of tennis match#Singles|singles]] (S), [[Types of tennis match#Doubles|doubles]] (D), and [[Mixed doubles tennis|mixed doubles]] (X) titles won by each player and each nation during the season, within all the tournament categories of the 2019 WTA Tour: the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments, the year-end championships (the [[WTA Tour Championships]] and the [[WTA Elite Trophy]]), the [[WTA Premier tournaments]] (Premier Mandatory, Premier 5, and regular Premier), and the [[WTA International tournaments]]. The players/nations are sorted by:

# total number of titles (a doubles title won by two players representing the same nation counts as only one win for the nation);
# cumulated importance of those titles (one Grand Slam win equalling two Premier Mandatory/Premier 5 wins, one year-end championships win equalling one-and-a-half Premier Mandatory/Premier 5 win, one Premier Mandatory/Premier 5 win equalling two Premier wins, one Premier win equalling two International wins);
# a singles > doubles > mixed doubles hierarchy;
# alphabetical order (by family names for players).

==Retirements==
{{Unreferenced section|date=March 2020}}

Following is a list of notable players (winners of a main tour title, and/or part of the WTA Rankings top 100 in singles or doubles, for at least one week; and wheelchair players) who announced their retirement from professional tennis, became inactive (after not playing for more than 52 weeks), or were permanently banned from playing, during the 2020 season:<ref>{{Cite web|date=1 November 2020|title=Wozniacki, Sharapova & the WTA stars who have retired in 2020|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/photos/1645266/wozniacki-sharapova-the-wta-stars-who-have-retired-in-2020|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref> <!-- Please, add new players in the Retirements section in alphabetical order in the corresponding position. -->
*{{flagicon|NED}} '''[[Marjolein Buis]]'''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Wheelchair tennis player Marjolein Buis forced to retire after 2020 {{!}} Marjolein Buis|url=https://marjoleinbuis.nl/news/wheelchair-tennis-player-marjolein-buis-forced-to-retire-after-2020/|access-date=2020-11-25|language=nl}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|ESP}} '''[[Estrella Cabeza Candela]]''' (''born 20 February 1987 in [[Los Palacios y Villafranca]], [[Spain]]'') turned professional in 2004 and reached a career high ranking of 95 in singles and 176 in doubles. Cabeza Candela also won 13 singles and 16 doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]]. In 2020, Cabeza Candela announced her retirement on the tour.<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 October 2020|title=La tenista Estrella Cabeza: «Me gustaría hacerles el camino más fácil a quienes yo entrene»|url=https://elcorreoweb.es/el-decano-deportivo/polideportivo/la-tenista-estrella-cabeza-me-gustaria-hacerles-el-camino-mas-facil-a-quienes-yo-entrene-GY6890019|website=elcorreoweb.es|language=es-ES}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|JPN}} '''[[Rika Fujiwara]]''' (''born 19 September 1981 in [[Tokyo]], [[Japan]]'') turned professional in 1997 and reached a career high ranking of 84 in singles and 13 in doubles. Fujiwara reached six WTA doubles finals during her career, winning one of them at the [[2012 e-Boks Open|Danish Open]] in 2012. Fujiwara also won 9 singles and 36 doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]]. Fujiwara's biggest highlight was at the [[2002 French Open]], where she reached the women's doubles semifinals partnering her compatriot [[Ai Sugiyama]]. In March 2020, Fujiwara announced her retirement after 23 years on the tour.
*{{flagicon|GER}} '''[[Julia Görges]]''' (''born 2 November 1988 in [[Bad Oldesloe]], [[West Germany]]'') (modern day [[Germany]]) turned professional in 2005 and reached a career high ranking of 9 in singles and 12 in doubles. Görges won 7 WTA singles titles in her career, including 2 premier level events and the [[2017 WTA Elite Trophy]]. She scored multiple career top ten wins in her career including back to back wins against the then World no. 1 [[Caroline Wozniacki]] during the clay season in 2011. Her best grand slam result was a semifinal at [[2018 Wimbledon Championships]]. Görges announced her retirement via social media on 21st October.<ref>{{Cite web|date=21 October 2020|title=WTA celebrates career of Julia Goerges|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1870592/wta-celebrates-career-of-julia-goerges|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Jamie Hampton]]''' (''born 8 January 1990 in [[Frankfurt]], [[West Germany]]'')  turned professional in 2009 and reached a career high ranking of 24 in singles and 74 in doubles. Hampton reached one WTA singles final during her career, losing to [[Elena Vesnina]] at the [[2013 Aegon International]]. She reached the fourth round at the [[2013 French Open]], and scored wins over multiple current and former top-10 players during her short-lived career, including [[Petra Kvitová]], [[Agnieszka Radwańska]] and [[Caroline Wozniacki]]. She had not played since January 2014 and, after undergoing six surgery attempts, decided to retire in May.<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 May 2020|title=Jamie Hampton says goodbye to 'first love' as she announces retirement|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1670847/jamie-hampton-says-goodbye-to-first-love-as-she-announces-retirement|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Vania King]]''' (''born 3 February 1989 in [[Monterey Park, California|Monterey Park]], California, [[United States]]'')  turned professional in 2006 and reached a career high ranking of 50 in singles and 3 in doubles. King reached three WTA singles finals during her career, winning one of them at the [[2006 PTT Bangkok Open|Bangkok Open]] in 2006. She was most known as a doubles specialist, winning fifteen titles in her career, with her biggest achievements coming in winning the women's doubles events at both [[2010 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] and the [[2010 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] in 2010, alongside [[Yaroslava Shvedova]]. King was hampered by an ankle injury throughout the final years of her career, and despite undergoing surgery in 2017, King decided to retire in February.<ref>{{Cite web|date=26 February 2020|title=Vania King set to step away from the game|url=http://baseline.tennis.com/article/87690/vania-king-set-step-away-game|access-date=2020-11-20|website=Baseline|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|SWE}} '''[[Johanna Larsson]]''' (''born 17 August 1988 in [[Boden, Sweden|Boden]], [[Sweden]]'') turned professional in 2006 and reached a career high ranking of 45 in singles and 20 in doubles. Larsson won two WTA singles titles during her career, including at her home event in [[2015 Swedish Open|Båstad]] in 2015, and enjoyed considerable success in doubles, winning 14 titles and reaching the year-end championships final in [[2017 WTA Finals|2017]] alongside [[Kiki Bertens]]. Larsson decided to retire in February.<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 February 2020|title=Johanna Larsson announces retirement from tennis|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1627738/johanna-larsson-announces-retirement-from-tennis|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|RUS}} '''[[Ekaterina Makarova]]''' (''born 7 June 1988 in [[Moscow]], [[Russian SFSR]], [[Soviet Union]]'') (modern day [[Russia]]) turned professional in 2004 and reached a career high ranking of 8 in singles in 2015 and [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|number 1]] in doubles in 2018. A six-time Grand Slam singles quarterfinalist with two semifinal appearances at the [[2014 US Open (tennis)|2014 US Open]] and [[2015 Australian Open]], Makarova also won three WTA singles titles during her career. She achieved phenomenal success in doubles, with 3 Grand Slam women's doubles titles alongside [[Elena Vesnina]] at the [[2013 French Open]], the [[2014 US Open (tennis)|2014 US Open]] and at [[2017 Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] in 2017, as well as the mixed doubles title at the [[2012 US Open (tennis)|2012 US Open]] alongside [[Bruno Soares]]. She also partnered Vesnina to gold in the women's doubles at the [[Tennis at the 2016 Summer Olympics|2016 Olympics]], and to the title at the [[2016 WTA Finals|WTA Finals]], also in 2016. Makarova announced her retirement at the [[2020 St. Petersburg Ladies' Trophy]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 January 2020|title=Ekaterina Makarova announces retirement from tennis|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1591420/ekaterina-makarova-announces-retirement-from-tennis|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|ESP}} '''[[María José Martínez Sánchez]]''' (''born 12 August 1982 in [[Murcia]], Spain)'' turned professional in 1998 and reached a career-high ranking of 19 in singles in 2010 and 4 in doubles, also in 2010. Through her career she won a total of 5 WTA titles in singles but focused primarily in doubles later in her career winning a total of 21 titles in that discipline. In doubles she reached the semi finals of Grand Slams 3 times; at the French Open in 2010 and 2012 and at the US Open in 2012. She reached the quarter finals of all slams in doubles. In 2009 she won the Tour Finals with long time doubles partner [[Nuria Llagostera Vives]]. In January 2020 she announced her retirement.<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 January 2020|title=Martínez Sánchez calls time on storied career|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1572614/mart-nez-s-nchez-calls-time-on-storied-career|access-date=2020-11-20|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
* {{flagicon|LUX}} '''[[Mandy Minella]]''' (''born 22 November 1985 in [[Esch-sur-Alzette]], [[Luxembourg]])'' turned professional in 2001, and reached career-high rankings of 66 in singles in 2012, and 47 in doubles in 2013. Winner of 2 doubles titles on the main tour (with [[Tímea Babos]]), Minella's best results came in WTA 125K events (with 1 singles and 3 doubles titles) and on the lower tier [[ITF Women's Circuit]]. She announced she would retire in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=10 October 2019|title=Tennis: Mandy Minella announces retirement - Delano - Luxembourg in English|url=http://delano.lu/d/detail/news/tennis-mandy-minella-announces-retirement/208111|website=Delano|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|AUS}} '''[[Jessica Moore (tennis)|Jessica Moore]]''' (''born 16 August 1990 in [[Perth]], [[Australia]])'' turned professional in 2008 and reached a career-high ranking of 132 in singles in 2008 and 55 in doubles in 2019. Moore won two WTA titles in doubles, as well as 4 singles and 31 doubles titles on the ITF Circuit. She also won silver at the [[Tennis at the 2010 Commonwealth Games|2010 Commonwealth Games]] in the women's doubles, partnering [[Olivia Rogowska]]. Moore announced her retirement at the [[2020 Australian Open|Australian Open]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 January 2020|title='Absolute class': Ash Barty's incredible act for beaten opponent at Australian Open|url=https://au.sports.yahoo.com/australian-open-2020-ash-barty-incredible-act-beaten-opponent-035209644.html|website=au.sports.yahoo.com|language=en-AU}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|BUL}} '''[[Aleksandrina Naydenova]]''' (''born 29 February 1992 in [[Plovdiv]], [[Bulgaria]])'', a former world number 95 in doubles, has been issued with a lifetime ban from the sport after an investigation by the Tennis Integrity Unit (TIU) found that she had partaken in match fixing activity multiple times between 2015 and 2019. Additionally, she was charged and found guilty of failing to co-operate with the TIU investigation. Alongside the ban, she has been fined $150,000 (US).<ref>{{Cite web|date=21 November 2020|title=Aleksandrina Naydenova Banned From Tennis For Life|url=https://www.tennis-tourtalk.com/66022/aleksandrina-naydenova-banned-from-tennis-for-life|access-date=2020-11-23|website=www.tennis-tourtalk.com|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|SUI}} '''[[Romina Oprandi]]''' (''born 29 March 1986 in [[Jegenstorf]], [[Switzerland]])'' turned professional in 2005 and reached career-high rankings of 32 in singles in 2013, and 112 in doubles in 2007. Oprandi won one WTA title in doubles, as well as 26 singles and 11 doubles titles on the ITF Circuit. In May 2020 she announced her retirement.<ref>{{Cite web|date=4 May 2020|title=Former Top 40 player Announces Retirement from Tennis|url=https://www.tennisworldusa.org/tennis/news/Tennis_Stories/87069/former-top-40-player-announces-retirement-from-tennis/|access-date=2020-11-20|website=Tennis World USA|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=9 May 2020|title=Si ritira Romina Oprandi, braccio d'oro e fisico di cristallo|url=https://www.tennisworlditalia.com/tennis/news/Editoriale/69600/si-ritira-romina-oprandi-braccio-d-oro-e-fisico-di-cristallo/|access-date=2020-11-25|website=Tennis World Italia|language=it}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|FRA}} '''[[Pauline Parmentier]]''' (''born 31 January 1986 in [[Cucq]], France)'' turned professional in 2000 and reached a career-high ranking of 40 in singles, in July 2008, and 121 in doubles in October 2019. Winner of 4 singles titles on the main tour and a total of 13 ITF titles. Her best result at Grand Slam level came in reaching the fourth round at her home slam the [[2014 French Open|French Open]] in 2014. She announced in January 2019 that this would be her last year on the tour.<ref>{{Cite web|date=30 September 2020|title=Billie Jean King Cup- 'Right time to retire', as Parmentier bids farewell|url=https://www.billiejeankingcup.com/en/news/317692.aspx|website=www.billiejeankingcup.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=29 September 2020|title=Roland-Garros : Pauline Parmentier, l'heure des adieux|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Tennis/Actualites/Roland-garros-pauline-parmentier-l-heure-des-adieux/1177288|access-date=2020-11-25|website=L'Équipe|language=fr}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|BRA}} '''[[Teliana Pereira]]''' (''born 20 July 1988 in [[Águas Belas]], [[Brazil]]'') turned professional in 2005 and reached a career-high ranking of 43 in singles, in October 2015, and 117 in doubles, in September 2013. Winner of 2 singles titles on the main tour and a 22 ITF titles in single and 10 in doubles. Her best result at Grand Slam level came in reaching the second round at  the French Open in 2014, 2015 and 2016. In September 2020 she announced her retirement.<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 September 2020|title=Ex-top 50, Teliana Pereira anuncia fim da carreira no tênis aos 32 anos: "Superei as expectativas"|url=https://globoesporte.globo.com/tenis/noticia/ex-top-50-teliana-pereira-anuncia-fim-da-carreira-no-tenis-aos-32-anos-superei-as-expectativas.ghtml|access-date=2020-11-25|website=ge|language=pt-br}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=8 October 2020|title=Billie Jean King Cup- Teliana Pereira se despide del tenis|url=https://www.billiejeankingcup.com/es/noticias/317706.aspx|website=www.billiejeankingcup.com}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|SVK}} '''[[Magdaléna Rybáriková]]''' (''born 4 October 1988 in [[Piešťany]], [[Slovakia]]'') turned professional in 2005, and reached career-high rankings of 17 in singles in 2018, and 50 in doubles in 2011. Winner of 4 singles titles and 1 doubles title on the main tour (with [[Janette Husárová]]), Rybáriková's best result is reaching the singles semifinals at [[2017 Wimbledon Championships]]. At first she has announced the retirement from tennis after the [[2020 Fed Cup finals]] in [[Budapest]], but later she announced her official retirement via social media on 29th October.<ref>{{Cite web|date=29 October 2020|title=Rybarikova makes retirement official in 2020|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1879786/rybarikova-makes-retirement-official-in-2020|access-date=2020-11-20|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|RUS}} '''[[Maria Sharapova]]''' (''born 19 April 1987 in [[Nyagan]], [[Russian SFSR]], [[Soviet Union]])'' (modern day [[Russia]]) turned professional in 2001 and reached the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|world no. 1]] spot for the first time in 2005. A five-time Grand Slam singles winner (she completed the career Grand Slam in 2012), Sharapova won 36 WTA singles titles and 4 doubles titles during her career. She won also the [[WTA Championships|WTA Finals]] in 2004 and the [[Fed Cup]] in 2008. She also won the silver medal in the women's singles at the [[Tennis at the 2012 Summer Olympics|2012 Olympics]]. Sharapova announced her retirement through social media in 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|date=26 February 2020|title='Sharapova retires with questions unanswered'|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/51652982}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Mitchell|first=Kevin|date=2020-02-26|title='I'm saying goodbye': Maria Sharapova announces tennis retirement|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2020/feb/26/im-saying-goodbye-maria-sharapova-announces-tennis-retirement|access-date=2020-11-15|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|ESP}} '''[[Sílvia Soler Espinosa]]''' (''born 19 November 1987 in [[Elche]], Spain)'' turned professional in 2003, and reached a career-high ranking of no. 54 in singles in 2012, and no. 39 in doubles in 2014. Soler Espinosa reached two WTA singles finals in her career, and won one doubles title alongside [[Andreja Klepač]] at the [[2014 Connecticut Open]]. She was a three-time Grand Slam quarterfinalist in doubles, and also reached the third round on three occasions in singles, twice at the US Open and once at Roland Garros. Soler Espinosa decided to retire in May.<ref>{{Cite web|date=May 15, 2020|title=Silvia Soler-Espinosa says heartfelt farewell to tennis|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1669590/silvia-soler-espinosa-says-heartfelt-farewell-to-tennis|access-date=2020-11-20|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|ESP}} '''[[Carla Suárez Navarro]]''' (''born 3 September 1988 in [[Las Palmas de Gran Canaria]], Spain)'' turned professional in 2003, and reached a career-high ranking of no. 6 in singles in 2016, and no. 11 in doubles in 2015. After coming to prominence by reaching the quarterfinals of the [[2008 French Open – Women's Singles|2008 French Open]] as a qualifier, Suárez Navarro became a steady top-twenty player, with her best seasons coming from 2013 to 2016. She reached six more quarterfinals in [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles events, and won 2 singles titles out of 11 finals (including a Premier 5 title), and 3 doubles titles out of 9 finals (finishing as runner-up of the [[2015 WTA Finals – Doubles|2015 WTA Finals]] in doubles, alongside [[Garbiñe Muguruza]]). She announced that the 2020 season would be her last on the circuit.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Karthikeyan|first=Rishi|date=2019-12-03|title=Carla Suarez Navarro to retire in 2020|url=https://www.essentiallysports.com/carla-suarez-navarro-to-retire-in-2020/|access-date=2020-11-20|website=EssentiallySports|language=en-US}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|USA}} '''[[Anna Tatishvili]]''' (''born 3 February 1990 in [[Tbilisi]], [[Georgian SSR]], [[Soviet Union]]'') (modern day [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]]) turned professional in 2005 and reached a career-high ranking of no. 50 in singles and 59 in doubles. She won one WTA doubles title in her career in [[2014 Generali Ladies Linz|Linz]] in 2014 alongside [[Raluca Olaru]], as well as reaching two further doubles finals and winning 11 ITF Tour singles titles. Her greatest achievement at Grand Slam level came in reaching the fourth round at the [[2012 US Open (tennis)|2012 US Open]]. Tatishvili decided to retire in March, after battling for several years with a persistent ankle injury.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Marija|date=2020-03-26|title=Anna Tatishvili announces immediate retirement from tennis|url=https://womenstennisblog.com/2020/03/26/anna-tatishvili-announces-retirement-from-tennis/|website=Women's Tennis Blog|language=en-US}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|DEN}} '''[[Caroline Wozniacki]]''' (''born 11 July 1990 in [[Odense]], [[Denmark]])'' joined the circuit in 2005, and reached the [[List of WTA number 1 ranked tennis players|world no. 1]] spot for the first time in 2010. She would spend a total of 71 weeks as number one which puts her at 9th on the all-time list as of January 2020. Wozniacki won a total of 30 WTA titles in singles and 2 WTA titles in doubles. Wozniacki reached three Grand Slam finals at the US Open in 2009 and 2014 and at the Australian Open 2018 where she won her first and only Grand Slam beating [[Simona Halep]]. She also won the WTA Championships Finals in Singapore 2017. Wozniacki retired after a third round defeat to [[Ons Jabeur]] at the 2020 Australian Open.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Wozniacki to retire after Australian Open|language=en-GB|work=BBC Sport|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/50690686|access-date=2020-11-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Wozniacki to retire following Australian Open|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/1519639/wozniacki-to-retire-following-australian-open|access-date=2020-11-15|website=Women's Tennis Association|language=en}}</ref>
