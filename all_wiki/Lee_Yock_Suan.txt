'''Lee Yock Suan''' ({{zh|t=|s=李玉全|p=Lǐ Yù Quán}}; born 30 September 1946) is a former [[Singaporean]] [[politician]]. A member of the country's governing [[People's Action Party]], he served in the [[Cabinet of Singapore|Cabinet]] from 1987 to 2004, and was a [[Parliament of Singapore|Member of Parliament]] (MP) from 1980 to 2006.

==Career==
Lee worked at Singapore's [[Economic Development Board]] from 1969 to 1980, initially as a projects officer before becoming Divisional Director (Industry). From 1980 to 1981, he served as Deputy Managing Director of the Petroleum Corporation of Singapore.<ref>[http://www.ara-asia.com/home/corp/BOD_lee.html Board of Directors: Lee Yock Suan] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120111164522/http://www.ara-asia.com/home/corp/BOD_lee.html |date=2012-01-11 }}, ara-asia.com, retrieved 22 March 2012.</ref>

===Political career===
Lee was first elected to Parliament at the [[1980 Singaporean general election|1980 general election]]. He served as an MP representing the [[Cheng San Single Member Constituency]] (1980–88), the [[Cheng San Group Representation Constituency]] (1988-2001) and the [[East Coast Group Representation Constituency]] (2001–06).

After serving as a [[Minister of State#Minor government ranks|Minister of State]] at the [[Ministry of National Development (Singapore)|Ministry of National Development]] and the [[Ministry of Finance (Singapore)|Ministry of Finance]] from 1983 to 1984, Lee was made the Acting [[Ministry of Manpower (Singapore)|Minister for Labour]] in 1985.

He became a full member of the Cabinet in 1987, and served as the Minister for Labour (1987–91), [[Ministry of Education (Singapore)|Minister for Education]] (1992–97), [[Ministry of Finance (Singapore)|Second Minister for Finance]] (1997–98), [[Ministry of Trade and Industry (Singapore)|Minister for Trade and Industry]] (1997–99), [[Minister for Information, Communications and the Arts (Singapore)|Minister for Information and the Arts]] (1999–2001), [[Ministry of the Environment and Water Resources|Minister for the Environment]] (1999–2001), [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Singapore)|Second Minister for Foreign Affairs]] (2001–04) and [[Prime Minister's Office (Singapore)|Minister in the Prime Minister's Office]] (2001–04).

Lee also served as the Deputy Chairman of the [[People's Association]] from 1984 to 1991, and as Chairman of the [[Singapore Labour Foundation]] from 1997 to 2002.

Lee stepped down from the Cabinet in 2004. He remained an MP until 2006, before retiring from politics at the [[2006 Singaporean general election|2006 general election]].<ref>[http://www.comp.nus.edu.sg/~wongls/icaas-web/news/stnews-apr06.html Ex-minister happy to step down] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110807064712/http://www.comp.nus.edu.sg/~wongls/icaas-web/news/stnews-apr06.html |date=2011-08-07 }}, ''The Straits Times'', April 2006.</ref>

==Education==
Lee was educated at [[Queenstown Secondary School|Queenstown Secondary Technical School]] and [[Raffles Institution]], before being awarded a [[President's Scholar]]ship to study at [[Imperial College London]] where he completed a [[Bachelor of Science|BSc (Hons)]] degree in [[Chemical engineering|Chemical Engineering]]. He subsequently completed a Diploma in Business Administration at the [[University of Singapore]].<ref>[http://infopedia.nl.sg/articles/SIP_1523_2009-06-28.html Lee Yock Suan] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120307141233/http://infopedia.nl.sg/articles/SIP_1523_2009-06-28.html |date=2012-03-07 }}, infopedia.nl.sg, retrieved 21 March 2012.</ref>

==Personal life==
Lee is an ethnic Hokkien<ref>{{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/historicaldictio0000corf|url-access=registration|quote=lee yock suan hokkien.|title=Historical Dictionary of Singapore|publisher=Scarecrow Press|author=Justin Corfield|page=[https://archive.org/details/historicaldictio0000corf/page/149 149]|isbn=9780810873872|date=2010-12-02}}</ref> and is married to Adeline Oh Choon Neo and has one son and one daughter. His son, [[Desmond Lee (Singaporean politician)|Desmond Lee]],<ref>[http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne+News/Singapore/Story/A1Story20110324-269753.html Yes to evolution, no to revolution] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150806032206/http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne+News/Singapore/Story/A1Story20110324-269753.html |date=2015-08-06 }}, ''My Paper'', 24 March 2011.</ref> like himself, is a politician who is currently a [[Parliament of Singapore|Member of Parliament]] for [[West Coast Group Representation Constituency|West Coast GRC]] and Minister for National Development.
