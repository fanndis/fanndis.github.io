==Career==

=== Trump Organization ===
Hicks began in [[public relations]] with the New York City firm Zeno Group.<ref name="ctpost" /> She joined public relations firm [[Matthew Hiltzik|Hiltzik Strategies]] in 2012, after meeting the firm's founder at a [[Super Bowl]] event, and worked there for [[Donald Trump]]'s daughter [[Ivanka Trump]], on her fashion line, and then on other Trump ventures.<ref name="GQ">{{cite web |last=Nuzzi |first=Olivia |date=June 20, 2016 |url=https://www.gq.com/story/hope-hicks-mystifying-triumph-donald-trump |title=The Mystifying Triumph of Hope Hicks, Donald Trump's Right-Hand Woman |work=[[GQ]] |accessdate=June 20, 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160620115755/http://www.gq.com/story/hope-hicks-mystifying-triumph-donald-trump |archive-date=June 20, 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Diamond |first1=Jeremy |date=November 27, 2017 |title=Hope Hicks: A witness to Trump's rise |work=CNN |url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/11/26/politics/hope-hicks-donald-trump-white-house/index.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171127021148/http://www.cnn.com/2017/11/26/politics/hope-hicks-donald-trump-white-house/index.html |archive-date=November 27, 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In August 2014, Hicks joined [[the Trump Organization]] full-time.<ref name="wp-one">{{cite web |last=Viebeck |first=Elise |date=July 27, 2015 |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2015/07/27/hope-hicks-flies-quietly-in-the-eye-of-the-trump-storm/ |title=Hope Hicks flies quietly in the eye of the Trump storm |work=[[The Washington Post]] |accessdate=June 20, 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160802144030/https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2015/07/27/hope-hicks-flies-quietly-in-the-eye-of-the-trump-storm/ |archive-date=August 2, 2016 }}</ref> She worked for Ivanka Trump inside [[Trump Tower]], helping expand her fashion label (the Ivanka Trump Collection) and modeling for her online store.<ref name=autogenerated9>{{Cite news |url=http://www.smh.com.au/lifestyle/celebrity/hope-hicks-and-kellyanne-conway--the-women-of-donald-trumps-inner-circle-20161114-gsop4c.html |title=Hope Hicks and Kellyanne Conway&nbsp;– the women of Donald Trump's inner circle |first=Jenna |last=Clarke |date=November 15, 2016 |work=The Sydney Morning Herald |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170227002053/http://www.smh.com.au/lifestyle/celebrity/hope-hicks-and-kellyanne-conway--the-women-of-donald-trumps-inner-circle-20161114-gsop4c.html |archive-date=February 27, 2017}}</ref> In October 2014, she began working directly for Donald Trump.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.politico.com/story/2017/11/16/hope-hicks-mueller-russia-probe-trump-244967 |title=Hope Hicks may hold the keys to Mueller's Russia puzzle |last=Samuelshohn |first=Darren |date=November 16, 2017 |work=Politico |access-date=November 26, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171126050700/https://www.politico.com/story/2017/11/16/hope-hicks-mueller-russia-probe-trump-244967|archive-date=November 26, 2017|url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Trump 2016 campaign ===
In January 2015, Donald Trump chose Hicks, who was 26 at the time, for the role of [[press secretary]] for his potential presidential campaign.<ref name="nymag">{{cite web |url=http://nymag.com/daily/intelligencer/2016/04/inside-the-donald-trump-presidential-campaign.html |title=Operation Trump: Inside the Most Unorthodox Campaign in Political History |last=Sherman |first=Gabriel |author-link=Gabriel Sherman |date=April 3, 2016 |work=[[New York (magazine)|New York]] |accessdate=June 20, 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160622091418/http://nymag.com/daily/intelligencer/2016/04/inside-the-donald-trump-presidential-campaign.html |archive-date=June 22, 2016 }}</ref><ref name="post">{{cite web |last=Tucker |first=Reed |date=September 25, 2015 |url=https://nypost.com/2015/09/25/meet-trumps-26-year-old-mystery-woman/ |title=Meet Trump's 26-year-old mystery woman |work=[[New York Post]] |accessdate=June 20, 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160625063355/http://nypost.com/2015/09/25/meet-trumps-26-year-old-mystery-woman/ |archive-date=June 25, 2016 }}</ref> Trump summoned her to his office and, as she tells it, "Mr. Trump looked at me and said, 'I'm thinking about running for president, and you're going to be my press secretary.{{'"}}<ref name=autogenerated9 /> Until that time, she had never worked in politics or volunteered on a campaign.<ref name=autogenerated7 /> After Trump's first primary victories, Hicks was asked to choose between staying with the Trump Organization or working on the campaign full-time. She initially decided to leave the campaign, but Trump convinced her to remain, and she stayed on as press secretary.<ref name="GQ" />

During the campaign, she played the role of gatekeeper to press members who wanted to speak with Trump, handling over 250 requests a day and deciding which reporters would be allowed to speak with him.<ref name="cosmo" /><ref name=autogenerated7/> Hicks also took dictation from Trump for his [[Twitter|tweets]], and then sent the text to another person in the Trump organization who sent the tweets from Trump's official account.<ref name=autogenerated9/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.yahoo.com/celebrity/10-things-know-hope-hicks-152314456.html |title=14 Things to Know About Hope Hicks, Donald Trump's 27-Year-Old Former Model Press Secretary |first=Michael |last=Sebastian |date=June 21, 2016 |website=Yahoo! News |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170604133512/https://www.yahoo.com/celebrity/10-things-know-hope-hicks-152314456.html |archive-date=June 4, 2017}}</ref> When in New York City, she would spend most of her day in Trump's office, handling inquiries from the press and taking dictation from him to tweet.<ref name="nymag" />

=== Trump administration ===
On December 22, 2016, it was announced that Hicks would become part of the [[Trump administration]], in the newly created position of the [[White House Director of Strategic Communications|White House director of strategic communications]]. In January 2017, Hicks was included on the [[Forbes 30 Under 30|''Forbes'' 30 Under 30]] list, having "served as a one-woman press team" for Trump's presidential campaign.<ref name=autogenerated6>{{cite web |title=Forbes 30 Under 30 |url=https://www.forbes.com/pictures/fjle45eeelm/hope-hicks-28/ |website=Forbes |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170106030349/http://www.forbes.com/pictures/fjle45eeelm/hope-hicks-28/ |archive-date=January 6, 2017 }}</ref>

On August 16, 2017, she was appointed as the interim [[White House Communications Director|White House communications director]] (the last director having been [[Anthony Scaramucci]]). ''[[Politico]]'' labeled her the "Untouchable Hope Hicks", as she was considered one of the few White House officials whose job was safe, and one of only two White House communications officials Scaramucci had announced were definitely staying when he was first hired.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://heavy.com/news/2017/08/hope-hicks-donald-trump-ivanka-melania-relationship-photos-communications-director-married-experience/ |title=Hope Hicks & Donald Trump: 5 Fast Facts You Need to Know |publisher=Heavy |date=August 16, 2017 |first=Jessica |last=McBride |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170816160807/http://heavy.com/news/2017/08/hope-hicks-donald-trump-ivanka-melania-relationship-photos-communications-director-married-experience/ |archive-date=August 16, 2017 }}</ref> She was appointed permanent White House communications director on September 12, 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/hope-hicks-named-white-house-communications-director-after-assuming-role-on-interim-basis-1505231198 |title=Hope Hicks Named Permanent White House Communications Director |work=The Wall Street Journal |date=September 12, 2017 |first=Rebecca |last=Ballhaus |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170912163250/https://www.wsj.com/articles/hope-hicks-named-white-house-communications-director-after-assuming-role-on-interim-basis-1505231198 |archive-date=September 12, 2017 }}</ref>

On February 27, 2018, Hicks gave nine hours of closed-door testimony to the [[United States House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|House Intelligence Committee]].
