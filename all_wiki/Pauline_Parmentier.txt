'''Pauline Parmentier''' ({{IPA-fr|pɔlin paʁmɑ̃tje, po-}}; born 31 January 1986) is a French professional [[tennis]] player.

Her career-high [[WTA rankings|WTA singles ranking]] is world No. 40, which she attained on 21 July 2008. On 30 April 2012, she peaked at world No. 89 on the WTA doubles rankings. She has won four singles titles on the [[WTA Tour]], as well as ten singles and three doubles titles on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]]. She competed in the [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|singles]] and [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's doubles|women's doubles]] events at the [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics|2008 Summer Olympics]], and has been playing for [[France Fed Cup team|France]] in the [[Fed Cup]] since 2010 (win/loss record: 7–14).

==Personal life==
Parmentier was born in the northern French town of [[Cucq]]. Parmentier's parents are named Dominique and Jean-Philippe and she has two older brothers named Olivier and Julien. Pauline began to play tennis at age six and trained at the Mouratoglou Tennis Academy for five years.<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/players/player/160515/title/pauline-parmentier-0|title=Pauline Parmemtier WTA website|work=WTA Tour official website}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Parmentier RG15 (4) (18684412844).jpg|thumb|Parmentier, 2015]]

===2000–2003===
In 2000, Parmentier played only one tournament on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]]; she lost her main-draw opening match in both the singles and doubles events of a $10k tournament held in November 2000 in the French commune of [[Villenave-d'Ornon]].<ref name="PaulineParmentierITFtennis">{{cite web|url=http://www.itftennis.com/procircuit/players/player/profile.aspx?playerid=100003780|title=ITF profile of Pauline Parmentier|publisher=ITF}}</ref>

In 2001, she played just three [[2001 ITF Women's Circuit|ITF]] tournaments ([[Villenave-d'Ornon]], [[Le Havre]] and [[Deauville]] - all held in November, $10k each) and only took part in their singles events; she was eliminated in the qualifying rounds of all of them.<ref name="PaulineParmentierITFtennis" />

Parmentier did not play a single ITF tournament in 2002.<ref name="PaulineParmentierITFtennis" />
She did not have any year-end WTA singles or doubles ranking for 2000, 2001 and 2002.<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" />

Parmentier played 13 tournaments in the [[2003 ITF Women's Circuit]], appearing in their singles or doubles events or both.<ref name="PaulineParmentierITFtennis" />

===2004–2005===
In May 2004, Parmentier made her [[2004 WTA Tour|WTA Tour]] singles debut at the [[2004 Internationaux de Strasbourg – Singles|Internationaux de Strasbourg]], losing in the second qualifying round to [[Henrieta Nagyová]].<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" /> At her Grand Slam debut at the [[2004 French Open]] (she received [[wild card (sports)|wild cards]] for both events), she lost in the second [[2004 French Open – Women's Singles Qualifying|singles qualifying]] round to [[Roberta Vinci]] and in the first round of the [[2004 French Open – Women's Doubles|women's doubles]].

It was in 2005 at the [[2005 French Open - Women's Singles|French Open]] and [[2005 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]] that Parmentier appeared in the singles main draw of a [[2005 WTA Tour|WTA]] tournament for the first two times in her career. At the French Open, she entered as a wild card and lost in the first round to [[Iveta Benešová]]. At the US Open, she won three qualifying matches to reach the main draw, where she lost to No. 2 seed [[Lindsay Davenport]] in the second round.<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" />

===2007: First WTA singles title===
Parmentier won three qualifying matches to reach the main draw of the [[2007 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]] where she defeated [[Tatjana Malek]] in the first round and fell to [[Martina Hingis]] in the second. She broke into the top 100 (at world No. 87) of the [[WTA rankings]] for the first time in her career on 10 September 2007 (her singles ranking was No. 106 on August 27, at the start of the 2007 US Open).<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" /> Parmentier won her first career [[2007 WTA Tour|WTA]] singles title at the [[2007 Tashkent Open|Tashkent Open]] as an unseeded player in October 2007; she did not drop a set during the tournament, defeating the three top seeds [[Dominika Cibulková]] (first round), [[Olga Govortsova]] (semifinals) and [[Victoria Azarenka]] (final)] en route to winning the title.
She also won three [[2007 ITF Women's Circuit|ITF]] singles titles in 2007.

===2008: Second WTA singles title===
On 18 February 2008, two days after losing in the semifinals of the Tier III [[2008 Cachantún Cup – Singles|Cachantún Cup]] in [[Viña del Mar]] to top seed [[Flavia Pennetta]], Parmentier made her debut in the top 50 of the singles rankings; she rose from world No. 53 at the start of the 2008 Cachantún Cup to No. 47.<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" /> She won the title at [[2008 Gastein Ladies – Singles|Bad Gastein]] in July - it was her second career WTA (and first Tier III) singles title. Seeded No. 4, she defeated top seed [[Ágnes Szávay]] in the semifinals before defeating qualifier [[Lucie Hradecká]] in the final. Parmentier was nominated to play in the [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics|2008 Summer Olympics]] after [[Mary Pierce]] and [[Amélie Mauresmo]] withdrew. She lost her [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|singles]] first-round match to the No. 16 seed [[Dominika Cibulková]] and her [[Tennis at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's doubles|women's doubles]] first-round match (she was paired with [[Tatiana Golovin]]) to the Indian pair of [[Sania Mirza]] and [[Sunitha Rao]] by walkover.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/tennis/7517948.stm|title=French stars to miss the Olympics|work=[[BBC Sport]]|date=21 July 2008|accessdate=22 July 2008}}</ref>

===2010===
Parmentier made her [[Fed Cup]] debut at the [[2010 Fed Cup World Group|World Group]] quarterfinal tie against the team of the [[United States Fed Cup team|United States]] in February 2010. She lost her first match against [[Melanie Oudin]] but won her second match against [[Christina McHale]] by the same scoreline. France lost the tie 1–4.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fedcup.com/en/players/player.aspx?id=800224642|title=Pauline Parmentier at the Fed Cup|publisher=Fed Cup official website}}</ref>

Parmentier qualified for the singles main draw of the [[2010 Sony Ericsson Open – Women's Singles|Sony Ericsson Open]] by defeating American teenager [[Lauren Davis]] and [[Tamarine Tanasugarn]] in the qualifying rounds; in the main draw she beat [[Sybille Bammer]] in the first round before losing to No. 25 seed [[Ana Ivanovic]] in the second round.

===2012===
In late May, the unseeded Parmentier upset the top seed [[Sabine Lisicki]] (who was then ranked world No. 12 in the singles ranking) in the first round of the [[2012 Internationaux de Strasbourg – Singles|Internationaux de Strasbourg]]. She then defeated [[Stéphanie Foretz]] in the second round and [[Alexandra Panova]] in the quarterfinals to reach her first WTA singles semifinal since winning the title in [[2008 Gastein Ladies – Singles|Bad Gastein]]; she lost her semifinal to her compatriot and unseeded wildcard [[Alizé Cornet]] in three sets.<ref name="ParmentierIstanbul2018renaissance">{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/parmentier-continues-renaissance-make-istanbul-final|title=Parmentier continues renaissance to make Istanbul final|publisher=WTA Tour official website|date=28 April 2018}}</ref>

At the [[2012 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]], as an unseeded player, Parmentier defeated No. 25 seed [[Yanina Wickmayer]] in the second round before losing to No. 5 seed [[Petra Kvitová]] in the third; it was the first time that she had advanced to the third round of the main draw in a Grand Slam tournament.

===2013===
In 2013, Parmentier won only one match ([[2013 ASB Classic – Singles|Auckland]], held in the first week of January) and exited the first round nine times in ten [[2013 WTA Tour|WTA]] singles main-draw events, including the Australian Open, French Open and Wimbledon. She missed several events in April and May because of a right shoulder injury. In the second half of the year, she played the singles events of eleven tournaments (ten of those were [[2013 ITF Women's Circuit|ITF]] tournaments), including the [[2013 US Open – Women's Singles Qualifying|US Open qualifying event]].<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" />

===2014: French Open fourth round===
In the first week of January, the unseeded Parmentier lost in the semifinals of the 25k [[2014 ITF Women's Circuit (January–March)|tournament]] in [[Hong Kong]] to [[Elizaveta Kulichkova]] in three sets after having defeated top seed [[Magda Linette]] in the first round and eighth-seeded [[Mayo Hibi]] in the quarterfinals.

At the [[2014 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]], Parmentier achieved her career-best singles showing in a Grand Slam tournament. She had received a main-draw wildcard and was ranked world No. 145 in the WTA rankings coming into that tournament. There she defeated three players then ranked in the top 100 (the No. 17 seed Roberta Vinci, the unseeded [[Yaroslava Shvedova]] and the unseeded [[Mona Barthel]]) before losing to the unseeded [[Garbiñe Muguruza]] in the fourth round.<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lavoixdunord.fr/archive/recup/sports/pauline-parmentier-c-est-un-truc-de-dingue-video-ia233b0n2175247?bot=1|title =Pauline Parmentier: "C’est un truc de dingue" (VIDÉO)|publisher=La Voix du Nord|date=31 May 2014}}</ref>

===2016===
In the first five months of 2016, Parmentier reached at least the last eight of the singles main draw in four [[2016 WTA Tour|WTA]] tournaments as a lucky loser, unseeded player or wildcard; she lost to No. 8 seed Dominika Cibulková in the semifinals in [[2016 Katowice Open – Singles|Katowice]] (that was her first singles semifinal appearance on the WTA Tour since the 2012 Internationaux de Strasbourg) and was defeated in the singles quarterfinals in [[2016 Monterrey Open – Singles|Monterrey]], [[2016 Grand Prix SAR La Princesse Lalla Meryem – Singles|Rabat]] and [[2016 Internationaux de Strasbourg – Singles|Strasbourg]].<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" /><ref name="ParmentierIstanbul2018renaissance" />

She was the No. 88 in the singles rankings coming into the [[2016 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]], defeating No. 31 seed Monica Niculescu in the first round and [[Irina Falconi]] in the second before losing to No. 8 seed [[Timea Bacsinszky]] in the third.<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" />

In July, the unseeded Parmentier reached her fifth and final [[2016 WTA Tour|WTA]] singles quarterfinal of the year at the [[2016 BRD Bucharest Open – Singles|Bucharest]] tournament, where she lost to [[Vania King]].<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" />

===2017===
In February, Parmentier played only one match (which was the fourth and final singles rubber) in the [[2017 Fed Cup World Group|Fed Cup World Group quarterfinal]] against [[Switzerland Fed Cup team|Switzerland]]. She lost it to [[Belinda Bencic]], what enabled Switzerland to take an unassailable 3-1 lead and sent France to the [[2017 Fed Cup World Group Play-offs|World Group Play-offs]]. Three weeks after her Fed Cup defeat, the unseeded Parmentier lost in the quarterfinals of the [[2017 WTA Tour|WTA Tour]] tournament in [[2017 Abierto Mexicano Telcel – Women's Singles|Acapulco]] to top seed [[Mirjana Lučić-Baroni]].

Coming into the Premier Mandatory [[2017 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|BNP Paribas Open]] held in March, Parmentier had never in her singles career beaten a player ranked in the top ten of the WTA singles rankings. In that tournament, Parmentier came close to doing so, holding a set point in the first set and leading 4–1 in the third set of her third-round match against world No. 2, [[Angelique Kerber]], before losing in three sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Tennis/Actualites/Pauline-parmentier-la-fin-est-cruelle/785342|title=Pauline Parmentier : «La fin est cruelle|publisher=L'Équipe|date=13 March 2017}}</ref> She also lost in the third round of the [[2017 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami Open]] (the next Premier Mandatory tournament) to world No. 11 [[Johanna Konta]], after causing an upset by beating No. 24 seed [[Tímea Babos]] in the second round.

After her good performances at the BNP Paribas Open and Miami Open, Parmentier lost in the first round of her next four tournaments (all of them WTA tournaments) - [[2017 Ladies Open Biel Bienne – Singles|Biel]], [[2017 Grand Prix SAR La Princesse Lalla Meryem – Singles|Rabat]], [[2017 Mutua Madrid Open – Women's Singles|Madrid]] and [[2017 Internationaux de Strasbourg – Singles|Strasbourg]]. At the Strasbourg tournament, she failed to convert two match points while leading 6–5 in the final set against the American qualifier [[Madison Brengle]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Tennis/Actualites/Pauline-parmentier-tombe-au-premier-tour-du-tournoi-de-strasbourg/803574|title=Pauline Parmentier tombe au premier tour du tournoi de Strasbourg|publisher=L'Équipe|date=22 May 2017}}</ref> She ended her run of four consecutive singles main-draw first-round defeats at the [[2017 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]] when she defeated [[Irina Khromacheva]] before losing to [[Carina Witthöft]] in the second round.

In July, she reached her second [[2017 WTA Tour|WTA Tour]] singles quarterfinal of the year at the tournament in [[2017 BRD Bucharest Open – Singles|Bucharest]], where she eliminated No. 5 seed, [[Elise Mertens]], in the second round before losing her quarterfinal match against No. 7 seed, Irina-Camelia Begu. In her third-last event of the year in [[2017 BGL Luxembourg Open – Singles|Luxembourg City]], Parmentier won three qualifying and three main-draw matches to reach her first and only singles semifinal of the 2017 WTA Tour, where she lost to Carina Witthöft in three sets (it was Witthöft's third straight win over Parmentier).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/puig-witthoeft-set-final-showdown-luxembourg|title=Puig, Witthoeft set up final showdown in Luxembourg|publisher=WTA Tour official website|date=20 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lexpress.fr/actualites/1/sport/tennis-pauline-parmentier-echoue-en-demi-finales_1954435.html|title=Tennis: Pauline Parmentier échoue en demi-finales|publisher=L'Express|date=20 October 2017}}</ref> In November, Parmentier narrowly failed to qualify for her first [[WTA 125K series]] singles final when she was beaten in three sets in the [[2017 Open de Limoges – Singles|semifinals]] by [[Monica Niculescu]] at the Open de Limoges. However, she reached her first career 125K [[2017 Open de Limoges – Doubles|doubles final]] at Limoges; she and her partner [[Chloé Paquet]] lost the final to [[Valeria Savinykh]] and [[Maryna Zanevska]].

===2018: Two more WTA singles titles ten years after her last one===
In the first 15 weeks of the year, Parmentier compiled a singles match record of two wins and 13 defeats. During that period, she won her first-round match but lost her second-round match in the singles main draw events at the [[2018 Taiwan Open – Singles|Taiwan Open]] and in [[2018 Engie Open de Seine-et-Marne – Singles|Croissy-Beaubourg]]. She registered eleven other singles defeats during that period by losing both her matches in the [[2018 Fed Cup World Group|Fed Cup World Group]] quarterfinal tie against [[Belgium Fed Cup team|Belgium]] on February 10–11, and her opening matches at seven [[2018 WTA Tour|WTA]] tournaments ([[2018 WTA Shenzhen Open – Singles|Shenzhen]], [[2018 Hobart International – Singles|Hobart]], [[2018 Australian Open – Women's Singles|Australian Open]], [[2018 Hungarian Ladies Open – Singles|Budapest]], [[2018 Abierto Mexicano Telcel – Women's Singles|Acapulco]], [[2018 BNP Paribas Open – Women's Singles|Indian Wells]] and [[2018 Miami Open – Women's Singles|Miami]]) and two [[2018 ITF Women's Circuit|ITF]] tournaments ([[2018 Engie Open Andrézieux-Bouthéon 42 – Singles|Andrézieux-Bouthéon]] and [[2018 Nana Trophy – Singles|Tunis]]).<ref name="PaulineParmentierWTAtennis" /> On April 21–22, Parmentier played in France's [[2018 Fed Cup World Group|Fed Cup World Group]] semifinal tie against the [[United States Fed Cup team|United States]] in [[Aix-en-Provence]]. She pushed two players ranked in the top 20 to tight losses, losing first to [[Sloane Stephens]] and then to [[Madison Keys]]; the Americans won the tie 3–2.<ref name="ParmentierIstanbul2018renaissance" />

At the [[2018 İstanbul Cup – Singles|Istanbul Cup]], held in the last week of April, Parmentier registered her first career singles win over a player ranked in the top ten of the WTA singles rankings in her 16th attempt by defeating the top seed and world No. 2, [[Caroline Wozniacki]], who retired in the quarterfinals, after leading 4–6, 6–3 due to a left abdominal injury.<ref name="ParmentierIstanbul2018renaissance" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/parmentier-advances-past-injured-wozniacki-istanbul|title=Parmentier advances past injured Wozniacki in Istanbul|publisher=WTA Tour official website|date=27 April 2018}}</ref> She then defeated the No. 7 seed, Irina-Camelia Begu, in the semifinals to reach her first WTA Tour singles final since winning the [[2008 Gastein Ladies – Singles|2008 Gastein Ladies]] title. In the final, she defeated the unseeded [[Polona Hercog]] to win her third career singles title. On 30 April, the day after winning the Istanbul Cup singles title, Parmentier rose from No. 122 (seven days earlier) to world No. 76 in the singles rankings.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Tennis/Actualites/Pauline-parmentier-s-est-imposee-a-istantbul-dix-ans-apres-son-dernier-titre/897089|title=Pauline Parmentier s'est imposée à Istanbul, dix ans après son dernier titre|publisher=L'Équipe|date=29 April 2018}}</ref>

She lost in the first and second round in her next two tournaments, in [[2018 Open de Cagnes-sur-Mer – Singles|Cagnes-sur-Mer]] and [[2018 Internationaux de Strasbourg – Singles|Strasbourg]] respectively. She entered the main draw of the [[2018 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]] as a wildcard, and defeated [[Chloé Paquet]] (another French wildcard) and Alizé Cornet (seeded No. 32) in the first and second rounds respectively, both in three sets. She lost to second seeded Caroline Wozniacki in the third round. Parmentier played only one pre-Wimbledon grass-court tournament in June, in [[2018 Fuzion 100 Southsea Trophy – Singles|Southsea]], where she was defeated by [[Kirsten Flipkens]] in the semifinals. Parmentier lost her opening matches in her next two tournaments ([[2018 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|Wimbledon]] and [[2018 Grand Est Open 88 – Singles|Contrexéville]]) to [[Taylor Townsend (tennis)|Taylor Townsend]] and [[Zheng Saisai]] respectively. Parmentier entered the [[2018 Bucharest Open – Singles|Bucharest Open]] as the No. 7 seed and defeated [[Alexandra Dulgheru]] in three sets. In her second-round match against [[Wang Yafan]], Parmentier was trailing 6–7, 0–3 when she was forced to retire because of a neck injury.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sport365.fr/tennis-wta-bucarest-parmentier-a-jete-leponge-7601359.html|title =Tennis – WTA – Bucarest : Parmentier a jété l'éponge|publisher=Sport365|date=18 July 2018}}</ref> The following week, she was beaten in the first round in [[2018 Moscow River Cup – Singles|Moscow]] by third-seeded [[Anastasija Sevastova]]. Parmentier entered the main draw of the [[2018 Connecticut Open – Singles|New Haven]] tournament as a lucky loser, losing in the first round to [[Anett Kontaveit]]. She lost her opening matches in her next two tournaments ([[2018 US Open – Women's Singles|US Open]] and [[2018 Oracle Challenger Series – Chicago – Women's Singles|Chicago]]) to [[Madison Keys]] and [[Dayana Yastremska]] respectively.
