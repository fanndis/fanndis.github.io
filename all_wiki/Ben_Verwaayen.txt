'''Bernardus Johannes Maria "Ben" Verwaayen''' [[Order of Orange-Nassau|Officier in de Orde van Oranje Nassau]] (born 11 February 1952) is a [[Netherlands|Dutch]] businessman and a general partner of Keen Venture Partners.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.keenventurepartners.com/#team|title=Keen Venture Partners {{!}}|website=www.keenventurepartners.com|language=nl|access-date=2018-05-18}}</ref>
He was [[Chief Executive Officer]] of [[telecommunications]] company [[Alcatel-Lucent]] from 2008 to 2013.

==Early life==
Verwaayen is the fifth of six children born to a family who owned a [[chemicals]] business in [[Driebergen]].<ref name="Telg2">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/money/main.jhtml?xml=/money/2008/09/07/ccprof107.xml&page=2|title=The Ben Verwaayen manifesto - Page2|publisher=The Telegraph|date=2008-09-07|access-date=2008-09-07 | location=London}}</ref> At school, he organised the first student parliament, and after graduating from [[Utrecht University]] with a degree in [[law]] and [[international relations]] in 1975, wanted to be a journalist or politician.<ref name="Telg2"/>

==Career==
On graduation, he decided to immediately undertake his [[National Service]] with the [[Royal Netherlands Army]], where he founded the ''Algemene Vereniging Nederlandse Militairen'' (General Association of Dutch soldiers), a [[trade union|union]] that existed until 1996.

On leaving the army, to enable him to continue his work with the trade union, he joined a subsidiary of [[ITT Corporation]] because it gave him time to sit on a Dutch state committee to reform the army.<ref name="Telg2"/> His first promotion came after he went to the [[European Union Parliament]] in [[Brussels]] to protest about the company's alleged role in the overthrow of [[Salvador Allende]]'s government in [[Chile]]; ITT responded by making him its Netherlands public relations chief.<ref name="Telg1">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/money/main.jhtml?xml=/money/2008/09/07/ccprof107.xml|title=The Ben Verwaayen manifesto|publisher=The Telegraph|date=2008-09-07|access-date=2008-09-07 | location=London}}</ref>

In 1988, Verwaayen became a director of [[PTT Telecom]], the state-owned Dutch telecoms group that was a forerunner of today's [[KPN]]; and then was appointed a board member of [[Lucent Technologies]] in 1997, moving to the United States. A committed [[Anglophile]],<ref name="Telg3"/> he became [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] of [[BT Group|BT]] in 2003.<ref>*website BTgroup [http://www.btplc.com/Thegroup/Theboard/BenVerwaayen/BenVerwaayen.htm Ben Verwaayen CEO] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080515200303/http://www.btplc.com/Thegroup/Theboard/BenVerwaayen/BenVerwaayen.htm |date=May 15, 2008 }} on 15 may 2008</ref> During his time there, he was also a board member for Dutch television production company [[Endemol]], and chaired the [[Confederation of British Industry]] committee on [[climate change]].<ref name="Telg3"/>  There was controversy over his management style at BT, with accusations that [[BT Global Services]] had to write down substantial losses and that Verwaayen was to blame.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/business/analysis-and-features/bts-livingston-is-drinking-at-the--lastchance-saloon-1686033.html|title=BT's Livingston is 'drinking at the last-chance saloon'|last=Evans|first=Simon|date=17 May 2009|website=The Independent|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090519071101/https://www.independent.co.uk/news/business/analysis-and-features/bts-livingston-is-drinking-at-the--lastchance-saloon-1686033.html|archive-date=19 May 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/newsbysector/mediatechnologyandtelecoms/telecoms/5325650/Ex-BT-boss-Ben-Verwaayen-in-firing-line-over-losses.html | work=The Daily Telegraph | location=London | title=Ex-BT boss Ben Verwaayen in firing line over losses | first=Nic | last=Fildes | date=2009-05-15 | access-date=2010-04-26}}</ref>

After standing down from BT on 1 June 2008 when [[Ian Livingston]] took over,<ref>Website Times Online [http://business.timesonline.co.uk/tol/business/industry_sectors/telecoms/article3708801.ece Ian Livingston to....] posted on 9 April 2008 visited: 15 May 2008</ref> there was speculation that he would pursue a career in Dutch politics.<ref name="Telg1"/> On 2 September 2008 the [[Alcatel-Lucent]] board of directors appointed Verwaayen as the company's chief executive officer, succeeding [[Patricia Russo]]. In March 2009, he forwent a €520,000 cash bonus after a pay freeze had been imposed on staff and a loss-making 2008.<ref>{{Cite news | url = http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/057ca204-171d-11de-9a72-0000779fd2ac.html | title = SocGen cancels executives' stock options | newspaper = Financial Times | last = Hollinger | first = Peggy | location = Paris | date = 2009-03-22 | access-date = 2009-03-22 }}</ref>  He was less restrained in later years; his salary increased by 30% between 2010 and 2012, even as losses continued at the company.<ref>{{citation | url = http://www.telecompaper.com/news/alcatel-lucent-unions-call-for-executive-salary-cuts | title = Alcatel-Lucent unions call for executive salary cuts}}</ref><ref>{{citation | url = http://www.alcatel-lucent.com/wps/DocumentStreamerServlet?LMSG_CABINET=Docs_and_Resource_Ctr&LMSG_CONTENT_FILE=Financial_Info/Income_Statements/IR-Alcatel-Lucent-20-F2011.PDF | title = Alcatel-Lucent 2011 Annual Report on Form 20-F | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20120610234118/http://www.alcatel-lucent.com/wps/DocumentStreamerServlet?LMSG_CABINET=Docs_and_Resource_Ctr&LMSG_CONTENT_FILE=Financial_Info%2FIncome_Statements%2FIR-Alcatel-Lucent-20-F2011.PDF | archivedate = 2012-06-10 }}</ref>

On 22 February 2013 the Alcatel-Lucent board of directors appointed [[Michel Combes]] as the company's CEO, succeeding Verwaayen, effective April 1.<ref>[https://www.reuters.com/article/2013/02/22/alcatel-ceo-idUSWEA00AMB20130222 Alcatel-Lucent names Michel Combes as CEO] Reuters, February 22nd 2013</ref> He joined [[Akamai Technologies]] as a director in November 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.akamai.com/html/about/board_directors.html|title=Board of Directors|work=akamai.com}}</ref>

In 2016, Verwaayen founded Keen Venture Partners, a [[venture capital]] firm providing early growth capital for technology companies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2016/11/04/keen-venture-partners/|title=Keen Venture Partners is a new European VC firm founded by ex-BT CEO – TechCrunch|website=techcrunch.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-18}}</ref>  Since April 2020, he has been Chairman of [[Renewi plc]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.renewi.com/en/about-renewi/our-role/waste-journal-articles/renewi-appoints-ben-verwaayen-as-non-executive-chairman|publisher=Renewi|date=9 March 2020|title=Renewi appoints Ben Verwaayen as Non-Executive Chairman}}</ref>

===Politics===
During his military service, Verwaayen founded the ''Algemene Vereniging Nederlandse Militairen'' (General Association of Dutch soldiers), a [[trade union|union]] that existed until 1996.

Verwaayen has been a longtime member of the [[People's Party for Freedom and Democracy]]. He sat in its executive for ten years, and also contributed to its election programme for the [[2006 Dutch general election|2006 Dutch elections]]. At various times, he was linked with a [[Cabinet of the Netherlands|Cabinet]] position. The current Dutch Prime Minister, [[Mark Rutte]], has named him as one of his closest advisors.<ref>{{cite book|last=Maarleveld|first=Derick-H.|title=In gesprek met Mark Rutte|year=2011|publisher=Bert Bakker|location=Amsterdam|isbn=978-90-351-3649-6|pages=238}}</ref>

===Honours===
Verwaayen has received various honorary awards and has been made ''[[Order of Orange-Nassau|Officier in de Orde van Oranje Nassau]]'' (Netherlands), ''[[Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire|Honorary Knight of the Order of the British Empire'' KBE]] (United Kingdom) and ''[[Légion d'honneur|Chevalier de la Légion d'honneur]]'' (France).

==Personal life==
Married to Helena, the couple have two children.<ref name="Telg3">{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/money/main.jhtml?xml=/money/2008/09/07/ccprof107.xml&page=3|title=The Ben Verwaayen manifesto - Page3|publisher=The Telegraph|date=2008-09-07|access-date=2008-09-07 | location=London}}</ref> They currently reside in Paris, but had a family home in [[Haslemere]], [[Surrey]] where Verwaayen hosted a party in summer 2008, attended by 1,300 guests including former [[Vodafone]] CEO [[Arun Sarin]], UK government ministers [[Stephen Twigg]] and [[John Denham (politician)|John Denham]].<ref name="Telg2"/> Verwaayen, a tennis player himself, put on a celebrity tennis match between [[John Lloyd (tennis)|John Lloyd]] and [[Ilie Năstase]],<ref name="Telg2"/> and is also a fan of [[Arsenal F.C.]].<ref name="Telg3"/>
