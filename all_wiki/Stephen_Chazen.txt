'''Stephen Chazen''' (born 1946) is an [[United States|American]] businessman. He is the former president and chief executive officer (CEO) of [[Occidental Petroleum]] ({{NYSE|OXY}}).<ref>{{cite news|url = https://www.chron.com/business/energy/article/Stephen-Chazen-Magnolia-aims-to-be-10-company-13244164.php| title = Stephen Chazen: Magnolia aims to be $10B company in 5 years|date = September 20, 2018| work = Houston Chronicle}}</ref>

==Early life==
He was born in 1946.<ref name="nndb">[http://www.nndb.com/people/982/000169475/ nndb]</ref> He received a Bachelor of Science in Geology from [[Rutgers University]], a master's degree in Finance from the [[University of Houston]] and a PhD in Geology from [[Michigan State University]].<ref name="nndb"/><ref name="businessweek">[http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=293292&ticker=OXY Bloomberg BusinessWeek]</ref><ref name="forbes">[https://www.forbes.com/profile/stephen-chazen/ Forbes]</ref>

==Career==
He worked as a Managing Director in Corporate Finance and Mergers and Acquisitions at [[Merrill Lynch]].<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="forbes"/>

In 1994, he started his career at [[Occidental Petroleum]] (OXY), a publicly traded oil corporation then headquartered in [[Los Angeles, California]] and the fourth largest oil producer in the United States.<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="forbes"/><ref name="ft">Guy Chazan, [http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/63ffb8ca-a071-11e2-88b6-00144feabdc0.html#axzz2aDUvIz3n Occidental denies fight over Chazen heir], ''[[The Financial Times]]'', April 8, 2013</ref> He served as Executive Vice President-Corporate Development at Occidental Petroleum from 1994 to 1999, and as its Executive Vice President-Corporate Development from 1999 to 2004.<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="forbes"/> He served as its Senior Executive Vice President from 2004 to 2007, and as its Chief Financial Officer from 2004 to 2007.<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="forbes"/> He served as its President since December 2007 until 2016 and as its Chief Executive Officer since May 5, 2011 through April 29, 2016.<ref name="businessweek"/><ref name="forbes"/><ref>[http://www.oxy.com/AboutOxy/WhoWeAre/Pages/MessageFromCEO.aspx Message from the CEO] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130719183103/http://www.oxy.com/AboutOxy/WhoWeAre/Pages/MessageFromCEO.aspx |date=2013-07-19 }}</ref> He replaced [[Ray R. Irani]], who went on to serve as Chairman.<ref name="ft"/> He has denied conflict with Irani.<ref name="ft"/> 

According to a study by the [[Graziadio School of Business and Management]] at [[Pepperdine University]], under his leadship, OXY earned US$3.4 billion in economic profit in 2011, an increase of $1.6 billion from 2010, making him the value-generation business leader in [[Southern California]].<ref name="latimeshsu">Tiffany Hsu, [http://articles.latimes.com/2012/may/03/business/la-fi-mo-occidental-ceo-20120502 Occidental's Stephen Chazen tops list of value-generating CEOs], ''[[The Los Angeles Times]]'', May 03, 2012</ref>

He served on the Board of Directors of the [[American Petroleum Institute]].<ref name="forbes"/> Additionally, he has served on the Boards of the [[LyondellBasell|Lyondell Chemical Company]], [[Premcor]] and [[Washington Mutual]].<ref name="forbes"/>

As of August 2016, he is reported to be worth over US$128 million, due to his stock holdings in Occidental Petroleum and other companies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.insidermole.com/insider/chazen-stephen-i|title=STEPHEN I  CHAZEN Insider Trading Overview|website=www.insidermole.com|access-date=2016-08-09}}</ref>

He serves on the Board of Trustees of the [[Aquarium of the Pacific]] and [[Catalina Island Conservancy]].<ref name="forbes"/> He is a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]].<ref name="nndb"/>

In 2019, Chazen was asked to rejoin Occidental Petroleum as a board member.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Lombardo|first=Christopher M. Matthews and Cara|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/former-occidental-ceo-could-emerge-as-mediator-with-icahn-11564142044|title=Icahn Looks to Occidental’s Ex-CEO for Help in Fight With Company|date=2019-07-26|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2020-03-15|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref>
