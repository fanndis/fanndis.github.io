'''Florence Leontine Mary Welch'''<ref name="Timesbio">{{cite news |last=Patterson |first=Sylvia |url=http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/music/article6838997.ece |title=Behind the success of Florence and the Machine |work=[[The Sunday Times]] |date=20 September 2009 |accessdate=7 March 2010 |location=London}} [http://thegirlwithoneeye.skyrock.com/2917693461-Behind-the-Success-of-Florence-and-the-Machine-The-Sunday-Times-20.html mirror]</ref> (born 28 August 1986)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/news/florence-and-the-machine/46975 |title=Florence and the Machine open Reading Festival with secret birthday gig |work=NME |date=28 August 2009 |accessdate=18 June 2011}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{cite web |url=http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/celebrity/biographies/florence-welch |title=Florence Welch |work=[[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]] |accessdate=18 June 2011}}</ref> is an English singer, the lead vocalist and primary songwriter of the [[indie rock]] band [[Florence and the Machine]]. Their debut studio album, ''[[Lungs (album)|Lungs]]'', was released in 2009; on 17 January 2010, the album reached the top position on the [[UK Albums Chart]] after being on the chart for 28 consecutive weeks. ''Lungs'' won the [[Brit Awards|BRIT Award]] for Best British Album in 2010. The band's second album, ''[[Ceremonials]]'', released in October 2011, debuted at number one in the UK and number six on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. The band's third album, ''[[How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful]]'', was released in 2015 to positive reviews from [[Music journalism|music critic]]s and topped the UK and US albums chart.<ref>{{Citation|title = How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful|url = https://www.metacritic.com/music/how-big-how-blue-how-beautiful/florence-+-the-machine|accessdate = 2015-06-05}}</ref><ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jun/08/florence-the-machine-third-uk-no-1-album "Florence + the Machine scores third No 1 album in the UK"]. The Guardian. Retrieved 27 July 2015</ref><ref>{{cite news|first=Keith|last=Caulfield|title=Florence + the Machine Scores First No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6590835/florence-machine-first-no-1-album-billboard-200|work=Billboard|date=10 June 2015|accessdate=10 June 2015}}</ref> The band's fourth album, ''[[High as Hope]]'', was released in June 2018 and has sold over one million copies worldwide.

In 2018, Welch released a book titled ''Useless Magic'', a collection of lyrics and poems written by her, along with illustrations.

with Evelyn Welch|publisher=Association of Art Historians |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305051235/http://www.aah.org.uk/projects/oral-history/interviews/interview-with-evelyn-welch |archive-date = 5 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1982/08/08/style/james-welch-to-wed-evelyn-samuels.html |title=James Welch to wed Evelyn Samuels |work=[[The New York Welch is the niece of satirist [[Craig Brown (satirist)|Craig Brown]]<ref name="Ryan"/> via Brown's wife and Welch's aunt, Frances Welch,<ref name="West">{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/obituary-colin-welch-1285737.html|title=Obituary: Colin Welch|first=Richard|last=West|date=29 January 1997|work=The Independent}}</ref> and granddaughter of [[Colin Welch]] (James Colin Ross Welch), former deputy editor of ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' and former ''[[Daily Mail]]'' parliamentary sketchwriter, originally of [[Cambridgeshire]].<ref name="West"/> Welch's maternal uncle is actor and director [[John Stockwell (actor)|John Stockwell]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spin.com/2011/11/florence-welch-her-fear-treadmills-lady-gaga-and-ceremonials/ |title=Florence Welch on Her Fear of Treadmills, Lady Gaga, and 'Ceremonials' |first=John |last=Sellers |date=21 November 2011 |work=Spin |accessdate=6 October 2015}}</ref> 
	 	  	
During her youth, Welch was encouraged by her Scottish paternal grandmother, Cybil Welch (n√©e Russell),<ref name="West"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Instagram - Florence Welch |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BqSJPqHDkkr/ |website=Florence's Instagram |publisher=Instagram |accessdate=19 November 2018}}</ref>  to pursue her performing and singing talents.<ref name="Patterson">{{cite web|url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/culture/music/article184981.ece|title=Behind the success of Florence and the Machine|first=Sylvia|last=Patterson|date=20 September 2009|work=The Sunday Times}}{{subscription required}}</ref> Welch's deceased grandmothers inspired numerous songs on Florence and the Machine's debut album ''Lungs''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/music/news/a320963/florence-welch-new-songs-about-my-dead-grandma/|title=Florence Welch: "New songs about my dead grandma"|first=Lewis|last=Corner|work=Digital Spy|date=23 May 2011}}</ref> Welch also sang at family weddings and funerals.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=https://www.fuse.tv/2015/06/florence-welch-the-machine-facts|title=15 Things You Probably Didn't Know About Florence Welch|work=Fuse|access-date=2018-02-27}}</ref>

Welch's parents divorced when she was thirteen, and her mother eventually married their next-door neighbour, Professor [[Peter Openshaw (physician)|Peter Openshaw]]. Around this time, her maternal grandmother, who had [[bipolar disorder]], died by suicide.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Taysom |first1=Joe |title=Florence Welch Talks About Alcohol and Her Grandmother's Suicide |url=https://faroutmagazine.co.uk/florence-welch-talks-about-her-battle-with-alcohol-and-grandmothers-suicide/ |website=Far Out Magazine |publisher=Far Out Magazine |accessdate=19 November 2018}}</ref> In Florence and the Machine's 2018 single [[Hunger (Florence and the Machine song)|''Hunger'']], she opened up for the first time about a teenage eating disorder. She's also spoken of being a highly imaginative and fearful child. "I learned ways to manage that terror ‚Äì drink, drugs, controlling food..."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wiseman |first1=Eva |title=Florence Welch: I Wonder Sometimes, Did I Dream Too Big? |url=https://www.theguardian.com/global/2018/jun/24/florence-welch-interview-i-wonder-sometimes-did-i-dream-too-big |website=The Guardian |publisher=The Guardian |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref>
	
Welch was educated at [[Thomas's London Day Schools|Thomas's London Day School]] then went on to [[Alleyn's School]], [[South East (London sub region)|South East London]], where she did well academically.<ref name="Timesbio"/> However, Welch often got in trouble in school for impromptu singing and for singing too loudly in the school's choir.<ref name="Timesbio"/> Despite an early love of reading and literature, she was also diagnosed with mild [[dyslexia]] due to problems with spelling, alongside dyspraxia, a [[developmental coordination disorder]] that doesn't affect her reading ability, but caused issues with organization.<ref>{{cite web |title=Florence Welch: Quirky Mind Behind Machine |url=http://www.dyspraxiausa.org/in-the-news/florence-welch-quirky-mind-behind-machine/ |website=Dyspraxia USA |publisher=Dyspraxia USA |accessdate=28 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Simalis |first1=Linda |title=Why Florence is a Star, not a Machine |url=https://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/why-florence-is-a-star-not-a-machine/news-story/0c77e26b62d3f87f6741d5b38f8ea3f4 |website=Daily Telegraph |publisher=Daily Telegraph |accessdate=28 June 2018}}</ref> Music and books gave her reprieve from what she felt made her different from others. "I used reading as a form of escape. I was shy and sensitive, and so reading gave me a safe space."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Petrarca |first1=Emilia |title=Welch, Book Club Enthusiast, Gets Candid About the Literature That Changed Her |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/florence-welch-book-club-enthusiast-gets-candid-about-the-literature-that-changed-her-life |website=W Magazine |publisher=W Magazine |accessdate=28 June 2018}}</ref> Upon leaving secondary school and "just bumming around Camberwell where I lived, working at a bar and thought that I should start doing something with life", Welch studied at [[Camberwell College of Arts]] before dropping out to focus on her music.<ref name="Timesbio" /> Initially, she had intended to take a year out from her studies to "see where the music would go and then it started going somewhere so [she] never went back".<ref name="May">{{cite web|url=http://heartymagazine.com/features/florence-and-the-machine-interview-photos-with-hearty-magazine|title=Florence and the Machine|first=Hana|last=May|date=14 December 2009|work=Hearty Magazine}}</ref>

==Career==
===2006‚Äì2010: Florence and the Machine and ''Lungs''===
[[File:FATM ABC.jpg|thumb|size=500px|right|Florence and the Machine performing at the [[O2 ABC Glasgow]] during their [[Lungs Tour]]]]
According to Welch, the band name "Florence + the Machine" had "started off as a private joke that got out of hand. I made music with my friend, who we called Isabella Machine, to which I was Florence Robot. When I was about an hour away from my first gig, I still didn't have a name, so I thought 'Okay, I'll be Florence Robot/Isa Machine', before realising that name was so long it'd drive me mad".<ref name="Timesbio"/><ref name="naming">{{cite web |last=Bell |first=Sean |url=http://www.heraldscotland.com:80/life-style/real-lives/a-piece-of-my-mind-florence-welch-of-florence-and-the-machine-1.820514 |title=A piece of my mind: Florence Welch of Florence and the Machine |work=[[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]] |publisher=Herald & Times Group |date=26 July 2009 |accessdate=6 March 2010}}</ref> In 2006, Welch's performances with [[Isabella Summers]] in small London venues under the joint name Florence Robot/Isa Machine<ref>{{Cite web|title=Florence & The Machine|url=https://www.discogs.com/de/artist/994835-Florence-And-The-Machine?subtype=Miscellaneous&anv=Florence+%26+The+Machine&filter_anv=1&type=Unofficial|website=discogs|access-date=2020-05-07}}</ref> began to attract notice.{{Citation needed|date=August 2010}}
In 2007, Welch recorded with a band named Ashok, who released an album titled ''Plans'' on the Filthy Lucre/About Records label. This album included the earliest version of her later hit "[[Kiss with a Fist]]", which at this point was titled "Happy Slap".<ref>{{cite web |last=Parkin |first=April |url=https://www.gigwise.com/news/30153/ |title=Ashok ‚Äì 'Plans' (Filthy Lucre) |publisher=[[Gigwise]]. ===2015‚Äì17: ''How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful''===
In February 2015, Florence and the Machine announced their third album, ''[[How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful]]'', which was released on 1 June 2015. The album reached #1 in many markets including the US, the UK, Australia, and Canada. The record spawned two top 40 UK hits, and earned three Grammy nominations.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/florence-machine|title=Florence & The Machine|date=2018-05-22|work=GRAMMY.com|access-date=2018-10-21|language=en}}</ref>

During June 2015, [[Dave Grohl]] of the [[Foo Fighters]] broke his leg on stage prior to his band's upcoming [[Glastonbury Festival]] headline performance, causing Florence and the Machine to be the headline band. They headlined the festival for the first time on 26 June 2015.<ref name="BBC News 26 June 2015">{{cite web | title= Glastonbury: Florence and the Machine step up with headline set |url=  https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-33295606| last= Youngs| first= Ian| date= 26 June 2015 | website= [[BBC News Online]] | accessdate= 26 June 2015}}</ref>

In September 2016, during an interview with [[Heat Radio]], American singer [[Lady Gaga]] revealed that she and Florence had recorded a song together. The track, titled "Hey Girl", was later featured on Gaga's fifth album ''[[Joanne (album)|Joanne]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y11P4QTmDY0|title=Lady Gaga - "I'm a very simple person, though I might appear complicated"|last=heatworld|date=9 September 2016|access-date=21 May 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> Footage of their studio session was featured in Gaga's Netflix documentary ''Gaga: Five Foot Two''.

In March 2017, Welch appeared in ''[[Song to Song]]'' directed by [[Terrence Malick]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/71725-lykke-patti-iggy-and-more-every-musician-in-terrence-malicks-song-to-song/|title=Lykke, Patti, Iggy, and More: Every Musician in Terrence Malick's Song to Song|website=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]|first=Amy|last=Phillips|date=March 7, 2017|accessdate=March 8, 2017}}</ref>

In May 2017, Welch contributed a song titled "[[To Be Human]]" to the ''[[Wonder Woman (2017 film)|Wonder Woman]]'' soundtrack. Co-written with [[Rick Nowels]], the song is performed on the film's soundtrack by [[Sia (musician)|Sia]] and [[Labrinth]].

===2018‚Äìpresent: ''High as Hope''===
[[File:Florence Welch 2018 - Florence and the Machine (High as Hope Tour).jpg|thumb|right|Welch performing at [[KeyArena]] in [[Seattle]] during the [[High as Hope Tour]], 2018]]
On April 12, 2018, Florence and the Machine released a song titled "A Sky Full of Song" and an accompanying music video on YouTube, directed by AG Rojas. The song was released for Record Store Day on April 21, which supports brick and mortar record stores; a limited edition 7" vinyl was also released.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/flo_tweet/status/984534502801321984|title=Twitter|website=mobile.twitter.com|access-date=2018-04-13}}</ref> Also in 2018 "[[Hunger (Florence and the Machine song)|Hunger]]" was released. The book showcases her lyrics and poetry, alongside corresponding artwork from the time of her first album ''Lungs'' to her 2018 release ''High as Hope''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/06/14/arts/music/florence-and-the-machine-high-as-hope.html|title='I Never Thought I Would Talk About It.' ===Influences===
During interviews, Welch has cited singers [[Grace Slick]], [[Alanis Morissette]] and Stevie Nicks as influences and "heroes."<ref name="ThisIsLondon">{{cite web |url=http://www.thisislondon.co.uk/lifestyle/florence-welch--my-london-6747994.html |title=Florence Welch ‚Äì My London |work=[[London Evening Standard]] |date=30 July 2009|accessdate=17 March 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite interview|location=Toronto|interviewer=Matt Schichter|last=Welch|first=Florence|year=2014|title=Florence + the Machine Interview|via=YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_3qeCZH4xUA|work=[[MSN]] Music}}</ref> She told ''[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]'' in 2010, ‚ÄúI‚Äôm pretty obsessed with [Stevie] Nicks, from her style to her voice. I like watching her on [[YouTube]] and her old performances, the way she moves and everything.‚Äù<ref>{{cite web |last1=Reed |first1=Ryan |title=Hear Florence + the Machine Cover Silver Springs |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/hear-florence-and-the-machine-cover-fleetwood-macs-silver-springs-698342/ |website=Rolling Stone |accessdate=19 November 2018}}</ref>

She has also listed in her early influences the likes of [[John Cale]], [[Otis Redding]], Siouxsie, [[David Byrne]] and [[Lou Reed]].<ref>{{cite web |first=Joshua |last=Glass |url=http://www.documentjournal.com/article/john-cale-florence-welch |url-status=unfit |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161205195236/http://www.documentjournal.com/article/john-cale-florence-welch |title=John Cale Muses on Poetry, Sobriety, and Hood By Air with Florence Welch|work=documentjournal.com|date= 30 November 2016|accessdate=30 November 2016|archive-date=5 December 2016}}</ref> In a review of ''Ceremonials'', Jody Rosen of ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' described Florence and the Machine's style as "dark, robust and romantic", deeming the ballad "Only If for a Night" as a mix of "classic soul and midnight-on-the-moors English art rock".<ref name="rollingstone"/> Welch stated that her lyrics related to [[Renaissance]] artists : "We're dealing with all of the same things they did: love and death, time and pain, heaven and hell".<ref name="LAT">{{cite web |url=http://www.latimesmagazine.com/2011/01/visionary-vixen.html |title=Visionary Vixen|work=[[Los Angeles Times Magazine]] |publisher=Los Angeles Times Communications |date=3 January 2011 |accessdate=11 March 2013 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110911182616/http://www.latimesmagazine.com/2011/01/visionary-vixen.html |archive-date=2011-09-11}}</ref>  Welch has used religious imagery in her music and performances, though she has stated, "I'm not a religious person. Sex, violence, love, death, are the topics that I'm constantly wrestling with, it's all connected back to religion."<ref name="NME">{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/news/florence-and-the-machine/58020#iXYCpui7lsJLU3OP.99 |title=Florence And The Machine: "I love demons and exorcism"|work=[[NME]] |date=4 July 2011 |accessdate=18 November 2013}}</ref>

Nick Welch, her father, contributed a "rock and roll element to the family mix"; in his twenties, he lived in a [[West End of London|West End]] squat and attended the Squatters' Ball organised by [[Heathcote Williams]] where [[The 101ers]] played regularly.<ref name="q_odell">{{cite journal |last=Odell |first=Mike |date=May 2010 |title=Florence Attacks! |journal=[[Q magazine|Q]] |publisher=[[Bauer Media Group]] |issue=286 |pages=46‚Äì52}}</ref> A self-confessed "frustrated performer", if Nick, as he put it, "nudged Flo in any way, it's only been to listen to the [[Ramones]] rather than [[Green Day]]."<ref name="q_odell"/> Evelyn, Welch's mother, had an equally strong, yet completely different influence on her daughter. A visit to one of her mother's renaissance lectures left teenage Florence deeply impressed. She explained, "I aspire to something like that, but with music. I hope that my music has some of the big themes‚Äîsex, death, love, violence‚Äîthat will still be part of the human story in 200 years' time."<ref name="q_odell"/>

===Image===
Welch is known for her distinctive clothing style, often performing concerts wearing light [[Gucci]] dresses, [[barefoot]] and without [[jewellery]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.fr/wedding/inspiration/articles/florence-welch-stage-wedding-inspiration-bridal-fashion-attire-alessandro-michele-gucci-lingerie-dress-non-traditional-florence-andthe-machine/67541|title=Florence Welch's "Ceremonial" Gucci stage dress|website=Vogue Paris|language=fr|access-date=2019-02-13}}</ref> ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' described her style as [[Bohemianism|Bohemian]] and called her "the queen of Bohemian style."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/florence-welch-bohemian-tour-style|title=Florence Welch Proves She's Still the Queen of Bohemian Style|website=Vogue|language=en|access-date=2019-02-13}}</ref>

When discussing her fashion style, Welch said that, "For the stage, it's [[The Lady of Shalott]] meets [[Ophelia]]...mixed with scary [[Goth subculture|gothic]] bat lady. But in real life I'm kind of prim."<ref>{{cite web|last=Marcus |first=Laura |url=http://www.venuszine.com/articles/fashion/looks/7531/Style_Idol_Florence_Welch |title=Style Idol: Florence Welch |work=[[Venus Zine]] |date=9 August 2010 |accessdate=23 September 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120401014322/http://www.venuszine.com/articles/fashion/looks/7531/Style_Idol_Florence_Welch |archive-date=1 April 2012 }}</ref> Welch often mixes artistic influences both in her fashion style and music, with a strong nod towards the style of the [[Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailyartmagazine.com/pre-raphaelite-florence-welch/|title=Florence Welch - Perfect Model for the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood|website=DailyArt Magazine}}</ref> 2011 saw [[Gucci]] dressing her for her summer tour and a performance at the [[Chanel]] runway show at [[Paris Fashion Week]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web |url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/newsweek/2011/10/23/florence-welch-on-her-breakup-and-new-album-ceremonials.html|title=Florence's Dark Side of Fame Newsweek 23 October 2011|work=[[The Daily Beast]]|publisher=[[The Newsweek Daily Beast Company]]|first=Jesse|last=Ellison|date=23 October 2011|accessdate=18 March 2012}}</ref> Welch describes 1970s American [[drag queen]] troupe [[The Cockettes]] and French [[chanson]] singer [[Fran√ßoise Hardy]] as fashion mentors.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-hook/466942/florence-welchs-fashion-its-not-inner-turmoil-its-total-escapism |title=Florence Welch's Fashion: 'It's Not Inner Turmoil. ==Personal life==
Welch considers herself an introvert, and is passionate about reading and literature. She has held many events with her fan-run book club, Between Two Books. "It‚Äôs a huge generalization to say that all readers are introverts; I'm sure there's a lot of extroverted bookworms out there, but, for me, it's nice to know people of similar inclinations can actually come together in a social way and talk about something that is inherently solitary."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Petrarca |first1=Emilia |title=Florence Welch, Book Club Enthusiast, Gets Candid About Literature |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/florence-welch-book-club-enthusiast-gets-candid-about-the-literature-that-changed-her-life |website=W Magazine |publisher=W Magazine |access-date=13 July 2018}}</ref>

Although many of her songs contain religious themes and elements, Welch has said she does not follow any particular religion. "I went to Catholic school, and the first songs I remember liking were hymns. I find it's nice to mix the mundane and the magical, the irrelevant with the huge themes. Sex, love, death, marriage, guilt‚Äîmix that with seeing a huge sky or going for a walk or turning the page of a book. Living is dealing with the everyday and the notion that you're going to die."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gunderson |first1=Edna |title=Florence + High Profile |url=https://www.pressreader.com/usa/usa-today-us-edition/20110131/284417030510285 |newspaper=USA Today |access-date=13 July 2018 |date=31 July 2011}}</ref>

Welch has been open about her struggles with anxiety and depression, as well as with alcohol. Many of her songs reflect these issues.<ref>{{cite web |title=Florence Welch reveals struggle with alcoholism, depression |url=https://www.1057thepoint.com/music-news/florence-welch-reveals-struggle-with-alcoholism-depression/ |website=105.7 The Point |publisher=105.7 The Point |access-date=1 December 2018}}</ref>

In 2015, Welch broke her foot after leaping off the stage at the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella Festival]].<ref name="The Independent 15 April 2015">{{cite news |last= Battersby   |first= Matilda |title= Florence Welch breaks foot leaping offstage at Coachella|url= https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/coouchella-florence-welch-breaks-foot-leaping-offstage-at-coachella-10177186.html| date=15 April 2015|newspaper= [[The Independent]] |location=London| access-date=27 July 2015 }}</ref> She revealed that she used to drink alcohol before every performance, telling ''Billboard'': "I'm quite shy, really‚Äîthat's probably why I used to drink a lot. But I don't anymore. When I finally took time off to make this new record, I had time to strengthen. And when I was coming back into the fray, I really didn't want to lose that. I thought I could go dive-bomb back into it, but look what happened. I dived into it and literally broke myself."<ref name=":3"/>

In 2016, Welch voiced her support for Remain during the [[2016 United Kingdom European Union membership referendum|EU referendum]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/eu-referendum-brexit-remain-who-do-celebrities-support-david-beckham-jk-rowling-a7094751.html|title=The celebrities that support Brexit (and the ones backing Remain)|work=The Independent|access-date=2018-11-27|language=en-GB}}</ref> on that issue. Welch is also a vocal advocate for [[LGBT]] rights, and regularly  waves the [[Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)|rainbow flag]] at her concerts, particularly during her song "[[Spectrum (Say My Name)]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stern |first1=Bradley |title=Florence Welch Holds Rainbow Flag, Leads 'Love Is Love' Chant in Concert |url=http://popcrush.com/florence-the-machine-rainbow-flag-love-chant-how-beautiful-tour-brooklyn/ |website=Pop crush |access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Rini |first1=Jen |title=Firefly Recap: A Weekend of Love, Awareness, and Music |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2016/06/20/firefly-music-festival-recap/86131342/ |website=USA Today |publisher=USA Today |access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> In 2019, Welch expressed her support for women's rights during concerts in Las Vegas, Nevada, Chicago, Illinois, Raleigh and Columbia, Maryland. She encouraged her audience to donate to the [[ACLU]] instead of buying concert merchandise.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BxnkzeAlfWC/|title=@florencethemachinebr on Instagram: "Florence + The Machine ‚Äì Las Vegas ‚Äì 05.17.19 Legend written by @florencethemachinebr üëá Eu tenho que dizer que neste momento meu cora√ß√£o‚Ä¶"|website=Instagram|access-date=21 May 2019}}</ref> In 2018, she tweeted her support for the removal of the [[Eighth Amendment of the Constitution of Ireland]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Irish Abortion Referendum: Musicians Encourage Votes, Tweets |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8457893/irish-abortion-referendum-musicians-encourage-votes-tweets |website=Billboard |access-date=18 May 2019}}</ref> The removal passed and legalised abortion access within the country.
