{{short description|Most-subscribed YouTube channels}}
On the video platform [[YouTube]], a subscriber to a channel is a user who, by selecting that channel's subscribe button, has chosen to receive content released by the channel. Each user's subscription feed consists of videos recently published by channels to which the account is subscribed.<ref>{{cite web |authorlink1=24 June 2019 |title=The Quickstart Guide to YouTube – Lesson 2: YouTube Basics |url=https://creatoracademy.youtube.com/page/lesson/subscriber-advantage?cid=bootcamp-foundations&hl=en#strategies-zippy-link-2 |website=YouTube Creator Academy |publisher=YouTube |accessdate=25 June 2019 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.today/20190624030336/https://creatoracademy.youtube.com/page/lesson/subscriber-advantage#strategies-zippy-link-2 |archivedate=24 June 2019 }}</ref> The ability to subscribe to users was introduced in October 2005,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://youtube-global.blogspot.com/2005/10/its-been-awhile-since-our-last-update.html|title=Official YouTube Blog – Tuesday, October 25, 2005|publisher=[[Google]]|website=Official YouTube Blog|date=25 October 2005|accessdate=5 November 2013 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091230154224/http://youtube-global.blogspot.com/2005/10/its-been-awhile-since-our-last-update.html |archivedate=30 December 2009}}</ref> and the website began publishing a list of its "most subscribed Members" in April 2006.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lots 'o New Stuff |url=https://youtube.googleblog.com/2006/04/lots-new-stuff.html |website=Official YouTube Blog |publisher=Google |accessdate=19 January 2019 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091230141502/http://youtube-global.blogspot.com/2006/04/lots-new-stuff.html |archivedate=30 December 2009 }}</ref>

An early archive of the list dates to May 2006, at which time [[Smosh]], with fewer than three thousand subscribers, occupied the number one position.<ref name="YouTube2005"/> Since then, at least ten other YouTube channels have possessed the platform's largest subscriber count; these include [[Judson Laipply]], [[Brooke Brodack|Brookers]], [[geriatric1927]], [[lonelygirl15]], [[nigahiga]], [[Fred Figglehorn|Fred]], [[Ray William Johnson|Ray]] (formerly RayWilliamJohnson), [[PewDiePie]], and [[YouTube (channel)|YouTube]] (formerly YouTube Spotlight). The most-subscribed channel as of November 2020 is [[T-Series (company)|T-Series]], an Indian music video publisher operated by the entertainment company of the same name. With a subscriber count of 161 million, the channel has held this distinction since April 2019.

===PewDiePie vs T-Series===
{{Main|PewDiePie vs T-Series}}
In mid-2018, the subscriber count of the Indian music video channel [[T-Series (company)|T-Series]] rapidly approached that of Swedish web comedian and Let's Player [[PewDiePie]], who at the time was the most-subscribed user on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Huddleston Jr. |first1=Tom |title=This Bollywood YouTube Channel Is on the Verge of Bumping 'PewDiePie' from His Top Spot |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/08/31/t-series-could-soon-pass-pewdiepie-as-youtubes-biggest-channel.html |website=[[CNBC]] |publisher=[[NBC Universal]] |accessdate=8 March 2019 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180831203455/https://www.cnbc.com/2018/08/31/t-series-could-soon-pass-pewdiepie-as-youtubes-biggest-channel.html |archivedate=31 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Rakin |first1=Ethan |title=PewDiePie Could Lose His King of YouTube Crown to a Channel Called T-Series |url=https://www.businessinsider.sg/pewdiepie-could-lose-his-king-of-youtube-crown-to-a-channel-called-t-series/ |website=[[Business Insider Singapore]] |accessdate=8 March 2019 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190308185426/https://www.businessinsider.sg/pewdiepie-could-lose-his-king-of-youtube-crown-to-a-channel-called-t-series/ |archivedate=8 March 2019}}</ref> As a result, fans of PewDiePie and T-Series, other YouTubers and celebrities [[PewDiePie vs T-Series|showed their support for both channels]]. During the competition, both channels gained a large amount of subscribers at a rapid rate. The two channels surpassed each other in subscriber count on a number of occasions in February, March, and April 2019.<ref name="Trenholm"/><ref name="Hamilton"/><ref name="Spangler"/><ref name="BBCNews">{{cite web |title=PewDiePie Concedes Defeat to YouTube Rival T-Series |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-47042761 |website=[[BBC]] |accessdate=2 April 2019 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190401111044/https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-47042761 |archivedate=1 April 2019}}</ref>
