The '''Sandra Day O'Connor Institute''' is a non-profit organization based in [[Phoenix, Arizona]], USA, whose mission is to "continue Justice [[Sandra Day O’Connor]]’s legacy and lifetime work to advance civics education, civic engagement and civil discourse".<ref name="Mission">{{cite web | url=http://oconnorinstitute.org/about/missionopportunity/ | title= Mission Statement | publisher=Sandra Day O'Connor Institute | accessdate=December 15, 2018 }}</ref> The institute was established with the retired [[United States Supreme Court|U.S. Supreme Court Justice]] [[Sandra Day O'Connor]] from Arizona in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://asunow.asu.edu/20151020-sandra-day-oconnor-institute-announces-future-home-arizona-center-law-and-society-0|title=Sandra Day O’Connor Institute announces future home at Arizona Center for Law and Society|author=|date=20 October 2015|website=ASU Now: Access, Excellence, Impact|accessdate=2 January 2019}}</ref> Leaders and luminaries who have spoken at the O'Connor Institute include General [[Colin Powell]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://northcentralnews.net/2013/business/powell-to-discuss-leadership-at-event/|title=Powell to discuss leadership at event|author=|date=|website=northcentralnews.net|accessdate=2 January 2019}}</ref> President [[George W. Bush]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://paradisevalleyindependent.com/events/traveling-exhibit-of-artwork-by-president-george-w-bush-coming-to-arizona/|title=Traveling exhibit of artwork by President George W. Bush coming to Arizona|date=August 15, 2018}}</ref> and the former [[Soviet Union]] President [[Mikhail Gorbachev]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bizjournals.com/phoenix/blog/business/2012/04/michael-bidwill-flies-mikhail.html |title=Michael Bidwell Flies|date=2012 |website= bizjournals.com|accessdate=2019-07-21}}</ref>

==History==
The institute, a non-profit, nonpartisan organization, was originally founded as O'Connor House to oversee saving and preserving O'Connor's historic 1958 [[adobe]] family home, which was slated for demolition. The house in [[Paradise Valley, Arizona]], was painstakingly dismantled brick by brick and successfully reassembled in a new location in [[Tempe, Arizona|Tempe]]'s [[Papago Park]] near the [[Arizona Historical Society]] in 2009. The O'Connor Institute conserves the house.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.azcentral.com/story/news/local/tempe/2017/04/28/sandra-day-oconnors-former-house-center-civility-tempe-gets-flung-mud-adobe/100905274/|title=Sandra Day O'Connor's former house, a center of civility in Tempe, gets flung with mud|author=|date=|website=azcentral|accessdate=2 January 2019}}<br>- {{Cite web|url=http://www.azcentral.com/style/hfe/decor/articles/2010/03/24/20100324sandra-day-oconnor-historic-house-rebuilt-papago.html|title=Historic O'Connor house rebuilt in Papago Park|author=Kara G. Morrison |date=March 24, 2010 |work=The Arizona Republic }}</ref> In 2015, Justice O'Connor and the organization's Board of Directors authorized the renaming from O'Connor House to the Sandra Day O'Connor Institute to better represent the broader spectrum of programs and emphasis on civil discourse, civic engagement and civics education.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://azbigmedia.com/business/law/oconnor-house-names/|title=O’Connor House becomes Sandra Day O’Connor Institute|date=March 30, 2015}}</ref> In 2019, it was listed on the [[National Register of Historic Places]] as the [[Sandra Day O'Connor House]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.azcentral.com/story/news/local/tempe/2019/07/19/sandra-day-oconnors-house-added-in-tempe-national-register-historic-places/1779271001/|title=Sandra Day O'Connor's house in Tempe added to the National Register of Historic Places|last=O'Sullivan|first=Serena|website=azcentral|language=en|access-date=2019-11-05}}</ref>

===Camp O'Connor===
Conceived in 2014 by O'Connor, the goal of Camp O'Connor is to "educate, inspire and encourage the next generation of citizens…to develop a deeper understanding of our democracy".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.raisingarizonakids.com/2019/03/camp-oconnor-offers-free-weeklong-civics-camp-middle-school-students/|title=Camp O'Connor offers free civics camp to middle-school students June 5–9}}</ref> The O'Connor Institute delivers this five-day "democracy boot camp" for middle school students each summer in Phoenix.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://asunow.asu.edu/20180921-arizona-impact-asu-law-host-sandra-day-o%E2%80%99connor-day-downtown-phoenix-campus|title=ASU Law to host 'Sandra Day O’Connor Day' at Downtown Phoenix campus|author=|date=21 September 2018|website=ASU Now: Access, Excellence, Impact|accessdate=2 January 2019}}</ref> Due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19]] pandemic, Camp O'Connor 2020 was postponed to 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=admin|date=2020-05-22|title=Sandra Day O'Connor Institute launches online O'Connor U|url=https://www.raisingarizonakids.com/2020/05/sandra-day-oconnor-institute-launches-online-oconnor-u/|access-date=2020-06-16|website=Raising Arizona Kids Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Camp O'Connor Civics Challenge ===
In 2020, the O'Connor Institute launched the Camp O'Connor Civics Challenge, a national competition for 6th - 9th grade students. For the Challenge, "Students will...choose one civics topic from a list of options, receive instructions, and then produce a creative video on that topic, three minutes or less in length. Winners will be awarded Apple products." The Civics Challenge launched together with the Institute's online education initiative known as '''O'Connor U'''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=O’Connor U launched by Sandra Day O'Connor Institute|url=https://www.yourvalley.net/stories/sandra-day-oconnor-institute-launches-oconnor-u,161280|access-date=2020-06-16|website=Your Valley|language=en}}</ref>

=== Digital Library and Resource Center ===
The Sandra Day O’Connor Digital Library "showcases Justice O’Connor’s historic accomplishments across her decades of work in public service"<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sandra Day O'Connor Institute launches extensive digital library to honor Justice's 90th birthday|url=https://www.12news.com/article/news/local/arizona/sandra-day-oconnor-turns-90/75-65bb09be-4c4c-4d90-b1bb-6e5b2448b47e|access-date=2020-06-16|website=12news.com|language=en-US}}</ref> and "catalogs the life and work of Justice Sandra Day O’Connor, the first woman to serve on the United States Supreme Court."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Research Library and Archives by The Sandra Day O'Connor Institute|url=https://oconnorinstitute.org/civic-programs/oconnor-history/sandra-day-oconnor-policy-archives-research-library/|access-date=2020-06-16|website=Sandra Day O'Connor Institute}}</ref> According to the Institute, the launch of the Digital Library has meant that, "For the first time, Justice O’Connor’s body of work across her decades in public service is available in an easily accessible, searchable format." The Library is the "world's most comprehensive online collection of Justice Sandra Day O'Connor's life and work.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Institute|first=Sandra Day O’Connor|title=Sandra Day O'Connor Digital Library Launched|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/sandra-day-oconnor-digital-library-launched-301016983.html|access-date=2020-06-16|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en}}</ref>

===Issues and Answers Forums===
The institute hosts debates about various policies featuring speakers from all sides of each issue. Past topics have included [[independent expenditure]]s, anonymous campaign finance contributions, so-called "[[dark money]]",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://paradisevalleyindependent.com/events/oconnor-institute-host-panel-discussion-dark-money/|title=O’Connor Institute to host panel discussion on ‘Dark Money’|date=March 1, 2018}}</ref> legalization of recreational marijuana,<ref>https://asuevents.asu.edu/content/issues-answers-forum-regulation-and-taxation-marijuana,</ref> and water and drought in the American Southwest.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theshow.kjzz.org/content/734389/forum-planned-address-arizonas-water-issues|title=Forum Planned To Address Arizona's Water Issues|date=December 6, 2018|website=KJZZ's The Show}}</ref>

===Sandra Day O'Connor Scholarship===
Each year, the institute provides a competitive scholarship for a promising law student at the [[Sandra Day O'Connor College of Law]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://paradisevalleyindependent.com/events/supreme-evening-of-jazz-benefits-oconnor-scholarship/|title=Supreme Evening of Jazz benefits O'Connor scholarship|date=July 24, 2017}}<br>- {{Cite web|url=http://oconnorinstitute.org/civic-programs/sandra-day-oconnor-law-scholarship/|title=Sandra Day O'Connor Law Scholarship - The O'Connor Institute|website=Sandra Day O'Connor Institute}}</ref>

=== Emerging Leaders Network ===
The Emerging Leaders Network (ELN) hosts guest speakers, organizes social events, and produces informational materials on civic engagement for young adults ages 18-40.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://chamberbusinessnews.com/2019/01/30/program-set-to-engage-and-encourage-younger-voters/|title=Program set to engage and encourage younger voters|last=Ciaramella|first=Sierra|date=2019-01-30|website=Chamber Business News|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-05}}</ref> Past speakers at ELN events have included [[Arizona Attorney General]] [[Mark Brnovich]] and [[Maricopa County Sheriff's Office|Maricopa County Sheriff]] [[Paul Penzone]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oconnoreln.org/events/|title=Events|website=O'Connor Emerging Leaders Network|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-05}}</ref>

=== "Civics for Life" Podcast ===
The O'Connor Institute's monthly podcast features interviews with guests in public service or academia whose work intersects with the Institute's focus on civil discourse, civics education, and civic engagement. Guests have included [[Governor of Vermont]] [[Phil Scott (politician)|Phil Scott]], former [[List of ambassadors of the United States to Afghanistan|U.S. Ambassador to Afghanistan]] [[Karl Eikenberry]], and former [[President of Latvia]] [[Vaira Vīķe-Freiberga|Vaira Vike-Freiberga]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/show/60h3u9s3jhIPM77oLSyYmj|title=ELN Civics for Life|website=Spotify|language=en|access-date=2019-11-05}}</ref>
