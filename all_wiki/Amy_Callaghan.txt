'''Amy Callaghan''' (born 21 May 1992)<ref>https://www.twitter.com/AmyCallaghanSNP/status/1263508154152890368</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thenational.scot/news/18166711.watch-amy-callaghans-brilliant-maiden-speech-snp-mp/|title=WATCH: Amy Callaghan's 'brilliant' maiden speech as SNP MP|website=The National|access-date=2 April 2020|quote=The 27-year-old, who unseated then Lib Dem leader Jo Swinson in the General Election in December, spoke about Scottish independence and protecting the NHS from the Tories.}}</ref> is a [[Scottish National Party]] (SNP) politician who was elected to the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom]] at the [[2019 United Kingdom general election in Scotland|2019 general election]] as the [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[East Dunbartonshire (UK Parliament constituency)|East Dunbartonshire]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/politics/constituencies/S14000018|title=Dunbartonshire East parliamentary constituency - Election 2019|via=www.bbc.com}}</ref> She unseated the then [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrat]] [[Leader of the Liberal Democrats|leader]], [[Jo Swinson]] with a narrow majority of 149 votes or 0.3%, overturning her majority of 5,339 votes [[2017 UK general election|two years earlier]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2019/dec/13/jo-swinson-lib-dems-on-course-for-grim-night|title=Jo Swinson quits as Lib Dem leader after losing her own seat|last=Walker|first=Peter|date=13 December 2019|work=The Guardian|access-date=14 December 2019|last2=Murphy|first2=Simon|issn=0261-3077|last3=Brooks|first3=Libby}}</ref>

== Background ==
Callaghan grew up in [[Clydebank]] and studied politics at the [[University of Strathclyde]].<ref name=bbc>{{cite web |last1=Diamond |first1=Claire |title=Election 2019: Who is the young SNP MP who beat Jo Swinson? |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-50808566 |website=[[BBC News]] |accessdate=17 December 2019 |date=17 December 2019}}</ref>

Callaghan was diagnosed with [[melanoma]] when she was 19 and cites her experience of cancer as motivation to safeguard the [[NHS]], saying "it can never be jeopardised".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scotsman.com/news/politics/general-election-2019-who-is-amy-callaghan-the-msp-who-beat-jo-swinson-1-5061924|title=General Election 2019: Who is Amy Callaghan, the MSP who beat Jo Swinson?|website=www.scotsman.com|access-date=13 December 2019}}</ref> She has been cancer-free since 2014.<ref name=bbc />

== Political career ==
Prior to her election, she worked as the office manager for MSP [[Rona Mackay]]. She has also previously served on the [[Children's Panel]].<ref name="bbc" />

She was selected as the [[Scottish National Party]]'s candidate for the seat of [[East Dunbartonshire (UK Parliament constituency)|East Dunbartonshire]] in September 2019.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.kirkintilloch-herald.co.uk/news/politics/snp-choose-candidate-to-fight-east-dunbartonshire-seat-1-5014248|title=SNP choose candidate to fight East Dunbartonshire seat|website=Kirkintilloch Herald|access-date=14 December 2019}}</ref> At the December 2019 general election, Callaghan won the seat, beating the incumbent MP and [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrat]] leader, [[Jo Swinson]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Diamond|first=Claire|date=17 December 2019|title=Who is the young SNP MP who beat Jo Swinson?|website=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-50808566|access-date=3 May 2020}}</ref>

In January 2020, she was selected as the SNP's Spokesperson for Pensions and Intergenerational Affairs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thenational.scot/news/18143239.snp-westminster-reshuffle-sees-big-changes-party/|title=These are the big changes as the SNP Westminster team reshuffles|website=The National|access-date=5 March 2020}}</ref> In March 2020, she was selected as a member of the House of Commons [[Health and Social Care Select Committee]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://committees.parliament.uk/committee/81/health-and-social-care-committee/news/144999/health-and-social-care-committee-membership-agreed/|title=Health and Social Care Committee membership agreed - Committees - UK Parliament|website=committees.parliament.uk|access-date=5 March 2020}}</ref> She was later discharged from the Committee in June 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Business without Debate - Hansard|url=https://hansard.parliament.uk/Commons/2020-06-29/debates/417917F3-86A7-4E72-8A84-C4512D8B9C73/BusinessWithoutDebate|access-date=2020-07-31|website=hansard.parliament.uk}}</ref>

In June 2020, Callaghan's office announced that she had suffered a [[brain haemorrhage]], and had undergone emergency neurosurgery.<ref>{{cite tweet |author=Office of Amy Callaghan|user=AmyCallaghanSNP |number=1272438889412734977 |date=15 June 2020 |title=Statement from the Office of Amy Callaghan MP |language=en |access-date=15 June 2020 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20200615081840/https://twitter.com/AmyCallaghanSNP/status/1272438889412734977 |archive-date=15 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-53049438| title= SNP MP Amy Callaghan suffers brain haemorrhage| website=[[BBC News]]| date=15 June 2020| accessdate=15 June 2020| archive-date=15 June 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200615150627/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-53049438}}</ref> She spent four months recovering in the Physically Disabled Rehabilitation Unit at [[Queen Elizabeth University Hospital]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-54555089 |title=SNP MP Amy Callaghan out of hospital after brain haemorrhage |website=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=15 October 2020 |accessdate=15 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite tweet |user=amycallaghansnp |number=1316726910571905026 |title=After 4 months in hospital, today I've been discharged. I'm coming home. To the miracle team at @NHSGGC PDRU - now my second family - thank you. I can never, ever repay you. I was wheeled in here. Now I'm walking out. I'm just getting started too |access-date=2020-10-15 |date=15 October 2020}}</ref>
