'''Brent Redstone''' (born&nbsp;{{circa}}&nbsp;1951) is an American [[lawyer]], [[heir]] and [[entrepreneur]].

==Biography==
Brent Dale Redstone was born in 1951, the son of [[Sumner Redstone]], né Rothstein, and Phyllis Gloria Raphael. He is the brother of [[Shari Redstone]], and grandson of [[Michael Redstone]]. He has two daughters, one of them Keryn Redstone.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2016/biz/news/sumner-redstone-granddaughter-sues-shari-redstone-1201828535/|title=Sumner Redstone’s Granddaughter Turns Up Heat, Accuses Aunt Shari of ‘Emotional Abuse and Treachery’|last=Rainey|first=James|last2=Rainey|first2=James|date=2016-08-02|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2020-04-19}}</ref>

==Career==
Like his father, Brent graduated from [[Harvard University]] and became a lawyer. He earned a law degree from [[Syracuse University]] in 1976. Also like his father, Brent worked as an attorney – practicing as a [[criminal prosecutor]] in [[Boston, Massachusetts|Boston]] for 14 years – prior to joining National Amusements, the family business. He is currently Licensed by the [[Colorado Bar Association]].<ref>[https://articles.latimes.com/2007/jul/30/business/fi-shari30 Eller, Claudia] "Father-daughter bond shattered"; ''LA Times''; 30 July 2007.</ref>

During his brief tenure at [[Viacom (2005–present)|Viacom]], Brent was removed from the board of parent company [[National Amusements]], Inc., in 2003. In 2006, he sued his father and sister, later dropping the lawsuit after an undisclosed settlement was reached, which reportedly included the buyout of Brent's one-sixth stake in National Amusements.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.denverpost.com/search/ci_5164430|title=Search Results for "ci_5164430"|website=The Denver Post|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-19}}</ref> Shari Redstone owned 20% of National Amusements and Sumner Redstone owned 80%.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Marr|first=Merissa|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052970203937004578078911082391282|title=Redstone Daughter in Succession Mix|date=2012-10-26|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2020-04-19|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> National Amusements holds a controlling stake in [[CBS Corporation]], [[Viacom (2005–present)|Viacom]], [[MTV Networks]], [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]], [[Paramount Pictures]], and are equal partners in [[MovieTickets.com]].

In 2016, the Redstone family entered into legalities over whether Sumner Redstone is mentally competent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/cotown/la-et-ct-sumner-redstone-lawsuit-granddaughter-keryn-redstone-20160419-story.html|title=Sumner Redstone's granddaughter looks to enter legal fray, exposing a new family rift|date=2016-04-20|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-19}}</ref> At the time, he used an [[iPad]] to communicate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/04/03/sumner-redstone-communicates-with-an-ipad-programmed-with-a-profanity.html|title=Sumner Redstone now communicates with an iPad programmed with his own voice spouting a profanity|last=Franck|first=Thomas|date=2018-04-03|website=CNBC|language=en|access-date=2020-04-19}}</ref>
