'''Linda (Bahnson) Avey''' is a U.S. biologist and entrepreneur. She is known for launching and commercializing the consumer genetics industry in the US.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/2007/11/ff-genomics/|title=23AndMe Will Decode Your DNA for $1,000. Welcome to the Age of Genomics|last=Goetz|first=Thomas|date=2007-11-17|work=Wired|access-date=2019-05-22|issn=1059-1028}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/17/us/17dna.html|title=My Genome, Myself: Seeking Clues in DNA|last=Harmon|first=Amy|date=2007-11-17|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-05-22|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

== Early years ==

Avey was born in 1960, in [[South Dakota]], United States. She attended [[Augustana University]], where she received a Bachelor of Arts in biology in 1982.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.linkedin.com/in/lindaavey/#education-section|title=Linda Avey|website=Linkedin|access-date=May 5, 2018}}</ref>

Avey began her scientific career in 1982 at [[University of California]], Irvine as a staff research associate.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Bahnson|first=Linda S.|last2=Buckpitt|first2=Alan R.|date=1986|title=Naphthalene metabolism by human lung microsomal enzymes|url=https://ucdavis.pure.elsevier.com/en/publications/naphthalene-metabolism-by-human-lung-microsomal-enzymes|journal=Toxicology|volume=41|issue=3|pages=333–341|doi=10.1016/0300-483X(86)90186-1|issn=0300-483X|pmid=3775781}}</ref> In 1985 she moved into various sales and business development in the fields of biopharmaceutical and academic research based in [[San Francisco]], [[Boston]], [[San Diego]], and [[Washington, D.C.|Washington, DC]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/realspin/2017/02/15/the-privacy-delusions-of-genetic-testing/#5f7c15651bba|title=The Privacy Delusions Of Genetic Testing|last=Flows|first=Capital|work=Forbes|access-date=July 20, 2018}}</ref> She worked for Perlegen Sciences (2003-2005), coordinating the world's first genome-wide association studies,<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Maraganore|first=Demetrius M.|last2=de Andrade|first2=Mariza|author2-link=Mariza de Andrade|last3=Lesnick|first3=Timothy G.|last4=Strain|first4=Kari J.|last5=Farrer|first5=Matthew J.|last6=Rocca|first6=Walter A.|last7=Pant|first7=P. V. Krishna|last8=Frazer|first8=Kelly A.|last9=Cox|first9=David R.|date=November 2005|title=High-Resolution Whole-Genome Association Study of Parkinson Disease|journal=American Journal of Human Genetics|volume=77|issue=5|pages=685–693|issn=0002-9297|pmc=1271381|pmid=16252231|doi=10.1086/496902}}</ref> and for [[Affymetrix]] on the translational medicine team (2005-2006) with the goal of identifying genetic markers for diagnostic tests<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://bigthink.com/experts/lindaavey|title=Linda Avey|website=Big Think|access-date=May 31, 2018}}</ref> She also held positions at [https://www.tibco.com/products/tibco-spotfire Spotfire], [[Chemdex.com|Chemdex]], [[Applied Biosystems]], [https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=32884 PerSeptive Biosystems], Molecular Dynamics, and [https://www.waters.com/waters/en_US/About-Waters-Corporation-/nav.htm?cid=134614448&locale=en_US Waters Associates].
