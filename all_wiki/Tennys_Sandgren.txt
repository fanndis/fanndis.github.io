'''Tennys Sandgren II''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|t|ɛ|n|ɪ|s|_|ˈ|s|æ|n|d|ɡ|r|ən}} {{respell|TEN|iss|_|SAND|grən}};<ref>{{cite web|title=The pronunciation by Tennys Sandgren himself|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/-/media/player-names/tennys-sandgren.mp3|work=ATPWorldTour.com|accessdate=2018-01-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ObesKKuZKtE|title=5 Questions with Tennys Sandgren|website=YouTube|author=Atlantic Tire Championships|date=2016-09-15|accessdate=2018-01-18}}</ref> born July 22, 1991) is an [[United States|American]] professional tennis player who broke into the Top 100 of the ATP rankings toward the end of 2017 after competing mainly on the [[ATP Challenger Tour]] for many years. Born and raised in [[Gallatin, Tennessee]], he played two years of college tennis at the [[University of Tennessee]] before launching his professional career.

==Early life==
Sandgren's parents, South African Lia Lourens and American David Sandgren, met at a tennis club in Johannesburg. They married and moved to Tennessee in 1988 with Sandgren's older brother, Davey.

Sandgren, who was named Tennys after his Swedish great-grandfather, was home-schooled and is coached by his mother. Davey Sandgren is also a tennis player who achieved a career-high ATP ranking of 800 in doubles in 2009.

==Junior career==
As a junior, Sandgren compiled a singles win/loss record of 70-38 (and 53-35 in doubles), reaching as high as No. 9 in the combined ITF junior world rankings in April 2009.<ref name=ITFTennis>{{cite web|title=ITF Tennis Juniors - Player Profile|url=http://www.itftennis.com/juniors/players/player/profile.aspx?playerid=100088762|publisher=ITF Tennis|accessdate=January 9, 2013}}</ref>

==College career==
Sandgren was a January midseason addition to the Tennessee Volunteers' 2010 tennis roster, joining older brother, Davey, who was a senior All-American on the team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tennys Sandgren Joins Volunteers|url=http://www.utsports.com/sports/m-tennis/spec-rel/010810aaa.html|work=utsports.com|publisher=University of Tennessee|accessdate=January 9, 2013}}</ref> Coached by [[Sam Winterbotham]] and [[Chris Woodruff]], the younger Sandgren immediately strengthened the middle of the Vols' singles lineup, going 10-0 in [[Southeastern Conference]] play at the No. 4 position to help the team win the SEC regular-season and tournament titles.<ref name=utsportsbio>{{cite web|title=Tennys Sandgren Profile|url=http://www.utsports.com/sports/m-tennis/mtt/sandgren_tennys00.html|work=utsports.com|publisher=University of Tennessee|accessdate=January 9, 2013}}</ref> The team reached the NCAA team finals that year.

As a sophomore, Sandgren reached the semifinals of the NCAA Singles Championships, losing to teammate, roommate and doubles partner [[Rhyne Williams]] in three sets. He finished the season with a 37-6 record and his 10-1 record in conference helped the Vols to an SEC regular-season title. He finished his career with a 60-12 singles record (83.33 percent), the third-best career winning percentage in Tennessee history.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tennessee Tennis Record Book|url=http://grfx.cstv.com/photos/schools/tenn/sports/m-tennis/auto_pdf/2011-12/misc_non_event/MTennisRecordBook.pdf|work=utsports.com|publisher=University of Tennessee|accessdate=January 9, 2013}}</ref>

He was also a member of the [[USTA]] Summer Collegiate Team after his freshman and sophomore seasons.

==Professional career==
===2011–2016: Early years, Futures success and first Challenger title===
[[File:Tennys Sandgren Challenger of Dallas, Feb 2013.jpg|thumb|250px|right|Sandgren in action during the [[2013 Challenger of Dallas]]]]
Sandgren turned professional in 2011, playing mostly at the [[ITF Men's Circuit|ITF Futures]] level through 2012. In 2013, his ATP ranking remained inside the top 300 for nearly the entire year, allowing him to play on the Challenger Tour for much of the season. In his final tournament of the year, Sandgren won his first final at Challenger level in [[JSM Challenger of Champaign–Urbana|Champaign]] to crack the Top 200 for the first time.

In 2014, Sandgren missed half of the year after undergoing hip surgery near the beginning of the season.<ref>{{cite web|title=ATP No. 136 Tennys Sandgren on the rise after hip surgery|url=http://www.tallahassee.com/story/sports/2017/04/24/atp-no-136-tennys-sandgren-rise-after-hip-surgery/100846526/|website=Tallahassee Democrat|accessdate=20 January 2018}}</ref> Upon returning to the court, it took him nearly a year to get back to the Top 250 and the Challenger level. Sandgren returned to the Top 200 in November 2016, when he reached his second career Challenger final in [[Columbus Challenger|Columbus]], almost three years after he first achieved these two milestones.

===2017: Challenger Tour breakthrough, top 100===
In 2017, Sandgren reached five Challenger finals, winning two of them in [[Tempe Challenger|Tempe]] and [[Savannah Challenger|Savannah]]. He qualified for his first tournament on the [[ATP World Tour]], the [[U.S. Men's Clay Court Championships]] in [[Houston]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Tennys Sandgren Bio ATP World Tour Tennis|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/en/players/tennys-sandgren/sl08/bio|website=ATP World Tour|publisher=ATP Tour, Inc.|accessdate=20 January 2018}}</ref> Sandgren's success earned him a wildcard berth into the main draw of the [[2017 French Open|French Open]]. In his [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] debut, Sandgren lost in the first round to [[Mikhail Kukushkin]].<ref>Bergman, Justin. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/tennis/tennys-sandgrens-game-and-name-on-the-rise-in-australia/2018/01/19/d43c6d2a-fcda-11e7-9b5d-bbf0da31214d_story.html Tennys Sandgren’s game, and name, on the rise in Australia.] Washington Post: January 19, 2018. Retrieved January 21, 2018.</ref> A semifinals appearance in [[Czech Open (tennis)|Prostejov]] helped him to break into the Top 100. He recorded his first two ATP World Tour wins at the [[Washington Open (tennis)|Washington Open]] in early August, including a victory over No. 20 [[Nick Kyrgios]]. At the [[2017 US Open – Men's Singles|U.S. Open]], Sandgren lost in the first round to No. 7 [[Marin Cilic]].

===2018: Australian Open quarterfinal, maiden ATP final===
[[File:Sandgren RG18 (49) (42077234085).jpg|thumb|200px|right|Sandgren at the [[2018 French Open]]]]
Sandgren began the 2018 season with a first-round loss to [[Gilles Simon]] in [[2018 Maharashtra Open – Singles|Pune]]. He followed with a loss to [[Casper Ruud]] in the second qualifying round at the [[2018 ASB Classic – Men's Singles|Auckland Open]]. Sandgren received entry into the main draw of the tournament as a [[lucky loser]] following the withdrawal of [[Kyle Edmund]]. In the first round of the main draw, he lost in three sets to [[Hyeon Chung]], winner of the inaugural [[Next Generation ATP Finals]] in [[Milan]], [[Italy]], two months prior. Making his [[Australian Open]] main draw debut, Sandgren won his opening match against French player [[Jeremy Chardy]]. In the second round, he defeated [[2014 Australian Open – Men's Singles|2014 champion]] and ninth seed [[Stan Wawrinka]], marking his first victory against a Top 10 ranked player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/tennis/australian-open-tennys-sandgren-demolishes-stan-wawrinka-to-move-into-third-round-in-melbourne/news-story/8569c357e75d46d61b0e2beefe6e1f94|title=Australian Open: Tennys Sandgren demolishes Stan Wawrinka to move into third round in Melbourne|website=Herald Sun|date=19 January 2018}}</ref> Following a victory over [[Maximilian Marterer]] in the third round, Sandgren defeated world No. 5 [[Dominic Thiem]] in Sandgren’s first five-set match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/sport/tennis/tennis-giant-killer-tennys-sandgrens-fairy-tale-run-goes-on-with-dominic-thiem-upset|title=Giant-killer Tennys Sandgren's fairy-tale run goes on with Dominic Thiem upset|website=The Straits Times|date=22 January 2018}}</ref> He lost to Chung in the quarterfinals in straight sets.<ref>{{cite web|title=From Milan To Melbourne, Chung Keeps Surging|url=http://www.atpworldtour.com/en/news/chung-sandgren-australian-open-2018-qf|website=atpworldtour.com|accessdate=24 January 2018}}</ref>

As the No. 1 seed, Sandgren was defeated 1-6, 2-6 in the first round of qualification for the [[2018 Argentina Open – Singles|Argentina Open]] by world No. 188 [[Facundo Bagnis]]. He lost in the second round of the [[2018 Rio Open|Rio Open]], the first round of the [[2018 Brasil Open|Brasil Open]], the second round in [[2018 BNP Paribas Open|Indian Wells]] and the first round in [[2018 Miami Open|Miami]].

At the beginning of the clay season, Sandgren reached his first final on the ATP World Tour at the [[2018 U.S. Men's Clay Court Championships – Singles|U.S. Men's Clay Court Championships]], but lost to [[Steve Johnson (tennis)|Steve Johnson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tennis.com/pro-game/2018/04/johnson-successfully-defends-houston-title-against-inspired-sandgren/73340/|title=Johnson successfully defends Houston title against inspired Sandgren|website=Tennis.com|date=15 April 2018}}</ref>

Sandgren lost in the first round of the [[Monte Carlo Open]] to [[Philipp Kohlschreiber]] in straight sets. He suffered another first-round loss in [[2018 Barcelona Open Banco Sabadell|Barcelona]] to [[Malek Jaziri]], also in straight sets.

Following a first-round loss against [[Frances Tiafoe]] at the [[2018 Estoril Open|Estoril Open]], Sandgren suffered his fourth consecutive first round loss, losing in straight sets to [[Denis Shapovalov]] at the [[2018 Mutua Madrid Open|Madrid Open]].

After having reached the quarterfinals at the [[2018 Geneva Open|Geneva Open]], Sandgren was knocked out in the first round of the [[2018 French Open – Men's Singles|French Open]] by world No. 177 [[Hubert Hurkacz]]. He lost in the first round of the doubles tournament in straight sets.

In the first round of [[2018 Wimbledon Championships – Men's Singles|Wimbledon]], Sandgren was knocked out by eventual champion [[Novak Djokovic]] in straight sets, winning only six games.

In the first round of the [[2018 US Open – Men's Singles|U.S. Open]], Sandgren beat [[Viktor Troicki]] in straight sets before losing to Djokovic in four sets in the second round.

===2019: First ATP title===
In January, Sandgren won his maiden ATP Tour title at the [[2019 ASB Classic|Auckland Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.atptour.com/en/news/sandgren-beats-norrie-in-auckland-2019-to-win-first-title|title=Super Sandgren: Tennys Wins Maiden Title In Auckland|website=ATP Tour|date=12 January 2019}}</ref> He achieved his career high in rankings at 41 on January 14. However, after the title, Sandgren suffered a 9-match tour-level losing streak, which he finally snapped at Wimbledon, where he reached the fourth round before losing to Sam Querrey. He then reached the quarterfinals at Newport and the third round at the U.S. Open. At the end of the season, a fractured toe kept him inactive, and his ranking sank back out of the top 100.

===2020: Second Australian Open quarterfinal===
Sandgren again made it to the quarterfinals of the Australian Open, where he lost in five tight sets against [[Roger Federer]], after holding (and losing) seven match points.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/28/sports/tennis/roger-federer-australian-open.html|title=Roger Federer Wins Tough Quarterfinal, Beating Tennys Sandgren|website=New York Times|date=28 January 2020}}</ref>

==World TeamTennis==
Sandgren has played two seasons with [[World TeamTennis]] starting in 2015 when he debuted in the league with the [[California Dream (tennis)|California Dream]] and then again in 2018 with the [[Washington Kastles]]. It was announced that he will join the [[Orlando Storm]] during the 2020 season set to begin July 12.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://orlandostorm.com/2020/04/10/orlando-storm-announces-2020-roster/?blog_id=8 |title=Orlando Storm Announces 2020 Roster |website=OrlandoStorm.com |date=April 10, 2020 }}</ref>

Sandgren posted a 9-6 singles record throughout the season, posting a winning percentage (games won-lost) of 53%, the second-highest in the league. He also paired up with [[Ken Skupski]] in men's doubles as well as [[Jessica Pegula]] in mixed doubles to help the Storm earn a No. 3 seed in the WTT Playoffs. The Storm would ultimately fall to the [[Chicago Smash]] in the semifinals.

==Twitter controversy==
{{Criticism section|date=September 2020}}Sandgren's activity on [[Twitter]] came under scrutiny during the [[2018 Australian Open]] when it was noted that he was a follower of a number of right wing individuals and organizations, including co-founder and former leader of the [[English Defence League]], [[Tommy Robinson (activist)|Tommy Robinson]], and activist Nicholas Fuentes.{{Dubious|date=September 2020}} 

Sandgren has debated racial injustice in America with fellow tennis player James Blake on Twitter. During the exchange he denied systemic racism exists in the US because the country elected a black president. Sandgren tweeted "I just don't know how a country that practices systematic racism elected a black pres, twice”.<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2018/jan/22/tennys-sandgren-alt-right-australian-open-twitter</ref>

When questioned on his [[social media]] activity, Sandgren said he found some of the alt-right content interesting, but that to fully support alt-right beliefs would be incompatible with his Christian faith.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/tennis/42776652|work=The BBC|title=Australian Open 2018: Tennys Sandgren says he is not a far-right sympathiser|date=22 January 2018|accessdate=22 Jan 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2018/jan/22/tennys-sandgren-alt-right-australian-open-twitter|work=The Guardian|title=Tennys Sandgren forced to deny far-right sympathies at Australian Open|date=22 January 2018|accessdate=22 Jan 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/tennis/2018/01/22/australian-open-surprise-package-tennys-sandgren-denies-support/|work=The Telegraph|title=Australian Open surprise package Tennys Sandgren denies support for American alt-right|date=22 January 2018|accessdate=22 Jan 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/tennis/2018/01/22/tennys-sandgren-australian-open-social-media-links-tweets-alt-right/1053072001/|work=USA Today|title=Australian Open: Tennys Sandgren faces questions about social media links to alt-right movement|date=22 January 2018|accessdate=22 Jan 2018}}</ref> He later clarified: "Definitely not all 'alt-right' content is interesting, just some individuals' specific content".

Within 48 hours of the controversy emerging, Sandgren had deleted several years of [[Twitter#Tweets|tweets]], claiming he wanted to create a "version of a cleaner start".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2018/jan/22/tennys-sandgren-alt-right-australian-open-twitter|title=Tennys Sandgren deletes tweets after denying far-right sympathies at Australian Open|website=The Guardian}}</ref>
