'''Latifur Rahman''' (1 March 1936 – 6 June 2017) was the 10th [[Chief Justice]] and the 2nd [[Chief Adviser of Bangladesh]].<ref name=passes>{{cite news|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/backpage/justice-latifur-passes-away-1416580|title=Justice Latifur passes away|date=June 6, 2017|accessdate=June 6, 2017|newspaper=The Daily Star}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Rahman was born in [[Jessore (city)|Jessore]] on 1 March 1936. His father was the lawyer Khan Bahadur Lutfur Rahman.<ref name=dies>{{cite news|url=http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2017/06/06/former-chief-adviser-justice-latifur-rahman-dies-at-81|title=Former chief adviser Justice Latifur Rahman dies at 81|newspaper=bdnews24.com|date=6 June 2017|accessdate=7 June 2017}}</ref> His maternal uncle Nurul Huda served as a High Court judge. He earned his master's in English literature at the [[University of Dhaka]] in 1956 and later completed his bachelor's in law from the same university.<ref name=dies/> He served as a faculty member of Shahid Suhrawardy College.

==Career==
Rahman started his lawyer career with the Dhaka High Court membership in 1960. He was an apprentice to Bangladesh's first [[attorney general]] MH Khandaker.<ref name=dies/> He became a permanent High Court judge in 1981 and an Appellate Division judge on January 15, 1990.<ref name=passes/>

Rahman became the [[Chief Justice]] on 1 January 2000 and retired from the position on 28 February 2001. He was the chief adviser of the [[caretaker government]] from 15 July 2001 to 10 October 2001 which oversaw the [[2001 Bangladeshi general election|eighth parliamentary election in Bangladesh]].<ref name=passes/>

Rahman died at Samorita Hospital in Dhaka on 6 June 2017.<ref name=passes/>
