[[Vice admiral (Royal Navy)|Vice Admiral]] '''Sir Robert Walmsley''', {{postnominals|country=GBR|size=100%|sep=,|KCB|FREng|FRSA|FIET}} (born 1 February 1941) is a retired senior officer of the [[Royal Navy]] who served as [[Chief of Defence Procurement]] at the UK [[Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)|Ministry of Defence]] from 1996 to 2003.

==Early career==
Robert Walmsley was born in [[Aberdeen]], Scotland, on 1 February 1941, a son of the anatomist [[Robert Walmsley (anatomist)|Robert Walmsley]] and Dr Isabel Mary Walmsley.<ref name="ukwhoswho">{{cite web |title=Walmsley, Sir Robert |url=https://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/10.1093/ww/9780199540884.001.0001/ww-9780199540884-e-38750/version/12 |website=ukwhoswho |accessdate=4 March 2020}}</ref> He was educated at [[Fettes College]] in [[Edinburgh]].<ref name="ukwhoswho"/><ref name=fettes>[http://fettescom.site.securepod.com/history/distinguished.htm Distinguished Pupils] Fettes College</ref> He joined the [[Royal Navy]] as a [[Britannia Royal Naval College|Dartmouth]] Cadet in 1958,<ref name=cox>[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/main.jhtml?xml=/sport/2002/03/22/sbquot23.xml "The men who found fame after the Boat Race"], The Daily Telegraph, 22 March 2002.</ref> and went on to read Mechanical Sciences at [[Queens' College, Cambridge]],<ref>[http://www.queens.cam.ac.uk/general-information/historical-fun/queens-college/eminent-alumni Eminent alumni] Queens' College, Cambridge</ref> before taking up a range of seagoing appointments, mainly in submarines, developing an expertise in nuclear propulsion. During his naval career he was at times Chairman of the Naval Nuclear Technical Safety Panel and Director General, Submarines. For three years, he was the [[Assistant Chief of the Defence Staff]] for Communications, Command, Control and Information Systems. His final naval appointment (in 1994) was as [[Third Sea Lord|Controller of the Navy]]<ref name=fettes/> and member of the Navy Board as a vice admiral. He was appointed a [[Knight Commander of the Order of the Bath]] in 1995.

Walmsley is a former [[Cambridge Blue]], having coxed the light blue boat to victory in the 108th Oxford and Cambridge [[Boat Race]] in 1962.<ref name=cox/> He is a [[Fellow]] of the [[Royal Academy of Engineering]],<ref name="List of Fellows">{{cite web|title=List of Fellows|url=http://www.raeng.org.uk/about-us/people-council-committees/the-fellowship/list-of-fellows}}</ref> the [[Royal Society of Arts]], and the [[Institution of Engineering and Technology]] (IET), and is an Honorary DSc of [[Cranfield University]].<ref>[http://www.cranfield.ac.uk/alumni/graduation/hongrads/hongrads1970-07.pdf Honorary Graduates] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716231840/http://www.cranfield.ac.uk/alumni/graduation/hongrads/hongrads1970-07.pdf |date=16 July 2011 }} Cranfield University</ref>

==Chief of Defence Procurement==
[[File:Defense.gov News Photo 011026-D-9880W-054.jpg|thumb|right|Walmsley (2nd from right) at [[The Pentagon]] in 2001]]
After retiring from the navy, Walmsley held the position of [[Chief of Defence Procurement]] at the UK [[Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)|Ministry of Defence]] from 1996<ref>[http://www.prnewswire.co.uk/cgi/news/release?id=27012 "Robert Walmsley appointed Chief of Defence Procurement"], PR Newswire on behalf of Ministry of Defence, 24 April 1996.</ref> until his retirement on 30 April 2003. He retired from the [[British Civil Service|Her Majesty's Civil Service]] after serving longer in the Defence Procurement post than any of his predecessors. During Walmsley's period in post he led the transformation of the procurement organisation into the [[Defence Procurement Agency]] (DPA, now [[Defence Equipment and Support]]) and made numerous appearances as a witness to [[Select committee (United Kingdom)|Parliamentary select committees]].<ref>[https://publications.parliament.uk/pa/cm199900/cmselect/cmdfence/219/21908.htm Select Committees, Session 1998–99] Parliament</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1967, he married Christina, they had one son and two daughters, and divorced in 2009.<ref name="ukwhoswho"/> In 2010, he married Alexandra Ashbourne.<ref name="ukwhoswho"/>

{{HMS|Sutherland|F81|6}} was launched by Christina, Lady Walmsley, at that time the wife of Sir Robert, in 1996.<ref>[http://www.royalnavy.mod.uk/The-Fleet/Ships/Frigates/HMS-Sutherland HMS Sutherland] Royal Navy website</ref> This launch made naval history since Lady Walmsley broke with tradition and used a bottle of [[The Macallan|Macallan Whisky]]; up until this time Royal Navy ships had always been launched with a bottle of champagne.

Walmsley's daughter, [[Emma Walmsley|Emma]], is the chief executive of the global pharmaceutical company [[Glaxo Smithkline]].
