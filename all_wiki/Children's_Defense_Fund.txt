The '''Children's Defense Fund''' (CDF) is an American [[501(c) organization|501(c)(3)]] [[nonprofit organization]] that focuses on child advocacy and research. It was founded in 1973 by [[Marian Wright Edelman]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = Charity Navigator Rating – Children's Defense Fund|url = http://www.charitynavigator.org/index.cfm?bay=search.summary&orgid=3481#.VrfTMzYrKRs|website = Charity Navigator|access-date = 2016-02-07}}</ref>

==History==
The CDF was founded in 1973, citing inspiration from the [[Civil Rights Movement]], with the goal of improving federal policies concerning child welfare and public education systems.<ref name=":1">{{Cite book|title = Encyclopedia of Human Services and Diversity|url = https://books.google.com/books?id=Spd0BAAAQBAJ|publisher = SAGE Publications|date = 2014-09-05|isbn = 9781483346656|language = en|first = Linwood H.|last = Cousins}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title = Children's Defense Fund Joins Forces With JJ Abrams To Celebrate Founder Marian Wright Edelman's Vision|url = https://www.forbes.com/sites/tiffanypham/2014/09/11/childrens-defense-fund-joins-forces-with-jj-abrams-to-celebrate-founder-marian-wright-edelmans-vision/#5c85c74f67c0|website = Forbes|access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref>

CDF is headquartered in Washington, D.C., and has offices in several states around the country: [[California]], [[Minnesota]], [[New York (state)|New York]], [[Ohio]], [[Mississippi]], [[South Carolina]], [[Tennessee]] and [[Texas]]. CDF programs operate in 28 states.{{citation needed|date=February 2016}}

== Activities ==
Since its founding, the CDF has lobbied for passing legislation related to its goals including the [[Education for All Handicapped Children Act]]<ref name=":1" /> in 1975 (now known as the [[Individuals with Disabilities Education Act]]) and the Adoption Assistance and Child Welfare Act in 1980. Its legislative interests have also included [[Head Start Program|Head Start]], [[Medicaid]], Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP), and the Child Income Tax Credit.<ref name=":1" />

The CDF has run several public awareness campaigns, including the Adolescent Pregnancy Prevention Campaign in 1986,<ref>{{Cite web|title = Teen Pregnancy in America Today|url = http://www.huffingtonpost.com/marian-wright-edelman/teen-pregnancy-in-america_b_134167.html|website = The Huffington Post|access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref> the [[gun violence]] prevention campaign, and ending child poverty.

The CDF's programs include a modern [[Freedom Schools]] program launched in 1993 for child enrichment through reading,<ref>{{Cite web|title = For the love of learning: Freedom Schools go above and beyond helping kids grow|url = http://www.kansascity.com/news/local/community/816/article693774.html|website = kansascity|access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Freedom Schools programs come to Sacramento for first time|url = http://www.sacbee.com/news/local/education/article2603045.html|website = sacbee|access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref> a Beat the Odds program launched in 1990 that hosts awareness events and awards partial college scholarships,<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|title = Jennifer Garner, Conan O’Brien Honor Amazing Kids at Beat the Odds Awards|url = https://variety.com/2013/scene/news/jennifer-garner-conan-obrien-honor-amazing-kids-at-beat-the-odds-awards-1200926429/|website = Variety|access-date = 2016-02-08|language = en-US|first = Alex|last = Stedman}}</ref> and a Youth Advocacy Leadership Training fund.{{citation needed|reason=needs further research from third-party sources.|date=February 2016}}

In recent years, CDF funds generated numerous child advocacy reports. These reports range in topic, from ending child poverty and minority incarceration rates (and the [[school to prison pipeline]]) to gun safety concerning children.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title = Navigating the 'school-to-prison-pipeline' for minorities|url = http://www.scpr.org/blogs/multiamerican/2012/03/08/7883/navigating-the-school-to-prison-pipeline-for-minor/|website = Southern California Public Radio|date = 2012-03-08|access-date = 2016-02-08|first = Southern California Public|last = Radio}}</ref><ref>[http://www.childrensdefense.org/programs-campaigns/protect-children-not-guns/ Protect Children, not Guns]</ref>

In 2008, the CDF was among the charities receiving donations from the "[[Idol Gives Back]]" televised fundraising event.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Simon Cowell Donates Paycheck to Idol Gives Back|url = http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20423954,00.html|website = PEOPLE.com|access-date = 2016-02-08}}</ref>

Numerous notable individuals have been actively involved with the charity. J. Abrams|J.J. Abrams]] has funded CDF Freedom Schools in the past.<ref name=":2" />
