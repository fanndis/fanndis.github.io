'''Shana Knizhnik''' (born August 22, 1988<ref name = "Knizhnik">{{Cite web|title = Shana Knizhnik|url = https://twitter.com/shanakn|website = Twitter|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref>) is an [[United States|American]] [[lawyer]] and [[author]] from [[Philadelphia]].<ref name = "NYU Law">{{Cite web|title = Notorious R.B.G. Blogger Shana Knizhnik '15 Makes Her Mark On Campus And Off|url = http://www.law.nyu.edu/news/shana-knizhnik-notorious-rbg-tumblr|website = NYU School of Law|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref> She is best known for her ''New York Times'' [[The New York Times Best Seller list|bestselling book]],<ref name = "Times">{{Cite web|title = Best Sellers - The New York Times|url = https://www.nytimes.com/best-sellers-books/2015-11-15/hardcover-nonfiction/list.html|website = New York Times|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref> ''Notorious R.B.G.: The Life and Times of Ruth Bader Ginsburg'', co-written with [[MSNBC]] reporter [[Irin Carmon]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Irin Carmon and Shana Knizhnik - Notorious RBG: The Life and Times of Ruth Bader Ginsburg|url = http://www.laweekly.com/event/irin-carmon-and-shana-knizhnik-notorious-rbg-the-life-and-times-of-ruth-bader-ginsburg-7489483|website = LA Weekly|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Knizhnik is Jewish.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/shanakn/status/827186334574006278|url=https://twitter.com/shanakn/status/827186334574006278|access-date=2020-09-21|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> She graduated from [[Julia R. Masterman School]] (2006), the [[Columbia College, Columbia University]] (2010),<ref>{{Cite web|title = Students Named To Forbes "30 Under 30"|url = https://www.college.columbia.edu/news/students-named-forbes-30-under-30|website = Columbia College|access-date = 2016-12-17}}</ref> and the [[New York University School of Law]] (2015).<ref name = "NYU Law"/> While at Columbia, Shana competed in the [[American Mock Trial Association]] and served as an Assistant Coach for Columbia Mock Trial.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.collegemocktrial.org/SpotlightKnizhnik.pdf|title=AMTA Spotlight: Distinguished Alumna, Shana Knizhnik}}</ref> During her time at NYU, she served as an Articles Editor of the [[New York University Law Review]] and served on the Boards of the Coalition for Law and Representation and OUTLaw and performed in the NYU Law Revue and the [[a cappella]] group Substantial Performance.<ref name = "NYU Law"/> She interned at the [[American Civil Liberties Union]], the [[Public Defender Service for the District of Columbia]], and the [[Legal Aid Society]].<ref name = "Huffington">{{Cite web|title = Author Profile: Shana Knizhnik|url = http://www.huffingtonpost.com/author/shana-knizhnik|website = Huffington Post|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref> Upon her graduation, Knizhnik went on to clerk for [[Dolores Sloviter]] of the [[United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit]].<ref name = "NYU Law"/><ref name = "Huffington"/><ref name = "Forbes">{{Cite web|title = 2016 30 Under 30: Media - Shana Knizhnik|url = https://www.forbes.com/pictures/flji45ffil/shana-knizhnik-27|website = Forbes|access-date = 2016-12-17}}</ref> Following her clerkship, she worked as a Legal Fellow at the [[American Civil Liberties Union]] of the District of Columbia.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.acludc.org/en/press-releases/aclu-dc-sues-dc-police-entering-trans-activists-home-and-arresting-her-without|title=ACLU-DC Sues D.C. Police For Entering Trans Activist's Home And Arresting Her Without a Warrant|date=2017-02-28|work=ACLU of DC|access-date=2017-09-25|language=en}}</ref>

==Notorious R.B.G.==
While attending NYU Law in June 2013, she started the [[blog]] Notorious R.B.G. in response to several dissenting opinions authored by [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] Justice [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Notorious RBG - Interview with Jeffrey Toobin|url = https://www.c-span.org/video/?400230-1/irin-carmon-shana-knizhnik-notorious-rbg|website = C-SPAN|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref> The blog quickly went viral and gained media attention within its first few days.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Davis|first1=Allison|title = NYU Law Student Is Making Ruth Bader Ginsburg A Meme|url = http://nymag.com/thecut/2013/06/nyu-law-student-is-making-bader-ginsburg-a-meme.html|date=June 27, 2013}}</ref> Knizhnik created Notorious R.B.G. shirts to go along with the blog, of which she sold roughly 2,000 within the first two months.<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Dale|first1=Maryclaire|title = Ginsburg: Gay Marriage Shows Constitution's Genius|url = http://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/sdut-ginsburg-gay-marriage-shows-constitutions-genius-2013sep06-story.html|date=September 6, 2013}}</ref>

In January 2015, it was announced that Knizhnik would co-author a biography of Justice Ginsburg with [[Irin Carmon]], to be titled ''Notorious R.B.G.: The Life and Times of Ruth Bader Ginsburg''.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Stoeffel|first1=Kat|title=Notorious R.B.G. Gets Her Own Biography, From the People Who Made Her a Meme|url=http://nymag.com/thecut/2015/01/meme-blogger-will-co-author-rbgs-biography.html|date=January 7, 2015}}</ref> The book was released in October 2015<ref>{{Cite web|title = Notorious RBG - Irin Carmon, Shana Knizhnik - Hardcover|url = http://www.harpercollins.com/9780062415837/notorious-rbg|website = HarperCollins US|access-date = 2016-12-16}}</ref> and debuted at #7 on ''The New York Times'' [[The New York Times Best Seller list|Best Seller]] list.<ref name = "Times"/> Carmon and Knizhnik appear in the 2018 documentary, ''[[RBG (film)|RBG]].''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/rbg-review-1076848|title='RBG': Film Review {{!}} Sundance 2018|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-05-18|language=en}}</ref>

In 2016, Knizhnik was named one of ''[[Forbes 30 under 30]]'' in Media.<ref name = "Forbes"/>
