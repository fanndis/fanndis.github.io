On May 26, 2017, Jeremy Joseph Christian fatally stabbed two men and injured a third after he was confronted for shouting racist and [[Islamophobia|anti-Muslim]] slurs at two teenage Black girls, Destinee Mangum and Walia Mohamed, on a [[MAX Light Rail]] train in [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref name=Hassan>Adeel Hassan, [https://www.nytimes.com/2020/02/21/us/white-supremacist-guilty-of-killing-2-who-came-to-aid-of-black-teens.html White Supremacist Guilty of Killing 2 Who Came to Aid of Black Teens], ''New York Times'' (February 21, 2020).</ref><ref name="GreenGuilty">{{cite news|url=https://www.oregonlive.com/news/2020/02/jeremy-christian-guilty-on-all-counts-in-max-stabbings-trial.html|title=Jeremy Christian guilty on all counts in MAX stabbings trial|date=February 21, 2020|first=Aimee|last=Green|work=[[The Oregonian]]|accessdate=February 21, 2020}}</ref> Two of the victims, Ricky John Best of [[Happy Valley, Oregon|Happy Valley]] and Taliesin Myrddin Namkai-Meche of Portland, were killed; the third victim, Micah David-Cole Fletcher, survived serious wounds.

Jeremy, a [[white supremacist]]<ref name=Hassan/> and self-described [[White nationalism|white nationalist]],<ref name="Frankel"/> was arrested and indicted soon after the attack on charges of murder, attempted murder, and other crimes,<ref name=VanderHart>{{cite news |last1=VanderHart |first1=Dirk |title=Jeremy Trial Delayed, Despite Objections From Victims |url=https://www.opb.org/news/article/jeremy-christian-murder-trial-delayed-until-january-2020/ |accessdate=February 22, 2020 |work=[[Oregon Public Broadcasting]] |date=May 3, 2019 |language=en}}</ref><ref name=WilsonWontSeek>{{cite news |last1=Wilson |first1=Conrad |title=Prosecutors Won't Seek Death Penalty In Jeremy Christian In TriMet Stabbings Case |url=https://www.opb.org/news/article/trimet-stabbings-2017-jeremy-christian-trial-death-penalty/ |accessdate=February 22, 2020 |work=Oregon Public Broadcasting |date=October 30, 2019 |language=en}}</ref> including a separate [[hate-crime]] assault the day before the fatal attack.<ref name=GreenReport>Aimee Green, [https://www.oregonlive.com/news/2020/02/jeremy-christian-guilty-on-all-counts-in-max-stabbings-trial.html Jeremy Christian guilty on all counts in MAX stabbings trial, including murder and hate crimes], ''The Oregonian''/OregonLive (February 21, 2020).</ref> In February 2020, following a [[jury trial]] ,Jeremy was found guilty on all counts.<ref name="GreenGuilty"/> Christian was sentenced on June 24, 2020, to two life terms.<ref name=TrueLife/>

The attack was widely condemned by the Portland community, politicians, and civil rights groups, some of which said it represented a rise in [[Hate speech in the United States|hate speech]],<ref name="ABC"/> [[Racism in the United States|racism]],<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wilson|first1=Jason|title=Portland's dark history of white supremacy|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2017/may/31/portland-white-supremacy-racism-train-stabbing-murder|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=May 31, 2017|accessdate=June 1, 2017|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170531233705/https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2017/may/31/portland-white-supremacy-racism-train-stabbing-murder|archivedate=May 31, 2017}}</ref> and [[Islamophobia in the United States|Islamophobic incidents in the United States]].<ref name="ABC"/><ref name="CNN"/><ref>{{cite news|last1=Sylvester|first1=Terray|title=Suspect in fatal Portland attack yells about 'free speech' at hearing|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-muslims-portland-idUSKBN18Q11F|work=[[Reuters]]|date=May 31, 2017|accessdate=May 31, 2017|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170531034147/http://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-muslims-portland-idUSKBN18Q11F|archivedate=May 31, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Chadha|first1=Janaki|title=In wake of attack, Portland coalition hopes to show most stand against racism, islamophobia|url=http://www.oregonlive.com/politics/index.ssf/2017/05/in_wake_of_attack_portland_coa.html|newspaper=[[The Oregonian]]|date=May 30, 2017|accessdate=June 1, 2017|quote=A coalition of Portland community groups plan to hold a large rally downtown Sunday to stand against what they see as growing far-right extremism, racism and islamophobia in the city.|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170531015021/http://www.oregonlive.com/politics/index.ssf/2017/05/in_wake_of_attack_portland_coa.html|archivedate=May 31, 2017}}</ref>

==Investigation==
Christian was booked into the [[Multnomah County, Oregon|Multnomah County]] jail.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/05/27/us/portland-train-attack-muslim-rant.html|title=Two Killed in Portland While Trying to Stop Anti-Muslim Rant, Police Say|last=Haag|first=Matthew|date=May 27, 2017|work=[[The New York Times]]|last2=Fortin|first2=Jacey|accessdate=May 28, 2017|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170528064635/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/05/27/us/portland-train-attack-muslim-rant.html|archivedate=May 28, 2017}}</ref> The FBI assisted Portland police in the investigation.<ref name="OPBToAppear">{{cite news|last1=Solomon|first1=Molly|title=Suspect In Fatal Portland Stabbing To Appear In Court Tuesday|url=http://www.opb.org/news/article/portland-oregon-train-stabbing-jeremy-christian-suspect-court/|publisher=[[Oregon Public Broadcasting]]|date=May 28, 2017|accessdate=May 28, 2017|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170528213830/http://www.opb.org/news/article/portland-oregon-train-stabbing-jeremy-christian-suspect-court/|archivedate=May 28, 2017}}</ref> Renn Cannon, of the Portland [[FBI field office]], said at the time, "It's too early to say whether last night's violence was an act of domestic terrorism or a [[Hate crime laws in the United States|federal hate crime]]."<ref name=":1">{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/05/26/us/portland-train-stabbing/index.html|title=FBI joins Portland stabbings investigation|first1=Tony|last1=Marco|first2=Jason|last2=Hanna|first3=Steve|last3=Almasy|date=May 27, 2017|publisher=[[CNN]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170528014330/http://www.cnn.com/2017/05/26/us/portland-train-stabbing/index.html|archive-date=2017-05-28|url-status=live|accessdate=May 28, 2017}}</ref>

==Theft from a victim==
In the early morning of June 2, 2017, police arrested 51-year-old George Elwood Tschaggeny, who lived in a small homeless camp near the platform where the train stopped after the attack. According to police and prosecutors, Tschaggeny removed the [[wedding band]] from Best's finger and stole Best's backpack and wallet. He was arrested after police viewed surveillance video showing the theft and obtained a community tip on the thief's identity. According to police, he was wearing Best's ring when he was arrested and had the backpack and contents of Best's wallet.<ref name="TschaggenyAP">{{cite news|url=http://medfordmailtribune.or.newsmemory.com/publink.php?shareid=12f444536|title=Man arrested in theft of victim's ring|first1=Steven|last1=DuBois|date=June 4, 2017|agency=Associated Press|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171225203301/http://medfordmailtribune.or.newsmemory.com/publink.php?shareid=12f444536|archivedate=December 25, 2017}}</ref><ref>Noelle Crombie, [http://www.oregonlive.com/portland/index.ssf/2017/06/man_accused_of_stealing_max_vi.html Man removed MAX victim's wedding ring as he lay dying, police say] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170607220328/http://www.oregonlive.com/portland/index.ssf/2017/06/man_accused_of_stealing_max_vi.html |date=2017-06-07 }}, ''The Oregonian''/OregonLive (June 3, 2017).</ref> Tschaggeny was a [[heroin addict]].<ref name=20Days>Aimee Green, [https://www.oregonlive.com/portland/2018/09/man_who_stole_max_stabbing_vic.html Man who stole MAX stabbing victim's wedding ring gets another 20 days in jail], ''The Oregonian''/OregonLive (September 10, 2018).</ref><ref name=ApologizesStole>[http://www.oregonlive.com/portland/index.ssf/2017/11/heroin_addict_who_stole_weddin.html Heroin addict who stole wedding ring from MAX stabbing victim apologizes] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171226020742/http://www.oregonlive.com/portland/index.ssf/2017/11/heroin_addict_who_stole_weddin.html |date=2017-12-26 }}, ''[[Oregon Live]]'', Aimee Green, November 14, 2017. ==Prosecution of Jeremy Joseph Christian==
===Indictment and pretrial proceedings===
Christian was charged with two counts of aggravated murder and one count of [[attempted murder]], as well as three misdemeanors: two counts of second-degree intimidation and one count of being a felon possessing a restricted weapon.<ref name="CNN"/><ref name="Wang"/> At his [[arraignment]] on May 30, 2017, Christian began yelling a variety of slogans as soon as he entered the courtroom, shouting: "Get out if you don't like free speech. You call it terrorism, I call it patriotism. You hear me? Die."<ref name="EllisMcLaughlinPark"/><ref name="BBCArraignment">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-40096993|title=Portland stabbing: Jeremy Joseph Christian appears in court|website=[[BBC News]]|publisher=[[BBC]]|location=London, England|date=May 30, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170531121741/http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-40096993 |archive-date=May 31, 2017}}</ref> He also yelled: "Leave this country if you hate our freedom—death to [[Anti-fascism|antifa]]!"<ref name="BBCArraignment"/>

On June 6 the Multnomah County [[grand jury]] [[Indictment|indicted]] Christian on 15 counts. Of these, 11 related to the May 26 stabbings (two counts of aggravated murder, one count of attempted [[Aggravation (law)|aggravated]]  murder, one count of first-degree assault, one count of second-degree assault, five counts of unlawful use of a weapon, three counts of second-degree intimidation and two counts of menacing) and four related to conduct the day before the stabbing, when Christian threw a plastic bottle at a black woman at the [[Interstate/Rose Quarter MAX Station]].<ref>Maxine Bernstein, [http://www.oregonlive.com/portland/index.ssf/2017/06/indictment_returned_against_ac.html Indictment returned against accused MAX attack killer Jeremy Christian] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170606224600/http://www.oregonlive.com/portland/index.ssf/2017/06/indictment_returned_against_ac.html |date=2017-06-06 }}, ''The Oregonian''/Oregon Live (June 7, 2017).</ref><ref>[http://media.oregonlive.com/portland_impact/other/REDACTEDjeremychristianindictmentjune617.tif%20(1).pdf Indictment: ''State of Oregon v. Jeremy Joseph Christian'', Circuit Court of the State of Oregon for Multnomah County] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180228192643/http://media.oregonlive.com/portland_impact/other/REDACTEDjeremychristianindictmentjune617.tif%20(1).pdf |date=2018-02-28 }} (June 6, 2017).</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Dubois|first1=Steven|title=Man indicted in double-fatal Portland, Oregon, train attack|url=https://abcnews.go.com/US/wireStory/man-indicted-double-fatal-portland-oregon-train-attack-47874936|accessdate=8 June 2017|publisher=ABC News|date=6 June 2017|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170607152043/https://abcnews.go.com/US/wireStory/man-indicted-double-fatal-portland-oregon-train-attack-47874936|archivedate=7 June 2017}}</ref><ref name="Sylvester"/>

===Politicians and officials===
[[List of mayors of Portland, Oregon|Portland Mayor]] [[Ted Wheeler]] called the victims of the attack "heroes" in a statement released on May 27. He continued saying, "Their actions were brave and selfless, and should serve as an example and inspiration to us all." [[Governor of Oregon]] [[Kate Brown]] echoed this sentiment, calling on all Oregonians to come together: "Let's not let hate and fear divide us. Instead let's take the example of the good Samaritans who sacrificed their lives for the safety of others and unite for a kinder, gentler Oregon."<ref name=":1" /> In the wake of the stabbings, the mayor called on the federal government to withhold a permit for a "Trump Free Speech Rally" at [[Terry Schrunk Plaza]], a [[federal enclave]] adjacent to city hall. ===Organizations===
The [[Anti-Defamation League]], in a statement by its CEO [[Jonathan Greenblatt]], offered its condolences to the victims, praised the heroism of bystanders who intervened, and said: "The deadly attack in Portland is not a rare or isolated event. Rather, this is the latest in a long string of violent incidents connected to right-wing extremists in the United States... This consistent threat requires consistent resources and we call on our leaders to condemn this brutal act of terror and craft policy to counter all forms of violent extremism, including white supremacy."<ref>[http://www.timesofisrael.com/adl-condemns-brutal-oregon-killings-by-anti-muslim-attacker/ ADL condemns 'brutal' Oregon killings by anti-Muslim attacker] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170528140518/http://www.timesofisrael.com/adl-condemns-brutal-oregon-killings-by-anti-muslim-attacker/ |date=2017-05-28 }}, ''Times of Israel''/Associated Press (May 28, 2017).</ref>

The [[Council on American–Islamic Relations]] asked President Trump to denounce "rising bigotry" and violence against Muslims. The organization's executive director said, "President Trump must speak out personally against the rising tide of Islamophobia and other forms of bigotry and racism in our nation that he has provoked through his numerous statements, policies and appointments that have negatively impacted minority communities."<ref name="CNN" /> Imtiaz Khan, who serves as president of the Islamic Center of Portland, said: "We are very sad. Ramadan started just a couple of hours ago. We are very sorry for the two men who tried to do the right thing.... Of course people from the Muslim community are concerned. And, unfortunately, the easy targets are women because of the headscarf."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wamsley|first1=Laurel|title=White Supremacist Charged With Killing 2 In Portland, Ore., Knife Attack|url=http://wprl.org/post/2-dead-1-injured-after-stabbing-portland-ore|publisher=[[WPRL]]|date=May 27, 2017|accessdate=May 28, 2017|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170527175524/http://wprl.org/post/2-dead-1-injured-after-stabbing-portland-ore|archivedate=May 27, 2017}}</ref> Khan also praised the two men who were slain, saying they "really sacrificed everything. They really stood up for the values of the Constitution."<ref name=":0" />

Harris Zafar with the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community in Portland said the actions of the men who stepped in to defend the Muslim women inspired him.<ref name="Dowling" /> The Asian Pacific American Network of Oregon expressed grief for the tragedy and hoped it would "draw Muslim communities together".<ref name="Oregonian1" />
