The '''Secretary of State for International Trade''', also referred to as the '''International Trade Secretary''',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/speeches/international-trade-secretary-wto-address |title=International Trade Secretary WTO address |publisher=GOV.UK |date=9 October 2019 |accessdate=29 October 2020 |quote=International Trade Secretary Liz Truss' first WTO address, at the International Chamber of Commerce Global Dialogue on Trade Reform}}</ref> is a senior [[Minister of the Crown]] within the [[Government of the United Kingdom]], and head of the [[Department for International Trade]]. The office forms part of the [[Cabinet of the United Kingdom|British Cabinet]]. Since it was created, the International Trade Secretary also holds the office of [[President of the Board of Trade]] in conjunction<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2017/oct/12/liam-fox-only-official-member-new-uk-board-of-trade |title=Liam Fox ridiculed for being only member of new UK board of trade |publisher=The Guardian |date=12 October 2017 |accessdate=3 November 2017 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/ministers/secretary-of-state-for-international-trade |title=Secretary of State for International Trade and President of the Board of Trade |publisher=GOV.UK |date= |accessdate=2016-07-18}}</ref>. 

The current Secretary of State for International Trade is [[Liz Truss]], [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|MP]] since her appointment by Prime Minister Boris Johnson, in February 2020.

==Responsibilities==
Corresponding to what is generally known as a [[Commerce minister|commerce minister]] in many other countries, the International Trade Secretary's remit includes:

* Forming new [[Free trade agreements of the United Kingdom]] 
* Managing the development of freeports across Britain
* Maintaining current trade agreements with other countries
* Supporting British businesses with [[international trade]]<ref>https://www.gov.uk/government/ministers/secretary-of-state-for-international-trade</ref>

==History==
The office was created by [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] [[Theresa May]] shortly after she took office on 13 July 2016, following the [[2016 United Kingdom European Union membership referendum|2016 EU referendum]]. The office's powers were taken from the trade functions of the [[Secretary of State for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy|Secretary of State for Business, Innovation and Skills]],<ref>[https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2016/992/made The Secretaries of State for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy, for International Trade and for Exiting the European Union and the Transfer of Functions (Education and Skills) Order 2016] Article 9(1).</ref> which was recreated as the Secretary of State for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy<ref>[https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2016/992/made The Secretaries of State for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy, for International Trade and for Exiting the European Union and the Transfer of Functions (Education and Skills) Order 2016] Article 3.</ref> as part of a wider government reorganisation.
