The '''2017 WTA Tour''' was the elite professional [[tennis]] circuit organised by the [[Women's Tennis Association]] (WTA) for the 2017 tennis season. The 2017 WTA Tour calendar comprises the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments (supervised by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF), the [[WTA Premier tournaments]] (Premier Mandatory, Premier 5, and regular Premier), the [[WTA International tournaments]], the [[Fed Cup]] (organized by the ITF), the year-end championships (the [[WTA Tour Championships]] and the [[WTA Elite Trophy]]). Also included in the 2017 calendar is the [[Hopman Cup]], which is organized by the ITF and does not distribute ranking points.<ref name=WTACalendar2016>{{cite web|work=wtatennis.com|publisher=WTA Tour, Inc.|title=2017 WTA calendar|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/SEWTATour-Archive/Archive/AboutTheTour/TourCalendar2017.pdf|access-date=2016-12-21|format=PDF}}</ref>

==Statistical information==
These tables present the number of [[Types of tennis match#Singles|singles]] (S), [[Types of tennis match#Doubles|doubles]] (D), and [[Mixed doubles tennis|mixed doubles]] (X) titles won by each player and each nation during the season, within all the tournament categories of the 2017 WTA Tour: the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments, the year-end championships (the [[WTA Tour Championships]] and the [[Commonwealth Bank Tournament of Champions|Tournament of Champions]]), the [[WTA Premier tournaments]] (Premier Mandatory, Premier 5, and regular Premier), and the [[WTA International tournaments]]. The players/nations are sorted by: 1) total number of titles (a doubles title won by two players representing the same nation counts as only one win for the nation); 2) cumulated importance of those titles (one Grand Slam win equalling two Premier Mandatory/Premier 5 wins, one year-end championships win equalling one-and-a-half Premier Mandatory/Premier 5 win, one Premier Mandatory/Premier 5 win equalling two Premier wins, one Premier win equalling two International wins); 3) a singles > doubles > mixed doubles hierarchy; 4) alphabetical order (by family names for players).

==Prize money leaders==
For the first time since the 2001 season, at the age of 37, Venus Williams topped the money list with $5,468,741. The top-37 players earned over $1,000,000.  Martina Hingis and Yung-Jan Chan each made over $1.4 million by playing exclusively in doubles tournaments.  It was the 4th consecutive year that a player earned over $1,000,000 in doubles events.

== Retirements ==
Following is a list of notable players (winners of a main tour title, and/or part of the [[WTA Rankings]] top 100 (singles) or (doubles) for at least one week) who announced their retirement from professional tennis, became inactive (after not playing for more than 52 weeks), or were permanently banned from playing, during the 2017 season:

*{{flagicon|ITA}} [[Alberta Brianti]] - The former world number 55 announced her retirement in 2017.
*{{flagicon|RUS}} [[Ekaterina Bychkova]] - The former world number 66 announced her retirement in 2017.{{citation needed|date=May 2017}}
*{{flagicon|JPN}} [[Kimiko Date]] – The former world number 4 announced she will retire for a second time after the 2017 Japan Open.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://mainichi.jp/english/articles/20170828/p2g/00m/0sp/086000c|newspaper=The Mainichi|title=Tennis: Date announces retirement for second time|date=28 August 2017|access-date=28 August 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170828183752/https://mainichi.jp/english/articles/20170828/p2g/00m/0sp/086000c|archive-date=28 August 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|SRB}} [[Vesna Dolonc]] – The former world number 84 announced her retirement from professional tennis in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/sport.296.html:649999-Dolonc-zavrsila-igracku-karijeru |publisher=Vercenje Novosti |title=Dolonc završila igračku karijeru |language=sr |trans-title=Dolonc Ends Her Playing Career |date=14 February 2017 |access-date=3 July 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|RUS}} [[Vera Dushevina]] – The former singles world number 34 and doubles world number 27 announced her retirement from professional tennis on 15 August 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rsport.ria.ru/tennis/20170815/1124299163.html|title=Теннисистка Вера Душевина завершила карьеру и сосредоточится на тренерской работе|trans-title=Tennis player Vera Dushevina retired from tennis and will concentrate on coaching|publisher=R-Sport|author=Alexey Filippov|language=ru|date=15 August 2017|access-date=15 August 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|SVK}} [[Daniela Hantuchová]] – The former world number 5 and four-time mixed doubles grand slam champion announced her retirement from professional tennis in July 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/tennis/wimbledon/wimbledon-2017-daniela-hantuchova-retires-professional-tennis-announcement-instagram-video-a7827346.html|title=Wimbledon 2017: Former World No 5 Daniela Hantuchova announces her retirement from professional tennis|newspaper=Independent|date=6 July 2017|access-date=6 July 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|SUI}} [[Martina Hingis]] – The former world number 1 in both singles and doubles, having turned professional in 1994. First retired in 2002 due to injuries, she had a comeback in 2006 after three years of inactivity and retired for the second time in 2008 after being banned for drug usage. She then announced a comeback in 2013, before retiring for a third and final time after the [[2017 WTA Finals]].
*{{flagicon|USA}} [[Liezel Huber]] – The former doubles world number 1 and seven-time doubles grand slam champion announced her retirement from professional tennis in April 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://newyorktennismagazine.com/article8535/seven-time-grand-slam-doubles-champion-liezel-huber-joins-nyjtl-cary-leeds-center |title=Seven-Time Grand Slam Doubles Champion Liezel Huber Joins NYJTL Cary Leeds Center |publisher=NY Tennis Magazine |date=27 April 2017 |access-date=2 July 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|USA}} [[Melanie Oudin]] – The former world number 31 and [[2011 US Open – Mixed Doubles|US Open mixed champion]] announced her retirement from professional tennis in August 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/oudin-announces-retirement-professional-tennis|title=Oudin announces retirement from professional tennis|publisher=WTATennis|date=18 August 2017|access-date=18 August 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|ISR}} [[Shahar Pe'er]] – The former world number 11 announced her retirement from professional tennis in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/shahar-peer-announces-retirement-tennis |title=Shahar Peer Announces Retirement From Tennis |publisher=wtatennis.com WTA Tour, Inc.  |date=28 February 2017 |access-date=3 July 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|RUS}} [[Nadia Petrova]] – The former world number 3 announced her retirement from professional tennis in January 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/petrova-announces-retirement-tennis |title=Former World No.3 Nadia Petrova officially announced her retirement from tennis after nearly two decades on tour. |publisher=wtatennis.com WTA Tour, Inc. |date=11 January 2017 |access-date=3 July 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|GBR}} [[Jocelyn Rae]] – The former doubles world number 67 announced her retirement from professional tennis in December 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wtatennis.com/news/britain%E2%80%99s-jocelyn-rae-announces-decision-retire-tennis|title=Britain’s Jocelyn Rae announces decision to retire from tennis|publisher=wtatennis.com WTA Tour, Inc. |date=14 December 2017|access-date=14 December 2017}}</ref>
*{{flagicon|AUS}} [[Jarmila Wolfe]] – The former world number 25 and [[2013 Australian Open – Mixed Doubles|Australian Open mixed champion]] announced her retirement from professional tennis in January 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/tennis/injury-forces-jarmila-wolfe-to-retire-at-the-age-of-29/news-story/fa05810f3628b17c378c8faf4f243f5d |title=Injury forces Jarmila Wolfe to retire at the age of 29|publisher=[[Herald Sun]] official website |date=11 January 2017 |access-date=11 January 2017}}</ref>
