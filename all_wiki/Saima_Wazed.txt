'''Saima Wazed Hossain''' (born 9 December 1972)<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.theindependentbd.com/home/printnews/61747|title=Sheikh Hasina's 70th birthday today |work=The Independent |location=Dhaka |access-date=20 April 2018}}</ref> is a Bangladeshi [[autism]] activist. She is the daughter of Bangladesh's Prime Minister, [[Sheikh Hasina]]. She is a member of the [[World Health Organization]]'s 25-member Expert Advisory Panel on mental health.<ref name="bdn9Aug2015" /> To her family, she is known simply as "Putul".

==Early life and education==
She was born to [[Sheikh Hasina]], the present Prime Minister of Bangladesh, and [[M. A. Wazed Miah]], a nuclear scientist.<ref name="bdn9Aug2015">{{cite news |title=Autism campaigner Putul listens to the troubles of people with disabilities, stresses on inclusive development|url=http://bdnews24.com/health/2015/08/09/autism-campaigner-putul-listens-to-the-troubles-of-people-with-disabilities-stresses-on-inclusive-development|work=bdnews24.com|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> Her brother is [[Sajeeb Wazed Joy]].<ref>{{cite news |title=India to give Bangladesh $1bn line of credit|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/India-to-give-Bangladesh-1bn-line-of-credit/articleshow/5434965.cms|work=The Times of India |accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=Bangladesh Country Study Guide Strategic Information and Developments.|date=2012|publisher=Intl Business Pubns USA|isbn=978-1438773896|pages=125}}</ref> She graduated from [[Barry University]].<ref name="bnew">{{cite news |title=Saima Wazed Putul gets Distinguished Alumni award from Barry University|url=http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2015/02/26/saima-wazed-putul-gets-distinguished-alumni-award-from-barry-university|work=bdnews24.com|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> She is a licensed school psychologist.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Islam Hasib|first1=Nurul|title=Disability campaigner Putul congratulates Bangladesh team, says they make nation proud|url=http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2015/09/02/disability-campaigner-putul-congratulates-bangladesh-team-says-they-make-nation-proud|work=bdnews24.com|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Sheikh Hasina with Obamas.jpg|300px|thumb|Saima Wazed, First Lady Michelle Obama, Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina and President Barack Obama in New York, September 2009]]
She organized the first South Asian conference on Autism in 2011 in Dhaka, Bangladesh.<ref>{{cite news |title=Saima Wazed Hossain says autism campaign will not lose steam|url=http://bdnews24.com/health/2015/08/17/saima-wazed-hossain-says-autism-campaign-will-not-lose-steam|work=bdnews24.com|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> She is the chairperson of National Advisory Committee on Autism and Neurodevelopmental disorders.<ref>{{cite news |title=Saima Wazed: Comprehensive approach required for disability issues|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/2015/aug/29/saima-wazed-comprehensive-approach-required-disability-issues|work=Dhaka Tribune|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> She campaigned for “Comprehensive and Coordinated Efforts for the Management of Autism Spectrum Disorders” resolution at the World Health Assembly which adopted the resolution, [[Autism Speaks]] praised her for spearheading "a truly global push for support for this resolution".<ref>{{cite web|title=Resolution brings formal commitment to action on developmental disabilities by more than 50 countries|url=https://www.autismspeaks.org/science/science-news/who-adopts-autism-resolution|website=autismspeaks.org|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> She is a member of [[World Health Organization|World Health Organization's]] 25-member Expert Advisory Panel on mental health.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hossain Shaikh|first1=Emran|title=Putul made WHO adviser|url=http://www.dhakatribune.com/politics/2014/jun/03/putul-made-who-adviser|work=Dhaka Tribune|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref>

In November, 2016, Wazed had been elected as chairperson of International Jury Board meeting of [[UNESCO]] for Digital Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/country/saima-wazed-elected-unescos-jury-board-chief-1312591|title=Saima Wazed elected chairperson of International Jury Board meeting of UNESCO|work=The Daily Star|accessdate=28 July 2017|date=10 November 2016}}</ref>

In April 2017, Wazed has been designated as [[WHO]] Champion for Autism” in South-East Asia.<ref>[http://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/nation/2017/04/02/saima-wazed-new-champion-autism/ Saima Wazed new WHO champion for autism - ''Dhakatribune'']</ref> In July, 2017 she became the Goodwill Ambassador of the [[World Health Organization]] (WHO) for autism in South-East Asia Region.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/country/saima-wazed-hossain-putul-made-world%20health-organisation-who-goodwill-ambassador-for-autism-1429174|title=Saima Wazed made WHO goodwill ambassador for autism|work=The Daily Star}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thefinancialexpress-bd.com/2017/07/07/76160/WHO-appoints-Saima-Wazed-as-Goodwill-Ambassador|title=WHO appoints Saima Wazed as Goodwill Ambassador|work=The [[Financial Express (Bangladesh)]]|accessdate=26 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.india.com/news/agencies/hossain-made-who-goodwill-ambassador-for-autism-in-south-asia-2310008/|title=Hossain made WHO goodwill ambassador for autism in South Asia|date=10 July 2017|accessdate=30 July 2017|publisher=[[India Today]]}}</ref>

==Award==
In 2016, Wazed has conferred [[World Health Organization|World Health Organization's]] South-East Asia Region Award for Excellence in Public Health.<ref>{{cite news |title=Saima Wazed wins WHO award|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/saima-wazed-wins-who-award-40748|work=The Daily Star|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> In 2017, she has been awarded the ''International Champion Award'' for her outstanding contribution to the field of autism. She received a distinguished alumni award from [[Barry University]] for her activism.<ref name="bnew" /><ref>[http://www.thedailystar.net/country/prime-minister-sheikh-hasina-daughter-saima-wazed-international-champion-award-fighting-autism-new-york-un-1438948 Saima Wazed awarded for fighting autism - The Daily Star]</ref><ref>[http://m.bdnews24.com/en/detail/bangladesh/1369951 Saima received International Champion award for work on autism in South-East-Asia - bdnews24.com]</ref>

==Personal life==
Saima is married to Khandakar Masrur Hossain Mitu, son of [[Khandaker Mosharraf Hossain]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Son's & Daughters of Bangladeshi Political Leaders|url=http://parisvisionnews.com/articles/5732-sons-a-daughters-of-bangladeshi-political-leaders.html|website=parisvisionnews.com|accessdate=11 February 2016}}</ref> The couple have 4 children.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.unesco.org/sites/default/files/saima_wazed_hossain_bio_en.pdf|title=Saima Wazed Hossain bio|publisher=UNESCO|accessdate=26 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://m.bdnews24.com/en/detail/bangladesh/205607|title=Hasina's daughter Putul gives birth to daughter|work=Bdnews24.com}}</ref>
