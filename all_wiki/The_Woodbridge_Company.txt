'''The Woodbridge Company Limited''' is a [[Canada|Canadian]] private [[holding company]] based in [[Toronto]], Ontario. It is the primary investment vehicle for members of the family of the late [[Roy Thomson, 1st Baron Thomson of Fleet|Roy Thomson]], the first [[Baron Thomson of Fleet]].<ref name=bbg/> [[David Binet]] has been the president and chief executive officer of the company since 2012.<ref name="David Binet">{{cite web |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/globe-investor/woodbridge-selects-long-time-executive-david-binet-as-president/article5817126/ |title=Woodbridge selects long-time executive David Binet as president |work=The Globe and Mail |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref>

==Main holdings==
Woodbridge is the principal and controlling shareholder (62.35%) of [[Thomson Reuters]], a multinational [[media conglomerate]].<ref>[http://fileshare.gcs.thomsonreuters.com/digital/annual-report-2015/downloads/annual-report-2015-thomson-reuters.pdf 2015 Annual Report Regulatory Filing] 156p</ref> Thomson Reuters was formed in 2008, when the [[Thomson Corporation]] acquired [[Reuters]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Haycock |first=Gavin |author2=MacMillan, Robert |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/industryNews/idUSN1438977620080418 |title=Thomson Reuters debuts amid global market jitters |accessdate=2020-06-03 | date=2008-04-17}}</ref> 

In late 2010, Woodbridge sold its 40% interest in [[CTVglobemedia]], a Canadian media company with ownership of the [[CTV Television Network]], to [[BCE Inc]]. Woodbridge held the largest (85%) interest in ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'' newspaper until August 2015, when it obtained 100% of The Globe and Mail Inc. by acquiring the remaining 15% owned by BCE.<ref>{{cite web|author=Canada |url=http://www.newswire.ca/en/releases/archive/September2010/10/c8647.html|title=Woodbridge Acquires Direct Ownership of The Globe and Mail|publisher=CNW|date=2010-09-10|accessdate=2011-01-05}}</ref>

==Ownership==
Woodbridge is the primary investment vehicle for members of the family of the late [[Roy Thomson, 1st Baron Thomson of Fleet|Roy Thomson]], the first [[Baron Thomson of Fleet]].<ref name=bbg>{{cite web |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=1173195 |title=The Woodbridge Company Limited |work=Bloomberg |accessdate=2018-03-08}}</ref> [[David Thomson, 3rd Baron Thomson of Fleet|David Thomson]] and his brother, [[Peter Thomson (race car driver)|Peter Thomson]], became chairmen of Woodbridge in 2006 upon the death of their father, [[Kenneth Thomson, 2nd Baron Thomson of Fleet|Kenneth Thomson]].

According to ''[[Canadian Business]]'' magazine, Sherry Brydson (child of Irma Thomson, one of Roy Thomson's two daughters) holds the largest stake in the family company.<ref name=Brydson>{{cite web|title=Canada’s Richest People 2015: Meet Sherry Brydson, Canada’s Richest Woman|url=http://www.canadianbusiness.com/lists-and-rankings/richest-people/sherry-brydson-richest-woman-in-canada-thomson-family/|website=Canadian Business|accessdate=11 March 2015}}</ref> It is estimated that she holds 23.47% of the company's shares.<ref name=Brydson/><ref name="Financial Post">{{cite web |url=http://business.financialpost.com/news/james-pattison-takes-crown-as-canadas-richest-as-new-information-reveals-david-thomsons-fortune-smaller-than-thought |title=James Pattison takes crown as Canada’s richest as new information reveals David Thomson’s fortune smaller than thought |work=Financial Post |accessdate=2018-03-31}}</ref>
