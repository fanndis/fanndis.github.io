{{short description|Multi-volume biographical collection}}
The '''''Dictionary of Irish Biography''''' ('''DIB''') is a [[biographical dictionary]] of notable [[Irish people]] and people not born in the country who had notable careers in Ireland, both [[Northern Ireland|north]] and [[Republic of Ireland|south]].<ref name="DIBPrint">[http://www.cambridge.org/gb/knowledge/isbn/item2427450/?site_locale=en_GB Dictionary of Irish Biography 9 Volume Set], [[Cambridge University Press]]</ref> It was published as a nine-volume set in 2009 by [[Cambridge University Press]] in collaboration with the [[Royal Irish Academy]] (RIA), and contains about 9,000 entries.<ref name="DIBPrint"/> An online version is available on a subscription basis, and new entries are added to this periodically.<ref>[http://dib.cambridge.org/home.do Dictionary of Irish Biography home page]</ref> In October 2020 the RIA announced that the online DIB will become [[open access]] in spring 2021, with funding from the [[Higher Education Authority]], [[Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade]], and [[Dublin City Council]] Libraries.<ref>{{cite press release |title=The DIB will be free-to-access in spring 2021 |url=https://www.ria.ie/dib-will-be-free-access-spring-2021 |website=Royal Irish Academy |accessdate=23 October 2020 |language=en |date=12 October 2020}}</ref>
