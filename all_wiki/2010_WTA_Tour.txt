The [[Women's Tennis Association]] (WTA) Tour is the elite professional [[tennis]] circuit organized by the WTA. The '''2010 WTA Tour''' calendar comprises the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments (supervised by the [[International Tennis Federation]] (ITF)), the [[WTA Premier tournaments]] (Premier Mandatory, Premier 5 and regular Premier), the [[WTA International tournaments]], the [[Fed Cup]] (organized by the ITF), and the year-end championships (the [[Commonwealth Bank Tournament of Champions]] and the [[WTA Tour Championships]]). Also included in the 2010 calendar is the [[Hopman Cup]], which does not distribute ranking points and is organized by the ITF.

==Notable stories==

===Serena Williams' season===
{{main|2010 Serena Williams tennis season}}
Serena Williams began the season ranked World No. 1, having enjoyed an impressive [[2009 WTA Tour|2009 season]] in which she won the Australian Open and Wimbledon (she would successfully defend both titles this year) and reclaimed the World No. 1 ranking on two occasions. Williams began her season by successfully defending her [[2010 Australian Open &ndash; Women's Singles|Australian Open]] title, defeating [[Justine Henin]] in a three-set final.<ref name="Serena-Australian">https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2010/jan/30/serena-williams-henin-australian-open-final</ref>{{deprecated inline|certain=yes|date=October 2019}} She also successfully defended her [[2010 Wimbledon Championships &ndash; Women's Singles|Wimbledon]] title, defeating surprise finalist [[Vera Zvonareva]] in the final, 6&ndash;3, 6&ndash;2.<ref>https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/wimbledon/7870125/Wimbledon-2010-Serena-Williams-defeats-Vera-Zvonareva-to-win-womens-final.html</ref>

However, shortly after winning Wimbledon, Serena Williams would injure her foot at a German restaurant where she was celebrating her victory, but it didn't deter her from playing in an exhibition match against [[Kim Clijsters]] in front of a world-record tennis crowd that same week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/2010-07-17-serena-williams-injury_N.htm|title=Serena Williams cuts foot on broken glass, will need surgery - USATODAY.com|website=usatoday30.usatoday.com}}</ref> The foot injury ended up being very serious enough to necessitate surgery,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2010/sep/03/serena-williams-silence-tendon-injury|title=Serena Williams reveals details of her serious foot injury|first=Kevin|last=Mitchell|date=2 September 2010|website=the Guardian}}</ref> and as a result she missed the rest of the season and would not return to top-level tennis until June 2011. She was forced to miss her first [[2010 US Open &ndash; Women's Singles|US Open]] since 2003,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/sports/tennis/usopen10/news/story?id=5480178|title=Serena withdraws from U.S. Open, citing foot|date=20 August 2010|website=ESPN.com}}</ref> and also had to withdraw from the [[2010 WTA Tour Championships|year-end championships]] having qualified by virtue of winning Wimbledon. The injury worsened late in the year; despite accepting a wildcard into [[2010 Generali Ladies Linz|Linz]], she had to withdraw from that tournament, with her wildcard entry later allocated to former World No. 1 [[Ana Ivanovic]].<ref name="Serena-Linz">{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/9078310.stm|title=Serena set to miss rest of season|date=11 October 2010|via=news.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> Despite progress in her recovery from foot surgery, she lost the World No. 1 ranking to Danish youngster [[Caroline Wozniacki]]. Williams then announced her withdrawal from the [[2011 Australian Open]] in November.<ref>https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2011/jun/13/serena-williams-blood-clot-wimbledon</ref>

===Justine Henin's comeback===
Former World No. 1 [[Justine Henin]] announced her comeback to the WTA Tour in September last year, in the aftermath of [[Kim Clijsters]]' victory at the [[2009 US Open &ndash; Women's Singles|2009 US Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/tennis/8267941.stm|title=Henin plans 2010 tennis comeback|date=22 September 2009|via=news.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> She had previously not played at the highest level since suffering a third round defeat to [[Dinara Safina]] (who went on to make the final of the [[2008 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|French Open that year]]) at the [[2008 Qatar Telecom German Open]]. Henin's comeback began impressively, reaching the final of the [[2010 Brisbane International]] in which she lost to her [[Clijsters&ndash;Henin rivalry|compatriot and rival]] Kim Clijsters in a final set tiebreak.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2010/jan/10/justine-henin-injured-brisbane-comeback|title=Justine Henin's comeback stalled by injury sustained in epic final|first=Nigel|last=Henderson|date=10 January 2010|website=the Guardian}}</ref> She had defeated [[Nadia Petrova]] and [[Ana Ivanovic]] en route. She then reached the final of the Australian Open, in which she was defeated by [[Serena Williams]] in three sets,<ref name="Serena-Australian"/> having defeated fifth seed [[Elena Dementieva]] in the second round, 27th seed [[Alisa Kleybanova]] in the third, qualifier [[Yanina Wickmayer]] in the fourth, 19th seed [[Nadia Petrova]] in the quarter-finals and [[Zheng Jie]] in the semi-finals en route.

She then participated at her first [[2010 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|French Open]] since 2007, where she was defeated in the fourth round by eventual finalist [[Samantha Stosur]], ending a 24-match winning streak at the tournament dating back to 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/frenchopen/7790501/French-Open-2010-Justine-Henin-crashes-out-as-Sam-Stosur-reaches-last-eight-in-Paris.html|title=French Open 2010: Justine Henin crashes out as Sam Stosur reaches last eight in Paris|first=Telegraph|last=staff|date=31 May 2010|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}}</ref> Prior to the defeat against Stosur, Henin had played her first three-set match at the tournament, also since 2005, when she defeated [[Maria Sharapova]] in the third round.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/frenchopen/7785938/French-Open-2010-Justine-Henin-defeats-Maria-Sharapova.html|title=French Open 2010: Justine Henin defeats Maria Sharapova|date=30 May 2010|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}}</ref> Later, at [[2010 Wimbledon Championships &ndash; Women's Singles|Wimbledon]], Henin again reached the fourth round, but suffered a three-set loss to rival Clijsters.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2010/jun/28/wimbledon-2010-kim-clijsters-justine-henin|title=Wimbledon 2010: Kim Clijsters fights back to overpower Justine Henin|first=Robert|last=Kitson|date=28 June 2010|website=the Guardian}}</ref> During the match, she suffered an elbow injury which would later rule her out of the [[2010 US Open &ndash; Women's Singles|US Open]], that same injury would lead to her second (and final) retirement in January 2011.

===Ana Ivanovic's season===
The first half of [[Ana Ivanovic]]'s 2010 season appeared to follow on from her poor [[2009 WTA Tour|2009 season]], as her confidence and game continued to disintegrate since she won the [[2008 French Open &ndash; Women's Singles|2008 French Open]]. She started the season ranked World No. 22 and spent almost the entire season ranked outside the Top 20. She was able to reach the semi-finals at the [[2010 Brisbane International|Brisbane International]] and the [[2010 Internazionali BNL d'Italia|Rome Masters]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2010-05-07/ivanovic-serves-up-big-improvement/426496|title=Ivanovic serves up big improvement|date=7 May 2010|website=abc.net.au}}</ref> but those would be her best results in the first half of the season, as she dropped out of the WTA's Top 50 for the first time since 2005 with a second round loss to [[Anastasija Sevastova]] at [[2010 BNP Paribas Open &ndash; Women's Singles|Indian Wells]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.womenstennisblog.com/2010/03/14/kuznetsova-upset-ivanovic-out-of-indian-wells-and-top-50/|title=Kuznetsova upset, Ivanovic out of Indian Wells and Top 50 - Women's Tennis Blog|date=14 March 2010|website=womenstennisblog.com}}</ref> On court results did not improve throughout the year, and Ivanovic dropped to a low of World No. 65 by July.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2010/09/high-strung/308175/|title=High Strung|first=Tom|last=Perrotta|website=theatlantic.com}}</ref> Adding to a growing season of disappointment, Ivanovic also suffered the ignominy of winning the [[wooden spoon (award)|wooden spoon]] at [[2010 Wimbledon Championships &ndash; Women's Singles|Wimbledon]], having finished at the end of the tournament's longest losing chain after losing in the first round.<ref>[http://www.people.co.uk/sport/other/tennis/2010/07/04/passing-shots-ana-picks-up-wooden-spoon-102039-22380115/ Passing Shots: Ana picks up wooden spoon - Tennis - Other Sport - Sport - People.co.uk]</ref>

However, Ivanovic would begin to turn her season around at the [[2010 Western & Southern Financial Group Women's Open &ndash; Singles|2010 Western & Southern Financial Group Women's Open]], entering the tournament having lost 17 of her last 29 matches dating back to August 2009 and having dropped to World No. 62. A first round upset win over recent [[2010 Bank of the West Classic &ndash; Singles|Stanford]] champion [[Victoria Azarenka]]<ref name="Cincinnati2010">{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2010-08-10/ivanovic-turns-back-clock-in-cincinnati/939386 |title=Ivanovic turns back clock in Cincinnati |publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation |date=10 August 2010}}</ref> sparked a run to the semi-finals, where she was forced to retire against [[Kim Clijsters]] due to a foot injury; nevertheless, she re-entered the World's Top 40 following her run at Cincinnati.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.womenstennisblog.com/2010/08/14/ivanovics-cincinnati-fairytale-hampered-by-injury-clijsters-into-finals/|title=Ivanovic's Cincinnati fairytale hampered by injury, Clijsters into finals - Women's Tennis Blog|date=14 August 2010|website=womenstennisblog.com}}</ref> With no rankings points to defend for the remainder of the year, Ivanovic reached the fourth round of the [[2010 US Open &ndash; Women's Singles|US Open]], the first time she had gotten that far at a Grand Slam tournament since [[2009 Wimbledon Championships &ndash; Women's Singles|Wimbledon in 2009]]. She defeated [[Ekaterina Makarova]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/2010-08-31-us-open-ana-ivanovic_N.htm|title=Ivanovic says she is finding fun, building confidence after long fall - USATODAY.com|website=usatoday30.usatoday.com}}</ref> [[Zheng Jie]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/tennis/usopen/7975902/US-Open-2010-Ana-Ivanovic-storms-past-Zheng-Jie-to-reach-third-round.html|title=US Open 2010: Ana Ivanovic storms past Zheng Jie to reach third round|first=Telegraph staff and|last=agencies|date=1 September 2010|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}}</ref> and [[Virginie Razzano]] before being crushed by defending and eventual champion [[Kim Clijsters]] in the fourth round.

Ivanovic continued to maintain her recent good form after the US Open; she was able to reach the quarter-finals in [[2010 China Open (tennis)|Beijing]] where she was defeated by [[Caroline Wozniacki]], who eventually replaced [[Serena Williams]] as the new [[List of WTA number 1 ranked players|World No. 1]] by winning the tournament.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wwos.ninemsn.com.au/glanceview/121611/caroline-wozniacki-new-world-no1.glance|title=Caroline Wozniacki - new world No1|website=ninemsn.com.au|access-date=2013-02-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101012230954/http://wwos.ninemsn.com.au/glanceview/121611/caroline-wozniacki-new-world-no1.glance|archive-date=2010-10-12|url-status=dead}}</ref> She then accepted a late wildcard entry into [[2010 Generali Ladies Linz|Linz]] after Williams withdrew due to her ongoing foot surgery,<ref name="Serena-Linz"/> where she won her first title in two years after defeating [[Patty Schnyder]], 6&ndash;2, 6&ndash;1, in the year's shortest final; the title returned Ivanovic to the world's Top 30,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.bettor.com/Generali-Ladies-Linz-2010-Ana-Ivanovic-beats-Patty-Schnyder-to-end-her-title-drought-a34532|title=Generali Ladies Linz 2010: Ana Ivanovic beats Patty Schnyder to end her title drought|website=bettor.com|access-date=2013-02-05|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130407183946/http://blogs.bettor.com/Generali-Ladies-Linz-2010-Ana-Ivanovic-beats-Patty-Schnyder-to-end-her-title-drought-a34532|archive-date=2013-04-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> and qualified her for the [[2010 Commonwealth Bank Tournament of Champions]] in Bali. In her final tournament of the year, she defeated [[Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova]] and [[Kimiko Date-Krumm]] en route to reaching her second final in three weeks, where she defeated [[Alisa Kleybanova]] to win her second title of the year and return to the world's Top 20 for the first time in more than one year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/2010-10-17-weekly-net-post_N.htm|title=Weekly net post: Welcome back Ana Ivanovic. We missed you - USATODAY.com|website=usatoday30.usatoday.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2010/11/08/resurgent-ivanovic-wins-bali-crown.html|title=Resurgent Ivanovic wins Bali crown|first=The Jakarta|last=Post|website=thejakartapost.com}}</ref> Ivanovic ended her season by winning 21 of her last 27 matches, having lost 17 of her last 29 beforehand.

==Statistical information==
These tables present the number of [[Types of tennis match#Singles|singles]] (S), [[Types of tennis match#Doubles|doubles]] (D), and [[Types of tennis match#Mixed doubles|mixed doubles]] (X) titles won by each player and each nation during the season, within all the tournament categories of the 2010 Sony Ericsson WTA Tour: the [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] tournaments, the Year-end championships, the [[WTA Premier tournaments]] and the [[WTA International tournaments]]. The players/nations are sorted by:

1) total number of titles (a doubles title won by two players representing the same nation counts as only one win for the nation);<br>
2) highest amount of highest category tournaments (for example, having a single Grand Slam gives preference over any kind of combination without a Grand Slam title); <br>
3) a singles > doubles > mixed doubles hierarchy; <br>
4) alphabetical order (by family names for players).

''To avoid confusion and double counting, these tables should be updated only after an event is completed.''
