The '''VH1 Trailblazer Honors''', also known as the '''Logo Trailblazer Honors''', is the only televised LGBT awards ceremony in the United States. It is an annual awards event founded in 2014 to recognize persons and entities who have made significant contributions towards minority empowerment and civil activism. It has been described as a combined "queer State of the Union, Hall of Fame, and Oscars." The event is aired on [[Logo TV]] and [[VH1]].

== About ==
The VH1 Trailblazer Honors, also known as Logo Trailblazer Honors, is an annually televised awards event, founded in 2014, that recognizes persons and entities who have made significant contributions towards minority empowerment and civil activism.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://shortyawards.com/1st-socialgood/logo-trailblazer-honors|title=Logo Trailblazer Honors|website=The Shorty Awards|access-date=2018-06-22}}</ref><ref name=":11">{{Cite web|url=https://wtop.com/national/2018/06/vh1s-trailblazer-honors-to-highlight-tv-pioneer-ryan-murphy/|title=VH1’s Trailblazer Honors to highlight TV pioneer Ryan Murphy|date=2018-06-07|website=WTOP|access-date=2018-06-22}}</ref> The event is the only LGBT awards ceremony televised in the [[United States]].<ref name=":10" /> It has been described as a combined "queer State of the Union, Hall of Fame, and Oscars."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2016/06/trailblazer-honors-logo-review-orlando-harvey-fierstein/488726/|title=Logo's 'Trailblazer Honors' Highlights the Urgency of LGBT Pride After Orlando|last=Kornhaber|first=Spencer|date=2016-06-26|work=The Atlantic|access-date=2018-06-22|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2018, the event was aired on [[Logo TV]] and [[VH1]].<ref name=":11" />

In the 2014 inaugural event, [[Bill Clinton]] recognized [[Edith Windsor]] and [[Roberta A. Kaplan]] for their role in the [[Defense of Marriage Act]]. [[Jason Collins]] was presented the honor by [[Lance Bass]]. [[Demi Lovato]] honored cast members of [[Orange Is the New Black]], including [[Danielle Brooks]], [[Laverne Cox]], [[Lea DeLaria]], [[Taryn Manning]], and [[Samira Wiley]]. Singer [[Michael Stipe]] honored [[John Abdallah Wambere]]. Young community leaders were introduced by [[Daniel Radcliffe]]. The event included musical performances by [[Sia (musician)|Sia]], [[New York City Gay Men's Chorus]], [[A Great Big World]], and the band Exousia. The event was attended by celebrities including [[Joe Manganiello]], [[Ed Sheeran]], [[Jared Leto]], [[Macklemore & Ryan Lewis]], [[Kylie Minogue]], [[Pete Wentz]], [[Ariana Grande]], [[Iggy Azalea|Iggy Azaela]], [[Tegan and Sara]], and [[Laura Jane Grace]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.glaad.org/blog/bill-clinton-honor-edie-windsor-logo-lgbt-trailblazer|title=Bill Clinton to honor Edie Windsor on Logo as LGBT trailblazer|last=Bolles|first=Alexandra|date=2014-06-23|work=GLAAD|access-date=2018-06-23}}</ref>

In 2015, [[Miley Cyrus]] gave the opening address through a recorded message voicing support for marriage equality and the queer community. [[Barack Obama]] gave an address through a video message on the progress of LGBT people.<ref name=":7" /> [[Raven-Symoné]] and [[Martin Luther King III]] were speakers at the event. Musical performances included [[Adam Lambert]] and the [[Bleachers (band)|Bleachers]]. Other speakers were [[Laura Jane Grace]], [[Samira Wiley]], [[Tituss Burgess]], [[Tyler Posey]], [[Kelly Osbourne]], [[Betty DeGeneres]], [[Violet Chachki]], and [[Frankie Grande]].<ref name=":7">{{Cite news|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/06/27/logo-trailblazer-honors-l_n_7674834.html|title=Miley Cyrus Opens Logo's 2015 'Trailblazer Honors,' Obama Addresses Crowd|last=Nichols|first=JamesMichael|date=2015-06-27|work=Huffington Post|access-date=2018-06-22}}</ref><ref name=":6">{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/news/raven-symone-martin-luther-king-iii-logo-trailblazer-honors-1201528149/|title=Raven Symone, Martin Luther King III Among Speakers at Logo’s Trailblazer Honors|last=Friedlander|first=Whitney|date=2015-06-25|work=Variety|access-date=2018-06-23|language=en-US}}</ref> [[Jane Fonda]], [[Lily Tomlin]], [[Judith Light]], and [[Ian McKellen]] were also on stage.<ref name=":9" /> This was the first year that the honor "Social Trailblazer" was added. Logo fans voted between four nominees that used social media to advocate for the LGBT community. Nominees included [[Connor Franta]], [[Joey Graceffa]], Jackson Bird, and [[Gaby Dunn]]<ref name=":9">{{Cite news|url=https://www.out.com/popnography/2015/6/11/adam-lambert-perform-logos-trailblazer-honors|title=Adam Lambert to Perform at Logo’s Trailblazer Honors|last=Steinbach|first=Jesse|date=2015-06-11|work=OUT|access-date=2018-06-23}}</ref> with Franta eventually being named the winner. On behalf of his deceased partner, [[Bayard Rustin]], [[Walter Naegle]] accepted the honor.<ref name=":8">{{Cite news|url=http://www.newnownext.com/equality-pioneers-speak-at-logos-trailblazer-honors/06/2015/|title=Equality Pioneers Speak At Logo's Trailblazer Honors|last=Tharrett|first=Matthew|date=2015-06-29|work=LOGO News|access-date=2018-06-23}}</ref> [[Judy Shepard|Judy]] and [[Dennis Shepard]] were recognized as "Trailblazing Parents" for co-founding the [[Matthew Shepard Foundation]].<ref name=":10">{{Cite news|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/06/23/matthew-shepard-foundation-trailblazing_n_7646442.html|title=Judy And Dennis Shepard To Be Honored As 'Trailblazing Parents' At Logo 'Trailblazer Honors'|last=Nichols|first=JamesMichael|date=2015-06-23|work=Huffington Post|access-date=2018-06-23|language=en-US}}</ref>

At the 2017 event, musicians, [[Hayley Kiyoko]], [[Alex Newell]], and [[Wrabel]] honored [[Cyndi Lauper]] with a performance of "[[True Colors (Cyndi Lauper song)|True Colors]]."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7841946/cyndi-lauper-true-colors-tribute-performance-video-logo-trailblazer-honors-2017|title=Logo's Trailblazer Honors: Hayley Kiyoko, Alex Newell & Wrabel Tribute Cyndi Lauper With 'True Colors' Performance|last=Crowley|first=Patrick|date=2017-06-23|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-06-23}}</ref>

The awards given in 2016 took time to remember the 49 victims of the [[Orlando nightclub shooting]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/feature/nbc-out/logo-honors-orlando-victims-lgbtq-icons-trailblazer-ceremony-n598511|title=Logo Honors Orlando Victims, LGBTQ Icons at Trailblazer Ceremony|last=Latimer|first=Brian|date=24 June 2016|work=NBC News|access-date=2018-06-22|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2018, the fifth annual Trailblazer Honors event took place at [[Cathedral of St. John the Divine]], which is one of the first religious institutions in [[New York City]] supporting LGBTQ causes.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://people.com/tv/ryan-murphy-vh1-trailblazer-honoree-exclusive/|title=Lea Michele, Sterling K. Brown and More Celebrate 'Visionary' Ryan Murphy's VH1 Trailblazer Honor|last=Corinthios|first=Aurelie|date=2018-06-19|work=People|access-date=2018-06-22|language=en}}</ref> The event was attended by activists and celebrities including [[Janet Mock]], [[Bebe Rexha]], the cast of [[Pose (TV series)|''Pose'']], and finalist from [[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 10)|''RuPaul's Drag Race'']].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8462327/vh1-2018-trailblazer-honors-moments|title=5 Standout Moments From VH1's 2018 Trailblazer Honors|last=Youtt|first=Henry|date=2018-06-22|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-06-22}}</ref>
