'''Katherine Langford''' (born 29 April 1996)<ref name="biography.com">{{Cite web |url=https://www.biography.com/people/katherine-langford |title=Katherine Langford: Actress (1996–) |website=[[Biography.com]] |publisher=[[A&E Networks]] |access-date=28 May 2018 |archivedate=7 August 2019 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190807230302/https://www.biography.com/actor/katherine-langford |url-status=live}}</ref> is an Australian actress.<!--Langford is NOT notable as a singer or musician, as she does not meet the requirements as they are listed in [[WP:MUSICBIO]]--> She starred as [[Hannah Baker]] in the first two seasons of the [[Netflix]] television series ''[[13 Reasons Why]]'' from 2017 to 2018, for which she received a nomination for the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Drama]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=King |first1=Brittany |title=13 Reasons Why Star Katherine Langford Talks Teen Depression: 'It's Not a Beautiful Tragedy, It's Hell' |url=http://people.com/tv/13-reasons-why-katherine-langford-teen-depressionl/ |accessdate=4 April 2017 |work=People |date=31 March 2017}}</ref> She also had supporting roles in the films ''[[Love, Simon]]'' (2018) and ''[[Knives Out]]'' (2019).

==Early life==
Langford was born in [[Perth]], Western Australia,<ref name="biography.com" /> and raised in [[Applecross, Western Australia|Applecross]], a riverside suburb of Perth.<ref name="perthmod">{{Cite web |url=https://thewest.com.au/entertainment/celebrity-gossip/netflix-star-katherine-langford-reminisces-on-happy-days-at-perth-mod-ng-b88481496z |title=Netflix star Katherine Langford reminisces on happy days at Perth Mod |last=McRae |first=Rose |date=19 May 2017 |website=The West Australian |access-date=23 May 2017}}</ref> She is the eldest daughter of Elizabeth Langford (née Green), a pediatrician, and Stephen Langford, a flying doctor and director of medical services at the [[Royal Flying Doctor Service of Australia|Royal Flying Doctor Service]] Western Operations.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://parentingisforever.com.au/the-author.html |title=The Author (Dr. Elizabeth Green) |website=Parenting is Forever- a paediatrician's tips for parents, teachers and carers |access-date=1 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://uwap.uwa.edu.au/collections/stephen-langford |title=Dr. Stephen Langford |website=UWA Publishing |access-date=12 March 2018}}</ref><ref name="perthmod"/><ref name=":4"/> Her younger sister, [[Josephine Langford]], is also an actress.

Langford began voice lessons with Heidi Lake in 2005, and received [[Classical music|classical]], [[jazz]], and [[Contemporary classical music|contemporary]] vocal training. She was offered a place at [[Perth Modern School]]<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://thewest.com.au/news/wa/reasons-for-saving-perth-mod-ng-b88485304z |title=Katherine Langford joins fight to save Perth Modern |date=26 May 2017 |work=The West Australian |access-date=16 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://au.news.yahoo.com/wa/a/35664464/13-reasons-why-katherine-langford-fights-to-save-perth-modern-school/#page1 |title=13 Reasons Why star joins fight to save Perth school |access-date=16 June 2017}}</ref> for her senior high years, where she studied music and drama, and was sports captain and a nationally ranked swimmer.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://thefifthsense.i-d.co/en_us/article/katherine-langford/ |title=who's that girl: meet katherine langford |last=Stone |first=Russell |date=16 April 2017 |website=i-D Magazine |access-date=23 April 2017}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite news |url=https://www.pressreader.com/australia/the-sunday-times/20170115/282892320350320 |title=Katherine Langford – A young star seriously on the rise |last=Christmass |first=Pip |date=15 January 2017 |work=The Sunday Times |access-date=20 April 2017}}</ref>

Initially during her time at high school, Langford was interested in medicine and politics, in addition to musical theatre. Langford appeared in the school's production of ''Hotel Sorrento'' in 2013<ref name=":0">{{cite news |url=http://www.rgmwa.com.au/uploads/1b60624e0ac26ed197c3554e83c7ad50d6269aa2.pdf |title=Katherine Langford |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170412144101/http://www.rgmwa.com.au/uploads/1b60624e0ac26ed197c3554e83c7ad50d6269aa2.pdf |archive-date=12 April 2017 |publisher=RGM Artists Group |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.perthmodern.wa.edu.au/News/Events/tabid/144/articleType/ArticleView/articleId/53/HOTEL-SORRENTO-July-2-and-July-3.aspx |title=UPCOMING EVENTS |date=20 June 2013 |website=Perth Modern School |access-date=20 April 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170422033149/http://www.perthmodern.wa.edu.au/News/Events/tabid/144/articleType/ArticleView/articleId/53/HOTEL-SORRENTO-July-2-and-July-3.aspx |archive-date=22 April 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and graduated that same year.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.perthmodernschool.wa.edu.au/Portals/0/Documents/PerthModernSchool%20news%20Apr2013-8s%5B1%5D.pdf |title=Perth Modern News April 2013 |date=April 2013 |website=Perth Modern School |access-date=1 April 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180402225429/http://www.perthmodernschool.wa.edu.au/Portals/0/Documents/PerthModernSchool%20news%20Apr2013-8s%5B1%5D.pdf |archive-date=2 April 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

After graduating high school, Langford was determined to become an actor. However, she was rejected from every acting school she applied to, on the grounds she was too young and did not have enough life experience. This led her to begin enrolling in acting classes and workshops in Perth, juggling three part-time jobs, and later finding herself an agent.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |url=https://www.smh.com.au/lifestyle/celebrity/katherine-langford-i-feel-lucky-to-have-a-new-platform-to-talk-about-important-issues-20170615-gwrifn.html |title=Katherine Langford: I feel lucky to have a new platform to talk about important issues |last=Marriner |first=Cosima |date=16 June 2017 |website=The Sydney Morning Herald |access-date=18 June 2017}}</ref><ref name=":3" /> From 2014 to 2015, Langford studied at the Principal Academy of Dance & Theatre Arts, majoring in Music Theatre, and appeared in a production of ''[[Godspell]]''. She was then one of five selected to participate in the [[National Institute of Dramatic Arts]] Advanced Actors Residency in 2015.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web |url=http://www.daughterstkilda.com/scarlett/ |title=Katherine Langford – Scarlett |website=Nexus Production Group |access-date=20 April 2017}}</ref> In the same year, she trained at Nicholson's Academy of Screen Acting and portrayed the role of [[Juan Perón]]'s mistress in the 2015 production of ''[[Evita (musical)|Evita]]''.<ref name=":0" /> Langford was offered a position in the Bachelor of Arts program in Acting at the [[Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts]] and intended to begin studies in 2016.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" /> However, she never enrolled and instead pursued professional roles.<ref name=":1" />

==Career==
Langford first appeared in several small independent films, including ''Story of Miss Oxygen'' (2015), ''Imperfect Quadrant'' (2016), and ''Daughter'' (2016).<ref name=":0"/><ref name=":2"/> She portrayed the lead character in ''Daughter'', which debuted at the [[2016 Cannes Film Festival]].<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/ya-adaptation-13-reasons-why-casts-dylan-minnette-121259866.html |title=YA adaptation '13 Reasons Why' casts Dylan Minnette, Katherine Langford |date=9 June 2016 |agency=AFP |accessdate=4 April 2017}}</ref> In 2016, after declining the offer from Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts, Langford auditioned for ''[[Will (TV series)|Will]]'', a television series centered on the young life of [[William Shakespeare]]. She did not get the role, which was instead given to [[Olivia DeJonge]].<ref name=":1"/>

Langford auditioned for the role of American high school student [[Hannah Baker]] in the mystery teen drama TV series ''[[13 Reasons Why]]'', over [[Skype]]. She had only 10 days to get an [[O-1 visa]] as she had not worked in the United States before.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Keaney |first1=Quinn |title=How Netflix's 13 Reasons Why Is the Most Important YA Adaptation Yet |url=https://www.popsugar.co.uk/celebrity/13-Reasons-Why-Interview-Dylan-Minnette-Katherine-Langford-43403798 |work=PopSugar Celebrity UK}}</ref> She has received critical acclaim for her work on the show.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Montgomery |first1=Daniel |title=Emmy spotlight: Newcomer Katherine Langford ('13 Reasons Why') is a revelation as a suicidal teen |url=http://www.goldderby.com/article/2017/katherine-langford-13-reasons-why-emmy-awards/ |accessdate=10 April 2017 |work=Goldderby |date=10 April 2017}}</ref> Langford researched the role, speaking with a representative of the sexual assault awareness campaign "It's On Us" and a psychiatrist who specializes in adolescence.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Highfill |first1=Samantha |title='13 Reasons Why' Star Katherine Langford Opens Up About 'Tough' Scenes |url=https://ew.com/tv/2017/04/04/13-reasons-why-katherine-langford-tough-scenes/ |work=Entertainment Weekly |date=4 April 2017}}</ref> In December 2016, she signed with the [[William Morris Endeavor]] agency.<ref>{{cite news |title=WME Signs 'Thirteen Reasons Why' Star Katherine Langford (Exclusive) |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/wme-signs-thirteen-reasons-why-star-katherine-langford-956178 |accessdate=12 April 2017 |work=The Hollywood Reporter |date=11 April 2017}}</ref> Langford reprised the role in the second season of the series, released on 18 May 2018.<ref>{{cite news |title="13 Reasons Why" Star Katherine Langford Says Hannah Will Have a HUGE Role In Season 2 |url=https://www.seventeen.com/celebrity/movies-tv/a13602396/13-reasons-why-star-katherine-langford-says-hannah-will-have-a-huge-role-in-season-2/ |accessdate=27 May 2018 |work=Seventeen |date=14 November 2017}}</ref> On 25 May 2018, Langford confirmed that she would not return as Hannah Baker in the third season of the series.<ref>{{cite news |title=13 Reasons Why: Katherine Langford says goodbye to Hannah Baker |url=https://ew.com/tv/2018/05/25/13-reasons-why-katherine-langford-goodbye-hannah-baker/ |accessdate=27 May 2018 |work=Entertainment Weekly |date=25 May 2018}}</ref>

Langford appeared in her first feature film, ''The Misguided'', an independent comedy-drama by Shannon Alexander, which premiered in January 2018.<ref>https://www.flickeringmyth.com/2018/01/trailer-misguided-starring-13-reasons-whys-katherine-langford/</ref> She also starred as Leah in the 2018 film ''[[Love, Simon]]'', an adaptation of the coming-of-age novel ''[[Simon vs. the Homo Sapiens Agenda]]'', by [[Becky Albertalli]].<ref name="variety">{{cite web |last1=McNary |first1=Dave |title='Veep' Star Tony Hale Joins Jennifer Garner in 'Simon vs. the Homo Sapiens Agenda' (EXCLUSIVE) |url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/tony-hale-simon-vs-the-homo-sapiens-agenda-1202005032/ |website=Variety |accessdate=21 April 2017 |date=9 March 2017}}</ref><ref name="deadline">{{cite web |last1=Hipes |first1=Patrick |title=Katherine Langford Lands Female Lead In 'Simon Vs The Homo Sapiens Agenda' |url=https://deadline.com/2017/01/simon-vs-the-homo-sapiens-agenda-katherine-langford-movie-1201884059/ |website=Deadline |accessdate=21 April 2017 |date=12 January 2017}}</ref>

On 12 September 2018, it was announced that Langford had been cast in the web television series ''[[Cursed (2020 TV series)|Cursed]]''. Set in an re-imagined [[King Arthur|Arthurian]] world, Langford portrays Nimue, a teenage girl destined to become the [[Lady of the Lake]]. The series is based on the novel of the same name by [[Frank Miller (comics)|Frank Miller]] and [[Tom Wheeler (writer)|Tom Wheeler]]. It premiered on [[Netflix]] in July 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://deadline.com/2018/09/katherine-langford-cast-cursed-netflix-arthurian-series-13-reasons-why-star-zetna-fuentes-direct-frank-miller-1202463244/ |title='13 Reasons Why's Katherine Langford To Star In Netflix's Arthurian Series 'Cursed'; Zetna Fuentes To Direct |last=Andreeva |first=Nellie |publisher=Deadline |date=12 September 2018 |accessdate=27 October 2018}}</ref> 

In October 2018, she was cast in ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]''. However, her scenes were cut from the final film, with the [[Russo brothers]] revealing Langford had been cast as a teenage version of [[Tony Stark (Marvel Cinematic Universe)|Tony Stark]]'s daughter, Morgan. Her scene was removed as the change in actors confused the audience, and the directors realized there was no emotional connection with the character's older version.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2019/05/avengers-endgame-katherine-langford-role-details-why-cut |title=Why Avengers: Endgame and the Russos Cut Katherine Langford's Role |last=Bradley |first=Laura |date=6 May 2019 |website=HWD|access-date=6 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Katherine Langford: "Fantasy fans haven't had a lot of female role models" |url=https://www.nme.com/tv-interviews/katherine-langford-cursed-interview-13-reasons-why-2712902 |website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM |accessdate=1 August 2020 |date=22 July 2020}}</ref> In 2020, she said she was willing to return to the role.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dumaraog |first1=Ana |title=Katherine Langford Opens Up About Being Cut From Avengers: Endgame |url=https://screenrant.com/avengers-endgame-katherine-langford-tony-stark-cut-scene/ |website=ScreenRant |accessdate=1 August 2020 |date=27 July 2020}}</ref>

Langford co-starred in ''[[Knives Out]]'', a 2019 [[murder mystery]] film.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://collider.com/knives-out-katherine-langford-jaeden-martell-interview/|title=Katherine Langford and Jaeden Martell Talk 'Knives Out' and Instagram|last=Weintraub|first=Steve 'Frosty'|date=25 November 2019|website=Collider|language=en-US|access-date=30 November 2019}}</ref> The ensemble cast received critical acclaim.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/11/27/782165138/knives-out-a-classic-comic-mystery-of-uncommon-sharpness|title='Knives Out,' A Classic Comic Mystery Of Uncommon Sharpness|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=30 November 2019}}</ref>
