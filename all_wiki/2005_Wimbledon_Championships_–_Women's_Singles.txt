{{main|2005 Wimbledon Championships}}

[[Venus Williams]] defeated compatriot and world No. 1 [[Lindsay Davenport]] in the final, 4–6, 7–6<sup>(7–4)</sup>, 9–7 to win the Ladies' Singles tennis title at the 2005 Wimbledon Championships.<ref name="Barrett">{{cite book|last=Barrett|first=John|title=Wimbledon: The Official History|date=2014|publisher=Vision Sports Publishing|isbn=9-781909-534230|edition=4th}}</ref> It was the longest Wimbledon women's final match in history, lasting two hours and 45 minutes,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/4644569.stm|title=Williams regains Wimbledon title|date=2 July 2005|work=[[BBC Sport]]|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> over an hour longer than that year's men's final. She became the first woman since [[Helen Wills]] in [[1935 Wimbledon Championships – Women's Singles|1935]] and the first in the [[Open era]] to win the Wimbledon title after saving a match point against her.<ref name="Venus">{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/07/03/sports/tennis/in-an-epic-wimbledon-final-williams-prevails.html|title=In an Epic Wimbledon Final, Williams Prevails|date=3 July 2005|work=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref> This was Venus's third Wimbledon singles title, fifth Grand Slam singles title overall, and her first since the [[2001 US Open (tennis)|2001 US Open]].<ref name="Venus" /> Venus dropped only one set the entire tournament, to Davenport in the final. [[Maria Sharapova]] was the defending champion, but lost to Venus Williams in the semifinals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2005/jul/01/wimbledon2005.wimbledon|title= Venus drives out Sharapova |date=1 July 2005|work=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref>

The first week of the tournament was headlined by two major upsets – [[Justine Henin|Justine Henin-Hardenne]]'s first round defeat marked the first time in the [[Open Era]] the reigning [[2005 French Open – Women's Singles|French Open]] champion had lost their opening match at Wimbledon, while [[Serena Williams]]'s loss in the third round was her earliest exit at any Grand Slam tournament since the [[1999 French Open – Women's Singles|1999 French Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/tennis/henin-becomes-first-big-name-to-fall/2005/06/22/1119321789521.html|title=Henin becomes first big name to fall|date=23 June 2005|work=[[The Age]]|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/wimb/2005-06-25-day-6_x.htm|title=Serena ousted at Wimbledon; Federer, Roddick advance|date=25 June 2005|work=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=29 June 2017}}</ref>
