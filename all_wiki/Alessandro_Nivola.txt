'''Alessandro Antine Nivola''' (born June 28, 1972) is an American actor and producer. He has been nominated for many awards including a [[Tony Award]], a [[Critics Choice Award]], and an [[Independent Spirit Award]] and has won a [[Screen Actors Guild Award]], a [[British Independent Film Award]] (BIFA), and the Best Actor Award at the 2017 [[Tribeca Film Festival]] among others.

He has starred in Hollywood blockbusters such as ''[[Face/Off]]'', ''[[Jurassic Park III]]'' and the upcoming Sopranos feature film prequel ''[[The Many Saints of Newark]]'', as well as in critically acclaimed independent films, among them ''[[Disobedience (2017 film)|Disobedience]]'', ''[[A Most Violent Year]]'', ''[[Junebug (film)|Junebug]]'' and ''[[Laurel Canyon]]''. He has also starred three times on Broadway, most recently opposite [[Bradley Cooper]] in the 2014 revival of ''[[The Elephant Man]]'' which earned him a Tony Award nomination.

As a producer he runs King Bee Productions which made two seasons of the HBO comedy ''[[Doll & Em]]'' and the Independent Spirit Award nominated feature film ''[[To Dust]]''.

He is married to actress and director [[Emily Mortimer]].

==Career==
Nivola graduated from Yale University with a BA in English in 1994 and a year later made his Broadway debut opposite [[Helen Mirren]] in ''[[A Month in the Country (play)|A Month in the Country]]'' earning a Drama Desk Award nomination.<ref>{{cite web |title=A Month in the Country Production History |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/shows/backstage.php?showid=311791 |website=Broadwayworld.com}}</ref> Shortly after that came his breakthrough performance in John Woo’s feature film ''[[Face/Off]]'' (1997) playing Nicolas Cage’s brother Pollux Troy. In the ensuing years he has starred in many films including ''[[Mansfield Park]]'' (1999), ''[[Love’s Labour’s Lost]]'' (2000), ''[[Jurassic Park 3]]'' (2001), ''[[Laurel Canyon]]'' (2002 - Independent Spirit Award nomination), ''[[Junebug (film)|Junebug]]'' (2005), ''[[Goal! (film)|Goal!]] 1'' & ''2'' (2005, 2007), ''[[Coco Before Chanel]]'' (2009), ''[[Ginger and Rosa]]'' (2012), ''[[American Hustle]]'' (2013 - Screen Actors Guild Award, Critics Choice Award), ''[[A Most Violent Year]]'' (2014), ''[[Selma (film)|Selma]]'' (2014 - Critics Choice Award nomination), ''[[The Neon Demon]]'' (2016), ''[[One Percent More Humid]]'' (Best Actor Award 2017 TriBeCa Film Festival),<ref>{{cite web |last1=Cox |first1=Gordon |title=Tribeca Film Festival 'Keep the Change,' 'Bobbi Jene' Win Top Awards |url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/tribeca-film-festival-award-winners-2017-full-list-1202401812/#! |website=Variety.com}}</ref> ''[[You Were Never Really Here]]'' (2017), ''[[Disobedience]]'' (2017 - British Independent Film Award, London Critics Circle Film Award nomination),<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wiseman |first1=Andreas |title=British Independent Film Awards: 'The Favourite' Wins A Record Ten Awards |url=https://deadline.com/2018/12/the-favourite-british-independent-film-awards-wins-record-ten-awards-1202512500/ |website=Deadline.com}}</ref> and ''[[The Art of Self-Defense (2019 film)|The Art of Self Defense]]'' (2019).  

He will next be seen as Dickie Moltisanti, the lead role in ''[[The Many Saints of Newark]]'', [[David Chase]]’s feature film prequel to his heralded television series ''[[The Sopranos]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vlessing |first1=Etan |title='Sopranos' Prequel Film Taps Alessandro Nivola For Lead Role |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/alessandro-nivola-play-dickey-moltisanti-sopranos-prequel-film-alan-taylor-direct-1163195 |website=hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref>

Nivola has also worked frequently in television, starring opposite [[Robert DeNiro]] in [[Barry Levinson]]’s Madoff family biopic ''[[The Wizard of Lies]]'' (2017), as well as the TNT miniseries ''[[The Company (TV miniseries)|The Company]]'' (2007), the UK Chanel 4 series ''[[Chimerica]]'' (2019), the Netflix film ''[[The Red Sea Diving Resort]]'' (2019), and the upcoming BBC miniseries ''[[Black Narcissus]]''.

On stage, in addition to ''[[A Month in the Country (play)|A Month in the Country]]'', he starred on Broadway in 2013 in ''[[The Winslow Boy]]'' (Outer Critics Circle Award nomination), and in 2014 in ''[[The Elephant Man]]'' opposite [[Bradley Cooper]] (Tony Award nomination), and off-Broadway in the [[Ethan Hawke]] directed [[Sam Shepard]] play ''[[A Lie of the Mind]]'' (2010) with [[Laurie Metcalfe]]. He also starred opposite [[Gwyneth Paltrow]] in the 1995 [[Williamstown Theater Festival]] production of Shakespeare's ''[[As You Like It]]''.

In 2013 Nivola established King Bee Productions with his wife [[Emily Mortimer]]. The company produced two seasons of the half hour comedy ''[[Doll & Em]]'' for HBO and BSkyB. He also produced ''[[To Dust]]'' starring [[Matthew Broderick]] which won the Audience Award at the 2018 TriBeCa Film Festival and was nominated for a 2020 Independent Spirit Award.

==Personal life==
Nivola married British actress [[Emily Mortimer]] in [[Buckinghamshire]] in January 2003. The couple have two children and live in [[Boerum Hill]], [[Brooklyn]].<ref name="Vincentelli">{{cite news |last1=Vincentelli |first1=Elizabeth |title=Alessandro Nivola: My Brooklyn |url=https://nypost.com/2013/11/16/alessandro-nivola-my-brooklyn/ |access-date=21 February 2019 |publisher=New York Post |date=November 16, 2013}}</ref> Nivola became a British citizen as Mortimer became an American citizen.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/8646018/Emily-Mortimer-interview-I-hope-my-kids-dont-look-like-my-father.html|title=Emily Mortimer interview: 'I hope my kids don't look like my father'|work=Telegraph|last=de Bertodano|first=Helena|publisher=Telegraph Media Group Limited}}</ref>
