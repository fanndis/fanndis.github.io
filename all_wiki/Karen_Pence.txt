'''Karen Sue Pence''' ([[Birth name|née]] '''Batten''', formerly '''Whitaker'''; born January 1, 1957) is an American [[schoolteacher]], painter, and the current [[Second Lady of the United States|second lady of the United States]] since 2017. She is married to the [[List of vice presidents of the United States|48th]] and current [[Vice President of the United States|vice president of the United States]], [[Mike Pence]]. She was formerly the [[First Lady]] of [[Indiana]] from January 14, 2013 to January 9, 2017.

==Early life and education==
Pence was born as Karen Sue Batten<ref>{{cite web |author=Staff Reports |url=http://www.therepublic.com/2016/07/14/karen-pence-holding-political-office-always-important-to-mike-pence/ |title=Karen Pence: Holding political office always important to Mike Pence |newspaper=[[The Republic (Columbus, Indiana)|The Republic]] |date=January 13, 2013 |access-date=July 24, 2016}}</ref> at [[McConnell Air Force Base]] in [[Kansas]]<ref>[https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-second-lady-karen-pence-military-spouses-fort-carson-colorado/]</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cosmopolitan.com/politics/news/a62233/karen-pence-facts/|title=7 Things to Know About Karen Pence, VP Mike Pence's Wife|date=March 29, 2017|publisher=Cosmopolitan}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/305457/karen-pence-secret-marriage-karen-whitaker-mike-pence-indiana/ |title=Karen Pence's secret first marriage |newspaper=[[SpinMedia|Death and Taxes]] |department=Politics |first=David |last=Bixenspan |date=October 4, 2016 |access-date=December 13, 2016 }}</ref> on January 1, 1957,<ref>{{cite web |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161129211507/http://greatagain.gov/meet-future-second-lady.html |archive-date=November 29, 2016 |url=http://greatagain.gov/meet-future-second-lady.html |title=Meet the Future Second Lady |publisher=[[Presidential transition of Donald Trump|Trump for America]] |access-date=January 15, 2017 |url-status=dead}}</ref> the daughter of Lillian ([[née]] Hacker; 1931–2004)<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tributes.com/obituary/show/Lillian-R.-Barcio-89232475 |title=Lillian R. Barcio Obituary |publisher=Tributes, Inc. |website=tributes.com |access-date=December 13, 2016 }}</ref> and John M. Batten (1931/1932–1988), a [[United Airlines]] official.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/6856360/the_indianapolis_star/ |title=John Batten dies; ex-airline official |access-date=December 13, 2016 |date=March 6, 1988 |newspaper=The Indianapolis Star |department=newspapers.com }}</ref> Her parents divorced when she was very young, and her mother married Bernard Barcio in 1967.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2S5jDwAAQBAJ&q=divorce&pg=PT249|title=Pence: The Path to Power|last=Neal|first=Andrea|date=2018-08-01|publisher=Indiana University Press|isbn=9781684350384|language=en}}</ref> She grew up in the [[Broad Ripple Village, Indianapolis|Broad Ripple Village]] neighborhood of [[Indianapolis]], where she graduated as valedictorian from [[Bishop Chatard High School]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://alumni.bishopchatard.org/karen-pence/ |title=Karen (Batton) Pence, '75, Indiana's First Lady |publisher=Bishop Chatard High School |access-date=December 13, 2016 |date=September 19, 2016 }}</ref><ref name=Parker /> Pence attended nearby [[Butler University]] where she studied to become a teacher, and minored in art.<ref name=indystar>{{cite news |last1=Rudavsky |first1=Shari |title=Karen Pence is right at home |url=http://www.indystar.com/story/life/2013/12/12/karen-pence-is-right-at-home/3997289/|access-date=July 15, 2016|newspaper=[[The Indianapolis Star]]|date=December 12, 2013}}</ref> She received both a [[Bachelor of Science]] (B.S.) and a [[Master of Science]] (M.S.) in [[elementary education]] from Butler University.<ref name=Maloney />

==Career==
Pence has taught at John Strange Elementary, Acton Elementary, Fall Creek Elementary, and the [[The Orchard School (Indianapolis)|Orchard School]], all in Indianapolis.<ref name=indystar/>

After the birth of her first child, Pence took a class in [[watercolor painting]]. This led to a career painting portraits of houses<ref name=indystar/> and historic buildings.<ref name=sprvl /> She has completed as many as thirty-five paintings a year, some on commission and selling others at local art fairs.<ref name=Maloney />

While Mike Pence was in Congress, Karen worked at [[Immanuel Christian School (Springfield)|Immanuel Christian School]], a private [[Christian school]] in [[Springfield, Virginia]], as an arts teacher for twelve years.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Eli Rosenberg|date=15 January 2019|title=The school that hired Karen Pence requires applicants to disavow gay marriage, trans identity|work=[[The Washington Post]]|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/arts-entertainment/2019/01/16/school-that-hired-karen-pence-requires-applicants-disavow-gay-marriage-trans-identity/|accessdate=30 September 2019|quote=the second lady had taught at the school for 12 years previously when Mike Pence was a member of Congress.}}</ref>

==First lady of Indiana==
Pence was the [[First Lady|first lady]] of Indiana during her husband's term as governor of the state from 2013 to 2017. In her first year of the role, she established the Indiana First Lady's Charitable Foundation to "promote individuals and organizations that encourage children, families, and the arts", also offering grants and scholarships.<ref name=Maloney>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.cosmopolitan.com/politics/news/a62233/karen-pence-facts/ |title=Who Is Karen Pence? 7 Facts About VP Mike Pence's Wife. |magazine=[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]] |date=March 20, 2017 |access-date=June 23, 2017 |last=Maloney |first=Maggie}}</ref>

While first lady in 2015, Pence started a small business named "'That's My Towel!' Charm" which makes metal charms for attaching to towels so they can be more easily identified when among others.  The business was put on hold when Mike Pence became a vice presidential candidate.<ref name=Maloney />

==Second lady of the United States==
[[File:Karen Pence official portrait (cropped).jpg|upright|left|thumb|Pence's first official portrait]]
[[File:Mike and Karen Pence Gold Star Wives 06.jpg|thumb|Mike and Karen Pence with [[Gold Star Wives of America]], April 2017]]
Pence became the [[Second Lady of the United States|second lady of the United States]] on January 20, 2017, succeeding [[Jill Biden]]. She hired Kristan King Nevins as her chief of staff; Nevins had served in the same position under former first lady [[Barbara Bush]].<ref name=Parker /><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/02/07/politics/karen-pence-chief-of-staff-kristan-king-nevins/ |title=Second Lady Karen Pence hires chief of staff |first=Betsy |last=Klein |date=February 7, 2017 |publisher=CNN |department=Politics |access-date=June 23, 2017 }}</ref> As second lady, Pence intends to raise awareness of [[art therapy]], to which she was first exposed when visiting a Washington hospital during her husband's tenure as a congressman.<ref name=sprvl>{{cite news |url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2017/feb/17/vp-pences-wife-aims-to-raise-awareness-about-art-t/ |title=Karen Pence, the vice president's wife, aims to raise awareness about art therapy |newspaper=[[The Washington Times]] |agency=Associated Press |date=February 17, 2017 |access-date=June 23, 2017 |first=Darlene |last=Superville }}</ref><ref name=kleinart /> In October 2017, she visited the campus of [[Florida State University]] to highlight the university's art therapy program, which dates back to the 1990s.<ref name="orlandosentinel2ndladykaren">{{cite news|last1=Rohrer|first1=Gray|title=2nd Lady Karen Pence unveils art therapy initiative at FSU|url=http://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/politics/political-pulse/os-karen-pence-fsu-art-therapy-20171018-story.html |accessdate=October 20, 2017|work=Orlando Sentinel|date=October 18, 2017}}</ref>

Pence is a [[honey bee]] conservation advocate. While first lady of Indiana, she had a [[beehive]] installed at the [[Indiana Governor's Residence]]. She continued to raise awareness of honey bee habitat destruction and the importance of [[pollination]] in 2017 by having a beehive installed at the official vice presidential residence, [[Number One Observatory Circle]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://time.com/4807915/karen-pence-bees-beehive/ |title=Karen Pence Unveils Beehive With 20,000 Bees at Vice Presidential Residence |date=June 6, 2017 |magazine=Time |first=Ashley |last=Hoffman |access-date=November 17, 2020 }}</ref>

===2019 return to teaching===
In January 2019, it was reported that Pence was returning as the arts teacher for Immanuel Christian School,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Eli Rosenberg |title=The school that hired Karen Pence requires applicants to disavow gay marriage, trans identity |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/arts-entertainment/2019/01/16/school-that-hired-karen-pence-requires-applicants-disavow-gay-marriage-trans-identity/ |accessdate=30 September 2019 |work=[[The Washington Post]] |date=15 January 2019 |quote=the second lady had taught at the school for 12 years previously when Mike Pence was a member of Congress.}}</ref> said in a statement that she was "excited to be back in the classroom and doing what I love to do," and that she had "missed teaching art."

The school had previously been criticized and accused of [[homophobia]] for not admitting [[LGBT]] students, posting a policy wherein it is permitted to turn away students who engage in, uphold, or accept "sexual immorality, homosexual activity, or bisexual activity", with the policy also applying to parents and employees.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2019/01/15/second-lady-karen-pence-stepping-back-into-classroom/2584374002/ |title=Second Lady Karen Pence is stepping back into the classroom at a school that bans gay students|publisher=USA Today|date=January 15, 2019|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref> Pence was harshly criticized in the media and by LGBT advocates and advocacy organizations in the days following the announcement.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.foxnews.com/politics/karen-pence-slammed-by-lgbt-activists-for-taking-job-at-school-that-bans-gay-teachers|title=Karen Pence slammed by LGBT activists for taking job at school that bans gay teachers|publisher=Fox News|date=January 16, 2019|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref> Vice President Pence defended his wife's profession and decision, accusing her critics of attacking religious education. He said that he and his wife were "used to the criticism", but that he was angered over the criticism of his wife, opining that "to see major news organizations attacking Christian education is deeply offensive to us", and that it "should stop".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bustle.com/p/how-mike-pence-defended-his-wife-karens-job-at-anti-lgbtq-school-will-baffle-you-15836309|title=How Mike Pence Defended His Wife Karen's Job At An Anti-LGBTQ School Will Baffle You|publisher=Bustle|date=January 17, 2019|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref>

==Family and personal life==
While in high school, she met her first husband, John Steven Whitaker. They were married on August 4, 1978, in [[Brewster County, Texas]], and later divorced.<ref>{{cite web|title=Texas Marriages, 1966-2010|url=https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:VTYS-FZY |website=familysearch.org|accessdate=5 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ancestry.com/sharing/9363309?h=d3b27f |title=Indiana, Marriage Certificates, 1958–2005: Michael Richard Pence |website=ancestry.com |access-date=December 13, 2016 }}</ref><ref name=Parker>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/karen-pence-is-the-vice-presidents-prayer-warrior-gut-check-and-shield/2017/03/28/3d7a26ce-0a01-11e7-8884-96e6a6713f4b_story.html|title=Karen Pence is the vice president's 'prayer warrior,' gut check and shield|last=Parker|first=Ashley|date=March 28, 2017|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=March 28, 2017}}</ref> Whitaker was a medical student during their marriage.<ref name=Parker />

Karen met Mike Pence while she was playing guitar at Mass at St. Thomas Aquinas Church,<ref name=indystar/> a Catholic church in Indianapolis they both attended.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/acts-of-faith/wp/2016/07/15/what-it-means-that-mike-pence-called-himself-an-evangelical-catholic/ |title=What it means that Mike Pence called himself an 'evangelical Catholic' |newspaper=The Washington Post |first=Michelle |last=Boorstein |date=July 18, 2016 |access-date=December 13, 2016 }}</ref> Their first date included ice skating at the Indiana State Fairgrounds.<ref name=Chokshi>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/20/us/politics/who-is-karen-pence.html |title=Who Is Karen Pence? A Brief Introduction to the Second Lady |date=January 20, 2017 |access-date=June 23, 2017 |first=Niraj |last=Chokshi |newspaper=The New York Times }}</ref> After about nine months of dating,<ref name=Chokshi /> they became [[Engagement|engaged]] in August 1984<ref name=indystar/> and married on June 8, 1985.<ref name=indystar/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/6890339/the_columbus_herald/ |title=Pence-Whitaker [marriage announcement] |newspaper=The Columbus Herald |department=newspapers.com |access-date=December 13, 2016 |date=June 21, 1985 }}</ref><ref name="PotentialSecondLady">{{cite news|last1=Glum|first1=Julia|title=Who Is Karen Pence? Meet Mike Pence's Wife, Potential US Second Lady From Indiana |url=http://www.ibtimes.com/who-karen-pence-meet-mike-pences-wife-potential-us-second-lady-indiana-2391680|access-date= July 15, 2016 |newspaper=International Business Times|date=July 14, 2016}}</ref> They were both [[Roman Catholic]] and later converted to [[evangelical Christianity]] by 1995.<ref name=Parker /> The couple has three children: Michael (serving in the [[U.S. Marine Corps]]),<ref name=kleinart>{{cite news |url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/04/14/politics/karen-pence-art-therapy-asia-pacific/ |title=Karen Pence highlights art therapy with Asia-Pacific trip |publisher=CNN |date=April 14, 2017 |first=Betsy |last=Klein |access-date=June 23, 2017 |department=Politics }}</ref> [[Charlotte Pence Bond|Charlotte]], and Audrey.<ref name=indystar/><ref name=Maloney /> Pence has lived most of her life in Indiana, though the entire family moved to Washington, D.C. for the twelve years that husband Mike was a congressman from Indiana before his election as governor of Indiana.<ref name=Maloney /><ref name=sprvl /> She is a trained pilot.<ref name=indystar/><ref name=Maloney />

Pence is known for her dedication to promoting art as a way of healing.<ref name="StarkHealingArts">{{cite news |last1=Stark |first1=Liz|title=How Indiana First Lady Karen Pence Came to Champion the Healing Power of the Arts |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/indiana-lady-karen-pence-champion-healing-power-arts/story?id=41152258|access-date=August 16, 2016 |publisher=ABC News|date=August 12, 2016}}</ref> She provided the watercolor illustrations for her daughter Charlotte's 2018 children's book, ''[[Marlon Bundo's A Day in the Life of the Vice President]]'', the proceeds from which are given to charities, including an art therapy program.<ref name=wpost>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/hes-bunny-of-the-united-states-and-now-hes-the-hero-of-a-childrens-book-too/2018/03/13/28c53e3e-2626-11e8-b79d-f3d931db7f68_story.html |title=He's Bunny of the United States. And now he's the hero of a children's book, too. |first=Roxanne |last=Roberts |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=March 14, 2018 |access-date=March 19, 2018 }}</ref>
