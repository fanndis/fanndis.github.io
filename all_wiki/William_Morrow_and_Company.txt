'''William Morrow and Company''' is an American [[publishing]] company founded by [[William Morrow (publisher)|William Morrow]] in 1926.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YCD-DAAAQBAJ&q=William+Morrow+and+Company+founded+in+1926&pg=PT52|title=Orphan Train: A Novel by Christina Baker Kline (Trivia-On-Books)|last=Books|first=Trivion|date=2016-09-07|publisher=Trivia-On-Books|language=en}}</ref> The company was acquired by [[Scott Foresman]] in 1967, sold to [[Hearst Corporation]] in 1981, and sold to [[News Corporation (1980–2013)|News Corporation]] (now [[News Corp (2013–present)|News Corp]]) in 1999.<ref>{{Cite news| issn = 0362-4331| title = SCOTT, FORESMAN PLANS ACQUISITION; Textbook Publisher Would Add William Morrow| work = The New York Times| access-date = 2019-04-30| date = 1966-10-18| url = https://www.nytimes.com/1966/10/18/archives/scott-foresman-plans-acquisition-textbook-publisher-would-add.html}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news| issn = 0362-4331| last = McDowell| first = Edwin| title = Hearst Agrees to Acquire Morrow for $25 Million| work = The New York Times| access-date = 2019-04-30| date = 1981-02-13| url = https://www.nytimes.com/1981/02/13/books/hearst-agrees-to-acquire-morrow-for-25-million.html}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news| issn = 0362-4331| last1 = Carvajal| first1 = Doreen| last2 = Fabrikant| first2 = Geraldine| title = News Corp. Agrees to Buy Two Publishers From Hearst| work = The New York Times| access-date = 2019-04-30| date = 1999-06-18| url = https://www.nytimes.com/1999/06/18/business/news-corp-agrees-to-buy-two-publishers-from-hearst.html}}</ref> The company is now an imprint of [[HarperCollins]].

William Morrow has published many renowned fiction and non-fiction authors, including [[Ray Bradbury]], [[Michael Chabon]], [[Neil Gaiman]], [[Erle Stanley Gardner]], [[B. H. Liddell Hart]], [[Elmore Leonard]], [[Steven D. Levitt]], [[Steven Pinker]], [[Judith Rossner]], and [[Neal Stephenson]].

[[Francis Thayer Hobson]] was President and later Chairman of the Board of William Morrow and Company.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://research.hrc.utexas.edu:8080/hrcxtf/view?docId=ead/00330.xml |title=Thayer Hobson: An Inventory of His Papers in the Manuscript Collection at the Harry Ransom Humanities Research Center  |publisher=[[University of Texas]] [[Harry Ransom Center]] |access-date=2008-06-24 }}</ref>
