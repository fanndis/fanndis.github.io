'''Mogens Jensen''' (born 31 October 1963) is a Danish politician who served as the [[Minister for Food, Agriculture and Fisheries (Denmark)|Minister for Food, Agriculture and Fisheries]], [[Minister for Gender Equality (Denmark)|Minister for Gender Equality]] and [[Minister for Nordic Cooperation (Denmark)|Minister for Nordic Cooperation]] from 2019 to November 2020, when he was forced to resign following a botched handling of an [[Cluster 5|order to cull]] the Danish [[mink]] population.<ref name="skandale">{{Cite web|date=2020-11-18|title=Mogens Jensen trækker sig som fødevareminister efter minkskandale|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/mogens-jensen-traekker-sig-som-foedevareminister-efter-minkskandale|access-date=2020-11-18|website=DR|language=da-DK}}</ref> 

==Early life==
Jensen was born in [[Nykøbing Mors]] to Harry Jensen and Ebba Møller Jensen. 

==Political career==
Jensen was elected member of [[Folketinget]] for the [[Social Democrats (Denmark)|Social Democrats]] in the [[2005 Danish general election|2005 elections]]. 

In the government of Prime Minister [[Helle Thorning-Schmidt]], Jensen served as Minister for Trade and Development Cooperation from 2013 until 2014. In response to the introduction of an [[Uganda]]n law imposing harsh penalties for homosexuality in 2014, he led Denmark's move to divert 50 million crowns ($9 million) of development aid away from the Ugandan government.<ref>Elias Biryabarema (February 24, 2014), [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-uganda-gaybill/ugandan-president-signs-anti-gay-bill-defying-the-west-idUSBREA1N05S20140224 Ugandan president signs anti-gay bill, defying the West] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref> Also, under his leadership, Denmark joined the [[Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank]] (AIIB) in 2015.<ref>Sui-Lee Wee (March 30, 2015), [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-asia-aiib-denmark/denmark-applies-to-join-china-backed-aiib-investment-bank-idUSKBN0MP04P20150330 Denmark applies to join China-backed AIIB investment bank] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref>

Jensen was appointed [[Minister for Food, Agriculture and Fisheries (Denmark)|Minister for Food, Agriculture and Fisheries]], [[Minister for Gender Equality (Denmark)|Minister for Gender Equality]] and [[Minister for Nordic Cooperation (Denmark)|Minister for Nordic Cooperation]] in the [[Frederiksen Cabinet]] from 27 June 2019.<ref>{{ft.dk link}}. Retrieved on 3 July 2019.</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Deleuran Müller|first1=Thea|title=Danmarks nye regering er nu på plads: Se hele Mette Frederiksens ministerhold her |url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/politik/danmarks-nye-regering-er-nu-paa-plads-se-hele-mette-frederiksens-ministerhold-her |website=dr.dk |language=Danish|accessdate=3 July 2019|date=27 June 2019}}</ref> He saw himself forced to resign as minister on the 18th of November 2020 due to the mishandling of a case, where – in an attempt to prevent harmful [[Cluster 5|coronavirus]] mutations in the [[mink]] population – the [[Mink industry in Denmark|Danish mink fur industry]] had been ordered by the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration to cull the entire Danish mink population without proper legal provision.<ref name="skandale" />

In addition to his role in parliament, Jensen served as member of the Danish delegation to the [[Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe]] from 2008 until 2019. He has in the past served as the Assembly's rapporteur on sports governance (2018).<ref>[https://assembly.coe.int/nw/xml/News/News-View-EN.asp?newsid=6937&lang=2&cat= Call for a major overhaul of sports governance structures and practices] [[Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe]], press release of January 24, 2018.</ref>
