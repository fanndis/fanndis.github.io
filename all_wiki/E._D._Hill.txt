'''Edith Ann "E.D." Hill''', (born July 27, 1961) known professionally as '''E.D. Donahey''' during her second marriage, is an American [[journalist]] and [[news presenter]] for [[CNN]].  She has been a news anchor and radio host and formerly worked for the [[Fox News Channel]].<ref name=Arena/><ref name="Arena-Cancel"/>

==Early life and education==
She was born '''Edith Ann Tarbox''' in the USA.

Hill earned her [[bachelor's degree]] in [[journalism]] from the [[University of Texas at Austin]], located in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], Texas, where she was a member of [[Delta Gamma|Delta Gamma Sorority]].

==Career==
She was a [[VJ (media personality)|VJ]] for [[VH1]] from September 1986 to August 1987. Moving from there to journalism, she spent time at [[WPXI-TV]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=_J1SAAAAIBAJ&sjid=IG4DAAAAIBAJ&pg=5459%2C2228155|title=WPXI anchor Tarbox loves digging for news|last=Vancheri|first=Barbara|date=8 August 1987|work=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|access-date=7 September 2016|via=Google News}}</ref> [[WHDH-TV]], and [[WABC-TV]] where she was brought in to co-anchor the station's morning newscast as well as its just launched midday broadcast. During this time Hill was still using her maiden name, Edye Tarbox.

In 1998, Hill joined the [[Fox News Channel]], where she served as an anchor for ''[[Fox and Friends]]'' from 1998 to 2006. She later moved to the 2:00 p.m. hour after serving as host of the 11:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. block of ''[[Fox News Live]]'' from 2006 until her show ''[[America's Pulse]]'' was canceled in 2008. She also co-hosted [[Bill O'Reilly (commentator)|Bill O'Reilly's]] ''[[The Radio Factor]]'' for five years. She had also substituted for Bill O'Reilly on ''[[The O'Reilly Factor]].''

In 2007, the television-industry blog ''[[TV Newser]]'' reported that it had been given a copy of an internal Fox News Channel memorandum that gave the following description of ''America's Pulse'': "During this hour, E.D. will present the news in a fair & balanced way with her own passionate style and sense of humor."<ref>{{cite web

==="Terrorist fist jab" incident===
On the June 6, 2008, episode of ''America's Pulse'', Hill asked whether a [[fist bump]] between then-presidential candidate [[Barack Obama]], and his wife [[Michelle Obama|Michelle]], after the final 2008 Presidential Democratic primaries was a "terrorist fist jab". Hill, introducing an upcoming discussion before a commercial break, posited that the gesture was either "A fist bump? A pound? [or] A terrorist fist jab?," but never explained the term when the segment continued after the break.<ref>{{cite news |title=Fox News Asks If Michelle and Barack Did a 'Terrorist Fist Jab'|url=http://blogs.abcnews.com/politicalpunch/2008/06/fox-news-asks-i.html |publisher = [[ABC News]] |date=June 9, 2008 |accessdate=June 10, 2008}}</ref> The incident set off a controversy among bloggers and television commentators.<ref>Politico: [http://www.politico.com/blogs/michaelcalderone/0608/Fistjabbing_with_the_enemy.html Fist-jabbing with the enemy.]  June 9, 2008.</ref><ref>''[[Morning Joe]]'', [[NBC News]], [http://www.nbcnews.com/id/22425001 June 10, 2008].</ref><ref>''[[Countdown with Keith Olbermann]]'', NBC News, [http://www.nbcnews.com/id/22425001 June 9, 2008].</ref><ref>''[[Verdict with Dan Abrams]]'', NBC News, [http://www.nbcnews.com/id/22425001 June 9, 2008].</ref> Hill apologized for her comments the next day.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.adweek.com/tvnewser/hill-apologizes-for-terrorist-tease/20670/  |title=Hill Apologizes For "Terrorist" Tease| date=June 10, 2008|author1=SteveK |website=[[TV Newser]]}}</ref> The next week, the [[Fox News Channel]] announced that Hill's show ''America's Pulse'' had been canceled. [[Martha McCallum]]'s show ''[[The Live Desk (U.S. TV program)|The Live Desk]]'' would become two hours (1&ndash;3 p.m.), and [[Trace Gallagher]] would become a co-host with McCallum.<ref>{{cite news |title=Fox News Changes: "Terrorist Fist Jab" Anchor E.D. Hill Loses Her Show, Laura Ingraham In at 5PM|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2008/06/10/fox-news-changes-terroris_n_106306.html |work= [[The Huffington Post]] |date= June 10, 2008|accessdate= June 10, 2008 |first=Danny |last=Shea}}</ref>

In November 2008, after the general election, it was announced that Hill's contract with Fox would not be renewed. She would continue with the network until her contract expired. Senior Vice President of Programming at Fox Bill Shine says that he "chose not to renew E.D.'s latest contract" but noted that "Hill has been a valued contributor to the success of FNC over the years, and we wish her all the best".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2008/11/17/ed-hill-leaves-fox-news-c_n_144513.html|title=E.D. Hill To Leave Fox News Channel| author = Graham, Nicholas|date=2008-11-18| work = [[The Huffington Post]] |accessdate= November 18, 2008}}</ref>

On October 14, 2008, Hill was a guest co-host of the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] daytime show ''[[The View (U.S. TV series)|The View]]'', then returned to co-host again on September 23, 2009 and September 25, 2009, while [[Elisabeth Hasselbeck]] was on maternity leave;<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2009/08/20/meghan-mccain-ed-hill-to_n_263871.html|title=Meghan McCain, E.D. Hill to Guest Host ''The View'' During Elisabeth Hasselbeck Maternity Leave| author = Shea, Danny|date= August 20, 2009| work = [[The Huffington Post]] |accessdate= September 24, 2009}}</ref> she also hosted on July 28, 2010, while [[Barbara Walters]] was on leave.

In February 2011, it was announced that she would be joining the staff of ''[[In the Arena]]'' with [[Eliot Spitzer]] on CNN,<ref name=Arena>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2011/02/25/AR2011022504995.html|title= Kathleen Parker Leaves CNN's 'ParkerSpitzer'|author= Farhi, Paul | work =[[The Washington Post]]|date= February 25, 2011}}</ref> but just five months later CNN elected to cancel the show instead.<ref name="Arena-Cancel">{{cite web |date=July 6, 2011 |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/07/06/eliot-spitzer-show-cancel_n_891418.html |title=Eliot Spitzer Show Canceled on CNN |website=[[The Huffington Post]]|author1=Shea, Danny| accessdate=July 6, 2011}}</ref>

[[Glenn Beck]] announced on his GBTV Show August 20, 2012 that E.D. Hill would be part of GBTV's on-site coverage of the 2012  Republican National Convention in Tampa, FL.

On October 6, 2012, Hill moderated [[The Rumble in the Air-Conditioned Auditorium]], a debate between [[Bill O'Reilly (political commentator)|Bill O'Reilly]] and [[Jon Stewart]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Memmott and Deutsch|first=Carol and Lindsay|title=O'Reilly, Stewart 'rumble' debate brings the humor|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/tv/2012/10/06/jon-stewart-oreilly-rumble/1617481/|publisher=USA Today|accessdate=7 October 2012|date=October 6, 2012}}</ref>

===Awards===
Hill won a local [[Emmy Award]] for Outstanding News Special while working for [[WHDH-TV]] in Boston (1990â€“91), and has also received a [[Golden Quill Award]] for live spot news reporting.

===Books===
In November 2005, [[William Morrow and Company|William Morrow]] published Hill's first book, a collection of personal profiles entitled ''Going Places: How America's Best and Brightest Got Started Down the Road of Life''. Her next book, ''I'm Not Your Friend, I'm Your Parent'', was published in the United States 2007 and published in China in 2010.

==Personal life==
On April 29, 1989, while a news anchor at [[WPXI-TV]] in [[Pittsburgh]], she married Marc Philip Weill (the son of financier [[Sanford Weill]]), at the time a vice president of [[Morgan Stanley Smith Barney|Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Company]] in New York City, New York.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/1989/04/30/style/edith-a-tarbox-a-news-anchor-weds-executive.html | work= [[The New York Times]] | title=Edith A. Tarbox, a News Anchor, Weds Executive | date= April 30, 1989 | accessdate= May 1, 2010}}</ref> They had two children and divorced in 1995.<ref name="people.com">{{cite news | url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20350597,00.html |date=March 8, 2010 |issue=Volume 73, No. 9 |title=An Agonizing Choice| author1=Triggs, Charlotte | author2=Hamm, Lisa|work=People Magazine}}</ref><ref name="Gasparino">{{cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB97484416768928828|title=Citigroup's Marc Weill Left Firm to Battle Drug Habit|last=Gasparino|first=Charles|author2=Joann S. Lublin|date=22 November 2000|work=Wall Street Journal|accessdate=8 October 2012}}</ref>

Her second marriage was to J. David Donahey, with whom she has one son.

In February 2002, she announced her engagement to her boyfriend of two years venture capitalist Joe Hill. They married June 1, 2002. He has three children from a previous marriage, and together they have a daughter and a son.<ref name="people.com"/>

In February 2010, she underwent a preventative [[double mastectomy]].<ref name="people.com"/><ref name="Gasparino"/>
