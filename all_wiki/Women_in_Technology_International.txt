'''Women in Technology International''' ('''WITI''') is a non-governmental organisation promoting the achievements of women in technology<ref name="witi2018">{{Cite web|url=https://www.witi.com/about/|title=About - WITI|website=WITI|language=en|access-date=2018-11-06}}</ref> and extending support, opportunities, and inspiration.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ZDgEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22International+network+of+women+in+technology%22&pg=PA84|title=InfoWorld|last=Inc|first=InfoWorld Media Group|date=1994-10-17|publisher=InfoWorld Media Group, Inc.|language=en}}</ref><ref name="2018year">{{Cite web|url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/228650433|title=The Inter-American Year of Women: LACCEI Recognizing Successes and Challenges in Gender Equity and Equality in Engineering Education in the Americas|website=ResearchGate|language=en|access-date=2018-11-09}}</ref> It was founded by Carolyn Leighton in 1989<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/bizwomen/news/profiles-strategies/2014/05/carolyn-leighton-has-made-a-business-out-of.html?page=all|title=Carolyn Leighton has made a business out of pushing more women into tech |website=www.bizjournals.com|access-date=2019-01-20}}</ref> as the International Network of Women in Technology.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://career.vt.edu/job-search/search-tools/workplace-diversity.html|title=Workplace diversity resources|access-date=2018-11-09|language=en}}</ref><ref name="2018year" /> It was renamed to the WITI Professional Association in 2001 when it acted as a trade association for women in technology.<ref name="witi2018" />

It is known for producing the [[List of Women in Technology International Hall of Fame inductees|Women in Technology International Hall of Fame]] which inducts women who have made a significant contribution to technology.

Started from an email group in 1989, by 2012 the group had grown to 2 million people and become a leading organisation for women in technology.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/ivo-lukas/women-tech_b_1583348.html|title=Top Five Women Tech Organizations: Building Networks and Bridging the Gap|last=Lukas|first=Ivo|date=2012-06-12|website=Huffington Post|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-20}}</ref>
The 2014 WITI 25-year conference Powering Up! included speakers such as [[Gwynne Shotwell]], [[President (corporate title)|President]] and [[Chief Operating Officer]] at [[SpaceX]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.witi.com/center/conferences/2014/summit/|title=Overview - WITI's Women in Technology Summit: Powering Up! - June 1-3, 2014 in Santa Clara, CA|website=www.witi.com|access-date=2019-01-20}}</ref> In 2017, Randstad Technologies announced a partnership with WITI to address gender [[underrepresented groups]] in the workplace and encourage girls and women to pursue technology education and careers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/randstad-technologies-announces-partnership-with-women-in-technology-international-300488552.html|title=Randstad Technologies announces partnership with Women in Technology International|last=Technologies|first=Randstad|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en|access-date=2019-01-20}}</ref>
