'''Mike Donilon''' is an American attorney and campaign consultant who serves as the designated [[Senior Advisor to the President of the United States|Senior Advisor]] to president-elect [[Joe Biden]]. Prior, Donilon was working as a partner at [[AKPD Message and Media]]. Between 2009 and 2013,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://akpdmedia.com/newsroom/biden-sr-adviser-mike-donilon-joins-akpd-as-partner/ |title=Biden Senior Adviser, Mike Donilon Joins AKPD As Partner |access-date=March 12, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140313001227/http://akpdmedia.com/newsroom/biden-sr-adviser-mike-donilon-joins-akpd-as-partner/ |archive-date=March 13, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> he served as Counselor to the Vice President under Vice President [[Joe Biden]]. Prior to his White House appointment, Donilon worked with the vice-presidential candidate to help him prepare for the debates and also as a traveling advisor. He has been an advisor and consultant to Vice President Biden since 1981.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://change.gov/newsroom/entry/vice_president_elect_biden_announces_key_staff_positions/ |title=Vice President-elect Biden announces key staff positions for the Vice President's office |access-date=November 26, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081126213146/http://change.gov/newsroom/entry/vice_president_elect_biden_announces_key_staff_positions/ |archive-date=November 26, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
He is the son of Theresa A. Donilon (née Conway), who worked two jobs, as a school secretary and a message service operator while also leading a school secretary and janitors' union; and Edward T. Donilon, who was a product control manager and a local school board chairman.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Theresa Ann Conway Donilon (1926-1998) - Find A...|url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/47175308/theresa-ann-donilon|access-date=2020-09-17|website=www.findagrave.com|language=en}}</ref> His father was of Irish descent, and his maternal grandparents had immigrated from [[Ireland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~battle/celeb/donilon.htm |title=Tom Donilon ancestry |publisher=Freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com |accessdate=April 17, 2015}}</ref> Donilon is a graduate of [[La Salle Academy, Providence|La Salle Academy]] in [[Providence, Rhode Island]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.lasalle-academy.org/podium/default.aspx?t=204&tn=Washington+Post+Does+Lenghty+Profile+on+La+Salle+Alum+Tom+Donilon+%2773&nid=579838&ptid=2218&sdb=False&pf=pcrw&mode=0&vcm=False|title= Washington Post Does Lengthy Profile on La Salle Alum Tom Donilon '73|date= December 22, 2010|publisher =LaSalle Academy|accessdate= April 21, 2014}}</ref> He earned a Bachelor of Arts and [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Georgetown University]].

== Career ==
Mike Donilon has advised candidates and worked on their campaigns in numerous important races including [[Douglas Wilder]]'s historic campaign and victory for governorship of [[Virginia]] (1989), [[Harris Wofford]]'s upset victory over [[Dick Thornburgh]] to become Senator for the state of Pennsylvania, and [[Bill Clinton]]'s successful run for the White House in 1992.

Donilon has also worked on campaigns for Governor [[Jon Corzine]] (D-NJ), Senator [[Bill Nelson (politician)|Bill Nelson]] (D-FL), Senator [[Jack Reed (politician)|Jack Reed]] (D-RI), Senator [[Sheldon Whitehouse]] (D-RI), Senator [[Joe Lieberman|Joe Liberman]] (I-CT), Representative [[Dick Gephardt]] (D-MO), Senator [[Chris Dodd]] (D-CT), Senator [[John Edwards]] (D-NC), Vice President [[Al Gore|Al Gore]] (D-D.C), Senator [[John Kerry]] (D-MA), and former Senator [[Mark Dayton]] (D-MN).<ref>{{Cite book|last=Jamieson|first=Kathleen Hall|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AkSABbzMfEIC&q=michael+donilon&pg=PA58|title=Electing the President, 2004: The Insiders' View|date=September 29, 2011|publisher=University of Pennsylvania Press|isbn=978-0-8122-0481-0|language=en}}</ref> In 2006 and 2008, Donilon claims he was "instrumental in the campaigns for Senator [[Claire McCaskill]] (D-MO), Senator [[Robert Menendez]] (D-NJ), Senator [[Mark Udall]] (D-CO), and Senator [[Jeanne Shaheen]] (D-NH), working to create the campaign ads for those races paid for by independent expenditure of the [[Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee]]."{{Attribution needed|date=June 2010}}

===Biden administration===
As his longtime advisor, Mike Donilon held significant influence over Joe Biden's 2020 campaign for president.<ref name="Lizza">{{cite web |last1=Lizza |first1=Ryan |title=Why Biden's Retro Inner Circle Is Succeeding So Far |url=https://www.politico.com/news/magazine/2019/12/19/biden-2020-campaign-president-advisers-087410 |website=Politico |accessdate=17 November 2020 |language=en |date=12 December 2019}}</ref> He helped develop Biden's campaign strategy that had a three-pronged message: "that the election was about the 'soul of the nation'; that the threatened middle class was the 'backbone of the nation'; and that what was most needed was to 'unify the nation.' Only Biden could restore the nation's soul, repair its backbone, and unify it.”<ref name="Lizza" /> "This is really about character and values as opposed to issues and ideology," said Mike Donilon, Biden's chief strategist, when speaking to the ''New York Times''.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Steinberger|first=Michael|date=July 23, 2019|title=Joe Biden Wants to Take America Back to a Time Before Trump|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/07/23/magazine/joe-biden-2020.html|access-date=May 20, 2020|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>
