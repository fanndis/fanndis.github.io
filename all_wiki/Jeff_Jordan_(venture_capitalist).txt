'''Jeff Jordan''' is an American venture capitalist at the [[Silicon Valley]] firm [[Andreessen Horowitz]] and the former President and CEO of [[OpenTable]].<ref name="SF Biz Times">{{cite news|last=Hoge|first=Patrick|title=Jeff Jordan resigns as OpenTable executive chairman|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/sanfrancisco/news/2011/12/20/opentable-executive-chair-jordan-resigns.html|accessdate=14 September 2012|newspaper=San Francisco Business Times|date=20 December 2011}}</ref>

==Education==
Jordan graduated from [[Amherst College]] with a BA in Political Science and Psychology and received an MBA from the [[Stanford University Graduate School of Business]].<ref name=Wealthfront>{{cite web|title=Our Board of Directors |url=https://www.wealthfront.com/board |work=Board of Directors |publisher=Wealthfront |access-date=14 September 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120731074238/https://www.wealthfront.com/board |archive-date=31 July 2012 |df= }}</ref>

==Early career==
From 2004 to 2006, Jordan was the president of [[PayPal]].<ref name="WSJ June 2011">{{cite news|last=McMahan|first=Ty|title=Andreessen Horowitz Hires OpenTable Chairman Jeff Jordan|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2011/06/30/andreessen-horowitz-hires-opentable-chairman-jeff-jordan/|accessdate=14 September 2012|date=30 June 2011}}</ref> Prior to that, Jordan was senior vice president and general manager at [[eBay]] North America for five years,<ref name="WSJ June 2011" /> where he ran eBay.com and led eBay’s acquisitions of PayPal and [[Half.com]].<ref name=VentureBeat>{{cite news|last=Lynley|first=Matthew|title=Andreessen-Horowitz adds former PayPal exec Jeff Jordan|url=https://venturebeat.com/2011/06/30/jeff-jordan-paypal-andreessen-horowitz/|accessdate=14 September 2012|newspaper=VentureBeat|date=30 June 2011}}</ref>  Before eBay, Jordan held various executive roles at [[Hollywood Video|Hollywood Entertainment]] (CFO)<ref name="gsb.stanford.edu">[http://www.gsb.stanford.edu/advisorycouncil/jordan.html Jeffrey D. Jordan, MBA ’87 | Stanford Graduate School of Business]</ref> and [[The Walt Disney Company]]. Prior to his stint at the Walt Disney Company, Jordan was a management consultant at the Boston Consulting Group upon graduating from the Stanford Graduate School of Business.<ref name="PR Newswire">{{cite web|title=Hollywood Entertainment Corporation Hires Jeff Jordan Chief Financial Officer|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hollywood-entertainment-corporation-hires-jeff-jordan-chief-financial-officer-76064822.html|work=Press Release|publisher=PR Newswire|accessdate=14 September 2012}}. </ref>

Jordan was President and CEO of OpenTable from 2007 to 2011.<ref name=Wealthfront /> In May 2009, OpenTable’s initial public offering raised $31.4 million, giving the company a market capitalization of $432 million.<ref name="WSJ May 2009">{{cite news|last=Austin|first=Scott|title=OpenTable IPO Prices At $20, Better Than Expected|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2009/05/20/opentable-ipo-prices-at-20-better-than-expected/|accessdate=14 September 2012|newspaper=The Wall Street Journal|date=20 May 2009}}</ref> OpenTable shares rose 72 percent during its first day of trading on [[NASDAQ]]<ref name="WSJ May 2009" /> and its IPO was considered an “extremely healthy” one during the economic recession.<ref name="TechCrunch May 2009">{{cite news|last=Schonfeld|first=Erick|title=OpenTable Has A Healthy IPO. Shares Shoot Up 40 59 Percent, Market Cap Passes $600 Million|url=https://techcrunch.com/2009/05/21/opentable-has-a-healthy-ipo-shares-shoot-up-40-percent-market-cap-hits-600-million/|accessdate=14 September 2012|newspaper=TechCrunch|date=21 May 2009}}</ref>

Jordan also serves on the board of directors for OpenTable, [[Wealthfront]]<ref name=Wealthfront /> and [[Zoosk]].<ref name=CrunchBase>{{cite web|title=Jeffrey Jordan|url=http://www.crunchbase.com/person/jeffrey-jordan|work=CrunchBase Profile|publisher=CrunchBase|access-date=14 September 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120916004229/http://www.crunchbase.com/person/jeffrey-jordan|archive-date=16 September 2012|df=}}</ref> In June 2017, he joined the Cadre board, a real estate investing company Andreessen Horowitz is investing in.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-06-06/startup-s-ties-to-kushners-came-with-benefits-until-it-got-complicated|title=Startup's Ties to Kushners Came With Benefits, Until It Got Complicated|date=2017-06-06|work=Bloomberg.com|access-date=2017-06-21}}</ref> He's also been an active investor and adviser in Silicon Valley for several years, with involvements in Pure Digital (the maker of the [[Flip Video]] camera later acquired by [[Cisco]]), [[Hotwire.com]] (acquired by [[Expedia]]), Tiny Prints (acquired by [[Shutterfly]]) and [[CafePress]] (which went public in 2012).<ref name="NYT DealBook">{{cite news|last=Rusli|first=Evelyn M.|title=OpenTable's Chairman Joins Andreessen Horowitz|url=https://dealbook.nytimes.com/2011/06/30/opentables-chairman-joins-andreessen-horowitz/|accessdate=14 September 2012|newspaper=The New York Times|date=30 June 2011}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
In April 2012, Jordan along with Andreessen Horowitz General Partners [[Marc Andreessen]], [[Ben Horowitz]], John O’Farrell, [[Scott Weiss]], and [[Peter Levine (venture capitalist)|Peter Levine]] pledged to give half of their lifetime income from venture capital to charity.<ref name=Reuters>{{cite web|last=McBride|first=Sarah|title=Andreessen Horowitz partners pledge income to charity|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2012/04/25/us-andreessenhorowitz-charity-idUSBRE83O1CV20120425|publisher=Reuters|accessdate=3 August 2012}}</ref>
