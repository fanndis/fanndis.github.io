'''Nagavara Ramarao Narayana Murthy''' (born 20 August 1946) is an Indian [[billionaire]] businessman. He is the co-founder of [[Infosys]], and has been the chairman, chief executive officer (CEO), president, and chief mentor of the company before retiring and taking the title chairman emeritus.<ref>{{cite news|title=From disruptors to financial magnates, here are a few IITians who made their mark on Indian and global turf|url=https://www.firstpost.com/business/from-disruptors-to-financial-magnates-here-are-a-few-iitians-who-made-their-mark-on-indian-and-global-turf-7022781.html|publisher=Firstpost|accessdate=22 July 2019}}</ref><ref name="auto1">{{cite news |url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-business/Infosys-chairman-Narayana-Murthy-calls-it-a-day/articleshow/9655929.cms |title=Infosys chairman Narayana Murthy calls it a day |work=The Times of India |date=19 August 2011 |accessdate=19 August 2011}}</ref><ref name="auto">{{cite news |url=http://ibnlive.in.com/news/narayana-murthy-steps-down-as-infosys-chairman/177070-7.html |title=Friday farewell: Narayana Murthy logs out of Infosys |work=IBNLive |date=20 August 2011 |accessdate=20 August 2011}}</ref> 

Murthy was born and raised in [[Sidlaghatta|Shidlaghatta]], [[Karnataka]]. He graduated from [[National Institute of Engineering]], [[University of Mysore]] with a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering and a master's degree from the [[Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur]].

Before starting Infosys, Murthy worked at [[Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad]] as the chief systems programmer, and Patni Computer Systems in Pune (Maharashtra). He started Infosys in 1981 and was the CEO from 1981 to 2002 as well as the chairman from 2002 to 2011. In 2011, he stepped down from the board and became the chairman emeritus. In June 2013, Murthy was appointed as the executive chairman for a period of five years.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/ites/infosys-appoints-narayana-murthy-as-executive-chairman-of-the-board-kv-kamath-steps-down/articleshow/20378690.cms |title=Infosys appoints Narayana Murthy as Executive Chairman of the board, KV Kamath steps down |publisher=[[The Economic Times]] |date=1 June 2013 |accessdate=1 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Narayana Murthy Appointed as Additional Director and Executive Chairman of Infosys Board|url=http://www.btvin.com/videos/watch/321/narayana-murthy-returns-to-revive-infosys|publisher=[[Bloomberg TV India]]}}</ref>

Murthy has been listed among the 12 greatest entrepreneurs of our time by ''Fortune'' magazine.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/business-news/CorporateNews/Narayana-Murthy-is-among-Fortune-s-12-greatest-entrepreneurs/Article1-832212.aspx |title=Narayana Murthy is among Fortune's 12 'greatest entrepreneurs' |publisher=[[Hindustan Times]] |date=28 March 2012 |accessdate=1 June 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120615003345/http://www.hindustantimes.com/business-news/CorporateNews/Narayana-Murthy-is-among-Fortune-s-12-greatest-entrepreneurs/Article1-832212.aspx |archive-date=15 June 2012 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> He has been described as the "father of the Indian IT sector" by ''Time'' magazine for his contribution to outsourcing in India.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/2007/article/0,28804,1652689_1652372_1652367,00.html |title=INDIA'S INFLUENTIALS N.R. Narayana Murthy |publisher=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |accessdate=1 June 2013 |date=15 August 2007}}</ref> Murthy has been honoured with the [[Padma Vibhushan]]<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2008-01-26/news/27713021_1_padma-vibhushan-padma-awards-second-highest-civilian-award|title=LN Mittal, Ratan Tata, Narayana Murthy get Padma Vibhushan|publisher=Economic Times|date=26 January 2008|accessdate=2 June 2013}}</ref> and [[Padma Shri]] awards.<ref name="karnataka" /> He is the father-in-law of [[Rishi Sunak]], the British MP and [[Chancellor of the Exchequer]].

==Early life and education==

Narayana Murthy was born on 20 August 1946 in [[Sidlaghatta|Shidlaghatta]], [[Chikkaballapura district]], [[Karnataka]]<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/he-studied-in-kannada-medium-till-class-10/article1521724.ece | location=Chennai, India | work=The Hindu | first=K.V. | last=Subramanya | title=He studied in Kannada medium till Class 10 | date=9 March 2011}}</ref> into a middle-class [[Brahmin]] family.<ref>https://qz.com/india/1038178/sudha-murthy-wife-of-infosys-founder-nr-narayana-murthy-recalls-being-only-girl-in-her-engineering-college/</ref> After completing his school education, he went to the [[National Institute of Engineering]] and graduated in 1967 with a degree in [[Electrical Engineering]]. In 1969 he received his master's degree from the [[Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://teck.in/narayana-murthys-childhood.html|title=Narayana Murthy's Childhood|accessdate=1 June 2013}}</ref><ref name="YouTube">{{cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6zJNc5KvWg0|title=Narayana Murthy on Achievers' Club|accessdate=1 June 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
Murthy first served as a [[Research Associate]] under a faculty at [[IIM Ahmedabad]] and then later served as the chief systems programmer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.india-seminar.com/2000/485/485%20interview.htm |title=485 Interview with N.R. Narayana Murthy |publisher=India-seminar.com |accessdate=7 March 2013}}</ref> There he worked on India's first time-sharing computer system<ref name="YouTube" /> and designed and implemented a BASIC interpreter for [[Electronics Corporation of India Limited]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.in.com/narayana-murthy/biography-424.html|title=Business Leaders NARAYANA MURTHY|accessdate=2 June 2013}}</ref> He started a company named Softronics. When that company failed after about a year and a half, he joined [[Patni Computer Systems]] in [[Pune]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maheshonline.com/an-interview-with-narayana-murthy/ |title=An Interview With Narayana Murthy |work=maheshone |first=Prannoy |last=Roy |date=29 March 2008 |accessdate=7 March 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111228140208/http://www.maheshonline.com/an-interview-with-narayana-murthy/ |archive-date=28 December 2011 }}</ref>

Murthy and his six software professionals founded [[Infosys]] in 1981<ref name="studyrays">{{cite news|url=http://studyrays.com/info/alltimegreat/business/nr-narayana-murthy.php|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130630091509/http://studyrays.com/info/alltimegreat/business/nr-narayana-murthy.php|url-status=dead|archive-date=30 June 2013|title=NR Narayana Murthy's Profile, Founders of Infosys Technologies|accessdate=1 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.infosys.com/newsroom/press-releases/pages/infosys-announces-leadership-succession.aspx |title=Infosys Announces Leadership Succession |work=Infosys |date=30 April 2011 |accessdate=7 March 2013}}</ref> with an initial capital investment of Rs 10,000, which was provided by his wife [[Sudha Murthy]].<ref name=yentha>{{cite web |url=http://www.yentha.com/news/view/1/13720 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130209210438/http://www.yentha.com/news/view/1/13720 |url-status=dead |archive-date=9 February 2013 |title=Infosys Chairman Narayana Murthy Steps Down |work=yentha.com |date=20 August 2011 |accessdate=20 August 2011 }}</ref> Murthy served as the CEO of Infosys for 21 years from 1981 to 2002<ref name="studyrays" /> and was succeeded by co-founder [[Nandan Nilekani]].<ref name=yentha /> At Infosys he articulated, designed and implemented the Global Delivery Model for IT services outsourcing from India.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.infosys.com/about/management-profiles/Pages/narayana-murthy.aspx|title=Management Profiles, N. R. Narayana Murthy |publisher=Infosys Limited|accessdate=1 June 2013}}</ref> He was the chairman of the board from 2002 to 2006, after which he also became the Chief Mentor.<ref name=yentha /><ref>[http://www.nbcnews.com/id/14411071 Non-Executive Chairman – MSN Report].</ref> In August 2011, he retired from the company, taking the title chairman Emeritus.<ref name="auto1"/><ref name="auto"/>
[[File:Nagavara Ramarao Narayana Murthy.jpg|thumb|N R Narayana Murthy in Ahmedabad in 2015]]

Murthy serves as an independent director on the corporate board of [[HSBC]] and has served as a director on the boards of [[DBS Bank]], [[Unilever]], [[ICICI]] and [[NDTV]].<ref name=NM-B-02>{{cite news| url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/nr-narayana-murthy/ | work=Forbes |title=Forbes profile: N.R. Narayana Murthy |accessdate=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref name=NM-B-01>[http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=398008&ticker=398006 Bloomberg Businessweek profile – N. R. Narayana Murthy CBE]</ref> He also serves as a member of the advisory boards and councils of several educational and philanthropic institutions,<ref name=NM-B-02/><ref name=NM-B-01/> including [[Cornell University]], [[INSEAD]], [[ESSEC]], [[Ford Foundation]], the [[UN Foundation]], the Indo-British Partnership, Asian Institute of Management, a trustee of the [[Infosys Prize]], a trustee of the [[Institute for Advanced Study]] in Princeton, and as a trustee of the Rhodes Trust.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk/page/trustees |title=The Rhodes Trust and Trustees – The Rhodes Trust |publisher=Rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk |accessdate=8 November 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110807173028/http://www.rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk/page/trustees |archive-date= 7 August 2011 }}</ref> He is also the Chairman of the Governing board of [[Public Health Foundation of India]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Narayana Murthyto chair PHFI|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/article2219732.ece|accessdate=19 July 2011|newspaper=The Hindu|date=12 July 2011|location=Chennai, India}}</ref> He serves on the Asia Pacific Advisory Board of [[BT Group plc|British Telecommunications]].<ref name=NM-B-01/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.unfoundation.org/who-we-are/board/n-r-narayana-murthy.html |title=United Nations Foundation – N. R. Narayana Murthy (India) |publisher=Unfoundation.org |date=20 August 1946 |accessdate=8 November 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120308113141/http://www.unfoundation.org/who-we-are/board/n-r-narayana-murthy.html |archive-date=8 March 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2005 he co-chaired the [[World Economic Forum]] in [[Davos]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/money/2005/jan/31spec.htm |title=How to build a great company |publisher=Rediff.com |date=31 January 2005 |accessdate=8 November 2011}}</ref>

On 1st June 2013, Murthy returned to Infosys as the Executive chairman and Additional Director. On 12th June 2014 it was announced that Murthy would step down as the Executive chairman effectively on 14th June. He would continue as the Non-Executive chairman until 10th October. On 11th October, Murthy was to be designated as the chairman Emeritus.<ref name="bp">{{cite web|title=Vishal Sikka to be CEO and MD of Infosys, Murthy Non-Executive Chairman|date=12 June 2014|url=http://news.biharprabha.com/2014/06/vishal-sikka-to-be-ceo-and-md-of-infosys-from-august-1/|publisher=news.biharprabha.com|accessdate=12 June 2014}}</ref>

Murthy also serves on the strategic board which advises the national law firm, [[Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas]], on strategic, policy and governance issues.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Vyas|first1=Maulik|title=Cyril Shroff ropes in business luminaries like Narayana Murthy, Deepak Parekh, Uday Kotak and others for advisory board of his law firm|url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/services/consultancy-/-audit/cyril-shroff-ropes-in-business-luminaries-like-narayana-murthy-deepak-parekh-uday-kotak-and-others-for-advisory-board-of-his-law-firm/articleshow/48417617.cms|accessdate=10 August 2015|work=The Economic Times}}</ref> He is also a member of [[IESE Business School|IESE's]] International Advisory Board (IAB).<ref>[https://www.iese.edu/about/leadership/ ''Members of IESE's International Advisory Board''], iese.edu</ref>

In 2016, Murthy spoke with Harvard Business Review Ascend<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hbrascend.org/topics/how-to-be-a-better-manager-an-interview-with-n-r-narayana-murthy/ |title=How To Be a Better Manager – An Interview with N. R. Narayana Murthy
 |publisher=hbrascend.org |date=26 November 2017 }}</ref> on the subject of "How To Be a Better Manager", where he shared tips that he has learned from his own life.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hbrascend.org/topics/how-to-be-a-better-manager-an-interview-with-n-r-narayana-murthy/|title=How To Be a Better Manager - An Interview with N. R. Narayana Murthy|website=hbrascend.org|language=en|access-date=2020-02-14}}</ref>

In 2017, Murthy raised concerns over alleged corporate governance lapses at Infosys, however the company went on to deny these claims.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.livemint.com/Companies/eeK2B7ISo6JWXZlSBnecMO/Infosys-directors-said-Sikka-not-CEO-material-Narayana-Murth.html|title=Narayana Murthy raises issues at Infosys again|last=Sood|first=Anirban Sen,Varun|date=2017-08-18|website=Livemint|language=en|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref>

==Personal life==
His wife, [[Sudha Murthy]], is a social worker and author, and does philanthropic work through the Infosys Foundation.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.livemint.com/2009/12/18203315/Gururaj-Deshpande--The-serial.html |title=Gururaj Deshpande – The serial entrepreneur |author=Venkatesha Babu |newspaper=[[Mint (newspaper)|Mint]] |publisher=[[HT Media Ltd]] |date=18 December 2009 |accessdate=22 March 2011}}</ref> 

Murthy has two children, a son [[Rohan Murty]] and a daughter Akshata Murthy.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.indiatoday.com/itoday/20050221/power4.html |title=India Today 2005 Power List |publisher=Indiatoday.com |date=21 February 2005 |accessdate=8 November 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090830101733/http://www.indiatoday.com/itoday/20050221/power4.html |archive-date=30 August 2009 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> In June 2013, Rohan joined Infosys as an executive assistant to his father.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/ites/rohan-murty-to-join-infosys-as-narayana-murthys-executive-assistant/articleshow/20379563.cms | work=The Economic Times | title=Rohan Murty to join Infosys as Narayana Murthy's executive assistant | date=1 June 2013}}</ref> He left Infosys in June 2014.<ref name="bp"/> Akshata is married to [[Rishi Sunak]] since 2009, and he is the UK's [[Chancellor of the Exchequer]] and [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] MP for [[Richmond (Yorks) (UK Parliament constituency)|Richmond, Yorkshire]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.weddingsutra.com/celeb_wed/celeb_akshata.asp | work=WeddingSutra | title=Akshata Murthy and Rishi Sunak's simple no tamasha, traditional wedding | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130713082105/http://www.weddingsutra.com/celeb_wed/celeb_akshata.asp | archive-date=13 July 2013 | df=dmy-all }}</ref>
