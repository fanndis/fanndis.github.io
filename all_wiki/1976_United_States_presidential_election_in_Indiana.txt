The '''1976 United States presidential election in Indiana''' was held on November 2, 1976. Incumbent President [[Gerald Ford]] won the state of [[Indiana]] with 53% of the vote, carrying the state's 13 electoral votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uselectionatlas.org/RESULTS/state.php?f=0&fips=18&year=1976|title=1976 Presidential General Election Results - Indiana|accessdate=May 12, 2016}}</ref> He defeated [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] candidate Jimmy Carter in Indiana by a margin of 7.62 points.

==Analysis==
Ford won Indiana by a comfortable margin, of 7.62%. Carter did, however, make substantial headway in Indiana, significantly improving from [[George McGovern]]'s [[1972 United States presidential election in Indiana|1972]] performance in the state. Ford did well throughout most parts of the state. Carter managed to keep Ford's victory in Indiana under ten points by winning most of Southern Indiana. [[Vigo County, Indiana|Vigo County]] home to [[Terre Haute, Indiana|Terre Haute]], is a bellwether county that has gotten every presidential election right, except twice, since [[1892 United States presidential election in Indiana|1892]]. This election was no exception. Carter won Vigo County, and the presidency.

{{As of|2020|11|alt=As of the [[2020 United States presidential election|2020 presidential election]]}}, this is the last election in which [[Dearborn County, Indiana|Dearborn County]], [[Dubois County, Indiana|Dubois County]], [[Martin County, Indiana|Martin County]], [[Ohio County, Indiana|Ohio County]], and [[Owen County, Indiana|Owen County]] voted for a Democratic Presidential candidate.<ref name="how">Sullivan, Robert David; [http://www.americamagazine.org/content/unconventional-wisdom/how-red-and-blue-map-evolved-over-past-century ‘How the Red and Blue Map Evolved Over the Past Century’]; ''America Magazine'' in ''The National Catholic Review''; June 29, 2016</ref>

==Hillary Clinton’s role in the Carter campaign==
One of the Carter campaign's state organizers in Indiana was [[Hillary Clinton|Hillary Rodham]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.indystar.com/story/news/politics/behind-closed-doors/2015/10/02/hillary-clinton-indiana-basketball-indianoplace-email/73218878/ |title='Indianoplace'? That's what Hillary Clinton called us |last=Groppe |first=Maureen |date=October 7, 2015 |website=www.indystar.com |publisher=Indy Star |access-date=December 29, 2017 |quote=}}</ref> Rodham (who would later adopt [[Bill Clinton|her husband]]'s surname of ''Clinton'') would go on to serve as a [[First Lady of the United States|First Lady]], Senator and [[United States Secretary of State|Secretary of State]]. Forty years later, during [[Hillary Clinton 2016 presidential campaign|her turn as the Democratic nominee for president]], Carter gave a speech in support of her candidacy saying, "During my 1976 presidential run, a young woman moved to Indianapolis to help turn out the Indiana vote for me. Forty years later, I will proudly cast my vote for that same woman to be the next President of the United States."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://politics.blog.ajc.com/2016/08/30/jimmy-carter-makes-fund-raising-plea-for-hillary-clinton/ |title=Jimmy Carter makes fund raising plea for Hillary Clinton |last=Sheinin |first=Aaron Gould |date=August 30, 2016 |website=www.ajc.com |publisher=AJC |access-date=December 29, 2017 |quote=}}</ref>
