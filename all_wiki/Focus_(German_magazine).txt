'''''Focus''''' (styled as '''''FOCUS''''') is a [[German language|German-language]] [[news magazine]] published by Hubert Burda Media.<ref>{{cite web|title=15 Jahre Fakten, Fakten, Fakten|periodical=Focus Online|url=http://www.focus.de/politik/deutschland/focus_aid_233990.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2008-01-18|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Focus feiert 20-jähriges Bestehen|periodical=Der Standard|url=http://derstandard.at/1356427624828/Focus-feiert-20-jaehriges-Bestehen|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2013-01-14|language=de}}</ref> Established in 1993 as an alternative to the ''[[Der Spiegel]]'' weekly news magazine,<ref>{{citation|surname1=Ekkehard Kohrs|periodical=Bonner General-Anzeiger|title=Burdas Kampfansage an Augstein|page=3|date=1993-01-19|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Rainer Hoffmann|periodical=Neue Zürcher Zeitung|title=Bunter Spiegel|date=1993-01-21|language=de
}}</ref> since 2015 the editorial staff has been headquartered in Germany's capital of [[Berlin]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Nachrichtenmagazin: Focus zieht nach Berlin um|periodical=Spiegel Online|url=http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/gesellschaft/nachrichtenmagazin-focus-zieht-nach-berlin-a-1052314.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2015-09-10|language=de}}</ref> Alongside Spiegel and [[Stern (magazine)|Stern]], Focus is one of the three most widely circulated German weeklies.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Ein starkes Führungs-Trio|page=66|date=2003-11-20|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Nordkurier|title=Focus, Spiegel, Stern: Die Großen im tiefen Wandel|page=25|date=2014-08-27|language=de
}}</ref> The concept originated from [[Hubert Burda]] and [[Helmut Markwort]],<ref>{{citation|surname1=Heidrun Plewe|periodical=Horizont|title=Wenige glaubten zunächst an den Erfolg|page=20|date=1993-12-17|language=de
}}</ref> who went from being Editor-in-chief to become publisher in 2009 and since 2017 has been listed in the publication's masthead as Founding Editor-in-chief.<ref>{{cite web|title=Focus-Chefredakteur Markwort geht|periodical=Zeit Online|url=http://www.zeit.de/wirtschaft/2009-10/focus-markwort-chefredakteur|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2009-10-29|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ende einer Ära: Magazin-Gründer Helmut Markwort gibt Focus-Herausgeberschaft ab|periodical=Meedia|url=http://meedia.de/2017/01/17/ende-einer-aera-magazin-gruender-helmut-markwort-gibt-herausgeberschaft-des-focus-ab/|access-date=2017-07-18|last=Marvin Schade|date=2017-01-17|language=de}}</ref> The current Editor-in-chief of Focus as of March 2016 is [[Robert Schneider (journalist)|Robert Schneider]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Neuer Chefredakteur für den Focus|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|url=http://www.tagesspiegel.de/medien/robert-schneider-folgt-auf-ulrich-reitz-neuer-chefredakteur-fuer-den-focus/12860868.html|access-date=2016-10-30|last=Sonja Álvarez|date=2016-01-21|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Neuer Focus-Chef kommt von der Super Illu|periodical=Spiegel Online|url=http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/gesellschaft/focus-robert-schneider-wird-neuer-chefredakteur-a-1073147.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2016-01-21|language=de}}</ref>

== History ==
Under the code name "Zugmieze", work commenced on Focus in the summer of 1991.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Kress Report|title=Eine Zugmieze macht Karriere|page=8|date=2013-01-10|language=de
}}</ref> In October 1992, [[Hubert Burda Media]] announced plans for a new weekly news magazine.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Focus: Burda will es wissen|page=46|date=1992-10-09|language=de
}}</ref> Observers initially gave the project only little chance for success.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Harald Kurz|periodical=Horizont|title=Zugmieze: Zugpferd oder Katzenjammer?|page=56|date=1992-09-18|language=de
}}</ref> Several attempts of other publishers to establish a competitor to Spiegel and Stern magazines had previously failed. The first edition arrived on the newsstands on January 18, 1993 and was already sold out on the next day.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Uli Baur|periodical=Focus Magazin|title=Das war unser Start|date=2012-03-19|language=de|url=http://www.focus.de/magazin/memo/memo-des-chefredakteurs-das-war-unser-start_aid_725162.html|access-date= 2016-10-30
}}</ref> The subline of the Focus was "the modern news magazine", and Helmut Markwort became the magazine's first Editor-in-chief.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Heinz Pürer, Johannes Raabe|title=Presse in Deutschland|edition=3.|publisher=UVK|publication-place=Konstanz|page=263|isbn=978-3-8252-8334-6|date=2007|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Frankfurter Neue Presse|title=Ein Mann der Fakten|page=1|date=2011-12-08|language=de
}}</ref> The lead story on the alleged comeback of [[Hans-Dietrich Genscher]] as the successor of [[Richard von Weizsäcker]] in the office of the Federal President was later proven to be a canard.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Klaus Schmeh|title=David gegen Goliath: 33 überraschende Unternehmenserfolge|publisher=Redline|publication-place=Frankfurt am Main|page=[https://archive.org/details/davidgegengoliat0000schm/page/34 34]|isbn=3-8323-1057-6|date=2004|language=de|url=https://archive.org/details/davidgegengoliat0000schm/page/34}}</ref> There was an equal mix of positive and negative reviews: Whereas journalists tended to take a critical view towards Focus, advertisers had consistently favorable responses.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Brennpunkt Focus am Montag|page=2|date=1993-01-22|language=de
}}</ref> Observers saw in Focus above all a challenger of Spiegel, whereas the publisher viewed US magazines such as [[Newsweek]] and [[Time (magazine)|Time]] as its role model.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Saarbrücker Zeitung|title=Focus kontra Spiegel|date=1993-01-16|language=de
}}</ref>

After five issues, Focus had some 15,000 subscribers, and with circulation of over 300,000 copies sold, it was a commercial success from the beginning.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Heidrun Plewe|periodical=Horizont|title=Focus liegt zur Zeit gut im Plan|page=25|date=1993-02-19|language=de
}}</ref> The magazine played a decisive role in the expansion of Hubert Burda Media's market position.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Burda kostet Focus-Erfolg aus|page=6|date=1994-04-01|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Handelsblatt|title=Investitionen kräftig ausgeweitet: Verlag auf der Focus-Welle|page=16|date=1994-07-22|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Burda-Konzern wächst im letzten Jahr dank Focus|date=1995-05-12|language=de
}}</ref> In mid-1994, a Dutch court ruled that due to brand disputes, Focus was no longer allowed to be sold in [[Belgium]], the [[Netherlands]] and [[Luxembourg]].<ref>{{citation|periodical=Deutscher Drucker|title=Focus nicht mehr in den Benelux-Laender|page=4|date=1994-08-25|language=de
}}</ref> Unfazed by this, Focus continued its growth strategy,<ref>{{citation|periodical=Deutscher Drucker|title=Nachrichtemagazine: Burda bleibt mit Focus auf Erfolgskurs|page=4|date=1994-08-04|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Peter Turi|periodical=Horizont|title=Mit Fakten, Fakten auf dem Weg zur Spitze|page=70|date=1995-03-17|language=de
}}</ref> later even occasionally eclipsing Spiegel in advertising sales.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Die Tageszeitung|title=Fakten zu Focus|page=14|date=1995-12-01|language=de
}}</ref> Burda further enhanced its international profile, for example, through a cooperation agreement with the US media group, [[Ziff Davis]].<ref>{{citation|periodical=Deutscher Drucker|title=Burdas Focus-Verlag kooperiert mit Ziff-Davis|page=6|date=1994-07-07|language=de
}}</ref> The year 1996 saw the launch of the Internet portal, [[Focus Online]], and under the name of [[Focus TV]], the group entered into production of TV content.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Thomas Voigt|periodical=Horizont|title=Markwort gebiert weitere Focus-Kinder|page=36|date=1996-01-19|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Focus-Online-Währung|page=12|date=1996-01-12|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Isabella Hofmann|periodical=Wirtschaftsblatt|title=Focus bald auch im TV: Neues Fernsehmagazin startet im März auf Pro7|page=7|date=1996-02-06|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Focus TV liefert Beiträge für Frauensender tm3|date=1996-08-08|language=de
}}</ref> Focus developed into one of the leading German news magazines.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Barbara Held|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Ende des Amüsements|page=31|date=1997-11-30|language=de
}}</ref> In 1997, its publisher, Hubert Burda, was lauded for the magazine's "creative and groundbreaking innovations", among other accolades.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Hubert Burda erhält den Medienpreis für Focus|page=108|date=1997-10-30|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Darmstädter Echo|title=Focus ist Hubert Burdas Erfolgsstory|date=2000-02-09|language=de
}}</ref>

Over the first five years of its existence, Focus repeatedly created a sensation with its interviews of key celebrities, e.g. in 1996 with real estate mogul Jürgen Schneider after his arrest, or in 1997 with Leo Kirch in the aftermath of his media group's collapse.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Focus|title=5 Jahre Focus|pages=160–161|date=1998-01-12|language=de
}}</ref> The Focus concept was also successfully adapted outside of Europe,<ref>{{citation|periodical=Wirtschaftswoche|title=Focus auf Weltkurs|page=66|date=1998-06-18|language=de
}}</ref> e.g. in the form of Época magazine by the Brazilian media house [[Grupo Globo]].<ref>{{citation|periodical=Welt am Sonntag|title=Erfolgsrezept verkauft: Focus do Brasil|page=54|date=1998-04-26|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Burda vergibt Lizenz für Focus nach Brasilien|page=6|date=1998-04-30|language=de
}}</ref> At the beginning of 1999, Focus for the first time achieved higher readership than Spiegel,<ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Platztausch: Focus erreicht mittlerweile mehr Leser als der Spiegel|page=39|date=1999-01-28|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Frankfurter Rundschau|title=Media-Analyse: Focus überholt den Spiegel|page=8|date=1999-01-28|language=de
}}</ref> and in the years to follow, managed to further expand its lead position.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Sächsische Zeitung|title=Media-Analyse: Focus vergrößert Vorsprung|page=17|date=2001-01-25|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Klaus Koch|periodical=[[Südkurier]]|title=Totgesagt und sehr lebendig|date=2003-01-13|language=de
}}</ref> The year 2000 saw the launch of [[Focus Money (magazine)|Focus Money]], an off-shoot of the news magazine, dedicated to business and financial topics.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Deutscher Drucker|title=Focus Verlag plant ein zweites Magazin|page=2|date=1999-10-14|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Ulrike Simon|title=Geld, Geld, Geld: Focus legt sich heute mit Focus Money einen Ableger zu|page=43|date=2000-03-30|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Das Magazin für den Positiv-Denker|page=50|date=2001-05-10|language=de
}}</ref> In 2001, a division for Internet topics was created at Focus,<ref>{{citation|periodical=Die Welt|title=Euphorie bei Focus: Mehr Leser, neues Ressort|page=33|date=2001-02-01|language=de
}}</ref> in addition to pooling Internet activities of Focus Digital in the joint venture, [[Tomorrow Focus]].<ref>{{citation|periodical=Handelsblatt|title=Tomorrow und Focus Digital fusionieren|page=11|date=2001-08-09|language=de
}}</ref> Thus, the Focus magazine publishing company was once again primarily responsible for printed publications, yet participated in Tomorrow Focus.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Financial Times Deutschland|title=Die Firma|page=36|date=2001-01-10|language=de
}}</ref>

In 2004 the first personnel changes occurred at Focus:<ref>{{citation|surname1=Martin-Werner Buchenau|periodical=Handelsblatt|title=Burda regelt Markwort-Nachfolge|page=18|date=2004-11-10|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Cathrin Hegner, Volker Schütz|periodical=Horizont|title=Ich wollte die Nachfolge präjudizieren|page=38|date=2004-11-18|language=de
}}</ref> In addition to his duties as Editor-in-chief, Helmut Markwort also assumed the position of publisher, and [[Uli Baur (journalist)|Uli Baur]] was promoted from Deputy Editor-in-chief to Editor-in-chief.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Pierre Schrader|periodical=Horizont|title=Mehr Markwort für Focus|page=14|date=2004-11-11|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Focus|title=Focus mit erweiterter Führung|pages=161–161|date=2004-11-15|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Focus-Tandem|page=31|date=2004-11-10|language=de
}}</ref> Under the new leadership, in 2005 Focus teamed up with Hörverlag, an audio publisher, to create the download portal Claudio for audio books.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Miriam Hebben|periodical=Horizont|title=Printmarken hören auf Claudio.de|page=42|date=2005-12-08|language=de
}}</ref> In 2006, Focus became embroiled in the journalist scandal of the Bundesnachrichtendienst, the German Intelligence Services.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Thüringer Allgemeine|title=Hausfriedensbruch und Kollegenverrat|date=2006-05-13|language=de
}}</ref> In exchange for money and benefits in kind, several journalists had offered the intelligence agency to collect and disclose information on investigative journalists and their sources.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Eric Gujer|periodical=Neue Zürcher Zeitung|title=Deutsche Journalisten als willige Helfer: Kritik im Geheimbericht zur BND-Affäre an den Medien|page=3|date=2006-05-24|language=de
}}</ref> The [[Federal Intelligence Service (Germany)|German Intelligence Services]] in turn put Focus journalists under observation as informants.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Frank Jansen|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Die Spitzel vom Dienst|page=2|date=2006-05-13|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Christian Rath|periodical=Badische Zeitung|title=Geheimdienst sucht Leck|date=2006-05-19|language=de
}}</ref> Irrespective of the public debate surrounding the affair, Focus continued its development, yet like all news magazines, was grappling with declining circulation.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Marie Waldburg|periodical=Bunte|title=15 Jahre Focus|page=104|date=2008-01-24|language=de
}}</ref> At the end of 2009, it was announced that Helmut Markwort was stepping down as Editor-in-chief.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Hans-Peter Siebenhaar|periodical=Handelsblatt|title=Weimer löst Markwort bei Focus ab|page=15|date=2009-10-30|language=de
}}</ref> [[Wolfram Weimer]], founder of the political magazine Cicero, was appointed as his successor.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Focus|title=Neues Führungs-Duo für Focus|pages=156–156|date=2009-11-02|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Silja Elfers|periodical=Horizont|title=Konservativ, klug und umsichtig|page=10|date=2009-11-05|language=de
}}</ref> Observers saw in Markwort's departure a fundamental "change in direction".<ref>{{citation|surname1=Joachim Huber|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Richtungswechsel: Helmut Markwort gibt Focus-Chefredaktion ab, Wolfram Weimer kommt|page=31|date=2009-10-30|language=de
}}</ref> Even prior to Weimer's taking up his duties, the publisher staged a relaunch of Focus.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Andrea Rungg|periodical=Financial Times Deutschland|title=Burda wechselt Markwort bei Focus aus|page=2|date=2009-10-30|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Spiegel|title=Schneller Umbau beim Focus|page=103|date=2009-11-02|language=de
}}</ref>

After only one year, Weimer left Focus and his position was left unfilled, which lead to Baur becoming the sole Editor-in-chief.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Welt kompakt|title=Abgang nach nur einem Jahr|page=31|date=2011-07-27|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Jürgen Scharrer|periodical=Horizont|title=Rückschlag für Focus|page=2|date=2011-07-28|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Hans-Peter Kastenhuber|periodical=Nürnberger Nachrichten|title=Debatten-Focus ist gescheitert|page=24|date=2011-07-30|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Wolfram Weimer verlässt Chefredaktion|periodical=Spiegel Online|url=http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/gesellschaft/wochenmagazin-focus-wolfram-weimer-verlaesst-chefredaktion-a-776656.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2011-07-26|language=de}}</ref> According to media reports, publisher Markwort and Baur had "rejected" the new direction of the Focus and "ultimately obstructed it more and more heavily". Weimer was said to have wanted to position the magazine as "more up-market and more political".<ref>{{cite web|title=Focus: Chefredakteur Weimer gibt auf|periodical=Süddeutsche Zeitung|url=http://www.sueddeutsche.de/medien/machtkampf-um-nachrichtenmagazin-focus-chefredakteur-weimer-gibt-auf-1.1124695|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2011-07-26|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Berliner Zeitung|title=Erfolg des Alten|page=30|date=2011-07-27|language=de
}}</ref> The paid circulation of single copies "more and more heavily" declined under Weimer's and Baur's leadership, dropping below the 100,000 mark.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Joachim Frank|periodical=Frankfurter Rundschau|title=Baur gegen Weimer|page=36|date=2011-07-21|language=de
}}</ref> In 2013, [[Jörg Quoos]] took over as Editor-in-chief of the magazine,<ref>{{cite web|title=Jörg Quoos: Eine Reform für den Focus|periodical=Hamburger Abendblatt|url=http://www.abendblatt.de/kultur-live/article112435868/Joerg-Quoos-Eine-Reform-fuer-den-Focus.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2013-07-07|language=de}}</ref> and Baur became publisher.<ref>{{cite web|title=Uli Baur: Es geht um klaren Journalismus|periodical=Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung|url=https://www.faz.net/aktuell/feuilleton/medien/im-gespraech-uli-baur-es-geht-um-klaren-journalismus-11746909.html|access-date=2016-10-30|last=Michael Hanfeld|date=2012-05-10|language=de}}</ref> Quoos gave Focus a more political orientation and, in particular, reduced the share of practical tips.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Birte Bühnen|periodical=Kress Report|title=Focus: Jörg Quoos setzt politische Akzente|page=11|date=2013-01-25|language=de
}}</ref> For example, the magazine ran an exposé on soccer manager Uli Hoeness's voluntary disclosure of his tax evasion and landed the scoop on the so-called "Nazi-era treasure trove" of art collector [[Cornelius Gurlitt (art collector)|Cornelius Gurlitt]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Fußball: Steuerermittlungen gegen Hoeneß nach Selbstanzeige|periodical=Focus Online|url=http://www.focus.de/sport/fussball/fussball-steuerermittlungen-gegen-hoeness-nach-selbstanzeige_aid_966240.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2013-04-20|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Britta Schultejans|periodical=Aachener Nachrichten|title=Wertvoller Kunstschatz mit dunkler Vergangenheit|date=2014-11-03|language=de
}}</ref> In 2014, Ulrich Reitz followed as new Editor-in-chief, as the publisher and Quoos had a difference in opinion on the future direction of the magazine.<ref>{{cite web|title=Chefredakteurs-Karussell: Jörg Quoos muss beim Focus gehen, Ulrich Reitz kommt|periodical=Meedia|url=http://meedia.de/2014/08/26/breaking-joerg-quoos-verlaesst-den-focus-neuer-chefredakteur-wird-ulrich-reitz/|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2014-08-26|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Focus wechselt Chefredakteur aus|periodical=Zeit Online|url=http://www.zeit.de/wirtschaft/2014-08/focus-chefredakteur-quoos-reitz|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2014-08-26|language=de}}</ref> Among other steps, Reitz completed the process initiated under Quoos, relocating Focus from [[Munich]] to Berlin in the year 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Warum der Focus nach Berlin umzieht|periodical=Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung|url=https://www.faz.net/aktuell/feuilleton/medien/das-magazin-focus-zieht-von-muenchen-nach-berlin-13795693.html|access-date=2016-10-30|last=Michael Hanfeld|date=2015-09-10|language=de}}</ref> Only a small editorial staff remained in the Bavarian state capital.<ref>{{cite web|title=Der Focus zieht zu großen Teilen nach Berlin|periodical=DWDL|url=http://www.dwdl.de/nachrichten/52564/der_focus_zieht_zu_grossen_teilen_nach_berlin/|access-date=2016-10-30|last=Uwe Mantel|date=2015-09-10|language=de}}</ref> In 2016, there was again a change at the head of the editorial staff:<ref>{{citation|periodical=Welt kompakt|title=Wieder ein Wechsel beim Focus|page=10|date=2016-01-22|language=de
}}</ref> Robert Schneider, formerly Editor-in-chief of [[Superillu]],<ref>{{citation|periodical=Hamburger Abendblatt|title=Super Illu-Chefredakteur leitet bald den Focus|page=22|date=2016-01-22|language=de
}}</ref> replaced Reitz, who remained until the end of the year, responsible for the topics of politics and debate.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Imre Grimm|periodical=Hannoversche Allgemeine Zeitung|title=Aus für Reitz: Chefwechsel beim Focus|page=19|date=2016-01-22|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|surname1=Ulrike Simon|periodical=Horizont|title=Der Nächste, bitte!|page=4|date=2016-01-28|language=de
}}</ref>

In 2017, Focus announced the closure of its offices in Munich and [[Düsseldorf]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Focus verlässt München und baut Stellen ab|periodical=Horizont|url=http://www.horizont.net/medien/nachrichten/Hubert-Burda-Media-Focus-verlaesst-Muenchen-und-baut-Stellen-ab-156961|access-date=2017-05-22|last=David Hein|date=2017-03-30|language=de}}</ref> along with reorganizations in the editorial staff.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nachrichtenmagazin: Focus schließt Büros in München und Düsseldorf|periodical=Handelsblatt|url=https://www.handelsblatt.com/unternehmen/it-medien/nachrichtenmagazin-focus-schliesst-bueros-in-muenchen-und-duesseldorf/19593844.html|access-date=2017-05-22|date=2017-03-30|language=de}}</ref> Since then, the magazine is now created completely in Berlin.<ref>{{cite web|title=Focus schließt Büros in München und Düsseldorf|periodical=DWDL|url=https://www.dwdl.de/nachrichten/60820/focus_schliet_bros_in_mnchen_und_dsseldorf/|access-date=2017-05-22|last=Alexander Krei|date=2017-03-30|language=de}}</ref> Later in the year, Hubert Burda Media launched a broad-based reader campaign entitled "Menschen im Focus" (people in focus).<ref>{{cite web|title=Leserkampagne Menschen im Focus: Wie Burda dem Wochenmagazin ein neues Image verpasst|periodical=Meedia|url=http://meedia.de/2017/05/11/leserkampagne-menschen-im-focus-wie-burda-dem-wochenmagazin-ein-neues-image-verpasst/|access-date=2017-05-22|last=Gregory Lipinski|date=2017-05-11|language=de}}</ref>

== Circulation ==
Focus is one of the most widely circulated news magazines and has a proportionally large share in the [[advertising market]].<ref name="Kaltenhäuser">{{citation|surname1=Bettina Kaltenhäuser|title=Abstimmung am Kiosk|publisher=Springer|pages=93–96|isbn=3-8244-4617-0|date=2005|language=de
}}</ref> Like Spiegel and Stern, Focus has sustained significant declines over the past years, however, dropping from 782,685 to 438,055 sold copies from the 4th quarter of 1998 to the 2nd quarter of 2017.<ref name="IVW" /> Individual sales experience relatively wide fluctuation, depending upon the cover story. The share of subscriptions in the paid circulation is currently roughly 37%, recently increasing slightly.<ref name="IVW">{{cite web|title=Quartalsauflagen|publisher=Informationsgemeinschaft zur Feststellung der Verbreitung von Werbeträgern|url=http://www.ivw.eu/print/quartalsauflagen/quartalsauflagen|access-date=2016-10-30|language=de}}</ref>

== Profile ==
While Focus positioned itself as a news magazine alongside Spiegel, the latter in turn decidedly distanced itself from Focus, dismissively referring to it as the "Munich Illustrated", among other things.<ref name="Kaltenhäuser" /> Both publications differ in their editorial approaches:<ref>{{citation|surname1=Rudolf Walter Leonhardt|periodical=Die Zeit|title=Focus und Spiegel im Vergleich|date=1997-05-09|language=de|url=http://www.zeit.de/1997/20/spiegelf.txt.19970509.xml|access-date= 2016-10-30
}}</ref> Spiegel puts an emphasis on complex political, economic and social analysis, whereas Focus, in addition to [[Political journalism|political reporting]], also caters to topics of everyday relevance from the realms of family, health, finances and career.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Horizont|title=Die aktuelle Woche im Focus|page=38|date=1994-01-21|language=de
}}</ref> However, Spiegel has recently published more articles featuring practical tips, modeled after Focus.<ref>{{cite web|title=Spiegel|periodical=turi2|url=http://www.turi2.de/tag/spiegel/|access-date=2017-08-03|language=de}}</ref> With comparatively shorter texts, more graphics, more impactful visuals and ranking lists, readers are offered information that is easier to absorb.<ref name="Kaltenhäuser" /> From the beginning, journalism offering practical tips has been an essential element of Focus.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Jürgen Scharrer|periodical=Horizont|title=Focus traut sich mehr als gedacht|page=10|date=2010-01-28|language=de
}}</ref> According to the news magazine's own statements, its target group includes people who "are involved in society, in politics and in their professions, thanks to their active processing of information and especially due to their strong appetite for information".<ref>{{citation|periodical=Handelsblatt|title=Neues Nachrichtenmagazin heißt Focus|page=26|date=1992-10-06|language=de
}}</ref> When the magazine launched, observers referred to this group as "info elites".<ref>{{citation|surname1=Mathias Bröckers|periodical=Die Tageszeitung|title=Neues vom Infoismus|page=16|date=1993-01-19|language=de
}}</ref> Focus is generally considered to be in the mainstream [[Civic political culture|civil political spectrum]].<ref>{{citation|surname1=Katja Hertin, Joachim Huber|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Was ist ein nichtlinkes Magazin, Herr Markwort?|page=26|date=1998-01-14|language=de
}}</ref>

== Criticism ==
Major controversy erupted over a Focus story on a mission involving Germany's elite counter-terror unit, [[GSG 9]], in the village of [[Bad Kleinen]], leading to staff offices being searched in January 1994.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Nürnberger Nachrichten|title=Justiz ermittelt gegen den Focus|date=1994-01-13|language=de
}}</ref> Law enforcement authorities investigated the magazine because confidential sources had been quoted, e.g. an assessment report from the [[Federal Criminal Police Office (Germany)|Federal Criminal Police Office]], the Bundeskriminalamt, and the pocket agenda of the killed [[Red Army Faction|RAF]] terrorist, [[Wolfgang Grams]]. According to information by Stern, documents found in Focus offices provided the decisive clue to identifying the informant.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Gerd Rosenkranz|periodical=Die Tageszeitung|title=Verraten und verkauft|pages=16–17|date=1996-10-09|language=de
}}</ref>

In 1995, Focus reported on business difficulties involving the Hamburg private bank, Mody.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Patricia Werner|periodical=Stern|title=Da können viele ihr Geld verlieren|date=1996-03-28|language=de
}}</ref> As a result, it experienced a [[bank run]] by many depositors, and one day after the magazine article appeared, the bank had to close its doors until further notice.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Spiegel|title=Banken: Groß fahrlässig|date=1995-10-23|language=de|url=http://www.spiegel.de/spiegel/print/d-9223510.html|access-date= 2016-10-30
}}</ref> The shareholders subsequently held Focus directly responsible for the illiquidity.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Focus soll für Mody-Aktionäre zahlen|date=1996-02-22|language=de
}}</ref> The Press Tribunal of the Hamburg District Court concurred, ordering Focus to pay restitution for damages.<ref>{{citation|periodical=Börsen-Zeitung|title=Mody Bank: Schwierige Liquidation|page=11|date=1997-07-23|language=de
}}</ref> In appeal proceedings, this decision was reversed by Hamburg's Higher Regional Court,<ref>{{citation|periodical=Die Welt|title=Kein Schadenersatz für Mody-Bank|page=38|date=1999-03-31|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Handelsblatt|title=Focus muß nicht für Pleite zahlen|page=4|date=1999-03-31|language=de
}}</ref> and Germany's [[Federal Court of Justice]] denied the plaintiff's appeal on points of law.<ref>{{cite web|title=Focus gewinnt Prozess gegen Mody Privatbank|periodical=New Business|url=http://www.new-business.de/medien/detail.php?nr=29722|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2002-10-15|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Focus gewinnt Prozess gegen Mody Privatbank|periodical=Horizont|url=http://www.horizont.net/medien/nachrichten/-Focus-gewinnt-Prozess-gegen-Mody-Privatbank-40346|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2002-10-15|language=de}}</ref> Focus welcomed the end of the legal disputes as a "victory for freedom of the press".<ref>{{citation|periodical=Focus|title=Prozess: Sieg für die Pressefreiheit|date=2002-10-21|language=de|url=http://www.focus.de/finanzen/news/prozess-sieg-fuer-die-pressefreiheit_aid_208324.html|access-date= 2016-10-30
}}</ref>

A study at the [[TU Dresden]] university from the year 2014 involved research on the synchronization of news and advertising. Researchers found that, both in Spiegel and Focus, reporting on companies was more frequent, friendlier and more frequently mentioned products, the more the companies purchased advertising.<ref>{{citation|surname1=Lutz M. Hagen, Anne Flämig, Anne-Marie In der Au|periodical=Publizistik: Vierteljahreshefte für Kommunikationsforschung|title=Synchronisation von Nachricht und Werbung: Wie das Anzeigenaufkommen von Unternehmen mit ihrer Darstellung in Spiegel und Focus korrelliert|volume=Volume 59|issue=4|publisher=Springer|publication-place=Wiesbaden|issn=0033-4006|date=2014|language=de
}}</ref>

Focus reporting on the [[2015–16 New Year's Eve sexual assaults in Germany]] created controversy.<ref>{{cite web|title=Schwarze Hände auf nackter, blonder Frau: Focus wegen Köln-Cover im Sexismus-Shitstorm|periodical=Meedia|url=http://meedia.de/2016/01/08/schwarze-haende-auf-nackter-blonder-frau-focus-wegen-populistischem-cover-im-shitstorm/|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2016-01-08|language=de}}</ref> The cover page showed hand prints in black paint on a white, naked woman; it was criticized as a "naked insult" and considered "racist".<ref>{{citation|surname1=Sonja Álvarez|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|title=Nackte Beleidigung|page=21|date=2016-01-11|language=de
}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Titel der Schande|periodical=Die Tageszeitung|url=http://www.taz.de/!5267901/|access-date=2016-10-30|last=Lalon Sander, Anna Böcker|date=2016-01-09|language=de}}</ref> Unlike the German daily newspaper, ''[[Süddeutsche Zeitung]]'', which had also caused an uproar with a polarizing depiction, Focus refused to apologize and justified the cover as a symbolic depiction of "what happened in Cologne".<ref>{{cite web|title=Rassistische Titelbilder: Süddeutsche entschuldigt sich, Focus nicht|periodical=Spiegel Online|url=http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/gesellschaft/focus-und-sueddeutsche-zeitung-eine-entschuldigung-eine-rechtfertigung-fuer-titel-a-1071334.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2016-01-10|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Medien-Diskussion geht weiter: Wir bilden ab, was leider passiert ist|periodical=Der Tagesspiegel|url=http://www.tagesspiegel.de/medien/focus-chef-verteidigt-umstrittenes-cover-medien-diskussion-geht-weiter-wir-bilden-ab-was-leider-passiert-ist/12810242.html|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2016-01-11|language=de}}</ref> The [[German Press Council]] received several complaints about Focus,<ref>{{cite web|title=Beschwerden gegen Focus-Titelbild beim Deutschen Presserat|periodical=Deutschlandfunk|url=http://www.deutschlandfunk.de/sexismus-vorwuerfe-beschwerden-gegen-focus-titelbild-beim.1818.de.html?dram:article_id=342138|access-date=2016-10-30|last=Renate Bölingen|date=2016-01-11|language=de}}</ref> but rejected all of them.<ref>{{cite web|title=Keine Rüge für den Focus: Presserat weist alle Beschwerden zur Berichterstattung in Köln ab|periodical=Meedia|url=http://meedia.de/2016/03/11/keine-ruege-fuer-den-focus-presserat-weist-alle-beschwerden-zur-berichterstattung-in-koeln-ab/|access-date=2016-10-30|date=2016-03-11|language=de}}</ref>
