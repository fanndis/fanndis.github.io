'''Infobase Publishing''' is an American [[publishing company|publisher]] of [[reference book]] titles and textbooks geared towards the North American library, secondary school, and university-level curriculum markets. Infobase operates a number of prominent [[imprint (trade name)|imprint]]s, including Facts On File, Films for the Humanities & Sciences, Cambridge Educational, Chelsea House (which also serves as the imprint for the special collection series, "Bloom's Literary Criticism" under the direction of [[literary critic]] [[Harold Bloom]]), and Ferguson Publishing.

==History==
The private equity firm [[Veronis Suhler Stevenson]] bought Facts on File and Chelsea House in 2005.<ref>{{Cite web | title = Berkery, Noyes & Co. advises Exeter Capital Partners on the sale of Facts on File to Veronis Suhler Stevenson | accessdate = 2014-08-23 | url = http://www.berkerynoyes.com/news/pr_56.aspx }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | title = News Briefs | work = PublishersWeekly.com | accessdate = 2014-09-06 | url = http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/print/20050822/32750-news-briefs.html }}</ref> Infobase bought Films for the Humanities & Sciences in 2007 and the ''[[World Almanac]]'' in 2009.<ref>{{Cite web | last = Content | first = Library Journal Archive | title = Infobase Buys Films Media Group | work = Library Journal | accessdate = 2014-08-23 | url = http://lj.libraryjournal.com/2007/06/industry-news/infobase-buys-films-media-group/ }}</ref> In 2017, Infobase acquired The Mailbox lesson plans and ''Learning'' magazine.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Infobase Announces Acquisition of The Mailbox {{!}} Infobase| accessdate = 2018-10-16| url = https://www.infobase.com/press_releases/infobase-announces-acquisition-of-the-mailbox/}}</ref> Veronis Suhler Stevenson sold Infobase to another private equity firm, Centre Lane Partners, in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web| last = CNBC| title = Centre Lane Partners Acquires Infobase from VSS| accessdate = 2018-10-16| date = 2018-01-08| url = https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/08/globe-newswire-centre-lane-partners-acquires-infobase-from-vss.html}}</ref>

As well as nonfiction works in print, Infobase and its imprints publish a selection of works in digital, audio-visual and [[online database]] formats.{{citation needed|date=April 2017}}

Facts On File has been publishing books since 1941.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fofweb.com/Tutorials/AboutFOF.aspx|title=About Facts on File|author=<!--- None specified --->|work=FOFWeb|publisher=Facts on File|location=United States|accessdate=June 25, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612144340/http://www.fofweb.com/Tutorials/AboutFOF.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-date=2018-06-12}}</ref> It was owned by [[CCH (company)|CCH]] from 1965 to 1993.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Facts on File sold by CCH| work = Publishers Weekly| accessdate = 2019-10-15| date = 1993-04-19| url = http://link.galegroup.com/apps/doc/A13700324/LitRC?sid=googlescholar}}</ref> The publisher publishes general reference and trade books.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.publishersglobal.com/directory/publisher-profile/3454/|title=Facts on File, Inc.|author=<!--- None specified --->|work=PublishersGlobal|publisher=Impello Inc.|location=[[Calgary]]|accessdate=June 25, 2017}}</ref>

Chelsea House was founded in 1966. It is known for multi-volume reference works.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Haights Cross Completes Three Purchases| work = PublishersWeekly.com| accessdate = 2019-10-15| url = https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/print/19991220/25050-haights-cross-completes-three-purchases.html}}</ref>
