'''Jennifer Morgan''' (born March 13, 1971) is an American technology executive. She is the former Co-Chief Executive Officer at [[SAP SE]].<ref>[https://news.sap.com/2020/04/morgan-departs-klein-continues-as-sap-ceo/ SAP Co-Chief Executive Officer Christian Klein Continues as CEO, Jennifer Morgan Departs] SAP, 20 April 2020, retrieved 21 April 2020</ref><ref name="Philadelphia Inquirer October 2019">{{cite web |last1=DiStefano |first1=Joseph |title=SAP taps Philly’s Jennifer Morgan, Christian Klein to replace Bill McDermott at CEO|url=https://www.inquirer.com/business/sap-jennifer-moran-christian-klein-ceo-bill-mcdermott-20191010.html#loaded |website=Philadelphia Inquirer |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> Morgan became the first American woman ever appointed to the SAP Executive Board in 2017.<ref name="Bloomberg October 2019">{{cite web |last1=Grant |first1=Nico |title=SAP Makes Surprise Leadership Change as CEO McDermott Steps Down|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-10-10/sap-appoints-co-ceos-morgan-klein-to-replace-mcdermott |website=Bloomberg |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> Morgan is the first female Chief Executive of SAP, and she is the first female CEO of a company on the [[DAX]] index.<ref name="Yahoo! October 2019">{{cite web |last1=Petzinger |first1=Jill |title=SAP’s Jennifer Morgan becomes first woman to lead a DAX company|url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/saps-jennifer-morgan-becomes-first-woman-to-head-up-a-dax-company-081315807.html |website=Yahoo! |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Morgan was born and raised in [[Northern Virginia]],<ref name="Philadelphia Inquirer October 2019">{{cite web |last1=DiStefano |first1=Joseph |title=SAP taps Philly’s Jennifer Morgan, Christian Klein to replace Bill McDermott at CEO|url=https://www.inquirer.com/business/sap-jennifer-moran-christian-klein-ceo-bill-mcdermott-20191010.html#loaded |website=Philadelphia Inquirer |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> just outside of [[Washington, DC]]. She studied at [[James Madison University]].<ref name="Bloomberg">{{cite web |title=Jennifer Morgan|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/profile/person/17665476 |website=Bloomberg |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> Following her graduation, Morgan began to work at [[Andersen Consulting]] (now [[Accenture]]) where she met her husband, Michael.<ref name="Philadelphia Inquirer October 2019">{{cite web |last1=DiStefano |first1=Joseph |title=SAP taps Philly’s Jennifer Morgan, Christian Klein to replace Bill McDermott at CEO|url=https://www.inquirer.com/business/sap-jennifer-moran-christian-klein-ceo-bill-mcdermott-20191010.html#loaded |website=Philadelphia Inquirer |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
Following her employment at Andersen Consulting, Morgan served in a business development role at [[Siebel Systems]] from 2000 to 2004.<ref>{{cite web | title=Jennifer Morgan |url=https://www.linkedin.com/in/jenbmorgan/ |website=linkedin.com |publisher=LinkedIn Corp |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> Morgan joined SAP in 2004 as part of the company's public sector business.<ref name="Philadelphia Inquirer September 2018">{{cite web |last1=Reyes |first1=Juliana |title=SAP’s Jennifer Morgan is the only Philly exec on Fortune’s 2018 Most Powerful Women list|url=https://www.inquirer.com/philly/news/jennifer-morgan-most-powerful-women-sap-motherhood-20180924.html |website=Philadelphia Inquirer |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> From there, Morgan rose quickly within SAP leadership. In just five years, Morgan went from President of SAP Regulated Industries to the President of the Americas & Asia Pacific Japan.<ref name="Philadelphia Inquirer September 2018">{{cite web |last1=Reyes |first1=Juliana |title=SAP’s Jennifer Morgan is the only Philly exec on Fortune’s 2018 Most Powerful Women list|url=https://www.inquirer.com/philly/news/jennifer-morgan-most-powerful-women-sap-motherhood-20180924.html |website=Philadelphia Inquirer |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> In 2017, Morgan was appointed to the SAP executive board<ref name="Wall Street Journal August 2017">{{cite web |last1=Fuhrmans |first1=Vanessa |title=SAP goes after the gender pay gap|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/sap-goes-after-the-gender-pay-gap-1502676120|website=Wall Street Journal |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref> and in April 2019 she became President of the SAP Cloud Business Group.<ref name="Reuters April 2019">{{cite web |title=SAP's head of cloud business latest top departure in restructuring |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/sap-restructuring/saps-head-of-cloud-business-latest-top-departure-in-restructuring-idUSL8N21O04U |website=Reuters |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref>  In October 2019, Morgan and fellow SAP Executive Board member [[Christian Klein]] were named Co-Chief Executive Officers of SAP following the departure of CEO [[Bill McDermott]].<ref name="Market Watch October 2019">{{cite web |last1=Murphy |first1=Mike |title=SAP CEO Bill McDermott steps down; shares rise
|url=https://www.marketwatch.com/story/sap-ceo-bill-mcdermott-steps-down-shares-rise-2019-10-10?link=MW_latest_news |website=Market Watch |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref>

Beginning in 2015, while she was President of SAP North America, Morgan led efforts to close the [[gender pay gap]] at SAP. After independent audits of Morgan's initiative, SAP raised salaries by over $1 million across the company, which resulted in 99% of all men and women being paid equally.<ref name="Wall Street Journal August 2017">{{cite web |last1=Fuhrmans |first1=Vanessa |title=SAP goes after the gender pay gap|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/sap-goes-after-the-gender-pay-gap-1502676120|website=Wall Street Journal |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Fortune January 2019">{{cite web |last1=Pressman |first1=Aaron |title=This New Podcast Aims to Give "Emerging Leaders" In Business Some Savvy Advice|url=https://fortune.com/2019/01/28/podcast-sap-jennifer-morgan-leadership/ |website= Fortune |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref>

In January 2019, Morgan launched her podcast, ''A Call to Lead''. The podcast targets mid-career professionals and discusses leadership and career advancement. Morgan's podcast guests have included academics, business professionals, and government officials (including Former Second Lady [[Jill Biden]], [[Simon Sinek]], and others).<ref name="Fortune January 2019">{{cite web |last1=Pressman |first1=Aaron |title=This New Podcast Aims to Give "Emerging Leaders" In Business Some Savvy Advice|url=https://fortune.com/2019/01/28/podcast-sap-jennifer-morgan-leadership/ |website= Fortune |accessdate=October 11, 2019}}</ref>

On April 30, 2020, Morgan stepped down from her position as co-CEO citing a mutual decision with the company board.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2020/04/21/and-then-there-was-one-co-ceo-jennifer-morgan-to-depart-sap/|title=And then there was one: Co-CEO Jennifer Morgan to depart SAP|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/08926bc2-52b4-4825-856a-d75bda891fa4|title=Subscribe to read {{!}} Financial Times|website=www.ft.com|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref>

== Recognition ==
In 2019, Fortune Magazine named Morgan ranked 43rd on Fortune Magazine's list of the 50 Most Powerful Women in Business.<ref name="Fortune 2019">{{cite web |title=Jennifer Morgan #43|url=https://fortune.com/most-powerful-women/2019/jennifer-morgan/ |website= Fortune |accessdate=11 October 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, Morgan was ranked 49th on Forbes' list of Most Powerful Women in the World.<ref name="Forbes 2019">{{cite web |title=Jennifer Morgan #49 |url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/jennifer-morgan/?list=power-women#7e9d998f2192 |website= Forbes |accessdate=22 December 2019}}</ref> Morgan was ranked 55th on Forbes’ list of Most Powerful Women in the World for 2018.<ref name="Forbes 2018">{{cite web |title=Jennifer Morgan #55 |url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/jennifer-morgan/?list=power-women#47653daa2192 |website= Forbes |accessdate=11 October 2019}}</ref>

In 2017, the [[New York Hall of Science]] honored Morgan with the Distinguished Leadership Award.<ref name="New York Hall of Science 2017">{{cite web |title=2017 DISTINGUISHED LEADERSHIP AWARD JENNIFER MORGAN, SAP |url=https://nysci.org/2017-distinguished-leadership-award-jennifer-morgan-sap/|website=New York Hall of Science |accessdate=October 4, 2019}}</ref>
