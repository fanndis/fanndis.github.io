'''Anne Finucane''' is an American banker who is vice chair of [[Bank of America]]. She leads the bank's [[environmental, social and corporate governance]] committee. She has worked in the banking industry since 1995, when she joined [[FleetBoston Financial|Fleet Financial]], which later merged with the short-lived [[BankBoston]] to become part of Bank of America. She is a powerful woman in banking, and she serves on the boards of several organizations. She lives in [[Lincoln, Massachusetts]].

==Early life and education==
Finucane was born to an [[Irish Americans|Irish-American]] family, and raised as the fourth of six children in [[Newton, Massachusetts]].<ref name=Image>{{Cite news|url= https://www.nytimes.com/2012/01/15/business/at-bank-of-america-the-image-officer-has-a-lot-to-fix.html|title=The Image Officer with a Lot to Fix|date=January 14, 2012|accessdate=January 10, 2018|work=[[The New York Times]]|first1=Louise|last1=Story|first2=Gretchen|last2=Morgenson|issn=0362-4331|oclc=1645522}}</ref> The lineages of both her parents trace back to [[County Cork]].<ref name="IrishAmerica">{{cite journal|title=Anne Finucane/Bank of America|journal=[[Irish America (magazine)|Irish America]]|url=http://irishamerica.com/2011/01/anne-finucane/|accessdate=January 10, 2018|issn=0884-4240}}</ref> Her father, William, was a lawyer who worked for the [[New England Patriots|Boston Patriots]] and local banks. Her mother, Mary, was a homemaker and distant relative of [[Tip O'Neill]].<ref name=Image/> Finucane was friends with one of [[Robert Q. Crane]]'s daughters, and Crane introduced Finucane to the then Mayor of Boston, [[Kevin White (mayor)|Kevin White]]. Following completion of her studies at the [[University of New Hampshire]], Finucane worked in the mayor's arts office.<ref name=Image/>

==Career in banking==
Finucane entered the banking industry when she joined [[FleetBoston Financial|Fleet Financial]] in 1995, serving as head of corporate affairs and marketing.<ref name=Image/> She worked to improve the bank's reputation following a [[subprime lending]] crisis, and helped in company acquisitions.<ref name=Image/> Fleet Financial later merged with [[BankBoston]], then [[Bank of America]].<ref name=Image/> Finucane became FleetBoston Financial's executive vice president for corporate marketing and communications.<ref name="Power">{{cite journal|title=Power: The 100 Women Who Run This Town|journal=[[Boston (magazine)|Boston]]|date=October 5, 2010|url=http://www.bostonmagazine.com/2010/10/05/power-the-100-women-who-run-this-town/2/|accessdate=January 10, 2018|publisher=[[Metrocorp]]}}</ref> She was Bank of America's global chief strategy and marketing officer after the [[financial crisis of 2007–2008]] when the bank lost 55% of its value, "symboliz[ing] all that was wrong" with US banks.<ref name=Image/><ref name="Portillo">{{cite journal|last1=Portillo|first1=Caroline McMillan|title=Straight talk from the so-called 'brutal' Anne Finucane, one of banking's most powerful women|journal=[[The Business Journals]]|date=September 22, 2014|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/bizwomen/news/profiles-strategies/2014/09/straight-talk-from-the-so-called-brutal-anne.html?page=all|accessdate=January 4, 2018|publisher=[[American City Business Journals]]}}</ref><ref name="Kosoff">{{cite journal|last1=Kosoff|first1=Maya|title=Uber Gets a New Front-Runner in the Race to Replace Travis Kalanick|journal=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|date=July 21, 2017|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2017/07/uber-gets-a-new-front-runner-in-the-race-to-replace-travis-kalanick|accessdate=January 10, 2018|publisher=[[Condé Nast]]}}</ref>
