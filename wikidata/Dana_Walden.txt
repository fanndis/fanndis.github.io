'''Dana Walden''' (born October 13, 1964) is an American businesswoman and the Chairman of Entertainment, [[Walt Disney Television]].

== Early life and education ==
Dana Freedman was born to a [[American Jews|Jewish]] family<ref>[http://forward.com/sisterhood/342253/the-worlds-most-powerful-jewish-women/ Jewish Daily Forward: "The World’s Most Powerful Jewish Women" by Laura E. Adkins] June 15, 2016</ref> in Los Angeles in 1964. Her father was in the travel industry and her mother was a dancer. As a teenager, Walden rode [[show hunter]]s competitively. She earned a degree in communications from the [[University of Southern California]].<ref name="LA Times"/>

== Career ==
Following her graduation, Walden worked for the public relations firm Bender, Goldman & Helper as Larry Goldman's assistant. She spent five years there, rising to the role of vice president. She then managed marketing and communications for talk show host [[Arsenio Hall]].<ref name="LA Times"/> She worked on campaigns for ''[[The Arsenio Hall Show]]'' and ''[[Star Trek: The Next Generation]]''.<ref name="THR"/> Media executive [[Lucie Salhany]] recruited Walden to work in public relations for [[20th Century Fox Television]] in 1992. Following a "[[Jerry Maguire]] moment" during a presentation at a Fox corporate retreat in the mid-1990s, Walden was made a programming executive.<ref name="LA Times">{{cite news|last1=Flint|first1=Joe|title=Dana Walden is obsessed with TV|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2011/oct/02/business/la-fi-ct-himi-walden-20111002|work=Los Angeles Times|date=October 2, 2011}}</ref><ref name="THR">{{cite news|last1=Rose|first1=Lacey|title=20th TV's Dana Walden On Her Career-Changing 'Jerry Maguire' Moment|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/women-entertainment-dana-walden-fox-397427|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=December 5, 2012}}</ref>

In 1999, Walden and Gary Newman were made co-chairs and CEOs of the Fox studio.<ref name="LA Times"/> In 2014, the pair were promoted to chairperson-CEOs of Fox Television Group.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Littleton|first1=Cynthia|title=Dana Walden, Gary Newman Take Over Fox Broadcasting in Restructuring|url=https://variety.com/2014/tv/news/dana-walden-gary-newman-take-over-fox-broadcasting-in-restructuring-1201262005/|work=Variety|date=July 14, 2014}}</ref>

In October 2018, it was announced that Walden would leave Fox after [[Proposed acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney|the acquisition of 21st Century Fox's key entertainment assets]] by [[The Walt Disney Company]], and become a chairwoman of Disney Television Studios and the [[Disney-ABC Television Group]]. The acquisition excludes the [[Fox Broadcasting Company]] itself, which will be led by [[AMC (TV channel)|AMC]]'s Charles Collier.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/19/business/media/fox-network-charlie-collier.html|title=Fox Network Is Replacing Chief With Charlie Collier of AMC|work=The New York Times|access-date=2018-11-22|language=en}}</ref><ref name=nov16bombshell>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/channing-dungey-abc-karey-burke-1203031129/|title=Channing Dungey Exits ABC; Karey Burke Named Network President|last=Holloway|first=Daniel|date=2018-11-16|work=Variety|access-date=2018-11-22|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/channing-dungey-exits-abc-karey-burke-take-as-entertainment-president-1162211|title=Channing Dungey Exits ABC; Karey Burke to Take Over as Entertainment President|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-11-22|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/disney-fox-peter-rice-dana-walden-analysis-1202972856/|title=Analysis: Peter Rice, Dana Walden Tasked With Forging New Path for Production at Enlarged Disney|last=Littleton|first=Cynthia|date=2018-10-08|work=Variety|access-date=2018-11-22|language=en-US}}</ref> In one of first decisions as head of Disney programming after the merger with Fox was approved in March 2019, Walden signed writer, director and producer [[Drew Goddard]] to an exclusive, multiyear contract with Fox Television.<ref>https://variety.com/2019/tv/news/oscar-nominated-drew-goddard-inks-exclusive-overall-deal-with-20th-century-fox-tv-1203169254/</ref>

=== Other roles ===
Walden is a boardmember of the [[Los Angeles Zoo]]. She is a supporter of the animal rescue organization Chance for Bliss.<ref name="LA Times"/> She also serves on the board of [[Live Nation Entertainment]].<ref name="lyvboard">{{cite web | title=Dana Walden Joins Live Nation Board | work=Hollywood Reporter | url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/dana-walden-joins-live-nation-board-1118543 | accessdate=August 15, 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Walden is married and has two children. She lives in [[Brentwood, Los Angeles]].<ref name="LA Times"/> Her grandmother Rose Freedman was a survivor of the [[Triangle Shirtwaist Factory fire]] who lived to 107.<ref>{{cite news |last=Martin |first=Douglas |date=February 17, 2001 |title=Rose Freedman, Last Survivor of Triangle Fire, Dies at 107 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2001/02/17/nyregion/rose-freedman-last-survivor-of-triangle-fire-dies-at-107.html |work=The New York Times |access-date=December 20, 2018 }}</ref>
