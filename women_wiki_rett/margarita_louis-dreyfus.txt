'''Margarita Louis-Dreyfus''' (née '''Bogdanova'''; born 1 July 1962) is a Russian-born Swiss [[billionaire]] businesswoman, chairperson of [[Louis Dreyfus Group]].

==Early life==
Born '''Margarita Olegovna Bogdanova''' ({{lang-ru|Маргарита Олеговна Богданова}}) in [[Saint Petersburg|Leningrad]], she was raised by her grandfather, an electrical engineer, and studied law in Moscow and economics in Leningrad.

==Career==
On 29 August 2016, Louis-Dreyfus and the [[Marseille]] mayor, [[Jean-Claude Gaudin]], stated during a press conference with [[Frank McCourt (executive)|Frank McCourt]] that McCourt had agreed in principle to purchase the French [[Ligue 1]] football club, [[Olympique de Marseille]], owned by Louis-Dreyfus. The purchase deal was completed for a reported price tag of 45 million euros on 17 October 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-soccer-marseille-sale-idUSKCN1141JB|title=Former LA Dodgers owner McCourt to buy Marseille|date=29 August 2016|publisher=|via=Reuters}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/sports/sportsnow/la-sp-frank-mccourt-olympique-de-marseille-20160829-snap-story.html|title=French soccer club thinks Frank McCourt led Dodgers to 'tremendous economic success'|first=Los Angeles|last=Times|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/37685865|title=Marseille: Frank McCourt promises £180m investment after buying Ligue 1 club|publisher=BBC|date=17 October 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1988, on a Zurich to London flight, she met [[Robert Louis-Dreyfus]]. They married in 1992 and had three sons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://madame.lefigaro.fr/societe/margarita-louis-dreyfus-tsarine-affaires-270713-440620 |title=Margarita Louis-Dreyfus, la tsarine des affaires - Madame Figaro |language=fr |website=Madame.lefigaro.fr |date= |accessdate=2017-02-23}}</ref> Margarita, who had been working for a circuit-board equipment seller, became a full-time wife and mother.<ref name=Bloomberg>{{cite news|last1=Katz|first1=Alan|title=Louis-Dreyfus Widow Chairman Ousts Men Running Commodities Giant|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2012-01-31/louis-dreyfus-widow-ousts-men-running-commodities-giant-as-newest-chairman.html|accessdate=10 November 2014|work=Bloomberg|date=31 January 2012}}</ref> She took over as chairman of the [[Louis Dreyfus Group]] in 2009, following her husband's death from leukemia.<ref name=Forbes>{{cite web|title=Margarita Louis-Dreyfus|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/margarita-louis-dreyfus/|website=Forbes|accessdate=10 November 2014}}</ref>

In 2016, she had a net worth of $9.5 billion. She is a Swiss citizen living in Zurich with her three sons (Éric born in 1992, and twins, Maurice and Kyril, born in 1997), according to ''[[Forbes]]''.<ref name=Forbes/> Her partner is [[Philipp Hildebrand]], the former head of the Swiss central bank.<ref name=Bloomberg2>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-01-04/louis-dreyfus-chairwoman-pregnant-with-twins-at-53-plans-leave|website=Bloomberg Business|title=Louis Dreyfus Chairwoman, Pregnant With Twins, Plans Leave|publisher=Bloomberg|accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref> She gave birth to twin girls on 21 March 2016.<ref name=Guardian>{{cite web|title=Margarita Louis-Dreyfus gives birth to twins|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/mar/21/swiss-billionaire-margarita-louis-dreyfus-birth-twins-53|accessdate=21 March 2016}}</ref>

Margarita is the second cousin once removed (by marriage) of American actress [[Julia Louis-Dreyfus]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Douglas H.|last=Martin|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/14/business/global/14louis-dreyfus.html|title=Robert Louis-Dreyfus, Turnaround Specialist, Dies at 63|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|publisher=[[New York Times Company]]|location=New York City|date=July 14, 2009|page=B10}}</ref>
