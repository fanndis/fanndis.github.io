'''Ruth Porat''' (born 1958) is an American business executive, serving since 2015 as the [[chief financial officer]] (CFO) of [[Alphabet Inc.]] and its subsidiary [[Google]].<ref name="GOOG">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/maggiemcgrath/2015/03/24/google-poaches-ruth-porat-from-morgan-stanley/|title=Google Lures CFO Ruth Porat From Morgan Stanley|magazine=Forbes|first1=Maggie|last1=McGrath|date=March 24, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|author=Patricia Garcia|url=http://www.vogue.com/12928445/ruth-porat-google-most-powerful-women-in-tech/|title=Ruth Porat Is Google's First Female CFO: 10 Other Powerful Women in Tech|magazine=Vogue|accessdate=2015-03-27}}</ref><ref name="Forbes" /> Porat was CFO and [[executive vice president]] of [[Morgan Stanley]], from January 2010 to May 2015<ref>{{Cite news|last=Popper|first=Nathaniel|last2=Dougherty|first2=Conor|date=2015-03-24|title=Google Hires Finance Chief Ruth Porat From Morgan Stanley (Published 2015)|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/03/25/technology/ruth-porat-google-morgan-stanley.html|access-date=2020-11-17|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>  

In 2019, Porat was listed as the 19th most powerful woman in the world by ''[[Forbes]]'',<ref name="Forbes 2019 Most Powerful Women"; <ref name="Forbes">{{cite magazine|magazine=[[Forbes]]|title=World's Most Powerful Women: Ruth Porat|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/ruth-porat/|date=August 2011}}</ref> and seventh on ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'''s Most Powerful Women list in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.fortuneconferences.com/most-powerful-women-summit-2018 | title=Most Powerful Women Summit 2018 &#124; Fortune Conferences}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Porat was born to a Jewish family<ref>{{cite news|last=Shamah|first=David|url=http://www.timesofisrael.com/new-google-cfo-ruth-porats-family-a-mirror-of-american-jewry/|title=New Google CFO Ruth Porat's family a mirror of American Jewry|newspaper=The Times of Israel|date=2015-03-02|accessdate=2015-03-27}}</ref> in [[Sale, Greater Manchester|Sale, Cheshire]], England,<ref name="cfr-bio" /> the daughter of Dr. Dan and Frieda Porat.<ref name="wedding" /><ref>[http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/sfgate/obituary.aspx?pid=160146291 Dr. Frieda Porat's obituary]</ref> She moved at a young age to [[Cambridge, Massachusetts]], where her father was a research fellow in the physics department at [[Harvard University]]. Her father three years later relocated the family to [[Palo Alto, California]], where he worked at the [[SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory]] for 26 years.<ref>{{cite news| newspaper=The Wall Street Journal|title=A Dossier on Morgan Stanley's New CFO Ruth Porat|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/deals/2009/12/08/a-dossier-on-morgan-stanleys-new-cfo-ruth-porat/|date=December 8, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/ruth-porat-google-alphabet-biography-2016-7|title=The incredible rise of Ruth Porat, CFO at one of the most valuable companies in the world|work=Business Insider|access-date=2017-12-13|language=en}}</ref> Porat has a bachelor of arts degree in economics and international relations from [[Stanford University]] and holds a master's degree in [[industrial relations]] from [[London School of Economics]] and an MBA with distinction from [[Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Alphabet|title=Ruth Porat to Join Google as Chief Financial Officer|url=https://abc.xyz/investor/news/releases/2015/0324.html|date=March 24, 2015}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== Morgan Stanley ===
Porat began her career at Morgan Stanley in 1987 and left in 1993 to follow Morgan Stanley president [[Robert F. Greenhill]] to [[Smith Barney]]<ref name="dealbook-pitfalls">{{cite news|newspaper=The New York Times|title=Dealbook: A Female Wall St. Financial Chief Avoids Pitfalls That Stymied Others|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/11/10/business/10porat.html?pagewanted=all&_r=0|first1=Suzanne|last1=Craig|date=November 9, 2010}}</ref> and returned to Morgan Stanley in 1996. Before becoming CFO, she served as vice chairman of investment banking from September 2003 to December 2009 and the global head of the Financial Institutions Group from September 2006 to December 2009. She was previously co-head of technology investment banking and worked for Morgan Stanley in London.<ref name="cfr-bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.cfr.org/staff/b19354|title=Ruth Porat|publisher=[[Council on Foreign Relations]]}}</ref> While a banker at Morgan Stanley, she was credited with creating the European debt financing that saved [[Amazon.com|Amazon]] from collapse during the [[Dot-com bubble|dot-com melt down]] in 2000.<ref>''The Everything Store: Jeff Bezos And The Age Of Amazon'', [[Little, Brown & Co.]], p. 101, (New York 2013)</ref><ref>"The Little-Known Deal That Saved Amazon From The Dot-Com Crash," Timothy B. Lee, Vox, (April 5, 2017)</ref> Her financial partner during the Internet investment banking craze was [[Mary Meeker]], the godmother to Porat's three children.<ref name="dealbook-pitfalls" />

During the financial crisis, Porat led the Morgan Stanley team advising the [[United States Department of the Treasury]] regarding [[Fannie Mae]] and [[Freddie Mac]], and the [[New York Federal Reserve]] Bank with respect to [[AIG]].<ref>{{cite book|first1=Andrew Ross|last1=Sorkin|author-link=Andrew Ross Sorkin|title=Too Big to Fail|isbn=978-0-670-02125-3|pages=[https://archive.org/details/toobigtofailinsi00sork/page/372 372, 382]|publisher=[[Viking Press]]|year=2009|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/toobigtofailinsi00sork/page/372}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|journal=[[The Deal (magazine)|The Deal]]|title=When Treasury Calls|url=http://www.thedeal.com/magazine/ID/019479/dealmakersold/when-treasury-calls.php|date=September 2008|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130427034716/http://www.thedeal.com/magazine/ID/019479/dealmakersold/when-treasury-calls.php|archive-date=2013-04-27}}</ref>  In the 2011 [[HBO]] movie ''[[Too Big to Fail (film)|Too Big to Fail]]'', Porat is played by [[Jennifer van Dyck]].<ref>{{IMDb title | 1742683 | Too Big To Fail}}</ref> In May 2011, she presented to the [[Bretton Woods Committee|Bretton Woods]] Committee hosted by the [[International Monetary Fund]] in [[Washington, D.C.]], on post-crisis reform and financial legislation, and to the [[World Economic Forum]] in [[Davos]] in 2013 on "trust" levels within and of the financial sector.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=The Bretton Woods Committee|title=2011 Bretton Woods Annual Meeting: Risks to the Global System|url=http://www.brettonwoods.org/node/390|date=May 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Edelman (firm)|Edelman]]|title=2013 Edelman Trust Barometer|url=http://www.edelman.com/insights/intellectual-property/trust-2013/|date=January 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[World Economic Forum]]|title=Ruth Porat|url=http://www.weforum.org/contributors/ruth-porat|date=December 2013}}</ref>

In 2013, it was reported that President [[Barack Obama]] would nominate Porat as the next [[Deputy Secretary of the Treasury]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Bloomberg News]]|title=Obama Considering Morgan Stanley's Porat for Treasury Job|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2013-01-14/obama-said-to-consider-morgan-stanley-s-porat-for-treasury-post.html|date=January 14, 2013}}</ref> However, it was reported later by [[Bloomberg News]] and ''[[The New York Times]]'' that Porat had contacted White House officials to withdraw her name from consideration because of improving conditions at Morgan Stanley and the acrimonious confirmation process inflicted upon then Treasury Secretary-nominee [[Jack Lew]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Bloomberg News|title=Morgan Stanley's Porat No Longer Interested in Treasury Post|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2013-03-28/morgan-stanley-s-porat-no-longer-interested-in-treasury-post.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|newspaper=The New York Times|title=Ruth Porat Withdraws Name From Deputy Treasury Race|date=March 28, 2013|url=https://dealbook.nytimes.com/2013/03/28/ruth-porat-withdraws-name-from-deputy-treasury}}</ref>

Porat's career was analyzed in the [[McKinsey & Company]] study "How Remarkable Women Lead".<ref>{{cite book|first1=Joanna|last1=Barsh|first2=Susie|last2=Cranston|first3=Geoffrey|last3=Lewis|title=How Remarkable Women Lead: The Breakthrough Model for Work and Life|publisher=[[Crown Books]]|year=2010|isbn=978-0307461704}}</ref>  She was named "Best Financial Institutions CFO" in a poll conducted by ''[[Institutional Investor (magazine)|Institutional Investor]]'' for its "2014 All-America Executive Team".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Details|url=https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/research/4633/best-cfos|access-date=2020-11-17|website=Institutional Investor|language=en-gb}}</ref>

=== Google ===
On March 24, 2015, it was announced that Porat would join [[Google]] as its new CFO as of May 26, 2015.<ref name="GOOG" /> ''Bloomberg Business'' reported that her hiring deal amounted to $70 million.<ref>{{cite magazine|magazine=[[Bloomberg Business]]|title=Google Agrees to Pay New CFO Ruth Porat $70 Million by 2016| url = https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-03-26/google-agrees-to-pay-new-cfo-ruth-porat-70-million-by-2016 |first1=Michael|last1=Moore|date=March 26, 2015}}</ref> She has been credited with boosting Google's share price by reorganizing the company and imposing financial discipline.<ref>"Google Makes So Much Money, It Never Had To Worry About Financial Discipline--Until Now". ''[[Bloomberg BusinessWeek]]'', 8 December 2016.</ref>  For the "2018 All America Executive Team", she was named "Best Internet CFO" by ''[[Institutional Investor (magazine)|Institutional Investor]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Whyte |first1=Amy |title=The 2018 All-America Executive Team: What Makes a Top CEO |url=https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/b15hrwskzqkk33/the-2018-all-america-executive-team-what-makes-a-top-ceo |accessdate=2 December 2019 |work=Institutional Investor |date=7 November 2017}}</ref> Porat spoke at the ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'' Most Powerful Women Summit in Dana Point, California, on October 19, 2016, in her capacity as CFO of [[Alphabet Inc.]] and [[Google]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pressman |first1=Aaron |title=Data Sheet—Amazon, Google, and Microsoft Have Plenty to Celebrate Right Now |url=https://fortune.com/2017/10/27/data-sheet-amazon-google-microsoft-stock-earnings/ |accessdate=2 December 2019 |work=Fortune |date=27 October 2017}}</ref>  At [[Google]], in addition to Finance, Porat also has Business Operations, "People Ops", [[Google]]'s human resources function, Real Estate and Work Place Services reporting to her. She was paid $46 million in 2020<ref>{{Cite web|title=UNITED STATES SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE COMMISSION Washington, D.C. 20549|url=https://abc.xyz/investor/static/pdf/20200429_alphabet_10Q.pdf?cache=1a4ecd7|access-date=2020-11-27}}</ref>, $47 million in 2018, $688,000 in 2017, and $39 million in 2016<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000130817919000205/lgoog2019_def14a.htm#lgooga044|title=ALPHABET INC Schedule 14A|last=Page|first=Larry|last2=Brin|first2=Sergey|date=April 30, 2019|website=U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|access-date=Aug 31, 2019|last3=Hennessy|first3=John L.}}</ref>

==Board membership ==

She is a member of the Board of Directors of [[Stanford University]] Management Company,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ruth Porat {{!}} SIEPR|url=https://siepr.stanford.edu/events/speakers/ruth-porat|access-date=2020-11-27|website=siepr.stanford.edu}}</ref> the University's endowment, the Board of Directors of [[The Council on Foreign Relations]],<ref name="cfr-bio"/> and a member of the Board of Directors of [[The Blackstone Group]]<ref>{{Cite news|last=Staff|first=Reuters|date=2020-06-25|title=UPDATE 1-Blackstone adds Alphabet CFO Ruth Porat to its board|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/blackstone-group-moves-idCNL4N2E2285|access-date=2020-09-21}}</ref>.  She previously served on the Board of Trustees of [[Stanford University]],<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Stanford University]]|work=Stanford Report|title=Board of Trustees welcomes two new members who bring 'wisdom and expertise'|url=http://news.stanford.edu/news/2010/august/trustees-porat-pritzker-081710.html|date=August 17, 2010}}</ref> the Borrowing Advisory Committee of the [[United States Treasury]],<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[US Department of the Treasury]]|title=Treasury Borrowing Advisory Committee Members|url=http://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/quarterly-refunding/Pages/members-index.aspx}}</ref> and the Board of Trustees of the [[Economic Club of New York]].<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[The Economic Club of New York]]|title=Trustees and Officers|url=http://econclubny.com/trusteesandofficers.asp|accessdate=August 1, 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130921061154/http://econclubny.com/trusteesandofficers.asp|archive-date=September 21, 2013}}. </ref> She is a member of the Advisory Council of the Hutchins Center on Fiscal and Monetary Policy at the [[Brookings Institution]],<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.brookings.edu/about/media-relations/news-releases/2014/0327-hutchins-center-advisory-council|title=Advisory Council Announced: Hutchins Center on Fiscal and Monetary Policy at Brookings|date=March 27, 2014|publisher=[[Brookings Institution]]}}</ref>and the Economic Strategy Group at the [[Aspen Institute]]. 

== Political views ==
Porat supported Senator [[Hillary Clinton]] when she ran for president in 2008, hosting a fundraiser at her apartment in [[The Dakota]] in New York City,<ref>{{cite news|newspaper=The Washington Post|title=A Morning At The Dakota|url=http://voices.washingtonpost.com/44/2008/02/19/a_morning_at_the_dakota_1.html|date=February 2008}}</ref> and did the same in 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hillary tells celeb friends she could beat Trump in a 'landslide'|url=https://pagesix.com/2016/03/31/hillary-tells-celeb-friends-she-could-beat-trump-in-a-landslide|access-date=2020-11-27}}</ref> In 2011, Porat expressed her support for increased taxes on the wealthy and declared on the topic of significant spending decreases that "we cannot cut our way to greatness".<ref>{{cite news|work=The Huffington Post|title=Morgan Stanley CFO Ruth Porat: Raise Taxes On The Rich|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/12/03/ruth-porat-taxes_n_1127584.html|date=December 2011}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Porat has been married to Anthony Paduano, a partner in the law firm Paduano & Weintraub, since 1983.<ref name="wedding">{{cite news|newspaper=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1983/12/18/style/ruth-porat-wed-to-law-student.html|title=Ruth Porat Wed To Law Student|date=December 18, 1983}}</ref> Porat is a survivor of [[breast cancer]].<ref>{{cite AV media|publisher=[[Big Think]]|title=Ruth Porat, Battling Cancer by Going to Work|url=http://bigthink.com/videos/battling-cancer-by-going-to-work|date=November 2010}}</ref>

In September 2015, Porat reportedly paid $30 million for a house in [[Palo Alto, California|Palo Alto]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/ruth-porat-google-alphabet-biography-2016-7|title=The incredible rise of Ruth Porat, CFO at one of the most valuable companies in the world|work=Business Insider|access-date=2017-12-13|language=en}}</ref>
