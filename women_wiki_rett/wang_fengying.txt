'''Wang Fengying''' (born Oct. 1970)<ref name="bambooinnovator.com"/> is a Chinese businesswoman, and the CEO and deputy chairman of [[Great Wall Motor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fortune.com/most-powerful-women-asia-pacific/wang-fengying-7/|title=Wang Fengying|author=|date=14 September 2015|website=fortune.com|accessdate=2 November 2017}}</ref><ref name="bloomberg.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/markets/stocks|title=Stocks|author=|date=|website=Bloomberg.com|accessdate=2 November 2017}}</ref>

Wang Fengying was born in [[Baoding]] in Hebei province in the north of China.<ref name="bambooinnovator.com">{{cite web|url=https://bambooinnovator.com/2013/10/22/wang-fengying-chinas-only-female-motor-industry-ceo/|title=Wang Fengying, China’s only female motor industry CEO|author=|date=22 October 2013|website=bambooinnovator.com|accessdate=3 November 2017}}</ref>

In 1991, Wang earned a bachelor's degree from the [[Tianjin Institute of Finance]], followed by a M.Sc. (majoring Finance) in 1999.<ref name="bambooinnovator.com"/><ref name="bloomberg.com"/>

Wang joined [[Great Wall Motor]] in 1991, aged 21.<ref name="bloomberg.com"/><ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://www.china.org.cn/top10/2012-12/28/content_27537983_18.htm|title=Top 25 most powerful Chinese businesswomen of 2012 - China.org.cn|first=|last=蔺丽瑶|date=|website=www.china.org.cn|accessdate=2 November 2017}}</ref> In 2003, she became the chief executive (CEO) of Great Wall Motor.<ref name="auto"/>

In 2017, she was assessed as being the 62nd most powerful woman in the world by Forbes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/feng-ying-wang/?list=power-women|title=Feng Ying Wang|author=|date=|website=forbes.com|accessdate=2 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/2/#tab:overall|title=World's Most Powerful Women|author=|date=|website=forbes.com|accessdate=2 November 2017}}</ref> In March 2018, speaking to the [[National People's Congress]], she presented her views about the Great Wall Motor's aim to double their vehicle sales by 2025 to 2 million units annually, helped by the push from increased electric and hybrid plug-in car production.<ref name="reuters-2018">{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-china-autos-great-wall-motor/great-wall-seeks-to-double-vehicle-sales-by-2025-plans-electric-car-push-idUSKCN1GK0EP|title=Great Wall seeks to double vehicle sales by 2025, plans electric car push|author=Muyu Xu, Norihiko Shirouzu|date= 8 March 2018|website=Reuters, reuters.com|accessdate=28 April 2018}}</ref>
