'''Safra A. Catz''' (Hebrew: צפרא כץ, born December 1, 1961) is an American billionaire banker and technology executive. She is the CEO of [[Oracle Corporation]]. She has been an executive at Oracle  since April 1999, and a board member since 2001. In April 2011, she was named co-president and [[chief financial officer]] (CFO), reporting to founder [[Larry Ellison]].<ref name="allth1"/> In September 2014, Oracle announced that Ellison would step down as CEO and that [[Mark Hurd]] and Catz had been named as joint CEOs.<ref>{{cite web|title=Oracle Board Appoints Larry Ellison Executive Chairman and CTO. Safra Catz and Mark Hurd Appointed CEO.|url=http://investor.oracle.com/financial-news/financial-news-details/2014/Oracle-Board-Appoints-Larry-Ellison-Executive-Chairman-and-CTO/default.aspx|publisher=Oracle Corporation|accessdate=18 September 2014}}</ref>

==Early life==
Catz was born in [[Holon]], Israel,<ref name="Garner">{{cite web |url= http://www.iht.com/articles/2006/12/18/bloomberg/bxoracle.php|title=Heir apparent at Oracle is credited with growth strategy|author=Rochelle Garner|work=[[International Herald Tribune]] |date= 2006-12-19}}</ref> to Jewish parents.<ref>{{cite news|author=Ruth Eglash|title=Jewish women who rule! (according to Forbes)|url=http://www.jpost.com/International/Jewish-women-who-rule-according-to-Forbes|accessdate=10 September 2013|newspaper=[[Jpost]]|date=August 23, 2012}}</ref><ref>[http://jewishvoiceny.com/index.php?option=com_content&id=1897:the-worlds-most-powerful-jewish-women&Itemid=325 Jewish Voice New York: "The World's Most Powerful Jewish Women" By Jen Levey] September 5, 2012</ref> Her father was an immigrant from Romania.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Oracle CFO Safra Catz announces expansion outside Bucharest, reveals Romanian origins |first=Andrei |last=Chirileasa |publisher=Romania-Insider.com|date=May 20, 2014 |accessdate=March 10, 2015 |url=https://www.romania-insider.com/oracle-cfo-safra-catz-announces-expansion-outside-bucharest-reveals-romanian-origins/}}</ref> She moved from Israel to [[Brookline, Massachusetts]] at the age of six.

Catz graduated from [[Brookline High School]].<ref name="fortune"/> She earned a bachelor's degree from the [[Wharton School]] of the [[University of Pennsylvania]] in 1983 and a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from the [[University of Pennsylvania Law School]] in 1986.<ref name="Garner"/><ref name="Fortune">{{cite web|title=Oracle's enforcer – Safra Catz|url=http://archive.fortune.com/2009/09/08/technology/oracle_safra_catz.fortune/index.htm}}</ref>

==Career==
Catz was a banker at [[Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette]],<ref name="fort2005"/> serving as a managing director from February 1997 to March 1999 and a senior vice president from January 1994 to February 1997 and previously held various investment banking positions since 1986. In 1999, Catz joined Oracle as senior vice president. She has been a non-executive director of Oracle subsidiary [[Hyperion Solutions]] since April 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marketwatch.com/investing/stock/orcl/insiders?pid=654|title=ORCL Safra Ada Catz Insider Trades for Oracle Corp.|publisher=marketwatch.com }}</ref> She has been a member of the executive council of TechNet since March 2013. She was a director of PeopleSoft Inc since December 2004 and Stellent Inc. since December 2006.<ref>Workday’s $10B plan to outsell Oracle. Accounting Today. https://www.accountingtoday.com/articles/workdays-10b-plan-to-outsell-oracle-in-hr-software</ref><ref>Quiénes son las madres tecnológicas más poderosas del mundo, by Desiree Jaimovich. Infobae. https://www.infobae.com/tecno/2016/10/16/quienes-son-las-madres-tecnologicas-mas-poderosas-del-mundo/</ref>

Catz joined Oracle Corporation in April 1999.<ref name="allth1">[http://allthingsd.com/20110425/oracle-co-president-safra-catz-adds-cfo-duties-as-jeff-epstein-leaves/ Oracle Co-President Safra Catz Adds CFO Duties as Jeff Epstein Leaves], an April 25, 2011 article from allthingsd.com</ref>  Catz became a member of the company's [[board of directors]] in October 2001 and president in early 2004.<ref name="allth1"/><ref>[http://www.oracle.com/us/corporate/press/Executives/016342.htm Safra A. Catz | Executive Biography<!-- Bot generated title -->] from Oracle.com</ref>  She is credited for having driven Oracle's 2005 efforts to acquire software rival [[PeopleSoft]] in a $10.3 billion takeover.<ref name="fort2005">[https://www.forbes.com/lists/2005/11/O9O7.html Safra Catz]  from the ''Forbes'' 2005 list of The Most Powerful Women. {{retrieved|accessdate=2012-09-30}}</ref> Catz is also the company's CFO, serving temporarily in that role from November 2005 to September 2008, and from April 2011 to the present.<ref name="allth1"/>  [[Mark Hurd]] joined her as co-president in 2010.<ref name="allth1"/>

In 2009 she was ranked by ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'' as the 12th most powerful woman in business.<ref name="fortune">{{Cite journal| last = Lashinsky | first = Adam| title =The Enforcer | journal = Fortune| volume = 160 | issue = 6 | pages = 117–124 |url= http://brainstormtech.blogs.fortune.cnn.com/2009/09/10/the-enforcer-who-is-oracles-safra-catz/ | date = September 28, 2009}}</ref> In 2009 she was ranked by ''[[Forbes]]'' as the 16th most powerful businesswoman.<ref>{{ cite news | url = https://www.forbes.com/lists/2009/11/power-women-09_The-100-Most-Powerful-Women_Rank.html | title = The 100 Most Powerful Women | work = Forbes.com | date=August 19, 2009}}</ref> In 2014, she was ranked 24th.<ref name=Forbes14>{{cite web|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/#tab:overall|website=Forbes|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=24 June 2014}}</ref> According to an Equilar analysis published by ''Fortune'', she was in 2011 the highest-paid woman among [[Fortune 1000]] companies, receiving an estimated US$51,695,742 in total remuneration.<ref name="toppay2011">{{cite web| url= https://money.cnn.com/gallery/magazines/fortune/2012/09/27/25-highest-paid-women.fortune/ | title= 25 highest-paid women – Safra A. Catz | date= September 27, 2012 | work= [[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]] | publisher= [[CNNMoney]] | accessdate= 2012-09-30}} {{update after|2014}}</ref>

Catz is a lecturer in accounting at the [[Stanford Graduate School of Business]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Stanford GSB: Safra A. Catz|url=https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/faculty/safra-catz|accessdate=2016-03-02}}</ref> Catz was a director of [[HSBC Group]] from 2008 to 2015.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/newsbysector/banksandfinance/11992799/HSBC-board-shake-up-brings-former-Diageo-boss-Paul-Walsh-aboard.html|title=HSBC board shake-up brings former Diageo boss Paul Walsh aboard|author=Szu Ping Chan and Tim Wallace|date=13 November 2015|newspaper=[[Daily Telegraph]] }}</ref>

After the election of Donald Trump, Catz was one of several high-profile CEOs, including [[Tim Cook]], [[Sheryl Sandberg]] and [[Jeff Bezos]], invited to talk with the then president-elect about potentially taking up a position in the incoming administration.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-04-12/trump-team-talked-to-oracle-s-safra-catz-about-an-administration-post|title=Trump Team Talked to Oracle's Safra Catz About an Administration Post|date=2017-04-12|work=Bloomberg.com|access-date=2017-07-20}}</ref> According to Bloomberg, she was considered for the post of U.S. Trade Representative or Director of National Intelligence.<ref name=":0" />

Catz is the highest paid female CEO of any U.S. company as of April 2017, earning $40.9 million after a 23% drop in her total compensation relative to 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestreet.com/story/14093373/1/oracle-s-safra-catz-is-highest-paid-female-ceo.html|title=Oracle's Safra Catz is Highest Paid Female CEO|last=Keitz|first=Anders|date=2017-04-23|work=TheStreet|access-date=2017-07-20|language=en-US}}</ref>

Catz was elected to the board of directors of [[The Walt Disney Company]] in December 2017, effective  February 2018.<ref name="latimesdisneyaddstwoboardmembers">{{cite news|last1=James|first1=Meg|title=Disney adds two board members from tech world|url=http://www.latimes.com/business/hollywood/la-fi-ct-disney-board-catz-desouza-20171207-story.html|accessdate=December 16, 2017|work=The Los Angeles Times|date=December 7, 2017}}</ref>
