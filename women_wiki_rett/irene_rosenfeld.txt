'''Irene Blecker Rosenfeld''' (born May 3, 1953) is an American businesswoman who was the chairman and chief executive officer (CEO) of [[Mondelēz International]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fortune.com/2017/08/02/mondelez-ceo-irene-rosenfeld-stepping-down/|title=Mondelez CEO Irene Rosenfeld to Step Down|author=|date=|website=fortune.com|accessdate=March 18, 2018}}</ref> Rosenfeld's career began at [[Dancer Fitzgerald Sample]], a New York City [[advertising agency]]. She later joined [[General Foods]] consumer research, and then led [[Frito-Lay]] as CEO and chairwoman.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life and education==
Rosenfeld was born to a Jewish family in [[Westbury, New York]], the daughter of Seymour and Joan Blecker. She has one younger sister, Linda Blecker. Her father's parents were Romanian Jews, her mother's grandparents were German Jews. She later attended [[W. Tresper Clarke High School]] in Westbury, NY.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://business.timesonline.co.uk/tol/business/industry_sectors/consumer_goods/article7017876.ece|title=Login|work=Timesonline.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Sandro Rosell |url=http://jewishvoiceny.com/index.php?option=com_content&id=1897:the-worlds-most-powerful-jewish-women&Itemid=325 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402165315/http://jewishvoiceny.com/index.php?option=com_content&id=1897:the-worlds-most-powerful-jewish-women&Itemid=325 |archive-date=2015-04-02 |title=Jewish Voice |website=Jewishvoiceny.com |date= |accessdate=2017-02-26}}</ref> She holds a PhD in marketing and statistics, a master's degree in business, and a bachelor's degree in psychology from [[Cornell University]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=616556&ticker=MDLZ&previousCapId=32854&previousTitle=PEPSICO%20INC|title=Executive profile: Irene B. Rosenfeld|publisher=Businessweek|accessdate=March 1, 2013}}</ref> where she also excelled at sports, like basketball, volleyball and tennis, often serving as team captain, which she cites as "a key factor in my leadership development."<ref>[https://www.exceptionalchicagoans.com/irene-rosenfeld The Executives' Club of Chicago] ''Exceptional Chicagoans'': "Irene Rosenfeld", 2017. [https://web.archive.org/web/20171012044022/https://www.exceptionalchicagoans.com/irene-rosenfeld Archived copy]</ref>

==Career==
Rosenfeld has been involved in the food and beverage industry for about 30 years. Her first job was at [[Dancer Fitzgerald Sample]] [[advertising agency]] in New York City; she later joined [[General Foods]] in consumer research.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/2010/10/22/rosenfeld-cadbury-frito-intelligent-investing-kraft.html|title=Next Week's Guest: Irene Rosenfeld|author=Alexandra Zendrian|date=October 25, 2010|work=Forbes.com}}</ref>

In 2004, Rosenfeld was appointed chairwoman and CEO of Frito-Lay, a division of [[PepsiCo]], where she focused on product promotion.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.cornell.edu/stories/2012/03/hatfield-lecturer-details-krafts-dramatic-turnaround|title=Rosenfeld details Kraft Foods' dramatic turnaround  |publisher=Cornell Chronicle|date=March 8, 2012}}</ref>

Rosenfeld was appointed CEO of [[Kraft Foods]] In June 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kraftfoodscompany.com/About/profile/Irene-Rosenfeld-Bio.aspx|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090615011206/http://www.kraftfoodscompany.com/About/profile/Irene-Rosenfeld-Bio.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 15, 2009|title=Irene RosenfeldChairman and CEO|author=|date=June 15, 2009|accessdate=March 18, 2018}}</ref> She joined General Foods which later became a part of Kraft. Among her many accomplishments at Kraft, she led the restructuring and turnaround of key business in the US, Canada and Moscow. She is active in a number of industry and community organisations, including the Economic Club of Chicago. She was appointed to the additional post of chairman in March 2007, following [[Altria Group]]'s spin-off of Kraft.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

In 2008, she was named sixth on [[The Wall Street Journal]]'s "50 Women to Watch" list.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB122608990529609245#project%3DTOP50WOMEN%26articleTabs%3Dinteractive|title=The Wall Street Journal 50 Women to Watch in 2008 - WSJ|date=November 11, 2008|work=WSJ|url-access=subscription}}</ref> Rosenfeld has been listed multiple times as one of the 100 most powerful women in the world by ''Forbes''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/lists/2010/11/power-women_2010.html|title=World's Most Powerful Women List 2010|work=Forbes.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/wealth/power-women|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|work=Forbes.com}}</ref> where she was ranked at 15th, just behind [[Oprah Winfrey]], in 2014.<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.forbes.com/wealth/power-women#p_2_s_arank |title=The world's 100 most powerful women |website=Forbes.com |date= |accessdate=August 24, 2011}}</ref>

In 2010, Rosenfeld earned total compensation of $19.3 million, placing her 48th on Forbes Executive Pay.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.forbes.com/profile/irene-b-rosenfeld/3845|title=Sheikha Mozah Bint Nasser Al-Missned|author=Zina Moukheiber|work=Forbes.com|access-date=September 1, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120420054615/http://people.forbes.com/profile/irene-b-rosenfeld/3845|archive-date=April 20, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Rosenfeld is a member of the Economic Club of Chicago. She serves on the board of directors for the [[Grocery Manufacturers Association]] and the [[Cornell University]] board of trustees. She sits on the board of directors of the [[Consumer Goods Forum]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theconsumergoodsforum.com/1-wweare/1.3-board/index.asp|title=History of The Consumer Goods Forum|work=Theconsumergoodsforum.com|access-date=November 11, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120831074012/http://www.theconsumergoodsforum.com/1-wweare/1.3-board/index.asp|archive-date=August 31, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In August 2011, Kraft said it planned to split into two publicly traded companies, one focusing on its international snack brands like [[Trident gum]] and [[Oreo]] cookies and the other on its North American groceries business that includes [[Maxwell House]] coffee and [[Oscar Mayer]] meats.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/topic/pnr-kraft-foods-plans-to-split-into-2-companies-20110804,0,3771108.story |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120728121010/http://www.chicagotribune.com/topic/pnr-kraft-foods-plans-to-split-into-2-companies-20110804,0,3771108.story |url-status=dead |archive-date=July 28, 2012 |title=Kraft Foods plans to split into 2 companies |newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]] |accessdate=December 5, 2011}}</ref>

On December 5, 2011, Kraft announced that Rosenfeld would stay on as chairman of the $31 billion global snacking company, which would be called Mondelēz International, Inc. [[Tony Vernon]], the president of Kraft Foods North America, would become CEO of the $17 billion North American grocery business, which would keep the Kraft Foods name.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/business/breaking/chi-kraft-chooses-leaders-for-separate-companies-20111205,0,6435818.story|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120206135407/http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2011-12-05/business/chi-kraft-chooses-leaders-for-separate-companies-20111205_1_ceo-irene-rosenfeld-kraft-chairman-kraft-foods-north-america|archive-date=2012-02-06|title=Kraft chooses leaders for separate companies |first=Emily Bryson |last=York |newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]] |date=December 5, 2011}}</ref>

[[Mondelez International|Mondelez]] announced in August 2017 that [[Dirk Van de Put]] would replace Rosenfeld as CEO of the company following her retirement in November 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2017/08/01/mondelez.html|title=Dirk Van de Put named CEO of Mondelēz as long-time CEO Irene Rosenfeld retires|last=CNBC|date=2017-08-02|access-date=2017-08-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/11/14/business/food-irene-rosenfeld-retires-mondelez.html |title=A Big Deal in Big Food, Irene Rosenfeld Retires From Mondelēz |first=David |last=Gelles |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |date=November 14, 2017}}</ref>
