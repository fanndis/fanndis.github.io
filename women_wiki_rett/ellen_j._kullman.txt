'''Ellen J. Kullman''' (born January 22, 1956) is a United States [[Executive officer|business executive]]. She was formerly [[Chairman|Chair]] and [[Chief executive officer|Chief Executive Officer]] of [[DuPont|E. I. du Pont de Nemours and Company]] ("DuPont") in Wilmington and is a former director of [[General Motors]]. ''[[Forbes]]'' ranked her 31st of the 100 Most Powerful Women in 2014.<ref name=Forbes14>{{cite news|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/#tab:overall|website=www.forbes.com|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=24 June 2014}}</ref> Kullman retired from DuPont on October 16, 2015.<ref name=retired>{{cite news|last1=de la Merced|first1=Michael J.|title=DuPont Chief Executive Ellen Kullman to Retire|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/10/06/business/dealbook/dupont-chief-executive-ellen-kullman-to-retire.html?_r=0|accessdate=5 October 2015|work=Dealbook|publisher=New York Times|date=5 October 2015}}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:Ellen J. Kullman - World Economic Forum Annual Meeting Davos 2009.jpg |thumb|right| Kullman at the annual meeting of the [[World Economic Forum]] in [[Davos]], Switzerland, January 30, 2009]]

Kullman began her business career at [[General Electric]] and joined DuPont in 1988 as marketing manager in the company's [[medical imaging]] business.<ref name="newera" /><ref name="forbesbio" /> In her later role as executive vice president she was responsible for four of [[DuPont#Current activities|DuPont's business platforms]] as well as for leading the company's growth in markets outside the USA.<ref name="newera" />

Kullman's retirement was announced by DuPont on October 5, 2015 (effective October 16, 2015) following a battle with activist investor [[Nelson Peltz]] and Trian Fund Management, his investment firm. Peltz sought 4 board seats and argued that DuPont needed to cut costs and meet financial targets, but had lost a shareholder vote earlier in the year.<ref name=retired/>

==Controversy==

On March 26, 2018, Kullman's alma mater [[Tufts University]] announced they would be awarding her an honorary degree. In the weeks leading up to graduation, this was met by University-wide outrage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tuftsdaily.com/features/2018/04/17/students-faculty-respond-choice-ellen-kullman-commencement-speaker/|title=Students, faculty respond to choice of Ellen Kullman as commencement speaker|date=2018-04-17|website=The Tufts Daily|language=en|access-date=2019-08-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tuftsdaily.com/opinion/editorial/2018/04/03/editorial-ellen-kullman-bizarre-choice-commencement-speaker/|title=Editorial: Ellen Kullman is a bizarre choice for commencement speaker|date=2018-04-03|website=The Tufts Daily|language=en|access-date=2019-08-07}}</ref> During her speech at the University's graduation, almost a dozen students protested with signs and turned their backs to her.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www3.bostonglobe.com/metro/2018/05/20/tufts-graduates-protest-former-dupont-ceo-during-commencement-speech/fB7ZE81Td2yluVhkw4RNnJ/story.html|title=Tufts students protest former DuPont CEO during commencement speech|date=May 20, 2018|access-date=2019-08-07}}</ref>
