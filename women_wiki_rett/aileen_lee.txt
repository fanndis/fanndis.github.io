Lee coined the often-used Silicon Valley term [[Unicorn (finance)|unicorn]] in a ''[[TechCrunch]]'' article "Welcome To The Unicorn Club: Learning from Billion-Dollar Startups" as profiled in ''[[The New York Times]]''. A unicorn is generally defined as a privately held startup that has a $1 billion valuation or more – something rare (like a unicorn).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/07/05/unicorns-a-fitting-word-for-its-time-and-place|author= Manjoo, Farhad|date = 5 July 2015|title=Unicorn: A Fitting Label for Its Time and Place|work=The New York Times}}</ref>

== Education ==
Lee earned her bachelor's degree from the [[MIT Sloan School of Management]] in 1992. After MIT, she worked as a financial analyst for two years at [[Morgan Stanley]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/12/06/business/aileen-lee-of-cowboy-ventures-aim-to-see-beneath-the-surface.html|title=Aileen Lee of Cowboy Ventures: Aim to See Beneath the Surface|last=Bryant|first=Adam|date=2015-12-03|newspaper=The New York Times|access-date=2016-11-17|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> She earned her MBA from [[Harvard Business School]] in 1997.<ref name="insiders" />

== Career ==
Lee joined [[Kleiner Perkins]] (KPCB) in 1999 and was the founding CEO of [[RMG Networks]], a company backed by KPCB. Lee worked at Kleiner Perkins for 13 years and left in 2012.<ref>{{cite web |title=Recode: Women in tech are held to tougher standards than men — and that has to change, investor Aileen Lee says |url=https://www.recode.net/2016/10/3/13144260/aileen-lee-cowboy-ventures-kleiner-perkins-gender-diversity-women-in-tech-recode-decode-podcast}}</ref>

In 2012, she left KPCB to start seed-stage venture firm Cowboy Ventures.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2017/07/03/vc-aileen-lee-just-offered-some-very-specific-advice-to-female-founders-looking-for-funding/|title=VC Aileen Lee just offered some very specific advice to female founders looking for funding|last=Loizos|first=Connie|work=TechCrunch|access-date=2017-11-16|language=en}}</ref> In 2017, Lee added [[Ted Wang]] to the firm as a general partner.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pando: Aileen Lee on hiring Ted Wang and making VC more "human" |url=https://pando.com/2017/01/24/cowboy-ventures-aileen-lee-hiring-ted-wang/}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Crunchbase: Cowboy Ventures Closes Capital For $95 Million Third Fund |url=https://news.crunchbase.com/news/cowboy-ventures-closes-capital-for-95-million-third-fund/}}</ref>

Cowboy Ventures is one of the first female-led venture capital firms.<ref>{{cite web |title=Fast Company: Tech's Actual Gender Numbers Are Vague And Grim. A Small Tweak Could Change That |url=https://www.fastcompany.com/3043319/startups-gender-diversity-numbers-crunchbase}}</ref> Over the past six years, Cowboy Ventures has received three large funds, the most recent reaching $95 million.<ref>{{cite web |title=TechCrunch: Cowboy Ventures just rounded up $95 million for its third fund |url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/08/07/cowboy-ventures-just-rounded-up-95-million-for-its-third-fund/}}</ref><ref name=recentendeavors>{{cite web |title=Pitchbook: Q&A: Aileen Lee on Cowboy Ventures' latest fund, getting women on her cap table and what keeps her motivated |url=https://pitchbook.com/news/articles/qa-aileen-lee-on-cowboy-ventures-latest-fund-getting-women-on-her-cap-table-and-what-keeps-her-motivated}}</ref>

Through Cowboy Ventures, Lee has made investments in many early-stage companies, including August,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.startupgrind.com/blog/vc-corner-aileen-lee-of-cowboy-ventures/|title=VC Corner: Aileen Lee of Cowboy Ventures|website=www.startupgrind.com|access-date=2016-06-01}}</ref> [[Dollar Shave Club]],<ref name=":0" /> Accompany<ref name=":2" /> and [[Tally Technologies]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2016/05/19/tally-raises-15-million-for-app-to-make-credit-cards-less-expensive-easier-to-manage/|title=Tally raises $15 million for app to make credit cards less expensive, easier to manage|last=Kolodny|first=Lora|website=TechCrunch|access-date=2016-06-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/sanjose/blog/techflash/2016/05/credit-card-manager-tally-raises-15m-to-cut.html|title=San Francisco-based credit card management app Tally raises $15 million - Silicon Valley Business Journal|website=Silicon Valley Business Journal|access-date=2016-06-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://angel.co/aileenlee|title=Aileen Lee|last=|first=|date=|website=angel.co|publisher=|access-date=}}</ref> She is a public advocate of increasing the number of female founders and investors in the Silicon Valley.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2017/07/03/vc-aileen-lee-just-offered-some-very-specific-advice-to-female-founders-looking-for-funding/|title=VC Aileen Lee just offered some very specific advice to female founders looking for funding|last=Loizos|first=Connie|work=TechCrunch|access-date=2017-12-18|language=en}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
In 2018, Lee co-founded All Raise, a nonprofit organization which seeks to increase the amount of funding that female investors receive.<ref name=recentendeavors/> The organization was founded as a collective by more than 30 venture capitalists who advocate for increasing the presence of women in venture capital.<ref>{{cite web |title=Fast Company: This is how we get more women in venture capital |url=https://www.fastcompany.com/90233436/this-is-how-we-get-more-women-in-venture-capital}}</ref> Lee described the organization's importance in saying “We believe that by improving the success of women in the venture-backed tech ecosystem, we can build a more accessible community that reflects the diversity of the world around us.”<ref>{{cite web |title=TechCrunch: All Raise wants to increase the amount of venture funding female founders receive |url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/04/03/allraise-wants-to-increase-the-amount-of-venture-funding-female-founders-receive/}}</ref>
