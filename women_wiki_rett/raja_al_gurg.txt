'''Raja Al Gurg''' is a businesswoman who lives in [[Dubai]] in the [[United Arab Emirates]].She is the Managing Director of the 
[[Easa Saleh Al Gurg Group]] and a Board Member of the [[Dubai Chamber of Commerce and Industry]]. Raja Easa Al Gurg has been ranked 28th 
in the Arab Power 2018 listing of the 100 Most Powerful Arabs, compiled by Gulf Business 
magazine rising from the previous ranking in 2017.<ref name=gulfbusiness>{{cite web|url=http://gulfbusiness.com/lists/top-100-most-powerful-arabs-2018/
 |title=Top 100 most powerful Arabs |publisher=GulfBusiness.com |date= |accessdate=2018-03-04}}</ref> She was the 
most powerful Emirati Businesswoman in Forbes Middle East's 2017 ranking <ref name=orbesmiddleeast1>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbesmiddleeast.com/en/powerful-emirati-businesswomen
 |title=Powerful Emarati Business women |publisher=forbesmiddleeast.com |date= |accessdate=2017-08-28}}</ref> and the third most influential businesswoman in the Arab world in 
Forbes Middle East's 100 Most Powerful Arab Businesswomen for 2017,<ref name=orbesmiddleeast2>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbesmiddleeast.com/en/list/top-100-powerful-arab-businesswomen-2017/
 |title=Top 100 powerful Arab Business women |publisher=forbesmiddleeast.com |date= |accessdate=2017-08-28}}</ref> ranking. She was listed 90th among ‘The World’s 100 Most 
Powerful Women’ by Forbes for 2017.<ref name=orbesmiddleeast3>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbesmiddleeast.com/en/three-arab-women-on-the-worlds-100-most-powerful-women-list/
 |title=Three Arab Women On The World’s 100 Most Powerful Women List|publisher=forbesmiddleeast.com |date=2017-11-06 |accessdate=2019-08-18}}</ref>

==History==
Raja Al Gurg graduated from [[Kuwait University]] in 1977 with a degree in English Literature and served as headmistress of Zabeel Secondary School for Girls from 1978 to 1989.<ref name=AIWF-bio>http://www.aiwfonline.com/Files/CommemorativeConference/Dec%202007%20Dubai%20Conference%20Programme.pdf</ref> In 1989 Al Gurg joined the Board of Directors of the [[Easa Saleh Al Gurg Group]] and is currently the Group's Managing Director.

In September 2003, Al Gurg, along with Sheikha [[Lubna Al-Qasimi]], led a delegation of Dubai businesswomen to the US - Arab Economic Forum.<ref name="AMEinfo-delegation to US">{{cite web|url=http://www.ameinfo.com/news/Detailed/28535.html |title=Delegation from Dubai Business Women's Council (DBWC) attends landmark U.S. - Arab Economic Forum &#124; Tejari |publisher=AMEinfo.com |date= |accessdate=2012-10-20}}</ref>

In December 2019, Dr. Raja Easa Al Gurg released her autobiography, entitled "Raja Al Gurg - An Autobiography." She is the first Emirati businesswoman to write and release an autobiography. <ref>https://www.algurg.com/News-Details/Dr-Raja-Al-Gurg-unveils-her-autobiography/24158</ref>

Forbes placed Al Gurg in third place on its 2020 100 Most Powerful Arab Businesswomen List. 18 women from the UAE made this year's list, followed closely by Egypt with 16. <ref>https://www.algurg.com/News-Details/Dr-Raja-Al-Gurg-is-third-in-Forbes-100-most-powerful-Arab-businesswomen-ranking/16125</ref>

==Other positions==
Apart from her position with the Easa Saleh Al Gurg Group, Al Gurg also holds a number of positions with other companies and organisations. 
In March 2020, the National Bank of Fujairah appointed Al Gurg as the new Deputy Chairperson of its board. She takes over this position from her father, His Excellency Easa Saleh Al Gurg. <ref>https://nbf.ae/en/media-content/announcements/press-release/2020/25-03-2020</ref>
She is the President of [[Dubai Business Women Council]];<ref name=dbwc>{{cite web|url=http://www.dbwc.ae/about-us/dbwc-board-members
 |title=DBWC Board Members  |publisher=dbwc.ae }}</ref> Vice Chairperson of the Board of Directors, [[Dubai Healthcare City Authority]];
<ref name=dhcc>{{cite web|url=https://www.dhcc.ae/Portal/en/media-center/news/9/5/2018/dhca-excellence-awards-ceremony-2018.aspx
 |title=DHCA Excellence Awards  |publisher=dhcc.ae }}</ref> 
Board member of the Dubai Chamber Of Commerce & Industry (DCCI) and Dubai Women's Association. Raja Al Gurg is the [[first Emirati woman]] on the board 
of HSBC Bank Middle East Limited<ref name=hsbc>{{cite web|url=https://gulfnews.com/business/sectors/banking/raja-easa-al-gurg-hsbc-s-independent-non-executive-director-1.1326559
 |title=Raja Al Gurg HSBC |publisher=gulfnews.com |date= |accessdate=2014-04-30}}</ref> and is also on the advisory board of Coutts Bank, the wealth division of the Royal Bank of Scotland Group. Raja Al Gurg is also on the board of trustees for the [[Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum Global Initiatives]] - one of the largest development and community foundations in the Middle East.<ref name=emirates247>{{cite web|url=https://www.emirates247.com/news/government/mohammed-establishes-global-initiatives-foundation-2015-12-29-1.615452
 |title=Mohammed establishes Global Initiatives Foundation |publisher=emirates247.com |date= |accessdate=2015-12-29}}</ref> 
She was awarded with an Honorary Doctorate at Queen's University Belfast for services to business and commerce.<ref name=HonoraryDoctorate>{{cite web|url=http://www.qub.ac.uk/News/Allnews/LeadingArabBusinesswomanandUniversityofMassachusettsPresidentawardedbyQueens.html
 |title=Honorary Doctorate  |publisher=www.qub.ac.uk }}</ref>
