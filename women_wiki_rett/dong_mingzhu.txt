'''Dong Mingzhu''' ({{zh|c=董明珠|p=Dǒng Míngzhū}}; born August 1954) is a Chinese businesswoman who serves as Chairwoman of [[Gree Electric]].<ref name="forbes">{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/dong-mingzhu/|title=Dong Mingzhu|last=|first=|date=|website=Forbes|publisher=Forbes|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref>

==Early life==
Dong Mingzhu was born the youngest of seven children in a working-class family in [[Nanjing]], capital of [[east China]]'s [[Jiangsu]] province, in August 1954.{{sfnb|Han Xiao|2017|p=1}} When she was a child, she wanted to be a [[soldier]].<ref name=NYT1/> Dong graduated from a specialized institute in Wuhu, city in Anhui province in 1975, with a degree in Statistics.  After graduation, Dong got an administrative job at local government [[chemistry]] [[laboratory]] in Nanjing for 15 years.<ref name="NYT1" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.scmp.com/business/companies/article/1388584/succeeding-degrees|title=Succeeding by Degrees|last=Ren|first=Daniel|date=23 December 2014|website=South China Morning Post|publisher=South China Morning Post|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1990, at age 36, recently widowed Dong left her three-year-old son to his grandmother and quit her job at the government research facility in order to move to the more economically developed [[Shenzhen]] in [[Guangdong]] province and to find a new job, but moved to [[Zhuhai]] soon.{{sfnb|Han Xiao|2017|p=12}}{{citation needed|date=May 2018}}

Joining Haley (Gree Electric's predecessor) as a salesperson, she made her mark from the start, recovering massive debts in a little over one month. By 1994, Dong was made head of sales, where she upgraded sales process. By the time Gree Electric went public in 1996, Dong was deputy president, and later company president in 2001.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.brand-intl.cn/WebSite/article.aspx?id=118|title=Dong Mingzhu: A Powerful Self-made Chinese Businesswoman|last=|first=|date=|website=Brand International|publisher=Brand International|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref>  In 2012, she gained the position of Gree Electric's chairwoman as well. She was also the chairwoman of Gree Electric's parent company [[Gree Group]] until November 2016.

During her tenure, Dong developed Gree Electric into the world's largest household air conditioning unit maker, and China's largest household appliance maker (generating a record breaking 140 billion Yuan revenue in 2014). Gree Electric's sales shifted from traditional to online, contributing significantly to the company's 2014 record breaking revenue.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://en.yibada.com/articles/87359/20151121/gree-s-dong-mingzhu-tops-fortune-influential-businesswomen-china.htm|title=Gree's Dong Mingzhu Top's Fortune's Most Influential Businesswomen in China|last=Borromeo|first=EL|date=21 November 2015|website=Yibada|publisher=Yibada|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref> Gree Electric's company stock has risen 2300% during her stay.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://fortune.com/most-powerful-women-asia-pacific/dong-mingzhu-4/|title=MPW Asia-Pacific 2015|last=|first=|date=|website=Fortune|publisher=Fortune|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref> Under her leadership, Gree Electric developed [[solar energy]], China's [[smartphone]] market, [[robotics|robotic technology]], [[recycling|recycled treatment centers]] nationwide, and acquired [[electric car]] maker Yinlong in March 2016.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/pictures/fdgk45jklj/dong-mingzhu-61-china/#6b619b083563|title=Asia Power Women 2016|last=Scott|first=Mary E.|date=6 April 2016|website=Forbes|publisher=Forbes|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref>

In January 2019, she was reelected chairwoman of Gree Electric.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/201901/17/WS5c4024b8a3106c65c34e50b5.html|title=Dong Mingzhu reelected as Gree head|website=China Daily|date=17 January 2019|author=Tan Xinyu|accessdate=13 June 2019}}</ref> 2 months later, she applied a cut on value-added taxes to reach more competitive prices and aim for an aggressive international development despite the US-China trade war going on.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3001725/forget-trade-war-chinas-home-appliance-queen-insists-gree|title=China's home appliance queen Dong Mingzhu backs ambitious Gree Electric target, plays down trade war|website=South China Morning Post|date=15 March 2019|author=Cissy Zhou|accessdate=13 June 2019}}</ref>

Dong has displayed a strong nationalistic streak over the year, and has been a member of the 10th, 11th, and 12th [[National People's Congress]].<ref name=":1" /> She is a member of the [[China Democratic National Construction Association]], as well as a member of the 10th Executive Committee of All-china Women's Federation.<ref name="NYT1" />  Dong currently holds senior positions in a dozen industry, women's and charity organizations, and has taught university business classes.<ref name=":1" />

==Personal life==
Dong married soon after graduating from university, but was widowed when her son turned two-year-old.<ref name=":1" /> Her husband died of illness in 1984.{{sfnb|Han Xiao|2017|p=11}} Her son nicknamed "Dongdong" ({{zh|s=东东|labels=no}}).{{sfnb|Han Xiao|2017|p=11}} She never remarried.<ref name="NYT1" /> ''[[The New York Times]]'' called her "one of the toughest businesswomen in China."<ref name=NYT1>{{cite web|last=Tatlow|first=Didi Kristen|title=Setting the Pace With Toughness|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/01/27/world/asia/27iht-dong27.html?pagewanted=all |work=The New York Times|accessdate=5 March 2013}}</ref>
