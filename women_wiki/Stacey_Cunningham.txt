'''Stacey Cunningham''' is a banker, and the [[List of presidents of the New York Stock Exchange|67th president]] of the [[New York Stock Exchange|New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)]]. She is the second female president of the NYSE, but the first to hold full leadership of the exchange.

After interning with the NYSE during the 1990s, Cunningham became a trading floor clerk, continuing to work at the exchange until 2005. She took time off to complete a culinary training course, then working at the [[NASDAQ|Nasdaq]] stock exchange as director of capital markets and head of sales for U. S. transaction services. In 2012, Cunningham rejoined the NYSE, and became the exchange's chief operating officer in 2015.

On May 22, 2018, Cunningham was appointed president of the NYSE. In November 2018, she was listed as one of [[100 Women (BBC)|BBC's 100 Women.]] <ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-46225037|title=BBC 100 Women 2018: Who is on the list?|date=2018-11-19|work=BBC News|access-date=2018-11-21|language=en-GB}}</ref>

== Early life ==
As a child, Cunningham had a strong interest in math and science in school.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2018/jul/20/stacey-cunningham-new-york-stock-exchange-first-female-president|title='We need a call to action': Stacey Cunningham, the NYSE's first female president|last=Rushe|first=Dominic|date=July 20, 2018|work=The Guardian|access-date=August 18, 2018|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> She later studied at [[Lehigh University]], graduating in 1996 with a bachelor's degree in industrial engineering.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/markets/2018/05/22/stacey-cunningham-first-woman-lead-nyse/631886002/|title=The New York Stock Exchange's new president is a woman, at last|work=USA TODAY|access-date=May 23, 2018|language=en}}</ref> She has five siblings<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://time.com/5362170/stacey-cunningham-nyse/|title=Stacey Cunningham, Head of the New York Stock Exchange, Talks History as She Changes It|website=Time|language=en|access-date=August 19, 2018}}</ref> and when she was young her father worked at a brokerage firm.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2018/05/nyse-president/561047/|title=The New York Stock Exchange Has Its First Woman President. Is She on a Glass Cliff?|last=Vara|first=Vauhini|work=The Atlantic|access-date=May 26, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:New_York_Stock_Exchange_August_2017_01.jpg|left|thumb|The [[New York Stock Exchange]] (NYSE)]]
In 1994, while still a university student, Cunningham completed a summer internship at the NYSE.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://time.com/money/5286940/new-york-stock-exchange-stacey-cunningham/|title=Meet the Next President of the New York Stock Exchange, a 43-Year-Old Former Intern Who Briefly Left to Become a Chef|website=Money|language=en|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref> Two years later, she began working there as a trading floor clerk.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=https://money.cnn.com/2018/05/22/investing/nyse-woman-president/index.html|title=NYSE will be run by a woman for the first time in 226-year history|last=Isidore|first=Chris|work=CNNMoney|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref> At the time, Cunningham was one of the few dozen female employees on the trading floor – compared to over a thousand male employees.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/new-york-stock-exchange-to-have-first-female-leader-in-226-year-history-1526955129|title=New York Stock Exchange to Have First Female Leader in 226-Year History|last=Hope|first=Bradley|date=May 22, 2018|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=May 23, 2018|last2=Osipovich|first2=Alexander|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> She spent eight years employed as a specialist for the [[Bank of America]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestreet.com/world/4-things-to-know-about-new-nyse-head-stacey-cunningham-14597072|title=Stacey Cunningham: From Intern to Head of the New York Stock Exchange|last=Grant|first=Kinsey|date=May 22, 2018|work=TheStreet|access-date=May 22, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2005, feeling frustrated with the lack of technological transition at the NYSE,<ref name=":5">{{Cite news|url=http://fortune.com/2018/05/22/nyse-president-stacey-cunningham-woman/|title=Meet Stacey Cunningham, the One-Time NYSE Intern Who Will Be the Exchange's First Woman President|work=Fortune|access-date=May 23, 2018|language=en}}</ref> she left the exchange and studied at the [[Institute of Culinary Education]], working briefly as a chef at a restaurant.<ref name=":2" />

From 2007 to 2011, Cunningham worked at the [[NASDAQ|Nasdaq]] stock exchange, first as director of capital markets and then as the head of sales for U.S. transaction services.<ref name=":0" />

Cunningham rejoined the NYSE in 2012. She served as the exchange's chief operating officer from 2015 to 2018.<ref name=":0" /> Her job involved handling the exchange's cash equities markets, relationship and product management, and internal governance services.<ref name=":6">{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-interconti-exc-management-nyse/nyse-appoints-stacey-cunningham-as-first-female-president-idUSKCN1IN0BT|title=NYSE appoints Stacey Cunningham as first female president|last=Editorial|first=Reuters|work=U.S.|access-date=May 22, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

On May 22, 2018, at the age of 43,<ref name=":4" /> Cunningham was named the 67th president of the NYSE, taking over from [[Thomas W. Farley|Thomas Farley]].<ref name=":0" /> Cunningham is the first woman to be appointed president of the NYSE.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/05/22/nyse-president-says-june-ipo-calendar-is-strong-as-she-takes-on-new-role.html|title=NYSE president says June IPO calendar is 'strong' as she takes on new role|last=Moyer|first=Liz|date=May 22, 2018|work=CNBC|access-date=May 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":3" /><ref name=":6" /><ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodmorningamerica.com/news/story/meet-woman-lead-nyse-226-years-55351824|title=What to know about the first female president of the New York Stock Exchange|date=May 22, 2018|website=ABC News|language=en|access-date=May 23, 2018}}</ref> Although [[Catherine R. Kinney|Catherine Kinney]] had been co-president of the exchange in 2002, the position had not entailed full leadership at the time, being firmly overseen by the CEO or chairman of the NYSE.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/thetwo-way/2018/05/22/613353836/a-woman-has-been-named-as-nyse-ceo-it-only-took-226-years|title=A Woman Has Been Named As NYSE President. It Only Took 226 Years|work=NPR.org|access-date=May 23, 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":4" /> In an interview with reporters, Cunningham said that she has handled working in traditionally male-dominated environments – such as stock exchanges or engineering school – by never showing any doubt about "whether or not I should be where I was.”<ref name=":5" />
