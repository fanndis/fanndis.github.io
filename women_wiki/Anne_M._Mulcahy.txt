'''Anne M. Mulcahy''' (born October 21, 1952) is the former chairperson and [[chief executive officer|CEO]] of [[Xerox Corporation]]. She was named CEO of Xerox on August 1, 2001, and chairwoman on January 1, 2002. In addition to serving on the Xerox board, she has been a member of the boards of directors of Catalyst, [[Citigroup]] Inc., [[Fuji Xerox]] Co. Ltd. and [[Target Corporation]].

She was selected as 'CEO of the Year 2008' by ''[[Chief Executive (magazine)|Chief Executive]]'' magazine.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chiefexecutive.net/ME2/Audiences/Default.asp?AudID=257093CD337F495B86A6A07046702F8C |title=2008 CEO of the Year Award: Anne Mulcahy, Xerox CEO |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091103115359/http://www.chiefexecutive.net/ME2/Audiences/Default.asp?AudID=257093CD337F495B86A6A07046702F8C |archivedate=2009-11-03 }}</ref> She announced her retirement as CEO on May 21, 2009 prior to the company's annual shareholder meeting.<ref name="rnews_May2009">http://www.rnews.com/Story_2004.cfm?ID=72467&rnews_story_type=18&category=10</ref>

==Early life and education==
Mulcahy was born in [[Rockville Centre, New York]] on October 21, 1952. She was the only daughter in her family and attended Catholic school as a child. She received a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in English and Journalism from [[Marymount College, Tarrytown|Marymount College of Fordham University]] in [[Tarrytown, New York]].<ref>{{cite news|last=STERN|first=IRENA CHOI|title=EDUCATION; A Diploma for the Ages|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/05/12/nyregion/education-a-diploma-for-the-ages.html?src=pm|accessdate=8 March 2012|newspaper=The New York Times|date=12 May 2002}}</ref>

==Career at Xerox==
Mulcahy joined Xerox as a field sales representative in 1976 and rose through the ranks. From 1992–1995, Mulcahy was vice president for human resources, responsible for compensation, benefits, human resource strategy, labor relations, management development and employee training. She became chief staff officer in 1997 and corporate senior vice president in 1998. Prior to that, she served as vice president and staff officer for Customer Operations, covering South America and Central America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and China.
<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sec.gov/spotlight/xbrl/2006/ammulcahy_bio.pdf|title=Anne M. Mulcahey|access-date=2017-11-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170825203416/https://www.sec.gov/spotlight/xbrl/2006/ammulcahy_bio.pdf|archive-date=2017-08-25|url-status=dead}}</ref> Though never intent on running Xerox, she was selected by the board of directors in 2001. Later in her tenure, she ordered a restructuring which cut annual expenses by $1.7 billion, cut the workforce by 25,000 jobs, and sold $2.3 billion in noncore assets in order to reduce Xerox's long-term debt.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.referenceforbusiness.com/biography/M-R/Mulcahy-Anne-M-1952.html|title=Anne M. Mulcahy 1952— Biography - Leading by listening, sources for further information|website=www.referenceforbusiness.com|language=en|access-date=2017-11-15}}</ref> {{Citation needed|date=July 2011}}

When she became CEO on Aug 1, 2001 the stock price was $8.25, and on Jan 1, 2002 when she became chairwoman the stock price was $10.05. On May 21, 2009, the day she announced her retirement as CEO, the stock price was $6.82.{{Citation needed|date=July 2011}}

Mulcahy served on four other Boards of Directors besides Xerox. She also served on Catalyst, Citigroup, Fuji Xerox and Target Corp. A letter sent to Citi shareholders on March 26, 2009 by the labor union, American Federation of State County & Municipal Employees (AFSCME), recommended shareholders vote against re-electing six of their directors<ref name="afscme.org">{{cite web|url=http://www.afscme.org/press/25934.cfm |title=AFSCME Demands Accountability at Citigroup |publisher=afscme.org |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101130163118/http://www.afscme.org/press/25934.cfm |archivedate=2010-11-30 }}</ref>

==Magazine Opinion==
''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' named Mulcahy one of 50 women to watch in 2005 and ''[[Forbes]]'' magazine ranked her at the sixth position among the Most Powerful Women in America in 2005. In 2009, she was ranked 15th.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/lists/2009/11/power-women-09_The-100-Most-Powerful-Women_Rank.html|title=The 100 Most Powerful Women|work=Forbes.com}}</ref> In 2008, she was selected by [[U.S. News & World Report]] as one of America's Best Leaders.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usnews.com/articles/news/best-leaders/2008/11/19/americas-best-leaders-anne-mulcahy-xerox-ceo.html |title=America's Best Leaders: Anne Mulcahy, Xerox CEO |accessdate=2008-11-25 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081210054608/http://www.usnews.com/articles/news/best-leaders/2008/11/19/americas-best-leaders-anne-mulcahy-xerox-ceo.html |archivedate=10 December 2008 |url-status=live }}</ref>
