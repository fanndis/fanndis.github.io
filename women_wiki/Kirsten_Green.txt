'''Kirsten Green''' (born 1971/1972) is an American venture capitalist, the founder and managing partner of Forerunner Ventures.<ref name="wsj.com">[https://www.wsj.com/articles/a-day-in-the-life-of-silicon-valley-power-player-kirsten-green-1507049782/ "A Day in the Life of Silicon Valley Power Player Kirsten Green"] WSJ, October 3rd, 2017</ref>

== Early life ==
Green was born and raised outside of [[San Francisco]], [[California]]. She attended the [[University of California, Los Angeles|University of California Los Angeles]], where she obtained a degree in business economics.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/kirsten-green/|title=Kirsten Green|work=Forbes|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
Following her studies at UCLA, Green worked as a stock analyst for [[Montgomery Securities]] covering retailers.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/26/technology/top-tier-venture-capitalists-investors.html|title=A Rare Venture Capitalist â€” Female and Retail-Focused|last1=Benner|first1=Katie|date=2017-03-26|work=The New York Times|access-date=2018-02-20|last2=Merced|first2=Michael J. de la|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> For several years, she worked as an angel investor and independent consultant before founding San Francisco-based Forerunner Ventures in 2010, where she is also the managing director.<ref>[https://www.recode.net/2017/9/13/16296366/code-commerce-forerunner-ventures-kirsten-green-retail/ "The investor behind startups with billion dollar exits explains how retail needs to change"] Recode, September 13th, 2017</ref>

On August 14, 2020, ''[[Daily Front Row]]'' listed Green as one of a group of high profile investors who purchased ''[[W magazine]]'', a troubled fashion magazine.<ref name=fashionweekdaily2020-08-14/>

== Honors and recognition ==
Green was listed in the [[Forbes list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women]] in 2017, 2018, and 2019, with ranks of 95, 89, and 95, respectively.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/carolinehoward/2017/11/01/the-worlds-most-powerful-women-in-tech-2017-still-a-minority-their-clout-is-growing/ |title=The World's Most Powerful Women In Tech 2017: Still A Minority, Their Clout Is Growing |date=1 November 2017 |work=[[Forbes]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/bizcarson/2018/12/04/most-powerful-women-in-tech-in-2018/ |title=Most Powerful Women In Tech In 2018: With Greater Power Comes Greater Responsibility |date=4 December 2018 |work=[[Forbes]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/bizcarson/2019/12/12/most-powerful-women-in-tech-in-2019-beyond-ceoswomen-dominate-the-c-suite/ |title=Most Powerful Women In Tech In 2019: Beyond CEOs,Women Dominate The C-Suite |date=12 December 2019 |work=[[Forbes]]}}</ref>

She was included in [[TIME|Time's]] 100 Most Influential People; named a Top 20 Venture Capitalists by ''[[The New York Times]]'' in 2017 and 2018; and was on [[Forbes]]' 2017 and 2018 [[Midas List]]s.

She was named VC of the Year at [[TechCrunch]]'s 2017 Crunchies Awards, and listed on ''Vanity Fair's'' New Establishment list. Green is a founding member of the female mentorship collective All Raise, and champions women in the tech industry. She gave a [[SXSW]] keynote speech on diversity in a male-dominated industry.

== Personal life ==
Green is married, has two children, and lives in [[San Francisco]].<ref name="forbes.com"/>
