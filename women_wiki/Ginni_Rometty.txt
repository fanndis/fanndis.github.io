'''Virginia Marie "Ginni" Rometty''' (born July 29, 1957) is an American business executive, serving as executive chairman of IBM. She previously served as chairman, president and CEO of [[IBM]], becoming the first woman to head the company.<ref name="female">{{cite web|url=http://www.businessweek.com/news/2011-10-25/ibm-names-rometty-to-succeed-palmisano-as-first-female-ceo.html|title=IBM Names Rometty to Succeed Palmisano as First Female CEO|date=25 October 2011|publisher=[[Bloomberg BusinessWeek]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130102134922/http://www.businessweek.com/news/2011-10-25/ibm-names-rometty-to-succeed-palmisano-as-first-female-ceo.html|archive-date=2 January 2013|accessdate=25 October 2011}}</ref><ref name="Bloomberg-26092012" /><ref name="INVESTORPLACE">{{cite web|last1=Aluise|first1=Susan J.|title=America's 10 Most Powerful Female CEOs|url=http://investorplace.com/2012/05/the-top-10-female-ceos/10/#.U76npbErh9l|website=InvestorPlace|accessdate=10 July 2014|date=May 10, 2012}}</ref> Prior to becoming president and CEO in January 2012, she first joined IBM as a systems engineer in 1981 and subsequently headed global sales, marketing, and strategy.<ref name="BARRONSblue" /><ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> While general manager of IBM's global services division, in 2002 she helped negotiate IBM's purchase of [[PricewaterhouseCoopers]] IT consulting business, becoming known for her work integrating the two companies.<ref name="WALLSTREETante" /><ref name="SORORITY"/> As CEO, she focused IBM on analytics, [[cloud computing]], and cognitive computing systems.<ref name=":0"/><ref name="FINANCIALREVIEWsurge"/>

Rometty's tenure as IBM's CEO was marked by noteworthy awards, including by [[Bloomberg L.P.|''Bloomberg's'']] [[50 Most Influential (Bloomberg Markets ranking)|50 Most Influential People in the World]],<ref name="FIFTYMOST"/> ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune's]]'' "50 Most Powerful Women in Business", ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s 20 Most Important People in Tech and [[Forbes]]' America's Top 50 Women In Tech.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/ginni-rometty/?list=top-tech-women-america |title=Ginni Rometty |magazine=[[Forbes]]}}</ref><ref name="MOSTIMOPRTANTtech" /><ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/><ref name="CNN"/><ref name="FORTUNERometty"/> Her tenure has also been met by fierce criticism relating to executive compensation bonuses, layoffs, outsourcing, and presiding over 24 consecutive quarters of revenue decline.<ref name="CNNgrowth" /><ref name="247WALLGO"/><ref name="Fortune Magazine"/>

==Early life and education==
Ginni Rometty was born on July 29, 1957, in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]], as Virginia Marie Nicosia.<ref name=FT>{{cite web |last1=Waters |first1=Richard |title=More than a big smile on Big Blue's face |url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/53160726-008b-11e1-ba33-00144feabdc0.html#axzz37erUoLRr |accessdate=16 July 2014 |work=October 28, 2011|publisher=[[Financial Times]]}}</ref><ref name="dailynw2010"/><ref name="INVESTORPLACE"/> Growing up outside Chicago, she was the eldest of four children in an [[Italian-American]] family.<ref name="BARRONSblue"/> Her parents divorced and her father left when she was fifteen years old, and her mother subsequently took on multiple jobs to support the family while Rometty looked after the household in the evenings.<ref name="dailynw2010" /><ref name=FT/><ref name="FINANCIALTIMEStett">[https://www.ft.com/content/57ca1244-ad26-11e4-bfcf-00144feab7de "Lunch with the FT: Ginni Rometty"], Gillian Tett, ''[[Financial Times]]'', February 6, 2015</ref>

She began attending [[Northwestern University]] in Illinois in 1975 on a scholarship from [[General Motors]], where she interned between her junior and senior years.<ref name="FINANCIALTIMEStett"/><ref name="SORORITY"/> Rometty was also a member of the [[Kappa Kappa Gamma]] sorority, eventually serving as its president.<ref name="SORORITY">{{cite journal |url=http://fortune.com/2012/09/20/ibms-ginni-rometty-looks-ahead/ |title=IBM's Ginni Rometty looks ahead |first=Jessi |last=Hempel |journal=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]] |date=8 October 2012}}</ref> She graduated with high honors from the [[Robert R. McCormick School of Engineering and Applied Science]] at Northwestern University in 1979,<ref name="dailynw2010"/><ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> receiving a bachelor's degree in [[computer science]] and [[electrical engineering]].<ref name="wired2011"/><ref name="dailynw2010">{{cite web| url= http://www.dailynorthwestern.com/nu-appoints-5-new-members-to-board-of-trustees-1.2276760 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100629040428/http://www.dailynorthwestern.com/nu-appoints-5-new-members-to-board-of-trustees-1.2276760 |archive-date=June 29, 2010 | title= NU appoints 5 new members to Board of Trustees | date= June 25, 2010 | publisher= [[Northwestern University]] | accessdate=2011-10-26}}</ref> She has received honorary doctoral degrees from [[Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute]] (2014)<ref name="RENSSELAER">{{cite web|title=Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute Graduates 1,613 in 208th Commencement Ceremony |url=http://news.rpi.edu/content/2014/05/24/rensselaer-graduates-1613-208th-commencement-ceremony|accessdate=4 April 2015}}</ref> and [[Northwestern University]] (2015).<ref name="NORTHWESTERN">{{cite web|title=IBM executive, Northwestern alumna Virginia Rometty to speak at 2015 commencement|url=http://dailynorthwestern.com/2015/03/05/campus/ibm-executive-northwestern-alumna-virginia-rometty-to-speak-at-2015-commencement/|accessdate=4 April 2015}}</ref> She also received an honorary degree from [[North Carolina State University]].<ref>{{cite web |title=NC State Commencement |url=https://news.ncsu.edu/2019/04/ibm-ceo-to-give-nc-state-spring-commencement-speech/ |website=news.ncsu.edu |accessdate=31 January 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
===1979–1990s: GM and IBM technical positions===
After graduation in 1979, Rometty went to work for [[General Motors Institute]]<ref name="wired2011"/> in [[Flint, Michigan|Flint]],<ref name="SORORITY"/> where she was responsible for application and systems development.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> In 1981 she joined [[IBM]] as a [[systems analyst]]<ref name=FT/> and [[systems engineer]] in Detroit.<ref name="wired2011">{{cite magazine| url= https://www.wired.com/wiredenterprise/2011/10/virginia-rometty/ | title= IBM Names Virginia Rometty as First Female CEO | date= October 25, 2011 | magazine= Wired | accessdate= 2011-10-26}}</ref><ref name="SORORITY"/> Initially working with clients in the insurance industry,<ref name="WALLSTREETante"/> she spent her first ten years at IBM in technical positions.<ref name=FT/> The ''[[New York Times]]'' writes that she "quickly moved up to a series of management jobs,"<ref name="NYT2011"/> where she worked with clients in insurance, banking, telecommunications, manufacturing and health care.<ref name="SORORITY"/><ref name="NYT2011"/> She spent the 1990s working in sales,<ref name="SORORITY"/>  and by the late 1990s was helping clients such as [[Prudential Financial, Inc.]] with their internet features.<ref name="WALLSTREETante"/> She joined IBM's Consulting Group in 1991.<ref name="NYT2011"/>

===2000–2011: IBM management===
While general manager of IBM's global services division,<ref name="SORORITY"/> in 2002 she championed<ref name="NYT2011"/> and helped negotiate the purchase of Monday, the consulting arm of professional services firm [[PricewaterhouseCoopers]]<ref name="SORORITY"/> for $3.5 billion.<ref name="NYT2011">{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/10/26/technology/ibm-names-a-new-chief.html | title= I.B.M. Names Virginia Rometty as New Chief Executive | date= October 25, 2011 | newspaper= [[The New York Times]] | accessdate=2011-10-26}}</ref><ref name="CNN"/> The acquisition was the "largest in professional services history"<ref name="LACCcouncil"/> and launched IBM in the services business.<ref name="CNN"/>  While serving as senior vice president of IBM Global Business Services,<ref name="INVESTORPLACE"/><ref name="LACCcouncil"/> Rometty then received her "big break"<ref name=FT/> at IBM  when she was given the task of integrating PricewaterhouseCoopers and its consultants with IBM.<ref name="WALLSTREETante">[https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052970204505304577000222269670752 "IBM's Rometty Kept on Rising"], Spencer E. Ante And  Joann S. Lublin, ''[[Wall Street Journal]]'', October 27, 2011</ref> In 2002 ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' named her to its 2002 Global Business Influential list. From 2005 until 2009 she was the senior vice president of Global Business Services at IBM, and she also became senior vice president of Enterprise Business Services-IBM Global Services in July 2005.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> Among other roles, she served as general manager of IBM Global Services, Americas, as well as general manager of IBM's Global Insurance and Financial Services Sector.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/><ref name="LACCcouncil"/> She was also managing partner at IBM Business Consulting Services, Inc. and general of manager of Insurance Industry Group.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/>  Rometty received the Carl Sloane Award 2006 from the Association of Management Consulting Firms.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/><ref name="LACCcouncil"/>

Laid out by Rometty and other IBM executives,<ref name="SORORITY"/><ref name="INVESTORPLACE"/> in 2007 IBM announced<ref name="BARRONSblue"/> a five-year growth plan<ref name="CNN"/> concerning revenue growth and capital allocation.<ref name="BARRONSblue"/> Among other strategies, the "2015 Roadmap"<ref name="SORORITY"/><ref name="INVESTORPLACE"/> outlined moving IBM away from the hardware industry to focus on businesses such as software and services.<ref name="INVESTORPLACE"/> Rometty became senior vice president and group executive for sales, marketing, and strategy in 2009,<ref name="NYT2011"/><ref name="wired2011"/> focusing on the company's "fast-growing analytics unit".<ref name="NYT2011"/>  In January 2009, she was placed in charge of IBM's sales force,<ref name="WALLSTREETante"/> serving as senior vice president of global sales and distribution until 2010.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> During this time she pushed the development of IBM's growth-markets unit, which had been created in 2008 to focus on emerging markets such as [[Brazil]] and [[Vietnam]].<ref name="WALLSTREETante"/>  From 2010 until 2012 she was an IBM senior vice president, and from 2010 until 2012, she was IBM's Group Executive of Sales, Marketing & Strategy.<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> In 2011, [[CNN]] reported that she was is "credited with spearheading IBM's growth strategy by getting the company into the [[cloud computing]] and analytics businesses. She was also at the helm of readying [[Watson (computer)|Watson]], the ''[[Jeopardy!]]'' playing computer, for commercial use."<ref name="CNNpalmisano">{{cite web|url=https://money.cnn.com/2011/10/25/technology/ibm_ceo_palmisano_steps_down/?source=cnn_bin|title=IBM CEO Sam Palmisano to step down|date=25 October 2011|publisher=[[CNN]]|accessdate=25 October 2011}}</ref>

===2012–2020: Leadership of IBM===
On October 25, 2011, IBM announced that she was to be the company's next president and CEO,<ref name="wired2011"/><ref name="INVESTORPLACE"/> becoming the ninth chief executive in its history.<ref name="wired2011"/><ref name="SORORITY"/> Her role as IBM's first female chief received note in the press,<ref name="female"/> with former CEO [[Sam Palmisano]] responding that her selection had "zero to do with progressive social policies."<ref name="NYT2011"/> She became president and CEO on January 1, 2012, also taking on the additional role of IBM chairman on October 1, 2012<ref name="BLOOMBERGbio"/> when Palmisano retired.<ref name=Bloomberg-26092012/> With plans to take IBM out of unprofitable business lines<ref name="FINANCIALTIMEStett"/> and citing [[big data]] and [[analytics]] as IBM's "next big growth machine,"<ref name="BARRONSblue"/> in 2014 she brokered a partnership for [[Apple (company)|Apple]] to design applications for IBM's enterprise customers. Later that year she announced that IBM would partner with [[SAP SE|SAP]] on cloud computing and with [[Twitter]] on data analytics, and in 2015 she also brokered a partnership with [[Box (company)|Box]].<ref name="BUSINESSJOURNALSsigns">[https://www.bizjournals.com/bizwomen/news/latest-news/2015/06/ibm-ginni-rometty-sign-yet-another-silicon-valley.html "IBM, Ginni Rometty sign yet another Silicon Valley partner"], ''Business Journals'', Caroline McMillan Portillo, June 24, 2015</ref> Rometty had IBM spend $8.5 billion acquiring around 30 companies between 2012 and 2015,<ref name=":0"/> and by 2016 she had overseen the divestment of about $7 billion in commoditized assets such as chip manufacturing.<ref name="Fortune Magazine"/>

In May 2017, ''Austin Business'' reported that Rometty had successfully shifted IBM away from "shrinking businesses such as computers and operating system software, and into higher-growth areas like artificial intelligence."<ref name="AUSTINBUSINESS"/><ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|title = Fortune's Most Powerful Women List|last = Bellstrom|first = Kristen|date = September 15, 2015|journal = Fortune}}</ref><ref name="FORBESlaw">[https://www.forbes.com/sites/adrianbridgwater/2018/03/20/ibm-ceo-rometty-proposes-watsons-law-ai-in-everything/#cc466754d087 "IBM CEO Rometty Proposes 'Watson's Law': AI In Everything"], Adrian Bridgewater, ''Forbes'', March 20, 2018</ref><ref name="FINANCIALREVIEWsurge">[http://www.afr.com/technology/ibm-grants-ceo-ginni-rometty-a-record-bonus-after-share-surge-20170207-gu78gr "IBM grants CEO Ginni Rometty a record bonus after share surge"], ''Financial Review'', February 7, 2017</ref> On June 28, 2017, she was awarded the [[KPMG]] Inspire Greatness Award.<ref name="KPMG">[https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/kpmg-honors-stem-pioneer-ginny-rometty-for-inspiring-next-generation-of-women-leaders-300481604.html "KPMG Honors Stem Pioneer Ginni Rometty for Inspiring Next Generation of Women Leaders"], [[Cision]], June 28, 2017</ref> In January 2018, she announced IBM's first quarter of year-over-year revenue increase since 2012, with particular growth in areas such as data, [[blockchain]], and the cloud.<ref name="NEWYORKstreak">[https://www.nytimes.com/2018/01/18/business/ibm-revenue.html "IBM Ends 22-Quarter Streak of Falling Revenue"], Steve Lohr, ''[[The New York Times]]'', January 18, 2018</ref> By 2018, she stated that around half of IBM's 9,043 patents in 2017 were in AI, cloud computing, cybersecurity, blockchain and quantum computing.<ref>[http://www-03.ibm.com/press/us/en/pressrelease/53581.wss "IBM Breaks Records to Top U.S. Patent List for 25th Consecutive Year"], IBM, January 9, 2018</ref>

On January 30, 2020, it was announced that Rometty would be stepping down as IBM's CEO to be replaced by [[Arvind Krishna (executive)|Arvind Krishna]]. She remains executive chairman.<ref>https://www.wsj.com/articles/ginni-rometty-stepping-down-as-ibm-ceo-11580420650</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2020/01/30/ibm-ceo-rometty/|title=IBM’s Ginni Rometty is stepping down as CEO|last=McGregor|first=Jena|date=30 January 2020|website=Washington Post|language=en|access-date=2020-02-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Lohr|first=Steve|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/30/technology/ginni-rometty-ibm-ceo.html|title=Ginni Rometty to Step Down as C.E.O. of IBM|date=2020-01-30|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-02-28|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Virginia Nicosia married Mark Anthony Rometty,<ref name="TIMEShusband"/> a private-equity investor, in 1979.<ref name="BARRONSblue">[https://www.barrons.com/articles/ginni-rometty-reinventing-big-blue-1401514830 "Ginni Rometty: Reinventing Big Blue"], ''[[Barron's (newspaper)|Barron's]]'', Leslie P. Norton, May 31, 2014</ref> With no children, the couple divides their time between New York and [[Bonita Springs, Florida]].<ref name="TIMEShusband">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/11/05/business/a-ceos-support-system-a-k-a-husband.html?pagewanted=all |title=A C.E.O.'s Support System, a k a Husband|last=Stewart|first=James B.|date=5 November 2011|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=3 May 2013}}</ref> She goes to [[Broadway shows]] and participates in scuba diving as a hobby.<ref name="SORORITY"/> In 2014, Rometty became the third female member of the [[Augusta National Golf Club]], following the lead of [[Condoleezza Rice]].<ref name="AUGUSTA">{{cite web|url=http://espn.go.com/golf/story/_/id/11871158/virginia-rometty-becomes-3rd-female-member-augusta-national|title=Virginia Rometty becomes 3rd female member of Augusta National - ESPN|work=ESPN.com|accessdate=17 November 2014}}</ref>
