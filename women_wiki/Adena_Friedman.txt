'''Adena T. Friedman''' (born 1969) is an American businesswoman. She currently serves as the President and CEO of [[Nasdaq]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Adena Friedman|url=http://www.nasdaqomx.com/aboutus/company-information/ourleadership/adena-friedman}}</ref> She was formerly the managing director and CFO of [[The Carlyle Group]]. Initially joining Nasdaq in 1993, she returned to Nasdaq from Carlyle in May 2014 as President of Global Corporate and information technology solutions.<ref name="Merced">{{Cite news|last=Merced|first=Michael J. de la|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/15/business/dealbook/nasdaq-names-a-new-chief-executive-adena-f.html|title=Nasdaq Names a New Chief Executive: Adena Friedman|date=2016-11-14|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-03-11|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Adena Friedman returns to Nasdaq|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2014/05/12/nasdaq-managementchanges-idUSL3N0NY1EU20140512|newspaper=Reuters|date=May 12, 2014}}</ref> She was named the CEO of Nasdaq in January 2017, the first woman to lead a global exchange.<ref name="Adena Friedman">{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/adena-friedman/|title=Adena Friedman|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2020-03-11}}</ref>

Forbes has repeatedly listed Friedman as one of the world's most powerful women on their annual ''Power Women'' list.<ref name="Adena Friedman"/>

==Early years==
Born Adena Robinson Testa and raised in metropolitan [[Baltimore]], she is the daughter of Michael D. Testa, a managing director at [[T. Rowe Price]], and Adena W. Testa, an attorney in the Baltimore law firm of Stewart, Plant & Blumenthal.<ref name=NYTWedding>[https://www.nytimes.com/1993/08/22/style/weddings-adena-r-testa-michael-friedman.html New York Times: "Weddings; Adena R. Testa, Michael Friedman"] August 22, 1993</ref>

== Education ==
She attended [[Roland Park Country School]]. She earned a BA in political science from [[Williams College]] and an MBA from Vanderbilt University's [[Owen Graduate School of Management]].<ref name="NYTWedding" />

== Career ==
After graduating from Vanderbilt University, Friedman joined Nasdaq in 1993. At Nasdaq, she served as the head of data products and as Chief Financial Officer, amongst others.<ref name="Merced"/>

She left Nasdaq in 2011 to join private investment firm Carlyle Group as Chief Financial Officer and Managing Director, a position she held until 2014.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Johnson|first=Kieron|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-48205206|title=The taekwondo black belt who runs the Nasdaq|date=2019-05-13|work=BBC News|access-date=2020-03-11|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Friedman returned to Nasdaq as president and Chief Operating Officer. She became CEO in January 2017, replacing Robert Greifeld.<ref name="Merced"/>

According to Forbes ''Power Women'' list, Friedman sees Nasdaq as an "''engine for capitalism,''" and has advocated for “''bringing companies back to the public market and making investing accessible.”'' As CEO, she is “''focused on diversifying Nasdaq into a technology company with a focus on growth opportunities, such as data research services.''”<ref name="Adena Friedman"/>

In April 2019, Friedman gave a TED talk titled “''What’s the future of capitalism?''.”<ref>{{Citation|last=Friedman|first=Adena|title=What's the future of capitalism?|url=https://www.ted.com/talks/adena_friedman_what_s_the_future_of_capitalism|language=en|access-date=2020-03-11}}</ref> She also participated in The Economist magazine ''The World in 2020'' issue with a column titled "''Ideas for modernizing capitalism'',” predicting that “''the fight for the soul of the global economy will heat up''.”<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://worldin.economist.com/edition/2020/article/17512/ideas-modernising-capitalism|title=Ideas for modernising capitalism|website=worldin.economist.com|language=en|access-date=2020-03-11}}</ref>

During the 2020 COVID-19 crisis, Friedman was featured on The New York Times Corner Office "''Logged On From the Laundry Room: How the C.E.O.s of Google, Pfizer and Slack Work From Home''" article, sharing perspectives of company CEOs working from home due to the pandemic. She shared her decision to split key personnel at the exchange to two teams to ensure work continuity and described her work from home routine during the crisis. The story included Alphabet CEO Sundar Pichai and Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff, among others.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Gelles|first=David|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/23/business/corner-office-coronavirus.html|title=Logged On From the Laundry Room: How the C.E.O.s of Google, Pfizer and Slack Work From Home|date=2020-03-23|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-04-01|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

== Boards ==
Friedman has served as a Class B director to the Federal Reserve Bank of New York since December 2018, the only Exchange CEO to ever have done this.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newyorkfed.org/newsevents/news/aboutthefed/2018/oa181127b|title=Adena Friedman Elected as a Class B Director to New York Fed Board of Directors - FEDERAL RESERVE BANK of NEW YORK|website=www.newyorkfed.org|access-date=2020-03-11}}</ref><ref name="Adena Friedman"/> She also serves on the board of FCLTGlobal, a non-profit organization that researches tools that encourage long-term investing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fcltglobal.org/news/blog/article/2020/01/02/fcltglobal-appoints-new-board-directors-and-strategic-advisors|title=FCLTGlobal Appoints New Board Directors and Strategic Advisors|website=www.fcltglobal.org|access-date=2020-03-11}}</ref>
