'''Shari Ellin Redstone''' (born April 14, 1954) is an American media executive with a background in numerous aspects of the entertainment industry and related ventures.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/shari-redstone/ |title=Shari Redstone |work=Forbes |date=2012-04-18 |accessdate=2014-01-15 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130629025536/http://www.forbes.com/profile/shari-redstone/ |archivedate=June 29, 2013 }}</ref> She currently serves as the chairman of [[ViacomCBS]] and president of [[National Amusements]]; she formerly served as the vice-chairman of [[CBS Corporation]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbscorporation.com/ourcompany-board.php?id=118&member=109 |title=Sumner M. Redstone |publisher=CBS Corporation |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref> and [[Viacom (2005–2019)|Viacom]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.viacom.com/aboutviacom/Pages/boardofdirectors.aspx|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080120212219/http://www.viacom.com/aboutviacom/Pages/boardofdirectors.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 20, 2008|title=Viacom|date=January 20, 2008}}</ref> Through National Amusements, Redstone and her family are majority owners of [[CBS]], [[Comedy Central]], [[BET]], [[Showtime Networks]] and the film studio [[Paramount Pictures]].

Redstone is included in [[Time 100|''Time Magazine's'' 100]] Most Influential People of 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shari Redstone: The 100 Most Influential People of 2020|url=https://time.com/collection/100-most-influential-people-2020/5888411/shari-redstone/|access-date=2020-09-23|website=Time}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Redstone is the daughter of Phyllis Gloria Raphael and [[Sumner Redstone]] and is the sister of [[Brent Redstone]]. Her grandfather was [[Michael Redstone]], who was the original founder of National Amusements, the parent company that now owns the majority of the Redstone media empire. She graduated with a bachelor of science degree from [[Tufts University]] in 1975. She received her law degrees at [[Boston University School of Law]] in 1978 (J.D.) and in 1980 (LLM). She practiced corporate law, estate planning and criminal law in the Boston area before joining National Amusements.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=1103051 |title=List of Private Companies Worldwide, Letter - Businessweek |publisher=Investing.businessweek.com |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref>

==Career==
===1990s–2016===
Since 1999, she has been president of National Amusements, one of the top ten movie exhibitors in the United States.<ref name="natoonline">{{cite web|url=http://www.natoonline.org/statisticscircuits.htm|title=statisticscircuits|publisher=natoonline.org|accessdate=2014-01-20|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130701043857/http://www.natoonline.org/statisticscircuits.htm|archivedate=2013-07-01}}</ref> Redstone has expanded the company's international footprint and its exploration of new technologies.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://mobile.bloomberg.com/news/2011-07-09/shari-redstone-says-u-s-has-too-many-cinemas-thousands-should-close |title=Shari Redstone Says U.S. Has Too Many Cinemas, 'Thousands' Should Close - Bloomberg |publisher=Mobile.bloomberg.com |date=2011-07-08 |accessdate=2014-01-15 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140116085541/http://mobile.bloomberg.com/news/2011-07-09/shari-redstone-says-u-s-has-too-many-cinemas-thousands-should-close |archivedate=2014-01-16 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bigscreen.com/journal.php?id=1245 |title=Foxboro, MA: National Amusements Debuts New Upscale Cinema Concept at Patriot Place - The BigScreen Cinema Guide |publisher=Bigscreen.com |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.upenn.edu/almanac/v48/n32/cinema.html |title=4/30/2002, The Bridge: Cinema De Lux - Almanac, Vol. 48, No. 32 |publisher=Upenn.edu |date=2002-04-30 |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/national-amusements-moves-to-a-digital-future-with-sony-4k-digital-cinema-projectors-104531924.html |title=National Amusements Moves to a Digital Future with Sony 4K Digital Cinema... - PARK RIDGE, N.J., Oct. 7 /PRNewswire/ |publisher=Prnewswire.com |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/sumner-redstones-daughter-shari-launches-228295 |title=Sumner Redstone's Daughter Shari Launches Media, Tech Investment Firm |publisher=The Hollywood Reporter |date=2011-08-26 |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref>

In 2000, while at National Amusements, Redstone entered the [[Russia]]n theater market, giving patrons there the opportunity to experience high-end luxury theaters.<ref>[http://origin.nai.digitalbungalow.com/about/press-release.asp?release=243] {{dead link|date=March 2014}}</ref>  In 2010, Redstone and her partners purchased the theaters that they had built and formed '''Rising Star Media''',<ref>{{cite news|first=Claudia |last=Eller |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2010-jan-08-la-fi-ct-redstone8-2010jan08-story.html |title=Shari Redstone buys Russian theaters from her family firm|work=Los Angeles Times |date=2010-01-08 |accessdate=2020-06-24}}</ref> of which she was chairman, and turned it into the top grossing cinema chain in that country.<ref name="latimes1">{{cite news|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/entertainmentnewsbuzz/2011/06/shari-redstone-russia-rising-star-media-sold.html |title=Shari Redstone's Rising Star Media sold to Russian theater chain|work=Los Angeles Times |date=2011-06-06 |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref> Redstone and her partners then sold Rising Star Media to Russian theater operator [[List of movie theater chains#Russia|Cinema Park]] in 2011.<ref name="latimes1"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/robertlenzner/2011/06/09/shari-redstone-makes-huge-profit-by-selling-russian-theaters-for-190-million/ |title=Shari Redstone Makes Huge Profit By Selling Russian Theaters For $190 Million |work=Forbes |date=2011-09-06 |accessdate=2014-01-15 |first=Robert |last=Lenzner}}</ref>

In 2007, Redstone and her father feuded publicly over issues of corporate governance and the future of the cinema chain.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.boston.com/business/globe/articles/2007/09/19/redstone_says_he_relies_on_his_instinct/|title=Redstone says he relies on his instinct|publisher=The Boston Globe |date=2007-09-19 |accessdate=2014-01-15 |first=Jenn |last=Abelson}}</ref> Documents have been made public which verify that, as part of a settlement from Sumner's first divorce, all of Sumner's stock is in irrevocable trusts that will be left to his grandchildren.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/news/business/la-fi-shari1oct01,1,4236064.story?coll=la-headlines-business-enter |title=Redstone family rift may not be healed|work=Los Angeles Times |date=2007-08-02 |accessdate=2014-01-15 |first=Claudia |last=Eller}}</ref>

In 2011, Redstone became co-founder and managing partner of Advancit Capital, LLC,<ref name="latimes2">{{cite news|first=Meg |last=James |work=Los Angeles Times |url= https://www.latimes.com/business/la-xpm-2011-aug-27-la-fi-ct-shari-redstone-fund-20110827-story.html|title=Shari Redstone launches investment firm Advancit Capital|date=2011-08-27 |accessdate=2020-06-24}}</ref> an investment platform focusing on early stage in media, entertainment and technology.<ref name="latimes2"/> In addition, she is co-chairman of MovieTickets.com,<ref name="cbscorporation1">{{cite web|url=http://www.cbscorporation.com/ourcompany-board.php?id=118&member=110 |title=Shari Redstone |publisher=CBS Corporation |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref> Inc. and is a member of the Board of Directors and Executive Committee for the National Theatre Owners Association (NATO).<ref name="cbscorporation1"/>

===2016–present===
On February 3, 2016, her father resigned as CBS executive chairman after questions arose about his mental competency. CBS's board then offered Shari Redstone the position of non-executive chair, but she declined.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fortune.com/2016/02/03/sumner-redstone-resigns-as-cbs-executive-chairman/|title=Sumner Redstone Resigns as CBS Executive Chairman|agency=Reuters|date=3 February 2016|work=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref> The CBS board announced that [[Les Moonves]] replaced Sumner Redstone as CBS Chairman.<ref name="usatoday.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/2016/02/04/viacom-names-philippe-dauman-new-board-chairman-replacing-sumner-redstone/79815766/|title=Viacom names Philippe Dauman as new board chairman, replacing Sumner Redstone|date=4 February 2016|work=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref> In 2016, Redstone, who at the time was vice chairperson of CBS and Viacom and president of National Amusements (the controlling shareholder of CBS and Viacom), gave the following statement on succession: "my father's Trust states his intention that I succeed him as (non-executive) Chairman at CBS and Viacom, and also names me as a Trustee after his death." She stated that she wanted the chairs of each company to be "not a Trustee of my father's trust or otherwise intertwined in Redstone family matters," and she nominated Les as the CBS chair.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Shari Redstone {{!}} CBS Corporation|url = https://www.cbscorporation.com/people/shari-redstone/|website = www.cbscorporation.com|access-date = 2016-02-11}}</ref>

On February 4, 2016, against Shari Redstone's desires,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2016/02/viacom-names-philippe-dauman-chairman-after-shari-redstone-rejects-position-1201696360/|title=Viacom Names Philippe Dauman Chairman Succeeding Sumner Redstone|author=David Lieberman|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/913ae5be-cb46-11e5-be0b-b7ece4e953a0.html#axzz3zDcqH7ce|title=Succession battle engulfs Sumner Redstone's media empire|work=[[Financial Times]]|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-02-04/viacom-s-board-names-dauman-chairman-replacing-aging-redstone|title=Viacom's Board Names Dauman Chairman, Replacing Aging Redstone|author=Chris Palmeri, Lucas Shaw|date=4 February 2016|work=[[Bloomberg.com]]|accessdate=4 February 2016}}</ref> Viacom's board of directors named [[Philippe Dauman]], already the CEO and president, the chairman, replacing Sumner Redstone.<ref name="usatoday.com"/>

On January 17, 2018, the ''[[Wall Street Journal]]'' reported that Shari Redstone was pushing for CBS Corp. to merge with Viacom, with Redstone also gathering names for new board members at CBS.<ref name=wsj-flint>{{cite news |last=Hagey |first=Keath |date=January 17, 2018 |title=Shari Redstone Wants New CBS Directors, Renews Push for Merger With Viacom |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/shari-redstone-wants-new-cbs-directors-renews-push-to-merge-cbs-and-viacom-1516217045 | work=[[The Wall Street Journal]] |location=New York City, New York |access-date=January 18, 2018}}</ref>

On August 13, 2019, the ''[[Associated Press]]'' reported that Shari Redstone became the chair of the board for the reuniting of CBS and Viacom as ViacomCBS.<ref name=reunitedAP>{{cite news |url=https://www.staradvertiser.com/2019/08/13/breaking-news/cbs-viacom-to-reunite-as-media-giants-bulk-up-for-streaming/ |title=CBS, Viacom to reunite as media giants bulk up for streaming |newspaper=[[Star-Advertiser]] |location=[[Honolulu]] |via=[[Associated Press]] |date=August 13, 2019 |access-date=August 13, 2019}}</ref>

===Philanthropy===
As of January 2018, Redstone is a member of the board of directors at Combined Jewish Philanthropies and the board of trustees at [[Dana Farber Cancer Institute]].<ref name="cbscorporation1"/>  She is also on the board of directors of The [[National Center on Addiction and Substance Abuse]] (CASA) at Columbia University<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.casacolumbia.org/templates/PressReleases.aspx?articleid=325&zoneid=46 |title=Press Releases |publisher=CASAColumbia |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref> and the [[John F. Kennedy Library Foundation]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jfklibrary.org/About-Us/JFK-Library-Foundation/Board-of-Directors.aspx |title=Board of Directors - John F. Kennedy Presidential Library & Museum |publisher=Jfklibrary.org |date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref> Redstone recently joined the board and executive committee of "Our Time", a mass-membership organization that stands for the economic interests and political inclusion of young Americans aged 18–30. She sits on the local advisory board and executive committee for BUILD, a nonprofit organization that uses entrepreneurship to propel low income youth through high school and into college.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://buildinboston.org/board-build-in-boston/ |title=Local Advisory Board|publisher= BUILD Greater Boston|date= |accessdate=2014-01-15}}</ref>
