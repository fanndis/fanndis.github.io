'''Ana Patricia Botín-Sanz de Sautuola O'Shea''', [[Order of the British Empire|DBE]] (born 4 October 1960) is a Spanish banker who has served as the executive chairman of [[Santander Group]] since 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-29149536|title=Santander appoints Ana Botin as chairwoman|work=BBC News}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ms Ana Botín-Sanz de Sautuola y O'Shea|url=https://www.santander.com/csgs/Satellite/CFWCSancomQP01/en_GB/Corporate/Investor-Relations/Ms-Ana-Botin-Sanz-de-Sautuola-y-OShea.html|website=www.santander.com|language=en}}</ref> She is the fourth generation of the Botín family to hold this role. Prior to this she was chief executive officer (CEO) of Santander UK, a role she held from December 2010 until her assumption of the chairmanship.

In February 2013, she was ranked the third most powerful woman in the UK by ''[[Woman's Hour]]'' on [[BBC Radio 4]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b007qlvb/features/power-list-100|title=BBC Radio 4 - Woman's Hour - The Power List 2013|work=BBC}}</ref> In 2019, ''[[Forbes]]'' ranked her the 8th [[Forbes list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|most powerful woman]] in the world, having been in the top 10 for the last 4 years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/|title=World's Most Powerful Women|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Botín is the daughter of Spanish banker [[Emilio Botín]], who was the executive chairman of Spain's [[Grupo Santander]], and [[Paloma O'Shea]]. She received her high school education at [[St Mary's School, Ascot]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/7c182ab8-9c33-11e4-b9f8-00144feabdc0|title=London: Sexism and the City|first1=Harriet|last1=Agnew|first2=Patrick|last2=Jenkins|date=16 January 2015|website=Financial Times|accessdate=20 March 2018}}</ref> She studied economics at [[Bryn Mawr College]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.abc.es/economia/20140915/abci-ana-botin-perfil-201409142239.html|title=Ana Botín, la discreta sombra de su padre|last=Pérez|first=María Jesús|date=2014-09-15|work=ABC.es|access-date=2018-06-20|language=es|trans-title=Ana Botín, the discreet shadow of her father}}</ref>

==Career==
Botín worked at [[JP Morgan]] in the US from 1980 to 1988.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.worldfinance.com/banking/ana-botin-the-most-powerful-woman-in-finance|title=Ana Botín: the most powerful woman in finance|website=www.worldfinance.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-20}}</ref> In 1988, she returned to Spain and began working for the Santander Group. During that time, she was involved in the bank's 1997 acquisition of a 51 percent stake in Banco Osorno y La Union, the largest bank in Chile, for $495 million.<ref>John Tagliabue (1 January 1997), [https://www.nytimes.com/1997/01/01/business/spain-s-santander-hopes-to-become-no-1-in-latin-america.html  Spain's Santander Hopes to Become No. 1 in Latin America] ''[[The New York Times]]''.</ref> In 2002, she became the executive chairman of the Spanish bank, [[Banesto]]. In November 2010, Botín succeeded [[António Mota de Sousa Horta Osório|António Horta Osório]] as chief executive of Santander UK.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dealbook.nytimes.com/2010/11/04/ana-patricia-botin-to-head-santander-uk/|title= Ana Patricia Botin to Head Santander UK|work=The New York Times|accessdate=10 January 2013|date=4 November 2010}}</ref>

In 2013, Botín was appointed a director of [[the Coca-Cola Company]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vcstar.com/news/2013/jul/18/coca-cola-board-elects-ana-botin-as-a-director/|title=Coca-Cola board elects Ana Botin as a director|publisher=|accessdate=4 January 2018}}</ref>

In September 2014, Botín was appointed chair of the [[Santander Group]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.santander.com/csgs/Satellite/CFWCSancomQP01/en_GB/Corporate/Press-Room/2014/09/10/Ana-Botin-unanimously-appointed-to-chair-the-board-of-Banco-Santander-.html|title=Ana Botín, unanimously appointed to chair the board of Banco Santander|publisher=}}</ref> She is the fourth generation of the Botín family to hold this role.<ref name=Telegraph>{{cite news|title=Emilio Botín - obituary|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/11095293/Emilio-Botin-obituary.html|accessdate=4 August 2015|work=Daily Telegraph|date=14 September 2014}}</ref> Since taking charge she has brought in more international board members, embraced technology and strengthened the US and Latin America management teams.<ref>Patrick Jenkins (9 June 2017), [https://www.ft.com/content/b429851a-4d01-11e7-a3f4-c742b9791d43 Santander’s Ana Botín has no trouble playing hardball] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref>

== Other activities ==
In 2015, [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] [[David Cameron]] of the United Kingdom named Botín to become a member of his business advisory board.<ref>Elizabeth Rigby and Sarah Gordon (27 July 2015), [https://www.ft.com/content/48ee9cfe-3476-11e5-b05b-b01debd57852 David Cameron replaces his entire business advisory board] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref> In 2020, the [[International Monetary Fund]]'s Managing Director [[Kristalina Georgieva]] appointed her to an external advisory group to provide input on policy challenges.<ref>Andrea Shalal and David Lawder (10 April 2020), [https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-imf/imfs-georgieva-creates-external-advisory-panel-on-pandemic-idUSKCN21S1O1 IMF's Georgieva creates external advisory panel on pandemic] ''[[Reuters]]''.</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1983, Botín married fellow banker Guillermo Morenés y Mariátegui, son of the [[Marquess of Borghetto|9th Marquess of Borghetto]], a wealthy landowner.<ref name=ElMundo>{{cite news|last1=Ruiz|first1=Borja|title=Guillermo Morenés, el consorte de la nueva primera dama de la banca|url=http://www.elmundo.es/loc/2014/09/13/54133b4cca47410a028b4571.html|accessdate=4 August 2015|work=El Mundo|date=13 September 2014}}</ref> They have three children: Felipe Morenés Botín, Javier Morenés Botín, and Pablo Morenés Botín.

The family owns a large estate in [[Ciudad Real]], south of Madrid.<ref>Patrick Jenkins (September 12, 2014) [https://www.ft.com/content/5a74de3a-390d-11e4-9526-00144feabdc0 A worldly scion of Spanish finance picks up the torch] ''[[Financial Times]]''.</ref> In 2010, Morenés y Mariátegui bought a six-bedroom home in [[Belgravia]], London.<ref name=Bloomberg_02>{{cite news|last1=Penty|first1=Charles|title=Banking Queen Botin Plans U.K. IPO in Santander Succession Test|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2011-03-08/banking-queen-botin-plans-u-k-ipo-seen-determining-santander-succession|accessdate=4 August 2015|work=Bloomberg|date=8 March 2011}}</ref> They also own a house in the Swiss ski resort of [[Gstaad]].<ref name=ElMundo />
