'''Susan Diane Wojcicki''' ({{IPAc-en|w|ʊ|ˈ|tʃ|ɪ|t|s|k|i}} {{respell|wuu|CHITS|kee}}<ref>{{YouTube|a07s88y7f-k|"#DearMe: Susan Wojcicki, CEO of YouTube"}}</ref>; born July 5, 1968)<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.familytreelegends.com/records/39461?c=search&first=Susan&last=Wojcicki |title=California Births, 1905 - 1995 |publisher=Familytreelegends.com |accessdate=March 15, 2014}}</ref> is the [[CEO]] of [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Gustin|first=Sam|title=Google Ad Chief Susan Wojcicki: 'The Book Isn't Finished'|url=https://www.wired.com/epicenter/2011/05/susan-wojcicki/|access-date=10 September 2011|newspaper=Wired.com|date=3 May 2011}}</ref> She is the longest tenured CEO in the history of YouTube.

Wojcicki was involved in the founding of [[Google]], and became Google's first marketing manager in 1999. She later led the company's online advertising business and was put in charge of Google's original video service. After observing the success of YouTube, Wojcicki proposed the acquisition of YouTube by Google in 2006, and has served as CEO of YouTube since 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last=Orescovic|first=Alexi|title=Google taps longtime executive Wojcicki to head YouTube|url=http://in.reuters.com/article/2014/02/05/us-google-youtube-idINBREA141Y420140205|work=Reuters|date=February 5, 2014}}</ref>

Wojcicki has an estimated net worth of $580 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/susan-wojcicki/#3e30abe03ae3|title=#38 Susan Wojcicki|website=Forbes|access-date=2018-10-27}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Susan Diane Wojcicki was born on July 5, 1968 to [[Esther Wojcicki]], an educator of [[Jewish]] descent,<ref>{{cite web|last1=Tramiel|first1=Preeva|title=Esther Wojcicki: A Jewish mother of the tech revolution|url=https://jwa.org/blog/esther-wojcicki-jewish-mother-of-tech-revolution|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref> and [[Stanley Wojcicki]], a [[Polish Americans|Polish American]] physics professor at [[Stanford University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/06/18/how-the-wojcickis-parents-raised-23andme-founder-youtube-ceo.html|title=How Anne and Susan Wojcicki's parents raised the founder of 23andMe and the CEO of YouTube|last=Clifford|first=Catherine|date=2018-06-18|website=CNBC|language=en|access-date=2019-05-14}}</ref> She has two sisters: Janet Wojcicki, (PhD, anthropologist and epidemiologist)<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sellers|first1=Patricia|title=Before Google, the Wojcicki girls learned from Mom|url=http://fortune.com/2012/02/01/before-google-the-wojcicki-girls-learned-from-mom/|website=Fortune.com|access-date=16 December 2014}}</ref> and [[Anne Wojcicki]], founder of [[23andMe]].<ref>[https://www.cnbc.com/2019/05/08/i-raised-2-successful-ceos-and-a-doctor-here-is-one-if-the-biggest-mistakes-parents-make.html I raised 2 successful CEOs and a doctor—here’s one of the biggest mistakes I see parents making], Esther Wojcicki, Published Wed, May 8, 2019, cnbc.com.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/06/18/how-the-wojcickis-parents-raised-23andme-founder-youtube-ceo.html|title=How Anne and Susan Wojcicki's parents raised the founder of 23andMe and the CEO of YouTube|last=Clifford|first=Catherine|date=2018-06-18|website=CNBC|language=en|access-date=2019-10-28}}</ref> She grew up on the Stanford campus with [[George Dantzig]] as a neighbor.<ref name="fastcompany2014a" /> She attended [[Gunn High School]] in [[Palo Alto, California]], and wrote for the school newspaper.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://fortune.com/2012/02/01/before-google-the-wojcicki-girls-learned-from-mom/ |title=Before Google, the Wojcicki girls learned from Mom |magazine=Fortune Magazine |date=February 1, 2012 |first=Patricia |last=Sellers |access-date=October 4, 2014 }}</ref>

Wojcicki's first business was selling "spice ropes" door-to-door at age 11. A humanities major in college, she took her first computer science class as a senior.<ref name=":0"/>

Wojcicki studied history and literature at [[Harvard University]] and graduated with honors in 1990. She originally planned on getting a PhD in economics and pursuing a career in academia but changed her plans when she discovered an interest in technology.<ref name="fastcompany2014a" />

She also received her Master's of Science in economics from the [[University of California, Santa Cruz]] in 1993 and a [[Master of Business Administration]] from the [[UCLA Anderson School of Management]] in 1998.<ref name="mercurynews2011">{{cite news|url=http://www.mercurynews.com/ci_17286427 |title=Susan Wojcicki: The most important Googler you've never heard of |newspaper=San Jose Mercury News |date=February 7, 2011 |first=Mike |last=Swift |access-date=October 4, 2014 }}</ref>

==Career==
In September 1998, the same month that Google was incorporated, founders [[Larry Page]] and [[Sergey Brin]] set up office in Wojcicki's garage in [[Menlo Park, California|Menlo Park]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/tech/techinvestor/corporatenews/2007-07-04-google-wojcicki_N.htm |title=The house that helped build Google |newspaper=USA Today |first=Jefferson |last=Graham |date=July 5, 2007 |access-date=October 4, 2014 }}</ref> Before becoming Google's first marketing manager in 1999, Wojcicki worked in marketing at [[Intel Corporation]] in [[Santa Clara, California]],<ref name="fastcompany2014a">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3033957/the-woman-behind-the-superlatives-three-things-you-need-to-know-about-susan-wojcicki |title=The Woman Behind the Superlatives: Three Things You Need to Know About Susan Wojcicki |magazine=The Fast Company |date=August 6, 2014 |first=Nicole |last=Laporte |access-date=October 4, 2014 }}</ref> and was a management consultant at [[Bain & Company]] and R.B. Webber & Company.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://ideas.time.com/contributor/susan-wojcicki/ |title=Susan Wojcicki |magazine=Time |access-date=October 4, 2014 }}</ref> At Google, she worked on the initial viral marketing programs, as well as the first [[Google Doodle]]s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/alumni/100-inspirational-alumni/susan-wojcicki-inspirational-100-alumna|title=Susan Wojcicki - "Inspirational 100" Alumna|publisher=UCLA Anderson School of Management|access-date=October 4, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=NCvdUbeWS60C&q=susan+wojcicki+At+Google%2C+she+worked+on+the+initial+viral+marketing+programs%2C+as+well+as+the+first+Google+Doodles&pg=PA15|title=Think Quarterly: Innovation (US)|language=en}}</ref> Wojcicki also worked on the development of [[Google Images]] and [[Google Books]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Susan Wojcicki - CEO @ YouTube|url=http://www.crunchbase.com/person/susan-wojcicki|website=[[TechCrunch#CrunchBase|CrunchBase]]|publisher=[[TechCrunch]]}}</ref>

In 2003, Wojcicki helped lead the development of [[Google AdSense|AdSense]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Profile Of Susan Wojcicki: Mother Of AdSense|url=https://searchengineland.com/profile-of-susan-wojcicki-mother-of-adsense-11629|date=2007-07-05|website=Search Engine Land|access-date=2020-05-31}}</ref> She served as its first product manager, and for her efforts, was awarded the Google Founders' Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://usatoday30.usatoday.com/tech/techinvestor/corporatenews/2007-07-04-google-wojcicki_N.htm|title=The house that helped build Google - USATODAY.com|website=usatoday30.usatoday.com|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref> She became Google's senior vice president of Advertising & Commerce, and oversaw the company's advertising and analytic products, including [[AdWords]], [[AdSense]], [[DoubleClick]], and [[Google Analytics]].<ref name="mercurynews2011" />

[[YouTube]], then a small start-up, was successfully competing with Google's Google Video service, overseen by Wojcicki. Her response was to propose the purchase of YouTube.<ref name="mercurynews2011" />

She handled two of Google's largest acquisitions&nbsp;— the $1.65 billion purchase of [[YouTube]] in 2006 and the $3.1 billion purchase of [[DoubleClick]] in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/bizwomen/news/latest-news/2017/08/wojcicki-emo-could-deter-women-from-tech-careers.html?page=all|title=Wojcicki: Memo could deter women from tech careers|last=Stangel|first=Luke|date=August 10, 2017|website=BizJournals}}</ref>

===CEO of YouTube===
In February 2014, Wojcicki became the CEO of YouTube.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://techcrunch.com/2014/02/05/susan-wojcicki-youtube/|title=Google Ads SVP Susan Wojcicki Takes Over At YouTube|access-date=June 12, 2014}}</ref> She was named one of ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s 100 most influential people in 2015<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Grazer |first=Brian |url=http://time.com/3822770/susan-wojcicki-2015-time-100/ |title=The 100 Most Influential People |magazine=TIME |date=April 16, 2015 |access-date=October 5, 2015}}</ref> and described in a later issue of ''Time'' as “the most powerful woman on the Internet.”<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Luscombe |first=Belinda |url=http://time.com/4012832/meet-youtubes-view-master/ |title=Meet YouTube's Viewmaster |magazine=TIME |date=August 27, 2015 |access-date=October 5, 2015}}</ref>

While Wojcicki has been CEO of YouTube, it has reached 2 billion logged-in users a month<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tubefilter.com/2019/02/05/youtube-2-billion-monthly-users/|title=YouTube Hits 2 Billion Monthly Users, As Number Of Channels With 1 Million Subscribers Doubled Last Year|date=2019-02-05|website=Tubefilter|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-02}}</ref> and announced that users were collectively watching one billion hours a day.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://mashable.com/2017/02/27/youtube-one-billion-hours-of-video-daily/#hs1RRV4k_Sqx|title=YouTube just hit a huge milestone|last=Hamedy|first=Saba|work=Mashable|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://fortune.com/2017/06/23/youtube-1-5-billion-monthly-users-mobile-video/|title=YouTube Claims 1.5 Billion Monthly Users in Latest Ad Sales Pitch|work=Fortune|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en}}</ref> There are localized versions of YouTube in 100 countries around the world, available in 80 languages. Since taking on the role of CEO, YouTube's percentage of female employees has risen from 24 to nearly 30 percent.<ref name="Wojcicki">{{Cite news|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2017/03/how-to-break-up-the-silicon-valley-boys-club-susan-wojcicki|title=Exclusive: How to Break Up the Silicon Valley Boys' Club|last=Wojcicki|first=Susan|work=The Hive|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en}}</ref>

[[File:Mateusz Morawiecki wraz z Susan Wojcicki prezesem YouTube odwiedzili Centrum Nauki Kopernik.jpg|thumb|Wojcicki next to Polish Prime Minister [[Mateusz Morawiecki]] in Warsaw, Poland, 11 November 2018]]
Wojcicki has overseen the development and release of new YouTube applications and experiences designed to cater to users interested in family<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2015/02/23/hands-on-with-youtube-kids-googles-newly-launched-child-friendly-youtube-app/|title=Hands On With "YouTube Kids," Google's Newly Launched, Child-Friendly YouTube App|last=Perez|first=Sarah|work=TechCrunch|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en}}</ref> gaming,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/aug/26/youtube-gaming-live-website-apps|title=Google launches YouTube Gaming to challenge Amazon-owned Twitch|last=Dredge|first=Stuart|date=2015-08-26|work=The Guardian|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> and music<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theverge.com/2015/11/12/9723496/youtube-music-app-offline-background|title=YouTube Music is here, and it's a game changer|work=The Verge|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en-US}}</ref> content. Under her leadership, the company has developed additional forms of monetization for YouTube creators, including channel memberships, merchandise, and Super Chat.<ref>{{Cite web|title=YouTube is adding more ways for creators to make money|url=https://www.theverge.com/2019/7/11/20690295/youtube-vidcon-2019-monetization-features-super-stickers|last=Farokhmanesh|first=Megan|date=2019-07-11|website=The Verge|language=en|access-date=2020-05-31}}</ref> She also oversaw the launch of YouTube's advertisement-free subscription service, [[YouTube Premium]] (formerly known as YouTube Red),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://venturebeat.com/2015/10/21/google-wants-you-to-pay-9-99-per-month-for-ad-free-youtube/|title=Google wants you to pay $9.99 per month for ad-free YouTube {{!}} VentureBeat|website=venturebeat.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-10-26|date=2015-10-22}}</ref> and its [[Over-the-top media services|over-the-top]] internet television service [[YouTube TV]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-39124092|title=YouTube takes on cable with new service|last=Lee|first=Dave|date=2017-03-01|work=BBC News|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Under Wojcicki, YouTube has tightened its policy on videos it regards as potentially violating its policies on hate speech and violent extremism.<ref>{{Cite web|title=YouTube's tougher harassment policy aims to address hate speech, veiled threats and repeat offenders|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2019/12/11/youtubes-tougher-harassment-policy-aims-to-address-hate-speech-veiled-threats-and-repeat-offenders/|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-31}}</ref> The more stringent policies came after ''[[The Times]]'' showed that "ads sponsored by the British government and several private sector companies had appeared ahead of YouTube videos supporting terrorist groups" and several large advertisers withdrew their ads from YouTube in response.<ref>{{cite news|last=Alba|first=Davey|title=YouTube's Ad Problems Finally Blow Up in Google's Face|url=https://www.wired.com/2017/03/youtubes-ad-problems-finally-blow-googles-face|newspaper=WIRED|date=25 March 2017}}</ref> The enforcement policies have been criticized as censorship.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hern|first=Alex|title=To censor or not to censor? YouTube's double bind|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2017/mar/21/youtube-advertisers-censorship|newspaper=theguardian|date=21 March 2017}}</ref> Some YouTubers argue that the demonetization system is way too strict, causing any remotely "edgy" content getting demonetized and in some cases even resulting in the creators channel being removed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vkDWHiphPz4}}</ref>
During the controversy surrounding [[Logan Paul]]'s YouTube video about a person that committed suicide, Wojcicki said that Paul did not violate YouTube's three strike policy and did not meet the criteria for being banned from the platform.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theverge.com/2018/2/12/17006074/youtube-ceo-logan-paul-susan-wojcicki|title=YouTube's CEO says Logan Paul doesn't deserve to be kicked off the platform|last=Newton|first=Casey|date=12 February 2018|website=The Verge|accessdate=18 October 2018}}</ref>

Wojcicki has emphasized educational content as a priority for the company, and on July 20, 2018, announced YouTube Learning to invest in grants and promotion to support education-focused creator content.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tubefilter.com/2018/10/23/youtube-20-million-investment-learning-channel/|website=www.tubefilter.com|access-date=2020-05-31}}</ref>

On October 22, 2018, Wojcicki criticized Article 13 of the [[Copyright Directive|European Union Copyright Directive]] that would give YouTube the sole responsibility for removing copyrighted content, saying it would pose a threat to content creators' ability to share their work.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theverge.com/2018/10/22/18008406/article-13-copyright-directive-youtube-susan-wojcicki-robert-kyncl|title=YouTube CEO says EU regulation will be bad for creators|last=Alexander|first=Julia|date=2018-10-22|work=The Verge|access-date=2018-10-27}}</ref>

==Boards==
In 2014, Wojcicki joined the board of [[Salesforce]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Susan Wojcicki|url=https://www.salesforce.com/company/leadership/bios/bio-wojcicki/|access-date=2020-09-18|website=Salesforce.com|language=en}}</ref> She also serves on the board of [[Room to Read]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Board of Directors|url=https://www.roomtoread.org/leadership-board/board-of-directors/|access-date=2020-09-18|website=www.roomtoread.org}}</ref> an organization that focuses on literacy and gender equality in education, and is a board member of [[UCLA Anderson School of Management]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Susan Wojcicki ('89) - "The most important Googler you've never heard of"|url=https://blogs.anderson.ucla.edu/anderson/2011/02/susan-wojcicki-89-the-most-important-googler-youve-never-heard-of.html|access-date=2020-09-18|website=UCLA Anderson School of Management Blog}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Wojcicki married Dennis Troper on August 23, 1998, in [[Belmont, California]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.paloaltoonline.com/weekly/morgue/community_pulse/1998_Nov_11.WEDDI111.html |title=Weddings |newspaper=Palo Alto Weekly |date=November 11, 1998 |access-date=October 4, 2014 }}</ref> They have five children. On December 16, 2014, ahead of taking her fifth maternity leave, Wojcicki wrote an op-ed in ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' about the importance of paid maternity leave. She is often quoted talking about the importance of finding balance between family and career.

In addition to her U.S. citizenship,<ref>[{{cite web|title=Forbes Profile Susan Wojcicki|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/susan-wojcicki/|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref> she is a Polish citizen.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kafka|first1=Peter|title=New YouTube Boss Susan Wojcicki Talks Talent, Music and M&A (Q&A)|url=http://recode.net/2014/08/12/new-youtube-boss-susan-wojcicki-talks-talent-music-and-ma-qa/|website=[[Recode|Re/code]]|access-date=16 December 2014|date=August 12, 2014}}</ref><ref>[https://www.wsj.com/articles/susan-wojcicki-paid-maternity-leave-is-good-for-business-1418773756 ''Paid Maternity Leave Is Good for Business''], The Wall Street Journal, 16 December 2014</ref><ref>[http://next.gazeta.pl/next/7,151003,21560668,prezes-youtube-wlasnie-przyjechala-do-polski-a-to-dopiero-poczatek.html#BoxBizImg ''Prezes YouTube wlasnie przyjechala do Polski a to dopiero poczatek''], Gazeta.pl, 28 March 2017</ref> Her grandfather, {{ill|Franciszek Wójcicki|pl}}, was a [[People's Party (Poland)|People's Party]] and [[Polish People's Party (1945–49)|Polish People's Party]] politician who had been elected MP during the [[Polish legislative election, 1947]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Franciszek Wójcicki|date=2018-02-28|url=https://pl.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Franciszek_W%C3%B3jcicki&oldid=52616862|work=Wikipedia, wolna encyklopedia|language=pl|access-date=2020-01-07}}</ref> Her grandmother, [[Janina Wójcicka Hoskins]], was a Polish-American librarian at the [[Library of Congress]], responsible for building the largest collection of Polish material in the United States.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Zalewski|first=Wojciech|date=2011-10-01|title=Janina Wójcicka Hoskins (1912–1996): Portrait of an Esteemed Librarian|journal=Slavic & East European Information Resources|volume=12|issue=4|pages=224–236|doi=10.1080/15228886.2011.623117|s2cid=144135260|issn=1522-8886}}</ref>

=== Advocacy ===
Wojcicki has advocated in favor of the expansion of paid family leave,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/susan-wojcicki-paid-maternity-leave-is-good-for-business-1418773756|title=Paid Maternity Leave Is Good for Business|last=Wojcicki|first=Susan|date=2014-12-16|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2017-10-26|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> helping Syrian refugees,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://time.com/4373471/susan-wojcicki-world-refugee-day/|title=Susan Wojcicki: Refugees Are Trying to Escape Terror—Not Create It|website=Time|access-date=2017-10-26}}</ref> countering gender discrimination at technology companies,<ref name="Wojcicki"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://money.cnn.com/2017/08/09/technology/youtube-ceo-gender-discrimination-response/index.html|title=YouTube CEO Susan Wojcicki on gender discrimination: It still hurts|last=Isidore|first=Chris|work=CNNMoney|access-date=2017-10-26}}</ref> and getting girls interested in computer science and prioritizing coding in schools.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/susan-wojcicki/tech-industry-gender-gap_b_9089472.html|title=Closing the Tech Industry Gender Gap|last=Wojcicki|first=Susan|date=2016-01-27|website=Huffington Post|language=en-US|access-date=2017-10-26}}</ref>

Wojcicki endorsed Democratic candidate [[Hillary Clinton]] in the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 presidential election]].<ref>Mandelbaum, R., [https://www.forbes.com/sites/robbmandelbaum/2016/09/23/more-business-leaders-sign-on-with-clinton/#4d1ff10750b0n More Business Leaders Sign On With Clinton], ''Forbes'', September 23, 2016.</ref>
