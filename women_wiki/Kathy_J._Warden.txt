'''Kathy J. Warden''' is an American business executive who serves as [[chief executive officer]] and [[President (corporate title)|president]] of [[Northrop Grumman]], as of 2019.

==Education==
Warden earned a bachelor's degree at [[James Madison University]] in 1992,<ref name="Sasser">{{cite news |last1=Sasser |first1=Matthew |title=Future CEO of Fortune 500 company on board of visitors |url=https://www.breezejmu.org/news/future-ceo-of-fortune-company-on-board-of-visitors/article_540a7136-d224-11e8-9d75-bbd7fd7a438f.html |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |work=[[The Breeze (newspaper)|The Breeze]] |publisher=[[James Madison University]] |date=October 18, 2018}}</ref> and a master's degree in [[business administration]] at [[George Washington University]] in 1999.<ref name="FB">{{cite news |last1=De Lea |first1=Brittany |title=Northrop Grumman new CEO: What to know about Kathy Warden |url=https://www.foxbusiness.com/business-leaders/northrop-grumman-new-ceo-what-to-know-about-kathy-warden |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |publisher=[[Fox Business Network]] |date=July 13, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=GWSB Graduate Named Northrop Grumman CEO |url=https://business.gwu.edu/gwsb-graduate-named-northrop-grumman-ceo |publisher=[[George Washington University]] |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |date=July 18, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Warden is a [[cybersecurity]] and [[information technology]] expert.<ref name=Fortune>{{cite journal |title=#22: Kathy Warden |journal=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]] |url=http://fortune.com/most-powerful-women/michelle-gass-21/ |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> Early in her career, she worked for [[General Electric]] for nearly a decade, and held executive roles at Veridian Corporation and [[General Dynamics]].<ref name=FB/><ref name="WaPo">{{cite news |title=Northrop Grumman CEO will step down |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2018/07/12/northrop-grumman-ceo-will-step-down |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |work=[[The Washington Post]] |date=July 12, 2018 |first1=Christian |last1= Davenport |first2=Aaron |last2=Gregg}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |title=Northrop's CEO to Depart Next Year as Defense Giant Elevates COO |journal=[[IndustryWeek]] |date=July 13, 2018 |url=https://www.industryweek.com/leadership/northrops-ceo-depart-next-year-defense-giant-elevates-coo |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> Warden was also a principal in a [[venture capital]] firm, where she helped companies improve their [[business model]]s and [[electronic publishing]] services.<ref name="Hutzell">{{cite news |last1=Hutzell |first1=Rick |title=People to watch: Kathy Warden, head of local Northrop Grumman unit |url=https://www.capitalgazette.com/news/ph-ac-cn-people-watch-warden-0103-20160103-story.html |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |work=[[The Capital]] |date=January 3, 2016}}</ref>

Warden joined [[Northrop Grumman]] in 2008, initially serving as vice president and general manager of the company's cybersecurity business.<ref name="Mehta">{{cite web |last1=Mehta |first1=Aaron |title=Northrop to change CEO |url=https://www.defensenews.com/breaking-news/2018/07/12/northrop-grumman-changes-ceos/ |website=[[Defense News]] |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> In 2015, while serving as president of Northrop Grumman Information Systems,<ref>{{cite news |title=Northrop Grumman reorganization means changes in Maryland |url=https://www.capitalgazette.com/blogs/marketplace/ph-ac-cn-northrop-grumman-1119-20151118-story.html |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |work=The Capital |date=November 18, 2015}}</ref> she was included in ''[[Federal Computer Week]]''{{'s}} "Federal 100" list; the magazine credited her for increasing Northrop Grumman's participation in the [[CyberPatriot]] program and creation of the Advanced Cyber Technology Center, and for overseeing $1.5 billion in contracts for the 2014 fiscal year.<ref name="FCW">{{cite journal |title=Federal 100: Kathy Warden |journal=[[Federal Computer Week]] |date=March 16, 2015 |url=https://fcw.com/articles/2015/03/16/fed100_warden-kathy.aspx |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> Warden began her tenure as president of the Mission Systems sector in January 2016, when the company merged its [[information system|Information Systems]] and Electronics Systems sectors.<ref name=Fortune/><ref name="Set">{{cite journal |last1=Bach |first1=Natasha |title=The Fortune 500 Is Set to Get Another Female CEO |journal=Fortune |date=July 13, 2018 |url=http://fortune.com/2018/07/13/new-northrop-grumman-ceo-kathy-warden-fortune-500-women/ |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Wilkers">{{cite journal |last1=Wilkers |first1=Ross |title=Northrop CEO Wes Bush to retire, Kathy Warden named successor |journal=[[Washington Technology]] |date=July 12, 2018 |url=https://washingtontechnology.com/articles/2018/07/12/northrop-ceo-transition.aspx |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> She has served as president and COO of Northrop Grumman since September 2017,<ref name=Wilkers/> and her tenure as CEO started on January 1, 2019.<ref name=Fortune/>

Warden has been credited with leading the integration of Orbital ATK, now [[Northrop Grumman Innovation Systems]].<ref name=Fortune/><ref name="Neibauer">{{cite journal |last1=Neibauer |first1=Michael |title=Orbital ATK is now part of Northrop Grumman |journal=Washington Business Journal |date=June 7, 2018 |url=https://www.bizjournals.com/washington/news/2018/06/07/orbital-atk-is-now-part-of-northrop-grumman.html |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> She was elected to Northrop Grumman's [[board of director]]s since her appointment to the CEO role in mid-2018.<ref name=FB/><ref name=Set/>

===Board service and recognition===
Warden served on the [[Presidency of Bill Clinton|Clinton administration]]'s Internet Advisory Council.<ref name=FB/><ref name=Hutzell/> She has a position on the board of the [[Wolf Trap National Park for the Performing Arts]].<ref name=Hutzell/><ref>{{cite web |title=Wolf Trap Foundation Board Members |url=https://www.wolftrap.org/about/leadership/board.aspx |publisher=Wolf Trap Foundation |accessdate=December 12, 2018}}</ref> She serves on the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond]] board, as of mid-2018.<ref name=FB/> She also works with the [[Aspen Institute]]'s computer security strategy group.<ref name=Sasser/>

She joined James Madison University's board of visitors in October 2018.<ref name=Sasser/> Previously, Warden served on the board of the university's College of Business, starting in 2016.<ref name=Sasser/> She received a Business Achievement Award from James Madison University's [[Beta Gamma Sigma]] chapter in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Briggs |first1=Stephen |title=CoB Alumna Receives Beta Gamma Sigma Business Achievement Award |url=https://www.jmu.edu/news/cob/2018/04/23-cob-alumna-receives-beta-gamma-sigma-business-achievement-award.shtml |publisher=James Madison University |accessdate=November 30, 2018 |date=April 23, 2018}}</ref>

Warden was included in ''[[Washingtonian (magazine)|Washingtonian]]''{{'s}} 2017 list of the "most powerful women" in Washington, D.C.,<ref name="Milk">{{cite journal |last1=Milk |first1=Leslie |title=The Most Powerful Women in Washington |journal=[[Washingtonian (magazine)|Washingtonian]] |date=October 2, 2017 |url=https://www.washingtonian.com/2017/10/02/the-most-powerful-women-in-washington/ |accessdate=December 3, 2018}}</ref> as well as the magazine's 2018 list of "Washington's Top Tech Leaders".<ref name="Guinto">{{cite journal |last1=Guinto |first1=Joseph |title=Tech Titans 2018: Washington's Top Tech Leaders |journal=Washingtonian |date=September 20, 2018 |url=https://www.washingtonian.com/2018/09/20/tech-titans-2018-washingtons-top-tech-leaders/ |accessdate=December 3, 2018}}</ref> She has ranked number 22 and number 80 on ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]''{{'s}} and ''CEOWORLD'' magazine's lists of "most powerful women", respectively.<ref name=Fortune/><ref name="Batchelor">{{cite journal |last1=Batchelor |first1=Megan |title=These are the 50 most powerful woman in U.S. business for 2018 by Fortune magazine |journal=CEOWORLD |date=October 16, 2018 |url=https://ceoworld.biz/2018/10/16/these-are-the-50-most-powerful-woman-in-u-s-business-for-2018-by-fortune-magazine/ |accessdate=December 3, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Papadopoulos">{{cite journal |last1=Papadopoulos |first1=Anna |title=World's Most Powerful Women for 2018 |journal=CEOWORLD |date=November 12, 2018 |url=https://ceoworld.biz/2018/11/12/most-powerful-women-in-the-world-2018/ |accessdate=December 3, 2018}}</ref>
