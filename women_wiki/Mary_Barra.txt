'''Mary Teresa Barra''' (née '''Makela'''; born December 24, 1961) is an American [[Women in business|businesswoman]] who has been the chairman and CEO of [[General Motors|General Motors Company]] since January 15, 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Muller|first=Joann|title=Exclusive: Inside New CEO Mary Barra's Urgent Mission To Fix GM|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/joannmuller/2014/05/28/exclusive-inside-mary-barras-urgent-mission-to-fix-gm/|access-date=2020-11-23|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref> She is the first female [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] of a major automaker.<ref name="nyt">{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/11/business/gm-names-first-female-chief-executive.html | title=G.M. Names First Female Chief Executive | last1=Vlasic | first1=Bill | work=[[The New York Times]] | date=December 10, 2013 | accessdate=December 11, 2013}}</ref><ref name="General Motors">{{cite web | url=http://media.gm.com/content/media/us/en/gm/news.detail.html/content/Pages/news/emergency_news/1210-gm-execs.html | title=Dan Akerson to Retire as GM CEO in January 2014 Mary Barra to Become Next CEO; Dan Ammann Named President
| publisher=[[General Motors]] | accessdate=December 10, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/02/05/general-motors-gm-earnings-q4-2019.html|title=GM swings to a loss in the fourth quarter as 40-day strike erodes profits|last=Wayland|first=Michael|date=2020-02-05|website=CNBC|language=en|access-date=2020-02-06}}</ref> On December 10, 2013, GM named her to succeed [[Daniel Akerson|Dan Akerson]] as [[chief executive officer]].<ref name="nyt" /> Prior to being named CEO, Barra served as the executive vice president of Global Product Development, Purchasing, and Supply Chain at General Motors.<ref name="nyt"/><ref name="General Motors"/>

==Early life==
Barra was born in [[Royal Oak, Michigan]]. Barra's parents are of [[Finnish American|Finnish]] descent.<ref name=CNNFortune>{{cite news|last=Taylor|first=Alex|title=Mary Barra: GM's next CEO?|url=http://management.fortune.cnn.com/2012/12/17/gm-mary-barra/|work=CNN/Fortune|accessdate=December 11, 2013|date=December 17, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131214071920/http://management.fortune.cnn.com/2012/12/17/gm-mary-barra/|archive-date=December 14, 2013}}</ref><ref name=HelsinginSanomat>{{cite web|last=Niskakangas|first=Tuomas|title=New CEO of automotive icon is of Finnish descent|url=http://www.helsinkitimes.fi/business/8707-new-ceo-of-automotive-icon-is-of-finnish-descent.html|date=December 16, 2013|work=[[Helsinki Times]]}}</ref> Barra's ancestors are from the village of [[Kankaanpää, Säkylä|Kankaanpää]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.aamulehti.fi/a/24138832 |title=Trumpin uusi neuvonantaja on moottorimimmi – osaa sanoa suomeksi "Onks tankissa bensaa?" |last1=Tapio |first1=Ilari |date=December 13, 2016 |website=[[Aamulehti]] |language=fi |access-date=July 8, 2019 |quote=Amerikkalaisen autojätin toimitusjohtajan Mary Barran sukujuuret ovat Mäkelän torpassa Köyliön Kankaanpään kylässä.}}</ref> in [[Satakunta|Satakunta province]] in Finland, from the former municipality of [[Köyliö]]. Her grandfather, Viktor Mäkelä, moved to the [[United States]] and married Maria Luoma, a Finnish immigrant from [[Teuva]]. They lived in [[Minnesota]], in the small mining town of [[Mountain Iron]]. They had three children, including a son named Reino,<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://blogs.wsj.com/corporate-intelligence/2013/12/10/a-look-at-mary-barra-gms-first-female-ceo/ | title=A Look at Mary Barra, GM's First Female CEO | work=The Wall Street Journal | date=December 10, 2013 | accessdate=December 11, 2013}}</ref> also called Ray. Her father Ray married a second-generation Finnish American named Eva Pyykkönen, and Mary was born in 1961. Ray worked for 39 years at the [[Pontiac (automobile)|Pontiac]] car factory in [[Detroit]], and the family lived in [[Waterford Township, Oakland County, Michigan|Waterford, Michigan]]. Barra attended Waterford schools in Michigan. She is a graduate of [[Waterford Mott High School]].

== Education ==
Barra graduated from the General Motors Institute (now [[Kettering University]]) in 1985, where she obtained a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in [[electrical engineering]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Meet Mary Barra, Kettering graduate ‘85 {{!}} Kettering University Online|url=https://online.kettering.edu/news/2015/09/10/meet-mary-barra-kettering-graduate-%E2%80%9885|access-date=2020-11-23|website=online.kettering.edu|language=en}}</ref> Barra was inducted into the engineering honor society [[Tau Beta Pi#Undergraduate eligibility requirements|Tau Beta Pi]].<ref name="taubetapi2018">{{Cite web|url=https://www.tbp.org/other/NAEclass18.cfm|title=Tau Beta Pi - NAE class 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200402082529/https://www.tbp.org/other/NAEclass18.cfm|archive-date=2 April 2020|publisher=[[Tau Beta Pi]]|access-date=2020-04-02|quote=Mary T. Barra, Michigan Zeta '85|location=[[Knoxville, Tennessee]]}}</ref> She then attended [[Stanford Graduate School of Business]] on a GM fellowship, receiving her [[Masters in Business Administration]] degree in 1990.<ref name=bb/>

==Career==

=== General Motors ===
Barra started working for General Motors in 1980 as a [[Cooperative education|co-op student]], when she was 18 years old.<ref name=shm/> Her job was checking fender panels and inspecting hoods, and she used this job to pay for her college tuition.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://work.qz.com/1175673/general-motors-ceo-mary-barra-on-her-leadership-style-and-career/|title=GM CEO Mary Barra says too many women quit their jobs for the wrong reason|work=Quartz at Work|access-date=2018-04-13|language=en-US}}</ref> She subsequently held a variety of engineering and administrative positions, including managing the [[Detroit/Hamtramck Assembly]] plant.<ref name=bb>{{cite web | url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=40221799&privcapId=61206100 | title=Mary Barra: Executive Profile & Biography | work=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]] | access-date=December 11, 2013}}</ref>

In February 2008, she became vice president of Global Manufacturing Engineering. In July 2009, she advanced to the position of vice president of Global [[Human Resources]], which she held until February 2011, when she was named executive vice president of Global Product Development.<ref name=bb/><ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/01/21/business/21auto.html | title=G.M. Names New Leader for Global Development | last1=Bunkley | first1=Nick | last2=Vlasic | first2=Bill | work=The New York Times | date=January 20, 2011 | access-date=December 11, 2013}}</ref> The latter position included responsibilities for design; she has worked to reduce the number of [[automobile platforms]] in [[List of GM platforms|GM]].<ref name="nyt"/> In August 2013, her vice president responsibility was extended to include Global Purchasing and Supply Chain.<ref name=gm>{{cite web | url=http://www.gm.com/company/aboutGM/GM_Corporate_Officers/mary_barra.html | title=About GM: Mary T. Barra | publisher=General Motors| access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref>

When Barra took over as chief executive of General Motors in January 2014, she became the first female head of an automobile manufacturer.<ref>[http://www.new-corner.com/rebuilding-a-giant-mary-barra-ceo-general-motors/ Rebuilding a Giant: Mary Barra, CEO, General Motors], New Corner, June 5, 2015</ref>

During her first year as CEO, General Motors issued 84 [[safety recall]]s involving over 30 million cars.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.zacks.com/stock/news/159304/general-motors-gm-safety-recalls-add-up-to-84-in-2014l | title=General Motors (GM): Safety Recalls Add Up to 84 in 2014 | publisher=Zacks Equity Research | access-date=January 5, 2015}}</ref> Barra was called before the Senate to testify about the recalls and deaths attributed to the faulty ignition switch.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/2014/07/23/gm-ignition-senate-mary-barra-editorials-debates/13068081/ | title=GM reverses openness pledge: Our view | publisher=USA Today | access-date=July 23, 2014 | date=July 23, 2014}}</ref> Barra and General Motors also came under suspicion of paying for awards to burnish the CEO and corporation's image during that time.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.autoblog.com/2015/01/21/gm-buying-awards-mary-barra-report/ | title=Is General Motors buying awards for Mary Barra? | publisher=Autoblog | access-date=January 21, 2015}}</ref> The recalls led to the creation of new policies encouraging workers to report problems they encounter in an attempt to change company culture.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://work.qz.com/1175673/general-motors-ceo-mary-barra-on-her-leadership-style-and-career/|title=GM CEO Mary Barra says too many women quit their jobs for the wrong reason|work=Quartz at Work|access-date=February 26, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

As CEO, Barra made GM move into driverless and electric-powered cars through acquisitions including Strobe, a startup in driverless technology.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/10/09/business/general-motors-driverless.html|title=G.M. Acquires Strobe, Start-Up Focused on Driverless Technology|last=Vlasic|first=Bill|date=October 9, 2017|work=The New York Times|access-date=October 17, 2017|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> In 2017 GM began to sell the Chevy Bolt EV, beating rival Tesla to the first electric car priced under $40,000 with a range of 200 miles.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/a-day-in-the-life-of-gm-ceo-mary-barra-1461601044|title=A Day in the Life of GM CEO Mary Barra|last=Ross|first=Christopher|date=April 26, 2016|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=November 15, 2017|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref>

In 2017, Barra was the highest paid [[Detroit Three]] executive, with a total remuneration of $21.96 million.<ref name="detroitnews.com">{{cite web|url=https://detroitnews.com/story/business/autos/general-motors/2018/04/27/gm-proxy-mary-barra-compensation/34313299/|title=GM paid CEO Mary Barra nearly $22M in 2017}}</ref> In November 2018, Barra announced the closure of five North American plants and 14,000 worker [[lay off]]s.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/11/26/gm-unallocating-several-plants-in-2019-to-take-3-billion-to-3point8-billion-charge-in-future-quarters.html|title=GM to halt production at several plants, cut more than 14,000 jobs|first=Robert|last=Ferris|date=November 26, 2018}}</ref> Her decision was criticized by [[President Trump]], who threatened to remove the company's government subsidies in response.<ref name="auto"/>

=== Disney ===
In August 2017, she was elected to the board of [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Golum|first1=Rob|last2=Welch|first2=David|title=Barra elected to Walt Disney board|url=http://www.autonews.com/article/20170824/OEM02/170829852/barra-elected-to-walt-disney-board|publisher=Automotive News|date=August 24, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=General Motors Chairman Mary T. Barra Joins Disney Board of Directors|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/gm-chairman-mary-t-barra-disney-board-directors-1202538221/|last=McNary|first=Dave|date=2017-08-24|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2020-05-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=GM CEO Mary Barra Joins Disney's Board Amid Successor Search|url=https://fortune.com/2017/08/24/walt-disney-gm-general-motors-mary-barra/|website=Fortune|language=en|access-date=2020-05-01}}</ref> She was the 12th person elected to this board, and the fourth woman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/disney-adds-general-motors-ceo-board-directors-1032380|title=Disney Adds General Motors CEO to Board of Directors|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en|access-date=2019-07-26}}</ref>

=== Other boards and councils ===
Barra was a member of the [[General Dynamics]] board of directors. She serves on the board of directors of the Detroit Economic Club and Detroit Country Day School.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dykema.com/news-press-kellett-elected-to-detroit-economic-club-board_041015.html|title=Dykema Chairman and CEO Peter Kellett Elected to the Detroit Economic Club Board of Directors|website=www.dykema.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theoaklandpress.com/news/detroit-country-day-school-announces-new-trustees-including-gm-ceo/article_f3d4a985-3f8e-5e98-a477-182d5e586370.html|title=Detroit Country Day School announces new trustees, including GM CEO Mary Barra|website=The Oakland Press|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hometownlife.com/story/news/local/birmingham/2014/10/12/gm-ceo-mary-barra-joins-detroit-country-day-school-board-trustees/17041271/|title=GM CEO Mary Barra joins Detroit Country Day School Board of Trustees|last=Grossman|first=Jay|website=HometownLife|language=en|access-date=2020-02-19}}</ref> She is a member of the Stanford University Board of Trustees, the Stanford Graduate School of Business Advisory Council, and the [[Duke University]] Board of Trustees.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|last=Burden|first=Melissa|title=GM CEO Barra joins Stanford University board|url=https://www.detroitnews.com/story/business/autos/general-motors/2015/07/15/gm-ceo-barra-joins-stanford-university-board/30181281/|access-date=2020-02-14|website=Detroit News|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Three Join Board of Trustees|url=https://today.duke.edu/2020/07/three-join-board-trustees|access-date=2020-07-03|website=today.duke.edu|language=en}}</ref>

==Awards and honors==
Barra was listed as 35th on [[Forbes list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|''Forbes'' Most Powerful Women]] list, in 2013, rising in rank to second most powerful, in 2018.<ref name=Forbes14>{{cite news|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/#tab:overall|website=Forbes|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=June 26, 2018}}</ref>

In May 2014, she delivered the [[commencement address]] for [[University of Michigan]]'s Ann Arbor campus at [[Michigan Stadium]], and received an [[honorary degree]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/mary-barra/ | title=Mary Barra | work=[[Forbes]] | last=Goudreau|first=Jenna | year=2012|accessdate=September 7, 2012}}</ref>
In 2018, she received an honorary Doctorate from [[Duke University]].

Barra was first in [[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'s Most Powerful Women list in 2015, up from second the year before.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Mary Barra|url = http://fortune.com/most-powerful-women/mary-barra-1/|website = Fortune|accessdate = October 19, 2015}}</ref>

She remained in the number one spot in [[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]'s Most Powerful Women of 2017<ref>{{Cite web|title = Mary Barra|url = http://fortune.com/most-powerful-women/|website = Fortune|accessdate = April 3, 2017}}</ref> and Number 5 on [[Forbes]] World's 100 Most Powerful Women List in the same year.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women/list/|title=World's Most Powerful Women|work=Forbes|access-date=March 29, 2018|language=en}}</ref>

In April 2014, Barra was featured on the cover of [[Time (magazine)|Time]]'s "100 Most Influential People in the World."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Iacocca|first1=Lee|title=Mary Barra: The World's 100 Most Influential People|url=http://time.com/collection-post/70843/mary-barra-2014-time-100/|work=TIME.com}}</ref>

In December 2016, Barra joined a business forum assembled by [[Donald J. Trump|then President-Elect Donald Trump]] to provide strategic and policy advice on economic issues.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bryan|first1=Bob|title=Trump is forming an economic advisory team with the CEOs of Disney, General Motors, JPMorgan, and more|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/trump-strategic-and-policy-forum-includes-dimon-iger-schwarzman-2016-12|accessdate=June 1, 2017|publisher=Business Insider|date=December 2, 2016}}</ref> However, she left the forum in 2017, following Trump's response to the Charlottesville protests.

Barra was elected to the [[National Academy of Engineering#Membership|National Academy of Engineering]] in February 2018.<ref>{{cite press release |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|url=https://www.nae.edu/178117.aspx|archive-date=8 February 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180208182205/https://www.nae.edu/178117.aspx|title=National Academy of Engineering Elects 83 Members and 16 Foreign Members|date=2018-02-07|publisher=[[National Academy of Engineering]]|access-date=2020-04-02|quote=For leadership in automotive manufacturing, product engineering, and product development.|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|date=Spring 2018|title=Class of 2018 Elected : New Members|url=https://www.nae.edu/File.aspx?id=181157#page=49|url-status=live|journal=The Bridge|publisher=[[National Academy of Sciences]]|volume=48|issue=1|page=47|issn=0737-6278|location=[[Washington, D.C.]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200402090303/https://www.nae.edu/File.aspx?id=181157|archive-date=2 April 2020}}</ref> In September 2018, Barra was awarded the Yale Chief Executive Leadership Institute's Legend in Leadership Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://som.yale.edu/news/2018/09/chief-executive-leadership-institute-honors-mary-t-barra-with-legend-in-leadership-award|title=Chief Executive Leadership Institute Honors Mary T. Barra with Legend in Leadership Award|date=2018-09-24|website=Yale School of Management|language=en|access-date=2019-07-19}}</ref>

In ''[[Institutional Investor (magazine)|Institutional Investor]]''{{'}}s yearly survey of top executives, the 2019 All-America Executive Team, Barra achieved the first place in the autos and auto parts sector.<ref name="Whyte">{{cite news |last1=Whyte |first1=Amy |title=These Are America's Top Executives Jamie Dimon, Tim Cook, and Mary Barra are among members of II's 2019 All-America Executive Team |url=https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/b1bsssqc9w0p5v/These-Are-America-s-Top-Executives |accessdate=29 March 2019 |work=Institutional Investor |date=November 13, 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Barra is married to consultant Tony Barra, whom she met while studying at [[Kettering University]], and has two children and two dogs.<ref name="nyt"/> They live in [[Northville Township, Michigan|Northville]], a suburb of Detroit. She also owns an apartment in [[Downtown Detroit]]<ref name=wsj>{{cite news| last=Bennett | first=Jeff | title=Longtime Insider Is GM's First Female CEO | url=https://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702303560204579249952766187032?mod=WSJ_hps_LEFTTopStories | work=[[The Wall Street Journal]] | author2=Murray, Sara | pages=A1, A10 | date=December 11, 2013 | accessdate=December 11, 2013}}</ref>

She has named the [[Chevrolet Camaro]] and the [[Pontiac Firebird]] as her favorite cars.<ref name="shm">{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/11/business/mary-barra-gms-new-chief-speaking-her-mind.html | title=Mary Barra, G.M.'s New Chief, Speaking Her Mind | work=The New York Times | date=December 10, 2013 | accessdate=December 11, 2013}}</ref> Barra can speak a little [[Finnish language|Finnish]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://yle.fi/uutiset/osasto/news/mondays_papers_barras_finnish_roots_amorella_aftermath_and_tamperes_unemployment_problem/6986456|title=Barra's Finnish roots|date=December 16, 2013|website=Yle Uutiset|language=en|access-date=2019-06-11}}</ref>
