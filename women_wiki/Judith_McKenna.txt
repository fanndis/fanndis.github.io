'''Judith McKenna''' (born 1966) is a British businesswoman. She has been CEO of [[Walmart]]'s international division since 2018. Previously, she held leadership roles within Walmart's US division and [[Asda]], a subsidiary of Walmart International. 

==Early life==
McKenna was born in [[Middlesbrough]], England, in 1966.<ref name="BBC4">{{cite news |title=Judith McKenna |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/2HKCHP01CWwp4vG5c0pVLhl/judith-mckenna |work=[[BBC Radio 4]] |accessdate=30 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="MostSenior">{{cite news |title=Judith McKenna becomes most senior woman in UK food retail as Walmart international boss |last1=Armstrong |first1=Ashley |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/business/2018/01/17/judith-mckenna-becomes-senior-woman-uk-food-retail-walmart-international/ |work=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |date=January 17, 2018 |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref><ref name="managementtoday.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://www.managementtoday.co.uk/judith-mckenna-mt-interview/article/1124087|title=Judith McKenna: The MT Interview|publisher=Management Today|accessdate=5 June 2018}}</ref> She was educated at a local comprehensive school, then earned a law degree at [[University of Hull]].<ref name="managementtoday.co.uk"/> She qualified as a chartered accountant with [[KPMG]].<ref name="Sting">{{cite news |title=How Asda takes sting out of the crunch |last1=Wood |first1=Zoe |url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/nov/16/asda-interview-judith-mckenna |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=November 15, 2008 |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
McKenna started her career with KPMG, followed by [[Carlsberg Group|Carlsberg Tetley]] and [[Allied Domecq]], and joined [[Asda]] in 1996.<ref name="MostSenior"/> McKenna became [[chief financial officer]] of Asda in 2001, a position she held until she was promoted to [[chief operating officer]] in July 2011.<ref name="managementtoday.co.uk"/><ref name="Sting"/>

At Asda, McKenna worked with David Cheesewright, whom she would later succeed at Walmart International.<ref name="LeadWalmartInternational">{{cite news |title=Judith McKenna to lead Walmart International, Cheesewright to retire |last1=Souza |first1=Kim |url=https://talkbusiness.net/2018/01/judith-mckenna-to-lead-walmart-international-cheesewright-to-retire/ |work=Talk Business & Politics |date=January 17, 2018 |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> McKenna moved to the U.S. to lead strategy and development for Walmart International in 2014.<ref name="LeadWalmartInternational"/> The following year, she became chief operating officer for Walmart U.S.<ref name="LeadWalmartInternational"/> As COO, McKenna and Walmart U.S. president CEO Greg Foran led a $2 billion investment in higher wages, training, and technology.<ref name="BiggestDeal">{{cite news |title=Meet the Woman Running Walmart's Biggest Deal Ever |last1=Kowitt |first1=Beth |url=http://fortune.com/longform/walmart-international-flipkart-judith-mckenna/ |work=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]] |date=September 25, 2018 |accessdate=January 7, 2019}}</ref> McKenna undertook initiatives to improve store associates' workplace, including efforts to clean stores, relax the dress code, and reinstituting Walmart Radio.<ref name="BiggestDeal"/> McKenna also brought to Walmart U.S. tools she used at Asda, including online ordering and in-store pickup.<ref name="BiggestDeal"/>

In January 2018, it was announced that McKenna would return to Walmart International, succeeding David Cheesewright as president and CEO.<ref name="LeadWalmartInternational"/><ref name="MostSenior"/> McKenna took the post in February, and leads strategy and operations in more than 25 countries including Mexico, Canada, China, and India.<ref name="MostPowerful">{{cite news |title=#14: Judith McKenna |url=http://fortune.com/most-powerful-women/judith-mckenna-14/ |work=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]] |date=2018 |accessdate=30 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="MostSenior">{{cite news |title=Judith McKenna becomes most senior woman in UK food retail as Walmart international boss |last1=Armstrong |first1=Ashley |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/business/2018/01/17/judith-mckenna-becomes-senior-woman-uk-food-retail-walmart-international/ |work=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |date=January 17, 2018 |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Flipkart">{{cite news |title=Walmart Agrees to Buy 77% Stake in Flipkart for $16 Billion |last1=Nassauer |first1=Sarah |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/walmart-agrees-to-buy-77-stake-in-flipkart-for-16-billion-1525864334 |work=[[The Wall Street Journal]] |date=May 9, 2018 |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> In 2018, she oversaw the $16 billion acquisition of 77 percent of [[Flipkart]], the largest online retailer in India.<ref name="MostPowerful"/><ref name="Flipkart"/>

McKenna was named to ''[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]''{{'}}s list of 50 Most Powerful Women in 2015, 2016, 2017,<ref name="MostSenior"/> and 2018,<ref name="Climbs">{{cite news |title=Walmart exec climbs Fortune's 50 Most Powerful Women list |last1=McKay |first1=Serenah |url=https://www.arkansasonline.com/news/2018/oct/02/walmart-exec-climbs-fortunes-50-most-powerful-wome/ |work=[[Arkansas Democrat-Gazette]] |date=October 2, 2018 |accessdate=November 30, 2018}}</ref> and 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/judith-mckenna/|title=Judith McKenna|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-12-17}}</ref>

==Personal life==
McKenna met her husband, Phil Dutton, former finance director of [[Punch Taverns]], when both worked at Asda.<ref name="MostSenior"/> They have two children.<ref name="MostSenior"/>
