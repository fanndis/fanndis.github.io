'''Melinda Ann Gates''' ([[née]] '''French'''; born August 15, 1964)<ref>{{cite web|last=Harris|first=Paul|title=A woman of substance|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2006/nov/26/theobserver|website=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=February 10, 2020|date=November 25, 2006|quote=Melinda Ann French was born in Dallas on 15 August 1964.}}</ref> is an American [[Philanthropy|philanthropist]] and a former general manager at [[Microsoft]].<ref name="Melinda Gates Biography">{{cite web|title=Melinda Gates|url=https://www.gatesfoundation.org/Who-We-Are/General-Information/Leadership/Executive-Leadership-Team/Melinda-Gates|website=gatesfoundation.org|publisher=Gates Foundation|accessdate=March 13, 2019}}</ref> In 2000, she co-founded the [[Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation]] with her husband [[Bill Gates]], the world's largest private charitable organization as of 2015.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Mathiesen|first=Karl|url=https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2015/mar/16/what-is-the-bill-and-melinda-gates-foundation|title=What is the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation?|date=March 16, 2015|work=The Guardian|access-date=February 25, 2020|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Gates has consistently been ranked as one of the world's most powerful women by [[Forbes]].<ref name="The World's Most Powerful Women 2018">{{cite web|title=Power Women|url=https://www.forbes.com/lists/power-women/#3d5d9f225a95|website=forbes.com|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=March 13, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Melinda Ann French was born on August 15, 1964 in [[Dallas|Dallas, Texas]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/gadgets-and-tech/features/office-romance-how-bill-met-melinda-855292.html|title=Office romance: how Bill met Melinda|date=June 27, 2008|work=The Independent}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Melinda Gates biography |url=https://www.biography.com/people/melinda-gates-507408 |website=biography.com |publisher=A&E Television Networks |accessdate=October 24, 2018 |date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> She is the second of four children born to Raymond Joseph French Jr., an [[aerospace engineer]], and Elaine Agnes Amerland, a [[homemaker]]. Melinda has an older sister and two younger brothers.<ref>[https://money.cnn.com/2008/01/04/news/newsmakers/gates.fortune/index2.htm "Melinda Gates goes public (pg. 2)"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120501013308/http://money.cnn.com/2008/01/04/news/newsmakers/gates.fortune/index2.htm |date=May 1, 2012 }}, cnn.com, January 7, 2008.</ref>

Melinda, a [[Catholic]],<ref name="tedcontraception">{{cite av media|url=http://www.ted.com/talks/melinda_gates_let_s_put_birth_control_back_on_the_agenda|title=Let's put birth control back on the agenda|work=TEDxChange|date=April 2012|author=Melinda Gates|accessdate=June 29, 2013}}</ref><ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/world/video/2012/jul/11/melinda-gates-catholic-contraceptives-video?newsfeed=true "Melinda Gates: 'I'm a Catholic, but women need access to contraceptives'"], Guardian.co.uk; retrieved June 29, 2013.</ref><ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/health-and-families/health-news/catholic-melinda-gates-defies-the-vatican-over-birth-control-funds-7936386.html "Catholic Melinda Gates defies the Vatican over birth control funds"], independent.co.uk; retrieved June 29, 2013.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/news/bill-gates-the-rolling-stone-interview-20140313?print=true|title=Bill Gates: The Rolling Stone Interview|date=March 13, 2014|first=Jeff|last=Goodell|work=Rolling Stone|accessdate=April 23, 2016}}</ref> attended St. Monica Catholic School, where she was the top student in her class.<ref name="Catholic 2">{{cite book|title=Bill Gates: Entrepreneur and Philanthropist|url=https://archive.org/details/billgatesentrepr0000lesi|url-access=registration|page=[https://archive.org/details/billgatesentrepr0000lesi/page/61 61]|quote=Melinda, a devout Catholic, wanted a religious wedding|author=Jeanne M. Lesinski|publisher=Twenty First Century Books|accessdate=March 10, 2011|isbn=9781580135702|year=2009}}</ref><ref name="Catholic 1">{{cite book|title=Business Week, Issues 3649–3652|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vo2ZAAAAIAAJ&q=Melinda+Gates+roman+catholic|quote=Raised a Roman Catholic and educated at a girls' Catholic high school, Melinda was encouraged to pursue her love of science|publisher=[[McGraw-Hill]]|accessdate=March 10, 2011|year=1999}}</ref> At age 14, Melinda was introduced to the [[Apple II]] by her father, which later inspired her interest in computer games and the [[BASIC]] programming language.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/gadgets-and-tech/features/office-romance-how-bill-met-melinda-855292.html|title=Office romance: how Bill met Melinda|date=June 27, 2008|website=The Independent|language=en|accessdate=March 13, 2019}}</ref>

Melinda graduated as [[valedictorian]] from [[Ursuline Academy of Dallas]] in 1982.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ursuline Receives Additional $2 Million Grant; Science, Math, Technology Building Task Force is Formed|url=https://www.gatesfoundation.org/Media-Center/Press-Releases/2007/05/Ursuline-Receives-Additional-2-Million-Grant-Science-Math-Technology-Building-Task-Force-is-Formed|website=Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation|accessdate=September 22, 2017}}</ref> She earned a [[bachelor's degree]] in [[computer science]] and [[economics]] from [[Duke University]] in 1986 and an [[Master of Business Administration|MBA]] from Duke's [[Fuqua School of Business]] in 1987.<ref name="StangeOyster2013">{{cite book|author1=Mary Zeiss Stange|author2=Carol K. Oyster|author3=Jane E. Sloan|title=The Multimedia Encyclopedia of Women in Today's World|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_BTKCgAAQBAJ&pg=PT386|date=January 9, 2013|publisher=SAGE Publications|isbn=978-1-4522-7037-1|pages=386–}}</ref> At Duke, Melinda was a member of the [[Kappa Alpha Theta]] sorority, Beta Rho Chapter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.nl/life-of-melinda-gates-2016-3/|title=The incredible life of Melinda Gates — one of the world's richest and most powerful women|date=November 1, 2017|accessdate=March 24, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
Gates' first job was tutoring children in mathematics and computer programming.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/3066355/how-melinda-gates-is-diversifying-tech|title=How Melinda Gates Is Diversifying Tech|last=Staff|first=Fast Company|date=January 9, 2017|website=Fast Company|language=en-US|accessdate=March 13, 2019}}</ref> After graduation, she became a marketing manager with [[Microsoft]], being responsible for leading the development of various multimedia products.<ref name=":1">{{cite book|title=Melinda Gates : Philanthropist and Education Advocate.|last=Cathleen.|first=Small|date=2017|publisher=Cavendish Square Publishing LLC|isbn=9781502627087|location=New York, NY|oclc=1039680502}}</ref> These included [[Microsoft Cinemania|Cinemania]], [[Encarta]], [[Microsoft Publisher|Publisher]], [[Microsoft Bob]], [[Microsoft Money|Money]], [[Microsoft Works|Works (Macintosh)]] and [[Microsoft Word|Word]].<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{cite news|title=Office Romance: How Bill met Melinda|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/gadgets-and-tech/features/office-romance-how-bill-met-melinda-855292.html|newspaper=The Independent|year=2008|accessdate=January 10, 2019}}</ref> She worked on [[Expedia]], which became one of the most popular travel booking websites. In the early 1990s, Gates was appointed as General Manager of Information Products, a position which she held until 1996.<ref name=":2">{{Cite book|last=Honders, Christine|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/921141343|title=Melinda Gates|isbn=978-1-5081-4832-6|location=New York|pages=8|oclc=921141343}}</ref> She left Microsoft that year to focus on starting a family.<ref name=":2" />

Gates served as a member of Duke University's Board of Trustees from 1996 to 2003.<ref>[http://www.fuqua.duke.edu/admin/extaff/news/publications/winter96/board.htm Gates Joins Trustees] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20120802153624/http://www.fuqua.duke.edu/admin/extaff/news/publications/winter96/board.htm |date=August 2, 2012 }}, Fuqua.duke.edu; retrieved June 29, 2013.</ref> She attends the annual [[Bilderberg Group]] conference and has held a seat on the Board of Directors of [[The Washington Post]] company since 2004.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ghco.com/news-releases/news-release-details/melinda-french-gates-elected-director-washington-post-company|title=Melinda French Gates Elected a Director of The Washington Post Company|website=Graham Holdings Company|language=en|accessdate=March 14, 2019}}</ref> She was also on the Board of Directors at [[Drugstore.com]], but left in August 2006 to focus on philanthropy projects.<ref>{{cite web|title=Officers and Directors|url=http://investor.shareholder.com/drugstore/management.cfm|publisher=Drugstore.com|year=2005|archive-date=February 7, 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060207063601/http://investor.shareholder.com/drugstore/management.cfm|accessdate=February 14, 2008}}</ref> Since 2000, Gates has been active in the public eye, stating "As I thought about strong women of history, I realized that they stepped out in some way".<ref name=":1" /> This has helped her work become recognized, while shaping and delivering goals of the [[Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation]]. As of 2014, Bill and Melinda have donated US$28 billion to the Foundation.<ref>[https://www.forbes.com/profile/bill-gates Bill Gates' philanthropy profile], forbes.com; retrieved April 23, 2016.</ref> In 2015 Gates founded Pivotal Ventures as a separate, independent organization to identify, help develop and implement innovative solutions to problems affecting U.S. women and families.<ref>[https://www.pivotalventures.org/#who-we-are Pivotal Ventures-Who we are], pivotalventures.org; retrieved April 17, 2020.</ref>

===Writer===
In 2019, Gates debuted as an author with the book ''The Moment of Lift: How Empowering Women Changes the World''. Former President [[Barack Obama|Obama]] starred in a comedy sketch in order to promote it.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oprahmag.com/entertainment/a27434548/melinda-gates-moment-of-lift-book-tour-barack-obama/|title=Barack Obama Is the Ultimate Funny Guy in a Video Made for Melinda Gates|first=Michelle|last=Darrisaw|date=May 10, 2019|website=Oprah Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.geekwire.com/2019/melinda-gates-draws-huge-crowd-hometown-finale-moment-lift-tour-surprise-intro/|title=Melinda Gates draws huge crowd for hometown finale of 'Moment of Lift' tour, with surprise intro|date=May 10, 2019|website=GeekWire}}</ref> The book highlights the failure to acknowledge women's unpaid work, drawing on feminist economist [[Marilyn Waring|Dame Marilyn Waring]]'s book ''[[If Women Counted]]''.<ref name="abcnews">{{cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/US/book-excerpt-melinda-gates-moment-lift-empowering-women/story?id=62573695|title=Melinda Gates' 'The Moment of Lift'|website=ABC News}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Bill og Melinda Gates 2009-06-03 (bilde 01).JPG|thumb|Melinda and her husband, [[Bill Gates|Bill]], 2009]]
Melinda began dating [[Microsoft]] CEO [[Bill Gates]] in 1987, after meeting him at a trade fair in New York.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/3428721.stm|title=Profile: Bill Gates|work=BBC News|date=January 26, 2004}}</ref> In 1994, she married Gates in a private ceremony held in [[Lanai]], [[Hawaii]]. They have three children: Jennifer, Phoebe and Rory Gates.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/microsoft-founder-bill-gates-daughter-jennifer-gates-life-2018-1|title=Inside the life of Bill Gates' daughter Jennifer, an elite equestrian who stands to inherit 'a minuscule portion' of her father's $110 billion fortune and is engaged to accomplished Egyptian equestrian Nayel Nassar|last=Bose|first=Áine Cain, Debanjali|website=Business Insider|access-date=April 20, 2020}}</ref> The family resides in [[Xanadu 2.0]], an [[earth sheltering|earth-sheltered mansion]] overlooking [[Lake Washington]] in [[Medina, Washington]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-12-16|title=Peek inside Bill Gates' $124 million mansion Xanadu 2.0 - Take a look at Bill Gates' new house|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/nation-world/peek-inside-bill-gates-124-million-mansion-xanadu-2-0/pool-with-underwater-music-system/slideshow/56019673.cms|access-date=2020-08-15|website=The Economic Times}}</ref> The family also owns an oceanfront residence in [[Del Mar, California]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Vasishta|first=Jeff|date=2020-04-24|title=Bill and Melinda Gates Pick Up Del Mar Oceanfront Mansion|url=https://variety.com/2020/dirt/moguls/bill-and-melinda-gates-pick-up-del-mar-oceanfront-mansion-1234590980/|access-date=2020-08-15|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Molnar|first=Phillip|date=2020-04-24|title=Report: Bill and Melinda Gates buy a $43-million Del Mar home|url=https://www.latimes.com/business/real-estate/story/2020-04-24/report-bill-and-melinda-gates-buy-43million-del-mar-home|access-date=2020-08-15|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref>

Gates and her husband were suggested as possible vice-presidential picks in the [[2016 United States presidential election]], according to a [[Podesta emails|reportedly hacked email]] published by [[WikiLeaks]] from [[Hillary Clinton]]'s campaign chairman, [[John Podesta]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Read the Surprising List of Hillary Clinton's Potential Running Mates|magazine=Time|url=http://time.com/4534899/hillary-clinton-wikileaks-running-mates/}}</ref>

==Awards and recognition==
In 2002, Melinda and Bill Gates received the Award for Greatest Public Service Benefiting the Disadvantaged, an award given out annually by [[Jefferson Awards for Public Service|Jefferson Awards]].<ref>[http://www.jeffersonawards.org/pastwinners/national Jefferson Awards website] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101124043935/http://jeffersonawards.org/pastwinners/national |date=November 24, 2010 }}, jeffersonawards.org; accessed April 23, 2016.</ref>

In December 2005, Melinda and Bill Gates were named by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' as [[Time Magazine Person of the Year|Persons of the Year]] alongside [[Bono (U2)|Bono]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://content.time.comhttp//content.time.com/time/specials/packages/0,28757,2016788,00.html?xid=fblike|title=Persons of the Year 2005 - TIME|website=TIME.com|language=en-us|access-date=April 21, 2020}}</ref> Melinda and Bill Gates received the Spanish [[Prince of Asturias Award|Prince of Asturias Award for International Cooperation]] on May 4, 2006, in recognition of their world impact through charitable giving.<ref>[http://www.fpa.es/en/prince-of-asturias-awards/international-cooperation "2012 Laureates&nbsp;– Prince of Asturias Awards"], fpa.es; retrieved June 2, 2013.</ref>

In November 2006, Melinda was awarded the Insignia of the [[Order of the Aztec Eagle]], together with Bill, who was awarded the Placard of the same order, both for their philanthropic work around the world in the areas of health and education, particularly in [[Mexico]], and specifically in the program "Un país de lectores".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coachabilityfoundation.org/single-post/Melinda-Gates|title=Talk about us: Melinda Gates|website=Empowering Entrepreneurs Women {{!}} Coachability Foundation|language=es|accessdate=March 14, 2019}}</ref>

[[File:Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation front.JPG|thumb|Front building of the [[Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation]] in [[Seattle]]]]

In May 2006, in honor of her work to improve the lives of children locally and around the world, Seattle Children's Hospital dedicated the Melinda French Gates Ambulatory Care building<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seattlechildrens.org/about/history/|title=Seattle Children's History|website=Seattle Children’s Hospital|language=en|access-date=April 21, 2020}}</ref> at [[Seattle Children's]] (formerly Children's Hospital and Regional Medical Center).<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20100616134718/http://seattlechildrens.org/about/history/name-history/ How Our Name Evolved], Seattlechildrens.org; retrieved June 29, 2013.</ref> She chaired a campaign for the hospital to fundraise $300 million to expand facilities, fund under-compensated and uncompensated care, and grow the hospital's research program to find cures and treatments.<ref>[http://www.seattlechildrens.org/media/press-release/2006/02/000199 Seattle Children's Hospital Unveils $300 Million Capital Campaign&nbsp;– $200 Million Already Raised] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130603093525/http://www.seattlechildrens.org/media/press-release/2006/02/000199/ |date=June 3, 2013 }}, seattlechildrens.org; retrieved June 29, 2013.</ref>

In 2007, Gates received an honorary doctorate in medicine from the [[Karolinska Institute]] in Stockholm, Sweden.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.ki.se/melinda-gates-honorary-doctor-at-karolinska-institutet|title=Melinda Gates honorary doctor at Karolinska Institutet {{!}} Karolinska Institutet Nyheter|website=news.ki.se|language=en|access-date=April 21, 2020}}</ref> In 2009, she and her husband received honorary degrees from the [[University of Cambridge]]. Their benefaction of $210 million in 2000 set up the [[Gates Cambridge Scholarships|Gates Cambridge Trust]], which funds postgraduate scholars from outside the UK to study at the University.<ref name="nyu1">{{cite web|title=Gates Cambridge Trust|url=https://www.nyu.edu/academics/awards-and-highlights/global-awards/scholarships/gates-cambridge-trust.html|publisher=New York University|accessdate=November 30, 2016}}</ref><ref name="cam1">{{cite web|title=Gates Cambridge Trust announces new Provost|url=https://www.cam.ac.uk/news/gates-cambridge-trust-announces-new-provost|website=University of Cambridge|publisher=University of Cambridge|date=May 13, 2013}}</ref> Lastly, she was awarded an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters by Duke University in 2013 as a tribute for her philanthropic commitment.

She was ranked #3 in ''[[Forbes]]'' 2013, 2014, 2015 and 2017 lists of the [[Forbes Magazine's List of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|100 Most Powerful Women]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/power-women|work=Forbes|title=The World's 100 Most Powerful Women 2013|accessdate=April 23, 2016}}</ref> #4 in 2012 and 2016 and #6 in 2011 and [[Forbes list of The World's 100 Most Powerful Women#2004 (top 10 selection)|2018]].

She was awarded the [[UCSF]] medal in 2013.<ref>{{cite web |title=UCSF Medal |url=https://chancellor.ucsf.edu/chancellor-awards/ucsf-medal |website=Office of the Chancellor |accessdate=1 July 2020 |language=en}}</ref> Gates was appointed an honorary [[Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] in 2013 for services to philanthropy and international development.<ref>{{cite news|title=Angela Ahrendts and Melinda Gates made (honorary) Dames of the British Empire|url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2014/jan/28/angela-ahrendts-melinda-gates-made-dames|work=theguardian.com|accessdate=May 21, 2014}}</ref> In recognition of the foundation's philanthropic activities in India, Bill and Melinda jointly received India's third-highest civilian honor, [[Padma Bhushan]], in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/advani-bachchan-dilip-kumar-get-padma-vibhushan/article6821421.ece|title=Advani, Bachchan, Dilip Kumar get Padma Vibhushan|author=PTI|work=The Hindu|accessdate=April 23, 2016}}</ref> In 2016, [[President of the United States|President]] [[Barack Obama]] awarded Gates and her husband with the [[Presidential Medal of Freedom]] for their philanthropic efforts.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2016/11/16/president-obama-names-recipients-presidential-medal-freedom |title=President Obama Names Recipients of the Presidential Medal of Freedom |date=November 16, 2016 |website=whitehouse.gov |publisher=[[The White House]] |accessdate= November 16, 2016}}</ref>

==Women in technology==
Gates' experience of a male-dominated workplace at Microsoft inspired her to encourage more women in the computing field.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/melinda-gates-reveals-aggressive-male-dominated-life-microsoft-she-calls-us-redesign-workplace/1459159|title=Melinda Gates reveals 'aggressive,' male-dominated life at Microsoft as she calls on all of us to redesign the workplace|website=www.campaignlive.co.uk|accessdate=March 14, 2019}}</ref> In September 2016, she announced her desire on increasing the diversity in the workplace, especially in the technology industry, stating: "Every company needs technology, and yet we’re graduating fewer women technologists. That is not good for society. We have to change it."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://money.com/money/4514589/melinda-gates-women-technology-jobs/|title=Melinda Gates Wants More Women in Tech Jobs|website=Money|language=en|accessdate=March 13, 2019}}</ref> Gates also spoke about this topic at the 2017 [[Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing]], an annual series of conferences.<ref>{{cite web|title=Melinda Gates on women in tech, her first love, and the origins of Comic Sans|url=https://www.geekwire.com/2017/live-melinda-gates-grace-hopper-celebration-women-computing/|website=GeekWire|date=October 4, 2017}}</ref>

In the 2016 annual letter from the Gates Foundation, Gates said women should "spend more time doing paid work, starting businesses, or otherwise contributing to the economic well-being of societies around the world. The fact that they can't, holds their families and communities back."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gates|first1=Bill and Melinda|title=Two Superpowers We Wish We Had|url=https://www.gatesnotes.com/2016-Annual-Letter|website=gatesnotes.com}}</ref>{{non sequitur|date=September 2019|reason=This quote becomes incredibly weird without proper context. Why should women spend more time doing such activities? Most importantly: why can't they?}}
