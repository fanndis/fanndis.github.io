'''Dame Donna Langley-Shamshiri''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|DBE}} (born 1968<ref>''[https://www2.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2bbdb7d4bb Donna Langley]''. In: bfi.org.uk, Access date October 10, 2020.</ref>) is a British movie executive who is Chairwoman of [[Universal Pictures]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/donna-langley/|title=Donna Langley|website=Forbes}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/universal-s-donna-langley-unveils-new-executive-team-1181694|title=Universal's Donna Langley Unveils Executive Team|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en|access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref> She was profiled in ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]''{{'}}s "Power of Women" issue in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|author1=Jordyn Holman|title=Donna Langley Provides Resources and Mentorship to Women in Vital Voices Program|url=https://variety.com/2014/more/features/donna-langley-mentors-women-in-vital-voices-program-1201323085/|accessdate=15 August 2015|work=Variety|date=October 7, 2014}}</ref> 

==Career==
Langley began her career as a production executive at [[New Line Cinema]]. In 2001, she became senior VP of production at Universal Pictures.<ref>{{cite news|author1=Alexandra Cheney|title=Donna Langley's Star Rises With Enlarged Role at Universal Pictures|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/news/donna-langleys-star-rises-with-enlarged-role-at-universal-pictures-1201069580/|accessdate=15 August 2015|work=Variety|date=January 26, 2014}}</ref> 

In her time at Universal, Langley has overseen such film franchises as [[The Fast and the Furious|Fast & the Furious]] as well as [[Despicable Me (franchise)|Despicable Me]] and the [[Bourne (film series)|Bourne]] movies.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/exec/donna-langley/|title=Donna Langley|last=Ramsay|first=Derek|date=2017-10-07|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref> Her work also includes overseeing the global specialty division, Focus Features, as well as DreamWorks Animation, acquired by Universal in 2016.<ref name=":0" />

Throughout her career, she has supported mentorship opportunities for women.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/moiraforbes/2018/12/20/movie-mogul-donna-langley-on-breaking-barriers-and-box-office-records/|title=Movie Mogul Donna Langley On Breaking Barriers And Box Office Records|last=Forbes|first=Moira|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref> Langley helped launch The Hollywood Reporter's Women in Film Mentorship program.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.global-ambassadors.org/people/donna-langley/|title=Donna Langley|website=Global Ambassadors|language=en|access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref> She also serves as an ambassador and board member for the nonprofit [[Vital Voices]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vitalvoices.org/people/donna-langley/|title=Donna Langley|website=Vital Voices|language=en|access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref>

Langley was appointed [[Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (DBE) in the [[2020 New Year Honours]] for services to film and entertainment.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=62866|supp=y|page=N23|date=28 December 2019}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Langley is married with two children, and lives in Los Angeles with her family.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" />
